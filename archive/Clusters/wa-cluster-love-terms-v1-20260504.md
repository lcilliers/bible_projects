## §3 What this picture tells us

### 3.1 Hebrew love is fragmented across 11 clusters + FLAG

There is no Hebrew "love" cluster. The expected core — aheb / ahavah / chesed / racham / dod — is split:

- **ahavah** (the noun) is in **H002 Joy & Rejoicing** (because love appears in joy contexts)
- **aheb** (the verb, friend-sense) is in **H039 Peace & Well-being** (because friendship-love appears in shalom contexts)
- **ahav** (lover, illicit) is in **H043 Lust & Fornication**
- **chesed** + **racham** (covenantal love-loyalty) is in **H019 Covenantal Core** (the 180-term mega-cluster)
- **rachum** (compassionate) is in **H046 Iniquity & Forgiveness**
- **rachamani** (compassionate) is in **H011 Mourning & Weeping**
- **machmal** + **chemlah** (compassion) are in **H049/H054 distress clusters**
- **rachamin** (Aramaic compassion) is in **H041 Aramaic Faith & Strength**
- **yedidut** (beloved) is in **H003 Enmity & Aversion** ⚠ — a probable error
- **dod** + **yadid** (beloved family) are in **FLAG** — uncategorised

There is no single "love" cluster. Every Hebrew love-term is interpreted by the cluster as an *adjacency* to whatever else is happening — joy, shalom, fornication, mourning, distress, the covenantal core. The Hebrew love-as-a-faculty disappears as a structural unit.

### 3.2 Greek love is fragmented across 7 clusters + FLAG, but with one clean island

The phil- family clusters cleanly into **G008 Phileō Love & Affection** — the only place where love is the dominant theme. But:

- **agapē** (the noun) is buried in **G009 Spirit-Fruit Virtues**
- **agapaō** (the verb) is buried in **G026 Truth, Love & Holiness** (a mega-cluster)
- **agapētos** (beloved — 60 verses including the divine voice) is in **FLAG** — not assigned at all

So even on the Greek side: agapē/agapaō/agapētos — the three forms of the *primary* NT love word — are split across three different buckets (one cluster each + FLAG).

### 3.3 The deepest finding

**The clustering treats love as adjacency, not as anchor.** The vector space groups terms by *shared verses* and *shared semantic features*, both of which place love-terms next to whatever they appear with — joy, peace, lust, mourning, covenant, etc. There is no signal in the input data that says "love is a faculty in itself" — that is a *theological/anthropological* judgement, not a vector-similarity judgement.

This is the structural argument for **a faculty-based grouping** as a separate layer on top of the cluster ecosystem. The clusters tell you "love appears alongside X" (which is descriptively true). The faculty-based grouping would say "these terms are *expressions of the inner-being capacity to love*" (which is the analytical question).

If the inner-being faculty is the actual unit of analysis (as the program reset proposed), then the right next step is to define a "love-faculty" set independently of the clusters, then *use the cluster ecosystem to track which clusters absorb the faculty terms*. That preserves the diagnostic value of the clusters without forcing the faculty to live in any one cluster.
