## Section 2: Semantic Characterisation

### 2.1 The Triple Meaning of Ru.ach — the Foundation of the Registry

Reading all 194 H7307G verses and all 137 H7307H verses establishes the foundational fact of the spirit vocabulary: **ru.ach carries three meanings simultaneously — wind, breath, and spirit — and the biblical writers exploit this three-way identity as a theological argument, not merely a linguistic convenience.** John 3:8 makes this explicit in Greek (pneuma, the NT heir of ru.ach): "the wind blows where it wishes... so it is with everyone born of the Spirit." The same word names the physical and the spiritual movement, precisely because both are divine, sovereign, and invisible-to-origin. Reading the full Hebrew corpus confirms this is not a later Greek development — it is rooted in the Hebrew triple sense from Genesis onwards.

**Pattern 1 — Ru.ach as the divine creative wind/breath.** Gen 1:2 — "the Spirit of God (ru.ach Elohim) was hovering over the face of the waters." The creation account opens with the ru.ach of God as the active divine presence before light, order, or life exist. The same term appears in the dry-bones vision: Eze 37:9 — "Prophesy to the breath (ru.ach)... and say to the breath (ru.ach), Thus says the Lord God: Come from the four winds (ru.ach), O breath (ru.ach), and breathe (na.phach) on these slain." Four uses of ru.ach in two verses — wind, breath, spirit, and divine command all converge. Psa 33:6 — "by the word of the Lord the heavens were made, and by the breath (ru.ach) of his mouth all their host." Psa 104:30 — "when you send forth your Spirit (ru.ach), they are created, and you renew the face of the ground." The Spirit/wind/breath of God is the agent of creation, renewal, and eschatological resurrection.

**Pattern 2 — Ru.ach as the human spirit — the inner person in its most transcendent dimension.** Psa 31:5 — "Into your hand I commit my spirit (ru.ach)" — the prayer Jesus prays from the cross (Luk 23:46). Psa 51:10 — "create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit (ru.ach) within me." Psa 51:17 — "a broken spirit (ru.ach); a broken and contrite heart, O God, you will not despise." Pro 16:32 — "he who rules his spirit (ru.ach) than he who takes a city." Pro 18:14 — "a man's spirit (ru.ach) will endure sickness, but a crushed spirit — who can bear?" Pro 20:27 — "the spirit of man (ne.sha.mah) is the lamp of the Lord, searching all his innermost parts." Ecc 12:7 — "the dust returns to the earth as it was, and the spirit (ru.ach) returns to God who gave it." The human ru.ach is the dimension of the person that is directed Godward, that carries the most intimate moral self-knowledge, and that returns to God at death.

**Pattern 3 — Ru.ach as the Spirit given upon and within persons for prophetic and charismatic activity.** Num 11:17,25,26 — the Spirit that is on Moses is distributed to the 70 elders, and they prophesy. Judg 3:10 — "the Spirit of the Lord was upon him" (Othniel). Judg 6:34 — "the Spirit of the Lord clothed Gideon." 1Sa 16:13 — "the Spirit of the Lord rushed upon David." 2Sa 23:2 — "the Spirit of the Lord speaks by me." The charismatic Spirit rushes upon leaders, judges, prophets, and kings for specific purposes, then may depart (1Sa 16:14 — "the Spirit of the Lord departed from Saul"). This is the Spirit as divine empowerment for a task — not the permanent indwelling of the new covenant.

**Pattern 4 — The eschatological Spirit — the new covenant promise.** Reading the prophetic corpus reveals the programme's most important Spirit-cluster: Isa 32:15 — "until the Spirit is poured upon us from on high." Isa 42:1 — "I have put my Spirit upon him." Isa 44:3 — "I will pour my Spirit upon your offspring." Isa 61:1 — "The Spirit of the Lord God is upon me, because the Lord has anointed me." Eze 11:19 — "I will give them one heart, and a new spirit I will put within them." **Eze 36:26-27** — "I will give you a new heart, and a new spirit I will put within you... and I will put my Spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes." Eze 37:14 — "I will put my Spirit within you, and you shall live." Joe 2:28-29 — "I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh." Zec 12:10 — "I will pour out on the house of David... a spirit of grace and pleas for mercy." The eschatological Spirit is no longer the rushing empowerment of the charismatic era but the interior indwelling that transforms the will, enables obedience, and reconstitutes the person from within.

**Eze 36:26-27 as the OT's definitive Spirit statement.** Reading all H7307G verses confirms: Eze 36:26-27 is the hinge of the entire OT Spirit theology. It names four simultaneous actions:
1. A new heart given (replacing the old heart of stone)
2. A new spirit placed within (the person's inner orientation renewed)
3. The heart of stone removed (the resistant will cleared)
4. My Spirit placed within you (the divine ru.ach taking up permanent residence)

The distinction between "a new spirit I will put within you" (the human spirit renewed) and "my Spirit I will put within you" (the divine Spirit given) is analytically crucial. Both are promised in the same breath. This is the OT's most precise statement of the relationship between the human spirit and the divine Spirit — they are distinct but they are co-located: the new human spirit and the indwelling divine Spirit occupy the same inner space, which is both heart (v.26) and inner being (v.27).

### 2.2 Ne.sha.mah — The Animating Breath

Reading all 24 ne.sha.mah verses confirms the term's role as the more specific vocabulary for the divine breath of life.

Gen 2:7 — "breathed into his nostrils the breath of life (ne.shmat cha.yim), and the man became a living being (ne.phesh cha.yah)." This is the most theologically dense single verse for the relationship between the divine breath (ne.sha.mah), the human spirit, and the soul. The action: God breathes his ne.sha.mah into the dust; the result: the dust becomes a living ne.phesh. The ne.sha.mah is the divine in-breath that animates the creaturely person. The man does not have a ne.sha.mah as a possession — he becomes a living ne.phesh because God breathed his ne.sha.mah into him.

Job 32:8 — "it is the spirit (ne.sha.mah) in man, the breath of the Almighty, that makes him understand." Ne.sha.mah is here the source of human cognitive capacity — understanding comes from the divine breath, not from the creaturely self alone. Job 33:4 — "the Spirit of God has made me, and the breath (ne.sha.mah) of the Almighty gives me life." Ne.sha.mah and ru.ach of God are in synonymous parallelism: both describe the divine animating action. Pro 20:27 — "the spirit of man (ne.sha.mah) is the lamp of the Lord, searching all his innermost parts." The ne.sha.mah as the divine searchlight within the person — the dimension of the human being through which God sees the innermost self.

Psa 150:6 — "let everything that has breath (ne.sha.mah) praise the Lord!" The doxological function: every creature who carries the divine animating breath is summoned to return praise. Isa 42:5 — God "who created the heavens... who gives breath (ne.sha.mah) to the people on it and spirit (ru.ach) to those who walk in it." The two terms used together: ne.sha.mah as the animating breath, ru.ach as the broader spirit/life-force.

Isa 57:16 — "For I will not contend forever... for the spirit (ru.ach) would grow faint before me, and the breath (ne.sha.mah) of life that I made." God restrains his anger because the creaturely spirit cannot withstand divine wrath — the ne.sha.mah is the creatureliness of the person before the Creator.

**Ne.sha.mah/ru.ach distinction:** Ne.sha.mah is the specifically animating, life-giving divine breath — what makes dust into a person (Gen 2:7), what enables understanding (Job 32:8), what constitutes the inner searchlight of conscience (Pro 20:27). Ru.ach is the broader vocabulary covering wind, breath, spirit, disposition, and the Spirit of God in its full activity. Ne.sha.mah is interior and animating; ru.ach is both interior and exterior, both human and divine, both creaturely and cosmic.

### 2.3 The NT Spirit — Pneuma and the Paraclete Theology

Reading all 341 G4151G pneuma verses confirms the NT vocabulary covers four distinct pneuma registers, all expressed by the same word:

**Register 1 — The Holy Spirit as personal divine agent.** The Gospels and Acts provide the most concentrated pneuma vocabulary in the corpus. The Holy Spirit:
- Conceives Jesus (Mat 1:18,20; Luk 1:35)
- Descends at Jesus's baptism "like a dove" (Mat 3:16; Joh 1:32-33)
- Drives/leads Jesus into the wilderness (Mat 4:1; Mar 1:12)
- Speaks through prophets and apostles (Act 1:16; 2Pe 1:21)
- Is given at Pentecost (Act 2:4)
- Cannot be blasphemed without eternal consequence (Mat 12:31-32)
- Falls, comes upon, and fills persons (Act 2:4; 4:8,31; 10:44; 13:9)
- Directs the mission of the church (Act 8:29; 13:2; 16:6-7)
- Is the content of baptism alongside water (Mat 28:19; Joh 3:5)
- Bears witness with the human spirit (Rom 8:16)

**Register 2 — The indwelling Spirit as the ground of the new life.** Paul's Spirit theology in Rom 8 is the NT's most sustained analysis: Rom 8:2 — "the law of the Spirit of life has set you free." Rom 8:9 — "you are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if in fact the Spirit of God dwells in you. Anyone who does not have the Spirit of Christ does not belong to him." Rom 8:14 — "all who are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God." Rom 8:15-16 — "you have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!' The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God." The indwelling Spirit is: the condition for belonging to Christ, the agent of sonship, the source of the "Abba" cry, and the co-witness to the human spirit of sonship.

**Register 3 — The Paraclete — the Johannine Spirit theology.** Reading the John 14-16 pneuma verses establishes the most personal and relational Spirit theology in the corpus. Joh 14:17 — "the Spirit of truth, whom the world cannot receive, because it neither sees him nor knows him. You know him, for he dwells with you and will be in you." The Spirit's presence is an interior knowing — the world cannot receive him because reception requires the capacity for interior relationship. Joh 14:26 — "the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, he will teach you all things and bring to your remembrance all that I have said to you." Joh 15:26 — "the Spirit of truth, who proceeds from the Father." Joh 16:8 — the Spirit convicts the world. Joh 16:13 — "when the Spirit of truth comes, he will guide you into all the truth, for he will not speak on his own authority, but whatever he hears he will speak, and he will declare to you the things that are to come." The Paraclete is a teaching, remembering, guiding, convicting, testifying personal agent. He is not an influence or a force but a person with attributes of speech and knowledge.

**Register 4 — The unclean/demonic spirits.** Reading the exorcism material carefully: the unclean spirits (pneumata akatharton) are: named (Mar 5:9 — "Legion, for we are many"), possessing, convulsing, crying out, recognising Jesus ("I know who you are — the Holy One of God"), subject to command ("Come out!"), and capable of transfer (from man to pigs). The most analytically significant observation: the unclean spirit vocabulary establishes the spirit realm as genuinely populated — there are spirits that are not divine and not human. The demonic spirit vocabulary is the shadow of the divine Spirit vocabulary. Reading them together: the Holy Spirit is what the unclean spirit perverts — both are real, both are present within persons, both shape behaviour from within. The person filled with the Holy Spirit and the person tormented by an unclean spirit are both inhabited — but by radically different presences.

### 2.4 Hagios — The Holy Spirit's Name and the Community's Holiness

Reading all 210 G0040G hagios verses reveals two primary registers:

**The Holy Spirit as hagios** (the most concentrated use): The Spirit's name contains its nature. "Holy Spirit" (pneuma hagion) is not merely a title — hagion names the Spirit's essential character as distinct from all that is unholy. Mat 12:32 — "whoever speaks against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven." The unforgivable sin is specifically against the *Holy* Spirit — the holiness of the Spirit is what defines the boundary that cannot be crossed without permanent consequence.

**The saints/holy people (hagioi) as the Spirit's community**: Rom 1:7 — "called to be saints (hagios)." 1Co 1:2 — "sanctified in Christ Jesus, called to be saints." The community is named by the same word as the Spirit: they are hagioi because the Holy One (hagios) has taken up residence in them. Lev 19:2 — "you shall be holy, for I the Lord your God am holy" — the command for holiness is grounded in divine holiness. 1Pe 1:15-16 quotes it in the NT context: "as he who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, since it is written, 'You shall be holy, for I am holy.'" The Spirit communicates divine holiness to the community; the community becomes hagioi because the pneuma hagion dwells in them.

The Act 7:51 verse is analytically critical: "You stiff-necked people, uncircumcised in heart and ears, you always resist the Holy Spirit." Resistance to the Holy Spirit is identified with uncircumcised heart — the hardened-heart vocabulary from the heart registry directly invoked in the Spirit context. The heart-spirit boundary is not merely theological — it is the specific site where the human person either receives or resists divine presence.

### 2.5 Na.phach, Re.pha.im, Ov, and the Peripheral Vocabulary

**H5301 na.phach (to breathe, 12 verses).** Gen 2:7 — "breathed into his nostrils the breath of life" — na.phach + ne.sha.mah together form the act of divine animation. Eze 37:9 — "Prophesy to the breath (ru.ach); come from the four winds (ru.ach), O breath (ru.ach), and breathe (na.phach) on these slain." The dry-bones vision uses na.phach for the prophetic act of divine re-animation. Joh 20:22 — "he breathed on them and said to them, 'Receive the Holy Spirit'" — the NT parallel uses emphusaō (the Greek equivalent of na.phach), explicitly recalling Gen 2:7. The Johannine Pentecost is a second creation: the same divine in-breathing that animated Adam now animates the apostolic community with the Holy Spirit.

**H7496 re.pha.im (shades, 8 verses).** These are the departed dead in Sheol — the "shades" who exist in a diminished, non-vigorous state. Job 26:5 — "the dead (re.pha.im) tremble under the waters." Psa 88:10 — "do you work wonders for the dead? Do the departed (re.pha.im) rise up to praise you?" Isa 26:14 — "they are dead, they will not live; they are shades, they will not arise." The re.pha.im vocabulary defines the boundary: the spirit without the living ru.ach is a shade — present but diminished. Isa 26:19 — "your dead shall live; their bodies shall rise" — the resurrection promise is the reversal of the re.pha.im condition: the ru.ach returns and the shade becomes fully alive.

**H0178 ov (medium, 16 verses).** The ov vocabulary establishes the prohibition dimension of the spirit registry: the attempt to access the spirit realm through human agency rather than divine disclosure. Lev 19:31 — "do not turn to mediums (ov) or necromancers." The ov/medium is literally "the one who has a familiar spirit" — an entity accessed through divination. 1Sa 28 — Saul's consultation of the medium at Endor: the desperate king who has lost the Spirit of the Lord now seeks access to the spiritual realm through the forbidden channel. The structural irony: Saul loses the ru.ach of the Lord (1Sa 16:14) and turns to the ov — the prohibited spirit-substitute. The ov vocabulary defines by negation what the genuine Spirit-relationship is: received, not accessed; given, not summoned.

**H1100I be.liy.ya.al (Belial, 11 verses).** The worthlessness vocabulary entered the spirit registry through 2Co 6:15 — "What accord has Christ with Belial?" In the NT Belial becomes the name of the adversarial spirit-power, the anti-Spirit. The 11 OT verses establish be.liy.ya.al as the character of the person who operates without worth, integrity, or restraint — the contrast to the person "filled with the Spirit." Be.liy.ya.al is the character of the spirit-less person: literally "without benefit" (be.li + ya.al).

**H7307J ru.ach:temper (10 verses).** Reading all 10: these are the spirit-as-disposition verses — the inner inclination that can be ruled or surrendered. Pro 16:32 — "he who rules his spirit (ru.ach) than he who takes a city." Pro 17:27 — "he who has a cool spirit (ru.ach) is a man of understanding." Pro 29:11 — "a fool gives full vent to his spirit (ru.ach)." Ecc 7:9 — "be not quick in your spirit (ru.ach) to become angry." The temper/disposition sub-gloss is the inner spirit as the person's habitual orientation — what the person is inclined toward by nature and character. This is the site where the human spirit and the cultivated character meet: the ruled spirit is the person whose inner disposition has come under the governance of wisdom.

**H7308 ru.ach Aramaic (11 verses).** All in Daniel. Dan 4:8-9, 4:18, 5:11-14 — Daniel has "the spirit of the holy gods" (ru.ach elahim qad.di.shin) — the divine Spirit recognisable to a Babylonian king by its effects: light, understanding, wisdom, and excellent spirit. Dan 5:20 — Nebuchadnezzar's spirit "hardened so that he dealt proudly" — the haughty spirit as the diagnostic of the loss of right relationship. Dan 6:3 — Daniel "had an excellent spirit" distinguishing him above all others. The Aramaic ru.ach vocabulary operates in the court of a pagan empire and yet perfectly identifies what the Hebrew ru.ach Elohim names: the Spirit of God produces recognisable excellence, wisdom, and moral clarity in whoever carries it, regardless of cultural context.

**H0328B at (softly, 6 verses).** Reading all 6: at entered this registry because Isa 19:3 uses it in the spirit-of-confusion context ("the spirit of the Egyptians within them will be emptied out") — the "softly/gently" preposition adjacent to spirit vocabulary. Its own 5 verses (Gen 33:14, 2Sa 18:5, 1Ki 21:27, Job 15:11, Isa 8:6) are entirely non-spirit in content. The registry entry is peripheral bleed. The one relevant adjacency: Isa 8:6 — "because this people has refused the waters of Shiloah that flow gently (at)" — the gentle/quiet flow as a metaphor for the Spirit's unforced movement (parallel to Joh 3:8).

## Section 3: Dimensional Placement

### 3.1 Circle Two — Spirit, Soul, Body

**Primary circle:** Spirit dimension — the spirit registry is definitionally the spirit-circle vocabulary.

But reading all 1,331 verses establishes the more precise mapping:

**Spirit as the transcendent-creaturely interface.** The human ru.ach/pneuma is neither purely divine nor purely creaturely. It is the dimension of the human person that: returns to God at death (Ecc 12:7), is the site of the divine in-breath (Gen 2:7), carries the ne.sha.mah that makes understanding possible (Job 32:8), and is the specific address of the Holy Spirit's indwelling (Eze 36:27; Rom 8:16; Gal 4:6). The spirit is the interface — it is the most creaturely thing God can occupy and the most God-like dimension of the creature.

**The spirit-soul relationship.** Reading the spirit and soul vocabularies together (now that both registries are complete) resolves the programme's most contested inner-being boundary:

The key text is 1Th 5:23 — "may your whole spirit (pneuma) and soul (psuchē) and body (sōma) be kept blameless." This is the NT's only explicit tripartite statement. Reading it against the OT: the soul (ne.phesh/psuchē) is the experiencing, desiring, living-creature dimension; the spirit (ru.ach/pneuma) is the God-oriented, transcendent, animating dimension. They are not the same, and they cannot be collapsed into each other.

Heb 4:12 — "the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul (psuchēs) and of spirit (pneumatos), of joints and marrow." The Word divides what the person cannot divide themselves — the soul-spirit boundary is not self-evident to introspection. Only divine penetration discloses it. This verse resolves PH2-182-001 (soul/spirit boundary): the boundary is real but not naturally discernible.

1Co 15:44-46 — "it is sown a natural body (sōma psuchikon), it is raised a spiritual body (sōma pneumatikon)... The first man Adam became a living being (psuchēn zōsan); the last Adam became a life-giving spirit (pneuma zōopoioun)." The psuchikos/pneumatikos distinction is the eschatological anthropology of the programme: the natural mode (psuchikon) is animated by the living soul (ne.phesh/psuchē); the spiritual mode (pneumatikon) is constituted by and for the Spirit. These are not two different types of people but two modes of the same person — the first mode natural/creaturely, the second mode Spirit-constituted/eschatological.

**The spirit-heart relationship.** The most practically important cross-registry finding: the heart (lev/kardia) is the site of the spirit's indwelling. Eze 36:26-27 — "I will give you a new heart... a new spirit I will put within you... my Spirit within you." Both heart (lev) and spirit (ru.ach) are renewed together in the same divine action. They are co-located but not identical. The heart is the cognitive-volitional-moral centre; the spirit is the God-oriented, transcendent dimension. The new heart and the indwelling Spirit are the new covenant's gift to the person as a whole.

Act 7:51 — "uncircumcised in heart and ears, you always resist the Holy Spirit." The hardened heart resists the Spirit — the heart's condition is the most direct determinant of whether the Spirit is received or refused. The heart-spirit relationship is the programme's most important structural pair: what Pro 4:23 says about the heart ("from it flow the springs of life") and what Eze 36:27 says about the Spirit ("I will cause you to walk in my statutes") belong together as the two dimensions of the renewed inner person.

### 3.2 The Six Dimensions — Spirit's Unique Profile

The spirit vocabulary's engagement with the six dimensions is unlike every other word in the programme:

**Feeling (Affective):** Isa 66:2 — "this is the one to whom I will look: he who is humble and contrite in spirit." Psa 51:17 — "a broken and contrite spirit." The spirit can be broken, troubled (Dan 2:1), vexed (Act 17:16), and refreshed (1Co 16:18). Affective engagement is real but secondary to the spirit's other dimensions.

**Thinking (Cognitive):** Job 32:8 — "the breath of the Almighty makes him understand." 1Co 2:10-11 — "the Spirit searches everything, even the depths of God... no one comprehends the thoughts of God except the Spirit of God." The Spirit is the cognitive agent of divine self-knowledge. 1Co 14:14-15 — "if I pray in a tongue, my spirit prays but my mind is unfruitful." The spirit's cognition is real but operates differently from the mind (nous). The spirit prays where the mind cannot follow.

**Choosing (Volitional):** Mat 26:41 — "the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak." The spirit is the willing dimension — it is where genuine desire for God resides. Eze 36:27 — "I will cause you to walk in my statutes" — the Spirit makes obedience possible by acting on the will from within. This is the Spirit's most distinctive volitional role: not commanding from outside but enabling from within.

**Relating (Relational):** The Spirit of adoption (Rom 8:15), the Paraclete (Joh 14-16), the fellowship of the Spirit (2Co 13:14, Phi 2:1) — the Spirit constitutes and sustains the community of persons who share a Spirit. 1Co 12:13 — "in one Spirit we were all baptized into one body." The Spirit is the relational medium of the community.

**Orienting (Spiritual/God-ward):** This is the spirit's primary dimension — the dimension that makes the spirit what it is. Psa 139:7 — "where shall I go from your Spirit? Or where shall I flee from your presence?" The Spirit is where God's presence meets the human person most intimately. Joh 4:24 — "God is Spirit (pneuma), and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." The spirit is the dimension through which genuine worship — encounter with the divine — is possible.

**Being (Moral/Character):** Gal 5:22 — "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control." The Spirit produces character. Pro 20:27 — the ne.sha.mah is "the lamp of the Lord" — the spirit as the site of moral searchlight. The broken and contrite spirit (Psa 51:17) is the spirit in the correct moral posture before God.

## Section 4: Divine Dimension

### 4.1 God as Spirit — Joh 4:24

"God is Spirit (pneuma), and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth."

Reading this against the entire 1,331-verse corpus, Joh 4:24 is the most compact statement of the Spirit's ontological grounding: God himself is pneuma. The Spirit of God is not a divine attribute or a divine activity — it is the divine nature itself. This is the NT parallel to 1Jo 4:8 ("God is love") — both are statements of divine ontology, not merely divine activity.

The implication for the human spirit vocabulary: if God is pneuma, and if the human spirit (ru.ach/pneuma) is the divine breath in the creature, then the spirit dimension of the person is the point at which the creaturely most closely mirrors the divine nature. Gen 1:26 — "let us make man in our image" — the image of God in the person is most directly the spirit.

### 4.2 The Spirit's Trinitarian Position — Confirmed by Reading

Reading all NT pneuma verses in sequence confirms: the Spirit cannot be discussed in the NT without reference to the Father and the Son. The Spirit:
- Is "of the Father" (Mat 10:20), "of the Son" (Gal 4:6), "of Christ" (Rom 8:9), "of Jesus Christ" (Phi 1:19), "of the Lord" (2Co 3:17), "of God" (1Co 2:14; 3:16) — all the same Spirit
- Proceeds from the Father (Joh 15:26)
- Is sent in the Son's name (Joh 14:26)
- Was given through the Son's breath (Joh 20:22)
- Brings the community into Trinitarian fellowship: 2Co 13:14 — "the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit"

The Spirit is not an independent divine agent — he is the divine presence in its most immediate and interior mode, the mode by which the Father and the Son take up residence in the person.

### 4.3 The Spirit Given, Received, and Grieved

Three divine-human Spirit actions confirmed across the full corpus:

**Given:** God gives the Spirit (1Th 4:8), sends the Spirit (Gal 4:6), pours out the Spirit (Joe 2:28; Act 2:33), breathes the Spirit (Joh 20:22), baptises with the Spirit (Mat 3:11). The Spirit's origin is always divine gift, never human achievement.

**Received:** The response is receiving (Act 2:38), being filled (Luk 1:41; Act 2:4; 4:8), being led by (Rom 8:14), walking by (Gal 5:16), keeping in step with (Gal 5:25). The human role is receptive orientation, not generation.

**Grieved and resisted:** Isa 63:10 — "they rebelled and grieved his Holy Spirit." Eph 4:30 — "do not grieve the Holy Spirit of God, by whom you were sealed." 1Th 5:19 — "do not quench the Spirit." Act 7:51 — "you always resist the Holy Spirit." The Spirit can be grieved, quenched, resisted, and tested (Act 5:9). This establishes the Spirit's personhood: only a person can be grieved; forces and powers cannot be grieved. The language of grieving is the most direct evidence of the Spirit's personal nature.

This is the most radical statement about the body in the NT: it is not a prison for the spirit but the temple of the Spirit.

### 5.2 The Spirit and the Bones — Eze 37

The vision makes explicit what Gen 2:7 implied: the body without the spirit is dead matter. The spirit is not an add-on to an already-living body — it is what makes the body alive at all. The eschatological resurrection is the giving of the Spirit into the reconstituted body.

### 6.2 The Five Causative Pathways for the Spirit

**God → Spirit → person:** The primary pathway throughout. The Spirit does not self-generate — he is given, sent, poured out, breathed, and sealed by the Father and the Son. The Spirit is always the divine initiative reaching the human person.

**Spirit → transformation:** Eze 36:27 — "I will put my Spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes." **Person → Spirit (resistive):** Grieving (Eph 4:30), quenching (1Th 5:19), resisting (Act 7:51), testing (Act 5:9). The person can obstruct the Spirit's work. This is the freedom-dimension of the Spirit relationship: the Spirit does not override human agency but works through the yielded person and is blocked by the resistant one.

## Section 7: Canonical Verse Analysis

### 7.1 Anchor Verse — Eze 36:27

*"And I will put my Spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes and be careful to obey my rules."*

Having read all 1,331 verses, this is the registry's most programme-relevant statement. Four elements:

"I will put my Spirit within you" — the Spirit is not earned, sought, or generated. It is placed — an act of divine initiative. The placement is interior: within you (be.qir.be.khem — in your innermost parts, the qe.rev of the qe.rev).

"Cause you to walk in my statutes" — the Spirit's primary work is moral and volitional, not just experiential. The Spirit doesn't merely give feelings of God's presence; he causes walking. The causative form: the Spirit is the agent; the walking is the result. This resolves the deepest question of the entire programme: how the person who is morally disabled (Jer 17:9 — the deceitful heart) actually obeys. The answer is not greater effort but divine in-breathing.

"Be careful to obey my rules" — the double emphasis: the Spirit produces both the desire (walking) and the carefulness (be.careful). The Spirit addresses both the volitional (will you walk?) and the cognitive (will you attend carefully?) dimensions simultaneously.

Read alongside Eze 36:26: the new heart and the indwelling Spirit are the two gifts given together. The heart is the human faculty renewed (from stone to flesh); the Spirit is the divine presence given (from outside to inside). Neither gift is sufficient alone — the new heart without the Spirit is an improved will without divine enabling; the Spirit without the new heart encounters a hardened will. Together they constitute the full new covenant.

### 7.2 Gen 2:7 — The Creation Breath

*"Then the Lord God formed the man of dust from the ground and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living being."*

Having read all 24 ne.sha.mah and 12 na.phach verses, this verse stands as the most foundational statement in the entire programme about the relationship between the divine and human inner lives. Three elements:

"Formed the man of dust from the ground" — the material origin of the person is earth/dust. The person is creaturely through and through. No part of the person is originally or inherently spiritual.

"Breathed into his nostrils the breath of life (ne.shmat cha.yim)" — the divine initiative: God performs the most intimate possible act — direct breath-to-breath contact between Creator and creature. The ne.sha.mah is the divine breath becoming the human breath.

"The man became a living being (ne.phesh cha.yah)" — the result is not a spirit-person but a soul-person: the animating divine breath produces a living ne.phesh. The spirit dimension is not separate from the person — it is the animating ground of the whole living being.

**Cross-registry resolution (PH2-182-002):** The soul is constituted by the divine breath entering the dust-body. The ne.sha.mah is not the same as the ne.phesh — the breath animates the body and the result is a living soul. Spirit (divine in-breath) + body (dust) = soul (living being). This is the anthropological ground of the programme.

### 7.3 Rom 8:15-16 — The Spirit of Adoption

*"For you did not receive the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear, but you have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!' The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God."*

Having read all 341 pneuma verses, this passage stands as the NT's most concentrated statement about the Spirit's relational and identity-forming work. Three elements:

"Not the spirit of fear but the Spirit of adoption" — the Spirit's work is identity-constitutive: it relocates the person from fear (the condition of slavery) to sonship. The two spirits are contrasted: the spirit of fear characterises the slave's relationship to the master; the Spirit of adoption characterises the child's relationship to the Father. PH2-061-010 resolved: the Spirit is the mechanism by which the fear that characterises the spirit of slavery is replaced by the confidence of sonship.

"By whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!'" — the Spirit produces the most intimate possible address to God. Gal 4:6 confirms: "God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, crying, 'Abba! Father!'" The Spirit in the heart produces the cry of the Spirit in the Son. The community's prayer is not self-generated but Spirit-generated — the Abba-cry is the Spirit praying in the person.

"The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit" — the Spirit's testimony and the human spirit's testimony are co-witness. Neither alone is sufficient: the human spirit's sense of sonship needs divine confirmation; the Spirit's testimony engages the human spirit's own witness. The co-witness pattern: spirit to Spirit, creature to Creator, interior to interior. This verse provides the most precise description of Spirit-human spirit communication in the corpus.

### 7.4 1Co 2:10-11 — The Spirit's Epistemic Function

*"These things God has revealed to us through the Spirit. For the Spirit searches everything, even the depths of God. For who knows a person's thoughts except the spirit of that person, which is in him? So also no one comprehends the thoughts of God except the Spirit of God."*

Having read all 341 pneuma verses, this is the most analytically important passage for the programme's epistemological claims. The parallel structure:

Human plane: "who knows a person's thoughts except the spirit of that person?" Only the human spirit has interior access to the human person's depths. External observation cannot penetrate to the inner life.

Divine plane: "no one comprehends the thoughts of God except the Spirit of God." The same principle: only the Spirit has interior access to the divine depths.

**Cross-plane conclusion:** The Spirit given to believers (v.12) is the Spirit who searches the depths of God — the believer, through the Spirit, receives interior access to the divine self-knowledge. The Spirit's epistemic function is the ground of all revelation: what is known about God is known because the Spirit who knows God's depths has disclosed it.

**Cross-registry connection to heart registry:** The kardiognōstēs (God who knows hearts — Act 1:24, 15:8) is the divine attribute of knowing what humans cannot. The Spirit who searches everything (1Co 2:10) is how that heart-knowledge operates: the Spirit-as-searcher is the dynamic form of the kardiognōstēs attribute.

### 7.5 Joh 3:8 — The Freedom of the Spirit

*"The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear its sound, but you do not know where it comes from or where it goes. So it is with everyone who is born of the Spirit."*

Having read both H7307H ru.ach:breath/wind (137 verses) and G4151G pneuma (341 verses), this is the single verse where the triple sense of ru.ach/pneuma is most compressed. Wind (physical phenomenon), breath (life-force), and Spirit (divine agent) are all in the one saying. The same freedom that belongs to wind belongs to Spirit: both are beyond human control, comprehension of origin, or tracking of destination.

The verse resolves a perennial misunderstanding: the Spirit's work cannot be systematised, controlled, or predicted. The person born of the Spirit has the same quality — the spiritual life cannot be explained by natural cause-and-effect. This is the Spirit's freedom principle: he moves where he will, giving where he chooses (1Co 12:11 — "distributing to each one individually as he wills").

### 7.6 1Th 5:23 — The Tripartite Person

*"Now may the God of peace himself sanctify you completely, and may your whole spirit and soul and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ."*

Having now read the soul (182), heart (183), and spirit (184) registries in full, this verse stands as the programme's most direct statement of the tripartite structure. The three terms:

**Pneuma (spirit):** The God-oriented, transcendent dimension — the dimension that returns to God (Ecc 12:7), that is the site of the Spirit's indwelling (Eze 36:27; Rom 8:16), that prays where the mind cannot follow (1Co 14:14).

**Psuchē (soul):** The experiencing, living-creature dimension — the ne.phesh that was constituted at Gen 2:7, that desires (ne.phesh as appetite), that is the whole living person (ne.phesh as person). The soul is the individual as a living being with inner experience.

**Sōma (body):** The material, embodied dimension — the temple of the Spirit (1Co 6:19), the medium of bodily worship (Rom 12:1), the site of resurrection (1Co 15:44).

The prayer is for all three to be "kept blameless" — holiness is not a spirit-only project. The God of peace sanctifies the whole person: spirit, soul, and body together. This confirms the programme's governing principle: the inner-being characteristics are not independent entities but integrated dimensions of one person.

## Section 8: Scientific Analysis

### 8.1 Primary Scientific Fields

**Consciousness science** (primary) — the question of what the spirit is maps most directly onto the question of consciousness: what makes a person a person, what gives rise to self-awareness, what is the relationship between mind and brain. The ne.sha.mah as "the lamp of the Lord searching all innermost parts" (Pro 20:27) is a pre-scientific consciousness account.

**Neuroscience of spiritual experience** (secondary) — the phenomenology of Spirit-experience (being filled, carried away, troubled in spirit, refreshed in spirit) has been studied in neuroscientific and psychological contexts.

### 8.2 Landmark Findings

**Finding 1 — The hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers, *The Conscious Mind*, 1996).** The "hard problem" — why there is subjective experience at all, why physical processes give rise to inner life — maps directly onto the ne.sha.mah question. Job 32:8 — "the breath of the Almighty makes him understand" — is not a scientific claim but it addresses the same problem: subjective understanding is not explained by the complexity of the physical substrate. The ne.sha.mah is the biblical vocabulary for what Chalmers calls the explanatory gap: why is there something it is like to be a person?

**Finding 2 — Attachment and the secure base (Bowlby, Ainsworth).** The Spirit of adoption (Rom 8:15) that enables the Abba-cry maps structurally onto secure attachment: the child with a secure base can explore freely, is not paralysed by separation anxiety (fear), and can cry out to the attachment figure in distress. The Spirit's work of replacing the fear-spirit with the sonship-spirit is the theological equivalent of secure attachment formation. The transformation from fearful-slavish to confident-filial is both psychological and pneumatological.

**Finding 3 — Integration and the relational mind (Siegel, *Mindsight*).** Siegel's account of psychological integration — the coherent functioning of all aspects of the person — maps onto the 1Th 5:23 tripartite holiness: "sanctify you completely" (holotelēs — entirely/completely) describes the integration of spirit, soul, and body under the Spirit's sanctifying work. Siegel's insight that integration requires relationship (the mind is relational as well as individual) is consistent with the Spirit's relational mode: the Spirit takes up residence in the person and constitutes the person's relationship with the Father and the Son from within.

**Finding 4 — Flow states and intrinsic motivation (Csíkszentmihályi, Deci and Ryan).** The Spirit's description as enabling people to "walk in my statutes" (Eze 36:27) — making obedience intrinsically motivated rather than externally compelled — maps onto the self-determination theory distinction between autonomous motivation (intrinsic, identified) and controlled motivation (external, introjected). The Spirit transforms moral behaviour from controlled motivation (law as external constraint) to autonomous motivation (law written on the heart, Jer 31:33). 2Co 3:17 — "where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom" — the Spirit produces the freedom that is intrinsic motivation.

### 8.3 Convergence and Divergence

**Structural convergence:** The biblical tripartite structure (spirit, soul, body) maps structurally onto the three-domain account of the person in contemporary psychology: the experiential/affective (soul), the cognitive/volitional (heart/mind), and the transcendent/relational (spirit). Not a perfect mapping, but a structural family resemblance.

**Phenomenological convergence:** The somatic effects of Spirit-presence described across the corpus — trembling before the Spirit (Hab 3:2), being filled (Eph 5:18), refreshment (1Co 16:18) — align with documented phenomenology of religious experience (William James, *The Varieties of Religious Experience*, 1902).

**Register divergence:** Science describes spiritual experience from the outside — as a category of human phenomenology with neurological correlates. The biblical vocabulary describes the Spirit from within the divine-human relationship — as a person who speaks, grieves, intercedes, and dwells. These are not competing descriptions but different registers of access.

**Genuine divergence — Spirit as personal agent.** The most radical claim in the spirit registry — one with no scientific parallel — is that the Spirit is a personal divine agent who speaks (Act 13:2), intercedes (Rom 8:26), searches (1Co 2:10), grieves (Eph 4:30), distributes gifts individually as he wills (1Co 12:11), and is one with the Father and the Son (2Co 13:14). This is not a description of a force, an influence, or a state of consciousness. It is a person. No scientific account of spiritual experience or consciousness addresses this claim — it is genuinely outside the scope of empirical investigation.

**Genuine divergence — the tripartite structure.** The 1Th 5:23 tripartite (spirit, soul, body) as a description of the human person's actual constitution has no scientific parallel. Contemporary science describes the person in biophysical-neurological terms, with the mind as an emergent property of the brain. The spirit as a distinct non-physical, God-oriented dimension of the person is not a scientific category. The biblical claim is that the person is tripartite by divine design — not a scientific hypothesis but a theological statement about the created constitution of human beings.
