## Programme Flag — PF-VCB007-001: Instruction enhancement — relevance filter for grammatical particles

**Raised:** 2026-04-01 | **Batch:** VCB-007 | **Stage:** VCB classification

**Finding:** The governing filter (Section 3, WA-VerseContext-Instruction-v2.1) does not explicitly address terms that are grammatical or functional particles (pronouns, quantifiers, interrogatives, entreaty particles) rather than lexical inner-being terms. The current filter criteria (Section 3.1/3.2) are framed for terms that directly name inner states, capacities, or qualities. When applied to particles, the filter requires an implicit intermediate step that the instruction does not articulate.

**Proposed addition to Section 3 (new subsection 3.5 or extension of 3.3):**

Where a term is a grammatical or functional particle — including pronouns, universal quantifiers, interrogatives, entreaty particles, discourse markers, and similar functional words — the relevance filter is applied as follows:

A grammatical particle passes the filter if, in this specific verse, it does one or more of the following to the inner-being content of the clause:
- **Directs** the inner-being content toward or from a subject (e.g. a pronoun identifying the agent of an inner state)
- **Intensifies** the inner-being engagement (e.g. an entreaty particle marking the moment when inner urgency breaks into speech)
- **Qualifies the scope** of an inner-being characteristic in a way that changes its meaning (e.g. a universal quantifier applied to an inner state — *all* the heart, *every* intent)
- **Discloses an inner orientation** through the form of the utterance (e.g. a rhetorical question whose force reveals the speaker's or subject's inner posture)

A grammatical particle does **not** pass the filter if it functions purely as:
- Social register or politeness marker with no inner-bearing
- Syntactic infrastructure (subject pronoun where the predicate carries no inner-being content)
- Temporal or logical discourse connector (e.g. *now* as transition marker)
- Procedural or logistical qualifier

**Rationale:** Established during VCB-007 classification of H4994 na (195 verses), where the filter as stated required an implicit intermediate judgement not covered by the existing criteria. The approach used — isolating particles that have a specific bearing on the verse from or to the inner being, operational, making it specific, stronger or different — produced analytically sound and consistent results and should be codified.

**Action required:** Update WA-VerseContext-Instruction to add this as a new subsection (suggested: Section 3.5 — Grammatical and functional particles). Version increment: minor (v2.1 → v2.2). Claude AI to draft the updated section for researcher review at end of VCB-007 batch or at a suitable document maintenance session.

**Applies from:** VCB-007 onward. Retrospective application not required (no prior grammatical particle terms identified in VCB-001–006).

## Programme Flag — PF-VCB007-002: Standing instruction — no sampling; all verses must be inspected individually

**Raised:** 2026-04-01 | **Batch:** VCB-007 | **Stage:** VCB classification

**Finding:** During classification of H1992 hem.mah (189 verses), Claude AI identified a pattern across the corpus and moved toward a corpus-level assessment rather than completing a verse-by-verse determination. This is not compliant with the governing instruction (Section 6.2 Step 1: "Read every verse… Do not skip").

**Standing instruction — applies from this point forward, no researcher confirmation required:**

1. **Every verse must be individually inspected**, regardless of how uniform the corpus appears. Pattern recognition across a corpus does not substitute for per-verse assessment. A verse that appears structurally identical to others may carry a contextually distinct inner-being engagement that only becomes visible on close reading.

2. **Grammatical particle terms with large corpora** (pronouns, quantifiers, interrogatives, discourse markers) are subject to the same rule. The expectation of a high set-aside rate does not reduce the inspection obligation. Each verse is assessed on its own terms.

3. **All-verses-fail outcomes for large-corpus grammatical terms** are expected to be common — this is not an error requiring researcher confirmation each time, provided the per-verse inspection has been completed in full and the outcome is documented. Claude AI will record the assessment basis for each verse (or verse group where the assessment basis is genuinely identical and explicitly stated) and proceed without stopping to request confirmation, unless a genuinely ambiguous case arises that requires researcher judgement.

4. **Claude AI will not ask whether to proceed** with full reads of remaining large-corpus grammatical terms. The instruction to proceed with full reads stands as a programme discipline for all terms in the batch.

**Action required:** Add this as a standing procedural note in WA-VerseContext-Instruction Section 6.2 Step 1 (or as a new Step 1a). Suggested wording:

> *Sampling is not appropriate. Each verse has its own context and must be individually inspected, regardless of corpus size or apparent uniformity. Pattern recognition across a corpus does not substitute for per-verse assessment. This applies equally to lexical terms and to grammatical or functional particle terms.*

**Version increment:** minor (v2.1 → v2.2, combined with PF-VCB007-001 instruction addition).
