### repentance registry 135

> **Source list:** `High Confidence`

**Hebrew**
- **shuv** (שׁוּב) — to turn, return — the core OT repentance concept (Joel 2:12-13, Jer 3:12-14)
- **nacham** (נָחַם) — to be sorry, to comfort — used of God relenting and human regret (Jer 31:19, Job 42:6)

**Greek**
- **metanoia** (μετάνοια) — repentance — change of mind and direction (Matt 3:2, Acts 2:38, 2 Cor 7:10)
- **metamelomai** (μεταμέλομαι) — to regret, feel remorse (Matt 27:3 — Judas; 2 Cor 7:8)

**Conceptual Search Terms:** `turning`, `conversion`, `change of mind`, `contrition`, `returning to God`

**STEP Bible Suggestion:** Search metanoia in NT — it is the dominant term. 2 Cor 7:10 distinguishes godly sorrow leading to metanoia from worldly sorrow. In OT search shuv — it appears hundreds of times and is the primary call to repentance in the Prophets.
