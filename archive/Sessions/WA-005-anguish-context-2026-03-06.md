## odunē — Rom 9:2

**Reference:** Rom 9:2
**Term:** odunē (G3601)
**Language:** Greek
**Classification:** CORE
**Genre:** epistle
**Speaker or subject:** Paul, in the opening of Romans 9 before his treatment of Israel's unbelief
**Situation:** Paul is establishing the depth of his concern for Israel before arguing that God's word has not failed.
**The inner-being moment:** The verse records: "I have great sorrow and unceasing anguish in my heart" — odunē names unceasing anguish explicitly located in the heart (kardia); the anguish is persistent rather than episodic, and its cause is Paul's grief over Israel's spiritual condition.
**What the person does with the inner state:** Paul names the anguish openly and then proceeds to the theological argument; the inner state is not suppressed but disclosed as the emotional ground of his concern.
