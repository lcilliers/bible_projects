This study include a deep analysis of words associated with the inner being.  The words were collated from an analysis of Framework B: a study on the spirit soul and body, and drawn from Claude AI training on standard lexical reference works (BDB, HALOT, BDAG, Thayer) and biblical anthropology scholarship. It reflects the established semantic fields of the primary inner being terms.

Inner being words were extracted from the analysis of the c Inner Being Characteristics. Inner being characteristics are the non-physical, internal states, capacities, and expressions that constitute a person's invisible life — encompassing how a person thinks, feels, chooses, relates, and orients themselves toward meaning, others, and God.

This definition was developed as an evaluative filter — a single sentence precise enough to apply to any word or text passage to determine whether its meaning qualifies as an inner being characteristic. It is not a theological definition of the soul or spirit, but a working definition for the purpose of word extraction and analysis. The term 'inner being' was defined as the broader totality of a person's non-physical existence — everything that is not the body, encompassing spirit and soul together. Soulish characteristics' were defined separately as the specific functions and expressions belonging to the psyche/nephesh — thought, emotion, will, desire, conscience, self-awareness — a subset of inner being, not the whole. The distinction was established: inner being = spirit + soul (the complete non-physical person). Soulish = what belongs specifically to the soul's domain within that inner being. All soulish characteristics are inner being, but not all inner being is soulish. The definition was deliberately kept broad enough to capture both soulish and spiritual characteristics, since the objective is to identifying all inner being words from source documents without pre-sorting them into spirit or soul categories. The five functional domains embedded in the definition — thinking, feeling, choosing, relating, orienting toward meaning/others/God — were chosen because they cover the full operational range of the inner person as revealed in both biblical and general usage. The definition intentionally excludes physical or bodily characteristics. Words describing purely external behaviour, biological processes, or material reality do not qualify unless they are being used to express an internal state (e.g. 'groaning' qualifies because Scripture uses it to express an internal spiritual or emotional state, not merely a physical sound).

Three groups of words were discovered:  Those that have a high probability of relating to the soul, and are used in the Bible; those that may relate to the soul but may not explicitly be used as such in the bible, and words that are not included in the above but have a lexical reference to the inner being words.
