### gladness registry 186 single-part

> **Source list:** `High Confidence`

Gladness covers the full range of joyful, pleasant, and celebratory inner experience across both Testaments. The primary Hebrew terms are שָׂמֵחַ (sa.me.ach) and שִׂמְחָה (sim.chah), the adjectival and nominal forms of the central gladness/joy root, while טוֹב (tov) and its cognates extend the field into goodness and pleasantness as inner states. Greek terms include χαρά (chara), εὐφροσύνη (eufrosunē), εὐφραίνω (eufrainō), and related forms denoting joy, celebration, and shared gladness.
