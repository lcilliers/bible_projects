### ambition registry 003

> **Source list:** `Low Confidence / Inferred`  

**⚠ Inference Note:** Ambition is a morally neutral modern term that Scripture handles through two distinct concepts — selfish ambition (always negative) and godly zeal/aspiration (positive). The neutral modern sense is ABSENT.

**Hebrew**

  - **qana** (קָנָא) — zeal, jealous ambition — can be positive (Num 25:11) or negative (Num 5:14) *(flag: PARTIAL — covers the zealous driving force behind ambition)*
  - **gav** (גָּאַב) — to be proud, to aspire — pride-driven ambition (Ps 131:1 — negatively) *(flag: INFERRED — Ps 131:1 (I do not occupy myself with things too great) implies ambition's negative form)*

**Greek**

  - **eritheia** (ἐριθεία) — selfish ambition, factious self-seeking (Gal 5:20, Phil 2:3, James 3:14-16) *(flag: PARTIAL — eritheia covers selfish ambition specifically — the negative form)*
  - **philotimeomai** (φιλοτιμέομαι) — to be ambitious, to aspire — used positively in NT (Rom 15:20, 2 Cor 5:9, 1 Thess 4:11) *(flag: PARTIAL — philotimeomai is the positive form of ambition: aspiring to honour God)*
  - **zelos** (ζῆλος) — zeal — the driving energy of ambition, positive or negative (2 Cor 11:2, Gal 4:18) *(flag: PARTIAL — zelos covers the motivational energy dimension)*

**Conceptual Search Terms:** `selfish ambition`, `zeal`, `aspiration`, `self-seeking`, `striving`

**STEP Bible Suggestion:** INFERRED: Distinguish eritheia (selfish ambition — always negative, Gal 5:20, Phil 2:3) from philotimeomai (godly ambition/aspiration — positive, Rom 15:20, 2 Cor 5:9). These are the two poles of ambition in Scripture. Search both separately in STEP. Phil 2:3 places selfish ambition in direct contrast to humility.
