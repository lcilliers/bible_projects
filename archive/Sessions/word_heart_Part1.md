### heart registry 183 --- Part 1: Hebrew

> **Source list:** `STEP Bible — heart 183`

**heart** is the primary biblical term for the inner seat of human personhood, encompassing intellect, will, emotion, and moral disposition. In Hebrew the dominant root cluster is *lev/le.vav/lib.bah*, covering the full range of inner life from courage and resolve to spiritual hardness and wounding. The body-adjacent terms (*qe.rev*, inner parts; *tu.chot*, inner parts; *cheq*, bosom; *chov*, breast; *cha.lats*, loins) locate the seat of personhood in the physical body. In Greek *kardia* and its compounds (hard-hearted, dull of heart, heart-knower, etc.) dominate, with the *splanchnon* cluster (affection/entrails, compassionate) functioning as a visceral register of deep emotion. Heart terms intersect with soul (*ne.phesh*, *psuchē* — XREF 182), conscience (*kil.yah* — XREF 026), and desire (*cha.shaq* — XREF 043).

**Hebrew note:** Core roots *lev*/*le.vav* (H3820/H3824) are the most frequent inner-being terms in the OT, appearing across wisdom, prophetic, and narrative literature. Body-adjacent terms broaden the somatic register.

**Conceptual Search Terms:** heart, inner being, mind, will, conscience, emotion, desire, courage, hardness, compassion, affection, entrails

**STEP Bible Suggestion:** Search *lev*, *le.vav*, *kardia* and compounds; also *splanchnon* cluster and body-adjacent Hebrew terms.
