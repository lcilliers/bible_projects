### strength registry 187 --- Part 3: Greek terms

> **Source list:** `High Confidence`

Strength in the Greek New Testament centres on two major word-groups: the ischus family (inherent strength, ability, the capacity to exert force) and the dunamis family (power, miraculous enabling, dynamic capacity). Further terms cover authority (exousia), firmness (stereoō), and weakness as the negative pole (asthenēs, adunatos, anischus). This part covers all Greek terms; Part 1 and Part 2 cover Hebrew terms.
