## 5. Key Analytical Findings

**Governing state, not characteristic.** Righteousness functions as a governing state of the person, not a characteristic alongside others. Never directly defined in the text; its meaning is evidenced entirely by what the person is and does. The OT assumes some persons are righteous; the NT reverses this (Rom 3:10) before resolving it through faith in Christ.

**The transferability reversal.** Ezekiel 18:20 states righteousness is non-transferable. Romans 5:19 states that by one man's obedience the many will be made righteous. This reversal is resolved structurally through M26-A1-007: the Messiah's righteousness is the sole basis for righteous transfer, because the Messiah's righteousness is itself the righteousness of God.

**The Ezekiel anomaly.** M26-A2-002 presents righteousness as precarious, forfeitable, and non-accumulative — assessed at the moment of turning, not across a lifetime. This stands in sharp contrast to the settled-identity material (Psalms/Proverbs) where righteousness is stable, protected by God, and described as an enduring root. Both streams are present; the tension is not resolved within the OT.

**Silence at spirit level.** Across the entire M26 cluster, the spirit (ruach/pneuma) is not named as the location or organ of righteousness. Conscience is identified as the primary faculty for the moral-judicial dimension (M26-E, linking to M26-C). This is a significant silence: righteousness is the dominant moral category in Scripture, yet its inner location is the heart, the whole inner being, or the self — not the spirit specifically.

**The Gen 18 intercession.** The Abraham intercession for Sodom (Gen 18:23–28) reveals that the presence of even a few righteous persons carries protective power over a whole city. The righteous community (M26-A2-015) is not merely an identifiable group but a force whose presence or absence determines outcomes for those around it.

**Pilate's six declarations.** The Roman governor declares Christ innocent six times across the four gospels (Joh 18:38, 19:4, 19:6; Luk 23:4, 14, 22). This sustained testimony from an external, hostile, pagan authority is among the strongest structural witnesses to Christ's righteousness in the NT. Recovered from BOUNDARY through verse-by-verse reading and assigned to M26-A1-007.
