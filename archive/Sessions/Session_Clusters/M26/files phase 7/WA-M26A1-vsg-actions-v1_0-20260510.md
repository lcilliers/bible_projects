**Description:**

### ACTION-A07

**Type:** New VSG — G7: Defining characteristic of the Messiah — becomes incarnate  
**Status:** Pending researcher confirmation  
**Identified:** 2026-05-10  

**Description:**
> Term names righteousness as the defining quality of the coming Messianic figure — the Righteous One, the righteous Branch, the righteous Servant. This righteousness is not merely attributed but constitutive: the Servant's righteousness enables him to make many accounted righteous (Isa 53:11); the Branch is named "The Lord is our righteousness" (Jer 23:6, 33:16); the Messianic king is "righteous and having salvation" (Zec 9:9). In the NT these figures are identified as Jesus, who is denied by the crowd (Act 3:14), whose coming was betrayed (Act 7:52), who is called "Jesus Christ the righteous" (1Jo 2:1), and who in dying gave "the righteous for the unrighteous" (1Pe 3:18). The righteousness of the Messiah is not separate from God's own righteousness — it is its incarnate expression.
