## Key findings from Phase 8

### Cluster-level findings (from cross-sub-group review)

**Finding 1 — Complete silence at spirit level**
Across all 8 sub-groups and 955 active verses, no verse explicitly locates any dimension of righteousness in the human spirit (ruach/pneuma). The silence is total and uniform — not a data gap but a structural finding. Constitutional location is in the heart, the mind, and relational standing before God.

**Finding 2 — Heart as primary constitutional location**
The heart is the primary constitutional location of M26 across all sub-groups: the inscribed law (A2-005), the inner orientation of uprightness (B), the hard and impenitent heart of wickedness (G, Rom 2:5), the heart that God's righteous judgment confronts. The heart is the primary constitutional site in all modes.

**Finding 3 — Righteousness as governing state**
Confirmed across all sub-groups: righteousness functions as a governing state (not one characteristic among peers). The cluster's internal architecture reflects this — A1 is the source, A2 is the derived governing state, and all other sub-groups (B, C, D, E, F) are specific modes of this governing reality. G defines it by its opposition.

**Finding 4 — Conscience as primary faculty for the judicial dimension**
The conscience is the inner faculty most specifically engaged by the cluster's judicial dimension. Felix's alarm (A1-006), the autokatakritos (E), and the purified conscience (C-adjacent: Heb 9:14) all confirm conscience as the primary judicial inner faculty. The reckoning faculty (F) operates alongside in the cognitive-deliberative mode.

**Finding 5 — F (Moral Reckoning) as the inner appropriation mechanism for C (Justification)**
These two sub-groups are structurally linked: the declared reality of justification (C) requires the reckoning faculty (F) for its inner appropriation. "Count yourselves dead to sin and alive to God" (Rom 6:11) is the instruction to apply F to the declared reality of C. This connection is invisible when each sub-group is treated in isolation.

**Finding 6 — Origin asymmetry**
Righteousness originates externally (from God — received through faith, new birth, divine declaration). Wickedness originates internally (from the hardened heart — the person's own sustained refusal). The asymmetry is structural: righteousness cannot be self-generated; wickedness is self-generated. This directly relates to the God-dependency finding from the pre-Phase 8 external reference debate.

**Finding 7 — Pervasive eschatological orientation**
Every sub-group in M26 has an eschatological trajectory. The present experience of righteousness in every mode is consistently anticipatory. M26 is the most eschatologically oriented cluster analysed in the programme to date.

**Finding 8 — The A2/C tension (unresolved)**
The Ezekiel forfeiture model (A2-002: righteousness is forfeitable, non-accumulative, non-transferable) stands in documented tension with the NT's declared security of the justified person (C: Rom 8:34 — "who is to condemn? Christ Jesus intercedes"). Documented without resolution. The programme should carry this tension forward to Session C and Session D.

### Sub-group specific findings (selected)

**A1 — God Righteousness:** God's righteousness is not one attribute among others but the architectonic quality of divine being from which all other sub-groups derive their coherence. It operates through proclamation, through the Messiah, through covenant faithfulness, through saving acts, and as the standard that exposes human unrighteousness by contrast. Seven distinct modes identified across nine VSGs.

**A2 — Human Righteousness:** The governing state of the human person. Compound — 26 VSGs reveal extensive internal differentiation. Two streams: the OT conditional/forfeitable stream (Ezekiel) and the NT received-through-faith stream. Both are present and must be held together. The non-transferability principle (Eze 18:20) and its resolution through the Messiah (2Cor 5:21) is one of the cluster's most important theological findings.

**B — Moral Uprightness:** Inner disposition of straightness. Distinct root family from A (y.sh.r vs. ts.d.q). Primarily an OT-dominant vocabulary. Engages mouth/lips as primary body-part channel. Silent on immediate response and on eschatological orientation (two structural silences for B).

**C — Justification:** Declarative judicial act. OT mode (acquitting the actually innocent) differs from NT mode (justifying the ungodly by faith) — same terms, different constitutional logic. The justification of the ungodly (Rom 4:5) is the most theologically distinctive statement in the cluster.

**D — Avenging Justice:** Response sub-group — all D-terms require a prior wrong as trigger. Personal autonomous vengeance is excluded; commissioned vengeance is legitimate. The eschatological dimension is prominent (Rev 6:10; 19:2).

**E — Condemnation:** The structural counterpart to C. Autokatakritos (self-condemned, Tit 3:11) is exclusively creaturely — the conscience turned against the self. No divine analogue exists. Conscience is the primary faculty engaged.

**F — Moral Reckoning:** The only sub-group naming itself primarily as a faculty. Located in the mind (cognitive-deliberative). The sub-group most explicitly engaged with the eschatological comparative (Rom 8:18: suffering vs. glory). Constitutively linked to C as its inner appropriation mechanism.

**G — Structural Opposites:** Defines the cluster's boundary by opposition. The ra.sha/tsad.diq pairing is the most persistent antithetical pair in the OT. Wickedness is a governing orientation, not an isolated lapse; it produces progressive hardening and narrowing of moral capacity.

## Revision record — post-debate correlation review

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Trigger:** Researcher uploaded four prior debate documents (god-righteousness, human-righteousness, human-righteousness-by-term, external-reference) for correlation check before DB directive preparation.

**Review finding:** The tier work correctly captured the major structural findings from the debates. Four specific gaps were identified — omissions of nuance established in the debates but not carried through into the tier findings with full precision.

**Four additions made to the consolidated findings documents:**

**Gap 1 — Conduct/state bidirectionality** (Part 1, T1.2.3 A2 working description; Part 2, T2.10.2 cluster note)
The relationship between the governing state and its conduct expression is bidirectional, not one-directional. The dominant direction is state → conduct as evidence (Mat 25:37: righteous serve without calculation). But Ezekiel's framework (Eze 18:5–9; Deu 6:25) presents the reverse: conduct can constitute the state, not merely evidence it. Both directions are operative and neither cancels the other.

**Gap 2 — Contamination dynamic of self-generated righteousness** (Part 1, T1.3.2 A2 entry)
The resistance to self-generated righteousness extends beyond exclusion to active contamination. Five verses named: Isa 64:6 (polluted garment), Deu 9:4–6 (self-attributed righteousness explicitly denied as operative cause), Luk 18:9 (contempt as inner consequence), Mat 23:28 (outer facade masking inner lawlessness). The counterfeit form produces specific destructive inner consequences, not merely absence of the real thing.

**Gap 3 — OT implicit / NT explicit as a single finding at two levels of resolution** (Part 4, new Cross-cluster finding 9)
The OT names persons as righteous without always articulating the God-relation explicitly; close reading consistently reveals it. The NT makes the dependency categorical (Rom 3:10; 5:19; Phili 3:9). These are not two different findings — they are the same finding at different focal lengths. The programme's OT and NT findings on God-dependency are aligned, not in tension.

**Gap 4 — The Phili 3:9 two-righteousness distinction** (Part 1, T1.3.3 A2 entry)
Paul's Phili 3:9 contrast between "a righteousness of my own that comes from the law" (counted as rubbish, Phili 3:8) and "that which comes through faith in Christ, the righteousness from God" names two structurally different things, not two points on a spectrum. The self-generated version is real enough to name and contrast, but is not the same state. This is where the NT's account of human righteousness becomes most precise.
