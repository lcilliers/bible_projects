### T2.3 — Heart

**T2.3.1** — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?

**[A1 — God Righteousness]** E. Indirectly — kardiognōstēs (heart-knower, Act 1:24; 15:8, in VSG M26-A1-002) names God as the one whose righteousness penetrates to the heart. God's righteous judgment reaches the heart — "God's righteous judgment" (Rom 2:5) confronts the "hard and impenitent heart" (same verse, VSG 3207-001). The heart is the location God's righteousness addresses in the human, not the location it inhabits in God.

**[A2 — Human Righteousness]** E. Yes — explicitly. VSG M26-A2-005 is "law written on the heart" — the internalisation of God's righteous standard within the heart of the person. "I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts" (Jer 31:33) and the new covenant parallel (Heb 8:10; 10:16). The heart is the constitutional location where righteousness as an inner principle is inscribed. Additionally, the hunger for righteousness (Mat 5:6, VSG M26-A2-006) is an appetite of the whole inner person, centered in the heart as the integrating organ of the inner life.

**[B — Moral Uprightness]** E. Yes — VSG 1211-001 contains the uprightness-of-heart language: yo.sher is used in contexts that describe an upright heart as distinct from upright conduct. The inner orientation of straightness has the heart as its constitutional seat. "The upright in heart" is a recognisable category in the Psalms and wisdom literature.

**[C — Justification]** S. Justification as an act is not explicitly located in the heart in the verse evidence. The result of justification (new standing before God) is not described as a heart-state. Silence.

**[D — Avenging Justice]** E. Indirectly — Rom 2:5 (VSG 3207-001) names the "hard and impenitent heart" as the heart condition that "stores up wrath" — the heart's condition is what makes divine avenging justice necessary. The inner determination to entrust vengeance to God (6841-002: "leave it to the wrath of God") is a heart-level resolution of will.

**[E — Condemnation]** E. Yes — "not to condemn you, for I said before that you are in our hearts, to die together and to live together" (2Cor 7:3, VSG 738-001). The condemnation-discourse reaches the heart — the heart as the location of the deep bond that Paul distinguishes from condemnation. Self-condemnation (autokatakritos) operates through the conscience — which is heart-adjacent in biblical anthropology. 1Jo 3:20–21 (not in this cluster's verses directly, but known): "whenever our heart condemns us, God is greater than our heart" — the heart as the location of self-condemnation. This reference is from known context, not from named verse in this cluster — recording as marginal rather than primary evidence.

**[F — Moral Reckoning]** E. Yes — "you thought that I was one like yourself. But now I rebuke you and lay the charge before you" (Psa 50:21, in the a.rakh group) — the reckoning act is associated with what is laid before God. More directly: "God tests the minds and hearts" (Psa 7:9, echoed in VSG M26-A2-013) — the heart is one of the organs that the reckoning faculty of God penetrates in its judicial assessment. The human reckoning (logizomai) operates in the mind, but the object of assessment is the heart-state.

**[G — Structural Opposites]** E. Yes — explicitly. "Hard and impenitent heart" (Rom 2:5, VSG 3207-001) — wickedness in G is constitutionally located in the heart as the governing organ of the inner person's orientation. The hard heart is the internal locus of sustained wickedness.

**[CLUSTER — T2.3]** E. The heart is the primary constitutional location of righteousness in M26. The pattern across sub-groups is: righteousness as written law (A2-005, heart as the location of inscribed righteous standard), as inner orientation of straightness (B, heart as the seat of uprightness disposition), and as the site of moral hardness when righteousness is absent (G, heart as the locus of wickedness). The heart is where righteousness is received (A2), oriented (B), and resisted (G). God's righteousness addresses the heart in its judgment (A1-002, F). The cluster's primary constitutional home is the heart.

**T2.3.2** — What does the heart-location reveal?

The heart-location also reveals the depth of the resistance: the hard heart (Rom 2:5) is not resistant at the surface level of behavior but at the integrating center. ### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**T2.7.1** — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?

**[A2 — Human Righteousness]** E. Soul expressing through body. The righteous person's inner state expresses itself through the mouth (speech) and through the hands (action). **[B — Moral Uprightness]** E. Soul expressing through body. The inner disposition of uprightness in the heart produces upright speech from the lips. Same direction as A2: inner-to-outer, soul-to-body expression.

**[E — Condemnation]** E. Soul expressing through body (and back). The mouth expresses the inner condition — but in E there is also a feedback loop: "by your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned" (Mat 12:37). The words expressed from the inner condition become the grounds of the verdict directed back at the person. The direction here runs both ways: inner→mouth (expression) and then mouth→verdict (feedback that determines the standing). The body part (mouth) becomes the medium of both expression and judgment.

**[G — Structural Opposites]** E. Soul expressing through body. The wicked person's inner condition expresses itself through the mouth (Job 15:6) and through the hands (Psa 71:4). Direction: inner-to-outer.

---

**T2.7.2** — What is the consequence of that directionality?

**[CLUSTER]** E. The consistent inner-to-outer direction reveals that righteousness in M26 is constitutionally generated internally and expressed externally — not the reverse. The person does not become righteous by performing righteous speech or righteous acts; the righteous person's speech and acts express what is already true of them internally. This has significant implications: behavioral reform alone cannot produce righteousness; the inner source must be addressed. The E-feedback loop (words→verdict) is the partial exception: while the inner condition is primary, the words spoken do carry consequences that return to the person as verdict.

### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**T2.9.1** — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[A1 — God Righteousness]** E. Within God himself — eternal, uncreated, underived. "The Lord is righteous in all his ways" (Psa 145:17); his righteousness "endures forever" (Psa 111:3). The origin of God's righteousness is not traceable to any external source; it is constitutive of his nature.

**[A2 — Human Righteousness]** E. External origin — received from God, not self-generated. The primary OT mode: credited righteousness (Gen 15:6 — Abraham believed God, and it was counted to him as righteousness, cross-referenced in VSG 3410-001). The primary NT mode: justification by faith in Christ (3194-001; Tit 3:7). The verse "everyone who practices righteousness has been born of him" (1Jo 2:29, VSG M26-A2-014) locates the origin in God himself: the new birth is the origin of the practice of righteousness. Human righteousness does not originate from within the person — it originates from God and is received.

**[B — Moral Uprightness]** E. Both external and internal. The path of uprightness is taught (Pro 4:11: "I have led you in the paths of uprightness") — suggesting external formation. But the person who walks in uprightness fears the Lord (Pro 14:2) — suggesting that uprightness in B arises from an inner orientation of reverence that the person has, not merely from external instruction. The origin in B is formation-through-instruction leading to an internalized disposition.

**[C — Justification]** E. External origin — from God toward the person. "Being justified by his grace" (Tit 3:7); "God who justifies" (Rom 4:5, VSG 3194-001). Justification originates entirely outside the person: it is God's declarative act, not the person's self-achievement.

**[D — Avenging Justice]** E. External origin (for legitimate avenging justice) — the commission comes from God. "Vengeance is mine, says the Lord" (Rom 12:19) — the origin of legitimate retribution is divine. The commissioned human avenger (Rom 13:4) exercises a delegated authority that originates from God, not from within themselves.
