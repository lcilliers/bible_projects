### 5. H6662 *tsad.diq* — operational analysis (159 OT verses)

**Task:** Analyse how righteousness *operates* in the 159 H6662 verses. The researcher's framing: in most verses something happens to righteousness, or it makes something happen.

**Method:** All 159 verses read individually. Structure allowed to emerge from the text.

**Eight operational groups identified:**

| Group | Description | Approx. verses |
|-------|-------------|---------------:|
| 1 | Attracts divine action (protection, deliverance, reward, hearing) | ~30 |
| 2 | Produces outward expression (speech, character, communal benefit, orientation to God) | ~25 |
| 3 | Generates stable outcomes (material, household, legacy) | ~20 |
| 4 | Makes person a target (wicked plots, systemic injustice, collective judgment) | ~20 |
| 5 | Precarious personal standing — can be forfeited (Ezekiel cluster) | ~8 |
| 6 | Legal/forensic category (innocent party, just cause, acquittal) | ~15 |
| 7 | Community identity (congregation, generation, tents) | ~10 |
| 8 | Defines a life path (way, manner of walking) | ~7 |

**Three structural observations recorded:**

1. **Ezekiel is anomalous** — the only OT cluster treating righteousness as dynamic and precarious. Contrasts with the settled-identity pattern in Psalms and Proverbs.
2. **Groups 1 and 4 are structurally paired** — righteousness simultaneously attracts divine favour and human hostility. Operates in a field of tension.
3. **The term does not define itself** — no verse in the 159-verse set states what righteousness *is* internally. The content is assumed, not stated.

**Decision:** Observations appended to the by-term file. NT/OT comparison analysis to follow.

### 9. Proposition test — righteousness as state, structural parallel, God-dependency

**Proposition under test:** "Being righteous is a state not a characteristic — it is derived from what it represents. Its evidence of existence comes from what the object is or does. In this, there is similarity in how it works between God and man — except that in the human, it cannot be attained without God. Taking God out of the equation leaves the human unable to be in a state of righteousness."

**Verdict: Confirmed — with three qualifications.**

**Element 1 — State not characteristic:** Substantially supported. Righteousness governs thought, speech, desire, conduct, stability simultaneously — behaviour of a state not a trait. Qualification: some verses list it alongside other virtues. Precise claim: righteousness is a *primary governing orientation* whose evidence permeates the whole person.

**Element 2 — Evidence comes from what the object is or does:** Strongly confirmed. In every named individual, righteousness is inferred from action, response, or divine commendation — never directly observed. Mat 23:28 and Mat 25:37 are the strongest textual confirmations. Qualification: Ezekiel sometimes reads as conduct constituting the state rather than evidencing it (Eze 18:5–9).

**Element 3 — Structural parallel between God and human:** Confirmed. Operational structure identical — never directly defined, evidenced by outputs, recognisable through what the subject is and does. Difference is ontological not structural: God's righteousness is self-grounded, permanent, and the source; human righteousness is derived, God-dependent, and the recipient form.

**Element 4 — Cannot be attained without God:** Strongly confirmed at five levels simultaneously:
1. The *standard* is God's (Eze 18:9)
2. The *assessment* is God's (Gen 7:1, 1Ki 8:32)
3. The *relational orientation* is toward God (Mal 3:18, Hab 2:4, 2Sa 23:3)
4. The *source* in NT is explicitly God through Christ (Rom 3:10, 5:19, Phili 3:9)
5. The *attempt* without God produces a counterfeit (Luk 18:9, Mat 23:28, Phili 3:6)

**Additional finding from the test:** Righteousness in the human is not merely *influenced* by God — it is *constituted* by the relationship with God. Remove that relationship and the very framework within which righteousness means anything disappears with it: the standard, the assessment, the relational content, and the source all reside in God.

### 10. External reference debate — human sciences and dictionary definitions

**Trigger:** Researcher challenged the God-dependency finding. A finding that significant should be tested against what human experience independently recognises.

**Human sciences finding:** The structural dependency on an external reference point is universal across moral psychology, virtue ethics, Kohlberg's moral development stages, and positive psychology. No credible framework produces self-grounded sustained moral character. Apparent exceptions (secular humanists, Stoics) share the structural dependency but differ on the content of the reference point. The finding stands; the human sciences sharpen rather than challenge it.

**Unresolved question flagged:** Is what individuals outside a theistic framework exhibit the same state the programme identifies, or a functional approximation? Appropriate for tier review; not resolvable from Scripture or human sciences alone.

**Dictionary and etymology finding:** Five substantive observations, of which the most significant:

- *D5 — Righteousness behaves differently from other inner-being characteristics:* The English word was coined by Tyndale in 1526 specifically to translate *tsedek* and *dikaios*. It has no semantic history independent of Scripture. This inverts the usual relationship — the concept is prior, the English word is derivative. The programme is working at the concept's origin point.

- *D2 — Oldest definitions encode state, not action:* Old English *rihtwīs* (right + manner/condition) is a state descriptor from the beginning. The drift toward action-based and feeling-based definitions is later and weaker.

- *D3 — External reference point progressively removed in modern popular definitions:* The cultural drift toward self-referential definitions mirrors the scriptural warning about counterfeit righteousness.
