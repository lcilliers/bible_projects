## Observations — M15-B

**OBS-B-001 — Dan 11:30 and Dan 11:37: bin as "pay attention to" — directional attending, not inner comprehension**
Dan 11:30 (vr_id 27692, span "attention") and Dan 11:37 (vr_id 27694, span "attention") use bin to mean "pay attention to / turn one's attention toward." This is a use of bin as directional perceptive attending rather than as inner comprehension of meaning. Both are assigned to 932-004 (failure/hardening/absence of understanding). The 932-004 description does not fit — neither verse describes a failure of understanding but rather a deliberate directional choice of attention. These verses may be in the wrong VCG.

**OBS-B-002 — 1Ki 3:21: bin as "look closely" — physical perceptive attention, not inner comprehension**
The bin span for 1Ki 3:21 is "closely" — the mother looked closely at the child. This is careful physical examination. The VCG description for 932-001 (constituted inner perceptive faculty) is broadly correct in the sense that bin enables right perception, but this verse is a physical perceptive act, not the cognitive-spiritual comprehension that characterises most 932-001 verses. Worth noting as a peripheral use.

**OBS-B-003 — Job 30:20: bin as "only look" — possible non-comprehension use**
Job 30:20 uses bin in the sense of God looking at Job without responding — "you only look at me." The span is perceptive attending but with the emphasis on the absence of response, not on comprehension. This is a difficult use assigned to 932-003 (God's comprehension of the human person). The verse is about God's apparent inattention in response rather than his comprehending perception. The VCG fit is loose.

**OBS-B-004 — Job 31:1: bin as "gaze" — the perceptive faculty directed toward a forbidden object**
Job 31:1 uses bin for the gaze that would be a covenant violation — "how then could I gaze at a virgin?" The span names the inner perceptive faculty as capable of moral direction or misdirection. This use is distinctive — it is not about comprehension of meaning but about the directed perceptive attention of the eye. Assigned to 932-001. This is a boundary use of bin.

**OBS-B-005 — Ezr 8:15: bin as administrative review — correctly set aside**
Ezr 8:15 is correctly set aside as no_inner_being. The bin span here is administrative examination — reviewing a list of people. No inner-being content.

**OBS-B-006 — Hos 13:2: te.vu.nah as "skillfully" applied to idol-making — craft skill in service of idolatry**
Hos 13:2 uses te.vu.nah for idols skillfully made. This is the same inner capacity for understanding applied to craft, but directed toward a forbidden object. Assigned to 524-002 (human inner capacity enabling discernment). The VCG description ("enabling wisdom, right conduct, and discernment, received from God") does not fit this verse — te.vu.nah here enables craftsmanship in service of idolatry, not right conduct. The verse is a misfit within 524-002.

**OBS-B-007 — Eze 28:4: te.vu.nah as the prince of Tyre's inner capacity for material gain — self-serving understanding**
Eze 28:4 uses te.vu.nah for the prince of Tyre's self-enriching wisdom and understanding. Like Hos 13:2, this is the inner capacity directed toward self rather than God. Assigned to 524-002. The same observation applies — the VCG description does not accommodate the self-serving or idolatrous uses of te.vu.nah.

**OBS-B-008 — Isa 10:13: bin as the Assyrian king's self-attributed understanding — wisdom claimed as self-sourced**
Isa 10:13 uses bin for the Assyrian king's claim: "by my wisdom, for I have understanding." This is the inner perceptive faculty claimed as the person's own achievement, not received from God. Assigned to 932-004 (failure/hardening/absence of understanding). The assignment is questionable — the king claims understanding, not that he lacks it. The verse is about arrogant self-attribution rather than the absence of understanding. It may fit better in a group about self-sourced or misdirected understanding.

**OBS-B-009 — VCG 932-004 holds multiple distinct meanings**
Reading across the 932-004 verses, the VCG description ("failure, hardening, or absence of inner understanding — spiritual blindness, incapacity to perceive God or moral reality, and the rhetorical limits of human comprehension before God") encompasses at least four distinct patterns:
(a) Judicial withholding of understanding (Isa 6:9, 6:10, Mar 4:12, Luk 8:10, Mat 13:13, 13:14, Act 28:26, 28:27)
(b) Universal failure of human comprehension (Rom 3:11, Hos 4:14, Jer 4:22, Isa 1:3)
(c) Confrontation with the limits of human understanding before divine mystery (Job 38:18, 38:20, 36:29, 26:14, Job 9:11)
(d) Directional attending (Dan 11:30, Dan 11:37)
These are meaningfully different situations. The VCG description holds them together under a broad label, but the inner-being meaning is not uniform.

**OBS-B-010 — VCG 816-002 spans two sub-groups: M15-B and elsewhere**
VCG 816-002 is assigned to M15-B but the description is "understanding as the inner reception and grasp of revealed truth — the capacity to perceive insight into mystery." Several of its verses appear in M15-B (Deu 4:6, Dan 9:22, Isa 40:14, Pro 24:12), but the group is associated with sunesis (G4907) in M15-B's term list while the Hebrew bin and bi.nah rows are also assigned to it. The VCG spans terms from multiple groups. This is a cross-term VCG that needs verification.

**OBS-B-011 — Luk 1:17: fronesis translated "wisdom" — unusual rendering**
Luk 1:17 uses fronesis but it is rendered as "wisdom" in the span ("to the wisdom of the just"). Fronesis typically names practical understanding/insight, not sophia-wisdom. The verse describes the spirit and power of Elijah turning the disobedient to the phronesis of the just. Assigned to 531-001 (understanding as inner-being capacity enabling transformed living). The assignment is reasonable, but the rendering "wisdom" in the span obscures the distinction between fronesis and sofia.

**OBS-B-012 — Jam 3:13: epistēmōn as genuine knowing verified by conduct — single verse group**
VCG 1207-001 has only one verse (Jam 3:13). The verse names the wise and understanding (sophos + epistēmōn) person as one who demonstrates the quality through conduct. Epistēmōn names genuine knowing as an inner quality that is verified outwardly. The single-verse group is thin but the meaning is distinct from the other M15-B VCGs.

**OBS-B-013 — Deu 4:6 appears twice: both bin and bi.nah rows assigned to 816-002 rather than their primary VCGs**
Both the bin row (vr_id 173365) and the bi.nah row (vr_id 7060) for Deu 4:6 are assigned to 816-002 rather than 932-001 and 523-002 respectively. The verse describes Israel's understanding and wisdom as what their covenant obedience will be recognised as by the nations. The assignment to 816-002 (reception and grasp of revealed truth) is defensible but the primary meaning of the verse in relation to its spans is the visible demonstration of inner understanding through obedient conduct — possibly better in 932-001 and 523-002.
