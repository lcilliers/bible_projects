## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

Reading through all 298 verse meanings, the following meaning-patterns emerge.

### VCG-A-01 — Wisdom as constituted inner quality of character: cha.kham as holistic inner endowment
*Wisdom as a defining inner quality of the person — not a skill or technique but a whole-person orientation that shows in perception, speech, conduct, and relationships. The quality is positive and characterises those who are genuinely wise.*

**Wisdom governing speech and correction:**
- Pro 1:5, 1:6 — the wise hear and increase in learning; the wise produce words of depth
- Pro 9:8, 9:9 — the wise receive correction and grow wiser
- Pro 10:8 — the wise of heart receive commandments
- Pro 12:15 — the wise man listens to advice
- Pro 13:1 — the wise son hears his father's instruction
- Pro 15:12, 15:31 — the wise person is the source of correction; those who receive life-giving reproof dwell among the wise
- Pro 25:12 — the wise reprover to a listening ear

**Wisdom in relationships and community:**
- Pro 10:1, 15:20, 23:24, Pro 13:20 — the wise son makes his father glad; whoever walks with the wise becomes wise
- Pro 11:30 — whoever captures souls is wise
- Pro 14:3 — the lips of the wise preserve them
- Pro 12:18 — the tongue of the wise brings healing
- Pro 16:14 — the wise man appease's a king's wrath
- Pro 29:8, 29:9, 29:11 — the wise turn away wrath; wisdom in encounter with folly; the wise quietly hold back the spirit

**Wisdom as orientation of the heart:**
- Pro 10:14 — the wise lay up knowledge
- Pro 16:21, 16:23 — the wise of heart are called discerning; the heart of the wise makes speech judicious
- Pro 18:15 — the ear of the wise seeks knowledge
- Ecc 7:4 — the heart of the wise is in the house of mourning
- Ecc 8:1, 8:5 — wisdom makes the face shine; the wise heart knows the proper time and the just way

**Wisdom producing outcomes:**
- Pro 3:35, 14:24 — the wise inherit honour; the crown of the wise is their wealth
- Pro 14:1 — the wisest of women builds her house
- Pro 21:22 — the wise man scales the city of the mighty
- Pro 24:5 — the wise man is full of strength
- Ecc 7:19 — wisdom gives strength to the wise man more than ten rulers
- Psa 107:43 — whoever is wise attends to these things and considers God's steadfast love
- Hos 14:9 — whoever is wise will understand God's ways

**Wisdom's limits and vulnerabilities:**
- Psa 49:10 — even the wise die; mortality equalises wisdom and folly
- Ecc 2:16, 2:19 — of the wise as of the fool no enduring remembrance; who knows whether the heir will be wise?
- Ecc 6:8, 8:17 — what advantage has the wise? Even the wise cannot find out God's work
- Ecc 9:1 — the wise are in the hand of God
- Deu 16:19 — a bribe blinds the eyes of the wise
- Ecc 7:7 — oppression drives the wise into madness

### VCG-A-03 — Professional court wise men as institutional class: cha.kham / chak.kim as class title
*The court class of professional wise men — summoned for interpretation, giving counsel, named as the foil for what Spirit-given wisdom can do. Their title and incapacity stand together in the Daniel narratives.*

**Egyptian and Persian courts:**
- Gen 41:8 — Pharaoh called all the wise men of Egypt; none could interpret
- Exo 7:11 — Pharaoh summoned the wise men and sorcerers
- Est 6:13 — Haman's wise men deliver a judgment of doom against him

**Babylon — foil for Spirit-given wisdom:**
- Dan 2:12, 13, 14, 18, 21, 24, 27, 48 — the wise men cannot interpret; Daniel intercedes for them; Daniel appointed over them
- Dan 4:6, 4:18 — wise men summoned and unable; Daniel's Spirit-given capacity distinguished
- Dan 5:7, 5:8, 5:15 — the wise men cannot read the writing

**General institutional references:**
- Job 3:14 — counselors and kings rest in Sheol
- Job 34:2, 34:34 — Elihu addresses the wise as competent hearers
- Job 15:2, 15:18 — the wise man's speech should reflect his inner quality; wise men's tradition
- Jer 50:35, 51:57 — Babylon's wise men are among those God will destroy

### VCG-A-04 — Wisdom receiving instruction and growing: cha.kham as receptive and cumulative

### VCG-A-21 — sofizo: making wise (positive) and devising myths (negative)

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-A-001 to OBS-A-014 carried forward from v1** — see wa-m15-a-verse-meanings-v1-2026-05-11.md for full text of all 14 observations.

**Additional observations from VCG derivation:**

**OBS-A-015 — The cha.shav H2803G group spans three meaningfully distinct uses**
Sacred craft design (VCG-A-07), secular craft use (VCG-A-08), and evil scheming (VCG-A-09) all share the term but name genuinely different inner acts. Divine purposive devising (VCG-A-10, 2Sa 14:14) is a fourth distinct use. The current database groupings do not clearly separate these.

**OBS-A-016 — VCG-A-04 and VCG-A-01 are closely related**
The receptivity-to-instruction dimension of wisdom (VCG-A-04) is a sub-pattern within the holistic inner quality (VCG-A-01), not a separate meaning. In the VCG analysis they are grouped separately because the emphasis and wording are distinct, but they may be held together with a note.

**OBS-A-017 — sofizo needs its own VCG with two sub-groups**
The two uses (making wise / devising myths) are opposite in direction. They cannot be held together. VCG-A-21 notes this; the database needs to separate them.

**OBS-A-018 — tu.shiy.yah consistently names wisdom as an effective resource, not wisdom as character**
The tu.shiy.yah group (VCG-A-11) is distinct from cha.kham (VCG-A-01) in that tu.shiy.yah emphasises effectiveness and substance — sound wisdom as the real thing that works. Cha.kham names the person and their quality; tu.shiy.yah names the resource and its effectiveness.
