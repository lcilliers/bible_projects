## Section 1 — Verse Meanings

*(See wa-m15-c-verse-meanings-v1-2026-05-11.md for full verse-by-verse table — 602 verses)*

Key terms and confirmed meaning patterns:
- **H1847 da.at** — knowledge as inner possession (wisdom literature); knowledge of God as the orienting relational content of the inner person; absence of da.at as the ground of destruction; knowledge as what Proverbs aims to impart; da.at as the instrument of the Servant's justifying work (Isa 53:11 — unique)
- **H3045 ya.da** — covenantal relational knowing of God and being known by God; knowing through experienced acts ("then you shall know"); failed/absent covenantal knowing; God's comprehensive knowing of persons; moral knowledge guiding right action; self-knowledge; limits of human knowing before divine mystery; knowing as elective/intimate (Amo 3:2)
- **H1844 de.ah** — knowledge as a divine attribute; eschatological knowledge of God filling the earth (Isa 11:9, Hab 2:14); Elihu's perfect-knowledge claim
- **H1843 de.a** — formed inner opinion or judgment; divine perfect knowledge
- **H4486 man.da** — knowledge as a divine gift; reason as the defining inner seat of humanness (Dan 4:34/36 — the most anthropologically significant use in the cluster)
- **G6063 oida** — settled inner conviction about theological, moral, or existential truths; identity-shaping knowledge; self-knowledge; God's knowing of persons and the Spirit
- **G0050 agnoeō** — culpable inner ignorance of God's ways; neutral informational unawareness (the two uses need separation)

## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

Reading through all 602 verse meanings, the following patterns emerge from the text. M15-C is the largest sub-group; the patterns are numerous but coherent.

### VCG-C-01 — da.at as inner possession: knowledge as an inner quality of the wise person
*Knowledge as what the wise person holds inwardly — the inner capacity/content that Proverbs aims to produce, that the wise accumulate and guard, and that gives the person strength.*

- Pro 1:4, 1:7, 1:22, 1:29 — knowledge as what Proverbs gives; its ground is fear of the Lord; fools hate it; it was offered and refused
- Pro 2:5, 2:6, 2:10 — finding the knowledge of God as the goal; from God's mouth come knowledge; knowledge pleasant to the soul
- Pro 8:9, 8:10, 8:12 — right to those who find knowledge; knowledge rather than gold; Wisdom holds knowledge
- Pro 9:10 — the knowledge of the Holy One is insight
- Pro 10:14, 12:1, 12:23, 13:16, 14:6, 14:7, 14:18 — the wise lay up knowledge; discipline produces knowledge; prudent man conceals knowledge; every prudent man acts with knowledge; knowledge easy to a man of understanding; no knowledge in the fool's words; the prudent are crowned with knowledge
- Pro 15:2, 15:7, 15:14 — wise tongue commends knowledge; lips of the wise spread knowledge; heart of understanding seeks knowledge
- Pro 17:27, 18:15, 19:2, 19:25, 19:27 — restraint of words is knowledge; intelligent heart acquires knowledge; desire without knowledge is not good; reprove a man of understanding and he gains knowledge; straying from words of knowledge
- Pro 20:15, 21:11, 22:12, 22:17, 22:20, 23:12 — lips of knowledge are precious; the wise gains knowledge when instructed; God keeps watch over knowledge; apply your heart to my knowledge; thirty sayings of counsel and knowledge; apply your ear to words of knowledge
- Pro 24:4, 24:5, 29:7, 30:3 — by knowledge the rooms are filled; a man of knowledge enhances his might; wicked cannot understand knowledge; Agur has no knowledge of the Holy One
- Ecc 1:16, 1:18, 9:10 — heart has experience of knowledge; increasing knowledge increases sorrow; no knowledge in Sheol
- Job 13:2 — what you know, I also know [knowledge as the ground of dispute]
- Job 33:3 — what my lips know they speak [da.at as inner content expressed in speech]
- Mal 2:7 — lips of a priest should guard knowledge [priestly possession for the community]

- Isa 33:6 — abundance of salvation, wisdom, and knowledge as God's gift

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-C-001 through OBS-C-008 carried forward from v1** — see wa-m15-c-verse-meanings-v1-2026-05-11.md.

**Additional observations from VCG derivation:**

**OBS-C-009 — ya.da has the widest range of any term in M15-C, spanning at least nine meaningfully distinct patterns**
VCG-C-06 through VCG-C-12 cover: elective covenantal knowing, divine self-disclosure through event, God's comprehensive knowing of persons, self-knowledge, moral knowledge, Israel's covenantal failure, and limits of human knowing before mystery. The existing 958-001 through 958-005 VCG structure maps approximately to these, but the "then you shall know" pattern (VCG-C-07) spans 958-001 and 958-005 in ways that need clarification.

**OBS-C-010 — The "then you shall know that I am the Lord" pattern (VCG-C-07) is one of the most frequent theological patterns in the OT**
Across Exodus, Joshua, Isaiah, Joel, Zechariah, and elsewhere, the repeated pattern — God acts so that persons will know him — frames divine action as self-disclosing. This pattern is a central theological mechanism in the OT and warrants explicit attention in Session D synthesis.

**OBS-C-011 — Jer 22:16 is the programme's most explicit equation of knowing God with doing justice**
"He judged the cause of the poor and needy. Is not this to know me?" This verse defines da.at in relational-ethical terms. Knowing God is not abstract cognition but enacted justice. It is the defining verse for understanding M15-C in relation to the justice/righteousness cluster.

**OBS-C-012 — Dan 4:34/36 (man.da as reason) is the most anthropologically significant use in M15-C**
Reason's loss is the loss of personhood; reason's restoration is the restoration of dignity. This is the programme's clearest statement that an inner capacity (man.da) is constitutive of humanness. It warrants explicit synthesis-level attention.

**OBS-C-013 — Isa 53:11 (da.at as instrument of the Servant's justifying work) is unique in the cluster**
"By his knowledge shall the righteous one make many to be accounted righteous." No other verse in M15-C makes da.at the instrument of justification. It is a remarkable theological use that stands apart. It warrants highlighting alongside the Gen 15:6 imputation material from M15-D.

**OBS-C-014 — 1Cor 8:1 and 13:2 (oida without love) bridge M15-C and C17 (relational-grace)**
Knowledge divorced from love is named as hollow. These two verses establish the dependence of inner knowing on its relational orientation — knowledge without love inflates rather than builds. The connection to the C17 relational-grace cluster is direct and warrants attention in Session D synthesis.

**OBS-C-015 — agnoeō splits into two genuinely different meanings (VCG-C-20 and VCG-C-21)**
Culpable ignorance of God's character (Act 13:27, Rom 10:3, etc.) and neutral informational unawareness ("I do not want you to be uninformed") are both present in the 1838-001 group. They need separation.
