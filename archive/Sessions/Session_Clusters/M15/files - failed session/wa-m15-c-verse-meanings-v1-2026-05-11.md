## Observations — M15-C

**OBS-C-001 — VCG 1838-001 (agnoeō/culpable ignorance) used for non-culpable contexts**
Several verses assigned to 1838-001 do not carry the meaning of culpable spiritual ignorance:
- Psa 19:2 — night reveals knowledge (positive disclosure, not ignorance)
- Hos 6:6 — the knowledge of God rather than burnt offerings (positive statement of what God desires)
- Num 24:16 — who knows the knowledge of the Most High (positive description of the seer's capacity)
- Mar 9:32, Luk 9:45 — disciples' concealed understanding (set-aside; judicial withholding, not culpable ignorance)
- Rom 1:13, Rom 6:3, Rom 7:1, 1Cor 10:1, 1Cor 12:1, 1Th 4:13, Heb 5:2, 2Cor 1:8, 2Cor 6:9, Gal 1:22 — these are mostly P-status or use agnoeō in the neutral sense of "uninformed/unaware" rather than culpable spiritual ignorance
The VCG description ("culpable spiritual ignorance") does not fit the neutral informational uses of agnoeō (e.g. "I do not want you to be uninformed"). Two distinct uses of agnoeō are present: (a) culpable ignorance of God's character/ways, and (b) simple informational unawareness. These need separate VCGs.

**OBS-C-002 — VCG 958-001 carries at least four distinct covenantal knowing patterns**
Reading all 112 verses in 958-001, the description "covenantal/relational knowing between God and Israel" is correct but covers several meaningfully distinct patterns:
(a) God's election knowing of Israel (Amo 3:2, Deu 34:10 face-to-face, Hos 13:5 wilderness)
(b) Israel knowing God through experienced acts (the repeated "then you shall know that I am the Lord" pattern in Exodus, Joshua, Isaiah, Zechariah)
(c) Israel failing to know God while claiming to (Hos 2:8, 8:2, 5:4, 1Sa 2:12)
(d) Persons seeking covenantal assurance from God (Gen 15:8, Job 23:3, 24:1)
These are not the same inner-being meaning. Pattern (b) alone accounts for a substantial portion of the group and could be its own VCG — it is specifically the "knowing-through-event" pattern of divine self-disclosure producing recognition.

**OBS-C-003 — Jer 22:16 is a defining verse for the relationship between da.at and justice**
"Is not this to know me?" (Jer 22:16) directly equates knowing God with doing justice for the poor. This is one of the most significant definitional statements in the cluster — da.at is not abstract cognitive knowing but relational-ethical orientation. This verse deserves highlighting in any synthesis work on M15-C.

**OBS-C-004 — Isa 53:11: da.at as the instrument of the Servant's justifying work**
"By his knowledge shall the righteous one, my servant, make many to be accounted righteous." Da.at here is the means by which justification occurs — the Servant's inner knowing is the instrument of atonement. This is a unique and significant use of da.at that stands apart from all other verses in the cluster. It warrants separate attention in Session D synthesis.

**OBS-C-005 — Dan 4:34 and 4:36: man.da as reason — the inner seat of humanness**
The two uses of man.da for Nebuchadnezzar's restored reason are the most anthropologically significant uses of any term in 962-002. Man.da is what makes the person human — its loss is the loss of personhood, its restoration is the restoration of dignity. This is the programme's clearest statement that reason (man.da) is a defining inner capacity of the human person. It deserves explicit highlighting in synthesis.

**OBS-C-006 — 1Cor 13:2 and 1Cor 8:1: knowledge divorced from love — the hollow inner capacity**
Two verses explicitly name knowledge as capable of existing without love and being emptied of worth by that separation:
- 1Cor 8:1: knowledge without love puffs up rather than builds up
- 1Cor 13:2: understanding all mysteries without love = nothing
These verses establish that inner knowledge is not self-validating — its worth depends on its orientation toward love. This is a significant finding for the synthesis of inner knowing with the relational-grace cluster (C17).

**OBS-C-007 — The "then you shall know" pattern in 958-001 and 958-005**
Across Isaiah 40–55, Zechariah, Ezekiel (from the broader OT corpus), and Exodus, a repeated pattern appears: God acts, and the result is that persons will know — know that he is Lord, that he sent his messenger, that he is in the midst. This is a distinctive theological pattern: knowing as the product of divine action rather than human seeking. VCG 958-005 partially captures this but it is spread across both 958-001 and 958-005. The pattern is more coherent than the current VCG structure suggests.

**OBS-C-008 — P-status and SA verses: ya.da has a high rate of non-inner-being use**
Ya.da has the largest verse count in M15-C (across all VCGs). The P-status and SA verses reveal that a substantial proportion of ya.da uses are purely informational (knowing facts, knowing persons in the social sense, knowing where something is) with no inner-being content. The set-aside rate appears appropriate, but the P-status verses require individual review — some will belong in existing VCGs, others may be correctly pending. |
