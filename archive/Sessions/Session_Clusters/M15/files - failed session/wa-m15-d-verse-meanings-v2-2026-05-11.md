## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

Reading through all verse meanings, the following meaning-patterns emerge from the text. These are grouped by what the verses actually say, not by pre-formed category.

---

### VCG-D-01 — Divine moral imputation: crediting or not-crediting righteousness/guilt before God
*The inner divine act of reckoning a moral status to a person — positive (faith counted as righteousness; act counted as righteousness) or negative (bloodguilt imputed; iniquity not counted). The reckoning is judicial and moral, not merely classificatory. The subject is always God or divine authority.*

- Gen 15:6 — God counted Abram's faith as righteousness
- Lev 7:18 — a wrongly eaten offering is not credited to the worshipper
- Lev 17:4 — bloodguilt is imputed to the one who does not bring the sacrifice
- Psa 32:2 — blessed is the man against whom the Lord counts no iniquity
- Psa 106:31 — Phinehas's zeal was counted to him as righteousness
- Mal 3:16 — those who feared the Lord esteemed his name [positive valuation directed Godward — adjacent but distinct: the subject is human, not divine]

*Note: Mal 3:16 is human esteeming of God, not divine imputation to the human. It belongs in VCG-D-03 (evaluative regard of worth) rather than here. The existing assignment to 3335-001 conflates these two directions.*

---

### VCG-D-02 — Inner self-examination: deliberate attending to one's own ways, forming a judgment
*The person turns attention inwardly and forms an assessment of their own conduct, situation, or moral standing — not reckoning another, but deliberating about oneself or one's circumstances.*

- Gen 38:15 — Judah forms a (mistaken) judgment about Tamar from what he sees
- 1Sa 1:13 — Eli forms a (mistaken) judgment about Hannah from observing her
- Job 6:26 — Job challenges his friends' assumption that despairing speech can be judged like ordinary words
- Job 35:2 — Elihu challenges Job's inner conviction of his own rightness before God
- Job 41:32 — one would think the deep to be white-haired [perceptual impression formed from observation]
- Psa 73:16 — the psalmist labours to form a judgment about the prosperity of the wicked
- Psa 77:5 — the psalmist considers the days of old to find orientation
- Psa 119:59 — thinking on one's own ways produces reorientation toward God's testimonies
- Psa 144:3 — God thinks of / attends to the human person [divine cognitive attending]
- Isa 5:28 — army's power creates the impression of flint-hard hooves [perceptual impression]
- Jer 23:27 — false prophets think to make the people forget God's name [purposive inner thinking — better placed in M15-E planning group]

*Note: Jer 23:27 is not self-examination but deliberate harmful plotting — it belongs in M15-E (3334-001 devising evil). The existing assignment to 3336-001 is a partial fit but the purposive character is primary.*

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-D-001 — VCG 5099-002 misassignment: Gen 3:6 and Job 5:12 do not fit**
Gen 3:6 (sa.khal — desired to make one wise) is assigned to 5099-002 (abstaining from taste as inner grief/penitence). The verse has nothing to do with abstaining from taste. Job 5:12 (a.rum — crafty) is similarly misassigned.

**OBS-D-002 — VCG 5099-002 misassignment: Isa 53:3 (cha.shav — esteemed)**
"We esteemed him not" is an evaluative regard statement, not abstaining from taste. The span does not match the VCG.

**OBS-D-003 — The imputation/reckoning pattern (VCG-D-01) is theologically significant**
Gen 15:6, Psa 32:2, and Psa 106:31 carry the specific meaning of divine moral crediting of righteousness or non-imputation of iniquity. These are foundational imputation texts. The existing 3335-001 conflates them with Mal 3:16 (human esteeming of God, not divine crediting to humans) — the two directions should be separated.

**OBS-D-004 — sa.khal shows two distinct but related meanings**
VCG-D-04 (wisdom-in-action producing success) and VCG-D-05 (perceptive discernment seeing clearly) are genuinely distinct even though both are sa.khal. Daniel's uses (12:3, 12:10) push sa.khal into an eschatological register — the wise who understand the end will shine — which is an extension of the perceptive discernment cluster.

**OBS-D-005 — diakrinō (VCG-D-08 and VCG-D-09) carries genuinely distinct meanings**
Discriminating judgment between things/persons (VCG-D-08) and inner self-division of doubting (VCG-D-09) are not the same inner reality. The VCG split is correct and should be maintained.

**OBS-D-006 — Heb 5:14 (aisthētērion) stands alone as VCG-D-11**
This verse establishes discernment as cultivated rather than given — a distinctive finding. It has no near parallels in the sub-group.

**OBS-D-007 — cha.shav evaluative-regard group: classificatory uses (VCG-D-03b) have thin inner-being content**
Territorial and legal classification uses (Deu 2:11, 2:20, Jos 13:3, Lev 25:31, 2Sa 4:2) carry marginal inner-being content. These are candidates for set-aside in a future pass.
