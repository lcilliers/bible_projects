### VCG-E-03 — Noble inner purposing: counsel as expression of moral character
*The inner purposing of the noble or morally oriented person — counsel as the outward expression of a sound inner character, including the case where the moral character is reflected back in what is planned.*

- Isa 32:8 — the noble plans noble things, and on noble things he stands
- Pro 12:20 — those who plan peace have joy
- 1Ki 12:28 — taking counsel toward idolatry (negative moral purposing expressed through counsel — included here as the counsel-expressing-character pattern, however in the negative direction)
- Ezr 4:5 — bribed counselors frustrating a good purpose (hostile counsel as character expression, negative)

*Note: The positive noble-purposing use (Isa 32:8) may stand alone as a thin group, but its contrast with the scoundrel's devising (Isa 32:7 — VCG-E-02) is the rhetorical point. It names the same inner-purposing capacity directed toward moral good.*

### VCG-E-04 — Divine counsel: God's sovereign purposive intent directed toward nations and history
*God forms and executes sovereign intention that cannot be annulled or redirected. His counsel is self-originating, irresistible, and encompasses the whole of history. Multiple human terms (ya.ats, cha.shav, za.mam) converge in expressing this.*

**God's irresistible sovereign purposing (ya.ats):**
- Isa 14:24 — as I have planned, so shall it be, and as I have purposed, so shall it stand
- Isa 14:26 — this is the purpose purposed concerning the whole earth
- Isa 14:27 — the Lord of hosts has purposed, and who will annul it?
- Isa 19:12 — what the Lord of hosts has purposed against Egypt
- Isa 19:17 — the purpose that the Lord of hosts has purposed against them
- Isa 23:8, 9 — who has purposed this against Tyre? The Lord of hosts has purposed it
- Isa 40:14 — whom did he consult? (God's wisdom is self-originating)
- Jer 49:20 — the plan the Lord has made against Edom
- Jer 50:45 — the plan the Lord has made against Babylon

**God's resolute purposing that does not relent (za.mam):**
- Jer 4:28 — I have spoken; I have purposed; I have not relented
- Jer 51:12 — the Lord has both planned and done what he spoke
- Lam 2:17 — the Lord has done what he purposed; he has carried out his word commanded long ago

**Nebuchadnezzar's sovereign human purposing (cha.shav + ya.ats together):**
- Jer 49:30 — Nebuchadnezzar has made a plan and formed a purpose [both terms]

### VCG-E-05 — Divine purposive planning with conditional openness to relenting
*God forms purposive intent that is held open — the plan is real and announced, but its execution is conditional on human response. This is different from VCG-E-04 (irresistible) — here the divine intent can be withdrawn if the people turn.*

- Jer 18:8 — I will relent of the disaster that I intended to do to it
- Jer 26:3 — that I may relent of the disaster that I intend to do to them
- Jer 36:3 — the disaster that I intend to do to them
- Jer 18:11 — I am shaping disaster against you and devising a plan against you [conditional — Return!]
- Jer 48:2 — in Heshbon they planned disaster against Moab
- Jer 50:45 — the purposes he has formed against the Chaldeans
- Lam 2:8 — the Lord determined to lay in ruins the wall of Zion
- Mic 2:3 — against this family I am devising disaster

### VCG-E-06 — Purposive divine and human intent working through the same event toward opposite ends
*The same event carries both human purposive intent (evil) and divine purposive intent (good) — the two work through each other. Gen 50:20 is the defining verse.*

- Gen 50:20 — you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good [cha.shav, both directions]

*This may be a single-verse VCG but it names a genuine and distinct meaning: parallel purposing through the same act. Isa 10:7 (Assyrian king's purpose vs. God's purpose through him) is an adjacent pattern but not identical — it is human destruction used by God, not the same event carrying opposite meanings simultaneously.*

*Isa 10:7 — his heart does not so think; but it is in his heart to destroy — belongs in VCG-E-02 (hostile inner purposing) for the human side, but the verse implies the divine use-pattern in context. Keep as noted.*

### VCG-E-07 — God's purposive care for the individual: divine inner attending and planning for the needy
*God forms purposive thought directed toward individual persons — specifically those who are poor, suffering, or needing — as an expression of attentive covenantal care.*

- Psa 40:17 — the Lord takes thought for me [cha.shav]
- Psa 16:7 — I bless the Lord who gives me counsel; in the night my heart instructs me [ya.ats — divine counsel in the night]

*Note: Psa 16:7 partially overlaps with VCG-E-01 (human counsel) but the subject is God instructing the person through the night — it belongs here as divine attentive purposing toward the individual.*

### VCG-E-08 — God's purposive planning for his people's welfare: the plans-for-welfare pattern
*God forms specific purposive plans for the welfare and future of his people — not reactive, not conditional, but a formed positive intent with named content (welfare, hope, future).*

- Jer 29:11 — I know the plans I have for you, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope

*This stands alone as a defining verse. It is distinct from VCG-E-04 (irresistible sovereign purposing) because the content is explicitly positive welfare-intent rather than judgment. It is distinct from VCG-E-05 (conditional) because it is not presented as conditional. It belongs in its own group.*

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-E-001 — Jer 29:11 is a defining verse for divine purposive planning for welfare (VCG-E-08)**
The plans-for-welfare verse stands alone as a distinctive divine-purposing pattern — positive, specific, non-conditional. It deserves explicit attention in Session D.

**OBS-E-002 — za.mam as irreversible divine purposing (VCG-E-04): distinct from conditional cha.shav (VCG-E-05)**
The distinction between irresistible divine purpose (za.mam, Isa 14 ya.ats) and conditional divine intent (cha.shav, Jer 18, 26, 36) is a significant theological finding — two different modes of divine willing in the inner life of God as presented in Scripture.

**OBS-E-003 — Gen 50:20 (VCG-E-06): the definitive parallel-purposing verse**
The same word (cha.shav) names both human evil intent and divine good intent through the same events. This is the most explicit statement of the divine-human purposing pattern in the programme.

**OBS-E-004 — Isa 9:6 (Wonderful Counselor) is the messianic summit of ya.ats**
The name Wonderful Counselor (ya.ats) places the Messiah as the supreme expression of divine counsel — wisdom directed perfectly toward those governed. This is the telos of the VCG-E-01 human counsel trajectory.

**OBS-E-005 — Luk 22:42 names the tension between human and divine willing in Christ**
The verse contains both human willing (if you are willing / not my will) and divine will (but yours, be done). The submission of Christ's human purposive will to the Father's sovereign will is the programme's most significant NT willing-in-tension statement.

**OBS-E-006 — 2Pe 3:9 (VCG-E-10): divine willing directed universally toward all**
God's not-wishing-any-to-perish is one of the clearest NT statements of the scope of divine wanting. Important for Session D synthesis.

**OBS-E-007 — VCG-E-19 and VCG-E-20 (shit): the split is appropriate**
The active attending and the failure of attending are genuinely distinct inner-being states. The split in existing VCGs 3578-A and 3578-B correctly captures this.
