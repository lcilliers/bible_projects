**Sub-group description:** Terms that name the inner turning of the mind on received material — musing, pondering, the inner dialogue of self-reasoning, and the contemplative considering of a person before God. Activity-oriented (distinct from static thought-content).

## Observations — M15-F

**OBS-F-001 — si.ach (242-001 and 242-002) carries two distinct forms of meditative activity**
The split is correct and meaningful: 242-001 names meditative dwelling on God's works, word, and promises (Psalms 77, 119 repeatedly, 143, 145), while 242-002 names complaint and lament as the inner expression of distress (Job 7:11, Psa 55:17) and, in two cases, telling/proclaiming of God's works (1Ch 16:9, Psa 105:2). The 242-002 proclaiming uses are the outward turn of meditative inner dwelling. The complaint uses are the outward expression of inner anguish. These are functionally distinct: devotional attending versus distress-voicing versus proclamation. The proclaiming uses within 242-002 sit closer to 242-001 than the complaint uses do.

**OBS-F-002 — Psa 119: si.ach and si.chah constitute the highest concentration of meditation language in the OT**
Seven si.ach uses in Psa 119 (vv. 15, 23, 27, 48, 78, 148 — all G status) plus two si.chah uses (vv. 97, 99) make Psalm 119 the programme's defining text for meditative inner engagement with God's word. The sustained all-day meditation of v. 97 is the statement; the rest show the practice. The connection between meditation and understanding (v. 99) is particularly significant.

**OBS-F-003 — Heb 4:12 and Mat 9:4 both assign to enthumēsis: two quite different uses**
Heb 4:12 (the word discerns thoughts and intentions) names enthumēsis as the inner conceived thought-objects that the word penetrates. Mat 9:4 (Jesus knowing their thoughts) names enthumēsis as the conceived inner deliberations that his knowing perceives. Act 17:29 (art and imagination) names enthumēsis as the human inner conceiving of what God is like. These three span: thought-objects exposed by the word, thought-objects known by Jesus, and thought-objects produced by human religious imagination. VCG 917-001 correctly captures all three as inner cognitive acts of conception.

**OBS-F-004 — dialogismos (1081-001 and 1081-002) is correctly split**
1081-001 names evil thoughts/reasoning from the heart (Mat 15:19, Mar 7:21, Rom 1:21, 1Cor 3:20, Jam 2:4) — the corrupt deliberative movements that produce sin. 1081-002 names disputed opinions/doubts/quarreling (Luk 2:35, 5:22, 6:8, 9:46/47, 24:38, Rom 14:1, Phili 2:14, 1Ti 2:8) — inner deliberative questioning that may or may not be culpable. The split correctly distinguishes moral corruption from contested opinion.

**OBS-F-005 — dialogizomai (1184-001 and 1184-002) is correctly split**
1184-001 names inner individual reasoning/questioning (Mar 2:6/8, Luk 1:29, 3:15, 5:21/22, 12:17, 20:14) — the heart deliberating with itself. 1184-002 names communal shared reasoning/discussing (Mat 16:7/8, 21:25, Mar 8:16/17, 9:33) — reasoning together as a group. The split correctly distinguishes individual inner deliberation from group discourse.

**OBS-F-006 — Psa 19:14 (hig.ga.von): heart-meditation paired with mouth-words as joint worship offering**
"Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in your sight." This verse frames inner meditation and outward speech as two dimensions of the same worship-offering. It is one of the clearest statements of the inward-outward structure of worship in the Psalter, and locates hig.ga.von as the inner counterpart of spoken word.

**OBS-F-007 — Dan 7:8 (se.khal): the sole verse for its VCG — contemplative vision-attending**
Se.khal in Daniel names a focused contemplative attention applied to revelatory content — not discursive reasoning but attending carefully to what is seen in the vision. This is a rare term with a distinctive use: the inner perceptive attending that mediates prophetic comprehension. The single verse is sufficient to establish the meaning.
