## Observations — M15-G

**OBS-G-001 — ra.yon (3449-001): all six uses are thought-content alarmed by revelation**
The Daniel uses of ra.yon form a consistent pattern: Nebuchadnezzar's dream-thoughts (2:29, 2:30), Daniel's responding thoughts (4:19), Belshazzar's terrified thoughts (5:6, 5:10), and Daniel's vision-thoughts (7:28). In every case, the ra.yon is an inner thought-content produced in response to divine revelation — and in four of the six cases the thoughts alarm or disturb the person. Ra.yon is specifically the thought-content that arises at the intersection of the human inner life with divine disclosure. Note: in the main transcript session note (from the prior session referenced in memory), Dan 4:19 and Dan 5:6 were flagged as appearing in M15-A; this is the M15-G record, which is correct.

**OBS-G-002 — noēma (1188-001): spans hardening, blinding, capture, corruption, and guarding**
The six noēma uses cover the full range of what can happen to the inner cognitive faculty: it can be hardened (2Cor 3:14), blinded (2Cor 4:4), corrupted and led astray (2Cor 11:3), captured and brought into obedience (2Cor 10:5), or guarded by God's peace (Phili 4:7). Satan's scheming noēma (2Cor 2:11) completes the picture. This is the programme's most comprehensive term for the inner cognitive faculty as a site of spiritual contest — it can be acted upon by God, by Satan, and by truth.

**OBS-G-003 — Heb 4:12 carries both enthumēsis (M15-F) and ennoia (M15-G)**
The word of God discerns both enthumēsis (conceived thoughts — reflective activity, M15-F) and ennoia (intentions — formed thought-content, M15-G). The verse thus unites both sub-groups: the word penetrates the dynamic activity of reflection and the static content of formed intention. This is the programme's most complete statement of divine penetration of the inner life.

**OBS-G-004 — mad.da (960-001 and 960-002): knowledge-as-inner-capacity and thought-as-inner-consciousness**
The three 960-001 uses (2Ch 1:10/11/12) name mad.da as the inner capacity for wise governance requested and granted — knowledge as what equips the inner person for a task. The two Dan 1 uses (960-001) name mad.da as learning given by God — formed knowledge content. The Ecc 10:20 use (960-002) applies mad.da to the private thought-life as moral seat — "even in your thoughts." This is a significant extension: mad.da moves from governance-knowledge to the inner moral consciousness of private thought.

**OBS-G-005 — frēn (2493-001): a single verse establishing thinking as a maturity-faculty**
1Cor 14:20 is the sole verse for frēn — be infants in evil, but be mature in thinking. This frames frēn as the faculty of mature inner thought, distinguishable from evil and capable of development. A single verse is sufficient to establish that in the NT the faculty of thinking (frēn) is understood as a capacity that grows toward maturity.

**OBS-G-006 — Psa 146:4 (esh.to.nah): plans that perish at death — a mortality marker**
The single verse names the formed thoughts/plans of a person as what ceases when breath departs. This is a significant anthropological observation: inner purposing belongs to the living, and mortality brings it to nothing. It implies that inner thought-content is constitutively linked to life — a finding relevant for Session D synthesis of inner-being and the human condition before God.

**OBS-G-007 — Psa 139:2 and 139:17 (re.a): same word for human thoughts known by God and God's thoughts precious to the psalmist**
The symmetry is striking: in v. 2 God knows the psalmist's re.a from afar; in v. 17 God's own re.a are precious and vast. The same word applies to the inner thought-content of both the human person and God — a theological symmetry in which both the human and God have formed inner thoughts, but at incomparable scales.
