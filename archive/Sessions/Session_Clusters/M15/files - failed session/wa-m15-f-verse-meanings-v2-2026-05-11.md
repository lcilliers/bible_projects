## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

---

### VCG-F-01 — Meditative dwelling on God's works and mighty acts: si.ach as sustained inner pondering
*The person turns inward attention to God's past acts — creation, history, deliverance, wondrous works — and dwells upon them. The attention is sustained, intentional, and productive of awe or orientation.*

- Psa 77:3 — when I remember God, I moan; when I meditate, my spirit faints [attending that intensifies distress]
- Psa 77:6 — let me meditate in my heart; then my spirit made a diligent search
- Psa 77:12 — I will ponder all your work and meditate on your mighty deeds
- Psa 64:9 *(from M15-D, sa.khal)* — they ponder what God has done [si.ach is not the term here — correct sub-group placement already confirmed]
- Psa 143:5 — I meditate on all that you have done; I ponder the work of your hands
- Psa 145:5 — on your wondrous works I will meditate

*Note: Psa 64:9 uses sa.khal not si.ach — confirmed in correct sub-group.*

---

### VCG-F-02 — Meditative dwelling on God's law, precepts, and word: si.ach as law-meditation
*The sustained inward dwelling on God's law, precepts, statutes, and promises — the defining meditative practice of the devoted person. The object is always divine instruction. All uses from Psalm 119.*

- Psa 119:15 — I will meditate on your precepts and fix my eyes on your ways
- Psa 119:23 — even though princes plot against me, your servant will meditate on your statutes
- Psa 119:27 — make me understand the way of your precepts, and I will meditate on your wondrous works
- Psa 119:48 — I will lift up my hands toward your commandments and meditate on your statutes
- Psa 119:78 — as for me, I will meditate on your precepts
- Psa 119:148 — my eyes are awake before the night watches, that I may meditate on your promise
- Pro 6:22 — when you awake, they will talk with you [wisdom's inner conversation with the person who holds it]

---

### VCG-F-03 — All-day sustained meditation as the ground of understanding: si.chah
*Sustained, all-day meditative engagement with God's testimonies as the practice that produces understanding — more than teachers can give.*

- Job 15:4 — you are doing away with the fear of God and hindering meditation before God
- Psa 119:97 — oh how I love your law! It is my meditation all the day
- Psa 119:99 — I have more understanding than all my teachers, for your testimonies are my meditation

---

### VCG-F-04 — Complaint and lament as inner distress voiced: si.ach as outward expression of suffering
*The inner dwelling on distress that finds outward expression in complaint, moaning, or lament. The si.ach is the voicing of what has been turned over inwardly.*

- Job 7:11 — I will complain in the bitterness of my soul
- Psa 55:17 — evening and morning and at noon I utter my complaint and moan
- Psa 69:12 — I am the talk of those who sit in the gate, and drunkards make songs about me [others' si.ach about the psalmist — derisive talk]

*Note: Psa 69:12 applies si.ach to others' speech about the psalmist, not the psalmist's own meditation. It is a related but distinct use — si.ach as talk-about rather than complaint-from.*

---

### VCG-F-05 — Telling/proclaiming of God's wondrous works: si.ach turned outward
*The meditative inner dwelling on God's wondrous works turns outward into proclamation — the result of meditation is speech that tells what God has done.*

- 1Ch 16:9 — tell of all his wondrous works!
- Psa 105:2 — tell of all his wondrous works!
- Isa 53:8 — who considered that he was cut off out of the land of the living? [the absence of si.ach — no one attended to the Servant's death as meaningful]

*Note: Isa 53:8 names the failure of this form of si.ach — no one pondered the Servant's death. It belongs in this group as the negative case (attending not given) rather than in the complaint group.*

---

### VCG-F-06 — Inner musing that burns to groaning and speech: ha.gig as prayer-substance
*Deep inner reflective engagement that intensifies inwardly — becoming fire in the heart — and is then offered to God as the substance of prayer.*

- Psa 5:1 — give ear to my words, O Lord; consider my groaning
- Psa 39:3 — as I mused, the fire burned; then I spoke with my tongue

---

### VCG-F-07 — Heart-meditation producing understanding: ha.gut as the contemplative inner engagement
*A single verse. The meditation of the heart is named as what produces understanding — the reflective inner engagement before God is the generative activity from which wisdom flows.*

- Psa 49:3 — the meditation of my heart shall be understanding

---

### VCG-F-08 — Heart-meditation offered to God alongside spoken words: hig.ga.von as worship substance
*The heart's meditative activity offered to God as the inward counterpart of spoken prayer — what the mouth says and what the heart meditates are both presented.*

- Psa 19:14 — let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in your sight
- Lam 3:62 — the lips and thoughts of my assailants are against me all the day [hostile meditations of others — hig.ga.von applied to the adversary's inner thoughts]

*Note: The two hig.ga.von uses move in opposite directions — Psa 19:14 is the psalmist's meditation offered to God (worship); Lam 3:62 is the adversary's thoughts directed against the psalmist (hostility). These are the same word applied in contrasting relational directions. They may need separate groups or a single group with noted polarity.*

---

### VCG-F-09 — Focused contemplative attention directed at a revelatory vision: se.khal
*A single verse. The focused, careful attending to what is seen in a prophetic vision — a deliberate inner act of considering what appears.*

- Dan 7:8 — I considered the horns, and behold, there came up among them another horn

---

### VCG-F-10 — God declaring the inner thought of the human person: se.ach
*A single verse. God knows and declares to man what his thought is — the creative God who forms mountains also knows and discloses the inner thought of the creature.*

- Amo 4:13 — he who declares to man what is his thought, who makes the morning darkness

---

### VCG-F-11 — Evil thoughts arising from the heart: dialogismos as corrupt inner reasoning
*The evil reasoning or thoughts that originate in the heart — not incidental but structural, preceding and generating sinful action. They are the inner source of moral failure.*

- Mat 15:19 — out of the heart come evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false witness, slander
- Mar 7:21 — from within, out of the heart of man, come evil thoughts [parallel passage]
- Rom 1:21 — they became futile in their thinking, and their foolish hearts were darkened
- 1Cor 3:20 — the Lord knows the thoughts of the wise, that they are futile
- Jam 2:4 — making distinctions and becoming judges with evil thoughts

---

### VCG-F-12 — Disputed opinions, doubts, and inner questioning: dialogismos as contested inner deliberation
*The inner reasoning that is in dispute — either doubts arising in the heart that disturb faith, or contested opinions that produce quarrelling within the community.*

- Luk 2:35 — thoughts from many hearts may be revealed [hidden inner deliberations exposed]
- Luk 5:22 — when Jesus perceived their thoughts [inner questioning known to him]
- Luk 6:8 — he knew their thoughts [same pattern]
- Luk 9:46 — an argument arose among them [shared disputed reasoning surfacing]
- Luk 9:47 — Jesus knowing the reasoning of their hearts [internal deliberation known to him]
- Luk 24:38 — why do doubts arise in your hearts?
- Rom 14:1 — welcome him, but not to quarrel over opinions
- Phili 2:14 — do all things without grumbling or disputing
- 1Ti 2:8 — lifting holy hands without anger or quarreling

### VCG-F-13 — Individual inner reasoning and questioning: dialogizomai as the heart deliberating with itself
*The inner act of the individual person reasoning within themselves — the heart raising questions, forming impressions, or trying to understand. Can be neutral, perplexed, or in the presence of Jesus.*

- Mar 2:6, 8 — scribes questioning in their hearts; Jesus asking why they question
- Luk 1:29 — Mary tried to discern what sort of greeting this might be
- Luk 3:15 — all were questioning in their hearts concerning John
- Luk 5:21, 22 — scribes questioning; Jesus perceiving their questioning
- Luk 12:17 — the rich man thought to himself, 'What shall I do?'
- Luk 20:14 — the tenants said to themselves, 'This is the heir. Let us kill him.'

---

### VCG-F-14 — Communal shared deliberation: dialogizomai as reasoning together
*The disciples or others reasoning together as a group — discussing a shared situation, usually producing an inadequate response that Jesus addresses.*

- Mat 16:7, 8 — disciples discussing among themselves about bread; Jesus challenging their reasoning
- Mat 21:25 — the leaders discussed among themselves how to answer about John's baptism
- Mar 8:16, 17 — disciples discussing about bread; Jesus naming their hardened hearts
- Mar 9:33 — disciples discussing on the way about greatness

---

### VCG-F-15 — Inner conceived thoughts perceived by Jesus or exposed by the word: enthumēsis
*The inner conceived thoughts of persons — the cognitive acts of the heart — that are directly accessible to Christ's perception or to the living word of God.*

- Mat 9:4 — Jesus knowing their thoughts [enthumēsis]
- Mat 12:25 — knowing their thoughts [enthumēsis]
- Act 17:29 — the divine being is not like gold or silver or stone, an image formed by the art and imagination of man [enthumēsis of human religious imagination]
- Heb 4:12 — the word discerns the thoughts and intentions of the heart [enthumēsis alongside ennoia]

---

### VCG-F-16 — Deliberate inner reflection on a situation: enthumeomai as considered attending
*The deliberate inner act of reflecting upon and considering a situation — sustained, purposive attending that precedes a decision or response.*

- Mat 1:20 — as he considered these things, an angel appeared to him in a dream [Joseph reflecting on Mary's pregnancy]
- Mat 9:4 — why do you think evil in your hearts? [enthumeomai — inner reflective thinking]
- Act 10:19 — while Peter was pondering the vision, the Spirit said to him [deliberate inner attending to the vision's meaning]

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-F-001 — si.ach and si.chah both belong to the meditation family but carry different emphases**
Si.ach covers a broader range: law-meditation (VCG-F-02), attending to God's works (VCG-F-01), complaint (VCG-F-04), and proclamation (VCG-F-05). Si.chah is specifically the all-day sustained meditation that produces understanding — a narrower, more intensive form. The distinction between these as meanings within the meditation family is clear from the text.

**OBS-F-002 — Psalm 119's si.ach uses constitute the defining OT text for law-meditation**
Six of the seven law-meditation uses come from Psalm 119, and all are G-status. This cluster is the programme's most concentrated meditation text and provides the structural spine for VCG-F-02.

**OBS-F-003 — Hig.ga.von carries both devotional and hostile uses (VCG-F-08)**
Psa 19:14 (heart's meditation offered to God) and Lam 3:62 (assailants' thoughts against the psalmist) use the same word in opposite relational directions. This is an inner quality that can be either worship-directed or hostility-directed depending on who is meditating and toward whom.

**OBS-F-004 — dialogismos (VCG-F-11 and VCG-F-12) correctly distinguishes evil-from-heart from disputed-opinion**
The evil thoughts that originate in the heart (VCG-F-11 — structural moral corruption) and the disputed opinions/doubts that arise in contested situations (VCG-F-12 — epistemically uncertain deliberation) are genuinely different inner-being realities. The VCG split is appropriate.

**OBS-F-05 — Isa 53:8 belongs in VCG-F-05 as the negative case of meditation-about-the-servant**
"Who considered that he was cut off?" — the si.ach that no one directed toward the Servant's death. The absence of meditative pondering is itself a finding about the generation that witnessed it.
