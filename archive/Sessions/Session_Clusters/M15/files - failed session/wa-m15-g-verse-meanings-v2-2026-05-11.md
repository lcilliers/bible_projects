## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

---

### VCG-G-01 — Knowledge as inner capacity for wise governance and discernment: mad.da
*Mad.da names the formed knowledge held within that equips the person for a task — specifically governance and discernment. The knowledge is requested, given by God, and enables wise leadership.*

- 2Ch 1:10 — give me now wisdom and knowledge to govern this people
- 2Ch 1:11 — because you asked for wisdom and knowledge for yourself that you may govern
- 2Ch 1:12 — wisdom and knowledge are granted to you
- Dan 1:4 — youths endowed with knowledge, understanding, learning
- Dan 1:17 — God gave them learning and skill in all literature and wisdom

---

### VCG-G-02 — Inner consciousness as the moral seat: mad.da as thought-life
*Mad.da used for the thought-life or inner consciousness as the place where private moral evaluation occurs — even private thoughts carry accountability.*

- Ecc 10:20 — even in your thoughts, do not curse the king

---

### VCG-G-03 — Thoughts alarmed by revelatory dream-visions: ra.yon
*Inner thought-content produced in response to divine revelation through dreams or visions — in every case the ra.yon disturbs or alarms the person receiving or hearing of it. Includes the thoughts the queen counsels the king not to allow to master him.*

- Dan 2:29 — to you as you lay in bed came thoughts of what would be after this
- Dan 2:30 — in order that you may know the thoughts of your mind
- Dan 4:19 — Daniel was dismayed for a while, and his thoughts alarmed him
- Dan 5:6 — the king's color changed, and his thoughts alarmed him
- Dan 5:10 — let not your thoughts alarm you or your color change
- Dan 7:28 — my thoughts greatly alarmed me, and my color changed; but I kept the matter in my heart

*Pattern: ra.yon is specifically the thought-content that arises at the intersection of human inner life with divine revelation — and in all cases it disturbs. The thoughts of Dan 7:28 are kept inwardly ("I kept the matter in my heart") — an additional note about how this alarming inner content is managed.*

---

### VCG-G-04 — Inner thoughts known by God and precious to the psalmist: re.a
*The psalmist's thoughts are transparent to God, discerned from afar. God's own thoughts are precious and vast beyond counting. The same word applies in both directions, creating a symmetry of mutual thought-content.*

- Psa 139:2 — you discern my thoughts from afar
- Psa 139:17 — how precious to me are your thoughts, O God! How vast is the sum of them!

---

### VCG-G-05 — Ease-induced inner contempt for suffering: ash.tut
*A single verse. The inner attitude of those who are comfortable — from their ease, they form inner contempt for those whose feet slip into misfortune.*

- Job 12:5 — in the thought of one who is at ease there is contempt for misfortune

---

### VCG-G-06 — Plans and thoughts that perish at death: esh.to.nah as mortality marker
*A single verse. The inner purposings and plans of a person belong to the living — on the day their breath departs, these plans perish. Inner thought-content is constitutively linked to life.*

- Psa 146:4 — on that very day his plans perish

---

### VCG-G-07 — The inner cognitive faculty as site of spiritual contest: noēma
*The mind as inner cognitive faculty is not neutral — it can be hardened, blinded, led astray, or guarded. Satan works through it; God guards it; the apostolic mission takes its thoughts captive. All six uses locate noēma as a contested inner terrain.*

**Acted upon negatively:**
- 2Cor 3:14 — their minds were hardened [unable to receive what the old covenant communicates]
- 2Cor 4:4 — the god of this world has blinded the minds of the unbelievers
- 2Cor 11:3 — your thoughts will be led astray from a sincere and pure devotion to Christ

**Contested and acted upon positively:**
- 2Cor 2:11 — we are not ignorant of his [Satan's] designs [noēma used for the adversary's scheming mind]
- 2Cor 10:5 — take every thought captive to obey Christ
- Phili 4:7 — the peace of God will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus

---

### VCG-G-08 — The faculty of mature thinking: frēn
*A single verse. The capacity for moral and rational discernment — named as a faculty that can be childlike or mature. The command is to be mature in this faculty while being infants in evil.*

- 1Cor 14:20 — do not be children in your thinking; in your thinking be mature

---

### VCG-G-09 — Inner thoughts and purposes of the heart; the way of thinking to be adopted: ennoia
*The inner thought-content and purposive orientation of the heart — what only God's word can discern (Heb 4:12), and the way of thinking to be taken on as armour (1Pe 4:1).*

- Heb 4:12 — discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart [ennoia alongside enthumēsis]
- 1Pe 4:1 — arm yourselves with the same way of thinking

---

### VCG-G-10 — The purposeful inner intent of the heart requiring repentance: epinoia
*A single verse. The inner thought-disposition that animated Simon's attempt to purchase the Spirit — a purposeful intent that requires repentance and forgiveness.*

- Act 8:22 — pray to the Lord that the intent of your heart may be forgiven you

---

### VCG-G-11 — Hidden inner thoughts known directly to Jesus: dianoēma
*A single verse. The hidden inner thought-content of persons that is directly accessible to Jesus's perception — he knows what they are thinking before they speak.*

- Luk 11:17 — but he, knowing their thoughts, said to them...

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-G-001 — ra.yon (VCG-G-03): all six uses are thought-content alarmed by revelation**
The pattern is consistent and distinctive: ra.yon is the inner thought-content produced at the intersection of human inner life and divine disclosure. In every case it disturbs. Dan 7:28 adds the management of this alarming content — the matter is kept in the heart.

**OBS-G-002 — noēma (VCG-G-07): the most comprehensively contested inner term in M15-G**
Hardening, blinding, being led astray, being captured, being guarded — noēma carries the full range of what can happen to the inner cognitive faculty under spiritual pressure. It is the programme's richest NT term for the mind as site of spiritual contest.

**OBS-G-003 — Psa 139:2 and 139:17 (re.a): symmetry of human and divine thought-content**
The same word names: the psalmist's thoughts known to God (v.2), and God's thoughts precious to the psalmist (v.17). This symmetry — person and God both having re.a, at incomparable scales — is a significant observation for Session D synthesis on the divine-human inner interface.

**OBS-G-004 — Heb 4:12 connects M15-F (enthumēsis) and M15-G (ennoia)**
The word of God discerns both the cognitive acts of reflection (enthumēsis — M15-F) and the formed inner intentions (ennoia — M15-G). The verse bridges both sub-groups, naming the word's comprehensive penetration of the inner life.

**OBS-G-005 — Psa 146:4 (esh.to.nah): inner purposing as constitutively linked to life**
The plans that perish at death disclose that inner thought-content belongs to the living — it is not separable from the life of the person. This is relevant for Session D synthesis of inner-being and mortality.
