## Assessment codes
- **FIT** — description correctly characterises the verse set as it stands after moves
- **FIT-MINOR** — description is broadly correct; minor wording clarification would improve precision
- **MISMATCH** — description does not accurately characterise the actual verse content
- **INCOMPLETE** — description is partly correct but omits a significant part of the verse set's content
- **NEEDS-UPDATE** — description must be rewritten because moves have changed the verse set composition
- **NEEDS-UPDATE-SCOPE** — description is correct for the core but needs scope adjustment after moves in or out

For each UPDATE needed: the current description is quoted, the problem is stated, and a replacement is proposed.

### 528-001 — cha.kham as constituted inner quality
**Current:** "cha.kham in Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and historical narrative names wisdom as a constituted inner quality of the person — not a role or a skill but who they are. The wise person is characterised by this quality: they receive instruction, they are cautious, they hold back their spirit, their heart is in the right place. The quality is relational — it shows in how they relate to others, to rebuke, to advice."
**Assessment:** FIT-MINOR
**Problem:** "Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and historical narrative" is too narrow — the group includes Job (Job 5:13, 9:4, 15:2/18, 17:10 etc.), Psalms (Psa 49:10, 107:43), and prophetic books (Hos 13:13, 14:9). The description's characterisation of the quality is correct.
**Action:** UPDATE-DESC
**Proposed:** "cha.kham names wisdom as a constituted inner quality of the person — not a role or a skill but who they are. The wise person is characterised by this quality across Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Job, Psalms, and historical and prophetic narrative. They receive instruction, hold back their spirit, and orient their heart rightly. The quality is relational — it shows in how they relate to others, to rebuke, and to counsel; and it is mortal — wisdom does not exempt from death or oppression."

### 528-004 — cha.kham/cha.shav as craft skill
**Current:** "cha.kham and cha.shav H2803G name an inner capacity for skilled craft that is explicitly Spirit-given. The skill resides in the heart/mind and is directed toward sacred construction. This is not general competence — it is an endowment for holy work."
**Assessment:** MISMATCH (after moves in: Exo 36:8, 2Ch 2:14 cha.shav rows; verse set already contained secular craft rows — Isa 40:20, Jer 10:9, Eze 27:8/9, Amo 6:5, 2Ch 26:15, 1Ch 22:15)
**Problem:** The description claims the group is exclusively Spirit-given sacred construction. It is not. Multiple verses name secular, commercial, military, and idolatrous craft skill using the same terms. The Spirit-given sacred cluster is the primary and largest use, but the description creates a false boundary.
**Action:** UPDATE-DESC (required)
**Proposed:** "cha.kham and cha.shav H2803G name an inner capacity for skilled craft and design. The primary cluster is Spirit-given: God fills the craftsmen with a spirit of skill (Exo 28:3, 31:3, 36:1/2), and the capacity is explicitly directed toward sacred construction — the tabernacle, its vestments, and the temple. The same inner capacity for craft devising appears in secular, commercial, and military contexts (Tyre's pilots and craftsmen, military machines, idol-makers, instrument-inventors), where the inner faculty is identical but the direction differs. The Spirit-given sacred cluster is the theological core; the secular uses share the same inner capacity applied to different ends."

### 528-005 — cha.kham as governance qualification / institutional role
**Current:** "cha.kham here names wisdom as the qualification for a role — the wise man sought for appointment, the wise men of the court, those who have the inner capacity for governance, law, and counsel.
