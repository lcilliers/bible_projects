## Observations — M15-BOUNDARY

**OBS-B-001 — logos (1171-001): the inner-dwelling and conscience-addressing uses form a coherent pattern**
Of the 11 G-status logos verses in 1171-001, a consistent pattern emerges: the logos is the word that (a) dwells within the person (Col 3:16 — "dwell in you richly"), (b) addresses the conscience (2Cor 4:2 — "commend ourselves to everyone's conscience"), (c) reveals inner condition (1Cor 1:18 — folly to some, power to others), (d) fulfils the law as inner moral principle (Rom 13:9, Gal 5:14), and (e) is evaluated by what inner power it carries (1Cor 4:19). The logos in these uses is not mere speech but word that acts upon and within the inner person.

**OBS-B-002 — dialegō (1080-001): Paul's systematic method of inner-faculty engagement**
The 13 dialegō uses in Acts and epistles reveal a consistent portrait of Paul's evangelistic method: reasoning in synagogues, reasoning in public halls, reasoning from Scripture, aiming to persuade. The method consistently targets the inner life through the engagement of reason. Act 24:25 is the clearest case — the topics he reasons about (righteousness, self-control, coming judgment) produce alarm in Felix's inner person. Dialegō in this group names reasoning as the outer form whose goal is inner persuasion.

**OBS-B-003 — gumnazō (7419-001 and 7419-002): inner formation is trainable in both directions**
Three verses train toward godliness and discernment (1Ti 4:7, Heb 5:14, Heb 12:11), one trains toward greed (2Pe 2:14). The same mechanism — habitual practice forming the inner person — applies whether the outcome is virtue or vice. This is a significant finding: the inner person is not fixed but is formed through repeated patterns. The VCG split correctly distinguishes the two directions.

**OBS-B-004 — suneidō (454-001): conscience-faculty in both moral-complicity and cognitive-awareness modes**
Act 5:2 (Sapphira's co-knowing of the deception — moral complicity) and Act 12:12 (Peter realising where he is — cognitive recognition) and Act 14:6 (Paul learning of the plot — becoming aware) represent the two poles of suneidō. The VCG description correctly notes both: moral-complicity as primary conscience characteristic, and cognitive-recognition as broader semantic range. These belong to the same inner faculty — the conscious awareness that underlies both moral knowing and factual recognition.

**OBS-B-005 — Hos 11:3 (tir.gal): the most relational verse in the BOUNDARY group**
"I who taught Ephraim to walk; I took them up by their arms, but they did not know that I healed them." This verse names God's intimate teaching-to-walk with the pathos of unrecognised nurture. The inner-relational dynamic is tender parental care meeting the ignorance of the child. The BOUNDARY placement is correct — tir.gal is a teaching/formation term rather than a wisdom characteristic — but the verse's relational content is significant for Session D synthesis of the divine-human inner interface.

**OBS-B-006 — hermēneia and diermēneutēs (5864-001 and 5865-001): interpretation as a community inner-life building gift**
The three uses (1Cor 12:10, 14:26, 14:28) establish interpretation as a Spirit-given capacity that enables the community's inner life to be built up through tongues. Without interpretation, tongues cannot address the inner person of the hearer — only the speaker's own inner communion with God remains. The community's edification depends on the inner gift being made intelligible.

**OBS-B-007 — eikō (7333-001): simile as inner-state characterisation**
Both Jam 1:6 (doubter like a wave) and Jam 1:23 (hearer-not-doer like a mirror-forgetter) use eikō to characterise an inner condition through comparison. The simile reveals what the inner state is like. This is the term's distinctive contribution: not naming the inner state directly but characterising it through a figure that illuminates its nature. BOUNDARY placement is correct — eikō is a comparator, not an inner characteristic — but the two uses are significant portraits of inner instability and unreceptive hearing.

**OBS-B-008 — logos (P-status): high volume of set-aside verses**
Among the 11 logos G-status verses above, many more logos verses are set aside (SA) as no_inner_being content across the BOUNDARY table. This is consistent with logos being a broad-semantic term whose inner-being relevance is specific to the dwelling/conscience-addressing/power-disclosing uses identified in 1171-001. The set-aside rate is appropriate.
