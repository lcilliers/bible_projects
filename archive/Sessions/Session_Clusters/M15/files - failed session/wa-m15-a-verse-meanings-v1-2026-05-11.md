## Observations — M15-A

**OBS-A-001 — Database routing anomaly: cha.shav row at Exo 36:8 assigned to 4458-001**
The H2803G cha.shav row for Exo 36:8 (vr_id 54622) is assigned to VCG 4458-001 (human wisdom overturned by the cross — a NT Corinthian category). The verse describes craftsmen making the tabernacle curtains with cherubim skillfully worked. This is sacred craft construction, not the cross-wisdom opposition. All comparable cha.shav craft verses are correctly in 528-004. This appears to be a database error.

**OBS-A-002 — Database routing anomaly: cha.shav row at 2Ch 2:14 assigned to 4458-001**
The H2803G cha.shav row for 2Ch 2:14 (vr_id 54608) is assigned to VCG 4458-001. The verse describes Huram-abi's capacity to execute any design assigned to him, alongside Solomon's craftsmen. This is craft skill in sacred construction. Same error pattern as OBS-A-001.

**OBS-A-003 — Database routing anomaly: 1Ki 2:9 assigned to 4458-001**
The H2450 cha.kham row for 1Ki 2:9 (vr_id 7343) is assigned to VCG 4458-001. The verse is David's deathbed instruction to Solomon: "you are a wise man; you will know what you ought to do." This is a direct positive attribution of wisdom to Solomon as a qualification for action. It is not a cross-wisdom opposition passage. The assignment to 4458-001 appears to be a database error. The verse belongs in 528-005 (wisdom as qualification) or 528-001 (wisdom as constituted inner quality).

**OBS-A-004 — Cross-sub-group span routing: ra.yon verses appearing in M15-A**
Dan 4:19 and Dan 5:6 carry an H7476 ra.yon span. The ra.yon term belongs to M15-G (VCG 3449-001 — thoughts alarmed by revelation). These verses appear in the M15-A verse table, most likely because of their association with Dan chapters containing cha.kham and chokh.mah terms. The ra.yon rows in M15-A should be verified against the database — they may be correctly routed to M15-G and appearing here only because the comprehensive report shows all spans present in a verse, or they may be incorrectly routed to M15-A.

**OBS-A-005 — VCG description mismatch: 528-004 includes both sacred craftwork and secular skilled work**
The VCG 528-004 description reads: "cha.kham and cha.shav H2803G name an inner capacity for skilled craft that is explicitly Spirit-given. The skill resides in the heart/mind and is directed toward sacred construction." However, the verse set includes Eze 27:8, 27:9 (Tyre's skilled pilots and caulkers — commercial maritime craft), Jer 10:9 (skilled craftsmen making idols), Isa 40:20 (skilled craftsman setting up an idol), 1Ch 22:15 (general craftsmen), and 2Ch 26:15 (military machines). These are not Spirit-given sacred construction. The description does not match the full range of verses in the group. The description needs revision to account for craft skill as an inner capacity more broadly, with the Spirit-given sacred construction as a subset, not the defining characteristic.

**OBS-A-006 — VCG 528-005 scope ambiguity: professional wise men as class vs wisdom as appointment qualification**
VCG 528-005 description: "wisdom as a capacity that qualifies persons for institutional responsibility." The verses span three distinct situations: (a) Egyptian court wise men summoned to interpret (Gen 41:8, Exo 7:11) — professional class, (b) Moses selecting leaders with wisdom as qualification (Deu 1:13, 1:15), and (c) prophetic/judgment contexts where wise men are named as a class to be destroyed or shamed (Jer 50:35, 51:57, Isa 44:25, Isa 29:14). The destruction of the wise men is a different thing from wisdom as appointment qualification. The group is holding verses with quite different inner-being meanings under one description.

**OBS-A-007 — Job 30:22: tu.shiy.yah translated as "toss" — text and meaning uncertain**
The span for Job 30:22 renders tu.shiy.yah as "toss." This is an unusual and disputed translation. The standard meaning of tu.shiy.yah is sound wisdom or effective capacity. The meaning of the term in this verse is uncertain and may not carry the inner-being content that characterises the other tu.shiy.yah verses. This verse warrants separate attention. It is currently assigned to 527-001 (sound wisdom as effectiveness).

**OBS-A-008 — Dan 11:30: bin span carries "attention" meaning, not understanding as inner faculty**
Dan 11:30 (vr_id 27692) uses bin in the sense of paying attention to / turning toward — "he shall pay attention to those who forsake the holy covenant." This is a directional action, not the inner perceptive faculty. The verse is assigned to 932-004 (failure/absence of understanding) in M15-B, where the bin span appears again. The meaning here is closer to "direct attention toward" than to understanding as a cognitive-spiritual faculty. Worth flagging for M15-B review.

**OBS-A-009 — Amo 6:5: cha.shav as inventing music instruments — secular creative devising**
Amo 6:5 uses cha.shav for inventing instruments of music in a context of self-indulgent ease. The same inner capacity for devising that appears in sacred craft construction is here directed toward entertainment. Currently assigned to 528-004. The VCG description (sacred construction, Spirit-given) does not fit this verse. This is another case where OBS-A-005 applies.

**OBS-A-010 — 2Sa 14:14: cha.shav as divine purposive devising — distinct from both craft and scheming**
2Sa 14:14 uses cha.shav for God devising means for restoration — not craftsmanship, not human scheming. It is divine intentional planning directed toward a merciful outcome. Currently in VCG 1174-001 (creative inner faculty of design — craftsmanship or providential mercy). The description tries to hold both craft design and divine mercy-planning together. These are genuinely different things and the VCG description is strained. The verse belongs more naturally in M15-E (deliberative planning, purposive intent).

**OBS-A-011 — 1Cor 3:10: sofos translated "skilled" — commendation of genuine competence, not cross-opposition**
1Cor 3:10 (vr_id 208338) describes Paul as a "skilled master builder." Sofos here names genuine competence positively — not the human-wisdom-overturned-by-cross pattern of VCG 4458-001. The verse is assigned to 4458-001, which is the wrong VCG. The meaning is closer to 528-004 (Spirit-given skill for sacred work) or 528-001 (wisdom as constituted inner quality). This appears to be a database error.

**OBS-A-012 — Jam 3:13 (sofos row): wisdom visible in conduct — does not fit 4458-001**
Jam 3:13 (sofos row, vr_id 208344) is assigned to 4458-001. The verse calls for wisdom to be demonstrated through good conduct and meekness — a positive characterisation of the wise person visible in their works. This is not the cross-wisdom opposition. The verse belongs in a group describing wisdom visible in a person's character and conduct — closer to 532-003 (sofia visible in a person) or 528-001.

**OBS-A-013 — froneō group (996-001): two distinct uses present — inner orientation as set-on-object vs relational concern**
Reading the froneō verses, two uses are present: (a) the mind set on divine vs earthly things as a directional inner orientation (Mat 16:23, Mar 8:33, Rom 11:25, Rom 12:16, Phili 3:19, Col 3:2), and (b) relational concern and communal alignment (Phili 1:7, 2:2, 2:5, 4:2, 4:10, 2Cor 13:11, Gal 5:10). These are not the same inner-being meaning. The first is about the direction of the whole person's habitual attention; the second is about relational attentiveness and shared mind. Both fit the VCG description broadly, but the description conflates two meaningfully different expressions. The existing VCG note already observes this (1825 and 1826 combined), but the full verse set reveals the distinction more clearly.

**OBS-A-014 — sofizo: two opposite uses present in 4458-001**
2Ti 3:15 (sofizo = making wise through Scripture — positive, transformative) and 2Pe 1:16 (sofizo = cleverly devised myths — negative, fabricating) are both assigned to 4458-001. They are opposite uses of the same term. 2Ti 3:15 is a positive transformative use; 2Pe 1:16 names what the apostles explicitly did not do. Neither fits the cross-wisdom opposition pattern of 4458-001. These verses belong in separate groups.
