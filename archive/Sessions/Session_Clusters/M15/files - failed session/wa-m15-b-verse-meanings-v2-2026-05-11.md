## Section 1 — Verse Meanings

*(See wa-m15-b-verse-meanings-v1-2026-05-11.md for full verse-by-verse table — 287 verses)*

Key terms and confirmed meaning patterns:
- **H0995 bin** — the inner perceptive faculty enabling comprehension, discernment, and right judgment; governance qualification; Spirit-given understanding; gained through seeking the word; failed or absent (human and judicial); God's perceptive comprehension of the human person; confronting limits before divine mystery; directional attending (Dan 11:30/37 — distinct use)
- **H0998 bi.nah** — the noun form of the same faculty; often paired with wisdom; something to be sought and acquired; Spirit-given to the Messiah; constitutive of the righteous person's orientation
- **H8394 te.vu.nah** — understanding as a Spirit-given capacity for sacred work; divine creative and governing attribute; growing with age/experience; protective inner quality; applied to idolatry (Hos 13:2 — misfit); self-enriching understanding (Eze 28:4 — misfit)
- **G4920 suniēmi** — inner comprehension activated or blocked; judicial withholding; Christ opening minds; universal failure (Rom 3:11)
- **G4907 sunesis** — inner comprehension of mystery as divine gift; loving God with understanding
- **G4908 sunetos** — the intelligent/discerning person excluded from divine disclosure; intelligence as openness to the word
- **G0801 asunetos** — the foolish inner condition; absence of the perceptive faculty
- **G5428 fronesis** — practical insight as divine gift; moral orientation of the just
- **G1990 epistēmōn** — genuine knowing verified by conduct (single verse)

## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

Reading through all 287 verse meanings, the following patterns emerge from the text.

---

### VCG-B-01 — bin / bi.nah as constituted inner perceptive faculty: understanding governing speech, relationships, and perception
*Understanding as a substantive inner quality of the person — what they are characterised by. The faculty is active, receptive, and productive: it governs what the wise person perceives, how they speak, and how they relate. The person of understanding is defined by this quality.*

**Understanding seated in the heart/mind:**
- Pro 14:33 — wisdom rests in the heart of a man of understanding
- Pro 15:14 — the heart of him who has understanding seeks knowledge
- Pro 16:21 — the wise of heart is called discerning
- Pro 17:24 — the discerning sets his face toward wisdom
- Pro 17:28 — the silent fool is deemed intelligent [simulated understanding]
- Pro 18:15 — an intelligent heart acquires knowledge
- Pro 28:11 — a poor man who has understanding will find him out
- Psa 32:9 — be not like a horse or a mule, without understanding [understanding as what distinguishes the human person]
- Psa 49:20 — man in his pomp yet without understanding is like the beasts that perish
- Hos 4:14 — a people without understanding shall come to ruin

**Understanding governing speech:**
- Pro 10:13 — on the lips of him who has understanding, wisdom is found
- Pro 11:12 — a man of understanding remains silent [understanding producing restraint]
- Pro 14:29 — whoever is slow to anger has great understanding
- Pro 15:21 — a man of understanding walks straight ahead
- Pro 17:27 — he who has a cool spirit is a man of understanding
- Pro 28:2 — with a man of understanding and knowledge, stability will long continue

**Understanding as a receptive and productive quality:**
- Pro 14:6 — knowledge is easy for a man of understanding
- Pro 19:25 — reprove a man of understanding, and he will gain knowledge
- Pro 17:10 — a rebuke goes deeper into a man of understanding
- Pro 20:5 — a man of understanding will draw out deep purposes
- Pro 28:7 — the one who keeps the law is a son with understanding
- Pro 28:16 — a ruler who lacks understanding is a cruel oppressor
- Pro 29:7 — a wicked man does not understand such knowledge [understanding blocked by wickedness]

### VCG-B-07 — Judicial withholding of understanding: suniēmi / bin as blocked comprehension
*Understanding deliberately withheld by God — the inner faculty is present but its activation is prevented by divine judgment. Hearing without receiving; perception without comprehension.*

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-B-001 through OBS-B-013 carried forward from v1** — see wa-m15-b-verse-meanings-v1-2026-05-11.md.

**Additional observations from VCG derivation:**

**OBS-B-014 — bin has a genuinely wide semantic range in M15-B**
Reading across all 287 verses, bin spans: (a) constituted inner quality (VCG-B-01), (b) governance qualification (VCG-B-02), (c) Spirit-given gift (VCG-B-03), (d) gained through seeking (VCG-B-04), (e) God's comprehension of the human (VCG-B-05), (f) failed/absent understanding (VCG-B-06), (g) judicial withholding (VCG-B-07), (h) activated comprehension (VCG-B-08), (i) universal failure (VCG-B-09), (j) limits before mystery (VCG-B-10), and (k) directional attending (VCG-B-11). This is the widest semantic range of any single term in the cluster. The existing VCG 932 family covers this but the multiple distinct patterns within it are analytically significant.

**OBS-B-015 — te.vu.nah has a strong sacred-craft pole and a strong divine-attribute pole**
The two clearest te.vu.nah patterns are: Spirit-given intelligence for sacred craft (VCG-B-03) and God's understanding as his creative and governing attribute (VCG-B-12). The protective/productive inner quality group (VCG-B-13) is a third distinct use. The non-sacred/self-serving uses (VCG-B-14) are a fourth. The existing VCG descriptions do not cleanly separate these four.

**OBS-B-016 — suniēmi's judicial-withholding and activated-reception poles are equally strong**
The NT uses of suniēmi are almost evenly divided between understanding being blocked/withheld (VCG-B-07, VCG-B-09) and understanding breaking through — whether commanded (VCG-B-08) or given by Christ (Luk 24:45). The judicial pattern (citing Isa 6) applies to Israel's unbelief; the breaking-through pattern applies to disciples and the mission to Gentiles.

**OBS-B-017 — Psa 49:20 is a defining verse for understanding as what distinguishes the human person**
"Man in his pomp yet without understanding is like the beasts that perish." This verse frames understanding as the inner quality that constitutes human dignity — its absence reduces the person to the level of animals, regardless of status. It warrants attention in Session D synthesis on inner-being and humanness.
