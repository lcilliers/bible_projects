## Section 2 — Derived Verse Context Groups

---

### VCG-B-01 — Reasoned discourse as engagement of the inner life: dialegō
*Paul's method of reasoning in synagogues and public venues — persuasive discourse aimed at reaching and convincing the inner person. The goal is inner persuasion; the form is reasoned argument. The effect can be conviction (Felix alarmed), communal growth, or stubborn resistance.*

**Productive reasoning aimed at the inner person:**
- Act 17:2 — Paul reasoned with them from the Scriptures on three Sabbath days
- Act 17:17 — he reasoned in the synagogue and in the marketplace
- Act 18:4 — he reasoned in the synagogue every Sabbath, trying to persuade
- Act 18:19 — he went into the synagogue and reasoned with the Jews
- Act 19:8 — speaking boldly, reasoning and persuading about the kingdom of God
- Act 19:9 — reasoning daily in the hall of Tyrannus
- Act 20:7, 9 — Paul talked with them, prolonging speech until midnight
- Act 24:12 — Paul was not found disputing with anyone [denial of disruptive form]
- Act 24:25 — as he reasoned about righteousness, self-control, and the coming judgment, Felix was alarmed
- Heb 12:5 — the exhortation that addresses you as sons [Scripture's dialegō toward the reader]
- Jude 9 — Michael disputing with the devil about the body of Moses

**Hostile disputation:**
- Mar 9:34 — they had argued about who was the greatest [the disciples' self-directed reasoning]

---

### VCG-B-02 — The word as inner-dwelling and conscience-addressing reality: logos (1171-001 uses only)
*The logos in these uses is not merely speech but a word that acts within and upon the inner person — dwelling, addressing the conscience, summing the law, revealing inner condition, evaluated by its inner power.*

- Rom 13:9 — the commandments are summed up in this word: "you shall love your neighbour as yourself" [logos as inner moral principle summing the law]
- 1Cor 1:18 — the word of the cross is folly to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God [logos differentiating inner responses]
- 1Cor 2:13 — we impart this in words taught by the Spirit, interpreting spiritual truths [logos as Spirit-taught inner content]
- 1Cor 4:19 — I will find out not the talk but the power [logos evaluated by what inner power it carries]
- 1Cor 14:19 — five words with my mind to instruct rather than ten thousand in a tongue [mind-directed logos reaching the hearer's inner person]
- 2Cor 4:2 — by the open statement of the truth we commend ourselves to everyone's conscience [logos as conscience-addressing word]
- Gal 5:14 — the whole law is fulfilled in one word: "you shall love your neighbour as yourself" [logos as inner moral totality]
- Eph 4:29 — only such as is good for building up, that it may give grace to those who hear [logos evaluated by inner grace-effect]
- Col 3:16 — let the word of Christ dwell in you richly [logos as inner-dwelling presence]

### VCG-B-03 — Conscious inner awareness: suneidō as moral complicity and cognitive recognition
*The same inner faculty of conscious awareness operates in two registers: (a) moral co-knowing — the inner awareness of shared participation in a morally charged act; and (b) cognitive recognition — becoming aware of a situation and acting on it.*

**Moral co-knowing (complicity):**
- Act 5:2 — with his wife's knowledge he kept back some of the proceeds [suneidō — shared inner awareness of the deception]

**Cognitive recognition:**
- Act 12:12 — when he realized this, he went to the house of Mary [suneidō — conscious coming-to-awareness producing purposive response]
- Act 14:6 — they learned of it and fled to Lystra [suneidō — awareness producing urgent action]

---

### VCG-B-04 — Interpretive capacity as expression of inner wisdom: pe.sher
*A single verse. Knowing the interpretation of a thing is named as the mark of the wise person — the inner wisdom that discerns meaning. The wisdom is so thoroughgoing that it reshapes the outward bearing.*

- Ecc 8:1 — who knows the interpretation of a thing? A man's wisdom makes his face shine, and the hardness of his face is changed

---

### VCG-B-05 — Intimate teaching-to-walk: tir.gal as tender, unrecognised nurture
*A single verse. God teaching Ephraim to walk — the intimate relational dynamic of the caring parent taking up the child's arms. The pathos is that the one taught did not know who healed them.*

- Hos 11:3 — yet it was I who taught Ephraim to walk; I took them up by their arms, but they did not know that I healed them

*Note: This verse belongs to M15 BOUNDARY because tir.gal is a formation/teaching term rather than an inner characteristic. However, the verse's inner-relational content — God's purposive care expressed in intimate enabling, met by ignorance — is significant for Session D synthesis on the divine-human inner interface.*

---

### VCG-B-06 — Translation revealing the inner theological meaning of a name: methermēneuō
*The act of translation that discloses what a name means at its theological core — Immanuel meaning God-with-us.*

- Mat 1:23 — which means, God with us

*Note: Act 13:8 (naming Elymas's name) is P-status — factual translation without inner-being content. Only Mat 1:23 carries inner-being relevance (the name's translated meaning discloses God's incarnate presence).*

---

### VCG-B-07 — Outward insistence arising from inner conviction: diabebaioō
*The act of asserting or insisting with confidence — the outward form of settled inner certainty. The two uses both name insistence without adequate inner grounding (first use) or with proper grounding (second use).*

- 1Ti 1:7 — desiring to be teachers of the law, making confident assertions without understanding [insistence without the inner substance that should ground it]
- Tit 3:8 — I want you to insist on these things [insistence grounded in the trustworthy word — directed by Paul as appropriate inner conviction]

---

### VCG-B-08 — Simile characterising an inner state: eikō
*The simile reveals what an inner condition is like — the comparison illuminates the nature of inner instability or unreceptive hearing.*

- Jam 1:6 — the doubter is like a wave of the sea that is driven and tossed [simile of inner instability]
- Jam 1:23 — he is like a man who looks at his face in a mirror and immediately forgets [simile of unreceptive hearing that leaves no inner trace]

---

### VCG-B-09 — Inner habituation through training: gumnazō forming the person toward godliness or vice
*The inner person is formed through repeated practice — the same mechanism (habitual exercise) produces either godliness and discernment (positive) or greed (negative). The inner disposition is trainable in both directions.*

**Training toward godliness and discernment:**
- 1Ti 4:7 — train yourself for godliness
- Heb 5:14 — those who have their powers of discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil
- Heb 12:11 — it yields the peaceful fruit of righteousness to those who have been trained by it [discipline as training mechanism]

**Training toward vice:**
- 2Pe 2:14 — they have hearts trained in greed [same mechanism, opposite outcome]

---

### VCG-B-10 — Spirit-given interpretation as a community inner-life building gift: hermēneia and diermēneutēs
*The Spirit-given capacity to interpret tongues — enabling what is spoken in tongues to become intelligible inner content for the community. Without interpretation, tongues cannot build up the inner life of others; only private inner communion with God remains.*

- 1Cor 12:10 — to another the interpretation of tongues [hermēneia as Spirit gift]
- 1Cor 14:26 — each one has... an interpretation. Let all things be done for building up [hermēneia as contribution to communal edification]
- 1Cor 14:28 — if there is no one to interpret, let each keep silent in church and speak to himself and to God [diermēneutēs — absence of interpreter redirects tongues to private inner communion]

## Section 4 — Observations

**OBS-B-001 — logos (VCG-B-02): the inner-dwelling and conscience-addressing uses form a coherent pattern**
The nine G-status logos verses in 1171-001 consistently present the word as acting within the inner person — dwelling, addressing the conscience, summing the law into its moral principle, and being evaluated by what it produces in the hearer's inner life. This is distinct from logos as mere speech-act.

**OBS-B-002 — dialegō (VCG-B-01): Paul's method as systematic inner-faculty engagement**
The consistent portrait across 13 dialegō uses in Acts: reasoning from Scripture, reasoning in public venues, persuading — the method targets inner persuasion through reasoned discourse. Act 24:25 is the clearest case: reasoning about righteousness, self-control, and judgment alarms Felix's inner person.

**OBS-B-003 — gumnazō (VCG-B-09): the same mechanism forms the inner person in opposite directions**
Godliness and greed are both named as products of habitual inner training. This is a significant anthropological observation: the inner person is not fixed but formed through repeated patterns, and the direction of formation depends on what is practiced.

**OBS-B-004 — suneidō (VCG-B-03): one inner faculty, two registers**
Moral complicity (co-knowing shared participation) and cognitive recognition (becoming aware of a situation) are both expressions of the same conscious-awareness faculty. The programme's suneidō group correctly identifies both as belonging to the same inner faculty.

**OBS-B-005 — Hos 11:3 (tir.gal): connection to M15-C covenantal knowing**
"They did not know that I healed them" parallels the M15-C pattern of Israel not knowing God even while experiencing his acts. The ignorance in Hos 11:3 (of the healing God) is the same ignorance as Hos 2:8 (they did not know it was I who gave her the grain). The two verses speak to the same inner-being failure from different angles.

**OBS-B-006 — hermēneia and diermēneutēs (VCG-B-10): interpretation as threshold between outer expression and inner reception**
Without interpretation, the community's inner life cannot be built up through tongues — the gift of interpretation is the threshold between outer form and inner content for hearers. This frames interpretation as a faculty that enables inner reception, not merely external communication.
