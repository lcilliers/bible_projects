## Observations — M15-D

**OBS-D-001 — VCG 5099-002 misassignment: Gen 3:6 and Job 5:12 do not fit**
Gen 3:6 (sa.khal — desired to make one wise) is assigned to 5099-002 (abstaining from taste as inner grief/penitence). The verse has nothing to do with abstaining from taste; it is about the desirability of wisdom as the motivation for transgression. The assignment appears to be a database error.
Similarly, Job 5:12 (a.rum — crafty) is assigned to 5099-002. The verse is about God frustrating the devices of the crafty — not about abstaining from taste. Another misassignment.

**OBS-D-002 — VCG 5099-002 misassignment: Isa 53:3 (cha.shav — esteemed) assigned here**
Isa 53:3 (H2803H cha.shav, span "esteemed") is assigned to 5099-002. The verse is "we esteemed him not" — an evaluative regard (cha.shav), not abstaining from tasting. The span does not match the VCG.

**OBS-D-003 — The imputation/reckoning pattern (3335-001) is theologically significant**
Three verses carry the specific meaning of divine moral crediting of righteousness or non-imputation of iniquity: Gen 15:6 (counted as righteousness), Psa 32:2 (counts no iniquity), Psa 106:31 (counted as righteousness for Phinehas). These are foundational texts for the theology of imputed righteousness. The VCG description correctly names this as moral crediting/imputing. These verses deserve explicit attention in Session D synthesis alongside the covenantal knowing material from M15-C.

**OBS-D-004 — sa.khal (519-001 and 519-002) shows a consistent wisdom-success-God alignment**
Reading across all sa.khal verses, the inner quality consistently produces either: (a) wise conduct aligned with God and Torah that results in prosperity/success (519-002), or (b) perceptive discernment that recognises God's acts and acts in accord with his will (519-001). The two VCGs are distinct but closely related. The Daniel uses (12:3, 12:10) push sa.khal toward an eschatological register — the wise who understand the end will shine. This represents an extension of the inner quality toward its ultimate expression.

**OBS-D-005 — diakrinō (1285-001 and 1285-002) carries genuinely distinct meanings**
The two VCGs for diakrinō are correctly distinguished:
- 1285-001: the inner act of discriminating judgment — discerning between persons or things, including wrongful discrimination, communal weighing of prophecy, and failure to discern the Lord's body
- 1285-002: the inner self-division of doubting — the heart split between faith and hesitation
These are not the same inner-being meaning. The VCG split is appropriate and should be maintained.

**OBS-D-006 — Heb 5:14 (aisthētērion) is the sole verse for its VCG and is distinctive**
The single verse for 818-001 identifies moral discernment as a trained, exercised inner capacity — not innate but developed through practice. This is one of the few explicit statements in the NT about discernment as a cultivated rather than given faculty. It deserves highlighting in synthesis.

**OBS-D-007 — cha.shav evaluative-regard group: many verses have thin inner-being content**
The 3335-002 and 3336-002 VCGs contain several verses where cha.shav is used for classificatory or territorial reckoning (Deu 2:11, Deu 2:20, Jos 13:3, Isa 29:16/17/32:15) that carry little inner-being content. These are uses of cha.shav in the sense of "counted as" for purposes of legal or geographical categorisation. The inner-being content is marginal. These may be candidates for set-aside or a separate thin-content group in a future pass.
