### Coherence assessment
C22 is a structurally heterogeneous cluster. The registries share minimal inner-being affinity:
- Ambition (Reg 3) and foolishness (Reg 63) have natural moral-character proximity — both name dispositions of the inner person that orient it away from wisdom and virtue.
- Character (Reg 20) names the quality of the proven inner person — adjacent to moral-character dimensions but distinct (it names the stable result of testing, not a specific vice or virtue).
- Vulnerability (Reg 206) names the state of the exposed, uncovered, or defenceless person — primarily Dependence/Creatureliness and Emotion territory, with strong Relational Disposition content.
- Experience (Reg 58) is structurally different: its dominant terms (ra.ah, energeō, energēma, energēs) are predominantly PROPERTY terms describing how inner realities are activated, penetrated, or operative — not named characteristics in themselves.
- Consciousness (Reg 27, 0 groups) and recognition (Reg 129, 0 groups) are present in name only.

Assessment: The cluster lacks a strong unifying inner-being affinity. The most coherent sub-grouping is {ambition, foolishness, character} — a moral-dispositional triad. Vulnerability and experience are analytically distant from this triad and from each other. The cluster's coherence is best described as a residual category for terms that do not fit primary clusters more clearly. No individual registry appears analytically misplaced in the sense of clearly belonging to another cluster — but the overall unity is weak.

### Boundary observations
- Reg 58 (experience): the dominant terms (ra.ah = to see/appear; energeō/energēma/energēs = to work/operative) are strongly property-term in nature. The groups are highly diverse. This registry's cluster placement reflects the English word "experience" rather than a coherent Hebrew/Greek term family. No immediate reassignment case is clear — the groups capture legitimate inner-being content. Note for Session D synthesis.
- Reg 206 (vulnerability): large (34 groups), predominantly built on nakedness vocabulary (Hebrew ʿārom/ʿerwāh/ʿeryāh family; Greek gumnos/gumnotēs/gumnazō family). Strong internal coherence within vulnerability itself; weak connection to ambition/character/foolishness.
- Reg 3 (ambition): the actual ambition-core terms (eritheia, filotimeomai) are a minority. The majority of terms are the philoxenos/philoxenia (hospitality) and philantropōs/filautos/filofronōs cluster — these are relational-disposition terms associated with ambition only loosely (perhaps as its counterparts or context). This is noteworthy — the registry's inner content is primarily hospitality/love vocabulary, not ambition vocabulary. Note for Phase B.
