## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God-relation is present in the **majority** of occurrences. divine_involvement=agent in **24/42**: na.cham *of God* — God as the one who relents/turns from a planned judgment (Gen 6:6–7; Exo 32:14; 2Sa 24:16; 1Ch 21:15; Jer 18:8,10; 26:3,13,19; 42:10; Amo 7:3,6; Jon 3:10; 4:2; Joe 2:13–14). God is **bearer/agent** of the relenting, not its object. On the human side, the turning is **directed toward God** (Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart, that the LORD is God"; Joe 2:13 "Return to the LORD your God"; 2Ch 6:37 turn-and-plead). divine_involvement=object=1 (Jer 15:6 "weary of relenting" — the relenting itself). So the relation runs both ways: God relents (agent), and the human turns *toward* God.

**T0.1.2** — ★ The characteristic **is borne by God himself** at full strength — the divine-relenting band (agent=24) is the larger half of the data. This is the single most distinctive T0 finding for this unit: the same lexical movement (na.cham) names both a divine act (God turning from wrath/harm — Exo 32:14; Jon 3:10) and a human act (Job 42:6 "repent in dust and ashes"; Jer 31:19 "after I had turned away, I relented"). So *turning/relenting* sits squarely **within the divine nature** (relenting from judgment) and is **reflected in the creature** (contrite turning-back). ⚑ SYNTH: this is the bilateral characteristic — the human turning is the image-analogue of the divine relenting; both are a *change of course away from harm*.

**T0.2.1** — Purpose is stated where the turning is framed conditionally: the human turn **averts the disaster** and **secures blessing/life** — "mend your ways… and the LORD will relent of the disaster" (Jer 26:13); "if that nation turns from its evil, I will relent" (Jer 18:8); "who knows whether he will not… leave a blessing behind him" (Joe 2:14); be.ra.khah "blessing" co-occurs ×2 (Deu 30:1; Joe 2:14). The divine relenting's stated role: it **spares** (2Sa 24:16 "It is enough; stay your hand"; 1Ch 21:15). ⚑ SYNTH (↔ #443): the turning is the **entry-state** of the resolution sequence — the inner posture of appeal that meets the divine relenting.

**T0.2.2** — **Both, by experiencer.** ★ The human turning reads as **created design rightly exercised** — it is the right/commanded response (valence righteous=8 + commanded=2: Deu 4:39; Job 42:6; Jer 26:3; Joe 2:13–14; Lam 3:21), the way back from the fallen condition. The divine relenting reads as a **constant of the divine nature** (the credal formula "gracious… slow to anger… relents over disaster", Joe 2:13; Jon 4:2) — not "fallen," but God's standing mercy-disposition. The lone sinful=1 (Isa 44:19 — the idolater who does *not* "consider/turn") shows the turning's **absence** as the fallen condition.

**T0.2.3** — **Largely SILENT.** No explicit future-fullness / eschatological orientation is tagged. Inferential only: the "turn and live" register (the shuv hinge to M10) implies an orientation toward restored life, but no occurrence in §B carries a future-state tag. Labelled inferential, not a finding.

**T0.3.1** — The aspect of divine likeness instantiated: the **capacity to change course away from harm** — to relent/turn. Grounded in T0.1.2: God relents from planned disaster (Exo 32:14; Jon 3:10), and the contrite person turns from evil (Jer 8:6 "no man relents of his evil" = the absence; Job 42:6 = the presence). The likeness is a *moral pivot*: the capacity to reverse a settled trajectory.

**T0.3.2** — ★ **Shared, but asymmetrically directed.** The same affect/act inhabits both, with opposite poles: God relents **from wrath toward mercy** (turning from harm he had planned — Exo 32:12 "Turn from your burning anger"; cha.ron "burning anger" ×2); the person turns **from evil toward God** (turning from sin — ra.ah "evil/harm" ×16 as the thing turned-from). The divine instance is the *restraint of just wrath*; the human is the *abandonment of unjust course*. One movement, two directions.

**T0.3.3** — Where present, the image is rightly ordered (the contrite turning — Job 42:6; Jer 31:19); where **absent**, the image is disordered — Isa 44:19 the idolater "no one considers, nor… recall it to mind" (shuv, valence sinful); Jer 8:6 "no man relents of his evil… everyone turns to his own course" (turning *back into* sin). The evidence shows both states, and names the refusal-to-turn as the disordered condition.

**T0.4.1** — **Largely SILENT / inferential.** No occurrence in §B is tagged typologically. ⚖ BORDER: the divine relenting that spares at the threshing floor (2Sa 24:16; 1Ch 21:15 — the future temple site) carries covenantal-typological weight in the canon, but it is **not tagged** in this evidence — labelled inferential, outside-extract. ↔ BOND / ⚖ BORDER: the NT repentance verb **metanoeō (G3340)** carries this characteristic forward but is **owned elsewhere** (Reg 112/135), so the OT→NT typological arc must be reached for in synthesis, not resolved here.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: **repentance / turning** — the inner *turn-back*. The name signals a **directional act** (a re-orientation, an about-face) that is also **affective** (contrition, regret) — a faculty in operation, not a state one is in. type=action 42/42 (100%) confirms the "act" reading at full strength.

**T1.1.2** — At the definitional level the two primary terms split the concept: **na.cham** (34) carries the *affective* pole (be-sorry / relent — regret, contrition, divine relenting; faculty=affect on all 34); **shuv** (7) carries the *volitional-directional* pole (return / turn-back / call-to-mind; faculty=memory+volition). So the vocabulary itself shows repentance as **affect + volition fused** — sorrow-that-turns. ud (H5749A, 1) is a thin "return/repeat" outlier (faculty=none).

**T1.1.3** — The name carries a **relational/directional** implication: turning is always *from* something and *toward* something. The §B data grounds the "from": partner ra.ah "evil/harm" ×16 (turning *from* evil; God relenting *from* the harm), relational=from on the divine-relenting cases (Exo 32:12,14; Jer 18:10; 1Ch 21:15). The "toward": relational=to on the human cases (Deu 4:39; Lam 3:21 "call to mind"; Joe 2:13 "Return to the LORD"). Constitutively directed.

**T1.2.1** — Primarily an **act** (type action 42/42) — but a *bilateral* act: a human contrite/volitional act (the turn) and a divine relenting act. There is no status/condition form among the focus terms (unlike unit #5 reconciliation, which is a state). Repentance is the cluster's **verb-life characteristic** — the §10 CHARACTERISTIC data-signal at full strength.

**T1.2.2** — **Compound.** na.cham = affect (contrition/relenting); shuv = memory+volition (the deliberate turn-back). The full act fuses **remorse (affect) + re-orientation (volition) + recollection (memory)** — exactly the cognitive-affective-volitional structure the science (T7.3) requires for full repentance. Constituent elements: an affective movement (sorrow/relenting) and a volitional turn (return), often with a memory hinge (shuv "recall/call-to-mind": Deu 30:1; Isa 46:8; Lam 3:21).

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: the **hardened refusal to turn** — the unrepentant persistence in a course. Grounded directly: Jer 8:6 "no man relents (na.cham) of his evil… everyone turns to his own course"; Isa 44:19 the idolater who does *not* "recall it to mind" (shuv, sinful); Jer 15:6 "you keep going backward" (the inverse turning). The opposite is not a separate lexeme but the **negated / wrong-direction turning** (turning *to* one's own course, turning *back* into evil — the lone sinful shuv).

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes the **fixed, unalterable purpose**: Eze 24:14 "I will not relent" (na.cham negated — God's irrevocable judgment); Num 23:19 / 1Sa 15:29 "God is not man, that he should change his mind" (na.cham negated — the immutability seam); Jer 4:28 "I have not relented, nor will I turn back." So the characteristic excludes/resists **irreversibility** — where the course is fixed, the turning is denied. ⚑ SYNTH: na.cham thus has a **paradoxical double seam** — God both relents (Exo 32:14) and does-not-relent (1Sa 15:29) — read per occurrence, never per lemma.

**T1.3.3** — Where repentance (the inner turn) ends, **forgiveness/atonement** begins (the divine response that releases the debt — units #1/#2). ↔ BOND: repentance is the *human side* of the bi-level resolution (H2 in §10: the CHARACTERISTIC is transformed/turned); forgiveness/atonement is the *divine side* (the RECORD is forgiven). It is *not* the remedy itself; it is the movement that meets the remedy.

**T1.4.1** — Mode per verse: realised as **Niphal na.cham** (the relenting/being-sorry stem — dominant; e.g. Exo 32:14 HVNw3ms) and **Hiphil/Qal shuv** (the causative "bring-back / call-to-mind" and the simple turn — Deu 4:39 HVhq2ms; Jon 3:10 HVqp3cp). Manner of functioning: **affective-volitional re-orientation** of the whole person (human) or of a declared purpose (divine). ★ The stem distributes by experiencer — Niphal na.cham carries most of the divine relenting; the human turning leans on shuv.

**T1.4.2** — ★ Varies by **experiencer and direction**, not by constitutional level. Same root, two operations: the human contrite turn (heart-seated, righteous/commanded) vs the divine relenting (narrated divine act, neutral, agent). The decisive axis is *who turns* (self/addressed ~17 = human; other/agent ~24 = divine) and *from what* (from evil = human; from wrath/harm = divine).

**T1.4.3** — **Yes, prominently.** The turning operates through a **communicative/imperative mode** on the human side: commanded valence=2 (Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart"; Isa 46:8 "recall it to mind, you transgressors") plus the addressed imperatives (Exo 32:12 "relent!"; Joe 2:13 "Return to the LORD"; Jer 26:13 "mend your ways"). experiencer=other(addressed)=4. The turning is *enjoined* — "turn and live."

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response, where tagged (immediate_response field): the divine relenting issues immediately in **sparing** (2Sa 24:16 "stay your hand"); the human turning issues in **contrite affect / shame** (Jer 31:19 "I struck my thigh; I was ashamed" — the turn followed by ka.lam/bosh shame, the conscience-output) and in **hope** (Lam 3:21 "this I call to mind, and therefore I have hope"). Most occurrences tag no immediate response.

**T1.5.2** — **Varied.** The tagged responses differ by experiencer: divine → sparing/stayed judgment; human → shame, contrition, hope. No single uniform immediate response.

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: ★ the human turning establishes a **restored relational standing** — it averts disaster and opens blessing (Jer 18:8; Jer 26:13; Joe 2:14 be.ra.khah; Deu 30:1) and re-grounds the person toward God (Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart that the LORD is God"). The divine relenting establishes **withheld judgment / preserved covenant** (Psa 106:45 "remembered his covenant, and relented"; za.khar "remember" + be.rit "covenant"). The over-time product is **a changed trajectory** (from harm-bound to spared/blessed).

**T1.6.3** — The sustained effect (changed trajectory / restored standing) differs from the immediate response (the shame/sparing of the moment): the immediate is the **episodic pivot** (the relent, the contrite blow, the stayed hand); the sustained is the **re-grounded relationship and averted future** (covenant remembered, blessing left behind, "turn and live").

**T1.7.1** — Conditions under which it takes hold rightly: a **heart engaged** (heart-seated 9× — the turn must be "from the heart": 1Ki 8:47 / 2Ch 6:37 "turn their heart"; Joe 2:13 "rend your hearts") and **knowledge/recognition of God** (co-partner ya.da "to know" ×4: Deu 4:39; Joe 2:14; Jon 3:9; Jon 4:2 — "I knew you are a gracious God"). The human turn takes hold where the heart turns and God's mercy is known.

**T1.7.2** — Conditions under which it is **blocked/distorted**: the absence of consideration/insight — Isa 44:19 "no one considers, nor… recall it to mind" (the idolater cannot turn for lack of da.at/te.vu.nah, knowledge/discernment); Jer 8:6 "no man relents of his evil… they have not spoken rightly" (no honest self-appraisal); the *fixed divine purpose* blocks the divine relenting (Eze 24:14; Jer 4:28 "I will not relent"). ⚠ HYGIENE / **SILENT on adversarial interference** — no spirit-distortion tagged in §B. (Science T7.3: "hardness of heart" — the progressive suppression of moral self-monitoring — is the analogue; biblical anchor Isa 44:19 / Jer 8:6.)

**T1.7.3** — Inner-being state where the characteristic is present-but-not-taken-up: the **un-turning** state — turning *to one's own course* (Jer 8:6 "everyone turns to his own course, like a horse plunging headlong into battle") and turning *back into* evil (the lone sinful shuv; Jer 15:6 "you keep going backward"). The person who is called to turn but does not is in a state of **headlong persistence**.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Per verse, seat is named in **10 of 42**: **heart=9, soul=1** (32 unseated). Heart-engagement leads decisively: co-seated lev/levav "heart" ×7 (Gen 6:6 "grieved him to his heart"; Deu 4:39; Deu 30:1; 1Ki 8:47 / 2Ch 6:37 "turn their heart"; Isa 44:19; Isa 46:8; Joe 2:13 "rend your hearts"; Lam 3:21), ne.phesh "soul" ×1 (Jer 26:19). The heart is the seat where the turning is named.

**T2.1.2** — ★ The pattern indicates the turning is **heart-seated when interior** — the constitutional centre of the turn is the **heart** (the will/understanding/conscience composite). The 32 unseated occurrences are dominated by the **divine relenting** (na.cham of God is mostly *not* heart-seated — God's relenting is narrated as a declared act, not located in an organ), which is exactly why interior seatedness is moderate (~24%) not dominant. It **never engages spirit or mind explicitly** (mind/da.at appears only as a co-partner, not as the seat of the turning itself). ⚑ SYNTH: heart-seatedness is the human-turning signature; its absence marks the divine relenting.

**T2.10.1** — Constitutional movement: **mostly none / SILENT.** Two occurrences tag origin=received-from-outside (Jer 4:28; Jer 18 register — the divine purpose "spoken/purposed" from God's settled counsel), which is a within-God origin, not a spirit→soul→body movement. No cross-level constitutional movement (spirit→soul→body) is tagged for the human turning. Inferential only: "rend your hearts" (Joe 2:13) implies the turn reaches the inmost seat. Labelled inferential.

**T2.7.1** — **SILENT.** No body-link is tagged for the turning as a constitutional seat. (Jer 31:19 "I struck my thigh" is a bodily *expression* of contrition accompanying the turn, not a constitutional body-seat of it — labelled inferential, an expressive gesture not a T2.7 link.)

**T2.9.1** — Origin: ★ **bilateral.** The **human turn** is generated *within the person* (heart-seated, self-experiencer — Job 42:6; Jer 31:19) under the prompt of God's word/instruction ("after I was instructed", Jer 31:19). The **divine relenting** originates in **God's own disposition** (the credal "gracious… abounding in steadfast love", Joe 2:13; Jon 4:2 — che.sed/ra.chum/chan.nun co-partners) and his remembered covenant (Psa 106:45). origin=received-from-outside tagged ×2 (Jer 4:28; the divine purpose). Mostly not stated (the field is sparse).

**T2.9.2** — ★ Origin is **multiple and experiencer-dependent**: within-person (the human contrite turn), within-God (the divine relenting, rooted in che.sed/covenant), and prompted-from-outside (the divine word that summons the human turn). It changes with context along the experiencer axis.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.1 Perception — T3.1.1 / T3.1.2 / T3.1.3:** **SILENT** — no perceptive-faculty (inner senses / discernment) engagement tagged for the focus terms. (Inferential only: God "saw what they did" before relenting, Jon 3:10 — but that is the *cause*, not perception as the turning's faculty.)

**T3.2 Cognition — T3.2.1** — **Yes, as a co-engaged faculty.** shuv "recall / call to mind" carries a cognitive-recollective edge, and the turning's *conditions* engage cognition: co-partner ya.da "to know" ×4 (Deu 4:39; Joe 2:14; Jon 3:9; Jon 4:2), za.khar "to remember" (Psa 106:45; Isa 46:8). The idolater's failure-to-turn is a **cognitive** failure (Isa 44:19 "no knowledge or discernment" — da.at/te.vu.nah). **T3.2.2** — Cognition supplies the *recognition* that grounds the turn (knowing God is gracious enables turning toward him, Jon 4:2). **T3.2.3** — The pattern shows the turning is **not raw feeling**: it is contrition *informed by recognition* — but cognition is the **enabling condition / co-partner**, not the seat (faculty tag on the focus terms is affect/volition, not cognition). ⚑ SYNTH.

**T3.3 Memory — T3.3.1** — **Yes — definitively, for shuv.** faculty=memory is tagged on all 7 shuv "return: recall" occurrences (Deu 4:39; Deu 30:1; 1Ki 8:47; 2Ch 6:37; Isa 44:19; Isa 46:8; Lam 3:21) — the turn-back is often a **"calling to mind"** (Lam 3:21 "this I call to mind, and therefore I have hope"; Isa 46:8 "recall it to mind"). **T3.3.2** — Memory operates as the *hinge* of the turn: re-membering God / the covenant / the law re-orients the person (Deu 30:1 "call them to mind among all the nations"). **T3.3.3** — ★ The pattern shows repentance has a **memory architecture** — the turn-back is structurally a *return-to-mind*. This is a distinct sub-finding: shuv welds **memory + volition** (the deliberate recollection that turns the will). Unique to the shuv band; absent from na.cham.

**T3.4 Affect — T3.4.1** — **Yes — definitively, for na.cham.** faculty=affect is tagged on **all 34** na.cham occurrences — the be-sorry/relent core is affective. Human contrition (Job 42:6; Jer 31:19) and divine relenting (Exo 32:14; Jon 3:10) both register as affect. **T3.4.2** — ★ The affect operates **differently by experiencer**: human na.cham = **contrition/regret** (sorrow over wrong — Job 42:6; the shame-cluster of Jer 31:19); divine na.cham = **relenting compassion** (the mercy that turns from wrath — Joe 2:13 che.sed/ra.chum; restraint of cha.ron "burning anger" ×2). **T3.4.3** — Universality of the affect tag on na.cham confirms affect is the **affective core** of this characteristic (paired with shuv's volition). The cluster's genuine inner-being characteristic is **affect-led on one term, volition-led on the other**.

**T3.5 Creativity — all:** **SILENT** — no creative/imaginative faculty engaged by turning.

**T3.6 Volition — T3.6.1** — **Yes — definitively, for shuv.** faculty=volition tagged on all 7 shuv occurrences (and on the M45 shuv "to return: repent" co-partners that pervade §B). The turn-back is a **volitional re-direction of the will** (Jer 26:3 "every one turn from his evil way"; Jon 3:10 "they turned from their evil way"). **T3.6.2** — Volition operates as **choice to reverse course**, and it is *commanded* (the imperative seam, T1.4.3) — a will summoned to turn. Session B #375 grounds this: "a volitional decision — 'from the heart'… commanded." **T3.6.3** — ★ The pattern shows the turning's volitional core lives on **shuv**, not na.cham — confirming the per-term faculty split (affect=na.cham, volition=shuv). The full characteristic is **contrition-that-decides** (affect + volition together).

**T3.7 Agency — T3.7.1** — **Inferential / partial.** The divine relenting is an act of **divine agency** (divine_involvement=agent 24 — God *acts* to spare: 2Sa 24:16 "stay your hand"). On the human side, agency is the *enactment* of the turn (mending ways, Jer 26:13). **T3.7.2 / T3.7.3** — Not tagged as an agency faculty per se (the action-type is affective/volitional, not agentive-initiation in the tagged sense). ★ Note the asymmetry: the *divine* side is strongly agentive (God the relenting actor); the *human* side is volitional-then-enacted. Largely **SILENT** as a discrete faculty tag.

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation — T3.8.1** — **Yes, operating through valence.** Though the focus terms tag affect/volition (not moral_evaluation directly), moral assessment runs *through* the turning: valence righteous=8 / commanded=2 / sinful=1 rate whether the turn is rightly directed (Job 42:6 righteous; Joe 2:13 commanded) or wrong-directed (Isa 44:19 / the lone sinful shuv — turning the wrong way). **T3.8.2** — Moral evaluation determines the *direction* of the turn (from evil = right; into evil = wrong). **T3.8.3** — ★ The turning is **morally evaluable by direction** — the same act is righteous (turn from evil) or sinful (turn to evil). The co-partners ra.sha "be wicked" / a.vah "to pervert" / cha.ta "to sin" (1Ki 8:47; 2Ch 6:37) supply the moral object the turn reverses.

**T3.9 Conscience — T3.9.1** — **Yes — at the human-contrition cases.** The turn engages the conscience (the inner witness of sin/conviction) where it pairs with shame and self-judgment: Jer 31:19 "after I had turned away, I relented… I was ashamed, and I was confounded" (na.cham + bosh/ka.lam — conviction); Job 42:6 "I despise myself, and repent." **T3.9.2** — Conscience operates as the **conviction that drives the turn** — the self-condemnation that issues in contrition. **T3.9.3** — ★ The pattern: conscience is engaged on the **human contrite** side (self-experiencer), *absent* from the divine relenting (God does not turn under conviction). A clean experiencer-discriminator. ⚑ SYNTH.

**T3.10 Conscientiousness — all:** **SILENT** — the integrated moral-awareness+volition+action composite is not tagged as such; its components (volition T3.6, conscience T3.9, moral-eval T3.8) are engaged separately but not as the integrated faculty.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity — T3.11.1** — **Yes — the turn is constitutively relational.** The turning is *toward God* (Joe 2:13 "Return to the LORD"; Deu 4:39) and re-opens the God-human bond (2Ch 6:37 turn-and-plead; Psa 106:45 covenant remembered). **T3.11.2** — It operates to **restore the relational connection** broken by sin — the turn re-engages the bond. **T3.11.3** — ★ The pattern shows the turning is **relationally directed** (its object is the relationship with God, not a private inner state) — the human turn and the divine relenting *meet* in the restored bond. ↔ BOND: this is where #4 meets #1/#2 (forgiveness) and #5 (reconciliation).

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1 God→Human — T4.1.1** — **Yes — the dominant interface.** divine_involvement=agent in 24/42: God relents *toward* the human — turning from the disaster he had planned against them (Exo 32:14; 2Sa 24:16; Jer 18:8; 26:13,19; 42:10; Jon 3:10; Amo 7:3,6). **T4.1.2** — ★ The basis is **responsive AND rooted in covenantal character**: responsive to the human turn ("if that nation turns from its evil, I will relent", Jer 18:8; "when God saw… how they turned… God relented", Jon 3:10) and grounded in his **standing disposition** ("gracious and merciful, slow to anger… and he relents over disaster", Joe 2:13; Jon 4:2; Psa 106:45 covenant + che.sed). Not arbitrary — conditioned on the turn and on covenant mercy. **T4.1.3** — God's relenting shows his disposition as **mercy that restrains just wrath** — willing to reverse judgment when the person turns (the obverse of his holiness).

**T4.2 Human→God — T4.2.1** — **Yes — the human turning IS the movement toward God.** The contrite turn is Godward: Joe 2:13 "Return to the LORD your God"; Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart that the LORD is God"; 2Ch 6:37 "turn their heart… and plead with you"; Job 42:6 (before God). **T4.2.2** — The inner posture required: **contrition + recognition** — a rent heart (Joe 2:13 "rend your hearts"), self-abasement (Job 42:6 "I despise myself… in dust and ashes"; Jer 31:19 shame), and knowing God is gracious (Jon 4:2). **T4.2.3** — ★ The human→God direction shows the relationship is **restorable by turning** — the person re-enters the bond through the contrite return. This is the positive pole of the God-interface (vs M06's enmity-pole).

**T4.3 Human→Giving — T4.3.1** — **SILENT / not the unit's interface.** The turning in §B operates **Godward (vertical)**, not person-to-person. No occurrence shows one person extending repentance *to another*. (Horizontal repair belongs to forgiveness — units #1/#2 — not to the turning itself.) **T4.3.2 / T4.3.3** — SILENT.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — ★ **Yes — transformation, by experiencer.** The human turn **changes both condition and orientation**: Jer 31:19 traces the full arc — "after I had turned away (shuv), I relented (na.cham), and after I was instructed… I was ashamed" — a change of the person's orientation to their wrong. The divine relenting changes the **person's condition** externally (the disaster is averted — 2Sa 24:16; Jon 3:10). **T5.1.2** — Reversibility: the human turn can be **reversed** (the lone sinful shuv = turning *back*; Jer 15:6 "you keep going backward") — repentance is not irreversibly fixed; the divine "I will not relent" (Eze 24:14) shows the *judgment* can become irreversible. ⚑ SYNTH: turning is reversible until a course is fixed.

**T5.2.1** — **Yes — a sequence is shown.** Jer 31:19 gives the clearest inner-state sequence: **turn-away → relent (contrition) → instruction → shame/confusion → bearing the disgrace** (a before→during→after of the contrite turn). The Solomon-temple register gives a corporate sequence: **sin → turn the heart → repent → plead → (divine relenting)** (1Ki 8:47 / 2Ch 6:37). ⚑ SYNTH (↔ #443): this is the inner-state sequence of the resolution movement, with the turning as its **entry-state**.

**T5.3.1** — Mechanism of change: **instruction / discipline and encounter**. Jer 31:19 "after I was instructed" — the turn comes *through correction* (discipline). Job 42:6 — the turn comes *through divine encounter* (after God's speech from the whirlwind). The divine relenting's mechanism: **seeing the human turn** (Jon 3:10 "God saw… how they turned… God relented"). **T5.3.2** — ★ The mechanism differs by context: human turn ← instruction/encounter/affliction; divine relenting ← witnessing the human turn (or remembering covenant, Psa 106:45).

**T5.4.1** — **Yes — affliction is a context/trigger of the turn.** The captivity setting drives the turn (1Ki 8:47 / 2Ch 6:37 "in the land to which they have been carried captive… repent"); the disaster threatened is what the turn responds to (Joe 2; Jer 26). Suffering is the **context that prompts** the turning. **T5.4.2** — ★ Affliction **occasions** the turn (drives the person to turn) but the evidence does not show suffering *deepening or refining* the characteristic itself — it is a trigger, not a refiner. Labelled: trigger-role grounded; refiner-role SILENT.

**T5.5.1** — **Largely SILENT / inferential.** No occurrence tags the turning as participation in a *longer sanctification arc*. Inferential: the "turn and live" register implies a re-set toward a formed life, and Jer 31:19's "instructed" implies ongoing formation — but no developmental-arc tag. Labelled inferential.

**T5.6.1** — **SILENT** — no eschatological-fullness orientation tagged (see T0.2.3). The turning is oriented to the *immediate* averting of disaster / restoration of the present bond, not a tagged future state.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms: **na.cham** (H5162H "to be sorry: relent", 34 — root sense *comfort/be-sorry → relent*); **shuv** (H7725N "to return: recall", 7 — root sense *return/turn-back*); **ud** (H5749A "to return", 1, thin outlier). Root meanings show the two poles: na.cham = the **affective relenting/contrition**; shuv = the **directional turn-back**. ⚖ BORDER: the NT terms **metanoeō/metanoia/metamellomai** carry this concept in the NT but are **owned elsewhere** (Reg 112/135) — M11 holds the **OT remnant**.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **verb throughout** (type=action 42/42) — na.cham predominantly **Niphal** (the relenting/being-sorry — Exo 32:14), occasionally **Hithpael** (Num 23:19; 1Sa 15:29 — "change his mind"); shuv as **Hiphil** (causative "bring-back / call-to-mind" — Deu 4:39; Isa 46:8) and **Qal** (simple turn — Jon 3:10). The verbal range shows the turning is **act, not status** — a faculty in operation (the §10 CHARACTERISTIC signal).

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: na.cham spans **divine relenting** (Exo 32:14; Jon 3:10) ↔ **human contrition/repentance** (Job 42:6; Jer 31:19) ↔ **regret over an action** (Gen 6:6 "regretted that he had made man") ↔ the **negated immutability** ("not… change his mind", Num 23:19). shuv spans **physical/cognitive return** ("call to mind", Lam 3:21) ↔ **moral turn** (1Ki 8:47 "turn their heart"). A breadth from a divine attribute to a private contrite act.

**T7.1.4** — ★ **Yes — the vocabulary marks distinct aspects.** It distinguishes the **affective** aspect (na.cham — relent/be-sorry) from the **volitional-directional** aspect (shuv — turn/return), and within na.cham the **divine-relenting** from the **human-contrition** sense (by experiencer, not by lemma). Disposition-vs-act is *not* lexically split here (both terms are act-verbs) — the split is **affect-pole vs volition-pole**.

**T7.1.5** — Structural-opposite term: **no dedicated antonym lexeme**; the opposite is expressed by **negation** ("I will not relent", Eze 24:14; "not… change his mind", 1Sa 15:29) and by the **wrong-direction turn** ("turns to his own course", Jer 8:6; the sinful shuv). The absence-of-turning is named by the *failure verbs* (Isa 44:19 "no one considers").

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **none in the focus set** (no nominal "the penitent / the one who turns" among na.cham/shuv/ud). The participial "transgressors" (Isa 46:8 pa.sha) names the *un*-turned, not the turner. Inferential: the wider shuv field generates *shovav* "backslider" (the un-turner) — outside-extract, labelled inferential.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: **none** — repentance is not "sought from another"; it is *enacted by the self* and *summoned by the prophet*. The plead-verb cha.nan (1Ki 8:47 "plead") accompanies the turn but is the appeal *to God*, not a seeking-of-repentance.

**T7.1.8** — ★ OT↔NT continuity: the OT carries the turning on **shuv (volitional turn)** and **na.cham (affective relenting/contrition)**; the NT concentrates it on **metanoeō (G3340) "change-of-mind/repent"** — a shift of emphasis from the *directional turn* (shuv) to the *mind-change* (metanoeō). ↔ BOND / ⚖ BORDER: metanoeō is **owned by Reg 112/135, not M11** (SD pointer 112-SD001 names the na.cham↔metanoeō overlap "at the intersection of cognitive reorientation and mind-change"). **The OT→NT arc must be reached for in synthesis** — M11 holds only the OT remnant (a coverage-migration parallel to M10's atonement).

**T7.1.9** — Newly-coined NT term: **metanoia/metanoeō** is the NT's concentrated repentance vocabulary, but it is **not in this M11 set** (owned elsewhere). Recorded here only to flag the migration; not analysed.

**T7.1.10** — The full vocabulary arc shows the turning operating across **a divine attribute (God relents), a human contrite act (repent in dust and ashes), a volitional moral turn (turn from evil), and a cognitive return (call to mind)** — the widest range being the na.cham double-seam (God relents / God does-not-relent) read per occurrence.

**T7.2.1** — Function of the primary term: na.cham is typically the **main verb of a divine speech or narration** ("the LORD relented", Exo 32:14; "I relent of the disaster", Jer 42:10) — with God as subject and the disaster/harm as the thing relented-from; on the human side it is the **main verb of a first-person confession** (Job 42:6; Jer 31:19). shuv governs the **imperative/conditional clause** of the call to turn (Joe 2:13; 1Ki 8:47).

**T7.2.2** — Literary form (by §B distribution): **prophecy dominant** (Jeremiah ~9, Joel, Jonah, Amos, Zechariah, Isaiah, Ezekiel) — the prophetic call-to-turn and the divine-relenting oracle; **narrative** (Gen 6; Exo 32; 1Sa 15; 2Sa 24; 1Ch 21; Jon); **wisdom/poetry** (Job 42; Psalms; Lamentations). Responsible interpretation must weight the **prophetic oracle** most — where the conditional turn↔relent structure is stated; the **narrative** cases (1Sa 15; Num 23) carry the immutability counter-seam.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure (representative): the prophetic argument runs **conditional** — *if the person/nation turns from evil (shuv), then God relents (na.cham) of the disaster* (Jer 18:8; 26:3; Jon 3:9–10); and the immutability counter-argument runs **categorical** — *God is not man, that he should change his mind* (Num 23:19; 1Sa 15:29). The two are reconciled per occurrence by the *kind* of relenting (relenting-from-judgment-on-the-turner vs not-lying-about-his-settled-purpose).

**T7.2.4** — Contextual settings: **covenantal** (Psa 106:45 covenant remembered; Joe 2 credal formula), **liturgical/penitential** (Joe 2:13 "rend your hearts"; the temple-prayer of 1Ki 8 / 2Ch 6), **judicial** (the disaster-pronounced-then-relented oracles, Jer 26), and **narrative-historical** (Gen 6; Exo 32; Jon). The settings show the turning as the **hinge of covenant judgment-and-mercy**.

**T7.2.5** — Primary anchor (authorial judgement): ★ **Joe 2:13–14** — "rend your hearts and not your garments; **Return** (shuv) to the LORD your God, for he is gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love; and he **relents** (na.cham) over disaster. Who knows whether he will not **turn and relent**…?" It holds **both poles in one place**: the human turn (shuv, heart-seated, "rend your hearts") AND the divine relenting (na.cham, rooted in the credal mercy formula), with the conditional hope structure between them.

**T7.2.6** — The anchor shows what no other §B verse shows together: the **bilateral characteristic in a single text** — the commanded human turn *and* the divine relenting *and* their ground (God's che.sed character) *and* the conditional hope ("who knows…") — the whole turning↔relenting structure in one breath.

**T7.3.1** — Human-science field (science extract §2): **clinical / moral psychology — regret psychology and behaviour-change research** (Zeelenberg & Pieters on regret; Prochaska & DiClemente's transtheoretical *stages of change*; Miller & Rollnick's motivational interviewing). The science explicitly names *shuv* as "the core cognitive-volitional structure of repentance as an about-face."

**T7.3.2** — Where science illuminates: the **stages-of-change** model coheres with the §B finding that repentance is a **process with constituents** (affect+volition+memory, T1.2.2) — not a binary event; and the science's tripartite seat (cognitive recognition + emotional remorse + volitional turn) **matches the lexical split** exactly: cognition (ya.da co-partner) + affect (na.cham) + volition (shuv). Regret-that-motivates-change matches the contrition→turn arc of Jer 31:19.

**T7.3.3** — Divergence: science treats repentance as a **purely horizontal/intrapersonal** process (regret over changeable actions, behaviour modification). The §B evidence is **constitutively vertical** — every human turn is *toward God* (Joe 2:13; Deu 4:39) and meets a **divine relenting** that science has no category for (the divine na.cham, agent=24, is wholly outside science's scope). The divine-relenting half of the bilateral characteristic is the deepest divergence.

**T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced by science: motivational interviewing's **ambivalence stage** (simultaneous desire-to-change and resistance) prompts the question whether the OT turning-vocabulary carries the *ambivalent / partial* turn or only the *completed* one. §B leans toward **completed/refused** poles (Job 42:6 completed; Jer 8:6 refused; the lone sinful shuv = relapse) — the **partial/ambivalent** turn is **not clearly tagged**. ⚑ SYNTH / ❓ CLARIFY: worth a closer pass on whether "you keep going backward" (Jer 15:6) and the relapse-shuv encode the ambivalence stage.
