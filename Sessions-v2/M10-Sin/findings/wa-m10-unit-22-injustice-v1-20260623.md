## A. General / non-verse observations (copied from the source digests)

**Definition (DB):** The inner state/disposition of doing wrong toward persons — unjust conduct/character, the moral faculty bent toward wrongdoing against others. Cross-register flag: **M26**.

**From the R2p injustice sub-ring (Ring 2 close, §1 — 77 occ):** the social-judicial **doing of wrong**, set against righteousness — *mishpat* (justice), *tsedeq* (righteousness), *tsaddiq* (the righteous), *yashar* (upright) **recur** as the contrast field.

**Done across the faculties / organs:**
- in **court** — "you shall do no injustice in **court**" (Lev 19:15; 19:35);
- in **speech** — "is there injustice on my **tongue**?" (Job 6:30); "my **lips** will not speak falsehood" (Job 27:4);
- in the **hands** — Psa 7:3.

**Heart-seated:** "in your **hearts** you devise wrongs" (Psa 58:2); "the inward mind and **heart**… are deep" (Psa 64:6); the fool's corrupt **heart** (Psa 53:1). These are the genuine inner-being seatings (among the 8 clean HEART-seatings of Ring 2).

**Ezekiel's apostasy refrain:** the righteous who **"turns from righteousness and does injustice"** dies — Eze 3:20; 18:24–26; 33:13–18. Injustice framed as **apostasy**, touching *shuv* (turn), *cha.ta* (sin), *ma'al* (breach of faith). This binds injustice to the sin-core and the turning/repentance dynamic.

**NT adikos (11 occ):** **all carry faculty=moral_evaluation**, contrasted with *dikaios* (the righteous). adikia/adikos = the moral category of the unrighteous person/conduct.

**Hebrew av.lah:** *al.vah*'s empty gloss is **resolved by reading** = "the unjust" (Hos 10:9, touching *cha.ta*) — note this resolution is logged at the lemma level in R2p.

**Structural signature (from R2p audit §2):** faculty populated chiefly on the **NT adik- moral_evaluation** tags (part of the 16/207 faculty-tagged); the Hebrew aspect-terms carry **faculty absent — heart engaged via co-occurrence** (Psa 58:2; 64:6 the cleanest inner-being evidence in Ring 2). type spans status (the unjust character) and action (doing injustice); object_type = person (the party wronged).
