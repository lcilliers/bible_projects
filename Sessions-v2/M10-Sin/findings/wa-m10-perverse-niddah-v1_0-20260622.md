## SENSE: "menstruation" (2)

### Lev 12:2 (OT) — "…If a woman conceives and bears a male child, then she shall be unclean seven days. As at the time of her menstruation, she shall be unclean."
**Terms (2, both focus):** *ta'me* (H2930A, M10c) · *nid'dah* (H5079, M10c)
- ***nid'dah* "menstruation"** — *mode:* Heb noun fem sing construct. *type:* status. *faculty:* — (not itself a faculty). *how:* governed by *ta'me* "be unclean" (H2930). *cause:* bearing a male child. *experiencer:* other (the woman). *valence:* **neutral**. *Silent:* location, origin, object, divine, intensity, immediate_response, relational.
- ***ta'me* "unclean"** — *mode:* Heb verb Qal 3fs (an action/becoming). *type:* action. *cause:* bearing a male child. *object_type:* person (the woman; `object`="days" is the duration, a mechanical mis-grab). *experiencer:* other. *valence:* neutral. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, immediate_response.
- **Synergy:** *nid'dah* (the impurity-status) and *ta'me* (the becoming-unclean) are **partners** naming one event: childbirth places the woman in an impurity-status *as at* menstruation, for a fixed term (seven days).
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity here is a **bodily status with a temporal boundary**, arising from a natural bodily event (childbirth/menstruation), borne by the person, **morally neutral** — not a fault but a condition. No inner-seat is named; it attaches to the person as a whole.
- **Flags:** `object`="days" is a mechanical mis-grab (duration, not object) — `object_type`=person is authoritative. valence=neutral is correct (bodily, not moral).

### Lev 12:5 (OT) — "But if she bears a female child, then she shall be unclean two weeks, as in her menstruation. And she shall continue in the blood of her purifying for sixty-six days."
**Terms (4):** *ta'me* (focus) · *nid'dah* (focus) · *to'har* ×2 "purity/clearness" (H2892A/B, M12, co)
- ***nid'dah* "menstruation"** — status; *how:* continue (H3427, *yashav* — to remain/dwell); *object_type:* situation (the "blood"); *experiencer:* other; *valence:* neutral. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, intensity.
- ***ta'me* "unclean"** — action (Qal 3fs); *object_type:* person; *valence:* neutral. *Silent:* the rest.
- ***to'har* "purifying" (×2)** — status; *how:* continue (H3427); *object_type:* situation; *valence:* neutral. The **purity/cleansing pole** present in the verse.
- **Synergy:** *nid'dah*/*ta'me* (impurity) are set directly against *to'har* (purifying) — the verse holds **both poles**: the impure period and the **blood of purifying** that follows, with fixed durations (two weeks, sixty-six days).
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity is shown as **one phase of a bounded cycle** — impure status → a measured term → a "purifying" phase. The presence of *to'har* makes the **clean↔impure→cleansed lifecycle** explicit in a single verse; the state is bodily, durational, neutral.
- **Flags:** valence neutral correct; *to'har* appears twice (H2892A "purity" + H2892B "clearness") — one purifying referent rendered by two lemma-senses (note, not error).

## SENSE: "impurity" (4)

### Lev 15:19 (OT) — "When a woman has a discharge, and the discharge in her body is blood, she shall be in her menstrual impurity for seven days, and whoever touches her shall be unclean until the evening."
**Terms (2, both focus):** *ta'me* · *nid'dah*
- ***nid'dah* "impurity"** — status; *location:* **flesh** (genuine bodily seat — the discharge is "in her body"); *how:* be (H1961); *cause:* blood discharge in her body; *experiencer:* other; *intensity:* "all (touches)"; *valence:* neutral. *Silent:* faculty, origin, divine, relational.
- ***ta'me* "unclean"** — action; *location:* flesh; *cause:* **touching a woman in her menstrual impurity**; *object_type:* person (`object`="evening" = duration mis-grab); *intensity:* all (touches); *relational:* until (evening); *valence:* neutral.
- **Synergy:** the impurity (*nid'dah*) is **seated in the flesh** by the bodily discharge; *ta'me* then **transmits** it — *whoever touches* her becomes unclean till evening. Partner pair = the state + its contagion.
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity here is **bodily-seated (flesh)** and, crucially, **transmissible by contact** — it spreads from the person to those who touch, for a bounded time. It is a condition with **reach beyond the bearer**, yet morally neutral.
- **Flags:** `object`="evening" duration mis-grab (object_type=person authoritative); location=flesh is a real bodily seat (not the nephesh artefact).

### Lev 15:26 (OT) — "Every bed on which she lies… shall be to her as the bed of her impurity. And everything on which she sits shall be unclean, as in the uncleanness of her menstrual impurity."
**Terms (2, both focus):** *nid'dah* "impurity" · *ta'me* "unclean" (H2931, adjective)
- ***nid'dah* "impurity"** — status; *how:* sits (H3427); *object_type:* situation (`object`="uncleanness"); *intensity:* "all (everything)"; *experiencer:* other; *valence:* neutral. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine.
- ***ta'me* "unclean"** (H2931, *quality* — adjective, distinct from the H2930 verb) — quality; *how:* sits (H3427); *intensity:* all; *valence:* neutral.
- **Synergy:** the impurity-status (*nid'dah*) **extends to objects** — bed, seat — which become *ta'me* (unclean-quality). The contagion of Lev 15:19 is here shown reaching **things**, not only persons.
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity **radiates onto the inanimate** (what she lies/sits on) — the state is environmental as well as personal; still neutral, still bodily in origin.
- **Flags:** note the **two ta'me lemmas** — H2930A (verb, to-become-unclean) vs **H2931 (adjective, unclean-quality)**; the corpus carries both, and they do different work (becoming vs being). Record for the lemma index.

### Lev 20:21 (OT) — "If a man takes his brother's wife, it is impurity. He has uncovered his brother's nakedness; they shall be childless."
**Terms (1, focus):** *nid'dah* "impurity"
- ***nid'dah* "impurity"** — status; *how:* uncovered (H1540, *galah*); *object_type:* situation (`object`="brother's"); *experiencer:* other; *valence:* **sinful**. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, intensity, immediate_response, relational.
- **Synergy:** no inner-being co-term — *nid'dah* stands alone as the **verdict on an act**: the forbidden union "is impurity."
- **Inner-being reality:** here impurity is **not a bodily state but a moral verdict on a forbidden act** — the same word that named neutral menstrual impurity now names a transgression. The bodily category is **applied morally**; valence flips neutral→sinful.
- **Flags:** **valence flip is genuine evidence** (neutral as body, sinful as act) — the extract distinguishes correctly here, unlike its over-tagging elsewhere. The status/characteristic question sits exactly on this flip — recorded, not resolved.

### Num 19:21 (OT) — "…The one who sprinkles the water for impurity shall wash his clothes, and the one who touches the water for impurity shall be unclean until evening."
**Terms (2, both focus):** *nid'dah* "impurity" · *ta'me* "unclean"
- ***nid'dah* "impurity"** — status; *how:* **wash** (H3526, *kabas* — to launder); *object_type:* abstract (`object`="clothes"); *experiencer:* other. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, intensity, valence.
- ***ta'me* "unclean"** — action; *object_type:* person (`object`="evening" mis-grab); *relational:* until. *Silent:* most.
- **Synergy:** here *nid'dah* is the **"water for impurity"** — the purification-water itself; paradoxically the cleansing agent is named by the impurity word, and even its handler becomes *ta'me* till evening. Impurity and its remedy are bound in one rite.
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity is embedded in a **cleansing apparatus** (washing, sprinkling) — the state is the kind of thing that has a **prescribed removal**, yet the removal-process itself transmits a lesser impurity. The lifecycle includes a **ritual remedy**.
- **Flags:** `object` mis-grabs ("clothes"/"evening"); valence silent (the rite is procedural).

- **Flags:** three impurity-lemmas in one verse (*nid'dah*/*ta'me*-H2931/*tum'ah*) — the corpus's impurity vocabulary co-densifies; relate at the lemma index.

### Eze 18:6 (OT) — "…does not defile his neighbor's wife or approach a woman in her time of menstrual impurity,"
**Terms (2, both focus):** *ta'me* "defile" (H2930A, Piel) · *nid'dah* "menstrual impurity"
- ***ta'me* "defile"** — action (**Piel** — causative/transitive: to *defile* another); *object_type:* person (the neighbour's wife); *experiencer:* other; *immediate_response:* approach woman; *valence:* sinful. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, cause, divine, intensity, relational.
- ***nid'dah* "menstrual impurity"** — status; *how:* approach (H7126, *qarav*); *object_type:* abstract; *valence:* sinful. *Silent:* the rest.
- **Synergy:** in the **righteous-man catalogue**, *ta'me* (Piel — to *defile*) the neighbour's wife stands beside not approaching a woman in *nid'dah* — two avoided defilements, one moral (adultery), one ritual.
- **Inner-being reality:** here the **Piel of ta'me** shows impurity as something **one person actively inflicts on another** ("defile his neighbour's wife") — impurity as a **transitive moral act**, set in a list defining the righteous. The bodily *nid'dah* sits alongside as the ritual boundary. The conduct, not a seat, is in view.
- **Flags:** Piel stem is the key measure (causative "defile" vs Qal "become unclean") — record at the lemma index; valence sinful (moral catalogue).

- **Flags:** valence sinful (moral); the simile (*relational*="like") is the load-bearing structure — the bodily term is doing **figurative** work here.

## SENSE: "filthy thing" (1)

### Lam 1:17 (OT) — "…Jerusalem has become a filthy thing among them."
**Terms (2):** *nid'dah* "filthy thing" (focus) · *na'cham* "comfort" (H5162G, M05, co)
- ***nid'dah* "filthy thing"** — status; *experiencer:* other; *valence:* sinful. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, how, object, cause, divine, intensity, relational.
- ***na'cham* "comfort"** (co) — action (Piel); *faculty:* **affect**; *object_type:* God/Lord; *divine_involvement:* object; *valence:* neutral.
- **Synergy:** Jerusalem has *become nid'dah* — an unclean/outcast thing — and (partner clause) "there is none to **comfort** (*na'cham*)" her. The impurity-status and the absence of comfort sit together: to be *nid'dah* is to be shunned, uncomforted.
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity as **identity and social exile** — the city *is* a "filthy thing," an object of avoidance; the term carries the **menstruant's set-apartness** transposed to a people's disgrace and isolation. The affective co-term (*na'cham*, comfort, withheld) shows the **relational consequence** of the impure status: shunning.
- **Flags:** valence sinful; *na'cham* contributes the only faculty (affect) in the verse — the impurity term itself names no faculty (status), the inner-being colour comes from the co-term.

## Lemma-level note (evidence only; held open)
Across its 16 verses *nid'dah* presents in two registers that the corpus itself links: a **bodily impurity** (menstruation/childbirth/discharge — *valence neutral*, seated in **flesh**, transmissible by contact to persons and things, of bounded duration, with a purifying counter-pole *to'har*) and a **moral/figurative defilement** (forbidden unions; the land/conduct defiled "*like* a menstruant"; Jerusalem "become a *nid'dah*"; idolatrous "filth" carried out of the temple — *valence sinful*). The **valence flip** (neutral↔sinful) tracks the register, and the corpus marks the bridge explicitly with the simile of Eze 36:17. *ta'me* appears in **two lemmas** here — H2930 (verb: become/defile, incl. the Piel "defile another") and H2931 (adjective: unclean-quality). Whether *nid'dah* is a "status/condition" or a "characteristic with its own lifecycle" is **not adjudicated here** — both registers are recorded for that later debate.
