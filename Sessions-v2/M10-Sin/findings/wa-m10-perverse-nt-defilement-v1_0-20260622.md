## *molunō* (G3435) "to defile, soil, stain, make impure" (3)

### 1Cor 8:7 (NT) — "…their conscience, being weak, is defiled."
**Terms (5):** ***molunō* "defiled"** (F) · *suneidesis* "conscience" (G4893, M47, co) · *eidōlon* "idol" (M27, co) · *eidōlothutos* "idol-food" (M27, co) · *asthenēs* "weak" (M24, co)
- ***molunō* "defiled"** — *mode:* Gk verb pres-pass-ind 3s (an action undergone). *type:* action. *location:* **conscience**. *experiencer:* other. *valence:* sinful. *Silent:* faculty, origin, object, cause, divine, intensity, immediate_response, relational.
- ***suneidesis* "conscience"** (the seat) — *type:* status; *faculty:* **moral_evaluation + conscience**; *location:* conscience; *how:* defiled (G3435). The verse's seat-and-faculty bearer.
- *eidōlon* / *eidōlothutos* — the idol / idol-food whose eating is the occasion; *asthenēs* "weak" qualifies the conscience (faculty=affect). (Their `location`=conscience is engine propagation from the co-seated *suneidesis*, not their own seat.)
- **Synergy:** the **conscience** (*suneidesis*, co-seated) is the thing **defiled** (*molunō*); the cause is eating idol-food (*eidōlothutos*/*eidōlon*) while the conscience is **weak** (*asthenēs*). Defilement acts *on a faculty*.
- **Inner-being reality:** defilement here **reaches a named inner faculty — the conscience** — and is conditioned by its **weakness**: a weak conscience is defiled by an act it cannot bear. Defilement is not only bodily/ritual but an **injury to the moral-evaluative faculty**.
- **Flags:** co-terms' `location`=conscience is propagated from *suneidesis* (attribute the seat to *suneidesis*); *suneidesis* valence "neutral" (the faculty itself), *molunō* "sinful" (the act).

### Rev 3:4 (NT) — "…people who have not soiled their garments…"
**Terms (2):** ***molunō* "soiled"** (F) · *axios* "worthy" (M22, co)
- ***molunō* "soiled"** — action (aor-act-ind 3p); *object_type:* thing (garments); *cause_clause:* "are worthy"; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* walk; *relational:* not / their. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, intensity.
- *axios* "worthy" — quality; *cause:* not soiling their garments; *valence:* righteous.
- **Synergy:** **not** soiling (*molunō*) one's **garments** ↔ being **worthy** (*axios*) and walking "in white." Garments figure conduct/purity of life.
- **Inner-being reality:** defilement as a **figurative staining of one's manner of life** (garments) — its absence is worthiness; here defilement is moral-conduct, externalised as cloth.
- **Flags:** object=garments is figurative (conduct); valence sinful (the staining), righteous (the worthiness).

### Rev 14:4 (NT) — "It is these who have not defiled themselves with women, for they are virgins…"
**Terms (1, F):** ***molunō* "defiled themselves"**
- ***molunō*** — action (aor-pass-ind 3p, reflexive); *object_type:* person (women); *cause:* relations with women; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* are virgins; *relational:* not / with. *Silent:* faculty, location, origin, divine, intensity.
- **Synergy:** no inner co-term — *molunō* stands as the **sexual defilement** the 144,000 have avoided ("virgins").
- **Inner-being reality:** defilement as **sexual self-defilement** — contracted through relations; its avoidance marks consecration. Bodily-relational, morally framed.
- **Flags:** none beyond the standard.

## *molusmos* (G3436) "contamination, defilement" (1)

### 2Cor 7:1 (NT) — "…let us cleanse ourselves from every defilement of body and spirit, bringing holiness to completion in the fear of God."
**Terms (5):** ***molusmos* "defilement"** (F) · *katharizō* "cleanse" (M12, co) · *hagiōsunē* "holiness" (M22, co) · *fobos* "fear" (M01, co) · *sarx* "flesh/body" (M47, co-seated)
- ***molusmos* "defilement"** — *type:* status; *origin:* **received-from-outside**; *how:* cleanse (G2511, *katharizō*); *experiencer:* **self**; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* (holiness to) completion. *co-seated:* *sarx* (flesh/body). *Silent:* faculty, location(beyond the sarx-seat), object, cause, divine, intensity.
- *katharizō* "cleanse" — action; *origin:* received-from-outside; *experiencer:* self; *valence:* commanded — the removal act.
- *hagiōsunē* "holiness" — status; *valence:* commanded — the goal.
- *fobos* "fear" — status; *faculty:* affect + perception; *divine:* object ("fear of God") — the frame in which cleansing is done.
- **Synergy:** **defilement (*molusmos*) of body and spirit** is the thing **cleansed** (*katharizō*) **by the self**, so as to **complete holiness** (*hagiōsunē*) "in the **fear** (*fobos*) of God." Defilement, cleansing, holiness, and fear are bound in one movement.
- **Inner-being reality:** defilement here explicitly **reaches "body and spirit"** — both the *sarx* (co-seated) and the spirit — and is **self-cleansed**, with holiness as the completed goal under the fear of God. This is the **widest seating** in the family: defilement contaminates the whole person, flesh and spirit, and is removable by a self-directed cleansing.
- **Flags:** "of body and spirit" is the load-bearing phrase (sarx co-seated; spirit named in text though not separately tagged); origin=received-from-outside is a genuine derivation.

## *miasmos* (G3393) "corruption, pollution, defilement" (1)

### 2Pe 2:10 (NT) — "…those who indulge in the lust of defiling passion and despise authority…"
**Terms (5):** ***miasmos* "defiling passion"** (F) · *epithumia* "desire/lust" (M28, co) · *tolmētēs* "bold" (M08, co) · *authadēs* "self-willed" (M08, co) · *kuriotēs* "authority" (M23, co)
- ***miasmos* "defiling passion"** — *type:* status; *location:* **flesh**; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* despise authority. *Silent:* faculty, origin, how, object, cause, divine, intensity, relational.
- *epithumia* "lust" — status; *faculty:* **affect**; *location:* flesh; *valence:* sinful — the desire *miasmos* qualifies ("lust of defiling passion").
- *tolmētēs* / *authadēs* — bold / self-willed (faculty volition); *kuriotēs* — the authority despised.
- **Synergy:** *miasmos* (defiling) qualifies *epithumia* (lust) — "the lust of **defiling** passion" — **seated in the flesh**, issuing in **despising authority** (immediate_response) and self-will (*authadēs*).
- **Inner-being reality:** defilement here is a **quality of fleshly desire** — a polluting passion of the *sarx* that drives contempt for authority. Defilement and **disordered desire** fuse: the passion is itself "defiling."
- **Flags:** location=flesh shared across co-terms (the verse's seat); *miasmos* names no faculty (status) — affect comes from *epithumia*.

## *akatharsia* (G167) "impurity — moral filthiness, esp. sexual" (10)

*Across all ten, type = status, the term names no faculty of its own (verse faculty/seat comes from co-terms), and it stands in the works-of-the-flesh / vice-lists opposed to holiness and righteousness. Per-verse:*

### Rom 1:24 (NT) — "…God gave them up in the lusts of their hearts to impurity…"
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *location:* **heart**; *valence:* sinful; *relational:* to. *co-seated:* *kardia* (heart). Co: *epithumia* "lusts" (faculty affect, location heart), *atimazō* "dishonour" (their bodies), *kardia* "heart" (faculty affect+cognition+perception+conscience).
- **Synergy:** the **lusts of the heart** (*epithumia*, co-seated in *kardia*) issue **to impurity** (*akatharsia*) and the dishonouring of bodies. Impurity is the terminus of the **heart's** disordered desire.
- **Inner-being reality:** impurity is **seated in / proceeds from the heart's lusts** — a heart-rooted moral filthiness, expressed in bodily dishonour. The most explicitly **heart-located** impurity in the family.

### Gal 5:19 (NT) — "Now the works of the flesh are evident: sexual immorality, impurity, sensuality,"
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *location:* **flesh**; *object_type:* abstract; *valence:* sinful. *co-seated:* *sarx*. Co: *aselgeia* "sensuality."
- **Synergy / reality:** impurity listed among the **works of the flesh** (*sarx* co-seated) — here impurity is **flesh-seated**, a product of the flesh set against the Spirit (context). Bodily-moral.

### Rom 6:19 (NT) — "…you once presented your members as slaves to impurity and to lawlessness… now present your members as slaves to righteousness…"
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *location:* flesh; *how:* presented (G3936); *object_type:* abstract (members); *valence:* sinful. *co-seated:* *sarx*. Co: *anomia* "lawlessness", *dikaiosunē* "righteousness", *hagiasmos* "holiness/sanctification", *doulos* "slave."
- **Synergy / reality:** the **members** (bodily faculties) are **presented as slaves** either to impurity+lawlessness or to righteousness→sanctification. Impurity is a **master one's members serve** — a servitude of the body's capacities, opposed to righteousness/holiness. Defilement framed as **enslaving allegiance**.

### Col 3:5 (NT) — "Put to death therefore what is earthly in you: sexual immorality, impurity, passion, evil desire, and covetousness, which is idolatry."
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* "is idolatry." Co: *epithumia* "desire", *pleonexia* "covetousness"(=idolatry), *eidōlolatreia* "idolatry", *nekroō* "put to death."
- **Synergy / reality:** impurity is among the "earthly" things to be **put to death** (*nekroō*) — listed with disordered desire and covetousness-as-idolatry. Defilement is a thing **mortified**, treated as the old self to be killed. (The remedy here is death, not washing.)

### 2Cor 12:21 (NT) — "…many of those who sinned earlier and have not repented of the impurity, sexual immorality, and sensuality that they have practiced."
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *valence:* sinful; *immediate_response:* practiced; *relational:* of. Co: *metanoeo* "repent" (object_type abstract = impurity), *pentheo* "mourn", *aselgeia* "sensuality."
- **Synergy / reality:** impurity is the object of **(un)repentance** (*metanoeo*) and apostolic **mourning** (*pentheo*) — a practiced filthiness to be **repented of**. Defilement here has a **remedy of repentance**, and its persistence grieves.

### Eph 4:19 (NT) — "They have become callous and have given themselves up to sensuality, greedy to practice every kind of impurity."
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *valence:* sinful. Co: *apalgeō* "become callous" (lose all sensitivity), *aselgeia* "sensuality", *pleonexia* "greed."
- **Synergy / reality:** impurity is practiced by those who have **become callous** (*apalgeō* — lost moral sensation) and **given themselves up** to sensuality. Defilement follows a **deadening of moral feeling** — a hardened-into-it dynamic.

### Eph 5:3 (NT) — "But sexual immorality and all impurity or covetousness must not even be named among you, as is proper among saints."
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *how:* named (G3687); *valence:* **forbidden**; *immediate_response:* proper. Co: *pleonexia* "covetousness."
- **Synergy / reality:** impurity is **not even to be named** among saints — a defilement excluded from the community's very speech; set against what is "proper." Defilement as **incompatible with holiness/saintly identity**.

### 1Th 4:7 (NT) — "For God has not called us for impurity, but in holiness."
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *how:* called (G2564); *valence:* **forbidden**; *relational:* for. Co: *kaleō* "call" (divine agent), *hagiasmos* "holiness."
- **Synergy / reality:** the **divine call** (*kaleō*, God agent) is **not to impurity but to holiness** (*hagiasmos*). Impurity is the **antithesis of the calling**; holiness its replacement.

### 1Th 2:3 (NT) — "For our appeal does not spring from error or impurity or any attempt to deceive,"
- ***akatharsia* "impurity"** — status; *valence:* sinful. Co: *dolos* "deceit", *paraklēsis* "appeal/encouragement."
- **Synergy / reality:** the apostolic **appeal** (*paraklēsis*) is **not from impurity** (nor error nor deceit) — impurity as a possible **corrupt motive** disavowed. Defilement located in the **motive/source** of speech.

### Mat 23:27 (NT) — "…you are like whitewashed tombs, which outwardly appear beautiful, but within are full of dead people's bones and all uncleanness."
- ***akatharsia* "uncleanness"** — status; *valence:* sinful. Co: *hupokritēs* "hypocrite" (faculty perception).
- **Synergy / reality:** uncleanness fills the **inside** ("within") behind a beautiful **outside** — the hypocrite's **inner/outer split**. Defilement is the hidden **interior content** masked by external show. (Echoes the OT "abomination within / appearance without.")

## Lemma-family note (evidence only; held open)
The NT defilement family is **moral impurity**, and — unlike the OT bodily *nid'dah* — it is repeatedly **seated in named inner realities**: the **conscience** (*molunō*, 1 Cor 8:7), **body *and* spirit** (*molusmos*, 2 Cor 7:1), the **flesh** (*akatharsia* Gal 5:19, Rom 6:19; *miasmos* 2 Pe 2:10), and the **heart's lusts** (*akatharsia* Rom 1:24); and once as the **hidden interior** behind a clean exterior (Mat 23:27). It is consistently the **antithesis of holiness/righteousness** (*hagiasmos*, *hagiōsunē*, *dikaiosunē*), bound to **disordered desire** (*epithumia*), and addressed by a range of **remedies** — cleansing (*katharizō*), putting to death (*nekroō*), repentance (*metanoeo*), exclusion ("not named"). *valence* runs sinful/forbidden, with holiness/righteousness as the righteous/commanded counter-pole. Whether this is "the status/expression of sin" or "a characteristic (defilement) with its own lifecycle (contract → seat → spread/deaden → cleanse/mortify/repent)" is **not adjudicated** — both are recorded.
