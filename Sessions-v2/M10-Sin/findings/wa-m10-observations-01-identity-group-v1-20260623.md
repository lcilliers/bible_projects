## Observations (each grounded)

**O1 — The identity object is a CLASSIFICATION, not a faculty in operation.**
- *Evidence:* #23 `type=status 258/258`, `faculty=moral_evaluation 258/258` — uniform and term-intrinsic; the file's own words: "a classification, not a movement" (A1). #16 `type=quality` throughout; faculty term-intrinsic (HM `volition` / HA `moral_evaluation`), explicitly "not a verse-level reading" (§sig).
- *So:* the object names **what a person IS** (a moral category), not an inner motion. The faculty tag is the word's classifying function, not a seated inner state.

**O2 — It describes the inner being CONSTITUTIVELY but the interior is THIN / BOUNDED.**
- *Evidence:* #23 — genuine wicked-heart seats are **~5** (Psa 10:13; 28:3; Pro 10:20; 15:28; 21:4); the +69-verse recovery added **almost none** to the interior (nephesh ×1 ="life"), "confirming the interior is bounded… a real distribution, not a gap" (A4). nephesh co-seats (14) are mostly "life/person", not the wicked's seat. #16 — object_type person/none; no interior seat reported.
- *So:* the object **declares** a moral core but rarely **opens** the interior. The identity is carried as a **verdict label**; the interior content is concentrated in a few Psalms/Proverbs texts, not pervasive.

**O3 — It is DEFINED BY CONTRAST (a relational pole-pair), not standalone.**
- *Evidence:* #23 — "defined by what it is *not*"; ra'sha pairs with tsaddiq in nearly every Wisdom/Histories verse (Gen 18:23,25; Deu 25:1; 1 Ki 8:32; Psa 1; Psa 37; Mal 3:18); **81 shared verses with M26** (A3). #16 — sinner-vs-righteous is the defining frame (Psa 1; Mat 9:13 "not the righteous but sinners"; Luk 18:13 the sinner vs the self-righteous).
- *So:* the identity is **read off the righteous↔wicked / righteous↔sinner pole-pair** — it needs its opposite to exist. The opposite (righteousness) sits in **M26, external to M10**.

**O4 — It is FORENSIC at root — a court-verdict that hardens into character.**
- *Evidence:* #23 face 1 "forensic verdict": "condemning the guilty / vindicating the righteous" (Deu 25:1–2; 1 Ki 8:32; 2 Ch 6:23), "the man in the wrong" (Exo 2:13), Pharaoh "I and my people are in the wrong" (Exo 9:27). #16 "a verdict on a person" (Luk 5:8; 7:39; 19:7).
- *So:* the object originates as a **judicial verdict** (the guilty party) and extends into a **standing character-type** ("the wicked", "the sinner"). A verdict-become-identity.

**O5 — It is REVERSIBLE — the verdict can be exited (a tension inside the object).**
- *Evidence:* #23 face 4 "turn and live": the wicked is addressable/warnable/reversible — **14 of 44** prophetic occurrences carry the turn/live/pardon field (Eze 18/33; Isa 55:7) (A2). This stands in stated tension with face 3 (the fixed two-destinies).
- *So:* the identity is a **status with an exit**, not an absolute essence — it links to repentance (M11). The object holds an unresolved internal tension: fixed-destiny vs reversible.

**O6 — It is DIVINE-FACING — partly constituted by God's posture toward it.**
- *Evidence:* #23 A5 — God **tests** the wicked's interior (Psa 7:9), **hates** (Psa 11:5, God's nephesh), holds it an **abomination** (Pro 15:8,9), **judges** it (Psa 1:4,6; 37), and **calls it to turn**. 
- *So:* the object is defined not only from the human side but by **God's relation to it** — a relational/divine-facing identity, not a neutral trait.

**O7 — The two identity-units (#16 sinner / #23 wicked) are tightly bound — possibly one object.**
- *Evidence:* Gen 13:13 fuses them ("wicked, great **sinners**"); Psa 1 sets the sinner alongside the wicked and the scoffer; both are the "person-defined-by-sin" (#16 BORDER ↔ #23). The distinction offered: chronic-**doer** ("sinner", chat.ta/hamartōlos) vs settled-**character** ("wicked", ra'sha).
- *So:* ⚖ BORDER (a §10 consolidation call): one identity-object with two intensities (doer→character), or two distinct verdicts. The evidence binds them but also distinguishes degree.

## Candidate label (held open)
**"Moral verdict-identity" / inner-being person-type** — a **classification of the whole person** (a verdict become a standing character), **defined contrastively** against its opposite, **forensic at root**, **reversible**, and **divine-facing**. It describes the inner being by **declaring its moral core**, not by opening its interior.

## §G — Bias-guard (where the definition out-runs the evidence)
- **⚠ The DB definition of #23 claims a rich corrupt interior** — "will corrupted, God actively excluded from the mind (practical atheism), inner orientation persistently bent toward harm, conscience suppressed or displaced." **The evidence supports this only in a BOUNDED set** (~5 heart-seats + the Psa 10 "no God" / Psa 36:1 texts) — it is **concentrated, not pervasive** (O2). *Do not let the definition's rich portrait be read as the evidential norm.* The interior-rich reading is itself one face (face 2), bounded to specific Psalms/Proverbs.
- **⚠ "Practical atheism / will corrupted" is a strong interpretive frame** resting largely on Psa 10:4 ("all his thoughts are 'there is no God'") and Psa 36:1 — powerful but **few texts**. Grounded, but narrow; flag before generalising.
- **The forensic→character→reversible span (O4/O5)** is genuinely in the evidence (cited verses) — but whether it is *one* object holding a tension, or several, is **not** decided by the evidence; it is a §10 call. Recorded as tension, not resolved.
- Cross-language (chat.ta / hamartōlos) parallels are **recorded side by side, not fused** (per the evidence-first rule).
