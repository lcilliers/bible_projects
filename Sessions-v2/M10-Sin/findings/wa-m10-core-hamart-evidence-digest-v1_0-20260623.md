## 2. Diversity observed (faces — HELD OPEN, not conclusions)

**(a) Sin as power / dominion / enslaving force (the distinctly Pauline register — Romans 5–8).** This is the strongest single behaviour of the noun, and it is concentrated in Romans. Sin is an **agent/sovereign**: it "came into the world" and "reigned" (Rom 5:12,21), "increased" (5:20), is told **not to "reign in your mortal body"** (Rom 6:12) and has/has-no **"dominion"** (Rom 6:14, *kurieuō* "to lord over"); persons are **"slaves of sin" / "enslaved to sin" / "sold under sin" / "set free from sin"** (Rom 6:6,16,17,18,20,22; 7:14, with *douleuō/doulos/douloō* M36 partners); sin **"dwells within me"** (Rom 7:17,20,23,25), **"seized an opportunity," "deceived," "killed"** (Rom 7:8,11), the **"law of sin"** wages war (Rom 7:23,25; 8:2). Joh 8:34 carries the same image: "everyone who practices sin is a **slave to sin**." 1Cor 15:56 "the **power** of sin is the law" (*dunamis* partner). This face accounts for most of the 154 `object_type=none` (sin as absolute personified noun). (object_type/abstract here = "world/death/grace/righteousness" relations, not a governed wrong.)

**(b) hamartōlos — the sinner-type / moral-category person (the Gospels' "sinners").** The adjective-substantive (45 occ, type=`quality`) names a class of person: **"tax collectors and sinners"** (Mat 9:10,11; Mar 2:15,16; Luk 5:30; 7:34; 15:1,2), **sinner vs righteous** (Mat 9:13; Mar 2:17; Luk 5:32; 15:7; 18:13 "God, be merciful to me, a sinner"), **"a sinful man" / "a sinner"** as a verdict on a person (Luk 5:8; 7:37,39; 19:7; Joh 9:16,24,25,31), and the Pauline/general moral category ("while we were still **sinners**" Rom 5:8; "made **sinners**" Rom 5:19; "Gentile **sinners**" Gal 2:15; "to save **sinners**" 1Ti 1:15; "separated from **sinners**" Heb 7:26; "ungodly **sinners**" Jude 15). The "sinful generation" (Mar 8:38) and "sinful men/flesh" (Luk 24:7; Rom 8:3) sit here too.

**(c) Sin as act / committed (the verb hamartanō, and hamartēma).** The act of sinning: "if your brother **sins** against you" (Mat 18:15,21; Luk 17:3,4), "I have **sinned**" (Mat 27:4; Luk 15:18,21), "who **sinned**, this man or his parents?" (Joh 9:2,3), "all have **sinned**" (Rom 3:23; 5:12), "every other **sin** a person commits" + hamartēma (1Cor 6:18), "if we go on **sinning** deliberately" (Heb 10:26), the 1 John habitual-sinning set (1Jo 1:10; 2:1; 3:6,8,9; 5:16,18), angels "when they **sinned**" (2Pe 2:4). hamartēma (the 4 occ): Mar 3:28,29 (the unforgivable/eternal sin); Rom 3:25 (former sins passed over); 1Cor 6:18.

**(d) Sin and forgiveness / bearing / cleansing.** A very large behaviour: sin is **forgiven** (Mat 9:2,5,6; Mar 2:5,7,9,10; Luk 5:20,23,24; 7:47,48,49; 11:4; Joh 20:23 — the paralytic/forgiveness cluster, *afiēmi* M11 partner), **"forgiveness of sins"** proclaimed (Mat 26:28; Mar 1:4; Luk 1:77; 3:3; 24:47; Act 2:38; 5:31; 10:43; 13:38; 26:18; Col 1:14), **blotted out / washed away** (Act 3:19; 22:16), **covered / not counted** (Rom 4:7,8), **borne / died for** ("the Lamb of God who **takes away** the sin of the world" Joh 1:29; "Christ died **for our sins**" 1Cor 15:3; "gave himself **for our sins**" Gal 1:4; "**propitiation** … passed over former sins" Rom 3:25; "made him to be **sin** who knew no sin" 2Cor 5:21), **purification / sacrifice for sins** (Heb 1:3; 2:17; 5:1,3; 7:27; 9–10 sacrifice register), **confessed** (Mat 3:6; Mar 1:5), **covered/saved** ("cover a multitude of sins" Jam 5:20). 1 John's confess/cleanse seam: "if we say we have not sinned…" (1Jo 1:10), "an **advocate** with the Father" (1Jo 2:1). (Touches forgiveness/remedy and divine-record registers — recorded, not fused.)

**(e) The universal-condition register.** All are under sin: **"all have sinned and fall short"** (Rom 3:23), "all … are **under sin**" (Rom 3:9), "imprisoned everything **under sin**" (Gal 3:22), sin "came into the world … and so death spread to all because all sinned" (Rom 5:12), "knowledge of sin" through the law (Rom 3:20; 7:7), "born in utter sin" (Joh 9:34), "die in your sin(s)" (Joh 8:21,24).

**(f) Bounded interior seat (the NT constitutional seats — 13 co-seated occ).** These are the genuine interior signal — few but distinct, and they are the **NT constitution** (pneuma/sarx/nous/psuchē/kardia/suneidesis), not the Hebrew ne.phesh/lev:
- **spirit (pneuma, 4):** "every sin … will be forgiven, but blasphemy against the **Spirit** will not" (Mat 12:31; Mar 3:29); "the law of the **Spirit** of life has set you free … from the law of sin" (Rom 8:2); "the **Spirit** is life … although the body is dead because of sin" (Rom 8:10).
- **flesh (sarx, 3):** "our **sinful** passions … while we were living in the **flesh**" (Rom 7:5); "with my **flesh** I serve the law of sin" (Rom 7:25); "in the likeness of **sinful flesh** … he condemned sin in the **flesh**" (Rom 8:3). The Romans flesh↔sin link.
- **mind (nous, 2):** "the law of my **mind** … captive to the law of sin" (Rom 7:23); "I serve the law of God with my **mind**, but with my flesh … the law of sin" (Rom 7:25).
- **soul/life (psuchē, 2):** "Consider him who endured from sinners such hostility … that you may not grow weary [in your **souls**]" (Heb 12:3); "will save his **soul** from death and cover a multitude of sins" (Jam 5:20).
- **heart (kardia, 1):** "you … were once slaves of sin [but] have become **obedient from the heart**" (Rom 6:17).
- **conscience (suneidesis, 1):** "sinning against your brothers and wounding their **conscience** when it is weak, you sin against Christ" (1Cor 8:12).
The interior is **bounded (13 of 191) and heavily Romans-weighted** (pneuma/sarx/nous/kardia 10 of 13 in Rom 6–8), consistent with the pešaʿ pattern that the *status/verdict* sin-terms carry little intrinsic interior — yet here the **flesh/mind/spirit web is the explicit Pauline anthropology of sin's dwelling-place** (recorded, not interpreted).

**(g) Sin as a divine-record / accumulating tally.** "fill up the **measure** of their sins" (1Th 2:16), "the **sins** of some are conspicuous, going before them to judgment … others appear later" (1Ti 5:24), "do not **hold** this sin against them" (Act 7:60), the Lord "will not **count** his sin" (Rom 4:8). (Accumulation/record register — recorded.)
