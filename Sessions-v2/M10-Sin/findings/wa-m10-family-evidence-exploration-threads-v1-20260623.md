## Thread 1 — ATONEMENT as an emergent cross-cutting mechanism (HIGH SIGNIFICANCE)

**Observation (researcher):** the **chat.tat "sin offering"** sense is an **atonement mechanism**; atonement is a **central biblical concept**; *"I am not sure in which unit this is encapsulated."* And (Thread 5): the **M10c purification rituals are part of the atonement mechanisms** too.

**What the evidence shows (held open):** the atonement-mechanism evidence is **scattered, not encapsulated**, across at least three places in the current unit-set:
- **chat.tat H2403H "sin offering"** (102 occ, cultic, Lev/Num) — read under the ḥāṭāʾ digest; currently maps to **M10 #14 Forgiveness**.
- **kip.pu.rim "atonement"** (8 occ) + the *ka.phar* "cover/atone" partner — Ring 4 remedy; also **M10 #14**.
- **M10c purification** (ta.me → "purify/cleanse"; priestly "pronounce clean"; washing; Lev 16:30 "atonement… clean from all your sins") — read under the M10c digest, **M10c #1 ritual**.

**The gap:** there is **no unit for "atonement / the mechanism of dealing-with-sin"** as such. It is bundled into **#14 "Forgiveness sought and received"** (which is the *inner movement + divine response*, not the cultic apparatus) and split off into M10c #1. So the central concept sits across a unit boundary.

**Open questions (for the researcher / §10 — not decided here):**
1. Is **atonement its own logical unit** (a "mechanism" object distinct from sin-status, guilt, and forgiveness)?
2. Or is it a **cross-cutting concept** that several units feed (sin-offering + purification + covering + Day of Atonement), to be assembled as a layer rather than a unit?
3. Or does it belong primarily to **another cluster** — **M11 (Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration)** or a sacrifice/cult cluster — with the M10 family only contributing the sin-side?

**Evidence task (if/when directed):** collate ALL atonement-mechanism evidence already gathered (chat.tat sin-offering + kip.pu.rim/ka.phar + M10c purification + a.sham guilt-offering) into one cross-cutting evidence view — *without* deciding the unit question — so the encapsulation choice can be made on the full picture.

## Thread 2 — a.von is a COLLECTIVE / PROCESS term (three separate objects)

**Observation (researcher), confirming the ʿāwōn digest:** the three a.von senses are **definitely separate objects**, not one:
- **crime (H5771G)** = *a description / classification of an act* — "it does not name the act" (it classifies it as criminal).
- **guilt (H5771H)** = *an inner-being phenomenon* (the liability felt/borne).
- **punishment (H5771I)** = *an act of retribution / judgement* (external).
*"maybe a.von is a collective term that describes a process of different elements."*

**Implication (held open):** a.von may be a **process/lifecycle word** binding act-classification → inner guilt → retributive judgement under one lemma. If so:
- The current units **#18 "Iniquity as accumulated moral crime" (H5771G)** and **#17 "Guilt as inner-being state" (H5771H)** are two of the three elements; the **punishment element (H5771I, 16 occ)** has **no home unit** and edges toward the **external pole / judgement** (cf. the standing rule that punishment/judgement is the external pole, not an inner-being state).
- Only the **guilt** element is squarely inner-being; crime is a classification; punishment is external.

**Evidence task (if/when directed):** decompose a.von by sub-entry into the three process-elements with their verse-evidence, and map each to its (current or needed) home — flagging the punishment element as external-pole and the crime element as a classification, not an act.

## Thread 3 — pešaʿ: split in the evidence-gathering phase

**Observation (researcher):** *"pesa — split in the evidence gathering phase."*

**What the evidence already carries (from the pešaʿ digest, to be formalised as a split):**
- **pe.sha (noun, H6588) = "transgression" — a standing wrong** (status) that is carried, borne, blotted-out, forgiven, accumulated ("for three transgressions and for four").
- **pa.sha (verb, H6586) = the act of rebelling** (action), itself splitting into:
  - **God-ward rebellion** (Isa 1:2; Jer 2:8; Eze 2:3; Hos 7:13) — relational defiance of the covenant Lord;
  - **political revolt** (2Ki 1:1; 3:5; 8:20; 1Ki 12:19; 2Ch 10:19) — vassal/nation against a king (several `neutral`-valence).

**Evidence task (if/when directed):** formally split the pešaʿ evidence into these distinct strands as separate evidence sub-units (noun-status vs verb-act; within the act, God-ward vs political), so each is tracked and mapped on its own terms rather than collapsed under one lemma.
