## 2. Diversity observed — organised by the FOUR DEFILEMENT MECHANISMS (faces — HELD OPEN, not conclusions)

The corpus resolves into four logical units. Each is a distinct *mechanism* by which defilement is acquired or applied.

### (a) Ritual / bodily defilement-state — **[M10c #1]**

Defilement **acquired by bodily contact** (a corpse, a bodily discharge, menstruation, skin disease) **OR by authoritative verdict / categorical designation** (the priest "pronounces unclean"; certain animals unclean by nature). This is the **largest single block and the home of the `neutral` valence**; concentrated in Lev 11–15 and Num 19. The state bears on **sacred participation, communal life, and conscience**, but carries no intrinsic moral fault.

- **Contact with a corpse / the dead:** "Whoever touches the dead body of any person shall be unclean seven days" (Num 19:11; 19:14,16,22; 9:6,7,10; Lev 11:24–40 carcasses; Lev 22:4). The **water-for-impurity** (nid.dah) ritual addresses this defilement (Num 19:21).
- **Bodily discharges:** "When any man has a discharge from his body, his discharge is unclean" (Lev 15:2; 15:4–27 the whole discharge code; semen Lev 15:16–18,32).
- **Menstruation (nid.dah):** "she shall be in her menstrual impurity for seven days, and whoever touches her shall be unclean" (Lev 15:19; 15:20,24,25,26,33; childbirth Lev 12:2,5; Lev 18:19; Eze 18:6).
- **Skin disease / "leprous disease":** the priest **"shall pronounce him unclean"** — defilement by **verdict** (Lev 13:3,8,11,15,20,22,25,27,30,44,46; garment/house Lev 13:51–59; 14:36–46; the leper's own cry "Unclean, unclean" Lev 13:45).
- **Categorical / by nature:** "Every animal that parts the hoof but is not cloven-footed… is unclean to you" (Lev 11:8,26,47; 20:25; clean/unclean alike may eat the common meat Deu 12:15,22; 15:22 — the unclean *person* here is **neutral**, not sinful).
- **The distinction itself:** "distinguish between the holy and the common, and between the unclean and the clean" (Lev 10:10; 11:47; 20:25; Eze 22:26; 44:23 — defilement as one pole of a taught classificatory order; opposite = M12).

### (b) Moral-inner defilement-state — **[M10c #2]**

Defilement as **inner corruption of conscience / will / heart** by desire, habitual sin, or idolatry; the **locus is the inner person, not external contact**. This is the **primary NT register** (akatharsia, molunō, molusmos, miasmos) plus a Hebrew metaphor tail. **This is where the genuine inner seats sit** (see §3, cited here):

- **Heart / desire (kardia):** "God gave them up **in the lusts of their hearts** to **impurity**" (Rom 1:24 — akatharsia co-seated with kardia "heart" + epithumia "desire").
- **Flesh (sarx):** "you once presented your members as slaves **to impurity**" (Rom 6:19 — akatharsia co-seated with sarx); "let us cleanse ourselves from every **defilement of body and spirit**" (2Cor 7:1 — molusmos co-seated with sarx); "the works of the **flesh**… impurity" (Gal 5:19 — akatharsia co-seated with sarx).
- **Conscience (suneidesis):** "their **conscience**, being weak, **is defiled**" (1Cor 8:7 — molunō co-seated with suneidesis).
- **Habitual moral filth (vice-list register):** Eph 4:19 ("greedy to practice every kind of impurity"); Eph 5:3,5; Col 3:5 ("impurity, passion, evil desire"); 1Th 2:3; 4:7 ("God has not called us for impurity, but in holiness"); 2Cor 12:21 (unrepented impurity); 2Pe 2:10 ("the lust of **defiling passion**", miasmos); Mat 23:27 ("within are full of… all uncleanness", akatharsia of the hypocrite); Rev 3:4 ("not **soiled** their garments", molunō); Rev 14:4 ("not **defiled** themselves with women", molunō).
- **Hebrew metaphor of inner uncleanness:** "I am a man of **unclean lips**" (Isa 6:5); "Who can bring a **clean** thing out of an **unclean**? There is not one" (Job 14:4); "they became unclean **by their acts**" (Psa 106:39); "I am not unclean" (the self-protestation, Jer 2:23).

### (c) Corporate / covenantal defilement — **[M10c #3]**

Defilement **extending from persons to land, sanctuary, and the divine name** through moral defection (idolatry, sexual betrayal, bloodshed). The agent is the covenant people; the object is corporate/holy space.

- **The land defiled / vomits out its inhabitants:** "Do not make yourselves unclean… for by all these the nations… have become unclean" (Lev 18:24); "You shall not **defile the land** in which you live, in the midst of which I dwell" (Num 35:34; Deu 21:23); "they **defiled it** by their ways and their deeds" (Eze 36:17 — with nid.dah "menstrual impurity" as the simile; 36:18).
- **The sanctuary / holy name defiled:** "the nations… have **defiled your holy temple**" (Psa 79:1); "they have **defiled my sanctuary**" (Eze 23:38); "the house of Israel shall no more **defile my holy name**" (Eze 43:7); "carry out the **filth** (nid.dah) from the Holy Place" (2Ch 29:5); cf. defiling the tabernacle Lev 15:31.
- **Sexual / marital betrayal as defilement of a person:** the adultery ordeal (Num 5:13,14,20,28,29 "she has defiled herself"); Lev 18:20; 20:21 (nid.dah "it is impurity"); Eze 18:6,11,15 ("defiles his neighbor's wife"); 22:10,11; 23:7,13,17,30 (idolatry-as-whoredom).
- **Idolatry-as-defilement:** Eze 20:18,26,31,43 ("defile yourselves with their idols"); 22:3,4 (the city makes idols "to defile herself"); Hos 5:3; 6:10; Jer 2:23; 19:13 (houses "defiled like Topheth").
- **Nazirite / dedicated-state defilement:** "shall he make himself unclean" — even for kin — "because his separation to God is on his head" (Num 6:7,9,12); the priest's restricted corpse-contact (Eze 44:25).

### (d) Defilement by external spiritual agency — **[M10c #4]**

The **"unclean spirit" (akathartos pneuma)** inhabiting / controlling a person: **the will is overridden, faculties suppressed, communal access severed; expulsion = restoration / wholeness.** This is where the **pneuma co-seated (16) mostly sits — pneuma as AGENT, not seat** (§3). Almost the entire `spiritual-being` object_type block.

- **Inhabiting / controlling the person:** "a man **with an unclean spirit**" (Mar 1:23; 5:2); "He **has** an unclean spirit" (Mar 3:30; 7:25); "many a time **it had seized him**… driven by the demon into the desert" (Luk 8:29).
- **Commanded / cast out by authority:** "He **commands** even the unclean spirits, and they obey him" (Mar 1:27; Luk 4:36); "**rebuked** the unclean spirit" (Mar 9:25; Luk 9:42); "**Come out** of the man, you unclean spirit!" (Mar 5:8); "gave them **authority over** unclean spirits, to cast them out" (Mat 10:1; Mar 6:7).
- **Going-out / wandering / returning:** "When the unclean spirit **has gone out** of a person, it passes through waterless places…" (Mat 12:43; Luk 11:24); the herd of pigs (Mar 5:13).
- **Healing / cure as the outcome:** "those who were **troubled** with unclean spirits **were cured**" (Luk 6:18; Act 5:16; 8:7 — defilement paired with nosos "illness").
- **Eschatological agency:** "three **unclean spirits** like frogs" (Rev 16:13); Babylon "a haunt for every **unclean spirit**" (Rev 18:2); Mar 3:11; Luk 4:33.

## 3. Field hygiene / CC verify-list (flags, no DB change)

- **⚠ pneuma-as-unclean-spirit (the prominent flag):** of the **16 pneuma co-seated**, essentially all are the **akathartos pneuma / "unclean spirit"** of mechanism (d) — Mat 10:1; 12:43; Mar 1:23,26,27; 3:11,30; 5:2,8,13; 6:7; 7:25; 9:25; Luk 4:33,36; 6:18. Here **pneuma is the EXTERNAL DEFILING AGENT (the demon), NOT the human person's spirit-seat.** **Do NOT count these as inner-being seating.** The interior signal of M10c is therefore **far smaller** than the raw co-seated tally suggests.
- **ne.phesh (7) — person / life idiom, flag:** the ne.phesh co-occurrences (Lev 11:43,44; 20:25; Deu 12:15; Eze 4:14; 23:17; Hos 9:4) are the **"soul: myself / appetite / life" idiom** ("do not defile *yourselves*"; the soul that eats), not ne.phesh as an interior faculty being defiled. Flag as person/life idiom, not seat. (Hos 9:4 "soul: appetite" is the nearest to a genuine appetitive reading.)
- **The genuine inner seats (cite — small but real):** **kardia 1** (Rom 1:24), **sarx 3** (Rom 6:19; 2Cor 7:1; Gal 5:19), **suneidesis 1** (1Cor 8:7). These five are the only genuine interior-faculty seatings in the group; all sit in mechanism (b).
- **valence — the neutral block is CORRECT, not an error:** the 84 `neutral` occ are the ritual-state usages (menstruation, corpse-contact, skin disease, non-kosher animals) — **rightly non-moral.** Verify only that nothing *genuinely sinful* is mis-tagged neutral. Spot-check note: most `neutral` are clean (Lev 11–15, Num 19); the moral-defection occ are correctly `sinful`/`forbidden`. **One to flag:** Eze 9:7 "**Defile** the house" tagged `neutral` (a divine command of judgment — register debatable); Eze 44:25 corpse-contact `neutral` (correct). No clear neutral-over-tag of a moral act found.
- **`righteous`-valence (4) — Josiah's reform:** 2Ki 23:8,10,16 ("**defiled** the high places / Topheth") and Isa 30:22 ("you will **defile** your carved idols") read defilement as a **tool of iconoclastic reform** (rendering a pagan site cultically dead). Valence `righteous` is defensible — the *act of defiling* is approved though the *result* is uncleanness. Flag as a register inversion, recorded not resolved.
- **object text vs object_type:** trust object_type (the known rule). Several `object` fields capture an adjacent noun ("evening", "days", "case", "priest") rather than the true grammatical object — these are the "until evening" / "seven days" time-phrases of the ritual code, not real objects. The `none` on object_type 113 is largely **correct silence** (the adjective "unclean" predicated of a thing/person with no governed object).
- **faculty term-intrinsic:** none on all 226 — correct silence; the lemma names a state/act, not a faculty.
- **No truncation:** book spread is contiguous; counts reconcile to the architecture (ta.me 107+59 · nid.dah 16 · akathartos 29 · akatharsia 10 · molunō 3 · molusmos 1 · miasmos 1 = 226).

## 5. Held open (no conclusions)

- Whether the **non-moral ritual-state** (a) and the **moral-corruption** (b/c) senses are **one defilement concept on a continuum** or **two distinct phenomena that share a lemma** — the valence split (sinful 96 / neutral 84) is the sharpest evidence either way; recorded, not resolved.

## 10. Note — prime §10 "OTHER object-kind" test case

**Defilement raises the clearest "OTHER object-kind" question in the M10 family.** The M-model assumes a finding is a *moral characteristic* of a *self-incurred status*. M10c breaks both assumptions:

- **Mechanism (a)** is a **ritual STATE that is not a moral characteristic** and is frequently **not self-incurred** — it is **contracted by contact** (touching a corpse, a discharge, a carcass) or **imposed by authoritative verdict** (the priest "pronounces unclean"), often with **valence = neutral**. A menstruating woman or a corpse-handler is *unclean* without having *sinned*.
- **Mechanism (d)** is a state **imposed by an external agent** (the unclean spirit) — the human is the *site* of defilement, not its author; the will is overridden.

Both cases describe a person who *is* unclean without being *morally* unclean and often without *acting* at all. **Flag M10c as a prime §10 test case** for whether the model needs an "OTHER object-kind" (a non-moral, possibly non-self-incurred condition) alongside characteristic / status / action. Recorded, not resolved.
