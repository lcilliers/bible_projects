## 2. What SP4 adds
- **Histories — the kings' pagan imitation.** The recurring formula: kings "did according to the **abominations of the nations**" (1 Ki 14:24; 2 Ki 16:3; 21:2,11; 2 Ch 28:3; 33:2; 36:8), centrally child sacrifice ("burned his son", 2 Ki 16:3; 2 Ch 28:3). Reform = **removal**: "Josiah took away all the **abominations**" (2 Ch 34:33, *sur*).
- **The idol itself as abomination.** "An **abomination** is he who chooses [the idol]" (Isa 41:24); the idol-maker who "falls down before an **abomination**" with no "knowledge or discernment" — "none considers in his **heart**" (Isa 44:19, *lev* co-seated, *te'vunah*/*da'at*).
- **Shamelessness (new interior thread — inverse of Ezekiel's self-loathing).** "Were they **ashamed** when they committed **abomination**? No… they did not know how to **blush**" (Jer 6:15; 8:12, *bosh*/*kalam*). The abominators' conscience is seared — they cannot feel shame at abomination. This sits directly against Eze 36:31's self-loathing: the same act produces shame in the penitent and none in the hardened.
- **Abomination as faithlessness / treachery.** "Judah has been **faithless**, and **abomination** has been committed… profaned the sanctuary" (Mal 2:11, *ba'gad* [R1]).
- **Hypocritical cult as abomination.** "incense is an **abomination** to me" — legitimate offering rejected when joined to iniquity (Isa 1:13, *a'ven*).
- **Defilement (M10c) continues pervasively:** defiled high places (2 Ki 23:13, *ta'me*+*shiqquts*+*mash.chit*[R3]); polluted the house (2 Ch 36:14, *ta'me*+*ma'al*); land filled with abominations (Ezr 9:11, *nid'dah*+*tum'ah*); "defiled my land… made my heritage an **abomination**" (Jer 2:7); "polluted my land with the carcasses of their detestable [idols]" (Jer 16:18, *shiqquts*+*a'von*+*chat'tat*).
- God "**hates**" it (Jer 44:4, *sa'ne*), "could no longer **bear**" it (Jer 44:22); it "never came into **my** heart" (Jer 32:35 — God's heart, *cha'ta*).

## 3. to'evah COMPLETE — the faces (held open, not concluded)
Across 108 occurrences *to'evah* is a verdict of **detestableness** (overwhelmingly *to the Lord*), worn in distinct ways by corpus:
- **Law:** cultic/conduct verdict on acts and objects; no interior.
- **Wisdom:** enters the **heart** (crooked/arrogant heart, wicked thoughts); runs on an **abomination/delight** polarity; **reciprocal** (what fools/the wicked detest).
- **Ezekiel:** the prophetic **catalogue**; **defiles** the sanctuary (M10c); the **heart goes after** it; the **death-line** of the two ways; to be **turned from/removed**; triggers **self-loathing/shame/grief**.
- **Histories/Prophets:** the **idol** itself; the kings' **pagan imitation**; **shamelessness**; **faithlessness**.
Constant across all: relational to **God's revulsion**, and a pervasive **defilement (M10c) bridge**. No single reading adopted.
