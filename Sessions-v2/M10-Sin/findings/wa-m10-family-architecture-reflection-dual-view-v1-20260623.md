## 1. The model — two complementary views of one object

A **true characteristic** that surfaces inside M10 + M10b + M10c does so **because it is part of the *sin operation***. The *same* characteristic has (or should have) **its own cluster** where it is analysed **as a characteristic in its own right**. These are **two complementary views of one object — not a duplication:**

| | View A — **operation-view** | View B — **characteristic-view** |
|---|---|---|
| Where | inside M10/M10b/M10c | the concept's own cluster |
| What it shows | the thing **as an element/participant in the sin operation** | the thing **as a characteristic in itself** |
| Example | **M10b** wickedness/evil/abomination (how evil operates *within* sin) | **M27** "Evil, Wickedness and Abomination" (evil as its own characteristic) |

So **M10b ≠ a duplicate of M27** — M10b is evil *seen in the sin operation*, M27 is evil *seen as a characteristic*. The near-identical names are the **signature of the dual-view**, not redundancy.

This aligns with what the programme already half-knew (memory `feedback_ontology_typed_relationships`: the cluster is scaffolding, not the real object; `feedback_characteristic_is_typed_term_in_verse`). The M10 family has now made it explicit and unavoidable.

## 2. The deeper shift — linear partition vs multi-dimensional operation

- **Until now** the clusters were a **linear split of concepts** — a partition, one concept per box, mutually exclusive.
- **But** the inner life, and **sin in particular, is a multi-dimensional *operation*** — one concept participates **across several dimensions/operations at once** (evil is *a characteristic* AND *an element of the sin operation* AND *the object of divine revulsion* AND …).
- A linear partition **cannot hold a multi-dimensional operation.** The same object legitimately appears in multiple clusters under different views.
- **We do not yet have the keys** — the conceptual framework that maps the dimensions and says, for any object, which views it has and where each is analysed. This reflection names the problem; it does not solve it.

Whether the "none" cases (guilt, the sin-status core) *should* have a sibling, or are correctly sin-only, is exactly what we lack the keys to decide.

## 4. What this does to the §10 gate (reframing — held open)

The gate question "characteristic vs status vs other" is now sharper, with a **candidate criterion** (to be tested, not adopted):
- A **characteristic** = an object with a **characteristic-view cluster of its own**; its appearance in M10 is the *operation-view*.
- A **status** (e.g. sin itself, guilt) = an object whose **only home is the sin operation** — no independent characteristic-cluster.
- An **"other" object** (e.g. M10c ritual-state, defilement-by-external-agency, atonement-mechanism) = something that fits neither — a *state*, a *mechanism*, or a *relation/pole-pair* rather than a faculty-characteristic.
- The gate must therefore define **the dimensions/views first**, then say which views each object has and where each is analysed — i.e. it must address the §2 multi-dimensional problem, not just sort objects into three bins.

## 5. Not resolved — and honestly so
We **do not have the keys**. This note (a) records the dual-view model, (b) shows the arrangement is inconsistent, and (c) reframes the gate. It does **not** decide the dimensions, fix the inconsistency, or re-architect the clusters. Those wait until the framework (the "keys") is found — likely an iterative, evidence-driven discovery, not a one-shot design.
