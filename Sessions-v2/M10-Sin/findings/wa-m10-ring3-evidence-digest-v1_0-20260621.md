## 2. Evidence — the ring is heterogeneous (documented, not yet acted on)

Reading every verse, "consequences" does not hold together as one behaviour. Four distinct things sit here:

**(a) External agents / instruments / places of destruction — not sin, not inner being.**
*mash.chit* is dominantly the *destroyer* or means of destruction: the Passover "destroyer" (Exo 12:13), the "destroying weapon" (*mash.chet*, Eze 9:1), "arrows for destruction" (Eze 5:16), the "destroying mountain" Babylon (Jer 51:25), a "trap" (Jer 5:26), even a place-name "Mount of Corruption" (2 Ki 23:13). These are executors/instruments of judgment, mostly God's.

**(b) Physical / cosmic decay and mortality — outcome, not inner being.**
*fthora* "perishable" of the mortal body and creation (Rom 8:21; 1 Cor 15:42, 50; Col 2:22); *diaftheirō* "outer self wasting away" (2 Cor 4:16); *ma.she.chat* a sacrificial animal's "mutilation/blemish" (Lev 22:25); Daniel's ruined appearance (Dan 10:8).

**(c) Genuine MORAL CORRUPTION operating on the inner being — this belongs nearer the dynamics than the consequences.**
*ftheirō* corrupts "good morals" (1 Cor 15:33), leads "thoughts astray from devotion" (2 Cor 11:3, *noēma*), the "old self corrupt through deceitful desires" (Eph 4:22, *epithumia*); *fthora* "corruption in the world through desire" (2 Pe 1:4) and reaped from "the flesh" (Gal 6:8, *sarx*); *diaftheirō* "depraved in mind" (1 Ti 6:5, *nous*). Touch points: **nous, noēma (mind), sarx (flesh), epithumia (desire), the old self.** This is real inner-being-operation evidence — corruption *of* the mind/self — sitting inside the "consequences" ring.

**(d) Mis-sorted senses — *cha.val* (the flagged anomaly), now resolved.**
*cha.val* H2254B is **multi-sense**, and the lexicon's "to bind" is **not borne out by the verses**. Stems and senses: "acted corruptly" against God (Neh 1:7, Qal — a *sin act*), "I will not offend" (Job 34:31, Qal — a *sin act*), "my spirit is **broken**" (Job 17:1, Pual — an *inner-being state*), "brings destruction on himself" (Pro 13:13, Niphal), "to **ruin** the poor" (Isa 32:7, Piel), "uncleanness that **destroys**" (Mic 2:10, Piel). So *cha.val* spans *act-corruptly* / *be-broken* / *destroy* — it does not sit cleanly in Ring 3, and "to bind" is a wrong anchor for this registry.
