## Jer 4:14
> "O Jerusalem, wash your heart from evil, that you may be saved. How long shall your wicked thoughts lodge within you?"

**Terms in the verse (4):** *lev* "heart" (H3820A, M47) · *ra'ah* "evil" (H7451I, M27) · *machashavah* "thoughts" (H4284, M14) · *a'ven* "wicked" (H0205G, **M10b — focus**)

### Per-term lexical evaluation (all parts interpreted, not transcribed)

**lev — "heart"** (target: *heart*; co-occurring)
- *sense / lemma_meaning:* the heart as the comprehensive inner man — mind, will, understanding. Here it is the **subject acted upon**: the thing to be washed.
- *type:* status (a standing seat, not an act). *faculty:* the broadest in the verse — cognition, perception, volition, conscience: the heart is the integrating inner organ.
- *location:* heart / inward-parts — the verse's shared seat (all four terms sit here). *how:* **wash** (H3526, *kabas* — to launder, as cloth). *relational:* "from" — washed *from* the evil.
- *Reading:* the heart is presented as a seat that can be **cleansed** — laundering language applied to the inner man, not the body.

**ra'ah — "evil"** (target: *evil*; co-occurring)
- *sense / lemma_meaning:* evil as **harm / what is harmful** — the substance the heart is to be rid of.
- *type:* status. *faculty:* affect + cognition + moral_evaluation — a broad signature (it is felt, thought, and judged).
- *location:* heart / inward-parts. *how:* "saved" (H3467); *relational:* "from" — removal of this evil is tied to **being saved**.
- *Reading:* the evil here is a harmful content lodged in the heart whose **removal is salvific**.

**machashavah — "thoughts"** (target: *thoughts*; co-occurring)
- *sense / lemma_meaning:* thought / device — inner **cognitive content / devising**.
- *type:* status. *faculty:* cognition. *location:* heart / inward-parts. *how:* **lodge** (H3885, *lun* — to stay the night, dwell). *experiencer:* Jerusalem (addressed).
- *Reading:* the wicked thoughts **dwell / stay overnight** in the heart — a persistence dimension.

**a'ven — "wicked"** (target: *wicked*; **focus**)
- *sense / lemma_meaning:* wickedness — "trouble, wickedness, sorrow" (carries an affective/trouble undertone).
- *type:* status. *faculty:* affect + moral_evaluation (dual). *location:* heart / inward-parts. *how:* **lodge**; *object:* "within"; *relational:* "long" ("how long").
- *Reading:* the wickedness **lodging within** — the "wicked thoughts" *are* this *a'ven*; it is the inner wickedness that dwells.

### How the terms relate in this verse
The verse is built around the **heart** (*lev*) as seat. Within it **lodge** (*lun* — dwell/stay) **wicked thoughts** (*machashavah*), which the verse names as **wickedness** (*a'ven*) and characterises as **evil/harm** (*ra'ah*). The imperative is to **wash** (*kabas*) the heart **from** that evil, "**that you may be saved**"; the rhetorical "**how long** shall they lodge" presses their **persistence**. All four share the seat *heart / inward-parts*, and their faculties stack: the heart integrates cognition–perception–volition–conscience, the evil is felt-thought-judged, the thoughts are cognitive, the wickedness affective-moral.

### Inner-being reality evidenced here (neutral)
This verse evidences the **heart as a container-seat** whose contents can be **evil thoughts that dwell and persist** ("lodge", *lun*) — and as something that can be **cleansed**: the inner man is **washed** (*kabas*, laundering) *from* evil, and that cleansing is tied to **being saved**. So the inner being behaves here as a seat that (i) holds and harbours persistent evil cognition, and (ii) is capable of being laundered clean of it, with salvific consequence. Notably, **purity/cleansing language is applied to the heart itself**, not only to the body — a datum to set beside the bodily-defilement material (relation noted, not interpreted).

### Flags (caution on the extract's own tags — held open, not inherited)
- The extract tags *valence = sinful* on **all four** terms, **including *lev* "heart."** The heart here is the **seat to be cleansed**, not itself the wickedness; the blanket "sinful" tag over-reaches and must not be read as "the heart is sinful." (Exactly the status-vs-characteristic conflation to keep open.)
- Four clusters meet in one verse (M47/M27/M14/M10b). The inner-being reality is the **joint** picture; reading *a'ven* alone "against the sin-core" would fragment it — which is the bias being corrected.
