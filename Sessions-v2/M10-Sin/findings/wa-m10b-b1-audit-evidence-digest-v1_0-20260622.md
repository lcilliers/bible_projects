## 2. The structural signature of the wicked-family (the strongest single audit finding)
- **type = status: 258 / 258.** Every occurrence is a *status*, not an action or quality-event.
- **faculty = moral_evaluation: 258 / 258.** Uniform and term-intrinsic — the wicked-family names a **moral verdict on a person/thing**, not a faculty-seated inner state. This is the cleanest field-uniformity seen in any M10 unit so far, and it anchors what the wicked-family *is* at the data level: a classification, not a movement.

## 3. Seating (co-seated across B1)
22 co-seatings: *nephesh* 14, heart-family (*lev* 5, *levav* 2, *libbah* 1) 8. The *nephesh* set is mostly "life/person" (the wicked threatening or losing life; the psalmist's own soul) — not the wicked's seat. **Genuine wicked-heart seatings (~5):** the heart that denies accountability (Psa 10:13), evil in the hearts behind peace (Psa 28:3), the heart "of little worth" (Pro 10:20), the heart that "pours out evil" (Pro 15:28), the "proud heart [that is] sin" (Pro 21:4). The wicked's **interior is bounded to these texts**, confirmed by the recovery adding almost none.

## 4. Touch points — what the wicked-family reaches
- **Sin-core, heavy:** *a'von* ×9, *cha'ta* ×8, *chat'tat* ×7, *chat'ta* ×3, *pe'sha* ×3 (= 30 core touches) — the wicked is the one who sins / bears iniquity / transgresses.
- **Within M10b:** *a'ven* (evil) ×7, *to'evah* (abomination) ×6 — the wicked is bound to evil and is the object of abomination.
- **Rings:** *a'vel*/injustice [R2] ×5, *ba'gad*/treachery [R1] ×4, plus *tah.pu.khah*, *a'sham*, *ma'al*.
- **Cross-satellite (M10c):** *ta'me*/*ta'hor* (clean/unclean) at Ecc 9:2 — the "great leveling."
- **Dominant external antonym:** *tsaddiq* (righteous) — pervasive but external to M10 (the righteousness field). The wicked-family is defined as much by this contrast as by its sin-core touches.

## 5. The lemma profiles
- ***ra'sha* (the wicked person) — five coexisting strands** (held open): (1) forensic verdict; (2) characterised interior (bounded — §3); (3) the fixed two-destinies antithesis (dominant); (4) the reversible condition summoned to turn and live (Ezekiel/Isaiah); (5) the theodicy / wisdom-crisis — the wicked who prospers and seems to escape the order (Psa 73; Ecclesiastes).
- ***re'sha* (abstract "wickedness")**: a commodity/possession ("treasures of wickedness", "wicked scales", Mic 6:10–11), a **bondage** to be loosed ("loose the bonds of wickedness", Isa 58:6), a **confessed wrong** before God ("we acknowledge our wickedness… for we have sinned", Jer 14:20, with *a'von*+*cha'ta*), something **sown and reaped** (Hos 10:13), and **self-implicating** ("your wickedness concerns a man like yourself", Job 35:8).
- ***mirsha'at***: wickedness attributed to a person ("that **wicked woman**", 2 Ch 24:7).
