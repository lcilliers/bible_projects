## 2. NEW in Psalms — the wicked's interior (recorded, not concluded)
SP1 showed a person-level verdict with thin inner content. Psalms opens the wicked's **inside**, and the diversity must be held, not flattened:
- **Soul's desire, boasted:** "the wicked **boasts of the desires of his soul**" (Psa 10:3, *nephesh* co-seated, *ta'avah* desire, *halal* boast).
- **Heart denies accountability:** "says **in his heart**, 'You will not call to account'" (10:13, *lev*); "**all his thoughts** are, 'There is no God'" (10:4).
- **Evil concealed in the heart:** "speak peace… while **evil is in their hearts**" (28:3, *levav*, *a'ven* co-term).
- **Transgression's inner voice:** "**Transgression speaks** to the wicked **deep in his heart**; there is no fear of God before his eyes" (36:1, *lev*, *pe'sha* core).
- **Pride / self-exaltation:** "in **arrogance** the wicked pursue the poor" (10:2, *ga'avah*); "in the **pride of his face**" (10:4); 36:11.

So in Psalms *ra'sha* carries a **describable inner profile** — desire, godless thought, heart-denial, concealed evil, an inner address by transgression, pride. This complicates (does not overturn) the SP1 reading; both are evidence.

## 3. God's posture toward the wicked's inner being
- **Tested:** "you **test the minds and hearts**" (7:9, *kilyah*+*libbah*) — God examines the wicked's interior.
- **Hated:** "his **soul hates** the wicked" (11:5, God's *nephesh*).

## 4. The wicked's conduct and fate (continuing motifs)
- **Conduct:** violence (17:9), bow/arrow against the upright-in-heart (11:2; 37:14), prowling (12:8), plotting and gnashing (37:12), pursuing the poor (10:2), borrowing without repaying (37:21).
- **Fate:** chaff in the wind (1:4), perish (1:6; 37:20), return to Sheol (9:17; 31:17), snared in their own hands (9:16), arms broken (37:17), "be no more" (37:10), "many sorrows" (32:10), put to shame (31:17).
- **Axis intact:** righteous vs wicked (*tsaddiq*) frames Psa 1 and Psa 37 throughout.

## 5. Field hygiene
- Genuine heart-seatings: 7:9, 10:13, 28:3, 36:1 (and 11:2 = the *upright's* heart, the victim's). Genuine soul: 10:3 (the wicked's soul-desire).
- Artefacts / non-seat: 17:9, 17:13 *nephesh*="life" (the psalmist's life threatened/delivered); 11:5 *nephesh* = **God's** soul (the wicked is the object of divine hatred, not the seat).
- *che.sed* "shame" mis-gloss surfaces again at 32:10 (the verse is *steadfast love*) — same gloss artefact flagged in M10/M05; for the CC verify-list.

## 6. Holding the question open
Do **not** yet decide whether wickedness is "only" a person-verdict or also an inner-being condition. SP1 and SP2 point different directions; Proverbs (SP3) and the Prophets (SP4) will add more. Recorded as divergent evidence.
