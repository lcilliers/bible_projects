# M10 — Sin, Guilt and Transgression

## [M10 #1] Wilful sinning
- **DEF:** The will committing a known wrong — sinning as a deliberate relational act directed against God, another person, or the self, where the person knows what they are doing and does it.
- **TERMS:** cha.ta (H2398) · hamartanō (G0264) · paraptōma (G3900) — owner M10.
- **DB:** 264. **CHAT:** partial — *paraptōma* read in Ring 2 (Gk transgression-status; remedy-adjacent, grace-paired Rom 5; Eph 2:1,5). cha.ta/hamartanō = CORE, not re-read.
- **X-REG:** —. **GAP:** the core sin-act verbs (cha.ta, hamartanō) need the neutral per-verse read; "wilfulness" marker (intent) not yet evidenced per-occurrence.

## [M10 #2] Unintentional sinning
- **DEF:** Sin committed without deliberate intent — real and morally consequential even though the inner person did not consciously defy God's command; conscience engages once the act surfaces to awareness.
- **TERMS:** cha.ta (H2398) — owner M10.
- **DB:** 213. **CHAT:** none (CORE not re-read). **GAP:** the intent/awareness distinction (vs #1) is the analytic core and is unread under the new method — DB-only.

## [M10 #3] Confession
- **DEF:** The moment the inner person names a committed sin as sin — first-person confession, corporate declaration, or the conscience voicing an act already done. Conscience in confessional mode.
- **TERMS:** cha.ta (H2398) · hamartanō (G0264) — owner M10.
- **DB:** 250. **CHAT:** none. **X-REG:** (confession ↔ repentance — cf. **M11**). **GAP:** confession is a *movement*, likely shared with M11; needs read + the §10 status/characteristic call.

## [M10 #4] Conscience suppression
- **DEF:** The inner person who does not recognize, denies, or suppresses awareness of their own sinful state — conscience absent, self-blinded, or actively claiming innocence where guilt is real.
- **TERMS:** cha.ta (H2398) · hamartanō (G0264) — owner M10.
- **DB:** 250. **CHAT:** none. **SEAT:** conscience (suneidesis) is the implied seat — only 2 suneidesis co-seats family-wide; check coverage. **GAP:** core unread; conscience-seat evidence thin.

## [M10 #5] Refusal to repent
- **DEF:** The will that persistently refuses to turn from a sinful course when departure is available and known to be required — turning actively withheld.
- **TERMS:** cha.ta (H2398) · hamartanō (G0264) · chat.tat (H2403B) — owner M10.
- **DB:** 410. **CHAT:** none directly; the *turn/shuv* counter-movement appears in M10b (ra'sha "turn and live", a'ven "turn from"). **X-REG:** ↔ **M11**. **GAP:** core unread; the "refusal" (vs the turn) needs evidencing.

# M10c — Defilement and Impurity

> **Whole-cluster status (updated 2026-06-23):** M10c has now been **freshly read** under the neutral method — `wa-m10-core-m10c-evidence-digest` (**MC**), 226 occ / 203 verses, organised by the four mechanisms below (superseding the scratch files). Headline evidence finding: defilement is **largely a STATE, not a moral act** (valence neutral 84 vs sinful 96) and the **pneuma co-seated (16) is mostly the *unclean spirit* as external agent, not a human spirit-seat** — genuine inner seats are only ~5 (sarx/kardia/conscience, all in mechanism (b)). M10c is the prime **§10 "other object-kind"** test case.

## [M10c #1] Ritual defilement-state
- **DEF:** The inner-being condition of ritual uncleanness — being unclean as it bears on sacred participation, communal life, and conscience. Acquired by two mechanisms (bodily contact with a defiling agent; authoritative verdict/categorical designation). Inner content: awareness of contamination, watchful restraint, distress at worship-exclusion, conscience formed by purity categories, duty not to transmit. Structural opposite: M12.
- **TERMS:** akathartos (G0169) · ta.me (H2930A verb) · ta.me (H2931 adj) · nid.dah (H5079) — owner M10c.
- **DB:** 260. **CHAT:** scratch only (niddah, 16 verses). **X-REG:** **M12** (opposite). **GAP:** the largest M10c unit — essentially unread under method; ta.me two-lemma split (verb/adj) to honour.

## [M10c #2] Moral-inner defilement-state
- **DEF:** Defilement as the inner condition of moral corruption — conscience, will, heart corrupted by desire, habitual sin, idolatrous attachment; locus is the inner person, not external contact. Primary NT register + Hebrew metaphor (Isa 6:5; 64:6; Job 14:4). Inner content: conscience defiled (1 Cor 8:7), dominion-of-flesh slavery (Rom 6:19; 1:24; Eph 4:19), body-and-spirit purification (2 Cor 7:1), idolater = heart impurity (Eph 5:5).
