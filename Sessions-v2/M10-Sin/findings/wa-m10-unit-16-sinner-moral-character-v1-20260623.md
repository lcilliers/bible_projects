## A. General / non-verse observations (collated from the source digests)

**Definition (DB):** the constitutive inner moral identity of the person defined by sin — not the act or the condition but **what kind of person they are at their moral core**; the sinner as a recognisable category.

**From the hamart- digest (HM §2b — the NT "sinner"):** hamartōlos (G0268, 45 occ, type=`quality`) names a **class of person**: the Gospels' **"tax collectors and sinners"** (Mat 9:10,11; Mar 2:15,16; Luk 5:30; 7:34; 15:1,2); **sinner vs righteous** as a contrast pair (Mat 9:13; Mar 2:17; Luk 5:32; 15:7; 18:13 "God, be merciful to me, a sinner"); **a verdict on a person** ("a sinful man" / "a sinner" — Luk 5:8; 7:37,39; 19:7; Joh 9:16,24,25,31); and the Pauline/general moral category ("while we were still **sinners**" Rom 5:8; "made **sinners**" Rom 5:19; "Gentile **sinners**" Gal 2:15; "to save **sinners**" 1Ti 1:15; "separated from **sinners**" Heb 7:26; "ungodly **sinners**" Jude 15). The "sinful generation" (Mar 8:38) and "sinful men/flesh" (Luk 24:7; Rom 8:3) sit here too.

**From the ḥāṭāʾ digest (HA §2c — the OT "sinner"):** chat.ta (H2400, all 19 occ `quality` / `moral_evaluation` / `sinful`; topsense sinners 15 / sinner 6) — a **standing person-type, not an act**: Gen 13:13 ("the men of Sodom were wicked, great **sinners** against the Lord"); the Psalter's sinner-vs-righteous frame (Psa 1:1,5 "the way of sinners … sinners [will not stand] in the congregation of the righteous"; 25:8; 26:9; 51:13; 104:35); the wisdom warning (Pro 1:10 "if **sinners** entice you"; 13:21; 23:17); the prophetic verdict (Isa 1:28; 13:9; 33:14; Amo 9:8,10); Num 32:14; 1Sa 15:18; 1Ki 1:21.

**Structural signature (this unit):** type=**quality** throughout (the moral-category person, not the act `action` or the standing wrong `status`) · faculty: HM tags hamartōlos `volition` (the NT family-wide tag — see hygiene); HA tags chat.ta `moral_evaluation` — **both term-intrinsic, not verse-level** · valence sinful · object_type predominantly person/none. The person-identity face across two languages: the Greek substantive and the Hebrew person-noun name the same "kind of person."

**Field hygiene (from HM §3 / HA §3):**
- **⚠ HYGIENE — gloss/sense tension on hamartōlos:** glossed "sinful" but functions **substantivally "sinner(s)"** in most occ (gloss="sinful", sense="sinners/sinner"). The person-label reading is the one in play — do not collapse to the adjective.
- **⚠ HYGIENE — faculty is term-intrinsic, not verse-level:** hamartōlos carries `volition` (the NT family-wide tag); chat.ta carries `moral_evaluation`. Neither is a verse-level inner-faculty reading — verse-level inner engagement must read from co-terms / co-seated, not these tags.
- **⚠ HYGIENE — object vs object_type:** trust object_type (the known rule); several rows carry `object` text that is a partner/predicate, not a governed wrong.

**Flags requiring deeper synthesis (surfaced, NOT resolved):**
- **⚖ BORDER — pair with #23 (Wickedness as settled person-identity).** Both #16 and #23 name the **person-defined-by-sin** — the sinner (chat.ta / hamartōlos) and the wicked (ra'sha). **⚑ SYNTH — the sinner/wicked identity-pair:** these are two faces of the constitutive sin-identity; whether they are one person-identity unit or two distinct verdicts (chronic-doer "sinner" vs settled-character "wicked") is the §10 consolidation call. Gen 13:13 already fuses them ("wicked, great **sinners**"); Psa 1 sets sinner alongside the wicked/scoffer. **Surface, not resolved.**
- **⚑ SYNTH — status vs quality vs action (the POS seam; HM §5 / HA §5).** Across both families the noun-"sin"/person-"sinner"/verb-"to sin" split tracks part-of-speech (status / quality / action). Whether "the sinner" (quality) is a genuinely distinct ontological object from "sin" (status) and "to sin" (action), or one phenomenon under three forms, is a §10 test case — recorded, not resolved.
- **⚑ SYNTH — the sinner-vs-righteous axis is constitutive.** Both terms are defined *against* righteousness (Psa 1 the two ways; Luk 18:13 the sinner vs the self-righteous; Mat 9:13 "not the righteous but sinners"). The person-identity is a relational/contrastive category, not a standalone trait — surface for the synthesis (the identity is read off the righteous/sinner pole-pair).
- **⚠ HYGIENE — cross-language parallel recorded, NOT fused (HM §4):** the Hebrew chat.ta and the Greek hamartōlos person-labels are noted as parallel evidence; per the evidence-first rule they are **recorded side by side, not merged into one cross-language finding** here.

**↔ BOND (cross-cluster):** **M26 (Righteousness)** — the sinner-vs-righteous contrast pair is the defining axis (Psa 1; Mat 9:13; Luk 18:13); **M05**, **M37**, **M45** (per the unit's bond set); and the **#23 (Wickedness)** person-identity pairing within M10 (the ra'sha settled-character verdict).
