# M10 — "Is it an inner-being characteristic?" — recognition read of the 12 single units

- **File:** wa-m10-recognition-read-single-units-v1-20260623.md · **v1 · 2026-06-23 · Author:** Claude Code.
- **Status:** a **§10-gate INPUT** — my evidence-grounded *recognition read*, NOT a verdict. Answers the researcher's question: of the 12 single units, which would I *not* recognise as an inner-being characteristic? Grounded in the gathered evidence (faculty / co-seated seating / `experiencer` / `type` / definition). To be tested at the §10 definitional exploration.
- **Working criterion:** an *inner-being characteristic* = an **operation or disposition OF the inner being** (a faculty in operation). Contrast object-kinds: **STATUS/identity** (a standing condition or person-type, not an operation) · **OUTWARD act/expression** (speech, political act — an output, not the inner state) · **MECHANISM** (a route/process) · **EXTERNAL agent** (not the person's interior at all).

## Would NOT recognise as an inner-being characteristic (7)

| # | Unit | Why not | Object-kind it looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| **8** | Political revolt | faculty=none · experiencer=**other** (reported *of nations/kings*) · **no inner seating**; a nation rebelling against a king | **outward/relational act** |
| **9** | Sinful speech | speech is an **output**; the unit IS the verbal act (its inner *root* is heart-corruption, but that root is the characteristic, not the speech) | **outward expression** |
| **28** | Evil through speech | same — blasfēmeō is the **verbal act** of defiance/slander | **outward expression** |
| **16** | The sinner as moral character | the DB definition itself: "**not the act or the condition** but **what kind of person** they are at their moral core" — a person-type | **identity / status** |
| **23** | Wickedness as settled **person-identity** | the name says it; type=**status 258/258**; interior **bounded (~5 seats)**; it *defines the wicked person*, it isn't a faculty operating | **identity / status** |
| **24** | Evil as **constitutional inner nature** | a **constitutional condition** ("the structural condition… the constitutive nature"), not an operation; **and** it contains the **"evil one" (Satan)** — an external spiritual agent, not the person's interior | **status/condition** (+ external agent) |
| **10** | Specialised mechanisms | a heterogeneous grab-bag — enticement (deleazō), hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai), apostasy (apostasia); routes by which sin operates | **mechanism(s)** |

## Pattern (feeds §10)
The "not a characteristic" set sorts into the **same object-kinds the architecture-reflection predicted**:
- **identity / status** (the person *is* X, or stands in condition X): #16, #23, #24 — these mirror the family-file STATUS units (#11–13 sin-as-condition/power/record) and M10c defilement-states.
- **outward act / expression** (the inner state *outputs* X): #8, #9, #28 — the speech pair (#9/#28) and the political act (#8).
- **mechanism**: #10.
- **genuine inner-being characteristics** (a faculty *in operation*): #20, #21, #27 (+ #22 if the disposition reading wins over the conduct reading).

**Implication for §10:** the cluster is **not uniformly "characteristics"** — it holds at least four object-kinds (characteristic · status/identity · outward-act · mechanism), exactly the multi-dimensional picture. The "characteristic vs status vs other" definitions should be set so these sort cleanly — and the **outward-act** kind (speech, political revolt) may be the one most easily mistaken for a characteristic because it's *sinful*, when it is really the *expression* of an inner characteristic that lives elsewhere.
