**(a) pe.sha as a standing wrong that is carried, borne, and removed.** The noun behaves as an object that can be acted upon: **blotted out** (Isa 43:25; 44:22; Psa 51:1), **forgiven / covered** (Psa 32:1,5; Exo 34:7; Num 14:18; Mic 7:18), **confessed** (Psa 32:5; 51:3), **borne by another** (Isa 53:5 "pierced for our transgressions"; 53:8; 53:12), **turned-from** (Eze 18:28,30,31; 33:12), **remembered/not-remembered** (Isa 43:25; Psa 25:7). This is the strongest single behaviour of the noun. (Touches remedy/forgiveness and divine-record registers — recorded, not fused.)

**(b) pa.sha as rebellion against God** (God-ward verb): "children… have **rebelled against me**" (Isa 1:2); Jer 2:8,29; 3:13; Eze 2:3; 20:38; Hos 7:13; 8:1; Isa 66:24. Relational defiance of a covenant Lord; object_type=God.

**(c) pa.sha as political revolt** (person/situation verb): vassal nations/kings — Moab rebels against Israel (2Ki 1:1; 3:5,7), Edom/Libnah revolt against Judah (2Ki 8:20,22; 2Ch 21:8,10), the northern tribes against the house of David (1Ki 12:19; 2Ch 10:19). **(d) Bounded interior seat.** The 7 co-seated occ are the whole inner-being signal:

The interior is **bounded, not pervasive** (7 of 127), concentrated in Psalms/Isaiah — consistent with the M10b pattern that the *status/verdict* terms carry little intrinsic interior.

**(e) Measured, counted accumulation.** Amos's "**For three transgressions… and for four**" (Amo 1:3,6,9,11,13; 2:1,4,6) treats pešaʿ as a counted, accruing tally against nations (object_type=situation). (Accumulation/record register — recorded.)
