## 2. Evidence — what the 8 verses carry

- **All cultic/legal, never "sinful" in valence** — commanded (2), neutral (3), righteous (2). The remedy behaves as the inverse of the wrong, as expected.
- **Grammar:** all the masculine-plural noun *kippurim* ("atonements") — a provision/state, not an act (the *acting* is carried by the partner verb).
- **Core synergy is the covering verb.** Touch points are dominated by **ka.phar / kip.per "to cover / atone" (8)** — atonement's constant partner is the act of covering — with **chat.tat "sin offering" (CORE ×3)** and *cha.ta* (×1). So *kippurim* the noun leans on *kaphar* the verb and the *chat.tat* offering: the remedy names a **covering applied to sin**.
- **What atonement is applied to (read from the verses):** the **altar** (Exo 29:36; 30:10), the **persons/their lives** (Exo 30:16 "for your lives"), and individuals ("for him", Num 5:8; "for you", Lev 23:28). Largely **objective and external** — blood on horns, atonement money, a ram.

## 3. The one inner-being thread (documented, not concluded)

Reading with an open mind, atonement is not entirely external. On the **Day of Atonement the people must "afflict yourselves / your souls" (nephesh, Lev 23:27)** — the self/soul is humbled as part of the atonement day (the partner verb is *a.nah* "to afflict"). And atonement is made **"for your lives" (nephesh, Exo 30:16)**. So there is a thread where the remedy touches the inner being — the soul afflicted, the life ransomed — alongside the dominant objective/cultic covering. **Recorded as a touch point to weigh at synthesis; no claim made yet about what it means.**
