## 1. Coverage

## 2. Seven threads in Ezekiel

**(a) The prophetic catalogue / lawsuit.** The prophet is commissioned to expose the city's abominations: "make known to Jerusalem her **abominations**" (16:2); "declare to her all her abominations" (22:2); "let them know the abominations of their fathers" (20:4); the temple tour of escalating "greater abominations" (8:6,9,13,15,17). The content: idolatry, harlotry/whoring (16; 23, *taznut*), bloodshed ("the bloody city", 22:2), child sacrifice (16:20–21), adultery and incest (22:11; 33:26), oppression and usury (18:12–13).

**(b) Defilement (M10c) — strongest bridge here.** Abominations **defile the sanctuary**: "you have **defiled my sanctuary** with all your detestable things" (5:11, *shiqquts* + *ta'me*); "defiles his neighbour's wife" (22:11; 33:26, *ta'me*); the temple defiled (43:8); "uncircumcised in heart and flesh… **profaning my temple**" (44:7). Touch: *ta'me* ×4, *nid'dah* ×1, *shiqquts* ×4. The *to'evah*↔M10c link is densest in Ezekiel.

**(c) The heart that goes after abominations (interior).** "I have been broken over their **whoring heart** that has departed from me… all their abominations" (6:9, location=heart); "those whose **heart goes after** their detestable things and their abominations" (11:21, location=heart); "uncircumcised in **heart** and **flesh**" (44:7, *ba'sar* co-seated). The interior dimension SP2 opened continues — the heart is the organ that pursues abomination.

**(d) Abomination as the death-line of the two ways (Eze 18).** "commits **abomination**… he shall surely die" (18:12–13); the righteous who turns and "does the same **abominations** that the wicked person does… shall die" (18:24 — touching *shuv*, *cha'ta*, *ma'al*, *ra'sha*, *a'vel*, *tsaddiq*). Abomination is the act that forfeits life — the dividing line.

**(e) Turn from / remove abominations (remedy link).** "**Repent and turn away** from your idols… from all your abominations" (14:6, *shuv*); "they will **remove** all its detestable things and abominations" (11:18, *sur*). The turn-and-live theme (cf. *ra'sha* SP4) attaches to *to'evah*.

**(f) The inner response — self-loathing, shame, grief.** Upon remembering: "you will **loathe yourselves** for your iniquities and your abominations" (36:31; 6:9); "they shall bear their **shame** and the abominations they committed" (44:13, *kelimmah*). And the faithful remnant: "put a mark on the foreheads of the men who **sigh and groan** over all the abominations" (9:4, *a'nach*). Abomination provokes an inner recoil — in the guilty (self-loathing) and in the faithful (grief).

**(g) God's revulsion / judgment.** Abominations bring wrath and the withdrawal of pity: "my eye will not spare… while your abominations are in your midst" (7:4,9, *chamal*/*chus*); they provoke (8:17, *ka'as*); judgment "according to all your abominations" (5:9; 7:3–9). Pride link: "**haughty** and did an abomination" (16:50, *ga'vah*); pride + abominable images (7:20, *ga'on* + *nid'dah*).

## 3. Touch points & seating

## 4. Held open
With Ezekiel, *to'evah* now spans: a relational verdict (to the Lord), a cultic-conduct catalogue, a defiler of the sanctuary (M10c), a thing the heart goes after, the death-line of the two ways, a thing to be turned from/removed, and the trigger of self-loathing/shame/grief. The Law/Wisdom/Prophets faces all differ. No reading adopted; remainder (Histories + Isa/Jer/Mal) is SP4.
