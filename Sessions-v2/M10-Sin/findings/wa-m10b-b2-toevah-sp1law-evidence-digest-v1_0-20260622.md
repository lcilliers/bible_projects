## 1. Coverage
*to'evah* in the Law = **21/108**, all read. type=status, faculty=moral_evaluation throughout — a **classification/verdict**, not an inner state. **object_type=God ×10** (dominant): abomination is overwhelmingly relational — *detestable to the Lord*.

## 2. What *to'evah* is in the Law (distinct from *ra'sha*)
Where *ra'sha* was the wicked **person** (with a bounded interior), *to'evah* is a verdict on **acts and objects** as detestable. The domains:
- **Sexual transgression:** "lie with a male as with a woman… it is an abomination" (Lev 18:22; 20:13); unlawful remarriage after defilement (Deu 24:4).
- **Idolatry / images:** carved images (Deu 7:25–26; 27:15), pagan worship (Deu 12:31), strange gods (Deu 32:16).
- **Forbidden cult practice:** divination, child sacrifice — "the abominable practices of those nations" (Deu 18:9–12; 20:18); blemished sacrifice (Deu 17:1); prostitute's fee in the house of the Lord (Deu 23:18).
- **Dishonesty:** false weights / "all who act dishonestly" (Deu 25:16, *a'vel*).
- **Disordering of kinds:** cross-dressing (Deu 22:5).
- **The nations' practices** that defiled the land (Lev 18:26–30).

## 3. Defined by God's revulsion (the relational core)
*to'evah* is fixed not by an internal faculty but by **the Lord's reaction to it**:
- **Hatred:** "every abominable thing that the Lord **hates**" (Deu 12:31, *sa'ne*).
- **Jealousy / anger:** "with abominations they **provoked him to anger**… stirred him to **jealousy**" (Deu 32:16, *qana*/*ka'as*).
- **Israel's required posture:** "you shall utterly **detest and abhor** it" (Deu 7:26, *ta'av*).
So abomination is a **relational status** — what is detestable *to* God and to be detested *by* his people.

## 4. Cross-links (noted, not synthesised)
- **Defilement (M10c):** abominations make the **land unclean** (Lev 18:27, *ta'me*); Deu 24:4 (*ta'me*). A direct to'evah↔M10c bridge.
- **Sin-core:** "so you **sin** against the Lord" (Deu 20:18); Deu 24:4 (*cha'ta*).
- **Injustice (R2):** dishonest dealing (Deu 25:16, *a'vel*).
- **Desire:** "you shall not **covet** the silver or gold… lest you be **ensnared**" (Deu 7:25, *chamad*) — the abominable object draws desire.

## 5. Inner-being
**None in the Law.** No co-seated, no location; faculty is the term-intrinsic moral_evaluation. Law *to'evah* is purely a relational-cultic-moral verdict on conduct and objects. (Whether Proverbs/Ezekiel add interior content — e.g. "a proud heart is an abomination" — is for SP2–SP3; not assumed here.)
