## A. General / non-verse observations (collated from the source digests, flagged)

### A0. Coverage (what was read)
- **Batch 3 = 83 occurrences, all read** (B3 audit §1): *a'ven* **69** (Psalms 31, Job+Pro+1Sa 20, Isaiah+Prophets 18 — read out at 69, plan said 80), *ro'a* **14**. Coverage-checked; no truncation.
- *a'ven* senses (per-occurrence): predominantly **moral-evil** (evildoers / iniquity / evil / wicked / crime / wrong) with a "trouble" affective tail and a minority idol/vanity/nonsense sense.

### A1. Structural signature — the MOST interior M10b batch (B3 audit §2; Bsum §3)
- **Interior seatings: 13 / 83 (~16%)** — heart/soul/inward. Proportionally the **highest of any M10b batch** (wicked *ra'sha* ≈ 5/258 ≈ 2%; abomination ≈ 9/140 ≈ 6%; evil ≈ **16%**). The Hebrew-evil family engages the inner being **far more** than the verdict-families do. *This is the densest interior of the evil terms.*
- **Type:** status 65, **action 18** — more action-typed than *ra'sha*/*to'evah* (almost purely status). The Hebrew-evil terms name evil that is **done/worked**, not only a standing verdict.
- **Faculty (DUAL):** *a'ven* = **[affect, moral_evaluation]** on all 69 — moral judgment **plus affective weight** (its "trouble/sorrow" tail); *ro'a* = **no faculty** on all 14 (abstract "badness"). Neither matches the moral_evaluation-only verdict of the wicked/abomination families.

### A2. The GENERATIVE metaphor — evil conceived → given birth (the signature) ⚑ SYNTH
Across **three corpora** *a'ven* is consistently **gestated and brought forth** — an evil that is *generated*, not a static verdict (SP1 §3; SP2 §2; SP3 §2; B3 audit §3):
- **Psalms:** "the wicked **conceives** evil, is **pregnant** with mischief, gives **birth** to lies" (**Psa 7:14**) — *a'ven* gestated in the inner person and birthed as deceit.
- **Wisdom:** "they **conceive** trouble and give **birth** to evil, and their **womb** prepares deceit" (**Job 15:35**); "those who **plow** iniquity and **sow** trouble **reap** the same" (Job 4:8, *charash*/*a'mal*).
- **Prophets:** "they **conceive** mischief and give **birth** to iniquity" (**Isa 59:4**).
- ⚑ **SYNTH — this is the clearest CHARACTERISTIC-like lifecycle in the family — a key §10 test case (process vs status).** Unlike the verdict-families (a *standing* condition), *a'ven* describes a **process with a lifecycle**: conceived → devised/cherished → chosen → worked out → uttered. If any M10 unit reads as a characteristic-in-operation rather than a status, it is this one. Surface for §10; do not resolve.

### A3. Devised / harboured in the heart — the strongest interior of the evil terms (SP1 §3; SP2 §3; SP3 §2; B3 audit §3)
*a'ven* is something the heart **devises, gathers, cherishes, inclines to, lodges as thoughts** — and then **utters**:
- "**evil is in their hearts**" behind peaceful speech (Psa 28:3, *levav*); "his **heart gathers iniquity**" (Psa 41:6, *lev*); "if I had **cherished iniquity in my heart**, the Lord would not have listened" (Psa 66:18) — *a'ven* harboured inwardly **blocks prayer**; "let not my **heart incline** to any evil" (Psa 141:4); "**iniquity and trouble are within** it" (Psa 55:10, inward-parts); "he **plots** trouble on his bed" (Psa 36:4, *chashav*).
- "a **heart that devises** wicked plans" (Pro 6:18, *lev*, *machashavah*, *charash* — of the seven abominations of Pro 6).
- "the fool's **heart** is busy with iniquity" (Isa 32:6, *lev*+*nephesh*); "**wash your heart** from evil… how long shall your **wicked thoughts** lodge within you?" (Jer 4:14, *lev*+inward-parts, *machashavah*) — the heart is the seat to be **washed** of it; "men who **devise** iniquity" (Eze 11:2, *chashav*); "those who **devise** wickedness… **on their beds**" (Mic 2:1).
- And worked out as **speech**: "under his tongue are mischief and **iniquity**" (Psa 10:7); "the words of his mouth are **trouble** and deceit" (Psa 36:3).
- Heart/soul/inward seatings across *a'ven*: Psa 28:3, 41:6, 55:10, 66:18, 141:4; Isa 32:6 (+soul), 66:3 (soul); Jer 4:14 — **~8**, proportionally the densest interior signal of any M10b lemma (SP3 §4).

### A4. Chosen / turned-from — the volitional dimension and the remedy seam (SP2 §4; SP3 §2; B3 audit §5)
*a'ven* is something the **will engages** — chosen, turned to, turned from, put away:
- "if iniquity is in your **hand**, **put it far away**" (Job 11:14, *av'lah*); "[God] commands that they **return from** iniquity" (Job 36:10); "do not **turn to** iniquity, for **this you have chosen** rather than affliction" (Job 36:21, *bachar* choose — iniquity vs affliction as an object of choice).
- "let the unrighteous man [forsake] his **thoughts**… **return** to the Lord… he will abundantly **pardon**" (Isa 55:7, *shuv*/*racham*/*salach*); "take away… the speaking of **wickedness**" (Isa 58:9, *sur*).
- ⚑ **SYNTH — the turn-from / remove theme (repentance & reform)** is strong across both lemmas (B3 audit §5): *sur* ×4 + *shuv* — evil/iniquity to be **put away, turned from, the heart circumcised** (Isa 1:16; Jer 4:4 "circumcise… your hearts"; Jer 25:5; 26:3; Job 11:14; 36:10; Isa 55:7). Links the unit to the repentance/remedy seam. ↔ **BOND M11 (Repentance)** implicit here; surface for synthesis.

### A5. ro'a — two streams under one lemma (B3 audit §4)
- **Moral stream:** "the **evil of [their] deeds / way**" (Deu 28:20; Isa 1:16; Psa 28:4; Jer 21:12; 23:2; 44:22) — repeatedly the object of a **turn-from / remove** call (Isa 1:16, *sur*; Jer 4:4 "circumcise… your hearts"; Jer 23:22; 25:5; 26:3, *shuv*); "the **evil of your heart**" (1 Sa 17:28, *za'don*). This is the **evil-deeds register** (the unit definition's tail).
- **Affective stream:** "**sadness** of face… the **heart**" (Ecc 7:3); "**sadness of the heart**" (Neh 2:2). The "badness" registers as the **heart's grief** as well as its evil — the affective "sadness of heart" counterpart to *a'ven*'s affective tail.
- ⚖ **BORDER — ro'a's dual streams** (moral badness vs affective sadness) sit under one lemma with **no faculty** assigned; the affective stream is arguably an inner-state in its own right, the moral stream an evil-deeds register. Per-occurrence routing already applied; surfaced for §10.

### A6. Minority / secondary senses of a'ven (SP2 §5; SP3 §2)
- **Idolatry equivalence:** "presumption is as **iniquity** and idolatry" (1 Sa 15:23, *teraphim*; *chat'tat*, *meri* rebellion) — Samuel equates rebellion/presumption with iniquity and idolatry.
- **Idol / vanity sense (minority, attested):** "their soul **delights** in their [idol-offering]" (Isa 66:3, sense=idol, *nephesh*+*chaphets*+*shiqquts*); "the household gods utter **nonsense**" (Zec 10:2, sense=nonsense) — *a'ven* as idolatrous emptiness / false utterance (cf. "Beth-aven").
- **Judged deeds:** "the **workers of iniquity**" meet calamity/disaster (Job 31:3); God "sees" and "stores up" iniquity (Job 11:11; 21:19); "the mouth of the wicked **devours iniquity**" (Pro 19:28); evildoers meet destruction/terror at justice (Pro 10:29; 21:15); "woe to those who **decree iniquitous decrees**" (Isa 10:1); "works of iniquity… thoughts of iniquity" (Isa 59:6–7).
- **The prophet made to see it (theodicy):** "why do you make me **see iniquity**?" (Hab 1:3).
- **Denial:** the adulteress "I have done no **wrong**" (Pro 30:20).

### A7. The dominant Psalms idiom — "workers of iniquity / evildoers" (po'alei a'ven) (SP1 §2)
*a'ven* is repeatedly the **wrought evil of the wicked enemies**: "you hate all **evildoers**" (Psa 5:5); "depart from me, all you **workers of evil**" (Psa 6:8); the evildoers who "eat up my people" (Psa 14:4; 53:4), flourish then perish (92:7,9), boast (94:4), lay snares (141:9), are cut off (101:8). Here *a'ven* names the **deeds/agents** the psalmist seeks deliverance from — the active malice of the enemy.

### A8. Touch & seating, hygiene (SP1 §4; SP2 §6; SP3 §4; B3 audit §5, §7)
- co-seated across B3: *lev* 5, *levav* 3, *nephesh* 2 = 10; 13 heart/soul/inward locations (§A1).
- **Comparatively self-standing:** touch into M10 is **light** — *sur*[R2] ×4 (turn/remove), *ra'sha*/*to'evah*/*shiqquts*/*re'sha*[M10b], *av'lah*/*av'val*[R2], *chat'tat*[CORE], *ba'gad*[R1]. Unlike B2's heavy M10c defilement bridge, *a'ven*/*ro'a* travel mostly with **external** trouble/deceit/lies terms — *a'mal* (trouble/mischief), *mir'mah* (deceit), *she'qer* (lies), *sa'ne* (God's hatred of evildoers).
- ⚠ **HYGIENE — the "trouble" sense** (Psa 36:3,4; 55:3,10) is the **affective tail** of *a'ven*, recorded as part of its range, **not a separate homonym** (SP1 §4).
- ⚠ **HYGIENE — multi-sense handled per-occurrence:** *a'ven* (moral-evil dominant; trouble/idol/nonsense tail); *ro'a* (dual moral/affective). *che.vel* "pain" and *che.sed* "shame" mis-glosses are on the verify-list (not present in B3) (B3 audit §7).

### A9. Cross-references and §10 flags
- ⚑ **SYNTH — this is the clearest CHARACTERISTIC-like lifecycle in the family — a key §10 test case (process vs status).** (See A2; the unit brief flags it as the §10 process-vs-status case.)
- ↔ **BOND M14 (Deceit)** — *a'ven* repeatedly births/travels with *mir'mah* (deceit), *she'qer* (lies): conceived→births deceit (Psa 7:14; Job 15:35), words "trouble and deceit" (Psa 36:3).
- ↔ **BOND M47 (Constitution)** — the heart/inward as the seat where *a'ven* is devised, cherished, lodged as thoughts, and to be washed (A3).
- ↔ **BOND M15 (Wisdom)** — the *machashavah*/devising of plans (Pro 6:18; Jer 4:14; Eze 11:2) is the cognitive faculty bent to evil; the fool's heart "busy with iniquity" (Isa 32:6).
- ↔ **BOND M30** — *(per the unit brief.)*
- ⚖ **BORDER — gradient note (Bsum §4, §6):** the impression of a B1→B2→B3 *verdict → detestableness → interior* gradient (with *a'ven* as the interior pole) does **not** survive B4 (Greek evil spans the whole range and adds personification). Hold *a'ven* as a **co-present facet** (the interior/generative one), not the terminus of a developmental line. Surface for §10.
