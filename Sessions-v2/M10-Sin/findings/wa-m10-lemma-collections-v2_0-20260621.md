## 1. Method (restated, corrected)

The first-level grouping is the **lemma** — the lexeme as the lexicon defines it. One collection per lemma. Grouping key is the transliteration, which folds together the same lexeme's Strong's sense-suffixes (e.g. *a.von* crime/guilt/punishment) and its verb/noun pair (e.g. *ma.al*), because those are *sense* and *kind* breakouts and we are explicitly **not** mixing breakouts into the first level. The lexicon definition (`lemma_meaning`) is shown for each, as the intrinsic-sense anchor.

**Coverage:** 54 lemma collections; member counts sum to **1,462** (every focus occurrence placed; none dropped).

## 3. Folding decisions for you to ratify (transparency)

Four collections fold more than one Strong's code; lexically each is one lemma, but you may want any split back out:

- **L01 chat.tat** folds the *wrong* sense ("sin", 159) **and the cultic remedy** ("sin offering", 102) and "punishment" (2). One lexeme, three senses — the largest collection, and it straddles wrong/remedy.
- **L03 a.von** folds crime(162)/guilt(27)/punishment(18) — the deed→guilt→penalty span in one lexeme.
- **L06 ma.al** folds verb (be unfaithful) + noun (unfaithfulness).
- **L08 a.sham** folds verb (be guilty) + noun (guilt / guilt offering).

Two `lemma_meaning` anomalies to verify on read: **L30 cha.val** (lexicon "to bind" vs gloss "to destroy") and **L52 al.vah** (empty meaning).

## 4. What this is — and what comes next

This is the **first level only**: the lemma collections, lexicon-led, nothing else mixed in. It is the stable spine. The next levels, worked *within* and *across* these collections, are where the breakouts and the verses come in:

- **Level 2 (within a lemma):** read the lemma's verses, with its lexicon meaning as the lens, to see how that one lexeme actually operates — its sense-range, valence, whose it is, what it acts against, whether it touches the inner being.
- **Level 3 (across lemmas):** group lemma collections by likeness of behaviour into the higher constructs (characteristic / status / something new) — only after the within-lemma reads.

No reading or typing is done here. The lemma is the floor we build up from.
