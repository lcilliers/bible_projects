## Finding 1 — M10's sin footprint is OT-weighted, and FOCUS-filtered
- **OT/NT split (focus verses):** **1,495 OT / 409 NT** (78% OT). The NT sin footprint in M10 is small.
- **It is focus-filtered by design:** the corpus holds the **inner-being-relevant, OWNER** occurrences of the sin terms — *not every sin-mention*. A sin word that is a co-term in another cluster's verse, or span-filtered as not-inner-being, is not counted here. So "fewer than expected" is partly **by construction** (focus-filter), not necessarily a hole.
- **Not truncation:** the programme-wide STEP 60-cap sweep (2,396 terms) already cleared truncation; the core counts (cha.ta 213, chat.tat 272, a.von 207, hamartia 105…) reconcile to STEP. The "fewness" is the focus-filter, **not** dropped verses.
- **⚠ But one NT-sin coverage point to confirm:** the NT sin terms' M10 focus-count is **well below their STEP totals** — e.g. **hamartia 105 in M10 vs ~173 in the NT**. The ~68 difference is **span-filter / cross-cluster** (hamartia occurrences not read as inner-being focus, or owned elsewhere), **not** truncation — but it is worth a deliberate confirmation that none are wrongly excluded. (Coverage check owed.)

## Finding 2 — the atonement OT→NT "shift" is a VOCABULARY MIGRATION across clusters (grounded)
Where the atonement/redemption/forgiveness vocabulary actually sits (mti_terms cluster):

| Term | gloss | Cluster home |
|---|---|---|
| chat.tat H2403H | sin-offering | **M10** |
| kippurim H3725 | atonement (noun) | **M10** |
| **kaphar H3722** | **atone / cover (the OT verb)** | **M11 / M38** (not M10!) |
| hilastērion G2435 · hilasmos G2434 | propitiation | **M38** |
| katallagē G2643 · katallassō G2644 | reconciliation | **M05 / M11** |
| aphesis G0859 · aphiēmi G0863 | forgiveness / release | **M11** |
| kopher H3724 | ransom | **T2** |
| padah H6299 | ransom | **M38** |

**Reading (grounded):** M10 keeps only the **OT cultic remnant** — the sin-offering (chat.tat H2403H) and the atonement *noun* (kippurim). **Everything else of the atonement concept has migrated:** the OT cover-*verb* (kaphar) and the whole NT development — propitiation, reconciliation, forgiveness, ransom — live in **M38 (Salvation/Redemption), M11 (Forgiveness), M05 (Love)**. So the **"shift of atonement from OT to NT" is visible as a literal dispersal of the concept's vocabulary out of M10 into the salvation/forgiveness clusters.** This is *why* M10's atonement looks thin and OT-only — and it **confirms SDX-02** (atonement is a cross-cluster mechanism, not an M10 object) with concrete data.

## Finding 3 — CANDIDATE GAP: major redemption terms are absent from the registry entirely
- **apolutrōsis (G0629, redemption), lutron (G3083, "ransom" — Mat 20:28 "give his life as a ransom"), lutroō (G3084, redeem), gaal (H1350, the kinsman-redeemer)** are **NOT IN the DB at all** (no mti_terms row, no registry word).
- These are **foundational redemption/atonement terms.** Their absence is either:
  - **(a) by-design** — redemption is a *divine/external act* (God ransoms/redeems), not an *inner-being characteristic*, so it falls outside the ~214-word inner-being scope; OR
  - **(b) a coverage gap** — the redemption strand of atonement was never registered.
- **Decision needed** (not resolved here): is the redemption vocabulary deliberately out-of-scope (external/divine act), or a gap to fill? This bears directly on whether the programme can speak to the OT→NT atonement development at all.
