## 1. Coverage
ra.sha in the Prophets (Isa 11, Jer 5, Eze 20, Dan 1, Mic 1, Hab 3, Zep 1, Mal 2) = **44/44**, all read. co-seated: *nephesh* ×4. **Core-binding is heaviest here:** *a'von* ×7, *cha'ta* ×3, *chat'tat* ×3, *pe'sha* ×1 — in the Prophets *ra'sha* is paired directly with the sin-core ("turn from his **sin** / **iniquity**"), unlike Wisdom where it was paired with the *tsaddiq* antonym.

## 2. THE MAJOR NUANCE — the wicked who can turn and live (recorded, not concluded)
**14 of 44** occurrences carry the turn/live/pardon field (*shuv* / *chayah* / *salach* / *racham*). This is a different *ra'sha* from Wisdom's fixed destiny:
- **Ezekiel — reversible state.** "If a wicked person **turns away** from all his sins… he shall surely **live**" (18:21); "Have I any **pleasure in the death of the wicked**… rather that he should **turn**… and **live**?" (18:23); "when a wicked person turns… he shall **save his life**" (18:27); the watchman warns "the wicked to **turn** from his way" (3:18–19; 33:8–9); "I have **no pleasure in the death of the wicked**, but that the wicked **turn**… and **live**" (33:11; also 33:14,15,19).
- **Isaiah — forsake and be pardoned.** "Let the wicked **forsake his way**… let him **return** to the Lord… he will abundantly **pardon**" (Isa 55:7, *shuv*/*racham*/*salach*).

So in the prophetic summons, *ra'sha* names a condition one is **called out of** — addressable, warnable, reversible — bound to the sin-core and met by remedy. This stands in tension with the Wisdom "two fixed destinies" and the SP1 forensic verdict. **All are held as evidence.**

## 3. Other prophetic nuances (diversity to keep)
- **Incorrigibility (counter-current):** "If favor is shown to the wicked, he does **not learn righteousness**… he deals corruptly" (Isa 26:10) — favor does not reform; a tension with the turn-theme.
- **Restlessness:** "the wicked are like the **tossing sea**… **no peace** for the wicked" (Isa 57:20–21; 48:22).
- **Theodicy — the wicked prospers:** "Why does the **way of the wicked prosper**?" (Jer 12:1); the wicked surround/swallow the righteous, justice perverted (Hab 1:4,13).
- **Eschatological:** "the wicked shall **act wickedly**… none of the wicked shall **understand**" (Dan 12:10).
- **The Servant numbered with them:** "they made his grave **with the wicked**… though he had done no violence" (Isa 53:9).
- **Judgment motifs:** storm/wrath bursts on the wicked's head (Jer 23:19; 30:23); broken staff/scepter (Isa 14:5); cut off — righteous and wicked alike (Eze 21:3–4); trodden to ashes (Mal 4:3); crushed head (Hab 3:13). The *tsaddiq/ra'sha* distinction persists (Mal 3:18).

## 4. Field hygiene
- co-seated *nephesh* ×4 (Eze 3:19; 18:27; 33:9) — in the turn-or-die clauses, *nephesh*="soul/life" (the life saved or lost). Borderline between seat and "life"; recorded.
- location=flesh (Jer 25:31; Eze 21:4) = "all flesh" (humanity-wide judgment), not an inner seat.
- Eze 13:22 location=heart — "you have **disheartened** the righteous… encouraged the wicked" — the seat reading is loose here; recorded with caution.

## 5. The four faces of ra.sha (across SP1–SP4) — held open, not resolved
The completed read shows *ra'sha* is **not one thing**:
1. **Forensic verdict** — the guilty party in court (SP1).
2. **Characterised interior** — heart-denial, soul-desire, pride, godless thought (SP2–SP3).
3. **Object of divine abomination** and antithesis of the righteous, with fixed destinies (SP3).
4. **Reversible condition** — the wicked summoned to turn and live (SP4).
These coexist in the evidence. **No single reading is adopted.** This diversity is itself the finding to carry forward.
