## 1. Coverage & sense
*a'ven* in Psalms = **31/69**, all read. Senses: evildoers 13, iniquity 7, evil 6, trouble 4, crime 1 — predominantly **moral-evil**, with the "trouble" sense blending in. **faculty = [affect, moral_evaluation]** on all 31 — *a'ven* uniquely carries an **affective** dimension alongside the moral verdict (unlike *ra'sha*/*to'evah*, moral_evaluation only).

## 2. The dominant Psalms idiom — "workers of iniquity / evildoers" (*po'alei a'ven*)
*a'ven* is repeatedly the **wrought evil of the wicked enemies**: "you hate all **evildoers**" (5:5); "depart from me, all you **workers of evil**" (6:8); the evildoers who "eat up my people" (14:4; 53:4), who flourish then perish (92:7,9), boast (94:4), lay snares (141:9), are cut off (101:8). Here *a'ven* names the **deeds/agents** the psalmist seeks deliverance from — the active malice of the enemy.

## 3. The strongest interior signal in M10b — a'ven seated and generated in the heart
Five of 31 are heart/inward-seated — proportionally far more interior than *ra'sha* (≈5 / 248) or *to'evah* (≈9 / 108):
- "**evil is in their hearts**" behind peaceful speech (28:3, *levav*).
- "his **heart gathers iniquity**" (41:6, *lev*).
- "if I had **cherished iniquity in my heart**, the Lord would not have listened" (66:18) — *a'ven* harboured inwardly blocks prayer.
- "let not my **heart incline** to any evil" (141:4) — the prayer against the heart's inclination to *a'ven*.
- "**iniquity and trouble are within** it" (55:10, inward-parts).
And the **generative metaphor**: "the wicked **conceives evil**, is **pregnant** with mischief, gives **birth** to lies" (7:14) — *a'ven* gestated in the inner person and birthed as deceit; "he **plots** trouble on his bed" (36:4, *chashav*). So *a'ven* is not only a verdict but something the heart **devises, gathers, cherishes, inclines to, and brings to birth** — and then utters: "under his tongue are mischief and **iniquity**" (10:7); "the words of his mouth are **trouble** and deceit" (36:3).

## 4. Touch points & hygiene
- co-seated: *levav* (28:3), *lev* (41:6); locations heart ×4 + inward-parts ×1.
- Touch into M10/M10b is **light**: *ra'sha*[M10b] ×1, *ba'gad*[R1] ×1. *a'ven* travels mostly with **external** co-terms — *a'mal* (trouble/mischief), *mir'mah* (deceit), *she'qer* (lies), *sa'ne* (God's hatred of evildoers, 5:5). It is comparatively self-standing.
- The "trouble" sense (36:3,4; 55:3,10) is the affective tail; recorded as part of *a'ven*'s range, not a separate homonym.

## 5. Held open
On the Psalms evidence *a'ven* looks like a **more interior, generative** term than the wicked/abomination families — evil as conceived-and-cherished-in-the-heart and worked out as deeds/speech, carried by a dual affect+moral faculty. Whether Job/Proverbs (SP2) and Isaiah/Prophets (SP3) sustain or qualify this is next. No reading adopted.
