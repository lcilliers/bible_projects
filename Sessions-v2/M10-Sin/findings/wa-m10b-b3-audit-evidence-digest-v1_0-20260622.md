## 1. Coverage
**Batch 3 = 83 occurrences, all read:** *a'ven* 69, *ro'a* 14. (Plan said 80; *a'ven* read out at 69.) Coverage-checked; no truncation.

## 2. The structural signature — the most interior M10b batch
- **Interior seatings: 13 / 83 (~16%)** — heart/soul/inward. Proportionally the **highest of any M10b batch** (wicked ≈ 5/258 ≈ 2%; abomination ≈ 9/140 ≈ 6%; evil ≈ 16%). The Hebrew-evil family engages the inner being far more than the verdict-families do.
- **Type:** status 65, **action 18** — more action-typed than *ra'sha*/*to'evah* (which were almost purely status). The Hebrew-evil terms name evil that is *done/worked*, not only a standing verdict.
- **Faculty:** *a'ven* = **[affect, moral_evaluation]** on all 69 (dual — moral judgment plus affective weight); *ro'a* = **no faculty** on all 14 (abstract "badness"). Neither matches the moral_evaluation-only verdict of the wicked/abomination families.

## 3. a'ven — the interior, generative evil (complete profile)
Evil that is **conceived and given birth** (Psa 7:14; Job 15:35; Isa 59:4), **devised in the heart** and lodged as **thoughts** (Psa 28:3; 41:6; 66:18; 141:4; Isa 32:6; Jer 4:14; Eze 11:2; Mic 2:1), **chosen or turned from** (Job 36:21; Isa 55:7), and **worked out** as deeds and speech ("workers of iniquity"). Dual affect+moral faculty; minority idol/vanity sense (Isa 66:3; Zec 10:2). The most "inner-process" term in M10b.

## 4. ro'a — the badness of deeds, and the sadness of the heart
Two streams under one lemma:
- **Moral:** "the **evil of [their] deeds / way**" (Deu 28:20; Isa 1:16; Psa 28:4; Jer 21:12; 23:2; 44:22) — repeatedly the object of a **turn-from / remove** call (Isa 1:16, *sur*; Jer 4:4 "circumcise… your hearts"; Jer 23:22; 25:5; 26:3, *shuv*). "the **evil of your heart**" (1 Sa 17:28, *za'don*).
- **Affective:** "**sadness** of face… the **heart**" (Ecc 7:3); "**sadness of the heart**" (Neh 2:2). The "badness" registers as the heart's grief as well as its evil.

## 5. Shared structures across B3
- **The turn-from / remove theme (repentance & reform):** *sur* ×4 + *shuv* — evil/iniquity to be **put away, turned from, the heart circumcised** (Isa 1:16; Jer 4:4; 25:5; 26:3; Job 11:14; 36:10; Isa 55:7). Strong across both lemmas.
- **Heart as seat:** co-seated *lev* 5, *levav* 3, *nephesh* 2 = 10; 13 heart/soul/inward locations (§2).
- **Comparatively self-standing:** touch into M10 is light — *sur*[R2] ×4, *ra'sha*/*to'evah*/*shiqquts*/*re'sha*[M10b], *av'lah*/*av'val*[R2], *chat'tat*[CORE], *ba'gad*[R1]. Unlike B2's heavy M10c defilement bridge, B3 travels mostly with external trouble/deceit/lies terms (*a'mal*, *mir'mah*, *she'qer*).

## 6. The M10b picture so far (held open — three batches in)
- **B1 wicked (*ra'sha*):** whole-person moral **verdict** (status, moral_evaluation), bounded interior, defined by the *tsaddiq* antithesis; five faces incl. turn-and-live and theodicy.
- **B2 abomination (*to'evah*/*shiqquts*):** **detestableness** verdict relational to God; interior in Wisdom; **heavy M10c defilement bridge**; abomination of desolation.
- **B3 evil (*a'ven*/*ro'a*):** the **most interior** — evil **conceived/devised in the heart, worked out, and to be turned from**; dual moral+affect; self-standing.
A gradient is emerging from *verdict* (B1) → *relational detestableness* (B2) → *interior process* (B3) — **recorded as a pattern to test against B4 (Greek evil) and B5, not a conclusion.**
