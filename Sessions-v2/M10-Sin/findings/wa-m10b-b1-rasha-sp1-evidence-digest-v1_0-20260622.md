## 2. What ra.sha is (evidence)
- **The wicked person** — the standing noun-of-character "the wicked / the guilty / the criminal." Verdict on a person, not a movement within one.
- **Defining axis: righteous vs wicked (*tsaddiq*).** Nearly every verse pairs *ra'sha* with *tsaddiq / tsadeq / tsedaqah* (Gen 18:23,25; Exo 23:7; Deu 25:1; 1 Ki 8:32; 2 Ch 6:23; 2 Sa 4:11; Job 9:22). The antonym sits in the **righteousness field (external to M10)** — *ra'sha* is defined by what it is *not*, more than by co-occurrence with the sin-core (which it touches only lightly: *cha.ta* ×1, *chat.tat* ×1).
- **Forensic "guilty / in the wrong."** In court settings *ra'sha* = the party condemned: "condemning the **guilty**… vindicating the righteous" (Deu 25:1–2; 1 Ki 8:32; 2 Ch 6:23); "the man **in the wrong**" (Exo 2:13); Pharaoh's confession "I and my people are **in the wrong**" (Exo 9:27, with *cha.ta*).
- **Job — the fate / portion of the wicked.** Job concentrates on destiny: the wicked's light is put out (18:5; 21:17), their hope fails (11:20), their joy is brief (20:5), "the counsel of the wicked is far from me" (21:16; 22:18), "the portion of a wicked man with God" (20:29; 27:13).

## 3. Inner-being threads
Sparse, as expected for a person-level verdict:
- Job 11:20 — location=soul, *ne.phesh* co-seated, but ="life" ("their hope is to breathe their last") — the **person/life idiom artefact**, not a seat.
- The wicked's **counsel** (*e.tsah*, Job 10:3; 21:16; 22:18) — a cognitive *output* of the wicked, but *e.tsah* is a co-term, not a seat within *ra'sha*.

## 4. Field hygiene
- **faculty=moral_evaluation ×41** — term-intrinsic (the verdict "wicked"), not a seat. Consistent with *ra'sha* being a moral classification.
- co-seated: only *ne.phesh* ×1 (artefact, above).
- origin=received-from-outside ×3 — on "the counsel of the wicked is far **from** me" (Job 21:16; 22:18) and Job 38:13; reflects the "from" preposition, not a true origin claim. Minor.

## 5. Conceptual note (evidence, not yet synthesis)
*ra'sha* describes the **moral status of the person** — "the wicked one" — paralleling the core insight that *sin describes the status of the inner being, not a characteristic of it*. M10b wickedness looks, on this first pass, like a **person-level verdict** rather than an inner-being faculty-state. To be tested across SP2–SP4 and the rest of M10b.
