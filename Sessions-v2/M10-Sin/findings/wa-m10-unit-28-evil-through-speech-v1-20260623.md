### A1. Structural signature — speech as ACTION (B5 audit; B4 audit)
- ***blasfēmeō* is type=action, valence=sinful 11/11** (one forbidden, Rom 14:16) — it names a **deed of the mouth**, not a standing status. This distinguishes #28 (the verbal **act**) from the constitutive-nature unit #24 (the **quality/status** the act flows from) and from the speech-noun unit #9 (the *blasfēmia* noun — see A6).
- ***kakopoios* mixes status and action:** "evildoer" as a person-label (1 Pe 2:12,14; 4:15 — type=status) and "doing evil" as a deed (Joh 18:30 — type=action; 1 Pe 3:16 "slandered as evildoers" — the slander-deed). ***atopos*** = quality ("nothing **wrong/out-of-place**", Act 25:5).
- **faculty = moral_evaluation** is term-intrinsic; minimal interior (these are speech/conduct terms — B5 §2). The unit's interior content reads from the **co-terms and the hardened-will pattern** (A4), not from the terms' own seating.

### A2. blasfēmeō — the verbal sin against GOD'S NAME (the defiant face) ⚑ SYNTH
- The core of #28: **blaspheming/cursing God's name**, climaxing in the **unrepentant defiance under judgment** (B5 §2; Bsum §2): "the name of God is **blasphemed** among the Gentiles **because of you**" (Rom 2:24, cause = Israel's disobedience); "**blaspheming** his name and his dwelling" (Rev 13:6); and the Revelation bowls — "they **cursed** the God of heaven… and **did not repent**" (Rev 16:9, touch *metanoeō*; +*exousia*), "**cursed** the God of heaven for their pain… and did not repent of their deeds" (16:11, +*ponos* travail, *metanoeō*), "**cursed** God for the plague of the hail" (16:21, cause = the severe plague falling on people). ⚑ SYNTH — **the hardened will reads divine judgment as occasion for further defiance, not repentance** — judgment intensifies the blasphemy rather than breaking it. This defiance-under-judgment pattern is the distinctive inner content of #28. **Surface.**

### A3. blasfēmeō — slander of persons / the good / spiritual beings (the hostile face)
- The same verb also **slanders humans, the good, and spiritual beings** (B5 §2): "why am I still being **denounced** as a sinner?" / "let not your good be **slandered**" (Rom 3:8 "slanderously charged"; 14:16, touch *agathos* — the good misrepresented as evil); "when **slandered**, we entreat" (1 Cor 4:13, *parakaleō*); "do not let what you eat with thanks be **denounced**" (1 Cor 10:30, *charis*/*eucharisteō*); and **blaspheming spiritual beings/glories** ("**blaspheme** the glorious ones", Jude 8; "**blaspheme** all that they do not understand", Jude 10). The verb covers the whole field of **hostile/deceptive speech** — against God, against the good, against persons, against glories.

### A4. ⚑ SYNTH — the inner pattern: a hardened will inverting moral reality
- Drawing the faces together (held open): #28 evidences **three speech-modes of the same inner wickedness** — (i) **defiant blasphemy** against God, hardened against repentance even under judgment (A2); (ii) **hostile slander** charging the innocent / the good (A3, the *kakopoios* slander of 1 Pe 3:16 "slandered as evildoers"); (iii) **deceptive misrepresentation** that inverts moral reality (the good "slandered" as evil, Rom 14:16; calling Jesus a *kakopoios*, "if this man were not **doing evil** we would not have delivered him", Joh 18:30 — the wicked label the innocent). ⚑ SYNTH — the unit's interior is **the hardened will that reads judgment/the-good through inverted moral categories**, expressed outward as speech. Evidence only; not resolved.

### A5. ↔ BOND — blasphemy also DEFILES the flesh (the M10c link) ↔ BOND

Blasphemy is not only verbal but **defiling** — bridging #28 to the defilement units (#29–32). (B5 §2; Bsum §3.)
