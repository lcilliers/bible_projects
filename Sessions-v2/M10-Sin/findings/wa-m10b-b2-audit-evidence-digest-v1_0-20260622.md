## 2. The structural signature of the abomination-family
- **type = status 139 / 140** (the one exception, Hos 9:10 "**became** detestable", is *quality* — a transformation, see §4). The family is a **verdict of detestableness**, not an inner movement.
- **Relational object:** object_type = God 21 + the pervasive "to the Lord / in my sight" frame; the rest are abstract practices (25), idols/things (15), spiritual-beings (3 — the foreign gods *are* "the abomination of" their nations), situations (7), persons (7). Abomination is fixed by **what God (or, reciprocally, a moral agent) detests**.

## 3. THE dominant structural finding — the M10c defilement bridge
The abomination-family is interwoven with **defilement** more tightly than with anything else: **~23 touches to M10c** — *ta'me* ×15, *nid'dah* ×3, *tum'ah* ×1, plus the purity contrasts *ta'her*/*ta'hor* ×3 and *akathartos* ×1. Abominations **defile the sanctuary, the land, the self** (Eze 5:11; 20:7; 37:23; Jer 7:30; 32:34; 2 Ki 23:13; 2 Ch 36:14; Ezr 9:11). *to'evah* and *shiqquts* travel together ("their detestable things and their abominations") and both defile. **This is the strongest cross-satellite link in M10b — M10b(abomination) ↔ M10c(defilement) are structurally joined.** (Hold for synergy.)

## 4. Interior threads (real but bounded; held open)
~9 heart engagements + soul/transformation notes:
- **The heart goes after them:** "those whose **heart** goes after their detestable things" (Eze 11:21); "the **eyes** feast on" them (Eze 20:7).
- **The heart is the abomination:** crooked/arrogant heart (Pro 11:20; 16:5); "seven abominations in his **heart**" (Pro 26:25).
- **The soul delights in them:** "their **soul** delights in their abominations" (Isa 66:3, *nephesh* + *chaphets*).
- **Values inversion:** "what is exalted among men is an **abomination** in the sight of God… God knows your **hearts**" (Luk 16:15, *kardia*).
- **Becoming what you love:** "they **became detestable like the thing they loved**" (Hos 9:10) — the one *quality*-typed occurrence; idolatry assimilates the worshipper to the detestable object.
- **Shame axis:** self-loathing in the penitent (Eze 36:31) vs **shamelessness** in the hardened ("did not know how to blush", Jer 6:15; 8:12).
