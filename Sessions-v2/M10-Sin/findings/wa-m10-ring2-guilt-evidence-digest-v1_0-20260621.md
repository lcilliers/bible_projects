## 3. Inner-being threads (documented, not concluded)

Four genuine threads, recorded as evidence for the synthesis:

- **The conscience operation: realize-guilt → confess.** The recurring Levitical sequence — "when he comes to know it and **realizes his guilt**… and **confesses** the sin" (Lev 4:13, 4:22, 4:27, 5:3–5, 6:4; Num 5:6). *a.sham* is the verb of coming-to-awareness of guilt. This is the strongest inner-being *process* in the band — guilt as something the inner being comes to *know*.
- **Guilt borne / mounting as a weight.** "Now they must **bear their guilt**" with "their **heart** is false" (Hos 10:2, *lev* co-seated); "our **guilt has mounted up to the heavens**" (Ezr 9:6); "in great guilt" (Ezr 9:7,13,15). Guilt behaves as an accumulating, borne liability — a status one is under.
- **Guilt coupled with shame.** In the Ezra confession guilt rises alongside shame: "I am **ashamed** and **blush**… our guilt mounted up" (Ezr 9:6, *bosh*/*kalam*; *bo.shet* Ezr 9:7). Guilt and shame co-occur in the inner being.
- **Guilt drives the turn to God.** "until they **acknowledge their guilt** and **seek my face**, and in their distress earnestly seek me" (Hos 5:15). Guilt-awareness moves the person Godward.

Also notable: Isa 53:10 — "when his **soul** makes an offering for guilt" (the Servant's *nephesh* bearing the guilt-offering); and the guilt-offering noun *a.sham* (1 Sa 6; Pro 14:9; Ezr 10:19) is the **remedy** sense embedded here — cross-reference to Ring 4 (remedy).
