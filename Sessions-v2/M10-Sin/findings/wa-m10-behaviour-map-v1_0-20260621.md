## 1. Approach (researcher-directed)

We are not assuming M10 resolves into characteristics. We assemble **collections of similar behaviour** — grouping occurrences by how the term *operates* in its verse — and let the relationships between collections clarify before we decide whether each becomes a characteristic, a status, or some construct we don't yet have a precedent for. There is currently **no precedent for "status"** in the programme; these collections are the raw material from which one (or several) may be defined.

**What "behaviour" means here.** Each occurrence's behaviour is carried by its read-resolved fields: its grammatical **kind** (act / state / character), its **valence** (wrong vs prescribed-remedy), whose it is (**stance**: self / other / addressed), what it is **against** (God / person / abstract), where it is **seated** (soul / heart / flesh…), whether it is **received from outside**, and **God's role** toward it. These are verse-read evidence, not imposed categories.

**Honest limit.** The lexical fields encode only a fraction of what each verse says (per dos-and-donts §0). This assembly is therefore a *scaffold*: the binding behaviour of each collection is **confirmed and refined by reading its verses** in the next stage. No finding is asserted here (GR-PROC-002 / GR-PROG-009).

## 4. What this is already showing (observation only — not findings)

- The cluster's behaviour is dominated by **state over act over character** (805 / 410 / 84) — consistent with "a series of statuses" more than a set of affects.
- A real **stance axis** exists: sin is mostly *attributed to another* (642) but a substantial band is *owned by self* (209) and another *charged to the hearer* (175). How a person stands toward sin — owns it, is charged with it, or names it in a third party — is itself behaviour worth a collection.
- The **inner-being seat** is engaged in only ~10% of occurrences, and when it is, it is **soul and heart** far more than flesh. Where sin is seated is a narrow but sharp signal.
- The **remedy** band behaves as a clean inverse and may belong to a different construct entirely.

These are seams in the data, held open — not conclusions about what *type* each collection is.
