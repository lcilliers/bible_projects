## 4. Why it went astray, and the standing discipline (carry forward)

The researcher named the failure modes explicitly. They are recorded here so the next session does not repeat them.

**The drift, in two forms:**
1. **Analytics before evidence.** The corpus's size/complexity pulled the session into deriving meaning, typing characteristics, and running synergy **before** all evidence (core + rings + M10b + M10c) was gathered. The symptom: *"when a digest starts hardening into a thesis, that is the symptom to stop."*
2. **Conflating the batching step with the evidence step.** The correct next step was small — *read one field (the lemma) per verse, reduce to the unique-lemma list, and use that list as the batch entity.* Instead the session began hand-writing interpreted per-verse analysis lemma-by-lemma, producing neither a clean batch list nor clean evidence — and at ~1,900 verses it could not finish.

**The named standing failure mode (researcher's own words):** *"I default to imposing structure and over-building, and I scale before confirming the step."* Corrective: **confirm the exact step and its output shape on ONE tiny example, get a yes, then scale** — and keep batching separate from evidencing.

**The governing discipline (GR-PROG-001/005 + researcher rules):**
- **Build & synergise EVIDENCE first; derive MEANING last.** Invent nothing unsupported; let meaning follow the evidence, even where uncomfortable. **No characteristic/status typing, no cross-ring/cross-cluster synergy, no conclusions until all evidence is gathered.**
- **Read for *difference*, not confirmation.** "There is more to discover, more diversity to add, more nuances to uncover."
- **Verse-first:** read every focus occurrence in full (lemma_meaning + verse text + the co-term web together). **No sampling.**
- **Two-AI split:** Claude AI reads/judges/authors; **CC does all DB work.** CAI proposes no DB writes — only "CC verify-list" flags for researcher review.
- **Seat the inner being via the `co-seated` compound role**, not the sparse `location`/`faculty` fields (§6.3).

## 6. Consolidated evidence (held open — observations, not conclusions)

> **Trust caveat for this whole section.** This evidence was gathered under the **core-and-rings architecture** (M10) and a **term-keyed, status-leaning** reading (M10b). ### 6.1 M10 Rings

**Ring 1 — qualifiers/dynamics (9 lemmas, 137 occ).** *ma'al* (unfaithfulness, 62), *ba'gad* (treachery, 39), *a'vah* (bend/distort, 16), *sa'rah* (defection, 8), *sa'laph* (pervert, 6), *deleazō* (entice, 3), *pu'qah* (staggering of conscience, 1), *apostasia* (1), *sunupokrinomai* (1).
- *ma'al* **fuses to the core** — co-occurs with *cha'ta*/*chat'tat*/*a'von* 17×; Levitical formula "commits breach of faith **and sins**" (Lev 5:15; 6:2; Num 5:6).
- *a'vah* pairs with *cha'ta* 5× in confession "we have **sinned** and acted **perversely**" (1 Ki 8:47; 2 Ch 6:37).
- *ba'gad* sits on a fidelity/righteousness axis (touches core only 2×; field = *ra'sha*/*tsad'diq*/*be'rit*).
- Co-seats recorded: *a'vah*→heart/turning; *sa'rah*→heart + somatic; *deleazō*→desire/soul/greed; *pu'qah*→heart+conscience.
- **Stem evidence:** *a'vah* dominantly Hiphil (causative "make crooked"); *sa'laph* Piel (intensive); *ba'gad* Qal.
- **Cautions:** *nephesh*=person idiom on the Levitical *ma'al* laws (not a seat); spurious origin tags (Pro 2:22; 2 Ch 26:18); `object` sparse (53/137 verbs lack it).

**Ring 2 — extensions/aspects (24 lemmas, 207 occ).** Five sub-fields: guilt, injustice, transgression-status (Gk), perversity, blasphemy.
- **Guilt (a'sham 40, ash'mah 17, a'shem 3 = 60):** the **liability the core incurs** (touches *cha'ta*×7, *chat'tat*×5, *a'von*×5). Inner-being *awareness*: "realizes his guilt… and **confesses**" (Lev 4–6; Num 5:6). Borne as weight: "bear their guilt" + "false **heart**" (Hos 10:2); "guilt mounted to the heavens" (Ezr 9:6). Drives turning: "acknowledge guilt and **seek my face**" (Hos 5:15). Coupled with shame (Ezr 9:6–7). Servant's *soul* makes guilt-offering (Isa 53:10 — remedy cross-ref).
- **Injustice (av'lah, a'vel, adikos 11 …, 77):** against righteousness; heart-seated ("in your **hearts** you devise wrongs" Psa 58:2; 64:6). Ezekiel refrain: the righteous who "turns from righteousness and does injustice" dies (Eze 3:20; 18:24–26; 33:13–18).
- **Transgression-status, Gk (paraptōma, parabasis…, 30):** juridical/relational; minimal interior; *paraptōma* is **remedy-adjacent** (paired with grace — Rom 5; Eph 2:1,5; 2 Cor 5:19).
- **Perversity (tah'pu'khah…, 18):** heart-seated — "perverted **heart** devises evil" (Pro 6:14); "your **heart** utters perverse things" (Pro 23:33).
- **Blasphemy (22):** two senses — against God/Spirit (Mat 12:31; Joh 10:33) and slander of people (Mar 7:22; Eph 4:31). Decisive heart-seating: "out of the **heart**… slander" (Mat 15:19).
- **Ring-2 audit:** of 207 — faculty only 16 (NT moral_evaluation/affect; Hebrew aspect-terms lack the field), but **8 genuine heart-seatings** (Psa 58:2; 64:6; 53:1; Pro 6:14; 23:33; Hos 10:2; Mat 15:19; Job 34:10) — the cleanest inner-being evidence in Ring 2. **Pattern: faculty empty but the heart engaged via co-occurrence** — the trigger for the co-seated index (§6.3).

**Ring 3 — consequences (7 lemmas, 38 occ).** *Heterogeneous — to be split at synthesis.*
- (a) External agents/instruments/places (Passover destroyer Exo 12:13; destroying weapon Eze 9:1; trap Jer 5:26; place-name 2 Ki 23:13) — not sin, not inner being.
- (b) Physical/cosmic decay (*fthora* of body/creation Rom 8:21; 1 Cor 15:42,50; *diaftheirō* outer self 2 Cor 4:16) — outcome, not inner being.
- (c) **Genuine moral corruption operating on the inner being** — sits nearer *dynamics* than consequences: corrupts "good morals" (1 Cor 15:33); thoughts led "astray" (*noēma*, 2 Cor 11:3); "old self corrupt through **deceitful desires**" (*epithumia*, Eph 4:22); "depraved in **mind**" (*nous*, 1 Ti 6:5). Touches nous/noēma/sarx/epithumia.
- **Caution:** *cha'val* lemma_meaning "to bind" **not borne out** (act-corruptly / be-broken / destroy) — CC re-gloss candidate. *mash'chit* multi-sense (destroyer / trap / place-name).

**Ring 4 — remedy (kip'pu'rim, 8 occ).** Cultic covering; masculine-plural noun (provision, not act). Core synergy: *ka'phar* "cover/atone" ×8 + *chat'tat* "sin offering" ×3. Largely objective/external (altar, lives, persons). **Inner thread (documented):** Day of Atonement "**afflict your souls**" (*nephesh*, Lev 23:27); atonement "for your **lives**" (Exo 30:16). **Remedy extent held open:** kip'pu'rim (8) **+** the "sin-offering" sense embedded in core *chat'tat* (≈102) **+** "guilt-offering" in *a'sham* (≈9) — to be assembled together at pull-together (so the CORE must be read before remedy can be closed).

### 6.2 M10b — wickedness / abomination / evil (588 occ, all read; five families)

**Grand structural signature:** type = **status 492/588 (84%)**, quality 65 (Greek "evil" adjective), action 31 (Hebrew worked-evil). Consistent with the working "sin = status" hypothesis — *but this is the lean to be tested, not a verdict.*

- **B1 — ra'sha (the wicked person; 248 + re'sha 9 + mirsha'at 1).** type=status 258/258; faculty=moral_evaluation 258/258 (term-intrinsic — defines the wicked, **not** an inner state). Defined by antithesis with *tsad'diq*. **The FOUR/FIVE FACES (held open, coexisting):**
  1. **Forensic verdict** — the guilty party in court (Deu 25:1; 1 Ki 8:32; 2 Ch 6:23).
  2. **Characterised interior** (bounded to Psalms/Proverbs) — heart-denial (Psa 10:13), soul-desire (Psa 10:3), pride, concealed evil (Psa 28:3).
  3. **Fixed two-destinies antithesis** — cut off / perish / chaff (Psa 1:4; 37 passim; Pro 10–15); dominant after recovery.
  4. **Reversible condition** — summoned to **turn and live** (*shuv*/*chayah*/*salach*/*racham*; Eze 18:21,23,27; 33:11; Isa 55:7).
  5. **Theodicy / wisdom-crisis** — the wicked who *prospers* (Psa 73:3,12; Ecc 7:15; 8:14; 9:2) — newly prominent after the +69 truncation recovery.
  - **Caution:** genuine wicked-*heart* seatings are only ~5 (Psa 10:13; 28:3; Pro 10:20; 15:28; 21:4); the interior is **bounded, not pervasive** (recovery added almost none). *nephesh* co-seats (14) are mostly "life/person" idiom.
- **B2 — abomination (to'evah 108, shiqquts 25, bdelugma 6, bdeluktos 1 = 140).** type=status 139/140; verdict fixed by **what God detests** (object: God 21, practices 25, idols 15). **The M10c bridge: ~23 touches** (*ta'me*/*nid'dah*/*tum'ah*) — abominations **defile** sanctuary/land/self (Eze 5:11; 20:7; 37:23; Jer 7:30). Faces: Law (cultic verdict, no interior) · Wisdom (heart-engaged: crooked/arrogant heart Pro 11:20; 16:5; abomination/delight polarity Pro 11:1; 12:22; 15:8) · Ezekiel (catalogue; heart "goes after" 6:9; 11:21; self-loathing 36:31) · Histories/Prophets (idol; pagan imitation; shamelessness "did not blush" Jer 6:15; 8:12). **Shame ↔ shamelessness axis** flagged.
- **B3 — Hebrew evil (a'ven 69, ro'a 14 = 83).** **The most interior, process-oriented M10b family** (~16% interior — highest). type more action-typed; *a'ven* faculty=[affect, moral_evaluation] on all 69 (dual). **Signature: the generative metaphor — evil is conceived and given birth** ("conceives evil… gives birth to lies" Psa 7:14; "conceive trouble… birth evil… womb prepares deceit" Job 15:35; "conceive mischief… birth iniquity" Isa 59:4 — across three corpora). Evil **devised in the heart** (Pro 6:18; Isa 32:6; Jer 4:14 "wash your heart"), **chosen / turned from** (*bachar*/*sur*/*shuv*). *ro'a* runs two streams: moral ("evil of deeds", object of turn-from) and affective ("sadness of heart" Ecc 7:3; Neh 2:2).
- **B4 — Greek evil (ponēros 63, kakia 11, ponēria 7, kakopoios 5, atopos 1, faulos 1 = 88).** type=quality 64. **Spans the whole range at once** — and carries one face unique to Greek: **"the evil one" (Satan / the cosmic adversary)**, ~11 by referent (Mat 6:13; 13:19,38; Joh 17:15; Eph 6:16; 1 Jo 2:13–14; 5:18–19) — **no Hebrew M10/M10b term carries this.** Strong interior (evil heart/thoughts/treasure: Mat 9:4; 12:34–35; 15:19; Mar 7:21–23; Heb 3:12), **conscience** thread (*suneidesis*: Heb 10:22; 1 Pe 3:16 — direct contact with the M10 core), good/evil polarity (*agathos*), malice as the vice **to put away** (1 Pe 2:1; Col 3:8; Eph 4:31).
- **B5 — NT wrongdoing (blasfēmeō 11, adikia 8).** *blasfēmeō* = the **verbal sin** against God's name (Rom 2:24; Rev 16:9,11,21); also defiles the flesh (Jude 8, *miainō* — M10c link). *adikia* = unrighteousness, antithesis of righteousness/truth (Rom 1:18; 3:5), forgivable under the new covenant (Heb 8:12).

**M10b cross-cutting findings (held open):**
- **Not a developmental line.** An initial B1→B2→B3 gradient (verdict → detestableness → interior) **did not survive B4**, which spans everything at once. Hold the families as **co-present facets** of the evil/wickedness field, not a sequence.
- **Interior concentrates in the *evil/abomination* terms (a'ven, ponēros, to'evah, ro'a), NOT in the *wicked-person* term ra'sha.**
- **Recurring across families:** righteous/wicked & good/evil **antithesis**; **turn-from / put-away** (repentance & reform); relation to **God's revulsion/wrath**; **defilement** link to M10c.
- **M10b ↔ M10c is structurally joined** (26 touches, strongest from B2). This is the concrete evidence that the satellites belong together.

### 7.3 The corrected per-verse evidence schema — THE CROWN JEWEL of the redesign
`wa-m10-perverse-schema-spec-v1_0-20260622.md` — the method the continuation should run. Read it in full; the essentials:
- **Unit = the whole verse** with **all** active terms (focus + co-terms); overlapping verses evidenced **once**, keyed to the full term inventory.
- **~18 lexical fields**; the derivation rules are non-negotiable:
  1. **Measure, not English** — every value from the original-language measure (lemma · morphology · per-occurrence `sense` · co-occurring tagged terms), never the English string.
  2. **Silence = NONE** — an absent field is genuine silence; say "silent," never impute.
  3. **`object_type` is authoritative; `object` text is a hint** (~13% imprecise) — trust `object_type` on disagreement.
  4. **`faculty` is TERM-INTRINSIC ONLY** — the **verse's** faculty is built **from the co-terms**, not from the head term's faculty. (This is precisely what Pass-1 got wrong.)
  5. **`location` = a constitutional-seat lemma present in the verse** (sense-gated); but **seating is more reliably read from the `co-seated` compound role** (§6.3).
  6. **`origin`** partial (from-source preposition only); **`cause`** read-resolved from the ki/hoti/gar clause (`cause_clause` is a superseded hint).
  7. **`valence` held OPEN as a tag, not a verdict** — flag where it over-applies (tagged "sinful" on *lev* "heart", on ritual contact-uncleanness).
  8. **`compound` = the synergy web** (partner / qualifier / co-seated); fan out to each co-term's full record.
- **"Evidencing a verse" =** read from the measure → evaluate **every** term's **every** field (honouring silence, trusting object_type, faculty term-intrinsic) → relate terms through the compound web → state the verse's inner-being reality **neutrally**, flagging extract tags that over-reach. **No architecture, no status/characteristic verdict, no cross-verse synthesis at the evidence stage.**
