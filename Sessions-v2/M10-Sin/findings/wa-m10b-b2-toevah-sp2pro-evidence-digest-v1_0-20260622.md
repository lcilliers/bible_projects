## 1. Coverage
*to'evah* in Proverbs = **21/108** (incl. the double at Pro 29:27), all read. The Wisdom profile **differs from the Law's** in three ways recorded below.

## 2. NEW vs the Law — abomination enters the interior
The Law located *to'evah* only in conduct/objects. Proverbs places it **in the heart**:
- "Those of **crooked heart** are an abomination to the Lord" (Pro 11:20, *lev* co-seated).
- "Everyone who is **arrogant in heart** is an abomination to the Lord" (16:5, *lev*).
- "there are **seven abominations in his heart**" (26:25, location=heart) — the deceiver's interior.
- "The **thoughts of the wicked** are an abomination to the Lord" (15:26, *machashavah*).
- The Pro 6:16–19 list ("seven that are an abomination to him", *nephesh*="himself") includes "a **heart that devises** wicked plans."
So in Wisdom, the abomination is not only what one *does* but what one *is within* — the proud/crooked/devising heart.

## 3. NEW — the abomination / delight polarity (to'evah ↔ ratson)
Four times *to'evah* is set against *ratson* (delight / acceptance / favour): "A false balance is an abomination… a just weight is his **delight**" (11:1); crooked heart vs blameless = abomination vs delight (11:20); lying lips vs faithful = abomination vs delight (12:22); the wicked's sacrifice vs the upright's prayer (15:8). Abomination is the **negative pole of God's regard** — the structural opposite of what he accepts.

## 4. NEW — reciprocity / relative valence
Abomination is not only "to the Lord"; what one detests tracks one's moral orientation:
- "to turn away from evil is an **abomination to fools**" (13:19) — the fool's revulsion at repentance.
- "the scoffer is an **abomination to mankind**" (24:9).
- "An unjust man is an abomination **to the righteous**, but one whose way is straight is an abomination **to the wicked**" (29:27) — mutual revulsion.
- Wisdom's own posture: "wickedness is an abomination **to my lips**" (8:7).

## 5. Detestable conduct (continuing the Law's relational core, object_type=God)
False balance / unequal weights (11:1; 20:10,23, *mirmah*), lying lips (12:22, *sheqer*), the devious person (3:32), justifying the wicked / condemning the righteous (17:15), the wicked's sacrifice/way (15:8,9; 21:27 "with **evil intent**"), prayer of one who won't hear the law (28:9).

## 6. Touch points & seating
- co-seated: *lev* (heart) ×2 + the Pro 26:25 heart location; *nephesh* ×2 (6:16 God's "self"; 13:19 the fool's soul-desire).
- Touch: ***ra'sha*[M10b] ×5** — the wicked's sacrifice/way/thoughts are the abomination (a strong *to'evah*↔*ra'sha* bridge within M10b); *a'vel*[R2] ×2 (injustice); *ta'hor*[M10c] ×1 (15:26 "gracious words are **pure**" — abomination vs purity); *chat'tat*[CORE] ×1 (24:9 "the devising of folly is **sin**").

## 7. Held open
Across SP1–SP2, *to'evah* shows two faces: the **Law's** cultic/conduct verdict (no interior) and the **Wisdom's** interior-engaging, polarity-structured, reciprocal abomination. Both relational to God's revulsion, but Wisdom extends the object of that revulsion into the heart. Ezekiel (SP3, 41 occ) next — the "abominations" of Jerusalem and the temple. No reading adopted.
