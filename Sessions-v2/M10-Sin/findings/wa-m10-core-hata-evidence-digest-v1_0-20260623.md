## 2. Diversity observed (faces — HELD OPEN, not conclusions)

- **chat.tat H2403B = "sin" (the standing wrong):** 160 occ, all `status`, all `sinful`; top sense sin 88 / sins 67.
- **chat.tat H2403H = "sin offering" (the cultic remedy):** 110 occ, all `status`, sense **sin offering 102 + sin offerings 4**; valence neutral 57 / commanded 31 / (none) 7 / sinful 14 / righteous 1.
- **Cultic concentration:** the 107 sin-offering verses cluster in **Lev 47 · Num 36 · Exo 3 · Eze 14 · 2Ch 3** (examples: Exo 29:14,36; 30:10; Lev 4:8,20,21,24,25; Lev 16:30 "atonement… cleanse you… clean from all your sins"). The word that names the offense also names its purification (Num 15:27 carries BOTH in one verse: cha.ta "to sin" + chat.tat "sin offering"). Recorded as the group's defining face; treated carefully — the offering occ are cultic, **not sinful-valence**.

**(b) The verb cha.ta as the act of sinning, in three registers.** H2398 (211 action occ; faculty=moral_evaluation; topsense sinned 79 / sin 51 / committed 19):
- **(b1) Levitical legal "if anyone sins" frame — unintentional sin + conscience-on-awareness.** "If anyone sins **unintentionally** in any of the Lord's commandments" (Lev 4:2,22,27; 5:15; Num 15:24,27,28); "and realizes his guilt" (Lev 4:27); "If anyone sins in that he hears a public adjuration… yet does not speak, he shall bear his iniquity" (Lev 5:1). Object_type frequently (none) or abstract; the sin is defined by the breached commandment, not a governed object.
- **(b2) Prophetic / penitential register.** Psa 51:2,4,5 ("Against you, you only, have I sinned… in sin did my mother conceive me"); 2Sa 24:10 ("I have sinned greatly"); 1Ki 8:47 ("We have sinned and have acted perversely"); Exo 32:30 ("You have sinned a great sin"). **(c) The sinner as a moral character — chat.ta (H2400).** All 19 occ are `quality` / `moral_evaluation` / `sinful` (topsense sinners 15 / sinner 6): Gen 13:13 ("the men of Sodom were wicked, great sinners against the Lord"); Psa 1:1,5; 25:8; 26:9; 51:13; 104:35; Pro 1:10; 13:21; 23:17; Isa 1:28; 13:9; 33:14; Amo 9:8,10; Num 32:14; 1Sa 15:18; 1Ki 1:21. **(d) The status-nouns chet / cha.ta.ah — sin as a borne, recorded, removable standing.** chet (H2399, 33 status) and cha.ta.ah (H2401, 8 status) behave as an object that is pardoned / cleansed / written: Isa 40:2 ("her sins… double from the Lord's hand"); Jer 17:1 ("The sin of Judah is **written with a pen of iron**… engraved on the tablet of their heart"); Lev 16:30 ("clean… from all your sins"). ## 5. Held open (no conclusions)

Whether the offering belongs in the sin characteristic at all, or only in forgiveness, is held open (see §10 note).
- Whether (b1) unintentional, (b2) penitential, and (b3) relational "sin against" are one phenomenon of cha.ta in different settings, or distinct faces — the verb carries all three with one form.
- The Levitical "if anyone sins" legal frame (Lev) vs the prophetic/penitential register (Psa 51) vs the relational "sinned against God/you" register — recorded as three settings, not resolved into one.
- Interior bounded to ≈15 genuine heart/flesh seats — do not over-read sin as heart-seated; the nephesh occ are mostly life/person idiom.

## §10 note — status vs characteristic (the remedy seam)
The ḥāṭāʾ group forces the question for the whole cluster: **chat.tat carries both the sin and the sin-offering in one lemma.** If M10 "Sin" is the *status/characteristic* of being-in-sin, the **sin-offering (H2403H, 110 occ, neutral/commanded valence, Lev/Num cultic)** may not belong to the sin-status unit at all — it is the *removal* of the status, properly the remedy/forgiveness unit (#14). Recorded as the central status-vs-characteristic test for M10: is "sin offering" a face of sin, or of its undoing? Held open.
