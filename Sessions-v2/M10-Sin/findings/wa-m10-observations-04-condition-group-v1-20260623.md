- **Sources read:** §A of the sin-noun family (#11/#12), #24, and the defilement family (#29–31).

## Observations (each grounded)

**O1 — A condition/state is a STANDING MODE the inner being is IN or UNDER — not an act, not a faculty in operation.**
- *Evidence:* #11 "the **state under which every person stands**" (Rom 3:23; "all… **under sin**" Rom 3:9; "imprisoned everything **under sin**" Gal 3:22); #24 "the **structural condition** of the inner person"; defilement "the inner-being **condition** of ritual uncleanness". type reads `status` (the sin-nouns; adikia 8/8) / `quality` (#24's "evil" adjective).
- *So:* the object describes the inner being by its **situation/mode** — what it *is in / under* — not by something it *does*.

**O2 — Condition is a distinct lens from IDENTITY (Group 1), but they fuse.**
- *Evidence:* identity (#16/#23) classifies the **person** (what kind); condition (#11/#24/defilement) names the **state** the inner being is in. But #24's "**constitutive nature**" is both a condition *and* nearly an identity (the heart's default); #11's universal-condition shades into the sinner-type.
- *So:* a real distinction (state vs person-type) with **genuine overlap** at the edges — flag, don't force a clean line.

**O3 — The condition ARISES / HOLDS in different ways — four sub-types in the evidence.**
- **(a) UNIVERSAL / structural (#11):** the state **all stand under**, not acquired — the human situation. "All have sinned and fall short" (Rom 3:23); "death spread to all because all sinned" (Rom 5:12); innate: "in sin did my mother conceive me" (Psa 51:5).
- **(b) CONSTITUTIONAL / innate-nature (#24):** built **into the nature** — the heart an "**evil treasure**" the person draws on (Mat 12:34–35; Luk 6:45), "out of the heart come evil thoughts…" (Mat 15:19), "an **evil, unbelieving heart**" (Heb 3:12). The default output of the inner being.
- **(c) ENSLAVING POWER / bondage (#12):** sin as an **agent-force that masters the will** — it "**reigned**" (Rom 5:21), has "**dominion**" (Rom 6:14), persons "**enslaved to sin / sold under sin / set free from sin**" (Rom 6:6,16–22), it "**dwells within**" (Rom 7:17,20); "the **power** of sin" (1 Cor 15:56). The condition is **captivity**.
- **(d) CONTRACTED / ritual (#29–31):** a state **acquired by contact or authoritative verdict**, often **non-moral** (Lev 11–15; Num 19; menstruation; corpse-contact).
- *So:* "condition/state" is **not uniform** — it spans innate-universal, constitutional, bondage-to-a-power, and contracted-by-contact. The *how-it-holds* differs across the sub-types.

**O4 — At the bondage end (#12), CONDITION edges into AGENCY.**
- *Evidence:* #12 personifies sin as a sovereign — sin "reigns", "dwells", "enslaves", "deceived and killed" (Rom 7:11), wages war ("the law of sin", Rom 7:23). Parallel: #24's "**the evil one**" (Satan, ~11 occ — Mat 6:13; Eph 6:16; 1 Jo 5:19) is an **external personal agent**.
- *So:* the condition of bondage is **produced/held by an agent-like force** (sin-as-power), blurring **condition ↔ agency** (→ SDX-07). Held open: is sin-as-power a *condition* or an *agent*?

**O5 — Defilement proves a CONDITION can be NON-MORAL — the cleanest evidence that "state" is its own object-kind.**
- *Evidence:* defilement valence **neutral 84 / sinful 96**; ritual uncleanness (menstruation, corpse-contact, skin-disease) is **unclean but not sinful**.
- *So:* a "condition/state" can exist **without being a moral wrong** — decisively separating *state* from *sin-as-wrong*. This is the strongest single proof that condition/state is a distinct kind, not just "sin."

**O6 — The condition is defined RELATIONALLY — by its REMEDY and its OPPOSITE.**
- *Evidence:* defilement ↔ **purity (M12)** (the structural opposite, all defilement units); enslaving-sin ↔ **freedom** ("set free from sin… slaves of righteousness", Rom 6); universal-condition ↔ **grace/salvation** (#14; M38 51 shared verses); the **atonement seam** dissolves the recorded condition (Lev 16:30 "clean… from all your sins").
- *So:* a condition is described by **what dissolves it** (remedy) and its **pole-opposite** — directly linking SDX-02 (atonement) and SDX-05 (pole-pairs).

## Candidate label (held open)
**CONDITION / STATE** — a **standing mode the inner being is IN or UNDER** (not an act or operation), describing it by its **situation**. It arises variously — **universal** (the human situation), **constitutional** (built into the nature), **bondage** (captive to a mastering power), **contracted** (acquired by contact/verdict) — and is defined by its **remedy and opposite**. Crucially, it **need not be a moral wrong** (defilement). Distinct from *identity* (which classifies the person) though overlapping at the constitutional edge.

## §G — Bias-guard
- **⚠ condition ↔ identity overlap (O2)** — do not force a clean boundary. #24 is both a condition and a near-identity; #11 shades into #16. The lenses differ (state vs person-type) but the objects touch.
- **⚠ #24 "constitutional / humans constitutionally incline to evil"** rests on Mat 12:34–35 + the heart-treasury texts + Heb 3:12 — **~8 heart-seatings of 88** ponēros occ. Most ponēros is the bare adjective "evil" or the verdict-faces (evil generation/deeds/eye, A8). **Do not read the constitutional-heart claim as pervasively evidenced** — it is a strong frame on a bounded set (same caution as #23's "practical atheism").
- **⚠ #12 "the will captive / sin reigns"** is the **Pauline register** (Romans 5–8) specifically — powerful but register-bound; and it **edges into personification/agency** (O4). The `faculty=volition` tag on the hamart- nouns (cross-language seam) bears on whether sin-as-condition is a *status* or a *will-state* — surfaced, not resolved.
- **⚠ defilement's non-moral finding (O5)** is the **strongest leg** here (valence data) — but note ~31 of those verses were earlier scratch-read; the MC clean read confirms the distribution. Solid.
- The four sub-types (O3) are each grounded, but whether they are **one kind or several** is a §10 call, not settled by the evidence.
