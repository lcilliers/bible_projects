# M07 — Characteristic C: Conscience — shame at one's own sin
**File:** wa-m07-char-c-conscience-sin-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (adds verse-read analysis).
**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 22 — bosh, ka.lam, bo.shet, ke.lim.mah (OT penitential) + entrepō, entropē (NT admonitory). Testament: both.

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** Here shame is the **inward acknowledgment of one's own guilt before God** — penitent shame, felt *coram Deo*. Unlike characteristic A (shame at a failed reliance) the cause is moral: the person's own sin. Its definitive image is the unliftable face: "O my God, I am ashamed and blush to **lift my face to you**, my God, for our iniquities have risen higher than our heads" (Ezr 9:6); "to you, O Lord, belongs righteousness, but to us **open shame**… because we have **sinned against you**" (Dan 9:7-8). Shame is measured against God's righteousness and owned in confession.

**Two registers.** The OT material is penitential **confession** in prayer and covenant-lawsuit (Ezr 9; Dan 9; Jer 3:25; 31:19; Eze 16; 36; 43). The NT material is **admonitory** — corrective shame deliberately provoked to turn a believer back: "that he may be **ashamed**" (2Th 3:14), "I say this to your **shame**" (1Cor 6:5; 15:34), and pointedly *not* in contempt but in love: "I do not write these things to make you ashamed, but to admonish you as my **beloved children**" (1Cor 4:14).

**The purpose is stated, repeatedly — this is shame that works.** It is ordered toward turning: "after I was **instructed**, I struck my thigh; I was ashamed, and I was confounded" (Jer 31:19 → "I relented"); "that you may **remember and be confounded, and never open your mouth again** because of your shame, **when I atone for you**" (Eze 16:63); and it is the precondition God sets before restoration: "that they may be **ashamed of their iniquities**; and they shall measure the plan" of the temple (Eze 43:10-11). God even **commands** it: "Be ashamed and confounded for your ways, O house of Israel" (Eze 36:32). Shame here is the doorway God opens to confession, cessation, atonement, and restoration.

**The sequence and the faculties.** The verses lay out a movement — *instruction/memory → recognition of guilt → shame/confoundedness → bodily expression → turning*: Jer 31:19 (instructed → ashamed → confounded → struck the thigh → relented); Eze 16:61-63 (remember → be ashamed/confounded → silenced → atoned). It therefore engages **conscience** (the witness of guilt, Ezr 9:6), **memory** (Eze 16:61,63), **cognition** (knowing the sin, Dan 9:8), **moral evaluation** (the verdict against God's standard), **affect** (the blush/confoundedness), and **volition** (the turning, the silenced mouth). It integrates these into a single penitent response — conscientiousness in the full sense.

**The body.** Conviction shows outwardly: the unliftable face and blush (Ezr 9:6), the struck thigh (Jer 31:19), the silenced mouth (Eze 16:63), lying down in shame (Jer 3:25). Soul/heart expressing through the body.

**Significance for the inner being.** This characteristic is the **conscience operating rightly** — the inner witness that registers one's own wrong before a holy God and turns the person toward restoration. It is the morally-fitting use of the shame-faculty; its absence and inversion are characteristic C-neg. Crucially, the evidence shows shame functioning not as mere damage but as a God-given hinge of repentance and a precondition of atonement.

### Internal movements (grounded)
- **C1 Penitential confession before God:** Ezr 9:6,7; Dan 9:7,8; Jer 3:25.
- **C2 Instructed/remembered conviction → turning:** Jer 31:19; Eze 16:54,61,63; 36:32; 43:10,11.
- **C3 NT admonitory (corrective, in love):** 1Cor 4:14; 6:5; 15:34; 2Th 3:14; Tit 2:8.

### Pattern notes
- **Faculty:** affect (tagged); but the verses engage conscience, memory, cognition, moral-evaluation and volition together (field tags under-record this).
- **Experiencer:** self (confession) and other-addressed (covenant address / admonition). **Valence:** sinful / commanded / righteous — the moral weight is intrinsic.
- **Divine involvement:** addressee (prayed-to), agent/commander (Eze 36:32), atoner (Eze 16:63). **Seat:** face/body expression, memory, heart-turning (verse-located, not field-tagged).
- **Boundary:** vs A — the cause is one's *own sin* (not a failed trust); vs C-neg — this is the faculty *alive and operative*, C-neg its absence/inversion; vs D — penitent self-recognition, not an imposed standing.
