# M07 — Findings for Characteristic D: Disgrace as a borne condition / standing (imposed, deserved, or vicarious)
**File:** wa-m07-char-d-borne-disgrace-findings-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0**. Verse-read re-answer.
**Evidence:** 136 occ — the cluster's largest, most heterogeneous band: bosh, ke.lim.mah, bo.shet, qa.lon, bushah, ke.lim.mut, bosh.nah; with the disgrace pole of atimia/aischunē/entropē. It covers **disgrace one *carries*** — imposed by enemies, decreed in God's judgment, deserved by sin, or **borne innocently/vicariously**. Two sub-bands flagged: **D1 imposed-verdict**; **D8 vicarious/innocent disgrace** (Isa 50:6; Heb 12:2; Psa 69:7).

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — God stands at both ends: he **decrees disgrace in judgment** ("you shall **bear your disgrace**," Eze 16:52,54) and he **removes it** ("you will **forget the shame** of your youth," Isa 54:4). The **baseline** is imageal: "the man and his wife were both naked and were **not ashamed**" (Gen 2:25) — unfallen humanity without disgrace.
- **T0.1.2** — Borne by the creature; yet at the band's apex it is **borne by the Servant / by Christ** for others (Isa 50:6; Heb 12:2) — disgrace voluntarily assumed.
- **T0.2.1** — Disgrace functions as the **marked standing** of one exposed — by sin (Ezr 9:7), by judgment (Eze 16), by enemies (Psa 69:19), or **for God's sake** (Psa 69:7).
- **T0.2.2** — Gen 2:25 shows the **created design** as shame-*less* integrity; borne disgrace is therefore a **fallen** condition — except where assumed innocently (the Servant), which redeems it.
- **T0.2.3** — Yes — the **removal** of borne disgrace is promised: "you will not be put to shame… you will forget the shame of your youth and remember the reproach of your widowhood no more" (Isa 54:4).
- **T0.3.1** — Bears on the image as **exposure of the self before others/God** — nakedness (Gen 2:25; Eze 16) is the figure of a standing laid bare.
- **T0.3.3** — Present as a **wound to standing**; its **removal** (Isa 54:4) restores the image's honour.
- **T0.4.1** — **Christological apex**: "who for the joy set before him endured the cross, **despising the shame**, and is seated at the right hand of God" (Heb 12:2); foreshadowed in the Servant who "**hid not his face from disgrace and spitting**" (Isa 50:6). Direction = OT Servant → NT Christ; disgrace borne unto vindication.

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **a borne condition of disgrace/dishonour** — a standing one carries, whether laid on, deserved, or assumed.
- **T1.1.2** — bosh (be in disgrace), ke.lim.mah / bo.shet / bushah / ke.lim.mut / bosh.nah (shame, disgrace, ignominy), qa.lon (dishonour); atimia/aischunē (the borne pole).
- **T1.1.3** — **Constitutional/relational** — a state of the self in its standing before others and God.
- **T1.2.1** — A **condition/standing** (state), with the **act of bearing** it (Eze 16:52 "bear your disgrace"; Heb 12:2 "despising the shame").
- **T1.2.2** — Compound — the **felt disgrace** (affect) joined to the **objective standing** of dishonour; bosh tagged affect+cognition.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: **honour / vindication** (char B; "instead of shame, a double portion," cf. G).
- **T1.3.2** — Excludes the secure standing of the unashamed (Gen 2:25; Isa 54:4).
- **T1.3.3** — Not the *inflicting* of disgrace (char E) nor the felt collapse of trust (char A) — the **borne standing** itself.
- **T1.4.1** — Stative ("are disgraced," "shame has covered our face") and **borne-active** ("bear your disgrace," "despising the shame").
- **T1.4.2** — Several modes: **imposed** (judgment, Eze 16; Jer), **deserved** (Ezr 9:7; Pro 13:18), **enemy-heaped** (Psa 69:19), **innocently/vicariously borne** (Isa 50:6; Heb 12:2). [D1/D8 flags]
- **T1.4.3** — Yes — addressed ("**bear** your disgrace," Eze 16:52) and confessed ("we are in great **shame**," Ezr 9:7).
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate signs: **the face covered** ("dishonour has **covered my face**," Psa 69:7; "shame has covered our face," Jer 51:51), **nakedness/exposure** (Eze 16), turning to the **lowest place** in disgrace (Luk 14:9).
- **T1.5.2** — Consistent somatic image: covered/lowered face, exposure.
- **T1.6.1** — **Durative by nature** — disgrace is a *standing* that persists ("everlasting… shall not be forgotten," Jer 20:11 of the persecutors' shame; the removal in Isa 54:4 presupposes long-borne shame).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate exposure vs the **abiding stigma** that lingers until removed.
- **T1.7.1** — "Right" bearing appears in the **innocent/vicarious** sub-band: the Servant sets his face like flint while bearing disgrace (Isa 50:6-7); Christ **despises** (scorns) the shame (Heb 12:2).
- **T1.7.2** — Partial — the disgrace is heaped by enemies (Psa 69:19) but God overturns it.
- **T1.7.3** — In the vindicated, the threatened disgrace **does not finally hold** (Isa 50:7-8; Heb 12:2).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- **T2.1.1** — Seat shown as **the face** ("dishonour has covered my face," Psa 69:7; Jer 51:51) and the **body's exposure/nakedness** (Gen 2:25; Eze 16:37; Isa 50:6 "my back… my face"); bosh's affect locates the felt component in the inner self.
- **T2.1.2** — Recorded as a **standing of the whole self** before others/God, surfacing on the face and body.
- **T2.7.1** — Strong body link: **face covered/lowered, back struck, nakedness exposed** — disgrace is the most **embodied** of the bands.
- **T2.9.1** — Origin is **plural**: laid on by God's judgment (Eze 16), incurred by sin (Ezr 9:7; Pro 18:3), heaped by enemies (Psa 69:19), or **assumed** for others (Isa 50:6).
- **T2.9.2** — **Multiple origins** — the defining heterogeneity of D.
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: disgrace **settles onto/over** the self (covering the face), or is **lifted off** (Isa 54:4), or **taken up** (Heb 12:2).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
- **T3.1.1** — Perception: disgrace is **seen** — exposure "in the sight of the nations" (Eze 16:37,41); "all who **see** me mock me" (cf. Psa 22 context).
- **T3.2.1** — Cognition engaged (bosh tagged) — the **awareness of one's marked standing** ("you know my **reproach**… my shame and my dishonour," Psa 69:19).
- **T3.4.1** — Affect central — the **felt weight** of borne disgrace (Psa 69:7,19; Job 10:15 "filled with disgrace").
- **T3.4.2** — Produces the downcast, face-covering response; in the vicarious case, endured without flinching (Isa 50:7).
- **T3.6.1** — Volition at the apex: the Servant **chooses** not to hide his face (Isa 50:6); Christ **chooses** to despise the shame (Heb 12:2) — disgrace **willingly borne**.
- **T3.7.1** — Agency in the **bearing** ("**bear** your disgrace," Eze 16:52) — the self made to carry the standing.
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation **discriminates**: disgrace **deserved** for sin (Ezr 9:7; Pro 13:18; Eze 16) vs **innocent/vicarious** (Isa 50:6; Psa 69:7 "for your sake"; Heb 12:2). The band's moral charge is **not uniform**.
- **T3.9.1** — Conscience at the deserved pole: "I am **ashamed and blush** to lift my face to you… our iniquities have risen" (Ezr 9:6-7) — borne disgrace fused with guilt (overlaps char C).
- **T3.11.1** — **Relational capacity central** — disgrace is a standing **before others and God**; "for your sake I have **borne reproach**" (Psa 69:7) makes it a Godward relation.
- **T3.3 / 3.5 / 3.10** — Silent.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **imposes** disgrace in judgment (Eze 16; Jer 13:26) and **removes** it (Isa 54:4); he **knows** the sufferer's reproach (Psa 69:19).
- **T4.1.2** — Basis: **judicial** (against sin) and **redemptive** (the disgrace removed / borne by the Servant).
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God: disgrace **borne for God's sake** (Psa 69:7) and **confessed** before God (Ezr 9:6-7) — the disgraced self turned Godward.
- **T4.2.2** — Movement of **lament and confession** under borne disgrace.
- **T4.3.1** — Person → person: enemies/mockers **heap** disgrace (Psa 69:19; the lowest place, Luk 14:9) — though that *inflicting* is char E's focus; here it is the **standing received**.
- **T4.4.1** — Person ← person (received) is the band's centre — disgrace **suffered from** enemies, nations, accusers.
- **T4.4.2** — The honourable response: **endure** (Isa 50:7), **despise the shame** (Heb 12:2), **confess** (Ezr 9).
- **T4.5.2** — Crosses to **the nations** extensively (Moab, Egypt, Babylon, Edom in Jer/Eze) and to **corporate Israel** (Ezr 9:7; Jer 51:51).
- **T4.6.x** — Silent.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
- **T5.1.1** — Transformative: borne disgrace **removed/reversed** — "you will **forget** the shame of your youth" (Isa 54:4); the Servant's disgrace turns to **vindication** (Isa 50:7-8); Christ's shame to **enthronement** (Heb 12:2).
- **T5.2.1** — Sequence: sin/judgment → borne disgrace → (for the penitent/vindicated) **removal/reversal**.
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: disgrace **laid on** (judgment), **incurred** (sin), or **assumed** (vicarious), and **lifted** by God's salvation.
- **T5.4.1** — Operates squarely **within suffering and judgment** — exile, defeat, mockery, the cross.
- **T5.4.2** — Suffering is the very **medium** in which disgrace is borne — and, redemptively, transfigured (Heb 12:2).
- **T5.5.1** — Sanctification: bearing **reproach for God** (Psa 69:7) prefigures the disciple's bearing of Christ's reproach.
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological: disgrace **finally removed** for God's people (Isa 54:4) and **finally fixed** on the impenitent (Jer 20:11; 23:40 long-shame motif).

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with **reproach** (cherpah), **nakedness/exposure**, **mocking**, **judgment**, **confession of sin**.
- **T6.2.1** — Within D, disgrace **follows** sin or assault and **precedes** either removal (penitent/vindicated) or perpetuity (impenitent).
- **T6.3.2** — D is **produced by** char E's inflicting and by God's judgment; it is **answered by** char B's vindication. [flag: cross-char]
- **T6.4.1** — Shares bosh/bo.shet/ke.lim.mah/qa.lon/atimia/aischunē with A, B, E, G.
- **T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbours: **char E** (E inflicts what D bears), **char B** (B's vindication is D's reversal), **char G** (G is the axis on which D's pole sits), and **char C** (the deserved/penitent overlap, Ezr 9). [flag for cross-char confirmation]
- **T6.5.2** — Boundary: D = the **borne standing**; E = the **act**; A/C = the **felt episode**; G = the **axis**.
- **T6.5.3** — Internally, D needs the **D1 (imposed-verdict)** vs **D8 (innocent/vicarious)** distinction — a moral, not merely degree, difference. [flag retained]

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
- **T7.1.1** — bosh (BWŠ), bo.shet, ke.lim.mah, bushah, ke.lim.mut, bosh.nah, qa.lon; atimia, aischunē, entropē.
- **T7.1.2** — Stative verbs + abstract **disgrace-nouns** (the band is noun-heavy — disgrace as a *thing borne*).
- **T7.1.3** — Range: disgrace / dishonour / ignominy / reproach / shame-as-standing.
- **T7.1.4** — Yes — a **state/standing** emphasis (the nouns) vs the **act** of bearing (Eze 16:52; Heb 12:2).
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite term: honour/glory; vindication (char B).
- **T7.1.6** — Silent (no person-type noun).
- **T7.1.7** — Silent.
- **T7.1.8** — OT→NT: the **Servant's borne disgrace** (Isa 50:6) and the psalmist's "**reproach for your sake**" (Psa 69:7, cited of Christ in Rom 15:3; Joh 2:17 context) consummate in **Christ despising the shame** (Heb 12:2) — borne disgrace becomes the very path to glory.
- **T7.1.9** — Silent.
- **T7.1.10** — Range: disgrace as a borne standing across judgment, lament, wisdom, and the cross.
- **T7.2.1** — Functions as **lament, judgment-oracle, confession, wisdom-maxim, and gospel proclamation**.
- **T7.2.2** — **Prophecy** dominant (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel — the bulk), **psalm** (lament), **wisdom** (Proverbs, Job), **narrative** (Gen 2:25; 1Sa 20:34), **epistle** (2Cor 11:21; Heb 12:2), **gospel** (Luk 14:9).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Ezr 9:6-7): our iniquities have mounted → therefore we are **in shame** → confessed before God. And (Heb 12:2): **despising** the shame → therefore **seated** at God's right (shame-through-to-glory).
- **T7.2.4** — Settings: exile and judgment, individual/corporate lament, the passion of the Servant/Christ.
- **T7.2.5** — Anchors: **Psa 69:7** — "for your sake I have **borne reproach**; **dishonour has covered my face**" (borne, even Godward) — paired with **Isa 50:6 / Heb 12:2** (the innocent/vicarious apex) and **Gen 2:25** (the shame-less baseline).
- **T7.2.6** — These anchors uniquely show disgrace as a **standing that can be laid on, deserved, or assumed** — and, in Christ, **borne unto glory** — the full moral range no single verse holds.
- **T7.3.1** — Lens: the **stigma / spoiled-identity** and **honour-shame** literatures (disgrace as a socially-marked, borne standing) and the **exposure/nakedness** strand [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec2/3].
- **T7.3.2** — Illumination: clinical "stigma as a borne, durable mark" closely fits D's noun-heavy, standing-centred disgrace (Eze 16; Psa 69). [source: science extract Sec2]
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: D's **deserved** disgrace (a just verdict on sin, Ezr 9) and its **vicarious/innocent** bearing (Isa 50:6; Heb 12:2) are moral-theological categories the descriptive stigma literature does not carry. [source: science extract T7.3.3]
- **T7.3.4** — Gap: "**despising the shame**" (Heb 12:2) — disgrace *scorned and transcended* en route to honour — has no clean clinical analogue. [source: science extract]
