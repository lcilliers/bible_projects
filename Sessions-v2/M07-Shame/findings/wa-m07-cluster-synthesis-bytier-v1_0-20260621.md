# M07 — Cluster-Wide Synthesis by Tier, with Cross-Characteristic and Boundary Findings
**File:** wa-m07-cluster-synthesis-bytier-v1_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21
**Draws on:** the twelve characteristic findings v2_0 (A, B, C, C-neg, D, E, F, G, H, J, K, RESID) and wa-m07-phase2-assessment-v1_0-20260621.md.
**Cluster:** M07 — Shame, Disgrace and Humiliation. 359 focus occurrences, 302 verses, 31 lemmas.

## Method note
This synthesis reads **upward** from the twelve verse-grounded characteristic findings into one cluster picture, tier by tier (T0–T7). Three registers are kept distinct:
- **Observation** — what the verses, across the characteristics, show.
- **Cluster finding** — a pattern that holds across characteristics, traceable to the underlying evidence.
- **Interpretation / boundary judgement** — a structural or relevance call. The six carried boundary flags are presented in Part 3 as **findings-with-recommendations for researcher decision**, not as resolved changes.

# PART 1 — Synthesis by tier

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
**Observation.** God stands at **both ends** of shame across the cluster: as the one who **exposes / imposes / abases** (A exposes false trust; D decrees disgrace in judgment, Eze 16:52; F abases pride, Isa 2:11-17; E is dishonoured by sin, Rom 2:23; G turns glory to shame, Hos 4:7) **and** as the one who **removes / vindicates / reverses** (B keeps the truster from shame, Isa 49:23; D removes it, Isa 54:4; G turns shame to honour, Isa 61:7; Zep 3:19). The **baseline of the image is shame-less integrity** — "naked and not ashamed" (Gen 2:25). The **apex** is the Servant/Christ bearing disgrace unto glory — "despising the shame… seated at the right hand of God" (Heb 12:2; Isa 50:6).

**Cluster finding.** In M07, shame is fundamentally a **relational-standing reality before God, with God as its decisive arbiter in both directions.** The image is portrayed as a **trusting, honour-bearing being**: its shame marks misdirected trust (A), a marred moral faculty (C-neg), a borne standing (D), or pride under judgment (F); its vindication is God's reversal (B, G). The capacity for shame is **created design** (its loss is ruin, C-neg); the *occasion* of shame is **fallen** (Gen 2:25 baseline) — except where disgrace is **assumed innocently** (the Servant), which redeems it.

## T1 — Definition
**Observation.** The cluster's shame vocabulary is not one thing. It spans a **felt collapse of falsified trust** (A), the **vindication/non-shaming of trust** (B), **penitential conscience-shame** (C), the **absence/inversion of the faculty** (C-neg), **borne disgrace as standing** (D), **inflicted dishonour/contempt** (E), the **abasement of pride** (F), the **honour↔shame axis** (G), the **norm of the disgraceful** (H), the **innocence antonym** (J), **silencing/defilement** (K), and a **non-shame sense** (RESID).

**Cluster finding.** Shame in M07 is a **family of related but distinct phenomena**: a felt **affect** (A, C), a relational **standing** (D, G), an inflicted **act** (E), a **status-movement** (F), a **norm** (H), a **faculty** (C/C-neg), and an **outcome of trust** (A/B). The **compound (affect + cognition)** signature is tagged on the bosh-heavy bands (A, B, C-neg, D). Internal **opposites** are explicit: honour/glory (G), vindication (B), innocence (J), and shamelessness-as-inversion (C-neg).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
**Observation.** M07 rarely names a **constitutional seat** (spirit/soul/heart). Where inner seat-language appears it is the **heart/conscience** (Psa 119:80 blameless heart; Gen 20:5 integrity of heart; C's conscience). Far more often the location is the **face and body**: covered/lowered face (D — Psa 69:7; Jer 51:51), the unblushing forehead (C-neg — Jer 3:3; 6:15), the radiant face (B — Psa 34:5), the face set like flint while bearing disgrace (B/D — Isa 50:7), haughty eyes (F), washed hands (J), struck back (Isa 50:6).

**Cluster finding.** Shame in M07 is **overwhelmingly embodied and registered on the face/body** — the face is its primary theatre (covered, lowered, blushing, radiant, flint-set). The inner seat, where present, is the **heart/conscience** (C, J, B). **Boundary observation / additional-discovery flag:** the programme's governing **spirit–soul–body partition is *not* directly illuminated by M07** — shame surfaces on the body and engages heart/conscience, but these verses do not address the tripartite boundary. M07 contributes to the **faculty map**, not to the constitutional-boundary question; this gap should be noted at programme level.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
**Observation.** Faculties engaged across the cluster: **affect** (all felt bands), **cognition** (the trust/expectation bands A, B; awareness of standing, D), **moral evaluation** (pervasive — C, C-neg, D, E, F, G, H, J), **conscience** (C the seat; C-neg its searing; J integrity; B "good conscience," 1Pe 3:16), **volition** (B taking refuge; C-neg refusing shame; D/Servant bearing; E inflicting; J keeping clean), **agency** (E the agent pole), and **relational capacity** (pervasive). **Not** engaged cluster-wide: **memory (T3.3), creativity (T3.5), conscientiousness (T3.10).**

**Cluster finding.** The inner-being faculties most engaged by shame are **moral evaluation, conscience, relational capacity, and affect** — shame is a **moral-relational-affective complex**, not a bare emotion. The sharpest faculty finding is the **conscience axis**: shame is the conscience's **affective signal at moral failure** (C), and its **searing/inversion is moral ruin** (C-neg, Jer 6:15 "did not know how to blush"). C and C-neg are the **same faculty operative and failed** — a paired finding.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
**Observation.** The cluster maps a dense relational web. **God→human:** exposes (A), vindicates (B), imposes & removes disgrace (D), abases pride (F), reverses the axis (G), is dishonoured by sin (E). **Human→God:** trust/refuge (B — the heart of the Godward movement), confession under disgrace (D, Ezr 9:6-7), reverence (G, Heb 12:9), innocence brought to the altar (J, Psa 26:6); and negatively, the refusal of shame Godward (C-neg, Jer 3:3). **Person→person:** contempt/dishonour inflicted (E), disgrace borne from enemies (D), respect/regard (G). **Person←person:** receiving dishonour (E, D), with a distinct **honourable response** — rejoice (Act 5:41), despise the shame (Heb 12:2), do not repay reviling (1Pe 3:9). **Covenantal/nations:** shame crosses to the **nations** extensively (A, B, D, F, G). **Spiritual beings:** **absent** (T4.6 silent cluster-wide; only idols *qua* empty, A).

**Cluster finding.** M07 is the cluster **most constituted by relational interface** — shame *is* standing before God and others. The **Godward interface concentrates in B** (trust→vindication); the **human giving/receiving in E/D**. A distinct **NT development** is the **honourable bearing of inflicted shame** (endure, rejoice, despise it, do not retaliate — Act 5:41; Heb 12:2; 1Pe 3:9). **Boundary finding:** the **absence of any spiritual-being interface** is itself a cluster-wide result — in M07's verses shame is a **God-and-neighbour** reality, not an angelic/demonic one.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
**Observation.** Shame's arcs in the cluster: it is **removed/reversed** (B vindication; D, Isa 54:4; G, shame→honour) — the dominant redemptive movement; it is **seared/lost** (C-neg) — the pathological movement; it is **borne through suffering unto vindication** (B, D, the Servant/Christ); and it is **eschatologically weighted** throughout (B at his coming, 1Jo 2:28; D final removal; G final exchange; F the day of the Lord; C-neg "their end is destruction," Phil 3:19).

**Cluster finding.** M07 has a strong **eschatological spine**: shame and honour are **finally and decisively sorted by God** — the believing not put to shame, the proud abased, the shamed exalted, the impenitent fixed in disgrace. A **sanctification thread** runs alongside: bearing reproach **for God** (Psa 69:7; 1Pe 4:16) and **despising the shame** (Heb 12:2). This tier intersects the programme's **transformation/renewal** register (shame removed = renewal).

## T6 — Structural Relationships  *(this tier is the cross-characteristic core — developed fully in Part 2)*
**Observation.** The lemmas are **shared across bands** (bosh in A/B/C-neg/D/E; ka.lam in A/B/C/C-neg/E/RESID; cha.pher, qa.lon, ke.lim.mah, atimia across several), with each band reading the **same term under a different characteristic** by its use in the verse.

**Cluster finding.** M07 is **not a flat list of twelve parallel characteristics** but a **structured field** organised by three faculty/standing axes (trust, conscience, honour-standing), a normative frame (H/J), and two edges (K/RESID). The shared-lemma pattern **confirms the characteristic-perspective model** (GR-PROG-006): the unit is the term-in-its-use, not the term. *(Architecture in Part 2.)*

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
**Observation.** The cluster rests on a **Hebrew core** — **bosh (BWŠ) as the master root** (108 occ), with ka.lam (KLM), cha.pher, qa.lon, ke.lim.mah, bo.shet, sha.phel — and a **Greek set** — aischunō/kataischunō, atimazō/atimia, exoutheneō, entrepō, aischros/aschēmosunē, fimoō. **Literary forms:** prophecy dominates (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel), with heavy psalm (lament/trust, esp. B), wisdom (Proverbs/Job, esp. E's household-shame and F), narrative, epistle (the NT bands), gospel, and apocalyptic.

**Cluster finding (OT→NT).** Continuity is **strongest in B** — the Zion-stone "**not put to shame**" (Isa 28:16) taken up verbatim in Rom 9:33; 10:11; 1Pe 2:6 — and in **D** — the Servant's borne disgrace (Isa 50:6) consummated in "despising the shame" (Heb 12:2). The **NT develops the agent vocabulary** (exoutheneō, atimazō — E) and the **honourable-bearing motif** (Act 5:41; Heb 12:2; 1Pe 3:9).

**Cluster finding (science lens, T7.3).** The science extract illuminates the **descriptive** shape — shame-psychology (felt shame), honour-shame anthropology (standing), stigma (borne disgrace), disgust-purity (propriety/defilement), contempt (agent pole). The biblical material **adds a moral-theological axis the clinical frame lacks**: **appropriate/commanded shame** (vs the clinical "shame is maladaptive"), **shamelessness as ruin** (vs "less shame is healthier"), and **vicarious/redemptive shame** (Heb 12:2) with no clinical analogue. # PART 2 — Cross-characteristic architecture (the M07 field)

**Cluster finding — M07 is organised by three axes, a normative frame, and two edges:**

**1. The trust axis — A ↔ B.** The same faculty (trust/hope) with opposite outcomes by **object**: a **false object → shame** (A), the **true object (God) → vindication** (B). This is the cluster's **theological spine** and its **clearest OT→NT continuity** (B's "not put to shame"). *Finding:* A and B are two halves of one faculty, divided by the object of trust, not by degree.

**2. The conscience axis — C ↔ C-neg.** The same faculty (conscience) **operative** (penitential shame at one's own sin, C) and **failed** (seared / refused / inverted, C-neg). *Finding:* shame is the conscience's affective signal at moral failure; its absence is moral ruin (Jer 6:15). C-neg is the **privation-and-inversion** of C.

**3. The honour-standing axis — G, with D and E as its poles, and F as movement along it.**
- **G is the axis itself** (honour/glory ↔ shame/dishonour), reversed by God in both directions.
- **D is the borne pole** (disgrace carried) and **E is the inflicting pole** (dishonour/contempt done).
- **F is the judgment-movement** down the axis (the abasement of pride).
*Findings:* **(i) E produces D** — the agent's dishonouring (E) creates the borne disgrace (D) in the other; one event, two poles. **(ii) B reverses D** — vindication is the removal of borne disgrace; Isa 54:4 sits on their seam. **(iii) G is the scale on which D and E move** — supporting the reading of G as an axis rather than a seventh parallel band. **(iv) F's abasement of pride feeds the axis** — the lowering of the proud is registered as shame on the honour-standing scale, with F bordering M09 (humility).

**4. The normative frame — H and J.** **H** supplies the **standard** (what counts as disgraceful); **J** supplies the **antonym** (innocence — the unashamed clean state). Together they **bound the field conceptually**: H says what *ought* to occasion shame; J shows what shame's *absence* looks like.

**5. The edges — K and RESID.** **K** is mostly **behavioural/physical** (silencing, casting-off), inner-being-relevant only at ni.dah (defilement, Lam 1:8). **RESID** is a **non-shame sense** of ka.lam (harm/injure). Both are gathered for completeness and flagged for set-aside/exclusion.

**Cross-char synthesis finding.** The twelve "characteristics" are best understood as **positions within a structured field**, not as twelve equal, independent inner states. The felt/borne/agent core (A, B, C, C-neg, D, E) carries the inner-being weight; G is the axis that organises the standing-bands; F is a status-judgment at the M07/M09 seam; H and J are the conceptual frame; K and RESID are edges. This structure should govern the **chapter's organisation** and the eventual **Session D** cross-registry mapping.

# PART 3 — Boundary findings and carried flags (recommendations for researcher decision)

Per GR-PROC-004 these are **proposals**, not applied changes. Each states the evidence, a recommendation, and the decision required.

**Flag 1 — D sub-bands: D1 (imposed-verdict) vs D8 (innocent/vicarious).**
Evidence: D's 136 occ span **deserved** disgrace (Ezr 9:7; Pro 13:18; Eze 16), **enemy-imposed** disgrace (Psa 69:19), and **innocent/vicarious** disgrace (Isa 50:6; Heb 12:2; Psa 69:7 "for your sake"). The moral difference is real, not degree.
*Recommendation:* **retain D as one characteristic** but carry a **documented two-part sub-distinction**; surface the **vicarious sub-band** (Servant/Christ) prominently in the chapter — it is theologically central. *Decision required:* approve the sub-band documentation, or split D.

**Flag 2 — F and the M09 (humility) boundary.**
Evidence: sha.phel is a **status-movement** (high→low), not an affect; "lowly in spirit will obtain honour" (Pro 29:23) is humility language.
*Recommendation:* **retain F in M07** as the "abasement / judgment-of-pride" pole of the honour-shame field, **with a cross-reference to M09**; flag sha.phel as a possible **shared term** with M09 (shared-verse model). *Decision required:* keep in M07, route to M09, or treat as cross-linked.

**Flag 3 — G: axis vs characteristic.**
Evidence: G's verses are the **honour↔shame exchange** and the **reverence pole** (entrepō); its lemmas also appear in D and E.
*Recommendation:* present **G as the organising axis** of the standing-bands (D, E, F) in the chapter, **not as a seventh parallel characteristic**; treat the **entrepō reverence/regard** pole as the positive complement to E's contempt. *Decision required:* approve axis-treatment, or retain G as a discrete band.

**Flag 4 — J: relevance of the innocence antonym.**
Evidence: niq.qa.von / qe.ha.von mean **innocence/cleanness** — the **antonym** of shame, not shame.
*Recommendation:* **retain J as the contrast pole** for definitional clarity (brief), explicitly marked **antonym**; consider whether these terms belong **primarily to a purity/righteousness registry**. *Decision required:* keep as antonym-pole, or relocate.

**Flag 5 — K: set-aside vs retain.**
Evidence: fimoō/afōnos/apoballō are **silencing/casting acts**, not inner states; ni.dah (Lam 1:8) is genuine defilement-disgrace.
*Recommendation:* **retain ni.dah** as M07 defilement-disgrace; **set aside** fimoō/afōnos/apoballō as non-inner-being. *Decision required:* approve the partial set-aside.

**Flag 6 — RESID: exclude.**
Evidence: ka.lam at 1Sa 25:7,15 is the **"harm/injure" sense**, no inner-being shame content.
*Recommendation:* **formally exclude** from M07 shame analysis (sense-split). *Decision required:* approve exclusion.

# PART 4 — Gaps, additional discovery, and cross-registry candidates

**Where additional data/discovery may be needed:**
- **Constitutional boundary (programme governing question).** M07 does **not** illuminate the spirit–soul–body partition (Part 1, T2). If the programme needs M07's contribution to that question, the answer is that shame is **embodied (face/body) and seated in heart/conscience** — it does not adjudicate the tripartite boundary. Flag for the programme-level constitutional synthesis.
- **Faculty map completeness.** Memory, creativity, and conscientiousness are **unengaged** by M07's shame vocabulary (Part 1, T3). If the programme expects every faculty to be touched per cluster, this is a true absence, not a coverage gap.
- **Spiritual-being interface.** Entirely **absent** in M07 (Part 1, T4). If cross-registry work expects an adversarial-shame interface (e.g., the accuser motif), it is **not present in these lemmas' verses** and would have to come from another registry.

**Cross-registry connection candidates (for Session D, when the relevant clusters complete):**
- **M01 (Fear/Dread):** A's collapse-cluster (shame + dismay + dread + terror) binds shame to fear — a strong semantic-overlap / co-occurrence link.
- **M08 (Pride/Arrogance):** F (abasement of pride) and G (glory→shame for the proud) connect directly — theological/semantic-opposition link.
- **M09 (Humility/Meekness):** F's "lowly in spirit obtains honour" (Pro 29:23) — shared-root/shared-term link.
- **Honour/glory registry (if/when scoped):** G's honour↔shame axis is the natural seam.
- **A guilt/conscience registry:** C and C-neg connect to guilt and conscience (note the prior G4893 suneidēsis ownership lies with Registry 112/mind).

# Close
M07 reads as a **structured field of shame** before God and neighbour, organised by a **trust axis** (A/B), a **conscience axis** (C/C-neg), and an **honour-standing axis** (G, with D borne / E inflicted / F abasing), framed by a **norm** (H) and an **antonym** (J), with two **edges** (K, RESID). Its theological centre of gravity is the movement from shame to honour that God works — culminating in the One who **despised the shame** and was seated in glory (Heb 12:2). The outstanding work is **interpretive**: the six boundary decisions in Part 3, then the M07 chapter built on this field-structure, and in due course the cross-registry links in Part 4.
