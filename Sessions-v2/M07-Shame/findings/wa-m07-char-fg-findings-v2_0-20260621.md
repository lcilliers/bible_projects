# F — Abasement: the bringing-low of pride (status reversal high↔low) — 22 occ (all sha.phel)
**Evidence:** sha.phel throughout — the divine lowering of the haughty, set against exaltation. **No affect/faculty tag** on any occurrence: F names a **status movement**, not a felt state. [boundary flag: M09 / "action that produces shame"]

## T0
- **T0.1.1** — God is the **sole agent** of the lowering: "he **brings low** and he exalts" (1Sa 2:7); "it is God who executes judgment, **putting down** one and lifting up another" (Psa 75:7); "the Lord alone will be exalted" (Isa 2:11,17).
- **T0.1.2** — Borne by the **proud** whom God lowers; never by God.
- **T0.2.1** — Functions as the **judicial reversal of pride** — bringing the lofty down to where the humble are raised.
- **T0.2.2** — Engages **pride** as a fallen inner orientation that God's design-order opposes ("the haughty eyes you **bring down**," Psa 18:27).
- **T0.3.1** — Bears on the image via the **posture of the heart** — haughtiness vs lowliness — that God honours or abases (Pro 29:23).
- **T0.4.1** — Eschatological/typological at Isa 2:11-17 — "the day of the Lord" against all that is proud — the final humbling before the Lord alone exalted.

## T1
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **being brought low in status** — abasement, the lowering of height/pride.
- **T1.1.2** — sha.phel (be/become low, sink, be humbled, be abased).
- **T1.1.3** — Relational/positional — a movement **down the honour-status axis**.
- **T1.2.1** — An **action/status-change** (not an affect).
- **T1.2.2** — Simple at the lexical level (a status verb), but **morally loaded** by its object (pride).
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: **exaltation / being lifted up** ("makes high the low tree," Eze 17:24; "obtain honour," Pro 29:23).
- **T1.3.3** — Not felt shame; the **lowering of standing** that pride incurs — bordering M09 humility. [flag]
- **T1.4.1** — Causative/passive verbs ("he abases," "is brought low").
- **T1.5.1 / T1.6.x / T1.7.x** — Silent — F describes a status outcome, not a response with somatic onset, duration, or malfunction.

## T2
- **T2.1.1** — No inner seat; the thing lowered is **height/pride** ("the **lofty pride** of men shall be brought low," Isa 2:17) — a posture, registered in "**haughty eyes/looks**" (Isa 2:11; 5:15; Psa 18:27).
- **T2.7.1** — Body image: **eyes/looks** (haughty), the high brought to the **dust** (Isa 29:4).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: the **pride** of the one lowered occasions it; the **agent** is God.
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: **downward**, God acting on the proud (high→low).

## T3
- **T3.8.1** — **Moral evaluation** engaged: pride is **judged** and lowered; lowliness is honoured ("he saves the lowly," Job 22:29; "he who is lowly in spirit will obtain honour," Pro 29:23).
- **T3.6.1** — Volition at the human edge: pride is a **chosen self-exaltation** that God reverses (Jer 13:18 "take a lowly seat").
- **T3.11.1** — Relational — the proud are lowered **before God and others** (the honour-status order).
- **T3.1 / 3.2 / 3.3 / 3.4 / 3.5 / 3.7 / 3.9 / 3.10** — Silent — no perception/cognition/memory/affect/creativity/agency/conscience content tagged or shown; F is a status verb.

## T4
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **abases the proud** (Isa 2; 5; 13; Job 40:11 "abase him").
- **T4.1.2** — Basis: **judgment on pride/haughtiness**.
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God (implied imperative): **humble yourself / take the low seat** (Jer 13:18).
- **T4.5.2** — Crosses to **nations and rulers** (the king and queen mother, Jer 13:18; the proud world, Isa 13:11).
- **T4.3 / 4.4 / 4.6** — Silent.

## T5
- **T5.1.1** — Formative reversal: pride **brought low**, lowliness **lifted** — the moral order God enforces (Pro 29:23).
- **T5.4.1** — Operates within **judgment** (the day of the Lord, Isa 2:12).
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological humbling (Isa 2:11-17).
- **T5.2 / 5.3 / 5.5** — Silent.

## T6
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with **pride/haughtiness** (ga'avah, gevohim), **exalt** (the antithesis), eyes/loftiness.
- **T6.3.2** — F is the **action/status-movement** that can *produce* shame; nearest the abasement edge of char G (the honour-status axis). [flag: M09 boundary; action-vs-state]
- **T6.4.1** — sha.phel unique to this band (no lemma overlap with the felt bands).
- **T6.5.1** — Boundary with **M09 (humility)** — "lowly in spirit" (Pro 29:23) is humility language; F's M07 claim rests on its tie to pride and the honour-shame field. [flag]

## T7
- **T7.1.1** — sha.phel (ŠPL — be low, be abased).
- **T7.1.3** — Range: brought low / humbled / abased / laid low.
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite: exalted/lifted up.
- **T7.2.2** — **Prophecy** dominant (Isaiah heavily; Eze; Jer), **wisdom** (Pro 25:7; 29:23; Job), **psalm** (75:7; 147:6; 18:27), **narrative-song** (1Sa 2:7; 2Sa 22:28).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Pro 29:23): pride → brings low; lowly-in-spirit → obtains honour (antithetic).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Pro 29:23** — "One's pride will **bring him low**, but he who is lowly in spirit will obtain honour" — the inner orientation (pride) tied to the status outcome (abasement), with the M09 seam visible.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor uniquely links **pride (inner)** to **abasement (status)** and names the humble counter-pole.
- **T7.3.1–.4** — Lens: **pride/humiliation and status** psychology [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec2]; illumination — abasement as enforced status-loss parallels humiliation dynamics; divergence — F's agent is **God's judgment**, and the humble are **honoured**, a theological reversal; gap — F is **status, not affect**, so the clinical shame-feeling frame mostly does not engage. [source: science extract]

**Genuine Silence in F: ~55 of 126 — largely structural.** The entire affect/somatic/process register (much of T3, T1.5–1.7, T2.9 onset-detail, T5.2–5.3) is empty **because sha.phel names a status movement, not a felt inner state.** F's real content sits in T0/T3.8/T4.1/T6/T7 — divine agency, the judgment of pride, the honour-status axis, and the M09 boundary.

# G — Honour↔shame / glory↔shame reversal (relational standing) — 19 occ
**Evidence:** atimia (honour/dishonour pairing), entrepō (respect/regard pole), kataischunō (1Cor 1:27), bo.shet, ke.lim.mah, qa.lon. The **axis** on which standing moves — shame and honour/glory as exchangeable poles, reversed by God in both directions. [status flag: axis-vs-characteristic; reverence pole]

## T0
- **T0.1.1** — God is the **agent of reversal on the axis**: "I will **change their shame into praise**" (Zep 3:19); "instead of your shame… a double portion" (Isa 61:7); and downward, "I will **change their glory into shame**" (Hos 4:7). And the cross-shaped inversion: "God chose what is **weak**… to **shame the strong**" (1Cor 1:27).
- **T0.1.2** — Both poles assigned by God to creatures; God himself is the **glory** that defines the axis.
- **T0.2.1** — Shame/honour function as the **two ends of relational standing**, with movement (reversal) the key phenomenon.
- **T0.2.2** — Reveals standing as a **God-conferred** reality — vessels "for honourable" or "dishonourable use" at the potter's right (Rom 9:21).
- **T0.3.1** — The image lives on an **honour↔shame continuum**; the entrepō "respect/regard" pole is the inner posture that **confers** honour (Heb 12:9).
- **T0.4.1** — Eschatological reversals: shame→honour for the people (Isa 61:7; Zep 3:19) and glory→shame for the proud (Hos 4:7; Hab 2:16), often final.

## T1
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **standing as a two-poled axis** (honour/glory ↔ shame/dishonour) and **movement along it**.
- **T1.1.2** — atimia (dishonour, paired with doxa/timē), entrepō (regard/reverence; or be-shamed), bo.shet/ke.lim.mah/qa.lon (shame, exchanged with glory).
- **T1.1.3** — **Relational/positional** — defined by where one stands on the honour-shame scale.
- **T1.2.1** — A **standing/quality** and its **reversal** (an event).
- **T1.2.2** — Compound — binds **shame** to its opposite **honour/glory** as matched terms.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite pole built in: **honour/glory** (the axis contains its own antithesis).
- **T1.3.3** — More **axis than discrete state** — threads through D (the borne pole) and E (the inflicting pole). [flag]
- **T1.4.1** — Stated as **exchange** ("instead of," "turned into," "changed into") and as **regard** (entrepō "they will respect").
- **T1.5.x / T1.6.x** — Partial: the reversal is often **eschatological and enduring** ("a double portion… everlasting joy," Isa 61:7) rather than a momentary response.
- **T1.7.x** — Silent.

## T2
- **T2.1.1** — No inner seat; standing is **conferred and displayed** (vessels of honour/dishonour, Rom 9:21; 2Ti 2:20); the **face/name** carry it (honour turned to shame, Psa 4:2).
- **T2.7.1** — Body/figure: vessels, garments, "sown in dishonour, raised in glory" (1Cor 15:43, the body itself).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: **God's conferral/reversal** of standing.
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: **along the axis**, in either direction, by God.

## T3
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation: standing is reordered **morally** — the proud's glory → shame (Hos 4:7), the lowly's shame → honour (Isa 61:7).
- **T3.11.1** — **Relational capacity central** — standing **is** a relational reality; the entrepō pole is the inner act of **regarding/reverencing** another's standing (Heb 12:9 "we respected our earthly fathers"; Mat 21:37 "they will respect my son").
- **T3.4.1** — Affect at the edge: "**to my shame**, I must say" (2Cor 11:21).
- **T3.1 / 3.2 / 3.3 / 3.5 / 3.6 / 3.7 / 3.9 / 3.10** — Silent — G is about conferred standing, not a process of perception/memory/volition/conscience.

## T4
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **reverses standing** (Isa 61:7; Zep 3:19; Hos 4:7; 1Cor 1:27).
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God: **reverence/regard** Godward — "shall we not much more be subject to the **Father**?" (Heb 12:9) — the honour-conferring inner posture.
- **T4.3.1** — Person→person: **respect** (the vineyard-son, Mat 21:37; Mar 12:6; Luk 20:13) or its refusal ("neither feared God nor **respected man**," Luk 18:2,4).
- **T4.5.2** — Crosses to nations and the proud (Hab 2:16; Hos 4:7) and the church (vessels, 2Ti 2:20).
- **T4.6.x** — Silent.

## T5
- **T5.6.1** — Strongly eschatological: the **final exchange** of shame for glory (Isa 61:7) and glory for shame (Hab 2:16; Hos 4:7); the resurrection reversal "sown in dishonour, **raised in glory**" (1Cor 15:43).
- **T5.1.1** — Transformative reversal of standing (shame→praise, Zep 3:19).
- **T5.4.1** — Set within judgment (downward) and restoration (upward).
- **T5.2 / 5.3 / 5.5** — Silent.

## T6
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with **glory/honour** (doxa, timē, kavod), **praise**, **boast**, and the shame-cluster.
- **T6.3.2 / T6.5.1** — G is best read as the **axis** underlying D (borne pole) and E (inflicting pole), and the **reverence pole** complements E's contempt. [flag: axis-vs-characteristic]
- **T6.4.1** — Shares atimia/bo.shet/ke.lim.mah/qa.lon/kataischunō/entrepō with D and E.

## T7
- **T7.1.1** — atimia (dishonour), entrepō (regard/shame), kataischunō, bo.shet, ke.lim.mah, qa.lon.
- **T7.1.4** — Note the **entrepō duality**: same root yields "be ashamed" and "regard/reverence" — shame and respect as two faces of regard for standing.
- **T7.1.5** — The contained opposite: **honour/glory** (timē/doxa/kavod).
- **T7.1.8** — OT→NT: the prophetic **shame↔glory exchange** (Isa 61:7; Hos 4:7) carries into the NT's honour/dishonour-vessel and resurrection-glory language (Rom 9:21; 1Cor 15:43).
- **T7.2.2** — **Prophecy** (Isa, Zep, Hos, Hab), **epistle** (Rom, 1-2Cor, 2Ti), **gospel/parable** (Mat 21; Mar 12; Luk 18; 20), **psalm** (4:2).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure: "**instead of** X (shame) — Y (honour)" / "**changed** glory **into** shame" (exchange formulae).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Isa 61:7** — "instead of your **shame** there shall be a **double portion**" — the axis and its God-wrought reversal in one line; paired with **1Cor 1:27** (the cross-shaped reversal of worldly standing).
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor shows standing as a **reversible axis** in God's hand, not a fixed state.
- **T7.3.1–.4** — Lens: **honour-shame social dynamics** (status as a relational good that can be conferred or stripped) [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec2]; illumination — the honour/shame axis is exactly the social-anthropological model; divergence — God **reverses** the axis eschatologically and cross-shapedly (1Cor 1:27), inverting worldly honour; gap — the **reverence/regard** pole (entrepō) as a positive inner posture is under-treated in the clinical shame literature. [source: science extract]

**Genuine Silence in G: ~50 of 126 — structural.** As an **axis** rather than a felt state, G leaves the inner-process tiers (perception, memory, volition, conscience, somatic onset) empty; its dense content is in standing, reversal, divine agency, eschatology, the relational/reverence interface, and the literary tiers. [axis-vs-characteristic flag retained]
