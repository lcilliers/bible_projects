# M07 — Findings for Characteristic C: Conscience — shame at one's own sin
**File:** wa-m07-char-c-conscience-sin-findings-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (v1_0 was scripted field-aggregation; this v2_0 is a verse read).
**Method:** every question answered by reading C's 22 verses and applying judgement; **Silent only where the text is genuinely silent.**
**Evidence (22 occ):** bosh, ka.lam, bo.shet, ke.lim.mah (OT penitential) + entrepō, entropē (NT admonitory). Core verses: Ezr 9:6,7; Dan 9:7,8; Jer 3:25; Jer 31:19; Eze 16:52,54,61,63; 36:32; 43:10,11; 1Cor 4:14; 6:5; 15:34; 2Th 3:14; Tit 2:8.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — God is the reference point and prompt: shame is felt *before him* ("ashamed… to lift my face to you, my God," Ezr 9:6), grounded in his righteousness against the person's sin ("to you, O Lord, belongs righteousness, but to us open shame… because we have sinned against you," Dan 9:7-8), commanded by him ("Be ashamed and confounded for your ways," Eze 36:32), produced through his instruction (Jer 31:19), and resolved by his atonement (Eze 16:63). Roles: addressee, instructor, commander, atoner.
- **T0.1.2** — Borne only by the creature — God never bears shame-at-sin; yet it exists *only* by relation to his righteousness (Dan 9:7). Exclusively creaturely, wholly God-referenced.
- **T0.2.1** — Purpose is stated repeatedly: it leads to remembering, being confounded, and ceasing ("that you may remember and be confounded, and never open your mouth again," Eze 16:63), to confession (Dan 9; Ezr 9), to relenting/turning (Jer 31:19), and is the precondition God sets before restoration ("that they may be ashamed of their iniquities; and they shall measure the plan," Eze 43:10-11). NT: corrective — "that he may be ashamed" (2Th 3:14), "do not go on sinning" (1Cor 15:34).
- **T0.2.2** — Both: the *capacity* (conscience, shame at one's own wrong before God) reads as created design; its *occasion* (sin to be ashamed of) belongs to the fallen condition. [inferential]
- **T0.2.3** — Yes — oriented toward atonement and restoration: "when I atone for you" (Eze 16:63), the restored temple (Eze 43:10-11), the new-covenant turning (Jer 31:19).
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates moral answerability before God — the image as a being who can recognise and own its wrong against a holy standard. [inferential]
- **T0.3.2** — Exclusively creaturely; it is the creature's penitent analogue *over against* God's righteousness, not a likeness God shares.
- **T0.3.3** — Where present (Ezr 9; Dan 9; Jer 31:19) the image's moral witness is operative and rightly oriented; its absence is the shameless condition (char C-neg).
- **T0.4.1** — Typological/covenantal: the shame→atonement pattern (Eze 16:63) points beyond itself to the divine act of atonement; direction = human penitence pointing toward God's atoning act. [inferential]

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — The inward acknowledgment of one's own guilt before God — penitent shame. Carried by bosh/ka.lam (be ashamed, blush, be confounded), bo.shet/ke.lim.mah (the disgrace owned in confession), and NT entrepō/entropē (corrective shame).
- **T1.1.2** — Hebrew = the felt-and-confessed shame of the guilty (bosh + "confounded"; bosheth/kelimmah owned, "let our dishonor cover us," Jer 3:25). Greek = entrepō/entropē, shame as a turning-inward provoked by admonition.
- **T1.1.3** — Vertical and inward: shame felt *Godward* (the face that cannot be lifted to God, Ezr 9:6) and turned on the self. Relational force is coram Deo.
- **T1.2.1** — Spans act → condition: the act of being ashamed/blushing (bosh, ka.lam — action) which, owned, is borne as a state (bosheth, kelimmah — status).
- **T1.2.2** — Compound: integrates moral recognition (knowing the sin), affect (shame/blush), memory, and willed cessation/confession — Eze 16:63 binds remember + be confounded + "never open your mouth."
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: shamelessness (char C-neg) — the refusal/inability to be ashamed at sin; resolution-opposite is the atonement that removes the ground of shame.
- **T1.3.2** — Resists self-justification and the covering of sin; excludes the presumption of lifting the face to God while guilty (Ezr 9:6 cannot).
- **T1.3.3** — Not social embarrassment (char A) nor disgrace inflicted by enemies (char E); it is self-recognised guilt before God. The boundary is the *cause*: one's own sin, Godward.
- **T1.4.1** — Modes: spontaneous confession (Ezr 9:6); commanded imperative ("Be ashamed," Eze 36:32); instructed outcome ("after I was instructed… I was ashamed," Jer 31:19); admonitory provocation (NT entrepō). Hebrew stems: bosh Qal/Hithpael, ka.lam Niphal.
- **T1.4.2** — Yes — from spontaneous conviction (Ezr/Dan) to commanded (Eze 36:32) to externally-provoked correction (NT).
- **T1.4.3** — Strongly: commanded ("Be ashamed," Eze 36:32), addressed ("that you may be ashamed," Eze 16), and confessed in speech (Dan 9; Ezr 9 prayers; "I say this to your shame," 1Cor 6:5; 15:34).
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: blushing and inability to lift the face (Ezr 9:6), being confounded (Jer 31:19; Eze 16:63), striking the thigh (Jer 31:19), falling silent / "never open your mouth again" (Eze 16:63), lying down in shame (Jer 3:25). Somatic-penitential and verbal-cessation.
- **T1.5.2** — Consistent in kind: downcast, silenced, self-abasing across the penitential verses.
- **T1.6.1** — Establishes a penitent orientation over time: turning from sin (Jer 31:19 relented), cessation (Eze 16:63), readiness to receive restoration (Eze 43:10-11).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the felt collapse (blush, confounded, silenced); sustained = the settled repentant orientation and the ending of the sin.
- **T1.7.1** — Takes hold rightly when joined to instruction (Jer 31:19 "after I was instructed") and to acknowledgment of God's righteousness (Dan 9:7).
- **T1.7.2** — Blocked by the shameless condition (char C-neg, "did not know how to blush") and, in C's own evidence, by "having no knowledge of God" (1Cor 15:34). No adversarial-spirit interference shown — **Silent** on that sub-part.
- **T1.7.3** — The person in whom the occasion is present but does not take hold is the shameless one (char C-neg).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- **T2.1.1** — Though the coded seat is largely untagged, the verses locate it across faculties/body: the face ("lift my face," Ezr 9:6) and blush (bodily expression); memory ("remember… and be ashamed," Eze 16:61,63); the mind/heart ("after I was instructed," Jer 31:19) turning; the mouth (silenced, Eze 16:63); the thigh struck (Jer 31:19).
- **T2.1.2** — Not a single seat: conscience here runs memory → moral recognition → affect → bodily/verbal expression → the will's turning. The heart is the implicit seat of the turning.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link runs **soul/heart expressing through the body**: downcast face, blush, struck thigh, silenced mouth — inner conviction shown outwardly.
- **T2.9.1** — Origin is God-prompted (instruction, Jer 31:19; command, Eze 36:32) *and* arises from the person's recognition of guilt (Ezr 9; Dan 9).
- **T2.9.2** — Multiple: external prompt (God's word/instruction) and internal recognition both attested.
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: from God's instruction/word onto the person → inward conviction → outward bodily/verbal expression → turning. A definite sequence.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
- **T3.1.1** — Perception engaged: "describe the temple, that they may be ashamed of their iniquities" (Eze 43:10) — perceiving the holy pattern exposes the sin; "remember and be confounded" (Eze 16:63). [moderate]
- **T3.1.2** — It sharpens inner sight of one's own deeds against God's standard.
- **T3.1.3** — Perception engaged as the recognition that triggers the shame, not as sensory perception per se.
- **T3.10.1** — Conscientiousness (integrated moral awareness + volition + action) is engaged: Jer 31:19 runs instructed → ashamed → relented → struck thigh — awareness, will, and act together.
- **T3.10.2** — It integrates the moral self into a single penitent response — recognition issuing in turning and confession.
- **T3.10.3** — Across the verses this integration is the characteristic's signature (Ezr 9; Dan 9; Jer 31:19; Eze 16:63).
- **T3.11.1** — Relational capacity engaged vertically: the shame is felt within the God-relationship (Ezr 9:6; Dan 9:7-8) and, in the NT, within the church family.
- **T3.11.2** — It restores right relational standing by owning the breach before God.
- **T3.11.3** — Predominantly God-directed (vertical), secondarily community (NT admonition).
- **T3.2.1** — Cognition strongly engaged: it requires knowing one's sin ("ashamed of all that you have done," Eze 16:54; "because we have sinned," Dan 9:8).
- **T3.2.2** — It is cognitively mediated — recognition of guilt is constitutive, not incidental.
- **T3.2.3** — Cognition engaged in every verse where the sin is named as the ground of the shame.
- **T3.3.1** — Memory explicitly engaged: "you will remember your ways and be ashamed" (Eze 16:61); "that you may remember and be confounded" (Eze 16:63).
- **T3.3.2** — Memory is the trigger — recalling the deeds produces the shame.
- **T3.3.3** — Where memory of sin is named, shame follows; a clear memory→shame link.
- **T3.4.1** — Affect engaged: the felt shame, blush, and confoundedness (Ezr 9:6; Jer 31:19).
- **T3.4.2** — Produces the painful, self-lowering feeling of guilt before God.
- **T3.4.3** — Affect present throughout, but bound to moral recognition (not free-floating feeling).
- **T3.5.1** — **Silent** — no engagement of the creative faculty in this characteristic's evidence.
- **T3.6.1** — Volition engaged: it issues in willed turning/cessation — "I relented" (Jer 31:19), "never open your mouth again" (Eze 16:63), "do not go on sinning" (1Cor 15:34).
- **T3.6.2** — It moves the will from the sin toward turning; the will's response completes the characteristic.
- **T3.6.3** — Volitional turning is the consistent outcome where the verses follow the shame through.
- **T3.7.1** — Agency engaged at the response: striking the thigh, confessing, ceasing — the person acts. [moderate]
- **T3.7.2** — It mobilises penitent action (confession, reform).
- **T3.7.3** — Agency appears as the penitent's responsive acts, not as initiating power.
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation strongly engaged — the characteristic *is* moral self-assessment against God's standard (valence sinful/righteous/commanded).
- **T3.8.2** — It renders a verdict on the self: guilty before a righteous God (Dan 9:7).
- **T3.8.3** — Moral framing present on nearly every occurrence; this is the characteristic's core faculty.
- **T3.9.1** — Conscience is the defining faculty here — the acute inner witness of sin and guilt (Ezr 9:6 "our iniquities… our guilt"; Dan 9 confession). This characteristic *is* conscience operating.
- **T3.9.2** — It convicts and lowers the self, opening the way to confession and turning.
- **T3.9.3** — Conscience-engagement is universal across C's evidence — its constitutive faculty.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: he prompts and commands the shame ("Be ashamed… declares the Lord God," Eze 36:32) and instructs toward it (Jer 31:19).
- **T4.1.2** — Covenantal and responsive — within the covenant lawsuit (Eze 16; Jer 3), responsive to sin, ordered toward atonement (Eze 16:63).
- **T4.1.3** — That he commands shame-then-atonement shows a disposition toward restoration, not bare condemnation — shame is the door he opens to cleansing. [inferential]
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God: confession/prayer Godward (Ezr 9:6 "O my God, I am ashamed… to lift my face to you"; Dan 9; Jer 3:25 "we have sinned against the Lord").
- **T4.2.2** — Requires humility, acknowledgment of God's righteousness and one's guilt, the lowered face.
- **T4.2.3** — Shows a penitent, covenant-aware relationship — the person owns guilt before a righteous God and seeks restoration.
- **T4.3.1** — Person → person: NT admonitory shame — one provokes shame in another to correct ("that he may be ashamed," 2Th 3:14; 1Cor 6:5; 15:34).
- **T4.3.2** — Done in love, not contempt — "not… to make you ashamed, but to admonish you as my beloved children" (1Cor 4:14): parental care.
- **T4.3.3** — The admonisher must speak from blameless standing — "sound speech that cannot be condemned" (Tit 2:8).
- **T4.4.1** — Receiving: the admonished one is to take up the shame and turn (2Th 3:14).
- **T4.4.2** — Uptake is blocked by "having no knowledge of God" (1Cor 15:34) and by the shameless condition (C-neg).
- **T4.4.3** — The one who meets it but does not take it up is the shameless (char C-neg).
- **T4.5.1** — Operates within covenant bonds (Israel; the church as "beloved children," "brothers").
- **T4.5.2** — Predominantly covenantal/in-community — distinct from imposed shame on the nations (char D/E).
- **T4.5.3** — Scope: those in covenant relationship with God / within the believing community.
- **T4.6.1** — **Silent** — no angelic/adversarial interface in C's evidence.
- **T4.6.2** — **Silent.**
- **T4.6.3** — **Silent.**

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
- **T5.1.1** — Yes — it transforms both condition and orientation: "after I was instructed… I was ashamed… I relented" (Jer 31:19) — shame is the hinge of repentance.
- **T5.1.2** — The turning it produces is presented as enduring — cessation, "never open your mouth again" (Eze 16:63). [enduring in the evidence]
- **T5.2.1** — Sequence is explicit: turned away → instructed/relented → ashamed → confounded → struck thigh (Jer 31:19); and remember → be ashamed/confounded → silenced → atoned (Eze 16:61-63).
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: instruction/discipline and confrontation with one's deeds (Jer 31:19; Eze 43:10 "describe the temple, that they may be ashamed"); in the NT, admonition.
- **T5.3.2** — Varies — spontaneous conviction (Ezr/Dan), commanded (Eze 36:32), instructed (Jer 31:19), externally admonished (NT).
- **T5.4.1** — Operates within affliction: shame borne amid exile/judgment (Ezr 9:7; Dan 9 captivity context); affliction is its setting.
- **T5.4.2** — The affliction of exile brings the sin to memory and deepens the penitent shame (Eze 36; Dan 9). [inferential]
- **T5.5.1** — Central to sanctification — shame-at-sin is the penitent turn that precedes and enables restoration (Eze 43:10-11).
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatologically oriented — toward final atonement/restoration where the shame is resolved (Eze 16:63; 36; 43; Jer 31).

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics
- **T6.1.1** — Within C's verses, shame co-occurs with guilt/iniquity (Ezr 9:6,7; Dan 9), with "confounded" (the bosh/ka.lam pairing), and with disgrace/dishonour owned ("let our dishonor cover us," Jer 3:25).
- **T6.1.2** — It sits at the junction of guilt, memory, and repentance — the affective face of conviction. [inferential]
- **T6.2.1** — Within C, shame follows instruction/recognition and precedes turning/atonement (sequence above).
- **T6.2.2** — Developmental and causal — the shame drives the turn.
- **T6.3.1** — Produces repentance/turning (Jer 31:19 "I relented"). 
- **T6.3.2** — Produced by conviction/instruction and the recognition of guilt.
- **T6.3.3** — A constituent element of conviction/repentance. [inferential — cross-char confirmation pending]
- **T6.4.1** — Shares lemmas with chars A, B, D, E, G: bosh, ka.lam, bo.shet, ke.lim.mah (same terms, different use); entrepō/entropē shared with E/G.
- **T6.4.2** — Root sharing: bosh/bo.shet share the BWŠ root; ka.lam/ke.lim.mah share the KLM root — the same roots generate both the felt-shame and the borne-disgrace terms used here.
- **T6.4.3** — Conscience-shame is lexically continuous with disgrace-shame; what distinguishes it is the Godward, self-at-own-sin orientation, not the vocabulary.
- **T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbours: char A (felt shame) and char D (borne disgrace). Distinction: C is shame at one's *own sin before God*; A is shame at falsified trust; D is disgrace as imposed/borne standing. [flag for cross-char confirmation]
- **T6.5.2** — Precise boundary = the *cause*: own sin coram Deo (C) vs external defeat (A) vs another's act (E).
- **T6.5.3** — Difference of **direction** (self-at-own-sin, Godward) and **moral register** (conviction), not merely degree.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
- **T7.1.1** — Primary terms: bosh (BWŠ — be ashamed, confounded), ka.lam (KLM — be humiliated, blush), bo.shet (shame), ke.lim.mah (disgrace), entrepō/entropē (turn-in, be made ashamed).
- **T7.1.2** — Both verbs (bosh, ka.lam, entrepō) and nouns (bo.shet, ke.lim.mah, entropē) — the act of being shamed and the borne state.
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: blushing/being confounded → bearing disgrace → being admonished to shame.
- **T7.1.4** — Verb (act of being ashamed) vs noun (borne shame); the conscience-use foregrounds the act + recognition.
- **T7.1.5** — Absence/opposite: shamelessness ("did not know how to blush," cross-ref C-neg); atonement removes the ground (Eze 16:63).
- **T7.1.6** — **Silent** — no person-type term in C's evidence.
- **T7.1.7** — No dedicated seeking-lemma, but the confessional-prayer form (Dan 9; Ezr 9) is the vehicle of seeking. [partial]
- **T7.1.8** — OT = penitential conviction before God (Hebrew terms); NT = admonitory shame toward repentance (entrepō). Continuity: shame as the doorway to turning; development: NT foregrounds relational correction within the church.
- **T7.1.9** — **Silent** — no NT coinage; entrepō/entropē are existing terms used in the corrective sense.
- **T7.1.10** — Full arc: act + state, Hebrew + Greek, conviction → admonition — shame functioning to turn the person from sin.
- **T7.2.1** — The term governs the confession (Ezr 9:6 verb heads "I am ashamed… to lift my face"), the imperative (Eze 36:32), or the corrective clause ("I say this to your shame," 1Cor 6:5).
- **T7.2.2** — Forms: penitential prayer (Ezr 9; Dan 9), prophetic covenant-lawsuit (Eze 16; 36; 43; Jer 3; 31), epistolary admonition (1Cor; 2Th; Tit) — each read on its own terms.
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Dan 9:7-8): premise (God is righteous) + premise (we have sinned) → conclusion (ours is the open shame).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: covenantal/judicial (lawsuit, exile) and liturgical (confessional prayer).
- **T7.2.5** — Primary anchor: **Ezr 9:6** — "I am ashamed and blush to lift my face to you, my God, for our iniquities…" — the fullest expression of conscience-shame before God.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor uniquely shows shame as the *inability to lift the face to God* under the weight of guilt — the vertical, coram-Deo essence the other verses assume.
- **T7.3.1** — Lens: clinical/moral psychology — the shame/guilt distinction (Lewis; Tangney & Dearing) [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract, Sec1/3].
- **T7.3.2** — Illumination: this characteristic sits closest to science's *guilt* pole — shame at the deed, leading to reparative turning — which matches the repentance outcome the verses show (Jer 31:19). Honour-shame framing adds the Godward, public dimension.
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: science generally treats shame as maladaptive; here shame at sin is *appropriate and God-commanded*, ordered toward restoration (Eze 36:32; 43:10-11) — a normative reversal of the clinical default.
- **T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced: science's somatic markers (downcast face, hiding, gaze-aversion) are present in the verses (blush, the unliftable face, struck thigh) and worth tagging explicitly; and the shame→repentance mechanism invites testing against the programme's repentance/turning vocabulary.
