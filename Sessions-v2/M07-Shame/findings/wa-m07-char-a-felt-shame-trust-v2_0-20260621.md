# M07 — Characteristic A: Felt shame at falsified trust / dashed expectation
**File:** wa-m07-char-a-felt-shame-trust-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (v1_0 = occurrence list only; v2_0 adds verse-read analysis).
**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 20 (bosh ×16, cha.pher ×2, ka.lam ×2 — all OT, all Hebrew).
**Prior:** wa-m07-deepread-analysis-v1-20260621.md; evidence set wa-m07-evidence-allocc-v1-20260621.json.

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** In this band, shame is not first a moral feeling about a wrong done; it is the *experiential exposure of a trust that proved false*. The person (or nation) leaned on something — an idol, an ally, a hope — and the thing gave way; the shame is the inner collapse of that misplaced reliance. The diagnostic is the **object of trust**, and across the band it is overwhelmingly an idol or a false refuge: "carved idols… metal images" (Isa 42:17), "the makers of idols" (Isa 45:16), "Chemosh… Bethel, their confidence" (Jer 48:13), "worthless idols" (Psa 97:7), "his idols… images are false, and there is no breath in them" (Jer 51:17), "his idol" (Hos 10:6), and the political hopes "Cush their hope and Egypt their boast" (Isa 20:5). The shame transfers from the emptiness of the trusted object to the exposure of the one who trusted it.

**Why it is the only band tagged affect *and* cognition.** The verses justify the double faculty directly. Shame here is a *falsified expectation* — a cognitive event — felt as dismay: "They are ashamed because they were confident; they come there and are disappointed" (Job 6:20). The reliance was a judgement about what would hold; when the judgement is proven wrong, the knowing and the feeling fail together. This is why the band's vocabulary pairs shame with a whole cluster of collapse-states: **confounded** (ka.lam — Isa 41:11; 45:16; Jer 14:3), **dismayed** (cha.tat — Isa 20:5; 37:27; Jer 14:4), **terrified** and **dread** (Isa 44:11), **anxiety** and "melt in fear" (Jer 49:23). Shame is the affective face of an expectation that has just been falsified.

**How it operates — voice and direction.** The experiencer is "other" in every occurrence: this is shame *pronounced over* the wrongly-trusting in the prophetic oracle voice (Moab, Damascus, Egypt's clients, the idol-makers), declared as the certain outcome of false reliance rather than narrated as a first-person feeling. Its valence sits mostly **neutral** (a stated consequence) shading to **sinful** where the misplaced trust is idolatry (Isa 44:9,11; Psa 97:7; Hos 10:6) — the moral weight enters through the *object*, not the emotion itself.

**Somatic expression.** Where the band touches the body it is the gesture of dashed hope: "they are ashamed and confounded and **cover their heads**" (Jer 14:3; cf. 14:4, the farmers with no rain), "turned back" (Isa 42:17), "melt… troubled like the sea" (Jer 49:23). The shame of failed trust shows outwardly as covering, turning, dissolving.

**The contrastive apex.** One verse inverts the field and exposes its logic: "the moon will be confounded and the sun ashamed, for the Lord of hosts reigns… and his glory" (Isa 24:23). Here even the brightest created lights are "ashamed/confounded" before the true glory. Set against this, the whole band reads as the shaming of every false radiance and false refuge before the one trustworthy object — which is exactly the positive pole gathered under characteristic B (trust in God that is *not* put to shame).

**Boundary (read from the cause).** This band is defined by its *cause*: the falsification of trust. That distinguishes it from characteristic C (shame at one's *own sin* before God — there the cause is moral guilt, not a failed reliance) and from characteristic E (disgrace *inflicted* by an agent). Where a verse's shame arises because the trusted object collapsed, it belongs here.

**Significance for the inner being.** This characteristic shows the inner person as constitutively a *trusting / relying* faculty, and shame as the experiential signal that the trust was placed in what cannot bear it. It is the negative diagnostic of where the self has anchored itself — and so it sits directly on the programme's question of the inner being's orientation and its interfaces (here, the interface with idols and false refuges, over against God).

### Internal movements (grounded)
- **A1 — Idol-trust exposed.** Isa 42:17; 44:9; 44:11; 45:16; Jer 51:17; Psa 97:7; Hos 10:6; Jer 48:13 (Chemosh/Bethel). Shame as the verdict on trusting the breathless/false.
- **A2 — Political/false-refuge trust exposed.** Isa 20:5 (Cush/Egypt); Jer 49:23 (Damascus, "bad news"); 2Ki 19:26 / Isa 37:27 (the strengthless inhabitants); Jer 14:3,4 (the failed cisterns / no rain).
- **A3 — The falsified-confidence core.** Job 6:20 ("confident… disappointed"); Zec 9:5 ("hopes are confounded"). The explicit confidence→disappointment movement.
- **A4 — Abashment before the true glory (contrastive).** Isa 24:23 — false radiance confounded before the Lord's glory; the hinge to characteristic B.

### Pattern notes
- **Faculty:** affect + cognition on all bosh occ (the band's signature); affect on cha.pher/ka.lam.
- **Experiencer:** other ×20 — prophetic-oracle declaration, not first-person.
- **Valence:** neutral (consequence) dominant; sinful where the trust is idolatry.
- **Seat:** none tagged — recorded as situational/relational (the shame of a failed reliance), not located in a constitutional seat.
- **Co-terms:** trust (ba.tach), confidence (miv.tach), expectation (mab.bat), boast (ha.lal), deception (she.qer), and the collapse-cluster confounded/dismayed/dread/anxiety.
