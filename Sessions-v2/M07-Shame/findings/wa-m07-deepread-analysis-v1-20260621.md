## H0954 bosh — 108 occ — lemma: "to put to shame, be ashamed, be disconcerted, be disappointed"
Faculty tag: affect + cognition (the only M07 term carrying cognition). The verses justify this: bosh repeatedly couples shame with *confounded / dismayed / disappointed* — a cognitive collapse of expectation, not affect alone.

Within-lemma movements the verses actually show (each grounded in cited verses):

**(b1) Shame as the collapse of misplaced confidence / falsified trust.** The dominant pattern. Job 6:20 "ashamed because they were confident… and are disappointed"; idol-trusters put to shame (Isa 42:17; 44:9,11; 45:16; Jer 10-related 51:17; Psa 97:7; Hos 10:6); "ashamed because of Cush their hope and Egypt their boast" (Isa 20:5); "ashamed of Chemosh… their confidence" (Jer 48:13); "hopes are confounded" (Zec 9:5). Inner state = exposure of a trust whose ground gave way. This is the seat of the cognition tag.

**(b2) Shame / not-shame as the stake of trust in God (vindication axis).** Petition form: "let me not be put to shame, for I take refuge in you" (Psa 25:2,20; 31:1,17; 71:1; 119:116). Affirmation: "in you they trusted and were not put to shame" (Psa 22:5). Here not-shame = vindicated/rescued; shame = trust shown vain. The state is covenantal — bound to whether hope in God is honoured. Honour is the positive pole of the same axis.

**(b3) Shame as imposed judicial / eschatological verdict.** Imprecation + oracle: "let them be put to shame who seek my life" (Psa 35:4; 40:14; 70:2; 71:13; 83:17; 109:28; 129:5); nations/idols put to shame across Isa/Jer/Mic/Zep/Zec. Shame here is a *status assigned / public dishonour*, often with God as agent (Psa 14:6; 44:7; 53:5), sometimes carrying its felt residue (cover face, wail, turn back — Jer 48:39; 51:51).

**(b4) Shame as conscience-response to one's own sin before God.** Ezr 9:6 "I am ashamed and blush to lift my face to you… for our iniquities"; Jer 31:19 "I was ashamed and confounded, because I bore the disgrace of my youth"; Eze 16:63 "be confounded… because of your shame, when I atone for you"; Eze 36:32 (commanded) "Be ashamed and confounded for your ways." Shame here is the fitting inward acknowledgment of guilt — a conscience function.

**(b5) Shamelessness as moral defect (the negative space of b4).** Jer 6:15 = 8:12 "Were they ashamed when they committed abomination? No… they did not know how to blush." The *capacity* to feel shame at wrong is treated as a moral good; its absence is corruption. This is a second-order observation about the faculty itself.

**(b6) Relational / household disgrace one party brings on another.** Proverbs, experiencer=None: "a son who brings shame" (Pro 10:5; 17:2; 19:26; 29:15); "she who brings shame is like rottenness in his bones" (Pro 12:4). Honour–shame social fabric; the shame is borne by the household, not (only) the actor.

**(b7) Plain social embarrassment / propriety (low-affect).** Gen 2:25 "naked and not ashamed" (pre-shame innocence); 2Ki 2:17 "urged him till he was ashamed"; 2Ki 8:11 "embarrassed." Ordinary social-affect register, no moral/covenantal load.

Note on seat: location populated only 8× (soul 7, heart 1) — shame is rarely localised to a constitutional seat in the text; it is overwhelmingly relational/situational.

## H3637 ka.lam — 38 occ — lemma: "to insult, shame, humiliate, blush, be ashamed, be reproached; (also) to harm/injure"
Faculty tag: affect. But the verses show a strong **transitive/agent** band absent from bosh.

**(k1) Humiliation as injury done TO another (transitive).** Rut 2:15 "do not reproach her" (forbidden); Job 19:3 "cast reproach upon me… ashamed to wrong me"; Pro 25:8 "your neighbour puts you to shame"; Pro 28:7 "a companion of gluttons shames his father." Shame here is an *act of social injury*, the agent side of the cluster.
**(k2) Face / military honour-loss.** Num 12:14 "shamed seven days" (spitting in face); 2Sa 10:5 = 1Ch 19:5 "greatly ashamed" (beards cut off); 2Sa 19:3 "ashamed when they flee in battle." Honour–face register.
**(k3) Shame AT one's own iniquity — can be righteous/desired (conscience).** Eze 16:54,61; 36:32; and esp. Eze 43:10-11 (valence=righteous) "that they may be ashamed of their iniquities" — being ashamed of sin is the *sought* response that precedes restoration. Confirms a conscience function where shame is morally good.
**(k4) Blush / the capacity to be ashamed (shared axis with bosh b4-b5).** Ezr 9:6 "blush"; Jer 6:15 = 8:12 "did not know how to blush"; Jer 3:3 "you refuse to be ashamed" — shamelessness as defect.
**(k5) Distinct sense: harm/injure (NOT shame).** 1Sa 25:7,15 "we did them no harm… missed nothing." The lemma also carries "injure"; these occurrences are the harm-sense, not inner shame — flag as possible non-inner-being or separate-sense (relevance check needed).

## H1322 bo.shet — 29 occ — lemma: "shame" (noun)
**(bo1) Shame as a borne condition / covering / garment.** "clothed with shame" (Job 8:22; Psa 35:26; 109:29; 132:18); "shame has covered my face" (Psa 44:15); "wrapped in their own shame as a cloak" (Psa 109:29). Shame as an enveloping standing, imposed or borne — not a momentary affect.
**(bo2) The deserved disgrace of sin / idolatry; metonym for idols.** Dan 9:7,8 "to us belongs open shame… because we have sinned"; Jer 3:24,25; 11:13; Hab 2:10 "devised shame for your house"; Hos 9:10 "the shameful thing" (= idol). bosheth frequently names the disgrace intrinsic to sin, sometimes standing in for the idol itself.
**(bo3) Shame↔honour reversal by God.** Isa 61:7 "instead of your shame… a double portion"; Zep 3:19 "change their shame into praise." A condition God reverses.
**(bo4) Lament — a life/days spent in shame.** Jer 20:18 "spend my days in shame."

## H3639 ke.lim.mah — 29 occ — lemma: "disgrace, reproach, dishonour, confusion, insult, ignominy"
**(ke1) Borne disgrace as a covering over the face (twin of bo1).** "dishonor has covered my face" (Psa 69:7; 44:15; 51:51). Public reproach worn visibly.
**(ke2) Corporate / national reproach — borne, then removed by God.** Eze 34:29; 36:6,7,15; 39:26; 44:13 "bear / suffer the reproach of the nations," with God's promise to end it. A collective dimension distinct from individual shame.
**(ke3) Disgrace as consequence of folly / sin.** Pro 18:13 "his folly and shame"; Eze 16:52,54.
**(ke4) Honour→shame reversal (negative direction).** Psa 4:2 "how long shall my honour be turned into shame?"
**(ke5) Innocent / vicarious disgrace borne willingly (Servant).** Isa 50:6 "I hid not my face from disgrace and spitting" — disgrace accepted by the righteous sufferer (pairs with bosh Isa 50:7).

## H8213 sha.phel — 22 occ — lemma: "to be/become low, sink, be humbled, be abased" — NO faculty tag on any occurrence
The verses are not about felt shame at all. Every occurrence is **positional/status lowering, specifically the abasement of pride/haughtiness**, almost always paired with "exalt/lift up" and "haughty/proud/lofty":
- 1Sa 2:7; 2Sa 22:28 = Psa 18:27 "the haughty eyes you bring down"; Job 40:11 "abase the proud"; Psa 75:7; 147:6; Pro 25:7; Pro 29:23 "pride brings him low, but he who is lowly in spirit obtains honour"; Isa 2:9,11,12,17; 5:15; 10:33; 13:11; 25:11 "lay low his pompous pride"; 26:5; Eze 17:24; 21:26 "exalt the low, abase the high."
**Observation:** sha.phel names a *movement of status* (high→low), the divine humbling of pride, not an inner affect. It is the structural inverse of exaltation. **Boundary item:** belongs to the honour/status axis and overlaps M09 (humility/meekness — "lowly in spirit"); whether it is an M07 inner-being characteristic, an M09 term, or the *action that produces* shame is a discovery decision (verse evidence here points to status, not affect).

## H7036 qa.lon — 17 occ — lemma: "shame, disgrace, dishonour, ignominy" — faculty: affect
**(q1) Disgrace as the deserved wisdom-literature consequence of folly/pride.** Pro 3:35 "fools get disgrace"; 6:33; 11:2 "when pride comes, then comes disgrace"; 13:18; 18:3; 22:10 — disgrace systematically paired against honour for the wise. Wisdom-literature concentration.
**(q2) Disgrace as public exposure / shameful uncovering under judgment.** Jer 13:26 "lift up your skirts… your shame will be seen"; Nah 3:5; Hab 2:16 "show your uncircumcision"; Isa 22:18. Shame as forced exposure of a city/nation.
**(q3) Glory↔shame exchange.** Hos 4:7 "change their glory into shame"; Hab 2:16 "shame instead of glory."
**(q4) Shame embraced — moral perversion.** Hos 4:18 "their rulers dearly love shame."
**(q5) Felt disgrace under affliction/guilt.** Job 10:15 "filled with disgrace."

## H2659 cha.pher — 16 occ — lemma: "to be ashamed, confounded, abashed" — faculty: affect; **location=soul on 4 occ** (the most interiorised term)
**(c1) The "confounded / disappointed" partner of bosh** — appears in the very same imprecatory Psalms beside bosh: Psa 35:4,26; 40:14; 70:2; 71:24; 83:17. Carries the dashed-expectation / disorientation sense; Job 6:20 "ashamed because they were confident… disappointed."
**(c2) The vindication pole (never ashamed).** Psa 34:5 "those who look to him are radiant, their faces shall never be ashamed" — the positive outcome of trust (twin of bosh b2).
**(c3) Cosmic abashment before God's glory.** Isa 24:23 "the moon confounded and the sun ashamed, for the Lord of hosts reigns."
**(c4) Most soul-seated shame term.** location=soul (nephesh) on Psa 35:4; 40:14; 70:2; Jer 15:9 — chapher is shame registered in the soul, the strongest internal-seat signal in the cluster.

# EMERGENT STRUCTURE — derived from the COMPLETE read of all 359 occurrences
(Bottom-up; each band is what the verses actually do, not an imposed category. Boundaries between B/G and C/H are genuine interpretive decisions flagged below.)

**A. Felt shame at falsified trust / dashed expectation** — the experiential collapse when confidence proves vain; carries a cognitive disorientation (confounded/dismayed). bosh(b1), chapher(c1), Job 6:20, idol-trust verses. The core felt inner state; the only band tagged affect+cognition.

**B. The trust→vindication axis (shame vs honour as the outcome of hope in God)** — "let me not be put to shame, for I take refuge in you" / "hope does not put us to shame" / "whoever believes will not be put to shame." Psalms + Pauline continuity, incl. eschatological confidence-at-judgment (NT). bosh(b2), chapher(c2), kataischunō, aischunō, 1Jo 2:28.

**C. Conscience — shame at one's own sin (the morally-fitting inward response)** — can be righteous/commanded. Ezr 9:6; Jer 31:19; Eze 16/36/43; kalam(k3).

**C-neg. Shamelessness — the absence/inversion of the shame-faculty as moral defect** — "did not know how to blush," "glory in their shame," "love shame." Jer 6:15=8:12; 3:3; Phili 3:19; Hos 4:18. (Negative space of C; possibly its own characteristic.)

**D. Disgrace as a borne condition / standing (covering, garment); deserved by sin or imposed by God** — bosheth, kelimmah, bushah, qalon, atimia, aischunē, kelimmut, qiqalon, boshnah. Enveloping status, often judicial; incl. "the shameful thing"=idol metonym.

**E. Humiliation/dishonour/contempt as an act done to another (agent pole) + contempt as a heart-disposition** — kalam(k1), atimazō, kataischunō(dishonour), exoutheneō (despise), qalah, paradeigmatizō; verbal-abuse edge loidoria/kakologeō.

**F. Abasement — the bringing-low of pride (status reversal high↔low)** — shaphel (all 22, no affect). Divine humbling of the haughty. **Borders M09 (humility/meekness).**

**G. The honour↔shame / glory↔shame reversal as relational standing before God & community** — a cross-cutting axis recurring through D and entrepō (respect↔shame): Isa 61:7; Zep 3:19; Psa 4:2; Hos 4:7; 1Cor 15:43. May be an axis rather than a discrete characteristic.

**H. Propriety — what is "shameful/disgraceful" (decency norms)** — aischros, aschēmosunē; the normative judgment of disgraceful conduct/exposure.

**EDGE / RELEVANCE-DECISION items (verse evidence points away from inner-being state):**
- **J. Innocence / cleanness (antonym pole)** — niqqavon, qehavon. Clean hands / innocent heart; the moral opposite of disgrace, not shame itself.
- **K. Silencing, cast-off, defilement** — fimoō, afōnos, apoballō, nidah. Behavioural/physical/defilement; largely not inner-being states.
- **Sense-split:** kalam "harm/injure" (1Sa 25:7,15) is a non-shame sense of the lemma.
