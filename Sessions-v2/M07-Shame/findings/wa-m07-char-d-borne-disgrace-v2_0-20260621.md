# M07 — Characteristic D: Disgrace as a borne condition / standing (covering); imposed or deserved
**File:** wa-m07-char-d-borne-disgrace-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (adds verse-read analysis).
**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 136 — the cluster's largest and most heterogeneous band: bosh (45), ke.lim.mah (27), bo.shet (22), qa.lon (14), atimia (7), ka.lam (6), cha.pher (5), bu.shah (4), aischunē (3), + bosh.nah, ke.lim.mut, qi.qa.lon (1 each).
**Prior:** wa-m07-deepread-analysis-v1-20260621.md; evidence set wa-m07-evidence-allocc-v1-20260621.json.

> **Scope note (flagged for the interpret phase).** This band gathers shame *as a borne standing* — what a person or people **wears, bears, or is sentenced to** — as distinct from the felt collapse of trust (char A) or the relational verdict on trust (char B). Reading the 136 verses, D is wide enough to contain at least two sub-structures that the interpret phase should weigh separating: **(i) the imposed judicial verdict** (shame *pronounced over* nations, idolaters, and the wicked), and **(ii) innocent/vicarious borne disgrace** (the Servant, Christ) which is the moral opposite of deserved disgrace. These are kept here, fully analysed and flagged, not pre-split.

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** In this band shame is a **standing**, not primarily a passing feeling — a condition of dishonour that one *bears*, *wears*, or is *sentenced to*. Its grammar is telling: people are **"clothed with shame"** (Job 8:22; Psa 35:26; 109:29; 132:18), **"wrapped in their own shame as a cloak"** (Psa 109:29), with **"shame… covered my face"** (Psa 44:15; 69:7; 51:51), commanded to **"bear your disgrace"** (Eze 16:52,54; 32:24; 44:13). Shame is something put on, covering the person — and the recurring somatic locus is the **face** (covered, "on all faces," Eze 7:18; the skirts lifted so "your shame will be seen," Jer 13:26; Nah 3:5).

**Six movements the verses show.**
1. **The imposed judicial verdict** — much of bosh. In the prophetic oracle shame is *pronounced* as sentence over the nations and the wicked: Moab (Jer 48:1,20,39), Egypt (46:24), Babylon (50:2; 51:47), Damascus, the wise men (8:9), the idol-makers, "the house of Israel shall be shamed: they, their kings, their officials" (2:26). Here shame is a declared status, often with the Lord as the one who imposes or guarantees it.
2. **Borne disgrace as covering/garment** — ke.lim.mah, bo.shet, bu.shah: the cloak/covering imagery above. Shame as an enveloping condition worn visibly.
3. **The deserved disgrace of sin and idolatry** — bo.shet frequently *is* the disgrace intrinsic to the idol ("the **shameful thing** has devoured," Jer 3:24; altars "to shame," Jer 11:13; "consecrated themselves to the shameful thing," Hos 9:10; "devised shame for your house," Hab 2:10).
4. **The deserved disgrace of folly and pride (wisdom register)** — qa.lon: "When **pride** comes, then comes **disgrace**" (Pro 11:2); "the wise will inherit honour, but **fools get disgrace**" (Pro 3:35; 6:33; 13:18; 18:3). Disgrace as the lawful wage of folly, paired against honour.
5. **Corporate / national reproach borne, then removed by God** — ke.lim.mah in Ezekiel: "bear / **suffer the reproach of the nations**" (Eze 34:29; 36:6,7,15; 39:26), with God's promise to end it ("I will not let you hear anymore the reproach"). A collective standing God lifts.
6. **Dishonour as standing over against glory** — atimia: "It is **sown in dishonour**; it is raised in glory" (1Cor 15:43); vessels "for **dishonourable use**" (Rom 9:21; 2Ti 2:20); "through honour and dishonour" (2Cor 6:8). Dishonour as a quality/standing contrasted with glory (the hinge to char G).

**Two flagged specials within D.**
- **Public exposure / uncovering** as the form disgrace takes under judgment: "I myself will lift up your skirts over your face, and your **shame will be seen**" (Jer 13:26; Nah 3:5; Mic 1:11 "nakedness and shame"; Rev 3:18). Disgrace enacted as forced exposure.
- **Innocent / vicarious borne disgrace** — the moral inversion of the whole band: the Servant, "I gave my back to those who strike… I hid not my face from **disgrace** and spitting" (Isa 50:6), and Christ, "who… endured the cross, **despising the shame**" (Heb 12:2). Here disgrace is *accepted by the guiltless*, the opposite of the deserved standing elsewhere in D. [flag for interpret phase]

**Shame ordered toward repentance.** Even within this judicial band, disgrace is sometimes a means, not only a sentence: "**Fill their faces with shame, that they may seek your name**, O Lord" (Psa 83:16); and the restoration verses promise its removal ("instead of your shame there shall be a double portion," Isa 61:7 — the hinge to char G).

**Significance for the inner being.** This characteristic shows shame as the inner person's **standing of honour or dishonour before God and the community** — a status borne on the self, frequently visible on the face, that can be imposed as sentence, deserved through sin or folly, suffered corporately, or (exceptionally) accepted innocently. It engages the inner being as the bearer of public worth/standing: shame here is what the self *is reckoned to be* in the eyes of God and others, not merely what it feels.

### Pattern notes
- **Faculty:** affect (Greek/Hebrew); the imposed-verdict bosh verses also carry cognition. **Type:** action + status — the largest concentration of *status/condition* terms in the cluster (bo.shet, ke.lim.mah, qa.lon, atimia).
- **Experiencer:** predominantly other / other-addressed (the band is about disgrace *borne by* or *pronounced over* a party). **Valence:** sinful (deserved) and neutral (declared) dominant; a few righteous (Ezr 8:22; the Servant verses).
- **Seat:** the **face** recurs as the somatic site (covered, on all faces, exposed); otherwise relational/situational. **how-field:** clothed / covered / wrapped / bear / suffer — the covering-and-bearing grammar.
- **Divine involvement:** God as the one who imposes the verdict, guarantees it, or lifts it from his people. **Co-terms:** reproach (cher.pah), dishonour, glory (the contrast pole), and the covering verbs.
- **Boundary:** D = shame as a *borne standing*. Overlaps A (idol-shame felt), B (enemy-shame = the vindication pole), E (some imposed cases shade into the agent pole), G (glory↔shame reversal), C ("bear your disgrace for your sins" sits next to conscience). Defining note: a *condition worn/borne/sentenced*, not the felt collapse (A) or the relational verdict on trust (B).
