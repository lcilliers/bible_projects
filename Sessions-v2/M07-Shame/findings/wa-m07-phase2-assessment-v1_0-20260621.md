**Key point for the researcher:** unlike the v1_0 scripted findings, **none of the remaining Silence is recoverable by re-reading** — it is either structural to the band type, a true faculty/interface absence, or a cross-phase deferral. ## PART B — Findings stated without direct verse evidence cited

These are answers that rest on **inference or interpretive judgement** rather than a quoted verse. They are legitimate (the catalogue's T0 design questions *ask* for theological inference), but they are flagged so you can review them as interpretation, not data.

**Explicitly tagged `[inferential]` (12 instances):** A ×4, B ×1, C ×6, H/J/K/RESID file ×1.

**Systematic categories of uncited inference:**
1. **Divine-image / created-design inferences (T0.2.2, T0.3.1–.3.3).** Across every band these answers reason *from* the shame phenomenon *to* the design of the image (e.g., "the capacity to be shamed presupposes a trusting faculty made for God"). This is **theological inference**, not a verse statement — appropriate to the tier, but not evidence-grounded. Present in all 12 bands.
2. **Cross-characteristic relationship claims (T6.3.2, T6.5.1).** Statements that one band "produces," "is the antonym of," or "is the axis under" another (e.g., E produces D; C-neg is the failure-state of C; G is the axis under D/E). These are **structural judgements** awaiting cross-char confirmation — each is marked `[flag: cross-char]`.
3. **Science-lens answers (T7.3.1–.3.4).** Every T7.3 answer is attributed in-line to the science extract (`[source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract …]`), **not** to Scripture, and is kept distinct from the biblical analysis per the science-placement rule. These are sourced, but the source is the extract, not the verses.

**Assessment:** no answer in the *evidential* tiers (T1–T5, T7.1–T7.2) asserts a verse-claim without a citation — after Phase 2, every such claim names its verse(s). The uncited material is confined to (1) the T0 image-inferences, (2) the flagged cross-char judgements, and (3) the attributed science lens — all three being interpretive by the catalogue's own design, and all marked as such.
