# M07 — Characteristic C-neg: Shamelessness — the absence or inversion of the shame-faculty
**File:** wa-m07-char-cneg-shamelessness-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (adds verse-read analysis).
**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 8 — bosh, ka.lam, qa.lon (OT); aischunē (NT).

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** This is the **negative image of conscience-shame (characteristic C)**: the inner condition in which the shame-signal at one's own sin is **dead, refused, or inverted**. Scripture treats it not as freedom from shame but as a grave moral-spiritual defect, and attaches judgment to it.

**Three forms the verses show.**
1. **Inability — the atrophied faculty.** "Were they ashamed when they committed abomination? No, they were not at all ashamed; **they did not know how to blush**" (Jer 6:15 = 8:12). The capacity to feel shame at one's own wrong is simply gone — and the very next clause is judgment: "therefore they shall fall."
2. **Refusal — willed hardening.** "You have the **forehead of a whore**; you **refuse to be ashamed**" (Jer 3:3). Here the brazen face is the deliberate inverse of the blush — shame is not absent by accident but refused.
3. **Inversion — shame embraced and boasted.** "They **glory in their shame**, with minds set on earthly things" (Phili 3:19); "their rulers **dearly love shame**" (Hos 4:18); "casting up the **foam of their own shame**" (Jude 13). The faculty is reversed: what should produce shame produces pride.

**The face as the diagnostic.** In characteristic C true shame shows as the blush and the unliftable face; here shamelessness shows as the **brazen forehead** "of a whore" (Jer 3:3). The face is the somatic site of both the presence and the absence of shame.

**The moral company it keeps.** Shamelessness recurs with sexual immorality (za.nah — Jer 3:3; Hos 4:18) and abomination (to.e.vah — Jer 6:15; 8:12), and with a disordered mind ("their god is their belly… minds set on earthly things," Phili 3:19, co-term froneō "to reason"). It is consistently bound to judgment: "they shall fall" (Jer 6:15), "their end is destruction" (Phili 3:19), "the gloom of utter darkness reserved forever" (Jude 13).

**Significance for the inner being.** This characteristic establishes, by its absence, that the **capacity to feel shame at one's own wrong is itself a moral good** — a working conscience. Its loss (inability), its hardening (refusal), and its perversion (glorying) are three degrees of corruption of that faculty. For the programme's question this is a second-order finding about the inner being: the shame/conscience faculty can be alive (char C), or dead, refused, or inverted (char C-neg), and the latter is treated as a defining mark of the reprobate condition.

### Internal movements (grounded)
- **Cn1 Inability ("could not blush"):** Jer 6:15; Jer 8:12.
- **Cn2 Refusal (the brazen face):** Jer 3:3.
- **Cn3 Inversion (glory in / love shame):** Phili 3:19; Hos 4:18; Jude 13.

### Pattern notes
- **Faculty:** the negative space of conscience/moral-evaluation — the verses concern the *failure* of the shame-faculty, so faculty tags are sparse by nature.
- **Experiencer:** other / none (indictment voice). **Valence:** sinful (and the neutral-tagged Jer 6:15/8:12 are plainly indictments).
- **Seat:** the face/forehead (brazenness) as the inverse of the blush. **Co-terms:** abomination (to.e.vah), fornication (za.nah), disordered reasoning (froneō).
- **Boundary:** strictly the *absence/inversion* of the shame-faculty (esp. char C). Distinct from every band where shame is present and felt.
