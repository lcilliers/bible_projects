**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 2 — ka.lam (H3637) in its "harm / injure" sense. [FLAG: non-shame sense — exclude from M07 shame analysis.]

## Analytical narrative

**What this is.** Both occurrences are the **"harm / injure" sense** of ka.lam, which is a distinct sense of the lemma from "shame / humiliate": "your shepherds have been with us, and **we did them no harm**, and they missed nothing" (1Sa 25:7); "the men were very good to us, and **we suffered no harm**" (1Sa 25:15). Here ka.lam means to do injury, not to shame — and both verses *negate* it ("no harm"). There is **no inner-being shame content** in either verse.

**Significance / disposition.** These are a genuine **lexical sense-split**: the term entered the M07 extraction by lemma, but in these two verses it is not the shame-sense. They are gathered here so that **no verse is filtered out without record**; the interpret-phase decision is to exclude them from the M07 shame analysis as a non-shame sense of ka.lam.

### Pattern notes
- **Valence:** neutral. **Experiencer:** self (the speakers, David's men / Nabal's servant). **Boundary:** outside the inner-being shame field — sense-split, exclude. Flag retained.
