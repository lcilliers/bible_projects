# M07 — Characteristic H: Propriety — what is "shameful / disgraceful" (decency norms)
**File:** wa-m07-char-h-propriety-norms-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0** (adds verse-read analysis).
**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 9 — aischros (4), aischunē (2), aschēmosunē (2), qi.qa.lon (1).

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** This band is not shame *felt* or *borne* but the **normative judgement of what counts as disgraceful** — the community/moral standard of decency. Its grammar is "**it is shameful** to…": "it is **disgraceful** for a wife to cut off her hair" (1Cor 11:6); "it is **shameful** for a woman to speak in church" (1Cor 14:35); "it is **shameful** even to speak of the things they do in secret" (Eph 5:12); teaching "for **shameful** gain" (Tit 1:11).

**The disgust/exposure substrate.** Several occurrences attach the norm to bodily exposure and indecency: "**shameless acts**" (aschēmosunē, Rom 1:27); "keeping his garments on, that he may not go about **naked** and be seen **exposed**" (Rev 16:15); "the **shame** of your nakedness" (Rev 3:18); "show your **uncircumcision**… shame" (Hab 2:16, qi.qa.lon). And the renouncing of it: "we have renounced **disgraceful**, underhanded ways" (2Cor 4:2).

**Significance for the inner being.** H engages the inner being's **moral-evaluative** faculty operating as a *standard* — the held sense of what is disgraceful conduct or speech, against which behaviour is judged. It draws on the purity/disgust dimension (indecency, nakedness) elaborated into a moral-cultural norm. It is the *norm side* of shame: not the feeling, but the measure that determines what *ought* to occasion shame.

### Pattern notes
- **Valence:** sinful throughout (the conduct judged disgraceful). **Experiencer:** other / none (norm stated of conduct).
- **Boundary:** the *norm* of what is shameful (esp. bodily/sexual propriety), distinct from felt shame (A/C) and borne disgrace (D). Overlaps D (qi.qa.lon, aischunē exposure) and the science extract's "indecency/innocence" section.
