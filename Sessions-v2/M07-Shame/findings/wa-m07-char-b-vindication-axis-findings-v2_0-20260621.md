# M07 — Findings for Characteristic B: Trust → vindication axis (shame vs honour as the outcome of hope in God)
**File:** wa-m07-char-b-vindication-axis-findings-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0**. Verse-read re-answer.
**Evidence:** 56 occ — bosh (heavy in the Psalter), kataischunō (the NT pole), cha.pher, ka.lam, aischunō. The band's signature is the formula **"shall not be put to shame"** for the one who trusts God, set against the shaming of the adversary. Richest OT→NT continuity in the cluster.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — God is the **active vindicator**: "those who wait for me shall not be put to shame" (Isa 49:23); "the Lord God helps me; therefore I have not been disgraced" (Isa 50:7); "in you they trusted and were not put to shame" (Psa 22:5). Shame and its absence are dispensed by God toward those related to him by trust.
- **T0.1.2** — Not borne by God; God is the one who **keeps the truster from** shame and **lays it on** the adversary (Psa 35:4).
- **T0.2.1** — The role shown: shame functions as the **negative verdict that trust in God averts** — "hope does not put us to shame" (Rom 5:5). Its purpose is to mark the truster as vindicated and the treacherous as exposed (Psa 25:3).
- **T0.2.2** — Both: the **trusting faculty** is created design (made to rest in God and be honoured); the *threat* of shame is the fallen world's pressure that God's vindication answers.
- **T0.2.3** — Yes, strongly eschatological: "when he appears we may have confidence and **not shrink in shame** at his coming" (1Jo 2:28); "everyone who believes in him will not be put to shame" (Rom 10:11) — a final, public non-shaming of faith.
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the image as a **God-trusting being whose hope is honoured** — "those who look to him are radiant, and their faces shall never be ashamed" (Psa 34:5).
- **T0.3.2** — The honouring is God's; the trusting and its vindication are the creature's experience of the relation.
- **T0.3.3** — Present and rightly ordered: the image functioning as designed — resting in God and therefore unashamed.
- **T0.4.1** — The Zion-stone oracle (Isa 28:16 → Rom 9:33; 10:11; 1Pe 2:6) is typological: the one who believes on the cornerstone "will not be put to shame," fulfilled in Christ — direction = OT promise consummated in the NT.

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **vindication vs shame as the outcome of trust** — to "not be put to shame" is to be publicly upheld by God; to "be put to shame" (of the adversary) is to be exposed and defeated.
- **T1.1.2** — bosh (be ashamed) and kataischunō (put to shame), characteristically **negated** for the truster; the affect+cognition complex (confident hope vindicated).
- **T1.1.3** — Directional/relational: defined by the **trust-relation to God** and the contrasting relation of the adversary.
- **T1.2.1** — An **outcome/event** of vindication or shaming.
- **T1.2.2** — Compound: confident expectation (cognition) + the felt security/relief or dread (affect); bosh tagged affect+cognition throughout.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite within the band: the **shaming of the enemy/treacherous** (Psa 25:3; 35:4; 83:17) — the same axis inverted onto the adversary.
- **T1.3.2** — It excludes self-confidence apart from God — the treacherous "shall be ashamed" (Psa 25:3).
- **T1.3.3** — It is **not** felt shame at one's own sin (char C) nor the bearing of disgrace (char D); it is the *averted/inflicted verdict* keyed to trust.
- **T1.4.1** — Operates in **petition** ("let me not be put to shame," Psa 25:2; 31:1), **confession of confidence** ("I have not been disgraced," Isa 50:7), and **promise** ("you shall not be put to shame," Isa 45:17).
- **T1.4.2** — Three grammatical modes: prayer (jussive/cohortative), testimony (perfect), promise/citation (imperfect/future).
- **T1.4.3** — Yes — heavily in **direct address to God** (the Psalter's "let me not be put to shame").
- **T1.5.1** — For the truster the response is **radiance / unshaken standing** ("their faces shall never be ashamed," Psa 34:5; "I have set my face like a flint," Isa 50:7); for the adversary, being "turned back" and "dismayed."
- **T1.5.2** — Two consistent responses by party (vindicated radiance vs adversary's rout).
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: an **everlasting** non-shaming — "saved… with everlasting salvation; you shall not be put to shame… to all eternity" (Isa 45:17).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the rescue/relief; sustained = the abiding, even eternal, security of the vindicated.
- **T1.7.1** — Right operation: trust **rightly placed in God** is the condition under which shame is averted ("none who wait for you shall be put to shame," Psa 25:3).
- **T1.7.2** — Partial: the adversary's slander/reviling assaults the truster (1Pe 3:16), but God's vindication overturns it.
- **T1.7.3** — The threatened shame **fails to take hold** of the truster precisely because God upholds (Psa 119:116).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- **T2.1.1** — Seat language present: the **heart** — "may my heart be blameless in your statutes, that I may not be put to shame" (Psa 119:80); and the **face** as the site of vindication/radiance (Psa 34:5; Isa 50:7). Refuge is "in you" — the trust seated in the God-relation.
- **T2.1.2** — Located in the heart's trust and shown on the face; relationally anchored in God.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link: **face/countenance** — radiant and unashamed (Psa 34:5), set like flint (Isa 50:7).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: arises from the **God-relation of trust** — the non-shaming is *given by God* in response to refuge taken in him.
- **T2.9.2** — Single relational source (trust in God), with the adversary's shame as its judicial complement.
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: from God (the upholder) toward the truster (kept from shame) and against the adversary (put to shame).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
- **T3.1.1** — Perception engaged: "having my **eyes fixed** on all your commandments" (Psa 119:6) — the gaze that keeps one from shame.
- **T3.2.1** — Cognition central — **confident expectation / hope**: "hope does not put us to shame" (Rom 5:5); "my eager expectation and hope that I will not be at all ashamed" (Phil 1:20). bosh tagged cognition throughout.
- **T3.2.2** — The cognition is a **settled assurance** that God will vindicate.
- **T3.4.1** — Affect engaged — security, radiance, relief (Psa 34:5), and its averted opposite, dread.
- **T3.4.2** — Produces confidence/boldness (Phil 1:20; 1Jo 2:28 "confidence") rather than the dismay of the shamed.
- **T3.6.1** — Volition engaged — the act of **taking refuge / waiting / clinging**: "in you, O Lord, do I take refuge" (Psa 31:1); "I cling to your testimonies" (Psa 119:31); "those who **wait** for you" (Psa 25:3).
- **T3.6.2** — The will actively casts itself on God as the ground of non-shame.
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation present: the **righteous-vs-treacherous** sorting — "they shall be ashamed who are wantonly treacherous" (Psa 25:3); the wicked/Zion-haters shamed (Psa 129:5).
- **T3.9.1** — Conscience engaged at 1Pe 3:16 — "**a good conscience**, so that… those who revile your good behaviour may be put to shame" — clear conscience as the ground of vindication.
- **T3.11.1** — Relational capacity is the band's centre — the **trust-bond to God** that is honoured rather than shamed.
- **T3.11.2** — It exhibits the relation to God as the secure ground of standing.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **keeps the truster from shame and vindicates** — "the Lord is his refuge" (Psa 14:6); "you have saved us… and have put to shame those who hate us" (Psa 44:7).
- **T4.1.2** — Basis: **covenant faithfulness / God's promise** — "uphold me according to your **promise**… let me not be put to shame" (Psa 119:116).
- **T4.1.3** — Disposition: God as **helper and refuge**, disposed to honour the trusting (Isa 50:7; Psa 25:20).
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God: the band's heartbeat — **trust, wait, take refuge, hope, call upon, cling** (Psa 22:5; 25:2; 31:1; 119:31). This is the most God-directed band in the cluster.
- **T4.2.2** — The movement is **dependence/petition** — casting oneself on God against the threat of shame.
- **T4.2.3** — Expressed in prayer and testimony before God and "before kings" (Psa 119:46).
- **T4.3.1** — Person → person: the **adversary** is the one shamed — enemies, accusers, the treacherous, Zion-haters (Psa 35:4; 71:13; 129:5).
- **T4.4.1** — Person ← person: the truster **receives reviling/slander** from adversaries (1Pe 3:16) but is vindicated by God against it.
- **T4.5.2** — Crosses to **the nations / Zion's enemies** (Psa 83:17 "let them be put to shame… forever"; 129:5).
- **T4.6.x** — Silent — no angelic/adversarial-spirit interface in B's verses.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
- **T5.1.1** — Formative: trust **sustained through trial** matures into vindication — "not put to shame in evil times; in the days of famine they have abundance" (Psa 37:19).
- **T5.2.1** — Sequence: cried/trusted → rescued → **not put to shame** (Psa 22:5) — trust precedes and grounds the vindication.
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: **God's intervention** in response to refuge taken in him.
- **T5.4.1** — Operates squarely **within suffering**: famine, enemies, slander, the cross — "if anyone suffers as a Christian, let him **not be ashamed**" (1Pe 4:16); Christ honoured "whether by life or by death" (Phil 1:20).
- **T5.4.2** — Suffering is the very arena where non-shame is proven.
- **T5.5.1** — Sanctification-adjacent: hope **produced through** affliction "does not put us to shame, because God's love has been poured into our hearts" (Rom 5:3-5).
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological: final non-shaming at Christ's appearing (1Jo 2:28; Rom 10:11) — the consummation of the axis.

- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with **trust** (ba.tach), **refuge** (cha.sah), **hope** (qa.vah / elpis), **wait**, **salvation**, and the adversary-cluster (enemies, the treacherous).
- **T6.1.2** — Shows shame/honour as the **outcome term of the trust-faculty** — the affective-relational payoff of hope. [inferential]
- **T6.2.1** — Within B, trust **precedes** vindication; the shaming of enemies **follows** their hostility to the truster.
- **T6.3.2** — Produced by the **trust-relation to God** (non-shame for the truster) and by hostility to God's people (shame for the adversary).
- **T6.4.1** — Shares bosh/kataischunō/cha.pher/ka.lam with chars A, C, D, E, G.
- **T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour **char A** (same trust-faculty; B = true object → vindication, A = false object → shame) and **char D** (the enemy-shaming overlaps D's borne disgrace and E's imprecation). [flag for cross-char confirmation]
- **T6.5.2** — Boundary with A is the **object of trust**; with D/E, B reads the *same* shaming under the lens of the truster's vindication.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
- **T7.1.1** — bosh (BWŠ), kataischunō (kata + aischunō), cha.pher, ka.lam, aischunō.
- **T7.1.2** — Petitionary jussives, perfect-tense testimony, future/citation promise.
- **T7.1.3** — Range: not-ashamed / not-disgraced / not-confounded / vindicated; and (of enemies) put-to-shame / dismayed / turned-back.
- **T7.1.5** — The contrast term within the band is the **shaming of the adversary**.
- **T7.1.8** — **The clearest OT→NT continuity in M07**: the Psalter's "none who wait for you shall be put to shame" (Psa 25:3) and Isaiah's Zion-stone "will not be put to shame" (Isa 28:16) are taken up verbatim by Paul (Rom 9:33; 10:11) and Peter (1Pe 2:6) and extended to faith in Christ.
- **T7.1.9** — Silent — kataischunō is an existing compound, not a coinage.
- **T7.1.10** — Range: the vindication/non-shaming of trust across lament, promise, and gospel.
- **T7.2.1** — Functions as **petition, testimony, and promise** that trust will be vindicated.
- **T7.2.2** — Literary forms: **psalm** (lament/trust) dominant; **prophecy** (Isaiah); **epistle** (Romans, 1Pe, Phil, 1Jo); **gospel** (Luk 13:17, adversaries put to shame).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Psa 22:5): they cried/trusted (ground) → were rescued (act) → **were not put to shame** (conclusion).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: individual/communal lament and prayer; apostolic exhortation under suffering.
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Psa 25:3 / Rom 10:11** — "none who wait for you shall be put to shame" → "everyone who believes in him will not be put to shame" — the axis stated and consummated.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor uniquely binds **trust** to **non-shame** as a covenant promise carried from Psalter to gospel.
- **T7.3.1** — Lens: psychology of **hope/secure attachment** and the social vindication of standing [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec1/2].
- **T7.3.2** — Illumination: the secure, "unshamed" standing of one held by a trusted other parallels the believer's confidence before God. [source: science extract]
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: the band grounds non-shame **eschatologically and theologically** (God's promise, Christ's appearing) — beyond what a this-worldly attachment frame supplies. [source: science extract T7.3.3]
- **T7.3.4** — Gap: the **public, forensic** vindication ("before kings," Psa 119:46; at his coming, 1Jo 2:28) exceeds the clinical, intrapsychic account of shame-relief. [source: science extract]
