# M07 — Characteristic J: Innocence / cleanness (the antonym pole)

**Phase:** Evidence gather + ANALYSIS (verse-read). **Occurrences:** 8 — niq.qa.von (4), qe.ha.von (4). [FLAG: relevance pending interpret phase.]

> **Relevance flag (for interpret phase).** These terms mean **innocence / cleanness** — the moral-clean state, **not shame**. They sit in M07 as the **antonym pole** that defines shame by its absence. Whether J belongs to M07 (as the contrast pole) or to a purity/righteousness cluster is deferred.

## Analytical narrative

**What this characteristic is.** Innocence is the **clean inner state that gives no ground for shame** — and it is consistently located in **both the heart and the hands**: "In the **integrity of my heart** and the **innocence of my hands** I have done this" (Gen 20:5); "I **wash my hands in innocence** and go around your altar, O Lord" (Psa 26:6); "in vain have I kept my **heart clean** and **washed my hands** in innocence" (Psa 73:13). The inner integrity (heart) and the outer act (hands) together constitute innocence.

**Its absence as a diagnosis.** "How long will they be **incapable of innocence**?" (Hos 8:5) — said of idolatrous Samaria; the loss of innocence is the entry into shame-exposed territory.

**Significance for the inner being.** J is the **positive contrast** that the rest of M07 presupposes: shame attaches to defect, exposure, and guilt; innocence is the state without those marks — a clean heart and clean hands before God. As the antonym pole it clarifies what shame *is* by showing what its absence looks like: moral and ritual integrity, the unashamed standing of the clean.

### Pattern notes
- **Valence:** righteous (the clean state). **Experiencer:** self (the one protesting/keeping innocence). **Seat:** heart + hands (inner integrity + outer act).
- **Boundary:** the **antonym** of shame/disgrace — not shame itself. Relevance flag retained.
