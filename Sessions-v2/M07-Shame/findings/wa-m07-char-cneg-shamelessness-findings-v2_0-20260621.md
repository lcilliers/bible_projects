# M07 — Findings for Characteristic C-neg: Shamelessness (absence / inversion of the shame-faculty)
**File:** wa-m07-char-cneg-shamelessness-findings-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0**. Verse-read re-answer.
**Evidence:** 8 occ — Jer 6:15 / 8:12 (bosh + ka.lam), Jer 3:3 (ka.lam), Hos 4:18 (qa.lon), Phil 3:19 & Jude 13 (aischunē). The faculty is **dead, refused, or inverted (loved/gloried in)**.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — God is the one who **diagnoses the missing faculty**: "Were they ashamed when they committed abomination? No… they did not know how to blush" (Jer 6:15; 8:12) — a divine indictment of a self that can no longer register shame.
- **T0.1.2** — Borne by the creature; God names its loss, never bears it.
- **T0.2.1** — The role shown is the **failure-state** of shame: where it should signal abomination it is silent ("not at all ashamed").
- **T0.2.2** — Reveals the faculty as **created design** (the capacity to blush) and its loss/inversion as the **fallen** pathology — the seared conscience.
- **T0.2.3** — Silent — no future-fullness statement (the band describes ruin, not restoration).
- **T0.3.1** — Negatively instantiates the moral image: to be unable to blush (Jer 6:15) is a **marred image**, a self that has lost a God-given moral signal.
- **T0.3.2** — Creaturely pathology; not a divine attribute.
- **T0.3.3** — Its **absence** signals an image in advanced disorder — moral perception severed from moral affect.
- **T0.4.1** — Silent.

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **the non-functioning of shame** — its death ("did not know how to blush," Jer 6:15), its refusal ("you refuse to be ashamed," Jer 3:3), or its inversion ("they glory in their shame," Phil 3:19; "their rulers dearly love shame," Hos 4:18).
- **T1.1.2** — Carried by **negated** bosh/ka.lam (be ashamed → were not), and by qa.lon / aischunē taken as the *object loved or gloried in* — shame embraced rather than felt.
- **T1.1.3** — Constitutional: a defect/perversion of the faculty itself, not merely an outward act.
- **T1.2.1** — A **state/condition** (the faculty's deadness) more than an act.
- **T1.2.2** — Compound — it negates the affect+cognition complex of shame (Jer 6:15 tags both faculties) and, in Phil 3:19 / Hos 4:18, inverts the valuation.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: a **functioning** shame-faculty (chars A and C).
- **T1.3.2** — It excludes the restraining check that shame provides — "the forehead of a whore" that will not colour (Jer 3:3).
- **T1.3.3** — It ends where genuine contrition begins; its hallmark is precisely the refusal/inability to feel what char C feels.
- **T1.4.1** — Expressed as a **negation** ("were not ashamed") or an **inversion verb** ("glory in," "love").
- **T1.4.2** — Two modes: deadness (Jer) and perverse delight (Hos 4:18; Phil 3:19; Jude 13).
- **T1.4.3** — Silent.
- **T1.5.1** — The "response" is the **absence of response** — no blush, no covering, where shame would produce them.
- **T1.5.2** — Consistent absence/inversion.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: a settled hardness — "they did not know how to blush; **therefore** they shall fall" (Jer 6:15) — the deadened faculty issues in ruin.
- **T1.6.3** — The condition is durative (a standing incapacity), not a momentary lapse.
- **T1.7.1** — This band **is** the answer to "what does shame look like when it fails" — it is the malfunction itself.
- **T1.7.2** — Partial: the faculty is **distorted** — but the verses ascribe the distortion to the person's own hardening, not to an external spirit.
- **T1.7.3** — Yes — present-but-not-taking-hold is the essence: the occasion for shame arises (abomination) and the faculty does not engage (Jer 6:15).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- **T2.1.1** — No abstract seat tagged, but the loss is registered in the **face/forehead**: "the forehead of a whore; you refuse to be ashamed" (Jer 3:3); "did not know how to blush" (Jer 6:15).
- **T2.1.2** — Located as a **conscience-level** deadness expressed in the unblushing face.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link: the **absent blush** — the somatic sign of shame that fails to appear.
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: a **self-hardening** through repeated abomination (Jer's context) — the faculty atrophies by use against it.
- **T2.9.2** — Single mechanism (hardening), with the inverted-delight variant (Phil/Hos).
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: the occasion for shame meets a faculty that **no longer moves**.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
- **T3.1.x** — Partial: a failure of **moral perception** — they do not perceive abomination as shameful (Jer 6:15).
- **T3.2.x** — Cognition tagged (Jer 6:15) — the knowing that should accompany shame is absent ("did not **know** how to blush").
- **T3.3.x / T3.5.x** — Silent.
- **T3.4.1** — Affect is the faculty **defined by its absence/inversion** here — the proper shame-affect is missing (Jer) or perversely positive (Hos 4:18 "love," Phil 3:19 "glory").
- **T3.4.2** — Where present, the affect is **misdirected delight** in what should bring shame.
- **T3.6.1** — Volition engaged — "you **refuse** to be ashamed" (Jer 3:3) — a willed rejection of the faculty.
- **T3.6.2** — The will is set against the conscience's signal.
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation is **inverted**: shame is "loved" and "gloried in" (Hos 4:18; Phil 3:19) — the moral compass reversed.
- **T3.8.2** — It calls evil good — embracing what ought to be renounced.
- **T3.9.1** — Conscience is **the** engaged faculty — here **seared/dead**: the inner witness that should accuse is silent (Jer 6:15), refused (Jer 3:3), or perverted.
- **T3.9.2** — Conscience fails to accuse — the diagnostic core of the band.
- **T3.9.3** — Engaged precisely by its non-operation.
- **T3.10.1** — Conscientiousness is **absent** — moral awareness no longer issues in restraint or action.
- **T3.11.1** — Relational capacity engaged: the shamelessness is public/covenantal — "love shame" rulers (Hos 4:18) corrupt the people; "enemies of the cross" (Phil 3:19).

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **exposes and judges** the shameless ("therefore they shall fall," Jer 6:15).
- **T4.1.2** — Basis: the response to entrenched, unrepented abomination.
- **T4.2.1** — Inverted human→God: the shameless **refuse** the contrition God seeks ("you refuse to be ashamed," Jer 3:3).
- **T4.3.1** — Person→person: shameless **rulers** propagate the corruption (Hos 4:18); the "enemies of the cross" lead others astray (Phil 3:19).
- **T4.5.2** — Within the covenant people (Judah's priests/prophets, Jer 6:13-15) and among false teachers (Phil/Jude).
- **T4.6.x** — Silent.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
- **T5.1.1** — A **negative formation** — the faculty is *deformed* over time, not transformed for good.
- **T5.2.1** — Sequence implied: repeated abomination → loss of the blush → falling (Jer 6:15).
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: **habituation to abomination** sears the conscience.
- **T5.4.1** — Set against judgment (withheld rains, Jer 3:3) that still fails to provoke shame.
- **T5.5.1** — Silent (anti-sanctification, if anything).
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological edge at Phil 3:19 ("their **end** is destruction") and Jude 13 ("wandering stars, for whom the gloom of darkness is reserved").

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
- **T7.1.1** — Negated bosh (BWŠ) and ka.lam (KLM); qa.lon (shame, here *loved*); aischunē (shame, here *gloried in*).
- **T7.1.2** — Negated finite verbs (Jer); plus noun-as-object-of-delight (Hos 4:18; Phil 3:19).
- **T7.1.3** — Range: not-ashamed / refuse-shame / love-shame / glory-in-shame.
- **T7.1.5** — The opposite term is functioning shame (chars A/C).
- **T7.1.8** — OT (Jer, Hos) → NT (Phil, Jude) continuity: the **seared conscience** motif carries into the apostolic warning against the shameless.
- **T7.2.1** — Functions as **indictment** — the prophetic/apostolic charge that the faculty is dead or inverted.
- **T7.2.2** — Prophecy (Jer, Hos) and **epistle** (Phil, Jude).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Jer 6:15): they were not ashamed → they did not know to blush → **therefore** they shall fall.
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: indictment of corrupt leaders / false teachers.
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Jer 6:15 / 8:12** — "they did not know how to blush" — the definitive statement of the dead faculty.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor uniquely shows shame as a faculty that can be **lost**, not only felt.
- **T7.3.1** — Lens: the clinical picture of a **blunted/absent shame signal** and moralised "shamelessness" [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec1].
- **T7.3.2** — Illumination: science's account of shame as an adaptive social-moral signal makes the *loss* of it legible as a deficit, matching "did not know how to blush." [source: science extract]
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: where some clinical framing treats *less* shame as healthier, the text treats the **absence** of shame as ruin (Jer 6:15). [source: science extract T7.3.3]
- **T7.3.4** — Gap: the **inverted-delight** form ("glory in their shame," Phil 3:19) is a moral-spiritual category the clinical literature does not centre. [source: science extract]
