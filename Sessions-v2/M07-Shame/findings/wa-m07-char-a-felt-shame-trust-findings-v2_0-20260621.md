## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — God appears as the **true glory before whom false radiance is shamed** ("the moon confounded and the sun ashamed, for the Lord of hosts reigns… and his glory," Isa 24:23) and as the one who exposes the idols' emptiness ("their images are false, and there is no breath in them," Jer 51:17). Otherwise the shame is pronounced over idol-trusters.
- **T0.1.2** — Borne only by the creature (the wrongly-trusting); God is never the bearer — he is the true glory over against which false trust is shamed.
- **T0.2.1** — The role shown is **exposure/disabusing**: the shame reveals the trusted object as profitless ("the things they delight in do not profit," Isa 44:9) and the false lights as eclipsed by God's glory (Isa 24:23).
- **T0.2.2** — Both: the *capacity* to be shamed by failed trust presupposes the trusting faculty (created design); its *occasion* — reliance on idols/false refuges — belongs to the fallen condition. [inferential]
- **T0.2.3** — Yes — the horizon of the Lord's reign before which false radiance fades: "the Lord of hosts reigns on Mount Zion… his glory will be before his elders" (Isa 24:23).
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates, negatively, the image as a **trusting/relying being** — shame marks the misdirection of a faculty made to rest in God. [inferential]
- **T0.3.2** — Exclusively creaturely; God does not trust-and-fail. This is the creature's experience of misplaced reliance.
- **T0.3.3** — Where present, it marks a self that has anchored on the wrong object — a disordering of the image's trust. [inferential]
- **T0.4.1** — Typological/eschatological at Isa 24:23: the shaming of the false lights before the reigning Lord prefigures God's final vindication over every rival; direction = the divine glory establishing the pattern.

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — Carried by bosh, cha.pher, ka.lam ("be ashamed / confounded / disappointed"). Essential nature: the **inner collapse of a falsified trust**.
- **T1.1.2** — Hebrew only: bosh (be ashamed, disconcerted, disappointed), cha.pher (be confounded, abashed), ka.lam (be humiliated, confounded) — a confusion-plus-shame complex.
- **T1.1.3** — Directional toward the **object trusted** (idol, nation, refuge); the shame turns on the relation between truster and the thing trusted.
- **T1.2.1** — A **kind of event/act** (type=action throughout) — being shamed/confounded.
- **T1.2.2** — Compound: fuses **cognitive falsification** (expectation proven false) with **affect** (dismay/dread); co-terms confounded, dismayed, terrified, dread, anxiety.
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **trust vindicated / not put to shame** (char B) — the same faculty with a true object.
- **T1.3.2** — It excludes the security the idol promised; it is a passive collapse, resisting nothing actively. [partial]
- **T1.3.3** — Not shame at one's own moral sin (char C) nor inflicted disgrace (char E): it is the exposure of a *failed reliance*.
- **T1.4.1** — Operates as a verb (action), characteristically in the **prophetic declarative** ("shall be put to shame"); Hebrew Qal.
- **T1.4.2** — Largely uniform — oracle declaration over the wrongly-trusting.
- **T1.4.3** — Partly: some occurrences are addressed ("be ashamed because of Cush their hope," Isa 20:5 addressed to the confident), but most are third-person declaration.
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: **covering the head** (Jer 14:3,4), **turning back** (Isa 42:17), **melting / troubled like the sea** (Jer 49:23), terror (Isa 44:11). A somatic collapse.
- **T1.5.2** — Consistent — collapse / covering / turning.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: the **standing exposure** of the trust as vain — the truster left without the refuge they leaned on.
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the felt dismay and covering; sustained = the abiding verdict that the trusted thing was empty.
- **T1.7.1** — Silent — A frames shame as a negative outcome, not a faculty with conditions for "right" operation.
- **T1.7.2** — Silent — no distortion-by-another-spirit content.
- **T1.7.3** — Silent.
