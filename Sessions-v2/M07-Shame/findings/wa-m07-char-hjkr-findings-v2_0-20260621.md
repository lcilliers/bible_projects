# H — Propriety: what is "shameful / disgraceful" (decency norms) — 9 occ
**Evidence:** aischros (1Cor 11:6; 14:35; Eph 5:12; Tit 1:11), aischunē (2Cor 4:2; Rev 3:18), aschēmosunē (Rom 1:27; Rev 16:15), qi.qa.lon (Hab 2:16). The **norm** of what counts as disgraceful conduct/speech/exposure.

## T0
- **T0.1.1** — God/Christ is the **norm-giver and judge of the disgraceful**: "I counsel you to buy… white garments… that the **shame** of your nakedness may not be seen" (Rev 3:18); "lest he go about naked and be seen **exposed**" (Rev 16:15).
- **T0.2.1** — Shame functions as the **moral measure** marking certain conduct/speech/exposure as not-to-be-done.
- **T0.2.2** — Reveals a **created sense of propriety** ("does not nature itself teach you…", 1Cor 11:14 context) turned to judge fallen indecency. [inferential]
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the image's **moral-evaluative standard** — the held knowledge of what is fitting vs disgraceful.
- **T0.4.1** — Silent.

## T1
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is the **normative judgement** "it is shameful to…" — a standard of decency, not a felt episode.
- **T1.2.1** — A **property/evaluation** predicated of conduct (disgraceful, shameful).
- **T1.2.2** — Compound where it joins moral judgement to the **disgust/exposure** substrate (nakedness, indecency: Rom 1:27; Rev 16:15).
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: the honourable/fitting/decent.
- **T1.3.3** — Not the *feeling* of shame (A/C) nor the *bearing* of disgrace (D) — the **measure** that says what ought to occasion them.
- **T1.4.1** — Predicative ("it is **shameful** for…", 1Cor 14:35; Eph 5:12).
- **T1.5.x / T1.6.x / T1.7.x** — Silent — H names a norm, not a response with onset, duration, or malfunction.

## T2
- **T2.1.1** — No inner seat; the norm attaches to **the body and speech** — hair (1Cor 11:6), speaking in assembly (14:35), things "done in secret" spoken of (Eph 5:12), nakedness (Rev 3:18; 16:15).
- **T2.7.1** — Strong body link — propriety governs **bodily appearance and exposure**.
- **T2.9.x / T2.10.x** — Silent (a standard, not a movement within a person).

## T3
- **T3.8.1** — **Moral evaluation** is the engaged faculty — operating as a **held standard** that classifies conduct as disgraceful.
- **T3.8.2** — It renders verdicts: long hair (1Cor 11:6), women's speaking (14:35), shameful gain (Tit 1:11), "disgraceful, underhanded ways" (2Cor 4:2) — all judged not-fitting.
- **T3.1 perception** — partial: indecency must be **perceived as such** to be judged.
- **T3.11.1** — Relational/communal — the norm is **church- and culture-embedded** (1Cor 11; 14; Tit 1).
- **T3.3 / 3.5 / 3.6 / 3.7 / 3.9 / 3.10** — Silent — H is a standard, not a process of memory, will, agency, or conscience-accusation.

## T4
- **T4.1.1** — God/Christ sets and enforces the norm (Rev 3:18; 16:15).
- **T4.3.1** — Person→community: leaders teaching "shameful gain" disgrace whole households (Tit 1:11) — a norm **violated toward others**.
- **T4.5.2** — Norms stated for the **church** and against pagan indecency (Rom 1:27).
- **T4.2 / 4.4 / 4.6** — Silent.

## T5
- **T5.4.1** — Tied to judgment-exposure (Hab 2:16 "shame instead of glory… show your uncircumcision"; Rev 16:15).
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological exposure motif (Rev 3:18; 16:15 — the day that exposes the naked).
- **T5.1 / 5.2 / 5.3 / 5.5** — Silent.

## T6
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with nakedness/indecency, secrecy, gain.
- **T6.3.2 / T6.5.1** — H supplies the **standard** that the felt/borne bands presuppose; nearest neighbour char D (qi.qa.lon, aischunē exposure overlap). [flag: cross-char]
- **T6.4.1** — Shares aischunē/qi.qa.lon with D and G.

## T7
- **T7.1.1** — aischros (shameful/disgraceful), aischunē (shame/disgrace), aschēmosunē (indecency), qi.qa.lon (shameful spewing/disgrace).
- **T7.1.3** — Range: disgraceful / shameful / indecent / not-fitting.
- **T7.2.2** — **Epistle** (1Cor, Eph, Tit, 2Cor), **prophecy** (Hab), **apocalyptic** (Rev).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Eph 5:12** — "it is **shameful** even to speak of the things they do in secret" — the norm in its purest predicative form.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor shows shame as a **community standard of speech/conduct**, distinct from felt shame.
- **T7.3.1–.4** — Lens: **disgust-based moral norms** and bodily propriety/indecency [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec2/3]; illumination — the exposure/nakedness motif maps to disgust-purity; divergence — the text **commands** the norm where the clinical frame describes it; gap — the eschatological exposure (Rev) is theological, not clinical. [source: science extract]

**Genuine Silence in H: ~45 of 126** — the entire process register (T1.5–1.7, T2.9–2.10, T5.1–5.3, much of T3) is empty **because H is a norm, not a felt or borne episode.** This is legitimate Silence, not a coverage failure.

# J — Innocence / cleanness (the antonym pole) — 8 occ [FLAG: relevance]
**Evidence:** niq.qa.von & qe.ha.von at Gen 20:5, Psa 26:6, Psa 73:13, Hos 8:5. The **clean inner state** that gives no ground for shame — defined in **heart + hands**.

## T0
- **T0.1.1** — Innocence is held **before God**: "I wash my hands in innocence and go around **your altar**, O Lord" (Psa 26:6); the protest of integrity is made to God (Gen 20:5).
- **T0.2.1** — Functions as the **unashamed clean standing** — the positive contrast that shame negates.
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the image as capable of **moral integrity** (clean heart, clean hands).

## T1
- **T1.1.1** — Nature: **moral/ritual cleanness** — the absence of the marks that bring shame.
- **T1.3.1** — It **is** the opposite pole of disgrace; J defines shame by its absence.
- **T1.3.3** — Not shame; the **antonym**. [relevance flag — may belong to a purity/righteousness cluster]

## T2
- **T2.1.1** — Explicit **dual seat**: "the **integrity of my heart** and the **innocence of my hands**" (Gen 20:5) — inner disposition + outer act.
- **T2.7.1** — Strong body link: **hands** washed in innocence (Psa 26:6; 73:13) — the bodily enactment of inner cleanness.

## T3
- **T3.8.1** — **Moral evaluation** engaged as a **positive self-attestation** of cleanness.
- **T3.9.1** — Conscience-adjacent: the **integrity of heart** (Gen 20:5) is a clear-conscience claim before God.
- **T3.6.1** — Volition: "I **keep** my heart clean and **wash** my hands" (Psa 73:13) — an active maintaining of innocence.
- **T3.11.1** — Relational — innocence asserted **toward God** at his altar (Psa 26:6).
- **T3.1–3.5, 3.7, 3.10** — Silent.

## T4
- **T4.2.1** — Human→God: innocence **brought before God** in protest/worship (Gen 20:5; Psa 26:6).
- **T4.1.1** — God as the one who **reckons** integrity ("I know that you did this in the integrity of your heart," Gen 20:6 context).
- **T4.3 / 4.4 / 4.5 / 4.6** — Silent.

## T5
- **T5.5.1** — Sanctification-adjacent: keeping the heart clean (Psa 73:13) — though Asaph questions its payoff ("in vain").
- **T5.1–5.4, 5.6** — Silent.

## T6
- **T6.3.2 / T6.5.1** — J is the **contrast pole** the shame-bands presuppose; nearest neighbour the felt-shame bands (their absence). [flag: cross-char + relevance]
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with clean/wash/integrity/heart/hands.

## T7
- **T7.1.1** — niq.qa.von (innocence/cleanness), qe.ha.von (innocence/blamelessness).
- **T7.1.3** — Range: innocence / cleanness / blamelessness / integrity.
- **T7.2.2** — **Narrative** (Gen 20:5), **psalm** (26:6; 73:13), **prophecy** (Hos 8:5 — incapacity for innocence).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Gen 20:5** — "in the **integrity of my heart** and the **innocence of my hands**" — the dual inner/outer seat of the unashamed clean state.
- **T7.3.1–.4** — Lens: **innocence/purity** as the unashamed condition [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec3]; illumination — the clean-hands motif parallels the "moral cleanliness" intuition; divergence/gap — J is the **antonym**, so most shame-psychology questions do not apply. [source: science extract]

**Genuine Silence in J: ~50 of 126** — most process and interface questions are empty **because innocence is the absence/antonym of shame**, not shame itself (relevance flag retained). The engaged answers cluster in seat (heart+hands), moral evaluation, conscience, and the Godward interface.

# K — Silencing / cast-off / defilement (behavioural-physical edge) — 14 occ [FLAG: inner-being relevance]
**Evidence:** fimoō ×8 (silence/muzzle), afōnos ×3 (mute), apoballō ×2 (cast off), ni.dah ×1 (Lam 1:8, defilement). Mostly **not inner-being states**; ni.dah is the one genuine shame-content.

## Engaged answers (where the band actually speaks)
- **T1.1.1** — The band's terms denote **acts**: silencing/muzzling (fimoō: Mat 22:34; 1Cor 9:9), muteness (afōnos: Act 8:32), casting off (apoballō: Mar 10:50), and **defilement** (ni.dah: "she became **filthy**… they have seen her **nakedness**," Lam 1:8).
- **T1.5.1** — The one shame-adjacent **response** is being **reduced to silence** in defeat/exposure — "he was **speechless**" (Mat 22:12, the guest without a wedding garment).
- **T2.7.1** — Body link: muzzling the mouth (fimoō), throwing off the cloak (apoballō), nakedness exposed (Lam 1:8).
- **T3.8.1 / T3.11.1** — Engaged **only** at ni.dah: defilement issuing in **despising** and exposed **nakedness** (Lam 1:8) — a moral-relational disgrace following sin ("Jerusalem sinned grievously").
- **T4.1.1** — At Lam 1:8 the disgrace is the **judicial consequence** of sin, with former honourers now despising her.
- **T5.4.1** — ni.dah sits in a **judgment-lament** (Lamentations) — disgrace as the fruit of sin.
- **T7.1.1** — fimoō (muzzle/silence), afōnos (voiceless), apoballō (throw off), ni.dah (impurity/defilement).
- **T7.2.2** — **Gospel/narrative** (fimoō rebukes, Mar 1:25; 4:39), **epistle** (1Cor 9:9; 1Ti 5:18), **apocalyptic/prophetic** edge, **lament** (Lam 1:8).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor (for the inner-being content): **Lam 1:8** — defilement → despising → exposed nakedness — the only verse carrying clear M07 disgrace.
- **T7.3.x** — Lens: **defilement/contamination** strand of shame [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec2/4]; the silencing terms fall **outside** the shame-psychology frame.

**Genuine Silence in K: ~95 of 126 — and largely by design.** Apart from ni.dah (Lam 1:8) and the oblique "speechless" of Mat 22:12, fimoō/afōnos/apoballō are **acts of silencing/casting-off, not inner-being states**, so the inner-faculty, interface, and developmental tiers are empty. The likely interpret-phase outcome is to **set aside** the silencing/casting terms and **retain ni.dah's defilement-disgrace**. Relevance flag retained.

# RESID — Sense-split / residual (non-shame sense) — 2 occ [FLAG: exclude]
**Evidence:** ka.lam in its **"harm / injure"** sense, both negated: "we did them **no harm**" (1Sa 25:7); "we suffered **no harm**" (1Sa 25:15).

## Engaged answers (the only ones the verses support)
- **T1.1.1** — Here ka.lam means **to do injury**, a distinct sense from "to shame/humiliate"; both occurrences **negate** it.
- **T7.1.1** — ka.lam (H3637) — same lemma as the shame-sense, but the **"harm/injure" sense**.
- **T7.1.10** — Demonstrates the lemma's **sense-range** beyond shame (a genuine lexical split).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **1Sa 25:7** — "your shepherds… we did them **no harm**" — plainly the injury-sense, no inner-being content.

**Genuine Silence in RESID: 122 of 126 — by design.** These two verses carry **no inner-being shame content**; they are gathered only so that no ka.lam occurrence is filtered out without record. Interpret-phase decision: **exclude from M07 shame analysis** as a non-shame sense. Flag retained.
