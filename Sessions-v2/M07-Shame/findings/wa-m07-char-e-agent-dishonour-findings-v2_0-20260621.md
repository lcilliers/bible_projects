# M07 — Findings for Characteristic E: Dishonouring / despising another (agent pole) + contempt-disposition
**File:** wa-m07-char-e-agent-dishonour-findings-v2_0-20260621.md · 2026-06-21 · **Supersedes v1_0**. Verse-read re-answer.
**Evidence:** 58 occ — ka.lam (transitive humiliation), exoutheneō (despise/contempt), atimazō (dishonour), bosh (household-shaming), kataischunō, entrepō/entropē (admonitory), loidoria/kakologeō/paradeigmatizō (verbal abuse), cha.pher, qa.lah, aischunē, bo.shet. The **agent pole**: shame/contempt *inflicted on* another — exposing the heart's power to withhold or assault honour.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- **T0.1.1** — The dishonour can be aimed **at God himself**: "you **dishonor me**" (Joh 8:49); "you **dishonor God** by breaking the law" (Rom 2:23) — the inner being's capacity to despise reaches even its Maker. Conversely, dishonour borne **for God** is honour: "rejoicing that they were counted worthy to **suffer dishonor for the name**" (Act 5:41).
- **T0.1.2** — Borne by the one dishonoured; **inflicted** by the agent — and God can be its object (Joh 8:49).
- **T0.2.1** — Contempt functions as the **withholding/assaulting of due honour** — a disposition that reckons another (or God) worthless.
- **T0.2.2** — Reveals a **created capacity to assign honour** (design) turned to its sinful inverse, contempt (fallen) — "let no one **despise** him" (1Cor 16:11) presupposes the duty to honour.
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the image's **relational-evaluative power**: to honour or despise the worth of another image-bearer (Jam 2:6, dishonouring the poor) and of God.
- **T0.3.3** — Its sinful exercise marks a **disordered image** — the heart that despises the brother (Rom 14:10) or dishonours God (Rom 2:23).
- **T0.4.1** — Typological at Act 4:11: "the stone **rejected/despised** by you, the builders" — the despised one becomes the cornerstone; God reverses the contempt of men.

## T1 — Definition
- **T1.1.1** — The nature is **the act/disposition of dishonouring or despising another** — humiliating (ka.lam), holding in contempt (exoutheneō), treating shamefully (atimazō), reviling (loidoria).
- **T1.1.2** — exoutheneō (count as nothing), atimazō (un-honour), ka.lam (humiliate/insult transitively), kataischunō (put to shame), entrepō (shame, admonitorily).
- **T1.1.3** — **Directional toward another** — the act/disposition terminates on a second party (or God).
- **T1.2.1** — Both an **act** (humiliating, reviling) and a **disposition** (contempt, scorn) — exoutheneō especially names the settled inner stance.
- **T1.2.2** — Compound: a **scornful valuation** (cognition/affect) issuing in **dishonouring action**.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: **honouring / esteeming** another (the entrepō "respect" pole; cf. char G).
- **T1.3.3** — Not the felt shame of the *recipient* (A/C) nor the borne standing (D) — this is the **doer's** side.
- **T1.4.1** — Transitive verbs of action; some **imperatival prohibitions** ("do not despise," Rom 14:3; 1Th 5:20; "do not reproach her," Rut 2:15).
- **T1.4.2** — Modes: sinful infliction (most), **righteous endurance** of dishonour (Act 5:41), and **loving admonition** (entrepō, 1Cor 4:14; 2Th 3:14).
- **T1.4.3** — Yes — frequently **commanded/prohibited** in direct address ("let no one despise…").
- **T1.5.1** — The agent's act produces, in the other, humiliation/being-made-low; the **disposition** shows as scorn, mockery (Luk 23:11 "treated him with contempt and **mocked**").
- **T1.5.2** — Varied by sub-movement (scorn, reviling, beating-and-shaming, admonishing).
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: contempt as a **settled orientation** (Rom 14, the habitual despising of a brother).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate act vs durative disposition both present.
- **T1.7.1** — "Right operation" appears only as **loving admonition** — shame deployed redemptively, "to **admonish** you as my beloved children" (1Cor 4:14).
- **T1.7.2** — Partial — the contempt is the agent's own sin, not externally induced.
- **T1.7.3** — Silent.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- **T2.1.1** — No single seat tagged; contempt is a **disposition of the heart/will** (the inner stance that counts another as nothing), enacted bodily (beating, spitting — Num 12:14; Mar 12:4).
- **T2.7.1** — Body link: enacted through **the mouth** (reviling, mocking, speaking evil — loidoria, kakologeō) and **the hand** (beating, Luk 20:11).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: arises from the **agent's own valuation** of the other as low/worthless (exoutheneō); for household-shame, from foolish/lawless conduct (Pro 17:2; 19:26).
- **T2.10.1** — Movement: **outward from the agent onto the other** (or onto God).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
- **T3.1.1** — Perception engaged as **mis-valuation** — seeing the other as "of no account" (2Cor 10:10; 1Cor 1:28).
- **T3.2.1** — Cognition: the **reckoning** of another's worth as nil (exoutheneō = to count as nothing).
- **T3.4.1** — Affect: **scorn/disdain** (Luk 18:9 "treated others with contempt"), mockery (Luk 23:11).
- **T3.6.1** — Volition/agency strongly engaged — the band is **agentive**: one chooses to dishonour, revile, or (rightly) admonish.
- **T3.7.1** — Agency: the agent **acts upon** another — humiliating, casting reproach, holding up to public disgrace (Heb 6:6).
- **T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation central and **double-edged**: despising the brother is **forbidden** (Rom 14:10); dishonouring God is **sin** (Rom 2:23); yet admonitory shame and suffering dishonour for Christ are **righteous** (1Cor 4:14; Act 5:41).
- **T3.8.2** — It renders verdicts on conduct (the household-shamer, Pro 19:26) and is itself judged (contempt condemned).
- **T3.11.1** — **Relational capacity is the core** — the faculty by which the inner being **assigns or withholds honour** to others and to God.
- **T3.11.2** — Exhibits the relation as **honour-bearing or honour-denying** — the moral charge of how one regards another.
- **T3.9 conscience** — partial: admonitory shame (2Th 3:14 "that he may be ashamed") aims to **awaken** the other's conscience.
- **T3.3 / 3.5 / 3.10** — Silent.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces
- **T4.1.1** — God → human: God **vindicates the despised** (the rejected stone, Act 4:11) and is **dishonoured by sin** (Rom 2:23).
- **T4.2.1** — Human → God (negative): **dishonouring God** (Joh 8:49; Rom 2:23) — contempt aimed Godward.
- **T4.3.1** — **Person → person is the band's centre**: despising/dishonouring/reviling/humiliating another — brother (Rom 14:10), poor man (Jam 2:6), servant/son in parables (Mar 12:4; Luk 20:11), prophet/apostle (Gal 4:14).
- **T4.3.2** — The act **withholds or assaults** the other's honour.
- **T4.4.1** — Person ← person (received): being **despised/dishonoured** — Paul "weak… of no account" (2Cor 10:10), the apostle not scorned (Gal 4:14), the despised-yet-chosen (1Cor 1:28).
- **T4.4.2** — The honourable response to received dishonour: **rejoice** (Act 5:41), do not repay reviling for reviling (1Pe 3:9).
- **T4.5.2** — Within the church (Rom 14; 1Cor; Jam 2) and toward Christ/apostles.
- **T4.6.x** — Silent.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension
- **T5.1.1** — Formative on the **agent's character**: habitual contempt marks the self-righteous (Luk 18:9); and the disciple is **formed by suffering dishonour** worthily (Act 5:41).
- **T5.2.1** — Sequence in the parables: servant sent → **dishonoured/beaten** → judgment (Mar 12; Luk 20).
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: the agent's **scornful valuation** issuing in act.
- **T5.4.1** — Operates within **persecution/suffering** — dishonour inflicted on Christ's servants (Act 5:41; Gal 4:14).
- **T5.5.1** — Sanctification: **not repaying** reviling, blessing instead (1Pe 3:9) — the redemptive handling of contempt.
- **T5.6.1** — Eschatological at Heb 6:6 — those who "**hold up to contempt**" the Son of God; and the reversal of the rejected stone (Act 4:11).

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation
- **T7.1.1** — ka.lam (humiliate, transitive), exoutheneō (count as nothing), atimazō (dishonour), kataischunō, entrepō/entropē, loidoria (reviling), kakologeō (speak evil), paradeigmatizō (public disgrace), cha.pher, qa.lah, aischunē, bo.shet.
- **T7.1.2** — Transitive verbs + agent-nouns; prohibitions and imperatives.
- **T7.1.3** — Range: despise / dishonour / humiliate / revile / hold-in-contempt / shame (admonitory).
- **T7.1.4** — Yes — a **disposition/act split** within the band: exoutheneō (settled contempt) vs ka.lam/atimazō (the inflicting act).
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite term: honour/esteem (timaō; the entrepō respect pole).
- **T7.1.6** — Silent (no person-type noun).
- **T7.1.8** — OT→NT continuity: the **household-shamer** of Proverbs (bosh) and the transitive ka.lam carry into the NT's developed vocabulary of contempt (exoutheneō, atimazō) — and into dishonouring/honouring **God**.
- **T7.2.1** — Functions as **prohibition, narrative report, and admonition**.
- **T7.2.2** — **Epistle** dominant (Rom, 1Cor, Gal, Jam, 1Pe), **gospel/parable** (Mar 12; Luk 20; 23), **narrative** (2Sa 10; 1Ch 19), **wisdom** (Proverbs), **prophecy** (Isa 16:14; 3:5).
- **T7.2.3** — Logical structure (Rom 2:23): you boast in the law → you break the law → therefore you **dishonour God**.
- **T7.2.4** — Settings: church-life disputes (Rom 14; 1Cor 11), persecution (Act 5; Gal 4), kingship/diplomacy (2Sa 10).
- **T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Rom 14:10** — "why do you **despise** your brother?" — contempt as a forbidden inner disposition toward another image-bearer; paired with **Joh 8:49 / Rom 2:23** for contempt aimed at God.
- **T7.2.6** — The anchor shows the **relational-evaluative faculty** at its sharpest: the heart's power to count a brother — or God — as nothing.
- **T7.3.1–.4** — Lens: psychology of **contempt** (the other-directed "moral emotion" that lowers another's status) and **humiliation** [source: wa-m07-shame-scienceextract Sec1/2]; illumination — contempt-as-status-denial maps onto exoutheneō; divergence — the text makes contempt **sin** (esp. toward God), and commends **suffering** dishonour, beyond the descriptive frame; gap — the **admonitory/redemptive** use of shame (1Cor 4:14) is not a clinical category.
