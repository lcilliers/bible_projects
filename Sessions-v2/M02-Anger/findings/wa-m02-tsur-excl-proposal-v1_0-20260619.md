## Issue
`tsur` (Strong's **H6696B**) carries the seed gloss **"to provoke"** and was admitted to M02 (Anger) on that basis (32 focus occurrences). At verse level, the per-occurrence ESV senses do **not** support an anger/provocation reading.

## Evidence (decisive)
Of the 32 occurrences, **0 carry an anger/provocation sense.** Every occurrence reads as the *besiege / bind / enclose* sense of tsur:
- besiege / besieged / besieging / lay siege / siege: ~24
- bind / bind up / tied up / bagged / hem / enclose: ~6
- "stirring up" (Judg 9:31): 1 — context is inciting/raising a force against a city, not inner anger
- "on" (1Ki 20:1): 1 — parse artifact of "encamped against"

The morphology is uniformly the qal of the besiege/bind verb. No occurrence sits in an inner-being affect context.

## Recommendation
Exclude all 32 `tsur` (H6696B) occurrences from M02. They are a mis-seed arising from the "to provoke" gloss colliding with the dominant besiege/bind sense. None engages an inner-being characteristic (fails GR-PROG-007 at the term level in every verse).

**If approved**, the consequence is: M02 focus-cluster occurrences drop 703 → 671; no characteristic C1–C7 loses members (tsur was never assigned to one). A CC patch would set the relevant term/occurrence status to excluded with exclusion_reason = "sense-contamination: besiege/bind sense of tsur, not anger/provocation".

## Open question for researcher
Judg 9:31 ("stirring up") is the single occurrence with any incite-flavour. Exclude with the rest, or hold for a closer verse read? (Recommendation: exclude — the sense is military incitement, not an inner-being state.)
