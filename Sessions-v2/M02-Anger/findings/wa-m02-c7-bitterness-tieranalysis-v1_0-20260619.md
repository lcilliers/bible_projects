## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — Relation to God: none of the four verses predicates bitterness of God; God does not bear pikria. In Heb 12:15 the "root of bitterness" is set in explicit contrast to "the grace of God" that the community must not fail to obtain. So bitterness stands **opposed to grace**, never borne by God. Record: no divine-bearer relation.

**T0.1.2** — Across C7 bitterness is borne only by the **creature**, and always as a **disorder** (Act 8:23 Simon; Rom 3:14 the depraved mouth; Eph 4:31 the believer to put it off; Heb 12:15 the defiling root). *Interpretation:* bitterness is a wholly fallen-condition characteristic — it images nothing of God and has no righteous form in this set.

**T0.2.1** — Stated purpose/effect: bitterness **"causes trouble" and "defiles many"** (Heb 12:15); it fills the mouth with "curses" (Rom 3:14); it accompanies "the bond of iniquity" (Act 8:23). Its effect is corrupting and contaminating. Record none beyond these.

**T0.2.2** — valence (sinful 3, forbidden 1) confirms bitterness belongs **wholly to the fallen condition** — it is to be "put away" (Eph 4:31, the forbidden case) and is a mark of the unregenerate mouth (Rom 3:14) and of a heart "not right before God" (Act 8:21,23). No created/holy register appears.

**T0.2.3** — Orientation toward future fullness: none — bitterness is oriented only to **corruption** (defiling many, Heb 12:15), the opposite of the grace toward which the community presses. **Silent** on any positive future orientation.

**T0.3.1** — Divine-likeness aspect: none. Bitterness images nothing of God; it is the inner corrosion grace removes (Eph 4:31). **Silent** on a positive image-aspect.

**T0.3.2** — Shared/creaturely: a purely **creaturely disorder** — not a shared attribute. Record: not borne by God.

**T0.3.3** — Where bitterness is present it indicates a **heart not right before God** (Act 8:21,23 "your heart is not right... you are in the gall of bitterness") and a condition that **defiles** community (Heb 12:15). It marks corrupted inner standing.

**T0.4.1** — Typological/eschatological use: the **"root of bitterness"** of Heb 12:15 alludes to Deu 29:18 ("a root bearing poisonous and bitter fruit" — covenant apostasy), giving bitterness a **covenant-warning** resonance. Direction: an OT covenant-warning applied to the NT community. Otherwise none.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: **Bitterness**. The name signals a **settled inner sourness/resentment** that corrupts speech and community. Sense: "bitterness" (pikria) in all four.

**T1.1.2** — Primary term: **pikria** (Greek, "bitterness") — from pikros ("sharp, bitter" to taste), used metaphorically for **resentful, harsh inner disposition** (Eph 4:31) and corrupt speech (Rom 3:14). The single term carries the whole characteristic in this set.

**T1.1.3** — Directional/relational: bitterness is implicitly **against** others — it issues in "curses" against people (Rom 3:14) and "defiles many" relationally (Heb 12:15); it is listed among relationship-destroying vices to put off (Eph 4:31 "bitterness... wrath... anger... clamor... slander"). Record the relational, corrupting direction.

**T1.2.1** — A **condition/status** (all four are status-type nouns) — bitterness is a settled state, not an act. "Gall of bitterness" (Act 8:23), "full of... bitterness" (Rom 3:14), "all bitterness" (Eph 4:31), "root of bitterness" (Heb 12:15) all name a state.

**T1.2.2** — **Compound** in three of four — bitterness is listed alongside partner vices: with **iniquity** (Act 8:23 "bond of iniquity"); with **cursing** (Rom 3:14); with **wrath, anger, clamor, slander, malice** (Eph 4:31, co-terms orgē/kakia/blasfēmia); and it "defiles" (miainō, Heb 12:15). It rarely stands alone — it clusters with anger and malice.

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **the grace of God** and the kindness that replaces it — Heb 12:15 opposes the bitter root to "the grace of God"; Eph 4:32 opposes the bitterness of 4:31 to "be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving." Grace/kindness/forgiveness stands against bitterness.

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes **kindness and forgiveness** — the remedy is to "put away... and be kind... forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you" (Eph 4:31–32). Bitterness resists forgiveness.

**T1.3.3** — It ends where **wrath/anger** (C1) shade into a *settled* resentment — bitterness is the **chronic, settled** form listed beside the more acute "wrath and anger" (Eph 4:31), distinguished as the sour residue rather than the hot flare.

**T1.4.1** — Modes: as **"gall of bitterness"** (a poisoning condition, Act 8:23); as **bitter speech** ("mouth full of... bitterness," Rom 3:14); as a **disposition to be put off** (Eph 4:31); as a **"root" that springs up** and spreads (Heb 12:15). The root-mode (latent then springing up and defiling) is the most developed.

**T1.4.2** — Yes — mode varies across the four: a present poisoning state (Act 8:23), corrupt speech (Rom 3:14), a vice to renounce (Eph 4:31), a latent-then-spreading root (Heb 12:15). Same noun, four facets.

**T1.4.3** — Communicative/spoken mode: yes — bitterness issues in **speech** ("their mouth is full of curses and bitterness," Rom 3:14). Recorded; the other three are dispositional.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response: in Eph 4:31 the called-for response is to **"put it away"** (the one immediate_response value in the data); in Heb 12:15 the response to guard against is the trouble and defilement it causes. Otherwise **silent** on an immediate response.

**T1.5.2** — Not enough data to vary — only Eph 4:31 states a response (put it away). **Silent** elsewhere.

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: bitterness **"springs up... causes trouble... defiles many"** (Heb 12:15) — its sustained effect is **spreading contamination** of the community. This is the clearest effect in the set.

**T1.6.3** — The data does not distinguish an immediate flash from a sustained effect except in Heb 12:15, where the sustained effect is **communal defilement**. Otherwise **silent**.

**T1.7.1** — Conditions where it "takes hold": bitterness takes root in a **heart not right before God** (Act 8:21,23) and where the community **"fails to obtain the grace of God"** (Heb 12:15). The condition is a grace-deficient, unforgiving heart.

**T1.7.2** — It is **removed/blocked** by repentance (Act 8:22 "repent... pray that... the intent of your heart may be forgiven") and by putting it off in favour of kindness and forgiveness (Eph 4:31–32). No verse attributes bitterness to another spirit — **silent** on that.

**T1.7.3** — Where bitterness takes hold, the person is shown **bound in iniquity and unforgiven** until repentance (Act 8:23) and the community **defiled** (Heb 12:15). It is a corrupting, spreading condition.

**T6.4.1** — Shared vocabulary: pikria is **distinct** from the orgē/thumos anger lemmas but is **listed with** them (Eph 4:31) — shared collocation, not shared root. It links lexically to the **defilement** field via miainō (Heb 12:15).

**T6.4.3** — The vocabulary picture shows C7 is a **distinct, narrowly-attested disposition** (one term, four verses) that sits within the anger cluster by **collocation** (Eph 4:31) and by its shared corrupting effect, not by shared lexis.

**T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour: **C1 human anger** (listed immediately beside it, Eph 4:31 "bitterness and wrath and anger"). The precise distinction: anger is the **acute flare**; bitterness is the **chronic, settled sourness** underlying it.

**T6.5.2** — Apparent overlap: bitterness and "wrath/anger" appear together (Eph 4:31); the **precise boundary** is duration/register — bitterness is the **settled resentful root**, wrath/anger the **active outbursts**. Bitterness also links to **defilement** (Heb 12:15), distinguishing its **contaminating** character.

**T6.5.3** — Distinction from C1 is one of **chronicity** (settled resentment vs acute anger); from the defilement field one of **emphasis** (resentful disposition vs ritual/moral staining), though Heb 12:15 joins them.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary term/root: **pikria** (G4088, "bitterness"), from **pikros** ("sharp, bitter"); cognate verb **pikrainō** ("to make bitter"). Root sense: **bitter to the taste**, extended metaphorically to **resentful, harsh inner disposition** and corrupt speech.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **noun/status only** in this set (all 4) — bitterness named as a **state/condition** ("gall of bitterness," "root of bitterness"), never as a verb or quality-adjective here. The range is narrow: a settled noun-state.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: within four verses — a **poisoning condition** ("gall of bitterness," Act 8:23), **corrupt speech** ("full of... bitterness," Rom 3:14), a **vice to renounce** (Eph 4:31), a **spreading root** (Heb 12:15). All negative; breadth limited to facets of one resentful disposition.

**T7.1.4** — Distinguishing terms: none internal to the set (single term). The **"gall" (cholē)** and **"root" (rhiza)** and **"defile" (miainō)** are the accompanying images that distinguish the facets (poison / latent source / contamination). Recorded.

**T7.1.5** — Opposite/absence term: **the grace of God** (Heb 12:15) and **kindness/forgiveness** (Eph 4:32, chrēstos/forgiving) function as the structural opposites. Idiomatic, not a single antonym lexeme.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: none in this set (no "bitter-type person" noun; pikros as a person-descriptor — e.g. "do not be harsh/bitter to them," Col 3:19, pikrainō — lies just outside the focus set). Record none here; flag Col 3:19 as adjacent.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: none. Record none.

**T7.1.8** — OT→NT relationship and **scope gap**: this focus set is **NT-only (pikria)**. The **Hebrew bitterness field is absent from the set** — marah/mar ("bitter; bitterness of soul"): Naomi "call me Mara, for the Almighty has dealt very bitterly" (Ruth 1:20–21); Hannah "bitterness of soul" (1Sa 1:10); Job "I will speak in the bitterness of my soul" (Job 7:11; 10:1); "the bitter in soul" (Job 3:20); Hezekiah (Isa 38:15,17). That field includes **grief-laden, non-sinful** bitterness (bitterness of affliction), which the NT pikria set does **not** capture. **This is a known gap**: C7 as constituted reflects only the NT resentment-bitterness, not the OT affliction-bitterness. Flagged for researcher review and possible cross-cluster check with M03 (Grief). *(Inferential/flagged — not in the focus set.)*

**T7.1.9** — NT-coined term: pikria is not NT-coined (classical Greek), but its **metaphorical-ethical use** for resentful disposition (Eph 4:31) and the **"root of bitterness"** covenant-allusion (Heb 12:15, echoing Deu 29:18 LXX) are characteristic NT/LXX developments. Recorded.

**T7.1.10** — Full vocabulary arc: within this set, a **single noun** (pikria) presenting bitterness as a **uniformly sinful, settled, contaminating disposition** — poison (Act 8:23), corrupt speech (Rom 3:14), vice to renounce (Eph 4:31), defiling root (Heb 12:15). The fuller biblical arc (including OT affliction-bitterness) lies outside the set (T7.1.8).
