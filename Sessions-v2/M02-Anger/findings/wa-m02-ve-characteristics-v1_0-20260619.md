## The seven characteristics

### C1 — Human kindled / burning anger (affect) — 228 occ
What the verse is about: the heated inner affective state arising in a human experiencer — anger kindled, burning, fury — directed at a person, a situation, or (rarely) an abstract.
Principal terms (context-level): cheˈmah, chaˈrah, orgē, qaˈtsaph, qeˈtseph, kaˈaˈs (vexation).
Grounding references: Gen 4:5–6 (Cain); Gen 30:2 (Jacob at Rachel); Gen 27:44; Gen 49:7.
Faculty: affect. Type: status + action. Experiencer: human (self / other).

### C2 — Divine wrath (God as possessor / agent) — 145 occ
What the verse is about: anger as God's own possession or agency — the LORD's wrath / fury / burning anger kindled or poured out, characteristically in judgement.
Principal terms (context-level): cheˈmah, chaˈron (burning anger), chaˈrah (God as agent), qeˈtseph, qaˈtsaph, aˈnaph.
Grounding references: Exo 15:7; Exo 22:24; Exo 32:10; Num 11:1.
In-scope via the Divine-Human Correspondence dimension — the same affect-vocabulary named of God. divine_role: possessor / agent.
Researcher decision DEC-2: held distinct from C1 (experiencer NOT collapsed to a perspective).

### C3 — Provoking to anger (causative / relational elicitation) — 85 occ
What the verse is about: the relational/covenantal act of arousing anger in another — overwhelmingly provoking God to anger (idolatry, rebellion). The eliciting interface, not the experiencer's own affect.
Principal terms: kaˈas (provoke to anger); plus chaˈrah / qaˈtsaph / orgē / cheˈmah where God is the object provoked.
Grounding references: Deu 4:25; Deu 9:18; Exo 32:11; Num 22:27.
divine_role: object. Relational direction inherent.

### C4 — Jealousy / zeal — 77 occ
What the verse is about: possessive/protective ardour — jealousy and zeal; envy at the negative pole; God's covenantal jealousy.
Principal terms: qinˈah, qaˈna, qanˈna, qanˈno.
Grounding references: Gen 30:1; Gen 37:11 (human envy); Exo 20:5; Exo 34:14 (divine jealousy — "the LORD, whose name is Jealous").
Researcher decision DEC-4: retained within M02 (not split to its own cluster). Spans human envy and divine jealousy.

### C5 — Indignation / displeasure / sullenness — 49 occ
What the verse is about: a cooler, often moral or sulking displeasure — indignation, fury, denunciation, sullen mood.
Principal terms: zaˈam, zaˈaph, zalˈaˈphah, zaˈeph.
Grounding references: Num 23:7–8 (Balaam — "denounce"); 1Ki 20:43; 21:4 (Ahab — "sullen and angry").
Texture note (DQ-3): a denunciation / cursing edge in zaˈam ("denounce", "accursed", "abhorred").

### C6 — Strife / quarrel / contention — 83 occ
What the verse is about: interpersonal and forensic contention — strife, quarrel, dispute, lawsuit, controversy.
Principal terms: riv, eris, antilogia, matsˈtsah, meˈriˈvah, matsˈtsut, logomachia.
Grounding references: Gen 13:7–8 (Abram/Lot herdsmen); Exo 17:7 (Massah/Meribah); Exo 23:2–3 (lawsuit).
Texture note (DQ-2): faculty-NONE; riv's dominant senses are forensic (cause / lawsuit / case / indictment / controversy). This is an external relational event, not an inner affect.
Researcher decision DEC-3: retained as a characteristic. **VC-stage caveat:** because it is faculty-NONE, each riv-family occurrence must pass the term-level inner-being relevance filter (GR-PROG-007) — does *this term, in this verse,* engage or qualify an inner-being characteristic? — rather than being admitted wholesale.

### C7 — Bitterness — 4 occ
What the verse is about: settled resentment / bitterness (NT).
Principal terms: pikria.
Grounding references: Eph 4:31; Heb 12:15 ("root of bitterness"); Act 8:23; Rom 3:14.
THIN (4 occ). May be carried as a sub-facet of C1 or stand alone; resolve at VC stage.
