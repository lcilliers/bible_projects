## T0 — God-relation, role, divine image, typology

The cluster is saturated with relation to God, but in structurally distinct roles. God is the **bearer** of wrath (C2: Exo 15:7; Gen 18:30), jealousy (C4: Exo 20:5; 34:14), and indignation (C5: Psa 78:49; Isa 10:5); the **object** provoked to anger and to jealousy (C3: 1Ki 14:9; Deu 4:25 / C4: Deu 32:16,21); the **contending party** in covenant-lawsuit and the **advocate** of the oppressed (C6: Hos 4:1; Mic 6:2; Pro 22:23); and the **addressee** of the angry, indignant, or cause-committing person (C1: Gen 4:6; C6: Jer 11:20). Human anger (C1) is borne by the creature with God only as addressee. Bitterness (C7) alone holds **no** divine-bearer relation — it stands explicitly opposed to "the grace of God" (Heb 12:15).

On whether the characteristic is **shared** with God or merely a creaturely analogue: the burning-heat affect (C1/C2), jealousy/zeal (C4), and indignation (C5) are genuinely **shared** under the same vocabulary or root — primarily divine attributes the creature reflects. Just contention (C6) is a creaturely analogue of God's righteous controversy; quarrelsome strife is a purely creaturely disorder. Bitterness (C7) is shared with nothing in God.

On **created design versus the fallen condition**, the cluster is morally graded by bearer and direction. Divine wrath, jealousy, and indignation belong to holy design — they have no fallen register, the fallenness lying in their objects. Human anger leans fallen (Gen 4:5–8; Pro 29:22) yet engages created-design moral response where it answers genuine wrong (Mar 3:5). Provoking God is fallen (Deu 4:25; 31:29); provoking "to love and good works" is redeemed design (Heb 10:24). Jealousy/zeal splits — zeal and divine jealousy belong to holy design (Num 25:11; Isa 9:7), envy to the fall (Gen 30:1; Pro 14:30). Strife splits — human quarrel is fallen, just cause-pleading and the divine lawsuit belong to the moral order of justice. Bitterness is wholly fallen (Eph 4:31).

A **future fullness** orients the divine side: wrath toward the coming "wrath" (Rom 9:22; Rev 14:10; 16:19); indignation toward an appointed end (Dan 11:36; Isa 10:25); the "zeal of the LORD" toward covenant consummation (Isa 9:7; 37:32); the divine controversy toward a day of recompense (Isa 34:8); and even provoking-to-jealousy toward Israel's salvation (Rom 10:19). Human anger and bitterness carry no positive forward orientation.

**Divine image and typology.** The image-aspect running through the cluster is the capacity for **kindled moral response** — instantiated where it images God (righteous anger, Mar 3:5; zeal, Num 25:11; righteous indignation, Psa 119:53; just contention, Pro 22:23) and counterfeited where distorted (envy, sullen indignation, sinful strife). Bitterness images nothing of God. Typologically, the **divine instance establishes the pattern**: divine wrath grounds the eschatological "wrath to come"; the zeal of the LORD is messianic (Isa 9:7); the covenant-lawsuit *rîb* points to the covenant-court reality (Mic 6:1–2); the "root of bitterness" (Heb 12:15) carries a covenant-warning resonance (Deu 29:18). The human poles are largely silent on typology in the NEW set — **noted gap:** OLD read Phinehas' atoning zeal typologically (human→divine); this is flagged for verification (audit item, below).

**Cluster-level T0 finding.** M02's governing T0 result is the **Divine–Human Correspondence**: the same kindled-response vocabulary names a divine attribute and its creaturely image, graded morally by *who bears it* and *toward whom*, with bitterness as the single member that images nothing of God.

## T1 — Name, structure, opposites, modes, immediate and sustained effects, conditions

**Two vocabulary architectures.** A shared burning-heat family — *cheˈmah, chaˈrah, chaˈron, qaˈtsaph, qeˈtseph, orgē* — supplies **only** C1, C2, and C3, differentiated by relation to the subject (experienced / borne / aroused). C4 (*qinˈah/qaˈna*), C5 (*zaˈam/zaˈaph*), C6 (*riv/eris*), and C7 (*pikria*) each run on a distinct root, joined to the cluster by collocation and concept rather than shared word-stock. The names accordingly signal thermal arousal (C1/C2), causative elicitation (C3), possessive ardour (C4), cooler settled displeasure (C5), two-party contention (C6), and settled sourness (C7).

**Kind of phenomenon.** The cluster is status-heavy — the affects are named more as states than acts — the exception being C3, which is action-dominant because it names a *doing* (provoking another). C6 is an external relational event (faculty-NONE in 82/83), and C7 a settled disposition. The characteristics are typically **compound**, fusing with their neighbours: wrath with jealousy ("the fire of his jealousy," Zep 1:18; "anger and jealousy," Deu 29:20), wrath with indignation ("his wrath, indignation, and distress," Psa 78:49), and bitterness heading a vice-chain (Eph 4:31).

**Structural opposites and boundaries.** Each has an opposite the evidence names: slowness to anger and the soft answer (C1: Pro 15:1,18); mercy/favour as wrath turns away (C2: Jos 7:26; Psa 85:3); contentment against envy and indifference against zeal (C4: Pro 14:30); favour against sullenness (C5); peace against strife (C6: Pro 20:3); and grace, kindness, forgiveness against bitterness (C7: Eph 4:31–32). The boundaries among C1/C2/C3 are **relational** (bearer/direction), which is why their edges leak; C4–C7 are bounded by distinct roots and registers.

**Modes** span kindling verbs, poured-out and filling states (C1/C2), causative *hiphil* (C3), a possessive attribute / consuming fire / "spirit that comes over" (C4), a judicial staff / denouncing speech / scorching heat / sullen withdrawal (C5), quarrel / lawsuit / covenant-lawsuit / cause-committing / ecclesial dissension (C6), and gall / bitter speech / vice / spreading root (C7). A **speech-based mode** runs across the cluster: commanded restraint ("fret not," Psa 37:8), God declaring his wrath and jealousy (Eze 36:5), verbal provocation (1Sa 1:6), indignation as denunciation (Num 23:7), strife as intrinsically verbal contention (1Ti 6:4), and bitterness issuing in cursing (Rom 3:14).

**Immediate and sustained effects** are varied per characteristic but follow a common shape: an immediate flash (fallen face, outburst, withdrawal, decisive act, separation) and a sustained settling (anger lodging into strife/transgression; wrath into desolation or restored covenant; envy into bodily decay; indignation into festering scheme; strife into division; bitterness into spreading contamination).

**Conditions.** Each takes hold "rightly" on a true ground — moral perception of genuine wrong (C1: Mar 3:5; Gen 34:7), covenant violation (C2), a real offence (C3), threat to a rightful bond (C4: Num 5:14; Exo 34:14), alignment with God's standard (C5: Psa 119:53), a genuine justice claim (C6: Pro 22:23) — and is blocked or turned by restraint, intercession/repentance, non-uptake, will-redirection, God's blessing (Balaam, Num 23:8), forbearance, or repentance-and-put-on. **A uniform cluster-level negative:** no verse in any characteristic attributes the affect to an adversarial or angelic spirit — the "spirit of jealousy" (Num 5:14) is a disposition that comes over a person, not an external being.

## T2 — Constitutional location and movement

**Seat.** The cluster is sparsely seat-marked (about 45 of 671 occurrences locate a level), which itself indicates the affects are treated as whole-person rather than housed in one faculty. Where a seat appears it is chiefly the **heart** (dominant) and **soul**, and notably the **body**: envy "makes the bones rot" (Pro 14:30), divine indignation leaves "no soundness in my flesh" (Psa 38:3), and zeal stirs the "inward parts" (Isa 63:15). Body links read as expressive/indicative (the inner state showing outward) rather than as the seat. C6 is mostly faculty-NONE (an event between parties); C7 carries no term-intrinsic seat, the heart implied only by context (Act 8:21).

> **Cluster-level gap (flagged, S1/L1).** The NEW T2.1.1 audit registers heart/soul/flesh/conscience but **under-represents the spirit level**. The OLD set carried explicit spirit-level findings — rage as "felt inner heat in his spirit" (Eze 3:14), Paul's spirit "provoked" (Act 17:16), and Spirit-empowered anger (1Sa 11:6, a Framework A interface). Whether these are absent from the extract or simply not surfaced in the audit needs checking; if present, the spirit-level seat of C1/C3 is a genuine cluster-level finding currently missing.

**Origin** is multiple across the cluster — generated within the person (C1, C4 envy, C5 sullen), intrinsic to God (C2, C4 divine, C5 divine), arising from the provoker (C3), coming over a person as the marital "spirit of jealousy" (C4: Num 5:14), produced by godly grief (C5: 2Cor 7:11), or grounded in an inner affect or God's just claim (C6). No generational origin and no adversarial-spirit origin are evidenced.

**Movement.** The dominant movement is **relational/external** — party→party (Gen 13:7), God→person (Jer 4:4), person→God (provoking, Deu 4:25; committing one's cause, Jer 11:20) — rather than an internal spirit→soul→body cascade. No internal constitutional sequence is evidenced for any characteristic; bitterness alone shows a latent-root → springs-up → defiles-many movement (Heb 12:15).

**Cluster-level T2 finding.** M02's affects move along **relational axes, not an internal faculty-cascade**, and are seated (where seated at all) in the heart/soul with a striking somatic reach in envy and divine indignation — confirming the three-kinds structure (C1–C5 affect, C6 event, C7 chronic disposition).

## T3 — Faculty engagement

**Affect is the dominant faculty** for C1–C5 (C1 196/228; C2 140/145; C3 78/85; C4 70/77; C5 45/49); C6 and C7 are faculty-NONE — strife an external event, bitterness a named disposition. **Perception** is consistently the trigger-gate: anger, jealousy, indignation, and strife are kindled by what is *heard* or *seen* (Est 3:5; Gen 30:1; Psa 119:53; 1Sa 25:39), the affect being fed by perception rather than reshaping it. **Cognition** is appraisal-bound — each affect presupposes a moral verdict on a wrong, and the wisdom material shows it clouding judgment once it settles (Ecc 7:9; Pro 29:22). **Memory** is thin and operates as backdrop (covenant memory), except C3, where provocation of God is **historical and cumulative** (Deu 9:7; 2Ki 21:15). **Creativity** is uniformly silent across all seven — a clean cluster-level negative.

**Volition** is strongly engaged and the cluster is treated as **governable**: anger placed under command ("refrain… forsake," Psa 37:8), envy commanded against (Pro 23:17), bitterness to be put off (Eph 4:31), strife to be refused (Pro 20:3; 2Ti 2:24). **Agency** is strongly action-issuing (C1/C2/C4/C5 drive acts of judgment, violence, or atoning intervention); C6 is agency exercised between parties; C7 is agency-corrupting (cursing, trouble).

**Moral-evaluation** is the defining faculty-overlap and is graded exactly by bearer and direction: C2 is wholly bound to moral evaluation (always a response to real wrong); C1 is morally double-valued; C3 double-valued by direction; C4 and C5 tri-valued; C6 split (just cause vs fleshly quarrel); C7 uniformly sinful. **Conscience** is engaged only at specific edges — the cause-committing edge (C6: Jer 11:20, the God who tests heart and mind), the deserved-judgment edge (C5: Mic 7:9, "I will bear the indignation… because I have sinned"), and the repentance edge (C7: Act 8:22). **Conscientiousness** marks the cluster as a **test-site of integrated self-governance** — anger to master (Gen 4:7), zeal to direct (Num 25:11), indignation to integrate (2Cor 7:11), bitterness to guard against communally (Heb 12:15). **Relational capacity** is engaged constitutively across all seven: the affects arise within and bear on bonds; strife *is* the rupture of relationship; bitterness is relationally corrosive (Heb 12:15) — C6 and C7 cannot exist without a second party.

**Cluster-level T3 finding.** M02 is a field of **high-arousal, relationally-embedded affects bound to moral evaluation and placed under the will** — with strife and bitterness as the two members that are not themselves inner affects but the outward event and the chronic residue the affects produce.

## T4 — Relational directions and interfaces

**Every direction is populated.** *God→human:* wrath (Exo 22:24), jealousy for and against his people (Zec 1:14; Deu 29:20), indignation (Isa 10:5), covenant-lawsuit (Mic 6:2). *Human→God:* provoking to anger and jealousy (Deu 4:25; 32:21), zeal for God (Num 25:11; Psa 69:9), bearing God's indignation penitently (Mic 7:9), committing one's cause to God (Jer 11:20), and even anger directed at God (Jon 4:1). *Human→human:* anger (Gen 4:5), envy (Gen 30:1), strife (Gen 13:7), provocation (1Sa 1:6), bitterness and cursing (Rom 3:14) — and the positive "stir up one another to love" (Heb 10:24).

The **basis** of God's extension is consistently **covenantal and responsive** — conditional on sin or covenant-breach and relentable on repentance (Jon 3:10; 2Ch 12:7) — revealing God as holy and committed (C2), passionately devoted (C4), morally reactive yet bounded (C5: Isa 10:25), and a righteous Judge who both indicts and advocates (C6). The **relational scope** is wide to universal: covenant-internal yet crossing to the nations (C2/C4/C5/C6), with bitterness narrower (community and speech).

**Spiritual-being interface — a near-uniform silence.** Across the cluster the characteristics are not shown operating toward angelic or adversarial beings; the nearest link is idolatry's false-god/"demon" objects drawing divine jealousy and provocation (Deu 32:16–17). No adversarial weaponising of the characteristic and no angelic mediation is evidenced (the exception being wrath executed through an instrument of judgment). This clean negative is itself a cluster-level finding worth recording for the programme's spiritual-interface question.

## T5 — Transformation, sequence, mechanism, suffering, sanctification

**Transformation and reversibility.** Most characteristics effect a change of condition or relationship, and most are presented as **reversible**: divine wrath and indignation are repeatedly turned away, quenched, or relented (Jos 7:26; Jon 3:10; Isa 10:25); envy is corrected "in the sanctuary" (Psa 73:17); bitterness is "put away" and replaced by kindness (Eph 4:31–32). The two terminal outputs — strife and bitterness — are addressed instead by **prevention** (keep aloof; watch that no root springs up: Pro 20:3; Heb 12:15). The cluster thus portrays the inner being as relationally embedded and **morally plastic** — these affects are rarely fixed fate.

**Sequence — the cluster lifecycle.** The sequence data resolves into one chain: a **provocation (C3)** arouses an **affect (anger C1, wrath C2, jealousy C4, or the cooler indignation C5)**; the affect is borne rightly or sinfully; and, if unmastered, it issues **outward as strife (C6)** or settles **inward as bitterness (C7)**. M02 is therefore not seven parallel topics but a connected system with provocation at the front, the affects at the centre, and strife and bitterness as the two terminal outputs.

**Mechanism** divides into arousal/elicitation (a perceived wrong, idolatry, taunting) and attenuation (restraint, the soft answer, intercession, reframing, repentance, deliberate put-off). **Suffering** stands in a double relation — the affects are both produced by and productive of suffering — and, redirected Godward, suffering turns the affect toward prayer, confession, or committing the cause to God (1Sa 1:10; Mic 7:9; Jer 11:20). In the **sanctification arc**, the cluster supplies both vices to put off (anger, bitterness, strife: Eph 4:31; Col 3:8) and a righteous form that is the *fruit* of repentance (godly indignation and zeal, 2Cor 7:11); divine wrath itself functions as covenant discipline shaping the people over time (Lev 26:28; Isa 57:17).

## T6 — Cross-characteristic relations

The co-occurrence and "produced-by" data confirm the **internal causal structure**: C3 (provoking) is causally prior and arouses C1/C2/C4/C5 (Deu 32:16,21; Psa 78:58–59); the inner affects in turn **produce** C6 strife as their downstream relational output ("pressing anger produces strife," Pro 30:33; "jealousy and strife," 1Cor 3:3; "hatred stirs up strife," Pro 10:12); and C7 bitterness sits as the settled root or residue heading the anger list (Eph 4:31; Heb 12:15).

**Vocabulary** confirms the architecture: one burning-heat root spans C1/C2/C3 (bearer and relation, not vocabulary, distinguish them), while C4/C5/C6/C7 each have their own root, related by collocation and concept. **Nearest-neighbour boundaries** are: C1↔C2 (bearer and moral standing); C3↔C4 (the act of arousing vs the affect aroused); C4↔C2 (possessive ardour over a bond vs judicial heat, which fuse in God — "fire of his jealousy," Zep 1:18); C5↔C1/C2 (cooler/judicial/sulking vs hot); C6↔C1 (an external event vs an inner affect); C7↔C1 (chronic settled resentment vs acute flare).

> **Cluster-level synergies to draw out here (S3, S6).** (i) Although C4 runs on a distinct root, its **fire imagery** ("fire of his jealousy," Zep 1:18; "vehement flame," Song 8:6) binds it to the burning family at the level of conceptual metaphor, not just collocation. (ii) C5 (sullen, settled indignation) and C7 (settled bitterness) together form a **chronic/settled pole** against the **acute/kindled** C1/C2 — a cross-characteristic axis the per-characteristic boundaries imply but do not state.

## T7 — Lexical/linguistic, anchors, human-science

**Vocabulary arc.** Each characteristic carries a full semantic arc; C4 is the most semantically unified (one root holding divine jealousy, human zeal, and rivalrous envy, its valence set entirely by direction and object), while C6's *riv* splits from an OT-unified contention-term into NT vocabulary that **moralises** quarrelling as a work of the flesh. C7 is NT-only *pikria* (four verses); the OT *marah/mar* "bitterness of soul" field (Ruth 1:20; 1Sa 1:10; Job 7:11) — which includes grief-laden, non-sinful bitterness — lies **outside** the focus set, the known scope gap flagged for C7's membership review.

**Anchors.** The cluster's anchor verses are Gen 4:5–6 (C1, anger kindled in the face and addressed by God before it acts), Exo 32:10–14 (C2, wrath kindled to consume, met by intercession, relented), Deu 4:25 (C3, the deliberate arousal of God's wrath, with Heb 10:24 as the positive pole), Exo 34:14 (C4, jealousy as God's very name), Isa 10:5 with 2Cor 7:11 (C5, divine judicial and human repentant indignation), Mic 6:2 with 1Cor 3:3 (C6, God's covenant-lawsuit and strife as the fruit of the flesh), and Heb 12:15 (C7, a latent root that springs up, troubles, and defiles many).

**Human science.** The most useful lenses are emotion theory (C1), moral psychology (C3, C5), social and evolutionary psychology (C4, C6), and resentment/rumination research (C7). The **deepest divergence** is that science has no category for **holy wrath** or **divine jealousy** (C2, C4) — these lie outside its descriptive scope; science also tends to pathologise anger, whereas Scripture sustains a righteous form of anger, indignation, and zeal.

## Integrative cluster statement

Read across all eight tiers, M02 is **one heat-architecture under three relations (C1 human, C2 divine, C3 the act of arousing) plus four distinct inner phenomena (C4 jealousy/zeal, C5 indignation, C6 strife, C7 bitterness)**, organised by a single lifecycle — provocation → affect (borne rightly or sinfully) → strife outward or bitterness inward — and graded morally by bearer and direction. Its deepest theological result is the **Divine–Human Correspondence**: the capacity for kindled moral response is a point of likeness to God, righteous when it images him and corrupt when distorted, with bitterness as the lone member that images nothing of God. Its constitutional result is that these are **whole-person, relationally-moving affects** seated (where seated) in the heart and soul with a somatic reach, not an internal faculty-cascade — strife being their outward relational event and bitterness their chronic inward residue.
