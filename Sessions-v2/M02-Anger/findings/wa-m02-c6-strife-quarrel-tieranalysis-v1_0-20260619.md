## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — Relation to God: God **has a controversy/indictment (rîb)** with His people and the nations — "the LORD has a controversy with the inhabitants of the land" (Hos 4:1); "the LORD has an indictment against his people" (Mic 6:2); "an indictment against the nations" (Jer 25:31) — div_role agent. God is also the one to whom a person **commits his cause** (Psa 35:23; Lam 3:58 "you have taken up my cause") and who **pleads the cause** of the oppressed (Pro 22:23; Psa 74:22). Most human-strife occurrences have no God-relation (Gen 13:7).

**T0.1.2** — Across C6, contention is **borne by God** as covenant-lawsuit (Hos 4:1; Mic 6:2) and by the **creature** as quarrel/strife (Gen 13:7; 1Cor 3:3). *Interpretation:* the divine instance is **judicial covenant-contention**; the creaturely instance ranges from righteous cause-pleading (1Sa 24:15) to sinful quarrelling (1Cor 3:3). The shared term riv links God's just controversy and human strife.

**T0.2.1** — Stated purpose/effect: human strife **divides and harms** — herdsmen's strife forces separation (Gen 13:7–9); "pressing anger produces strife" (Pro 30:33); strife of the flesh marks immaturity (1Cor 3:3). God's controversy **summons to judgment/repentance** (Mic 6:2). The committed cause **secures vindication** (Lam 3:58–59; 1Sa 24:15). Record none where unstated.

**T0.2.2** — valence confirms the split: **sinful 33 / forbidden 1** (the quarrel/strife pole — Pro 17:14; 1Cor 3:3; Tit 3:9) vs **neutral 41** (the judicial-cause and divine-lawsuit pole — Exo 23:2; Mic 6:2). Human quarrelsome strife belongs to the fallen condition; just cause-pleading and the divine lawsuit belong to the moral order of justice.

**T0.2.3** — Future-fullness orientation: the divine controversy is oriented to a **day of vindication/recompense** ("the day of vengeance, the year of recompense for the cause of Zion," Isa 34:8); the committed cause looks to future **vindication** (Lam 3:58–59). Record where present; the human-quarrel pole has none.

**T0.3.1** — Divine-likeness aspect: the aspect instantiated is **just contention for the right** — pleading the cause of the oppressed images God who "will plead their cause" (Pro 22:23); committing one's cause to the righteous Judge images trust in His justice (Jer 11:20). Sinful quarrelling images nothing of God.

**T0.3.2** — Shared/creaturely: just cause-contention is a **creaturely analogue** of God's righteous controversy; quarrelsome strife is a purely creaturely disorder. The rîb vocabulary is shared between God's lawsuit and human dispute.

**T0.3.3** — Where a person commits their cause to God it indicates **faith in the righteous Judge** (Jer 11:20); where a person is given to strife it indicates a **fleshly, immature condition** (1Cor 3:3 "you are still of the flesh... there is jealousy and strife among you").

**T0.4.1** — Typological use: the **covenant-lawsuit (rîb)** form is typological-covenantal — God's formal indictment summoning the mountains as witnesses (Mic 6:1–2) points to the covenant-court reality. Direction: divine instance establishing the pattern. Elsewhere narrative/wisdom, no typology.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: **Strife / quarrel / contention**. The name signals an **external relational/forensic conflict** — strife, quarrel, dispute, lawsuit — not an inner affect. Senses: strife 18, cause 16, dispute 9, case/lawsuit 9, quarrel(ing) 7, indictment 3.

**T1.1.2** — Primary terms: riv (Hebrew, "strife, dispute, legal case, controversy"; verb "to contend/plead a cause") — the dominant term, spanning quarrel and lawsuit; Greek eris ("quarrel, strife," a work of the flesh, Gal 5:20), antilogia ("dispute, hostility"), logomachia ("quarrel about words"). Definitionally a **contention between parties** — interpersonal or forensic.

**T1.1.3** — Directional/relational: contention is inherently **between two parties** — "a quarrel between" herdsmen (Gen 13:7), a "cause between a man and his neighbour" (Deu 25:1), God's controversy "with" His people (Hos 4:1). It is irreducibly two-party.

**T1.2.1** — Predominantly a **condition/status** (noun, 80 of 83) — strife/a dispute/a cause is a *thing* that exists between parties (Pro 17:14 "the beginning of strife"), not chiefly an act of the inner being. (The single affect/act occurrence is the prideful "dispute" of Luk 22:24.)

**T1.2.2** — **Compound** where it engages the inner being: strife joins **anger** ("pressing anger produces strife," Pro 30:33), **jealousy** ("jealousy and strife," 1Cor 3:3), **pride** (the dispute over greatness, Luk 22:24), and a cluster of **works of the flesh** (eris, Gal 5:20 context; Rom 1:29). As a bare legal term it is simple (Deu 17:8).

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **peace / a quiet, unifying disposition** — "it is an honour for a man to keep aloof from strife" (Pro 20:3); slowness to anger that "quiets contention" (Pro 15:18). Peace stands against strife.

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes **forbearance and unity**: the wise "keep aloof" (Pro 20:3); believers are charged to **avoid** quarrels (Tit 3:9; the Lord's servant "must not be quarrelsome," 2Ti 2:24). Strife resists peaceable forbearance.

**T1.3.3** — It ends where **anger/jealousy** (its inner cause, C1/C4) begins — strife is the *outward event* those affects produce (Pro 30:33; 1Cor 3:3) — and where mere **legal procedure** begins (the inner-being-peripheral "cause/lawsuit," Deu 17:8). It is contention itself, not the affect behind it.

**T1.4.1** — Modes: a **quarrel/strife** between parties (Gen 13:7); a **legal cause/lawsuit** (Exo 23:2–3; Deu 25:1); a **covenant-lawsuit** God brings (Mic 6:2); a **cause committed to God** for vindication (Jer 11:20; Lam 3:58); **fleshly quarrelling/dissension** in the church (1Cor 3:3; Tit 3:9). Multiple modes.

**T1.4.2** — Yes — mode varies sharply by context: **interpersonal** quarrel (Gen 13:7), **forensic** case (Deu 25:1), **divine** covenant-lawsuit (Hos 4:1; Mic 6:2), **prayerful** cause-committing (Jer 11:20), **ecclesial** dissension (1Cor 3:3). Same term, very different registers.

**T1.4.3** — Communicative/spoken mode: yes, intrinsically — contention is conducted in **speech** (pleadings; "quarrels about words," 1Ti 6:4; the dispute "arose among them," Luk 22:24); God's controversy is a **spoken indictment** (Mic 6:1–2). Recorded.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response: strife issues in **separation** (Abram and Lot part, Gen 13:8–9); the dispute over greatness draws **Jesus' correction** (Luk 22:24–26); the committed cause draws **God's action to vindicate** (Lam 3:58–59). Record none where unstated.

**T1.5.2** — Varied: separation (Gen 13:8), rebuke (Luk 22:25), or divine vindication (Lam 3:58). No single immediate response.

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: strife, sustained, **escalates** ("the beginning of strife is like letting out water; so quit before the quarrel breaks out," Pro 17:14) and **divides** communities (1Cor 1:11; 3:3). God's controversy, sustained, brings **judgment** (Hos 4:1–3 the land mourns). The committed cause yields lasting **redemption** (Lam 3:58). Record none where unstated.

**T1.6.3** — Immediate response is the flash (separation/rebuke); sustained effect is the **escalation/division or vindication** (Pro 17:14; 1Cor 3:3; Lam 3:58). Episode vs settled result.

**T1.7.1** — It "operates" (rightly, valence neutral) as **just cause-pleading** — the oppressed whose cause God pleads (Pro 22:23; 23:11), the innocent committing their cause to God (Jer 11:20; 1Sa 24:15). The condition is a genuine claim of justice.

**T1.7.2** — It is **distorted** (valence sinful) as **quarrelsomeness of the flesh** — from jealousy (1Cor 3:3), pride (Luk 22:24), "pressing anger" (Pro 30:33), an "unhealthy craving for controversy" (1Ti 6:4); it is **forbidden** to the Lord's servant (2Ti 2:24; Tit 3:9). No verse attributes strife to another spirit — **silent** on that.

**T1.7.3** — Where strife is present in the fleshly form, the person is shown **immature and divisive** ("of the flesh... behaving only in a human way," 1Cor 3:3) or **proud** (Luk 22:24, contending for greatness against Christ's example).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Locations (only where the inner being is engaged): **soul** — "the LORD will plead their cause and rob of life those who rob them" (Pro 22:23); "you have taken up my cause... you have redeemed my life" (Lam 3:58); "consider him who endured such hostility... lest you grow weary in your souls" (Heb 12:3, antilogia); **conscience|heart** — "O LORD... who test the heart and the mind... to you have I committed my cause" (Jer 11:20; 20:12); **flesh** — "he is entering into judgment with all flesh" (Jer 25:31). 77/83 locate no seat (Gen 13:7) — record none.

**T2.1.2** — The pattern: most strife occurrences locate **no seat** (an external event), confirming the faculty-NONE finding. Where a seat appears it is the **heart/soul** of one **committing a cause to God** (Jer 11:20; Lam 3:58) or **wearying under hostility** (Heb 12:3) — the inner being is engaged only at the cause-committing/endurance edge, not in strife as such. Any body link (Jer 25:31) is **indicative** of universal judgment.

**T2.10.1** — Movement: strife moves **between parties** (external; Gen 13:7); the divine controversy moves **from God onto** the people (Hos 4:1); the committed cause moves **from the person up to God** (Jer 11:20). Strife is also **received-from-outside** as assault on the innocent (Psa 74:22; 1Ti 6:4). No internal spirit→soul→body sequence; no angelic source. Record the party-to-party and person-to-God movements.

**T2.7.1** — Body direction: thin — "all flesh" comes under God's contention (Jer 25:31); souls "grow weary" under hostility (Heb 12:3). Where a link exists it runs **the contention bearing down on the person's soul/flesh** (Heb 12:3; Jer 25:31). No soul→body expression proper to the characteristic — mostly **silent**.

**T2.9.1** — Origin: strife **originates between parties from an inner cause** — **anger** (Pro 30:33), **jealousy/flesh** (1Cor 3:3), **pride** (Luk 22:24), **hatred** ("hatred stirs up strife," Pro 10:12). The divine controversy originates **in God's covenant claim** (Hos 4:1). Stated sources, where given, are inner affects (the flesh) or God's just claim.

**T2.9.2** — Origin is **multiple**: fleshly affects (Pro 10:12; 30:33; 1Cor 3:3), God's covenant claim (Hos 4:1), and a genuine injustice provoking a just cause (1Sa 25:39). It changes with context.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.1.1** — Perception: strife is triggered by perceived **insult/wrong** — Nabal's insult provokes the cause David would have pursued (1Sa 25:39); the dispute over greatness follows the perception of rank (Luk 22:24). Perception is the gate where the inner being is engaged.

**T3.1.2** — Effect on perception: contention **fixes** parties on the grievance (1Cor 1:11–12 partisan alignment); no verse states it distorting the senses generally — otherwise **silent**.

**T3.1.3** — Pattern: where C6 engages the inner being it is **perception-triggered**; the procedural-legal occurrences engage perception only formally.

**T3.2.1** — Cognition: engaged in the **"quarrels about words/controversy" form** — "an unhealthy craving for controversy and for quarrels about words" (1Ti 6:4); "foolish controversies... quarrels about the law" (Tit 3:9). Strife here is a **disorder of reasoning** (logomachia).

**T3.2.2** — Effect on cognition: such strife **degrades** thought into "envy, dissension, slander, evil suspicions" (1Ti 6:4); committing one's cause to God **settles** the mind in trust (Jer 11:20).

**T3.2.3** — Pattern: the controversy-form **engages and corrupts cognition** (1Ti 6:4; Tit 3:9); the legal-procedural form engages cognition only as formal adjudication.

**T3.3.1** — Memory: thin — the divine controversy invokes **remembered covenant** ("no faithfulness... no knowledge of God," Hos 4:1). Direct memory-faculty engagement is limited — mostly **silent**.

**T3.3.2** — **Silent** on strife acting on a memory faculty.

**T3.3.3** — The thinness indicates strife is framed as a **present event**, covenant memory only the backdrop of the divine lawsuit.

**T3.4.1** — Affect: **almost never** the term's own faculty (NONE 82/83). The single affect-engaging occurrence is the **prideful dispute** over greatness (Luk 22:24). Otherwise strife engages affect only **as its cause** (anger/jealousy produce it, Pro 30:33; 1Cor 3:3), not as its own faculty.

**T3.4.2** — Effect on affect: strife **inflames** the affects it springs from (escalating, Pro 17:14) and wearies the soul (Heb 12:3).

**T3.4.3** — The near-total faculty-NONE pattern is the **defining finding**: strife is **not an inner affect** but an external event produced by inner affects — the basis of the GR-PROG-007 caveat.

**T3.5.1** — Creativity: not engaged. **Silent.**

**T3.5.2** — **Silent.**

**T3.5.3** — Absence indicates no relation to the originative faculty.

**T3.6.1** — Volition: engaged where the will is summoned **away** from strife — "keep aloof from strife" (Pro 20:3); "have nothing to do with foolish controversies; they breed quarrels" (2Ti 2:23–24) — and where one **chooses** to commit a cause to God rather than self-avenge (1Sa 24:15; Jer 11:20).

**T3.6.2** — Effect on volition: fleshly strife **captures** the will toward division (1Cor 3:3); the disciplined will **withdraws** (Pro 20:3) or **redirects** to God's justice (Jer 11:20).

**T3.6.3** — Pattern: strife engages the **will** chiefly as something to be *refused* (Pro 20:3; 2Ti 2:24) or *redirected* (commit the cause to God, Jer 11:20).

**T3.7.1** — Agency: strife is **enacted agency between parties** (the quarrel "between" herdsmen, Gen 13:7; the lawsuit pursued, Deu 25:1); committing a cause is the agency of **appeal to God** (Lam 3:58); God's controversy is His **judicial agency** (Mic 6:2).

**T3.7.2** — Effect on agency: strife **drives** separation (Gen 13:9), partisan division (1Cor 1:11), or — redirected — **rest in God's action** (1Sa 24:15; Pro 22:23).

**T3.7.3** — The pattern shows strife as **agency exercised between parties** — destructive when fleshly, just when it is God's controversy or a cause rightly committed to Him.

**T3.8.1** — Moral evaluation: engaged at both poles — fleshly strife is **condemned** (Gal 5:20 context; 1Cor 3:3; Tit 3:9); God's controversy **is** a moral indictment (Hos 4:1; Mic 6:2); a just cause is **morally vindicated** (1Sa 24:15). valence: sinful 33, forbidden 1, neutral 41.

**T3.8.2** — Effect: strife **expresses** a moral state — the fleshly verdict on the quarreller (1Cor 3:3) and God's verdict on the covenant-breaker (Mic 6:2).

**T3.8.3** — Pattern: contention is **morally split** — sinful when fleshly quarrelling, righteous/neutral when it is God's just controversy or the oppressed's cause (the valence sinful-33/neutral-41 split confirms this).

**T3.9.1** — Conscience: engaged where the one committing a cause appeals to God who **tests heart and mind** — "O LORD of hosts, who judges righteously, who tests the heart and the mind... to you have I committed my cause" (Jer 11:20; 20:12). The conscience appeals to the searching God.

**T3.9.2** — Effect on conscience: committing the cause to the heart-testing God **rests** the conscience in His justice (Jer 11:20). Recorded; elsewhere not stated.

**T3.9.3** — The Jeremiah cases indicate the cause-committing form engages **conscience** (the heart known to God), though strife as such does not.

**T3.10.1** — Conscientiousness: engaged where the integrated moral person **refuses** strife and **commits** the cause to God (Pro 20:3; Jer 11:20; 2Ti 2:24).

**T3.10.2** — Effect: exercised conscientiousness **avoids** strife and entrusts justice to God (Pro 20:3; Jer 11:20); its failure yields **fleshly division** (1Cor 3:3).

**T3.10.3** — The pattern marks the *response to* strife (avoid it / commit the cause to God) as a **test-site of integrated character**, even though strife itself is not an inner faculty.

**T3.11.1** — Relational capacity: strife is **purely relational** — it exists only between parties (Gen 13:7; Deu 25:1; Hos 4:1) and is the **breakdown** of relational capacity (division, 1Cor 1:11; separation, Gen 13:9).

**T3.11.2** — Effect on relational capacity: strife **fractures** bonds (Abram/Lot separate, Gen 13:9; the church divides, 1Cor 3:3); committing one's cause to God **preserves** the relationship from vengeance (David spares Saul, 1Sa 24:12,15).

**T3.11.3** — The pattern shows C6 is **constitutively relational** (it cannot exist without two parties) and is chiefly the **rupture** of relationship — its inner-being relevance lies in the affects that drive it and the trust that refuses it.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1.1** — God-toward-human: yes — God **contends** with humans in covenant-lawsuit (Hos 4:1; 12:2; Mic 6:2; Jer 25:31) and **pleads the cause** of the oppressed (Pro 22:23; 23:11; Psa 74:22).

**T4.1.2** — Basis: **covenantal and responsive** — God's controversy is grounded in covenant unfaithfulness (Hos 4:1 "no faithfulness... no knowledge of God") and His cause-pleading in His commitment to justice for the weak (Pro 22:22–23). Conditional on the moral situation.

**T4.1.3** — What it shows of God's disposition: God is a **righteous Judge** who both **indicts** covenant-breakers (Mic 6:2) and **advocates** for the oppressed (Pro 22:23) — His contention reveals committed justice.

**T4.2.1** — Human-to-God: yes — the person **commits/pleads their cause to God** ("to you have I committed my cause," Jer 11:20; "plead my cause, O LORD," Psa 35:1,23; "you have pleaded the cause of my soul," Lam 3:58). The movement is the appeal of the wronged to the righteous Judge.

**T4.2.2** — Inner posture required: **trust in God's justice and surrender of self-vengeance** — "may the LORD judge between me and you" (1Sa 24:12,15). The posture is faith that lets God contend.

**T4.2.3** — What it shows: committing one's cause to God shows a relationship of **trust in His righteous judgment** (Jer 11:20; Lam 3:58) — the wronged person rests in God rather than self-avenging.

**T4.3.1** — Person-to-person extension: yes — strife/quarrel is "extended" between persons (Gen 13:7; the Corinthian quarrels, 1Cor 1:11; brother against brother, 1Cor 6:6 context). It operates as mutual contention.

**T4.3.2** — Inner conditions accompanying it: **anger, jealousy, pride, hatred** drive it (Pro 10:12; 30:33; 1Cor 3:3; Luk 22:24); an "unhealthy craving for controversy" (1Ti 6:4).

**T4.3.3** — What the giver must have/become: no virtue is required to start a quarrel; it requires a **fleshly** condition (1Cor 3:3). To rightly contend for a *just cause* one must have a genuine claim and entrust it to God (1Sa 24:15). Recorded.

**T4.4.1** — Receiving: the other party **receives** the strife as conflict (Gen 13:7) or the just/divine contention as **indictment** (Mic 6:2; Jer 25:31). Uptake is endured contention.

**T4.4.2** — Conditions of uptake: a **shared stake** (land, Gen 13:7; rank, Luk 22:24; doctrine, 1Ti 6:4) draws parties in; **covenant guilt** makes one the object of God's controversy (Hos 4:1).

**T4.4.3** — The person who meets contention and does not take up the quarrel is shown **keeping aloof / yielding** (Abram yields to Lot, Gen 13:8–9; the honourable man keeps aloof, Pro 20:3).

**T4.5.1** — Within bonds vs across distance: strife operates **within close bonds** (kinsmen, Gen 13:7–8; brothers in the church, 1Cor 1:11) and **across** them (the nations, Jer 25:31; legal cases between strangers, Deu 25:1). Both.

**T4.5.2** — Covenantal scope: God's controversy is **covenant-internal** (against Israel, Hos 4:1; Mic 6:2) and **crosses** to the nations (Jer 25:31). Human strife crosses any line. Both.

**T4.5.3** — Relational scope: **wide** — kinsmen (Gen 13:7), the church (1Cor 3:3), neighbours in court (Deu 25:1), the oppressed and their oppressors (Pro 22:23), God and His people (Mic 6:2), God and the nations (Jer 25:31). Contention reaches across the relational field.

**T4.6.1** — Spiritual beings: not shown operating toward angelic/adversarial beings (the nearest is "the rebellion of Korah," Jude 11, antilogia — human rebellion). **Silent** on a spiritual-being interface.

**T4.6.2** — Adversarial site: not evidenced as a site of adversarial weaponising. **Silent** (recorded as not evidenced).

**T4.6.3** — Angelic mediation: not evidenced. **Silent.**

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — Transformation: strife transforms **relationship and condition** — it forces separation (Gen 13:9), divides a church (1Cor 3:3); God's controversy transforms a people toward **judgment or repentance** (Mic 6:2–8); a committed cause transforms the wronged toward **vindication/redemption** (Lam 3:58). Record none where unstated.

**T5.1.2** — Reversible/irreversible: strife is **resolvable** — quit before it breaks out (Pro 17:14), keep aloof (Pro 20:3), make peace (Gen 13:8–9); once escalated it can become **entrenched** division (1Cor 1:11; 3:3). Both, depending on response.

**T5.2.1** — Sequence: **inner affect (anger/jealousy/pride) → strife → division/escalation** (Pro 30:33; 1Cor 3:3; Pro 17:14); **wrong suffered → cause committed to God → vindication** (1Sa 24:12–15; Lam 3:58); **covenant sin → God's controversy → judgment** (Hos 4:1–3). Record none where no sequence appears.

**T5.3.1** — Mechanism of change: **escalation** ("letting out water," Pro 17:14) for unchecked strife; **withdrawal/peacemaking** (Gen 13:8; Pro 20:3) and **entrusting to God** (Jer 11:20) for resolution; **judicial indictment** for God's controversy (Mic 6:2). Record none where unstated.

**T5.3.2** — Differs: strife is changed by **avoidance/peacemaking** (Pro 20:3), by **entrusting the cause to God** (1Sa 24:15), or, for the guilty, by **God's judgment** (Mic 6:2). Context-dependent.

**T5.4.1** — Relation to suffering: strife **produces suffering** (the oppressed whose cause must be pleaded, Pro 22:22–23; souls wearied by "hostility," Heb 12:3) and arises amid the suffering of **injustice** (1Sa 25:39; Lam 3:58).

**T5.4.2** — What suffering does to it: the suffering of injustice drives the wronged to **commit their cause to God** rather than retaliate (1Sa 24:12–15; Lam 3:58); enduring "hostility" without growing weary is set as the **formative** path (Heb 12:3, looking to Christ who endured antilogia). Suffering can turn contention Godward.

**T5.5.1** — Sanctification arc: the *response to* strife participates in sanctification — the believer is formed by **refusing quarrelsomeness** (2Ti 2:24; Tit 3:9), **keeping aloof** (Pro 20:3), and **enduring hostility** while looking to Christ (Heb 12:3). Fleshly strife is the **mark of immaturity** to be outgrown (1Cor 3:1–3).

**T5.6.1** — Eschatological orientation: the divine controversy is oriented to a **day of recompense** (Isa 34:8); the committed cause looks to **final vindication** (Lam 3:58–59). Record where present; the human-quarrel pole has none.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**T6.1.1** — Co-occurring characteristics: **anger** ("pressing anger produces strife," Pro 30:33; "a hot-tempered man stirs up strife," Pro 15:18 — C1), **jealousy** ("jealousy and strife," 1Cor 3:3 — C4), **hatred** ("hatred stirs up strife," Pro 10:12 — M06), **pride** (the dispute over greatness, Luk 22:24 — M08). Strife co-occurs as the **product** of these affects.

**T6.1.2** — Co-occurrence places C6 as the **outward fruit** of the inner anger/jealousy/hatred/pride family — the relational event those characteristics generate, sitting at the boundary of the inner-being field.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms/roots: **riv** (H7379, "strife, dispute, legal cause, controversy"; verb "to contend, plead a cause") — root sense of **contending/conducting a case**; Greek **eris** ("quarrel, strife"), **eritheia** ("rivalry"), **antilogia** ("dispute, contradiction, hostility"), **logomachia** ("word-battle"). Roots converge on **two-party contention**, interpersonal or forensic.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: predominantly **noun/status** (80 of 83 — "strife/a cause," Pro 17:14) with a verbal "contend/plead" use (riv as verb, 1Sa 24:15) and one participial "love of dispute" (filoneikos). The noun-dominance shows the characteristic is a **state of contention between parties**, not an inner act.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: a single Hebrew term (riv) spans **interpersonal quarrel** (Gen 13:7), **legal lawsuit/case** (Deu 25:1), **covenant-lawsuit/indictment** (Mic 6:2), and **pleading/contending for a cause** (1Sa 24:15; Pro 22:23). The Greek adds **factional rivalry** (eritheia), **verbal quarrelling** (logomachia), and **hostility** (antilogia). Breadth: private quarrel → court case → covenant-court → just advocacy.

**T7.1.4** — Distinguishing terms: **the cause vs the quarrel** — riv as a just "cause" (Pro 22:23) vs sinful "strife" (Pro 17:14); **act vs status** — the verb "contend/plead" (1Sa 24:15) vs the noun "strife" (Pro 17:14). The Greek distinguishes **factional rivalry** (eritheia) from **verbal quarrel** (logomachia).

**T7.1.5** — Term for the opposite/absence: not a single lexeme; the opposite is **peace** and the wisdom of **keeping aloof** (Pro 20:3) and **not being quarrelsome** (2Ti 2:24, amachos "not contentious" — a near-antonym). Recorded.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **filoneikos** ("contentious, dispute-loving," Luk 22:24 context) and the "contentious/quarrelsome man/wife" of Proverbs (Pro 21:19; 26:21) — a person-type for the habitually contentious. Recorded.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: the **cause-pleading** vocabulary functions as a "seeking justice from another" term — "plead my cause, O LORD" (Psa 35:1,23; 43:1); riv as appealing to God to contend for one. Recorded (a seeking-of-vindication term).

**T7.1.8** — OT→NT relationship: the Hebrew riv (covenant-controversy and just cause) gives way in the NT to **eris/eritheia/logomachia** as **vices of the flesh** to be put off (Gal 5:20; 1Cor 3:3; Tit 3:9), while righteous advocacy is carried by other vocabulary. Continuity of "contention"; development that **moralises** quarrelling as a fleshly vice and relocates righteous advocacy.

**T7.1.9** — NT term: **logomachia/logomacheō** ("word-battle," 1Ti 6:4; 2Ti 2:14) is a distinctively NT-era formation marking **doctrinal quarrelling**; **eritheia** ("selfish rivalry/factionalism") likewise. Their coinage marks a NT concern with **church-dividing strife**. Recorded.

**T7.1.10** — Full vocabulary arc: from the OT **riv** holding quarrel, lawsuit, covenant-controversy, and just advocacy in one root, to the NT splitting of **fleshly strife** (eris/eritheia/logomachia, to be put off) from righteous advocacy — an arc from a unified contention-term to a moralised division between sinful quarrelling and just contention.

**T7.2.1** — Function of the primary term in its primary verse: in Mic 6:2 riv ("indictment/controversy") is the **governing legal metaphor** of the oracle. In Pro 30:33 riv ("strife") is the **result clause** — "pressing anger produces strife" — naming the social fruit of the inner affect.
