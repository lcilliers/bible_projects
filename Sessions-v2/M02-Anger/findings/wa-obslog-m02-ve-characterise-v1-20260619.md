### F3 — Candidate distinct inner-being characteristics (INTERPRETATION — grounded, provisional)
Derived per GR-PROG-006 (what the verse is about), grounded in verse evidence; boundaries provisional pending researcher direction and verse-read confirmation.
1. Kindled / burning anger as affect — the heated inner state arising in a person (chaˈrah "kindled/burn", cheˈmah "fury/rage", aˈnaph, qaˈtsaph). e.g. Gen 4:5–6; Gen 30:2; Gen 27:44.
2. Wrath as divine possession/agency (divine wrath) — same affect named of God (cheˈmah, chaˈron, qeˈtseph, orgē; chaˈrah w/ God as agent). e.g. Exo 15:7; Exo 32:10; Exo 22:24. In-scope via Divine-Human Correspondence.
3. Provoking to anger (causative / relational elicitation) — arousing anger in another, predominantly God (kaˈas "provoke to anger"; chaˈrah w/ God as object). e.g. Deu 4:25; Deu 9:18; Exo 32:11. About the relational interface, not the experiencer's own affect.
4. Jealousy / zeal — protective/possessive ardour; envy at the negative pole; divine jealousy as covenantal (qinˈah, qaˈna, qanˈna). e.g. Gen 30:1; Gen 37:11 (human envy); Exo 20:5; Exo 34:14 (divine jealousy).
5. Indignation / displeasure / sullenness — cooler, often moral or sulking displeasure; includes denunciation (zaˈam) and sullen mood (zaˈeph: Ahab, 1Ki 20:43; 21:4). Distinct texture from hot wrath.
6. Strife / quarrel / contention — interpersonal/forensic contention (riv, eris, antilogia, logomachia). faculty=NONE; many occurrences are legal (lawsuit/case/indictment/pleadings/controversy). An external relational/forensic event, not an inner affect.
7. Bitterness — settled NT resentment (pikria, 4 occ; Eph 4:31; Heb 12:15; Act 8:23; Rom 3:14).
