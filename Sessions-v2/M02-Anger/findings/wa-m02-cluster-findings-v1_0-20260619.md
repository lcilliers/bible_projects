## F1 — Two vocabulary architectures in one cluster *(guide: T1, T7)*

**Observation.** The cluster does not run on a single vocabulary. A **shared burning-heat family** — cheˈmah, chaˈrah, chaˈron, qaˈtsaph, qeˈtseph, kaˈaˈs, aˈnaph, orgē — supplies **C1 (human anger), C2 (divine wrath), and C3 (provoking)** and *only* those three. The lemma map is unambiguous: cheˈmah appears in C1/C2/C3 (60/51/4), chaˈrah in C1/C2/C3 (56/26/6), orgē in C1/C2/C3 (23/5/5), and so on for every burning-heat term. By contrast **C4 (jealousy/zeal: qinˈah/qaˈna), C5 (indignation: zaˈam/zaˈaph), C6 (strife: riv/eris), and C7 (bitterness: pikria) each run on a distinct root that is shared with no other characteristic.**

**Interpretation.** M02 is therefore **one affect under three relations plus four distinct affects/events**. C1/C2/C3 are not three vocabularies but one heat-architecture differentiated by *relation to the subject*: the same kindling is **experienced** (C1, human bearer), **borne** (C2, divine bearer), or **aroused** (C3, God or a person as object). The boundary between them is relational, not lexical — which is why their edges leak (see F10). C4–C7, having their own roots, are genuinely separate inner phenomena that sit in the cluster by conceptual kinship and co-occurrence, not by shared word-stock. The "burning/heat" root meaning (chaˈron "burning"; cheˈmah "heat, fury, also venom/poison" — Deu 32:33; Psa 58:4) is the conceptual centre of gravity; jealousy adds "ardour," indignation "denunciation/scorching," strife "contention," bitterness "sourness."

## F2 — The Divine–Human correspondence runs through the whole cluster *(guide: T0, T4)*

**Observation.** God appears in a defined role in a large share of occurrences: as **bearer** (possessor/agent) of wrath (C2: 144), jealousy (C4: 22), and indignation (C5: 18); as the **object** provoked to anger (C3: 60) and to jealousy (C4: 8); as **contending party** in the covenant-lawsuit (C6: agent 4, object 5); and as **addressee** in prayer across several (C1, C5, C6). The same vocabulary that names God's wrath (cheˈmah/chaˈron) names human anger (C1); the same root that names God's jealousy (qanˈna, used *only* of God — Exo 20:5; 34:14) names human zeal and envy (C4).

**Interpretation.** Anger, wrath, jealousy, and indignation are presented **primarily as divine attributes that the creature images** — rightly (Phinehas' zeal "with my jealousy," Num 25:11; Moses' anger at the calf, Exo 32:19; righteous indignation, Psa 119:53) or in distortion (envy, Gen 30:1; sullen indignation, 1Ki 21:4). This is the cluster's strongest contribution to the programme's Divine-Human Correspondence dimension: the inner capacity for kindled moral response is a point of likeness to God, and its disorder is the corruption of that likeness. **Bitterness (C7) is the one exception** — never borne by God, set explicitly against "the grace of God" (Heb 12:15); it images nothing of God and has no righteous form.

## F3 — One cluster, three constitutional kinds *(guide: T2, T3)*

**Observation.** By the term-intrinsic **faculty** field the cluster splits sharply:
- **C1–C5 are affects** — faculty=affect dominates (C1 196/228, C2 140/145, C3 78/85, C4 70/77, C5 45/49), frequently overlapping **moral_evaluation** (C1 31; C4's "jealous God" tagged moral_evaluation).
- **C6 is a relational event** — faculty-NONE in 82/83 (strife is not an inner state but a contention between parties).
- **C7 is a settled disposition** — faculty-NONE in all 4 (bitterness named as a condition, not analysed as a faculty-act).

Where a **constitutional seat** is named (sparse — 45/671), it is the **heart** (dominant), **soul**, and notably the **body**: envy "makes the bones rot" (Pro 14:30, heart|flesh); divine indignation leaves "no soundness in my flesh" (Psa 38:3); zeal reaches the "inward parts" (Isa 63:15). By **type**, the cluster is **status-heavy** (the affects are named as states more than acts), the exception being **C3 (provoking), which is action-dominant** (69/85) because it names a *doing*.

**Interpretation.** M02 is not a uniform field of "emotions." It holds (i) genuine **inner affects** seated in the heart/soul and at times felt in the body, (ii) an **external relational event** (strife) that the affects *produce* but which is not itself an inner state, and (iii) a **settled resentful disposition** (bitterness). This tripartite shape governs how the cluster relates to the inner being: C1–C5 are constitutive of it; C6 is downstream of it; C7 is a chronic corruption of it. The status-heavy typing suggests Scripture treats anger more as a **condition one is in** than an act one does — except provocation, which is inherently an act done to another.

## F4 — The cluster is morally graded by bearer and direction *(guide: T0.2, T1.7, T3.8; valence)*

**Observation (valence, CC-confirmed current).** The moral framing differs systematically by characteristic:

| Characteristic | neutral | righteous | sinful | forbidden | commanded |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| C1 Human anger | 117 | 13 | 58 | 8 | 1 |
| C2 Divine wrath | 97 | 7 | 7* | 0 | 0 |
| C3 Provoking | 15 | 3 | 57 | 4 | 1 |
| C4 Jealousy/zeal | 28 | 24 | 16 | 5 | 0 |
| C5 Indignation | 29 | 4 | 7 | 2 | 0 |
| C6 Strife | 41 | 0 | 33 | 1 | 0 |
| C7 Bitterness | 0 | 0 | 3 | 1 | 0 |

*\*C2's 7 "sinful" tags are anomalous for divine wrath — see F10.*

**Interpretation.** The valence is patterned, not random. **Divine-borne** characteristics (C2) are overwhelmingly **neutral/righteous** — God's wrath is never sin. **Human-Godward** provocation (C3) is overwhelmingly **sinful** — provoking God is the gravest misuse. **Jealousy/zeal (C4)** is the most balanced — its single root carries righteous zeal (24), sinful envy (16), and neutral/legal jealousy (28), confirming its tri-valent character. **Strife (C6)** divides between neutral just-cause and sinful quarrelling. **Bitterness (C7)** is uniquely **wholly negative**. The cluster as a whole demonstrates that the *same* inner capacity (kindled response) is righteous, neutral, or sinful **depending on who bears it and toward whom it is directed** — the programme's clearest case that moral valence is set by relation, not by the affect itself.

## F5 — The cluster has an internal causal structure *(guide: T6)*

**Observation.** The characteristics are not a flat list; the co-occurrence and "produced-by" data show a chain:
- **Provocation (C3) → anger/jealousy (C1/C2/C4):** "provoked him to jealousy... to anger" (Deu 32:16,21); "provoked him... full of wrath" (Psa 78:58–59). C3 is action-typed and causally prior.
- **Anger/jealousy/hatred/pride → strife (C6):** "pressing anger produces strife" (Pro 30:33); "jealousy and strife" of the flesh (1Cor 3:3); "hatred stirs up strife" (Pro 10:12); the prideful dispute over greatness (Luk 22:24). C6 is faculty-NONE and causally downstream — the relational *output* of the affects.
- **Bitterness (C7)** sits as the **settled root** heading the anger list — "bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor" (Eph 4:31), "root of bitterness springs up" (Heb 12:15) — i.e. the chronic residue underlying the louder forms (inferential, from word-order and the root-image).
- **Indignation (C5)** is the **cooler/judicial sibling** of wrath, paired with it ("wrath, indignation," Psa 78:49) and shading toward grief at its sullen edge (1Ki 21:4) and its godly-grief edge (2Cor 7:11).

**Interpretation.** Read structurally, M02 maps a **lifecycle of kindled response**: a provocation arouses an affect (anger, jealousy, or the cooler indignation), the affect is borne rightly or sinfully, and — if unmastered — it issues outward as strife or settles inward as bitterness. The cluster is therefore not seven parallel topics but a **connected system** with provocation at the front, the affects at the centre, and strife/bitterness as the two terminal outputs (outward and inward). This is the natural spine for any Session B/C synthesis and for the eventual constitutional mapping.

## F6 — The relational interfaces are symmetrical and reversible *(guide: T4, T5)*

**Observation.** Every direction of the inner-being relational map is populated:
- **God → human:** wrath (Exo 22:24), jealousy *for* and *against* (Zec 1:14; Deu 29:20), indignation (Isa 10:5), covenant-lawsuit (Mic 6:2).
- **Human → God:** provoking to anger/jealousy (Deu 4:25; 32:21), zeal *for* God (Num 25:11; Psa 69:9), bearing God's indignation penitently (Mic 7:9), committing one's cause to God (Jer 11:20).
- **Human → human:** anger (Gen 4:5), envy (Gen 30:1), strife (Gen 13:7), provocation (1Sa 1:6), bitterness/cursing (Rom 3:14) — and the **positive** "stir up to love" (Heb 10:24).

A recurring feature is **reversibility/turnability**: divine wrath and indignation are repeatedly "turned away," "quenched," or "relented" on repentance/intercession (Jos 7:26; 2Ki 22:17; Jon 3:10; Exo 32:14); envy is corrected "in the sanctuary" (Psa 73:17); bitterness is "put away" and replaced by kindness (Eph 4:31–32).

**Interpretation.** The cluster portrays the inner being as **relationally embedded and morally plastic**: these affects arise *within* relationships (covenant, family, community) and *bear on* them, and most are presented as **changeable** — God's by His own will toward the penitent, the human's by repentance, reframing, or deliberate put-off. The affect is rarely treated as fixed fate; it is something turned, quenched, or put away. (Strife and bitterness, the terminal outputs, are correspondingly addressed by *prevention* — keep aloof, watch the root — Pro 20:3; Heb 12:15.)

## F7 — Anger in M02 is densely bound to sin and to the moral order *(guide: T3.8, T6, T7)*

**Observation.** Beyond the anger lemmas, the most frequent cluster-wide co-occurrence partners are **sin and evil vocabulary**: chaˈta "to sin" (15), chatˈtat "sin" (13), raˈah "evil/distress/harm" (12+9). Anger also co-occurs heavily with **the heart** (lev "co-seated," 10), **the face** (aph, the anger idiom, 14), **destruction** (shaˈchat "to ruin" 10; kaˈlah "to consume/expend" 10), and **hearing** (shaˈma "to hear," 13) and **serving** (aˈvad, 11 — idolatrous service). The faculty overlap **affect|moral_evaluation** is itself substantial (C1 31; C4 5; C3 5).

**Interpretation.** The cluster's affects are not free-floating feelings; they are **welded to the moral order**. Anger appears as a response *to* sin (provocation by idolatry/evil, aˈvad/chaˈta), as God's verdict *against* sin (wrath, chatˈtat), and as itself morally evaluated (the affect|moral_evaluation overlap). The "hearing" co-occurrence ties anger to the covenant word heard and disobeyed (and resonates with Registry 213 *listen*). The destruction co-occurrence (shaˈchat/kaˈlah) shows the affects' frequent terminal in judgment. This is the cluster's deepest theological observation: **M02's inner states are the affective dimension of the covenant moral order** — kindled by its breach, expressed as its enforcement, and themselves judged by it.

## F8 — Developmental and eschatological orientation *(guide: T5)*

**Observation.** Several characteristics carry an explicit forward horizon: divine wrath as the coming "wrath" (Rom 9:22; Rev 14:10; 16:19) and bounded "indignation" with an appointed end (Isa 10:25; Dan 11:36); jealousy/zeal as the "zeal of the LORD" that "will do this" (Isa 9:7; 37:32) and the consuming "fire of my jealousy" at the end (Zep 3:8); strife as awaiting the "day of recompense for the cause of Zion" (Isa 34:8). Within the sanctification arc, the cluster supplies both vices to put off (anger, bitterness, strife — Eph 4:31; Col 3:8) and a righteous indignation that is *fruit* of repentance (2Cor 7:11).

**Interpretation.** The cluster is **eschatologically loaded on its divine side and formatively addressed on its human side**: God's wrath/jealousy/indignation point to a consummation of judgment; the human affects are placed under a programme of formation (put off the sinful forms, exercise the righteous — zeal, godly indignation). The same word (e.g. indignation) is at once a divine end-time instrument and a sanctifying human fruit — a compact instance of the cluster's divine-human correspondence across time.

## F9 — Cross-cluster bridges *(guide: T6.4; flagged for Session D)*

**Observation / Flag.** The data shows defined seams to other clusters:
- **→ M03 Grief.** kaˈaˈs (H3708B) is glossed "vexation/grief" and is read "grief" in C1 (Psa 6:7; 31:9; Pro 17:25) — anger and grief share this term. And the OT "bitterness of soul" field (marah/mar — Ruth 1:20; 1Sa 1:10; Job 7:11), *outside* C7's NT-only focus set, is grief-borne bitterness. Two distinct anger↔grief bridges.
- **→ M06 Hate / M08 Pride.** Strife (C6) is produced by hatred ("hatred stirs up strife," Pro 10:12) and pride (the dispute over greatness, Luk 22:24).
- **→ M10c Defilement.** Bitterness "defiles many" (miainō, Heb 12:15) — a lexical link to the defilement field.
- **→ Love.** Jealousy stands beside love "strong as death" (Song 8:6); provocation can be "to love and good works" (Heb 10:24).

**Interpretation.** These are not noise but **genuine cluster boundaries** that Session D (cross-cluster synthesis) should carry: the anger↔grief seam (via kaˈaˈs and OT bitterness-of-soul) is the most substantial and may bear on C7's membership and on M03.

## F10 — Data-quality and method observations *(guide: T7.3.4; carried for researcher review)*

**Flags.**
1. **C1/C2 boundary leakage.** Because C1↔C2 are one vocabulary split only by divine_involvement (a read-resolved field), their edges leak: divine-subject verses (Exo 4:14; 2Ki 13:3) fell into C1, and C2 carries **7 occurrences tagged valence=sinful** — anomalous for God's wrath. These need verse-confirmation; the split rests on a read-resolved field and so carries a **circularity caution** (the boundary cannot be validated from the same read field without independent verse re-reading).
2. **object_type under-population (programme-wide).** Raw `object` is a mechanical hint; `object_type` is read-authoritative but incomplete in places (e.g. C2 object_type=God read as possessor-genitive, not target). Any object-based signature for these characteristics is therefore provisional.
3. **C6 procedural sense.** A material share of C6 is judicial-procedural ("lawsuit/case," Exo 23:2–3; Deu 17:8) that is **inner-being-peripheral** and should be filtered out at VC classification per GR-PROG-007; only the flesh-strife, cause-committing, and covenant-lawsuit strands engage the inner being.
4. **C7 scope.** C7 is NT-only pikria (4 verses); the OT marah/mar bitterness-of-soul field is excluded — membership review recommended (F9).
5. **tsur exclusion.** 32 tsur occurrences (besiege/bind, 0/32 anger) are mis-seeded; exclusion proposal drafted, pending CC patch on approval.
6. **`mode` field absent** from the M02 extract schema (0/703) — no morphological-mode data; sourced from `morph` only if required.
