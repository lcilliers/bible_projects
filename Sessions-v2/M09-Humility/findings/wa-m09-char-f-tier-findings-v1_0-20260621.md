## T0 — Divine Image  *(6/9)*

- **T0.1.2** — Creaturely faculty of the will, but **God-upheld** (Psa 51:12) — not borne by God as God.

- **T0.3.1** — [inferential] instantiates the creature's **free devotion** mirroring God's own freely-giving generosity.

## T1 — Definition  *(14/18)*
- **T1.1.1** — Names the **voluntary, glad yielding of the will** in devotion.
- **T1.1.2** — *na.div* (Heb: willing, noble, generous) — free, noble willingness of heart.
- **T1.1.3** — **Volitional** (freely-given) and **directional** (God-ward offering).
- **T1.2.1** — A **disposition/quality of the will**, expressed in the act of offering.
- **T1.2.2** — **Compound**: volition + generosity/gladness + devotion.
- **T1.3.1** — Opposite: a **grudging or coerced** heart; the unwilling spirit.
- **T1.3.2** — Resists compulsion and reluctance.
- **T1.3.3** — **Not** coerced compliance; it is the **willing interior** that obedience (D) and submission (E) may or may not have.
- **T1.4.1** — Possessive-modal ("of a willing/generous heart").
- **T1.4.2** — **Consistent** — heart/spirit willingness across the offering contexts.
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: **bringing the offering** (Exo 35:22 "brought… dedicating an offering").
- **T1.5.2** — **Consistent**: willingness → bringing/giving.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: a willing spirit **upholds/steadies** the person (Psa 51:12) — a stabilising inner state.
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the gift brought; sustained = the upheld willing spirit.
- **T1.7.1** — Right operation in a genuinely **consecrated** heart (2Ch 29:31 "you have consecrated yourselves… bring").
