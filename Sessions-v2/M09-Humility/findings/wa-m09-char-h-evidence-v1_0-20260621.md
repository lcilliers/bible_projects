## Definition
**Measured gentleness toward the failing, grounded in shared weakness** — the moderation of one's own feeling (*metrio-patheō*, "to feel in measure") so as to deal gently rather than harshly. A single occurrence; tied to M05 Gentleness per researcher direction.

## Inner-being signature
- **Kind:** action; **faculty = affect** — note this is *affect-moderation*.
- **Sole occurrence:** Hebrews 5:2 — the high priest "can deal gently with the ignorant and wayward, since he himself is beset with weakness". The gentleness is *enabled by* felt solidarity in weakness.
- **Valence:** righteous.

## Tie-in with M05 (per direction) — and a real nuance
- M05 carries **Gentleness (E)** as a full characteristic, rooted in **praus/prautēs**, and explicitly maps its boundary with M09: *"gentleness = manner toward others; humility = posture before God / concerning self"* (M05 synthesis, T6). M05 lists humility and meekness together at Colossians 3:12 — the textual hinge between the clusters.
- **Faculty divergence (finding):** M05 tags its gentleness (praus) as **cognition-only, never affect** ("mind-governed restraint"). M09's H term **metriopatheō is tagged affect** — literally the *moderation of feeling*. So H is gentleness on its **affective** side (measured feeling from shared weakness), where M05's praus-gentleness is on its **cognitive** side (mind-governed restraint). H therefore *complements* M05's gentleness on the faculty it leaves silent, rather than duplicating it.

## Cross-cluster links
- **M05 Gentleness (E):** primary kin; H is best read as the **affect-moderation face of gentleness**, with M05 holding the cognitive-restraint face.
- **M05 Compassion (B) / shared-weakness:** Hebrews 5:2's "beset with weakness" borders compassion-from-solidarity.
