## T0 — Divine Image  *(9/9)*
- **T0.1.1** — Lowliness is **predicated of Christ** ("I am gentle and lowly in heart", Mat 11:29) and God **gives grace to the humble** (Jam 4:6; 1Pe 5:5). God both **bears** it (in Christ) and **responds** to it with grace.
- **T0.1.2** — **Yes — uniquely borne by God-in-Christ** (Mat 11:29), the one M09 characteristic predicated of the incarnate Son as his own settled heart-state. So it images Christ directly (contrast A, never borne by God).
- **T0.2.1** — Purpose: it **receives grace** (Jam 4:6; 1Pe 5:5), **gives rest to the soul** (Mat 11:29), and **orders community** (Phil 2:3; Eph 4:2). The counterfeit (Col 2:23) "has an appearance of wisdom" but is "of no value against the flesh" — a purpose-failure.
- **T0.2.2** — **Both** — restored/commanded good (the put-on virtue) and, in its counterfeit, a fallen distortion (Col 2).
- **T0.2.3** — [inferential] grace-to-the-humble orients toward ongoing/eschatological grace.
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates **Christ-likeness of lowly-heartedness** (Mat 11:29).
- **T0.3.2** — **Shared** between God (Christ) and the person (unlike A) — Christ is lowly in heart; believers put on the same.
- **T0.3.3** — Presence = Christlike, grace-receiving image; the counterfeit/haughtiness (Col 2; Rom 12:16) = distorted image.
- **T0.4.1** — **Christological** (Mat 11:29 — "learn from me", the divine instance establishing the pattern); direction divine→human.

## T1 — Definition  *(18/18)*
- **T1.1.1** — Names a **borne trait of lowly-mindedness** toward self and others.
- **T1.1.2** — *tapeinos* (lowly/humble), *tapeinofrosunē* (lowliness-of-**mind**: *tapeinos*+*phrēn*), *tsa.nu.a* (modest/humble) — definitional: humble-mindedness.
- **T1.1.3** — **Constitutional** (heart/mind-seated) + **relational** (toward others) implication.
- **T1.2.1** — A **disposition/quality** (borne trait); *tapeinos* tagged **affect**.
- **T1.2.2** — **Compound** — *tapeinofrosunē* literally compounds lowliness + mind: low self-estimate + other-regard + affect.
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **pride / haughtiness** (Rom 12:16 "do not be haughty"; the "puffed-up… sensuous mind", Col 2:18).
- **T1.3.2** — Resists conceit, vainglory, selfish ambition (Phil 2:3).
- **T1.3.3** — **Not** the act of self-humbling (A) nor imposed low estate (C); and crucially **not** the counterfeit self-abasement/asceticism (Col 2) that mimics it.
- **T1.4.1** — Adjective (lowly), noun (humility), modal "with all humility" (Act 20:19; Eph 4:2), imperative "put on / clothe yourselves" (Col 3:12; 1Pe 5:5).
- **T1.4.2** — **Varies** — described trait (Mat 11:29), commanded virtue (Col 3:12), counterfeit (Col 2).
- **T1.4.3** — **Communicative** — commanded/addressed ("clothe yourselves… with humility"); operates in community conduct.
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: **grace received** (Jam 4:6; 1Pe 5:5 "God… gives grace to the humble").
- **T1.5.2** — **Consistent** grace-pairing (Jam 4:6 = 1Pe 5:5, both citing Pro 3:34).
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: **rest to the soul** (Mat 11:29) and **unity/forbearance** in the body (Eph 4:2–3).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = grace received; sustained = soul-rest and community concord.
- **T1.7.1** — Right operation in a heart that **counts others more significant** (Phil 2:3) and **associates with the lowly** (Rom 12:16).
- **T1.7.2** — Distorted by **self-made religion / asceticism** (Col 2:23) and haughtiness; the counterfeit is "puffed up… by his sensuous mind" (Col 2:18) — pride masquerading as humility. (Col 2:18 ties it to **"worship of angels"** — an angelic-object distortion; see T4.6.)
- **T1.7.3** — Present-but-not-holding = **the counterfeit**: the form (asceticism) without the substance ("of no value… against the flesh", Col 2:23).

## T2 — Constitutional Location  *(6/6)*
- **T2.1.1** — Seated in **heart, soul, mind**: "lowly in heart… rest for your souls" (Mat 11:29); "inward parts" (Col 3:12); *tapeinofrosunē* = mind (*phrēn*) by composition. The counterfeit relocates to **flesh/mind** (Col 2:18, 23).
- **T2.1.2** — Pattern: true lowliness is **heart-deep**; the counterfeit is **flesh-seated** ("self-made… severity to the body", Col 2:23). The seat itself distinguishes true from false humility.
- **T2.10.1** — [inferential] "put on / clothe" (Col 3:12; 1Pe 5:5) is a **donning** onto the person (clothing-image), not a constitutional movement across levels.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link in the **counterfeit** — "severity to the body" (Col 2:23, false asceticism); the true trait runs heart→conduct (expressive).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: **received / put on** (Col 3:12; 1Pe 5:5) and **learned from Christ** (Mat 11:29 "learn from me"); the counterfeit is **self-made** (Col 2:23).
- **T2.9.2** — Two origins by valence: true = received/learned from Christ; counterfeit = self-generated.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties  *(cognition, affect, volition, agency, moral-eval, conscientiousness, relational engaged; perception/memory/creativity/conscience none-or-inferential)*
- **T3.2 Cognition** — engaged — *tapeinofrosunē* is lowliness of **mind**; "count others more significant" (Phil 2:3) and "never be wise in your own sight" (Rom 12:16) are cognitive self-estimates; pattern: cognition engaged as **right self-reckoning**.
- **T3.4 Affect** — engaged (*tapeinos* = affect); "rest for your souls" (Mat 11:29) — a felt lowliness/soul-rest.
- **T3.6 Volition** — engaged — "put on / clothe yourselves with humility" is volitional **donning**; Phil 2:3 chosen other-regard.
- **T3.7 Agency** — engaged — humility shapes action toward others (serving "with all humility", Act 20:19).
- **T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — engaged — humility is a right moral self-assessment; the counterfeit is morally judged ("of no value", Col 2:23); pattern: moral-evaluation central (true vs false humility).
- **T3.10 Conscientiousness** — engaged — integrated humble conduct (serving, bearing with one another, Eph 4:2).
- **T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **strongly engaged** — humility is fundamentally **toward others** (Phil 2:3; Rom 12:16; Eph 4:2; 1Pe 5:5 "toward one another"); the **defining outward axis** of B.
- **T3.1 / T3.3 / T3.5 / T3.9** (perception, memory, creativity, conscience) — none/[inferential]; pattern: B is **cognitive-affective-relational**, not perceptual/memorial/creative.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological  *(19/20)*
- **T7.1.1** — *tapeinos* (low/humble), *tapeinofrosunē* (*tapeinos*+*phrēn* "mind"), *tsanua* (modest) — roots = **low + mind**.
- **T7.1.2** — Adjective + noun — **disposition-words** (borne quality).
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from socially-low → humble-minded (moral virtue).
- **T7.1.4** — The **disposition** member of the *tapein-* field (vs act A, condition C); *tapeinofrosunē* names the virtue specifically.
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite: **pride** (*huperēphania*, Jam 4:6 / 1Pe 5:5 "God opposes the proud"); haughtiness (Rom 12:16).
- **T7.1.6** — Person-type: *tapeinos / tsanua* "the humble (person)" (Pro 11:2; Jam 4:6).
- **T7.1.8** — OT (*tsanua*, modest) → NT — the NT **coins/revalues** lowliness from the pagan-negative *tapeinos* (servile) into a Christic moral excellence.
- **T7.1.9** — **tapeinofrosunē** — effectively a NT coinage/revaluation; the coinage shows the NT making lowliness a **virtue**.
- **T7.1.10** — Arc: socially-low/servile (pagan negative) → Christic humble-mindedness (NT virtue), with a counterfeit (asceticism).
- **T7.2.1** — Function: predicate adjective / object of "put on" — naming the trait borne.
- **T7.2.2** — Forms: Gospel (Mat 11:29), epistle paraenesis (Col/Eph/Phil/1Pe), wisdom (Pro 11:2), **polemic** (Col 2, against false humility).
- **T7.2.3** — Logic: "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble" (Jam 4:6; 1Pe 5:5); "count others more significant" (Phil 2:3).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: community paraenesis, christological (Mat 11:29), polemical (Col 2).
- **T7.2.5 / .6** — Anchor **Mat 11:29** — lowliness **as Christ's own settled heart-state**, the source of soul-rest, grounding the human trait in the incarnate person.
- **T7.3.1** *(science §1)* — positive/moral psychology of humility (Tangney; VIA; Davis other-/self-focused; pseudo-humility).
- **T7.3.2** — Illuminates: Davis's **other-focused vs self-focused** humility maps to B's two faces (Phil 2:3 / Rom 12:16); **dispositional** humility (stable orientation) maps to B (vs situational A/C); Tangney's "secure, not destabilised by criticism" coheres with the restful lowly heart.
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: science frames humility horizontally (self-appraisal/other-focus); B grounds it **christologically** (put on Christ's lowliness; receive grace).
- **T7.3.4** — **Strong hit:** the science's named **pseudo-humility** (self-focused self-abasement) is **directly instantiated** in B's counterfeit *tapeinofrosunē* (Col 2:18, 23) — the cluster's clearest verse-instance of the framework's surfaced category.
- **T7.1.7** — [SILENT–data] no term for seeking the trait from another.
