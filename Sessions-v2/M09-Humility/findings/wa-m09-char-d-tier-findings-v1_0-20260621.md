## T0 — Divine Image  *(9/9)*
- **T0.1.1** — Obedience runs **to God/Christ/the gospel/the faith/the truth** (1Pe 1:2 "obedience to Jesus Christ"; Act 6:7 "obedient to the faith"; Rom 1:5; 16:26 "obedience of faith"; 2Th 1:8 "obey the gospel"; Heb 5:9 "obey him"); and **Christ himself obeys** (Heb 5:8 "learned obedience"; Rom 5:19 "by the one man's obedience"). God/Christ is both **object** and (in Christ) **bearer**.
- **T0.1.2** — **Yes — borne by Christ** (Heb 5:8; Rom 5:19), whose obedience is **salvific** ("the many will be made righteous"). Obedience images the **Son's obedience to the Father**.
- **T0.2.1** — Purpose: it **brings salvation/righteousness** (Heb 5:9 "source of eternal salvation to all who obey"; Rom 5:19; 1Pe 1:22 "purified your souls by obedience"; Phil 2:12) and **orders the household** (Eph 6:1, 5; Col 3:20, 22).
- **T0.2.2** — **Both** — created design (right ordering under God/legitimate authority) and the **means of redemption** (Christ's obedience); its **mis-aim** (Rom 6:12, 16 obey sin) is the fallen distortion.
- **T0.2.3** — **Yes** — oriented to **eternal salvation** (Heb 5:9) and glory.
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the **Son's filial obedience** in the believer (Rom 5:19; Heb 5:8).
- **T0.3.2** — **Shared** — Christ obeys (Heb 5:8); believers obey; the same characteristic in God-incarnate and creature.
- **T0.3.3** — Presence = the obedient/faith-filled image; absence ("do not obey the gospel", Rom 10:16; 2Th 1:8) = the rebellious image **under judgment**.
- **T0.4.1** — **Christological** (Rom 5:19; Heb 5:8 — the divine instance establishing the pattern) and **covenantal** (the obedience of faith, Rom 1:5).

## T1 — Definition  *(18/18)*
- **T1.1.1** — Names the **will's heeding/compliance with authority** — the will that "hears-under" (*hupo-akouō*).
- **T1.1.2** — *hupakouō* (verb, obey/hearken), *hupakoē* (noun, obedience), *yiqqehah* (Heb). Roots: **hearing-under** (Grk *akouō*); Heb *yaqah* "to heed."
- **T1.1.3** — Directional (under authority), **perceptual-root** (hearing), volitional-relational implication.
- **T1.2.1** — An **act** (*hupakouō*) and a **status** (*hupakoē*).
- **T1.2.2** — **Compound** — perception (heeding) + volition (compliance) + relation (to authority); "from-the-heart" obedience adds affect/sincerity (Rom 6:17; Eph 6:5).
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **disobedience / rebellion** (2Th 1:8; Rom 10:16; *disobedience*, 2Cor 10:6).
- **T1.3.2** — Resists rebellion, insubordination, autonomy-from-God.
- **T1.3.3** — **Not** the submission-posture (E) — it is the **active complying**; meets self-humbling (A) at the cross (Phil 2:8).
- **T1.4.1** — Verb (imperative — children/slaves obey; indicative — "obeyed"; participle) and noun (a state, "your obedience").
- **T1.4.2** — **Varies by direction** — God-ward (gospel/faith/Christ) vs human-authority-ward (parents/masters/Abraham); and by **valence** (right obedience vs Rom 6:12 forbidden obey-sin).
- **T1.4.3** — **Commanded/addressed** (Eph 6:1, 5; Col 3:20, 22) and **"obey the gospel"** (heeding a proclamation — speech-based).
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: **action follows** ("By faith Abraham obeyed… and went out", Heb 11:8); obedience "received… with fear and trembling" (2Cor 7:15).
- **T1.5.2** — **Consistent** — obedience issues immediately in compliant action.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: **righteousness / salvation / purified souls** (Rom 5:19; Heb 5:9; 1Pe 1:22) and a settled obedient character ("your obedience known to all", Rom 16:19).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the compliant act; sustained = the formed obedient character and its fruit.
- **T1.7.1** — Right operation **from the heart / in sincerity** (Rom 6:17 "obedient from the heart"; Eph 6:5; Col 3:22 "not… eye-service") and **with reverent fear** (2Cor 7:15; Eph 6:5).
- **T1.7.2** — Distorted when **mis-aimed at sin** (Rom 6:12 "obey its passions"; Rom 6:16 "slaves of the one whom you obey") or **withheld** (disobedience to the gospel).
- **T1.7.3** — Present-but-not-holding = **eye-service** (Col 3:22) — obedience in form without heart.

## T2 — Constitutional Location  *(6/6)*
- **T2.1.1** — **Heart and soul**: "obedient from the heart" (Rom 6:17); "sincerity of heart" (Eph 6:5; Col 3:22); "purified your souls by your obedience" (1Pe 1:22); "inward parts" (2Cor 7:15). Faculty at the operative level uniformly **perception** (hearing/heeding).
- **T2.1.2** — Pattern: true obedience is **heart-and-soul-seated** ("from the heart"), distinguishing it from outward **eye-service**; obedience that **purifies the soul** (1Pe 1:22) reaches the deepest level.
- **T2.10.1** — [inferential] obedience responds to an **external authority/proclamation** (the word obeyed comes onto the person) while **originating in the heart** — a mixed in-and-onto movement.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link: obedience is **enacted bodily** (Heb 11:8 "went out"; servants' service); heart→body **expressive**; Eph 6:5 "obey… according to the flesh" (the bodily sphere of service).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: **response to authority/proclamation** (obey the gospel/faith — from hearing) and **from the heart** (Rom 6:17); Christ's obedience **learned through suffering** (Heb 5:8).
- **T2.9.2** — Origin **multiple** — response to external authority + internal heart-source; for Christ, learned/formed.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties  *(D is the most faculty-rich char: perception/cognition/affect/volition/agency/moral-eval/conscientiousness/relational engaged)*
- **T3.1 Perception** — **the defining faculty**: obedience IS "hearing-under" (*hupakouō* from *akouō*); "obey the gospel" = heeding the proclamation (2Th 1:8); "faith comes from hearing" (Rom 10:16–17). Pattern: perception is **constitutive** (obedience's root is hearing).
- **T3.2 Cognition** — engaged — "take every thought captive to obey Christ" (2Cor 10:5 — the mind brought to obedience).
- **T3.4 Affect** — engaged — "fear and trembling" (2Cor 7:15; Eph 6:5); "from the heart" (sincerity/affection).
- **T3.6 Volition** — **strongly engaged** — obedience is the will's compliance and **allegiance** ("slaves of the one whom you obey", Rom 6:16).
- **T3.7 Agency** — **strongly engaged** — obedience issues in action (Heb 11:8 went out; servants serve).
- **T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — engaged — obedience measured against the standard obeyed; "obedience leading to righteousness" (Rom 6:16); the forbidden obey-sin (Rom 6:12) is judged. Pattern: moral-evaluation determines obedience's worth **by its object**.
- **T3.10 Conscientiousness** — **paradigmatically engaged** — obedience **is** the integrated moral-awareness + volition + action (the catalogue's definition); "obedient from the heart" issuing in conduct (Rom 6:17).
- **T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **strongly engaged** — obedience is **constituted in relation to an authority** (God, parents, masters, Christ); always to-someone.
- **T3.9 Conscience** — [inferential] "fear and trembling" (2Cor 7:15) as conscientious response.
- **T3.3 / T3.5** (memory, creativity) — none; pattern not memorial/creative.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces  *(17/18 — D engages every human interface)*
- **T4.1.1 / .2 / .3** — Christ "became the source of eternal salvation to **all who obey**" (Heb 5:9) — God **responds to obedience with salvation**; **conditional** ("to all who obey"); shows God **saves the obedient**.
- **T4.2.1 / .2 / .3** — **Primary axis**: obedience **to God/Christ/gospel/faith** (1Pe 1:2; Act 6:7; Rom 1:5; Heb 5:9; 2Cor 10:5); requires the **heeded submission of will** in reverent fear; shows the person **ordered under God's authority in faith**.
- **T4.3.1 / .2 / .3** — Rendered to **human authority** — children→parents (Eph 6:1; Col 3:20), slaves→masters (Eph 6:5; Col 3:22), Sarah→Abraham (1Pe 3:6), Philemon→Paul (Phm 21); inner condition = **sincerity/heart, not eye-service**; must heed **from the heart**.
- **T4.4.1 / .2** — Obedience **elicited/received** by an authority — "your obedience… as you received him" (2Cor 7:15); "when your obedience is complete" (2Cor 10:6); "confident of your obedience" (Phm 21); uptake requires recognition of legitimate authority.
- **T4.5.1 / .2 / .3** — Operates within **ordered relations** (household) **and across to all nations** — the obedience of faith "among all the nations" (Rom 1:5; 16:26 — universal scope).
- **T4.6.1 / .2** — [inferential] "take every thought captive to obey Christ" sits within **spiritual warfare** (2Cor 10:3–5, destroying strongholds) — obedience is the **goal of the warfare** against false arguments; disobedience is the adversarial counter.
- **T4.6.3** — [SILENT–data] no angelic mediation of obedience shown.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental  *(9/9 — D heavily salvific/formative)*
- **T5.1.1** — **Yes** — obedience **purifies souls** (1Pe 1:22), **makes righteous** (Rom 5:19), **works salvation** (Phil 2:12; Heb 5:9) — changes condition and orientation.
- **T5.1.2** — [inferential] the fruit (righteousness/salvation) tends toward permanence.
- **T5.2.1** — **Yes** — hearing → obeying → righteousness/salvation/purified soul (Rom 10:16–17; 1Pe 1:22).
- **T5.3.1 / .2** — Mechanism: **Christ "learned obedience through what he suffered"** (Heb 5:8 — formation through suffering); the believer's obedience formed by **hearing/heeding** — differing across contexts.
- **T5.4.1 / .2** — **Yes** — Heb 5:8: suffering is the **school of obedience**; suffering **forms/proves** it.
- **T5.5.1** — **Central** — obedience **purifies the soul** (1Pe 1:22) and is the **working-out of salvation** (Phil 2:12) — a core sanctification engine.
- **T5.6.1** — **Yes** — obedience leads to **eternal salvation** (Heb 5:9); the obedient are saved/glorified.

## T6 — Structural Relationships  *(13/13)*
- **T6.1.1 / .2** — Co-occurs with **faith** ("obedience of faith", Rom 1:5; 16:26; Act 6:7 → M31), **fear/trembling** (2Cor 7:15; Eph 6:5; Phil 2:12 → M01), **self-humbling** (Phil 2:8 → A), **suffering** (Heb 5:8), **slavery/service** (Rom 6:16; *doulos* → M36). Place: the **junction of faith and action**.
- **T6.2.1 / .2** — Obedience **follows hearing/faith** and **precedes righteousness/salvation**; **accompanies** fear and humility (Phil 2:8, 12) — causal/developmental.
- **T6.3.1 / .2 / .3** — **Produces** righteousness/salvation/purified soul; **produced by** faith/hearing and (in Christ) by suffering; **constituent of faith** (the "obedience of faith") and meets self-humbling at the cross.
- **T6.4.1 / .2** — *hupakoē/hupakouō* share the **akouō (hear) root** with hearing/listening (M-listen, Registry 213) and the **hupo- (under)** prefix with submission (*hupotassō*, E/M23).
- **T6.5.1 / .2 / .3** — Nearest = **E submission** — obedience = **active complying (act)**, submission = **ordered placing-under (posture)**; also near **A** (Phil 2:8) — distinguished by **kind** (heeding-act vs lowering-act).

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological  *(20/20)*
- **T7.1.1** — *hupakouō/hupakoē* (*hupo* "under" + *akouō* "hear" = **hear-under, heed**); *yiqqehah* (Heb). Root = **hearing-under**.
- **T7.1.2** — Verb (act) + noun (state) — obedience as both **act and abiding state**.
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: heeding/hearkening → compliance → the settled state → **"the obedience of faith"** (faith itself as obedience).
- **T7.1.4** — Distinguishes **act** (verb) from **state** (noun); **God-ward** (obedience of faith) from **human-ward** (household).
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite: **disobedience/unbelief** (*apeitheō / parakoē* — "do not obey the gospel", 2Th 1:8; Rom 10:16).
- **T7.1.6** — Person-type: "**obedient children**" (1Pe 1:14); *doulos* "slave… of obedience" (Rom 6:16).
- **T7.1.7** — [SILENT–data] no term for *seeking obedience as a gift* — one commands it.
- **T7.1.8** — OT (*yiqqehah*, rare — peoples' obedience to the ruler, Gen 49:10) → NT (*hupakoē*, richly developed) — major **development**: obedience becomes the **faith-response to the gospel** and the **christological act** that justifies.
- **T7.1.9** — **"obedience of faith"** (*hupakoē pisteōs*, Rom 1:5; 16:26) — a distinctively Pauline construction fusing obedience and faith.
- **T7.1.10** — Arc: heeding → compliance with authority → the obedience of faith → **Christ's salvific obedience**.
- **T7.2.1** — Function: main verb/noun naming the response demanded or rendered.
- **T7.2.2** — Forms: narrative (Gen 49:10; Heb 11:8), epistle paraenesis (household codes; Rom; 1Pe), wisdom (Pro 30:17), christological exposition (Heb 5; Rom 5:19).
- **T7.2.3** — Logic: "obey the gospel or face judgment" (2Th 1:8); "by the one man's obedience the many will be made righteous" (Rom 5:19); "obedient from the heart" → freed for righteousness (Rom 6:17).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: soteriological/covenantal (obedience of faith), household/ecclesial (codes), christological (Heb 5; Rom 5).
- **T7.2.5 / .6** — Anchor **Rom 5:19** — obedience's **christological-salvific apex** ("by the one man's obedience the many will be made righteous"); note Rom 6:17 (heart-interior) and Heb 5:8 (learned).
- **T7.3.1** *(science §3)* — social/organisational psychology of obedience (Milgram; **compliant vs internalised**; legitimate authority).
- **T7.3.2** — Illuminates: the **compliant-vs-internalised** distinction maps precisely onto D's **eye-service vs from-the-heart** obedience (Col 3:22 vs Rom 6:17); Milgram's legitimate-authority illuminates the household codes.
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: Milgram's dominant message is the **danger** of obedience; D makes the **identity/legitimacy of the one obeyed decisive** — obedience to God/Christ is salvific, and obedience to **sin** is the real danger (Rom 6:12, 16). The biblical "obey God rather than sin" inverts Milgram's caution (extract T7.3.3).
- **T7.3.4** — **Direct hit on the extract's surfaced gap (T7.3.4):** D's **"from the heart" / "sincerity" / "not eye-service"** verses (Rom 6:17; Eph 6:5; Col 3:22) explicitly mark **internalised** (inner-agreement) obedience against hollow compliance — the biblical pass **locates and confirms** the distinction the science predicted.
