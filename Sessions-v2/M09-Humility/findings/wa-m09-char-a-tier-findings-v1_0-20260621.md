## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design  *(coverage 9/9)*
- **T0.1.1** — God is the **object/terminus and responsive agent** of the act: it is done "before the Lord / before me / before God / with your God" (1Ki 21:29; 2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 33:12 divine-role=object; Mic 6:8), and God responds — relenting wrath or exalting (Jam 4:10 God=agent "he will exalt"; 1Pe 5:6).
- **T0.1.2** — Never borne by God as God; it is the **creature's act toward God** — with the unique exception of the **incarnate Son** (Phil 2:8 "being found in human form he humbled himself"). So it belongs to right creaturely relation, yet is christologically grounded.
- **T0.2.1** — Stated effects: averts/defers wrath (2Ch 12:7, 12; 32:26), brings forgiveness and healing (2Ch 7:14), and precedes exaltation (Mat 23:12; 1Pe 5:6). Role: it turns the person from sin to mercy.
- **T0.2.2** — **Both.** Its necessity answers the fallen condition (it confronts "the pride of his heart", 2Ch 32:26) while it images the right created posture of creature before Creator.
- **T0.2.3** — **Yes** — oriented to future exaltation: "whoever humbles himself will be exalted" (Mat 23:12; Luk 14:11; 18:14); "at the proper time he may exalt you" (1Pe 5:6).
- **T0.3.1** — Via Phil 2:8 it instantiates the **Christ-pattern of self-emptying condescension**; otherwise it instantiates the creature's acknowledgement of God's exclusive height.
- **T0.3.2** — **Exclusively creaturely** (the creature lowering before God), patterned christologically in the incarnate Son — not a mirrored divine attribute.
- **T0.3.3** — Absence marks the hardened/proud image (2Ch 36:12 Zedekiah "did not humble himself"; 33:23 Amon vs Manasseh); presence marks the turning/restored image.
- **T0.4.1** — Typological in two directions: **christological** (Phil 2:8 — the divine instance establishing the pattern) and **covenantal** (2Ch 7:14 — the human movement toward God under covenant promise).

## T1 — Definition  *(coverage 17/18; 1 silent)*
- **T1.1.1** — Named "self-humbling before God": a **reflexive act** (humble *oneself*) with a **God-ward terminus**.
- **T1.1.2** — *ka.na* (Heb: bow/subdue/be low), *tapeinoō* (Grk: make low), *tsa.na* (Heb: be modest/walk humbly) — a verb of lowering used reflexively for voluntary self-abasement before God.
- **T1.1.3** — Carries a **directional** (downward, God-ward), **relational** (before God), and **constitutional** (heart-seated) implication.
- **T1.2.1** — **An act** (volitional action) — action-type throughout (the reflexive/chosen lowering).
- **T1.2.2** — **Compound**: volition (choosing to lower) + affect/contrition (penitence, weeping) + relational orientation (before God), often bundled with prayer/seeking (2Ch 7:14 "humble… pray and seek").
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **pride / self-exaltation** ("whoever exalts himself", Mat 23:12; "pride of his heart", 2Ch 32:26).
- **T1.3.2** — Resists self-exaltation, presumption, and hardness of heart.
- **T1.3.3** — It is **not** an imposed lowering of estate (that is C) nor the settled trait (that is B); it ends where the lowering ceases to be chosen.
- **T1.4.1** — Modes: reflexive verb; **imperative** ("Humble yourselves", Jam 4:10; 1Pe 5:6); narrative past (the kings "humbled themselves"); **gnomic present** (Gospel maxim).
- **T1.4.2** — **Yes** — commanded (NT exhortation) vs narrated act (OT kings) vs general maxim (Gospels).
- **T1.4.3** — **Yes** — commanded and addressed ("you humbled yourself", 2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 34:27), accompanied by confession ("The Lord is righteous", 2Ch 12:6).
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate sequel: divine relief (wrath turns, 2Ch 12:7, 12) or exaltation (Jam 4:10; 1Pe 5:6); inner-side, penitence with torn clothes and weeping (2Ch 34:27).
- **T1.5.2** — **Varied by corpus** (Chronicles: wrath-relief; Gospels/Epistles: exaltation) but **consistent** in that a divine response always follows.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: a **restored covenant standing** (forgiveness, healing, 2Ch 7:14) and a settled lowliness that receives grace (→ B).
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = the relief/exaltation; sustained = the restored standing and abiding lowliness.
- **T1.7.1** — Right operation under a **penitent/tender heart** (2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 34:27), acknowledged guilt (2Ch 12:6), and distress driving to God (2Ch 33:12).
- **T1.7.2** — Blocked by **pride of heart** (2Ch 32:26) and **hardness** (Zedekiah, 2Ch 36:12, though God's word came through Jeremiah). No adversarial-spirit interference evidenced.
- **T1.7.3** — [SILENT–data] No verse shows a *hollow* self-humbling present-but-not-taking-hold; the negative cases are outright refusal, not failed uptake.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries  *(coverage 6/6)*
- **T2.1.1** — Located in the **heart**: "uncircumcised heart" (Lev 26:41), "penitent/tender heart" (2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 34:27), "pride of his heart" (2Ch 32:26). No spirit/soul/mind named.
- **T2.1.2** — The pattern (**heart only**) indicates self-humbling is a **heart-act** — the volitional-affective centre — not a cognitive or spirit-level event.
- **T2.10.1** — **None** — no movement across constitutional levels; the act originates within and bends the person's own heart.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link is **inner→body, expressive**: contrition shown bodily (torn clothes, weeping, 2Ch 34:27).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: **generated within the person** (one humbles oneself), prompted by hearing God's word (2Ki 22:19 "when you heard").
- **T2.9.2** — **Single** origin (within-person), consistently prompted by encounter with God's word or threat.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties  *(coverage 33/33 — engaged faculties answered; non-engaged faculties answered at the pattern level)*
- **T3.1 Perception** — .1 engaged at the **trigger**: humbling follows "when you heard" God's word (2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 34:27); .2 perception prompts the act; .3 pattern — perception is the *antecedent* of self-humbling, not its content.
- **T3.2 Cognition** — .1 engaged as **acknowledgement** ("The Lord is righteous", 2Ch 12:6 — recognition of God's justice and one's guilt); .2 reorders self-assessment; .3 cognition present as confession/judgement of self.
- **T3.3 Memory** — .1 none; .2 no effect; .3 [SILENT–data on .1/.2] memory **never engaged** — indicates self-humbling is not a memorial act.
- **T3.4 Affect** — .1 engaged: penitence, tenderness, weeping, distress (2Ki 22:19; 2Ch 34:27; 33:12); .2 the act is emotionally loaded contrition; .3 affect is **integral** to the act.
- **T3.5 Creativity** — .1 none; .2 no effect; .3 creativity **never engaged** — the act originates nothing new; it bends an existing will.
- **T3.6 Volition** — .1 strongly engaged — the act is reflexive/chosen ("Humble yourselves"); .2 the will bends itself against pride; .3 self-humbling is **paradigmatically volitional** (its defining faculty).
- **T3.7 Agency** — .1 engaged — the person initiates the act; .2 agency exercised as self-lowering; .3 it is an **agentive** act (something one does, not undergoes — the C-contrast).
- **T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — .1 engaged — self measured against God's standard ("the Lord is righteous"; pride judged, 2Ch 32:26); .2 reorders moral self-estimate; .3 moral evaluation is **constitutive** (the act is a right judgement of self before God).
- **T3.9 Conscience** — .1 engaged — conviction/penitence (penitent heart, acknowledged guilt); .2 conscience drives the turning; .3 conscience is **active** as the witness of sin that precipitates the act.
- **T3.10 Conscientiousness** — .1 engaged — integrates moral awareness + volition + action ("turn from their wicked ways", 2Ch 7:14); .2 issues in changed conduct; .3 the act is an **integrated** moral-volitional-active turning.
- **T3.11 Relational Capacity** — .1 engaged — constituted **before God** (a relational bending); .2 reorders the God-relation toward dependence; .3 relational capacity is **the very frame** of the act (it exists only in relation to God).

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension  *(coverage 9/9)*
- **T5.1.1** — **Both** — changes the person's **condition** (spared/forgiven) and **orientation** (pride → lowliness).
- **T5.1.2** — **Reversible** as a returnable posture (kings humble across reigns); the christological instance (Phil 2:8) is once-for-all.
- **T5.2.1** — **Yes** — hearing God's word → conviction/penitence → self-humbling → divine relief/exaltation.
- **T5.3.1** — Mechanism: **encounter** (with God's word/threat) and **discipline/affliction** ("in his distress… humbled himself greatly", 2Ch 33:12).
- **T5.3.2** — **Differs**: word-encounter (Josiah, Hezekiah) vs distress-in-captivity (Manasseh).
- **T5.4.1** — **Yes** — affliction is a primary **occasion/producer** of the act (Manasseh; the kings under threat).
- **T5.4.2** — Suffering **elicits and deepens** the humbling.
- **T5.5.1** — **Participates** — the repeated covenant call (2Ch 7:14) is formative discipline shaping the people toward grace-receiving lowliness.
- **T5.6.1** — **Yes** — oriented to eschatological **exaltation/reversal** ("will be exalted"; "at the proper time").

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics  *(coverage 13/13)*
- **T6.1.1** — Co-occurs with repentance/turning (*shuv*, 2Ch 7:14), prayer/seeking (*atar/darash*), weeping (*bakhah*), sin/guilt (*chattat/maal/avon*), and **obedience** (Phil 2:8).
- **T6.1.2** — Its place: the **penitential hinge** of the inner-being landscape — where sin meets mercy.
- **T6.2.1** — **Precedes** divine mercy/exaltation; **accompanies** repentance.
- **T6.2.2** — Relationship is **causal/responsive** (the human act conditions the divine response).
- **T6.3.1** — Produces the **ground of forgiveness/exaltation**.
- **T6.3.2** — Produced by **conviction and affliction**.
- **T6.3.3** — A **constituent of repentance**; meets **obedience (D)** at the cross (Phil 2:8 "humbled himself by becoming obedient").
- **T6.4.1** — Shares *ka.na* and *tapeinoō* with **C** (being-brought-low) and the *tapein-* root with **B**.
- **T6.4.2** — **Yes** — the *tapein-* root spans A/B/C; *ka.na* spans A/C (one lexical field, differentiated by voice/agency).
- **T6.4.3** — Shows act (A), trait (B), and condition (C) are **one semantic field** read apart by voice and agency.
- **T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour **C**; distinguished by **agency/voice** (chosen/reflexive vs imposed/passive).
- **T6.5.2** — Boundary: the same verb is A when the subject lowers *himself* before God, C when he *is lowered* by God/circumstance/enemy.
- **T6.5.3** — Distinction is of **kind and direction**, not degree.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation  *(coverage 18/20; 2 none-recorded)*
- **T7.1.1** — *ka.na*, *tapeinoō*, *tsa.na* — root meanings of **lowering / bringing-down**.
- **T7.1.2** — Grammatical range = **verb** (reflexive/Niphal/Hiphil/imperative/participle) — operates as an act (done, commanded, narrated).
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from **subjugation** ("be subdued") → **voluntary self-abasement** → **repentant bending before God**.
- **T7.1.4** — **Yes** — voice distinguishes **act** (A, reflexive) from **condition** (C, passive).
- **T7.1.5** — **Yes** — opposite/absence terms: pride/self-exaltation (*gavah* "height"; *hupsoō*; *huperēphanos*).
- **T7.1.6** — **Yes** — person-type term *tsanua / tapeinos* "the humble (person)".
- **T7.1.7** — **None** — no term for *seeking this act from another*; the seeking-terms (*darash/atar*) seek God, not the characteristic.
- **T7.1.8** — *ka.na* (OT: king-before-God, wrath-relief) → *tapeinoō* (NT: universal maxim + christological) — **continuity of the lowering act, developed from national-covenant to universal/christological**.
- **T7.1.9** — **None** newly coined for the *act* (the NT coinage *tapeinofrosunē* names the trait, → B).
- **T7.1.10** — Full arc: **subjugation → voluntary repentant self-abasement → christological self-emptying**.
- **T7.2.1** — Function: the **main verb** of its clause — "X humbled himself before the Lord."
- **T7.2.2** — Forms: **OT narrative** (dominant, Chronicles/Kings), wisdom/prophecy (Mic 6:8), Gospel maxim, epistle exhortation; narrative requires reading within the reign-evaluation, the maxim as principle.
- **T7.2.3** — Logic: **conditional-covenantal** ("if… humble… then I will hear") and **reversal** ("whoever humbles… will be exalted").
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: **covenantal** (Chronicles' retribution theology), **penitential/liturgical** (prayer, torn clothes), **eschatological** (the reversal).
- **T7.2.5** — Primary anchor: **2Ch 7:14** (fullest covenant sequence); christological apex **Phil 2:8**.
- **T7.2.6** — 2Ch 7:14 uniquely shows self-humbling as the **hinge of covenant restoration** (humble → pray → seek → turn → God hears/forgives/heals).
- **T7.3.1** *(science, per M09 extract §1)* — Lens: **positive/moral psychology of humility** (Tangney; VIA; Davis other-/self-focused; contriteness as acute humility).
- **T7.3.2** — Illuminates: the **situational-vs-dispositional** distinction maps A (act) to humbling experiences (failure/affliction) generating humility — illuminating the affliction-trigger (Manasseh "in distress"). Tangney's accurate self-appraisal illuminates the relinquished "pride of heart".
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: science frames humility as **accurate self-appraisal/other-focus**; A is **God-ward repentant self-abasement before a holy God** — a vertical, theological referent the self-appraisal frame does not reach (the extract's own T7.3.3 register-divergence point).
- **T7.3.4** — The framework's **pseudo-humility** (self-focused self-abasement) surfaces a category A's verses **do not** instantiate (its negatives are outright refusal); it appears instead as *false humility/asceticism* in B (Col 2:18, 23). Worth the cross-note; not a gap in A.
