## Definition
**The volitional self-giving of a "willing/generous heart (or spirit)"** to God's service and offering — the freewill, glad yielding of one's will and goods. Handled in M09 *as far as it engages the inner being* (researcher direction), with cross-cluster links recorded.

## Inner-being signature (its distinctness from the rest of M09)
- **Kind:** status/quality; **faculty = volition** — the only volition-led characteristic in the cluster (the others are action/affect/perception).
- **Seat:** **heart** (Exodus 35:5 "generous heart"; 35:22 "willing heart"; 2 Chronicles 29:31) and **spirit** (Psalm 51:12 "a willing spirit").
- **Direction:** God-ward — the offering/contribution to the Lord.
- **Valence:** uniformly **righteous**.
- **Affective pole:** *positive* (generous, glad) — the opposite affective pole from C "downcast". This is why F sits awkwardly under "Humility/Submission".

## How F touches the inner being (its M09-relevant aspect)
F is a humility/submission term only at one point: it is the **inner faculty by which the will is freely yielded** — submission *from the inside*, given rather than extracted. Exodus 35 (the willing heart bringing the offering) and Psalm 51:12 (the willing spirit upholding the penitent) show the inner posture of glad, voluntary devotion that *underlies* obedience and submission without being lowliness itself.

## Cross-cluster links (inferential — GR-PROG-009)
- **M36 (service/ministry):** the offering/contribution + freewill-service context (1 Chronicles 28:21 "every willing man… for the service of the house of God"); avodah "service" co-occurs.
- **M05 (kindness/generosity):** the "generous heart" sense (Exodus 35:5) borders generosity/kindness.
- **M04 (joy/gladness):** willing, glad giving borders glad-heartedness (inferential).
- **D/E within M09:** F is the *interior willingness* that makes obedience/submission freely given rather than coerced.
