## T0 — Divine Image  *(5/9)*
- **T0.1.1** — God is the one **glorified by** the submission "from your confession of the gospel of Christ" (2Cor 9:13); the pastoral cases (1Ti 2:11; 3:4) are human-ward order, not God-predicated.
- **T0.1.2** — **Never borne by God** in-set; creaturely throughout (Christ's subjection sits under *hupotassō*, M23, not here).
- **T0.2.1** — Purpose: it **glorifies God** (2Cor 9:13), **orders the household** (1Ti 3:4), and is the **posture of learning** (1Ti 2:11 "learn quietly with all submissiveness").
- **T0.2.2** — **Both** — ordered relations (created design) and right response; Gal 2:5 shows it can be wrongly *demanded* (fallen distortion).
- **T0.3.2** — **Creaturely** (the creature ordering itself under authority).

## T1 — Definition  *(13/18)*
- **T1.1.1** — Names the **ordering of self under authority** — a posture of yieldedness.
- **T1.1.2** — *hupotagē* (Grk: a placing/arranging-under) — a noun of subjection.
- **T1.1.3** — **Relational/subordinative** implication ("under").
- **T1.2.1** — A **status/posture** (borne), not an act.
- **T1.2.2** — Largely **simple**, presupposing recognition of the authority (light cognitive constituent).
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **self-assertion / insubordination / rebellion**.
- **T1.3.2** — Resists defiance and self-will.
- **T1.3.3** — **Not** active compliance (that is obedience, D); it is the *placing-under* itself.
- **T1.4.1** — Noun (2Cor 9:13); modal "with all submissiveness" (1Ti 2:11); adjective "submissive" (1Ti 3:4).
- **T1.4.2** — **Varies**: gospel-confessing (2Cor 9:13) vs household-ordered (1Ti) vs **refused** (Gal 2:5).
- **T1.4.3** — **Speech/learning mode**: 1Ti 2:11 submission is the posture of **learning under teaching**.
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: the **settled ordered posture** of the relationship/household.
- **T1.7.1** — Right under **legitimate, God-honouring authority** (2Cor 9:13).
- **T1.7.2** — Rightly **withheld** where it would betray the gospel: "we did not yield in submission… that the truth of the gospel might be preserved" (Gal 2:5).
- **T1.5.1 / T1.5.2 / T1.6.3 / T1.7.3** — [SILENT–data] no immediate inner response, and immediate-vs-sustained not distinguished, in-set.

## T7 — Evidential/Methodological  *(13/20)*
- **T7.1.1** — *hupotagē*, root *hupo-tassō* "to arrange under."
- **T7.1.2** — Noun (+ cognate adjective/adverb) — a **borne posture**.
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: subjection / submissiveness / the state of being ordered under.
- **T7.1.4** — Distinguishes **posture** (noun, here) from **act** (the verb, M23).
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite: insubordination/rebellion (no in-set term; Gal 2:5 is the **limiting case**).
- **T7.1.10** — Arc: submission as **gospel-grounded, truth-limited ordered subjection**.
- **T7.2.1** — Function: names the posture in the household/ecclesial argument.
- **T7.2.2** — Form: **epistle** (Pauline + Pastoral); read within household/church-order.
- **T7.2.3** — Logic: Gal 2:5 (truth-of-gospel **> submission**); 2Cor 9:13 (submission **→ glorifies God**).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: **ecclesial/household order**; polemical (Gal 2:5).
- **T7.2.5 / T7.2.6** — Anchor **2Cor 9:13** — shows submission **flowing from confession and glorifying God** (its God-ward ground).
- **T7.3.1** *(science §3)* — **social/organisational psychology** of submission/obedience (Milgram; compliant vs internalised; voluntary submission).
- **T7.3.2** — Illuminates **compliant-vs-internalised**: 2Cor 9:13 "from your confession" = **internalised** submission; 1Ti household = ordered/structural.
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: Milgram frames submission as **danger**; the biblical material makes the **identity of the authority decisive** — submission to God-honouring authority is good and is **rightly refused** where it betrays truth (Gal 2:5) (extract T7.3.3).
- **T7.3.4** — Surfaces the **inner-agreement** dimension — 2Cor 9:13 carries it; worth testing which submission terms carry inner agreement vs mere compliance (extract T7.3.4).
