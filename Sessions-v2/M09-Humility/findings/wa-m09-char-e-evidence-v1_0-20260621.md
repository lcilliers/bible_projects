## Definition
**The ordering of oneself under another's authority** — subordination as an inner posture of yieldedness. Distinct in flavour from active obedience (D): submission is the *placing-under*; obedience the *complying-with*.

## Inner-being signature
- **Kind:** *status* (a borne posture).
- **Direction:** relational/subordinative — "to / under / for".
- **Valence:** **commanded** in the household/order contexts (1 Timothy 2:11; 3:4); **righteous** as gospel-confession (2 Corinthians 9:13); **sinful** where submission would betray the gospel (Galatians 2:5 "we did not yield in submission" — a *refused* submission that is right to refuse).

## Sub-patterns
1. **Gospel-confessing submission (God-ward, voluntary)** — 2 Corinthians 9:13 "your submission… from your confession of the gospel".
2. **Ordered/household submission** — 1 Timothy 2:11 "with all submissiveness"; 1 Timothy 3:4 children "submissive".
3. **Refused submission (rightly withheld)** — Galatians 2:5 — submission is not an absolute; it is wrong when it would compromise truth. A key boundary finding.
