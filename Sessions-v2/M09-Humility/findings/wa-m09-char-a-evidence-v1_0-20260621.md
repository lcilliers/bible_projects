## Definition
The **chosen inner act of lowering oneself before God** — a volitional self-abasement, characteristically bound to repentance, by which a person (or people) bends the will and heart under God and so turns away threatened judgement. It is an *act done*, not a condition suffered (contrast C) and not a settled trait (contrast B).

## Inner-being signature (observation → interpretation)
- **Kind:** overwhelmingly *action* (the term is the doing of self-lowering); the faculty field is mostly absent because the term names an act rather than a faculty — its inner content is read from seat and object.
- **Seat:** where located, the **heart** (Leviticus 26:41 "uncircumcised heart"; 2 Kings 22:19 and 2 Chronicles 34:27 "your heart was penitent/tender"; 2 Chronicles 32:26 "the pride of his heart"). The act is heart-seated even when the verb is outward.
- **Object / direction:** God-ward — "before the Lord / before me / before God / with your God". This is the discriminator from C (which is directed by circumstance or enemy).
- **Experiencer:** self or addressed-self ("humble *yourselves*"); the act is reflexive.
- **Valence:** **righteous → commanded**; its *absence* is marked sinful (2 Chronicles 36:12 "did not humble himself"; 2 Chronicles 33:23 contrast with Manasseh).

## Sub-patterns (verse-likeness)
1. **The Chronicles/Kings repentance-relief pattern** — self-humbling averts or defers wrath: 1 Kings 21:29 (Ahab); 2 Chronicles 12:6–7, 12; 2 Chronicles 32:26; 2 Chronicles 33:12. The act is the hinge between sin and mercy.
2. **The commanded covenant call** — 2 Chronicles 7:14 "if my people… humble themselves"; James 4:10 "Humble yourselves before the Lord"; 1 Peter 5:6 "under the mighty hand of God"; Micah 6:8 "walk humbly with your God".
3. **The Gospel reversal frame** — Matthew 18:4; 23:12; Luke 14:11; 18:14 "whoever humbles himself will be exalted" (cross-cutting axis; see flags).
4. **The Christological maximum** — Philippians 2:8 "he humbled himself" (paired with obedience-to-death; the A↔D hinge).

## Cross-links
- **D Obedience:** Philippians 2:8 fuses self-humbling with obedience ("humbled himself by becoming obedient"). A and D meet at the cross.
- **M10 (sin) / M45 (repentance):** co-terms chattat "sin", maal "unfaithfulness", shuv "repent/return", bakhah "weep" recur around the act.

## Interpret-phase flags
- **Fork A+B (open):** A (act) vs B (disposition) are close; the verse-likeness keeps them distinct (chosen act vs borne trait), but a merge into a single "humility" head is defensible. Carried for decision.
- **Leviticus 26:41** ("if their… heart is humbled") sits at the A/C edge — covenant turning vs being-brought-low; routed to A on the repentance-response reading. Flagged.
