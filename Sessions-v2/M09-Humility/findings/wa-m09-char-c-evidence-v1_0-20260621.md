## Definition
**Lowness as an imposed or circumstantial condition** — being made low by God, by enemies, or by want — together with its inner correlate (a downcast spirit, a bowed heart). This characteristic is *suffered or undergone*, not chosen (contrast A) and not a virtue (contrast B). **The inner↔outer boundary runs through it** (see flags).

## Inner-being signature
- **Kind:** *status / condition*; experiencer is typically the **patient**, not the agent.
- **Divine role:** in several, God is the **agent** of the lowering (2 Chronicles 28:19 "the Lord humbled Judah"; 2 Corinthians 12:21 "my God may humble me") — humiliation as discipline/judgement.
- **Inner correlate:** the **affect** pole — *tapeinos* "downcast" (2 Corinthians 7:6, God "comforts the downcast"); the **heart** pole — Psalm 107:12 "he bowed their hearts down".
- **Valence:** mostly **neutral** (a condition, not a moral act); the moral weight sits on the response to it.

## Sub-patterns
1. **Made low by God (discipline/judgement)** — 2 Chronicles 28:19; 2 Corinthians 12:21; Job 40:12 (the proud to be brought low).
2. **Made low by enemies / hardship** — Psalm 106:42 "brought into subjection"; Psalm 107:12 "bowed… with hard labor"; Philippians 4:12 "how to be brought low".
3. **Low social estate** — Luke 1:48 "the humble estate of his servant"; Luke 1:52 "those of humble estate"; James 1:9 "the lowly brother"; James 1:10 "in his humiliation".
4. **The downcast affect** — 2 Corinthians 7:6 (the inner pole most clearly seated).
5. **Bodily/somatic lowness (outer edge):** Acts 8:33 "in his humiliation"; Philippians 3:21 "our lowly body" — lowness of the body itself.

## Cross-links
- **M03 (grief/sorrow):** "downcast" and "bowed hearts" border affliction-affect; bakhah "weep" co-occurs.
- **M38 (salvation/deliverance):** the low estate is the ground God reverses (Luke 1:48–52, Magnificat) — links to exaltation/deliverance.
