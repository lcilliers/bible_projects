## 2. Provisional characteristics

Tiered by evidential confidence. Each gives: definition (interpretation), the terms carrying it, occurrence weight, the verse-likeness grounding (observation, refs + short quotes <15 words), and the dominant inner-being signature.

### Tier 1 — Core (well-evidenced)

**A. Self-humbling before God** — *the chosen inner act of lowering oneself before God, characteristically bound to repentance and yielding the wrath/judgement it averts.*
- Terms: ka.na (self-humbling sense), tapeinoō (reflexive), tsa.na, tsa.nu.a.
- Weight: ~20+ occurrences (the Chronicles/Kings spine + Gospel/Epistle reflexives).
- Grounding: 2Ch 32:26 *"Hezekiah humbled himself for the pride of his heart"*; 2Ki 22:19 *"your heart was penitent… you humbled yourself before the Lord"*; 2Ch 7:14 *"if my people… humble themselves, and pray"*; Jam 4:10 *"Humble yourselves before the Lord"*; 1Pe 5:6 *"under the mighty hand of God"*; Mic 6:8 *"walk humbly with your God"*.
- Signature: action; experiencer self / addressed; divine object = God; valence righteous→commanded; frequently seated in **heart**.

**B. Lowliness as settled disposition** — *humility as an abiding interior orientation — lowly-mindedness toward self and esteem of others.*
- Terms: tapeinos (disposition sense), tapeinofrosunē (true-humility sense).
- Weight: ~12 occurrences.
- Grounding: Mat 11:29 *"I am gentle and lowly in heart"*; Phil 2:3 *"count others more significant than yourselves"*; Col 3:12 *"put on… humility, meekness"*; Eph 4:2 *"with all humility and gentleness"*; Rom 12:16 *"associate with the lowly"*; Act 20:19 *"serving the Lord with all humility"*.
- Signature: quality/status; faculty = **affect**; relational (toward one another); valence righteous→commanded.

**C. Being brought low / lowly estate / humiliation** — *lowness as an imposed or circumstantial condition — made low by God, enemies, or want — with its inner correlate (downcast spirit, bowed heart).*
- Terms: tapeinōsis, tapeinos (estate/downcast sense), ka.na (subjection/passive sense), tapeinoō (passive 'brought low').
- Weight: ~12 occurrences.
- Grounding: Luk 1:48 *"humble estate of his servant"*; Phil 4:12 *"how to be brought low"*; 2Cor 7:6 *"God… comforts the downcast"*; Psa 107:12 *"he bowed their hearts down"*; Psa 106:42 *"brought into subjection"*; Jam 1:10 *"the rich in his humiliation"*.
- Signature: status/condition; experiencer often patient, not agent; divine = agent in several; **inner↔outer boundary runs through this characteristic** (see fork 3).

**D. Obedience** — *the inner submission of the will expressed as heeding/compliance with an authority; the will that "hears under."*
- Terms: hupakouō, hupakoē, yiq.qe.hah (and the Luk 17:10 command-frame).
- Weight: ~31 occurrences (largest band).
- Grounding: Rom 6:17 *"obedient from the heart"*; Rom 1:5 *"the obedience of faith"*; Phil 2:8 *"obedient to the point of death"*; Eph 6:1 *"Children, obey your parents"*; Eph 6:5 *"obey your earthly masters… with a sincere heart"*; Heb 5:8 *"he learned obedience"*; 1Pe 1:22 *"purified your souls by your obedience"*.
- Signature: action/status; faculty = **perception** (hear-under), seat heart/soul; two objects — **God-ward** (gospel, faith, Christ) and **human-authority-ward** (parents, masters, husband); valence righteous→commanded.

**E. Submission / yieldedness** — *the ordering of oneself under another's authority — subordination as an inner posture, distinct in flavour from active obedience.*
- Terms: hupotagē (co-reinforced by hupotassō, tagged M23).
- Weight: 4 focus occurrences (+ co-term reach).
- Grounding: 1Ti 2:11 *"learn quietly with all submissiveness"*; 2Cor 9:13 *"your submission… from your confession of the gospel"*; Gal 2:5 *"we did not yield in submission"*; 1Pe 5:5 *"be subject to the elders"*.
- Signature: status; relational/subordinative; valence commanded (and once 'sinful' where submission would betray the gospel — Gal 2:5).

### Tier 2 — Provisional, relevance-flagged (decide before detail work)

**F. Willing-heartedness / freewill devotion** — *the volitional self-giving of a "willing heart/spirit" to God's service and offering.*
- Term: na.div (5).
- Grounding: Exo 35:5 *"whoever is of a generous heart"*; Exo 35:22 *"all who were of a willing heart"*; Psa 51:12 *"uphold me with a willing spirit"*.
- Signature: faculty = **volition**; seat heart/spirit; valence righteous; God-ward.
- ⚠ **Relevance question:** this is *positive willingness/generosity*, not lowliness — affectively the opposite pole from "downcast." It connects to submission only as *yielding one's will/goods to God*. Possible scope mismatch (a nichoach-type question, cf. M04). **Fork 4.**

**G. Dignity / grave bearing** — *weighty, seemly, respectable bearing.*
- Term: semnotēs (3).
- Grounding: 1Ti 2:2 *"godly and dignified in every way"*; 1Ti 3:4 *"with all dignity"*; Tit 2:7 *"integrity, dignity"*.
- ⚠ **Relevance question:** gravity/respectability is a different register from humility/meekness/submission; it co-occurs in the pastoral *household codes* (1Ti 3:4 pairs dignity with submissive children) but is not itself a lowliness term. **Likely set-aside or relocation. Fork 5.**

**H. Gentle forbearance / moderation of feeling** — *measured gentleness toward the failing, grounded in shared weakness.*
- Term: metriopatheō (1).
- Grounding: Heb 5:2 *"deal gently with the ignorant and wayward"*.
- Signature: faculty = affect. **Thin singleton**; meekness-adjacent — may fold into a meekness note (which mostly lives in M05). **Fork 5 (grouped).**

### Set-aside candidates (term-level filter, GR-PROG-007)

- **okneō "delay / be slow" (Act 9:38** *"come… without delay"***)** — carries no inner-being humility/submission content at term level. Likely mis-assigned. **Propose set-aside (fork 6).**
- **diatassō "to command / direct" (Luk 17:10)** — the *verse* is humility-relevant (*"we are unworthy servants… only done our duty"*), but the *focus term* names the commanding, not the servant's humility. Fails the term-level filter as a focus term; better treated as co-term context. **Propose set-aside (fork 6).**

## 3. Cross-cutting axes (noted for the synthesis phase — NOT characteristics)

- **The humble↔exalted reversal.** A recurring theological dynamic, not a separate inner state: Mat 23:12; Luk 14:11; Luk 18:14; Jam 4:6 *"opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble"*; 1Pe 5:6. It cuts across A, B, and C.
- **God-ward vs human-authority axis.** Structures both Obedience (D) and Submission (E); worth holding as an axis rather than splitting each characteristic in two.

- **Submission verb elsewhere.** **hupotassō "to subject" is tagged to M23**; only the noun hupotagē sits in M09 focus. The submission picture is split across clusters.
