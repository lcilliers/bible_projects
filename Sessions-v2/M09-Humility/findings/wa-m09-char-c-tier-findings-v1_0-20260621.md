## T0 — Divine Image  *(9/9)*
- **T0.1.1** — God is the **active agent** of lowering ("the Lord humbled Judah", 2Ch 28:19; "my God may humble me", 2Cor 12:21; the command to abase the proud, Job 40:12) **and of reversal** ("he has… exalted those of humble estate", Luk 1:52). Act 8:33 = **Christ's** humiliation (Isa 53).
- **T0.1.2** — **Yes — assumed by Christ** (Act 8:33; the lowly body, Phil 3:21, conformed from his). Otherwise God is the **agent who lowers/reverses**, not the bearer. Pattern: a creaturely condition the incarnate Son **takes on**.
- **T0.2.1** — Effects: **disciplinary** (2Ch 28:19 judgment; 2Cor 12:21 lest Paul be proud), **occasions comfort** (2Cor 7:6 "comforts the downcast"), and is the **ground God reverses** (Luk 1:52; Jam 1:9).
- **T0.2.2** — **Fallen condition** primarily (results from sin/judgment/affliction), redemptively used by God — **both**, weighted fallen.
- **T0.2.3** — **Strong future orientation** — reversed: "exalted those of humble estate" (Luk 1:52); the lowly brother's "exaltation" (Jam 1:9); "our lowly body… like his glorious body" (Phil 3:21).
- **T0.3.1** — Instantiates the creature's **dependence/contingency** and, christologically, the Son's **condescension** (Act 8:33; Phil 3:21).
- **T0.3.2** — Creaturely condition **assumed by Christ** (shared via incarnation).
- **T0.3.3** — The low estate itself is a circumstance (not an image-index); God's **reversal** of it shows the image's destiny (exaltation/glory).
- **T0.4.1** — **Christological + eschatological** — Act 8:33 (Christ's humiliation), Phil 3:21 (lowly→glorious body), Luk 1:52 (Magnificat reversal); the divine assumes the low and reverses it.

## T1 — Definition  *(14/18)*
- **T1.1.1** — Names an **imposed / circumstantial low condition** (undergone).
- **T1.1.2** — *ka.na* (be subdued/brought low), *tapeinoō* (be humbled), *tapeinos* (downcast/lowly), *tapeinōsis* (humiliation/low estate) — lowness **undergone**.
- **T1.1.3** — **Condition/status** implication (undergone, not chosen); usually externally caused.
- **T1.2.1** — A **condition/status** (the patient's state), not an act.
- **T1.2.2** — Largely **simple** (a state), with an **affective correlate** (downcast).
- **T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **exaltation / being lifted up** (Luk 1:52; Jam 1:9); height/glory.
- **T1.3.2** — It is the **absence** of exaltation/status; being undergone, it "resists" nothing.
- **T1.3.3** — **Not** the chosen act (A) nor the virtue (B); ends where the lowering becomes voluntary (A) or settled trait (B).
- **T1.4.1** — Passive verb (be brought low), noun (humiliation), adjective (downcast/lowly).
- **T1.4.2** — **Varies** — divine discipline (2Ch 28:19; 2Cor 12:21), enemy-affliction (Psa 106:42; 107:12), social estate (Luk 1:48, 52; Jam 1:9–10), want (Phil 4:12), **somatic** (Phil 3:21).
- **T1.5.1** — Immediate response: **mourning** (2Cor 12:21 "I may have to mourn") and **comfort received** (2Cor 7:6 God "comforted the downcast").
- **T1.5.2** — **Varied** — grief (downcast) or **learned contentment** (Phil 4:12 "I have learned… how to be brought low").
- **T1.6.1** — Sustained: **dependence/contentment** (Phil 4:12) and **readiness for comfort/exaltation**.
- **T1.6.3** — Immediate = downcast grief/comfort; sustained = learned contentment / awaited exaltation.
- **T1.7.1** — [inferential] rightly borne when received as **discipline** (2Cor 12:21) or with **learned contentment** (Phil 4:12).
- **T1.4.3 / T1.7.2 / T1.7.3** — [SILENT–data] not a speech mode; blocking/non-uptake conditions not described.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries  *(6/6 — the inner↔outer boundary)*
- **T2.1.1** — **Heart** — "he bowed their hearts down with hard labor" (Psa 107:12); **affect** — "downcast" (2Cor 7:6); **body** — "our lowly body" (Phil 3:21). The condition reaches from circumstance into heart and body.
- **T2.1.2** — Pattern (the **inner↔outer boundary**, fork 3): the **inner** correlate is heart/affect (bowed heart, downcast spirit); the **outer** is body/estate (lowly body, humble social estate). Humiliation **straddles** the boundary — its inner-being relevance is carried by the heart/affect pole (Psa 107:12; 2Cor 7:6), its outer edge by the somatic/social pole (Phil 3:21; Luk 1:48).
- **T2.10.1** — **Movement onto the person from an external source**: God or enemies bring the lowering **onto** the person (2Ch 28:19; Psa 106:42 enemies; 2Cor 12:21 God) — external→person direction.
- **T2.7.1** — Body link **both directions**: the condition **is** bodily (Phil 3:21, somatic lowness awaiting transformation); and bodily affliction **feeds back** to bow the heart (Psa 107:12 hard labour → bowed heart).
- **T2.9.1** — Origin: **external** — divine discipline (2Ch 28:19; 2Cor 12:21), enemy/circumstance (Psa 106:42; 107:12; Phil 4:12), or social birth-estate (Luk 1:48). Not self-generated.
- **T2.9.2** — Origin **multiple** (God / enemies / circumstance / estate), varying by context.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological  *(18/20)*
- **T7.1.1** — *ka.na, tapeinoō, tapeinos, tapeinōsis* — roots of **lowering / being-low**.
- **T7.1.2** — **Passive verb / noun / adjective** — the **condition/state** grammar (undergone).
- **T7.1.3** — Semantic range: social low estate → affliction/humiliation → bodily lowness.
- **T7.1.4** — The **condition** member of the *tapein-* field (vs act A, disposition B) — voice/grammar distinguishes.
- **T7.1.5** — Opposite: **exaltation** (*hupsoō*, Luk 1:52; *doxa* "glory", Phil 3:21).
- **T7.1.6** — Person-type: *tapeinos* "the lowly / those of humble estate".
- **T7.1.8** — OT (*ka.na*, national subjection/judgment) → NT (*tapeinōsis/tapeinoō*, individual + christological + eschatological) — the low estate becomes **christologically assumed and eschatologically reversed**.
- **T7.1.9** — *tapeinōsis* "humiliation/low estate" — NT develops the condition-noun (Luk 1:48; Phil 3:21).
- **T7.1.10** — Arc: subjection/judgment → affliction/low estate → christological humiliation → eschatological glorification.
- **T7.2.1** — Function: predicate/object naming the state the person is in or brought into.
- **T7.2.2** — Forms: narrative (2Ch), psalm (Psa 106–107), Gospel canticle (Luk 1 Magnificat), epistle (2Cor/Phil/Jam), prophecy-quote (Act 8:33 = Isa 53).
- **T7.2.3** — Logic: the humble-exalted **reversal** (Luk 1:52; Jam 1:9–10); discipline-against-pride (2Cor 12:21).
- **T7.2.4** — Setting: judicial/disciplinary (2Ch), liturgical (Magnificat), eschatological (Phil 3:21), affliction (2Cor; Phil 4).
- **T7.2.5 / .6** — Anchor **Luk 1:52** — the low estate as the **object of God's reversing mercy** (his characteristic lifting of the low); note Phil 3:21 (somatic eschatology).
- **T7.3.1** *(science §1)* — humility psychology, **involuntary/situational pole** (situationally-triggered humility; humbling experiences).
- **T7.3.2** — Illuminates: **situationally-triggered** humility (failure/suffering/loss reduce inflated appraisal) maps to C (discipline/affliction — 2Ch 28:19; 2Cor 12:21; Phil 4:12); contriteness's somatic quality maps to bodily lowness (Psa 107:12; Phil 3:21).
- **T7.3.3** — Divergence: science studies humbling experiences for their psychological effect (reduced narcissism); C frames the low estate **theologically** as discipline and the ground of God's reversal — a meaning the effect-account doesn't carry.
- **T7.3.4** — **Structural confirmation:** the science's **situational-vs-dispositional** distinction maps cleanly onto the **A (act) / B (disposition) / C (situational condition)** split — the verse evidence confirms the three-fold distinction the framework predicts.
