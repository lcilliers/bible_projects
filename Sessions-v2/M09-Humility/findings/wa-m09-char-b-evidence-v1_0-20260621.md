## Definition
**Humility as an abiding interior orientation** — lowly-mindedness toward oneself and a corresponding esteem of others. Not the act of self-lowering (A) nor an imposed low condition (C), but the *borne quality* of a humble heart.

## Inner-being signature
- **Kind:** *quality / status* (a borne trait), with the adjective *tapeinos* tagged **faculty = affect** — humility as a felt interior set, not merely behaviour.
- **Seat:** heart and soul (Matthew 11:29 "lowly in heart… rest for your souls"; Colossians 3:12 inward-parts).
- **Direction:** toward **others** and toward **self-estimate** (Philippians 2:3 "count others more significant"; Romans 12:16 "associate with the lowly… never be wise in your own sight").
- **Valence:** righteous → commanded; received from God ("clothe yourselves with humility", 1 Peter 5:5; "put on… humility", Colossians 3:12).

## Sub-patterns
1. **The clothed/put-on virtue** — Colossians 3:12; 1 Peter 5:5; Ephesians 4:2; Acts 20:19. Humility as donned character, listed with gentleness/patience.
2. **The Christ-pattern interior** — Matthew 11:29 "I am gentle and lowly in heart" (the only place the disposition is predicated of Christ as his own settled state).
3. **The grace-magnet** — James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5 "God… gives grace to the humble" (the disposition as the object of divine favour).
4. **COUNTERFEIT POLE (flagged):** *tapeinofrosunē* at Colossians 2:18 and 2:23 carries sense "asceticism", **valence = sinful** — self-made, body-severe false humility seated in "flesh/mind". The *same lemma* names true humility and its counterfeit; a key finding for the interpret phase.

## Cross-links
- **M05 Gentleness (E):** humility and meekness are listed together (Colossians 3:12; Ephesians 4:2) — the M09↔M05 hinge. M05 distinguishes them: gentleness = manner toward others; humility = posture concerning self/before God.
- **M08 (pride/arrogance):** the structural opposite; huperēphanos "arrogant" is the recurrent antonym co-term ("God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble").

## Interpret-phase flags
- **Fork A+B (open):** see A.
- **2 Corinthians 10:1** ("humble when face to face") routed to B as demeanour/disposition, but reads as outward bearing — B/C edge; flagged.
- **Counterfeit tapeinofrosunē** (Colossians 2): decide whether the sinful-asceticism sense is held within B as its counterfeit pole or split out.
