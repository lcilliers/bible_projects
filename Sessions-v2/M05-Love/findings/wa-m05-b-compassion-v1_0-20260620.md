## §1 — Definition & scope of the characteristic

Compassion is the cluster's **visceral, other-regarding response to another's plight** — the inward stirring, often seated in the womb or bowels, that moves a person to spare, pity, show mercy, or give relief to the vulnerable or the one liable to judgment.

The band gathers: Hebrew **racham / rachamim / rachum / rachamani** (H7355 / H7356 / H7349 / H7362) "to have compassion / compassion / compassionate", cognate with *rechem*, the womb; **chus** (H2347, to pity); **chamal / chemlah / machmal** (H2550 / H2551 / H4263, to spare / sparing); the Aramaic **rachamin** (H7359). Greek **eleeō / eleos / eleēmōn / eleēmosunē** (G1653 / G1656 / G1655 / G1654) "to have mercy / mercy / merciful / alms"; **oiktirmos / oiktirmōn / oikteirō** (G3628 / G3629 / G3627, compassion); the *splanchna* family — **splanchnizō / eusplanchnos / polusplanchnos** (G4697 / G2155 / G4184), moved in the bowels; **sumpatheō / sumpathēs** (G4834 / G4835, sympathy); **eleeinos** (G1652, pitiable).

## §5 — Observation (grounded in the 257 distinct occurrences above)

- **Affective, with a strand of seeing.** Faculty is *affect* in 255 occurrences plus a marked *perception* strand (30): compassion is tied to sight — the recurring idiom "your eye shall (not) pity" makes the eye the organ through which the plight is registered.
- **Enacted.** Type is *action*-heavy (143 vs *status* 89, *quality* 23): mercy is *shown*, the needy *spared*, alms *given*.
- **Mostly positive, but uniquely BOUNDED.** Valence is *righteous* (145) / *neutral* (62) / *commanded* (14), yet carries a distinctive **forbidden (7) + sinful (7)** register found in almost no other band: pity is *forbidden* in specific judicial and holy-war contexts — "your eye shall not pity" in lex talionis (Deu 19:13, 19:21; 25:12) and apostasy / idolatry cases (Deu 7:16; 13:8), and "do not spare" Amalek (1Sa 15:3). Saul's misplaced sparing of Agag is the *sinful* counter-case (1Sa 15:9, 15:15). Compassion is thus right toward the needy yet wrong where it obstructs justice or covenant fidelity.
- **The most somatically-seated affection in the cluster.** Where a seat is named: *soul* (9), *inward-parts / bowels* (4 — Isa 63:15; Jer 31:20 "my heart yearns"; Luk 1:78 "the tender mercy [*splanchna*] of our God"), *heart* (1), *conscience* (1). Hebrew *rachamim* (womb) and Greek *splanchna* (bowels) locate compassion in the viscera.
- **Primarily a divine attribute.** Divine involvement is *agent*-dominant (60) — God has compassion far more often than humans in this band — with *possessor* (22) and *giver* (13): "his mercy is very great" (1Ch 21:13); "gracious and merciful, slow to anger" (Joe 2:13); "the tender mercy of our God" (Luk 1:78).
- **Other-directed.** *Other* (105) + *other-addressed* (48) outweigh *self* (40); object is characteristically a *person* (69), often the weak, needy, or guilty.

## §6 — Interpretation (labelled; inferential where marked)

- **Compassion is the affection of the stronger toward the weaker.** Its object is the vulnerable or the one liable to judgment, and its dominant divine-agency profile reads as a revealed attribute of God that humans are called to mirror — the mirroring logic is **INFERENTIAL (GR-PROG-009)** and labelled.
- **The forbidden-pity material is a genuine boundary on the characteristic, not an anomaly.** The corpus does not treat compassion as a flat universal: it is bounded by justice and covenant fidelity. This is recorded as data, not editorial judgment.
- **A strong body→inner-being datum.** The womb / bowels seat (*rachamim* / *splanchna*) is among the clearest evidence in M05 that an inner affection is felt in the body — material for the spirit–soul–body interface.

## §7 — Reflection (cultural reach and tentative application — not prescriptive)

- **Ethically charged material requiring careful framing.** The "eye shall not pity" and "do not spare Amalek" texts are liable to misreading and are sensitive across cultures and interest groups; any reader-facing treatment must hold them in their judicial / covenantal frame and must not generalise them. I flag this as a place needing careful framing — not a conclusion.
- **The somatic seat travels poorly between cultures.** Bowels / womb as the seat of mercy is vivid in Hebrew and Greek and opaque in modern English "compassion" — relevant for translation-aware audiences.
- **Mercy is enacted** (alms, sparing, relief), tying the inner affection to concrete action; application noted, not concluded.
