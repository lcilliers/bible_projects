## §1 — Definition & scope of the characteristic

Comfort is the cluster's characteristic of **coming alongside another in distress** — to console the grieving and to strengthen, urge, and encourage them onward. Its hinge term **parakaleō / paraklēsis** (G3870 / G3874) spans both registers at once: *to comfort* and *to exhort / appeal*. The band gathers Hebrew **nacham** (H5162, to comfort, lit. "be sorry"), **tanchum** (H8575, consolation), **lavav** (H3823, to encourage); and Greek **parakaleō / paraklēsis** with **oikodomeō / oikodomē** (G3618 / G3619, to build up / edify — the metaphorical strengthening sense, kept in scope per CC verification). Where compassion (B) *feels* toward the afflicted, comfort *acts* toward them.

## §5 — Observation (grounded in the 237 distinct occurrences above)

- **The most enacted band in the cluster.** Type is *action* (188) far above *status* (48): comfort is *done* to another — comforted, urged, encouraged, built up — not borne as a state.
- **Two registers under one hinge term.** Sense splits between **consolation** (comfort the grieving — Gen 50:21 "comforted them"; Rut 2:13; nacham / tanchum / paraklēsis) and **exhortation / appeal** (urge, beg, exhort — parakaleō "I appeal to you", "they begged him"). *parakaleō* carries both — to encourage is at once to soothe and to summon.
- **The band most about RECEIVING ministry.** Experiencer shows an unusually high *self* share — *other* (86) but *self* (52) and *other-addressed* (33): comfort is frequently *received* ("my soul refuses to be comforted", Psa 77:2), unlike love, compassion, and kindness, which are predominantly *given*.
- **More neutral-valenced.** Valence is *neutral* (87) ≈ *righteous* (77) + *commanded* (35), *sinful* (3) — because "appeal / urge / beg" is often a neutral speech-act rather than a moral state.
- **God as the comforter, comfort passed on.** Divine involvement is *agent*-led (29): "the Father of mercies and God of all comfort, who comforts us in all our affliction, so that we may be able to comfort those who are in any affliction" (2Cor 1:3–4); *origin* = received-from-outside (5). Comfort received from God is relayed to others.
- **Faculty affect with a cognition strand.** *affect* (230) + *cognition* (16): the exhortation / appeal sense engages the mind (persuasion), not only feeling.
- **Edification seat.** "comfort your hearts" (2Th 2:17), "strengthening the souls of the disciples" (Act 14:22), "encourage your hearts" (Col 4:8; Eph 6:22) — *heart* (8), *soul* (4).

## §6 — Interpretation (labelled; inferential where marked)

- **D is the cluster's come-alongside characteristic** — its defining motion is *toward* another's distress to relieve and strengthen. The double sense of *parakaleō* (console + exhort) shows comfort and summons as one act: to encourage is both to soothe and to urge onward.
- **A received→given circuit.** Comfort received from God is passed to others (2Cor 1:4), paralleling love's "from God" circuit (1Jn 4). The link between the two circuits is **INFERENTIAL (GR-PROG-009)** and labelled.
- **oikodomeō sits here as metaphorical strengthening.** Its literal "build" sense (tombs, houses) is non-inner-being and is tagged *sinful / neutral* in the data; the band holds only the *edify / build up* sense, which CC's faculty evidence confirmed as genuinely inner-being.

## §7 — Reflection (cultural reach and tentative application — not prescriptive)

- **The double register fragments in translation.** English "encourage" blurs *console* and *urge*; reader-facing output should distinguish them.
- **The high self-experiencer share makes D the band most about receiving ministry** — the comforted become comforters — relevant to application but noted, not concluded.
- **"Building up" as a metaphor for inner strengthening** is culturally vivid; flagged for the edification / expression dimension.
