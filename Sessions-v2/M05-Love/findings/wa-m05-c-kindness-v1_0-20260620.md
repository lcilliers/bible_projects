## §1 — Definition & scope of the characteristic

Kindness is the cluster's disposition of **settled, loyal benevolence** — the standing goodness that keeps faith and acts for another's good. Its Old-Testament core is **chesed** (H2617), covenant "steadfast love" / loyal-kindness, predominantly God's; its New-Testament stream is **chrēstos / chrēstotēs / chrēsteuomai** (kindness) and the **agathos** family (goodness, good works, the good conscience); **chasid** (H2623) names the person who embodies it — the godly / loyal one, the "saint". The band also gathers **filanthrōpia** (benevolence) and **filagathos** (lover of good). Where love (A) is an affection that fastens on an object and compassion (B) a stirring toward the weak, kindness is the *kept commitment* — love made loyal and durable.

## §5 — Observation (grounded in the 323 distinct occurrences above)

- **A borne quality, not an act — the most disposition-like band.** Type is *status* (180) + *quality* (127) with only 13 *action*: kindness / goodness is a standing attribute one *has* and *keeps*. The contrast with comfort (almost all action) and the near-absence of action verbs is the band's clearest structural mark.
- **Less faculty-saturated than the affections.** *affect* is tagged on 172 of 323 — chesed-as-covenant-quality often carries no faculty tag, being a relational and moral *property* as much as a felt affection.
- **Overwhelmingly righteous; it "sins" by absence, not misdirection.** Valence is *righteous* (172) + *neutral* (43) + *commanded* (16), with only **7 *sinful***. Those are deficient or counterfeit kindness — withholding it (Job 6:14), the cruel man (Pro 11:17), self-proclaimed chesed (Pro 20:6; 19:22), fickle love "like a morning cloud" (Hos 6:4). Where love sins by wrong *object*, kindness fails by *absence* or *pretence*.
- **Strongly God-associated, as a kept property.** Object type is *abstract* (32) ≈ *God* (31) > *person* (19); divine involvement is **possessor-dominant (35)** — chesed *belongs* to God: "his steadfast love endures forever" (1Ch 16:34); "show me the steadfast love of the Lord" (1Sa 20:14). Where compassion was *agent*-dominant (God *does* it), chesed is *possessor*-dominant (it *is* God's, a covenant property He keeps) — with *object* (15), *agent* (9), *giver* (4) following.
- **Sense centred on covenant loyalty.** "steadfast love" dominates, with a goodness sub-stream (*good*, *good deeds*, *good conscience*), *kindness / kindly*, *loyalty / loyally*, and *godly* (the chasid).
- **Seat includes the conscience.** Where a seat is named: *heart* (15), *soul* (14), *conscience* (5), *flesh* (5) — the *conscience* via the "good conscience" (agathos: 1Ti 1:5; 1:19; 1Pe 3:16; Act 23:1). Goodness uniquely touches the conscience.

## §6 — Interpretation (labelled; inferential where marked)

- **C is the cluster's covenant-fidelity characteristic.** Not a momentary affection but a *kept commitment* — steadfast love that endures, loyalty that does not fail. Its possessor-dominant divine profile frames chesed first as a property of God's character; the call for humans to keep the same is **INFERENTIAL (GR-PROG-009)** and labelled.
- **A possible OT→NT continuity — INFERENTIAL.** The Hebrew chesed (covenant steadfast-love) and the Greek chrēstotēs / agathōsynē (kindness / goodness as fruit of the Spirit, Gal 5:22) occupy one field; whether they form a single developing line is flagged inferential pending the cross-testament read.
- **The characteristic personified.** The chasid is the one who *embodies* chesed — an inner-being characteristic becoming an identity ("the godly", "the saints"). Relevant to how the programme handles characteristic-to-identity.

## §7 — Reflection (cultural reach and tentative application — not prescriptive)

- **chesed is the single most translation-contested term in the cluster** (mercy / lovingkindness / loyalty / steadfast love). Reader-facing output must hold its covenant-loyalty core rather than reduce it to sentiment.
- **The good-conscience seat** ties goodness to moral self-awareness — material for the conscience dimension of the programme.
- **The "deficient kindness" cases** (withholding, boasting, fickleness) frame kindness as *faithfulness*, not display — application noted, not concluded.
