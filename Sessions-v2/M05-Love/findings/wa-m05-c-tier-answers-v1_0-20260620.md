## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — Yes, pre-eminently **God as possessor**: "Oh give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; for his steadfast love endures forever!" (1Ch 16:34); the great self-revelation "The Lord, the Lord, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love" (Exo 34:6). Also **object** ("show me the steadfast love of the Lord", 1Sa 20:14) and **giver** ("God gave Daniel favor", Dan 1:9).

**T0.1.2** — Borne by God as a defining, enduring attribute — his steadfast love "endures forever" (1Ch 16:34) and is proclaimed as his name (Exo 34:6). Possessor-dominant: chesed is presented first as something that *is God's*, which the person then keeps derivatively — marking it the most God-anchored characteristic of the cluster.

**T0.2.1** — Yes — kindness keeps covenant and does good: "deal loyally with the sons of Barzillai" (1Ki 2:7); the agathos stream is "doing honest work … so that he may have something to share" (Eph 4:28). It leads the person to faithful, beneficent action.

**T0.2.2** — **Created design**, restored and commanded: it images God's own covenant character (Exo 34:6) and is "put on" by the renewed (Col 3:12), fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22 kindness/goodness). Its failures (cruelty, withholding) belong to the fallen condition.

**T0.2.3** — Yes — chesed's "forever" (1Ch 16:34) orients toward an enduring future; the agathos stream toward "every good work" prepared for the person (the good-works trajectory). It moves toward a fullness of covenant faithfulness.

**T0.3.1** — The likeness of **faithful, loyal goodness** — God who keeps "covenant and steadfast love" (1Ki 8:23) reproduced in the person who deals loyally (1Ki 2:7) and does good (Eph 4:28).

**T0.3.2** — **Shared**, and most directly of the cluster — chesed is God's name (Exo 34:6) and the human's covenant duty ("your loyalty to your friend", 2Sa 16:17); the human keeps the same loyalty God keeps.

**T0.3.3** — Presence images God's faithfulness (the chasid, "your godly one", Deu 33:8); absence is faithlessness or cruelty — "a cruel man hurts himself" (Pro 11:17), "many a man proclaims his own steadfast love, but a faithful man who can find?" (Pro 20:6). **Partly inferential** as image-doctrine.

**T0.4.1** — Yes — **covenantal, divine establishing the pattern**: Exo 34:6's self-revelation of "abounding in steadfast love" is the covenant name that all later chesed (divine and human) reflects; chesed "endures forever" (1Ch 16:34) points to the everlasting covenant fidelity realised in Christ.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Kindness / steadfast love. The name signals **settled, loyal benevolence** — the kept commitment that does good and keeps faith; love made durable.

**T1.1.2** — Hebrew **chesed** (covenant steadfast-love, loyalty, kindness), **chasad** (be kind), **chasid** (the godly/loyal one); Greek **chrēstos/chrēstotēs** (kind/kindness), the **agathos** family (good/goodness). Definitionally chesed binds *kindness* to *covenant loyalty* — a faithfulness that the Greek "kindness/goodness" terms render in the NT.

**T1.1.3** — The name carries a **covenantal-relational** implication: chesed is loyalty *within a bond* — "your loyalty to your friend" (2Sa 16:17), "keeping covenant and steadfast love" (1Ki 8:23). It is benevolence that is also fidelity.

**T1.2.1** — A **disposition / borne quality** — the most disposition-like in the cluster: *status* (180) + *quality* (127), only 13 *action*. Kindness is something one *is* and *keeps* ("are merciful kings", 1Ki 20:31), not chiefly a discrete act.

**T1.2.2** — **Compound**: chesed fuses kindness (benevolence) with loyalty/faithfulness (covenant fidelity) — hence its constant pairing with *emet/emunah* ("steadfast love and faithfulness") and *berit* (covenant). The agathos stream adds moral goodness and good works.

**T1.3.1** — Its structural opposite is **cruelty and faithlessness** — "a cruel man hurts himself" (Pro 11:17); the unfaithful who cannot keep chesed (Pro 20:6 "a faithful man who can find?").

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes **withholding and pretence**: "He who withholds kindness from a friend forsakes the fear of the Almighty" (Job 6:14); self-display — "many a man proclaims his own steadfast love" (Pro 20:6) — and fickleness, "your love is like a morning cloud" (Hos 6:4).

**T1.3.3** — Kindness is **not mere feeling and not a moment's favour**: it is *kept* love. It ends where benevolence is unfaithful or boasted — the fickle and the self-proclaiming chesed are its counterfeits (Hos 6:4; Pro 20:6).

**T1.4.1** — It operates as a **borne, kept quality** expressed in faithful dealing — "deal loyally" (1Ki 2:7), "showed kindness" (1Sa 15:6). Grammatically the band is noun/adjective-heavy (a standing property), rarely a finite act-verb.

**T1.4.2** — Yes — mode varies by **direction**: God's chesed kept toward his people (1Ki 8:23); the person's chesed kept toward friend (2Sa 16:17), kin (1Ki 2:7), and the vulnerable (1Sa 15:6 kindness shown to the Kenites). The agathos stream operates as active good-doing (Eph 4:28).

**T1.4.3** — Yes — chesed is **invoked and commanded in speech**: the request "show me the steadfast love of the Lord" (1Sa 20:14), the charge "deal loyally" (1Ki 2:7), and its praise "his steadfast love endures forever" (1Ch 16:34).

**T1.5.1** — The immediate response kindness shows is **faithful beneficence** — dealing loyally, showing kindness, doing good ("I will deal kindly with Hanun … for his father dealt kindly with me", 1Ch 19:2).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent — kindness characteristically answers prior kindness in kept faithfulness (1Ch 19:2), and where it fails it does so by withholding (Job 6:14), not by a varied response.

**T1.6.1** — Over time chesed **establishes covenant trust and a faithful character** — the chasid ("godly one", Deu 33:8; "saints/faithful ones", 1Sa 2:9) — and, in the agathos stream, a "good conscience" (1Ti 1:5).

**T1.6.3** — The immediate response is an act of loyal kindness; the sustained effect is the **formed faithfulness** of the chasid — a standing identity of covenant loyalty (1Sa 2:9; 2Ch 6:41).

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold rightly under **the fear of the Lord and covenant fidelity** — withholding it "forsakes the fear of the Almighty" (Job 6:14); it is kept by those who "keep covenant" (1Ki 8:23).

**T1.7.2** — It is blocked by **cruelty, self-interest, boasting, and fickleness** (Pro 11:17; Pro 20:6; Hos 6:4) and by forsaking the fear of God (Job 6:14). **No adversarial spirit** is shown distorting it — **Silent**.

**T1.7.3** — The person in whom chesed is present but does not take hold is the **boaster of his own steadfast love** (Pro 20:6) and the one whose love is "like a morning cloud … that goes early away" (Hos 6:4) — chesed professed but not kept.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — **Heart** (15 — "with all their heart", 1Ki 8:23 context of keeping covenant), **soul** (14), **conscience** (5 — the agathos "good conscience", 1Ti 1:5; Act 23:1), **flesh** (5 — the appeal of "a good conscience", 1Pe 3:21). Distinctively, chesed engages the **conscience** through the goodness stream more than any other M05 characteristic.

**T2.1.2** — The pattern shows chesed seated in the **heart/soul** (the covenant-keeping centre) and uniquely touching the **conscience** (good conscience). It is **less faculty-saturated** than the affections (affect tagged on only ~half), indicating a relational/moral *property* as much as a felt state.

**T2.10.1** — Yes — chesed moves **from God onto the person** (Dan 1:9 "God gave Daniel favor"; received-from-outside, 8 occurrences) and is kept *within* the heart toward others; God's chesed reaches the person and is mirrored outward.

**T2.7.1** — The body link is faint and **expressive/indicative** (the "good conscience" appealed to in baptism, 1Pe 3:21); chesed is not a somatic characteristic. Largely **Silent** on an emphatic body link.

**T2.9.1** — **Bestowed by God** (Dan 1:9; God's own chesed as source, Exo 34:6) and **kept by the person** as covenant duty (1Ki 2:7). Origin is divine gift mirrored in human fidelity.

**T2.9.2** — **Multiple** — God as the original possessor and giver (Exo 34:6; Dan 1:9), the person as keeper — and stable across contexts (chesed is, by definition, the *enduring* form).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.1.1 / T3.1.2 / T3.1.3 · Perception** — **Silent.** No occurrence engages the perceptive faculty; chesed is a kept disposition, not a perceiving.

**T3.2.1 · Cognition (engage?)** — Lightly. Goodness engages knowing in the "good conscience" and discernment of good from evil ("Bad company ruins good morals", 1Cor 15:33; "doing honest work", Eph 4:28) — but chesed itself is not a knowing.

**T3.2.2 · Cognition (effect)** — Where engaged, it disposes the mind toward the good and faithful course; mostly minimal.

**T3.2.3 · Cognition (pattern)** — Cognition is marginal — chesed is borne and kept, not reasoned; its near-silence here marks it a property of fidelity rather than thought.

**T3.3.1 · Memory (engage?)** — Yes, lightly — chesed is bound to **remembrance**: God "remembers" his steadfast love and covenant (the zakhar co-occurrence, 10×; "remember … your steadfast love", Psa 25:6 register).

**T3.3.2 · Memory (effect)** — God's remembering of his chesed secures the covenant; the person's chesed is "good deeds … remembered" (the agathos register, 2Ch 32:32).

**T3.3.3 · Memory (pattern)** — Memory engagement is real but secondary — chesed is rooted in *remembered covenant*, consistent with its loyalty-character.

**T3.4.1 · Affect (engage?)** — Partially (affect 172 of 323): chesed has a warm, loving register ("love", "lovingkindness"), but it is **less affect-saturated** than love or compassion — much of it is borne covenant property without a felt-state tag.

**T3.4.2 · Affect (effect)** — Where affective, it is the warmth of loyal love (chesed as "lovingkindness"); but its defining note is fidelity more than feeling.

**T3.4.3 · Affect (pattern)** — The partial affect-tagging marks chesed as **a quality of will-and-relationship more than of feeling** — distinguishing it from the affect-saturated love and compassion.

**T3.5.1 / T3.5.2 / T3.5.3 · Creativity** — **Silent.** No engagement with imagination or origination.

**T3.6.1 · Volition (engage?)** — Yes — chesed is **kept by the will**: "deal loyally" (1Ki 2:7), "show kindness" (1Sa 15:6); its failure is the will that withholds (Job 6:14). Kindness/goodness is also commanded (Gal 5:22 fruit; Col 3:12 put on).

**T3.6.2 · Volition (effect)** — It directs the will to faithful benevolence and good works (Eph 4:28); its disorder is a will that boasts its own chesed or lets it lapse (Pro 20:6; Hos 6:4).

**T3.6.3 · Volition (pattern)** — Chesed's "kept" character makes it a **virtue of the faithful will** — loyalty sustained by choice over time, not a passing affect.

**T3.7.1 · Agency (engage?)** — Yes — kindness/goodness acts: dealing loyally (1Ki 2:7), doing good and sharing (Eph 4:28; Gal 6:6), "good deeds" (2Ch 32:32).

**T3.7.2 · Agency (effect)** — It moves the person to faithful beneficence — covenant kindness shown, good works done.

**T3.7.3 · Agency (pattern)** — Though status-dominant, where agency appears it is **faithful action flowing from the borne disposition** — the good person does good because they *are* good (the agathos logic).

**T3.8.1 · Moral evaluation (engage?)** — Yes, strongly — the agathos stream *is* moral evaluation: assessing and doing the *good* — "doing honest work" (Eph 4:28), "good morals" (1Cor 15:33), and chesed weighed against faithlessness (Pro 20:6).

**T3.8.2 · Moral evaluation (effect)** — It establishes the standard of the good and faithful; its failure (cruelty, boasting) is judged against it.

**T3.8.3 · Moral evaluation (pattern)** — Overwhelmingly righteous (172) with the few sinful cases being **deficient or counterfeit** kindness (withholding, boasting, fickleness) — chesed "sins" by *absence/pretence*, not by misdirection (unlike love).

**T3.9.1 · Conscience (engage?)** — Yes — distinctively, through the goodness stream: "the aim of our charge is love that issues from a pure heart and a good conscience" (1Ti 1:5); "having a good conscience" (1Pe 3:16); "I have lived my life before God in all good conscience" (Act 23:1).

**T3.9.2 · Conscience (effect)** — Goodness yields and rests on a clear conscience; rejecting it makes "shipwreck of their faith" (1Ti 1:19).

**T3.9.3 · Conscience (pattern)** — C is the **one M05 characteristic that substantially engages the conscience** (5 occurrences via "good conscience") — goodness and moral self-witness are joined.

**T3.10.1 · Conscientiousness (engage?)** — Yes — chesed integrates faithful awareness, will, and action: keeping covenant "with all their heart" (1Ki 8:23) and doing good works as a settled pattern (2Ch 32:32).

**T3.10.2 · Conscientiousness (effect)** — It yields a dependable, faithful character — the chasid whose loyalty can be relied upon (2Sa 16:17).

**T3.10.3 · Conscientiousness (pattern)** — Chesed is the cluster's clearest case of **character-level conscientiousness** — a stable, conscience-bearing fidelity expressed in consistent good.

**T3.11.1 · Relational capacity (engage?)** — Yes, constitutively — chesed *is* covenant-relational equipment: "your loyalty to your friend" (2Sa 16:17), kindness shown across relationships (1Ch 19:2; 1Sa 15:6).

**T3.11.2 · Relational capacity (effect)** — It binds persons in durable loyalty — the kept faithfulness that sustains covenant bonds (1Ki 8:23) and friendship (2Sa 16:17).

**T3.11.3 · Relational capacity (pattern)** — Strongly other-directed (other 97 + addressed 69 vs self 28), chesed is **relational fidelity** — its whole nature is keeping faith with another.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1.1 · God→human** — Yes, dominantly: God's steadfast love kept toward his people — "keeping covenant and steadfast love" (1Ki 8:23); "abounding in steadfast love" (Exo 34:6); "God gave Daniel favor" (Dan 1:9).

**T4.1.2 · basis** — **Covenantal** — chesed is kept "with your servants who walk before you with all their heart" (1Ki 8:23); grounded in God's character and oath, enduring "forever" (1Ch 16:34). Faithful, not merely responsive.

**T4.1.3 · what it shows of God** — That God is **faithful** — his goodness is not fickle but kept; "the Lord is good; for his steadfast love endures forever" (1Ch 16:34) shows a disposition of unwavering loyal love.

**T4.2.1 · human→God** — Yes — the chasid's devotion and the appeal for God's chesed: "let your priests … be clothed with salvation, and let your saints rejoice in your goodness" (2Ch 6:41); "show me the steadfast love of the Lord" (1Sa 20:14).

**T4.2.2 · inner posture required** — **Covenant fidelity and the fear of the Lord** — walking "before you with all their heart" (1Ki 8:23); withholding kindness "forsakes the fear of the Almighty" (Job 6:14).

**T4.2.3 · what it shows** — That the person relates to God as **covenant partner** — the chasid is the one loyal to the covenant, mirroring God's loyalty (Deu 33:8; 2Ch 6:41).

**T4.3.1 · human→human giving** — Yes — chesed extended person to person: "deal loyally with the sons of Barzillai" (1Ki 2:7); "you showed kindness to all the people of Israel" (1Sa 15:6); honest sharing (Eph 4:28).

**T4.3.2 · giver's inner conditions** — **Faithfulness and the fear of God** (Job 6:14), a "good conscience" (1Ti 1:5), and freedom from self-display (Pro 20:6).

**T4.3.3 · what the giver must have received/become** — They must have **received God's chesed** and become faithful — chesed answers prior chesed ("for his father dealt kindly with me", 1Ch 19:2), and the renewed "put on … kindness" (Col 3:12).

**T4.4.1 · human→human receiving** — Yes — kindness is received in kept loyalty ("with such loyalty they met me", 1Ki 2:7) and as favour ("God gave Daniel favor … in the sight of the chief", Dan 1:9).

**T4.4.2 · conditions accompanying / blocking uptake** — Uptake accompanies covenant trust; it is blocked by the cruelty or fickleness of the giver (Pro 11:17; Hos 6:4).

**T4.4.3 · state of one who meets it but does not take it up** — **Silent / partial** (cf. T1.7.3 — the unfaithful who do not keep chesed).

**T4.5.1 · within bonds vs across distance** — Primarily **within covenant bonds** (friend, kin, people — 2Sa 16:17; 1Ki 2:7) but extended across to others (the Kenites, 1Sa 15:6; Daniel among foreigners, Dan 1:9).

**T4.5.2 · within covenant only, or crossing** — Chesed is **covenant-defined** at its core (kept within the bond) yet **crosses** in acts of kindness to outsiders (1Sa 15:6) — covenant-shaped but not covenant-confined.

**T4.5.3 · relational scope** — Friends, kin, the people of God, and outsiders shown kindness; its scope is the network of covenant and the vulnerable within reach. No class is excluded by the characteristic itself.

**T4.6.1 / T4.6.2 / T4.6.3 · Spiritual beings** — **Silent.** No occurrence shows chesed operating toward, attacked by, or mediated through angelic or adversarial beings.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1 · Transformation** — Yes — chesed forms the **faithful character** (the chasid) over time and, as goodness, transforms conduct ("Let the thief no longer steal, but rather … do honest work", Eph 4:28 — a reorientation of the person's whole pattern).

**T5.1.2 · reversible / irreversible** — **Silent** on reversibility, though the fickle chesed "like a morning cloud" (Hos 6:4) and the "shipwreck" of a rejected good conscience (1Ti 1:19) show kindness/goodness can lapse.

**T5.2.1 · Sequence of inner states** — Yes, in the reform pattern: from stealing to honest work to sharing (Eph 4:28) — a sequence from old conduct to good. Chesed otherwise is shown as a kept constant rather than a staged movement.

**T5.3.1 · Mechanism of change** — By **covenant fidelity sustained** and by **received goodness reproduced** (1Ch 19:2 — kindness answering prior kindness); the agathos stream by reform of conduct (Eph 4:28).

**T5.3.2 · mechanism differ across contexts** — Yes: God's chesed by sovereign faithfulness (Exo 34:6); the chasid's by covenant loyalty kept; the agathos reform by deliberate change of conduct (Eph 4:28).

**T5.4.1 · Suffering / affliction** — Yes — chesed is the refuge in distress: "I am in great distress. Let me fall into the hand of the Lord, for his mercy/steadfast love is very great" (1Ch 21:13 register; cf. chesed's "endures forever" as ground of hope in affliction). 

**T5.4.2 · what suffering does to it** — Affliction **drives the person to rely on God's chesed** — the enduring steadfast love is the thing appealed to when all else fails (1Ch 16:34; the lament-to-praise pattern).

**T5.5.1 · Formation and sanctification** — Yes, centrally — kindness/goodness is **fruit of the Spirit** (Gal 5:22), "put on" by the chosen (Col 3:12), and the chasid is the sanctified, covenant-loyal person (1Sa 2:9; 2Ch 6:41). It forms the faithful character over the whole arc.

**T5.6.1 · Eschatological trajectory** — Yes — chesed "endures forever" (1Ch 16:34) and the chasid is preserved ("He will guard the feet of his faithful ones", 1Sa 2:9). Present faithfulness anticipates the everlasting covenant love.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**T6.1.1 · Co-occurrence** — Chesed co-occurs above all with **faithfulness/truth** (emet/emunah — "steadfast love and faithfulness", 36×), **covenant** (berit, 12×), **covenant-keeping** (shamar, 17×), **remembrance** (zakhar, 10×), **compassion** (rachamim, 10×), **justice and righteousness** (mishpat/tsedaqah, 16×), and **love** (agapē/agapaō). The agathos stream co-occurs with truth and the good conscience.

**T6.1.2 · what co-occurrence shows** — Chesed sits at the **heart of the covenant-faithfulness complex** — fused with faithfulness, covenant, justice, and compassion (the "steadfast love and faithfulness / justice and righteousness" formulae). It is the relational glue of God's covenant character.

**T6.2.1 · Sequential relationship** — Yes — chesed **accompanies covenant-keeping** ("keeping covenant and steadfast love", 1Ki 8:23) and **grounds** compassion and forgiveness; it is consistently *paired* rather than sequenced — chesed-and-emet move together.

**T6.2.2 · what the sequence shows** — The relationship is **constitutive/correlational** — chesed and faithfulness are two faces of one covenant fidelity; chesed and compassion are companion mercies (Exo 34:6).

**T6.3.1 · does it produce another?** — Yes — chesed produces **trust and the chasid's loyalty**, and the agathos goodness produces **good works** and a clear conscience (1Ti 1:5; Eph 4:28).

**T6.3.2 · is it produced by another?** — It flows from **God's covenant character** and is reproduced by **received kindness** (1Ch 19:2); the chasid's chesed is produced by God's prior chesed.

**T6.3.3 · constituent of / constituted by another?** — Chesed is a **constituent of God's covenant goodness** (paired with faithfulness, compassion, justice in Exo 34:6) and, with compassion and humility, a constituent of the "compassionate heart … kindness" put on by the chosen (Col 3:12).

**T6.4.1 · shared vocabulary** — Chesed shares the **mercy field** with compassion (B — the "merciful and gracious … steadfast love" formula joins them) and the **agathos/chrēstos** "good/kind" terms reach into the moral-goodness field; "love" (agapē) co-occurs (chesed as covenant-love).

**T6.4.2 · shared root architecture** — The **chesed/chasad/chasid** root generates the band's Hebrew core (kindness, to-be-kind, the godly one); the **agath-** and **chrēst-** roots generate the Greek goodness/kindness stream — distinct roots converging on one characteristic.

**T6.4.3 · what the sharing shows** — The chesed↔compassion overlap shows them as **adjacent covenant mercies** (kept loyalty vs felt stirring); the agathos overlap shows kindness and moral goodness as one field in the NT.

**T6.5.1 · nearest neighbour and distinction** — The nearest neighbour is **compassion (B)**. The distinction: chesed is the **borne, durable, kept loyalty** (status/quality, possessor-dominant, "endures forever"); compassion is the **felt, often momentary visceral stirring** toward a plight (affect/action, agent-dominant). Chesed keeps; compassion is moved.

**T6.5.2 · precise boundary at overlap** — Where they overlap (Exo 34:6 "merciful and gracious … abounding in steadfast love"), the boundary is **duration and typing** — compassion the immediate mercy, chesed the steadfast love that endures.

**T6.5.3 · distinction of degree, kind, direction, or level?** — One of **kind and duration** (kept disposition vs felt movement); against love (A) the distinction is **fidelity vs affection** — chesed is love made loyal and durable.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1 · primary terms and root meanings** — Hebrew **chesed** (covenant steadfast-love/loyalty/kindness), **chasid** (the loyal/godly one); Greek **chrēstos/chrēstotēs** (kind/kindness), **agathos** (good). Roots converge on **benevolence bound to fidelity**.

**T7.1.2 · grammatical range** — Noun (chesed, chrēstotēs, agathos as substantive "good"), verb (chasad, chrēsteuomai "be kind", agathopoieō "do good"), adjective (chasid "godly", chrēstos "kind", agathos "good"). The range shows kindness as **disposition, act, and quality**, weighted to the borne forms.

**T7.1.3 · semantic range** — From covenant loyalty (1Ki 8:23) through kindness/favour (Dan 1:9), goodness and good works (Eph 4:28), to the personified "godly one/saints" (Deu 33:8; 2Ch 6:41). A range from steadfast love to moral goodness to covenant-faithful identity.

**T7.1.4 · terms distinguishing aspects** — Yes — **chesed** (borne loyal disposition) is distinguished from the act of *doing* good (agathopoieō, agathoergeō), and **chasid** names the *person-type* distinct from the quality. The disposition/act/person distinction is fully lexicalised.

**T7.1.5 · term for the opposite/absence** — No single antonym in band, but the opposite is named — **cruelty** ("a cruel man", Pro 11:17) and faithlessness ("a faithful man who can find?", Pro 20:6); the agathos opposite is **ponēros** ("evil", co-occurring).

**T7.1.6 · person-type term** — Yes, strongly — **chasid** (the godly/loyal one, "your saints/faithful ones", 1Sa 2:9; 2Ch 6:41; Deu 33:8) is the dedicated person-type for the one who embodies chesed.

**T7.1.7 · supplication / seeking term** — Yes — the request **"show me the steadfast love"** (*asah chesed* — "show me the steadfast love of the Lord", 1Sa 20:14) is the seeking form for chesed from another.

**T7.1.8 · OT↔NT continuity / development** — OT **chesed** (covenant steadfast-love/loyalty) develops into NT **chrēstotēs / agathōsynē** (kindness/goodness as fruit of the Spirit, Gal 5:22) and **filanthrōpia** (benevolence). Continuity of loyal benevolence; the covenant-loyalty dimension is carried implicitly into the NT kindness terms.

**T7.1.9 · NT-coined term** — **None** clearly coined; the NT renders chesed through existing Greek goodness/kindness vocabulary rather than a coinage. **Silent.**

**T7.2.5 · primary anchor verse** — **Exo 34:6** — "The Lord, the Lord, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love" — most fully and directly expresses chesed's essential character as God's self-named covenant loyalty. **1Ch 16:34** is the doxological anchor ("endures forever").

**T7.2.6 · what the anchor shows that no other shows** — Exo 34:6 alone **places chesed within God's self-revealed name** — making steadfast love not merely something God does but part of who God declares himself to be, the covenant character all human chesed reflects.

**T7.3.1 · most useful human-science framework** — **Behavioural science / positive psychology** — prosocial-behaviour and kindness research (Batson; Curry's finding that acts of kindness raise the doer's wellbeing; agreeableness as the prosocial trait).
