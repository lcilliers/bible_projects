## §1 — Definition & scope of the characteristic

Love is the cluster's **core bonding affection** — the inward movement that attaches a person to another, commits the self toward the good (or the possession) of what is loved, and, when disordered, fastens on the wrong object. It is the headline characteristic of M05 and its moral pivot.

The band gathers: Greek **agapaō / agapē** (G0025 / G0026), the volitional, covenantal love-word; **fileō / filos / filia** (G5368 / G5384 / G5373), affectionate and friendship love; and the kinship compounds — **filadelfia / filadelfos** (brotherly), **filostorgos** (familial-tender), **filandros / filoteknos** (husband- / child-loving). Hebrew **ahav / ahavah / ahev / ahav** (H0157 / H0160 / H0158) "to love / love / lover", with the beloved-and-lovely terms **yedidut, naeh, prosfilēs**. Embodied tokens of affection complete it: **cheq** (H2436, bosom / embrace / arms), **filēma / katafileō** (G5370 / G2705, kiss). **isopsuchos** (like-minded) marks affectional unity.

## §5 — Observation (grounded in the 300 distinct occurrences above)

- **Dominantly affective, with a will/value strand.** Faculty is *affect* in 263 occurrences, with smaller *moral_evaluation* (13), *cognition* (13) and *perception* (11) strands: to love is partly to value and choose, not only to feel.
- **As much done as borne.** Type is *action* (149) ≈ *status* (135), with little *quality* (16) — love is more enacted than the borne dispositions (contrast gentleness, almost all status).
- **Valence flips on the OBJECT — the highest disordered load in the cluster.** 138 *righteous* + 70 *commanded*, but **24 *sinful* + 1 *forbidden*** — more negative-valence occurrences than any other M05 band. Every disordered case is love aimed at the wrong object: the world (1Jn 2:15, the lone *forbidden*; 2Ti 4:10; Joh 3:19 "loved the darkness"), money / gain (2Pe 2:15), human glory (Joh 12:43), status and best seats (Luk 11:43; Mat 23:6), one's own life (Joh 12:25), falsehood (Rev 22:15), and the disordered attachments of 1Ki 11:2 and 2Sa 13:15. Same faculty, opposite moral worth — set entirely by what is loved.
- **Other-directed.** *Other* (95) + *other-addressed* (65) outweigh *self* (38).
- **It engages the whole inner being.** When a seat is named it is *heart* (24) + *soul* (15) + *mind* (3); the Great Commandment names them together — "love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind" (Mat 22:37; Mar 12:30; Luk 10:27). Jonathan "loved him as his own soul" (1Sa 18:3; 20:17). No other M05 affection is shown engaging heart, soul, and mind at once.
- **A divine act and a divine gift.** Divine involvement: God as *object* (22, the one to be loved), *agent* (20, God loves), *possessor* (7), *giver* (2). 1 John anchors the circuit — love is "from God" (1Jn 4:7; *origin* = received-from-outside), "if God so loved us, we also ought to love one another" (1Jn 4:11).
- **Embodied tokens.** *cheq* carries the loved one in the bosom / arms (Isa 40:11 the shepherd; Num 11:12), and the kiss expresses affection — including its perversion in Judas's betraying kiss (Luk 22:48; Mat 26:48–49, tagged *sinful*).

## §6 — Interpretation (labelled; inferential where marked)

- **Love is the cluster's defining affection and its moral hinge.** Alone among the bands it carries a large disordered register, *because* love is constitutively directional — it must have an object, and the object sets its worth. Love is therefore not a flat good but an orientation requiring right ordering. The connection to a doctrine of rightly-ordered love (*ordo amoris*) is **INFERENTIAL (GR-PROG-009)** and labelled as such.
- **The agapaō / fileō distinction is present but not lexically sharp.** Both tag *affect*; *agapaō* carries *moral_evaluation* somewhat more often. Whether the fields bear out the traditional volitional-vs-affectionate split is **additional discovery flagged** — the current data does not cleanly separate them.
- **Whole-inner-being engagement.** The Great-Commandment seat-cluster (heart + soul + mind + strength) is the strongest evidence in M05 that one of its affections engages the *entire* inner being rather than a single faculty — directly relevant to the programme's spirit–soul–body question.

## §7 — Reflection (cultural reach and tentative application — not prescriptive)

- **The most overloaded word in the field.** "Love" carries at least four registers here — covenant/volitional (*agapē*), affectionate (*philia*), embodied (kiss / embrace), and disordered (love of world / self) — collapsed into one English word. Reader-facing output should resist flattening them.
- **The disordered-love evidence speaks to contemporary application,** but the verses locate the danger in the *object*, not the faculty; I record this without moralising.
- **Love is bodily-expressed** (kiss, embrace, bosom), a datum for the body / expression dimension of the spirit–soul–body interface — flagged, not developed.
