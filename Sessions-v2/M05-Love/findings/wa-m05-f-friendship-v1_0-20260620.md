## §1 — Definition & scope of the characteristic

Friendship is the cluster's characteristic of the **bonded peer relationship** — the standing affectionate tie of friend to friend. It is the most horizontal and most descriptive band: "friend" names a relational *status* more than a felt act or an enacted disposition. The band gathers Greek **filos** (G5384, friend), **filia** (G5373, friendship), **filofrōn** (G5391, friendly); and Hebrew **re.eh** (H7463, friend / companion). Where love (A) is the affection itself, friendship is the *standing bond* within which such affection lives.

## §5 — Observation (grounded in the 32 distinct occurrences above)

- **A relational quality, not an act or a felt state.** Type is *quality* (28 of 32) with a little *status* (4) and no *action* — the only band so dominated by *quality*. "Friend" is a standing relational designation.
- **Predominantly neutral and descriptive.** Valence is *neutral* (20) with only *righteous* (2), *sinful* (2), *commanded* (1): "friend(s)" mostly names a relationship without moral charge, and the dominant sense is simply *friend / friends*.
- **The most horizontal band — no divine involvement.** *divine_involvement* is **empty**: friendship is the human-to-human tie. The single vertical reframing is Jesus' elevation of it — "Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends" and "I have called you friends" (Joh 15:13–15) — where the lone *soul* seat appears.
- **It takes moral colour from its object.** The two *sinful* occurrences are "friendship with the world is enmity with God" (Jam 4:4) — friendship, like love, coloured by what it is friendship *with*.
- **Affectional faculty.** *affect* (28) keeps the band within the inner-being field on the strength of the bond's affection, even where the surface use is descriptive.

## §6 — Interpretation (labelled; inferential where marked)

- **F is the cluster's peer-bond characteristic** — a symmetrical relationship between equals, distinct from love (affection toward an object), compassion (toward the weak), and kindness (loyal benevolence toward another).
- **SCOPE FLAG — INFERENTIAL.** The quality-dominant, neutral, divine-empty profile suggests friendship functions here more as a relational *category* than as an inner state. It is the band most open to the question whether "friend" is an *inner-being characteristic* or a *relationship label*. It is kept in scope on its *affect* faculty and the Joh 15 elevation, but the question is flagged for researcher review.
- **The Jam 4:4 case** shows friendship's worth, like love's, set by its object (world vs God).

## §7 — Reflection (cultural reach and tentative application — not prescriptive)

- **"Friend" is thin as an inner-being term in this corpus** — largely descriptive. The richer inner content of friendship (loyalty, affection) is carried by neighbouring bands: *philia*-love in A and chesed-loyalty in C. Reader-facing output should note that friendship's inner substance lives mainly next door.
- **"Friend of God", and Jesus calling disciples "friends",** reframe a horizontal category vertically — a significant but singular datum, flagged not developed.
