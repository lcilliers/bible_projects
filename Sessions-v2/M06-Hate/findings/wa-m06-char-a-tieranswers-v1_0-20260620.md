## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God-relation is present in 25 of 175 occurrences: God as **object** of hatred in 10 (Num 10:35; Deu 12:31; 2Ch 19:2; Psa 68:1; 81:15; 83:2; 139:21; Mal 2:16 — "those who hate the LORD"), and God as **agent** who hates in 15 (Deu 12:31; 16:22; Job 16:9; Psa 11:5; Pro 6:16; Isa 1:14; 61:8; Hos 9:15; Amo 6:8; Zec 8:17; Mal 1:3 "Esau I have hated"). So the relation runs both ways: God hates (evil, false worship) and God is hated.

**T0.1.2** — The characteristic **is borne by God himself**, not only by the creature: 15 agent-occurrences show divine hatred, directed at evil, injustice, and false worship (Pro 6:16 the things the LORD hates; Isa 1:14 "my soul hates" the corrupted feasts). This places aversive affect — specifically *hatred of evil* — within the divine nature, and so within what the image can reflect, while the bulk of human occurrences (sinful 83) show its fallen distortion.

**T0.2.1** — Aggregate (no single verse states a purpose universally). Where a role is shown, the **immediate-response** field gives effects: hate "works ruin" (Pro 26:28), "brings shame" (Pro 13:5), and aligns with loving violence (Psa 11:5). The commanded seam shows a positive role: hatred of evil as part of the fear of the LORD (Pro 8:13).

**T0.2.2** — **Both.** The commanded/righteous seam (22 occ: Exo 18:21; Psa 97:10; Amo 5:15; Psa 119:104,113,128,163; 139:21–22; Pro 8:13) reads as created design — rightly-ordered aversion to evil; the dominant sinful framing (83) reads as the fallen condition (causeless or self-serving hatred).

**T0.2.3** — **SILENT.** No eschatological/future-fullness orientation is tagged for any C-A occurrence (the eschatological abhorrence material sits in C-C, not here).

**T0.3.1** — Inferential, grounded in T0.1.2: the aspect of divine likeness instantiated is **holy aversion to evil** — the capacity to reject what is contrary to God's character (God hates evil; the righteous are commanded to hate evil, Psa 97:10).

**T0.3.2** — **Shared** between God and person, but valence-differentiated: God hates only evil/injustice (agent cases all object evil); the human capacity is the same affect, rightly or wrongly directed.

**T0.3.3** — Inferential: where a person hates *evil* (righteous seam) the image is rightly ordered; where a person hates *without cause* or hates *the good/God* (Psa 139:21 inverse; obj=God 10) the image is disordered. The evidence shows both states.

**T0.4.1** — **Mal 1:3** ("Esau I have hated, Jacob I have loved") carries election/covenant language; its typological extension (Rom 9:13) lies outside this OT extract and is **labelled inferential**. No other clearly typological use is tagged.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: *aversive affect* — felt, settled rejection of an object. The name signals an **emotion (affect)** that is also **directional** (it has an object) and **settled** (a standing orientation, not a momentary reaction).

**T1.1.2** — Primary Hebrew `sa.ne` (143) "to hate"; nominal `sin.ah` (19) "hatred"; `sa.tam` (8) the harboured grudge; Aramaic `se.na` (2). At the definitional level they show hatred as both an **act** (sa.ne, action 143) and a **standing state** (sin.ah, status).

**T1.1.3** — The name carries a **relational/directional** implication: hatred is always *of* something or someone (object_type person 75, abstract 27, God 10) — it is constitutively directed.

**T1.2.1** — Predominantly an **act** (type action 153) with a **condition/status** form (status 21, `sin.ah`); one quality occurrence (`sa.ni` "hated", adjectival). So: primarily act, secondarily standing condition.

**T1.2.2** — **Compound** on the evidence: every `sa.ne` occurrence is tagged **affect + cognition** (143) — hatred here fuses *feeling* with *appraisal* (one hates what one has judged). Constituent elements: an affective rejection and a cognitive evaluation.

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **love** (`'ahav`, cluster M05). The evidence is direct — love is the stated *cause* of the contrasting hatred (Gen 29:31 Jacob's greater love for Rachel → Leah hated; Gen 37:4), and M05 co-occurs 12× in C-A verses; `ba.rakh` "to bless" appears as the partner term opposite hating (Gen 24:60).

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes/resists love, blessing, and acceptance of its object (the love→hate contrast in Gen 29; the bless↔hate pairing).

**T1.3.3** — It is *not* mere anger (a reactive episode — see C/T7.3): the data shows it as a **settled** affect (status form `sin.ah`; harboured `sa.tam`), distinct from momentary reaction. It ends where appraisal-free reaction (anger, M02) or worth-judgement-only (contempt, C-B) begins.

**T1.4.1** — Mode per verse: realised grammatically as Qal verb (`sa.ne`, action) and as noun (`sin.ah`, status). The `mode` field is empty across the cluster, so finer mode data is not provided. The manner of functioning is **affective-cognitive rejection** of an object.

**T1.4.2** — Varies by **valence/direction**, not by constitutional level: the same root operates as condemned hatred (sinful 83) and as commanded hatred of evil (commanded/righteous 22). Direction (toward person / toward evil / toward God) is the main axis of variation.

**T1.4.3** — Yes, in part: the **commanded** occurrences (Exo 18:21; Psa 97:10; Amo 5:15) operate through a communicative/imperative mode — hatred of evil is *enjoined*. Otherwise mostly narrated, not spoken.

**T1.5.1** — Where shown (immediate_response, 9 occ): hate issues immediately in ruin (Pro 26:28), shame (Pro 13:5), pursuit (Eze 35:6), and loving violence (Psa 11:5). Most occurrences (166) tag no immediate response.

**T1.5.2** — **Varied** — the few tagged responses differ (ruin, shame, pursuit, violence); no single consistent immediate response.

**T1.6.1** — Over time it establishes a settled orientation of rejection (the `sin.ah`/`sa.tam` standing-state and harboured-grudge forms attest a durable condition); Pro 26:28 ties it to lasting "ruin."

**T1.6.3** — Distinct from the immediate response (T1.5) in that the sustained effect is the **standing disposition itself** (hatred as `sin.ah`), whereas the immediate response is its episodic output (ruin, shame).

**T1.7.1** — Operates *rightly* where its object is evil/falsehood (the righteous seam: Psa 97:10; 119:104; Pro 8:13) — i.e. under the inner condition of the fear of the LORD (Pro 8:13 explicitly).

**T1.7.2** — Operates *wrongly/distortedly* where it is causeless or self-interested (sinful 83); the data shows causes such as preference/jealousy (Gen 29:31; 37:4) and prior violation turning to revulsion (2Sa 13:15 Amnon). **SILENT** on adversarial-spirit interference (none tagged for C-A).

**T1.7.3** — **SILENT** — the lexical does not isolate a "present-but-not-taken-up" inner state for C-A.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Per verse, seat is named in 23 of 175: **soul 16, heart 8, flesh 1** (the rest unseated). Soul-engagement leads (e.g. Isa 1:14 "my soul hates").

**T2.1.2** — The pattern indicates aversive affect is **seated more in the soul than the heart**, with the body engaged only marginally (1 flesh). 152 unseated occurrences show the term often names the act without locating it. It never engages spirit, mind, or conscience explicitly.

**T2.10.1** — Movement: **received-from-outside** is tagged in 3 occurrences (origin), suggesting hatred can come *onto* a person; otherwise no constitutional-level movement (spirit→soul→body) is tagged. Mostly **none**.

**T2.7.1** — **SILENT** — with only 1 flesh-link and no direction tagged, body-direction is not evidenced for C-A.

**T2.9.1** — Origin: mostly **not stated** (172); **received-from-outside** in 3. Within-person generation is implied by cause cases (jealousy, Gen 29:31) but not tagged as an origin field — labelled inferential.

**T2.9.2** — Origin is **single where tagged** (received-from-outside) and otherwise unstated; it does not visibly change with context in the tagged data.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1 God→Human — T4.1.1** — **Yes:** divine **agent** in 15 occ — God's hatred directed at evil, false worship, and (Mal 1:3) Esau. **T4.1.2** — Basis is **responsive/covenantal** where shown: God hates the corrupted worship his people bring (Isa 1:14), the things that are evil (Pro 6:16) — a response to evil, not arbitrary. **T4.1.3** — It shows God's disposition as **opposed to evil** — his hatred is the obverse of his holiness.

**T4.2 Human→God — T4.2.1** — **Yes (as its inverse):** 10 occ have God as object of human hatred ("those who hate the LORD/you", Psa 68:1; 81:15; 83:2; 139:21). This is the negative pole of the human-to-God interface — enmity rather than seeking. **T4.2.2** — The posture is rejection/opposition (no inner posture of seeking is shown). **T4.2.3** — It shows a relationship of **enmity** with God in those who hate him; Psa 139:21 shows the righteous counter-aligning with God *against* such haters.

**T4.3 Human→Giving — T4.3.1** — **Yes:** person-to-person hatred is the largest object class (person 75) — Esau/Jacob, Joseph's brothers (Gen 37:4), Amnon (2Sa 13:15). It operates as withdrawal of love/blessing toward the other. **T4.3.2 / T4.3.3** — **SILENT** — the lexical does not specify giver inner-conditions for hatred (these are framed for positive characteristics).

**T4.4 Human→Receiving — T4.4.1** — Inferential: the **received-from-outside** origin (3 occ) and the "hated wife/son" law (Deu 21:16) show hatred *landing on* a person from another. **T4.4.2 / T4.4.3** — **SILENT** (no uptake-condition data).

**T4.5 Boundaries — T4.5.1** — Evidence shows hatred operating **within existing bonds** (brothers: Gen 37:4; family: 2Sa 13; co-wives: Gen 29) and across them; the most acute cases are *within* kinship bonds. **T4.5.2** — Crosses covenantal boundaries (hatred of Israel's enemies and of God's enemies, Psa 83:2). **T4.5.3** — Relational scope is broad: kin, community, enemies, and God.

**T4.6 Spiritual Beings — all:** **SILENT** — no angelic/adversarial-being interface tagged for C-A (object_type spiritual-being appears once in the cluster, not in C-A).

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**T6.1.1** — Adjacent characteristics co-occurring in C-A verses (non-M06 clusters): **M44 (17), M15 wisdom (16), M25 (16), M47 (15), M10b (12), M05 love (12), M10 (11), M14 (11), M26 (10), M23 (9)**, with M03 grief, M04 joy, M01 fear, M02 anger also present. Within M06, C-A co-occurs with C-C (3), C-B (2), C-F (1), C-D (1). **T6.1.2** — The strong co-occurrence with **love (M05)** and **wisdom (M15)** places aversive affect on the love↔hate axis and within the wisdom literature's moral evaluation of hatred.

**T6.2.1** — Sequence: love/preference reliably **precedes** the hatred in the narrative cases (Gen 29:31; 37:4). **T6.2.2** — The relationship reads as **causal** there (greater love for one produces hatred of the other).

**T6.3.1** — Produces: ruin/shame in the object (Pro 26:28; 13:5) — but these are effects on the *other*, not another inner characteristic of the hater; **largely SILENT** on producing another characteristic. **T6.3.2** — Produced by love-preference/jealousy in the cause cases (inferential link to M05 / jealousy). **T6.3.3** — Inferential: science (T7.3) treats contempt (C-B) as a *constituent* of hate (Sternberg) — so C-B may be a constituent of C-A; flagged as inferential, to test in consolidation.

**T6.4.1** — Vocabulary sharing: the `sa.ne` root generates the standard OT word for **enemy** ("the one who hates," `sone'`), shared with the enmity field (C-E) and with relational-opposition contexts across the programme. **T6.4.2** — Root-level: yes — `sa.ne` → both the verb "hate" and the participle "enemy/hater," a single root generating affect (C-A) and adversary (C-E) senses. **T6.4.3** — The sharing shows aversive affect and adversarial enmity are **etymologically linked** — the felt hatred and the relational opponent issue from one root.

**T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour: **C-B (appraisive contempt).** Distinction: C-A is `affect`-led (felt rejection) while C-B is `moral_evaluation`-led (a verdict of worthlessness); C-A is **bivalent** (commanded hate of evil exists), C-B has **no righteous seam**. **T6.5.2** — The boundary: where the inner movement is *feeling-with-appraisal* it is C-A; where it is *appraisal-of-worth without the affective tag* it is C-B (na.ats/ba.zah are faculty-absent acts). **T6.5.3** — A distinction of **kind** (faculty: affect vs evaluation), reinforced by valence (bivalent vs uniformly sinful).

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms: `sa.ne` "to hate" (143), `sin.ah` "hatred" (19), `sa.tam` "harbour animosity" (8), Aramaic `se.na` (2). Root sense: settled aversion/rejection of an object.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **verb** (Qal `sa.ne`, action 153), **noun** (`sin.ah`, status 19), **adjective/passive participle** (`sa.ni` "hated", 1). The range shows hatred as **act, standing condition, and ascribed status**.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from interpersonal aversion (Gen 37:4) through harboured grudge (`sa.tam`) to morally-charged hatred of evil/falsehood (Psa 119) and divine hatred (Mal 1:3) — a breadth spanning private feeling to covenantal-theological judgement.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — the vocabulary distinguishes **act** (`sa.ne`) from **condition** (`sin.ah`) and from **harboured/persistent** animosity (`sa.tam`). Disposition-vs-act is lexically marked.

**T7.1.5** — Structural-opposite term: **love** (`'ahav`, M05) — not within the C-A set itself but its evidenced antonym (Gen 29:31 contrast; Mal 1:2–3 love/hate pairing). No dedicated "absence-of-hate" term in the set.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **yes** — the `sa.ne` participle "the one who hates / enemy" (`sone'`), the standard OT word for a personal enemy (e.g. "those who hate him", Gen 24:60; "those who hate you", Psa 139:21).

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: **none** — hatred is not sought from another; no such term in the set.

**T7.1.8** — OT↔NT: C-A is **OT-only** (all 175 OT); the Aramaic `se.na` (Daniel-era) is the only non-Hebrew form. NT continuity of the *affect* would be carried by Greek `miseō` (outside this M06 focus set) — **labelled inferential / outside-extract**.

**T7.1.9** — Newly-coined NT term: **none** in the C-A set (no NT terms).

**T7.1.10** — The full vocabulary arc shows aversive affect operating across **private emotion, persistent disposition, moral judgement, and divine attribute** — the widest valence range in the cluster.

**T7.2.1** — Function of the primary term: typically the **finite verb governing the clause** (Qal `sa.ne`), with the hater as subject and the hated as object (Gen 37:4 "his brothers… hated him").

**T7.2.2** — Literary form (by book): **psalm 49, wisdom 41, law/narrative 40, prophecy 24, narrative-history 19**, with 2 apocalyptic. Responsible interpretation must weight the **psalmic and wisdom** settings most — where hatred is morally evaluated (Psalms 119/139; Proverbs 8) rather than merely narrated.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure (representative): in the wisdom/psalm material the argument runs *the fear of the LORD entails hatred of evil* (Pro 8:13), and *alignment with God entails hatred of his enemies* (Psa 139:21–22) — premise (love of God/good) → conclusion (hatred of evil).

**T7.2.4** — Contextual settings: **covenantal** (Mal 1:2–3; Deu), **liturgical/devotional** (Psalms), **judicial** (Exo 18:21 hating dishonest gain), and **familial-narrative** (Gen 29/37; 2Sa 13).

**T7.2.5** — Primary anchor (candidate, **authorial judgement**): **Psa 139:21–22** — "Do I not hate those who hate you, O LORD?… I hate them with complete hatred." It fuses affect (`sin.ah`), the cognitive-moral alignment, the God-interface (both human→God and counter-hatred), and the bivalence in a single text.

**T7.2.6** — That anchor shows what no other C-A verse shows together: **righteous hatred explicitly aligned to God's own**, naming the affect, its object, its moral warrant, and its directedness in one place.

**T7.3.1** — Human-science field (from science extract §1): **social psychology / affective science** — hatred as a sustained, dispositional negative orientation (distinct from reactive anger).

**T7.3.2** — Where science illuminates: **Sternberg's duplex theory** (negation of intimacy + passion + devaluation) coheres with the lexical finding that `sa.ne` fuses **affect + cognition** — the "devaluation/diminution" component matches the cognitive co-tag, and the affective component matches the universal affect tag. Hatred-as-stable-orientation matches the `sin.ah`/`sa.tam` standing-state forms.

**T7.3.3** — Divergence: science treats hatred as **pathological, to be reduced**; the biblical evidence includes a **commanded/righteous hatred of evil** (22 occ) and **divine hatred** (15 agent) that science has no normative framework to evaluate. This is the central divergence for C-A.

**T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced: science's reactive-vs-dispositional distinction prompts checking whether C-A's hatred is **situationally triggered or characterologically settled** — the lexical leans settled (status/grudge forms), which the per-verse cause data (Gen 29; 2Sa 13) only partly qualifies; worth a closer verse pass on the episodic cases.
