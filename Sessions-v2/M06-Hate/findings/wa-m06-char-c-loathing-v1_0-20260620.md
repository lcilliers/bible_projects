## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

**Evidence correction to the v1_0 label.** `faculty` is **absent on all 29 occurrences**. This characteristic is *not* a distinctly-tagged interior faculty in the extract. Lexically it is two things:

1. the **act of treating-as-detestable** — `ta.av` "abhor/detest" (21) and `bdelussomai` "abhor" (2), action 23; and
2. the **ritual/category noun for a detestable thing** — `she.qets` "detestation" (4) and `de.ra.on` "abhorrence" (2), status 6.

The "visceral repugnance (interior)" framing from v1_0 is only partly supported. The **only somatic signal is the flesh seat on 3 occurrences** (`she.qets` ×2, `de.ra.on` ×1), and these sit with the **forbidden** ritual valence (the Levitical unclean categories, e.g. unclean creatures that "are detestable"). The interior/visceral reading rests on those 3 flesh cases, not on a faculty tag.

Like C-A, it carries a **small bivalent seam**: `ta.av` abhorring *evil* reads commanded/righteous (1 each), while the detestable *thing* reads forbidden (3).

**Distinctness from C-B is real on the evidence:** C-B's `ba.ash` is `moral_evaluation` + unseated (an appraisal); C-C is faculty-absent + the **only flesh-seating set** in the cluster (the ritual/somatic detestable). Seat and faculty keep them apart, so `ba.ash` stays in C-B, not here.
