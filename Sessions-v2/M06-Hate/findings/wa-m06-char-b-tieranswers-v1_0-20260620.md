## T0 ‚Äî Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** ‚Äî God-relation present in ~18 occ: God as **object** of contempt in 15 (Num 16:30; 2Sa 12:14; Psa 10:3,13; 74:10; Isa 1:4; Jer 23:17; Eze 28:24,26 ‚Äî spurning/despising the LORD/the Holy One), God as **agent** in 3 (Deu 32:19; Lam 2:6; Mal 2:9 ‚Äî God's judicial rejection).

**T0.1.2** ‚Äî Predominantly **borne by the creature, not God**: contempt is overwhelmingly a human posture *toward* God (object 15) and only marginally something God does (agent 3, and there judicial). Unlike C-A (substantial divine hatred), appraisive contempt is **not a divine attribute** but a creaturely act ‚Äî its rare divine cases are God *rejecting* the unfaithful, not devaluing worth.

**T0.2.1** ‚Äî No created purpose stated. Where an effect appears (immediate_response 5): contempt issues in the servant being "despised" (Isa 53:3) and in disbelief (Num 14:11).

**T0.2.2** ‚Äî Reads as the **fallen condition**: uniformly sinful (47) / neutral (16) with **no righteous or commanded seam at all** ‚Äî there is no rightly-ordered contempt in the evidence.

**T0.2.3** ‚Äî **SILENT** ‚Äî no future-fullness orientation tagged.

**T0.3.1** ‚Äî Inferential: contempt instantiates a **disordering** of the image ‚Äî the devaluing of one made in God's image ‚Äî rather than any divine likeness. Recorded as inferential.

**T0.3.2** ‚Äî **Exclusively creaturely** in practice: the human despising of God/neighbour has no positive divine analogue here (God's 3 agent cases are judicial rejection, not worth-devaluation).

**T0.3.3** ‚Äî Inferential: contempt directed at God (Psa 10:13 "the wicked renounces/spurns God") indicates a damaged image-relation in the contemnor; contempt suffered (Isa 53:3) indicates nothing defective in the *object*.

**T0.4.1** ‚Äî **Isa 53:3** (`ba.zah`, the servant "despised and rejected") functions **typologically/christologically** ‚Äî the human pointing toward the suffering of the Messiah; direction: human contempt establishes the pattern the servant bears. Labelled as the one tagged typological case.

## T1 ‚Äî Definition

**T1.1.1** ‚Äî Programme name: *appraisive contempt* ‚Äî a downward judgement of worth, treating-as-worthless. The name signals an **evaluative** inner movement (a verdict), not primarily a felt emotion.

**T1.1.2** ‚Äî Hebrew `na.ats` "spurn", `ba.zah` "despise" (acts); `buz` "contempt", `ne.a.tsah`, `she.at`, `biz.za.von` (the verdict-nouns); `ba.ash` "be/become odious" (the object-side). Definitionally: an **act of spurning** plus a **standing verdict of worthlessness**.

**T1.1.3** ‚Äî The name carries a **hierarchical/relational** implication: contempt places its object *below* the contemnor on a scale of worth (cf. science ¬ß2, Gottman).

**T1.2.1** ‚Äî A **condition/status verdict** (`buz`, status 20) expressed through **acts** (`na.ats`/`ba.zah`, action 49). Two-register: verdict + act.

**T1.2.2** ‚Äî **Compound/two-register:** an interior `moral_evaluation` verdict (buz, ba.ash ‚Äî 19) plus faculty-absent **acts** of spurning (na.ats, ba.zah ‚Äî 47). `ba.ash` adds an **object-side** element ("becoming odious"), the converse pole of actively despising.

**T1.3.1** ‚Äî Structural opposite: **honour / esteem** (`kabod`; the honour cluster). `ba.zah` "despise" is the standard antonym of `kabad` "to honour"; Isa 53:3 "we esteemed him not" names the absence of esteem.

**T1.3.2** ‚Äî At its edge it excludes esteem, regard, and acknowledgement of worth.

**T1.3.3** ‚Äî It is *not* felt hatred (C-A): contempt can be **cold** (no affect tag on na.ats/ba.zah/buz). It ends where the affective rejection (C-A) or the visceral repugnance (C-C, flesh-seated) begins.

**T1.4.1** ‚Äî Mode: realised as Qal verbs of spurning/despising (action 49) and as verdict-nouns (status 20). The `mode` field is empty cluster-wide. Manner: an **evaluative downgrading** of the object.

**T1.4.2** ‚Äî Varies by **object** more than by level: contempt of God (Num 16:30), of persons (Isa 53:3), of the offering/sacred (1Sa 2:17), and self-rendered-odious (`ba.ash`, 2Sa 16:21). No constitutional-level variation tagged.

**T1.4.3** ‚Äî Inferential/partial: `na.ats` of the LORD's word (Num 15:31) operates against a *spoken* command (despising the commandment); not itself a speech-act of the contemnor in most cases. Mostly **none**.

**T1.5.1** ‚Äî Immediate response (5 occ): "despised" outcome (Isa 53:3), disbelief (Num 14:11), war/escalation (1Ch 19:6). Most (64) tag none.

**T1.5.2** ‚Äî **Varied** ‚Äî no consistent immediate response.

**T1.6.1** ‚Äî Sustained effect: the **standing verdict** itself (buz as a status); `ba.ash`'s "becoming a stench" (2Sa 10:6) is a durable spoiled standing. Otherwise sparse.

**T1.6.3** ‚Äî The sustained effect is the **fixed low valuation / spoiled standing**; the immediate response is its episodic expression (an act of spurning, a refusal to believe).

**T1.7.1** ‚Äî **SILENT** ‚Äî no "right operation" conditions, since contempt has no righteous seam here.

**T1.7.2** ‚Äî Distortion conditions: contempt is occasioned by sin and presumption ‚Äî David's deed giving cause to despise the LORD (2Sa 12:14), breaking the commandment (Num 15:31), pride (Eze 28). No adversarial-spirit tag.

**T1.7.3** ‚Äî **SILENT**.

## T7 ‚Äî Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** ‚Äî Primary terms: `na.ats` "spurn" (25), `ba.zah` "despise" (13), `buz` "contempt" (11). Root sense: to treat/regard as of no account.

**T7.1.2** ‚Äî Grammatical range: **verb** (na.ats, ba.zah, ba.ash ‚Äî action 49), **noun** (buz, ne.a.tsah, she.at, biz.za.von ‚Äî status 20). The range shows contempt as **both the act of spurning and the standing verdict**.

**T7.1.3** ‚Äî Semantic range: from spurning a command/sacred thing (Num 15:31; 1Sa 2:17), through despising a person (Isa 53:3), to becoming-odious (`ba.ash`, 2Sa 16:21) and nominal contempt/scorn (`buz`, Job 12:5).

**T7.1.4** ‚Äî Yes ‚Äî disposition/verdict (`buz` noun) vs act (`na.ats`/`ba.zah` verb) vs object-side condition (`ba.ash` "be odious"). Three aspects marked.

**T7.1.5** ‚Äî Opposite/absence term: **honour/esteem** (`kabad`/`kabod`) ‚Äî the standard antonym of `ba.zah`; "we esteemed him not" (Isa 53:3) names its absence.

**T7.1.6** ‚Äî Person-type term: **partial** ‚Äî `ba.zah`'s passive "the despised one" names the *object* (Isa 53:3); no dedicated agent-noun for "one who habitually despises" in the set.

**T7.1.7** ‚Äî Supplication/seeking term: **none**.

**T7.1.8** ‚Äî OT‚ÜîNT: C-B is **OT-only**; NT continuity of contempt (Gk `exouthene≈ç`, `kataphrone≈ç`) is **outside this focus set** ‚Äî labelled inferential.

**T7.1.9** ‚Äî Newly-coined NT term: **none** in set.

**T7.1.10** ‚Äî The vocabulary arc shows contempt operating across **cultic, royal, prophetic, and personal** registers ‚Äî always a downward worth-verdict, never positively valued.

**T7.2.1** ‚Äî Function: the primary verb governs the clause with the contemnor as subject and the despised as object (Isa 53:3 "he was despised", passive ‚Äî the object foregrounded).

**T7.2.2** ‚Äî Literary form: **prophecy 24, psalm 12, narrative-history 11, wisdom 11, law/narrative 9**. The prophetic and psalmic weighting requires reading contempt within **covenant-lawsuit and lament** settings (despising God / being despised before enemies).

**T7.2.3** ‚Äî Logical structure (representative): *because* the offender has spurned the LORD's word/deed, judgement follows (Num 15:31; 2Sa 12:14) ‚Äî offence (contempt of God) ‚Üí consequence.

**T7.2.4** ‚Äî Settings: **covenantal/judicial** (Num 15:31; Deu 32:19), **liturgical-lament** (Psa 10; 74; 123), **royal** (Eze 28), **servant-prophecy** (Isa 53).

**T7.2.5** ‚Äî Primary anchor (candidate, **authorial judgement**): **Isa 53:3** ‚Äî "He was despised and rejected by men‚Ä¶ and we esteemed him not." It states contempt's essence (a worth-verdict, `ba.zah`), its relational and public character, its opposite (esteem), and its typological reach in one verse.

**T7.2.6** ‚Äî That anchor shows what no other C-B verse shows: contempt **borne by the innocent/righteous one** ‚Äî separating the verdict from any actual unworthiness in its object, and carrying the christological pattern.

**T7.3.1** ‚Äî Field (science ¬ß2): **moral psychology / emotion research** ‚Äî contempt and disgust.

**T7.3.2** ‚Äî Where science illuminates: **Gottman** names contempt the most destructive relational emotion ‚Äî communicating the other as fundamentally inferior ‚Äî which matches C-B's `moral_evaluation` verdict and its relational object-class (person 22). **Haidt/Rozin** disgust illuminates the `ba.ash` "stink/odious" register as olfactory-disgust extended into the moral domain.

**T7.3.3** ‚Äî Divergence: science treats contempt as **dysfunctional/to-be-reduced**; the biblical evidence frames the gravest cases as **contempt of God** (object 15) ‚Äî a covenant-theological category science does not evaluate. (Note: unlike C-A, C-B has *no* "appropriate contempt" seam, so the divergence is narrower than for hatred.)

**T7.3.4** ‚Äî Gap surfaced: science's **contempt-vs-disgust distinction** prompts disentangling C-B (cold contempt) from C-C (visceral loathing) ‚Äî which the lexical already separates by faculty/seat; the `ba.ash` case sits on the seam and merits a verse-level check.
