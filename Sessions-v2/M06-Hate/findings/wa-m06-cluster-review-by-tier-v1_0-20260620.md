## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**Cluster finding.** M06 displays a clear **divine-instance gradient**. At one end, **hatred and loathing *of evil* are borne by God himself** (C-A divine agent ×15; C-C divine agent ×3) — these are holy aversions with a created-design pole. At the other end, **enmity (C-E) and cruelty (C-F) have no divine instance at all** — purely creaturely, wholly fallen. Between them, **contempt (C-B)** and **reproach (C-D)** are chiefly things done *to* God (object ×15 and ×18) which God answers — God as the one despised/defied, and (C-D) the one who lifts disgrace.

**Per-characteristic contribution.**
- **C-A** — the cluster's strongest image-bearing case: God hates evil/false-worship (Pro 6:16; Isa 1:14; Mal 1:3), so *hatred of evil* is a shared, valence-differentiated likeness. Typology: Mal 1:2–3 (election).
- **C-C** — divine abhorrence of bloodshed/pride (Psa 5:6; Amo 6:8); the **only characteristic with an eschatological T0.2.3** (everlasting abhorrence, Dan 12:2). Typology: de.ra.on (final judgement).
- **C-D** — God is the defied object (Goliath/Rabshakeh) *and* the redemptive remover of reproach (Jos 5:9; Zep 3:18); eschatological removal of shame. Typology: Psa 69 (reproach borne for God / christological).
- **C-B** — predominantly creaturely contempt *of* God (Num 16:30; Psa 10:13); God's agency is rare and **judicial** (Deu 32:19). Typology: Isa 53:3 (the despised servant).
- **C-E** — **silent**: no God-relation tagged (the covenant-court adversary register sits outside the extract).
- **C-F** — **the sharpest negative finding**: zero divine involvement — cruelty has **no holy analogue**, the only such characteristic in M06.

**Pattern.** The "holy end" of M06 is *aversion/loathing of evil*; the "fallen end" is *enmity/cruelty*; *contempt/reproach* are the provocations against God. Created-design vs fallen (T0.2.2) tracks the bivalence gradient (below).

## T1 — Definition

**Cluster finding.** Across the six, M06 instantiates **the full range of inner-being phenomenon-kinds**: an **act** (C-A `sa.ne`), a **verdict** (C-B `buz`), a **condition** (C-D `cher.pah`), a **visceral/ritual repudiation** (C-C `ta.av`/`she.qets`), a **relation** (C-E `antidikos`), and a **character quality** (C-F `a.rits`). The cluster is not one kind of thing negatively valenced — it is the negative pole expressed in act, verdict, condition, relation, and disposition.

**Per-characteristic kind (T1.2.1) and compound structure (T1.2.2).**
- **C-A** act+status; compound **affect+cognition** (felt + appraised).
- **C-B** verdict (status) + act; two-register (moral_evaluation verdict + faculty-absent acts + `ba.ash` object-side).
- **C-C** act + object-noun; dual **ritual/somatic + moral** register.
- **C-D** condition + act; **affect-condition (cher.pah) + faculty-absent act (cha.raph)** — the clearest condition/act pairing.
- **C-E** relation (status); **simple**.
- **C-F** quality; quality + partial-affect (`a.rits` ferocity).

**Structural opposites (T1.3.1) — the positive characteristics M06 opposes.** C-A↔**love** (M05); C-B↔**honour/esteem**; C-C↔**delight/clean**; C-D↔**honour/glory**; C-E↔**friendship/peace**; C-F↔**mercy/compassion**. *Consolidation point:* M06 is the systematic negative of **love, honour, delight, peace, and mercy** — a multi-front "anti" cluster, not the inverse of a single positive.

**Speech-mode (T1.4.3).** Strongest in **C-D** (taunt is intrinsically a speech-act); present as *commanded* speech in C-A and C-C (hate/detest evil); as insolent mistreatment in C-F (`hubrizō`); largely absent in C-B and C-E.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**Cluster finding.** When seated at all, M06 sits in **soul and heart**; **flesh is engaged only by C-C** (the ritually detestable); **spirit, mind, and conscience are never engaged** anywhere in the cluster. A large share is **unseated** — the outward/relational/dispositional terms (C-D social, C-E entirely, C-F mostly).

**Per-characteristic seat.**
- **C-A** soul (16) > heart (8) > flesh (1) — soul-leaning; the deepest-seated.
- **C-B** soul (7) ≈ heart (6); never flesh — cognitive/evaluative.
- **C-C** **flesh (3)** + soul (3) + heart (2) — the **only flesh-seating set** (ritual detestable); body link **indicative**.
- **C-D** heart (5) > soul (3); 103 unseated — **social/relational** location (science §4).
- **C-E** **entirely unseated** — a relation, not a located state.
- **C-F** soul (4) + flesh (1); 34 unseated — a disposition.

**Origin/movement (T2.9–T2.10).** Mostly unstated; **received-from-outside** appears in C-A (3), C-D (2), C-C (1), C-E (1) — hatred and reproach can arrive *onto* a person from another. No spirit→soul→body movement tagged.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**Cluster finding (the major T3 result).** M06 engages a **narrow faculty band**: only **affect**, **cognition** (as appraisal), and **moral_evaluation** are tagged anywhere. **Eight of the eleven faculties are never engaged across the whole cluster** — perception, memory, creativity, volition, agency, conscience, conscientiousness, and relational-capacity are *silent at the tag level* in all 431 occurrences. Where these faculties are arguably implicated (volition in commanded hatred; relational-capacity throughout; conscience-suppression in cruelty), the connection is **inferential**, not tagged.

**Faculty-depth ordering across the six:**
1. **C-A** — affect (175, all) **+ cognition** (143) — the deepest interior engagement; hatred as feeling-fused-with-judgement.
2. **C-B** — **moral_evaluation** (19) — the evaluative faculty; contempt as a worth-verdict (only 3 affect).
3. **C-D** — **affect** on the borne-disgrace pole (`cher.pah` 72); the act-pole (`cha.raph`) is faculty-absent.
4. **C-F** — **partial affect** (`a.rits` 21, ferocity); the `akhzar`-family faculty-absent.
5. **C-C** — **no faculty** (act/category); interior signal only via 3 flesh-seats.
6. **C-E** — **no faculty** (relation); blanket silence across all 33 T3 questions.

*Consolidation point:* the inner/outer boundary does **not** run between characteristics; it runs **through** them. C-B (verdict + acts), C-D (felt disgrace + taunt), and C-F (ferocity + named trait) each carry an interior pole and a faculty-absent outer pole. Any "interior vs expression" modelling must operate *within* characteristics, not by sorting whole characteristics to one side (this is the evidence's answer to the earlier IC3).

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**Cluster finding.** M06 is intensely relational, and its God-interface is **asymmetric and direction-specific**:
- **God → human** (T4.1): God hates evil (C-A 15), abhors evil (C-C 3), removes/imposes reproach (C-D 9), judicially rejects (C-B 3). **C-E, C-F: none.**
- **Human → God** (T4.2): the dominant negative vectors — **despising God** (C-B 15), **taunting/defying God** (C-D 18), **hating God** (C-A 10). **C-C, C-E, C-F: none.** Human hostility *toward God* is carried by contempt, reproach, and hatred.
- **Human → human** (T4.3–T4.5): person-directed in every characteristic — C-A (75), C-D (27), C-B (22), C-C (7), C-E (3), C-F (acts on victims). Reproach is notably **borne/received** (C-D self 17); cruelty is **suffered** by victims (C-F).
- **Spiritual beings** (T4.6): only **C-C** (object spiritual-being ×1 — idols/false worship) and **C-E** (`antidikos` cosmic-adversary sense — inferential flag, untagged). Otherwise silent.

**Per-characteristic vector summary.** C-A both God-directions + the person mass; C-B human→God dominant; C-C God→human + the spiritual-being case; C-D both God-directions + the borne-disgrace; C-E person-role only (no God); C-F victims only (no God).

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**Cluster finding.** T5 is the cluster's **thinnest tier** — the lexical extract is per-occurrence and carries little transformation/sanctification data, so most T5 questions are **SILENT** across all six characteristics. Three threads carry real evidence:
- **Eschatology (T5.6):** **C-C** (everlasting abhorrence, Dan 12:2) and **C-D** (removal of reproach at restoration, Joe 2:19; Zep 3:18) — the cluster's only eschatological orientations.
- **Suffering (T5.4):** **C-D** — reproach *is* borne social suffering (Psa 69; Lam 3:61); **C-F** — cruelty is the *agent* of affliction (Luk 18:32; Act 14:5; 1Th 2:2). Reproach is suffering *received*; cruelty is suffering *inflicted*.
- **Mechanism of change (T5.3):** only **C-D** shows one — divine intervention *removing* reproach (Jos 5:9).

**Per-characteristic.** C-A, C-B mostly silent (a few eliciting sequences only); C-E entirely silent (no developmental dimension — consistent with its role-only nature); C-C, C-D, C-F carry the eschatology/suffering content above.

*Gap note:* T5's heavy silence is a **data-shape limitation** (per-occurrence lexical), not evidence of absence. A verse-narrative pass (e.g. Gen 37 Joseph; 2Sa 13 Amnon; the Saul–David arc) would likely surface developmental sequences the lexical extract cannot.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**Cluster finding (the key structural result).** The six characteristics are not merely parallel negatives — they are **compositionally related**, and the science framework (Sternberg's duplex theory, §1) maps onto the cluster's own internal structure:
- **Contempt (C-B, devaluation)** and **loathing (C-C, revulsion)** read as **constituents of hatred (C-A)** — the "devaluation" and "negation-of-intimacy/disgust" components of the duplex model. The internal co-occurrence and the faculty logic support treating C-B and C-C as **elements that can compose C-A**, not only as neighbours.
- **Enmity (C-E)** is the **relational outworking** of aversive affect — the `sa.ne` root itself generates "enemy" (`sone'`), so C-A (feeling) and C-E (relation) are **one phenomenon across two characteristics**.
- **Cruelty (C-F)** is the **dispositional end** of the same hostility continuum as C-E (relation vs character-trait).
- **Reproach (C-D)** is the **public voicing** of contempt — the strongest internal co-occurrence is **C-B↔C-D (5)** (verdict → taunt).

**Vocabulary/root sharing (T6.4).** `sa.ne` → "enemy" links **C-A↔C-E**; `ba.ash`/`ta.av` disgust-register links **C-B↔C-C**; `cher.pah` honour–shame links **C-D** to the honour cluster.

**Distinctions (T6.5) — nearest-neighbour map.**
- C-A ↔ C-B: felt rejection vs cold worth-verdict (kind: affect vs evaluation; valence: bivalent vs sinful).
- C-B ↔ C-D: inner verdict vs voiced/borne disgrace (mode).
- C-A ↔ C-C: affective vs visceral/ritual; soul vs flesh (constitutional level).
- C-E ↔ C-F: relation vs disposition (kind).
- C-E ↔ C-A: relation vs feeling (the enemy concept shared).

**External clusters.** **Love (M05)** recurs across C-A/C-D/C-F (the love↔hate axis, and love as a *cause* of hate); **wisdom (M15)** frames the moral evaluation of hatred and cruelty; **grief (M03), anger (M02), fear (M01)** cluster around the *reproached/hated person's* experience (esp. C-D).

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**Cluster finding.** M06 is **OT-weighted**: of 34 terms, NT vocabulary appears only in **C-C** (`bdelussomai`), **C-E** (`antidikos`), and **C-F** (`hubrizō`, `afilagathos`). C-A, C-B, and C-D — the cluster's core mass — are **OT-only**, so a cross-testament characterisation rests heavily on the OT, with NT continuity carried mainly by the abhorrence line (`ta.av`→`bdelussomai`).

**Lexical/semantic (T7.1).**
- *Grammatical spread across the cluster:* verbs (acts), nouns (verdicts/conditions), adjectives (qualities), participles (person-types) — the full range.
- *Person-type terms (T7.1.6):* strongest in **C-F** (`a.rits`/`akhzari` "the ruthless/cruel one"), **C-E** (`antidikos`/`yariv` "adversary/opponent"), and **C-A** (`sone'` "the hater/enemy"). C-B/C-C/C-D lack clean agent person-types.
- *Opposites (T7.1.5):* love, honour, delight/clean, glory, peace, mercy (see T1).
- *OT↔NT continuity (T7.1.8):* clean only for **C-C** (`ta.av`→`bdelussomai`); the affect (`miseō`), contempt (`kataphroneō`), and reproach (`oneidismos`) continuations lie **outside the focus set** — flagged for any cross-testament claim.
- *NT coinage (T7.1.9):* **C-F** `afilagathos` ("hating good", 2Ti 3:3) — cruelty conceptualised as privation of love-of-good.

**Anchor verses (T7.2.5; authorial-judgement candidates).** C-A **Psa 139:21–22** (righteous hatred aligned to God's) · C-B **Isa 53:3** (the despised servant) · C-C **Deu 7:26** (commanded detestation; Lev 11 ritual / Amo 6:8 divine) · C-D **Psa 69** (reproach borne for God) · C-E **Exo 23:22** (enmity within God's covenant defence) · C-F **Pro 12:10** (cruelty as the wicked's disposition).

**Literary form (T7.2.2).** The cluster lives chiefly in **psalm, wisdom, and prophecy**, with narrative carrying the personal cases. The **psalmic/lament** weighting is decisive for C-A and C-D (hatred and reproach as the cry of the afflicted before God); **wisdom** for the moral evaluation (C-A, C-B, C-F); **Levitical law + apocalyptic** uniquely for C-C.

**Human-science (T7.3).** The science review's four sections map onto the six characteristics and illuminate them as follows:
- **§1 (hate) → C-A:** Sternberg's duplex theory (devaluation + disgust + passion) coheres with C-A's affect+cognition fusion *and* with the T6 finding that C-B/C-C are its constituents.
- **§2 (contempt/disgust) → C-B + C-C:** Gottman (contempt as the most relationship-destructive emotion) → C-B; Haidt/Rozin (bodily→moral disgust) → C-C's flesh-seated ritual + moral registers.
- **§3 (hostility/cruelty) → C-E + C-F:** reactive/proactive aggression and moral disengagement (Bandura) → C-F's trait-vs-act structure and "unmoved by suffering."
- **§4 (rejection/reproach) → C-D:** Eisenberger (social pain = physical pain) coheres with `cher.pah` being **affect-tagged** — disgrace internalised as felt pain.

**Principal science divergence (T7.3.3) — cluster-level.** Science treats this whole field as **pathology to be reduced**; the biblical evidence contains **commanded/righteous hatred and abhorrence of evil** (C-A, C-C), **divine hatred/abhorrence** (C-A, C-C), and the categorical point that **cruelty alone has no divine instance** (C-F) — normative and theological categories science has no framework to evaluate.

## Consolidated open questions (carried for researcher decision)

Grounded by this tier review; these refine the earlier IC1–IC4:

1. **Compositional modelling (new, from T6).** The evidence supports treating **C-B (devaluation)** and **C-C (revulsion)** as *constituents* of **C-A (hatred)** (Sternberg), and **C-E/C-F** as the *relation/disposition* poles of hostility. Should the cluster map be **layered** (C-A as composite; C-B/C-C as its elements; C-E/C-F as hostility's relation/trait) rather than six flat parallels? (Supersedes/sharpens IC1.)
2. **C-E status (from T3/T0/T5).** C-E is silent across all of T3, all of T5, and all of T0 (no faculty, no development, no God-relation). On the evidence it is a **relational-interface layer**, not a standalone inner-being characteristic. Confirm demote-to-layer, or retain. (IC1/IC3.)
3. **Inner/outer (resolved direction, confirm).** The boundary runs *through* C-B, C-D, C-F — model an **expression sub-layer within** characteristics, not a flat split. (IC3.)
4. **Bivalence (from T0/valence).** Commanded/righteous hatred and abhorrence live only on `sa.ne`/`ta.av` (C-A, C-C). Treat as a **valence-axis** within those characteristics rather than a separate characteristic. (IC4.)
5. **Overlap-term placements (carried).** `mas.te.mah` (C-A↔C-E), `shut` (C-A↔C-B), `hubrizō` (C-F↔C-D), `sho.tet` (re-place/exclude), `sa.ni` (C-A↔borne-condition). (IC2.)
6. **NT thinness (from T7).** Cross-testament claims for C-A/C-B/C-D need terms outside the focus set (`miseō`/`kataphroneō`/`oneidismos`); flag whether to widen the NT seed before any continuity finding.
