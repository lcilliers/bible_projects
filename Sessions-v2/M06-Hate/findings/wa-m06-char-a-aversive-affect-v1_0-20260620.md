## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

The faculty signal is unanimous: **all 175 occurrences tag `affect`**, and on `sa.ne` (143) `cognition` is co-tagged in *every single occurrence* — in this corpus hatred is not raw feeling but feeling fused with appraisal: one hates what one has judged. That fusion is the defining mark of this characteristic.

Grammatically it is predominantly an **action** (153) — something a person does toward an object — with a nominal **status** form (`sin.ah`, 19) naming the standing condition of hatred itself.

When a constitutional seat is named (23 of 175; 152 unseated), it leans to the **soul (16)** over the **heart (8)**, with a single flesh case. On the evidence here, settled hatred is seated in the soul more than the heart.

The object is most often a **person (75)**, with a substantial **abstract (27)** share and **ten God-as-object** cases — hatred is directed at persons, at qualities/things, and at God.

The decisive finding is **valence**: sinful (83) leads, but a coherent **righteous (19) / commanded (3)** seam — carried *entirely by `sa.ne`* — presents hatred of evil and falsehood as a rightly-ordered orientation of the inner being; a single forbidden case marks the inverse. Aversive affect is therefore **bivalent within one root**, not a uniformly negative passion.

The **divine interface runs both ways inside this one characteristic**: God as the agent who hates (15) and God as the object hated (10).
