## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God-relation present in 3 occ, all as **agent**: God abhors (Psa 5:6 the bloodthirsty; Psa 106:40 his own heritage in judgement; Amo 6:8 "the LORD abhors the pride of Jacob"). No God-as-object cases.

**T0.1.2** — **Borne by God himself** in the abhorrence-of-evil sense: divine abhorrence (agent 3) targets bloodshed, pride, and corruption. So loathing-of-evil, like hatred-of-evil (C-A), has a **divine instance** — it is not exclusively creaturely.

**T0.2.1** — Where shown: the commanded detestation serves to **separate the person from the abominable** (Deu 7:26 "you shall utterly detest it… it is devoted to destruction"). Effect at Rev 21:8: the detestable share the fiery judgement.

**T0.2.2** — **Both.** A created-design role appears in the commanded/righteous seam (Deu 7:26 detest the devoted thing; Psa 119:163 abhor falsehood; Rom 2:22 abhor idols); a fallen instance appears in the forbidden case (Deu 23:7 "you shall not abhor" the brother-nation — wrongful loathing).

**T0.2.3** — Partial: the **eschatological** abhorrence (de.ra.on, Isa 66:24 / Dan 12:2 "everlasting abhorrence"; Rev 21:8) orients toward a final state — the only characteristic in M06 with a tagged eschatological reach.

**T0.3.1** — Inferential: rightly-directed loathing instantiates **God's repudiation of evil/uncleanness** (God abhors pride, Amo 6:8); wrongly-directed loathing (Deu 23:7) disorders it.

**T0.3.2** — **Shared** between God and person (God abhors evil; the person is commanded to detest the abominable), valence-differentiated as in C-A.

**T0.3.3** — Inferential: a person who abhors falsehood/idols (Psa 119:163; Rom 2:22) shows a rightly-ordered repudiation faculty; one who abhors the brother (Deu 23:7) shows it disordered.

**T0.4.1** — **de.ra.on** (Isa 66:24; Dan 12:2 "shame and everlasting abhorrence") is used **eschatologically** — the final state of the rejected; direction: pointing beyond the immediate to the last judgement. Tagged typological/eschatological.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: *loathing* — visceral repugnance / detestation. The name signals a **repudiating revulsion** (treating as abominable), with a bodily/ritual colouring.

**T1.1.2** — `ta.av` "abhor/detest/loathe" (the act, 21); `she.qets` "detestation", `de.ra.on` "abhorrence" (the object/condition nouns); Greek `bdelussomai` "abhor". Definitionally: to **recoil from as abominable**.

**T1.1.3** — Carries a **separation** implication — to abhor is to set apart from oneself as unclean/abominable (Deu 7:26).

**T1.2.1** — Predominantly an **act** (action 23) with a **status/object** form (`she.qets`/`de.ra.on`, status 6) naming the detestable thing/condition.

**T1.2.2** — On the evidence, **simple at the faculty level** (no faculty tag), but bears a **dual register**: a **ritual/somatic** mode (flesh-seated `she.qets`/`de.ra.on`) and a **moral** mode (`ta.av` of falsehood/idols).

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **delight / desire** (to long-for, the homonymic counter-sense) and, on the ritual axis, **clean/holy** vs detestable (Lev 11). No opposite term inside the C-C set.

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes acceptance, desire, and contact — it pushes the abominable away (Deu 7:26 separation).

**T1.3.3** — It is *not* cold contempt (C-B, evaluation/unseated): loathing carries a **visceral/somatic** edge (the only flesh-seated set) and a **ritual** register C-B lacks.

**T1.4.1** — Mode: Qal/Piel verbs of abhorring (action 23) and object-nouns (status 6). `mode` field empty. Manner: **repudiating recoil**.

**T1.4.2** — Varies by **register**: ritual/dietary (Lev 11 `she.qets`, flesh-seated) vs moral (Psa 119:163 abhor falsehood) vs eschatological (Isa 66:24 `de.ra.on`).

**T1.4.3** — Partial: the **commanded** detestation (Deu 7:26) operates through an imperative/communicative mode. Otherwise none.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response (1 occ): the detestable are cast into the fire (Rev 21:8). Otherwise none tagged.

**T1.5.2** — **SILENT** — too few tagged (1) to assess consistency.

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: a durable condition of **being-abominable / everlasting abhorrence** (de.ra.on, Dan 12:2) — a permanent repudiated state.

**T1.6.3** — The sustained effect (everlasting abhorrence) is a **fixed final condition**; the immediate response (consigned to fire) is its episodic enactment.

**T1.7.1** — Operates rightly where its object is the abominable/false (Deu 7:26; Psa 119:163; Rom 2:22) — under a condition of covenant fidelity.

**T1.7.2** — Distorted where directed at the brother (Deu 23:7 forbidden). No adversarial-spirit tag.

**T1.7.3** — **SILENT**.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms: `ta.av` "abhor/detest/loathe" (21); `she.qets` "detestation" (4); `de.ra.on` "abhorrence" (2); Gk `bdelussomai` (2). Root sense: to recoil from as abominable/unclean.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **verb** (ta.av, bdelussomai — action 23), **noun** (she.qets, de.ra.on — status 6). Range shows loathing as **the act of abhorring and the category of the abominable**.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: ritual/dietary detestable (Lev 11), moral abhorrence of falsehood/idols (Psa 119:163; Rom 2:22), divine abhorrence of evil (Amo 6:8), eschatological abhorrence (Dan 12:2). Narrow root, broad register.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — act (`ta.av`) vs object/condition (`she.qets`/`de.ra.on`). Act vs condition marked.

**T7.1.5** — Opposite/absence term: **delight/desire** (conceptual) and **clean/holy** (ritual axis); none inside the set.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **none** — `she.qets`/`de.ra.on` name the abominable *thing/condition*, not a person-type.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: **none**.

**T7.1.8** — OT↔NT: **the only M06 characteristic with NT continuity** — Hebrew `ta.av` → Greek `bdelussomai` (Rom 2:22 abhor idols; Rev 21:8 the detestable). Continuity of the **abhorrence act** across Testaments, carrying the ritual→moral→eschatological arc.

**T7.1.9** — Newly-coined NT term: **none**; `bdelussomai` is the LXX/NT counterpart, not a coinage.

**T7.1.10** — The vocabulary arc shows loathing operating from **bodily/ritual detestation through moral abhorrence to eschatological repudiation** — uniquely spanning flesh, soul, and last things.

**T7.2.1** — Function: the primary verb governs the clause with the abhorrer as subject (Amo 6:8 "the LORD abhors…"); the object-nouns function as predicate categories ("they are detestable", Lev 11:11).

**T7.2.2** — Literary form: **prophecy 10, psalm 6, law 4, wisdom 4**, plus **epistle 1 (Rom 2:22)** and **apocalyptic 1 (Rev 21:8)**. The legal (Levitical) and apocalyptic forms require reading loathing within **purity law** and **final-judgement** frames.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure: *because* the thing is devoted/abominable, the person must utterly detest and separate from it (Deu 7:26) — abomination → mandated repudiation.

**T7.2.4** — Settings: **liturgical/purity-legal** (Lev 11), **covenantal** (Deu 7; 23), **prophetic-judicial** (Amo 6:8), **eschatological** (Dan 12; Rev 21).

**T7.2.5** — Primary anchor (candidate, **authorial judgement**): **Deu 7:26** — "you shall utterly detest it and abhor it, for it is devoted to destruction." It states the act (`ta.av`), the object (the abominable), the separation purpose, and the commanded valence together. (Lev 11:11 is the ritual-category anchor; Amo 6:8 the divine-abhorrence anchor.)

**T7.2.6** — That anchor shows what others do not together: loathing as **a commanded inner posture of separation** from the abominable — the design role of the faculty made explicit.

**T7.3.1** — Field (science §2): **moral psychology / disgust research** (Haidt, Rozin).

**T7.3.2** — Where science illuminates: the **bodily-disgust → moral-disgust** model maps C-C's dual register precisely — the flesh-seated ritual detestable (`she.qets`, contamination aversion) and the moral abhorrence of falsehood/idols. The `ba.ash`/`ta.av` stink-and-abhor vocabulary reflects the olfactory-contamination basis science identifies.

**T7.3.3** — Divergence: science treats disgust as **contamination-avoidance to be habituated**; the biblical evidence includes **commanded and divine** abhorrence of evil (Deu 7:26; Amo 6:8) as normative — a category science evaluates only as adaptive/maladaptive.

**T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced: science's contempt/disgust split prompts confirming whether the cluster's "abhorrence" vocabulary is doing **disgust-work (C-C)** or **contempt-work (C-B `ba.ash`)** verse-by-verse — the seam case the discovery already flagged.
