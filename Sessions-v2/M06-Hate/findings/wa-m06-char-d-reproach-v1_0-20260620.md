## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

The faculty tags split this characteristic into **two poles with almost no overlap**:

1. **An inner affective condition** — `cher.pah` "reproach" (72), tagged `affect` + `status` on *every* occurrence. This is the **borne, felt disgrace** of being reproached. That a status-noun is affect-tagged throughout is significant for the programme's question: in this corpus, **borne disgrace is registered as an interior affective state**, not merely a social fact.
2. **An outward verbal act** — `cha.raph` "to taunt" (38, faculty-absent, action) plus `shim.tsah` "derision" (1). This is the **speech that inflicts** the disgrace.

The split is near-total (every `cher.pah` = affect/status; every `cha.raph` = faculty-absent/action), making C-D the **clearest "inner condition + outward expression" pairing** in the whole cluster.

**The act-side is strongly God-directed:** `cha.raph` has God as object (10) or addressee (2) in 12 of 38 — the taunting/defying of God (the Goliath / Rabshakeh / imprecatory-lament register). On the condition-side, `cher.pah` shows divine **agent** in 9 — reproach borne in relation to God.

**Seat** is almost entirely absent (103 of 111); the handful seated split heart/soul.

**Valence:** sinful (67) leads, but a large **neutral (30)** share reflects that much "reproach" is simply the *narrated condition* of disgrace, not itself a moral act.

## 4. Membership validation (per term)

- **`cher.pah` (H2781, ×72) — CONFIRMED core; the inner-condition pole.** `affect` + `status` on every occurrence — the felt, borne disgrace. The cluster's second-largest term after `sa.ne`.
- **`cha.raph` (H2778A, ×38) — CONFIRMED; the outward-act pole.** faculty-absent, action, near-uniformly sinful (35/38); markedly God-directed (object 10 / addressee 2).
- **`shim.tsah` (H8103, ×1) — CONFIRMED.** Nominal "derision"; faculty-absent, aligns with the expression pole.

**Inner/outer-layer flag (evidence-based, bears on IC3):** C-D is *not* a purely outward set. Its dominant member `cher.pah` is affect-tagged — the felt disgrace is genuinely interior. So if the faculty-absent terms are pushed into a separate "expression/boundary" layer, `cher.pah` is the counter-example that must stay on the interior side. Presented for your IC3 decision.
