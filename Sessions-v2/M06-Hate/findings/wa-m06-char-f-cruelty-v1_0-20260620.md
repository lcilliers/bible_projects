## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

This characteristic is dominantly a **quality / disposition** (quality 31 of 38) — a settled *trait* of a person (ruthless, cruel), not an episodic state — and is near-uniformly **sinful** (33 of 38).

The faculty signal splits: `a.rits` "ruthless" (20) is **affect**-tagged — the cluster's main cruelty term carries an *inner ferocity* — while the `akhzar`-family "cruel / cruelty" (11) is **faculty-absent**, naming the trait from outside.

**Seat** is mostly absent; a few soul (4) and one flesh case.

Two candidate members **pull elsewhere on the evidence** rather than confirming as dispositional cruelty:
- `hubrizō` (5) is an **action** ("mistreat / insult / treat insolently"), which sits closer to the insolent-speech register of **C-D** than to a character trait.
- `sho.tet` "scourge" (1) is a **status** noun naming an *instrument of affliction* (neutral valence), fitting neither the dispositional-cruelty pole nor C-D's derision cleanly.

These two are the evidence's answer to the v1_0 `sho.tet` overlap flag: it does not fit C-F well, and `hubrizō` only partly does.
