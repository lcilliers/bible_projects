## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

This is the **smallest and most relational/outer** characteristic in the cluster: 9 occurrences, **all faculty-absent, all unseated**, and predominantly **neutral** in valence (5 of 9; only 1 sinful). These terms name a **relational role or position** — the opponent (`antidikos`, `ya.riv`), one who is hostile (`a.yav`), the adversary who rises against (`qim`) — rather than an inner state or a moral act.

Because faculty is absent throughout and valence is mostly neutral, C-E engages the inner being only **by implication**: the role of "adversary" presupposes hostility, but the term itself names the standing-against, not the feeling behind it.

This is the converse of C-A: where `mas.te.mah` (kept in C-A) carried the *affect* of animosity, C-E carries the *relation* of opposition with no affect tag.
