## 3. Evidence-grounded synthesis

> _Authored from the tallies above and the appendix below; see chat/obslog for the reading. Every claim ties to a count or a cited verse._

This characteristic splits cleanly by faculty into **two registers**:

1. A nominal **verdict of worthlessness** carrying `moral_evaluation` — `buz` "contempt" (11) and `ba.ash` "be/become odious" (8), 19 occurrences. This is the interior appraisal: a judgement that something is of no worth.
2. A set of **acts of spurning/despising** that carry **no interior faculty at all** — `na.ats` (25), `ba.zah` (13), `ne.a.tsah` (5) — 47 faculty-absent occurrences. These name the outward act that *expresses* contempt rather than the appraisal itself.

So C-B straddles the inner/outer boundary in its own right: contempt is part inner verdict, part outward act of spurning.

**Grammar:** action 49 (spurn/despise) versus status 20 (the "contempt/scorn" nouns).

**The dominant relational vector is toward God.** Object_type God appears 10 times, and divine involvement is **object 15** against agent 3 — in this corpus contempt is markedly *directed at God or the holy* (the spurning of the Holy One), far more than it is something God does. This is the opposite skew to C-A, where God was as often agent as object.

**Valence is the clean contrast with C-A:** contempt is **overwhelmingly sinful (47) with no righteous or commanded seam at all**. Where aversive affect (C-A) was bivalent within a single root, appraisive contempt has no rightly-ordered use in this corpus.

**Seat:** when seated (12 of 69), soul (7) and heart (6) are near-even; `she.at` "scorn" notably seats in the soul.

- **`shut` (H7590, ×3) — REVIEW (faculty mismatch).** Sense "despise" fits contempt, but the lexical faculty is **`affect`**, not `moral_evaluation` — which would pull it toward C-A. Flagged for your placement / dual-tag call (IC2); not silently moved.
