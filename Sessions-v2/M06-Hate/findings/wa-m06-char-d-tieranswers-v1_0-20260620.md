## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God-relation present in ~22 occ: God as **object** of taunt in 18 (1Sa 17:26,36 Goliath defying "the armies of the living God"; 2Ki 19:4,16,23; 2Ch 32:17; Isa 37; Psa 74:10,18,22; 79:12; 89:51 — reproaching/defying God), God as **agent** in 9 (Jos 5:9 "I have rolled away the reproach of Egypt"; Psa 119:22; Jer 23:40; Joe 2:19; Zep 3:18 — God imposing or removing reproach), and **addressee** in 2.

**T0.1.2** — God is **the object reproached** (his name defied — Goliath, Rabshakeh) and **the agent who removes or imposes reproach** (Jos 5:9; Zep 3:18 removal; Jer 23:40 imposition). The **borne affective disgrace** (`cher.pah`, affect 72) is creaturely; what is divine is being the *target* of reproach and the *one who lifts* it. So reproach engages God relationally without the felt-disgrace being a divine attribute.

**T0.2.1** — Where shown (immediate_response 13): reproach issues in flight (Psa 31:11), being covered with shame (Psa 71:13), the enemy reviling God's name (Psa 74:18), and — positively — its **removal** upon keeping God's testimonies (Psa 119:22) and at restoration (Joe 2:19; Zep 3:18).

**T0.2.2** — Reads as the **fallen condition** in its act-pole (sinful 67 taunting) and as a **borne affliction** in its condition-pole (neutral 30, the narrated state of disgrace). Its *removal* is a redemptive act of God (Jos 5:9; Zep 3:18).

**T0.2.3** — **Yes:** an eschatological orientation toward the **removal of reproach/shame** at restoration (Joe 2:19 "never again… a reproach"; Zep 3:18–20). The future fullness is the lifting of disgrace.

**T0.3.1** — Inferential: in the act-pole, taunting **disorders** the image (defying God, degrading a neighbour); in the condition-pole, borne reproach for God's sake (Psa 69) participates in a **righteous suffering** pattern. Recorded inferential.

**T0.3.2** — The felt-disgrace is **creaturely**; God's role (reproached / remover-of-reproach) is the divine side. Not a shared attribute but a relational interface.

**T0.3.3** — Inferential: reproach borne *for God's sake* (Psa 69:7,9) indicates a rightly-aligned image bearing affliction; reproach *of God* (Goliath) indicates the reviler's disordered relation to God.

**T0.4.1** — **Psa 69** (the reproach borne for God's sake — "the reproaches of those who reproach you have fallen on me", v.9) is used **typologically/christologically** (the human pointing toward the Messiah who bears reproach; cf. its NT use). Direction: the human bearer establishes the pattern fulfilled in Christ. Labelled the principal typological case.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: *reproach / taunt / derision* — verbal hostility and the borne disgrace it inflicts. The name signals a **two-sided** reality: an outward act of degrading speech and an inward/social condition of shame.

**T1.1.2** — `cher.pah` "reproach" (the borne condition, 72); `cha.raph` "to taunt/defy" (the verbal act, 38); `shim.tsah` "derision" (1). Definitionally: the **act of degrading by speech** and the **state of being disgraced**.

**T1.1.3** — Carries a **public/relational** implication — reproach exists in the space between persons and communities (science §4), and a **directional** one (taunt is aimed *at* a target, often God).

**T1.2.1** — A **condition/status** (`cher.pah`, status 73 — the borne disgrace) *and* an **act** (`cha.raph`, action 38 — the taunt). The cluster's clearest condition+act pairing.

**T1.2.2** — **Compound:** an interior **affective condition** (cher.pah, affect 72 — felt disgrace) plus a faculty-absent **outward act** (cha.raph). The two poles are nearly disjoint in the data.

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **honour / glory** (`kabod`). Reproach is the removal/inverse of honour; its *lifting* restores honour (Jos 5:9; Zep 3:19–20).

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes honour, vindication, and good name — it degrades standing.

**T1.3.3** — It is *not* the inner worth-verdict of contempt (C-B): reproach is **expressed and borne** (verbal act + social condition), whereas contempt can remain an unexpressed evaluation. C-D begins where the verdict goes public.

**T1.4.1** — Mode per verse: `cher.pah` realised as a noun naming the condition (status 73); `cha.raph` as a Piel verb of taunting/defying (action 38). Manner: **degradation by speech** and **the bearing of disgrace**.

**T1.4.2** — Varies by **pole and target**: the act-pole targets God (18), persons (Nabal, 1Sa 25:39), and communities; the condition-pole is borne by the self (self 17) and others (44).

**T1.4.3** — **Yes — strongly communicative:** `cha.raph` is intrinsically a **speech-act** (taunting, defying, reviling — 1Sa 17:26; Psa 74:18 "reviles your name"). This is the cluster's most speech-based characteristic.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response (13 occ): flight (Psa 31:11), being covered/shamed (Psa 71:13), the enemy's reviling (Psa 74:18); and removal of reproach on covenant fidelity (Psa 119:22).

**T1.5.2** — **Varied** — responses range from flight/shame (in the reproached) to defiant reviling (in the taunter) to removal (in restoration).

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect: a durable **state of disgrace** — "everlasting reproach" (Jer 23:40), reproach "among the nations" (Psa 44:13) — a standing social condition.

**T1.6.3** — The sustained effect is the **lasting borne disgrace**; the immediate response is its episodic enactment (a taunt uttered, a flight, a shaming).

**T1.7.1** — In the condition-pole, reproach is rightly **borne** when suffered for God's sake (Psa 69:7; 119:22 reproach removed through fidelity).

**T1.7.2** — In the act-pole, taunting arises from defiance/pride (Goliath, 1Sa 17:26; Rabshakeh, 2Ki 19). No adversarial-spirit tag.

**T1.7.3** — **SILENT**.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Seat named in 8 of 111: **heart 5, soul 3** (rest unseated). Borne disgrace, when seated, sits in heart/soul.

**T2.1.2** — Pattern: reproach is **largely unseated (103)** — fitting its **social/relational** location (science §4: it exists "between persons", not as an individual inner state). The few seated cases (heart/soul) are the *felt* disgrace pole (cher.pah). Never flesh.

**T2.10.1** — **received-from-outside** tagged twice — reproach comes *onto* the person from another (consistent with its relational nature). Otherwise none.

**T2.7.1** — **SILENT** — no body link.

**T2.9.1** — Origin: **mostly not stated** (109); **received-from-outside** (2) — reproach is sourced in another's speech/action.

**T2.9.2** — Origin is **external where tagged** (from the reproacher); single, not multiple.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1 God→Human — T4.1.1** — **Yes:** God as agent **removing** reproach (Jos 5:9; Joe 2:19; Zep 3:18) and **imposing** it in judgement (Jer 23:40; Eze 22:4). **T4.1.2** — Basis **covenantal/responsive** — removal at restoration, imposition at unfaithfulness. **T4.1.3** — Shows God as the **vindicator who lifts disgrace** from his people and the judge who imposes it.

**T4.2 Human→God — T4.2.1** — **Yes — the dominant act-vector:** God as object of taunt in 18 (defying the living God — Goliath, Rabshakeh; reviling God's name — Psa 74:18). **T4.2.2** — The posture is **defiance/blasphemy** — the inverse of worship. **T4.2.3** — It shows reproach-of-God as **the gravest provocation**, which God himself answers (Psa 74:22 "arise, plead your cause").

**T4.3 Human→Giving — T4.3.1** — **Yes:** person-to-person taunting (person 27) — Nabal (1Sa 25:39), the mocking of the afflicted (Pro 17:5 "whoever mocks the poor insults his Maker"). **T4.3.2/3** — **SILENT** (no giver-condition data).

**T4.4 Human→Receiving — T4.4.1** — **Yes — strongly:** reproach is **borne/received** (self 17; received-from-outside 2) — the reproached bears disgrace from others (Neh 1:3; Psa 69; Lam 3:61). **T4.4.2/3** — The uptake is involuntary suffering; **SILENT** on conditions of refusal.

**T4.5 Boundaries — T4.5.1** — Operates within bonds (covenant community shamed before nations, Psa 44:13) and across (enemy nations taunting Israel/God). **T4.5.2** — Crosses covenantal boundaries (Gentile reproach of Israel and God; Pro 17:5 crosses to the Maker). **T4.5.3** — Scope: God, the covenant people, the afflicted, and enemies.

**T4.6 Spiritual Beings — all:** **SILENT** — no angelic/adversarial interface tagged for C-D.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — Inferential: borne reproach is a **context for** affliction that the Psalter shows being **transformed into vindication** (Psa 119:22 reproach removed; Zep 3:19–20) — but transformation of the *person* is not directly tagged. **T5.1.2** — SILENT.

**T5.2.1** — Sequence (cause→state): an offence/defiance → reproach inflicted (1Sa 17:26 defiance → taunt; Gen 34:14 → reproach). A relational before→after.

**T5.3.1** — Mechanism (where shown): **divine intervention** removes reproach (Jos 5:9; Joe 2:19) — change by encounter/restoration rather than gradual formation. **T5.3.2** — SILENT.

**T5.4.1** — **Yes:** reproach operates as **a form of affliction/suffering** — it *is* social pain borne by the afflicted (Psa 69; Lam 3:61; Neh 1:3). **T5.4.2** — The evidence shows the reproached crying out and being vindicated; suffering does not strengthen the reproach but occasions appeal to God.

**T5.5.1** — Inferential: bearing reproach for God's sake (Psa 69) participates in a **formation-through-suffering** arc; not tagged as such. Labelled inferential.

**T5.6.1** — **Yes:** an eschatological orientation toward the **final removal of reproach** (Joe 2:19; Zep 3:18–20) — restoration replaces disgrace.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**T6.1.1** — Co-occurring non-M06 clusters: **M44 (11), M07 (11), M41 (10), M02 anger (7), M25 (6), M03 grief (6), M01 fear (6), M08 (5), M22 (5), M04 joy (5), M05 love (4)**. Within M06: **C-B contempt (5)**, C-A (1), C-C (1).

**T6.1.2** — The C-B↔C-D pairing (5) confirms **contempt (verdict) and reproach (its public voicing)** as a working unit; co-occurrence with anger (M02), grief (M03), and fear (M01) reflects the *reproached person's* emotional context.

**T6.2.1** — Sequence: contempt (C-B) often **precedes/accompanies** reproach (the verdict issuing in the taunt); defiance precedes the taunt of God (1Sa 17). **T6.2.2** — The relationship reads **causal/expressive**.

**T6.3.1** — Produces: grief/shame in the reproached (co-occurrence with M03), and provokes God's vindication (Psa 74:22). **T6.3.2** — Produced by **contempt (C-B)** and by defiance/pride. **T6.3.3** — Reproach is the **outward act that expresses** the contempt-verdict — so C-D may be the *expression-constituent* paired with C-B. Flagged for consolidation.

**T6.4.1** — Vocabulary sharing: `cher.pah` shares the **honour/shame** field with the honour cluster (reproach as inverse of honour); `cha.raph` the **defiance** register with enmity (C-E). **T6.4.2** — Root-level: limited; the sharing is conceptual (shame↔honour) more than root-level. **T6.4.3** — Shows reproach sits on the **honour–shame axis**, linking M06 to the honour/glory characteristics.

**T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour: **C-B (contempt).** Distinction: C-B is the **inner worth-verdict** (moral_evaluation, often unexpressed); C-D is the **voiced/borne disgrace** (the taunt + the felt shame, affect-tagged). **T6.5.2** — Boundary: an unexpressed devaluation = C-B; a degrading speech-act or a borne social shame = C-D. **T6.5.3** — Distinction of **mode** (inner verdict vs outward expression / borne condition).

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms: `cher.pah` "reproach" (72), `cha.raph` "to taunt/defy/revile" (38). Root sense: to expose to shame / cast a taunt.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **noun** (cher.pah — the condition, status 73), **verb** (cha.raph — the act, Piel, action 38). The split itself shows the **condition vs act** structure.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from personal insult (1Sa 25:39) through national disgrace (Psa 44:13; Neh 1:3) to the defiance of God himself (1Sa 17:26; 2Ki 19) — private to cosmic-covenantal.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — **condition** (`cher.pah`) vs **act** (`cha.raph`) is the defining lexical distinction (status vs action).

**T7.1.5** — Opposite/absence term: **honour/glory** (`kabod`) — reproach is its removal; vindication (Jos 5:9; Zep 3:19) names the reversal.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **partial** — `cha.raph`'s participle "the reviler/taunter" names the agent; `cher.pah` names the borne condition, not a person-type.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: **none** (reproach is not sought; though appeal *against* reproach is frequent — Psa 119:22).

**T7.1.8** — OT↔NT: C-D is **OT-only**; its NT continuity runs through Greek `oneidismos`/`oneidizō` (reproach borne by Christ, Rom 15:3; Heb 13:13) — **outside this focus set**, labelled inferential. Psa 69's NT use is the bridge.

**T7.1.9** — Newly-coined NT term: **none** in set.

**T7.1.10** — The vocabulary arc shows reproach operating across **insult, disgrace, defiance-of-God, and (in restoration) its removal** — the cluster's most public, honour–shame-bound characteristic.

**T7.2.1** — Function: `cher.pah` typically the **predicate/object naming the state** ("we have become a reproach", Psa 44:13); `cha.raph` the **finite verb of defiance** ("he has defied the armies of the living God", 1Sa 17:36).

**T7.2.2** — Literary form: **prophecy 38, psalm 31, narrative-history 25, wisdom 8, lament 3**. The **psalmic/lament** weighting is decisive — reproach is overwhelmingly the cry of the afflicted before God.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure: in the lament material, *because* the enemy reproaches God's name and people, God is appealed to *to act* (Psa 74:18–22) — reproach (premise) → plea for vindication (conclusion).

**T7.2.4** — Settings: **liturgical-lament** (Psa 69; 74; 79), **military-covenantal** (1Sa 17; 2Ki 19 — defiance of the living God), **restoration-prophetic** (Joe 2; Zep 3).

**T7.2.5** — Primary anchor (candidate, **authorial judgement**): **Psa 69** (esp. v.7,9,19–20) — the reproach borne *for God's sake* ("for your sake I have borne reproach… the reproaches of those who reproach you have fallen on me"). It unites the felt-disgrace (`cher.pah`), the God-relation, the bearing-for-God, and the typological reach.

**T7.2.6** — That anchor shows what no other C-D verse shows together: **reproach borne vicariously for God**, fusing the interior affective disgrace with the covenant/christological pattern.

**T7.3.1** — Field (science §4): **social psychology — ostracism and rejection research** (Leary, Baumeister, Williams).

**T7.3.2** — Where science illuminates: **Eisenberger's** finding that social rejection activates physical-pain circuitry coheres with the lexical result that borne reproach (`cher.pah`) is tagged **affect** — social disgrace registered as felt pain. Reproach/taunt as **instruments of social degradation** (combining devaluation with public humiliation) matches the C-B→C-D verdict→voicing pairing.

**T7.3.3** — Divergence: science locates reproach **purely "between persons", not as an inner state** (§4); the biblical evidence **internalises** it (affect-tagged) *and* makes God a party to it (object 18 / agent 9) — reproach of and from God, which science's interpersonal frame does not contain.

**T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced: science's honour–shame-culture findings prompt reading C-D within an **honour–shame frame** explicitly, and checking whether the cluster includes **restoration/removal-of-reproach** strategies — which the data does (Jos 5:9; Zep 3), a point worth foregrounding.
