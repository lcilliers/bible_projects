## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — Programme name: *adversarial enmity* — a settled relational opposition (standing-against). The name signals a **relation/position**, not a feeling.

**T1.1.2** — `ya.riv` "opponent/one who contends", `antidikos` "opponent/adversary", `a.yav` "be hostile", `qim` "adversary/those who rise up". Definitionally: **the party that stands against another**.

**T1.1.3** — Carries a **purely relational/directional** implication — enmity is constituted by the *against*-relation.

**T1.2.1** — A **condition/status** (status 8 — the standing of being an opponent), with one **act** form (`a.yav` "be hostile", action 1).

**T1.2.2** — **Simple** — a single relational stance; no compound interior structure evidenced.

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite: **alliance / friendship / peace** (the covenant-friend vs adversary contrast). No opposite term in the set.

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes alliance, peace, and shared cause.

**T1.3.3** — It is *not* the felt hatred of C-A (no affect) nor the dispositional cruelty of C-F (no quality of character) — it is **only the relation of opposition**.

**T1.4.1** — Mode: realised as opponent-nouns (status 8) and one verb of being-hostile. Manner: **standing against**.

**T1.4.2** — Varies by **setting**: legal (the courtroom `antidikos`, the NT terms), military/national (Exo 23:22 enemies of Israel), and personal dispute (`ya.riv`, Job).

**T1.4.3** — Partial: the legal-adversary sense operates in a **judicial/communicative** setting (a case argued); not a speech-act of the term itself.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate response (1 occ): the adversaries are "cut off / consumed" (Job 22:20). **T1.5.2** — **SILENT** — too few to assess.

**T1.6.1 / T1.6.3** — **SILENT** — no sustained inner effect tagged.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms: `ya.riv` "opponent", `antidikos` "adversary/opponent", `a.yav` "be hostile", `qim` "adversary". Root sense: one who contends/stands against.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range: **noun** (the opponent — status 8) and **verb** (`a.yav` be hostile — action 1). The range shows enmity as **chiefly a named relation**, only marginally an act.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: legal opponent (`antidikos`), military/national enemy (Exo 23:22), personal contender (`ya.riv`, Job) — narrow, all within the *opposition* field.

**T7.1.4** — Partial: `ya.riv`/`antidikos` (the opponent as person) vs `a.yav` (to be hostile, the act). Person vs act marked; disposition-vs-condition not differentiated.

**T7.1.5** — Opposite term: **friend/ally / peace** — none inside the set.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type term: **yes — definitionally** — `ya.riv`, `antidikos`, `qim` *are* person-type terms (the opponent/adversary). C-E is almost entirely person-type vocabulary.

**T7.1.7** — Supplication/seeking term: **none**.

**T7.1.8** — OT↔NT: C-E is the **most NT-weighted** characteristic relative to its size (4 of 9 NT) — Greek `antidikos` continues the adversary concept (legal and cosmic). Continuity of the **opponent role** across Testaments.

**T7.1.9** — Newly-coined NT term: **none** (`antidikos` is classical Greek, not a coinage).

**T7.1.10** — The vocabulary arc shows enmity as a **stable relational position** spanning court, battlefield, and dispute — with little interior content.

**T7.2.1** — Function: the term functions as **subject/object naming a party** ("your adversary", Matt 5:25; "my adversary", Job 31:35) — a role in the sentence, not a predicate of inner state.

**T7.2.2** — Literary form: **gospel 3, prophecy 2, law 1, wisdom 1, psalm 1, epistle 1** — spread thin; the **gospel/legal** settings (the courtroom adversary) dominate the NT cases.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure: in the legal setting, *settle with your adversary quickly lest judgement follow* (the NT `antidikos` material) — opposition → counsel to reconcile before judgement.

**T7.2.4** — Settings: **judicial** (the `antidikos` court), **covenantal-military** (Exo 23:22), **dispute** (Job).

**T7.2.5** — Primary anchor (candidate, **authorial judgement**): **Exo 23:22** — "I will be an enemy to your enemies and an adversary to your adversaries" — the clearest statement, placing human enmity within God's covenant defence of his people. (Job 31:35 anchors the personal/legal sense.)

**T7.2.6** — That anchor shows what others do not: enmity **framed by God's covenant alignment** — God himself taking the adversary's side against his people's adversaries.

**T7.3.1** — Field (science §3 + outside-inventory): **clinical/social psychology** for human hostility/opposition; **outside science** for the legal-covenantal adversary register (per the science extract's outside-inventory).

**T7.3.2** — Where science illuminates: hostility-as-stable-orientation (Type-A cognitive hostility) coheres with C-E's status-type, unseated, settled character — opposition as a standing posture rather than an episode.

**T7.3.3** — Divergence: science treats the adversary relation sociologically; the biblical material adds the **covenant-lawsuit / divine-court** register (God as defender; the cosmic accuser) that science does not address — the principal divergence for C-E (flagged in the science outside-inventory).

**T7.3.4** — Gap surfaced: science's reactive/proactive distinction and the legal-adversary register together prompt a **verse-level check of `antidikos`** to confirm whether any C-E case carries the cosmic-adversary (spiritual-being) sense the tags did not capture.
