## 1. Corpus at a glance (observation)

- **397 verses · 431 M06 focus-cluster occurrences · 34 unique terms** (30 Hebrew, 1 Aramaic, 4 Greek — `ta.av` carries two Strong's, H8581 / H8374).
- Hebrew/OT carries the cluster almost entirely; the NT contributes only four low-frequency terms (`hubrizō`, `antidikos`, `bdelussomai`, `afilagathos`).
- The corpus is **top-heavy**: `sa.ne` "to hate" (143) and `cher.pah` "reproach" (72) together are ~half of all occurrences; a long tail of 20+ terms appears 1–4 times.

## 2. The inner-being signal (observation)

Aggregated across all 431 occurrences:

| Field | Dominant values |
|---|---|
| **faculty** | affect 271 · cognition 143 (always co-tagged on `sa.ne`) · moral_evaluation 19 · **absent 141** |
| **type** | action 269 · status 130 · quality 32 |
| **location (seat)** | absent 376 · soul 33 · heart 21 · flesh 5 |
| **valence** | sinful 246 · neutral 116 · righteous 21 · commanded 4 · forbidden 4 |
| **object_type** | person 135 · abstract 56 · **God 38** · thing 11 |
| **divine_involvement** | God-as-object 36 · God-as-agent 30 |

Two signals stand out before any grouping is attempted:

1. **A third of the cluster carries no inner faculty at all** (141 faculty-absent occurrences). These are not gaps in the data — they are terms that name an *outward act* (taunting, mistreating), a *relational status* (opponent, adversary), or a *borne social condition* (reproach, disgrace). M06 is therefore not a purely interior cluster; a large share of it lives on the inner→outer boundary.
2. **Hatred is bivalent.** Alongside the dominant *sinful* framing sits a smaller but coherent *righteous / commanded* seam (25 occurrences, almost all `sa.ne`, concentrated in Psalms and Proverbs — "hate evil", "hate falsehood"). The same root names both a condemned passion and a commanded orientation.

## 3. Candidate inner-being characteristics (interpretation)

The lexical fields decompose the cluster into **six** distinguishable characteristics — richer than the three-word label. Each is grounded in the faculty/type/seat signal, not imposed.

**C-A · Aversive affect — felt hatred / settled rejection**
The emotional core: an inner turning-away from a person or thing.
*Terms:* `sa.ne` (143), `sin.ah` (19), `sa.tam` (8), `se.na` [Aram.] (2), `mas.te.mah` (2), `sa.ni` (1).
*Basis:* faculty **affect** (with cognition co-tagged on `sa.ne` — felt *and* judged); type action/status; the most-seated characteristic (soul ×16, heart). Spans the full valence range, including the commanded seam.

**C-B · Appraisive contempt — judging-as-worthless / disdain**
A downward verdict on another's worth; treating-as-nothing.
*Terms:* `na.ats` "spurn" (25), `ba.zah` "despise" (13), `buz` "contempt" (11), `ne.a.tsah` (5), `she.at`/`shut` "scorn/despise" (6), `biz.za.von` (1).
*Basis:* faculty **moral_evaluation** / cognition (or absent where the term is the verdict itself); type action+status. The inner movement is evaluative, not primarily affective — distinct from C-A.

**C-C · Loathing — visceral repugnance / detestation**
Aversion with a bodily, often ritual, colouring (the "detestable / unclean").
*Terms:* `ta.av` "abhor/loathe" (21), `she.qets` "detestation" (4), `de.ra.on` "abhorrence" (2), `bdelussomai` (2).
*Basis:* the only sub-set to seat in **flesh** (`she.qets` ×2, `de.ra.on` ×1); valence carries the Levitical/Deuteronomic *forbidden* and *commanded* cases. Repugnance register is distinct from both felt hatred (C-A) and cold disdain (C-B).

**C-D · Reproach / taunt / derision — verbal hostility & borne disgrace**
Hostility expressed in speech, and the social condition it inflicts.
*Terms:* `cher.pah` "reproach" (72), `cha.raph` "to taunt" (38), `shim.tsah` "derision" (1).
*Basis:* `cher.pah` is a **status** noun — the *borne* condition of disgrace (affect-status); `cha.raph` is the verbal **act**, frequently aimed at God (×16). Straddles inner (experienced shame) and outer (social fact).

**C-E · Adversarial enmity — settled relational opposition**
A standing-against another; the disposition of being an opponent.
*Terms:* `ya.riv` "opponent" (3), `antidikos` "opponent" (4), `a.yav` "be hostile" (1), `qim` "adversary" (1); `mas.te.mah` "animosity" overlaps here.
*Basis:* faculty **absent**, type **status**; a relational orientation rather than an episodic feeling.

**C-F · Cruelty / ruthlessness — dispositional character quality**
Not an episode but a trait: the settled cruelty of a person.
*Terms:* `a.rits` "ruthless" (20), `akh.za.ri` (8), `akh.zar` (2), `akh.ze.riy.yut` (1), `hubrizō` "mistreat" (5), `sho.tet` "scourge" (1), `afilagathos` "hating good" (1).
*Basis:* type **quality** dominates; faculty mostly absent (`a.rits`, `afilagathos` tag affect). Names character, not a state.

**How the six relate to the label:** *Hate* → C-A + C-C; *Contempt* → C-B + C-D; *Hostility* → C-E + C-F.

## 4. Cross-cutting axes (interpretation)

Beyond the six groupings, three axes cut across the whole cluster and are themselves inner-being findings under the programme's governing question:

- **Valence axis — condemned vs commanded.** Hatred/abhorrence is not monovalent. The commanded/righteous seam (`sa.ne`/`ta.av` of *evil* and *falsehood*, Psa 97:10; 119:104,113,128,163; 139:21–22; Pro 8:13; Amo 5:15; Exo 18:21) presents hatred as a *right ordering of the inner being toward evil* — the inverse of the sinful cases.
- **Divine-interface axis.** Two directions co-exist: **human → God** hostility (`cha.raph` ×16, `sa.ne` ×10, `na.ats` ×8 directed at God) and **God → evil** rejection (`sa.ne` ×11, `sa.tam` ×3, `ta.av` as divine agent). This is a relational-interface characteristic, not only an intra-human one.
- **Constitutional-seat axis.** Where seated: **soul (33) > heart (21) > flesh (5)**; the flesh-seating concentrates on the visceral/ritual terms (C-C). Most occurrences (376) name no seat — consistent with the large outward/relational share.

## 5. Reflection — meaning, impact, and where additional discovery may be needed

- **Anthropological weight.** M06 is unusual among the affect clusters in how much of it is *outward and relational*: a third names acts, statuses, and conditions rather than interior faculties. If the programme's spirit–soul–body model treats clusters as primarily interior, M06 will need an explicit inner/outer-expression layer, or several terms will sit awkwardly.
- **Interpretive sensitivity across traditions.** The *commanded hatred of evil* seam and the *God-as-agent* cases (divine hatred/abhorrence) are read differently across Jewish and Christian interpretive traditions, and the imprecatory-Psalm material (Psa 139:21–22) is contested ground. A reader-facing Session C document will need to handle these with care rather than flattening them. *(Flagged as a downstream concern, not resolved here; no external sources introduced.)*
- **Where the data is thin.** The NT contribution is four low-frequency terms; any cross-testament characterisation of this cluster rests almost entirely on the OT. If a Hebrew↔Greek continuity claim is wanted, the present extract may be **insufficient** and a wider NT term-seed check would help.
- **Co-occurrence not yet used.** 581 co-occurring (non-M06) terms sit in these verses (e.g. blessing as the partner of cursing/hating in Gen 24:60). These were not analysed here; they are likely informative for the relational and antithesis structure (love↔hate, bless↔reproach) but are out of scope for this first distillation.
