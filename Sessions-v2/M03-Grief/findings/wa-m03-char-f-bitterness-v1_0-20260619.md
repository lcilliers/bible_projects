## 1. What this characteristic is
F names grief **as embittered condition** — the soul made bitter. It carries a moral colouring the other characteristics lack (`mar` reads as moral-evaluation), and is the one felt-state idiom the corpus seats squarely in the soul.

## 13. Synthesis notes (provisional)
- **Observation:** quality/action; **soul-seated** (soul 10 vs heart 3) — the clearest seat signal of any small characteristic; object abstract/person; valence neutral 18 with sinful 3; God appears as agent (2) and **giver** (1).
- **Interpretation:** F is distinctively the *bitter soul* idiom, and uniquely it can be **God-given**: Naomi names herself bitter by the LORD's hand (Rut 1:13,20), and God fills Job with bitterness (Job 9:18) — bitterness as a divinely-administered grief.
- **Reflection:** F's moral edge (the `mar` moral-evaluation tag) plus its God-as-giver cases make it the most theologically loaded small characteristic; the held-open anger-bleed (`ma.rar`) sits adjacent and may inform the F/M02 boundary later.
