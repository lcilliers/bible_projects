## 1. What this characteristic is
C names grief **as enacted observance** — the act and condition of mourning and lament, typically over death or loss. It is what grief *does* outwardly and ritually, distinct from the felt state (A) it expresses.

## 13. Synthesis notes (provisional)
- **Observation:** **action**-dominant (78/130) — mourning is enacted; heart-seated where seated; object split between a *person* (28, the bereaved-for) and a *situation* (36); morally regulated — neutral 94 but with commanded (7), righteous (5), forbidden (4), sinful (2).
- **Interpretation:** C is the most socially and morally governed characteristic: mourning can be *commanded* and *forbidden*, which none of the felt-states are. Its causes are death (Gen 37:35; Gen 50) and divine judgement (1Sa 6:19; 15:35).
- **Reflection:** The strong C↔D pairing (19 shared verses) suggests mourning and weeping are a near-fused expressive pair in the corpus; synthesis should weigh whether they are one expression or two.

## 14. Gaps / further discovery
- The C↔D fusion (19) needs a rule for whether shared verses are double-counted in synthesis; commanded/forbidden valence cases (11) deserve individual verse-reads as they bear on the moral framing of mourning.
