## 1. What this characteristic is
A names grief **as the interior state itself** — the felt ache of pain, sorrow, anguish — as distinct from its expression (weeping D, mourning C, sighing E) or its outward pressure (distress/constriction B). It is the inner being *in pain*, named directly.

## 2. Vocabulary (the lemmas that carry it)
- **Occurrences:** 104 term-occurrences across **101 verses**.
- **Lemmas:** lupeō×21, makh.ov×15, ya.gon×14, ka.av×8, e.tsev×6, ke.ev×6, ats.tse.vet×5, perilupos×5, tu.gah×4, odunaō×4, its.tsa.von×3, o.tsev×3, ponos×3, odunē×2, de.a.von×1, ma.a.tse.vah×1, qe.pha.dah×1, a.tsav×1, sullupeō×1
- **Contextual senses:** pain×21, sorrow×20, sorrowful×10, anguish×7, grieved×6, sorrows×4, grief×3, grieve×3, wounds×2, distressed×2
- The field is bilingual at its core: Hebrew `makh.ov`, `ya.gon`, `ka.av`/`ke.ev`, `e.tsev` answered in Greek by `lupeō`, `perilupos`, `odunaō`/`odunē`, `ponos`.

## 3. Grammatical character (type)
- status×63, action×34, quality×7.
- *Observation:* predominantly **status** (a state borne) over **action** — consistent with A being the state itself rather than an act.

## 4. Seat — where it is located in the inner being
- heart×11, soul×6, flesh×1.
- *Observation:* where a seat is named, **the heart predominates** (heart 11 / soul 6 / flesh 1). Felt pain is a heart-state more than a soul-state in this corpus.
- *Caveat:* a seat is named in only ~3 of 101 verses; most occurrences locate no seat.

## 5. Cause — what arouses it (incl. ra.ah / external-adversity domain)
Cause is read-resolved on **29** of 101 verses. The eliciting causes fall into recognisable kinds:
- **External adversity / harm (the ra.ah domain):** taskmasters oppressing Israel (Exo 3:7); harm befalling Benjamin (Gen 42:38); the boy not with them (Gen 44:31); contempt of neighbouring peoples (Eze 28:24).
- **Divine action as cause:** God's wounding and shattering (Job 5:18); being struck down by God (Psa 69:26); the Lord adding pain upon Baruch (Jer 45:3); the consequence pronounced in Eden (Gen 3:16–17).
- **Relational / moral cause:** godly grief leading to repentance (2Cor 7:9); the demand to sell all (Mat 19:22 / Mar 10:22 / Luk 18:23); craving for money (1Ti 6:10); a third question from Jesus (Joh 21:17); parting that will not be reversed (Act 20:38).
- **Cognitive cause:** increasing knowledge (Ecc 1:18); toil-filled days (Ecc 2:23).

*Interpretation:* the researcher's point on `ra.ah` is borne out here — external adversity is a real and frequent **cause** of inner pain, even though `ra.ah` seldom shares a verse with an A-term. Its causal role is carried by the cause dimension, not by within-verse compounding.

## 6. Object — what the pain is about
- object_type: situation×14, abstract×14, person×10, thing×1, God×1.
- *Observation:* pain is most often about a **situation** or an **abstract** loss, and notably about a **person** (10) — pain *for* or *over* someone.

## 7. Divine involvement
- Role recorded: **agent** in 10 verses — 2Cor 7:11, 2Cor 7:9, Deu 28:65, Eph 4:30, Gen 3:16, Gen 3:17, Isa 14:3, Isa 50:11, Jer 45:3.
- *Observation:* where God figures, it is overwhelmingly as the **agent** who brings or adds the pain (Eden, Job, Psalm 69, Jeremiah 45). No occurrence records God as the *bearer* of this pain in A.
- *Reflection (interpretive):* this raises a synthesis question — divine-caused sorrow sits beside human-relational sorrow under one vocabulary; the moral framing (§9) is where they separate.

## 8. Intensity
- all (all)×5, many (great)×2, all (all); all (every)×1, very (very)×1, all (for)×1, many (Many)×1, many (many)×1.
- *Observation:* intensity markers are sparse; where present they universalise ("all", "many", "very") rather than grade.

## 9. Valence — moral framing
- neutral×61, sinful×9, righteous×3, forbidden×1.
- *Observation:* mostly **neutral** (61); a **sinful** reading on 9 (e.g. the Eden consequence, money-craving sorrow of 1Ti 6:10); **righteous** on 3 (godly grief, 2Cor 7).
- *Interpretation:* the same felt pain is morally neutral by default but can be framed as the fruit of sin or as the right grief that turns to repentance — the valence comes from context, not the term.

## 10. Immediate response — what the pain produces
- Recorded responses are varied and verse-specific (e.g. Gen 3:16 "rule over"; rich-man narratives "had possessions" / "went away"); no single dominant downstream reaction. Treated as low-signal for A.

## 11. Working together — co-occurring characteristics
Verses in A that also engage another characteristic: C×4, E×4, B×2, I×2.
- *Observation:* A pairs most with **mourning (C)** and **sighing/groaning (E)** — the felt state surfacing as audible grief — and with **distress (B)** and **debilitation (I)**.
- *Reflection:* this is the first evidence of the "characteristics working together" the researcher anticipated: inner pain (A) is frequently the seated state that the expressive characteristics (C, E) and the pressure characteristic (B) make visible.

## 12. Representative verses (reference-led; full text in the extract/DB)
- **Gen 3:16–17** — pain as the pronounced consequence; God as agent; seat unstated.
- **Job 5:18 / Psa 69:26** — pain attributed to God's wounding.
- **Ecc 1:18** — pain that grows with knowledge (cognitive cause).
- **2Cor 7:9** — "godly grief" framed as righteous, leading to repentance.
- **1Ti 6:10** — sorrow as the self-inflicted fruit of money-craving.
- **Rev 21:4** — the promised end of pain.

## 13. Synthesis notes (provisional)
- **Observation:** A is a status-state, heart-seated where seated, neutral by default, bilingually stable, most often about a situation/abstract loss or a person.
- **Interpretation:** its defining axis is **cause** — external adversity, divine action, relational/moral occasion, or cognition — and its moral colour is contextual, splitting cleanly into sin-fruit vs godly-grief at the edges.
- **Reflection:** A appears to be a *hub* state that the expressive characteristics (C/E) and pressure characteristic (B) externalise; synthesis across the cluster should test whether A is upstream of those, or co-arising.

## 14. Gaps / further discovery
- Seat is unnamed in most verses — the heart-predominance is suggestive, not settled.
- The A↔C/E "working together" count (n≤4 each) is small; the relationship is better tested via shared verse-context across the cluster than via the few same-verse compounds.
- `relational` and `origin` fields were near-empty for A and not used; confirm whether that is genuine silence or under-population.
