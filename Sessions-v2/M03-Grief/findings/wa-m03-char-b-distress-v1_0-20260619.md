## 1. What this characteristic is
B names grief **as pressure and narrowing** — the Hebrew root sense of `tsar`/`tsa.rah` is *straits, narrowness, being hemmed in*. This is the inner being under constriction from an encircling adversity, answered in Greek by `thlibō` (to press) and `stenochōreō` (to crush into a narrow place).

## 13. Synthesis notes (provisional)
- **Observation:** overwhelmingly status (109/141), **soul-seated** where seated (soul 12 vs heart 4) — the inverse of A's heart-bias; object is dominantly a *situation* (32); valence almost entirely neutral; God appears as agent (12).
- **Interpretation:** B is grief as the soul under external siege: the causes are the most consistently *external* of any characteristic — enemy besieging (Deu 28:57), many evils coming (Deu 31:17,21), military threat — which is precisely the `ra.ah` domain operating through the cause field.
- **Reflection:** B and A may be two faces of one event: the situation presses (B, soul) and is felt as ache (A, heart). The seat split (soul vs heart) is a real, testable contrast, not an artefact, though seats are named on a minority of verses.
