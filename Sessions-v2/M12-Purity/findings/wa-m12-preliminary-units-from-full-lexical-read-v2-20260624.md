## §3. The three challenged terms — disposition FROM the evidence (not dismissed, not imposed)

- **GIVING (na.tan, 1,065).** The per-verse evidence shows **predominantly material/transactional giving** — sense give/gave/given, object **hand/land/inheritance**, object_type thing-abstract 823/1065 (77%). BUT a real minority co-seats with **lev (heart) ×29, che.sed (steadfast-love) ×21, ba.rakh (bless) ×28, ne.phesh ×46** — a heart/covenant giving thread. **The field that would settle whether a giving-CHARACTERISTIC exists — the *manner* of giving (willing/generous vs hand-over) — is empty (`how` = 0% of 1,065).** → Verdict: **UNRESOLVED, with a named lexical gap.** A giving characteristic is *plausible* (the heart/che.sed co-seating) but **cannot be grounded on the current lexical**; deciding it would require the manner-layer the lexical lacks. Held as a candidate; flagged ⚠ LEXICAL-GAP (how). *(Not noise — it is curated M12 content; not a characteristic-by-assertion — the evidence doesn't yet carry it.)*
- **FORMING (tsur H6696C + morfoō).** The evidence is **1 occurrence total** (morfoō "formed", Gal 4:19, co-seated birth-pangs); **tsur H6696C "to form" returns 0 occurrences** in the extract. → **Not an evidenced M12 unit.** The creativity/forming hypothesis has **no lexical basis** in M12 as it stands. ⚠ Coverage question owed: is tsur H6696C genuinely near-empty, or a corpus gap?
