## §LRT — the seven checks (is the lexical fully revealing for HC-1?)

**Two runs, because HC-1 mixes two natures:** the **state** adjectives (qa.dosh, hosios) and the **act** verbs (qa.dash, na.zar). The expectedness differs, so the LRT is run for each.

**1. All fields considered? (coverage)** — run as **act** (all 165, the act-dominant unit):

| field | coverage | distinctness | note |
|---|---|---|---|
| sense | 165/165 (100%) | 32 | holy/consecrated/consecrate/dedicate/sanctify/separate |
| object | 132/165 (**80%**) | **78 distinct** | rich — *what* is consecrated |
| object_type | 132/165 (80%) | 6 | person 52 · thing 43 · God 18 · abstract 15 · situation 3 · spiritual-being 1 |
| experiencer | 118/165 (72%) | 3 | other 61 · other(addressed) 38 · self 19 |
| valence | 160/165 (**97%**) | 5 | commanded 83 · righteous 49 · neutral 22 · **sinful 5 · forbidden 1** |
| **how (manner)** | **7/165 (4%)** | 7 | **RED FLAG — nature=act expects manner** |

Constants (descriptive only): type action 155 / quality 10; faculty 3 (near-absent — not a faculty lemma); lemma_meaning 100%.

State run (qa.dosh H6918H + hosios G3741, 10 occ): sense 100%, **valence 100%** (righteous 8 / neutral 1 / commanded 1), **object 0%**, object_type 0%, experiencer 30%, how 70%. → **the state lemmas are fully revealing for their nature** (the holy-STATE register is captured; no object, as a state has none).

**2. Expectedness / what's missing.** Act-nature DEMANDS *sense · object · how · experiencer*. Three of four are populated and rich (sense 100%, object 80%/78-distinct, experiencer 72%). **`how` (manner of consecrating) at 4% is the one nature-expected field that is empty → RED FLAG.** This is the *exact* field the expression-vs-operation call needs.

**3. LOCATE the missing `how` (the manner of consecrating).** Three searches:
- **(a) the 7 populated `how` values are NOT manner-of-the-act.** They all sit on the **adjective qa.dosh** and describe an *attendant disposition toward the holy one*, not how a thing is consecrated: Num 16:7 `chooses`, Deu 33:3 `followed`, 2Ki 4:9 `know`, Psa 34:9 `fear`, Psa 106:16 `jealous`, Dan 8:24, Heb 7:26 `fitting`. So the verb's manner-coverage is effectively **0%**.
- **(b) verse_text manner-words: 7/165 (4%)** — and even these are mostly the *act* word, not its *manner*: `set apart` (Jos 20:7, 1Ch 23:13, Neh 12:47 — that IS consecrate, restated), `abstain` (Lev 22:2, Zec 7:3 — na.zar's own sense, restraint), and **one true manner: `freewill`** (Ezr 3:5, a freewill offering consecrated). No willingly / wholeheartedly / grudgingly / liberally cluster like na.tan had.
- **(c) bound co-terms where manner could live** (LRT recovery): qa.ra "call" (M37) ×9, sha.mar "keep/obey" (M30) ×7, lev "heart" (M47) ×6, na.tan "give" (M12) ×6, ma.al/cha.ta (M10, sin) ×9, tum.ah (M12, impurity) ×4. These are **partners in the rite (keep/call/give/atone), not manner-adverbs.** lev ×6 is the only inner-being binding, and it is thin (e.g. Exo 28:3 "wise of heart" make the garments — heart of the *artisan*, not the manner of consecrating).

→ **The manner is GENUINELY ABSENT (LRT category c), not displaced.** Unlike na.tan — where manner sat recoverably on willing/heart-bound partner words in ~3–5% — qa.dash carries no manner anywhere, and there is no willing/heart subset to recover. The act is predicated on its **object and its commission** (who commands / who is set apart), not on a disposition of the consecrator.

**4. Contradiction.** 23/165 are action-type with **no object AND no how** (bare "be holy" / "consecration" as a noun-event). Not a true contradiction — these are the result-state usages (sense "holy/holiness", e.g. "shall be holy") where the verb is stative-resultative, so neither manner nor object applies. The **counterfeit set** (valence sinful/forbidden ×6) is the productive tension: a "consecrate" act with a *negative* valence — see §5.

**5. Odd one out / the counterfeit register (6 verses — USE this, it is real evidence).** Consecration directed at the **wrong object**:
- **Hos 9:10** na.zar — *"consecrated themselves to **shame** (Baal-peor)"* → spiritual-being, sinful.
- **Isa 65:5** qa.dash — *"I am too **holy** for you"* (self-styled, hypocritical holiness) → sinful.
- **Isa 66:17** qa.dash — *"sanctify themselves to go into the **gardens**"* (pagan rite) → sinful.
- **Eze 14:7** na.zar — *"**separates** himself from me and sets up idols"* → sinful.
- **Judg 17:3** qa.dash — *"I **dedicate** the silver to the Lord... to make a carved image"* (a corrupted dedication) → sinful.
- **Lev 27:26** qa.dash — *"no one may **dedicate** the firstborn... it already belongs to the Lord"* → forbidden (a dedication that is not the consecrator's to make).
→ The act is **direction-neutral in itself**: setting-apart can be toward God (righteous/commanded) or toward an idol/self/the impermissible (sinful/forbidden). The **valence is supplied by the object and the warrant, not by the act-lemma.** This is decisive for §OBJECT-TYPE.

**6. Parts play together?** Yes, into ONE coherent reading split by nature: the **adjective** (qa.dosh/hosios) names a **STATE** (no object, valence-righteous) — the holy condition; the **verb** (qa.dash/na.zar) names the **ACT that confers/recognises that state** (rich object 78-distinct, experiencer varies, valence object-supplied). The two are the result and its cause. No fragmentation.

**7. Verdict — NOT fully revealing on manner; fully revealing on object + valence + agency.** The lexical richly carries *what* is consecrated (object 80%/78-distinct), *who* consecrates / *who is set apart* (experiencer + divine_involvement), and the *direction/legitimacy* (valence incl. the counterfeit). It does **not** carry the *manner* of consecrating (`how` ~0% real) — **but here the gap is informative, not a defect:** unlike giving, there is no hidden willing/heart subset; the manner is absent because the act's inner content is **not** a manner-disposition but its **object + warrant**. The expression-vs-operation call **is decidable** without the manner field (see §OBJECT-TYPE), because the object/experiencer/valence evidence is rich and points the same way. ⚑ Logged as a lexical-remediation item all the same (manner on qa.dash unpopulated), but it does **not** block this unit's conclusion.

## §OBJECT-TYPE — the reading that FLOWS FROM the evidence

**Split by nature, as the evidence forces (§LRT-6).**

### 1. The holy-STATE — qa.dosh / hosios (adjective): a **STATE / CONDITION**
- Evidence: **object 0/10** (a state acts on nothing), **valence 100%** righteous-dominant, sense holy/holiness/saints. The valence is near-uniform righteous → the holy condition itself.
- → **STATE.** Routes as the condition that the act (below) confers and recognises. (Parallels PU-1/PU-2 cleanness as a state; holiness is the positive separation-state, cleanness the freedom-from-defilement state — to relate in §route.)

### 2. The consecrating-ACT — qa.dash / na.zar (verb): an **EXPRESSION**, not a characteristic-in-operation
The KEY test the task names. The act is decided from per-verse **object + experiencer + valence**, not from type=action:

- **It produces/recognises the STATE (the expression signature).** sense runs consecrate → "be holy"; the act *makes a thing/person holy* (Exo 30:29 "consecrate them, that they may be most holy"; Exo 40:9 "consecrate it... so that it may become holy"). The act's output **is** the state in (1). That is the defining mark of an EXPRESSION (act → produces the characteristic/state; you follow the **object** + the binding), per the expression lens §1.
- **The object varies enormously and is overwhelmingly EXTERNAL — 78 distinct objects, person 52 / thing 43 / God-or-name 18+** (altar, tent, garments, firstborn, priests, the Sabbath day, a field, God's name). An inner faculty-in-operation acts *from* an inner seat on its own movements; this act sets **external things and persons** apart. The object profile is that of an **act upon objects**, not a faculty operating.
- **The agency varies (human consecrates / God consecrates / God consecrates His own name) and the act is the SAME act** — only the agent and object change. A characteristic-in-operation would be a stable inner faculty of *one* experiencer; here the "experiencer" is whoever performs the rite (priest, Moses, God), which is the profile of a **transitive act**, not a faculty.
- **The valence is supplied by the OBJECT, not the act** (§LRT-5 counterfeit): the *same* lemma reads righteous (set apart to God) or sinful/forbidden (consecrate to shame/idols/self). A characteristic carries its own moral colour; an **expression** is direction-neutral and takes its valence from *what it is aimed at* — exactly what the counterfeit register shows. **This is the strongest single discriminator** and it points to EXPRESSION.
- **The manner-gap is consistent with EXPRESSION, not against it** (§LRT-3/7): an expression's own `how` may be empty; its inner content lives in the binding or the object/warrant — here in the **object + the divine commission** ("I the Lord sanctify you," Exo 31:13; "consecrate them to serve me as priests"). There is no willing/heart manner-subset to make it a *devotion-characteristic*; the small lev co-seating (×6) is incidental, not a manner-of-devotion thread.

→ **HC-1 resolves into TWO objects: (a) holiness = a STATE (adj), (b) consecration = an EXPRESSION (verb) that confers that state.** Consecration is **NOT** a characteristic-in-operation (devotion-acting): the per-verse evidence — external varying object, agent-varying, object-supplied valence (counterfeit), absent manner — is the profile of an **act that sets a thing/person apart as holy (produces the state)**, i.e. an expression, decided from the binding/object, not from type=action. *Where a human reflexively self-consecrates (the small genuine subset, e.g. priests Exo 19:22, Levites 2Ch 29:5/19) there is a flicker of devotion-as-disposition — flag ❓ as the one place a characteristic reading could be tested — but it is a minority and still object-directed (consecrate self **to/before** the Lord), so it does not overturn the expression reading for the unit.*

### Routing implication (for §route, not decided here)
- holy-STATE → relate to the cleanness states (PU-1/PU-2) as the positive separation-condition.
- consecration-EXPRESSION → studied **through its binding**: object (what is set apart) + warrant (divine command / God's name) + the small inner-being edge (self-consecration disposition; lev co-seat). Its inner "characteristic home" is **devotion/belonging-to-God**, made visible by the act — but M12 builds **no** standalone consecration-characteristic by assertion.

## §flags

- ⚑ **LEXICAL-REMEDIATION (manner on qa.dash):** `how` ~0% real on the consecrating verb. Unlike na.tan this is **genuine absence** (no recoverable willing/heart subset), so it does not need a split-marking; the recommended remediation is to note that qa.dash's inner content is carried by **object + warrant**, not manner, and to populate `how` only on the rare self-consecration cases if a disposition is in-verse. Non-blocking for HC-1's conclusion.
- ⚖ **State-vs-act fork inside one unit:** HC-1 must be filed as TWO objects (holy-STATE adj + consecration-EXPRESSION verb), not one — the LRT two-run coverage forced this and the object-type test confirms it.
- ❓ **Self-consecration subset (devotion candidate):** the genuinely-human reflexive cases (priests/Levites consecrate *themselves*) are the only place a devotion-characteristic could be argued; small and still object-directed. Test in step 4–5; do not assert.

- ⚠ **Counterfeit register (6 verses):** consecration to idols/shame/self (Hos 9:10, Eze 14:7, Isa 65:5/66:17, Judg 17:3, Lev 27:26) — real evidence that the act is direction-neutral; keep in-cluster as the negative/false-consecration contrast (relate to NP-1 defilement, last note).
