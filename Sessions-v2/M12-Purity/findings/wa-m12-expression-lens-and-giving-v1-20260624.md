# M12 — the EXPRESSION lens (giving as a worked case) — emerging object-type

- **File:** wa-m12-expression-lens-and-giving-v1-20260624.md · **v1 · 2026-06-24 · Author:** Claude Code.
- **Origin:** researcher insight (2026-06-24) on na.tan "give": *giving is not a characteristic — it is the **expression** of one; it binds with something else — the manner of giving, the reason for giving, when to give, how to give — and all of that is in the inner being.*
- **Status:** a development of the **still-emerging object-type lens** (step 4–5 of the corrected sequence). Grounded below on the giving evidence; offered as a type to test per unit, **not** a grid to impose. Recalls — and disciplines — the M10 "expression/outflow" kind that failed cold-read validation.

## §1. The principle

An **EXPRESSION** is an observable **act** (an action-lemma) that *manifests* an inner characteristic. The act itself is external; the inner-being content is **what binds to it**:
- the **manner** (freely / willingly / whole-heartedly vs grudgingly),
- the **reason** (love, che.sed, faithfulness, fear, manipulation),
- the **when** and the **how** (discernment, timing, secrecy vs display).

You study an expression by **following the binding** — to the manner-term and to the characteristic it co-expresses — **not** by treating the act as the characteristic. Where the manner is **not captured** in the lexical (an empty `how`), you say so; you do not infer the characteristic from the bare act. *(This is the discipline M10's "speech diagnoses the heart" lacked: it claimed the act WAS the diagnosis. Here the act only points; the binding carries the content.)*

## §2. Worked case — giving (na.tan, H5414G), grounded

- **The bare act is transactional:** sense give/gave/given; object **hand / land / inheritance**; object_type thing-abstract 77%; `how` = **0%** (the manner is *not* on na.tan).
- **The manner is in the verse, on bound words (27 cases):** *"give **freely**… your heart shall **not be grudging**"* (Deu 15:10); *"look **grudgingly**"* (Deu 15:9); *"offer **willingly**… of your own have we given"* (1Ch 29:14); **freewill** offering (Lev 23:38; Deu 16:10); *"**willingly** give"* (Judg 8:25); **liberally** (Deu 15:14); a **whole heart** (1Ch 29:17-19).
- **The binding terms often belong elsewhere:** the manner "freewill / willing-heartedness" is **M09's** characteristic (M09 char "Willing-heartedness / freewill devotion"); the "give freely vs grudging" disposition is generosity; che.sed (M05) and blessing (M39) co-seat heavily (56 cases), most of them **God** giving — divine generosity expressed as giving.

→ **na.tan in M12 is an EXPRESSION.** It anchors no M12 characteristic; it is the vehicle through which **generosity / willing-heartedness / che.sed / faithfulness** (its registry home) become visible. Its inner content lives in the **bound manner-terms** (partly in M09/M05), and where unbound the lexical cannot carry a characteristic (the `how`-gap is the honest limit). *Not noise; not a giving-characteristic-by-assertion; an expression whose characteristic is followed through its binding.*

## §3. Implication for the shape of M12 (to TEST in step 4, not assume)

The act/state grammar (a legitimate lemma property) maps onto the expression/characteristic distinction:
- **Candidate EXPRESSIONS (act-lemmas):** cleanse (ta.her / katharizō), purify (hagnizō), consecrate / dedicate (qadash), give (na.tan), anoint (enchriō). Each to be studied by its binding (manner/reason + the state it produces).
- **Candidate CHARACTERISTICS / CONDITIONS (state-lemmas):** clean (tahor/katharos), pure (hagnos), holy (qadosh adj / hosios), innocent / blameless (naqi / tamim), incorruptible (aftharsia), unclean (tum.ah, the negative pole).
- **Open question (genuinely):** is a given act an *expression of* a state (cleanse → cleanness), or a *characteristic-in-operation* in its own right (e.g. consecrating as the faculty of devotion acting)? That is exactly what the deep evidence + object-type test must decide per unit — **the binding evidence settles it, not the grammar alone.**

## §4. What this changes operationally
- **Object-type test (step 4) gains a third reading** alongside characteristic and state: **EXPRESSION** (act manifesting a characteristic). Decided from per-verse evidence: an expression shows a varying **object** it acts on + a **manner/reason binding** (in-verse manner-words or co-seated characteristic-terms); its own `how`/manner may be empty (then the content is in the binding or uncaptured).
- **For na.tan specifically:** reclassify (preliminary) from "unit/UNRESOLVED" to **EXPRESSION**; do not build a giving-characteristic in M12; record the binding (manner → M09 willing-heartedness; reason → M05 che.sed / M39 blessing; faithfulness = its registry home) as the cross-cluster note.

## §G — bias-guard
- The principle is **grounded** (27 manner-bound verses + 56 binding co-seatings, cited), not asserted; the giving-characteristic is *declined* precisely because the bare act doesn't carry it.
- Held as an **emerging** type — applied to na.tan (clear case), proposed for the other act-lemmas **as a test**, not a verdict. The eisegesis risk (forcing every act into "expression") is real; the deep evidence per unit governs.
- The cross-cluster bindings (M09/M05/M39) are the **last** note, after the in-cluster reading — not a re-export.

*The expression lens — giving is the expression of a characteristic; the inner being is the manner/reason/when/how that binds to the act; the act-lemma is studied through its binding, never as the characteristic. Grounded on na.tan; offered as the third object-type reading for step 4.*
