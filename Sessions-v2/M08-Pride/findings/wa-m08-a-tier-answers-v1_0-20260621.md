## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — Where God appears in A's verses he is its **object** or its **opponent**, never its bearer: the self lifts itself *against* him — "I will ascend… above the stars of God" (Isaiah 14:13); "exalt himself… above every god" (Daniel 11:36); a "high hand… reviles the Lord" (Numbers 15:30) — and God is the agent who abases it — "putting down one and lifting up another" (Psalm 75:7).

**T0.1.2** — Self-exaltation is **never borne by God**; it is exclusively creaturely and creaturely-sinful. The only legitimate lifting is God's own act of exalting the lowly — "the horns of the righteous shall be lifted up" (Psalm 75:10). The pattern indicates that exaltation is a divine prerogative: it is right only as God's act or gift, never as the creature's self-assertion — "the Lord alone will be exalted" (Isaiah 2:11, 17).

**T0.2.1** — Its effect in the person is consistently destructive: it leads the person to forget God (Deuteronomy 8:14), defy him (Numbers 15:30), claim deity (Ezekiel 28:2), and fall (Proverbs 16:18).

**T0.2.2** — The role belongs to the **fallen condition**. Self-exaltation is uniformly disordered and has no place in created design; its legitimate analogue (being exalted) is God's act, not the creature's self-lifting.

**T0.2.3** — None. A has no orientation toward a future fullness; its only future is to be brought low — "the lofty pride of men shall be brought low" (Isaiah 2:17).

**T0.3.1** — None as a direct instantiation. *Inferential:* A is a counterfeit grasp at the exaltedness that belongs to God and is God's to confer — the creature reaching for the height that reflects God, illegitimately (Ezekiel 28:2). [labelled inferential]

**T0.3.2** — Exclusively creaturely as sin; the legitimate counterpart (being exalted) is God's prerogative and gift, not shared with the creature as self-exaltation.

**T0.3.3** — Its presence indicates the divine image **disordered** — the creature usurping the Creator's place, "I am a god" (Ezekiel 28:2); "I will make myself like the Most High" (Isaiah 14:14). Its abasement restores right order.

**T0.4.1** — Eschatological: A is abolished at the day of the Lord, when "the Lord alone will be exalted" (Isaiah 2:11, 17). *Inferential:* Isaiah 14:13–14 has been read typologically of the adversary's self-exaltation (human pointing to a supra-human pattern of rebellion) — noted as inferential, not established from A's evidence alone.

*Coverage T0: 9 of 9 answered (2 carry an inferential element).*

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — The characteristic is *self-exaltation*: the lifting of the self above its proper place. The name signals an active, vertical, comparative motion of the heart.

**T1.1.2** — The primary terms are height-words applied to the self: rum (to be high, lift up, exalt), ga.vah (to be high/haughty), ga.ah (to rise up), the loftiness-nouns go.vah and rum (H7312), Greek hupsēlos (high), Aramaic zud (Daniel 5:20). Definitionally, A is *to make or become high*.

**T1.1.3** — The name carries an **upward** direction (above brothers, above God), a **comparative** relation (over others), and a **heart-located** constitution — "your heart be lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14).

**T1.2.1** — Primarily an **act** — the verb-dominated motion of lifting ("I will ascend", Isaiah 14:13) — which, when it settles, becomes the state of B (settled pride).

**T1.2.2** — Compound: it combines an inflated self-assessment, a comparison that ranks the self above others or God, and the move to enact or claim that height — visible together in "your heart is proud… 'I am a god'" (Ezekiel 28:2). [structural reasoning, grounded]

**T1.3.1** — Its structural opposite is the heart *not* lifted up — humility/lowliness of heart: "my heart is not lifted up" (Psalm 131:1); "humility comes before honor" (Proverbs 18:12).

**T1.3.2** — At its edge it excludes remembrance of and dependence on God ("you forget the Lord", Deuteronomy 8:14) and submission to ordained limits ("lifted up above his brothers… turn aside from the commandment", Deuteronomy 17:20).

**T1.3.3** — A is *not* the settled state of pride (B), the verbal display (D), or the defiant act against authority (C1) — though it issues in all three; it is specifically the *motion* of lifting. Where the height settles it becomes B; where it speaks, D; where it defies command, C1.

**T1.4.1** — A operates as the heart's own verb — "heart lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14), the heart raising itself — and as transitive self-exaltation, "He shall exalt himself" (Daniel 11:36). Manner: internal lifting becoming outward display (lofty eyes, haughty neck).

**T1.4.2** — Yes: the mode varies from internal heart-motion (Deuteronomy 8:14) to outward bodily expression — "how lofty are their eyes" (Proverbs 30:13); "haughty neck" (Psalm 75:5) — to verbal claim (Isaiah 14:13). Direction varies between above-others and above-God.

**T1.4.3** — Yes — it operates communicatively through boastful self-declaration: "You said in your heart, 'I will ascend'" (Isaiah 14:13); "speak with haughty neck" (Psalm 75:5); "the boastful look in his eyes" (Isaiah 10:12).

**T1.5.1** — The first inner response shown is the forgetting/abandoning of God — "your heart be lifted up, and you forget the Lord" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "their heart was lifted up; therefore they forgot me" (Hosea 13:6).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent in direction (self-exaltation → displacement of God), varied in expression (forgetting, defying, claiming deity) — Hosea 13:6; Daniel 11:36.

**T1.6.1** — Over time it establishes a settled haughtiness (the condition of B) and an orientation of self-trust and God-forgetfulness, and it sets the trajectory toward the fall — "Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall" (Proverbs 16:18).

**T1.6.3** — The immediate response is a single turning (forgetting/defying God); the sustained effect is the entrenched haughty condition and the eventual being-brought-low — the long arc from Deuteronomy 8:14 (immediate) to Daniel 5:20 ("brought down from his kingly throne").

**T1.7.1** — A takes hold under conditions of prosperity, strength, and satiety — "when he was strong, he grew proud" (2 Chronicles 26:16); "they were filled… their heart was lifted up" (Hosea 13:6). *(A never operates rightly — flagged; the question's "operate rightly" branch is [structural] inapplicable.)*

**T1.7.2** — It is resisted by deliberate remembrance and lowliness — "my heart is not lifted up… I do not occupy myself with things too great" (Psalm 131:1) — and by the fear of God — "do not become proud, but fear" (Romans 11:20). On distortion by another spirit: **SILENT [data-silence]** — A's verses state no adversarial-spirit interference.

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, the person is humbled/lowly in spirit — the one who does not lift the heart (Psalm 131:1) or who humbles himself over it: "Hezekiah humbled himself for the pride of his heart" (2 Chronicles 32:26).

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — The dominant seat is the **heart** — "heart lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14; Daniel 11:12; Ezekiel 28:2; Hosea 13:6; 2 Chronicles 32:25; Proverbs 18:12) — with one soul-and-heart locus (Psalm 13:2) and one spirit locus ("his spirit was hardened", Daniel 5:20). It is expressed through the **eyes** ("lofty eyes", Proverbs 30:13; "haughty eyes", Proverbs 6:17) and the **neck** (Psalm 75:5); in the NT it engages the **mind** ("what is exalted among men", Luke 16:15).

**T2.1.2** — The pattern shows a heart-deep seat that surfaces through the eyes and neck. The body link is **expressive** (the haughty eyes/neck display the inner lifting), not the seat. Levels never made the seat: conscience and memory function as casualties (T3.3, T3.9), not as where A resides.

**T2.10.1** — Movement runs **inward → outward**: the lifted heart shows in lifted eyes and stiffened neck (soul/spirit expressing through the body) — Proverbs 30:13. No movement onto the person from an external source is stated.

**T2.7.1** — The body link runs **soul/spirit → body** (expressive): the lifted heart manifests in lofty eyes and haughty neck, and the bodily display is itself judged — "the haughty eyes you bring down" (Psalm 18:27).

**T2.9.1** — A originates **within the person** — the heart lifts itself; no verse attributes it to an external giver. It is *occasioned* by prosperity (Deuteronomy 8:14) but the lifting is the heart's own act.

**T2.9.2** — Single and consistent: within-person generation, the occasion (fullness/strength) constant; no generational or received origin appears.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.1 Perception** — **T3.1.1** A engages perception through sight: "how lofty are their eyes" (Proverbs 30:13); "lifted your eyes to the heights" (2 Kings 19:22). **T3.1.2** It elevates and re-directs the gaze — looking down on others, looking past God. **T3.1.3** The lifted/lofty eye is A's signature perceptual engagement across the verses.

**T3.2 Cognition** — **T3.2.1** A engages cognition in its NT register (hupsēlos, cognition-tagged): "what is exalted among men is an abomination" (Luke 16:15); "Never be wise in your own sight" (Romans 12:16). **T3.2.2** It distorts self-estimation, rating the self too high — the refusal of which is "things too great… for me" (Psalm 131:1). **T3.2.3** Cognition is engaged chiefly in the NT; the OT register is heart-disposition, not deliberation.

**T3.3 Memory** — **T3.3.1** A engages memory by its **failure**: "you forget the Lord" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "their heart was lifted up; therefore they forgot me" (Hosea 13:6). **T3.3.2** It suppresses remembrance of God and his deliverances. **T3.3.3** Forgetting-God is a defining downstream of A — a strong, non-obvious engagement of the memory faculty by deletion.

**T3.4 Affect** — **T3.4.1** Direct affective engagement is minimal; A is a posture more than a felt emotion, with an implied self-satisfied elation in fullness — "filled… heart lifted up" (Hosea 13:6). [implied affect labelled inferential] **T3.4.2** Little affective effect is stated. **T3.4.3** The pattern shows A as a stance of the heart, not primarily a feeling — the affective pole of the cluster belongs to D (boasting).

**T3.5 Creativity** — **T3.5.1 / T3.5.2 / T3.5.3 — SILENT [data-silence].** No verse in A engages imagination or origination.

**T3.6 Volition** — **T3.6.1** A is willed: "He shall do as he wills… exalt himself" (Daniel 11:36); the fivefold "I will ascend… I will set my throne" (Isaiah 14:13). **T3.6.2** It sets the will on self-ascent; conversely the will can refuse it — "I do not occupy myself with things too great" (Psalm 131:1) — and humble it (2 Chronicles 32:26). **T3.6.3** A is strongly volitional: a chosen lifting, answerable by a chosen humbling.

**T3.7 Agency** — **T3.7.1** A engages agency: the lifted self acts from its height — "his heart shall be exalted, and he shall cast down tens of thousands" (Daniel 11:12); "hands that shed innocent blood" (Proverbs 6:17). **T3.7.2** It drives domineering and violent action. **T3.7.3** A characteristically issues in high-handed agency.

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — **T3.8.1** A engages moral evaluation both as its object and as a distorter: it is judged sin — "Haughty eyes and a proud heart… are sin" (Proverbs 21:4) — and it inverts evaluation: "vileness is exalted" (Psalm 12:8); "exalts folly" (Proverbs 14:29). **T3.8.2** It corrupts the person's moral assessment, elevating what should be debased. **T3.8.3** A is consistently evaluated as sin and consistently corrupts evaluation.

**T3.9 Conscience** — **T3.9.1 / T3.9.2 — SILENT [data-silence]:** A's verses do not show the conscience's witness operating. **T3.9.3** *Inferential:* by producing the forgetting of God (T3.3), A dulls the conscience's ground — labelled inferential, not stated.

**T3.10 Conscientiousness** — **T3.10.1** A engages conscientiousness by undermining it: "his heart may not be lifted up… that he may not turn aside from the commandment" (Deuteronomy 17:20). **T3.10.2** It erodes obedient moral follow-through. **T3.10.3** A is corrosive of conscientious obedience.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **T3.11.1** A engages relational capacity by lifting the self above others: "lifted up above his brothers" (Deuteronomy 17:20); "my enemy be exalted over me" (Psalm 13:2). **T3.11.2** It corrupts relation into hierarchy and contempt. **T3.11.3** A is intrinsically relationally damaging — it cannot coexist with genuine fellowship among equals.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — Both: A reorients the person (from God-dependence to self-trust/God-forgetting) and finally changes their condition (high → brought low) — Deuteronomy 8:14; Daniel 5:20.

**T5.1.2** — The downfall is presented as decisive — "with none to raise him up" (Jeremiah 50:32); "brought down from his kingly throne" (Daniel 5:20) — reversible only by self-humbling *before* the fall (2 Chronicles 32:26).

**T5.2.1** — Yes: a clear sequence — fullness/strength → heart lifted up → forgetting/defying God → settled haughtiness → being brought low (Deuteronomy 8:14; Proverbs 16:18).

**T5.3.1** — Two mechanisms: A's downfall comes by **divine abasement** ("I will bring you down", Obadiah 4; Jeremiah 49:16); its prevention by **self-humbling** ("Hezekiah humbled himself", 2 Chronicles 32:26).

**T5.3.2** — The mechanism differs by context: imposed judgment (the proud abased) versus chosen humbling (the proud self-humbled before judgment).

**T5.4.1** — A **produces** affliction (its own downfall, "to his destruction", 2 Chronicles 26:16) and arises in prosperity — the opposite of affliction.

**T5.4.2** — Suffering, or its threat, **breaks** A: under threatened wrath "Hezekiah humbled himself… so that the wrath of the Lord did not come" (2 Chronicles 32:25–26). Affliction is its corrective.

**T5.5.1** — A's role in formation is wholly cautionary: it is the danger prosperity breeds, against which vigilance and humbling are the formative response (Deuteronomy 8:14).

**T5.6.1** — A's present operation points toward its **eschatological abolition**, not a fullness — "the Lord alone will be exalted in that day" (Isaiah 2:11, 17).
