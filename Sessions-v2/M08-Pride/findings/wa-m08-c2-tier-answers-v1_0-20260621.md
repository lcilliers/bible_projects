## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Insolence/contempt toward others*: pride turned outward as derision, contempt, and oppression of people. The name signals an outward, relational discharge.

**T1.1.2** — zed ("insolent, presumptuous"), za.don ("insolence" → strife), ra.hav ("be insolent/boisterous"), shal.le.tet ("brazen"). Definitionally, the bearing-down of the high on the low.

**T1.1.3** — Outward and **against** others — the relational pole of pride.

**T1.2.1** — An **act/behaviour** (derision, oppression) issuing from the settled disposition (B).

**T1.2.2** — Compound: the settled pride of B + its outward, contemptuous discharge upon people.

**T1.3.1** — Its opposite is humility expressed as regard for others — "be subject to the elders… clothe yourselves with humility toward one another" (1 Peter 5:5, the inverse of the insolent youth).

**T1.3.2** — It excludes regard, pity, and due honour ("they close their hearts to pity", Psalm 17:10).

**T1.3.3** — C2 is not the defiance toward God (C1) nor the inner height (B); it is contempt enacted upon the neighbour.

**T1.4.1** — It operates as contemptuous action: "The insolent utterly deride me" (Psalm 119:51); "smear me with lies" (119:69); "have dug pitfalls for me" (119:85).

**T1.4.2** — It varies — derision (mocking), defamation (smearing), entrapment (pitfalls), oppression (Psalm 119:122), and social disorder ("the youth… insolent to the elder", Isaiah 3:5).

**T1.4.3** — Yes — chiefly communicative/verbal: derision and lies (Psalm 119:51, 69); "speak arrogantly" (Psalm 17:10).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate effect falls on the **other** — the sufferer derided, smeared, trapped (Psalm 119:51, 85). The proud agent's inner response is largely unstated [data-silence].

**T1.5.2** — Consistent in its outward target (the lowly/righteous); the agent's interior is consistently unstated.

**T1.6.1** — It produces strife — "By insolence comes nothing but strife" (Proverbs 13:10) — and entrenched oppression.

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the contemptuous act on the other; sustained = strife and the hardened oppressor (B).

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold where pride is settled (B) and pity is closed off (Psalm 17:10); never operates rightly [structural].

**T1.7.2** — Resisted by humility-toward-one-another (1 Peter 5:5). On another spirit — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, the person clothes itself in humility toward others (1 Peter 5:5).

*Coverage T1: 18 of 18 (T1.5.1 part data-silence on the agent's interior).*

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Where seated, the **heart** — the "brazen" heart (Ezekiel 16:30); hearts "closed to pity" (Psalm 17:10). The named soul is often the **sufferer's** ("a band of ruthless men seeks my life", Psalm 86:14). The agent's seat is mostly unstated.

**T2.1.2** — The pattern shows C2 as predominantly **outward act** with little stated interior — a faculty-absent expression whose seat is supplied by B (the settled heart behind it).

**T2.10.1** — **SILENT [data-silence]** / [structural]: no constitutional movement stated; C2 is the outward discharge of B.

**T2.7.1** — Body link via the speaking mouth and the closed heart (Psalm 17:10) — expressive; otherwise none.

**T2.9.1** — Within the proud person (rooted in B); occasioned by settled pride. *(Origin of the act = the settled disposition.)*

**T2.9.2** — Single (within-person, from the settled pride of B).

**T3.5 Creativity** — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

**T3.6 Volition** — **T3.6.1** Engaged in the chosen oppression — the insolent deliberately "dug pitfalls" and "spread a net" (Psalm 119:85; 140:5). **T3.6.2** It directs the will against the neighbour. **T3.6.3** C2's volition is largely instrumental — willed harm.

**T3.7 Agency** — **T3.7.1** Strongly engaged: deride, smear, dig pitfalls, oppress, pursue the poor (Psalm 119:51, 69, 85, 122; 10:2). **T3.7.2** It is action against people. **T3.7.3** C2 is, above all, an agency of contempt.

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — **T3.8.1** Judged — the insolent are "accursed" (Psalm 119:21); their insolence "comes nothing but strife" (Proverbs 13:10). **T3.8.2/3** It overrides regard for the other's worth.

**T3.10 Conscientiousness** — **T3.10.1** Engaged negatively — the insolent "do not live according to your law" (Psalm 119:85). **T3.10.2/3** C2 couples contempt for people with disregard of God's law.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **T3.11.1** Centrally engaged — C2 *is* a relational characteristic: contempt, derision, and oppression of the neighbour, elder, and lowly (Psalm 123:4; Isaiah 3:5). **T3.11.2** It destroys due regard and connection. **T3.11.3** C2 is the relational faculty turned to harm.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1 Divine — God to Human** — **SILENT [structural]**: not extended by God.

**T4.2 Divine — Human to God** — **T4.2.1** Indirect: the insolent's contempt for people is bound to disregard of God — "who wander from your commandments" (Psalm 119:21); "they do not live according to your law" (119:85). **T4.2.2/3** Its God-ward posture is disregard, mediated through contempt for the neighbour.

**T4.3 Human — Giving** — **T4.3.1** C2 *is* directed from one person toward another — but as **harm, not gift**: derision, defamation, oppression (Psalm 119:51, 69). The "giving" frame is inverted: what is extended is contempt. **T4.3.2/3 — [structural]** for the giver-conditions of a genuine good (n/a — C2 extends harm).

**T4.4 Human — Receiving** — **T4.4.1** The other **receives** C2 as injury; the sufferer's uptake is endurance and fidelity — "The insolent utterly deride me, but I do not turn away from your law" (Psalm 119:51). **T4.4.2/3** The faithful sufferer does not take up insolence in return.

**T4.5 Human — Boundaries** — **T4.5.1** It operates across relational distance (oppressor → the lowly) and within social order (youth → elder, Isaiah 3:5). **T4.5.2** It crosses covenantal lines — the insolent within and the ruthless without (Psalm 86:14). **T4.5.3** Its scope is the neighbour, the elder, the poor — those beneath the proud's regard.

**T4.6 Spiritual Beings** — **SILENT [data-silence]** (T4.6.1–3).

*Coverage T4: ~12 answered, ~6 SILENT (T4.1 structural; T4.6 data; T4.3 partial). Unlike A/B, C2 substantively answers the human-interface tiers — it is the cluster's outward-relational characteristic.*

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — In the **sufferer**, C2 occasions a tested fidelity ("but I do not turn away from your law", Psalm 119:51); in the agent, it hardens contempt.

**T5.1.2** — **SILENT [data-silence]** on reversibility in the agent.

**T5.2.1** — Sequence (sufferer's side): contempt met → endurance → continued obedience (Psalm 119:51, 69).

**T5.3.1** — Mechanism: in the sufferer, perseverance under contempt; in the agent, hardening. (No transformation of the agent shown.)

**T5.3.2** — Differs by side (sufferer formed; agent hardened).

**T5.4.1** — C2 is a **context of suffering** for its victims — the righteous afflicted by the insolent (Psalm 86:14; 119:121–122).

**T5.4.2** — Suffering under C2 **refines the sufferer's** fidelity (Psalm 119:51, 69) — a notable inversion: the characteristic's developmental fruit appears in the one it is aimed at, not the one who bears it.

**T5.5.1** — For the sufferer, C2 participates in sanctification as tested endurance; for the agent, it is the disorder formation must overcome.

**T5.6.1** — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

*Coverage T5: ~7 answered, ~2 SILENT (T5.1.2, T5.6.1). C2's developmental content sits unusually on the sufferer's side.*

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — zed ("insolent, presumptuous"), za.don ("insolence"), ra.hav ("be boisterous/insolent"), shal.le.tet ("brazen, imperious"). Root sense: a brazen pushing against others.

**T7.1.2** — Adjectives/substantives ("the insolent") and abstract nouns ("insolence"). The substantival form shows a **person-type** ("the insolent") as much as an act.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from "insolent/presumptuous" through "brazen/imperious" to "boisterous" — the bearing of one who will not be checked.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — abstract ("insolence", za.don) versus person-type ("the insolent", zed).

**T7.1.5** — Yes — the opposite is humility/regard for others (1 Peter 5:5).

**T7.1.6** — Yes, prominently: "the insolent" (zed) as a settled person-type (the Psalm 119 antagonist).

**T7.1.7** — None.

**T7.1.8** — The OT insolence vocabulary (zed, za.don) has no single NT successor term; its sense is carried in the NT by "despise authority"/"abusive" terms — a partial continuity.

**T7.1.9** — None newly coined.

**T7.1.10** — The arc shows insolence as a recognised social-religious type — the brazen oppressor of the faithful.

**T7.2.1** — In Psalm 119, zed names the antagonist whose contempt frames the psalmist's fidelity: "The insolent utterly deride me, but I do not turn away from your law" (119:51).

**T7.2.2** — Forms: psalm (the lament/Torah-psalm of Psalm 119), prophecy (Isaiah 3:5), wisdom (Proverbs 13:10). The lament form carries C2 from the **sufferer's** vantage — essential to its reading.

**T7.2.3** — The argument (Psalm 119): the insolent oppress; the faithful endure and keep the law — contempt does not move the righteous.

**T7.2.4** — Settings: liturgical/lament (Psalm 119), social-prophetic (Isaiah 3), wisdom (Proverbs).

**T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Psalm 119:51** — the insolent's derision met by the sufferer's fidelity — C2 seen from beneath.

**T7.2.6** — Psalm 119:51 shows what no agent-focused verse does: C2's defining shape is the contempt the faithful *endure*, not merely the contempt the proud *feel*.

**T7.3.1** — *(science, M08 extract §3)* The social psychology of power and dominance is the most useful lens — C2 is the dominance pole enacted on others.

**T7.3.2** — *(science)* Keltner's power paradox — power reduces empathy and increases self-focus (extract §3) — illuminates "they close their hearts to pity" (Psalm 17:10) and the contempt of the proud for the poor.

**T7.3.3** — *(science)* Science describes dominance as adaptive status-seeking; the biblical material judges contempt for the lowly as sin and frames it from the victim's side (extract T7.3.3) — a register and vantage divergence.

**T7.3.4** — *(science)* Science attends to the dominant agent; the biblical material's attention to the **oppressed sufferer** surfaces an aspect (fidelity under contempt) the dominance literature does not centre.

*Coverage T7: 20 of 20.*
