## 1. Definition (working)

Pride as a **settled, entrenched condition** — the height once attained and now borne as a standing disposition. Where A is the act of lifting, B is the state of being high. Distinctively, B is borne not only by individuals but by **whole peoples**.

## 2. Evidence base

**98 occurrences gathered**, of which the core settled-pride band is the majority; the remainder are literal-height (set-aside candidates) and one divine-majesty occurrence. Lemmas: ga.on (H1347, 28), ga.a.vah (H1346, 12), ge.eh (H1343, 9), ga.vo.ah (H1364, 9), huperēfanos (G5244, 5), ge.vah (H1466, 3), gav.hut (H1365, 2), ge (H1341, 1), ge.ah (H1344, 1), sha.chats (H7830, 1), ra.hav (H7295, 1), ge.vah (H1467, Aram., 1), huperēfania (G5243, 1), ge.ut (H1348, 4), and the height-nouns ma.rom (H4791, 13) and qo.mah (H6967, 7).

### 2a. Core: pride as a standing state
- **Corporate / national.** ga.on is overwhelmingly "the pride of [a people/power]" — "the pride of Jacob" (Amos 6:8; 8:7), "the pride of Israel" (Hosea 5:5; 7:10), "the pride of Moab" (Isaiah 16:6), "the pride of Egypt" (Ezekiel 32:12), "the pride of Assyria… laid low" (Zechariah 10:11), "the pride of Philistia" (Zechariah 9:6). It is repeatedly the object of judgment: "I will break the pride of your power" (Leviticus 26:19).
- **The proud as a type/class.** "everyone who is proud" (Job 40:11–12); "the contempt of the proud" (Psalm 123:4); "the Lord tears down the house of the proud" (Proverbs 15:25).
- **Heart/soul/spirit-seated.** "Everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination" (Proverbs 16:5); "a haughty look and an arrogant heart" (Psalm 101:5); "the patient in spirit is better than the proud in spirit" (Ecclesiastes 7:8); "the pride of Jacob" with soul (Amos 6:8). The whole inner being is described as proud.
- **The NT frame.** "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5); "scattered the proud in the thoughts of their hearts" (Luke 1:51, seated mind+heart).

### 2b. Divine-frame occurrence (→ cluster analysis)
ge.ut at Isaiah 26:10 — "the **majesty** of the Lord" (righteous; divine object). God's majesty, the legitimate pole; tagged, not folded into the human characteristic.

### 2c. Per-occurrence filter — literal height (set-aside candidates, not dropped)
ma.rom and qo.mah are predominantly literal: "the **heights** of the mountains… tallest cedars" (2 Kings 19:23; Isaiah 37:24); "the height of his **stature**" (1 Samuel 16:7); "it towered **aloft**" (Ezekiel 19:11). A minority are the proud's metaphorical loftiness: "set his nest **on high**" (Habakkuk 2:9); "your **lofty** dwelling" (Obadiah 3); "**loftily** they threaten oppression" (Psalm 73:8). The split is per-occurrence; literal occurrences are flagged for occurrence-level set-aside.

## 3. Analysis (observation → interpretation)

**B is the state, A is the act.** The same heart-imagery recurs, but as a condition predicated of a person or people rather than a motion. "Pride is their necklace; violence covers them as a garment" (Psalm 73:6) — pride worn as settled adornment.

**The corporate dimension is distinctive.** Through ga.on, B is characteristically a property of nations. This sets B apart from the more individual A: pride here is a collective, structural disposition that whole peoples bear and that God judges as peoples.

**B is the root-disposition behind contempt and oppression.** ga.a.vah and ge.eh repeatedly show settled pride issuing in action against the lowly: "In arrogance the wicked hotly pursue the poor" (Psalm 10:2); "The arrogant have hidden a trap for me" (Psalm 140:5); "speak insolently against the righteous in pride and contempt" (Psalm 31:18). **Interpretation:** B feeds C2 (insolence toward others) — the settled state is the soil from which contemptuous action grows. (Multi-membership of ga.a.vah noted, not relocated.)

**B is the precise object of God's standing opposition.** The corpus's most concentrated statements that God abases the proud and exalts the lowly cluster here: "God opposes the proud" (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5); "those who walk in pride he is able to humble" (Daniel 4:37); "the Lord… will lay low his pompous pride" (Isaiah 25:11); "his pride will bring him low" (Proverbs 29:23). Pride is not merely disapproved — it is the disposition God has set himself against.

**The humility counter-pole is named.** Unlike A, B repeatedly defines itself against its opposite: "gives grace to the **humble**" (James 4:6); "he saves the **lowly**" (Job 22:29); "better to be of a **lowly** spirit" (Proverbs 16:19); "**humility** comes before honor" (Proverbs 18:12). The pride↔humility axis is structural here and links M08 to M09 (Humility).

## 4. Synthesis

Settled pride is the entrenched height — pride as a standing condition of the heart, soul, and spirit, and characteristically of whole peoples. It is the soil from which contempt and oppression of the lowly grow (feeding C2), and it is the disposition against which God's action is most explicitly directed: the corpus's clearest "God opposes the proud / exalts the humble" statements gather here. Where A is aroused (by prosperity) and momentary, B is a given, durable state — and it is consistently set over against humility, making the pride↔humility axis the cluster's organising contrast.

## 5. Expected but absent — special attention

1. **Cause — genuinely sparse here, and that is itself a finding (not a method gap).** Where A had a recoverable prosperity-cause, B's national-pride occurrences mostly assert the state *without* an eliciting cause — pride is treated as a standing fact of a people (Hosea 5:5; Zechariah 9:6; Isaiah 16:6). A few echo prosperity (Ezekiel 16:49, Sodom's "excess of food, prosperous ease"; Ezekiel 28, wealth). **This A(aroused)↔B(settled) cause-asymmetry is a real distinction to carry to the synthesis**, not a silence to eliminate.
2. **Faculty — absent (0 tagged).** Same disposition-not-faculty pattern, but B is located across heart/soul/spirit, suggesting a pervasive whole-inner-being orientation rather than a localised faculty. The felt/affective texture of settled pride is not captured. → an affect pass would help.
3. **`how` captures judgment, not mechanism.** The populated `how` values are overwhelmingly God's response (break, cut off, lay low, oppose), not how pride operates internally. **Interpretation:** the corpus's interest in settled pride is in its *judgment*, not its inner mechanism. Where mechanism appears it is outward — "His beautiful ornament they used for pride… abominable images" (Ezekiel 7:20): pride feeding idolatry.
4. **ga.on: attitude vs pomp (recoverable per-occurrence reading).** A minority of ga.on occurrences denote the external pomp/glory prided in, not the inner attitude — "the splendor and **pomp** of the Chaldeans" (Isaiah 13:19); "the **pride** of your power" = the sanctuary (Ezekiel 24:21, neutral). Most are the inner attitude. → resolved per occurrence in any reader-facing pass.
5. **Humility counter-pole present (expected, and confirmed).** The opposite of B is named far more than for A; this is the cluster's organising axis and a Session-D link to M09.

## 6. Reflection (labelled — not a conclusion)

- The corporate/national dimension resonates with collective identity, institutional and national pride; I hold this descriptively rather than pressing application.
- **Cross-cultural:** "the pride of [nation]" speaks directly to honour and collective-identity cultures; the God-opposes-the-proud frame reframes status as theologically precarious. → flag for reader-facing.
- **Cross-cluster:** the pride↔humility axis is a clear M08↔M09 (Humility) link — a Session-D candidate.
- **Additional discovery:** per-occurrence resolution of ga.on attitude-vs-pomp; confirmation of the A-aroused / B-settled cause distinction across the corpus; an affect pass.
