## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Conceit/puffed-up*: the self **inflated** in its own estimation. The name signals swelling — empty enlargement, not solid height.

**T1.1.2** — fusioō ("to inflate, puff up", from fusa, bellows), fusiōsis ("a puffing up"), tufoō ("to wrap in smoke, befog, make conceited"), huperfroneō ("to think too highly"), hupsēlofroneō ("to be high-minded"). Definitionally, to be swollen in mind.

**T1.1.3** — An **inward swelling of the mind** that, unlike A, adds no real height — only volume.

**T1.2.1** — A **condition** of the mind (being puffed up) issuing in conduct.

**T1.2.2** — Compound: an inflated self-estimate + its source (knowledge without love) + its outward divisiveness.

**T1.3.1** — Its structural opposite, named in the text, is **love** — "Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (1 Corinthians 8:1).

**T1.3.2** — It excludes love, edification, and right self-measure ("not to think of himself more highly than he ought", Romans 12:3, the corrective register).

**T1.3.3** — E is not the elevation of A (real height claimed) nor the boast of D (declared object); it is the **empty swelling** of the mind — much volume, no substance ("puffed up without reason", Colossians 2:18).

**T1.4.1** — It operates as a cognitive state: "puffed up" (fusioō, 1 Corinthians 4:18; 5:2; 8:1) and "conceited" (tufoō, 1 Timothy 3:6; 6:4).

**T1.4.2** — It varies in occasion: puffed up by knowledge (1 Corinthians 8:1), by partisanship (1 Corinthians 4:6), by status (1 Timothy 3:6), by controversy (1 Timothy 6:4).

**T1.4.3** — **SILENT [data-silence]** on a distinct speech-mode (though it produces "envy, dissension, slander", 1 Timothy 6:4).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate fruit is divisive comparison — "puffed up in favor of one against another" (1 Corinthians 4:6).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent: inflation expressed as the elevation of self over fellow-believers.

**T1.6.1** — It produces "envy, dissension, slander, evil suspicions" (1 Timothy 6:4) and resistance to correction (1 Corinthians 4:18).

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the swollen comparison; sustained = the strife and unteachability it breeds.

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold where **knowledge runs ahead of love** (1 Corinthians 8:1) and where status comes too soon ("not a recent convert, or he may become puffed up", 1 Timothy 3:6). Never operates rightly [structural].

**T1.7.2** — Resisted by love that builds up (1 Corinthians 8:1) and by sober self-measure (Romans 12:3). On another spirit — *inferential:* the new convert "puffed up" may "fall into the condemnation of the devil" (1 Timothy 3:6).

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, the person holds knowledge under love and keeps a sober estimate of self.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — fusioō ("inflate, puff up", from fusa, bellows), fusiōsis ("a puffing up"), tufoō ("to befog with smoke → make conceited"), huperfroneō / hupsēlofroneō ("think too highly / be high-minded"). Roots: bellows-wind and smoke — **emptiness made to look like substance**.

**T7.1.2** — Verbs (fusioō, tufoō — the puffing/befogging) and the noun fusiōsis (the puffed-up state). The verb-dominance shows E as an ongoing inflation.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from "puffed up" (literal swelling-metaphor) to "conceited/befogged" — the loss of clear sight in self-importance.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — verb (fusioō, the inflating) versus noun (fusiōsis, the inflated condition).

**T7.1.5** — Its named opposite is **love** (1 Corinthians 8:1) — uniquely, the structural opposite is given in the text, and it is love, not humility.

**T7.1.6** — Partial — "the conceited" (tetufōmenos, 1 Timothy 6:4; 2 Timothy 3:4) functions as a person-type.

**T7.1.7** — None.

**T7.1.8** — E has **no OT counterpart** — it is a NT (Pauline) characterisation; the bellows/smoke imagery is new, marking a development specific to the apostolic correction of the Corinthian mind.

**T7.1.9** — fusioō and tufoō are effectively the **NT's distinctive coinage-register** for this inner swelling — their vividness (bellows, smoke) marks a fresh naming of conceit.

**T7.1.10** — The arc is narrow and NT-bound: the inflation of a mind that has knowledge without love.

**T7.2.1** — In the anchor, fusioō carries the contrast of the whole sentence: "Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (1 Corinthians 8:1).

**T7.2.2** — Forms: epistle (1 Corinthians, Colossians, 1–2 Timothy). The pastoral-corrective argument requires reading E as a named fault under apostolic admonition.

**T7.2.3** — The key argument: knowledge inflates, love edifies; therefore knowledge must be governed by love (1 Corinthians 8:1).

**T7.2.4** — Settings: pastoral and ecclesial (community division, eldership qualification — 1 Timothy 3:6).

**T7.2.5** — Anchor: **1 Corinthians 8:1** — "Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" — the verse that defines E and names its cure.

**T7.2.6** — 1 Corinthians 8:1 shows what no other verse does: E's root is **knowledge severed from love**, and its remedy is not less knowledge but love.

**T7.3.1** — *(science, M08 extract §2)* The cognitive component of narcissism (belief in one's superiority) is the most useful lens for E.

**T7.3.2** — *(science)* Narcissism research on the superiority-belief (extract §2) illuminates E's inflated self-estimate; the "knowledge puffs up" dynamic matches the cognitive-overconfidence findings.

**T7.3.3** — *(science)* Science treats overconfidence as a cognitive bias; the biblical material frames E as a **moral** disorder cured by love, not merely a calibration error (extract T7.3.3).

**T7.3.4** — *(science)* Science does not pair conceit with **love** as its corrective; the verse evidence's antithesis (knowledge vs love) is an aspect the framework does not supply — no further verse gap, but a point where the text leads the science.
