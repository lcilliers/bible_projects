## 1. Definition (working)

Pride as **inflation** — the mind's empty swelling, an over-rating of the self grounded in knowledge, standing, or wealth that has no real substance behind it. Where A *elevates* the self (height), E *inflates* it (air).

## 2. Evidence base

**13 occurrences**, almost entirely NT and Corinthian-concentrated: fusioō (G5448, 7 — all in 1 Corinthians / Colossians), tufoō (G5187, 3), fusiōsis (G5450, 1), huperfroneō (G5252, 1), hupsēlofroneō (G5309, 1). hupsēlos (G5308) is treated under A.

## 3. Analysis (observation → interpretation)

**The image is inflation, not elevation.** fusioō (to puff up, from the bellows) and tufoō (to be wrapped in smoke/vapour) present pride as empty swelling: "puffed up without reason by his sensuous mind" (Colossians 2:18); "puffed up with conceit and understands nothing" (1 Timothy 6:4); "swollen with conceit" (2 Timothy 3:4). This distinguishes E from A: A raises the self to a height; E fills it with air.

**Its characteristic cause is knowledge.** "This 'knowledge' puffs up, but love builds up" (1 Corinthians 8:1). Conceit inflates over what one knows or one's spiritual standing — the besetting danger of the recent convert (1 Timothy 3:6) and the rich (1 Timothy 6:17).

**Its antithesis is love, not (primarily) humility.** Where B defines itself against humility, E defines itself against love: "knowledge puffs up, but **love** builds up" (1 Corinthians 8:1); "**love**… is not arrogant" (1 Corinthians 13:4). The contrast is build-up vs puff-up — substance vs air.

**It is cognitive and divisive.** The faculty tags are cognition (huperfroneō, hupsēlofroneō): "not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think" (Romans 12:3). And it breeds faction: "puffed up in favour of one against another" (1 Corinthians 4:6); listed with "quarrelling… conceit, and disorder" (2 Corinthians 12:20).

**Interpretation.** E is the cognitive register of pride — the self over-estimated in the mind, hollow at the centre, and socially corrosive. It is the form pride takes around knowledge and new standing, answered not by being told to lower oneself (A's correction) but by love that builds the other up.

## 4. Synthesis

Conceit is pride as empty inflation: the mind swollen with an over-estimate of itself, grounded in knowledge, office, or wealth but hollow at the core ("puffed up… understands nothing"). Its imagery is the bellows and the smoke; its seat is the mind; its cause is knowledge; its antithesis is the love that builds others up rather than the self. It is the besetting pride of the newly-advanced and a seedbed of faction — the cognitive, NT-developed register of the cluster.

## 5. Expected but absent — special attention

1. **Cause is present and specific (unlike A/B).** E names its elicitor — "knowledge puffs up" (1 Corinthians 8:1). A genuine datum present; E is the best-caused characteristic in the cluster.
2. **Faculty under-tagged on the core terms.** The cognition tag sits on huperfroneō/hupsēlofroneō, but the inflation core (fusioō, tufoō) carries **no** faculty tag despite being plainly mental. → minor flag: recommend CC review whether fusioō/tufoō should carry a cognition (or affect) faculty.
3. **No OT membership — a cross-testament finding, not a gap.** E is essentially absent from the OT; the OT renders pride as height/loftiness (A/B), not inflation. This looks like a genuine NT development of the pride vocabulary (the inflation metaphor), not a coverage hole — the OT simply uses different imagery. → record as a cross-testament register-shift (consistent with D's §5.3), distinct from D's *possible* coverage gap.
4. **Antithesis differs from B's.** E's opposite is love; B's is humility. The two counter-poles (love / humility) are worth carrying to the synthesis as distinct correctives.

## 6. Reflection (labelled — not a conclusion)

- E's "knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" has obvious bearing on intellectual and spiritual life; held descriptively.
- **Cross-cultural:** the knowledge/status inflation E targets is recognisable across cultures, but its framing as a *Corinthian* church problem (spiritual-gift and knowledge rivalry) is community-specific. → flag for reader-facing.
- The love/humility split between E and B is a candidate observation for the cluster synthesis.
