## 1. Definition (working)

The **active, heart-seated motion of raising oneself above one's proper place** — comparatively above other people, and ultimately above God. Its master image is verticality: lifting up, exalting, ascending, soaring, towering, setting oneself on high.

## 2. Evidence base

**62 focus occurrences**, six lemmas: rum (H7311A, 32), ga.vah (H1361, 15), rum (H7312, 5), go.vah (H1363, 5), hupsēlos (G5308, 3), ga.ah (H1342, 1), zud (H2103, Aramaic, 1). *(go.vah and rum H7312 are the noun/abstract form of the lifted-up state and straddle B — multi-membership noted, not relocated.)*

### 2a. Core self-exaltation (the majority — human self-lifting)
- **Seated in the heart.** The lifting happens *in the heart*: "your heart be lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "his heart shall be exalted" (Daniel 11:12); "your heart is proud… 'I am a god'" (Ezekiel 28:2); "their heart was lifted up; therefore they forgot me" (Hosea 13:6); "before destruction a man's heart is haughty" (Proverbs 18:12). The seat field is explicit "heart" in 23 of 62; once "soul/heart" (Psalm 13:2), once "spirit" (Daniel 5:20). Where the seat is unstated the verses do not contradict it.
- **Directed above others.** "his heart… lifted up above his brothers" (Deuteronomy 17:20); "my enemy be exalted over me" (Psalm 13:2).
- **Directed above / against God — the terminal form.** "I will ascend to heaven; above the stars of God" (Isaiah 14:13); "exalt himself… above every god… against the God of gods" (Daniel 11:36); "make your heart like the heart of a god" (Ezekiel 28:2); a "high hand… reviles the Lord" (Numbers 15:30); "raised your voice… against the Holy One" (2 Kings 19:22).

### 2b. Divine-frame occurrences (God lifts up / brings low — NOT human pride)
Carried within A's lemmas but belonging to the evaluative frame (→ cluster analysis, per fork 5): "it is God who… putting down one and lifting up another" (Psalm 75:7); "the horns of the righteous shall be lifted up" (Psalm 75:10); "the great in height will be hewn down" (Isaiah 10:33, divine agent); "the Lord is high" (Psalm 138:6); "the Lord alone will be exalted" (Isaiah 2:11, 17). These are retained here and tagged, not folded into the human characteristic.

### 2c. Per-occurrence filter / cross-membership (flagged, not dropped — FA-13)
- Exodus 14:8 — "going out **defiantly**" (high hand = Israel's bold exodus; valence blank). The idiom here is neutral bold action, not self-exaltation. → occurrence-level set-aside candidate.
- 1 Kings 11:26, 11:27 — "**lifted up his hand against the king**" (Jeroboam's revolt). The idiom is political rebellion, not heart-pride. → cross-membership/​filter candidate.
- Numbers 15:30 — "**high hand**" = defiant sin against the Lord. Primary membership **C1** (presumption/defiance); cross-listed in A as the self-exalting form of defiance.
- Proverbs 14:29 — "a hasty temper **exalts folly**" (rum of folly, not the self). → filter candidate.
- Psalm 12:8 — "vileness is **exalted** among the children of man" (social elevation of evil, not a person's self-exaltation). → filter candidate.
- Job 10:16 — "were my head **lifted up**, you would hunt me" (Job; neutral metaphor of being hunted). → filter candidate.

## 3. Analysis (observation → interpretation)

**Valence.** Of 59 valenced occurrences, 45 are sinful, 7 neutral, 6 forbidden, 1 righteous. The neutral band is almost entirely the divine-frame and idiom occurrences (2b/2c); the forbidden band is prohibition ("Do not lift up your horn", Psalm 75:4–5; "be not proud", Jeremiah 13:15; "do not become proud", Romans 11:20); the single righteous occurrence is God's vindication of the righteous (Psalm 75:10), not human self-exaltation. **Interpretation:** human self-initiated lifting-up is never righteous in this corpus. Exaltation is legitimate only when God is its agent or object — a structural asymmetry that anticipates D's "boast in the Lord."

**Object / direction.** object_type splits abstract (24), person (15), God (6). Self-exaltation is intrinsically **comparative and relational** — it raises the self *above* something. Its endpoint is the displacement of God: the proud heart proceeds to forget him (Deuteronomy 8:14; Hosea 13:6), revile him (Numbers 15:30), and finally to claim deity (Ezekiel 28:2; Daniel 11:36; Isaiah 14:13).

**Cause — a recovered motif (field-silent, verse-loud).** The `cause` field is populated in only 2 of 62. Yet the verses carry a strong, repeated cause: **prosperity, strength, wealth, and height precede the lifting of the heart** — "you… are full… then your heart be lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "they had grazed… were filled… their heart was lifted up" (Hosea 13:6); "when he was strong, he grew proud" (2 Chronicles 26:16); "your heart has become proud in your wealth" (Ezekiel 28:5); "because it towered high… its heart was proud" (Ezekiel 31:10). This is a field/method silence, not an evidence silence — see §5.

**The pride→fall structure.** The corpus repeatedly pairs the lifting with a coming descent, usually by God's hand: "to bring them down" (2 Samuel 22:28); "Pride goes before… a fall" (Proverbs 16:18); "I will bring you down" (Jeremiah 49:16; Obadiah 4); "he was brought down from his kingly throne" (Daniel 5:20); "the lofty pride of men shall be brought low" (Isaiah 2:17). Self-exaltation is presented as structurally self-defeating against the God who alone exalts and abases.

**Experiencer.** "other" (32) and "other (addressed)" (9) dominate; only one "self" — and it is the villain's self-quote, "I will ascend" (Isaiah 14:13). The single believing first-person use is a **denial**: "my heart is not lifted up" (Psalm 131:1). **Interpretation:** self-exaltation is named in the other or repudiated in the self; it is almost never owned in the first person.

**NT cognitive register.** The three hupsēlos occurrences add a mental form — "what is exalted among men is an abomination" (Luke 16:15); "do not become proud, but fear" (Romans 11:20); "Do not be haughty… Never be wise in your own sight" (Romans 12:16) — all in warning mood. This bridges A to E (conceit) without collapsing into it: here the image is still height (hupsēlos = high), not inflation.

## 4. Synthesis

Self-exaltation is the heart's vertical motion: it lifts the self above its station, drawing on the fuel of success, and aims — at its limit — at the place of God. It is comparative (above brothers, over enemies) before it is absolute (above God), and its characteristic fruit is the forgetting and defying of God. It is presented as **legitimate only in reverse** — as something God does (raising the lowly, exalting the righteous) — never as a self-generated good. The corpus binds it tightly to a downward counter-motion: the high are brought low. Within M08 it is the **active counterpart to B (settled pride)**: A is the lifting; B is the height once attained.

The verses lean "within-person" (the heart lifts *itself*), but the field cannot confirm. → flag for a future origin pass (additional discovery needed).
3. **Divine relation — UNDER-CAPTURED.** `divine_involvement` is populated only 4/62, surprisingly low for the most God-relational characteristic. The God-relation is real but lives in `object_type=God` (6) and the verse argument, not the role-field. → note for cluster analysis; do not read the low count as "God largely absent."
4. **Affect — EXPECTED, LARGELY ABSENT.** Only 3 faculty tags (all cognition). One might expect self-exaltation to carry an affective signature (elation, self-satisfaction). The corpus presents it as a **posture of the heart**, not a felt emotion — consistent with the heart-seat / faculty-absent pattern across M08. Whether self-exaltation has an affective core is under-determined by this extract. → an affect/faculty pass on A's terms would help (additional discovery).
5. **A genuinely expected element that IS absent: righteous human self-exaltation.** One might expect a positive "lifting of the head" (honour/vindication). It does not occur self-initiated here; human lifting-up is righteous only when God performs it (Psalm 75:10). The asymmetry is itself a finding (§3, valence), not a gap to be filled.

## 6. Reflection (labelled — not a conclusion)

- The prosperity→pride cause and the pride→fall structure carry an obvious application caution (success, not adversity, as the spiritual danger-point), but the corpus states this descriptively; I do not press it into a rule.
- **Cross-cultural note:** verticality-as-pride (high/low, above/below) is a near-universal metaphor; what is distinctive here is the *theological vertical* — the self raised against God, with God as the sole legitimate exalter. Honour-shame readers (for whom status-elevation is socially central) will weigh the "lifted up above his brothers" material differently from individualist readers. → flag for the reader-facing phase.
- **Additional discovery that would help:** an origin pass; an affect/faculty pass on A's terms; and a test of whether the prosperity-cause motif recurs in B and C (cross-characteristic pattern).
