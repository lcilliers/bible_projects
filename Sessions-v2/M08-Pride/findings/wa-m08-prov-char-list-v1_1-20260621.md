## Cross-cutting observations

1. **Seat = the heart, dominantly.** "your heart be lifted up" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "his heart was proud" (Ezekiel 28:2; 2 Chronicles 32:25); "everyone who is arrogant in heart" (Proverbs 16:5); "the pride of your heart" (Obadiah 3). The `faculty` field is mostly absent because these terms are dispositions/orientations of the whole self, heart-seated — not discrete faculties.
2. **M08 carries a substantial legitimate / righteous pole** (boasting *in the Lord*; God's majesty; God opposing the proud and exalting the lowly). This is the most distinctive structural feature and is preserved chiefly in characteristic D and in the cluster-level divine frame.
3. **The height/exaltation metaphor is the master image** — pride conceived spatially as the self raised above its proper place, above others and above God.

## Provisional characteristics (7) — hypotheses grounded in the cited verses

### A — Self-exaltation: lifting the self up above its place
Active motion of raising oneself — heart "lifted up", exalting self, lofty/haughty eyes, ascending above one's station.
- *Grounding:* "your heart be lifted up, and you forget the Lord" (Deuteronomy 8:14); "I will ascend to heaven… I will set my throne on high" (Isaiah 14:13); "how lofty are their eyes" (Proverbs 30:13).
- *Core lemmas:* rum (H7311A, 32), ga.vah (H1361, 15), ga.ah (H1342), zud (H2103, Aram. — straddles C1), rum (H7312), go.vah (H1363), hupsēlos (G5308, shared E).

### B — Settled pride: the entrenched state of haughtiness / loftiness
Stative condition — "the proud", "a haughty spirit", "the pride of Moab/Judah/Assyria"; frequently corporate/national.
- *Grounding:* "Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall" (Proverbs 16:18); "the pride of Israel testifies to his face" (Hosea 5:5); "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5).
- *Core lemmas:* ga.on (H1347, 28), ga.a.vah (H1346, 12), ge.eh (H1343, 9), huperēfanos (G5244, 5), plus ge.vah (H1466), gav.hut (H1365), ge (H1341), ge.ah (H1344), sha.chats (H7830), ra.hav (H7295), ge.vah (H1467, Aram.), huperēfania (G5243), and human-pride occurrences of ge.ut (H1348).

### C1 — Presumption / defiance toward God and appointed authority
Acting above one's station against God's command or appointed authority — overreach, defiance, self-will against what is ordained.
- *Grounding:* "acts presumptuously by not obeying the priest… or the judge" (Deuteronomy 17:12); "the prophet who presumes to speak a word in my name that I have not commanded" (Deuteronomy 18:20); "she has proudly defied the Lord" (Jeremiah 50:29); "Bold and willful… they despise authority" (2 Peter 2:10).
- *Core lemmas:* zid (H2102, 9), za.don (H2087, partly), tolmētēs (G5113), authadēs (G0829), zud (H2103, Aram., straddles A).

### C2 — Insolence / contempt toward other people
Insolent contempt, derision, and oppression directed at others — the haughty turned against neighbour, elder, or the lowly.
- *Grounding:* "the insolent utterly deride me" (Psalm 119:51); "let not the insolent oppress me" (Psalm 119:122); "the youth will be insolent to the elder" (Isaiah 3:5); "By insolence comes nothing but strife" (Proverbs 13:10).
- *Core lemmas:* zed (H2086, 13), ra.hav (H7292), shal.le.tet (H7986), za.don (H2087, partly).

> **C1/C2 straddle note:** zed and za.don each appear in both directions (e.g. zed "wander from your commandments", Psalm 119:21 → toward God; "the insolent oppress me" → toward others). These are split **per occurrence** in the detail phase, not by whole lemma.

### D — Boasting and glorying: verbal/affective self-display (TWO POLES)
- **Sinful pole:** boasting in flesh/works/law/riches/wisdom/might/idols/self; empty bragging. "Let not the wise man boast in his wisdom…" (Jeremiah 9:23); "Do not boast about tomorrow" (Proverbs 27:1); "All such boasting is evil" (James 4:16).
- **Righteous pole:** glorying in the Lord, the cross, one's weakness, or fellow believers in Christ. "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 1:31; 2 Corinthians 10:17); "boast… of my weaknesses" (2 Corinthians 12:9); "I have reason to be proud of my work for God" (Romans 15:17).
- *Core lemmas:* kauchaomai (G2744, 32), kauchēsis (G2746, 12), kauchēma (G2745, 11), ha.lal (H1984H, 10, predominantly sinful pole), alazōn (G0213), alazoneia (G0212).
- *Both poles are intrinsic and must be preserved.*

### E — Conceit / being puffed-up: inflated self-estimation (mind-leaning, NT)
Cognitive over-inflation — "puffed up", "swollen with conceit", thinking more highly of oneself than one ought.
- *Grounding:* "this 'knowledge' puffs up, but love builds up" (1 Corinthians 8:1); "puffed up with conceit and understands nothing" (1 Timothy 6:4); "not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think" (Romans 12:3).
- *Core lemmas:* fusioō (G5448, 7), tufoō (G5187, 3), fusiōsis (G5450), huperfroneō (G5252), hupsēlofroneō (G5309); hupsēlos (G5308) shared with A.

### F — Self-love / self-sufficiency
The self turned in on itself as its own centre/god.
- *Grounding:* "people will be lovers of self" (2 Timothy 3:2); "you say in your heart, 'I am, and there is no one besides me'" (Isaiah 47:8); "In the pride of his face the wicked… 'There is no God'" (Psalm 10:4); "the pride of life… is from the world" (1 John 2:16).
- *Lemmas:* filautos (G5367), a.din (H5719); overlaps authadēs (C1) and alazoneia (D, "pride of life").
