F is never borne by God; it is the creature speaking as if it were God.

**T0.2.1** — It leads the person to a false security ("I shall not sit as a widow", Isaiah 47:8) and to being unloving toward others ("lovers of self… unloving, unforgiving", 2 Timothy 3:2–3).

**T0.2.2** — Fallen condition.

**T0.2.3** — None.

**T0.3.2** — Exclusively creaturely.

**T0.3.3** — Its presence shows the image at its furthest disorder — the creature treating itself as ultimate.

**T0.4.1** — *Inferential:* Isaiah 47's self-sufficient Babylon ("I am, none besides me") reads typologically of every power that deifies itself; otherwise [data-silence].

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Self-love/self-sufficiency*: the self turned ultimately on itself as both object of love and ground of security. The name signals the **terminus** of pride — the self as its own end.

**T1.1.2** — filautos ("self-loving", phil- + autos), a.din ("luxuriant, given to pleasure, self-secure"). The two terms name the two faces: self-**love** (filautos) and self-**sufficiency/ease** (a.din).

**T1.1.3** — An **inward curvature** — love and trust bent back on the self rather than directed to God and neighbour.

**T1.2.1** — A **disposition/condition** (a settled orientation of love and confidence), not an act.

**T1.2.2** — Compound: self-directed love + self-grounded security — the affective and the volitional curved inward together.

**T1.3.1** — Its structural opposite is **love of God and neighbour** ("you shall love the Lord… and your neighbour"); F is "lovers of self" set against "lovers of God" (2 Timothy 3:2, 4).

**T1.3.2** — It excludes dependence on God and regard for others ("unloving, unforgiving, slanderous", 2 Timothy 3:3).

**T1.3.3** — F is not the act of lifting (A), the height (B), the defiance (C1), the contempt (C2), the boast (D), or the inflation (E); it is the **settled self-orientation** those movements terminate in.

**T1.4.1** — It operates as a settled self-predication — "you said in your heart, 'I am, and there is no one besides me'" (Isaiah 47:10) — and as a list-named character trait ("lovers of self", 2 Timothy 3:2).

**T1.4.2** — It varies between corporate self-sufficiency (Babylon, Isaiah 47) and individual self-love (the last-days character list, 2 Timothy 3:2).

**T1.4.3** — Yes — it speaks the self-sufficient claim "in the heart" (Isaiah 47:8, 10).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate correlate is **false security** — "I shall not sit as a widow or know the loss of children" (Isaiah 47:8).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent: a settled, complacent self-confidence.

**T1.6.1** — It establishes complacent security and a love-disordered character (2 Timothy 3:2–4); it sets up **sudden ruin** ("these two things shall come to you in a moment", Isaiah 47:9).

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the complacent self-assurance; sustained = the love turned inward and the ruin it invites.

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold in unchecked prosperity and power ("you felt secure in your wickedness", Isaiah 47:10). Never operates rightly [structural].

**T1.7.2** — Resisted by the love of God and neighbour that decentres the self (2 Timothy 3:2–4 by antithesis). On another spirit — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, love is directed to God and others rather than the self.

*Coverage T1: 18 of 18 (answered chiefly from the two terms and the Isaiah 47 anchor).*

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — filautos ("self-loving", phil-autos), a.din ("luxuriant, voluptuous, self-secure"). Roots: *loving the self* and *settled in ease* — affect and security both turned inward.

**T7.1.2** — Adjectives — filautos ("self-loving", a person-type) and a.din ("given to ease"). The adjectival form names a **character**, not an act.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from "self-loving" (the disordered love) to "luxuriant/secure" (the complacent ease) — narrow but pointed.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — filautos (the disposition of self-love) and a.din (the condition of self-secure ease) name the two faces.

**T7.1.5** — Its opposite is named in context: **lovers of God** versus "lovers of self" (2 Timothy 3:2, 4); love of neighbour.

**T7.1.6** — Yes — filautos *is* the person-type ("lovers of self").

**T7.1.7** — None.

**T7.1.8** — F's OT register (a.din, self-secure ease — Isaiah 47) and its NT register (filautos, self-love — 2 Timothy 3:2) are **not lexically continuous** but are conceptually one — the self made ultimate; a thematic, not verbal, continuity.

**T7.1.9** — filautos is effectively a **NT naming** of self-love as a character vice — a fresh lexicalisation of the cluster's terminus.

**T7.1.10** — The arc is short but decisive: from self-secure ease (Isaiah 47) to self-love heading the last-days vice-list (2 Timothy 3:2) — the self as its own god and its own beloved.

**T7.2.1** — In the anchor, a.din/the "I am" claim carries Babylon's whole self-deifying boast: "you said in your heart, 'I am, and there is no one besides me'" (Isaiah 47:10).

**T7.2.2** — Forms: prophecy (the taunt-oracle against Babylon, Isaiah 47) and epistle (the vice-list, 2 Timothy 3). The taunt-oracle requires reading the "I am" as the usurpation of God's self-naming.

**T7.2.3** — The argument (Isaiah 47): the self that claims "I am, none besides me" will meet, suddenly, the very losses it denied — self-sufficiency invites ruin.

**T7.2.4** — Settings: judicial/eschatological (the oracle of Babylon's fall) and ethical (the last-days character description).

**T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Isaiah 47:8, 10** — "I am, and there is no one besides me" — F's essence as the creature usurping God's self-predication.

**T7.2.6** — Isaiah 47:8–10 shows what no other verse in the cluster shows: pride's terminus is **practical self-deification** — the self speaking the words that belong to God alone.

**T7.3.1** — *(science, M08 extract §2/§4)* Narcissism (self-love as trait) and self-enhancement research are the most useful lenses for F.

**T7.3.2** — *(science)* The self-enhancement bias (extract §4) and trait-narcissism (extract §2) illuminate F's settled self-reference and false self-sufficiency.

**T7.3.3** — *(science)* Science frames self-love on a spectrum (healthy self-regard to pathological narcissism); the biblical material frames F as **idolatry of the self** — the creature claiming God's "I am" — a register science does not reach (extract T7.3.3; the theological distinctive).

**T7.3.4** — *(science)* Science cannot supply the category F's anchor requires — the **self usurping divine self-predication**; this is the cluster's clearest outside-science edge, already flagged in the extract's outside-science inventory (the ontological majesty of God).
