## 1. Definition (working)

The audible, affective overflow of the heart's confidence — the point at which the inner state declares its **ground**. Uniquely in M08, boasting is morally **bivalent**: the identical act is sinful or righteous entirely according to its *object*.

## 2. Evidence base

**69 occurrences.** kauchaomai (G2744, 32), kauchēsis (G2746, 12), kauchēma (G2745, 11), ha.lal (H1984H, 10), alazōn (G0213, 2), alazoneia (G0212, 2). kauchaomai/kauchēma carry `faculty=affect` — D is the one characteristic with a consistent faculty tag.

### 2a. Sinful / forbidden pole — object = flesh, self, works, riches, wisdom, idols
"Let not the wise man boast in his wisdom… the mighty in his might… the rich in his riches" (Jeremiah 9:23); "boast of the abundance of their riches" (Psalm 49:6); "make their boast in worthless idols" (Psalm 97:7); "boast in your flesh" (Galatians 6:13, seated in flesh); "boast in the law" (Romans 2:23); "you boast in your arrogance. All such boasting is evil" (James 4:16); "so that no one may boast" re works (Ephesians 2:9); "no human being might boast in the presence of God" (1 Corinthians 1:29). The "pride of life" belongs here (1 John 2:16, seated in flesh).

### 2b. Righteous / commanded pole — object = the Lord, Christ, the cross, weakness, God
"Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 1:31, commanded; 2 Corinthians 10:17); "far be it from me to boast except in the cross" (Galatians 6:14); "glory in Christ Jesus and put no confidence in the flesh" (Philippians 3:3); "I will boast… of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest" (2 Corinthians 12:9); "Let the lowly brother boast in his exaltation" (James 1:9, commanded); "we rejoice in God" (Romans 5:11); the seat here can be the conscience — "the testimony of our conscience" (2 Corinthians 1:12).

### 2c. Neutral band — Paul's measured apostolic self-reference
"boast only with regard to the area… God assigned to us" (2 Corinthians 10:13); "not… beyond limit" (2 Corinthians 10:15); proverbial observation (Proverbs 20:14).

## 3. Analysis (observation → interpretation)

**The discriminator is the object/ground, not the act.** The same verb, the same affect, points either to the flesh/self (sin) or to the Lord (righteousness). Moral status is wholly a function of the ground — which is why boasting can be both "evil" (James 4:16) and "commanded" (1 Corinthians 1:31). **This is the theological centre of M08:** boasting per se is not condemned; *misplaced* boasting is.

**The righteous pole inverts pride's logic.** "I will boast… of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me" (2 Corinthians 12:9). The legitimate boast glories not in strength but in weakness, displaying God's power rather than the self's. This is the precise antithesis of A (self-exaltation): instead of lifting the self, one lowers the self to exalt God. D's righteous pole is the **redemptive counter-movement** to A and B.

**Boasting reveals where trust is placed.** The sinful boast is bound to reliance — "those who trust in their wealth and boast of their riches" (Psalm 49:6); "trusted in her treasures… boast of your valleys" (Jeremiah 49:4); "rely on the law and boast in God" (Romans 2:17). **Interpretation:** what one boasts in externalises what one trusts; D is diagnostic of the heart's confidence.

**Seat tracks the object.** Unlike A/B (heart-dominant), D's seat varies with its ground — flesh for the fleshly boast (Galatians 6:13; 1 Corinthians 1:29), soul for "boasts of the desires of his soul" (Psalm 10:3), conscience for the godly boast (2 Corinthians 1:12), and the contrast "what is in the heart" vs "outward appearance" (2 Corinthians 5:12).

## 4. Synthesis

Boasting is the heart's confidence made audible — the inner state declaring its ground before an audience (before God, before the churches, before the Lord at his coming). Uniquely in M08 it is morally bivalent: boasting in the flesh, in works, in riches, in the self is condemned, while boasting in the Lord, in the cross, in one's own weakness is not merely allowed but commanded. The righteous pole inverts the whole cluster's logic — glorying in weakness so that God's power is displayed — and so stands as the redemptive answer to self-exaltation (A) and settled pride (B). What a person boasts in reveals what a person trusts.
