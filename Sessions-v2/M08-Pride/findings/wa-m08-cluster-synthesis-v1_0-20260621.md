## 0. The characteristics in view

Seven characteristics, kept distinct by researcher decision (bundling reduces accuracy):

- **A — Self-exaltation:** the active lifting of the self (rum, ga.vah, ga.ah; hupsēlos).
- **B — Settled pride:** the entrenched, often corporate, state of haughtiness (ga.on, ga.a.vah, ge.eh; huperēfanos).
- **C1 — Presumption / defiance toward God and authority:** the self-willed refusal of ordained limit (zid, za.don; authadēs, tolmētēs).
- **C2 — Insolence / contempt toward other people:** pride turned outward as derision and oppression (zed, za.don, ra.hav, shal.le.tet).
- **D — Boasting / glorying:** the declared exultation, two-poled by its object (kauchaomai, kauchēsis/kauchēma, ha.lal; alazōn, alazoneia).
- **E — Conceit / puffed-up:** the inflation of the mind (fusioō, tufoō, fusiōsis; huperfroneō, hupsēlofroneō).
- **F — Self-love / self-sufficiency:** the terminus — the self as its own god and beloved (filautos; a.din).

## 1. The field model (the cluster's internal architecture)

*This section is framing — it states the structure the by-tier synthesis below substantiates from the verses.*

The cluster is not a list of synonyms but a single reality worked out in modes that relate to one another along a small number of axes:

**The core disposition.** **B (settled pride)** is the resting condition of the family — the entrenched height of heart that the others arise from or express. It carries the cluster's defining theological frame: "God opposes the proud, but gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6).

**The vertical axis — act and state (A ↔ B).** **A** is the *motion* of self-lifting; **B** is the *state* of height attained. They share the height-roots g-'-h and r-w-m, and the seam where one becomes the other is visible in a single verse: "it towered high… its heart was proud of its height" (Ezekiel 31:10). A → B is developmental (lifting settles into condition).

**The directional axis — against whom (C1 ↔ C2).** From the settled disposition the pride discharges outward in two directions, sharing the root z-d: **C1** against God and his appointed authority ("she has proudly defied the Lord", Jeremiah 50:29), **C2** against other people ("the insolent utterly deride me", Psalm 119:51). One disposition, two objects.

**The voiced form, two-poled (D).** **D** is pride made audible — but uniquely it bifurcates by object. Its sinful pole voices A/B ("you boast in your arrogance", James 4:16); its righteous pole voices faith and is *commanded* ("Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord", 1 Corinthians 1:31). D shares ha.lal with praise.

**The cognitive corner (E).** **E** is pride localised in the mind — inflation rather than elevation (the bellows fusioō, the befogging smoke tufoō). It meets A at the high-minded compounds (hupsēlofroneō) but differs in kind: hollow swelling, not solid height. Its named opposite is **love**, not humility (1 Corinthians 8:1).

**The terminus (F).** **F** is where the whole movement arrives: the self made ultimate. A reaches *toward* God's place ("I will make myself like the Most High", Isaiah 14:14); F *occupies* it in speech ("I am, and there is no one besides me", Isaiah 47:8). F is small in lexical base and decisive in structure.

**The single counter-current.** Only **D's righteous pole** runs the other way — glory referred away from the self to God. It is the cluster's one point of created-design positivity, eschatological hope, and constructive relation.

**The unifying logic.** The whole field is organised around one theological reality: the creature's relation to the exaltedness, majesty, and glory that belong to God alone. Pride is the disordered seizure of it; right boasting is its rightly-ordered return. The seat is the **heart** throughout; the occasion is **prosperity/fullness/sufficiency**; the end is being **brought low** — except where the boast is in the Lord, whose end is glory.

## 2. Synthesis by tier

### T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

Across all seven, the characteristic is **never borne by God** — a uniform finding. God enters the cluster's verses as the **object** the self exalts against (A: "above the stars of God", Isaiah 14:13; C1: "proudly defied the Lord", Jeremiah 50:29; F: the self taking God's own "I am", Isaiah 47:8) or as the **opponent and judge** who abases it (A/B: "God opposes the proud", James 4:6; "the Lord tears down the house of the proud", Proverbs 15:25). The one place the lemma-family names God is its legitimate counterpart — "the majesty of the Lord" (ge.ut, Isaiah 26:10): the divine reality the creature's pride counterfeits. The cluster's governing theological claim, then, is that **exaltation, majesty, and glory are God's prerogative**; pride is the creature's illegitimate seizure of them, "the Lord alone will be exalted" (Isaiah 2:11, 17).

On created design versus the fall, six of the seven are **wholly fallen** — they have no place in created design and no orientation toward future fullness; their only future is to be brought low. **D is the sole exception**, and the exception is structurally important: D answers "both" (T0.2.2) — its God-directed pole belongs to created design (boasting in the Lord is commanded) and its self-directed pole to the fall — and it alone carries an eschatological orientation, "we boast in hope of the glory of God" (Romans 5:2). D's right pole alone instantiates the divine image rightly (the creature glorying in its source); the rest, and F most extremely, are the image's **counter-form** — the creature in God's place.

Typology runs through three points: A's "I will ascend" read traditionally of the adversary (Isaiah 14:13, inferential); D's redirection to the cross ("boast… in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ", Galatians 6:14, christological); and F's self-deifying power (Isaiah 47, the creature usurping divine self-predication).

*Essay seam:* the cluster's deepest theme is set here — pride as the creature's disordered grasp at what is God's, with right boasting as its redeemed inverse. Isaiah 14:14 ("like the Most High") and Isaiah 47:8 ("I am, none besides me") frame the disorder; 1 Corinthians 1:31 ("boast in the Lord") states the cure.

### T1 — Definition

The seven name one reality — the self made high or made much of — in distinct **modes**: act (A), settled condition (B), self-willed defiance (C1), outward contempt (C2), declared exultation (D), mental inflation (E), and terminal self-orientation (F). The **kinds** differ accordingly: A is an act, B and F are conditions/dispositions, C1 and C2 are acts issuing from disposition, D is an affect-laden speech-act, E is a condition of the mind.

The **structural opposite** is, for most, humility/lowliness of heart ("my heart is not lifted up", Psalm 131:1; "gives grace to the humble", James 4:6) — but two exceptions are diagnostic: **E's** opposite is **love** ("Knowledge puffs up, but love builds up", 1 Corinthians 8:1), and **D's** right pole excludes not pride-of-heart but **self-reliance** ("so that no one may boast", Ephesians 2:9; "no confidence in the flesh", Philippians 3:3). The cluster's opposite is therefore primarily humility, with love and trust naming the boundary at its cognitive and declarative edges.

Three further uniformities. First, the cluster is a **speaking** family: every characteristic has a communicative mode — A's "I will ascend" (Isaiah 14:13), B's arrogant speech (Isaiah 9:9), C1's presumptuous word (Deuteronomy 18:20), C2's derision (Psalm 119:51), D's boast, F's "I am" (Isaiah 47:10). Second, the **conditions of taking hold** are consistently fullness and sufficiency — prosperity and strength (A: 2 Chronicles 26:16; B: Ezekiel 16:49; F: Isaiah 47:10), and, for E, knowledge ("knowledge puffs up", 1 Corinthians 8:1). Third, **none operates rightly** except D's God-directed pole — the only licit form in the family.

### T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

The **heart is the dominant seat** across the cluster (A, B, C1, C2, D, F are heart-located: "your heart be lifted up", Deuteronomy 8:14; "arrogant in heart", Proverbs 16:5; "you said in your heart, 'I am'", Isaiah 47:10). **B** engages the whole inner being — heart, soul, and spirit together (Proverbs 16:5; Amos 6:8; Ecclesiastes 7:8) — confirming pride as a **whole-self disposition** rather than a discrete faculty; this is why the faculty field reads as "absent" so often: the seat is the person, not an organ of the soul. **E is the exception** that proves the rule — it alone is seated in the **mind/cognition** ("puffed up… by his sensuous mind", Colossians 2:18) — and **D** alone foregrounds **affect** ("my soul makes its boast in the Lord", Psalm 34:2).

The **body link is expressive throughout** and never the seat: the haughty eyes and neck (A, B: Proverbs 30:13; Psalm 75:5), the stiffened neck (C1: Nehemiah 9:16), the boasting mouth (D). The direction is uniform — soul/spirit expressing outward through the body (the inner height shown in the lifted eye), "the haughty eyes you bring down" (Psalm 18:27).

**Origin** is uniformly **within the person**, self-generated and occasioned by prosperity — with one structurally crucial exception: **D's right pole originates in God-given knowledge received** ("that he… knows me", Jeremiah 9:24). No characteristic states an external-spirit origin; the only adversarial thread is inferential (Job 41:34, Leviathan "king over all the sons of pride"). This near-total silence on a spiritual-being origin is noted for Session D (§4).

### T3 — The Inner Faculties

The cluster engages the faculties **selectively**, and the pattern is itself a finding:

- **Volition** is engaged across most of the family and is the **core** of C1 (self-will, authadēs — "Bold and willful", 2 Peter 2:10); pride is substantially a matter of the will (A's fivefold "I will", Isaiah 14:13; D's chosen object, Galatians 6:14).
- **Agency** is strong wherever pride acts on others — A casting down (Daniel 11:12), B and C2 oppressing (Psalm 10:2; 119:85), C1 defying (Deuteronomy 1:43). The proud act from their height.
- **Relational capacity** is **damaged by all** — the self lifted above brothers, the lowly held in contempt (Deuteronomy 17:20; Psalm 123:4) — and is where **C2** concentrates. **D alone** carries a **constructive** relational mode: pride *in another* that honours them ("great is my pride in you", 2 Corinthians 7:4).
- **Moral evaluation** is doubly engaged: pride **is** judged (an abomination, sin — Proverbs 16:5; 21:4) and pride **inverts** evaluation (exalting folly and vileness — Psalm 12:8; calling wickedness safety — Isaiah 47:10; arrogance displacing grief over sin — 1 Corinthians 5:2).
- **Cognition** concentrates in **E** (the cognitive characteristic) and in **D's** right pole (knowledge of God); it is the NT register of **A** (high-mindedness).
- **Memory** is engaged by its **failure** — self-exaltation and settled pride both produce the forgetting of God ("their heart was lifted up; therefore they forgot me", Hosea 13:6; "they do not return to the Lord", Hosea 7:10). A shared, non-obvious finding (A, B).
- **Affect** is isolated in **D** — the cluster's one affect-bearing characteristic ("I will boast all the more gladly", 2 Corinthians 12:9); elsewhere pride is disposition, not feeling.
- **Conscience** is engaged chiefly by **suppression** (C1: "they do not tremble as they blaspheme", 2 Peter 2:10); otherwise largely silent.
- **Perception** appears as the **haughty eye** (A, B: Proverbs 30:13) and little else.
- **Creativity** is **uniformly silent** across all seven — a clean cross-cluster data-silence, not a gap in the reading.

*Synthesis:* the cluster's faculty-signature is **volition + agency + relational damage + moral inversion**, with affect isolated in D, cognition in E, a shared memory-failure (forgetting God) in A and B, and creativity uniformly absent.

### T4 — Relational Interfaces

This tier carries the cluster's **defining negative shape**. God does **not extend** pride to the person (T4.1), and pride is **not given or received** between persons as a gift (T4.3, T4.4): these are structural silences across A, B, C1, E, and F. Pride is not a relational good that passes between persons — it is self-generated and self-referring.

**D inverts this entirely.** Boasting is God-ward (T4.2: "in God we have boasted continually", Psalm 44:8), and it can pass constructively from person to person ("the proof… of our boasting about you", 2 Corinthians 8:24). D is the cluster's relational outlier precisely because it alone is object-directed rather than self-enclosed.

The **human-to-God** interface (T4.2) is, across the family, the cluster's worst and its best at once. Its worst is **usurpation and defiance** (A, C1, F: "I will make myself like the Most High", Isaiah 14:14; "proudly defied the Lord", Jeremiah 50:29; "I am, none besides me", Isaiah 47:8) and **disregard** (B, C2: "they do not return to the Lord", Hosea 7:10). Its best is **D's worshipful boasting in the Lord** — the same interface redeemed.

On **boundaries** (T4.5), pride operates across every relational distance — above brothers within the covenant (Deuteronomy 17:20), over enemies and the lowly (Psalm 13:2; 123:4), within the believing community (E: "one against another", 1 Corinthians 4:6) — and B adds a **corporate** mode (peoples bearing pride together: "the pride of Moab", Isaiah 16:6).

The **spiritual-being interface** (T4.6) is **near-uniformly silent**: the only threads are inferential — Isaiah 14 (A, the adversary's self-exaltation), Job 41:34 (B, Leviathan over the proud), 2 Peter 2:10 (C1, blaspheming the glorious ones), 1 Timothy 3:6 (E, "the condemnation of the devil"). No confirmed angelic mediation appears. This is carried to Session D (§4).

### T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

The cluster's developmental arc is **uniform and severe**: prosperity/fullness → pride (lifting, settling, defying, vaunting) → displacement of God and contempt of others → **being brought low**. The downfall comes by **divine judgment**, imposed and often sudden ("I will bring you down", Obadiah 4; "ruin shall come upon you suddenly", Isaiah 47:11); the **only off-ramp is self-humbling**, chosen before the fall (Hezekiah, "humbled himself for the pride of his heart", 2 Chronicles 32:26; Nebuchadnezzar, "those who walk in pride he is able to humble", Daniel 4:37).

Pride's relation to **suffering** (T5.4) is, for most of the family, that of a **producer** (it brings its own downfall) arising in **ease, not affliction** — and affliction or its threat **breaks** it (2 Chronicles 32:25–26). Two inversions stand out. **D's righteous pole** operates *in* suffering and is *perfected* by it — "I will boast of my weaknesses… that the power of Christ may rest upon me" (2 Corinthians 12:9); "we boast in our sufferings" (Romans 5:3): the cluster's one positive relation to affliction, where pride's logic is turned inside out. **C2** inverts differently: its developmental fruit appears not in the proud agent but in the **sufferer** who endures contempt and keeps faith ("The insolent utterly deride me, but I do not turn away from your law", Psalm 119:51).

The **eschatological** trajectory (T5.6) is uniformly **abolition** — the pride of the nations laid low at the day of the Lord (Isaiah 13:11; Zechariah 10:11) — except, again, **D's right pole**, oriented to glory (Romans 5:2).

### T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

This tier states the architecture of §1 from the verse evidence.

- **A ↔ B** — act and state, sharing g-'-h / r-w-m; A precedes and settles into B; both precede the fall ("Pride goes before destruction… a haughty spirit before a fall", Proverbs 16:18). The seam is Ezekiel 31:10.
- **C1 ↔ C2** — one disposition, two directions, sharing the z-d root; both produced by B (the settled pride that discharges outward).
- **D** — the voiced form; its sinful pole follows A/B (pride made audible, James 4:16), its righteous pole follows faith. D shares **ha.lal** with **praise** (a cross-cluster link to the praise/joy field) and **alazoneia** with the "pride of life" (1 John 2:16).
- **E ↔ A** — the high-minded compounds (hupsēlofroneō) join them at the mind; they differ in kind (E's hollow inflation vs A's solid elevation). E follows knowledge ungoverned by love (1 Corinthians 8:1) and produces division.
- **F** — the terminus on which the family converges; F's nearest neighbour is A (aspiration toward God's place vs arrival at it), and F co-heads the vice-list with D (alazōn) and B (huperēfanos) in 2 Timothy 3:2.

**Vocabulary/roots (T6.4) across the cluster:** g-'-h (A, B), r-w-m (A, B), z-d (C1, C2), hupsēlo-/fron- (A, E), kauchaomai (D), ha.lal (D ↔ praise), filautos (F). The root-sharing maps the family: height binds A–B, presumption binds C1–C2, and the mind binds A–E; D and F stand on their own terms (boasting; self-love), D reaching outward to praise.

### T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**Lexical (T7.1).** The cluster's vocabulary is built on five images: **height** (g-'-h, r-w-m, hupsēl-), **boiling-over/presumption** (z-d), **making-much-of** (kauchaomai, ha.lal), **inflation** (fusioō/tufoō — bellows and smoke), and **self-love** (filautos). It is unusually rich in **person-types** — the proud (ge.eh, huperēfanos), the haughty, the insolent (zed), the braggart (alazōn), the self-lover (filautos): pride is heavily lexicalised as a settled **character**, not only an act. The opposites are the humility/lowliness vocabulary, with **love** (E) and **trust** (D) at the edges; there is **no supplication/seeking term** in the whole family — pride, uniquely among inner-being characteristics, is never sought from another. Across the Testaments the height-image continues (rum → hupsoō); the principle "God opposes the proud, gives grace to the humble" (Proverbs 3:34) crystallises in the NT (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5); **D's** boasting theology is **developed** by Paul into boasting-in-weakness; **E** is a NT naming (bellows/smoke) with no OT counterpart; **F's** filautos is a NT lexicalisation of the terminus.

**Literary (T7.2).** The evidence spans every major genre, each requiring its own reading: the prophetic **taunt-oracle** against the nations' pride (Isaiah 14; 47), the **proverb** (Proverbs 16:18), the **lament from the sufferer's vantage** (Psalm 119, where C2 is seen from beneath), royal **narrative** (Daniel; Chronicles), and the **pastoral correction** of the epistles (D and E in the Corinthian correspondence). The **anchors**, taken together, form the cluster's master-frame: A = Isaiah 14:13–14; B = James 4:6 / Proverbs 16:18; C1 = Deuteronomy 17:12 / Jeremiah 50:29; C2 = Psalm 119:51; D = Jeremiah 9:23–24 / 1 Corinthians 1:31; E = 1 Corinthians 8:1; F = Isaiah 47:8, 10. The pair **Isaiah 14:14 and Isaiah 47:8** frames the disorder from aspiration to arrival; **1 Corinthians 1:31** states the redeemed alternative.

**Human science (T7.3) — attributed to the M08 science extract.** The science lenses map onto the family: the **status/dominance** literature (extract §3, including Keltner's power paradox) onto A and C2; **narcissism** research (extract §2) onto B, C1, and F; the **cognitive-superiority** component onto E; **impression-management/self-promotion** (extract §4) onto D's sinful pole; and Tracy & Robins' **two-facet** model (extract §1) onto D's authentic/hubristic split. The consistent **register-divergence** (extract T7.3.3): science problematises only *pathological* pride and treats achievement-based pride as healthy, whereas the biblical material judges pride **categorically before God** — even legitimate pride becomes disordered when it forgets the giver. Three things the science cannot reach are the cluster's theological distinctives: **God's ontological majesty** (the divine edge of the vocabulary), **D's righteous God-directed boast** (boasting in weakness so another's power may rest), and the **theological gravity** the Law assigns presumption (C1). One science-surfaced **gap** (extract T7.3.4) is carried forward, not asserted: the vulnerable-narcissism finding (grandiosity defending against shame) suggests testing whether the cluster's arrogance masks shame — an **M07 ↔ M08** question that the verse evidence does **not** itself raise.

## 3. Cross-cutting findings (the cluster's distinctive shape)

1. **One reality, seven modes, one seat.** The family is a single disposition — the creature making itself high or much-of — seated in the **heart** and worked out as act (A), state (B), defiance (C1), contempt (C2), boast (D), inflation (E), and terminus (F). Grounded throughout T1–T2.
2. **The theological axis is the organising principle.** Every characteristic is defined by its relation to the exaltedness that belongs to God (T0): pride seizes it, F occupies it, D returns it. The cluster cannot be read as merely social or psychological without losing its centre (T0; T7.3.3).
3. **A single legitimate pole, located entirely in D.** The cluster's one created-design, eschatologically-hopeful, constructively-relational, suffering-perfected form is the boast in the Lord (T0.2.2, T4.2, T4.3, T5.4, T5.6 — all D). This is the cluster's most distinctive structural feature.
4. **The relational interface is the diagnostic.** Pride's structural silence — not extended by God, not given or received between persons (T4.1/T4.3/T4.4) — holds across A, B, C1, E, F and is **inverted by D alone**. The shape of the silence *is* the finding.
5. **Faculty-signature: will, agency, relational damage, moral inversion** — with affect isolated in D, cognition in E, memory engaged by failure (forgetting God) in A and B, and **creativity uniformly silent** (T3).
6. **One arc, one off-ramp.** Prosperity → pride → downfall, with **self-humbling** the only escape and **suffering** the corrective — except D's right pole, where weakness is embraced and perfected (T5).
7. **The vocabulary is character-deep and seeking-less.** Pride is richly lexicalised as a person-type and has **no term for being sought from another** (T7.1) — a lexical confirmation of its self-referring nature.
