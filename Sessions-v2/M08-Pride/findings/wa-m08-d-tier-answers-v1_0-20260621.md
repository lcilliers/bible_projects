# WA — M08 · Characteristic D (Boasting / Glorying — Two Poles): Tier-Catalogue Findings

**Method:** as the A template. **Standing note:** D is a single act (glorying) differentiated by its **object** into two poles — a *sinful* pole (boasting in flesh/self/works) and a *righteous/commanded* pole (boasting in the Lord, the cross, weakness). Answers track both poles per question. D is the cluster's only consistently **affect-bearing** characteristic and its only one with a legitimate, commanded form.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — Uniquely in the cluster, the God-relation is **positive in one pole**: God is the proper *object* of right boasting — "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 1:31); "let him who boasts boast in this, that he understands and knows me" (Jeremiah 9:24). In the sinful pole God is displaced by self ("you boast in your arrogance", James 4:16).

**T0.1.2** — God does not boast, but glory **belongs to** God, and the creature's boasting is right only when returned to him (1 Corinthians 1:31). The pattern indicates that glorying is a created capacity rightly ordered toward God and disordered when turned on the self.

**T0.2.1** — In its right pole it leads the person to exult in God and his works (Psalm 34:2, "my soul makes its boast in the Lord"); in its sinful pole, to self-display and false confidence (James 4:16).

**T0.2.2** — **Both** — uniquely: D belongs to created design in its God-directed pole and to the fallen condition in its self-directed pole.

**T0.2.3** — **Yes** — D is oriented toward future fullness: "we boast/rejoice in hope of the glory of God" (Romans 5:2); boasting now anticipates the glory to come. (Unique eschatological orientation in the cluster.)

**T0.3.1** — In its right pole D instantiates the creature's proper **glorying in God** — the image rendering glory to its source.

**T0.3.2** — The right pole is a creaturely act directed to God (glory is God's; the creature returns it); the sinful pole is the creaturely distortion.

**T0.3.3** — Right boasting expresses the image rightly ordered; self-boasting expresses it disordered — the same capacity, two directions.

**T0.4.1** — **Christological:** "far be it from me to boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ" (Galatians 6:14) — boasting is redirected to the cross. Typological direction: the human glorying re-anchored in Christ's saving work.

*Coverage T0: 9 of 9 — the fullest T0 in the cluster (the only positive God-relation).*

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Boasting/glorying*: the act of exulting in and declaring the worth of an object. The name signals an **object-driven** act — its character is set by what is boasted in.

**T1.1.2** — kauchaomai ("to boast, glory, exult"), kauchēsis / kauchēma ("the boasting / the thing boasted in"), ha.lal ("to praise, boast, shine"), alazōn / alazoneia ("braggart / vain boasting"). Definitionally, to make much of something out loud.

**T1.1.3** — A **communicative, affect-laden** act whose moral value is wholly determined by its object and direction.

**T1.2.1** — An **act** with strong affective content (exultation) — not a settled state.

**T1.2.2** — Compound: an affective exultation + a declared object + a direction (toward God or toward self/others).

**T1.3.1** — Its structural opposite is **trust/dependence** that glories in another rather than the self — and, for the sinful pole, the boasting in self that the right pole excludes ("not because of works, so that no one may boast", Ephesians 2:9).

**T1.3.2** — The right pole excludes self-reliance ("we put no confidence in the flesh", Philippians 3:3); the sinful pole excludes dependence on God.

**T1.3.3** — D is not the settled height (B) nor the inner self-lifting (A); it is the **declared exultation** that may run either way.

**T1.4.1** — It operates as speech-act: "my soul makes its boast in the Lord" (Psalm 34:2); "I will all the more gladly boast of my weaknesses" (2 Corinthians 12:9).

**T1.4.2** — It varies entirely by **object**: boasting in the Lord, the cross, weakness, hope (right); boasting in flesh, works, wisdom, riches, tomorrow (sinful — Jeremiah 9:23; James 4:13–16).

**T1.4.3** — Inherently communicative — boasting *is* a speech mode (commanded in 1 Corinthians 1:31; condemned in James 4:16).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate inner correlate is **exultation/joy** — "my soul shall exult/boast in the Lord" pairs boasting with rejoicing (Psalm 34:2).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent in carrying affect; varied in valence (joyful confidence in God vs self-satisfied vaunting).

**T1.6.1** — Right boasting establishes a settled confidence in God ("we are his… who glory in Christ Jesus", Philippians 3:3); wrong boasting establishes false security (James 4:16, "all such boasting is evil").

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the exultant act; sustained = the confidence (or false confidence) it settles into.

**T1.7.1** — Right boasting operates rightly under **knowledge of God** ("that he knows me", Jeremiah 9:24) and the cross (Galatians 6:14).

**T1.7.2** — It is distorted when its object becomes the self/flesh/works (Jeremiah 9:23; Ephesians 2:9). On another spirit — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

**T1.7.3** — Where the right object is lost, boasting becomes the "vain boasting" of the braggart (alazoneia, James 4:16; 1 John 2:16).

*Coverage T1: 18 of 18 — D's two-pole, object-driven definition is the richest in the cluster.*

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Seated in the **heart/soul with affect**: "my soul makes its boast in the Lord" (Psalm 34:2); the inner exultation of glorying. The sinful pole is located in the heart's false confidence (James 4:16) and "the flesh" (Philippians 3:3–4).

**T2.1.2** — D is the cluster's **affective** characteristic — seated where feeling and exultation meet declaration. Its body link is the **mouth** (boasting is spoken).

**T2.10.1** — Movement inner→outer: the heart's exultation issues in declared boast. No external-source movement stated.

**T2.7.1** — Body link via the **speaking mouth** — the boast is the exultation voiced (expressive, soul→body).

**T2.9.1** — Right boasting originates in **knowledge of God received** (Jeremiah 9:24) — partly God-given; wrong boasting originates in the self's false confidence.

**T2.9.2** — Origin is **dual**: the right pole grounded in God-given knowledge; the sinful pole self-generated.

*Coverage T2: 6 of 6.*

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.1 Perception** — **T3.1.1** Minimal as inner-sense; the right pole rests on *knowing* God (cognition, not perception). **T3.1.2/3 — SILENT [data-silence]** for perception proper.

**T3.2 Cognition** — **T3.2.1** Engaged: right boasting is grounded in **knowledge** — "that he knows me" (Jeremiah 9:24); wrong boasting on a false estimate of wisdom/works (Jeremiah 9:23; 1 Corinthians 1:31 context). **T3.2.2** It orders (or disorders) the mind's self-estimate relative to God. **T3.2.3** D's cognition is object-discriminating — what one knows truly determines whether boasting is right.

**T3.3 Memory** — **SILENT [data-silence]** mostly; *inferential:* right boasting recalls God's deeds (Psalm 44:8, "in God we have boasted continually").

**T3.4 Affect** — **T3.4.1** Centrally and uniquely engaged — boasting carries exultation/joy: "my soul makes its boast in the Lord; let the humble hear and be glad" (Psalm 34:2); "I will boast all the more gladly" (2 Corinthians 12:9). **T3.4.2** It is the cluster's seat of positive affect (when rightly directed) and of self-satisfied affect (when not). **T3.4.3** D is the **affective pole of the whole cluster** — the one characteristic where feeling is integral, not incidental.

**T3.5 Creativity** — **SILENT [data-silence]**.

**T3.6 Volition** — **T3.6.1** Engaged: boasting is chosen and its object willed — "far be it from me to boast except in the cross" (Galatians 6:14, a willed re-anchoring). **T3.6.2** The will selects the object that makes the boast right or wrong. **T3.6.3** D's volition is the choosing of an object.

**T3.7 Agency** — **T3.7.1** The boast is an act of self-presentation; the sinful pole acts to display (James 4:13–16). **T3.7.2** It externalises the inner valuation. **T3.7.3** D consistently issues in declared action (speech).

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — **T3.8.1** Centrally engaged: D is the cluster's clearest site of **moral discrimination by object** — "all such boasting is evil" (James 4:16) versus boasting commanded "in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 1:31). **T3.8.2** The same act is righteous or sinful by its object. **T3.8.3** D shows that the inner-being value of an act can be wholly object-determined.

**T3.9 Conscience** — **SILENT [data-silence]**; *inferential:* right boasting in the cross presupposes a conscience cleansed (Galatians 6:14).

**T3.10 Conscientiousness** — **T3.10.1** Engaged in the right pole as integrated God-directed confidence (Philippians 3:3); in the wrong pole as presumption about the future ("you ought to say, 'If the Lord wills'", James 4:15). **T3.10.2/3** D's conscientiousness is the ordering of confidence under God's will.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **T3.11.1** Engaged: boasting is **relational and audience-directed** — Paul boasts *about* the churches ("I have great pride in you", 2 Corinthians 7:4) and *before* others (2 Corinthians 8:24); the sinful pole vaunts over others. **T3.11.2** It can build up (right, edifying pride in others) or wound (vaunting). **T3.11.3** D uniquely shows a **constructive relational** mode — pride *in another* that honours them.

*Coverage T3: ~27 answered; ~6 SILENT (T3.1 partial, T3.3 inferential, T3.5, T3.9 inferential — data-silence). D answers affect, cognition, moral evaluation, and relational capacity more fully than any other M08 characteristic.*

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1 Divine — God to Human** — **T4.1.1** Indirect but present: God **gives the ground** of right boasting (the knowledge of himself, the cross) — "that he knows me… for in these things I delight" (Jeremiah 9:24). **T4.1.2** Grace-based: right boasting rests on what God has done, "not because of works" (Ephesians 2:9). **T4.1.3** It shows God inviting the creature to glory in him rather than itself.

**T4.2 Divine — Human to God** — **T4.2.1** D's central register: boasting in the Lord *is* a God-ward act of worship and confidence — "in God we have boasted continually" (Psalm 44:8); "my soul makes its boast in the Lord" (Psalm 34:2). **T4.2.2** It requires the inner posture of dependence — glory referred away from the self to God. **T4.2.3** Right boasting shows a relationship of confident, dependent exultation in God.

**T4.3 Human — Giving** — **T4.3.1** Engaged constructively: one person boasts *about* another to honour them — "great is my pride/boasting in you" (2 Corinthians 7:4); "show… the proof of your love and of our boasting about you" (2 Corinthians 8:24). **T4.3.2** Genuine extension requires real ground for the commendation (Paul's boasting is vindicated by the Corinthians' conduct). **T4.3.3** It presupposes a relationship of love and confidence.

**T4.4 Human — Receiving** — **T4.4.1** The one boasted about **receives** honour and is summoned to live up to it (2 Corinthians 8:24, "the proof… before the churches"). **T4.4.2** Right uptake is the vindication of the boast; the sinful counterpart is flattery received. **T4.4.3** **SILENT [data-silence]** on refusal.

**T4.5 Human — Boundaries** — **T4.5.1** Right boasting operates within bonds (Paul and his churches); sinful boasting vaunts across distance (over rivals). **T4.5.2** It crosses covenantal lines in both poles. **T4.5.3** Its scope: in the right pole, the community edified; in the wrong, the self exalted over others.

**T4.6 Spiritual Beings** — **SILENT [data-silence]** (T4.6.1–3).

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**T5.1.1** — Right boasting **transforms orientation** — it re-anchors the self away from flesh toward God/cross (Galatians 6:14; Philippians 3:3). Both condition and orientation are addressed.

**T5.1.2** — The re-anchoring is presented as a settled stance ("far be it from me", Galatians 6:14) — durable, though requiring repeated choice.

**T5.2.1** — Sequence (right pole): self-confidence relinquished → boasting redirected to God/weakness → Christ's power rests (2 Corinthians 12:9).

**T5.3.1** — Mechanism: **encounter with weakness and grace** — "My grace is sufficient… my power is made perfect in weakness" (2 Corinthians 12:9). Right boasting is formed through felt insufficiency.

**T5.3.2** — Differs by pole: the right pole is formed through weakness; the wrong pole is fed by prosperity and presumption (James 4:13–16).

**T5.4.1** — **Strongly engaged:** right boasting operates *in* suffering — "we boast in our sufferings" (Romans 5:3); "I will boast of my weaknesses" (2 Corinthians 12:9). **T5.4.2** Suffering **perfects** right boasting by emptying the self of false ground — D's most distinctive developmental move: the inversion of pride's logic, where one glories in weakness so that Christ's power may rest.

**T5.5.1** — Right boasting participates in sanctification as the ongoing re-anchoring of confidence in God rather than self (Philippians 3:3).

**T5.6.1** — **Eschatological:** boasting "in hope of the glory of God" (Romans 5:2) anticipates the coming glory — present exultation reaching toward future fullness.

*Coverage T5: 9 of 9 — D's developmental and eschatological content is the richest in the cluster.*

**T6.1.1** — The sinful pole co-occurs with A/B (pride expressed — James 4:16, "boast in your arrogance"); the right pole co-occurs with **trust/faith** and **joy** (Psalm 34:2 pairs boasting with gladness — an M04 link).

**T6.1.2** — D sits at the **expressive edge** of the cluster — pride's outward voice — but uniquely branches into a righteous, faith-aligned form.

**T6.2.1** — Sinful boasting **follows** A/B (self-exaltation/settled pride → vaunting); right boasting follows faith.

**T6.2.2** — Causal/expressive (pride → sinful boast) and developmental (faith → right boast).

**T6.3.1** — Sinful D produces false security; right D produces confident dependence (Philippians 3:3).

**T6.3.2** — Sinful D is produced by A/B; right D by faith/knowledge of God.

**T6.3.3** — D is the **expressed/declared** aspect; A and B are the dispositions it can voice; trust is the disposition the right pole voices.

**T6.4.1** — Shares ha.lal with **praise/M04** (ha.lal = praise/boast/shine) and the kauchaomai-family is distinct to D; alazoneia overlaps the "pride of life" (1 John 2:16).

**T6.4.2** — The ha.lal root spans boasting and praise — a root-level link between M08 and the praise/joy field.

**T6.4.3** — The ha.lal sharing shows right boasting and praise to be near-neighbours — exultation in God voiced.

**T6.5.1** — Nearest neighbour (sinful pole): A/B (pride voiced). Nearest neighbour (right pole): **trust/faith** — distinguished because right boasting *refers glory away from the self*, the exact inverse of A.

**T6.5.2** — The boundary with A/B is **direction of glory** (to self vs to God); with trust, D is the *declared* form of the same God-ward confidence.

**T6.5.3** — The distinction is one of **object/direction**, not constitutional level — the same act (glorying) running to opposite termini.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — kauchaomai / kauchēsis / kauchēma ("boast, glory; the act; the thing/ground boasted in"), ha.lal ("praise, boast, shine"), alazōn / alazoneia ("braggart, vain-boasting"). Roots: to make-much-of (Greek), to shine/praise (Hebrew).

**T7.1.2** — Verb (kauchaomai — the act), and two nouns distinguishing the *act* (kauchēsis) from the *ground* (kauchēma) — a grammatical range that lexicalises object-dependence.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from worshipful exultation ("boast in the Lord") to empty vaunting (alazoneia) — the widest valence-range in the cluster.

**T7.1.4** — Yes — kauchēsis (the act) versus kauchēma (the ground/object) — exactly the disposition/object distinction D's two poles turn on.

**T7.1.5** — The opposite/absence is **boasting excluded** — "so that no one may boast" (Ephesians 2:9) — and the trust that puts "no confidence in the flesh" (Philippians 3:3).

**T7.1.6** — Yes — a person-type: alazōn, "the braggart" (Romans 1:30; 2 Timothy 3:2).

**T7.1.7** — None.

**T7.1.8** — The OT ha.lal "boast" continues into NT kauchaomai, and the OT principle "let him who boasts boast… that he knows the Lord" (Jeremiah 9:24) is taken up directly by Paul (1 Corinthians 1:31) — strong continuity with apostolic development.

**T7.1.9** — The kauchaomai-family is not newly coined but is **developed** by Paul into a theology of boasting-in-weakness (2 Corinthians 11–12) — a NT deepening rather than a coinage.

**T7.1.10** — The full arc runs from praise/shine (ha.lal) through worshipful boasting in God to the cross-and-weakness boasting of Paul, and, on the dark side, to the braggart's vain-boasting.

**T7.2.1** — In the anchor, kauchaomai carries the whole imperative: "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 1:31), quoting Jeremiah 9:24.

**T7.2.2** — Forms: prophecy (Jeremiah 9), psalm (Psalm 34; 44), and epistle (1–2 Corinthians, Galatians, Philippians, James, 1 John). The epistolary argument (Paul's boasting discourse) requires attention to its polemical, object-discriminating logic.

**T7.2.3** — The key argument: boasting is excluded *as to its sinful object* (works, flesh, wisdom, riches) and commanded *as to its right object* (the Lord, the cross, weakness) — the value follows the object (Jeremiah 9:23–24; 1 Corinthians 1:31; Galatians 6:14).

**T7.2.4** — Settings: covenantal/prophetic (Jeremiah), liturgical (Psalms), and pastoral-polemical (Corinthian correspondence).

**T7.2.5** — Anchor: **Jeremiah 9:23–24 / 1 Corinthians 1:31** — "let him who boasts boast… that he knows the Lord" — the verse stating the right object that reorders the whole act.

**T7.2.6** — Jeremiah 9:23–24 shows what no other verse states so plainly: boasting is not abolished but **re-objected** — the creature is to glory, but in God.

**T7.3.1** — *(science, M08 extract §4)* Impression-management / self-promotion (Goffman; Jones & Pittman) is the most useful lens for the **sinful** pole; the two-facet model (extract §1) maps the right/wrong split onto authentic vs hubristic registers.

**T7.3.2** — *(science)* Self-promotion research (extract §4) illuminates the social-display logic of the braggart (alazōn); Tracy & Robins' authentic-pride (extract §1) partly illuminates the legitimacy of boasting grounded in real ground (Paul's vindicated boasting, 2 Corinthians 8:24).

**T7.3.3** — *(science)* Science treats some self-promotion as adaptive and even authentic pride as healthy; the biblical material judges *all* self-directed boasting as excluded and commands boasting only in God (extract T7.3.3) — a sharper, object-based criterion than science's healthy/pathological axis.

**T7.3.4** — *(science)* Science has no category for the **righteous, God-directed boast** — boasting in weakness so that another's power may rest (2 Corinthians 12:9). This is the cluster's clearest point where the verse evidence exceeds the science (extract note on the theological distinctive).

*Coverage T7: 20 of 20.*
