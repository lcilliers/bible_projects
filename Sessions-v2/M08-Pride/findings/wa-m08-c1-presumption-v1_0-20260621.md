## 1. Definition (working)

Pride enacted as **overreach against God's command and his appointed authority** — presuming to act, speak, or go where one was not authorised; the will set defiantly against the ordained order. C1 and C2 share the z-d root (zid, za.don) but point in different directions; membership is assigned per occurrence.

## 2. Evidence base

**~15 occurrences** (per-occurrence assignment from the shared root): zid (H2102 — Deuteronomy 17:13; 18:20; 1:43; Nehemiah 9:16; 9:29; Jeremiah 50:29), za.don (H2087 — Deuteronomy 17:12; 18:22; 1 Samuel 17:28), tolmētēs (G5113 — 2 Peter 2:10), authadēs (G0829 — 2 Peter 2:10; Titus 1:7), zed (H2086 — Psalm 19:13; Jeremiah 43:2), and zud (H2103, Aramaic — Daniel 5:20, cross-listed from A).

## 3. Analysis (observation → interpretation)

**The core sense is presumptuous overreach against ordained authority.** The Deuteronomy texts are legal/cultic: "the man who acts presumptuously by not obeying the priest… or the judge… shall die" (Deuteronomy 17:12); "the prophet who presumes to speak a word in my name that I have not commanded" (Deuteronomy 18:20); "you rebelled against the command of the Lord and presumptuously went up" (Deuteronomy 1:43). Presumption is treated as a **capital matter** — it strikes at the ordained order itself.

**It is defiance of God, climaxing in open challenge.** "they acted presumptuously and did not obey your commandments… stiffened their neck" (Nehemiah 9:16, 29); "she has proudly defied the Lord, the Holy One" (Jeremiah 50:29); in the Aramaic, "his heart was lifted up… he dealt proudly" (Daniel 5:20).

**The NT register is self-will against authority.** "Bold and willful… they despise authority… blaspheme" (2 Peter 2:10, tolmētēs + authadēs); and the disqualifying trait for an overseer: "He must not be arrogant [self-willed]" (Titus 1:7). The one meaningful faculty tag here is **volition** (authadēs) — C1 is the will set on its own course against the ordained.

**Interpretation.** C1 is the *vertical defiance* facet of pride: where A lifts the self and B settles into height, C1 turns that height into active refusal of God's command and his appointed mediators (priest, judge, prophet). Its inner root is named as a hardened will — "stiffened their neck," "the evil of your heart" (1 Samuel 17:28) — and its outward form is disobedience that the texts judge as deserving death.

## 4. Synthesis

Presumption is pride become defiance: the proud self refusing the boundary God's word and God's appointed authorities set. It is consistently grave — a capital offence in the Law, "proud defiance" of the Lord in the prophets, self-willed contempt of authority in the apostolic writings. Its faculty is the will; its posture is the stiffened neck. C1 is the point at which M08 touches rebellion: pride is no longer merely a height but a refusal.

## 5. Expected but absent — special attention

1. **Inner state under-described; behaviour foregrounded.** C1 is presented mostly through *acts* ("acted presumptuously," "did not obey," "went up") rather than an inner condition. The inner root surfaces only twice — "the evil of your heart" (1 Samuel 17:28) and the volition tag on authadēs. **Interpretation:** the inner dimension of C1 is real but thin in the lexical; it is largely carried by the settled pride of B and expressed as the defiant act. This is a genuine characteristic-shape, not a method gap.
2. **Faculty noise — flag.** zid carries a mechanical `faculty=perception` tag on all nine occurrences; given zid's lemma ("to boil over → act presumptuously"), this perception tag looks like a disambiguation artefact, not a meaningful faculty (FA-14). The meaningful faculty here is volition (authadēs). → recommend CC review of the zid faculty tag.
3. **Cause mostly silent.** Where present, the cause is the self-exaltation that precedes the defiance ("Babylon proudly defying," Jeremiah 50:29; "Babylon's self-exaltation against God," Jeremiah 50:31). C1's cause is largely internal (the proud will) rather than an external elicitor — consistent with B's settled-state character.
4. **Seat rarely named** (flesh, 2 Peter 2:10; heart, 1 Samuel 17:28). Expected heart-seat is implied but seldom stated; do not over-read its absence.

## 6. Reflection (labelled — not a conclusion)

- C1 speaks to authority, obedience, and the limits of the self before God; its legal gravity in Deuteronomy will read very differently across traditions with different views of mediated authority. → flag for reader-facing.
- The "stiffened neck" image links C1 to the broader biblical rebellion vocabulary — a possible Session-D cross-reference beyond M08.
