## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God is B's **opponent**, never its bearer: "God opposes the proud" (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5); "the Lord tears down the house of the proud" (Proverbs 15:25). The one occurrence where the lemma names God is its legitimate counterpart — "the majesty of the Lord" (ge.ut, Isaiah 26:10).

**T0.1.2** — Settled pride is never borne by God; it is exclusively creaturely-sinful. But its lemma-family touches God's **majesty** (ge.ut). The pattern indicates pride is the creature's illegitimate seizure of the majesty that belongs to God alone (Isaiah 26:10).

**T0.2.1** — It leads the person to oppress the lowly (Psalm 10:2), to refuse God ("they do not return to the Lord", Hosea 7:10), and to be brought low (Isaiah 25:11).

**T0.2.2** — Fallen condition.

**T0.2.3** — None; B's only future is to be laid low (Isaiah 13:11).

**T0.3.1** — None direct. *Inferential:* a counterfeit of the divine majesty (ge.ut, Isaiah 26:10) the creature seizes. [inferential]

**T0.3.2** — Exclusively creaturely (distortion); the legitimate analogue (majesty) is God's.

**T0.3.3** — Its presence shows the image disordered — the creature bearing a majesty that is God's; its abasement ("those who walk in pride he is able to humble", Daniel 4:37) restores order.

**T0.4.1** — Eschatological: the pride of the nations is laid low at the day of judgment (Isaiah 13:11; Zechariah 10:11). No christological use [data-silence].

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Settled pride*: the entrenched state of haughtiness. The name signals a condition/status, not an act.

**T1.1.2** — ga.on, ga.a.vah, ge.eh (g-'-h, "be high/proud"), huperēfanos ("showing oneself above"), and the noun-family. Definitionally, the condition of being-high.

**T1.1.3** — A condition (not motion), frequently **corporate** ("the pride of Moab"), seated across heart/soul/spirit.

**T1.2.1** — A **condition/status**, and a person-type ("the proud") — not an act.

**T1.2.2** — Compound: settled self-elevation + entrenchment + (often) corporate scope.

**T1.3.1** — Humility/lowliness — "gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6); "he saves the lowly" (Job 22:29).

**T1.3.2** — It excludes humility, dependence on God, and the fear of the Lord ("The fear of the Lord is hatred of evil. Pride and arrogance… I hate", Proverbs 8:13).

**T1.3.3** — B is not the act of lifting (A) nor the verbal boast (D); it is the settled height the others arise from or express.

**T1.4.1** — It operates as a stative condition ("his heart is proud") and a possessed attribute ("the pride of Egypt", Ezekiel 32:12).

**T1.4.2** — It varies between individual ("arrogant in heart", Proverbs 16:5) and corporate ("the pride of Assyria", Zechariah 10:11).

**T1.4.3** — It speaks: "say in pride and in arrogance of heart" (Isaiah 9:9); "with their mouths they speak arrogantly" (Psalm 17:10).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate response is contempt and oppression of others — "In arrogance the wicked hotly pursue the poor" (Psalm 10:2) — and refusal of God (Hosea 7:10).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent: contempt of others plus distance from God.

**T1.6.1** — Over time it sustains oppression and even idolatry — "His beautiful ornament they used for pride… their abominable images" (Ezekiel 7:20) — and draws God's standing opposition.

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the contemptuous act; sustained = the entrenched condition God opposes and abases.

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold in prosperity and ease — Sodom's "excess of food, and prosperous ease" (Ezekiel 16:49); wealth (Ezekiel 28:5). It never operates rightly [structural — the "rightly" branch is inapplicable].

**T1.7.2** — Resisted by humility and the fear of the Lord (Proverbs 8:13). On another spirit: *inferential* — "he is king over all the sons of pride" (Job 41:34) sets Leviathan over the proud; otherwise [data-silence].

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, the person is "lowly in spirit" (Proverbs 16:19) and humbled (Daniel 4:37).

*Coverage T1: 18 of 18 (T1.7.2 part inferential/data).*

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**T2.1.1** — Seated across the **whole inner being**: heart ("arrogant in heart", Proverbs 16:5; "haughtiness of his heart", Jeremiah 48:29; thoughts of the heart, Luke 1:51), soul ("the pride of Jacob", Amos 6:8; "the yearning of your soul", Ezekiel 24:21), and spirit ("the proud in spirit", Ecclesiastes 7:8).

**T2.1.2** — The pattern shows a **pervasive** orientation — heart, soul, and spirit all engaged — not a localised faculty. Body links are minimal and expressive (the haughty look, Psalm 101:5).

**T2.10.1** — No movement from an external source; settled within the person, and within a people as a standing corporate condition. [no movement: structural]

**T2.7.1** — Body link minimal — the "haughty look" (Psalm 101:5; Isaiah 5:15) is expressive, soul→body; otherwise none.

**T2.9.1** — Within the person, and corporately within a people; occasioned by prosperity (Ezekiel 16:49).

**T2.9.2** — Single (within-person), with a corporate variant (within-a-people).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**T3.2 Cognition** — **T3.2.1** Located in the thoughts: "scattered the proud in the thoughts of their hearts" (Luke 1:51). **T3.2.2** It frames the mind's self-assessment too high. **T3.2.3** B inhabits the thinking of the heart, not only its feeling.

**T3.3 Memory** — **T3.3.1** Engaged by failure — "yet they do not return to the Lord… nor seek him" (Hosea 7:10). **T3.3.2** It suppresses returning-to-God. **T3.3.3** God-forgetfulness recurs as B's memory-effect, as in A.

**T3.4 Affect** — **T3.4.1** A disposition rather than a felt affect; faculty largely absent. **T3.4.2** Little stated affective effect. **T3.4.3** B is a settled orientation, not an emotion (the affective pole is D).

**T3.5 Creativity** — **SILENT [data-silence]** (T3.5.1–3).

**T3.6 Volition** — **T3.6.1** Willed conduct: "the one who acts in pride" (Psalm 31:23); "those who walk in pride" (Daniel 4:37). **T3.6.2** It directs the will toward self-assertion. **T3.6.3** B is a chosen way of walking, answerable by humbling.

**T3.7 Agency** — **T3.7.1** Strongly engaged: "hotly pursue the poor" (Psalm 10:2); "The arrogant have hidden a trap" (Psalm 140:5); "violence covers them" (Psalm 73:6). **T3.7.2** It drives oppression and violence. **T3.7.3** B characteristically acts against the lowly.

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — **T3.8.1** B is judged — "Everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination to the Lord" (Proverbs 16:5). **T3.8.2** It corrupts the person's evaluation (esteeming the self too high). **T3.8.3** B is consistently evaluated as abomination.

**T3.9 Conscience** — **SILENT [data-silence]** (T3.9.1–2); *inferential* dulling via refusal to return to God (T3.9.3).

**T3.10 Conscientiousness** — **T3.10.1** Undermines obedience — "they do not return to the Lord" (Hosea 7:10). **T3.10.2** It erodes obedient follow-through. **T3.10.3** B is corrosive of obedience.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **T3.11.1** B is relationally expressed as contempt — "the contempt of the proud" (Psalm 123:4); oppression of the poor (Psalm 10:2). **T3.11.2** It converts relation into domination and scorn. **T3.11.3** B is the disposition from which insolence toward others (C2) proceeds.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**T7.1.1** — ga.on, ga.a.vah, ge.eh (g-'-h, "be high/proud"), huperēfanos (huper + phainō, "show oneself above"). Root: height/loftiness as a settled condition.

**T7.1.3** — Semantic range: from the inner attitude ("haughtiness of heart") through external pomp ("the splendor and pomp of the Chaldeans", Isaiah 13:19) to corporate pride ("the pride of Moab").

**T7.1.4** — Yes — abstract-state nouns (ga.on) versus person-type terms (ge.eh).

**T7.1.5** — Yes — the opposite is the humility/lowliness vocabulary ("the humble", "the lowly").

**T7.1.6** — Yes, prominently: "the proud" (ge.eh, huperēfanos) as a settled person-type.

**T7.1.7** — None: there is no term for seeking pride from another.

**T7.1.8** — The OT g-'-h pride vocabulary maps to NT huperēfanos/huperēfania, with the "God opposes the proud, gives grace to the humble" principle (Proverbs 3:34) crystallised in the NT (James 4:6; 1 Peter 5:5) — continuity with sharpened formulation.

**T7.1.9** — None newly coined; huperēfanos is the settled Greek term [data on coinage: none].

**T7.1.10** — The arc shows pride as a settled condition — individual and corporate, OT through NT — consistently God-opposed.

**T7.2.1** — In the wisdom anchor, ga.on is the subject of the aphorism: "Pride goes before destruction" (Proverbs 16:18).

**T7.2.2** — Forms: prophecy (oracles against the nations' pride), wisdom (Proverbs), psalm, and NT epistle (James, 1 Peter). The oracle-against-nations form requires attention to corporate address.

**T7.2.3** — The key argument is antithetical: pride → abasement; "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6).

**T7.2.4** — Settings: judicial/eschatological (oracles), wisdom (proverb), liturgical (psalm).
