## T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — *Presumption/defiance*: overreach against God's command and his appointed authority. The name signals a willed refusal of ordained limit.

**T1.1.2** — zid ("to boil over → act presumptuously"), za.don ("presumption, insolence"), authadēs ("self-willed"), tolmētēs ("bold/daring"). Definitionally, to push beyond one's authorisation.

**T1.1.3** — A directional defiance — **against** command and authority; a disposition of the self-willed will.

**T1.2.1** — An **act** issuing from a disposition (the self-willed heart) — "acts presumptuously" (Deuteronomy 17:12).

**T1.2.2** — Compound: elevated self-regard (from B) + a will that refuses ordained limit (the defiant act).

**T1.3.1** — Its opposite is submission/obedience and the fear of God — "all the people shall hear and fear and not act presumptuously again" (Deuteronomy 17:13).

**T1.3.2** — It excludes obedience, the fear of the Lord, and the trembling due before God ("they do not tremble as they blaspheme", 2 Peter 2:10).

**T1.3.3** — C1 is not the mere height of pride (B) nor the insolence toward people (C2); it is the will's refusal of God's command and mediators.

**T1.4.1** — It operates as defiant action — "presumptuously went up" (Deuteronomy 1:43); "presumes to speak a word… I have not commanded" (Deuteronomy 18:20).

**T1.4.2** — It varies: against the cult (priest), against the court (judge), against the prophetic word, and directly against the Lord (Jeremiah 50:29).

**T1.4.3** — Yes — communicative defiance: the prophet who "presumes to speak" (Deuteronomy 18:20); "they blaspheme" (2 Peter 2:10).

**T1.5.1** — Its immediate response is disobedience — "stiffened their neck and did not obey" (Nehemiah 9:16).

**T1.5.2** — Consistent: refusal of the ordained word.

**T1.6.1** — It establishes a hardened, self-willed posture and incurs judgment (Deuteronomy 17:12).

**T1.6.3** — Immediate = the act of disobedience; sustained = the hardened defiance and its sentence.

**T1.7.1** — It takes hold where the heart is already proud/self-willed (1 Samuel 17:28, "the evil of your heart"); never operates rightly [structural].

**T1.7.2** — Resisted by the fear of God (Deuteronomy 17:13). On another spirit — [data-silence], though 2 Peter 2:10's "blaspheme the glorious ones" sets C1 against angelic beings (see T4.6).

**T1.7.3** — Where checked, the person fears and does not presume again (Deuteronomy 17:13).

*Coverage T1: 18 of 18.*
