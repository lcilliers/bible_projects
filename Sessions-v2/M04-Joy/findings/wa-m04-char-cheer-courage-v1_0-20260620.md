## 4. What the lexical surfaces — facets of the characteristic

*Observation grounded in §3; interpretation labelled where it goes beyond the field values.*

1. **It is morale, not elation.** Greek *euthumeo* carries faculty **perception** and the sense *take heart / good cheer* — a deliberate steadying of the inner being under threat, closer to courage than to joy.

2. **It is self-summoned and faces a threat.** Experiencer is mostly *self* (3); object_type includes a *threat* — 'take heart' is spoken into danger (e.g. shipwreck), with the cause being an assurance of no loss of life.

3. **Hebrew *balag* is the lighter 'smile/gleam' sense** — a brightening of countenance, the visible edge of cheer.

## 5. Boundary and data-quality notes

- **BOUNDARY — likely overlaps a fortitude/hope cluster.** The courage-under-threat structure of *euthumeo* may place it outside joy proper. Researcher decision required; very low volume.
