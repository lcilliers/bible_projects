## 1. What the data is (observation)

A per-term-in-verse lexical decomposition of every verse where an M04 term occurs. Across the seven batches:

- 1,103 verse records · 1,236 focus-cluster term-occurrences · **60 distinct focus terms** (Hebrew, Aramaic, Greek).
- The interpretive fields (cause, location, divine_involvement, object_type, valence) are verse-read-resolved; the structural fields (faculty, type, mode) are mechanically derived from the original-language measure layer.

Three corpus-level signals frame everything below:

| Signal | Reading |
|---|---|
| **Faculty** | affect 1160 · cognition 409 · volition 24 · perception 18 · moral_evaluation 13 · memory 1 |
| **Type** | action 534 · status 364 · quality 334 |
| **Experiencer** | other 449 · other-addressed 180 · self 155 |
| **Directed at (object_type)** | abstract 146 · person 100 · situation 96 · God 89 · thing 32 |
| **Valence** | righteous 488 · neutral 448 · commanded 83 · sinful 73 · forbidden 9 |
| **God's role** | agent 79 · object 46 · possessor 21 · addressee 20 · giver 17 |

The centre of gravity is the **affective** faculty, but with a heavy **cognitive/evaluative co-tag** — joy in this cluster is rarely "bare feeling"; it is bound up with judging something good, fitting, or worthy. Notably the corpus is mostly joy *described of others or commanded to others* (629 of 784 experiencer-tagged) rather than first-person reported, and joy is frequently *directed* (at God, persons, situations) rather than free-floating.

## 2. Candidate distinct inner-being characteristics (interpretation)

These seven groupings emerge from the lemma meanings, faculty tags and directedness. They are offered as candidates, not settled categories. Occurrence / distinct-verse tallies are exact; the *assignment of a term to a group* is interpretive.

### A. Rejoicing and gladness — the affective act of joy (occ 545 / verses 446)
The core. An inner state of joy and its outward expression. Carries both *status* terms (joy as a condition: simchah, chara, sasson, masos, gilah) and *action* terms (the doing of rejoicing: samach, gil, sus, agalliao, chairo). At its edge it becomes bodily expression — *renanah* (ringing shout), *salad* (to leap for joy) — joy that breaks into voice and movement. Includes *makarismos* (blessedness/joy) and *eufrosune* (cheer/gladness).

*Inner-being reading:* joy as an affective state of the inner being, with a strong tendency to externalise.

### B. Delight — pleasure taken in an object (occ 189 / verses 186)
Distinguished from A by **directedness**: not "to be glad" but "to delight *in*." chaphets / chephets / chemed (to delight in, take pleasure in, desire), eudokeo (to be well-pleased — and the only group carrying a clear **volition** tag), anog / oneg / taanug / ednah (luxuriant, exquisite delight), shaashuim (delight/enjoyment), hedeos/hedista (gladly, with pleasure). The state has an object and often a will-component — one delights in, chooses, is pleased with.

*Inner-being reading:* an affect that is relational and directional, frequently shading into desire and choice.

### C. Pleasantness — the agreeable quality of what pleases (occ 339 / verses 335)
This group describes the **quality of the object**, not the subject's state: tov (good/pleasant), naim / naem / noam (pleasant, sweet, lovely), arestos (pleasing). Grammatically these are *quality* terms. They name what it is about a thing that makes it a fit object of A or B.

*Inner-being reading — and a caution:* whether "pleasantness" is an inner-being *characteristic* at all is open. It may be better read as the object-pole that the genuine inner states (A, B) are directed toward. See §3.

### D. Wonder, marvel and amazement (occ 97 / verses 95)
pala / pele (to be marvellous, surpassing, extraordinary), temah (Aramaic, wonder), thaumazo / thauma / thambeo (to marvel, be amazed, astounded). Faculty here is **cognition + affect together** (and *moral_evaluation* for pele) — wonder is an affect produced by an act of evaluation: something exceeds expectation. This is arguably a *distinct* inner-being characteristic (awe/astonishment), not a species of joy.

### E. Thankfulness and gratitude (occ 16 / verses 16)
eucharistia, eucharistos. Faculty is **perception / memory**, not affect — gratitude as a recognition-response to a benefit received and remembered. Distinct in kind from A–D.

### F. Cheer, good courage and morale (occ 7 / verses 7)
balag (to gleam, smile), euthumeo (to keep up one's courage — faculty **perception**), eupsucheo (to be of good cheer). This shades toward *courage/morale* and may overlap a different cluster (fortitude/hope) rather than joy proper.

### G. Soothing / pleasing-aroma (occ 43 / verses 43)
nichoach — "a soothing/pleasing aroma." No faculty tag. In most uses this is the **satisfaction of God** at sacrifice, not a human inner state. Its standing as a human inner-being characteristic is doubtful (see §3).

## 3. Reflection — boundaries, gaps, and what needs deciding

**The cluster name under-describes the term set.** "Joy, Gladness and Delight" cleanly covers A, B and (object-side) C. Groups **D wonder, E gratitude, F cheer/courage, G soothing** are candidate *distinct* characteristics. They are either (i) genuine sub-characteristics of M04, (ii) co-terms swept in by verse co-occurrence, or (iii) members of other clusters. This is the first interpretive fork and needs a researcher decision before grouping hardens.

**tov (H2896A) is doing too much work (DQ-M04-01).** It is the single largest term (306 occ, a quarter of the cluster) and dominates group C — yet its per-occurrence senses are overwhelmingly evaluative *"good / better / well / best"* (well over 200 of 306), with object_type populated on only 15. Every one of the 306 carries the same affect+cognition faculty tag, which looks like a uniform mechanical assignment rather than a per-verse judgement. Under the term-level relevance filter (GR-PROG-007), many tov occurrences may not engage an inner-being characteristic at all. A relevance pass on tov is likely needed; until then group C's weight is overstated.

**Is "pleasantness" (C) an inner state or an object property?** The A/B states are borne by a person; C describes the thing that pleases. If the programme's unit is the *inner being's* characteristic, C may belong as the *object-pole* of delight rather than as a characteristic in its own right. Decision required.

**nichoach (G) — divine satisfaction, not human inner state (BOUNDARY-M04-03).** The 43 "soothing aroma" occurrences sit mostly with God as the one pleased. Relevance to the *human* inner being is doubtful and should be tested verse-by-verse.

**Data-quality: sha'a (H8173B, DQ-M04-02).** Glossed "to delight" but its lemma_meaning reads "to be smeared over, be blinded" — a probable homograph or seed error. Six occurrences; verify before use.

**A moral axis runs through the cluster.** 82 occurrences are *sinful* or *forbidden* joy/delight — rejoicing in evil, illicit pleasure, gloating. This is not a separate group but a **valence axis** cutting across A, B and C. It is a real and uncomfortable feature of the inner-being picture (joy is not morally self-justifying) and should be retained, not smoothed away.

**A divine dimension runs through it too.** God appears as agent (rejoices), possessor and giver of joy in 137 occurrences. Whether "joy God has / God gives" is treated as part of the human-inner-being study or held as an interface datum is a programme-level question.

### Where additional discovery would help
- A **term-level relevance pass** (GR-PROG-007) on the high-volume broad terms — tov above all — to separate inner-being uses from purely evaluative ones.
- **Co-term synergy fan-out** (the extract supports it) to see which of D/E/F/G occur *only alongside* a core A/B term versus standing on their own — direct evidence for whether they are swept-in co-terms or independent characteristics.
- Confirmation of the **owning-cluster** for cheer/courage (F) and gratitude (E), which may be claimed by other clusters.
