## 4. What the lexical surfaces — facets of the characteristic

*Observation grounded in §3; interpretation labelled where it goes beyond the field values.*

1. **This is an object-property, not an inner state.** Type is overwhelmingly *quality* (291). The terms describe the thing that is good/pleasant/lovely, naming what makes it a fit object of rejoicing (A) or delight (B).

2. **The senses are predominantly evaluative 'good', not affective 'delight'.** *good* (165), *better/Better* (54), *well* (9), *best* (6) dominate; only a minority — *pleasant* (13), *please/pleases* (15), *beautiful/lovely/attractive* (~12) — sit in the pleasure field. See the boundary note: most of *tov* is evaluation, not affect.

3. **A small pleasant-and-sweet Hebrew band.** Apart from *tov*, the *naim/naem/noam/naaman* group carries a focused sense of pleasantness, sweetness, loveliness, and *no'am* (the 'pleasantness/favour' of the Lord) — a more genuinely affective-aesthetic strand.

4. **When read, it attaches to God and to situations.** Object_type is read in only 24 cases (God 6, situation 6, person 5) — the quality is frequently predicated of God's name, God's goodness, or a state of affairs.

5. **It is occasionally seated.** Location is read in 32 (heart 16, soul 13) — 'pleasant to the heart/soul' — where pleasantness is felt as well as predicated.

6. **Its causes centre on God.** Read causes cluster on God's name, God's steadfast love and goodness, and God's own pronouncement that the created thing 'was good'.

## 5. Boundary and data-quality notes

- **DQ-M04-01 — tov (H2896A, 306 occ) relevance:** *tov* is a quarter of the whole cluster and the bulk of this characteristic, yet its per-occurrence senses are overwhelmingly evaluative ('good/better/well/best'); object_type is populated on only 15/306; and all 306 carry an identical affect+cognition faculty tag that looks like a uniform mechanical assignment. Under the term-level relevance filter (GR-PROG-007) many *tov* occurrences likely do not engage an inner-being characteristic. This characteristic's weight is overstated until a relevance pass separates inner-being uses from purely evaluative ones.
- **BOUNDARY — is 'pleasantness' an inner-being characteristic at all?** The states in A and B are borne by a person; C describes the thing that pleases. It may belong as the *object-pole* of delight rather than as a characteristic in its own right. Researcher decision required.
