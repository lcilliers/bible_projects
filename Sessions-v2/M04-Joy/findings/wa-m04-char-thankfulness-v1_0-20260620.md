## 4. What the lexical surfaces — facets of the characteristic

*Observation grounded in §3; interpretation labelled where it goes beyond the field values.*

1. **It is not affect but perception/memory.** Faculty is **perception (15) + memory (1)** — gratitude as the recognition and holding-in-mind of a good received, not a felt elation. This is the strongest reason to treat it as distinct.

2. **It is God-directed.** Object_type read as *God* (4); divine involvement *addressee* (4). Thanksgiving is offered *to* God.

3. **It is markedly commanded.** Valence is *righteous* (8) and *commanded* (6) — gratitude appears as something enjoined more than spontaneously reported.

4. **It attaches to concrete benefits.** Read causes: grace reaching more people, a ministry supplying the saints' needs, joy felt on others' behalf, receiving God's created things — gratitude is tethered to identifiable gifts.

## 5. Boundary and data-quality notes

- **BOUNDARY — distinct characteristic / owning cluster.** The perception/memory faculty and the 'response to benefit' structure mark gratitude as distinct from joy. It may belong to a thanksgiving/praise cluster rather than M04. Researcher decision required.
