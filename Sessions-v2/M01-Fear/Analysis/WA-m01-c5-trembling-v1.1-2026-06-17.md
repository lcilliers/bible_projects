## 1. The signature — trembling crosses the whole constitutional range

This is what distinguishes trembling from every prior characteristic. The lexical evidence places it at **every constitutional level and beyond**:
- **Spirit** — Isa 66:2, "him who is humble and contrite in spirit and **trembles at my word**." The cluster's only clear spirit-location, and its deepest, most contrite form.
- **Soul / heart** — Deu 28:65 (heart+soul, "a trembling heart"); Eze 32:10.
- **Body** — Job 4:14 ("trembling, which made all my **bones** shake"); Hab 3:16 ("my **body** trembles; my **lips** quiver… rottenness enters my **bones**").
- **Cosmos** — Exo 19:18 (Sinai trembled); the earth/mountains quake at God's presence.

So trembling is **fear in motion across the spirit-soul-body boundary** — beginning in the inner being (even the spirit) and moving outward to the body and the created order. It is the characteristic where fear becomes *visible and bodily*.

## 2. Causation — God makes things tremble

The strong **Hiphil** (25) and `divine_involvement` agent (16) converge: trembling is frequently **caused**, characteristically by God's action — the Lord rising up, **roaring like a lion** (Hos 11:10), descending on Sinai. Causes (9 resolved): "God's anger," "the Lord roaring like a lion," "the day of the Lord coming near," "the ark of God," "the angel's word to Daniel." Theophany and divine word make creatures and creation tremble.

## 3. Reverent / commanded trembling

The righteous+commanded minority (20) shows trembling as a **right response to God**: "The Lord reigns; **let the peoples tremble**!" (Psa 99:1, commanded); the contrite who "tremble at my word" (Isa 66:2); the people who "**come trembling** to the Lord" (Hos 11:10–11, righteous return). This overlaps reverent fear (C1) but in **bodily** form — reverence that shakes the body, not only orients the will.

## 4. Synergy (compound, 85 filled)

Co-terms: within-cluster fear/dread/trembling/shuddering terms; **heart** (7); **hand** (5, the body part); **throne** (3, trembling before the enthroned Lord); and a clear **contrast set** — **to rest** (5) + **peace** (3) + **security** (3): trembling's opposite is **rest/peace/security** (it shares dread's "security" and terror's "peace").

## 5. T0.4 candidates (inferential)

**Theophanic** — Sinai (Exo 19:18), the Lord roaring (Hos 11:10): the divine presence makes creatures and creation tremble. **Eschatological** — "the day of the Lord coming near" (Joel) as a cause of trembling. **Christological** — the NT *tromos/entromos* (8) includes "fear and trembling" before God's work (e.g. Php 2:12); verse-check pending. **Covenantal** — none prominent. Routed to T7.2.

## 6. Deep-dive synthesis

Trembling is the **somatic, outward-moving act** of fear that **crosses the constitutional range** (spirit → soul → body → cosmos), is characteristically **caused by God** (causative Hiphil; theophany), can be **commanded** as the right bodily response to the reigning Lord, and stands against **rest/peace/security**. It is where fear becomes visible.
