## Summary
Cowardice / timidity is the **culpable fear** — fear named as a moral fault. It is the cluster's outlier on two axes: it is the only characteristic that is predominantly a **trait** (quality 3 of 5) and the only one that is predominantly **sinful** (valence sinful 4, forbidden 1). All NT Greek (*deilia* / *deilos*), it is set against the **Spirit-given disposition of power, love, and self-discipline** ("God gave us a spirit not of *deilia* but of power and love and self-control," compound power/love/self-discipline), and listed among the disqualified ("the cowardly… their portion in the lake of fire," Rev 21:8, with idolaters and the faithless).

## Tiers
- **T0.** God never the experiencer (0); divine role **giver 1** — but as the disposition God **did not give** (its opposite, the spirit of power/love/self-discipline, is the gift). T0.4: NT 5; christological/redemptive contrast (Christ's "let not your hearts be troubled… my peace," Joh 14:27 context) — inferential → T7.2.

- **T5.** Near-silent (one redemptive adjacency: the gift that replaces it).

## Reflection
Cowardice completes the cluster as its **moral negative pole**: where reverent fear (C1) is the commanded, created-design fear, cowardice is the forbidden, sinful failure of fear's right ordering — fear that has become unbelief and faithlessness. It is the only characteristic the data treats chiefly as a **vice** (sinful) and a **trait** (quality), and the only one explicitly answered by a **named divine gift** (the spirit of power, love, self-discipline). For the inner-being question it marks the point where fear is not merely felt or inflicted but **morally evaluated and condemned** — and set against faith, love, and the indwelling Spirit. Very small (5), all NT, so the trait/sinful pattern is clear but the sample is narrow.
