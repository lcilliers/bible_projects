## 1. The conflation to clear first

Three different things were collapsed in v2.0. They are distinct:

1. **The VE `purpose` / `typology` *fields*** — excluded from generation by design (no term-level lexical basis). Correctly excluded.
2. **Tier T0.2 (Created Purpose)** — a *question*, and it **is** assessable from the lexicon (valence as created-design-vs-fallen-response; immediate_response as what the characteristic equips). This was assessed in v2.0.
3. **Tier T0.4 (Typological Significance)** — also a *question*, and **also assessable** — not from a typology field, but as a **candidate-surfacing** exercise from the fields that carry "pointing beyond the immediate." This is what v2.0 wrongly dismissed.

The principle: **the lexicon surfaces typological candidates and grades the signal; it does not confirm typology.** Confirmation that a characteristic is *deployed* to point toward a greater reality is a canonical-literary judgment (T7.2). So a T0.4 lexical pass produces *inferential candidates* labelled as such (per GR-PROG-009), and routes confirmation to T7.2.

## 2. The three registers and their lexical markers

T0.4.1 names three registers. Each has a different lexical home:

| Register | Where the lexicon carries the signal |
|---|---|
| **Covenantal** | the **compound / co-term** layer (covenant, name, oath) — *not* cause/object |
| **Eschatological** | `cause` / `object` referencing the day of the Lord, judgment, the end; **reference distribution** (concentration in prophetic/apocalyptic books) |
| **Christological** | **NT testament** + `object` / `cause` / `verse_text` referencing Jesus and his **divine acts**; the *redirection* of an OT God-directed characteristic onto Christ |

## 3. Direction (T0.4.2)

The direction of a typology — human instance pointing to the divine, or divine instance establishing the pattern — is read from the **testament comparison**: does an OT human→God pattern get **taken up and redirected** in the NT toward Christ? If the NT applies to Jesus the field-profile (object, divine_involvement, cause) that the OT reserved for God, that is lexical evidence that **the divine instance (now in Christ) establishes/fulfils the pattern** — a divine→human direction of fulfilment.

## 4. Procedure

1. Scan `cause` and `object` for eschatological and christological markers; scan **compound** for covenantal co-terms.
2. Tabulate by **testament**; isolate NT occurrences whose `verse_text` ties the characteristic to Christ's person/acts.
3. Assess **direction** by comparing the OT and NT field-profiles for the same characteristic.
4. Label every result **inferential candidate**; state the strength of signal.
5. Where a register's markers are **absent or thin**, record that as the lexical basis for T0.4.3 ("note the silence") — a graded silence, not a blanket "excluded."
6. Route all candidates to **T7.2** for verse-literary confirmation.

## 5. Honest limits

- **Keyword scans over-match.** A first "eschatological" scan on the word *coming* caught "trouble coming upon him" and "destroyer coming" — historical judgment, not eschatology. Markers must be register-specific and verse-confirmed; the scan yields candidates, never conclusions.
- The lexicon cannot establish typology; it can only show **where the characteristic is pointed beyond the immediate** and hand that to T7.2.

## 6. Worked example — M01 (Fear), applying the method

- **Christological — a real, strong candidate.** 23 NT fear occurrences arise in the context of **Jesus' divine acts**: terror when he calms the sea (Mat 8:26 / Mar 4:41 / Luk 8:25), walks on water (Mat 14:26), at the transfiguration (Mat 17:7), at the cross/earthquake (Mat 27:54), and "fear seized all… God has visited his people" when he raises the dead (Luk 7:16). The fear/awe the OT reserves for Yahweh is, in the NT, aroused by Christ. **Direction (T0.4.2):** the divine instance — Christ — draws the creature's fear; the OT God-fear pattern is fulfilled in fear-before-Christ.
- **Eschatological — thin.** Fear of God's *judgments* (Psa 119:120, "my flesh trembles… I am afraid of your judgments"; Job 22:4), and the prophetic day-of-judgment register. Present but limited and easily over-counted.
- **Covenantal — present in the compound layer.** "covenant" appears as a co-term ~20× across M01 (10 in reverent fear) — fear sits inside the covenantal frame, though as co-occurrence rather than a typological deployment per se.

For reverent fear (C1) the christological candidate is the strongest T0.4 signal; for ordinary fear (C2) the same NT Jesus-act occurrences apply (the disciples' fright), with the redirection-to-Christ pattern shared.

## 7. Net for the analyses

T0.4 in C1 and C2 v2.0 should be replaced: not "excluded by design," but **a graded set of inferential candidates** — christological (strong), eschatological (thin), covenantal (present via compound) — each flagged for T7.2 confirmation. (Retrofit pending researcher confirmation of this method.)
