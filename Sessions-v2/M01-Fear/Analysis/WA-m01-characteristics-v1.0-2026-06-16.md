## Method note (so the basis is visible)

- **Derived from, not imposed.** The characteristics below were grouped from the actual `lemma_meaning` and per-occurrence `sense` values of the focus terms — not from a prior theology of fear. The grouping step is, unavoidably, **interpretive**: the senses themselves are observed; the lines drawn between them are my proposal.
- **Anchored on the lemma, senses attached.** A characteristic is named from what the lemma meanings have in common; the ESV `sense` words that occur under it are listed as its members, with counts (n).
- **Valence is shown only where it sharply differentiates** (e.g. reverent vs forbidden fear), since you found that field valuable. It is read-resolved, so treat it as strong-but-checkable.
- **Terms can serve more than one characteristic.** Several lemmas span families (e.g. one term glossed "to fear, tremble, revere, dread"). Where that happens the term appears under more than one heading; this is noted, not forced into one box.

Counts are occurrence counts on focus terms. Three groups at the end are **not** inner-being characteristics (outliers, external results, and probable sense-noise) and are kept separate.

## A. The characteristics (proposed)

### 1. Reverent fear / awe (chiefly toward God)
The God-directed, honouring form of fear. Strongest differentiator in the data: of 174 occurrences with God as the object, valence is *righteous* (76) or *commanded* (47), with *forbidden* only 6.
- **Senses:** fear, feared, awe, awesome, awesome deeds, reverence, revere, reveres, in awe, stood in awe, god-fearing, worship, respect, respects, fearful awe.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H3372H "to fear: revere" (fear, reverence, awesome), H3373 "fearing, reverent, afraid", H4172A/B "fear, reverence, terror", H6345 "dread, fear, awe, religious awe", G6015 "fear, reverence, awe", and reverent uses of G5401 "fear, terror".
- **Valence signature:** righteous / commanded dominant.

### 2. Fear / being afraid (apprehension of danger)
The general affect of being afraid — the cluster's core and largest characteristic. Frequently *forbidden* ("do not be afraid") or *neutral*.
- **Senses:** fear, afraid, feared, fears, frightened, fearful, fearfully, be afraid, make them afraid, scare, frighten.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H3372G "to fear: revere" — danger sense (frightening/DANGER), G5399 "to fear, be afraid, alarmed", H3374 "fear, terror, fearing", H3025 "(Qal) to fear, dread, be afraid", H7297 "(Qal) to fear", H3016 "(Qal) fearful, fearing", H1763 (Aramaic) "to fear".
- **Valence signature:** forbidden / neutral dominant (note the contrast with #1).

### 3. Dread (anticipatory fear of a coming threat)
Forward-looking apprehension; the weight of something feared ahead.
- **Senses:** dread, dreads, dreadful, dreaded.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H6343 "terror, dread", H6342 "to fear, tremble, revere, dread", H2844A "fear, terror", H4034 "fear, terror", H0366 "terrible, dreadful".

### 4. Terror (overwhelming / often externally-sourced fear)
The strong, sometimes externally inspired or inflicted, form.
- **Senses:** terror, terrors, terrifying, terrify, terrified, sheer terror, terror-stricken, inspire terror, strike terror, terrible, fearsome.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H0367 "terror, dread", H4032 "fear, terror", H2851 "terror", H1091 "terror, destruction, calamity, dreadful event", H1205 "terror, dismay", H2847 / H2849 / H2866 "terror", H2283 "terror, a reeling (in terror)", H4637 "awful shock, crash, terror", G1630 "frightened, terrified", G5398 "fearful, dreadful, terrible", G5400 "fearful event/thing", H8606 "shuddering, horror".

### 5. Trembling / quaking (the body shaken by fear)
Fear realised as physical agitation.
- **Senses:** tremble, trembling, trembled, trembles, quake, quaked, quakes, quivering, come trembling, shake, shakes, shaken.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H7264 "tremble, quake, rage, quiver, be agitated", H2729 "to tremble, quake, be startled, be terrified", H7460 / H7461A / H7461B "to tremble, quake / trembling", H2730 "trembling, fearful, afraid", H7268 / H7269 "trembling, quivering, quaking", H7578 "trembling", H6426 "(Hithpael) to shudder, tremble", G5156 "trembling, fear", G1790 "trembling, terrified".

### 6. Shuddering / horror (recoil and bristling)
The shrinking, hair-raising recoil response.
- **Senses:** horror, shuddering, shudder, shocked, bristle, bristles, look aghast.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H6427 "shuddering, trembling", H2189 "a horror, trembling, object of terror", H8178A "terror, horror", H8175A "to shudder, shiver, dread, bristle (with horror)", H2113 "a horror, an object of terror, a trembling".

### 7. Dismay / being shattered (loss of inner strength)
Fear that breaks composure — the inner being collapses or fails.
- **Senses:** dismayed, shattered, broken, broken down, faintness, confusion.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H0926 "to disturb, alarm, terrify, be anxious, be afraid", H2865 "to be shattered, be dismayed, be broken, be abolished", H0927 (Aramaic) "(Pual) to frighten, alarm, dismay", H0928 "dismay, sudden terror or ruin, alarm", H1670 "faintness, dismay, failure of mental energy", H1205 "terror, dismay".

### 8. Alarm / sudden fright (startle and panic)
The abrupt, reactive onset of fear.
- **Senses:** alarmed, alarm, startled, panic, sudden, haste, hurried, rushed.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H0927 (Aramaic) — alarm/haste sense, H8429 (Aramaic) "(P'al) to be startled, be alarmed", G4422 / G4426 "(passive) to be startled, frightened", G1719 "afraid, terrified", H7374 "trembling, panic".

### 9. Anxiety / disquiet (anxious care, inner unrest)
The settled, gnawing form — worry and distress rather than acute fright.
- **Senses:** anxiety, anxious, cares, thoughts, troubled, trouble, distress, distressed, greatly distressed, anguish, unrest.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H1674 "anxiety, anxious care, care", H8312 "disquieting thoughts, thoughts", H2731 "fear, anxiety, quaking, trembling, (extreme) anxiety, anxious care", H3735 (Aramaic) "(Ithp'el) to be distressed, be grieved", G0085 "to be troubled, distressed", H7661 "cramp, agony, anguish".

### 10. Astonishment / being awe-struck (stunned, dumbfounded)
The overwhelmed-and-stilled response; borders on awe (#1) but is the *stunned* reaction rather than the honouring one.
- **Senses:** astounded, astonished, utterly astounded, amazement, amazed, greatly amazed, wonder, thrill, deeply moved.
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** H8539 "to be astounded, be stunned, be amazed, be dumbfounded", G1568 "(passive) to be overwhelmed with wonder, distressed, alarmed", G2285 "amazement, astonishment, wonder", G1569 "(utterly) astonished", H8429 (Aramaic) — astonished sense.

### 11. Cowardice / timidity (defective or culpable fear)
Fear named as a fault — the failure of courage. Small but distinct; valence leans *sinful / forbidden*.
- **Senses:** cowardly, timid, fear (as timidity).
- **Representative glosses/lemmas:** G1169 "afraid, cowardly, timid", G1168 "to be afraid, cowardly, timid", G1167 "timidity, cowardice".

## B. Not inner-being characteristics (kept separate)

### B1. Constraint / compulsion — membership flag
`anankē` (G0318, "necessity"), 9 occurrences, all valence=neutral, senses *necessity / compulsion / under compulsion / hardships / affliction / distress*. These read as constraint or hardship, not fear. Carried over from the v1.1 membership flag; a candidate for exclusion from M01.

### B2. External result / object of terror (not the inner state itself)
Terms whose captured sense names what terror *produces* or its external object — desolation, ruin, destruction, calamity — rather than the inner state. ~25 occurrences.
- H8047G "destroyed/waste" (desolation, waste — 15), H4288 "destruction, ruin, terror" (ruin, ruins, destruction — most uses), H1091 / H2866 "calamity" uses.
- *Open question:* is the external desolation in scope as a manifestation of terror, or out of scope as a result?

### B3. Probable homonym / sense-noise
Focus terms whose captured `sense` is plainly not an inner state — likely a wide lemma or homonym pulled in on the wrong sense (~12 occurrences):
- H4867 "wave" → senses *waves, breakers*; H6178 "dreadful" → *gullies*; H4035 "fear, terror" → *barn*; H2119B "to shrink back, crawl away" → *crawl, crawling*; H6125 "oppression, pressure" → *oppression*.
- These confirm the v1.0 sense-purity flag: even on focus terms, a small slice of `sense` values are not inner-being content.

## C. Reflection (held lightly)

- **The sharpest real seam is moral, not lexical.** The same affect ("fear," "afraid") splits by *valence* and *object* into a commanded/righteous fear of God (#1) and a forbidden/neutral fear of danger (#2). That split is carried by the read-resolved fields, not by the vocabulary — the lemma "frightening(DANGER)" sits under both. This is the most consequential thing the cluster reveals so far, and it bears directly on the inner-being question.
- **The boundaries between #3–#8 are soft.** Dread, terror, trembling, shuddering, dismay, and alarm overlap heavily in the lemmas (one term routinely spans several). Whether these are *distinct characteristics* or *facets of one terror-state* is a granularity decision, not something the data settles on its own.
- **Awe (#1) and astonishment (#10) touch but differ** — one honours, one is stunned. A few terms (e.g. G1568) sit on the line.

## D. Where the data is thin / what would help

- The grouping rests on `lemma_meaning` + `sense`; it has **not** been verse-checked. A pass that reads the actual verse for borderline terms (#3/#4, #7/#8, B2) would firm the lines.
- Greek and Aramaic terms are present but few; the characteristics are OT-Hebrew-weighted.
- `faculty` does not help differentiate (affect throughout); it cannot be used to separate these characteristics.

## E. Open interpretive choices (please steer)

1. **Granularity** — keep ~11 characteristics, or collapse the overlapping terror-family (#3–#8) into fewer, broader ones?
2. **Anchor** — should a characteristic be defined by the **lemma** (as here) or by the per-occurrence **sense** (which would let one term feed several characteristics by use)?
3. **Reverent vs ordinary fear** — keep #1 and #2 as two characteristics (the data supports it via valence/object), or treat reverence as a *mode* of one fear characteristic?
4. **Outliers** — exclude B1 (anankē) and B3 (noise) now; decide B2 (external desolation) in or out?
