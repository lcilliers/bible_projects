## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (new)

**T0.1 Divine Nature Reflected.** Across all M01, God is **never the experiencer of fear** (0 occurrences); `divine_involvement` records God only as **object** (98 here), **agent** (4), or **giver** (2). So Scripture does **not** attribute fear to God as a divine affect (T0.1.2 — not attributed). The silence is significant (T0.1.3): reverent fear images **not what God is but how the creature rightly stands before God.** It is a creaturely orientation toward the Creator, not a property of the divine nature — which distinguishes it from characteristics Scripture does attribute to God.

**T0.2 Created Purpose.** Framed overwhelmingly **commanded/righteous** (150 of 195) and producing obedience and worship (T1.5/T1.6), reverent fear equips the person for **right relation and glad obedience to God**. T0.2.2 — the commanded/righteous weighting suggests it belongs to **created design** (a proper orientation to be cultivated), not merely a response to the fall — a contrast with ordinary fear (C2). T0.2.3 (future fullness) — the lexicon is silent; noted.

**T0.3 Image-Bearer Expression.** Reverent fear **instantiates the creature's acknowledgement of the Creator** — the right posture of the image-bearer before God. T0.3.2 — it is an **exclusively creaturely analogue** (God does not fear); it is not shared between God and the person. T0.3.3 — its absence or misdirection (the sinful/forbidden cases: not fearing God, fearing other gods) marks a **disordered** image.

**T0.4 Typological Significance.** Assessed as inferential candidate-surfacing (per the T0.4 method), not as a typology field. Within C1's own (OT-Hebrew) data: **covenantal** — present, via the compound layer (covenant co-term 11) — reverent fear sits inside the covenant frame; **eschatological** — thin, one clean marker (Joel 2:11, cause "the great and awesome day of the Lord"); **christological** — **not evidenced in C1's own data** (NT occurrences 1, Jesus-act 0), because the lemma anchor is OT-only. The christological pattern (the OT fear-of-God redirected onto Christ) is carried by C2's NT terms — and would join this characteristic under a valence-based scope (see Open items). All candidates labelled inferential; confirmation routed to T7.2.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1 Name.** H3372H "to fear: revere" (107), H3373 "fearing, reverent" (64), H4172A *mo.rah* (11) + H4172B *mo.ra* (11) = 22, H6345 "religious awe" (1), G6015 "reverence, awe" (1) = **195**. The root fuses *fear / reverence / awe* — the fear vocabulary in a reverent register.

**T1.2 Kind.** A **God-directed, morally-required disposition**, felt as affect, held as a standing orientation (type quality 48 alongside action 123) — closest reference dimension Relational Disposition with Moral-Character weight; not reducible to a label.

**T1.3 Boundary.** The lexical opposite is not "no emotion" but **fear misdirected** (forbidden 4 — other gods) or **withheld where owed** (sinful 10 — not fearing the Lord).

**T1.4 Modes of Operation.** The grammar carries three modes: **Qal active** (92, "you fear"), **Niphal** (≈27, *nora* "to-be-feared/awesome" — God as the quality feared), and **Qal imperative** (8, the commanded mode). (Lexical `mode` field empty.)

**T1.5 Immediate Response.** `immediate_response` (19) — the first enacted responses: **withholding the son** (Gen 22:12), **keeping the Sabbaths** (Lev 19:3), **keeping the commandments** (Deu 6:2), **building for the name** (2Ch 6:33), **cleaving** (Pro 24:21), **joy** (Psa 65:8). Consistent in kind: obedience and worship.

**T1.6 Sustained Effect.** `type`=quality (48) and durative `intensity` ("all your days," "forever") frame reverent fear as a **lifelong standing orientation** — what it produces over time is a settled God-fearing character.

**T1.7 Conditions of Reception.** The valence-blocked cases mark the conditions: reverent fear is **absent** where it is withheld from God (sinful) and **distorted** where redirected to other gods (forbidden) — the lexical signal of non-reception.
