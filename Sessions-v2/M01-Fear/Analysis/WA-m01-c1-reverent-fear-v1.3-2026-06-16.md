## T1 — Definition (from the lexicon)

**Name.** Carried by H3372H "to fear: revere" (107), H3373 "fearing, reverent, afraid" (64), H4172 "fear, reverence, terror" — two registered forms H4172A *mo.rah* (11) and H4172B *mo.ra* (11) = **22** — H6345 "dread, fear, awe, religious awe" (1), and one Greek term G6015 "fear, reverence, awe" (1). **Sum: 107 + 64 + 22 + 1 + 1 = 195.** The root range fuses *fear*, *reverence*, and *awe* within a single lemma — at the definitional level this is the fear vocabulary in a reverent register, not a separate word for "reverence."

**Grammatical axis (mode/morph — newly read).** `stem`: Qal 92 · Niphal 31 · non-verb 72. The split is semantically loaded:
- **Qal active** ("you fear," "they feared") — the person as fearing subject.
- **Niphal** (≈27 participle forms, *nora*) — the "to-be-feared / awesome" form, where God is the **object/quality** of fear ("the great and awesome God"). This is grammatically distinct from active fearing.
- **Qal imperative 2mp** (HVqi2mp, 8) — the grammatical home of the **commanded** valence ("you shall fear").

So the lexicon carries three grammatical registers of one characteristic: *active fearing*, *God-is-fearsome*, and *commanded fearing*. (The lexical `mode` field itself is empty — 0/195; the grammar is in `morph`/`stem`.)

**Kind (working description).** An **affect-seated** state (`faculty`=affect, all 195) that is **God-directed** (`object_type`=God on 95 of 122 named; `divine_involvement`=object on 98) and **morally framed as right or required** (`valence` righteous 104 / commanded 46 = 77%). It is **as much a settled trait as an act** (`type` quality 48 alongside action 123). It is also **enjoined rather than introspected**: `experiencer` is other 52 / other-addressed 40 / self 7 — reverent fear is overwhelmingly *commanded to a second party* ("you shall fear") and only rarely a reported first-person experience. Against the reference dimension vocabulary it reads closest to **Relational Disposition** with **Moral-Character** weight and an affective core — but, per the framework, Kind is not reducible to a label. The data's description: *a God-directed, morally-required disposition, felt as affect, held as a standing orientation, and commanded rather than introspected.*

**Boundary (lexical contrast).** The data marks the structural opposite from within the same vocabulary: 4 `valence`=forbidden and 10 `valence`=sinful — reverent fear *misdirected* (other gods, Judg 6:10) or *withheld where owed* (Exo 9:30). The lexical boundary is not "no emotion" but **fear pointed at the wrong object, or absent toward the right one.**

**Totality frame (intensity).** `intensity` is dominated by the *kol* "all" token (~24 of 35 filled), alongside durative ("forever," "always," "as long as") and "very/greatly." Reverent fear is framed in terms of **wholeness and universality** ("all your days," "all peoples"), not intensity of feeling — a totalising, not a heightening, qualifier.

## T3 — Inner Faculties (moderate; corrected)

The **term's own `faculty` is affect on all 195** — uniform, so the field alone says only that reverent fear is felt affectively. **Correction to v1.2:** v1.2 concluded the characteristic engages "neither cognition nor volition." The **compound** field shows otherwise at the verse level: reverent fear co-operates with **knowing** (*ya.da* "to know" 6) and **remembering** (*za.khar* "to remember" 4) as partner terms. So the characteristic is felt through affect but **operates together with cognition/memory** — the faculty *field* is term-intrinsic and uniform; the faculty *engagement* is visible in the compound co-terms. (Volition is not directly evidenced as a co-term, though the obedience nexus in §C1 implies a volitional outworking.)

## T5 — Relational Interfaces (rich)

**Divine interface — two directions.**
- *Human → God (dominant):* the human fears God. `object`="Lord" 37 / "God" 17 / "name" 5; `object_type`=God 95; `divine_involvement`=**object 98**. Causes (resolved, 24) are God's attributes and acts — greatness, might, awesomeness, "great things God has done," "the great and awesome day," and **God's forgiveness** (Psa 130:4).
- *God → human (newly read):* `divine_involvement`=**giver 2** (Deu 11:25) — God **lays** the fear of Israel on the nations. So reverent-fear vocabulary also runs God-ward-outward: God *causes* fear, not only *receives* it. `relational` carries the directional sense (to 5, of 3, **above 2** — "above all gods"; with, for, on).

**Human interface.** `object_type`=person 21. The reverence verb is **commanded toward parents** (Lev 19:3, "shall revere his mother and his father," commanded); also covers **fear of a threatening person** (Gen 32:11, Esau, neutral) and **dread of humans felt by creatures** (Gen 9:2).

**Spiritual-beings interface.** Evidenced negatively and uniformly: `object_type`=spiritual-being, all condemned — Judg 6:10 (forbidden), 2Ki 17:7 (sinful). A genuine contribution to an interface the framework flags as a catalogue gap.

**Stance.** `experiencer` (other 52 / addressed 40 / self 7) shows the divine and human interfaces are operated chiefly through **address and command**, not first-person report — reverent fear is something Scripture *enjoins on the hearer* far more than it *describes being felt*.

## T6 — Formative / Developmental (lightly evidenced; upgraded from "near-silent")

**Behavioural fruit (immediate_response — newly read, 19 of 195).** The lexicon records what reverent fear *issues in*: **withholding the son** (Gen 22:12), **keeping the Sabbaths** (Lev 19:3), **keeping all the commandments** (Deu 6:2), **building for the name** (2Ch 6:33), **joining/cleaving** (Pro 24:21), and even **joy** (Psa 65:8). (In the non-reverent anchor uses, the same field records avoidance — "back to the house" Deu 20:8, "hurry away" Judg 7:3 — which belong to ordinary fear, not this characteristic.) So the developmental fruit of reverent fear, on the lexical evidence, is **obedience, covenant-keeping, worship, and joy.**

**Standing-orientation and duration.** `type`=quality (48) frames it as a settled trait; the durative `intensity` tokens ("all your days," "forever," "always") frame it as a **lifelong** orientation. Beyond fruit and duration, sequence/process/transformation are not in the lexicon — that arc still needs verse-literary (T8.2) evidence.

## §C1 — Compound field (synergy evidence; 170 of 195 = 87%)

The compound field — the single most-filled relational field — lists each co-term with a role (partner / qualifier / co-seated). Reporting its contents is **field-completeness, not the advancing of T7 structural findings**; most co-terms here are **operational context**, and they feed T1/T3/T5/T6 directly. The nexuses:

- **Obedience / service** — *sha.mar* "to keep: obey" (10) + *sha.ma* "to hear: obey" (5) + *a.vad* "to serve" (5): reverent fear pairs, as a partner term, with obeying and serving (→ T6 outworking).
- **Covenant / oath / faithfulness** — *be.rit* "covenant" (10) + *sha.va* "to swear" (4) + *che.sed* "kindness" (4) + *e.met* "faithfulness" (3): fear sits inside the covenantal frame (→ T5).
- **God's manifest power & name** — *shem* "name" (16) + *yad/ze.ro.a* "hand/arm: power" (11+7) + *ka.vod* "glory" (4) + *strength* (3): the arouser/object is God's **self-revelation**, in qualifier role (→ T1 cause / T5).
- **Cognition / memory** — *ya.da* "to know" (6) + *za.khar* "to remember" (4): the faculty co-operation that corrects T3.
- **Life** — *chay.yim* "life" (7): reverent fear linked to life/longevity (→ T6).

**Held for T7 (parked):** the co-terms that are themselves other inner-being characteristics — *bo.shet* "shame" (4) and *cha.ta* "to sin" (4) — are noted but not interpreted, since relating this characteristic to *other characteristics* is T7, which is held silent.

## Reflection (held to the data)

- The completeness pass strengthens, rather than overturns, the v1.2 thesis: reverent fear is constituted by **relational direction and moral framing**. But it adds three things the verse-read fields alone missed — the **grammatical axis** (active / God-is-fearsome / commanded), the **behavioural fruit** (obedience, covenant, worship, joy), and the **cognition co-operation** (knowing, remembering) that corrects the "affect-only" reading.
- The richest single source of relational insight is the **compound field**, which is mostly *operational* for this characteristic (covenant, name, obedience, knowing) rather than other-characteristic synergy — so reporting it does not breach T7-silence; only the shame/sin partners are genuinely T7 and are held.
- The **stance finding** (commanded/addressed, rarely first-person) is significant for the inner-being question: Scripture's reverent-fear vocabulary is weighted toward *enjoining* the disposition on the hearer, not *describing* its felt interior — a clue about how this characteristic is taught rather than reported.
