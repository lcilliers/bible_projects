## First-round structural observations

**Type — a STATE, not an act.** status **52** (65%) · action 24 · quality 3. This is the headline and the sharpest contrast with C1 (a trait/act) and C2 (76% action): dread is predominantly a **condition one is in** — an abiding apprehension — not an event or a settled virtue.

**Faculty — pure affect.** affect 80, no blends (contrast C2's perception coupling).

**Valence — mostly unframed.** neutral **57** (71%) · righteous 9 · sinful 8 · forbidden 5 · commanded 1. Dread is largely **morally neutral** — a state that befalls, neither chiefly commanded (C1) nor chiefly forbidden (C2).

**Object — often none.** Only 34 of 80 carry a named object (abstract 12, person 10, God 8, situation 4). Nearly half of dread is **objectless** — a diffuse, free-floating apprehension.

**Experiencer — reported of others.** other 35 · addressed 13 · self 7.

**Location — sparse.** heart 6, flesh 1.

**Stem — largely nominal.** non-verb 55 (the noun *pachad* H6343), Qal 22. Dread is carried more as a **noun/state** than an active verb.

## First-round reading (descriptive)

Dread already looks like the **abiding, often objectless, morally-neutral state** of apprehension — distinct from reverent fear (a commanded disposition) and ordinary fear (a forbidden episodic reaction). The deep dive will test its divine-agency pattern (God *sending* dread), its causes, and the dread-vs-security contrast.
