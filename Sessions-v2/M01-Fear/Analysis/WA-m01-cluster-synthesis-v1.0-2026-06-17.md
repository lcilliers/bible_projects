## 1. What holds the family together

Across all 922 verses and 2,645 term-occurrences of M01, four observations recur in every characteristic and bind them into one family:

1. **Fear is creaturely.** God is **never** the experiencer of any fear term anywhere in the data (0 of 2,645). God appears only as the *object* of fear (165), its *agent/sender* (82), its *giver* (7), or its *addressee* (2). This is the single most consistent structural fact of the cluster and the anchor of every T0 reading.
2. **The discriminator is object + valence, not lemma.** The same vocabulary is commended toward God and forbidden toward circumstance. Membership by lemma and membership by data-signature diverge substantially (the C1 convergence test: 81 of 195 overlap). This is *the* methodological tension — see §5.
3. **Affect is the common faculty;** only two characteristics engage a second (ordinary fear blends perception; anxiety blends cognition).
4. **Every characteristic has a distinct foil** — fear is defined as much by what it is set against as by what it is.

**Three families by `type`:** dispositional trait (C1, C11) · episodic act (C2, C5, C7, C8, C10) · abiding state (C3, C4, C6, C9).

**The foil pattern splits cleanly.** The state/expression fears (C3 dread, C4 terror, C5 trembling, C6 horror, C7 dismay) share a **settledness** foil — security, peace, rest, composure. The trait and faculty fears have **relational/volitional** foils — reverence's misdirection, ordinary fear's courage, anxiety's comfort, cowardice's power-love-self-discipline. This split is itself a structural finding: the *state* fears are answered by stillness; the *disposition* fears are answered by a re-ordered inner life.

**The moral poles bracket the family.** Reverent fear (commanded, the created-design fear) and cowardice (sinful, the culpable fear) sit at opposite ends, and they share no verses (see §3).

## 3. The synergies (verse-level co-occurrence)

Where two characteristics' terms appear in the same verse, the lexicon is showing them operating together. The pattern:

**The twin hubs — fear (C2) and trembling (C5).** These are by far the most connected (co-occurrence degree 32 and 31), and they pair most strongly **with each other** (12 shared verses — the "fear and trembling" pairing, e.g. Psa 55:5, "Fear and trembling come upon me, and horror overwhelms me"). Fear is the affective core; trembling is its bodily expression; together they are the gravitational centre of the family.

**The dense core triangle — fear + trembling + dread (C2/C5/C3).** Dread is the third hub (degree 23); the three pair pairwise (C2+C3 = 7, C3+C5 = 7). This triangle is the cluster's centre of mass: the felt fear, its bodily shaking, and the abiding apprehension.

**Dismay (C7) as the secondary connector.** Degree 16, reaching trembling (4), fear (4), dread (3), shuddering (1), terror (1), astonishment (2) — dismay is where the collapse of composure meets the other fears.

**The reverent boundary.** Reverent fear (C1) co-occurs chiefly with ordinary fear (C2, 5 verses) and dread (C3, 4) — the empirical face of the C1↔C2 scope question (§5).

**The isolated periphery.** Terror (C4, degree 5), alarm (C8, 3), anxiety (C9, 3), astonishment (C10, 3) are weakly connected — each tends to **stand alone** in its verse. Terror's low connectivity is notable given the famous "Terror and dread fall upon them" of Exo 15:16: terror is overwhelmingly a *solitary, total* state, not a companion fear.

**Cowardice (C11) is completely isolated — 0 co-occurrences.** It never shares a verse with any other fear term in the data. The one fear treated as a moral vice is also the one lexically severed from the rest of the family. This is a genuine finding (and a candidate for verse-literary follow-up): cowardice is set apart not only morally (sinful) and grammatically (a trait) but relationally.

## 4. The shape of the whole

Read together, the lexicon presents fear as a field running between two poles. At one end is the fear that is **the beginning of wisdom** — reverent, commanded, God-directed, productive of obedience. At the other is **cowardice** — sinful, faithless, condemned, the fear God explicitly does not give. Between them sit the felt and inflicted fears: the affective core (ordinary fear) and its bodily expression (trembling), bound to the abiding apprehensions (dread, terror, horror) and the collapse of composure (dismay), with the sudden startle (alarm), the stunned arrest (astonishment), and the purely interior worry (anxiety) at the edges. The same vocabulary travels the whole range; **object and valence, not the word chosen, decide where a given instance falls.**

## 5. Aspects requiring further research

**HIGH — the scope decision (the gate).** Whether each characteristic is bounded by lemma or by data-signature (object + valence) is unresolved and governs all counts, boundaries, and dedup. The C1↔C2 overlap (28 occurrences by lemma; 5 co-occurring verses) is the clearest case: a valence-based scope would move the reverent-of-God uses out of ordinary fear and into reverent fear, and would carry the christological typology candidate with them. **Until this is decided, the membership of every characteristic is provisional.** *(Researcher decision.)*

**HIGH — verse-literary confirmation (T7.2) of the T0.4 candidates.** The typology candidates were surfaced inferentially and explicitly not confirmed: christological (strongest in C2, C10), theophanic (C4, C5), with C3/C6/C7/C9 silent. Each needs reading in literary and canonical context before any typological claim is made.

**MEDIUM — the Formative arc (T5) is near-silent throughout.** No characteristic showed a developmental/transformational arc in the lexical fields. Whether fear *forms, deepens, is tested, and is transformed* is a question the verse-literary layer must carry; the lexicon cannot.

**MEDIUM — the constitutional data is thin except for trembling.** Only trembling (C5) populates the spirit→soul→body→cosmos range; the others locate sparsely (mostly heart, a little soul). The spirit-soul-body boundary question — central to the programme — rests heavily on C5 (Isa 66:2 the contrite spirit; Hab 3:16 the body) and on verse_text the location field did not capture (e.g. the body language of Dan 5:6). Worth a dedicated body-direction pass across the cluster.

**MEDIUM — cowardice's isolation.** The 0-co-occurrence finding is real but rests on only 5 occurrences, all NT. Its relation to faith/unbelief (Mat 8:26, "O you of little faith") is a T6 + T7.2 matter.

**LOW–MEDIUM — small-sample characteristics.** C9 (anxiety, 13), C10 (astonishment, 14), C6 (horror, 17), C8 (alarm, 23), C11 (cowardice, 5) carry clear profiles but narrow samples; their distinctive patterns (anxiety's cognition-blend and divine-absence; cowardice's sinful-trait pattern) should be confirmed as related clusters (e.g. anxiety/worry elsewhere) are analysed.

**LOW — residual and boundary terms.** H1481C (10) and H6206 (15) are genuine fear terms not cleanly placed (dread/awe-leaning); shared terms (G1568 C7/C10; H2731 C5/C9; H8429 C8/C10; H0927/H0928 C7/C8) sit in one characteristic with a noted overlap. All resolve only after the scope decision. H8047G (desolation/horror), H4288 (ruin), and the homonym noise (H4867 waves, etc.) remain correctly set aside as external-result/noise.
