## Summary
Terror is the **acute, overwhelming, soul-deep state** of fear — the most state-like (status 90%) and most morally-neutral (96%) characteristic, carried **entirely as a noun**. Its distinctive marks: it is something **God spreads** on his enemies (divine involvement agent-only), it **freezes** the person ("still as a stone," Exo 15:16), it reaches the **soul** rather than the heart, it recurs as *terror on every side* and *terror in the land of the living*, and its lexical foil is **peace**. It is the intensified pole of the dread family.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
God never its experiencer (0); divine role **agent only** (7) — terror inflicted, never felt, never revered. A creaturely condition under overwhelming threat, not a cultivated design. **T0.4 (inferential):** eschatological (Luk 21:11; Heb 10:27) and theophanic/christological (Heb 12:21 Sinai; Mar 9:6 transfiguration) candidates present in NT verse_text; covenantal none; routed to T7.2.

## Reflection (significance and affected perspectives)
- **Terror is the limiting case of fear-as-state.** Where reverent fear is a disposition and ordinary fear an act, terror is a pure *condition* — nominal, objectless, neutral. For the inner-being question it marks the point where fear ceases to be something the person *does* and becomes something the person *is in*, reaching the soul and freezing agency ("still as a stone").
- **Divinely-spread terror** will read differently across communities (an instrument of holy-war judgement); the data presents it neutrally and as agent-only.
- **The foil is peace, not courage or security** — each fear characteristic so far has a different lexical opposite (reverent fear: misdirection/withholding; ordinary fear: courage; dread: security; terror: peace), which is itself a finding about how the cluster is structured.
