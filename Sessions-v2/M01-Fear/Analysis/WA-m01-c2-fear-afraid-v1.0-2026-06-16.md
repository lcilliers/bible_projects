**Faculty — affect, now coupling with perception.** affect 336 · **affect+perception 43** (11%). C1 had no blends; C2 fear couples with perceiving — fear of what is seen/registered.

**Valence — dominated by what is forbidden.** neutral 152 · **forbidden 107** · righteous 65 · commanded 29 · sinful 26. The headline inverts C1: where reverent fear was mostly *commanded*, ordinary fear is the fear most often **forbidden** ("do not be afraid").

**Object — varied.** person 67 · situation 58 · God 42 · abstract 19 · thing 8 · spiritual-being 5 · threat 2 (201 named of 379). Ordinary fear targets people, circumstances, and God — far more varied than C1's God-dominated object.

**Experiencer — felt more often than reverent fear, still mostly addressed.** other-addressed 137 · other 131 · **self 34** (vs C1's 7). The "addressed" weight is the *fear-not* address; but first-person fear is materially present.

**Location — heart, now with flesh.** heart 23 · flesh 4 · soul 3 (34 of 379). Heart dominant again; **flesh** appears (somatic), which C1 lacked.
