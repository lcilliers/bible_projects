## 8. Deep-dive synthesis

Ordinary fear is the **episodic, perceived, magnitude-bearing affect** that Scripture most often **forbids** when its object is a circumstance or person, and **commends** only when its object is God. Its partnered opposite is **courage/strength** ("be strong, do not fear"); its bodily form is **trembling**; its outworking is **dismay and flight**. The reverent minority within its vocabulary is the bridge to C1.
