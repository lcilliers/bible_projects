## T1 — Definition
**Name.** 8 terms across Hebrew (H3372G, H3374, H3025, H3016, H7297), Aramaic (H1763), Greek (G5399 phobeō, G5401 phobos). The Greek pair carries the characteristic into the NT directly. The shared core: to fear / be afraid.
**Grammatical axis.** Predominantly **Qal active** (186) — the person as fearing subject — with few Niphal (11). Unlike C1, there is no large "to-be-feared" (Niphal/awesome) group: ordinary fear is something one *does*, not a quality God *is*.
**Kind (from data).** An **episodic affect** (type action 289) that **couples with perception** (faculty affect+perception 43), comes in **degrees** (intensity "greatly"), is **felt and reacted to** (experiencer self 34; responses rich), and is **morally negative by default** (valence forbidden 107 / neutral 152) except when directed at God. Closest reference dimension: **Emotion — Negative**, with a perceptual edge — but not reducible to the label.
**Boundary (lexical contrast).** Drawn two ways in the data: (a) the **forbidden** mass (107) — fear that is commanded *away*; and (b) the **courage/strength** compound partner ("be strong, do not fear") — the lexical opposite is not calm but **strength/trust**.

## T2 — Constitutional Location (sparse)
`location` on 34 of 379: **heart 23**, **flesh 4**, soul 3 (plus blends). Heart dominant, but the appearance of **flesh** (absent in C1) seats ordinary fear partly in the body — consistent with its trembling co-state. Otherwise unlocated; spirit/mind not named. Silence noted.

## T3 — Inner Faculties (moderate)
`faculty` = affect 336 + **affect+perception 43**. The perception coupling is a genuine T3 finding: ordinary fear is **felt in response to what is perceived** — it engages the perceptual faculty as reverent fear did not. Compound co-terms add **cognition** ("to know" 8, "knowledge" 7) — fear operating with knowing/recognising. So C2 engages affect + perception + (via co-terms) cognition.

## T5 — Relational Interfaces (rich)
**Divine.** Two directions: God as **object** of fear (57) and God as **agent** sending fear (20). Direction runs both human→God (reverent minority) and God→human (inflicted terror). Causes include encounter/exposure before God.
**Human.** `object_type`=person 67 — fear of enemies, kings, crowds (the largest single object). The **fear-not address** (experiencer addressed 137) is overwhelmingly a *person-to-person* reassurance ("do not be afraid of them").
**Spiritual-beings.** Minor (spiritual-being 5), mixed framing.
**Stance.** Heavily **addressed** — the characteristic is operated through the *command not to fear* far more than through first-person report.

## T6 — Formative / Developmental (moderate)
**Behavioural fruit (immediate_response, 86).** Ordinary fear issues in **dismay, trembling, panic, paralysis, and flight** — the opposite outworking to reverent fear's obedience and worship. **Magnitude** (intensity "greatly") and **episodic type** (action) frame it as a spiking event rather than a forming trait. No transformation/sequence in the lexicon beyond this.
