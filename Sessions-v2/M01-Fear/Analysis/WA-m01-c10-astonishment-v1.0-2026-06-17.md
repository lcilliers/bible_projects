## Summary
Astonishment / being awe-struck is the **stunned, dumbfounded reaction** — the inner being arrested by something overwhelming or unbelievable. It borders reverent awe (C1) but is the *stunned* response rather than the *honouring* one. An episodic act (action 12), mostly neutral, frequently provoked by **God's astonishing work** ("Be astounded! For I am doing a work…", and "the pillars of heaven… are astounded at his rebuke," Job 26:11) and by overwhelming sights (Psa 48:5, "they were astounded… in panic… took to flight").

## Reflection
Astonishment is the **arrest of the inner being** before the overwhelming or unbelievable — close kin to reverent awe but without its honouring orientation; close kin to dismay but stunned rather than shattered. Its most distinctive trigger is **God's astonishing work** ("I am doing a work that you would not believe"), which makes it the cluster's bridge between fear and wonder. Small (14), held lightly; G1568 is shared with dismay (C7) and is assigned here on its awe-struck gloss with the overlap noted.
