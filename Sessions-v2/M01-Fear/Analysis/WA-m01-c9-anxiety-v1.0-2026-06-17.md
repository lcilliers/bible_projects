## Summary
Anxiety / disquiet is the **inner unrest of worried care** — the settled, gnawing condition distinct from acute fright. It is the most **interior and most cognitive** of the fears, and uniquely **has no divine involvement at all** (0): it is a purely creaturely inner disturbance. Located in the **heart, soul, and inner parts**, it engages **cognition** (the only fear besides ordinary fear to blend a second faculty), and its relief is **comfort/consolation**: "Anxiety in a man's heart weighs him down, but a good word makes him glad" (Pro 12:25); "When the cares of my heart are many, your consolations cheer my soul" (Psa 94:19).

- **T2.** **Soul 2 / heart 2 / inner-parts** (compound "entrails: inner parts") — the most interiorly-located fear; reaches the heart and the gut. (Pro 12:25 heart; Psa 94:19 heart→soul.)
- **T3.** **Affect + cognition** (2 of 13 explicitly; the disquieting-*thoughts* sense throughout) — anxiety is the fear that **thinks**: worried rumination. With ordinary fear's perception-blend, one of only two characteristics engaging a second faculty.
- **T4.** **Human:** anxiety over persons and circumstances (bad news; a sick friend, Php 2). **No divine interface** (0) — neither feared-of-God nor God-sent.
- **T5.** Near-silent (one adjacency: anxiety relieved by consolation — a movement, not an arc).

- **T7.** Hebrew + a little Aramaic/Greek; *deagah* core; inner-unrest sense.

## Reflection
Anxiety is the **most psychologically interior** characteristic in the cluster, and its profile is distinctive on two counts that bear directly on the inner-being question: it **engages cognition** (worried thought, not just affect), and it is **purely creaturely** — the lexicon never sets it God-ward (neither fear *of* God nor fear *from* God). Its relief is not courage, security, or peace (the other fears' foils) but **a good word / consolation** — comfort that addresses the thinking heart. Small (13), so held lightly, but the cognitive and the divine-absence patterns are clear.
