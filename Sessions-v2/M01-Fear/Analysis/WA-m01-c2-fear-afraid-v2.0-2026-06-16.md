## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (new)

**T0.1 Divine Nature Reflected.** As across all M01, God is **never the experiencer of fear** (0). For ordinary fear, `divine_involvement` is **object 57** and **agent 20** — God is feared and **sends** fear, but does not feel it. Fear is not a divine attribute (T0.1.2); the silence (T0.1.3) places this characteristic on the creaturely side of the image.

**T0.2 Created Purpose.** Here the evidence leans the **opposite way to C1**. Ordinary fear is framed **forbidden 107 / neutral 152**-dominant, and its enacted fruit is **dismay and flight** (T1.5) — so the data presents it largely as a **condition to be addressed rather than a design to be cultivated** (T0.2.2: closer to a fallen-condition response than created design). *Held with care:* some neutral fear is ordinary creaturely self-preservation, so a flat "fallen only" reading would overreach — the created-purpose status of ordinary fear is **genuinely mixed** in the lexical evidence, and the forbidden weighting is the dominant, not the only, signal.

**T0.3 Image-Bearer Expression.** A creaturely analogue (God does not fear). The forbidden-dominant pattern suggests this form of fear marks **the creature under threat** more than an image-bearing excellence — its commendable form is the God-directed minority that overlaps C1.

**T0.4 Typological Significance.** Not lexically derivable; noted per T0.4.3.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1 Name.** 8 terms — Hebrew (H3372G, H3374, H3025, H3016, H7297), Aramaic (H1763), Greek (G5399 *phobeō*, G5401 *phobos*). The Greek pair makes the characteristic **genuinely bi-testamental** (OT 253 / NT 126).

**T1.2 Kind.** An **episodic affect** (type action 289 = 76%) that **couples with perception** (T3.1), comes in **degrees** (intensity "greatly"), and is **morally negative by default** (forbidden+neutral) except toward God. Reference dimension: Emotion — Negative with a perceptual edge; not reducible.

**T1.3 Boundary.** Drawn two ways: the **forbidden** mass (107) — fear commanded *away* — and the **courage/strength** compound partner ("be strong and do not fear"). The opposite of ordinary fear is **strength/trust**, not calm.

**T1.4 Modes of Operation.** Predominantly **Qal active** (186) — fear as something one *does*; few Niphal (11); **no "to-be-feared/awesome" group** (contrast C1). Communicative mode (T1.4.3): the **"fear not" address** is the characteristic's speech-based operation — it is most often *spoken against* to a hearer.

**T1.5 Immediate Response.** `immediate_response` (86, rich): **dismay, trembling, panic, paralysis, devouring, flight/avoidance.** The first response to fear is disturbance and withdrawal.

**T1.6 Sustained Effect.** Thin in the lexicon — ordinary fear is episodic (action-typed), not a forming trait (quality only 5). What it produces over time is not carried by the fields; noted.

**T1.7 Conditions of Reception.** Fear is not "received" as a gift; the relevant condition is the **forbidden** framing — the inner posture under which this fear is to be **refused/resisted** ("do not be afraid"). The `relational` field's dominant token is the negation ("not" 25).
