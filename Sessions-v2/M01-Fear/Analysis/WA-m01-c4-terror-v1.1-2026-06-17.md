## 1. Divine agency — terror is sent, never revered

`divine_involvement` is **agent on all 7** filled (no object, no giver). Terror is exclusively something **God spreads/inflicts** — there is no "terror *of* God" as a reverent object here (contrast C1, and even C3's small object set). The signature: **Exo 15:16, "Terror and dread fall upon them… they are still as a stone."**

## 2. Response — paralysis / petrification

`immediate_response` (6) and the verses behind it show terror issuing in **paralysis**: *still as a stone* (Exo 15:16), pressure *heavy* (Job 33:7), being *held* on "the day of the anger of the Lord" (Lam 2:22). Where ordinary fear flees (C2) and dread shakes (C3), terror **freezes**.

## 3. Signature motifs

Two recur in the lexicon:
- **"Terror on every side"** (*magor missabib*) — Psa 31:13 ("the whispering of many — terror on every side"), Lam 2:22, Jeremiah's denunciation contexts.
- **"Terror in the land of the living"** — the Ezekiel motif, carried by the compound co-terms *alive* (6), *kinsfolk* (6), *community* (6): the fallen warriors who once *spread terror in the land of the living*.

## 4. Synergy (compound, 35 filled)

Beyond the Ezekiel motif co-terms: **shame** (3), **trembling** (2) — the somatic co-state — and, contrastively, **peace** (2). Terror's lexical foil in this data is **peace**.

## 5. Soul-location

The few located occurrences touch the **soul/nephesh** (Psa 31:13; Jer 14:19, "does your soul loathe Zion") more than the heart — terror reaches the innermost personal seat, fitting its overwhelming character.

## 6. Cause

12 resolved: military/enemy threat ("spreading terror in the land of the living," "enemy sword on every side," "many enemies scheming"), **deep darkness** ("terrors of deep darkness," Job), **the road / mortality** ("terrors in the way," Ecclesiastes), and **denunciation** ("many whispering and denouncing him"). Terror's arousers are overwhelming external threat and the approach of death.

## 7. T0.4 candidates (inferential)

The cause-field scan showed none, but the **NT verse_text** carries real candidates (a reminder to read verse_text, not only `cause`):
- **Eschatological:** Luk 21:11 ("terrors and great signs from heaven"), Heb 10:27 ("a fearful expectation of judgment").
- **Theophanic / christological:** Heb 12:21 (Sinai — "so terrifying… Moses said, 'I tremble with fear'"), Mar 9:6 (the disciples "terrified" at the transfiguration). Direction: the divine/theophanic instance evokes terror.
- **Covenantal:** none. Confirmation routed to T7.2.

## 8. Deep-dive synthesis

Terror is the **acute, overwhelming, soul-deep state** that God **spreads** on his enemies, that **freezes** the person ("still as a stone"), recurs as *terror on every side* and *in the land of the living*, and stands against **peace**. It is the intensified, purely-nominal pole of the dread family.
