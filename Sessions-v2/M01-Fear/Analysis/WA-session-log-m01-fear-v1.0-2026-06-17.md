## 3. Analytical conclusions so far (lexicon-grounded)

- **A T0 finding holding across all M01:** God is the *object* of fear (165), its *agent* (82), its *giver* (7), or *addressee* (2) — **never its experiencer (0)**. Fear is not a divine attribute; it sits on the creaturely side of the image.
- **C1 Reverent Fear** — a God-directed, morally-required **disposition** (commanded/righteous 77%); defined by object + valence, not by lemma; produces obedience and worship; OT-weighted in its anchor.
- **C2 Fear / Being Afraid** — an **episodic** affect (action 76%), **forbidden**-dominant, **perception**-coupled; its opposite is **courage/strength**, not calm; genuinely bi-testamental; carries the **strong christological T0.4 candidate** (fear at Jesus' divine acts).
- **C3 Dread** — a **state** (status 65%), mostly **neutral**, often **objectless**, characteristically **divinely sent** (agent 12); its opposite is **security**; OT-only; T0.4 silent.
- The three are a coherent contrast set: reverent disposition / forbidden episodic reaction / inflicted abiding state — distinguished primarily by `type`, `valence`, `object`, and `divine_involvement`.
- The extract itself improved markedly across versions (the five-file fan-out: `cause` resolved, `valence` added, full data-dictionary).
