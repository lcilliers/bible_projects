## Summary

Dread is the **abiding, often objectless, morally-neutral state of apprehension** — a condition one is *in* (status-typed), not an act one does or a virtue one holds. Its distinctive mark is **divine agency**: more than any other fear so far, dread is something **God sends** upon his enemies ("the dread of the LORD fell upon them"). It **arrives** ("the thing I dreaded has come upon me," Job 3:25), the body registers it as **trembling**, and its lexical opposite is **security** — the righteous dwell secure *without* dread (Pro 1:33). It is OT-only in this anchor, with no typological candidates.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
God is never the experiencer of dread (0); the divine role is **agent** (12) — dread inflicted, not felt. As a mostly-neutral state (valence neutral 57) it reads as a **creaturely condition under threat**, not a cultivated design; its one commendable form is the small *dread of God* (righteous 4). **T0.4 (inferential):** christological, eschatological, and covenantal candidates are all **absent** — a graded silence for dread, recorded per the T0.4 method.

## Reflection (significance and affected perspectives)
- **Dread is constituted as a state, not an act** — which is why valence mostly disengages (a condition is not commanded or forbidden the way an act is). For the inner-being question this matters: dread describes *how a person is*, a settled apprehensive condition, more than *what a person does*.
- **The divine-agency pattern is theologically weighty and will read differently across communities.** That dread is predominantly something **God sends** (agent 12) frames it as an instrument of divine action against enemies — a portrait some traditions will receive as just judgement and others will find hard. The data presents it without resolving that; the valence stays neutral.
- **Dread vs security is the live contrast** — the lexicon pairs dread not with courage (as ordinary fear) nor with reverence (as C1) but with **security/safety**. The relief of dread, in this data, is *security*, characteristically located with the righteous.
- I would hold application here: whether divinely-sent dread is paradigmatic or occasional is a verse-literary/canonical (T7.2) question the lexicon does not settle.
