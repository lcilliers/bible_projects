## 1. Divine agency — dread is something God SENDS

`divine_involvement`: **agent 12** · object 6 · giver 2. Unlike reverent fear (God as object) and ordinary fear (mixed), dread's dominant divine role is **agent** — God **inflicts** dread. The pattern is the "dread of the LORD / of the Jews / of Mordecai **fell upon** them" (Esther; conquest texts): dread sent on enemies as an instrument of divine action. So dread is, more than the other fears, a **weapon in God's hand**.

## 2. Valence × object — neutral throughout; righteous = God

| valence | objects |

| righteous (9) | God 4 · abstract 3 (none 2) |

Neutral dominates across every object; the small righteous set is **dread of God** (the reverent overlap, *pachad* of the LORD). Dread is rarely commanded or forbidden — it is mostly a state simply reported.

## 3. Cause — the dreaded thing arriving, and divinely-sent dread

14 resolved causes. The signature case: **"the dreaded thing actually befalling him"** (Job 3:25, "the thing that I fear comes upon me") — dread as **anticipation realised**. Others: "God being with the righteous," "the fear of the Lord upon them," "the Lord fighting against Israel's enemies," "fear of the Jews/Mordecai falling on them" (Esther). Causes split between **anticipated calamity arriving** and **divinely-inflicted dread on enemies**.

## 4. Synergy (compound, 67 filled)

Co-terms: **to tremble** (6) + **trembling** (4) — the somatic co-state; **to come/come** (6) — the *arriving* quality of dread; within-cluster fear/dread terms; **heart** (4); **life** (3); **distress: harm** (3); and a contrast pair — **righteous** (3) / **wicked** (3) / **security** (3): the dread that falls on the wicked set against the **security of the righteous** (Pro 1:33, "will dwell secure… without dread of disaster"). Dread's lexical opposite, in this data, is **security/safety**.

## 5. Behavioural responses (immediate_response, 14)

Sparse but telling: **shake**, **dismayed**, things that **rise/creep** (the cosmic tremor before dread), assurance, "become salvation." Dread issues in shaking and dismay — and, in the divine-deliverance cases, gives way to security.

## 6. Objectlessness — a diffuse state

46 of 80 carry **no named object**. Dread is the most **free-floating** of the fears so far — frequently a condition in which a person or people *is*, without a specified target. This corroborates the status-dominant `type` (a state, not a directed act).

## 7. Deep-dive synthesis

Dread is the **abiding, often objectless state of apprehension** that God characteristically **sends** upon his enemies, that **arrives** ("the thing I dreaded came upon me"), that the body registers as **trembling**, and whose lexical opposite is **security**. It is morally neutral by default — a condition, not a commanded or forbidden act. OT-only in this anchor.
