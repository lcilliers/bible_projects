## First-round structural observations

**Type — an action (the body shaking).** action **83** (73%) · status 19 · quality 10. Unlike the state-dominant Dread (C3) and Terror (C4), trembling is back to an **event/act** — something the body *does*.

**Stem — verbal, with a strong causative.** Qal 53 · **Hiphil 25** · Hithpael 4 · non-verb 31. The Hiphil (causative, "make tremble") is prominent — trembling is frequently **caused**, often by God (`divine_involvement` agent 16).

**Faculty — affect** (all 113).

**Valence — mostly neutral, but commandable.** neutral 83 (73%) · **righteous 13 · commanded 7** · sinful 7 · forbidden 3. The righteous+commanded minority (20) is real: trembling can be the **right bodily response to God** ("let the peoples tremble," Psa 99:1).

**Object — varied; God notable.** situation 28 · God 14 · abstract 14 · person 10 (66/113 named).

**Experiencer — reported of others** (other 64 · addressed 10 · self 6) — including the earth, mountains, and nations.

**Location — and here is the distinctive thing.** Only 10 located, but they span constitutional levels: **heart 6, soul, and — uniquely — spirit (Isa 66:2)**, plus heart+soul / flesh+heart blends. No other characteristic so far has reached the spirit level.

## First-round reading (descriptive)

Trembling is the **somatic act** of fear — the body shaking — frequently **God-caused** (causative Hiphil), and uniquely **spanning the constitutional range** (spirit → soul → heart, and outward). The deep dive will trace that inner-to-outer movement and the reverent/commanded trembling.
