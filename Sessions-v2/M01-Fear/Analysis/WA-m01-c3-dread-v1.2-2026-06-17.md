## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
**T0.1.** God is never the experiencer of dread (0); divine role is **agent 12** (sends dread), object 6, giver 2. Dread is a creaturely state God can *inflict* but does not *feel* — firmly on the creaturely side of the image.
**T0.2.** Mostly **neutral** valence (57) and status-typed — dread reads as a **creaturely condition under threat**, neither a cultivated design (C1) nor primarily a forbidden disorder (C2). Created-purpose signal is weak; mostly a state that befalls.
**T0.3.** A creaturely analogue; its commendable form is the small *dread of God* (righteous, 4).
**T0.4.** Candidate-surfacing (inferential): **christological — none** (no NT in anchor); **eschatological — none** (no day-of-the-Lord/judgment cause); **covenantal — none** (no covenant co-term). T0.4 is **silent for dread** on the lexical evidence — a graded silence, not "excluded."

## T1 — Definition
**T1.1 Name.** *pachad* core — H6343 (noun, 48), H6342 (verb, 25); plus H0366/H2844A/H4034. OT Hebrew only.
**T1.2 Kind.** A **state/condition** (status 52) of abiding apprehension — not an act or a virtue.
**T1.3 Boundary.** The lexical opposite is **security/safety** (compound "security" 3; Pro 1:33 the righteous dwell secure *without dread*).
**T1.4 Modes.** Largely **nominal** (non-verb 55) — dread is carried as a noun/state more than an active verb.
**T1.5 Immediate Response.** shake, dismay, cosmic tremor (immediate_response 14).
**T1.6 Sustained Effect.** As a status it *is* the sustained condition; the lexicon does not separate a further downstream effect.
**T1.7 Conditions of Reception.** Often inflicted, not received by choice; the relevant condition is its **arrival** ("the thing I dreaded came upon me," Job 3:25).
