## Summary
Alarm / sudden fright is the **abrupt startle response** — being suddenly alarmed, especially by **dreams, visions, and thoughts**. Its lexical home is striking: **Aramaic-concentrated in Daniel** (*behal*, H0927), where dreams and visions repeatedly **alarm** the king and the seer ("a dream that made me afraid… alarmed me," Dan 4:5; "his thoughts alarmed him," Dan 5:6). Mostly neutral, an episodic act, with a Greek startle vocabulary in the NT.

## Reflection
Alarm is the **sudden-onset** fear — distinct from the abiding states (dread/terror) by its abruptness, and from trembling by its trigger (often a **dream or vision** rather than a present threat). Its Danielic concentration ties it to the disturbance of **revelatory dreams** — fear at the uncanny and the unknown future. Small and cross-lingual; broader claims limited.
