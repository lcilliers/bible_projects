## 1. Identity
Anticipatory, forward-looking apprehension — a weight of something feared ahead, **a condition one is *in*** (T1.1.1). The ***pachad* core**: H6343 *pa.chad* (noun), H6342 (verb), with H2844A *chat*, H4034 *me.go.rah*, H0366 *a.yom* "terrible" — OT Hebrew (T1.1.2). A **condition/state** (status 52 / action 24), largely **nominal** — operates as a state named, not an act done (T1.2.1/T1.4.1). Its name carries a **temporal** implication (forward-looking) (T1.1.3). Appears **simple** at first encounter (purely affective) (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
God's role is chiefly **agent** (14) — "the dread of the LORD fell"; the character imaged is God's **dread-inspiring might against his enemies**, with a small reverent object set (7) (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2); a creaturely response, exclusively a creaturely analogue (T0.1.3/T0.3.2). Expresses **creaturely finitude/vulnerability** — the image under threat, not an excellence (T0.3.1); presence reveals the creature under threat, valence mostly neutral (T0.3.3). Typology: **silent** — christological 0 / eschatological 0 / covenantal 0 (T0.4.1/0.4.3).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
**Neutral 57-dominant** — a state one is in, not an equipping; small "dread of God" (righteous 9) the commendable residue (T0.2.1). Reads as **fallen-condition response** (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **shaking / dismay / the dreaded thing arriving** (Job 3:25) (T1.5.1). As an abiding **status**-typed state, what it "produces over time" is the **continuance of the condition** (T1.6.1); immediate and sustained largely coincide (T1.6.3).

## 4. Constitution
**Heart** 6; soul silent; flesh 1 (T2.3.1; T2.2; T2.6.1). **Silent on spirit and mind** (T2.1; T2.4). Origin **received-from-outside** (2), and **God-sent** (agent) when inflicted on enemies (T2.9.1/2.9.3). Movement: **onto the person from outside** ("the dread came/fell upon") (T2.10.1).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, affect-only at the faculty level (T3.4). Lighter cognitive co-terms (know/understand/remember) (T3.2/T3.3). **Silent** on volition, agency, perception-as-faculty, moral evaluation, creativity, conscience (neutral-dominant — morally inert) (T3.6–3.11). Its anticipatory dimension is best lit by clinical anxiety research alongside c9 (T7.3.1).

## 6. Relational interfaces
God object 17 (minority reverence) (T4.2.1). Persons as the dread-inspiring party (dread of persons 11) (T4.3.1); received from a threatening party (addressed 13) (T4.4.1). God's agent-mode (sending dread on opposition) is **responsive/judicial** (T4.1.2). **Silent** on the spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.4).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on transformation/sanctification (T5.1/T5.5). **Concentrated in the suffering literature** — 15 Job/Lam refs; `cause` = "calamity from God and his majesty" (Job 3:25; Job 4:14, "dread came upon me, and trembling, which made all my bones shake") — a product and inner context of affliction (T5.4). Faint sequence: dread giving way to **security** in deliverance (Pro 1:33) (T5.2). **Silent** on eschatological trajectory (T5.6.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **security / safety** (*be.tach*, *ma.oz*) — Pro 1:33, "whoever listens to me will dwell secure… without dread of disaster" (T1.3.1); excludes security/ease (T1.3.2). Distinguished from **c4 (terror)** by **form + intensity**: dread = verb-capable **state that arrives**, opposite security; terror = nominal, overwhelming, soul-deep, opposite peace (T6.5.1). The ***pachad* root** reaches from dread into c1's reverent awe (H6345) — threat-fear and God-fear are lexically continuous (T6.4.2). Within the terror-complex, dread **produces trembling (c5)** (Job 4:14) (T6.3.1). Word-paired with terror (Deu 11:25) — accompanying rather than sequential (T6.2.1).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **wisdom (Job) + prophecy 23 + psalm 15** — a wisdom/suffering word, read in the Job dialogues (T7.2.2). Logical structure: affliction lament — "the thing I dread befalls me" (Job 3:25), premise(dread) realised as conclusion(calamity arrives) (T7.2.3). Anchor **Job 3:25** uniquely names dread's **self-fulfilling arrival** (T7.2.6). Science lens: **clinical anxiety research** (anticipatory dimension) (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Silences:* spirit, mind, typology, eschatological trajectory, volition/agency/moral faculties; transformation/sanctification. Dread is the affliction-register fear par excellence, but morally inert in the fields.

## 11. Essence
Dread is the **anticipatory state that arrives** — the forward-leaning weight of feared calamity, most at home in Job's suffering, in which "the thing I dread befalls me." Its foil is security; its kin is terror; its root reaches, surprisingly, into reverent awe.
