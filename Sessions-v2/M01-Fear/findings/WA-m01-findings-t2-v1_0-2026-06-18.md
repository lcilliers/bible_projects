# T2.1 · Spirit-Level Location

## T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?

**Finding.** Almost never. **Spirit-location occurs in only two characteristics, one record each:**

- **c5 (Trembling)** — **yes**, the deepest case in the cluster: Isa 66:2 — *"this is the one to whom I will look: he who is humble and contrite in spirit and trembles at my word."* Trembling reaches the spirit.
- **c7 (Dismay)** — **yes**, one case: Ecc 7:9 — *"Be not quick in your spirit to become angry…"* (the dismay term *bahal* = hasty/dismayed, located in the spirit).
- **c1, c2, c3, c4, c6, c8, c9, c10, c11** — **silent** on spirit-level location (not named).

## T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?

**Finding.** **No characteristic is primarily spirit-level.** Even c5 and c7, the only two with a spirit record, are dominated by heart/soul/body location. The cluster as a whole is **not** a spirit-level family.

- **c5** — spirit is the *deepest reach* of a characteristic that spans the whole range; it is not its primary seat (heart 7 / soul 2 / spirit 1 / flesh 1).
- **c7** — one spirit case against soul 5 / heart 3; not primarily spirit-level.
- **c1–c4, c6, c8–c11** — silent; none originates at the spirit level. Recorded.

## T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**Finding.** Where it appears, spirit-location marks the **deepest, most personal register** of the characteristic:

- **c5** — reveals that trembling can be the response of the *innermost person* — the contrite spirit's reverent response to God's word (Isa 66:2). Its **deepest form is positive**: a sought, commended state, distinct from terror-stricken bodily shaking. This is the cluster's clearest witness that a fear-characteristic can reach the spirit.
- **c7** — reveals dismay/haste touching the spirit's governing of disposition ("not quick in your spirit").
- All others — no spirit-depth evidenced; recorded as the cluster's general non-engagement of the spirit level.

## T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** Spirit-level location is **silent in nine of eleven characteristics**: c1, c2, c3, c4, c6, c8, c9, c10, c11. Only c5 and c7 carry a single spirit record each. The cluster is overwhelmingly **not** spirit-located.

# T2.10 · Constitutional Movement

## T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?

**Finding.** One characteristic is the cluster's movement witness; a second pattern (downward arrival onto the person) recurs more widely.

- **c5 (Trembling)** — **the key witness:** it crosses the **whole constitutional range** — spirit (Isa 66:2), through soul/heart, **outward to the body** (Job 4:14 — *"dread came upon me, and trembling, which made all my bones shake"*; Hab 3:16 — *"my body trembles; my lips quiver… my legs tremble beneath me"*), and even the **cosmos** (Exo 19:18 Sinai; Psa 99:1 — *"let the earth quake!"*). Movement is **inner-to-outer** — the inner state made visible.
- **c2/c3 (received-from-outside origin)** — movement **onto** the person from outside (the "fear came / fell upon" idiom): Job 4:14; the dread "falls upon".
- **c4/c6/c7** — fear/horror/dismay that **falls upon / seizes** peoples (downward-onto), but no internal level-crossing.
- **c1, c8, c9, c10, c11** — **silent** on constitutional movement.

## T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?

**Finding.**

- **c5** — the pattern is **spirit/soul → body → cosmos**: an inner state expressing outward through the body and, in theophany, registering in creation. The body is the **visible crossing** of the spirit-soul-body boundary (T2.7).
- **c2/c3/c4/c6/c7** — the pattern is **external → onto the person** (arrival/befalling), not internal sequence.
- Others — no pattern (silent). Recorded.

## T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No constitutional movement is evidenced for **c1, c8, c9, c10, c11** (silent). c2/c3/c4/c6/c7 show only **downward arrival** (external-onto-person), not internal level-crossing. Only **c5** shows genuine internal-to-outer movement across the full range.

**Finding.** **Not applicable — no mind-location is evidenced.** Where mind-like content appears (worried *thoughts*, alarming *thoughts*), it is engaged as **cognition (faculty, T3.2)**, not as a constitutional seat. Recorded as silence with the faculty cross-reference.

# T2.6 · Body — Significance

## T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**Finding.** Body-linkage appears as **`flesh` (location field)** and as **named body parts (verse_text)**:

- **flesh (location):** c1 (3), c2 (3), c3 (1), c5 (1), c6 (1), c7 (1).
- **named body parts (verse_text):** **c5** — bones, lips, legs/body (Job 4:14 — *"made all my bones shake"*; Hab 3:16 — *"my lips quiver… my legs tremble"*); **c8** — limbs, colour, knees (Dan 5:6 — *"his limbs gave way, and his knees knocked together"*).
- **c6** — the **bristling flesh** (H8175A "to shudder/bristle (with horror)").
- **c4, c9, c10, c11** — **no specific body-part link** (c4/c9 are interior; c10/c11 sparse). Recorded.

## T2.6.2 — If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?

**Finding.** The link is chiefly **expressive** and **indicative**:

- **c5** — **expressive**: the bodily shaking *expresses outward* the inner state (the soul/spirit made visible) — the most developed body-significance in the cluster.
- **c8** — **indicative/expressive**: the body **registers** the sudden alarm (colour changes, limbs fail) — the body as the visible sign of the startle.
- **c6** — **expressive**: the flesh bristles — visceral recoil.
- **c1/c2/c3/c7** — flesh-location is **indicative** (the state seated in/registered by the body) but not developed.
- Others — silent. Recorded.

## T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No body-part link is evidenced for **c4, c9, c10, c11** (c4 reaches the soul not the body; c9 is interior; c10/c11 sparse).

# T2.7 · Body — Direction

## T2.7.1 — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?

**Finding.** Where evidenced, the direction is **soul/spirit → body (outward expression)**:

- **c5** — **soul/spirit expresses through the body**: the inner fear moves outward into trembling bones, lips, legs (Job 4:14; Hab 3:16) — the clearest directional case.
- **c8** — **inner startle → body**: the alarming thought issues in failing limbs and changed colour (Dan 5:6) — soul-to-body.
- **c6** — inner horror → bristling flesh (soul-to-body).
- **c1/c2/c3/c7** — flesh-location present but **direction not developed** in the evidence (recorded as indicative seating, direction not specified).
- **c4, c9, c10, c11** — **silent** (no body link).
- *No characteristic shows clear body-to-soul feedback in the lexicon* — recorded.

## T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**Finding.** The consequence (chiefly from c5): fear is **an inner state that becomes visible** — the body is the site where the inner condition crosses into the observable. For the programme's governing question this is the cluster's strongest constitutional witness: trembling is *the inner state made outward*, and the spirit-soul-body boundary is shown to be **permeable in the outward direction**.

## T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No body-characteristic link (hence no direction) is evidenced for **c4, c9, c10, c11**.

# T2.9 · Origin and Source

## T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally — is it generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally?

**Finding.** Where `origin` is recorded it is **always "received-from-outside"** — fear arrives **onto** the person; none of the four options "generated-within / bestowed-by-God / generational" is recorded.

- **c2** received-from-outside 9, **c1** 8, **c3** 2.
- **c4, c5, c6, c7, c8, c9, c10, c11** — `origin` **silent** (the field carries no value).
- *Adjacent evidence:* c1 records God as **giver** (Deu 11:25) — a bestowal of fear *onto enemies*, which is a divine source for the fear *others* feel, not the experiencer's own origin. c11 records the **opposite** disposition as God-given (2Ti 1:7). These are divine-source signals carried in `divine_involvement`, not `origin`.

## T2.9.2 — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?

**Finding.** The `origin` field is **single-valued and uniform** (received-from-outside) wherever present — so the *recorded* origin is **singular**. But the cluster's wider evidence shows **multiple effective sources**: external threat (received-from-outside), **divine sending** (agent — God spreads dread/terror/dismay), and **God's instilling** (giver). The lexical `origin` field captures only the first; the `divine_involvement` field captures the others. Recorded as: origin-field singular, effective sources multiple (cross-referenced to T4.1).

## T2.9.3 — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts evidenced in Scripture?

**Finding.** Yes, by characteristic and context:

- **c2** — chiefly **received-from-outside** (threat perceived), but also **God-sent** in some contexts (agent 16).
- **c3/c4/c7** — predominantly **God-sent** (agent) when inflicted on enemies, vs **arriving from outside** when experienced by the person.
- **c1** — **instilled by God** (giver) in the conquest contexts (Deu 11:25), vs arising in reverent response elsewhere.
- **c9** — arises from **internal cares / external bad news** — but `origin` field silent; effective source is circumstantial.
- **c5, c6, c8, c10, c11** — `origin` silent; context-variation not recorded in the field. Noted.
