# T7.1 · Lexical and Semantic Analysis

## T7.1.1 — What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms for this characteristic — and what do their root meanings reveal?

**Finding (primary term + root, per characteristic):**

- **c1** — **H3372/H3373 *yare*** (root *yr'*: fear / reverence / awe) + H4172 *mo.rah*, H6345 *pach.dah* (awe); Greek G6015 *deos*, reverent G5401 *fobos*. The root **fuses fright and reverence** — the cluster's defining lexical fact.
- **c2** — **H3372 *yare*** (danger sense) + H3374 *yir.ah*; Greek **G5399 *phobeō* / G5401 *phobos*** (root: flight/fright). Bi-testamental.
- **c3** — ***pachad*** (H6343 noun / H6342 verb): dread / to be in dread.
- **c4** — **H0367 *emah*, H4032 *magor*, H2851 *chittit*** (terror); Greek G1630/G5398/G5400.
- **c5** — ***ragaz* (H7264), *charad* (H2729)**: to tremble/quake; Greek **G5156 *tromos***.
- **c6** — **H8175 *sa'ar* (to bristle/shudder), H2189 *za'avah* (horror)**: recoil/bristling.
- **c7** — **H0926 *bahal* (to dismay), H2865 *chatat* (to be shattered)**: collapse/breaking.
- **c8** — Aramaic **H0927 *behal*** (alarm), H8429 *tevah* (startle); Greek G4422 *ptoeō*.
- **c9** — **H1674 *de'agah* (anxiety), H8312 *sar'appim* (disquieting thoughts)**; Greek G0085 *ademoneo*.
- **c10** — **H8539 *tamah* (be astounded)**; Greek G2285 *thambos*, G1568 *ekthambeo*.
- **c11** — **G1167 *deilia* / G1169 *deilos*** (timidity/cowardice) — root: cowardly shrinking.

## T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term — and what does that range reveal about how the characteristic operates?

**Finding (morphology per characteristic):**

- **c1** — **verb (Qal 92, Niphal 31) + adjective (48) + noun (23)**: the range (active verb / "to-be-feared" Niphal / "reverent" adjective / "fear" noun) reveals reverent fear as **act, quality experienced, and standing disposition** at once.
- **c2** — **verb-dominant (Qal 188)**: ordinary fear is chiefly something one *does*.
- **c3** — **noun-dominant (52) + verb (Qal 22)**: dread is chiefly a named **state**.
- **c4** — **entirely nominal (47 + adj)**: terror is a pure **condition**.
- **c5** — **verb with strong causative (Qal 53, Hiphil 25, Hithpael 5)**: trembling is acted, undergone, and **caused**.
- **c6** — **nominal (12) + verb (3)**: a state, with the bristle-verb.
- **c7** — **Niphal passive dominant (37) + Piel/Hiphil causative (18/7)**: dismay is **undergone** (made-shattered) and **inflicted**.
- **c8** — Aramaic Pael/Hithpeel + nominal: abrupt act.
- **c9** — **nominal (17)**: a settled state.
- **c10** — **Qal (8) + Greek passives**: a stunned reaction undergone.
- **c11** — **nominal/adjectival**: a settled trait.

## T7.1.3 — What is the semantic range of the primary term — across what breadth of meaning does it operate?

**Finding.** The breadth is widest for the root-sharing terms and narrowest for the isolated ones:

- ***yare* (c1/c2)** — the broadest: **fear → be afraid → revere → stand in awe → worship** — spans the cluster's full moral range in one verb (lemma_meaning "to fear, reverence, terror"). This breadth is why valence/object must do the disambiguating.
- ***pachad* (c3, into c1)** — **dread → terror → religious awe** (reaches reverent awe in H6345).
- ***ragaz/charad* (c5)** — **tremble → quake → be agitated → be terrified** (physical + emotional).
- **Narrow-range terms:** *de'agah* (c9, anxiety only), *deilia* (c11, cowardice only), *bahal* (c7, dismay/haste) — single-sense.

## T7.1.4 — Does the vocabulary include terms that distinguish distinct aspects — disposition versus act, received versus given, condition versus quality?

**Finding.** **Yes — the vocabulary itself encodes the act/state/disposition distinctions** (type field + grammar):

- **Disposition vs act:** H3373 *yare* **adjective** ("reverent, god-fearing", a disposition) vs H3372 *yare* **verb** ("to fear", an act) — the same root distinguishes the standing disposition from the act.
- **Condition vs quality:** the **nominal terror/dread** vocabulary (c3/c4 — a condition) vs the **quality** weighting of reverent fear (c1, type quality 48).
- **Received vs given:** the **giver** divine-role (God *lays* fear, Deu 11:25) vs the **received-from-outside** origin (T2.9) — fear given by God vs arriving on the person.
- **Inflicted vs undergone:** c5/c7 **causative** stems (Hiphil/Piel — fear *made* to happen) vs **passive** (Niphal — fear *undergone*).

## T7.1.5 — Does the vocabulary include a term for the structural opposite or absence of this characteristic?

**Finding.** **Not within M01's own vocabulary.** The cluster has **no intra-cluster antonym lemma**; the opposite is expressed two ways:
- **By negation** — the "fear **not**" formula (the relational field's dominant token "not", T1.7), i.e. the *absence* commanded, not a positive antonym word.
- **By cross-cluster vocabulary** — courage/strength, peace, rest, security, trust (the foils mapped in T6/register X1, X4). These belong to **other clusters**, not M01.
- The one near-antonym *inside* the affective field is **c11 cowardice** as *defective* fear — but that is a disorder of fear, not its opposite.
Recorded: M01 carries no positive antonym term; antonyms are cross-cluster (→ Session D, register X1/X4).

## T7.1.6 — Does the vocabulary include a person-type term — a term for the one who habitually possesses or exercises this characteristic?

**Finding.** **Yes — two, at the moral poles:**

- **The God-fearer** — **H3373 *yare* (adjective): "fearing, reverent"** — names the person who habitually fears God: Exo 18:21 — *"men who fear God"*; 1Ki 18:3 — *"Obadiah feared the LORD greatly"*; Gen 42:18 — *"I fear God."* A standing person-type (the "God-fearing" man).
- **The coward** — **G1169 *deilos*: "cowardly"** — names the person characterised by culpable fear: Rev 21:8 — *"as for the cowardly… their portion…"*
- No person-type term for the middle (state) fears (dread/terror/trembling/dismay/alarm/anxiety/astonishment) — these name conditions, not character-types. Recorded.

## T7.1.7 — Does the vocabulary include a supplication or seeking term — a term for the act of seeking this characteristic from another?

**Finding.** **No — silent across the cluster.** Fear is not *sought* from another; there is no supplication-for-fear term. The nearest adjacency (not a fear-seeking term) is **crying out to God** under fear (a deliverance plea, e.g. the cry in affliction, → M38), which seeks *relief from* fear, not fear itself. Recorded as cluster-wide silence.

## T7.1.8 — What does the relationship between the OT Hebrew vocabulary and the NT Greek vocabulary reveal about continuity or development across the Testaments?

**Finding.** **Continuity in reverent fear; development at two points:**

- **Continuity:** the OT ***yare* / fear-of-the-LORD** is carried by NT **G5399/G5401 *phobeō/phobos*** — the reverent fear of God persists (e.g. Php 2:12 "with fear and trembling"; Rev 14:7 "Fear God").
- **Development 1 — christological redirection:** in the NT the fear/awe the OT reserves for Yahweh is **aroused by Christ** (calming the sea, the transfiguration — T0.4/c2) — a development of *object*, not vocabulary.
- **Development 2 — moralisation:** the NT adds **G1167/G1169 *deilia/deilos* (cowardice)** — fear named as a **moral fault** (2Ti 1:7; Rev 21:8), a register the OT vocabulary does not carry as a distinct lemma.
- **Language data:** OT 880 / NT 162; the cluster is OT-weighted, with the NT contributing the christological and moral developments.

## T7.1.9 — Is there a term newly coined in the NT period for this characteristic — and if so, what does that coinage reveal?

**Finding.** No term is demonstrably **coined** in the NT period from the lexical data, but the NT **distinctively deploys**:
- ***deilia/deilos* (cowardice, c11)** — fear as vice, set against the Spirit's gift (2Ti 1:7) — revealing the NT's **moral evaluation** of fear.
- ***ekthambeo* (utterly astonished, c10)** and ***ademoneo* (distressed, c9)** — intensive/interior NT terms.
These reveal the NT's interest in **fear's moral status and interior intensity** rather than a new coinage. Recorded as "distinctively NT, not newly-coined per the data" (a definitive coinage claim would need lexical-historical work beyond this extract — inferential).

## T7.1.10 — What does the full vocabulary arc reveal about this characteristic's complete semantic range?

**Finding.** The complete arc runs **from worship to vice**: **reverent awe (c1) → ordinary fright (c2) → anticipatory dread (c3) → overwhelming terror (c4) → bodily trembling (c5) → appalled horror (c6) → shattering dismay (c7) → sudden alarm (c8) → settled anxiety (c9) → stunned astonishment (c10) → culpable cowardice (c11)**. The range is structured on **direction (Godward vs threatward), intensity (fright → terror), kind (act → state → trait), constitutional reach (heart → spirit/body/cosmos), and moral status (commanded → neutral → sinful)**. The single most consequential lexical fact: **one root (*yare*) carries both the commended and the forbidden poles** — the cluster's unity and its central seam are the same fact.

# T7.2 · Verse and Literary Interpretation

## T7.2.1 — What is the function of this characteristic's primary term within its primary verse — what role does it play in the sentence and argument?

**Finding (syntactic/argumentative role, per characteristic anchor):**

- **c1** — **commanded predicate**: "you shall **fear** the LORD your God" (Lev 19:14) / "that you may **fear**…" (Deu 6:2, purpose clause) — the term carries the **imperative/purpose** of covenant obedience.
- **c2** — **negated imperative**: "**Fear not**, I am the one who helps you" (Isa 41:13) — the term is what is **forbidden**, grounded by God's presence.
- **c3** — **subject/complement of arrival**: "the thing I **dread** befalls me" (Job 3:25) — the dreaded thing as the arriving subject.
- **c4** — **subject that falls**: "**Terror** and dread fall upon them" (Exo 15:16) — terror as the agent-noun of God's act.
- **c5** — **jussive/imperative of worship**: "let the peoples **tremble**!" (Psa 99:1); "serve the LORD with fear, and rejoice with **trembling**" (Psa 2:11) — trembling as commanded response.
- **c6** — **predicate of overwhelm**: "horror **overwhelms** me" (Psa 55:5).
- **c7** — **negated/causative pair**: "Do not be **dismayed**… lest I **dismay** you" (Jer 1:17) — both undergone and inflicted in one clause.
- **c8** — **predicate of startle**: "his thoughts **alarmed** him" (Dan 5:6).
- **c9** — **subject weighing**: "**Anxiety** in a man's heart weighs him down" (Pro 12:25).
- **c10** — **imperative of wonder**: "wonder and be **astounded**" (Hab 1:5).
- **c11** — **predicate-noun of the gift's negation**: "a spirit **not of fear** but of power…" (2Ti 1:7).

- **c4** — **prophecy 22 + wisdom (Job) 14**: terror in **prophetic judgement** and **Joban affliction**.
- **c5** — **prophecy-dominant (47)** + narrative 34: trembling in **theophany/prophetic** settings (Sinai, day of the LORD).

**Interpretive requirement:** the cluster spans **every major genre**; responsible reading must not flatten a Joban lament-dread (c3), a covenant-command reverence (c1), and an epistolary vice (c11) into one "fear."

## T7.2.3 — What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence — what are the premises and conclusions?

**Finding (representative argument structures):**

- **Reverent fear → wisdom/obedience (c1):** premise "fear the LORD" → conclusion "keep his commandments / live" (Deu 6:2, purpose-result). The wisdom maxim "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom" runs premise(fear)→conclusion(wisdom).
- **"Fear not" + ground (c2):** command ("fear not") + **ground clause** ("**for** I am with you" / "I am the one who helps you", Isa 41:13) — the argument is *imperative grounded in God's presence*.
- **Contrast argument (c11):** "God gave us a spirit **not** of fear **but** of power and love and self-control" (2Ti 1:7) — antithesis structure (vice negated, virtue affirmed).
- **Worship paradox (c5):** "Serve the LORD with fear, **and** rejoice with trembling" (Psa 2:11) — fear and joy held together (a deliberate paradox).
- **Affliction lament (c3/c4):** "the thing I dread befalls me" (Job 3:25) — premise(dread) realised as conclusion(calamity arrives).

# T7.3 · Human Science Frameworks

> Source: `wa-m01-fear-scienceextract-v1_1-20260513.md`, the cluster's standing T7.3 reference. The science is an **interpretive lens alongside** the verse evidence; it does not confirm theological readings (those route to T7.2). The extract covers five science sections (threat detection; somatic arousal; anxiety; awe/reverential; constraint) plus an outside-science inventory.

## T7.3.1 — Which human science framework is most useful as an interpretive lens for this characteristic?

**Finding (primary fields, mapped to characteristics):**

- **Affective neuroscience** — the amygdala-centred model and the **fight-flight-freeze** response (LeDoux; Panksepp's FEAR system) — most useful for the **acute fears**: c2 (fear), c4 (terror), c8 (alarm/startle).
- **Psychophysiology / somatic research** (Cannon; James-Lange; Damasio; Porges' polyvagal) — most useful for the **somatic fears**: c5 (trembling), c6 (shuddering) — and the **freeze/collapse** register (c4 paralysis, c7 dismay-collapse).
- **Clinical psychology — anxiety research** (Beck; Borkovec; Barlow) — most useful for **c9 (anxiety)** and the anticipatory dimension of **c3 (dread)**.
- **Psychology of awe / psychology of religion** (Keltner & Haidt; Otto's numinous *tremendum/fascinans*) — most useful for **c1 (reverent fear/awe)** and **c10 (astonishment)**.
- **Stress/coercion research** (Lazarus & Folkman; Seligman's learned helplessness) — most useful for fear under **external constraint** (the affliction contexts, cf. T5.4).

## T7.3.2 — Where the human science framework illuminates the verse evidence, what does it reveal?

**Finding.**

- **Somatic density:** science (somatic-marker theory, psychophysiology) illuminates **why fear is so extensively embodied** in the cluster — the trembling/quivering/staggering vocabulary maps onto sympathetic-nervous-system and musculoskeletal expression. The extract flags the cluster's somatic density as *unusually high* — corroborating the programme's T2.6/T2.7 finding that fear is a body-crossing characteristic (c5).
- **Conditional structure:** the science "emergence" analyses illuminate that **fear is a response to a configuration** (threat + appraisal + perceived coping), not a fixed state — coherent with the cluster's object/cause structure (T4).
- **Awe's downstream effects:** awe research (self-diminishment, openness, prosocial orientation) illuminates how **reverent fear (c1)** produces obedience/worship — the "self before the vast" matches the creature-before-Creator posture (T0.3/T4.2).
- **Social/developmental:** the external-influences material illuminates the **communal and generational** fear contexts Scripture addresses (fear of the oppressed; fear transmitted in community).

## T7.3.3 — Where the verse evidence and the human science framework diverge, what does the divergence reveal?

**Finding (the two divergences the extract names — load-bearing):**

- **Normativity.** Science treats fear as **adaptive/maladaptive** — functional, value-neutral. Scripture treats certain fears as **categorically right** (reverent fear of God — the beginning of wisdom) and others as **categorically destructive** (fear that drives out trust/love; cowardice). **Science has no category for "commanded fear that is simultaneously a virtue and a rightly-ordered response."** This divergence is exactly the cluster's central seam (T1.3/T0.2 — valence) and confirms that the **moral/relational framing is theological, not scientific**. Reductive error is likely if the cluster's theology of fear is mapped straight onto clinical anxiety-management.
- **Vertical dimension.** The science account of what shapes fear is **horizontal/immanent** (heredity, parenting, circumstance). The scriptural account adds a **vertical** dimension: **God** as the one who both **instils appropriate fear** (giver, Deu 11:25) and **addresses inescapable threat** (the cry to God; "fear not, I am with you"). The divergence reveals the programme's distinctive: the *object and normativity* of fear, not its phenomenology, is where Scripture exceeds science.

## T7.3.4 — Does the human science framework surface any aspect of this characteristic that the verse evidence has not yet addressed — and does that absence require further verse investigation?

**Finding (gaps surfaced by science — flagged for verse investigation):**

- **Freeze/dissociative register (Porges' polyvagal theory).** Science distinguishes the **freeze/shutdown** response from aroused trembling. The cluster's terror-paralysis (c4, "still as a stone") and dismay-collapse (c7) may carry a **dissociative/shutdown** register distinct from the arousal register (c5 trembling). **Worth testing in the verse evidence** whether any terms carry a shutdown rather than arousal sense — a genuine open question for T7.2/Session B.
- **Intolerance of uncertainty (Dugas).** May illuminate how the **anxiety/perplexity** terms (c9; the "at a loss / perplexed" register) map onto future-uncertainty — worth checking against the c9 verse evidence.
- **Hereditary/developmental fear-proneness (Kagan's behavioural inhibition; ACEs).** Science identifies temperament and early environment as shapers of fear-proneness. The verse evidence has **no direct parallel** (the lexicon does not address temperament) — noted as a science-only dimension, **not** requiring verse investigation (it falls outside the lexicon's scope), but worth recording as a boundary of the biblical witness.
