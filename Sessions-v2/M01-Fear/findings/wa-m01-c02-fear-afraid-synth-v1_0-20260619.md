# M01 c2 — Fear / being afraid: synthesis across all tiers

- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on ordinary fear, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.

## 1. Identity
The plain affect of being afraid — the cluster's **core and largest member** — signalling episodic apprehension of danger (T1.1.1). Terms: H3372G *ya.re* (danger sense), H3374 *yir.ah*, H3025/H3016 *ya.gor*, H7297 *ra.hah*, Aramaic H1763 *de.chal*, and Greek **G5399 *fobeō*** — the Greek pair makes c2 genuinely **bi-testamental** (T1.1.2). It is an **act** (episodic affect — 76% action, 289 of records; T1.2.1), predominantly **Qal active** ("something one does"), with no "to-be-feared" group (contrast c1) (T1.4.1). It is a **perceptual-affective compound** — couples affect with perception (43 affect+perception blends) (T1.2.2). The same *yare* lemma it shares with c1 operates as ordinary or reverent **by object and valence** (T1.4.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
God is the **object** (71) and his self-revelation arouses fear (theophany, divine power — Exo 20:20 context) (T0.1.1); never its experiencer (T0.1.2). The silence marks it as a creaturely response (T0.1.3); exclusively a creaturely analogue (T0.3.2). It instantiates the image **only in its God-directed minority**; otherwise it expresses the creature under threat (T0.3.1). Presence is **mixed**: God-directed fear is right; forbidden fear ("do not be afraid") is a failure to trust — the "fear not" formula treats its presence as something to be removed (T0.3.3). Typology: **christological — the strongest candidate in the cluster.** NT occurrences arouse fear at **Jesus' divine acts** — calming the sea (Mar 4:41), the transfiguration (Mat 17:7), the cross/earthquake (Mat 27:54). The fear the OT reserves for Yahweh is, in the NT, aroused by Christ; direction is **fulfilment** (T0.4.1–0.4.2). Eschatological: Rev 14:7; Rev 18:10.

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Predominantly **forbidden 106 / neutral 128**; its fruit is dismay and flight; purpose-as-design is weak, its commendable form the God-directed minority overlapping c1 (T0.2.1). Reads as **fallen-condition response (dominant)**, but **held as mixed** — some neutral fear is ordinary creaturely self-preservation, so a flat "fallen only" reading would overreach (T0.2.2; the cluster's T0 reading most needing T7.2 corroboration). Immediate response: **dismay, trembling, panic, flight/avoidance** — disturbance and withdrawal — consistent in direction, varied in form (T1.5.1–1.5.2). Thin/near-silent on sustained effect (episodic) (T1.6.1).

## 4. Constitution
**Heart** is the leading seat (23 — the most of any characteristic; "their heart melted") (T2.3.1); soul 4, flesh 3 (T2.2; T2.6.1). **Silent on spirit and mind** (T2.1; T2.4). Origin **received-from-outside** (9 — fear "comes/falls upon" via perceived threat) and also **God-sent** in some contexts (agent 16) (T2.9.1/2.9.3). Movement is **onto the person from outside** (T2.10.1).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, affect-dominant (T3.4). Strong **cognitive co-operation** (*ya.da* "to know" 7, *da.at* 6, *bi.nah* 3) yet can **bypass** deliberate cognition in the fast immediate alarm — mixed, not cleanly resolved (T3.2). **Perception is the faculty that most distinguishes ordinary fear from reverent**: it **presupposes** perception — the affective response *to* what is seen/heard (Mat 14:26) (T3.1). The **"fear not" command addresses the will** (fear is to be refused) — an indirect volitional engagement (T3.6); it can **impair agency** (flight/avoidance) or, when resisted, leave it intact (T3.7). Moral evaluation partial — the "forbidden" framing evaluates it as a failure to trust (T3.8). Fear of others can **impair relational connection**, restored by reassurance (T3.11).

## 6. Relational interfaces
God-object 63 (the reverent overlap) — fear before God in theophany/exposure (T4.2.1). The **clearest reception interface in the cluster**: experiencer **addressed 131** — fear is overwhelmingly operated through **person-to-person address**, most often the reassurance "do not be afraid of them"; the giving is of *relief* from fear (Gen 15:1, "Fear not, Abram"; T1.4.3/T4.4.1). Persons make others afraid (object_type person 65) (T4.3.1). Operates **across distance** (enemies, kings, crowds) and crosses covenant boundaries (1Sa 4:7) (T4.5.1–4.5.2). Spiritual-beings: **forbidden** fear of other gods (2Ki 17:35) and dread of "a god" (1Sa 4:7) (T4.6.1). God's disposition toward his own is **reassuring/liberating** ("Fear not, I am with you," Isa 41:13) (T4.1.3).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on a transformation arc (T5.1). Present in **Job/Lam** (14 refs — fear at "all his suffering," "seeing Job's calamity") — a product/context of affliction (T5.4). Silent on sanctification (T5.5). **Strongest eschatological orientation** of the cluster — apocalyptic refs in Daniel, Joel, Revelation (17), oriented toward the **hour of judgement** (Rev 14:7); present Godward fear anticipates the universal reverence at the reckoning (T5.6).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **courage / strength / trust** ("be strong and do not fear" — *am.mits*, *cha.zaq*, *ko.ach*) (T1.3.1); commanded *away* (forbidden 106) (T1.3.2). Nearest neighbour **c1** (same root; distinguished by valence/object) and contrasted with **c9** (anxiety: settled/cognitive/interior vs c2 episodic/perceptual) (T6.5.1). c2 **produces trembling (c5)** and **dismay/flight (c7)** — the "fear not, neither be dismayed" pairing treats dismay as fear's collapse-product (T6.3.1). Cross-cluster partners: **shame / courage** (T6.1.1; held → Session D). Co-occurs heavily within the terror-complex.

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **narrative-dominant (122)** + prophecy 72 + gospel 60 — "fear not" scenes need attention to speaker/situation (T7.2.2). Logical structure: the **grounded imperative** — command ("fear not") + ground clause ("for I am with you," Isa 41:13) (T7.2.3). Anchor **Isa 41:13** uniquely ties the "fear not" to **God's hand-holding presence** as antidote (T7.2.6). Science lens: **affective neuroscience** (amygdala, fight/flight/freeze) (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Inferential:* cognition-bypass reading; agency-impairment; relational-impairment. *Held as mixed:* design-vs-fall status. *Silences:* spirit, mind, sustained effect, transformation, sanctification.

## 11. Essence
Ordinary fear is the cluster's **core, episodic, perceptually-triggered** member — apprehension of danger, mostly forbidden where it should yield to trust, yet not wholly fallen (creaturely self-preservation residue). It is the chief site of the gospel's recurring word, "Fear not… I am with you," and in the NT it is the fear Christ's divine acts arouse.
