# M01 c6 — Shuddering / horror: synthesis across all tiers

- **File:** `wa-m01-c06-shuddering-synth-v1_0-20260619.md` · **Date:** 2026-06-19 · **Prefix:** WA · **Version:** v1_0
- **Characteristic:** c6 — shuddering / horror (fear named as recoil/bristling). **Primary anchor:** Psa 55:5 — "Fear and trembling come upon me, and horror overwhelms me."
- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on horror, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.
- **Grounding source:** `WA-m01-findings-NEW-merged-bytier-v1-20260619.md`. **Discipline:** no-new-claims; tier refs and verse anchors retained; inferential/silence labels preserved. **Note:** c6 is a **thin** characteristic — several tiers are silent, recorded honestly as such.

## 1. Identity
Fear named as **recoil / bristling** — appalled revulsion (T1.1.1). Terms: H6427 *pal.la.tsut* "shuddering", H2189 *za.a.vah* "horror", H8178A / H8175A *sa.ar* "to shudder/bristle", H2113 *ze.va.ah* — OT Hebrew (T1.1.2). A **state** (recoil — status 12 / action 3) (T1.2.1), largely **nominal** (T1.4.1); name is **constitutional (flesh — bristling)** (T1.1.3); appears **simple** (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Agent** (6) only — the character imaged is God as the one whose acts appal (**thin**) (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2); a creaturely analogue (T0.3.2). Expresses **creaturely finitude/vulnerability** — the image under threat (T0.3.1); presence reveals the creature under threat (neutral) (T0.3.3). Typology: **silent** — no NT, no eschat/covenant/christological markers (T0.4.3).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Neutral 14 — recoil at the appalling, **no equipping** (T0.2.1); **fallen-condition response** (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **being seized / overwhelmed** — Psa 55:5, "horror overwhelms me"; Job 18:20, "horror seizes them" — consistent (recoil) (T1.5.1–1.5.2). As a status state, its sustained form is the **continuance of the condition** (T1.6.1).

## 4. Constitution
Heart 1; soul 2; **flesh** (the **bristling flesh** — H8175A "to shudder/bristle with horror") (T2.3.1; T2.2; T2.6.1). Body-link is **expressive** — the flesh bristles, visceral recoil; direction **inner horror → bristling flesh** (soul-to-body) (T2.6.2/T2.7.1). **Silent on spirit and mind** (T2.1; T2.4). Falls upon / seizes the person (downward-onto) (T2.10.1).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, single-faculty (T3.4). Lighter cognitive/memory co-terms (T3.2/T3.3). **Silent** across volition, agency, moral evaluation, perception-as-faculty, creativity, conscience (T3.6–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
**Silent / thin** on a person actively extending or receiving it (T4.3.4/T4.4.4). **Silent** on Godward operation (no Godward-reverent object) (T4.2.4) and on the spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.4). Relational-boundary evidence thin/silent (T4.5.4).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on transformation/sequence/mechanism/sanctification (T5.1/5.2/5.3/5.5). Present in Job in lesser measure (2 refs) (T5.4). **Silent** on eschatological trajectory (T5.6.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **composure** — not lexically marked; set against composure in the verse (Isa 21:4, "My heart staggers; horror has appalled me") (T1.3.1); boundary-by-contrast rather than active exclusion (T1.3.2). Distinguished from **c5 (trembling)** by quality (recoil/bristling vs shaking) and from **c7 (dismay)** by direction (recoil-from vs collapse-of) (T6.5.1). Within the terror-complex it sits **beside trembling and dismay** (Isa 21:4, horror "turned… into trembling") and is **produced by fear+trembling** as the **culminating overwhelm** (Psa 55:5) (T6.1.1/T6.2.1/T6.3.2); constitutively includes **recoil + trembling** (T6.3.3). Terror-glossed breadth spans c3/c4/c6 (T6.4.2).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **prophecy 12** — horror in prophetic oracle (T7.2.2). Anchor **Psa 55:5** uniquely stacks the **fear → trembling → horror** sequence in one verse (T7.2.6). Science lens: **psychophysiology / somatic research** (the recoil/bristling as somatic expression), alongside c5 (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Silences (extensive):* spirit, mind, typology, Godward operation, spiritual-beings, eschatology, sequence/mechanism, and all moral/volitional faculties. c6 is a thin, somatic, neutral-valence member — to be handled with restraint in prose.

## 11. Essence
Horror is fear as **appalled recoil** — the bristling flesh, the overwhelm that follows fear and trembling (Psa 55:5). It is a thin, neutral, somatic member of the terror-complex, set against composure, with little to say about God, the image, or the long arc.
