# T4.1 · Divine Interface — God to Human

## T4.1.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?

**Finding.** Yes, in **two modes** — God as **agent** (sends/causes fear) and God as **giver** (instils, or instils its opposite):

- **Agent (God sends fear):** present in nearly every characteristic — c7 (20), c5 (17), c2 (16), c3 (14), c1 (13), c4 (8), c6 (6), c8 (2), c9 (1), c10 (1). God **spreads/sends** dread, terror, trembling, dismay on his enemies: Exo 15:16 — *"Terror and dread fall upon them; because of the greatness of your arm"*; Psa 99:1 — *"let the peoples tremble!"*
- **Giver (God instils):** c1 (2 — Deu 11:25, *"The LORD your God will lay the fear of you and the dread of you on all the lands"*), c2 (3), c3 (3), c5 (1), c8 (1).
- **Giver of the opposite:** c11 (1) — 2Ti 1:7, the disposition God gives is **not** fear but power/love/self-control.
- **c9 (Anxiety)** — agent only 1; the **least** God→human of any fear.

## T4.1.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?

**Finding.** The basis differs by mode:

- **Inflicted fear (agent)** is **responsive/judicial** — God sends terror/dread/dismay **against opposition** (on enemies, the nations): Jer 1:17 — *"Do not be dismayed by them, lest I dismay you before them"* (dismay as consequence). Basis = God's act in judgement.
- **Instilled fear (giver, c1 Deu 11:25)** is **covenantal and grounded in God's act, not a condition met** — God lays the dread of Israel on the lands as part of the conquest promise (C1 T4.1: "basis is God's act, not a condition met").
- **Withheld/replaced fear (c11, c2 reassurance)** is **unconditional grace toward his own** — Rom 8:15; 2Ti 1:7; the "fear not, I am with you" assurance (Isa 41:13). Basis = God's presence and adoption.

## T4.1.3 — What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?

**Finding.** A **two-faced disposition keyed to relationship**:

- **Toward enemies/opposition:** God's disposition is **judicial might** — he is fearsome, spreading terror/dread/dismay (c3/c4/c5/c7 agent-dominant). Exo 15:16; the "dread of the LORD."
- **Toward his own/covenant people:** God's disposition is **reassuring and liberating** — he **removes** fear ("fear not, I am with you," Isa 41:13) and **gives its opposite** (the Spirit of adoption, Rom 8:15; power/love/self-control, 2Ti 1:7). He does **not** will his children to live in fear.
- This split (inflict vs liberate) is itself a major T4 finding about God's disposition.

## T4.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No characteristic is wholly silent on God→human operation (every one carries at least an agent or giver record), but it is **near-silent in c9 (anxiety — agent 1 only)** and **c10 (astonishment — agent 1)**. Recorded: c9/c10 carry the faintest God→human interface.

# T4.2 · Divine Interface — Human to God

## T4.2.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?

**Finding.** Yes — **God is the single largest object of fear** (object_type=God 203 cluster-wide), and the Godward movement is **worship, reverence, and covenant obedience**:

- **c1 (Reverent fear)** — the dominant witness: object=God 98; the human **fears/reveres God** as worship and obedience (Lev 19:14 — *"you shall fear your God: I am the LORD"*).
- **c2 (Fear/afraid)** — object=God 63 (the reverent overlap, ~28 righteous/commanded): fear before God in theophany/exposure.
- **c5 (Trembling)** — object=God 13; **commanded worship-trembling**: Psa 2:11 — *"Serve the LORD with fear, and rejoice with trembling"*; Php 2:12 — *"work out your own salvation with fear and trembling."*
- **c3** God 17, **c7** God 6, **c4** God 2, **c9** God 1, **c10** God 1 — minority Godward reverence in each.
- **c6, c8, c11** — **silent / no Godward object** (c11's relation to God is the negative "little faith" rebuke, not a Godward fear).

## T4.2.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?

**Finding.** The inner posture is **acknowledgement, reverence, and humble submission**:

- **c1** — acknowledgement and reverence; the cause-fields include God's attributes/acts and his **forgiveness**: Psa 130:4 — *"with you there is forgiveness, that you may be feared."*
- **c5** — **contrite, humble** submission: Isa 66:2 — *"he who is humble and contrite in spirit and trembles at my word."*
- **c2** — exposure/guilt before divine power resolving into reverence (the righteous/commanded minority).
- Others (c3/c4/c7/c9/c10) — minority reverent posture; thin. Recorded.

## T4.2.3 — What does the human-to-God direction of this characteristic reveal about the person's relationship with God?

**Finding.** It reveals the relationship as **creature-before-Creator** — the right posture of the image-bearer (cf. T0.3). Godward fear is the cluster's **commendable** form: where directed at God with reverence (c1), or as commanded worship-trembling (c5), it expresses **right relation and glad obedience**; Psa 2:11 holds fear and rejoicing together. The relationship imaged is one of **reverent dependence**, not cringing dread (the cringing forms are the misdirected/forbidden ones). Recorded.

## T4.2.4 — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** Human→God operation is **silent in c6 (shuddering), c8 (alarm), c11 (cowardice)** — none has a Godward-reverent object (c11's only God-relation is the negative rebuke). Faint in c9/c10 (God object 1 each).

# T4.3 · Human Interface — Giving

## T4.3.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another — and if so, how does it operate in that giving?

**Finding.** Yes, in two forms — **inspiring/striking fear in another**, and **extending reverence to another**:

- **Inspiring terror (c4):** a powerful person **makes others afraid**: Pro 20:2 — *"The terror of a king is like the growling of a lion."* The terror vocabulary includes "strike terror / inspire terror" senses.
- **Extending reverence (c1):** reverence **commanded toward parents**: Lev 19:3 — *"Every one of you shall revere his mother and his father."* Here the "giving" is the rendering of due reverence.
- **c2** — persons make others afraid (enemies, kings, crowds — object_type person 65).
- **c3 (dread of persons 11), c7 (person 14), c5 (person 9)** — persons as the dread/dismay-inspiring party.
- **c6, c8, c9, c10, c11** — **silent / thin** on a person actively extending the characteristic.

## T4.3.2 — What inner conditions or orientations in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?

**Finding.** The enabling condition in the giver is **power/authority** (to inspire fear) or **due standing** (to receive reverence):

- **c4/c2** — the giver of fear holds **threatening power** (a king, an army, an enemy) — Pro 20:2.
- **c1** — the recipient of reverence holds **due standing** (parents; God) — reverence is owed to those in rightful authority.
- Other characteristics — **silent** on giver-conditions. *(Note: the catalogue's framing assumes a positive characteristic being "given"; for fear, "giving" is chiefly inspiring fear in another, which is morally neutral-to-negative — recorded as such, inferential where the moral reading is added.)*

## T4.3.3 — What does the evidence reveal about what the person must have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?

**Finding.** **Largely silent / not-applicable for a fear cluster.** Fear is not a virtue one matures into before extending; "giving" fear is a function of **position/power** (a king inspires terror) rather than of inner formation. The one formation-adjacent case is **reverence**: a person must **acknowledge rightful authority** before rendering due reverence (c1). All other characteristics — **silent**. Recorded as N-A with the c1 exception.

## T4.3.4 — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** The giving direction is **silent in c6, c8, c9, c10, c11**, and only **functional (power-based), not formative**, in c2/c3/c4/c5/c7. Genuine "giving" in the catalogue's intended (virtue-extension) sense applies only loosely to **c1 (reverence rendered)**.

# T4.4 · Human Interface — Receiving

## T4.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another — and if so, how does it operate in that reception?

**Finding.** Yes — chiefly through the **"fear not" address** (the *removal* of fear extended from one to another) and the **arrival of inflicted fear**:

- **c2 (Fear/afraid)** — the clearest interface: experiencer **addressed 131** — fear is overwhelmingly **operated through person-to-person address**, most often the **reassurance** "do not be afraid of them" (the giving is of *relief* from fear).
- **Receiving inflicted fear:** persons **receive** terror/dread/dismay that another inspires — Gen 45:3 — *"his brothers could not answer him, for they were dismayed at his presence"* (dismay received from Joseph's self-disclosure).
- **c1** — reverence **received** by parents/those in authority (Lev 19:3, from the other side).
- **c3 (addressed 13), c7 (addressed 14), c4 (addressed 6)** — fear received from a threatening party.
- **c6, c9, c10, c11** — **thin/silent** on a reception interface.

## T4.4.2 — What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another person?

**Finding.** Reception of fear is **enabled by perceived threat/power** and **blocked by trust/courage**:

- The **"fear not" address** works to **block** reception — its aim is that the hearer **not** receive the fear ("do not be afraid of them"). The blocking condition is **trust** (often grounded in "I am with you").
- Reception is **enabled** where the other is perceived as overwhelming (a king, Joseph's authority — Gen 45:3).
- **c1** — reception of reverence is enabled by acknowledgement of due authority.
- Other characteristics — **silent**. Recorded.

## T4.4.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic from another but does not receive it?

**Finding.** The one who **does not receive** the fear another would inspire stands in **courage / trust / composure**:

- **c2** — the one who heeds "fear not" stands in **trust** (Isa 41:13 — *"Fear not… I am the one who helps you"*).
- **c11** — by contrast, the one who *should* stand in faith but does not is **cowardly / of little faith** (Mat 8:26).
- Other characteristics — **silent / not-applicable** (the inflicted-state members are received passively, not refused). Recorded.

## T4.4.4 — If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** The receiving direction is **silent in c6, c9, c10**, and **negative-only in c11**. It is richly evidenced only in **c2** (the "fear not" address) and present in c1/c3/c4/c7.

# T4.5 · Human Interface — Boundaries

## T4.5.1 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance or difference?

**Finding.** Yes — fear operates **both within bonds and across distance**, and the difference is morally significant:

- **Within bonds:** **reverence within the family/covenant** (c1 — parents, Lev 19:3; the covenant people's fear of God). Operates as **right ordering** of close relationships.
- **Across distance:** **fear of enemies, foreign kings, hostile crowds** (c2 person 65; c3 person 11; c4 — Pro 20:2; c7 — Gen 45:3 before the powerful). Operates as **apprehension across hostility/difference**.
- **c1** explicitly operates **across both** — covenantal (fear of God) and non-covenantal (apprehension of Esau, Gen 32:11 — *"I fear him, that he may come and attack me"*).

## T4.5.2 — Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?

**Finding.** It **crosses covenantal boundaries**:

- **c1** — reverent fear is covenantal (fear of the covenant LORD) **and** crosses into ordinary apprehension (Esau).
- **c2/c3/c4/c5/c7** — fear of non-covenant parties (Philistines, foreign kings, enemies) is freely evidenced (1Sa 4:7; Pro 20:2) — fear is **not confined to covenant relationships**.
- **God's instilling** of fear reaches **the nations** (Deu 11:25) — explicitly across the covenant boundary.
- Recorded: the cluster is **not covenant-bounded**; only the *commended* (reverent) form is covenant-centred.

## T4.5.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic — who is included and who is not?

**Finding.** The relational scope is **wide**: objects of fear span **God (203), persons (141), situations (119), abstractions (81), spiritual beings (11), things (10), threats (4)**. Included: the covenant God, family, enemies, kings, crowds, nations, spiritual beings, and impersonal situations/threats. **Not** included as an *object*: fellow covenant-members in good standing are not characteristically feared (fear of persons is chiefly of the threatening or the authoritative). Recorded with the distribution.

## T4.5.4 — If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** Relational-boundary evidence is **thin/silent in c6, c8, c9, c10, c11** (small, mostly abstract-object characteristics). It is rich in c1/c2/c3/c4/c5/c7.

# T4.6 · Spiritual Beings Interface

## T4.6.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial — and if so, how?

**Finding.** Yes — chiefly as **forbidden fear of other gods**, plus **startle at a perceived spirit** and **the spirit of bondage**:

- **c1** — **forbidden/sinful** fear of other gods: Judg 6:10 — *"you shall not fear the gods of the Amorites"*; 2Ki 17:7 (Israel's sin).
- **c2** — **forbidden** fear of other gods: 2Ki 17:35 — *"You shall not fear other gods or bow yourselves to them"* (also 17:37–38); and neutral dread of "a god" (1Sa 4:7, the Philistines).
- **c8** — startle at a perceived spirit: Luk 24:37 — *"they were startled and frightened and thought they saw a spirit."*
- **c11** — the disposition God did **not** give, set against the Spirit (2Ti 1:7); Rom 8:15 names "the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear."
- **c7** — 2 spiritual-being records (incl. Psa 104:29, creatures dismayed at God's hidden face).
- **c3, c4, c5, c6, c9, c10** — **silent** on a spiritual-being interface.

## T4.6.2 — Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity — something that can be attacked, distorted, or weaponised by adversarial spiritual powers?

**Finding (inferential where it goes beyond the field).** The evidence gives one direct and one strong inferential signal:

- **Rom 8:15 (c1/c2 register)** — **"the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear"** names fear as a condition of **bondage** that the Spirit of adoption replaces — the clearest evidence that fear can be a site of spiritual bondage. (Direct.)
- **Idolatrous fear (c1/c2)** — fear **misdirected to other gods** is a distortion the verses forbid (Judg 6:10; 2Ki 17:35) — fear redirected away from God toward spiritual rivals. (Direct, as a forbidden distortion.)
- **c11** — cowardice as the disposition opposed to the Spirit's gift (2Ti 1:7) — inferentially a vulnerability the adversary exploits.
- Other characteristics — **silent** on adversarial weaponisation. Recorded; claims beyond the verse labelled inferential.

## T4.6.3 — Is this characteristic communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry in the evidence?

**Finding.** **Almost entirely silent.** No characteristic shows fear *mediated through angelic ministry* as a faculty-relation. The one adjacency: in the "fear not" scenes an **angel** is sometimes the speaker who **removes** fear (e.g. Gen 21:17, the angel of God to Hagar — in c2's reassurance set) — angelic ministry **relieves** fear rather than communicating it. Recorded as silence on positive angelic mediation, with the fear-relieving adjacency noted.

## T4.6.4 — If the evidence is silent on the spiritual beings interface, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** The spiritual-beings interface is **silent in c3, c4, c5, c6, c9, c10**. It is evidenced (chiefly as forbidden idolatrous fear, startle, or bondage) in **c1, c2, c7, c8, c11**.
