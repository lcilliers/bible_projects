# M01 c1 — Reverent fear / awe: synthesis across all tiers

- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on reverent fear, drawing c1's thread across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.

## 1. Identity
The name fuses **fear + reverence + awe** — fear in a reverent register, a God-directed honouring disposition, not mere fright (T1.1.1). It is the **richest and most positive member** of the cluster. Primary terms: the *yare* root itself (H3372H *ya.re* "to fear: revere", H3373; H4172A/B *mo.rah*; H6345 *pach.dah* "dread/awe"; Greek G6015 *deos*, reverent uses of G5401 *fobos*) — the root carries fear-and-reverence as one range (T1.1.2). It is a **disposition** held as a standing orientation (action 123 / status 66 / quality 48; T1.2.1), operating in three modes: Qal active ("you fear"), Niphal *nora'* ("to-be-feared/awesome" — God as the quality feared), and the commanded imperative (T1.4.1). It is **composite**: fuses affect with cognition and memory ("to know", "to remember" co-terms) — not reducible to a single label (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
God is the **object** of c1 in 128 records, and the quality feared is his own awesomeness; the cause-fields name his attributes, acts, and his **forgiveness** (Psa 130:4) (T0.1.1). God is never its experiencer (T0.1.2). c1 carries the cluster's **most meaningful silence** about God: reverent fear images "not what God is but how the creature rightly stands before God" — a creaturely orientation toward the Creator (T0.1.3). It is the **only characteristic that positively instantiates an image-bearing excellence** — the creature's right acknowledgement of the Creator (T0.3.1) — and it is an exclusively creaturely analogue, not shared with God (T0.3.2). Its **absence or misdirection is the disorder** (not fearing God, sinful 14; fearing other gods, forbidden 5 — Judg 6:10; 2Ki 17:7); its presence marks a rightly-ordered image (T0.3.3). Typology: **covenantal** (reverent fear sits inside the covenant frame); eschatological thin (Joe 2:11); **christological absent in c1's own OT-anchored data** (T0.4.1).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
c1 **equips the person for right relation and glad obedience to God** — valence righteous 110 + commanded 52 (the refined reverent set stronger still). Its enacted fruit is obedience and worship (Lev 19:3, reverence of parents/Sabbath; Deu 6:2, keeping commandments) (T0.2.1). It reads as **created design** — a proper orientation to be cultivated (T0.2.2). Sustained, it produces a **lifelong standing orientation** — a settled God-fearing character ("all the days of your life," Deu 6:2) (T1.6.1–1.6.2). Its immediate response is uniformly **obedience and worship**, highly consistent (T1.5.1–1.5.2); the immediate acts mature into a settled character (T1.6.3).

## 4. Constitution
Located chiefly in the **heart** (13 — the dominant seat), with soul 5 and flesh 3 (T2.3.1; T2.6.1). It is **silent on spirit-level and on mind-location** (T2.1; T2.4 — mind is 0 across the whole cluster). Origin, where recorded, is **received-from-outside** (8), with God as **giver** in the conquest context (Deu 11:25, the fear of Israel laid on the nations) carried under `divine_involvement` (T2.9.1). No internal constitutional movement is evidenced (T2.10.3).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, affect-dominant with a knowing blend (T3.4.1). It **operates with sound cognition** — *ya.da* "to know" 6, *cha.kham* "wise" 3 — felt as affect but operating with knowing (neither impairs nor bypasses; *inferential*) (T3.2). It is the cluster's **recollective** member, operating alongside memory (*za.khar* "to remember" 4 — recalling God's acts/commands; *inferential*) (T3.3). It engages **volition** faintly (*ba.char* "to choose"; the obedience fruit implies a volitional outworking; *inferential*) and **enables agency** toward obedience (T3.6/T3.7). It is the **ground of right moral orientation** — the commanded/righteous fear from which obedience flows, deepening moral seriousness (T3.8). It carries the cluster's lone **conscience** trace (*suneidesis* 1 — too thin to build on) and comes closest to **conscientiousness** (moral awareness + volition + action co-occur; *inferential*) (T3.9/T3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
**God is the dominant object** (object=God 98) — the human reveres God as worship and obedience (Lev 19:14, "you shall fear your God: I am the LORD") (T4.2.1). The required posture is **acknowledgement, reverence, humble submission**, grounded in God's attributes and his forgiveness (Psa 130:4) (T4.2.2); the relationship imaged is **creature-before-Creator**, reverent dependence (T4.2.3). God **instils** it covenantally (Deu 11:25), on the basis of his act, not a condition met (T4.1.2). At the human interface, c1 is the **rendering of due reverence** to rightful authority (parents — Lev 19:3) (T4.3.1/T4.3.3). It operates **within bonds** (family/covenant) and **across distance** (apprehension of Esau, Gen 32:11) (T4.5.1). Spiritual-beings interface: its **forbidden** form is fear of other gods (Judg 6:10) (T4.6.1).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
The cluster's one genuinely **orientation-forming / transformative** member — it forms a standing Godward orientation over a lifetime (*inferential*; T5.1.1). The only characteristic participating in the **sanctifying arc** — a lifelong orientation forming a God-fearing character that issues in obedience (*inferential*; T5.5.1–5.5.2). Present in Job in modest measure (6 refs) (T5.4). Eschatological markers thin — Joe 2:11; Rev 11/18 (T5.6.1).

## 8. Within the cluster
Its structural opposite is not "no emotion" but **fear misdirected** (other gods) or **withheld where owed** (T1.3.1). Nearest neighbour is **c2 (ordinary fear)** — same *yare* root — distinguished by **valence + object**: reverent = Godward, commanded/righteous; ordinary = threat-facing, forbidden/neutral. The seam is **moral, not lexical** (T6.5.1; T1.3.3). It also borders **c10 (astonishment)**: awe honours, astonishment is stunned (T6.5.1). The *pachad* root reaches from dread (c3) into c1's reverent awe (H6345), showing threat-fear and God-fear are lexically continuous (T6.4.2). Cross-cluster, c1's leading partners are **wisdom (M15) and righteousness (M26)** — "the fear of the LORD is the beginning…" (T6.1.1; held → Session D). Co-occurrence is broad.

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: the **broadest** of the cluster — narrative, psalm, prophecy, wisdom, gospel, epistle (T7.2.2). Logical structure: premise "fear the LORD" → conclusion "keep his commandments / live" (Deu 6:2); the wisdom maxim fear→wisdom (T7.2.3). Anchor **Psa 130:4** uniquely grounds reverent fear in **forgiveness/grace** — fear *because of* mercy, not terror (T7.2.6). Science lens: **psychology of awe / religion** (Otto's numinous; self-diminishment, prosocial orientation) illuminates how reverent fear produces obedience/worship — "self before the vast" matching creature-before-Creator (T7.3.1–7.3.2).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Inferential:* operates-with-cognition; recollective (memory); volitional outworking; conscientiousness; sanctifying/formative role. *Silences:* spirit-location, mind-location, creativity, transformation-as-event, christological typology in c1's own data. Present these as the findings labelled them (GR-PROG-009).

## 11. Essence
Reverent fear is the cluster's **created, image-bearing, formative** member: the rightly-ordered, Godward register of fear — grounded in God's holiness *and* his forgiveness — that knows, remembers, obeys, and forms a God-fearing life. Where it is absent or misdirected, the image is disordered.
