# M01 c8 — Alarm / sudden fright: synthesis across all tiers

- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on alarm, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.
- **Grounding source:** `WA-m01-findings-NEW-merged-bytier-v1-20260619.md`. **Discipline:** no-new-claims; tier refs and verse anchors retained; inferential/silence labels preserved. **Note:** small characteristic (action 14 / quality 5 / status 1); several tiers silent.

## 1. Identity
Fear named by its **abruptness** — a startle, sudden onset (T1.1.1). Terms: Aramaic **H0927 *be.hal*** (Daniel), H8429 *te.vah* "be startled" + Greek G4422/G4426 *ptoeō/pturomai* "to frighten", G1719 *emfobos* (T1.1.2). An **abrupt act** (T1.2.1), Aramaic Pael/Hithpeel + nominal — abrupt onset (T1.4.1). Name is **constitutional (limbs/colour)** and **temporal (sudden)** (T1.1.3). Appears **simple** (T1.2.2). Operates differently **by trigger** — dreams/visions (Daniel) vs sudden events (T1.4.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Agent** (2) / **giver** (1) — God-sent alarming visions (Daniel) — imaging God as the **source of revelatory dread** (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2); a creaturely analogue (T0.3.2). Expresses **creaturely finitude** under sudden encounter (T0.3.1); presence reveals the startled creature (neutral) (T0.3.3). Typology: **christological candidate** — startle at sudden divine/uncanny manifestation (NT startle vocabulary) (T0.4.1).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Neutral 18 — **startle, not a design** (T0.2.1); **fallen-condition response** (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **physical startle** — limbs giving way, colour changing, faces bowed (Dan 5:6 context) — consistent (somatic startle) (T1.5.1–1.5.2). Thin/near-silent on sustained effect (episodic) (T1.6.1).

## 4. Constitution
**Heart-silent** — its seat is the **body** (limbs/colour/knees, expressed in verse_text, not the location field) (T2.3.3; T2.6.1). Body-link is **indicative/expressive** — the body **registers** the sudden alarm (Dan 5:6) (T2.6.2); direction **inner startle → body** (T2.7.1). It surfaces **the thoughts** as the disturbed seat (Dan 7:28, "my thoughts greatly alarmed me") — the cognitive trigger more than a constitutional subset (T2.5.1). **Silent on spirit, soul, and mind-location** (T2.1; T2.2; T2.4).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective** (affect on all but one record, a field gap) (T3.4). Its "thoughts" are **trigger-content, not discernment** — minimal cognitive co-operation (T3.2). **Impairs agency** — limbs give way (Dan 5:6) (T3.7). **Silent** on volition, moral evaluation, perception-as-faculty, creativity, conscience (T3.6/T3.8–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
**Silent** on a Godward-reverent object (T4.2.4). Startle **at a perceived spirit**: Luk 24:37, "they were startled and frightened and thought they saw a spirit" — its spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.1). The **"do not be alarmed" address** (calming) is its speech-mode (forbidden 2) (T1.4.3). Thin on giving/receiving interfaces (T4.3.4/T4.4.4); thin/silent on relational boundaries (T4.5.4).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on transformation/sequence/mechanism/sanctification (T5.1/5.2/5.3/5.5). **No Job/Lam presence** — silent on the suffering relationship (its context is the Daniel court) (T5.4.3). Present in **apocalyptic narrative** (Daniel's visions) but as **alarm at the vision**, not a trajectory toward fullness — apocalyptic-context, not eschatological-trajectory (T5.6.1).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **self-control / calm** (*enkrateia*; the "do not be alarmed" address) (T1.3.1). Distinguished from **c7 (dismay)** by onset (abrupt startle vs shattering; they share H0927) and from **c10 (astonishment)** by tone (fright vs stunned wonder; they share H8429) (T6.5.1). It is **vocabulary-bridged** to its neighbours via these two shared lemmas (T6.4.1) but shows **no productive/constitutive relation** to another characteristic (T6.3.4) and **no consistent sequence** (T6.2.3). Co-occurrence thin.

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **prophecy 12 (Daniel) + gospel 6** — alarm in apocalyptic court-narrative (Daniel), requiring attention to the revelatory-vision form (T7.2.2). Anchor **Dan 5:6** uniquely renders the **somatic startle** in clinical detail (T7.2.6). Science lens: **affective neuroscience** (the startle/alarm response) (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Silences (extensive):* spirit, soul, heart-location, mind; suffering; sequence/mechanism; most moral/volitional faculties. A small, Daniel-centred, somatic-startle member.

## 11. Essence
Alarm is fear as **sudden somatic startle** — the king's colour changing and knees knocking (Dan 5:6) — most often triggered by a dream, vision, or uncanny appearance. It registers in the body, not the located heart, and its calming word is "do not be alarmed."
