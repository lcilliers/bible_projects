- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on astonishment, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.
- **Grounding source:** `WA-m01-findings-NEW-merged-bytier-v1-20260619.md`. **Discipline:** no-new-claims; tier refs and verse anchors retained; inferential/silence labels preserved. **Note:** small characteristic (action 13 / status 1 / quality 1); several tiers silent or small-sample.

## 1. Identity
Fear named as **stunned arrest** — being dumbfounded; **borders awe** (T1.1.1). Terms: H8539 *ta.mah* "to be astounded" + Greek G2285 *thambos*, G1568 *ekthambeo*, G1569 *ekthambos*, Aramaic H8429 (T1.1.2). An **act/event** (a stunned reaction undergone — Qal + Greek passives) (T1.2.1/T1.4.1). **Relatively neutral as to direction** — the trigger varies (T1.1.3). Appears **simple** (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Agent** (1) — God's astonishing **work** as trigger: Hab 1:5 ("a work that you would not believe") — imaging God's **wonder-working** (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2); a creaturely analogue (T0.3.2). Expresses the creature **arrested before the overwhelming** — bordering the reverent acknowledgement of c1 but **stunned rather than honouring** (T0.3.1); valence-neutral (T0.3.3). Typology: **theophanic/christological** — astonishment at God's "unbelievable work" (Hab 1:5) and NT crowds astounded at Jesus' acts (T0.4.1).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Neutral 13 — the stunned arrest before God's work; **borders wonder but not framed as equipping** (T0.2.1); **fallen-condition response** in the typology (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **being stunned / running** — panic-flight in the overwhelming case (Psa 48:5); small sample (IR n=2), consistent within the few cases but too narrow for a strong claim (T1.5.1/1.5.2).

## 4. Constitution
Heart 1; status 1 (T2.3.1). **Silent on spirit, soul, mind, and body-part link** (sparse) (T2.1; T2.2; T2.4; T2.6.3). **Silent** on constitutional movement (T2.10.3).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective** (affect on all but one record, a field gap) (T3.4). Minimal/no cognitive co-operation (T3.2). **Silent** across volition, agency, moral evaluation, perception-as-faculty, creativity, conscience (T3.5–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
God object 1 — minority Godward reverence (T4.2.1). The **divine work** establishes the pattern; the human astonishment is the answering creaturely response (T0.4.2). **Thin/silent** on giving and receiving interfaces (T4.3.4/T4.4.4), relational boundaries (T4.5.4), and the spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.4).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on transformation/sequence/mechanism/sanctification (T5.1/5.2/5.3/5.5). A single Job datum (T5.4). Present in prophetic and Gospel-crowd settings; **silent** on eschatological trajectory (T5.6.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **composed comprehension** — the stunned arrest sits **between honour and collapse** (T1.3.1). Distinguished from **c1 (awe)** by orientation (awe **honours**; astonishment is **stunned**, not honouring — c10 ends where honouring begins) and from **c8 (alarm)** by tone (stunned wonder vs fright; they share H8429) (T6.5.1; T1.3.3). Among the **thinnest** in co-occurrence; **no** productive/constitutive relation, **no** consistent sequence (T6.2.3/T6.3.4).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: prophecy 5 + gospel 6 — astonishment in prophetic and Gospel-crowd settings (T7.2.2). Anchor **Hab 1:5** uniquely ties astonishment to **God's unbelievable work** (T7.2.6). Science lens: **psychology of awe / religion** (Keltner & Haidt; Otto's *tremendum/fascinans*), alongside c1 (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Small-sample caveat:* immediate-response and several interfaces rest on very few records. *Silences:* spirit, soul, mind, body-link; sequence/mechanism; sanctification; eschatology; most faculties. A thin member sitting on the awe/wonder border.

## 11. Essence
Astonishment is fear as **stunned arrest** — the creature dumbfounded before God's unbelievable work (Hab 1:5). It borders awe but stops short of honouring: stunned rather than reverent, sitting between honour and collapse.
