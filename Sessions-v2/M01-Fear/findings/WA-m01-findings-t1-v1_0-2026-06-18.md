# T1.1 · Name and Naming

## T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?

**Finding.** The eleven names are deliberately differentiated points on a single affective field — *fear* — graded by register, intensity, grammatical kind, and moral direction. The naming itself signals the cluster's central seam: the same affect is named **reverent** when Godward and rightly-ordered, and named **fear/terror/dread** when threat-facing.

- **c1 — Reverent fear / awe (chiefly toward God):** the name fuses *fear + reverence + awe* — fear in a reverent register; signals a God-directed honouring disposition, not mere fright.
- **c2 — Fear / being afraid (apprehension of danger):** the plain affect of being afraid; the cluster's core and largest member; signals episodic apprehension.
- **c3 — Dread:** anticipatory, forward-looking apprehension; signals a weight of something feared ahead, a condition one is *in*.
- **c4 — Terror:** the overwhelming, often inflicted pole; signals acute, soul-deep fear.
- **c5 — Trembling / quaking:** fear named by its **bodily** realisation; signals the body shaken — the visible crossing of the inner state.
- **c6 — Shuddering / horror:** fear named as **recoil/bristling**; signals appalled revulsion.
- **c7 — Dismay / being shattered:** fear named as **collapse of composure**; signals the breaking of inner strength.
- **c8 — Alarm / sudden fright:** fear named by its **abruptness**; signals a startle, sudden onset.
- **c9 — Anxiety / disquiet:** fear named as **settled worried care**; signals interior, gnawing unrest.
- **c10 — Astonishment / being awe-struck:** fear named as **stunned arrest**; signals being dumbfounded — borders awe.
- **c11 — Cowardice / timidity:** fear named as a **moral fault**; signals culpable, defective fear.

## T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?

**Finding.** Yes, and the implications differ by characteristic — itself an orientation:

- **Directional/relational (Godward):** c1 (reverent — toward God), and the Godward minority of c2.
- **Constitutional (bodily):** c5 (trembling — names the body), c6 (shuddering/bristling — flesh), c8 (startle — limbs/colour in the verses).
- **Constitutional (interior):** c9 (anxiety — heart/inner parts), c4 (terror — soul-deep), c7 (dismay — inner strength broken).
- **Moral:** c11 (cowardice — names a fault), c1 (reverent — names a duty).
- **Temporal:** c3 (dread — forward-looking), c8 (alarm — sudden onset).
- **Relatively neutral as to direction:** c10 (astonishment — the trigger varies). Recorded.

## T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?

**Finding.** Most are **simple** at first encounter (a single affect), but several show **constituent structure**:

- **c1** — composite: fuses fear + reverence + awe, and blends affect with cognition/memory ("to know", "to remember" co-terms) — not reducible to a single label.
- **c2** — couples affect with **perception** (43 affect+perception blends) — a perceptual-affective compound.
- **c9** — couples affect with **cognition** (worried thought) — anxiety "thinks".
- **c5** — couples an inner state with **bodily expression** (a constitutional compound; see T2).
- **c7** — couples collapse with **shame** (strong compound partner) — dismay + disgrace travel together.
- **c3, c4, c6, c8, c10, c11** — appear **simple** (purely affective, single-faculty) at first encounter. Recorded.

# T1.3 · Boundary

## T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — the inner-being reality that stands against or excludes it?

**Finding.** A striking result: **each characteristic has a different lexical opposite**, drawn from its compound co-terms — itself a finding about how the cluster is structured.

- **c1** — opposite is not "no emotion" but **fear misdirected** (other gods, forbidden 5) or **withheld where owed** (sinful 14).
- **c2** — **courage / strength / trust** (compound *am.mits* "strong" 5, *cha.zaq* "strengthen" 9, *ko.ach* "strength" 3): "be strong and do not fear".
- **c3** — **security / safety** (*be.tach* 2, *ma.oz* 1): Pro 1:33 — *"whoever listens to me will dwell secure and will be at ease, without dread of disaster."*
- **c4** — **peace** (*sha.lom* 2).
- **c5** — **rest / quiet / security** (*ra.ga* "to rest" 2, *na.chat* "quietness", *sha.a.nan* "secure" 2, *be.tach* 2, *nu.ach* "to rest").
- **c6** — **composure** — not lexically marked; set against composure in the verse: Isa 21:4 — *"My heart staggers; horror has appalled me."*
- **c7** — **rest / quiet** (*nu.ach*, *sha.an* "to rest" 2, *sha.qat* "to quiet" 2) **and courage** (the "fear not, be not dismayed" formula).
- **c8** — **self-control / calm** (*enkrateia* "self-control"; the "do not be alarmed" address, forbidden 2).
- **c9** — **comfort / consolation / gladness** (*tan.chum* "consolation", *sha.qat* "to quiet"): Pro 12:25 — *"Anxiety in a man's heart weighs him down, but a good word makes him glad."*
- **c10** — borders awe (honouring) and dismay (collapse); its opposite is **composed comprehension** — the stunned arrest sits between honour and collapse.
- **c11** — **power, love, self-discipline** and **peace** (*dunamis*, *sōfronismos*, *eirēnē*): 2Ti 1:7; Joh 14:27 — *"Peace I leave with you; my peace I give to you… Let not your hearts be troubled, neither let them be afraid."*

**Structural note:** the *state/expression* fears (c3/c4/c5) share a **settledness** foil (security/peace/rest); the *relational/volitional* fears (c1/c2/c11) have relational or volitional foils (right-direction / courage / Spirit-gift). A pattern to test at T6.

## T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?

**Finding.**

- **c1** excludes/resists **idolatrous fear** (fearing other gods) and **refusal to fear God**.
- **c2** resists **trust and courage** while present — and is itself commanded *away* (forbidden 106).
- **c11** explicitly excludes **faith** ("O you of little faith", Mat 8:26) and the Spirit-given power/love/self-discipline.
- **c3** excludes **security/ease** (Pro 1:33).
- **c4** excludes **peace**; **c5** excludes **rest**; **c7** excludes **composure**; **c9** excludes **gladness/comfort**.
- **c6, c8, c10** — no explicit object of resistance lexically marked beyond their foils; recorded as boundary-by-contrast rather than active exclusion.

## T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?

**Finding (the soft boundaries, mostly within the cluster — full resolution held for T6):**

- **c1 ≠ c2:** reverent fear ends, and ordinary fear begins, at **valence + object** (the same lemma *ya.re* serves both); the seam is moral, not lexical.
- **c1 ≠ c10:** awe (honouring) vs astonishment (stunned) — c10 ends where honouring begins.
- **c3 / c4 / c5:** dread (state, arrives, ↔security) / terror (nominal, overwhelming, soul-deep, ↔peace) / trembling (act + bodily expression, crosses constitutional levels, ↔rest) — distinct in grammatical form and constitutional reach though sharing the *pachad*/terror sense.
- **c6 vs c5 vs c7:** shuddering (recoil/bristling) vs trembling (shaking) vs dismay (collapse).
- **c8 vs c5/c9:** alarm (sudden onset, often dream/vision-triggered) vs trembling (panic H7374 shared) vs anxiety (bewilderment H8541 shared).
- **c9 ≠ c2:** anxiety (settled, cognitive, interior, near-no divine involvement) vs ordinary fear (episodic, perceptual).
- **c11 ≠ all:** cowardice is the only one that is a **trait** and chiefly **sinful** — fear become vice.

All cross-boundaries are noted; precise resolution is a **T6** task (held), recorded as such.

# T1.4 · Modes of Operation

## T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?

**Finding (from JSON morphology + type):** the grammatical form is the chief evidence of mode.

- **c1** — three modes: **Qal active** (92, "you fear"), **Niphal** *nora'* (31, "to-be-feared/awesome" — God as the quality feared), and **Qal imperative** (the commanded mode).
- **c2** — predominantly **Qal active** (188) — fear as something one *does*; minimal Niphal (11); **no "to-be-feared" group** (contrast c1).
- **c3** — largely **nominal** (52 nouns) — operates as a state named, not an act done.
- **c4** — **entirely nominal** (47 nouns + adjectival) — operates purely as a condition.
- **c5** — verbal with a strong **causative Hiphil** (25) alongside Qal (53) and Hithpael (5) — operates both as something done and something *made* to happen.
- **c6** — largely **nominal** (12) — a state.
- **c7** — **Niphal passive dominant** (37) — operates as something one *is made* (broken); also causative Piel/Hiphil (God shatters).
- **c8** — Aramaic Pael/Hithpeel + nominal — abrupt onset.
- **c9** — largely **nominal** (17) — a settled state.
- **c10** — Qal (8) + Greek passives — a stunned reaction undergone.
- **c11** — **nominal/adjectival** — a settled disposition.

## T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?

**Finding.** Yes — pre-eminently **c1, c2, and c5**:

- **c1/c2** — the **same lemma** (*ya.re*) operates as **reverent** (Godward, commanded) or **ordinary** (threat-facing, forbidden) depending on **object and valence** — direction changes the mode entirely.
- **c5** — operates across the full **constitutional range**: as deep as the spirit (Isa 66:2), through soul/heart, out to the body (Hab 3:16), and the cosmos (Sinai) — the mode varies by constitutional level.
- **c3/c4/c7** — operate differently by **direction**: divinely-inflicted (agent) on enemies vs experienced by the person.
- **c8** — operates differently by **trigger**: dreams/visions (Daniel) vs sudden events.
- **c6, c9, c10, c11** — no strong context-variation evidenced beyond valence; recorded.

## T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — and if so, how does it function?

**Finding.**

- **c2 — the clearest:** the **"fear not" address** is the characteristic's speech-based mode — it is most often *spoken against* to a hearer (experiencer "addressed" dominant). Gen 15:1 — *"Fear not, Abram, I am your shield."*
- **c1** — the **commanded mode** (imperatives: "fear the LORD") and address to parents (Lev 19:3) — speech that *enjoins* the fear.
- **c7** — the **"be not dismayed" address** (Jer 1:17 — *"Do not be dismayed by them, lest I dismay you before them."*).
- **c8** — the **"do not be alarmed" address** (calming).
- **c11** — addressed by rebuke ("Why are you afraid?", Mat 8:26) and by promise (Joh 14:27).
- **c3, c4, c5, c6, c9, c10** — **no characteristic speech-mode** evidenced (they are described/reported, not characteristically commanded). Recorded as silent.

# T1.6 · Sustained Effect

## T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?

**Finding.** Sustained effect is **carried richly only by c1**; for most others the lexicon is thin or the characteristic *is itself* the abiding state.

- **c1** — produces a **lifelong standing orientation**: `type`=quality (48) + durative intensity ("all your days", "forever") = a settled God-fearing character. Deu 6:2 — *"that you may fear the LORD your God, you and your son and your son's son… all the days of your life."*
- **c3, c4, c6, c9** — these *are* abiding states (status-typed); what they "produce over time" is the continuance of the condition (dread/terror/horror/anxiety persisting).
- **c5** — produces the **trembling heart** as a sustained affliction: Deu 28:65 — *"the LORD will give you there a trembling heart…"*
- **c11** — produces a settled **trait/character defect** (quality 3) — the vice persists.
- **c2, c7, c8, c10** — **thin/near-silent** on sustained effect (episodic, action-typed); recorded as silent (the fields do not carry a forming trait).

## T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?

**Finding.**

- **c1** establishes a **settled reverent character** — the God-fearing person; orientation of glad obedience.
- **c5** establishes the **trembling heart** (a state of chronic dread under judgement, Deu 28:65).
- **c9** establishes a **weighed-down, careworn** inner condition (until relieved by consolation, Psa 94:19 — *"When the cares of my heart are many, your consolations cheer my soul."*).
- **c11** establishes **timidity as character** — and, by contrast, the gift establishes power/love/self-discipline (2Ti 1:7).
- **c3/c4/c6** establish the **abiding apprehensive condition** itself.
- **c2, c7, c8, c10** — **silent** (no sustained quality established in the fields). Recorded.

## T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?

**Finding.**

- **c1** — **yes, clearly**: immediate response = discrete acts of obedience/worship; sustained effect = a settled God-fearing *character*. Act matures into orientation.
- **c5** — yes: immediate = the shaking; sustained = the trembling *heart* (a chronic state).
- **c9** — partial: immediate = heart weighed down; sustained = the careworn condition — same in kind, extended in time.
- **c3/c4/c6** — **no meaningful difference**: the immediate response and the sustained state coincide (the condition is both onset and continuance).
- **c2, c7, c8, c10, c11** — **silent / not distinguished** in the fields (c11's trait is sustained but its immediate response sample is n=1). Recorded.

# T1.7 · Conditions of Reception

> Note: "reception" fits this cluster awkwardly — fear is rarely *received as a gift*. The findings read T1.7 as **the inner conditions under which the characteristic takes hold, is refused, or is rightly/wrongly held.**

## T1.7.1 — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?

**Finding.**

- **c1** — genuine reception of reverent fear is **enabled by acknowledgement of God** and is partly **instilled by God** (giver 2; Deu 11:25 — *"The LORD your God will lay the fear of you and the dread of you on all the lands"*); its right reception issues in obedience.
- **c5** — the **contrite and humble spirit** is the condition for its right (reverent) form: Isa 66:2 — *"this is the one to whom I will look: he who is humble and contrite in spirit and trembles at my word."*
- **c2** — ordinary fear "takes hold" via **perception of threat** (the received-from-outside origin); it is not received as a gift.
- **c3/c4/c7** — these are **inflicted/arriving** conditions (befalling, not chosen) — "reception" is passive undergoing.
- **c9** — arises from **cares / bad news** (Pro 12:25 context).
- **c11** — its opposite is the **God-given** disposition (2Ti 1:7); cowardice "takes hold" through failure of faith.
- **c6, c8, c10** — onset is by **encounter with the appalling / sudden / overwhelming**; no inner enabling condition beyond exposure. Recorded.

## T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?

**Finding.**

- **c1** — reverent fear is **blocked** where withheld from God (sinful 14) and **distorted** where redirected to other gods (forbidden 5) — the lexical signal of non-reception.
- **c2** — its right (commanded-away) condition is to be **refused/resisted**: the "fear not" formula; the dominant `relational` token is the negation ("not"). Trust/courage blocks ordinary fear.
- **c11** — **faith** blocks cowardice ("Have you still no faith?", Mar 4:40); the Spirit of power/love/self-discipline prevents it.
- **c9** — **a good word / consolation** dissolves anxiety (Pro 12:25; Psa 94:19).
- **c3/c4/c5/c7** — their foils (**security/peace/rest/composure**) are the conditions that prevent or relieve them.
- **c6, c8, c10** — relief conditions thin/unmarked; recorded.

## T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?

**Finding.**

- **c1** — the one who does **not** receive reverent fear is in a **disordered** state: not fearing God, or fearing idols (Judg 6:10; 2Ki 17:7).
- **c2** — the one who **refuses** ordinary fear (receives the "fear not") stands in **trust/courage** — the commended state.
- **c11** — the one who does not overcome cowardice remains in **little faith / faithlessness** (Mat 8:26; Rev 21:8 lists "the cowardly" with "the faithless").
- **c9** — the one not consoled remains **weighed down**.
- **c5** — the one who does not tremble rightly (contrite) yet is made to tremble stands under **judgement** (Deu 28:65).
- **c3, c4, c6, c7, c8, c10** — these are undergone rather than received/refused; the "non-reception" frame does not cleanly apply — recorded as **not-applicable / silent** for these, with the inflicted-state reading noted.
