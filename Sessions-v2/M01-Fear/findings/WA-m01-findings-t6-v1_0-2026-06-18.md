# T6.1 · Co-occurrence

## T6.1.1 — Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?

**Finding (within M01 + cross-cluster data).**

**Within M01 — the fear characteristics co-occur heavily** (the cluster verses routinely stack several fear terms):
- **c5 (Trembling)** co-occurs most broadly — with fear (c2), dread (c3), terror (c4), horror (c6): Psa 55:5 — *"Fear and trembling come upon me, and horror overwhelms me"* (c2+c5+c6 in one verse); Job 4:14 — *"dread came upon me, and trembling"* (c3+c5).
- **c6 (Horror)** sits beside trembling (c5) and dismay (c7): Isa 21:4 — *"horror has appalled me… turned for me into trembling."*
- **c3/c4 (Dread/Terror)** co-occur (Deu 11:25 — *"the fear of you and the dread of you"*; the terror/dread word-pairs).
- **c7 (Dismay)** co-occurs with terror (shares H1205) and with fear ("fear not, neither be dismayed").

**Cross-cluster — most frequent partners** (co-term counts): **shame** (41, M07), **righteousness** (39, M26), **wisdom** (22, M15), **sin** (21, M10), **love** (17, M05), plus the **peace/rest** foil (46) and **trust** (25). Per characteristic the leading cross-cluster partner is: c7→**shame** (the dismay↔shame pairing, strongest in the cluster), c1→**wisdom**/righteousness, c2→**shame**/courage, c5→**rest**/righteousness.

## T6.1.2 — What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?

**Finding.** Two patterns:

- **Within M01:** fear is an **internally networked family** — the state/expression members (dread, terror, trembling, horror, dismay) habitually appear together, confirming they are **facets of one terror-complex** at the verse level even though lexically distinct (supports the "soft boundaries" finding of the derivation doc).
- **Cross-cluster:** fear sits at a **busy junction** of the inner-being landscape — bordered by **shame** (the disgrace that accompanies collapse), **its foils** (peace/rest/trust/courage), the **moral clusters** (righteousness, sin), the **cognitive cluster** (wisdom — "the fear of the LORD is the beginning…"), and the **affective neighbours** (joy, grief, anger). Its dense bordering with both moral and affective clusters marks fear as a **hub** characteristic.

## T6.1.3 — If no significant co-occurrence patterns emerge, note this explicitly.

**Finding.** Co-occurrence is **significant**, not absent — for every characteristic except the small, isolated ones: **c9 (Anxiety)** and **c11 (Cowardice)** show the **thinnest** co-occurrence (c9's partners are its consolation foils; c11's are its power/love/self-discipline contrast). Recorded: no characteristic is co-occurrence-silent, but c9/c11 are sparse.

# T6.2 · Sequential Relationships

## T6.2.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?

**Finding (within-M01 sequences, from verse_text).**

- **Fear/dread → trembling:** the most consistent sequence — the inner fear (c2/c3) **issues in** bodily trembling (c5): Job 4:14 — *"dread came upon me, and trembling"*; Psa 55:5 — *"Fear and trembling come upon me."* Trembling **follows** the inner fear (the inner state made outward — cf. T2.7).
- **Fear/trembling → horror:** Psa 55:5 continues — *"…and horror overwhelms me"* (c6 as the culminating overwhelm).
- **Horror ↔ trembling:** Isa 21:4 — horror "turned… into trembling."
- **Dread + terror:** word-paired (Deu 11:25), accompanying rather than sequential.
- Cross-cluster sequence (inferential): **dismay → shame** (c7 + M07) travel together — the shattering and the disgrace (held → Session D).

## T6.2.2 — What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?

**Finding.** Within M01 the relationship is **causal-expressive**: inner fear/dread **causes/expresses as** trembling (the body shaken *by* the fear), which can **intensify into** horror/overwhelm. This is not developmental (no maturation) but **constitutive-causal** — one fear-state produces another's bodily/intensified form. The dread→trembling→horror chain (Psa 55:5; Job 4:14) is the clearest case. Cross-cluster (dismay→shame) is **correlational** in the data, causal only inferentially. Recorded.

## T6.2.3 — If no sequential pattern is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No sequential pattern is evidenced for **c1 (reverent fear), c8 (alarm), c9 (anxiety), c10 (astonishment), c11 (cowardice)** — these do not consistently precede/follow another characteristic. Genuine sequence sits in the **c2/c3 → c5 → c6** terror-complex.

# T6.3 · Causal and Constitutive Relationships

## T6.3.1 — Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one, and by what mechanism?

**Finding (within M01).**

- **Fear/dread (c2/c3) produce trembling (c5)** — mechanism: the inner affect **realised in the body** (Job 4:14; "the fear came upon me" → bones shake). The most direct production in the cluster.
- **Fear (c2) produces dismay/flight (c7)** — the "fear not, neither be dismayed" pairing treats dismay as fear's collapse-product.
- **Terror (c4) produces paralysis** (an immediate-response, T1.5, not another characteristic).
- Cross-cluster (held → Session D, inferential): reverent fear (c1) **produces** wisdom/obedience (M15); fear **produces** shame (M07) in the dismay context.
- **c6, c8, c9, c10, c11** — no productive relation to another characteristic evidenced.

## T6.3.2 — Is this characteristic produced by another — and if so, which one?

**Finding.**

- **Trembling (c5) is produced by fear/dread (c2/c3)** — the converse of T6.3.1 (Job 4:14).
- **Horror (c6) is produced by fear+trembling** (Psa 55:5 — the overwhelm that follows).
- **Dismay (c7) is produced by fear** and (cross-cluster, inferential) accompanies **shame**.
- Cross-cluster (held): fear is **relieved/displaced by** peace/rest, trust, love, salvation — i.e. produced-against by its foils (the antonym clusters).
- **c1, c2, c3, c4, c8, c9, c10, c11** — not evidenced as produced-by another M01 characteristic (c2/c3 are sources, not products).

## T6.3.3 — Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another characteristic constitute part of this one?

**Finding.** Within M01 the **terror-complex** shows constitutive nesting:

- **Terror (c4) is the intensified pole of dread (c3)** — terror "is" overwhelming dread (the derivation doc treats c4 as the intensified end of the *pachad*/terror family); the two are **constitutively related** (degree).
- **Trembling (c5) is the bodily constituent** of the fear-experience — the somatic element that the inner fears (c2/c3/c4) include when realised in the body.
- **Horror (c6)** constitutively includes **recoil + trembling**.
- Cross-cluster (held → Session D): **shame** may be a constituent of **dismay** (c7) — the disgrace within the shattering — to be resolved with M07.
- **c1, c8, c9, c10, c11** — no constitutive nesting evidenced.

## T6.3.4 — If no causal or constitutive relationship is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** No causal/constitutive relationship is evidenced for **c1, c8, c9, c10, c11** (within M01). The causal/constitutive structure is concentrated in the **c2/c3/c4/c5/c6/c7 terror-complex**.

# T6.4 · Vocabulary and Root Sharing

## T6.4.1 — Does this characteristic share vocabulary terms with other characteristics in the programme?

**Finding.**

- **Within M01 — three shared lemmas** (the only cross-characteristic vocabulary overlap): **H0927 *be.hal*** (c7 dismay + c8 alarm), **H1205 *be.a.tah*** (c4 terror + c7 dismay), **H8429 *te.vah*** (c8 alarm + c10 astonishment). All sit at adjacent boundaries (dismay/alarm; terror/dismay; alarm/astonishment).
- **Cross-cluster** (held → Session D): one notable **homonym/vocabulary bridge** — **che.sed**, which appears as *"shame/reproach"* (→ M07) and as *"kindness/steadfast love"* (→ M05); fear co-occurs with both senses. Other cross-cluster co-terms are partner vocabulary, not shared lemmas (recorded in the register).

## T6.4.2 — Does vocabulary sharing extend to root-level architecture — a shared root that generates terms across two or more characteristics?

**Finding (within M01).** Yes — root families span characteristics:

- ***pachad* root** — generates terms in **c3 (dread:** H6343/H6342) and **c1 (reverent:** H6345 *pach.dah* "religious awe") — the same root yields both dread and reverent awe (a root bridging threat-fear and God-fear).
- ***chatat/chittah* terror root** — generates **c4 (terror:** H2851 *chit.tit*, H2866 *cha.tat*) and **c7 (dismay:** H2865 *cha.tat* "to be shattered") — a root bridging terror and dismay.
- **terror-sense breadth** — terror-glossed terms span c3/c4/c6 (the derivation doc lists H8606 "shuddering, horror" under terror).
- **c1's *yare* root** is shared with **c2** (H3372H reverent / H3372G danger) — the **same verb**, split by valence/object — the cluster's deepest root-level architecture point.

## T6.4.3 — What does vocabulary sharing reveal about the conceptual relationship between characteristics?

**Finding.** The root architecture reveals fear as **one semantic field differentiated by register**, not a set of unrelated affects:
- the *yare* root carrying both **reverent** and **ordinary** fear (c1/c2) shows the reverence-vs-fright distinction is **register/direction within one root**, not two vocabularies (the cluster's most consequential finding, T1.4.2);
- the *pachad* root reaching from **dread to reverent awe** (c3/c1) shows threat-fear and God-fear are **lexically continuous**;
- the *chatat* root bridging **terror and dismay** (c4/c7) shows the overwhelm and the collapse share an architecture.
The conceptual relationship is therefore **one rooted field with morally/relationally differentiated branches**.

## T6.4.4 — If no significant vocabulary sharing is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**Finding (explicit).** Vocabulary sharing is **significant** within M01 at the **root** level (yare, pachad, chatat) though **slight at the whole-lemma level** (only 3 shared lemmas). The small, isolated characteristics — **c9 (anxiety), c11 (cowardice)** — share **no** vocabulary with the others (distinct roots: *deagah*, *deilia*); recorded as vocabulary-isolated.

# T6.5 · Distinctions

## T6.5.1 — Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?

**Finding (the within-M01 distinction map — completion of the "→T6" boundary work):**

- **c1 (Reverent) ↔ c2 (Ordinary):** nearest neighbours (same *yare* root). **Distinguished by valence + object:** reverent = Godward, commanded/righteous; ordinary = threat-facing, forbidden/neutral. Direction, not vocabulary.
- **c1 (Awe) ↔ c10 (Astonishment):** both honour the overwhelming. **Distinguished by orientation:** awe **honours**; astonishment is **stunned** (dumbfounded), not honouring.
- **c3 (Dread) ↔ c4 (Terror):** both *pachad*/terror sense. **Distinguished by form + intensity:** dread = verb-capable **state that arrives**, opposite **security**; terror = **nominal**, overwhelming, **soul-deep**, opposite **peace**.
- **c4 (Terror) ↔ c5 (Trembling):** **Distinguished by kind:** terror = nominal **state**; trembling = **act/bodily expression** crossing constitutional levels, opposite **rest**.
- **c5 (Trembling) ↔ c6 (Horror):** **Distinguished by quality:** trembling = **shaking**; horror = **recoil/bristling** (revulsion).
- **c6 (Horror) ↔ c7 (Dismay):** **Distinguished by direction:** horror = **recoil** from; dismay = **collapse** of inner strength.
- **c7 (Dismay) ↔ c8 (Alarm):** share H0927. **Distinguished by onset:** dismay = **shattering** (Niphal passive); alarm = **abrupt startle** (often dream/vision-triggered).
- **c8 (Alarm) ↔ c10 (Astonishment):** share H8429. **Distinguished by tone:** alarm = **fright**; astonishment = **stunned wonder**.
- **c9 (Anxiety) ↔ c2:** **Distinguished by tempo + faculty:** anxiety = **settled, cognitive, interior** (least divine involvement); ordinary fear = **episodic, perceptual**.
- **c11 (Cowardice) ↔ all:** the only **trait/vice** — distinguished by being **morally culpable** fear.

## T6.5.2 — Where the evidence shows apparent overlap, what is the precise boundary?

**Finding.** The boundaries are drawn by **valence/object** (c1/c2), **grammatical form** (c3 verb-state vs c4 nominal), **constitutional reach** (c5 crosses levels), **quality of response** (c6 recoil vs c5 shake vs c7 collapse), and **onset** (c8 sudden vs c7 shattered). The three shared lemmas mark the precise overlap points: **H1205** (c4/c7 — terror that shatters), **H0927** (c7/c8 — dismaying alarm), **H8429** (c8/c10 — startled astonishment). These are the **seams** where one characteristic shades into the next.

## T6.5.3 — Is the distinction between this characteristic and its nearest neighbour one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?

**Finding.** All four kinds of distinction operate, mapped:

- **Direction:** c1↔c2 (Godward vs threat); c1↔c10 (honouring vs stunned); c6↔c7 (recoil vs collapse).
- **Degree (intensity):** c3↔c4 (dread vs overwhelming terror).
- **Kind:** c4↔c5 (state vs act/expression); c11 vs all (trait/vice vs affect).
- **Constitutional level:** c5 (crosses spirit→body→cosmos) vs the heart/soul-bound others; c9 (interior/cognitive) vs c2 (perceptual).

This four-way differentiation is itself a finding: the cluster is **not** organised on a single axis but on **direction × degree × kind × constitutional level**.

## T6.5.4 — If no significant distinction work is required, note this explicitly.

**Finding.** Distinction work **is** required and is substantial across the cluster; it is **least** required for **c11 (cowardice)**, which is sharply set apart from all others (the only trait/vice) and needs no fine boundary-drawing. Recorded.
