# T0

## T0.1.1 — In this verse, is the characteristic predicated of God or otherwise related to God; if so, in what relation (God as the one who bears it, acts, gives it, or is its object)? Record the relation, or record that it is not related to God here.

What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**Finding.** The fear cluster does not image an affect *in* God; it images God's **holiness, power, and covenant lordship as that before which the creature rightly stands**. What the cluster reveals about God is consistently **the object-side**: God is the One who is rightly feared, who acts, and who gives — not one who fears. The character of God surfaced is his awesome holiness (the Niphal *nora'*, "to-be-feared"), his saving power, and his forgiveness.

Per characteristic:

- **c1 (Reverent fear/awe).** The richest witness. God is the **object** of fear in 128 records and the quality feared is his own awesomeness. Cause-fields name God's attributes/acts and his **forgiveness**: Psa 130:4 — *"But with you there is forgiveness, that you may be feared."* The Niphal *nora'* register ("awesome") makes God himself the thing feared. → Images God's holiness and forgiving lordship.
- **c2 (Fear/afraid).** God as **object** (71) and his self-revelation arouses fear (theophany, divine power): Exo 20:20 context; the "fear of the LORD" causes. → Images God's power and presence.
- **c3 (Dread).** God's role is chiefly **agent** (14) — "the dread of the LORD fell"; the character imaged is God's **dread-inspiring might against his enemies**. Small reverent object set (7).
- **c4 (Terror).** Agent-dominant (8 of ~11 divine) — God **spreads terror**: Exo 15:16 — *"Terror and dread fall upon them; because of the greatness of your arm, they are still as a stone."* → Images God's overwhelming might in deliverance/judgement.
- **c5 (Trembling).** Agent (17) — God **makes** creation and peoples tremble; Psa 99:1 — *"The LORD reigns; let the peoples tremble!"* → Images God's reigning lordship.
- **c6 (Shuddering/horror).** Agent (6) only; the character imaged is God as the one whose acts appal. Thin.
- **c7 (Dismay).** Agent (20) — God **shatters/dismays** enemies. → Images God's power to break.
- **c8 (Alarm).** Agent (2)/giver (1) — God-sent alarming visions (Daniel). → Images God as the source of revelatory dread.
- **c9 (Anxiety).** Agent (1) only — anxiety images **almost nothing** of God's nature; it is near-wholly creaturely. (Significant near-silence.)
- **c10 (Astonishment).** Agent (1) — God's astonishing **work** as trigger: Hab 1:5 ("a work that you would not believe"). → Images God's wonder-working.
- **c11 (Cowardice).** Giver (1) — but of its **opposite**: 2Ti 1:7 — *"God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control."* God's character imaged by contrast: he is the giver of courage, not of cowardice.

## T0.1.2 — Across the characteristic's verses, is it ever borne by God himself or only by the creature, and what does that pattern of presence or absence indicate for its place in the human person and in the divine image?

Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?

**Finding.** **No.** In none of the eleven characteristics is fear attributed to God as his own experience (`divine_involvement` = *experiencer*: **0 of 975**). The attribution is uniformly object/agent/giver — God is feared, sends fear, or gives its opposite, but never feels it. The significance for the human person: fear is marked as a **creaturely** reality, not a divine perfection the person shares.

Per characteristic: **c1–c11 all report God-as-experiencer = 0.** Explicit confirmations are stated in each analysis (e.g. C1 T0.1.2 "not attributed"; C2 "never the experiencer"; C3/C4/C5/C7 "God never the experiencer"; C9 "and almost no divine involvement"; C11 "giver — of the opposite"). No characteristic is silent here — every one carries the zero.

## T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image? `[refit: question now folded]`

Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?

**Finding.** The silence is **uniform and significant**: because God is never the experiencer, the cluster locates fear on the **creaturely side of the image** — it images *how the creature rightly stands before God*, not what God is. The silence is strongest and most telling at the two poles:

- **c1** — the silence is most meaningful: reverent fear images "not what God is but how the creature rightly stands before God" (C1 T0.1.3) — a creaturely orientation toward the Creator.
- **c9 (Anxiety)** — the silence is doubled: not only is God not its experiencer, he is barely involved at all (agent 1). Anxiety sits furthest from the divine image of any fear — a purely creaturely disquiet.
- **c2, c3, c4, c5, c6, c7, c8, c10** — same creaturely placement; the silence marks each as a response/condition of the creature, not a divine attribute.
- **c11 (Cowardice)** — the silence carries a moral edge: not only does God not possess it, the disposition he *gives* is its opposite (2Ti 1:7), so the silence marks cowardice as a **disordered** creaturely state. (See T0.3.3.)

No characteristic is silent on the question itself; the silence *is* the finding, recorded for all eleven.

# T0.2 · Created Purpose

## T0.2.1 — Does this verse state any purpose, role, or effect the characteristic serves in the person — what it leads the person to be, do, or become? Record it if stated; otherwise record none.

What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created — what does it equip the person to be, do, or become?

**Finding.** Purpose **splits sharply by valence**, and the split is the cluster's most consequential T0 result. One characteristic is framed as equipping; the rest are framed largely as conditions endured or to be resisted.

- **c1 (Reverent fear).** Equips the person for **right relation and glad obedience to God**. JSON valence: righteous 110 + commanded 52 = 162 of 238 (the refined reverent set is more strongly commanded/righteous still — C1: 150 of 195). Its enacted fruit is obedience and worship (Lev 19:3 reverence of parents/Sabbath; Deu 6:2 keeping commandments). → equips for worship and obedience.
- **c2 (Fear/afraid).** Predominantly **forbidden 106 / neutral 128**; its fruit is dismay and flight. Purpose-as-design is weak; its commendable form is the God-directed minority that overlaps c1. → largely a condition to be addressed, with a creaturely self-preservation residue (held with care — see T0.2.2).
- **c3 (Dread).** Neutral 57-dominant; a state one is *in*, not an equipping. Small "dread of God" (righteous 9) is the commendable residue.
- **c4 (Terror).** Neutral 50 of 53; no equipping purpose evidenced — an overwhelming condition.
- **c5 (Trembling).** Neutral 77, with a **created-design signal** in the commanded/righteous forms (commanded 7 + righteous 13): the contrite spirit that trembles at God's word (Isa 66:2) and trembling commanded before the reigning Lord (Psa 99:1). → a bodily form that, rightly directed, equips reverent submission.
- **c6 (Shuddering).** Neutral 14; recoil at the appalling — no equipping.
- **c7 (Dismay).** Neutral 74; collapse of composure — not a design.
- **c8 (Alarm).** Neutral 18; startle — not a design.
- **c9 (Anxiety).** Neutral 20; its relief is comfort/consolation (Pro 12:25; Psa 94:19) — a condition to be relieved, not an equipping.
- **c10 (Astonishment).** Neutral 13; the stunned arrest before God's work — borders wonder but not framed as equipping.
- **c11 (Cowardice).** Sinful 4 / forbidden 1 — the **anti-purpose**: the failure of what the person is equipped to be; answered by the gift of "power and love and self-control" (2Ti 1:7).

## T0.2.2 — Across the evidence, does the characteristic's role read as belonging to created design, to the fallen condition, to both, or as not determinable?

Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**Finding.** The cluster spans the full range, and the **valence field is the discriminator**:

- **Created design:** **c1** (commanded/righteous dominant — a proper orientation to be cultivated), and the commanded/righteous strand of **c5** (trembling before the reigning Lord).
- **Fallen-condition response (dominant):** **c2** (forbidden-dominant), **c3, c4, c6, c7, c8, c10** (neutral states of threat/collapse), with **c2** explicitly *held as mixed* — some neutral fear is ordinary creaturely self-preservation, so a flat "fallen only" reading would overreach (C2 T0.2.2).
- **Disordered/sinful (fall expressed as vice):** **c11** (sinful-dominant cowardice).
- **Purely creaturely-internal, valence-neutral:** **c9** (anxiety) — neither cultivated design nor framed as sin; an inner disquiet of the finite creature.

**Both, therefore, across the cluster**: reverent fear belongs to created design; ordinary/overwhelming/collapse fears read as fallen-condition responses; cowardice as vice. *Flag (carried from C2):* the created-purpose status of ordinary fear (c2) is the one T0 reading most in need of verse-literary (T7.2) corroboration.

# T0.3 · Image-Bearer Expression

## T0.3.1 — From the characteristic's God-relation (T0.1) and its role (T0.2), what aspect of the divine likeness, if any, does it instantiate in the person? Record the aspect, or record none.

In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person — what aspect of being made in God's likeness does it instantiate?

**Finding.** Only **c1** positively instantiates an image-bearing excellence; the rest express the image **negatively or by creaturely limitation**.

- **c1** — instantiates the creature's **acknowledgement of the Creator** — the right posture of the image-bearer before God (C1 T0.3.1).
- **c5** — instantiates the image in its **contrite, submissive** form: the spirit that trembles at God's word (Isa 66:2) and before the reigning Lord (Psa 99:1).
- **c2** — instantiates the image only in its God-directed minority; otherwise expresses the creature under threat.
- **c3, c4, c6, c7, c8** — express **creaturely finitude/vulnerability** (the image under threat or collapse), not an excellence.
- **c9** — expresses the **limits of the finite, caring creature** (worried care).
- **c10** — expresses the creature **arrested before the overwhelming** — bordering the reverent acknowledgement of c1 but stunned rather than honouring.
- **c11** — expresses a **disordered** image (vice) — see T0.3.3.

## T0.3.2 — Across the evidence, is the characteristic shared between God and the person, or an exclusively creaturely analogue to something in God?

Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue to something in God?

**Finding.** **Exclusively a creaturely analogue** for every characteristic — because God is never the experiencer (T0.1.2), no form of fear is *shared* between God and the person. Stated for all eleven:

- **c1** — "an exclusively creaturely analogue (God does not fear); it is not shared" (C1 T0.3.2).
- **c2–c11** — same: each is a creaturely analogue; the divine side is only object/agent/giver. None is shared. No characteristic dissents; none is silent.

## T0.3.3 — Where the characteristic is present or absent in a person, what does that indicate about the condition of the divine image in them — or is no such indication evidenced?

What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?

**Finding.** Presence/absence reads **oppositely for c1 vs the rest**, which is itself a structural finding:

- **c1** — its **absence or misdirection is the disorder**: not fearing God (sinful 14) or fearing other gods (forbidden 5) marks a **disordered image** (C1 T0.3.3; Judg 6:10; 2Ki 17:7). Here the *presence* of reverent fear marks a rightly-ordered image.
- **c11** — its **presence is the disorder**: cowardice present = a disordered image, fear become vice and unbelief (sinful 4); answered by the gift of power/love/self-control (2Ti 1:7). Here *absence* (replaced by courage) marks the rightly-ordered image.
- **c2** — mixed: presence of God-directed fear is right; presence of forbidden fear ("do not be afraid") is a failure to trust — the "fear not" formula treats its presence as something to be removed.
- **c5** — presence in contrite/commanded form is right (Isa 66:2); presence as terror-stricken collapse is creaturely distress.
- **c3, c4, c6, c7, c8, c9, c10** — presence chiefly reveals the **creature under threat/limitation** rather than a moral verdict on the image; valence is mostly neutral, so these are diagnostic of condition more than of image-order. Recorded as such.

What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?

**Finding.** The eleven names are deliberately differentiated points on a single affective field — *fear* — graded by register, intensity, grammatical kind, and moral direction. The naming itself signals the cluster's central seam: the same affect is named **reverent** when Godward and rightly-ordered, and named **fear/terror/dread** when threat-facing.

- **c1 — Reverent fear / awe (chiefly toward God):** the name fuses *fear + reverence + awe* — fear in a reverent register; signals a God-directed honouring disposition, not mere fright.
- **c2 — Fear / being afraid (apprehension of danger):** the plain affect of being afraid; the cluster's core and largest member; signals episodic apprehension.
- **c3 — Dread:** anticipatory, forward-looking apprehension; signals a weight of something feared ahead, a condition one is *in*.
- **c4 — Terror:** the overwhelming, often inflicted pole; signals acute, soul-deep fear.
- **c5 — Trembling / quaking:** fear named by its **bodily** realisation; signals the body shaken — the visible crossing of the inner state.
- **c6 — Shuddering / horror:** fear named as **recoil/bristling**; signals appalled revulsion.
- **c7 — Dismay / being shattered:** fear named as **collapse of composure**; signals the breaking of inner strength.
- **c8 — Alarm / sudden fright:** fear named by its **abruptness**; signals a startle, sudden onset.

- **c11 — Cowardice / timidity:** fear named as a **moral fault**; signals culpable, defective fear.
