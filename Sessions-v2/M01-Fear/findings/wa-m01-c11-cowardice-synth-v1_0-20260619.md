# M01 c11 — Cowardice / timidity: synthesis across all tiers

- **File:** `wa-m01-c11-cowardice-synth-v1_0-20260619.md` · **Date:** 2026-06-19 · **Prefix:** WA · **Version:** v1_0
- **Characteristic:** c11 — cowardice / timidity (fear as a moral fault). **Primary anchor:** 2Ti 1:7 — "God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control."
- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on cowardice, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.
- **Grounding source:** `WA-m01-findings-NEW-merged-bytier-v1-20260619.md`. **Discipline:** no-new-claims; tier refs and verse anchors retained; inferential/silence labels preserved. **Note:** small, NT-only characteristic (quality 3 / action 1 / status 1); the cluster's only trait/vice.

## 1. Identity
Fear named as a **moral fault** — culpable, defective fear (T1.1.1). Terms: **all NT Greek** — G1169 *deilos* "timid/cowardly", G1168 *deiliaō*, G1167 *deilia* "timidity" (T1.1.2). The cluster's **only quality-dominant member** — a **trait** (T1.2.1), nominal/adjectival, a settled disposition (T1.4.1). Name carries a **moral** implication from the outset (names a fault) (T1.1.3). Appears **simple** (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Giver (1) — but of its *opposite*:** 2Ti 1:7, "God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control." God's character is imaged **by contrast** — he is the giver of courage, not of cowardice (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2). The silence here carries a **moral edge**: the disposition God *gives* is its opposite, so the silence marks cowardice as a **disordered** creaturely state (T0.1.3). It expresses a **disordered image** (vice) (T0.3.1). Presence/absence reads **oppositely to c1**: its **presence is the disorder** — fear become vice and unbelief (sinful 4); answered by the gift of power/love/self-control; **absence** (replaced by courage) marks the rightly-ordered image (T0.3.3). Typology: **christological/redemptive by contrast** — the timidity rebuked by Christ ("Why are you afraid?", Mat 8:26) answered by his peace (Joh 14:27) and the Spirit's gift (2Ti 1:7); direction = the divine gift establishes the pattern the cowardice fails to meet (T0.4.1–0.4.2).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
**Sinful 4 / forbidden 1** — the **anti-purpose**: the failure of what the person is equipped to be, answered by the gift of "power and love and self-control" (T0.2.1). **Disordered/sinful (fall expressed as vice)** (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **failure of nerve/faith** — Mat 8:26, "Why are you afraid, O you of little faith?" (small sample, n=1) (T1.5.1/1.5.3). Sustained, it establishes **timidity as character** — a settled trait/defect; by contrast the gift establishes power/love/self-discipline (T1.6.1–1.6.2).

## 4. Constitution
Heart 1 (sparse) (T2.3.1). **Silent on spirit, soul, mind, and body-part link** (T2.1; T2.2; T2.4; T2.6.3). *(Note: 2Ti 1:7's "spirit" is read in the findings as the Spirit's gift, not a spirit-level constitutional location for cowardice — hence c11 is recorded silent on spirit-location; the second spirit-level case in the cluster is c7, Ecc 7:9.)* **Silent** on origin and on constitutional movement (T2.9/T2.10.3).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, single-faculty (T3.4). The **failure of volition** to act in faith (rebuked as "little faith"); its remedy is the Spirit of **self-control** (a volitional capacity) (T3.6). **Morally engaged as the negative pole** — the sinful fear, named a fault and listed among the disqualified (Rev 21:8) (T3.8). Impairs faithful relational standing (failure before Christ) (T3.11). Silent on perception, memory, creativity, conscience as faculties (T3.1–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
**No Godward-reverent object** — its only God-relation is the **negative "little faith" rebuke** (T4.2.4). The one who does not overcome it remains in **little faith / faithlessness** (Mat 8:26; Rev 21:8 lists "the cowardly" with "the faithless") (T4.4.3). The disposition God did **not** give, set against the Spirit (2Ti 1:7); **Rom 8:15** names "the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear" — inferentially a vulnerability the adversary exploits (T4.6.1–4.6.2). **Thin/silent** on giving and receiving interfaces (T4.3.4/T4.4); thin on relational boundaries (T4.5.4).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
The **negative of formation** — answered by the Spirit-given power/love/self-control (2Ti 1:7), implying a sanctifying replacement (*inferential*; T5.5.1–5.5.2). **No Job/Lam presence** — silent on the suffering relationship (its context is NT moral exhortation) (T5.4.3). Its Rev 21:8 is a **judgement-verdict on the cowardly — a terminus, not a trajectory** (T5.6.1/5.6.3). Silent on transformation/sequence/mechanism in the fields (T5.1/5.2/5.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **power, love, self-discipline** and **peace** (*dunamis*, *sōfronismos*, *eirēnē*) — 2Ti 1:7; Joh 14:27, "Let not your hearts be troubled, neither let them be afraid" (T1.3.1). Explicitly **excludes faith** ("O you of little faith") and the Spirit-given power/love/self-discipline (T1.3.2). **c11 ≠ all** — the only one that is a **trait** and chiefly **sinful**; sharply set apart, needing no fine boundary-drawing (T1.3.3/T6.5.1/6.5.4). **Vocabulary-isolated** — distinct root (*deilia*), shares no lemma with the others; among the **thinnest** in co-occurrence (its partners are its power/love/self-discipline contrast); **no** productive/constitutive relation to another characteristic (T6.4.4/T6.1.3/6.3.4). Cross-cluster: the **fear-vs-faith/unbelief** node — cowardice as faithlessness is the key node (held → Session D; register X8).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **gospel 3 + epistle 1 + apocalyptic 1** — cowardice in NT moral exhortation, read against the Gospel rebukes and epistolary ethics (T7.2.2). Logical structure: the **contrast/antithesis** — "a spirit **not** of fear **but** of power and love and self-control" (2Ti 1:7) (T7.2.3). Anchor **2Ti 1:7** uniquely names fear's **divinely-given antidote** (T7.2.6). Science lens: bears on the **normativity divergence** (T7.3.3) — science has no category for fear-as-vice; cowardice is treated **categorically** as a moral fault, a theological, not clinical, framing.

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Inferential:* sanctifying-replacement reading; adversarial-vulnerability reading. *Silences:* spirit, soul, mind, body-link; suffering; sequence/mechanism; most faculties. *Boundary clarification carried:* 2Ti 1:7 "spirit" is the Spirit's gift, not a spirit-level location for cowardice (recorded divergence from the OLD findings, which read it as spirit-location).

## 11. Essence
Cowardice is the cluster's **single vice** — fear become moral fault and unbelief, the failure of nerve rebuked by Christ ("Why are you afraid, O you of little faith?") and listed among the disqualified (Rev 21:8). It is answered not by another fear but by a gift: "a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control."
