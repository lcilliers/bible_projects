# M01 c9 — Anxiety / disquiet: synthesis across all tiers

- **Purpose:** consolidated grounding for prose on anxiety, across all seven tiers. Foundation layer — not the essay.

## 1. Identity
Fear as **settled worried care** — interior, gnawing unrest (T1.1.1). Terms: H1674 *de.a.gah* "anxiety", H8312 *sar.ap.pim* "disquieting thoughts", H7661 *sha.vats* "agony" + Aramaic H3735 *ke.ra*, Greek G0085 *ademoneo* "be distressed" (T1.1.2). A **state** of inner unrest (status 16), largely **nominal** (T1.2.1/T1.4.1). Name is **constitutional (interior)** (T1.1.3). A **cognitive compound** — couples affect with cognition: anxiety **"thinks"** (worried thought) (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Agent** (1) only — anxiety images **almost nothing** of God's nature; it is **near-wholly creaturely** (a significant near-silence) (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2). The silence here is **doubled**: not only is God not its experiencer, he is barely involved at all — anxiety sits **furthest from the divine image of any fear**, a purely creaturely disquiet (T0.1.3). Expresses the **limits of the finite, caring creature** (worried care), not an excellence (T0.3.1); valence-neutral (T0.3.3). Typology: NT present (Php 2, Paul's anxiety for Epaphroditus) but **no typological candidate** — silent (T0.4.3).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Neutral 20 — its relief is **comfort/consolation** (Pro 12:25; Psa 94:19) — a condition to be relieved, not an equipping (T0.2.1). **Purely creaturely-internal, valence-neutral** — neither cultivated design nor framed as sin; the inner disquiet of the finite creature (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **the heart weighed down** — Pro 12:25, "Anxiety in a man's heart weighs him down" — consistent (weighing-down, relieved by a good word) (T1.5.1–1.5.2). Sustained, it establishes a **weighed-down, careworn** inner condition until relieved by consolation (Psa 94:19, "your consolations cheer my soul") (T1.6.1–1.6.2).

## 4. Constitution
Heart 3, soul 2, and — distinctively — the **inner parts / "entrails"** as a visceral soul-subset (the seat of churning care; heart → soul → gut) — the **most interiorly-located** fear (T2.5.1–2.5.2; T2.2). **Silent on spirit, mind, and body-part link** — it is interior (T2.1; T2.4; T2.6.3). Origin from **internal cares / external bad news** (field silent; effective source circumstantial) (T2.9.3). **Silent** on constitutional movement (T2.10.3).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, affect-dominant with a **cognition** blend (T3.4). **The cognitive fear** — affect+cognition (Psa 139:23, "Try me and know my thoughts!"; the "disquieting thoughts" sense): anxiety is the fear that **thinks** (worried rumination), engaging and arguably **impairing** cognition — the careworn heart weighed down (Pro 12:25) (*inferential*; T3.2). **Silent** on volition, agency, moral evaluation, perception-as-faculty, creativity, conscience (T3.6–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
God object 1 — the **least God→human** of any fear (agent only 1) (T4.1.4/T4.2.1). **Thin/silent** on giving and receiving interfaces (T4.3.4/T4.4.4) and on the spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.4); thin on relational boundaries (T4.5.4). Its relief comes through a **word/consolation** extended by another (Pro 12:25; Psa 94:19).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
A genuine **relief-sequence**: anxiety **gives way to** gladness/consolation — Pro 12:25, "a good word makes him glad"; Psa 94:19, "your consolations cheer my soul" — revealing anxiety as a state **awaiting comfort**; mechanism = **encounter/intervention** (the good word) (*inferential*; T5.2.1–5.2.2/T5.3.1). **No Job/Lam presence** — silent on the suffering relationship (its context is wisdom/epistle) (T5.4.3). Silent on sanctification and on eschatological trajectory (T5.5/T5.6.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **comfort / consolation / gladness** (*tan.chum*, *sha.qat*) (T1.3.1); excludes gladness/comfort (T1.3.2). Distinguished from **c2 (ordinary fear)** by **tempo + faculty**: anxiety = settled, cognitive, interior (least divine involvement); ordinary fear = episodic, perceptual (T6.5.1/T1.3.3). **Vocabulary-isolated** — shares no lemma with the others (distinct root *deagah*); among the **thinnest** in co-occurrence (its partners are its consolation foils) (T6.4.4/T6.1.3); **no** productive/constitutive relation to another characteristic (T6.3.4). Cross-cluster, it borders **joy** (relief) and its consolation foils (held → Session D).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: prophecy 9 + narrative 5 — anxiety across wisdom/prophecy/epistle (T7.2.2). Anchor **Pro 12:25** uniquely names anxiety's **relief by a word** (T7.2.6). Science lens: **clinical psychology — anxiety research** (Beck; Borkovec; Barlow) — the most apt lens; a flagged open question (Dugas' *intolerance of uncertainty*) on how the anxiety/perplexity register maps onto future-uncertainty, worth checking against the c9 verse evidence (T7.3.1/7.3.4).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Inferential:* cognition-impairment; relief-sequence/mechanism. *Open question:* intolerance-of-uncertainty mapping (→ T7.2). *Silences:* spirit, mind, body-link; suffering(Job); sanctification; eschatology; most faculties. Its near-total **divine silence** is itself the load-bearing finding.

## 11. Essence
Anxiety is the cluster's **interior, cognitive, near-wholly creaturely** fear — the careworn, ruminating heart "weighed down," reaching even the gut, and **furthest from the divine image** of any fear. Its turn is not a virtue grown but a relief received: "a good word makes him glad."
