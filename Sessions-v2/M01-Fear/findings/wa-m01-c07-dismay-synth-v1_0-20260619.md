## 1. Identity
Fear named as **collapse of composure** — the breaking of inner strength (T1.1.1). Terms: H0926 *ba.hal* "to dismay", H2865 *cha.tat* "to be shattered/dismayed", H0928 *be.ha.lah*, H1670 *de.a.vah*, + Aramaic H0927 *be.hal* (T1.1.2). An **event of collapse** — act-typed (action 79) but **passively undergone** (T1.2.1); **Niphal passive dominant** (37 — "one is *made* broken"), also causative Piel/Hiphil (God shatters) (T1.4.1). Name is **constitutional (inner strength broken)** (T1.1.3). A **compound** — couples collapse with **shame** (dismay + disgrace travel together) (T1.2.2).

## 2. God-relation, image, typology
**Agent** (20) — God **shatters/dismays** enemies — imaging God's **power to break** (T0.1.1). Never God's experiencer (T0.1.2); a creaturely analogue (T0.3.2). Expresses **creaturely finitude/vulnerability** — the image under collapse (T0.3.1); presence reveals the creature under threat (neutral) (T0.3.3). Typology: **silent** — no NT; eschat/covenant/christological markers absent (T0.4.3).

## 3. Created purpose / design-or-fall
Neutral 74 — **collapse of composure, not a design** (T0.2.1); **fallen-condition response** (T0.2.2). Immediate response: **composure failing, knees giving way, fleeing** — turn back, hasty word, "gave way knees" — consistent (collapse) (T1.5.1–1.5.2). Thin on sustained effect distinct from the collapse-event (T1.6).

## 4. Constitution
**Soul 5** is its leading location, with heart 3 — dismay reaches the **personal core**, the breaking of inner strength (T2.2.1–2.2.2). It carries the cluster's **second spirit-level case**: Ecc 7:9, "Be not quick in your spirit to become angry" (the *bahal* "hasty/dismayed" sense, located in the spirit) — touching the spirit's governing of disposition (T2.1.1/2.1.3). Flesh 1 (T2.6.1). **Silent on mind** (T2.4). Falls upon / seizes the person (downward-onto) (T2.10.1).

## 5. Faculties
Constitutively **affective**, single-faculty (T3.4). Lighter cognitive/memory co-terms (T3.2/T3.3). **Impairs agency** — knees give way, composure fails (T3.7). **Silent** on volition, moral evaluation, creativity, conscience at the faculty level (neutral-dominant) (T3.6/T3.8–3.10).

## 6. Relational interfaces
God object 6 (T4.2.1). At the human interface, **dismay is received from a powerful/self-disclosing party** — Gen 45:3, "his brothers could not answer him, for they were dismayed at his presence" (Joseph's self-disclosure) (T4.4.1); persons as the dismay-inspiring party (person 14) (T4.3.1). God's agent-mode is **responsive/judicial** — Jer 1:17, "Do not be dismayed by them, lest I dismay you before them" (dismay as consequence) (T4.1.2). 2 spiritual-being records (incl. Psa 104:29, creatures dismayed at God's hidden face) (T4.6.1).

## 7. Over time, suffering, eschatology
Silent on transformation/sequence/mechanism/sanctification (T5.1/5.2/5.3/5.5). **Concentrated in the suffering literature** — 10 Job/Lam refs; `cause` = "trouble coming upon him," "seeing disaster close upon them" (T5.4). **Silent** on eschatological trajectory (T5.6.3).

## 8. Within the cluster
Opposite: **rest / quiet** (*nu.ach*, *sha.an*, *sha.qat*) **and courage** (the "fear not, be not dismayed" formula) (T1.3.1); excludes composure (T1.3.2). Distinguished from **c6 (horror)** by direction (collapse-of vs recoil-from) and from **c8 (alarm)** by onset (shattering / Niphal passive vs abrupt startle) — the two share H0927 (T6.5.1). **Produced by fear** (the "fear not, neither be dismayed" pairing treats dismay as fear's collapse-product) (T6.3.1). The *chatat* root bridges **terror (c4) and dismay (c7)** (T6.4.2). Its **strongest cross-cluster partner is shame** (M07 — the shattering + the disgrace; co-term weight 41, the strongest other-characteristic partner in the cluster) — held → Session D (T6.1.1; cross-cluster register X2).

## 9. Interpretive lens
Genre: **prophecy 48 + wisdom 15** — dismay in prophetic judgement and Job (T7.2.2). Anchor **Jer 1:17** uniquely pairs **undergone and inflicted** dismay in one clause ("be not dismayed… lest I dismay you") (T7.2.6). Science lens: **psychophysiology** (the freeze/collapse register, alongside c4) (T7.3.1).

## 10. Inferential flags & silences
*Held → Session D:* the dismay↔shame pairing (X2, M07) — the strongest cross-cluster bond in M01; mark inferential where prose touches it. *Silences:* mind; typology; eschatology; sequence/mechanism; most moral/volitional faculties.

## 11. Essence
Dismay is fear as **collapse** — the shattering of inner strength, knees giving way, composure failing — undergone passively and, in God's hand, inflicted judicially ("lest I dismay you"). It reaches the soul, briefly touches the spirit, and travels with shame more than with any other characteristic.
