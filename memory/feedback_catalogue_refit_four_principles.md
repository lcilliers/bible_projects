**Researcher four principles for the catalogue refit (2026-06-09).** Applied in
`research/investigations/wa-catalogue-refit-two-layer-v1-20260609.md`. Extends
[[feedback_verse_level_extraction_feeds_tiers]].

**(a) Redefined-characteristic lens.** "Characteristic" in the tier questions is **confusing** — it literally
means *anything that relates to the inner being* (the typed term-in-verse), **not** the distilled/grouped
entity (love, grace). Read every "this characteristic" as "*this term, here*." Questions phrased about the
distilled entity are inherently SYNTH.

**(b) Drop redundant / construct questions.** Anything tied to retired programme constructs: **T1.8**
Dimension Classification, **T6.7** Dimensional Sharing, **T2.8** Body-Deposit (DNA/generational — speculative),
**T5.7** Deposit Consequence, **T6.6** Shared Verse Anchors, **T1.2.3** "best working description"; defer
**T7.3** Human Science (external lens).

**(c) De-force the inducing questions.** The tell is *"what does X reveal/suggest about Y"* / *"in what way
does it express Z"* — these **prompt/force findings or extract what doesn't exist**. Rephrase to a neutral
**check**: *"is X present here? record it; if not, silent."* A checklist is good; a forcing question goes too
far ([[feedback_clarifications_reusable_but_state_not_induce]]).

**(d) Two layers.** **Verse-grounded** answers (from one verse's evidence) = **L1/L2** — these ARE the
verse-extraction record (sense, type, mode, location, origin, faculty, attributed-to-God, purpose,
immediate-response, produces, relational-direction, suffering-context, literary-setting, co-occurrence-array).
**Cross-verse / cross-term / cross-cluster** answers = **SYNTH** (the `.2`/.3 "reveal/pattern" sub-questions,
over-time effects, T4 bases, T5 arc, T6 relationships) — computed from the records, not asked at the verse.
The `.1` "is-it/which/does-this-verse-show" sub-questions are mostly L1/L2; the `.2`/`.3` are SYNTH.
**Decisions D1–D4 pending researcher markup before refitting the catalogue + rebuilding the L2 extraction.**
