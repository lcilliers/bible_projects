FEEDBACK (2026-06-16): when asked to review a per-characteristic lexical analysis (e.g. M01-c1 reverent-fear vs the VE items), my first pass only validated the statistics (recomputed the counts the doc cited — all exact). The researcher corrected: "it is not only a statistical exercise — it is about what insight is hidden in the lexical." A correct review goes **field by field through the WHOLE VE catalogue** and asks, for each: what does it carry for this characteristic, did the analysis comment on it, what insight is left unextracted.

**Why:** the analysis mined ~9 of 17 derivable fields well but parked/skipped the richest ones — `compound` (87% filled, the synergy web: obedience/covenant/cognition nexuses), `immediate_response` (behavioural fruit — Abraham, Sabbath, law-walking, joy), `experiencer` (commanded-to-others stance), `mode` (Qal vs Niphal "awesome"), how/intensity/origin. Validating counts hides this entirely.

**How to apply:** for any lexical/VE review (a) recompute the cited numbers (cheap, do it), THEN (b) run a field-completeness audit — every VE field gets a row: fill-rate · carried insight · mined? · what's hidden. Parking T7 *findings* ≠ leaving the `compound` *field* uncommented. Note where a field is excluded BY DESIGN (purpose/typology, 01b §4d) vs silent. Recommend the analysis template require a field-completeness pass (a sentence per field: value or noted silence).
