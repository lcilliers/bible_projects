FEEDBACK (2026-06-14, recurring — researcher: "this is not the first time"): I have a standing tendency to treat the analytical pipeline as "the work" and **under-weight the governance / operational layers that keep it reliable**. In one session the reconstruction had to be corrected three times for omitting, in turn: the schema/architecture drift, then **filing** governance ([[filing-is-first-class-governance]]), then **operational / safeguard** governance ([[operational-governance-git-backup-manifest]]).

**How to apply:** in any audit, reconstruction, status review, or "what governs this" task, explicitly enumerate and cover every governance layer — **analytical pipeline · filing / file-org · git/commit · backups & recovery · manifest · interaction / cost** — not just the pipeline. Treat omitting a layer as a defect, and sweep the governance layers *first*. The orientation map `docs/project-orientation-core-memory-map.md` is the checklist. See [[reference-core-memory-orientation-map]] · [[project_reconstruction_baseline_20260614]].
