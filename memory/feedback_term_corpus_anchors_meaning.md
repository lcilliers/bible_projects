**Proven by the 2026-06-05 methodology reality-test** (4 M01 verses, one per complexity group;
`research/investigations/verse-analysis-methodology-test-20260605.md`). CC analysed each verse cold; the
researcher graded **all four wrong, each differently** — and they were exactly the §0 AI biases:

- **Deu 1:19 — FAIL (narrowing):** set aside "terrifying" as physical; but it *names an inner-being state*
  (terror), the wilderness is only its cause/situation. In scope.
- **Exo 1:17 — PARTIAL (imposed framing):** read fear as "controlling"; but the reverence is *strengthening*
  resolve (greater than Pharaoh's threat) — positive, not constraining.
- **Exo 3:6 — OPEN (false confidence):** asserted "reverent dread"; genuinely unsettled (court custom of not
  looking at a superior? *frightened* vs *reverent*?). Should have emitted the open question.
- **Lev 19:3 — WRONG PAIRING (forced structure):** wove M30 obedience into M01's meaning; but the two spans
  are independent — see [[feedback_span_pairing_and_reciprocal_findings]] influence test.

**The lesson:** **reading a verse cold, in isolation, is the problem.** Confident single-verse readings
reproduce narrowing / imposed-framing / false-confidence / forced-structure. This is the gravest data risk
(verse-meaning soundness) made empirical.

**The remedy (runs through all four): the term's CORPUS.** Read the span against the term's *other
occurrences* — that is what settles **valence** (fear-of-God positive/negative, controlling/strengthening),
**sense** (frightened vs reverent), and the **influence test** for siblings. This is the analogy of
Scripture (Seven Principles 6) and the old v2_9 "per-term meaning corpus", pulled **forward into the meaning
stage**.

**How to apply:**
- **Term-anchored, not verse-cold:** take a term, read its whole corpus together so each occurrence is
  calibrated against the term's range; derive each verse's meaning from that vantage. (Also more efficient —
  one term, all its verses, read once.)
- **Emit open questions as first-class output** where the corpus doesn't settle it; do **not** invent a
  confident answer.
- Researcher review remains the catch — it caught all four here (the foundations' predicted mitigation).
