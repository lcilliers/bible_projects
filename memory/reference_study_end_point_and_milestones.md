The end point settled in `wa-study-foundations.md` §d (2026-06-04):

- **Two-part end point.** (1) A **materially-evidenced corpus of findings** = what Scripture says about
  the inner being (completeness is a judgement call, not a gate). (2) That corpus is the **source for
  multiple end products**: short essays, Bible-study guides, ebooks/printed books, sermon series.
- **DB corpus is the substrate** — findings of the parts and collections of the parts; keep enriching it
  in the DB, make no key observation outside it (reinforces [[reference_analysis_rules_finding_lifecycle]]).
- **Both reference AND narrative** (Q1): a reference source (verse-meaning findings substantiated by raw
  data) + narratives that rework/apply it per audience — different layers of one product, not alternatives.
- **Clusters are vehicles; the final form DISSOLVES them** (Q2) into cross-cluster thematic groupings —
  e.g. how the faculties of the inner being manifest; how God's character reflects in His creation of the
  human being; how the gifts of the Spirit manifest; and more. (Sharpens [[feedback_no_forced_structure_audit_surfaces_analysis_compensates]]:
  synthesis supersedes cluster lines, doesn't just read across them.)
- **Whole-study scope = a finding for every verse** (Q3), across all registry words' Hebrew/Greek
  equivalents. This is COVERAGE not a content quota — every verse addressed, and silence is a valid
  finding (§c) — i.e. the "no orphans" discipline, not forced completion.
- **Three audiences, one groundwork** (Q4): scholar · leader/preacher/teacher · ordinary person.
  Groundwork serves all; audience articulation happens in the narrative layer.
- **Three milestones replace completion-pressure** (Q5): M1 data complete & captured for all terms
  (valuable in itself) · M2 all clusters analysed → first complete findings corpus · M3 narratives gain
  public traction. M1 is CAPTURE; soundness is still earned via the sift + the verse-meaning audit
  ([[project_next_action_audit_surface_verses]]).
