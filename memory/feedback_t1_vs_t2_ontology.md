The T1 / T2 / FLAG distinction in the named-characteristic clusters (M01..M46) is **ontological**, not lexical. Affinity / candidate scans must apply this test before recommending migrations.

**T1** — the term's core meaning *is* an inner-being characteristic in operation.
- *love* (the act/disposition of loving), *fear* (the experience of fearing), *compassion* (the impulse to spare another), *anger*, *joy*, *hope*.
- These belong in M-clusters.

**T2 / FLAG** — the term's meaning *enhances, describes, modifies, or is impacted by* a characteristic. It is NOT the faculty being exercised.
- **Recipient/object** of the characteristic — *beloved* is someone whose status results from love being directed at them; the faculty is in the lover, not the beloved. *Despised, hated, the feared one* — same pattern.
- **Effect/state produced** by the characteristic — *kindness-shown-to-me-as-favor* (recipient-side) vs *to-be-kind* (faculty-side).
- **Modifier** — *deeply, completely, perpetually* (qualifier tier).
- **Co-occurring object** — *companion, friend* (a relational status, not the relating-faculty itself).

**Why:** The unit of analysis is the inner-being capacity. *Love* is a faculty the inner being exercises. *Beloved* is a status produced when that faculty is exercised toward someone. Conflating the two collapses the analytical structure of the program-reset model.

**How to apply:** When proposing T1 / M-cluster migrations from T2 or FLAG, the test is: *"Is this term describing the faculty in operation, or is it describing what happens to / around / because of the faculty?"* Only the first answer warrants T1/M-cluster placement.

Worked example (2026-05-05): scan over T2+FLAG for M05 candidates surfaced agapētos, dod (×3), yadid as Tier 1 LIKELY by semantic + keyword signal. Researcher correctly rejected all five — they're recipients of love, not love-as-faculty. None migrated to M05. The lexical "beloved" signal was misleading because *beloved* is the status of the loved-one, not love itself.

**Implication for affinity scanning:** semantic similarity + keyword match are *necessary* but not *sufficient*. The ontological test (faculty-vs-recipient/effect/modifier) must be applied before recommending. Future scans should phrase candidates as "potentially adjacent" rather than "should move to" until the researcher confirms the faculty test.
