**The data model in plain terms (researcher, 2026-06-03):** a pointer belongs to a term; a term has
verses; a term belongs in a (possibly more than one, related) cluster. **The unit that moves is the
TERM.** When a term is mis-placed or moves, its **VCGs and findings move WITH it** — you never peel a
finding or a pointer off on its own and ship it to another cluster.

**Reprocessing:** if a term moves into a cluster after that cluster's Tier 1–7 was already processed,
the cluster is **reprocessed** so the impact of the new term on the cluster is properly analysed.

**Work the cluster as a whole.** Do NOT do piecemeal cross-cluster re-allocations of individual
pointers/findings divorced from their term and the cluster's other structures (sub-groups, VCGs).
That fragmentation is what creates orphaned bits and "merry-go-round" risk.

**Content and context is king — it leads all analysis.** Decide by what the term/verse actually
*means in context*, never by the mechanical classification (`cluster_link` is a hint, not the decision).

**Why:** on 2026-06-03 I drifted into atomized pointer/finding routing by `cluster_link` (e.g. M46
Abundance: routed 30 peace findings + 19 peace pointers to M33 Peace while leaving the reg-117 peace
*terms* in M46's sub-groups — splitting findings from their term). That is incoherent. Supersedes the
narrow framing of [[feedback_route_dual_cluster_findings]]: the dual-finding idea is right, but it is
realised by moving/sharing the **term** and reprocessing, not by shuffling loose pointers.

**How to apply:**
- Remediate a cluster by examining **its terms** (and their verses/VCGs/findings/sub-group placement) as a whole — is each term content-appropriate to this cluster?
- A term that doesn't belong → move the **term + its verses + VCGs + findings** to the cluster its content fits; the receiving cluster reprocesses.
- A term whose content genuinely spans clusters → it is shared/represented in each, becoming findings in each at their analysis.
- Never resolve an A6/A7 item by relocating the lone pointer/finding while its term stays put.
