Researcher direction 2026-06-05, resolving the M10-D VCG-redo finding (the "punishment" block).

**The principle.** "Punishment" in its core sense is **not an inner state** — it is an external, relational
action or consequence imposed on a person (by judge, parent, society, or God) in response to wrongdoing.
Its home is **justice / consequence / sanction**, not the interior life; the agent and the location of the
act are outside the person. The same goes for the judicial/retributive family.

**But it sits on the border — distinguish two things:**
1. **Punishment is the EXTERNAL POLE of a relationship whose INTERNAL pole is a genuine inner-being word.**
   What punishment responds to / produces *is* interior — guilt, remorse, contrition, self-condemnation,
   shame, a wounded/accusing conscience, felt suffering. So map punishment to its inner counterparts and
   **cross-reference** it there, rather than housing it as inner: conscience (reg 26), shame (reg 146),
   repentance (reg 135), sorrow (reg TBC — **154 is "stupor", not sorrow**; grief is M03).
2. **The discipline/correction sub-family STRADDLES** — the external act is defined by its **inner formative
   purpose**: Hebrew מוּסָר *musar* / יָסַר *yasar* (Proverbs — chastening aimed at inward transformation);
   Greek παιδεία *paideia* (Heb 12 — training whose telos is inner shaping). These are **partly inner-facing**
   in a way "punishment" proper is not — treat as a boundary case, not a clean exclusion.

**Grounded in the data (M10):** עָוֹן *avon* collapses sin + guilt + punishment in one word; the DB already
splits it by suffix — `H5771G` "iniquity: crime" (162), `H5771H` "iniquity: guilt" (27, **inner**), `H5771I`
"iniquity: punishment" (18, **external**). The 18 `H5771I` verses are the external pole, currently mis-housed
in sub-group **M10-D "Guilt as inner-being state."**

**Retributive/judicial families to watch (external pole):** penalty, sanction, retribution, recompense,
judgment, condemnation, sentence, chastisement, vengeance, requital. Hebrew פָּקַד *paqad* (visit/punish),
נָקַם *naqam* (vengeance), שָׁפַט *shaphat* (judge). Greek κόλασις *kolasis*, τιμωρία *timoria*, δίκη *dike*,
ἐκδίκησις *ekdikesis*.

**A parallel third pole (M10-H, 2026-06-05): the PHYSICAL pole.** Beyond external-consequence, watch the
**physical/bodily/cosmic decay** sense — perishing flesh, wasting away, creation's "corruption", moth-decay
(1Cor 15 *perishable*, 2Cor 4:16 *wasting away*, Rom 8:21). That is **not an inner state** either (= the
`physical_only` set-aside). So the scope tool has **three poles: inner-being core · external pole
(consequence) · physical pole (decay)** — and a single English word ("corruption") can span all three, only
separable by reading the actual verses.

**How to apply:** when a span's core sense is external consequence/sanction (or physical decay), classify it
**external-pole / physical-pole — not inner-being core**; **cross-reference to the inner counterpart** registry (don't silently set aside — it
still informs the inner word it pairs with, [[feedback_setaside_verses_inform_word_meaning]]); flag the
discipline sub-family as **straddling**. Refines [[feedback_inner_being_full_scope]] (full scope, but the
*inner* state is the bar — and the external pole is named *as* external, then linked, not absorbed).
