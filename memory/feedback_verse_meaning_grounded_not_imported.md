**Researcher quality check (2026-06-10):** "make sure you are not adding narrative into the verses that is
not supported by the verse itself (even if the narrative is plausible and potentially true)." CC was over-
reaching — importing context beyond the verse, which is exactly the contamination [[feedback_term_corpus_anchors_meaning]]
and the state-not-induce rule guard against.

**The standard — "verse + surrounding verses" (refined 2026-06-10).** Legitimate grounds for a verse meaning:
- the **term's lexical sense** (corpus-anchored), the **grammar/stem/morphology**;
- the **other terms actually present in this verse** (co-occurrence);
- **the surrounding verses / passage (pericope)** — fully acceptable and often *required* to understand the
  verse. Reading Gen 4:5 MAY use Abel's murder (4:8); Mat 18:34 MAY use the application (4:35); Psa 73:3 MAY
  use the sanctuary resolution (73:17). These are within the passage, so they ground the meaning.

**Do NOT embellish with concepts NOT reflected in the verse or its surrounding verses (even if true/plausible):**
- **cross-BOOK references / canonical connections** (no "cited by Paul in Rom 10:19" when reading Deu 32:21);
- imported **systematic theology** not present in the passage;
- invented or plausible-but-absent ideas the passage does not contain.

**Test:** *is the concept present in the verse or its surrounding verses?* yes → use it; external import → drop.
Synthesis ACROSS passages / terms / clusters (and canonical connections) still belongs to the **SYNTH layer**,
per [[feedback_catalogue_refit_four_principles]] two-layer split.

**Remediation:** existing M02 (~171), M01, M15 findings are LEFT AS-IS (the imported content is "potentially
true", not false). Discipline applies to all NEW verse-reads going forward. Watch the meaning paragraph and
the relational_implication/produces_effect fields especially — those are where the import crept in.
