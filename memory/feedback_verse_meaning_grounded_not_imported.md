**Researcher quality check (2026-06-10):** "make sure you are not adding narrative into the verses that is
not supported by the verse itself (even if the narrative is plausible and potentially true)." CC was over-
reaching — importing context beyond the verse, which is exactly the contamination [[feedback_term_corpus_anchors_meaning]]
and the state-not-induce rule guard against.

**The standard — "verse + immediate context".** Legitimate grounds for a verse meaning:
- the **term's lexical sense** (corpus-anchored), the **grammar/stem/morphology**;
- the **other terms actually present in this verse** (co-occurrence);
- the **directly-adjacent context needed to CONSTRUE the verse** (e.g. that "the master" is a parable figure;
  the clause the verse answers in the immediately surrounding verse).

**Do NOT fold in (even when true):**
- distant narrative outcomes (reading 4:5 must not narrate the murder of 4:8);
- the parable's application / moral (reading Mat 18:34 must not assert "the master images God / the heavenly
  Father will so deal" — that is v.35);
- cross-book references / canonical connections (no "cited by Paul in Rom 10:19");
- book-level themes ("sets up the book's closing lesson");
- systematic theology beyond the verse.

These belong to the **SYNTH layer** (cross-verse/term/cluster), per [[feedback_catalogue_refit_four_principles]]
two-layer split. The verse record states what the verse says; synthesis reasons across verses.

**Remediation:** existing M02 (~171), M01, M15 findings are LEFT AS-IS (the imported content is "potentially
true", not false). Discipline applies to all NEW verse-reads going forward. Watch the meaning paragraph and
the relational_implication/produces_effect fields especially — those are where the import crept in.
