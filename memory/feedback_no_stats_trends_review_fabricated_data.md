**The point (2026-06-11, on seeing the DROP-code findings extract):** the user found the DROP-code findings
"peppered with made up data" and not worth the time to validate. Their conclusion: *"managing and reviewing
this project with statistics and trends is absolutely very dangerous and misleading."*

look orderly and can read as "we're on track," but they mask fabricated / templated / forced content
underneath. A clean-looking distribution is NOT evidence of sound data. The uniform per-family counts
(M04/M05/M06 = 24 each, M10 = 66 each) were the tell that those findings were generated one-per-question
regardless of evidence — i.e. invented.

**How to apply:**

  trend with "this does not verify the content."

- Treat the legacy Session B / Session D / cluster_finding / forced-catalogue findings as suspect until
  content-validated; prefer the verse-read meaning layer (grounded, [[feedback_verse_meaning_grounded_not_imported]]).

  validated ([[project_audit_sequencing_clusters_then_meaning]] data-too-dirty-to-trap; [[project_session_d_moot]]).
