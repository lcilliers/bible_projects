**The audit is central and must be self-critical (researcher, 2026-06-04).** The audit step did not
exist before; V3 surfaced it as one of the most important steps. It must **not** run as a fixed
checklist. It must regularly and critically interrogate *itself*: *Am I doing my job? Am I asking the
right questions? Am I inspecting the data from the right angles? Are there angles I do not cover?* New
angles are added to the audit as they are recognised — the audit's own coverage is part of what
completeness means. (This is the meta-level of [[feedback_no_forced_structure_audit_surfaces_analysis_compensates]]:
the audit surfaces all objects *and* keeps questioning whether it is surfacing them from enough angles.)

**First emergent angle — verse-meaning corroboration (open; nothing adopted yet).** Verse meaning
(Pass A) has had only cursory review, with reliance on the *ultimate findings* to expose skew. But
meaning is the foundation that rules all analytics and its error rate is high (the gravest data risk,
see [[feedback_no_forced_structure_audit_surfaces_analysis_compensates]] / study-foundations §b) — so
catching skew only downstream is late, costly, and may never surface. The researcher is questioning
whether to corroborate meaning actively. Candidate approaches (weigh, don't auto-adopt): anchor each
meaning to its STEP lexical sense + several translations and flag divergence; surface contested/multiple
readings explicitly in Pass A; carry a contestability/confidence marker + lexical basis per meaning;
targeted researcher review of the highest-leverage meanings (anchors, high-impact verses) that the
audit surfaces. If adopted, the audit carries a standing angle: *are verse meanings corroborated, and
are the contested ones surfaced?*

Captured in `Workflow/methodology/wa-study-foundations.md` §b ("Emerging" block). Decision pending.
