Emerged 2026-06-04 (foundations session). A cluster's finalisation is **iterative**, and clusters that
**work together** (share synergy / a finding bears on more than one) must be **finalised together**, not
signed off in isolation.

**Why:** clusters are vehicles for analysis, not the reality ([[reference_study_end_point_and_milestones]]);
meaning routinely spans them (multi-belonging → a finding in each). A cluster signed off alone, before its
synergetic neighbours are analysed, will have provisional cross-cluster findings whose wording and
substance are not yet settled — so its "done" is false. The final form will in any case dissolve clusters
into cross-cluster groupings.

**How to apply:** don't treat cluster sign-off as a one-pass per-cluster gate. Expect to revisit a cluster
when an interdependent neighbour is analysed; settle shared/synergy findings across the set before any of
them is called complete. Plan finalisation by synergy-group, not strictly one cluster at a time.
