**Researcher refinement (2026-06-09).** Surfaced when the read-dedup ranking flagged `ya.da` "to know"
(368 verses, M15) as a suspect large group.

"To know" **does not stand on its own feet — ask: to know *what?*** `ya.da` is predominantly a **T2
qualifier** of its object (know God / know good and evil / know a person), **not** a standalone T1
characteristic. **Only in a few cases** is "to know" used as a generic T1 in its own right (knowledge as a
standing thing — "the knowledge of the LORD shall fill the earth"). So most of ya.da's 368 verses are
*know-OBJECT* and should route **per verse-occurrence** to the **object's** characteristic/cluster, not sit
as M15 Wisdom T1.

**Why:** the same lesson as [[feedback_qualifier_routes_per_verse_occurrence]] but applied to a faculty-VERB
itself — a transitive inner-life verb is often the qualifier, with the real characteristic being what it
acts on. T1/T2 is decided in the verse read ([[feedback_t1_vs_t2_ontology]]), and the term is the unit of
movement ([[feedback_term_is_the_unit_of_movement]]).

**How to apply:** don't treat high-frequency transitive faculty-verbs (know, seek, remember, fear-as-revere,
keep/guard) as automatically T1 in their home cluster. In the verse-level read, test whether the verb is
*qualifying an object* (→ T2, route to the object) or *naming the standing faculty* (→ T1). Needs more
digging/refinement before reclassifying — flagged, not yet executed.
