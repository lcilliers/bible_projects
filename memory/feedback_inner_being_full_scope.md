Inner being covers the **entire human inner life** — moral evaluation, agricultural/material satisfaction, political deference, sensory pleasure, etc. — not just theological or God-directed content. Human characteristics that are purely human, without an explicit spiritual link, are still in scope.

**Why:** In M04 (Joy/Delight/Wonder), AI's Phase 3/5/7 synthesis routed ~322 verses to BOUNDARY because they lacked an obvious God-directed register. The user spotted this as systematic spiritualising bias — Pass A verse meanings are NEUTRAL; the bias lives in the synthesis layer (cross-routing, sub-group design, dissolution decisions), not in the data layer. Treating "no clear spiritual link" as grounds to set aside loses ~30–50% of legitimate inner-being content per cluster.

**How to apply:**
- When drafting cluster-instruction §s on scope (Phase 1 examples, Phase 3 cross-routing discipline, Phase 5 sub-group design): the bar for inclusion is "inner-being content," not "theological content." Explicitly call out moral evaluation, wellbeing, sensory pleasure, comparative goodness, volitional deference as legitimate inner-being registers.
- When AI proposes to BOUNDARY a verse with the reasoning "no clear theological/spiritual link," push back: ask whether the verse describes a human inner state, and if yes, route it into a sub-group rather than parking it.
- Pass A verse-meaning prep (API-driven) is unbiased — no need to re-spec that. The fix is at synthesis (Phases 3, 5, 7, 8) only.
