## Headline

- **The RECORD / ledger object-kind is IMPOSED.** Across all 1,904 verses / every lexical field, the strings `record`, `ledger`, `tally`, `inscrib` = **0 occurrences**. The forensic-accounting frame (debt · account · record · measure) is **not present in any lexical field of any sin-term occurrence**. Where the observation doc reads "record" (Jer 17:1 "engraved", Isa 40:2 "her sins double", Rom 4:8 "not count"), the accounting vocabulary lives on **partner verbs** (cha.rash "engraved", logizomai "count", za.khar "remember"), never on the sin-noun. The sin-term itself is uniformly `sense=sin/iniquity`, `type=status`.
- **The a.von crime/guilt/punishment "tri-fold" is REAL but mislocated.** It does NOT live in three per-verse senses of one lemma; it is the **sub-entry split** (H5771G iniquity / H5771H guilt / H5771I punishment). `lemma_meaning="crime"` and `type=status` are **uniform across all three** (162 + 27 + 18 occ, 100%). Within each sub-entry the sense is near-constant (G=93% iniquity, H=89% guilt, I=89% punishment). So it is one lemma carved into three glossed sub-entries — not a per-verse arc the verses display. **And the "punishment" tail (H5771I) is external-pole** (penalty borne), not inner-being.
- **IDENTITY is SUPPORTED as a classification but OVERREACH on "settled person-identity / corrupt interior".** `type` is lemma-constant (ra'sha = status 237/237; sinner G0268 = quality 45/45) — so "classification" is a tag, not a verse reading; but the legit per-verse fields (object_type=person, experiencer=other) corroborate a person-directed verdict in a **bounded** subset, NOT a pervasive opened interior.
- **Imported-meaning rate: 5 of 8 claims (62.5%)** import meaning the lexical does not carry — consistent with the pilot's 6/7 and §2's prediction that imposition concentrates in the object-kind superstructure.

## §B. The RECORD / ledger object-kind — verdict in full

**Verdict: IMPOSED.** Test applied (framework KEY TEST): *is there ANY lexical field carrying record/debt/ledger, or is it imposed?*

- Corpus-wide string scan of every `lexical` blob (sense, object, object_type, how, compound, lemma_meaning, valence, experiencer):
  - `record` = **0** · `ledger` = **0** · `tally` = **0** · `inscrib` = **0**
  - `debt` = 16 — **all on G0859 afesis "forgiveness"** (release-of-debts gloss for *forgiveness*, not a sin-term denoting debt).
  - `account` = 4 — on da.rash "account/reckoning", ra'sha, cha.ta, ponēros (verbs/partners, never the sin-noun as "an account").
  - `engrav` = 7 — all on **cha.rash** (plow/engrave/devise), a partner verb; Jer 17:1 included.
  - `reckon`/`count` = on da.rash, **logizomai** (G3049) — the *counting verb*, partner to hamartia.
- **Conclusion:** the accounting/forensic-weight frame is constructed from **partner predicates in a small set of verses** (engrave, count, remember, mark, blot) and elevated to an object-kind. The sin-terms themselves are uniformly `status` wrongs. "Sin as divine record" is a true *theological synthesis of what God does with sin* (remembers/counts/blots) but it is **not the lexical denotation of the sin-term** — it is read in. Same failure as the pilot's #13 (IMPOSED + LEXICAL-GAP at Jer 17:1).

The defensible residue: sin is a **status** that God is repeatedly said (by partner verbs) to **remember, not-count, and blot**. That supports a "**sin is borne/answered-for before God and can be remitted**" reading — *liability*, loosely — but **not** "record/ledger/account" as the object's kind.

## §C. The a.von tri-fold — verdict in full

**Verdict: the distinction is SUPPORTED (it is lexically encoded in the sub-entries); the "process/arc/one-object" framing is OVERREACH.** Test applied: *does the per-verse sense/object show three senses, or one sense read three ways?*

- It is **neither**. It is **three sub-entries** each carrying its own near-mono sense:
  - H5771G → iniquity (93%), H5771H → guilt (89%), H5771I → punishment (89%).
  - `lemma_meaning`="crime" and `type`="status" are **identical across all three** → the split is a **glossing decision**, not a per-verse phenomenon and not a typological one.
- So the three meanings are real and lexically separated — but they do not appear as an *arc within a verse* and the framework's worry ("one sense read three ways") is **not** the failure here; the opposite is closer (three pre-sorted entries presented as one object's internal process).
- **H5771I (punishment) is external-pole** and should not sit inside an inner-being RECORD/LIABILITY kind (CONTRADICTED for scope).

## §D. IDENTITY — verdict in full

**Verdict: SUPPORTED as a person-directed verdict-label (bounded); OVERREACH on "settled identity / corrupt interior"; the "classification not movement" claim is TERM-CONSTANT.**

- `type` is the spine of the "classification" claim and `type` is a **lemma-constant** (ra'sha status 237/237; sinner quality 45/45) → reading "it's a classification, not a movement" off `type` is TERM-CONSTANT.
- The **legit per-verse fields do corroborate**, in a bounded way: object_type=person (ra'sha 43/237; G0268 7/45), experiencer=other — a verdict *directed at persons*. So "sinner/wicked = a person-type" is genuinely in the per-verse data for the cited verses.
- The **over-reach** is the rich settled interior ("practical atheism, will corrupted, conscience suppressed"): lev co-seating appears in ~5 verses, the "no God" texts are 2. The lexical shows a **verdict-label applied to people**, with interior content **concentrated, not pervasive** — exactly as the doc's own §G admits.
