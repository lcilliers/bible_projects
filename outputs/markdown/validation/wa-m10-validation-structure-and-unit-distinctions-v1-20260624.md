## HEADLINE

**The 32-unit structure is largely an interpretive overlay the lexical fields do not ground.** The per-verse lexical evidence (sense/object/object_type/experiencer/how/valence) **separates ~1 of the 7 ḥāṭāʾ units** (#2 Unintentional, lexically marked by `object="unintentionally"`). The other 6 (Wilful, Confession, Conscience-suppression, Refusal-to-repent, Habitual, Contagious) + #15 Generational are **distinguished only by reading the verse_text**, not by any per-verse lexical field. The unit files **say so themselves** ("distinguished only by reading", "per-occurrence assignment deferred to §10"). The ra'sha identity units collapse to a single lemma-constant. **Overall imported-meaning rate on the structural claims: ~80% (8 of 10 headline claims OVERREACH/IMPOSED/TERM-CONSTANT/LEXICAL-GAP).** This is the same order of magnitude as the pilot's 6/7 and is the *expected* result given §2.

## A. THE UNIT-DISTINCTION TEST (the central result)

**Inductive question (asked before the labels):** pull every ḥāṭāʾ-family focus occurrence; do the legit per-verse fields separate 7 units, or is the 7-way split a frame the lexical can't carry?

### A.1 The mechanical floor (the act-verbs that carry units #1–7)
Units #1–7 are all carried by the **act-verb** layer of the ḥāṭāʾ family (cha.ta H2398 + the NT hamartanō G0264 — the noun/offering senses belong to #11–14/#16, not the act-mode units, per the unit file's own §A).

| Field | cha.ta H2398 (213 occ) | act-verbs cha.ta+hamartanō (250 occ) | bearing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **how** (operational/modal) | **1/213 (0.5%)** | **1/250 (0.4%)** | the field that would carry "wilful / unintentional / habitual / suppressed" is **all but absent** |
| object | 146/213 (69%) | 165/250 (66%) | carries relational *target*, not *manner* |
| sense | 100% | 100% | "sinned/sin/committed" — does not vary by unit |
| distinct legit-field signatures | **75 across 213 occ** | — | the signatures track grammatical **person/relational-target**, not the 7 manner-distinctions |

The top signatures are `(sinned, -, self)`, `(sin, -, other)`, `(sinned, God, self)`, `(committed, abstract, other)` — i.e. **who sinned and against what/whom**, never **how** (wilfully? habitually? in suppression?). The manner that defines units #1, #4, #5, #6 lives in the verse predicate, which the `how` field does not capture (0.4% coverage).

The 7-way split is an interpretive layer the lexical cannot ground. **This is corroborated by the unit file's own admissions** — "Units #2, #7, #15 are all carried by the same verb cha.ta and are distinguished only by reading, not by lemma… Per-occurrence assignment beyond the clear cases is DEFERRED to §10."

### A.3 The ra'sha identity test
The ra'sha family (H7563/H7562/H7564/H4849, 217 focus occ) is **not a set of separable units at all**:
- `sense` = "wicked" on **194/217 (89%)**; `type` = status on 215/217; **`faculty` = moral_evaluation on 217/217 (distinct = 1)**.
- It is **one lemma-identity** ("the wicked person"), constant across its occurrences. Any sub-unit division (the identity vs its sub-faces) would be read off faculty/type/sense **lemma-constants** → **TERM-CONSTANT**. `object_type` varies only 29% (5 values), insufficient to sub-divide.

## B. POLE-PAIRS (cross-cluster) — verdicts

The pole-pair claim is that M10 objects are one half of an inverse pair (sin↔righteousness, written↔blot, defilement↔purity, corruption↔incorruptibility). Tested for a **same-verse lexical link** vs a constructed cross-verse synthesis.

| Pole-pair | Lexical test | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **#13 "written/record" ↔ "blot out"** (Jer 17:1 ↔ Isa 43:25) | Jer 17:1 chat.tat: `how=None`, **no "record/written/ledger" in any field** (it is in verse_text "engraved" only). Isa 43:25 chat.tat: `how="remember"`, **no field links it to Jer 17:1**. Two different verses, two different sense-readings, no lexical bond. | **IMPOSED** (constructed cross-verse synthesis) + **LEXICAL-GAP** (the "record" predicate is absent from Jer 17:1's fields). Matches pilot #1/#2. |
| **defilement ↔ purity** (M10c ↔ M12) — claimed for "ALL FOUR" defilement units | A **genuine same-verse clean↔unclean link exists in 17/226 (7%)** of defilement occ — the Levitical purity-law contrast (Lev 10:10, 11:47, 20:25: ta.me `compound` = `ta.hor "pure" — partner`). | **SUPPORTED (partial, 7%)** at verse level; **IMPOSED** as the "ALL FOUR units have M12 as structural opposite" cluster-level claim — 93% carry no purity partner. |
| **corruption ↔ incorruptibility** (fthora ↔ aftharsia) | **1/18** fthora occ has aftharsia as same-verse compound partner (1Co 15:50 — and that verse isn't even a defilement focus verse). | **IMPOSED** — a single co-occurrence generalised to a pole-pair. |
| **sinner ↔ righteous / sin ↔ righteousness** (M10↔M26) | This pair has the **strongest** independent grounding (81 shared verses M10↔M26 per the bonds run; co-occurrence is per-verse and legitimate). | **SUPPORTED** (the one robust pole-pair) — but note it rests on **co-occurrence**, not on a lexical "opposite" field. |

**Pole-pair summary: 1 robustly supported (sin↔righteousness, via co-occurrence), 1 partially (defilement↔purity 7%), 2 imposed.** The pole-pair as a *general structural law* ("M10 objects are halves of pairs") is **OVERREACH** — it holds where independent co-occurrence carries it and is imposed where it rests on isolated cross-verse construction.

## C. DUAL-VIEW verdict

The dual-view (operation-view inside M10 ↔ characteristic-view in the concept's own cluster; e.g. M10b-wickedness ↔ M27) is **a cluster-architecture meta-claim — it makes NO per-verse lexical claim.** It is about *where a concept is analysed*, not *what a verse says*. There is therefore **nothing in the lexical to ground or refute it** — it is, by its own statement, a "GOVERNING-CANDIDATE conceptual reflection. HELD OPEN — not a resolution… We do not have the keys." 

- **Verdict: NOT LEXICALLY GROUNDABLE (asserted, self-labelled held-open).** The document is honest about this (it does not claim lexical support). The one place it touches data — the §3 "consistency check" — is read off the cluster list and the bonds table (co-occurrence), not off per-verse fields. The dual-view is a reasonable organising hypothesis but it is **asserted at the conceptual level, not earned from the lexical**. No imposition *onto the verses* (it doesn't touch them); the risk is that it becomes a frame the unit-assignments are later bent to fit.

## §G — Bias-guard on this validation

- The validator is Claude and carries the same eisegetic reflex. The guard applied: **(a)** induction-first (read the fields' coverage/variance *before* the labels); **(b)** null-default (every claim IMPOSED until earned); **(c)** faculty/type/lemma_meaning excluded from per-verse evidence and re-confirmed mechanically here (faculty 0/69); **(d)** verse_text used only as a gap signal, never to win; **(e)** verdicts checked against the mechanical aggregates (the 0.4% how-coverage, the 75 signatures, the 0% faculty variance), not narrative.
- **A finding of "low imposition" would itself be suspect.** The 80% rate is consistent with the framework's §2 mechanics and the pilot's 6/7 — it is the *expected* magnitude, which is itself a check that the method was not relaxed.
- **A genuine caution against over-finding:** the validation does **not** claim the 32 units are *wrong* as theological readings — many are sound readings of the verse_text. The precise finding is narrower and falsifiable: **the per-verse LEXICAL fields do not, on their own, separate or ground 28 of the ~32 unit-distinctions; the distinctions live in the verse text + verb morphology + frame.** That is a statement about what the lexical evidence base can carry, which is exactly the mandate. The honest counter-weight: the unit files **already flag this themselves** (the ⚖ BORDER / "deferred to §10" / "is it a real fact or a tag?" markers are pervasive) — so the structure is provisional-by-design, not asserted-as-settled. The risk is that §10 synthesis *resolves* those deferrals by reading from the lemma-constants rather than re-deriving from sense/object/experiencer.
