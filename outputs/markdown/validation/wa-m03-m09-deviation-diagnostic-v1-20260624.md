## §2. Why M03–M06/M08–M09 are NOT the M10 failure

The M10 imposition was: an **object-kind ontology** that split a SINGLE term-family (ḥāṭāʾ #1–7) into many units by interpreted per-verse context, and read per-verse object-kinds off **lemma-constants** (type/faculty). The older clusters do the opposite:

- They **cluster by term-family** and define each characteristic by **its terms' lexical meaning** (root sense, register, gloss). E.g. M03: Weeping(9 terms)/Mourning(11)/Sorrow(8) — distinct vocabularies.
- Where they cite `type`/`faculty`, it is as a **grammatical property of the lemma** ("these are quality terms"; "the only group with a volition tag") — the *legitimate* use the M10 remediation explicitly endorsed, **not** a per-verse object-kind claim.
- Several are **bias-aware**: M04 G and M03 flag doubtful inclusions ("standing as a human inner-being characteristic is doubtful"). This is induction reflecting on the evidence — the opposite of the reflex.

So the deviation is **immaterial for six of the seven** at the characteristic-reasoning level. **The gut holds.**

## §3. The exception — M07 (Shame)

M07 deviated in BOTH generations:
- **Old set:** 16 shared shame-terms split across 6 characteristics by interpreted **aspect** (experienced-state / enforced-abasement / relational-dishonour / moral-evaluation / contempt-produced) — the M10 shared-term-split signature.
- **New set:** abandons term-grounding for **interpreted axes/dynamics** ("Felt shame at falsified trust", "Trust→vindication axis", "Honour↔shame reversal", "Propriety norms") — organising by conceptual dynamic, not by term.
- **Why here:** shame is a relational/honour concept whose natural divisions are *dynamics*, not distinct vocabularies — the strongest pull toward organise-by-frame. The overlapping vocabulary made the pull irresistible.

M07 warrants a **full cold-read validation** (the M10 treatment) before any onward use; it is the one cluster where "it made sense but it's not right" likely applies.
