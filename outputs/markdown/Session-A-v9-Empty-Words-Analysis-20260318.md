## Genuine Gaps — New Entry Recommended (11)

These words have a real and productive lexical presence in Scripture that is not covered anywhere in the current registry. These are priority additions to the programme.

### 12 · betrayal → recommend rename: **treachery**
**Primary anchor:** H0898 (bagad, to act treacherously; 51 occurrences)  
**Supporting terms:** H4820 (mirmah, deceit in covenant contexts; 39 occurrences); G4273 (prodotēs, traitor; 3 NT occurrences)  
**Rationale:** No adequate coverage exists. Betrayal as a relational breach of covenantal trust is a distinct inner-being concept from deceit. H0898 has a concentrated presence in the Psalms and prophets in the context of relational faithlessness. "Treachery" is the more precise biblical-lexical term; "betrayal" can be the registry heading.

### 25 · conformity → recommend rename: **likeness**
**Primary anchor:** G3339 (metamorphoō, to transform/be conformed; 6 occurrences)  
**Supporting terms:** G4833 (symmorphos, conformed to; 2 occurrences — Rom 8:29, Phil 3:21); G3345 (metaschēmatizō, to change form; 5 occurrences)  
**Rationale:** The inner transformation into Christlikeness is a distinct and high-value theological concept not covered by any existing entry. Links naturally with transformation (#161). "Likeness" is the more productive lexical anchor (H1823 demuth, G3667 homoiōma also relevant).

### 37 · darkening → recommend rename: **blindness (spiritual)**
**Primary anchor:** G4656 (skotizō, to darken; 8 occurrences — including Rom 1:21, Rev 9:2)  
**Supporting terms:** G4457 (pōrōsis, hardening/blindness; 3 occurrences — Mk 3:5, Rom 11:25, Eph 4:18); G5185 (tuphlos, blind, in inner-state uses); H6272 (atam, to be darkened; rare)  
**Rationale:** The darkening of the mind and heart as an inner-being state (Rom 1:21, Eph 4:18) is a significant anthropological concept not represented in any existing registry. Distinct from hardness (#74) in its cognitive/perceptive dimension.

### 38 · deadness
**Primary anchor:** G3498 (nekros in its inner-state sense — Eph 2:1, Col 2:13; ~7 targeted verses)  
**Supporting terms:** H4191 (muth in spiritual-death contexts); G2348 (thnēskō, to die, in spiritual uses)  
**Rationale:** Spiritual deadness as an inner state is a distinct anthropological category in Paul's theology. Related to hardness (#74) but semantically separate — deadness describes absence of spiritual life while hardness describes resistance. A small but theologically significant verse set.

### 82 · identity → recommend rename: **name**
**Primary anchor:** H8034 (shem, name/identity/character; 864 occurrences)  
**Supporting terms:** G3686 (onoma, name; 228 NT occurrences, particularly in identity and revelation contexts); H3045 (yada, to know — God's knowing of persons)  
**Rationale:** In biblical anthropology, a person's name is their identity — character, reputation, and relational standing before God. H8034 is one of the most frequent Hebrew terms and has a rich inner-being dimension (Prov 22:1; Isa 43:1; Rev 2:17). The concept of being known by name as identity is central to the programme's themes.

### 84 · image of God → recommend rename: **image**
**Primary anchor:** H6754 (tselem, image/likeness; 17 occurrences — Gen 1:26-27 central)  
**Supporting terms:** H1823 (demuth, likeness; 25 occurrences); G1504 (eikōn, image; 23 NT occurrences — Col 1:15, 2 Cor 4:4, Rom 8:29)  
**Rationale:** **Highest priority gap in the programme.** The imago Dei terms do not appear in any existing registry. These are foundational to the entire anthropological framework of the Soul Word Analysis Programme. H6754 and G1504 together provide one of the most theologically concentrated verse sets in the corpus. This entry should be among the first new additions.

### 101 · laziness → recommend rename: **sloth**
**Primary anchor:** H6103 (atslah, slothfulness; 15 occurrences — concentrated in Proverbs)  
**Supporting terms:** H6102 (atsel, sluggard; 14 occurrences — Proverbs); H7503 (raphah, to be slack/let drop; ~46 occurrences); G3636 (oknēros, lazy/slothful; 3 NT occurrences)  
**Rationale:** A clearly defined semantic field not covered by existing entries. The Proverbs concentration makes this a significant wisdom-literature inner-being term. "Sloth" or "laziness" are both viable registry headings; "sloth" is closer to the Hebrew term's sense.

### 119 · personhood → confirm under soul or add **being**
**Primary anchor:** H5315 (nephesh as the ontological living being — distinct from nephesh as soul/inner life)  
**Supporting terms:** G5590 (psychē as the individuated self — distinct from psychē as soul/emotion)  
**Rationale:** Partially overlaps with **soul** (#182) but the ontological dimension of personhood (the person as a whole being, not just their inner life) deserves explicit treatment. Recommend first confirming whether soul (#182) covers the personhood dimension of nephesh explicitly. If not, a dedicated entry for **being** (the living creature as such) would fill the gap without duplicating soul.

### 136 · resentment
**Primary anchor:** H7107 (qatsaph, to be wrathful/resentful; 46 occurrences)

H7107 has 46 occurrences and does not appear to be covered in any existing registry. The Psalms and prophets carry the primary verse set.

### 161 · transformation

**Supporting terms:** G0342 (anakainōsis, renewal/transformation; 2 occurrences — Rom 12:2, Tit 3:5); G3345 (metaschēmatizō; 5 occurrences); H2498 (chalaph, to renew/pass through; ~29 occurrences in renewal contexts)

### 169 · vulnerability
