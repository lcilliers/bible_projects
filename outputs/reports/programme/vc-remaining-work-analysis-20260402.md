## 4. Suggested Approaches

### Option A: Pre-filter high-frequency particles and function words

Before building batches, have Claude AI do a quick gloss-level triage of the 71 terms with 100+
verses. For terms that are clearly grammatical particles (gam/also, ad/till, atten/you),
function words, or physical nouns with no plausible inner-being use, approve bulk all-verses-fail
classification. Claude Code applies the set-aside inserts directly without needing to pass
the full verse corpus through a classification session.

**Estimated impact:** Could remove 5,000-8,000 verses from the pipeline immediately.

### Option B: Focused single-term batches for large-corpus terms

Terms with 200+ verses (about 15 terms) could be processed in dedicated single-term
batches. Claude AI focuses on one term at a time, with the option to sample rather than
inspect every verse (per the v2.2 all-verses-fail rule for large corpora where individual
inspection confirms the pattern).

### Option C: Continue standard batching

Standard 2,000-2,500 verse batches as before. Large terms naturally consume whole batches.
Safest but slowest -- approximately 25 more batches needed.

### Recommended: Option A + B

1. Claude AI triages the 71 high-frequency terms by gloss (this report provides the list)
2. Researcher approves bulk all-verses-fail for obvious particles/function words
3. Claude Code applies set-aside inserts for approved terms
4. Remaining large-corpus terms processed in focused batches
5. Standard batching continues for the smaller terms

This could reduce remaining work from ~25 batches to ~15-18, concentrating analytical
effort on terms that genuinely engage the inner being.
