## Mode assessment (#4) — result

**Mode is confirmed bedrock — the morph_code *is* the mode, needing only a parse.** Across the 472 term-occurrences:

| part of speech | n | what "mode" is |
|---|---|---|
| Hebrew **verb** | 120 | the **stem** is the operative mode — Qal 65 · Hiphil 30 · Piel 11 · Niphal 8 · Hithpael 3 · Pual 3 |
| Hebrew noun / adjective | 91 / 42 | nominal form — "operates as a thing / state", not a verbal mode |
| Greek noun / verb / adj | 48 / 11 / 5 | the Greek parse (case; tense·voice·mood for verbs) |

- **Verse-specific *and* mechanical** — the good combination. The same root appears as **Qal** in one verse and **Hiphil/Piel** in another (e.g. *shuv* H7725O: Qal in Neh 9:28, Hiphil in Psa 78:38), and the per-occurrence morph captures it. Mode does **not** need the read.
- **Expected answer** = a parsed label: Heb verb → `[stem] [aspect] [pers/num]` (e.g. *Piel imperfect 3ms*); nominal → `noun/adjective [state]`; Greek → the parse. Parse-to-label is a fixed morph-code → label mapping.

**Coverage gap (bounded, explainable):**
- **116 occurrences carry no morph_code — but they are T2 / function words** (particles, pronouns, "not", "from", "hand"). They need no mode.
- **38 are *content* terms lacking morph_code — and most are the M47 Constitution seats** (H3824 *levav*, H5315 *nephesh*, H3820A *lev*, H1320 *basar*, G4893 *suneidesis*, G2588 *kardia*) — M47 was created (2026-06-10) *after* the morph backfill ran, so those terms were never morph-tagged.

**Conclusion / what mode needs:** the field is reliable as designed — bedrock, per-occurrence, no read. The only work is (a) the **morph-code → mode-label** parse mapping, and (b) a **targeted morph backfill for ~38 content terms (mostly the M47 seats)**. No interpretive step, no signal-list, no sense dependency.

### Notes from this pass
- **Location is the cleanest** — its signals are a small closed set of seat + body nouns; a dictionary lookup on the verse's words gets most of it (silence = NONE). Best first field to operationalise.
- **Faculty signals split two ways** — the *act* verbs (see/know/remember) are in-verse signals; but a term whose **own gloss is a faculty** (a fear-term → affect) is a *direct* signal carried by the term itself. Both feed #7; neither is imputed beyond a present signal.
- **Attributed-to-God needs pronoun resolution** — "he/you" only counts when the subject is God; that's a small read on the subject, not a free judgment.
- **Relational overlaps with origin** — "given to / poured into" carries both a direction (#13) and a source (#6); the same node can feed two fields.
- These are **iteration 1 from 50 verses**; the lists grow as more verses are inspected and the residue (un-signalled cases) is examined.
