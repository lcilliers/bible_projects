## The differential is live ‚Äî same pair, opposite effect (exactly the researcher's point)
- **Deu 28:66:** assurance *absent* ‚Üí **dread present**.
- **Isa 12:2:** trust *present* ‚Üí **fear absent**.
Same axis (Fear ‚Üî Trust/Assurance), **opposite sign by presence/absence.** "What the relationship does"
differs ‚Äî and that difference *is* the finding.

## The fear‚Üîreverence shade gets RESOLVED by the read (what morph couldn't do)
`pa.chad`/Qal is one stem, but the verse decides the shade:
- **Hos 3:5** ‚Üí **reverence** (object = "the Lord and his goodness" ‚Üí devotion).
- **Deu 28:66 / Isa 19:16** ‚Üí **dread/terror** (object = threat/judgment).

read decides.

## Do groupings emerge WITHOUT being imposed? ‚Äî yes
From 14 verses, the findings self-sort into recognisable **operation-modes of fear** (not pre-set sub-groups):
1. **Dread from absence/threat** (Deu 28:66‚Äì67; `de.a.gah` scarcity verses) ‚Äî fear as the vacuum-state.
2. **Reverent fear ‚Üí devotion** (Hos 3:5) ‚Äî the worship pole.
3. **Fear displaced by trust/strength** (Isa 12:2) ‚Äî the resolution pole.
4. **Terror as God's instrument** (Exo 23:27; 15:16) ‚Äî fear deployed.
5. **Fear evoked by divine power/purpose** (Isa 19:16‚Äì17) ‚Äî the response pole.
6. **Guilt‚Üídread before holiness** (Isa 33:14) ‚Äî the sinner's fear.

These are **roll-ups over the findings**, not a structure imposed before reading ‚Äî which is exactly the test
the method checkpoint set ("do sub-groups fall out or must they be forced?"). **They fall out.**
