# Catalogue refit — redefined characteristic · drop redundant · de-force · two-layer split

> **Review · v1 · 2026-06-09 · CC.** Applies the researcher's four principles to the 189 tier questions:
> (a) read "characteristic" as the **redefined concept** — *anything that relates to the inner being, at the
> typed-term-in-verse grain* — not the distilled cluster (love/grace); (b) **drop redundant/construct**
> questions (dimensions etc.); (c) **de-force** questions that induce/extract findings that may not exist —
> rephrase to a neutral *check* (silence valid); (d) split every question into **L1/L2 (verse-grounded)** vs
> **SYNTH (read across verses / terms / clusters)**. **The L1/L2 set IS the verse-extraction** (the 13-field
> record); SYNTH is the roll-up/relationship layer. For researcher markup.

---

## (a) The redefined-characteristic lens
Throughout, **"this characteristic" = the inner-being thing being examined at the verse — a typed term-in-verse**
(e.g. *this `ya.re`-occurrence*), NOT the cluster. So "does this characteristic engage cognition" reads as
"does *this term, here,* engage cognition." Questions phrased about the *distilled* entity (the cluster's
overall nature) are inherently **SYNTH**.

## (c) FORCING — rephrase to a neutral check (silence first-class)
The tell is *"what does X reveal / suggest about Y"* and *"in what way does it express Z"* — these **prompt a
finding**. Rephrase to *"is there evidence of X here? — record it; if not, silent."*
| forcing question | de-forced |
|---|---|
| T0.1.x *"what does it reveal about God's nature"* | **Is the term related to God in this verse?** (yes/no) |
| T0.2.2 *"original design or response to the fall?"* | drop the binary; **note only if the verse states a purpose** |
| T0.3.1/.3 *"in what way does it express the divine image… what does presence/absence reveal"* | **Does the verse tie the term to the image of God?** (rare; mostly silent) |
| T2.x.2 *"what does X-location reveal"* | **Is it located at X here?** (the reveal is SYNTH) |
| T3.x.2 *"does it enable/deepen/bypass/impair faculty X"* | **Which faculty(ies) does the term engage here?** (effect = SYNTH) |
| T3.x.3 / T1.5.2 / T1.6.x *"what does the pattern reveal"* | SYNTH only — not a verse question |
| T5.x *"does suffering deepen/test/reveal/produce it"* | **Does the verse place the term in suffering?** (yes/role); the rest SYNTH |

## (d) TWO-LAYER split — L1/L2 (verse-grounded) vs SYNTH (across)

*across* verses/terms/clusters → **SYNTH**. The `.1`/"is it / which / does this verse show" sub-questions are
mostly L1/L2; the `.2`/"reveal" and `.3`/"pattern" sub-questions are SYNTH.

## Net result
- **L1/L2 verse-extraction fields** (confirming + extending the 13-field spec): `sense_applied · type ·
  compound · mode · constitutional_location · origin · faculty · attributed_to_God · purpose_equips ·
  typology_direction · immediate_response · produces_effect · relational_direction · suffering_context ·
  relational_implication · literary_setting · co-occurrence-array`.
- **SYNTH layer** (the roll-up + relationship/dynamic): the `.2/.3` reveals, the over-time effects, T4 bases,
  T5 arc, T6 cross-cluster — computed from the verse records, not asked at the verse.
- **Dropped:** T1.8, T2.8, T5.7, T6.6, T6.7, T1.2.3 (and defer T7.3).
- **De-forced:** the "what does X reveal / express" prompts → neutral *is-it-present-here* checks, silence
  first-class (state-not-induce).
