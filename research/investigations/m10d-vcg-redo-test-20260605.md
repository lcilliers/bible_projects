## What reading the actual verses surfaced that meaning-grouping missed

1. **The genuine inner-being core (G-A, the conscience) is currently scattered.** "We are guilty… the
   distress of his soul" (Gen 42:21), "pangs of conscience" (1Sa 25:31), "realises his guilt",
   "acknowledge their guilt" — the *felt/realised* guilt that actually *is* an inner state — sits split
   across VCG-01, VCG-05 and VCG-03. Reading the verses **isolates it** as the heart of the sub-group.
2. **A whole block is mis-scoped (G-F = VCG-04, ~18 verses).** These use the iniquity→**punishment** sense
   ("bear their punishment", Eze 4:4–6 etc.) — the **external penalty**, not guilt-as-inner-state. They sit
   inside a sub-group *named* "Guilt as inner-being state." Reading the actual text exposes the scope error;
   the meaning/keyword grouping (which kept them coherent as VCG-04 but under the wrong roof) did not.
3. **Two senses were merged that read distinctly:** *bearing/imputation* (G-C, "on me be the guilt") vs
   *atonement/removal* (G-E, "guilt taken away") — VCG-03 fuses them; and *conscience* (G-A) vs *forensic
   liability* (G-B) — VCG-01 fuses them.

## Verdict

**Reading actual verses produced a different and more honest grouping than the meaning-derived VCGs — at
feasible cost (one pass for 97 verses).** It did three things the early-meaning route did not:
- **isolated the true inner-being sense** (conscience/felt guilt) instead of scattering it;
- **caught a scope error** (the punishment block is not an inner state) that a label like "guilt" had
  papered over;
- **separated merged senses** (bearing vs atonement; conscience vs liability).

This is exactly the early-bias the phase-restructure predicted: a per-verse meaning/keyword set, formed
early, had grouped on the surface word ("guilt"/"punishment") and carried verses under a sub-group label
that the **actual text** contradicts. The fix — read the verses in context, together — works, and is
affordable.

## Caveats / honesty
- My 6 groups are **one analyst's reading**; some verses are genuinely borderline (Jer 2:22 G-D vs G-E;
  Gen 44:16 G-A vs G-D). That ambiguity is *surfaced*, not hidden (the method's intent).
- This is **one sub-group of one cluster** — a single data point. But it is a clean positive signal for the
  read-actual-verses approach.
- **Open question it raises:** if G-F ("punishment") is not an inner state, does it belong in M10 at all, or
  should the iniquity→punishment sense be its own (non-inner) treatment? A scope decision for the researcher.

## RESOLVED (researcher, 2026-06-05) — G-F is the external pole

See `feedback_external_pole_not_inner_state`. **Punishment is NOT an inner state** — it is the **external
pole** (an imposed consequence; agent + location outside the person) of a relationship whose **inner pole**
is conscience / shame / sorrow / repentance / felt guilt. Data confirms it precisely:

- All **18 G-F verses are `H5771I` עָוֹן "iniquity: punishment"** — the external sense of *avon*. The DB
  already splits the word: `H5771G` crime (162) · `H5771H` guilt (27, **inner**) · `H5771I` punishment (18,
  **external**). The sub-group re-merged the external pole under "guilt as inner-being state" — the error.
- **Disposition:** treat G-F as **external-pole — not inner-being core**; **cross-reference** to the inner
  counterparts (conscience reg 26, shame reg 146, repentance reg 135, sorrow reg TBC — *154 is "stupor", not
  sorrow*), rather than housing or silently dropping it.
- **Watch the discipline/correction sub-family** (musar/yasar, paideia) — it **straddles** (external act,
  inner formative telos) and is a boundary case, not a clean exclusion.

So the read-actual-verses method not only caught the mis-scope — the researcher's external/inner-pole frame
gives the **principled, reusable resolution** for every "border" consequence-word across the study.
