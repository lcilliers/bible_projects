# Which catalogue questions do L2 (per-term, per-verse lexical-meaning) findings cover?

> **Investigation · v1 · 2026-06-09 · CC.** L2 = the per-term, per-verse **lexical meaning** finding (what a
> term means *here*; mechanical first, API where needed). **Not cross-term.** Classifying all 242 active
> catalogue questions (T0–T7 + untiered C/F/L/M/WS) by how L2 covers them:
> **DIRECT** (L2's lexical finding *is* the answer, aggregated) · **EVIDENCE** (L2 is the primary per-verse
> input, but the catalogue answer is a characteristic-level classification/synthesis) · **BEYOND** (cross-term,
> dynamic, relational, or theological — above L2). Read-only.

---

## Headline

L2 is the **lexical evidence layer.** It **directly answers** the lexical tier (T7.1) and — thanks to the
morph work — the *kind* and *modes* questions (T1.2, T1.4); it **provides the verse-level evidence** that the
identity / constitution / faculty tiers (T1–T3) classify from; and it **does not touch** the theological,
interface, transformation, or relationship tiers (T0, T4, T5, T6) — which are exactly the cross-term /
dynamic synthesis layers you excluded from L2.

**Rough split of the 242:** DIRECT ≈ 17 · EVIDENCE ≈ 80 · BEYOND ≈ 145.

## What this means for the layering

- **L2 is the floor.** It answers the lexical questions outright and is the **evidence base** for the
  identity/constitution/faculty classifications (T1–T3). Those classifications are a **mid-layer roll-up**
  over L2 — not L2 itself, but not possible without it.
- **The cross-term tiers (T6) and the dynamic tiers (T1.5–1.7, T4, T5) are the layer ABOVE** — the
  typed-relationship findings (Fear↔Trust, Sin→Repentance) that you correctly said are *not* L2. They answer
  T6 (relationships), T4 (interfaces), T5 (transformation).
- **T0 (theology) sits above everything** as interpretive synthesis.

So the catalogue itself confirms the stack: **L2 lexical → (T1–T3 identity/constitution/faculty roll-up) →
(T4/T5/T6 relational-dynamic, the cross-term layer) → (T0 theological).** L2 carries the lexical floor and
feeds the next rung; it is not expected to answer the relational or dynamic tiers.

## One caveat
The catalogue questions are **characteristic-level** (universal scope — asked of each word). L2 produces
**verse-level** findings. So even the DIRECT ones are answered by *aggregating* L2 across the word's verses —
L2 is the per-verse evidence; the catalogue answer is its roll-up.
