**1. Refuted on its own terms.** 8 of the 41 verses have *no* active related term at all — at these, G3958's (orphaned) record is the only term evidence in the verse, so deleting it loses the verse entirely:
Act 1:3 · Act 9:16 · Act 28:5 · Gal 3:4 · Heb 9:26 · Heb 13:12 · Luk 9:22 · Luk 17:25.

**2. Refuted more deeply (the important point).** The 33 "covered" verses are covered by terms carrying *different meanings* — fear (G5399/M01), will (G2307/M29), Christ (G5547), judge (G2919), kingdom (G0932/M23), faith (G4100/M31) … — not "to suffer". **Verse-retention ≠ meaning-retention.** The *paschō* "suffering" lexical datum is unique to G3958 in nearly every verse: its nearest cognates G3804 *pathēma* "suffering" and G3844 *para* are themselves `delete_flagged=1`, and only G4841 *sumpaschō* "to suffer with" (1Cor 12:26, M03) is genuinely suffering-adjacent and active. So "another active term in the same verse preserves the evidence" does **not** hold for the term's own meaning.

**3. Data fault exposed.** Both G3958 `mti_terms` rows are `delete_flagged=1`, yet **41 verse records are active (df=0) and *all 41 are orphaned* (`mti_term_id` NULL)**. The term is registry-deleted but corpus-live and unlinked. This is exactly why `a_verses`-by-`mti_term_id` read **0 active** while the legacy `term_id` link read **41 active** — the count silently misses orphaned-but-live records.

**Implication for the unclustered-terms remediation.** The rule "delete an unclustered term with `a_verses=0` because its evidence lives under a co-occurring active term" is **unsafe as a general rule**: it (a) misses orphaned-but-live records (the `mti_term_id` NULL case), and (b) conflates verse-*presence* with meaning-*preservation*. The delete/cascade decision for the held 84 must therefore be **per-term** — checking the `term_id`-linked active records (not just `mti_term_id`) and whether the term's specific meaning is uniquely carried in its verses — not a blanket cascade.
