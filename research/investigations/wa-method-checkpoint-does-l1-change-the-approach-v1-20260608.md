## So: build on V3_2 — BUT the L1 work *did* change it (don't pretend otherwise)

The honest part: this is **not** "V3_2 unchanged + more data." The L1 work forced **one real shift in
emphasis** and several **additions**:

**The one real shift — the primary output moves:**
- **Was (V3_2 as written):** the goal is to *characterise each cluster* — L2 sub-groups → L6 characteristic
  assessment → L7 cluster synthesis. The cluster is the deliverable; cross-cluster (L8) is the tail.
- **Now:** the goal is a **typed-relationship finding for every verse** — the verse-relationship web is the
  deliverable; the **cluster-characterisation (L6/L7) demotes to a secondary roll-up view**, and the
  **relationship layer (L8) promotes to central.** The cluster becomes a **work-batch + a roll-up**, not the
  analytical end.

**The additions (all to the finding/record, not the spine):**
- thing-type on things (status/action/quality, from morph);
- relationship-type + effect on findings (the typed edge);
- the morph **stem as the operation-mode** ("method A → produces Y");
- the **three link-classes** as the synergy treatment;
- **meaningful-absence** (KIN-not-met / structural holes) as a finding-type;
- **verse-coverage discipline**: a cross-cluster verse is analysed **once** and shared (no double work);
  the cluster is the entry-order, never a boundary.

## Why NOT a clean-sheet "verse/relationship-first" rebuild

A pure verse-graph approach (drop all cluster machinery) **converges** with the above, because both make the
verse the unit and the typed relationship the finding. The only difference is whether the cluster is the
*processing batch* (keep) or merely a *derived view* (drop). But:
- You still need **some** work-batching of ~17k verses; faculty/cluster batching is as good as any and already
  exists.
- The **DB findings model, cross-cluster link tables, and dual-cluster-finding handling are already built** —
  a rebuild pays twice ([[feedback_no_rework_paid_twice]]).
- The cluster **roll-up is itself a wanted product** (one of the end-point's multiple outputs).

So a rebuild buys almost nothing the evolution doesn't, and costs the existing machinery.

## The one open question to decide *as we build* (not pre-decided)

Under the re-weighting, **does the V3_2 upper machinery (L2 sub-groups, L6 characteristic assessment) still
earn its place**, or should it be **lightened** to a roll-up over the verse-level findings? The risk is
forcing cluster structure the data resists ([[feedback_no_forced_structure_audit_surfaces_analysis_compensates]]).
**Recommendation:** keep L1 (verse establishment, now type/keyword/morph-equipped) and the finding as the
record; treat L2–L7 as **roll-up views built from the verse findings**, not as a structure imposed *before*
the verses are read. Prove this on the first faculty (Fear) before generalising.

## Verdict

**Stick with and build on V3_2 — yes.** The L1 work did **not** point to a different *approach*; it
**confirmed the verse-unit spine** and pointed to a real **evolution**: the **verse-level typed-relationship
finding becomes primary, cluster-characterisation becomes a roll-up, the relationship layer becomes central,
and the finding gains type + effect structure.** That is an enrichment and re-weighting of V3_2, not a
replacement — and it reuses, rather than discards, what's already built.
