## Key debate and thinking

### Sexuality — where it sits

Researcher challenged the positioning of sexuality as a constitutional layer alongside spirit, soul, and body. That positioning was correct to challenge — it does not stand.

Debate identified what Scripture is doing with the male-female construct:
- God describes himself as Father — gendered relational identity
- The church is the bride of Christ — the whole redeemed community in feminine relational terms
- Marriage as the type of the divine-human covenant
- Male and female roles used as types and analogies throughout Scripture

Conclusion: Scripture uses the male-female construct as a typological and analogical language — a way of expressing relational, creative, and covenantal realities that transcend the biological. Sexuality is a created analogue — a physical and relational reality that images and participates in a deeper structure of reality.

Researcher: characteristics are not categorised by sexuality, but their expression is heavily influenced by it.

Sexuality is not:
- A constitutional layer alongside spirit, soul, and body
- A faculty in the same sense as volition or cognition
- Simply a biological attribute like skin colour

DNA carries its biological definition. But DNA does not exhaust what sexuality is. Sexuality's full significance — including its typological function in Scripture — points toward a tier not yet defined at that point in the debate.

### T0 — Divine Image and Created Design introduced

Researcher proposed a new tier: the image of God, created purpose, why exist, the expression of God's character and creation in the human being. It sits above relational and directional influences on and between characteristics — but it defines, shapes, directs, and gives purpose to the characteristics.

This is where sexuality finds its home — not as a constitutional layer or a faculty, but as a created expression of God's relational and creative nature imprinted into the human person.

T0 positioning agreed: it sits prior to T1 as T0. It is present at every level of the programme but comes fully into its own in Session D, where the question of why all these characteristics function together to make up the human being is asked.

T0 name agreed: Divine Image and Created Design.

T0 dual function:
- At Session B level: what does this characteristic reveal about the divine image? What created purpose does it serve?
- At Session D level: what does this entire constellation of characteristics reveal about what it means to be made in the image of God?

### Memory as a distinct faculty

Researcher confirmed memory is distinct from knowing. Memory is not physical — the brain aids storage and recall but does not house memory itself. The dead remember in Scripture (Luke 16). The book of life is the ultimate record — worth being separately considered. Memory works on characteristics, is not a characteristic itself. "Remember the Lord your God" is the result of several faculties working together: action, memory, knowledge, desire, fear, love, thankfulness.

Conclusion: Memory is a distinct faculty — the temporal depth of the inner person, holding and retrieving inner-being reality across time.

### Imagination — part of creativity

Researcher confirmed imagination is part of creativity, not a separate faculty. Imagination is the inward movement; creativity is the outward bringing-forth. Two aspects of one faculty.

Revised creativity definition: the faculty of origination — encompassing imagination (conceiving what does not yet exist) and bringing it into being. Grounded in the image of the Creator God.

### Conscience and conscientiousness

Researcher confirmed conscience is close to conviction, sin awareness, guilt — different from moral evaluation. Moral evaluation as a faculty may be conscientiousness combined with action combined with volition.

Three distinct elements in the moral-evaluative cluster:
- Moral evaluation — the capacity to assess against a standard
- Conscience — the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction
- Conscientiousness — the integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action working together
