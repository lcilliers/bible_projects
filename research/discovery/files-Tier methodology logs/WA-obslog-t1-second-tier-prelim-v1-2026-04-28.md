## T1 — Essential Nature of the Characteristic

**Tier scope:** What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic — what is it, where does it originate, how does it operate, and what does it produce? This includes the emotional, cognitive, volitional, moral, and spiritual dimensions of the characteristic's essential nature, and covers the full spirit-soul-body constitution of the person as the field within which the characteristic operates.

---

## Second-Tier Components — Proposed

### T1.1 — Origin and Source
*What is the originating source of this characteristic — where does it come from, and who or what initiates it?*

Questions positioned here:
- What is the structural disposition of this characteristic — what kind of originating phenomenon is it?
- What does the characteristic identify as its originating source?
- What does the characteristic reveal about the origin of the human capacity to seek or receive it?

**Positioning notes:**
Q001 and Q017 are near-duplicates but subtly distinct. Q001 asks about structural disposition (what kind of thing is the origin); Q017 asks who or what is specifically named as source. Both retained as separate prompts.
Q018 (origin of human capacity to seek) sits primarily here but has a secondary touch on T4 (volitional). Primary home is T1.1.

---

### T1.2 — Structure and Form
*What kind of phenomenon is this characteristic — is it an act, a disposition, a condition, or a quality? Is it simple or complex in its internal structure?*

Questions positioned here:
- Does this characteristic describe a one-time act or an ongoing condition — and how are these distinguished?
- Does the primary verse name more than one function or dimension of this characteristic — and if so, what is the relationship between them?
- What does the primary verse reveal about the essential character of this characteristic?

**Positioning notes:**
Q009 (act vs. ongoing condition) has a temporal dimension but its primary question is structural — what kind of thing is it. Temporal detail is a sub-dimension of structure at this level.
Q042 (primary verse, essential character) functions as a broad characterisation opener for T1 analysis. It anchors the entire T1 enquiry before more specific questions are applied.
Q045 (multiple functions/dimensions) asks about structural complexity — how many things this characteristic is doing simultaneously.

---

### T1.3 — Modes of Operation
*What are the distinct ways in which this characteristic operates within and through the inner person — including any communicative, speech-based, or embodied modes?*

Questions positioned here:
- What are the distinct modes of operation of this characteristic in the inner being?
- Is there a discernible sequence or movement in the way this characteristic operates through the inner person?
- In what form does a key verse present this characteristic — and what does that form enable?
- What effect does this characteristic in communicative or speech form produce in those who receive it?

**Positioning notes:**
Q062 and Q063 form a pair probing the communicative/speech mode of the characteristic. These are a specific sub-mode of operation. They are retained here rather than isolated because the communicative form is one mode among several that the broader question covers.
Q015 (sequence of operation) sits here — it asks how the characteristic moves operationally through the person, which is a question about mode and sequence of operation, not about what it produces.

---

### T1.4 — What the Characteristic Produces — Immediate Response
*What is the first or immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?*

Questions positioned here:
- What does this characteristic produce as a natural immediate inner response in the one who receives it?
- What is the first inner-being response to receiving this characteristic unexpectedly?
- What inner state does a key verse identify as the first response to receiving this characteristic?

**Positioning notes:**
The immediate response is separated from the sustained or developing effect (T1.5) because the first response often has its own character — surprise, recognition, disorientation — before the characteristic takes hold. This distinction is analytically significant.

---

### T1.5 — What the Characteristic Produces — Sustained Effect
*What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time — what states, qualities, capacities, and orientations does it establish or cultivate?*

Questions positioned here:
- What does this characteristic produce in the inner being of the recipient?
- Does this characteristic produce enabling capacity in the inner being — and if so, what kind?
- What character quality does this characteristic produce in the inner being?
- What quality of inner-being stability does this characteristic produce?
- What orientation does the stability produced by this characteristic carry?
- What does a key verse identify as the concrete content of what this characteristic gives?
- What is the sequence of inner-being states as this characteristic takes hold in the person?
- What practical outcome does a key verse show this characteristic producing for the recipient?
- What does a recipient's response in a key verse reveal about their inner experience of receiving this characteristic?
- What inner-being state does the argument structure in a key verse produce in the recipient?

**Positioning notes:**
This component carries the most questions (10) and covers the sustained, developing effect of the characteristic as distinct from the immediate response (T1.4).
Three sub-dimensions are visible within this component:
- Produced states and qualities (what the inner being becomes)
- Produced capacities (what the inner being can now do)
- Sequence of development (how the inner being moves through states over time)
These may warrant splitting in a later refinement pass if analysis depth requires it.

---

### T1.6 — Structural Opposite and Absence
*What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — what inner-being logic or orientation stands against it — and what does absence or withdrawal of the characteristic reveal?*

Questions positioned here:
- What inner-being logic or orientation functions as the structural opposite of this characteristic?
- What does a verse about the withdrawal or absence of this characteristic reveal about its role?
- What does a verse about this characteristic in extremity reveal about its depth of operation?

**Positioning notes:**
The structural opposite defines the characteristic by contrast — it sharpens the positive definition by naming what the characteristic is not. This is a standard analytical tool in characterisation.
Absence and extremity (Q075, Q076) probe the same boundary from different angles: what happens when it is gone, and what happens when it is pressed to its limits? Both reveal the characteristic's essential function.

---

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception and Non-Reception
*What inner conditions enable, block, or distort the reception of this characteristic — and what is the inner state of the person who receives it without recognising it, or does not receive it at all?*

Questions positioned here:
- What inner condition or orientation is identified as the ground of genuine expression of this characteristic?
- What is the inner-being condition of the person who has received this characteristic but failed to recognise it?
- What does this characteristic reveal about the inner-being condition of the person who does not receive it?

**Positioning notes:**
This component asks about the inner conditions on the receiving side — not the moral or volitional conditions (which belong in T3 and T4) but the baseline inner state that correlates with reception or non-reception.
The distinction from T3 and T4: T1.7 is descriptive (what is the state?); T3 asks specifically about moral structure; T4 asks specifically about the will.

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

Questions positioned here:

Questions positioned here:

## Consolidations and Splits Made

**Splits:**
- T1.7 (original sketch: "conditions that open or close reception") was split into T1.6 (structural opposite and absence) and T1.7 (conditions of reception). The structural opposite is analytically distinct from the conditions of reception — it characterises the characteristic from the negative side rather than describing the recipient's inner state.
- T1.5 (original sketch: "what it produces") was split into T1.4 (immediate response) and T1.5 (sustained effect). The immediate response has its own analytical character.

**Removed:**
- T1.6 from original sketch (direction of movement) was removed from T1 and confirmed as belonging in T2. Direction of movement is a constitutional/locational question, not an essential nature question.

**Added:**
- T1.8 (dimension classification) — not in original sketch; required by the programme's classification system
- T1.9 (constitutional level) — not in original sketch; required to bridge T1 and T2 without conflating them
