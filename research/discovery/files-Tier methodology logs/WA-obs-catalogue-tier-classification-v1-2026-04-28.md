## Observations

**1. T5 and T1 are the largest groups.** T5 (Relational Interfaces, 26) and T1 (Essential Nature, 29) carry the most questions. This is consistent with the catalogue's origin in the study of Grace — a word whose operation is intrinsically relational and whose essential nature is the primary subject of the programme.

**2. T4 (Volitional) is smaller than expected.** Only 10 questions sit clearly in T4. This may reflect a genuine characteristic of the catalogue, or it may indicate that volitional questions are embedded within T1 and T3 questions in ways that make them hard to isolate without splitting.

**3. Inter-tier group is small (4 questions).** The researcher's instinct was correct — most questions fall cleanly in one tier. The 4 inter-tier questions all involve a genuine conceptual tension that requires editorial resolution.

**4. Gap confirmed — other spiritual beings.** No questions address the third relational interface (T5.5). This is a structural gap in the current catalogue.

**5. Q071 classification note.** Q071 (logical structure of a key argument) was classified in T8 rather than T5. Its primary question is about interpretive method — how an argument is structured — rather than relational content. The relational implication follows from the interpretation, not from the question itself.
