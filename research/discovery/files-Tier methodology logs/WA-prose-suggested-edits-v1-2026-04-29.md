## Edit 1 — Defining Inner Being

**Section:** `prog_purp_defining_inner_being` · Section id 5 · sort 5

**Current wording:**

> *Inner-being characteristics are the non-physical, internal states, capacities, and expressions that constitute a person's invisible life — encompassing how a person thinks, feels, chooses, relates, and orients themselves toward meaning, others, and God.*

> The definition deliberately holds spirit and soul together under the term "inner being". Inner being is the totality of the person's non-physical existence — everything that is not the body, encompassing spirit and soul together. Soulish characteristics are a subset of that totality — the specific functions and expressions that belong to the *psyche / nephesh*: thought, emotion, will, desire, conscience, self-awareness. All soulish characteristics are inner-being characteristics; not all inner-being characteristics are soulish. The spiritual dimension — the person's orientation toward God, the capacity for divine encounter, the operations of the spirit — belongs to the inner being without being reducible to soul function.

**Issue:**

The definition names "thinks, feels, chooses, relates, and orients" as five dimensions of the inner life. The tier framework debate has established that these are better understood as expressions of distinct inner faculties — perception, cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, and relational capacity — rather than five sealed domains. The current wording does not foreclose this, but it frames the five as the operational range rather than as examples of a richer faculty structure.

Additionally, the soul description — "thought, emotion, will, desire, conscience, self-awareness" — lists faculties as if they are soulish characteristics rather than the faculties through which soulish characteristics operate. The tier framework has established that the soul is the seat of the faculties and their expression into characteristics, not a list of characteristics itself.

**Proposed wording:**

Replace the soul description paragraph with:

> The definition deliberately holds spirit and soul together under the term "inner being". Inner being is the totality of the person's non-physical existence — everything that is not the body, encompassing spirit and soul together. Soulish characteristics are a subset of that totality — the specific functions and expressions that belong to the *psyche / nephesh* at the level of the innermost personal experience. The soul is the seat of the inner faculties — perception, cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience — and their expression into the characteristics the programme studies. All soulish characteristics are inner-being characteristics; not all inner-being characteristics are soulish. The spiritual dimension — the person's orientation toward God, the capacity for divine encounter, the operations of the spirit — belongs to the inner being without being reducible to soul function.

**Note:** The five operational dimensions ("thinks, feels, chooses, relates, and orients") in the definition sentence itself may be retained as a reader-accessible entry point, with the fuller faculty structure established in the tier framework prose that follows. A cross-reference to the T3 tier document is appropriate here once that document is produced.

## Edit 2 — Key Methodological Principles

**Section:** `prog_meth_principles` · Section id 13 · sort 13

**Current wording:**

> **Words are not forced into preconceived categories or assumptions.** Dimensions of the inner being are identified from the verse evidence; groups are formed from what the verses engage; findings emerge from the questions applied to the data. A category that cannot be grounded in verse evidence is not a category the programme uses.

**Issue:**

The tier framework debate established that the dimension vocabulary — Emotion (Positive), Emotion (Negative), Cognition, Volition, Moral Character, Relational Disposition, Vitality / Existence, Transformation, Agency / Power, Dependence / Creatureliness — is an open vocabulary, not a closed taxonomy. The current wording already states this implicitly ("dimensions are identified from the verse evidence") but does not make explicit that the dimension vocabulary itself is subject to revision as the analytical work deepens.

Additionally, the wording frames "dimensions" as the primary category. The tier framework has introduced a richer category structure — tiers with second-tier components — that sits alongside the dimension vocabulary. The prose should acknowledge that the dimension vocabulary serves the analysis without constraining it.

**Proposed wording:**

Replace the current paragraph with:

> **Words are not forced into preconceived categories or assumptions.** Dimensions of the inner being are identified from the verse evidence; groups are formed from what the verses engage; findings emerge from the questions applied to the data. A category that cannot be grounded in verse evidence is not a category the programme uses. The programme's current dimension vocabulary — the set of labels used to classify the kind of inner-being phenomenon a word names — represents the programme's current best reading of the evidence. It is open: as the analytical work across the full registry deepens, the vocabulary is subject to revision where the evidence warrants it.

## Edit 3 — The Observation Question Catalogue

**Section:** Any section describing the observation question catalogue and its five chapters.

**Current wording (representative — from wa-sessionb-analysis-readiness-v1_7-20260427.md reference and programme prose):**

The five chapters of the Session B analysis output are described as:
1. Word Characteristic Summary
2. Word Impact Description
3. Annotated Verse Evidence
4. Original Language Vocabulary
5. Connections and Research Pointers

**Issue:**

The tier framework debate has established that these five chapters do not map cleanly to the analytical tiers T1–T8. The chapter structure is an output structure; the tier structure is an analytical structure. They are complementary but not identical. The existing chapter structure does not include explicit treatment of:

- T0 (Divine Image and Created Design) — not yet surfaced explicitly in any chapter
- T2 (Constitutional Location) — partially addressed in Chapter 1 but not systematically
- T3 (Inner Faculties) — partially addressed across chapters but not as a named analytical domain

This is noted as a structural gap for future instruction revision rather than an immediate prose edit. The five-chapter output structure may need a sixth chapter or a T0-level framing section once the tier framework is fully defined.

**Action:** Flag for review when the full tier second-tier component structure is complete. No immediate prose edit proposed — this requires a more substantial instruction revision.

## Edit 4 — Session D Orientation

**Section:** `prog_instr_session_d` · Section id 51 · sort 122

**Current wording:**

> Session D works in the opposite direction from Sessions A, B, and C: where those stages are word-driven — entering through a Hebrew or Greek term and following the corpus outward — Session D begins with questions about the inner life that the programme as a whole is trying to answer, and asks what the full accumulated body of word-study data reveals in the aggregate.

**Issue:**

The tier framework debate established that T0 (Divine Image and Created Design) is the tier that comes fully into its own in Session D. The governing question of the programme — what the human being is, in the image of God, and what all these characteristics together constitute — is the Session D question. The current Session D description does not name this explicitly.

**Proposed addition** (insert after the current opening sentence):

> The governing question that Session D is designed to answer is T0's question: what does this entire constellation of inner-being characteristics reveal about what it means to be made in the image of God — what is the human being designed to be, do, and become? Individual word studies can surface facets of this question; Session D is where the facets are held together and the answer is assembled. T0 is present at every level of the programme, but Session D is where it comes into its own.

## Edit 5 — Corpus Prose Word Summaries

**Section:** Registry summaries in wa-corpus-prose-20260428.md — the short descriptive paragraphs at the head of each word entry.

**Current examples:**

> Contrition is the genuine, deep sorrow of someone who has done wrong and knows it — not the surface shame of being caught but the interior grief of having actually caused harm or broken trust.

> Forgiveness is the release of a legitimate claim against someone who has wronged you — the decision not to hold the debt against them, to cancel the account of injury.

**Issue:**

These summaries are well-written and do not require revision for accuracy. However, they currently describe characteristics at the level of their relational and experiential reality without explicitly signalling their constitutional location, faculty engagement, or place in the divine image. As the tier framework is applied, the summaries will become the T1 Definition entry for each word — the short-form statement of Name, Kind, and Boundary.

The current summaries function as Name descriptions. They do not yet carry Kind (the constitutional location and faculty encapsulation) or Boundary (the structural opposite). As the tier framework matures, each summary should be revisited to confirm it carries all three T1 elements in concise form.

**Action:** No immediate edit. Note that each word summary is the T1 Definition entry in embryo. When T1 framework work is applied word by word, each summary is reviewed against the three T1 elements and updated where any element is absent or underdeveloped.

**Specific observations on current four words:**

*Contrition (030):* Name is clear. Boundary is present ("not the surface shame of being caught"). Kind is implied ("genuine, deep sorrow") but does not yet encapsulate constitutional location or faculty engagement. Add: the spirit-level location and the conscience faculty as primary engagement points.

*Fellowship (062):* Name is clear. Kind is implied (shared participation) but the soul-level location and relational capacity faculty are not named. Boundary is not explicitly present — what is the structural opposite of fellowship? Isolation, or something more specific?

*Forgiveness (064):* Name is clear. Kind is present ("release of a legitimate claim"). Boundary is partially present ("decision not to hold the debt"). The divine image dimension is significant here — forgiveness is first a divine act, then a human one modelled on it. This T0 dimension is not yet in the summary.

*Goodness (067):* Name is clear. Kind needs development — goodness as settled character quality, located in the heart, engaging the moral evaluation faculty. Boundary is not explicit.
