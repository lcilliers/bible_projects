## agonia — Luk 22:44

**Reference:** Luk 22:44
**Term:** agonia (G0074)
**Classification:** CORE
**Genre:** narrative
**Speaker or subject:** Jesus, in the Garden of Gethsemane, praying alone before his arrest.
**Situation:** Jesus has withdrawn from the disciples and is praying to the Father. The surrounding verses record his prayer that the cup might pass from him, accompanied by his submission to the Father's will. The disciples are nearby but sleeping. His arrest is imminent.

**What the person does with the inner state:** Jesus prays more earnestly; the agony intensifies prayer rather than suppressing it. The inner state is directed outward toward the Father in a more concentrated form of address.

## Situation and Genre Summary

The CORE verses in the agony cluster are distributed across lament, prophecy, narrative, and apocalyptic genres, with no single genre dominating. Lament (Job, Psalms, Luke-Acts) and prophecy (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Micah) provide the largest contributions to the CORE set. The wisdom genre contributes indirectly through the lament framework of Job. Epistle and apocalyptic each contribute one CORE verse (Acts 20:38; Revelation 12:2).

The range of human situations in the CORE verses is wide. It includes: encounters with God or news of divine power that produce overwhelming inner anguish (chil in Psa 48:6; chul in Deu 2:25; chil in Jer 6:24 and 50:43); extreme physical suffering and proximity to death (sha.vats in 2Sa 1:9; basanizō in Mat 8:6); the interior of prayer under divine-human strain (agonia in Luke 22:44); the paradoxical orientation of inner fidelity within unsparing pain (chi.lah in Job 6:10); grief at relational loss or separation (odunaō in Luk 2:48; Act 20:38); eschatological torment after death (odunaō in Luk 16:24-25); the absence of expected anguish as diagnostic of inner hardening (chul in Jer 5:3); and the constitutive ongoing anguish of the wicked life (chul in Job 15:20). The commanded anguish of Mic 4:10 is unusual in the corpus — the inner state is here directed rather than merely experienced.

The voice distribution across the CORE verse set is mixed. First-person individual voice is present in Luke 22:44 (Jesus), Job 6:10 (Job), 2Sa 1:9 (Saul's reported speech), Luk 2:48 (Mary), and Jer 5:3 (Jeremiah addressing God). First-person plural (corporate voice) is present in Jer 6:24, Isa 26:17-18. Third-person observer voice dominates the Proverbs-wisdom frame of Job 15:20 and the prophetic announcements of Jer 22:23, Jer 50:43, Mic 4:9, Isa 13:8, Psa 48:6, and Deu 2:25. Narrative description is primary in Mat 8:6, Rev 12:2, Act 20:38, and Luk 16:24-25. The odunaō verse set is notable for its distribution across all three voices within a single term: first-person report (Luk 2:48), direct address from an observer (Luk 16:24-25 with Abraham naming the rich man's state), and third-person narrative description (Act 20:38).

## Peripheral Verse Flags

Hos 13:13 (che.vel): This verse is classified AMBIGUOUS rather than PERIPHERAL, but its spiritual content warrants Phase 2 attention. The birth-pangs are applied to a son's spiritual failure to present himself at the critical moment — the question of whether the agony belongs to the birth process or to the inner state of the one who fails to respond is genuinely open. Phase 2 should determine whether the pangs name an external circumstance or an inner-being state of crisis.

Act 2:24 (odin): This verse is classified AMBIGUOUS. The LXX confusion between hebel as pang and hebel as cord makes the primary referent of odin uncertain here. The resurrection context — God loosing the pangs/cords of death — may carry inner-being significance that cannot be determined from Phase 1 data alone. Phase 2 should address the Hebrew-Greek semantic shift directly.

Mar 6:48 (basanizō): Classified PERIPHERAL (disciples making headway against the wind). The physical exertion is the referent, not inner agony. No Phase 2 flagging warranted.

Mat 14:24 (basanizō): Classified PERIPHERAL (boat beaten by waves). Same; no inner-being referent. No Phase 2 flagging warranted.

No other PERIPHERAL verses in this cluster contain situational features warranting Phase 2 flagging beyond what their classification captures.
