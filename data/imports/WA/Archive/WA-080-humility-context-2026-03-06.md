## a.na.vah — 2Sa 22:36 / Psa 18:35

**Reference:** 2Sa 22:36; Psa 18:35
**Term:** a.na.vah (H6038) / an.vah variant
**Language:** Hebrew
**Classification:** CORE
**Genre:** psalm / narrative
**Speaker or subject:** David, in his song of deliverance recorded both in 2 Samuel 22 and Psalm 18
**Situation:** David is recounting the full scope of God's saving acts on his behalf across his life. The verse reflects on the quality of the divine relationship that produced his greatness.
**The inner-being moment:** The verse records: "your gentleness made me great" — a.na.vah/an.vah names a quality of God himself (divine gentleness/humility) as the active cause of the human person's greatness. The inner quality of God toward the person is what elevates the person. The divine a.na.vah is the precondition of human flourishing.
**What the person does with the inner state:** David in response to the divine gentleness attributes his greatness to that quality — the inner disposition of God toward him, not David's own power, is the ground of his standing.
**Bundling note:** 2Sa 22:36 and Psa 18:35 are parallel texts of the same Davidic song preserved in two books with a variant spelling (a.na.vah in 2Sa; an.vah in Psa). Both are consolidated into one record. an.vah is flagged "Exclude word This word only" in the source file as a variant form.
