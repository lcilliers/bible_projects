## 1. Work Completed This Session (since previous log)

### 1.1 Registry 103 — love (56 terms, 1,156 verses) ✅

This was the largest registry in the batch. The registry covered the full range of love vocabulary in both Testaments — Greek phi- and agap- families, Hebrew ahavah/chesed/racham clusters, and their negations (hate, lust, mercilessness).

**Key analytical decisions:**

**Greek love vocabulary:** agapao (571, 130v all relevant), agape (562, 104v all relevant), agapetos (555, 72v all relevant) — the core NT love cluster. Five groups formed for agapao (love of God by person; divine love toward person; love of neighbour; mutual love among believers; disordered love). Three groups for agape (divine love as source; love as inner virtue; mutual love in community). Two groups for agapetos (Christ as beloved Son; believer as beloved).

**Disordered-love cluster:** filarguria (love of money), filarguros (money-loving), filedonos (pleasure-loving), filia (friendship with world), filoprotevo (love of being first) — all relevant, all naming inner dispositions as character failures or distortions.

**Chesed (H2617A, 169 verses):** Three groups: God's steadfast love as defining inner attribute (001 — the vast Psalm corpus); human covenantal loyalty as mirror of divine chesed (002); chesed's failure or withdrawal as condition producing ruin (003, including Hos 6:6 — "I desire steadfast love and not sacrifice").

**H3045 (ya.da, 421v) parallel note:** The love registry has structural parallels with the knowledge registry — both show the same inner-being bifurcation between God's knowing/loving of the person and the person's knowing/loving of God as the ground of covenant life.

**H8130 (sane — to hate, 139v):** A single comprehensive group covering hatred as inner moral reality in both its righteous form (God's hatred of evil; psalmist's hatred of evil aligned with God) and disordered form (brothers hating Joseph; Amnon's hatred after lust; the last-days catalogue).

**Researcher decisions:**
- H1736 (mandrake, 4v): all-verses-fail confirmed — plant name, inner-being in surrounding narrative not carried by term.
- H7468 (reut — neighbour, 6v): all-verses-fail confirmed — 1 borderline candidate (Jer 9:20) set aside.

All five meditation terms relevant throughout. The siach/sichah cluster (H7879, H7881 — complaint/meditation) is notable: the same word covers both complaint and meditation, reflecting the OT understanding that the sustained inner address to God — whether in anguish or in devotion — is a single practice. ### 1.6 Registry 111 — mercy (15 terms, 150 verses) ✅

The mercy registry is the final and richest in mercy vocabulary. Key terms:

**H2603A (chanan — be gracious, 72v, 71 relevant):** Num 6:25 (Aaronic blessing) and Psa 51:1 (David's confession) as anchors. The vast petition corpus of the Psalms — "be gracious to me, O Lord" — represents the fundamental inner posture of the creature before the Creator.

**G1653 (eleeo — to have mercy, 28v):** Three groups — divine mercy as sovereign grace (Rom 9 corpus); the cry for mercy as inner anguish directed to Jesus (Bartimaeus, the Canaanite woman, the ten lepers); and the human act of mercy that receives mercy in return (the beatitude).

**G1656 (eleos — mercy, 26v):** The Magnificat/Benedictus cluster (Luk 1:50-78) forms the heart of 983-001 — God's tender mercy as the "sunrise from on high" visiting those in darkness.

**Propitiation cluster** (hilaskomai, hilasmos, hilasterios, hileos): all form a tightly related group around Christ as the mercy seat — the place where wrath is turned and mercy flows. Luk 18:13 (the tax collector's cry) and Heb 9:5 (the mercy seat) as anchors.

**G3628/3629 (oiktirmos/oiktirmon — compassion):** "The Father of mercies and God of all comfort" (2Cor 1:3) and "compassionate hearts" (Col 3:12) as anchors — God's inner character as the ground and pattern for the believer's inner disposition.
