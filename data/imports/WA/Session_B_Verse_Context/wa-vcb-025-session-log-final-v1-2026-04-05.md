## Analytical observations (for Session B awareness — not classification conclusions)

Registry 210 is emerging as one of the most inner-being-dense registries in the programme. Several patterns are visible across the 23 groups:

1. **The Hebrew/Greek parallel structure**: *mut/thnēskō* (verbal forms of dying) and *ma.vet/thanatos* (noun forms of death) show overlapping but not identical group structures. Both carry the "moral consequence" and "inner orientation toward death" groups; thanatos additionally has the NT-specific identification-with-Christ group and the "extreme inner anguish" group.

2. **The NT distinction**: between being "dead in sin" (nekros group 1), "dead to sin" (nekros group 2), and "dying with Christ" (sunapothnēskō, thanatos group 3) is linguistically precise and theologically important. These are three distinct inner-being dynamics in the NT's understanding of the person's relationship to death.

3. **The wisdom literature contribution**: Proverbs supplies a dense concentration of ma.vet and mut verses connecting death to folly, wickedness, rejection of discipline — building a moral anatomy of death's causes.

4. **Praise/remembrance as the defining inner-being capacity absent in death**: across mut group 4, ma.vet group 4, and Isa 38:18 — the dead are repeatedly defined by their inability to praise God. This is a consistent OT inner-being marker for the condition of death.

These observations are flagged for Session B — not drawn as conclusions here.
