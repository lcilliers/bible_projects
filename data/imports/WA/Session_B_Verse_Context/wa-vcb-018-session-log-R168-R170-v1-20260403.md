## Analytical observations

### Registry 168 — uprightness

**H3477G ya.shar — the dominant OT uprightness term (107 relevant verses, 6 groups):**
The group structure reveals three distinct modes in which ya.shar operates:
1. The *regnal assessment formula* ("did right in the eyes of the Lord") is the OT's primary mechanism for evaluating the moral orientation of a king's reign — a sustained programme-wide resource for studying how the will aligns with or departs from divine moral standards.
2. The *moral self-determination formula* ("right in his own eyes") is the Judges/Proverbs counterpoint — where the inner being substitutes its own judgment for God's standard. Judg 17:6 and Pro 21:2 together capture the diagnosis.
3. **Ecc 7:29** stands alone as the OT's most direct anthropological claim: "God made man upright, but they have sought out many schemes." This is an explicit statement of original moral constitution and its departure — significant for Session B and potentially for Session D synthesis on the structure of the human inner being.

**H5227 no.khach — high set-aside rate (17/24) as expected:**
The Ezekiel cluster (14:3, 14:4, 14:7) is analytically distinctive: "setting the stumbling block of iniquity *before their faces*" names the idols as the primary relational object occupying the inner being's attention. The "before" here is not spatial but orientational — the inner being is turned *toward* the idol. This is a significant inner-being use of a positional preposition and merits attention in Session B.

**H5229 ne.kho.chah — uprightness through its resistance:**
All four verses name uprightness through its absence, refusal, or impossibility. Isa 30:10 ("do not prophesy to us what is right; speak to us smooth things") is the most direct — a formal corporate refusal of inner-being truth. The cluster documents the inner being's capacity to resist what is right.

### Registry 170 — weakness

**The G/H suffix split is analytically significant:**
The split produces two entirely different theological registers. The G-suffix terms (inner weakness) are uniformly and richly inner-being. The H-suffix terms (illness) are uniformly physical — 0 relevant across 34 verses. This is not a classification failure; it is a confirmation that the term split is correctly capturing a genuine distinction in the semantic range of astheneia/astheneō/asthenēs.

**The Pauline weakness theology is concentrated and coherent:**
Across G0769G, G0770G, and G0772G, Paul develops a sustained theology of inner-being weakness: (a) weakness as the condition requiring the Spirit's help (Rom 8:26), (b) weakness as the site of divine power (2Cor 12:9), (c) weakness as the posture enabling solidarity (1Cor 9:22), (d) weakness of conscience as the inner-being vulnerability that communal discernment must protect (1Cor 8:7–12). Session B will have rich material across these terms.

**Mat 26:41 / Mar 14:38 — the spirit/flesh contrast:**
"The spirit is willing but the flesh is weak" is one of the NT's most compact inner-being dualism statements. The willingness of the spirit and the weakness of the flesh are both inner-being states, and the verse names the tension between them. This is significant for the programme's mapping of spirit-soul-body architecture.
