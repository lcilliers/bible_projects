## 3. Key analytical findings

### H1992 hem.mah (they [masc.])
All 189 verses individually inspected. All-verses-fail confirmed. The pronoun in every verse identifies the subject or object of predicates that may carry inner-being content, but the pronoun itself does not direct, intensify, qualify, or disclose inner-being content. Closest candidates reviewed and rejected: Isa 66:3 (nepesh is the inner-being locus, not hem.mah), Jer 4:22 (cascading moral indictment borne entirely by predicates). This outcome is the expected result per PF-VCB007-001.

### H4310 mi (who?)
157 verses inspected. 63 relevant (40% yield ratio). 10 groups identified after consolidation from 11 candidates (851-011 absorbed into 851-005). The interrogative pronoun is markedly more inner-being productive than hem.mah because rhetorical questions disclose inner orientations, challenge inner moral standing, and give form to existential states. 10 groups span: awe/wonder, terror/inadequacy before God, misplaced trust, the unknowable heart, helplessness before sovereignty, grief/pity, spiritual receptivity/faith, contempt/defiance, moral courage/standing, and optative longing.

**Session B flag:** The interrogative form is itself a mode of inner-being disclosure. This is analytically significant: the programme's treatment of grammatical particles that function rhetorically as inner-being instruments should be noted as a finding distinct from mere vocabulary.
