## 2. Key analytical findings — Registry 060

### H5414G na.tan
Relevance yield: 124 of 242 (51%). The term is inner-being relevant wherever giving expresses or discloses an inner attribute of the giver, directly targets inner-being capacities, or names giving as the expression of an inner orientation.

Seven groups covering the full inner-being range of giving:
- **872-001** — Divine giving as covenant faithfulness (the most numerous group: ~36 verses)
- **872-002** — Divine giving as compassion and love in response to need (~28 verses)
- **872-003** — Giving of inner-being capacities: spirit, wisdom, understanding, songs, strength (~6 verses)
- **872-004** — Divine giving as moral judgement — giving into consequence (~9 verses)
- **872-005** — Human giving as the expression of inner orientation: self-giving, votive, sacrificial (~17 verses)
- **872-006** — Giving/withholding of glory and honour as inner-being act of worship (~7 verses)
- **872-007** — Eschatological giving that transforms inner conditions (~9 verses)

**Defining verses:**
- Hos 11:8 — *How can I give you up, O Ephraim? My heart recoils within me; my compassion grows warm and tender* — divine inner anguish at the cost of love; na.tan at its most inner-being
- Job 1:21 — *The Lord gave, and the Lord has taken away; blessed be the name of the Lord* — human inner orientation expressed through na.tan
- Isa 61:3 — giving as inner transformation: ashes → beauty, mourning → gladness, faint spirit → praise
- Job 38:36 — *who has given understanding to the mind?* — na.tan directly targeting inner-being capacity
- Neh 9:20 — *you gave your good Spirit to instruct them* — na.tan of the Spirit as the supreme inner-being gift

**Session B flag:** The concept of divine giving as the material expression of inner character (love, faithfulness, compassion, moral response) is a distinctive contribution of na.tan to the programme's understanding of the inner being. The term reveals that giving is itself an inner-being act — it discloses the giver's inner orientation at the moment of giving.
