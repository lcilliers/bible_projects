## SBF-036-001

**Term:** H1793A dak.ka (contrite, mti_id 7553) and H1793B dak.ka (dust, mti_id 7557)
**Flag type:** Homograph sub-entry analytical question
**Raised at:** VCB-036 classification — 2026-04-13

**Observation:**
H1793A (contrite) and H1793B (dust) are homograph sub-entries of dak.ka that share the same two verse records — Psa 34:18 and Isa 57:15 — accessed via different mti_term_ids (7553 and 7557 respectively).

The verse_context records for both terms will reference the same verses with identical inner-being content. The programmatic distinction between "contrite" and "dust" as separate sub-entries raises the question: does this distinction carry inner-being analytical significance?

**The case that it does:** In Psa 34:18, dak.ka refers to the crushed in spirit — the inner posture of brokenness. In Isa 57:15, dak.ka describes the contrite spirit as the condition of divine indwelling. Whether the "dust/powder" semantic field of H1793B contributes a distinct inner-being nuance (humiliation before God, reduction to nothing as the ground of divine mercy) is worth assessing in Session B.

**Implication for Session B:**
Session B should determine whether groups 7553-001 and 7557-001 can be treated as the same inner-being characteristic seen through two lexical faces, or whether the dust/contrite distinction opens a genuinely distinct dimension of meaning. This affects whether the two groups merge at Session D level.
