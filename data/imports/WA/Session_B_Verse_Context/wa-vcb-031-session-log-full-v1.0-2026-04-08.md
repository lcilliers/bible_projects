## 5. Methodological discovery — characteristic-perspective grouping model

During flag register review, a significant structural issue was identified in the grouping methodology and a more fundamental programme-level insight emerged. This is the most important part of the session log.

### 5.1 The grouping error identified

The initial groups were term-centric: group descriptions named what the term *does*, not what the verse *is about*. Examples:

- "Hearing as the inner receptive faculty" — names the term's function
- "Ear as the inner organ of spiritual receptivity" — names the term's anatomical role

The correct formulation is characteristic-perspective: group descriptions should name the inner-being characteristic the verse cluster is primarily about, with the term's role stated accurately.

Revised examples:
- "Faith reception — akoē as the channel through which faith arrives"
- "Spiritual receptivity and its refusal — o.zen as the inner faculty through which divine word is received"

### 5.2 The two-type distinction

Working from VCB-031 data, two functional types of terms were identified:

**Characteristic terms** — name an inner-being state or movement that exists in its own right (fear, love, faith, pride, humility)

**Property terms** — describe how a characteristic operates, is received, expressed, intensified or resisted. Listen/hear terms are predominantly property terms. They qualify the *condition* of characteristics rather than naming the characteristics themselves.

The spectrum: pure characteristic → relational characteristic → property/act term → functional term

Key finding: **the same property term can relate to different characteristics across its verse corpus, and can relate to the same characteristic in opposite ways** (hearing as the channel of faith vs refused hearing as the expression of heart-hardness).

### 5.3 The vertical pass model

The programme's foundational challenge is that the data is many-to-many: a verse simultaneously involves multiple terms, multiple characteristics, multiple dimensions. The current pipeline tries to force this through a single sequential channel.

The insight articulated during this session: **the data does not like single; it is fundamentally multi-dimensional.** Rather than trying to keep the entire corpus at the same level of depth horizontally, the programme should slice vertically — go deep on a focused characteristic or term cluster, hold the rest as available context, and augment on demand.

This produces a **face model**: each analytical pass is a cut through the data from a particular angle. No single face shows everything. The underlying data is complete and consistent regardless of which face is being examined.

### 5.4 The set_aside_reason field

A key practical consequence: set-aside verses under the current model include a significant category of verses where inner-being content is present but carried by another term (reason: `wrong_face`). Under the vertical pass model, these verses are not permanently irrelevant — they are relevant from a different face. The `set_aside_reason` field enables rediscovery.

Query for rediscovery: `SELECT * FROM verse_context WHERE set_aside_reason = 'wrong_face' AND is_relevant = 0;`

### 5.5 Implications for the programme

- VCB-031 groups have been rebuilt under the characteristic-perspective model
- The vertical pass experiment will proceed using Jer 7:24, Rom 10:17, and Isa 55:3 as the initial test cases
- The governing instruction (WA-VerseContext-Instruction) requires a v2.5 update encoding the revised group formation rule
- Session B instruction will need to accommodate the characteristic-perspective model
- Prior completed batches are not immediately affected — they will be revisited when their registries enter Session B
