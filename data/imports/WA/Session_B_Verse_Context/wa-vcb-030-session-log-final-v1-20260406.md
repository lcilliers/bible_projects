## 6. Analytical observations for R15 (boastfulness)

*Observation only — interpretation is Session B's domain.*

The *kbd* root cluster reveals a striking semantic architecture. The physical sense (heaviness, weight) extends inward in multiple directions:

- **Toward the will:** *ka.ved* names the hardening of the heart — the will's resistance to God becomes heavy/weighted (H3513H Groups A and C)
- **Toward the moral conscience:** sin and iniquity acquire weight; guilt is experienced as a burden too heavy to bear (H3513H Group B)
- **Toward the faculty of perception:** the ears/heart can be made heavy — spiritual receptivity dulled or closed (H3513H Group C; H3513J Group A)
- **Toward experiential suffering:** anguish, divine discipline, and grief are measured in weight (H3513H Group D)
- **Toward divine encounter:** *ka.vod* (glory) names the manifest weight of God's presence — the same root that describes physical heaviness becomes the word for the overwhelming reality of God encountered by human beings
- **Toward human dignity:** *ka.vod* also names the honour/worth bestowed on persons — a dignity that can be received, cultivated through character, lost in judgment, or exchanged for lesser things

The connection between the registry word "boastfulness" and this root cluster is not immediately obvious from the primary glosses. Session B will need to examine how *ka.vod* as honour/dignity relates to the inner orientation of self-regard, pride, and boastfulness. The Group 1861-004 (honour as character-fruit) and Group 1861-005 (honour as social condition) are likely the most analytically relevant for Registry 15's governing theme.
