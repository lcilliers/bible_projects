**FLAG 1 — mti:1049 G2162 eufēmia [good report] (Registry 121)**

*Full verse text:*
2Cor 6:8 — "through honor and dishonor, through slander and **praise** [*eufēmia*]. We are treated as impostors, and yet are true;"

*Context:* Paul describes the paradoxical conditions of apostolic life — honour and dishonor, slander and good report exist side by side. The term *eufēmia* ("good report, praise") is one of four paired contrasts.

*Filter question:* Does "good report" through this term engage the inner being — or is it a social-condition label only?

*Assessment:* The verse is rich in inner-being content (Paul's sustained integrity despite external contradictions), but the term *eufēmia* itself names a social condition (what others say about Paul), not the inner state. Removing "praise/good report" from the verse does not change the inner-being content. Per §3.5 test: the term is a social-reception marker functioning as a contrast-pair element.

*Patch consequence:*
- Option A (AVF confirmed): Verse_record_id 34144 set aside — is_relevant = 0, no group. No other verse exists for this term.
- Option B (Override — include): Create group 1049-001 "Good report as one of the paradoxical conditions the apostle navigates — the social reception that neither defines nor distorts the inner integrity of authentic ministry." Anchor: 2Cor 6:8.

*Claude AI assessment:* Option A (AVF) is analytically the stronger call. The term is a social-condition label. However, the verse is in a passage of intense inner-being content and there is a case for retention under §3.3 (retain in uncertainty). The decision turns on whether *eufēmia* as a social-reception term passes the filter when embedded in an inner-being passage. My recommendation: **Option A (set aside)**, unless you wish to retain for §3.3 reasons.
