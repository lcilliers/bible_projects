## Pre-classification Survey: H1931 (he/she/it)

**Term:** H1931 (`hu`) — he/she/it — mti_term_id=849 — Registry 057-evil
**Verses:** 291 across 9 books (Gen 60, Num 60, Isa 60, Job 49, Neh 23, Hos 19, Mic 11, Nah 5, Hab 4)

**Survey finding:**
H1931 is a demonstrative/personal pronoun. Its 291 verses represent instances where the pronoun occurs in proximity to terms in the Evil registry — it is not itself an inner-being term. Survey of 35 sample verses across all major book concentrations confirms the following pattern without exception:

- Target words are pronouns, demonstratives, or referential particles (he, she, it, that, they, them, this, who)
- The pronoun functions as a grammatical pointer to a subject — a person, place, thing, or divine actor
- No verse engages the inner being *through the pronoun itself*
- Any inner-being content in these verses is carried by other terms, not by H1931

**Assessment:** All-verses-fail. The pronoun H1931 has no inner-being function of its own. It names no act, state, capacity, or expression. Section 3.4 (expressions as inner-being evidence) does not apply — a pronoun is not an act of expression. The inner-being content in any verse that contains H1931 is carried by other lexical terms.

**Researcher decision (RD-VCB006-002 confirmed):** Treat as all-verses-fail. No groups created. All 291 verses set aside (is_relevant=0).

**Programme flag:** PROG-VCB006-001 — H1931 is registered under Registry 057-evil. Its presence in the evil term inventory warrants a note to Claude Code: no verse_context anchor exists for this term; any registry completion check must not gate on H1931 having an anchor. The term carries no inner-being content; this is not a gap but a structural characteristic of the term.
