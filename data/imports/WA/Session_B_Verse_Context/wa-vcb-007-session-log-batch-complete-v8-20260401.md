## 6. Analytical highlights by registry

### Registry 057 — Evil
The two large grammatical terms H4310 mi (who?) and H3605 kol (all/every) produced significant inner-being content when functioning as vehicles of theological questioning, moral scope, or communal inner condition. Key: mi (who is this?) as the instrument of awe, wonder, and confrontation with the unknowable. Kol (all) as the quantifier of total inner moral condition. Both will be important in Session B.

### Registry 058 — Experience
H7200G ra.ah (to see) is analytically exceptional — 163 relevant from 273 total (60% yield). The semantic range from physical seeing to inner spiritual transformation is the widest of any single term in this batch. Job 42:5 (now my eye sees you) is among the programme's most significant inner-being verses. G1754 energeō (to work/operate) produced the key inner-being insight that both divine and corrupting powers operate within the inner person (Gal 5:6, Eph 2:2, Phil 2:13, Heb 4:12).

### Registry 059 — Faith
The highest-yield registry (97%) and the most analytically rich in this batch. The faith-faithfulness-treachery spectrum spans: faith as inner act of trust (G4102G pistis, G4100 pisteuō), faithfulness as chosen inner quality (H0530 e.mu.nah, H0571G e.met), treachery as inner-outer disjunction (H0901 ba.god, H0898 ba.gad, H4603 ma.al). Key verse: Jer 3:10 — treachery defined as the absence of whole-heartedness. Key verse: Mar 9:24 — I believe; help my unbelief. Key verse: Lam 3:23 — great is your faithfulness.

### Registry 060 — Faithfulness
H5414G na.tan (to give) — 124 relevant from 242 (51% yield). Seven groups mapping the inner-being functions of giving: covenant giving, compassion giving, giving of inner-being capacities, judgement giving, human self-giving, giving/withholding glory, eschatological transformation. Key verse: Hos 11:8 — how can I give you up? — divine inner anguish at the cost of love. Key verse: Neh 9:20 — gave your good Spirit to instruct them. Key verse: Isa 61:3 — ashes to beauty, mourning to gladness, faint spirit to praise.

### Registry 061 — Fear
43 terms; 68 groups; 543 relevant from 564 (96% yield). Analytically the richest single registry in the batch by term count. Key contributions: (1) the "Fear not" formula as a distinct inner-being category (~60 verses); (2) theophanic fear as the programme's fullest mapping of divine-encounter phenomenology; (3) the fear-of-the-Lord cluster as the inner-being foundation of wisdom, covenant faithfulness, and worship; (4) the trembling sub-cluster differentiating types of inner fear expression; (5) anxiety, discouragement, and sorrow as chronic inner fear conditions. Key verse: Psa 56:3 — when I am afraid, I put my trust in you. Key verse: 1Jo 4:18 — perfect love casts out fear.

## 10. Instruction update v2.2 — draft scope

**Changes to incorporate from PF-VCB007-001:**
Add Section 3.5 — Relevance filter for grammatical/functional particles:
> A grammatical or functional particle (pronoun, quantifier, conjunction, adverb) passes the inner-being filter if it: (a) directs inner-being content by orienting a question, command, or statement toward an inner-being subject; (b) intensifies an inner-being state or act; (c) qualifies the scope or universality of an inner-being condition; or (d) discloses an inner orientation through the manner in which it frames inner-being content.
> A particle does not pass if it is: (a) purely syntactic with no semantic contribution to inner-being content; (b) a social register marker without inner-being implication; (c) a temporal connector without inner-being referent; or (d) procedural filler.

**Changes to incorporate from PF-VCB007-002:**
Amend Section 6.2, Step 1a:
> Full inspection is non-negotiable. Every verse must be individually read and assessed, regardless of corpus size, apparent uniformity of use, or grammatical/functional character of the term. All-verses-fail for large-corpus grammatical terms may proceed without researcher confirmation where the full inspection has been completed and documented.
