## Methodological notes — Registry 121

**Grammatical particles:** Both H0259 *e.chad* (one, 181v) and H0587 *a.nach.nu* (we, 61v) were assessed under §3.5. The "one" particle yielded 4 inner-being groups (covenantal oneness, corporate unity, uniqueness/cost, experiential weight). The "we" pronoun yielded 2 groups (corporate confession, corporate commitment/lament). The vast majority of uses in both cases were set aside as numerical/counting or narrative speech markers.

**H1984B/H1984H split:** The praise vs. boast senses of *halal* are handled as separate OWNER terms — 3 and 2 groups respectively. The distinction between directed-toward-God praise and directed-toward-self boasting produces analytically important inner-being differentiation.

**H3034 ya.dah:** The dual sense of "give thanks" and "confess" (acknowledge sin) is retained in 3 groups. The confession sense (Group 1029-002) is among the more significant inner-being clusters in this registry — it connects inner transparency, guilt, and divine forgiveness in a direct chain.

**G1391 doxa / glory:** One of the most complex terms in this batch. 4 groups distinguish: the glory of God as orientation-object, transforming glory, doxological purpose, and eschatological glory. Key anchors include Eph 3:16 (glory → Spirit strengthening → inner being) and 2Cor 3:18 (glory transformation).
