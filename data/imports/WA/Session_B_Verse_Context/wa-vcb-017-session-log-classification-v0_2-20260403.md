Five pronoun forms (359 + 57 + 234 + 359 + 14 = 1,023 verses) all confirmed all-verses-fail under Section 3.5. Pronouns are purely referential and satisfy no part of the relevance filter. Exception noted: H5462 sa.gar (87 verses) produced 1 relevant verse — Psa 17:10 "They close their hearts to pity" — where the shutting function was applied directly to the heart.

**Reg 157 (temptation) — rich inner-being density confirmed:**
High yield as expected. 64 of 86 verses relevant across 16 terms. The peirazō/peirasmos cluster is thematically coherent — temptation as assault on inner desire and will; testing as revealing inner faith or hardness. The stumbling vocabulary (proskomma, proskoptō, ptaiō) confirmed as primarily inner moral/faith language in Paul and the Catholic Epistles.

**Reg 158 (terror) — mixed results:**
Terror terms split cleanly: terms naming the inner state of terror experienced by persons (bal.la.hah, chit.tit, be.ha.lah, be.a.tah, ze.va.ah, chit.tah, me.chit.tah) are relevant; terms naming peoples or lands as objects of horror/desolation (za.a.vah, chag.ga, sham.mah) are set aside because the inner-being content is once-removed (in the observers, not in the term). Interesting case: raq (particle) contributed 3 relevant verses including Gen 6:5 — "every intention of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually" — one of the most programmatically significant inner-being verses in the corpus.

**Reg 159 (testimony) — Psalm 119 cluster:**
The testimony vocabulary (e.dah mti=6547, e.dut mti=1163) generated a dense Psalm 119 cluster where testimonies are the object of inner delight, the heart is inclined toward them, and the soul keeps them. This is a significant inner-being corpus for the testimony registry — the relationship between the divine word/testimony and the inner orientation of the devotee.
