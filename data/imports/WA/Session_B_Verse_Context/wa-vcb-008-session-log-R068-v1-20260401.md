## Classification Decisions

### G5483 — two groups
(1) God's gracious giving and forgiving — divine act grounded in the gift of the Son. (2) Human act of forgiving — horizontal release patterned on divine forgiveness received.
Set aside: physical healing (Luk 7:21), legal/procedural releases (Act 3:14, 25:11, 25:16).

### G5485 — four groups
(1) Grace as sovereign unmerited divine disposition — justification, salvation, calling.
(2) Grace as relational sphere and power — standing in grace, inner transformation, identity.
(3) Grace as relational favour — goodwill between persons; Lukan favour vocabulary.
(4) Grace as animating power of apostolic mission — the word of grace, commendation to grace, thankfulness.
Set aside: procedural political favours (Luk 17:9, Act 24:27, 25:3, 25:9).

### G5487 — single group
Both verses name the divine act of bestowing grace as a defining characterisation of the recipient.

### H2580 — three groups
(1) Divine relational favour — God's inner disposition toward those he knows, producing standing and access. Anchor: Exo 33:17 (divine knowing as ground of favour) + Zec 12:10 (eschatological outpouring of grace as Spirit).
(2) Relational favour between persons — the inner-relational standing sought and received across the Genesis-through-Esther narrative.
(3) Favour as inner character quality — the gracious disposition expressed in speech, wisdom, and conduct.
Set aside: ornamental/physical metaphors in Proverbs (garland, adornment, graceful doe), Ecc 9:11 (wisdom observation about unpredictable outcomes), Nah 3:4 (seductive physical attractiveness).

### H8469 — single group, two anchors
All 18 verses engage the inner act of earnest supplication. Two anchors: Dan 9:3 (most complete statement of supplicating posture) and Zec 12:10 (the supplicating spirit as eschatological divine gift — the inner disposition of pleading given by God).

**Note:** Zec 12:10 appears in both H2580 and H8469 with different verse_record_ids (167055 and 167194 respectively) — these are distinct term occurrences in the same verse. This is expected; the patch builder should treat them as separate records.
