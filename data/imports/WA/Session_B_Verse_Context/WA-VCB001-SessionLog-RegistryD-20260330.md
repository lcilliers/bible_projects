## 5. Key analytical findings — Registry 006

**Finding 1 — Anointing as Spirit-reception:** The NT anointing terms (*chriō*, *chrisma*, *christos*) consistently name anointing as the occasion of Spirit-endowment. The anointing characteristic in the NT is intrinsically pneumatological — the outer act produces an inner Spirit-presence and inner capacity.

**Finding 2 — Anointing as inner perception:** *Enchriō* (Rev 3:18 — anoint the eyes of Laodicea), *epichriō* (Joh 9:6/11 — anoint the blind man's eyes), and *chrisma* (1Jo 2:27 — anointing teaches from within) all connect anointing to the gift or restoration of inner perception/knowledge. The anointing characteristic enables seeing and knowing.

**Finding 3 — Anointing and its inverse:** The *exaleifō* (blotting out) and *sha.men* (making fat/dull) terms name the inverse of anointing: erasure of the inner record (forgiveness), and dulling of the inner heart (judgment). The *sukh* terms name the deliberate absence of anointing as spiritual practice (mourning, fasting). The anointing characteristic is defined by its presence, its absence, and its inverse.

**Finding 4 — Anointing passes through death:** *Christos* is anointed not for immediate glory but for suffering and death (Luk 4:18 — poor, captive, blind; Mar 14:8 — burial anointing; 1Cor 15:3 — Christ died). The defining trajectory of the anointing characteristic in its NT fulfilment is death before exaltation. This inverts the expected OT pattern.

**Finding 5 — Corporate dimension:** The anointed person's inner condition has corporate consequences (Lev 4:3 — priest's sin brings guilt on people; 1Sa 16:13 — Spirit rushes upon David in the midst of his brothers). Anointing is not a private inner event.

**Analytical anomaly — G0220 aleimma registration error:** All 11 verses assigned to *aleimma* (anointing) are in fact "rooster crowed" narratives. This is a confirmed registration error requiring Claude Code correction.
