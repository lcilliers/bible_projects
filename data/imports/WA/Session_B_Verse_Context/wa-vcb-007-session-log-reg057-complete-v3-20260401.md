## 4. Key analytical findings — Registry 057

### H2161 za.mam / H4209 me.zim.mah (plan / plot)
Two related terms covering the inner act of purposeful intentionality — from human malicious scheming to divine resolute purpose. The same roots apply across the widest moral range. Flag for Session B: resolved intentionality as a shared inner-being category; the concept of purposeful inner planning spans human and divine subjects.

### H7562 re.sha (wickedness)
Wickedness as personal inner moral quality (Group 846-001) and wickedness expressed outwardly in conduct (Group 846-002). Section 3.4 applied — conduct passes because moral quality originates in inner disposition.

### H4994 na (please)
Inner-being relevant in ~25 of 195 verses. The particle is relevant only where request becomes plea — the social act of asking becomes the outer expression of an inner condition. Two groups: entreaty to God (845-001) and urgent entreaty between persons (845-002). Section 3.4 applied throughout.

### H1992 hem.mah (they)
All 189 verses set aside. The referential pronoun identifies inner-being agents but does not itself direct, intensify, or qualify inner-being content. Expected outcome per PF-VCB007-001.

### H4310 mi (who?)
63 of 157 verses relevant (40%). The interrogative is itself an inner-being instrument: rhetorical questions disclose inner orientations, challenge inner moral standing, and give form to existential states. 10 groups spanning the full range of inner orientations disclosed through the question form. Session B flag: the interrogative form as a mode of inner-being disclosure.

### H3605 kol (all)
41 of 285 verses relevant (14%). The universal quantifier is inner-being relevant where it makes claims about inner states total or universal — leaving no part of the inner life exempt. 7 groups. Anchor verse: Gen 6:5 — 'every intention of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually.' This is kol at its most analytically powerful as an inner-being qualifier.
