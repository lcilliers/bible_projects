## Key Analytical Observations (cross-registry)

1. **Soul vocabulary (Reg 182)** is the largest and most analytically rich registry. nepesh and psuchē span the full range of inner-being experience — from the soul as essential person, through desire, longing, anguish, worship, covenant bond, and post-mortem continuity. The appetite sub-sense (H5315L) is the most directly inner-being of the nepesh variants.

2. **Spirit vocabulary (Reg 184)** is dominated by the Holy Spirit corpus. The human spirit (pneuma of the person) appears in distinct groups — willing spirit vs. weak flesh, the spirit that prays and worships, the spirit troubled in the presence of God. The neshamah (breath) terms connect breath directly to understanding, moral awareness, and the lamp-of-God function (Pro 20:27).

3. **Heart vocabulary (Reg 183)** — already classified in the session that preceded this context window — centred on lev/levav as the comprehensive inner person, including will, mind, memory, and emotional depth.

4. **Yielding vocabulary (Reg 180)** — the paradidōmi cluster is exceptionally rich: 12 groups covering betrayal, passion, self-giving, apostolic tradition, stewardship, judicial delivering over, and the inner act of self-entrustment to God.

5. **Worth and holiness** — the sha.vah/e.rekh cluster engages value-assessment as an inner disposition. The incomparability of God and the soul's comparative self-understanding are significant.

6. **Flesh/spirit contrast** (Reg 185) — confirmed at multiple levels: inner moral condition, the fleshly vs. spiritual person, passions of the flesh at war with the soul.
