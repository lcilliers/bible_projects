## Structural Observations — Registry 43

**The anatomy of desire revealed across 34 terms:**

Registry 43 has produced one of the programme's richest single-registry datasets. Across the 34 terms, the inner-being faculty of desire is revealed as:

1. **Directionally neutral** — desire is not inherently good or bad. Its moral character is determined entirely by its object and orientation. The same faculty that seeks God (ba.qash 486-001, zēteō 511-001) also craves what destroys (hav.vah 464-001, orexis 497-001).

2. **Multi-modal** — desire expresses itself as longing (ka.saph, sha.aph), asking (sha.al, be.a), choosing (ba.char), seeking (zēteō, ba.qash), willing (thelō, thelēma, tse.va), delighting (che.phets, cha.phets, ra.tson), craving (ta.a.vah, a.vah), and longing-as-force (te.shu.qah, cha.shaq). These are not synonyms but distinguishably different inner movements within the same faculty.

3. **Bidirectional** — the same terms name both human and divine desire. God seeks, chooses, wills, desires, and delights. This is significant inner-being data about God's personhood. Session B and Session D will need to address whether the programme's characterisation of the human inner being is reflexively illuminated by these divine parallels.

4. **Structurally convergent across Hebrew and Greek** — G2212 (zēteō) and H1245 (ba.qash) produced near-identical six-group architectures. G2309/G2307 (thelō/thelēma) parallel H6634/H7522 (tse.va/ra.tson) in showing will as directed, accepted, or denied. This convergence is itself inner-being data.

5. **Provisional hold clusters** — H2530B and H2836B produce 28 provisional hold verse records. These must be handled in patch construction.

**Programme flag (Session D):** The divine desire data accumulated in Registry 43 (groups naming God's own inner willing, choosing, desiring, and delighting) constitutes a significant sub-dataset. Session D synthesis should explicitly address the relationship between human and divine desire as characterised by this registry.
