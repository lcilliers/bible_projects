## Analytical observations

### Registry 171 — whoredom

**The literal/metaphorical distinction resolves cleanly:**
Both literal and metaphorical fornication are inner-being. The 13 set-asides are biographical status labels (Rahab the prostitute in narrative, Jephthah's parentage) or incidental references where the term names a category without engaging the inner-being act or disposition. The filter held consistently throughout.

**Key anchors of programme significance:**

**Num 15:39** (H2181, group 1221-003): "not to follow after your own heart and your own eyes, which you are inclined to **whore** after." This is the clearest OT text linking za.nah directly to the inner mechanism of desire — heart and eyes as the organs of spiritual whoredom. The verse places fornication explicitly inside the inner-being, naming the inclination as the root. This is a prime candidate for Session B cross-reference work.

**Hos 4:12** (H2181, group 1221-002): "a **spirit of whoredom** has led them astray." Whoredom as a spirit — an inner driving disposition — is one of the OT's most concentrated inner-being formulations. The spirit of whoredom is personified as an inner power that leads the will away from God.

**Hos 5:4** (H2183, group 1220-001): "the **spirit of whoredom is within them**, and they know not the Lord." The "within them" (be.qir.bam) names the interiority explicitly. The link between this inner disposition and the cognitive failure to "know the Lord" is significant for Session B analysis of how inner conditions impair inner faculties.

**Eze 6:9** (H2181, group 1221-005): "I have been broken over their **whoring heart** that has departed from me and over their **eyes that go whoring** after their idols." The "whoring heart" is the programme's most direct OT naming of the inner being as the site of spiritual infidelity. The parallel with eyes names a dual mechanism: heart (inner orientation) and eyes (inner perception directed outward toward idols).

**Hos 4:11** (H2184, group 1222-002): "whoredom, wine, and new wine, which **take away the understanding**." This verse links fornication directly to the impairment of inner cognitive capacity. The mechanism — whoredom depletes understanding — is significant for the programme's mapping of how sin affects inner faculties.

### Registry 172 — wickedness

**H7563 ra.sha corpus (178 relevant / 180 verses): the dominant wickedness vocabulary:**
The corpus is coherent and rich. The five groups map distinct theological functions:
- The wicked's **inner character** (thoughts, heart, desires) — the concentrated inner-being description of the type
- The **way** of the wicked and the call to forsake it — orientation as inner-being movement
- **Transgression speaking deep in his heart** (Psa 36:1) — one of the OT's most striking images of inner-being moral anatomy
- The **righteous/wicked antithesis** — the structural moral division in Psalms and Proverbs
- **Ezekiel's turning theology** — the wicked person's capacity to turn, the theological assertion that God has no pleasure in the death of the wicked

**Psa 36:1 requires separate group status:**
"Transgression speaks to the wicked deep in his heart" — this image of transgression as an inner voice occupying the deep interior of the wicked person is unique. It names the moral inner-being as a site where evil has taken residence and speaks. This is a significant datum for Session B analysis of how wickedness structures the inner life.

**Isa 55:7 as anchor for "the way of the wicked":**
"Let the wicked forsake his **way**, and the unrighteous man his **thoughts**" — the parallel of way and thoughts is itself an inner-being formulation: the way names the outer direction of life; the thoughts name the inner life. The call to forsake both names the inner-being as the place where transformation must begin.

**H3644G ke.mo (37 relevant / 54 verses): Section 3.5 working well:**
The comparison particle passes at a high rate when the comparison names inner-being qualities, suffering, or character (the inner-being mode of comparison) and fails when it is purely physical, geographical, or procedural. The "as yourself" cluster (Lev 19:18/19:34) is analytically significant — the reflexive inner measure for love is a programme-wide reference point.
