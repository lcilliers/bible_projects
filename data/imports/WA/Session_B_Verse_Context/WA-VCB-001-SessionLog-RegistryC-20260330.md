## 5. Key analytical findings

### Registry 004 — anger

- **Seven-dimensional anatomy:** Kindling, intensity, duration, direction, governance, legitimacy, resolution. Not a single inner state but a multi-dimensional characteristic.
- **Exo 34:6 (slow to anger):** Governing principle — anger and steadfast love paired opposites; anger transient, love eternal.
- **Mar 3:5:** Righteous anger and grief as simultaneous inner states; anger need not be sin.
- **Isa 54:8:** Temporal asymmetry — anger momentary, love everlasting.
- **Thermal metaphors:** Hebrew anger vocabulary consistently thermal (kindled, burning, heat). Session B to examine whether NT anger uses a different phenomenological framework.
- **H7264 ra.gaz:** The most semantically wide-ranging anger term — covers rage, fear-trembling, grief-disturbance, and provoking divine agitation. Hab 3:16 is the programme's richest description of somatic response to divine power.

### Registry 005 — anguish

- **Spatial model:** The *tsr*-root and NT *thlibō*-family share the core metaphor — anguish as narrowing; deliverance as spaciousness.
- **Anguish as prayer-driver:** Multiple terms name anguish as the condition that specifically produces the cry to God. Programme-level pattern.
- **Anguish and receptivity failure:** Exo 6:9 (qo.tser ruach — broken spirit) — anguish can close the inner ear below the threshold of response. Two opposing patterns; Session B to examine which conditions determine the direction.
- **Anguish as formation:** Rom 5:3 (*thlipsis*), 2Cor 4:17, 2Cor 8:2 — affliction produces endurance, character, generosity. The anguish characteristic serves a formative function.
- **Labour-birth as anguish metaphor:** The anguish characteristic is consistently named through a generative metaphor — anguish as labour that may produce new life.
- **16 AVF terms:** Highest count in any registry so far; reflects the breadth of the semantic net.
