## 2. Key analytical findings — Registry 058

### energeō cluster (G1754, G1755, G1756)
The three *energe-* terms together name a theological concept of inner operativeness — divine power, hostile forces, and human capacities (faith, prayer, love) as genuinely active forces in the inner life. All 24 verses relevant. Phil 2:13 (God working in you both to will and to work) and Heb 4:12 (the word active in dividing soul and spirit, discerning thoughts and intentions) are standout inner-being verses. Flag for Session B: inner operativeness as a distinct inner-being category; the inner being as dynamic rather than static.

### H7200G ra.ah (to see: see)
163 of 273 verses relevant (60% yield) — the highest yield ratio in VCB-007 to date. Ten groups covering: theophanic appearing (858-001), divine seeing of the inner moral life (858-002), seeing that triggers inner response (858-003), spiritual blindness (858-004), deliberate not-seeing as moral discipline (858-005), seeing God as transforming encounter (858-006), seeing as faith-act (858-007), prophetic/Spirit-enabled seeing (858-008), seeing as moral witness and obligation (858-009), divine seeing of suffering or pride (858-010). Three dual-context verses identified: Gen 6:2, Isa 35:2, Num 24:17.

Session B flag: ra.ah reveals that seeing is itself a multi-modal inner-being act. The term carries the full range of the inner-being's relationship with perception: from spiritual blindness to prophetic vision, from moral obligation to faith-reception, from the hiddenness of God to direct encounter with divine glory. Job 42:5 ('now my eye sees you') is the programme's defining verse for the culminating inner-being experience of direct divine encounter.
