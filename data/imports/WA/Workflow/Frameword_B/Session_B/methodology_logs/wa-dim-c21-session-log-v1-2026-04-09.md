## 8. Analytical Observations

**The Transformation / Death-and-Resurrection convergence:** Several nekros and thanatos groups in Reg 210 were assigned Transformation rather than Vitality/Existence — specifically those describing dying-to-sin (1378-002), dying-with-Christ (7427-003), and mortification (7422-001 → Volition). The cluster thus contains not only "transformation toward new life" vocabulary (Regs 134, 202) but also "transformation through death" vocabulary (Reg 210). This is analytically significant: the Pauline theology of death-and-resurrection appears structurally as a Transformation pattern, not merely a Vitality/Existence pattern.

**Two Dependence/Creatureliness groups:** Groups 1379-003 and 7423-003 (parallel Hebrew terms mut and ma.vet) both describe God's sovereignty over death as the ground of inner trust. These form a Hebrew parallel pair within the deadness registry that consistently generates Dependence/Creatureliness rather than Agency/Power. The automated label (Agency/Power) captured God's action; the analytical correction captured the human inner posture. This is a pattern worth noting in Session B: the same divine act can generate two different inner-being characteristics in the corpus (agency in the divine subject; dependence/trust in the human subject).

**Identity/Selfhood groups in Reg 207:** Two skotos/skotia groups (7432-001 and 7433-001) were assigned Identity/Selfhood — a dimension that has appeared infrequently in earlier clusters. The darkness vocabulary generates a unique "you were darkness / you are light" identity-level claim that is distinct from cognitive darkening or moral participation in dark works.
