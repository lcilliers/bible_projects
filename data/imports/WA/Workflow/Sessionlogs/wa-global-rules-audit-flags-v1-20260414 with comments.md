## FLAG-004 — GR-PROG-007 wording clarity

**Rule as written:**
> "The inner-being relevance filter is applied to the specific term's use in the verse, not to the verse's general theme or content. A verse about covenant renewal may use a given term in a purely legal sense with no inner-being engagement for that specific term."

**What it means in practice:**
When classifying whether a verse is relevant to a registry, the question is always: does *this specific term*, in *its specific use in this verse*, engage the inner being? Not: does this verse have inner-being content somewhere in it?

Example: A verse containing both *lev* (heart) and a legal particle. The verse passes the filter for *lev* because the word heart carries inner-being content here. The same verse may fail the filter for the legal particle because the particle's function in this verse is purely procedural — the inner-being content is carried by *lev*, not by the particle.

This rule is already operative in Verse Context practice — GR-PROG-007 makes it explicit programme-wide.

**Action needed:** Confirm the rule text is clear and correctly understood, or request revised wording.

This rule definition is wrong - and indeed has been the cause for many errors:  The rule should read:
When classifying whether a verse is relevant to a registry, the question is always: does *this specific term*, in *its specific use in this verse*, do one of the following: a) engage the inner being b) refers to a characteristic of the inner being in such a way that it qualifies, operates on or clarify the characteristic - the characteristic(s) does not have to be named in the verse specific, but it can be inferred. Is the specific term implicated in a inner being characteristic.
