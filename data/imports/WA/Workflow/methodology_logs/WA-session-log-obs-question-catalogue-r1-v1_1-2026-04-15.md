## Section 1 — Word Characteristic Summary

---

### Pair 001

**[OBS]** The word is defined as a disposition of a greater party toward a lesser — arising from within the giver, not from the quality of the recipient.

**Q:** What is the structural disposition of the word — where does it originate?

---

### Pair 002

**[OBS]** The word is described as a free, uncompelled choice — to give rather than withhold — not triggered by merit.

**Q:** What determines whether the word is extended or withheld?

---

### Pair 003

**[OBS]** The word shows up in four distinct forms in the inner life: as something received, as a sustained condition, as specific capacity, and as character quality.

**Q:** What are the distinct modes of operation of the word in the inner being?

### Pair 004

**[OBS]** The first form — as something received — creates real outcomes in the recipient: access, protection, standing, the sense of being known and held.

**Q:** What does the word produce in the inner being of the recipient?

### Pair 005

**Q:** What does the word produce in the relational position of the recipient toward another?

### Pair 006

**Q:** What does the word reveal about the disposition of God toward the human being?

### Pair 009

**[OBS]** The second form is a sustained inner condition — an ongoing state of standing, not a transaction that concludes. It is distinguished from the act of receiving.

**Q:** Does the word describe a one-time act or an ongoing condition — and how are these distinguished?
