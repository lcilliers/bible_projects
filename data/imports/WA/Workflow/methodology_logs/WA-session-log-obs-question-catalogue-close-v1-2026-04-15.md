## 1. What Was Done This Session

This session developed a method for extracting an observation-question catalogue from word study reports, tested it across three words (grace, forgiveness, love), and refined both the method and the catalogue in the process.

### 1.1 Method development

**Grace (Report 1 — v1 then v1.1):**
An initial extraction produced 115 observation-question pairs. The question discipline was then refined by four rules:
- No composite questions — one concept, one answer per question
- Word-neutral formulation — the question does not name the word or assume its properties
- Verse questions formulated as analytic probes, not summaries of what the verse says
- Etymology questions tease out the observation; the question cannot be the observation itself

The three-axis template was confirmed: every question about what the word produces or reveals is asked across all three axes — **inner being / God / others** — regardless of whether the word has anything to say about all three. Absence of an answer is itself data.

The revised Grace catalogue (v1.1) produced 147 pairs.

**Forgiveness (Report 2):**
The existing catalogue was applied to the forgiveness study. Each question was answered ANSWER / PARTIAL / SILENT / N/A. SILENT questions that should have an answer were flagged [SHOULD ANSWER]. Observations in the forgiveness study not reached by any existing question were identified, and 14 new questions were added to the catalogue (F-001 to F-014).

**Love (Report 3):**
The same process applied. 14 further new questions added (L-001 to L-014). Love produced the highest answer rate (85/117 questions answered fully), consistent with its position as the programme's most lexically and conceptually rich word.

### 1.2 Catalogue state at session close

The catalogue now contains:
- **147 core questions** (from Grace v1.1)
- **14 forgiveness extensions** (F-001 to F-014)
- **14 love extensions** (L-001 to L-014)
- **Total: 175 questions**

The catalogue is not a static checklist. It grows with each word. Questions added during one word's analysis are carried forward to the next word as standard questions.

## 2. Methodology Insight Captured at Session Close

### 2.1 The two-pass structure identified

Through working across three reports, the shape of the analytical process became clear. Session B's engagement with the data will require **two structured passes** through the question catalogue, plus a synthesis pass. The full process is:

**Pass 1 — Standard catalogue application**
Apply all current catalogue questions to the word's data. For each question, record the answer from the data. Note PARTIAL and SILENT results explicitly. Flag [SHOULD ANSWER] where the study is silent but the question should have an answer. This pass is systematic and complete — every question asked of every word.

**Pass 2 — Gap inspection**
Re-read the data in full after the standard pass. Identify observations in the data that were not reached by any existing catalogue question. For each such observation, formulate a new question using the same discipline (word-neutral, single-concept, non-composite). Add each new question to the catalogue immediately — it is now a standard question for all subsequent words. Record the observation-question pair in the current word's record.

**Pass 3 — Synergy and interpretive deepening**
Re-read all answers from Passes 1 and 2 together. Ask what the answers reveal in combination — what patterns, tensions, or questions arise from holding the answers together that were not visible in the individual answers. Formulate follow-on questions arising directly from the answers. These are word-specific interpretive questions — they are not added to the universal catalogue, but they are recorded in the current word's record. This pass is where the analytical depth specific to each word is developed.

### 2.2 The distinction between catalogue questions and interpretive questions

**Catalogue questions** are word-neutral and universal. They are asked of every word. They grow iteratively — each new word may add questions that are then carried forward to all subsequent words. They are the stable scaffolding of the analysis.

**Interpretive questions** are word-specific. They arise from the answers to catalogue questions — from the particular combination of data that a specific word yields. They are not added to the universal catalogue, but they are recorded in the word's own analytical record. They drive the deeper analytical work of Session B and the richer observations of Session C.

The distinction matters because: catalogue questions ensure no systematic gap; interpretive questions ensure no analytical shallowness.

### 2.3 The iterative catalogue as programme infrastructure

The catalogue is programme infrastructure, not a one-time deliverable. Its value increases with each word processed:
- Each new word tests which questions have answers and which do not
- Absent answers may indicate a gap in the study or a genuine absence in the data — both are informative
- New questions added from one word become standard probes for all subsequent words
- Over time, the catalogue converges on a complete set of questions that the programme has determined are relevant to inner-being vocabulary

By the time the catalogue has been tested across all five reports in this session, then applied to the remaining words in the programme, it will constitute a validated question set for inner-being lexicography — useful not only for Session B analysis but as a specification for what Session C must address.

### 2.4 Questions that emerged but have not yet been answered

Several questions appeared across all three words but were SILENT or PARTIAL in all cases. These are candidate areas for further investigation:

- **Seeking as a connection** (Q122–Q123): Not named as a primary connection in forgiveness or love, and only partially in grace. Whether this reflects genuine structural independence or a gap in the studies should be examined when the seeking word study is available.
- **Mourning as produced by the word** (Q035/Q136): Present in grace (Zechariah 12:10 — grace produces mourning as first fruit), partially in forgiveness (repentance-mourning deferred), absent in love. Pattern not yet clear.
- **First inner response to receiving the word unexpectedly** (Q032/Q054): Grace develops this (fear first), forgiveness partially (awe named), love does not examine the receiver's first response. A consistent gap.
- **Forward orientation of the stability produced** (Q037): Fully developed in grace (hope), underdeveloped in forgiveness, developed in love (eschatological). The variation itself may be analytically significant.
