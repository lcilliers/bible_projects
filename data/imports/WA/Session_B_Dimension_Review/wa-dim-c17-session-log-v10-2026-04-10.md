The atonement-prayer-supplication vocabulary of Reg 111 is constitutively about the creature's condition before God: guilt that requires atonement (3169-001, 3171-001, 3173-001), life that cannot be self-ransomed (3170-001), the cry for mercy from one with no other recourse (981-002), the humble plea before those with power (985-001, 985-002), and prayer as the expression of creaturely need (987-001, 987-002). The atonement system is not primarily a moral mechanism but a creaturely-dependence mechanism: it addresses the creature's fundamental condition of guilt-before-God.

**Two Divine-Human Correspondence groups (982-001 and 991-001):** These are significant and carefully distinguished. The mercy seat (kap.po.ret, 982-001) is the physical locus of divine-human encounter — the place where God and the human representative meet in mercy. The hilasterios (991-001) is the living fulfilment of the mercy seat in Christ — received by faith, not ritual. Both are genuine Divine-Human Correspondence because both define the structural point at which the divine-human relationship is constituted in mercy. No other mercy group reaches this dimension.
