## Phase A — Cluster Assignment Review Summary

**Coherence:** C04 groups the desiderative vocabulary of the inner being — appetite, desire, hope, longing, passion, yearning, craving. The desiderative core is analytically coherent and genuine.

**Semantic bleed identified:** 65 of 172 groups (38%) carry primary content outside the desiderative register, falling into five patterns:
- Life/vitality (12 groups — Registry 8 entirely): חַי/חָיָה family; zero desiderative content
- Volitional/choosing (18 groups — primarily Registry 43): will, election, sovereign purpose
- Petition/prayer (11 groups — Registry 43): asking, inquiring, petitioning
- Trust/refuge (12 groups — Registry 78): resting confidence vs. forward-straining hope
- Consuming/exhausting and divine wrath (6 groups — Registry 102): כָּלָה family non-desiderative range
- Other: compassion (2), defilement/illness/attentiveness (4)

**Cluster thinness finding:** C04 is not thin in group count (172 groups) but is thin in desiderative density. Of 172 groups, 107 (62%) are genuinely desiderative. Registry 8 and Registry 115 contribute zero desiderative groups.

**Thinness causes identified:**
1. Classification artefact: English registry labels (appetite, passion) attracted XREF terms whose primary semantic range is non-desiderative
2. Theological proportionality: genuine desiderative vocabulary may be proportionally smaller than volitional or spiritual-relational vocabulary in Scripture — to be tested in Session B
3. Possible programme coverage gap: desiderative terms may exist unregistered or in other clusters — Session D sweep required

**Recommendation:** No immediate reassignments proposed. Anchored groups (499-001, 509-001, 509-002) noted and not re-assessed per researcher instruction. Thinness question captured as Session D pointer. Registry 8 and 115 thinness captured as Session B findings.

**Researcher decision on Phase A:** Confirmed — proceed to Phase B. Anchored groups to stand unless another dimension becomes dominant. Cross-group interrelationships are a Session D matter.

### Phase C — Cross-cluster patterns and vocabulary observations

#### Pattern 1: Divine will vs. human will — a systematic correction

Twelve automated labels have been corrected across this session. The dominant correction pattern is groups where the subject is God (divine will, divine election, divine desire) that were labelled Volitional/Will or Spiritual/God-ward. The correct label for divine inner acts as theological attributes is Theological/Divine-Human. This applies consistently to:
- 456-003 (divine will as sovereign purpose)
- 479-001 (God's election)
- 479-002 (God's choice of a place)
- 480-004 (sovereign desire of God)
- 481-004 (divine inner desire)
- 502-002 (Jesus asking the Father)
- 513-002 (God's own inner willing)
- 514-002 (divine will as sovereign purpose)

This is a programme-wide pattern likely present in other clusters — any group where God is the subject of a volitional or desiderative act should be Theological/Divine-Human, not Volitional/Will. This is a Session D pointer.

#### Pattern 2: Misplaced trust and misdirected desire — moral register primary

A consistent finding: groups naming trust in inadequate objects, desire directed toward idols, or confidence misplaced in human power are primarily Moral/Conscience, not Spiritual/God-ward. The automated keyword matching was drawn to the spiritual vocabulary without registering the misdirection. Groups affected:
- 384-002, 390-002, 396-002, 397-002 (Registry 78)
- 464-001, 469-001, 481-003 (Registry 43)

#### Pattern 3: Hope as inner capacity vs. hope as inner act

Registry 78 reveals a theologically significant distinction within hope vocabulary: some groups name hope as an inner act (orienting, setting, waiting — Spiritual/God-ward), while others name hope as an inner quality or capacity (characterising the person, present or absent — Character/Disposition or Affective/Emotional). Groups 385-001 (hope as fundamental capacity), 401-002 (hope alongside faith and love), and 402-001 (expecting nothing in return) all name hope as a structural dimension of the person rather than an act. This is a Session B pointer.

#### Pattern 4: Moral disorder as the dominant frame for misdirected desire

The highest concentration of Moral/Conscience assignments in C04 comes from the desire vocabulary (21 groups total). Scripture characterises disordered desire — coveting, lust, idolatrous longing, morbid craving — consistently as moral conditions rather than merely as psychological states. This gives the moral dimension an unexpectedly strong presence in a cluster expected to be primarily desiderative or affective. Session B should examine whether this concentration reflects a deliberate scriptural pattern: desire unmoored from God is consistently described as moral disorder.

#### Vocabulary tension 1: Existential inner states

Groups 4742-001 (living in inner-being suspension), 511-006 (paradox of self-preservation), and 401-003 (hopelessness as desolation without God) all describe existential conditions of the inner person — states of being rather than acts or dispositions. The current vocabulary does not have an 'Existential' or 'Ontological' dimension. These have been assigned to Affective/Emotional as the closest available label, but this may be inadequate. Flagged as vocabulary question.

#### Vocabulary tension 2: Somatic expression of inner states

Groups 483-001 (straining, fainting) and 484-001 (panting, gasping) name inner states expressed through bodily urgency. 484-001 has been assigned Somatic/Embodied; 483-001 has been assigned Affective/Emotional. The distinction between bodily-expression-of-inner-state and inner-state-with-somatic-quality is not cleanly captured by the current vocabulary. Flagged.
