### Significant finding — 7013-001 (a.yil, ram)

During Phase B of group 7013-001, anchor verse 1 Sam 15:22 was read: *"to obey is better than sacrifice, and to listen than the fat of rams."* The inner-being weight of this verse is carried not by *a.yil* (ram) but by two terms used in comparison: H8085H (sha.ma — to hear/obey) and H7181 (qashav — to attend/listen). Neither term is registered in the programme.

**Researcher identified the root cause:** STEP Bible treats H8085 suffix variants as sub-glosses of the same entry, not as independently linked related terms. The Session A pipeline follows STEP's relational architecture and consequently does not surface sub-gloss variants (H, G, I) as candidates for independent extraction. H8085H was never extracted because STEP never presented it as a related term to any registered entry. H7181 was extracted, but its related-word set in STEP is identical to that of H8085G — masking the sha.ma/qashav relationship.

**Programme significance:** H8085H (sha.ma — to hear/obey) is one of the most foundational inner-being verbs in the Hebrew Bible. A verse as significant as 1 Sam 15:22 — the clearest Old Testament declaration of the hierarchy of inner-being orientations toward God — is not covered by any registered term for its actual inner-being vocabulary. The gap is not isolated; other suffix variant pairs across the programme may have produced the same omission.

**Pointers raised:**
- DIM-187-001 (Session B): explore what the valuation of 1 Sam 15:22 reveals about the hierarchy of inner-being orientations; the relationship between sha.ma and qashav as paired acts
- DIM-187-SD001 (Session D, HIGH priority): programme-level backward validation sweep required — for every registered term, check whether STEP sub-gloss variants were independently assessed for extraction eligibility

**Description revised** to: *"A.yil as the sacrificial measure against which the superior value of inner obedience and attentive listening is declared — the ram's fat named as less than hearing and heeding God (1 Sam 15:22)."*

### Reflection on classification oversight

The question was raised as to why this gap was not surfaced during Verse Context classification over six weeks of intense programme work. Assessment: The VCB instruction (§6.6.1) does provide the mechanism — Session B flags may be raised when "a term's classification raises an interpretive question that goes beyond the filter decision." Reading 1 Sam 15:22 carefully enough to judge *a.yil* relevant should have surfaced the observation that the terms carrying the inner-being weight were absent from the corpus.

The failure was one of depth of reading rather than missing rules. The instruction gave sufficient scope; the execution did not use it fully. The root cause is structural — STEP's sub-gloss architecture — but the analytical failure is acknowledged.

### Key analytical observations

**1. Agency/Power ↔ Dependence/Creatureliness polarity**
The most structurally significant pattern in C20. The same terms that name the exercise of divine power (Agency/Power, GOD) repeatedly generate their human counterpart — the inner posture of creaturely trust and dependence. This is not incidental overlap: C20's inner-being content is substantially organised around the recognition that all human capacity derives from and returns to God. The polarity appears to be a structural feature of the biblical account of human strength. This warrants Session B attention across all five registries.

**2. Relational Disposition as dominant mode in a strength cluster**
Relational Disposition appears more frequently than expected (73 groups, 20% of total). The cluster is named for strength and power vocabulary, but a significant portion of its inner-being content is about the *orientation toward* God as the source of strength — not the exercise of capacity. The *me.od* (strength/very) intensifier groups, the *oz* groups, the *che.zeq* groups, the *ko.ach* groups all consistently show the inner person oriented toward God as the grounding of their capacity. This suggests the cluster's inner-being contribution may be less about power-in-use and more about power-as-trust.

**3. Divine-Human Correspondence**
Two groups (6892-001 H6105A a.tsom; 6893-001 H6105B a.tsam) assigned dimension 11. Same verse set (Ps 40:12; Ps 139:17) spans God's thoughts and human iniquity in the same quality (vastness/uncountability). These two groups are also a programme data observation: two distinct lexical entries cover identical inner-being content — an artefact of STEP's sub-gloss architecture applied to extraction.

**4. Intensification phenomenon (me.od groups)**
Me.od (H3966) produced six groups, each carrying a different dimension depending on what is intensified: Volition (love), Emotion-Negative (fear, anger, grief), Emotion-Positive (praise), Moral Character (moral gravity). The same particle travels across all inner-being registers. This raises a vocabulary question for future consideration: is intensification itself dimensionally relevant, or is it always secondary to the content being intensified? Not resolved here; noted for future vocabulary review.

**5. Dimension correction — 2908-001**
Group 2908-001 (mis.rah, dominion — Isa 9:6-7) carried automated dimension Somatic/Embodied (KEYWORD_STRONG). This is evidently wrong — the group describes messianic governance as the expression of the ruler's inner character. Corrected to Moral Character in Phase C.
