## Vocabulary Observations

**1. Agency/Power ↔ Dependence/Creatureliness polarity:** C20 reveals these two dimensions as structurally paired. The same terms that name the exercise of divine power (Agency/Power, GOD) repeatedly generate their human counterpart — the inner posture of creaturely dependence and trust. This is not incidental: the cluster's inner-being content is substantially organised around the recognition that all human capacity derives from and returns to God. The polarity itself may be analytically significant for Session B.

**2. Relational Disposition as dominant mode:** Relational Disposition appears with unexpectedly high frequency across all five registries. Many strength/power/authority terms are used not to name the exercise of capacity but the *orientation toward* God as the source of strength. The inner-being content of this cluster is substantially about the *grounding* of the person in God rather than the exercise of power itself.

**3. Divine-Human Correspondence (dimension 11):** Two groups (6892-001 H6105A, 6893-001 H6105B) carry the same inner-being phenomenon — vastness spanning God's thoughts and human iniquity in the same verse set (Ps 40:12; Ps 139:17). Both assigned Divine-Human Correspondence / NONE.

**4. Intensification phenomenon (me.od groups 654-001 through 654-006):** The same particle (me.od) intensifies joy, grief, anger, love, fear, and moral gravity with equal force across six distinct groups. Each group received a different dimension depending on what is intensified. This raises the question of whether intensification is itself a dimension-relevant phenomenon or a modifier that travels across all dimensions. Not currently addressed by the vocabulary.

**5. Unregistered foundational terms (7013-001):** H8085H (sha.ma — to hear/obey) and H7181 (qashav — to attend/listen) are unregistered in the programme. These terms carry the inner-being weight in 1 Sam 15:22 — the verse that declares obedience and attentive listening greater than sacrifice. Root cause: STEP Bible's sub-gloss architecture.
