The cluster vocabulary spans a continuous semantic field: what it means to be morally whole, pure, true, faithful, upright, innocent, and sincere.

The cluster is therefore internally coherent in its semantic domain and will produce mutually illuminating Session B analysis.

### Boundary observations

**1. faithfulness (Reg. 60) — moderate boundary concern**

The XREF terms for faithfulness are grammatical and common verbs — notably H5414G *natan* (to give). The groups under faithfulness are almost entirely framed around divine giving, not human character traits. While divine faithfulness is legitimately part of the moral-character domain, these groups function more as divine-action descriptions than inner-being character observations about the human person. This does not warrant reassignment — divine character is rightly within the cluster — but it creates a distinct sub-domain within C10 that Session B should note.

**2. innocence (Reg. 90) — significant internal heterogeneity**

Registry 90 (innocence) accounts for 33 of 111 groups, including several grammatical/functional terms (H2005 *hen*, H2008 *hennah*, H2009 *hinneh*) that were classified into C10 through their inner-being verse contexts. These presentational particles have a wide range of uses. The groups include genuinely substantive inner-being content (self-presentation before God, confession, moral verdict) but also one group (5630-001 *hennah* — temporal duration of inner state) and several that feel more like framing devices than character terms. This does not indicate misassignment — the verse context classifications were the appropriate filter — but it does mean that innocence is a highly heterogeneous registry that will need careful handling in Phase B.

**3. truthfulness (Reg. 164) — vocabulary fit tension**

The registry word is "truthfulness" but the primary term *alētheia* (G0225) operates across a very wide semantic range in the New Testament: truth as transforming force, truth as theological identity (Spirit of truth, Christ as truth), truth as the gospel received inwardly, and truth as personal integrity. The registry label "truthfulness" captures only one dimension of this term's actual inner-being range. This is a vocabulary observation, not a misassignment — the groups are validly within C10 — but Session B should explore whether "truthfulness" is the right English registry label or whether the term better names something like "truth-orientation." This is flagged as a Session D pointer.

**4. Size**

The instruction notes that clusters exceeding 13 active words warrant review; C10 is at 10 and the groups are distributed across coherent sub-domains. No size-based split is recommended, but session management will need to be disciplined.
