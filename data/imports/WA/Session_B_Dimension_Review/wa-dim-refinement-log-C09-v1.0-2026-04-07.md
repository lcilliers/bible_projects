## Phase A — Cluster Assignment Review

**Coherence assessment:**

C09 groups the character-vice vocabulary — the negative inner dispositions of the ungoverned or self-asserting person. It is the analytical mirror of C08. Where C08 named the virtuous inner life (humility, patience, self-control, endurance), C09 names its inversions: pride, stubbornness, rebellion, hardness, sloth, weakness, and boastfulness.

The binding affinity is the *self-asserting or self-collapsing* inner person — either inflating beyond proper limits (pride, boastfulness, stubbornness, rebellion) or failing to engage (sloth, weakness). Hardness links them: the hardened inner being neither submits nor acts rightly.

**Major structural feature — the dual-register of exaltation vocabulary:** Registry 123 (pride) contains extensive vocabulary (ga.on, ga.ah, rum, ra.mam, ka.vod) whose roots apply to both human pride and divine majesty. The same Hebrew word that names the arrogant person names God's transcendent greatness. This dual-register is not a classification problem — it is the most analytically important feature of the registry and of the cluster. The pride vocabulary in Scripture is always in relationship with the divine majesty vocabulary because pride is the inner attempt to claim what belongs to God alone.

**Boundary observations:**
- Reg 15 (boastfulness) contains the ka.vod (glory/honour) vocabulary — this entered through XREF connections and covers: God's manifest glory (Spiritual/God-ward), human honour as character quality (Character/Disposition), and human honour as relational-social condition (Relational/Social). These groups are assessed for their actual content.
- Reg 128 (rebellion) contains extensive sur (to turn aside) sub-gloss groups — largely volitional and character content about turning from God.
- Group 6077-002 (anchor=0, related=0) is the orphaned group from DIM-128-001. It has a context_description but zero verses. It will be assessed for dimension assignment but its analytical weight is minimal.
- Reg 170 (weakness) is analytically unusual within C09 — weakness in Paul is not simply vice but the paradoxical site of divine power. This dual register (weakness as limitation vs. weakness as grace-context) will require careful Phase C work.

**Recommendation:** Confirm C09 as-is.
