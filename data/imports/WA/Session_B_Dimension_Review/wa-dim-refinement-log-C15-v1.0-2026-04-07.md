### Coherence assessment

C15 is the God-ward relational engagement cluster — the inner life as it reaches toward, responds to, and rests in God. The inner-being affinity is strong: faith names the inner disposition of trust-reliance; trust names the inner resting in God; seeking names the inner directional orientation toward God; prayer and intercession name the inner acts of address to God; praise names the inner orientation of God-directed exaltation; worship names the comprehensive inner orientation of devoted service. Reverence would belong naturally here as the inner posture of awe before the divine. Together these registries constitute the inner-being vocabulary of what it means to be oriented toward God.

The cluster is analytically coherent. Faith and trust are closely related and will require careful differentiation in Session B. Prayer (Reg 122) and pray (Reg 212) are essentially the same domain — a note for Session D on possible consolidation. Intercession (Reg 94) is a specialised subset of prayer. Praise (Reg 121) and worship (Reg 176) overlap substantially — both name the inner orientation of God-directed devotion.

### Boundary observations

**Reg 121 (praise) — 50 groups, highly diverse:** The praise registry contains groups spanning doxa/glory vocabulary (G1391), corporate identity terms (H0587 anachnu — "we", H0259 echad — "one"), and specific terms (H1966 helel — "Day Star", H7623A shavach — "to soothe"). The majority are core praise vocabulary with strong inner-being content. A handful (H0587, H0259) are more broadly used terms whose inner-being relevance is contextual. Phase B will assess individually.

**Reg 176 (worship) — 37 groups, also broad:** The worship registry contains the avodah family (H5647 abad and derivatives), which spans service, labour, work, and bondage. Several groups are about human work-vocation and labour rather than devotional orientation. Two groups reference pitfalls/destruction (H7825 shachit, H7816 shachut) and one names "dog" as a moral type (G2965 kuon). These are clearly XREF-connected terms whose inner-being relevance varies. Phase B will flag where necessary.

**Reg 59 (faith) — 34 groups:** Contains significant unfaithfulness/treachery vocabulary (bagad, maal, bogedet) alongside faith/faithfulness vocabulary. This is analytically appropriate — the faith registry naturally includes its antonyms as the negative pole. Not a coherence concern.

**Reg 138 (reverence) — 0 groups:** Second programme gap noted (cf. Reg 137 in C14). The reverence domain has no verse context evidence. Flags for Session D.
