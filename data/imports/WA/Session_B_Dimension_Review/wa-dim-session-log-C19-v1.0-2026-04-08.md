### Notable Phase C findings

**Divine-Human Correspondence dominates (37%).** The identity/image/name/likeness cluster naturally spans the God-human boundary. The vocabulary describes what the human person *is* before God and *receives from* God, making the correspondence structure intrinsic rather than incidental.

**Imago Dei triple convergence.** Groups 1359-001 (H6754 tse.lem), 1360-001 (H1823 de.mut), and 1361-001 (G1504 eikōn) — three different terms for image/likeness in Hebrew and Greek — all independently arrive at Divine-Human Correspondence for the same inner-being phenomenon (Gen 1:26-27). This is the most robust evidence in the programme so far for the imago Dei as the ontological ground of dimension 11.

**Calling registry most concentrated (54%).** 13 of 24 calling groups are Divine-Human Correspondence. The calling vocabulary is structurally divine-human: God calls, names, constitutes; the human receives, responds, and is formed. Group 727-001 (krazō — "Abba! Father!") is the programme's most acute instance: the Spirit acting from within the human inner being, producing an expression that is simultaneously divine and human.

**Transformation emerges significantly (4 groups).** The manna (divine humbling/testing), Nebuchadnezzar's mind changed to beast-likeness (divine punitive transformation), progressive renewal toward the divine image (2 Cor 3:18), and conformity to Christ through suffering (Phil 3:10). C19 shows transformation operating in both directions — toward the divine image and away from it.

**Automated dimension corrections.** Several automated labels were revised. The Moral/Conscience label on multiple groups was tested against the verse evidence; in most cases it was confirmed but sometimes reassigned to Moral Character, Relational Disposition, or Divine-Human Correspondence. Identity/Selfhood labels were consistently tested — in most cases the identity content was attributed to GOD's constitutive act rather than the human's own identity formation, yielding Divine-Human Correspondence rather than Identity/Selfhood (which does not exist in the v1.6 vocabulary).
