## Vocabulary Observations

No new dimension proposals are required. All 135 groups map to the confirmed v1.4 vocabulary.

**Recurring vocabulary tensions noted — for Session B:**

1. **Moral Character vs Relational Disposition boundary** — several groups in holiness, consecration, and testimony sit at the boundary between stable inner quality (Moral Character) and directional inner orientation toward God (Relational Disposition). In groups where both are present, the assignment follows which inner-being engagement the description names as primary. Session B should attend to how these two dimensions interrelate in the holiness/consecration domain.

2. **Agency/Power — GOD vs HUMAN distribution** — across anointing, consecration, and blessing, Agency/Power appears with both GOD and HUMAN as dominant subjects in closely related groups. This is a genuine cluster pattern: God's appointing/consecrating/blessing and the human's exercise of prophetic or covenantal capacity are in a tight relationship. Session B will need to examine whether these represent the same inner-being phenomenon (commissioning/capacity) or distinct dimensions.

3. **Transformation — GOD-agent groups** — multiple Transformation groups have GOD as dominant subject (167-001, 179-002, 4697-001, 4701-001, 4701-002).
