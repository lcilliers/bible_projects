## Key Cross-Cluster Findings (programme-level)

**1. C08/C09 symmetry — virtue and vice share the same dimension structure:**
Both clusters lead with Character/Disposition and Spiritual/God-ward. Biblical virtue and vice are primarily characterological and God-relational — not merely behavioural or social. This is a foundational anthropological claim of the programme's vocabulary.

**2. The divine-address pattern (C06 × C08):**
DIM-33-SD001: the divine "Fear not" (ya.re, C06/Reg 61) and the divine "Take heart" (tharseō, C08/Reg 33) are structurally parallel speech-acts — God addressing the inner being directly to displace fear and produce courage. This is the programme's first confirmed cross-cluster parallel at the dimension-pattern level.

**3. The pride/majesty dual-register (C09/Reg 123):**
Human pride and divine majesty share the same Hebrew roots. Pride in Scripture is the human attempt to claim what belongs to divine majesty alone. The vocabulary itself encodes this theological claim.

**4. Weakness as theological inversion (C09/Reg 170 × Reg 123):**
DIM-170-SD001: Paul's weakness/strength reversal and boasting-in-weakness are a coherent paradoxical inner-being theology crossing C08 and C09. Session D needs to examine this.

**5. The sanctified/destructive axis (C07):**
Zeal, jealousy, and envy vocabulary (zēlos, qa.na, qin.ah) span destructive passion and sanctified devotion through direction alone — the inner capacity is identical; what changes is its object. This is a fundamental finding about how the inner being relates to passion.

**6. The "Volitional/Capacity" label:**
Non-standard. Appeared in C07 (3 groups), C08 (5 groups). All corrected to "Volitional/Will." Claude Code should audit `wa_dimension_index` programme-wide and report the full count of remaining "Volitional/Capacity" entries.

**7. "Sin & Vice" label:**
Non-standard in the current dimension vocabulary (it predates the dimension classification phase and should not appear as an active dimension label). Appeared in C09 (1 group, corrected). Claude Code should audit programme-wide and report count.
