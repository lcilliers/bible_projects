## Vocabulary Observations (not requiring immediate decision)

1. **Tripartite pattern in C06:** The cluster consistently shows three inner-being registers for fear/dread terms: orientation-toward-God (Spiritual/God-ward), raw affective state (Affective/Emotional), and collapse of inner resolve (Volitional/Will). This pattern warrants Session D attention.

2. **Somatic/Embodied dimension:** Two groups in Registry 61 (1744-001 sa.ar; 1792-001 ra.ad) explicitly name somatic expressions of inner terror. The cluster shows that fear is not merely mental — it produces involuntary bodily response named as such in the text.

3. **Registry 146 (shame) Relational/Social density:** Shame is unique in the cluster for generating the highest proportion of Relational/Social dimension groups. Shame in Scripture is inherently a social-inner phenomenon — it requires a community, a standard, and a witness.

4. **Identity/Selfhood is rare in C06:** Appears only twice — 5006-001 (covenantal negation of belonging, Reg 44) and 312-001 (choosing not to be ashamed, Reg 146). The second is the more distinctive: the one moment where Identity/Selfhood is primary in the shame vocabulary is when the person actively *refuses* shame as an identity act.

5. **Grammatical particle challenge confirmed (1159-001, raq):** A dimension label applied to a particle refers to the particle's contextual function, not its inherent semantic content. This is a known programme-level pattern documented in prior sessions. Resolution is adequate for current purposes.
