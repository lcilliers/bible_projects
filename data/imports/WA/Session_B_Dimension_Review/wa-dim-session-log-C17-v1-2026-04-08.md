## Key Analytical Findings

### 1. Relational Disposition dominance (119 groups, 42%)
C17 is the most Relational Disposition–dense cluster reviewed. This is not an artefact of vocabulary application — it reflects Scripture's thoroughgoing encoding of inner-being life in relational terms. The cluster shows that love, compassion, mercy, grace, kindness, covenant, fellowship, peace, and reconciliation are, at their core, descriptions of *how the inner person is oriented toward another*. This has structural significance for Session B: the inner-being is fundamentally relational before it is individual.

### 2. GOD-dominant groups (72 groups, 25%)
C17 contains the highest concentration of GOD-dominant groups in the programme so far. These include:
- **Relational Disposition/GOD:** God's chesed, racham, agapē, charis, chen, shalom-giving, covenant initiations, jealousy, relational silence and its breaking
- **Transformation/GOD:** Atonement (kipper, kaphar), forgiveness (salach, aphiēmi), propitiation (hilasmos), Spirit-impartation, making-peace (eirēnopoieō), reconciliation (katallagē)
- **Moral Character/GOD:** Forgiveness as divine attribute (sallach, selichah), compassion as God's character standard
- **Agency/Power/GOD:** God's sovereign mercy, God's disturbing power in creation

Together these constitute the most sustained portrait of God's inner being available in the dataset. Session D should synthesise this data programme-wide.

### 3. Transformation/GOD concentration (14 of 23 Transformation groups)
The atonement vocabulary (kipper/kaphar family, hilasmos, hilastērios, katallagē, eirēnopoieō, salach) produces a cluster of Transformation groups where God is the agent of inner-standing restoration. This is structurally significant: in C17, the majority of inner transformation is not human-initiated self-change but divine-initiated restoration. Session B should examine what this asymmetry reveals about the inner person's dependence for transformation on a power outside itself.

### 4. Automated label reassignments — key patterns
- `Theological/Divine-Human` (15 groups) → Relational Disposition/GOD in 11 cases; Transformation/GOD in 3; Moral Character/GOD in 1
- `Character/Disposition` (21 groups) → Moral Character in 15; Relational Disposition in 6
- `Identity/Selfhood` (9 groups) → Moral Character in 7; Transformation in 2
- `Somatic/Embodied` (1 group) → Emotion — Positive with Session D pointer

### 5. Vocabulary tension — Relational Disposition breadth
C17's 119 Relational Disposition groups divide meaningfully into: (a) God's inner disposition toward persons, (b) the human person's orientation toward God, and (c) the human person's orientation toward other persons. These are analytically distinct and Session B should attend to this three-way structure within the single dimension.
