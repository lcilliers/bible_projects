### Key analytical findings

**1. Spiritual/God-ward dominates (50/122 = 41%).**
Joy, gladness, wonder, comfort, and awe are characteristically God-directed in
Scripture, not free-floating affective states. The automated keyword-matching
had misclassified many of these as Affective/Emotional or Relational/Social.
This is the most significant corrective finding of the review.

**2. Gratitude is dispositional, not affective.**
All three gratitude terms (eucharisteō, eucharistos, eucharistia) assigned
Character/Disposition. Gratitude is a stable inner reorientation of the whole
person — its absence is diagnostic of inner corruption (Rom 1:21). This finding
is analytically significant for the programme's understanding of gratitude.

**3. Abiding joy is dispositional, not affective.**
Group 378-001 (chara) assigned Character/Disposition — the abiding inner state
that characterises the life of faith. Parallel with gratitude in R69.

**4. Theological/Divine-Human is a distinct and confirmed dimension.**
13 groups assign divine inner states — God's delight, pleasure, exultation — as
constitutive of the human inner-being condition. These cannot be collapsed into
Spiritual/God-ward without losing the divine/human distinction.

**5. The teleological gap.**
Groups 378-002 (chara — joy as the goal of the inner life completed in God) and
6701-001 (miph.la.ah — wondrous works designed to evoke wonder) point toward a
category the existing vocabulary does not name: the inner life as oriented toward
a divinely intended goal. This is flagged for vocabulary extension consideration
in Session D.
