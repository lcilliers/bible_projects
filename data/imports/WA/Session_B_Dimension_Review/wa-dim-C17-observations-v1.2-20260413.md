## Phase A — Cluster Assignment Review: C17

**C17 cluster words (all):** compassion (23), covenant (34), fellowship (62), forgiveness (64), grace (68), kindness (99), love (103), mercy (111), peace (117), reconciliation (130)

### Internal coherence assessment

C17 is the programme's relational-grace cluster. The inner-being affinity holding this cluster together is the family of outward-directed dispositions rooted in God's character and expressed in human relational life: grace, mercy, love, compassion, kindness, forgiveness, peace, covenant, fellowship, reconciliation. These are not discrete emotions — they are relational orientations that constitute the texture of inner life directed toward God and others.

The cluster has a dual axis:
- **Vertical axis:** how these qualities operate in the divine-human relationship (God's grace toward humans; human orientation toward God)
- **Horizontal axis:** how these qualities operate in human-human relationships (love, kindness, fellowship, forgiveness, peace as inter-human inner dispositions)

This dual axis is analytically coherent. The words belong together because they all name or serve the inner dispositions that constitute right relationship — whether with God or with others.

### Boundary observations

**Covenant (Reg 34):** covenant is structurally different from the others — it is the relational framework or container rather than the inner disposition itself. However, covenant is constituted by inner-being commitments (loyalty, faithfulness, steadfast love/chesed) and produces inner states (security, belonging, obligation). It belongs here as the structural context for the relational-grace vocabulary, though it sits toward the outer edge of the cluster's semantic range.

**Reconciliation (Reg 130):** two groups only — undersized. Both describe the inner-relational restoration of enmity. Coherent with C17; not a boundary case.

**Fellowship (Reg 62):** the chavar root family raises an interesting boundary question. Several of the newly classified terms (wound, spell, joining) sit at some distance from the relational-fellowship core. However the core koinōnia groups (873-001, 873-002) and the companion/companionship groups are clearly within C17 scope. The wound and spell groups are the outer edge — but they landed here through the chavar root connection, and the programme's inclusion default (RD-PC-001) supports retention.

### Cross-registry root families

From the root family extract (68 families), C17 shows the expected high density of overlapping root families — particularly the CHESED, RACHAM, CHEN, and SHALOM root complexes which span multiple C17 registries. This is a marker of the cluster's internal coherence: the same Hebrew root families generate vocabulary across love, mercy, compassion, grace, and kindness simultaneously.

**Notable root overlap:** The CHEN root (grace/favour — Reg 68) and CHESED root (mercy/steadfast love — Reg 111) are also represented in love (Reg 103) and compassion (Reg 23). This root-level overlapping is a Session D signal — already well-captured in the existing SD pointer record (DIM-068-SD024, DIM-068-SD025, DIM-023-SD017).

### Recommendation

Confirm cluster C17 as-is. No reassignment proposals. Note Reg 130 metadata anomaly for Claude Code.
