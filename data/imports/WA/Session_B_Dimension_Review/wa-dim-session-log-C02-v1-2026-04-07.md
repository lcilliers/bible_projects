## 4. Programme-Level Principles Established

Two governing principles confirmed by researcher during C02 session that apply programme-wide:

### 4.1 Cognitive vs. Volitional Dimension Distinction

> **The fact that the will can act upon a faculty does not make that faculty volitional.**

The dimension names the *nature of the capacity itself*, not the relationship through which it can be engaged. Applied to:
- 979-001 (ze.kher — remembrance): Volitional/Capacity → Cognitive/Mind. The commanded act of remembering is volitional; the *capacity* to hold in living consciousness is cognitive.
- 1188-001 (noēma — mind's contents): Volitional/Capacity → Cognitive/Mind. The mind's contents can be directed by volitional and spiritual forces; the *faculty* remains cognitive.

**Scope:** Governs all future dimension assignments across all clusters and Session B.

### 4.2 Theological/Divine-Human vs. Spiritual/God-ward Distinction

> **Theological/Divine-Human:** inner-being engagements where the *constitutive structure* of the phenomenon is the divine-human relationship itself. The phenomenon would not exist in its present form apart from that relationship as its constitutive frame.

> **Spiritual/God-ward:** the soul's own faculties oriented *toward* God. The inner act originates in the soul and moves toward God as its object or direction. The faculty belongs to the soul; God is its direction, not its constitutive source.

**Scope:** Governs all future dimension assignments. Should be added to the dimension vocabulary reference before Session B begins.

### Interpretive observations (grounded in data)

**Cognitive/Mind dominates (44%)** — as expected for a cluster built on epistemic vocabulary. This is not noise; it reflects the genuine cognitive character of knowing, reasoning, planning, and understanding in biblical usage.

**The divine-relational pole is substantial (33% combined: Spiritual/God-ward + Theological/Divine-Human).** This is analytically significant. Cognitive vocabulary in this cluster is not primarily self-referential — it is heavily oriented toward God or constituted by the divine-human relationship. The cluster shows that cognition in Scripture is inseparable from the soul's directional relationship to God.

**Moral/Conscience (15%) is well-represented.** The cluster reveals that cognitive acts — reasoning, planning, counsel, wisdom — carry a persistent moral dimension. The direction of the cognitive faculty (toward God, toward deception, toward self) determines its moral character. This is the mechanism of the directionally-determined faculty pattern.

**Volitional/Will (3.5%) is distinct but limited.** Purpose vocabulary (R126) contributes most of these; deliberate inner resolve is distinguishable from cognitive deliberation.

**Identity/Selfhood (0.6%) — single instance.** 963-003 (knowledge constituting personhood, R100) stands alone in this cluster. The Danielic narrative grounds rationality as the defining human capacity. This warrants a Session B observation for connection to Image of God themes.

### Significant corrections from automated labels

Of 171 groups, the automated label was confirmed as-is in approximately 45 cases. The remaining 126 received either corrections or new assignments. High correction rates were driven by:

1. **Cognitive/Mind over-applied by keyword matching** — many groups labelled Cognitive/Mind by keyword were correctly Spiritual/God-ward or Theological/Divine-Human when the direction of the knowing was read properly.
2. **Volitional/Capacity misapplied** — several groups naming cognitive faculties (remembrance, mind's contents) were incorrectly labelled Volitional because the will can direct them; corrected by programme-level principle 4.1 above.
3. **Character/Disposition over-applied** — several wisdom-registry groups labelling moral orientations as stable character types were correctly Moral/Conscience when the governing content was the moral direction, not a stable disposition.
4. **Spiritual/God-ward vs. Theological/Divine-Human** — multiple groups involving divine-human relational knowing required reclassification once the Theological/Divine-Human definition was sharpened (principle 4.2 above).
