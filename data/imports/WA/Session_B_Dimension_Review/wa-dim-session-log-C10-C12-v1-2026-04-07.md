## Cross-Session Analytical Developments

### 1. The Moral Spectrum: C10 / C11 / C12 as a triad
These three clusters together map the moral inner-being domain comprehensively:
- **C10** — moral character: what the inner person *is* in virtue (faithfulness, purity, integrity, uprightness)
- **C11** — moral failure: what the inner person *does* in transgression (sin, wickedness, deceit, corruption)
- **C12** — moral defilement: what *corrupts* the inner person from within (abomination, impurity, whoredom, lust)

Session D should map the relationships between these three clusters as the programme's moral architecture.

### 2. Programme vocabulary standards confirmed and extended
Three key vocabulary boundaries are now established with consistency across C10–C12:
- **Sin & Vice** = inner *condition* of moral failure or defilement (not evaluative)
- **Moral/Conscience** = evaluative/judicial moral dimension (assessment, verdict, act)
- **Theological/Divine-Human** = divine inner disposition toward humanity or divine-human encounter; *not* human moral evaluation

### 3. Affective/Emotional now active across clusters
The dimension has appeared in C11 (15 groups) and C12 (4 groups). The question of whether C10 groups with affective content should be revisited remains as Session D pointer DIM-57-SD001.

### 4. Volitional/Capacity non-standard label

### 5. Redemptive vocabulary in defilement registries
New covenant transformation (C12 abomination) and salvation vocabulary (C12 debauchery) appear in defilement registries via XREF paths. Session D pointer DIM-001-SD001 raises this as a possible programme-wide pattern.
