### Coherence assessment

C11 is the moral-failure domain — the counterpart of C10's moral-character cluster. Where C10 maps positive inner qualities (faithfulness, integrity, purity, uprightness), C11 maps their opposites: the inner orientations, conditions, and acts that constitute moral failure before God.

The cluster holds together clearly. Corruption, deceit, evil, hypocrisy, iniquity, perverseness, sin, transgression, wickedness, and treachery all name aspects of the inner being oriented away from God and toward moral failure. The registries illuminate each other: sin names the act, iniquity names the condition, transgression names the boundary-crossing, wickedness and evil name the character quality, corruption names the decay, deceit and hypocrisy name the dissimulating inner orientation, and perverseness names the structural distortion. Together they map the full landscape of inner moral failure in Scripture.

This is one of the programme's most important and richest clusters — the moral-failure domain is pervasive across both Testaments and the vocabulary is diverse.

### Boundary observations

**1. evil (Reg. 57) — significant internal heterogeneity, same pattern as innocence in C10**

Registry 57 (evil) is by far the largest in the cluster at 46 groups. Like innocence (C10), it includes several grammatical and functional terms — notably H3605 *kol* (all, 7 groups), H4310 *mi* (who?, 10 groups), H4994 *na* (please, 2 groups), and H2161 *zamam* (to plan, 2 groups). These terms produce groups across a very wide inner-being range — awe, longing, grief, terror, volitional intention, self-knowledge, spiritual receptivity. The result is that "evil" as a registry contains groups that have little to do with evil per se. This is not a misassignment — these terms were classified through their verse evidence — but it is a significant observation for Session B: the evil registry functions partly as a container for inner-being framing particles, just as innocence did in C10.

**2. sin (Reg. 147) — also contains particle terms**

Registry 147 (sin) similarly includes the particle H0408 *al* (not, 9 groups) which produces groups across many inner states (fear, anger, grief, mourning, rejoicing, trust, pride, cognitive orientation, spiritual expression). Again, this is the result of verse context classification, not misassignment. The same pattern noted above applies.

**3. iniquity (Reg. 89) — afflicted person groups**

Two groups in iniquity (926-001, 926-002) are classified under H6041 *ani* (afflicted). These describe the experience of the afflicted/poor person and God's compassion toward them — groups that belong in the moral-failure cluster insofar as affliction is the consequence of moral-wrong done *to* the person, but they represent a distinct inner-being perspective (the victim, not the perpetrator). Noted for Session B.

The Affective/Emotional label was flagged as a *gap* in the C10 session (DIM-90-SD001, DIM-92-SD001) — it was absent from the vocabulary. Its presence here as an existing label in the automated classifier is a finding: the programme vocabulary *does* include Affective/Emotional, at least in the automated keyword system, though it was not applied to any C10 groups. This resolves the C10 vocabulary gap question partially — the label exists; it was simply not triggered in C10. Phase C will assess whether the assignments using this label are appropriate.

`Volitional/Capacity` (1 group: 5312-001) appears to be a keyword-match variant or error. The standard label in the programme is `Volitional/Will`. This will be corrected in Phase C.
