## Phase A — Cluster Assignment Review

**C17 — 10 registries, 284 groups**

**Coherence assessment:** C17 is a semantically rich and tightly coherent cluster. The unifying inner-being affinity is covenantal relational quality — the cluster holds together the full spectrum of how God and persons are oriented toward each other in the covenant relationship: love (103), compassion (23), mercy (111), grace (68), kindness (99), forgiveness (64), covenant itself (34), fellowship (62), peace (117), and reconciliation (130). Every registry engages either the character of God's relational disposition toward persons, the human disposition in response, or the framework (covenant, peace, reconciliation) within which these dispositions operate. The inner-being affinity is direct and strong.

**Boundary observations:**

1. **Registry 99 — kindness** contains a significant number of xref/particle terms (H5971 *am* variants, H2088/2090/2097/2098 *zeh/zo/zoh/zu* demonstratives, H0120G *adam*, H5978 *immadi*, H5974 *im*, H5973B *meïm*). These arrived via xref registration from the primary kindness terms. Their groups are classified; inner-being content is present but often indirect. This is an analytic challenge at Phase C, not a clustering issue — all belong here.

2. **Registry 34 — covenant** includes H7620I *shavua* (week) and H1585 *gemar* (salutation). Both have thin but valid inner-being connections (prophetic weeks as covenantal schedule; formal salutation as peace-invocation). Borderline but defensible within covenant.

3. **Registry 117 — peace** is the cluster's largest (69 groups) and most semantically diverse — spanning rest, silence, stillness, blessing, jealousy (divine), soothing aroma, and quiet. The peace vocabulary in Hebrew is structurally wide. No reassignment warranted; the diversity is inherent to the shalom semantic field.

**Size:** 10 registries — within bounds.

**Recommendation:** ☑ Confirm C17 as-is. No reassignments proposed.

## Vocabulary Observations

**No new dimension proposals required.** All 284 groups map to the confirmed v1.4 vocabulary.

**Key observation — Somatic/Embodied label (1581-001, katafileō):** The legacy automated label attempted to capture the bodily dimension of the kiss as inner-being expression. The v1.4 vocabulary does not have a Somatic/Embodied dimension. The inner states expressed through the kiss (love, grief, contrition, reunion) map to Emotion — Positive as the dominant valence. The absence of a somatic dimension has not created a vocabulary gap, but Session B should attend to whether the embodied expression of inner states merits separate analytical treatment across the programme. This is noted as a Session D pointer below.

**Vocabulary tension observed — Relational Disposition breadth in C17:** This cluster reveals just how much inner-being content Scripture encodes in the relational register. Relational Disposition is the single largest dimension (preliminary count: ~120 groups). This is not a vocabulary problem — the breadth is genuine. But Session B should explore whether Relational Disposition in C17 has internal sub-categories (e.g., God's relational disposition *toward* persons vs. persons' relational orientation *toward* God vs. persons' orientation *toward* others) that merit analytical distinctions without requiring a new dimension.

## Session B/D Pointers Captured

**Session B findings (to be inserted in patch):**

1. **DIM-103-001** — Reg 103 (love): The love registry spans the broadest relational vocabulary in the programme — from agapē to phileō to eros to chesed, and includes the love of God for humanity (GOD dominant), human love toward God, mutual love among persons, and disordered love. Session B should map the full spectrum of love's inner-being architecture: what distinguishes the different forms, how they interrelate, and what the coexistence of eros/philia/agapē in the same registry reveals about the inner-being's capacity for love.

2. **DIM-117-002** — Reg 117 (peace): The peace registry's inner-being content is unusually wide — spanning the felt inner state of rest/peace (Emotion — Positive), the inner posture of quietness before God (Moral Character/Relational Disposition), the divine giving of rest (Transformation/GOD), and God's own inner states of rest, restraint, and jealousy. Session B should examine what shalom names as an inner-being reality: is it primarily a felt state, a relational condition, a divine gift, or an eschatological reality — and how do these dimensions interrelate?

3. **DIM-111-001** — Reg 111 (mercy): The atonement/propitiation vocabulary (kipper, kaphar, kapporet, hilasmos) within the mercy registry produces a cluster of Transformation/GOD groups (moral standing transformed through atoning act). Session B should examine the relationship between mercy as divine inner disposition (Relational Disposition/GOD) and atonement as its structural mechanism — how God's inner relational orientation of mercy is expressed in transformative acts.

4. **DIM-34-001** — Reg 34 (covenant): Three groups show the new covenant as inner transformation (765-003, 766-002, 777-001) — law written on the heart, new covenant through Spirit, new self/creation. Session B should examine the covenant registry's inner transformation dimension: how the trajectory from Sinai (obedience required externally) to the new covenant (transformation from within) is encoded in the Dimension Review data.

**Session D pointers (to be inserted in patch):**

1. **DIM-103-SD001** — Reg 103 (love): Group 1581-001 (katafileō, kiss) was assigned Emotion — Positive for the inner states expressed through the physical act. The legacy Somatic/Embodied label raised a genuine question about whether Scripture encodes inner-being content in bodily expression in ways the current vocabulary does not capture. Session D should assess whether a Somatic dimension is warranted across the programme — specifically for groups where the inner-being content is carried by or expressed through physical/bodily acts (kiss, embrace, prostration, anointing, tears). This may require a v1.4 vocabulary revision.

2. **DIM-17-SD001** — cluster-level: C17 shows a significant concentration of Relational Disposition groups with GOD as dominant subject — the divine inner being as relationally oriented toward persons. These span love (God's chesed and agapē), compassion (divine racham), mercy (God's eleos), grace (God's charis/chen), and peace (God's shalom-giving). Session D should synthesise across C17 and other clusters (C16 Transformation/GOD; future clusters) to characterise the inner being of God as Scripture describes it — his relational dispositions, his inner emotional range, and what this reveals about the divine nature in relation to the human person created in his image.
