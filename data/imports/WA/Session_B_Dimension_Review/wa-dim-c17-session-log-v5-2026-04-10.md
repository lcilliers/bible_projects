### Analytical observations

**Relational Disposition is the dominant dimension for Reg 23** — 16 of 22 groups. The compassion vocabulary of Scripture is overwhelmingly a vocabulary of oriented-toward-another inner states: pity, tender mercy, fellow-feeling, sympathy. This confirms the Phase A assessment of C17 as a relational-disposition cluster.

**Two Divine-Human Correspondence groups** (1635-001, 729-001) both reflect the structural relationship between divine and human compassion — one through the mirroring principle (2Sa 22:26), one through the maternal metaphor (Isa 46:3). These are analytically important for Session D: they show how the human compassion vocabulary maps onto or grounds the divine.

**The chesed groups (H2617B in Reg 23) map across three dimensions**: divine relational disposition (1633-001), human moral character (1633-002), and human dependence (1633-003) — showing that chesed names different aspects of the inner person depending on whether it describes God's character, the human character quality God requires, or the human orientation of trust in God's chesed. This tri-partite mapping is a Session B finding for Reg 23.

**The sympathy cluster (G-terms)** — sumpathēs, sumpaschō, metriopatheō, sumpatheō — all corrected from Agency/Power to Relational Disposition. The sun- prefix compounds consistently name relational solidarity rather than power exercise. This may be a pattern worth noting for Session D across the Greek compassion vocabulary.
