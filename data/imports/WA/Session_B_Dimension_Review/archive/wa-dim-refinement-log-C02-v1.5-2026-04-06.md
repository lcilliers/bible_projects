## Research Finding — Directionally-Determined Inner Faculty Pattern
**Date:** 2026-04-06 | **Refinement log version:** v0.7
**Full detail:** wa-research-note-directional-faculty-v1-2026-04-06.md

### Summary

A recurring pattern identified across Registries 108, 127, 160, 174, 49, 126 during Phase B: the same inner faculty or act appears in morally or directionally opposite forms within the same term. The faculty is not split between two different capacities — it is the same capacity deployed in opposite directions. Three sub-patterns:

- **Sub-pattern A** — Directional opposition: higgayon (H1902H), re.a (H7454) — same reflective act, Godward vs. against another
- **Sub-pattern B** — Capacity and its corruption: a.rum (H6175), or.mah (H6195), fronimos (G5429) — prudence vs. craftiness as the same perceptive faculty
- **Sub-pattern C** — Relational symmetry across the divine-human boundary: re.a — term operates from both sides simultaneously

### Open research question

Is this a new dimension of the inner life, or a structural principle about how inner dimensions work? Cannot be resolved from C02 data alone. Carry into Session B and Session D.

## Theological/Divine-Human Correction Pass — C02
**Date:** 2026-04-06 | **Refinement log version:** v1.0

### Corrected definitions (researcher confirmed)

**Theological/Divine-Human:** Inner-being engagements where the *constitutive structure* of the phenomenon is the divine-human relationship itself — the soul's inner state defined by its position within that relationship (being formed, known, called, addressed, constituted as creature). The phenomenon would not exist in its present form apart from the relationship as its constitutive frame.

**Spiritual/God-ward:** The soul's own faculties oriented *toward* God. The inner act originates in the soul and moves toward God as its object or direction (seeking, fearing, trusting, worshipping, remembering, petitioning). The faculty belongs to the soul; God is its direction, not its constitutive source.
