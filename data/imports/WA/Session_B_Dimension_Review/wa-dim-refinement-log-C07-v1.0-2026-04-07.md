## Phase A — Cluster Assignment Review

**Coherence assessment:**

C07 groups together the reactive-negative drive vocabulary of the inner being: anger, wrath, bitterness, envy, hatred, indignation, jealousy, zeal, and resentment. The binding affinity is the *drive* dimension of negative inner states — these are not merely states that are experienced passively (like fear or shame in C06), but states that generate energy and direct it outward. Anger rises against something; envy resents a rival's standing; hatred orients the whole person against another; zeal (positive or negative) presses toward a goal.

**Internal complexity:** The cluster contains a significant built-in tension. Several core terms carry both a negatively-valenced and a positively-valenced register:
- **Zeal (181)** — spoudē/spoudazō can name the zealous commitment of the godly servant or the dangerous fervour of the zealot. In envy (56), the zeal vocabulary (zēlos, zēloō, qa.na) shows the same dual range: consuming devotion to God vs. consuming resentment of rivals.
- **Jealousy (96)** — parazēloō names the provocation of jealousy as a divine redemptive instrument. The same inner capacity that is destructive in human rivalry is part of God's strategy for Israel's restoration.
- **Indignation** — the inner heat of righteous moral outrage at evil is analytically distinct from reactive anger at personal affront.

This dual-register pattern is the most important structural feature of C07. It distinguishes C07 from C06 (fear, shame) where the negative valence is less ambiguous.
