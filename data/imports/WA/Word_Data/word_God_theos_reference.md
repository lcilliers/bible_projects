## Meaning

God, usually refers to the one true God. In a very few contexts it refers to a pagan god or goddess. "The Son of God" as a title of Jesus emphasises his unique relationship to the Father. "The god of this age" refers to the devil.

Specific uses:
- A deity — Acts 7:43; 1 Cor. 8:5
- An idol — Acts 7:40
- God, the true God — Mt. 3:9, and frequently
- God, possessed of true godhead — Jn. 1:1; Rom. 9:5
- From the Hebrew, applied to potentates — Jn. 10:34–35
- τῶ θεῶ as an intensive term from the Hebrew meaning "exceedingly" — Acts 7:20; possibly 2 Cor. 10:4

---

## LSJ dictionary entry (summary)

θεός — God, the Deity, in general sense, both singular and plural. Used of the one true God, of pagan divinities, of natural phenomena (e.g. Zeus as rain-bringer, Poseidon as earthquake-sender), of rulers as divine titles, and metaphorically of abstract things such as wealth or fortune. In the feminine, θεός also means goddess, especially of Athena at Athens. Used in comparative form θεώτερος meaning "divine."

Dialectal forms: Boeotian θιός, Laconian σιός, Cretan θιός, Doric θεύς.

## Note on relevance to registry 061 (fear)

θεός was surfaced by STEP's fear cluster search because of the high co-occurrence of God as the *object* of fear and reverence throughout Scripture. The compound concept יִרְאַת יהוה / יִרְאַת אֱלֹהִים ("fear of the Lord / fear of God") is the theological centre of the fear word group and will need direct treatment in the registry 061 Session B analysis.

θεός is not itself an inner-being word and is not included in the fear registry term list. This file is held as a reference resource for that analytical work.
