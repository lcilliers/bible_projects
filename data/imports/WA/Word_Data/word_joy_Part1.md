### joy
**Hebrew**
  - **simchah** (שִׂמְחָה) — joy, gladness (Ps 16:11, Neh 8:10)
  - **sason** (שָׂשׂוֹן) — exultation, joy, delight (Is 35:10, 51:3, Ps 45:7)
  - **gil** (גִּיל) — to rejoice, exult — especially in God (Ps 35:9, Hab 3:18)
**Greek**
  - **chara** (χαρά) — joy — a settled state distinct from circumstances (Gal 5:22, Phil 4:4, John 15:11, Rom 14:17)
  - **agalliao** (ἀγαλλιάω) — to exult, leap for joy (Luke 1:47, John 8:56, 1 Pet 1:8)
**Conceptual Search Terms:** `gladness`, `delight`, `exultation`, `rejoicing`, `blessedness`
**STEP Bible Suggestion:** Search chara in NT — Phil 4:4 commands joy as a state independent of circumstances. John 15:11 presents joy as Christ's own joy filling the believer. In OT search simchah in Psalms and Nehemiah. Neh 8:10 (the joy of the LORD is your strength) is a key text.
