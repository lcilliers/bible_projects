### heart registry 183 --- Part 2: Greek

> **Source list:** `STEP Bible — heart 183`

**heart** is the primary biblical term for the inner seat of human personhood, encompassing intellect, will, emotion, and moral disposition. In Hebrew the dominant root cluster is *lev/le.vav/lib.bah*, covering the full range of inner life from courage and resolve to spiritual hardness and wounding. In Greek *kardia* and its compounds (hard-hearted, dull of heart, heart-knower, etc.) dominate, with the *splanchnon* cluster (affection/entrails, compassionate) functioning as a visceral register of deep emotion. Heart terms intersect with soul (*ne.phesh*, *psuchē* — XREF 182), conscience (*kil.yah* — XREF 026), and desire (*cha.shaq* — XREF 043).

**Greek note:** *Kardia* (G2588) is the NT's primary heart term; compound adjectives (hard-hearted, dull, bold, heavy, proud) are largely NT hapax or rare terms. *Splanchnon* (G4698) functions in an affective/visceral register distinct from the cognitive-volitional sense of *kardia*.

**Conceptual Search Terms:** heart, inner being, mind, will, conscience, emotion, desire, courage, hardness, compassion, affection, entrails

**STEP Bible Suggestion:** Search *kardia* and compounds; also *splanchnon* cluster and courage/faint-heart Greek terms.
