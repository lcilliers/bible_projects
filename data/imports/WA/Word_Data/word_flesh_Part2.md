### flesh registry 185 --- Part 2: Greek

> **Source list:** `High Confidence`

Flesh denotes the physical substance of living creatures — the meat, body tissue, and bodily envelope of human and animal life. The Greek term σάρξ (sarx) is the dominant word, covering flesh as physical substance, human nature in its earthly and moral weakness, and the sphere of creaturely existence opposed to the Spirit. Related terms extend the field to fleshly disposition, meat for consumption, bodily mutilation, and moral excess associated with fleshly appetite. This part covers the Greek terms; Part 1 covers the Hebrew.

Greek terms: aselgeia, thnēsimaios, sarx, sarkikos, kreas, katatomē, eksarkizomai
