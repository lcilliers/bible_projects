## Heading

`Mission`

## Body

> This research programme began in January 2026 from a desire to understand the human spirit, soul, and body more deeply than existing accounts allowed. As the work developed, a conviction settled: an honest account of the human inner being is incomplete without a parallel account of the Holy Spirit. The two stand in relation to one another — the human inner life is shaped, inhabited, and addressed by the Spirit throughout Scripture — and neither can be studied at depth while leaving the other undefined.
>
> The mission that followed was straightforward in statement and substantial in commitment: take a deep dive. Investigate both subjects on their own terms; let Scripture lead the evidence; hold the enquiry open long enough to reach findings rather than assumptions. The commitment produced two elaborate documents, each a collection of deep-dive studies in its own right:
>
> - **Framework A — *The Holy Spirit: Origin, Nature, Work, Character, and Interaction. A Five-Thousand-Year Investigation from Genesis to the Present Day.*** An investigation of the Holy Spirit across the full biblical canon and beyond it into the receiving tradition.
> - **Framework B — *Spirit, Soul, and Body: The Composition of Human Beings. A Comprehensive Biblical and Scientific Investigation.*** An investigation of the human person as Scripture describes the composition, and as scientific enquiry approaches the same subject from a different direction.
>
> The research programme whose prose is gathered here is a continuation of that commitment — a sustained deep-dive into the inner-being vocabulary of Scripture, conducted within the frame Framework B established. The mission, in its present form, is to map in detail what Scripture reveals about the characteristics, operations, and interrelationships of the human inner being, word by word, verse by verse, with the discipline that the scale of the corpus requires.

## Authorship notes — what I did with the source material

Your message supplied the origin, the motivation, the decision, and the two Framework titles. I turned these into a mission record by:

- **Anchoring the date** — "January 2026" stated explicitly, so the record is time-bound rather than vague.
- **Strengthening the theological framing** — your "Without a better understanding of the Holy Spirit, the former would be incomplete" becomes a stated intellectual conviction: the two stand in relation, and neither can be studied at depth while leaving the other undefined. This honours the substance of your framing without paraphrasing it away.
- **Keeping your voice** — "take a deep dive" is preserved verbatim and given its own sentence. I considered paraphrasing to "conduct an extensive investigation"; rejected that as sterilising.
- **Presenting the two frameworks as a list, not prose.** Their titles are proper-noun content of substantial length; a list treats them with the weight they deserve and makes them reference-clean for a reader. The italicised subtitle format distinguishes the main title from the qualifying sub-title.
- **Closing with the continuation.** The final paragraph bridges from the two frameworks to the current programme. This is the authorship call I flagged to you — whether the mission record should extend into the present or stop at the frameworks. I decided to extend, because stopping at the frameworks leaves the reader asking "and so why are we here now?" with no answer until sub-section 1.4. The closing paragraph is deliberately brief so it does not encroach on 1.3 (wider programme) or 1.4 (this inner-being programme); those records will handle the detail.
