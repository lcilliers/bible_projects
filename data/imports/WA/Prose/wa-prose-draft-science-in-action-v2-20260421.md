The relationship between Scripture and contemporary science is described conceptually in the sub-section on the two lenses. This sub-section describes the operational consequence: how the scientific lens is engaged within the programme's work, at what point, through what instrument, and with what discipline.

The governing premise is the second-lens position. Scientific literature relevant to the inner being is genuinely vast — psychology, neuroscience, physiology, behavioural science, developmental and cognitive psychology, and evolutionary biology each address territory the programme's corpus covers. Treated as a co-equal research programme, the scientific material would demand a second corpus-level effort matching the biblical one in scope and rigour. The programme does not attempt this. It requires representative and reliable coverage of the landmark findings in each relevant field, held alongside the biblical analysis as the second lens through which the same territory is read. This single decision — science as second lens rather than co-equal lens — resolves the scope problem and shapes everything that follows in scientific engagement.

**When science enters the work.** Scientific engagement happens during Session B and is sequenced after the biblical pass is complete. Steps one to six of Session B's analytical protocol are the biblical pass — semantic characterisation, dimensional placement, divine and somatic dimensions, relational and causative analysis, anchor-verse work. Steps seven and eight are the scientific pass. Steps nine and ten are the integrating synthesis. The sequencing is a methodological commitment. Running the two passes simultaneously risks the scientific frame distorting the biblical reading before the biblical analysis is complete, particularly for words where the scientific literature is substantial (shame, fear, grief, and comparable affective territory). The biblical-first discipline guards against that risk.

**The three-question structure.** The scientific pass proceeds through three questions. First, what is the primary scientific field for this word? Field assignment shapes the rest of the pass. Second, what are the landmark findings in that field for this characteristic? These are the settled, well-replicated observations that researchers in the field would cite — not a literature review. Third, where does the scientific finding engage the biblical analysis? One substantive paragraph per word, identifying the specific point of convergence or divergence. This third step is the analytical heart of the pass, and the only step that requires judgement rather than retrieval.

**The six scientific fields.** The programme works with six fields. Each has a different relationship to the inner-being territory and a different density of overlap with the word corpus.

- *Neuroscience* addresses the body-soul boundary and the somatic dimension — the neural correlates of inner states, the brain-body connection, and how emotion is implemented biologically. It is the primary field for words where the biological substrate of the inner state is central to its meaning.
- *Clinical and social psychology* carries the largest overlap with the corpus. It covers emotion theory, attachment, moral psychology, shame and guilt research, the psychology of will, motivation, and virtue. Most Session B words draw on this field.
- *Physiology and epigenetics* addresses how inner states are written into the body — stress physiology, the HPA axis, the bodily expression of grief and joy, and the generational transmission of patterns. It is the primary field for somatic-weighted characteristics and for the small set of words engaging generational transmission.
- *Behavioural science* addresses the relational circle — how inner states are expressed in behaviour and social context, how they are induced in others, and how cooperation, competition, and reciprocity shape inner life. It serves the causative and relational dimensions of the corpus.
- *Developmental and cognitive psychology* addresses the cognitive circle and the formation of character — how cognitive capacities develop, how moral character is formed, and how wisdom, conscience, and identity take shape over time. It serves the cognitive and character vocabulary of the corpus.
- *Genetics and evolutionary biology* addresses the created endowment — what is built into the human creature by nature, the heritability of temperament and trait patterns, and the evolutionary origins of social emotions. It serves the baseline body-soul vocabulary and the words naming the human creature as constituted.

Most words have a clear primary field; some span two. The primary-field assignment is stated at the head of the scientific pass and governs which part of the reference shelf is consulted first.

**The reference shelf.** The instrument that makes the scientific pass tractable is a curated reference shelf — a set of foundational texts and landmark papers organised by field, chosen for breadth of coverage against the corpus. The shelf is not a reading list to complete before analysis begins; it is a standing reference consulted during the scientific pass of each word. Where the shelf carries a text of high relevance to the word being analysed, that text is the primary scientific reference for the session. Where a word falls outside the shelf's coverage, a targeted search fills the gap, and a recurring gap across multiple words signals that the shelf should be extended. The shelf is extended by the work rather than finalised before it.

**The no-database decision.** The programme does not build a structured scientific database to match its biblical database. The decision rests on three points. A parallel scientific database at the depth of the biblical one would constitute a second programme of comparable scale — plausibly years of work — and would defer synthesis. The data types are not structurally equivalent: biblical data is lexical and classifiable, while scientific findings are arguments, observations, and interpretations that require judgement to apply. And the programme does not need it — the second-lens role is served by reliable knowledge of landmark findings, which the reference shelf provides.

**Convergence and divergence.** The analytical work of the scientific pass is organised by a framework that distinguishes three kinds of convergence and three kinds of divergence. Convergence can be phenomenological — the two accounts describing the same experience from different angles. It can be mechanistic — science describing the how of what Scripture describes as a reality. It can be structural — the architecture of the inner life having the same shape in both accounts. Divergence can be register divergence — the accounts addressing different levels of the same reality and only appearing to conflict. It can be reductive divergence — a scientific account that reduces the inner-being characteristic to its biological substrate and loses dimensions the biblical account treats as primary. It can be genuine divergence — claims in actual tension that cannot be harmonised by reframing. The discipline is to name the kind of convergence or divergence in play. Genuine divergence is flagged for the cross-registry synthesis phase rather than resolved prematurely at the word level.

**What the pass produces.** The output of the scientific pass for each word is a concise annotation: the primary field, the two or three landmark findings drawn on, and the paragraph identifying the convergence or divergence with the biblical analysis. The annotation is carried into the word's Session B record and appears in the published word study as the scientific engagement for that word.
