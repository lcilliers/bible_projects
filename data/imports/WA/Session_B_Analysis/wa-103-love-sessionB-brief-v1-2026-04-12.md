## 2. Meaning Findings

### Primary semantic picture
Love (Reg 103) is not a single inner-being characteristic but a family of orientations unified by the capacity for attachment and self-giving. The vocabulary divides into four semantic registers:

**Register 1 — Willed, active love (agapao/agape, ahavah):** Love as chosen orientation of the whole person — commanded, directed, measurable by cost. The defining characteristic is directionality: agapao is entirely defined by its object. Love of God, love of neighbour, love of enemy, love of the world, love of darkness — the same verb applied to radically different objects, showing love as a capacity that can be rightly or wrongly ordered. Not primarily a feeling.

**Register 2 — Covenant faithfulness (chesed/hesed):** Love as the inner quality of settled relational commitment — the loyalty that does not withdraw when circumstances become costly. Chesed is the moral spine of the OT inner life. It names both divine steadfast love and the human virtue that mirrors it. Its highest form is commanded above ritual (Hos 6:6). Its absence is moral and spiritual ruin (Hos 6:6; Mic 6:8).

**Register 3 — Compassionate/visceral love (racham/rachamim):** Love as an inner movement of mercy grounded in the body's most intimate space — the womb (rechem). This register is constitutively somatic: divine compassion is described through womb-nursing metaphor (Isa 49:15) and inner-parts movement (Jer 31:20; Isa 63:15). Racham is the most bodily of the love vocabulary and the most deeply linked to God's response to suffering.

**Register 4 — Natural affection/friendship (phileo and compound family):** Love as warmth of personal attachment, mutual self-giving in friendship, and the natural bonds of family and community. The philo- compound family is the NT's most systematic taxonomy of loves — every compound names love's orientation toward a specific object, virtuous or disordered (philotheos vs philēdonos; philarguria; philadelphia).

### Significant semantic tensions
- **Agapaō vs phileō (John 21):** Group 572-003 is built around this dialogue. The programme's grouping treats the distinction as analytically real, not stylistic variation. Phileō names natural warmth; agapaō names the willed, total love Peter is uncertain he can claim.
- **Love as feeling vs act:** The vocabulary resists any purely affective reading. Chesed names a disposition not a feeling. Agapaō is commanded of the will. Yet racham is visceral and Song 8:6 is passionate. The full picture holds affective, volitional, and dispositional dimensions simultaneously.
- **Disordered love:** The same vocabulary names both the highest virtue (agapē in 1Cor 13) and its corruption (agapaō directed toward darkness in Joh 3:19; philarguria as root of all evil in 1Ti 6:10). Disorder is misdirection, not absence.

## 3. Divine Dimension Summary

**12 of 66 owner groups have dominant_subject = GOD (18%)**

**Dominant pattern:** God gives and constitutes love before commanding it. The prior divine act is structural: 1Jo 4:19 ("we love because he first loved us") is the most compact statement of this pattern, but it runs through every major love term. The causal direction is consistent: divine love → human love. Human love does not generate divine love; it responds to and participates in it.

**Five divine involvement patterns identified:**
1. God as primary agent of love (Jer 31:3, Joh 3:16, Gal 2:20)
2. God's love as constitutive attribute — what God *is* (Exo 34:6, Psa 63:3, Lam 3:22)
3. God's love directed toward particular persons (Psa 127:2, Jer 12:7, Rom 1:7)
4. God's love mediated through Spirit/Christ (Rom 5:5, 1Jo 4:19)
5. Divine compassion as womb-love — bodily analogue (Isa 49:15, Jer 31:20, Isa 63:15)

**Theological concentration:** The Father-Son love (Mat 3:17 — "my beloved Son") is named in one group as the origin-point of all love in the NT. Joh 17:26 ("the love with which you loved me may be in them") makes the Father-Son love the content of what is given to believers. This is the deepest divine dimension datum in the registry.

**Eschatological dimension:** Three forms — everlasting love (Jer 31:3), eschatological return to divine goodness (Hos 3:5; Jer 31:12-14), universal knowledge of God in the new covenant replacing neighbour-instruction (Jer 31:34).

**Divine-human relationship characterisation:** God's love is prior, constitutive, costly, particular, and bodily-analogue. Human love is derivative, responsive, and parallel in structure to divine love. The derivativeness is not diminishing — it is what makes human love possible.

## 4. Somatic Signature

**Total somatic verse hits (owner terms):** 259 (corrected; initial scan incorrectly matched "steadfast")

**Primary somatic registers:**
- **Heart (57 verses):** Love's primary address. Commanded with the heart (Mat 22:37); received in the heart (Rom 5:5); written on the heart (Pro 3:3); hidden in the heart — hatred as concealed inner state (Lev 19:17). The heart is simultaneously the site of love's reception, expression, and concealment.
- **Kiss (22 instances, concentrated in G2705/G5370):** Love's primary physical gesture. Appears at love's fullest expression (Luk 15:20 — prodigal's return; Luk 7:38 — woman's devotion) and its most devastating violation (Luk 22:48 — Judas' betrayal). The kiss is the body's concentrated enactment of inner relational reality.
- **Womb/inner parts (racham vocabulary):** Structurally somatic — the womb etymology grounds divine compassion in the body's most intimate space. Jer 31:20 ("my heart yearns for him" — meeh, inner parts/bowels) and Isa 63:15 ("the stirring of your inner parts") give divine compassion a felt bodily dimension.
- **Tears/weeping (6-7 instances):** Love overflowing into bodily expression. Most concentrated in Luk 7:38 (woman's devotion) and Act 20:37 (farewell scene).

**Somatic pattern:** Love originates as an inner state (heart/spirit level) and its intensity is revealed through bodily overflow when it cannot be contained. The body is love's evidence, not its source. One exception: Song 5:2 ("I slept, but my heart was awake") — love persists below bodily consciousness.

## 5. Spirit-Soul-Body Provisional Classification

**Classification: Spirit-Soul Interface**

Love at its fullest (agapē, chesed) is received from outside the human person — poured into hearts by the Spirit (Rom 5:5), preceded by God's prior love (1Jo 4:19). This is spirit-primary in origin. As experienced and exercised, love operates characteristically at the soul level — the settled disposition of the inner life, the affective-volitional orientation of the heart. Love's bodily expression (kiss, embrace, tears, running) is regular and genuine but secondary — it manifests love rather than constituting it.

**Confidence: High**

**Distinction from compassion (racham):** Compassion may belong to Soul-Body Interface rather than Spirit-Soul Interface, given its stronger somatic etymology (womb/bowels). The relationship between love and compassion in the spirit-soul-body framework is a Session D question.

## 8. Cross-Word Questions for Session D

The 27 SD pointers (DIM-103-SD003 through SD028, plus pre-existing SD001–SD002) are the full question set. Key analytical questions for Session D:

**Q1 — The love-compassion-mercy triad:** Are love, compassion, and mercy structurally distinct inner states or a single family with internal differentiation? The RACHAM root spanning all three, and the shared verse corpus of 196 verses (love/compassion), suggests a very close relationship. Is compassion love-responding-to-suffering, and mercy love-overriding-just-consequence?

**Q2 — The chesed-goodness nexus:** Does the TOV root connection (love + goodness) reveal that divine goodness is the content of divine love — what love gives? Psa 25:7 placing tuv and chesed in parallel is the key verse.

**Q3 — Love and power (C20 overlap):** All 8 dimensions shared with strength/might/authority/dominion cluster. The 1Cor 13 claim that love is greater than gifts of power implies deliberate tension. Is love the form power takes in the kingdom, and power the form love takes in creation?

**Q4 — Gal 5:22 and the Spirit-fruit hierarchy:** Is love the source from which all other Spirit-fruits flow? If so, love is not one virtue among others but the generative ground of the Spirit-formed inner life. Session D would need to examine each Spirit-fruit registry against this claim.

**Q5 — Forgiveness → love → joy causal chain:** Luk 7:47 (forgiveness produces love), Gal 5:22 (love produces joy). Is there a causal sequence running through the inner life: received forgiveness → love → joy → peace? The co-occurrence data supports the existence of this chain at the verse level.

**Q6 — The new covenant integration:** Jer 31:34 integrates love, knowledge of God, forgiveness, and calling in a single eschatological vision. Is this the programme's master template for what the redeemed inner life looks like — and can Session D reconstruct it from its component registries?
