## 2. Meaning Findings

### Primary semantic picture

Forgiveness in this corpus operates through a single governing act-structure: the deliberate release of a legitimate hold. The vocabulary family (*aphiēmi*, *aphesis*; *sālach*, *sallāch*, *selichah*) consistently names an act of letting-go — what is held is real, the right to hold it is genuine, and the decision to release it is wilful. This is true whether the holding is of a debt (forgiveness proper), a home (discipleship leaving), a gate (permitting access), or a loyalty (abandonment). The forgiveness sense is the theologically and morally concentrated form of this act-structure.

### Significant tensions

**The word-family is broader than the concept.** Three of the fourteen groups (5376-001/002/003) concern the leave/abandon sense of *aphiēmi*, not forgiveness in the usual sense. Their presence in the registry is analytically justified — they establish the governing act-structure — but their inclusion means the corpus is not uniformly about forgiveness. This tension is already present in Session C and is correctly handled there.

**OT objectivity vs. NT subjectivity.** The Levitical corpus (group 5379-001) presents forgiveness as covenantally objective: correct ritual → forgiveness conferred, regardless of the worshipper's inner state. The NT corpus consistently pairs forgiveness with repentance and faith as inner-being preconditions. These are not contradictions but successive covenantal grammars. Session D should examine whether this shift is analytically significant for the inner-being character of forgiveness.

**Forgiveness as available vs. forgiveness as withheld.** The corpus contains both — God's characteristic readiness to forgive (5380-001, 880-001) and God's documented refusals (Deu 29:20, 2Ki 24:4, Lam 3:42, Mar 3:29). Session C handles this correctly in the word study.

### Senses underrepresented or missing from Session C

- The permit/access-granting sense (5378-001/002) is present in Section 4 but its structural significance as *the same act-form as forgiveness* could be named more explicitly.
- Luk 23:34 (Jesus forgiving executioners from the cross) is a non-anchor verse of exceptional significance — forgiveness extended without waiting for repentance — not mentioned in Session C.

## 3. Divine Dimension Summary

**Dominant pattern:** God is the originator, model, and guarantor of forgiveness. In the OT, *sālach* is exclusively divine — no human is ever the subject of this verb. In the NT, forgiveness is granted through Christ's authority (claimed as divine prerogative) and Christ's blood. The human capacity to forgive is presented as derivative and responsive — not self-generated.

**Divine-human relationship type:** Derivative and responsive. Human forgiveness is emphatically modelled on and conditioned by divine forgiveness received (Mat 6:14-15; Mat 18:35; Luk 7:47).

**Eschatological dimension:** Present but not dominant. Jer 31:34 is the programmatic new-covenant statement. Heb 10:18 signals the eschatological finality — where forgiveness is complete, sacrifice ends. Luk 4:18 frames the messianic proclamation of *aphesis* (liberty/forgiveness) in Jubilee-eschatological terms.

## 4. Somatic Signature

**Body-part vocabulary present:** tears/weeping (Luk 7:47 context — not the anchor verse text itself but the surrounding pericope); *splagchnizomai* (Mat 18:27 — bowel-movement metaphor for visceral compassion preceding forgiveness); *kardia* (heart — Mat 18:35); *haima* (blood — Eph 1:7, but this is Christ's blood, not the human person's).

**Somatic pattern:** Diffuse. The forgiveness act itself is not consistently somatically expressed. Somatic involvement is concentrated in:
1. The *reception* of forgiveness (weeping, prostration — expression)
2. The *compassion* that precedes extending forgiveness (*splagchnizomai* — visceral origin)
3. The OT *ritual mechanism* of forgiveness (sacrificial acts — instrument)

**Somatic classification:** Expression (primary); Instrument (secondary, OT only).

## 5. Spirit-Soul-Body Provisional Classification

**Classification: Spirit-soul interface**

Forgiveness at its fullest expression is a spirit-level characteristic — originated in God's own disposition, received as a divine act, capable of producing transformative inner states (love in Luk 7:47; reverential fear in Psa 130:4) that exceed natural capacity. At the practical exercise level, it operates as a soul-level decision of the relational will, within human reach when commanded (Luk 17:3-4). The spirit is the source; the soul is the exercise ground.

**Confidence: Medium.** Session D synthesis may clarify the spirit-soul boundary further.
