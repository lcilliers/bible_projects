The relationship between Scripture and contemporary science is described conceptually in the sub-section on science and the Bible. This sub-section describes the operational consequence: how the scientific lens is engaged within the programme's work, at what point, and through what instrument.

The governing premise is the second-lens position. Scientific literature relevant to the inner being is vast — psychology, neuroscience, physiology, behavioural science, developmental and cognitive psychology, and evolutionary biology each address territory the programme's corpus covers. Treated as a co-equal research programme, the scientific material would demand a second corpus-level effort matching the biblical one in scope and rigour. The programme does not attempt this. It requires representative and reliable coverage of the landmark findings in each relevant field, held alongside the biblical analysis as the second lens through which the same territory is read.

**When science enters the work.** Scientific engagement is structured as a pre-analytical asset — a cluster science review produced once for each cluster, before the cluster's analytical session begins. It is not produced during Session B, and it is not interleaved with the biblical pass. The cluster architecture is stable; the reviews are produced from it as a one-off exercise and made available when the cluster is analysed. During Phase 8 of Session B, when T7.3 prompts are reached, the analyst consults the cluster science review rather than conducting research in-session. Phase 8 remains an application exercise throughout: the biblical pass applies the catalogue prompts to verse evidence; the science prompts apply the catalogue prompts to the pre-prepared review. Running the two passes simultaneously would risk the scientific frame distorting the biblical reading before the biblical analysis is complete, particularly for clusters where the scientific literature is substantial.

**The cluster science review.** The cluster science review is produced by feeding the cluster's gloss list and this framework document to an AI in a dedicated production session. The AI reads the full gloss list, groups the glosses by human science phenomenon, identifies the primary scientific field or fields for each section, and notes any glosses that fall outside human science coverage. The document sections itself according to how science categorises the territory — not according to the programme's sub-group structure, which is determined by the biblical evidence and may cut the material differently. Where science treats different glosses as genuinely distinct phenomena, the review produces separate sections even where the programme's sub-groups do not distinguish them. Where the programme's sub-groups map onto science's categories, the alignment is noted but does not constrain the review's structure. One review document is produced per cluster.

**How the review answers T7.3 prompts.** The four T7.3 prompts are answered by consulting the cluster science review:

- *T7.3.1 (primary field)* — read directly from the review's field assignment for the relevant section.
- *T7.3.2 (where science illuminates)* — apply the review's landmark findings to the specific findings already established in the biblical pass. The question is: where does this scientific account make the finding more coherent or complete?
- *T7.3.3 (divergence)* — identify where the review's scientific account and the biblical findings are in tension. Genuine divergence is noted for Session D cross-cluster synthesis rather than resolved at cluster level.
- *T7.3.4 (gaps surfaced by science)* — note any aspect of the cluster's content that the scientific account addresses but the verse evidence has not yet reached. Flag for the set-aside verse pool or as a cross-cluster pointer.

**The six scientific fields.** The programme works with six fields.

- *Neuroscience* — body-soul boundary and somatic dimension. Neural correlates of inner states, the brain-body connection, how emotion is implemented biologically.
- *Clinical and social psychology* — soul operations. Emotion theory, attachment, moral psychology, shame and guilt research, the psychology of will, motivation, and virtue. Most clusters draw primarily on this field.
- *Physiology and epigenetics* — body circle and generational transmission. Stress physiology, the HPA axis, the bodily expression of grief and joy, the inheritance of patterns across generations.
- *Behavioural science* — relational circle. How inner states express in behaviour and social context, how cooperation and competition shape inner life, the social functions of emotion.
- *Developmental and cognitive psychology* — cognitive circle and the formation of character. How cognitive capacities develop, moral development, conscience, executive function and self-regulation, character formation.
- *Genetics and evolutionary biology* — the created endowment. What is built into the human creature by nature, the heritability of temperament, the evolutionary origins of social emotions, the genetic basis of empathy and conscience.

The six fields are the primary organising framework. Where the gloss data warrants greater precision, sub-fields may be named within a field — for example, affective neuroscience within neuroscience; moral psychology, judgement and decision-making, or psychology of awe within clinical and social psychology; vocational psychology or sport psychology within developmental and cognitive psychology. Sub-field naming is descriptive and does not alter the six-field structure. Where a cluster spans multiple fields, the primary field is named first for each section.

**Outside-science coverage.** Every cluster contains glosses that fall outside human science's domain. This is not a gap in the programme's science engagement — it is a structural feature of the material. The theological vertical dimension of the vocabulary (divine acts, covenantal categories, eschatological realities, the ontological claims of salvation and sanctification) is systematically outside science's scope. Science addresses human experience, not divine action. The cluster science review names outside-science content explicitly in a dedicated section for each cluster, and treats it as a positive finding: identifying where the biblical account addresses territory that science does not reach is itself analytically significant for Session D synthesis.

**Clusters with thin science coverage.** Some clusters are predominantly outside human science's domain. Where a cluster's core content is theological in the sense described above — redemption, propitiation, divine calling, prophetic revelation — the cluster science review will be short. The review covers only the experiential edge of the cluster: what human beings report experiencing in relation to the cluster's content, where that experience is within science's scope. The review does not force scientific framing onto theological content, and a short honest review is preferred over a padded one.

**The reference shelf.** The scientific pass uses a curated reference shelf — a set of foundational texts and landmark papers organised by field, chosen for breadth of coverage against the corpus. The shelf is not a reading list to complete before analysis begins; it is consulted during the production of the cluster science reviews. Where the shelf carries a text of high relevance to the cluster being reviewed, that text is the primary scientific reference for the section. Where a cluster falls outside the shelf's coverage, a targeted search fills the gap. A recurring gap across multiple clusters signals that the shelf should be extended.

**The no-database decision.** The programme does not build a structured scientific database to match its biblical database. Three reasons. A parallel scientific database at the depth of the biblical one would constitute a second programme of comparable scale and would defer synthesis. The data types are not structurally equivalent: biblical data is lexical and classifiable, while scientific findings are arguments, observations, and interpretations that require judgement to apply. And the programme does not need it — the second-lens role is served by the cluster science reviews.

**Convergence and divergence.** The analytical work of the T7.3 prompts is organised by a framework that distinguishes three kinds of convergence and three kinds of divergence. Convergence can be phenomenological — the two accounts describing the same experience from different angles. It can be mechanistic — science describing the mechanism of what Scripture describes as a reality. It can be structural — the architecture of the inner life having the same shape in both accounts. Divergence can be register divergence — the accounts addressing different levels of the same reality and only appearing to conflict. It can be reductive divergence — a scientific account that reduces the inner-being characteristic to its biological substrate and loses dimensions the biblical account treats as primary. It can be genuine divergence — claims in actual tension that cannot be harmonised by reframing. Genuine divergence is flagged for the cross-cluster synthesis phase rather than resolved at the cluster level.

**What the review produces.** The cluster science review for each cluster contains: the science sections (each with field label, landmark findings, and the two or three key researchers or frameworks), and the outside-science inventory. The review is the standing reference document for the cluster's T7.3 work and is carried into the cluster's Session B record as a named input.
