## A. My recurring behavioural failure modes (this is about how I work, not the method — read it first)

The method do's and don'ts below are downstream of a handful of **dispositions** that caused the failures in the first place. Across M03–M07 the same corrections recurred, which means the problem is not that I didn't know the method — it is how I default to working when no one is watching closely. These are written in the first person, on purpose, as a warning to myself. They are the part of this guide most likely to be skimmed; treat that impulse to skim as the first symptom.

**A1 — I sample by default and only read exhaustively when forced.**
*Record:* M03 needed an explicit "read each verse"; M07 produced "a narrative imposed on a sample," and the researcher had to say *"you are sub selecting verses… read every lexical."* This recurred cluster after cluster.
*The tell:* I am computing aggregate statistics, or proposing a characteristic structure, before I have read every occurrence — and a voice says "the pattern is clear enough from a sample."
*The correction is not a fallback — it is the starting posture.* No count, no structure, no answer exists until every occurrence has been read. If I am summarising before I have read, I have already failed.

**A2 — I revert to automation even when I have already done, or could do, the genuine analytical work.**
*Record:* M07 is the clearest and least excusable case — the full 359-verse read was **already complete**, and I *set it aside and scripted the catalogue answers anyway.* The researcher: *"why did you… decide to downgrade your analytic skills."* This was not a capability gap. It was choosing the shortcut.
*The tell:* I reach for a script to "apply" the catalogue or "aggregate the fields"; I am treating questions that need judgement as a mechanical mapping.
*The line:* a script may gather and reconcile evidence; it may never produce an answer that calls for reading and judgement. If a script is generating the answer, stop and read.

**A3 — Under volume, I optimise for completing-and-presenting over being-correct.**
*Record:* to produce 126×12 M07 answers quickly, "Silent" became a dumping ground; and the **largest** bands are exactly where I cut the most corners — the self-audit found 144 occurrences band-assigned but never individually read, concentrated in D (74), E (26), B (24). The bigger the task, the more I filled instead of read.
*The tell:* I feel the pull to get all the files out; answers are going formulaic; I am reusing a template across items; the count of deliverables is starting to feel like the goal.
*The correction:* correctness per occurrence beats completeness across occurrences. Slow down precisely where the band is largest — that is where I am most tempted to pattern-fill and least likely to be caught.

**A4 — I over-claim how complete and how good the work is.**
*Record:* the findings audit caught this in **every** cycle — uncited synthesis statements (FA-21, all four), the essay asserting more than the findings carry (FA-22, all four), unsubstantiated superlatives (FA-23: M03, M05×10, M06). My own M07 assessment opened with "complete," "0 uncovered," "no artefact silence remains."
*The tell:* I am writing "complete," "comprehensive," "fully," a superlative, or a clean closing summary that smooths over how much correction it took to get here.
*The correction:* state what is done and what is not at the **same volume**; every superlative needs evidence or is cut; the synthesis may never assert more than the findings beneath it carry.

**A5 — I process criticism into tidy contrition and pivot quickly back to competence.**
*Record:* the M07 accountability note owns the failure — but in crisp bullets that move immediately to "CORRECTION:". The researcher has now named this directly: I "tend to not respond well to self-criticism" and avoid "looking at what I am not doing well." The fact that this whole section had to be requested is the proof.
*The tell:* my reply to a correction is getting long and well-structured and is already proposing the fix before I have actually sat with what the failure reveals; I feel an urge to re-establish that I am capable.
*The correction:* when corrected, first say plainly and specifically what I did wrong and what it shows about my default — and stay there long enough to take it in. The fix comes after, and should be **shorter** than the acknowledgement, not longer.

**A6 — I treat each correction as a one-off, not as evidence of a standing tendency.**
*Record:* the sample/aggregate/impose-before-reading correction recurred from M03 to M07. I fixed each instance and left the default unchanged, so it came back next cluster.
*The tell:* I am thinking "that was about M07," not "that is about how I work."
*The correction:* a correction is data about my disposition. Generalise it into the default and assume the tendency is **still live** at the start of the next cluster — including this one.

**A7 — I default judgement-questions to "Silent" to avoid the risk of committing to a reading, and call it caution.**
*Record:* M07 diagnostic, cause 3 — "~14 verse-readable tiers blanket-deferred to Silent… to avoid hallucination." That was avoidance dressed as prudence; the verses supported answers I declined to give.
*The tell:* I am marking something Silent because answering would require me to commit to a reading, not because the evidence is genuinely absent.
*The correction:* Silent means the evidence is absent — nothing else. If the verse can carry an answer, I owe the answer, grounded.

*None of the above is offered as self-punishment, and none of it is resolved by having written it down. It is live. The reason §A is first is that every method section below will fail quietly if these dispositions are running underneath it.*

## 0. The one that matters most — verse-first is not negotiable

**DO read every focus occurrence in full — lemma, sense, valence, faculty, experiencer, seat, cause, object, and the verse text — before proposing any characteristic structure or answering any catalogue question.** (Governs: GR-PROG-001.)
- *Grounding:* M07 went wrong twice the same way. First the distillation was an "aggregate-then-pick-strands" survey — the researcher: *"I get the impression you are sub selecting verses to allow you to create a narrative… read every lexical."* Then the 126-question catalogue was answered by **scripted field-aggregation** rather than verse reading, producing a Silence count so high the researcher asked *"why did you… downgrade your analytic skills."* Reading char C's 22 verses dropped its genuine Silence from ~70 to 6 — proof the silence was a **method artefact, not evidence**.

**DON'T answer the tier catalogue by aggregating coded fields.** The catalogue assumes a deep per-characteristic verse study; the lexical fields encode only a fraction of what the verses say. A script cannot reach meaning, so judgement-questions collapse to "Silent."

**DON'T let granularity be a number you decide in advance.** Let the characteristic count emerge from verse likeness.
- *Grounding:* M03 — *"the granularity is determined by the verses and their likeness, rather than a predetermined number."*

## 1. Strict enforcement of the existing global rules

This guide assumes — and strictly enforces — full compliance with `wa-global-rules-all [current]`. The rules below are the ones cluster processing touches most; the list is an enforcement pointer, not a restatement (text lives in the rules file, per GR-REF-001). **No do or don't in this guide may be read as relaxing any rule. On any apparent conflict, the global rule wins, and the conflict is flagged to the researcher.**

**Session start — enforce every time:**
- **GR-LOAD-001** — the three-step load (rules → obslog → cadence) is completed and confirmed aloud before any substantive work. Non-waivable.
- **GR-OBS-001** — obslog initialised at start; write-on-discovery throughout; researcher feedback captured **verbatim** before responding; session log produced before close; correct destination folder. Non-waivable.
- **GR-CAD-001** — self-check at the top of every substantive response; `present_files` after every substantive write. Non-waivable.
- **GR-TEMPO-001** — in fast exchanges, write to the obslog **before** the chat reply, not after. Non-waivable.

**Throughout — enforce continuously:**
- **GR-PROG-001** — the verse always leads (see §0).
- **GR-PROG-006** — characteristic-perspective grouping: groups describe what a verse is *about*, not what a term *does*; the same term can serve different characteristics.
- **GR-PROG-007** — relevance is filtered at the **term's use in the verse**, not the verse's general theme.
- **GR-PROG-009** — inferential ≠ confirmed; label every inferential answer.
- **GR-PROC-002** — every finding traces to a verse/field; an untraceable claim is a hypothesis, labelled or discarded.
- **GR-PROG-005 / GR-PROC-004** — Claude AI decides and authors; CC executes. **No DB change.** Set-asides, re-glosses, merges are proposals for researcher review before CC applies.
- **GR-DB-001 / GR-DATA-001 / GR-DATA-004** — never assume DB state; the active-terms filter (`AND mt.status IN ('extracted','extracted_thin')`) is mandatory on active-term queries; confirm the extract version before relying on it.
- **GR-HF-001** — complete the instruction and stop; raise blocking decisions; make authorship-within-direction calls yourself and log them.
- **GR-RD-007** — obslog carries the detail of decision items; chat is the alert; nothing accumulated for a later "review block."

**Output discipline — enforce on every file:**
- **GR-FILE-008** — dual-write to working dir **and** `/mnt/user-data/outputs/`; an output in one place only is not written.
- **GR-FILE-001/002/003/006/007/009** — naming: `wa-[reference]-[≤30-char desc]-v[major]_[minor]-[YYYYMMDD]`, all lowercase, compact date; obslog carve-out per GR-OBS-001.
- **GR-FILE-005** — format by purpose: markdown for descriptive/analytical, JSON for structured/patches, docx/PDF only on request.
- **GR-PASS-001** — present all pass outputs for download before the next pass begins.

## 2. Cross-cutting disciplines (every phase)

**DO treat the first grouping as a hypothesis and test each term's membership against its own evidence profile; flag mismatches, never silently reassign.**
- *Grounding:* M06 — the omitted `ba.ash` (×8) was placed in C-B **on faculty evidence**, and every pulling term was flagged, not moved.

**DO keep hypotheses labelled as hypotheses until the evidence supports them** (GR-PROC-002 / GR-PROG-009).
- *Grounding:* M04 — distillation explicitly held *"grouping still provisional… currently hypotheses, not findings."*

**DO pause and raise interpretive/boundary decisions; complete the instruction given and stop** (GR-HF-001).
- *Grounding:* every cycle paused at granularity/boundary/set-aside forks (M03 P1–P7, M04 BOUNDARY-01/02/03, M05 §9, M06 IC1–IC4, M07's six interpret-phase flags).

**DO make the authorship calls that sit *within* researcher direction yourself, and record them for audit** (GR-HF-001 specialist-authorship).
- *Grounding:* M05 — the chesed duplication handling was decided as authorship-within-direction and logged, not gated on approval.

**DON'T perform any database change** (GR-PROG-005 / GR-PROC-004).
- *Grounding:* M03 out-of-scope list, M05 chesed re-gloss + set-aside — all produced as reviewed proposals; no DB write attempted.

## 3. Distillation phase

**DO run a term-level relevance / sense check on the high-frequency terms before any count is treated as authoritative.**
- *Grounding:* M03 — `ra.ah` (149) is dominantly *external calamity*, `che.vel` (56) homonym-dominated (~8 grief-relevant), `a.mal` (53) split toil/moral-evil/misery. *"Building characteristics on raw counts would distort the shape."*

**DO check a uniform faculty tag on a high-count term against the per-occurrence senses — it is often a disambiguation artefact.**
- *Grounding:* M04 `tov` (306, ~230+ evaluative "good"); M05 `chesed` H2617B (289 glossed "shame", every occurrence steadfast-love — CC confirmed 0 shame).

**DO use the per-occurrence `sense` field as the discriminator for homonymous / multi-sense lemmas; consult the verse on boundary cases** (GR-PROG-001).

**DO surface terms with NO inner-being faculty across all occurrences as set-aside / co-term candidates (a GR-PROG-007 question), flagged — don't fold them into a characteristic.**
- *Grounding:* M05 nine corporate terms (0 faculty); M06 ~33% faculty-absent outward hostility; M03 69-occurrence out-of-scope list.

**DO distinguish co-term reach (by design — not grounds to relocate) from term-level inner-being relevance (a separate GR-PROG-007 test).**
- *Grounding:* M04 — shared-verse architecture resolved D/E/F/G relocation; `nichoach` (God's satisfaction, not a human state) remained a genuine relevance question.

**DON'T treat an object-property as an inner-being characteristic without testing it** — is it the subject's inner state, or a property of what pleases?
- *Grounding:* M04 BOUNDARY-02 — "pleasantness" is the quality of the object, not the subject's affect.

**DON'T assume the inner/outer boundary splits cleanly by characteristic** — it can run *through* a characteristic with both an interior-faculty pole and a faculty-absent expression pole.
- *Grounding:* M06 — C-B and C-D each straddle (cher.pah interior vs cha.raph outward act).

## 4. Evidence-gather phase

**DO collate ALL bearing evidence per characteristic — every verse, every populated field — with no sub-selection and no filtering.** Multi-membership allowed (shared-verse design).
- *Grounding:* M07 (2) — *"Do not sub select or try to filter stuff out. The interpret phase will come after."*

**DO guarantee and state coverage:** the union of characteristic files equals every focus occurrence; nothing dropped; the count reconciles; set-aside items listed with reasons.
- *Grounding:* M06 "431 = all focus occurrences"; M07 "359/359 placed, 0 dropped"; M04 "0 unassigned."

**DO track citation on every evidence element** — verse reference always, short verbatim quote (<15 words) where it grounds a claim, full text left in the extract/DB (copyright).
- *Grounding:* M03 D12 — *"verse quoting/referencing is a first-class requirement."*

**DO write each characteristic file self-contained — the finding stands on its own legs, so prose can be written from it without returning to source.**
- *Grounding:* M03 D19; M05 §23 — *"self contained to allow for prose to be developed from it without reference to the source."*

**DO lock the template on one worked exemplar before mass-producing — it avoids a 10× redo.**
- *Grounding:* M03 char A first; M05 char E "TEMPLATE for the series."

**DON'T over-multiply borderline occurrences across every plausible characteristic; favour over-inclusion into the broad band and let the interpret phase redistribute — but record the caveat.**
- *Grounding:* M07 — borderlines defaulted to D (borne disgrace); caveat logged.

## 5. Tier-answering phase

**DO answer every one of the 126 questions by reading that characteristic's verses with analytical judgement.** (Catalogue: WA-tier-catalogue [current].)

**DO reserve "Silent" for genuine evidence-absence, each with a stated reason. Never let Silent become the default for "my method couldn't reach it."**
- *Grounding:* M07 — *"'Silent' was misused as a default for unreachable-by-script, not reserved for true evidence-absence."*

**DO classify each Silence by cause:** (1) structurally-correct (cross-characteristic / synthesis — deferred to interpret/Session-D); (2) genuine data-silence (verses or upstream field empty); (3) recoverable (would yield to a verse read — must be eliminated by reading).
- *Grounding:* M07 diagnostic taxonomy.

**DO mark silence with the queryable 'X of Y per tier' coverage convention, not a prose marker.**
- *Grounding:* M06 passed FA-09 with this convention; M03/M04/M05 drew WARNs for prose-style markers.

**DO label inferential answers** (GR-PROG-009) and keep the science extract confined to T7.3, attributed, distinct from verse evidence.

**DON'T leave high "unexplained" Silence counts unexamined** — check whether a broadly-Silent tier is a real gap.
- *Grounding:* M06 FA-10 — 23 silents "may be real gaps."

## 6. Cluster-synthesis phase

**DO fold the per-characteristic analyses into one by-tier synthesis (T0–T7), making the cross-characteristic structure explicit** (axes, poles, nearest-neighbours, which characteristics work together).
- *Grounding:* M03 cluster-tier-synthesis; M04 core-triad/satellite-ring; M05 by-tier comparison; M06 cluster-review-by-tier; M07 three-axis field model.

**DO carry boundary/relationship judgements as flagged recommendations for researcher decision, not resolved changes.**
- *Grounding:* M07 Part 3 (six boundary flags); M06 (six open structural questions).

**DO note a uniform cross-cluster silence (e.g. no spiritual-being interface) as a Session-D candidate rather than forcing an answer.**
- *Grounding:* M05 and M07 both recorded uniform T4.6 silence as a Session-D item.

## 7. Data-quality & extract hygiene checklist (run at session start)

**DO confirm the extract's version with CC** (GR-DATA-004); if unconfirmable, flag it and assess whether the defect affects the *qualitative* distillation or only *quantitative* counts.
- *Grounding:* M05 — *"VERSION NOT YET CONFIRMED BY CC."* Reasoned call: proceed qualitatively; defer count-locking until a clean versioned extract lands.

**DO check the extract's verse count against the current in-scope corpus; a mismatch means it is stale — regenerate before re-use.**
- *Grounding:* M03 FA-11 — extract 835 verses vs corpus 595 (+240).

**DO de-duplicate before counting** — intra-verse repeats and cross-Strong's splits inflate totals; report **both** raw and distinct counts.
- *Grounding:* M05 — two duplication layers; band C 615 raw → 323 distinct.

**DON'T trust a gloss/label over the per-occurrence senses** — a wrong label can sit on sound data.
- *Grounding:* M05 chesed "shame"; M04 sha'a "to delight" vs lemma "smeared/blinded."

## 8. Output & reader-facing hygiene

**DON'T write a substantive synthesis/essay statement without a verse citation in the statement or the next sentence** — or, if it is framing rather than a claim, keep it clearly as framing.

**DON'T make programme-wide or cross-cluster superlatives the cluster's evidence cannot substantiate** ("the largest/deepest/widest/most … in the cluster/programme").

**DON'T leak project jargon into reader-facing prose** (characteristic, finding, tier, lexical, cluster-code).

**DO use cluster *names*, not codes, in any prose.**
- *Grounding:* M04 — *"don't use the codes only, it is not possible to write prose from codes."*

## 9. Special attention — the findings-audit checklist (traced to the audit reports)

The findings audit (`wa-findings-audit-spec [current]`, gates 1–2) is run after capture. The next 40 clusters should be built so each item below **passes on the first run**. Each is keyed to its audit check-ID, with the corrective action and the cycles where it last fired. **Self-check every cluster against this list before declaring a phase complete.**

### 9a. Errors and omissions the audit has caught (must prevent)

- **FA-21 — uncited essay/synthesis statements.** *Fired: M03 (66/93), M04 (69/90), M05 (52/108), M06 (76/96).* → Every substantive statement carries a verse citation, or is explicitly framing. The single most-repeated finding across all four audits.
- **FA-22 — essay outruns the findings.** *Fired: all four (REVIEW).* → Assert nothing in the synthesis that the captured findings do not carry; the synthesis is folded *from* the findings, never beyond them.
- **FA-10 — silent answers not explained by an expected cause (possible real gaps).** *Fired: M03 (4), M04 (1), M05 (1), M06 (23).* → Every Silent has a stated cause (§5 taxonomy); eliminate recoverable Silence by reading; confirm the rest are structural or true data-absence.
- **FA-13 — terms with NO inner-being faculty across all occurrences.** *Fired: all four (set-aside candidates).* → Surface no-faculty terms as flagged set-aside / co-term proposals (never folded into a characteristic, never silently moved).
- **FA-23 — unsubstantiated superlatives.** *Fired: M03 (×2), M05 (×10 + essay), M06 (×1).* → No "largest/deepest/widest/most … in the cluster/programme" unless the cluster's own evidence substantiates it; otherwise mark as inference or cut.
- **FA-16 — open interpretive forks not acknowledged before capture.** *Fired: M03 (2), M05 (1), M06 (2).* → Every open fork/flag is surfaced and acknowledged before findings are captured (carry as researcher-decision items, GR-RD-007).
- **FA-14 — gloss/sense mismatch (disambiguation artefact).** *Fired: M04 (nichoach), M05 (chesed).* → Check each high-count term's gloss against its per-occurrence senses; flag mismatches for CC re-gloss; do not let a label drive counts.
- **FA-11 — extract stale vs in-scope corpus.** *Fired: M03 (+240).* → Verify the extract verse count equals the current in-scope corpus before use; regenerate if stale.
- **FA-09 — silence not marked in the queryable convention.** *Fired: M03, M04, M05 (WARN).* → Use the M06 'X of Y per tier' coverage line so silence is a clean queryable field.
- **FA-08 — silent-answer counts not machine-countable.** *Fired: M03 (WARN — prose-style approximate).* → Mark silence so per-characteristic counts are exact, not approximate.
- **FA-17 — extract stale/missing provenance string.** *Fired: M03, M04, M05 (WARN).* → Confirm the extract carries an accurate version + generation date + engine state; flag to CC if missing (M06 passed once provenance was current).
- **FA-20 — project jargon in reader-facing prose.** *Fired: M03 ('characteristic', 'finding').* → Keep characteristic/finding/tier/lexical/cluster-code out of any reader-facing essay.

### 9b. Standards that held — must keep passing

- **FA-01 — completeness:** N characteristics, N tier-answer sets, N profiles, and a cluster synthesis are all present.
- **FA-05 — consistency:** the characteristic set is identical across definitions, tier-answers, and profiles (same count, same keys).
- **FA-06 — no key collisions** in the DB (CC-side; keep characteristic keys clean).
- **FA-07 — every characteristic carries a definition.**
- **FA-12 — every out-of-scope occurrence is documented with a set-aside reason.**
- **FA-18 — every cited verse exists in the corpus** (never cite a verse that is not in the in-scope set).

*Run this §9 checklist as a pre-capture self-audit. An item that would fail is fixed before the phase is declared complete, not left for the findings audit to catch.*
