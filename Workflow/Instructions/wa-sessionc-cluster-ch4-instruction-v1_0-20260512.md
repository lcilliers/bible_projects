## 1. What this chapter does

Chapter 4 takes each characteristic in the study in turn and goes deep on **where in the inner person it lives**. For every characteristic, you write a single section that answers:

- **Where it lives.** Which named inner location does the verse evidence place this characteristic in — heart, mind, will, conscience, soul, spirit, body? Is it located narrowly (one named seat) or broadly (across the whole person)? Are there divine-versus-human differences in location?
- **Which inner capacities it engages.** Of the standard inner capacities — perception, thought, memory, feeling, conscience, will, agency — which ones does this characteristic call into action, and in what relationship to one another?

This is the **first** of the per-characteristic deep dives. It establishes the inner topography for the study. Later chapters will pick up movement (Ch 5), relationships (Ch 6), and the view from outside Scripture (Ch 7).

---

## 2. Structure of the chapter

The chapter input file lays out the structure for you. You fill the AI-WRITE zones, in order:

1. **Chapter opening (~40–80 words).** A brief framing sentence or two announcing what this chapter covers. Do not say "this is the T2/T3 chapter" — instead describe what you are about to do in the reader's terms ("Where these characteristics live in the inner person…").
2. **Per-characteristic sections, in order.** One per non-BOUNDARY characteristic. Each section header is provided; you write only the body prose.
3. **No closing summary.** The chapter ends with the last per-characteristic section. The study's overall closing belongs to Chapter 8.

---

## 3. Per-characteristic section — what to write

For each characteristic's section (~800–1500 words):

**Open** with where the characteristic sits in the inner person — the single most defensible inner location given the evidence — and quote the key verse that most clearly shows it.

**Develop** the inner location with secondary evidence: other key verses that confirm, extend, or qualify the primary location.

**Then turn to the inner capacities** the characteristic engages. Move through the relevant capacities in turn — perception, thought, memory, feeling, conscience, will, agency — citing key verses where the evidence shows the capacity active.

**Where the evidence shows a divine-versus-human distinction** in location (e.g. God's wisdom belongs to him as attribute; human wisdom is heart-located), make that distinction explicit and cite the verses on each side.

**Where the evidence is silent** on a specific inner location or capacity, name the silence rather than fill it. Silent findings in the evidence block are explicit signals of what *not* to claim.

**Close** the section with a sentence that captures what the inner topography of this characteristic looks like as a whole — but do not preview what is to come in later chapters.

---

## 4. Length

**~800–1500 words per characteristic section.** This is descriptive guidance, not a cap. If a characteristic has rich evidence and the discussion needs more space, take it. If a characteristic has thin evidence and 800 words would pad, write fewer. The chapter's overall length is whatever the evidence supports.

**Length never constrains comprehensiveness.** Every analytical claim warranted by the evidence belongs in the prose.

---

## 5. Citation rules for this chapter

Per the shared style/method document, every analytical claim is grounded by a quoted verse. For Chapter 4 specifically:

- **Each per-characteristic section quotes at least three key verses verbatim.** The required minimum is listed inside the AI-WRITE zone in the input file.
- **At least one quoted verse must come from the meaning group that names the inner location** (typically the verses tagged with constitutional or location language in the evidence below your zone).
- **The first quoted verse in each section should establish the primary inner location** named for the characteristic.

Citations use standard biblical references (Pro 16:23, Isa 40:28, Rom 8:5). Never use VCG codes, tier codes, or finding-ids in the prose.

---

## 6. How to read the evidence in the input

For each characteristic, the chapter input gives you:

- **A profile line** (provided at the top of the input file across all characteristics, for cross-section consistency).
- **The sub-group description** from the analytical record (one paragraph).
- **All key verses for the characteristic**, quoted verbatim with their meaning-group context description.
- **All T2 evidence** (the analytical record's findings on where the characteristic lives — constitutional and location language).
- **All T3 evidence** (the analytical record's findings on which inner capacities are engaged).
- **All T2 and T3 silences** (where Scripture is silent on a location or capacity, named explicitly).

You weave from these. You do not need to cite the findings — the findings are your **sources**; the prose is *your* synthesis. Cite the **verses** the findings draw from.

---

## 7. Handling BOUNDARY characteristics

The cluster's BOUNDARY characteristic (supporting terms that are not themselves inner-being characteristics) is **not treated in this chapter**. It receives a one-paragraph acknowledgement in Chapter 2 and a mention in Chapter 8. The chapter input file omits it for Chapter 4.

## 9. Quality bar

A chapter-4 section passes the quality bar when:

1. The primary inner location is stated and cited within the first 100 words.
2. At least three key verses are quoted verbatim.
3. Inner capacities are addressed in turn, each grounded by at least one verse.
4. Divine-human distinctions in location, where the evidence shows them, are made explicit.
5. Silences in the evidence are not papered over.
6. No jargon from the shared instruction's avoid-list appears in the prose.
7. The section reads as continuous essayistic prose, not a list of findings restated.
