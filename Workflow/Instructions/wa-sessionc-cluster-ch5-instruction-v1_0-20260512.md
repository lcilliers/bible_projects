## 1. What this chapter does

Chapter 5 takes each characteristic in the study in turn and goes deep on **how the characteristic works in the inner person**. Where Chapter 4 established the inner topography (where the characteristic lives, which capacities it engages), Chapter 5 sets that topography in motion. For every characteristic, you write a single section that answers:

- **How it moves.** Between God and the human person (downward — gift, command, indwelling; upward — knowing, asking, receiving). Between persons (teaching, counsel, exhortation, accord). In covenant. Against opposition (resistance, suppression, hardening, deception).
- **How it is formed.** Conditions that produce it (fear of the Lord, humility, instruction, practice, the Spirit). Disciplines that deepen it. Trials and sufferings that test and refine it. The eschatological trajectory — where the characteristic is going.
- **How it modulates.** Modes of operation (immediate response vs. sustained character; act vs. condition; received vs. exercised). What turns it on and what turns it off.

This chapter draws on three lenses together: how it moves (T4), how it is formed (T5), and the modes captured under what the characteristic is (T1). The chapter's task is to synthesise these into a single account of the characteristic in motion.

## 2. Structure of the chapter

The chapter input file lays out the structure. You fill the AI-WRITE zones, in order:

1. **Chapter opening (~40–80 words).** A brief framing sentence or two announcing what this chapter covers. Do not say "this is the T4/T5 chapter" — describe what you are about to do in the reader's terms ("How these characteristics work — how they move, how they are formed, how they deepen…").
2. **Per-characteristic sections, in order.** One per non-BOUNDARY characteristic. Each section header is provided; you write only the body prose.
3. **No closing summary.** The chapter ends with the last per-characteristic section. The study's overall closing belongs to Chapter 8.

---

## 3. Per-characteristic section — what to write

For each characteristic's section (~800–1500 words):

**Open** with the most distinctive aspect of how this characteristic works — usually the dominant direction of movement (e.g. wisdom moves downward from God and outward through speech; knowledge moves both as informational content and as covenant relation). Anchor the opening with a key verse that captures the movement.

**Develop the movement.** Trace the characteristic's flow:
- Where it comes from (origin: divine gift, divine command, the Spirit, human reception)
- How it is exchanged or transmitted between persons
- Where it goes (expression: speech, conduct, governance, community)
- How opposition acts against it (refusal, hardening, suppression, counterfeit)

**Then turn to formation.** How the characteristic comes into being and deepens within a person — the disciplines, conditions, and trials that produce it. Where the evidence shows the characteristic tested or refined, name that.

**Then turn to modes.** Where the evidence shows the characteristic operating in distinct modes (immediate vs. sustained; act vs. quality; exercised vs. constituted), name those modes and cite the verses that hold each mode.

**Where the evidence shows a divine-versus-human distinction** in how the characteristic works — for example, divine wisdom operates as effortless attribute while human wisdom must be sought, received, and grown — make that distinction explicit and cite verses on each side.

**Where the evidence is silent** on a movement or formation aspect, apply the **silence principle from the shared style document §6**: name the silence only when its absence is meaningful (expectation-then-absence or silence-that-shapes). Do not catalogue routine absences.

**Close** the section with a sentence that captures the characteristic's working life as a whole. Do not preview what is to come in later chapters.

---

## 4. Length

**~800–1500 words per characteristic section.** Descriptive guidance, not a cap. Rich evidence warrants more space; thin evidence warrants less. **Length never constrains comprehensiveness** — every analytical claim warranted by the evidence belongs in the prose.

---

## 5. Citation rules for this chapter

Per the shared style/method document, every analytical claim is grounded by a quoted verse. For Chapter 5 specifically:

- **Each per-characteristic section quotes at least three key verses verbatim.** The required minimum is listed inside the AI-WRITE zone in the input file.
- **At least one quoted verse must hold a movement claim** (e.g. wisdom given, knowledge sought, understanding opened) — the verses tagged as movement-relevant in the T4 evidence.
- **At least one quoted verse should hold a formation claim** — a verse where the characteristic is grown, tested, transmitted, or transformed (T5 evidence).
- **The opening of each section should cite a verse that captures the characteristic's most distinctive movement.**

Citations use standard biblical references. Never use VCG codes, tier codes, or finding-ids in the prose.

## 6. How to read the evidence in the input

For each characteristic, the chapter input gives you:

- **A profile line** (provided at the top of the input file across all characteristics, for cross-section consistency).
- **All T4 evidence** (how the characteristic moves — directions, agents, recipients, oppositions).
- **All T5 evidence** (how the characteristic is formed, deepens, is tested, is transformed).
- **All T1 evidence** (modes, definitional aspects, immediate vs. sustained, act vs. condition).
- **All T4 / T5 / T1 silences** (where Scripture is silent on a movement or formation pattern, named explicitly — apply the silence principle).
- **All key verses for the characteristic**, quoted verbatim with their meaning-group context description.

You weave from these. Cite the verses the findings draw from, not the findings themselves.

---

## 7. Avoiding overlap with Chapter 4 and Chapter 6

- **Where the characteristic lives** (inner location, faculties engaged) was the subject of Chapter 4. Do not re-establish location here. You may *refer* to location when relevant (e.g. "wisdom that has settled in the heart now expresses itself through…") but the chapter's centre is movement and formation, not seating.
- **How the characteristic relates to other characteristics in this study** (its pairings, opposites, productions) is the subject of Chapter 6. Do not catalogue inter-characteristic relationships here. Where the evidence shows formation by another characteristic (e.g. "wisdom begins with the fear of the Lord"), that is in scope for Chapter 5 — the formation pattern is what matters. The fuller mapping of relationships belongs to Chapter 6.
- The general principle: Chapter 5 is about the characteristic *in motion within and around the person*. Adjacent material that supports that motion is in scope; adjacent material that maps to a different chapter's purpose is not.

## 10. Quality bar

A chapter-5 section passes the quality bar when:

1. The opening sentence states the characteristic's most distinctive movement and cites a verse for it.
2. At least three key verses are quoted verbatim across the section.
3. Movement, formation, and modes are all addressed (each anchored by at least one quoted verse) unless the evidence is genuinely silent on one — in which case the silence is named only if meaningful per §6 of the shared style document.
4. Divine-human distinctions in how the characteristic works, where the evidence shows them, are made explicit.
5. Silences in the evidence are surfaced only where meaningful — routine absences are not catalogued.
6. No content has crossed into Chapter 4 territory (location/capacities) or Chapter 6 territory (inter-characteristic mapping).
7. No jargon from the shared instruction's avoid-list appears in the prose.
8. The section reads as continuous essayistic prose, not a list of findings restated.

## 11. Completion procedure

Per shared style §12, on completion you:

1. **Write** the chapter.
2. **Reverse audit** (shared §10): walk every finding in every EVIDENCE block in scope; confirm each is either present in the prose, justifiably omitted as routine silent, or redundant with a covered finding. Walk every key verse in the section's cite list; confirm each is quoted verbatim at least once. Confirm T4, T5, and T1 each engaged.
3. **Self-review pass** (shared §11): repetition → overreach → padding → tonal drift → structural drift.
4. **Return** the finished chapter file.
