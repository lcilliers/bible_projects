# Cluster Essay — Writing & Style Template (CC-authored, cluster-level, by tier)

- **Type:** Authoring template for Claude-Code-written cluster essays. **Version:** v1_0 · **Date:** 2026-06-20
- **Derived from:** `wa-sessionc-cluster-style-method-v1_1`, `wa-global-sessionc-prose-rule-v1_1`, the Session C chapter instructions (ch1–ch7).
- **Use:** load this with a cluster's captured prose-foundation (the `prose_section` cluster-synthesis + per-characteristic syntheses) to write one **cluster-level essay, organised by tier, for the general reader**.

---

## 1. What is being written
A **plain-English published study** of one family of related inner-being characteristics from Scripture. **One essay per cluster.** The reader is an intelligent general reader with **no familiarity with this project** or its vocabulary — they read it as a serious essay on the Bible's treatment of a theme.

## 2. Voice and tone
- **Essayistic** — continuous prose, topic sentences carry the argument; not bullet lists.
- **Evidential** — every analytical claim is grounded by a **quoted verse**, never by general impression.
- **Reverent but not devotional** — a study of Scripture, not a sermon; no liturgical phrasing.
- **Confident, not hedging** — drop "perhaps / it could be argued / in some sense" when the evidence is plain.
- **Third person**; avoid first-person plural ("we see…"). State the evidence and let it speak.

## 3. Completeness (the governing quality rule)
**Do not omit findings for the sake of simplicity.** Every observation in the foundation must be reflected in the prose. On completion run a **reverse audit**: walk every finding in the foundation and confirm it is either (a) present in the prose, (b) a *routine* silence justified under §6, or (c) redundant with one already covered. Anything else was missed — add it. Comprehensiveness over tidiness.

## 4. Structure — by tier (rendered as reader-facing themes, never as codes)
Organise the essay as sections following the eight lenses below. **Never name the codes**; use the theme. Order may be adjusted for flow, but every lens with evidence must be engaged.

| Lens (theme — use this) | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **The divine pattern** | what Scripture says about God in this characteristic |
| **What it is** | name, definition, kind, what it is and is not, the forms it takes |
| **Where it lives in the inner person** | heart, soul, spirit, mind, body — its inner home and origin |
| **Which inner capacities it engages** | perceiving, thinking, remembering, choosing, acting, moral sense, conscience |
| **How it moves** | between God and the person, and between persons |
| **How it is formed, tested, transformed** | over time, in suffering, toward the end |
| **How it relates to other inner characteristics** | what it pairs with, opposes, produces, follows, shares words with |
| **The view from outside Scripture** | what physical and clinical sciences observe, and where Scripture exceeds them (briefer; bring general knowledge where the foundation is thin, without overclaiming) |

## 5. Words to avoid (filter out — this is the general-reader rule)
| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| cluster | "this study", "this family of characteristics" |
| characteristic codes (c1, M01-A, A–J), tier codes (T0, T2.1.1) | the characteristic's plain name ("reverent fear", "dread"); never the code |
| sub-group / VCG / meaning group | "form", "facet", describe inline |
| finding / observation / the record | "what the evidence shows", "the pattern", "the verses tell us" |
| valence | "moral weight", "rightly-ordered vs disordered", "commanded / forbidden" in plain terms |
| divine_involvement / object_type / experiencer | describe plainly ("God is the one feared", "the object of fear is…") |
| constitutional location / inner faculty / register | "where it lives in the inner person", "the inner capacity", "form" |
| inferential (as a label) | render the substance with honest qualification — "the verses stop short of saying so outright, but…", "the evidence points toward…" |
| raw internal counts ("973 of 975") | plain quantifiers — "in almost every instance", "the single exception", "by far the most common" |
- **No project-wide superlatives** ("the most X in this research") unless the foundation states programme-wide data.
- Fine to use: God, Scripture, heart, mind, soul, spirit, will, conscience, inner life/person, character, attribute, evidence, pattern, theme, and the characteristics' plain names.

## 6. Citation discipline
- Every analytical claim is **grounded by a quoted verse** — name it in standard biblical citation ("as Psa 130:4 makes plain…") and **quote it verbatim** (inline for short, indented block for long).
- Never cite an internal code (finding-id, tier, VCG, prompt). The reader sees only Scripture references.
- Where a particular set of verses shapes the reading, describe that context inline in the reader's terms.

## 7. The silence principle
Name a silence **only when it is meaningful** — either an expectation set then not met, or where the absence is what gives the characteristic its shape. **Routine absence needs no comment.** Do not list silences as inventory.

## 8. Derive-from-the-foundation (no new claims)
Every claim must trace to the captured foundation. Do **not** introduce literary framings, interpretive moves, or superlatives that the foundation does not carry. If writing surfaces a genuine new insight, **flag it** for capture as a finding — do not assert it as prose.

## 9. Self-review on completion
After the reverse audit, read start to finish and fix: repetition (same point twice), overreach (a claim beyond its verse), padding (words without content), tonal drift (devotional / first-person / hedging), jargon-leak (any §5 term that slipped through), and structural drift (a section reading as a list rather than essay).

## 10. Order of operations
1. Write the essay, section by section, across all eight lenses with evidence.
2. Reverse audit (§3): every foundation finding covered or justified-silent.
3. Self-review (§9).
4. Return one Markdown file: `wa-{CLUSTER}-{name}-essay-v{n}-{YYYYMMDD}.md`.
