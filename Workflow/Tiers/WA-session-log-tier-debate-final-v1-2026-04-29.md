## Session Summary

A two-day debate (2026-04-28 to 2026-04-29) establishing the first-tier observation framework for the WA programme. The session began with a catalogue of 147 generic observation questions and ended with a complete, resolved first-tier framework of eight analytical domains (T0–T7).

## What Was Established

### The First-Tier Framework — T0 through T7

The framework defines the complete scope of analytical enquiry for every inner-being characteristic studied in the programme. Eight first-tier domains:

| Tier | Title | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| T0 | Divine Image and Created Design | Purposive frame — present throughout; comes fully into its own in Session D |
| T1 | Definition | Definition in the making — Name, Kind, Boundary — visited at start and end of analysis |
| T2 | Constitutional Location and Boundaries | Where the characteristic is located and where it originates |
| T3 | The Inner Faculties | Through which inner capacities the characteristic operates |
| T4 | Relational Interfaces | God, other humans, other spiritual beings |
| T5 | Formative and Developmental Dimension | How the characteristic shapes the person over time |
| T6 | Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics | Inter-characteristic mapping |
| T7 | Evidential and Methodological Foundation | Epistemological basis — underlies all tiers |

### Key Clarifications and Decisions

**T0 — Divine Image and Created Design** is a new tier, not previously named in the programme. It is the purposive envelope within which all analytical tiers operate. At the individual characteristic level it asks what the characteristic reveals about the divine image and created purpose. At the programme level — Session D — it asks what the full constellation of characteristics reveals about what it means to be made in the image of God. Sexuality finds its primary home here — not as a constitutional layer or faculty but as a created expression of God's relational and creative nature, used typologically throughout Scripture.

**T1 — Definition** is a definition in the making, not a fixed definition. Name is stable; Kind and Boundary are both reframeable as research proceeds. Kind is not a dimension label — it is a descriptive synthesis encapsulating the characteristic within its constitutional location, the faculties it engages, and its impact on the person. T1 is visited twice: at the beginning to orient the enquiry and at the end to receive what the research has revealed.

**T2 — Constitutional Location** was substantially clarified. The body is an interface layer, not the seat of characteristics. Constitutional locations are not physical organs. The brain interfaces cognition with the physical world but does not house it. Soul is the seat of the faculties and their expression — broader than emotion or volition. Heart and mind are named subsets of the soul; other subsets may exist and are held open. DNA is the design print of the body, not a separate constitutional location. Body parts are examined for their significance (emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, mediating), direction (soul→body, body→soul, both), and deposit (constitutional deposit from sustained operation, including generational consequence). Origin and source of a characteristic sits in T2 — where it comes from constitutionally. The why of that origin is examined across T3, T4, and T6.

**T3 — The Inner Faculties** replaced the former T3 (Moral and Conscience Structure) and T4 (Volitional and Agency Structure). The inner faculties are the constitutional equipment the person brings to every inner-being encounter — not locations and not characteristics. Eleven faculties established: perception (with six inner-sense analogues including a sixth sense of spiritual discernment provisionally seated in the spirit), cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, relational capacity. Volition has three distinct aspects: capacity (God-given), interaction (choosing one shapes others), constraints (DNA, formation, history). Moral evaluation is a cluster — conscience (faculty), shame and guilt (responses), the law on the heart (standard). Memory is distinct from cognition — the temporal depth of the inner person, not housed in the brain. Creativity includes imagination as its inward movement. Sexuality is not a faculty — it belongs in T0.

**T4 — Relational Interfaces** (formerly T5) now gives full standing to the other spiritual beings interface. Angels and adversarial powers are named explicitly. The catalogue gap in this interface is acknowledged but the interface itself stands as a defined analytical domain.

**T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension** (formerly T6). Unchanged in substance; renumbered.

**T6 — Structural Relationships** (formerly T7). Unchanged in substance; renumbered.

**T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation** (formerly T8). T7 is qualitatively different from T0–T6 — it is the epistemological basis underlying all tiers, not a peer analytical domain. Three components: lexical and semantic analysis, verse and literary interpretation, human science frameworks (as sanity checks, not co-equal sources).

### The Dimension Vocabulary

The programme's 10-dimension vocabulary is confirmed as open — not a closed taxonomy. It provides reference categories for pattern recognition and inter-characteristic comparison. Kind (in T1) is not reducible to a dimension assignment. The vocabulary is subject to revision as the analytical work deepens.

### Second-Tier Work Completed

Preliminary second-tier components established for T1 (9 components), T2 (9 components), T3 (faculty list, component structure pending), T7 (3 components). All others pending.
