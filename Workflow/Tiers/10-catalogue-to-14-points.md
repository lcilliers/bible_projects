# How each of the 14 L1/L2 points fits the catalogue questions — and what is required beyond them

> For each **live** catalogue question (226 active, pulled from `wa_obs_question_catalogue`, `deleted=0`, 2026-06-14): the question in words, **what each of the 14 L1/L2 points is intended to deliver toward it**, and **what reading/operation would be required to satisfy the question in full** beyond the 14. The 14 points are described by their *intent / promise* (what they produce per typed term-in-verse). No counts, no verdicts. (Expanded 2026-06-14 to name the required operation per question.)

---

## Part 1 — the 14 L1/L2 points: what each is intended to deliver

Each point is one field produced for a single **typed term-in-verse**:

1. **Sense applied** — the one specific sense the term carries *in this verse*, and where it sits in the term's range (from the STEP sense-set; cannot diverge from the lexicon).
2. **Type** — action (an act done) · status (a condition/disposition borne) · quality (an attribute).
3. **Compound** — simple or compound, and which parts combine.
4. **Mode** — the operative grammatical/stem form and the manner the term functions here, incl. context-, direction-, or constitutional-level dependence visible in the verse.
5. **Constitutional location** — spirit · soul · heart · mind · other soul-subset · named body part, every level evidenced, or NONE.
6. **Origin** — within-person · received-from-outside · bestowed-by-God · carried-generationally · not-stated.
7. **Faculty** — perception · cognition · memory · affect · creativity · volition · agency · moral-evaluation · conscience · relational — or NONE.
8. **Attributed to God** — predicated of / related to God in this verse (yes/no + how).
9. **Purpose / equips** — what the verse says the term equips the person to be/do/become, or not-stated.
10. **Typology direction** — human→divine · divine→human · none.
11. **Immediate response** — the first/most immediate inner-being response shown in this verse, or SILENT.
12. **Produces / effect** — what the term produces in the inner being in this verse.
13. **Relational implication** — directional/relational/constitutional force the term carries in this verse.
14. **Literary setting** — the literary form carrying this verse + contextual setting where evident.

## Part 1b — the operations that satisfy a question *beyond* a single term-in-verse

These are the named reading/computation steps used in "To satisfy in full" below:

- **Surrounding verses** — read the verse's passage (the verses around it); the 14 points capture only the single term-in-verse, not its passage context.
- **This term, rolled up** — aggregate the 14-field captures across all this term's verses (a computation over what L1/L2 already produced; *no new read*).
- **This term, re-read** — read the term's own corpus *together* (where rolling up the fields is not enough and the cross-verse meaning must be read).
- **Related / cognate terms** — read the term's family (root family, near-synonyms, the opposite term, person-type term, seeking term) and their verses.
- **Other characteristics' verses** — read the co-occurring / neighbouring characteristics' terms and verses (cross-cluster).
- **Scripture-wide synthesis** — canonical/theological reflection across Scripture (divine nature, image, typology, eschatology).
- **External framework** — a human-science lens.
- **Judgement on captured fields** — an interpretive classification made over the captured values (usually combined with one of the reads above).
