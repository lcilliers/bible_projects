## Purpose and Scope

This document identifies where the first-tier observation framework (T0–T8) requires additions or revisions to the existing programme prose. Edits are limited to the impact of the first-tier structure — the existence, naming, and nature of T0–T8 as the analytical framework governing the programme's observation work.

Second-tier impacts — the specific changes to programme prose that arise from second-tier component findings (e.g. the soul as seat of faculties, DNA as design print) — are deferred until the second-tier work is more complete.

Each entry states the affected section, the nature of the impact, and either a proposed addition or a proposed revision.

## Edit 1 — New section required: The Observation Framework

**Affected section:** Programme prose — Chapter 2 (Research Methodology) or Chapter 3 (Disciplines, Tools, and Evidential Principles)

**Nature of impact:** The first-tier framework (T0–T8) does not currently appear as a named structure anywhere in the programme prose. It is a new structural element that governs all Session B analytical work. It requires a dedicated prose section.

**Proposed new section title:** The Observation Framework

**Proposed content (draft):**

The observation framework defines the complete scope of analytical enquiry for every inner-being characteristic the programme studies. It is the discipline that ensures Session B analysis is consistent, deep, and broad across all words — applied in the same way to every word, so that findings across words are produced on the same analytical footing and the accumulated data is coherent.

The framework has two layers. The first tier defines nine major analytical domains — T0 through T8 — that together constitute a complete characterisation of any inner-being characteristic. The second tier defines the constituent components within each first-tier domain — the specific prompts and questions that analytical work within that domain addresses.

The framework functions as a discipline, not a checklist. Not every domain will be populated for every characteristic — some domains will be sparse or silent for a given word. The framework defines what could be explored; the evidence determines what is found. Applied uniformly across the programme, it prevents the systematic omission of any area of the inner being and ensures that no word is characterised more completely or more narrowly than another by analytical accident.

The nine first-tier domains are: T0 (Divine Image and Created Design), T1 (Definition), T2 (Constitutional Location and Boundaries), T3 (The Inner Faculties), T5 (Relational Interfaces), T6 (Formative and Developmental Dimension), T7 (Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics), and T8 (Evidential and Methodological Foundation). [Note: T4 is pending formal resolution — its content has been absorbed into T3.] Each domain is defined in the observation framework document.

**Placement:** This section should appear in the methodology chapter, after the description of Session B and before or alongside the description of the observation question catalogue. It is the structural frame within which the catalogue operates.

## Edit 2 — New section required: T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**Affected section:** Programme prose — Chapter 1 (Programme Purpose) or as a subsection of the new Observation Framework section

**Nature of impact:** T0 is a new first-tier domain that has not previously been named in the programme prose. Its introduction changes the programme's self-description in an important way: the analytical work is not only characterising inner-being phenomena but doing so within the frame of the *imago Dei* — the human person as image-bearer of God. This is a purposive frame that governs the whole programme, not only a single analytical domain.

**Proposed addition to Programme Purpose section:**

The programme's analytical work is conducted within a purposive frame: the human person as image-bearer of God. Every inner-being characteristic exists within that frame — the characteristics studied are not merely psychological or relational phenomena but constitutive features of persons made in the image of God. The observation framework's T0 domain — Divine Image and Created Design — holds this frame explicitly. At the level of the individual characteristic, T0 asks what the characteristic reveals about the nature of God whose image the person bears and what created purpose it serves. At the level of the programme as a whole, T0 asks what the entire constellation of inner-being characteristics reveals about what it means to be made in the image of God. This programme-level T0 question is the governing concern of Session D.

**Placement:** Insert into or after the Mission section (prog_purp_mission, Section id 2).

## Edit 3 — Revision required: Session D Orientation

**Affected section:** `prog_instr_session_d` · Section id 51 · sort 122

**Nature of impact:** T0 (Divine Image and Created Design) comes fully into its own in Session D. The current Session D description does not name this. The governing question of the programme — what the human being is, in the image of God, and what all these characteristics together constitute — is the Session D question. The Session D description should name T0 explicitly.

**Current wording (opening sentence):**
> Session D works in the opposite direction from Sessions A, B, and C: where those stages are word-driven — entering through a Hebrew or Greek term and following the corpus outward — Session D begins with questions about the inner life that the programme as a whole is trying to answer, and asks what the full accumulated body of word-study data reveals in the aggregate.

**Proposed addition** — insert as second paragraph:

> The governing question that Session D addresses is the T0 question: what does this entire constellation of inner-being characteristics reveal about what it means to be made in the image of God — what is the human person designed to be, do, and become? Individual word studies surface facets of this question; Session D is where the facets are held together and the answer is assembled from the evidence. T0 is present at every stage of the programme, but Session D is where it comes into its own as the integrating frame for all that the per-word and cluster-level work has produced.

## Edit 4 — Revision required: Key Methodological Principles

**Affected section:** `prog_meth_principles` · Section id 13 · sort 13

**Nature of impact:** The first-tier framework (T0–T8) is the structural instrument through which the standing catalogue of questions is organised and applied. The current principles section describes the catalogue but does not name the framework that organises it. The principle of uniform analytical application should be updated to reference the observation framework as the governing structure.

**Current wording:**
> **Analysis reads the data as a whole; it does not pick and choose angles.** The analytical work for each word runs through a standing catalogue of questions that every word is worked through in the same way. The catalogue is applied in full. A word's answer to every question is part of its record.

**Proposed revision:**
> **Analysis reads the data as a whole; it does not pick and choose angles.** The analytical work for each word is structured by the observation framework — nine first-tier analytical domains (T0–T8) that together constitute a complete characterisation of any inner-being characteristic. Within each domain, a standing catalogue of questions is applied in the same way to every word. The catalogue is applied in full. A word's answer to every question is part of its record. The framework ensures that no analytical domain is selectively applied or omitted.

## Edit 5 — Revision required: Defining Inner Being

**Affected section:** `prog_purp_defining_inner_being` · Section id 5 · sort 5

**Nature of impact:** The current definition of inner-being characteristics is the programme's working filter for registry membership. It is not directly affected by the first-tier framework. However, the framework introduces T0 as a purposive frame that sits above the definition — the definition says what qualifies as an inner-being characteristic; T0 says why those characteristics exist and what they reveal. The existing section should acknowledge this relationship without revising the definition itself.

**Proposed addition** — insert as a final paragraph to the section:

> The working definition is an evaluative filter — it determines what qualifies as an inner-being characteristic for the programme's scope. It does not address why those characteristics exist or what they reveal about the nature of the person who bears them. That purposive question is held by T0 (Divine Image and Created Design) in the observation framework — the frame within which every characteristic the definition admits is subsequently examined. The definition opens the scope; T0 frames what the programme is looking for within it.

## Edit 6 — Addition required: Session B Analysis Output description

**Affected section:** `prog_instr_session_b_output` · Section id 68 · sort 120

**Nature of impact:** The current Session B description names three sub-stages (2a comprehensive reading, 2b Q&A partitioning, 2c analytic word output — five chapters). The five-chapter structure predates the first-tier framework. The relationship between the five chapters and the nine first-tier domains is not yet fully resolved — this is noted as a structural gap requiring future instruction revision. The current prose should acknowledge that the observation framework governs the analytical scope, with the chapter structure as the output form.

**Proposed addition** — insert after the description of the three sub-stages:

> The analytical scope of Session B is governed by the observation framework — nine first-tier domains (T0–T8) that define the complete analytical territory for each word. The five-chapter output structure (Word Characteristic Summary, Word Impact Description, Annotated Verse Evidence, Original Language Vocabulary, Connections and Research Pointers) is the output form through which findings across the framework domains are presented. The relationship between the framework domains and the chapter structure is under development; as the framework matures, the chapter structure will be reviewed and updated to ensure full alignment.
