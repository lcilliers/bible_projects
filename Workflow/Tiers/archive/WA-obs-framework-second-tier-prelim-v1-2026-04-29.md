## Purpose and Status

The second tier defines the constituent components within each first-tier domain. These components serve as the analytical prompts that ensure complete and consistent treatment of every characteristic across each first-tier area.

Second-tier work is in progress. This document captures what has been established so far. Entries marked **[PENDING]** have not yet been examined. Entries marked **[PARTIAL]** have been started but not completed. Entries marked **[COMPLETE — PRELIMINARY]** represent the current working state, subject to revision as debate continues.

The 147 questions from the observation catalogue (wa-obs-catalogue-generic-v1-20260426.json) are the first evidence base for second-tier component derivation. They have been classified by first-tier domain. The second-tier work takes those classified questions and derives the components they collectively address — then identifies gaps where current questions do not cover a component that should exist.

## T1 — Definition
**Status: [COMPLETE — PRELIMINARY]**

Second-tier components derived from 29 classified questions and two identified gaps. All components subject to revision.

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T1.1 | Name and Naming | What is this characteristic called, and what does the name signal? Includes primary Hebrew and Greek terms and root meaning at definitional level |
| T1.2 | Structure and Form | What kind of phenomenon is this — act, disposition, condition, or quality? Simple or complex in internal structure? |
| T1.3 | Modes of Operation | What are the distinct ways this characteristic operates within and through the inner person — including communicative or speech-based modes? |
| T1.4 | Immediate Response | What is the first or immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic? |
| T1.5 | Sustained Effect | What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time — what states, qualities, capacities, and orientations does it establish? |
| T1.6 | Structural Opposite and Absence | What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — and what does absence or withdrawal reveal about its role? |
| T1.7 | Conditions of Reception and Non-Reception | What inner conditions enable, block, or distort reception — and what is the state of the person who receives without recognising, or does not receive? |
| T1.8 | Dimension Classification | What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic — and what evidence supports that classification? [GAP — no current question covers this directly] |
| T1.9 | Constitutional Level | At what level of the human constitution does this characteristic primarily operate — spirit, soul, or body? [GAP — no current question covers this at T1 level] |

**Notes:**
- T1.4 and T1.5 were split from a single original component — immediate response has distinct analytical character from sustained effect
- T1.6 was split from conditions of reception — the structural opposite is a definitional tool, not a reception condition
- Direction of movement removed from T1 — confirmed as belonging in T2
- T1.8 and T1.9 are gaps requiring new prompts

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
**Status: [COMPLETE — PRELIMINARY]**

Second-tier components derived from 12 classified questions. Subject to revision.

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T2.1 | Spirit-Level Location | Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level — and what does that location reveal? |
| T2.2 | Soul-Level Location | Is this characteristic a soul-level phenomenon — and what does that reveal about its place in the innermost personal experience? |
| T2.3 | Heart | Is this characteristic located in the heart — the integrating centre of knowing, willing, feeling, and moral awareness? |
| T2.4 | Mind | Does this characteristic engage or locate in the mind — the faculty of thought and discernment within the soul? |
| T2.5 | Other Soul Subsets | Does the verse evidence surface soul-level locations beyond heart and mind? [Held open — not forced] |
| T2.6 | Body — Significance | When Scripture links this characteristic to a body part, what is the significance of that link — emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating? |
| T2.7 | Body — Direction | Which way does the body-characteristic relationship run — soul expressing through body, body feeding back to soul, or both — and with what consequence? |
| T2.8 | Body — Deposit | Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including generationally? |
| T2.9 | Constitutional Movement | How does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — spirit to soul, soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions? |

**Notes:**
- DNA is the design print of the body — it appears within T2.8 (deposit) rather than as a separate location
- Body parts explicitly examined within T2.6 and T2.7 rather than treated as a general body location
- T2.8 cross-references T6 — the deposit mechanism is T2; the developmental consequence is T6
- Three analytical anchors for body examination: significance, direction, deposit

## T3 — The Inner Faculties
**Status: [PARTIAL — faculty list established; component structure not yet defined]**

The faculty list has been established through debate. The second-tier component structure — how each faculty is examined in relation to a specific characteristic — has not yet been formally defined.

**Faculty list (current):**

| Faculty | Description |
|---|---|
| Perception | The inner senses — analogues to the five physical senses and possibly a sixth faculty of spiritual discernment |
| Cognition | Knowing, understanding, discerning — transcends the body; not housed in the brain |
| Memory | The temporal depth of the inner person — holding and retrieving inner-being reality across time; distinct from present cognition |
| Affect | Feeling and emotional experience |
| Creativity | The faculty of origination — encompassing imagination and the capacity to bring into being what did not exist; grounded in the image of the Creator God |
| Volition | The God-given capacity to choose — constitutionally given, operating under the constraints of DNA, formation, and circumstance |
| Agency | The capacity to act, initiate, trigger, and make happen — distinct from volition |
| Moral evaluation | The capacity to assess against a standard — right, wrong, good, true |
| Conscience | The acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — distinct from moral evaluation |
| Conscientiousness | The integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action |
| Relational capacity | The constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person |

**Anticipated second-tier component structure (not yet debated):**
For each faculty, T3 will ask:
- Does this characteristic engage this faculty — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair this faculty?
- What does this characteristic reveal about the nature and limits of this faculty?

**Open questions within T3:**
- The sixth sense — intuition beyond the five inner-sense analogues; exact nature and relationship to spirit not yet resolved
- Whether the faculty list is complete
- Volition's constraints — sitting at boundary of T2 and T3; to be resolved when T3 component structure is formally debated
- Volition's interaction across characteristics — touching T7; to be resolved in T7 second-tier work
