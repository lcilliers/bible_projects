### Step 4 — Adding interpretive disciplines

Researcher asked whether the discipline of understanding/interpreting Scripture needed to add other first-tier analytical disciplines.

Claude identified four distinct interpretive disciplines implicit in the catalogue:

- **Textual and Verse-Level Interpretation** — how the word functions within its verse
- **Literary and Contextual Reading** — what literary form and context shape the word's meaning
- **Canonical Scope and Development** — how the word operates across the Testaments
- **Lexical and Semantic Analysis** — formal original language analysis

This led to separating T6 (Linguistic and Lexical Foundation) into two distinct first-tier areas:

And adding Canonical Development as a third interpretive area.

### Step 7 — Human spirit discussion

Researcher asked: where would the human spirit sit in T1–T7?

Claude's analysis showed the spirit is present across multiple tiers:
- **T2** — as a constituent element defining one of the spirit-soul-body boundaries (the upper boundary, not only the body)
- **T5** — as the primary site of encounter with God
- **T1** — as the seat of certain inner states (spiritual perception, discernment)
- **T6** — as a formative and transformational site (renewal of spirit)
- **T3 and T4** — as morally and volitionally significant (willing spirit, contrite spirit, hardened spirit)

**Key conclusion:** The human spirit does not sit cleanly in any single first-tier area. It is present across T1, T2, T3, T4, T5, and T6 — which is expected from a constituent element of the human person rather than a characteristic of it.

**Consequence for tier naming:** T2 was renamed from "Embodied Location" to "Constitutional Location and Boundaries" to open the tier upward to spirit and soul, not only downward to the body.

**Researcher decision:** The spirit belongs in all the tiers. The tier 1 wording must not be exclusive — the spirit is fundamentally part of the scope. Each tier 1 title needs a short description of scope.

### Step 8 — Tier 1 titles with scope descriptions

Claude produced revised titles and scope descriptions for T1–T7, ensuring the spirit is not implicitly excluded from any tier:

**T1 — Essential Nature of the Characteristic**
*Scope: What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic — what is it, where does it originate, how does it operate, and what does it produce? This includes the emotional, cognitive, volitional, moral, and spiritual dimensions of the characteristic's essential nature, and covers the full spirit-soul-body constitution of the person as the field within which the characteristic operates.*

**T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries**
*Scope: Where is this characteristic located or expressed within the constitution of the human person — spirit, soul, or body — and how does it move across those boundaries? This includes somatic and organ location, spatial and directional language, the spirit-level seat of the characteristic, and the interface between inner reality and outward expression.*

**T3 — Moral and Conscience Structure**
*Scope: What is this characteristic's relationship to the moral constitution of the person — including guilt, merit, virtue, conscience, and moral failure at the level of spirit, soul, and body? This includes the conditions under which moral structure opens or closes the characteristic, and its role in judicial and accountability contexts.*

**T4 — Volitional and Agency Structure**
*Scope: What is this characteristic's relationship to the human will and to human agency — does it operate through, alongside, beneath, or despite the will? This includes the spirit-level and soul-level dimensions of volition, the relationship between divine initiative and human effort, and the inner posture required to seek or exercise the characteristic.*

**T5 — Relational Interfaces**
*Scope: How does this characteristic operate across the relational interfaces of the human person — with God, with other humans, and with other spiritual beings? This includes the direction of the characteristic's operation across each interface, the boundaries within which it operates, and the spirit-level dimension of relational encounter.*

**T6 — Formative and Developmental Dimension**
*Scope: How does this characteristic shape the person over time — what is the process, direction, and mechanism of change at the level of spirit, soul, and body? This includes transformation, sanctification, formation through suffering, and the eschatological trajectory of the characteristic.*

**T7 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics**
*Scope: How does this characteristic relate to adjacent inner-being characteristics — what are the connections, dependencies, sequences, and distinctions? This includes co-occurrence patterns, causal and constitutive relationships, shared vocabulary, and the structural position of this characteristic within the wider map of the inner person.*

### Step 9 — T8 repositioned as epistemological foundation

Researcher clarified the nature of T8:

- T1–T7 describe the **body of understanding** — substantive areas constituting a complete characterisation
- T8 describes the **evidential and methodological foundation** — the disciplines and sources from which understanding in T1–T7 is derived and validated

T8 is not a peer of T1–T7 but sits beneath all of them as the epistemological basis.

Researcher further clarified: the programme is defining its **source of knowledge** (Scripture primary, human sciences as light sanity check) into each of T1–T7. The scope of T1–T7 is not limited — a full human sciences treatment of any single tier represents a lifetime of scholarly work. The programme applies Scripture as governing source with human sciences providing a light check.

**Revised T8:**

**T8 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation**
*Scope: The sources and disciplines from which understanding of the characteristic is derived, validated, and interpreted across T1–T7. Scripture is the primary and governing source. Human science frameworks serve as interpretive lenses and light sanity checks — they illuminate and organise what Scripture describes but do not add to it.*

- T8.1 Lexical and semantic analysis — original language vocabulary, root meanings, grammatical forms, semantic range
- T8.2 Verse and literary interpretation — word function within the verse, literary form, logical structure, contextual reading
- T8.3 Canonical scope and development — continuity and development across the Testaments, LXX bridging, NT coinage
- T8.4 Human science frameworks — psychological, moral-philosophical, developmental, sociological, and anthropological disciplines as interpretive lenses
