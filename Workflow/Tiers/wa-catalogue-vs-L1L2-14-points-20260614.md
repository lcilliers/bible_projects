# How each of the 14 L1/L2 points fits the catalogue questions

> For each **live** catalogue question (226 active, pulled from `wa_obs_question_catalogue`, `deleted=0`, 2026-06-14), the question in words and **what each of the 14 L1/L2 points is intended to deliver toward it**. The 14 points are described by their *intent / promise* (what they are designed to produce per typed term-in-verse), not by what any run actually produced. "Beyond the 14" simply names, in words, what the question additionally asks that none of the 14 deliver. No counts, no verdicts.

---

## Part 1 — the 14 L1/L2 points: what each is intended to deliver

Each point is one field produced for a single **typed term-in-verse**. Its promise:

1. **Sense applied** — the one specific sense the term carries *in this verse*, and where that sits in the term's range. Built from the term's STEP sense-set, so it cannot diverge from the lexicon.
2. **Type** — what kind of inner-being phenomenon the term is here: an **action** (an act done), a **status** (a condition/disposition borne), or a **quality** (an attribute).
3. **Compound** — whether the term, as it operates here, is **simple** or **compound**, and which parts combine.
4. **Mode** — the operative grammatical/stem form and the distinct manner the term functions in the inner person here, including any context-, direction-, or constitutional-level dependence visible in the verse.
5. **Constitutional location** — where the verse *explicitly* locates the term constitutionally (spirit · soul · heart · mind · another soul-subset · a named body part), every level evidenced, or NONE.
6. **Origin** — where the term originates constitutionally in this verse: generated within the person · received from outside · bestowed by God · carried generationally · not-stated.
7. **Faculty** — which inner faculty(ies) the term engages here, from the fixed set: perception · cognition · memory · affect · creativity · volition · agency · moral-evaluation · conscience · relational — or NONE.
8. **Attributed to God** — whether the term is predicated of / related to God in this verse (yes/no), with a note on how.
9. **Purpose / equips** — what the verse indicates the term equips the person to be, do, or become (the purpose it serves here), or not-stated.
10. **Typology direction** — if the verse uses the term typologically, the direction (human→divine or divine→human), or none.
11. **Immediate response** — the first / most immediate inner-being response shown in this verse to encountering or bearing the term, or SILENT.
12. **Produces / effect** — what the term produces in the inner being in this verse (the effect it brings about here).
13. **Relational implication** — what directional, relational, or constitutional implication or force the term carries in this verse.
14. **Literary setting** — the literary form carrying this verse, and where evident its contextual setting (judicial · liturgical · covenantal · communal · eschatological).
