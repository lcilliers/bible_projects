**The correction (per researcher)**: Inner being IS the entire being. The programme's stated scope (CLAUDE.md §1: "the inner life of mankind") and the cluster overview's "human inner being" carry NO implicit theological qualifier. Pure-human inner-being content — morally-positive, morally-negative, circumstantial, relational, vertical, horizontal — is **all in scope**.

**Evidence collected during the session**:

### (e) Cross-cluster co-occurrence check before routing — NEW (added late-session)

**The principle**: before routing a verse to another cluster (or transferring a term wholesale), CC must check whether the verse has secondary impact / clarification / role / relationship in the source cluster. The current ROUTE-TO-CLUSTER disposition is too lossy — it treats verses as cluster-exclusive when they often carry cross-cluster analytical content.

**Empirical evidence collected**: 180 of 322 M04-BOUNDARY verses (**56%**) co-occur with other clusters' active terms in the same biblical reference. Top co-occurrence patterns:

Routing to M22 Praise without analysis loses the "Lord is good AND ready to forgive AND abundant in lovingkindness" cross-characteristic structure that is analytically significant for M04's understanding of "pleasantness of divine character."

**Structural addition**: v2_5 must add a cross-cluster co-occurrence assessment step before any verse routing. This connects to T6 prompts (which already exist) — but T6 currently runs at Phase 9 AFTER routing decisions. The new step puts T6-style relationship analysis BEFORE the routing decision so cross-cluster relationships are captured in source-cluster findings rather than evaporating.
