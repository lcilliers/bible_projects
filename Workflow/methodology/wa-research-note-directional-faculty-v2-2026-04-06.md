## 1. The Pattern That Emerged

During Phase B of the Dimension Review for Cluster C02, a recurring pattern was identified across multiple registries and terms. In each case, the same inner faculty or act appears in the data in two morally or directionally opposite forms — not as two different faculties, but as the same faculty directed differently.

## 2. The Analytical Observation

The pattern is not a classification artefact. The automated classifier struggled with these groups precisely because they do not fit a single dimension category — and the reason they do not fit is that the inner faculty itself is **morally underdetermined**. The same capacity for inner deliberation, reflection, estimation, or discernment produces opposite moral outcomes depending on its orientation.

This is distinct from saying that inner faculties are neutral. The inner faculty is not absent of moral character — but its moral character is not fixed by the capacity itself. It is **determined by the direction in which the faculty is deployed** and by the relational posture it expresses.

Three sub-patterns are visible within the broader pattern:

**Sub-pattern A — Directional opposition (same act, opposite moral direction):**
Higgayon, re.a, sha.ma, dialogismos. The same inner act of reflection, thought, hearing, or reasoning can be directed Godward (devotion, obedience, treasuring) or against another or toward self (scheming, hardness, self-deception). The faculty is identical; the moral character is determined entirely by orientation.

**Sub-pattern B — Capacity and its corruption (same faculty, wise or self-serving):**
A.rum, a.rom, or.mah, fronimos, sofos, cha.kham. Prudence and craftiness are not two different capacities — they are the same inner capacity for perceptive, discerning judgment deployed either in service of wisdom and truth or in service of deceptive self-interest. The determining factor is what the capacity serves.

**Sub-pattern C — Relational symmetry across the divine-human boundary:**
Re.a specifically. The term operates symmetrically — naming the act from both sides of the divine-human relationship. Human inner thoughts are known by God; God's thoughts are treasured by the human. The same cognitive interiority is the medium in both directions.

## 3. The Research Question

**Is this a new dimension of the inner life — or a structural principle about how inner dimensions work?**

There is a meaningful difference:

**If it is a new dimension:** Scripture may be naming a distinct aspect of inner being — something like *the capacity for inner direction* or *the orientational character of inner faculties* — as a coherent inner-being reality. In that case the 12-category vocabulary is incomplete, and a category needs to be added or restructured to capture it.

**If it is a structural principle:** The directional character of inner faculties may be a general truth about the inner life that cuts across all dimensions — not a dimension itself, but a principle about how dimensions work. In that case no new category is needed, but the principle should govern how all dimension assignments are made and how Session B characterises each faculty.

**The data from C02 alone is insufficient to resolve this question.** C02 is the cognitive cluster — it is possible that this directional pattern is especially prominent here because cognitive faculties are inherently more underdetermined than emotional states. The pattern may be less pronounced in affective or volitional clusters — or it may appear there too, which would strengthen the case for a structural principle operating across the whole inner life.

**What Session B must investigate:** When analysing each word in C02, Session B should explicitly ask: does this faculty appear in both positive and negative moral orientations in Scripture? If so, what determines the direction? Is the determinant named explicitly (e.g. love, pride, fear of God, the heart's orientation), or is it left implicit?

**What Session D must synthesise:** Across all clusters — is the directional-determination pattern a consistent feature of the inner life as Scripture presents it? If yes, does Scripture treat this as a unified phenomenon (naming it, describing it, commanding attention to it), or only show it implicitly in how the vocabulary works? The answer to this question will shape how the final product presents the structure of the inner being.

## 4. Implications for the Current Dimension Review

For the groups listed in Section 1, the immediate practical consequence is:

- **Do not force a single dimension on groups that genuinely span two poles.** The dual-pole character is part of the data, not a classification failure.
- **For groups where both poles are present in the same group** (5883-001, 3453-001): the description names both poles and the dimension reflects the governing cognitive structure, with a note on the directional range.
- **For groups where the two poles are in separate groups** (1081-001/002, 4487-001/002, 4486-001/002, 522-001/002, 4458-001/002): each group gets its own dimension, but the Session B finding explicitly links them as opposite poles of the same faculty.
- **For the dimension vocabulary:** The existing 12 categories do not include a category for this pattern. This is an open question to carry into Session B and D — not a problem to fix now.
