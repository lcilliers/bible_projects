## 2. The Problem — How It Was Identified

During C15 review, Leroux uploaded an extract of all 349 groups currently classified as Theological/Divine-Human across 89 registries and 21 clusters. Reading that extract revealed the classification was systematically misfiring. Groups were labelled Theological/Divine-Human not because they characterised a specific kind of human inner-being engagement, but because:

- The group described a divine attribute (God's wrath, jealousy, delight) — i.e. it was about God's inner life, not the human person's
- The group described a human inner state (rage, indignation, trembling) that happened to arise in a theological context (divine judgment, covenant consequence)
- The group described a constitutively relational encounter with God (Spirit indwelling, heart circumcision, divine transformation) — which is a different kind of thing from either of the above

All three were being labelled identically. Leroux's diagnostic: "to have rage, show indignation, be incensed — all of this is examples of making it all spiritual — it is nonsense. Someone that has no particular spirituality will all be able to have these terms working in their soul."

---

## 3. The Broader Diagnosis

The problem is not limited to Theological/Divine-Human. It is systemic. Two distinct mechanisms are driving it.

### 3.1 — Forcing groups into pre-existing buckets

The 12-category dimension vocabulary was generated as a starting hypothesis — keyword matches, not derived categories. The Dimension Review was designed to validate or correct label assignments against that vocabulary. But validation assumes the vocabulary is fundamentally adequate. It is not — because the vocabulary was never derived from a principled theory of what an inner-being characteristic is.

The result: every group is being sorted into the closest available bin. This is a classification exercise, not a characterisation exercise. The programme question requires characterisation: *what does this group reveal about how the inner person actually works?*

### 3.2 — Cluster assignment contaminating dimension judgement

The clusters were formed purely as processing batches — groups of registries processed together for operational convenience. They were never intended as an analytical classification of inner-being domains.

In practice, the cluster's apparent theme has been priming dimension assignments. A group in C15 (faith/worship/praise) gets Spiritual/God-ward because everything in the cluster is God-oriented. A group in C13 (guilt/repentance) gets Moral/Conscience because the cluster's theme is moral accountability. The cluster identity is doing analytical work it was never designed to do.

### 3.3 — The group description cannot be relied on alone

This was already established earlier in the programme: the group description (context_description) is a summary produced during Verse Context classification, not a neutral characterisation of the verse evidence. The anchor verses must be read alongside the description to form a reliable judgement. The Dimension Review as currently operating is primarily working from descriptions — the verse evidence is not being systematically engaged.

---

## 4. The Root Cause — No Theory of What a Characteristic Is

The deepest problem is that the programme has no working definition of what constitutes an inner-being characteristic. Leroux identified the missing framework explicitly:

- **What qualifies something as an inner-being characteristic?** (vs. a circumstance, a divine act, a social behaviour, a theological category)
- **What is its valence?** Positive/negative — and on what basis? Intrinsic nature, direction, or outcome? (Anger can be righteous or destructive — is it neutral by nature, bifurcated, or something else?)
- **What is its kind?** Cognitive, affective, volitional, dispositional — but are these defined precisely enough to be diagnostic? Is "mind" one kind of thing, or many? Is "emotion" a category or a family?
- **What is its direction?** Inward (self-oriented), outward (other-oriented), upward (God-ward), downward (into the body)?
- **What is its relationship to agency?** Something the person does, or undergoes? Stable (disposition) or transient (state or act)?
- **What is its impact?** On the person, on others, on God-relationship?
- **What is its development?** Can it be cultivated, suppressed, transformed?

Without answers, any bucket system is arbitrary. Any assignment into it is a guess dressed as a classification.

---

## 5. The Open Question That Must Be Answered First

Before any framework can be designed, the programme's fundamental orientation must be clarified. The governing question — *characterise the inner workings of the human person as described in Scripture* — admits two readings:

**Reading A — Scripture as evidence source:** The inner-being characteristics are universal human characteristics, discoverable through Scripture but not exclusive to the theologically formed person. The theological context of a verse is evidence about the characteristic, but the characteristic itself belongs to the human person as such. Leroux's comment ("someone with no particular spirituality") points toward this reading.

**Reading B — Scripture as theological frame:** The programme is specifically studying the inner person as constituted before God — shaped by creation, fall, and redemption. The theological frame is not a contamination of the data but intrinsic to the object of study.

These are not mutually exclusive, but they produce different frameworks, different treatments of divine-attribute groups, and different answers to the "universality" question.

**This is the most important question to resolve before the new framework is designed.**

## 7. What the New Session Needs to Produce

The new session should not begin with classification work. It should produce:

1. **A working definition of an inner-being characteristic** — what it is, what its constitutive properties are, what distinguishes it from a theological reference, a circumstance, a divine act, or a social behaviour

2. **Resolution of the Reading A / Reading B question** — what is the programme actually studying?

3. **A principled dimension vocabulary** — derived from (1) and (2), tested against a sample of actual group/verse data, not imposed on it

4. **A revised Dimension Review instruction** — encoding the corrected methodology so that all remaining clusters (C16 onward) are reviewed under the correct framework

5. **A decision on C13–C15 patches** — apply as-is (provisional), hold, or regenerate under the new framework
