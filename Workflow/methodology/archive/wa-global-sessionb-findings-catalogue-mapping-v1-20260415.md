## Purpose

This document analyses the 171 Session B pointer findings against the master observation-question catalogue (v2.0). It determines:

- **Group A** — findings whose substance maps to an existing catalogue question
- **Group B** — findings that are sub-catalogue flags (data, term, or group-level) not addressable by catalogue questions
- **Group C** — findings that identify catalogue gaps — structural questions not yet in the catalogue

---

## Preliminary Observation — the level problem

The catalogue questions operate at **word-study level** — they are applied to a completed word study and ask about a word's overall inner-being character, vocabulary, and connections.

The 171 findings were generated at **Dimension Review level** — they flag specific groups, terms, dimensional classifications, and data conditions that Session B must examine. They sit one level below where the catalogue questions operate.

This means the catalogue and the findings address different analytical layers. The catalogue will not absorb all 171 findings. What the analysis below identifies is:

1. Which findings raise questions that are, in substance, catalogue-level questions — and can be framed using existing catalogue questions as the analytical lens for Session B
2. Which findings are purely sub-catalogue data flags — resolved through the verse data, not through catalogue application
3. Which findings reveal structural questions not yet in the catalogue — genuine gaps the findings expose

### A1 — Directionally-determined term findings → L-003 and L-010

**Catalogue questions:** L-003 (does the word have an inherent directionality — is it always toward an object, and does the choice of object determine its moral character?) and L-010 (does the word's structural opposite operate under the same moral logic as the word itself?)

**Findings in this group:**

- **DIM-4-001 (anger):** Anger governance as inner-being challenge — mastery as the moral direction of the same capacity. Maps to L-003 and Q040 (does the word displace, supplement, or reconstitute human effort?).
- **DIM-56-001 (envy):** The full positive-negative dual register of zeal/jealousy — the same inner passion sanctified or destructive depending on direction. Maps precisely to L-003 (directionality determines moral character) and L-010 (the structural opposite operates under the same moral logic).
- **DIM-128-002 (rebellion):** Sur (turning aside) as the mechanism for both rebellion and repentance — direction alone determines which. Maps to L-003.
- **DIM-127-002 (reasoning):** Dialogismos as directionally-determined — corrupt inner reasoning vs. legitimate deliberation in the same root. Maps to L-003.
- **DIM-123-001 (pride):** Human pride vs. divine majesty sharing the same vocabulary roots — direction determines whether the characteristic is legitimate or usurped. Maps to L-003 and L-009 (is the word named as constitutive of divine essence?).
- **DIM-43-002 (desire):** Misdirected desire vs. rightly-ordered desire — moral valence determined by direction of desire. Maps to L-003 and L-006 (does the vocabulary include a systematic taxonomy of misdirected forms?).
- **DIM-160-004 (thought):** Re.a, dialogismos, and fronimos each operating across opposite moral orientations in the same registry. Maps to L-003 and F-002 (can the word's structure be inverted?).

**Action for Session B:** When analysing these words, apply L-003, L-010, and F-002 as the primary catalogue lens for the directional structure finding.

## Group C — Catalogue Gap Candidates

These findings raise structural questions not yet in the catalogue. Each is genuinely word-neutral and universal — it would apply to any word in the programme.

---

### Gap C-1: The word defined by negation

**Finding source:** DIM-44-001 (despair) — the la/lo negation vocabulary builds inner-being content through naming what is absent. The word reveals inner states by negating them. This is a structural feature of how the inner being is named: sometimes a characteristic is defined by the absence of its opposite.

**Gap in catalogue:** No question asks whether the word's vocabulary constructs its meaning primarily through negation — naming what is absent rather than naming what is present — and what this structural inversion reveals about the inner-being reality being named.

**Proposed question:** Does the word's vocabulary construct its meaning primarily through negation — naming the absent, refused, or withheld rather than the present — and if so, what does this structural inversion reveal about the inner-being reality it describes?

---

### Gap C-2: The word as the ground of another word's possibility

**Finding source:** DIM-001-001 (abomination) — the new covenant transformation group appears in the abomination registry, placing new covenant inner transformation as the divine answer to abomination. DIM-131-001 (rejection) — covenantal rejection as the precondition of life from the dead. DIM-039-001 (debauchery) — salvation vocabulary appearing in the debauchery registry.

**Gap in catalogue:** F-009 asks whether the word functions as a prerequisite for another spiritual act. But no question asks the reverse: whether an adjacent or structurally opposite word contains within its data the evidence of another word as its resolution or remedy — whether the word carries within it the signature of another word as its answer.

**Proposed question:** Does the word's verse corpus contain evidence of another word as its structural answer or remedy — does the word carry within its data the signature of the characteristic that resolves or transforms it?

---

### Gap C-3: The inner-being condition as creational baseline

**Finding source:** DIM-168-001 (uprightness) — Eccl 7:29 as the only group explicitly addressing the original moral constitution of the human person and its departure. DIM-206-001 (vulnerability) — the pre-fall / post-fall inner-being contrast as a structural framework (Gen 2:25 vs. Gen 3:10).

**Gap in catalogue:** No question asks whether the word names a creational baseline condition — a state of the inner person prior to moral failure — and whether the word's evidence includes a before-and-after structure encoding both the original condition and its departure.

**Proposed question:** Does the word name a creational baseline — a condition of the inner person prior to moral failure — and does the verse evidence include a before-and-after structure contrasting the original condition with its departure or corruption?

---

### Gap C-4: The word functioning across both sides of the divine-human boundary simultaneously

**Finding source:** DIM-160-004 (thought) — re.a terms known by God and precious to the psalmist, the same relational faculty operating from both sides simultaneously. DIM-204-001 (name) — naming as inner-being constitutive act structurally parallel in both Testaments across both Hebrew and Greek vocabulary.

**Gap in catalogue:** Q121 asks whether any vocabulary term holds both the human and divine expression simultaneously in a single term. But no question asks whether any verse places the same inner-being faculty on both sides of the divine-human boundary at once — the human's faculty and God's engagement with that faculty operating simultaneously in the same verse.

**Proposed question:** Does any verse place the same inner-being faculty simultaneously on both sides of the divine-human boundary — the human exercising it and God engaging with the same faculty in the same verse — and what does this dual-sided operation reveal?

---

### Gap C-5: The word as diagnostic of the inner-being condition of a group or community, not only an individual

**Finding source:** DIM-135-001 (repentance) — nearly every human inner-being act has a divine counterpart in the same registry. DIM-131-001 (rejection) — Israel's covenantal rejection as a corporate inner-being reality. DIM-171-001 (whoredom) — heart-departure as a corporate covenantal phenomenon.

**Gap in catalogue:** The catalogue questions ask about the individual inner being throughout. No question explicitly asks whether the word describes a corporate or communal inner-being condition — a state of the community's collective orientation — and what the relationship is between the individual and corporate expressions of the same characteristic.

**Proposed question:** Does the word describe a corporate or communal inner-being condition — a state of the community's collective orientation — as well as an individual one? And if so, what is the relationship between the individual and corporate expressions?

---

### Gap C-6: The word with a structural relationship to a different register of itself

**Finding source:** DIM-201-002 (image) — bearing the image (static ontological state) vs. being transformed toward it (dynamic process) as two aspects of a single inner-being reality. DIM-155-001 (submission) — relational submission to human authority vs. submission before God as two structurally related but distinct forms.

**Gap in catalogue:** No question asks whether the word has two registers that are structurally related — not modes of operation (Q003) but a static form and a dynamic form, or an ontological form and a participatory form — and what the relationship between those registers is.

**Proposed question:** Does the word have a static and a dynamic register — an ontological form (a state or condition the person is in) and a participatory form (a process of becoming or moving toward) — and if so, what is the relationship between them?

## Recommendation

**Do not attempt to force all 171 findings into catalogue questions.** The findings operate at a different level. The correct relationship is:

- When Session B reaches a word, the relevant findings for that word are surfaced
- The findings that are Group A are addressed using the identified catalogue question as the analytical frame
- The findings that are Group B are resolved through direct verse-data examination
- Group C gaps should be considered for addition to the master catalogue as new universal questions

The six Gap C questions should be reviewed and, if adopted, added to the master catalogue as the next extension series (proposed prefix: **X-** for cross-corpus extensions, or the initial of the word that triggered each, but given they arise from the corpus-wide review rather than a single word, a neutral prefix is appropriate).
