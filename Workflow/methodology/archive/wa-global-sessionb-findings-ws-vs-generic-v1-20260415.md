## Classification Criteria

**Word-specific (WS):** The finding is only intelligible in the context of that particular word's data. The question it raises is tied to specific terms, groups, or verses that belong to this word and cannot be meaningfully asked of another word.

**Generic (G):** The finding describes a structural pattern that could arise in any word's analysis. The underlying question is applicable across the programme regardless of which word is being analysed.

**Mixed (M):** The finding has a word-specific trigger (a particular term or verse) but articulates a generic structural question that the trigger merely instantiates.

The classification is about the *question the finding asks of Session B*, not about the evidence that triggered it.
