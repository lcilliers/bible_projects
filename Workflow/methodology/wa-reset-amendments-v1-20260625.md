## A. New SHARED governing principle — *measurement informs, never decides*

> **Insert** as a new principle in **both** docs' principle sections (`lexical reset §1`, `synthesis-B §1`). This is the parent of amendments B and E.

**P0 — Measurement informs the eye; it never makes the decision.** Quantitative facts about the corpus — occurrence count, co-occurrence, distribution, association strength — are **descriptive and revelatory only**. They surface candidates and show shape. They **never gate**:
- **existence** — a phenomenon attested **once** exists (amendment B);
- **inclusion** — a verse is never admitted *because* it is frequent, nor a movement named *because* it recurs;
- **exclusion** — a verse or phenomenon is never dropped *because* it is rare or sits off-cluster;
- **validity** — whether a pattern is a real inner-being dynamic (vs a linguistic/genre artifact) is a **qualitative read**, not a frequency.

> *Rationale.* The corpus is finite, closed, and authoritative; frequency tracks emphasis and spread, not truth or membership. Clustering and statistics naturally reward density and treat sparse points as noise — the exact failure mode this principle forbids. Measurement is a **lens held to the data, never a sieve laid over it.**

## B. The SINGLETON rule — count is never the existence criterion

> **Insert** into `lexical reset §6` and `synthesis-B §1` + edit `synthesis-B §5 (L2 method)`.

**B1 — A movement of one is a movement.** A recurring shape and a once-attested shape are both **findings**; cardinality is a *property recorded about* a movement, not the *criterion for its being* one. The unit (the movement) is **decoupled from recurrence**. Recurrence, when present, is recorded as **spread/emphasis metadata** — informative, never qualifying.

**B2 — Clustering groups; it does not filter.** L2 groups verses by shared functional shape and emits **every distinct shape present, each with its membership (cardinality 1..n)**. **No group is discarded for low cardinality.** A singleton is a group of size one and is carried forward with the same status, areas (§3), and traceability as any dense movement.

**B3 — Frequency-neutral in both directions (the validity gate).** Because count cannot include or exclude, the gate against *spurious* movements (shapes that co-recur from Hebrew syntax, genre, or parallelism rather than any inner-being dynamic) is **validity, read qualitatively** — never rarity. Thus:
- a **rare** shape that reads as a real dynamic → **kept**, studied in its own right;
- a **frequent** shape that reads as a linguistic artifact → **discarded**, despite its count.

This is the complement of the singleton rule: the two together say *attestation includes, validity excludes, frequency does neither.*

> **Concrete edits to existing text:**
> - `synthesis-B §1`, replace "A movement is named when the shape recurs" → "A movement is named when the shape is **attested** (a single verse suffices); recurrence is recorded as spread, not required for naming."
> - `synthesis-B §5 (L2)`, replace "verses whose … configuration co-recurs form a candidate movement" → "verses are **grouped by shared configuration**; **each distinct configuration is a candidate movement, cardinality 1..n; no group is dropped for low cardinality**. Validity (real dynamic vs artifact) is then read per candidate — frequency-neutral."
> - `synthesis-B §1 emergence test`: keep "≥ some movements the old taxonomy had no name for"; **add a second standing test** — "and every candidate passes a **validity read** (real inner-being dynamic, not a syntactic/genre artifact) decided on the read, never on count."

> *Rationale.* The prior wording made recurrence the existence criterion, which would silently delete real singleton phenomena — exactly what aspect (a) forbids. Splitting *attestation* (existence) from *validity* (artifact-screen) from *count* (spread) lets a thing said once stand, while still screening artifacts — without ever using frequency to do either job.

## C. CLUSTER FORMATION & STATUS — the partition is a workspace, not a meaning

> **Insert** as a new numbered section in `synthesis-B` (suggested §2a, before the cascade), referenced from `lexical reset §6`.

**C1 — A cluster is an operational partition, not an ontological claim.** The cluster set (currently 47) is **how the corpus is sliced so L1–L4 are tractable** — a workspace, not a category. A cluster boundary **carries no embedded meaning**; nothing about the inner being is asserted by where a verse is filed. A cluster is a *starting neighbourhood for attention*, nothing more.

**C2 — How a cluster is formed (established practice).** The ~3,500 Greek and Hebrew terms surfaced from STEP Bible (each with its associated verses) are **loosely coupled by gloss** into **47 clusters**. A cluster is a **logical unit for bringing a term's associated verses into focus together** — a batching device for analysis. Two established mechanics make it provisional, not fixed:
- **Term mobility** — a term can be **moved to another cluster** where it is better placed; the initial gloss-coupling is correctable, not binding.
- **Focus-time boundary-collapse** — verses are analysed **when their cluster comes into focus**; and **at the moment a verse comes into focus, the lexical analysis binds it to other clusters, collapsing the cluster boundaries *for that specific verse*.** The partition holds for batching; it dissolves at the point of analysis.

> *Consistency note.* The clusters are coupled **by gloss** — the very English-surface signal that P2 forbids for *analysis*. This is acceptable **only because the cluster carries no analytical weight**: it is a workspace for gathering verses, not a claim about them. Were the cluster an analytical category, building it on gloss would reintroduce the homograph error-class. The gloss-coupling is safe precisely to the degree the cluster means nothing (C1).

**C3 — Boundary-permeability (the founding insight, one level up).** The focus-time boundary-collapse (C2) is **driven by the lexical record**: the **binding-web** field (lexical reset §3) records, per verse, co-occurring inner-being terms and their relation — and those co-terms routinely belong to *other* clusters. Compounds, shared object-kind, shared operation-shape, and pole-opposition all cross the partition. Therefore:
- the partition is **provisional and porous** by construction (and explicitly correctable — term mobility, C2);
- a verse's **home cluster is one fact about it; its edges to other clusters are equally real facts**, activated the moment the verse is in focus;
- the inner-being picture is assembled **across** clusters (L4), not within any one.

> *Rationale.* "Boundaries won't hold" was the lesson that retired the parts-grid. The same is true of clusters: they are a convenience of slicing, and the lexical analysis itself shows them leaking. Stating this prevents the partition from being mistaken for structure, and licenses the related-verse mechanism (D) as a *first-class* part of the method rather than a patch.

## D. "RELATED VERSES" — definition, and the established B+D pointer mechanism

> **Insert** as a new section in `synthesis-B` (suggested §5a), feeding L2/L3/L4. This also resolves the cross-cluster **join** that the evaluation flagged. It **defines** how relatedness is detected (D1), then specifies how the **established** pointer mechanism (D2) carries it.

**D1 — Related ≠ frequent.** A verse is *related* to a movement under focus by a **relation signal**, not by how often anything occurs. The signals are **read off the L1 edge-record already produced**:

| Relation signal | Source (L1 edge) | What it relates |
|---|---|---|
| **shared shape** | (operation, object-kind, effect, transition, valence) configuration | verses enacting the same movement, any cluster |
| **binding-web tie** | relational web / binding (§3) | verses whose head term co-occurs with / binds to the focus term |
| **shared object-kind** | object / target (§3) | verses directed at the same kind of object (e.g. toward-God) |
| **pole-opposition** | transition / becomes; binding (pole-opposite role) | verses naming the movement's opposite / boundary |
| **shared seat addressed** | faculty/seat addressed (§4) | verses addressing the same seat when the movement runs |
| **cognate/root tie** | bedrock lemma + family | act↔state↔identity of one root across clusters |

**D2 — The B+D pointer mechanism (established practice).** A **pointer is a deferred observation recorded in the database against a verse or a cluster**, that **re-surfaces when that verse or cluster next comes into focus**, to be resolved and taken into account then. It is the project's way of *raising* something now and *resolving* it later — without holding the whole web in one pass (the direct answer to the Part B "can't hold it all at once" failure: the web is traversed **across time, on focus**, not simultaneously).

- **What is recorded in a pointer:** the observation itself + the **D1 relation signal** that occasioned it (why this verse/cluster bears on the focus) + its origin (the verse/cluster/movement that raised it) — so on re-surfacing it is actionable and traceable (P5/P7).
- **B-pointers (L2/L3, Session B):** observations raised within or between clusters; re-surface when the referenced verse/cluster is analysed at cluster scope.
- **D-pointers (L4, Session D):** cross-cluster observations; re-surface when the referenced cluster comes into cross-cluster focus.
- **Open-ended by construction:** a pointer can be raised **whenever** a relation is noticed — including by the **discovery-lookout** (lexical reset §5) when a verse implies a tie to another cluster. So the pointer set **grows as analysis proceeds**; L4 is **not bounded by what any prior framing pre-laid** (the evaluation's L4-seeding concern is answered by the mechanism itself — new cross-cluster ties become new D-pointers as they are seen).

> *Integration note.* The discovery-lookout is the **raiser**; the pointer is the **carrier**; focus is the **trigger**; the D1 signals are the **vocabulary** of what a pointer asserts. A lookout flag that references another cluster/verse → recorded as a B/D pointer → re-surfaces on focus → resolved into (or attached to) a movement.

**D3 — Assembly model `[RESEARCHER DECISION]`.** When related verses are brought into focus, how is a movement assembled across them? Two models; they have different mechanics and failure modes — choose one (or a stated mix):
- **(i) Recognise-then-attach** *(recommended default, and the one consistent with the singleton rule).* Recognise the movement from verses rich enough to show its shape; **attach** sparser single-edge verses (cause-only, effect-only) to the **named** movement as evidence instances by their D1 signal. Each verse is preserved in its own right; nothing is force-merged.
- **(ii) Fragment-stitch.** Reconstruct a movement never wholly present in any verse by combining an antecedent-fragment from some verses with an effect-fragment from others. More powerful, but the join key must be explicit — and if that key is the shared lemma, the **term silently re-becomes the unit**, contradicting synthesis-B §1.

> *Recommendation.* Default to **(i)**; permit **(ii)** only with a declared, non-lemma join key, so the movement stays the unit. Either way: **a sparse verse joins an existing movement; it is never deleted for being sparse** (ties back to B2).

## E. MEASUREMENT INSTRUMENTS — the revealing lenses (non-deciding)

> **Insert** as a new section in `synthesis-B` (suggested §5b). Governed by P0 (amendment A).

**E1 — Instruments surface candidates; the read disposes.** These build areas, associations, and relationships **without forcing things together or pushing them apart**. They are **inputs to the eye, never classifiers.** Each produces candidates that a qualitative read then admits, relates, or sets aside.

| Instrument | What it reveals | Plugs in at | Non-deciding constraint |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Co-occurrence heat-map** (term×term, term×cluster, shape×cluster) | graded association density — *where the warmth is* | L2/L3 (binding-web visualised) | a **cold** cell never excludes; a **hot** cell never auto-includes |
| **Association/affinity matrix** (movement×movement) | how movements bind / trigger / oppose — the cross-movement web | L3/L4 | strength is shown, not thresholded into membership |
| **Distribution histogram** (a term/shape/movement across books, genres, clusters) | spread & concentration — emphasis, not validity | all levels | a tall bar is emphasis; a single bar is a **singleton kept** (B1) |
| **Edge/network view** (nodes = terms or movements; weighted edges = co-occurrence) | **emergent areas without hard lines** — density as topology | L3/L4 | dense regions are *observed*, not drawn as boundaries |

> *Rationale.* This is aspect (b)'s statistical request, disciplined by P0. The heat-map and network views are the right tools precisely because they render structure as **graded** (warm/cool, dense/sparse) rather than **partitioned** — they show association without committing to a boundary, which is what "without forcing it together or pushing it away" requires. The histogram domesticates count: it makes spread *visible* while P0/B1 forbid spread from *deciding*.

**E2 — Bias-guard.** No instrument output is ever stored as a finding or a membership. Its only outputs are **(a)** a candidate related-verse set (→ D), **(b)** a candidate movement-association (→ L3/L4 web), or **(c)** a spread description recorded *on* a movement. The finding is always the **read**, walkable down to verses (P5/P7).

## F. Embedded evaluation points (standing guidance)

> Short rules to fold into the named sections so the reasoning is present at every run.

- **F1 — Tiered clustering key** (`synthesis-B §5 L2`). The L2 key mixes **dense** fields (operation, object-kind — populated on most verses) and **sparse** fields (effect, transition — NONE on most). **Cluster primarily on the dense fields; let the sparse fields *subdivide or enrich* a candidate only where present.** Never cluster verses together on **shared absence** (NONE-effect + NONE-transition is not a similarity).
- **F2 — Validity gate is a named step** (`synthesis-B §5`, at L2 and generalised). Distinct from the emergence test: emergence guards against the *old grid re-steering*; the **validity gate** guards against *found artifacts*. Both run; neither uses count.
- **F3 — Areas-checklist is a checklist, not a grid** (`synthesis-B §3`). The areas inherited from the Tiers (T0–T7) are a **completeness checklist** for describing a movement — they must not become classification bins. Watch: if every emergent movement maps cleanly to an old tier, treat it as a signal the checklist is steering, and re-run the emergence test.
