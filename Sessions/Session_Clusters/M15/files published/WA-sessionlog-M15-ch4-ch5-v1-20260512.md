## 5. Key decisions and principles established this session

**Silence principle (sharpened during Chapter 4 review):** Name a silence only when its absence is meaningful — either because something in the evidence creates an expectation that is then not met, or because the absence itself gives the characteristic its shape. Routine absences require no comment. This principle was formalised into style-method v1.1 §6.

**Coverage-before-reduction sequence:** The quality control process established here — coverage audit first, reduction second — is the correct order. Reduction passes must not remove coverage that was correctly there. Reverse audit (style-method v1.1 §10) formalises this for Chapter 5 onward.

**Chapter boundary discipline:** Chapter 5 makes no reference to inner locations as established findings (that is Chapter 4's work). It refers to location only when movement requires it — e.g. "wisdom that has settled in the heart now expresses itself through speech." This discipline was maintained throughout and should carry forward to subsequent chapters.

**Verbatim quote requirement:** All analytical claims grounded by verbatim verse quotation. Where a verse was referenced without quotation in an initial draft, it was treated as a gap in the audit. The minimum is not a citation but a quote.
