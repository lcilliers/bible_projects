### Verse-grounded observations — written on discovery

**OBS-P3-001** — ya.da (H3045) shows a fundamental structural division in its confirmed verses. One register names knowing as *relational recognition and covenantal acknowledgement* — Gen 18:19 ("I have chosen him that he may command his children"), Exo 5:2 ("I do not know the Lord"), 1Sa 2:12 ("did not know the Lord"), 1Sa 3:7 ("did not yet know the Lord"), Hos (multiple). This is not information transfer but constitutive relational orientation. A second register names knowing as *epistemological awareness of facts or states* — Gen 41:21 ("no one would have known they had eaten"), Gen 48:19 ("I know, my son, I know"), 1Sa 14:3 ("did not know that Jonathan had gone"). The two registers are distinct inner-being phenomena: the first is foundational to sub-group C's relational knowing; the second is closer to factual awareness (which may need a sub-group of its own or integration with oida).

**OBS-P3-002** — cha.kham (H2450) confirmed verses cluster in three distinct contexts: (a) craft-wisdom as Spirit-given inner endowment — Exo 28:3 ("all the skillful whom I have filled with a spirit of skill"), Exo 31:6, 35:10, 35:25 — this is wisdom-as-craft-competence, located in the heart and Spirit-given; (b) wisdom as holistic character quality — Pro 10:8 ("wise of heart will receive commandments"), 14:16 ("one who is wise is cautious and turns away from evil"), 2Sa 14:20 ("wisdom like the wisdom of the angel of God"); (c) court/advisory wisdom — Gen 41:8,33,39 (Pharaoh seeking wise men), Dan 2/5 wise men of Babylon. Observation: the craft-wisdom register and the character-wisdom register are distinct inner phenomena, but cha.kham covers both. This does not require two sub-groups — it reflects the breadth of the term. Both senses evidence wisdom as an inner endowment rather than merely an achieved skill.

**OBS-P3-003** — bin (H0995) confirmed verses consistently name understanding as *inner perception of what is being communicated or revealed* — 1Sa 3:8 ("Eli perceived that the Lord was calling the boy"), Job 23:5 ("understand what he would say to me"), Dan 8:16 ("make this man understand the vision"), Dan 9:22 ("given you insight and understanding"), Dan 10:12 ("from the first day that you set your heart to understand"). The faculty engaged is distinctly perceptive-cognitive, not merely volitional. Critically, Dan 10:12 shows that bin is linked to humbling oneself — understanding here is not purely intellectual; it has a relational-disposition dimension (setting heart toward God enables the perceptive faculty). This is a cross-group observation.

**OBS-P3-004** — bi.nah (H0998, H0999) verses confirm the faculty of understanding as an inner capacity: Job 28:12,20,28 ("where is the place of understanding?" ... "the fear of the Lord, that is wisdom, and to turn away from evil is understanding"), Dan 8:15 ("sought to understand it"), Dan 9:22, 10:1 ("he understood the word and had understanding of the vision"), Isa 11:2 ("Spirit of wisdom and understanding"). Job 28:28 is a defining verse: *turning away from evil is understanding* — the faculty of understanding is not separable from moral orientation. This blurs the A/B boundary: wisdom and understanding share a common moral root.

**OBS-P3-005** — te.vu.nah (H8394) confirmed verses: Exo 31:3, 35:31 (paired with knowledge and skill — Spirit-given), Job 26:12 ("by his understanding he shattered Rahab"), Job 32:11 ("wise sayings"), Pro 10:23, 11:12, 14:29 ("slow to anger has great understanding"), Pro 14:33 (wisdom rests in the heart of a man of understanding), Isa 11:2. These confirm te.vu.nah as a holistic inner faculty — not merely cognitive but constitutionally linked to emotional regulation (Pro 14:29: slow to anger = great understanding). This is a strong finding: understanding-as-faculty and emotional-self-governance are linked in te.vu.nah's semantic field.

**OBS-P3-006** — ya.ats (H3289) verses consistently show *giving or receiving counsel as an inner-being act*: 2Sa 16:23 ("counsel of Ahithophel was as if one consulted the word of God"), 2Sa 17:7 ("this time the counsel that Ahithophel has given is not good"), Isa 9:6 ("Wonderful Counselor"), Pro 12:20 ("those who plan peace have joy"), Pro 13:10 ("with those who take advice is wisdom"). The counsel dynamic consistently involves an inner act of purposive discernment — not mere advice-giving but the expression of inner deliberative wisdom. ya.ats bridges the E (deliberative planning) and A (wisdom as character) sub-groups. A counsellor is wise because their inner wisdom produces sound deliberative output.

**OBS-P3-007** — da.at (H1847) confirmed verses: Gen 2:9,17 (tree of knowledge of good and evil — da.at as a named inner capacity differentiated from other goods); Exo 31:3, 35:31 (Spirit-given triad: skill + intelligence + knowledge); Isa 5:13 ("exile for lack of knowledge"); Isa 11:2 ("Spirit of knowledge"); Dan 12:4 ("knowledge shall increase"). The tree of knowledge verses are pivotal: da.at names an inner capacity so constitutive that acquiring it transforms the person's standing before God. This is not factual information but a constitutive inner orientation — moral epistemology. The Exodus triad (skill + intelligence + knowledge) shows da.at operating as one of three Spirit-given inner capacities, paired with te.vu.nah.

**OBS-P3-008** — sa.khal (H7919A) confirmed verses show a persistent pattern: the term names prudent/successful action that expresses an inner orientation of wisdom applied practically. 1Sa 18:5,14,15,30 (David "had success" / "was successful" — sa.khal as outcomes of inner prudent wisdom); Job 34:35 ("without insight"), Dan 9:13 ("gaining insight by your truth"), Dan 11:33 ("wise among the people shall make many understand"), Dan 12:3 ("those who are wise shall shine"). The Daniel verses reveal sa.khal operating as a spiritual-epistemological insight capacity, not merely pragmatic cleverness. This pushes sa.khal toward the A/B boundary — it is wisdom-as-prudent-application, which straddles wisdom-as-character (A) and discernment-as-faculty (D).
