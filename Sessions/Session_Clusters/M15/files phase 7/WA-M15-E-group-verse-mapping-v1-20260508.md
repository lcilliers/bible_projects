## Key analytical findings from verse reading

**OBS-E-001 — Gen 50:20 is the definitive directional-neutrality verse for M15-E.**
"You meant evil against me, but God meant it for good" — cha.shav H2803I applied to both human evil-devising (Group 1766) and divine welfare-purposing (Group 1767) in the same verse with the same term. The inner capacity of purposive planning is completely directionally neutral; what it serves depends entirely on the agent and the object.

**OBS-E-002 — Isa 9:6 belongs in Group 407, not 406.**
"Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace" — the ya.ats-root in "Counselor" names deliberative counsel as a constitutive attribute of the messianic person. This is divine purposive counsel at its highest expression, not counsel in opposition. Moving to Group 407 is analytically necessary.

**OBS-E-003 — Rev 17:17 (gnōmē, Group 1788) is the most theologically significant gnōmē verse.**
"God has put it into their hearts to carry out his purpose by being of one mind and handing over their royal power to the beast, until the words of God are fulfilled." The deliberate purposive gnōmē of the beast's followers is simultaneously their own inner intent and the instrument of divine sovereignty. Human deliberate planning as the vehicle of divine will — the same phenomenon as Gen 50:20 but now at the eschatological level.

**OBS-E-004 — Psa 16:7 (ya.ats, Group 405) reveals the heart as the channel of received counsel.**
"I bless the Lord who gives me counsel; in the night also my heart instructs me." Divine counsel is given, then received and processed by the heart. The heart as the organ that internalises and applies what God has counselled — the movement from divine ya.ats to heart-instruction.

**OBS-E-005 — Directional neutrality is the structuring principle of M15-E.**
Groups 405/406 (ya.ats), 1766/1767 (cha.shav H2803I), 1647/1648 (boulomai human/divine), 1787/1788 (gnōmē human/divine placed) — every major term-pair shows the same phenomenon: the inner deliberative faculty serving either righteousness or evil, and either human or divine agents, without restriction.
