## Key analytical findings

**OBS-G-006 (ra.yon):** The Daniel corpus shows ra.yon consistently producing somatic effects — alarm, changed colour, knocking knees. The inner thought-content is not merely cognitive; it erupts into the body. This establishes a direct inner-thought-to-body-state pathway through ra.yon. Dan 7:28 adds the deliberate counter-action: Daniel 'kept the matter in his heart' despite the physical effects — an act of inner containment.

**OBS-G-004 (re.a):** Psalm 139 deploys re.a bidirectionally: the human's thoughts are known by God (v.2); God's thoughts toward the human are precious (v.17). The same term carries both directions. This is the only term in M15-G that shows this reciprocal structure — inner thought-content as the medium of mutual knowing between God and the human person.

**OBS-G-005 (esh.to.nah):** Plans perish at death (Psa 146:4). This is the starkest mortality-definition of any inner content in the cluster: the thought-content does not survive the departure of the breath. Connects to the broader M15 question of what is perishable vs what is imperishable in human inner life.

**OBS-G-010/G-011 (noema / ennoia):** Together these two NT terms articulate the full vulnerability spectrum of formed inner thought-content. Noema is what can be attacked from without (blinded, hardened, led astray, captured). Ennoia is what is held within as intent and can be adopted as armour. Heb 4:12 places both under the discernment of the word of God — neither the arising thoughts (F-8, enthumesis) nor the held intentions (G-11, ennoia) are hidden from it.

**F/G boundary confirmed from reading:** M15-F terms name the activity of inner musing and reflective processing (si.ach, ha.gig, dialogizomai, enthumeomai etc.). M15-G terms name the formed content that is held, arising, or at risk (ra.yon, noema, ennoia, mad.da, re.a etc.). The boundary holds: activity vs content.
