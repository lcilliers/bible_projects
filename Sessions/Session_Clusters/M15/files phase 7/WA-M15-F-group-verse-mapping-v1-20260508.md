## Key analytical findings

**Psa 39:3 (ha.gig, F-3):** The causal sequence is unique in M15-F: inner musing -> heart-heat -> compelled speech. Ha.gig is the only term that explicitly names the somatic effect of meditation — the heart becoming hot. This links meditative inner activity to body-state.

**Heb 4:12 (enthumesis, F-8):** Definitively distinguishes enthumesis (arising thoughts) from ennoia (held intentions) — two distinct inner phenomena. The word of God discerns both and the difference between them. This distinction will be foundational for M15-G (which handles thought-content).

**Mat 15:19 / Mar 7:21 (dialogismos, F-10):** Dialogismos heads the vice list in both Gospels — it is the first-order inner phenomenon from which all subsequent sins flow. The heart is the source; dialogismos is its immediate product; murder, adultery, etc. follow from the dialogismos.

**Mar 2:8 (dialogizomai, F-12):** The scribes' dialogizomai is heart-located; Jesus perceives it in his spirit. A cross-faculty transaction between persons: one person's heart generates questioning that another person's spirit perceives. Two inner faculties of different persons interact.
