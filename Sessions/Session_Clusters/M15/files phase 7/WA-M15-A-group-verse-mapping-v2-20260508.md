## Group A-1 — Wisdom as Spirit-given inner craft-skill for sacred work

**Status:** NEW GROUP (existing code 1751 partially overlaps but needs refinement — see note)
**Description from verses:** cha.kham and cha.shav H2803G name an inner capacity for skilled craft that is explicitly Spirit-given. The skill resides in the heart/mind and is directed toward sacred construction. This is not general competence — it is an endowment for holy work.
**Anchor verse:** Exo 28:3 ("all the skillful whom I have filled with a spirit of skill, that they make Aaron's garments") — the Spirit-filling is named as the source of the inner craft-skill. cha.kham = skillful; the Spirit fills them with skill (cha.kham). This is the definitional verse.

**Note on Jer 10:9, Isa 40:20, Amo 6:5:** These verses show the same inner craft-design capacity serving wrong objects (idols, luxury). The capacity itself is the same; what it serves differs. They belong in A-1 as instances of the craft-wisdom faculty, not in A-4 (inverted wisdom), because the term names the skill, not the person's self-orientation. However, the verses carry a secondary observation: craft-wisdom is directionally neutral — the same inner endowment can serve sacred or profane ends.

**Note on 2Sa 14:14 (vr=54610):** "God devises means so that the banished one will not remain an outcast" — cha.shav H2803G applied to divine purposive action. This verse does not show craft-wisdom; it shows deliberate planning. The term here means "devise purposively" not "design craftily." → **Set aside from A-1; belongs in M15-E (deliberative planning).** *This is a case where the registry-era assignment (group 1751) is incorrect — the verse text does not show craft-skill but divine purposive planning.*

## Group A-2 — Wisdom as holistic character quality in the wise person

**Status:** RETAINED AND REFINED (from existing codes 2473 and 2474 — see note on split)
**Description from verses:** cha.kham in Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and historical narrative names wisdom as a constituted inner quality of the person — not a role or a skill but who they are. The wise person is characterised by this quality: they receive instruction, they are cautious, they hold back their spirit, their heart is in the right place. The quality is relational — it shows in how they relate to others, to rebuke, to advice.
**Anchor verse:** Pro 14:16 ("one who is wise is cautious and turns away from evil, but a fool is reckless and careless") — wisdom as the inner orientation that produces right conduct. The cha.kham is identified by their characteristic response to moral danger.

**Note on existing codes 2473 and 2474:** Reading the verses shows that 2473 (cha.kham wisdom-character) and 2474 (cha.kham in governance/appointment) are not cleanly separate. Some 2474 verses describe wisdom as a qualification for office — that is a functional-institutional use (A-3 below). Other 2474 verses describe the wise person in terms of character. The split will be resolved in the verse-by-verse assignment below.
