### M15-A-VCG01 — Wisdom as God's own attribute

_The term names wisdom as belonging to God, to Christ as the embodied wisdom of God, or to Wisdom personified as God's own deliberative inner capacity. Inner-being phenomenon: divine inner attribute. The verses describe wisdom not as something held by humans but as something inhering in God himself — depths of God, attribute of the Lamb, content of doxology, what God possesses in fullness._

**Anchor:** `[209169]` **Dan 2:20** · `H2452 chokh.mah` (wisdom)

> Dan 2:20 Daniel answered and said : “ Blessed be the name of God forever and ever , to whom belong wisdom and might .
> 
> **Anchor rationale:** Names wisdom as belonging to God in direct address — 'to whom belong wisdom and might'. The simplest, most direct statement of divine ownership: wisdom is God's, not a quality he received or was given. Sets the phenomenon clearly without additional layers.

### M15-A-VCG02 — Wisdom as inner endowment given by God to a named recipient

_The term names wisdom as a divinely-given inner endowment placed into a specific named person — Jesus growing in it, Solomon receiving it in answer to prayer, Daniel receiving it through the Spirit, the sanctuary craftsmen filled with the Spirit of skill, apostolic believers receiving it through Christ. Inner-being phenomenon: received inner capacity. The common feature is that the verse names the gift, the giver, or the recipient — wisdom comes into the person from God._

**Anchor:** `[7498]` **Jam 1:5** · `G4678 sofia` (wisdom)

> Jam 1:5 If any of you lacks wisdom , let him ask God , who gives generously to all without reproach , and it will be given him .
> 
> **Anchor rationale:** Most explicit statement of the giving structure: God gives wisdom to the one who asks, generously and without reproach. Names the giver, the recipient, and the act of giving. Covers the whole group's logic in a single verse — the wisdom is not already held but received on request.

### M15-A-VCG03 — Wisdom as the settled inner character of the wise person

_The term names wisdom as the standing inner quality of a person — not the moment of receiving, but the constituted character. The wise person walks, hears instruction, restrains impulse, governs speech, builds, perceives the right time, is teachable, is recognised by their fruits. Inner-being phenomenon: constituted character. Wisdom as a settled inner disposition that shapes what the person does across the whole of their life._

**Anchor:** `[7390]` **Pro 16:23** · `H2450 cha.kham` (wise)

> Pro 16:23 The heart of the wise makes his speech judicious and adds persuasiveness to his lips .
> 
> **Anchor rationale:** Names wisdom as seated in the heart and as shaping what comes out of the person — speech. 'The heart of the wise makes his speech judicious.' Shows the constituted inner quality (heart) producing its constituted outward expression (judicious speech). Captures the whole-person character nature of the phenomenon — wisdom is not an act but a disposition that governs.

## 3. Proposed set-aside — 39 verses

_Reason: `no_inner_being_phenomenon`._
