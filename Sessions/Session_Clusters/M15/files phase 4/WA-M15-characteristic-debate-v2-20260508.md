## 1. What the verses show — summary observations before grouping

The systematic verse reading produced 25 recorded observations (OBS-P3-001 through OBS-P3-025). The key findings that reshape the v1 groupings are:

**Finding 1 — ya.da has two structurally distinct registers.** Relational/covenantal knowing (Gen 18:19, Exo 5:2, 1Sa 2:12, 3:7 — "did not know the Lord") is a different inner phenomenon from epistemological awareness of facts (Gen 48:19, 1Sa 14:3). The first is constitutive relational orientation; the second is factual awareness. These likely belong in different sub-groups.

**Finding 2 — wisdom and understanding share a moral root.** Job 28:28 ("to turn away from evil is understanding"), Dan 10:12 (setting heart toward God enables understanding), Pro 14:29 (slow to anger = great understanding) — the A/B boundary is real but penetrated by shared moral ground. The separation is analytical, not ontological.

**Finding 3 — froneō names settled inner mindedness, not a perceptive faculty.** Phili 2:5 ("have this mind... which is in Christ Jesus"), Phili 3:19 ("minds set on earthly things") — froneō names the whole inner orientation of the person. It belongs with wisdom-as-character (A), not discernment (D).

**Finding 4 — sōfrosunē names inner orderliness/soundness.** Act 26:25 contrasts it with madness — it names the ordered inner state. This is more governance/self-regulation than wisdom or discernment. Boundary territory.

**Finding 5 — dialegō in Acts is outward rational argumentation, not inner musing.** May belong in D (exercise of rational capacity) or BOUNDARY, not F (meditation/reflection).

**Finding 6 — logismos is conscience-adjacent** (Rom 2:15 — "conflicting thoughts accuse or excuse... alongside conscience"). This places it closer to D/H than G (static thought-content).

**Finding 7 — The cha.shav variants split across sub-groups** by semantic emphasis: H2803I devise/plan → E; H2803J assess/judge → C/D; H2803G design/craft → A or BOUNDARY.

**Finding 8 — BOUNDARY candidates are confirmed.** logos, meristēs, autarkeia, gumnazō, diabebaioō, tir.gal, eikō, methermēneuō, diermēneutēs, hermēneia, pe.sher — none carry the inner characteristic as primary semantic load.

## 2. Revised provisional sub-groups

### M15-A: Wisdom as holistic inner character and orientation

**Terms proposed:**  
sofia (G4678), sofos (G4680), sofizo (G4679), chokh.mah (H2452), cha.kham (H2450), chak.kim (H2445), tu.shiy.yah (H8454), se.khel (H7922) [provisional — see OQ-P3-004], asofos (G0781) [structural opposite, held here], froneō (G5426) [moved from D — see Finding 3], fronimos (G5429)

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Gen 41:39 ("there is none so discerning and wise as you are" — bin + cha.kham together); 2Sa 14:20 ("wisdom like the wisdom of the angel of God"); Phili 2:5 ("have this mind... which is yours in Christ Jesus"); Pro 14:16 ("one who is wise is cautious and turns away from evil")

**What the verses show:** Wisdom is named as a holistic inner quality — a settled orientation of the whole person that shapes perception, decision, and character. It is Spirit-given (Exo 28:3, 31:3,6), covenantally oriented (1Sa 2:12 — not knowing the Lord = opposite of wisdom), and morally constitutive. The Philippians uses of froneō show the same holistic inner-orientation pattern.

**Structural opposite:** folly, asofos, the fool whose heart does not receive commandments

---

### M15-B: Understanding as inner perceptive faculty

**Terms proposed:**  
bin (H0995), bi.nah (H0998, H0999), sunesis (G4907), suniēmi (G4920), te.vu.nah (H8394), fronesis (G5428) [provisional — see OQ-P3-009], asunetos (G0801) [structural opposite, held here], sunetos (G4908), epistēmōn (G1990)

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Dan 10:12 ("from the first day that you set your heart to understand and humbled yourself"); Job 28:28 ("to turn away from evil is understanding"); Pro 14:29 ("slow to anger has great understanding"); Dan 9:22 ("given you insight and understanding"); Act 28:26-27 ("hear but never understand... their heart has grown dull")

**What the verses show:** Understanding is a distinct perceptive faculty — the inner capacity to receive, perceive, and grasp what is being revealed or communicated. Crucially, the verses show this faculty is not purely cognitive: Dan 10:12 links setting the heart to understand with humbling oneself before God — the faculty of understanding is enabled by a certain inner disposition. Job 28:28 makes turning from evil definitional of understanding. The faculty is morally conditioned. Act 28:26-27 shows understanding blocked by heart-dullness.

**Key distinction from A:** Wisdom (A) is the holistic quality; understanding (B) is the perceptive faculty. One can be wisdom's *outcome* without being wisdom's *process*.

### M15-C: Knowledge as inner content, relational knowing, and covenantal recognition

**Terms proposed:**  
ya.da (H3045) [relational/covenantal register only — OBS-P3-001], da.at (H1847), de.a (H1843), de.ah (H1844), mad.da (H4093), man.da (H4486), oida (G6063), agnoeō (G0050) [spiritual ignorance = structural opposite]

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Gen 18:19 ("I have chosen him... to keep the way of the Lord" — ya.da as relational election-knowledge); Exo 5:2 ("I do not know the Lord"); 1Sa 2:12 ("worthless men — did not know the Lord"); Isa 11:2 ("Spirit of knowledge"); Dan 12:4 ("knowledge shall increase")

**What the verses show:** The knowledge sub-group is anchored by relational/covenantal knowing — knowing as a constitutive orientation toward a person (especially God). da.at of God is the relational foundation of all moral epistemology. The absence of this knowing (Exo 5:2, 1Sa 2:12, Isa 5:13 — "exile for lack of knowledge") is moral and relational catastrophe, not merely informational deficit. The tree of knowledge (Gen 2:9,17) names da.at as a constitutive inner capacity that reorients the whole person. Factual-epistemological ya.da uses (Gen 48:19, Gen 41:21,31) are also in this sub-group but represent a less morally loaded register.

### M15-D: Discernment, practical judgment, and moral perception

**Terms proposed:**  
sa.khal (H7919A), a.rum (H6175), a.rom (H6191), diakrinō (G1252), diaginōskō (G1231), ta.am (H2938) [confirmed non-physical uses], gnōmē (G1106) [deliberative-judgment sense], logismos (G3053) [moved from G — OBS-P3-017], se.khal (H7920)

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Pro 14:8 ("wisdom of the prudent is to discern his way"); Gen 3:1 ("the serpent was more crafty" — a.rum showing directional neutrality); Rom 2:15 ("conflicting thoughts [logismos] accuse or excuse" alongside conscience); 2Sa 19:35 ("can I discern what is pleasant" — ta.am); Dan 8:23 ("understands riddles" — bin showing overlap)

**What the verses show:** Discernment names the applied perceptive act — judging a situation wisely, making the right moral-practical distinctions. sa.khal in Daniel names spiritual-epistemological insight applied in context of moral urgency (Dan 9:13, 12:3). a.rum in Proverbs consistently describes the inner quality that enables right reading of situations (Pro 12:16, 14:8, 14:15). logismos in Rom 2:15 operates alongside conscience in moral self-assessment — this locates it in the judgment/discernment sub-group rather than static thought-content.

**Key distinction from B:** Understanding (B) is the faculty of receiving/perceiving revelation. Discernment (D) is the faculty of judging situations and making moral-practical distinctions. Understanding is more receptive; discernment is more evaluative.

---

### M15-E: Deliberative planning, purposive counsel, and purposeful intent

**Terms proposed:**  
ya.ats (H3289), bouleuō (G1011), boulomai (G1014), za.mam (H2161), uts (H5779), a.shit (H6246), a.shat (H6245B), shit (H7896K), protithēmi (G4388), pronoia (G4307), chesh.bon (H2808), gnōmē (G1106) [resolution/decision sense], cha.shav H2803I [planning sense]

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
2Sa 16:23 ("counsel of Ahithophel was as if one consulted the word of God"); Gen 50:20 ("you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good"); Isa 9:6 ("Wonderful Counselor"); Pro 12:20 ("those who plan peace have joy"); Dan 11:24 ("he shall devise plans against strongholds")

**What the verses show:** The planning sub-group names an inner capacity of purposive forward-orientation — forming intent, devising plans, giving and receiving counsel. Gen 50:20 is paradigmatic: the same planning faculty (cha.shav) operates in both human malevolent scheming and divine redemptive purpose — the same faculty, opposite objects. 2Sa 16:23 elevates the counsel of Ahithophel to the level of divine consultation — wise deliberative counsel is understood in Scripture as an expression of divine wisdom mediated through a human inner capacity.

### M15-F: Meditative and reflective inner activity

**Terms proposed:**  
si.ach (H7878) [confirmed uses], si.chah (H7881), se.ach (H7808), ha.gig (H1901), ha.gut (H1900), hig.ga.von (H1902H) [confirmed uses], enthumeomai (G3392), enthumēsis (G1761), dialogizomai (G1260) [inner-dialogue sense], ennoia (G3372)

**Note on dialegō:** H1256 dialegō (Acts uses) likely belongs in BOUNDARY or D — the Acts evidence is outward argumentation, not inner musing. To be resolved in Phase 4 (OQ-P3-010, new).

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Psa 77:6 (confirmed group: meditation/musing); Luk 12:17 ("thought to himself, 'What shall I do?'" — dialogizomai as inner deliberative self-address); Act 17:29 ("imagination of man" — enthumēsis as inner creative thought)

**What the verses show:** Meditation and inner reflection name the inner turning of the mind on received material — the responsive, receptive inward activity of the person musing before God or reasoning within themselves. This is distinct from planning (E, which is projective) and from thought-content (G, which is static). The activity-nature of F is what defines it.

### M15-G: Inner thought-content — the mind's formed thoughts

**Terms proposed:**  
noēma (G3540), epinoia (G3375), dianoēma (G1270), ash.tut (H6248), ra.yon (H7476), re.a (H7454), esh.to.nah (H6250), cha.shav H2803J [assessment/judgment sense] [partially — overlaps with D]

**Note on logismos:** Moved to D following OBS-P3-017.

**Verse anchor for this group:**  
Dan 5:6 ("his thoughts alarmed him" — ra.yon as inner thought with somatic effect); Dan 7:28 ("my thoughts greatly alarmed me, and my color changed, but I kept the matter in my heart" — ra.yon held in the heart)

**What the verses show:** Thought-content names what is held within — the formed thoughts of the mind, the inner repository. Dan 7:28 is definitional: ra.yon names thoughts that are held in the heart after alarming the whole person — they are inner objects. noēma in Phili 4:7 ("guard your hearts and your minds") names the thought-content of the mind. These terms name what the mind holds, not what the mind does.

---

### M15-H: Inner awareness, spiritual knowing, and the ignorance-boundary

**Terms proposed:**  
suneidō (G4894) [subject to Phase 4 review of Act 14:6], agnoeō (G0050) [moved to C as structural opposite — or held here as awareness-ignorance boundary term]

**Observation:** This provisional sub-group (H) is the weakest of the eight. The verse reading has clarified that agnoeō's confirmed uses are best understood as the structural opposite of M15-C (relational/covenantal knowledge). suneidō in Act 14:6 may be a simple factual-awareness use rather than the conscience-aware use. This sub-group may dissolve in Phase 4, with its terms redistributed to C (agnoeō as structural opposite) and BOUNDARY (suneidō, logos).
