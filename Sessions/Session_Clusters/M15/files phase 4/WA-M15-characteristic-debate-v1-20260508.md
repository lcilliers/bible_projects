## 1. The cluster and its scope

M15 contains 90 terms (43 Hebrew, 47 Greek) covering the inner-being vocabulary of wisdom, understanding, and knowledge across both testaments. With 1,730 active verses and representation across all major biblical corpora, this is one of the largest clusters in the programme.

The cluster is not semantically uniform. Reading all 90 glosses against the T1 characteristic definition reveals at minimum seven distinct phenomena within the cluster boundary. This means M15 will require multiple sub-groups. The sub-group structure below is the Phase 3 hypothesis to be tested.

---

## 2. The T1 characteristic test

A distinct inner-being characteristic (per the programme's T1 definition) has:
- Identifiable constitutional location (spirit, soul, heart, mind, conscience, will)
- Engages a distinguishable set of inner faculties
- Produces recognisable impact on the person
- Has a structural opposite
- Can be distinguished by causal direction or directional object

This test is applied to each candidate group below to determine whether it names a distinct characteristic or merely a descriptive/qualifying function.

## 3. Candidate sub-groups — provisional

### M15-A: Wisdom as holistic inner character

**Terms proposed:**  
sofia (G4678), sofos (G4680), sofizo (G4679), asofos (G0781), chokh.mah (H2452), cha.kham (H2450), chak.kim (H2445), tu.shiy.yah (H8454), se.khel (H7922) [provisional — may also belong to B]

**Rationale:**  
These terms name wisdom as a constituted inner quality — a settled orientation of the whole person. Wisdom here is not merely knowledge possessed, nor a process of understanding, but a characteristic that shapes how a person perceives, decides, and acts. The Hebrew ḥokmāh and Greek sophia share this holistic character: they name the person, not just the activity. tu.shiy.yah (sound wisdom, effective wisdom) and se.khel (prudent wisdom with skill) are adjacent; both name the constitutive quality rather than the process.

**Structural opposite:** folly (foolishness, ǎwelet, mōriyā, asofos, mōria) — the absence or inversion of the inner wisdom orientation.

**Location:** primarily heart/spirit-level — wisdom is seated in the whole inner person, not merely the cognitive faculty.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING. This sub-group names a distinct, constituted, reversible inner quality.

**Open question OQ-P3-001:** asofos (unwise) and asunetos (senseless) — these are structural opposites of wisdom and understanding respectively. Should they form their own sub-group (negative characteristic) or be held within A and B? Proposed: hold within their respective sub-groups as structural-opposite terms pending Phase 4 verse evidence.

### M15-B: Understanding as inner faculty

**Terms proposed:**  
bin (H0995), bi.nah (H0998, H0999), sunesis (G4907), suniēmi (G4920), te.vu.nah (H8394), fronesis (G5428), sunetos (G4908), asunetos (G0801)

**Rationale:**  
These terms name the faculty of understanding — the inner capacity to perceive meaning, distinguish, and comprehend. Unlike wisdom (which is a holistic character quality), understanding is more specifically a cognitive-perceptive faculty. bin in Hebrew is "to separate, to distinguish" at root — it names the inner act of discernment/perception. te.vu.nah and bi.nah are its noun forms. In Greek, sunesis is the capacity to bring things together into understanding; suniēmi is the verb of that capacity. fronesis is understanding expressed in practical wisdom — judgment. sunetos (intelligent, comprehending) and asunetos (senseless, failing to understand) name the presence/absence of the faculty.

**Structural opposite:** asunetos (senseless, failing to understand); more broadly, the hardened heart that will not understand (Isa 6:9-10 pattern).

**Location:** primarily mind/heart — the inner perceptive faculty.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING. Understanding is a distinct inner faculty, distinguishable from wisdom (character) and knowledge (content).

**Tension with A:** se.khel (H7922) appears at the boundary between wisdom-as-character and understanding-as-faculty. Phase 4 will determine by verse pattern.

### M15-C: Knowledge as inner content and act of knowing

**Terms proposed:**  
ya.da (H3045), da.at (H1847), de.a (H1843), de.ah (H1844), mad.da (H4093), man.da (H4486), oida (G6063), epistēmōn (G1990), gnōmē (G1106) [partially — gnōmē also overlaps E]

**Rationale:**  
These terms name knowledge as both inner content (what is held in the knowing self) and the act of coming to know. ya.da is the most versatile Hebrew knowing-verb — covering intimate relational knowing, moral epistemology, divine self-disclosure, and limits of human understanding. da.at and de.ah are its noun forms. man.da and mad.da are the Aramaic/late Hebrew equivalents found especially in Daniel. oida in Greek names what is known (as settled inner content), in contrast to ginōskō (coming to know in process). epistēmōn names one who is knowing/learned.

**Knowledge vs Understanding:** Knowledge (group C) is the inner content held — what one knows. Understanding (group B) is the inner faculty of perception — the capacity by which one grasps. The two are structurally related but distinct: one can have knowledge without understanding it; one can exercise understanding of what one did not previously know.

**Structural opposite:** ignorance (agnoeō, unknowing); the epistemological darkness named in several prophetic texts.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING. Knowledge as inner content and covenantal relationship is a distinct inner-being phenomenon.

**Note:** gnōmē overlaps with E (planning/purpose) — its "resolution/opinion/judgment" sense belongs in C as settled inner assessment; its "deliberate purpose" sense in E. Phase 4 will determine by verse pattern.

### M15-D: Discernment and practical judgment

**Terms proposed:**  
sa.khal (H7919A), a.rum (H6175), a.rom (H6191), fronimos (G5429), froneō (G5426), frēn (G5424), fronesis (G5428) [overlaps B], diakrinō (G1252), diaginōskō (G1231), sōfroneō (G4993), sōfronizō (G4994), sōfrosunē (G4997), ta.am (H2938) [confirmed non-physical uses only]

**Rationale:**  
These terms name a cluster of related capacities oriented toward practical, applied judgment — the inner capacity to perceive a situation rightly, assess it wisely, and respond appropriately. sa.khal is "to be prudent, to act wisely" — wisdom applied to concrete circumstances. a.rum (shrewd, prudent) and a.rom (craftiness — same root, directionally variable: prudent in the righteous, cunning in the serpent). fronimos (prudent/wise in NT) and froneō (to think/mind as orientation) name the practical bent of the inner person. diakrinō and diaginōskō name the act of judging/distinguishing in specific situations. The sōfronizō/sōfroneō/sōfrosunē cluster names mental soundness — the settled, ordered inner state of right mind.

**Boundary question OQ-P3-002:** The sōfrosunē cluster (mental soundness) sits at the boundary between wisdom/discernment (M15) and self-control/sobriety (potentially a different cluster). Phase 4 must determine whether the verses primarily evidence an inner orientation of wisdom or an inner quality of self-regulation. If the latter dominates, cluster reassignment may be warranted.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING provisionally. This sub-group is the most heterogeneous of the candidates; Phase 4 may split it.

### M15-E: Deliberative planning and counsel

**Terms proposed:**  
ya.ats (H3289), bouleuō (G1011), boulomai (G1014), za.mam (H2161), uts (H5779), a.shit (H6246), a.shat (H6245B), shit (H7896K), protithēmi (G4388), pronoia (G4307), chesh.bon (H2808), gnōmē (G1106) [deliberative sense]

**Rationale:**  
These terms name the inner activity of deliberate planning — forming purpose, devising a course of action, taking counsel. ya.ats is the classic Hebrew term for counsel/advising (both giving counsel and taking it). bouleuō and boulomai name deliberate willing and purposing. za.mam and uts name purposeful scheming/planning. protithēmi is to set forth in advance (purposive intent). pronoia is foresight. chesh.bon is calculation — the deliberative reckoning that precedes action. a.shit/a.shat/shit are minor Hebrew terms for the act of "setting" one's mind to a purpose.

**Structural opposite:** rashness, impulsiveness — acting without inner deliberation.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING. Deliberative planning names a distinct inner activity — the forward-oriented purposing of the will/mind toward future action.

### M15-F: Meditative and reflective inner activity

**Terms proposed:**  
si.ach (H7878) [confirmed uses], si.chah (H7881), se.ach (H7808), ha.gig (H1901), ha.gut (H1900), hig.ga.von (H1902H) [confirmed uses only], dialogizomai (G1260), dialogismos (G1261), dialegō (G1256), enthumeomai (G3392), enthumēsis (G1761)

**Rationale:**  
These terms name the inner activity of meditation, musing, and reflective thought — the person turning something over inwardly, pondering before God or within themselves. si.ach and si.chah name both the inner musing and its outward expression in speech (the same root). ha.gig and ha.gut name the inner sound of meditation — the low murmuring thought of the heart. The Greek dialogizomai/dialogismos cluster names inner reasoning-as-internal-dialogue (the Lucan uses of "they reasoned within themselves" are paradigmatic). enthumeomai/enthumēsis name the inward arousal of thought — what springs up within.

**Distinction from E (planning):** Meditation is responsive and receptive — it turns over what has been received. Planning is projective — it devises what will be done. Both are inner activities but with opposite directional orientations: meditation turns inward on received material; planning projects outward toward future action.

**Distinction from G (thought-content):** Meditation is an activity; thought-content names what is in the mind. Phase 4 will check whether any F-group terms straddle this line.

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING. Meditative inner activity is a distinct, directed inner phenomenon.

### M15-G: Inner thought-content (the mind's contents)

**Terms proposed:**  
noēma (G3540), logismos (G3053), ennoia (G3372), epinoia (G3375), dianoēma (G1270), ash.tut (H6248), ra.yon (H7476), re.a (H7454), esh.to.nah (H6250), cha.shav variants (H2803G/I/J — devising/design/thinking senses)

**Rationale:**  
These terms name not an activity but the content of the inner mind — thoughts as objects, as things held or formed within. noēma is "thought, the product of thinking" — what the mind has produced. logismos is "reasoning, calculation, thought" — the thing reasoned. ennoia, epinoia are "thought-content, conception." The Aramaic and Hebrew equivalents (ra.yon, re.a, ash.tut, esh.to.nah) name "thought/purpose" as inner content, especially in Ecclesiastes and Daniel.

**Tension with F (reflection):** The distinction between thought-as-content (G) and reflection-as-activity (F) is subtle. In Phase 4 this will be tested by verse: does the term name a thing held within, or an activity done within?

**T1 verdict:** CHARACTERISTIC-BEARING (as the inner repository of thought-content — this is a genuine inner-being locus, the "what the mind holds").

### M15-H: Inner knowing as recognition, awareness, and conscience

**Terms proposed:**  
suneidō (G4894), agnoeō (G0050) [confirmed uses], logos (G3056) [confirmed inner-dwelling uses only], psuchikos (G5591)

**Rationale:**  
These terms name specific aspects of inner knowing that are relationally or morally oriented. suneidō is "to know with" — joint awareness; the closest NT equivalent to conscience as shared knowing. agnoeō in its confirmed uses names inner spiritual ignorance — the condition of not knowing what one should know. logos in its confirmed inner-dwelling uses (5 NT verses) names the word-as-inner-reality, addressing conscience and spirit. psuchikos names the "natural/unspiritual" person — one whose knowing is limited to the non-pneumatic register.

**Open question OQ-P3-003:** Whether this forms a true sub-group or whether suneidō/logos are better held in BOUNDARY pending Phase 4. psuchikos may be TYPE 2. Proposed: hold as provisional sub-group H; Phase 4 to determine.

## 6. What Phase 4 must resolve

The control read will test each provisional group bidirectionally:
- Direction 1: does each term's verse evidence fit the proposed group?
- Direction 2: does the term resist the proposed group and suggest a different placement?

Given M15's size (90 terms, 7–8 provisional sub-groups), Phase 4 will be systematic: work through the BOUNDARY candidates first (these may simplify the main sub-group count), then work through the overlapping terms, then confirm the settled terms.
