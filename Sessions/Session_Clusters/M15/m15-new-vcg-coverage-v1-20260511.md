## New VCGs defined per sub-group

### A

- **VCG-A-01** — Wisdom as constituted inner quality of character: cha.kham as holistic inner endowment
  - _Wisdom as a defining inner quality of the person — not a skill or technique but a whole-person orientation that shows in perception, speech, conduct, and relationships. The quality is positive and characterises those who are genuinely wise._
- **VCG-A-02** — Wisdom as governance qualification: cha.kham as inner capacity required for leadership
  - _Wisdom as the specific qualification needed for the exercise of leadership and governance — it is the inner quality that enables just rule, right appointments, and effective governance._
- **VCG-A-03** — Professional court wise men as institutional class: cha.kham / chak.kim as class title
  - _The court class of professional wise men — summoned for interpretation, giving counsel, named as the foil for what Spirit-given wisdom can do. Their title and incapacity stand together in the Daniel narratives._
- **VCG-A-04** — Wisdom receiving instruction and growing: cha.kham as receptive and cumulative
  - _The wise person's inner quality makes them open to instruction, correction, and growth — wisdom receives and is enriched. Opposed to the fool who trusts only their own judgment._
- **VCG-A-05** — Self-attributed wisdom: cha.kham as false inner claim
  - _Persons who attribute wisdom to themselves without having it — the inner claim is exposed as false, hollow, or dangerous._
- **VCG-A-06** — Wisdom destroyed or absent in judgment: cha.kham as what God removes
  - _God destroys, removes, or withholds wisdom from those who rely on it against him — the wise men of nations are rendered helpless, made fools, or destroyed._
- **VCG-A-07** — Sacred craft wisdom: cha.kham and cha.shav H2803G as God-given inner capacity for sacred construction
  - _The inner capacity for skilled design and execution, explicitly God-given (filled with a spirit of skill), directed toward the construction of sacred objects and spaces. Both cha.kham (the persons) and cha.shav (the designing/executing) are included._
- **VCG-A-08** — Craft skill applied to secular or non-sacred purposes: cha.kham and cha.shav in craft outside the sanctuary
  - _The same inner capacity for craft skill and design applied outside the sacred context — to ships, idols, military machines, and instruments of music._
- **VCG-A-09** — cha.shav H2803G as evil devising: scheming against persons
  - _The same inner devising capacity directed toward harm — plotting, scheming, purposing destruction against persons or groups. Confirmed as distinct from sacred craft use._
- **VCG-A-10** — cha.shav H2803G as divine purposive devising: forming a means of mercy
  - _A single verse. God devises means so that the banished one will not remain an outcast — this is divine inner purposing directed toward a merciful outcome, not craft design or human scheming._
- **VCG-A-11** — tu.shiy.yah: sound wisdom as effective inner resource
  - _Sound wisdom as a substantial, effective inner capacity — held by God, available to the upright, able to sustain and protect, violated by isolation, equated with the fear of God._
- **VCG-A-12** — se.khel: discernment as personal inner quality producing good outcomes
  - _A distinctive inner quality of practical wisdom — discernment, good sense, shrewd perception — that produces favourable outcomes and is recognisable in persons._
- **VCG-A-13** — Aramaic wisdom terms: chokh.mah / chak.kim as divine wisdom given to Daniel
  - _Wisdom as God's exclusive gift — given to Daniel, belonging to God, not a product of professional training. The Aramaic terms operate consistently in the Daniel narratives as marking the contrast between institutional wisdom (which fails) and Spirit-given wisdom (which succeeds)._
- **VCG-A-14** — sofos / sofia: human wisdom overturned by the cross
  - _Human wisdom as a category that the cross renders inadequate, foolish, and subject to divine judgment. God hides revelation from the wise and reveals it to children. The wisdom of the world is folly before God._
- **VCG-A-15** — sofia: wisdom hidden in Christ and given from above
  - _Divine wisdom as the content of what God has hidden in Christ and gives as a gift — the alternative to human wisdom, with named qualities, Spirit-given, belonging to God and the Lamb._
- **VCG-A-16** — sofia: wisdom that has the appearance but not the substance
  - _A named false wisdom — earthly, unspiritual, demonic — that looks like wisdom but does not come from above. Opposed to the wisdom from above._
- **VCG-A-17** — sofia as the mode of proclamation and instruction
  - _Wisdom as the inner quality that shapes how the gospel and community teaching are offered — the wise way of walking toward outsiders, teaching with wisdom, admonishing with wisdom._
- **VCG-A-18** — fronimos: practical shrewd wisdom expressed in action and stewardship
  - _The inner quality of practical wisdom that shows in foresighted action — preparation for what is needed, trustworthy stewardship in the master's absence, shrewd navigation of a hostile world._
- **VCG-A-19** — froneō: the inner orientation of the mind set on divine or earthly things
  - _The habitual inner direction of the whole person — what the mind is set on. The command is to set the mind on things above; the warning is against setting it on earthly or self-oriented things._
- **VCG-A-20** — froneō: relational concern and communal alignment
  - _The inner orientation directed toward another person or the community — attention, care, shared mind, alignment in conflict resolution._
- **VCG-A-21** — sofizo: making wise (positive) and devising myths (negative)
  - _Two opposite uses of the same term — one naming Scripture's transformative work of making the person wise for salvation; the other naming what the apostles explicitly did not do (devise clever myths)._

### B

- **VCG-B-01** — bin / bi.nah as constituted inner perceptive faculty: understanding governing speech, relationships, and perception
  - _Understanding as a substantive inner quality of the person — what they are characterised by. The faculty is active, receptive, and productive: it governs what the wise person perceives, how they speak, and how they relate. The person of understanding is defined by this quality._
- **VCG-B-02** — Understanding as governance qualification: bin / bi.nah as what leadership requires
  - _Understanding paired with wisdom as the inner quality that qualifies persons for governance, judgment, and appointments. The capacity to discern rightly between situations, persons, and cases._
- **VCG-B-03** — Spirit-given understanding: bin / bi.nah / te.vu.nah as a divine endowment
  - _Understanding as explicitly God-given — the inner capacity placed within the person by God or the Spirit, not acquired through human effort. Includes craft intelligence for sacred work and the Messiah's Spirit-given understanding._
- **VCG-B-04** — Understanding gained through seeking and engagement with the word and covenant
  - _Understanding as the fruit of deliberate orientation toward God — seeking, keeping the precepts, holding to the covenant. The faculty grows and deepens through the person's active engagement._
- **VCG-B-05** — God's perceptive comprehension of the human person: bin / bi.nah as divine knowing
  - _God's inner perceptive faculty directed toward the human person — his understanding of thoughts, deeds, plans, and the heart. Divine understanding of the human is comprehensive and intimate._
- **VCG-B-06** — Failed or absent understanding: bin as the person who cannot perceive
  - _Understanding absent in persons or groups — whether through their own failure, the consequence of wickedness, the absence of the divine gift, or Israel's characteristic blindness._
- **VCG-B-07** — Judicial withholding of understanding: suniēmi / bin as blocked comprehension
  - _Understanding deliberately withheld by God — the inner faculty is present but its activation is prevented by divine judgment. Hearing without receiving; perception without comprehension._
- **VCG-B-08** — Christ opening minds to understand: suniēmi as receptive inner comprehension activated
  - _The positive activation of the inner perceptive faculty — Christ opening minds, understanding breaking through, the disciples receiving what had previously been closed to them._
- **VCG-B-09** — Universal failure of human inner comprehension: suniēmi / asunetos
  - _The total absence of inner understanding in fallen humanity — not a specific judicial act but a description of the general human condition before God._
- **VCG-B-10** — Limits of human understanding before divine mystery: bin / bi.nah facing what cannot be grasped
  - _The person exercises the inner perceptive faculty but reaches its boundary — divine works, cosmic scale, the mystery of God's ways are simply beyond human comprehension._
- **VCG-B-11** — Directional attending: bin as perceptive attention directed toward an object
  - _Bin used in the sense of directing perceptive attention toward something — looking closely, examining carefully, paying attention. Distinct from understanding as comprehension. The faculty is directed attention, not cognitive grasping._
- **VCG-B-12** — te.vu.nah as divine creative and governing attribute
  - _Understanding as a property of God himself — the attribute by which he made the heavens and subdued chaos. Self-originating and infinite._
- **VCG-B-13** — te.vu.nah as protective and productive inner quality growing with age and experience
  - _Understanding as an inner quality that benefits its possessor — protective, life-giving, associated with maturity and long experience._
- **VCG-B-14** — te.vu.nah applied to non-sacred or self-serving purposes
  - _The same inner capacity applied outside the sacred context — to idol-making or self-enrichment. The term is directionally neutral; its object determines its moral valence._
- **VCG-B-15** — suniēmi / sunesis: inner comprehension of mystery as divine gift
  - _The inner comprehension of what was hidden — the mystery of Christ, the mystery of God's will — given as a divine endowment, not acquired by human intelligence._
- **VCG-B-16** — sunetos / asunetos: the intelligent/discerning person excluded from divine disclosure; or the foolish excluded from wisdom
  - _Intelligence/discernment as a human category — which the cross reverses, from which divine mysteries are hidden, or which is commended as openness to the word._
- **VCG-B-17** — fronesis: practical wisdom / moral orientation as divine gift
  - _Practical insight as a divine endowment — enabling recognition of God's will, naming the moral orientation of the just, shaping the inner person toward wisdom in all things._
- **VCG-B-18** — epistēmōn: genuine knowing verified by conduct
  - _A single verse. Genuine knowing as an inner quality that must be demonstrated through conduct — not claimed but shown in meekness and good works._

### BOUNDARY

- **VCG-B-01** — Reasoned discourse as engagement of the inner life: dialegō
  - _Paul's method of reasoning in synagogues and public venues — persuasive discourse aimed at reaching and convincing the inner person. The goal is inner persuasion; the form is reasoned argument. The effect can be conviction (Felix alarmed), communal growth, or stubborn resistance._
- **VCG-B-02** — The word as inner-dwelling and conscience-addressing reality: logos (1171-001 uses only)
  - _The logos in these uses is not merely speech but a word that acts within and upon the inner person — dwelling, addressing the conscience, summing the law, revealing inner condition, evaluated by its inner power._
- **VCG-B-03** — Conscious inner awareness: suneidō as moral complicity and cognitive recognition
  - _The same inner faculty of conscious awareness operates in two registers: (a) moral co-knowing — the inner awareness of shared participation in a morally charged act; and (b) cognitive recognition — becoming aware of a situation and acting on it._
- **VCG-B-04** — Interpretive capacity as expression of inner wisdom: pe.sher
  - _A single verse. Knowing the interpretation of a thing is named as the mark of the wise person — the inner wisdom that discerns meaning. The wisdom is so thoroughgoing that it reshapes the outward bearing._
- **VCG-B-05** — Intimate teaching-to-walk: tir.gal as tender, unrecognised nurture
  - _A single verse. God teaching Ephraim to walk — the intimate relational dynamic of the caring parent taking up the child's arms. The pathos is that the one taught did not know who healed them._
- **VCG-B-06** — Translation revealing the inner theological meaning of a name: methermēneuō
  - _The act of translation that discloses what a name means at its theological core — Immanuel meaning God-with-us._
- **VCG-B-07** — Outward insistence arising from inner conviction: diabebaioō
  - _The act of asserting or insisting with confidence — the outward form of settled inner certainty. The two uses both name insistence without adequate inner grounding (first use) or with proper grounding (second use)._
- **VCG-B-08** — Simile characterising an inner state: eikō
  - _The simile reveals what an inner condition is like — the comparison illuminates the nature of inner instability or unreceptive hearing._
- **VCG-B-09** — Inner habituation through training: gumnazō forming the person toward godliness or vice
  - _The inner person is formed through repeated practice — the same mechanism (habitual exercise) produces either godliness and discernment (positive) or greed (negative). The inner disposition is trainable in both directions._
- **VCG-B-10** — Spirit-given interpretation as a community inner-life building gift: hermēneia and diermēneutēs
  - _The Spirit-given capacity to interpret tongues — enabling what is spoken in tongues to become intelligible inner content for the community. Without interpretation, tongues cannot build up the inner life of others; only private inner communion with God remains._

### C

- **VCG-C-01** — da.at as inner possession: knowledge as an inner quality of the wise person
  - _Knowledge as what the wise person holds inwardly — the inner capacity/content that Proverbs aims to produce, that the wise accumulate and guard, and that gives the person strength._
- **VCG-C-02** — da.at of God: the knowledge of God as the defining inner orientation of the covenantal person
  - _Knowledge of God as the substance of the inner life — what it is to know God relationally and ethically. Includes both positive possession and its absence._
- **VCG-C-03** — da.at as instrument of divine work and Spirit-endowment
  - _Knowledge as a Spirit-given capacity; as a divine attribute; as the instrument of the Servant's justifying work. These are cases where da.at has extraordinary theological weight._
- **VCG-C-04** — da.at as absent in the foolish and hollow
  - _Knowledge as what is absent, empty, or distorted — speech without knowledge; wisdom that has become falsehood._
- **VCG-C-05** — da.at as inner intent determining moral status of an act
  - _Da.at (or its absence) as what determines whether an act is intentional — presence of da.at/knowing means wilful; absence means unintentional. The inner moral condition determines culpability._
- **VCG-C-06** — ya.da as covenantal knowing between God and Israel: relational election knowing
  - _The most intimate form of ya.da — God's elective knowing of Israel and Israel's knowing of God as the substance of the covenant relationship. Not merely informational but constitutively relational and moral._
- **VCG-C-07** — ya.da as the "then you shall know" pattern: divine self-disclosure through event producing recognition
  - _A repeated pattern across the OT: God acts, and the purpose of the action is that persons will know — know that he is the Lord, that he is present, that he sent his messenger. The knowing is produced by the event, not by the seeker._
- **VCG-C-08** — ya.da as God's comprehensive knowing of persons: divine inner knowledge of the human
  - _God's knowing of the person's inner life — motives, character, acts, plans, future tendencies. Comprehensive, attentive, penetrating._
- **VCG-C-09** — ya.da as self-knowledge: the person's inner knowledge of their own condition
  - _The person's inner knowing of their own moral state, situation, character, or experience. Includes both honest and distorted self-knowledge._
- **VCG-C-10** — ya.da as moral knowledge guiding right action
  - _The inner moral knowing that directs the righteous person — knowing what is acceptable, what is just, what is right. Its absence names moral blindness; its presence names moral clarity._
- **VCG-C-11** — ya.da as Israel's failure to know God: broken or absent covenantal knowing
  - _Israel's characteristic failure to know God — receiving his gifts without recognising the giver; claiming to know while pursuing other gods; the people who do not perceive._
- **VCG-C-12** — ya.da as limits of human knowing before divine mystery
  - _Human ya.da encountering its own limits before God's work, purposes, and nature — cannot comprehend, cannot find, cannot know the end or the route._
- **VCG-C-13** — de.ah as the knowledge of the Lord filling the earth: eschatological covenantal knowing
  - _Knowledge of the Lord as the content of the eschatological promise — universal, filling, covering as the waters cover the sea._
- **VCG-C-14** — de.a as formed inner opinion or divine perfect knowing
  - _Elihu's inner formed judgment held inwardly and now to be expressed; or applied to God's perfect knowledge of his own works._
- **VCG-C-15** — man.da as reason: the defining inner seat of humanness
  - _Man.da names reason as the inner capacity whose loss reduces the person to the level of animals and whose restoration constitutes restored human dignity._
- **VCG-C-16** — oida as settled inner conviction shaping conduct and orientation
  - _The NT knowing that is not primarily relational or covenantal but convictional — settled inner knowledge about theological facts, moral patterns, and eschatological realities that should orient conduct._
- **VCG-C-17** — oida as identity-shaping knowledge: knowing who you are in Christ
  - _The "do you not know" pattern — Paul's repeated challenge to a community that possesses this knowledge but does not act from it. The knowing shapes identity and therefore conduct._
- **VCG-C-18** — oida as self-knowledge and God's knowing of persons
  - _The person's inner knowledge of their own condition; and God's knowing as the ultimate ground of moral accountability._
- **VCG-C-19** — oida as knowledge divorced from love: the hollow inner capacity
  - _Knowledge that exists without love is named explicitly as capable of being emptied of worth — inflating rather than building._
- **VCG-C-20** — agnoeō: culpable spiritual ignorance of God's ways and character
  - _The inner condition of not knowing God's ways, righteousness, or purposes — when the ignorance is morally significant, not merely informational._
- **VCG-C-21** — agnoeō: neutral informational unawareness (distinct from culpable ignorance)
  - _Uses of agnoeō in the sense of "I do not want you to be uninformed/unaware" — simple factual or situational unawareness without moral weight._

### D

- **VCG-D-01** — Divine moral imputation: crediting or not-crediting righteousness/guilt before God
  - _The inner divine act of reckoning a moral status to a person — positive (faith counted as righteousness; act counted as righteousness) or negative (bloodguilt imputed; iniquity not counted). The reckoning is judicial and moral, not merely classificatory. The subject is always God or divine authority._
- **VCG-D-02** — Inner self-examination: deliberate attending to one's own ways, forming a judgment
  - _The person turns attention inwardly and forms an assessment of their own conduct, situation, or moral standing — not reckoning another, but deliberating about oneself or one's circumstances._
- **VCG-D-03** — Evaluative regard of persons and things: assigning worth or category by reckoning
  - _One person (human or divine) evaluates another person, group, or thing by reckoning — assigning them a worth, category, or status. The reckoning is relational and evaluative, not judicial-moral as in VCG-D-01. Includes both positive and negative assessments, and both legitimate and contested evaluations._
- **VCG-D-04** — sa.khal as prudent wisdom-in-action: conduct aligned with God's ways producing success
  - _The inner quality of prudence expressed in action that is aligned with God's instruction, covenant, or character. The action is both wise (correctly oriented) and effective (produces prospered outcomes). Wisdom and success are co-present._
- **VCG-D-05** — sa.khal as perceptive prudent discernment: seeing clearly what is before you
  - _The inner capacity for perceptive attending — seeing God's acts, situations, or conditions with clarity and wisdom. The prudent person considers, ponders, observes rightly where others miss or fail to attend._
- **VCG-D-06** — a.rum / a.rom: prudent inner disposition as perceptive self-governance
  - _The inner quality of prudence in the sense of perceptive self-governance — seeing situations clearly and governing speech and conduct with care. Directionally neutral: serves righteousness (Proverbs uses) or deception (Gen 3:1, Job 15:5)._
- **VCG-D-07** — ta.am: taste as the faculty of inner discernment
  - _The palate's capacity to test and evaluate food serves as the image for the inner faculty of discerning and testing. Used directly of food-tasting and figuratively of testing words, situations, and the goodness of God._
- **VCG-D-08** — diakrinō as discriminating judgment: inner act of making evaluative distinctions between persons or matters
  - _The inner act of judging and distinguishing — discerning between persons, weighing speech, making or not-making discriminations. The judgment can be right (weighing prophecy, discerning the Lord's body) or wrong (partiality, unjust criticism)._
- **VCG-D-09** — diakrinō as inner self-division: the heart split between faith and hesitation
  - _The person's inner life is not unified — faith and doubt or hesitation coexist, producing wavering, instability, and inability to act from whole-hearted trust._
- **VCG-D-10** — logismos: inner arguments and conflicting thoughts as the site of conscience and spiritual contest
  - _The inner cognitive conflict — thoughts that accuse or excuse (conscience), and arguments or reasoning structures raised against God that must be brought into captivity. Both uses name the inner cognitive life as a contested site._
- **VCG-D-11** — aisthētērion: the cultivated inner faculty of moral discernment
  - _Single verse. Names moral discernment as a trainable inner faculty — not innate but developed through practice. The mature person's ability to distinguish good from evil is an acquired capacity._

### E

- **VCG-E-01** — Human counsel: wisdom given between persons shaping deliberation
  - _One person gives advice or counsel to another — the inner wisdom of the advisor expressed and offered to shape the recipient's purposes. The outcome depends on whether counsel is received or rejected._
- **VCG-E-02** — Hostile human counsel: devising harm through purposive inner planning against another
  - _The inner forming of purposive schemes or counsel directed toward harming another person or group. The devising is deliberate, premeditated, and inner — formed in the heart before being enacted._
- **VCG-E-03** — Noble inner purposing: counsel as expression of moral character
  - _The inner purposing of the noble or morally oriented person — counsel as the outward expression of a sound inner character, including the case where the moral character is reflected back in what is planned._
- **VCG-E-04** — Divine counsel: God's sovereign purposive intent directed toward nations and history
  - _God forms and executes sovereign intention that cannot be annulled or redirected. His counsel is self-originating, irresistible, and encompasses the whole of history. Multiple human terms (ya.ats, cha.shav, za.mam) converge in expressing this._
- **VCG-E-05** — Divine purposive planning with conditional openness to relenting
  - _God forms purposive intent that is held open — the plan is real and announced, but its execution is conditional on human response. This is different from VCG-E-04 (irresistible) — here the divine intent can be withdrawn if the people turn._
- **VCG-E-06** — Purposive divine and human intent working through the same event toward opposite ends
  - _The same event carries both human purposive intent (evil) and divine purposive intent (good) — the two work through each other. Gen 50:20 is the defining verse._
- **VCG-E-07** — God's purposive care for the individual: divine inner attending and planning for the needy
  - _God forms purposive thought directed toward individual persons — specifically those who are poor, suffering, or needing — as an expression of attentive covenantal care._
- **VCG-E-08** — God's purposive planning for his people's welfare: the plans-for-welfare pattern
  - _God forms specific purposive plans for the welfare and future of his people — not reactive, not conditional, but a formed positive intent with named content (welfare, hope, future)._
- **VCG-E-09** — Human volitional intent: boulomai naming purposive inner will in motion
  - _The human inner will/desire forming an intent and moving toward action. Covers a wide range from trivial preferences to morally significant choices. The inner volitional act is what is named._
- **VCG-E-10** — God's sovereign purposive will: boulomai naming divine willing in disclosure, creation, and redemption
  - _The inner purposive will of God or Christ — sovereign, self-originating, directing revelation, creation, gifts, and redemption. The will is not contingent on human response._
- **VCG-E-11** — Deliberate inner decision-making: bouleuō as the mind resolving upon a course of action
  - _The inner weighing of options leading to a resolved decision — the mind deliberating and settling upon a course of action._
- **VCG-E-12** — Deliberate resolve to harm: bouleuō naming purposive decision directed against another
  - _The inner will resolving upon a course of action specifically directed toward harming or killing another person._
- **VCG-E-13** — Considered personal judgment: gnōmē as authoritative inner resolution
  - _A considered inner judgment or resolution — formed with deliberation and offered with authority, whether individual (apostolic judgment) or shared (communal purpose). The resolution is personal and accountable._
- **VCG-E-14** — Purposive inner intention set before the will: protithēmi as planned goal
  - _The act of setting a goal or purpose before the will — either human (a travel intention repeatedly frustrated) or divine (God setting forth Christ as propitiation from an eternal purpose)._
- **VCG-E-15** — Purposeful foresight and provision: pronoia as governance foresight or deliberate inner provision
  - _The capacity for purposeful foresight — either in governance (anticipating and providing for those under care) or in deliberate provision for desire (making plans to gratify inner want)._
- **VCG-E-16** — The rational explanatory faculty: chesh.bon seeking the scheme of things
  - _The inner capacity for rational explanation and calculation — the mind seeking to understand the underlying structure of reality. Belongs to the living; ceases at death._
- **VCG-E-17** — Royal inner decisional planning: a.shit naming a king's purposive appointment
  - _A single verse. The king's inner act of purposing and planning an appointment, disclosed as grounded in Daniel's excellent spirit._
- **VCG-E-18** — The appeal for divine purposive attending: a.shat as hope for divine attention in crisis
  - _A single verse. The captain appeals for the god to give thought to the crew — the hope that divine inner purposing will be directed toward those in need of rescue._
- **VCG-E-19** — Active deliberate inner attending: shit as sustained purposive attention
  - _The inner-attending faculty deliberately directed and sustained — setting the heart to observe, consider, or take counsel within oneself. Produces learning, reorientation, and testimony._
- **VCG-E-20** — Failure or suppression of inner attending: shit as non-engagement
  - _The inner-attending faculty failing, collapsing, or being deliberately suppressed — the person who does not set their heart to what is before them._
- **VCG-E-21** — Communal deliberate purposing: uts as collective counsel-forming
  - _The community deliberately gathering its collective will to form a shared purpose in response to a significant situation._
- **VCG-E-22** — Human purposeful resolve: za.mam as intent formed and set out to achieve
  - _A human subject forms a purposeful intent and commits to carrying it out — the resolve of unified human purposing._

### F

- **VCG-F-01** — Meditative dwelling on God's works and mighty acts: si.ach as sustained inner pondering
  - _The person turns inward attention to God's past acts — creation, history, deliverance, wondrous works — and dwells upon them. The attention is sustained, intentional, and productive of awe or orientation._
- **VCG-F-02** — Meditative dwelling on God's law, precepts, and word: si.ach as law-meditation
  - _The sustained inward dwelling on God's law, precepts, statutes, and promises — the defining meditative practice of the devoted person. The object is always divine instruction. All uses from Psalm 119._
- **VCG-F-03** — All-day sustained meditation as the ground of understanding: si.chah
  - _Sustained, all-day meditative engagement with God's testimonies as the practice that produces understanding — more than teachers can give._
- **VCG-F-04** — Complaint and lament as inner distress voiced: si.ach as outward expression of suffering
  - _The inner dwelling on distress that finds outward expression in complaint, moaning, or lament. The si.ach is the voicing of what has been turned over inwardly._
- **VCG-F-05** — Telling/proclaiming of God's wondrous works: si.ach turned outward
  - _The meditative inner dwelling on God's wondrous works turns outward into proclamation — the result of meditation is speech that tells what God has done._
- **VCG-F-06** — Inner musing that burns to groaning and speech: ha.gig as prayer-substance
  - _Deep inner reflective engagement that intensifies inwardly — becoming fire in the heart — and is then offered to God as the substance of prayer._
