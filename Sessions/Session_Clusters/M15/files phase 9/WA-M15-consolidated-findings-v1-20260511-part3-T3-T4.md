### T3.1 — Perception

#### T3.1.1 — What perceptive capacity does this characteristic engage or constitute?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG01 (Pro 16:23): "a wise man's heart guides his mouth" — wisdom perceives the appropriate and guides expression. VCG04 (froneō): perception directed toward things of God vs things of flesh — wisdom-perception is orientation-perception. VCG08 (H7922 se.khel, Neh 8:8): grasping the meaning of Scripture — intelligibility as wisdom's perceptive mode.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG03 (1Ki 3:9): the discerning heart as the perceptive faculty for governance. VCG06 (Luk 24:45): Christ opening the disciples' minds — the perceptive faculty opened to receive disclosed content. M15-B is constitutively perceptive: understanding IS the act of perceiving distinctions.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 (Psa 139:6): divine comprehensive perception. VCG04 (Hos 6:3): human pursuit-perception of God. VCG10 (Gen 12:11): practical situational perception. VCG11 (Gen 4:1): intimate personal perception. M15-C spans the full perceptive spectrum — widest range in the cluster.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG05 (Job 12:11): the palate testing food as the model for inner discernment-perception. VCG01 (Isa 52:13 sa.khal): perceptive discernment of God's ways. M15-D IS a form of perception — discernment is constitutively perceptive.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — VCG08 (Pro 6:6 pronoia): observation as the basis for purposive foresight. M15-E engages perception primarily as input to planning, not as its core function.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — VCG04 (Dan 7:28 se.khal): focused contemplative attention on received content. VCG02 (Matt 1:20 enthumeomai): reflective perception of a complex situation. M15-F is second-order perception — dwelling on what has already been perceived.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4 noēma): blinding targets thought-content (the output of perception), not the perceptive act. M15-G is the content produced by perception, not the perceptive activity.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (1Cor 1:18): the logos reveals the state of the hearer's inner perceptive capacity — the same word is perceived as folly or power depending on inner state. M15-H is a diagnostic of perception, not itself perception.

**[BOUNDARY]** E — Jude 9 (dialegō): reasoned disputation engages deliberative cognition applied to contended claims. Perceptive engagement is thin in BOUNDARY's single active verse.

---

#### T3.1.2 — Is this characteristic itself a form of perception, or does it produce perception as an outcome?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Both: wisdom includes perceptive capacity (VCG08 understanding Scripture) and produces perceptive outcomes (the wise person sees what others miss). Wisdom is a wisdom-for-perceiving-rightly, not raw perception.

**[B — Understanding]** E — M15-B IS a form of perception. Bin, suniēmi name the perceptive act itself. Understanding does not produce perception as a secondary outcome — it is the perceptive act.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both: ya.da names the act of knowing (perception as act) and the state of having known (knowledge as possession). The characteristic is perception-as-act and perception-as-product simultaneously.

**[D — Discernment]** E — M15-D IS constitutively perceptive. Sa.khal, diakrinō, ta.am all name the discriminating perceptive act.

**[E — Counsel]** E — M15-E uses perception as input but is itself a volitional-deliberative act, not a perceptive one.

**[F — Meditation]** E — M15-F uses received perception as its material but is itself a dwelling activity, not a new perceptive act.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — M15-G is the content produced by perception — the outcome, not the activity.

**[H — Logos]** E — M15-H is a revelatory instrument — it produces perception (or reveals its absence) but is not itself a perceptive faculty.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Not applicable to BOUNDARY as mechanisms rather than characteristics.

---

#### T3.1.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the perceptive range of this characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Governance perception (VCG03 Solomon's governance discernment), directional perception (VCG04 things of God vs flesh), intelligibility perception (VCG08 understanding Scripture). Wisdom's perceptive range is oriented and directional — it perceives in relation to God and right action.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Range: understanding God's ways, prophecy, teaching, strategic situations. The range is broad but coherent: understanding is always the act of grasping the meaning-structure of something.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Widest perceptive range in M15: material (VCG10), relational (VCG04, VCG11), moral (VCG07), propositional (VCG08), divine-comprehensive (VCG01).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Range: prudent-action perception (VCG01), self-evaluative perception (VCG02), gustatory-testing perception (VCG05). All directed at discriminating distinctions.

**[E — Counsel]** E — Perceptive range is secondary: observation feeds foresight (VCG08 ant). Other VCGs focus on the volitional-deliberative act.

**[F — Meditation]** E — Range: inward (VCG04 Daniel's vision), retrospective (VCG02 Joseph), receptive (VCG03 God's law). All directed at received content.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — Perceptive range does not apply to M15-G as content-holder.

**[H — Logos]** E — Range is differentiated by the inner condition of the perceiver: the same logos perceived as folly or power (VCG01).

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Perceptive range not applicable.

### T3.2 — Cognition

#### T3.2.1 — What cognitive processes does this characteristic involve or activate?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG03 (1Ki 3:9): governance judgment. VCG04 (froneō): directional orientation of the mind. VCG08 (Neh 8:8 se.khel): comprehension of meaning. Cognitive processes: orientation-formation, governance-judgment, meaning-comprehension.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG06 (Luk 24:45): opening of the mind to disclosed content. VCG03 (1Ki 3:9): perceptive discernment in governance. Cognitive processes: opening, grasping, discerning.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (Job 19:25): conviction-formation. VCG04 (Hos 6:3): pursuit-cognition. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): accumulation. VCG09 (Act 17:30): cognitive re-orientation through repentance.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG04 (Gen 38:15 cha.shav): assessment from observation. VCG07 (2Cor 10:4 logismos): cognitive argumentation/strongholds. VCG03 (Gen 15:6): divine imputing/crediting. Processes: assessment, discrimination, imputation, argumentation.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG05 (Pro 16:9 cha.shav): inner cognitive devising. VCG02 (gnōmē, 1Cor 7:25): deliberate considered judgment. VCG06 (za.mam, Gen 11:6): unified purposing.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG01 (Luk 12:17 dialogizomai): interior monologue. VCG03 (Psa 119:97 si.ach): sustained meditative dwelling. VCG02 (Matt 1:20 enthumeomai): structured reflection.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4 noēma): formed cognitive content as target of spiritual warfare. VCG02 (Dan 4:5 ra.yon): spontaneous thought-content arising from inner states.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (1Cor 1:18): the logos disrupts and reorients cognitive categories. VCG03 (Gal 5:14): cognitive compression — the whole law held in one principle.

**[BOUNDARY]** E — Jude 9 (dialegō): deliberative reasoned disputation. Deliberative cognition is the mode of BOUNDARY's one active instance.

---

#### T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic operate at the level of understanding, reasoning, or some other cognitive function?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — M15-A operates at the integrative-orientational level: wisdom is cognition that has been directed, formed, and constituted — it is not raw reasoning but constituted inner orientation that produces consistent cognitive fruit.

**[B — Understanding]** E — M15-B operates at the grasping/perceiving level — the cognitive act of receiving and comprehending. It is the receptive-cognitive function.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — M15-C operates at the knowing-integrating level: recognition, relationship, and conviction are integrated — not analysis-only but recognition-through-relationship.

**[D — Discernment]** E — M15-D operates at the evaluative-cognitive level: forming assessments, making discriminations, reckoning weights.

**[E — Counsel]** E — M15-E operates at the deliberative-cognitive level: forming plans, resolving purposes, devising strategies. Prospective cognition.

**[F — Meditation]** E — M15-F operates at the dwelling-cognitive level: sustained recycling attention to held content.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — M15-G is the content-holding register: the storage and output level of cognition.

**[H — Logos]** E — M15-H operates at the transformative-cognitive level: the logos disrupts and reorients.

**[BOUNDARY]** E — Disputational-mediating cognition: reasoning toward resolution of contended claims.

---

#### T3.2.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the cognitive depth of this characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Depth: VCG07 (wisdom of this age) names the deepest cognitive orientation — the framework from which the person perceives everything. Wisdom is depth-constitutive.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Both surface and depth: surface (VCG08 grasping meaning) and depth (VCG06 Christ opening the mind — the deepest opening of understanding).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — M15-C spans surface (VCG10 practical recognition) to depth (VCG08 conviction through suffering, VCG01 divine comprehensive knowing). Strongly weighted toward depth.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Depth cognition dominates: VCG03 (divine crediting), VCG07 (strongholds requiring divine weaponry). Surface cognition in VCG04 and VCG05.

**[E — Counsel]** E — Depth: VCG03 (divine irresistible purpose), VCG06 (irrevocable human unified purpose). All three core VCGs involve deep-formed purposing.

**[F — Meditation]** E — Depth in VCG03 (Psa 119 — day-long dwelling that deepens love and understanding). Surface in VCG01 (self-deliberative inner monologue).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — Depth: VCG01 (noēma as strongholds — entrenched cognitive structures). VCG02 (alarming thoughts disturbing the whole person).

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01: depth cognitive transformation — the word of the cross reshapes the deepest cognitive frameworks.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Cognitive depth not applicable to BOUNDARY mechanisms.

### T3.3 — Memory

#### T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage memory — either as its basis, its product, or its object?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG09 (Pro 9:10 — "the beginning of wisdom is the fear of the LORD"): wisdom is built on the accumulated knowing of God's character and ways. Memory of who God is and what he has done is the basis of wisdom.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG06 (Luk 24:45 corpus): Christ enabling the disciples to understand what they had experienced and heard — memory of events and words now opened to comprehension.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG03 (Exo 7:5; Jos 4:24): redemptive knowing is built on memorial acts. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): knowledge as stored inner content ("lay up"). Memory is both basis and product of M15-C knowing.

**[D — Discernment]** S — No explicit memory engagement. Discernment may draw on accumulated knowledge but memory is not M15-D's named phenomenon.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG08 (Pro 6:6 pronoia): the ant provides a memory-pattern. Planning draws on accumulated wisdom-content (M15-A/C). Memory provides the stock from which purposes are formed.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03 (Psa 119:97): meditation is sustained return to received content — memory holds what meditation dwells upon. Meditation is memory-in-motion.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG02 (Dan 4:5): Nebuchadnezzar's dream-thoughts and troubled memory of them. VCG01 (noēmata as strongholds): entrenched long-term cognitive content.

**[H — Logos]** E — Col 3:16: "let the word of Christ dwell in you richly" — the logos held in memory and inner possession as its transforming medium.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Memory not evidenced in BOUNDARY's single active verse.

---

#### T3.3.2 — Is there evidence of the characteristic being stored, retrieved, or organised in the inner person?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG03 (Pro 10:14 cha.kham in wisdom corpus): wisdom accumulated and held — "the wise lay up knowledge" applies to wisdom as well as knowledge. Wisdom is stored as constituted character.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG05 (Heb 5:14): trained understanding held as a formed inner capacity — the result of accumulated practice stored as discernment-capacity.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG06 (Pro 10:14): "the wise lay up knowledge" explicitly describes organised inner holding of knowledge-content.

**[D — Discernment]** S — Stored discernment is inferential — the skilled discerner has trained capacity, but storage is not M15-D's named function.

**[E — Counsel]** S — No explicit evidence of counsel being stored.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03: the meditated content (God's law, his acts) is held in inner memory as the basis of sustained dwelling.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (noēmata as persistent strongholds): the thought-content is stored and entrenched — it does not dissipate but persists as structured inner content.

**[H — Logos]** E — Col 3:16: "dwell in you richly" — the logos is stored and accumulated in the inner person as a rich inner holding.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Not applicable.

---

#### T3.3.3 — Where the evidence is silent on memory engagement, note this explicitly.

**[D — Discernment]** S — Memory is silent in M15-D's direct evidence.

**[E — Counsel]** S — No verse in M15-E explicitly names memory as the mechanism of planning.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Memory is absent from BOUNDARY's evidence.

### T3.4 — Affect

#### T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage or produce affective states?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG04 (froneō, Rom 8:5-8): setting the mind on the flesh produces death; setting the mind on the Spirit produces life and peace. Wisdom-orientation produces affective outcomes (peace). VCG09 (Pro 9:10): "fear of the LORD" as the wisdom-foundation — fear as an affective orientation.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG06 (Luk 24:45): the disciples' understanding opened → joy and worship follow (Luk 24:52-53). Understanding produces affective response.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): pressing on to know the Lord carries desire-orientation. VCG05 (1Sa 2:12): absence of knowing the Lord is paired with destructive behaviour — lacking knowing produces destructive affect-expression.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG02 (1Cor 11:31): self-discernment has affective consequence (examined person avoids judgment). VCG05 (Job 12:11 ta.am): gustatory image has affective overtones.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG01 (Matt 1:19 boulomai): Joseph's resolution reflects compassion alongside purpose. VCG07 (Exo 7:23): Pharaoh's refusal to attend is affect-charged (hardness).

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03 (Psa 119:97): "Oh how I love your law!" — meditation is saturated with affective love. VCG04 (Dan 7:28): keeping the vision in heart despite fear — affect as the context of meditative holding.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG02 (Dan 4:5): alarming thoughts produce affective disturbance. VCG01 (noēma blinded): thought-content determines affective orientation.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (1Cor 1:18): the logos produces power-response (for the saved) or offence (for those perishing). VCG02 (Col 4:6): gracious speech as an affective discipline.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Affect not evidenced in BOUNDARY's single active verse.

---

#### T3.4.2 — Is this characteristic constitutively affective, or does it produce affect secondarily?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Wisdom produces affect secondarily: wisdom-as-character produces peace (via right orientation), but is not itself affective.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Understanding produces affect secondarily — understanding opened produces joy and worship (Luk 24:52-53).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Knowledge is primarily cognitive-relational; affect is its consequence.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Discernment is not constitutively affective; affect arises from the outcomes of discernment.

**[E — Counsel]** E — Purposive intent is primarily volitional-cognitive; affect provides colour to the purposing.

**[F — Meditation]** E — M15-F is constitutively affect-engaged at its richest (VCG03 — meditation deepens love). This is distinctive: M15-F is the sub-group where cognition and affect are most interwoven.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — M15-G is deeply affect-linked: thought-content produces or is produced by affective states (VCG02 — fear generates alarming thoughts; thoughts generate more fear). The relationship is bidirectional.

**[H — Logos]** E — M15-H is not constitutively affective, but its reception and expression carry affective loading.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Not applicable.

---

#### T3.4.3 — Where the evidence is silent on affective engagement, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG08 (settled conviction), VCG10 (practical knowing), VCG07 (moral knowing) do not speak explicitly to affect.

**[D — Discernment]** S — VCG01, VCG03, VCG06, VCG07 are silent on affective engagement.

**[E — Counsel]** S — VCG03, VCG05, VCG06, VCG08 are silent on affective engagement.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — VCG03 (knowledge placed as divine gift) is affectively neutral at point of placement.

### T3.5 — Creativity

#### T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage or constitute the creative capacity?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG10 (M15-A inner creative design): "all wise-hearted men in whom the LORD had put wisdom and understanding to know how to do" (Exo 36:1) — wisdom as inner creative capacity for sacred construction. The one sub-group in M15 where creative design is explicitly named.

**[B — Understanding]** S — Understanding as perceptive faculty does not directly constitute creative capacity.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — No direct evidence. Divine creative knowing (Pro 3:20) names God's creative act, not human creativity.

**[D — Discernment]** S — No creative capacity engagement evidenced in M15-D.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG05 (Pro 16:9 cha.shav devising): the heart devising its way is the inner creative-planning act. M15-E is the closest the cluster comes to inner creativity in its planning/devising mode.

**[F — Meditation]** S — M15-F is receptive-dwelling, not creative producing.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — M15-G does not directly engage creative capacity.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG02 (Col 4:6): gracious speech requires creative shaping — finding the right form for each person and context.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Not applicable.

---

#### T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity in the person?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG10 (Exo 36:1 — craftsmen wisdom-endowed for sacred construction): wisdom enables and deepens creativity — the Spirit-given wisdom directly produces craft skill and creative design capacity. Wisdom is the inner precondition of the creative act in this evidence.

**[B — Understanding]** S — Understanding does not directly enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity in M15-B's evidence. Understanding grasps what is; creativity produces what is not yet. Silence confirmed.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — Knowledge does not directly engage creativity in M15-C's evidence. Silence confirmed.

**[D — Discernment]** S — Discernment evaluates rather than creates. Silence confirmed.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — VCG05 (Pro 16:9 cha.shav — the heart devising its way): the devising function of purposive intent is the closest M15-E comes to enabling creativity — the inner cognitive planning act that conceives something not yet in existence. M15-E enables a form of generative inner purposing.

**[F — Meditation]** S — M15-F is receptive-dwelling, not generative. Silence confirmed.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — Thought-content is the product of cognitive activity, not the creative act itself. Silence confirmed.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG02 (Col 4:6 — gracious speech seasoned with salt): the communicated word requires creative shaping — finding the right form for each person and context is a creative act. M15-H enables communicative creativity.

---

#### T3.5.3 — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with creativity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Wisdom's direct engagement with creativity (VCG10 Exo 36:1) reveals it as the most broadly enabling of M15's characteristics — it is the only sub-group where creative capacity is explicitly constituted by the characteristic's reception. Wisdom is integrative: it includes creative, governance, communicative, and moral dimensions.

**[B — Understanding]** S — Understanding's silence on creativity reveals it as a receptive-perceptive faculty, not a generative one. Understanding grasps what exists; it does not produce what does not yet exist. The silence is definitional, not incidental.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — Knowledge's silence on creativity confirms it as a content-characteristic (what is known/held) rather than a production-characteristic. The silence reveals M15-C's constitutively receptive character.

**[D — Discernment]** S — Discernment's silence on creativity reveals it as evaluative rather than generative. The discriminating perceptive act assesses what is presented; it does not produce new content.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — M15-E's partial engagement with creativity (through the devising function of cha.shav) reveals purposive intent as the sub-group where inner life moves closest to creative generation — conceiving what does not yet exist in order to direct action toward it.

**[F — Meditation]** S — M15-F's silence on creativity reveals meditation as a receptive-sustaining activity. The meditative person dwells on what has been given; they do not generate new content in the meditative act.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — M15-G's silence on creativity reveals thought-content as a product-holding characteristic, not a generating one. Creativity may produce thought-content; M15-G names what is held, not the generating act.

**[H — Logos]** E — M15-H's engagement with creative shaping (gracious speech requiring creative form) reveals it as the characteristic where creativity most naturally appears in the expression direction. The logos must be shaped creatively for each communicative context.

### T3.6 — Volition

#### T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional capacity?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG04 (froneō, Rom 8:5-8): setting the mind on the Spirit vs the flesh — the wisdom-orientation requires volitional commitment. VCG06 (self-attributed wisdom): the volitional stance of claiming wisdom as a personal possession.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG05 (Heb 5:14): trained understanding requires volitional discipline in practice. VCG06 (Luk 24:45): opening of understanding is divine initiative, but the disciples' continued engagement with Scripture is volitional.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3 "let us press on"): volitional pursuit. VCG05 (1Sa 2:12 — not knowing as chosen condition): volitional refusal. VCG06 (Pro 10:14 — "lay up"): volitional accumulation.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG01 (Isa 52:13): committed discerning-action. VCG02 (1Cor 11:31 "if we judged ourselves"): volitional self-examination.

**[E — Counsel]** E — M15-E is the most volitionally characterised sub-group. VCG01 (boulomai): will/deliberate intention. VCG05: the heart planning ITS way. VCG06 (za.mam): unified volitional purposing.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03 (Psa 119:97 "all the day"): volitional commitment to sustained return. VCG04 (Dan 7:28): volitional holding of the vision in heart.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 10:5): "take every thought captive" — volitional management of thought-content required.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (Col 3:16 "let the word dwell"): volitional receiving and holding. VCG02 (Col 4:6 "let your speech be"): volitional discipline in expression.

**[BOUNDARY]** E — Jude 9: Michael's restraint is a deliberate volitional act of submission to divine authority.

---

#### T3.6.2 — Is this characteristic volitionally received, maintained, or refused?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — All three: volitionally received (the person who asks — Jas 1:5), maintained (the wise person who accumulates and guards), refused (the person who rejects wisdom's call — Pro 1:24-33).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Volitionally received (Heb 5:14 training); maintained (through sustained practice); and blocked (hardened hearts — M15-B VCG04 judicial withholding, which presupposes prior volitional hardening).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — All three: volitionally received (VCG04 pursuit), maintained (VCG06 "lay up"), refused (VCG05 chosen not-knowing).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Volitionally exercised (VCG02 self-examination), sustained (VCG01 pattern of sa.khal), and blocked (VCG06 cunning misdirected — a.rum when directed wrongly).

**[E — Counsel]** E — Volitionally formed (VCG05 heart plans), maintained (VCG02 considered judgment held), and refused (VCG04 — counsel rejected, as when David ignored Ahithophel later through Hushai's counter-counsel).

**[F — Meditation]** E — Volitionally sustained (VCG03 "all the day"). VCG01 deliberative self-monologue is partly involuntary (anxiety-driven).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — Volitional capture required (VCG01 2Cor 10:5); VCG02 dream-thoughts arising non-volitionally.

**[H — Logos]** E — Volitionally received (VCG01 "let it dwell") and volitionally expressed (VCG02 "let your speech be").

---

#### T3.6.3 — Where the evidence is silent on volition, note this explicitly.

**[B — Understanding]** S — VCG01 (God's divine understanding) is silent on human volition.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 and VCG02 (divine knowing) are silent on human volition.

**[D — Discernment]** S — VCG05 (tasting-as-discernment) is more reflexive than volitional.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG08 (pronoia — foresight) is more cognitive than volitional.

### T3.7 — Agency

#### T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic confer agency?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG01 (Pro 16:23): wisdom guides the mouth — communicative agency. VCG03 (1Ki 3:9): governance agency. VCG10 (Exo 36:1): creative-constructive agency.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG06 (Luk 24:45 → 24:46-49): understanding opened → the disciples are sent out. Understanding confers mission agency.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (Job 19:25): conviction enables sustained advocacy through suffering. VCG04: knowing God orients the whole life. VCG10: practical knowing enables correct action.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG01 (Isa 52:13): discernment enabling exaltation. VCG07 (2Cor 10:4): demolishing strongholds — spiritual warfare agency.

**[E — Counsel]** E — M15-E is pre-eminently an agency sub-group. VCG03: divine agency expressed through irresistible purpose. VCG05 (Pro 16:9): human heart planning produces action.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03 (Psa 119:99): meditation produces understanding that exceeds teachers — cognitive-practical agency. VCG01 (Luk 12:17 inverse): self-enclosed deliberation produces defective agency (the rich fool).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 10:5): taking thoughts captive is spiritual warfare agency. VCG03 (2Chr 1:10): God-given knowledge enables governance agency.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG02 (Col 4:6): gracious speech as communicative agency. VCG01 (1Cor 14:19): intelligible speech that reaches the inner person.

**[BOUNDARY]** E — Jude 9: Michael's authority-restrained response models appropriate agency under divine authority.

---

#### T3.7.2 — What kind of agency does this characteristic enable?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Governance agency (VCG03), communicative agency (VCG01), creative-constructive agency (VCG10), life-directional agency (VCG04).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Mission agency (Luk 24), communicative-teaching agency (Ne 8:8), governance agency (1Ki 3:9).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Advocacy agency through suffering (VCG08), covenantal life-orientation agency (VCG04), practical navigation agency (VCG10).

**[H — Logos]** E — The logos deepens conscientiousness in reception (Col 3:16 "let it dwell richly" — volitional, sustained, open receiving) and expression (Col 4:6 "always gracious" — disciplined, caring speech). M15-H's conscientiousness is the word carefully received and carefully given.
