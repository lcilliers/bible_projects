### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

#### T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Wisdom is explicitly and repeatedly attributed to God as an essential, not merely distributed, attribute. VCG01 (Dan 2:20 anchor): "to whom belong wisdom and might" — wisdom as divine possession. Rev 7:12 ascribes wisdom to God in doxology. Rom 11:33 names "the wisdom of God" as depth beyond human comprehension. The evidence reveals: wisdom characterises what God IS, not merely what he gives. The human characteristic therefore images a constitutive divine attribute.

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — Isa 40:28 (VCG01 anchor): "his understanding is unsearchable" — understanding is named as God's own attribute, self-sufficient, uncreated. Pro 3:19: "by understanding he established the heavens." The divine understanding is the ground of creation and governance — the human perceptive faculty images a capacity that underlies all reality.

**[C — Knowledge as inner content and covenantal knowing]**
E — Psa 139:6 (VCG01 anchor): "such knowledge is too wonderful for me; it is high; I cannot attain it" — God's knowledge of the human person is comprehensive, intimate, and unsurpassable. Rom 11:33: "the depth of the riches and wisdom and knowledge of God" — knowledge is named alongside wisdom as a divine depth. The character of God reflected: omniscient knowing that penetrates all inner-being reality.

**[D — Discernment and practical judgment]**
E — Isa 52:13 (VCG01 anchor, H7919A sa.khal): the Servant "shall act wisely" — the divine pattern of prudent-effective action is reflected in the discernment that enables right judgment. The discernment characteristic images God's capacity to act with precision and effectiveness.

**[E — Deliberative planning, counsel, and purposive intent]**
E — Isa 14:24 (VCG03 anchor): "as I have planned, so shall it be; as I have purposed, so shall it stand" — God's counsel is named as the irresistible expression of his sovereign will. The divine counsel/purposing images in the human person: the inner capacity for purposive forward-orientation. God's planning nature is what the human capacity for counsel and deliberate intent reflects.

**[F — Meditative and reflective inner activity]**
S — The meditative-reflective inner activity of M15-F is not explicitly attributed to God in the sub-group's verse evidence. God declares what is in the creature's thoughts (Amos 4:13, VCG04 context) — but this is knowledge of thoughts, not the meditative activity itself. Silence on God's own meditative activity.

**[G — Inner thought-content]**
E — Psa 139:17 (VCG02 anchor, H7454 re.a): "how precious to me are your thoughts, O God!" — God has formed thoughts (re.a) that are directed toward the human person. The thought-content characteristic images the divine capacity for formed, directed inner thoughts.

**[H — Logos: the word as inner-being engagement]**
E — Col 3:16 (VCG01 anchor): "the word of Christ dwell in you richly" — the logos is the divine word that enters and dwells in the inner person. 1Cor 1:24: Christ as "the wisdom of God" — logos is identified with the divine wisdom itself. The characteristic images God's own communicative-revelatory nature.

**[CLUSTER — all sub-groups]**
E — Across all sub-groups, there is a consistent pattern: each characteristic has an explicit divine version (God's wisdom, God's understanding, God's knowledge, God's counsel, God's thoughts, God's word) that precedes and grounds the human version. This is a cluster-level finding: M15 is not primarily a cluster of human capacities that happened to be attributed to God — it is a cluster of divine attributes that the human person is created to image and participate in. Dan 2:21: "he gives wisdom to the wise and knowledge to those who have understanding" — the giving structure assumes prior divine possession. The cluster's governing narrative is: divine attribute → human imaging.

#### T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Yes. Dan 2:20 (VCG01): "to whom belong wisdom and might." 1Cor 1:24: "Christ the wisdom of God." Rom 16:27: "the only wise God." Explicit and repeated.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Yes. Isa 40:28 (VCG01): "his understanding is unsearchable." Pro 3:19: "by understanding he established the heavens." Job 38:36 (VCG02 anchor): "who put understanding in the mind?" — the rhetorical question implies God's exclusive ownership.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Yes. Psa 139:6 (VCG01 anchor): God's knowledge of the person is "too wonderful." Rom 11:33: "wisdom and knowledge of God." God's knowing is everywhere attributed, comprehensively and without exception.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Isa 52:13 (VCG01 anchor): the Servant's wise action is an expression of divine pattern. The prudence/discernment characteristic is attributed to divine action in multiple prophetic texts.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Yes. Isa 14:24 (VCG03 anchor): God's plan, God's purpose — explicitly named as irresistible divine counsel. Jer 29:11 (VCG05 anchor): "plans to prosper you" — God's deliberate purposive intent toward his people.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** S — Not explicitly attributed to God in M15-F's verses. Silence.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Psa 139:17 (VCG02 anchor): God's thoughts (re.a) are directed toward the human person — explicitly attributed. God has formed inner thought-content directed at his creatures.

**[H — Logos]** E — Yes. John's prologue tradition (proximate to VCG01 corpus): the logos is divine in origin. 1Cor 1:24: the logos/wisdom of God is Christ himself. The word is explicitly of divine origin and character.

#### T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?

**[A, B, C, D, E, G, H]** Not applicable — Scripture is vocal on each of these. See T0.1.2.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]**
S — The silence is significant: meditative and reflective activity as an inner process appears to be a characteristically creaturely operation — responding to what has been received, pondering, turning over in the mind. The creature meditates on God's precepts (Psa 119:97 — VCG03 anchor); God does not meditate on human thoughts. The silence suggests M15-F is a creaturely capacity, not a divine attribute mirrored in the human. This is a finding: M15-F may be the one sub-group in this cluster that is specifically creaturely rather than image-of-God.

**[CLUSTER]**
E — The near-universal attribution of M15 characteristics to God (with M15-F as the one exception) is itself a finding: this cluster is overwhelmingly characterised by the divine-image pattern. The single exception (M15-F: meditative activity) may mark the creaturely-responsive mode as distinct from the divine-image mode within the cluster.

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

#### T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?

**[A — Wisdom]**
E — Pro 16:23 (VCG03 anchor): wisdom governs speech and conduct — the purpose is right ordering of the person's whole expression. VCG09 (Psa 111:10 anchor): wisdom constitutes covenantal fidelity — the purpose is right relationship with God. The dual purpose: inner-ordering and covenantal faithfulness.

**[B — Understanding]**
E — 1Ki 3:9 (VCG03 anchor): "to govern your people, to distinguish between right and wrong" — understanding equips for governance and moral discernment. Heb 5:14 (VCG05 anchor): training for distinguishing good from evil. The purpose: equipping for governance, moral navigation, and reception of disclosure.

**[C — Knowledge]**
E — Hos 6:3 (VCG04 anchor): "let us press on to know the LORD" — covenantal knowledge is the animating purpose of the person's relational knowing. Pro 1:7 (VCG06 anchor): "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" — knowledge serves the covenantal orientation. Hos 4:6 (VCG05 anchor): lack of knowledge destroys the people. The purpose: covenantal relationship with God and equipping for right life.

**[D — Discernment]**
E — Isa 52:13 (VCG01 anchor): sa.khal enables wise-effective action. 1Cor 11:31 (VCG02 anchor): diakrinō enables self-examination that avoids judgment. Gen 15:6 (VCG03 anchor): cha.shav in the divine crediting of faith — discernment serves both human self-knowledge and divine-human relational clarity. The purpose: enabling wise, effective, morally navigating action.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]**
E — 2Sa 16:23 (VCG04 anchor): the counselor is like consulting God — human counsel in governance serves divine-alignment in community decision-making. Jer 29:11 (VCG05 anchor): God's purposive intent is toward human flourishing — the human capacity for deliberate planning images this. The purpose: wise governance, aligned decision-making, purposive living.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]**
E — Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor): "oh how I love your law! It is my meditation all the day" — meditation serves the formation of love for God's word, which constitutes the inner person's orientation. Luk 5:22 (VCG01 anchor): deliberative reasoning serves decision and evaluation. The purpose: inner formation through engagement with received content.

**[G — Inner thought-content]**
E — 2Cor 4:4 (VCG01 anchor): Satan blinds the minds of unbelievers — the thought-content capacity is a site of both flourishing and attack. Psa 139:17 (VCG02 anchor): God's thoughts toward the person are precious — the capacity for formed thought enables receipt of and response to divine-directed thought. The purpose: holding formed inner content that orients the person.

**[H — Logos]**
E — Col 3:16 (VCG01 anchor): the word of Christ dwelling richly produces teaching, wisdom, and worship. 1Cor 14:19 (VCG02 anchor): intelligible speech instructs others. The purpose: the word in its inner-engaging mode equips the person for community formation, worship, and moral accountability.

#### T0.2.2 — Is this characteristic part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Both. VCG09 (Psa 111:10): wisdom rooted in fear of the Lord is the created design. VCG07 (1Cor 1:21 anchor): the worldly wisdom orientation exists as a fallen-condition alternative. The characteristic is primordially designed; the fallen condition creates counterfeit and distorted versions.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Both. VCG02 (Job 38:36): God placed understanding in the person — original design. VCG04 (Isa 6:10 anchor): understanding can be closed as judicial response to hardness — a fallen-condition dynamic. VCG05 (Heb 5:14): formation through practice implies development needed in the creaturely condition.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both. VCG07 (Gen 2:17 anchor): the knowledge of good and evil is specifically associated with the fall — it is acquired through transgression. VCG06 (Pro 1:7 anchor): knowledge as inner endowment is part of created design. VCG12 (Hab 2:14 anchor): universal knowledge of the LORD as eschatological destiny — the created design has a future completion.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Both. VCG06 (Gen 3:1 anchor): the shrewdness quality (a.rum) is present in the serpent — the characteristic exists before the fall but can serve either righteousness or cunning. VCG02 (1Cor 11:31): self-examination discernment is a response to the fallen condition (judging oneself to avoid judgment).

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Both. VCG05 (Jer 29:11): God's purposive planning is primordial and covenantal — the human imaging is designed. VCG06 (Lam 2:17 anchor): irrevocable purposed intent in judgment context — the characteristic exists in fallen-condition operations.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Both. VCG03 (Psa 119:97): meditation on God's law is a created-design activity. VCG01 (Luk 5:22): deliberative reasoning can produce corrupt thoughts (evil reasoning within the Pharisees' hearts) — a fallen-condition expression of the same capacity.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Both. VCG01 (2Cor 4:4): the capacity for thought-content is original design; its blindness is a fallen-condition condition. VCG02 (Psa 139:17): the capacity to hold precious thought-content is designed.

**[H — Logos]** E — Both. Col 3:16 (VCG01): the word dwelling richly is new-covenant restorative — it addresses the fallen condition. But the capacity to receive, hold, and be governed by the word is creaturely design.

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

#### T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called, and what does the name signal?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — H2450 cha.kham (wise) / H2452 chokh.mah (wisdom): root ch-kh-m — skilled, experienced, constituted in discernment. G4678 sofia: skilled in high sense. The name signals: constituted character quality, not mere information. Pro 16:23 (VCG03 anchor).

**[B — Understanding]** E — H0995 bin / H8394 te.vu.nah: root b-y-n — to separate, distinguish, perceive. G4920 suniēmi / G4907 sunesis: to bring together into comprehension. The name signals: active perceptive-separating-gathering — understanding is the act of discerning distinctions and unifying them into grasp. Isa 40:28 (VCG01 anchor).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — H3045 ya.da (to know): the broadest Hebrew knowing word — spans from experiential/relational knowing to factual knowing to sexual knowing (VCG11). H1847 da.at (knowledge): the inner content of knowing. G6063 oida: to have seen and therefore know — a knowing that comes from having been present. The name signals: knowledge is fundamentally relational-experiential, not merely cognitive storage. Hos 6:3 (VCG04 anchor), Pro 1:7 (VCG06 anchor).

**[D — Discernment]** E — H7919A sa.khal (be prudent): to have practical inner sense that produces effective action. G1252 diakrinō: to judge/discriminate/doubt — the inner act of making evaluative distinctions. H6175 a.rum (prudent): sharp-eyed inner perceptiveness that can serve wisdom or cunning (directionally neutral). The name signals: discernment is applied perception — the inner act of making right distinctions in concrete situations. Gen 3:1 (VCG06 anchor), 1Cor 11:31 (VCG02 anchor).

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — H3289 ya.ats (to advise): the act of giving or seeking counsel. G1014 boulomai: purposive willing — the inner volitional intent. H2803I cha.shav (to devise): purposive devising in the heart. G4307 pronoia: foresight — deliberate forward-looking intent. The name signals: counsel and purposing are forward-oriented inner activities — the person reaches forward in intention before acting. 2Sa 16:23 (VCG04 anchor), Jer 29:11 (VCG05 anchor).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — H7878 si.ach / H7881 si.chah (to muse / meditation): the inner activity of turning something over in the mind, dwelling on it. G1260 dialogizomai: to reason within oneself — interior deliberative reasoning. H7920 se.khal: to contemplate with focused attention. The name signals: reflective activity is a sustained inner movement — not static holding but active dwelling. Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor), Luk 5:22 (VCG01 anchor).

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — G3540 noēma (mind/thought): the formed content of the mind — thought as an object, not an activity. H7454 re.a / H7476 ra.yon: formed thoughts as inner objects. The name signals: thought-content is static compared to reflective activity (M15-F) — it is what the mind holds, not what the mind does. 2Cor 4:4 (VCG01 anchor), Psa 139:17 (VCG02 anchor).

**[H — Logos]** E — G3056 logos: the word — in its inner-engaging mode. The word that addresses the inner person: divine word (VCG01), human speech (VCG02), moral principle (VCG03). The name signals: the logos as an inner-being phenomenon is the word received into and operating on the person's inner life. Col 3:16 (VCG01 anchor).

#### T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Cha.kham (adjective → adjectival noun) reveals wisdom as character-state. Chokh.mah (noun) reveals it as a substantive inner reality. Sofizo (causative verb) reveals it as something another can produce in a person. The terms collectively show: wisdom is a constituted quality held in the heart that governs the whole person's operation. Pro 16:23 (VCG03 anchor).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Bin (verb) reveals it as an active perceptive act. Te.vu.nah (noun) reveals it as the faculty that performs the act. Sunesis (noun) reveals the comprehension produced. Suniēmi (verb) reveals the act of bringing together. The terms show: understanding is both a faculty (te.vu.nah, sunesis) and an exercise of that faculty (bin, suniēmi). 1Ki 3:9 (VCG03 anchor).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Ya.da (verb: to know in the full experiential-relational sense). Da.at (noun: the content of knowing). Oida (verb: to know from having been present or witnessed). Man.da / de.ah / de.a (various nouns for knowledge-content). The terms show knowledge as inherently relational and experiential — not an abstract repository but a living relational state. Gen 4:1 (VCG11 anchor) and Hos 6:3 (VCG04 anchor) reveal the range.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Sa.khal (verb): practical inner sense → effective action. Diakrinō (verb): to judge/separate/doubt. A.rum (adjective): sharp perceptiveness — directionally neutral. Ta.am (verb): to taste — the palate as figure for inner discernment (Psa 34:8, VCG05 anchor). The terms reveal: discernment operates through both analytic distinction (diakrinō) and intuitive-perceptive sensitivity (ta.am, a.rum). 1Cor 11:31 (VCG02 anchor).

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Ya.ats (verb/noun): to counsel / the counselor. Boulomai (verb): to will, to purpose. Gnōmē (noun): considered resolution — the formed position. Pronoia (noun): foresight — forward-seeing purposive intent. The terms reveal: purposive intent spans from advice-giving (social) to inner volitional will (personal) to irresistible divine purpose (VCG03 context). Isa 14:24 (VCG03 anchor).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Si.ach / si.chah (verb/noun): to muse, to meditate, to engage in sustained inner conversation with received material. Dialogizomai (verb): to reason within oneself — interior dialogue. The terms reveal: reflective activity is the inner self in sustained engagement with something beyond itself — it is inherently responsive. Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor).

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Noēma (Greek noun): the mind/thought as an object that can be blinded, captured, or guarded (2Cor 4:4, VCG01 anchor). Re.a / ra.yon (Hebrew nouns): formed thoughts as inner objects. The terms reveal: thought-content is an inner object, not a process — it can be acted upon from outside (blinded by Satan, 2Cor 4:4) or from within (guarded).

**[H — Logos]** E — Logos (Greek noun): word/speech/reason/account. In M15-H's inner-being mode: the word as address to and content for the inner person. 1Cor 14:19 (VCG02 anchor): "five words with my mind" — the logos here is the unit of inner-reaching communication.

#### T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Constitutional (the wise person IS); directional (toward God, VCG09; toward worldly ends, VCG07); relational (wisdom given by God to the asker, Jam 1:5 VCG02 anchor).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Receptive direction built in (the perceptive faculty receives what is given); relational (understanding given by God, Job 38:36 VCG02 anchor; opened by Christ, Luk 24:45 VCG06 anchor).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Constitutional and directional: a.rum is neutral in direction (Gen 3:1: serpent; Prov: prudent person) — the inner quality is constitutional but its direction is morally determined by the object toward which it is applied.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Responsive direction: the meditating person turns toward received material (si.ach on God's precepts, Psa 119:97). The direction is inward-and-toward-received-content.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — The thought-content is static but directional in its object: God's thoughts (re.a) are directed toward the human person (Psa 139:17); Satan's designs (noēmata) are directed against (2Cor 4:4 VCG01 anchor).

**[H — Logos]** E — Bidirectional: the divine word is addressed TO the inner person (VCG01); the human speech (VCG02) is addressed outward FROM the inner person. The logos name carries both receiving and expressing directions.

### T1.2 — Kind

#### T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — A constituted disposition functioning as orientation, quality, and operative capacity simultaneously. Pro 16:23 (VCG03 anchor): not an act performed but a quality exercised from the heart's constitution.

**[B — Understanding]** E — A perceptive faculty — the inner capacity to receive, distinguish, and grasp. Heb 5:14 (VCG05 anchor): formable; Isa 6:10 (VCG04 anchor): blockable. A dynamic capacity, not a static holding.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — A relational-covenantal state — knowing as a living relational orientation, not merely an information-holding. Hos 6:3 (VCG04 anchor): pressing to know the LORD is a relational posture. Pro 1:7 (VCG06 anchor): knowledge as a valued inner endowment accumulated and transmitted.

**[D — Discernment]** E — An applied perceptive act — the inner act of making right evaluative distinctions in concrete situations. 1Cor 11:31 (VCG02 anchor): self-judgment as a concrete application. Gen 15:6 (VCG03 anchor): divine crediting as evaluation.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — A forward-oriented volitional activity — purposing, planning, willing toward a future state. Jer 29:11 (VCG05 anchor): deliberate purposive devising in the heart.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — A sustained inner activity — the ongoing engagement of the mind with received content. Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor): meditation as continuous inner activity ("all the day").

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Formed inner objects — the mind's content as objects that can be held, captured, blinded, or disclosed. 2Cor 4:4 (VCG01 anchor): noēmata as objects that Satan blinds.

**[H — Logos]** E — A received inner content / an expressed inner quality — bidirectional. Col 3:16 (VCG01 anchor): the word dwelling in the person as inner-constituting content; 1Cor 14:19 (VCG02 anchor): the word as the expression of the mind outward.

**[BOUNDARY — structural characterisation note — T1.2.1]**
E — BOUNDARY terms in M15 serve the cluster in the following structural roles:
- **Functional-mediating roles:** G1328 diermēneutēs (interpreter), G2058 hermēneia (interpretation), G3177 methermēneuō (to mean), H6592 pe.sher (interpretation) — these name the process of transferring meaning across language or revelation boundaries. They are not inner-being characteristics but the mechanisms by which inner-being capacities (understanding, knowledge) are made accessible across relational/linguistic gaps.
- **Formative activity:** G1128 gumnazō (to train) — the process of forming the understanding faculty (M15-B/Heb 5:14 is its key context) — not the characteristic itself but its formation mechanism.
- **Speech acts and assertion:** G1226 diabebaioō (to insist) — the speech act of asserting with certainty; the inner-being relevance is the conviction that produces the insisting, not the insisting itself.
- **Comparator:** G1503 eikō (to resemble) — functional comparator; inner-being relevance is peripheral.
- **Inner-awareness function:** G4894 suneidō (be aware) — knowing by having witnessed/been present; sits at the boundary of M15-B and M15-C.
- **Disputational mediation:** G1256 dialegō (to dispute) — in Jude 9 (VCG01 anchor, M15-BOUNDARY-VCG01): Michael contending with the devil in disputation about Moses' body. The inner-being phenomenon here is the capacity for reasoned engagement at the spiritual-beings boundary — the dispute form taken by contested spiritual encounter. Unique evidence: the BOUNDARY sub-group's one active VCG records the use of this characteristic in angel-adversary interaction.
- **Governance-arbitration:** G3312 meristēs (arbiter) — the role of settling disputes; relevant to relational-governance contexts.
- **Teaching-activity:** H8637 tir.gal (to teach) — the activity of forming inner-being capacities in another through instruction.

BOUNDARY terms collectively characterise the *functional and relational infrastructure* through which M15's inner-being characteristics (wisdom, understanding, knowledge) are mediated, formed, transferred, and activated in persons and communities. They are not characteristics themselves but the mechanisms that serve them.

#### T1.2.3 — Current best working description of this characteristic

**[A — Wisdom]** E — A divinely-endowed constituted quality of the whole person, grounded in covenantal orientation toward God, expressed in the governance of mind, speech, and action, and distinguished from self-attributed or world-oriented substitutes. Primary constitutional location: heart and mind. Pro 16:23, Jam 1:5, Rom 8:5.

**[B — Understanding]** E — The inner capacity to receive, distinguish, and grasp what is disclosed — divine revelation, situation, meaning. Constituted in the person by divine endowment, formed through practice, exercised in receiving disclosed content, and capable of blockage. Primary location: heart (1Ki 3:9) and mind (Luk 24:45). Isa 40:28, 1Ki 3:9, Luk 24:45.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — The relational-covenantal inner state of knowing — spanning from God's comprehensive knowing of all things (VCG01) through the covenant-defining mutual knowing of God and people (VCG02/VCG04) to the content-based knowing of facts, persons, and skills (VCG10). Its defining mode is relational-experiential (ya.da), not merely cognitive storage. Its eschatological destiny is universal knowledge of the LORD (VCG12). Hos 6:3, Pro 1:7, Hab 2:14.

**[D — Discernment]** E — The applied inner perceptive act of making right evaluative distinctions in concrete situations — prudent assessment, moral discrimination, crediting, deliberative evaluation. Directionally neutral in some of its terms (a.rum serves wisdom or cunning; cha.shav devises for good or ill). Primary location: heart/inner reasoning. Isa 52:13, 1Cor 11:31, Gen 15:6.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — The inner capacity for forward-oriented purposive willing — forming deliberate intent, devising plans, giving and receiving counsel, making irrevocable decisions. Spans from the human counselor's wisdom (2Sa 16:23) to God's irresistible sovereign purpose (Isa 14:24). Primary constitutional location: the heart (Pro 16:9 proximate: "the heart plans its way"). 2Sa 16:23, Jer 29:11, Isa 14:24.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — The sustained inner activity of dwelling on received content — turning over God's precepts, pondering visions, reasoning within oneself about situations. Activity-oriented (distinct from static content). The person is actively engaged in an inner dialogue with something beyond themselves. Primary location: the heart (inner reasoning, Luk 5:22 VCG01 anchor) and the engaged mind (Psa 119:97 VCG03 anchor). Psa 119:97, Luk 5:22.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — The formed thoughts held within the mind — inner content as objects that can be captured, blinded, or disclosed. Static (distinct from the activity of M15-F). Satan can blind noēmata (2Cor 4:4 VCG01 anchor); God can know the inner thoughts of the heart; the person holds thought-content directed toward God (Psa 139:17 VCG02 anchor). 2Cor 4:4, Psa 139:17.

**[H — Logos]** E — The word as inner-being engagement — the divine or apostolic word that addresses, constitutes, and evaluates the inner person; the human speech that expresses the inner quality outward; the comprehensive moral principle held as inner standard. The logos characteristic spans receiving (divine word entering) and expressing (human speech proceeding from inner quality). Col 3:16, 1Cor 14:19, Gal 5:14.

### T1.3 — Boundary

#### T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — G0781 asofos (unwise); VCG06 (self-attributed wisdom, Pro 26:12 anchor); VCG07 (worldly wisdom, 1Cor 1:21 anchor). Three types of opposite: absence, counterfeit, and inversion.

**[B — Understanding]** E — G0801 asunetos (senseless/without understanding); VCG04 blockage pattern (Isa 6:10 anchor: the closed inner faculty).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG05 (Hos 4:6 anchor: not-knowing as destruction of the people); VCG09 (Rom 10:3 anchor: culpable ignorance). G0050 agnoeō (to be ignorant) is the lexical opposite.

**[D — Discernment]** E — The undiscerning/senseless state; VCG04's pattern of wrong assessment (Psa 73:16 anchor: "when I thought how to understand this, it seemed to me a wearisome task"); VCG06's direction-inverted shrewdness (Gen 3:1: the serpent).

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — The person without inner purpose; the refusal of counsel (VCG03 corpus: nations who refuse God's counsel); VCG07 (Exo 7:23 anchor: Pharaoh "not setting his heart" — the opposite of heart-directed purposive attention).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — VCG01 (Luk 5:22 corpus): evil reasoning within the heart — the reflective capacity turned toward self-justification rather than formation. Not the absence of reflection but its corruption.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4 anchor): blinded minds — thought-content capacity sealed from light. The opposite is blindness/captivity of thought-content.

**[H — Logos]** E — Eph 5:6 (VCG02 corpus): "empty words" — the structural opposite of logos as inner-being engagement is the speech that carries no inner reality, that deceives rather than constitutes.

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Self-sufficient wisdom (VCG06); worldly orientation (VCG07: 1Cor 1:21); unguarded speech and unrestrained impulse (VCG03 corpus).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Hardness of heart (VCG04: Isa 6:10); spiritual dullness; inability to grasp disclosed meaning.

#### T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Wisdom (M15-A) is not: mere cognition (M15-B/C boundary); self-generated achievement (VCG06 counterfeit); worldly prudence (VCG07). It ends where mere information-holding (M15-C) begins and where mere perceptive act (M15-B) begins.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Understanding (M15-B) is not: constituted wisdom-character (M15-A); held knowledge (M15-C); evaluative discernment (M15-D). It ends where its perceptive act produces its results — those results then belong to M15-C (knowledge) or serve M15-A (wisdom).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Knowledge (M15-C) is not: the perceptive faculty that grasps it (M15-B); the wisdom-character it serves (M15-A); the discernment that applies it (M15-D). Knowledge is the content held, not the faculty (M15-B) or the application (M15-D).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Discernment (M15-D) is not: the constituted wisdom-character (M15-A) it expresses; the perceptive faculty (M15-B) that feeds it; the knowledge content (M15-C) it applies. Discernment is the applied act of making right distinctions in situations.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Counsel and purposing (M15-E) is not: mere knowing (M15-C); constituted wisdom (M15-A); discernment as situational evaluation (M15-D). It is the forward-reaching volitional act that produces plan, decision, or counsel.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Meditative activity (M15-F) is not: the knowledge content it dwells on (M15-C); the thought-content it produces (M15-G); the wisdom-character it forms over time (M15-A). It is the process of sustained inner engagement, not the content or the character-result.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Inner thought-content (M15-G) is not: the reflective activity (M15-F) that forms it; the understanding-faculty (M15-B) that grasps it; the counsel-activity (M15-E) that uses it. It is the static formed content, not the process or the using.

**[H — Logos]** E — Logos as inner-being engagement (M15-H) is not: wisdom as constituted character (M15-A); the understanding faculty that receives it (M15-B); the knowledge content it conveys (M15-C). It is the word in its address-and-constituting function — the mode in which the word engages and forms the inner person.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

#### T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Pro 16:23 (VCG03 corpus): judicious speech, restraint of impulse, teachability. Psa 111:10 (VCG09 anchor): covenantal fidelity.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Heb 5:14 (VCG05 anchor): habitual discernment faculty, mature inner capacity for moral navigation.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Hos 6:3 (VCG04 anchor) corpus: deepening covenantal relationship with God. Pro 1:7 (VCG06 anchor) corpus: accumulated inner wisdom that is guarded and transmitted. VCG08 (Rom 8:28): settled inner conviction that provides stability.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Isa 52:13 (VCG01 anchor): effective wise action. 1Cor 11:31 (VCG02 anchor): self-awareness that avoids judgment. The sustained effect is: the person who exercises discernment over time develops reliable judgment.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — 2Sa 16:23 (VCG04 anchor): the trusted counselor's wisdom grows through sustained exercise. The deliberate planner over time becomes the person whose purposes align with outcomes.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor): "it is my meditation all the day" — sustained meditation on God's law constitutes love for it. Over time, the meditative activity shapes what the inner person loves and values.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — 2Cor 4:4 (VCG01 anchor) context: thought-content blinded remains blinded — sustained blindness hardens. Psa 139:17 (VCG02 anchor): thought-content directed toward God, over time, constitutes the person's relational orientation.

**[H — Logos]** E — Col 3:16 (VCG01 anchor): the word dwelling richly → all wisdom. The sustained presence of the word in the person produces: teaching capacity, worship, wisdom in community life.

#### T1.6.3 — Does sustained effect differ from immediate response?

**[A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H]**
E — Uniformly yes across all sub-groups. The immediate response is a specific act or opening; the sustained effect is a constituted quality or orientation formed over time. The pattern is consistent across the cluster: encounter/reception → immediate cognitive/volitional/affective opening → sustained formation of character/orientation/capacity. This is a cluster-level pattern.

**[CLUSTER]**
E — The consistent two-stage pattern (immediate → sustained) across all sub-groups suggests that M15 characteristics participate in character formation rather than merely providing momentary experiences. The cluster is formative in its sustained operation.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

#### T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable genuine reception?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Fear of the Lord (Psa 111:10 VCG09 anchor); humility-in-asking (Jam 1:5 VCG02 anchor); attentive hearing (VCG08: Neh 8:8).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Humility (1Ki 3:9: acknowledging inadequacy); practice (Heb 5:14 VCG05 anchor); openness to divine opening (Luk 24:45 VCG06 anchor — the disciples cannot open their own minds).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Covenantal orientation (Hos 6:3 VCG04 anchor: "let us press on to know"); fear of the Lord (Pro 1:7 VCG06 anchor: "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge").

**[D — Discernment]** E — Self-examination posture (1Cor 11:31 VCG02 anchor); taste/openness to experience (Psa 34:8 VCG05 anchor: "taste and see").

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Humility in seeking counsel (2Sa 16:23 VCG04 context: seeking counsel at all); setting the heart toward God's word (VCG07: the opposite of Pharaoh's refusal, Exo 7:23).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Love for God's law (Psa 119:97 VCG03 anchor: "oh how I love your law" — love enables and sustains meditation).

**[G — Inner thought-content]** S — No specific enabling conditions for receiving thought-content are named in M15-G's evidence. Silence.

**[H — Logos]** E — Openness to instruction (Gal 6:6 VCG01 corpus: "let the one who is taught the word share all good things"); receiving posture (Eph 1:13 VCG01 corpus: "when you heard the word of truth").

#### T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or distort reception?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Self-sufficiency (VCG06: Pro 26:12 — wise in own eyes); worldly orientation (VCG07: 1Cor 1:21).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Hardness of heart (VCG04: Isa 6:10); spiritual dullness; asunetos state (G0801).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG05 (Hos 4:6): "my people are destroyed for lack of knowledge" — implicit blocking by not seeking. VCG09 (Rom 10:3 anchor): self-righteousness blocks reception of God's righteousness/knowledge.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG06 (Gen 3:1): direction-inversion — when the shrewd capacity is turned away from God; VCG04 (Psa 73:16): "when I thought how to understand this, it seemed to me a wearisome task" — the difficulty of discernment when it requires honest engagement with troubling evidence.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Pharaoh's refusal to set his heart (VCG07: Exo 7:23 anchor): the inner posture of not directing attention toward God's word. Nations' refusal of God's counsel (VCG03 corpus).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — VCG01 (Luk 5:22 corpus): evil reasoning in the heart — reflective capacity turned toward self-serving judgment rather than formation. The blocking condition is the direction of the reflection, not its absence.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4 anchor): Satan blinds the minds — adversarial blinding is the primary blocking condition in this sub-group.

**[H — Logos]** E — Eph 5:6 (VCG02 corpus): "empty words" — deceiving speech that prevents receipt of the true logos. 1Cor 1:17 (VCG02 corpus): "words of eloquent wisdom" that empty the cross of power — the packaging that substitutes for the word itself.

#### T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters but does not receive?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG06 (Pro 26:12 anchor): "there is more hope for a fool than for a person wise in their own eyes" — self-wisdom as the inner state forecloses reception more completely than simple ignorance.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG04 (Isa 6:10 anchor): the judicially-closed inner state — hears but does not understand, sees but does not perceive. The faculty is active at surface level; closed at depth.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG09 (Rom 10:3 anchor): self-righteousness — the person who encounters God's righteousness but is occupied by their own projects. VCG05 (Hos 4:6): the people destroyed for lack — not refusing exactly but not seeking.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG04 (Psa 73:16 anchor): the person who finds discernment too difficult — the "wearisome task" — remains in confusion about the problem they cannot resolve.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — VCG07 (Exo 7:23 anchor): Pharaoh who "did not set his heart to this either" — the inner state is stubbornly oriented elsewhere, immune to signs.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — VCG01 (Luk 5:22): the Pharisees reasoning within themselves toward wrong conclusions — the reflective capacity active but uncorrected, producing self-justifying rather than formative results.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4 anchor): the blinded mind — thought-content capacity closed to the light of the gospel; the person holds thoughts but not the thought of God's glory.

**[H — Logos]** E — 1Cor 1:18 (VCG01 corpus): "the word of the cross is folly to those who are perishing" — the inner state of the person who encounters the logos but does not receive it is one where the word registers as foolishness, not power. The inner condition determines how the logos is received.

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

#### T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Cognitive-constitutive (constituted quality of heart-and-mind). Secondary: volitional (direction), relational (covenantal), creative (VCG10). Evidence: Pro 16:23 (heart of wise → judicious speech), Rom 8:5 (froneō — mind-orientation).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Cognitive-perceptive (the faculty of receiving and grasping). Secondary: volitional (formed through practice), moral-evaluative (distinguishing good from evil). Evidence: Isa 40:28, 1Ki 3:9, Heb 5:14.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Relational-covenantal (knowing as relational state). Secondary: cognitive (held content), moral (knowledge of good and evil, VCG07). Evidence: Hos 6:3 (pressing to know the LORD), Gen 4:1 (ya.da in intimate knowing), Amo 3:2 (elective knowing).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Cognitive-evaluative (making right distinctions through applied perception). Secondary: moral (distinguishing good from evil), volitional (crediting and imputing). Evidence: 1Cor 11:31 (self-judgment), Isa 52:13 (prudent effective action).

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Volitional (purposive willing and planning). Secondary: relational (counsel relationship), cognitive (forming the deliberate plan). Evidence: 2Pe 3:9 (boulomai — God's purposive willing), Jer 29:11 (purposive devising in the heart).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Cognitive-reflective (active mental engagement with received content). Secondary: affective (love formed through meditation, Psa 119:97). Evidence: Psa 119:97 (si.chah — all-day meditation), Luk 5:22 (dialogizomai — interior reasoning).

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Cognitive-static (formed thought-objects held in the mind). Secondary: relational (thought directed toward God or toward the person by God). Evidence: 2Cor 4:4 (noēmata — objects of adversarial blinding), Psa 139:17 (re.a — precious thoughts of God).

**[H — Logos]** E — Communicative-formative (the word in its inner-being-engaging mode). Secondary: moral (VCG03: comprehensive moral principle), cognitive (the word as content received). Evidence: Col 3:16 (word dwelling richly — constituting wisdom), 1Cor 14:19 (logos as intelligible speech).

#### T1.8.3 — Secondary dimensions and whether they compete

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Volitional, relational, creative — secondary dimensions do not compete with cognitive-constitutive primary; they extend it. Pro 16:23 (heart governing output) is unambiguously primary.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Volitional (practice) and moral-evaluative (distinguishing) are secondary and serve the primary perceptive function. No competition.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Cognitive (content) dimension is secondary to relational-covenantal primary. In VCG10 (practical knowing), the relational dimension recedes — these may be in minor tension within the sub-group. The relational-covenantal is definitionally primary (Hos 6:3, VCG04 anchor).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Moral-evaluative and volitional secondary dimensions serve the primary cognitive-evaluative. No competition.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — The relational (counseling relationship) and cognitive (plan formation) are secondary to the volitional primary. No competition; they are modes of the volitional expression.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — The affective secondary dimension (love formed through meditation, Psa 119:97) is a result of, not in competition with, the cognitive-reflective primary.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — The relational secondary dimension (thought directed toward/from God) gives the cognitive-static content its significance. No competition.

**[H — Logos]** E — The moral and cognitive secondary dimensions are modes of the communicative-formative primary. No competition.
