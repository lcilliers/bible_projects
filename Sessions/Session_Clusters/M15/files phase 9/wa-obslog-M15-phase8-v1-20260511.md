### PHASE 8 — M15-A — Catalogue pass

#### T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — The cluster's wisdom vocabulary is explicitly attributed to God himself with consistent regularity. VCG01 (19 verses) assembles evidence that wisdom belongs to God's own character: Dan 2:20 names wisdom and might as belonging to God; Job 12:13 attributes wisdom and might to God; Rev 7:12 ascribes wisdom to God in doxological form. The pattern shows wisdom is not merely something God distributes but something he inherently possesses — it characterises him. This grounds the human characteristic as a genuine reflection of divine nature, not merely an analogue.

**T0.1.2** — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Yes, explicitly and repeatedly. VCG01 contains 19 verses where wisdom is attributed to God or to divine figure (Christ as wisdom, Wisdom personified). Dan 2:20 ("wisdom and power are his"), 1Cor 1:24 (Christ as "the wisdom of God"), Prov 8 tradition (Wisdom as God's own deliberative capacity). The attribution reveals that wisdom at its source is not cognitive achievement but ontological character — it is what God is. The human characteristic therefore images a divine reality, not an abstract quality.

**T0.1.3** — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?

**S — [A]** Scripture is not silent here; it is extensively vocal. The sub-group's VCG01 speaks directly and frequently. No silence to note.

**T0.2.1** — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose of wisdom in the human person as created?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG03 (117 verses, anchor Pro 16:23) consistently shows wisdom equipping the person for wise conduct, discerning speech, restraint, and governance of relationships. The wise person hears instruction (Pro 8:33), governs speech (Pro 16:23), and navigates life rightly. The purpose embedded in VCG03 is relational and practical — wisdom orients the whole person toward right action in the world. It is not a contemplative reserve but an active inner equipping.

**T0.2.2** — Is this characteristic part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Both, evidenced across the cluster's VCGs. VCG02 (40 verses, anchor Jam 1:5) places wisdom as a divine gift — sought and received, which implies need or lack. VCG09 (3 verses, anchor Psa 111:10) roots wisdom in the fear of the Lord, which is the covenantal posture of the creature before God — suggesting created design, not merely fallen-condition remediation. VCG07 (34 verses, anchor 1Cor 1:21) shows a worldly wisdom that exists alongside and against divine wisdom — pointing to the fallenness dimension. The evidence does not permit a single-direction answer.

**T0.2.3** — Is there evidence that wisdom is oriented toward a future fullness?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG02 contains Jam 1:5 ("If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God") — the asking posture implies present lack and anticipated reception. 1Cor 2 (VCG07) hints at wisdom that is "mature" wisdom, disclosed among the mature — suggesting a developmental trajectory. VCG05 (Phili 2:2 anchor) names the shared inner orientation of a community "being of the same mind," which is an eschatological anticipation in Pauline thought. The evidence is present but not dominant — the orientation to future fullness is present but not the primary register of the sub-group.

**T0.3.1** — In what way does wisdom express the divine image in the human person?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG01–VCG03 together show that wisdom is an attribute of God that the human person can genuinely participate in — not through imitation but through endowment (VCG02) and formation (VCG03). The divine image is expressed specifically through the person's capacity to hold and exercise wisdom as a constituted inner quality: the wise person's heart governs their speech (Pro 16:23), their hearing, their restraint. This is not a skill the human acquires independently — it reflects the divine character precisely in being wisdom that is constituted, not merely operational.

**T0.3.2** — Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or a creaturely analogue?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — The evidence supports genuine sharing, not mere analogy. VCG02 shows divine wisdom placed into a human person — Solomon's wisdom is called "the wisdom of God" that God gave him (1Ki 3:12, carried in VCG02 corpus). James 1:5 makes the giving structure explicit: God gives wisdom to the one who asks, and gives it "generously." The category is participatory — the human wisdom that is given is a genuine extension of divine wisdom into the creature's inner person, not a different thing that resembles wisdom from a distance.

**T0.3.3** — What does the presence or absence of wisdom reveal about the condition of the divine image?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG06 (19 verses, anchor Pro 26:12) names the person who is wise in their own eyes — self-attributed wisdom. This is the counterfeit of genuine wisdom: the form without the divine source. It reveals that the divine image can be occupied by a self-constructed substitute that mimics wisdom without bearing it. VCG07 (worldly wisdom, anchor 1Cor 1:21) makes the same point: worldly wisdom is a constituted inner orientation that is structurally similar to divine wisdom but directionally inverted. The absence of genuine wisdom, or its displacement by these substitutes, reveals a fracture in the divine image precisely at the point where the person ought to image God's wisdom character.

**T0.4.1** — Does Scripture use wisdom typologically?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Yes. VCG01 contains 1Cor 1:24 ("Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God") — wisdom is not merely a concept but a Christological category. Christ is the wisdom of God in person. This is typological in the strong sense: the human characteristic points forward to and finds its ground in the incarnate Wisdom. Pro 8's Wisdom personified (present in the VCG01 corpus) prepares this typological reading.

**T0.4.2** — What is the direction of the typology?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — The divine establishes the pattern; the human instance participates in it. VCG01's evidence shows wisdom flowing from God → Christ → human person (VCG02). The typological direction is top-down: Christ as Wisdom grounds and funds the wisdom that human beings receive. This is confirmed by 1Cor 1:30 ("Christ Jesus, who became to us wisdom from God") — the participatory direction is explicit.

**T0.4.3** — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[A]** Not applicable — typological use is evidenced (see T0.4.1).

#### T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — What is this characteristic called, and what does the name signal?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — The dominant Hebrew term is H2450 cha.kham (wise) and H2452 chokh.mah (wisdom), with H8454 tu.shiy.yah (wisdom as effective practical capacity). The Greek is G4678 sofia (wisdom) and G4680 sofos (wise). The names signal wholeness of inner character: chokh.mah in its OT range is not intellectual skill alone but the governing of life from a constituted inner quality — the wise person is what they are, not merely what they know. Sofia in Greek carries both the intellectual and the practical — the Pauline usage especially contrasts it with mere speech (1Cor 1:17: "not with words of eloquent wisdom") revealing that sofia names something that underlies and exceeds speech.

**T1.1.2** — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — H2450 cha.kham: adjectival — the wise person is described in terms of character state. H2452 chokh.mah: nominal — wisdom as a quality that can be received, held, and exercised. G4678 sofia: nominal — wisdom as a substantive inner reality. G5426 froneō: verbal — the act of holding in the mind (VCG04: "to set the mind on"). G4679 sofizo: causative verbal — to make wise, which implies that another agent can constitute wisdom in a person. The range — adjectival, nominal, verbal, causative verbal — reveals that wisdom operates as attribute, substance, action, and produced-state simultaneously. It is a multi-modal inner reality.

**T1.1.3** — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Constitutional: the wise person is characterised, not merely informed. Relational: wisdom is given by God to the human person (Jam 1:5 — directional: from God downward). Covenantal: VCG09 (Psa 111:10 anchor) roots wisdom in the fear of the Lord — the relational orientation toward God is the constituting condition of wisdom. The name points simultaneously inward (constitutional quality) and upward (relational dependence on God).

**T1.2.1** — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — A constituted disposition that functions as an orientation, quality, and operative capacity simultaneously. VCG03 (Pro 16:23 anchor) shows wisdom as what the heart is — "the heart of the wise makes his speech judicious." This is not an act performed but a quality exercised. VCG04 (Rom 8:5 anchor) shows froneō as directional orientation — "to set the mind on." VCG02 shows wisdom as received endowment. The phenomenon is therefore: a divinely-endowed inner disposition that constitutes the person's orientation, governs their faculties, and produces characteristic behaviour.

**T1.2.2** — Is this characteristic simple or does it have constituent elements?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — It has constituent elements, distinguishable within the sub-group's VCG structure. VCG08 (Neh 8:8 anchor) names the perceptive-intelligibility element — the act of grasping meaning. VCG09 names the covenantal element — wisdom constituted by fear of the Lord. VCG10 (Exo 31:4 anchor) names the creative-design element. VCG04 names the directional-orientation element. Wisdom is therefore compound: a constituted quality (VCG03) that includes perceptive capacity (VCG08), covenantal grounding (VCG09), creative application (VCG10), and directional orientation (VCG04).

**T1.2.3** — Current best working description of this characteristic:

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Wisdom as holistic inner character (M15-A) is a divinely-endowed constituted quality of the whole person — grounded in covenantal orientation toward God, expressed in the governance of mind, speech, and action, and distinguished from self-attributed or world-oriented substitutes. Its primary constitutional location is the heart (governing speech and action) and the mind (directional orientation). It is received (VCG02), held as character (VCG03), and exercised across the whole range of life. Evidence: Pro 16:23, Jam 1:5, Rom 8:5.

**T1.3.1** — What is the structural opposite of wisdom?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — G0781 asofos (unwise) is the explicit lexical opposite in this sub-group. VCG06 (Pro 26:12 anchor) names the person "wise in their own eyes" as the functional opposite — not ignorance but self-constituted counterfeit. VCG07 (1Cor 1:21 anchor) names "worldly wisdom" as the directional opposite — oriented away from God. The structural opposites are therefore: (a) explicit absence of wisdom (asofos), (b) self-attributed substitute (VCG06), (c) directionally-inverted worldly wisdom (VCG07).

**T1.3.2** — What does wisdom explicitly exclude or resist?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG06 evidence excludes self-attribution: wisdom that is self-generated or self-claimed is the counterfeit. VCG07 excludes worldly-oriented wisdom: 1Cor 1:21 ("the world did not know God through wisdom"). VCG03 evidence — the wise person restrains impulse, governs speech — implies wisdom resists unguided impulse and unguarded speech.

**T1.3.3** — What is wisdom not — where does it end?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — Wisdom in this sub-group is not: (a) mere cognitive knowledge (that belongs to M15-C and M15-B); (b) mere clever speech (1Cor 1:17 explicitly disavows "words of eloquent wisdom" as the vehicle); (c) self-constituted achievement (VCG06 is its opposite); (d) worldly prudence (VCG07). It ends where mere cognition begins (M15-B boundary) and where self-sufficiency begins.

**T1.4.1** — In what distinct ways does wisdom operate within the inner person?

**[A — Wisdom as holistic inner character]**
E — VCG03: as constituted character — "the wise person is" not merely "does". VCG04: as directional orientation — "to set the mind on things above" (Col 3:2, froneō). VCG08: as grasping and intelligibility — the capacity to receive and understand what is communicated (Neh 8:8). VCG10: as creative-design inner faculty — wisdom operates in the forming of plans for sacred construction (Exo 31:4). VCG05: as shared orientation — wisdom binds a community in accord (Phili 2:2). Five distinct modes: constituted character, directional orientation, intelligibility, creative forming, communal accord.

The speech mode is a genuine operation of inner wisdom, not a secondary application.

#### T1 — Definition

**T1.1.1** — What is this characteristic called, and what does the name signal?

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — Primary Hebrew: H8394 te.vu.nah (understanding — from root ya.van/bin: to separate, distinguish, perceive). H0998/H0999 bi.nah (understanding — same root: to perceive by separating out distinctions). H0995 bin (to understand — verb: the act of perceiving). Greek: G4907 sunesis (understanding — from suniēmi: to send/bring together, i.e., to put things together to grasp meaning). G4920 suniēmi (to understand — verbal: the perceptive act itself). The name signals: understanding is fundamentally a perceptive-separating-grasping act — it involves discerning what is distinct and bringing it together into comprehension.

**T1.1.2** — What do primary terms reveal at definitional level?

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — H0995 bin (verb): the act of perceiving by distinguishing. Te.vu.nah (noun): the faculty that performs this act. Sunesis (Greek noun): the comprehension that results from bringing things together. Suniēmi (Greek verb): the act of unifying perception into understanding. The verbal forms reveal that understanding is primarily active — it is something the person does with their inner faculty, not merely something they passively hold.

**T1.1.3** — Directional, relational, or constitutional implications

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — Directional: understanding receives what is disclosed (VCG06 — the person's understanding is opened to receive Scripture). Relational: understanding can be given (divine → human direction, VCG02) and withheld (VCG04). Constitutional: understanding is a faculty that can be constituted in a person (VCG03, 1Ki 3:9). The name implies a perceptive-receiving faculty with a receiving direction built in.

**T1.2.1** — Kind of inner-being phenomenon

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — A perceptive faculty — the inner capacity to receive, distinguish, and grasp. VCG05 (Heb 5:14 anchor): "trained by constant practice to distinguish good from evil" — the faculty is formable through practice. VCG04 (Isa 6:10 anchor): the faculty can be blocked. The phenomenon is: an active inner perceptive capacity that receives disclosure, distinguishes meaning, and produces comprehension.

**T1.2.2** — Simple or constituent elements?

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — Constituent elements distinguished by the sub-group's VCGs: (a) receptive-perceptive act (VCG06: grasping revealed content); (b) constituted discernment faculty (VCG03: the person who has understanding); (c) formed-through-practice capacity (VCG05: Heb 5:14); (d) divinely-given endowment (VCG02). The VCG structure itself reveals distinct aspects of the faculty.

**T1.2.3** — Working description

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**
E — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty (M15-B) is the inner capacity to receive, distinguish, and grasp what is disclosed — divine revelation, situation, meaning. It is constituted in the person by divine endowment (VCG02), formed through practice (VCG05), exercised in grasping disclosed content (VCG06), and can be withheld or blocked by divine or moral action (VCG04). Its primary constitutional location is the heart (1Ki 3:9 — "a discerning heart") and the mind (Luk 24:45 — "opened their minds"). Evidence: Isa 40:28, 1Ki 3:9, Luk 24:45.

**[B — Understanding as inner perceptive faculty]**

Understanding ends where wisdom begins (as constituted character) and where knowledge ends (as content held).
