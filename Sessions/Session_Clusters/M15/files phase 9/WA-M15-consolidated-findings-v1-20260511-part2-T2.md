### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location (4 prompts)

#### T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?

**[A — Wisdom]** S — Wisdom as constituted character (M15-A) is not explicitly located at the spirit level in the sub-group's verses. The spirit is the origin channel for the giving of wisdom (VCG02: Exo 31:3 — Bezalel filled with the Spirit receives wisdom; Dan 5:14 — spirit of the gods associated with wisdom in Daniel), but the constituted wisdom itself is located in the heart (Pro 16:23 VCG03 anchor). Spirit-level location of the constituted wisdom: silent.

**[B — Understanding]** S — Same pattern as M15-A. The Spirit gives understanding; the understanding constituted is located in the heart (1Ki 3:9 VCG03 anchor) and opened in the mind (Luk 24:45 VCG06 anchor). Spirit-level location of the constituted faculty: silent.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — Knowledge as held content and covenantal knowing is not explicitly located at the spirit level. God's knowledge (VCG01) and human covenantal knowing (VCG04) are attributed but not specifically spirit-located. Spirit-level location: silent.

**[D — Discernment]** S — Discernment and practical judgment terms in M15-D are not explicitly spirit-located in the verse evidence. The inner act of discriminating (VCG02: 1Cor 11:31) and prudent action (VCG01: Isa 52:13) are not assigned a spirit-level site. Silence.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** S — Purposive willing (VCG01: boulomai) and deliberate devising (VCG05: H2803I) are inner volitional acts not specifically spirit-located in the evidence. God's irresistible counsel (VCG03) is divine-level but not specifically at the spirit-level constitutional location in the human person. Silence.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** S — Meditative activity is not explicitly spirit-located. Psa 119:97 (VCG03 anchor) and Luk 5:22 (VCG01 anchor) do not specify spirit-level location. Silence.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** S — Thought-content (noēmata, re.a, ra.yon) is not explicitly located at the spirit level. 2Cor 4:4 (VCG01 anchor): Satan blinds minds — the adversarial action is on the mind, not specifically the spirit. Silence.

**[H — Logos]** S — The word's dwelling (Col 3:16 VCG01 anchor) is located "in you richly" but not specifically at the spirit level. Spirit-level engagement with the logos is not explicit in M15-H's verses. Silence.

**[CLUSTER — T2.1.1]**
S — Spirit-level location is consistently SILENT across all sub-groups of M15. This is a cluster-level finding. The cluster's characteristics — wisdom, understanding, knowledge, discernment, counsel, meditation, thought-content, logos — are not explicitly assigned to the spirit level in any of the cluster's verse evidence. The spirit is consistently the origin-channel and giver of these characteristics, but not their named constitutional residence. This mirrors the pattern found in M06 and is now confirmed as a cluster-level silence for M15.

### T2.6 — Body — Significance (3 prompts)

**[B — Understanding]** E — The ear-understanding link is mediating: the ear is the receiving body-part through which understanding enters. Scripture uses the ear-body-part because hearing is the primary mode of receiving disclosed meaning (faith comes by hearing, Rom 10:17 proximate).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Gen 4:1 (VCG11 anchor): the body in intimate knowing is used by Scripture to name the deepest form of knowing — full personal union. The body-link here is indicative and comprehensive: ya.da at its most complete includes the body, revealing that knowing is ultimately whole-person, not merely cognitive.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Psa 34:8: the palate-discernment link is figurative: "taste and see" invites the listener to use inner discernment as they would use physical tasting — direct, immediate, experiential. The body-part link is a teaching device that reveals that discernment has an experiential-intuitive dimension, not only an analytical one.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** S — No body-part link to interpret.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Psa 39:3: the heart becoming hot — a somatic resonance that reveals meditation is not purely cognitive but involves the whole person, including the body's responsiveness to inner intensity.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** S — No body-part link to interpret.

**[H — Logos]** E — 1Cor 14:19: the mouth-speech link reveals that the logos as inner phenomenon must be expressed outward through the body to become effective in community. The body mediates the logos from inner dwelling to communal formation.

### T2.7 — Body — Direction (3 prompts)

#### T2.7.1 — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Soul → body direction: wisdom in the heart → judicious speech from the mouth (Pro 16:23). Unidirectional: outward-expressing.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Bidirectional: body → soul (ear hears, understanding receives); soul → body (understanding governs speech in VCG07 translation context). Both directions evidenced.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG11 (Gen 4:1): the body in intimate knowing is whole-person — bidirectional: the bodily union is simultaneously the mode and the expression of the deepest knowing. Both directions.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG05 (Psa 34:8): the palate-image runs soul → body in its figurative direction: inner discernment is LIKE the palate's testing. The figure is descriptive, not a literal body-soul directional claim.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** S — Not applicable (no body-link).

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — VCG03 (Psa 39:3): heart hot within → somatic resonance: soul → body direction. The inner intensity of meditation produces a bodily response.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** S — Not applicable (no body-link).

**[H — Logos]** E — Soul → body direction for expression (inner word → spoken word through mouth). Body → soul direction for reception (spoken word through ear → inner reception). Bidirectional.

---

#### T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — The soul → body direction reveals wisdom as originating in the inner person and expressing outward — it is not a learned behaviour but an inner quality that cannot be contained. The constituted wisdom of the heart governs speech because that is the nature of constituted inner quality: it produces what the person IS, not what they perform.

**[B — Understanding]** E — The bidirectionality reveals understanding as a relational faculty — it receives from outside (ear → understanding) and governs what it produces (understanding → speech in interpretation). Understanding is an interface faculty.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — The bidirectionality in VCG11 reveals that the most complete knowing is whole-person — cognitive, relational, and physical simultaneously. The body's involvement in the deepest knowing form (intimate union) reveals that knowing is not merely intellectual; at its fullest it encompasses all levels of personal reality.

**[H — Logos]** E — The bidirectionality of logos reveals it as the central communicative characteristic of the cluster: it is both received (word enters through hearing → inner dwelling) and expressed (inner word → spoken word → community formation). The logos is the characteristic that most completely spans the receiving-and-expressing arc.
