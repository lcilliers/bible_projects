### T5.1 — Nature of Transformation

#### T5.1.1 — What transformation does this characteristic undergo in the person over time?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG03 (Pro 4:18): "the path of the righteous is like the light of dawn, which shines brighter and brighter until full day." Wisdom grows progressively. VCG09 (Pro 9:10 — fear of the LORD as beginning): the beginning point presupposes a trajectory of deepening.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG05 (Heb 5:14): understanding trained through constant practice — progressive formation. VCG06 (Luk 24:45): Christ's opening as the beginning of a new capacity, not the completion of it.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3 "press on"): knowing God grows through pursuit. VCG06 (Pro 10:14 "lay up"): knowledge accumulates over time. VCG08 (Job 19:25): conviction deepened through testing in suffering.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG05 (Heb 5:14): discernment trained through repeated practice — progressive formation. VCG01 (sa.khal): producing success through sustained wise action implies ongoing formation.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG08 (Pro 6:6 pronoia): foresight develops through observation and wisdom. VCG04: counseling skill develops with practice and Spirit-gifting.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03 (Psa 119:97-99): sustained meditation produces measurable inner transformation — understanding surpassing teachers.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 10:5): the discipline of taking thoughts captive shapes the inner cognitive domain over time toward Christ's obedience.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (Col 3:16 "richly"): the dwelling of the word grows richer with sustained reception.

**[BOUNDARY]** S — BOUNDARY terms are mechanisms; they do not undergo personal transformation.

---

#### T5.1.2 — Does the characteristic itself transform, or does it transform the person?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Both: wisdom deepens (the path grows brighter) and the person is transformed by wisdom's forming of character over time.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Both: the understanding faculty deepens (VCG05 training); and the person is transformed by opened understanding (VCG06 — from confusion to commission).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both: knowledge-content deepens (VCG08 conviction through testing); and the person is transformed by the knowing of God (VCG04 — life-orientation shaped by knowing).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Both: discernment capacity deepens (VCG05 training); and discernment transforms the person (VCG01 — success in conduct; VCG02 — freedom from condemnation).

**[E — Counsel]** E — Both: purposive capacity deepens (VCG08 observational learning); and purposive intent transforms the person's life-direction (VCG05 — the formed purpose shapes the person's trajectory).

**[F — Meditation]** E — Both: meditative capacity deepens; and the person is transformed through sustained meditation on God's law (VCG03 — understanding deepens, love grows).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — M15-G's transformation is primarily of the person: the thought-content changes (is brought captive, VCG01) and through this change the person is transformed toward Christ's obedience.

**[H — Logos]** E — Both: the logos dwelling grows richer (VCG01 "richly"); and the person is transformed by the word's indwelling (VCG01 Jn 15:3 — inner cleansing).

---

#### T5.1.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the pace or stages of this transformation?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Progressive and long-term: the path growing brighter until full day implies a sustained trajectory. No evidence of sudden wisdom-formation except divine sovereign bestowing (VCG02 Solomon's prayer answered).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Two modes: progressive (VCG05 training through practice) and instantaneous (VCG06 Christ's opening — a definitive act that opens the faculty).

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Progressive (VCG04 pressing on, VCG08 conviction through sustained testing). Exception: VCG03 — knowing through historical redemptive events can be instantaneous in historical terms.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Progressive: VCG05 (Heb 5:14 "trained by constant practice" — no sudden formation except VCG03 instantaneous divine crediting).

**[E — Counsel]** E — Progressive: VCG08 (observational learning), VCG02 (apostolic formation through experience and Spirit-guidance).

**[F — Meditation]** E — Slow and cumulative: VCG03 "all the day" suggests sustained, proportional transformation.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — Both sudden (VCG01 — each act of taking thoughts captive is a discrete act) and cumulative (the pattern of the acts shapes the inner cognitive domain over time).

**[H — Logos]** E — Progressive: the word dwelling richly implies accumulative deepening. VCG01 (Jn 15:3): cleansing through the already-spoken word — past definitive act with ongoing effect.

### T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States

#### T5.2.1 — Is there a typical sequence of inner states associated with this characteristic?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Sequence: encounter with God's revelation (through law, teaching, experience) → humility and receptivity ("beginning of wisdom: fear of the LORD") → growing possession of wisdom-as-character → expressed in governance, speech, and life.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Sequence: undiscerning state (milk-only, Heb 5:13) → training-by-practice → trained discernment (mature, Heb 5:14). For divine opening: closed understanding → Christ's act → opened to comprehend Scripture.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Covenantal sequence: encounter with God's disclosure → volitional pursuit → relational knowing → orientation-formation. Conviction sequence: initial faith → affliction → maintained conviction → vindication anticipated.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Training sequence: immature/undiscerning → exposure to training → developing discernment. Divine crediting sequence: faith-act → divine evaluation → righteous-status credited.

**[E — Counsel]** E — Planning sequence: observation/awareness → deliberate forming of purpose → expressed in action → governed by God (Pro 16:9). Counseling sequence: inner wisdom → formed counsel → spoken to counselee → acted upon.

**[F — Meditation]** E — Meditation sequence: receive God's law → love it → meditate repeatedly → grow in understanding (VCG03). Reflection sequence: face complex situation → enter reflective state → receive disclosure or resolution (VCG02).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — Formation sequence: thought-content present → volitional capture → brought into Christ's obedience → inner cognitive domain aligned (VCG01 2Cor 10:5).

**[H — Logos]** E — Reception sequence: divine word sent → received openly → dwells richly → produces wisdom, teaching, admonishing, worship (Col 3:16). Expression sequence: internalised word → disciplined formation → gracious speech offered (Col 4:6).

**[BOUNDARY]** S — Not applicable.

**[E — Counsel]** E — Awareness of situation and self-limitation (VCG08 — learning from the ant implies recognising one's own lack of foresight).

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG03: love of God's law as precondition. VCG02: an unresolved inner situation requiring resolution.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG03: recognition of need and turning to God in prayer (Solomon's request).

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (Col 3:16): openness and community receptivity — "let it dwell."

### T5.3 — Mechanism of Change

#### T5.3.1 — What mechanisms does the evidence reveal for how this characteristic is formed, deepened, or lost?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Formation: divine bestowing (VCG02 Spirit-endowment), fear of the LORD (VCG09), hearing and accumulating (VCG03). Deepening: sustained practice in living wisely. Loss: self-attributed wisdom (VCG06) — the person who claims wisdom is closed to receiving it; foolishness as the failure to pursue (Pro 1 inverse).

**[B — Understanding]** E — Formation: constant practice (VCG05 Heb 5:14), divine opening (VCG06 Luk 24:45), sustained discourse (VCG07). Deepening: continued training. Loss: judicial withholding (VCG04) or moral hardening.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Formation: divine disclosure through historical events (VCG03), covenantal pursuit (VCG04), wise accumulation (VCG06). Deepening: sustained engagement through difficulty (VCG08). Loss: covenant-breach — not-knowing as relational rupture (VCG05).

**[D — Discernment]** E — Formation: constant practice (VCG05 Heb 5:14). Deepening: repeated self-examination (VCG02), sustained wise action (VCG01). Loss/perversion: misdirection of the perceptive faculty (VCG06 a.rum as cunning rather than wisdom when directed wrongly).

**[E — Counsel]** E — Formation: observation (VCG08 ant), received wisdom (VCG04), Spirit-guidance (VCG02). Perversion: directing purposive capacity toward evil (VCG05 Mic 2:1).

**[F — Meditation]** E — Formation: sustained repetitive practice (VCG03 "all the day") + divine provision of content. Perversion: the meditative inner activity turned self-ward (VCG01 rich fool's self-enclosed monologue).

**[G — Thought-content]** E — Formation: divine gift (VCG03). Change: volitional capture (VCG01 2Cor 10:5). Loss: adversarial blinding (VCG01 2Cor 4:4).

**[H — Logos]** E — Formation: volitional receiving and sustained holding (Col 3:16). Deepening: the word dwelling richly through ongoing reception. Loss: the word received but not held (seed among thorns pattern — outside M15-H's VCG anchors but within the logos tradition).

#### T5.3.2 — Is change primarily divine initiative, human effort, or both?

**Cluster-level pattern confirmed:**
All sub-groups show both divine initiative and human effort in different proportions:
- Divine initiative dominates: VCG02 of M15-A (Spirit-endowment), VCG06 of M15-B (Christ's opening), VCG03 of M15-C (redemptive self-disclosure), VCG03 of M15-D (divine crediting), VCG03 of M15-E (irresistible divine counsel), VCG03 of M15-G (divine knowledge-placement), VCG01 of M15-H (divine logos sent).
- Human effort dominates: VCG05 of M15-B and M15-D (constant practice), VCG04 of M15-C (volitional pursuit), VCG03 of M15-F (sustained "all the day" meditation), VCG01 of M15-G (taking thoughts captive).
- Both equally: M15-A (VCG09 fear of the LORD as human posture + divine wisdom that follows), M15-E (VCG05 human planning + VCG03 divine governance of outcomes — Pro 16:9).

#### T5.3.3 — What does the evidence reveal about resistance to formation or loss?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG06 (self-attributed wisdom) and VCG07 (worldly wisdom orientation): the primary resistance mechanisms. The person who is wise in their own eyes cannot receive wisdom; the person whose orientation is toward the world's wisdom is closed to divine wisdom.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG04 (judicial withholding): hardened hearts cannot understand. Moral blockage is the primary loss mechanism.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG05 (not-knowing as active resistance): Eli's sons as the type of chosen not-knowing. VCG09: culpable ignorance as sustained resistance.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG06 (a.rum misdirected): the neutral perceptive faculty becoming cunning when directed wrongly. The same capacity, wrongly directed, becomes its own perversion.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG06 (za.mam, Gen 11:6): human unified purposing can be so strong it requires divine intervention to disrupt. The resistance is not against the formation of purposing but against its wrong direction.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG01 (self-enclosed inner monologue): when meditative activity turns inward-self-ward, it becomes futile reasoning. The perversion mechanism: the same inner activity, redirected.

**[G — Thought-content]** E — VCG01 (2Cor 4:4): adversarial blinding — the external resistance mechanism in M15-G. The thought-content is actively targeted by the adversary.

**[H — Logos]** E — The logos not-received: the seed pattern (outside VCG anchors) as the loss mechanism. Reception requires ongoing openness.

### T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction

**[A — Wisdom]** E — VCG09 (Psa 119:71 — "it is good for me that I was afflicted, that I might learn your statutes"): suffering deepens wisdom through the learning it enforces. VCG01 (the cross as the wisdom of God — 1Cor 1:18-25): the ultimate wisdom is expressed through and produced by suffering.

**[B — Understanding]** E — VCG05 (Heb 5:14): the training that forms discernment implies disciplined effort — which can include difficulty. The maturity toward which understanding-training is directed is explicitly eschatologically tested.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (Job 19:25): the most direct evidence across M15. Job's knowledge of his Redeemer is forged and sustained through extreme suffering. The conviction is expressed through and tested by affliction.

**[D — Discernment]** E — VCG02 (1Cor 11:31): self-examination in the Eucharistic context — failure of self-discernment produces bodily illness and death (1Cor 11:30). The consequence of failed discernment is suffering.

**[E — Counsel]** E — VCG03 (Isa 14:24): the divine irresistible purpose operates through the suffering of the nations — God's plan includes affliction as its instrument.

**[F — Meditation]** E — VCG04 (Dan 7:28): Daniel's meditative holding of the terrifying vision is a form of affliction-management — keeping the vision in heart despite the distress it causes.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — Suffering's role not evidenced in M15-G's VCG corpus.

**[H — Logos]** S — Suffering's role not directly evidenced in M15-H's VCG corpus.

**[G — Thought-content]** S — No eschatological trajectory in M15-G's VCG evidence. Consistent with M15-G as an operational-creaturely capacity.

**[H — Logos]** E — VCG01 (1Cor 1:18 — the word of the cross announces the new age as present power). VCG03 (Gal 5:14): the logos of love-your-neighbour is the eschatological moral principle of the kingdom community.

This reveals M15-F as having both an activity and a condition aspect.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Yes. Noēma (noun): formed mind-content — the product of cognitive activity. Ennoia (noun): inner disposition, thought — a quality of the inner person. The three Greek nouns distinguish: raw formed thought (noēma — 2Cor 4:4), the dispositional inner quality (ennoia), and the purposive directed thought (epinoia). **[H — Logos]** E — The logos has no internal vocabulary differentiation in M15-H's term set — G3056 is the sole term. #### T7.1.9 — Is there a term newly coined in the NT period for this characteristic — and if so, what does that coinage reveal?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — G5428 fronēsis: practical wisdom/prudence in the NT (Luk 1:17 — "the wisdom of the righteous"; Eph 1:8 — "wisdom and insight"). Fronēsis is not entirely new (Aristotle) but its NT application to the wisdom of the righteous and to the Spirit's endowment reveals a distinctive NT development: practical wisdom as a Spirit-given capacity for godly living. Also: G4678 sophia applied to Christ personally (1Cor 1:24, 30 — "Christ the wisdom of God") — the personalisation of the abstract attribute as divine Person is a NT development building on Wisdom literature's personification.

**[B — Understanding]** E — G4907 sunesis: understanding as comprehension (Mk 12:33 — "to love God is worth more than all burnt offerings" — spoken with sunesis). The NT deployment of sunesis in specifically Christological and ethical contexts (Col 1:9 — "understanding in all wisdom") reveals a NT development: understanding as the faculty that grasps the mystery of Christ. G4908 sunetos: the understanding person (Matt 11:25 — hidden from "the wise and understanding"). The NT pattern of Christ opening understanding (Luk 24:45 — suniēmi) is a distinctively NT Christological development.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — G0050 agnoeō: to be ignorant (Act 17:30 — "times of ignorance God overlooked"). While agnoeō exists in classical Greek, its NT moral-theological loading (ignorance as culpable, as the condition that produces accountability rather than excusing it) is a distinctively NT development. Also: the gnōsis/epignōsis distinction in Paul (Col 1:9 — "knowledge [epignōsis] of his will") — epignōsis as full or thorough knowledge is a NT intensification of the knowing vocabulary applied to knowing God and his will.

**[D — Discernment]** E — G1252 diakrinō acquires a distinctive second NT meaning: doubt, self-division (Jas 1:6 — "ask in faith, with no doubting [diakrinō]"; Rom 4:20 — "he did not diakrinō with respect to the promise of God"). This is a significant NT development: the discrimination/evaluation root is turned inward to name the self-divided state of the one whose inner evaluative faculty is split between faith and doubt. This meaning does not appear in the LXX or classical usage.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — G4307 pronoia in NT acquires moral-ethical loading: "make no provision [pronoia] for the flesh to gratify its desires" (Rom 13:14). In classical and LXX usage pronoia is primarily cognitive foresight (planning ahead). The NT application to the moral discipline of not planning for fleshly gratification is a NT development — foresight as a morally charged responsibility. Also: G1013 boulēma (Rom 9:19 — "who has resisted his boulēma?") — divine boulēma as irresistible divine will is given specific Pauline development.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — G1760 enthumeomai: to ponder within (Matt 1:20 — Joseph's pondering before angelic disclosure; Matt 9:4 — Jesus perceiving the scribes' inner thoughts). The en + thumos compound root is NT-period usage: the emphasis on reflective activity within the deep inner movement (thumos) is a distinctive NT emphasis on the reflective-affective depth of inner deliberation. Not an entirely new coinage but a NT-specific deployment.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — G3540 noēma: the mind's formed content (2Cor 4:4; 2Cor 10:5 — "taking every noēma captive"). While noēma exists in classical Greek (Plato uses it for the mind's grasp), its NT deployment in a spiritual warfare context (adversarial blinding, taking captive) is a distinctively Pauline theological development. The noēma as contested inner cognitive territory — simultaneously the object of adversarial attack and of volitional governance — is a NT development of the term.

**[H — Logos]** E — G3056 logos as divine Person (Jn 1:1 — "in the beginning was the logos, and the logos was with God, and the logos was God"). This is the most theologically radical NT development in the cluster and arguably in the NT. The word-as-divine-Person transcends all classical or LXX uses: the logos is no longer merely the rational principle (Stoic), the divine word (OT prophetic tradition), or the wisdom-as-agent (Wisdom literature) — it is the pre-existent divine Person who becomes flesh (Jn 1:14). This coinage reveals that M15-H's characteristic is grounded in a Person, not merely in a principle or phenomenon.

#### T7.2.2 — What literary form carries the primary verse evidence — and what does that form require for responsible interpretation?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Primary anchor Pro 16:23 is Wisdom literature (Proverbs — didactic aphorism). The form requires attending to the aphoristic generalisation: Pro 16:23 does not describe one event but states a general truth about the wise person's character. The didactic form means the claim is about pattern, not occasion. Secondary evidence 1Cor 1:18-25 is Pauline epistle (community crisis context — worldly wisdom dividing the Corinthian community). The epistolary form requires attending to the audience and the polemic: Paul is correcting, not simply describing.

**[B — Understanding]** E — Primary anchor 1Ki 3:9 is narrative (historical narrative — Solomon's prayer at Gibeon). The narrative form requires attending to what the narrator commends: Solomon's request is presented approvingly by the narrator (1Ki 3:10 — "it pleased the LORD"). Responsible interpretation treats this as an endorsed model, not merely a report. Secondary evidence Heb 5:14 is Pauline epistle (pastoral argument — the author rebuking immaturity). The epistolary form requires attending to the context of rebuke: the claim about trained understanding is made in a context where the community has failed to develop it.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Primary anchor Hos 6:3 is prophetic oracle (covenant renewal call — the prophet calling the people back to covenantal knowing). The prophetic form requires attending to the covenant lawsuit context: the "let us" is not abstract aspiration but response to prophetic rebuke. Secondary evidence Psa 139:6 is Psalmic poetry (first-person address to God). The confessional poetic form requires attending to the personal-relational register: the psalmist is not making a theological proposition about divine omniscience but confessing overwhelming personal experience of being known.

**[D — Discernment]** E — Primary anchor Isa 52:13 is Prophetic servant song (Fourth Servant Song — redemptive poetry). The form requires attending to the typological-eschatological register: the Servant's sa.khal is not primarily a prudential observation but a redemptive announcement. Responsible interpretation requires reading the verse within its servant-song context, not extracting sa.khal as a general wisdom claim. Secondary evidence Gen 15:6 is narrative (covenant-establishment narrative). The form requires attending to the covenant context: the divine crediting is a constitutive covenant act, not merely a moral assessment.

**[E — Counsel and purposive intent]** E — Primary anchor Isa 14:24 is prophetic oracle (taunt-oracle against the king of Babylon). The form requires attending to the polemical context: God's irresistible counsel is declared against a human power that believed itself invincible. Responsible interpretation places the claim about counsel's irresistibility in a context of divine polemic against human pride, not in a context of abstract theodicy. Secondary evidence Pro 16:9 is Wisdom literature (aphorism). The didactic form requires attending to its generalising character: Pro 16:9 states a principle about the God-human relationship in planning, not an occasion.

**[F — Meditative and reflective activity]** E — Primary anchor Psa 119:97 is Psalmic poetry (acrostic devotional poem — the longest psalm). The form requires attending to the hyperbolic devotional register: "all the day" is not a literal time claim but an expression of the psalmist's total orientation. Responsible interpretation reads the psalm as devotional poetry whose hyperbolic form expresses intensity of commitment, not as a prescriptive rule about meditation hours. The acrostic form itself models the meditative practice the psalm describes: sustained, structured return to the same content.

**[G — Inner thought-content]** E — Primary anchor 2Cor 4:4 is Pauline epistle (apologetic argument — Paul defending his ministry against accusations). The epistolary form requires attending to the apologetic context: Paul is explaining why the gospel is not being received in Corinth, not making a general psychological claim about unbelief. Responsible interpretation places the blinding of noēmata within the specific missionary context Paul is addressing. Secondary evidence 2Cor 10:5 is Pauline epistle (same letter, different section — spiritual warfare argument). The military metaphor in 2Cor 10:4-5 requires responsible interpretation of the warfare imagery: the demolishing of strongholds is the work of the gospel's power, not literal military action.

**[H — Logos]** E — Primary anchor Col 3:16 is Pauline epistle (household code context — instruction for community life in Christ). The epistolary form requires attending to the communal-practical register: Col 3:16 is instruction for how the community is to live together, not primarily a theological proposition about the word. Responsible interpretation treats the verse as describing a community practice (word dwelling richly) that produces communal outcomes (teaching, admonishing, worship). Secondary evidence 1Cor 1:18 is Pauline epistle (apologetic/community-correction context). The form requires attending to the polemic: the logos of the cross is declared foolish by the worldly-wise in a specific community context of division.

#### T7.2.3 — What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence?

**[A — Wisdom]** E — Pro 16:23: premise — a wise heart exists as constituted character; conclusion — it guides speech with appropriate teaching. The argument moves from inner constitution to outer expression. 1Cor 1:18-24: premise — worldly wisdom judges by worldly standards; premise — God has made the cross foolishness to the wise; conclusion — the wisdom of God (Christ crucified) is inaccessible to self-attributed wisdom. **[B — Understanding]** E — Heb 5:14: premise — solid food belongs to the mature; premise — maturity is a faculty trained by constant practice; conclusion — trained discernment for moral distinction marks maturity.
