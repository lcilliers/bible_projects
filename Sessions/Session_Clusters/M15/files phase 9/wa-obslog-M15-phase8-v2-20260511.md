#### T3 — The Inner Faculties (11 components, 33 prompts)

**T3.1 — Perception**

**T3.1.1** — What perceptive capacity does this characteristic engage or constitute?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 (Psa 139:6): divine perceptive knowing is "too wonderful" — it names an order of perception beyond human reach. VCG04 (Hos 6:3): human knowing of God as perceptive pursuit — "let us press on to know." VCG10: practical perceptual knowing — Gen 12:11 Abraham perceives Sarah's beauty; situational awareness. VCG11: intimate knowing (Gen 4:1) — the most complete personal perception of another. M15-C engages the full perceptive spectrum: from situational recognition (VCG10) to relational apprehension (VCG04) to comprehensive divine perception (VCG01).

**T3.1.2** — Is this characteristic itself a form of perception, or does it produce perception as an outcome?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both: H3045 ya.da and G6063 oida name the act of knowing (perception as act), and also the state of having known (knowledge as possession). VCG08 (Job 19:25): "I know that my Redeemer lives" — settled perceptive conviction. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): "the wise lay up knowledge" — knowledge as accumulated perceptual deposit. The characteristic is perception-as-act and perception-as-product simultaneously.

**T3.1.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the perceptive range of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Range spans: (a) material/sensory: knowing physical facts (VCG10); (b) relational: knowing persons in covenant (VCG04, VCG11); (c) moral: knowing good and evil (VCG07); (d) propositional: settled conviction about truths (VCG08); (e) divine-comprehensive: God's knowing of all inner states (VCG01). No sub-group in M15 shows a wider perceptive range than M15-C.

**T3.2 — Cognition**

**T3.2.1** — What cognitive processes does this characteristic involve or activate?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08: cognitive formation of conviction (Job 19:25). VCG04: cognitive pursuit — "let us press on to know" (Hos 6:3). VCG06: cognitive accumulation and guarding (Pro 10:14). VCG09 (Act 17:30): cognitive re-orientation — the command to repent presupposes that ignorance-patterns can be cognitively broken. Cognitive processes: recognition, conviction-formation, accumulation, pursuit, and re-orientation.

**T3.2.2** — Does this characteristic operate at the level of understanding, reasoning, or some other cognitive function?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — M15-C operates primarily at the level of knowing (ya.da), which in its fullest sense integrates recognition, relationship, and conviction — not merely information-processing. VCG07 (Gen 2:9): moral knowing engages evaluative cognition. VCG03 (Exo 7:5): knowing-God-through-events involves historical-theological reasoning. The cognitive function is integrative — not analysis-only but recognition-through-relationship.

**T3.2.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the cognitive depth of this characteristic — surface cognition or depth cognition?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — M15-C spans both. VCG10: surface practical knowing (situational recognition). VCG01 (Psa 139:6): the deepest cognitive order — divine comprehensive knowing. VCG08: depth cognition — Job's sustained conviction through suffering. The evidence strongly weights M15-C toward depth cognition: relational, covenantal, conviction-oriented knowing predominates over surface informational knowing.

**T3.3 — Memory**

**T3.3.1** — Does this characteristic engage memory — either as its basis, its product, or its object?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG03 (Exo 7:5; Jos 4:24): redemptive knowing is built on memorial acts — the historical events that generate "that you may know." Memory is the basis of covenantal-historical knowing. VCG08: Job's conviction "I know my Redeemer lives" has a memorial depth — prior experience of God's nature informs present conviction.

**T3.3.2** — Is there evidence of knowledge stored, retrieved, or organised in the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG06 (Pro 10:14): "the wise lay up knowledge" explicitly describes knowledge as stored inner content. "Lay up" (H6845 tsa.fan) implies organised inner holding. The image is of a guarded inner treasury of knowledge.

**T3.3.3** — Where the evidence is silent on memory engagement, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG11 (intimate knowing) and VCG09 (culpable ignorance) do not speak to memory explicitly.

**T3.4 — Affect**

**T3.4.1** — Does this characteristic engage or produce affective states — emotions, desires, loves, or aversions?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): pressing on to know the Lord carries a desire-orientation — knowing as pursued. VCG12 (Hab 2:14): eschatological knowing carries implicit longing. VCG05 (1Sa 2:12): absence of knowing the Lord — the sons of Eli — is paired implicitly with their destructive behaviour; lack of knowing produces destructive affect-expression.

**T3.4.2** — Is this characteristic constitutively affective, or does it produce affect secondarily?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Knowledge in M15-C is not constitutively affective (like grief or joy). Affect arises secondarily from the knowing: knowing God produces orientation-of-love (VCG04); ignorance produces its own destructive patterns (VCG05). Knowledge is primarily cognitive-relational; affect is its consequence.

**T3.4.3** — Where the evidence is silent on affective engagement, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG08 (settled conviction), VCG10 (practical knowing), and VCG07 (moral knowing) do not speak explicitly to affect.

**T3.5 — Creativity**

**T3.5.1** — Does this characteristic engage or constitute the creative capacity of the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** S — No direct evidence in M15-C's corpus. VCG10 (practical knowing) may include skill-knowing but creative engagement is not the named phenomenon.

**T3.5.2** — Is there evidence of knowledge generating or requiring creative inner activity?

**[C — Knowledge]** S — Silence. Note: VCG06 (Pro 10:14 corpus) includes Pro 3:20 (by knowledge the deeps were broken open — divine creative knowing), but the creative act is divine, not the creativity inner-capacity of the human person.

**T3.5.3** — Where the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — Creativity is explicitly silent in M15-C's human inner-being evidence.

**T3.6 — Volition**

**T3.6.1** — Does this characteristic engage the volitional capacity of the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): "let us press on to know" — volitional choice to pursue knowing. VCG05 (1Sa 2:12): Eli's sons "did not know the Lord" — the text frames this as a chosen condition, not incapacity. VCG09 (Act 17:30): ignorance that God "overlooked" was not innocence but a volitional pattern.

**T3.6.2** — Is this characteristic volitionally received, volitionally maintained, or volitionally refused?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — All three: volitionally received (VCG04 pursuit); volitionally maintained (VCG06 "lay up knowledge," VCG08 conviction maintained through suffering); volitionally refused (VCG05 not-knowing as chosen stance). The volitional dimension is strong across M15-C.

**T3.6.3** — Where the evidence is silent on volition, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 and VCG02 (divine knowing) are silent on human volition — these name God's knowing, not the human's volitional engagement.

**T3.7 — Agency**

**T3.7.1** — Does this characteristic confer agency on the inner person — enabling action, direction, or initiative?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (Job 19:25): Job's knowledge of his Redeemer is the basis for his sustained advocacy of his own cause — knowing enables continued agency through suffering. VCG04: knowing God orients the whole life. VCG10: practical knowing enables correct action.

**T3.7.2** — What kind of agency does this characteristic enable?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08: sustained advocacy under affliction. VCG04: covenantal living. VCG10: practical navigation. The agency enabled is appropriate to the mode of knowing: relational knowing enables covenantal agency; practical knowing enables situational agency; conviction-knowing enables sustained agency through difficulty.

**T3.7.3** — Where the evidence is silent on agency, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG11 (intimate knowing) does not explicitly address agency beyond the union itself.

**T3.8 — Moral Evaluation**

**T3.8.1** — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluative capacity of the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG07 (Gen 2:9): the tree of the knowledge of good and evil is the central biblical instance. Moral knowing is explicitly its own VCG. VCG02: God's knowing-by-election has moral evaluation as its corollary — knowing in covenant implies moral accountability (Amos 3:2b).

**T3.8.2** — Is this characteristic constitutively moral, or does it acquire moral significance through use?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Mixed. VCG07 identifies a constitutively moral knowing (good/evil distinction). But M15-C's broader corpus shows that knowing acquires moral significance through its direction and object: knowing God is morally formative; refusing to know is morally accountable (VCG05, VCG09). The characteristic is not uniformly moral in constitution but is morally significant throughout.

**T3.8.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the relationship between this characteristic and moral formation?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Covenantal knowing (VCG04) is the primary engine of moral formation within M15-C: pressing on to know the Lord shapes life-orientation. Culpable ignorance (VCG09) names the moral consequence of refusing the knowing God makes available.

**T3.9 — Conscience**

**T3.9.1** — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (settled conviction — Job) carries conscience-proximate dynamics: Job's conviction of his own integrity before God. VCG07: moral knowing is the knowledge-base from which conscience draws its material. M15-C supplies the content for conscience (moral knowledge) but is not conscience itself.

**T3.9.2** — Is there evidence of this characteristic functioning as or alongside conscience?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Inferential: moral knowledge (VCG07) is the cognitive basis of conscientious discrimination. The relationship is that M15-C provides the knowing-material; conscience (a separate cluster) uses it. No verse in M15-C names conscience as such.

**T3.9.3** — Where the evidence is silent on conscience, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — No M15-C verse explicitly names conscience as the function engaged.

**T3.10 — Conscientiousness**

**T3.10.1** — Does this characteristic relate to conscientiousness — the diligent, careful exercise of inner capacity?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG06 (Pro 10:14): "the wise lay up knowledge" describes diligent accumulation — conscientiousness in the intellectual life. The contrast with the "mouth of the fool brings destruction" implies that knowledge-accumulation requires sustained care.

**T3.10.2** — Is there evidence of this characteristic requiring or producing conscientious inner effort?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): "let us press on" implies diligent effort. VCG08: maintaining conviction through sustained suffering is a form of conscientious inner faithfulness.

**T3.10.3** — Where the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG10 and VCG11 do not address conscientiousness explicitly.

**T3.11 — Relational Capacity**

**T3.11.1** — Does this characteristic engage or constitute the relational capacity of the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Yes — and M15-C is the cluster sub-group where relational capacity is most directly engaged. VCG02 (Amos 3:2): divine knowing as election — knowing-as-relationship. VCG04 (Hos 6:3): human pressing on to know God — relational knowing. VCG11 (Gen 4:1): intimate knowing as deepest relational capacity. VCG05: not-knowing God is relational failure.

**T3.11.2** — Is relational knowing itself a mode of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Yes. Relational knowing is not only produced by M15-C; it is M15-C's covenantal core. Ya.da in its fullest range names knowing-as-entering-relationship, not merely knowing-about.

**T3.11.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the relational depth of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG11 establishes the maximum relational depth (intimate union). VCG02 establishes the divine relational depth (elective covenant). VCG04 establishes the human pursuit-orientation. The evidence reveals that M15-C is constitutively relational at its semantic core — ya.da is fundamentally relational knowing.

**M15-C T3 complete. Proceeding to T4.**

#### T4 — Relational Interfaces (6 components, 24 prompts)

**T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human**

**T4.1.1** — What does the evidence reveal about how this characteristic operates in the God-to-human direction?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 (Psa 139:6): God's comprehensive knowing of the human — "you have searched me and known me." The divine-to-human knowing is total, inescapable, and precedes any human act. VCG02 (Amos 3:2): God's elective knowing — "you only have I known of all the families of the earth." God initiates covenant relationship through his knowing.

**T4.1.2** — What initiates the God-to-human movement of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG03 (Exo 7:5): divine initiative — historical redemptive acts generate knowing. God's acts (exodus, crossing, prophetic fulfilment) are the instruments of his self-disclosure. The initiative is entirely divine: not human inquiry but divine self-disclosure.

**T4.1.3** — What is the effect on the human inner being of receiving this characteristic from God?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): to know God is life-orienting. VCG03: the nations knowing that God is the Lord produces both fear and recognition of his sovereignty. VCG12 (Hab 2:14): eschatological knowing-of-God fills the earth as waters — the ultimate constitutive effect on humanity's inner state.

**T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God**

**T4.2.1** — What does the evidence reveal about how this characteristic operates in the human-to-God direction?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): "let us press on to know the Lord" — human pursuit of knowing God. VCG08: Job's assertions of knowing his Redeemer are addressed toward God, framing his sustained argument before God. VCG05 (1Sa 2:12): the absence of knowing-God as characterising those whose relationship with God is broken.

**T4.2.2** — What enables the human-to-God movement of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04: volitional pursuit. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): accumulated knowledge of God's ways. VCG08: sustained faith-conviction. The enabling conditions are volitional-covenantal: pressing on, accumulating, maintaining.

**T4.2.3** — Where the evidence is silent on human-to-God movement, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG10 (practical knowing) and VCG11 (intimate knowing between persons) do not speak to human-to-God movement.

**T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving**

**T4.3.1** — Does this characteristic operate in a human-to-human giving direction?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG03 pattern: Israel's knowing-testimony shapes the nations' knowing. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): "lips of the wise spread knowledge" — human-to-human transmission. VCG08: Job's articulation of conviction before his friends is knowledge-giving in a human context.

**T4.3.2** — What is the form of the giving — teaching, testimony, counsel, or something else?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG06: wisdom-lips as teaching instrument. VCG08: testimony-assertion. VCG03: redemptive-historical testimony shaping communal knowing. The form is consistently testimony and teaching — not mystical impartation.

**T4.3.3** — What inner condition in the giver enables this transmission?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG06: the wise person who has "laid up" knowledge — accumulated inner holding enables transmission. The giver must first possess to transmit.

**T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving**

**T4.4.1** — Does this characteristic operate in a human-to-human receiving direction?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): communal "let us" — receiving as community. VCG03: historical Israel receiving knowing through transmitted events. VCG06: the learner receiving from the wise.

**T4.4.2** — What inner condition enables genuine reception from another human?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04: volitional pursuit. VCG06: the contrast with the fool implies receptivity as the condition: the fool's mouth destroys; the wise person's receptive spirit accumulates. Openness and pursuit are the reception-conditions.

**T4.4.3** — Where the evidence is silent on receiving, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG08 and VCG11 do not speak to human-from-human receiving.

**T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries**

**T4.5.1** — Are there boundaries evidenced in how this characteristic is shared or withheld between persons?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG05 (1Sa 2:12): not knowing the Lord is a relational boundary marker — it separates those in covenant from those who are not. VCG09 (Act 17:30): culpable ignorance as a boundary between former times and present accountability. VCG07: moral knowing as a boundary marker — those who know good and evil from those who do not.

**T4.5.2** — What limits or defines the boundaries of this characteristic in human-human encounter?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Covenantal status is the primary boundary-defining principle for M15-C's relational knowing: those who know the Lord and those who do not. This is not a knowledge-privacy boundary but a covenantal-relational boundary.

**T4.5.3** — Where the evidence is silent on boundaries, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — No verse in M15-C speaks to deliberate concealment or privacy of knowledge (secrecy dynamics). VCG06 implies knowledge is transmitted, not hoarded.

**T4.6 — Spiritual Beings Interface**

**T4.6.1** — Does this characteristic operate in relation to spiritual beings (angels, adversarial beings)?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG01 (Psa 139:6): God's knowing is so comprehensive it encompasses the inner being fully — implicitly contrasting divine knowing with any creaturely knowing. VCG09 (Act 17:30): the times of ignorance — possibly including cosmic powers who operated within the "times" God overlooked.

**T4.6.2** — Is there evidence of spiritual beings affecting, blocking, or transmitting this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG07 (Gen 3): the serpent's role in offering knowledge of good and evil — adversarial transmission of moral knowing. This is the clearest instance of a spiritual being operating in the M15-C domain.

**T4.6.3** — Where the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG01 through VCG06, VCG08–VCG12 are silent on spiritual beings interface except inferentially.

**M15-C T4 complete. Proceeding to T5.**

#### T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension (7 components, 21 prompts)

**T5.1 — Nature of Transformation**

**T5.1.1** — What transformation does this characteristic undergo in the person over time?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): knowing God grows — "press on to know" implies movement. VCG06 (Pro 10:14): knowledge accumulates in the wise over time. VCG08: Job's conviction grows through suffering — the knowledge "I know my Redeemer lives" is a conviction forged through affliction, not received instantly.

**T5.1.2** — Does the characteristic itself transform, or does it transform the person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both: the knowledge itself deepens (VCG08 — conviction grows through testing); and it transforms the person (VCG04 — knowing God orients the whole life). The relationship is mutual-formative.

**T5.1.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the pace or stages of this transformation?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04: "let us press on" implies progressive, effortful movement. VCG08: transformation through suffering is sustained over time. No sudden-formation evidence in M15-C except VCG03 (redemptive-historical events that generate immediate knowing among the nations), which is an exception — divine-initiative knowing can be instantaneous in historical terms.

**T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States**

**T5.2.1** — Is there a typical sequence of inner states associated with this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — For covenantal knowing (VCG04): ignorance/absence → encounter with God's disclosure → volitional pursuit → relational knowing → orientation-formation. For conviction-knowing (VCG08): initial faith → affliction → maintained conviction → vindication anticipated.

**T5.2.2** — What inner state precedes genuine reception of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04: openness and volitional intent ("let us"). VCG09: absence of the "times of ignorance" condition — repentance opens knowing. VCG05: the prior state before election is not-knowing.

**T5.2.3** — What inner state follows sustained possession of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08: sustained conviction enables sustained advocacy even through suffering. VCG04: knowing God produces life-orientation. VCG06: accumulated knowledge produces the wise person's characteristic way of living and speaking.

**T5.3 — Mechanism of Change**

**T5.3.1** — What mechanisms does the evidence reveal for how this characteristic is formed, deepened, or lost?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Formation: divine disclosure through historical events (VCG03); covenantal pursuit (VCG04); wise accumulation (VCG06). Deepening: sustained engagement through difficulty (VCG08). Loss: relational breach — not-knowing as covenant rupture (VCG05).

**T5.3.2** — Is change in this characteristic primarily divine initiative, human effort, or both?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — Both, but distributed differently across VCGs: divine initiative dominates VCG01–VCG03; human effort characterises VCG04, VCG06, VCG08. The cluster picture is: God discloses → human pursues and accumulates → conviction forms.

**T5.3.3** — What does the evidence reveal about resistance to formation or loss of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG05: not-knowing as active resistance (Eli's sons). VCG09: culpable ignorance as sustained resistant condition. Loss through covenant-breach is the primary resistance pattern.

**T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction**

**T5.4.1** — Does suffering engage this characteristic — forming, deepening, or testing it?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08 (Job 19:25): the most direct evidence. Job's knowledge of his Redeemer is forged and sustained through extreme suffering. The conviction is not prior to affliction but is expressed through and tested by it.

**T5.4.2** — Is there evidence of this characteristic being produced by or emerging from suffering?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG08: conviction-knowing is deepened in suffering, though not solely produced by it. The suffering provides the context in which the knowing is tested and becomes explicit.

**T5.4.3** — Where the evidence is silent on suffering's role, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG04, VCG06, VCG10, VCG11, VCG12 do not speak to suffering's role in knowledge-formation.

**T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification**

**T5.5.1** — Does this characteristic participate in the sanctification of the inner person?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04 (Hos 6:3): knowing God is the covenantal orientation of the sanctified life. VCG08: conviction of the Redeemer's existence shapes the person's posture before God throughout affliction. VCG07: moral knowing (knowledge of good and evil) is the cognitive basis for moral discrimination that sanctification requires.

**T5.5.2** — What is the relationship between this characteristic and the formation of Christlikeness?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG04: knowing God presses toward the pattern of the one who fully knows and is fully known (NT: knowing Christ — Phil 3:10 is outside M15-C but the covenantal-knowing VCG carries this trajectory). VCG08: conviction of the Redeemer is proximate to NT knowing of Christ. Inferential but strong.

**T5.5.3** — Where the evidence is silent on sanctification, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** S — VCG10 (practical knowing), VCG11 (intimate knowing), VCG09 (culpable ignorance) do not speak directly to sanctification.

**T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory**

**T5.6.1** — Does this characteristic have an eschatological dimension — moving toward a future fullness?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG12 (Hab 2:14): the most explicit eschatological statement in M15-C. "The earth shall be full of the knowledge of the Lord as the waters cover the sea." This is not present partial knowing but eschatological total knowing.

**T5.6.2** — What is the eschatological form of this characteristic?

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG12: universal, comprehensive knowing of God — filling the earth as waters fill the sea. The image: not selective-covenantal knowing (VCG02) but global-saturating knowing. This is the eschatological telos of M15-C.

**T5.6.3** — Where the evidence is silent on eschatological trajectory, note this explicitly.

**[C — Knowledge]** E — VCG10, VCG11, VCG09 are silent on eschatological trajectory. The practical, intimate, and culpable-ignorance modes do not project eschatologically.

### T7.2.5 — Does any verse function as the primary anchor for this characteristic — the one that most fully and directly expresses its essential character?
