## §1 — Characteristics identified

Reading across all 8 term corpora (263 meanings) and keyword analytics, four distinct inner-being characteristics emerge. The ritual/bodily characteristic exceeds the §8.6 volume gate and is split into two sub-groups by a mechanism-vs-verdict axis. Total sub-groups: **5**.

### C1a — Bodily-contact defilement-state

The inner-being condition of ritual uncleanness acquired through physical contact with a defiling agent — corpse, bodily discharge, carcass, swarming creature, unclean fluid. The state is time-bound (typically until evening or seven days), transmitted through touch, carrying, proximity, or fluid contact, and resolved by washing and waiting. The inner-being content is present even within the ritual register: awareness that contamination has been incurred; the duty of watchful restraint over bodily action; distress at exclusion from worship; conscience responding to inadvertent violation; the social responsibility not to transmit one's state to others. Structural opposite: M12 (purity/wholeness).

**Keyword signature:** `impurity contact` (18), `status ritual` (22), `impurity bodily` (14), `contact transmitted` (4), `contamination spreading` (9), `duration evening` (8), `washing required` (4), `vigilance required` (4), `defilement transmitted` (5).

### C1b — Categorical/classificatory unclean-state

The inner-being condition of uncleanness determined by authoritative examination and verdict (priestly diagnosis), or by inherent categorical designation (animals declared unclean by nature, objects permanently defiled). The state may be permanent (certain materials destroyed; certain diseases chronic), progressive (spreading infection requires escalating verdict), or resolved by examination-and-clearance. The inner-being content: the duty of active moral-spiritual discernment in those who must distinguish clean from unclean (Lev 10:10, 11:47); the experience of enforced communal isolation and public self-declaration (Lev 13:45-46); grief at exclusion from worship (Num 9:7); conscience formed by maintained purity categories (Act 10:14). Structural opposite: M12 (purity/discernment).

**Keyword signature:** `status pronounced` (13), `authority ritual` (11), `examination diagnostic` (4), `contamination categorical` (6), `impurity inherent` (7), `condition persistent` (4), `exclusion communal` (7), `exclusion enforced` (3).

### C2 — Moral-inner defilement-state

Defilement as the inner condition of moral corruption — conscience, will, and heart corrupted by desire, habitual sin, and idolatrous attachment. The locus of defilement is the inner person itself; it is not acquired by contact but arises from volitional defection or persistent moral failure. This is M10c's primary NT register and extends into Hebrew prophetic metaphor. The inner-being content is fully explicit: conscience defiled (1Cor 8:7), body and spirit defiled (2Cor 7:1), impurity as prior master over the will (Rom 6:19), heart allegiance disorder equated with idolatry (Eph 5:5), the mind and conscience of the defiled person (Tit 1:15), the constitutional human condition of moral uncleanness (Job 14:4), the prophet's self-recognition of defiled lips (Isa 6:5), the community's inner corruption rendering worship worthless (Hag 2:14). Structural opposite: M12 (purity/holiness of the inner person).

**Keyword signature:** `will` token (74), `moral` token (41), `conscience` token (27), `heart defiled` (2), `heart` token (18), `defilement inner` (1), `defilement deliberate` (4), `purity demanded` (2), `passion indulged` (1), `conscience defiled` (1).

### C3 — Corporate/covenantal defilement

Defilement extending from persons or community to land, sanctuary, and the divine name — the sacral and relational pollution produced by corporate moral defection. This characteristic includes both the defilement of specific covenantal relationships (sexual betrayal, Nazirite violation, the ordeal law) and the wider prophetic register of idolatry defiling the sanctuary, people's persistent unfaithfulness contaminating sacred space, and reforming kings desecrating idolatrous sites. The inner-being dimension is the corporate will in defection from God, hearts unfaithful in covenant, and the community's inner corruption contaminating what is sacred. Eze 20:43 — the memory of defilement producing self-loathing — shows defilement functioning as the inner mechanism of corporate repentance. Structural opposite: M12 (purity/holiness of sacred space, divine name, covenant relationship).

**Keyword signature:** `sanctuary defiled` (4), `sacred desecrated` (4), `sacred profaned` (3), `idolatry` token (16), `heart unfaithful` (3), `heart rebellious` (2), `boundary sacred` (8), `defilement sexual` (4), `honour violated` (3), `covenant violated` (2).

### C4 — Defilement by external spiritual agency

The inner-being condition of defilement produced by an unclean spirit inhabiting, controlling, and corrupting a person. The mechanism is external — an unclean spiritual force enters and dominates the person's inner and bodily life. The result is a defilement-state in the person: will overridden, faculties suppressed (speech and hearing, Mar 9:25), bodily agency lost (Luk 8:29), communal access severed. The unclean spirits recognise and react to superior divine authority (Mar 3:11), resist expulsion (Act 8:7), and seek to re-inhabit persons once expelled (Luk 11:24). Restoration from this defilement-state is treated as wholeness (Luk 6:18). Structural opposite: M12 (purity/wholeness — the restored state after expulsion).

**Keyword signature:** `spirit unclean` (3), `spirit inhabiting` (2), `spirit afflicting` (2), `spirit restless` (2), `affliction spiritual` (2), `defilement external` (1), `power hostile` (1).
