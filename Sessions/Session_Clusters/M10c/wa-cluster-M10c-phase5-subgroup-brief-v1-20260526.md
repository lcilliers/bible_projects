### Three register-axes (Phase 3 AI observation)

The AI's Phase 3 cross-term observation #3 named three major registers present across multiple terms — these are the natural Phase 5 sub-group candidates:

1. **Ritual/bodily defilement** — foundational sub-register, predominantly Hebrew (H2930A ta.me verb, H2931 ta.me adj, H5079 nid.dah). Mechanisms: contact, category, bodily emission, leprosy, corpse, menstruation, dietary.
2. **Moral-inner defilement** — NT register (all 5 Greek terms) + Hebrew metaphorical extension (Eze 36:17, Isa 64:6, Hag 2:14). Defilement as inner-being state of moral corruption — Mat 15:18-20, Tit 1:15, 2Cor 7:1, 1Cor 8:7.
3. **Corporate/covenantal defilement** — land, sanctuary, name defiled by corporate sin; predominantly prophetic Hebrew (Ezekiel, Jeremiah, Hosea); within ta.me verb corpus.

A possible fourth distinct sub-group:
4. **Defilement by external spiritual agency** — unclean spirits sub-corpus of akathartos (~21V), where defilement is inhabited / controlled by hostile spiritual force. M27 cross-register strong.

These four together cover the corpus's register diversity. Phase 5 may settle on 3 (folding spiritual-agency into ritual or moral) or 4 sub-groups.

## Your task — per sub-group, design characteristics

For each candidate characteristic identified from the meanings, design a sub-group.

### Step 1 — Identify the characteristics

Read across the meaning corpora (§2 of constitution report) and the per-term keyword densities (§5 of analytics report). Name distinct **inner-being faculties / states** M10c's evidence expresses.

For a cluster of M10c's scale (263 verses, 8 terms), **typically 3–5 characteristics** is realistic.

**Candidate characteristics suggested by Phase 3 + keyword analytics:**

- **Ritual/bodily defilement-state** — the inner-being condition produced by contact with what defiles (corpse, leprosy, bodily emission, dietary, menstrual). Predominantly Hebrew foundational sub-register.
- **Moral-inner defilement-state** — defilement as the inner moral condition of corruption (Mat 15, 1Cor 8:7, 2Cor 7:1, Tit 1:15; Hebrew metaphorical extension Eze 36:17, Isa 64:6, Hag 2:14).
- **Corporate/covenantal defilement** — defilement extending from individuals to land, sanctuary, divine name; prophetic Hebrew on corporate sin.
- **Defilement by external spiritual agency** — unclean spirits inhabiting / controlling persons (akathartos sub-corpus). Possibly merged with ritual or kept distinct.
- (alternate axis) **Cleansing-focused defilement** — verses where the cleansing response is the foreground (Psa 51:7, Heb 9:14, Zec 13:1, 2Cor 7:1). Cross-register-rich with M11.

These cover ~3–5 candidates. Refine from the meaning evidence.

### Step 2 — Map each characteristic to its evidence

For each characteristic, note which terms' verses primarily evidence it. Terms will commonly span multiple characteristics (especially ta.me verb at 128V).

### Step 3 — Design sub-groups to carry the characteristics

- **Default:** one sub-group per characteristic. Codes: `M10c-A`, `M10c-B`, etc.
- **Volume-split alert:** if any characteristic captures >105V of the corpus, split that characteristic into multiple sub-groups by a named axis. The ritual/bodily characteristic likely captures the bulk of H2930A ta.me (verb 128V) + H2931 ta.me (adj 66V — bulk of ritual sub-corpus) → potentially >105V. **Volume-split needed if the merged ritual/bodily corpus exceeds 105V.**

Possible split axes for ritual/bodily:
- Mechanism: contact-defilement vs category-classification (animals/objects unclean by nature) vs bodily-emission-defilement
- Source: corpse / leprosy / nid.dah / dietary
- Hebrew vs Greek

Each sub-group's `core_description` names: (a) the characteristic it represents, (b) the split-axis if applicable, (c) the inner-being content.

### Step 4 — Assign every verse to exactly one sub-group

For each of the 263 Pass A meanings, record the sub-group assignment. **Term-level rules + per-verse exceptions** where needed. The 5 Greek terms + nid.dah + akatharsia are small enough to assign at term-level. The big terms (ta.me verb 128V, ta.me adj 66V, akathartos 30V) may need per-verse-range rules.

**Practical note on volume:** 263 verses is feasible for verse-by-verse listing if needed, but term-level rules with targeted per-verse exceptions (especially for ta.me verb which spans all three Hebrew registers) is the standard pattern. Output as JSON for CC's Phase 6 apply.

### Step 5 — No BOUNDARY sub-group

Phase 3 produced 0 BOUNDARY verdicts. No BOUNDARY sub-group.

### Step 6 — Flag SPLIT_SUBGROUP cases

If any sub-group contains VCG-level registers serving different characteristics, flag for Phase 7 attention.

### Step 7 — Multi-faceted terms

- **H2930A ta.me (verb, 128V)** — almost certainly multi-faceted. Spans ritual/bodily + moral-inner (via covenant/idolatry contexts) + corporate/covenantal (sanctuary/land/name defilement).
- **H2931 ta.me (adj, 66V)** — multi-faceted. Spans ritual classification + moral-existential (Job 14:4, Isa 64:6, Hag 2:14).
- **G0169 akathartos (30V)** — multi-faceted if unclean-spirits is split as a distinct sub-group from ritual/moral uses.
- **H5079 nid.dah (24V)** — primarily ritual/bodily but extends to moral-relational (Eze 36:17) and shame-related uses (Lam 1:17).

These multi-faceted terms get primary + secondary records.
