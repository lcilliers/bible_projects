## VCG design — M10c-D (5 VCGs)

### M10c-D-VCG-01 — Sexual and covenantal-relational defilement

**context_description:** The defilement-state produced by sexual violations of covenantal bonds — adultery, bestiality, violation of a person, incestuous or transgressive marriage, and the elaborated ordeal-of-jealousy law governing suspected adultery. The inner-being content is the will's moral defection in sexual terms, producing an objective defilement-state in the person and their relationship that exists independently of human detection (Num 5:13 — adultery defiles even without witnesses), carries objective bodily consequences (Num 5:27 — the ordeal water produces physical judgment where defilement is real), and operates across generations (Eze 18:6/11/15 — the righteous man consistently refuses, the wicked consistently transgresses, regardless of family heritage). The violation of the nearest relational bonds is the shared feature: neighbour's wife, daughter-in-law, sister, a man's own wife through betrayal (Gen 34). Niddah boundary violation is included here because Lev 18:19 treats it as a moral transgression of covenant not a mere ritual oversight, and Eze 22:10 presents violation of women in menstrual impurity as compounded defilement of the relational bond. Cross-register: M12 (purity as the positive state of covenant fidelity that sexual defilement violates); M10 (sin-act of sexual transgression as the generative act producing the defilement-state).

### M10c-D-VCG-02 — Idolatrous self-defilement

**context_description:** The defilement-state produced by the will's pursuit of idolatrous attachment — the community defiling itself through habitual devotion to false gods, spiritual whoredom with the nations, child sacrifice, and the deliberate installation of abominations in the place of worship. The inner-being content is the will's self-directed corruption through idolatrous pursuit: desire redirected toward idols the eyes feast on (Eze 20:7), inherited temptation requiring active refusal (Eze 20:18), persistent self-defilement closing relational access with God (Eze 20:31), the city's deliberate self-directed self-defilement (Eze 22:3-4), and the sisters' defilement through every idol they lust after (Eze 23:7). The reforming king's intentional defilement of idolatrous sites (2Ki 23) represents the inverse — the instrument of defilement wielded against false worship as an act of iconoclastic purification. The corporate idolatrous wealth and ornament becoming sources of defilement (Eze 7:19-20) shows the transformation of the good into defilement through pride-driven misuse. Cross-register: M10b (idolatry as the abomination-register — the moral-character judgement on idolatrous practice); M10 (sin-act of idolatry as the generative act); M29 (desire as vehicle of idolatrous self-defilement); M12 (holiness/purity as the state the self-defiled will has abandoned).

### M10c-D-VCG-03 — Sanctuary and sacred-space defilement

**context_description:** Defilement inflicted on or threatening the sanctuary — by enemy invasion destroying the sacred order (Psa 79:1), by the community's deliberate installation of idols and abominations in God's house (Jer 7:30, Jer 32:34, 2Ch 36:14 as the corporate fruit of habitual defection), by defiling God's sanctuary with detestable abominations severing the covenantal bond (Eze 5:11), by divine judgment using defilement as an instrument (Eze 9:7 — filling the courts with the slain), by compounding sanctuary and Sabbath desecration (Eze 23:38), and by the promise that in the restored order the holy name will no longer be defiled through whoring and association with death (Eze 43:7-8). Priestly vocational purity — guarding the sacred space by avoiding contact with death (Eze 44:25) — is included here as the institutional expression of the sanctuary-protection imperative. Cross-register: M12 (holiness of the sacred space as what defilement violates and what restoration restores); M10 (sin-act of idolatrous installation as the defilement-producing act); M10b (abomination as the moral-character quality of what is installed in the sacred space).

### M10c-D-VCG-04 — Land and communal-space defilement

**context_description:** Defilement extending from persons or community to the land itself — the moral-sacral pollution of shared inhabitable space through sin, bloodshed, and persistent moral defection. The land must not be defiled because God dwells among the people in it (Num 35:34). Leaving an executed body overnight defiles the land (Deu 21:23). The eastern tribes' land is unclean relative to the tabernacle's location (Jos 22:19). The people's entry into the gift-land brought defilement through abominable practices (Jer 2:7). The people's habitual conduct defiled the land they lived in — their ways as morally repugnant as menstrual impurity before God (Eze 36:17 × 2 terms). Idolatrous practice defiled the land and provoked divine wrath (Eze 36:18). The people's sin makes the land no place for rest (Mic 2:10). Disconnection from covenant relationship makes even sustenance spiritually contaminating (Hos 9:4). The inner-being content is the community's moral disposition expressed outwardly in persistent corrupting behaviour that renders the space they inhabit incompatible with divine habitation. Cross-register: M12 (purity/holiness of the land as what is being violated); M10 (sin-act as the generator of land-defilement).

### M10c-D-VCG-05 — Exile, judgment consequences, and corporate cleansing promise

**context_description:** Verses where the consequences of corporate defilement are projected outward into the physical and social condition of the people — expulsion, exile, judgment — or where the promise of divine cleansing reverses the defilement-state. Defiling persons removed from the camp because God dwells there (Num 5:3 — divine-presence incompatibility). The Nazirite's consecrated dedication is violated and nullified by corpse-proximity, requiring restarting (Num 6:7/9/12 — defilement nullifying consecration). Jerusalem designated a filthy thing among the nations (Lam 1:17). The exiles cried "Unclean! Away!" — defilement leading to total relational severance (Lam 4:15). Eating unclean bread in exile as the divinely ordained consequence of prior defilement (Eze 4:13), contrasted with the prophet's lifelong maintained purity (Eze 4:14). Exile to unclean land as judgment on the prophet-silencer (Amo 7:17). Unclean food as the consequence of exile from the holy land (Hos 9:3). The promise that the restored people will no longer defile themselves with idols (Eze 37:23). The promise of eschatological purification: a fountain for cleansing to remove sin and impurity (Zec 13:1). The failure of priests to teach clean/unclean discernment as a collapse of the community's inner formation (Eze 22:26). Priests instructed to teach the people to discern the unclean from the clean (Eze 44:23). The city's name declared defiled among the nations — inner corruption becoming publicly visible (Eze 22:5). Houses of idolatrous offering declared defiled like Topheth (Jer 19:13). Cross-register: M11 (cleansing as the promised response — Eze 37:23, Zec 13:1); M07 (corporate shame and expulsion — Lam 1:17, Lam 4:15, Eze 22:5); M12 (purity/holiness as the restored state the cleansing promise aims at).
