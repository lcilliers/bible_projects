## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across all four characteristics, the cluster reveals God's nature through a single convergent logic: every form of defilement is what God is not, and the revelation of his holiness is what makes the defilement visible. Characteristic 1 surfaces this through the incompatibility of the ritual state with divine habitation (Lev 15:31 — "lest they die in their uncleanness by defiling my tabernacle") and through the positive mirroring of Lev 11:44 ("I am holy... you shall not defile yourselves"). Characteristic 2 surfaces it through the moral standard that exposes the condition: God's holiness as the revealing force at Isa 6:5 and the divine calling defined as holiness at 1Th 4:7. Characteristic 3 extends the revelation to the sacral relational extensions of God's person — his name (Eze 43:7), his sanctuary (Eze 5:11), his land (Num 35:34) — showing that defilement harms what God has extended of himself into the covenant community's life. Characteristic 4 adds the dimension of authority: the spirits' involuntary prostration before Christ (Mar 3:11) reveals God as the sovereign before whom every defiling force must bow. The cluster as a whole thus surfaces a God whose holiness is positively expressed as authority (Char 4), as sacral personal extension (Char 3), as moral-calling standard (Char 2), and as categorical cleanness (Char 1) — and the defilement cluster is the negative space that makes all four dimensions visible.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's pattern on divine attribution is 3 S : 1 E — three characteristics explicitly absent from God's possession (Chars 1, 2, 4) and one singular exception (Char 3: Eze 20:26 — God "defiled them through their very gifts" as a judicial act). The three S characteristics reveal defilement as a creaturely condition that God cannot bear, does not generate, and actively opposes. The one E instance (Char 3) is analytically distinctive: it is judicial, not constitutive — God does not possess the corporate-covenantal defilement-state but deploys it as an instrument of punitive devastation against a community that has itself willfully defiled. The cluster pattern reveals: defilement is constitutively alien to God, but God can wield it as judgment — the condition that stands against divine holiness can be turned by divine sovereignty into an instrument of divine wrath. This judicial deployment is unique to Characteristic 3 precisely because that characteristic is the one whose domain is the covenantal community's shared life before God, the relationship in which judicial response takes its most direct form.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's combined silence on divine possession reveals a differentiated picture of the characteristic's place in the divine image. Characteristic 1's silence places the defilement-state within the creaturely domain of finitude and mortality — God cannot bear the condition because he is without body and without limitation by death; the silence locates the human person's susceptibility as part of their created embodied constitution. Characteristic 2's silence places the defilement-state as the marker of image-disruption — the divine image in the person is in inverse proportion to its presence. Characteristic 3's near-silence (with the Eze 20:26 exception) places the condition as belonging to the gap between covenantal calling and covenantal betrayal — it requires both the covenantal relationship and the community's departure from it. Characteristic 4's silence locates the condition as categorically alien to the divine nature, belonging to the adversarial-creaturely domain. Across the cluster, the silence progressively confirms that defilement is a multi-dimensional marker of creaturely distance from God — bodily-mortal (Char 1), moral-interior (Char 2), covenantal-relational (Char 3), and adversarial-spiritual (Char 4).

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster reveals a sharp asymmetry in creational purpose across the four characteristics. Characteristic 1 is the only one with an evidenced creational purpose: the discipline of managing the ritual defilement-state — washing, waiting, examination, maintaining the clean/unclean boundary (Lev 11:47, 10:10) — is explicitly formative, equipping the person for sacral stewardship and training the inner person in watchful restraint and conscience-responsiveness (Lev 11:44 — "be holy, for I am holy"). Characteristics 2, 3, and 4 are each S on creational purpose: all three are consistently framed as departures from the divine calling or the proper creaturely state, not as designed features for human formation. The asymmetry reveals that within the M10c cluster, only the ritual-defilement register has a positive creational function — it is a feature of the created sacral order, not simply a fallen condition. Characteristics 2, 3, and 4 belong to the fall's domain (moral defection, covenantal betrayal, adversarial spiritual attack). The cluster thus shows the defilement concept operating across both creation (Char 1 as designed sacral structure) and fall (Chars 2, 3, 4 as conditions of departed or invaded humanity).

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's four characteristics distribute across a spectrum from created-order to fallen-condition with a nuanced middle position. Characteristic 1 sits at the ambiguous boundary: bodily-discharge defilement (Lev 12, 15 — VCG-05) attaches to natural physiological processes not marked as sinful, suggesting a created-order feature; death-defilement (M10c-A-VCG-07) intersects with the fallen condition since death is the consequence of sin. Characteristic 2 is clearly post-fall: Job 14:4 ("who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean?") names the inherited condition of fallen humanity; Rom 6:19 describes slavery entered, not a created design. Characteristic 3 is clearly post-fall and covenantal: it presupposes the prior covenantal relationship as the context within which betrayal occurs — the defilement is the product of specific acts of apostasy by a community already in covenant. Characteristic 4 is clearly post-fall: the adversarial spiritual powers operate in the domain of opposition to God's order. The cluster as a whole thus positions defilement as (a) partially within the created-order at the embodied-ritual level (Char 1's physiological sub-register), (b) a consequence of the fall at the individual moral-inner level (Char 2), (c) a consequence of the fall at the corporate-covenantal level (Char 3), and (d) a consequence of the fall at the adversarial-spiritual level (Char 4).

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All four characteristics are E on future-fullness orientation, but the form of the orientation differs significantly across them. Characteristic 1's orientation is the prescribed cleansing provisions themselves — the standing remedy (Num 19:9) and the NT's eschatological revision of the category (Act 10:14/28 — "what God has made clean, do not call unclean"), signalling that the ritual defilement categories point toward an unrestricted access to divine presence. Characteristic 2's orientation is explicitly eschatological: Rev 3:4 (walking with Christ in white), Rev 14:4 (firstfruits following the Lamb), 2Cor 7:1 (bringing holiness to completion) — each pointing toward the consummated holiness from which the defilement-state separates the person. Characteristic 3's orientation is the most extensively developed: three OT prophetic eschatological texts (Eze 37:23, Isa 52:1, Zec 13:1) each promise a future state of permanent non-defilement and covenant restoration. Characteristic 4's orientation is through the expulsion-and-restoration pattern, with Mar 9:25's command "never enter him again" pointing toward a final state of permanent exemption from the characteristic. The cluster as a whole is uniformly oriented toward M12 (purity/wholeness) as its eschatological fullness, but the specific form of that fullness varies: covenant-access completeness (Char 1), personal moral holiness (Char 2), corporate non-defilement in the restored covenant (Char 3), and communal freedom from adversarial spiritual inhabitation (Char 4).

Characteristics 2, 3, and 4 express the image negatively — through its disruption. **T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely condition?**

The cluster as a whole positions the M10c domain as a specifically creaturely domain — the human person (and the covenant community) is uniquely susceptible to defilement across all four registers in ways that God is not. The unclean spirits' recognition of Christ's identity (Mar 3:11 — "You are the Son of God") is the most direct expression: even the adversarial agents who produce Characteristic 4 acknowledge that the divine person cannot be defiled by them.

The cluster together reveals the divine image as a multi-dimensional reality that can be disrupted at the bodily-creaturely level (Char 1), the moral-interior level (Char 2), the communal-vocational level (Char 3), and the volitional-agency level (Char 4) — each register carrying its own diagnostic of the image's condition.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The four names collectively reveal the M10c cluster's governing architecture. Each name encodes a different locus of the defilement-condition: *Ritual defilement-state* — locus is the person's sacral-cultic status, defined by bodily mechanism and authoritative verdict. *Moral-inner defilement-state* — locus is the interior of the person, conscience, will, and heart corrupted from within. *Corporate/covenantal defilement* — locus is the sacred relational order the community inhabits, extending from inner will outward to land, sanctuary, and divine name. *Defilement by external spiritual agency* — locus is the person's inner constitution as invaded and dominated by an external purposeful force. The four names together map the defilement concept across its full creaturely domain: from bodily-sacral status (Char 1) through individual moral-interior (Char 2) through corporate-relational environment (Char 3) through external-spiritual invasion (Char 4). All four share the term "defilement" — naming a condition, not an act. All four name a state the person or community is in, not something they do. This stative core is the cluster's definitional constant.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's vocabulary reveals a unified root with differentiated deployment. The ta.me root (H2930A verb + H2931 adj) is shared across Characteristics 1, 2, and 3 — the same Hebrew root carrying bodily-ritual defilement (Char 1), inner moral defilement (Char 2), and corporate-covenantal defilement (Char 3). The Greek akathartos (G0169) is shared across Characteristics 1, 2, and 4: in Char 1's NT register (Act 10:14 — categorical conscience), in Char 2's moral-inner register (2Cor 6:17 — "touch no unclean thing"), and in Char 4 exclusively as the modifier of pneuma ("unclean spirit"). This produces the cluster's most analytically significant vocabulary finding: G0169 akathartos operates in three registers simultaneously — as a conscience-held categorical conviction (Char 1), as a moral-inner condition descriptor (Char 2), and as a spiritual agent character-descriptor (Char 4) — showing that the same Greek term addresses the sacral, moral, and spiritual dimensions of defilement without differentiation. The ta.me root carries the OT's integrative weight across all three OT-dominant characteristics. Characteristic 4 alone is exclusively Greek, confirming its register as the NT's distinctive contribution to the defilement domain.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All four characteristics carry all three types of implication, but the specific content of each differs in ways that reveal the cluster's internal structure. Directionally: Characteristics 1 and 2 move toward the interior (Char 1 — toward restriction and remedy; Char 2 — inward from surface to conscience/will/heart core). Characteristics 3 and 4 move in opposite directions: Char 3 moves centrifugally outward (from inner corporate will to land, sanctuary, name); Char 4 moves from outside inward (external spiritual invasion entering the person). The cluster thus contains both an inward movement (Chars 1, 2) and outward-centrifugal (Char 3) and inward-invasion (Char 4) directions — revealing defilement as capable of moving in multiple constitutional directions simultaneously. Relationally: all four are constitutively relational but at different relational levels (bodily-sacral community for Char 1, individual-to-divine for Char 2, corporate-covenantal for Char 3, adversarial-spiritual for Char 4). Constitutionally: Chars 1, 2, and 4 each affect the whole person (body, soul, spirit) in different proportions; Char 3 affects both individual and community and extends to the environment (land, sanctuary, name).

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All four characteristics converge on condition as their primary classification — a state the person or community is in, not an act performed or a disposition held. The stative vocabulary is consistent: H2931 ta.me adj (unclean as a predicate), G0167 akatharsia (impurity as noun/state), echōn pneuma akatharton (having an unclean spirit — stative participial). However, the relationship between the condition and the person's own volitional engagement differs markedly across the four. Characteristic 1: condition at the perimeter of the will (the will engages with the condition through response — seeking remedy or refusing it — but does not generate the condition). Characteristic 2: condition generated by the will's own desire and habituation, then entrenching itself as a prior master over the will (Rom 6:19 — "slaves to impurity"). Characteristic 3: condition generated by the corporate will's defection and extending outward, with the will as the primary generating agent (Eze 23:7 — "she defiled herself with all the idols of everyone after whom she lusted"). Characteristic 4: condition imposed entirely without volitional cooperation, the will overridden rather than engaged or corrupted. The cluster reveals condition as the shared primary category and volitional engagement as the differentiating variable: from will-engaged-at-perimeter (Char 1), to will-self-generating (Char 2), to will-corporately-generating-externally-extending (Char 3), to will-completely-bypassed (Char 4).

## Appendix — Synthesis Prose

### Theme 1 — The Defilement Domain as a Unified Lexical Architecture

The four characteristics of the M10c cluster are not four separate conditions — they are four registers of one condition named by one lexical architecture. The Hebrew ta.me root (verb H2930A, adjective H2931) and the Greek akathartos/katharos root together constitute the cluster's vocabulary across all four registers: the same root names the defilement of ritual-bodily status (Characteristic 1), the defilement of the moral-inner constitution (Characteristic 2), the defilement of the corporate-covenantal sacred environment (Characteristic 3), and the character of the external adversarial agent that produces a defilement-condition in the person (Characteristic 4).

The most analytically significant expression of this unity is the three-register deployment of G0169 akathartos: the same Greek adjective names the person's ritual status (Act 10:14/28 — "common or unclean" — Characteristic 1), the inner moral condition from which the divine calling moves the person (2Cor 6:17 — "touch no unclean thing" — Characteristic 2), and the essential character of the adversarial spiritual agent (pneuma akatharton — "unclean spirit" — Characteristic 4). One word, three registers, one concept: incompatibility with holiness operating across the bodily, moral-inner, and adversarial-spiritual dimensions simultaneously.

The H5079 nid.dah term provides the cluster's most revealing bridge between registers. This term names the paradigm-case of bodily ritual defilement — menstrual impurity, the foundational physiological defilement-state — in Characteristic 1's Levitical corpus (Lev 12, 15). In Characteristic 3's Ezekiel corpus, the same term becomes the vehicle of divine moral revulsion: "their ways before me were like the uncleanness of a woman in her menstrual impurity" (Eze 36:17). The bodily paradigm becomes the metaphorical language for corporate moral repugnance before God. The vocabulary thus performs a conceptual bridge: what the body's most intimate physiological state teaches about defilement-as-incompatibility-with-holiness, the prophets apply to the community's collective moral conduct. The lexical architecture is not merely a verbal coincidence — it encodes the conceptual unity of the domain.

### Theme 2 — The Constitutional Depth Gradient

The M10c cluster reveals a constitutional depth gradient across its four characteristics that is one of the most analytically significant findings of the entire Phase 9 work. The gradient runs from the most surface-level constitutional engagement to the deepest:

Characteristic 1 operates primarily at the bodily-mechanism and social-sacral level. The defilement-state is acquired through bodily events (carcass-contact, discharge, skin disease, corpse proximity) and has its primary consequences at the level of social-sacral standing (exclusion from worship, community, sacred meals). The spirit level is not explicitly engaged.

Characteristic 3 operates at the corporate soul level and extends outward to the sacred environment. The defilement-state originates in the community's collective will, desire, and heart allegiance — soul-level faculties of the corporate body — and extends centrifugally outward to contaminate land, sanctuary, and divine name. The spirit level is not explicitly engaged.

Characteristic 2 penetrates from the soul level to the spirit level. The defilement-state originates at the soul's desiderative level (desire and will — Rom 1:24, Eph 4:19) but penetrates downward to the spirit level: 2Cor 7:1 explicitly locates the defilement "of body and spirit" — confirming that the characteristic has reached the person's deepest constitutional level from within.

Characteristic 4 arrives directly at the spirit level from outside. The unclean spirit's invasion bypasses the soul's volitional engagement entirely, operating at the person's deepest constitutional level from the outset. This is why Luk 8:29's chains and shackles fail comprehensively — they address the body, the lowest constitutional level, while the cause operates at the highest. Only divine authority at the spirit level (commanding the spirit) reverses the condition.

This depth gradient illuminates the cluster's internal logic: the characteristics that are hardest to resolve are the deepest. Characteristic 1's resolution is prescribed and achievable through bodily remedies (washing, waiting, water of impurity). Characteristic 2's resolution requires transformation of the inner person across both soul and spirit levels — a sustained process of cleansing, repentance, and reorientation (2Cor 7:1 — "bringing holiness to completion in the fear of God"). Characteristic 4's resolution requires divine authority that operates at the spirit level — the only power capable of commanding the adversarial spirit. The depth gradient is thus simultaneously a difficulty gradient: what reaches deepest requires the most comprehensive resolution.

### Theme 3 — The Volitional Engagement Spectrum

The four characteristics form a spectrum of volitional engagement that runs from will-at-perimeter to will-completely-bypassed — one of the cluster's most analytically productive findings:

Characteristic 1 (will at perimeter): the defilement-state arrives through bodily mechanism without volitional cooperation in most sub-registers. The will's engagement is at the perimeter — in avoidance discipline (Lev 11:43-44 — choosing not to touch the defiling agent), in remedy-seeking (Num 19:19-20 — presenting for sprinkling), and in the decisive moral fault of willful refusal of remedy (Num 19:20 — excision from the assembly). The condition itself is non-volitional in most cases; the person's moral accountability is in the response.

Characteristic 2 (will self-generating): the defilement-state is produced by the person's own volitional and dispositional life. The will is the primary generating force (Eph 4:19 — "given themselves up to sensuality"), and the condition progressively binds the very will that generated it (Rom 6:19 — "slaves to impurity"). The will is both the cause and the casualty.

Characteristic 3 (will corporately-generating): the defilement-state is produced by the community's sustained, deliberate corporate defection. The corporate will is fully functional and actively choosing wrongly (Eze 22:3 — "a city that makes idols to defile herself"; Jer 7:30 — "set their detestable things... to defile it"). This is the most volitionally engaged characteristic in the cluster — the defilement is the product of purposeful, conscientious corporate moral agency directed toward idolatrous desecration.

Characteristic 4 (will completely bypassed): the defilement-state arrives without any volitional participation. The spirit's invasion overrides the person's will entirely — "driven by the demon into the desert" (Luk 8:29). The will exists but cannot express itself. This is the only M10c characteristic where the person bears no moral accountability for the condition's arrival.

This spectrum has direct implications for how each characteristic relates to guilt and moral responsibility. Characteristics 2 and 3 are the conditions of moral guilt — the person and community are responsible for generating their own defilement through volitional defection. Characteristic 1 carries moral accountability at the perimeter (for the response) but not necessarily for the condition itself. Characteristic 4 carries no moral guilt for the condition — the person is a victim, not a moral agent, in relation to the characteristic's arrival. The cluster thus provides Scripture's most nuanced account of the relationship between human will and the defilement-condition: will can generate defilement (Chars 2, 3), engage it at the perimeter without generating it (Char 1), or be completely bypassed by it (Char 4).

### Theme 4 — The Relational Theology of the M10c Cluster

The cluster's most consistent finding across all 189 prompts and all four characteristics is a unified relational theology: defilement = isolation; restoration = relational recovery. This pattern appears in every register without exception.

Characteristic 1: the defilement-state excludes the person from worship (Num 9:7 — "kept from bringing the Lord's offering"), from communal residence (Lev 13:46 — "his dwelling shall be outside the camp"), and from sacred meals (Lev 7:21 — "cut off from among his people"). Restoration — through washing, waiting, priestly examination, and the water of impurity — restores these relational bonds: the person is re-admitted to worship, community, and sacred participation.

Characteristic 2: the defilement-state severs the vertical relational bond with God (Eph 5:5 — "has no inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God") and distorts the horizontal relational bond with others through concealment (Mat 23:27 — hidden inner defilement behind an acceptable exterior). The positive-inverse at Rev 14:4 — "they follow the Lamb wherever he goes" — shows the undefiled state as defined by the most intimate possible relational following of God.

Characteristic 3: the defilement-state severs every relational bond in the covenant community's life — the marital bond (Num 5 ordeal), the divine-covenantal bond (Eze 5:11 — divine withdrawal; Eze 20:31 — refused inquiry), the community-land bond (Mic 2:10 — no rest), the community-sanctuary bond (Eze 5:11), and the community-nations bond (Lam 1:17 — "a filthy thing among them"). The eschatological restoration restores the covenant formula — "they shall be my people, and I will be their God" (Eze 37:23) — the most fundamental relational bond.

Characteristic 4: the defilement-state severs every immediate relational bond — communal belonging (Mar 5:2 — "utterly cut off from clean, communal life"), family bond (Luk 9:42 — the boy must be "given back to his father"), and worship participation (Mar 1:23 — the synagogue disrupted). The healing at Luk 9:42 — "healed the boy, and gave him back to his father" — names the relational restoration as the primary feature of the characteristic's resolution.

The relational theology of M10c is not incidental — it is structural. Defilement is constitutively anti-relational: every register of defilement isolates the person or community from the relationships that constitute their human identity (with God, with community, with family, with land). Restoration is constitutively relational: every register of restoration repairs the severed bond. The M10c domain is fundamentally about the conditions that make genuine relationship with God and with others impossible, and the conditions that restore it.

### Theme 5 — The Cluster's Position in the Programme's Cross-Cluster Ecology

The M10c cluster's four open observations all confirmed at T6 reveal its position in the broader inner-being landscape as a nexus cluster — a cluster constituted by its relationships to adjacent clusters rather than standing alone.

The upstream adjacencies (confirmed by INTEGRATION_NOTEs #250 and the general M10 relationship): M10 (sin-act) produces Characteristics 2 and 3 — defilement is downstream from sin. M29 (desire) is the vehicle through which Characteristic 2's defilement is generated. M27 (cosmic-evil agents) produces Characteristic 4 — defilement by external spiritual agency is downstream from the adversarial spiritual beings' action.

The downstream adjacencies: M11 (Cleansing/Restoration) is the universal downstream product of every M10c register — every form of defilement calls cleansing into operation. M07 (Shame) is a secondary downstream product of Characteristics 1 and 3 in their stigma-and-exclusion sub-registers.

The structural opposite: M12 (Purity/Holiness) — every M10c characteristic is constitutively defined against M12. The most significant structural observation: several of the cluster's primary anchor verses (Lev 11:44, 2Cor 7:1) simultaneously anchor M10c characteristics and M12 findings, confirming that M10c and M12 are co-defined — each requires the other for definition.

The co-extensive adjacencies (confirmed by INTEGRATION_NOTEs #248 and #249): M27 is the co-extensive lens for Characteristic 4's entire 21-verse corpus — the same events are simultaneously M10c (defilement-state in person) and M27 (agent's nature). M10b (Wickedness/Abomination) is the co-extensive lens for Characteristic 3's Ezekiel idolatry corpus — the same acts are simultaneously M10c (defilement-condition produced) and M10b (abomination moral-character assessment).

The programme's cross-cluster ecology of the M10c domain can be summarised: M10 (sin-act) + M29 (desire) + M27 (cosmic-evil agents) → M10c (defilement-state across four registers) → M11 (cleansing/restoration) + M07 (shame/stigma) → M12 (purity/holiness as structural fullness). M10b (abomination) co-characterises Characteristic 3 from a different analytical angle. M10c is not an isolated cluster but is the condition-domain that receives the results of sin (M10), desire (M29), and adversarial spiritual agency (M27), and calls forth cleansing (M11), while being constitutively defined against holiness (M12).

### Theme 6 — The Eschatological Orientation of the Entire Cluster

One of the cluster's most important findings is the unanimous eschatological orientation of all four characteristics: every register of defilement is designed to end. The cluster's design is toward a future state of permanent non-defilement.

Characteristic 1's eschatological orientation is the least developed but is present in the red-heifer provision as a "sin offering" (Num 19:9 — pointing toward a cleansing economy beyond what the ritual can achieve) and in the NT categorical revision (Act 10:28 — "what God has made clean, do not call unclean" — an eschatological declaration that the old-covenant categorical system is being transformed by the inbreaking of the new).

Characteristic 2's eschatological orientation is the most explicitly NT: Rev 3:4 (walking with Christ in white), Rev 14:4 (firstfruits for God and the Lamb following the Lamb wherever he goes), and the "Way of Holiness" from which the unclean are excluded (Isa 35:8). The present moral-inner defilement-state is oriented toward an eschatological fullness of holiness — a permanent state in which the characteristic has no domain.

Characteristic 3's eschatological orientation is the most developed and the most textually explicit: three major prophetic texts address the future non-defilement of the covenant community directly. Eze 37:23 — "they shall not defile themselves anymore... I will save them from all the backslidings... and will cleanse them; and they shall be my people, and I will be their God" — permanent non-defilement as the mark of the restored covenant community and the restoration of the covenant formula. Isa 52:1 — the eschatological holy city from which no defilement is admitted. Zec 13:1 — the divine cleansing fountain opened to cleanse sin and uncleanness on that day. Characteristic 3 is the cluster's most eschatologically developed register precisely because the corporate/covenantal defilement is the most comprehensive and the most institutionally embedded — its resolution requires the most comprehensive eschatological divine action.

Characteristic 4's eschatological orientation is expressed through Mar 9:25's command "never enter him again" — pointing toward a final state of permanent exemption from the characteristic — and through the Matthean warning (Mat 12:43/Luk 11:24) about the spirit finding no rest after expulsion, implying an eschatological state in which the spirit finds no habitation at all, its domain permanently eliminated.

The cluster's unified eschatological vision is the four-register positive fullness: unrestricted access to divine presence (Char 1 — the barriers of the ritual system overcome), permanent holiness of the individual inner person (Char 2 — the completion of the present arc of cleansing), permanent non-defilement of the corporate covenant community (Char 3 — "they shall not defile themselves anymore"), and a community in which no unclean spirit can inhabit any person (Char 4). This fourfold eschatological fullness is M12 in its complete expression — the positive state that M10c's defilement domain is oriented toward and that defines the cluster's ultimate purpose: to name what must be definitively overcome for the fullness of the new creation to be realised.
