## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Characteristic 4 (Defilement by external spiritual agency) reveals God's character primarily through the dynamics of authority and restoration. The characteristic reveals what God is not (the source of defiling agency) and what God does (exercise sovereign authority over defiling agents and restore the defiled person). Mar 3:11 (M10c-E-VCG-03): unclean spirits "fell down before him and cried out, 'You are the Son of God'" — the spirits' recognition of divine identity reveals God as the one before whom every defiling spiritual force is compelled to bow. Luk 4:36 (VCG-03): "with authority and power he commands the unclean spirits, and they come out!" — God's authority over the defiling agents is sovereign and effective. Luk 6:18 (VCG-02): "those who were troubled with unclean spirits were cured" — restoration of wholeness is the divine act. The characteristic reveals a God whose authority is supreme over all defiling forces and whose disposition toward the defiled person is one of restorative power.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — The 21-verse corpus does not attribute the defilement-by-external-spiritual-agency condition to God. God is not a subject of this characteristic; he is its sovereign counterforce — the one whose authority commands the unclean spirits and whose power restores the defiled person. The absence of divine attribution confirms the characteristic as a condition that God opposes and reverses, not one he possesses or generates.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The silence confirms that defilement by external spiritual agency is categorically alien to the divine nature. God is holy; the unclean spirits recognise this by their response to Jesus (Mar 3:11 — falling before him, confessing "Son of God"). The silence suggests the characteristic belongs entirely to the adversarial-creaturely domain: it arises from the action of spiritual beings in opposition to God's holiness, affecting human persons who are vulnerable to this external force. The characteristic's place in the divine image is entirely negative: it is the disruption of the image-bearer's proper constitution by an alien spiritual power.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — The 21-verse corpus does not evidence a creational purpose for defilement by external spiritual agency. The characteristic is presented throughout as an affliction. Those troubled by unclean spirits are "cured" (Luk 6:18, VCG-02) — the language of cure presupposes a prior state of wholeness from which the condition is a departure. Mat 10:1 (VCG-03) frames the characteristic as something requiring "authority... to cast them out" — an opposition to be overcome. No creational purpose for the characteristic is evidenced.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design or a response to the fallen condition?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence strongly indicates this characteristic is a response to the fallen condition, not part of the original created design. The unclean spirits are "hostile, morally corrupting powers" (Mat 10:1 VCG-03) operating in opposition to divine order. Luk 8:29 (VCG-01): the spirit "had repeatedly seized the man, overpowering all human restraint" — the domination is experienced as an intrusion on the person's proper self-possession. The entire framework of the 21-verse corpus assumes that the defiled state is an aberration requiring correction. The characteristic belongs to the domain of adversarial spiritual opposition that operates in a fallen world.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes. The expulsion-and-restoration register (VCG-02) consistently points toward the restored state as the positive fullness. Luk 6:18 (VCG-02): "those who were troubled with unclean spirits were cured" — the healing points toward the wholeness that is the characteristic's structural opposite (M12). Luk 9:42 (VCG-01): "healed the boy, and gave him back to his father" — the restoration of the person to the family relationship pictures the positive fullness. Mat 12:43/Luk 11:24 (VCG-02): the spirit's post-expulsion restlessness implicitly points to a future state where such spirits find no access to any person — the eschatological fullness is a community in which the characteristic has no domain.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic expresses the divine image negatively — through its disruption. The image-bearing capacities violated are: will (Luk 8:29 — "repeatedly seized him, overpowering all human restraint"), speech and hearing (Mar 9:25 — "mute and deaf spirit"), and communal belonging (Mar 5:2 — "utterly cut off from clean, communal life"). These are precisely the faculties through which image-bearing operates. The characteristic reveals the divine image negatively by showing what is lost when an external spiritual force dominates the person.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely condition?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — Exclusively a creaturely condition. God is not inhabited, controlled, or defiled by unclean spirits; he is their sovereign authority before whom they bow (Mar 3:11, VCG-03). The characteristic presupposes a vulnerable creaturely person who can be invaded and dominated by an external spiritual force — a vulnerability that does not apply to God.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The presence of defilement by external spiritual agency reveals the divine image as disrupted and occupied by an alien power: will overridden (Luk 8:29), communicative faculties suppressed (Mar 9:25), person's proper selfhood dominated by the spirit's agenda. The image-bearing functions (willing, communicating, relating) are not destroyed but overridden. The absence of the characteristic — or its reversal through expulsion — reveals the image functioning: the person restored to their own will, faculties returned, and communal access restored (Luk 9:42 — "gave him back to his father").

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes. The pattern of unclean spirit possession, divine authority expelling the spirit, and the person restored — this narrative arc functions typologically for the NT's presentation of the kingdom of God. Mat 10:1 (VCG-03): authority over unclean spirits is paired with healing, framing it as a sign of the kingdom's arrival. Mar 3:11 (VCG-03) — the spirits' confession "You are the Son of God" — functions typologically as the cosmic acknowledgment of Christ's identity. The expulsion narratives function as enacted parables of the kingdom's defeat of the adversarial powers that hold humanity captive.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The direction is from the present historical instance toward the eschatological-divine antitype. The individual possession-and-expulsion events in the gospel narratives (Mar 1:23-27, Mar 5:2-13, Luk 8:29, Luk 9:42, VCG-01 and -02) are particular instances pointing forward to the comprehensive eschatological defeat of all adversarial spiritual powers. The divine instance — Christ's authority over unclean spirits — establishes the pattern: divine authority is supreme, and their defeat in individual cases anticipates their ultimate defeat at the consummation.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Typological use is evidenced at T0.4.1 and T0.4.2. Not applicable.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic is named "Defilement by external spiritual agency." Four elements are analytically significant. *Defilement*: the characteristic produces the same inner-being condition as Characteristics 1-3 — the person is in a defiled state. *External*: the source of the defilement is outside the person, not from bodily contact (Characteristic 1), moral self-corruption (Characteristic 2), or corporate moral defection (Characteristic 3). *Spiritual*: the defiling agent is a spiritual being. *Agency*: the defiling force is an agent — purposeful, self-aware, volitional. The name signals: a condition of defilement imposed by a purposeful, external spiritual agent onto the inner and bodily life of the human person.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — One Greek term carries this characteristic exclusively: G0169 akathartos (alpha-privative + katharos — "not clean/pure"), always appearing as the modifier of pneuma ("unclean spirit" — pneuma akatharton). At the definitional level the term names the spirit's character — it is the un-clean spiritual force whose nature is the absence of holiness and the presence of defilement. Unique to Characteristic 4: the term names the character of the agent producing the condition, not the condition of the person. In Characteristics 1-3, the ta.me/akatharsia vocabulary names the condition of the person; here akathartos names the character of the agent.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — All three. Directional: *external* signals an inward invasion — the defilement moves from outside the person into the person's inner being, the opposite of Characteristic 2 (originating within the person's own desires). Constitutional: the defilement penetrates the whole person — will (Luk 8:29 — overriding all restraint), faculties (Mar 9:25 — speech and hearing suppressed), and bodily agency (Luk 8:29 — driven into the desert). Relational: the characteristic positions the person dominated by the unclean spirit, severed from communal life (Mar 5:2), accessible to divine authority (Mar 3:11), and in need of external intervention (Mat 10:1).

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic is a condition — a state of being invaded and dominated by an external spiritual agent, not an act the person performs or a disposition they hold. Mar 1:23 (VCG-01): "a man with an unclean spirit" — the having is a condition. Luk 8:29 (VCG-01): the spirit "had repeatedly seized him" — the seizure is an event producing a persisting condition of domination. The condition arises through spiritual invasion that bypasses the person's will entirely — distinct from all three other M10c characteristics in this regard.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or composite?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Composite. Three VCGs identify distinct registers: VCG-01 (8V — the inhabited person's defilement-state: Mar 1:23, Mar 5:2/8, Mar 7:25, Mar 9:25, Luk 4:33, Luk 8:29, Luk 9:42), VCG-02 (8V — expulsion dynamics and restoration: Mat 12:43, Mar 1:26, Mar 3:30, Mar 5:13, Luk 6:18, Luk 11:24, Act 5:16, Act 8:7), VCG-03 (5V — authority over unclean spirits: Mat 10:1, Mar 1:27, Mar 3:11, Mar 6:7, Luk 4:36). Simple in that all 21 verses concern the same agent-type and person-type; composite in the different inner-being perspectives each VCG illuminates.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Defilement by external spiritual agency: the inner-being condition of a person whose will, faculties, and bodily agency are invaded and dominated by an unclean spirit — an external adversarial force that enters the person, overrides their self-possession (Luk 8:29 — "driven by the demon"), suppresses their communicative faculties (Mar 9:25 — "mute and deaf spirit"), severs their communal access (Mar 5:2), and subjects their bodily agency to the spirit's purposes. The spirits recognise and submit to superior divine authority (Mar 3:11, Luk 4:36, VCG-03) and resist expulsion (Act 8:7). Restoration reverses the condition, returning the person to wholeness (Luk 6:18 — "were cured") and communal belonging (Luk 9:42 — "gave him back to his father"). Structural opposite: M12 (purity/wholeness).

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — M12 (Purity/wholeness) is the structural opposite, confirmed by the restoration language throughout VCG-02. Luk 6:18 (VCG-02): "were cured" — wholeness is the positive M12 state restored. Luk 9:42 (VCG-01): "Jesus rebuked the unclean spirit and healed the boy" — healing is the positive state the spirit's presence had suspended. The structural opposite is not merely the absence of the spirit but the positive condition of wholeness — freedom from invasion, full possession of one's own faculties and bodily agency, restored communal access.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic explicitly suppresses: (1) speech and hearing — Mar 9:25 (VCG-01): Jesus addresses the spirit as "mute and deaf" — both faculties suppressed; (2) communal access — Mar 5:2 (VCG-01): "utterly cut off from clean, communal life"; (3) self-directed bodily agency — Luk 8:29 (VCG-01): "driven by the demon into the desert"; (4) the presence and authority of divine power — the spirits resist expulsion (Act 8:7 — crying out; Mar 5:8 — command must be issued); (5) restoration to familial/social belonging — Luk 9:42 — "gave him back to his father" implies the spirit had severed the familial bond.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Characteristic 4 is not: (1) Characteristic 1 (Ritual defilement-state) — Characteristic 4's defilement is produced by a spiritual agent, not bodily contact with a material defiling object; (2) Characteristic 2 (Moral-inner defilement-state) — Characteristic 4's condition arrives from outside the person without volitional cooperation; the person is not morally guilty for the condition; (3) Characteristic 3 (Corporate/covenantal defilement) — Characteristic 4 operates at the individual level through direct spiritual invasion; (4) M27 (Cosmic-evil agents) — Characteristic 4 is the defilement-state produced in the human person; M27 analyses the character and nature of the unclean spirits themselves. The two are complementary analytical lenses, not the same characteristic.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Four distinct ways. (1) Will-domination: Luk 8:29 (VCG-01) — "repeatedly seized him, overpowering all human restraint" and "driven by the demon" — the spirit overrides the person's own volitional direction. (2) Faculty-suppression: Mar 9:25 (VCG-01) — "mute and deaf spirit" — speech and hearing are suppressed, cutting off communicative capacity. (3) Communal-severance: Mar 5:2 (VCG-01) — the man among the tombs utterly cut off; Mar 7:25 — the child's affliction drives the mother to seek external help. (4) Violent bodily affliction: Mar 1:26 (VCG-02): "convulsing him and crying out"; Luk 9:42 (VCG-01): "the demon threw him to the ground and convulsed him."

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes. By severity: Mar 5:2/8 (VCG-01) — the Gerasene demoniac is the most severe case; Luk 8:29 — "repeatedly seized him, overpowering all human restraint." Mar 7:25 (VCG-01) — a child with an unclean spirit is a less severe case. By faculty affected: Mar 9:25 — specifically mute and deaf; Luk 8:29 — bodily agency overridden; Mar 1:26 — convulsing. By social setting: Mar 1:23 (VCG-01) — the synagogue; Mar 5:2 — social exclusion among tombs. Expulsion dynamics also vary: some depart with loud cries (Mar 1:26, Act 8:7); Mat 12:43/Luk 11:24 show the spirit's post-expulsion restlessness.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, prominently — the speech operates through the inhabited person, not as the person's own speech. Mar 1:23 (VCG-01): "he cried out" — the man cries out through the spirit's prompting. Mar 3:11 (VCG-03): "they fell down before him and cried out, 'You are the Son of God'" — the spirits use the person's voice to confess divine identity. Mar 1:26 (VCG-02): "crying out with a loud voice" at departure. Act 8:7 (VCG-02): "crying out with a loud voice, came out of many." The spirit appropriates the person's voice as its own instrument of declaration, resistance, and departure — the human voice becomes the medium of adversarial spiritual speech.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response to this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Two immediate response patterns are evidenced — in observers and in proxies, not in the affected person. For observers: Mar 1:27 (VCG-03) — amazement ("they were all amazed... 'What is this?'"); Luk 4:36 (VCG-03) — "they were all amazed" at Jesus's authority. For proxies seeking help: Mar 7:25 (VCG-01) — "immediately a woman... came and fell down at his feet" — urgent supplication. For the divine response: Mar 5:8 (VCG-01) — "Come out of the man, you unclean spirit!" is the immediate divine response to encountering the condition.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The immediate response varies by actor. (1) Divine response: consistently authoritative command — Mar 5:8, Mar 9:25, Luk 9:42. (2) Observer response: consistently amazement at the authority (Mar 1:27, Luk 4:36). (3) Spirit response: consistently submission despite resistance (Mar 1:26 — departing; Act 8:7 — crying out but departing). (4) Family/proxy response: immediate seeking of help (Mar 7:25 — the mother; Luk 9:42 — father receiving son back). (5) The affected person's own immediate response: not narrated — the condition suppresses the person's capacity for self-expression.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence is silent on the immediate inner-being response of the affected person from within the condition. In all 21 verses, the person in the defilement-state does not narrate or express their own immediate inner response — they are under the spirit's domination. This silence is analytically significant: Characteristic 4 is the only M10c characteristic where the affected person's own inner response to being in the condition is structurally inaccessible — the domination that constitutes the characteristic is what prevents the person from narrating their response to it.

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Two forms of sustained effect. (1) Progressive entrenchment of domination: Luk 8:29 (VCG-01) — "for many a time it had seized him" — sustained operation is a repeated pattern of seizure; all human attempts at restraint fail. The domination extends to all external remedies ("bound with chains and shackles, but he would break the bonds"). (2) Social and relational deterioration: Mar 5:2 (VCG-01) — the man among the tombs thoroughly isolated from communal life; sustained possession has reduced him to dwelling in a place of death. The sustained effect of the characteristic is total: will, faculties, communal belonging, and bodily agency all subsumed under the spirit's domination.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained possession establish?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Sustained possession establishes: (1) Total will-bondage without moral guilt — the person cannot break free (Luk 8:29 — breaking bonds yet "driven by the demon"); the will is externally overridden, not internally corrupted. (2) Communicative isolation — Mar 9:25 (VCG-01): "mute and deaf spirit" — sustained suppression of the communicative faculties enabling relationship with God and others. (3) Social-communal severance — Mar 5:2 (VCG-01): dwelling "among the tombs" in radical exclusion. (4) Bodily subjugation — the body becomes the instrument of the spirit's violent self-expression (convulsions — Luk 9:42; breaking chains — Luk 8:29; driven into the desert — Luk 8:29).

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes. The immediate event is the seizure — sudden and violent (Luk 9:42: "the demon threw him to the ground and convulsed him"). The sustained effect is domination that becomes a settled condition: Luk 8:29 — "many a time it had seized him" and he was kept in chains; Mar 5:2 — dwelling among the tombs. The immediate is episodic; the sustained is existential. The immediate response (observers' amazement, divine command) addresses the acute manifestation; the sustained condition requires the comprehensive restoration named at Luk 6:18 (cured) and Luk 9:42 (healed and returned to father).

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

The language throughout VCG-01 is stative: "a man with an unclean spirit" (Mar 1:23 — present condition); "a man who had an unclean spirit" (Mar 5:2 — echōn — having, as a present condition); "those afflicted with unclean spirits" (Act 5:16 — ochloumenoi — being afflicted, present participle of sustained condition). Act 5:16 — "those afflicted with unclean spirits" — the affliction is an ongoing state. The characteristic's resolution requires authoritative external intervention (divine command), not the person's own volitional response — confirming the condition classification.

**T1.8.3 — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, two secondary dimensions. (1) Affliction/suffering: Act 5:16 names those with unclean spirits alongside the "sick"; Luk 6:18 — "cured" — the healing terminology frames the condition as an affliction to be addressed. (2) Adversarial spiritual engagement: the spirits resist expulsion (Act 8:7 — crying out; Mat 12:43 — seeking re-entry), and authority is required to overcome them (Mat 10:1). The adversarial dimension is secondary to the condition dimension but essential for understanding how the condition is both produced and resolved.

## T2 — Constitutional Location (31 prompts)

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes. The characteristic is constituted by the interaction between an external pneuma and the human person. Luk 4:33 (VCG-01): "the spirit of an unclean demon" — the entity is a spirit; its presence in the person constitutes the defilement-state. Mar 9:25 (VCG-01): "you mute and deaf spirit, I command you, come out of him" — the command is addressed to the spirit as the entity inhabiting the person; the spirit's presence and expulsion define the condition. The external spirit inhabits and dominates the person at their deepest constitutional level.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, uniquely within the M10c cluster. The characteristic originates from an external spirit and its primary site of operation is the person's spirit-level constitution. The unclean spirit enters and operates at the spirit level, which is why the domination is total: Luk 8:29 (VCG-01) — the spirit's control overrides all bodily resistance (breaking chains), not because it acts at the bodily level first but because it controls the person at the deepest constitutional level. This distinguishes Characteristic 4 from Characteristic 2, where the defilement penetrates to the spirit level from the soul's desire-level inward; Characteristic 4 arrives at the spirit level directly from an external spiritual agent.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The spirit-level operation reveals Characteristic 4 as the deepest form of defilement in the M10c cluster — deeper even than Characteristic 2 (which reaches the spirit level but originates at the soul level). Characteristic 4 arrives at the spirit level directly, bypassing the soul's volitional engagement entirely. This explains why the characteristic produces the most comprehensive domination (Luk 8:29 — chains fail) and why divine authority alone (not human will, not human restraint) is the effective remedy — only a power superior to the spirit can address the condition at the constitutional level where the spirit operates.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence is not silent. The characteristic is constituted by the operation of an external pneuma at the person's spirit level. Not applicable.

The will is overridden (Luk 8:29 — "driven by the demon") — the will is a primary soul-level faculty. The characteristic thus operates at the soul level by suppressing the soul's faculties through the external spirit's spirit-level domination.

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The soul-level location reveals that the characteristic's domination extends from the spirit level downward through the soul-level faculties to the body. The spirit inhabits and controls at the spirit level; the soul's faculties are suppressed (will overridden, speech and hearing suppressed); the body is subject to violent external control (convulsions — Mar 1:26, Luk 9:42; breaking chains — Luk 8:29). This downward cascade reveals the constitutional comprehensiveness of the characteristic: it reaches and dominates every layer of the human person.

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**
