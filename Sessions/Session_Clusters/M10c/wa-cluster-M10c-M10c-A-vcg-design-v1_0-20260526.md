### M10c-A-VCG-01 — Unconscious contact defilement and conscience awakening

**context_description:** Defilement incurred through contact that the person may not immediately know has occurred; the guilt becomes morally real only when awareness arrives. The inner-being content is the temporal gap between defilement and recognition — conscience awakening after hidden violation. The defilement exists as an objective condition prior to any inner awareness; the conscience then names what the body has already incurred. Cross-register: M12 (purity as the state disrupted by the unknown contact).

### M10c-A-VCG-02 — Carcass-contact defilement

**context_description:** The unclean state incurred by touching, carrying, or eating the carcass of an unclean or dead animal — whether categorically forbidden (swarming creatures, paw-walking animals, non-compliant hoofed animals) or permitted animals in a dead state. The defilement is time-bound (typically until evening), acquired through bodily interaction with death in animal form, and resolved by washing. The inner-being content is the duty of watchful restraint over bodily actions near animal carcasses, and the awareness that the body's natural actions in the world carry ritual consequence. Extends to ingestion (Lev 17:15 — eating naturally dead or predator-killed animals). Cross-register: M12 (purity as the state the person must actively maintain through bodily vigilance).

### M10c-A-VCG-03 — Object contamination through material transmission

**context_description:** The unclean state transmitted from dead creatures to objects — vessels, food, water, ovens, seed, and other material surfaces. Defilement extends beyond the person who touches a carcass to the objects the carcass contacts; some contaminations are irreversible (earthenware must be broken; ovens destroyed). Moisture serves as a conductor of impurity. The inner-being content is the duty to guard what one allows to contact provisions and utensils — the contamination field radiates outward from death into the material world, and the person's watchfulness over their environment is a moral obligation. Cross-register: M12 (purity as the condition material objects can lose and that must be restored or removed where irreversible).

### M10c-A-VCG-04 — Volitional self-defilement

**context_description:** Defilement through swarming creatures framed explicitly as a matter of volitional self-corruption — the person is urged to refuse contact, making the choice not to defile oneself an act of the will. Divine holiness is the explicit ground: the call to abstain from defiling contact is tied to imitating the holy character of God. The inner-being content is the will's capacity to choose or refuse defilement, and the connection between that choice and the person's participation in God's holiness. This sub-register stands apart from mechanical or unconscious contact defilement by its foregrounding of the inner volitional choice. Cross-register: M12 (holiness as the positive state the will orients toward by refusing defilement).

### M10c-A-VCG-05 — Menstrual/physiological defilement cycle

**context_description:** The ritual unclean state arising from menstruation and childbirth — physiological in origin, not moral fault, time-bound, governed by the bodily reproductive and discharge cycle. The niddah condition is the paradigm case: the menstruating woman is unclean for seven days; the state radiates outward to all surfaces she contacts; it is transmissible through sexual contact. Childbirth impurity operates by the same paradigm (double duration for a female birth). Abnormal or extended discharge is governed by the same niddah framework. The inner-being content is the person's navigation of a recurring bodily state that creates ritual separateness — marking a rhythm of defilement and cleansing tied to the body's created processes. Cross-register: M12 (the purity cycle — the restored state at the end of each niddah period).

### M10c-A-VCG-06 — Bodily discharge contact defilement

**context_description:** The unclean state produced by bodily discharge from a man (genital discharge / zav condition) and its transmission — to surfaces he lies or sits on, to objects beneath him, to persons who touch his bed, his saddle, or his body. Defilement transmits through sustained contact, indirect contact, spittle, and unwashed hands; it renders each subsequent person or object unclean until evening after washing. Seminal emission and sexual intercourse with emission produce a similar temporary unclean state for both partners. The inner-being content is contamination radiating from the body outward through physical interaction, making the person a vector of impurity they must actively manage; the duty of bodily watchfulness extends to preventing the spread of one's own condition to others. Cross-register: M12 (purity restored by washing and waiting — the cleansing cycle).

### M10c-A-VCG-07 — Corpse-contact defilement

**context_description:** The most severe sub-register of bodily-contact defilement — contact with a human corpse produces a seven-day unclean state requiring the red-heifer ash (water of impurity) applied on the third and seventh days for removal. The defilement transmits through direct touch, carrying, spatial proximity (sharing a tent with a corpse), and field contact with bones or graves. It extends to objects within the tent (open vessels) and cascades through contact chains. Even those who perform or assist the purification rite incur a lesser temporary uncleanness. Willful refusal to cleanse after corpse-contact constitutes an act of defiling the sanctuary and results in being cut off. The inner-being content is acute: grief at exclusion from communal worship (Num 9:7); the weight of an unresolved unclean state clinging to the person (Num 19:13); the moral fault of the will that chooses to remain in the defiled state (Num 19:20); the communal responsibility not to spread death-defilement into sacred space. Cross-register: M12 (purity restored through the prescribed cleansing rite — the standing remedy); M11 (cleansing as the appointed response — niddah water as resource).

### M10c-A-VCG-08 — Sacred boundary and defilement scope

**context_description:** Verses that frame the scope, limits, and existential stakes of bodily defilement — establishing that uncleanness restricts sacred not secular eating (unclean and clean may eat ordinary food alike); that unchecked defilement is existentially dangerous because it threatens to contaminate the divine dwelling; that impurity as a tangible presence has accumulated in sacred space requiring consecrated removal; and that the land itself becomes saturated with the contagious defilement of surrounding peoples. The inner-being content is the conscience oriented to the sacred domain — understanding the boundary between defiled and clean as the boundary between ordinary life and access to holiness. Cross-register: M12 (purity as the condition required for sacred participation); M11 (cleansing as the response to accumulated sacral contamination — 2Ch 29:5).
