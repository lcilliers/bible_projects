## VCG design — M10c-E (3 VCGs)

### M10c-E-VCG-01 — Inhabitation and affliction: the possessed person's defilement-state

**context_description:** The inner-being condition of the person inhabited and dominated by an unclean spirit — will overridden, bodily agency lost, faculties suppressed, normal communal life severed. The unclean spirit's presence constitutes the person's defilement: the man in the synagogue is presented as an inhabited, controlled person whose inner state is defined by the corrupting spiritual presence within him (Mar 1:23). A man living among tombs, possessed and cut off from clean communal life, is marked as utterly severed from human community (Mar 5:2). The spirit is a distinct, addressable entity whose presence constitutes the man's defilement (Mar 5:8). A child's affliction drives her mother to seek help — the spirit's presence experienced as a tormenting, controlling affliction (Mar 7:25). The spirit's capacity to suppress speech and hearing shows it impairs the inner and bodily faculties of the person it inhabits (Mar 9:25). The compound phrase "spirit of an unclean demon" intensifies the defiling, hostile quality of the controlling presence (Luk 4:33). Persistent, irresistible control illustrates total domination of the person's will and bodily agency (Luk 8:29). The unclean spirit's violent, destructive action against the body evidences its corrupting hold (Luk 9:42). Cross-register: M12 (purity/wholeness as the state the inhabited person has lost — the defilement-state is the inverse of wholeness); M27 (the unclean spirit as a cosmic-evil agent — its character belongs to M27; the person's defilement-state belongs to M10c-E).

### M10c-E-VCG-02 — Expulsion, resistance, and restoration of wholeness

**context_description:** The expulsion event and its dynamics — the spirit's departure through violent convulsion and outcry (Mar 1:26), its destructive transfer and the resulting death of the pigs (Mar 5:13), vocal resistance at departure from many persons (Act 8:7), the spirit's restless drive to re-inhabit after expulsion (Mat 12:43; Luk 11:24 — the expelled spirit wanders, seeks rest, and plans return), and crucially, restoration: being freed from an unclean spirit is equated with a restoration of wholeness — the verse treats spiritual defilement as a form of affliction that healing reverses (Luk 6:18). Those afflicted with unclean spirits are brought for healing; unclean spiritual possession is a condition of suffering that healing reverses (Act 5:16). The accusation that Jesus's power derives from an unclean spirit (Mar 3:30) is included here because it addresses the expulsion-authority context — the accusation attempts to locate defilement at the very source of the expulsion-power. The inner-being content foregrounds both the dynamics of the defilement-condition (its resistance to removal, its radiating destructive force) and the restorative act of healing that reverses the defilement-state. Cross-register: M12 (wholeness as what healing restores — Luk 6:18 is the most direct evidence of purity/wholeness as the structural opposite of this defilement-state); M11 (healing as the cleansing/restoration response to defilement-by-spiritual-agency).

### M10c-E-VCG-03 — Authority over unclean spirits

**context_description:** The commissioning of authority over unclean spirits and the spirits' recognition of divine power. Unclean spirits represent hostile, morally corrupting powers over which authority is granted — the term names a category of spiritual agents that defile and afflict (Mat 10:1 — the commissioning of the twelve). Delegated authority over unclean spirits is given to the twelve — the verse frames unclean spirits as entities to be actively opposed and overcome, requiring granted power (Mar 6:7). The commanding authority over unclean spirits evokes amazement — the spirits' obedience reveals they are subject to a higher power; the spirits are defiling agents under authoritative restraint (Mar 1:27). Unclean spirits recognise and confess a superior divine identity before being commanded — their acknowledgment reveals self-awareness alongside their defiling, hostile nature (Mar 3:11). The spirits' compliance underscores that unclean spiritual forces yield to authoritative speech, revealing defilement as ultimately subject to divine power (Luk 4:36). The inner-being content: the commissioned persons are granted a delegated authority that operates against the defilement-producing agents; the spirits' recognition of divine superiority reveals the limits of their defilement-state as a power. Cross-register: M12 (divine purity/power as the force to which the defiling spirits submit); M27 (the spirits themselves as cosmic-evil agents whose recognition of divine authority is also an M27-register event).
