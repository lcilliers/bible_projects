## VCG design — M10c-C (5 VCGs)

### M10c-C-VCG-01 — Constitutional and existential moral uncleanness

**context_description:** Verses that address the human condition itself as inherently compromised before God — the impossibility of producing something clean from what is fundamentally unclean; the prophet's self-aware moral collapse when confronted by divine holiness; the corporate confession of total defilement that invalidates moral performance; and the community's inner moral state corrupting every act of worship. The inner-being content is the recognition of a defilement so deep and pervasive that it is not incurred by particular acts but characterises the inner person's constitution before God. Cross-register: M12 (divine holiness as the confronting force that reveals inner defilement — Isa 6:5; purity as the state this constitutional uncleanness lacks).

**Anchor:** vc_id 21568 (Job 14:4 H2931) — the rhetorical question asserts the utter impossibility of a clean thing arising from what is unclean, pointing to the constitutional condition of the human inner being as inherently compromised.

### M10c-C-VCG-02 — Impurity as flesh-dominion and habitual corruption

**context_description:** Impurity functioning as a prior master, slavery, or dominion over the will and bodily members — the condition to which unchecked heart-lust leads; the old self's passion and desire complex directing bodily members toward escalating moral disorder; impurity as morally chosen, persistent, and unrepented through a hardened will; the visible work of the flesh; callousness of conscience enabling greedy pursuit of sensuality; earthly inner drives to be mortified; impurity as the contrary inner condition divine calling opposes. Even motivation is susceptible — right motivation before God requires an inner life free from morally corrupt desires contaminating one's motives. The inner-being content is the will, desire, and bodily members held under the dominion of an impurity that has become habitual, entrenched, and unrepented. Cross-register: M10 (sin-act as the generative activity producing the impurity-state); M29 (unchecked desire as the vehicle of impurity's dominion).

### M10c-C-VCG-03 — Conscience and will as locus of defilement; deliberate self-purification

**context_description:** Verses where the conscience or will is the named and primary locus of defilement, or where deliberate self-purification from defilement penetrating the full inner-outer constitution of the person is called for. The conscience is defiled by acting against its own conviction (1Cor 8:7 — weak conscience defiled through eating food associated with idols without genuine moral conviction). Defilement penetrates both body and spirit and must be actively cleansed (2Cor 7:1). Passion-driven defilement of the will combines with contempt for authority — bold wilfulness suppressing reverence and restraint (2Pe 2:10). The deliberate will is called to separate from unclean things as the condition for divine welcome (2Cor 6:17), and to purify itself before bearing sacred vessels (Isa 52:11). Cross-register: M11 (cleansing as the active response called for — 2Cor 7:1 and Isa 52:11); M08 (pride and contempt as the will's posture accompanying defilement — 2Pe 2:10); M12 (holiness as the goal of the purification act).

### M10c-C-VCG-04 — Heart allegiance disorder and idolatrous impurity

**context_description:** The heart-allegiance dimension of moral-inner defilement — where impurity is equated with idolatry, arising from a heart that has displaced God with corrupt desire. The inner life is full of impurity despite outward appearances that attract human approval (Mat 23:27 — hypocrites). Being impure equals being an idolater — a heart allegiance disorder that disqualifies from divine inheritance (Eph 5:5). Impurity is incompatible with a holy inner identity and must be excluded from both conduct and speech (Eph 5:3). The Revelation sub-corpus extends the idolatrous-moral-defilement register to spiritual agents: three unclean spirits whose inner character is morally defiled, functioning as agents of deception sourced in darkness (Rev 16:13); a soul saturated with moral filth and adorned with seductive beauty (Rev 17:4); total spiritual and moral uncleanliness as the defining inner and environmental character of total corruption (Rev 18:2). Cross-register: M12 (holiness as the inner identity that impurity-as-idolatry violates); M10 (sin-act of idolatry as the generative source of the heart-defilement state); M27 (the agents of deception in Rev 16:13 as cosmic-evil agents — though their defilement-state is the M10c register here).

### M10c-C-VCG-05 — Maintained moral purity and consecrated non-defilement

**context_description:** The positive-inverse register within the moral-inner characteristic — the inner-being condition of those who have not defiled themselves, maintained through consecrated devotion and moral integrity. The few who have not soiled their garments have kept their inner and outward life undefiled amid a compromised community; their moral integrity is the basis of their worthiness to walk with Christ (Rev 3:4). Those who have not defiled themselves have kept bodies and inner lives pure, expressing total consecration to the Lamb with self-denial and devotion as the defining inner posture (Rev 14:4). The unclean are excluded from the holy Way, positioning moral purity as the condition of access to the path of holiness and divine restoration (Isa 35:8). The inner-being content is the sustained positive state of moral non-defilement — not as absence but as the active maintenance of inner integrity through consecration. Cross-register: M09 (humility/consecration — Rev 14:4's self-denial and devotion posture); M12 (purity as the positive state these verses embody).
