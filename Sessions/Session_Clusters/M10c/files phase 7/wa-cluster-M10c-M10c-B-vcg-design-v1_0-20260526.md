## VCG design — M10c-B (5 VCGs)

### M10c-B-VCG-01 — Discernment discipline

**context_description:** The cultivation of active moral-spiritual discernment as the inner-being faculty required for distinguishing clean from unclean — a perceptual and volitional discipline central to covenant life. Priests are called to this discernment as their vocational responsibility; the law's purpose is framed as producing this faculty in the community. Maintaining the clean/unclean distinction functions as a moral-identity boundary: blurring it renders the person detestable before God. Personal integrity is evidenced by maintaining freedom from uncleanness at the time of sacred giving. Cross-register: M12 (purity/discernment as the positive faculty M10c-B's characteristic opposes — knowing what is unclean is inseparable from knowing what is clean).

### M10c-B-VCG-02 — Priestly diagnosis and disease verdict

**context_description:** The unclean state determined by authoritative priestly examination of skin disease — the priest observes bodily markers (spreading eruption, raw flesh, burn sites, scalp itch, yellow hair), applies diagnostic criteria, and pronounces an unclean verdict. The verdict may be immediate (raw flesh, chronic established disease) or time-delayed (awaiting spread confirmation across re-examination days). Progressive worsening of a bodily condition escalates and confirms the impurity status. The inner-being content is the experience of bodily deterioration determining ritual standing through an external authority's judgment — the person's state of body is the evidence on which their social and religious identity rests. Cross-register: M12 (purity as the cleared state following a positive examination; the examination process as the mechanism of discernment).

### M10c-B-VCG-03 — Public declaration, isolation, and stigma

**context_description:** The experience of the declared unclean person — required to publicly cry out their own contaminated state, compelled to dwell alone, physically separated from the community for the full duration of the disease. The unclean state is an ongoing, sustained condition that defines social and relational exclusion. The inner reality of contamination is verbalized outward through ritual cry, marking a condition that isolates the self from the community. The declared verdict shapes the person's identity as one excluded from clean community life. Extends to the institutional enforcement of the sacred boundary (2Ch 23:19 — gatekeepers barring the unclean from the temple) and the existential reflection that the unclean and clean share the same mortal fate (Ecc 9:2 — the leveling force of death rendering external status distinctions moot). Cross-register: M07 (stigma, disgrace, and enforced social shame — the public cry and relational severing); M12 (purity as the condition of communal belonging the excluded person lacks).

### M10c-B-VCG-04 — Disease in objects and structures

**context_description:** The categorical unclean state applied to material objects — fabric infected with spreading disease must be burned; diseased stones from a house removed to an unclean disposal zone; scraped plaster deposited outside the city; a house where disease persists after repair is declared persistently unclean and demolished. The unclean designation marks designated receptacle zones for removed contamination, spatially containing defilement. Entering a shut-up diseased house transmits defilement to the person by mere proximity. The inner-being content is limited in these verses (Lev 13:59 and 14:36 note no direct inner-being content), but the categorical-contamination determination and the discipline of removing contamination from inhabited space reflects the same discernment and protective discipline as the personal disease verdict. Cross-register: M12 (the clean determination that the examination aims to produce or restore for material objects).

### M10c-B-VCG-05 — NT categorical conscience and covenantal status

**context_description:** The NT register where categorical clean/unclean convictions are formed in conscience, maintained against challenge, and ultimately revised by divine revelation — and where the clean/unclean status is extended to persons and relationships as a covenantal category rather than a personal moral verdict. Peter's conscience is deeply shaped by lifelong categorical purity conviction, resisting the divine vision urging change (Act 10:14, 11:8) — until divine revelation overturns the humanly constructed inner judgment that persons of other nations are defiled (Act 10:28). The children of a believing parent are considered holy rather than unclean on the basis of sanctifying covenantal influence — the unclean status here is relational, not personal guilt (1Cor 7:14). The mother of the Nazirite-dedicated child is commanded to eat nothing unclean — categorical avoidance by those nurturing one set apart for God (Judg 13:4). Cross-register: M12 (purity/holiness as the covenantal status these verses navigate the boundary of).
