## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The corporate/covenantal defilement characteristic reveals God's character primarily through the categories of divine holiness, covenantal faithfulness, and the sanctity of the divine name. The characteristic operates as what violates these divine realities. Num 35:34 (M10c-D-VCG-04): "you shall not defile the land in which you live, in the midst of which I dwell, for I the Lord dwell in the midst of the people of Israel" — God's dwelling presence establishes the boundary that defilement violates; his holiness requires a clean habitation. Eze 43:7 (M10c-D-VCG-03): "the house of Israel shall no more defile my holy name" — the divine name is a reality that can be defiled, revealing that God's name is a sacred relational extension of his person that the covenant people's conduct either honours or degrades. Eze 5:11 (M10c-D-VCG-03): defilement of the sanctuary provokes divine withdrawal — "I will withdraw; my eye will not spare" — revealing that God's covenantal presence is not unconditional and responds to covenantal defilement with withdrawal. The characteristic thus reveals God as one whose holiness, name, and dwelling presence constitute real relational vulnerabilities that the covenant people's corporate conduct directly affects.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, uniquely at Eze 20:26 (M10c-D-VCG-02): "I defiled them through their very gifts in their offering up all their firstborn, that I might devastate them. I did it that they might know that I am the Lord." God defiles Israel through their own defiant gifts as a judicial act — using the instrument of their own self-defilement against them as a form of punitive desolation. The attribution is judicial and singular: God does not normally possess or generate defilement, but he can deploy it as an instrument of wrath when the people's defiant self-defilement reaches the point of idolatrous child sacrifice. This attribution reveals the characteristic's most extreme form — when the community's defilement is used by God himself as the mechanism of its own devastation.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The near-total silence about God's possession of this characteristic (the Eze 20:26 exception being judicial and unique) confirms that the corporate/covenantal defilement-state is a specifically creaturely-covenantal condition. God's silence as a bearer of this characteristic reveals its essential nature: it is the condition that arises when creaturely covenantal persons — whose inner will, shared life, and sacred stewardship are oriented toward God — turn away from him in unfaithfulness. The characteristic requires a covenantal community in relation with a holy God; it cannot exist in God himself, but it exists in the gap between what the covenantal community is called to be and what they have become through corporate moral defection.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — The 83-verse corpus does not evidence a creational purpose for the corporate/covenantal defilement-state. Like Characteristic 2 (Moral-inner defilement-state), this characteristic is consistently framed in the evidence as a departure from the covenantal calling rather than as a created faculty serving human flourishing. The evidence is entirely about the condition arising through moral defection (VCG-01, -02, -03, -04) and being reversed through divine cleansing (VCG-05). No verse frames corporate/covenantal defilement as something designed for the community's formation or benefit. Its analytical significance is entirely through contrast with what the community is called to be.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The evidence strongly indicates this characteristic is a response to the fallen condition operating in a covenantal context. The corporate register is the site of the characteristic's primary evidence: Psa 106:39 (M10c-D-VCG-02) — "thus they became unclean by their acts, and played the whore in their deeds" — describes the defilement as arising from specific acts of apostasy within a people who were already in covenant with God. 2Ch 36:14 (M10c-D-VCG-03) — "they were exceedingly unfaithful, following all the abominations of the nations. And they polluted the house of the Lord" — frames the defilement as the corporate fruit of habitual defection from an existing covenant. The characteristic presupposes the covenantal relationship as prior; the defilement is what happens when that relationship is betrayed.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, substantially. The VCG-05 corpus carries three eschatological-restorative texts. Eze 37:23 (VCG-05): "they shall not defile themselves anymore with their idols... but I will save them from all the backslidings in which they have sinned, and will cleanse them; and they shall be my people, and I will be their God" — a promise of permanent non-defilement as the mark of the restored covenant community. Isa 52:1 (VCG-05): "there shall no more come into you the uncircumcised and the unclean" — the eschatological holy city excludes the defiling condition permanently. Zec 13:1 (VCG-05): "on that day there shall be a fountain opened... to cleanse them from sin and uncleanness" — the divinely provided fountain of cleansing addresses the corporate/covenantal defilement at a future eschatological moment. The characteristic is oriented toward a fullness in which the covenant community is permanently purified and the defilement-state is definitively ended.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The corporate/covenantal defilement-state expresses the divine image negatively — through its disruption. The image-bearing capacity that the characteristic reveals (by its absence) is the community's stewardship of sacred space, covenant relationship, and the divine name. These are image-bearing responsibilities: the covenant community is entrusted with land (Num 35:34 — God dwells in it), sanctuary (Eze 5:11 — God's holy place), and divine name (Eze 43:7 — the name that the community either honours or defiles). The corporate defilement-state reveals that the image-bearing community has failed its stewardship: it has defiled what God entrusted to it. Positively, the evidence shows that the proper exercise of the image-bearing capacity is the maintenance of covenantal fidelity, sexual integrity (VCG-01), and sacral stewardship — all of which the defilement-state violates.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely condition?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Exclusively a creaturely-covenantal condition, with the single judicial exception noted at T0.1.2. The corporate/covenantal defilement-state requires a community embedded in covenantal relationship with God — it is the condition of that community when it betrays the covenant. God as the covenant-giver and the holy One dwelling in the land does not share this condition; he is what the condition violates. Eze 5:11 makes this explicit: the people's defilement of the sanctuary provokes God's withdrawal — showing that God and the defilement-state are mutually exclusive realities.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The presence of the corporate/covenantal defilement-state reveals a specific failure of the image-bearing vocation: the community created to reflect divine holiness in the land, to maintain covenant fidelity, and to guard the divine name has instead become the source of the land's pollution, the sanctuary's desecration, and the name's defilement. Eze 36:17 (VCG-04): "their ways before me were like the uncleanness of a woman in her menstrual impurity" — the corporate conduct has become repugnant to divine holiness. The absence of the characteristic — the condition of the community before the defection begins, or after restoration — reveals the image functioning: land kept holy (Num 35:34), sanctuary guarded (Eze 44:23/25), covenant fidelity maintained. Eze 20:43 (VCG-02) reveals a distinctive image-bearing moment: the future community will remember its defilement and "loathe yourselves for all the evils that you have committed" — even the memory of the defilement serves the image-bearing vocation of repentance.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in two directions. First, the land-defilement register (VCG-04) and sanctuary-defilement register (VCG-03) function typologically in the prophetic corpus: the desecration of the physical land and temple in the pre-exilic prophets points forward to the eschatological restoration (Eze 37:23, Isa 52:1, Zec 13:1, VCG-05) in which both are permanently cleansed. The physical defilement and restoration is the type; the eschatological fullness is the antitype. Second, the cleansing fountain of Zec 13:1 (VCG-05) — "a fountain opened... to cleanse them from sin and uncleanness" — has a christological-typological dimension: the fountain that will address the corporate/covenantal defilement at a future eschatological moment is widely understood in the tradition as pointing toward the atonement. Within §3 alone, Zec 13:1 carries its own eschatological-promissory typological weight without requiring external cross-reference.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The direction runs from the historical-covenantal instance toward the eschatological-divine antitype. The defilement of land, sanctuary, and name through Israel's corporate moral defection (the present historical instance evidenced in the pre-exilic corpus) points forward to a future divine act of comprehensive cleansing that exceeds the ritual and political restorations of the OT period. Eze 37:23 (VCG-05): "I will save... and will cleanse them" — the divine subject of the future cleansing is God himself; the human corporate defilement (the type) requires divine action (the antitype). The typological direction is thus upward and forward: from creaturely corporate failure toward divine eschatological redemption.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Typological use is evidenced at T0.4.1 and T0.4.2. Not applicable.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The characteristic is named "Corporate/covenantal defilement." Three elements of the name are analytically significant. *Corporate*: the defilement extends beyond the individual to the community as a body — shared land, shared sanctuary, shared name, shared moral failure. *Covenantal*: the defilement operates within and against the covenantal relationship; it is betrayal of a specific relational bond between God and his people, not merely moral failure in general. *Defilement*: the state of contamination that results from the betrayal, spreading to the physical and sacral environment the community inhabits. The name thus signals: a condition of contamination that arises from corporate covenantal unfaithfulness and extends from the inner will of the community outward to land, sanctuary, and divine name.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Three terms carry this characteristic, all Hebrew (the characteristic has no NT Greek evidence in its corpus): H2930A (ta.me verb, 63V): "to be/become unclean, to defile" — the dominant term, capturing both the act of defiling and the resulting condition. Its verb form is critical for Characteristic 3: the characteristic is frequently expressed as an active defiling of the land (Eze 36:17/18, VCG-04), sanctuary (Eze 5:11, VCG-03), and name (Eze 43:7/8, VCG-03) by the community's acts — the verb captures the dynamic of defilement as an act with consequences for sacred objects and spaces. H2931 (ta.me adj, 11V): "unclean" — the stative form naming the condition of persons, spaces, or objects once defiled (Eze 22:5 — "your name is defiled"; Lam 4:15 — "Unclean!"; Jos 22:19). H5079 (nid.dah, 9V): "impurity, menstrual impurity" — in the corporate-prophetic register, nid.dah is used metaphorically for moral uncleanness: Eze 36:17 ("their ways before me were like the uncleanness of a woman in her menstrual impurity"), Lam 1:17 ("Jerusalem has become a filthy thing among them").

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — All three. Directional: the defilement moves outward from the inner will of the community to the land, sanctuary, and divine name — a centrifugal movement from the community's inner disposition outward to the sacred environment it inhabits. Relational: the characteristic is constitutively relational — it requires the prior covenantal relationship (God-Israel) as the context within which the defilement of name, sanctuary, and land makes sense; outside that relationship there would be no "name" to defile, no "holy place" to desecrate. Constitutional: the characteristic spans individual and community — VCG-01 shows individual acts (adultery, Nazirite defilement) that have corporate consequences; VCG-02 and VCG-03 show the community as a whole (the city, the land, the house of Israel) as the subject of defilement.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The characteristic is a condition with a strong dispositional-volitional generating force. As a condition: the land, sanctuary, and divine name are named as "defiled" — a stative condition of contamination that has been produced by the community's acts (H2931 ta.me adj — Eze 22:5: "your name is defiled"). As a dispositional-volitional generator: the evidence shows the acts of defiling arising from sustained corporate inner dispositions — habitual unfaithfulness (2Ch 36:14 VCG-03: "exceedingly unfaithful, following all the abominations of the nations"); persistent idolatrous lust (Eze 23:7 VCG-02: "she defiled herself with all the idols of everyone after whom she lusted"); deliberate will-acts (Jer 7:30 VCG-03: the sons of Judah "set their detestable things in the house that is called by my name, to defile it"). The characteristic is thus a condition (of land, sanctuary, name) produced by a dispositional-volitional inner reality (habitual unfaithfulness, desire-driven idolatry, deliberate desecration).

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or composite?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Composite. The 83-verse corpus distributes across five VCGs that identify distinct inner-being registers: VCG-01 (23V — sexual/covenantal-relational defilement: Gen 34, Num 5 ordeal, Lev 18 sexual prohibitions, Eze 18/22/33 sexual catalogues), VCG-02 (24V — idolatrous self-defilement: 2Ki 23 reforming king, Psa 106:39, Eze 20/22/23 idolatry corpus, Hos/Jer whoredom), VCG-03 (10V — sanctuary/sacred-space defilement: 2Ch 36:14, Psa 79:1, Jer 7:30/32:34, Eze 5:11/9:7/23:38/43:7/8/44:25), VCG-04 (9V — land defilement: Num 35:34, Deu 21:23, Jos 22:19, Jer 2:7, Eze 36:17/18, Hos 9:4, Mic 2:10), VCG-05 (17V — exile consequences and cleansing promise: Num 5:3/6:7/9/12, Isa 52:1, Jer 19:13, Lam 1:17/4:15, Eze 4:13/14/22:5/22:26/37:23/44:23, Hos 9:3, Amo 7:17, Zec 13:1). These five facets together constitute the corporate/covenantal defilement characteristic, held together by the shared structural feature: defilement of a sacred relational order.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Corporate/covenantal defilement: the condition of contamination that extends from the community's inner volitional disposition (unfaithfulness, idolatrous desire, deliberate desecration) outward to the sacred relational order it inhabits — land defiled (Num 35:34, Jer 2:7, Eze 36:17/18, VCG-04), sanctuary desecrated (Eze 5:11, 2Ch 36:14, VCG-03), and divine name degraded (Eze 43:7/8, VCG-03). It includes both covenantal-relational defilement (sexual betrayal of covenant bonds — VCG-01, ordeal law, Nazirite defilement) and the corporate prophetic register (idolatry defiling sacred spaces — VCG-02, VCG-03). Its unique inner-being contribution is Eze 20:43 (VCG-02): memory of defilement functioning as the inner mechanism of corporate repentance. Structural opposite: M12 (purity/holiness of sacred space, divine name, covenant relationship).

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — M12 (Purity/holiness of sacred space, divine name, and covenant relationship) is the structural opposite, confirmed throughout the corpus. The binary is explicit at Eze 44:23 (VCG-05): "they shall teach my people the difference between the holy and the common, and show them how to distinguish between the unclean and the clean" — the clean/unclean and holy/common binaries are paired as the governing distinctions. Eze 37:23 (VCG-05): "they shall not defile themselves anymore... and they shall be my people, and I will be their God" — the positive covenant formula ("my people / my God") is the structural opposite of the defilement-state; restored relationship is the positive. Isa 52:1 (VCG-05): "put on your beautiful garments, O Jerusalem, the holy city; for there shall no more come into you the uncircumcised and the unclean" — holiness of the city (M12) is the eschatological positive that the defilement opposes.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The corporate/covenantal defilement-state explicitly excludes: (1) divine presence in the defiled space — Num 35:34 (VCG-04): "you shall not defile the land in which you live, in the midst of which I dwell" — defilement and divine habitation are incompatible; (2) divine inquiry and engagement — Eze 20:31 (VCG-02): "shall I be inquired of by you, O house of Israel? As I live, declares the Lord God, I will not be inquired of by you" — persistent idolatrous self-defilement closes divine accessibility; (3) land possession and rest — Mic 2:10 (VCG-04): "this is no place to rest, because of uncleanness that destroys with a grievous destruction" — defilement disqualifies the land for habitation; (4) covenantal integrity and blessing — Num 5:27/28 (VCG-01): the defiled woman receives the curse; the undefiled receives blessing and children.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Characteristic 3 (Corporate/covenantal defilement) is not: (1) Characteristic 1 (Ritual defilement-state) — Characteristic 3 operates through moral defection and covenantal betrayal, not through bodily contact with defiling agents; Eze 44:25 (VCG-03) — priestly corpse-contact defilement — sits at the boundary; it is in Characteristic 3's corpus because of its priestly-vocational context (guarding the sanctuary), not because it is a ritual contact defilement; (2) Characteristic 2 (Moral-inner defilement-state) — Characteristic 3 extends to land, sanctuary, and name; Characteristic 2 remains within the individual inner person; the prophetic Ezekiel material in Characteristic 3 shows the community's inner disposition producing contamination of the external sacred environment, which is the distinctive Characteristic 3 feature; (3) M10 (Sin-act) — the sin-acts that produce Characteristic 3 belong to M10; Characteristic 3 is the defilement-state that results from those acts, not the acts themselves; (4) M10b (Wickedness/abomination) — the Ezekiel idolatry corpus carries both M10b and M10c flags; Characteristic 3 owns the defilement-state produced by the idolatrous acts; M10b owns the abomination character of those same acts.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The corporate/covenantal defilement-state operates in the inner person in four distinct ways. (1) As a condition of the corporate will in sustained defection: Eze 14:11 (VCG-02) — "that the house of Israel may no more go astray from me, nor defile themselves anymore with all their transgressions" — the defilement-state is the inner expression of a repeatedly straying will. (2) As self-deception regarding one's own condition: Jer 2:23 (VCG-02) — "how can you say, 'I am not unclean, I have not gone after the Baals'?" — the defiled inner condition is denied by the will that is in it; the characteristic includes self-deceived non-recognition of the defilement. (3) As memory functioning as repentance mechanism: Eze 20:43 (VCG-02) — "there you shall remember your ways and all your deeds with which you have defiled yourselves, and you shall loathe yourselves for all the evils that you have committed" — the memory of the defilement-state in exile becomes the inner generative mechanism of self-loathing and repentance. (4) As a condition observed and held by God: Eze 23:13 (VCG-02) — "I saw that she was defiled" — God's divine perception holds the defilement as an objective spiritual reality.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, across three axes. By object of defilement: the characteristic operates differently when it defiles a person (sexual betrayal — VCG-01), a place (land — VCG-04, sanctuary — VCG-03), or a name (divine name — Eze 43:7/8, VCG-03). The inner-being content shifts: personal defilement engages the will's volitional choice (Lev 18:20: "make yourself unclean"); sanctuary defilement engages the corporate leadership's habitual defection (2Ch 36:14: "exceedingly unfaithful"); land defilement engages the community's moral conduct over time (Eze 36:17: "habitual conduct defiled the land"). By direction: active self-defilement (Eze 22:3 — "a city that makes idols to defile herself") vs passive-receptive defilement (Eze 23:17 — "the Babylonians... defiled her with their whoring lust" — external agents defiling through corrupting relations). By outcome: some defiling acts produce judicial consequences in the body (Num 5:27 — ordeal-water producing physical judgment); others produce social expulsion (Lam 4:15 — "Unclean! Away!"); others produce divine withdrawal (Eze 5:11).

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, at three evidenced points. (1) Public declaration of the corporate defiled condition: Lam 4:15 (VCG-05) — "Unclean! Away!" directed at the exiles — the community's defilement is proclaimed publicly by those who encounter them; the speech act activates social expulsion. (2) Divine naming of the defiled condition: Eze 22:5 (VCG-05) — "your name is defiled" — God's speech act declares the corporate defilement as a public moral identity. (3) The ordeal law's verbal self-declaration: Num 5:13-29 (VCG-01) — the woman is brought before God; the word of the ordeal names the defilement as either present or absent; the divine verdict through the ordeal water either confirms or denies the defilement. (4) The prophetic challenge: Jer 2:23 (VCG-02) — "how can you say, 'I am not unclean'?" — prophetic speech confronts the community's self-deceptive denial of their defilement-state.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response to encountering this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Three immediate inner responses are evidenced, varying by register. (1) Restrained grief and simmering awareness (VCG-01 — Gen 34:5): Jacob's immediate response to the violation of Dinah is restrained, simmering awareness — "Jacob held his peace until they came"; the inner impact is named as felt violation. (2) Jealousy as immediate affective response (VCG-01 — Num 5:14): the spirit of jealousy comes over the husband when he suspects his wife has defiled herself — jealousy is the immediate inner response to suspected covenantal betrayal. (3) Self-loathing as future eschatological response (VCG-02 — Eze 20:43): in the restoration, the community will remember its defilements and loathe itself — this is a prospective immediate response named as the desired future inner state when the community finally perceives what it has done.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The immediate response varies significantly. The corporate idolatry register (VCG-02) shows self-deception as the dominant immediate inner response: Jer 2:23 — "how can you say, 'I am not unclean'?" — the community's immediate inner response is denial and self-justification rather than recognition. The exile register (VCG-05) shows shame and expulsion as the immediate social response: Lam 1:17 — Jerusalem becomes "a filthy thing among them"; Lam 4:15 — "Unclean! Away!" The ordeal register (VCG-01) shows the social mechanism of judgment as the immediate outcome. The variation shows that immediate response depends on who is perceiving the defilement: the community in the defilement-state denies it; God perceives it clearly (Eze 23:13); the surrounding nations expel the defiled community; prophetic speech confronts the self-deception.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The evidence is not uniformly silent — immediate responses are evidenced across VCGs (Gen 34:5, Num 5:14, Jer 2:23, Lam 4:15, Eze 23:13, Eze 20:43). However, the large majority of the prophetic-legal corpus (Lev 18, Num 35, Deu 21/24, Eze 5/7/9/22/36/43, Hos, Mic) describes the condition and its consequences without narrating a specific immediate inner response. The evidence base is substantially normative/prophetic rather than narrative.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Sustained corporate/covenantal defilement produces several documented inner-being consequences over time. Eze 20:26/31 (VCG-02): God allows the defilement to deepen through judicial release ("I defiled them through their very gifts") and persistent self-defilement results in God refusing to be inquired of — over time the defilement closes relational access with God. Eze 22:3-4 (VCG-02): the city "makes idols to defile herself" — the defilement becomes self-directed and deliberate over time, evolving from an incidental consequence to an intentional act. Jer 2:23 (VCG-02): sustained self-deception about one's own defilement develops — the community can no longer perceive its own condition. Lam 1:17/4:15 (VCG-05): the sustained defilement produces the outcome of being named "a filthy thing among the nations" and expelled — corporate moral identity becomes publicly defiled over time.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained possession of this characteristic establish?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Sustained corporate/covenantal defilement establishes: (1) inability to approach God — Eze 20:31 (VCG-02): persistent self-defilement closes divine accessibility; (2) corporate moral blindness to the condition — Jer 2:23 (VCG-02): "I am not unclean" — the self-deceptive claim; (3) land rendered inhabitable — Mic 2:10 (VCG-04): "no place to rest, because of uncleanness"; (4) sacred space desecrated and eventually destroyed — Psa 79:1 (VCG-03): temple defiled and Jerusalem ruined; (5) the corporate name becoming a byword of defilement — Eze 22:5 (VCG-05): "your name is defiled"; (6) social expulsion from the covenant community and from the land (Lam 4:15, VCG-05; Hos 9:3, VCG-05: "they shall eat unclean food in Assyria").

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. The immediate response varies from denial (Jer 2:23) to simmering grief (Gen 34:5) to jealousy (Num 5:14). The sustained effect is a comprehensive corporate deterioration: self-deception solidifying, divine access closing, land rendered uninhabitable, sanctuary destroyed, name defiled, community expelled. The sustained effect is thus more comprehensive and structurally damaging than any immediate response — it shows the characteristic as accumulating over time across all the relational and spatial domains the community inhabits, culminating in the exile and divine abandonment evidenced in the VCG-05 corpus.

---

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Three enabling inner conditions are evidenced. (1) Sustained unfaithfulness of the corporate will: 2Ch 36:14 (VCG-03) — "exceedingly unfaithful, following all the abominations of the nations" — habitual corporate unfaithfulness is the primary enabling condition. (2) Desire-driven idolatrous lust: Eze 23:7 (VCG-02) — "she defiled herself with all the idols of everyone after whom she lusted" — unchecked corporate desire for what lies outside the covenant is the engine. (3) Deliberate will-act of desecration: Jer 7:30 (VCG-03) — the sons of Judah "set their detestable things in the house that is called by my name, to defile it" — the will's deliberate choice to install what defiles in the sacred space. H2931 (ta.me adj, 11V): "unclean" — the stative form describing the resulting condition of persons, places, and names (Eze 22:5: "your name is defiled"; Lam 4:15: "Unclean!"). H5079 (nid.dah, 9V, from nādad — "to flee, to wander"): "impurity/menstrual impurity" — used metaphorically in this characteristic's register for the community's corporate moral repugnance before God (Eze 36:17: "like the uncleanness of a woman in her menstrual impurity"; Lam 1:17: "a filthy thing").

**T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term — and what does that range reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — H2930A ta.me operates in both qal (intransitive: "become unclean" — Lev 18:20: "make yourself unclean with her") and piel/hiphil forms (causative: "defile/render unclean" — Jer 7:30: "they have set... to defile it"; Eze 5:11: "you have defiled my sanctuary").
