### Step 3 — Sub-group definitions

**M10c-A — Bodily-contact defilement-state**
Characteristic: The inner-being condition of ritual uncleanness produced through contact with a defiling agent — corpse, bodily discharge, swarming creature, unclean animal carcass. The unclean state is time-bound, acquired through specific bodily action, and resolved by washing and waiting. Inner-being content: awareness of contamination, duty of watchful restraint over bodily action, distress at exclusion, conscience responding to inadvertent violation. Structural opposite (M12 purity) present throughout.

**M10c-B — Categorical/classificatory unclean-state**
Characteristic: The inner-being condition of uncleanness determined by priestly examination and verdict, or by inherent categorical designation. Applies to skin disease (leprosy), animal categories, and objects declared persistently unclean. The unclean state is announced by authority, may be permanent or progressive, and requires discernment from the priest. Inner-being content: the duty of moral-spiritual discernment (Lev 10:10, 11:47), the experience of enforced communal isolation and public self-declaration of contamination (Lev 13:45-46), the grief of exclusion (Num 9:7). Structural opposite (M12 purity/discernment) present.

**M10c-C — Moral-inner defilement-state**
Characteristic: Defilement as the inner condition of moral corruption — the conscience, will, and heart corrupted by desire, sin, and idolatrous attachment. The locus of defilement is the inner person itself; it is not acquired by contact but by volitional defection or persistent moral failure. This is M10c's primary NT register and extends into Hebrew prophetic metaphor. Inner-being content: conscience defiled (1Cor 8:7), body and spirit defiled (2Cor 7:1), impurity as prior master over the will (Rom 6:19), the heart's allegiance disorder equated with idolatry (Eph 5:5), the mind and conscience of the defiled person (Tit 1:15). Structural opposite (M12 purity/holiness) present.

**M10c-D — Corporate/covenantal defilement**
Characteristic: Defilement extending from persons or community to land, sanctuary, and the divine name — the sacral and relational pollution produced by corporate moral defection. The inner-being dimension: the corporate will defecting from God; hearts unfaithful in covenant (Eze 20:43 — memory of defilement producing self-loathing); the community's inner corruption rendering their offerings and sacred spaces defiled (Hag 2:14). This characteristic includes both the defilement produced by sin against others (sexual relational — Num 5, Gen 34, Lev 18) and the corporate apostasy register (idolatry defiling sanctuary and land — extensive Ezekiel). Structural opposite (M12 purity/holiness of sacred space and name) present.

**M10c-E — Defilement by external spiritual agency**
Characteristic: The inner-being condition of defilement produced by an unclean spirit inhabiting, controlling, and corrupting a person. The mechanism is external — an unclean spiritual force enters and dominates the person's inner and bodily life. The result is a defilement-state in the person: their faculties suppressed, their will overridden, their communal access severed. Inner-being content: total domination of will and bodily agency (Luk 8:29), suppression of speech and hearing (Mar 9:25), violent affliction (Luk 9:42), recognition of divine superiority by the spirits (Mar 3:11). Structural opposite (M12 purity/wholeness — Luk 6:18 "restored wholeness") present.
