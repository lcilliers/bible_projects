## Your task — per-term verdict

For each of the **8 terms** in §2 of the constitution report, read the term's full meaning corpus and assign exactly one verdict:

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **STAYS** | The term's meaning corpus aligns with M10c's characteristic (defilement-state register). Term remains in M10c. |
| **STAYS (with cross-register flag)** | Per v2_8 §6.3.2: even where the term's primary register lies elsewhere, **STAYS** if any verse in the corpus evidences a relationship with M10c's characteristic. Flag the primary register (e.g. M10 for sin-defilement; M11 for cleansing focus; M12 for purity opposite; M10b for abomination contexts; M07 for nid.dah social-stigma). |
| **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster_code}** | The term's meaning corpus aligns with a different cluster's characteristic AND no verse evidences any M10c-relational content. Accidental placement. Name the target cluster from §4. |
| **BOUNDARY** | The term's meaning corpus is supportive, qualifying, or genuinely undecided. Hold for researcher review. Cite the analytical question + which §6.3.1 valid reason applies. |

Each verdict must:
1. Cite specific verses or meaning patterns from the term's corpus.
2. Be grounded in the inner-being content of the meanings — not the gloss alone.

**Expected outcome:** all 8 terms STAY (the cluster is tightly defilement-focused). Cross-register flags likely on most of the eight.

## §6.3.2 — Verse-level relationship test for TRANSFERS (CRITICAL)

A TRANSFERS verdict requires more than "the term's meaning corpus aligns primarily with another cluster's characteristic." Corpus-primary-register reasoning alone is too aggressive.

**The test, verse by verse:**

1. Read every verse in the term's meaning corpus.
2. For each verse, ask: *does this verse evidence any relationship with or impact on M10c's characteristic (defilement-state register — the inner-being condition of uncleanness)?* "Relationship" is broad: direct evidence, structural opposite (purity-by-contrast, holiness-by-contrast), instrument-of (what defiles), response-to (cleansing), corporate effect (land/sanctuary/name defiled).

**Verdict rule:**

- If **any** verse evidences a relationship with M10c → **STAYS** with cross-register flag in the rationale.
- If **no** verse evidences any M10c relationship → **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster}** confirmed (accidental placement).

## Disallowed BOUNDARY reasons (per v2_8 §6.3.1)

BOUNDARY may **NOT** be assigned solely because:

- The term's meaning corpus is predominantly horizontal (ritual / cultic) rather than vertical (God-directed).
- The meanings describe sensory / material / bodily defilement rather than overtly spiritual content. **M10c is BY DEFINITION about bodily/material defilement extending into the inner being.** Do not let "bodily" be a BOUNDARY ground.
- The meanings describe an inner-being state you would prefer not to be in scope.

Valid BOUNDARY reasons (cite one of these three):

1. **Cluster-membership undecided** — corpus is genuinely on the borderline between M10c and another cluster.
2. **Homonymic / polysemic spread** — the term covers two or more distinct registers and may need sense-split.
3. **Supportive / qualifying register** — the term's meanings enhance / qualify M10c's characteristic without itself carrying it as a primary characteristic.

## Specific signals to attend to (M10c pre-debate notes from CC)

### A. Expected STAYS — clear primary defilement vocabulary

These are extremely likely to STAY (all 8 are defilement vocabulary by design):

- **H2930A ta.me (verb, 128V)** — the central defilement verb. Spans ritual (corpse / leprosy / nid.dah), corporate (defile land / sanctuary), and metaphorical (defile by sin / idolatry) uses. Almost certainly STAYS.
- **H2931 ta.me (adj, 66V)** — the "unclean" adjective. Ritual classification primarily; some moral-state uses (Isa 64:6 "we are all as an unclean thing"). STAYS.
- **H5079 nid.dah (24V)** — "impurity," primarily menstrual but extends to moral impurity (Eze 36:17). STAYS. Possible M07 cross-register flag on the social-stigma overtones (some uses connote shame/abhorrence).
- **G0169 akathartos (30V)** — Greek "unclean." NT register covers (a) unclean spirits, (b) unclean foods (Acts 10), (c) moral uncleanness (Eph 5:5, Rev 17:4). STAYS. The "unclean spirits" sub-corpus may carry M27 (idolatry/cosmic-evil) cross-register flag.
- **G0167 akatharsia (10V)** — Greek "impurity," NT-distinctive moral-defilement register (vice-list contexts: Rom 1:24, 6:19, 2Cor 12:21, Gal 5:19, Eph 4:19, 5:3, Col 3:5, 1Th 2:3, 4:7). STAYS — pure M10c inner-moral-defilement register.
- **G3435 molunō (3V)** — "to defile" verb. Rev 14:4 (not defiled with women), 1Cor 8:7 (conscience defiled), Rev 3:4 (not defiled garments). STAYS.
- **G3436 molusmos (1V)** — "defilement," 2Cor 7:1 "every defilement of body and spirit." STAYS — the locus classicus for M10c's inner-moral-defilement register.
- **G3394 miasmos (1V)** — "defilement," 2Pet 2:10 (those who follow the defiling passion of the flesh). STAYS.

### B. Cross-register watches

**M12 Purity (structural opposite — strongest flag).** Defilement defines itself against purity. Many ta.me uses sit in priestly distinguish-clean-from-unclean contexts (Lev 10:10, 11:47, Eze 22:26, 44:23). Most M10c terms likely carry an M12 cross-register flag as their structural opposite.

**M10 (sin-act → defilement-state boundary).** When ta.me describes the state produced BY sinning (Lev 18:24-28 "the land became defiled" by Canaanite practices; Eze 36:17 Israel defiled the land "by their way and by their deeds"), the verse evidences both registers — the sin-act (M10) producing the defilement-state (M10c). Cross-register flag expected.

**M10b (abomination contexts).** Some ta.me / nid.dah verses occur in idolatrous-abomination contexts (Eze "defiled with their idols"). M10b owns the moral-character abomination judgement; M10c owns the defilement-state. Cross-register expected.

**M11 (Repentance/Forgiveness/Restoration — cleansing response).** Verses focusing on the cleansing response to defilement (Psa 51:7 "purge me with hyssop"; Eze 36:25 "I will sprinkle clean water on you"; Heb 9:14 "purify your conscience"; 1Jn 1:7 "blood cleanses from all sin") sit on the M10c ↔ M11 boundary. Where the verse foregrounds the cleansing act, M11 cross-register flag.

**M07 (Shame/Disgrace).** Nid.dah's menstrual / social-stigma register carries shame connotations. Some uses may flag M07.

**M27 (Evil/Wickedness/Abomination — idolatry sub-register).** "Unclean spirits" (akathartos NT) belong squarely to M27's cosmic-evil-agent territory. The 7+ NT verses about unclean spirits will need careful handling — most likely STAYS with strong M27 cross-register flag.

### C. The NT inner-moral-defilement register (critical)

Several NT verses extend defilement INWARD — the locus classicus for M10c's inner-being claim:

- **Mat 15:18-20** — "what comes out of the mouth … defiles a person … evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, fornications, thefts, false witnesses, blasphemies. These are what defile a person."
- **Mar 7:15, 7:18, 7:20, 7:23** — same teaching
- **Tit 1:15** — "to the defiled both their mind and conscience are defiled"
- **2Cor 7:1** — "let us cleanse ourselves from every defilement of body and spirit"
- **1Cor 8:7** — "their conscience, being weak, is defiled"
- **Rev 21:27** — "nothing unclean shall ever enter it"

These verses are unambiguously M10c (inner moral defilement) and should evidence "any verse in the corpus" relationship even when the term's primary register is elsewhere.
