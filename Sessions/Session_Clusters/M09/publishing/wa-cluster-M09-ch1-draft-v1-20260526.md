## 1. What this study is

This study examines six related inner-life characteristics that together describe what it looks like for a human person to exist without self-assertion — before God and before others. The six are humility, lowliness, submission, contrition, meekness, and willing-heartedness, together with dignity as the settled character quality that gives the whole group its gravity. They are not a random collection of virtues. Each names a distinct form of non-self-assertive inner existence, and the six together map that territory comprehensively: from the voluntary downward movement of willed self-lowering, through the experience of having lowness imposed from outside, to the inner disposition of yielded will, the relational pattern of obedience lived out, the acute brokenness of contrition before God's holiness, the calibrated compassion that meekness enables, the settled moral gravity of dignity, and the freely-moving spirit of willing-heartedness. That these characteristics belong together is not an accident of proximity. They share a governing theme: the inner person who neither asserts nor promotes the self, in six distinct registers.

The cluster carries a strong pattern at the divine level. Where these characteristics are attributed to God, they are attributed not to the Father in abstract but to the incarnate Son: as Philippians 2:8 shows, "he humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." The Son's self-lowering and submission are the Christological hinge of the cluster — the two characteristics that achieve direct divine attribution do so because the Son genuinely takes them on in the Incarnation. The remaining characteristics are creaturely in a more exclusive sense. Contrition structurally presupposes moral failure before a holy God and cannot be applied to God himself without incoherence; dignity names a social-ordering quality of creaturely existence; meekness is grounded in shared creaturely weakness; willing-heartedness names the creature's freely-moving will toward God. The cluster is thus not simply a list of human virtues that mirror divine qualities; it is a portrait of the creaturely analogue of the divine character — the way the image of God is expressed through the forms of existence appropriate to creaturely life, asymmetry, and limitation.

The six characteristics are also internally dependent on one another. Humility and contrition meet at the edge of crisis: when humility — a stable posture of voluntary self-lowering — reaches its acute intensity under genuine moral confrontation, it becomes contrition's broken spirit. Humility and submission travel closely together, distinguished by direction: humility is the general posture of self-lowering, submission is that posture specifically directed toward legitimate authority. Meekness is what humility looks like in relational engagement with others in their weakness. Dignity is the settled character quality that gives the non-assertive person their gravity without performance. Willing-heartedness names the spirit that moves freely toward God in worship and service rather than holding back. These are not synonyms; they are neighbours that define each other by their distinctions.

The study treats each characteristic in turn, examining what it is, how it relates to the divine pattern, where it lives in the inner person, how it works, how the characteristics relate to one another, and what human and clinical sciences observe about the corresponding capacities; it closes with an account of what the study has and has not been able to establish.

Five boundary pairs are evidenced within M09 itself. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-3:* Isa57:15 directly evidences the boundary — contrition (dak.ka — acute crisis-state) co-occurs with lowliness (CHAR-1 — stable posture) but is not identical to it; humility is the stable disposition; contrition is the acute intensity-form it takes under specific moral-crisis conditions. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-2:* Phili2:8 holds both (humbled himself by becoming obedient) but the boundary is relational structure — humility is the general self-lowering posture; submission is the specifically authority-directed yielding. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-4:* Mat11:29 holds both (gentle and lowly in heart) — humility is the inner posture of self-lowering; meekness/gentleness is the quality of relational engagement that the posture enables. *CHAR-2 / CHAR-6:* 1Ch28:21 (willing and wholly at your command) holds both — submission is command-responsive; willing-heartedness is self-initiated from inner motivation. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-5:* humility is the active downward movement; dignity is the stable non-assertive settled position. These five intra-cluster boundary distinctions are the cluster's most significant analytical contribution to the inner-being landscape — M09 is a cluster of mutually defining adjacent characteristics.

- *T1.4.1* — _In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster covers four distinct modes of inner operation, and each characteristic is primary in one or two. *Initiating volitional act* (CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-6): the person initiates an inner movement — humbles themselves, yields the will, brings from the willing heart. *Stable character quality* (CHAR-5, CHAR-1 in its dispositional register): the characteristic is the settled inner property shaping all conduct. *Capacity governing engagement* (CHAR-4): the inner ability that shapes the quality of relational contact. *Acute experiential state* (CHAR-3): the inner condition the person finds themselves in, which they sustain rather than generate. The cluster-level finding: no single mode accounts for all six characteristics, which confirms M09's phenomenological diversity. The most critical distinction within the cluster is between CHAR-3 (primarily undergone, state-like) and CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-6 (primarily performed, act-like), with CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 occupying the intermediate capacity-and-quality register.

---
- *T1.4.2* — _Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Context-dependence is evidenced across the cluster with consistent patterns. All six characteristics show variation between the *vertical* (Godward) and *horizontal* (human-to-human) registers. The Godward register is primary for CHAR-3 Contrition (exclusively evidenced as before-God in this corpus) and dominant for CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness. Both CHAR-1 Humility and CHAR-2 Submission have extensively documented both directions (ka.na corpus = vertical; Eph4:2; Phili2:3 = horizontal for CHAR-1; household obedience + gospel obedience for CHAR-2). CHAR-4 Meekness and CHAR-5 Dignity are primarily evidenced in the horizontal/social register (dealing gently with the erring; household and teaching authority). The cluster-level finding: the six characteristics map to different positions on the vertical-horizontal axis, with no single characteristic spanning the full range as completely as CHAR-1 and CHAR-2. This mapping suggests the cluster can be read as a comprehensive account of non-self-assertive existence in both its divine-relational and human-community dimensions.

- *T1.4.3* — _Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — and if so, how does it function?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Speech-adjacent modes are present in four of the six characteristics; two are silent on this. CHAR-1 Humility: speech-adjacent — the humbling is expressed in verbal acknowledgment of God's rightness (2Ch12:6 — "the Lord is righteous") and in receiving God's word as personally claiming. CHAR-2 Submission: auditory-constitutive — the very root of hupakoē is "under-hearing"; the characteristic is constituted by reception-and-yielding to what is heard. CHAR-4 Meekness: indirectly speech-involved — the high priest's gentle dealing (Heb5:2) involves verbal intercession and instruction. CHAR-5 Dignity: directly speech-shaping — Tit2:7 ("in your teaching show… dignity") — the manner of teaching is the characteristic's communicative expression. CHAR-3 Contrition and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness are silent on communicative modes in their direct evidence. Cluster-level: CHAR-2's auditory-constitutive character (hupakoē — under-hearing) makes it unique in the cluster — it is the only characteristic whose very root encodes reception of heard speech as its constitutional origin. CHAR-5's communicative mode (teaching manner) makes it unique as the characteristic most directly expressed in and through verbal instruction.

- *T1.5.1* — _What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The immediate responses cluster into two families. *Divine-toward-person responses* (God's immediate response to the human state): CHAR-3 Contrition — God draws near and saves (Psa34:18); CHAR-1 Humility — God hears and extends mercy (2Ch12:7; Luk18:14). *Human-toward-world responses* (the characteristic's own immediate expression outward): CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness — immediate act of bringing (Exo35:22 — "brought"); CHAR-2 Submission — inner repositioning of will (cognitive-volitional self-assessment in Luk17:10); CHAR-4 Meekness — prompt responsive movement (Act9:38 — the expected unimpeded response); CHAR-5 Dignity — evoked ordered respect in others (1Ti3:4 — orderly compliance). CHAR-1's immediate response is the most multi-register: relational opening toward God (divine direction) and genuine regard toward others (horizontal direction). Cluster-level: the characteristic with the most immediate outward expression is CHAR-6 (willing heart → immediate bringing without friction); the characteristic with the most immediate divine response is CHAR-3 (crushed spirit → Lord draws near). The two poles illustrate the cluster's span: spontaneous outward-moving (CHAR-6) and inward-opened-toward-God (CHAR-3).

- *T1.5.2* — _Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Consistency within characteristics is broadly high; the more analytically significant finding is the *between-characteristic consistency pattern*. All six characteristics evidence a common direction of response: toward openness, toward others, toward God — never toward closure or self-protection. The one divergence is CHAR-6's two-register difference: VCG-01 (immediate act of bringing) and VCG-02 (immediate prayerful seeking of restoration — Psa51:12), which are consistent within their respective registers but different between them. This internal divergence in CHAR-6 is itself the most revealing cluster-level finding at this prompt: willing-heartedness is the characteristic most explicitly showing that the same characteristic's immediate expression differs by the moral-spiritual state of the person. The positive state: immediate outward act. The post-failure state: immediate prayer for restoration. No other M09 characteristic shows this two-register immediate-response pattern as explicitly.

- *T1.5.3* — _Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly._
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The inner phenomenology of *having* the characteristic (what it feels like from the inside) is universally silent across all six characteristics in this cluster. All six evidence the outward expressions or divine responses but not the first-person phenomenological experience of the person who has the characteristic. This cluster-wide silence is itself a finding: M09 is a cluster of *other-directed* characteristics whose evidence is primarily about effects on others and divine responses rather than about the subjective experience of the person possessing them. This distinguishes M09 from clusters (such as grief or joy clusters) where the phenomenology is directly addressed. The silence is consistent across all six and suggests that the characteristic of truly non-self-assertive existence is precisely that it does not call attention to itself, even in Scripture's description.

- *T1.6.1* — _What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The sustained productions reveal a cumulative portrait of the mature inner person shaped by M09. CHAR-1 Humility produces: wisdom (Pro11:2), contentment (Phili4:12), corporate harmony (Eph4:2), divine favour, teachability. CHAR-2 Submission produces: soul-purification (1Pe1:22), sincere brotherly love, learned costly dispositionality (Heb5:8), reliable character orientation. CHAR-3 Contrition produces: reviving of spirit and heart (Isa57:15) — the restoration that God's response to contrition generates. CHAR-4 Meekness produces: sustained self-awareness of shared limitation and habitual readiness-without-friction. CHAR-5 Dignity produces: sustained ordered social spheres — peaceful public life, orderly household, credible teaching. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness produces: habitual generous offering, wholehearted skilled service, sustained joyful devotion. Cluster-level: the productions together describe a person who is wise, loving, teachable, self-aware, socially ordered, and joyfully generous — the cumulative portrait of the M09-formed inner person. Notably, each characteristic produces something the others do not: wisdom (uniquely CHAR-1), love from a pure heart (uniquely CHAR-2), revived spirit and heart (uniquely CHAR-3), self-aware empathy (uniquely CHAR-4), social ordering (uniquely CHAR-5), joyful generosity (uniquely CHAR-6).

- *T1.6.2* — _What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Taking the six sustained-effect findings together reveals the inner-being profile of the person formed by the M09 cluster. The profile includes: *relational openness* (CHAR-1 — sustained humility keeps the person accessible to God's word); *inward purity and love capacity* (CHAR-2 — sustained submission purifies the soul and produces love); *revived vitality* (CHAR-3 — God's sustained response to contrition produces renewed spirit and heart); *self-aware empathic presence* (CHAR-4 — sustained possession establishes the capacity for genuine other-awareness grounded in one's own limitation); *composure and ordering influence* (CHAR-5 — sustained dignity orders the social spheres the person inhabits); *habitual generosity and joyful devotion* (CHAR-6 — sustained willing-heartedness establishes the pattern of beyond-required giving). Together these establish an inner person who is *open, pure, vital, empathic, composed, and generous* — the positive characterological portrait implicit in M09's six characteristics. No characteristic duplicates another's sustained production; the six are complementary rather than redundant.

- *T1.6.3* — _Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Yes, for all six characteristics, and the direction of difference is consistent across the cluster: the immediate response is *event-level* (a relational opening, a divine drawing near, an act of bringing); the sustained effect is *character-level* (wisdom, love, empathy, ordering influence). This event-to-character progression is the consistent transformation arc across M09 — the characteristic begins as an inner act or state and, through repetition and divine response, becomes a settled inner quality of the person. CHAR-1's movement from ka.na event to tapeinofrosunē disposition is the most vocabulary-rich instance of this progression. CHAR-2's movement from obedience-event to "obedient children" identity (1Pe1:14) is the most identity-forming. CHAR-3's movement from crushed-state to revived spirit-and-heart is the most constitutionally penetrating. CHAR-6's movement from single-act-of-bringing to habitual-generosity-pattern is the most agency-expanding. The cluster's sustained effects consistently represent a deepening of the immediate response into stable character.

- *T1.7.1* — _What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The enabling conditions form a convergent cluster of inner orientations. CHAR-1 Humility: heart-tenderness, capacity to receive God's word as personally claiming, absence of self-enclosure. CHAR-2 Submission: fear of God, sincere-heart genuineness, faith (trusting the authority's word before comprehending it). CHAR-3 Contrition: openness to genuine moral self-confrontation rather than self-defence. CHAR-4 Meekness: genuine awareness of one's own shared creaturely weakness (Heb5:2). CHAR-5 Dignity: not directly addressed in the three-verse corpus. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: genuine inner-motivated orientation toward God (VCG-01) or reception of divine gift (VCG-02). The cluster-level finding: the enabling conditions converge on *self-knowledge, openness, and genuine inner orientation toward God*. Two characteristics are notable: CHAR-4 specifies the enabling condition most precisely — the self-awareness of shared creaturely weakness is the one condition that uniquely grounds metriopatheō; CHAR-3 specifies it most negatively — the enabling condition is precisely the *absence* of self-justification and self-protection. Together the enabling conditions confirm that M09 characteristics are accessible to those who have genuinely reckoned with their own inner reality — they are not accessible to the self-enclosed.

- *T1.7.2* — _What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The blocking conditions across the cluster share a common structure: *self-enclosure in some form*. Pride and self-exaltation block CHAR-1. Eye-service and inner refusal block CHAR-2. Hardening, self-justification, and denial block CHAR-3. Prideful distance from one's own weakness (failing to acknowledge shared creaturely limitation) blocks CHAR-4. M08 proud self-display and moral frivolousness block CHAR-5. Compulsion and moral failure's burden block CHAR-6. The cluster-level finding: the blocking conditions map precisely onto the structural opposites identified at T1.3.1 — they are not separate findings but the same anatomy viewed from the inside. What blocks M09 characteristics is the same family of self-asserting, self-protecting, self-enclosing inner orientations that the cluster's characteristics resist and exclude. The cluster is internally coherent: what it is, it excludes; what excludes it is what it is not.

- *T1.7.3* — _What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The non-receiving person is most directly described for CHAR-1 (pride-in-operation: self-enclosed, self-elevating, resistant to divine address) and CHAR-2 (inner refusal: some form of self-assertion placing personal judgment over legitimate authority's claim). The other four characteristics are largely silent on this at the verse level — they describe givers and receivers who are present and responsive rather than those who encounter the characteristic and remain closed. The cluster-level inference: the silence on non-receiving states in CHAR-3 through CHAR-6 is consistent with the cluster's orientation toward those who are genuinely present and open. M09's evidence is weighted toward the person who has or is forming the characteristic; the anatomy of the hardened, resistant person who encounters M09 and refuses it is described most fully only in M09's structural opposite (M08 Pride and its distortions). The cluster effectively defers the detailed anatomy of non-reception to M08's evidence register.

- *T1.8.1* — _What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The six primary dimensions reveal the cluster's dimensional architecture. CHAR-1 Humility: *volitional-dispositional*. CHAR-2 Submission: *volitional*. CHAR-3 Contrition: *affective-existential*. CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness: *volitional-character* (capacity). CHAR-5 Dignity: *characterological* (comprehensive stable quality). CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: *volitional-affective*. Three patterns emerge at cluster level. (a) *Volitional dominance*: four of the six have volition as their primary dimension (CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-4, CHAR-6) — M09 is pre-eminently a volitional cluster, confirming the cluster's governing theme of non-coerced, freely-given inner orientation. (b) *The affective exceptions*: CHAR-3 is primarily affective-existential; CHAR-6 uniquely combines volitional with affective (the gladly-willing) — the affective dimension is present but not primary in most characteristics. (c) *The characterological outlier*: CHAR-5 is the only characteristic with a characterological-primary classification — making it the most broadly-reaching and the most stable, the cluster's "resting place" rather than its movement-characteristics.

- *T1.8.2* — _What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The dimensional classifications are confirmed by the convergence of grammatical and verse evidence. The volitional-primary characteristics (CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-4, CHAR-6) are all addressed in the imperative or in verb-dominant constructions: "humble yourselves" (Jam4:10), "obey" (multiple), "can deal gently" (Heb5:2 — capacity verb), "let him bring" (Exo35:5 — the willing heart generates the act). The affective-primary characteristic (CHAR-3) is addressed in participial and adjectival constructions that name states: "brokenhearted," "crushed in spirit" — person-states, not acts. The characterological characteristic (CHAR-5) is addressed in noun-of-quality form: semnotēs — the settled property of the whole person. This grammatical confirmation is itself a cluster-level finding: the grammatical forms of the primary terms encode the primary dimensions — imperative/verbal for volitional; adjectival/participial for affective-state; noun-of-quality for characterological.

- *T1.8.3* — _Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics carry secondary dimensions that confirm the cluster's internal coherence. The secondary dimensions are distributed so that each characteristic's secondary dimensions partially overlap with adjacent characteristics' primary dimensions: CHAR-1's secondary cognitive and affective dimensions overlap with CHAR-3 (affective primary) and CHAR-2 (cognitive-volitional capture at 2Cor10:5). CHAR-5's secondary volitional dimension overlaps with CHAR-1 and CHAR-2. CHAR-6's secondary relational-Godward dimension overlaps with CHAR-2 and CHAR-3. The cluster-level finding: the secondary dimensions create a web of mutual support — each characteristic in M09 is supported by secondary dimensions that resemble the primary dimensions of its neighbours. This accounts for the cluster's phenomenological cohesion: the six characteristics are genuinely distinct but constitutively adjacent, sharing dimensional architecture in a way that makes the cluster a unified family rather than an accidental collection.
