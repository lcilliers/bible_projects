## VCG design

### M09-D-VCG-01 — Gospel-obedience: the pattern of inner yielding in response to the gospel

**Context description:** This VCG carries the gospel-obedience register — obedience of faith as both the response to the gospel proclamation and the defining characteristic of gospel-formed life. Obedience of faith is the goal of Paul's apostolic mission to the nations (Rom1:5) and the culminating inner response to the gospel's worldwide disclosure (Rom16:26). Obeying the gospel and believing it are inseparable inner acts of the will (Rom10:16) — the gospel addressed to the ear demands an inner yielding, and many heard without yielding. The Gentiles' transformed response of word and deed orienting life toward God is the obedience Christ accomplishes through Paul (Rom15:18). The Roman community's publicly-known characteristic obedience — their will's disposition — is then redirected by Paul toward discernment of good (Rom16:19). The priests becoming obedient to the faith (Act6:7) are the most dramatic single instance of this pattern — a decisive reorientation of allegiance at the gospel's claim. Failing to obey the gospel (2Th1:8) names the opposite inner act with its eschatological consequence, defining the gospel-obedience pattern by its absence. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).

### M09-D-VCG-02 — Soteriological and sanctifying telos of obedience

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of obedience as a soteriological and sanctifying telos — the orientation of the will that connects the person to Christ's salvation and that is the Spirit's goal in sanctification. Christ becomes the source of eternal salvation only to those who obey him — obedience is the ongoing inner orientation of trust and submission that is the condition of receiving his benefit (Heb5:9). Obedience to the gospel as faith-rooted inner yielding to Christ's lordship expressing itself in generous giving and communal glorifying of God (2Cor9:13) sits here as the submission-to-Christ register. Obedience as the goal-state toward which the Spirit's sanctifying work directs believers — an inner orientation of the will toward Jesus Christ (1Pe1:2) — and as the defining identity of 'obedient children' whose will is structured against former passions (1Pe1:14). Refusing apostolic obedience (2Th3:14) is included here: the communal response designed to awaken the inner refusal to repentance, making non-obedience within the soteriological-obedience frame. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).

### M09-D-VCG-03 — Household and relational obedience patterns

**Context description:** This VCG carries the household and relational register of the submission-obedience pattern — obedience as a shaped quality within the authority structures of daily life. Children's obedience to parents is grounded in the Lord, making it a theologically motivated act of will rather than mere social compliance (Eph6:1); the same pattern in Col3:20 adds that the deepest reason for compliance is the Lord's pleasure. The overseer's children's submissiveness reflects the father's character and the household order he has cultivated — showing submission as a shaped, household-formed inner quality (1Ti3:4). Pro30:17 supplies the Hebrew wisdom parallel: obedience to a mother is the expected inner response whose absence marks fatal moral failure. Together these four evidence the pattern of submission-obedience as it is concretely shaped within family and relational authority structures — theologically grounded but expressed in the everyday register of household life. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).

### M09-D-VCG-04 — Personal and eschatological allegiance-submission

**Context description:** This VCG carries the personal and eschatological allegiance register of the submission pattern — obedience as a directed disposition of will toward a named authority in a personal or eschatological frame. Gen49:10 is the most distinctive: the willing inner yielding of allegiance directed toward Judah's coming ruler, framed as a future gathering of nations under his sovereign authority — eschatological submission as the culminating act of allegiance. Phile21 is personal and concrete: Paul's confidence in Philemon's reliable inner disposition of will oriented toward doing good beyond the minimum, oriented toward a named person within a specific relational request. Both verses evidence the pattern of submission as personally-directed allegiance toward an authority — one cosmic-eschatological, one immediate-relational. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).
