## §1 — Characteristics identified

Reading across all 109 verse meanings, M09's corpus evidences **six distinct inner-being faculties/states:**

### C1 — HUMILITY (two-register characteristic — volume-split required)

The most voluminous characteristic in M09. The verse corpus evidences two closely-related but distinct registers of the same inner-being faculty:

**C1a — Willed self-lowering:** The person's active, volitional inner act of placing themselves lower than their natural standing — before God, before others, or in response to divine address. The will chooses its own downward posture. This is the dispositional, active register of humility. Evidenced across the tapein- root family (voluntary instances), ka.na (voluntary humbling before God), tsa.na, tsa.nu.a.

**C1b — Experienced lowliness:** The state of finding oneself low — whether by social condition, external affliction, or God's judicial action. The person is in a lowly state; the faculty is the experience of that state and its inner-being implications. Evidenced in tapeinōsis throughout, tapeinos in its condition-senses, tapeinoō in its circumstantial/imposed instances, ka.na in its externally-imposed instances.

**Split-axis:** Voluntary self-lowering (dispositional/active) vs Experienced lowliness (condition/state/imposed). Both registers belong to one characteristic — C1. Phase 8.7 will bind M09-A and M09-B under a single `characteristic` row named HUMILITY.

**Volume justification for split:** Combined C1 corpus = ~50 vc_ids = 45.9% of 109 → exceeds 40% gate. Split produces M09-A (~37 = 33.9%) and M09-B (~13 = 11.9%), both within gate.

---

### C2 — SUBMISSION OF WILL (two-register characteristic — volume-split required)

The will's inner disposition of yielding to a legitimate authority — God, apostolic authority, the gospel, household structures. The characteristic is constituted in the relationship between the one who submits and the authority they yield to. The corpus evidences two registers:

**C2a — Inner disposition of will-submission:** Verses where the meaning explicitly locates the obedience/submission in the heart, conscience, will, or spirit — naming the inner state directly. The quality is described as a genuine inner posture, not merely an outer behaviour.

**C2b — Relational pattern of obedience:** Verses where the meaning describes obedience/submission as a lived pattern, a characteristic of the person or community, a relational telos, or its notable absence — the outer expression and corporate dimension of the same characteristic.

**Split-axis:** Inner-disposition (heart/conscience-located) vs Relational-pattern (lived expression/corporate/behavioural). Grounded in the science extract's distinction between internalised obedience and behavioural compliance, and confirmed in the meanings. Both registers carry the same characteristic; Phase 8.7 will bind M09-C and M09-D under a single `characteristic` row named SUBMISSION.

**Volume note:** Without diatassō's 11 flagged verses, C2 = 37 vc_ids (34%) — within gate. With diatassō's 11 included (per Option C decision), C2 = 47 vc_ids (43.1%) — over gate. Split produces M09-C (17 = 15.6%) and M09-D (30 = 27.5%), both within gate. diatassō's 11 non-M09-content verses are in M09-D and flagged for Phase 8.5 disposal.

**Cross-register flag carried forward:** G5218 hupakoē, G5219 hupakouō, H3349 yiq.qe.hah flagged M30 (Obedience — register-adjacent). G1299 diatassō flagged M23 (authority/directive — primary register; only Luk17:10 carries M09 content).

---

### C3 — CONTRITION

The acute, grief-laden inner brokenness of spirit that follows genuine confrontation with one's own moral failure. Distinct from dispositional humility — contrition is emotionally loaded, crisis-precipitated, spirit-located, and specifically responsive to the weight of sin or failure. God's nearness and restorative action is the direct correlate in both attestations. Evidenced by H1793A dak.ka.

Science resonance: the science extract identifies contrition as "the acute form of humility produced by genuine confrontation with one's failure" — distinct from dispositional humility and carrying a more somatic quality (crushed, broken posture).

---

### C4 — MEEKNESS / GENTLENESS

The inner capacity for calibrated, compassionate restraint — the quality of holding strength or capability without deploying it inappropriately. Meekness in this register is not absence of strength but governed strength; gentleness is not absence of force but measured, vulnerability-aware engagement. Evidenced by G3356 metriopatheō (measured compassionate restraint rooted in shared weakness) and G3635 okneō (volitional readiness as the absence of resistant hesitation), plus the gentleness dimension of G5011 tapeinos at Mat11:29.

Science resonance: the science extract defines this as "controlled strength — the capacity to restrain force when it could be used."

---

### C5 — DIGNITY

The inner quality of settled moral gravity and seriousness of character — grounded worth expressed as composed, non-self-displaying gravitas. Dignity in this register is the structural opposite of proud self-display: it is the security of character that does not need to prove itself through ostentation, aggression, or status-seeking. Evidenced by G4587 semnotēs in all three verses.

Cross-register flag carried forward: M08 (Pride/Arrogance/Boasting — semnotēs is the structural opposite of proud self-display; advisory for Phase 9 T6).

---

### C6 — WILLING-HEARTEDNESS

The inner state in which the will moves freely and gladly toward giving, service, or devotion without external compulsion. The willing spirit is not neutral compliance but a positively-charged inner disposition — the heart that is moved, not dragged. Includes the dimension of willing-heartedness as a divine gift that must be sought or restored (Psa 51:12 — penitential context). Evidenced by H5081G na.div in all five verses.

Cross-register flags carried forward: M04 (Joy/Gladness — spontaneous generous-delight dimension); M29 (Desire/Will — volitional dimension). Advisory for Phase 9 T6.

## §2 — Sub-group design

Eight sub-groups carrying six characteristics (C1 and C2 each volume-split into two sub-groups):

---

### M09-A — Humility as willed self-lowering

**Characteristic:** C1 (Humility) — C1a register (voluntary/dispositional)
**Split-axis:** Voluntary self-lowering — the willed, active inner posture of self-diminishment
**Label:** Humility — willed self-lowering

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the dispositional, active register of humility — the inner act by which a person chooses their own downward posture before God, before others, or in response to divine address. The meanings evidence this as a genuine inner faculty of the will: the person lowers themselves rather than asserting their status, counts others as more significant, or opens the self toward God in the opposite of proud self-claim. The voluntary humbling before God in the Hebrew register (ka.na) is particularly weighty in this sub-group, spanning the great figures of Israel's history choosing to humble themselves in response to God's word and judgment. The Greek tapein- root family supplies the most explicit vocabulary: tapeinofrosunē as the inner disposition of counting others more significant, tapeinoō as the willed act of self-lowering, tapeinos as the character-quality of the person who lives this way. Mic 6:8's comprehensive orientation of walking humbly with God and Pro 11:2's wisdom-opening humility complete the register. Luk 3:5 (tapeinoō — landscape imagery) is carried here pending Phase 5.5 set-aside resolution.

**Primary terms:** G5011 tapeinos (5 verses), G5012 tapeinofrosunē (7 verses), G5013 tapeinoō (9 voluntary instances + Luk3:5), H3665 ka.na (14 voluntary/negative instances), H6800A tsa.na (1), H6800B tsa.nu.a (1)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 37
**% of substantive corpus:** 33.9%

---

### M09-B — Lowliness as experienced/imposed state

**Characteristic:** C1 (Humility) — C1b register (condition/state/imposed)
**Split-axis:** Experienced lowliness — the state of being low rather than the act of self-lowering
**Label:** Lowliness — experienced and imposed state

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the condition-register of the humility characteristic — the state of finding oneself low, whether by social condition, circumstantial affliction, or God's judicial or transformative action on the inner person. The person is in a lowly state; the inner-being content is the experience of that state and its implications. Tapeinōsis carries the most explicit condition vocabulary: Mary's lowly estate, Christ's imposed humiliation, the creaturely body awaiting transformation, the rich person confronting their transience. The tapeinos verses in this sub-group include the downcast condition God meets (2Cor 7:6) and the lowly whose condition God reverses (Luk 1:52, Jam 1:9). Ka.na's imposed instances — God humbling as judicial act, Israel subjected under enemies, hearts bowed down by labor — belong here. The relationship between C1a and C1b is the relationship between the disposition that accepts lowliness and the experience of finding oneself there; both are humility.

**Primary terms:** G5011 tapeinos (3 condition verses), G5013 tapeinoō (2 imposed instances), G5014 tapeinōsis (4 verses), H3665 ka.na (4 imposed instances)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 13
**% of substantive corpus:** 11.9%

---

### M09-C — Submission as inner disposition of will

**Characteristic:** C2 (Submission of Will) — C2a register (heart/conscience-located)
**Split-axis:** Inner disposition — meanings that explicitly locate the submission in the heart, conscience, will, or spirit
**Label:** Submission — inner disposition of will

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the inner-disposition register of the submission-of-will characteristic — verses whose meanings explicitly name the inner location of the obedience or submission. The heart conforms to a pattern of teaching (Rom 6:17), the conscience is sustained in its obedience under pressure (Phili 2:12, Col 3:22), the will makes a decisive act to withhold submission or offer it (Gal 2:5), the soul itself is purified through obedience (1Pe 1:22), the will responds from sincere heart to Christ (Eph 6:5). Christ's obedience is a quality acquired by his will through costly engagement with suffering (Heb 5:8). Abraham's faith-driven inner response drives his action before he comprehends the destination (Heb 11:8). The disposition of fear and trembling as reverential inner orientation (2Cor 7:15) and the taking of every thought captive as the inner prelude to submission (2Cor 10:5, 10:6) are the most explicitly inner-located instances. G1299 diatassō Luk17:10 belongs here: the servant's inner posture of self-assessment rejecting merit, produced by the context of obedient direction-following.

**Primary terms:** G5218 hupakoē (6 verses), G5219 hupakouō (8 verses), G5292 hupotagē (2 verses), G1299 diatassō (1 verse — Luk17:10 only)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 17
**% of substantive corpus:** 15.6%
**Cross-register flags:** M30 (hupakoē, hupakouō — obedience register-adjacent); M23 (diatassō — primary register; 1 verse only in M09)

---

### M09-D — Submission as relational pattern of obedience

**Characteristic:** C2 (Submission of Will) — C2b register (relational pattern/lived expression)
**Split-axis:** Relational pattern — meanings describing obedience as a characteristic, corporate telos, or lived expression
**Label:** Submission — relational pattern of obedience

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the relational-pattern register of the submission-of-will characteristic — verses whose meanings describe obedience or submission as a lived pattern, a defining characteristic of the person or community, a relational goal, or its notable absence. The obedience of faith as the goal of Paul's apostolic mission and the result of gospel disclosure (Rom 1:5, 16:26), the community's publicly-known obedience (Rom 16:19), the pattern of obedience that defines the believer's identity (1Pe 1:14), and the soteriological telos of ongoing obedience connecting the believer to Christ (Heb 5:9) all belong here. The eschatological gathering of peoples in submission before Judah's coming ruler (Gen 49:10) and household-order submission as cultivated relational quality (1Ti 3:4) are also relational-pattern instances. G1299 diatassō's 11 verses with no M09 inner-being content are routed here as the authority-relationship register nearest to this sub-group's characteristic; they are **flagged for Phase 8.5 disposition review** and may be set aside or transferred at that phase.

**Primary terms:** G5218 hupakoē (8 verses), G5219 hupakouō (7 verses), G5292 hupotagē (2 verses), H3349 yiq.qe.hah (2 verses), G1299 diatassō (11 verses — flagged for Phase 8.5)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 30 (including 11 flagged diatassō verses)
**% of substantive corpus:** 27.5%
**Cross-register flags:** M30 (hupakoē, hupakouō, yiq.qe.hah — obedience register-adjacent); M23 (diatassō — primary register; 11 non-M09-content verses flagged for Phase 8.5)

---

### M09-E — Contrition

**Characteristic:** C3
**Label:** Contrition — crushed and broken spirit

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the contrition characteristic — the acute, grief-laden inner brokenness of spirit that follows genuine confrontation with one's moral failure. In both verses (Psa 34:18 and Isa 57:15), the inner state is located in the spirit and heart, and God's direct response of nearness and reviving is the correlate. Isa 57:15 explicitly pairs contrition with lowliness (placing it in relationship with M09-A's characteristic), and names God's dwelling with the contrite and lowly spirit as the relational outcome. This is the most acute, crisis-precipitated register in M09 — distinct from the dispositional humility of M09-A in its emotional weight and specific relationship to moral failure.

**Primary terms:** H1793A dak.ka (2 verses)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 2
**% of substantive corpus:** 1.8%

---

### M09-F — Meekness / Gentleness

**Characteristic:** C4
**Label:** Meekness — calibrated restraint and gentleness

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the meekness/gentleness characteristic — the inner capacity for governed, compassionate restraint. Three verse-contexts are included: G3356 metriopatheō in Heb 5:2 (the capacity for measured restraint grounded in shared creaturely vulnerability), G3635 okneō in Act 9:38 (the absence of reluctance and resistance — immediate volitional responsiveness), and G5011 tapeinos at Mat 11:29 as secondary assignment (Jesus's self-description as gentle and lowly in heart — the gentleness dimension, with lowliness primary in M09-A). The characteristic in this sub-group is the quality of holding capacity or will without deploying it with force or resistance — the inner disposition that governs itself gently. Mat 11:29 is a MULTI-FACETED verse: primary assignment M09-A (lowly in heart), secondary M09-F (gentle).

**Primary terms:** G3356 metriopatheō (1 verse), G3635 okneō (1 verse)
**Secondary:** G5011 tapeinos Mat11:29 (gentleness dimension)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 2 (primary) + 1 (secondary multi-faceted)
**% of substantive corpus:** 1.8–2.8%

---

### M09-G — Dignity

**Characteristic:** C5
**Label:** Dignity — grounded moral gravity

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the dignity characteristic — the inner quality of settled moral seriousness and gravitas that grounds composed, non-self-displaying conduct. In all three verses, semnotēs names an inner character quality of the person (not a social rank): the sober, weighty seriousness that characterises a peaceable, godly life (1Ti 2:2), the character gravity from which orderly household governance flows (1Ti 3:4), and the gravitas that authenticates teaching character (Tit 2:7). Note: 1Ti 3:4 also carries G5292 hupotagē (routed to M09-D) — two vc_ids at the same verse_record routing to different sub-groups; not a SPLIT_SUBGROUP case (different terms). Dignity as evidenced here is the structural opposite of proud self-display — a person of semnotēs does not need to assert themselves, perform status, or boast.

**Primary terms:** G4587 semnotēs (3 verses)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 3
**% of substantive corpus:** 2.8%
**Cross-register flag:** M08 (structural opposite — dignity precludes proud self-display; advisory for Phase 9 T6)

---

### M09-H — Willing-heartedness

**Characteristic:** C6
**Label:** Willing-heartedness — the freely-moved spirit

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the willing-heartedness characteristic — the inner state in which the will moves freely, gladly, and without compulsion toward giving, service, or devotion. In all five na.div verses, the freely-moved heart or willing spirit is what the offering or service flows from (Exo 35:5, 35:22, 1Ch 28:21, 2Ch 29:31) — the inner disposition that gives without being forced. Psa 51:12 adds a crucial dimension: the willing spirit as a divine gift that must be sought and restored after moral failure, pairing willing-heartedness with the joy of salvation in a penitential context. This places willing-heartedness in M09 as the positive volitional complement to submission — where submission yields to authority, willing-heartedness opens the will gladly toward God and community without the pressure of compulsion. The freely-moved will is the structural opposite of the resistant, self-asserting will of pride.

**Primary terms:** H5081G na.div (5 verses)
**Approximate vc_ids:** 5
**% of substantive corpus:** 4.6%
**Cross-register flags:** M04 (Joy/Gladness — spontaneous generous-delight dimension); M29 (Desire/Will — volitional dimension). Advisory for Phase 9 T6.
