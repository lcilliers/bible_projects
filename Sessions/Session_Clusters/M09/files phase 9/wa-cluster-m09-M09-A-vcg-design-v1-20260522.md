## VCG design

### M09-A-VCG-01 — Voluntary humbling before God: the Hebrew OT register

**Context description:** This VCG carries the Old Testament Hebrew register of the willed self-lowering characteristic — the inner act of ka.na (humbling before God) in response to divine word, impending judgment, or a recognised inner fault. The dominant structure is: inner pressure or divine address → voluntary act of humbling → God's corresponding response of mercy or restored relationship. The humbling is consistently perceived by God as reaching the inner person (heart, spirit) and is directly effective in altering divine action. Negative instances (Amon's refusal in 2Ch33:23; Zedekiah's refusal in 2Ch36:12) define the characteristic by its absence, showing that voluntary self-lowering before God is the expected inner posture. The Hebrew wisdom register contributes Pro11:2 (humility as the inner disposition that opens a person to wisdom, structurally opposed to pride) and Mic6:8 (walking humbly with God as the comprehensive inner orientation of the whole life). Together these 16 verses evidence the Hebrew-register expression of deliberate self-lowering before God as the foundational inner posture of the person of faith.

### M09-A-VCG-02 — Self-humbling as willed movement toward God: the Greek NT register

**Context description:** This VCG carries the Greek NT register of the verbal act of self-humbling (tapeinoō) directed Godward, where the willed downward movement of the self is the precondition for divine exaltation. The schema is consistent across these verses: the person who inwardly lowers themselves before God will be lifted up; the person who self-exalts will be brought down. The humbling is presented as a decisive inner posture of the will — choosing the lower place, bowing the self before God's sovereign hand, entering God's kingdom as a child rather than a status-claimant. The tax collector's posture in Luk18:14 and the child's posture in Mat18:4 are the paradigmatic expressions of this register. Luk3:5 (mountains made low — physical-prophetic landscape imagery) is included in this VCG per Phase 5 instruction but carries no inner-being content for the term; it is flagged as a **Phase 5.5 set-aside candidate** and will be set aside by CC patch at Phase 5.5.

### M09-A-VCG-03 — Self-humbling in apostolic and Christological vocation

**Context description:** This VCG carries the costlier, service-register expression of the willed self-lowering characteristic: humility not as the posture that wins divine exaltation, but as the inner quality of self-giving in vocation. Christ's self-humbling (Phili2:8) is the paradigm — a willed, downward movement of the self that accepts servanthood and death as acts of obedience to the Father. Paul's ministry expressions follow: voluntarily taking an unpaid, menial role so the Corinthians might be elevated (2Cor11:7), and serving the Lord with humility sustained through suffering and tears (Act20:19). The characteristic in this VCG is humility as a disposition that costs the person something — that diminishes rather than advantageously positions the self — and that is sustained over time or in extremis.

### M09-A-VCG-04 — Humility as inner relational posture

**Context description:** This VCG carries the relational-disposition register of the humility characteristic — humility as a quality of inner bearing toward God and community, contrasted with proud self-regard and social hierarchy. Jesus names lowliness explicitly in the heart as a self-description paired with gentleness (Mat11:29), inviting those weary of performance and status to find rest by learning this inner posture. The relational expressions are diverse: solidarity with those of low social station against haughtiness (Rom12:16), self-effacing personal presence that others may misjudge (2Cor10:1), the disposition that opens the will toward God rather than self-assertion (Jam4:6), and the willed mutual self-lowering within community that grounds receptivity to divine favour (1Pe5:5 — both vc_ids). Mat11:29 is MULTI-FACETED: primary placement here; secondary contribution to M09-F (gentleness dimension).

### M09-A-VCG-05 — Tapeinofrosunē as chosen inner character disposition

**Context description:** This VCG carries the Pauline epistolary register of tapeinofrosunē — the quality of humility as a chosen, genuine inner character disposition, placed alongside the other virtues of the renewed person. The cluster is defined by the deliberate, willed nature of humility as a character-garment (Col3:12) — something put on, not merely exhibited. The Colossian material (Col2:18 and Col2:23) adds a distinctive sub-theme: the exposure of false, performed humility that masks inner pride and achieves nothing against the flesh, implicitly defining genuine tapeinofrosunē as an actual inner transformation rather than an outward severity. Eph4:2 and Phili2:3 round out the register — humility as the foundational community-enabling disposition and as the cognitive-affective act of genuinely counting others more significant.
