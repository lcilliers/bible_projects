## VCG design

### M09-H-VCG-01 — The freely-moved willing heart: uncoerced offering and service

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of willing-heartedness as the freely-moved inner disposition that drives offering and service without external compulsion. In the Exodus tabernacle construction (35:5, 35:22), a freely-moved heart is explicitly named as the inner source from which outward giving flows — the willing inner disposition that originates in the heart produces the act of bringing. 1Ch28:21 adds skill alongside willingness — the willing person's inner readiness enables wholehearted participation in sacred work. 2Ch29:31 names the willing heart as the distinguishing inner condition of those who bring beyond-required offerings — spontaneous, internally-motivated generosity in worship. Together these four evidence the characteristic of willing-heartedness as the positive inner disposition of the will that offers freely: the opposite of the resistant, self-protecting, or compelled will. Cross-register flags M04 (spontaneous generous delight) and M29 (volitional readiness) travel with this VCG.

### M09-H-VCG-02 — The willing spirit as divine gift: penitential context

**Context description:** This VCG carries the distinctive register of willing-heartedness as a divine gift that must be sought and may be lost — evidenced in the single verse Psa51:12. The psalmist who has fallen into moral failure asks God to restore the joy of salvation and to uphold him with a willing spirit. Willingness here is located in the spirit and is presented not as a natural inner capacity but as a grace requiring divine sustenance after failure — the inner disposition of freely-offered devotion that the penitent can no longer sustain alone. This single verse adds a dimension to the willing-heartedness characteristic that the offering-context VCG does not carry: willing-heartedness is not only the natural expression of the freely-moved heart but also a vulnerability, something that can be depleted or broken by moral failure and requires restoration from God. The pairing with the joy of salvation places this in the register of willing-heartedness as a Spirit-sustained, joy-infused orientation of the will. Cross-register flags M04 (joy of salvation — immediate pairing in the verse) and M29 (volitional dimension — the spirit asked to be sustained in willing orientation) travel with this VCG.
