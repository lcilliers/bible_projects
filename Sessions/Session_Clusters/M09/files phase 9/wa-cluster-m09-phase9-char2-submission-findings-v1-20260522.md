## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus reveals God as a sovereign authority who requires and receives the inner yielding of the will from persons he has created and redeemed. The gospel-obedience cluster (M09-D-VCG-01 — Rom1:5; Rom16:26; Rom10:16; 2Th1:8) frames obedience of faith as the telos of divine disclosure — the mystery now revealed demands and elicits the will's submission. God's character is thus that of one who does not merely command external behaviour but seeks the inner allegiance of the will ("obedience of faith" — Rom1:5; "the will's trustful submission as the culminating response to revealed mystery" — Rom16:26). Rom5:19 adds the Christological dimension: God's own Son enacts the obedience that makes many righteous — implying that submission of will is not merely imposed on creatures but expressed by the Son in his relation to the Father.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus attributes submission explicitly to Christ: Heb5:8 — "although he was a son, he learned obedience through what he suffered" — the incarnate Son experiences submission as something acquired through costly suffering. Rom5:19 — Christ's obedience as the redemptive act that makes many righteous — positions the Son's will-submission as the structural pivot of salvation history. The attribution to Christ reveals that submission of will is not a deficit forced on the creature by divine power but a posture the Son himself takes in relation to the Father. In the human person, the characteristic therefore carries Christological dignity: the one who submits participates in the inner-relational posture of the Son toward the Father, not merely in a social compliance structure.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus does not evidence the Father possessing submission in the sense the Son does — the Father is consistently the one to whom Christ submits (Heb5:8 — "in submission to the Father"; the gospel as his command in Rom16:26). This asymmetry — Son submits, Father receives — suggests that submission in its creaturely form is specifically the Son's characteristic as the one who takes on creaturely relation with the Father in the Incarnation. For the human person, the implication is that submission reflects the image not by mirroring the Father's posture but by participating in the Son's: the human creature before God occupies the position the Son voluntarily occupies, and the characteristic is thus genuinely image-bearing through union with the one who exercises it fully.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created — what does it equip the person to be, do, or become?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences several purposes. First, submission equips the person to receive salvation: Heb5:9 — "he became the source of eternal salvation to all who obey him" — the obedient will is the condition for receiving Christ's benefit. Second, it equips the person to be formed in character: Heb5:8 — obedience learned through suffering is a quality shaped over time; 1Pe1:22 — obedience to truth "purifies the soul itself," producing sincere love from a pure heart. Third, it equips the person for genuine community: household obedience patterns (Eph6:1; Col3:20; 1Ti3:4) show submission as the disposition that enables ordered, functioning human structures. Fourth, it equips the person for covenant fidelity: the gospel-obedience cluster (Rom1:5; 16:26) frames obedience of faith as the telos of the person's reception of the gospel — what they are becoming through it.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence points to both but with different emphases in the two sub-groups. The M09-C heart/conscience-located evidence is largely addressed to persons already in the redemptive situation (believers — Rom6:12/17; Eph6:5; Phili2:12; Col3:22), suggesting submission as the renewed-person's characteristic response to their condition. However, Gen49:10 (yiq.qe.hah — the peoples' obedience to Judah's coming ruler) frames submission as an eschatological ordering rather than a fallen-condition response. Pro30:17 (obedience to parents as expected natural moral response) suggests submission to legitimate authority is part of created moral order. Heb5:8 (Christ learning obedience through suffering) implies submission is not automatic even for the Son — it is something developed through engagement with hardship, suggesting it belongs to the creaturely-relational structure, not only the fallen-remedial register.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. The obedience-of-faith pattern in Rom1:5 and Rom16:26 frames the characteristic as the goal toward which Paul's mission presses — a future fullness of the nations' will-submission to God. Gen49:10 explicitly eschatological: "to him shall be the obedience of the peoples" — the gathering of nations in submission to Judah's coming ruler is a future-oriented gathering. Heb5:9 — obedience as the condition for receiving salvation from the one "made perfect" — places the characteristic within the eschatological-salvific frame. 1Pe1:2 — obedience as the goal of the Spirit's sanctifying work — frames the Spirit's entire sanctifying activity as oriented toward the fullness of this inner posture. The characteristic thus has a telos: the full and genuine submission of the creature's will to Christ as Lord, which the present obedience of faith partially anticipates.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Submission expresses the divine image through the pattern of the Son's own inner life: the human creature's willing yield of its will to God mirrors the Son's submission to the Father (Heb5:8; Rom5:19). The characteristic instantiates the aspect of the divine image that involves relational responsiveness to legitimate authority — the image-bearer is made to be a creature in genuine relation with the Creator, and submission is the inner form that relation takes. 1Pe1:2 ("in the sanctification of the Spirit, for obedience to Jesus Christ") positions the Spirit's work as directed toward producing this image-quality in the person: being progressively shaped into the posture of willing yield to Christ.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Partially shared through the Son's Incarnation, not directly shared in the Father's mode. Heb5:8 and Rom5:19 evidence that the characteristic is genuinely present in the Son — not merely a creaturely analogue but the Son's own enacted posture in the Incarnation. However, the Father's possession of the characteristic in the same form is not evidenced — the Father is the authority to whom submission runs, not the one who submits. The characteristic is thus shared with the person of the Son in his incarnate mode, and is a creaturely analogue to the divine-Son relation with the Father, which the Incarnation makes directly accessible as a pattern for human submission.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences the absence of submission as indicating the image's deterioration in a person's relation to God. 2Th1:8 — "those who do not obey the gospel of our Lord Jesus" face divine judgment paired with "not knowing God" — the absence of submission is linked to fundamental relational rupture with God, not merely behavioural non-compliance. Rom6:16 — the will's habitual yielding to sin rather than righteousness discloses whose authority rules the inner person — the image is there but functioning in its inverted form, yielding to what destroys rather than what restores. Pro30:17 evidences the absence in the household register as a fatal moral failure. Conversely, the presence of submission — particularly obedience of faith (Rom1:5; Rom16:26) — marks the image functioning in right ordered relation to its Creator, with the person's will genuinely oriented toward God.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, most significantly in the Christological direction. Rom5:19 deploys Christ's obedience typologically as the structural counter to Adam's disobedience — Adam's rebellion is the negative type of which Christ's submission is the redemptive antitype. Gen49:10 ("to him shall be the obedience of the peoples") functions typologically: the gathering of nations in submission to Judah's coming ruler is the OT type that Christological fulfillment realises in the submission of faith to Christ (Rom1:5; 16:26 — "obedience of faith among all the nations"). The gospel-obedience language explicitly connects to this typological arc: the obedience that was prophesied for the nations becomes the obedience of faith produced by the gospel.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The typology runs in both directions. The Adam-Christ typology (Rom5:19) has the human negative instance (Adam's disobedience) pointing toward the divine positive instance (Christ's obedience) — human-to-divine direction. The Gen49:10 → Romans obedience-of-faith fulfilment runs the other direction: the OT eschatological promise (Gen49:10) is established as the divine-promissory pattern; the human response of faith-obedience (Rom1:5; 16:26) fulfils it — divine-to-human direction. Heb5:8 (Christ learning obedience through suffering) establishes the Christological paradigm that Paul picks up in Phili2:12 ("as you have always obeyed") — the divine instance (Christ's costly submission) establishes the pattern for the human community's sustained obedience.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Typological use is evidenced (see T0.4.1 and T0.4.2).

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

*Re-reading §3.A fresh for T1's lens: name/nature, kind, boundary, modes, response, sustained effect, conditions, dimension.*

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The characteristic is named Submission. The name signals an essentially relational and directional structure: to submit is to place oneself under (sub-mittere) — to position the self in relation to a legitimate authority. The name implies both an agent (the one who submits) and a recipient (the authority submitted to), confirming that this is a constitutively relational characteristic that cannot operate in isolation. The name also implies an act: submission is done, not merely possessed — though as the evidence shows, it becomes a disposition through repetition and formation.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Primary terms: *H3349 yiq.qe.hah* — the Hebrew term in Gen49:10 meaning obedience, yielding of allegiance. *G5218 hupakoē* — noun from hupo (under) + akouō (to hear) — literally "under-hearing," the attentive receptivity that hears and yields to what is heard. *G5219 hupakouō* — the verbal form: to hear under, to obey. *G5292 hupotagē* — noun from hupo (under) + tassō (to arrange, order) — being ordered under; the condition of being placed in ordered submission. The roots reveal that submission in both its Greek forms is constitutively auditory-relational (hupakoē — hearing-under) and structural-relational (hupotagē — ordered under). Both imply a legitimate structure within which the yielding occurs — not arbitrary capitulation but ordered responsiveness within a right relationship.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — All three. *Directional:* submission runs downward in the authority-structure sense — toward God (Rom1:5; Heb5:9), toward parents (Eph6:1; Col3:20), toward legitimate apostolic instruction (Phili2:12; 2Th3:14). *Relational:* the terms are constitutively relational — one submits to someone; the characteristic is always triangulated between the submitting person, the authority, and the ground of the submission (theological — "in the Lord," Eph6:1; "as to Christ," Eph6:5). *Constitutional:* Rom6:17 ("obedient from the heart") and Phili2:12 (sustained without external accountability — in "my absence") locate the characteristic constitutionally in the heart and conscience rather than in external compliance — it must originate in the inner person to be genuine.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences submission simultaneously as act, disposition, condition, and quality. As *act*: Gal2:5 ("we did not yield in submission even for a moment" — the volitional act that can be withheld); Heb11:8 (Abraham obeyed the call — a decisive volitional event). As *disposition*: Phili2:12 ("as you have always obeyed" — sustained inner orientation); 1Pe1:14 ("as obedient children" — the characteristic as a defining personal identity). As *condition*: 1Pe1:2 (obedience as the goal-state the Spirit's sanctifying work directs toward). As *quality*: Rom16:19 (obedience publicly known as a characteristic of the community's will). The M09-C/D split maps onto this: M09-C is primarily the act-and-disposition register; M09-D is primarily the quality-and-condition register of obedience as a known characteristic of persons and communities.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The characteristic has constituent elements visible at first encounter. The volume-split between M09-C (heart/conscience-located inner disposition) and M09-D (relational pattern of obedience-in-context) is itself a structural distinction: inner disposition versus lived expression. Within M09-C, constituent elements include: (a) the allegiance dimension — the will's positioning toward an authority (Rom6:16/17); (b) the cognitive-volitional dimension — taking every thought captive (2Cor10:5); (c) the sincerity/motive dimension — "with sincere heart" versus "eye-service" (Eph6:5; Col3:22); (d) the learned-and-costly dimension — obedience acquired through suffering (Heb5:8). These are genuinely distinct: a person might have behavioural compliance (M09-D register) without the heart-located genuineness (M09-C), and the Colossian and Ephesian material explicitly distinguishes these.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The current best working description, drawn from the evidence: *Submission is the inner disposition of willing yielding of the will to a legitimate authority — God, apostolic instruction, household structures, or the gospel — originating in the heart and conscience rather than from external pressure, expressed in the relational pattern of the person's life across varying contexts. It operates both as a decisive volitional act (Gal2:5; Heb11:8) and as a sustained character-orientation (Phili2:12; 1Pe1:14). In its deepest form it is not compliance but inner conformity — the will's genuine alignment with the authority's claim, rooted in trust, conscience, and (in the NT register) faith.* The M09-C register names the inner-disposition face; the M09-D register names the relational-pattern face of the same characteristic.

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus names the structural opposite as disobedience and inner refusal. Rom5:19 — "the one man's disobedience" (Adam) as the structural counter to "the one man's obedience" (Christ). 2Th1:8 — "those who do not obey the gospel" as the structural opposite of the obedient. Pro30:17 — the mocking eye that "scorns to obey" a mother. 2Th3:14 — the one who "does not obey" Paul's letter. The structural opposite is not merely external non-compliance but the inner refusal of the will to yield — the self that asserts its own authority over against the legitimate claim. Rom6:16 names this most precisely: the will's habitual yielding to sin rather than to righteousness is the structural opposite, and it determines whose servant one is.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus reveals several explicit exclusions. (a) *Eye-service and people-pleasing:* Eph6:5 and Col3:22 both explicitly contrast sincere-heart submission with "eye-service" and "people-pleasing" — the characteristic excludes compliance driven by social observation or approval. (b) *Yielding to false authority:* Gal2:5 — Paul's non-submission to false teachers is the principled exercise of the same capacity. The characteristic excludes submission to illegitimate or gospel-distorting authority. (c) *Mere ignorance-compliance:* 2Th1:8 frames disobedience as paired with "not knowing God" — genuine submission is not identical with mere ignorance-compliance or cultural conformity; it involves inner knowledge and orientation. (d) *Inner refusal:* 2Th3:14 frames the one who does not obey as having an "inner refusal" that the communal response must address.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence establishes two key boundaries. First, submission is not identical with *humility* (CHAR-1), though the two are closely related. Humility is the inner posture of self-lowering before God and others generally; submission is the will's yielding to a specific legitimate authority. Phili2:8 shows them working together — Christ "humbled himself by becoming obedient" — but the difference is that one can be genuinely humble without being in an act of specific authority-submission. Second, submission is not mere *compliance* without inner conviction. The entire M09-C corpus (heart-located inner disposition) distinguishes genuine submission from behavioural compliance — Phili2:12 (persistence without external accountability), Col3:22 (from sincerity of heart, not eye-service), 1Pe1:22 (obedience to truth that purifies the soul). Where compliance ends, submission — in its M09 register — begins.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Four distinct modes are evidenced. (a) *As volitional allegiance-positioning:* Rom6:16/17 — the will's habitual orienting toward an authority that constitutes whose servant one is; a continuous inner disposition of the will. (b) *As cognitive-volitional capture:* 2Cor10:5 — taking every thought captive to obey Christ; the characteristic engages the mind's reasoning and captures it for Christ's authority. (c) *As sincere-motive genuineness:* Eph6:5; Phili2:12; Col3:22 — the submission operates at the level of genuine inner motive (sincere heart, fear of God) rather than behavioural surface. (d) *As learned-costly-transformative process:* Heb5:8 — Christ learned obedience through suffering; 1Pe1:22 — obedience purifies the soul; the characteristic operates as a formative process that shapes the inner person over time.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. *Direction-vertical (human-to-God):* the gospel-obedience cluster (M09-D-VCG-01 — Rom1:5; 16:26; Heb5:9; 1Pe1:2) is Godward; obedience of faith as submission to divine revelation and Christ's authority. *Direction-horizontal (human-to-authority structures):* household obedience patterns (M09-D-VCG-03 — Eph6:1; Col3:20; 1Ti3:4) and apostolic-communal obedience (Phili2:12; 2Th3:14) are horizontal. *Constitutional level:* the M09-C sub-group evidences heart-level and conscience-level operation (deep inner disposition); the M09-D sub-group evidences the surface-level relational pattern. The characteristic operates at different constitutional depths depending on whether it is being formed (Heb5:8 — deep, costly formation) or expressed as a known community characteristic (Rom16:19 — relational pattern publicly known).

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, in a specific form. 1Pe3:6 — Sarah's obedience to Abraham "expressed through speech and inward disposition (calling him lord)" — the characteristic finds verbal expression in the form of address and acknowledgment. The naming of Christ as Lord in 1Pe1:2 (obedience to Jesus Christ) and the confession of the gospel (2Cor9:13 — "submission that comes from your confession of the gospel of Christ") also evidence a speech-adjacent mode: confession and acknowledgment are the verbal expression of the inner submission of will. The characteristic also has an auditory mode at its origin: hupakoē (under-hearing) — the will's yielding is constitutively responsive to what is heard; submission operates through the inner hearing of the word and its claim.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Where the corpus addresses immediate response, two distinct patterns emerge by sub-group. In M09-C (inner disposition), the immediate inner response is a cognitive-volitional repositioning: the person arrives at an inner assessment that determines their self-understanding and allegiance — Luk17:10 ("we are unworthy servants; we have only done what was our duty") is the self-assessment that immediately follows doing what was commanded; Rom6:17 ("obedience from the heart") names the immediate inner state of genuine transformation. In M09-D (relational pattern), the immediate relational response is the visible obedient act: Act6:7 — priests becoming obedient to the faith as a decisive reorientation; 2Cor7:15 — the Corinthians' receptive welcome of Titus with fear and trembling as the immediate expression of their inner obedience.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The response varies in form between the two sub-groups but is consistent in direction: both M09-C and M09-D evidence an inner repositioning of the will toward the authority. What varies is the constitutional level at which the response is named — M09-C names the heart and conscience level; M09-D names the relational pattern level. The common direction is the will's yielding rather than its self-assertion, whether that is expressed as inner self-assessment (Luk17:10), heart-conformity (Rom6:17), sincere-heart service (Eph6:5; Col3:22), or decisive community-level reorientation (Act6:7; Rom16:19).

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence is not broadly silent on immediate response (see T1.5.1 and T1.5.2). However, the eschatological instances (Gen49:10 — future gathering of nations; 1Pe1:2 — goal of the Spirit's sanctifying work) describe the characteristic as a future telos rather than an immediate response to present encounter; their immediate-response profile is implied rather than named.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences several sustained inner-being productions. (a) *Soul-purification:* 1Pe1:22 — "having purified your souls by your obedience to the truth" — sustained obedience to truth effects inner purification of the soul's deepest faculty. (b) *Sincere brotherly love:* 1Pe1:22 — the same verse evidences that purification through obedience produces "sincere brotherly love from a pure heart" — love is the fruit of sustained submission to truth. (c) *Learned costly dispositionality:* Heb5:8 — Christ learned obedience through suffering — the sustained engagement with authority even through cost shapes the disposition; Phili2:12 ("always obeyed") evidences the sustained disposition that outlasts the external support of apostolic presence. (d) *Reliable character-orientation:* Phile21 — Paul's confidence in Philemon's obedience as a reliable inner orientation "toward doing good beyond the minimum" — sustained submission produces trustworthy character.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained possession establish?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences the following sustained states: *Inward purity:* 1Pe1:22 — the soul purified by obedience to truth; *Love-capacity:* 1Pe1:22 — the love that flows from the pure heart formed by sustained obedience; *Fear of God:* Phili2:12 ("with fear and trembling") and Col3:22 ("fearing the Lord") — sustained submission cultivates the reverential posture toward God that is the foundation of genuine relationship; *Wise discernment:* Rom16:19 — Paul redirects the Romans' obedient disposition toward "wisdom as to what is good and innocence as to what is evil" — sustained obedience creates the capacity for discernment; *Identity-formation:* 1Pe1:14 — obedience defines the believer's identity as "obedient children," shaping the will's structural opposition to "former passions."

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. The immediate response (T1.5.1) is the repositioning of the will — a volitional act or inner self-assessment that follows the encounter with the authority's claim. The sustained effect is *characterological depth*: sustained obedience purifies the soul (1Pe1:22 — deeper than the volitional act), produces love (1Pe1:22 — an affective capacity), and creates the identity of "obedient children" (1Pe1:14 — a stable self-understanding). The progression is from act → disposition → identity → affective capacity. The immediate response is an inner positioning; the sustained effect is an inner formation that becomes character.

---

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus evidences several enabling inner conditions. (a) *Fear of God / reverential orientation:* Phili2:12 ("fear and trembling") and Col3:22 ("fearing the Lord") — the reverence that places God's authority above social pressure. (b) *Sincere-heart genuineness:* Eph6:5; Col3:22 — the undivided, undistorted inner motive that does not split between inner compliance and outward performance. (c) *Faith:* Heb11:8 — Abraham obeyed "by faith" — trusting the divine word more than personal comprehension; the obedience of faith (Rom1:5; 16:26) is enabled by the trust-orientation of faith. (d) *Conscience-function:* Phili2:12 (sustained without external accountability — conscience and fear of God replace social pressure) — the conscience functioning as the internal accountability when external accountability is removed.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus names several blocking conditions. (a) *Eye-service and people-pleasing:* Eph6:5; Col3:22 — the outward-performance orientation that substitutes social approval for genuine inner compliance; this distorts submission into performance. (b) *Self-assertion and inner refusal:* 2Th1:8 — "not knowing God" paired with disobedience suggests that genuine self-assertion against God's revealed authority blocks reception. (c) *Deference to false authority:* Gal2:5 — Paul's deliberate non-submission evidences that yielding to illegitimate authority would be a distortion rather than an expression of the characteristic; discernment of legitimate authority is a prerequisite. (d) *Yielding to bodily passions:* Rom6:12 — sin's reign producing obedience to bodily desires is the competing inner yielding that blocks submission to God; the characteristic is blocked by the competing submission to desire.

---

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The corpus describes this across several negative instances. 2Th1:8 — those who "do not obey the gospel" are in a state of "not knowing God" — relational rupture at the deepest level, with catastrophic eschatological consequence. 2Th3:14 — the one who refuses to obey apostolic instruction reveals "an inner refusal" that the communal response is designed to awaken to repentance — the blocking state is refusal, not merely ignorance. Pro30:17 — the eye that scorns to obey a mother is in a state of contemptuous dismissal of legitimate authority — a moral condition the text marks with severe consequence. The inner state of the non-receiver is consistent: some form of self-assertion or inner refusal that places the person's own judgment or desire above the legitimate authority's claim.

---

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The primary dimension is *volitional*: submission is constitutively the will's act and disposition. The Romans evidence is the clearest: Rom6:16/17 — the will's habitual yielding to sin or to righteousness determines whose servant one is; Rom6:17 — obedience "from the heart" is the will's genuine conformity. The imperative form throughout ("obey," "be subject") addresses the volitional capacity. Gal2:5 — the act of submission can be withheld — confirms it is the will's act. Phili2:12 — sustained without external support — confirms it is the will's disposition. The volitional dimension is primary because without the will's genuine engagement, what remains is performance (the M09-C/D split itself is premised on this: M09-C carries the genuine inner-disposition face precisely because genuine will-submission cannot be reduced to M09-D's relational pattern alone).

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Supporting evidence for the volitional-primary classification: Rom6:16/17 — the will's yielding determines the enslaved condition; Rom6:12 — believers commanded to resist the inner yielding to bodily passions (addressing the will's directional capacity); Heb11:8 — Abraham "obeyed" as a faith-driven inner response (volitional); Gal2:5 — non-submission as a decisive volitional act; Phili2:12 — the sustained inner disposition rooted in conscience without social support (will functioning without external enablement); 1Ti2:11 — the willing, whole-hearted receptivity of the learner's inner posture (volitional orientation). All these evidence the will as the primary constitutional faculty engaged.

---
