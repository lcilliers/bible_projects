## VCG design

### M09-B-VCG-01 — Imposed divine lowering: judicial and providential humbling

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of externally-imposed humbling — where God is the agent and the person or people experience being brought low as a consequence of unfaithfulness or as a judicial act upon pride. The humbling is not voluntary inner submission but a divinely-enacted lowering. 2Ch28:19 records God humbling Judah as a national consequence of Ahaz's unfaithfulness. Job40:12 names the forcible bringing-low of the proud as the proper divine response to arrogance. Psa106:42 records Israel subjected under enemies as the consequence of unfaithfulness. Psa107:12 is the most directly inner-being of these four: God bows hearts down through hard labor, acting on the inner faculty of the heart itself, using external affliction to produce inner collapse and dependence. Together these four evidence the characteristic of experienced lowliness as the reverse side of the humility register — what the absence of voluntary self-lowering brings, and what God's sovereign action looks like when it humbles.

### M09-B-VCG-02 — Social and condition lowliness in divine reversal

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of lowliness as a social or existential condition that stands in relation to divine reversals, reframings, or consolations. The verses evidence what it means to be in a lowly estate and how that condition is invested with spiritual significance. Mary's lowly estate (tapeinōsis) is the condition from which God lifts her — her social servant-status becomes the occasion for divine favour (Luk1:48). The humble of low estate whom God exalts while bringing down the mighty evidence lowliness as the social-spiritual condition God honours (Luk1:52). Christ's humiliation (Act8:33) is an imposed suffering-state — denied justice, life taken away — in its deepest relational dimension. The lowly brother is called to boast in spiritual elevation that transcends his earthly condition (Jam1:9). The rich person is called to boast in their humiliation — recognising their transience as a corrective inner posture that deflates wealth-rooted pride (Jam1:10). All five evidence lowliness as a condition that is neither the final word nor to be despised.

### M09-B-VCG-03 — Affliction-produced lowliness: the inner experience of being brought low

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of lowliness as an experienced inner state produced by affliction, suffering, circumstance, or God's action in response to others' failure. The inner-being content is the experience of being in a lowly, downcast, or depleted condition — and what the person does or learns within it. God's comfort actively meets the downcast condition of those pressed down by outward affliction (2Cor7:6). The painful experience of being humbled before a congregation over others' unrepentant sin produces grief and mourning within Paul (2Cor12:21). The creaturely lowly body names an objective mortal condition awaiting transformation by Christ's power (Phili3:21). Being brought low as a circumstantial, experiential state that Paul has traversed and learned to navigate with cultivated contentment (Phili4:12) represents the most distinctly inner-being instance — the quality of the person that enables them to endure lowness without loss of stability.
