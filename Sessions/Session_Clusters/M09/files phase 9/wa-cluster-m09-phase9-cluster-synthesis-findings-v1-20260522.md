## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics together image a God whose character is neither self-promoting nor coercive. Four of the six directly establish this by contrast or reflection: Humility (CHAR-1) most fully, through Christ's kenotic self-lowering in Phili2:8, images a God whose movement is downward toward the creature rather than upward in self-assertion; Submission (CHAR-2) images a God who desires the inner allegiance of the will, not only outer compliance — the gospel calls for obedience of faith as the culminating response to divine self-disclosure; Contrition (CHAR-3) images a God who is constitutionally drawn toward the broken inner state, simultaneously inhabiting transcendence and the crushed spirit (Isa57:15); Willing-heartedness (CHAR-6) images a God who calls for freely-given devotion over coerced contribution and actively restores the willing capacity when it is lost. Meekness and Gentleness (CHAR-4) and Dignity (CHAR-5) image God more obliquely — CHAR-4 through the priestly mediator's calibrated compassion (Heb5:2), CHAR-5 through the settled moral gravity that belongs to holiness without performance. The cluster-wide pattern is: M09 images the divine character through six variations on a single theme — *God neither asserts himself through force nor receives forced returns; he moves freely toward the creature and calls for freely-offered response*. The structural opposite of the cluster (M08 Pride/self-assertion) thus images the inversion of the divine character.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster divides cleanly on this question. Only CHAR-1 Humility and CHAR-2 Submission have explicit divine attribution — and exclusively through the incarnate Son: Mat11:29 (CHAR-1 — Jesus names himself "lowly in heart"), Phili2:8 (CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 jointly — the Son "humbled himself by becoming obedient"). CHAR-3 Contrition and CHAR-5 Dignity are not attributed to God directly; CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness is attributed obliquely through the high priestly type (Heb5:2). CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness is attributed only in the direction of God-toward-creature (God restores willing-heartedness in Psa51:12) but not as a quality God possesses. The cluster-level finding: M09 is the cluster in which the incarnate Son is the primary locus of divine attribution — the Christological concentration is the distinctive feature of the cluster's divine-attribution evidence. Characteristics without Christological attribution (CHAR-3, CHAR-5, CHAR-6) are exclusively creaturely in their direct evidence, grounding the cluster's dual structure: Christological core (CHAR-1, CHAR-2) surrounded by exclusively creaturely expressions.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The silences are analytically graduated across the cluster. CHAR-3 Contrition is necessarily silent — the characteristic structurally presupposes a being capable of moral failure before a holy God; divine possession would be constitutionally incoherent. CHAR-5 Dignity is silent because it names a creaturely social-ordering quality (character gravity in civic, household, and pedagogical contexts) that requires the creaturely social sphere for its expression. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness is structurally asymmetrical — it is the creature's freely-moving will *toward* God; God cannot be willing-hearted toward himself in the same directional sense. CHAR-4 Meekness is grounded in shared creaturely weakness (Heb5:2) — a constitutional ground unavailable to God in the same form. The cluster-level inference: the characteristics' silences are not accidental but trace the contour of the imago Dei — what the human person images is not a simple mirror of divine qualities but the *creaturely analogue* of divine character in the form appropriate to creaturely existence, asymmetry, and limitation. CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 alone achieve direct divine attribution through the Incarnation, which is itself the most radical case of creaturely-analogue-becoming-genuinely-divine.

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics evidence three distinct purpose-registers that together map the cluster's functional scope. *Receptive purposes* (characteristics that primarily open the person to receive what God gives): CHAR-3 Contrition — the crushed spirit creates interior openness to God's reviving; CHAR-1 Humility — the humble person receives wisdom (Pro11:2) and grace (Jam4:6). *Relational-structural purposes* (characteristics that order the person's relation to legitimate authority): CHAR-2 Submission — equips the person to receive salvation (Heb5:9) and be purified through obedience to truth (1Pe1:22); CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness — equips the person for genuine rather than compelled worship and service. *Social-ordering purposes* (characteristics that shape the quality of the person's presence in community): CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness — equips for compassionate mediating engagement with the erring; CHAR-5 Dignity — equips for ordered, non-coercive authority in civic, household, and pedagogical spheres. The cluster as a whole equips the person for the full range of non-self-assertive existence: receiving from God (CHAR-1, CHAR-3), responding to God (CHAR-2, CHAR-6), and inhabiting human community (CHAR-4, CHAR-5).

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of original design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster distributes across this question in a revealing pattern. CHAR-3 Contrition is most clearly a response to the fallen condition — both its verses presuppose moral failure. CHAR-2 Submission and CHAR-1 Humility are both registers: primarily corrective (the dominant ka.na and obedience-of-faith evidence is post-fall), but with creational dimensions (Mic6:8 — walk humbly; Heb5:8 — Christ's obedience has creational as well as redemptive dimensions). CHAR-4 Meekness (shared creaturely weakness as ground — Heb5:2), CHAR-5 Dignity (ordering quality of human social existence as designed), and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness (the freely-given heart in community worship contexts — Exo35, 2Ch29:31) are weighted toward original-design or creation-consistent registers, though each can be disrupted or intensified by the fallen condition. The cluster-level finding: M09 spans the full created-fallen spectrum, with CHAR-3 at one pole (exclusively responsive) and CHAR-5 at the other (primarily original-design), with the remaining four occupying the both-registers position in varying proportions.

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster divides sharply here. CHAR-1 Humility and CHAR-2 Submission have the most explicit eschatological orientation: CHAR-1 through the exaltation-of-the-humble pattern (Mat23:12; 1Pe5:6) and the bodily transformation awaited (Phili3:21); CHAR-2 through Gen49:10 (the peoples' eschatological submission), the obedience-of-faith-among-all-nations telos (Rom16:26), and the universal submission to Christ as the cluster's most expansive eschatological claim. CHAR-3 Contrition carries eschatological resonance in Isa57:15's reviving-toward-fullness trajectory. CHAR-4 Meekness carries implicit typological eschatological trajectory through the Aaronic-to-Christological priestly sequence (Heb5:2 → Heb4:15). CHAR-5 Dignity and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness are the most eschatologically silent in their direct evidence, though CHAR-6 carries tabernacle-offering typological resonance that points toward the permanent willing-spirit fullness of the new covenant. The cluster-level shape: M09's eschatological vision centres on CHAR-2 (universal submission to Christ as telos of gospel proclamation) and CHAR-1 (exaltation following voluntary self-lowering), with the other four characteristics playing supporting roles.

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Each of the six characteristics instantiates the imago Dei through a distinct mode of non-self-assertive existence. CHAR-1 Humility expresses it through the kenotic-self-giving dimension — imaging the Son's downward movement (Phili2:5–8). CHAR-2 Submission expresses it through the relational-responsiveness dimension — the creature genuinely oriented toward the Creator's authority, mirroring the Son's relation to the Father (Heb5:8). CHAR-3 Contrition expresses it through the moral-self-knowledge dimension — only the being made in God's image can exercise genuine honest self-confrontation before God's holiness. CHAR-4 Meekness expresses it through the mediating-compassion dimension — the image that reaches toward the erring with calibrated care rather than severity. CHAR-5 Dignity expresses it through the settled-moral-gravity dimension — the image-bearer reflecting the ordered, non-anxious weightiness of divine holiness without performance. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness expresses it through the freely-giving-toward-God dimension — the creature whose uncoerced generosity reflects divine liberality. Together the cluster instantiates a multi-faceted image: the person who genuinely images God in M09 is one who moves freely, receives openly, engages compassionately, and inhabits social existence with settled gravity — not self-asserting, not coercing, not performing.

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster contains both kinds. CHAR-1 Humility: partially shared — the divine Son takes on the characteristic genuinely (Phili2:8; Mat11:29), not merely analogically. CHAR-2 Submission: partially shared through the Son's incarnate posture, not shared in the Father's mode. CHAR-3 Contrition: exclusively creaturely — presupposes moral limitation before holiness, incoherent as divine possession. CHAR-4 Meekness: creaturely analogue — the compassionate restraint grounded in shared creaturely weakness is specifically creaturely. CHAR-5 Dignity: creaturely expression of a divine quality — the creature's participation in divine moral gravity, not direct sharing. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: creaturely in its specific directional form (creature toward God), imaging divine liberality without being identical to it. The cluster-level finding: M09 is structured around a Christological hinge — the two characteristics that have genuine divine attribution (CHAR-1, CHAR-2) are also those that the Son takes on in the Incarnation; the other four are exclusively or primarily creaturely, confirming that M09's characteristics are creatures-in-relation-to-God characteristics that the Son assumes in taking on creaturely existence.

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's diagnostic capacity is concentrated in three characteristics. CHAR-1 Humility is the most diagnostic: its absence is linked to pride (the image's most direct distortion — Pro11:2; 2Ch36:12; Col2:18), increasing guilt (2Ch33:23), and closed-self-enclosure against God's word. CHAR-2 Submission: its absence is named as "not knowing God" (2Th1:8) — relational rupture at the deepest level. CHAR-3 Contrition: its absence (hardening, self-justification) reveals an image that has closed against moral reality. CHAR-4 Meekness: its absence (severity or self-protective delay) reveals an image-bearer who has not genuinely reckoned with their own creaturely limitation. CHAR-5 Dignity: its absence (M08 proud self-display or frivolousness) reveals an image in distortion — either self-promotingly assertive or insufficiently weighty. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: its absence through moral failure (Psa51:12) reveals a diminishment of the image's offering-capacity. The cluster-level diagnostic: M09 as a whole diagnoses the condition of the divine image through the lens of self-assertion — every characteristic in the cluster, when absent, is replaced by some form of self-enclosure, self-promotion, or refusal of God's claim. The presence of the cluster's characteristics reveals an image functioning in its created non-self-assertive mode; the absence reveals the image in its post-fall self-asserting distortion.

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Five of the six characteristics carry typological dimensions; CHAR-5 Dignity alone is silent on typological use in its three-verse corpus. The cluster's typological richness is concentrated in CHAR-2 Submission (most explicitly — Gen49:10 → Rom1:5/16:26; Adam-disobedience → Christ-obedience in Rom5:19) and CHAR-4 Meekness (Aaronic high priest's metriopatheō → Christ's superior priestly sympathy). CHAR-1 Humility carries the OT kings' humbling patterns as types of Christ's definitive self-humbling (Act8:33 interpreting Isa53). CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness carries the tabernacle-offering type (Exo35) pointing toward the NT community as the Spirit's dwelling. CHAR-3 Contrition carries resonance through Isa57:15's Isaianic context (divine drawing near to the broken, anticipating the Incarnation). The cluster-level pattern: M09 is a typologically dense cluster, with its primary typological axis running through CHAR-2 (the Adam-Christ submission antithesis) and CHAR-4 (the Aaronic-Christological priestly sequence). The cluster's eschatological vision is largely mediated through these typological trajectories rather than through direct eschatological statement.

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster evidences both directions, and the direction varies by characteristic. *Human-to-divine* (human instance points toward Christ as antitype): CHAR-4 Meekness — Aaronic high priest's gentleness → Christ's superior priestly sympathy; CHAR-3 Contrition — the contrite person's experience of God's nearness anticipates fuller divine-presence in Incarnation and Spirit; CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness — OT tabernacle/temple free-offering → NT Spirit-given willing devotion. *Divine-to-human* (divine instance establishes the pattern for human): CHAR-1 Humility — Christ's self-humbling (Phili2:8) explicitly establishes the pattern for human humility (Phili2:3); CHAR-2 Submission — the Christological obedience (Heb5:8; Rom5:19) establishes the paradigm that gospel-obedience participates in. The cluster-level finding: M09 is the cluster where the typological arrows run most explicitly in both directions, with CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 having bidirectional typologies (human-to-Christ in OT patterns; Christ-to-human in NT paradigm). This bidirectionality is theologically significant: the cluster's characteristics are those the Son takes on (making divine-to-human direction possible) and those the OT prepares for him to fulfill (human-to-divine direction).

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Typological use is evidenced in five of six characteristics (see T0.4.1 and T0.4.2). CHAR-5 Dignity (semnotēs) is the sole characteristic without typological use in its three-verse corpus — it is addressed as a present community-ordering virtue without typological trajectory. This silence is itself analytically significant: CHAR-5 is the most present-oriented characteristic in the cluster, and its absence of typological use confirms the finding that it is a creation-order characteristic rather than a redemptive-historical one. The cluster-level note: typological silence in CHAR-5 is consistent with its characterological-primary classification — it is the stable social-ordering quality of created existence rather than a characteristic whose present form points toward a Christological or pneumatological fulfillment.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six names together reveal the cluster's organising logic. Three names signal relational direction: *Humility* (before God and others, downward), *Submission* (toward legitimate authority, under), *Willing-heartedness* (toward God, freely). Two names signal character quality: *Dignity* (settled inner gravity, non-assertive), *Meekness and Gentleness* (calibrated restraint and readiness, other-directed). One name signals an inner state: *Contrition* (crushed, broken, before God's holiness). The cluster-level reading: M09's names cluster around two axes — (a) *how the person is positioned relative to others* (Humility, Submission, Contrition — all naming postures-before), and (b) *what quality the person brings to social and relational existence* (Dignity, Meekness, Willing-heartedness — naming the character of the person in relation). The compound name Meekness and Gentleness is the only two-term name in the cluster, confirming its structurally composite character. All six names exclude self-assertion and self-promotion as constitutive elements — the names are the lexical index of the cluster's governing theme.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The vocabulary distributes across three linguistic traditions, and the distribution is analytically significant. *OT Hebrew only:* CHAR-3 Contrition (dak.ka — crushed, ground fine) and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness (na.div — freely-springing inner movement of the will). *NT Greek only:* CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness (metriopatheō — measured-within-bounds feeling; okneō-absence — non-hesitating readiness) and CHAR-5 Dignity (semnotēs — reverence-worthiness from the inside). *Both OT and NT:* CHAR-1 Humility (ka.na/tsa.na → tapein- family, with tapeinofrosunē as a likely NT coinage) and CHAR-2 Submission (yiq.qe.hah → hupakoē/hupakouō/hupotagē). This distribution reveals that the cluster's most acute/crisis characteristics (CHAR-3) and its most spontaneous/volitional characteristic (CHAR-6) are OT Hebrew in their primary vocabulary; the cluster's most social-ordering characteristics (CHAR-4, CHAR-5) are NT Greek; and the cluster's foundational characteristics (CHAR-1, CHAR-2) span both testaments with the NT producing new coinage (tapeinofrosunē, hupotagē) to name what the OT registered as act and event.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics carry all three implications, but with different weightings that reveal their distinctive identities. *Directionality* is most constitutively present in CHAR-2 Submission (the hupo- prefix encodes the downward-under direction into the root) and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness (the na.div root encodes the freely-springing forward movement). *Relational constitution* is most prominent in CHAR-4 Meekness — both terms are constitutively relational (metriopatheō names the quality of dealing-with; okneō names responsiveness-to). *Constitutional depth* is most explicitly encoded in CHAR-3 Contrition (dak.ka — the spirit and heart as named sites) and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness (heart and spirit both named). CHAR-5 Dignity is distinctive in being constitutional (inner quality) without being directional in the same strong sense as the others — it has no built-in directional movement, which is precisely what makes it a resting-place rather than a movement-characteristic. The cluster-level finding: M09 is a cluster in which most characteristics are constitutively in-motion (humbling, submitting, contriting, willing) with only CHAR-5 naming a settled state rather than a movement.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics distribute across the full phenomenological spectrum of inner-being phenomena. CHAR-1 Humility is all four simultaneously — act, disposition, condition, and quality. CHAR-2 Submission similarly occupies all four. CHAR-3 Contrition is primarily a *condition and state* — it is what the person finds themselves in rather than what they choose. CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness is primarily a *capacity and quality* — the inner ability to engage in a particular way. CHAR-5 Dignity is primarily a *stable character quality* — the settled property of the whole inner person. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness is primarily a *disposition* that issues in acts — the inner orientation of the will. The cluster-level finding: CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 are the most phenomenologically complex (spanning all four kinds), confirming their foundational status. CHAR-3 is the most condition-like (least volitional); CHAR-5 is the most quality-like (most characterological); CHAR-4 is the most capacity-like. Together the six cover the full range of what inner-being phenomena can be, which is itself a marker of M09's structural comprehensiveness.

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics have constituent elements, but the number and character of those elements varies. CHAR-1 Humility has the most constituents — cognitive (Phili2:3 — counting others more significant), affective (weeping — 2Ki22:19), volitional (self-lowering act), and authenticity (the Col2:18 problem of performed vs genuine). CHAR-2 Submission has four: allegiance-dimension, cognitive-volitional capture (2Cor10:5), sincerity-motive, and learned-costly formation. CHAR-4 Meekness has two clearly evidenced constituents: calibrated restraint (metriopatheō) and volitional readiness (okneō-absence) — terms distinct enough to be separately registerable. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness has two VCG-level constituents: freely-generated willingness (VCG-01) and divinely-restored willingness (VCG-02). CHAR-3 Contrition has two: brokenness and lowliness (Isa57:15). CHAR-5 Dignity has two: settled moral gravity and non-self-promoting self-possession. The cluster-level finding: the compound structure of CHAR-4 (two-term vocabulary) and CHAR-1/CHAR-2 (multi-element constituents) confirm their analytical complexity. CHAR-3 and CHAR-5 have the simplest constituent structure, which is consistent with their being more narrowly targeted: CHAR-3 at a specific inner-state; CHAR-5 at a specific character quality.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Placing the six best working descriptions in dialogue reveals the cluster's internal architecture. CHAR-1 (Humility): the inner disposition of voluntary self-lowering, engaging will, cognition, and affect. CHAR-2 (Submission): the willing yielding of the will to a legitimate authority, originating in heart and conscience. CHAR-3 (Contrition): the acute, grief-laden inner state of being crushed in spirit following genuine moral confrontation. CHAR-4 (Meekness and Gentleness): calibrated compassionate engagement with others in their weakness, combined with non-hesitating responsive readiness. CHAR-5 (Dignity): the settled inner quality of moral gravity and seriousness of character. CHAR-6 (Willing-heartedness): the freely-moved willing disposition of the heart and spirit toward giving and service to God. At cluster level, these descriptions form a coherent family: each describes a way of being internally oriented without self-assertion — by voluntary self-lowering (CHAR-1), yielding to authority (CHAR-2), genuine breaking before moral reality (CHAR-3), calibrated compassion toward others (CHAR-4), settled non-promotional gravity (CHAR-5), or freely-given devotion (CHAR-6). The unifying thread: *the inner person who neither asserts nor promotes the self, in six distinct registers*.

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's structural opposites, taken together, map the anatomy of self-assertion. CHAR-1's opposite: pride (hyperēphanos — self-elevation; the corpus names it explicitly and repeatedly). CHAR-2's opposite: disobedience and inner refusal (apeitheia — the will placed against legitimate authority). CHAR-3's opposite: hardening and self-justification (the spirit that does not register or respond to its own moral condition). CHAR-4's opposite: severity/harshness (disproportionate force toward the erring) and hesitant reluctance (self-protective delay). CHAR-5's opposite: M08 proud self-display (the assertion of worth through performance and status-seeking) and moral frivolousness (the absence of seriousness). CHAR-6's opposite: reluctant compliance and compelled giving — the will dragged rather than freely moving. The cluster-level anatomy: the opposites cluster around two poles — *self-elevating assertion* (pride opposing CHAR-1; proud self-display opposing CHAR-5; self-justification opposing CHAR-3) and *self-protective resistance* (inner refusal opposing CHAR-2; reluctance opposing CHAR-6; hesitation and severity opposing CHAR-4). M09 thus names both the positive characteristics and their opposites in a way that comprehensively describes non-self-assertive inner existence and its distortions.

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's exclusions form a coherent pattern. All six characteristics exclude the same underlying orientation: the self turned inward against God and others. The exclusions differ in their specific form. CHAR-1 explicitly excludes self-exaltation, conceit, and performed pseudo-humility (Col2:18/23). CHAR-2 excludes eye-service and yielding to false authority (Gal2:5 — submission withheld from false teachers). CHAR-3 excludes self-sufficiency, self-justification, and detachment from one's own moral condition. CHAR-4 excludes excessive severity (the opposite of metriopatheō) and self-protective hesitation (okneō). CHAR-5 excludes self-promotion, frivolousness, and unstable self-presentation. CHAR-6 excludes compelled compliance and self-generated willpower as a substitute for genuine willing-heartedness. Cluster-level: the exclusions confirm that M09 is not primarily against strength, authority, or achievement — it is specifically against the self-asserting and self-protective *inner orientations* that make a person closed to God and closed to others. The cluster is internally consistent in what it excludes.

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The intra-cluster boundaries are the most analytically productive boundaries in this prompt. Five boundary pairs are evidenced within M09 itself. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-3:* Isa57:15 directly evidences the boundary — contrition (dak.ka — acute crisis-state) co-occurs with lowliness (CHAR-1 — stable posture) but is not identical to it; humility is the stable disposition; contrition is the acute intensity-form it takes under specific moral-crisis conditions. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-2:* Phili2:8 holds both (humbled himself by becoming obedient) but the boundary is relational structure — humility is the general self-lowering posture; submission is the specifically authority-directed yielding. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-4:* Mat11:29 holds both (gentle and lowly in heart) — humility is the inner posture of self-lowering; meekness/gentleness is the quality of relational engagement that the posture enables. *CHAR-2 / CHAR-6:* 1Ch28:21 (willing and wholly at your command) holds both — submission is command-responsive; willing-heartedness is self-initiated from inner motivation. *CHAR-1 / CHAR-5:* humility is the active downward movement; dignity is the stable non-assertive settled position. These five intra-cluster boundary distinctions are the cluster's most significant analytical contribution to the inner-being landscape — M09 is a cluster of mutually defining adjacent characteristics.

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster covers four distinct modes of inner operation, and each characteristic is primary in one or two. *Initiating volitional act* (CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-6): the person initiates an inner movement — humbles themselves, yields the will, brings from the willing heart. *Stable character quality* (CHAR-5, CHAR-1 in its dispositional register): the characteristic is the settled inner property shaping all conduct. *Capacity governing engagement* (CHAR-4): the inner ability that shapes the quality of relational contact. *Acute experiential state* (CHAR-3): the inner condition the person finds themselves in, which they sustain rather than generate. The cluster-level finding: no single mode accounts for all six characteristics, which confirms M09's phenomenological diversity. The most critical distinction within the cluster is between CHAR-3 (primarily undergone, state-like) and CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-6 (primarily performed, act-like), with CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 occupying the intermediate capacity-and-quality register.

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Context-dependence is evidenced across the cluster with consistent patterns. All six characteristics show variation between the *vertical* (Godward) and *horizontal* (human-to-human) registers. The Godward register is primary for CHAR-3 Contrition (exclusively evidenced as before-God in this corpus) and dominant for CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness. Both CHAR-1 Humility and CHAR-2 Submission have extensively documented both directions (ka.na corpus = vertical; Eph4:2; Phili2:3 = horizontal for CHAR-1; household obedience + gospel obedience for CHAR-2). CHAR-4 Meekness and CHAR-5 Dignity are primarily evidenced in the horizontal/social register (dealing gently with the erring; household and teaching authority). The cluster-level finding: the six characteristics map to different positions on the vertical-horizontal axis, with no single characteristic spanning the full range as completely as CHAR-1 and CHAR-2. This mapping suggests the cluster can be read as a comprehensive account of non-self-assertive existence in both its divine-relational and human-community dimensions.

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Speech-adjacent modes are present in four of the six characteristics; two are silent on this. CHAR-1 Humility: speech-adjacent — the humbling is expressed in verbal acknowledgment of God's rightness (2Ch12:6 — "the Lord is righteous") and in receiving God's word as personally claiming. CHAR-2 Submission: auditory-constitutive — the very root of hupakoē is "under-hearing"; the characteristic is constituted by reception-and-yielding to what is heard. CHAR-4 Meekness: indirectly speech-involved — the high priest's gentle dealing (Heb5:2) involves verbal intercession and instruction. CHAR-5 Dignity: directly speech-shaping — Tit2:7 ("in your teaching show… dignity") — the manner of teaching is the characteristic's communicative expression. CHAR-3 Contrition and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness are silent on communicative modes in their direct evidence. Cluster-level: CHAR-2's auditory-constitutive character (hupakoē — under-hearing) makes it unique in the cluster — it is the only characteristic whose very root encodes reception of heard speech as its constitutional origin. CHAR-5's communicative mode (teaching manner) makes it unique as the characteristic most directly expressed in and through verbal instruction.

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The immediate responses cluster into two families. *Divine-toward-person responses* (God's immediate response to the human state): CHAR-3 Contrition — God draws near and saves (Psa34:18); CHAR-1 Humility — God hears and extends mercy (2Ch12:7; Luk18:14). *Human-toward-world responses* (the characteristic's own immediate expression outward): CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness — immediate act of bringing (Exo35:22 — "brought"); CHAR-2 Submission — inner repositioning of will (cognitive-volitional self-assessment in Luk17:10); CHAR-4 Meekness — prompt responsive movement (Act9:38 — the expected unimpeded response); CHAR-5 Dignity — evoked ordered respect in others (1Ti3:4 — orderly compliance). ## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Two characteristics are explicitly spirit-located: CHAR-3 Contrition (both verses — dak.ka-ruach in Psa34:18 and Isa57:15) and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness (Psa51:12 — ruach nedivah, willing spirit). Both are OT Hebrew characteristics; the spirit-explicit evidence is exclusively in the Hebrew OT corpus within M09. CHAR-1 Humility has spirit-level implications from the constitutional depth of its evidence but lacks explicit spirit-location naming; CHAR-2, CHAR-4, and CHAR-5 are not explicitly spirit-located in their corpora. The cluster-level finding: the two characteristics most explicitly located at spirit level (CHAR-3 and CHAR-6) are both OT Hebrew and both have exclusively OT corpora — confirming the OT Hebrew tradition's preference for explicit spirit-location vocabulary. The characteristics with NT-dominant evidence tend toward heart, conscience, and will as their named constitutional locations rather than spirit. This OT/NT vocabulary pattern for constitutional location is itself a cluster-level finding.

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates at the spirit level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — For CHAR-3 and CHAR-6, spirit-level origin is evidenced: contrition reaches the spirit first (Psa34:18 — crushed in spirit) and willing-heartedness must be restored at the spirit level (Psa51:12 — uphold me with a willing spirit). For the other four, spirit-level origin is implied by the constitutional depth of their evidence without being explicitly named. The cluster-level inference: the spirit level is evidenced as the constitutional origin of the cluster's two most acute inner states (CHAR-3 — the most acutely broken; CHAR-6 — the most freely-willing). That the two extremes of the cluster (most broken; most freely-given) are both spirit-located is analytically significant — it suggests the spirit level is where M09's characteristics are most constitutionally concentrated, even when not explicitly named.

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The spirit-level location of CHAR-3 and CHAR-6 reveals two different constitutional depths. For CHAR-3 Contrition: spirit-level location explains why genuine contrition cannot be coerced — external compulsion cannot reach the spirit to produce genuine crushing; the breaking must come from within through genuine confrontation with moral reality. For CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: spirit-level location explains why genuine willing-heartedness cannot be manufactured — the freely-springing spirit cannot be pushed from outside; when it is lost (Psa51:12), only divine upholding at the spirit level can restore it. The cluster-level implication for all six: M09 characteristics are constitutionally deep — they require formation at the innermost level of the person, whether that is explicitly named as spirit (CHAR-3, CHAR-6) or implied by the depth of transformation evidenced (CHAR-1's uncircumcised heart; CHAR-2's soul-purification in 1Pe1:22). M09 cannot be inhabited at the surface.

This silence is not evidence of shallower constitutional depth but of different vocabulary traditions: these characteristics' evidence is predominantly NT Greek, and the NT vocabulary tends to name will, heart, conscience, and soul as constitutional locations rather than spirit. CHAR-1 Humility's evidence implies spirit-level depth without naming it. The cluster-wide note: the silence on spirit-level location in four of six characteristics is a vocabulary effect of the OT/NT distribution rather than a finding about constitutional depth.

## T3 — Inner Faculties

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics engage the perceptive faculty but through different inner-sense channels. CHAR-4: through *accurate social perception* — perceiving both one's own limitation (self-ward) and the other's condition (other-ward) without distortion. CHAR-5: primarily through the *perceptual field created in others* — the person of dignity is perceived by others as genuinely authoritative without having to assert it. CHAR-6: through *reception of invitation* — the willing-hearted person perceives the call to give as genuinely directed to their inner freedom rather than as external compulsion. Cluster-level: CHAR-2's auditory-constitutive character (hearing-under) is the most distinctive — it is the only characteristic for which the perceptive faculty (specifically hearing) is encoded in the very root of the primary term. The cluster as a whole confirms that M09 characteristics are perceptive in the deepest sense: they require and enable genuine reception of reality without the distorting filter of self-protection.

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics enable and deepen the perceptive faculty; none impair it. The enabling mechanism differs by characteristic. CHAR-1 and CHAR-3 enable perception by removing the self-protective filter — the humble person (CHAR-1) and the contrite person (CHAR-3) are genuinely open to receiving reality because the self-enclosing distortion of pride has been lowered or broken. CHAR-4 enables perception of others by requiring genuine self-perception first — metriopatheō (Heb5:2) requires honest self-knowledge of one's own weakness before it enables accurate perception of the other's condition. CHAR-5 enables others' perception of the person — dignity removes the distorting noise of self-promotion so that the person is perceived clearly. CHAR-6 enables reception of invitations — the willing-hearted person receives the call to give without the filtering of reluctance. CHAR-2 enables full reception of the word heard — genuine submission enables the word to penetrate to the soul level (1Pe1:22) rather than being blocked by inner refusal. Cluster-level: M09 is a perception-enabling cluster — every characteristic in the cluster functions to remove the perceptive distortions that self-assertion produces.

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six engage the cognitive faculty; the engagement is most constitutive for CHAR-1 and CHAR-2, most presuppositional for the others. CHAR-1: Phili2:3 names a cognitive act (counting others more significant — hēgeomai) as the definition of humility; Pro11:2 directly links humility to wisdom as its cognitive companion. CHAR-2: 2Cor10:5 makes the cognitive faculty (taking every thought captive to Christ) the explicit battleground of submission. CHAR-3: requires genuine cognitive moral self-assessment as precondition for the characteristic. CHAR-4: requires cognitive self-assessment of shared weakness as its enabling ground. CHAR-5: requires ongoing cognitive calibration of appropriate gravity for each context. CHAR-6: cognitive skill must be deployed alongside willing-heartedness (1Ch28:21 — skill plus willingness); the characteristic enables wholehearted cognitive engagement. Cluster-level: CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 are the most distinctively cognitive among M09's characteristics — both explicitly name cognitive reordering as part of their constitutional identity. This distinguishes them from CHAR-3 through CHAR-6, where cognition is enabling rather than constitutive. The implication: M09's foundational characteristics (CHAR-1, CHAR-2) are cognitively demanding in a way the supplementary characteristics are not.

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

Cluster-level: M09 is an affect-enabling and affect-ordering cluster — each characteristic enables a specific form of genuine affective expression appropriate to its relational context. ## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**T4.1.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — God's operation toward the human person is evidenced across all six, but through three distinct forms. *God humbles and restores*: CHAR-1 — God both imposes lowliness (Psa107:12; 2Ch28:19) and responds to voluntary humbling with mercy (1Ki21:29; 2Ch12:7). *God produces and directs*: CHAR-2 — the Spirit produces the obedience of faith (1Pe1:2); God's gospel-word occasions the decisive inner reorientation. *God draws near and revives*: CHAR-3 — the most extensively evidenced God-to-human operation in the cluster; two divine actions in each verse (nearness + saving in Psa34:18; dwelling + reviving in Isa57:15). *God mediates through the appointed representative*: CHAR-4 — God's compassionate disposition toward the erring is mediated through the high priest's metriopatheō (Heb5:2). *God requires and shapes*: CHAR-5 — the apostolic directives reflect what God requires of leaders; formative-normative rather than direct bestowal. *God restores the willing spirit*: CHAR-6 — Psa51:12 is the most direct single-verse statement of God actively restoring a M09 characteristic in a person who has lost it. Cluster-level: CHAR-3 and CHAR-6 are the characteristics where God's operation toward the human person is most directly and explicitly evidenced. Notably, CHAR-6's Psa51:12 is the only verse in the cluster where God is explicitly named as the restorer of a specific characteristic after moral failure — making willing-heartedness the most divinely-dependent characteristic in the cluster.

## T5 — Transformation and Formation

**T5.1.1 — Does this characteristic produce transformation?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six characteristics produce transformation; the cluster's transformative spectrum runs from condition change to orientation change to identity change. *Most condition-changing*: CHAR-3 Contrition — the crushed state transitions to divine reviving (Isa57:15); CHAR-1 Humility (M09-B) — the covenantal condition is changed from judgment to mercy (2Ch12:7). *Most orientation-changing*: CHAR-1 (M09-A) — the inner posture shifts from self-assertion to relational openness; CHAR-6 — the inner orientation shifts from burdened/blocked to freely-offering (Psa51:12). *Most identity-forming*: CHAR-2 Submission — 1Pe1:14 names "obedient children" as the identity produced by sustained submission; Rom6:17 names the transformation as a change of whose servant the person is. CHAR-5 produces transformation in others rather than primarily in the person who has it — dignity transforms the relational conditions of those around the dignified person (orderly compliance, authenticated teaching, peaceable civic life). Cluster-level: CHAR-5 is uniquely *outward-transformative* — it produces transformation in the social sphere rather than primarily in the inner person. All other five characteristics produce transformation primarily in the inner person, with social effects following.

**T5.1.2 — Is the transformation reversible or irreversible?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster contains both, and the contrast is theologically significant. *Most explicitly reversible*: CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness — Psa51:12 directly evidences that the willing spirit can be lost through moral failure and must be sought again. CHAR-1 Humility in the covenantal-condition register: 2Ch33:23 (Amon's failure to humble as Manasseh had) suggests the transformation is not automatically transmitted. *Most conditionally durable*: CHAR-2 Submission — soul-purification (1Pe1:22) and identity-formation (1Pe1:14) suggest deep transformations, though 2Th3:14 evidences possible relapse. *Character-level transformations* (CHAR-4, CHAR-5): the capacity for metriopatheō and the quality of semnotēs are characterological and thus more stable, though not invulnerable to character-loss. Cluster-level: CHAR-6's explicit reversibility is unique in the cluster — it is the only characteristic whose corpus contains a direct narrative of the characteristic being lost and sought again (Psa51:12). This makes willing-heartedness M09's most fragile characteristic and the most clearly dependent on divine sustaining. The cluster's most stable transformation (CHAR-2 identity-level soul-purification) contrasts with its most fragile (CHAR-6 willing spirit subject to moral failure).

**T5.3.1 — What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's transformative mechanisms are diverse and non-overlapping. CHAR-1 Humility: volitional self-application (the person applies the characteristic to themselves through an act of will); divine imposition (God bows hearts down); word-reception (hearing God's word occasions the inner act). CHAR-2 Submission: decisive volitional reorientation; gradual formation through sustained practice; costly formation through suffering (Heb5:8 — the paradigmatic mechanism). CHAR-4 Meekness: compassionate calibrated engagement with the erring (metriopatheō as the quality of the encounter itself). CHAR-5 Dignity: character-gravity producing natural social ordering — the mechanism is the person's inner quality rather than external enforcement. CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness: inner free-movement (VCG-01) or divine upholding (VCG-02). Cluster-level: the most significant mechanism-contrast within the cluster is between CHAR-2's costly-formation-through-suffering (Heb5:8) and CHAR-6's freely-springing inner movement (na.div). CHAR-2's most distinctive mechanism requires suffering as the formative medium; CHAR-6's most distinctive mechanism requires the complete absence of compulsion. These are the two poles of M09's transformative mechanism range.

**T5.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's relationship to suffering varies systematically across the six characteristics. *Suffering as formative ground*: CHAR-2 — Heb5:8 (Christ learned obedience through suffering) is the cluster's most explicit suffering-as-formation statement; CHAR-4 — the high priest's "beset with weakness" (Heb5:2) grounds metriopatheō in shared creaturely suffering. *Suffering as characteristic constitutive* (the characteristic is itself a form of suffering): CHAR-3 Contrition — the crushed spirit and broken heart are themselves an inner suffering. *Suffering as context in which the characteristic is tested and sustained*: CHAR-1 Humility — Act20:19 (Paul's sustained humility through trials); CHAR-2 — 1Pe3:6 (obedience without fear of frightening things). *Suffering as characteristic disruptor*: CHAR-6 — moral-failure suffering disrupts the willing spirit; Psa51:12 is the post-disruption restoration prayer. *Suffering as absent context*: CHAR-5 — the corpus is silent on the characteristic's relation to suffering. Only CHAR-6 shows suffering as primarily disruptive. The cluster is broadly suffering-embracing in its transformative mechanisms.

## Appendix — Synthesis prose

### 1. The non-self-assertive cluster: a unified account across six characteristics

M09 is the programme's account of non-self-assertive inner existence in six registers. What unites the six is not a single vocabulary term, a single constitutional location, or a single relational target — it is the structural exclusion of self-assertion and self-promotion as constitutive inner orientations. The person formed by M09 is one who neither forces God nor forces others; who gives freely, yields genuinely, inhabits social existence with settled gravity, and can be broken by moral reality without being destroyed by it.

The six characteristics are not six degrees of a single virtue but six genuinely distinct forms of non-self-assertive inner existence. Humility (CHAR-1) is the foundational posture — the voluntary self-lowering that positions the person relationally before God and others. Submission (CHAR-2) is the volitional face of that posture directed toward legitimate authority — the will that genuinely yields rather than performing compliance. Contrition (CHAR-3) is the acute crisis-form — what humility looks and feels like when genuine moral failure meets genuine encounter with divine holiness. Meekness and Gentleness (CHAR-4) is the relational-engagement face — what the self-aware, self-lowered person brings to their dealings with the erring and needy. Dignity (CHAR-5) is the settled resting-place — the stable character gravity that does not need to assert, protect, or display. Willing-heartedness (CHAR-6) is the freely-springing generous face — the will that moves toward God and others without being pushed.

These six are mutually defining. Contrition and Humility co-occur in Isa57:15's "contrite and lowly in spirit" — they are not identical, but the contrite person is always also lowly. Humility and Submission co-constitute the single Christological act of Phili2:8 — "humbled himself by becoming obedient" — where the disposition and the directional yielding of the will cannot be separated. Dignity and Submission are causally related in 1Ti3:4 — the overseer's settled gravity (CHAR-5) is what produces the children's genuine ordered submission (CHAR-2) without coercive force. Willing-heartedness and Joy co-occur in Psa51:12 as parallel gifts of divine restoration — the two are not identical but are simultaneously recovered because they belong together in the freely-devoted inner life.

The cluster is also internally graduated by constitutional depth. The two spirit-and-heart-located characteristics (CHAR-3 and CHAR-6) are the deepest constitutional realities in the cluster — contrition crushes the spirit and breaks the heart; willing-heartedness must be restored at the spirit level by divine upholding. The characteristics of stable social ordering (CHAR-4 and CHAR-5) operate at the will and character levels without explicit spirit-location. CHAR-1 and CHAR-2, spanning both testaments and occupying both the heart and the will, are the constitutional bridges — they are deep enough to require transformation at the soul-purification level (1Pe1:22) while wide enough to shape every relational sphere.

### 2. The Christological hinge: CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 as the cluster's theological centre

The cluster's most striking theological feature is its Christological concentration. Only two of the six characteristics have explicit divine attribution — and both are through the incarnate Son. Mat11:29 attributes both humility (CHAR-1) and meekness (CHAR-4, in the compound "gentle and lowly") to Jesus in first-person self-description. This Christological concentration is not accidental — it is the theological reason why the cluster's characteristics are virtues rather than deficiencies. In Greco-Roman culture, tapeinos (lowly) was not a compliment; submission was a social description of the defeated or enslaved. The NT community's positive valorisation of these characteristics required not merely cultural resistance but Christological grounding: the divine Son voluntarily took on humility and submission, thereby transforming them from deficiency-markers into expressions of the divine character in creaturely form.

The implication at cluster level is that CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 are the cluster's Christological core. The other four characteristics (CHAR-3, CHAR-4, CHAR-5, CHAR-6) are exclusively or primarily creaturely in their direct evidence — they image divine qualities without being attributed to God. But CHAR-1 and CHAR-2 are the characteristics the Son genuinely takes on, making them characteristics in which the creature participates in the Son's own inner life rather than merely imitating a divine example. The cluster's call to humility (Col3:12; Phili2:3) is grounded in Phili2:5–8 — "let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus" — making the cluster's primary demand an invitation into Christological participation rather than mere moral improvement.

### 4. OT vs NT vocabulary arcs and what they reveal

The cluster's six characteristics distribute across the OT/NT vocabulary spectrum in a pattern that is analytically significant. The NT vocabulary development is consistently toward: dispositional (from event to character), positive (from deficiency-marking to virtue-naming), and structural (from single act to relational condition).

### 5. The CHAR-1 volume-split and its analytical significance (Observation #16)

The confirmed Observation #16 (CHAR-1 volume-split across M09-A voluntary self-lowering and M09-B experienced/imposed lowliness) is the most important intra-characteristic structural finding in the cluster. The two registers are analytically distinct but name the same characteristic faculty:

### 8. Unexpected cross-characteristic patterns

Three unexpected patterns surfaced during the 189-row synthesis pass not fully anticipated in the brief's suggested themes.

**CHAR-4's complete relational isolation from the Godward direction**: the synthesis confirmed that CHAR-4 Meekness and Gentleness is the only M09 characteristic without direct evidence of Godward operation in its verse corpus (Act9:38 and Heb5:2 both address the characteristic in human-toward-human register). This was expected at the per-characteristic level but is notable at the cluster level — CHAR-4 is genuinely the cluster's most horizontally-concentrated characteristic, naming not a posture before God but a quality of engagement with other humans. This makes CHAR-4 the cluster's most directly community-ethical characteristic — the one that most directly addresses how believers engage with one another in their weakness and need.

**The CHAR-3 / CHAR-6 deep-constitutional pairing**: the synthesis revealed that CHAR-3 Contrition and CHAR-6 Willing-heartedness share more dimensional architecture than their surface dissimilarity suggests. Both are explicitly spirit-and-heart-located (the only two in the cluster); both are OT-Hebrew-only in their primary vocabulary; both describe constitutional states that cannot be manufactured or coerced (the crushed spirit; the freely-springing willing spirit). Despite their apparent opposites (one crushed, one freely-moving), they share constitutional depth, OT Hebrew vocabulary tradition, and the feature that external compulsion cannot reach their constitutional level to produce them. This unexpected pairing suggests a deep structural connexion: the person who has been genuinely broken (CHAR-3) and genuinely restored is the same person who can freely give (CHAR-6) — Psa51 traces this arc in miniature, with contrition (Psa51:1–11) followed by the prayer for willing spirit (Psa51:12).

**CHAR-5's causal relationship to CHAR-2**: the synthesis confirmed and articulated the 1Ti3:4 finding more precisely than the per-characteristic batches alone: dignity (CHAR-5) produces submission (CHAR-2) in the household authority relationship through character-gravity rather than force. This causal relationship is the cluster's most significant finding for contemporary applications — it identifies character (settled non-coercive gravity) rather than external enforcement as the mechanism by which genuine ordered submission is produced in authority relationships. The practical implication: households, congregations, and institutions in which leaders have genuine CHAR-5 gravity will find CHAR-2 emerging in those they lead without requiring coercive enforcement; those that lack CHAR-5 and attempt to produce CHAR-2 through force will produce compliance rather than genuine submission.

### 9. Eschatological orientation and the M09 telos

The cluster's eschatological profile is anchored in CHAR-2 Submission's universal-submission telos. The obedience of faith among all nations (Rom1:5; 16:26) and the eschatological gathering of the peoples in submission to the coming ruler (Gen49:10) give CHAR-2 the widest eschatological scope of any single characteristic in the cluster — and possibly in the programme to date. The M09 cluster's eschatological vision is thus: all peoples willingly yielding allegiance to the one to whom all legitimate authority belongs.

CHAR-1 Humility's exaltation-pattern adds the individual dimension: every person who voluntarily takes the lower place now will be divinely lifted at the proper time (1Pe5:6). The individual and the universal are held together by the cluster's Christological anchor — the Son who humbled himself (CHAR-1) by becoming obedient (CHAR-2) was subsequently exalted to the name above every name (Phili2:9), such that all knees will bow and all tongues confess (Phili2:10–11 — the universal-submission eschatological fulfillment).

CHAR-6's tabernacle typology adds the worship-dimension: the OT willing-hearted offering for God's dwelling place (Exo35) points toward an eschatological fullness in which the whole community gives freely and gladly to God without the fragility that Psa51:12 presupposes — the present willing spirit is vulnerable; the eschatological willing spirit will be permanently upheld. CHAR-5's eschatological silence (the most present-oriented characteristic in the cluster) is analytically consistent with its role as an ordering quality of present social existence — it anticipates the eschatological only indirectly through the ordered community it shapes.

The cluster's eschatological contribution to the programme: M09 is the cluster that describes *what the creature will be toward God and toward others* when fully formed — willingly submitted (CHAR-2), freely giving (CHAR-6), genuinely humble (CHAR-1), empathically engaged (CHAR-4), characterized by settled gravity (CHAR-5), and capable of genuine contrition (CHAR-3) as the pathway back from failure to renewal. The eschatological fullness will not require CHAR-3 (no moral failure to occasion contrition) but will include all five other characteristics in their permanent, uninterruptible forms.

### 10. The cluster's distinctive contribution to the programme's inner-being account

M09 is the programme's most comprehensive account of the non-self-asserting will and character. Its distinctive contribution is not the identification of any single virtue but the mapping of the full landscape of non-self-assertive inner existence across six distinct registers — voluntary (CHAR-1), relational-authority (CHAR-2), crisis-acute (CHAR-3), mediating-compassionate (CHAR-4), settled-characterological (CHAR-5), and freely-generous (CHAR-6).

The cluster's analytical contribution to the programme's governing question ("what are the inner workings of the human being as described in Scripture?") is this: the human being in its most genuinely created form — before God, in relation to others, in the exercise of its will, and in the quality of its character — is constitutively non-self-assertive. This does not mean passive, suppressed, or self-effacing: CHAR-4's prompt responsive readiness, CHAR-6's agency-maximising generous giving, and CHAR-1's active volitional choice of the lower place all confirm that M09's account of the non-self-assertive person is of one who is more genuinely active, more fully expressed in giving and serving, than the self-asserting alternative. The person most fully formed by M09 is not less themselves — they are most genuinely themselves, expressed freely (CHAR-6), relationally (CHAR-4), with calibrated gravity (CHAR-5), in genuine submission to the one whose authority is genuinely legitimate (CHAR-2), with the openness that genuine encounter with God's holiness produces (CHAR-3), and from the foundational posture of voluntary self-lowering before the one from whom all genuine exaltation comes (CHAR-1).
