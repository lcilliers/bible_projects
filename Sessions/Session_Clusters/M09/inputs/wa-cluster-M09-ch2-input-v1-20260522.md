_Meaning of the term here:_ Jesus locates lowliness explicitly in the heart as a self-description; it is paired with gentleness and presented as the inner quality that makes his yoke restful for souls, inviting imitation.

**Phili 2:3** (G5012 tapeinofrosunē) [M09-A-VCG-05]

_Meaning of the term here:_ Humility is defined as counting others more significant than oneself—a willful inner reordering of self-estimation that directly opposes conceit and selfish ambition, making it a cognitive and affective act of the will.

**Key verses for this characteristic:**

_Meaning of the term here:_ God bows hearts down through hard labor so people fall with no helper; the humbling acts directly on the inner faculty of the heart, using external affliction to produce inner collapse and dependence.

**Phili 4:12** (G5013 tapeinoō) [M09-B-VCG-03]

_Meaning of the term here:_ Being brought low (tapeinoō) is a circumstantial, experiential state Paul has passed through and learned to navigate; here the inner quality is a cultivated contentment enabling him to endure lowness without loss of peace or stability.
