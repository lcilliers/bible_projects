## VCG design

### M09-G-VCG-01 — Semnotēs: settled moral gravity as authenticating inner character

**Context description:** This VCG carries the full dignity characteristic — the inner quality of settled moral seriousness and gravitas that grounds composed, non-self-displaying conduct in every context of Christian life and leadership. Semnotēs in 1Ti2:2 names the sober, weighty seriousness of character that shapes how believers carry themselves in public life under civil authority — the inner quality of a peaceful, godly life. In 1Ti3:4 it is the inner gravity from which the overseer's orderly household governance flows — a character quality visible in how his children respond to his authority. In Tit2:7 it is the gravitas and moral seriousness that must be visible in how Titus presents himself in teaching, authenticating his instruction. All three evidence the same inner quality — settled moral weight that does not need to prove, assert, or display itself — in three different relational contexts (civic, household, pedagogical). The cross-register flag M08 (structural opposite — dignity as the inner ground that precludes proud self-display) travels with this VCG into Phase 9 T6 findings.
