## VCG design

### M09-C-VCG-01 — Inner allegiance of will: what one obeys determines whose servant one is

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of submission as inner allegiance of the will — the idea that the will's habitual yielding discloses and constitutes whose authority rules the person. The Rom6 cluster names this most explicitly: obeying sin's desires (Rom6:12) is the negative inner yielding believers are commanded to resist; presenting oneself to any authority as slave determines whose servant one is (Rom6:16 — both hupakoē and hupakouō vc_ids evidencing the same verse from two terms); obedience located explicitly in the heart as genuine transformation of core disposition (Rom6:17). Luk17:10 (the sole diatassō M09 verse) joins this VCG: the servant who has done all that was directed adopts an inner posture of self-assessment that rejects merit — the will that has yielded to direction arrives at a stance of inner non-claim. Together these five evidences place the submission characteristic in the register of inner-allegiance: the will's orientation toward an authority is itself constitutive of the person's condition. Cross-register flag: M30 (obedience — register-adjacent); M23 (diatassō — primary register; Luk17:10 is the only M09-content verse).

### M09-C-VCG-02 — Cognitive-volitional submission: mind, conscience, and deliberate acts of will

**Context description:** This VCG carries the cognitive-volitional register of submission as an inner disposition — where the inner dimension of obedience is named in the mind's capture, the conscience's deliberation, and the will's active positioning. Taking every thought captive to Christ locates obedience at the cognitive boundary of the inner person (2Cor10:5); the completeness of a community's inner submission as the condition before discipline is applied (2Cor10:6) evidences submission as a corporate inner state. Receiving Paul's representative with fear and trembling shows submission as an inner disposition expressed through reverential posture (2Cor7:15). Gal2:5 is the most distinctive: Paul deliberately withholds submission from false teachers, showing submission as a decisive act of the will that can be consciously refused — inner conscience and conviction at stake in both the act and its refusal. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).

### M09-C-VCG-03 — Sincerity of heart: genuine inner motive against performance

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of submission-as-genuine-inner-motive — the cluster of verses that explicitly distinguish sincere, God-ward inner compliance from outward performance or social-pressure compliance. Eph6:5 names the requirement: obedience with sincere heart, as to Christ rather than as people-pleasers. Phili2:12 evidences a sustained inner disposition that must persist without external accountability — rooted in conscience and fear of God, not social pressure. Col3:22 names the same distinction most sharply: sincerity of heart and fear of the Lord replace eye-service. 1Ti2:11 adds the submissive learning posture — whole-hearted receptivity rather than resistance or self-assertion as the inner posture of the one under instruction. All four share the emphasis on inner genuineness of the submission act as distinct from performed or socially-compelled compliance. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).

### M09-C-VCG-04 — Learned, costly, and purifying submission

**Context description:** This VCG carries the register of submission as something acquired, transformative, or costly at the deepest levels of the inner person. Christ learned obedience through suffering — a quality not natural but trained, shaped by enduring pain in submission to the Father (Heb5:8); this makes the inner disposition of submission a product of costly formation, not merely a chosen posture. Abraham's obedience was faith-driven — a willed inner response that overrode the need for comprehension, trusting the divine word more than personal understanding (Heb11:8). Obedience to truth purifies the soul itself — the inner-being effect reaches to the deepest faculty, producing sincere love from a pure heart (1Pe1:22). Sarah's obedience to Abraham was sustained by courage and inward disposition alongside speech — will and courage together maintaining the obedient stance (1Pe3:6). These four evidence submission's effects on the inner person's deepest registers: it forms, purifies, and requires courage. Register-adjacent with M30 (Obedience).
