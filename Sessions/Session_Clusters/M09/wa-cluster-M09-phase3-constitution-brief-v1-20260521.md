## §6.3.2 — Verse-level relationship test for TRANSFERS (CRITICAL)

A TRANSFERS verdict requires more than "the term's meaning corpus aligns primarily with another cluster's characteristic." Corpus-primary-register reasoning alone is too aggressive.

**The test, verse by verse:**

1. Read every verse in the term's meaning corpus.
2. For each verse, ask: *does this verse evidence any relationship with or impact on M09's characteristic (humility, meekness, submission)?* "Relationship" is broad: direct evidence, structural opposite (pride-by-contrast), instrument-of, response-to, protective-against, in-tension-with.

**Verdict rule:**
- If **any** verse evidences a relationship with M09 → **STAYS** with cross-register flag in the rationale.
- If **no** verse evidences any M09 relationship → **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster}** confirmed (accidental placement).

The cross-register flag travels with the term into Phase 5/7 and informs Phase 9 T6 findings.

**For G1299 diatassō specifically:** the Pass A meanings on all 4 verses explicitly note "does not evidence inner-being content for the term." Under §6.3.2, this is a clean TRANSFERS candidate. The destination is your judgment — diatassō names directing/commanding; possibly M23 (authority/strength) or BOUNDARY if no clear destination.

## Disallowed BOUNDARY reasons (per v2_8 §6.3.1)

BOUNDARY may **NOT** be assigned solely because:

- The term's meaning corpus is predominantly horizontal (human-to-human) rather than vertical (God-directed).
- The meanings describe sensory / material / circumstantial inner-being rather than overtly spiritual inner-being.
- The meanings include external-action content (submission as bodily compliance) alongside inner-disposition content.
- The meanings describe an inner-being state you would prefer not to be in scope.

Inner being is the entire human inner life — no theological narrowing.

## Valid BOUNDARY reasons (cite one of these three)

1. **Cluster-membership undecided** — corpus is genuinely on the borderline between this cluster and another's; transfer destination unclear; researcher decision needed.
2. **Homonymic / polysemic spread** — the term's meaning corpus covers two or more distinct registers and the term may need sense-split treatment.
3. **Supportive / qualifying register** — the term's meanings enhance or qualify M09's characteristic without itself carrying it as a primary characteristic.
