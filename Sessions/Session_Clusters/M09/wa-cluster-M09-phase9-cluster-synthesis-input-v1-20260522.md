## §1 — Cluster overview

M09 (Humility, Meekness and Submission) holds 6 inner-being characteristics:

| Char | Short name | One-line definition |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Humility | The inner disposition of voluntary self-lowering before God and others. |
| 2 | Submission | The inner disposition of willing yielding to legitimate authority — heart-located submission of will, not merely external compliance. |
| 3 | Contrition | The acute form of humility produced by genuine confrontation with one's failure or with the holiness of God — crushed/broken spirit. |
| 4 | Meekness and gentleness | The inner quality of calibrated restraint and gentleness — controlled strength, capacity to hold force in measured response. |
| 5 | Dignity | Grounded moral gravity and seriousness of character — inner-grounded worth that does NOT require self-promotion or status-seeking. |
| 6 | Willing-heartedness | The freely-moved willing disposition of the heart/spirit. |

Each characteristic produced 189 findings in its Phase 9 batch (1,323 rows total in `cluster_finding`).
