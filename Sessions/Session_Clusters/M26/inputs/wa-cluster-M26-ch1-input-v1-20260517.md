- **Human Righteousness — State of being right** — Located in the character, conduct, and covenantal standing of the human person; the term names the state of being rightly oriented toward God and toward others — a governing inner condition from which just thought, truthful speech, generous action, stability under adversity, and appropriate response to God (fear, trust, joy, prayer) all flow.
- **Moral Uprightness: Inner Disposition of Straightness** — Located in the will and conduct of the person; engages the moral-volitional faculty through the governing metaphor of straightness versus crookedness; produces directness, equity, and reliability in action and relationship; structurally opposed by perverseness and crooked dealing; directed from the person outward in social and relational conduct rather than primarily from God, distinguishing it from the covenantal emphasis of M26-A.
- **BOUNDARY** — These terms support the cluster's judicial and evaluative activity without themselves constituting inner-being characteristics: aitia and aitios are procedural/role designations (charge, causer/culpable party), kardiognōstēs is a divine capacity that enables just judgment rather than a person's inner state, endikos qualifies outcomes as legally deserved rather than naming a character trait, and shephot/shephat designate the institutional act or office of judging.
- **Justification: Declaring or Making Righteous** — Located in the judicial act directed at a person's standing before God or a human court; engages the declarative-judicial faculty; produces a change in moral-legal status from condemned to vindicated or righteous; structurally opposed by condemnation (M26-E); directed from the judging party (usually God) toward the judged person, making this distinct from M26-A which names the state rather than the act of conferring it.
- **Avenging Justice: Retribution for Wrongdoing** — Located in the will and zeal of the avenger (divine or commissioned human agent); engages the retributive-judicial faculty in response to perceived injustice or wrong done; produces punishment, requital, or restoration of the violated right; structurally opposed by impunity and the toleration of wrong; directed from the avenger toward the wrongdoer, distinguishing it from M26-C (directed toward the innocent/accused) and M26-E (a verdict rather than an enacted response).
- **Condemnation: Judicial Sentence Against** — Located in the judicial pronouncement (divine or human) directed against a person found guilty; engages the judicial-evaluative faculty in its negative verdict; produces a declared state of guilt, penalty, or sentence; structurally opposed by justification (M26-C); directed from the judge toward the defendant — and reflexively inward in the case of self-condemnation (autokatakritos), which reveals the inner-being dimension of the group.
- **Moral Reckoning: Evaluative Faculty of Worth** — Located in the cognitive-judicial faculty of the mind; engages the evaluative-reckoning capacity that assigns worth, credits righteousness, or determines accountability; produces a credited status or recognised liability; structurally opposed by partial, arbitrary, or prejudiced assessment (adiakritos names the absence of that opposite); directed from the evaluating subject (God, self, or judge) toward the person or act being assessed.
- **Structural Opposites: Wickedness and Injustice** — Located in the character and will of the person; engages the moral-volitional faculty in the direction of perversity and covenant violation; produces conduct and inner status directly opposed to tsedaqah and dikaiosunē; these terms define the cluster's inner boundary by naming what righteousness is not; directed from the person away from the divine standard, making them the polar opposite of M26-A in causal direction.
