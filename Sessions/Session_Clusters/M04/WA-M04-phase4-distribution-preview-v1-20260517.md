Two thresholds tripped:

| Signal | Threshold | M04 actual | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Biggest register-family share | > 50% of STAYS corpus | **66.1%** (Joy / Rejoicing) | ⚠ EXCEEDED |
| Biggest-to-next ratio | > 3× | **3.0×** (539 / 178) | ⚠ AT THRESHOLD |
| Benchmark biggest-SG share (closed clusters) | M01 ~38%, M02 ~47%, M03 ~25% | 66.1% | ⚠ OUT OF RANGE |

**Diagnosis:** The Joy / Rejoicing register-family is **2–3× heavier than comparable clusters' biggest sub-group**. M01-A (320 v, 38% of M01 substantive), M02-B (263 v, 47% of M02 substantive), M03-A (170 v, 25% of M03 substantive) — all sit under 50%. M04's joy register at 66% suggests Phase 5 sub-group design should split it across **multiple** sub-groups, not house it in one.

## §5. Suggested Phase 5 brief guidance (what should have been there)

The brief should have flagged:

> **The Joy / Rejoicing register-family carries 539 of 816 STAYS verses (66%).** This is well above comparable clusters (M01-A 38%, M02-B 47%, M03-A 25%). Phase 5 should split this register into **multiple sub-groups** (e.g., 4–6 of them: vertical/God-directed, communal/worship, NT joy in Christ, paradoxical joy in suffering, inner-heart joy, eschatological joy, corrupt/inverted joy). Don't house it under one sub-group — the meaning corpus supports finer carving and the analytical work at Phase 7+ will be uneven if it doesn't happen at Phase 5.

This guidance, present in the Phase 5 brief, would have produced an M04 with ~10 sub-groups (similar in shape to M03's 8 substantive + BOUNDARY) instead of the current 6 substantive + BOUNDARY where one sub-group dominates.
