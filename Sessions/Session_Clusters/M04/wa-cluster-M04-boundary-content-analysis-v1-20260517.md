## Register-family analysis — what the verses are actually about

### A. The tov complex (230 verses, 71% of BOUNDARY) — 6 distinct sub-registers

Per AI's Phase 3 cross-routing flag 4, tov splits into 6 sub-registers. Sample meanings confirm:

1. **Moral / comparative goodness** ("better-than", "what is good") — the largest sub-register. Examples:
   - 1Sa 15:28 — "the kingdom given to one better than you" (moral comparative)
   - 2Sa 14:32 — "better to have remained in exile" (inner evaluation)
   - Psa 37:16 — "little owned by the righteous surpasses wicked's abundance" (moral comparative)

2. **Property / agricultural / material goodness** — "good land", "good things":
   - Jos 23:15 — "good things God promised" (pleasant blessings)
   - Pro 27:10 — "near neighbour better than distant brother" (relational goodness)

3. **Volitional-deference formula** — "seems good to you" / political delegation:
   - Est 3:11 — "act as seems good to you" (will-pleasure)
   - 2Sa 17:14 — "God ordained the good counsel be defeated" (decision-making)

4. **Wellbeing / "it may be well with you"** — covenantal-state goodness:
   - Deu 19:13 — "purging innocent blood that it may be well with you"

5. **Pleasantness as inner refresh** — small minority with genuine inner-joy content:
   - Pro 25:25 — "good news from far away like cold water to a thirsty soul" (inner refresh)
   - Psa 92:1 — "it is good to give thanks to the Lord" (fittingness of worship)

6. **Divine evaluative** — "God saw it was good" creation contexts:
   - Lam 3:38 — "good and bad both come from the mouth of the Most High" (divine sovereign assessment)

**Researcher disposition options for tov:**
- Split the corpus at Phase 12; route ~30–50 inner-delight verses into an M04 sub-group (M04-J pleasantness, or new); send ~180 verses to a future Goodness cluster
- Keep all 230 as BOUNDARY pending a future tov-specific decision
- Move all to a future Goodness cluster wholesale

### B. ni.cho.ach (43 verses) — Levitical "pleasing aroma" formula

100% sacrificial context. Every verse is some variant of "burnt offering rises as a pleasing aroma to the LORD":

- Lev 1, 3, 8, 23 (offerings, peace, ordination, festival contexts)
- Num 28–29 (festival sacrifice schedule)
- A few Ezekiel verses (misdirected worship to idols; God receiving restored Israel)

This is **God's reception of correct worship** — divine-pleasure-at-sacrifice, not human inner joy. The "aroma" is metonymic for sacrificial acceptance.

**Disposition**: most naturally fits M21 Prayer/Worship or a future Sacrifice/Worship cluster as a coherent 40-verse block. Not M04 inner-joy.

### C. Thanksgiving register (eucharistia 15 + eucharistos 1 = 16 verses)

Practice/posture of thankful prayer:

- Phil 4:6 — "thanksgiving with prayer and supplication"
- Col 2:7, 4:2 — thankfulness accompanying spiritual groundedness, prayer
- 1Th 3:9 — thankfulness as inexpressible response
- 1Ti 2:1 — thanksgiving as form of prayer
- Col 3:15 — gratitude as heart-disposition under Christ's peace

This is **thanksgiving as devotional practice**, not joy-feeling. Joy-toned thanks belongs to M04; pure thanksgiving register belongs to M22 Praise / M21 Prayer.

**Disposition**: most go to M22 Praise; a couple (1Th 3:9 "inexpressible overflow") may stay in M04 inner-joy.

### D. NT wonder/marvel — negative or shocked (thaumazō 5 + thauma 2 = 7 verses)

NOT awe-at-God. The verses are:

- Rev 13:3, 17:6, 17:7, 17:8 — marvelling at the beast / harlot / evil (4 verses — negative/dangerous wonder)
- Gal 1:6 — Paul astonished at Galatian apostasy (negative)
- 2Cor 11:14 — "no wonder" Satan masquerades (negated)

These are marvelling-at-evil, not the positive marvelling-at-God's-works that M04-I (wonder, pa.la 63v) holds.

**Disposition**: likely M14 Deceit / M16 Folly / M27 Evil — marvelling captured by the captivating thing rather than M04 inner-awe.

### E. Luxury / pampered ease (ta.a.nug 5 + a.nog 3 = 8 verses)

Material luxury + refinement, mostly in judgment contexts:

- Ecc 2:8 — concubines as "delight of the sons of man" (luxurious indulgence)
- Pro 19:10 — luxury unfitting for a fool
- Mic 1:16, 2:9 — children as "delight" + "delightful houses" (mourning context)
- Song 7:6 — beloved's "delights" (sensual)
- Deu 28:54, 56 — pampered man/woman under siege-curse (refinement inverted)
- Isa 47:1 — Babylon's stripped-away dainty status

**Disposition**: M46 Abundance (luxury register), or M28 Envy (covetousness of luxury), or M27 Evil (when used in judgment context).

### F. Erotic / illicit / corrupt delight (che.med 5 + a.las 2 + a.li.tsut 1 = 8 verses)

- che.med — desirable young Assyrian men in Ezekiel's adultery imagery (Eze 23:6, 12, 23); pleasant vineyards/fields lost to judgment (Amo 5:11, Isa 32:12)
- a.las — Job 20:18 (wicked's denied enjoyment); Pro 7:18 (seductress's call to illicit love)
- a.li.tsut — Hab 3:14 (warriors' predatory exultation devouring the poor)

**Lustful, illicit, or predatory** inner-delight — the morally corrupt face of joy.

**Disposition**: M27 Evil or M28 Envy/Covetousness, or split (corrupt-joy stays as a structural BOUNDARY observation).

### G. Doctrinal blessedness (makarismos 3 verses)

- Rom 4:6, 4:9 — Davidic blessedness as theological category (righteousness credited)
- Gal 4:15 — Galatians' lost "blessedness"

Doctrinal/abstract blessedness, not affective joy.

**Disposition**: M39 Blessing (its natural register).

### H. Pleasing as obedience-fitting (arestos 4 verses)

- Joh 8:29 — Jesus doing what pleases the Father
- Act 6:2, 12:3 — what is "acceptable/right" (procedural)
- 1Jo 3:22 — pleasing God through obedience

Volitional / obedience-fitting register.

**Disposition**: M30 Obedience or stays as inner-acceptance register.

### I. Astonishment (mixed awe+fear) — thambeō 3 verses

- Mar 1:27 — crowd astonished at Jesus' command over unclean spirits
- Mar 10:24 — disciples amazed at wealth-and-kingdom teaching
- Mar 10:32 — those walking toward Jerusalem astonished at Jesus' resolve

Wonder mixed with fear/shock — not pure marvel.

**Disposition**: M01 Fear (awe-fear register) or stays BOUNDARY.

## Headline observation

The 322-verse BOUNDARY count is large because of **one homonymic term (tov)** carrying 230 verses across 6 distinct sub-registers. Without tov, BOUNDARY is 92 verses spread across distinct register families that each have natural destinations (worship, blessing, evil, abundance, obedience, awe-fear).

The Phase 5 v2 sub-group design wisely separated these heterogeneous BOUNDARY contents from M04's substantive joy/delight/wonder/gladness sub-groups. The Phase 12 researcher decision will need to:

1. Decide tov's split (the biggest analytical question)
2. Route ni.cho.ach as a worship-block (43-verse coherent transfer)
3. Distribute the smaller register families to their natural destination clusters

No M04 analytical work was lost by holding these in BOUNDARY. The inner-joy register that M04 properly carves (sub-groups A–J, 816 verses) is unaffected.
