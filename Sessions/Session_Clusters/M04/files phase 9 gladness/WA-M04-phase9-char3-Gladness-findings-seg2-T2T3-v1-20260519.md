## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — No verse in the M04-L or M04-O corpus explicitly names the spirit (ruach/pneuma) as the constitutional seat of Gladness. Neh 9:20's "good Spirit" names the Spirit's quality as good but does not locate Gladness in the human spirit. Spirit-level location is not evidenced for this characteristic.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — Not evidenced. The primary constitutional language is cognitive-evaluative (the faculty of discernment) and situational-affective (warm inner response). No origin at the spirit level is evidenced.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — Not applicable — spirit-level location is not evidenced. The significance of this silence: unlike Characteristic 7 (Suffering-Joy, where Spirit-mediation is prominent) and some registers of Joy, Gladness does not appear as a Spirit-produced inner state in this corpus. Its depth is evidenced at the evaluative-cognitive and affective levels rather than at the spirit level.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** S — The evidence is silent on spirit-level location for Gladness across M04-L and M04-O.

---

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Partially. Lam 3:25 — "the Lord is good to those who wait for him, to the soul who seeks him" — the soul (nephesh) is named as the seeking agent whose waiting posture enables the experience of God's goodness. Lam 3:26 — "it is good that one should wait quietly" — the benefit is for the person as a whole, with soul implied as the seat of the waiting posture. Ecc 6:3 — "his soul is not satisfied with life's good things" — the soul is the seat of satisfaction/dissatisfaction with the good. These confirm soul-level location in the wellbeing-register of M04-O.

---

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Soul-level location in the Gladness corpus confirms that the soul's orientation (seeking, waiting, being satisfied or dissatisfied) determines whether Gladness is genuinely experienced. Lam 3:25's "the soul who seeks him" as the recipient of God's goodness shows Gladness is not available to any inner posture but specifically to the seeking soul. Ecc 6:3's unsatisfied soul is the inverse: the soul that is not oriented toward genuine goodness cannot receive it.

---

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Soul-level location is evidenced at the referenced verses; not fully silent.

---

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in specific registers. Ecc 5:20 — "God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart" (sa.s.von — M04-E overlap, but the tov-context: the heart is named as the seat of gladness-in-one's-lot). Jer 15:16 — "your words became to me a joy and the delight of my heart" (sa.s.von — again overlapping M04-E but paradigmatic for Gladness's heart-location). Within the M04-O corpus: Pro 12:25 — "a good word makes him glad" — the gladness produced is in the heart (the heart being weighed down by anxiety is what the good word lifts). Psa 119:111 — "your testimonies are my heritage forever, for they are the joy of my heart" (sa.s.von) — heart-location. In M04-L: the ki-tov declarations produce thanksgiving grounded in the heart's recognition of divine goodness.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Heart-location confirms that Gladness is not purely a cognitive judgment external to the person but an inner-being state that settles in the integrating centre. The heart is where Gladness takes up residence: in Jer 15:16 the word becomes "the delight of my heart" — it is received and held inwardly, not merely assessed externally. Pro 12:25's heart weighed down by anxiety that is relieved by a good word shows the heart as the seat where Gladness's presence or absence is most directly felt.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Heart-location is evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes — more prominently than for any other M04 characteristic. The M04-L-VCG-02 corpus is essentially an exercise of the evaluative-cognitive faculty: weighing, comparing, judging. Exo 18:17 (Jethro's experienced assessment); Rut 2:22 (Naomi's prudential reasoning); Job 34:4 ("let us know among ourselves what is good" — explicitly cognitive discernment); Mic 6:8 ("he has told you, O man, what is good" — knowing is the precondition for doing). The evaluative "better than" comparatives throughout M04-L-VCG-02 and M04-O-VCG-02 are cognitive acts of comparison.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Mind-location reveals Gladness as uniquely cognitively-constituted among M04 characteristics. The evaluative-gladness faculty is a function of the person's reasoning, discerning, and comparing capacities. This explains why Gladness is less susceptible to direct command than Joy (Phili 4:4) — one cannot simply be commanded to evaluate correctly; one must be formed in the capacity for accurate moral and relational assessment. The cognitive dimension is why the wisdom literature is the primary carrier of M04-L: Proverbs and Ecclesiastes are fundamentally training texts for the evaluative faculty.

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Mind-location is the most extensively evidenced constitutional location for Gladness; not silent.

---

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes: **bones** — Pro 15:30 ("good news refreshes the bones") — the deepest somatic structure is the recipient of gladness in the good-news register; the refreshment of good news penetrates to the body's depths. This is the most physicality-affirming instance of Gladness's constitutional location.

---

**T2.5.2 — What does bones-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The "bones refreshed" by good news (Pro 15:30) reveals that Gladness in its most intense experiential form reaches the person at their deepest physical level. The bones are the body's inner framework; their refreshment by good news shows Gladness as a holistic inner-being phenomenon — not merely cognitive or affective but reaching to the body's structural level. This is consistent with the somatic imagery of restoration in the broader M04 cluster (Psa 51:8 — bones asked to rejoice).

---

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Bones-location evidenced at Pro 15:30; not silent.

---

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes: (1) **Bones** — Pro 15:30 ("good news refreshes the bones") — the deepest structural body part; (2) **Face** — implicitly through the "smile again" of Psa 39:13 (M04-F corpus but adjacent — in M04-F-VCG-01: "before I depart and am no more, let me smile again" = let me recover a little of the brightness); the face as the outward sign of inner gladness; (3) **The whole body / person** — 1Sa 16:23 ("Saul was refreshed and was well") — the inner-being state of wellbeing extending to the whole person.

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Pro 15:30's bones-connection serves an **emphatic** function: it insists that genuine good news does not remain at a surface emotional level but penetrates to the person's physical depths. This is a similar rhetorical strategy to Psa 51:8's "bones that you have broken" — the bones stand for the deepest inner experience. Scripture links Gladness to the bones to convey its thoroughgoing, holistic character: genuine gladness-from-good-news restores the whole person.

---

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Body-part link evidenced at Pro 15:30; not silent.

---

**T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — **Inner to outer**: the good news received by the inner person (cognitively and affectively) refreshes the bones — the inner gladness moves outward to the body's structural level. There is no evidence of the body initiating gladness in this corpus (unlike some Pleasure instances where sensory input produces inner warmth). The direction is consistently: news/word/circumstance received by the inner person → inner gladness arises → body participates in the refreshment.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The inner-to-outer direction confirms that Gladness is an inner-originating state that expresses itself in and through the body, not a bodily state that generates inner gladness. This is significant: the body's refreshment is evidence of inner gladness, not its cause. Genuine gladness must first be received inwardly (cognitively through evaluation, affectively through response) before it reaches the body.

---

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Link is evidenced; see T2.7.1–T2.7.2.

---

**T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — The evidence does not explicitly address constitutional deposit from sustained Gladness. Pro 15:30's "bones refreshed" is an acute rather than a cumulative effect. No generational consequence or body-deposit language appears in the M04-L or M04-O corpus.

---

**T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — No direct evidence for deposit. The pattern of moral formation through sustained evaluative-gladness (the wisdom literature's training of the evaluative faculty) implies a dispositional deposit — the person whose inner life is habitually engaged in accurate moral evaluation becomes a person of formed moral discernment — but the corpus does not name this as a body-level deposit.

---

**T2.8.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** S — The evidence is silent on constitutional body-level deposit from sustained Gladness.

---

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Multiple origins: (1) **From God's character** — M04-L-VCG-01 (the ki-tov declarations, Gen 1's "God saw that it was good"): God's own goodness is the source from which the Gladness-quality radiates; (2) **From divine acts** — M04-O-VCG-03 and VCG-04: God's good hand (Ezr 7:9; 8:18; Neh 2:8, 18), covenantal promise-fulfilment (Jos 21:45; Jer 29:10; 33:14) — Gladness originates in the experienced reality of divine action; (3) **From speech** — M04-O-VCG-01: good news, apt words, gracious speech (Pro 12:25; 15:23, 30; Isa 52:7; Zec 1:13) — external speech received by the inner person; (4) **From the person's own evaluative act** — M04-L-VCG-02: the human person's moral discernment producing the recognition of what is good or not-good; Gladness here originates in the person's own correctly-functioning evaluative faculty.

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Multiple. The four origin pathways (divine character, divine acts, speech, evaluative faculty) each produce Gladness through a different route. This multiplicity is distinctive: Joy has a similar multiplicity but with the Spirit and commanded-will as prominent origins; Gladness's origins cluster around character-recognition, circumstance-response, and evaluative discernment.

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes: doxological contexts (ki-tov Psalms) → originates from recognising divine character; narrative-rescue contexts (M04-O-VCG-03) → originates from experienced deliverance; wisdom contexts (M04-L-VCG-02, 03) → originates from the person's own evaluative act; prophetic contexts (M04-O-VCG-04 — covenantal promises) → originates from trust in divine word.

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. The movement is cognitive recognition → affective warmth → bodily refreshment: (1) The evaluative act (M04-L) is cognitive; (2) The warm inner response (M04-O) is affective; (3) The bones refreshed (Pro 15:30) is somatic. The sequence shows Gladness descending from cognitive recognition through affective warmth to physical refreshment.

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The sequence for M04-O-VCG-01 (good news) is: speech received → inner evaluation as good → warm inner brightening → bodily refreshment (Pro 15:30). For M04-L-VCG-01 (divine character recognition): perceive God's goodness → declare it in praise → inner stability/gladness established. For M04-O-VCG-03 (wellbeing): God's good hand acts → inner recognition → communal resolve (Neh 2:18). Gladness moves consistently from cognitive-perceptive → affective → outward.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Constitutional movement is evidenced across the three-level sequence.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. Gladness is consistently perception-triggered: Gen 1:4–31 — God "saw" (ra'ah) that it was good — the perceptive act produces the evaluative declaration; Gen 40:16 — the baker "saw that the interpretation was favorable" — visual/perceptive recognition initiating inner brightening; 2Sa 18:27 — David identifies the runner perceptually and judges him "a good man"; Psa 97:11 (M04-B adjacent, but the pattern) — "light is sown for the righteous"; M04-O-VCG-01: hearing good news (Pro 15:30; Isa 52:7) — the auditory perceptive faculty is the trigger.

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens perception in its healthy form: the person who habitually evaluates rightly (M04-L-VCG-02) develops the perceptive capacity to see what is genuinely good or not-good in moral and relational situations. Misdirected Gladness impairs perception: Num 14:3 (fear distorting the perceptive evaluation of the land); Isa 39:8 (Hezekiah's self-referential perception missing the full implication of Isaiah's word). Accurate Gladness both requires and trains accurate perception.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is perception-dependent in a way that distinguishes it from Joy (which can be commanded even when circumstances are adverse) — Gladness requires the accurate perception of genuine goodness before it can arise. The pattern reveals that forming the perceptive-evaluative faculty is essential to Gladness's health: moral formation that trains perception trains Gladness.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes — this is the primary faculty for Gladness, as established at T1.8. The entire M04-L-VCG-02 corpus (45 verses of moral appraisal) is an exercise of the cognitive faculty: weighing conduct against standards of goodness (Exo 18:17; Neh 5:9; Pro 17:26; 18:5; Mic 6:8); comparing relational worth (Rut 4:15; 2Sa 18:3); assessing comparative value of wisdom over wealth (Pro 8:11; 16:16; 16:8). Job 34:4 ("let us know among ourselves what is good") explicitly names cognition as the faculty being exercised.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens cognition: sustained correct evaluation of what is genuinely good trains and sharpens the cognitive-moral faculty (the wisdom literature's training purpose). Misdirected Gladness (Isa 41:7's craftsman; Num 14:3's fear-evaluation) bypasses or distorts cognition — the person reaches a "good" verdict without actually exercising careful moral reasoning. The Psa 119:68 pattern ("you are good and do good; teach me your statutes") explicitly requests continued cognitive formation in divine goodness.

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is the most cognitively-constituted characteristic in M04. Its health is directly dependent on the quality of cognition: accurate cognitive evaluation of moral reality produces genuine Gladness; distorted cognition produces distorted Gladness. This makes Gladness uniquely susceptible to formation through instruction — the wisdom literature's training of the evaluative faculty is the primary formative vehicle.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. M04-O-VCG-02 (better-than comparatives) frequently engages retrospective memory: Hos 2:7 ("it was better for me then than now") — comparison of present adversity to remembered better past; Jon 4:3, 8 (Jonah's despairing comparison) — past memory of expectations shaping present evaluation. M04-O-VCG-04 (covenantal promise-goodness) engages a different memory: Jos 21:45 ("not one word of all the good promises had failed") — the cumulative memory of God's faithfulness is the ground for present confidence. M04-L-VCG-01's doxological praise formula ("for he is good, for his steadfast love endures forever") is itself a memory-rehearsal — the Psalms' repeated ki-tov declarations sustain communal memory of divine goodness.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens memory in its healthy form: the ki-tov liturgical tradition (M04-L-VCG-01) anchors communal memory of God's goodness through repeated declaration; Jos 21:45 consolidates memory of fulfilled promises as the ground of trust. Distorted Gladness distorts memory: Jer 44:17 (the people's false attribution of former wellbeing to idol-worship) shows memory corrupted to serve a wrong conclusion. Accurate Gladness both requires and reinforces accurate memory.

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness and accurate memory are mutually reinforcing: the person and community that correctly remember God's goodness (the ki-tov tradition) are the ones who inhabit genuine Gladness. The worship-memory practice of the Psalms (ki-tov as the liturgical anchor) functions as both memory formation and Gladness formation simultaneously.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in the M04-O register. The "good news refreshing the bones" (Pro 15:30), Saul "refreshed and well" (1Sa 16:23), the men of Jabesh "glad when they heard" (1Sa 11:9), the makarismos blessedness (Rom 4:6, 9; Gal 4:15 — a felt inner state of divine-favour happiness), and Lam 3:26's "wait quietly" (a gentle, sustained affective posture) are all affective in character. The M04-L-VCG-01 ki-tov declarations also have an affective dimension — gratitude is a felt inner state.

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens affect by grounding it in accurate evaluation: the person who genuinely evaluates God as good and receives that as an inner conviction experiences a more stable and deep gladness than one whose affect is merely circumstantially triggered. The "good news refreshing the bones" is a positive affective event; but it is grounded in the cognitive recognition that the news is genuinely good. Gladness does not bypass affect but channels it through cognitive evaluation, making the affective response more reliable and stable.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is more cognitively-grounded than the other M04 characteristics' affective expressions: Joy's gladness is more spontaneously affective; Gladness (Characteristic 3) is the version of positive inner-being warmth that is most closely integrated with cognitive evaluation. The pattern reveals Gladness as the characteristic where the intellect most directly shapes and grounds the affective response.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Partially. The M04-L-VCG-01 ki-tov declarations produce and are embedded in the Psalms — creative works generated from the inner recognition of divine goodness. Isa 52:7 (the good-news herald) is a creative-prophetic image (the beauty of the messenger's feet). The doxological praise tradition that grows from the ki-tov recognition involves creative expression (song, poetry, communal liturgy). However, creativity is not the dominant faculty of Gladness in this corpus — it is secondary to the cognitive and affective.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness enables creativity in its doxological expression: recognising divine goodness naturally produces the impulse toward praise-expression (the ki-tov formula moving into singing). Gladness does not bypass creativity but gives it its content and occasion.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness generates a particular form of creativity — the doxological and declarative. The creative expression that Gladness produces is primarily the expression of recognised goodness: testimony, praise declaration, and the written wisdom that codifies evaluative learning (Proverbs, Ecclesiastes as creative-literary crystallisations of the evaluative-gladness faculty).

---

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in several registers: (1) **Jeremiah's submission** (Jer 26:14 — M04-H-VCG-03 overlap, one verse in M04-L-VCG's cross-assign) — volitional deference to what seems good to the other; (2) **Lam 3:26** — "it is good to wait quietly" — the waiting is a volitional choice; (3) **The makarismos passages** (M04-O-VCG-04) — blessedness is received through faith, which is itself a volitional act; (4) **The wisdom-comparative evaluations** (M04-L-VCG-03) — choosing wisdom over gold (Pro 16:16) is a volitional act informed by the evaluative faculty.

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness enables volition by providing the cognitive-evaluative framework within which good choices are made: the person who accurately evaluates what is genuinely good (M04-L-VCG-02) has the inner clarity needed for right volitional choices. Gladness does not bypass volition but informs it. The Proverbs evaluative corpus is essentially a guide to informed volition — knowing what is good (evaluative Gladness) enables choosing what is good (volition).

---

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness and volition are related in the pattern of cognition-informs-choice: Gladness's cognitive face (accurate evaluation of the good) is the prerequisite for good volitional choices. The pattern reveals Gladness as the inner faculty that prepares the will — it is not the same as the will but it precedes and enables its right operation.

---

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. Neh 2:18 — the people's resolve to build ("let us rise up and build") was stirred by hearing of God's good hand — Gladness-recognition producing agency; 1Sa 11:9 — the men of Jabesh glad at the news of coming deliverance → implicit enabling of their sustained resolve; Lam 3:27 — "it is good for a man that he bear the yoke in his youth" — the gladness of formative discipline enables the agentive capacity of the mature person. The wisdom-evaluative corpus (M04-L-VCG-02, 03) produces agency through clarified moral vision: knowing what is genuinely good enables acting accordingly.

---

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness enables agency: Neh 2:18 is the clearest evidence — the gladness of knowing God's good hand is upon the work is what enables the community's collective agentive resolve. The covenantal-promise Gladness (M04-O-VCG-04) particularly enables sustained agency: knowing God's good promises as reliable (Jos 21:45) provides the inner confidence that enables continued faithful action.

---

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness functions as a moral-clarity and confidence-generating characteristic that enables agency: the person and community that accurately assess God's goodness and their own moral situation are those who can act from a stable, informed inner base. Gladness does not generate the impulse to action in the same way Joy does (Joy's energy propels action); rather, Gladness provides the stable cognitive-affective foundation from which reliable action proceeds.

---

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes — this is Gladness's most distinctive faculty engagement across the entire M04 cluster. M04-L-VCG-02 (45 verses) is almost entirely the moral evaluation faculty in operation: the systematic "is this good? is this not-good?" exercise of the Proverbs corpus; the comparative "better than" assessments; the "what is good?" investigations of Ecclesiastes; Mic 6:8's definitional statement of what human goodness is; Job 34:4's communal moral discernment process. Gladness in its evaluative-cognitive face IS the moral evaluation faculty as an inner-being characteristic.

---

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens moral evaluation: the person whose inner life is settled in the recognition of what is genuinely good (M04-L-VCG-01 — divine character goodness as the standard) has the reference point from which all other moral evaluation proceeds. Distorted Gladness bypasses moral evaluation: Isa 41:7's craftsman saying "it is good" over an idol is a short-circuit of genuine moral evaluation.

---

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is the most morally-constituted characteristic in M04. Its evaluative face (M04-L) is functionally identical with the moral evaluation faculty in operation — Gladness and moral evaluation are co-constitutive in this corpus. This is the most analytically distinctive feature of Characteristic 3 within the M04 cluster.

---

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. Eze 36:31 — "your deeds that were not good, and you will loathe yourselves" — the evaluative faculty turned inward in conviction: the recognition that one's own past deeds were not-good produces self-loathing; this is the moral conscience engaging through the Gladness-evaluation faculty. Pro 20:23 — "false scales are not good" — the moral conscience is engaged through the evaluative standard. 1Sa 26:16 — "this thing you have done is not good" — conscience speaks through the not-good verdict.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness's evaluative face deepens conscience: the person who accurately evaluates conduct against the standard of genuine goodness is exercising the same faculty as the conscience. Eze 36:31's self-loathing shows conscience operating through the evaluative-gladness faculty in its most convicting register. Distorted Gladness can bypass conscience: the craftsman who declares his idol-work "good" (Isa 41:7) without genuine moral evaluation has circumvented the conscience.

---

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The moral evaluation faculty (M04-L) and the conscience are closely allied in Gladness's evidence: both involve comparing reality against a standard of genuine goodness, and the correct operation of one requires and reinforces the correct operation of the other. Gladness is the characteristic most closely integrated with conscience in M04.

---

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. Mic 6:8 ("he has told you, O man, what is good… do justice, love kindness, walk humbly") integrates moral awareness (knowing what is good), volition (doing, loving, walking), and action — the full definition of conscientiousness. The Proverbs corpus (M04-L-VCG-02) consistently connects evaluative recognition of what is good with the call to live accordingly: Pro 2:20 ("walk in the way of the good") — the good recognised becomes the good lived. The covenantal-prompt "so that it may be well with you" (Deu 19:13; M04-O-VCG-03) links just action (conscientiousness) with experienced wellbeing (gladness).

---

**T3.10.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness deepens conscientiousness through the Mic 6:8 pattern: knowing what is good (evaluative Gladness) enables and motivates doing what is good (conscientiousness). The "so that it may be well with you" conditional in Deu 19:13 and similar texts shows that conscientiousness in right action produces the gladness-of-wellbeing, confirming the mutual reinforcement of Gladness and conscientiousness.

---

**T3.10.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness and conscientiousness form an integrated moral-inner cycle: accurate recognition of what is good (Gladness) → conscientious living toward the good → experienced wellbeing as Gladness's fruit → reinforcing the recognition that this is genuinely the good life. The Deuteronomic conditional structure ("do this good thing, so that it may be well with you") is the explicit biblical articulation of this cycle.

---

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**
