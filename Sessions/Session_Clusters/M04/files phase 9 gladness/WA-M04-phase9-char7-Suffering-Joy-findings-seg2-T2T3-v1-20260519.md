## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, explicitly. Luk 1:47 — "my spirit rejoices in God my Savior" — the spirit (pneuma) is named as the seat of Mary's agalliao exultation. Luk 10:21 — "he rejoiced in the Holy Spirit" — Jesus' Suffering-Joy (in the context of the disciples' exposure to rejection in mission) is located specifically within the Holy Spirit. These are the clearest spirit-level constitutional locations in the entire M04 cluster. Additionally, the broader agalliao context: 1Th 1:6 (adjacent — "joy of the Holy Spirit" received amid affliction) and Act 13:52 (disciples filled with joy and with the Holy Spirit) confirm the Spirit-level as the primary constitutional home for the most intense Suffering-Joy register.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, in its primary (agalliao) register. Luk 10:21 makes this explicit for Jesus; Luk 1:47 for Mary. The Spirit as the origin and enabler of the exultant quality of Suffering-Joy is confirmed: 1Th 1:6's "joy of the Holy Spirit" received in affliction names the Spirit as the producing agent. The ba.lag fragile register (M04-F) is not Spirit-located in this way — it is the unaided human attempt at cheerfulness, suggesting the spirit-level location is specific to the Spirit-enabled form and absent from the fragile-attempt form.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Spirit-level location reveals that Suffering-Joy in its fullest form is not a human psychological achievement but a divinely-produced inner state. This explains the paradox: the person who genuinely exults amid suffering is not stronger-willed or more emotionally resilient than others but is inhabited by the Spirit at the deepest constitutional level. Spirit-level location also explains the characteristic's transcendence of circumstances: what is produced at the spirit level is not accessible to the same distortions that affect heart-level and mind-level inner states, which is why it can coexist with and even arise through suffering that would overwhelm ordinary affective joy.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Spirit-level location is the most clearly evidenced constitutional location for this characteristic; not silent.

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, in one instance. Act 2:26 — "my heart was glad, and my tongue rejoiced" (Peter quoting Psalm 16:9 in the resurrection-promise context — agalliao on the tongue) — the heart is named as the seat of gladness that precedes the tongue's rejoicing. Job 9:27 (M04-F) — "put off my sad face" implicitly involves the heart as the source of the sorrowful countenance, but the term itself (ba.lag) doesn't explicitly name the heart. The heart is evidenced but not the dominant constitutional language — the spirit level is primary for this characteristic.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Act 2:26's heart-gladness followed by tongue-rejoicing shows the characteristic moving from the inner constitutional centre (heart) outward to expressive declaration — consistent with the broader inner-to-outer pattern across M04. The heart as the seat of the initial gladness (even in the Peter-quoting-David context) confirms that Suffering-Joy is not purely spirit-level but has a heart-level component in its expressed form.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Heart-location is evidenced at Act 2:26; not fully silent.

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Partially, through the cognitive-reframing acts: Jas 1:2 ("count it all joy") is a cognitive act — the counting/considering is a mental operation; Heb 12:2 ("for the joy that was set before him endured the cross") involves Christ's cognitive orientation toward the eschatological joy as the mental ground for endurance. These are not explicit mind-location statements but show cognitive involvement in sustaining Suffering-Joy.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The mind's role in Suffering-Joy is specifically in the **eschatological reorientation** that sustains it: by cognitively holding the future joy (reward in heaven, glory to be revealed) as more real than present suffering, the mind enables the spirit's joy to hold. This is the specific cognitive contribution: not reasoning one's way into joy but orienting one's cognitive frame around eschatological truth that grounds the Spirit's work.

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Mind-involvement is evidenced through cognitive reframing (Jas 1:2; Heb 12:2) but not as explicit constitutional seat-naming.

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes: **tongue** — Act 2:26 ("my tongue rejoiced") — the tongue as the expressive organ of inner exultation; **flesh/body** — Act 2:26 ("my flesh also will dwell in hope") — the body as the recipient of the hope that accompanies the rejoicing; **face** — Job 9:27 ("put off my sad face") — the face as the somatic expression of the inner struggle with cheerfulness; Psa 39:13 ("that I may smile again") — the face/countenance as the visible indicator of the barely-attainable brightening.

**T2.5.2 — What does tongue/face-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The tongue-rejoicing (Act 2:26) shows Suffering-Joy moving from the deepest inner level (spirit/heart) outward to expressive declaration — consistent with agalliao's leaping-expressive root meaning. The face's smile (Psa 39:13; implied in Job 9:27) as the desired visible sign of ba.lag shows the characteristic's outward-expressive quality even in its fragile form: the inner brightening seeks a visible form even when barely attainable.

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes: (1) **Tongue** — Act 2:26 ("my tongue rejoiced" — agalliao); (2) **Flesh** — Act 2:26 ("my flesh also will dwell in hope"); (3) **Face/countenance** — Job 9:27 ("put off my sad face"); Psa 39:13 ("that I may smile again").

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — (1) The tongue-link (Act 2:26) is **expressive** — the inner exultation of Suffering-Joy moves outward through the body's speech organ into declaration; (2) The flesh/hope-link (Act 2:26) is **indicative** — the body's hoped-for security is the outward evidence that the inner Suffering-Joy has a bodily dimension and is not merely abstract; (3) The face-smile-link (Job 9:27; Psa 39:13) is **emphatic** — the barely-sought smile is both the goal of ba.lag and the visible evidence of its presence; seeking to smile is seeking the somatically-visible form of inner cheerfulness.

---

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Body-part links are evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — **Inner to outer**: spirit/heart exults → tongue rejoices (Act 2:26); inner gladness → face smiles (Psa 39:13). The direction is consistently from the deepest constitutional level (spirit) outward through heart, tongue, and face. There is no evidence in this corpus of bodily state initiating the inner Suffering-Joy.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The inner-to-outer direction confirms that Suffering-Joy cannot be manufactured by external performance — the person who forces a smile (attempting ba.lag) without the inner Spirit-work merely performs what is not present. The genuine form requires the inner-being production (Spirit-level) before the tongue and face can genuinely express it. This is the key distinction between genuine Suffering-Joy (agalliao — inner Spirit-produced, expressed outwardly) and mere cheerfulness-performance (attempted ba.lag that may fail).

---

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Link is evidenced; inner-to-outer direction confirmed.

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Two clear origin points: (1) **From the Holy Spirit** — Luk 10:21 ("rejoiced in the Holy Spirit"); 1Th 1:6 (adjacent — "joy of the Holy Spirit") — the Spirit is the direct producing agent of agalliao's intensity; (2) **From faith in the unseen Christ** — 1Pe 1:8 ("though you do not now see him, you believe in him and rejoice") — faith as the inner volitional-cognitive act that positions the person to receive Spirit-joy; (3) **From eschatological orientation** — the "joy set before him" (Heb 12:2) and "reward in heaven" (Mat 5:12) as the cognitive-eschatological grounds that produce and sustain the inner orientation; (4) **From deliberate volitional reframing** — Jas 1:2 ("count it all joy") — the will's act of cognitive reframing as origin.

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Multiple — Spirit, faith, eschatological orientation, and volitional reframing each play a distinct originating role. The Spirit is primary; the others are enabling conditions or means through which the Spirit's origin reaches the person.

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes: direct divine encounter contexts (Luk 1:47; Luk 10:21) → Spirit origin; persecution contexts (Mat 5:12; 1Pe 4:13) → eschatological-orientation and volitional-command origin; coming-to-faith contexts (Act 16:34; Joh 8:56) → faith-reception origin; extreme-suffering contexts (M04-F — Job 9:27; Psa 39:13) → attempted-will origin without Spirit-enabling, producing the fragile ba.lag form.

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes. Spirit → heart → tongue (Act 2:26 — the clearest sequence: heart glad, tongue rejoiced, flesh in hope); Spirit → voice → body (Luk 10:21 — Jesus' spirit-rejoicing immediately becomes vocal prayer; the body of disciples gathered around him participating). Luk 1:47's "my spirit rejoices" also moves outward in the Magnificat's declaration.

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Spirit-level → heart-level → tongue/voice → communal participation. The pattern is the deepest-to-shallowest in constitutional terms, moving outward from the spirit to the body and into the community. This is also the fastest-moving of the M04 characteristics' constitutional movements — agalliao's "leaping" root meaning is reflected in the rapid outward movement from deep inner to immediate outer expression.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Constitutional movement is evidenced; not silent.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, but distinctively. Suffering-Joy is characterised by perception of what is not (yet) visible: 1Pe 1:8 ("though you do not now see him, you believe in him") — the rejoicing arises despite the absence of visible evidence; Joh 8:56 (Abraham "saw" Christ's day through faith-vision, not physical sight) — inner spiritual perception of future reality. Luk 1:44 (adjacent — "the baby in my womb leaped for joy" at the sound of Mary's greeting) — the most physicality-affirming perception-instance: hearing as the trigger of the most bodily form of rejoicing. M04-F: Psa 39:13 ("that I may smile again before I depart") — the perceptive capacity is directed toward the self and its diminishing life; ba.lag is triggered by mortality-perception.

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy deepens spiritual perception: 1Pe 1:8 (belief in the unseen Christ producing inexpressible joy) shows that Spirit-enabled Suffering-Joy opens the person to perceive realities that ordinary sensory perception cannot access. It does not bypass natural-world perception (the suffering is genuinely perceived and felt) but adds a second perceptive layer — the Spirit-enabled perception of eschatological reality that recontextualises the natural-world suffering.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy is the characteristic most directly associated with faith-perception — the inner-being capacity to perceive what is not yet visible. Its connection with the Spirit (who enables perception of divine realities) and its consistent grounding in eschatological realities (heaven's reward, Christ's glory) confirm it as the characteristic that most fully engages the perceptive faculty in its spiritual-discernment mode rather than its sensory mode.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, through specific cognitive acts: Jas 1:2 ("count it all joy") — counting/reckoning is an arithmetic-like cognitive act; Heb 12:2 ("for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame") — Christ's cognitive holding of the eschatological joy as more real than the present shame is a sustained cognitive orientation; Mat 5:12 ("for your reward is great in heaven") gives the cognitive ground for commanded rejoicing — a propositional truth that orients the cognitive frame.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy enables cognition by clarifying value hierarchy: the person who genuinely rejoices amid suffering has cognitively apprehended that eschatological reality is more determinative than present circumstance. This reordering of cognitive priority — the eternal over the temporal — is itself a form of cognitive clarity that Suffering-Joy both requires and reinforces.

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy's cognitive engagement is specifically eschatological — its cognitive dimension is the holding of future-reality as present-real. This distinguishes it from Gladness (whose cognitive primary is moral evaluation) and from Joy (whose cognitive ground is comprehension of God's acts). Suffering-Joy's distinctive cognitive mode is eschatological reframing: the present suffering is real but is contextualised by a greater real — the glory to come.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Partially. Joh 8:56 (Abraham's anticipatory joy at Christ's day) involves a prophetic forward-memory — the faith-memory of a promised future. Act 2:26 (Peter quoting Psalm 16 — "my heart was glad, and my tongue rejoiced") is a memory-act — the NT church's appropriation of David's hope as the basis for present confidence. The memory of Christ's suffering and resurrection (1Pe 4:13 — "share Christ's sufferings") grounds present Suffering-Joy in the remembered pattern of Christ's own suffering-to-glory.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy deepens memory in its redemptive-historical form: the person who rejoices in their sharing of Christ's sufferings (1Pe 4:13) is actively engaging the memory of Christ's own passion as the ground and pattern for their present experience. This is not ordinary biographical memory but the theological memory of salvation-history that grounds present suffering-exultation.

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Memory in Suffering-Joy operates primarily as **theological-historical memory** — the memory of Christ's suffering and resurrection that provides the template and ground for present Suffering-Joy. This distinguishes it from Gladness's evaluative-memory (which grounds present assessment in accurate recall of God's character and acts) — Suffering-Joy's memory is specifically christological and eschatological.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes — Suffering-Joy is primarily affective in its intense form: 1Pe 1:8 ("inexpressible and filled with glory") — the affective quality is named first and most prominently; Act 16:34 (the jailer "rejoiced along with his entire household") — an intense affective event in a crisis context; Luk 1:47 (Mary's spirit exults — an intense affective inner event in a vulnerable context); Luk 10:21 (Jesus' rejoicing in the Holy Spirit — an affective event in the Spirit). M04-F (ba.lag) is also primarily affective — the desired brightening of the inner person's felt condition.

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy deepens affect by revealing that the inner being's affective capacity is not limited by circumstances — the Spirit can produce genuine joy even when the natural affective response would be grief or fear. 1Pe 1:6 explicitly holds both: "grieved by various trials" (natural affect) alongside "in this you rejoice" (Spirit-produced affect). The characteristic does not bypass natural grief but adds a Spirit-produced layer of affect that coexists with it.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy reveals that the affective faculty has a Spirit-accessible depth below its naturally-triggered surface. The suffering genuinely affects the natural affective registers (grief, fear, heartsickness — M04-F); but the Spirit can produce genuine exultation at a level below or beyond those surface registers. This dual-layer quality of affect — natural grief and Spirit-joy coexisting — is the characteristic's most distinctive affective feature.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, in specific forms. Luk 1:47's Mary's exultation immediately moves into the Magnificat — a creative poetic-prophetic work generated from the agalliao state. Act 2:26 (Peter's appropriation of Psalm 16 in a Spirit-exultation context) shows the creative-interpretive act of applying past creative work (David's Psalm) to a new Spirit-context. M04-F's ba.lag-seeking also has a creative dimension: Job 9:27's deliberate attempt at cheerfulness ("I will put off my sad face, and be of good cheer") is a self-directed creative act of inner-state engineering.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy generates a specific form of creativity: the Spirit-produced exultation in adversity naturally seeks expression through prophetic poetry (the Magnificat; the tradition of lament-moving-into-praise in the Psalms). The creative act is both the expression of the inner state and the means of its consolidation.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy generates prophetic-creative expression as a natural overflow — the exultation that transcends circumstances presses toward poetry and praise. This is consistent with the agalliao root's leaping-outward quality: what arises in the deepest inner being seeks the most expressive outer form.

---

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, significantly. Mat 5:12 and Luk 6:23 ("rejoice in that day and leap for joy") — direct commands addressed to the will; Jas 1:2 ("count it all joy") — the most explicit volitional-cognitive act in the corpus; 1Pe 4:13 ("rejoice insofar as you share Christ's sufferings") — the command presupposes volitional engagement; Job 9:27 ("I will forget my complaint, I will put off my sad face, and be of good cheer") — Job considers the volitional act of redirecting his inner state (though he then realises he cannot sustain it — M04-F).

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy both requires and strengthens volition: the commands require the will to engage; the Spirit's enabling empowers the will to sustain what it cannot produce alone (Job's failed volitional attempt at ba.lag vs. the Spirit-enabled agalliao). Act 5:41 (apostles rejoicing → continuing to teach) shows Suffering-Joy enabling sustained volitional ministry despite ongoing opposition.

---

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy is the characteristic most explicitly requiring volitional engagement in M04: the commands are the most insistent (Jas 1:2's "count it ALL joy"; Mat 5:12's doubled command "rejoice AND be glad"). This volitional insistence confirms that the natural response to suffering is not joy and the will must be actively engaged by command. The Spirit enables what the will is commanded to do.

---

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes. Act 5:41 (apostles rejoicing that they were counted worthy to suffer for Jesus' name → continuing to teach — Suffering-Joy producing continued agentive mission); Act 16:34 (the jailer coming to faith in crisis → communal agentive response of bringing Paul and Silas into the house, setting food before them, entire household rejoicing — agency around the crisis); Luk 10:21 (Jesus' Spirit-rejoicing is immediately followed by the declaration to the Father and instruction to the disciples — the rejoicing generates continued agentive proclamation).

---

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy enables agency by detaching the will from circumstantial-dependent motivation: the apostle who rejoices in suffering is not waiting for circumstances to improve before acting; the Spirit-joy frees the will to act in the present regardless of external conditions. This is the most agentively-liberating characteristic in M04: it produces the capacity to act when circumstances most strongly suggest stopping.

---

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy is agentively generative precisely because it removes the circumstantial dependency of motivation. Where Joy (Characteristic 2) produces agency through the energy of gladness in what is good, Suffering-Joy produces agency through the freedom from requiring the presence of the good before acting. This is the characteristic's most distinctive contribution to the inner being's agency.

---

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Partially. 1Cor 13:6 (love "does not rejoice at wrongdoing, but rejoices with the truth" — adjacent to M04-D but the "rejoices with the truth" in the sunchairo corpus is a moral-evaluative rejoicing about what is genuinely right). Jas 1:2's "trials of various kinds" presupposes the moral evaluation that trials are genuinely adverse and the counter-intuitive reframing that they are also genuinely beneficial (v.3–4). The moral evaluation faculty is engaged but not primary — the characteristic's defining feature is not its moral evaluation of suffering but its transcendence of circumstances through Spirit-enabled joy.

---

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy does not bypass moral evaluation of suffering — 1Pe 1:6 acknowledges the grief as genuine and the trials as actually grievous; Jas 1:2 does not say trials are good (evaluating them as the good) but says to count them AS joy because of what they produce. The moral faculty is not bypassed but the characteristic operates at a different level — not evaluation of the suffering's goodness but transcendence of the evaluation through Spirit-grounding.

---

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy operates above and alongside moral evaluation rather than through it. The suffering is accurately evaluated as suffering; the characteristic does not require misrepresenting the suffering as actually good (which would be the evaluative route). Instead, the Spirit produces a genuine joy that coexists with accurate recognition of suffering's real cost.

---

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-7]** S — The M04-E-VCG-02 and M04-F corpus does not show Suffering-Joy engaging the conscience (the acute witness of sin, guilt, and conviction) directly. Act 16:34 (the jailer came to faith after apparent conviction — the surrounding narrative implies prior conscience-work, but the verse itself focuses on the rejoicing that follows). The characteristic's relationship with conscience is indirect and not evidenced in the primary corpus.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-7]** S — Not directly evidenced. Silent on conscience engagement in this corpus.

---

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** S — The silence suggests Suffering-Joy operates after conscience's work rather than engaging it — consistent with the broader M04 pattern (Joy follows conscience's convicting work; Suffering-Joy follows faith-reception). The characteristic presupposes the conscience has done its work of conviction and the person has come to faith; it is what follows, not what accompanies, the conscience's work.

---

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes. Act 5:41 (apostles rejoicing → continuing to teach daily in the temple and from house to house — the rejoicing in suffering produces and sustains conscientious mission-faithfulness); 1Pe 4:13's "insofar as you share Christ's sufferings" implies the conscientious engagement with Christ's mission that occasions the sharing of his suffering; Jas 1:2–4 (count trials as joy → steadfastness → complete works) shows Suffering-Joy producing the integrated-responsive-action-pattern that is conscientiousness.

---

**T3.10.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy deepens and enables conscientiousness specifically under opposition: the apostles who could rejoice in being counted worthy to suffer (Act 5:41) are the ones who continued teaching despite the opposition. Conscientiousness without Suffering-Joy would collapse under sustained persecution; Suffering-Joy provides the inner resource that sustains conscientious action when circumstances make it costly.

---

**T3.10.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy is the characteristic that enables conscientiousness to hold under pressure. It is therefore the critical inner-being resource for mission-faithfulness under persecution — the specific inner capacity that prevents the gap between moral awareness (I should continue) and action (I do continue) from being opened by suffering's pressure.

---

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes. Act 16:34 — the jailer's agalliao encompasses his entire household; the coming-to-faith joy is constitutively communal and relational; Luk 15:6, 9 (adjacent — sunchairo in M04-D, but the pattern is clear) — Suffering-Joy moves into communal celebration; Act 5:41 (apostles — a communal sharing of the suffering-joy experience); 1Pe 4:13 (the "we" implied in sharing Christ's sufferings is communal participation).

---

**T3.11.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy deepens relational capacity by creating a community formed around shared participation in Christ's suffering and anticipated glory (1Pe 4:13; Act 5:41). The communal experience of Suffering-Joy — rejoicing together despite or through shared adversity — creates bonds of peculiar depth and solidarity.

---

**T3.11.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Suffering-Joy is constitutively communal in its primary NT register: the agalliao passages consistently show it shared, celebrated, and expressed in community. The characteristic deepens relational bonds precisely because it is a sharing of the paradox — the community that has genuinely rejoiced together through suffering has a bond that shared prosperity cannot produce.
