**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The M04-L-VCG-01 corpus (32 verses) is the most concentrated evidence for Gladness as a divine attribute. God's evaluative-goodness declarations across Gen 1:4–31 (seven "God saw that it was good / very good" appraisals) reveal God as a being whose inner life includes the capacity to perceive, assess, and register gladness/satisfaction in what is genuinely good. Beyond creation: God is declared good in character ("good and upright" — Psa 25:8; "good and forgiving" — Psa 86:5; "The Lord is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made" — Psa 145:9; "The Lord is good, a stronghold in the day of trouble" — Nah 1:7). The ki-tov declarations throughout the Psalms (100:5; 106:1; 107:1; 118:1, 29; 135:3; 136:1) establish God's goodness as an intrinsic character attribute, not a situational response. Gladness is the inner state of one who recognises and inhabits what is genuinely good — and God's character is the archetype of that goodness. In M04-O: God's covenantal goodness produces Gladness in the people (Neh 2:18 — God's good hand upon Nehemiah becomes the testimony that stirs communal resolve; Ezr 6:22 — "the Lord had made them joyful" at the Feast of Unleavened Bread). God is the origin and exemplar of the Gladness the characteristic describes.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, explicitly and foundationally. Gen 2:18 — "it is not good that the man should be alone" — God evaluates and declares a deficiency; this is the evaluative-gladness faculty operating in its negative register: the good that is absent is recognised and named. The Gen 1 series (VCG-01) is the positive register: God perceives and declares goodness in what he has made. Psa 119:68 — "you are good and do good" — explicitly joins divine being-good (ontological Gladness-quality) and doing-good (action arising from it), a model the programme observation OBS-026 identifies as the being/doing pattern. Neh 9:20 — "you gave your good Spirit to instruct them" — the Spirit's quality of goodness is an attribute of the divine Gladness-character extended outward. The attribution reveals: Gladness in its evaluative-cognitive form (the recognition and declaration of what is truly good) is first God's own inner act before it is the human person's. The human capacity for Gladness-as-evaluative-recognition is an image-bearing participation in God's own goodness-perceiving inner life.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Scripture is not silent on divine goodness — it is extensively evidenced. The more analytically significant observation is the asymmetry within the corpus: M04-L-VCG-01 (divine character goodness) has 32 verses, making God the subject of goodness more prominently than any sub-group's human subject. This dominance reveals that Gladness-as-evaluative-recognition of the good is not primarily a human characteristic that God also happens to possess; it is a divine attribute that humans reflect. The human evaluative-gladness of M04-L-VCG-02 (moral appraisal of persons and actions) is the image-bearing participation in what God first exercises. No silence; instead, a positive finding about origination.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The evidence reveals three created purposes: (1) **Moral orientation** — M04-L-VCG-02 shows Gladness-as-evaluation guiding decisions, conduct, and community life (Jethro's assessment of Moses, Exo 18:17; Naomi's assessment of Ruth's best course, Rut 2:22; the repeated "not good" verdicts on injustice in Proverbs). The evaluative faculty equips the person to navigate moral reality accurately; (2) **Relational assessment** — M04-L-VCG-02 includes comparative evaluations of relational worth (Rut 4:15 — Ruth's love surpassing seven sons; 2Sa 18:3 — David's worth ten thousand soldiers) — the faculty serves honest relational perception; (3) **Receptive recognition of circumstantial goodness** — M04-O shows Gladness as the soul's warm inner response to genuinely good news, beneficial states, and received blessedness (Pro 12:25 — good word counteracting anxiety; Pro 15:30 — good news refreshing bones; Neh 2:18 — God's good hand motivating communal action). The characteristic equips the person to recognise and receive the good when it is genuinely present.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Strongly original design. The Gen 1 series (VCG-01) establishes the evaluative-gladness faculty as operating at creation itself — before the fall, God's own perception of the good in his work is the archetype. Gen 2:18's "not good" — the only negative in the creation narrative — shows the evaluative faculty precisely identifying a genuine lack (solitude) in an otherwise good creation. The M04-L-VCG-02 corpus in Proverbs, however, shows the evaluative faculty operating extensively in the fallen condition (assessing injustice, corruption, moral deficiency) — these uses are responses to the fallen world's moral complexity. M04-O's circumstantial Gladness similarly spans both: the soul's warm response to good news and blessing is a created-design capacity; its deployment in scenarios of exile, judgment, and adversity (Lam 3:26–27; Jon 4:3, 8; Hos 2:7) reflects operation in the fallen condition.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Partially. M04-O-VCG-04's covenantal-promise goodness register (Jer 29:10; 33:14; Jos 21:45; 23:16) shows God's good promises as the ground of present endurance — the soul's gladness in the present is anchored in future-fulfillment confidence. The makarismos verses (Rom 4:6, 9; Gal 4:15 — in M04-O-VCG-04) present blessedness as a declarative eschatological state. Lam 3:26 ("it is good to wait quietly for the salvation of the Lord") frames the present inner disposition of gladness-in-waiting as oriented toward a salvation not yet arrived. However, Gladness lacks the concentrated eschatological trajectory of Joy (Characteristic 2) — it is more characteristically present-responsive than future-oriented.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness expresses the divine image as the human person's capacity for evaluative recognition of genuine goodness — the inner faculty that can perceive, name, and be settled in what is truly good, mirroring God's own perception of goodness in creation. Mic 6:8 — "He has told you, O man, what is good; and what does the Lord require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?" — is the most explicit statement: God has declared what human goodness is, and the human person's capacity to recognise it (evaluative Gladness) and inhabit it (moral Gladness) is the image-bearing response. Psa 119:68's "you are good and do good; teach me your statutes" shows the divine being-good as the pattern the human person is being formed toward. The image is expressed as a correspondence in evaluative-moral faculty: God sees and declares good; the human person sees and declares good (or not-good) in imitation of that divine evaluative act.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Genuinely shared at the level of the evaluative faculty. The same term (tov) is used for God's evaluative declarations ("God saw that it was good" — Gen 1:4–31) and the human person's evaluative declarations ("it is not good that the man should be alone" — Gen 2:18; "the thing that you are doing is not good" — Exo 18:17; "Ruth's love is more to you than seven sons" — Rut 4:15). The human evaluative faculty is a real participation in the divine evaluative capacity, not merely a pale analogy. The programme observation OBS-026 (Psa 119:68) makes this explicit: God's being-good generates doing-good; the human person who evaluates correctly is participating in the same moral-recognition structure. In M04-O, God's covenantal goodness (good hand, good promises) is what produces the human person's gladness — confirming that the human Gladness-response corresponds to a genuine divine goodness-reality.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Presence reveals the image functioning: the person who accurately evaluates what is genuinely good (Mic 6:8's trio of justice, loving kindness, walking humbly; Naomi's wisdom in evaluating Ruth's best course; the Psalms' steady recognition of God's goodness as the ground of thanksgiving) shows the image in its designed operation. Distortion reveals the image degraded: the people who judged return to Egypt as "better than dying in the wilderness" (Num 14:3 — M04-O-VCG-02) are evaluating from fear rather than faith; the craftsmen who say "it is good" of their idol's soldering (Isa 41:7 — M04-L-VCG-02) show the evaluative faculty applied to a self-congratulating work that cannot respond or act; Jeremiah 44:17's people who attributed former wellbeing to idol-worship show the evaluative-gladness faculty seriously distorted by false memory. Absence reveals the image silenced: Eze 36:31 ("your deeds that were not good, and you will loathe yourselves") shows the evaluative faculty turned against the self in moral revulsion — the image partially restored through recognition of past failure, but accompanied by shame.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in specific registers: (1) **The Gen 1 creation-goodness as type** — God's "very good" declaration over creation (Gen 1:31) is the OT type pointing forward to the new creation (Isa 65:18 — God creates Jerusalem to be "a joy" and her people "a gladness" — gi.lah); the created goodness of the first creation is the type of the consummated goodness of the new; (2) **Psa 45:7's oil of gladness** (sa.s.von) applied christologically in Heb 1:9 — the anointed king's gladness beyond his companions is the OT type for Christ's eschatological anointing; (3) **The makarismos typology** (Rom 4:6, 9 — Abraham's blessedness as the type of the justified person's blessedness declared apart from works) — the OT blessedness pattern (in M04-O-VCG-04) is the type for NT declarative righteousness; (4) **God's good promises** (Jos 21:45; Jer 29:10; 33:14) as types of the final fulfillment in Christ — every good promise not one of which has failed finds its ultimate Yes in Christ (2Cor 1:20).

---

**T0.4.2 — What is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Both directions: (1) **Human-to-eschatological**: Gen 1:31's "very good" creation points forward to the new creation's perfected goodness; the OT blessing declarations (makarismos) point forward to NT declarative righteousness; (2) **Divine-to-human pattern**: Psa 119:68 ("you are good and do good") establishes the divine being-good/doing-good as the pattern the human person is formed toward; God's evaluative "good" in creation establishes the standard by which all creaturely evaluations operate. The bidirectional typology confirms Gladness as a characteristic that participates in the divine evaluative life and moves toward eschatological consummation.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Typological use is evidenced; not silent.

## T1 — Definition and Structural Analysis

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The characteristic is called Gladness. The name signals: (1) **Settled warmth** — gladness is a positively-coloured inner condition rather than an active surge (Exultation) or engaged participation (Joy); (2) **Cognitive-evaluative dimension** — gladness includes the soul's recognition that something is good, which the name captures by its diffuse, non-directed quality (one is glad rather than exulting at something); (3) **Inner condition rather than inner act** — the name points to a state rather than an event; the glad person inhabits gladness rather than performing it. The two sub-groups (M04-L and M04-O) fill the name: M04-L contributes the evaluative-cognitive face (the assessment that things are good); M04-O contributes the circumstantial-experiential face (the warm inner response to what is recognised as good). Together they name the settled, bright inner condition of a person or community that has accurately assessed and is responding to genuine goodness.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Primary terms: (1) **tov** (H2896A) — the dominant term across both sub-groups (83 M04-L instances + 50 M04-O instances = 133 total). Tov as noun/adjective/verbal-function names the quality of genuine goodness — pleasant, beneficial, agreeable, right, fitting. At the definitional level it reveals Gladness as rooted in objective goodness rather than subjective preference; (2) **makarismos** (G3108) — blessedness; 3 NT instances in M04-O-VCG-04 (Rom 4:6, 9; Gal 4:15). Makarismos names the inner state of one who has received divine favour; (3) **sa.s.von** (H8342) — appears in the M04-E-VCG-01 corpus which overlaps with Characteristic 3 at the VCG-01 level (sa.s.von as gladness-of-restoration). At definitional level: tov's breadth (moral, relational, circumstantial, divine-character goodness) establishes Gladness as the widest-scope positive inner-being characteristic in M04 — broader than Joy (which has its own primary vocabulary), though the two share the experience.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Moderate directional, strong cognitive, moderate relational implications: (1) **Cognitive-directional**: tov consistently points toward an object that is good — the glad person is glad about or in something; M04-L's evaluative appraisals ("better than," "not good," "good to those who wait") always name an object or situation; M04-O's circumstantial responses always have an occasion; (2) **Relational**: the "good man" (Pro 12:2; 13:22; 14:14) and the relational comparatives (Rut 4:15; 2Sa 18:3) show Gladness as relationally-embedded — it arises in and from relationships; (3) **Constitutional**: unlike Exultation and Joy, Gladness's primary constitutional language is not "heart" first — the tov corpus is more frequently directed at objects, circumstances, and character than at the heart's location. The heart is named in specific instances (Ecc 5:20; Jer 15:16 — but these are M04-E sa.s.von rather than M04-L/O tov), revealing Gladness as somewhat more cognitively-distributed than heart-centred.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness operates as a **condition and a quality** more than as an act: (1) **Condition** — "it is well with the man who deals generously" (Psa 112:5); "it shall be well with you" (Deu 19:13); Saul "was refreshed and was well" (1Sa 16:23) — these name an inner state of being-well, not an act of doing; (2) **Quality** — "a good man" (Pro 12:2; 13:22) names goodness as a stable character quality; "the Lord is good" (Psa 100:5) names divine goodness as an intrinsic attribute; (3) **Evaluative act** — M04-L-VCG-02's "better than" comparatives (Rut 4:15; 2Sa 18:3; Pro 8:11; 16:16) show Gladness as an act of judgment; but even here the act is a cognitive recognition rather than an affective surge. Gladness is primarily a condition/quality that the evaluative faculty perceives and the inner person inhabits.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Complex — two clearly distinct constituent elements map to the two sub-groups: (1) **The evaluative-cognitive face** (M04-L) — the inner faculty of moral and relational appraisal that perceives, names, and settles in what is genuinely good; this includes the divine evaluative act (VCG-01), human moral appraisal (VCG-02), and wisdom's surpassing-worth recognition (VCG-03); (2) **The experiential-circumstantial face** (M04-O) — the warm inner response to beneficial circumstance, good news, covenantal promise, and received wellbeing. The two faces are linked: the evaluative act (M04-L) produces the recognition that enables the experiential warmth (M04-O). A person who correctly evaluates that God's good promises are reliable will inhabit the warm inner gladness that such confidence produces. The integration_note carry-forward (M04-L as cognitive face, M04-O as experiential face) is confirmed as the structural key to Gladness.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is the inner state of settled, warm, positive brightness arising from accurate evaluation and recognition of what is genuinely good — in God's character, in circumstances, in persons, and in received blessedness. It is constituted by two integrated faces: an evaluative-cognitive face (the soul's assessment that something is good, better, or surpassing, grounded in moral and relational discernment) and an experiential-circumstantial face (the warm inner response to beneficial news, covenantal goodness, and circumstances assessed as genuinely good). It is less active than Joy (which rejoices in something) and more diffuse: it pervades the inner condition as a quality of being rather than an act of doing. Its primary constitutional home is distributed across cognitive and affective faculties without strong dominance of either.

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Multiple structural opposites evidenced: (1) **Not-good / moral deficiency** — the most frequent, through tov's negation: "it is not good" (Exo 18:17; 1Sa 2:24; 2Sa 17:7; Neh 5:9; Pro 17:26; 18:5; 20:23; 24:23; 28:21; Eze 18:18; 20:25; Eze 36:31; Isa 65:2) — the structural opposite of moral gladness is moral deficiency or wrong; (2) **Sorrow, grief** — in M04-O's circumstantial register, the opposite of gladness as wellbeing is sorrow: Lam 3:26's "wait quietly" implies prior anguish; Jon 4:3, 8's despairing "better to die" is the extreme inversion of circumstantial gladness; Hos 2:7's nostalgic "better then than now" shows the absence of present gladness; (3) **Mourning** — M04-E-VCG-01's sa.s.von corpus which overlaps Gladness shows gladness as what replaces mourning: Jer 31:13 ("I will turn their mourning into joy"); (4) **Self-loathing** — Eze 36:31 shows the not-good evaluation turned inward producing self-loathing, the negative terminus of the evaluative-gladness faculty when applied to one's own past conduct.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness explicitly excludes: (1) **False evaluation** — Num 14:3 (preferring Egypt as better is fear-distorted evaluation, not true gladness); Jer 44:17 (misattributing former wellbeing to idol-worship); Isa 41:7 (the craftsman's self-congratulatory "it is good" over an idol) — Gladness resists evaluation that is not grounded in reality; (2) **Self-righteous entitlement** — Deu 9:6 ("not because of your righteousness") directly corrects the inner assumption that the good land was merited; (3) **Premature or shallow evaluation** — Hezekiah's self-serving reception of Isaiah's word as "good" (Isa 39:8) because it means peace in his own lifetime — Gladness resists the cognitive shortcut of evaluating as good only what benefits the self; (4) **Despair-assessment** — Jon 4:3, 8 (Jonah's "better to die") is the extreme distortion of the circumstantial-gladness faculty. Gladness resists any inner assessment that is not grounded in genuine moral and covenantal reality.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Gladness is not: (1) **Joy** (Characteristic 2) — Joy is active, participatory, engaged; Gladness is settled, warm, and diffuse. The "joy and gladness" formulaic pairing (confirmed in Char 2 Phase 9) places them adjacent but distinct. Joy rejoices *in* something; Gladness is the inner condition of one whose assessment is settled in what is good; (2) **Exultation** (Characteristic 1) — Exultation surges upward; Gladness is warm and level; (3) **Delight** (Characteristic 4) — Delight is the soul's treasuring orientation toward a specific object; Gladness is more broadly distributed across circumstances, persons, and evaluations; (4) **Pleasure** (Characteristic 5) — Pleasure is receptive agreeableness through sensory and material channels; Gladness is cognitively-grounded warm inner brightness; (5) **Suffering-Joy** (Characteristic 7) — Suffering-Joy arises paradoxically through suffering; Gladness is characteristically the inner state of one whose circumstances or evaluation confirm that things are genuinely well.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Four modes: (1) **Divine-evaluative** — M04-L-VCG-01 (God's "it is good" appraisals across Gen 1; the ki-tov praise declarations) — Gladness operating at the highest level as the divine inner recognition of genuine goodness; (2) **Moral-comparative appraisal** — M04-L-VCG-02 (the human person weighing conduct, character, and choice against the standard of genuine goodness: "better than," "not good," "a good man") — Gladness as the cognitive-moral faculty; (3) **Wisdom's evaluative recognition** — M04-L-VCG-03 (Pro 8:11, 19; 16:8, 16, 19 — wisdom declared better than gold) — Gladness as the inner recognition of value hierarchies; (4) **Circumstantial warm response** — M04-O (all four VCGs — good news refreshing, wellbeing as flourishing, covenantal promise-gladness, makarismos blessedness) — Gladness as the affective inner warmth produced by receiving genuine goodness.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes: (1) **By direction**: the evaluative mode (M04-L-VCG-02) is primarily outward-directed — it assesses persons, actions, and circumstances; the wellbeing mode (M04-O-VCG-03) is inward-received — "it will be well with you"; (2) **By object**: moral evaluations produce the gladness of moral clarity; covenantal promise-gladness (M04-O-VCG-04) produces the gladness of anchored trust; circumstantial good news produces the gladness of refreshment; (3) **By constitutional emphasis**: the evaluative modes (M04-L) engage the cognitive faculty more prominently; the circumstantial and wellbeing modes (M04-O) engage the affective faculty more prominently; (4) **By register**: the divine-character declarations (M04-L-VCG-01) are doxological; the moral appraisals (M04-L-VCG-02) are practical-ethical; the wisdom-comparative (M04-L-VCG-03) is wisdom-formation.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, in two forms: (1) **Doxological declaration** — the ki-tov praise formulas (Psa 100:5; 106:1; 107:1; 118:1, 29; 135:3; 136:1) are verbal declarations of divine goodness that simultaneously express and reinforce inner gladness; the "give thanks" command consistently paired with the "for he is good" formula shows that gladness-declaration is intrinsic to worship; (2) **Good word/news** — M04-O-VCG-01 (Pro 12:25 — "a good word makes him glad"; Pro 15:23 — "a word in season, how good it is!"; Pro 15:30 — "good news refreshes the bones"; Isa 52:7 — the good-news herald; Zec 1:13 — gracious comforting words) — speech is the medium through which gladness is communicated and received; the apt, timely word is itself an instance of Gladness reaching the inner person through the channel of speech.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The immediate responses vary by mode: (1) **Good news register**: Pro 12:25 — "a good word makes him glad" — the immediate inner shift from anxiety to brightness; Pro 15:30 — good news "refreshes the bones" — physiological-inner penetration; Zec 1:13 — gracious words bring inner reassurance; Gen 40:16 — the baker's immediate inner opening to disclosure when he perceives the interpretation as "favorable"; (2) **Evaluative register**: no strong immediate-response language; the moral appraisals of M04-L-VCG-02 are deliberative rather than immediate; (3) **Wellbeing register**: 1Sa 16:23 — Saul "was refreshed and was well" — the immediate inner calm produced by music when the harmful spirit departed; (4) **Covenantal register**: Neh 2:18 — the people's resolve strengthened when they heard of God's good hand — immediate motivational response to gladness-testimony.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Varies by mode. The good-news register (M04-O-VCG-01) shows the most consistent and immediate inner response: words of genuine goodness produce immediate inner brightening (anxiety → gladness; weariness → refreshment). The evaluative register (M04-L-VCG-02) is more deliberative — its "better than" comparatives presuppose prior assessment rather than immediate reaction. The wellbeing register (M04-O-VCG-03) shows immediate response to relief (1Sa 16:23; 1Sa 11:9 — men of Jabesh glad when they heard of deliverance). The divine-character declarations (M04-L-VCG-01) produce immediate doxological response (the ki-tov formula is simultaneously recognition and declaration).

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-3]** E — M04-L-VCG-02's moral appraisals and M04-L-VCG-03's wisdom-comparative evaluations are largely silent on immediate inner response; they describe the cognitive judgment rather than the felt first reaction to encountering the characteristic. The Eze 36:31 recognition of "not good" deeds produces self-loathing (an immediate negative response), but this is the inverted register.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Sustained Gladness produces: (1) **Moral formation** — M04-L-VCG-02's persistent evaluative engagement with what is good and not-good forms the inner person's moral discernment capacity over time; Pro 2:20 ("walk in the way of the good") — sustained recognition of goodness as the standard shapes the path one walks; (2) **Covenantal stability** — M04-O-VCG-04's "good hand of God" awareness (Ezr 7:9; 8:18; Neh 2:8, 18) produces stable confidence in divine providential care; sustained recognition of God's good faithfulness builds inner resilience; (3) **Doxological orientation** — sustained engagement with the ki-tov declarations produces a person whose default inner posture toward God is thanksgiving and praise; (4) **Generosity** — M04-O-VCG-03: Psa 112:5 ("it is well with the man who deals generously") — sustained wellbeing produces generous disposition.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained gladness establish?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — (1) **Accurate moral perception** — sustained engagement with the evaluative faculty forms moral clarity: the person formed by Gladness's evaluative mode sees clearly what is genuinely good and what is not; (2) **Covenantal trust** — sustained gladness in God's promises (M04-O-VCG-04) establishes a trust-orientation that sustains through adversity; Jos 21:45 ("not one word of all the good promises had failed") as the inner foundation of ongoing trust; (3) **Wisdom** — M04-L-VCG-03's persistent preference for wisdom over gold (Pro 8:11; 16:16) establishes a value-hierarchy in the inner life that orients all choices; (4) **Quiet patience** — Lam 3:26 ("it is good to wait quietly for the salvation of the Lord") — sustained recognition that quiet waiting is the fitting inner posture establishes patience as a disposition.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. The immediate response in the good-news register is refreshment and brightening; the sustained effect is the formation of inner orientations and dispositions (moral clarity, covenantal trust, wisdom, patience). The immediate and sustained effects are consistent in kind (both involve recognition of genuine goodness) but differ in duration and constitutional depth. Immediate gladness is a response; sustained gladness is a formation.

---

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — (1) **Humility** — Deu 9:6 (God correcting Israel's inner sense of merit) reveals that genuine gladness in the good land requires the recognition that it is grace, not merit; pride distorts the evaluative faculty; (2) **Moral alignment** — Psa 73:1 ("God is good to Israel, to those who are pure in heart") — purity of heart is the condition for experiencing God's goodness as directed toward oneself; (3) **Seeking and waiting** — Lam 3:25–26 ("the Lord is good to those who wait for him, to the soul who seeks him… it is good to wait quietly") — patient seeking is the enabling posture for gladness-in-God; (4) **Integrity** — Pro 12:2 ("a good man obtains favor from the Lord") — moral character enables the recognition and reception of divine goodness; (5) **Comprehension** — Gen 40:16 (the baker received Joseph's interpretation as favorable only when he understood it as positive) — accurate understanding is a precondition for gladness's reception.
