## M04-L — Evaluative Goodness: Moral and Relational Appraisal of What is Surpassing (83 verses, NEW sub-group)

**No existing VCGs.** Full design from scratch.

Reading all 83 verses reveals three coherent phenomena within M04-L's characteristic register (tov as the inner-being faculty of moral or relational evaluation):

**VCG-01 — Divine character goodness ("the Lord is good")**: The recurring ki-tov / "the Lord is good" declarations — God's essential character goodness as the declared ground of praise, thanksgiving, and covenantal trust. Includes the Gen 1 creation-appraisal series (God seeing and declaring each work good) and the "Lord is good" Psalms/prophetic corpus.

**VCG-02 — Moral-evaluative appraisal of persons and actions**: verses where goodness or its absence is a moral-qualitative verdict on a person's character or an action's rightness — a "good man," "not good" declared about conduct, moral-comparative better-than involving character qualities, relational goodness as moral excellence.

**VCG-03 — Wisdom's evaluative comparative — character surpassing wealth**: the specific wisdom-literature sub-set where wisdom, understanding, or divine instruction is explicitly declared better than gold, silver, or jewels — a cluster within M04-L distinct from VCG-02's broader moral-character appraisal.

One cross-sub-group assignment: Jer 26:14 (Jeremiah's "do with me as seems good and right to you") is the volitional-deference formula belonging to M04-H-VCG-03.
