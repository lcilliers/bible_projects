## M04-K — Material and Sensory Pleasure: Luxury, Sensory Enjoyment, and Aromatic Sacrifice (67 verses, NEW sub-group)

**No existing VCGs.** Full design from scratch.

Reading all 67 verses yields three primary VCGs within M04-K's characteristic (inner-being-in-bodily-experience: pleasantness registered through senses or material conditions) plus four cross-sub-group assignments where the verse's phenomenon belongs more precisely elsewhere.

**VCG-01 — Sacrificial pleasing aroma to God (righteous worship):** the large ni.cho.ach corpus — offerings that produce a soothing/pleasing/acceptable aroma before God, indicating his inner reception of sacrifice as acceptable. Spans Genesis through Ezekiel (Gen 8:21; Exo; Lev; Num; Eze 20:41).

**VCG-02 — Sensory luxury and pampered ease:** verses where ta.a.nug and a.nog name the inner quality of refined living — delicate comfort, luxurious ease, sensory delight in pleasurable surroundings. Includes Babylon's daintiness (Isa 47:1), siege-curse reversal of refined persons (Deu 28:54, 56), the fool's unfitting luxury (Pro 19:10), Qohelet's sensory accumulation (Ecc 2:8), the beloved's delights (Song 7:6), and the women driven from delightful houses (Mic 2:9).

**VCG-03 — Wisdom better-than comparatives involving material goods:** the tov cluster where wisdom literature ranks inner or relational qualities above material wealth — a little with the fear of the Lord is better than great treasure (Pro 15:16); a dry morsel with quiet is better than a feast with strife (Pro 17:1); God's law is better than thousands of gold and silver (Psa 119:72); wisdom's gain is better than silver (Pro 3:14); integrity in poverty is better than crooked wealth (Pro 28:6); etc. The inner-being phenomenon is the soul's comparative valuation placing character or relational warmth above material abundance.

Four cross-sub-group assignments:
- Pro 7:18 (a.las, seductress inviting carnal delight) → M04-P-VCG-02 (lustful captivation)
- Psa 34:8 (taste and see that the Lord is good) → M04-G-VCG-02 (delight in YHWH)
- Zec 8:19 (fasts become cheerful feasts) → M04-B-VCG-01 (festival and feast worship joy)
- Mar 10:24 (disciples amazed at wealth/kingdom teaching) → M04-I-VCG-01 (wonder at God's marvellous works)

### VCG definitions for M04-K

**VCG: M04-K-VCG-01**  
Description: Sacrificial pleasing aroma — ni.cho.ach (soothing/acceptable aroma) produced by righteous sacrifice directed to God. The large corpus from Gen 8:21 through Eze 20:41 consistently evidences one inner-being phenomenon: the correctly-offered sacrifice produces an effect before God described as soothing, calming, and acceptable — the aroma pleases him, calms his regard, and signals his reception of the worshiper. Gen 8:21 is the archetype: the pleasing aroma moves God's heart to resolve against further judgment. From there the formula governs daily burnt offerings (Num 28:6, 8), festal offerings (Lev 23:13, 18; Num 28:24, 27, 29:2–36), grain and drink offerings (Lev 2:2, 9; Num 15:3–14), ordination rites (Lev 8:21, 28), and — in Eze 20:41 — the eschatological acceptance of restored Israel as itself a pleasing aroma. Lev 26:31 and Lev 2:12 evidence the formula's negation: the soothing aroma is withheld when the covenant relationship is broken or when the specific offering type does not qualify. The inner-being phenomenon is the divine reception of sacrifice — God's relational response to rightly-presented worship — experienced through the sensory-aromatic register of the cult.

**VCG: M04-K-VCG-02**  
Description: Sensory luxury and pampered ease — the inner quality of refined, delicate living as a defining characteristic of the person or entity. The register clusters around ta.a.nug (dainty delight, luxury) and a.nog (tender/refined/pampered): the most tender and refined man and woman under Deuteronomy's siege-curse (Deu 28:54, 56), whose characteristic luxury-orientation makes the curse's reversal all the more devastating; Babylon called "tender and delicate" (a.nog) whose cherished identity of empire is stripped by judgment (Isa 47:1); luxury as unfitting for a fool (ta.a.nug, Pro 19:10); Qohelet's accumulated concubines as "the delight of the sons of man" (ta.a.nug, Ecc 2:8); the beloved's delights as part of her total pleasantness (ta.a.nug, Song 7:6); and women driven from "delightful houses" (ta.a.nug, Mic 2:9). Added to this: the wicked man getting no enjoyment (a.las) from his trading profits — denied sensory-material pleasure as judgment (Job 20:18). The inner-being phenomenon across all is the experience of sensory/material ease and refinement as an inner register — ranging from righteous delight in the beloved to the idolatrous luxury of empire to the denied pleasures of the unjust.

**VCG: M04-K-VCG-03**  
Description: Wisdom better-than comparatives involving material goods — the soul's comparative valuation placing inner character, relational warmth, or moral quality above material abundance. The core of the register is the Proverbs tov-better-than-series: a little with the fear of the Lord over great treasure and trouble (Pro 15:16); a dinner of herbs with love over a fattened ox with hatred (Pro 15:17, the paradigm verse); a dry morsel with quiet over a feast with strife (Pro 17:1); God's law over thousands of gold and silver (Psa 119:72); wisdom's gain over silver (Pro 3:14); integrity in poverty over crooked wealth (Pro 28:6); slow-to-anger over the mighty (Pro 16:32); a good name over riches (Pro 22:1); and steadfast love as the most desired quality over wealth (Pro 19:22). Extended by: the righteous person's little over the wicked's abundance (Psa 37:16); Qohelet's handful of quietness over double-handed toil (Ecc 4:6); the cheerful heart's continual feast regardless of circumstance (Pro 15:15); not eating much honey as excess-caution (Pro 25:27); a wise youth over a unteachable king (Ecc 4:13); and prophetic instances: Josiah's justice over cedar-building (Jer 22:15), the good pasture consumed and destroyed by greedy leaders (Eze 34:18), and pleasant vineyards forfeited by injustice (Amo 5:11). The inner-being phenomenon is the soul's comparative estimation — the judgment that character, love, and wisdom are genuinely more pleasurable and satisfying than material wealth — even when the material is genuinely pleasant.
