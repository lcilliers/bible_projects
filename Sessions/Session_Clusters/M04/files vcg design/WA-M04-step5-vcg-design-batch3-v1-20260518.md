## M04-O — Circumstantial Gladness: Good News, Beneficial States, and Refreshing Circumstance (50 verses, NEW sub-group)

**No existing VCGs.** Full design from scratch.

Reading all 50 verses yields four coherent groupings within M04-O's characteristic of "soul's pleasant response to or recognition of beneficial circumstance":

**VCG-01 — Good news and refreshing word:** verses where a spoken word, message, or report gladdens or refreshes the inner person (Pro 12:25; 15:23; 15:30; 25:25; 2Sa 18:27; Isa 52:7; Zec 1:13; Gen 40:16; Isa 39:8).

**VCG-02 — Better-than comparatives: soul's assessment of circumstance:** the large cluster of tov-as-better-than verses where the inner being judges one state more pleasant or bearable than another (Exo 14:12; 1Sa 1:8; Pro 12:9; 21:9; 21:19; 25:7; 25:24; Ecc 4:3; 4:9; 6:9; 6:12; Lam 4:9; Hos 2:7; Jon 4:3; 4:8 — also several Ecclesiastes verses already in M04-B that are circumstantial-comparative in character).

**VCG-03 — Wellbeing as flourishing and it being well:** verses where "it is well" or "it may go well with you" names an experienced state of covenantal or circumstantial goodness (Deu 8:10; Deu 19:13; 1Sa 16:16; 1Sa 16:23; 1Sa 19:4; 1Sa 24:19; Neh 2:18; Job 13:9; Psa 112:5; Psa 128:2; Pro 3:4; Jer 44:17; Ecc 6:3; Ecc 6:12 [→ also straddles VCG-02]; Lam 3:26; Lam 3:27).

**VCG-04 — Good hand of God / covenantal promise goodness / blessedness:** providential favour evidenced in outcomes (Ezr 7:9; 8:18; Neh 2:8; Num 10:29; Jos 21:45; Jos 23:16; Jer 29:10; Jer 33:14; Deu 8:10 [straddles 3/4]; Rom 4:6; 4:9; Gal 4:15).

On careful verse-by-verse reading, VCGs 3 and 4 are closely related but distinguishable: VCG-03 is the experienced state of wellbeing in the person's circumstances; VCG-04 is the divine-source goodness (promised, providential, declarative) that grounds or enables that wellbeing. Both are valid phenomena within M04-O's register.

### VCG definitions for M04-O

**VCG: M04-O-VCG-01**  
Description: Good news and refreshing word — the inner-being response of gladness or refreshment to a word, message, or report that is received as good. Pro 15:30 is the vivid anchor: "good news refreshes the bones," showing that news whose content is genuinely good penetrates to the body's depths, reviving the whole person. Pro 12:25 names the mechanism: "a good word makes him glad," counteracting inner anxiety. Pro 15:23 extends it to the speaker's satisfaction: "a word in season, how good it is." Pro 25:25 supplies the paradigm simile: "like cold water to a thirsty soul, so is good news from a far country." The register continues through 2Sa 18:27 (the good man with good news), Isa 52:7 (good news of happiness, salvation, peace), Isa 39:8 (Hezekiah's reception of the prophetic word as good), Gen 40:16 (perception of favorable interpretation), and Zec 1:13 (the Lord's gracious and comforting words). The inner-being phenomenon is the soul's pleasant response to speech or news whose content it evaluates as good — spanning from the personal word of encouragement to the salvation-herald's announcement.  
Anchor: Pro 25:25  (vc_id=19665)  
Members: vc=63141, vc=17938, vc=19663, vc=17997, vc=17998, vc=19665, vc=18034, vc=17985, vc=17922

**VCG: M04-O-VCG-02**  
Description: Better-than comparatives — the soul's assessment of circumstances by relative pleasantness. The defining linguistic form is tov-as-comparative: "it is better (tov) than." The register is large and varied in subject: preference for modest sufficiency over empty pretension (Pro 12:9); for inner peace over domestic conflict (Pro 21:9; 21:19; 25:24); for earned honour over presumptuous self-promotion (Pro 25:7); for partnership over isolation (Ecc 4:9); for the attainable over the endlessly craved (Ecc 6:9); for non-existence over witnessing evil (Ecc 4:3); for death over unbearable life (Jon 4:3; 4:8); for the former covenantal relationship over present deprivation (Hos 2:7); for the sword over famine (Lam 4:9); and for Egypt-bondage over wilderness death (Exo 14:12). Even 1Sa 1:8 (Elkanah's question whether he is more to Hannah than ten sons) and Ecc 6:3 (soul not satisfied with life's good things) and Ecc 6:12 (who knows what is good?) belong here by their shared register of circumstantial comparison. The inner-being phenomenon across all instances is the soul's evaluative comparison of states — judging one circumstance more bearable, pleasant, or good than another, even when the comparison is desperate or inverted.

**VCG: M04-O-VCG-03**  
Description: Wellbeing as flourishing — it being "well" as an experienced state of inner and circumstantial goodness. The register covers: physical-inner restoration (1Sa 16:16; 16:23 — Saul refreshed and well when the harmful spirit departs); communal covenantal flourishing (Deu 8:10 — eating the good land's produce with thanksgiving; Deu 19:13 — it being well through just action; Psa 128:2 — it is well as the fruit of diligent work; Psa 112:5 — it is well with the generous man; Pro 3:4 — favour and success before God and man); the outcome of right relational action (1Sa 19:4; 1Sa 24:19 — good produced for Saul through David's loyalty); divine scrutiny as the test of inner genuineness (Job 13:9); the inner disposition of patient endurance valued as good (Lam 3:26; 3:27); misremembered wellbeing (Jer 44:17 — the people's false recall of former prosperity); and Qohelet's examination of unsatisfied souls (Ecc 6:3; Ecc 6:12). The inner-being phenomenon is the state of inner and circumstantial goodness — whether present, recalled, anticipated, or questioned — as something the person or community experiences as genuinely beneficial.

**VCG: M04-O-VCG-04**  
Description: God's good hand and covenantal promise-goodness — the inner confidence and assurance grounded in divine providential favour and in God's good promises as pledged and fulfilled. Two closely related sub-registers: (a) the good hand of God (Ezr 7:9; 8:18; Neh 2:8; 2:18) — God's enabling presence experienced as the source of outcome, generating inner confidence and community testimony; (b) God's good promises as the ground of hope and assurance (Num 10:29; Jos 21:45; Jos 23:16; Jer 29:10; Jer 33:14); (c) makarismos — divinely-bestowed blessedness (Rom 4:6; 4:9 — the inner state of the one to whom God counts righteousness apart from works; Gal 4:15 — the blessedness the Galatians once experienced in the gospel). The inner-being phenomenon across all is the soul's orientation toward a good whose source is divine — whether experienced as providential favour, covenantal promise, or declared blessedness.
