## M04-M — Pleasing as Obedience: Inner Orientation Toward What is Acceptable to God (16 verses, NEW sub-group)

**No existing VCGs.** Full design from scratch.

Reading the 16 verses reveals three coherent groupings:

1. **Sacrificial-obedience pleasing aroma** (ni.cho.ach in righteous worship): the correctly-presented offering produces a soothing, acceptable aroma before God — obedience expressed in cultic form. (Lev 1:17; Num 15:13)

2. **Doing what is good/right in God's sight** (tov + arestos as the moral standard of the acceptable life): a cluster of verses where the inner orientation of the person is directed toward what pleases or is right before God, ranging from Deuteronomic obedience-commands through Hezekiah's integrity appeal through John's Jesus-always-pleasing-the-Father through 1 John's obedience-as-answered-prayer. (Deu 6:18; Deu 12:28; 1Sa 12:23; 1Sa 15:22; Psa 38:20; Psa 125:4; Isa 38:3; Jer 22:16; Joh 8:29; Act 6:2; 1Jo 3:22)

3. **Covenantal promise-fulfilment goodness** (tov as God's beneficial words/things promised and delivered): the inner-being reception of God's good word as fulfilled covenantal promise. (Jos 23:14; Jos 23:15; Isa 3:10; Jer 22:16 — note Jer 22:16 straddles groups 2 and 3)

On careful reading, groups 2 and 3 are closely related: both describe the moral correspondence between living rightly before God and experiencing the good he promises. They are distinct enough in emphasis — one is the volitional act of doing what pleases, the other is the experienced covenantal outcome — but in a small corpus of 16 verses, combining them into one "obedient-orientation / covenantal-good" VCG alongside a separate sacrificial-aroma VCG is the most defensible structure.

## M04-C — NT Joy in Christ and the Spirit (16 verses, existing sub-group — eucharistia/eucharistos)

Existing VCGs:
- `M04-C-VCG-01` (anchor Luk 1:14, 27 members): Synoptic and incarnation joy.
- `M04-C-VCG-02` (anchor Joh 15:11, 15 members): Johannine joy — indwelling, fullness, permanent gladness.
- `M04-C-VCG-03` (anchor Act 2:28, 58 members): Pauline relational and community joy.
- `M04-C-VCG-04` (anchor Act 27:22, 13 members): Spirit-given, kingdom, and constant joy.
- `M04-C-VCG-05` (anchor Jam 1:2, 16 members): Suffering-paradox, corrupt, and morally complex joy.

Reading the 16 eucharistia/eucharistos verses: all are expressions of gratitude directed to God — as inner posture accompanying prayer, as communal fruit of grace, as constant heart-disposition commanded for believers, as heavenly doxological offering. The question is whether they belong in existing VCGs or need a new one.

The existing VCGs are all anchored on chairo/chara (gladness/joy) registers. Eucharistia (thanksgiving-gratitude) is a distinct inner-being act — directed specifically upward to God as a response to grace received, rather than gladness experienced in community or in the Spirit. The dominant Pauline pattern (thanksgiving accompanying prayer, thanksgiving overflowing from generosity, thanksgiving as constant inner stance) has its own coherence that does not map cleanly onto the existing five VCGs:

- VCG-03 (Pauline relational-community joy) is the closest candidate, but its description names "gladness in gospel fellowship, pastoral bonds, co-rejoicing, mutual encouragement" — a horizontal community-joy register. The eucharistia verses are vertically oriented (gratitude to God) rather than horizontally relational.
- VCG-04 (Spirit-given, kingdom, constant joy) is also a candidate for the "constant thanksgiving" subset (Eph 5:4; Phili 4:6; Col 4:2), but its description names joy-as-Spirit's-fruit and kingdom-constituent — which is not quite the eucharistia thanksgiving register.
- The Revelation doxological thanksgiving (Rev 4:9; 7:12) fits neither community joy nor constant-commanded-joy.

**Decision: CREATE-NEW-VCG M04-C-VCG-06** — Pauline and heavenly thanksgiving: eucharistia/eucharistos as the inner disposition and practice of gratitude directed to God, spanning personal prayer-accompaniment, community-generosity fruit, constant-stance command, and heavenly doxological worship. This is a distinct phenomenon within M04-C's NT register: not gladness in fellowship but gratitude-to-God as the defining inner posture of the believing community and the heavenly worshippers.

One exception: Act 24:3 (Tertullus's formal acceptance "with all gratitude" to Felix) is not genuine eucharistia in the theological register — it is diplomatic speech-act gratitude. It fits most naturally as a cross-sub-group assign to M04-C-VCG-03 (Pauline relational-community register, which already holds diplomatic and social expressions of joy in community), or as part of the new VCG if the VCG is understood broadly enough. On verse reading, this verse's "formal acknowledgment" meaning is sufficiently distinct from the God-directed thanksgiving of the remaining 15 that it warrants a different disposition.
