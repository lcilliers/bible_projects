**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Three divine registers: (1) **Divine beauty as Pleasure** — Psa 27:4 names the no.am (beauty/pleasantness) of the Lord himself as the supreme object the psalmist seeks — "to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord and to inquire in his temple"; the pleasantness is an intrinsic quality of God's own being, not merely of his acts; (2) **Divine responsive Pleasure at worship** — M04-K-VCG-01 (the ni.cho.ach corpus, 38 verses) reveals God as one who receives the soothing aroma of correctly-offered sacrifice with inward satisfaction; Gen 8:21 — "the Lord smelled the pleasing aroma, the Lord said in his heart" — God's inner response to Noah's offering is a settling, calming pleasure that moves him to covenant resolve; Num 28:2 — "my pleasing aroma" — God describes the offering as his own food, claiming the pleasure-relation personally; (3) **Divine covenantal pleasantness as character** — Psa 36:8 names "the river of your delights" as flowing from God himself; Psa 90:17 — "the favor/pleasantness of the Lord our God be upon us" — divine pleasantness as gracious character directed outward toward the community; Zec 11:7, 10 — the shepherd's staff named No'am (Pleasantness/Favour) representing the quality of divine covenantal grace toward the flock.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, in two primary registers: (1) **God's being as pleasant/beautiful** — Psa 27:4 (no.am as God's own character-quality perceptible in worship); Psa 36:8 (river of delights flowing from God); (2) **God's inner responsive pleasure at worship** — Gen 8:21 (God's heart moved by the soothing aroma); the entire ni.cho.ach corpus of M04-K-VCG-01 names an inner divine state of calmed, settled reception — God experiences the pleasing aroma as genuinely soothing to his regard. The attribution reveals: human Pleasure as the soul's positive receptive response to what is pleasant is grounded in a God who himself experiences pleasantness in his own being and in rightly-directed worship. The human capacity for pleasure in beautiful persons, agreeable speech, and material goodness images back the divine pleasure in his own beauty and in worship that is rightly offered.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence is not silent about divine Pleasure — but the divine pleasantness is more restricted in scope than the human. God's ni.cho.ach pleasure at worship is bounded by covenant conditions: Lev 26:31 ("I will not smell your pleasing aromas") shows divine Pleasure withheld from disobedient worship; Eze 20:41's future-oriented "as a pleasing aroma I will accept you" shows it restorable. The bounded quality of divine Pleasure at worship reveals that divine pleasantness is morally structured — it responds to genuinely correct worship and withdraws from its corruption (M04-P-VCG-01's misdirected ni.cho.ach). The human capacity for Pleasure has the same bounded quality: genuine pleasantness is appropriate to right objects; misapplied, it becomes luxury-curse (Deu 28:54–56) or illicit indulgence (Pro 9:17).

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose Pleasure serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Three created purposes: (1) **Receiving the goodness of creation and persons** — M04-J-VCG-02 shows Pleasure as the soul's receptive faculty for what is genuinely agreeable: wisdom pleasant to the soul (Pro 2:10), wisdom's ways as pleasantness (Pro 3:17), gracious words nourishing soul and body (Pro 16:24), the beloved's beauty received in wonder (Song 1:16; 7:6), Jonathan's companionship deeply pleasant (2Sa 1:26) — the human person is equipped to register and receive genuine agreeableness; (2) **Rightly ordering the pursuit of material good** — M04-K-VCG-03 (the wisdom better-than comparatives) shows the faculty of Pleasure being trained in wisdom to prefer modest integrity over corrupt abundance (Psa 37:16; Pro 15:16, 17; 16:32; 17:1) — the wise person's Pleasure is properly calibrated toward what is genuinely good, not merely materially satisfying; (3) **Participation in right worship** — M04-K-VCG-01 shows Pleasure as the relational mechanism of correct sacrifice: the ni.cho.ach that rises to God and evokes his inner settled reception is the human person's rightly-directed worship-expression participating in a genuine relational exchange.

---

**T0.2.2 — Is this characteristic part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Both, with strong original-design grounding: (1) **Original design** — Pro 3:17 ("her ways are ways of pleasantness") places wisdom's pleasantness in the created order; Psa 16:6 ("lines have fallen for me in pleasant places") describes a God-given inheritance of pleasant provision; Psa 36:8 ("river of your delights") is an attribute of God's house; the Song of Songs' celebration of bodily beauty and pleasantness (Song 1:16; 7:6) is original-design relational appreciation; (2) **Response to fallen condition** — the ni.cho.ach corpus (M04-K-VCG-01) is explicitly cultic and sacrificial, which is a fallen-condition institution addressing sin; Lev 4:31 (pleasing aroma alongside atonement) shows ni.cho.ach operating in a forgiveness-seeking context; M04-K-VCG-02's luxury-curse register (Deu 28:54, 56 — the delicate person's comfort destroyed) is entirely a fallen-condition phenomenon; (3) Pro 9:17 (stolen bread called pleasant) is the fallen-condition distortion of Pleasure: the same faculty that receives genuine pleasantness is deceived by illicit appearance.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 16:11** — "at your right hand are pleasures forevermore" — the clearest eschatological statement for Pleasure in the corpus: pleasures in God's presence as a permanent eschatological reality; (2) **Eze 20:41** — "as a pleasing aroma I will accept you, when I bring you out from the peoples and gather you" — future divine acceptance-as-pleasantness as the eschatological restoration goal; (3) **Zec 8:19** — former fasts becoming "cheerful feasts" — the transformation of mourning into Pleasure as eschatological reversal; (4) **Job 36:11** — "if they listen and serve him, they complete their days in pleasantness" — pleasantness as the fruit of faithful obedience with eschatological overtones; (5) **Psa 36:8** — river of delights as the eternal supply in God's house, pointing toward the New Jerusalem's river of life.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure expresses the divine image as the human person's capacity to receive and register genuine agreeableness — imaging the God who himself experiences his own beauty as pleasant (Psa 27:4's no.am as God's quality) and who receives correct worship with inner satisfaction (Gen 8:21's ni.cho.ach moving God's heart). The human person's Pleasure in beautiful persons (Song 1:16; 7:6), agreeable speech (Pro 16:24), wisdom's ways (Pro 3:17), brotherly unity (Psa 133:1), and the pleasantness of God himself (Psa 27:4; 16:11) images back the divine capacity for pleasurable reception. The image is specifically expressed in the receptive-appreciative dimension of the inner being — the person who can receive beauty, goodness, and agreeableness and respond to it inwardly is imaging the divine pleasure in his own beautiful and good character.

---

**T0.3.2 — Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Partially shared. The no.am/na.im/no.am vocabulary describes both: God's own beauty (Psa 27:4 — no.am as God's attribute); human pleasantness (2Sa 1:26 — Jonathan's na.em to David; Pro 3:17 — wisdom's no.am ways; Psa 133:1 — na.im of brotherly unity). The ni.cho.ach vocabulary is explicitly relational — it names an inner divine response that corresponds to a human worship-act: the human offers, God receives with pleasure; this is a shared-pleasure structure at the worship interface. God's own inner beauty (no.am) is not shared with humans in the same direct sense as cha.phets (volitional Delight) — but human Pleasure in God's beauty (Psa 27:4) is the creaturely analogue of God's delight in his own character.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — **Presence of rightly directed Pleasure** reveals the image functioning: the person who finds wisdom genuinely pleasant (Pro 2:10 — "knowledge will be pleasant to your soul"), who appreciates the pleasantness of brotherly unity (Psa 133:1), who gazes on God's beauty (Psa 27:4), and whose worship genuinely soothes God's regard (M04-K-VCG-01) is an image-bearer receiving the world rightly. **Absence / distortion**: Neh 9:17 (Israel failed to register God's wonders — wonder absent); Pro 9:17 (stolen bread called pleasant — the Pleasure faculty deceived by wrong objects); Deu 28:54–56 (the delicate person's Pleasure destroyed by the curse — the image's receptive capacity collapsed under judgment); Job 20:18 (the wicked getting no enjoyment from his profits — the image's natural Pleasure capacity blocked by moral corruption). The diagnostic: the person who finds genuine pleasantness in wisdom, in persons, and in God's presence has a functioning image; the person whose Pleasure has been captured by wrong objects or collapsed under judgment shows the image degraded.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, in the ni.cho.ach register most clearly: (1) **Cultic ni.cho.ach → Christ's self-offering** — the entire Levitical pleasing-aroma system is the OT type that the NT applies to Christ's sacrificial death (Eph 5:2 — "a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God" — not in this corpus but the type is established here); each Levitical offering producing ni.cho.ach is a type of the final offering that fully satisfies divine regard; (2) **Psa 27:4's gazing on no.am → eschatological vision** — the one thing the psalmist seeks (gazing on God's beauty in his temple) is the type of the final face-to-face beatific experience (Rev 22:4 — "they will see his face" — not in corpus but anticipated by Psa 27:4); (3) **Zec 11:7, 10's No'am staff → covenant pleasantness revoked** — the breaking of the Favour/Pleasantness staff typologically anticipates the rejection of the Good Shepherd (the NT applies Zec 11 to the thirty pieces of silver and Christ's betrayal — Mat 27:9–10); (4) **Psa 36:8's river of delights → the river of life** (Rev 22:1) — the eschatological typing of divine pleasantness.

---

**T0.4.2 — What is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Primarily **OT-type → NT/eschatological fulfilment**: the cultic ni.cho.ach system points forward to Christ's offering; the Zec 11 No'am staff points forward to the Good Shepherd's rejection; the Psa 27:4 / 36:8 divine-pleasantness language points forward to eschatological vision and the river of life. The direction is historical-typological: the OT forms of Pleasure-in-worship and divine-pleasure establish the pattern that the NT and eschatological realities fulfil and surpass.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Typological use is evidenced in the ni.cho.ach and No'am staff registers; not silent.

## T1 — Definition and Structural Analysis

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The characteristic is called Pleasure. The name signals: (1) **Receptive quality** — Pleasure names what is received and felt, not what is generated or directed; it is the soul's positive receptive response to what is pleasant; (2) **Agreeableness** — the name implies a felt quality of fit or agreeableness between the inner person and the object; (3) **Less directional than Delight** — Delight is always toward a specific treasured object; Pleasure is more ambient, received from surroundings, persons, and circumstances rather than focused on a single treasured object; (4) **Less active than Joy** — Pleasure is the inner being at ease in what is agreeable rather than actively exulting or rejoicing.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Primary terms: (1) **na.im** (H5273A) — pleasant, agreeable, lovely; 8 instances in M04-J; root na.em — to be pleasant, agreeable; the quality of what is fitting and agreeable to the inner sense; Psa 133:1; 135:3; 147:1; Psa 16:6; Job 36:11; Song 1:16; 2Sa 1:23; Pro 22:18; (2) **na.em** (H5276) — to be/feel pleasant; 7 instances in M04-J; the verbal experience of pleasantness; 2Sa 1:26 — "very pleasant have you been to me"; Pro 2:10 — "knowledge will be pleasant to your soul"; Pro 9:17; 24:25; Song 7:6; Psa 141:6; Isa 32:12; (3) **no.am** (H5278) — pleasantness, beauty, favour; 5 instances in M04-J; the noun naming the quality: Psa 27:4 (Lord's beauty); Psa 90:17 (Lord's favour); Pro 3:17 (wisdom's ways); Pro 15:26; 16:24; Zec 11:7, 10; (4) **na.a.man** (H5282) — pleasant plants: Isa 17:10; (5) **ni.cho.ach** (H5207) — soothing, pleasing (aroma); 38 instances in M04-K-VCG-01 — the dominant term for the sacrificial-worship Pleasure register; root: to rest, settle, give rest; the quality of the worship-aroma that calms and settles God's regard; (6) **a.nog** (H6026) — to be tender/delicate/luxuriant: Deu 28:54, 56 (the pampered person); Isa 47:1 (Babylon's delicateness); (7) **a.las** (H5965) — to exult, delight (in pleasure): Job 20:18 (the wicked gets no a.las from profits); Pro 7:18 (seductress's carnal a.las); (8) **ta.a.nug** (H8588) — daintiness, luxury: Pro 19:10 (luxury unfit for fools); Ecc 2:8 (concubines as "delight of the sons of man"); Mic 2:9 (delightful houses); Song 7:6; (9) **ed.nah** (H5730B) — pleasure, luxury: 2Sa 1:24 (luxurious scarlet); Psa 36:8 (river of delights); Jer 51:34 (Jerusalem's delicacies consumed); (10) **tov** (H2896A) in the M04-K-VCG-03 better-than-comparatives; (11) **thambeō** (G2284) — to be amazed: Mar 10:24 (disciples amazed at Jesus' teaching). At definitional level: the vocabulary cluster reveals Pleasure as covering a spectrum from the lightest agreeableness (na.im — pleasant, fitting) through the sensory-luxury of delicate living (a.nog, ta.a.nug, ed.nah) through the worship-pleasantness of correctly-offered sacrifice (ni.cho.ach) through the moral-comparative Pleasure of preferring integrity (tov better-than).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Receptive rather than directed** — the na.im/na.em vocabulary consistently describes a quality the inner person receives or recognises rather than generates or directs; (2) **Relational** — most M04-J instances are relational: Psa 133:1 (brotherly pleasantness), 2Sa 1:26 (Jonathan's pleasantness to David), Song 1:16; 7:6 (beloved's pleasantness), Pro 2:10 (wisdom pleasant to the soul), Pro 16:24 (gracious words nourishing soul and body); (3) **Constitutional** — Pro 2:10 names the soul explicitly ("pleasant to your soul"), Pro 16:24 names both soul and body ("sweetness to the soul and health to the body"); Pleasure is constitutionally distributed across soul and body in a way that no other M04 characteristic is as explicitly.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is primarily a **quality** and **condition** rather than an act or disposition: (1) **Quality** — na.im/na.em/no.am name a quality of what is pleasant (Psa 133:1 — unity is pleasant; Pro 3:17 — wisdom's ways are pleasantness); (2) **Condition** — the state of pleasantness enjoyed or inhabited: Psa 16:6 (lines fallen in pleasant places — an inherited condition); Job 36:11 (years in pleasantness — a condition of life); Psa 16:11 (pleasures at God's right hand — a permanent condition); (3) **Receptive experience** — Pro 2:10 (knowledge "will be pleasant to your soul" — a felt inner experience received when wisdom enters); Pro 16:24 (gracious words as received nourishment); (4) **Divine inner state** — Gen 8:21 (God's inner calmed response to ni.cho.ach — a state of settled reception). Pleasure is primarily the quality-condition of what is agreeable, received and inhabited by the inner person, rather than an act directed outward.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Two distinct constituent elements mapped to the two sub-groups: (1) **Relational-personal pleasantness** (M04-J) — the agreeableness of persons, bonds, speech, wisdom, and God's own beauty; the inner being's capacity to register and inhabit what is genuinely pleasant in relationships and in God; (2) **Sensory-material pleasure and worship-pleasure** (M04-K) — spanning four registers: (a) the soothing-aroma worship-Pleasure of correctly-offered sacrifice (VCG-01 ni.cho.ach, 38 verses); (b) sensory luxury and pampered ease (VCG-02 — a.nog, ta.a.nug, a.las, ed.nah); (c) the wisdom better-than comparatives (VCG-03 — tov) where material Pleasure is calibrated against moral worth; (d) the cross-assigned instances (Psa 34:8 taste-and-see, Zec 8:19 cheerful feasts, Mar 10:24 disciples' amazement). The characteristic is therefore structurally more complex than its "Pleasure" name implies: it spans the most refined relational warmth (Jonathan's companionship) through the most formal worship-Pleasure (ni.cho.ach) through the morally-calibrated better-than comparatives.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is the inner-being experience of pleasantness — the felt quality of agreeableness received from persons, relationships, creation, and material or sensory experience. It is the soul's positive receptive response to what is pleasant. Less directed than Delight (which focuses on a specific treasured object) and less active than Joy — Pleasure is the inner being's sense of agreeableness and satisfying ease in what surrounds it. The primary modes: (a) relational pleasantness — the agreeableness of persons, bonds, and speech; (b) worship-pleasure — the soothing reception of correctly offered sacrifice by God; (c) sensory-material pleasure — the comfortable ease of pampered living, properly calibrated by wisdom; (d) morally-calibrated comparative Pleasure — the wisdom verdict that modest integrity is more genuinely pleasant than corrupt abundance. Constitutional location: the soul (Pro 2:10), the body (Pro 16:24), and by extension the whole person in creation (Psa 16:6; 36:8).

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple structural opposites: (1) **Harshness/roughness** — the na.im/na.em vocabulary implicitly stands against harshness; 1Sa 25:3 (Nabal's harsh inner character contrasted with Abigail's discernment — adjacent); Pro 15:26 (gracious/pleasant words pure vs. wicked's abominable thoughts); (2) **Denied pleasure** — Job 20:18 ("no enjoyment" — a.las absent); Deu 28:54, 56 (delicate person's pleasure destroyed by judgment); (3) **Unacceptable worship** — Lev 26:31 ("I will not smell your pleasing aromas") — the ni.cho.ach withheld when the covenant is broken; the structural opposite of worship-Pleasure is God's non-reception; (4) **Folly's false pleasantness** — Pro 9:17 (stolen bread "pleasant" — the structural opposite of genuine Pleasure is deceived pleasure that masquerades as genuinely agreeable but leads to death); (5) **Strife and hatred as the opposite of relational Pleasure** — Pro 15:17 ("better a dinner of herbs where love is than a fattened ox and hatred") — hatred is the structural opposite of the relational pleasantness that makes community agreeable.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure explicitly excludes: (1) **Worship without covenant faithfulness** — Lev 26:31 (God will not smell pleasing aromas from the unfaithful) — the ni.cho.ach pleasure-relationship is bounded by covenant obedience; (2) **Corrupt luxury** — Isa 47:1 (Babylon's delicateness about to be stripped — pampered ease built on evil must end); Pro 19:10 (luxury unfitting for fools); (3) **Illicit sensual pleasure** — Pro 7:18 (the seductress's a.las as carnal indulgence); (4) **Excess of pleasant things** — Pro 25:27 ("not good to eat much honey") — even genuine pleasantness becomes not-pleasant in excess; (5) **Self-serving pleasant words without moral reality** — Isa 58:2 (adjacent — delight to seek God but without justice) — Pleasure in worship without moral correspondence is explicitly rejected.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is not: (1) **Delight** (Characteristic 4) — Delight is focused on a specific treasured object and is more volitionally directed; Pleasure is more ambient and receptive — it is the quality of what is agreeable rather than the orientation toward what is treasured; (2) **Joy** (Characteristic 2) — Joy actively participates; Pleasure passively receives; (3) **Gladness** (Characteristic 3) — Gladness is the cognitively-evaluated inner warmth of settled goodness; Pleasure is more immediately sensory and relational in its receptive quality; (4) **Wonder** (Characteristic 6) — Wonder is the inner being arrested by what exceeds comprehension; Pleasure is the inner being at ease in what is agreeably familiar; (5) **Luxury or hedonism** — M04-K-VCG-02's luxury register (Deu 28; Isa 47; Ecc 2) shows Pleasure in its excessive or cursed form; the characteristic itself is the genuine reception of what is genuinely pleasant, not the self-indulgent pursuit of pleasure; (6) **Mere sensory stimulation** — the ni.cho.ach corpus shows God's Pleasure at worship as an inner settled response, not mere sensory input; divine Pleasure at the soothing aroma is a theologically substantial inner-being event, not primitive sensory reaction.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Four operational modes: (1) **Perceptive-receptive mode** (M04-J-VCG-02) — the inner being receiving and registering what is pleasant in persons, speech, and creation: Psa 27:4 (gazing upon beauty), 2Sa 1:26 (Jonathan's pleasantness received by David's soul), Pro 2:10 (knowledge pleasant to the soul), Pro 16:24 (gracious words nourishing), Song 1:16; 7:6 (beloved's pleasantness perceived and appreciated); (2) **Worshipful-receptive mode** (M04-K-VCG-01) — the ni.cho.ach quality of correctly-offered sacrifice evoking God's inner settled pleasure; this is the worship-interface mode in which human offering evokes divine receptive Pleasure; (3) **Luxury-comfort mode** (M04-K-VCG-02) — the inner ease of refined, pampered existence: a.nog in Deu 28:54, 56; ta.a.nug in Pro 19:10; Ecc 2:8; Mic 2:9; (4) **Wisdom-calibrated comparative mode** (M04-K-VCG-03) — the tov better-than comparatives: training Pleasure to prefer modest integrity over corrupt abundance; Pleasure here is the inner faculty being formed by wisdom to assess what is more genuinely pleasant.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **By direction**: the ni.cho.ach mode moves from human to God (the worshiper offers → God receives with pleasure); the na.im/no.am mode moves from persons/creation to the inner being (what is pleasant in the world is received inwardly); the luxury mode is self-referential (the person's own inner ease in pampered conditions); (2) **By constitutional level**: na.im/no.am registers primarily in the soul (Pro 2:10 — "pleasant to your soul"); ni.cho.ach operates at the worship-level interface between human and divine; a.nog/ta.a.nug registers in the bodily dimension (Deu 28:54, 56 — somatic delicateness; Pro 16:24 — "health to the body"); (3) **By register**: the relational register (M04-J) is warm and personal; the worship register (M04-K-VCG-01) is formal and covenantal; the luxury register (M04-K-VCG-02) is private and bodily; the wisdom-comparative register (M04-K-VCG-03) is evaluative.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, in the speech-Pleasure register: (1) **Pro 16:24** — "gracious words are like a honeycomb, sweetness to the soul and health to the body" — speech as the medium through which Pleasure is communicated and received; the gracious word is itself an instance of pleasantness reaching the inner person; (2) **Pro 15:26** — "gracious words are pure" — pleasantness characterising speech as morally wholesome; (3) **Psa 45:1** — "my heart overflows with a pleasing theme" — the inner pleasantness expressing through speech in the form of praise poetry; (4) **Pro 22:18** — "it will be pleasant if you keep them within you, if all of them are ready on your lips" — wisdom's pleasantness residing within and ready to flow through speech. The speech-Pleasure link is the most distinctive communicative mode for this characteristic: genuinely pleasant speech is itself an inner-being state of pleasantness expressed outward and received inward.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering Pleasure?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The immediate responses differ by mode: (1) **Relational pleasantness** — an immediate inner recognition and warm reception: 2Sa 1:26 (David immediately experiencing Jonathan's companionship as pleasant); Song 1:16; 7:6 (the lover's immediate appreciative response to the beloved's beauty); (2) **Good words/speech** — immediate inner animation: Pro 12:25 (good word making glad); Pro 16:24 (gracious words providing immediate nourishment — honey simile implies instant effect); (3) **Worship-Pleasure (ni.cho.ach)** — Gen 8:21 (God's immediate responsive resolve in his heart upon smelling Noah's offering); the divine response to the pleasing aroma is immediate and decisive; (4) **Divine beauty** — Psa 27:4's longing to gaze on God's beauty is an aspiration rather than an immediate response, but the assumption is that the encounter with divine no.am produces immediate worshipful reception.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Generally consistent in direction (inner reception, warmth, nourishment) but varies in intensity and constitutional level: the relational pleasantness response is warm and affective; the worship-pleasure response (ni.cho.ach) on God's side is settled and resolving; the speech-pleasure response is nourishing; the sensory-luxury response is comfortable ease. All involve a positive receptive inner movement toward what is pleasant, but the depth, faculty, and duration vary by register.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** S — M04-K-VCG-03 (the wisdom better-than comparatives) does not evidence an immediate response to Pleasure — these are evaluative verdicts about what is more genuinely pleasant, not descriptions of a felt immediate response. Similarly, M04-K-VCG-01 on the human side (the person offering the sacrifice) is silent on the person's immediate inner state — the focus is on the divine reception. The luxury register (M04-K-VCG-02) is also largely silent on the immediate experiential response of the person enjoying luxury, focusing instead on the curse (Deu 28) or the catalogue (Ecc 2:8).

---

**T1.6.1 — What does Pleasure produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Sustained genuine Pleasure produces: (1) **Relational depth** — 2Sa 1:26's sustained friendship with Jonathan is the deepest relational pleasantness evidenced in the corpus; sustained experience of genuine relational Pleasure deepens the bond and its inner satisfaction; (2) **Nourishment and health** — Pro 16:24 ("sweetness to the soul and health to the body") — sustained gracious speech and its Pleasure nourishes the inner and outer person; (3) **Wisdom formation** — Pro 2:10's "knowledge will be pleasant to your soul" presupposes wisdom already dwelling in the heart; sustained Pleasure in wisdom produces the person formed by its ways (Pro 3:17 — "ways of pleasantness"); (4) **Worship relationship** — the twice-daily ni.cho.ach corpus (Exo 29:38–41 — morning and evening) sustains an ongoing relationship of divine favour; sustained right worship produces a continuous pleasantness of divine reception; (5) **Eschatological orientation** — Psa 16:11 (pleasures at God's right hand forevermore) implies a sustained present orientation toward God's presence producing the eschatological fullness.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, and orientations does sustained Pleasure establish?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Soul-pleasantness as a stable inner quality** — the person whose inner life is marked by genuine relational and wisdom Pleasure develops a stable inner agreeableness; Pro 3:17's "ways of pleasantness" names the settled orientation of one who walks in wisdom; (2) **Calibrated valuation of material goods** — the M04-K-VCG-03 wisdom-comparatives train the inner being to prefer integrity with modest means over corrupt abundance; sustained engagement with these comparatives forms a person who habitually evaluates what is genuinely more pleasant; (3) **Covenantal worship relationship** — sustained ni.cho.ach worship maintains the divine-human pleasure-relationship that enables covenant life; (4) **Pro 16:24's nourished person** — sustained reception of gracious words produces an inner person sustained in soul-health and body-health simultaneously.

---
