## Summary

M04 represents **7 distinct inner-being characteristics**. The 16 sub-groups distribute across these 7 as qualifiers, registers, and dimensions. One sub-group (M04-E) carries evidence for two characteristics and is noted accordingly.

The 7 characteristics: **Exultation · Joy · Gladness · Delight · Pleasure · Wonder · Suffering-Joy**

---

## Characteristic 1 — Exultation

**Definition:** The soul's active, surging, triumphant inner state directed at what is glorious or transcendent — above all at God. The inner being *rises* in exultant response. Distinguished from Joy by intensity and directedness; the person who exults is overwhelmed upward toward their object. In Scripture this is almost always Godward: the soul exults in YHWH's salvation, in his holy name, in his mighty acts.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-A | The Godward qualifier — exultation directed toward YHWH as object and ground; the primary and sole register of this characteristic in M04 |

---

## Characteristic 2 — Joy

**Definition:** The active inner experience of rejoicing — the soul's engaged, participatory delight in what is worth celebrating. Joy is less surging than Exultation and more active than Gladness. It is the inner being rejoicing *in* something: in the Lord, in community, in shared celebration, in what is promised and coming. The commanded "rejoice in the Lord always" is Joy — an active inner disposition of engagement, not merely a warm background feeling.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-B | The communal-festive qualifier — Joy enacted in community, structured by appointed feast and corporate worship; primary OT rejoicing vocabulary (sa.mach, sim.chah) |
| M04-C | The NT Spirit-and-Christ qualifier — Joy arising from faith in Christ, mediated by the indwelling Spirit, shaped by the gospel community; primary NT rejoicing vocabulary (chairo, chara) |
| M04-D | The solidarity qualifier — Joy constitutively shared: sought and completed through another's rejoicing (sunchairo) |
| M04-E *(partial)* | The eschatological qualifier — Joy oriented toward the not-yet; the soul's forward-engaged rejoicing grounded in divine promise of restoration |

**Note on M04-B and M04-C inter-relationships:** These sub-groups carry both the active-rejoicing register (Joy) and the settled-warm register (Gladness) within their verse corpora. This is not a problem to resolve here — it is precisely what the VCGs within these sub-groups will surface analytically. The inter-relationship between Joy and Gladness is expected to be one of the significant findings of Phase 9 for M04. The characteristic map assigns these sub-groups to Joy as their dominant phenomenon; the Gladness dimension within them will emerge from the verse-level analysis.

---

## Characteristic 3 — Gladness

**Definition:** The inner state of settled, warm, positive brightness — the heart that is glad, the soul whose inner condition is light and well. Gladness is less active than Joy (which rejoices *in* something) and more diffuse: a quality of inner being rather than a directed act. It includes the cognitive face of the same state — the soul's recognition that things are good, which produces and expresses the gladness. The person who is glad has a positively-coloured inner condition.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-O | The circumstantial qualifier — gladness as the soul's warm inner response to good news, beneficial states, and refreshing circumstance |
| M04-L | The evaluative-cognitive qualifier — the soul's inner appraisal of what is genuinely good; the cognitive face of gladness: the assessment that produces and expresses the glad inner state |

---

## Characteristic 4 — Delight

**Definition:** The inner state of treasuring and taking pleasure in a specific object — the soul's resting satisfaction in what it loves. Delight is more object-focused than Gladness and more receptive than Exultation. The person who delights has found something that satisfies their inner being: they rest in it, return to it, orient themselves toward it. Includes the affective pleasure of the soul in God's word, the volitional orientation of the will toward what pleases, and — in its disordered form — the soul's misplaced treasuring of wrong objects.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-G | The divine-word qualifier — affective delight in God's word, law, testimonies, and YHWH himself as the treasured object |
| M04-H | The volitional qualifier — delight as the will's orientation: sovereign willing-pleasure, directed inner resolve toward its object |
| M04-M | The obedience qualifier — delight as the soul's orientation toward what is acceptable and pleasing to God; the compliance register of volitional delight |
| M04-N | The relational-horizontal qualifier — delight in persons: parental attachment, the treasured nearness of a neighbour |
| M04-P | The corrupt qualifier — delight misdirected toward evil objects, persons, and practices; the disordered face of the same capacity |

---

## Characteristic 5 — Pleasure

**Definition:** The inner-being experience of pleasantness — the felt quality of agreeableness received from persons, relationships, creation, and material or sensory experience. Pleasure is the soul's positive receptive response to what is pleasant. Less directed than Delight (which focuses on a specific treasured object) and less active than Joy — Pleasure is the inner being's sense of agreeableness and satisfying ease in what surrounds it.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-J | The relational qualifier — pleasantness as the quality of what is agreeable in persons, bonds, speech, and creation |
| M04-K | The sensory-material qualifier — pleasure registered through bodily and material experience: luxury, the soothing aroma of righteous worship, sensory satisfaction |

---

## Characteristic 6 — Wonder

**Definition:** The inner state of astonishment and awe before what exceeds ordinary comprehension — the soul arrested and overwhelmed by what it cannot fully grasp. Wonder is the inner being stilled rather than surging: it confronts what is too great to simply rejoice at. Includes wonder as proclamation (declaring the marvellous), wonder as incomprehensibility (too great to comprehend), and wonder as dark astonishment (appalling awe at what is terrible or wrong). Distinguished from Exultation by its arrested, contemplative character; from Joy by its perceptual-receptive rather than participatory character.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-I | The primary register — wonder at God's extraordinary works, their incomprehensibility, and dark astonishment at what is appalling |

---

## Characteristic 7 — Suffering-Joy

**Definition:** The paradoxical inner capacity to find genuine joy *in* and *through* suffering — not merely alongside it, not despite it, but as an inner state that suffering itself produces or occasions. This is a distinct inner-being capacity from Joy (which rejoices in what is good) and from Gladness (which is the warm inner state of one whose condition is well): it is the soul's ability to genuinely rejoice when circumstances are against it, grounded in what suffering reveals or produces. The verse evidence is present: commanded rejoicing in trials (Jas 1:2), joy in tribulation producing proven character (Rom 5:3-5), rejoicing in present sufferings oriented to coming glory (1 Pet 1:6-8), and the fragile attempted cheerfulness of those under extreme adversity.

**Sub-groups:**

| Sub-group | Qualifier / dimension |
|---|---|
| M04-E *(partial)* | The suffering-paradox register — joy coexisting with and arising *from* suffering; forward-oriented rejoicing grounded in the promise of what suffering produces (M04-E's eschatological register serves Characteristic 2 — Joy; its suffering-paradox register serves this characteristic) |
| M04-F | The fragile-composure qualifier — cheerfulness as a desired or attempted inner state under extreme adversity; the tenuous, barely-attained face of the same capacity |

**Note on M04-E as a split sub-group:** M04-E carries two distinct registers within its 35-verse corpus. Its eschatological forward-rejoicing register (sa.s.von — gladness promised as reversal of sorrow; the soul's anticipatory engagement with restoration not yet present) belongs under Characteristic 2 — Joy. Its NT suffering-paradox register (agalliao — rejoicing in trials; joy that suffering itself occasions) belongs here. The VCG structure within M04-E will show which VCGs serve which characteristic. Phase 9 should treat M04-E's evidence under both Characteristic 2 and Characteristic 7 accordingly.

## Notes for Phase 9

1. **Inter-relationships between Joy and Gladness** (M04-B, M04-C): These sub-groups carry both registers. The VCG analysis will surface where the active-rejoicing phenomenon (Joy) and the settled-warm phenomenon (Gladness) operate within them. This inter-relationship is one of the anticipated significant findings of Phase 9.

2. **M04-E split**: Phase 9 should apply the 189 prompts to M04-E under both Characteristic 2 (Joy) and Characteristic 7 (Suffering-Joy), treating the two registers separately where the evidence permits.

3. **Delight's breadth**: Characteristic 4 spans five sub-groups (247 verses) covering the affective, volitional, obedience, relational, and corrupt registers of Delight. The inter-relationships between these registers — particularly between M04-G (affective delight in God) and M04-H (volitional delight as directed will) — are expected to be analytically significant.

4. **M04-L under Gladness**: The evaluative-cognitive qualifier of Gladness (the soul's appraisal that something is good) is the cognitive face of the Gladness characteristic, not a separate phenomenon. Phase 9 findings for M04-L should be integrated with M04-O's findings under Characteristic 3.
