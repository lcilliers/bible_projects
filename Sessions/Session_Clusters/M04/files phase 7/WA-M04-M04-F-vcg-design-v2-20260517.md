## M04-F-VCG-01 — Fragile Cheerfulness: Desired but Barely Attainable Inner Comfort

**Context description:** This VCG covers the entire M04-F corpus — the small but distinct register of cheerfulness as a desired or attempted inner state under extreme adversity. The ba.lag verb (3 verses from Job and Psalms) defines the register: Job considers cheerfulness as something he might attempt by an act of will but cannot sustain, the surrounding context making clear its impossibility (Job 9:27); Job pleads for a brief moment of cheer before death, naming the state as minimal and temporary (Job 10:20); the psalmist hopes to recover a fragile brightness before death, asking God to look away so he might 'smile again' (Psa 39:13). The mav.li.git noun (Jer 8:18) names the inner capacity for comfort that is utterly gone, replaced by grief — the prophet's sick heart. This is not the gospel-grounded suffering-joy of M04-E (which is paradoxical but confident); this is the vulnerable, tenuously hoped-for inner cheer that adversity barely leaves room for.
