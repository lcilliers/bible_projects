## M04-E-VCG-01 — OT Restoration Gladness: Joy Promised as Reversal of Sorrow

**Context description:** This VCG names the sa.s.von and gi.lah corpus: the OT register of gladness that is promised as the direct reversal of sorrow through God's redemptive acts. The sa.s.von noun carries the distinctive register of salvation-joy: the joy of the ransomed as they return to Zion (Isa 35:10; 51:11), gladness given by God to replace mourning (Jer 31:13), Jerusalem as a name evoking joy (Jer 33:9), the restoration of festive sound (Jer 33:11), fasts becoming seasons of rejoicing (Zec 8:19), oil of gladness poured over mourners (Isa 61:3), joy of drawing water from salvation's wells (Isa 12:3). The gi.lah corpus (Isa 35:2; 65:18) names the land's and the city's inner exuberance of new-creation renewal. The sub-register of joy removed by judgment is also here — sa.s.von naming what is stripped (Jer 7:34; 16:9; 25:10; Joe 1:12) — because these verses evidence the restoration-gladness characteristic by naming its devastating absence. The deliverance-joy of Esther (Est 8:16/17), the psalmist's personal salvation-joy (Psa 45:7; 51:8/12; 105:43; 119:111), and Jeremiah's personal word-joy (Jer 15:16) complete the corpus. The hollow festivity of Isa 22:13 (reckless joy in the shadow of death) is assigned here as the corrupted register within the sa.s.von corpus.

## M04-E-VCG-02 — NT Eschatological and Suffering-Paradox Exultation (agalliao)

**Context description:** This VCG names the agalliao corpus — NT exultation that spans the suffering-paradox and the eschatological horizon. The register is consistently forward-oriented and theologically grounded beyond present circumstance: the persecuted are commanded to exult because their reward is great in heaven (Mat 5:12), Mary's spirit exults in God as Saviour (Luk 1:47 — located in the spirit), Jesus rejoices in the Holy Spirit (Luk 10:21), Abraham's forward-looking exultation at seeing Jesus' day (Joh 8:56), the jailer's household-wide belief-born exultation (Act 16:34), rejoicing that coexists with grief over trials (1Pe 1:6), the inexpressible glory-filled exultation of believing in the unseen Christ (1Pe 1:8), rejoicing in present suffering oriented toward future full exultation at Christ's revelation (1Pe 4:13), the corporate eschatological exultation at the Lamb's marriage (Rev 19:7). Also: Act 2:26 (exultant rejoicing in the tongue from inward gladness seated in the heart), Joh 5:35 (crowds' transient surface-level rejoicing in John's light — evidences the register by contrast).
