## M04-B-VCG-01 — Festival and Feast Worship Joy: Rejoicing Before God at the Appointed Feasts

**Context description:** This VCG names the register of prescribed communal joy at Israel's sacred calendar — the appointed feasts and their accompanying worship. The Deuteronomy feast-commands are the defining corpus: rejoicing before God at the chosen sanctuary place is commanded for the entire household including servants, Levites, and sojourners (Deu 12:7, 12, 18; 16:11, 14; 26:11; 27:7; Lev 23:40). The joy is both commanded and expansive — no one is excluded, and the gladness is oriented toward God at his dwelling place. The Feast of Booths produces joy marked as historically unprecedented (Neh 8:17); the Feast of Unleavened Bread produces divinely-given gladness (Ezr 6:22). Joy is declared the proper inner disposition for serving God — its absence the root cause of judgment (Deu 28:47). Also included: the Chronicles passages where wholehearted freewill giving itself generates joy (1Ch 29:9 ×2) and the extended Hezekiah Passover celebration (2Ch 30:21–26).

## M04-B-VCG-02 — Sanctuary, Temple, and Ark Joy: Gladness at the Sacred Presence

**Context description:** This VCG names the joy arising specifically from the ark, the temple, and God's sacred dwelling — gladness at the covenant presence returning, the sanctuary being established, or worship being restored. The return of the ark to Jerusalem (2Sa 6:12; 1Sa 6:13), the temple dedications (1Ki 8:66; 2Ch 7:10; Ezr 3:12–13; Neh 12:27/43/44), the restoration of Levitical worship (2Ch 29:30; 23:18), and the great Passover of Hezekiah where God's house is the centre (Ezr 6:22 ×2) define this VCG. The Chronicles ark-procession scenes (1Ch 15:16/25; 12:40) and the Nehemiah wall-dedication (Neh 12:27/43/44) complete the corpus. The defining trait: joy at the recovery or establishment of the sacred space and its ordered worship, including the psalmist's gladness at going up to God's house (Psa 122:1).

## M04-B-VCG-03 — Coronation, National Deliverance, and Civic Joy

**Context description:** This VCG names the corporate inner gladness arising from national restoration, the coronation of a rightful king, military deliverance, or the reversal of civic oppression. The coronation scenes are defining: Solomon's anointing produces joy so intense the earth shakes (1Ki 1:40 ×2); Joash's crowning is received with communal joy set against Athaliah's horror (2Ki 11:14; 2Ch 23:13); the people rejoice after Athaliah's removal and the city quiets (2Ch 23:21; 2Ki 11:20). Military deliverance produces communal joy — Jabesh-gilead at news of rescue (1Sa 11:9), all Israel at Gilgal after victory (1Sa 11:15), the community at divine triumph over enemies (2Ch 20:27 ×2). The Purim corpus (Est 8–9) institutionalises the joy of deliverance from threatened destruction (Est 8:15/16/17; 9:17/18/19/22). Prophetic calls to communal rejoicing at God's coming salvation (Zep 3:14; Zec 2:10; 4:10) complete the register.

## M04-B-VCG-04 — Relational, Personal, and Wisdom-Life Gladness

**Context description:** This VCG names the register of gladness arising from human relationships, parental bonds, domestic life, and personal engagement with existence as a God-given gift. The Proverbs parental-joy corpus is the largest cluster: a father's deep rejoicing at a righteous or wise child (Pro 10:1; 15:20; 23:15/24/25; 27:11; 29:3; 17:21), marital gladness (Pro 5:18; Deu 24:5), the friend's earnest counsel and sensory gladness (Pro 27:9), communal gladness from just leadership (Pro 29:2/6). The Ecclesiastes life-embrace corpus carries the register of wisdom-joy: finding gladness in one's toil as God's gift (Ecc 5:19/20), commending joy as the best posture under the sun (Ecc 8:15 ×2), the young person urged to let the heart cheer (Ecc 11:9), joy as a God-given occupation of the heart (Ecc 5:20; 9:7). Also included: relational gladness (Judg 9:13/19; 19:3; 1Sa 19:5; Song 1:4; 3:11; Gen 31:27; Exo 4:14), personal rejoicing in Hannah (1Sa 2:1), and Proverbs moral-gladness observations (Pro 12:20/25; 13:9; 14:10/13; 15:13/21/23; 17:22; 21:15/17; Ecc 2:1/2/10/26; 3:12/22; 4:16; 7:4; 8:15; 10:19; 11:8).

## M04-B-VCG-05 — God-Directed Psalmist and Prophetic Gladness

**Context description:** This VCG names the large corpus of sa.mach and sim.chah verses in the Psalms and Prophets where rejoicing is explicitly directed toward God, flows from his saving acts, or is commanded as the right response to his governance. This is the sa.mach/sim.chah parallel of M04-A's gil_verb corpus: the same vertical register but carried by different terms. The Psalms sequence is dense — from God placing joy directly in the heart (Psa 4:7) through the soul's gladness grounded in trust (Psa 5:11; 9:2; 16:9; 33:21; 63:11; 64:10; 86:4) to the commanded joy of righteous people directed toward the Lord (Psa 32:11; 97:12; 149:2). The prophetic voice adds restoration-joy called forth by God's coming (Isa 25:9; 29:19; 35:10; 51:3/11; 55:12; 61:7; 65:13; 66:10) and eschatological transformation from fasting to joy (Zec 8:19). God's own gladness toward his people is also here (Zep 3:17 sim.chah). The Ezra/Nehemiah community's divinely-produced gladness (Ezr 6:22 ×2 is in VCG-02; these are the psalmist's seeking-God gladness).

## M04-B-VCG-06 — Inverted, Absent, Corrupt, and Judgment-Silenced Joy

**Context description:** This VCG names the verses where the M04-B rejoicing vocabulary evidences the characteristic through inversion, moral corruption, absence, or divine judgment. The register is large because sa.mach and sim.chah span the full moral range — genuine, misdirected, and absent joy alike. Enemy malice directed at the psalmist's stumbling (Psa 35:15/19/24/26/38:16), enemies' potential triumph (Psa 89:42), joy cruelly demanded from exiles (Psa 137:3). Corrupt and perverse gladness: wicked's feasting (Job 21:12), hollow transient joy (Job 20:5; 8:19 ma.s.vo.s), morally inverted gladness at another's harm (Pro 2:14; 17:5; 24:17; Eze 25:6; Eze 35:14/15; Eze 36:5; Hos 7:3; Obd 12). Joy stripped by divine judgment: Isaiah's judgment passages (Isa 16:10; 22:13; 24:7/8/11; 32:13/14), Jeremiah's silencing of communal gladness (Jer 7:34; 16:9; 25:10; 48:33), Lamentations' community desolation (Lam 2:15/17; 4:21; 5:15), Ezekiel's forbidden buying-joy (Eze 7:12) and Jerusalem's delight removed (Eze 24:25 ma.s.vo.s), prophetic warnings (Hab 1:15; Amo 6:13; Obd 12; Mic 7:8). Jonah's shallow self-focused joy (Jon 4:6 ×2), Haman's fragile pride-joy (Est 5:9/14), Job's moral self-examination (Job 31:25/29/22:19), Hosea's corrupted festival gladness (Hos 9:1), God's withheld rejoicing (Isa 9:17), and Isaiah's reckless festivity-facing-death (Isa 22:13) complete the register.
