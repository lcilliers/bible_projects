## M04-J-VCG-01 — Divine Pleasantness and the Pleasantness of Worship

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where pleasantness (no.am, na.im) describes God's own character, his covenant favour toward his people, and the pleasantness of worship directed toward him. The supreme object of longing: the no.am of the Lord — his beauty — is the one thing Psa 27:4 seeks to gaze upon in his temple. God's river of delights (ed.nah, Psa 36:8) is lavishly supplied in worship, overwhelming the person who abides in his house. The pleasantness/favour of the Lord is asked to rest upon the community (no.am, Psa 90:17), confirming and establishing their work. The Zechariah shepherd-staff texts (Zec 11:7/10 no.am) personify divine covenant favour as a staff named Pleasantness — breaking it signals the annulling of that gracious relational quality. Worship itself is pleasant: singing praise to God's name (Psa 135:3 na.im), praising God called pleasant alongside good and fitting (Psa 147:1 na.im). At God's right hand are pleasures forevermore (Psa 16:11 na.im — eternal delight in God's presence). The missing XREF 2Sa 23:1 (David the 'sweet psalmist of Israel' — na.im) is assigned here: the pleasantness of prophetic-worship speech directed toward God.

## M04-J-VCG-02 — Pleasantness of Persons, Speech, Wisdom, and Human Bonds

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where pleasantness is a quality experienced in human relationships, in wisdom's ways, in gracious speech, and in the created world as agreeable to the inner person. The defining anchor is David's lament over Jonathan — his companionship was deeply pleasant to him, surpassing even the love of women, the pleasantness located in the soul (na.em, 2Sa 1:26). Saul and Jonathan eulogized as lovely in life and death (na.im, 2Sa 1:23). Brotherly unity celebrated as pleasant and good (na.im, Psa 133:1). Wisdom's ways characterized as pleasantness itself — an experiential ease marking the path of those who follow her (no.am, Pro 3:17). Gracious words as honey nourishing soul and body (no.am, Pro 16:24), gracious words pure before the Lord (no.am, Pro 15:26), keeping wisdom's words within bringing inward pleasantness (na.im, Pro 22:18). The soul's pleasure when wisdom enters the heart (na.em, Pro 2:10). The beloved described as truly delightful, the lover's gaze evoking warm satisfaction (na.im, Song 1:16; na.em, Song 7:6). Pleasant years as fruit of obedience (na.im, Job 36:11), pleasant inheritance measured by God (na.im, Psa 16:6), psalmist's words pleasant when vindicated (na.em, Psa 141:6). Moral courage rewarded with pleasantness (na.em, Pro 24:25). Also the inverted/hollow instances: folly's deceptive pleasantness making the destructive feel agreeable (na.em, Pro 9:17), Israel's cherished plants as hollow substitute for remembering God (na.a.man, Isa 17:10), Jerusalem's choicest pleasures devoured by the enemy (ed.nah, Jer 51:34). The missing XREF Psa 81:2 (na.im) is assigned here by musical-instrument context (pleasant song as relational worship expression).
