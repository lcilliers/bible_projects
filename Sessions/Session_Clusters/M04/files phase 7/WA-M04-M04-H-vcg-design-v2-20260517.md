## M04-H-VCG-01 — Divine Pleasure in the Son and Sovereign Will: God Doing What He Pleases

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where delight is God's sovereign, unconstrained, purposeful will acting in history. The eudokeō baptism and transfiguration declarations (Mat 3:17; 12:18; 17:5; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22; 2Pe 1:17) are the NT anchor: the Father declares deep personal approval of the Son, with delight located in the soul (psychē, Mat 12:18) — the most explicitly inner divine statement. God's good pleasure to give the kingdom (Luk 12:32) and to save through the folly of preaching (1Cor 1:21), to reveal his Son to Paul (Gal 1:16), and to invest fullness in Christ (Col 1:19) extend the sovereign-will register. The OT parallel: God does all that he pleases across all creation, pleasure and power unified (Psa 115:3; 135:6); God's settled will and pleasure stands accomplished (Isa 46:10); his purpose runs through Cyrus (Isa 44:28), against Babylon (Isa 48:14), through the servant's suffering (Isa 53:10 ×2), and drives his word to its goal (Isa 55:11); God's delight in righteousness drives glorification of his law (Isa 42:21); God directs the king's heart wherever he pleases (Pro 21:1); the king does whatever he pleases — absolute royal volition as analogy (Ecc 8:3). God's will fixed toward judgment overriding intercession (1Sa 2:25) and sovereign determination of outcomes (Jon 1:14) complete this register.

## M04-H-VCG-02 — God's Relational Delight Toward Persons: Divine Pleasure as Redemptive Affection

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where divine delight is directed toward specific persons — Israel, David, Solomon, the psalmist, the righteous — and where this relational pleasure is the operative cause of rescue, protection, exaltation, and mercy. God delighted in Israel so his delight is the hinge of their fate (Num 14:8), God's willingness to receive offerings implies desire for life not death (Judg 13:23), God delighted in David as the cause of rescue (2Sa 22:20; Psa 18:19; 41:11), God's delight directed personally toward Solomon and Israel (1Ki 10:9; 2Ch 9:8; Psa 22:8), God delights in the person's way establishing their steps (Psa 37:23), God delights in truth in the inward being and secret heart (Psa 51:6 — the most interior statement), God's pleasure NOT in sacrifice but in attentive listening (Psa 40:6; Psa 51:16/19 — delight redirected from external to internal). God delights in steadfast love and faithfulness not ritual (Psa 147:10 by negation; Jer 9:24; Hos 6:6), God's delight in repentance not death (Eze 18:23/32; 33:11), God does not delight in blood of sacrificial animals (Isa 1:11), Samuel's challenge about God's pleasure — oriented toward the willing hearing heart (1Sa 15:22). The cha.phets adj corpus: God's delight toward hesed motivating pardon (Mic 7:18); God's inner nature constitutionally opposing wickedness (Psa 5:4). Also included: the ironic delight in the coming messenger of the covenant (Mal 3:1 adj) and eudokeō relational: Paul's delighting in the Thessalonians driving self-giving (1Th 2:8; 3:1 — costly resolve born of relational care), willing pleasure in generous giving (Rom 15:26/27).

## M04-H-VCG-03 — Human Volitional Willing and Personal Desire

**Context description:** This VCG names the large corpus of cha.phets/che.phets and human eudokeō where the delight-vocabulary marks human volitional direction: inner willingness, personal desire, deep longing, willed choice, and the direction of the will toward or away from its objects. The register spans across the full OT range: Shechem's desire driving urgent action (Gen 34:19), the levirate obligation clauses where willingness is the deciding criterion (Deu 21:14; 25:7/8; Rut 3:13), Jonathan's deep personal delight in David overriding loyalty command (1Sa 19:1), David's submission to God's sovereign will over him (2Sa 15:26), the Est 6 royal-delight sequence where the king's delight becomes the criterion for public honour (Est 6:6/7/9/11; 2:14), Job's frustrated desire to contend with God (Job 9:3; 13:3), the wicked's absent will toward God (Job 21:14), the psalmist's exclusive delight in God (Psa 73:25), the blessed man delighting in commandments (Psa 112:1 ×2; 119:35; 40:8), Solomon building all he desired (1Ki 9:1), wisdom surpassing all objects of desire (Pro 3:15; 8:11), love's own sovereign readiness as proper trigger (Song 2:7; 3:5; 8:4), the eunuchs' active choosing what pleases God (Isa 56:4), the Preacher's search for words of delight (Ecc 12:10). Also the che.phets of ordinary human desire: what Solomon or the queen of Sheba desired (1Ki 5:8/9/10; 9:11; 10:13; 2Ch 9:12), David's deepest longing secured by covenant (2Sa 23:5), delight in community of the faithful (Psa 16:3), delight driving inquiry into God's works (Psa 111:2), the excellent wife's willing hands (Pro 31:13), the inner capacity draining in old age (Ecc 12:1). Human eudokeō: Paul's strong inner preference to be with the Lord (2Cor 5:8), Paul's delight in weakness for Christ (2Cor 12:10).

## M04-H-VCG-04 — Negated, Absent, and Corrupt Pleasure: Delight Withheld, Misdirected, or Condemned

**Context description:** This VCG names the M04-H verses where the delight vocabulary evidences the characteristic by negation, moral condemnation, or the damaging consequences of misdirected desire. God has no pleasure in unfaithful priests (Mal 1:10), the accusation that God delights in evildoers is refuted (Mal 2:17 ×2), Israel will be called a land of delight when favour is restored (Mal 3:12 — naming what is currently absent), the people chose what God did not delight in (Isa 65:12 ×2), the soul delights in abominations (Isa 66:3 — misdirected delight toward what God abhors), God's inner disposition set against evil choices (Isa 66:4), people take no pleasure in God's word — deep inner alienation (Jer 6:10), Coniah marked by complete rejection (Jer 22:28), Moab as broken vessel for which no one has desire (Jer 48:38), peoples who take inner pleasure in conflict (Psa 68:30 — delight directed toward war/violence). The eudokeō negations: God not pleased with Israel in the wilderness — displeasure as consequence of unfaithfulness (1Cor 10:5), delight in unrighteousness as basis for judgment (2Th 2:12), God takes no pleasure in burnt/sin offerings (Heb 10:6/8 — negated delight exposing ritual insufficiency), God's soul has no pleasure in faith collapsing into retreat (Heb 10:38). Also: people pursuing self-pleasure on Sabbath and fasting day (Isa 58:3; 58:13 che.phets — misdirected desire away from God).
