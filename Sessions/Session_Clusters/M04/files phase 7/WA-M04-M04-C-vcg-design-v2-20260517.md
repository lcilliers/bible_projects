## M04-C-VCG-01 — Synoptic and Incarnation Joy: Gladness at the Breaking-In of the Kingdom

**Context description:** This VCG names the joy that characterises the Synoptic Gospels' register: gladness at the signs of the messianic arrival, the kingdom's breaking-in, and the narrative moments of encounter with Jesus. The magi's overflowing joy at the confirming star (Mat 2:10 ×2), the women's simultaneous fear and great joy at the resurrection (Mat 28:8), the word received with joy (Mat 13:20; Mar 4:16 — both noting its shallowness), the man selling everything in his joy at the kingdom-treasure (Mat 13:44), and the two-fold "enter into your master's joy" (Mat 25:21/23) define the Matthean register. The Lukan incarnation scenes are the OT-bridging cluster: the angel's 'Rejoice' to Mary (Luk 1:28), the unborn John's physical leap of joy at Mary's greeting (Luk 1:44 agalliasis), the promise of joy at John's birth (Luk 1:14 ×3), and the angel's proclamation of great joy for all people at Christ's birth (Luk 2:10). The Lukan parables of lost-found joy (Luk 15:5/7/10/32), Zacchaeus's welcome (Luk 19:6), the disciples' eruption of praise (Luk 19:37), and the post-ascension great joy (Luk 24:52) complete the corpus. Also included: the ched.vah OT cluster (1Ch 16:27; Neh 8:10 — joy as divine-presence attribute and strength-source, which theologically prefigures the NT register; Ezr 6:16 missing XREF assigned here by term+context).

## M04-C-VCG-02 — Johannine Joy: Indwelling, Fullness, and Permanent Gladness in Christ

**Context description:** This VCG names the distinctively Johannine theology of joy: joy that is transferred from Christ to the disciples, that becomes complete and full, that is permanently secured against removal, and that is rooted in abiding relationship. John the Baptist's joy complete in hearing the bridegroom's voice and stepping aside (Joh 3:29 ×2), Abraham's forward-looking exultation at seeing Jesus' day (Joh 8:56), Jesus glad for the disciples' sake through Lazarus's death (Joh 11:15), love-grounded joy prioritising the Father's glorification (Joh 14:28), Christ's own joy to be in the disciples and their joy made full through his words (Joh 15:11), the world's temporary blind joy vs the disciples' promised transformation (Joh 16:20 ×2), the mother's birth-joy displacing anguish entirely (Joh 16:21), joy permanently secured in the heart (Joh 16:22 ×2), joy as inner fullness achieved through asking in Jesus' name (Joh 16:24), Christ's own joy fulfilled within the disciples (Joh 17:13), rejoicing at sight of the risen Lord (Joh 20:20). The harvest-co-rejoicing (Joh 4:36) is also here — communal joy in united kingdom work.

## M04-C-VCG-03 — Pauline Relational and Community Joy: Gladness in Gospel Fellowship

**Context description:** This VCG names the vast Pauline corpus of joy in community, relational bonds, and the shared life of gospel fellowship. Paul's joy for the Thessalonians so great no thanksgiving can match it (1Th 3:9 ×2; 2:19/20), Philippians as his joy and crown (Phil 4:1 ×2), joy fulfilled at community unity (Phil 2:2 ×2), shared gladness in the face of sacrifice (Phil 2:17/18 ×2 each), gladness at Epaphroditus's return (Phil 2:28/29 ×2 each), rejoicing in the Lord as constant posture (Phil 3:1 ×2; 4:4 ×2; 4:10 ×2; 1Th 5:16), joy in others' faithfulness (2Jo 4; 3Jo 3/4; 2Jo 12). The Corinthian correspondence contributes joy contingent on relational health (2Cor 2:3 ×2), joy overflowing in affliction (2Cor 7:4; 8:2), pastoral joy amplified by another's refreshment (2Cor 7:7/9/13/16; 1Cor 16:17). Acts adds the communal and apostolic joy of the early church (Act 2:46 agalliasis; 8:8/39; 11:23; 12:14; 13:48; 15:3/31; 14:17 eufrosune; 2:28 eufrosune). Also: communal empathic solidarity (Rom 12:12/15), pastoral inner gladness (Rom 16:19), Hebrews leadership-joy (Heb 13:17), and Johannine epistolary fellowship-joy (1Jo 1:4 ×2; 2Jo 12). The 14 missing XREF pairs (Phil 1:4, 1:18, 1:25, 2:2, 2:17, 2:18, 2:28, 2:29, 3:1, 4:1, 4:4, 4:10; Php variants) are assigned here as sibling-OWNER pairs.

## M04-C-VCG-04 — Spirit-given, Kingdom, and Constant Joy

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where joy is explicitly produced by the Holy Spirit, constitutes the kingdom of God, is commanded as constant regardless of circumstance, or is eschatologically grounded. Joy listed as fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22 — the most definitive statement), listed as constituent of God's kingdom produced by the Spirit (Rom 14:17), God filling believers with joy through believing empowered by the Spirit (Rom 15:13), joy given by the Holy Spirit alongside affliction when the word is embraced (1Th 1:6), joy accompanying endurance and patience (Col 1:11), rejoicing always commanded without exception (1Th 5:16), divinely-conferred gladness as reward for loving righteousness (Heb 1:9 agalliasis), the eschatological joy at the final presentation of the blameless (Jude 24 agalliasis), the corporate eschatological rejoicing at the Lamb's marriage (Rev 19:7). The euthumeo cheerfulness grounded in divine assurance (Act 27:22/25; Jam 5:13) belongs here — grounded-in-promise rather than circumstance-dependent.

## M04-C-VCG-05 — Suffering-Paradox, Corrupt, and Morally Complex Joy

**Context description:** This VCG names the chairo/chara verses in M04-C that carry a counter-intuitive, morally complex, or paradoxical register. The commanded joy in persecution (Mat 5:12; Luk 6:23), the redirected joy from spiritual power to heavenly register (Luk 10:20), the people's joy at witnessing Jesus' deeds (Luk 13:17), the disciples' paradoxical rejoicing as constant amid outward sorrow (2Cor 6:10), the joyful acceptance of property loss (Heb 10:34), joy set before Jesus sustaining the cross (Heb 12:2), discipline's immediate absence of joy (Heb 12:11), trials counted as pure joy requiring deliberate reorientation (Jam 1:2), joy surrendered in genuine repentance (Jam 4:9), the inexpressible glory-filled joy of believing in the unseen Christ (1Pe 1:8), rejoicing in present suffering oriented toward future glory (1Pe 4:13). Also the morally dark instances: chief priests' corrupt joy at betrayal (Mar 14:11; Luk 22:5), Herod's self-serving gladness (Luk 23:8), earth-dwellers' perverse rejoicing at prophets' deaths (Rev 11:10).
