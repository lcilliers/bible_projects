## M04-BOUNDARY-VCG-01 — Aggregating VCG: All BOUNDARY Term Verses

**Context description:** This is a structural holding VCG covering all 322 verses of the 15 BOUNDARY terms. These terms were designated BOUNDARY at Phase 3 because their corpora did not clearly confirm M04 alignment, or the corpus was too small, too mixed, or analytically uncertain to render a confident STAYS verdict at that stage. The 15 BOUNDARY terms span two broad areas: (1) the **thankfulness/gratitude cluster** (eucharistia 15; eucharistos 1) — gratitude as a practice close to joy but primarily directed toward God as worship-act and communal orientation; (2) the **wonder-with-dread cluster** (thambeō 3; thauma 2; thaumazō 5) — astonishment that shades toward fear or deception rather than joyful awe; (3) the **goodness/pleasant corpus** (H2896A tov 230 — the largest, covering goodness across moral, evaluative, comparative, and relational registers far exceeding M04's joy characteristic) and (4) the **cultic pleasing-aroma corpus** (H5207 ni.cho.ach 43 — priestly sacrificial formula); (5) smaller single-verse or edge-case terms: blessedness (makarismos 3), erotic-attraction dominant (che.med 5), luxury/sensory (ta.a.nug 5; a.nog dainty 3), predatory exultation (a.li.tsut 1), illicit/denied pleasure (a.las 2), and te.mah (empty corpus). No characteristic-bearing analysis is conducted at Phase 7. Researcher disposition at Phase 12 will resolve term placements.

The 19 XREF rows that appear in the BOUNDARY section of the meanings report but whose OWNERs are assigned to other sub-groups (M04-A, C, D, J) are excluded from this VCG — they are already counted in their primary sub-groups. The 322 vc_ids listed below are the BOUNDARY-exclusive members.

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 32636 — Gen 8:21 — ni.cho.ach  
**Rationale:** "The pleasing aroma of Noah's offering registers in God's inner being as soothing — it moves him to resolve in his heart to relent from judgment, showing the offering's power to evoke divine favor." Gen 8:21 is the most analytically clear inner-being verse in the BOUNDARY corpus — naming God's inner resolve explicitly — making it a structurally clean anchor without requiring characteristic-bearing analysis.
