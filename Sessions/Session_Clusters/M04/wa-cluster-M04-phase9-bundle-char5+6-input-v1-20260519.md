## §2 — Characteristic definitions + sub-groups (per batch)

### §2.A — Characteristic 5 (Pleasure)

**Characteristic_id:** 5
**Definition:**

> The inner-being experience of pleasantness — the felt quality of agreeableness received from persons, relationships, creation, and material or sensory experience. Pleasure is the soul's positive receptive response to what is pleasant. Less directed than Delight (which focuses on a specific treasured object) and less active than Joy — Pleasure is the inner being's sense of agreeableness and satisfying ease in what surrounds it.

**Constituent sub-groups:** 2
**Total verses:** ~96

#### M04-J — Pleasantness and Relational Delight: The Agreeableness of Persons, Bonds, and Creation

**core_description:** Experiential register of pleasantness as a quality of what is agreeable, beautiful, and nourishing to the inner person. Receptive rather than exultant character. Spans divine beauty (no.am — the Lord's own being), relational warmth (na.em — Jonathan's companionship in the soul), communal harmony (na.im — brotherly unity), and gracious speech (no.am — honey to soul and body).

**Role under Pleasure:** The relational qualifier — pleasantness as the quality of what is agreeable in persons, bonds, speech, and creation.

**Verses in this sub-group:** 29

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-J-VCG-02` (20 verses) — Pleasantness of persons, speech, wisdom, and human bonds: experiential quality of what is agreeable in relationships and creation.
- `M04-J-VCG-01` (9 verses) — Divine pleasantness and worship pleasantness: no.am/na.im as attribute of God's character, covenant favour, and the agreeableness of worship.

### §2.B — Characteristic 6 (Wonder)

**Characteristic_id:** 6
**Definition:**

> The inner state of astonishment and awe before what exceeds ordinary comprehension — the soul arrested and overwhelmed by what it cannot fully grasp. Wonder is the inner being stilled rather than surging: it confronts what is too great to simply rejoice at. Includes wonder as proclamation (declaring the marvellous), wonder as incomprehensibility (too great to comprehend), and wonder as dark astonishment (appalling awe at what is terrible or wrong). Distinguished from Exultation by its arrested, contemplative character; from Joy by its perceptual-receptive rather than participatory character.

**Constituent sub-groups:** 1
**Total verses:** ~84

#### M04-I — Wonder at God's Marvellous Works: Awe Before the Extraordinary

**core_description:** Inner response of awe, marvel, and astonishment directed at God's extraordinary deeds, creative works, and the incomprehensibility of his ways. The contemplative and perceptual character distinguishes this from gladness. Includes wonder-as-proclamation, wonder-as-incomprehensibility, and wonder-as-dark-astonishment.

**Role under Wonder:** The primary register — wonder at God's extraordinary works, their incomprehensibility, and dark astonishment at what is appalling.

**Verses in this sub-group:** 84

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-I-VCG-02` (13 verses) — Wonder as incomprehensibility: too-difficult, beyond-reach, impossible barrier — human cognitive limits and affirmation of God's limitless power.
- `M04-I-VCG-01` (61 verses) — Wonder at God's marvellous works: proclaimed, remembered, thanked — the dominant register of M04-I.
- `M04-I-VCG-03` (10 verses) — Wonder inverted: dark astonishment, dread, and appalling shock — extraordinary vocabulary producing horror rather than joyful awe.
