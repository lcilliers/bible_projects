## T7 — Lexical, Literary, and Human Science Analysis

**T7.1.1 — What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms — and what do their root meanings reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Primary terms: (1) **na.im** (H5273A) — pleasant, agreeable; root na.em — to be pleasant, comfortable, agreeable; the quality of what is fitting and agreeable to the inner sense; the root carries gentleness and ease without harshness; (2) **na.em** (H5276) — to be/experience pleasant; the verbal experience of pleasantness; (3) **no.am** (H5278) — pleasantness, beauty, graciousness; the noun naming the quality: God's own beauty (Psa 27:4), wisdom's character (Pro 3:17); (4) **na.a.man** (H5282) — pleasant (adj): Isa 17:10's "pleasant plants"; (5) **ni.cho.ach** (H5207) — soothing, pleasing (aroma); root nuach — to rest, settle, give rest; the quality of what causes to settle, calm, and rest; the soothing quality of the worship-aroma that causes God's regard to settle into receptive acceptance; (6) **a.nog** (H6026) — to be tender, delicate, to luxuriate; (7) **a.nog adj** (H6028) — tender, delicate; (8) **a.las** (H5965) — to exult, delight (in pleasure); (9) **ta.a.nug** (H8588) — daintiness, luxury; (10) **ed.nah** (H5730B) — pleasure, luxury, delight; (11) **tov** (H2896A) in the comparative register; (12) **thambeō** (G2284) — to be amazed (Mar 10:24). Root synthesis: the na.em root names gentle agreeableness; the nuach root of ni.cho.ach names settling/rest as the mechanism of worship-Pleasure; a.nog/ta.a.nug name delicate, luxuriant ease; a.las names exultant pleasure.

---

**T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term — and what does that range reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — **na.im** (H5273A) functions primarily as an **adjective** — it attributes the quality of pleasantness to persons, places, and circumstances (Psa 133:1 — "pleasant it is"; Job 36:11 — "years in pleasantness"; Song 1:16 — "truly delightful"). **na.em** (H5276) functions as a **verb** (2Sa 1:26 — "you have been pleasant to me"; Pro 2:10 — "knowledge will be pleasant"; Pro 9:17 — "bread eaten in secret is pleasant"). **no.am** (H5278) functions as a **noun** (Psa 27:4 — "the beauty of the Lord"; Pro 3:17 — "ways of pleasantness"). The adjectival-verbal-nominal spread of the na.em root covers: the quality itself (no.am), the attribution of that quality to something (na.im), and the experience of that quality by a person (na.em). This grammatical completeness reveals Pleasure as both an objective quality of what is pleasant and a subjective experience of pleasantness felt by the inner person.

---

**T7.1.3 — What is the semantic range of the primary term?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — na.im/na.em/no.am ranges across: (1) **Divine character** — Psa 27:4 (God's own beauty/pleasantness); (2) **Relational warmth** — 2Sa 1:26 (Jonathan's pleasantness to David); Psa 133:1 (brotherly unity's pleasantness); (3) **Wisdom's character** — Pro 3:17 (wisdom's ways as pleasantness); (4) **Speech** — Pro 15:26; 16:24 (gracious words as pleasant); (5) **Praise** — Psa 135:3; 147:1 (praise as pleasant); (6) **Creation** — Psa 16:6 (pleasant places); (7) **Negative** — Pro 9:17 (stolen bread appearing pleasant — the only morally negative instance within this vocabulary). The semantic range is primarily positive-relational and aesthetic, with the negative instance (Pro 9:17) being the exception that proves the rule.

**T7.3.1 — Which human science framework is most useful as an interpretive lens?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Two frameworks: (1) **Aesthetics** — the philosophical study of beauty, taste, and sensory experience. Psa 27:4's "gaze upon the beauty of the Lord" and Song of Songs' celebration of the beloved's beauty engage aesthetics directly: the inner capacity for aesthetic appreciation, the experience of beauty as a distinctive form of pleasant inner response that is not merely preference but recognition of genuine quality; (2) **Positive psychology** — particularly Martin Seligman's PERMA model (Positive Emotions, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement) and the study of hedonia (pleasurable experience) vs. eudaimonia (flourishing). The M04-K-VCG-03 wisdom-comparatives (integrity with modesty > corrupt abundance) map directly onto positive psychology's finding that hedonic adaptation (the treadmill of seeking more pleasure) produces less wellbeing than eudaimonic engagement with genuinely meaningful life.

**T7.3.2 — Where the human science framework illuminates the verse evidence — what does it reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Aesthetics illuminates Psa 27:4: the "gaze upon the beauty of the Lord" is not passive looking but the active aesthetic-receptive engagement with a supremely beautiful object — what aesthetics calls the contemplative encounter with beauty. This illuminates why Pleasure in God's beauty requires "dwelling" (sustained presence) rather than a glance — genuine aesthetic experience of the most beautiful object requires time and attention. Positive psychology illuminates M04-K-VCG-03: the wisdom literature's consistent preference for modest-integrity-Pleasure over corrupt-abundance-Pleasure anticipates the research finding that relational and virtue-based wellbeing produces more durable positive experience than material wealth. Pro 15:17's "dinner of herbs where love is" maps onto positive psychology's finding that the quality of relational context determines the pleasantness of material experience more than the quality of the material itself.
