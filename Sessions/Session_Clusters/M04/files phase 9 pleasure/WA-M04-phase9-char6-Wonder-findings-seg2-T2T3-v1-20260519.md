## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-6]** S — No verse in the M04-I corpus explicitly names the human spirit (ruach/pneuma) as the constitutional seat of Wonder. The primary constitutional locations are the eyes/perception, the heart, and the mouth (in proclamation). Spirit-level location is not evidenced for this characteristic.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-6]** S — Not evidenced. Wonder arises at the cognitive-perceptive boundary — when what is encountered exceeds the mind's categories. This is a soul-level perceptive-cognitive event rather than a Spirit-level impartation. The absent Spirit-level evidence is consistent with Wonder's characteristically creaturely nature: it does not require Spirit-production but is the natural inner response of the finite being encountering the infinite.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does the absence of spirit-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The absence of spirit-level location reveals Wonder as a naturally-arising creaturely capacity — it does not require special Spirit-production to occur; the finite being naturally experiences Wonder when genuinely encountering what exceeds its comprehension. This distinguishes Wonder from Suffering-Joy (Spirit-produced) and from the highest registers of Joy (Spirit-filled). Wonder is the characteristic most available to unregenerate persons as well as the redeemed — any person who genuinely encounters the extraordinary will experience some form of Wonder. Neh 9:17's failure ("did not consider your wondrous works") is a failure of attention, not a Spirit-absence.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** S — The evidence is silent on spirit-level location for Wonder across all three VCGs.

---

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Partially and indirectly: (1) **Psa 119:129** — "your testimonies are wonderful; therefore my soul keeps them" — the soul is named as the faculty that responds to Wonder by keeping the testimonies; Wonder in God's word moves the soul to obedience; (2) **Psa 139:14** — "I praise you, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made; wonderful are your works; my soul knows it very well" — the soul explicitly knows the wonderful works; (3) The overall Psalmic proclamatory corpus (VCG-01) is addressed by the whole self, with the soul as the implicit experiential centre. Soul-level location is evidenced through the soul's response to wonder (keeping testimonies; knowing wonders well) rather than as a named seat.

---

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Psa 119:129's "my soul keeps them" in response to the testimonies being wonderful reveals that Wonder in God's word produces a soul-level obedience response — the wonderfulness of the testimonies motivates the soul to hold to them. This is analytically distinctive: Wonder is the cognitive-perceptive response, and the soul's keeping is the volitional consequence. Psa 139:14's "my soul knows it very well" places the knowing of Wonder at the soul level — an intimate, deeply personal knowing of God's wondrous works.

---

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Soul-level location is evidenced at Psa 119:129 and 139:14; not fully silent.

---

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 9:1** — "I will give thanks to the Lord with my whole heart; I will recount all of your wonderful deeds" — heart named as the seat of the whole-hearted thanksgiving that Wonder produces; (2) **Gen 8:21** (cross-assigned in M04-K but paradigmatic) — God "said in his heart" after smelling the pleasing aroma — while this is Pleasure territory, the pattern of inner resolve following receptive encounter is analogous; (3) **Psa 131:1** — "my heart is not lifted up; my eyes are not raised too high; I do not occupy myself with things too great and too marvellous for me" — the heart as the seat of the humble posture that rightly orients toward Wonder.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Psa 9:1's "whole heart" in response to Wonder's proclamation shows that genuine Wonder produces whole-hearted engagement — the heart is fully involved in the thanksgiving that Wonder overflows into. Psa 131:1's "my heart is not lifted up" shows that the rightly-ordered heart is the condition for genuine Wonder: the humble heart does not overreach. Heart-location reveals Wonder as requiring heart-humility as an enabling condition and producing heart-wholeness as an effect.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Heart-location is evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes — this is Wonder's primary constitutional location in its cognitive mode: (1) **Job 37:5** ("great things that we cannot comprehend") — mental comprehension as the cognitive faculty that Wonder encounters at its limits; (2) **Job 42:3** ("things too wonderful for me, which I did not know") — knowledge as the cognitive capacity that Wonder exceeds; (3) **Psa 119:27** ("make me understand the way of your precepts, and I will meditate on your wondrous works") — understanding as the prerequisite for meditating on wonders; (4) **Psa 131:1** ("I do not occupy myself with things too great and too marvellous for me") — the mind deliberately restraining itself from overreach into the incomprehensible; (5) **Isa 29:14** ("the wisdom of their wise men shall perish, and the discernment of their discerning men shall be hidden") — human wisdom and discernment (cognitive faculties) eclipsed by God's wonders.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Mind-location reveals Wonder as the characteristic that specifically arises at the cognitive boundary — it is the response when the mind's comprehension reaches its limit. This makes Wonder unique in M04: it is the only characteristic that is constitutively defined by the cognitive faculty's encounter with what exceeds it. The mind is both the faculty that encounters Wonder (by trying to comprehend what is incomprehensible) and the faculty that is formed by Wonder (into humility and sustained contemplation). Isa 29:14 shows that human wisdom/discernment can actually become an obstacle to Wonder when it becomes presumptuous — the formed mind must be humble to remain genuinely wonder-capable.

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Mind-location is the most prominently evidenced constitutional location for Wonder; not silent.

---

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: **the eyes** — Psa 118:23 ("it is marvellous in our eyes") — the visual perception organ as the specific site of Wonder-reception; Psa 119:18 ("open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things") — the eyes as the faculty seeking to receive Wonder; **the mouth** — Psa 9:1 ("I will recount"); Psa 78:4 ("tell to the coming generation") — the mouth as the expressive organ of proclaimed Wonder.

---

**T2.5.2 — What does the eyes/mouth-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — **Eyes**: Wonder in its VCG-01 register is specifically perceptual — it requires seeing (Psa 118:23 — "marvellous in our eyes") and the petition to see (Psa 119:18 — "open my eyes"). This visual emphasis is unique to Wonder among M04 characteristics. Wonder begins with a perceptive encounter. **Mouth**: Wonder consistently produces proclamation — the mouth as the organ through which inner Wonder overflows into speech. The proclamatory character of Wonder (tell of, sing of, declare, recount) is its most distinctive expressive mode, consistently using the mouth.

---

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Eyes and mouth as Wonder-locations are evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Eyes** — Psa 118:23; 119:18 — the primary perceptive organ of Wonder; (2) **Mouth** — the proclamatory dimension (tell, declare, sing, recount) — 1Ch 16:9, 24; Psa 9:1; 26:7; 78:4 — the expressive organ; (3) **Hands** — 2Ch 26:15 (Uzziah's machines and his fame spreading because "he was marvellously helped") — the body's productive capacity enabled by wonder-invoking divine assistance; (4) **The whole person prostrating** — Mar 10:32 (the amazed disciples following Jesus toward Jerusalem) implies bodily engagement alongside the inner state.

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **Eyes** — the link is **constitutive**: Wonder at God's acts begins with seeing (Psa 118:23 — "it is marvellous in our eyes") and Psa 119:18 asks for opened eyes specifically to behold wonders. The eyes are not merely metaphorical — genuine Wonder requires genuine perceptual encounter. (2) **Mouth** — the link is **expressive**: Wonder consistently overflows through speech into proclamation. The mouth-link reveals that Wonder is constitutively oral-doxological — it characteristically moves from inner astonishment to voiced declaration. The inner state seeks outward expression through the body's speech organ.

---

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Body-part links (eyes, mouth) are evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — **External-to-inner-to-outward**: God's marvellous work (external) → perceived by the eyes (perceptive body) → wonder arises in the mind/soul (inner) → proclaimed through the mouth (expressive body). The direction is: external encounter → inner arrest → outward proclamation. This is a three-stage sequence: perception → astonishment → expression.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The external-to-inner-to-outward direction reveals Wonder as inherently relational in its full expression: it is not self-contained but moves from encounter (external) through inner astonishment to communal proclamation (outward). The person who genuinely wonders cannot simply keep it internal — it moves through the mouth into declaration. This also explains why Wonder's failure (Neh 9:17 — "not mindful") is both an inner failure (not registering) and a communal failure (not declaring to others).

---

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Three-stage body links are evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Implied at the cognitive-dispositional level: (1) **Psa 71:17** ("from my youth you have taught me, and I still proclaim your wondrous deeds") — a lifetime of sustained Wonder has produced a person whose defining inner-expressive orientation is the proclamation of divine wonders; the sustained deposit is a proclamatory vocation; (2) **Psa 131:1**'s posture of not-overreaching implies a formed character of epistemic humility as the deposit of sustained appropriate Wonder; (3) **The generational-transmission pattern** (Psa 78:4 — "we will not hide them from their children") — sustained Wonder produces the disposition to pass on wonder-memory to the next generation; this is a formative cognitive deposit.

---

**T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Supporting: Psa 71:17 (lifelong proclamatory formation); Psa 131:1 (formed epistemic humility); Psa 78:4 (generational transmission disposition). No direct contradicting evidence.

---

**T2.8.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Constitutional deposit at the cognitive-dispositional level is implied; not silent.

---

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Multiple origins: (1) **From God's acts** — the dominant origin: Wonder arises in response to God's marvellous works (VCG-01 throughout — Exo 3:20; 15:11; 34:10; Psa corpus); (2) **From encountering the limit of comprehension** — VCG-02's pa.la in the "too hard/wonderful" register: Wonder at what exceeds human cognitive reach originates at the boundary of human understanding (Job 42:3; Jer 32:17, 27); (3) **From perceptive encounter with the extraordinary** — Mar 1:27; 10:24 (thambeō at Jesus' authoritative teaching/advance) — Wonder originates from direct encounter with a person or event that exceeds ordinary categories; (4) **From deliberate remembrance** — Psa 77:11; 105:5; 111:4 — Wonder re-generated through the deliberate inner act of calling to memory God's past wonders; this is the only instance where Wonder is self-originated (through voluntary memory-act).

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Multiple — divine acts, cognitive-limit encounters, direct perceptive encounter, and deliberate memory all originate Wonder through different pathways.

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: worship/historical-memory contexts → originates from deliberate remembrance of God's acts; prophetic-judgment contexts → originates from confronting what God will do that is beyond current comprehension (Isa 29:14 — wonder upon wonder); direct encounter contexts (Mark) → originates from immediate perceptive encounter with Jesus; epistemological-limit contexts → originates at the boundary of human comprehension (Job 42:3; Psa 131:1).

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: Eyes (perceptive body) → mind/inner being (cognitive astonishment) → soul (keeps the testimonies — Psa 119:129) → heart (whole-hearted thanksgiving — Psa 9:1) → mouth (proclaimed Wonder — 1Ch 16:9). The movement is perception-inward-outward in sequence.

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the sequence or pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The movement pattern is: perceptive encounter → cognitive arrest (Wonder arises) → inner-being response (soul keeps, heart thanks) → outward proclamation (mouth declares). This is the full Wonder-sequence, and it is the most consistently complete sequential pattern in M04: every genuine Wonder begins in perceptive encounter and ends in proclaimed declaration. The sequence's completeness reveals Wonder as a fully integrating characteristic — it unites the perceptive, cognitive, affective, and expressive dimensions of the person in a single responsive arc.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Constitutional movement across perception → cognition → soul/heart → mouth is evidenced; not silent.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes — more explicitly than any other M04 characteristic in the visual-perceptive mode: (1) **"Marvellous in our eyes"** (Psa 118:23) — sight as the perceptive organ of Wonder; (2) **"Open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things"** (Psa 119:18) — petition for perceptive opening; (3) **"They saw the deeds of the Lord, his wondrous works in the deep"** (Psa 107:24) — Wonder grounded in direct visual perceptive encounter; (4) **"Stop and consider the wondrous works of God"** (Job 37:14) — the imperative to attend perceptively; (5) **"Manoah and his wife were watching"** (Judg 13:19) — wonder-producing encounter witnessed perceptively; (6) **Mar 1:27** (thambeō at teaching heard and seen); **Mar 10:32** (seeing Jesus walk ahead in determination).

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder deepens perception: Psa 119:18's "open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things out of your law" shows that divine opening of perception enables Wonder; Psa 119:27's "make me understand… and I will meditate on your wondrous works" shows that Wonder-capacity grows with understanding. Wonder also depends on perception — its failure (Neh 9:17 — "not mindful") is a failure to perceive or register what has been seen. Wonder-failure is perceptive failure.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is the characteristic most directly dependent on and productive of genuine perception. It requires the person to genuinely see what is happening (Psa 107:24 — "they saw the deeds of the Lord") and it deepens the person's capacity to see (Psa 119:18 — open my eyes to behold). The pattern reveals perception-training as essential to Wonder-formation: communities that regularly tell of God's wondrous works (VCG-01 proclamatory tradition) are training the perceptive capacity of their members to see God's acts as extraordinary.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes — this is Wonder's primary faculty, as established at T1.8: (1) **"Things we cannot comprehend"** (Job 37:5) — comprehension as the cognitive faculty Wonder presses against; (2) **"Things too wonderful for me, which I did not know"** (Job 42:3) — knowledge as the measure of Wonder; (3) **"Make me understand… I will meditate on your wondrous works"** (Psa 119:27) — understanding enabling meditative engagement with Wonder; (4) **"The wisdom of their wise men shall perish"** (Isa 29:14) — human cognitive accumulation eclipsed by God's wonders; (5) **"Do you know the balancings of the clouds?"** (Job 37:16) — rhetorical challenge to human knowledge producing Wonder-recognition.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder simultaneously deepens and disciplines cognition: it deepens by motivating sustained meditation and inquiry into God's acts (Psa 119:27; 145:5); it disciplines by restraining overreach (Psa 131:1 — I do not occupy myself with things too marvellous for me). Wonder-as-epistemological-humility is the most distinctive cognitive-Wonder interaction: the person in genuine Wonder is cognitively active (meditating, considering, studying) but not overconfident (acknowledging the limits of comprehension).

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is the most cognitively-rich M04 characteristic in the perceptive-contemplative mode. Unlike Delight (which is cognitively engaged in wisdom evaluation) or Gladness (which is cognitively evaluative of moral goodness), Wonder's cognitive engagement is specifically at the boundary of comprehension — it is the response when cognition reaches its limit. The pattern reveals Wonder as the characteristic most directly involving the mind in a posture of humble but active engagement with what exceeds it.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes — memory is Wonder's second most prominent faculty: (1) **"Remember the wondrous works"** (1Ch 16:12; Psa 105:5; 111:4) — deliberate memory-engagement as a core Wonder-practice; (2) **"The wonders of old"** (Psa 77:11) — retrospective memory of past wonders as the inner strategy for sustaining faith through present distress; (3) **"Did not consider your wondrous works"** (Neh 9:17; Psa 106:7) — memory-failure as Wonder-failure; (4) **"He has caused his wondrous works to be remembered"** (Psa 111:4) — God himself ensuring his wonders remain in memory; (5) **"Do not hide them from their children"** (Psa 78:4) — generational memory-transmission as the Wonder-preserving act.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder deeply engages and strengthens memory: the entire VCG-01 proclamatory tradition is a memory-practice — the community that tells of God's wondrous works is simultaneously training and rehearsing its collective memory. God's own act of causing his wonders to be remembered (Psa 111:4) shows Wonder as designed to be memory-embedded. Wonder-failure is consistently named as a memory-failure (Neh 9:17 — "not mindful"; Psa 78:11 — "forgot his works").

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder and memory are inseparably linked in the VCG-01 corpus: Wonder without memory cannot sustain; memory without Wonder becomes mere historical knowledge. The Tell-Remember-Meditate triad (1Ch 16:9, 12; Psa 9:1; 77:11; 105:2, 5; 119:27) shows Wonder as a memory-embedded characteristic whose formation and sustaining requires deliberate memorial practice. This is why the VCG-01 liturgical-proclamatory corpus is so extensive (61 verses) — the entire Psalmic wonder-tradition is a memory-forming practice.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes, in the arrest-astonishment register: (1) **thambeō** (Mar 1:27; 10:24; 10:32) — the most affectively intense Wonder term: the disciples are seized with astonishment (an inner feeling state of overwhelming surprise); (2) **"I marvelled greatly"** (Rev 17:6) — John's intense inner feeling-response to the vision; (3) **VCG-03's dark astonishment** — Job 10:16 (wonders worked against Job); Gal 1:6 ("I am astonished") — Paul's troubled inner affective state; (4) **"Fearfully and wonderfully made"** (Psa 139:14) — the awe-component of Wonder at one's own creation is affective.

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder deepens positive affect toward God: VCG-01's wonder-at-God's-acts consistently produces thanksgiving, praise, and the joyful affect of wholehearted worship (Psa 9:1 — "whole heart"; 31:21 — "blessed be the Lord"). Dark Wonder (VCG-03) produces negative affect: Job 10:16's wonders-against-Job produce distress; Dan 11:36's astonishing blasphemy produces appalled dread; Gal 1:6's astonishment is troubled dismay. Wonder can deepen either positive or negative affect depending on its object.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder's affective dimension is secondary to its cognitive dimension but is real: the arrested quality of Wonder is felt as well as known. The pattern reveals that Wonder is the characteristic where cognitive encounter and affective arrest are most closely integrated — the mind cannot comprehend, and the affect registers the incomprehension as astonishment. The two dimensions (cognitive limit + affective arrest) are constitutive of genuine Wonder.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **The Psalmic proclamatory tradition** — the entire VCG-01 wonder-proclamation corpus represents creative acts: songs, psalms, and hymns generated from Wonder at God's acts (Exo 15:11's Song of the Sea is a primary creative-Wonder text; Psa 98:1 — "sing a new song" because "he has done marvellous things"); (2) **Psa 40:5** ("yet they are more than can be told") — Wonder's exceeding quality motivating the attempt at ever-new creative expression; (3) **2Ch 2:9** (the temple as "great and wonderful") — the creative work of the temple as wonder-architecture; (4) **Isa 9:6** ("Wonderful Counselor") — the prophetic creative act of naming the coming child's wonder-quality.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder enables and generates creativity: the inexhaustibility of God's wonders (Psa 40:5 — "more than can be told") motivates continued creative expression in new attempts to capture what cannot be fully told. The "new song" (Psa 98:1; 96:1) — the creative response to each new wonder — shows Wonder as the generative source of fresh creative expression. Wonder cannot be adequately expressed in existing forms, so it generates new ones.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is creatively generative specifically because its object (God's incomprehensible acts) can never be fully expressed — the inexhaustibility of the wonder-object perpetually motivates new creative attempts. The Psalmic tradition's breadth and variety (61 verses across many different psalms all proclaiming God's wondrous works) is itself the evidence of this creative generativity: each poet's encounter with divine wonders produces a new creative expression.

---

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Partially: (1) **Deliberate memory-act** — "remember the wondrous works" (1Ch 16:12; Psa 105:5) — the act of deliberate remembrance is volitional; Wonder's sustaining requires the will's choice to return to and rehearse God's acts; (2) **Proclamatory act** — "tell of all his wondrous works" (1Ch 16:9) — the act of telling/proclaiming is volitional; (3) **Restraint act** — Psa 131:1 ("I do not occupy myself with things too marvellous for me") — the volitional act of restraining oneself from overreach; (4) **Consecration** — Jos 3:5 ("consecrate yourselves") — the volitional moral preparation for receiving wonder.

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder-at-God enables volition by motivating action: Psa 119:129 ("your testimonies are wonderful; therefore my soul keeps them") — Wonder in God's word motivating the volitional act of keeping the testimonies; the wonderfulness of God's commands is what motivates obedience. Wonder-failure (Neh 9:17) leads to volitional rebellion — the absence of wonder removes the motivational ground for obedience.

---

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Psa 119:129's "wonderful… therefore my soul keeps them" is the most analytically significant volition-Wonder connection: Wonder in the testimonies motivates obedience. Wonder provides the motivational ground for volition: the person who is genuinely arrested by the wonderfulness of God's commands has a deep motivational reason to keep them beyond mere duty.

---

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Proclamatory agency** — "declare his glory among the nations, his marvellous works" (1Ch 16:24; Psa 96:3) — active going-out to declare; (2) **Generational-transmission agency** — "we will not hide them from their children, but tell to the coming generation" (Psa 78:4) — active intergenerational teaching; (3) **Jos 3:5** — Israel's active consecration in anticipation of God's wonders; (4) **Mar 1:27** — the crowd's immediately acting to question among themselves following the wonder-seizure; (5) **2Ch 26:15** — Uzziah being "marvellously helped" enabling his productive agency (making machines, defending the city).

---

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder in God enables agency through the missionary-proclamatory impulse: the person who has genuinely encountered divine wonders is driven to declare them (1Ch 16:24; Psa 96:3). The generational-transmission imperative (Psa 78:4) shows Wonder as agentively demanding — it cannot be held privately. Uzziah's being marvellously helped (2Ch 26:15) shows divine wonder-power enabling productive human agency.

---

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is the characteristic most agentively directed toward proclamation and teaching: the primary action it generates is not service or justice (Delight's obedience modes) but declaration — going out to tell, proclaim, sing, and pass on. Wonder's agency is inherently communicative and missional.

---

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Partially: (1) **Psa 118:23** ("this is the Lord's doing; it is marvellous in our eyes") — the evaluative act of recognising God as the agent of the marvellous is a form of moral-theological evaluation; (2) **Gal 1:6** — Paul's astonished moral evaluation of the Galatians' theological defection; his thauma is simultaneously a moral judgment that something wrong has happened; (3) **Rev 17:7** — the angel's "why do you marvel?" to John implies that some marvelling requires moral-evaluative redirection — not everything that produces Wonder deserves it; (4) **Psa 9:1** ("wonderful deeds" in the context of God's just judgment on the wicked — Psa 9:4–6) — Wonder at God's just judgments involves moral evaluation.

---

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder at God's justice (Psa 9) deepens moral evaluation: recognising God's acts as marvellous includes recognising that he acts with justice, which deepens the appreciation of moral reality. Rev 17:7's redirected wonder shows that Wonder can be applied inappropriately (marvelling at evil) — the angel's "why do you marvel?" is a moral-evaluative correction to misapplied Wonder. Genuine Wonder at the right object deepens moral evaluation; misapplied Wonder bypasses it.

---

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder and moral evaluation are connected through the right identification of what is genuinely marvellous: the person who wonders at the right things (God's extraordinary justice, mercy, and power) has a rightly calibrated moral-evaluative faculty; the person who wonders at the wrong things (the beast's apparent resurrection — Rev 13:3; the harlot's power — Rev 17:6) has a distorted one.

---

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-6]** S — The M04-I corpus does not directly evidence Wonder engaging the conscience (the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction). Job 42:3's "things too wonderful for me" comes after Job's confrontation with God in the whirlwind; the conscience may have been engaged prior to Wonder in that sequence, but Wonder itself is the response to the incomprehensible divine display rather than the conscience's direct engagement.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-6]** S — Not directly evidenced. Adjacent: the VCG-03 dark Wonder (astonishment at evil — Gal 1:6's troubled astonishment at doctrinal defection) operates alongside conscience in moral judgment, but Wonder itself is the astonishment response rather than the conscience's accusation. Not evidenced.

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes, indirectly: (1) **Psa 119:129** — "your testimonies are wonderful; therefore my soul keeps them" — the wonderfulness of the testimonies producing conscientious law-keeping; (2) **Jos 3:5** — "consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the Lord will do wonders" — the conscientious moral preparation as the condition for receiving Wonder; (3) **Num 28:2** (adjacent — careful offering of pleasing aroma) — conscientious worship-practice producing the worship-Pleasure relationship; the implied parallel: Wonder at God's acts motivating conscientious covenant-faithfulness.

**T3.10.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder enables conscientiousness by providing motivational grounding: Psa 119:129's "wonderful… therefore my soul keeps them" is the clearest evidence — the wonderfulness of God's word provides the deep motivational why for conscientious obedience. Conversely, Wonder-absence (Neh 9:17 — "not mindful of the wonders") directly produced covenant-unfaithfulness — the absence of Wonder removed the motivational ground for conscientious covenant-keeping.

**T3.10.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is the motivational ground for conscientiousness: the person who is genuinely arrested by the wonderfulness of God's commands, acts, and character has the deepest possible motivation for conscientious obedience. Psa 119:129's structure (Wonder → keeping) is analytically precise: Wonder precedes and produces conscientiousness as its natural fruit.

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes, in multiple dimensions: (1) **Communal proclamation** — the VCG-01 corpus is overwhelmingly communal: "we will not hide them from their children" (Psa 78:4); "declare his glory among the nations" (1Ch 16:24; Psa 96:3); "let the heavens praise your wonders, O Lord" (Psa 89:5) — Wonder is proclaimed in community, not held privately; (2) **Intergenerational relationship** — Psa 78:4's generational transmission is a relational act; (3) **Witness-relationship** — Exo 34:10 ("all the people among whom you are shall see the work of the Lord") — Wonder creates a witness-relationship with observers; (4) **2Sa 1:26** (Jonathan's love as "extraordinary" — pa.la) — Wonder as a quality of deep human relational experience (the extraordinary love that exceeded normal categories is named by the Wonder-term).

---

**T3.11.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder deepens relational capacity in two registers: (1) **Community-formation** — shared Wonder creates community; the people who together remember and proclaim God's marvellous works are bound by that shared wonder-memory (Psa 78:4); (2) **Depth of relational experience** — 2Sa 1:26's use of pa.la for Jonathan's extraordinary love shows Wonder as the capacity to recognise and be astonished by genuine extraordinary depth in human relationships.

**T3.11.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is inherently communal in its primary register: the VCG-01 proclamatory tradition is a community-sustaining practice. The pattern reveals that Wonder cannot remain private — its natural expression is communal declaration. This makes Wonder the most communally-directed characteristic in M04: its characteristic expressive mode is not personal prayer (like Delight) or individual formation (like Gladness through wisdom) but communal proclamation across generational boundaries.
