## T4 — Relational Interface

**T4.1.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder operating from God toward the human person?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes, in two primary God-to-human directions: (1) **God performing wonders toward Israel** — the dominant VCG-01 pattern throughout: God's marvellous deeds are the divine acts directed toward the human community that produce Wonder as the response; Exo 3:20 (wonders done in Egypt); Exo 34:10 (marvels "before all your people"); Psa 31:21 ("he has wondrously shown his steadfast love to me when I was in a besieged city") — divine wonders extended as saving acts toward the person in distress; Joe 2:26 ("the Lord your God, who has dealt wondrously with you") — wonder-acts as the mode of divine engagement with the covenant community; (2) **God opening the eyes to behold wonders** — Psa 119:18 ("open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things out of your law") — the petition implies God extends the capacity for Wonder as a gift; the ability to perceive the wondrous in Scripture is itself a God-to-human act.

---

**T4.1.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the basis for God's wonder-acts toward the human person?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Multiple bases: (1) **Covenantal faithfulness** — Exo 34:10 ("before all your people I will do marvels… it is an awesome thing that I will do with you") — covenantal relationship as the basis; (2) **Steadfast love** — Psa 31:21 ("wondrously shown his steadfast love") — hesed as the inner motivation of God's wonder-acts; Psa 107:8, 15, 21, 31 (wondrous works to the children of man, grounds for thanking steadfast love) — steadfast love and wondrous works are paired; (3) **Purpose and sovereign plan** — Isa 25:1 ("plans formed of old, faithful and sure") — God's wonder-acts executed according to long-planned purpose; (4) **Restoration** — Eze 20:41 (God acting wondrously at the regathering — adjacent); Mic 7:15; Joel 2:26 — restoration as the basis of renewed wonder-acts.

---

**T4.1.3 — What does God's extension of wonder-acts reveal about his disposition?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **Redemptive orientation** — the overwhelming weight of VCG-01 shows God's wonder-acts as redemptive: Exodus, conquest, healing, rescue at sea, restoration; his disposition is toward his people's salvation through extraordinary means; (2) **Exclusivity** — Psa 72:18; 86:10 ("who alone does wondrous things") — God's disposition to be the exclusive worker of genuine wonders reveals his jealousy for his own incomparable status; (3) **Exceeding generosity** — Psa 40:5 ("your wondrous deeds and your thoughts toward us; none can compare with you! They are more than can be told") — wonder-acts multiplied without limit; (4) **Pedagogical intent** — Exo 34:10 ("all the people among whom you are shall see the work of the Lord, for it is an awesome thing") — God extends wonders with the explicit purpose of being witnessed and acknowledged.

---

**T4.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Not silent; God's wonder-acts toward the human person are the dominant pattern of the entire VCG-01 corpus.

---

**T4.2.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder operating in the human person's movement toward God?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Wonder producing worship-movement** — the proclamatory corpus is consistently doxological: "sing to him, sing praises to him; tell of all his wondrous works" (1Ch 16:9) — Wonder moves the person toward God in praise; "I will give thanks to the Lord with my whole heart; I will recount all of your wonderful deeds" (Psa 9:1) — Wonder producing wholehearted Godward movement; (2) **Seeking to behold** — Psa 119:18 ("open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things out of your law") — Wonder-seeking in the law as movement toward God through his word; (3) **Contemplative movement** — Psa 119:27 ("make me understand the way of your precepts, and I will meditate on your wondrous works"); Psa 145:5 ("on your wondrous works, I will meditate") — sustained contemplative movement toward God through Wonder-meditation; (4) **Job's return** — Job 42:3 ("things too wonderful for me, which I did not know") — Job's Wonder-humility as the restored posture of a person returned to right relationship with God.

---

**T4.2.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **Attending and considering** — Job 37:14 ("stop and consider the wondrous works of God") — deliberate attention as the prerequisite; (2) **Wholehearted engagement** — Psa 9:1 ("with my whole heart") — half-hearted Wonder-movement toward God is insufficient; (3) **Humility and non-overreach** — Psa 131:1 ("my heart is not lifted up… I do not occupy myself with things too great and too marvellous for me") — the humble not-overreaching posture as enabling genuine Wonder; (4) **Prior consecration** — Jos 3:5 ("consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the Lord will do wonders") — moral preparedness enabling receptive Wonder; (5) **Memory-engagement** — Psa 77:11 (deliberate memory of the Lord's wonders as the inner strategy for Wonder-movement toward God through distress).

---

**T4.2.3 — What does the human-to-God direction reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The human-to-God direction reveals: (1) **A relationship defined by asymmetric greatness** — the person who genuinely wonders at God has correctly perceived the infinite qualitative difference between themselves and God; Wonder is the relational posture of the creature acknowledging the Creator's incomprehensible superiority; (2) **A relationship sustained by memory** — Psa 77:11's deliberate return to God through remembered wonders shows the relationship maintained through deliberate memorial practice rather than continuous fresh experience; (3) **A relationship that produces proclamation** — the human-toward-God movement in Wonder does not stay private but moves through the mouth into communal declaration (1Ch 16:9, 24); (4) **A relationship of epistemological humility** — Job 42:3's "things too wonderful for me, which I did not know" positions the restored relationship with God as marked by humble acknowledgment of the limits of one's own understanding.

---

**T4.2.4 — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Human-to-God Wonder is extensively evidenced through the entire VCG-01 proclamatory tradition; not silent.

**T4.3.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder being extended by one person toward another?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Generational transmission** — Psa 78:4 ("we will not hide them from their children, but tell to the coming generation the glorious deeds of the Lord… and the wonders that he has done") — the act of extending Wonder to the next generation by telling them of God's wondrous deeds; (2) **Nations-directed proclamation** — 1Ch 16:24 ("declare his glory among the nations, his marvellous works among all the peoples"); Psa 96:3 — extending Wonder outward toward peoples who have not yet encountered it; (3) **2Sa 1:26** (pa.la — Jonathan's love as "extraordinary") — the experience of extraordinary depth in relationship that one person extends to another; David receiving the wonder-quality of Jonathan's love; (4) **Communal worship** — Psa 89:5 ("let the heavens praise your wonders, O Lord") — the heavenly assembly's communal Wonder-extension.

---

**T4.3.2 — What inner conditions enable genuine extension of Wonder toward another?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **Prior personal Wonder** — the person who has genuinely experienced Wonder at God's acts can extend it to others; Psa 71:17's "from my youth you have taught me, and I still proclaim your wondrous deeds" shows sustained personal Wonder as the basis for sustained proclamatory extension; (2) **Generational faithfulness** — Psa 78:4's "we will not hide them from their children" implies an active decision not to interrupt the chain of Wonder-extension; (3) **Worship-community membership** — the Tell-Remember-Meditate tradition presupposes a worshipping community that sustains and transmits Wonder as a communal practice; (4) **Knowledge of God's acts** — 1Ch 16:12 ("remember the wondrous works") — knowing the wonders is the precondition for declaring them.

---

**T4.3.3 — What must the person have received or become before they can genuinely give Wonder?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **A genuine encounter with divine Wonder** — Psa 107:24 ("they saw the deeds of the Lord, his wondrous works in the deep") — direct perceptive encounter with divine wonders; (2) **Formed memory** — 1Ch 16:12; Psa 77:11; 105:5 — the Wonder-tradition must be internalised in memory before it can be extended to others; (3) **Epistemological humility** — Job 42:3's "things too wonderful for me" — the person who has recognised the limits of their own knowledge can genuinely give Wonder without distorting it with false comprehension; (4) **Consecrated inner life** — Jos 3:5 — the morally prepared person can both receive and extend Wonder.

---

**T4.3.4 — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Giving direction through generational transmission and missional proclamation is the defining characteristic of VCG-01's corpus; not silent.

---

**T4.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder being received from another?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Received from the worshipping community** — the entire VCG-01 Tell-Remember tradition is a received-tradition: "we will tell to the coming generation" (Psa 78:4) — the coming generation receives Wonder through being told; "he has caused his wondrous works to be remembered" (Psa 111:4) — God ensures the Wonder is receivable; (2) **Received from God directly** — Psa 119:18's "open my eyes, that I may behold" — Wonder received as a God-given perceptive capacity; (3) **2Sa 1:26** — David receiving Jonathan's extraordinary love; (4) **Mar 1:27** — the crowd receiving the wonder-shock of Jesus' authoritative exorcism; **Mar 10:24** — disciples receiving amazement from Jesus' teaching.

---

**T4.4.2 — What inner conditions enable or block reception?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Enabling: attending and considering (Job 37:14); opening the eyes (Psa 119:18 — petition for divine enabling); consecration (Jos 3:5); wholehearted engagement (Psa 9:1). Blocking: forgetting (Neh 9:17 — "not mindful"; Psa 78:11 — "forgot his works"); not considering (Psa 106:7 — "did not consider your wondrous works"); self-sufficient wisdom (Isa 29:14 — wisdom-accumulation that stops perceiving God's acts as extraordinary); dark wonder misapplied (Rev 17:7 — angel correcting John's misapplied marvelling).

---

**T4.4.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters Wonder but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — (1) **Hard-hearted and rebellious** — Neh 9:17 (wonder-failure → stiffened neck → rebellion); Psa 78:32 ("despite his wonders, they did not believe") — unbelief despite encountering the marvellous; (2) **Spiritually forgetful** — Psa 78:11; 106:7 — forgetting rather than denial; the inner being simply does not register the Wonder; (3) **Unchanged** — Psa 78:32 (sinned still despite wonders) — the wonder was present but produced no inner transformation because it was not genuinely received; (4) **Open to misapplication** — Rev 17:6, 7 — John marvels greatly at the harlot, which the angel immediately corrects; the person whose Wonder-capacity is not rightly anchored to God's acts alone can receive wrong objects.

---

**T4.4.4 — If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Receiving direction is evidenced through community-reception and individual perceptive encounter; not silent.

**T4.5.1 — Does the evidence indicate whether Wonder operates differently within existing bonds vs. across distance?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder operates across both: (1) **Within the covenant community** — the primary VCG-01 context is Israel's internal worship and memory tradition (the Psalms, Chronicles); (2) **Across national boundaries** — 1Ch 16:24; Psa 96:3 ("declare his glory among the nations, his marvellous works among all the peoples") — Wonder extends missionally outward to all peoples; Exo 34:10 ("all the people among whom you are shall see the work of the Lord") — the witness of Wonder is explicitly intended for surrounding nations; (3) **2Ch 26:15** — Uzziah's fame spreading "far" because he was marvellously helped — wonder-acts creating a reputation that extends across geographical distance.

---

**T4.5.2 — Does Wonder operate within covenantal contexts only?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — No — Wonder crosses covenantal boundaries in the missional proclamation register. Psa 107:24's seafarers who saw God's wondrous works in the deep are not necessarily Israelites — the sea-rescue wonders in Psa 107 are available to all who encounter divine rescue. Exo 34:10's "all the people among whom you are" explicitly includes non-Israelites as witnesses. The marvellous works in VCG-02's "is anything too hard for the Lord?" (Gen 18:14; Jer 32:17, 27) are directed at universal divine capability, not covenantal restriction.

**T4.5.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope?**

Wonder's scope is universal and trans-generational.

---

**T4.5.4 — If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Relational scope from covenant community to universal proclamation is evidenced; not silent.

The angel does not destroy John's Wonder but channels it toward proper understanding.

## T5 — Formation and Transformation

**T5.1.1 — Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Job's restoration** — Job 42:3 ("things too wonderful for me, which I did not know") — Wonder at God's incomprehensibility produces Job's restored inner posture; the divine whirlwind-encounter producing Wonder-humility is the hinge of Job's transformation from complaint to restored relationship; (2) **Psa 119:129** — "your testimonies are wonderful; therefore my soul keeps them" — Wonder in God's law producing obedient keeping; (3) **Mar 1:27** — the crowd's transformation from the routine synagogue assembly into a questioning, astonished community encountering something utterly new; (4) **The anti-type — Neh 9:17** — wonder-failure producing the transformation from covenant people into rebels; the failure to wonder precedes and enables the moral transformation toward rebellion.

---

**T5.1.2 — Is the transformation reversible or irreversible?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Varies: (1) **Reversible** — Neh 9:17's transformation from wonder to rebellion is reversed by God's mercy (the same verse: "but you are a God ready to forgive, gracious and merciful"); Mic 7:15's "as in the days when you came out of Egypt, I will show them marvellous things" shows wonder-based transformation restorable; (2) **Durable** — Psa 71:17's lifelong proclamatory orientation formed by sustained Wonder; (3) **Eschatologically permanent** — Isa 29:14's "wonder upon wonder" pointing toward transformations that the wise cannot reverse; Dan 12:6's end-time wonders producing the permanent transition to the final age.

---

**T5.1.3 — If the evidence is silent on transformation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Transformation evidence is present in multiple registers; not silent.

---

**T5.2.1 — Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Exo 34:10 sequence**: God announces marvels → all the people see the work of the Lord → it is an awesome thing; (2) **Psa 9:1 sequence**: wonder at wonderful deeds → wholehearted thanks → recounting/proclamation; (3) **Psa 119:129 sequence**: testimonies are wonderful → therefore the soul keeps them; (4) **Job 42:3 sequence**: encountered the incomprehensible → spoke beyond knowledge → now recognises things too wonderful for me → restored humility; (5) **Neh 9:17 sequence**: wonders performed → not mindful → stiffened neck → appointed leader to return to Egypt — a reverse sequence showing wonder-failure producing the sequence toward rebellion.

---

**T5.2.2 — What does the sequence reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Two opposing sequences: (1) **Upward arc** — divine wonder-act → perceptive encounter → Wonder arises → proclamation, obedience, humility, praise; (2) **Downward arc** — divine wonder-act → failure to perceive/remember → Wonder-absence → hardness → rebellion. The sequences reveal Wonder as the critical hinge characteristic: when genuine Wonder arises from encounter with God's extraordinary acts, it produces the upward arc; when it fails to arise (not-attending, not-remembering), the downward arc follows. No other M04 characteristic has such a clearly documented downward arc through its absence.

---

**T5.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on sequence, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Both upward and downward sequences are extensively documented; not silent.

---

**T5.3.1 — What mechanism does Wonder use to produce change?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Three mechanisms: (1) **Cognitive humbling** — the encounter with what exceeds comprehension (Job 37:5; 42:3; Psa 131:1) humbles the inner being into right creaturely posture; the mechanism is intellectual arrest; (2) **Memory-embedding** — God causing his wondrous works to be remembered (Psa 111:4); the mechanism of repeated memorial practice embedding wonder-recognition in the person's cognitive and affective formation; (3) **Proclamatory overflow** — wonder-at-God's-acts producing the impulse to declare them (1Ch 16:9, 24; Psa 9:1); the mechanism is the inner state overflowing into communal speech and action.

---

**T5.3.2 — Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: VCG-01 contexts → memory-embedding and proclamatory-overflow mechanisms (the liturgical-worship context); VCG-02 contexts → cognitive-humbling mechanism (the epistemological-limit context); VCG-03 dark Wonder contexts → disturbing-arrest mechanism (the troubled-astonishment and dread of VCG-03).

---

**T5.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on mechanism, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Three distinct mechanisms evidenced; not silent.

---

**T5.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder operating in relation to suffering or affliction?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 31:21** — "blessed be the Lord, for he has wondrously shown his steadfast love to me when I was in a besieged city" — Wonder at divine rescue arising precisely from the context of extreme suffering; (2) **Psa 77:11** — "I will remember the deeds of the Lord; yes, I will remember your wonders of old" — deliberate memory of past wonders as the inner strategy for sustaining faith through present distress; (3) **Job 10:16** (VCG-03) — wonders worked against Job in his suffering — dark Wonder at divine power directed against him; (4) **Lam 1:9** — Jerusalem's terrible fall (pe.le) as the dark wonder of judgment experienced in suffering; (5) **Deu 28:59** — "extraordinary afflictions" (pa.la) as the dark wonder of covenantal judgment.

---

**T5.4.2 — Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce Wonder?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Suffering both **tests Wonder** (does the person return to remembered wonders in distress — Psa 77:11?) and **produces Wonder** at God's extraordinary rescue acts (Psa 31:21 — Wonder arising precisely from the besieged-city context). Suffering also produces **dark Wonder** (Job 10:16; Lam 1:9 — the incomprehensibility of extreme suffering itself producing astonished dread). The most analytically significant: Psa 77:11 shows Wonder as the inner resource the Psalmist deliberately returns to under suffering — deliberate memory of past wonders is the therapeutic inner-strategy for navigating present distress.

---

**T5.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on the relationship to suffering, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The relationship to suffering is evidenced across rescue-wonder (VCG-01), dark wonder (VCG-03), and suffering-as-judgment contexts; not silent.

**T5.5.1 — Does the verse evidence show Wonder participating in the longer arc of character formation?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 71:17** — "from my youth you have taught me, and I still proclaim your wondrous deeds" — the fullest formation-arc evidence in the corpus: Wonder formed from youth producing a lifetime of proclamatory vocation; (2) **Psa 78:4** — "we will not hide them from their children, but tell to the coming generation" — the intergenerational formation arc: Wonder transmitted through communal telling forming each generation; (3) **Psa 131:1** — the formed epistemological humility of the person who has learned not to overreach; a character quality formed by sustained Wonder; (4) **Job's arc** — Job 42:3's humble Wonder following the whirlwind as the resolution of a life-arc through suffering and complaint toward restored relationship.

---

**T5.5.2 — What does the evidence reveal about Wonder's role in the formation arc?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Wonder is the formative thread of a life directed toward God in proclamation and praise: Psa 71:17's "from my youth… I still proclaim" shows Wonder as the characteristic that, when established early and sustained deliberately, becomes the defining inner-expressive orientation of a whole life. Psa 78's generational-transmission passage shows Wonder as the content of character formation passed between generations — not as a single experience but as a sustained practice of telling and remembering. Wonder's role in formation is as the doxological spine of the covenant life.
