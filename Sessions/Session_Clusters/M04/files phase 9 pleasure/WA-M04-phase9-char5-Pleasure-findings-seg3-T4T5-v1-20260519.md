## T4 — Relational Interface

**T4.1.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure operating from God toward the human person?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, in three primary God-to-human registers: (1) **God's pleasant character extended toward the community** — Psa 90:17 ("let the favor/pleasantness [no.am] of the Lord our God be upon us") — divine pleasantness as a character-quality that God extends toward his people; (2) **Divine beauty received by the worshiper** — Psa 27:4 ("to gaze upon the beauty [no.am] of the Lord") — God's own beauty as the object that the worshiper receives as Pleasure; God extends his beauty toward the seeking person; (3) **Divine river of delights** — Psa 36:8 ("you give them drink from the river of your delights [ed.nah]") — God lavishly supplying delight from his own inexhaustible source toward those who feast in his house; (4) **Eschatological acceptance as pleasing aroma** — Eze 20:41 ("as a pleasing aroma I will accept you") — God extending his receptive Pleasure toward restored Israel as a future-oriented covenantal gift; (5) **Pleasures forevermore** — Psa 16:11 ("at your right hand are pleasures forevermore") — God as the permanent source of pleasure in his own presence.

---

**T4.1.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends Pleasure toward the human person?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple bases: (1) **Covenantal faithfulness** — the ni.cho.ach corpus: God's receptive Pleasure at worship depends on covenant-obedient offering (Lev 26:31 — withheld from unfaithful); the basis is obedient worship; (2) **Seeking his presence** — Psa 27:4 (one who asks and seeks after God receives the Pleasure of his beauty); (3) **Grace and character** — Psa 36:8 (the river of delights is lavishly given to those in God's house — gracious supply); Psa 90:17 (divine pleasantness asked to rest upon the community — grace); (4) **Restoration** — Eze 20:41 (God will accept the restored Israel as a pleasing aroma after scattering — restoration as the basis of resumed Pleasure).

---

**T4.1.3 — What does God's extension of Pleasure reveal about his disposition?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Lavish generosity** — Psa 36:8's river of delights shows God as extravagant in his pleasantness-supply; (2) **Covenant faithfulness** — the ni.cho.ach corpus shows divine Pleasure as reliable and consistent toward those who worship rightly; (3) **Personal beauty** — Psa 27:4's no.am as God's own quality shows his disposition as inherently beautiful and agreeable — he is not merely useful but genuinely pleasant to encounter; (4) **Restorative orientation** — Eze 20:41's future-oriented acceptance shows God's disposition toward restored relationship as the ultimate goal; (5) **Eternal supply** — Psa 16:11's "forevermore" shows God's Pleasure-giving as permanently available, not exhausted.

---

**T4.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Not silent; God-to-human Pleasure is documented across five registers.

**T4.2.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure operating in the human person's movement toward God?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 27:4** — "one thing have I asked of the Lord, that will I seek after: that I may dwell in the house of the Lord… to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord" — the entire movement of the seeking person toward God is motivated by and culminates in Pleasure (gazing upon divine beauty); (2) **The ni.cho.ach worship acts** (M04-K-VCG-01) — the worshiper's act of offering correctly-prepared sacrifice is a movement toward God that produces the pleasing-aroma Pleasure relationship; human-to-God Pleasure operates through worship action; (3) **Psa 147:1** — "for it is good to sing praises to our God; for it is pleasant and a song of praise is fitting" — the pleasantness of praise as the experience of human worship-movement toward God; (4) **Psa 135:3** — "praise the Lord… for it is pleasant!" — pleasantness of worship as the felt quality of the human movement toward God in praise.

---

**T4.2.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Single-minded seeking** — Psa 27:4 ("one thing have I asked") — the one thing focused on God's beauty enables receiving his Pleasure; (2) **Covenant obedience** — the ni.cho.ach corpus: correct, prescribed worship is the enabling posture; (3) **Faithfulness** — Job 36:11 ("if they listen and serve him, they complete their years in pleasantness") — listening and serving as the inner posture that enables Pleasure to be the quality of one's years; (4) **Willingness to praise** — Psa 147:1; 135:3 — the posture of genuine praise enabling the felt pleasantness of worship.

---

**T4.2.3 — What does the human-to-God direction reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The human-to-God direction reveals: (1) **Relationship centred on beauty and pleasantness** — Psa 27:4 frames the entire relationship with God as oriented toward gazing on his beauty; the relationship is not merely duty but pleasure-in-God; (2) **Worship as a pleasure-exchange** — the ni.cho.ach structure shows worship as a genuine bidirectional pleasure relationship: the human offers rightly → divine Pleasure is evoked → the relationship is maintained pleasantly; (3) **The pleasantness of praise** — Psa 147:1; 135:3 show that the human person's movement toward God in praise is itself a pleasurable inner experience — worship is not merely correct but genuinely pleasant to those who engage it.

---

**T4.2.4 — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Human-to-God Pleasure is evidenced through seeking, worship, and praise; not silent.

**T4.3.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure being extended by one person toward another?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Speech-Pleasure extended** — Pro 16:24 (gracious words extended toward another, producing sweetness in their soul and health in their body); Pro 15:26 (gracious words pure — extended as morally wholesome Pleasure); Psa 45:1 (the psalmist's pleasing theme extended toward the king in praise-poetry); (2) **Relational pleasantness** — Psa 133:1's brotherly unity — each person in the community extending pleasantness to the others through harmonious living; (3) **Tactile pleasantness extended** — 2Sa 1:24 (Saul clothing the daughters of Israel luxuriously in scarlet — extending material Pleasure outward); (4) **Song of Songs** — Song 1:16; 7:6 — mutual pleasantness extended between the lovers; each appreciates and extends the pleasure of the other's presence.

---

**T4.3.2 — What inner conditions enable genuine extension of Pleasure toward another?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Inner pleasantness of character** — the person who extends genuine pleasant speech (Pro 16:24) must have an inward purity (Pro 15:26 — "gracious words are pure") — the inner state of purity enables the extension of genuine Pleasure; (2) **Genuine appreciation of the other** — Song 1:16; 7:6 — the lover's genuine delight in the beloved's beauty enables the pleasantness to be authentically extended; (3) **Harmonious inner orientation** — Psa 133:1's brotherly unity requires each person to be inwardly harmoniously oriented toward the community; (4) **Wisdom** — Pro 3:17's wisdom-pleasantness — the wise person's life naturally extends pleasantness to those around them.

---

**T4.3.3 — What must the person have received or become before they can genuinely give Pleasure?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Wisdom dwelling in the heart** — Pro 2:10's "wisdom will come into your heart" enables the Pleasure in knowledge that can then be extended to others in wise speech (Pro 16:24); (2) **Prior experience of divine Pleasure** — Psa 27:4's one who gazes on God's beauty has received the deepest Pleasure and can then extend pleasantness in praise (Psa 45:1's overflow); (3) **Genuine appreciation of beauty** — the lover in Song of Songs who genuinely experiences the beloved as pleasant (Song 7:6) can extend genuine appreciative Pleasure.

---

**T4.3.4 — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Giving direction is evidenced through speech, communal harmony, and relational appreciation; not silent.

**T4.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure being received from another person?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Received relational pleasantness** — 2Sa 1:26 (David receiving Jonathan's pleasantness; the quality of Jonathan's companionship was a Pleasure received by David's inner being); Song 1:16; 7:6 (the lover receiving the beloved's pleasantness); (2) **Received speech-Pleasure** — Pro 16:24 (gracious words received by the soul — an inner being receiving pleasant speech from another); Pro 2:10's "pleasant to your soul" implies a received quality; (3) **Received material pleasantness** — 2Sa 1:24 (daughters of Israel receiving the luxury of Saul's scarlet and gold); Jer 51:34 (Jerusalem's delicacies received — and then consumed by the enemy); (4) **Received communal Pleasure** — Psa 133:1 (each brother receiving the pleasantness of the unified community).

---

**T4.4.2 — What inner conditions enable or block reception?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Enabling: wisdom in the heart (Pro 2:10); seeking God's presence (Psa 27:4); openness to gracious speech (Pro 22:18 — "pleasant if you keep them within you"); receptivity within right relationships. Blocking: covenant unfaithfulness (Lev 26:31 — God will not smell the pleasing aromas); folly (Pro 19:10 — luxury unfit for fools); moral corruption (Job 20:18 — wicked getting no enjoyment from profits); judgment/curse (Deu 28:54, 56 — pleasure capacity destroyed).

---

**T4.4.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters Pleasure but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Unfit reception** — Pro 19:10 (the fool experiencing luxury as unfitting — the inner being constitutionally misaligned with the Pleasure it encounters); (2) **Blocked enjoyment** — Job 20:18 (the wicked man prevented from enjoying his profits — encountering material Pleasure but unable to receive it as genuine enjoyment); (3) **False reception** — Pro 9:17 (stolen bread appearing pleasant — the person receives what feels pleasant but is not genuinely so; the inner being is deceived); (4) **Grief at loss** — 2Sa 1:26 (David's distress upon losing Jonathan's pleasantness — the memory of received Pleasure intensifying the grief of its loss).

**T4.4.4 — If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.**

**T4.5.1 — Does the evidence indicate whether Pleasure operates differently within existing bonds vs. across distance?**

The ni.cho.ach worship-Pleasure operates within the covenantal relationship. The cross-boundary instance: Num 15:14 ("if a stranger is sojourning with you… and he wishes to offer a food offering, with a pleasing aroma to the Lord, he shall do as you do") — the worship-Pleasure relationship is extended to include resident foreigners who observe the same ritual conditions.

---

**T4.5.2 — Does Pleasure operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross boundaries?**

The relational-Pleasure register (M04-J) is not covenant-restricted — the pleasantness of wisdom's ways (Pro 3:17), gracious speech (Pro 16:24), and communal harmony (Psa 133:1) are available wherever wisdom is practiced and genuine agreeableness exists.

## T5 — Formation and Transformation

**T5.1.1 — Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Pro 16:24** — gracious-word Pleasure producing "sweetness to the soul and health to the body" — a holistic transformative healing effect on the whole person; (2) **Gen 8:21** — God's inner Pleasure at Noah's offering producing a divine resolve ("I will never again curse the ground") — the soothing aroma transforms God's stated disposition toward the fallen world; (3) **Psa 27:4** — sustained dwelling in God's house and gazing on his beauty produces transformation toward that beauty; (4) **Job 36:11** — listening and serving God producing "years in pleasantness" — transformation of life-quality through the obedient posture; (5) **Deu 28:54, 56** — the reverse: loss of Pleasure under the curse transforms the character of daily life and inner experience.

---

**T5.1.2 — Is the transformation reversible or irreversible?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Varies: (1) **The ni.cho.ach relationship** — reversible in both directions: Lev 26:31 (God stops receiving aromas — reversible by repentance); Eze 20:41 (God will again accept as pleasing aroma — restoration restoring the Pleasure relationship); (2) **The luxury-curse transformation** (Deu 28:54, 56) — presented as severe but reversible through covenant restoration; (3) **Pro 16:24's health to the body** — implies an ongoing sustaining effect rather than a permanent irreversible change; (4) **Eschatological Pleasure** (Psa 16:11) — "forevermore" implies permanent and irreversible eschatological transformation.

---

**T5.1.3 — If the evidence is silent on transformation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Transformation evidence is present across multiple registers; not silent.

---

**T5.2.1 — Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Pro 2:9–10 sequence**: wisdom enters heart → knowledge pleasant to soul → good path understood; (2) **Pro 16:24 sequence**: gracious words heard → sweetness to the soul → health to the body; (3) **Gen 8:21 sequence**: Noah offers → Lord smells → Lord says in his heart → divine resolve formed; (4) **Psa 27:4 sequence**: ask/seek → dwell in God's house → gaze on his beauty → inquiry in his temple; (5) **Job 36:11 sequence**: listen and serve God → complete days in prosperity → years in pleasantness.

---

**T5.2.2 — What does the sequence reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The dominant sequences reveal Pleasure as the fruit or outcome of a prior enabling condition: wisdom enters, then Pleasure follows (Pro 2:10); gracious words are received, then Pleasure flows (Pro 16:24); Noah obediently offers, then God's Pleasure responds (Gen 8:21). Pleasure is consistently the experiential fruit of something prior — right worship, wisdom, seeking, obedient service. It is received rather than generated.

---

**T5.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on sequence, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Sequences are evidenced across multiple clusters; not silent.

---

**T5.3.1 — What mechanism does Pleasure use to produce change?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Three primary mechanisms: (1) **Nourishment** — Pro 16:24's honey-simile: pleasant speech nourishes the soul and heals the body; the mechanism is saturation and sustenance; (2) **Soothing** — the ni.cho.ach mechanism: the pleasing aroma soothes and settles; the mechanism is calming of agitation into settled receptive state; (3) **Drawn beauty** — Psa 27:4's gazing upon God's beauty: the mechanism is aesthetic encounter drawing the person toward what is beautiful, producing the transformation of attraction and orientation.

---

**T5.3.2 — Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: worship contexts → soothing mechanism (ni.cho.ach — calming); speech contexts → nourishing mechanism (Pro 16:24 — honey-sustenance); seeking-God contexts → beauty-attraction mechanism (Psa 27:4 — drawn toward the beautiful); wisdom contexts → wisdom-formation mechanism (Pro 2:10 — wisdom entering the heart enabling Pleasure in knowledge).

---

**T5.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on mechanism, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple mechanisms evidenced; not silent.

---

**T5.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure operating in relation to suffering or affliction?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, primarily as contrast and consolation: (1) **Pro 15:15** — "all the days of the afflicted are evil, but the cheerful of heart has a continual feast" — the contrast positions Pleasure as the inner state that transcends circumstantial adversity; (2) **Psa 16:6** — "lines have fallen for me in pleasant places" — the psalmist's reception of pleasant inheritance in the context of a psalm expressing trust through difficulty; (3) **Job 36:11** — obedient service producing years of pleasantness in contrast to the adversity of the unobedient; (4) **Deu 28:54, 56** — the curse destroying Pleasure under siege-adversity — the most direct evidence of affliction eliminating Pleasure.

---

**T5.4.2 — Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce Pleasure?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Suffering primarily **tests and reveals** whether Pleasure is genuine: Pro 15:15 reveals that the cheerful-of-heart person maintains a feast-quality even in adversity, showing genuine inner Pleasure independent of circumstance. Deu 28:54, 56 shows suffering **destroying** Pleasure that was dependent on comfort — the siege reveals the fragility of luxury-Pleasure. The characteristic contrast: inner Pleasure (M04-J's relational and wisdom variety) survives adversity better than material Pleasure (M04-K-VCG-02's luxury register). Suffering does not produce Pleasure in this corpus in the way Suffering-Joy arises through suffering.

---

**T5.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on the relationship to suffering, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The relationship to suffering is evidenced through contrast and curse-context; not silent.

**T5.5.1 — Does the verse evidence show Pleasure participating in the longer arc of character formation?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Pro 3:17** ("her ways are ways of pleasantness, and all her paths are peace") — wisdom's pleasantness-quality is the characteristic of a whole life formed by wisdom; (2) **Pro 2:10** ("wisdom will come into your heart, and knowledge will be pleasant to your soul") — the long-arc formation of wisdom in the heart produces Pleasure in knowledge as the ongoing experiential quality; (3) **M04-K-VCG-03 wisdom-comparatives** — the sustained engagement with wisdom's better-than comparatives forms a person whose Pleasure is calibrated to prefer genuine goodness over material abundance; (4) **The ni.cho.ach worship practice** — twice-daily, weekly, festal; the sustained practice of worship-Pleasure forms the community's covenantal identity over time.

**T5.5.2 — What does the evidence reveal about Pleasure's role in the formation arc?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure plays a sustaining and reinforcing role in formation: wisdom-formation produces Pleasure in knowledge which reinforces continued wisdom-seeking (Pro 2:10); the pleasantness of wisdom's ways (Pro 3:17) makes the wisdom-path genuinely attractive and sustainable. Pleasure is the experiential quality that makes the formation path pleasant to walk — it is both a fruit of formation and a sustainer of it.

**T5.5.3 — If the evidence is silent on formation, note this explicitly.**
