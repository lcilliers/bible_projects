## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-5]** S — No verse in the M04-J or M04-K corpus explicitly names the human spirit (ruach/pneuma) as the constitutional seat of Pleasure. The primary constitutional locations are the soul and the body. Spirit-level location is not evidenced for this characteristic.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-5]** S — Not evidenced. Pleasure in this corpus arises at the soul-body interface (Pro 16:24) and in the soul's receptive response to what is agreeable — not at the spirit level. The characteristic's most distinctive constitutional feature is its explicit body-level engagement (Pro 16:24; Deu 28:54–56; Isa 47:1), which is the opposite of a spirit-level origin.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does the absence of spirit-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The absence of spirit-level location reveals Pleasure as the most constitutionally embodied positive characteristic in M04. Unlike Suffering-Joy (primarily spirit-level) and Delight (primarily inner-being/soul), Pleasure is the characteristic most directly connected to the body's sensory and material registers. This embodied quality is consistent with the ni.cho.ach aroma's physical-sensory character and the a.nog/ta.a.nug luxury register's explicit bodily orientation.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** S — The evidence is silent on spirit-level location for Pleasure across both sub-groups.

---

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, explicitly: (1) **Pro 2:10** — "knowledge will be pleasant to your soul (nephesh)" — the soul is named as the seat receiving Pleasure in wisdom/knowledge; (2) **Pro 16:24** — "gracious words are like a honeycomb, sweetness to the soul and health to the body" — soul named first as the primary recipient; (3) **Psa 16:11** — "pleasures forevermore" at God's right hand — the pleasures are experienced by the whole person, with the soul as the experiential centre (the Psalm is addressed by the soul to God); (4) **Psa 36:8** — "river of your delights" from which worshippers drink — soul-level receptive drinking image.

---

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Soul-level location confirms that Pleasure is genuinely an inner-being characteristic, not merely a surface sensory reaction. Pro 2:10's "pleasant to your soul" positions the inner person's deepest experiential self as the seat of Pleasure in wisdom — when genuine wisdom enters the heart, the soul experiences a qualitative pleasantness. Pro 16:24's "sweetness to the soul" shows that even speech-Pleasure penetrates to the soul level. The soul receives Pleasure from what is genuinely agreeable — this is the characteristic's primary constitutional function.

---

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Soul-level location is explicitly evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, indirectly: (1) **Pro 2:10** — "wisdom will come into your heart, and knowledge will be pleasant to your soul" — heart and soul are named in sequence; wisdom enters the heart first, and knowledge becomes pleasant to the soul as a result — the heart is the receiving organ whose state determines whether Pleasure in knowledge follows; (2) **Psa 45:1** — "my heart overflows with a pleasing theme" — the heart as the seat from which the pleasant poetic inspiration wells up; (3) **Gen 8:21** — "the Lord said in his heart" after smelling the pleasing aroma — the divine Pleasure at the offering is located in God's heart (lev); (4) **Pro 15:15** — "the cheerful of heart has a continual feast" — the heart's positive inner state producing a continuous Pleasure-quality to daily life.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Heart-location reveals that Pleasure is heart-mediated: the state of the heart determines the quality of Pleasure received. Pro 2:10's sequence (wisdom enters heart → knowledge pleasant to soul) shows the heart as the gateway: when the heart is rightly constituted by wisdom, the soul receives Pleasure in what is genuinely good. Pro 15:15's "cheerful of heart" confirms the heart as the seat whose positive orientation produces a continuous feast-quality of inner life — the inner disposition of the heart generates the felt pleasantness of daily experience. Gen 8:21 confirms: even divine Pleasure is located in God's heart.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Heart-location is evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-5]** S — No verse in the M04-J or M04-K corpus explicitly names the mind as the constitutional seat of Pleasure. The wisdom-comparative register (M04-K-VCG-03) engages the evaluative faculty which is cognitively located, but the better-than comparatives operate through the heart-soul rather than naming "mind" explicitly. Mind-location is not directly evidenced.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** S — Not directly evidenced; the cognitive-evaluative engagement with Pleasure (M04-K-VCG-03) operates through wisdom-formation of the heart and soul rather than through an explicitly named mind. The absence of explicit mind-location confirms Pleasure as primarily affective-receptive rather than cognitively-mediated (unlike Gladness whose primary dimension is cognitive-evaluative).

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** S — The evidence is silent on explicit mind-location for Pleasure.

---

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: **the whole person as receiving subject** — Psa 133:1's "how good and pleasant" is exclaimed over the whole community; Job 36:11's "years in pleasantness" names the whole life-quality; Psa 16:6's "pleasant places" names the whole inheritance-condition; these are not specifically located in heart, mind, or soul individually but name the whole-person experience of Pleasure in one's lot. Additionally: **the perceiving senses** — Psa 27:4's "gaze upon the beauty of the Lord" — the eyes as the perceptive organ; Psa 34:8 (in M04-K — "taste and see that the Lord is good") — the gustatory sense as a perception metaphor.

---

**T2.5.2 — What does the whole-person and sense-perceptive location reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The whole-person and sense-perceptive locations reveal Pleasure as the most holistically distributed characteristic in M04 — it registers across the whole person (life-quality, inheritance-condition), through the senses (sight, taste, smell/aroma), in the soul (Pro 2:10), and in the body (Pro 16:24). No other M04 characteristic is as explicitly multi-level in its constitutional distribution.

---

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple locations beyond heart and mind are evidenced; not silent.

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, multiple: (1) **Nose/smell** — the ni.cho.ach aroma corpus (M04-K-VCG-01) is centred on the olfactory sense: Gen 8:21 ("the Lord smelled the pleasing aroma"); Num 28:2 ("my pleasing aroma") — the nose as the organ through which worship-Pleasure reaches God; (2) **Mouth/tongue** — Pro 16:24 ("gracious words are like a honeycomb, sweetness to the soul") — the honey-simile invokes the gustatory sense; Psa 34:8 ("taste and see that the Lord is good" — cross-assigned) — explicit gustatory-sense invocation; (3) **Eyes** — Psa 27:4 ("to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord") — sight as the medium for divine-beauty Pleasure; (4) **Body overall** — Pro 16:24 ("health to the body") — the body as a whole receiving the healing benefit of gracious-word Pleasure; Deu 28:54, 56 (the delicate person's foot not setting on the ground — somatic delicateness); Isa 47:1 (tender and delicate — somatic luxury).

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — (1) **Nose (ni.cho.ach)** — the olfactory link is **mediating**: the physical aroma rises from the altar and reaches God through the olfactory dimension, making the sensory physical act the medium of the worship-Pleasure relationship; the physical smell mediates a spiritual-relational reality (God's reception of the worshiper's offering); (2) **Mouth/honey-sweetness** — Pro 16:24's honey simile is **emphatic**: it insists that genuinely pleasant speech nourishes as concretely and immediately as honey nourishes the body; the bodily taste-image serves to communicate the soul-level impact; (3) **Eyes** — Psa 27:4's "gaze" is **constitutive**: gazing upon God's beauty is the act that receives divine Pleasure; the eyes are not merely metaphorical but are the appropriate organ for encountering beauty; (4) **Body overall** — Pro 16:24's "health to the body" is **integrative**: pleasant speech heals the whole person, demonstrating that genuine Pleasure penetrates from word to soul to body.

---

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple body-part links are evidenced; not silent.

**T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Both directions: (1) **Body-to-soul** — the ni.cho.ach aroma rises from the offering (physical) and reaches God's inner being (divine inner response); in the human person: the perception of a pleasant aroma, sight, or taste produces an inner soul response (the olfactory, visual, and gustatory senses feeding inward to the soul); (2) **Soul-to-body** — Pro 16:24's gracious words received by the soul produce "health to the body" — the inner reception of pleasant speech flows outward to bodily health; the soul's Pleasure produces body-benefit; (3) **Body-as-signal** — Deu 28:54, 56 (the delicate body as the sign of Pleasure in pampered ease); the body's condition signals the inner state of Pleasure or its absence.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The bidirectional body-link reveals Pleasure as a constitutionally integrative characteristic: it is both received through bodily senses and communicated outward to bodily health. Unlike characteristics that are purely inner-being (no body link) or purely body-triggered (no inner-being consequence), Pleasure is simultaneously sensory-received and soul-expressed, body-triggered and body-healing. This bidirectionality makes Pleasure the most fully embodied positive inner-being characteristic in M04.

---

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Bidirectional body links are evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Implied but not explicitly named: (1) **Pro 16:24's "health to the body"** — sustained reception of gracious-word Pleasure produces an ongoing body-health, suggesting a depositional effect on physical wellbeing; (2) **The curse-inversion** — Deu 28:54, 56 shows that the sustained state of a.nog (pampered ease) leaves a person constitutionally shaped by that ease, so that when it is removed the collapse is catastrophic — the prior sustained Pleasure has formed a constitutional expectation and dependency; (3) **Wisdom-formed Pleasure** (M04-K-VCG-03) — sustained engagement with the wisdom better-than comparatives trains and forms the inner being to habitually evaluate what is genuinely more pleasant — a dispositional deposit.

---

**T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Supporting: Pro 16:24 (ongoing health as implied sustained effect); Deu 28:54, 56 (the delicate person's constitution shaped by sustained luxury); Pro 3:17 (wisdom's "ways of pleasantness" as a formed disposition). No direct evidence contradicts.

---

**T2.8.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Constitutional deposit is implied across multiple registers; not fully silent.

---

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple origins: (1) **From genuine persons, speech, and creation** (M04-J) — the pleasantness of Jonathan (2Sa 1:26), the beloved (Song 1:16; 7:6), wisdom (Pro 2:10; 3:17), and gracious speech (Pro 16:24) originates from the object itself being genuinely pleasant; the inner being receives what is genuinely present in the external reality; (2) **From divine character** — Psa 27:4's no.am as God's own beauty-quality; Psa 36:8's river of delights; the pleasantness originates in God and is received by the worshiper; (3) **From correctly-offered worship** — ni.cho.ach Pleasure originates in the obedient act of sacrifice that evokes God's receptive inner response; (4) **From wisdom's formation** — Pro 2:10 (wisdom coming into the heart enabling Pleasure in knowledge) — wisdom-entry is the originating event enabling Pleasure's reception; (5) **From false sources** — Pro 9:17 (stolen bread as apparent pleasantness) — misdirected Pleasure originates in deceptive appearances.

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Multiple — the most varied origin-set in M04, consistent with Pleasure's role as the broadly receptive characteristic: it receives from persons, speech, creation, God's character, and worship-acts.

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: worship contexts → originates from correctly-offered sacrifice evoking divine response; relational contexts → originates from the genuine pleasantness of persons and bonds; creation contexts → originates from the pleasant qualities of created things and places; wisdom contexts → originates from wisdom entering the heart and enabling Pleasure in knowledge; material contexts → originates from physical ease and luxury.

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Sensory → soul → body** — Pro 16:24 shows the full movement: gracious words received auditorily → "sweetness to the soul" (soul-level) → "health to the body" (body-level); (2) **Physical offering → inner divine response** — ni.cho.ach: the physical sacrifice rises → God smells (perceptive) → says in his heart (inner resolve); (3) **Divine beauty → inner response → worship** — Psa 27:4: God's beauty (divine) → gazing upon it (perceptive) → dwelling in his house (responsive action).

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the sequence or pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The dominant movement pattern is: external pleasant object/act → sensory reception → soul-level Pleasure → body-level health/effect. Pleasure uniquely in M04 moves consistently downward from perception through soul to body — it is the characteristic that most thoroughly integrates sensory, soul, and bodily dimensions in a single movement sequence. Pro 16:24 is the clearest evidence of this three-level sequence.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Constitutional movement across three levels is evidenced; not silent.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes — more explicitly than any other M04 characteristic: (1) **Sight** — Psa 27:4 ("to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord"); (2) **Smell** — Gen 8:21 ("when the Lord smelled the pleasing aroma"); the entire ni.cho.ach corpus is olfactory-mediated; (3) **Taste** — Psa 34:8 ("taste and see that the Lord is good" — cross-assigned to M04-K); (4) **Hearing** — Pro 16:24 (gracious words received auditorily); (5) **Touch** — Deu 28:54, 56 (the delicate woman who would not set her foot on the ground — touch-sensitivity as the somatic marker of pampered Pleasure).

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure deepens perception: the person who genuinely tastes God's goodness (Psa 34:8) perceives him more accurately; Psa 27:4's gaze upon God's beauty is perceptive engagement of the highest kind. Distorted Pleasure impairs perception: Pro 9:17 (stolen bread appearing pleasant — deceived perception evaluating the wrong object as pleasant); the ni.cho.ach withheld (Lev 26:31) implies the covenant-unfaithful person's worship-perception is distorted, offering what cannot reach God.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is uniquely perception-dependent among M04 characteristics — it arises from sensory encounter with what is genuinely pleasant. The pattern reveals that training perception rightly is essential to genuine Pleasure: the person whose perceptive faculty is attuned to genuine goodness receives genuine Pleasure; the person whose perception is distorted (by folly, false objects, covenant-unfaithfulness) receives counterfeit Pleasure that cannot sustain or nourish.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Partially, in the wisdom-comparative register: M04-K-VCG-03 (the better-than comparatives) require cognitive evaluation to assess which option is genuinely more pleasant — Psa 37:16 (little of the righteous better than abundance of wicked); Pro 15:16, 17; 16:32; 17:1 (various integrity-vs-abundance comparatives). This is a cognitive-evaluative engagement, albeit secondary to the primary affective-receptive dimension. Psa 34:8's "taste and see" also involves a cognitive element: the imperative to see (perceive and assess) alongside taste.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure in wisdom deepens cognition: Pro 2:10's "knowledge will be pleasant to your soul" — when wisdom is in the heart, the soul's Pleasure in knowledge reinforces the desire to know more; pleasantness in knowledge sustains cognitive engagement. Distorted Pleasure (Pro 9:17 — stolen bread) bypasses honest cognition by making the wrong thing feel pleasant, thereby short-circuiting the evaluation that would recognise its true character.

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure and cognition are indirectly related: Pleasure in wisdom sustains cognitive engagement; Pleasure-seeking without wisdom distorts and bypasses accurate cognition. The relationship is not direct (Pleasure is not a cognitive characteristic) but instrumental: right Pleasure reinforces right knowing; wrong Pleasure corrupts knowing.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Partially: (1) **2Sa 1:26** — David's lament for Jonathan recalls the pleasantness of their relationship; memory of pleasantness is what makes grief acute; (2) **Jer 51:34** — Jerusalem's delicacies devoured by Nebuchadnezzar — the memory of former pleasantness intensifies the sense of loss; (3) **Psa 77:11** (in M04-I, not directly Pleasure but adjacent) — the act of remembering pleasant past experiences of God sustains present endurance.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine pleasantness deepens memory: the most pleasant experiences (Jonathan's companionship — 2Sa 1:26; Jerusalem's former delicacies — Jer 51:34) are retained most vividly in memory precisely because they were so pleasant. The ni.cho.ach worship-Pleasure is designed to be repeated (daily, weekly, festal) as a memorial practice — the regularity of worship-Pleasure sustains and reinforces memory of the covenant relationship.

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure marks what is memorable: what is genuinely pleasant is genuinely held in memory; loss of the pleasant intensifies the memory of it. The repeated ni.cho.ach pattern in Leviticus/Numbers (daily sacrifices as memorial worship-Pleasure) deliberately embeds the covenant-Pleasure relationship in communal memory through bodily practice.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes — this is the primary faculty of Pleasure: (1) **M04-J-VCG-02's relational pleasantness** — 2Sa 1:26 (the affective warmth of Jonathan's companionship); Song 1:16; 7:6 (the lover's affective appreciation of the beloved's beauty); Psa 133:1 (the affective exclamation of communal pleasantness); (2) **Pro 2:10** (knowledge pleasant to the soul — a felt affective quality); (3) **Pro 16:24** (gracious words as sweetness — affective nourishment); (4) **Gen 8:21** (God's inner calmed, settled affective response to the soothing aroma); (5) **Psa 16:11** (pleasures forevermore — the affective quality of God's presence).

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure deepens and sustains positive affect: Pro 15:15's "cheerful of heart has a continual feast" shows positive inner-affect producing a sustained Pleasure-quality to all of daily experience; Pro 16:24's gracious words produce sweetness in the soul — affective nourishment. Distorted Pleasure corrupts affect: Pro 7:18's carnal a.las distorts affect by attaching it to a wrong object; Deu 28:54, 56 shows affect collapsed under judgment.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is the most affective-receptive of the M04 characteristics: its primary constitutive feature is the felt quality of agreeableness received inwardly. Joy surges upward; Suffering-Joy paradoxically transcends; Delight rests in a specific object; Pleasure is the ambient felt-pleasantness of what is genuinely agreeable — the most immediate, least mediated affective experience of genuine goodness in M04.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes: (1) **Psa 45:1** — "my heart overflows with a pleasing theme; I address my verses to the king; my tongue is like the pen of a ready scribe" — the pleasantness of the creative-poetic impulse arising from the heart; Pleasure in the subject-matter (the king's beauty and virtue) generates creative expression; (2) **Pro 16:24's gracious words** — the crafting of genuinely pleasant speech is a creative act; the one who produces words that are "like a honeycomb" engages the creative faculty in service of Pleasure; (3) **Pro 3:17** (wisdom's ways as pleasantness) — wisdom's creative work produces a life-path characterised by pleasantness.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure in a worthy subject (Psa 45:1 — the king's beauty) enables creative expression: the heart overflows, the tongue becomes agile. Pleasure functions as the creative motivating force when the object is genuinely pleasant — the pleasantness of what is experienced generates the impulse to express it in beautiful form.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is creativity-generating in its relational and beauty-appreciating register: when the inner person is genuinely pleased by what it perceives, creative expression follows naturally. Psa 45:1 is the clearest evidence — a heart overflowing with pleasing matter cannot but speak it. The pattern reveals Pleasure as one of the generative roots of creative expression in Scripture.

---

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Partially and indirectly: (1) **The worship-Pleasure mode** (ni.cho.ach) requires volitional obedience — the pleasing aroma arises from correctly and deliberately offered sacrifice; the worshiper must choose to offer what is prescribed; Lev 26:31's withholding of divine Pleasure from the unfaithful implies the faithful person's volitional offering is the enabling condition; (2) **Psa 27:4** ("one thing have I asked… that will I seek after") — seeking to gaze upon God's beauty is a volitional act; (3) **The wisdom-comparative register** (M04-K-VCG-03) — the better-than comparatives require the will to choose modest integrity over corrupt abundance.

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure in wisdom and right worship enables volition by providing clear grounds for choosing what is genuinely good: Pro 16:24's gracious-word Pleasure enables the will to prefer gentle speech; the wisdom-comparative Pleasure enables the will to choose integrity over material abundance. Distorted Pleasure (Pro 7:18's a.las; Pro 9:17's stolen bread) bypasses and corrupts volition by making wrong choices feel pleasant.

---

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Unlike Delight (where volition is constitutive) and Joy (where volition is involved through command), Pleasure engages volition instrumentally: right Pleasure supports right willing; wrong Pleasure corrupts and bypasses right willing. Pleasure is primarily receptive rather than volitional — but the quality of what is received as pleasant directly shapes and can corrupt volitional direction.

---

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, indirectly: (1) **Worship agency** — the ni.cho.ach corpus requires the worshiper to act (offer the sacrifice, wash the entrails, burn the fat) — agency in right worship produces the Pleasure-quality of the aroma; (2) **Psa 27:4's seeking** — "one thing have I asked… that will I seek" — the seeking act is agentive; (3) **Pro 15:26's gracious speech** — speaking pleasant words is an agentive act that extends Pleasure to others; (4) **Neh 9:17 negative** (in M04-I but adjacent) — Israel's failure to act in response to God's wonders shows agency failure as blocking the right Pleasure-response.

---

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure in right objects enables agency toward those objects: Psa 27:4's longing for God's beauty motivates the agentive seeking of his presence; Pro 24:25's delight at rebuking the wicked motivates moral courageous agency. In its distorted form (Pro 7:18; Pro 9:17), wrong Pleasure motivates agency toward destructive ends.

---

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is not primarily an agentive characteristic but it motivates agency toward its object: what is experienced as pleasant draws the person toward it. The ni.cho.ach corpus's link between obedient sacrificial action and divine Pleasure shows the most direct Pleasure-agency relationship: right worship-action produces the Pleasure-quality of the aroma; the action and the Pleasure are inseparable.

---

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, primarily in M04-K-VCG-03: (1) **Psa 37:16** (little of righteous better than abundance of wicked — moral-comparative evaluation); (2) **Pro 15:16** (little with fear of Lord better than great treasure with trouble — moral priority); (3) **Pro 15:17** (dinner of herbs with love better than feast with hatred — moral-relational evaluation); (4) **Pro 16:32** (slow to anger better than mighty — inner character better than external power — shared anchor with anger registry); (5) **Pro 19:22** (steadfast love most desirable — moral evaluation of character-quality over material goods). M04-K-VCG-03 is essentially the moral evaluation faculty applied to material Pleasure — the wisdom literature training the evaluative faculty to prefer what is genuinely more pleasant.

---

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Right Pleasure in wisdom enables and deepens moral evaluation: Pro 2:10's wisdom-in-heart producing Pleasure in knowledge enables the whole evaluative apparatus of wisdom including moral judgment. Wrong Pleasure bypasses moral evaluation: Pro 9:17's stolen-bread Pleasure makes the forbidden appear genuinely pleasant, preventing accurate moral evaluation of the act.

---

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure and moral evaluation are related through the wisdom-formation link: the person whose Pleasure is calibrated by wisdom evaluates moral choices more accurately; the person whose Pleasure is distorted by folly evaluates moral choices inaccurately. The wisdom better-than corpus (M04-K-VCG-03) is the explicit training programme for calibrating Pleasure to moral reality.

---

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Partially: (1) **Pro 15:26** — "thoughts of the wicked are an abomination to the Lord, but gracious words are pure" — the contrast between wicked thoughts (conscience-bad) and gracious words (conscience-pure) shows that Pleasure in speech is conscience-indexed; genuinely pleasant speech is also morally pure; (2) **Ni.cho.ach withheld** (Lev 26:31) — God's non-reception of the unfaithful's aromas implies the conscience of the worshiper should recognise that their worship has failed the covenant standard; the absent ni.cho.ach is an implicit conscience-signal; (3) **Job 20:18** (the wicked gets no enjoyment) — the blocked Pleasure of the wicked is itself a conscience-indicator: natural Pleasure is denied as a consequence of moral failure.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure in what is morally good enables conscience: the person who finds gracious words genuinely pleasant (Pro 15:26) has a conscience aligned with what is morally pure. Distorted Pleasure bypasses conscience: the seductress's a.las (Pro 7:18) makes the conscience-violating act feel pleasant, and the stolen bread's apparent pleasantness (Pro 9:17) bypasses the conscience's recognition of wrongdoing.

---

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The relationship between Pleasure and conscience is diagnostic: when genuine pleasantness and moral goodness align (Pro 15:26 — gracious words are both pleasant and pure; Pro 16:24 — pleasant words are health-giving), the conscience and the Pleasure faculty are in agreement. When they diverge (Pro 9:17 — stolen bread feels pleasant but is morally wrong), the Pleasure faculty has been captured by the wrong object against the conscience's proper witness.

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, in the ni.cho.ach worship register: (1) **Lev 1:9; 2:2; 3:5** (and throughout M04-K-VCG-01) — each offering must be carefully prepared (washed, properly arranged, fully burned) before producing the pleasing aroma; conscientiousness in ritual detail is what produces the worship-Pleasure; (2) **Num 28:2** — "you shall be careful to offer to me at its appointed time" — conscientiousness in timing is explicitly commanded as the condition for pleasing-aroma worship; (3) **Joh 8:29** (cross-assigned in M04-M, but paradigmatic) — "I always do the things that are pleasing" — continuous conscientious action as the form Pleasure-in-obedience takes.

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Yes, extensively — M04-J is almost entirely relational: (1) **2Sa 1:26** — Jonathan's pleasantness to David as the deepest relational warmth in the corpus; (2) **Psa 133:1** — brotherly unity as pleasant — communal relational pleasantness; (3) **Song 1:16; 7:6** — the beloved's pleasantness as received aesthetic-relational Pleasure; (4) **Pro 16:24** — gracious words as pleasant — the relational speech-act as Pleasure medium; (5) **Pro 3:17** — wisdom's ways as pleasantness — wisdom's relational character; (6) **Zec 11:7, 10** — the No'am staff as the covenant-favour relationship personified; (7) **Psa 27:4** — the pleasantness of dwelling in God's house as the relational intimacy-quality.

---

**T3.11.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Genuine Pleasure deepens relational capacity: 2Sa 1:26's Jonathan-David pleasantness is the relational depth evidenced by David's grief at the loss; the pleasantness of the relationship was constitutive of its depth. Pro 16:24's gracious-word Pleasure actively sustains relationships through speech. Distorted Pleasure impairs relational capacity: Pro 9:17's stolen-bread Pleasure exploits and degrades relationships; Jer 51:34's Nebuchadnezzar consuming Jerusalem's delicacies is destructive of the relational context.

---

**T3.11.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pleasure is the characteristic most directly generated through and sustained by relational bonds in M04. M04-J's entire corpus is relational pleasantness — the quality of what is agreeable between persons. The pattern reveals that the deepest human Pleasure in this corpus is relational (Jonathan-David; the beloved; brotherly unity) rather than material or sensory — relational pleasantness is the characteristic's most profound register.
