## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, in specific registers. Luk 1:47 — "my spirit rejoices in God my Savior" (Mary's Magnificat: the agalliao rejoicing is located explicitly in the pneuma/spirit). Luk 10:21 — "he rejoiced in the Holy Spirit" (Jesus' agalliao is located in and produced by the Holy Spirit). Act 13:52 — "the disciples were filled with joy and with the Holy Spirit" (joy and the Holy Spirit co-produced in the same constitutional event). Rom 14:17 — "joy in the Holy Spirit" (joy as a kingdom-constituent specifically in the Spirit). Gal 5:22 — joy as the Spirit's fruit. 1Th 1:6 — "joy of the Holy Spirit" as the accompanying state when the word is received in affliction. Phile 20 — "refresh my heart in Christ" (the Christ-dimension of inner joy). The spirit-level location is evidenced primarily in the NT agalliao and Spirit-fruit passages, and in Mary's Magnificat.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Partially. The NT Spirit-mediated joy (Act 13:52; 1Th 1:6; Gal 5:22; Rom 14:17) shows that in its most intense and paradoxical forms (joy in affliction, joy under persecution), Joy is produced at the spirit level by the Spirit himself. However, the dominant OT constitutional home is the heart (not the spirit), and the M04-B corpus (281 verses, the largest sub-group) does not name the spirit as the primary location. Joy originates at multiple constitutional levels depending on register: in the heart (primary for the OT communal-festive corpus), in the spirit (primary for the NT Spirit-produced paradoxical joy), and at the whole-being level for its most comprehensive expressions.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Spirit-level location for Joy reveals that its deepest and most paradox-transcending form — joy amid suffering, inexpressible joy in the unseen Christ (1Pe 1:8) — operates at the deepest constitutional level, below and beyond the affective and cognitive faculties. Mary's "my spirit rejoices" (Luk 1:47) locates this exultation in the innermost self before it reaches the heart and mind. Jesus' rejoicing "in the Holy Spirit" (Luk 10:21) shows that the purest divine-image expression of Joy is Spirit-mediated. The spirit-level location explains the characteristic's capacity to transcend adverse circumstances: joy operating at the spirit level is not accessible to the same distortions that affect heart-level and mind-level emotional states.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Spirit-level location is evidenced; not silent. It is more extensively evidenced in NT than OT registers.

---

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. Psa 86:4 — "Gladden the soul of your servant, for to you, O Lord, do I lift up my soul" (soul explicitly named as the seat of gladness to be produced by God). Psa 106:5 — "that I may rejoice in the gladness of your nation" (the aspirational soul-oriented joy of belonging to God's people). Eze 25:6 — "rejoiced with all the malice within your soul" (the soul as the corrupted source of malign joy). The soul appears as a locus of joy in specific passages, but less consistently than the heart.

---

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Soul-level location confirms that Joy reaches into the deepest personal identity of the individual — the soul (nephesh) is the person in their essential individuality and life-force. When the soul is the seat of gladness (Psa 86:4), this means Joy is not a peripheral emotional surface-event but an inner reality engaging the very core of personhood. Eze 25:6's "malice within your soul" as the source of corrupt joy shows that misdirected joy reaches just as deep — the distortion of Joy affects the soul's fundamental orientation.

---

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Soul-level location is evidenced at the referenced passages; not silent.

---

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes — the heart is the most extensively and consistently named constitutional locus for Joy across the entire corpus. Exo 4:14 (Aaron "will be glad in his heart"); Deu 28:47 (joyfulness "of heart" commanded); 1Ki 8:66 / 2Ch 7:10 (joyful and "glad of heart"); Psa 4:7 ("more joy in my heart"); Psa 19:8 ("precepts of the Lord… rejoicing the heart"); Psa 33:21 ("our heart is glad in him"); Psa 97:11 ("joy for the upright in heart"); Psa 105:3 ("hearts of those who seek the Lord rejoice"); Pro 14:10 ("no stranger shares its joy" — the heart's private joy); Pro 14:13 ("even in laughter the heart may ache"); Pro 15:13 ("a glad heart makes a cheerful face"); Pro 17:22 ("a joyful heart is good medicine"); Pro 15:30 ("the light of the eyes rejoices the heart"); Jer 15:16 ("joy and the delight of my heart"); Ecc 5:20 ("God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart"); Song 3:11 ("the gladness of his heart"); Joh 16:22 ("your hearts will rejoice"); Zep 3:14 ("rejoice and exult with all your heart"). The heart is the characteristic's primary constitutional home.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The heart's integrating role means Joy at the heart level is not a peripheral emotion but an inner state engaging the whole person's central volitional-affective-knowing faculty. Pro 15:13 directly connects heart-gladness to countenance (the outer expression of inner state) and contrasts it with sorrow crushing the spirit — showing the heart as the nexus where Joy or its absence determines the whole person's inner condition. The heart's Joy is the productive source of the person's orientation to the world: Deu 28:47's failure to "serve the Lord with joyfulness and gladness of heart" as the root cause of judgment reveals that the heart's inner posture before God is the most fundamental thing about the covenant person's standing.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Heart-location is the most extensively evidenced constitutional location in this corpus; not silent.

---

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Partially, as a precondition rather than the seat. Neh 8:12 — "great rejoicing, because they had understood the words that were declared to them" — comprehension (a mind-faculty act) produces joy; the joy is in the heart and expressed in eating and celebration, but the mind's understanding is its direct cause. The Ecclesiastes investigations (Ecc 2:1 — "I said in my heart, come now, I will test you with pleasure") show the mind directing the inner experiment, but the joy itself is located in the heart. No verse explicitly names the mind (as distinct from the heart-as-rational-faculty) as the seat of Joy.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The mind's role in Joy is as a precondition and director: the person who understands God's acts (Neh 8:12), perceives divine goodness (Psa 97:8 — "Zion hears and is glad"), or comprehends the gospel (Act 13:48 — "the Gentiles heard this, they began rejoicing") has the cognitive reception that enables the heart's joy. The mind provides the true account of what is happening; the heart responds with joy. The two are consistently sequenced: perception/comprehension → heart's gladness → expressed rejoicing.

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The mind is evidenced as a precondition of Joy (comprehension enables it) but is not named as its constitutional seat. The primary constitutional seat is the heart.

---

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes: (1) **The whole being / whole inner self** — Psa 16:9 ("my heart is glad, and my whole being rejoices") — Joy extends from the heart to encompass the totality of the inner person; (2) **The mouth and tongue as expressive organs** — Act 2:26 ("my tongue rejoiced") — in this agalliao verse, the tongue is named as the rejoicing organ, showing Joy's primary inner state (heart-gladness) expressing itself through the first inner-to-outer organ of speech; (3) **The bones** — Psa 51:8 ("let the bones that you have broken rejoice") — Joy's restoration is requested at the deepest somatic level.

---

**T2.5.2 — If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The whole-being location (Psa 16:9) reveals that Joy at its fullest does not stay confined to the heart but radiates through the entire inner person. This is consistent with the characteristic's expressive quality — Joy that truly takes hold of the heart moves through the whole being and out into voice, body, and action. The tongue-rejoicing (Act 2:26) shows Joy constitutively moving toward proclamation as its natural expression channel.

---

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Not silent; whole-being and tongue-locations are evidenced.

---

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, multiple: (1) **Bones** — Psa 51:8 ("let the bones that you have broken rejoice") — the bones as the body's deepest structural element asked to participate in restored Joy; (2) **Flesh** — Psa 16:9 ("my flesh also dwells secure") — the body's security as the outer consequence of inner-being Joy; (3) **Tongue** — Act 2:26 ("my tongue rejoiced") — the speech organ as the primary bodily channel of Joy's expression; (4) **Hands and dancing** — 2Sa 6:12 (ark brought "with rejoicing"; David dances before the Lord); Jer 31:13 ("young women rejoice in the dance") — the body's movement as Joy's physical expression; (5) **Face** — Pro 15:13 ("a glad heart makes a cheerful face") — the face as the most visible bodily indicator of inner Joy; (6) **Leaping body** — Luk 1:44 ("the baby in my womb leaped for joy") — the most extreme bodily expression of Joy, occurring in an unborn child; (7) **Womb** — Luk 1:44 as above.

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Three functions: (1) **Extension** — the joy that begins in the heart reaches through the whole person and out into the body (Psa 16:9; Pro 15:13) — showing Joy as a characteristic that encompasses the whole human being, not only the inner; (2) **Depth-emphasis** — bones and womb are the body's most hidden and deep levels; joy reaching to the bones (Psa 51:8) and leaping in the womb (Luk 1:44) signals that this inner state penetrates to the deepest physical levels of human existence; (3) **Expression** — tongue, hands, face, and dance are the body's expressive surfaces; their involvement shows Joy as a characteristic that naturally overflows from within outward into visible, audible expression.

---

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Body-part links are extensively evidenced.

---

**T2.7.1 — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The primary direction is **inner to outer**: heart gladness → cheerful face (Pro 15:13); heart rejoices → whole being rejoices → flesh dwells secure (Psa 16:9); soul rejoices → tongue rejoices (Act 2:26). The secondary direction — **outer to inner** — appears in the feast-context passages: eating, drinking, and feasting (Deu 16:13–15; 1Ki 8:66; Ecc 9:7) are outer acts prescribed to occasion inner gladness; the bodily act of feasting in God's presence is a means of producing and expressing the inner joy. Wine gladdens the heart (Psa 104:15; Ecc 10:19) — a bodily input producing an inner state. This bidirectionality is notable: Joy is not only inner-to-outer in expression but also cultivated through outer forms that produce inner states.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The bidirectionality reveals that Joy is both a spontaneous inner state that seeks outward expression and a cultivated inner state that can be formed through prescribed outer practices (feasts, singing, dancing, communal eating). This is why Deuteronomy commands the feast-joy — the commanded outer form of rejoicing before God is itself a means of producing and sustaining the inner state. The implication for formation: Joy can be cultivated through embodied communal practices, not only through direct divine encounter or inner resolve. The outer-to-inner direction is the wisdom of the feast commandments.

---

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Bidirectional body-characteristic link is evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Partially evidenced. Pro 17:22 — "a joyful heart is good medicine" — implies that sustained heart-gladness has a medicinal effect on the body, suggesting a positive constitutional consequence. Lam 5:15 — "the joy of our hearts has ceased; our dancing has been turned to mourning" — the sustained absence of joy produces a corresponding physical loss (dancing ended). Isa 24:7 ("all the merry-hearted sigh") shows that sustained joylessness produces a corresponding bodily-inner state of sighing. These suggest that sustained Joy produces a positive bodily-constitutional environment; its sustained absence produces a deflated physical-inner condition. No explicit generational consequence is evidenced.

---

**T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Supporting: Pro 17:22 (joyful heart as good medicine — medicinal metaphor implies body-level impact); Psa 51:8 (restored joy sought for "bones that you have broken" — the body's restoration is bound up with joy's restoration). Qualifying: the evidence describes outcomes and metaphors rather than explicit constitutional-deposit language. The direction is clear (sustained joy → bodily wellbeing; sustained joylessness → bodily-inner decline) but the mechanism is not spelled out.

---

**T2.8.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Not fully silent — medicinal and body-restoration evidence is present — but explicit constitutional-deposit language is absent. The finding is: sustained joy produces positive bodily consequences; its absence produces negative bodily consequences; the explicit deposit mechanism is not described.

---

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Multiple origins evidenced: (1) **From God** — "you have put more joy in my heart" (Psa 4:7); "the Lord had made them joyful" (Ezr 6:22); "God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart" (Ecc 5:20); "may the God of hope fill you with all joy" (Rom 15:13); Gal 5:22 (Spirit-fruit); — Joy as divinely produced in the inner being; (2) **From the encounter with God's acts** — the spontaneous joy at witnessing divine deliverance or presence (1Sa 6:13; Luk 24:41; Joh 20:20) — originates in the perception of divine reality; (3) **From the will's choice** — Phili 4:4 ("Rejoice always"); Jas 1:2 ("Count it all joy") — Joy initiated by deliberate volitional act of the will directed by God's command; (4) **From community** — M04-D (sunchairo) and Pauline joy over communities (1Th 3:9; Phili 4:1) — Joy produced by and in relational bonds; (5) **Prescribed practice** — feast-commandments (Deu 16:11–15) — Joy cultivated through divinely-ordained communal practices.

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Multiple. The evidence across the 15 VCGs of Characteristic 2 shows Joy originating through five distinct pathways (divine production, encounter, will, community, practice). No single origin is exclusive. The different sub-groups evidence different primary origins: M04-B-VCG-01 (feast-commandment joy) is practice-originated; M04-B-VCG-05 (God-directed psalmist joy) is encounter-originated; M04-C-VCG-04 (Spirit-fruit joy) is divinely-produced; M04-D (sunchairo) is community-originated.

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, with clear contextual patterns: Ritual/liturgical contexts (M04-B-VCG-01) → practice-originated; Narrative-rescue contexts (M04-B-VCG-03) → encounter-originated; Psalmist-prayer contexts (M04-B-VCG-05) → encounter and will combined; NT Spirit-and-suffering contexts (M04-C-VCG-04, 05) → Spirit-produced; Relational pastoral contexts (M04-C-VCG-03; M04-D) → community-produced; Wisdom contexts (Ecc, Pro) → divinely-provided or wisdom-cultivated. The multiplicity of origins is itself an important finding: Joy cannot be reduced to a single source but arises through the convergence of divine action, human will, community, and practice.

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. The movement is documented in multiple directions: (1) **Spirit → heart → body** — Spirit produces joy (Gal 5:22; 1Th 1:6), which is then located in the heart (Psa 4:7; Pro 17:22; Ecc 5:20) and then expressed through the body (face, dance, voice); (2) **Heart → whole being → body** — Psa 16:9 explicitly sequences heart → whole being → flesh; (3) **God → inner being → community** — God produces joy in the heart (Psa 4:7; Ezr 6:22), which then radiates into the community (Neh 12:43 — "the joy of Jerusalem was heard far away"); (4) **Body → heart** (bidirectional) — feast practices produce inner joy (Deu 16:13–15); wine gladdens the heart (Psa 104:15).

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The dominant sequence is: divine action / Spirit / God's word received → heart glad → whole being rejoices → body expresses → voice proclaims → community participates. This sequence is clearest in the Nehemiah dedication (Neh 12:27–43): appointed singers → gladness accompanies worship → great joy in Jerusalem heard far away. The sequence shows Joy as a radially-expanding characteristic — it begins at the innermost constitutional level (heart or spirit) and expands outward through the whole person into the community and into the world. The reverse sequence (body-to-heart) appears in the feast-commandments as the designed formation pathway: prescribed communal practice produces the inner state.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Constitutional movement is extensively evidenced in both directions.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, consistently. The evidence shows Joy arising from perception across all inner senses: (1) **Sight** — "When they saw the star, they rejoiced exceedingly" (Mat 2:10); "when she has found it" (Luk 15:9); "the disciples were glad when they saw the Lord" (Joh 20:20); Psa 97:8 ("Zion hears and is glad"); Isa 66:14 ("you shall see, and your heart shall rejoice"); (2) **Hearing** — Neh 8:12 (joy from understanding what was declared); Psa 34:2 ("let the humble hear and be glad"); "recognizing Peter's voice" (Act 12:14); (3) **Taste** — Ecc 9:7 ("eat your bread with joy") — taste-mediated festive joy; (4) **Spiritual discernment** — Act 11:23 (Barnabas "saw the grace of God, he was glad") — spiritual perception of divine grace producing joy. Perception is the most common immediate trigger for Joy in the corpus.

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens perception in its rightly-directed form: Psa 107:42 ("the upright see it and are glad") — joy at God's acts reinforces the attentiveness to divine action that produced it; Psa 126:3 ("the Lord has done great things for us; we are glad") — the glad perception of divine acts sustains the orientation toward God. Joy impairs perception in its misdirected form: Mat 13:20 (joy without root fails when testing comes — the initial perceptive joy was accurate but too shallow); Act 12:14 (Rhoda's overwhelming joy at Peter's voice means she fails to perceive his actual presence at the gate — joy can momentarily overwhelm practical perception). The rocky-ground passages (Mat 13:20; Luk 8:13) show Joy without depth actually preventing sustained engagement with reality.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is a perceptually-grounded characteristic whose depth depends on the depth of perception that grounds it. Surface-level perception → surface-level joy (rocky-ground hearer); accurate, deep perception of divine reality → sustained, transforming joy (Hab 3:18 type; Psa 97:8). The pattern reveals that the quality of a person's inner perception of who God is and what he has done is the single most important determinant of the quality and durability of their Joy.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, as a consistent enabling precondition. Neh 8:12 — joy arising "because they had understood the words declared to them" — the most direct evidence that cognitive comprehension produces Joy. Isa 25:9 — "we have waited for him… behold, this is our God" (recognitive cognition → exultant rejoicing). Act 13:48 — "when the Gentiles heard this, they began rejoicing" — hearing and cognitively receiving the word produces joy. 1Cor 13:6 — love "rejoices with the truth" — the cognitive-evaluative act of recognising truth is the object of Joy. Mat 25:21 ("enter into the joy of your master") — the cognitive understanding of what the master's joy is shapes the servant's aspiration.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens cognition in its healthy form: Psa 97:8 (joy at God's judgments reinforces the understanding of divine justice); Neh 8:12 (joy following understanding sustains the engagement with God's word). Joy can momentarily disrupt cognitive function in intense encounters: Luk 24:41 ("while they still disbelieved for joy" — joy so overwhelming that it temporarily outpaces belief); Act 12:14 (Rhoda's joy overrides practical reasoning). These disruptions are not presented as failures but as expressions of Joy's intensity exceeding cognitive capacity momentarily.

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Cognition and Joy are mutually reinforcing in the normal pattern: comprehension produces Joy; Joy sustains and reinforces the engagement with what is understood. The occasional instances of Joy temporarily exceeding cognitive function (Luk 24:41; Act 12:14) reveal Joy as a characteristic that in its most intense forms temporarily subordinates cognition to affect — the inner being is so flooded with gladness that ordinary reasoning is suspended. This is exceptional rather than normative.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. Psa 66:6 — "they passed through the river on foot. There did we rejoice in him" — the memory of the Exodus crossing is the ground for present communal rejoicing; the joy is retrospective and memory-anchored. Psa 106:5 — "that I may rejoice in the gladness of your nation" — the psalmist desires to enter into the joy of the nation's memory of God's acts. Psa 137:6 — "if I do not set Jerusalem above my highest joy" — the memory of Jerusalem as the place of joy is the psalmist's highest value. Psa 51:12 — "restore to me the joy of your salvation" — David's memory of pre-sin joy is the reference point for what he asks restored. The M04-E-VCG-01 restoration joy (sa.s.von) is essentially the promised restoration of what was and is remembered: Jer 33:11 (the voice of gladness returning after judgment silenced it) — memory of pre-judgment joy is the criterion of restoration.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens memory by celebrating what is remembered: the liturgical feast-joy (M04-B-VCG-01) is constitutively memorial — Deu 16:11 (rejoicing before the Lord "in all that you undertake, in which the Lord your God has blessed you") makes the memory of blessing the ground for present rejoicing. The feast is the regular practice of joy-anchored-in-memory. Conversely, Joy's absence in judgment strips away the memory-sustaining practices: Jer 7:34; 16:9 (silencing of the "voice of mirth and gladness, the voice of the bridegroom and the bride") removes the communal memory-keeping mechanisms that joy sustains.

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is a memory-anchoring and memory-sustaining characteristic: the feast practices (M04-B-VCG-01) that produce Joy are simultaneously practices of communal remembrance; to rejoice in what God has done is to remember it; to remember it rightly is to rejoice. Joy sustains the community's engagement with its own narrative of God's acts. The removal of joy under judgment is simultaneously the removal of the community's joy-memory practices — a catastrophic loss of inner-being coherence.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is the affective faculty in its most fully-engaged participatory expression. The heart's gladness (consistently across both OT and NT), the whole being's rejoicing (Psa 16:9), the Spirit's joy overflowing into the community (Act 13:52; 1Th 1:6), Mary's spirit-rejoicing (Luk 1:47), the inexpressible joy filled with glory (1Pe 1:8) — all are primary affective events. Joy is not primarily cognitive or volitional (though it engages both) but fundamentally affective: a positive, engaged inner feeling directed at its object.

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens the affective faculty by elevating it and giving it its rightful object: Psa 43:4 ("God my exceeding joy") — the affective capacity of the inner person reaching its highest object; Psa 16:11 ("fullness of joy in your presence") — the maximum affective capacity reached in God's nearness. Pro 17:22 ("joyful heart is good medicine") — Joy in its sustained form heals and strengthens the affective faculty rather than depleting it. The rocky-ground joy (Mat 13:20) shows affect without formation: real but unsustained, depleted by testing rather than deepened through it.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is the affective faculty at its designed peak when rightly directed: the person who rejoices in God has their affect working at its created purpose. The affective faculty was designed to be glad in God; when it is, it is at its fullest and most healthy. The pattern also shows that affect requires depth (root) to sustain Joy: the rocky-ground passages show affect without depth failing; the paradoxical joy under suffering (2Cor 6:10; 1Pe 1:6) shows affect with Spirit-grounding sustaining Joy where shallow affect cannot.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. The consistent association of Joy with music, poetry, and song is the primary creative evidence: 1Ch 15:16 (David appointing Levites to raise "sounds of joy" with instruments — organised creative music in service of Joy); Psa 30:11 (mourning turned to dancing — the creative-bodily form of joy); Jer 31:13 ("young women rejoice in the dance; young men and old shall be merry") — dance as Joy's creative-bodily expression; Neh 12:27 (gladness expressed through singing, cymbals, harps, lyres at the dedication); Isa 55:12 ("you shall go out in joy… the mountains and the hills before you shall break forth into singing, and all the trees of the field shall clap their hands") — creation's Joy expressed in personified creative response. The Psalms themselves are creative products of joy-oriented engagement with God.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy enables and generates creative expression: 1Ch 15:16 shows David's Joy producing organised creative music-making as its institutional form; the Psalms of this corpus are creative literary works produced from the inner experience of Joy-in-God. Joy does not bypass creativity but pressures it toward expression — the inner gladness seeks a form adequate to itself.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is a generative characteristic: its inner pressure naturally seeks and produces creative forms — song, poetry, dance, communal festivity. The feast-commandments (Deu 16:11–15) are in part the institutional recognition that Joy requires a creative-communal form to sustain and express itself. The pattern suggests Joy and creativity are mutually reinforcing: Joy presses toward creative expression; creative expression sustains and deepens Joy.

---

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, substantially. The commanded nature of Joy across both OT and NT is the primary evidence: Deu 16:11 ("you shall rejoice"); Lev 23:40 ("you shall rejoice before the Lord"); Phili 4:4 ("rejoice in the Lord always"); 1Th 5:16 ("rejoice always"); Jas 1:2 ("count it all joy… when you meet trials"). These are volitional commands — they presuppose that the will can initiate, sustain, and direct the inner state of rejoicing. Luk 10:20 (Jesus redirecting rejoicing from wrong object to right object) and Jas 1:2 (counting trials as joy by a cognitive-volitional act) are the clearest examples of will directly shaping Joy.

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy enables and strengthens volition by providing the inner motivation for action: 2Cor 8:2 (abundance of joy in poverty overflowing into generosity — Joy produces volitional giving); Neh 8:10 ("the joy of the Lord is your strength") — Joy enables the will's perseverance; Act 5:41 (apostles "rejoicing that they were counted worthy to suffer" — joy enabling volitional endurance under persecution). Joy does not bypass volition but works through it, providing the inner incentive for difficult volitional acts.

---

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy and volition have a constitutive relationship in both directions: the will can cultivate and direct Joy (commanded joy); Joy in turn enables and motivates the will (joy produces strength, generosity, endurance). This bidirectional relationship is what makes Joy a formation characteristic — it is both a goal of volitional formation and a resource for further volitional formation.

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. Joy consistently produces and motivates action: Act 8:39 (the eunuch "went on his way rejoicing" — joy motivating continued journey); 2Cor 8:2 (joy overflowing into generous giving — agency produced by joy); Isa 55:12 ("you shall go out in joy" — joy as the mode of redemptive departure); Act 5:41 (apostles rejoicing → continuing to teach in Jesus' name — joy motivating ongoing apostolic agency); Mat 13:44 ("in his joy he goes and sells all that he has" — joy as the inner motivator of radical action); Jer 31:13 (young women rejoice in the dance — joy producing movement and active festivity).

---

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy enables and motivates agency: the consistent pattern is that the inner state of gladness produces outward action (singing, going, giving, dancing, proclaiming). There is no evidence of Joy impairing agency except in the comic instance of Act 12:14 (Rhoda's joy briefly overriding practical action of opening the gate — quickly resolved). The general pattern is joy → enabled, motivated action.

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is agentively generative: persons in the full experience of Joy characteristically act from it — they go, they give, they sing, they proclaim, they persist. This makes Joy a reliable formation resource for agency: communities whose inner life is characterized by Joy will be communities that act, serve, and persist.

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, bidirectionally. (1) **Moral evaluation enabling Joy** — 1Cor 13:6 ("rejoices with the truth") — love's Joy is morally constrained by the capacity to evaluate what is truly good; Psa 97:8 (joy at God's "judgments" — moral appreciation of divine justice produces joy); Job 31:25, 29 (Job denies having rejoiced in wealth or at enemy's ruin — negating morally problematic joy forms); (2) **Joy enabling moral evaluation** — Psa 58:10 ("the righteous will rejoice when he sees the vengeance") — the righteous person's joy at justice being done reflects and reinforces moral evaluation.

---

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Rightly-directed Joy deepens moral evaluation: the person who rejoices in God's judgments (Psa 97:8) is thereby reinforcing their appreciation of divine justice. Misdirected Joy corrupts and bypasses moral evaluation: Pro 2:14 (those who "rejoice in doing evil") show Joy bypassing moral evaluation entirely; Eze 25:6 ("malice within your soul" as source of corrupt joy) shows Joy rooted in moral corruption producing perverse evaluation.

---

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is morally directional: the object of Joy is the most significant moral indicator. Rejoicing in God's judgments, in the truth, in righteousness → moral evaluation deepened; rejoicing in evil, in enemy's ruin, in idols → moral evaluation bypassed or corrupted. The characteristic's moral quality is therefore entirely determined by what it is directed at.

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, in a specific sequence. Psa 51:8, 12 — David's loss of joy is the direct consequence of his sin, and his restoration of joy is sought after genuine repentance (Psa 51:17 — the broken and contrite heart). The sequence is: conscience convicts → joy is lost (Psa 51:8) → repentance → restoration of joy (Psa 51:12). Ecc 8:15's commendation of joy "for God has already approved what you do" implies that conscience clearance is a precondition for genuine, untroubled joy. Pro 29:6 ("a righteous man sings and rejoices") implies that the righteous man's freedom from the snare of sin enables joy.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Genuine Joy follows conscience's work rather than bypassing it: Psa 51 shows conscience doing its full work (conviction, acknowledgement of sin) before Joy is restored. The misdirected-Joy passages (Pro 2:14; Eze 25:6) show joy that has bypassed conscience — these are the corrupted forms. Genuine Joy, in the evidence, consistently comes after or alongside a clear conscience; it does not precede or bypass it.

---

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The sequence conscience → (if needed: repentance) → joy is a consistent pattern where Joy is healthy. Joy that bypasses conscience is corrupted Joy. Joy that follows conscience's convicting and cleansing work is genuine. The Psa 51 pattern (broken → convicted → restored in joy) is the paradigmatic formation sequence for Joy.

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes. Deu 28:47 — failure to serve with "joyfulness and gladness of heart" as the root of covenant judgment — directly links conscientiousness (integrated moral-volitional responsive service) to Joy. The person who is conscientious in covenant service does so joyfully; the absence of joyfulness in service is itself a form of unfaithfulness. Isa 64:5 ("you meet him who joyfully works righteousness") joins Joy and righteous action as a unified inner-being posture. 1Ch 29:17 ("offering freely and joyously to you") joins conscientiousness (upright heart, freewill offering) with Joy as the appropriate inner spirit of morally-conscious giving.

---

**T3.10.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens conscientiousness by motivating and sustaining it: the Deuteronomic feast-joy (Deu 16:11–15) produces the inner condition of gladness that makes covenant service an act of delight rather than duty. Neh 8:10 ("the joy of the Lord is your strength") — the inner joy produced by hearing God's word enables conscientious engagement with the Law's demands. Joyless service (Deu 28:47) produces covenant unfaithfulness — conscientiousness without joy is structural unfaithfulness in the evidence.

---

**T3.10.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy and conscientiousness are constitutively linked: the conscientious covenant person is joyful in their service; the joyful person is motivated toward conscientious action. The removal of Joy produces joyless service; joyless service produces covenant failure. This is the moral-formation logic of Deu 28:47 — the deepest root of covenant unfaithfulness is not external disobedience but the inner loss of joyfulness in God's service.

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes, extensively — Joy is one of the most relationally-constituted characteristics in M04. (1) **God-to-human relational Joy** — God's Joy over his people (Luk 15:7; Zep 3:17; Jer 32:41); (2) **Human-to-God relational Joy** — "rejoice in the Lord" (Psa 32:11; Phili 4:4); (3) **Human-to-human relational Joy** — M04-B-VCG-04 is entirely relational (parental joy over children; spousal joy; friendship joy; communal joy); M04-D is constitutively relational (sunchairo — co-rejoicing); the Pauline joy over specific communities (1Th 2:19–20; 3Jo 4; Phili 4:1) names persons as the content of joy; (4) **Creation-to-creator relational Joy** — the cosmic rejoicing passages (Psa 96:11; 97:1; Isa 49:13) call all creation to rejoice before its Creator.

---

**T3.11.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy deepens relational capacity: M04-D (sunchairo) shows Joy as constitutively completing the relational bond — the co-rejoicing body is the rightly-ordered body; 1Jo 1:4 ("that our joy may be complete" through shared fellowship) and 2Jo 12 ("our joy may be complete" through face-to-face meeting) show Joy as the inner experience that reaches its fullness in genuine relational communion. The Pauline joy-in-communities shows that the relational bonds Paul has with specific people are the substance of his deepest Joy — persons are named as his joy (Phili 4:1; 1Th 2:20). Joy without genuine relational capacity (isolated, self-directed) is not fully evidenced as Joy in this corpus.

---

**T3.11.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Joy is constitutively relational — it reaches its fullness in and through genuine relational bonds. The M04-D sunchairo evidence is the most concentrated expression of this: co-rejoicing is a distinct inner-being phenomenon because Joy is structurally incomplete without being shared. The pattern reveals that Joy has a relational telos: present partial joy moves toward communal and divine-relational fullness (Joh 15:11; 16:24; 1Jo 1:4; Rev 19:7).
