## M04-A — Exultation in YHWH: Vertical Rejoicing Directed Toward God

**Core description:** This sub-group names the OT register of inner exultation directed explicitly toward YHWH himself as the object and ground of joy. The rejoicing here is God-oriented at its core: the soul exults in YHWH's salvation (Psa 35:9; Isa 61:10; 1Sa 2:1), the heart rejoices in God's holy name (Psa 13:5; Psa 32:11), the whole being exults because it is secure in him (Psa 16:9), the prophet resolves to rejoice in the Lord despite total material loss (Hab 3:18). God also appears as the subject: God's own inner delight and rejoicing directed toward his people and their restoration (Jer 32:41 — in all his heart and soul; Zep 3:17 — with loud singing; Isa 62:5 — as bridegroom over bride). The terms here are the OT's primary vertical-exultation vocabulary: gil_verb, gil, su.s, cha.dah, re.na.nah, te.ev. This sub-group is distinguished from M04-B (communal/festive rejoicing) by its vertical, God-directed orientation rather than communal-social dimension, and from M04-C (NT joy in Christ) by its OT theological frame.

**Terms (primary):**
- H1523 gil_verb (mti_id=362)
- H1524A gil (mti_id=359)
- H2302 cha.dah (mti_id=635)
- H2868 te.ev (mti_id=633)
- H7445 re.na.nah (mti_id=2683)
- H7797 su.s (mti_id=363)

**Terms (secondary):** cha.phets_verb (M04-H primary, some verses M04-A adjacent); sa.mach (M04-B primary, personal vertical-joy verses M04-A adjacent); sim.chah (M04-B primary, some M04-A adjacent).

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Psa 13:5 — "The heart is the explicit seat of rejoicing here, and it rejoices in God's salvation in response to trust in his steadfast love; inner joy is grounded in faithful relationship with God, not in outward circumstances."
- Jer 32:41 — "Rejoicing describes God's own inner delight in doing good to his people — rooted in all his heart and soul, it is the deepest volitional and affective commitment he has toward their restoration."
- Zep 3:17 — "God himself will rejoice over his people with loud singing — divine rejoicing is an inner delight directed toward the restored community, expressed outwardly in exultant song."
- Hab 3:18 — "Despite total material loss, the prophet resolves to rejoice in the Lord — the inner act of rejoicing is a willful, faith-driven choice directed entirely toward God as its object."
- Psa 43:4 — "God himself is called 'my exceeding joy' — the rejoicing is not merely felt before God but found in God as its very substance, making the altar-approach an approach to the source of the psalmist's deepest gladness."

## M04-B — Communal and Festive Rejoicing: Gladness in Worship, Feast, and Celebration

**Core description:** This sub-group names the OT's dominant communal-festive-worship register: inner gladness expressed and experienced together in the context of festivals, feasts, temple worship, and the rhythms of Israel's covenant life. The Deuteronomy feast-commands (Deu 12:7, 12, 18; 16:11, 14; 26:11; 27:7; Lev 23:40) establish the pattern — rejoicing before God at the appointed place is commanded for the entire household including servants, Levites, and sojourners. The Chronicles and Nehemiah celebration scenes (2Ch 30:21-26; Neh 12:27, 43) and the temple dedications (Ezr 3:12, 6:22) show this pattern enacted. The joy here is God-produced and socially expansive: it fills entire communities (Neh 12:43 — so intense it carried far away as sound), it motivates generous giving (1Ch 29:9, 17), it marks the coronation of rightful kings (1Ki 1:40; 2Ki 11:14) and the return of sacred objects (2Sa 6:12; 1Sa 6:13). The terms carrying this register — sa.mach (147), sim.chah (89), sa.me.ach (21), ma.s.vo.s (16) — are M04's largest verse-count bearers; all are routed here as primary because the communal-festive register is their plurality.

**Terms (primary):**
- H4885 ma.s.vo.s (mti_id=360)
- H8055 sa.mach (mti_id=365)
- H8056 sa.me.ach (mti_id=355)
- H8057 sim.chah (mti_id=357)

**Terms (secondary):** gil_verb (M04-A primary, some communal instances); su.s (M04-A primary, communal restoration instances).

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Deu 16:14 — "Rejoicing in the feast of Tabernacles is commanded for every household member and marginalized neighbor, showing that Israel's joy is to be shared outwardly and is not confined to the personally fortunate."
- Neh 12:43 — "The rejoicing is God-produced and pervasive—it fills the entire community including women and children—and is so intense it carries outward as sound heard far away, showing its deep communal and inner force."
- 1Ch 29:9 — "The people's rejoicing is caused by their own wholehearted freewill giving — joy arises from the inner alignment of the will with generous devotion, and David shares this gladness greatly."
- Psa 100:2 — "Serving the Lord is to be done with gladness — joy here is the interior quality that should animate and accompany the act of worship and service."
- Neh 8:10 — "The joy of the Lord is declared the source of the people's strength — it is positioned as the inner resource that replaces grief, grounding resilience not in self-effort but in God-given gladness on a holy day."

## M04-C — NT Joy in Christ and the Spirit: Gladness of the Gospel Community

**Core description:** This sub-group names the distinctively New Testament register: inner gladness arising from believing in Christ, from the indwelling Spirit, and from the gospel community's shared life. The Holy Spirit is named as the agent or source of joy (Gal 5:22 — fruit of the Spirit; Act 13:52 — filled with joy and the Holy Spirit; 1Th 1:6 — joy given by the Holy Spirit; Rom 14:17 — joy in the kingdom produced by the Spirit; Rom 15:13 — God fills believers with joy through believing). Rejoicing in the Lord is commanded as a constant, circumstance-independent inner stance (Phil 3:1; 4:4; 1Th 5:16 — "always"). Jesus' own joy is transferred to or indwells the disciples (Joh 15:11; 17:13). Joy arises from faith in the unseen Christ (1Pe 1:8 — inexpressible and filled with glory). The two dominant NT terms — chairo (58 verses) and chara (57 verses) — are both routed here as primary, carrying the full range of NT joy-in-Christ vocabulary. Also included: the Greek terms that are almost exclusively NT (agalliasis, euthumeo, eufrosune, eupsuched, oninemi) and ched.vah (Neh 8:10 — the joy of the Lord as strength, which theologically anticipates the NT register).

**Terms (primary):**
- G0020 agalliasis (mti_id=376)
- G2114 euthumeō (mti_id=2075)
- G2167 eufrosune (mti_id=377)
- G2174 eupsucheō (mti_id=630)
- G3685 oninemi (mti_id=379)
- G5463 chairo (mti_id=381)
- G5479 chara (mti_id=378)
- H2304 ched.vah (mti_id=356)

**Terms (secondary):** agalliao (M04-E primary, some M04-C adjacent); sa.s.von (M04-E primary, NT-adjacent restoration verses).

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Gal 5:22 — "Joy is listed as a fruit of the Spirit, locating it as a Spirit-produced inner quality of character rather than a self-generated emotion."
- Phil 3:1 — "Rejoicing in the Lord is commanded as a standing inner orientation for believers, framed as a safe and stabilizing spiritual posture that Paul will keep reminding them of."
- 1Pe 1:8 — "Joy here is described as inexpressible and filled with glory — it is the quality of the inner rejoicing that believers experience when trusting in the unseen Christ, exceeding what words or ordinary emotion can contain."
- Joh 15:11 — "Jesus' own joy is to be in the disciples and their joy made full through his words, making joy here a transferred, indwelling inner reality that finds its completion in abiding relationship with him."
- Act 13:52 — "Joy is presented as a filling — the disciples are 'filled with joy' — linking it directly to being filled with the Holy Spirit, suggesting joy as a Spirit-produced interior state."

## M04-D — Shared Communal Rejoicing: Joy as Solidarity and Co-Celebration

**Core description:** This sub-group names the register where rejoicing is constitutively shared — sought out, invited into, and completed in community. The sunchairo term (7 verses) defines the register precisely: the shepherd calls neighbours to co-rejoice at the found sheep (Luk 15:6), the woman invites friends to share her joy at the found coin (Luk 15:9), Elizabeth's mercy from God is shared by neighbours and relatives (Luk 1:58), the body members share gladness when one is honoured (1Cor 12:26), love co-rejoices with truth and refuses to rejoice at wrongdoing (1Cor 13:6). The Aramaic ched.vah (Ezr 6:16 — the returned exiles' communal joy at the temple's completion) represents the OT register. This sub-group is distinguished from M04-B (Israel's commanded festive rejoicing) by its focus on the act of sharing joy as an interpersonal bond rather than the festive-communal occasion as such.

**Terms (primary):**
- G4796 sunchairo (mti_id=382)
- H2305 ched.vah Aramaic (mti_id=2688)

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- 1Cor 12:26 — "Shared rejoicing is the inward solidarity of the body: when one member is honored, all experience gladness together, showing communal joy as the natural inner expression of belonging to one body."
- Luk 15:6 — "The shepherd calls friends and neighbors to share his joy over finding the lost sheep, showing that this inner delight is so full it cannot be contained but must be shared communally."
- Ezr 6:16 — "Joy marks the inner spirit of the returned exiles as they celebrate the temple dedication — it is the communal emotional response to the completion of the house of God after years of exile and effort."

## M04-E — Promised and Eschatological Joy: Gladness of Restoration and the Suffering-Paradox

**Core description:** This sub-group names two closely related registers that are analytically distinct from the other M04 sub-groups: (1) the forward-oriented, hope-grounded gladness promised as the direct reversal of sorrow through divine redemption and restoration; and (2) the distinctively NT paradox of joy coexisting with and even arising from suffering. These two registers belong together because both orient joy toward what is not yet fully present — restoration gladness is the joy of what God has promised and the NT suffering-joy is sustained by the hope of that same final reversal (1Pe 4:13 — rejoice now in suffering so as to exult at Christ's revelation). The OT restoration register: everlasting joy crowning the ransomed returning to Zion (Isa 35:10; 51:11), God pouring gladness to replace mourning (Jer 31:13; sa.s.von), the cities of joy transformed from desolation (Jer 33:11; Zec 8:19). The NT suffering-paradox register: rejoicing commanded in persecution because of heavenly reward (Mat 5:12; agalliao), joy coexisting with grief over trials (1Pe 1:6; agalliao), the joy that was set before Christ to sustain the cross (Heb 12:2; chara). The sa.s.von noun (22 verses) is the OT primary term; agalliao (11 verses) bridges both registers in the NT.

**Terms (primary):**
- G0021 agalliao (mti_id=380)
- H1525 gi.lah (mti_id=358)
- H8342 sa.s.von (mti_id=361)

**Terms (secondary):** chairo (M04-C primary, suffering-paradox instances M04-E adjacent); chara (M04-C primary, eschatological instances M04-E adjacent); sim.chah (M04-B primary, eschatological instances M04-E adjacent).

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Isa 35:10 — "Gladness is something the ransomed obtain — acquired through redemption and return — and it stands as the inner counterpart to sorrow and sighing which are driven away in its coming."
- Jer 31:13 — "Rejoicing is given by God as a direct replacement for sorrow — it is the inner emotional state he pours into his people as comfort, paired explicitly against mourning and sorrow as its opposite."
- Mat 5:12 — "The persecuted are commanded to exult (agalliao) in the face of suffering, because their reward is great in heaven — this exulting is a willful, faith-driven inner triumph that transcends present pain."
- 1Pe 1:6 — "Rejoicing coexists with grief over trials — the believer rejoices in their salvation even while being grieved, showing that this rejoicing is a resilient inner orientation toward future hope that holds alongside present pain."
- Rev 19:7 — "Rejoicing is commanded as the corporate inner response to the arrival of the Lamb's marriage — the call to rejoice and exult together is grounded in a great eschatological event, making joy here a worshipful, communal, and glory-directed exultation."

## M04-F — Cheerfulness Under Adversity: Fragile Inner Comfort Amid Suffering

**Core description:** This sub-group names the small but distinct register of cheerfulness as a desired or attempted inner state in the face of extreme adversity — not the paradoxical suffering-joy of M04-E (which is grounded in hope and gospel reality) but the more vulnerable, fragile inner cheer that is barely attainable or entirely absent in the face of grief and mortality. The ba.lag term (3 verses) provides the core: Job considers cheerfulness as something he might attempt by will but cannot sustain (Job 9:27), hopes for a brief moment of cheer before death (Job 10:20), and the psalmist hopes to recover fragile brightness before departure (Psa 39:13). The mav.li.git term (1 verse, Jer 8:18) names cheer as an inner capacity for comfort that is utterly gone, replaced by grief. This register is marked by the fragility and tenuousness of cheerfulness — its presence is marginal, hoped for, or conspicuously absent.

**Terms (primary):**
- H1082 ba.lag (mti_id=637)
- H4010 mav.li.git (mti_id=366)

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- Job 9:27 — "Job considers the inner act of being cheerful as something he could attempt by an act of will—putting off his sad face—but the surrounding context makes clear he cannot sustain it; cheerfulness is a desired but unattainable inner posture."
- Job 10:20 — "Job pleads for a brief respite from suffering so he might find a little cheer before death; the inner state of cheerfulness is presented as minimal and temporary, a small relief he can barely hope for amid overwhelming grief."
- Jer 8:18 — "The prophet's cheer — his inner capacity for comfort — is utterly gone, replaced by grief and a sick heart; the absence of this quality intensifies the weight of the people's devastation."

## M04-G — Delight in God's Word, Law, and Wisdom: Pleasure as Inner Orientation Toward the Divine

**Core description:** This sub-group names the inner register of pleasure taken specifically in God's word, law, testimonies, statutes, and wisdom as its object — and the closely related register of delight in YHWH himself as the direct recipient of the person's inner orientation. The Psalm 119 series is the OT anchor: sha.a.shu.im (119:24, 77, 92, 143, 174) and sha.a (119:16, 47, 70) name an affective inner pleasure that nourishes the soul, sustains through affliction (Psa 119:92 — the only thing that kept the psalmist from perishing), and shapes the will toward obedience (119:24 — testimonies as both delight and counsellors). The a.nog verb (Psa 37:4, 37:11, Job 22:26, 27:10, Isa 58:14) names delight-in-the-Almighty as the soul's proper orientation. The o.neg noun (Isa 58:13) calls the Sabbath itself a delight. The sunēdomai term (Rom 7:22) explicitly locates delight in God's law in the 'inner being.' The sa.lad term (Job 6:10) names inner exultation grounded in faithfulness to God's word under pain. The characteristic here is pleasure-as-inner-disposition directed toward God's word and law, distinguished from M04-A (exultation toward God as person) by the specific object — the word and law — and from M04-H (volitional pleasure) by its affective/pleasurable rather than will-executive character.

**Terms (primary):**
- G4913 sunēdomai (mti_id=802)
- H5539 sa.lad (mti_id=364)
- H6026 a.nog verb (mti_id=789)
- H6027 o.neg (mti_id=800)
- H8173B sha.a (mti_id=3844)
- H8191 sha.a.shu.im (mti_id=790)

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Psa 119:92 — "The law as delight is presented as the only thing that kept the psalmist from perishing in affliction; inner pleasure in God's word functioned as a life-preserving resource under extreme suffering."
- Rom 7:22 — "Delight is explicitly located in the 'inner being,' showing it as the deepest layer of the self; the object is God's law, creating a split between what the inner person delights in and what the flesh does."
- Psa 37:4 — "Delighting in the Lord is an inner orientation of the whole self directed toward God, and it is causally linked to God granting the heart's desires — delight in God aligns the will with his own giving."
- Psa 119:24 — "God's testimonies function as the psalmist's personal delight and also as trusted counselors, so the inner pleasure taken in them shapes decision-making and guides the will."
- Job 6:10 — "Job finds a source of inner exultation even in unsparing pain — his faithfulness to God's word; the joy does not come from comfort but from moral integrity, showing conscience and will as the seat of this rejoicing."

## M04-H — Volitional Delight: Inner Pleasure as Sovereign Will and Directed Desire

**Core description:** This sub-group names the register in which the Hebrew cha.phets and che.phets vocabulary — and the Greek eudokeō — function primarily as markers of inner volitional disposition: willing-pleasure, desire of the will, sovereign preference, and directed inner resolve. The corpus spans divine sovereignty (God's delight as his sovereign will governing history: Isa 44:28, 46:10, 53:10 — God's sovereign purpose accomplished through the servant's suffering; Jer 9:24 — God delights in steadfast love, justice, and righteousness as the exercise of his inner will), divine pleasure in persons (Num 14:8, 2Sa 22:20, Psa 18:19, 37:23, 41:11 — divine delight as the motivating cause of rescue and the operative ground of relationship), human volitional willing (Deu 21:14, 25:7; Rut 3:13 — willingness to redeem; 1Ki 9:1 — desire driving action; Song 2:7, 3:5, 8:4 — love's own readiness), and negated-pleasure marking rejection (Isa 1:11, Hos 6:6, Mal 1:10 — God's non-pleasure in ritual without faithfulness). This is M04's most analytically distinctive register: delight not as felt gladness but as inner will, sovereign purpose, and directed desire. It is distinguished from M04-G (affective law-pleasure) by its volitional-executive character and from M04-A (exultation directed toward God) by its focus on the orientation of the inner will rather than the emotional register of rejoicing.

**Terms (primary):**
- G2106 eudokeō (mti_id=2098)
- H2654A cha.phets verb (mti_id=788)
- H2655 cha.phets adj (mti_id=458)
- H2656 che.phets (mti_id=474)

**Terms (secondary):** sha.a.shu.im (M04-G primary, Pro 8:30-31 and Jer 31:20 have relational-divine-delight adjacent to M04-H).

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Jer 9:24 — "God delights in steadfast love, justice, and righteousness — his inner disposition is oriented toward these moral realities, and knowing this is the only legitimate ground for human boasting."
- 2Sa 22:20 — "God rescued David because he 'delighted in' him; this divine inner delight is presented as the motivating cause of rescue, rooted in God's favorable disposition toward David."
- Isa 53:10 — "The Lord's delight — his sovereign will — is what drives the servant's crushing and suffering; it is not arbitrary cruelty but purposeful divine resolve, showing that delight here is inseparable from redemptive intention."
- Song 2:7 — "Love is not to be stirred or awakened until it pleases — the delight is love's own readiness or timing, implying that love has an inner will or pleasure that must be respected rather than forced."
- Psa 1:2 — "Delight is the inner orientation of the blessed man toward God's law — not duty but pleasure — expressed in constant meditation day and night; the law becomes the heart's chosen object of joy."

## M04-I — Wonder at God's Marvellous Works: Awe Before the Extraordinary

**Core description:** This sub-group names the inner response of awe, marvel, and astonishment directed at God's extraordinary deeds, his creative works, and the incomprehensibility of his ways — the register of M04 that is distinguished from gladness by its contemplative and perceptual character. Pa.la names God's acts as marvellous (a quality that evokes inner awe) and the inner response of wonder those acts should produce: Psa 9:1 (whole-hearted recounting), Psa 78:4 (formative memory passed to generations), Psa 139:14 (the psalmist's praise grounded in being fearfully and wonderfully made), Job 42:3 (Job's humility before incomprehensible divine ways). Pe.le marks God as the one who does wonders surpassing ordinary comprehension: Exo 15:11 (incomparable acts inspiring awe), Isa 9:6 (the child named Wonderful), Isa 25:1 (wondrous fulfilled plans). Miph.la.ah (Job 37:16) names the balancing of clouds as evoking wonder at divine perfection. The corpus also includes the pa.la register of "too difficult / extraordinary" (Deu 17:8; Jer 32:17, 27 — nothing is too wonderful for God; Gen 18:14) and wonder-as-dark-astonishment (Dan 8:24; Dan 11:36; Lam 1:9 — appalling wonder at catastrophe). The inner characteristic across the corpus is wonder-as-awe: the particular register of inward astonishment before what surpasses human measure.

**Terms (primary):**
- H4652 miph.la.ah (mti_id=6701)
- H6381 pa.la (mti_id=1244)
- H6382 pe.le (mti_id=1243)

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Psa 139:14 — "The psalmist's praise is grounded in being fearfully and wonderfully made; wonder here is an attribute of God's creative works that the soul knows intimately and that evokes worshipful awe."
- Exo 15:11 — "God is acclaimed as doing wonders — his incomparable acts inspire awe and astonishment among those who witness them, pointing to a divine character that exceeds all human categories."
- Job 42:3 — "Job recognizes he spoke of things too wonderful for him — beyond his grasp; wonder here marks the boundary of human understanding, and Job's inner response is humility before the incomprehensible nature of God's ways."
- Psa 77:11 — "The psalmist deliberately calls to memory the Lord's wonders of old as a spiritual exercise — recalling wonder-acts is the inner strategy for sustaining faith through present distress."

## M04-J — Pleasantness and Relational Delight: The Agreeableness of Persons, Bonds, and Creation

**Core description:** This sub-group names the experiential register of pleasantness as a quality of what is agreeable, beautiful, and nourishing to the inner person. Distinguished from M04-A and M04-C by its receptive rather than exultant character, this register names the pleasant quality of things, persons, relationships, and speech that is noticed, appreciated, and inwardly received. The terms (na.im, na.em, no.am, na.a.man, ed.nah) cluster around the root meaning of pleasantness-as-felt-quality: the Lord's own being as the supreme object of longing (Psa 27:4 — no.am), the pleasantness of wisdom's ways (Pro 3:17), gracious words like honey nourishing soul and body (Pro 16:24), Jonathan's companionship deeply pleasant to David (2Sa 1:26 — in the soul), brotherly unity as pleasant (Psa 133:1), God's river of delights in worship (Psa 36:8 — ed.nah), eternal pleasures at God's right hand (Psa 16:11 — na.im). The register spans divine beauty, relational warmth, and the pleasantness of wise speech — all named by the experiential quality that makes them inwardly agreeable.

**Terms (primary):**
- H5273A na.im (mti_id=549)
- H5276 na.em (mti_id=798)
- H5278 no.am (mti_id=1654)
- H5282 na.a.man (mti_id=1656)
- H5730B ed.nah (mti_id=3837)

**Representative meanings (3-5 verses):**
- Psa 27:4 — "Gazing on the beauty of the Lord in his temple is the one supreme desire; pleasantness describes the Lord's own being — his beauty is the object of the psalmist's deepest longing and the goal of his inner seeking."
- 2Sa 1:26 — "David grieves that Jonathan's companionship was deeply pleasant to him, surpassing even the love of women; the inner relational quality is one of profound emotional warmth and closeness experienced in the soul."
- Pro 16:24 — "Gracious words are like honey — pleasantness that enters and nourishes soul and body alike, showing that pleasant speech has a healing, restorative inner impact."
- Psa 36:8 — "God's 'river of delights' is the source from which worshippers drink, making delight something lavishly supplied by God himself — an overwhelming pleasure experienced by those who abide in his house."
