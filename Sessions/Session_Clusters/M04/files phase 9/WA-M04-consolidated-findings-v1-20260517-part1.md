## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**T0.1.1** — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**[A]**
E — God possesses joy directed toward his people as a constituent of his nature. Jer 32:41 (su.s) locates divine rejoicing in "all his heart and soul" — the deepest volitional and affective core. Zep 3:17 names both exultation (gil_verb) and gladness (su.s) as God's inner delight toward his restored people, paired with quieting love. Isa 62:5 (su.s) portrays God rejoicing over Zion as a bridegroom over a bride; Isa 65:19 (su.s + gil_verb) names both terms for God's own delight. These establish that joy in God is relational, directional, and rooted in his deepest inner being.

**[H]**
E — M04-H VCG-01 reveals God's nature as sovereign will and pleasure enacted: Psa 115:3 and 135:6 (cha.phets) establish that God's pleasure is sovereign — he does whatever he pleases, pleasure and power unified. Mat 12:18 (eudokeō) explicitly locates divine delight in God's soul (psychē). Isa 53:10 (cha.phets + che.phets) reveals that divine delight extends into redemptive suffering — sovereign pleasure is inseparable from purposeful redemptive action. M04-H VCG-02 reveals God's relational delight as the operative cause of saving acts: Psa 18:19 (God delighted in David — cause of rescue); Psa 51:6 (God delights in truth in the inward being and secret heart — the most interior statement); Jer 9:24 (God delights in steadfast love, justice, righteousness); Eze 18:23; 33:11 (God takes no pleasure in the wicked's death, delighting in repentance).

**[C]**
E — M04-C VCG-01 reveals God's nature through the Father's declaration at Jesus' baptism and transfiguration (Mat 3:17; 17:5; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22 — eudokeō). M04-C VCG-02 (Joh 15:11) reveals that Christ possesses his own joy (chara) and intentionally transfers it to his disciples — divine joy is inherently communicative rather than self-contained.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Across M04-A and M04-H the cluster reveals that joy and delight are not merely human characteristics that God observes but constituents of God's own nature enacted in at least three modes: (1) God's sovereign delight as his unconstrained will (M04-H VCG-01); (2) God's relational delight toward persons as the basis of redemptive action (M04-H VCG-02, M04-A); (3) God's gift of joy actively placed into the human person (M04-B VCG-05, Psa 4:7; M04-C VCG-04, Gal 5:22). The human characteristic images a God who rejoices, delights, and transfers his joy to those he loves.

---

**T0.1.2** — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?

**[A]**
E — Yes, explicitly. Jer 32:41 (su.s): God rejoices in doing good to his people "with all his heart and all his soul" — nephesh named as seat. Zep 3:17 (su.s + gil_verb): God's rejoicing over his restored people with gladness and exultant singing. Isa 65:19 (su.s + gil_verb): God rejoices and is glad in Jerusalem. The attribution reveals that joy in the human person is not a creaturely compensation for finitude but a reflection of something at the centre of God's own being.

**[H]**
E — Explicitly, via eudokeō. Mat 12:18 (psychē — soul), Mat 3:17, Mar 1:11, Luk 3:22, Mat 17:5, 2Pe 1:17 attribute divine delight to God's own soul directed toward Christ. Luk 12:32 (eudokeō): "It is your Father's good pleasure to give you the kingdom." The attribution reveals divine delight as relational (toward persons) and generative (it gives rather than merely approves). Human delight mirroring this would be directed toward others and would generate rather than merely evaluate.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes. The explicit attributions (Jer 32:41; Mat 12:18 — soul/psychē named as seat) reveal two things: (1) joy in the human person reflects an attribute of the divine nature, not merely a creaturely necessity; (2) divine joy is always directional and generative — it moves toward its object and produces good there. Human joy in this cluster follows the same pattern when rightly operating.

**T0.1.3** — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?

**[B]**

God commands the feasts and produces joy in them (Neh 12:43; Ezr 6:22), but God himself is not portrayed as keeping festivals. The festive-communal register is primarily a creaturely response to divine goodness rather than a divine attribute imaged.

**[G, I, J]**

The silence suggests these registers describe the human person's inner orientation toward God and his world — creaturely equipment for engaging God's gifts, not mirrors of a shared divine attribute.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The silence is most pronounced in the experiential and receptive registers (M04-F, M04-G, M04-I, M04-J). These describe the human person's inner orientation toward God's world rather than a shared attribute. The implication is that these registers function as creaturely equipment for perceiving and receiving what God offers.

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

**T0.2.1** — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?

**[A]**
E — M04-A establishes that vertical rejoicing toward YHWH serves to bind the person to God in the most intimate inner relationship. Psa 43:4 (anchor): God is the psalmist's "exceeding joy" — not merely the occasion but the substance. Hab 3:18 reveals this purpose is fundamental: rejoicing toward God can be sustained in total material loss, not contingent on circumstances but on God himself.

**[B]**
E — M04-B establishes that communal and festive joy serves to create and sustain inclusive community before God. Deu 16:11 (anchor): joy deliberately structured to include every social stratum — servants, Levites, the vulnerable. Deu 28:47 reveals the stakes: joy in serving God is constitutive of covenant life, not decorative — its absence marks covenant breakdown.

**[C]**
E — M04-C establishes that NT joy serves to complete and sustain the community of believers. Joh 15:11: Christ's purpose is that his joy may be in them and their joy full — teleological aim. Phil 4:4 and 1Th 5:16 establish constant joy as the interior structural disposition of the community's life, equipping believers to maintain orientation toward Christ under all conditions including suffering.

**[G, H]**
E — M04-G establishes that delight in God's law serves to make obedience a matter of desire rather than duty. Psa 119:92: law-delight prevented perishing — the characteristic equips the inner person to maintain fidelity in extremis. M04-H establishes that volitional delight serves to align the human will with what is genuinely good: Psa 37:4 (anchor) — delighting in the Lord aligns the will with his giving; M04-H VCG-03 corpus — what a person delights in determines what they pursue (Gen 34:19; 1Ki 9:1; Psa 40:8).

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Joy, gladness, and delight serve at minimum four created purposes: (1) binding the person to God in willing, pleasurable relationship (M04-A, M04-G); (2) creating and sustaining covenant community inclusive of all social strata (M04-B); (3) sustaining orientation toward God even under adversity (M04-C VCG-05, M04-E); (4) aligning desire with what is genuinely good, making obedience an inner inclination rather than external compliance (M04-H). The cluster presents joy as the inner faculty that integrates will with good — what the person wants and what is good for them converge when this characteristic operates rightly.

---

**T0.2.2** — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**[A, G, H]**
E — Evidence indicates original created design. M04-G (Psa 119:92; Psa 37:4; Psa 40:8) presents delight in the Lord and his law as the normative posture of the human person before God — not a remedy for sin. M04-H's divine sovereign pleasure is pre-creation in scope (Isa 46:10). Pro 8:30 (sha.a.shu.im) places Wisdom's delight alongside God before creation.

**[C, E]**
E — NT registers carry a clear post-fall, redemptive dimension. M04-C joy is constituted by redemption (1Pe 1:8; Luk 24:52; Act 15:3; Gal 5:22; 1Th 1:6). Jam 1:2 (counting trials as joy) is explicitly a response to the fallen condition. M04-E VCG-01 sa.s.von — gladness as reversal of sorrow — is joy that would not exist without the fall.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The characteristic spans both registers. Original created design is most clearly evidenced in M04-G and M04-H (delight in God as the normative inner posture), M04-I (wonder at God's works as the natural creature-Creator response), and M04-J (pleasantness in a rightly ordered world). Response to the fallen condition is most clearly evidenced in M04-C, M04-E, and M04-C VCG-05.

**T0.2.3** — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?

**[A, C, E]**
E — Yes, consistently. M04-A: Psa 14:7 anticipates eschatological rejoicing at restoration; Isa 65:18 commands eternal rejoicing in the new creation. M04-C: Mat 25:21/23 ("enter into the joy of your master" — eschatological destination); Jude 24 (God presents the preserved believer before his glory in great exultation agalliasis); Joh 16:22/24 (joy permanently secured, filled to capacity). M04-E VCG-01: Isa 35:10 and 51:11 ("everlasting joy" resting on the ransomed as a crown). M04-J: Psa 16:11 ("pleasures forevermore" at God's right hand). Rev 19:7 (communal rejoicing at the Lamb's marriage — the eschatological culmination of M04-C and M04-E VCG-02).

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes. The cluster contains explicit eschatological markers across multiple sub-groups. Present joy is presented as anticipatory — a foretaste of what will be fully realised when God's redemptive purposes are complete. The suffering-paradox register (M04-C VCG-05, M04-E VCG-02) is intelligible precisely because present joy is not its own fullness: it is oriented toward a joy that surpasses and sustains it.

### T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression

**T0.3.1** — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?

**[A, H]**
E — The most direct divine-image expression is in directed delight: God delights in steadfast love (Mic 7:18), in truth in the inward being (Psa 51:6), in his Son (Mat 12:18), in his people's restoration (Jer 32:41). The human person who delights in the Lord (Psa 37:4; M04-G), in God's commandments (Psa 112:1; M04-H VCG-03), and in God's people (Psa 16:3) mirrors the same directional structure. The image is expressed not merely in the fact of rejoicing but in its object-direction: toward God and what God values.

**[B, D]**
E — The inclusive, communal character of feast joy (M04-B VCG-01) expresses the divine image socially: God's joy overflows toward others (Jer 32:41; Zep 3:17) and the human person made in his image is equipped to extend joy outward to include the vulnerable. M04-D (sunchairo, 1Cor 12:26) expresses the image through constitutive co-rejoicing: when one member is honoured, all share gladness — mirroring the joy that Christ transfers to his disciples (Joh 15:11) and the Father's joy in the Son (Mat 12:18).

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The divine image is expressed through (1) directed delight toward a specific object — God, his word, his people; (2) communicative joy — rejoicing that moves outward to include others; (3) volitional pleasure — choosing and sustaining joy through deliberate orientation; (4) generative joy — producing healing (Pro 17:22), praise, proclamation, and community.

---

**T0.3.2** — Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or exclusively a creaturely analogue?

**[A, H, C]**
E — Shared in the most direct sense. The same Hebrew and Greek terms are used of God and the human person within the same VCGs (su.s, sim.chah, cha.phets, eudokeō). M04-C VCG-02 goes beyond analogy: Christ's own joy is transferred into the disciples (Joh 15:11; 17:13) — not imitation but participation. The creaturely form is conditioned (responsive, requiring renewal, capable of perversion) while divine joy is sovereign and self-sustaining. But the underlying characteristic is structurally shared, not merely analogical.

---

**T0.3.3** — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?

**[A, B, H]**
E — Presence of rightly directed joy reveals the image functioning as intended: Psa 32:11 (righteous commanded to joy directed toward the Lord); Psa 97:12 (righteous directed to rejoice toward the Lord); Neh 8:10 (joy of the Lord as source of strength). Absence or misdirection reveals the image in corruption: M04-A VCG-02 (18 inverted/absent instances); M04-B VCG-06 (59 instances — silenced by judgment, misdirected, hollow). Deu 28:47 is explicit: joylessness in serving God is itself a covenantal failure, and judgment follows — joylessness marks a broken covenant and a diminished image. M04-H VCG-04 reveals the most corrupted form: 2Th 2:12 (condemned for taking pleasure in unrighteousness); Eze 25:6 (malice in the soul as the seat of corrupt joy). The condemned delight is not morally neutral — directed delight toward what God abhors is the image in deep corruption.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Diagnostic: (1) joy directed toward God and what he values = image functioning; (2) joy directed toward what God abhors or toward another's ruin = image corrupted; (3) absence of joy in circumstances that normatively produce it = image diminished or under judgment. The characteristic functions as one of the clearest indicators of the image's condition.

### T0.4 — Typological Significance

**T0.4.1** — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?

**[A, C]**
E — Yes. Joh 3:29 makes OT typology explicit: John the Baptist's joy as friend of the bridegroom stepping aside — the OT figure of joy at the sacred presence finds its NT referent. Luk 1:44 (unborn John's agalliasis at Mary's greeting) is fulfilment of the forerunner's joy at the bridegroom's approach. Mat 25:21/23 ("enter into your master's joy") deploys the master-servant relationship as the eschatological joy-destination.

**[B, E]**
E — Israel's festive joy at the temple-feasts functions typologically toward the eschatological feast. Rev 19:7 (Lamb's marriage — agalliao) stands as the NT counterpart of Israel's sacrificial worship. M04-E VCG-01 sa.s.von (Isa 35:10; 51:11) is deployed typologically as anticipation of the final return.

---

**T0.4.2** — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?

**[A, B, C, E]**
E — The direction runs consistently from human instance toward divine fulfilment, or from OT figure toward NT and eschatological reality. Joy at the ark's return (1Sa 6:13; 2Sa 6:12) anticipates the joy of the divine presence dwelling definitively among his people (Rev 19:7; Joh 15:11). Israel's feast joy anticipates eschatological celebration. OT restoration-gladness (sa.s.von) anticipates NT redemption-joy (agalliasis, 1Pe 1:8). The human or OT instance is the shadow; the divine or eschatological instance is the reality.

---

**T0.4.3** — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[F, G, J]**
S — M04-F (cheerfulness), M04-G (word-delight), and M04-J (pleasantness) do not show explicit typological deployment. They evidence the characteristic in its direct register — the human person relating to God's word, his beauty, and fragile inner comfort — without Scripture using these as explicit types pointing to a larger redemptive reality. These sub-groups describe interior relational states rather than public saving events that lend themselves to typological structuring.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

**T1.1.1** — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The programme name "Joy, Gladness and Delight" (M04) signals three related but distinguishable registers: (1) joy — the exultant, high-arousal response directed toward God and his acts (M04-A, M04-C); (2) gladness — the warmer, communal, festive inner state (M04-B, M04-D); (3) delight — the quieter, directed pleasure resting in something valued (M04-G, M04-H, M04-J). The compound signals that the cluster does not reduce to a single inner-being phenomenon but spans a family of positive inner states distinguished by intensity, direction, and constitutional location.

---

**T1.1.2** — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?

**[A]**
E — gil_verb (H1523) and gil (H1524A) carry the sense of spinning, whirling movement — high-arousal exultation that cannot remain still. Psa 43:4 (anchor): God named as "my exceeding joy" — the substance, not merely the occasion. su.s (H7797) carries vigorous, running gladness (Psa 19:5 — the sun's course; Isa 62:5 — bridegroom's gladness). re.na.nah (H7445) names triumphing-cry joy: inherently vocal, outward-directed exultation. cha.dah (H2302) names inner gladness of response to news (Exo 18:9 — Jethro's gladness at hearing God's acts).

**[B]**
E — sa.mach (H8055, verb) and sim.chah (H8057, noun) span the widest semantic range in the cluster: prescribed feast joy, civic joy, wisdom-life gladness, and inverted joy all use these terms — the most generalized positive inner state. ma.s.vo.s (H4885) names concentrated noun-form rejoicing as an identity-quality (Psa 48:2 — Mount Zion as "joy of all the earth"; Isa 62:5 — God's rejoicing as ma.s.vo.s).

**[C]**
E — chara (G5479) is the most programmatic NT term: a stable inner state that can be full, permanent, transferred, and Spirit-produced. agalliasis (G0020) and agalliao (G0021) carry exceeding exultation — high-intensity, eschatologically oriented (Luk 1:44 — physical immediacy; 1Pe 1:8 — inexpressible and full of glory). eufrosune (G2167) names glad-spiritedness at the communal table (Act 2:46; 14:17).

**[G, H]**
E — sha.a.shu.im (H8191) and sha.a (H8173B) carry playing with, dwelling in, savoring — sustained affective engagement with something valued (Psa 119:24 testimonies as personal delight and counsellors; Jer 31:20 God's tender cherishing of Ephraim). cha.phets (H2654A) and che.phets (H2656) reveal that delight is inseparable from willing — the same term names joy and the exercise of will (Psa 40:8; Isa 46:10). eudokeō (G2106) names good-pleasure as a willing, approving inner disposition — God's soul's approval (Mat 12:18) and human willing satisfaction (Rom 15:26; 2Cor 5:8).

**[I, J]**
E — pa.la (H6381) names extraordinary acts exceeding expectation — the inner response is wonder at what God has done (Psa 139:14 anchor). no.am (H5278) names pleasantness as a quality of being: the Lord's own pleasantness (Psa 27:4 anchor); wisdom's ways as pleasantness itself (Pro 3:17). na.em (H5276) names the experiential quality of what is agreeable in relationship (2Sa 1:26 — Jonathan's companionship pleasant to the soul).

---

**T1.1.3** — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — All three. **Directional:** the vocabulary consistently implies an object toward which joy moves — toward God (M04-A, M04-C, M04-G), toward God's people (M04-B, M04-D), toward God's law (M04-G), or away from God (inverted register, M04-A VCG-02, M04-B VCG-06, M04-H VCG-04). Directionless joy is not evidenced as a positive inner state; its absence or misdirection is the mark of corruption. **Relational:** joy in the cluster is constitutively relational — arising in response to someone or something, and seeking community (M04-D sunchairo; Psa 4:7 — God places it in the heart; Joh 15:11 — Christ's joy transferred). **Constitutional:** the terms carry depth-markers — heart (sim.chah; Psa 4:7), soul (sa.mach directed to the Lord; Isa 61:10; nephesh in Jer 32:41), spirit (agalliao in the spirit, Luk 1:47), inner being (sunēdomai in the inner being, Rom 7:22).

### T1.2 — Kind

**T1.2.1** — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?

**[A, B, C]**
E — The dominant registers show joy operating as an affective-volitional disposition: it is felt (Psa 4:7 — joy placed in the heart; Psa 16:11 — fullness of joy) and directed by the will (Hab 3:18 — resolve to rejoice; 1Th 5:16 — commanded always; Jam 1:2 — count trials as joy). Joy in its highest form is a disposition — a stable inner orientation of the whole person toward a valued object — not merely an episodic state.

**[G]**
E — M04-G shows joy as affective pleasure directed toward a cognitive object (God's law, testimonies, wisdom). Psa 119:24: testimonies as personal delight shaping decision-making. Psa 119:92: law-delight as existentially life-preserving. This register is contemplative — sustained dwelling-in rather than eruption.

**[H]**
E — M04-H shows joy primarily as volitional delight — the directive force of the inner will. Cha.phets is equally desire, will, and delight because they are unified in it: what the person delights in is what they choose and pursue (Gen 34:19 — delight driving urgent action; 1Ki 9:1 — Solomon builds all he desired; Psa 40:8 — delight as heartfelt willingness). This register shows joy as the animating force of agency.

**[I]**
E — M04-I shows joy (wonder) operating as a perceptive-cognitive response: wonder requires seeing and recognising the extraordinary (Psa 107:24 — seafarers see God's wondrous works in the deep; Psa 119:18 — wonder requires divine enablement to perceive; Psa 139:14 — the soul knows intimately). This is the most cognitively constituted register.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Joy is a family of inner-being phenomena sharing the common structure of positive, directed inner engagement with a valued object. Sub-groups differ in which faculty is primary: affect (M04-A, M04-B, M04-C); will (M04-H); pleasure-cognition (M04-G); perception (M04-I); receptive agreeableness (M04-J). The characteristic is neither purely act, nor purely disposition, nor purely condition: it encompasses all three, with different registers emphasising different aspects.

---

**T1.2.2** — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Complex in structure. The cluster reveals at minimum five constituent elements: (1) an affective component (felt gladness, inner warmth — M04-A, M04-B, M04-J); (2) a volitional component (directed willing, chosen delight — M04-H, M04-A Hab 3:18, M04-C Phil 4:4); (3) a cognitive component (recognition of what is good, true, or extraordinary — M04-I, M04-G); (4) a relational component (directed toward a person or personal God, communicated to others — M04-D, M04-C VCG-03); (5) an expressive component (joy as outward vocal or bodily expression — Psa 63:5; Neh 12:43; 1Cor 12:26). These constituents are not always co-present: M04-H can show volitional delight without high affect; M04-F shows the attempt at affective cheerfulness that the volitional component alone cannot sustain (Job 9:27).

---

**T1.2.3** — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Joy, gladness, and delight across M04 is best described as: a family of positive inner-being orientations — spanning exultation, gladness, and delight — in which the human person's affect, will, cognition, and relational capacity are engaged in positive directional response to what is valued, true, or extraordinary, with God himself as the supreme object. The characteristic ranges from high-arousal exultation in response to God's saving acts (M04-A) through sustained affective pleasure in God's word (M04-G) to the quiet receptive pleasantness of agreeableness in created bonds (M04-J). It is constitutional (located in heart, soul, and spirit), volitional (commanded and cultivated, not merely reactive), relational (directed toward a person, communicated to community), and teleological (oriented toward eschatological fullness).

### T1.3 — Boundary

**T1.3.1** — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?

**[A, B, E]**
E — The structural opposites evidenced are: (1) grief and mourning — the primary affective opposite (Psa 30:11 — God transforms mourning into gladness; Jer 31:13 — rejoicing as direct replacement for sorrow; Isa 35:10 — gladness displacing sorrow and sighing; Lam 5:15 — "the joy of our hearts has ceased; our dancing turned to mourning"); (2) joylessness as covenant judgment (Deu 28:47 — failure to serve with joy; Joe 1:16 — joy cut off from God's house; Jer 7:34 — voice of gladness silenced); (3) absent cheerfulness as the limit of M04-F (Jer 8:18 — inner capacity for comfort gone, replaced by grief).

**[H]**
E — M04-H VCG-04 establishes that the structural opposite of genuine delight is: divine non-pleasure or withholding of approval (Mal 1:10; Heb 10:6/8; Heb 10:38 — God's soul has no pleasure); and morally culpable delight in evil (2Th 2:12 — pleasure in unrighteousness as basis of judgment; Eze 36:5 — wholehearted joy over another's ruin as moral corruption).

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Structural opposites across the cluster: mourning/grief (primary affective opposite); joylessness under judgment (covenantal opposite); corrupt or misdirected joy (moral opposite — M04-A VCG-02, M04-B VCG-06); absent cheerfulness under extreme adversity (M04-F). Note: misdirected joy is not the absence of this characteristic but its perversion — the inner faculty functioning toward a wrong object.

---

**T1.3.2** — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?

**[A]**
E — Rightly directed joy toward God excludes enemy gloating (Psa 13:4), coexistence with mourning over the same occasion once God has acted (Psa 30:11), and fear-based servility that displaces joy in serving (Deu 28:47). Hab 3:18 shows that genuine God-directed joy resists circumstantial determinism — it is chosen against all material loss.

**[C]**
E — M04-C VCG-05 shows NT joy explicitly resisting: reduction to reactions to favourable circumstances (Jam 1:2 — trials counted as pure joy); grief monopolising the inner being (2Cor 6:10 — sorrowful yet always rejoicing); shallow rootlessness (Mat 13:20; Luk 8:13 — joy real but unable to sustain is exposed as insufficient).

**[H]**
E — M04-H VCG-04 shows delight toward evil explicitly excluded and condemned: Isa 58:3 (pursuing own pleasure on the fast day as direct opposite of honouring God); 2Th 2:12 (pleasure in unrighteousness as the basis of judgment). The characteristic actively resists misdirection toward what God abhors.

---

**T1.3.3** — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?

**[A vs B vs G]**
E — M04-A (exultation toward YHWH) is distinct from M04-B (communal/festive joy) in direction: M04-A is primarily vertical, God as object; M04-B is primarily communal, feast and community as context. M04-G (word-delight) is distinct from M04-A in mode: M04-A is high-arousal exultation; M04-G is sustained affective pleasure in a cognitive object.

**[H vs G]**
E — M04-H (cha.phets — volitional delight) is distinct from M04-G (sha.a.shu.im — affective delight) in that M04-H is primarily about the direction of the will — delight as what one chooses, purposes, pursues — while M04-G is about affective pleasure resting in an object. Psa 40:8 bridges both: "I delight to do your will; your law is within my heart."

**[I vs J]**
E — M04-I (wonder at God's works) is distinct from M04-J (pleasantness) in its cognitive-perceptive character: wonder requires encountering the extraordinary and being unable to fully comprehend it (Job 42:3 — things too wonderful for me). M04-J does not require incomprehensibility — it is the quality of what is agreeable, harmonious, and nourishing in ordinary relational experience.

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — This characteristic is not: (1) mere emotional happiness contingent on favourable circumstances (excluded by M04-C VCG-05 and M04-E VCG-02's suffering-paradox); (2) contentment or peace — adjacent but distinct, joy is positively directional and often high-arousal while contentment is settledness; (3) gratitude — related but distinct, gratitude is specifically the response to a received benefit while joy can be primary (Psa 37:4 — delighting in God before receiving anything); (4) the mere absence of grief — joy is positive, not merely the negation of mourning.

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**T1.4.1** — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Five distinct modes are evidenced: (1) Response mode: joy arising as direct inner response to an external stimulus — divine saving action (Psa 31:7; Neh 12:43), news of deliverance (Est 8:15), seeing the risen Lord (Joh 20:20). (2) Command mode: joy as commanded inner posture sustained through volitional commitment regardless of circumstance (Phil 4:4; 1Th 5:16; Jam 1:2; Hab 3:18). (3) Gift mode: joy received as divine gift infused into the person (Psa 4:7 — God places joy in the heart; Gal 5:22 — fruit of the Spirit; 1Th 1:6 — Spirit-given alongside affliction). (4) Transfer mode: joy communicated from one person to another (Joh 15:11 — Christ's own joy transferred; 2Cor 7:13 — Titus's refreshed spirit amplifies Paul's joy; 1Th 3:9 — Paul's joy for the Thessalonians overflowing before God). (5) Formation mode: joy as inner disposition sustained through relationship with God's word over time (Psa 119:92 — law delight sustaining through affliction; M04-G corpus generally).

---

**T1.4.2** — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes. By direction: joy toward God (M04-A — vertical, highest-intensity, gil_verb as spinning exultation) differs from joy toward community (M04-B/D — communal gladness) and joy toward God's word (M04-G — quieter, contemplative). By context: joy under adversity (M04-C VCG-05, M04-F) operates paradoxically — coexisting with grief and requiring deliberate volitional orientation; joy in festive contexts (M04-B VCG-01) is commanded and communal; joy in eschatological contexts (M04-E) is forward-oriented and hope-sustained. By constitutional level: heart-level gladness (Psa 13:5; Psa 33:21; Song 3:11) is intimate and personal; soul-level joy (Psa 35:9; 86:4; Isa 61:10) is deeper; spirit-level exultation (Luk 1:47; Luk 10:21) is most transcendent and Spirit-proximate. M04-F shows cheerfulness attempting the surface level without depth of ground and failing to sustain (Job 9:27).

---

**T1.4.3** — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?

**[A, B, I]**
E — Yes, strongly. M04-A and M04-B show joy consistently generating vocal expression: re.na.nah (triumphing-cry, Psa 63:5; 100:2) is inherently vocal. Neh 12:43: joy so intense it is heard far away. 1Ch 15:16: joy summoned through Levitical music (sim.chah). God's own rejoicing expressed as exultant singing (Zep 3:17). M04-I VCG-01 shows wonder consistently moving toward declaring God's wondrous works (1Ch 16:9 — tell of God's wondrous works; 1Ch 16:24 — declare among all nations; Psa 9:1 — recounting with the whole heart) — proclamation is wonder's communicative mode.

**[C]**
E — M04-C shows NT joy generating pastoral testimony and speech: Act 11:23 (Barnabas's inward rejoicing at grace immediately flows into exhortation); 2Cor 7:4 (Paul's overflowing joy expressed as confidence about the Corinthians); 3Jo 3 (rejoicing at hearing testimony of faithfulness). Joy functions as the inner motivating force for pastoral communication.

### T1.5 — Immediate Response

**T1.5.1** — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?

**[A, C]**
E — The most immediate responses evidenced are overflow into expression. Luk 19:6 (Zacchaeus receives Jesus joyfully and immediately acts); Luk 19:37 (disciples burst forth loudly in praise, driven by inner conviction); Act 12:14 (Rhoda's joy so immediate she forgets to open the gate); Mat 28:8 (great joy propels the women immediately to tell the disciples). Joy at its most immediate is an almost uncontainable outward movement. In M04-C VCG-02 (Joh 16:22; 20:20) the immediate inner-being response is the heart's gladness — the heart is the first named seat of the response.

---

**T1.5.2** — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The response is consistent in structure (positive, directed, often overflowing) but varies in form. The consistent element: joy when genuinely received produces movement toward an object, toward expression, or toward community — it does not remain contained. What varies is the mode of overflow: vocal praise (Psa 63:5; Neh 12:43), physical action (Luk 19:6; Mat 28:8), communal proclamation (1Ch 16:24), or inner settled gladness (Psa 16:11; 1Pe 1:8 — inexpressible, not necessarily immediately expressed). M04-F shows the exception: Job 9:27 — the attempt at cheerfulness produces no overflow because the ground is insufficient.

**T1.5.3** — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.

**[G, H]**
S — M04-G (word-delight) and M04-H VCG-01 (divine sovereign pleasure) are largely silent on what the immediate inner response to encountering these forms of delight is, because the delight is already the operative inner state rather than a response to something received. Psa 119:24 presents law-delight as a standing orientation, not an immediate response. M04-H VCG-01 (God doing what he pleases) describes the characteristic as constitutive of divine will, not as arising in response to anything. The silence is appropriate to the sub-group's register.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**T1.6.1** — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?

**[A, B]**
E — Sustained joy directed toward God produces a stable, non-circumstantial inner orientation. Psa 89:16 (gill_verb): "all day long" — joy sustained as continuous rejoicing in God's name. Deu 28:47 establishes by contrast: sustained joylessness in serving God produces covenant breakdown — its sustained presence produces covenant vitality. M04-B VCG-05: Psa 85:6 — God's revival aims at the people's rejoicing as its telos; rejoicing is the sustained fruit of divine renewing work.

**[G]**
E — M04-G shows that sustained delight in God's law produces life-preserving capacity: Psa 119:92 (the only thing that prevented perishing in affliction); Psa 119:174 (delight sustaining while waiting for deliverance). Over time, law-delight becomes a structural resource against collapse, not merely a pleasant experience.

**[C]**
E — M04-C shows that sustained joy in Christ produces: community unity (Phil 2:2 — Paul's joy complete when the community achieves unity); fruitfulness in ministry (Heb 13:17 — leaders who shepherd with joy rather than groaning benefit those under them); and a stable disposition that holds through suffering (2Cor 6:10 — sorrowful yet always rejoicing; 2Cor 8:2 — joy overflows in generous giving even in poverty).

---

**T1.6.2** — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained exposure establish?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Sustained operation of this characteristic establishes: (1) a non-circumstantial inner stability (M04-C Phil 4:4; M04-E — joy sustained through suffering because its ground is eschatological); (2) increased relational capacity and communal solidarity (M04-D sunchairo; M04-C VCG-03 — joy the goal and fruit of Paul's co-labour); (3) a strengthened faculty of perception toward God's acts (M04-I — wonder sustained through memory and proclamation, Psa 77:11; 105:5); (4) alignment of will with what is genuinely good (M04-H Psa 37:4; Psa 40:8 — delight in God aligning desire with his giving); (5) physical wellbeing as an established outcome (Pro 17:22 — joyful heart as good medicine — the most direct constitutional body-soul statement in the cluster).

---

**T1.6.3** — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes, consistently. The immediate response is overflow and expression (Neh 12:43 — heard far away; Mat 28:8 — urgent movement). The sustained effect is stability, alignment, and capacity: the person who has sustained joy over time is not perpetually in a state of emotional eruption but has an inner orientation that enables fruitful ministry (Heb 13:17), sustains under suffering (M04-C VCG-05), maintains the will aligned with God (Psa 37:4), and preserves life under affliction (Psa 119:92). The sustained effect is quieter and more structural than the immediate response.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

**T1.7.1** — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?

**[A]**
E — Trust in God's steadfast love (Psa 31:7 — "I will rejoice and be glad in your steadfast love" — trust as the inner condition enabling the rejoicing); seeking God (Psa 105:3 — hearts of those who seek the Lord rejoice; 1Ch 16:10 — hearts rejoice directed at God's holy name); wholehearted devotion (2Ch 15:15 — rejoicing over the oath grounded in wholehearted seeking).

**[B]**
E — Comprehension of God's word enables joy: Neh 8:12 — the people rejoiced because they understood what was declared to them. 2Ch 15:15 — wholehearted seeking as the inner condition for the joy that accompanies finding God. 1Ch 29:9/17 — freewill generosity from inner uprightness as the condition enabling community joy.

**[C]**
E — Belief in Christ without seeing him (1Pe 1:8 — the condition is faith directed toward an unseen Christ, producing inexpressible joy). Abiding in Christ's words (Joh 15:11 — "that my joy may be in you" follows from the vine-and-branches abiding; joy is the fruit of abiding relationship). Walking in truth (3Jo 4 — the greatest joy is hearing children walk in truth). Receiving the Spirit (1Th 1:6; Act 13:52 — filled with joy and Holy Spirit together).

**[G]**
E — Receptivity to God's law and word as valued goods: Psa 119:16 (delighting in statutes paired with not forgetting — memory as an enabling condition for delight). Psa 94:19 (God's consolations delighting the soul amid cares — inner openness to divine comfort as the condition).

---

**T1.7.2** — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?

**[A, B]**
E — Spiritual unfaithfulness blocks reception: Hos 9:1 (Israel told not to rejoice because their festive gladness is corrupted by spiritual prostitution — the spiritual foundation is betrayed). Jer 6:10 (people take no pleasure in God's word, ears uncircumcised and closed — deep inner alienation making reception impossible). Isa 22:13 — reckless defiant festivity in the face of coming judgment, substituting self-indulgent festivity for genuine joy.

**[H]**
E — Misdirected delight blocks genuine reception: Isa 58:2 — the people outwardly delight to seek God but the verse exposes hollow performance lacking the moral integrity genuine delight requires. M04-H VCG-04 — delight in what God does not delight in (Isa 66:3; Jer 6:10) closes the inner person off from receiving genuine joy. 2Th 2:12 — pleasure in unrighteousness as the condition that accompanies being condemned rather than receiving truth.

**T1.7.3** — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?

**[A, B]**
E — The person who encounters the call to joy but cannot receive it is characterised by: grief over the loss of what joy once named (Ezr 3:12 — elders weeping at the new temple's foundation while others shout for joy, the grief and joy indistinguishable in sound — both present, but the grief revealing what the elder's inner state cannot receive as uncomplicated gladness); hardness toward God's word (Jer 6:10 — ears uncircumcised, no pleasure in God's word); and joylessness that signals covenantal rupture (Deu 28:47/48 — the joyless person serving God is the same person whose enemies will rule over them in iron).

**[C]**
E — In M04-C VCG-01, the person who encounters kingdom-joy but does not receive it at depth is characterised by shallow rootlessness: Mat 13:20 (rocky-ground hearer receives the word immediately with joy but has no root — real but superficial); Luk 8:13 (joy without root falls away at testing). The inner state is genuine but insufficiently grounded — not counterfeit but constitutional.

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

**T1.8.1** — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The primary dimension is Affect (Emotional Experience). The cluster's vocabulary, across the dominant sub-groups (M04-A, M04-B, M04-C, M04-E), consistently names felt inner states: gladness, exultation, fullness of joy, inexpressible joy. The characteristic is felt rather than merely assessed or willed. Psa 4:7 (sim.chah in the heart), Psa 16:11 (fullness of joy in God's presence), Psa 86:4 (psalmist asks God to gladden his soul), 1Pe 1:8 (inexpressible and glory-filled joy) — all evidence the affective register as primary.

---

**T1.8.2** — What evidence supports this classification?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The body-impact evidence confirms the affective primary: Pro 17:22 (joyful heart as good medicine — felt gladness producing physiological effect); Psa 51:8 (broken bones asked to rejoice — anguish as felt in the body, joy sought as felt restoration); Luk 1:44 (unborn John leaps in the womb at agalliasis — the most physically immediate joy-response in the corpus). These verses show that the characteristic has a physiological dimension consistent with its being genuinely affective rather than merely cognitive or volitional.

---

**T1.8.3** — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes. Secondary dimensions evidenced: (1) Volition — M04-H (entire sub-group: cha.phets as will-delight) and M04-A Hab 3:18 (resolved to rejoice), M04-C Phil 4:4 (commanded always) show the characteristic operating volitionally. (2) Cognition — M04-I (wonder as a cognitive-perceptive phenomenon requiring divine enablement to see, Psa 119:18), M04-G (delight in law shaped by comprehension, Neh 8:12). (3) Relational Disposition — M04-D (sunchairo as constitutive co-rejoicing), M04-C VCG-03 (joy as the fruit and goal of gospel fellowship). These secondary dimensions do not compete with the affective primary but enrich it: this is not a purely reactive emotion (Affect alone) but an affect that integrates with volition, cognition, and relational capacity.
