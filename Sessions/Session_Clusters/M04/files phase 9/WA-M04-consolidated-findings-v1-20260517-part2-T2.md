## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**T2.1.1** — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?

**[C, E]**
E — Yes, in specific registers. Luk 1:47 (agalliao): Mary's spirit (pneuma) exults in God as Saviour — spirit explicitly named as the seat of this exultation. Luk 10:21 (agalliao): Jesus rejoiced in the Holy Spirit — the exultation located specifically in the Holy Spirit, the most transcendent constitutional level. Act 13:52: disciples filled with joy and Holy Spirit as simultaneous inner states, linking the two without collapsing them. Gal 5:22 (chara): joy as fruit of the Spirit — Spirit-produced rather than self-generated, implying spirit-level origin. 1Th 1:6 (chara): joy given by the Holy Spirit alongside severe affliction — Spirit as the agent and origin of the characteristic.

**[E]**
E — M04-E VCG-02 (agalliao): Luk 1:47 (spirit as seat); Act 2:26 (exultant rejoicing located in the tongue as outward expression of inward gladness seated in the heart, producing bodily hope — a constitutional sequence from spirit-adjacent inward gladness through heart to body). 1Pe 1:8 (chara): joy inexpressible and full of glory — the qualifier "full of glory" (doxēs) suggests the joy participates in the divine sphere, consistent with spirit-level location.

**[A]**
S — M04-A's primary corpus does not explicitly name the spirit as the seat of exultation, though the terms (gil_verb, su.s, re.na.nah) operate at the deepest inner level. The absence of explicit spirit-level naming in M04-A is notable — the sub-group locates exultation in the soul (Psa 35:9; 86:4) and heart (Psa 13:5; 32:11) without naming the spirit.

---

**T2.1.2** — Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?

**[C, E]**
E — For the NT registers, yes. Gal 5:22 (fruit of the Spirit) establishes Spirit-origin as the normative account of joy in the NT. 1Th 1:6 (Holy Spirit given alongside affliction — joy as Spirit-given, not circumstance-generated). Act 13:52 (filled with joy and Holy Spirit simultaneously). The Spirit-origin claim does not deny that joy is felt at the heart and soul level — it names the Spirit as the source, while the heart and soul are the constitutional locations where the Spirit-given joy is received and experienced.

**[A, B]**
S — The OT registers of M04-A and M04-B do not describe joy's origin as spirit-level; they describe it as arising from God's acts (Psa 31:7), from divine rescue (Neh 12:43 — explicitly attributed to God), and from wholehearted seeking (2Ch 15:15). The Spirit is not named as origin in these OT sub-groups.

---

**T2.1.3** — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**[C, E]**
E — Spirit-level location in Luk 1:47 and Luk 10:21 reveals that the highest and deepest form of this characteristic operates at the level of the person's spirit in direct response to the Spirit of God. Mary's spirit's exultation in God as Saviour is not mediated through emotional reaction to circumstances but arises from the spirit's recognition of and response to the divine act. Luk 10:21 (Jesus rejoicing in the Holy Spirit) is the most explicitly Trinitarian statement of joy in the corpus: the Son's spirit-level exultation within the fellowship of the Spirit, directed toward the Father. This locates the characteristic at the very centre of the divine-human personal relationship rather than at the periphery of emotional experience.

---

**T2.1.4** — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.

**[B, D, F, G, H, I, J]**
S — M04-B (communal/festive), M04-D (sunchairo), M04-F (cheerfulness), M04-G (word-delight), M04-H (volitional delight), M04-I (wonder), and M04-J (pleasantness) are all silent on spirit-level location. The spirit is not named as the seat or origin of the characteristic in any of these sub-groups. This is a significant and consistent pattern across the majority of M04: spirit-level location is evidenced only in the NT eschatological and Spirit-gift registers (M04-C VCG-04 and M04-E VCG-02). The broad silence implies that most of the cluster's operation is at the soul-heart level, with spirit-level location marking the highest and most eschatologically proximate form.

### T2.2 — Soul-Level Location

**T2.2.1** — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?

**[A]**
E — Yes, explicitly and repeatedly. Psa 35:9 (gil_verb + su.s — dual terms): "My soul will rejoice in the Lord, exulting in his salvation" — soul (nephesh) named as the subject of both verbs, making this the clearest double attribution of vertical exultation to the soul. Psa 86:4 (sa.mach): "Gladden the soul of your servant, for to you, O Lord, do I lift up my soul" — soul named both as the seat of gladness and as what is lifted toward God in the same verse. Isa 61:10 (gil_verb + su.s): "My soul shall exult in my God" — both terms, soul as explicit subject. Jer 32:41 (su.s): God rejoices over his people "with all his heart and all his soul" — nephesh as seat of divine rejoicing, the most explicit divine-soul-joy statement.

**[B]**
E — M04-B VCG-05: Psa 86:4 (sa.mach) — "gladden the soul of your servant" — soul as the explicit location of gladness sought from God. Deu 28:47: "because you did not serve the Lord your God with joyfulness and gladness of heart" — heart named alongside but distinct from the soul-register evidenced elsewhere. M04-B VCG-04 (Ecclesiastes corpus): Ecc 5:20 — "God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart" — heart named, and M04-B VCG-04 shows gladness as a heart-state consistently.

**[E]**
E — M04-E VCG-02: Luk 1:47 — "my spirit rejoices in God my Saviour" — while naming spirit, the verb agalliao elsewhere in this VCG is applied to the inner person at depth (Act 2:26: inward gladness producing tongue-expression and bodily hope). 1Pe 1:8: joy "inexpressible and full of glory" — the joy described is the inner experience of the whole person including soul.

---

**T2.2.2** — What does soul-level location reveal about the characteristic's place in innermost personal experience?

**[A]**
E — Soul-level location in Psa 35:9 and Isa 61:10 reveals that exultation toward God engages the whole self at the level of personal identity and life-principle. Nephesh (soul) in Hebrew denotes the living self, the breath-animated person — to say the soul exults is to say the person at the most fundamental level of what they are is engaged in this joy. The soul's rejoicing in God is not a peripheral emotional response but the living self oriented toward its source. Jer 32:41 (God's soul rejoicing) establishes that this is analogically shared: God's living self is also engaged in this characteristic.

**[B]**
E — Psa 86:4 ("gladden the soul of your servant") reveals that soul-level gladness is something only God can produce — the psalmist asks God to gladden his soul, not himself. This establishes soul-level joy as a gift that requires divine action to initiate, not a self-generated inner state. The soul is the location of received joy, not the generator of it.

---

**T2.2.3** — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.

**[D, F, I]**
S — M04-D (sunchairo), M04-F (cheerfulness), and M04-I (wonder) do not explicitly name the soul as the seat of the characteristic. M04-I operates at the cognitive-perceptive level (the mind and inner perception) rather than naming the soul. M04-D's constitutive co-rejoicing does not specify constitutional location. M04-F's fragile cheerfulness (Job 9:27; Psa 39:13) approaches soul-level but does not name it explicitly — the terms operate closer to the surface of inner experience.

### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**T2.6.1** — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**[A, B]**
E — Yes. Psa 63:5 (re.na.nah): "my soul is satisfied as with fat and rich food, and my mouth praises you with joyful lips" — the mouth and lips named as the body part through which inner joy expresses. Psa 51:8 (gil_verb): "let the bones that you have broken rejoice" — bones named as the constitutional body-location of felt anguish and sought gladness. Neh 12:43 (sa.mach + sim.chah): "the joy was heard far away" — joy expressing through sound audible at a distance, the whole body's vocal apparatus engaged. 1Ch 15:16 (sim.chah): "sounds of joy" through instruments — hands and instruments as mediators of joy's expression.

**[B VCG-04]**
E — Pro 17:22 (sa.me.ach, anchor): "a joyful heart is good medicine" and "a crushed spirit dries up the bones" — bones explicitly named as the body part affected by the inner state of joy or its absence. This is the cluster's most direct constitutional body-impact statement: inner gladness has a healing effect at the level of bones, while its absence desiccates them.

**[C, E]**
E — Luk 1:44 (agalliasis): "the baby leaped in my womb for joy" — the womb and the unborn body of John as the location of the joy-response, the most physically immediate and constitutionally deep body-location in the corpus. Act 2:26 (agalliao): "my tongue rejoiced; moreover my flesh will dwell in hope" — tongue and flesh both named as body locations for the characteristic's expression and consequence. Pro 16:24 (no.am, M04-J): "gracious words are like honey, sweet to the soul and healing to the bones" — bones again named as the location of speech-pleasantness's healing effect.

---

**T2.6.2** — What is Scripture doing by making the body-link — emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?

**[A, B, I]**
E — The body-links serve multiple functions: (1) Expressive: the most common function — the inner state of joy overflows into the body's vocal and gestural expression (Neh 12:43 heard far away; Psa 63:5 joyful lips; Luk 19:37 — disciples cry out loudly in praise). (2) Indicative: Pro 17:22 and Pro 16:24 indicate that the characteristic is not merely a subjective inner state but produces objectively measurable physical effects — bones are described as healed or dried depending on the inner state. (3) Emphatic: Luk 1:44 uses the unborn baby's leap to emphasise the transcendent quality and power of this joy — that it reaches the level of an unborn child's body communicates that this joy exceeds what rational adult reception alone can account for. (4) Functional: Act 2:26 — the tongue rejoicing functions as the outward declaration of the inner gladness, enabling the characteristic to produce proclamation.

### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**T2.7.1** — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?

**[A, B, C]**
E — The primary direction is inner to outer: the soul's gladness (Psa 35:9; 86:4) overflows into the mouth's praise (Psa 63:5), the body's movement (Luk 19:37 — disciples cry out loudly), the sound heard far away (Neh 12:43). The inner characteristic produces the body's expression. The secondary direction is bidirectional: Pro 17:22 shows the inner state (joyful heart) healing the body (bones), and the body-language of Luk 1:44 (the leap in the womb) runs from inner spiritual encounter through John's body to Mary's awareness — a body mediating an inner-being communication across persons.

**[B VCG-04]**
E — Pro 17:22 and Pro 16:24 show a clearly bidirectional structure: the inner state (joyful heart, gracious words received) produces physical effect (good medicine, healing to the bones). But the causal arrow runs inward-to-outward: it is the inner state that produces the bodily effect, not the bodily state that produces inner joy.

---

**T2.7.2** — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The consistent inner-to-outer directionality reveals that this characteristic is constitutionally originary at the inner level and expressive at the body level. The body does not generate joy; it receives, expresses, and is affected by it. The consequence: joy cannot be manufactured by body-actions alone — the characteristic must be present at the heart, soul, or spirit level before the body's expression is genuine. The false or hollow joy of M04-B VCG-06 (Isa 22:13 — reckless festivity; Jonah 4:6 — self-focused sensory joy) is characterised precisely by body-level expression (feasting, shade-enjoyment) without the proper inner-being ground. Body-expression without inner ground is the diagnostic of corrupt or shallow joy throughout the cluster.

---

**T2.7.3** — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[D, G, H]**
S — M04-D, M04-G, and M04-H do not evidence a body-characteristic directional link. These sub-groups describe the characteristic as operating entirely at the inner-being level without explicit engagement with body-direction.

### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**T2.8.1** — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body?

**[B]**

The sustained presence of a joyful heart is described as a medical benefit to the body; its sustained absence (crushed spirit) desiccates bones. If this language is read constitutionally rather than merely metaphorically, it suggests that the long-term inner state of joy produces a constitutional difference in physical well-being — the bones as the body's structural core are the named location of the sustained effect.

**[J]**
E — Pro 16:24 (no.am): "gracious words are like honey — sweet to the soul and healing to the bones" — bones again named as the location of the healing deposit from pleasantness received through speech. The pattern of bones-as-deposit-location across Pro 17:22 and Pro 16:24 is consistent and deliberate.

---

Act 2:26 (agalliao): "my flesh also will dwell in hope" — the body sharing in the settled expectation produced by the characteristic's sustained operation.

**[CLUSTER]**
S — No verse in the corpus explicitly describes a generational constitutional deposit — the bodily evidence is for the individual person's own frame, not for generational consequence. The gap is noted.

**T2.8.3** — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**T2.9.1** — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[A]**
E — M04-A shows a consistent pattern: the characteristic's origin is God's saving act perceived and received. Psa 31:7 — the psalmist's rejoicing originates in God's steadfast love and his knowing of the psalmist's affliction. Psa 31:7 and 33:21 together: the origin is trust in God's holy name — the characteristic arises in the person when the will is directed toward God in trust. Neh 12:43 (M04-B VCG-02): "God had made them rejoice with great joy" — the origin explicitly attributed to God as agent.

**[C, E]**
E — M04-C VCG-04 establishes Spirit-origin as normative for NT joy: Gal 5:22 (fruit of the Spirit — origin in the Spirit's work); 1Th 1:6 (given by the Holy Spirit — explicit agent); Rom 15:13 (God fills with joy through believing, by the power of the Spirit — God through Spirit). M04-C VCG-02: Joh 15:11 — origin in Christ's own joy transferred to the disciples. M04-E VCG-02: 1Pe 1:8 — origin in believing in the unseen Christ.

**[H]**
E — M04-H VCG-03 shows human volitional delight as internally originating — arising from the person's own directed will and inclination (Gen 34:19 — Shechem's delight in Dinah; Psa 40:8 — "I delight to do your will"; Song 2:7 — love's own readiness as the trigger). But M04-H VCG-02 shows that rightly ordered human delight is enabled by and responsive to divine delight (Psa 37:4 — delight in the Lord aligning the will with his giving; 1Ch 28:9 — willing mind as the inner disposition toward God).

---

**T2.9.2** — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — The origin is multiple and context-dependent: (1) Divine agency — God places joy in the heart (Psa 4:7), God makes the people joyful (Neh 12:43), Spirit produces joy (Gal 5:22); (2) Christ's transferred joy — a distinct NT origin unique to M04-C VCG-02 (Joh 15:11; 17:13); (3) Human volitional origination — what the person chooses and directs their will toward (M04-H VCG-03; Hab 3:18; Phil 4:4); (4) Reception of God's word — comprehension of divine instruction as origin of joy (Neh 8:12; Jer 15:16); (5) Response to God's acts — divine saving deeds as the stimulus (Psa 31:7; Est 9:22; Neh 12:43). These origins are not mutually exclusive: divine agency and human volitional response consistently co-operate (1Th 1:6 — given by the Spirit alongside receiving the word with joy).

---

**T2.9.3** — Does the origin change across different contexts?

**[CLUSTER]**
cluster_synthesis — Yes, meaningfully. In M04-F (cheerfulness under adversity), the origin is the person's own volitional attempt, and the evidence shows it is insufficient without divine support (Job 9:27 — the attempt at cheerfulness fails). In M04-C VCG-05 (suffering-paradox joy), the origin is eschatological grounding — the certainty of a future reward or the identity of Christ's suffering as the ground (Heb 12:2; 1Pe 4:13). In M04-B VCG-06 (inverted joy), the origin is morally corrupt — the will directed toward evil, and the joy arising from that misdirected delight (Eze 25:6 — malice in the soul as origin of Ammon's corrupt rejoicing). The origin determines the character of the joy: divine-originated joy is stable and non-circumstantial; volitionally-originated joy without divine ground is fragile (M04-F); evil-originated joy is condemned (M04-H VCG-04; M04-B VCG-06).

### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**T2.10.1** — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?

**[A, C, E]**
E — Yes.
