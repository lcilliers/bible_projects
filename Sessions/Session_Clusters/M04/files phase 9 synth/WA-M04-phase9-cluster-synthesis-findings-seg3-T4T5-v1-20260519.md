## T4 — Relational Interface

**T4.1.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — God-to-human operation is evidenced for all seven characteristics, but with a clear hierarchy of breadth: (1) **Delight (Char 4)** is the most extensively documented God-to-human characteristic in the cluster — six distinct divine registers (rescue from Delight, relational naming from Delight, parental cherishing as Delight, restoration from Delight, trinitarian Delight, and creative-Delight in Wisdom before creation). No other characteristic in M04 has as many distinct divine-to-human registers; (2) **Joy (Char 2)** is the second most extensively evidenced — God produces Joy in the heart (Psa 4:7), makes them joyful (Ezr 6:22), fills with Joy through the Spirit (Gal 5:22), and sends Christ's own Joy into the disciples (Joh 15:11); (3) **Gladness (Char 3)** operates from God primarily through his character (M04-L-VCG-01 — divine goodness as the archetype of all evaluative gladness) and through his providential acts (good hand, fulfilled promises); (4) **Wonder (Char 6)** — the entire VCG-01 corpus (61 verses) is God performing wonders toward Israel; God also opens the eyes to perceive wonders (Psa 119:18); (5) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)** operates from God primarily through the Holy Spirit (Luk 10:21; 1Th 1:6) — the most constitutionally immediate divine-to-human Suffering-Joy; (6) **Pleasure (Char 5)** and **Exultation (Char 1)** are less God-to-human in primary orientation (they are more human-toward-God), but God's exultation over his people (Zep 3:17; Jer 32:41) and God's pleasant beauty (Psa 27:4's no.am) confirm God-to-human operation in both.

---

**T4.1.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Three bases emerge from the comparative evidence: (1) **Covenantal faithfulness** is the primary stated basis for most characteristics: God's wonder-acts are covenantally grounded (Exo 34:10); God's Delight toward persons is covenantally motivated (Deu 30:9 — "as he took delight in your fathers"; Jer 31:20 — Ephraim as darling child through rebuke); God's Joy over his people is the joy of covenant restoration; (2) **Grace prior to merit** — the most theologically significant basis across Chars 3–4: Deu 9:6 (God's goodness toward Israel is not because of their righteousness); Jer 31:20's sha.a.shu.im persisting through rebuke; Pro 8:30–31's Wisdom-Delight in the children of man is grounded in creation, not covenant performance. God's Delight in the person precedes and produces the conditions for the person's faithfulness (Psa 37:4 — delight in the Lord, and he restructures the desires); (3) **Responsive recognition** — for Joy (Char 2): God's joy over the repentant sinner (Luk 15:7, 10) shows joy responsive to repentance; for Pleasure (Char 5): ni.cho.ach is responsive to correctly-offered worship; for Wonder (Char 6): God's wonders are performed in response to his people's need (rescue, restoration). The cluster reveals that God's extension of the cluster's characteristics is neither purely unconditional nor purely meritocratic but covenantally structured with grace prior to merit.

---

**T4.1.3 — What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative evidence reveals five dimensions of God's disposition: (1) **Tender relational attachment** — Delight (Char 4): Jer 31:20's sha.a.shu.im persisting through rebuke and God's heart yearning for Ephraim is the warmest relational-attachment evidence in the cluster; Exultation (Char 1): Jer 32:41's "with all my heart and all my soul" applied to God's rejoicing in doing good to his people — the fullest constitutional language applied to God's inner disposition; (2) **Pedagogical generosity** — Wonder (Char 6): God performing wonders with the explicit purpose of being witnessed and acknowledged (Exo 34:10 — "all the people among whom you are shall see"); (3) **Life-orientation** — Delight (Char 4): Eze 18:23; 33:11 — God's deepest Delight is toward life and restoration, not judgment; (4) **Lavish supply** — Pleasure (Char 5): Psa 36:8's river of delights; Psa 40:5's "more than can be told"; Psa 16:11's "pleasures forevermore"; (5) **Eschatological commitment** — Joy (Char 2): God's intention is everlasting Joy (Isa 35:10; 51:11); Suffering-Joy (Char 7): God commits to the suffering person through the Spirit's presence and the eschatological reward. The cluster's God-to-human pattern reveals a God whose inner life is characterised by the seven positive characteristics he extends toward the human person — he gives what he himself has.

**T4.2.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven evidence human-to-God operation, but with different relational profiles: (1) **Most dominantly human-to-God**: Exultation (Char 1) — the entire VCG-01 corpus is human exultation directed toward God (Psa 35:9; Isa 61:10; Hab 3:18); (2) **Extensively human-to-God**: Joy (Char 2) — M04-B-VCG-05 (90 verses of God-directed psalmist joy) and M04-C throughout; Wonder (Char 6) — the proclamatory corpus is consistently Godward (Psa 9:1; 1Ch 16:9); (3) **Devotionally human-to-God**: Delight (Char 4) — Psa 37:4 (delight yourself in the Lord), Psa 73:25 (nothing on earth I desire besides you), M04-G throughout; Gladness (Char 3) — ki-tov praise as Godward gladness-declaration; (4) **Worship-mediated human-to-God**: Pleasure (Char 5) — Psa 27:4 (seeking to gaze on God's beauty), ni.cho.ach worship-offering as human-to-God Pleasure-act; (5) **Paradox-grounded human-to-God**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — Luk 10:21 (Jesus' Spirit-rejoicing moving immediately into prayer to the Father); Luk 1:47 (Mary's spirit exulting in God my Savior). The cluster is primarily Godward: the seven characteristics are most fully realised when directed toward God as their object. Psa 43:4's "God my exceeding joy" names the characteristic's ultimate Godward orientation.

---

**T4.2.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The enabling postures across the seven share three common features: (1) **Wholehearted orientation** — required consistently across Exultation (Char 1: uprightness; Psa 32:11), Joy (Char 2: 2Ch 15:15 — "with all their heart and whole desire"), Delight (Char 4: undivided will; Psa 73:25), and Wonder (Char 6: Psa 9:1 — "whole heart"); divided or self-referential orientation blocks all seven; (2) **Trust / faith** — named as enabling for Joy (Psa 33:21), Suffering-Joy (1Pe 1:8), and implied for Exultation (Psa 13:5 — "I have trusted in your steadfast love"); (3) **Covenant faithfulness / obedience** — enabling for Pleasure (ni.cho.ach conditioned on correct worship), Delight (Psa 51:6's inward truthfulness), and Wonder (Jos 3:5's consecration). The most distinctive enabling posture: Wonder (Char 6) requires **attending and considering** (Job 37:14) — deliberate perceptive openness; Gladness (Char 3) requires **humility** (Deu 9:6). The cluster's enabling postures reveal that the human-to-God movement is not passive but requires active inner orientation and moral preparation.

---

**T4.2.3 — What does the human-to-God direction reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The most analytically significant cluster-level finding: the human-to-God direction of each characteristic reveals a different dimension of the covenant relationship: (1) **Exultation (Char 1)**: reveals the relationship as one of mutual relational triumph — the person exults in God; God exults over the person; the exultation is the evidence of the covenant's inner reality; (2) **Joy (Char 2)**: reveals the relationship's diagnostic centre — Deu 28:47's verdict makes joyfulness before God the criterion of genuine covenant faithfulness; (3) **Gladness (Char 3)**: reveals the relationship as grounded in accurate recognition of God's character; (4) **Delight (Char 4)**: reveals the relationship at its deepest — Psa 73:25 ("nothing on earth I desire besides you") is the fullest expression of a relationship in which God has displaced all alternative objects; (5) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: reveals the relationship as beautiful and agreeable — Psa 27:4 (gazing on God's beauty) names the relationship as an encounter with genuine pleasantness; (6) **Wonder (Char 6)**: reveals the relationship as asymmetric and epistemologically humble — the creature rightly overwhelmed before the Creator; (7) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: reveals the relationship as paradox-sustaining through the Spirit's presence — the deepest and most paradox-transcending closeness occurs through suffering. Together the seven reveal that the human-to-God relationship is simultaneously triumphant (Exultation), diagnostic (Joy), cognitively grounded (Gladness), volitionally singular (Delight), aesthetically pleasurable (Pleasure), asymmetrically humble (Wonder), and paradoxically transcendent (Suffering-Joy).

**T4.3.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Human-to-human extension is evidenced for all seven, but with very different distributions: (1) **Most extensively human-to-human**: Joy (Char 2) — the sunchairo corpus (M04-D), Pauline joy-in-communities, parental joy over children, the feast's communal extension; (2) **Most generationally-directed**: Wonder (Char 6) — the Tell-Remember-Meditate tradition as inter-human extension across generations; "we will not hide them from their children" (Psa 78:4) is the most explicit giving-to-another command in the cluster; (3) **Most intimate human-to-human**: Pleasure (Char 5) — M04-J's relational pleasantness, Jonathan-David, the Song of Songs lovers; gracious speech (Pro 16:24) extended toward another as Pleasure-gift; (4) **Most relational-protective**: Delight (Char 4) — 1Sa 19:1 (Jonathan's Delight in David enabling protective action at personal cost); 1Th 2:8 (Paul's Delight producing self-giving); (5) **Most evaluative-commendatory**: Gladness (Char 3) — the extending of good news and apt words (Pro 12:25; 15:23) and accurate relational testimony (Neh 2:18); (6) **Most communal-solidarity**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — Act 16:34's household-encompassing joy; Act 5:41's apostolic community sharing; (7) **Least developed**: Exultation (Char 1) — the human-to-human giving direction is the least documented in the corpus; its primary direction is toward God.

**T4.3.2 — What inner conditions enable genuine extension?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The enabling conditions across the seven converge on two shared requirements: (1) **Prior genuine reception** — the person who extends Joy (Char 2) toward another has received Joy themselves (sunchairo presupposes prior Joy); the person who extends Wonder (Char 6) has genuinely encountered divine wonders; the person who extends Gladness (Char 3) through good news has themselves been truthfully informed; (2) **Love for the other** — Joy (Char 2): 1Cor 13:6 (love's joy is other-directed); Delight (Char 4): 1Sa 19:1 (Jonathan's Delight in David enabling protective sacrifice); Suffering-Joy (Char 7): the jailer's household-inclusive rejoicing (Act 16:34) — the joy encompasses others through relational care. The characteristic most requiring the giver's prior formation: Wonder (Char 6) — the person can only transmit genuine wonder if they have been formed in it from youth (Psa 71:17). The characteristic most capable of immediate extension without prior formation: Joy (Char 2) — news-bearing Joy crosses relational distance even without prior relationship (Act 15:3 — bringing joy to brothers by news of Gentile conversions).

**T4.3.3 — What must the person have received or become before they can genuinely give?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative evidence reveals a consistent principle: **genuine giving requires prior genuine receiving**. Each characteristic's giving direction presupposes something in the giver: (1) **Exultation (Char 1)**: prior encounter with God's reality (Exo 18:9 — Jethro heard, then rejoiced); (2) **Joy (Char 2)**: genuine Joy in their own inner being (the sunchairo requires inner co-joy, not performed solidarity); (3) **Gladness (Char 3)**: prior accurate recognition of what is genuinely good (the apt-word speaker knows what is fitting — Pro 15:23); (4) **Delight (Char 4)**: prior Delight rightly directed (God's sha.a.shu.im in Ephraim persisting through rebuke models the prior-love that enables sacrificial giving); (5) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: inner pleasantness of character (Pro 16:24's gracious words require Pro 15:26's internal purity); (6) **Wonder (Char 6)**: formed personal Wonder from youth (Psa 71:17 — the person who still proclaims wondrous deeds in old age was taught from youth); (7) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: Spirit-enabled Spirit-joy (the jailer's Suffering-Joy encompasses his household because his is genuine, not performed).

**T4.4.1 — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Reception is evidenced for all seven. The most analytically significant comparative finding: the receiving direction reveals different primary mediums for each characteristic: (1) **Joy (Char 2)**: received primarily through news, report, and visible returning persons (Act 12:14; Phili 2:28; Act 15:3); (2) **Gladness (Char 3)**: received primarily through spoken words — the apt word, good news, testimony (Pro 12:25; Neh 2:18); (3) **Delight (Char 4)**: received primarily from divine action — rescue, exaltation, naming (Psa 18:19; Isa 62:4); (4) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: received from persons, their pleasantness, and their speech (2Sa 1:26; Pro 16:24); (5) **Wonder (Char 6)**: received from the community's witness-tradition and from direct encounter with God's wondrous acts (Psa 78:4; Psa 107:24); (6) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: received within community through shared faith-crisis (Act 16:34's household) and Spirit-transmission (Luk 1:44's in-utero response); (7) **Exultation (Char 1)**: received from God's exultation over the person (Zep 3:17) as the primary instance — human-to-human receiving is less documented. The mediums confirm the characteristics' different relational modes: Joy through news; Gladness through speech; Pleasure through persons; Wonder through tradition and encounter; Suffering-Joy through Spirit-in-community.

**T4.4.2 — What inner conditions enable or block reception?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The blocking conditions across the seven reveal a consistent cluster-level pattern: **self-referential closure and hardness block reception across all characteristics**. Specific blocking patterns: (1) **Fear and distorted evaluation** (Gladness Char 3 — Num 14:3); (2) **Hardened ears and uncircumcised ear** (Delight Char 4 — Jer 6:10); (3) **Forgetting** (Wonder Char 6 — Neh 9:17; Psa 78:11); (4) **Folly** (Pleasure Char 5 — Pro 19:10); (5) **Shallow grounding** (Suffering-Joy Char 7 — Joh 5:35); (6) **Broken relationship** (Joy Char 2 — 2Cor 2:3). The enabling condition shared most widely: **openness within covenant faithfulness** — every characteristic's reception requires an inner posture of genuine covenant relationship or moral alignment with the offering party. The most distinctive enabling condition: Wonder (Char 6) requires **deliberate attending and considering** (Job 37:14) — a level of active perceptive openness not explicitly required for the others.

**T4.4.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic from another but does not receive it?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The non-reception states across the seven form a coherent picture of inner-being dysfunction: (1) **Hardened rebellion** (Wonder Char 6 — Neh 9:17 → stiff-necked; Psa 78:32 → unbelief); (2) **Restlessness and self-accumulation** (Pleasure Char 5 — Ecc 2:8's sought Pleasure leaving the Preacher unsatisfied); (3) **Misdirected captivation** (Delight Char 4 — Rev 13:3 — the Delight-faculty attached to the wrong magnificent object); (4) **Grief and desolation** (Exultation Char 1; Joy Char 2 — the inner darkness of joy-absent communities: Isa 24:11); (5) **Distorted evaluation** (Gladness Char 3 — the person who cannot evaluate accurately remains in fear or self-deception); (6) **Unfit and restless** (Pleasure Char 5 — Pro 19:10 — luxury unfit; Job 20:18 — wicked getting no enjoyment); (7) **Overwhelming grief** (Suffering-Joy Char 7 — M04-F's heartsick state: Jer 8:18). The most severe non-reception state in the cluster: Wonder-failure producing rebellion (Neh 9:17) — no other characteristic's non-reception leads as directly to moral and covenantal catastrophe.

**T4.5.1 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing bonds versus across relational distance?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — A consistent cluster-level pattern: **all seven operate most fully within existing relational bonds; most can also cross relational distance but with reduced intensity**. Joy (Char 2) is the most carefully documented in this respect: Joh 16:22 and 2Jo 12 confirm that Joy reaches its completeness only in face-to-face proximity; Gladness (Char 3) explicitly confirms cross-distance operation (Pro 25:25 — "good news from a far country" is like cold water to a thirsty soul); Wonder (Char 6) has the widest cross-distance scope — its missional proclamation (1Ch 16:24; Psa 96:3) is explicitly directed across national and cultural boundaries. The exceptions to bond-preference: Wonder and Joy both operate cross-distance more effectively than Delight (Char 4), which is primarily bond-internal (the diplomatic idiom and relational attachment of M04-H-VCG-02 are both within existing relationships). Suffering-Joy (Char 7) shows no evidence of operating specifically across relational distance — it is consistently community-internal.

**T4.5.2 — Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven cross covenantal boundaries, but with different reach: (1) **Most boundary-crossing**: Wonder (Char 6) — Psa 107:24's non-Israelite seafarers; Exo 34:10's non-Israelite observers; 1Ch 16:24's "all peoples"; Joy (Char 2) — Luk 2:10 ("for all the people"); Psa 67:4 (nations invited to Joy); (2) **Crosses boundaries in their pan-human capacity**: Gladness (Char 3) — the evaluative faculty operates wherever persons make moral assessments (Jethro, Exo 18:17; Achish, 1Sa 29:9; Rut); (3) **Crosses boundaries through new-covenant faith**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — Act 16:34's Roman jailer; (4) **Partially bounded**: Pleasure (Char 5) — the ni.cho.ach worship register is covenant-conditioned; but the relational and wisdom registers (M04-J) are not covenant-restricted; (5) **Bounded in primary register, universal in scope**: Exultation (Char 1) — covenantally primary but the cosmic-rejoicing passages (Psa 96:11; 97:1) have universal scope. The cluster as a whole is covenantally grounded but not covenantally restricted in its ultimate eschatological scope.

**T4.5.3 — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative relational scopes across the seven reveal a spectrum: (1) **Widest scope**: Wonder (Char 6) — community → nations → heavenly assembly → eschatological universality; Joy (Char 2) — individual → household → community → nation → all peoples → all creation → God; (2) **Intimacy-weighted scope**: Pleasure (Char 5) — most intimate bonds (David-Jonathan; Song lovers; brotherly unity) as the primary register; (3) **Hierarchically-structured scope**: Delight (Char 4) — operates through all hierarchical relationships (diplomatic idiom spans kings, servants, commanders, and citizens); (4) **Community-primary**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — primarily faith-community scope; the household as the primary relational unit; (5) **Creation-encompassing**: Exultation (Char 1) — the cosmic-rejoicing passages expand exultation's scope to include the whole created order. The cluster as a whole moves from the most intimate bonds (Pleasure) through community and covenant (Joy, Gladness, Delight) to national witness (Wonder, Exultation) to cosmic celebration (Joy, Exultation) to eschatological consummation (all seven). The concentric-expanding scope is a cluster-wide pattern.

**T4.6.2 — Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity?**

## T5 — Formation and Transformation

**T5.1.1 — Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven produce transformation, but the nature of transformation varies: (1) **Condition-transformation**: Joy (Char 2) — the most extensively documented condition-transformer: mourning → dancing (Psa 30:11); sorrow → Joy (Joh 16:20); anxiety → peace (Phili 4:4–7); poverty → generosity (2Cor 8:2); (2) **Orientation-transformation** (changing relationship to condition without changing condition itself): Exultation (Char 1) — Hab 3:18 (total material loss unchanged; inner orientation transformed); Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — the suffering continues while the inner orientation toward it is transformed; (3) **Identity-transformation**: Delight (Char 4) — Isa 62:4 (Zion renamed "My Delight Is in Her" — her identity transformed by being the object of divine Delight); (4) **Character-transformation (long-arc)**: Delight (Char 4) — most explicitly through Psa 1:2's tree-planted-by-streams portrait; Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — Jas 1:2–4's completeness; (5) **Holistic bodily-soul transformation**: Pleasure (Char 5) — Pro 16:24's gracious words → sweetness to soul + health to body; Gen 8:21's soothing aroma → God's inner resolve. The cluster as a whole produces transformations across all levels: condition, orientation, identity, character, and body. No other cluster in the programme's positive-affect domain is as comprehensively transformative across all levels.

---

**T5.1.2 — Is the transformation reversible or irreversible?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative pattern reveals a consistent distinction across all seven: **present forms of transformation are reversible; eschatological forms are not**. (1) All seven show reversibility in their present-circumstantial forms: Joy can be lost through sin (Psa 51:8, 12); Delight can be withheld through faithlessness (Mal 1:10); Wonder can fail and produce rebellion (Neh 9:17 — reversed by God's mercy in the same verse); Exultation is stripped by judgment (Joe 1:16); (2) All seven show irreversibility in their eschatological forms: Joy — "no one will take your joy from you" (Joh 16:22); "everlasting joy" (Isa 35:10; 51:11); Delight — "My Delight Is in Her" as permanent identity (Isa 62:4); Pleasure — "pleasures forevermore" (Psa 16:11); Wonder — "wonder upon wonder" as the eschatological escalation (Isa 29:14); Suffering-Joy — unqualified glory-joy at the revelation (1Pe 4:13); (3) **Durable formations** — between reversible and permanent: Delight's formation of the "tree planted by streams" (Psa 1:3) and Suffering-Joy's proven character (Jas 1:3–4) are not permanent in the eschatological sense but are substantially more stable than circumstantially-grounded forms. The cluster's consistent present-reversible/eschatological-permanent pattern confirms its eschatological orientation.

---

**T5.1.3 — If the evidence is silent on transformation, note this explicitly.**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Transformation is evidenced for all seven; not silent.

**T5.2.1 — Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven document sequences, and the comparative sequences reveal a consistent cluster-level pattern: **all seven sequences begin with a prior condition of lack, distress, or longing — and end with the characteristic as the inner-being resolution or terminus**. The prior conditions: mourning (Joy, Exultation), anxiety (Gladness), affliction (Delight, Wonder, Suffering-Joy), seeking (Pleasure, Delight). The cluster as a whole does not present these characteristics as starting points but as destinations — what the person arrives at through trust, seeking, covenant faithfulness, or divine action. Suffering-Joy (Char 7) adds a second dimension: the sequence also continues beyond the characteristic — "counting it as joy → steadfastness → completeness" (Jas 1:2–4), making Suffering-Joy unique as both a terminus and a beginning of further formative sequence.

---

**T5.2.2 — What does the sequence reveal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Two cluster-wide findings from the sequence comparison: (1) **The characteristics are arrival-states, not starting-points** — the sequence evidence across all seven confirms that the cluster's characteristics are not commanded as raw acts (merely "be joyful") but as arrivals that require preconditions (trust, seeking, perceptive attending, Spirit-reception, covenant faithfulness). This positions the cluster's formation as trajectory-formation rather than state-imposition; (2) **Wonder (Char 6) is the only characteristic with a clearly documented negative sequence through its absence** — Neh 9:17's wonder-failure → stiffened neck → rebellion is the cluster's most analytically significant negative formation sequence. No other characteristic has as clearly documented a downward arc through its absence. This makes Wonder the cluster's hinge characteristic: when it functions (encounter → proclamation → faithfulness), the upward arc follows; when it fails (encounter → not-attending → forgetting → rebellion), the entire covenantal formation unravels.

**T5.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on sequence, note this explicitly.**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Sequences are evidenced for all seven; not silent.
