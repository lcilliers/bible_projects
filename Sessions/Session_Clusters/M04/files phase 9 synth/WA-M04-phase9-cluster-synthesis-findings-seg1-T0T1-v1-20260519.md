## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across the seven characteristics, two structural categories emerge: (1) **Characteristics attributed to God fully** — Exultation (Char 1), Joy (Char 2), Gladness (Char 3), and Delight (Char 4) are all explicitly attributed to God with the same vocabulary terms used for both divine and human instances. Delight is the most fully attributed of all, spanning five distinct divine registers (sovereign will, relational, moral, trinitarian, and creative). Joy is directly located in God's inner being (Zep 3:17; Joh 15:11). Exultation uses the same su.s/gil terms for both divine exultation (Jer 32:41; Zep 3:17) and human exultation. Gladness establishes the divine evaluative-cognitive act ("God saw that it was good" — Gen 1:4–31) as the archetype of all human evaluative gladness; (2) **Characteristics partially attributed or not attributed** — Pleasure (Char 5) is attributed to God partially (ni.cho.ach divine reception; Psa 27:4's no.am as God's own beauty), but with more restriction than Chars 1–4. Wonder (Char 6) is strikingly the one characteristic Scripture does not attribute to God — Zec 8:6 explicitly distances God from the wonder-response; instead God's character is wonder-inducing rather than wonder-experiencing. Suffering-Joy (Char 7) is attributed to God only in the incarnate Christ (Luk 10:21; Heb 12:2) — it is not a divine-attribute in the same register as Chars 1–4. The cluster therefore reveals a spectrum: from fully-shared divine characteristics (Exultation, Joy, Gladness, Delight) through partially-shared (Pleasure) through creaturely-response-only (Wonder) through incarnation-specific (Suffering-Joy). This spectrum is analytically significant — the more fully a characteristic is shared with God, the more it belongs to the original created design; the less shared, the more it is a response to creaturely finitude or fallen condition.

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The attribution pattern across the seven characteristics reveals a hierarchy of divine-human sharing. (1) **Delight** (Char 4) is the most comprehensively attributed divine characteristic in M04 — more extensively and in more registers than any other. This places Delight at the centre of God's inner life as evidenced in this cluster; (2) **Joy and Exultation** (Chars 2 and 1) share the same vocabulary with the divine instances and are explicitly located in God's heart (Jer 32:41 — "with all my heart and all my soul") — making them genuinely shared inner-being states; (3) **Gladness** (Char 3) establishes the divine evaluative act (Gen 1:4–31) as the archetype from which human evaluative gladness flows; (4) **Pleasure** (Char 5) is attributed more partially — the no.am of God's own beauty and the ni.cho.ach divine inner response to worship are real, but the full human range of pleasure (sensory, luxury, relational) is not mapped onto God; (5) **Wonder** (Char 6): its near-non-attribution is the most analytically significant silence in the cluster. Zec 8:6 is an explicit marker: Wonder is characteristically creaturely; (6) **Suffering-Joy** (Char 7): attributed only in the incarnate Christ — making this the one M04 characteristic whose divine grounding is entirely christological rather than divine-nature grounding. The cumulative pattern reveals the cluster's theological centre of gravity: the inner positive characteristics of the cluster are most fully divine when they involve directed pleasurable orientation toward what is genuinely good (Delight, Joy, Gladness, Exultation), less divine when they are primarily receptive-sensory (Pleasure), and not properly divine at all when they are specifically creaturely-perceptive-cognitive (Wonder) or incarnation-requiring (Suffering-Joy).

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Three categories of silence: (1) **Silence that reveals creaturely limitation** — Wonder (Char 6): the near-silence is the most theologically instructive silence in the cluster. Wonder is specifically the inner response of a finite being encountering the infinite. God does not experience Wonder because he does not encounter anything that exceeds his comprehension; he is the ground of all comprehension. This silence reveals Wonder as uniquely placed among M04 characteristics — it is the characteristic most fully dependent on creaturely finitude and therefore cannot be straightforwardly a "divine image" characteristic in the way that Joy or Delight are; (2) **Silence that is selective rather than total** — Pleasure (Char 5): God does not experience the full range of human sensory and luxury pleasure, but the divine pleasantness of his own character (no.am) and his responsive inner pleasure at worship (ni.cho.ach) are real. The silence on the material-luxury register of Pleasure is analytically significant: the dimension of Pleasure most tied to embodied creaturely existence is the dimension least attributable to God; (3) **Silence broken only by the incarnation** — Suffering-Joy (Char 7): the OT's relative silence on God's experiencing suffering-with-joy is not a silence about the characteristic's value but about its location. Its divine grounding is christological — it requires the incarnation to become fully visible as a divine inner-being characteristic. This silence reveals Suffering-Joy as the characteristic whose full theological meaning depends most directly on the NT's christological disclosure.

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven characteristics together serve a coherent network of created purposes that are functionally complementary: (1) **Alignment with divine reality** — Exultation (Char 1) and Joy (Char 2) orient the whole person toward God's reign and salvation; they equip the person to live from an accurate account of divine reality; (2) **Moral and evaluative calibration** — Gladness (Char 3) equips the person to accurately perceive and assess what is genuinely good; it is the inner faculty of moral and relational discernment. Wonder (Char 6) serves a corresponding humility-function: equipping the person to recognise the limits of their own comprehension before God; (3) **Motivational ground and formative orientation** — Delight (Char 4) is the primary formative characteristic: it aligns the will and structures the desires toward their proper objects, producing sustained meditation and wisdom; (4) **Receptive integration** — Pleasure (Char 5) equips the person to receive genuine agreeableness from creation, persons, and God's own character; it is the receptive-affective faculty that integrates the goodness of the world into the inner being; (5) **Paradoxical endurance** — Suffering-Joy (Char 7) equips the person specifically for the fallen condition: it enables continued inner engagement with God when circumstances are most adverse. Together the seven form a complete positive-inner-being ecosystem: alignment, moral calibration, formative motivation, receptive integration, cosmic humility, and adversarial endurance.

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven characteristics distribute along a spectrum from most-original to most-fallen-condition-specific: (1) **Most strongly original design** — Gladness (Char 3): Gen 1's evaluative "God saw that it was good" is pre-fall; Pleasure (Char 5): the Song of Songs' celebration of bodily pleasantness and Pro 3:17's wisdom-pleasantness are original-design; Delight (Char 4): Pro 8:30–31's Wisdom-delight before creation is explicitly pre-fall; (2) **Original design but fallen-condition deployment** — Exultation (Char 1) and Joy (Char 2) have both registers: cosmic joy at God's reign (original design) and salvation-exultation following rescue (fallen-condition deployment); Wonder (Char 6): the capacity for Wonder is original design, but most VCG-01's wonders arise from God's redemptive-historical acts in the fallen world; (3) **Most strongly fallen-condition response** — Suffering-Joy (Char 7): Suffering-Joy as a phenomenon arises only in the fallen condition; it is by definition the characteristic of a person or community under suffering. The cluster as a whole therefore spans from the pre-fall foundation of creation's goodness (Gladness, Pleasure, Delight) through characteristics that are original in design but formed by the fallen world (Joy, Exultation, Wonder) to the characteristic that exists only because the fallen condition exists (Suffering-Joy). This distribution confirms that M04 is a cluster whose inner-being content is simultaneously created-design positive affect and fallen-world redemptive-positive affect — it cannot be reduced to either.

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics evidence eschatological orientation, but in markedly different degrees and forms: (1) **Most strongly eschatological** — Suffering-Joy (Char 7): the characteristic is constitutively eschatological — its present form is inherently transitional ("that you may also rejoice and be glad when his glory is revealed" — 1Pe 4:13); Joy (Char 2): M04-E's sa.s.von corpus and the NT's "fullness of joy" passages (Joh 15:11; 16:24) make Joy the characteristic with the most extensively documented eschatological fullness; (2) **Clearly eschatological** — Exultation (Char 1): the Isaianic new-creation exultation passages (Isa 65:18; 66:10) are explicitly forward-oriented; Delight (Char 4): Isa 62:4's renamed Zion ("My Delight Is in Her"), Job 22:26's future delight in the Almighty, and Psa 16:11's "pleasures forevermore" all name an eschatological fullness; Wonder (Char 6): Isa 29:14's "wonder upon wonder" and Dan 12:6's end-time wonders point forward; (3) **Moderately eschatological** — Gladness (Char 3): more characteristically present-responsive than forward-oriented, but the makarismos passages (Rom 4:6, 9) and the good-promises corpus (Jos 21:45; Jer 29:10) have a forward trajectory; Pleasure (Char 5): Psa 16:11's "pleasures forevermore" and Eze 20:41's future acceptance are the primary eschatological instances. The consistent eschatological orientation of all seven characteristics confirms that M04 as a cluster names inner-being states that are simultaneously real-and-present and partial-and-anticipatory — the cluster's fullness is eschatological.

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven characteristics express the divine image through three distinct image-bearing patterns: (1) **Shared-faculty pattern** (Exultation, Joy, Gladness, Delight) — the human person possesses and exercises the same inner-being capacity that God exercises; the image-bearing is direct faculty-sharing; the human soul's exultation images the divine exultation; the human evaluative-gladness images the divine evaluative-gladness; (2) **Receptive-response pattern** (Pleasure, Wonder) — the human person expresses the image by responding appropriately to what God has made and who God is; Pleasure expresses the image as the receptive-appreciative capacity that mirrors divine pleasurable reception; Wonder expresses the image as the finite being's capacity to be rightly overwhelmed by the infinite — which is appropriate precisely because the finite person is an image of the infinite (the image-bearer knows they are not the original); (3) **Christological-participation pattern** (Suffering-Joy) — the image is expressed through participation in Christ's own inner-being pattern; Suffering-Joy is image-bearing by participation, not by direct faculty-sharing with God-in-himself. The cluster therefore reveals three distinct modes of image-bearing: sharing the divine faculty directly; responding to the divine appropriately as creatures; and participating in the incarnate Son's inner life through the Spirit. These three modes together constitute a comprehensive account of how the positive inner-being cluster images the divine.

**T0.3.2 — Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The sharing spectrum across the cluster: (1) **Genuinely shared (same vocabulary, same faculty)**: Exultation (Char 1 — su.s/gil for both), Joy (Char 2 — sa.mach/chairo for both; Joh 15:11 makes Christ's joy to dwell in the disciples), Gladness (Char 3 — tov as evaluative standard for both divine and human judgment), Delight (Char 4 — cha.phets/eudokeo for both; Pro 8:30–31's sha.a.shu.im shared between God and Wisdom and humanity); (2) **Partially shared** (same vocabulary, different register): Pleasure (Char 5 — no.am for God's beauty and for human pleasantness; ni.cho.ach for divine receptive pleasure and for human worship-act); (3) **Primarily creaturely**: Wonder (Char 6 — Zec 8:6 explicitly marks the boundary); Suffering-Joy (Char 7 — shared only through the incarnate Christ). The pattern reveals that the cluster's characteristics sit across the full Creator-creature divide, with the most positive-evaluative and volitional characteristics most fully shared and the most cognitive-perceptive (Wonder) and suffering-contextual (Suffering-Joy) least shared.

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Diagnostic power: the presence and direction of each M04 characteristic functions as a diagnostic of the image's condition, but in different registers: (1) **Object-directed diagnosis** — For Exultation (Char 1), Joy (Char 2), Delight (Char 4), and Pleasure (Char 5), the diagnostic is the direction of the characteristic, not its presence alone. Exultation in God reveals the image functioning; exultation in evil reveals the image distorted. The same capacity appearing in corrupted form (M04-P for Delight; inverted joy of Pro 2:14) shows the image's structure intact but its orientation reversed; (2) **Presence-or-absence diagnosis** — For Gladness (Char 3) and Wonder (Char 6), the diagnostic is more about the presence or absence of the right faculty. Wonder-failure (Neh 9:17) correlates directly with covenant rebellion — the failure to wonder at God diagnoses a heart that has made itself the measure. Gladness's evaluative failure (seeing evil as good, idols as genuinely productive) diagnoses moral blindness in the deepest sense; (3) **Constitutional-depth diagnosis** — Suffering-Joy (Char 7) is the most constitutionally-deep diagnostic: the person who genuinely rejoices amid suffering exhibits the Spirit's presence at the spirit level. M04-F's barely-sustained ba.lag shows the image's Suffering-Joy capacity present but not Spirit-empowered. Across all seven, the cluster functions as a comprehensive diagnostic of inner-being health — from the deepest constitutional level (Suffering-Joy at spirit level) through the will (Delight) through the cognitive faculty (Gladness, Wonder) through the affective faculty (Joy, Exultation) through the receptive-embodied faculty (Pleasure).

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics evidence typological use, with three major convergence points: (1) **Christological convergence at Psa 118:23** — "This is the Lord's doing; it is marvellous in our eyes" (quoted in Mat 21:42). The cluster as a whole forms a typological arch from creation (Char 3's Gen 1 goodness) through Exodus (Char 6's wonder-wonders) through Zec 9:9 (Char 1's commanded exultation at the coming king) through Isa 9:6 (Char 6's Wonderful Counselor) through Isa 53:10 (Char 4's atonement-Delight) to Heb 12:2 (Char 7's cross-Joy) to eschatological fullness.

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The dominant typological direction across the cluster is **OT → NT/eschatological fulfilment**, with a consistent escalation pattern: each OT instance is surpassed rather than merely repeated in the NT. The cluster's overall typological movement is from creation's goodness → Exodus wonder-acts → covenant joy → prophetic promises → incarnation → cross → eschatological fullness.

## T1 — Definition and Structural Analysis

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven names form a deliberately differentiated lexical set that maps the cluster's inner-being territory with precision. Three pairs and one unique: (1) **The surge-and-settle pair**: Exultation (Char 1 — active upward surge, triumphal, directed at God's glory) and Gladness (Char 3 — settled warm cognitive brightness, diffuse, evaluative). These are the poles of the cluster's positive-affect range: the upward active and the settled receptive; (2) **The participation-and-receptivity pair**: Joy (Char 2 — active participatory engagement, communal) and Pleasure (Char 5 — receptive agreeableness, ambient). Joy engages; Pleasure receives; (3) **The object-and-cognition pair**: Delight (Char 4 — specific object-focused treasuring, volitional) and Wonder (Char 6 — cognitive encounter with what exceeds comprehension, arrested). Delight settles into the object; Wonder is arrested by what cannot be contained in an object; (4) **The unique**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7 — hyphenated, paradox-naming) is uniquely named as a compound, signalling that it is a characteristic that holds two apparently opposed inner states as one. This naming is itself analytically significant: Suffering-Joy is the only characteristic that requires two nouns to name its essential paradoxical structure. Together the seven names signal the cluster's range: active-directed inner states (Exultation, Joy, Delight), receptive-ambient states (Pleasure, Gladness), cognitive-perceptive states (Gladness, Wonder), and the paradoxical-transcendent state (Suffering-Joy).

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — At the definitional level, the vocabulary across the seven characteristics reveals three structural features of the cluster: (1) **Breadth of the primary vocabulary** — the cluster spans from the single dominant term (Wonder's pa.la/pe.le — 47+ instances of one root) through the dual-characteristic (Joy's sa.mach/sim.chah — 147/89 OT instances) to the five-register vocabulary (Delight's sha.a.shu.im, a.nog, cha.phets, eudokeo, sunēdomai). Delight has the widest vocabulary breadth; Wonder has the most root-concentrated vocabulary; Joy has the largest single-term corpus; (2) **OT-NT continuity patterns** — the terms with the clearest OT-NT continuity are: Joy (sa.mach → chairo, both active rejoicing); Delight (cha.phets → eudokeo, both volitional pleasure); Suffering-Joy (OT ba.lag → NT agalliao — here is also the most significant OT-to-NT escalation: the fragile ba.lag is replaced/surpassed by the more intense agalliao). The terms with least NT development of their OT range: Gladness (tov has no single NT equivalent for its full evaluative range); Pleasure (na.im/no.am have no direct NT equivalents); (3) **Root meanings** — the root etymologies cluster around three movement types: leaping/surging (gil — spinning/leaping; agalliao — intensified leaping), settling/resting (no.am/nuach — pleasantness/rest; sha.a.shu.im — cherishing-resting), and being separated out/arrested (pa.la — set apart from the ordinary; thambeō — seized/arrested). These three movement types confirm the phenomenological differentiation of the cluster: surge, rest, arrest.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The names carry consistent implications across the cluster that differentiate the characteristics: (1) **Directional implications** — Exultation: always toward God or a specific ground (never self-directed in its legitimate form); Delight: always toward a specific object; Wonder: always toward what exceeds categories; Joy: toward God, persons, and what is celebratable; Gladness: toward what is genuinely good (though more diffusely than Delight); Pleasure: receptive from what surrounds the person; Suffering-Joy: toward eschatological and divine reality as a ground for present paradox; (2) **Relational implications** — Delight is the most explicitly relational in its name (it always names a relational direction); Pleasure has a strong relational register in M04-J (the pleasantness of persons); Joy is inherently communal (feast-joy, sunchairo); Suffering-Joy is communally shared (Act 16:34's whole household); Wonder is communally proclaimed (Tell-Remember-Meditate); Exultation moves outward from the inner person to communal expression; Gladness is distributed across relational appraisal; (3) **Constitutional implications** — Suffering-Joy's unique constitutional location (spirit-level, Char 7) is signalled by the paradox in the name; Delight's depth in the "inner being" (Rom 7:22) is suggested by its object-resting quality; Wonder's cognitive-perceptive primary is suggested by its arrested quality; Pleasure's embodied quality is consistent with its receptive-ambient name; Gladness's cognitive quality is consistent with its settled-brightness name.

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven characteristics distribute across four inner-being phenomenon types: (1) **Primarily acts that become dispositions**: Exultation (Char 1) and Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — both are volitional-affective acts that, when habituated, become sustained inner orientations; Exultation begins as a surge and becomes a standing orientation (Psa 89:16 — "all the day"); Suffering-Joy begins as a commanded act (Jas 1:2 — "count it all joy") and, when Spirit-enabled, becomes a sustaining orientation; (2) **Primarily dispositions generating acts**: Joy (Char 2) — the standing inner gladness ("Rejoice always" — Phili 4:4) that is expressed through specific acts of rejoicing; (3) **Primarily quality-conditions**: Gladness (Char 3) and Pleasure (Char 5) — both are conditions the person inhabits rather than acts they perform; the glad person is in a state of recognized-goodness; the person in Pleasure receives what is pleasant; (4) **Spanning multiple types**: Delight (Char 4) is the most complex, operating simultaneously as an act (Gen 34:19's immediate action), a disposition (Psa 1:2's delight-in-law as the blessed man's defining character), a state (a.nog's resting), and a quality (arestos and ni.cho.ach); Wonder (Char 6) is primarily a state (arrested astonishment) that generates acts (proclamation) and becomes a disposition (Psa 71:17's lifelong proclamatory vocation). The cluster therefore does not reduce to a single phenomenological type but is a genuine ensemble of different inner-being phenomena.

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics have constituent elements, but the structural complexity varies significantly: (1) **Most structurally complex**: Delight (Char 4) — five distinct sub-group registers (affective, volitional, obedience-orientation, relational-horizontal, corrupt); Joy (Char 2) — 15 VCGs across four sub-groups; (2) **Two-register structure**: Gladness (Char 3) — evaluative-cognitive face (M04-L) + experiential-circumstantial face (M04-O); Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — Spirit-enabled agalliao form + fragile ba.lag form; Pleasure (Char 5) — relational-personal pleasantness (M04-J) + sensory-material/worship pleasantness (M04-K); (3) **Three-register structure**: Wonder (Char 6) — proclaimed wonder (VCG-01) + incomprehensibility wonder (VCG-02) + dark wonder (VCG-03); (4) **Simplest structure**: Exultation (Char 1) — three VCGs but more unified than the others; the characteristic is structurally cleaner than the rest. The confirmed Observation #4 (M04-L + M04-O integration under Gladness) and the confirmed Observation #3 (M04-G/H/M/N/P integration under Delight) both confirm that multi-register structures within a characteristic do not make it multiple characteristics but show one inner-being reality operating through different faculties.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven working descriptions, when read comparatively, reveal the cluster's inner logic: (1) **Exultation (Char 1)**: the soul's active, surging, triumphant inner act of rising toward God in response to his reality — inherently directional (toward God), inherently relational, inherently expressive; (2) **Joy (Char 2)**: the active inner experience of rejoicing — a volitional-affective inner state that engages the whole person (heart, soul, whole being) in response to what is genuinely good; (3) **Gladness (Char 3)**: the inner state of settled, warm, positive brightness arising from accurate evaluation and recognition of what is genuinely good — the most cognitively-constituted of the seven; (4) **Delight (Char 4)**: the inner state of treasuring and taking pleasure in a specific object — the soul's resting satisfaction in what it loves — simultaneously affective and volitional; (5) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: the inner-being experience of pleasantness — the felt quality of agreeableness received from persons, relationships, creation, and material or sensory experience — the most constitutionally embodied; (6) **Wonder (Char 6)**: the inner state of astonishment and awe before what exceeds ordinary comprehension — the soul arrested and overwhelmed — the most cognitively-perceptive; (7) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: the paradoxical inner capacity to find genuine joy in and through suffering — the most Spirit-dependent, the most eschatologically-constituted. Read together, the descriptions form a complete inner-being positive-affect map: from the most active-surging (Exultation) through the communally-participatory (Joy) through the cognitively-settled (Gladness) through the object-resting (Delight) through the receptive-embodied (Pleasure) through the cognitively-arrested (Wonder) to the Spirit-paradoxical (Suffering-Joy).

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The structural opposites across the seven characteristics form their own thematic cluster: (1) **Mourning/grief** — the structural opposite of Exultation (Char 1), Joy (Char 2), and Suffering-Joy (Char 7); Jer 31:13's "mourning into joy" and Isa 61:3's "oil of gladness instead of mourning" confirm mourning as the cluster's primary opposite; (2) **Not-good/moral deficiency** — the structural opposite of Gladness (Char 3): the "not-good" verdicts (Gen 2:18; Exo 18:17; Neh 5:9; Pro 17:26 etc.) are Gladness's own vocabulary negated; (3) **No-delight / absence of pleasure** — the structural opposite of Delight (Char 4) and Pleasure (Char 5): "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked" (Eze 18:23) and "I will not smell your pleasing aromas" (Lev 26:31) are the structural opposites in the object-oriented characteristics; (4) **Forgetting / not-attending** — the structural opposite of Wonder (Char 6): Neh 9:17 ("not mindful of the wonders") and Psa 78:11 ("forgot his works") — Wonder's opposite is non-registration rather than active denial; (5) **Worldly grieving without transcendence** — the structural opposite of Suffering-Joy (Char 7): M04-F's Jeremiah 8:18 ("my joy is gone; grief is upon me; my heart is sick") shows the complete absence of the characteristic under unrelieved suffering. The cluster's opposites together name what the seven characteristics deliver the person from: mourning, moral blindness, loveless existence, rejection, ordinariness, rebellious forgetfulness, and unredeemed suffering.

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The exclusion and resistance patterns across the seven reveal a common cluster-level discipline: **all seven resist directed inner-state toward wrong objects**. The specific resistances: Exultation excludes exultation at evil (Pro 24:17) and hollow festivity (Hos 9:1); Joy excludes rejoicing at wrongdoing (1Cor 13:6) and joy without root (Mat 13:20); Gladness excludes false evaluation (Num 14:3; Isa 41:7) and premature or self-serving assessment (Isa 39:8); Delight excludes external ritual without inner transformation (Psa 40:6), self-directed pleasure (Isa 58:13), and delight in wrong objects (Psa 5:4); Pleasure excludes covenant-unfaithful worship-pleasure (Lev 26:31), corrupt luxury (Isa 47:1), and deceptive pleasantness (Pro 9:17); Wonder excludes overreach (Psa 131:1), complacency (the proclamatory imperatives resist forgetting), and dark wonder at evil (Rev 17:7); Suffering-Joy excludes performed hollow cheerfulness (M04-F), shallow grounding (Joh 5:35), and denial of suffering (1Pe 1:6). The cluster as a whole thus presents a consistently morally-structured positive-affect: not any positive inner state is good, but positive inner states directed at genuinely good objects in genuinely right ways.

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**
