## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The spirit-level distribution across the seven characteristics is the most analytically significant constitutional pattern in the cluster: only two of the seven are explicitly spirit-level located: (1) **Joy (Char 2)** — in its NT Spirit-mediated registers: Luk 1:47 ("my spirit rejoices in God my Savior"); Luk 10:21 ("he rejoiced in the Holy Spirit"); Act 13:52 ("filled with joy and with the Holy Spirit"); Gal 5:22 (Spirit-fruit); 1Th 1:6 ("joy of the Holy Spirit"); (2) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)** — the most explicitly spirit-level characteristic in M04: Luk 1:47; Luk 10:21; 1Pe 1:8's "inexpressible and filled with glory" presupposes spirit-level operation. The remaining five — Exultation (Char 1), Gladness (Char 3), Delight (Char 4), Pleasure (Char 5), and Wonder (Char 6) — are not explicitly spirit-level located. This distribution is not a deficiency in the five but reveals their constitutional nature: these five are characteristics of the soul and heart in their native operation, not requiring Spirit-impartation to arise. Spirit-level location appears specifically in the characteristics whose most intense and paradox-transcending forms require the Spirit's enabling (Joy in suffering-contexts; Suffering-Joy as such).

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Spirit-level origin is evidenced primarily for Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — confirmed as the most Spirit-constituted characteristic in the cluster. Joy (Char 2) is partially Spirit-originated in its NT intense registers but predominantly heart-originated in its OT communal registers. The remaining five characteristics originate primarily in the soul, heart, and cognitive faculty respectively: Exultation (soul and heart), Gladness (cognitive-evaluative faculty), Delight (soul and inner being), Pleasure (soul and body), Wonder (cognitive-perceptive faculty). The pattern confirms a spectrum: from the deepest Spirit-constitutional level (Suffering-Joy) through the partially Spirit-constituted (Joy in its most intense form) through the soul-heart constituted (Exultation, Delight) through the cognitively-constituted (Gladness, Wonder) to the bodily-constituted (Pleasure — the most constitutionally embodied of the seven).

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Spirit-level location in Joy (Char 2) and Suffering-Joy (Char 7) reveals these two characteristics as the cluster's most transcendence-capable: the Spirit operates below and beyond the affective and cognitive faculties, producing inner states that are not accessible to the same circumstantial distortions as heart-level and mind-level states. This explains why Suffering-Joy can coexist with genuine grief (1Pe 1:6), why Mary's spirit exults in a vulnerable situation (Luk 1:47), and why Jesus rejoices in the Holy Spirit in the context of the disciples' ministry exposure (Luk 10:21). Spirit-level operation is what enables the paradox to be genuine rather than performed: the joy that is "inexpressible and filled with glory" (1Pe 1:8) is not manufactured by the will or produced by the emotions but arises from below both through the Spirit's presence. The absence of spirit-level location in the other five characteristics reveals them as the cluster's constitutionally native positive states — present in the soul-heart-mind from creation rather than requiring special divine enabling.

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Soul-level location is evidenced in six of the seven characteristics: (1) **Exultation (Char 1)**: Psa 35:9 ("my soul will rejoice"), Isa 61:10 ("my soul shall exult"), Psa 63:5 (soul satisfied → joyful lips); (2) **Joy (Char 2)**: Psa 86:4 (soul to be gladdened), Eze 25:6 ("malice within your soul" as corrupt joy's seat); (3) **Gladness (Char 3)**: Lam 3:25 ("the Lord is good to those who wait for him, to the soul who seeks him"), Ecc 6:3 (soul unsatisfied with life's good things); (4) **Delight (Char 4)**: Mat 12:18 ("my soul is well pleased"), Isa 66:3 ("their soul delights in their abominations"), Heb 10:38 ("my soul has no pleasure"); (5) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: Pro 2:10 ("pleasant to your soul"), Pro 16:24 ("sweetness to the soul"); (6) **Wonder (Char 6)**: Psa 119:129 ("my soul keeps them" — in response to wonder), Psa 139:14 ("my soul knows it very well"); (7) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: not directly evidenced at the soul level — the primary constitutional location is spirit-level. This makes Suffering-Joy the one characteristic where the soul-level is not the primary locus.

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in innermost personal experience?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Soul-level location across six of the seven characteristics confirms that the cluster's positive inner-being states are not peripheral emotional events but engagements of the person's core self. The most analytically significant soul-location is Delight (Char 4): Mat 12:18's "my soul is well pleased" locates divine Delight in the soul; Isa 66:3's "their soul delights in their abominations" locates corrupt delight in the soul — confirming that Delight is the characteristic most deeply rooted in the soul's core orientation. The cluster as a whole reveals that the soul's native positive orientation is multi-faceted: it can exult (Char 1), rejoice (Char 2), be glad (Char 3), delight (Char 4), receive pleasantness (Char 5), and register wonder (Char 6) — these are all genuinely distinct soul-level states, not synonyms.

**[CLUSTER]** E — Heart-location is evidenced for all seven characteristics, but with markedly different prominence: (1) **Joy (Char 2)** is the most heart-located characteristic in the entire cluster — the heart is named in 47+ specific instances across the M04-B and M04-C corpora; it is the characteristic's primary constitutional home; (2) **Exultation (Char 1)**: Psa 13:5; 16:9; 32:11; Isa 66:14; Zec 10:7; Jer 32:41 (God's exultation "with all my heart"); (3) **Gladness (Char 3)**: Pro 12:25 (heart weighed down by anxiety, lightened by good word); Jer 15:16 (word as "joy and the delight of my heart"); (4) **Delight (Char 4)**: Psa 40:8 ("your law is within my heart"); Psa 1:2 (delight in law → meditation — heart primary); (5) **Pleasure (Char 5)**: Pro 2:10 (wisdom enters heart → knowledge pleasant to soul); Pro 15:15 ("cheerful of heart has a continual feast"); (6) **Wonder (Char 6)**: Psa 9:1 ("with my whole heart"); Psa 131:1 ("my heart is not lifted up"); (7) **Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: Act 2:26 ("my heart was glad"). The heart's central role across all seven confirms the OT anthropological priority of the heart as the integrating constitutional centre through which the cluster's characteristics pass.

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Heart-location across all seven characteristics confirms the heart's function in M04 as the integrating centre through which positive inner states are constituted and expressed. Three cluster-level findings: (1) **Joy's heart-centrality** (Char 2) confirms Deu 28:47's diagnostic weight — the failure to serve "with joyfulness and gladness of heart" is the root cause of covenant curse; the heart's posture before God is the most fundamental diagnostic of the covenant person's standing; (2) **The heart-gladness/heart-exultation gradient** — Joy (heart primary) and Exultation (heart strongly evidenced but soul equally primary) are both heart-characteristics but at different intensities; the heart's gladness is the steady state; its exultation is the elevated event; (3) **Heart as the gateway** — in Delight (Char 4) and Pleasure (Char 5), the heart is explicitly the gateway: wisdom enters the heart (Pro 2:10) enabling Pleasure in knowledge; the law within the heart (Psa 40:8) produces Delight-in-obedience. The heart here is the receiving-and-constituting organ rather than merely the seat of affect.

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

Delight in God is the most powerful driver of theological and spiritual cognition in the cluster.

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The origins across the seven reveal two primary categories and a distribution: (1) **Divine-production primary**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — the Spirit is the primary originating agent; Joy (Char 2) in its most intense forms — "the Lord had made them joyful" (Ezr 6:22), "God keeps him occupied with joy in his heart" (Ecc 5:20), Spirit-fruit (Gal 5:22); (2) **Multiple-origin**: all other five characteristics originate from multiple sources — from God's acts (response-mode), from within the person (native-capacity mode), from external persons and speech (reception-mode), and from deliberate volitional acts (choice-mode). **T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Yes, consistently across all seven. This contextual origin-shift confirms the characteristics as genuinely responsive inner-being states rather than fixed mechanisms.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Perceptive faculty engagement is evidenced across all seven, but with distinctive profiles: (1) **Most visually-perceptive**: Wonder (Char 6) — "marvellous in our eyes" (Psa 118:23); "open my eyes, that I may behold wondrous things" (Psa 119:18); Pleasure (Char 5) — "gaze upon the beauty of the Lord" (Psa 27:4); (2) **Most hearing-perceptive**: Gladness (Char 3) — "good news refreshes the bones" (Pro 15:30); Pro 12:25 (word making glad); Joy (Char 2) — Neh 8:12 (comprehension producing Joy); (3) **Most olfactory-perceptive**: Pleasure (Char 5) — the entire ni.cho.ach aroma corpus (38 verses); (4) **Spiritual/faith-perceptive**: Suffering-Joy (Char 7) — 1Pe 1:8 ("though you do not now see him, you believe") — perception of what is not visible; (5) **Inner-cognitive perceptive**: Gladness (Char 3) — the evaluative act of perceiving what is good; Wonder (Char 6) — perceiving what exceeds categories. The cluster as a whole is deeply perception-engaged, confirming that M04's positive inner states are not internally-generated emotions but responses to genuinely perceived realities. The cluster's perceptive richness spans all five sensory modes plus the inner perceptive faculties.

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The consistent pattern: rightly-directed characteristics deepen perception; misdirected characteristics distort or bypass it. (1) **Deepens**: Wonder (Char 6) — "open my eyes, that I may behold" — Wonder expands and deepens perceptive capacity; Delight (Char 4) — Psa 111:2 (delight in God's works drives sustained study); Joy (Char 2) — sustained joy at God's acts reinforces attentiveness to divine action; (2) **Distorts/bypasses**: Delight misdirected (Rev 13:3 — captivated perception that cannot see the beast's true nature); Joy without root (Mat 13:20 — initial accurate perception of goodness but too shallow to sustain); Wonder misapplied (Rev 17:6 — marvelling at the harlot); (3) **Unique pattern in Suffering-Joy (Char 7)**: deepens specifically the spiritual-perceptive faculty — enabling perception of what is not visible (1Pe 1:8). This is a form of perception-deepening unique in the cluster: not sharpening sensory perception but opening the inner being to eschatological realities not accessible through sensory channels.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster as a whole is perceptually grounded — all seven characteristics arise from or depend upon genuine perception of what is real. The pattern reveals that the quality of a person's inner perceptive engagement with reality determines the quality of their positive inner-being states. This is the cluster's first-order formative principle: train perception rightly and the positive characteristics follow; distort perception and the characteristics are distorted or misdirected. The Wonder (Char 6) corpus makes this most explicit through its Tell-Remember-Meditate pattern: the community that regularly rehearses God's wondrous acts is training its perceptive capacity to see God's acts as extraordinary. The implication extends to the whole cluster: liturgical and worship practices that repeatedly engage perception with God's acts, character, and promises are the primary formation mechanisms for the cluster's positive inner states.

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

The person and community that remember God's acts rightly inhabit the cluster's positive characteristics; the person and community that forget degrade toward their structural opposites.

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

The cluster presents three distinct affective modes — surge, settle, and receive — mapped to Exultation, Delight, and Pleasure respectively.

These six distinct affective modes together constitute a comprehensive account of the positive inner-being's affective repertoire — from the most active through the settled through the ambient through the cognitively-grounded through the cognitively-arrested through the paradoxically transcendent.

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Conscience engagement is partial and indirect across most of the seven: (1) **Mediated conscience engagement**: Joy (Char 2) — Psa 51:8, 12 shows Joy's absence as a diagnostic of sin; restoration of Joy follows conscience's work (conviction → confession → restoration of joy); (2) **Evaluative conscience**: Gladness (Char 3) — Eze 36:31 (loathing of one's own "not good" deeds) shows the Gladness-evaluation faculty producing conscience-like self-assessment; (3) **Implicit conscience-signal**: Pleasure (Char 5) — the withheld ni.cho.ach (Lev 26:31) is an implicit conscience-signal about covenant failure; Delight (Char 4) — God's "soul has no pleasure in him" (Heb 10:38) functions as a conscience-verdict; (4) **Post-conscience state**: Exultation (Char 1) — exultation is the state that follows conscience's completed work (Psa 51:8's restored rejoicing of broken bones); (5) **Silent**: Wonder (Char 6) and Suffering-Joy (Char 7) do not directly engage the conscience in the corpus.

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The rightly-directed cluster characteristics deepen conscience: the person formed in Gladness's evaluative faculty has a more accurate conscience; the person whose Joy is in the Lord has a conscience aligned with what genuinely rejoices God. Joy's Psa 51:8 sequence (sin → joy lost → conscience works → joy restored) is the paradigmatic example. The cluster does not replace conscience but fulfils what conscience opens up: the person whose conscience has been cleared by God's grace is the person who can receive the cluster's positive characteristics in their genuine form.
