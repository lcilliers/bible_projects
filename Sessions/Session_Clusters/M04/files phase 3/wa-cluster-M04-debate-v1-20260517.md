### G0020 agalliasis — joy (Greek; mti_id=376)

**Verdict:** STAYS  
**Rationale:** Reading the meaning corpus, this term names exultant inner joy consistently across 5 verses. Luk 1:14 frames it as a personal inner possession promised alongside gladness. Luk 1:44 locates it at the deepest bodily-spiritual level — the unborn John leaping in the womb. Act 2:46 places it as an inner disposition of the heart expressing itself communally. Heb 1:9 presents it as a divinely conferred inner exaltation as reward for righteousness. Jude 24 frames it as the exultant state in which God presents the preserved believer — eschatological, God-sourced joy. The corpus is entirely M04-register: inner exultation, personal possession, divinely conferred gladness.

### G0021 agalliao — to rejoice (Greek; mti_id=380)

**Verdict:** STAYS  
**Rationale:** 11 verses across Gospels, Acts, and Epistles, all evidencing exultant inner rejoicing as M04 core vocabulary. Mat 5:12 presents it as willful faith-driven inner triumph. Luk 1:47 locates it in the spirit directed toward God as Savior. Luk 10:21 locates Jesus' rejoicing in the Holy Spirit as grateful praise to the Father. 1Pe 1:8 names it as inexpressible and filled with glory — Spirit-wrought inner exultation. Rev 19:7 frames it as the corporate inner response to the Lamb's marriage. The corpus is uniformly exultation-register, M04 core.

### G0701 arestos — pleasing (Greek; mti_id=500)

**Verdict:** BOUNDARY  
**Rationale:** Reading the meaning corpus, this term names "what is pleasing / fitting / acceptable" across 4 verses. Joh 8:29 frames Jesus' doing what pleases the Father as the inner orientation of his will — a relational-moral disposition. Act 6:2 uses it as a reasoned sense of what is fitting, not inner emotional delight. Act 12:3 records others' approval as a political motivation with minimal inner-being content. 1Jo 3:22 frames pleasing God as the result of obedience — relational moral alignment. The corpus is primarily a "moral-relational approval" register rather than a joy or gladness register. The delight content is present but thin — the term names what pleases rather than the experience of being pleased. The corpus does not clearly fit another cluster either (not straightforwardly M13 Truth or M30 Obedience — it is approval/pleasure-directed). Held in BOUNDARY for researcher disposition at Phase 12.

### G2106 eudokeō — to delight (Greek; mti_id=2098)

**Verdict:** STAYS  
**Rationale:** 21 verses. The dominant register is divine delight/well-pleasure directed toward the Son (Mat 3:17, 12:18, 17:5, Mar 1:11, Luk 3:22, 2Pe 1:17), sovereign divine willing-pleasure (1Cor 1:21, Luk 12:32, Gal 1:16, Col 1:19, Isa 53:10 context), human willingness/delight in generous giving (Rom 15:26–27), strong inner preference (2Cor 5:8), Paul's relational delight/affection for the Thessalonians (1Th 2:8). Where delight is negated (Heb 10:6, 10:8, 10:38; 1Cor 10:5), its absence marks what God's inner approval requires, reinforcing the delight register. The Father's soul being "well pleased" (Mat 12:18, Heb 10:38) explicitly locates delight in the soul. Note: the volitional "willing" sense has tension with a future M29 Desire cluster, but STAYS is appropriate given the delight register is the dominant characteristic. This is core M04 vocabulary for divine and human delight.

### G2169 eucharistia — thankfulness (Greek; mti_id=891)

**Verdict:** BOUNDARY  
**Rationale:** 15 verses. The corpus spans multiple registers. Joy-toned gratitude is present: Phili 4:6 (thankfulness as the inner posture that accompanies prayer and is the antidote to anxiety), 1Th 3:9 (Paul's inner joy for the Thessalonians so full that no thanksgiving seems adequate — explicit joy-thanksgiving link), 2Cor 4:15 (expanding inner orientation of gratitude as grace reaches more people), Col 3:15 (thankfulness commanded as a heart disposition in the peace of Christ). These are genuinely M04-adjacent — grateful joy. However, the corpus also carries a substantial worship-practice and liturgical register: Rev 4:9 (ceaseless heavenly thanksgiving as core of worship), Rev 7:12 (thanksgiving ascribed to God as eternal attribute), 1Ti 2:1 (thanksgiving as prayer practice offered for all people), Col 4:2 (thankfulness accompanying watchful prayer). These are M21 Prayer or M22 Praise territory. The mixed corpus makes BOUNDARY the honest verdict. The joy-gratitude overlap is real and significant, but the thanksgiving-as-worship-practice register cannot be ignored. Researcher to decide at Phase 12 whether to split senses or route to M22.

### G2237 hēdonē — pleasure (Greek; mti_id=3090)

### H2896A tov — pleasant (Hebrew; mti_id=884)

**Verdict:** BOUNDARY  
**Rationale:** 230 verses — M04's largest term corpus. Reading the meaning corpus carefully: the corpus divides into multiple registers, the majority of which are not M04 inner-being joy/delight.

The dominant registers are: (a) Moral-property goodness — God's things are good, things are morally right/good, persons are morally good (the creation goodness declarations Gen 1:4–31; moral-quality assessments throughout Proverbs and Prophets; comparative "good/better" wisdom formulas across Proverbs and Ecclesiastes). (b) Volitional deference formulas — "do what seems good to you," "whatever pleases you," "if it seems good" — these name another person's preference or will, not an inner experience of delight (numerous instances from Genesis through Nehemiah). (c) Practical wellbeing / flourishing — "it will go well with you," "the good land," goodness as covenant benefit. (d) Divine attribute — the Lord is good, steadfast love endures, God's promise is good/reliable.

The minority M04-register instances are: Psa 135:3 (singing praise pleasant), Psa 133:1 (brotherly unity pleasant), Pro 15:15 (cheerful of heart experiences continual feast), Pro 15:58 (heart of man gladdened by wine), Ecc 5:18–19 (finding enjoyment in toil as God's gift), Ecc 2:24 (eating, drinking, finding enjoyment), Psa 16:6 (pleasant places as the psalmist's portion), Zec 8:19 (cheerful feasts), some wellbeing-as-inner-satisfaction passages.

The analytical assessment is clear: the 230-verse corpus is dominated by moral-quality and volitional-preference registers, with M04 inner-being pleasure/delight as a minority sub-register. Applying the guidance in the brief's §3 and §6: BOUNDARY is the appropriate verdict. If tov STAYS, M04 carries a diffuse "pleasantness/goodness" register that dilutes its core joy characteristic. If tov is BOUNDARIED, M04 drops to approximately 958 active verses but becomes more cohesive as a pure joy/delight cluster.
