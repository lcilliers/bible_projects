## M04-A — Exultant Joy in the LORD

**Core description:** This sub-group names the dominant register of M04: inner exultation, gladness, and rejoicing directed toward YHWH (or, in the NT, toward Christ and the Spirit) as its source, agent, and object. The joy here arises because of who God is, what he has done, or what he has promised, and is experienced as a profound inner state located in the heart (Psa 16:9; 33:21), the soul (Psa 35:9; 63:5; 86:4; Isa 61:10), the spirit (Luk 1:47), and the whole being (Psa 16:9; Hab 3:18). It includes gladness produced by God's saving acts (1Sa 2:1; Psa 13:5; Psa 21:1), delight in God's steadfast love (Psa 31:7; 90:14), God's own inner pleasure directed toward his people (Jer 32:41; Zep 3:17; Mat 3:17), joy commanded as a constant disposition of believers (Phil 3:1; 4:4; 1Th 5:16), and cheerfulness grounded in divine assurance even in adversity (Act 27:22; Jam 5:13). The register spans both joyful recognition of God's character and the overflow of that recognition into exultant praise, covering OT vertical rejoicing and NT Spirit-wrought gladness alike.

## M04-B — Communal and Festive Rejoicing

**Core description:** This sub-group names the distinctly corporate dimension of M04: inner gladness expressed and experienced together in worship, feast, celebration, and the rhythms of covenant life. The Deuteronomy feast-commands anchor the OT register — rejoicing is commanded for whole households including servants, Levites, and sojourners at the appointed places (Deu 12:7, 12, 18; 16:11, 14; 26:11; 27:7) — and the social-expansive quality carries through the Chronicler's temple-dedication celebrations (2Ch 30:21–26; Neh 12:43) and the NT's shared joy in community life (Act 2:46; sunchairo passages). The register is marked by the breadth of its participation (all Israel, women and children, foreign residents), the festive or celebratory occasion (temple dedication, Passover, coronation, Pentecost, festival), and the communal expression of gladness through music, sound, and shared presence.

**Terms (primary):**
- G4796 sunchairo (mti_id=382)
- H2305 ched.vah Aramaic (mti_id=2688)

**Terms (secondary):** sa.mach (M04-A primary), sim.chah (M04-A primary), chairo (M04-A primary), chara (M04-A primary), ched.vah-H2304 (M04-A primary), ma.s.vo.s (M04-A primary), gil (M04-A primary).

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- Deu 16:14 — Rejoicing in the feast of Tabernacles is commanded for every household member and marginalized neighbor, showing that Israel's joy is to be shared outwardly and is not confined to the personally fortunate.
- Neh 12:43 — The rejoicing is God-produced and pervasive — it fills the entire community including women and children — and is so intense it carries outward as sound heard far away, showing its deep communal and inner force.
- 1Cor 12:26 — Shared rejoicing is the inward solidarity of the body: when one member is honored, all experience gladness together, showing communal joy as the natural inner expression of belonging to one body.

## M04-C — Delight in God's Word and Law

**Core description:** This sub-group names the distinctive inner register of pleasure taken in God's word, law, testimonies, statutes, and precepts as its specific object. The Psalmist's repeated meditation on the Torah as "my delight" provides the definitional content — sha.a.shu.im across Psalm 119:24, 77, 92, 143, 174; sha.a at 119:16, 47, 70. Delight here is not merely intellectual assent but an affective inner pleasure that nourishes the soul (Psa 119:77 — delight in the law linked to continued life), sustains through affliction (Psa 119:92 — the law as delight is what prevented perishing), and shapes the will toward obedience (Psa 119:24 — testimonies as both delight and counsellors). Psalm 1:2 frames this as the defining inner disposition of the blessed person. The NT's inner-being delight in God's law (Rom 7:22 — "I delight in the law of God in my inner being") confirms the register crosses Testaments. The characteristic is specifically distinguished from obligation or duty by its pleasurable quality — obedience here is desire, not mere compliance.

**Terms (primary):**
- G4913 sunēdomai (mti_id=802)
- H8173B sha.a (mti_id=3844)
- H8191 sha.a.shu.im (mti_id=790)

**Terms (secondary):** cha.phets-2654A (Psa 40:8, 112:1, 119:35 — law-delight), che.phets (Psa 1:2 — law-delight), su.s (Psa 119:14, 162 — exultation in God's testimonies), sa.lad (Job 6:10 — faithfulness to God's word as source of inner exultation).

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- Psa 119:92 — The law as delight is presented as the only thing that kept the psalmist from perishing in affliction; inner pleasure in God's word functioned as a life-preserving resource under extreme suffering.
- Rom 7:22 — Delight is explicitly located in the 'inner being,' showing it as the deepest layer of the self; the object is God's law, creating a split between what the inner person delights in and what the flesh does.
- Psa 1:2 — Delight is the inner orientation of the blessed man toward God's law — not duty but pleasure — expressed in constant meditation day and night; the law becomes the heart's chosen object of joy.

## M04-D — Promised and Eschatological Joy

**Core description:** This sub-group names the forward-oriented, hope-grounded register of M04: joy that is promised as the reversal of present sorrow, the fruit of redemption and restoration, and the defining inner state of God's coming kingdom. The Isaianic vision anchors the OT register: the ransomed return with everlasting joy as a crown replacing sorrow and sighing (Isa 35:10; 51:11); God creates Jerusalem to be a rejoicing (Isa 65:18); mourners receive the oil of gladness (Isa 61:3). Jeremiah promises God will pour rejoicing into his people to replace their mourning (Jer 31:13). In the NT, Christ promises permanent joy no one can take (Joh 16:22), commands counter-intuitive rejoicing in suffering because of future eschatological reward (Mat 5:12; 1Pe 4:13), presents entry into the master's joy as the reward of faithful stewardship (Mat 25:21, 23), and frames the Lamb's marriage as the occasion for corporate exultation (Rev 19:7). The register is distinguished by joy's temporal orientation — it is promised, anticipated, and assured even before it arrives, held alongside present suffering as its certain future reversal.

**Terms (primary):**
- H1525 gi.lah (mti_id=358)

**Terms (secondary):** agalliao (Mat 5:12; 1Pe 1:6; 4:13; Rev 19:7), chairo (suffering-joy and eschatological passages), chara (Mat 25:21, 23; Joh 16:20, 22; Heb 12:2), su.s (Isa 35:1, 65:18-19), sa.s.von (Isa 35:10, 51:3, 51:11; Jer 31:13; Isa 61:3), sim.chah (Isa 35:10, 51:3, 51:11, 61:7; Jer 31:7, 33:11), gil_verb (eschatological passages), a.nog (Isa 66:11).

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- Isa 35:10 — Gladness is something the ransomed obtain — acquired through redemption and return — and it stands as the inner counterpart to sorrow and sighing which are driven away in its coming.
- Joh 16:22 — Joy here is a future inner-being state seated in the heart, promised by Christ's return; it is permanently secure because no one can take it away once given.
- Jer 31:13 — Rejoicing is given by God as a direct replacement for sorrow — it is the inner emotional state he pours into his people as comfort, paired explicitly against mourning and sorrow as its opposite.

## M04-E — Pleasantness and Relational Delight

**Core description:** This sub-group names the experiential register of pleasantness as a quality of what is agreeable, beautiful, and nourishing to the inner person — distinct from the exultation of M04-A in that the characteristic here names the pleasant quality of things, persons, relationships, and experiences rather than directing rejoicing toward God as object. No.am (wisdom's pleasant ways, Pro 3:17; gracious speech like honey, Pro 16:24; the beauty of the Lord's own being, Psa 27:4), na.im (Saul and Jonathan lovely; brotherly unity pleasant, Psa 133:1; praise pleasant, Psa 147:1; the pleasures at God's right hand, Psa 16:11), and na.em (Jonathan's companionship deeply pleasant to David; knowledge pleasant to the soul, Pro 2:10) provide the core content. The register includes aesthetic delight in persons (Song 7:6), the felt agreeableness of communal harmony and wise speech, and the resting inner satisfaction that comes from receiving what is genuinely nourishing (Isa 55:2 context; Pro 16:24). It is characterised by a quieter, more receptive quality than the exultation of M04-A — pleasantness that is noticed, appreciated, and inwardly received.

**Terms (primary):**
- H5273A na.im (mti_id=549)
- H5276 na.em (mti_id=798)
- H5278 no.am (mti_id=1654)
- H5282 na.a.man (mti_id=1656)
- H5730B ed.nah (mti_id=3837)

**Terms (secondary):** sha.a (Isa 11:8, 66:12 — carefree childlike delight, primary M04-C).

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- 2Sa 1:26 — David grieves that Jonathan's companionship was deeply pleasant to him, surpassing even the love of women; the inner relational quality is one of profound emotional warmth and closeness experienced in the soul.
- Pro 16:24 — Gracious words are like honey — pleasantness that enters and nourishes soul and body alike, showing that pleasant speech has a healing, restorative inner impact.
- Psa 27:4 — Gazing on the beauty of the Lord in his temple is the one supreme desire; pleasantness describes the Lord's own being — his beauty is the object of the psalmist's deepest longing and the goal of his inner seeking.

## M04-F — Wonder at God's Marvellous Works

**Core description:** This sub-group names the inner response of awe, marvel, and astonishment directed at God's extraordinary deeds, his creative works, and the incomprehensibility of his ways — the fitting inner disposition when God's acts exceed ordinary expectation and human comprehension. Pa.la names God's acts as marvellous (a quality that evokes inner awe in witnesses) and the inner response of wonder those acts should produce: Psa 9:1 (whole-hearted recounting of wonders), Psa 78:4, 11 (wonder as formative memory passed to generations), Psa 139:14 (the psalmist's praise grounded in being fearfully and wonderfully made), Job 42:3 (Job's humility before incomprehensible divine ways). Pe.le marks God as the one who does wonders surpassing ordinary comprehension: Exo 15:11 (incomparable acts inspiring awe), Isa 9:6 (the child named Wonderful), Isa 25:1 (wondrous fulfilled plans). Miph.la.ah (Job 37:16) names the balancing of clouds as evoking wonder at divine perfection. The inner characteristic here is wonder-as-awe: distinct from gladness (M04-A) and from fear (M01), it is the particular register of inward astonishment before what surpasses human measure, issuing in worshipful recognition.

**Terms (primary):**
- H6381 pa.la (mti_id=1244)
- H6382 pe.le (mti_id=1243)
- H4652 miph.la.ah (mti_id=6701)

**Representative meanings (3 verses):**
- Psa 139:14 — The psalmist's praise is grounded in being fearfully and wonderfully made; wonder here is an attribute of God's creative works that the soul knows intimately and that evokes worshipful awe.
- Exo 15:11 — God is acclaimed as doing wonders — his incomparable acts inspire awe and astonishment among those who witness them, pointing to a divine character that exceeds all human categories.
- Job 42:3 — Job recognizes he spoke of things too wonderful for him — beyond his grasp; wonder here marks the boundary of human understanding, and Job's inner response is humility before the incomprehensible nature of God's ways.
