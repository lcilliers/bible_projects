## T2 — Constitutional Location

**T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — No verse in the M04-G, M04-H, M04-M, M04-N, or M04-P corpus explicitly names the human spirit (ruach/pneuma) as the constitutional seat of Delight. The primary constitutional locations evidenced are the inner being (Rom 7:22), the heart (Psa 40:8; 1:2), the soul (Mat 12:18 — divine), and the inner being as the generative source (Psa 51:6 — "truth in the inward being"). Spirit-level location is not evidenced for the human person's Delight in this corpus.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — Not evidenced. The primary constitutional language for Delight's origin is the heart and the inner being — not the spirit. God's Spirit is not named as the producer of Delight in this corpus (contrast with Characteristic 7's Suffering-Joy where the Spirit is the explicit producing agent). Delight appears as a characteristic of the soul and heart that is constitutionally native rather than Spirit-produced.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does the absence of spirit-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The absence of spirit-level location reveals Delight as characteristically a soul-and-heart-level phenomenon — it is the inner being's own native capacity for pleasurable orientation rather than a Spirit-imparted transcendent state. This distinguishes Delight from Suffering-Joy (which requires Spirit-enabling to transcend circumstances) and from the most intense registers of Joy. Delight arises from within the person's own constitutional inner being as a natural capacity that directs itself toward objects. The question of its disorder is therefore entirely a question of which object the native capacity attaches to — not whether the Spirit is present.

---

**T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** S — The evidence is silent on spirit-level location for human Delight across all five sub-groups. Note: Mat 12:18 names God's soul (psychē) as the seat of divine Delight in the Son — this is divine rather than human, and psychē rather than pneuma.

---

**T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, directly: (1) **Mat 12:18** — "my soul (psychē) is well pleased" — divine Delight located in the soul, establishing soul-level as the constitutional seat; (2) **Isa 66:3** — "their soul (nephesh) delights in their abominations" — soul named as the seat of misdirected corrupt Delight; (3) **Isa 53:10** — "when his soul makes an offering for guilt, he shall see his offspring" — the servant's soul is the constitutional seat of the self-offering that accomplishes God's purpose/delight; (4) **Heb 10:38** — "my soul has no pleasure in him" — soul (psychē) as the seat of divine withheld Delight; (5) **Psa 73:25** — "nothing on earth that I desire besides you" — the soul's exclusive directing of desire; (6) **Psa 94:19** — "your consolations cheer my soul" — the soul receiving delight-as-consolation. Soul-level is the most consistently named constitutional location across both divine and human registers of Delight.

---

**T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Soul-level location reveals Delight as the characteristic most deeply rooted in the person's core self — not a surface emotional response but the orientation of the essential person toward a treasured object. Mat 12:18's "my soul is well pleased" places divine Delight at the deepest level of God's inner being; Isa 66:3's "their soul delights in their abominations" shows the depth of corruption when Delight is misdirected — the soul itself has been formed around the wrong object. Soul-level location explains why Delight is so formatively powerful: what the soul orients itself toward becomes the organizing centre of the person's inner life.

---

**T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Soul-level location is the dominant constitutional evidence; not silent.

---

**T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, explicitly: (1) **Psa 40:8** — "I delight to do your will, O my God; your law is within my heart" — heart is the seat where God's law resides as the object of Delight, and from which the will to do God's will flows; (2) **Psa 1:2** — "his delight is in the law of the Lord, and on his law he meditates day and night" — the heart as the seat of sustained delight-meditation; (3) **Psa 119:24** — "your testimonies are my delight; they are my counselors" — the testimonies as delight are what counsel the heart; (4) **Psa 112:1** — "the man who greatly delights in his commandments" — the heart-orientation of delight toward God's commands; (5) **Psa 16:3** — "in whom is all my delight" — the heart's pleasure directed toward the community of saints; (6) **1Sa 19:1** — "Jonathan delighted much in David" — the heart's relational delight.

---

**T2.3.2 — What does the heart-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Heart-location reveals that Delight is the integrating faculty of the heart — it is how the heart orients itself toward its object. Psa 40:8 is paradigmatic: the law within the heart is what the heart delights in, and that delight drives the will to do God's will. The heart does not first decide and then delight; it first delights (law within) and thereby wills rightly. Delight is therefore the heart's orientation-faculty — it is the mechanism by which the heart chooses its object and aligns the will toward it.

---

**T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Heart-location is extensively evidenced; not silent.

---

**T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Partially, in the volitional-cognitive dimension: (1) **Job 34:4** — "let us know among ourselves what is good" — though this is in M04-L (Gladness), the adjacent wisdom-comparative corpus in M04-H-VCG-03 (Pro 3:15; 8:11 — "nothing you desire can compare with her" — wisdom as the surpassing object of che.phets) engages the mind's evaluative faculty; (2) **Ecc 12:10** — "the Preacher sought to find words of delight" — the mind's aesthetic-intellectual search for truth expressed in pleasurable form; (3) **1Ch 28:9** — "a willing mind" (cha.phets adj) as the cognitive-volitional inner orientation for serving God; (4) **Psa 111:2** — "great are the works of the Lord, studied by all who delight in them" — Delight engaging the mind toward sustained inquiry. Mind-location is present but secondary to soul and heart.

---

**T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Mind involvement in Delight reveals the characteristic as having a wisdom-generating dimension when the object is God's works or truth: Psa 111:2 shows Delight driving study; Ecc 12:10 shows it driving the search for words. Delight in God's law (Psa 1:2 — meditation day and night) engages the mind as the instrument through which the heart's delight sustains itself. Delight does not bypass the mind but channels the mind toward its object — it is the heart-level orientation that directs the mind's engagement.

---

**T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Mind-involvement is evidenced in the wisdom-study dimension; not fully silent, though secondary.

---

**T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: (1) **The "inner being"** — Rom 7:22 ("I delight in the law of God, in my inner being") — the most precise and explicit constitutional location for Delight in the NT; sunēdomai locating Delight at the deepest self-level, in contrast to the flesh which operates at a different level; (2) **The will** — the cha.phets/che.phets volitional faculty is constitutionally located in the person's capacity for directed inner resolution; Isa 53:10 ("the will of the Lord") merges delight and volition; (3) **The "secret heart"** — Psa 51:6 ("you teach me wisdom in the secret heart") — the inward/secret part as where God's truth-delight is taught; this is the deepest inner constitutional layer beyond the surface heart.

---

**T2.5.2 — What does the "inner being" and "secret heart" location reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Rom 7:22's "inner being" (esō anthrōpos) is the most constitutionally significant location for Delight in the entire corpus. It reveals that the deepest layer of the person — below and beneath what the flesh does — genuinely delights in God's law. This creates the Pauline inner conflict: the deepest self (Delight in God's law) and the flesh (doing what the law prohibits) are in opposition. Delight's location at the "inner being" level means it is the constitutional orientation of the regenerate person's deepest self, not merely a surface preference.

---

**T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Inner being and secret heart locations are explicitly evidenced; not silent.

**T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: (1) **The foot** — Deu 28:56 ("the most tender woman would not venture to set the sole of her foot on the ground because she is so delicate/a.nog") — the foot as the somatic indicator of a.nog's physical register; the cursed loss of delight manifests in the foot touching degraded ground; (2) **The mouth** — Isa 55:2 ("listen diligently to me, and eat what is good, and delight yourselves in rich food" — a.nog) — eating/mouth as the somatic metaphor for Delight's nourishing quality; Isa 66:11 ("nurse and drink deeply with delight from her glorious abundance" — a.nog) — mouth and body engaged in the delight of restoration's abundance; (3) **The knee/hip** — Isa 66:12 (sha.a — being bounced on knees) — the body's most intimate parental care as the image of Delight's quality; (4) **The nose/smell** — ni.cho.ach aroma (M04-M-VCG-01; M04-P-VCG-01) — the soothing aroma reaching God (or idols) through the physical offering — the most unique body-link in the corpus, mediated through smell/aroma.

---

**T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — (1) The food/mouth/nursing images (Isa 55:2; 66:11) are **emphatic** — they insist that Delight in God and in his restoration is as physically real and deeply satisfying as nourishing food and the infant's nursing; the body's own experience of satisfaction is the image chosen to convey what soul-level Delight in God is like; (2) The ni.cho.ach aroma link is **mediating** — the physical aroma rises from the altar and reaches God (or in M04-P, the idol), conveying the inner disposition of the worshipper through a physical medium; the external aroma represents the inner orientation of obedient Delight (or its corrupt counterpart); (3) The bounced-on-knees image (Isa 66:12) is **expressive** — it gives somatic form to the quality of carefree, entirely-held, resting Delight in divine peace.

**T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Multiple body-part links are evidenced across the corpus; not silent.

**T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Both directions: (1) **Inner to outer** — the primary direction in the affective-receptive register: the soul's delight in God's restoration expresses through the body (nursing, eating, being bounced — Isa 55:2; 66:11–12); the inner orientation produces outward bodily participation; (2) **Outer to inner** — the ni.cho.ach aroma direction: the physical act of offering the correctly-prescribed sacrifice (Lev 1:17; Num 15:13) is the outer act that expresses and reinforces the inner orientation of obedient Delight toward God; the aroma is the outer form of an inner disposition; (3) **Outer as sign of inner loss** — Deu 28:56 — the delicate woman's foot touching the ground is the outward sign of the inner Delight (a.nog) destroyed by judgment; the body signals what the inner being has lost.

---

**T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The bidirectional link reveals Delight as fully embodied — it does not remain confined to the inner being but seeks expression through the body and can be reinforced through bodily acts. The ni.cho.ach aroma direction is particularly significant: it shows that the outer form of worship (correctly offered sacrifice) is not indifferent to the inner state but serves as the embodied expression and reinforcement of the inner orientation of Delight. This explains why misdirected ni.cho.ach (M04-P-VCG-01 — Eze 20:28) is so severe: the outer act reinforces and expresses the inner misdirection.

**T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence implies soul-level deposit most strongly: (1) **Isa 66:3 — "their soul delights in their abominations"** — the most direct evidence of a constitutional deposit from sustained misdirected Delight: the soul itself has been formed around the wrong object through sustained engagement with it; (2) **Psa 1:2 — "meditates day and night"** — sustained Delight in God's law drives sustained meditation which deposits wisdom and the disposition of the blessed man (Psa 1:1–3 — "like a tree planted by streams of water"); (3) **Psa 40:8 — "your law is within my heart"** — the internalization of the law in the heart is the constitutional deposit of sustained Delight in God's word. The evidence suggests that sustained Delight forms a deposit at the soul and heart levels, orienting these constitutional layers permanently around the object of Delight.

---

**T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Supporting: Isa 66:3's soul-level formation is the clearest evidence; Psa 1's "like a tree planted" is a formation-stability image showing that sustained delight-in-law produces a constitutional stability like a tree's root system. Psa 119's Delight-in-law as the thing that prevents perishing (v.92) — sustained delight has produced a character capable of surviving adversity. No evidence contradicts; the implicit direction is consistently: sustained Delight forms the inner person around its object.

**T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Multiple origins: (1) **From within the person's own inner being** — the native capacity of the soul to orient itself toward objects; Rom 7:22's "inner being" is the place of origin for the person's Delight in God's law — it arises from the deepest constitutive layer of the regenerate person; (2) **From God as object** — when Delight is directed toward God, God himself as the object can be said to originate the Delight (Psa 37:4's "delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart" implies that God as object restructures the origin of desire itself); (3) **From received word** — Jer 31:20 (Ephraim as God's darling child — divine Delight originates in God's relational cherishing); Psa 94:19 (God's consolations originating the soul's cheer/sha.a); (4) **From covenantal relationship** — M04-H-VCG-02's pattern shows divine Delight originating in God's covenantal affection (Deu 30:9; Isa 62:4); (5) **From corrupt formation** — M04-P shows Delight originating in the soul formed around wrong objects through habitual misdirection.

---

**T2.9.2 — Is the origin singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Multiple — more so than any other M04 characteristic, reflecting the characteristic's five-sub-group breadth. The origin varies by mode: inner-being constitution (affective Delight), divine sovereign will (God's cha.phets), covenantal relationship (God's pleasure in persons), received word (sha.a cheered by consolation), corrupt formation (M04-P's habituated misdirection).

---

**T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across different contexts?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: law-delight contexts → origin in the inner being's native affective orientation toward its proper object (Rom 7:22); sovereign-will contexts → origin in the divine will (Psa 115:3); relational-pleasure contexts → origin in the relational bond (Jer 31:20; Psa 18:19); corrupt contexts → origin in the soul's habituated misdirection (Isa 66:3); obedience contexts → origin in the will's alignment with what God approves (Joh 8:29; 1Jo 3:22).

---

**T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in the affective register: inner being/soul → heart (Psa 40:8 — law within the heart as the fruit of soul-level delight orientation); heart → sustained meditation → wisdom (Psa 1:2 — delight in law → meditation day and night → the blessed man's formation); inner state → body (Isa 55:2; 66:11–12 — soul's delight in restoration expressed in bodily images of eating and nursing). In the volitional register: divine cha.phets (inner sovereign pleasure) → immediate action (Psa 115:3 — "he does all that he pleases"; Gen 34:19 — immediate action from delight).

---

**T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The dominant movement pattern: soul-level orientation toward object → heart engagement → sustained meditation/meditation → wisdom formation → outward action/worship. This is a deep-to-surface movement from the inner being outward through successively more external layers. The volitional sovereign mode shows an immediate inner-to-outer translation (Psa 115:3; Gen 34:19) without the intervening formation stages — volitional Delight moves directly to action because the will is the agency-generating faculty.

---

**T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Constitutional movement is evidenced; not silent.

## T3 — Faculty Engagement

**T3.1.1 — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in several registers: (1) **Hearing** — the primary perceptive trigger for law-delight: Psa 119:16 ("I will delight in your statutes") — statutes heard and received; Jer 6:10 ("the word of the Lord is to them an object of scorn; they take no pleasure in it") — the blocked ear blocking Delight; (2) **Taste/eating** — Isa 55:2 ("eat what is good, and delight yourselves in rich food" — a.nog) — the gustatory metaphor for Delight's perceptive dimension; (3) **Smell** — ni.cho.ach aroma — the olfactory sense as the medium through which correct worship is perceived as pleasing; (4) **Sight** — Rev 13:3; 17:8 (the whole earth marveling at the beast's apparent resurrection) — visual perception triggering the captivation of corrupt Delight; (5) **Inner perception** — Psa 51:6 ("you delight in truth in the inward being") — God's perceptive regard penetrating to the inner being's truthfulness.

---

**T3.1.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight both deepens and can distort perception: (1) **Deepens**: Psa 111:2 ("great are the works of the Lord, studied by all who delight in them") — Delight in God's works drives sustained perceptive engagement; the person who delights in God's law reads and re-reads with increasing attention (Psa 1:2); (2) **Distorts**: Rev 13:3 (the marveling at the beast) — corrupt Delight produces a captivated perception that cannot see the beast's true nature; Isa 58:2 (delight to seek God without moral reality) — surface perceptive-religious engagement masking the absence of genuine inner Delight; Jer 6:10 (the closed ear taking no pleasure) — blocked Delight correlates with impaired perception.

---

**T3.1.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight functions as a perceptive lens: it shapes what the person attends to, dwells in, and is drawn toward. Delight in God's word produces sustained perceptive engagement with Scripture; Delight in the wrong object (beast, idols, abominations) produces captivated perceptive fixation on the wrong thing. The pattern reveals that Delight and perception are mutually shaping: Delight directs the perceptive faculty toward its object, and continued perception reinforces and deepens the Delight. This makes the formation of right Delight a perceptive-shaping act.

---

**T3.2.1 — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in the study-and-meditation register: (1) **Psa 1:2** — delight in the law → meditation day and night — Delight producing sustained cognitive engagement; (2) **Psa 111:2** — delight in God's works driving their study; (3) **Psa 119:24** — "your testimonies are my delight; they are my counselors" — Delight in testimonies producing the cognitive guidance that the testimonies provide; (4) **Ecc 12:10** — the Preacher seeking words of delight — cognitive-aesthetic search; (5) **1Ch 28:9** — "a willing mind" (cha.phets adj) — cognitive-volitional orientation; (6) **Pro 8:11** — nothing desired (che.phets) can compare with wisdom — the cognitive act of comparing wisdom against all other desired objects.

---

**T3.2.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight enables and deepens cognition: Psa 1:2 (sustained meditation driven by Delight); Psa 111:2 (study driven by Delight); Psa 119:24 (testimonies as counselors — Delight in them producing cognitive guidance). In its distorted form Delight can bypass cognition: Pro 18:2 (the fool takes pleasure only in expressing his opinion — self-directed Delight short-circuiting the cognitive engagement with understanding); Rev 13:3 (marveling at the beast — perceptive captivation bypassing cognitive assessment of the beast's true nature).

---

**T3.2.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is uniquely positioned as a cognitive-driver: unlike the other M04 characteristics, Delight consistently produces sustained cognitive engagement with its object (study, meditation, inquiry). The pattern reveals that the person's deepest Delight determines what they most consistently think about and explore. Properly directed Delight in God and his word is therefore the most powerful driver of theological and spiritual cognition — it produces a mind that returns repeatedly to its object.

---

**T3.3.1 — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: (1) **The Psa 119 sha.a pairings** — Psa 119:16 ("I will delight in your statutes; I will not forget your word") — Delight and memory are explicitly paired; the opposite of Delight in God's word is forgetting it; (2) **Jer 31:20** ("as often as I speak against him, I do remember him still") — God's sha.a.shu.im in Ephraim as a darling child sustains memory even through rebuke; divine Delight functions as a memory-anchor for the beloved; (3) **Hos 2:7** (adjacent — the people remembering that "it was better for me then than now" — the memory of former delight driving return); (4) **Psa 37:4** — "he will give you the desires of your heart" — the heart's desires shaped by Delight in God require memory of what God has given as a sustaining reference.

---

**T3.3.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight deepens memory in its healthy form: Psa 119:16's explicit pairing of delight and not-forgetting shows the two as mutually reinforcing — delight sustains memory, and memory returns the person to delight. God's sha.a.shu.im in Ephraim sustains God's own memory of the relationship through rebuke (Jer 31:20). Misdirected Delight can impair memory of genuine goodness: the people who find no pleasure in God's word (Jer 6:10) have effectively suppressed the memory of its truth.

---

**T3.3.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Psa 119:16's "delight/not forget" pairing is the most analytically significant: it reveals Delight as the memory-anchor for what the person treasures. The soul forgets what it does not delight in; the soul returns to what it does delight in. This makes Delight formation critical for memory formation: training the person's delight toward God's word simultaneously trains their memory to hold what matters most.

---

**T3.4.1 — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes — Delight's M04-G register (sha.a.shu.im, sha.a, a.nog) is primarily affective: (1) **sha.a.shu.im** (Psa 119 series) — the multiple-intensity noun for pleasurable cherishing; (2) **sha.a** (Psa 94:19 — "your consolations cheer my soul"; Isa 66:12 — bounced on knees) — affective carefree-pleasure quality; (3) **a.nog** (Psa 37:4 — "delight yourself in the Lord"; Isa 58:14 — "take delight in the Lord"; Isa 66:11 — drink deeply with delight) — luxuriating, resting, pleasurable affect; (4) **sunēdomai** (Rom 7:22 — "I delight in the law of God") — synergistic joy in, the most explicitly affective NT term for Delight in the inner being; (5) **che.med** (Eze 23:12 — lustful desirability) — affective captivation in the corrupt register.

---

**T3.4.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight deepens affect by giving it a specific worthy object: Psa 37:4 (resting in the Lord produces a quality of affective satisfaction that restructures the heart's desires); Psa 119:92 (Delight in God's law produces affective resilience sustaining the inner person through affliction). Misdirected Delight distorts affect: Eze 23:12 (che.med as lustful captivation — the affective faculty seized by the wrong object); Rev 13:3 (collective marvel as mass affective captivation). Delight shapes the affective faculty's object and therefore the quality and direction of the affective life.

---

**T3.4.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the most affectively-constituted characteristic in M04 alongside Joy — but Delight's distinctive affective quality is resting satisfaction (a.nog) and treasuring-warmth (sha.a.shu.im) rather than Joy's active exultation. The pattern reveals Delight as the characteristic that settles the affective faculty into a specific treasured object, producing stability rather than surge.

---

**T3.5.1 — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: (1) **Pro 8:30–31** — Wisdom as God's daily delight, "rejoicing before him always, rejoicing in his inhabited world and delighting in the children of man" — Wisdom's creative delight in the world and in humanity; this is the most creatively-charged delight in the corpus; the creation itself is the object of Wisdom's sha.a.shu.im; (2) **Ecc 12:10** — "the Preacher sought to find words of delight" — creative-literary work driven by the desire to find language that carries genuine pleasure; (3) **Psa 119's Delight in God's law as poetic corpus** — the entire Psa 119 sha.a.shu.im series is itself a creative work generated from Delight in the law; Delight in God's word produces the creativity of the psalmist's literary meditation.

---

**T3.5.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight enables creativity specifically by directing it toward its proper object: Wisdom's sha.a.shu.im in creation (Pro 8:30–31) produces the creative work of the inhabited world as an object of Delight; the Preacher's search for words of delight (Ecc 12:10) produces the literary creativity of Ecclesiastes. Delight in God's word produces the psalm's creative-poetic meditation. Misdirected Delight (M04-P) does not produce creative works evidenced in the corpus — it produces destructive captivation instead.

---

**T3.5.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is a creative-generative characteristic: Pro 8:30–31's Wisdom delighting in the children of man frames Delight as constitutive of the creative act — God and Wisdom delight in creating and in what they have created. Human creativity that arises from Delight in God's word and wisdom participates in this creative-delight pattern. The pattern reveals Delight as the inner source from which authentic creativity flows.

**T3.6.1 — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes — this is the dominant mode of M04-H's 184-verse corpus. The cha.phets/che.phets volitional register spans: (1) **Divine sovereign will** — Psa 115:3; 135:6; Isa 44:28; 46:10; 53:10; 55:11 — God's cha.phets as the operative principle of all divine action; (2) **Human directed desire and choice** — Gen 34:19 (Shechem's delight → immediate action); Psa 40:8 ("I delight to do your will") — the will oriented toward God's will by Delight; (3) **The diplomatic-preference idiom** (M04-H-VCG-03's 105 verses) — "as it seems good to you" throughout the historical books — volitional deference framed through the Delight vocabulary; (4) **Love's own timing** — Song 2:7; 3:5; 8:4 ("not stir up or awaken love until it pleases") — Delight attributed to Love itself as its inner volitional readiness.

---

**T3.6.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the primary shaper of volition in M04: (1) **Enables** — Psa 40:8 (Delight to do God's will enables willing obedience); Gen 34:19 (Delight driving immediate action — will energised by desire); (2) **Deepens** — God's sovereign cha.phets cannot be diverted or overridden (Psa 115:3; Isa 46:10) — perfect Delight produces perfect volitional consistency; the will shaped by Delight in God's law becomes a will that naturally chooses what it loves (Psa 1:2; 40:8); (3) **Distorts** — misdirected Delight drives the will toward wrong objects (Isa 65:12; 2Th 2:12 — the will shaped by pleasure in unrighteousness); desire without knowledge (Pro 19:2) shows Delight as a volitional driver that can produce disaster when unguided by wisdom.

**T3.6.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the most volitionally-constituted characteristic in M04, distinctly more so than Gladness (primarily cognitive), Suffering-Joy (primarily Spirit-produced), or Joy (primarily affective). The cha.phets/che.phets vocabulary's pervasive use for both divine sovereign will and human desire confirms that Delight and volition are structurally intertwined: Delight is what the will is when fully engaged with its proper or improper object. The pattern reveals that the deepest volitional structure of a person is their Delight — what they are directed toward with their deepest pleasure.

**T3.7.1 — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, extensively: (1) **Sovereign divine agency** — M04-H-VCG-01: God's cha.phets/eudokeo produces immediate, unrestricted action (Psa 115:3; 135:6; Isa 55:11; 44:28 — Cyrus fulfilling God's purpose); divine Delight is the most agentively-direct characteristic in the entire programme — God's pleasure immediately produces action; (2) **Human urgent action** — Gen 34:19 (Shechem's delight → "did not delay to do the thing") — the most dramatic human agency-from-Delight example; (3) **Diplomatic agency** — the M04-H-VCG-03 "as it seems good to you" formula throughout the historical books frames Delight as the standard by which agency is authorised and directed; (4) **Obedience-agency** — Joh 8:29 (Jesus always doing what pleases the Father — continuous agentive faithfulness produced by Delight-in-the-Father); 1Jo 3:22 (doing what pleases God → receiving prayer answers — agency motivated by and productive of Delight).

**T3.7.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the primary agentive driver in M04: it enables and energises agency directly (Gen 34:19; Joh 8:29), and in its sovereign divine form it is the sole operative principle of all action (Psa 115:3). Misdirected Delight impairs agency by directing it toward destructive ends: Jer 42:22 (desire for Egypt leading to disaster in Egypt — the will energised by Delight drives the person toward their destruction); Isa 65:12 (choosing what God did not delight in → sword — agency in destructive direction).

**T3.7.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the most agentively-powerful characteristic in M04: it directly energises the will and produces action without requiring intermediate steps. This is why the misdirection of Delight is so dangerous (M04-P, M04-H-VCG-04) — the same agency-generating power that in the righteous person drives obedience and mission drives the unrighteous person toward destruction with equal energy. Delight does not produce tentative or half-hearted action; Gen 34:19's "did not delay" and God's "he does all that he pleases" are at the same ends of the spectrum — both show Delight as immediately and fully agentive.

**T3.8.1 — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in M04-G and M04-M: (1) **M04-G's law-delight** — Psa 119's Delight in God's statutes, testimonies, commandments is simultaneously moral engagement: the psalmist who delights in the law is engaging the moral evaluation faculty through pleasurable orientation; Psa 40:8 ("I delight to do your will, O my God") — delight as moral alignment; (2) **M04-M-VCG-02** — the series of "doing what is good and right in God's sight" texts (Deu 6:18; 12:28; 1Sa 15:22; Joh 8:29; 1Jo 3:22) — all engage moral evaluation as the faculty that identifies what is pleasing to God; (3) **M04-H-VCG-02** — God's moral preferences revealed: what God delights in (steadfast love, justice, righteousness — Jer 9:24; Mic 7:18; Hos 6:6) is a moral declaration; (4) **M04-H-VCG-04** — the corrupt-Delight instances (Isa 65:12; 66:3; 2Th 2:12) engage moral evaluation by negation — pleasure in what God condemns reveals the moral inversion.

**T3.8.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight deepens moral evaluation when rightly directed: the person who delights in God's law meditates on its moral content continuously (Psa 1:2); the person who delights in doing God's will has an inward moral compass (Psa 40:8; Joh 8:29). Misdirected Delight impairs and bypasses moral evaluation: Isa 66:3 (soul delighting in abominations — the moral evaluation faculty overridden by the force of misdirected pleasure); 2Th 2:12 (pleasure in unrighteousness — those condemned had their moral evaluation replaced by an anti-moral Delight); Pro 18:2 (fool taking pleasure only in his own opinion — moral evaluation bypassed by self-referential delight).

**T3.8.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The relationship between Delight and moral evaluation is the most diagnostically significant in M04: the direction of a person's Delight reveals the state of their moral evaluation faculty. What a person delights in is what they judge to be good; if the Delight is misdirected, the moral evaluation faculty has been captured by the wrong object. This is Jer 9:24's theological logic reversed: the person who does not delight in what God delights in (steadfast love, justice, righteousness) has substituted their own standard for God's.

**T3.9.1 — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Partially: (1) **M04-H-VCG-04's negated Delight** as divine condemnation — Heb 10:38 ("my soul has no pleasure in him") is the divine-conscience-equivalent: God's withholding of delight functions as his verdict on the person who shrinks back; (2) **1Cor 10:5** ("with most of them God was not pleased, for they were overthrown in the wilderness") — God's displeasure as the conscience-verdict of history; (3) **Job 6:10** (sa.lad in M04-G-VCG-01 — "I would even exult in pain unsparing, for I have not denied the words of the Holy One") — Job's clear conscience expressed as a form of inner exultation even in pain; fidelity to God's word sustains the inner person against conscience-guilt; (4) **Psa 51:6** ("you delight in truth in the inward being") — God's delight in inner truth is the positive conscience-standard; where it is met, Delight follows; where it is absent, God's delight is withheld.

---

**T3.9.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight in God's word deepens conscience: the person whose Delight is in God's testimonies has an active, engaged moral sense aligned with divine standards. Delight in abominations (Isa 66:3) bypasses and suppresses conscience: the soul that delights in what God condemns has effectively silenced the conscience through the force of misdirected Delight. The most serious form of conscience-impairment in the corpus is 2Th 2:12's "pleasure in unrighteousness" — Delight so deeply misdirected that the conscience's witness is drowned.

---

**T3.9.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Conscience and Delight are mutually determinative: a healthy conscience enables authentic Delight in what is genuinely good; Delight in what is genuinely good reinforces and deepens conscience. Misdirected Delight is the mechanism by which conscience is suppressed — the pleasure taken in the wrong object generates its own justification, progressively silencing the inner witness. This is the pathway of Isa 66:3 and 2Th 2:12: sustained wrong Delight forms a soul in which conscience has been replaced by anti-conscience.

**T3.10.1 — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, primarily through M04-M and M04-H-VCG-02: (1) **Joh 8:29** ("I always do the things that are pleasing to him") — the most perfect integration of Delight and conscientiousness in the corpus: Jesus' continuous Delight-in-the-Father's-pleasure produces continuous conscientious action; the inner orientation (Delight) and the outward consistency of action (conscientiousness) are indistinguishable; (2) **1Jo 3:22** ("do what pleases him") — obedient Delight producing conscientious commandment-keeping; (3) **Psa 40:8** ("I delight to do your will") — Delight making conscientiousness natural and willing rather than reluctant; (4) **1Sa 15:22** ("to obey is better than sacrifice") — the conscientious inner orientation toward obedience as the form God's Delight takes.

---

**T3.10.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight enables and deepens conscientiousness: Joh 8:29 shows Delight producing unbroken conscientiousness — always doing what pleases the Father; 1Jo 3:22's structure (obedience → pleasing God → prayer answered) shows conscientiousness reinforced by Delight's relational framing (pleasing someone I delight in is the highest motivation for consistent action). In its distorted form, misdirected Delight bypasses conscientiousness: Isa 58:13 (seeking one's own pleasure on the Sabbath — self-Delight displacing the conscientious Sabbath-orientation).

**T3.10.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Joh 8:29 is the most analytically significant data point: it names the perfected integration of Delight and conscientiousness. "Always doing what pleases the Father" — the always is the conscientiousness; "what pleases the Father" is the Delight object. The pattern reveals that where Delight is perfectly directed (toward God's pleasure), conscientiousness is perfectly consistent. This is the inner logic of M04-M as a register of Delight: Delight-as-obedience-orientation is exactly the integration of Delight and conscientiousness that Jesus perfectly embodied and that 1Jo 3:22 prescribes for believers.

**T3.11.1 — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, pervasively: (1) **God's relational Delight in persons** (M04-H-VCG-02) — Psa 18:19 (delighted in → rescued); Num 14:8 (delights in us → brings us to the land); 1Ki 10:9 (delighted in Solomon → set on the throne); Isa 62:4 (delights in Zion → renames her); Jer 31:20 (Ephraim as darling child — sha.a.shu.im persisting through rebuke); Eze 18:23; 33:11 (no pleasure in wicked person's death — relational orientation toward life); Mic 7:18 (delights in steadfast love — relational Delight as the deepest expression of God's character); (2) **Human relational Delight** — 1Sa 19:1 (Jonathan delighting much in David — deep personal affection overriding familial loyalty); Psa 16:3 (all my delight in the saints); 1Th 2:8 (Paul's affectionate desire for the Thessalonians producing self-giving); (3) **Parental Delight** (M04-N) — Mic 1:16 (children of your delight — ta.a.nug as parental tender attachment); (4) **Corrupt relational Delight** (M04-N-VCG-02, M04-P-VCG-02) — Rev 13:3; 17:8 (marveling at the beast) and Eze 23:12 (lustful captivation) — the relational capacity corrupted.

**T3.11.2 — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight deepens genuine relational capacity: 1Sa 19:1 (Jonathan's Delight in David enabling him to protect David against his father's command); Jer 31:20 (God's Delight in Ephraim sustaining the relationship through rebuke — Delight as the relational bond that persists through discipline); 1Th 2:8 (Paul's affectionate desire for the Thessalonians producing self-sharing — Delight as the relational motor for self-giving). Corrupt Delight (M04-P; M04-N-VCG-02) distorts relational capacity into captivation, lust, or predation (Eze 23:12; Rev 13:3) — the relational faculty seized by the wrong object.

---

**T3.11.3 — What does the pattern reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the relational characteristic most closely tied to the motivational structure of relationships: what a person delights in determines who they protect (Jonathan and David), who they cherish (God and Ephraim), who they give themselves to (Paul and the Thessalonians), and who they become dangerously attached to (beast-captivation). The pattern reveals Delight as the relational-motivational engine — it is the inner disposition that drives relational action, making it the most powerful and most dangerous relational capacity in M04.
