## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence reveals Delight as a profoundly divine characteristic across three registers: (1) **Volitional-sovereign delight** — M04-H-VCG-01 establishes that God's delight is his sovereign will operating without external constraint: "Our God is in the heavens; he does all that he pleases" (Psa 115:3); "whatever the Lord pleases, he does, in heaven and on earth" (Psa 135:6); Isa 46:10 — "My counsel shall stand, and I will accomplish all my purpose" (che.phets); Isa 55:11 — God's word accomplishes what he purposes. Divine delight and divine sovereignty are identical: God's inner pleasure is the operative principle of all his acts; (2) **Relational delight in persons** — M04-H-VCG-02 shows God's delight directed personally and redemptively: "he rescued me, because he delighted in me" (Psa 18:19; 2Sa 22:20); God delighted in Solomon and set him on the throne (1Ki 10:9; 2Ch 9:8); Isa 62:4 — God's delight in Zion becomes her new name; "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked, but that the wicked turn and live" (Eze 18:23; 33:11); God delights in steadfast love, justice, and righteousness (Jer 9:24); "he delights in steadfast love" (Mic 7:18); (3) **Affective delight in God's own word and wisdom** — Pro 8:30–31 — Wisdom was daily God's delight before creation; Jer 31:20 — Ephraim is God's "darling child," sha.a.shu.im, his cherished treasured one; Mat 3:17; 12:18; 17:5; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22; 2Pe 1:17 — "this is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased" (eudokeo at baptism and transfiguration); Mat 12:18 — "my soul is well pleased" — delight located in God's own soul directed toward the Son.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, more extensively and programmatically than any other M04 characteristic. The attributions span five different registers: (1) God's sovereign volitional delight (Psa 115:3; 135:6; Isa 44:28; 46:10; 53:10; 55:11; Ecc 8:3; Jon 1:14) — his pleasure is his will; (2) God's relational delight in persons (Psa 18:19; 22:8; 37:23; 41:11; Num 14:8; 1Ki 10:9; Isa 62:4; Deu 30:9) — his pleasure motivates rescue, exaltation, and restoration; (3) God's moral delight — what he takes pleasure in and what he does not: "you delight in truth in the inward being" (Psa 51:6); "God does not delight in wickedness" (Psa 5:4); he takes no pleasure in sacrifice without inner transformation (Psa 40:6; 51:16; Isa 1:11; Heb 10:6, 8); he delights in steadfast love rather than sacrifice (Hos 6:6; Jer 9:24; Mic 7:18); (4) God's trinitarian delight — the Father's soul well pleased with the Son (Mat 12:18; eudokeo — the most explicit soul-location of divine Delight in Scripture); (5) God's creative delight — Wisdom as God's daily delight before creation (Pro 8:30); Jer 31:20's cherishing of Ephraim as a darling child. The breadth of attribution reveals: Delight is not incidental to God's character but is the integrating inner disposition that drives his will, his relational choices, his moral preferences, and his creative pleasure. Human Delight is a participation in the most comprehensive and fundamental feature of the divine inner life evidenced in M04.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-4]** S — Scripture is not silent; divine Delight is the most extensively attributed characteristic in this cluster. The analytically significant observation is not silence but selectivity: God explicitly withholds delight in specific contexts (ritual sacrifice without inner transformation, wickedness, faithlessness — Psa 5:4; 40:6; Isa 1:11; Mal 1:10; Heb 10:6–8) and explicitly directs it toward others (steadfast love, justice, righteousness, the Son, repentant persons). The pattern of divine delight and divine withholding-of-delight together reveal that Delight is a morally structured characteristic in God's inner life — not indiscriminate pleasure but directed toward genuine goodness and relational faithfulness.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The evidence reveals four created purposes: (1) **Alignment of the will with what is genuinely good** — Psa 40:8 ("I delight to do your will, O my God; your law is within my heart") — the will directed by Delight toward God's law enables natural, willing obedience rather than reluctant compliance; 1Ch 28:9 — "a willing mind" as the right orientation for serving God; (2) **Sustaining the inner person through adversity** — Psa 119:92 ("if your law had not been my delight, I would have perished in my affliction") — Delight in God's word is a life-preserving inner resource; similarly Psa 119:77, 143 — delight sustains through trouble and anguish; (3) **Orienting the inner being toward its proper object** — Psa 37:4 ("delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart") — Delight in God aligns the will with his giving, restructuring what the heart desires; (4) **Equipping moral discernment** — Psa 1:2 ("his delight is in the law of the Lord, and on his law he meditates day and night") — the delight-in-law faculty drives the sustained engagement that produces wisdom; the Preacher who "sought to find words of delight" (Ecc 12:10) names the inner faculty as a vehicle for discovering and expressing truth.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Both, with strong original-design grounding. (1) **Original design** — Pro 8:30–31 shows Delight operating before creation as Wisdom's daily pleasure before God and in the children of man — pre-fall, pre-human, grounding Delight as a created-design capacity anterior to the fall; Psa 37:4's promise ("delight yourself in the Lord… he will give you the desires of your heart") implies that properly directed Delight is what the person was constituted to experience; M04-H-VCG-03's volitional-preference idiom throughout the historical narratives shows human delight as a normal created capacity for personal judgment and preference; (2) **Response to fallen condition** — M04-P (corrupt delight — Eze 23:12; Hab 3:14; Isa 66:3) and M04-H-VCG-04 (negated and misdirected delight — Isa 65:12; 66:4; 2Th 2:12) are specifically fallen-condition phenomena — the misdirection of a created good toward wrong objects; M04-M (pleasing God as obedience orientation) in contexts of cultic correction (Lev 1:17; 1Sa 15:22; Hos 6:6) is also fallen-condition remedial; Delight-in-God's-word as a sustained life-preserving resource (Psa 119:92) is specifically operative in the fallen world's suffering.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in several registers: (1) **Eschatological delight in YHWH** — Isa 58:14 ("then you shall take delight in the Lord, and I will make you ride on the heights of the earth") — delight in the Lord as the eschatological fruit of Sabbath faithfulness; Job 22:26 ("for then you will delight yourself in the Almighty") — restored delight in God as the future state of the person brought back; (2) **Jerusalem as a land of delight** — Mal 3:12 ("you will be a land of delight, says the Lord of hosts") — the restored nation inhabiting its fullest delight-quality as the eschatological goal; Isa 62:4 — Zion renamed "My Delight Is in Her" as the eschatological reversal; (3) **Father's good pleasure giving the kingdom** — Luk 12:32 ("it is your Father's good pleasure to give you the kingdom") — divine delight as the willing eschatological gift; (4) **Isa 11:8's nursing child playing over the cobra's hole** — carefree sha.a in the eschatological age of peace when natural enmity is removed, pointing toward a fullness of fearless delight not yet present.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight expresses the divine image through two primary image-bearing patterns: (1) **Volitional delight as the image of divine sovereignty** — God's cha.phets is his sovereign will (Psa 115:3; 135:6); the human person's cha.phets/che.phets is a creaturely participation in the capacity for directed inner willing that resembles the divine. Gen 34:19 (Shechem's delight driving urgent action), Psa 40:8 (the psalmist delighting to do God's will), and 1Ch 28:9 (the willing mind) show human volitional delight as the image-bearing analogue of divine purposeful willing; (2) **Affective delight as the image of divine relational pleasure** — God's delight in persons (Psa 18:19; Isa 62:4; Jer 31:20) is the divine pattern; the human person's delight in God (Psa 37:4; Job 22:26; Isa 58:14), in God's word (Psa 119; Rom 7:22), and in fellow persons (1Sa 19:1; Psa 16:3; 1Th 2:8) images back the divine relational-pleasure capacity. Mic 6:8's "he has told you what is good" applies here: human Delight directed at God, his word, and righteous character is the created-image response to the divine inner pleasure that first directed itself toward humanity.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Genuinely shared, most extensively of any M04 characteristic. The same root terms appear for both: (1) cha.phets — used for God's sovereign will (Isa 53:10; Psa 115:3; 135:6), God's relational delight (Psa 18:19; 37:23; Eze 18:23), and human volitional delight and desire (Gen 34:19; Psa 40:8; Job 13:3); (2) eudokeo — used for the Father's delight in the Son (Mat 3:17; Col 1:19; 2Pe 1:17), and for human willing generosity (Rom 15:26–27), preference (2Cor 5:8), and contentment (2Cor 12:10). Pro 8:30–31 is unique: the same sha.a.shu.im term names both God's delight in Wisdom and Wisdom's delight in the children of man, creating a shared-delight architecture — God delights in Wisdom, Wisdom delights in humans, humans are constituted as delight-objects of God and delight-bearing agents. The sharing is thorough.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — **Presence** of rightly directed Delight reveals the image functioning: Psa 40:8 (delighting to do God's will with the law within the heart — image functioning at its most direct, Heb 10:7 applies this to Christ); Psa 1:2 (delight in the law driving sustained meditation — the blessed person's image operating through will and mind); Rom 7:22 (delight in God's law "in my inner being" — even in the struggling person, the deepest layer of the image is directed toward God's law). **Misdirected Delight** reveals the image distorted: Isa 66:3 ("their soul delights in their abominations") — the same delight-capacity directed at the opposite of its proper object; Jer 6:10 (no pleasure in God's word — the image closed to its proper object); 2Th 2:12 (pleasure in unrighteousness — the image inverted). M04-F (fragile horizontal delight in beast-captivation — Rev 13:3; 17:8) shows the most complete image-distortion: the delight faculty captivated by an antichrist figure rather than by God. Jer 6:10's uncircumcised ear that takes no pleasure in God's word is the diagnostic of a heart sealed against its proper object.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, richly: (1) **Christological — the Father's eudokeo** — the divine delight declared at baptism (Mat 3:17; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22) and transfiguration (Mat 17:5; 2Pe 1:17) is the OT covenant-pattern of divine pleasure in the servant (Isa 42:1 — "my beloved with whom my soul is well pleased" — the servant-song's divine pleasure typologically fulfilled in Christ); (2) **Redemptive typology — Isa 53:10** — "it was the will of the Lord to crush him; the will of the Lord shall prosper in his hand" — the most theologically charged divine-cha.phets in the corpus: God's delight directed toward the servant's suffering-for-sin as the mechanism of redemption; this is divine Delight as the hinge of atonement; (3) **Wisdom's delight as type of Christ** — Pro 8:30–31 (Wisdom as God's daily delight, delighting in the children of man) is the type that the NT applies christologically; (4) **The "land of delight" and "My Delight Is in Her"** (Mal 3:12; Isa 62:4) — OT types of the eschatological community that the NT's "body of Christ" fulfils; (5) **Luk 12:32** — "the Father's good pleasure to give you the kingdom" fulfils the OT pattern of divine delight as the ground of covenantal gift.

---

**T0.4.2 — What is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Both directions operate: (1) **Divine-to-human** — the divine eudokeo (Father's delight in the Son) establishes the pattern for the human person's delight in God (the human response images back the divine originating pleasure); Isa 53:10's divine cha.phets is the originating sovereign act that the human appropriation of redemption participates in; (2) **Human-to-eschatological** — Psa 37:4's "delight yourself in the Lord" is the present human act that anticipates the eschatological fullness of Job 22:26 and Isa 58:14; the present Delight in God's word (Psa 119) is the type of the eschatological resting in God. Isa 42:1's servant-delight is the human-typological figure pointing to Christ; the divine eudokeo at the baptism confirms it.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Typological use is the most extensively evidenced typological pattern in M04; not silent.

## T1 — Definition and Structural Analysis

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic is called Delight. The name signals: (1) **Object-focus** — Delight is always delight *in* something or someone; the name implies a specific treasured object that the inner person rests in and returns to; (2) **Receptive orientation** — unlike Exultation (active upward surge) or Joy (participatory engagement), Delight implies a resting, returning, orienting movement toward the beloved object; (3) **Affective-volitional integration** — the name in English carries both the emotional quality (it feels pleasurable) and the volitional quality (one chooses to delight, one finds and returns to what delights); this dual quality is exactly what the corpus confirms — M04-G is the affective face and M04-H is the volitional face; (4) **Scope across good and evil** — English "delight" can apply to both legitimate and illegitimate objects, which is consistent with M04-P's corrupt-delight register.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Primary terms span a wide vocabulary: (1) **sha.a.shu.im** (H8191) — delights, cherished things (noun, plural of intensity): used of God's darling child Ephraim (Jer 31:20), Wisdom as God's daily delight (Pro 8:30–31), the psalmist's delight in God's testimonies (Psa 119:24, 77, 92, 143, 174), Isa 5:7 (Israel as God's pleasant planting), Jer 31:20. The plural-of-intensity form signals the full, rich, multiple-faceted quality of treasured pleasure; (2) **sha.a** (H8173B) — to delight, play, carefree enjoyment: Psa 94:19 (God's consolations cheer the soul); Psa 119:16, 47, 70 (delight in statutes); Isa 11:8 (nursing child playing over cobra); Isa 66:12 (bounced on knees in peace); the root carries a carefree, playful quality of delight; (3) **a.nog** (H6026) — to be delicate, to delight oneself: Psa 37:4, 11; Job 22:26; 27:10; Isa 55:2; 58:14; 66:11; Deu 28:56 — the term implies a luxuriating, resting, pampered quality of inner satisfaction; (4) **o.neg** (H6027) — delight as noun (Isa 58:13); (5) **cha.phets** (H2654A) — verbal: to take delight in, to be pleased, to desire — the dominant M04-H verbal form covering both divine volitional pleasure and human desire/delight; (6) **che.phets** (H2656) — noun: delight, desire, matter — the dominant M04-H nominal form; (7) **cha.phets** adj (H2655) — delighting in; (8) **eudokeo** (G2106) — to be well pleased, think it good, take delight: 17 NT instances across M04-H-VCG-01 (divine pleasure in the Son) and VCG-02 (human willing-pleasure); (9) **arestos** (G0701) — pleasing, acceptable: M04-M-VCG-02 (Joh 8:29; Act 6:2; 1Jo 3:22); (10) **sunēdomai** (G4913) — to delight together with: Rom 7:22 (delight in God's law in the inner being); (11) **ta.a.nug** (H8588) — daintiness, delight: Mic 1:16 (parental delight); (12) **che.med** (H2531) — desire, delight, covetousness: Eze 23:6, 12, 23 (lustful captivation); (13) **a.li.tsut** (H5951) — exultation, predatory joy: Hab 3:14; (14) **ni.cho.ach** (H5207) — soothing aroma: M04-M-VCG-01 (correct worship) and M04-P-VCG-01 (misdirected worship); (15) **thaumazō** (G2296) — to marvel, wonder: Rev 13:3; 17:8 (beast-captivation). At definitional level: the vocabulary spans from playful carefree pleasure (sha.a) through luxuriating satisfaction (a.nog) through cherished treasuring (sha.a.shu.im) through directed volitional willing (cha.phets/eudokeo) through obedient-pleasing orientation (arestos) through corrupt-lustful desire (che.med) to predatory elation (a.li.tsut).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — (1) **Directional**: Delight always has an object it is directed toward — the directional implication is the most distinctive feature; Psa 73:25 ("there is nothing on earth that I desire besides you") shows Delight at its most purely directional — entirely toward God; (2) **Relational**: the relational implications are pervasive — Delight in persons (parental: Jer 31:20; Mic 1:16; interpersonal: 1Sa 19:1; Psa 16:3; 1Th 2:8; divine-human: Psa 18:19; Isa 62:4) shows that Delight is fundamentally relational in most of its registers; (3) **Constitutional**: Rom 7:22 ("in my inner being") is the most explicit constitutional location statement in the corpus — the deepest layer of the self; Mat 12:18's "my soul is well pleased" locates divine Delight in the soul (psychē); (4) **Volitional**: the cha.phets/che.phets vocabulary implies an act or orientation of the will — Delight is not merely felt but chosen and directed.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is a complex phenomenon spanning four kinds: (1) **Disposition** — the most common form: a stable inner orientation toward a specific object (sha.a.shu.im in Psa 119 series — the law as the psalmist's settled delight; Psa 1:2 — the blessed man's delight in the law as a defining character disposition; Psa 73:25 — God as the exclusive object of directed desire); (2) **Act** — cha.phets frequently marks a specific act of will: Gen 34:19 (Shechem's delight driving immediate action); Psa 40:8 (delighting to do God's will); the diplomatic "as it pleases you" idiom throughout M04-H-VCG-03; (3) **State** — a.nog (Psa 37:4, 11) and sha.a (Isa 66:12) name a resting, luxuriating condition of the inner being; (4) **Quality** — arestos (pleasing, acceptable) and ni.cho.ach (soothing aroma) name a quality of what the inner being produces or embodies before God. Delight is therefore an inner-being phenomenon that is simultaneously a disposition directing the will, an act of the will choosing its object, a state of resting satisfaction in the found object, and a quality of the person before God.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The most structurally complex characteristic in M04 — five distinct constituent registers correspond to the five sub-groups: (1) **Affective-receptive delight in God's word and God himself** (M04-G) — the soul's pleasurable orientation toward its divine object, sustained through meditation and resistant to adversity (sha.a.shu.im, sha.a, a.nog, sunēdomai); (2) **Volitional delight as directed will** (M04-H) — both divine sovereign pleasure and human directed desire; spans four VCGs: divine sovereignty (28), God's relational pleasure in persons (31), human volitional willing (105), negated/corrupt pleasure (20); (3) **Obedience-delight: the inner orientation toward what pleases God** (M04-M) — the soul directed toward what is acceptable (arestos, ni.cho.ach, tov-in-obedience); (4) **Relational horizontal delight** (M04-N) — pleasantness between persons (ta.a.nug, tov, thaumazō); (5) **Corrupt and misdirected delight** (M04-P) — the disordered face (ni.cho.ach misdirected, che.med lustful, a.li.tsut predatory). The structural complexity is itself analytically significant: Delight is the characteristic most fully showing the range from rightly-ordered inner pleasure to its complete corruption.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is the inner state of treasuring and taking pleasure in a specific object — the soul's resting satisfaction in what it loves. It is more object-focused than Gladness (which is a diffuse evaluative warmth) and more receptive than Exultation (which is an active upward surge). The person who delights has found an object that satisfies their inner being: they rest in it, return to it, orient themselves toward it. The characteristic operates in two primary integrated modes: an affective-receptive mode (sha.a.shu.im, sha.a, a.nog — the soul's pleasurable resting in a specific treasured object: God's law, God himself, wisdom, restored Zion) and a volitional-directive mode (cha.phets/che.phets/eudokeo — the will's directed inner pleasure that motivates action and expresses sovereign purpose). These two modes are not separate characteristics but the same inner-being capacity operating through different faculties — affect and will — on a treasured object. Constitutional primary location: the inner being (Rom 7:22), the heart (Psa 40:8; 1:2), and the soul (Mat 12:18). The characteristic also carries an obedience-orientation face (M04-M), a relational-horizontal face (M04-N), and a corrupt face (M04-P).

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Multiple structural opposites: (1) **No delight / absence of pleasure** — the most frequent: God "has no pleasure in the death of the wicked" (Eze 18:23; 33:11); "my joy is gone" (Jer 8:18 — mav.li.git, the gone-comfort); "they take no pleasure in it" [God's word] (Jer 6:10); "I have no pleasure in you" (Mal 1:10); Ecc 12:1 — old age when pleasure drains away; (2) **Disgust and rejection** — the opposite of God's delight in persons: "like a vessel no one cares for" (Jer 22:28; 48:38 — che.phets negated as total rejection); (3) **Self-directed pleasure without God** — Isa 58:13 — "seeking your own pleasure" on the Sabbath as the opposite of delight in God; (4) **Corrupt delight** — M04-P shows delight directed at wrong objects: Isa 66:3 ("their soul delights in their abominations"); 2Th 2:12 (pleasure in unrighteousness); (5) **Mourning as loss of delight** — Deu 28:56 (the delicate woman losing her delicateness); Jer 7:34; 16:9 (voice of gladness silenced). The structural opposite cluster is: no-delight (total absence), rejected-delight (object withheld), and misdirected-delight (object corrupted).

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight explicitly excludes: (1) **External ritual without inner transformation** — Psa 40:6; 51:16; Isa 1:11; Heb 10:6, 8 — God's delight is not engaged by sacrifice and offering without the accompanying inner reality; "to obey is better than sacrifice" (1Sa 15:22; M04-M-VCG-02); Hos 6:6 — God's desire is steadfast love, not sacrifice; (2) **Self-seeking pleasure on holy time** — Isa 58:13 — "not going your own ways, or seeking your own pleasure" on the Sabbath; (3) **Delight in wrong objects** — Psa 5:4 — "you are not a God who delights in wickedness"; Isa 65:12; 66:4 — the people "chose what I did not delight in"; 1Cor 10:5 — God was not pleased with most of Israel in the wilderness; Heb 10:38 — God's soul has no pleasure in the one who shrinks back; (4) **Impulsive desire without knowledge** — Pro 19:2 — "desire without knowledge is not good"; (5) **Misdirected worship-delight** — M04-P-VCG-01 (ni.cho.ach aroma to idols — Eze 6:13; 16:19; 20:28).

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Delight is not: (1) **Gladness** (Characteristic 3) — Gladness is the warm, cognitively-grounded inner state of one who has accurately assessed that things are genuinely good; Delight is more object-focused and more explicitly directed at a specific treasured person or thing; the distinction is that Gladness is diffuse recognition, Delight is specific attachment; (2) **Joy** (Characteristic 2) — Joy is active participatory engagement; Delight is receptive resting satisfaction; Joy exults in, Delight rests in; (3) **Exultation** (Characteristic 1) — Exultation is the upward surge of the inner being; Delight is the inward settling toward the beloved object; (4) **Pleasure** (Characteristic 5) — Pleasure is the receptive inner agreeable sensation through sensory and material channels; Delight is more specifically object-oriented and includes the volitional direction of the will; (5) **Love** — Love's choosing and cherishing overlaps with Delight but Love carries a stronger volitional commitment; Delight is the pleasurable dimension of Love; (6) **Mere preference** — the M04-H-VCG-03 "as it seems good to you" diplomatic idiom is preference and practical judgment, not Delight's deeper affective-volitional anchoring in a treasured object; the spectrum within M04-H is wide, and the surface preference instances (Est, Neh diplomatic formulas) are at the thinner edge of Delight's range.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Five distinct operational modes corresponding to the sub-groups: (1) **Affective-receptive mode** (M04-G) — the inner being luxuriating in and sustained by a specific treasured object (sha.a.shu.im in Psa 119's law-delight series; a.nog in Psa 37:4; sha.a in Isa 66:12's bounced-on-knees image); (2) **Sovereign-volitional mode** (M04-H-VCG-01) — delight as purposeful directed will: God's cha.phets producing action (Psa 115:3; Isa 53:10; 55:11); the king's volitional pleasure as his sovereign act (Est 6:6–11); (3) **Relational-delight mode** (M04-H-VCG-02) — delight directed toward persons: God's redemptive pleasure in persons producing rescue and exaltation (Psa 18:19; Isa 62:4); human relational delight (1Sa 19:1; Psa 16:3; 1Th 2:8); (4) **Obedience-orientation mode** (M04-M) — the inner being directed toward what pleases God, producing acceptable worship (arestos in Joh 8:29; 1Jo 3:22; ni.cho.ach in Lev 1:17); (5) **Corrupt mode** (M04-P) — the same faculty misdirected: lustful captivation (che.med — Eze 23:12), misdirected worship (ni.cho.ach — Eze 20:28), predatory elation (a.li.tsut — Hab 3:14).

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes: (1) **By object**: when directed toward God and his word (M04-G), Delight operates as the soul's resting orientation, producing sustained inner life; when directed toward persons (M04-H-VCG-02, M04-N), it operates as relational affection motivating care and action; when directed toward wrong objects (M04-P), the same faculty produces corruption and judgment; (2) **By constitutional level**: the affective-receptive mode (M04-G) engages the inner being (Rom 7:22) and heart (Psa 40:8) most deeply; the sovereign-volitional mode (M04-H-VCG-01) engages the will; the diplomatic-preference idiom (M04-H-VCG-03 surface instances) engages judgment and practical discernment at a shallower level; (3) **By register**: the divine sovereign mode operates without constraint (Psa 115:3); the human volitional mode operates under moral constraint — desire without knowledge is not good (Pro 19:2); the obedience mode (M04-M) operates under the frame of divine approval; (4) **By direction (God to human vs. human to God)**: God's delight toward the person (M04-H-VCG-02) is redemptive; the human person's delight toward God (M04-G, M04-H-VCG-03 in its righteous registers) is worshipful and volitional.

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, in two forms: (1) **Divine speech declaring delight** — the baptism/transfiguration declarations (Mat 3:17; 17:5; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22; 2Pe 1:17) — "this is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased" — are the most programmatic speech-acts of Delight in Scripture; the Father's eudokeo is declared by a voice from heaven; (2) **Diplomatic and practical speech** — the M04-H-VCG-03 "as it seems good to you" / "if it please the king" idiom throughout the historical books (Esther, Nehemiah, Samuel, Kings) is a speech-mode of Delight: the speaker verbally defers to or invokes the other's inner pleasure as the governing standard; this is communicative Delight in the relational-volitional sense. The communicative mode is thus both the highest divine declaration and the most ordinary human relational deference.

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering Delight?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Varies significantly by mode: (1) **Affective-receptive mode** (M04-G): immediate inner animation and sustained orientation — Psa 94:19 ("your consolations cheer my soul" — sha.a producing immediate inner brightening when cares overwhelm); (2) **Volitional mode** (M04-H): immediate action — Gen 34:19 ("the young man did not delay to do the thing, because he delighted in Jacob's daughter") — the most immediate response: delight translating directly and instantly into action; (3) **Relational-delight mode** (M04-H-VCG-02): immediate rescue and action — Psa 18:19 ("he rescued me, because he delighted in me") — divine delight in the person produces immediate saving action; (4) **Obedience mode** (M04-M): immediate appropriate action — Joh 8:29 ("I always do the things that are pleasing to him") — the person oriented toward what pleases God acts immediately in alignment with that pleasure.

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Varies by mode, with a consistent directional pattern: in all registers, Delight when present produces movement toward the object — whether immediate action (Gen 34:19), sustained meditation (Psa 1:2; 119:16), rescue (Psa 18:19), worship (Psa 37:4), or generosity (1Th 2:8). The immediate response is always a movement of the will or affect toward the object of Delight. The sovereign-volitional mode produces immediate sovereign action (God does what he pleases — Psa 115:3); the relational mode produces relational movement toward the beloved; the affective-receptive mode produces inner orientation and sustained engagement. What varies is the faculty (will vs. affect) not the directionality (toward the object).

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Sustained Delight produces: (1) **Life-preservation under adversity** — Psa 119:92 ("if your law had not been my delight, I would have perished in my affliction") — the single most striking sustained-effect statement in the corpus; (2) **Aligned desires** — Psa 37:4 ("delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart") — sustained Delight in God restructures the heart's desires to align with his giving; (3) **Sustained meditation and wisdom** — Psa 1:2 (delight in the law → meditating day and night → the blessed man's formation); Ecc 12:10 (the Preacher seeking words of delight → finding truth in delightful form); (4) **Productive will** — Isa 44:28; 46:10; 55:11 — God's sustained sovereign cha.phets producing accomplished purpose; (5) **Moral integrity** — Joh 8:29 (always pleasing the Father — the sustained effect of Delight-in-God's-pleasure producing consistent moral integrity); (6) **Corrupt formation** — Isa 66:3 ("their soul delights in their abominations") — sustained misdirected Delight forms a person deeply embedded in the wrong object.

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, and orientations does sustained Delight establish?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — (1) **Sustained will alignment** — the person whose Delight is in God progressively finds their desires conformed to his (Psa 37:4); (2) **Law-internalized** — Psa 40:8's "your law is within my heart" as the fruit of delight-in-obedience (M04-M); the inner law-delight produces a person whose will is no longer law-external but law-internal; (3) **Unbroken relationship with God** — Joh 8:29 — Jesus always doing what pleases the Father as the fruit of perfect sustained Delight; (4) **Eschatological orientation** — Job 22:26 (future delight in the Almighty) and Isa 58:14 (delight in the Lord as the promised outcome of Sabbath faithfulness) — sustained Delight establishes forward eschatological orientation; (5) **Worship as natural overflow** — the ki-tov Psalms (already addressed in Char 3, but here the cha.phets/sha.a.shu.im dimension): God's great works studied by those who delight in them (Psa 111:2) — sustained delight produces sustained engagement and eventually worship.

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes significantly. The immediate response is a movement of will or affect toward the object; the sustained effect is transformation of the person: desires restructured (Psa 37:4), the law internalized (Psa 40:8), the inner person sustained through adversity (Psa 119:92), wisdom formed (Psa 1:2). The immediate response is a directional movement; the sustained effect is constitutional change. In the corrupt mode, this distinction is equally stark: the immediate response is lustful attraction (Eze 23:12); the sustained effect is that "their soul delights in their abominations" (Isa 66:3) — the person's deepest inner faculty is now formed around the wrong object.

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of Delight?**

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters Delight but does not receive it?**

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The primary dimension is **Emotion-Positive / Relational Disposition in integrated combination**. Delight is not primarily cognitive (like Gladness) or primarily affective-Spirit (like Suffering-Joy) but is the integrated inner-being act of directing pleasurable attention and will toward a treasured object — which simultaneously involves affect (the pleasure) and relational disposition (the orientation toward). The M04-G corpus makes the affective-positive dimension primary; the M04-H volitional corpus makes the volitional dimension prominent; M04-M makes the obedience-orientation prominent. Given the characteristic's breadth, Emotion-Positive (the felt pleasurable quality) is the primary dimension with Relational Disposition (the directedness-toward) as a strong secondary.
