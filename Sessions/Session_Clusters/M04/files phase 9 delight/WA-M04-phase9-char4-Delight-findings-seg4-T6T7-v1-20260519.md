## T7 — Lexical, Literary, and Human Science Analysis

**T7.1.1 — What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms — and what do their root meanings reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Primary terms: (1) **sha.a.shu.im** (H8191) — plural of intensity from sha.a root; root meaning: to delight, play, caress; the plural intensification names not a single act of pleasure but accumulated, multiple-faceted cherishing — the richness of God's treasuring of Ephraim (Jer 31:20), Wisdom's treasuring of humanity (Pro 8:31), the psalmist's treasuring of God's testimonies (Psa 119:24); (2) **sha.a** (H8173B) — verb: to delight, play, sport; root: carefree enjoyment, playing without threat; the nursing-child-over-cobra (Isa 11:8) and bounced-on-knees (Isa 66:12) images bring out the playful-carefree quality; (3) **a.nog** (H6026) — to be delicate, to luxuriate, to delight oneself; root: delicateness, soft/luxurious quality; the most physically-textured of the Delight terms — Deu 28:56's delicate woman, Isa 55:2's rich food, Isa 66:11's deep drinking from abundance; (4) **cha.phets** (H2654A) — to take delight in, to desire, to be pleased; root: to bend down/bow toward; the most volitionally-oriented verbal root — the bending of the will toward its object; (5) **che.phets** (H2656) — delight, desire, matter, affair; the nominal form of H2654A; (6) **cha.phets adj** (H2655) — delighting in (1Ki 13:33; 21:6 — "any who would"); (7) **eudokeo** (G2106) — to think it good, to be well pleased; from eu (well) + dokeo (think/seem); the NT equivalent that carries both the affective-approval quality and the cognitive-positive assessment; the Father's eudokeo in the Son is the deepest NT deployment; (8) **sunēdomai** (G4913) — to rejoice/delight together with; compound: sun (together with) + hēdomai (to be pleased/enjoy); the co-delight register of Rom 7:22 — the inner being delighting in God's law in solidarity with it; (9) **arestos** (G0701) — pleasing, acceptable; from areskō (to please); the compliance/acceptability register of M04-M; (10) **ni.cho.ach** (H5207) — soothing, pleasing (as an aroma); root: to rest/settle; the aroma that settles or soothes — used for the inner-being condition of correctness in worship (or its corruption).

---

**T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term — and what does that range reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — **cha.phets** (H2654A) functions across all grammatical modes: (1) **Verbal** — Gen 34:19 (Shechem delighted in Dinah); Psa 40:8 (I delight to do your will); Isa 53:10 (it was the will of the Lord to crush him) — active volitional acts; (2) **Adjectival** (H2655) — 1Ki 13:33 ("any who would") — predicate adjective of willingness; (3) **Nominal** (H2656) — Psa 1:2 ("his delight is in the law"); 1Sa 15:22 ("has the Lord as great delight in burnt offerings") — stable nominal state or orientation; 2Sa 23:5 ("all my help and my desire"). The tri-form grammatical spread (verb/adjective/noun) reveals Delight as a characteristic that operates simultaneously as act (verbal), disposition (adjectival), and condition (nominal). This grammatical completeness distinguishes Delight from characteristics that operate primarily in one grammatical mode.

---

**T7.1.3 — What is the semantic range of the primary term?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — cha.phets/che.phets ranges across: (1) **Divine sovereign will** — Psa 115:3; 135:6; Isa 44:28; 46:10; 53:10; 55:11 — God's cha.phets as his operative purpose; (2) **Divine relational delight in persons** — Psa 18:19; 37:23; 51:6; Jer 9:24; Hos 6:6; Mic 7:18; (3) **Human strong personal desire** — Gen 34:19; Psa 73:25; Job 13:3; 1Ki 9:1; (4) **Human volitional preference in practical/diplomatic contexts** — the entire M04-H-VCG-03 diplomatic idiom; (5) **Negated pleasure** — Psa 5:4; Heb 10:6, 8; Jer 6:10; Mal 1:10; (6) **Love's own readiness** — Song 2:7; 3:5; 8:4. Semantic range runs from the most transcendent (God's sovereign will) to the most mundane (diplomatic preference) through the most intimate (love's readiness). The range is unique in M04.

---

**T7.1.4 — Does the vocabulary include terms that distinguish distinct aspects?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes, across the full vocabulary: (1) **sha.a.shu.im** — the treasuring-cherishing aspect (multi-faceted, accumulated pleasure); **sha.a** — the playful-carefree aspect; **a.nog** — the luxuriating-resting aspect; **o.neg** — the noun of that luxuriating quality; these four together distinguish the affective-register's nuances; (2) **cha.phets** (verb) vs. **che.phets** (noun) vs. **cha.phets** (adj) — distinguish the act, the condition, and the disposition of volitional Delight; (3) **sunēdomai** — the solidarity-Delight aspect (delighting together with, in inner-being); (4) **arestos** — the acceptability/pleasing-compliance aspect; (5) **ni.cho.ach** — the soothing-aroma aspect (the quality of what rises from correct worship); (6) **ta.a.nug** — the daintiness/tender-parental aspect; (7) **che.med** — the covetous-lustful aspect of corrupt Delight; (8) **a.li.tsut** — the predatory-exultation aspect of corrupt Delight.

**T7.1.8 — What does the OT Hebrew / NT Greek vocabulary relationship reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The relationship shows both strong continuity and significant NT development: (1) **Continuity** — eudokeo in the NT continues the cha.phets register of divine sovereign pleasure (Mat 3:17; Col 1:19; Gal 1:16) and human willing-delight (Rom 15:26–27; 2Cor 5:8; 12:10); the divine-pleasure vocabulary is directly continuous from OT to NT; (2) **Development** — the NT concentrates the divine-Delight vocabulary on the Father-Son relationship (the baptism/transfiguration eudokeo series) in a way the OT does not; eudokeo in the Father's declaration over the Son is the NT's most distinctive Delight contribution; (3) **Reduction** — the rich OT sha.a.shu.im/sha.a/a.nog affective-register has no single NT equivalent; sunēdomai (Rom 7:22) is the closest NT equivalent for the affective-inner-being Delight, but it appears only once; (4) **New obedience-vocabulary** — arestos (G0701) as the M04-M obedience-pleasing term appears only in the NT, naming the obedience-orientation of Delight with a specifically Hellenistic term.

The NT deployment of eudokeo in the Father-Son declaration (Mat 3:17) is theologically new but not lexically new.

**T7.1.10 — What does the full vocabulary arc reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The full vocabulary arc from sha.a.shu.im/sha.a/a.nog (affective-resting) through cha.phets/che.phets (volitional-sovereign) through arestos/ni.cho.ach (obedience-pleasing) through ta.a.nug (parental tenderness) through che.med/a.li.tsut (corrupt desire) reveals Delight as the most semantically complex and structurally comprehensive characteristic in M04. The arc spans from the most divinely transcendent (God's sovereign cha.phets declaring purpose — Isa 46:10) through the most intimately relational (Jer 31:20's sha.a.shu.im as God's cherishing of Ephraim-as-darling-child) through the most embodied (a.nog's luxuriating satisfaction) to the most corrupt (a.li.tsut's predatory elation). No other M04 characteristic spans this range.

**T7.2.1 — What is the function of the primary term within its primary verse?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — In Psa 37:4 ("delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart"), **a.nog** (hitpael — reflexive: "delight yourself") functions as the **commanded reflexive volitional act** — the psalmist is commanded to direct the luxuriating delight faculty toward the Lord. The hitpael form is theologically significant: it names a self-directed action of the inner being — the person is to actively orient themselves toward God as the object of a.nog. The second clause ("he will give you the desires of your heart") shows the consequential divine response: Delight in God restructures the heart's desires in alignment with what God gives. The primary term functions as the pivotal inner-being act on which the whole verse's promise turns.

**T7.2.2 — What literary form carries the primary verse evidence — and what does that form require?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Primary forms: (1) **Psalmic instruction/promise** (Psa 37:4; 73:25; 84:10; Psa 119 series) — requires reading as both individual and communal guidance for the inner life, not merely descriptive; (2) **Prophetic oracle** (Isa 58:13–14; 62:4; Jer 9:24; Jer 31:20; Eze 18:23; Mic 7:18) — requires reading as direct divine speech about God's own inner dispositions — the most authoritative register for divine-Delight claims; (3) **Wisdom instruction** (Psa 1:2; Pro 8:30–31; Ecc 12:10) — requires reading as formative teaching about the inner life's proper orientation; (4) **Narrative** (1Sa 19:1; Gen 34:19; Est 6:6–11) — requires reading as character evidence showing Delight in operation; (5) **Epistolary** (Rom 7:22; 1Th 2:8; 1Jo 3:22; 2Th 2:12; Heb 10:38) — requires reading as instruction to specific communities facing specific challenges; (6) **Divine declaration** (Mat 3:17; 12:18; 17:5) — requires reading as the most theologically weighted statement of divine Delight — not simile or metaphor but direct divine speech.

**T7.3.1 — Which human science framework is most useful as an interpretive lens?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Two frameworks: (1) **Self-determination theory** (Deci and Ryan) — the psychological framework distinguishing intrinsic motivation (doing something because it is inherently pleasurable and satisfying) from extrinsic motivation (doing it for external reward). **[CHAR-4]** E — Self-determination theory illuminates why Psa 40:8's "I delight to do your will" is constitutionally different from mere compliance: intrinsic motivation produces sustained, energised, self-generating engagement; extrinsic motivation produces resentful compliance that stops when the external pressure stops. **T7.3.3 — Where the verse evidence and the human science framework diverge — what does the divergence reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Self-determination theory treats intrinsic motivation as generated by the person's internal psycho-social processes. The verse evidence treats Delight in God as both the person's own genuine inner state (Rom 7:22 — genuinely the inner being's Delight) AND as given or restructured by God (Psa 37:4 — delight in the Lord → he gives the heart's desires; the desires being given are not the person's autonomous production). The divergence reveals that Scripture's Delight-in-God is simultaneously intrinsic (genuinely the person's own inner pleasure) and received (given and restructured by God) — a paradox that human science cannot account for within its framework of self-generated vs. externally-provided motivation. Attachment theory's primary divergence: secure human attachment depends on the attachment figure being consistently responsive; divine attachment (God's Delight) in the evidence is responsive to human faithfulness (Psa 37:23) but also precedes and grounds it (Jer 31:20's persistent cherishing). Divine attachment has no human analogue.
