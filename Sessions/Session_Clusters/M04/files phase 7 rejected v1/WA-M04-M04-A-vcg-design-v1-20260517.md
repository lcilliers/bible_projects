## M04-A-VCG-01 — Exultation in God: Joy Directed Toward YHWH as Object

**Context description:** This VCG names the core register of M04-A: inner exultation, gladness, and rejoicing whose explicit object is YHWH himself — not what he has done (VCG-02) but who he is. Verses in this group name God or his name as the direct locus of joy: the soul rejoices "in the Lord" (Psa 35:9; Isa 61:10), hearts rejoice toward his holy name (1Ch 16:10), God is called "my exceeding joy" (Psa 43:4), rejoicing "in the Lord" is commanded as a standing disposition (Phil 3:1; 4:4; 1Th 5:16; Hab 3:18). The psalmist's vertical orientation — seeking, finding, and exulting in God's presence — is the defining content. This includes the OT pattern of rejoicing "before the LORD" at the sanctuary and the NT call to constant gladness in the Lord as a characteristic of the believing person.

## M04-A-VCG-02 — Joy at God's Saving Acts: Gladness at What YHWH Has Done

**Context description:** This VCG names the register of inner gladness evoked by specific divine acts — deliverance, victory, provision, answered prayer, the return of the ark, the coming of salvation. The joy here is responsive: it arises in direct answer to something God has done. Verses include rejoicing at the Exodus deliverance (Exo 18:9; Isa 25:9), gladness at military victory with God's help (1Sa 11:15; 2Ch 20:27), joy at the return of God's covenant presence (1Sa 6:13; 2Sa 6:12 context), gladness when God acts wondrously on behalf of his people (Isa 29:19; Joe 2:21; Joe 2:23), and the Psalms of thanksgiving where joy follows experienced rescue (Psa 9:14; Psa 21:1; Psa 66:6). This VCG is distinguished from VCG-01 by its event-specific trigger — the gladness of response to a specific divine act rather than orientation toward God himself as the steady object of joy.

## M04-A-VCG-03 — Divine Joy / God's Delight Directed Toward His People

**Context description:** This VCG names the register of joy and delight where God is the subject — his inner pleasure, rejoicing, and delight directed toward persons or creation. This is the M04 characteristic enacted by God himself: God delights in the Son (Mat 3:17; 12:18; 17:5; Mar 1:11; Luk 3:22; 2Pe 1:17), God's sovereign pleasure as the operative will (1Cor 1:21; Gal 1:16; Col 1:19; Luk 12:32), God's joy over his people (Zep 3:17; Jer 32:41; Isa 62:4; Isa 62:5; Deu 30:9; Deu 28:63), God's delight in steadfast love and righteousness (Jer 9:24; Hos 6:6; Mic 7:18), God's delight in the repentance of the wicked over their death (Eze 18:23; 18:32; 33:11). Per programme-scope note: these are characteristic-in-operation — God's joy/delight enacted toward his people — and they belong in M04. Also included: Paul's delight/willing-pleasure in the Thessalonians (1Th 2:8; Rom 15:26; 15:27) as the human analogue.

## M04-A-VCG-04 — NT Joy in Christ and the Spirit

**Context description:** This VCG names the distinctively New Testament register of joy: gladness arising from believing in Christ, indwelt by the Spirit, and anchored in the gospel. Key markers are the Holy Spirit as agent or source of joy (Gal 5:22 — fruit of the Spirit; Act 13:52 — filled with joy and Holy Spirit; 1Th 1:6 — joy given by the Holy Spirit; Rom 14:17 — joy in the kingdom produced by the Spirit; Rom 15:13 — God fills with joy through believing), Christ's own joy given to or indwelling the disciples (Joh 15:11 — my joy in you; Joh 17:13 — my joy fulfilled in them), and joy arising specifically from believing in or receiving the word of Christ (Luk 2:10 — great joy for all people; Mat 2:10 — joy at the sign of his birth; Act 16:34 — faith as direct source of joy; 1Pe 1:8 — inexpressible joy of believing in unseen Christ). The NT epistles contribute sustained relational joy in Christ and the gospel community (Phil 1:4, 4:10; 2Cor 13:11; 1Jo 1:4; 3Jo 4).

## M04-A-VCG-05 — Joy in Suffering: Paradoxical and Counter-intuitive Gladness

**Context description:** This VCG names a distinctively NT register: inner rejoicing that persists through, or arises specifically because of, suffering, hardship, weakness, or dishonour endured for Christ. The content is consistently paradoxical — joy co-existing with grief (1Pe 1:6), joy in the face of persecution commanded as a conscious reorientation (Mat 5:12; Luk 6:23; Jam 1:2), gladness at suffering dishonour for Christ's name (Act 5:41), joy at weakness and calamity endured for Christ (2Cor 12:10), rejoicing in sufferings for the church (Col 1:24), joy while being poured out as a sacrifice (Phil 2:17). This register is the M04 equivalent of M03's mourning that leads to comfort — it is not joy despite circumstances but joy through them, grounded in the hope of sharing Christ's glory (1Pe 4:13) or in the paradox that weakness is strength (2Cor 12:10). Also included: Heb 12:2 (joy set before Jesus sustaining the cross) and Heb 10:34 (joyful acceptance of property loss).

The joy here is characteristically expressed — it cannot remain silent but finds its voice in the gathered assembly.

## M04-A-VCG-07 — Joy as Heart-Disposition: Gladness Located and Held in the Inner Person

**Context description:** This VCG names the verses that foreground the heart or inner being as the explicit seat of gladness — joy as a constitutional inner disposition rather than a response to a specific event or a direction toward a specific object. Proverbs is rich in this register: the glad heart as good medicine (Pro 17:22), the glad heart shining on the face (Pro 15:13), the cheerful heart experiencing a continual feast (Pro 15:15), God putting joy directly in the heart (Psa 4:7), the heart explicitly named as where gladness resides and where God plants it (Psa 33:21; Psa 16:9; Psa 19:8; Psa 86:4). This VCG also includes the NT verses on joy as a constant, maintained inner disposition regardless of circumstance (1Th 5:16 — "rejoice always"; Phil 4:4 — double-repeated command to rejoice in the Lord; 2Cor 13:11 — joy as an ongoing inner anchor of community life). The distinction from VCG-01 is the emphasis: VCG-01 names God as the object; VCG-07 names the heart as the location and disposition.

## M04-A-VCG-08 — Inverted and Corrupt Joy: The Morally Dark Register

**Context description:** This VCG names the verses where the joy vocabulary names an inner gladness that is misdirected, morally perverted, or corrupt — joy over another's suffering, treacherous delight, hollow or idolatrous rejoicing, self-congratulatory gladness. The VCG also includes Haman's fragile/corrupt gladness (Est 5:9; 5:14).

## M04-A-VCG-10 — Delight as Volitional Inner Pleasure: The Will-Pleasure Register

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where cha.phets and che.phets function primarily as markers of inner volitional disposition — willing-pleasure, desire of the will, sovereign preference — rather than emotional gladness. These are verses where "delight" names what the will is oriented toward or consents to: human willingness or unwillingness to perform a legal or relational duty (Deu 21:14; 25:7; 25:8 — delight in marrying; Rut 3:13 — willingness to redeem), inner desire driving action (Gen 34:19 — Shechem's desire for Dinah; 1Ki 9:1 — Solomon's desire to build; Est 2:14; 6:6-11 — royal favour as volitional disposition), the pleasure underlying agreement or preference (1Ki 5:8-10; 5:9; 10:13; 2Ch 9:12), and the Ecclesiastes passages where "no pleasure" names loss of the will's capacity to enjoy (Ecc 12:1). Also included: che.phets as sovereign divine purpose (Isa 44:28; 46:10; 48:14 — God's settled will that accomplishes all things). This VCG captures the will-dimension of M04 that differs from emotional joy.
