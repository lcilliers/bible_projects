# Sub-group M04-B — Communal and Festive Rejoicing (10 verses)

## M04-B-VCG-01 — Shared Rejoicing: Joy Expressed and Received Together

**Context description:** This VCG names the defining characteristic of M04-B: inner gladness that is constitutively shared — sought out, invited into, and completed in community. The sunchairo verses (Luk 1:58; 15:6; 15:9; 1Cor 12:26; 13:6; Phil 2:17; 2:18) establish the core phenomenon: joy at personal recovery or good fortune that cannot remain private but must call others in to share it. The shepherd calls neighbours to co-rejoice at the found sheep (Luk 15:6); the woman calls friends to share her joy at the found coin (Luk 15:9); Elizabeth's mercy from God is shared by neighbours and relatives (Luk 1:58); the body members share gladness when one is honoured (1Cor 12:26); love co-rejoices with truth rather than wrongdoing (1Cor 13:6). The Aramaic ched.vah at Ezr 6:16 anchors the OT side — the communal emotional response to the completion of the house of God after years of exile. The register is marked by the movement of joy from one to many, and by joy as an act of solidarity.

# Sub-group M04-C — Delight in God's Word and Law (16 verses)

## M04-C-VCG-01 — Law-Delight: Affective Pleasure in God's Word, Statutes, and Testimonies

**Context description:** This VCG names the concentrated inner register of pleasure taken in God's word, statutes, commandments, and testimonies as its specific, named object. The Psalm 119 series is the definitional corpus: sha.a and sha.a.shu.im name an affective inner pleasure (not duty, not intellectual engagement alone) that nourishes the soul (119:77 — divine mercy and delight in the law linked to continued life), sustains through affliction (119:92 — the law as delight is what prevented perishing), shapes decision-making (119:24 — testimonies as counsellors through the pleasure taken in them), and sets the one who delights in the law against the spiritually numb (119:70). Psalm 1:2 frames this as the defining inner disposition of the blessed person. Romans 7:22 explicitly locates the delight in God's law in the "inner being" — making this the most precise NT statement of the law-delight register. The characteristic is specifically distinguished from obligation: delight here is not compliance but desire.

## M04-C-VCG-02 — Relational and Parental Delight: God's Tenderness and the Carefree Delight of Peace

**Context description:** This VCG names the remaining sha.a and sha.a.shu.im verses that evidence a different register from law-delight: the tender, relational, and affective pleasure that characterises God's delight in wisdom and in his people, wisdom's delight in humanity, and the carefree, child-like delight that pictures the coming age of peace. Personified Wisdom was God's daily delight before creation (Pro 8:30); Wisdom's delight extends to the children of man (Pro 8:31); Israel is called God's pleasant planting — the delight is God's treasured affection for his people (Isa 5:7); God's tender attachment to Ephraim persists even through rebuke (Jer 31:20 — sha.a.shu.im as cherishing). The two sha.a verses (Isa 11:8; 66:12) contribute the child's carefree play over a cobra's hole and the infant cradled in nurturing comfort — pictures of fearless, innocent delight in the coming shalom. These are M04-C verses (sha.a primary) but their content is distinct from the law-delight of VCG-01.

# Sub-group M04-D — Promised and Eschatological Joy (24 verses)

## M04-D-VCG-01 — Joy of the Redeemed: Restoration Gladness and Promised Reversal of Sorrow

**Context description:** This VCG names the register of inner gladness that is promised as the direct reversal of sorrow through God's redemptive acts — joy given by God to replace mourning, sorrow, and desolation. The Isaiah restoration passages are the OT anchor: everlasting joy as a crown resting on the heads of the ransomed returning to Zion (Isa 35:10; 51:11), gladness paired with joy in the transformed Zion (Isa 51:3), the oil of gladness poured over mourners (Isa 61:3), gi.lah (Isa 35:2; 65:18) naming the inner exuberance of the land's renewal. Jeremiah contributes God's direct gift of rejoicing to replace sorrow (Jer 31:13) and the return of gladness to the restored city (Jer 33:9; 33:11). The Zec 8:19 transformation of fasts into feasts and Est 8:16-17 (joy accompanying rescue) complete the register. This VCG also includes the personal salvation-joy of Psa 45:7, 51:8, 51:12, 105:43, and 119:111 — where sa.s.von names the inner gladness of being personally redeemed and spiritually restored.

## M04-D-VCG-02 — Joy Removed: Communal Gladness Stilled and Silenced by Judgment

**Context description:** This VCG names the verses where sa.s.von and related terms describe the removal of communal joy by divine judgment — joy as what was and is now absent, its loss marking desolation, covenant consequence, and the silencing of normal life. These are not the eschatological reversal of VCG-01 but its inverse: the prophetic pattern of joy-as-what-is-taken-away. God silences the voice of gladness in Judah's cities (Jer 7:34); joy and mirth are removed before the people's eyes (Jer 16:9); rejoicing paired with mirth is banished by divine judgment (Jer 25:10); gladness dries up from the people as the crops fail (Joe 1:12); Isa 22:13 shows reckless festivity in the shadow of coming death. These verses evidence the M04-D characteristic by negation and loss — they define what gladness is by its devastating removal.

**Rationale:** Jer 7:34 (sa.s.von) is the most unambiguous divine-judgment-silencing of communal gladness — God explicitly names the removal of mirth and joy as the content of judgment, making it definitional for this VCG.

# Sub-group M04-E — Pleasantness and Relational Delight (28 verses)

## M04-E-VCG-01 — Divine Pleasantness: The Beauty and Favour of the LORD

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where pleasantness is attributed to God himself — his beauty, his character, or his covenantal favour — and experienced by persons who seek and encounter him. The psalmist's supreme desire is to gaze on the beauty of the Lord (Psa 27:4 — no.am names God's own being); the pleasantness of the Lord is asked to rest upon the community as an outward-flowing grace (Psa 90:17); God's river of delights lavishly supplies the worshippers who abide in his house (Psa 36:8 — ed.nah); the shepherd's staff named "Favor/Pleasantness" personifies the gracious quality of the covenant relationship (Zec 11:7), and its breaking ends that covenant grace (Zec 11:10). This VCG is distinguished by pleasantness as a quality of God toward his people — received from him, experienced in his presence, characterizing divine favour.

## M04-E-VCG-02 — Relational Pleasantness: The Felt Quality of Persons, Community, and Bonds

**Context description:** This VCG names the register where pleasantness is the quality perceived and experienced in persons, relationships, and community — the felt agreeableness of human bonds. David's eulogy of Saul and Jonathan as "lovely" captures the relational quality that made them beloved in life and lamented in death (2Sa 1:23). Jonathan's companionship was deeply pleasant to David, surpassing even the love of women (2Sa 1:26 — na.em). Brotherly unity is celebrated as pleasant (Psa 133:1 — na.im). The beloved in Song of Songs is addressed as beautiful and pleasant (Song 1:16; 7:6). Pleasant places as the psalmist's inheritance (Psa 16:6) and pleasantness of life years in obedience to God (Job 36:11) fill out the experiential-relational register. The luxury of Saul's provision for his daughters (2Sa 1:24 — ed.nah) and Jerusalem's choicest delights consumed by Nebuchadnezzar (Jer 51:34 — ed.nah) contribute the material/sensory dimension.

# Sub-group M04-F — Wonder at God's Marvellous Works (77 verses)

## M04-F-VCG-01 — Israel's Remembrance and Proclamation of God's Wondrous Works

**Context description:** This VCG names the dominant register of M04-F: the inner disposition of wonder at God's historical, redemptive, and creation wonders — held in memory, proclaimed publicly, and passed to the next generation as an act of faith and worship. The corpus is centred on the Psalms' meditation on divine wonders: Psa 9:1 (whole-hearted recounting), Psa 26:7 (proclaiming wonders as thanksgiving), Psa 78:4 (passing wonders to the next generation — formative memory), Psa 78:11 (forgetting wonders as moral failure), Psa 105:2, 5 (rehearsing wonders in song and memory), the Psa 107 refrain-series (wonder at God's extraordinary care as call to thanksgiving), Psa 111:4; 118:23; 136:4; 145:5 (wonder as the defining quality of God's acts). The Exodus proclamation (Exo 15:11 — pe.le) and the Chronicles calls to tell of God's wondrous works (1Ch 16:9, 12, 24) anchor the OT pattern. The inner characteristic is wonder-as-active-memory and wonder-as-proclamation-impulse — the astonishment that must be told.

## M04-F-VCG-02 — Wonder at the Incomprehensible: Awe Before God's Surpassing Ways

## M04-F-VCG-04 — Wonder Inverted: Awe-Inspiring Judgment, Devastation, and Dark Wonders
