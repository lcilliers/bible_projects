## 5. Term-by-term analytical decisions

### G0701 arestos (1 verse)
- Act 12:3 → M04-H: Herod proceeded because "it pleased the Jews" — their inner volitional approval as political motivation. Marginal but genuine inner-being content (collective approval as an inner state driving action). Assigned to M04-H rather than set aside under §1.1 scope.

### G2169 eucharistia (15 verses) + G2170 eucharistos (1 verse)
- All 16 → M04-C. Every verse carries genuine inner-being content of gratitude as a directed inner disposition. Thanksgiving in the NT corpus is consistently a positive inner orientation toward a benefactor (God), explicitly commanded, constitutionally located (spirit — 1Cor 14:16; heart — Col 3:15), and producing outward expression. The prior BOUNDARY designation reflected the spiritualisation bias (gratitude treated as "not quite joy"); under v2_5 scope it is clearly inner-being content appropriate to M04-C's NT gospel-community register.

### G2284 thambeō (2 verses) + G2295 thauma (2 verses) + G2296 thaumazō (3 verses)
- All 7 → M04-I. These are the wonder/astonishment terms. Mar 1:27 and Mar 10:32 (thambeō) fit M04-I's wonder-at-Jesus register. Rev 17:6 (thauma + thaumazō) and Rev 17:7 fit M04-I VCG-03 (dark astonishment register). Gal 1:6 (thaumazō) fits M04-I's dark-astonishment register (troubled inner reaction at extraordinary failure). 2Cor 11:14 (thauma negated) evidences the wonder-response even through its negation — the verse operates by invoking the wonder-state only to dismiss it.

### G3108 makarismos (3 verses)
- All 3 → M04-O. Blessedness as an inner state of spiritual happiness and favour received from divine declaration (Rom 4:6/9) or experienced and then lost (Gal 4:15). M04-O (Circumstantial Gladness) is the best fit: blessedness as a divinely granted beneficial inner condition, circumstantially received and capable of being absent.

### H2531 che.med (4 verses)
- Isa 32:12 → M04-J: pleasantness of productive agricultural land mourned as lost.
- Eze 23:6 and Eze 23:23 → M04-P: erotic-aesthetic attraction toward the Assyrian/Babylonian forces driving Israel's spiritual adultery — the sub-group's core_description explicitly cites Eze 23:12 as a founding example.
- Amo 5:11 → M04-K: material pleasantness of vineyards anticipated but denied as consequence of injustice.

### H5207 ni.cho.ach (35 verses)
- 32 → M04-K: the pleasing-aroma formula throughout Lev/Num + Gen 8:21 + Eze 20:41. God's receptive inner satisfaction at correct worship is genuine M04 content; M04-K's core_description explicitly includes this term. The negated instance (Lev 2:12 — not to be offered on the altar as a pleasing aroma) and the withdrawn instance (Lev 26:31 — God will not smell) are both assigned to M04-K because they evidence the characteristic through restriction and removal, not through its absence.
- 2 → M04-P: Eze 6:13 and Eze 16:19 — the ni.cho.ach aroma misdirected to idols; the same worship-inner-being act directed toward a corrupt object.

### H2896A tov (190 verses)
The dominant challenge. Six stable patterns emerged from verse-by-verse reading:

**Pattern 1 → M04-H:** The "seems good/best/right to you/him/them" formula (volitional-approval language). ~50 verses, spanning Sarai's authority (Gen 16:6) through Esther's repeated petitions to royal inner approval, through David's inner deliberation ("better for me than" — 1Sa 27:1), through Joab and Gideon's deference to inner judgment, through the Zechariah shepherd's appeal (Zec 11:12). God's own sovereign will is also named through this formula (Deu 30:9 "God's renewed delight in prospering Israel"; various "do what seems good to him/God" formulas). Some morally compromised instances are included (Judg 19:24, Gen 19:8) — §1.1 explicitly includes volitional inner states regardless of moral quality.

**Pattern 2 → M04-L:** God's character declared as good; human moral character appraised as good; comparative moral evaluations ("better than"). ~70 verses, the largest group. These include: all "for he is good, for his steadfast love endures forever" doxological refrains (Psa 100:5, 106:1, 107:1, 118:1/29, 135:3, 136:1 etc.); the Gen 1 creation assessments ("God saw that it was good"); comparative moral verdicts (1Sa 15:28 "better than you"; 2Sa 18:3 "worth ten thousand"); "not good" moral condemnations (Neh 5:9, 1Sa 26:16, Pro 17:26 etc.); and Mic 6:8 as the definitional anchor.

**Pattern 3 → M04-O:** Circumstantial wellbeing states — "it will go well with you/them," "it was better for me then," "the good hand of God upon him," beneficial states contingent on external factors. ~48 verses. Includes Saul's restoration to wellbeing through music (1Sa 16:23), Jonah's desperate comparative assessment of death as "better" (Jon 4:3/8), and the grim Lamentations inversions (Lam 4:9 — sword-death as "better" than famine-death).

**Pattern 4 → M04-M:** "What is good and right in God's sight" moral orientation — the obedience-goodness register (Deu 6:18, 12:28; Psa 38:20 "I follow after good"; Isa 38:3 "I have done what is good in your sight"; Jer 22:16 "then it was well — is not this to know me?"). ~14 verses.

**Pattern 5 → M04-G:** God's nearness/guidance/way as the surpassing good; comparative assessments that conclude God's presence is better than any alternative (Psa 73:28 "it is good to be near God"; Psa 84:10 "a day in your courts is better than a thousand"; Psa 118:8/9 "better to take refuge in the Lord"; Jer 6:16 "the good way" that restores the soul; Psa 143:10 "your good Spirit lead me"). ~12 verses.

**Pattern 6 → M04-B:** Ecclesiastes life-embrace gladness already present in M04-B VCG-04 (Ecc 2:24; 3:12/22; 5:18 — "nothing better than to eat and drink and find enjoyment"); worship goodness (Psa 92:1 "it is good to give thanks"; Psa 147:1 "it is good to sing praises"; Jer 33:11 doxology). Est 5:9 tov-lev (glad of heart) assigned to M04-B for cohesion with the existing sa.me.ach row for the same verse. ~10 verses.

A small number of tov verses were assigned to M04-J (Psa 45:1 heart overflowing with pleasing theme; Psa 133:1 good and pleasant brotherly unity; Rut 2:22 Naomi's inner assessment for Ruth's safety), M04-P (Mal 2:17 calling evil good as corrupt evaluative faculty), and M04-K (Eze 34:18 good pasture as material/sensory resource).

### H8588 ta.a.nug (1 verse)
- Mic 2:9 → M04-K: women driven from their delightful houses — domestic comfort and security as sensory-material inner-experienced good.

3. **Est 5:9 (vc=63224):** The tov-lev (glad of heart) compound in this verse is assigned to M04-B rather than M04-L to maintain cohesion with the existing sa.me.ach row (vc=23769) for the same verse in M04-B VCG-06.

4. **Deu 30:9 (vc=63170):** "God will again take delight in prospering you" uses tov vocabulary but the Pass A meaning confirms "God's renewed delight"; assigned to M04-H VCG-02 (God's relational delight toward persons) on the basis of the delight-in-prospering construction.

5. **Psa 73:28, 84:10, 118:8/9:** Assigned to M04-G rather than M04-L because the primary inner-being content is the soul's orientation toward God's nearness/presence as the supreme good — this is delight-in-God (M04-G's register) rather than evaluative comparison (M04-L's register), even though a comparative form is used.

6. **Sanity check — SET-ASIDE rate:** 0% SET-ASIDE across a 190-verse tov corpus. The §11A.4 sanity check flag (>50% SET-ASIDE triggers researcher review) is not triggered. The 0% rate is the analytically correct outcome under v2_5 §1.1 scope: tov always evidences an inner-being state (God's evaluative faculty, human volitional approval, circumstantial wellbeing, moral character quality). The only path to SET-ASIDE would be a purely external reference with no inner state implied — none of the 190 verses meet this criterion.
