## §1 — Characteristic 2 (Joy)

**Short name:** Joy
**Characteristic_id:** 2
**Definition:**

> The active inner experience of rejoicing — the soul's engaged, participatory delight in what is worth celebrating. Joy is less surging than Exultation and more active than Gladness. It is the inner being rejoicing in something: in the Lord, in community, in shared celebration, in what is promised and coming. The commanded 'rejoice in the Lord always' is Joy — an active inner disposition of engagement, not merely a warm background feeling.

**Constituent sub-groups:** 4
**Total verses in scope:** ~469

## §2 — Sub-groups (capacity organisers — not the analytical unit)

### M04-B — Communal and Festive Rejoicing: Gladness in Worship, Feast, and Celebration

**core_description:** OT dominant communal-festive-worship register. Rejoicing before God at appointed feasts commanded for entire households; temple celebrations; divinely produced and socially expansive gladness. Terms sa.mach (147), sim.chah (89), sa.me.ach (21), ma.s.vo.s (16) are routed here as primary.

**Role under Joy:** The communal-festive qualifier — Joy enacted in community, structured by appointed feast and corporate worship; primary OT rejoicing vocabulary (sa.mach, sim.chah).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 281

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-B-VCG-04` (64 verses) — Relational, personal, and wisdom-life gladness: joy in human bonds, parental delight, domestic life, Ecclesiastes life-embrace, Proverbs moral observations.
- `M04-B-VCG-01` (25 verses) — Festival and feast worship joy: prescribed communal gladness at appointed feasts directed toward God at his sanctuary.
- `M04-B-VCG-02` (22 verses) — Sanctuary, temple, and ark joy: gladness at the sacred presence returning, established, or worship restored.
- `M04-B-VCG-03` (21 verses) — Coronation, national, and civic deliverance joy: corporate gladness at legitimate kingship, military victory, or reversal of oppression.
- `M04-B-VCG-06` (59 verses) — Inverted, absent, corrupt, and judgment-silenced joy: misdirected, hollow, morally condemned, or divinely removed gladness.
- `M04-B-VCG-05` (90 verses) — God-directed psalmist and prophetic gladness: sa.mach/sim.chah rejoicing explicitly toward God, from his saving acts, or at his governance.

### M04-C — NT Joy in Christ and the Spirit: Gladness of the Gospel Community

**core_description:** Distinctively NT register: inner gladness arising from believing in Christ, from the indwelling Spirit, and from the gospel community. Holy Spirit named as agent or source of joy. Rejoicing in the Lord as constant commanded disposition. Core NT terms: chairo (58), chara (57), plus smaller Greek joy terms.

**Role under Joy:** The NT Spirit-and-Christ qualifier — Joy arising from faith in Christ, mediated by the indwelling Spirit, shaped by the gospel community; primary NT rejoicing vocabulary (chairo, chara).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 145

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-C-VCG-01` (27 verses) — Synoptic and incarnation joy: gladness at the messianic arrival, kingdom signs, lost-found parables, resurrection encounters.
- `M04-C-VCG-05` (16 verses) — Suffering-paradox, corrupt, and morally complex joy: commanded joy in persecution, joyful endurance, corrupt gladness of opponents.
- `M04-C-VCG-02` (15 verses) — Johannine joy: indwelling, fullness, permanent gladness — Christ's own joy transferred to disciples, joy made complete.
- `M04-C-VCG-03` (59 verses) — Pauline relational and community joy: gladness in gospel fellowship, pastoral bonds, co-rejoicing, mutual encouragement.
- `M04-C-VCG-04` (13 verses) — Spirit-given, kingdom, and constant joy: joy as Spirit's fruit, kingdom constituent, constant commanded stance, eschatological promise.
- `M04-C-VCG-06` (15 verses) — Pauline and heavenly thanksgiving — eucharistia/eucharistos as Godward gratitude. Thanksgiving as constitutionally located (heart; spirit), commanded, and producing community/devot

### M04-D — Shared Communal Rejoicing: Joy as Solidarity and Co-Celebration

**core_description:** Register where rejoicing is constitutively shared — sought out, invited into, and completed in community. Sunchairo defines the register: the shepherd calling neighbours to co-rejoice, the body members sharing gladness when one is honoured, love co-rejoicing with truth.

**Role under Joy:** The solidarity qualifier — Joy constitutively shared: sought and completed through another's rejoicing (sunchairo).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 8

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-D-VCG-01` (8 verses) — Shared joy: constitutive co-rejoicing — joy sought out, invited, and completed in community. sunchairo + ched.vah Aramaic.

---

### M04-E — Promised and Eschatological Joy: Gladness of Restoration and the Suffering-Paradox *(partial — this sub-group also serves another characteristic; see VCG details to identify which verses belong to the current characteristic)*

**core_description:** Two closely related registers: (1) forward-oriented gladness promised as reversal of sorrow through divine redemption; (2) NT paradox of joy coexisting with and arising from suffering. Both orient joy toward what is not yet fully present. OT primary term: sa.s.von (22). NT bridging term: agalliao (11).

**Role under Joy:** The eschatological qualifier — Joy oriented toward the not-yet; the soul's forward-engaged rejoicing grounded in divine promise of restoration.

**Partial register note:** Eschatological register (sa.s.von) — distinct from M04-E's suffering-paradox register which serves Characteristic 7.

**Verses in this sub-group:** 35

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M04-E-VCG-01` (24 verses) — OT restoration gladness: sa.s.von and gi.lah corpus — gladness promised as direct reversal of sorrow through God's redemptive acts.
- `M04-E-VCG-02` (11 verses) — NT eschatological and suffering-paradox exultation: agalliao — exulting beyond present circumstance, toward future glory.
