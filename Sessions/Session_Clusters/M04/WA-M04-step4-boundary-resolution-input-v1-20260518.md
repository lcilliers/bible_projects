### Sub-group core_descriptions

**M04-A** — Exultation in YHWH: Vertical Rejoicing Directed Toward God

> OT register of inner exultation directed explicitly toward YHWH as the object and ground of joy. The soul exults in YHWH's salvation, the heart rejoices in God's holy name, God's own inner delight is directed toward his people. Core OT vertical-exultation vocabulary: gil_verb, gil, su.s, cha.dah, re.na.nah, te.ev.

**M04-B** — Communal and Festive Rejoicing: Gladness in Worship, Feast, and Celebration

> OT dominant communal-festive-worship register. Rejoicing before God at appointed feasts commanded for entire households; temple celebrations; divinely produced and socially expansive gladness. Terms sa.mach (147), sim.chah (89), sa.me.ach (21), ma.s.vo.s (16) are routed here as primary.

**M04-C** — NT Joy in Christ and the Spirit: Gladness of the Gospel Community

> Distinctively NT register: inner gladness arising from believing in Christ, from the indwelling Spirit, and from the gospel community. Holy Spirit named as agent or source of joy. Rejoicing in the Lord as constant commanded disposition. Core NT terms: chairo (58), chara (57), plus smaller Greek joy terms.

**M04-D** — Shared Communal Rejoicing: Joy as Solidarity and Co-Celebration

> Register where rejoicing is constitutively shared — sought out, invited into, and completed in community. Sunchairo defines the register: the shepherd calling neighbours to co-rejoice, the body members sharing gladness when one is honoured, love co-rejoicing with truth.

**M04-E** — Promised and Eschatological Joy: Gladness of Restoration and the Suffering-Paradox

> Two closely related registers: (1) forward-oriented gladness promised as reversal of sorrow through divine redemption; (2) NT paradox of joy coexisting with and arising from suffering. Both orient joy toward what is not yet fully present. OT primary term: sa.s.von (22). NT bridging term: agalliao (11).

**M04-F** — Cheerfulness Under Adversity: Fragile Inner Comfort Amid Suffering

> Small but distinct register of cheerfulness as a desired or attempted inner state in the face of extreme adversity — fragile, barely attainable, or conspicuously absent. Distinct from M04-E's gospel-grounded suffering-joy by vulnerability and tenuousness rather than hope-confidence.

**M04-G** — Delight in God's Word, Law, and Wisdom: Pleasure as Inner Orientation Toward the Divine

> Affective inner pleasure taken specifically in God's word, law, testimonies, statutes, wisdom, and in YHWH himself as its direct object. Psalm 119 series is the anchor (sha.a.shu.im, sha.a). Distinguished from M04-H (volitional-will) by affective-pleasurable character.

**M04-H** — Volitional Delight: Inner Pleasure as Sovereign Will and Directed Desire

> Register in which cha.phets, che.phets, and eudokeo function primarily as markers of inner volitional disposition: willing-pleasure, sovereign purpose, directed inner resolve. Spans divine sovereignty (God's delight as his will governing history), divine pleasure in persons, human volitional willing, and negated-pleasure marking rejection.

**M04-I** — Wonder at God's Marvellous Works: Awe Before the Extraordinary

> Inner response of awe, marvel, and astonishment directed at God's extraordinary deeds, creative works, and the incomprehensibility of his ways. The contemplative and perceptual character distinguishes this from gladness. Includes wonder-as-proclamation, wonder-as-incomprehensibility, and wonder-as-dark-astonishment.

**M04-J** — Pleasantness and Relational Delight: The Agreeableness of Persons, Bonds, and Creation

> Experiential register of pleasantness as a quality of what is agreeable, beautiful, and nourishing to the inner person. Receptive rather than exultant character. Spans divine beauty (no.am — the Lord's own being), relational warmth (na.em — Jonathan's companionship in the soul), communal harmony (na.im — brotherly unity), and gracious speech (no.am — honey to soul and body).

**M04-K** — Material and Sensory Pleasure: Luxury, Sensory Enjoyment, and Aromatic Sacrifice

> Pleasantness as a sensory or material register. Includes the inner satisfaction of refined comfort and dainty living (a.nog, ta.a.nug — pampered ease in Deu 28, Isa 47, Ecc 2, Pro 19, Song 7); the soothing-aroma metonym for divine reception of correct worship (ni.cho.ach — Lev 1, 23; Num 15, 28); the wicked man's denied enjoyment (a.las — Job 20); the seductress's carnal delight (a.las — Pro 7); the disciples' amazement at Jesus' wealth-and-kingdom teaching (thambeō — Mar 10:24); and tov as material or sensory goodness (Psa 34 'taste and see'; Pro 22 favor as silver-gold; Jer 22 wellbeing as flourishing). Inner-being-in-bodily-experience: pleasantness registered through the senses or material conditions, ranging from righteous worship-reception to illicit luxury to siege-curse refinement under judgment.

**M04-L** — Evaluative Goodness: Moral and Relational Appraisal of What is Surpassing

> Tov used as the inner-being faculty of moral or relational evaluation — the soul's assessment that something is 'good,' 'better than,' or 'surpassing.' Includes Ruth's love-for-Naomi appraised as surpassing seven sons (Rut 4:15); God's chesed evaluated as surpassing life itself (Psa 63:3); the youths' 'good appearance' (Dan 1:4) as an external-quality appraisal; the people judging Egypt-return as better than Canaan (Num 14:3); 'good and bad' from God's mouth (Lam 3:38); and similar evaluative-comparative judgments where the soul names what is surpassing in worth. Inner-being-as-evaluation: the faculty by which a person appraises moral or relational worth.

**M04-M** — Pleasing as Obedience: Inner Orientation Toward What is Acceptable to God

> What is pleasing, fitting, or acceptable in God's sight — framed as the obedient soul's directed orientation. arestos verses (Joh 8:29 — Jesus doing what pleases the Father continually; Act 6:2 procedural acceptable; 1Jo 3:22 pleasing God through obedience as ground of answered prayer); tov verses where inner obedience-character is named as the 'good' that surpasses sacrifice (1Sa 15:22) and God's 'good things' as the obedience-consequence promises (Jos 23:15); ni.cho.ach as the soothing aroma of correct sacrificial obedience (Lev 1:17 torn bird; Num 15:13 every native's food offering). Inner-being-as-volitional-orientation toward the divine standard.

**M04-N** — Horizontal Relational Delight: Pleasantness Between Persons (Parental, Neighbour, Captivation)

> Inner-being pleasantness in the horizontal register — between persons rather than vertically Godward. Parental delight in children (ta.a.nug — Mic 1:16 'children of your delight'); the value of a nearby neighbour (tov — Pro 27:10 closer than distant brother) and its negation in the violent man (Pro 16:29); the world's collective marvelling-captivation at the beast's apparent resurrection (thaumazō — Rev 13:3, 17:8) as horizontal-attention drawn in awe. Inner-being-in-relationship: pleasantness, captivation, or wonder directed at persons, ranging from tender attachment to corrupted captivation.

**M04-O** — Circumstantial Gladness: Good News, Beneficial States, and Refreshing Circumstance

> Tov used to name circumstantial or situational pleasantness — the goodness of a circumstance, message, or report registered by the inner being. 'Good man with good news' (2Sa 18:27); the inner disposition of quiet waiting for salvation as 'good for the soul' (Lam 3:26); 'good news of happiness' of salvation and peace (Isa 52:7); the simple meal with love better than the feast with hatred (Pro 15:17); good news refreshing the bones (Pro 15:30). Inner-being-in-circumstance: the soul's pleasant response to or recognition of beneficial circumstance.

**M04-P** — Corrupt and Illicit Delight: Predatory, Lustful, and Misdirected Pleasure

> Inner-being delight in its corrupt or illicit modes — the morally negative face of pleasure. Predatory exultation devouring the poor in secret (a.li.tsut — Hab 3:14); Israel's lust for desirable Assyrian young men (che.med — Eze 23:12); misdirected sacrificial aroma in illicit worship on high places (ni.cho.ach — Eze 20:28); the wicked man choosing what is 'not good' as his path (tov as negation — Psa 36:4). Inner-being-as-disordered-delight: pleasure directed toward objects, persons, or practices contrary to right order.
