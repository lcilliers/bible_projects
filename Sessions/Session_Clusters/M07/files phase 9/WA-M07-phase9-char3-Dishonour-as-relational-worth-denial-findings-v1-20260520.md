## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

Joh 8:49 (vc=35000) — "I honor my Father, and you dishonor me" — places dishonour as a relational act directed at Jesus personally, revealing a God whose worth is real and whose engagement with persons is worthy of honour. Rom 9:21 (vc=34960) — the potter's sovereign right to assign vessels for honourable or dishonorable use — reveals God as the primary worth-assigning agent; human dishonour-assignments are derivative and fallible.

---

**T0.1.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Within the CHAR-3 corpus, God is the ultimate arbiter whose dishonouring of a vessel or person (Rom 9:21) is the authoritative assignment, contrasted with human worth-denial which may be arbitrary or unjust. God does not dishonour unjustly; human dishonour-extension frequently does.

The judge who "neither fears God nor respects man" (Luk 18:2, vc=34990) is failing to exercise a faculty that belongs to proper human functioning: the capacity to perceive and honour the worth of others.

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 as worth-denial is a response to the fallen condition — the failure of the worth-recognition capacity that belongs to the image. The failure takes two forms: active willful dishonour (VCG-01) and structural assigned dishonour (VCG-02). The created design included the worth-recognition capacity; its corruption produces CHAR-3.

The sharing is asymmetric: God honours perfectly; humans honour derivatively and can fail to honour (producing CHAR-3).

The absence of worth-recognition (the judge who neither fears God nor respects man) reveals the image's relational faculty as non-functional.

## T1 — Defining the Characteristic

**T1.1.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — "Dishonour as relational worth-denial." The name is precise: "dishonour" names the specific register (stripping of honour/worth); "as relational" specifies the operational field (it always operates between persons or between person and God); "worth-denial" names the mechanism (what is being denied is the worth the other person actually has). The name distinguishes CHAR-3 from CHAR-1 (felt inner shame) and CHAR-2 (enforced downward movement of standing): dishonour is specifically a relational act of worth-misassignment.

---

**T1.1.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Atimazō (G0818) — to dishonour, treat as worthless; root: atimos — without honour, without worth. Atimia (G0819) — dishonour as a state of assigned worth-denial. Entrepō (G1788) — "to have regard for" in its absence: the judge who "does not respect man" lacks the inner movement of regard that would recognise worth. Paradeigmatizō (G3856) — to expose publicly, to hold up to contempt. Together these terms reveal CHAR-3 as constituted by the failure or refusal of the worth-recognition act: one sees another person and assigns them less worth than they have.

---

**T1.1.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 is relational by definition — it always operates in the space between persons. Directionally, it moves from the dishonour-er toward the dishonoured. The constitutional implication is that CHAR-3 engages the moral-evaluative and relational faculties: it is produced by a failure of moral perception and has its primary effect in the relational standing of the dishonoured.

---

**T1.2.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 is primarily an act in VCG-01 — the willful act of dishonoring another: boasting in the law while breaking it (Rom 2:23, vc=35002); showing favoritism to the rich while dishonoring the poor (Jam 2:6); holding Christ up to contempt (Heb 6:6). In VCG-02, CHAR-3 is a condition — assigned or received standing (the potter's assignment, Rom 9:21; nature's teaching about disgrace, 1Cor 11:14). Both act and condition are present in the evidence.

---

**T1.2.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Two constituent elements: (1) Active worth-denial (VCG-01): the willful act of refusing to acknowledge or withdrawing acknowledgment of another's worth. (2) Assigned or structural dishonour (VCG-02): dishonour as an objective state assigned by sovereign authority without being the product of a malicious human will.

---

**T1.2.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Dishonour as relational worth-denial is the act or condition of treating a person as having less worth than they actually possess — either through the willful refusal to acknowledge worth (VCG-01) or through the objective assignment of diminished standing (VCG-02). It operates relationally, always with a dishonour-er and a dishonoured. Primary vocabulary: atimazō, atimia, entrepō (in its absence register), paradeigmatizō, kataischunō (in the head-covering symbolic-disrespect register).

---

**T1.3.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The structural opposite of CHAR-3 is honour — the active acknowledgment of another's worth. The evidence pairs dishonour and honour explicitly: "I honor my Father, and you dishonor me" (Joh 8:49); "some for honorable use, some for dishonorable" (Rom 9:21; 2Ti 2:20).

---

**T1.3.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 excludes the recognition of the other's worth. In VCG-01, what is excluded is the inner movement of regard (entrepō) — the judge who lacks inner deference toward God and persons has excluded the worth-recognition act from his inner repertoire.

---

**T1.3.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 is not CHAR-2 (enforced downward movement of standing): dishonour as worth-denial is a relational judgment that can be made without the external enforcement mechanism of CHAR-2. CHAR-3 is not CHAR-1 (the felt inner shame-experience of the one dishonoured): dishonour-as-worth-denial is the act directed at the person, not the inner experience of the person receiving it. CHAR-3 is not CHAR-5 (contempt producing shame): contempt is the attitudinal-mechanism of dismissal; dishonour-as-worth-denial names the relational verdict.

---

**T1.4.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Two operational modes: (1) Active willful dishonour (VCG-01): the inner disposition of refused-acknowledgment enacted toward specific persons — Jesus, the poor man, the Lawgiver, apostolic authority-bearers. (2) Structural or assigned dishonour (VCG-02): dishonour as an objective condition assigned by sovereign authority, created order, or received social treatment.

---

**T1.4.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 operates differently depending on whether the worth-denial is directed at God (Joh 8:49; Rom 2:23), at humans (Jam 2:6; Luk 18:2/4), or at divinely ordered authority structures (1Cor 11:4-5, vc=35009/35010). The God-directed mode carries the highest moral weight; the human-directed mode carries strong relational consequence; the authority-structure mode is mediated through symbolic bodily acts.

---

**T1.4.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 has a communicative mode through symbolic bodily acts (1Cor 11:4-5 — the covered and uncovered heads carrying messages of disrespect toward divinely ordered authority) and through explicit verbal declaration (Joh 8:49 — "you dishonor me" — the accusers' verbal engagement being itself the dishonouring act).

---

**T1.5.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The most immediate inner response to receiving CHAR-3 depends on the worth-denial mode. In the active-willful mode (VCG-01), the focus in most verses is on the dishonorer's inner disposition rather than the dishonoured's immediate response. Joh 8:49 — Jesus responding to dishonour by redirecting to the honoring of his Father — shows the possibility of a response that does not absorb the worth-denial but deflects it. In the structural mode (VCG-02), Paul endures dishonour "through honor and dishonor" (2Cor 6:8, vc=34963) without his inner integrity being altered.

---

**T1.5.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The evidence varies. Some verses focus on the dishonorer (Luk 18:2/4 — the judge's character trait); others on the dishonoured person's response (2Cor 6:8 — Paul's sustained integrity; Joh 8:49 — Jesus's redirection). No single consistent immediate response is described across the 15-verse corpus.

---

**T1.5.3**
**[CHAR-3]** S — The evidence is substantially silent on the immediate inner-being response of the one who receives dishonour. The corpus is focused on the dishonorer's inner disposition. Silent on this specific area.

---

**T1.6.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — In the active-dishonour mode (VCG-01), the sustained effect on the dishonorer is the deepening of the moral failure that dishonour represents: Rom 2:23 — boasting in the law while breaking it — is a self-contradicting inner stance that, sustained, produces the very judgment the person sought to escape. In the structural mode (VCG-02), the sustained effect on the dishonoured is the identity-condition of diminished standing — 2Ti 2:20's vessel for dishonorable use.

---

**T1.6.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — For the dishonorer: sustained CHAR-3 toward God or persons establishes moral self-contradiction (Rom 2:23), the erosion of the worth-recognition capacity (Luk 18:2/4 — the judge whose lack of regard is a recognized character trait), and exposure to the same dishonour one inflicts (Heb 6:6). For the dishonoured: sustained received dishonour either establishes identity-in-diminished-standing (VCG-02) or, in Paul's case, demonstrates that inner integrity can be sustained despite external dishonour (2Cor 6:8).

---

**T1.6.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The sustained effect for the dishonorer differs from the immediate: the immediate is the specific act of worth-denial; the sustained is the character formation in the direction of the failed worth-recognition capacity.

---

**T1.7.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The inner condition enabling CHAR-3's avoidance is the inner faculty of regard (entrepō): "fearing God and respecting man" (Luk 18:2) — the worth-recognition capacity operating properly. For the dishonoured, grounded knowledge of one's standing before God enables receiving external dishonour without inner displacement (2Cor 6:8 — Paul's sustained integrity).

---

**T1.7.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The inner condition that generates CHAR-3 is the inner disposition of self-sufficiency or self-deception: the self-contradicting inner stance of Rom 2:23 — boasting in the law while breaking it — shows how self-deception blocks genuine moral perception of others' worth.

---

**T1.7.3**
**[CHAR-3]** S — The evidence does not extensively describe the inner state of the person who encounters worth-denial directed at them but does not receive it as a blow to their standing. Silent on this specific prompt.

**T1.8.1**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The primary dimension is relational — CHAR-3 is constituted by the relation between persons and specifically by the misassignment of worth within that relation. It is not primarily affective, cognitive, or volitional — though it engages all of these — but relational: it is what one person does to another in the space between them.

---

**T1.8.2**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Joh 8:49 — "you dishonor me" — is relational: the dishonour is directed at Jesus personally in a relational exchange. Jam 2:6 — "you have dishonored the poor man" — is relational: the poor man's worth is being violated in the relational field of the community. The entrepō evidence (Luk 18:2/4) is relational: the judge's absent-regard fails the relational obligation toward God and persons.

---

**T1.8.3**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Secondary dimensions: (1) Moral-evaluative — the dishonoring of persons presupposes and corrupts a moral-evaluative faculty. (2) Volitional — the active-dishonour mode (VCG-01) is willful — it requires a volitional act of refused-acknowledgment. These secondary dimensions accompany the relational primary without competing with it.

## T2 — Constitutional Location

The Luk 18:2/4 judge: inner disposition (no fear of God, no respect for persons) -> outer conduct (unjust judicial decisions) -> relational effect (the widow's worth is denied in her legal case). The heart that boasts in the law while the will breaks it is a divided heart, and from this inner division the dishonoring of God flows. (2) From sovereign assignment (VCG-02): the potter's right, created order, received social treatment — external sources that assign dishonour-standings without the agency of either party being the primary origin.

**[CHAR-3]** E — The cognitive worth-assessment process is engaged and distorted in CHAR-3: the dishonorer makes a wrong cognitive judgment about worth. The structural-dishonour mode (VCG-02) is more cognitively neutral — it is the result of sovereign assignment rather than a cognitive failure.

The judge who "neither fears God nor respects man" (Luk 18:2) has lost the relational-capacity component of appropriate regard for the worth of others. ## T4 — Interfaces

**T4.1.1 through T4.1.4 — God to human**
**[CHAR-3]** E — God extends CHAR-3 in the structural-assignment mode (VCG-02): the potter's sovereign assignment of vessels for dishonorable use (Rom 9:21) is God's prerogative in the creation and governance of persons. The basis is God's authority over his own creation; the content is the objective assignment of standing according to God's sovereign purpose.

---

**T4.2.1 through T4.2.4 — Human to God**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Extensively evidenced in VCG-01. Joh 8:49 — "you dishonor me" (Jesus = God incarnate); Rom 2:23 — "you dishonor God by breaking the law"; Heb 6:6 — apostates "hold him up to contempt." The human-to-God direction of CHAR-3 is the active worth-denial of God's incarnate person and his moral authority. The inner posture required to avoid CHAR-3 toward God is described in its absence in Luk 18:2 — fearing God is the inner posture that prevents CHAR-3 toward God.

---

**T4.3.1 through T4.3.4 — Human giving**
**[CHAR-3]** E — VCG-01 is primarily about humans extending dishonour toward other humans or toward God. The unjust judge (Luk 18:2/4) extends worth-denial to the widow. The rich receive honor while the poor man is dishonored (Jam 2:6). The head-covering symbolic acts extend disrespect toward divinely ordered authority structures.

---

**T4.4.1 through T4.4.4 — Human receiving**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Paul receives dishonour (2Cor 6:8, vc=34963) without altering inner integrity — the key condition for receiving structural dishonour without inner displacement is the grounded knowledge of one's own standing before God. The poor man receives dishonour (Jam 2:6) — the evidence does not describe his inner response but implies that his worth before God is real regardless of the community's misassignment.

---

**T4.5.1 through T4.5.4 — Relational boundaries**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The head-covering material (1Cor 11:4-5) operates within the covenant community. Jam 2:6's dishonoring of the poor operates within the community of believers. The dishonoring of Jesus (Joh 8:49) crosses the covenant community's internal boundary. CHAR-3 appears to operate most damagingly within existing relational bonds where worth-recognition is expected and violated.

---

**T4.6.1 through T4.6.4 — Spiritual beings interface**
**[CHAR-3]** S — The 15-verse CHAR-3 corpus does not evidence worth-denial operating in relation to spiritual beings. Silent.

## T5 — Transformation

**T5.1.1 through T5.1.3 — Transformation**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 in its active mode produces moral self-contradiction in the dishonorer (Rom 2:23). Heb 6:6 shows apostatic dishonour of Christ as irreversibly damaging — "it is impossible to restore them again to repentance." In the structural mode (VCG-02), 2Ti 2:20 implies that the condition of dishonorable-vessel is tied to moral condition, implying reversibility through moral transformation (cleansing, 2Ti 2:21 context).

---

**T5.2.1 through T5.2.3 — Sequence**
**[CHAR-3]** E — For the dishonorer: defective inner worth-assessment -> willful act of worth-denial -> inner self-contradiction or moral hardening. For the dishonoured (in VCG-02 structural mode): sovereign or natural assignment of diminished standing -> received condition of dishonour -> sustained lived in that condition or transformation through moral change.

---

**T5.3.1 through T5.3.3 — Mechanism**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The mechanisms of CHAR-3's production: (1) Willful inner refusal (VCG-01): the decision not to acknowledge worth, motivated by self-righteousness, contempt, or self-deception. (2) Sovereign assignment (VCG-02): God's prerogative to assign standings according to his purpose and the moral condition of the person.

---

**T5.4.1 through T5.4.3 — Suffering and affliction**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The dishonoured person suffers CHAR-3 as a form of relational injustice: the poor man dishonored (Jam 2:6) suffers the relational injustice of worth-misassignment. Paul receives dishonour "through slander and praise" (2Cor 6:8) — an apostolic suffering condition. The widow who cannot get justice (Luk 18:2/4) suffers the consequence of the judge's CHAR-3 toward her.

---

**T5.5.1 through T5.5.3 — Formation and sanctification**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The negative: CHAR-3's active form represents a corruption of the worth-recognition capacity that formation is meant to heal. The positive: learning to see and honour the worth of others — especially the poor, the weak, those the world despises — is part of the formation of the image-bearer toward the likeness of God. Jam 2:6's condemnation of dishonoring the poor is precisely a formation instruction: the community must learn to see worth where the world sees none.

---

**T5.6.1 through T5.6.3 — Eschatological trajectory**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The structural-dishonour mode (VCG-02) points toward its own eschatological resolution: 2Ti 2:20's vessel for dishonorable use is implicitly oriented toward the possibility of cleansing (2:21 context). Rom 9:21's potter-and-clay raises the deepest eschatological question: sovereign assignment of dishonour-standings as a condition of creaturely existence, and whether eschatological transformation re-assigns those standings.

---

**T5.7.1 through T5.7.3 — Deposit consequence**
**[CHAR-3]** E — For the dishonorer: the sustained practice of worth-denial toward God or persons establishes an entrenched inner disposition (the Luk 18:2/4 judge's recognized character trait) and produces the self-undermining inner contradiction (Rom 2:23). The deposit in the dishonorer's inner person is the hardened incapacity for genuine worth-recognition. For the dishonoured in the structural mode: the assigned standing of dishonour may become an identity-condition (2Ti 2:20).

## T6 — Relationships with Other Characteristics

**T6.1.1 through T6.1.3 — Co-occurrence**
**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 co-occurs most significantly with CHAR-5 (shame produced by contempt): the contempt-mechanism (CHAR-5) is frequently the source of the worth-denial (CHAR-3). CHAR-3 also co-occurs with CHAR-1 (felt shame) as the relational cause of CHAR-1's felt experience in the dishonoured person.

---

**T6.2.1 through T6.2.3 — Sequential relationships**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Contempt/self-righteousness consistently precedes CHAR-3 in VCG-01 (the judge's absent-regard; Luk 18:9's self-righteousness producing contempt toward others). CHAR-3 (worth-denial) consistently precedes the felt shame of the dishonoured (CHAR-1 in its unjust-wound register). The sequence: inner disposition of self-sufficiency -> CHAR-3 (worth-denial) -> CHAR-1 (felt shame in the dishonoured).

## T7 — Lexical, Literary, and Scientific Analysis

**T7.1.1 through T7.1.10 — Vocabulary**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Primary terms: Atimazō (G0818): to dishonour, treat as without honour — active verb, the dishonoring act. Atimia (G0819): dishonour as a state — the condition of assigned or received diminished worth. Entrepō (G1788) in its absence register: the failure of the regard-faculty. Paradeigmatizō (G3856): to expose publicly, hold up to contempt. Roots: a-timē — without honour, without worth — the alpha-privative of honour. Structural opposite: timaō (to honour, to assign worth) and timē (honour as assigned worth-recognition). The evidence pairs these explicitly in Joh 8:49. The CHAR-3 corpus is entirely NT — the OT vocabulary for relational worth-denial (qa.lah, H7034; entrepō in its absence register) is thinner. The NT's christological context gives CHAR-3 its sharpest definition: dishonour as what is directed at Jesus personally and at God through human hypocrisy. Person-type: the "unjust judge" (Luk 18:2) is the closest person-type but is not a technical term. Supplication: the widow's persistent coming to the judge (Luk 18:3) is a supplication for the reversal of CHAR-3 — the only supplication-context in the corpus.

**T7.2.1 through T7.2.6 — Literary analysis**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Primary literary forms: parable (Luk 18:2-4 — the unjust judge); epistle (Rom 2:23; 2Cor 6:8; Jam 2:6; 1Cor 11:4-5); narrative (Joh 8:49). The parable form (Luk 18) uses the character of the dishonoring judge as a contrast-figure — his absent-regard makes the point precisely by its absence. Primary anchor: Joh 8:49 (vc=35000) — "I honor my Father, and you dishonor me" — the most compressed, relational, and personally directed expression of CHAR-3 in the corpus, revealing worth-denial as a relational act directed at a specific person in a specific exchange. This verse uniquely reveals worth-denial as a personal relational act within a face-to-face exchange, where the standard (honouring the Father) and the violation (dishonoring the Son) are simultaneously named.

**T7.3.1 through T7.3.4 — Human science framework**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Social psychology's research on in-group/out-group worth-assignment, status dynamics, and stigma is directly relevant to CHAR-3. The sociology of recognition (Hegel-derived, Honneth) is even more directly relevant: CHAR-3 is precisely the failure of recognition — the refusal to acknowledge another's worth within the relational field. The divergence: the sociology of recognition frames worth-recognition as socially constructed; the verse evidence grounds it in divine assignment (the poor man has worth before God regardless of the community's judgment). This divergence reveals CHAR-3's theological anchor: worth is not ultimately socially assigned but divinely given, and CHAR-3 violates that divine assignment.
