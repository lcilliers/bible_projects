## T1 — Defining the Characteristic

**T1.1.1**
**[CHAR-6]** E — "Innocence as structural counter to shame." The name has three components: "innocence" names the inner moral state (blameless conscience, clean will, hands free of deliberate wrongdoing); "as structural counter" identifies the function (not merely adjacent to shame but constitutively opposed to it — the inner state that makes guilt-shame structurally impossible or practically protects against it); "to shame" names the characteristic it counters (specifically guilt-shame, the M07-C register of shame before God). The name positions CHAR-6 as the positive inner-moral condition that CHAR-1's guilt-shame register presupposes the absence of.

---

**T1.1.2**
**[CHAR-6]** E — Niq.qa.von (H5356A): innocence, cleanness — from naqah (to be clean, to be free from guilt). The root sense is freedom from moral blemish or guilt-stain — the state of being morally uncontaminated by deliberate wrongdoing. Qe.ha.von (H5356B): in this context functions as a near-synonym or parallel term to niq.qa.von — the bluntness/cleanness of hands, pointing to the same moral-freedom condition. Both terms together locate CHAR-6 in the hands-and-heart moral-integrity domain: the inner will (heart) and the outward action (hands) both free from guilt-stain.

---

**T1.1.3**
**[CHAR-6]** E — CHAR-6 is constitutive: it names an inner state rather than a direction. However, it carries a directional implication as a counter: it moves against guilt-shame, protecting the person from the inner collapse that guilt-shame would produce. Constitutional implication: CHAR-6 is located primarily in the conscience (the moral-recognitive faculty that registers guilt or its absence) and in the will (the faculty whose freedom from deliberate wrongdoing constitutes the innocence).

---

**T1.2.1**
**[CHAR-6]** E — CHAR-6 is primarily a condition or quality — the inner moral state of being innocent, of having a clean conscience and blameless hands. It is not an act (though it is maintained through moral choices — Psa 26:6's "I wash my hands in innocence" suggests active maintenance). It is not a stable disposition in the sense of a permanent trait — Psa 73:13 shows it can be maintained but seems futile; Hos 8:5 shows it can be lost. CHAR-6 is a moral-condition that requires active maintenance and can be damaged or lost.

---

**T1.2.2**
**[CHAR-6]** E — Two constituent elements corresponding to the two VCGs: (1) Innocence as active inner defence (VCG-01): the functioning innocence — clean conscience, blameless will, hands free of deliberate wrongdoing — that enables approach to God without guilt-shame (Gen 20:5; Psa 26:6). (2) Innocence questioned and incapacity for innocence (VCG-02): the innocence whose protective function is doubted or whose loss is named — the psalmist's maintained innocence that seems futile (Psa 73:13), and Samaria's incapacity for innocence under sustained idolatry (Hos 8:5).

---

**T1.2.3**
**[CHAR-6]** E — Innocence as structural counter to shame is the inner moral state of a blameless conscience and clean-handed will — the condition in which the person is free from the deliberate moral failure that would produce guilt-shame before God or community. CHAR-6 is the positive moral condition that protects against M07-C's guilt-shame register: when CHAR-6 is present, the person can approach God without the barrier of guilt-shame; when CHAR-6 is absent or its capacity is lost, the guilt-shame condition of M07-C is inevitable. Primary vocabulary: niq.qa.von (H5356A) and qe.ha.von (H5356B), shared across the 4 verse-records (8 verse_context rows because of the two-term structure). Cross-register note: M12 (purity/holiness) is the terms' primary register; they are retained in M07 because every verse evidences the innocence-shame polarity directly.

---

**T1.3.1**
**[CHAR-6]** E — The structural opposite of CHAR-6 is guilt — the inner moral condition that produces guilt-shame and bars approach to God. The evidence names this opposite indirectly: Hos 8:5 — incapacity for innocence — names the state that is CHAR-6's absence, which is sustained in idolatry and sin. Psa 73:13's maintained innocence that seems futile would, if abandoned, produce the guilt-condition that would make guilt-shame appropriate.

---

**T1.3.2**
**[CHAR-6]** E — CHAR-6 explicitly excludes the deliberate moral failure that produces guilt. The "integrity of my heart and the innocence of my hands" (Gen 20:5) excludes intentional sin of the heart and willful wrongdoing of the hands. The "I wash my hands in innocence" (Psa 26:6) excludes participation in the conduct of the wicked — Psa 26:4-5 (contextually) shows that innocence is maintained by refusing association with deceitful, hypocritical, and evil persons.

---

**T1.3.3**
**[CHAR-6]** E — CHAR-6 is not CHAR-4 (shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment): CHAR-4 is the conscience's evaluative assessment of what is shameful in conduct or objects; CHAR-6 is the positive moral condition that protects the person against guilt-shame. CHAR-6 is not CHAR-1 (the felt experience of shame): CHAR-6 is the inner condition that, when present, prevents the felt shame-experience that CHAR-1 describes. CHAR-6 is related to but not identical with M12 (purity/holiness): M12's primary register is cultic and ritual purity; CHAR-6's specific function is the inner moral purity that protects against guilt-shame in the M07 context.

(2) Questioned or lost capacity (VCG-02): innocence whose protective function seems to fail (Psa 73:13 — maintained innocence appearing futile), or whose capacity is entirely lost through sustained idolatry (Hos 8:5 — incapacity for innocence).
