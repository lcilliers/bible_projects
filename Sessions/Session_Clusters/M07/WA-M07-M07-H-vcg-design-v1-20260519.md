## VCG M07-H-VCG-01 — Innocence as active inner defence: clean conscience before God and persons

**Description:** Verses where innocence — the integrity of will and conscience, the cleanness of hands reflecting freedom from deliberate wrongdoing — functions as an active inner claim or defence. Abimelech appeals to his innocence of heart and cleanness of hands as the explicit basis of his defence before God (Gen 20:5); the psalmist claims a clean conscience as the condition that allows him to approach God's altar in worship (Psa 26:6). In both verses, innocence is a present inner possession that grants standing — it protects the person from the shame of guilt-based accusation and enables approach to the holy. The M07 angle: innocence as the structural inner state that is the opposite of guilt-shame; the M12 cross-register flag: the purity content of these terms (clean hands, washed conscience) is the primary register that belongs to M12.

**Anchor:** vc=22817 — Gen 20:5 — niq.qa.von — "Innocence is located explicitly in the hands and the heart together, indicating a clean inner will and blameless outward action—Abimelech claims his conscience and conduct are both untainted." Most directly evidences innocence as an active inner claim before God — the conscience and conduct together forming the basis of the defence.

**Members (4 verses):**
- vc=22817 — Gen 20:5 — niq.qa.von — innocence of heart and hands; active defence before God (ANCHOR)
- vc=22821 — Gen 20:5 — qe.ha.von — cleanness of hands paired with heart-integrity; inner state free of wrongdoing
- vc=22819 — Psa 26:6 — niq.qa.von — clean conscience as basis for approaching God's altar
- vc=22823 — Psa 26:6 — qe.ha.von — inner moral integrity claimed at God's altar; overlaps with niq.qa.von

## VCG M07-H-VCG-02 — Innocence questioned and incapacity for innocence

**Description:** Verses where the innocence-shame polarity is evidenced through the questioning of innocence's protective function or through the incapacity of a people to attain it. The psalmist maintained inner purity of heart and cleanness of hands, but this innocence brought no visible protection or reward — it seemed futile (Psa 73:13). The people are incapable of attaining the inner moral state of innocence because persistent idolatry has disabled the faculty (Hos 8:5): "How long will they be incapable of innocence?" Both verses evidence the M07 characteristic from the negative side: the structural counter to shame is missing or seems to fail. The M12 cross-register flag: the incapacity for moral purity described in Hos 8:5 carries the primary purity-and-consecration content that belongs to M12; M07 holds the shame-sustaining dimension of that incapacity.

**Anchor:** vc=22818 — Psa 73:13 — niq.qa.von — "The psalmist kept his heart clean and his hands in innocence — but this inner moral purity now seems futile, suggesting that innocence as an inner disposition brought no visible reward or protection." Most directly evidences the innocence-shame polarity under pressure: innocence maintained but apparently non-protective, raising the question of its structural value as shame's counter.

**Members (4 verses):**
- vc=22818 — Psa 73:13 — niq.qa.von — maintained inner purity seeming futile; innocence apparently without protection (ANCHOR)
- vc=22822 — Psa 73:13 — qe.ha.von — maintained inner purity doubted; both terms evidencing the same crisis
- vc=22820 — Hos 8:5 — niq.qa.von — incapacity for innocence; God asking how long they are unable to attain purity
- vc=22824 — Hos 8:5 — qe.ha.von — dulled moral sensitivity; incapacity for innocence sustaining shamed condition
