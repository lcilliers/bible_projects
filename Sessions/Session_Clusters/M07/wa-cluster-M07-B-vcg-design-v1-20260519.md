## Reading observations

Reading across all 110 verses, six registers emerge:

1. **Shame on enemies/opponents invoked as just reversal** — the psalmist calling shame on those who plot against him; shame as the fitting counter-movement to hostile intent; God's vindicating act exposing enemies
2. **Shame from idolatry and misplaced religious trust** — shame on idol-makers, idol-worshippers, those who trusted in false gods; exposure of inner bankruptcy when idols fail
3. **Shame as natural fruit of moral failure** — folly inheriting disgrace, the adulterer's enduring dishonour, negligence producing family shame, wickedness generating contempt as natural companions
4. **Absence of shame / seared conscience** — the inability to blush; the wicked feeling no shame after abomination; hardened conscience dismissing moral accountability
5. **Shame as corrective produced by irreproachable conduct** — blameless conduct/speech causing opponents to be put to shame; the slanderer whose accusation is exposed as false; the corrective deployment of shame to restore community order
6. **Clothing/garment imagery for shame** — shame pictured as outer garment wrapping enemies; comprehensive inner and outward covering; the wicked "clothed" with disgrace

Six registers → 6 VCGs. Note: the garment-imagery register (6) is present across several VCGs; it is not sufficient to form its own VCG since the garment language appears within VCG-01 (enemies clothed with shame), VCG-02 (idolaters covered in shame), and VCG-03 (moral failure producing disgrace). The garment metaphor will be noted in relevant VCG descriptions rather than forming a separate group.

Five VCGs:
- VCG-01: Shame invoked on enemies as just reversal
- VCG-02: Shame from idolatry and misplaced religious trust
- VCG-03: Shame as natural fruit of personal moral failure
- VCG-04: Absence of shame — seared conscience and moral collapse

## VCG M07-B-VCG-01 — Shame invoked on enemies as just reversal of their hostility

**Description:** Verses where the psalmist or the community calls shame upon enemies, adversaries, and persecutors as the fitting inner and outward reversal of their hostile intent. The enemies sought the righteous person's ruin; shame is the divine verdict that exposes, collapses, and overturns their confidence. The dominant vocabulary is shame as "covering" the enemies (clothed with disgrace), shame "overtaking" them (suddenly, permanently), and shame as the divinely executed reversal that matches the inner malice of the aggressor. This is not mere imprecation but a theological claim: the enemies' shame is evidence that God has vindicated the sufferer. The psalmist praises God when enemies are put to shame because their disgrace confirms his help was real. Shame here falls on the guilty as the just consequence of aggression — fitting, proportionate, and vindicating.

**Anchor:** vc=34841 — Psa 53:5 — bosh — "God puts the attackers to shame by scattering their bones — divine judgment produces an inward terror and disgrace that exposes and undoes those who stood against his people." Divine action producing shame in enemies as direct consequence of their aggression; the link between God's judgment and the enemies' inner undoing is clearest here.

## VCG M07-B-VCG-02 — Shame from idolatry and false religious trust

**Description:** Verses where shame falls as the direct consequence of idolatry, false worship, or misplaced religious confidence. Idol-makers are destined for shame when their handiwork proves breathless and powerless. The inner bankruptcy of trusting in images — the willful blindness, the shared investment in falsehood — is exposed when the true God acts. The shame is both collective (all the companions of idol-craftsmen together) and specific (the goldsmith shamed by the images he crafted). The vocabulary of confusion, disorientation, and inner bewilderment is prominent: idol-worshippers go in confusion, are utterly undone, are turned back. Misplaced cultic trust belongs here too — sacrifices producing disgrace, the cherished idol carried off as tribute.

**Anchor:** vc=65571 — Jer 51:17 — bosh — "Shame exposes the inner bankruptcy of idol-making: the goldsmith is shamed by the very images he crafted, because those idols are false and breathless, making the craftsman's trust and labor foolish." Most directly names the mechanism — the shame arises from the object's inner bankruptcy (breathless, false) being exposed, producing shame in the one who trusted and laboured.

## VCG M07-B-VCG-04 — Absence of shame: the seared conscience and moral collapse

**Description:** Verses that evidence the terrifying moral condition of those who have lost the capacity for shame — who cannot blush, who feel no shame after committing abominations, whose inner faculty of moral sensitivity is wholly extinguished. The seared conscience is an analytic finding within M07-B: shame is the characteristic expected but absent. The capacity to register disgrace before wrongdoing is what the verses locate as missing, and the consequence is immediate — judgment follows, because the moral corrective has failed. The brazen forehead, the repeated commission of abomination without shame, the hardened dismissal of any possibility of judgment — these evidence the corruption of the inner faculty that shame is supposed to activate.

**Anchor:** vc=65553 — Jer 8:12 — bosh — "They feel no shame after committing abominations and have lost the ability to blush; the inner moral sense of shame is wholly absent, and judgment follows this seared conscience." The lost capacity for shame and its direct consequence (judgment) are clearest here.
