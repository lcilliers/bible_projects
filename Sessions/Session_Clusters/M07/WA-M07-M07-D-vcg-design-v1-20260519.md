## VCG M07-D-VCG-01 — Social and bodily humiliation: enforced downward movement in human community

**Description:** Verses where humiliation operates through visible, socially enacted demotion — the shaved beard that prevents re-entry to public life, the man expelled from the feast to the lowest place, communal exclusion, bodily posture forced into retreat, the king returning with face-shame. The characteristic is the enforced movement downward in social standing, made visible in the body and the public space. The inner shame is inseparable from the outward sign: the beard grows back before dignity can be restored; people sneak back like those fleeing in shame; public displacement is felt inwardly as dishonour. Includes the NT silencing-as-public-humiliation register (fimoō — the man without a wedding garment rendered speechless; Sadducees publicly silenced; Jesus silencing the demon), which is the social-demotion mechanism expressed through the withdrawal of voice before an audience. The vineyard parables' expected abasement that doesn't arrive (entrepō — respect for the son that the tenants refuse to render) is the negative case of this VCG — humiliation expected but withheld, evidencing its normal social function.

**Anchor:** vc=38517 — 2Sa 10:5 — ka.lam — "The men's humiliation is visible and social, felt so acutely in their bodies (shaved beards) that they cannot return to public life until the outward sign of disgrace has grown back." The inseparability of bodily sign and inner shame, and the social consequence of being barred from public life, is most directly evidenced here.

## VCG M07-D-VCG-03 — Divine abasement of pride: God bringing the lofty low

**Description:** Verses where humiliation is the direct, sovereign act of God against the inner disposition of pride and self-exaltation. The haughty looks brought down, lofty pride laid low, the high tree made low, the turban and crown removed — these evidence God's systematic counteraction of human arrogance. The mechanism is divine initiative: God alone executes the putting-down and lifting-up; abasement is not self-caused or socially determined but an act of divine will targeting the inner orientation of arrogance expressed in worldly elevation. The cosmic register (moon and sun ashamed before divine glory) and the community register (the inhabitants of the heights cast down, the city to the dust) both belong here. Pro 11:2 (disgrace as inevitable consequence of pride) and Pro 11:2 neighbour are the wisdom-tradition statement of the same principle operating through natural moral order.

**Anchor:** vc=37501 — Pro 11:2 — qa.lon — "Disgrace follows pride as its inevitable inner consequence—pride produces the very outcome it seeks to avoid, while humility carries wisdom, showing disgrace as the collapse of a self-inflated inner orientation." The wisdom-formulation of the pride-abasement principle — the clearest statement that disgrace is the natural inner consequence of the self-inflated orientation.

## VCG M07-D-VCG-04 — Shame carried into death and as enduring burden

**Description:** Verses where humiliation is not a momentary event but a permanent, carried condition — shame that accompanies warriors into the pit, persists beyond life, outlasts the terror they inspired in life, or restricts access indefinitely as an ongoing penalty. The Ezekiel underworld passages (Eze 32:24-30) concentrate this register: Elam's warriors, the northern princes, those who spread terror descend uncircumcised to the pit bearing their shame, their disgrace outlasting their power and reputation. The Levites barred from priestly nearness to God bear their shame as an ongoing inner-social penalty that restricts them indefinitely. Jeremiah 23:40 (perpetual, unforgettable reproach branded on identity) is the prophetic statement of the same register.
