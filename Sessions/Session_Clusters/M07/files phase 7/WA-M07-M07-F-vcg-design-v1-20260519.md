## VCG M07-F-VCG-01 — Shameful conduct and objects: the moral quality of what is shameful

**Description:** Verses where the characteristic is the moral-evaluative identification of conduct, actions, speech, or objects as shameful — conscience recognising what violates inner propriety, community norms grounded in created order, or God's standard. The aischros register (shameful as social-moral judgment): it is disgraceful to cut the hair in violation of accepted norms; speaking in the assembly is called shameful; deeds so degraded that even naming them is shameful; shameful financial gain corrupting the will. The idolatrous shameful objects also belong here: Baal as "the shame" consuming wealth and children, altars set up "to shame" filling Jerusalem's streets, Paul renouncing shameful underhanded ways. The mute idols (1Cor 12:2) whose total absence of will, voice, and agency makes them the paradigm of what is shameful — they are the contrast-case to the living Spirit, their voicelessness itself a marker of their shame.

**Anchor:** vc=34985 — Eph 5:12 — aischros — "Certain deeds done in secret are so morally degraded that even speaking of them is shameful — the shame is tied to the conscience's recognition that such things should not be named, let alone done." Most directly evidences the conscience's recognition function: the internal faculty of moral judgment identifying what violates inner propriety even to the point that speech about it is prohibited.

## VCG M07-F-VCG-02 — Indecent desires and moral disorder: disordered inner appetite

**Description:** Verses where the shameful characteristic is located specifically in disordered inner desire — the passionate inner drive toward what is morally indecent, the bodily-enacted moral disorder rooted in corrupted appetite, the inner failure of vigilance made visible as spiritual indecency. Romans 1:26-27 forms the core: dishonorable passions as the inner driving force, men committing shameless acts that are the physical expression of inner disorder. The indecency of moral-spiritual unpreparedness at Christ's coming (Rev 16:15) — the inner failure of vigilance — belongs here as the eschatological form of this register.

**Anchor:** vc=34959 — Rom 1:26 — atimia — "Dishonorable passions are the inner driving force behind exchanging natural relations for unnatural ones, showing dishonor as a corrupted desire operating at the level of felt appetite." The location of shameful in the appetitive inner faculty — passion as the driving force — is most directly evidenced here.

**Members (3 verses):**
- vc=34959 — Rom 1:26 — atimia — dishonorable passions as inner driving force (ANCHOR)
- vc=34966 — Rom 1:27 — aschēmosunē — bodily-enacted moral disorder rooted in disordered desire

## VCG M07-F-VCG-03 — Inverted conscience: glorying in shame / the perverted evaluative faculty

**Description:** Verses where the characteristic is not merely the existence of what is shameful but the inversion of the moral-evaluative faculty itself — the conscience that treats disgrace as honour, the will that loves what disgraces it. Romans 1:24 (God giving people over to dishonoring their own bodies — the conscience abandoned to sinful desire) is the mechanism underlying the inversion; Philippians 3:19 (glorying in what is actually shame) is its phenomenology; Hosea 4:18 (rulers' deep affection for dishonour) is its extreme form. These are not cases of moral failure producing shame but of the moral-evaluation apparatus being so corrupted that shame itself becomes the object of desire or the occasion for pride.

**Anchor:** vc=65486 — Phili 3:19 — aischunē — "These people's minds are set on earthly things and they glory in what is actually their shame — their conscience is inverted, treating disgrace as honor, revealing a corrupted inner orientation away from God." Most directly evidences the inversion: glorying in shame as the phenomenology of the corrupted evaluative faculty.

**Members (3 verses):**
- vc=35001 — Rom 1:24 — atimazō — God giving over to dishonoring own bodies; conscience abandoned
- vc=65486 — Phili 3:19 — aischunē — glorying in shame; conscience inverted; corrupted orientation (ANCHOR)
- vc=37514 — Hos 4:18 — qa.lon — rulers' deep affection for dishonour; shame as object of desire
