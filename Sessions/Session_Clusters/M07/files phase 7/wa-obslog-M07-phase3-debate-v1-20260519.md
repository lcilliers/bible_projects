### STEP 1 — CHARACTERISTIC IDENTIFICATION

Reading across the full meaning corpus of all 34 terms. Naming distinct inner-being faculties/states.

**Reading observations by register:**

**Core shame vocabulary (bosh 108v, bo.shet 29v, bu.shah 4v, bosh.nah 1v, ke.lim.mah 29v, ke.lim.mut 1v, aischunē 6v, aischunō 5v, cha.pher 16v):**
The overwhelming majority of the cluster's verse mass (c.200+ verses) evidences a primary experienced shame state — shame as the felt inner collapse of dignity, confidence, and standing. The meanings distinguish several registers within this core:

(a) Shame from misplaced trust — confident expectation collapses when the trusted object fails (idols, political alliances, human power). Bosh: Job 6:20, Isa 20:5, 30:5, Jer 48:13; bo.shet: Isa 30:3; cha.pher: Job 6:20.

(b) Shame as inner collapse before God — guilt-consciousness producing inability to approach the holy, face-falling before divine holiness. Bosh: Ezr 9:6, Jer 31:19; ka.lam: Ezr 9:6, 2Ch 30:15; ke.lim.mah: Eze 36:32, 43:10-11.

(c) Shame as social-relational wound — public disgrace, dishonour from others, the inner experience of being exposed and reduced in standing before peers. Bosh: 2Sa 19:5, Psa 44:15; ke.lim.mah: Psa 4:2, 44:15, 69:7; bo.shet: Psa 44:15, 69:19.

(d) Shame as moral consequence — disgrace as the natural fruit of sin, folly, or wickedness. Bosh: Pro 12:4, 17:2, 19:26; bo.shet: Pro (many); qa.lon: Pro 3:35, 11:2, 13:18, 18:3.

(e) Shame carried as enduring burden — shame that persists, accumulates, is "worn" or "borne". Ke.lim.mah: Eze 32:24-30, 34:29, 36:15, 44:13; ke.lim.mut: Jer 23:40; bo.shet: Dan 9:7-8.

(f) Shame as inward corrective — shame invoked as moral awakener, conscience-prod, corrective response to failure. Entropē: 1Cor 6:5, 15:34; entrepō: 2Th 3:14, Tit 2:8; bosh: Eze 36:32, Zec 13:4.

(g) Shame's absence as moral collapse — inability to blush as sign of seared conscience. Bosh: Jer 6:15, 8:12; ka.lam: Jer 6:15, 8:12; Zep 3:5.

**Observation:** (a)-(g) all belong to the same fundamental characteristic — shame as experienced inner state — but with distinct functional registers. Volume consideration: bosh alone = 108 verses. With bo.shet (29), ke.lim.mah (29), ka.lam (38), cha.pher (16), aischunē (6), aischunō (5) this characteristic could easily run to 250-300 verses = well over 40% of 527. Must volume-split.

Natural split axes within core shame:
- **Shame experienced / received** (the inner state felt by the subject) vs **Shame invoked upon enemies** (shame as the desired outcome for opponents)
- **Vertical shame** (shame before God, shame arising from spiritual failure, divine judgment producing shame) vs **Horizontal shame** (shame from and among humans — social, relational, circumstantial)
- **Shame as wound / burden** vs **Shame as corrective / awakener**

**Humiliation / abasement vocabulary (ka.lam 38v, sha.phel 24v BOUNDARY, qa.lah 2v, qi.qa.lon 1v):**
Ka.lam consistently evidences humiliation as a specific form of disgrace — the enforced downward social movement, the experienced loss of standing, the inner crush of being brought low. Sha.phel (BOUNDARY) evidences God's enforcement of abasement on the proud — primarily the reversal of pride/exaltation, with a secondary voluntary-lowness register. This cluster of meanings is distinct from the core shame-experience: humiliation is shame inflicted through a downward movement of standing, with the mechanism of abasement more prominent than the inner emotional texture.

Characteristic candidate: **Humiliation and abasement** — shame inflicted through social demotion, divine reversal of the proud, enforced downward movement of standing.

**Dishonour vocabulary (atimazō 7v, atimia 7v, kataischunō 12v, qa.lon 17v, qa.lah 2v, paradeigmatizō 1v):**
This register evidences dishonour as a relational category — the stripping of honour, the assignment of diminished worth, the treatment of persons as having less value than they possess. Distinct from core shame (felt inner collapse) and humiliation (enforced downward movement): dishonour is specifically the relational dynamic of worth-denial. Qa.lon: Pro 3:35, 6:33, 11:2, Job 10:15; atimazō: Mar 12:4, Luk 20:11, Joh 8:49; kataischunō: 1Cor 11:22, 2Cor 9:4.

Characteristic candidate: **Dishonour as relational diminishment** — the denial of worth, stripping of honour, social-relational treatment as worthless.

**Shameful as moral judgment (aischros 4v, aschēmosunē 2v, aischunē [Phili 3:19, 2Cor 4:2]):**
Several terms evidence shameful/indecency as a moral-evaluative category — actions and conducts judged as violating inner propriety, moral decency, or conscience. This is less about the felt experience of shame and more about the moral quality of what is shameful. Aischros: 1Cor 11:6, 14:35, Eph 5:12, Tit 1:11; aschēmosunē: Rom 1:27, Rev 16:15.

Characteristic candidate: **Shamefulness as moral judgment** — the moral-evaluative category of what is shameful/indecent; inner conscience's recognition of moral degradation.

**Contempt-producing-shame (exoutheneō 11v — cross-register M06 flag):**
As established in Phase 3 v2, exoutheneō evidences the contempt side of the contempt-shame dynamic. The characteristic this sub-group represents within M07 is: shame as the produced inner experience of being treated with contempt — the received face of dismissal, devaluation, and rejection. 1Cor 1:28 (the despised of the world), Act 4:11 (Jesus rejected by builders), Luk 23:11 (contempt-and-mocking of Jesus).

Characteristic candidate: **Shame produced by contempt and rejection** — the inner experience of being treated as worthless, dismissed, or rejected; the shame-receiving face of the contempt-shame dynamic.

**Verbal shaming (loidoria 2v, kakologeō 1v — cross-register M42 flag; also entrepō in its shame-inducing register):**
These terms evidence shame inflicted through speech — verbal attack on reputation, reviling, and the inner response to such attack. Related to but distinct from contempt (which is an inner attitude): verbal shaming is the speech-act mechanism by which shame is inflicted.

Characteristic candidate: May fold into the dishonour / contempt-producing-shame characteristics rather than warranting a standalone sub-group given the small verse count (3 verses). Decision: route loidoria and kakologeō to the contempt-producing-shame characteristic (they share the mechanism of directed dishonour); not a standalone sub-group.

**Silencing / speechlessness in shame (fimoō 8v, afōnos 3v — cross-register M42/M09 flags):**
Three of fimoō's verses and one of afōnos's carry the shame-silencing connection. But the majority of fimoō's verses (5/8) have no shame content, and afōnos similarly has 2/3 with no shame content. The shame-relevant verses from these terms (Mat 22:12, Mat 22:34, 1Pe 2:15 from fimoō; Act 8:32 from afōnos) express: inward collapse of self-defense, public silencing as humiliation, and bearing disgrace in silence. These route naturally to the core shame-experience characteristic rather than requiring a standalone sub-group. The cross-register flag travels forward.

Decision: fimoō's shame-relevant verses route to core shame / humiliation sub-groups; the non-shame verses (demons, animals, minimal content) will require specific routing decisions at verse level.

**Innocence-as-shame-shield (niq.qa.von 4v, qe.ha.von 4v — cross-register M12 flag):**
These terms evidence innocence as the inner state that protects against shame. This is a distinct register — not shame itself but the structural counter-state. The Phase 3 v2 finding: all 4 verses of each term engage the innocence-shame polarity. This warrants a standalone sub-group — the innocence/moral-purity register as shame's structural opposite and shield.

Characteristic candidate: **Innocence as inner shield against shame** — the moral-purity register that protects against or resolves the shame experience; the absence of guilt-based shame through blameless inner state.

**Throwing-away / confidence-loss (apoballō 2v — cross-register volitional flag):**
Mar 10:50 (abandoning shamed identity) and Heb 10:35 (not throwing away confidence before God). Two verses. These route naturally — Mar 10:50 to the core shame-experience (the shamed-identity register), Heb 10:35 to the shame-avoidance/protection register (confidence as shield).

### STEP 2 — CHARACTERISTICS NAMED

After reading across the full corpus, the following **6 substantive characteristics** are identified (plus BOUNDARY):

**CHAR-1: Shame as experienced inner state** — the felt, subjective experience of shame; the inner collapse of dignity, confidence, and standing under failure, guilt, exposure, or judgment. Primary terms: bosh, bo.shet, bu.shah, bosh.nah, ke.lim.mah, ke.lim.mut, aischunē, aischunō, cha.pher, entropē. Sub-characteristic registers: shame from misplaced trust; shame before God from sin; shame from exposure; shame as enduring burden; shame as corrective force; absence of shame as moral collapse.

**CHAR-2: Humiliation as enforced abasement** — shame inflicted through an active downward movement of standing; social demotion, dishonour through physical or relational exposure, the experience of being brought low by others or by God. Primary terms: ka.lam, qa.lah, qi.qa.lon. Secondary: bosh (many enforced-shame verses), ke.lim.mah (the "carried" and "borne" register).

**CHAR-3: Dishonour as relational worth-denial** — the relational dimension of shame; treating persons as of diminished or no worth; the stripping of honour from another through attitude, conduct, or judgment. Primary terms: atimazō, atimia, kataischunō, qa.lon, paradeigmatizō. Secondary: entrepō (the shame-causing register).

**CHAR-4: Shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment** — the category of what is morally shameful, indecent, or degraded; the conscience's recognition of moral disorder; conduct that violates inner propriety. Primary terms: aischros, aschēmosunē. Secondary: aischunē (Phili 3:19 inverted conscience; 2Cor 4:2 shame of deceptive conduct).

**CHAR-5: Shame produced by contempt and rejection** — the shame experience arising from being treated with dismissive contempt or active rejection; the received face of the contempt-shame dynamic. Primary terms: exoutheneō, loidoria, kakologeō, atimazō (Mar 12:4, Luk 20:11 register). Cross-register flag: M06 (contempt as the source-side).

**CHAR-6: Innocence as structural counter to shame** — the inner moral-purity state that protects against, resolves, or is the structural opposite of shame; blameless conscience as shield. Primary terms: niq.qa.von, qe.ha.von. Cross-register flag: M12 (innocence as primary purity register).
