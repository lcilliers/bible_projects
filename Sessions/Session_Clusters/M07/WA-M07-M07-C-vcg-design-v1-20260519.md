## VCG M07-C-VCG-01 — Guilt-shame before the holy: the face that cannot be lifted

**Description:** Verses where shame arises directly from guilt-consciousness before the holiness of God — the inner collapse of dignity in the divine presence, produced by the weight of accumulated sin, covenant betrayal, or ritual failure. The physical posture is the interpretive key: the face that cannot be lifted toward God (Ezra), the blushing that cannot be suppressed, the communal confession that owns shame as the deserved condition. Priests' conscience-driven shame moving them immediately to consecration, Ezra's anticipatory conscience-shame at the inconsistency between his words and his trust, Jerusalem's generations of guilt arriving at a visible condition of national disgrace — all evidence the inner dynamic of shame produced by standing in the awareness of God's holiness while bearing sin. The penitential posture of lying down in shame (Jer 3:25) and the commanded shame of Eze 16 also belong here — shame embraced as the appropriate inner response to sin confessed before God.

**Anchor:** vc=65495 — Ezr 9:6 — bosh — "Shame here is deeply inward — Ezra cannot lift his face toward God because guilt-consciousness fills him, the weight of corporate sin producing a felt inability to approach the holy." Most directly evidences the guilt-shame mechanism: the inward reality (guilt-consciousness) producing the physical sign (cannot lift face), with God as the reference point.

## VCG M07-C-VCG-02 — Trust in God as shield against shame: refuge and word

**Description:** Verses where the inner act of taking refuge in God, holding to his testimonies, waiting on him, or boldly declaring his word is the direct protective faculty against shame. Shame is named as the inner threat — the collapse that trust prevents. The psalmist's multiple pleas to not be put to shame while he takes refuge in God, his linking of blameless heart and God's statutes to shame-avoidance, his declaration that speaking God's word before kings does not produce shame — all evidence this register. These verses do not primarily concern guilt-shame (VCG-01) or promised future removal (VCG-04); they concern the present inner dynamic of trust-as-shield. Looking to God produces radiance rather than shame's face-collapse (Psa 34:5). Abiding in Christ produces the inner confidence that prevents recoiling at his appearing (1Jo 2:28).

**Anchor:** vc=34836 — Psa 22:5 — bosh — "Trust in God is presented as the inner disposition that protects against shame—the ancestors cried out, trusted, and were not put to shame, so shame is averted by reliance on God." Most directly names the mechanism: trust as the disposition that protects against shame, proven through the ancestors' experience.

## VCG M07-C-VCG-03 — Corrective shame awakening conscience toward God

**Description:** Verses where shame is deployed specifically as an inward corrective force directed toward the vertical relationship — shame awakening the community to its failure before God, distinguishing fatherly admonition from humiliating shame-induction, or shame silencing the self-justifying mouth before atonement. Paul's invocation of shame to awaken conscience (entropē — 1Cor 6:5; 15:34), his distinction between correction motivated by affection and shame-induction that crushes (1Cor 4:14), the corrective use of shame in communal discipline (2Th 3:14 in M07-B), reverence prompted by discipline (Heb 12:9) — these form the corrective-shame register. The silencing of Jerusalem's mouth by the weight of atonement (Eze 16:63) belongs here as the supreme case: shame not as active punishment but as the inner state that permanently closes the self-justifying voice when forgiveness has been received. Zechariah 13:4 (false prophets ashamed of their visions, abandoning deceptive markers) is the conscience-as-corrective register.

**Anchor:** vc=65575 — Eze 16:63 — bosh — "Shame silences the inner voice: confounded by atonement, Jerusalem will never open her mouth again, the shame so deep it shuts down self-justification as God makes full atonement for her deeds." Most definitionally evidences the corrective-shame function in the vertical register — shame produced not by punishment but by the weight of undeserved forgiveness, permanently closing the self-justifying mouth.

## VCG M07-C-VCG-04 — Promise: never put to shame in God's presence

**Description:** Verses where the promise of not being put to shame before God is the primary content — eschatological and covenantal guarantees that those who wait on God, who are saved by his everlasting salvation, or in whom God's presence dwells will not experience shame's inner collapse. The positive pole of the vertical-shame characteristic: God promises shame's permanent removal from his people's future experience. Distinct from VCG-02 (present trust as shield) by temporal orientation: these are covenant guarantees about what will not happen, not present-posture descriptions of the trusting faculty. Includes the nakedness-shame that Christ's garments cover (Rev 3:18 — the exposed inner condition of spiritual destitution remedied by divine provision).

**Anchor:** vc=65543 — Isa 45:17 — bosh — "Israel, saved by the Lord with everlasting salvation, will never be put to shame or confounded — the promised absence of shame is permanent, grounded in divine salvation that removes every basis for inner disgrace." Most directly evidences the promised-never-to-be-shamed register: the permanence and divine grounding of the promise.
