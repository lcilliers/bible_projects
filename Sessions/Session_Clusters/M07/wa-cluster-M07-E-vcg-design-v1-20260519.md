## VCG M07-E-VCG-01 — Active dishonour: refusing or withdrawing due regard toward persons

**Description:** Verses where dishonour is actively enacted through the refusal or withdrawal of due regard — a willful inner failure to acknowledge worth in another person or in God. The unjust judge who lacks any inner sense of regard or deference (and recognizes this as a character trait), the accusers who dishonour Jesus by willfully refusing to acknowledge his worth, social breakdown producing insolence toward the honourable, hypocrisy concealing actual contempt for the lawgiver behind religious pride, the poor man treated as if he had no worth. Violent contemptuous action against authority-bearers (the servants struck in the vineyard parables) and the symbolic bodily acts that convey disrespect toward divinely ordered authority (head coverings) belong here as the enacted forms of worth-denial. Publicly dishonouring Christ through wilful apostasy (Heb 6:6) is the extreme form.

**Anchor:** vc=35000 — Joh 8:49 — atimazō — "Jesus contrasts honoring the Father with his accusers dishonoring him, showing dishonor as a willful inner refusal to acknowledge the worth of another, here directed personally against Jesus." Most directly evidences the relational mechanism: a willful inner refusal, directed at a specific person, to acknowledge that person's worth.

- vc=34990 — Luk 18:2 — entrepō — judge lacking inner regard for God or persons; absence of deference

- vc=35000 — Joh 8:49 — atimazō — willful inner refusal to acknowledge Jesus's worth (ANCHOR)

## VCG M07-E-VCG-02 — Assigned or structural dishonour: standing, nature, and received treatment

**Description:** Verses where dishonour is not actively wilfully enacted but assigned, structurally determined, or received as social treatment. The potter's assignment of vessels to dishonorable purpose (Rom 9:21 — sovereign purpose, not personal rejection); nature teaching that certain practices are a disgrace (social-moral judgment grounded in created order, not personal hostility); dishonour as outward social treatment received by Paul without altering his inner integrity (2Cor 6:8); vessels in a household assigned to dishonorable use reflecting moral condition; the worker whose worth is protected from the dishonour of not receiving due payment (the muzzled ox analogies). These evidence dishonour as a structural/standing category rather than a relational act.

**Anchor:** vc=34960 — Rom 9:21 — atimia — "The potter's sovereign right to make one vessel for honor and another for dishonor shows that dishonor is an assigned standing or purpose, not an inner emotional state here." Most directly evidences dishonour as assigned standing rather than relational act.

**Members (6 verses):**

- vc=30403 — 1Cor 9:9 — fimoō — worker's right to benefit; not muzzling as protection from worth-denial
