  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Consistently across the cluster, the sustained effect differs significantly from the immediate response. The immediate response is acute (collapse, withdrawal, recoil); the sustained effect is formative (identity-shaping, conscience-forming or conscience-degrading, relational-access determining). The most significant cluster-level observation: the same immediate response (shame's collapse) can produce very different sustained outcomes depending on whether it is received as corrective, refused, voluntarily borne, or imposed unjustly. The determining factor — the will's orientation — brings the cluster's primary volitional theme back as the decisive long-term differentiator.
- *T1.7.1* — _What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The enabling conditions cluster around two themes across the six. (1) Operative conscience: across Chars 1, 4, and 6, the conscience's functioning capacity is the prerequisite for genuine reception of the shame-signal. The person whose conscience can register moral failure, recognise the shameful, and maintain innocence is equipped to receive the corrective signal that M07's characteristics provide. (2) Will oriented toward God: across Chars 1, 4, 5, and 6, the will's orientation toward God as the worth-standard and moral reference is what enables genuine reception — of shame's corrective signal (Char 1), of the shameful's proper identification (Char 4), of the contempt-produced wound without retaliation (Char 5), and of God's confirming verdict on innocence (Char 6).
- *T1.7.2* — _What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Two primary blocking conditions appear across the cluster. (1) Pride and self-sufficiency: Char 2's proud disposition that CHAR-2 must correct but that cannot self-correct; Char 3's self-righteous dishonour of others; Char 5's contempt arising from "trusted in themselves that they were righteous" (Luk 18:9). Pride blocks reception by making the shame-signal unrecognisable — the proud person cannot receive correction because they cannot perceive their own elevation as the problem. (2) Idolatry and substitute-standard: Char 4's inversion (earthly things replacing God's standard, Phili 3:19); Char 6's incapacity for innocence (sustained idol-worship eroding the moral will, Hos 8:5). Idolatry corrupts the evaluative faculty at its root — the conscience calibrated to the idol cannot register the shame-signal from God's holiness.
- *T1.7.3* — _What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Two distinct non-reception states emerge across the cluster. (1) Pathological hardening (Chars 1, 4, 6): the seared conscience (Jer 6:15), the inverted conscience that glories in shame (Phili 3:19), the incapacity for innocence (Hos 8:5). These are states of progressive inner damage — the faculty that should receive the signal has been so repeatedly refused that it no longer functions. (2) Volitional resilience (Chars 1, 5): the servant who sets the face like flint (Isa 50:7), Bartimaeus who throws off the shame-identity in response to Jesus's call (Mar 10:50). These are states of moral strength grounded in a higher worth-assignment — the person does not receive the shame-signal as damaging because God's assignment overrides the shame-system's verdict. Cluster-level distinction: refusing shame's damage through grounded confidence in God is resilience; refusing shame's corrective through pride or idolatry is pathological. The difference lies entirely in the orientation of the will.
- *T1.8.1* — _What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The six primary dimensions do not converge on a single category, confirming that M07 is multi-dimensional: Char 1 (emotional/affective primary), Char 2 (social/relational primary), Char 3 (relational primary), Char 4 (moral-evaluative primary — the most directly moral-dimension characteristic in the cluster), Char 5 (relational primary), Char 6 (moral primary). Cluster-level pattern: the relational dimension appears as primary in three characteristics (Chars 2, 3, 5) and as significant in all six. M07 is therefore most fundamentally a relational cluster — its characteristics describe what happens to persons in relation to God, to other persons, and to moral community. The moral dimension is the second most prominent (Chars 1, 4, 6). The affective dimension, while phenomenologically prominent in Char 1, is not the primary dimension of the majority.
- *T1.8.2* — _What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The relational primary across Chars 2, 3, and 5 is confirmed by the consistent social enactment of these characteristics: the shaved envoys cannot return to public life (Char 2 — 2Sa 10:5), the poor man's worth is violated in the community's relational field (Char 3 — Jam 2:6), the contemptuous dismissal operates through the relational attitude of one person toward another (Char 5 — Luk 18:9; Rom 14:3). All three characteristics are constitutively about what happens in the space between persons. Char 6's relational dimension (innocence enabling approach to God) confirms that even the positive counter-characteristic is primarily about a relational state — the person before God — rather than a private inner condition.
- *T1.8.3* — _Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The secondary dimensions across the six are consistent: moral (in Chars 2, 3, 5), affective (in Chars 1, 3, 5), volitional (in Chars 1, 3, 4, 5, 6). The absence of a single primary dimension that runs through all six is itself a cluster-level finding: M07 does not have a single constitutional dimension but a relational-moral-affective complex in which different characteristics foreground different aspects of the same unified shame-field.
