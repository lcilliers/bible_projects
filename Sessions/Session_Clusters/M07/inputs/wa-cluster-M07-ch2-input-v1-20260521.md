**Key verses for this characteristic:**

**Psa 53:5** (H0954 bosh) [M07-B-VCG-01]

_Meaning of the term here:_ God puts the attackers to shame by scattering their bones — divine judgment produces an inward terror and disgrace that exposes and undoes those who stood against his people.

_Meaning of the term here:_ Shame exposes the inner bankruptcy of idol-making: the goldsmith is shamed by the very images he crafted, because those idols are false and breathless, making the craftsman's trust and labor foolish.

**Pro 6:33** (H7036 qa.lon) [M07-B-VCG-03]
> Pro 6:33 He will get wounds and dishonor , and his disgrace will not be wiped away .
_Meaning of the term here:_ The adulterer gets wounds and dishonor that cannot be erased—dishonor here is an enduring mark on the person, a permanent loss of standing that even restitution cannot remove, showing shame's lasting inner and social damage.

**Jer 8:12** (H0954 bosh) [M07-B-VCG-04]
> Jer 8:12 Were they ashamed when they committed abomination ? No , they were not at all ashamed ; they did not know how to blush . Therefore they shall fall among the fallen ; when I punish them, they shall be overthrown , says the Lord .
_Meaning of the term here:_ They feel no shame after committing abominations and have lost the ability to blush; the inner moral sense of shame is wholly absent, and judgment follows this seared conscience.

**1Pe 3:16** (G2617 kataischunō) [M07-B-VCG-05]

_Meaning of the term here:_ A good conscience sustains good behavior in Christ, so that slanderers are put to shame — shame falls on the accusers whose inner moral judgment is exposed as false when confronted with blameless conduct.
