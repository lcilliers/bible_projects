## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Read across six characteristics, the cluster reveals six distinct angles on God's character — none duplicating another. Char 1 (shame as experienced inner state) reveals God as the guardian of creaturely dignity who takes personal responsibility for protecting those who trust him from shame's inner collapse (the Psalm supplication formula "let me not be put to shame" — Psa 25:2; 31:1 — presupposes this guardianship). Char 2 (enforced abasement) reveals God as the sovereign agent who corrects pride with terrifying force — "God alone executes judgment, putting down one and lifting up another" (Psa 75:7). Char 3 (dishonour as worth-denial) reveals God as the authoritative worth-assigner whose evaluation of persons cannot be overridden — the potter's sovereign right (Rom 9:21) is the supreme statement of this. Char 4 (shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment) reveals God as the moral standard: "open scrutiny before God" (2Cor 4:2) is the test against which all conduct stands. Char 5 (contempt-produced shame) reveals God as the contempt-reverser — choosing the despised to nullify the world's confident worth-assessments (1Cor 1:28; Act 4:11). Char 6 (innocence as structural counter) reveals God as the confirmer of genuine moral innocence — the one who verifies and acknowledges the innocence-claim (Gen 20:5-6). The cluster-level convergence: God operates simultaneously as guardian (Chars 1, 5), sovereign agent of correction (Char 2), authoritative worth-assigner (Chars 3, 5), moral standard (Char 4), and innocence-confirmer (Char 6). No single angle exhausts what M07 reveals about him.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics divide cleanly into two groups. Char 2 alone is explicitly attributed to God as agent: he brings low, casts the wicked down, changes glory into shame (1Sa 2:7; Psa 147:6; Hos 4:7). God exercises enforced abasement as a judicial act. The remaining five (Chars 1, 3, 4, 5, 6) are all explicitly silent on God possessing the characteristic: shame as felt experience, dishonour as received, shamefulness as conscience-judgment, contempt-produced shame, and innocence-as-shield are all exclusively creaturely states. Char 3 partially bridges this — God assigns dishonour-standings (Rom 9:21) — but does not undergo worth-denial from another. The cluster's pattern is therefore asymmetric: God is the primary actor within M07's field (abasing, assigning worth, reversing contempt, confirming innocence) but does not experience M07's characteristics as the creature does.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The near-unanimous silence across five of six characteristics establishes a consistent theological signal: M07's domain is distinctively creaturely moral vulnerability — the vulnerability to exposure, failure, degradation, contempt, and the loss of standing before others. God does not share this vulnerability; he addresses it from outside it. Char 6 sharpens this: innocence as the creaturely approximation to God's holiness is exclusively creaturely in its M07 function (as protection against shame), even though the holiness it approximates belongs to God. The cluster-level implication: M07's inner-being domain is one of the most distinctively post-fall domains in the programme — the created-design dimension is limited to the moral capacities (conscience, moral integrity of will and heart) that underlie Chars 4 and 6. All other characteristics are fallen-condition responses. God's silence from the characteristics he does not possess is matched by his richly evidenced activity in correcting, reversing, and resolving them.

**T0.2.1 — What purpose does this characteristic serve in the human person as created?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across the six, a consistent pattern emerges: five characteristics (Chars 1, 2, 3, 5, and Char 4 in its inverted form) serve no positive purpose in the original created design — they are responses to or products of fallenness. Gen 2:25 ("they were not ashamed") is the datum that anchors this: the pre-fall state is explicitly shame-free. Char 6 is the exception: the capacity for moral innocence — the blameless conscience and clean will — belongs to the created design itself. Char 4 occupies the middle: the conscience's capacity to recognise the shameful belongs to the created design (moral standard-sensitivity is an image-bearing faculty), though its specific shame-recognitive function presupposes the fallen world's moral failures. The cluster's overall verdict: M07 is primarily a post-fall cluster. Its positive created-design content is limited to the moral faculties (conscience, will) that Chars 4 and 6 presuppose — not to the shame-experiences and shame-mechanisms themselves.

---

**T0.2.2 — Is this characteristic part of original created design, fallen response, or both?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics fall into three positions. Position A — fallen-condition response only: Chars 1, 2, 3, 5. All presuppose a world of moral failure, violence, and broken worth-recognition that belongs to the fallen condition. Position B — both: Char 4. The conscience's evaluative capacity belongs to the created design; the shameful-as-evaluative-category arises from that capacity operating in a fallen world. Position C — both, with functions differentiated: Char 6. Innocence as an inner state belongs to the created design; innocence as a protective counter to shame is a fallen-condition function (in the unfallen state no protection against guilt-shame was needed because there was no guilt). The cluster's summary: M07 is primarily a fallen-condition cluster. The created-design residue is restricted to the moral faculties (Chars 4, 6), not the shame-states themselves.

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six evidence eschatological orientation, but with strikingly different densities. Char 1 has the highest promise-density in the entire cluster: the shame-removal corpus (Isa 54:4; 61:7; Joe 2:26-27; Zep 3:19; Isa 45:17) makes felt shame the M07 characteristic Scripture most explicitly promises to resolve. Char 5 has the paradigmatic reversal statement: Act 4:11 (rejected stone becoming the cornerstone) is the eschatological reversal of contempt-shame in its most compressed form. Char 2's eschatological horizon is the Day-of-the-Lord completion (all pride finally brought low) and 1Cor 15:43 (mortal dishonour raised in glory). Chars 3 and 5 share the trajectory of the world's false worth-assignments and contempt-verdicts being overturned at God's final word. Char 4's eschatological horizon is Rev 16:15 — conscience-vigilance oriented toward Christ's coming. Char 6's eschatological trajectory is through the Psa 73 lament — maintained innocence vindicated in the sanctuary, pointing toward final vindication. Cluster-level observation: eschatological promise-density is concentrated where suffering is most acutely personal (Chars 1 and 5). The eschatological resolution is most explicitly directed at the most personally felt wounds.

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics express the divine image through different moral faculties. Char 1 expresses it through the moral consciousness that can register shame — the image-bearer's capacity for self-assessment before the holy. Char 2 expresses it through the moral standing that can be exalted or abased — the reality of the image-bearer's standing before God. Char 3 expresses it through the worth-recognition capacity — the ability to see and honour genuine worth in others as God does. Char 4 expresses it through the conscience's moral-evaluative operation — the creaturely registration of God's moral character. Char 5 expresses it indirectly: the fact that contempt wounds presupposes the person has genuine worth rooted in the image. Char 6 expresses the image most directly through the positive moral faculty — the blameless will and clean conscience that mirror God's own moral purity in creaturely form. Cluster-level pattern: M07's expression of the divine image is concentrated in the moral dimensions — conscience, will, and the capacity for moral self-assessment and accountability before God.

---

**T0.3.2 — Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Unanimously creaturely across all six in the form the cluster names them. God is the agent (Char 2), standard (Chars 1, 4), assigner (Chars 3, 5), and confirmer (Char 6) — not the experiencer of shame, humiliation, dishonour, contempt, or innocence-as-shield. The one partial exception — God incarnate receiving Char 3's dishonour (Joh 8:49) and Char 5's contempt (Luk 23:11) — operates specifically in the creaturely context of the incarnation, not in the divine nature per se. The cluster-level finding: M07 identifies a domain of human inner-being experience that has no divine analogue. This unanimity is itself a significant analytical signal — the shame-field is one of the most distinctively creaturely clusters in the programme.

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — A single underlying principle emerges across the six: the appropriate presence of M07's moral faculties signals the image as operative; their corruption signals the image as damaged; their replacement by the opposite signals the image as severely degraded. Char 1: capacity for appropriate guilt-shame = operative moral consciousness; its absence (Jer 6:15 — "they did not know how to blush") = image-degradation. Char 4: functioning conscience = image-integrity; its inversion (Phili 3:19 — glorying in shame) = the image's moral-evaluative faculty corrupted at its root. Char 6: maintained innocence = the image's moral-will functional; Hos 8:5's incapacity-for-innocence = the image's moral-will damaged by sustained idolatry. At the cluster level: M07's characteristics function as diagnostic indicators of the image's moral condition. The three failure-modes (seared conscience, inverted conscience, incapacity for innocence) correspond to three degrees of image-degradation from least to most severe.

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six carry typological dimension, converging most powerfully in christological direction. Char 1 (voluntary shame-bearing) → Christ despising the shame (Heb 12:2) and the servant setting face like flint (Isa 50:7). Char 2 (mortal dishonour) → resurrection glory (1Cor 15:43). Char 3 (dishonour of the incarnate Son) → the passion's contempt narrative. Char 4 (nakedness and exposure) → moral-spiritual unpreparedness at Christ's coming (Rev 16:15). Char 5 (rejected stone) → the cornerstone of the resurrection (Act 4:11) — the most explicitly christological typological deployment in the cluster. Char 6 (innocence enabling approach) → Christ as the one whose perfect innocence alone fully enables approach to God. Cluster-level christological convergence: M07's characteristics collectively illuminate the passion (Chars 1, 2, 3, 5), the resurrection (Chars 2, 5), the parousia (Char 4), and the new covenant's approach to God (Char 6). The shame-field is among the cluster-fields through which the christological narrative runs most richly.

**T0.4.2 — Direction of typology?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Two directions operate across the cluster. (1) Human → divine: human unjust shame, humiliation, dishonour, and contempt-rejection all point toward and find their fullest expression in Christ's passion. The creaturely experience is the type; Christ's is the antitype and eschatological fulfilment. (2) Divine → human: God's election of the despised (Char 5 — 1Cor 1:28) establishes the pattern for the community's re-evaluation of worth; Christ's despising of the shame (Char 1 — Heb 12:2) establishes the pattern for the believer's shame-bearing. The bidirectionality is itself a cluster-level finding: M07's typological field does not move in one direction but participates in the christological exchange in which human shame becomes the site of divine entry and divine vindication becomes the resolution of human shame.

## T1 — Defining the Characteristic

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called, and what does the name signal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Reading the six names together, a precise inner taxonomy emerges. "Shame as experienced inner state" (Char 1) establishes the primary phenomenological register — the felt inner event. "Humiliation as enforced abasement" (Char 2) names the mechanism by which shame's outer form is produced. "Dishonour as relational worth-denial" (Char 3) names the relational act that violates the person's worth. "Shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment" (Char 4) names the evaluative-qualitative dimension — the conscience's naming of what is shameful. "Shame produced by contempt and rejection" (Char 5) names the specific contempt-mechanism and its product. "Innocence as structural counter to shame" (Char 6) names the positive moral condition that the other five presuppose and oppose. Together the six names constitute a systematic map: the experience (Char 1), the mechanism (Char 2), the relational act (Char 3), the evaluative quality (Char 4), the contempt-source (Char 5), and the protective counter (Char 6). No name is redundant; each covers a genuinely distinct dimension of the same shame-field.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's vocabulary divides into three lexical families across the six characteristics. Family 1 — the experiential register: bosh (H0954), bo.shet (H1322), ke.lim.mah (H3639), aischunē (G0152), kataischunō (G2617) — terms naming the felt experience or condition of shame. Primarily Char 1, with secondary presence in Chars 2 and 3. Family 2 — the mechanism/act register: ka.lam (H3637), sha.phel (H8213), atimazō (G0818), exoutheneō (G1848), qa.lon (H7036) — terms naming the enacted downward movement or contemptuous dismissal that produces shame. Primarily Chars 2, 3, and 5. Family 3 — the qualitative register: aischros (G0150), aschēmosunē (G0808) — terms naming the property of shamefulness as a quality of conduct or objects. Primarily Char 4. Char 6's vocabulary (niq.qa.von H5356A, qe.ha.von H5356B) stands alone as the only positively-framed vocabulary in the entire cluster. Cluster-level finding: M07 has the most lexically rich shame-vocabulary in the programme, distributed across experiential, mechanistic, and qualitative registers, with a single protective counter at Char 6.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six names collectively establish three consistent directional implications. (1) Downward movement: all five negative characteristics involve a person's standing being moved downward (Chars 1, 2, 3, 5) or conduct being assessed as below the proper moral level (Char 4). (2) Relational constitution: all six are inherently relational — they arise in, or are expressed through, relations between person and God (Char 1 across all three registers), person and persons (Chars 2, 3, 5), person and moral community (Char 4), and person and God in the cultic context (Char 6). (3) Constitutional location at soul level: all six operate primarily at the level of the person's inner moral life (conscience, will, relational standing, felt experience) rather than at the level of body alone or spirit alone.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Comparing the six across the condition/act/quality/disposition taxonomy reveals a pattern: Chars 1, 2, and 5 are primarily conditions (they fall upon the person from outside); Char 3 is primarily an act (willful refusal to acknowledge worth); Char 4 is primarily a quality (a property of conduct); Char 6 is a moral-condition that requires active maintenance. The cluster as a whole is condition-dominated: the majority of M07's verse-mass concerns what happens to persons (shame falls, humiliation is enforced, contempt is directed, worth is denied) rather than what persons do. The volitional exceptions (Char 3's willful dishonour; Char 4's evaluative conscience; Char 6's maintained innocence) reveal that the primarily receptive-condition field has a volitional layer that is decisive for how each characteristic is experienced.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All six are internally structured, and comparing their structures reveals the cluster's internal architecture. Char 1 is the most complex (six constituent elements, three sub-group registers). Char 6 is the most compact (two constituent elements). The cluster-level observation: each characteristic's internal structure reflects the multi-dimensional nature of the shame-field it covers — the characteristics are not simple binary states but internally articulated phenomena with distinct sub-registers, VCG differentiations, and operational modes. This structural complexity at the characteristic level produces the cluster's overall analytical richness.

---

**T1.2.3 — Best working description?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — At the cluster level, the six working descriptions collectively define M07 as the cluster of shame in all its forms — the inner-being domain in which human dignity, worth, and moral standing are exposed, stripped, violated, assessed, and (in Char 6) protected. The cluster covers: what shame feels like from within (Char 1), how it is imposed from without (Char 2), how it is enacted through relational worth-denial (Char 3), how it is identified as a quality of conduct (Char 4), how it is produced by contempt and received by the person (Char 5), and what the inner moral state is that protects against it (Char 6). The six descriptions together constitute a complete inner-being account of the shame-field: its phenomenology, mechanism, relational form, evaluative dimension, contempt-production, and moral counter.

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Comparing the six structural opposites reveals a consistent counterpart domain centred on honour: honour/dignity (Char 1), exaltation (Char 2), honour/worth-recognition (Char 3), the honourable/decent (Char 4), acceptance/worth-recognition (Char 5), and guilt as Char 6's inverse (guilt is what innocence excludes; its absence is what innocence protects). Several characteristics share primary structural-opposite vocabulary: timē (honour) and kabod (glory) stand against the shame-family across multiple characteristics. Cluster-level observation: the structural opposite domain is less internally differentiated than M07's own domain — multiple shaming-characteristics share a single honour-opposite. This confirms that M07's concern is with the varieties of shame's form rather than the varieties of honour's form. The honour-glory vocabulary belongs to another cluster's primary territory.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six characteristics collectively exclude a consistent set of inner-being states: (1) Upright confident self-presentation before God and community — Chars 1, 2, 3 all exclude the capacity to stand forward (the covered face, the stripped crown, the refused acknowledgment of worth). (2) Bold speech — Char 1 excludes it through silence; Char 2 through speechlessness (Mat 22:12); Char 3 through worth-denial's suppression. (3) Moral openness — Char 4 names what must remain hidden because it cannot bear scrutiny. (4) The worth-recognition that proper human community requires — Chars 3 and 5. (5) Approach to God without guilt's barrier — Char 6 in its absence-mode. At the cluster level: M07's characteristics name the barriers to the free, confident, morally grounded engagement with God and community that the image-bearer was designed for.

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six not-distinctions collectively establish M07's analytic boundaries. Key cross-characteristic distinctions maintained: CHAR-1 (inner experience) ≠ CHAR-2 (outer mechanism) — the felt shame vs the enforced abasement are analytically distinct even when co-occurring. CHAR-3 (relational act) ≠ CHAR-5 (specific contempt-mechanism and its received shame) — contempt is a subset of dishonour, not identical with it. CHAR-4 (evaluative quality) ≠ CHAR-1 (experiential state) — what the conscience names as shameful vs what the person experiences as shame. CHAR-6 (protective moral condition) ≠ M12 purity (that characteristic's primary register for niq.qa.von/qe.ha.von). What M07 is not at the cluster level: M06 (contempt as the source-side characteristic), M09 (voluntary humility as a positive virtue), and M12 (moral purity as the primary register). These three cluster-boundaries were each validated in Phase 3 and maintained through Phase 8.5.

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across the six, five primary operational modes emerge at the cluster level. (1) Passive reception — shame falls upon the person (Chars 1, 2, 5 all have received-condition registers). (2) Active willful production — someone extends shame toward another through acts of dishonour, contempt, or humiliation (Chars 2, 3, 5). (3) Internal moral assessment — the conscience evaluates and names the shameful (Char 4). (4) Moral maintenance — innocence is actively maintained or lost through sustained choice (Char 6). (5) Divine sovereign deployment — God brings low (Char 2), assigns worth (Char 3), reverses contempt-verdicts (Char 5), confirms innocence (Char 6). These modes are not mutually exclusive: in any given verse, multiple modes may operate simultaneously.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently by context or constitutional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster shows consistent contextual differentiation across two axes. Vertical context (person before God): Chars 1-M07C, 4, 6 — the primary register is shame/conscience/innocence in the person-to-God encounter. Horizontal community context (person among persons): Chars 1-M07A/B, 2, 3, 5 — the social, military, relational, and contempt registers. Cross-covenant: Chars 2 and 5 operate universally (God humbles all pride; contempt-produced shame can fall on anyone). Covenant-specific: Chars 4 and 6 operate primarily within the covenantal relation with God. This contextual differentiation reveals that M07 spans both the human-to-God and human-to-human relational fields, making it one of the programme's most relationally comprehensive clusters.

**T1.4.3 — Communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The most striking cluster-level pattern in the communicative mode is silence as shame's primary expression. Char 1 names this most directly (Eze 16:63 — "never open your mouth again because of your shame"; Act 8:32 — the silent lamb). Char 2 confirms it through the silenced Sadducees (Mat 22:34) and the speechless man without the wedding garment (Mat 22:12). Char 3's symbolic mode (the covered head of 1Cor 11:4-5) enacts the relational statement through gesture rather than speech. The exceptions: Char 4 is the one mode where shame produces speech rather than silence — the shameful is named, articulated, and renounced. Char 5's VCG-03 verbal-contempt register is entirely speech-based; the counter-command is to respond with blessing rather than reviling (1Pe 3:9). Cluster-level pattern: shame generally silences; shame's corrective deployment (Char 4) and the naming of contempt (Char 5) are the speech-based exceptions.

**T1.5.1 — Most immediate inner response?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Reading the six immediate responses together, a threefold cluster-level pattern emerges. (1) Collapse and withdrawal — the most common across Chars 1, 2, and 5. The immediate impact of shame, humiliation, or contempt is an inward collapse of confidence paired with outward retreat (face-covering, backward-turning, inability to re-enter public space). (2) Recoil and renunciation — Char 4's immediate response to recognising the shameful: the functioning conscience recoils and the will renounces (2Cor 4:2). (3) Confidence for approach — Char 6's immediate response to maintained innocence: the clean-handed person moves toward God's altar without the hesitation that guilt would produce (Psa 26:6). These three responses map the cluster's inner-being range from collapse-and-withdrawal at one pole to confidence-for-approach at the other.

**T1.5.2 — Consistent or variable immediate response?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Consistent within each characteristic's primary mode; variable across the cluster. The collapse-withdrawal pattern is the most consistent (Chars 1, 2, 5). The evaluative-recoil pattern is consistent for functioning Char 4. The confidence-for-approach pattern is consistent for functioning Char 6. Variable dimension: within Char 1, the voluntary-bearing mode (M07-A-VCG-03) produces willed steadiness rather than collapse (Isa 50:7 — "I have set my face like a flint"); within Char 5, Bartimaeus's cloak-throwing (Mar 10:50) is decisive agency rather than collapse. These volitional-override patterns represent the cluster's resilience capacity — where the will is engaged toward a stronger referent, shame's default collapse-response is transcended.

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce over time?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's sustained effects converge on three long-term trajectories. (1) Identity-formation around shame: Char 1's chronic inner wound (Jer 20:18), Char 2's VCG-04 enduring burden (shame carried into death, Eze 32), Char 5's beggar-identity (Bartimaeus's cloak as accumulated contempt-formed self-understanding, Mar 10:50). Unresolved shame deposits identity shaped around the wound. (2) Moral desensitisation: Char 1's seared conscience (Jer 6:15), Char 4's inverted conscience (Phili 3:19 — "glory in their shame"), Char 6's loss of innocence-capacity (Hos 8:5). The sustained effect of refused corrective shame and idolatry is progressive erosion of the conscience's functioning. (3) Relational foreclosure: Char 2's VCG-04 priests permanently barred from approach to God (Eze 44:13), Char 6's incapacity-for-innocence blocking approach. The sustained effect of shame's unresolved burden is the foreclosure of the relational access to God that the cluster's positive pole (Char 6) enables.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states and orientations does sustained possession establish?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's sustained states span a spectrum. At the negative pole: identity-in-shame (chronic burden carried as self-definition), moral blindness (seared or inverted conscience, incapacity for innocence), and relational foreclosure (barred access to God and community). At the positive pole: moral discernment (habituated conscience identifying and renouncing the shameful, Char 4), relational openness (sustained innocence enabling approach to God, Char 6), and the formation that comes from voluntarily borne shame (the disciplined endurance of Char 1's M07-A-VCG-03 register). Between these poles: the existential questioning of maintained innocence under apparent failure (Char 6's Psa 73:13 register) — innocence maintained but confidence in its protection tested.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Consistently across the cluster, the sustained effect differs significantly from the immediate response. The immediate response is acute (collapse, withdrawal, recoil); the sustained effect is formative (identity-shaping, conscience-forming or conscience-degrading, relational-access determining). The most significant cluster-level observation: the same immediate response (shame's collapse) can produce very different sustained outcomes depending on whether it is received as corrective, refused, voluntarily borne, or imposed unjustly. The determining factor — the will's orientation — brings the cluster's primary volitional theme back as the decisive long-term differentiator.

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable genuine reception?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The enabling conditions cluster around two themes across the six. (1) Operative conscience: across Chars 1, 4, and 6, the conscience's functioning capacity is the prerequisite for genuine reception of the shame-signal. The person whose conscience can register moral failure, recognise the shameful, and maintain innocence is equipped to receive the corrective signal that M07's characteristics provide. (2) Will oriented toward God: across Chars 1, 4, 5, and 6, the will's orientation toward God as the worth-standard and moral reference is what enables genuine reception — of shame's corrective signal (Char 1), of the shameful's proper identification (Char 4), of the contempt-produced wound without retaliation (Char 5), and of God's confirming verdict on innocence (Char 6).

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or distort reception?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Two primary blocking conditions appear across the cluster. (1) Pride and self-sufficiency: Char 2's proud disposition that CHAR-2 must correct but that cannot self-correct; Char 3's self-righteous dishonour of others; Char 5's contempt arising from "trusted in themselves that they were righteous" (Luk 18:9). Pride blocks reception by making the shame-signal unrecognisable — the proud person cannot receive correction because they cannot perceive their own elevation as the problem. (2) Idolatry and substitute-standard: Char 4's inversion (earthly things replacing God's standard, Phili 3:19); Char 6's incapacity for innocence (sustained idol-worship eroding the moral will, Hos 8:5). Idolatry corrupts the evaluative faculty at its root — the conscience calibrated to the idol cannot register the shame-signal from God's holiness.

---

**T1.7.3 — State of the person who encounters but does not receive?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Two distinct non-reception states emerge across the cluster. (1) Pathological hardening (Chars 1, 4, 6): the seared conscience (Jer 6:15), the inverted conscience that glories in shame (Phili 3:19), the incapacity for innocence (Hos 8:5). These are states of progressive inner damage — the faculty that should receive the signal has been so repeatedly refused that it no longer functions. (2) Volitional resilience (Chars 1, 5): the servant who sets the face like flint (Isa 50:7), Bartimaeus who throws off the shame-identity in response to Jesus's call (Mar 10:50). These are states of moral strength grounded in a higher worth-assignment — the person does not receive the shame-signal as damaging because God's assignment overrides the shame-system's verdict. Cluster-level distinction: refusing shame's damage through grounded confidence in God is resilience; refusing shame's corrective through pride or idolatry is pathological. The difference lies entirely in the orientation of the will.

---

**T1.8.1 — Primary inner-being dimension?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The six primary dimensions do not converge on a single category, confirming that M07 is multi-dimensional: Char 1 (emotional/affective primary), Char 2 (social/relational primary), Char 3 (relational primary), Char 4 (moral-evaluative primary — the most directly moral-dimension characteristic in the cluster), Char 5 (relational primary), Char 6 (moral primary). Cluster-level pattern: the relational dimension appears as primary in three characteristics (Chars 2, 3, 5) and as significant in all six. M07 is therefore most fundamentally a relational cluster — its characteristics describe what happens to persons in relation to God, to other persons, and to moral community. The moral dimension is the second most prominent (Chars 1, 4, 6). The affective dimension, while phenomenologically prominent in Char 1, is not the primary dimension of the majority.

The resolution of shame does not depend on peer-acceptance or social re-inclusion but on God's authoritative worth-declaration, which may precede and override the social dimension entirely.

Whether further verse investigation is needed or whether this is a genuine silence appropriate to Scripture's framework is a question for Session D synthesis.

## Appendix — Synthesis Prose

### Theme 1 — The CHAR-1 Volume-Split: Three Registers, One Characteristic

The most significant architectural decision in M07 was splitting Char 1 (Shame as experienced inner state) across three sub-groups: M07-A (unjust wound, 63 verses), M07-B (moral consequence, 111 verses), and M07-C (divine encounter, 50 verses). These are not three separate characteristics but three distinct causal pathways to the same inner-being event — the felt collapse of dignity and standing.

The cluster synthesis confirms that these three registers are more genuinely distinct than the volume-split terminology suggests. They differ not only in their cause but in their resolution-direction and transformation-potential.

M07-A (unjust wound) resolves through divine vindication. The unjust shame-wound is not self-correcting and does not dissolve through moral effort — it requires God to act on the person's behalf. The Psalm supplication formula ("let me not be put to shame") is concentrated here. The eschatological shame-removal promises (Isa 54:4; 61:7; Joe 2:26-27) address M07-A's condition directly — "you will forget the shame of your youth" is a divine promise to dissolve the memory-load that unjust shame creates.

M07-B (moral consequence) resolves through corrective response. The shame that arises from genuine moral failure can be received, produce contrition, and open toward corrective action. The paradigm cases are 2Ch 30:15 (shame activating immediate priestly consecration) and Jer 31:19 (instruction producing shame-recognition and return). Its destructive form — the seared conscience refusing the signal — is one of the cluster's most serious conditions (Jer 6:15; 8:12).

M07-C (divine encounter) resolves through the relational gateway. This is the most theologically positive register within Char 1: shame before God's holiness is not merely painful but redemptively structured. Eze 43:10-11 shows that Israel's being-ashamed is the precondition for receiving the full design of the temple — "only if Israel is ashamed of all they have done will the full design of the temple be disclosed to them." The shame of divine encounter is the posture from which genuine covenant relationship can be renewed.

These three resolution-directions (vindication, correction, gateway) are what most sharply distinguish the three registers. A person in M07-A's unjust wound cannot correct their way out of shame — they need divine vindication. A person in M07-B's moral-consequence shame who receives the signal can act correctively. A person in M07-C's divine-encounter shame is already in the position of openness to God that enables the fullest renewal. The three sub-groups are genuinely distinct pastoral-anthropological situations within the single characteristic of shame-as-experienced-inner-state.

### Theme 2 — The M06 ↔ M07 Contempt-Shame Relational Pair

The cross-cluster relationship between M06 (contempt) and M07-G (Char 5 — Shame produced by contempt and rejection) is one of the programme's clearest examples of a relational characteristic pair: M06 holds the contempt-er's inner act (contempt-projection as the inner disposition of treating another as of no account); M07's Char 5 holds the shame-recipient's inner experience (the received face of being treated as nothing). Neither is complete without the other: contempt without a recipient is not fully itself; shame produced by contempt without naming the source-mechanism is incomplete.

What the cluster synthesis confirms is that God's position within this pairing is consistent and decisive. In the Char 5 evidence, God consistently takes the side of the contempt-recipient against the contempt-er: 1Cor 1:28 (God choosing the despised to nullify the world's contempt-assessments), Act 4:11 (God making the rejected stone the cornerstone), Gal 4:14 (the community receiving Paul as an angel of God despite his weakness). God never appears as the contempt-er in Char 5's corpus; he appears exclusively as the one who overturns contempt-verdicts and vindicates the contempt-received. This is M07's most direct theological statement about where God stands within the shame-field.

The exoutheneō corpus (11 verses in M07-G VCG-01) is the most analytically concentrated evidence for the M06↔M07 dynamic. Every verse shows the contempt-er's inner attitude (self-righteous trust in one's own righteousness — Luk 18:9; dismissive low regard for fellow believers — Rom 14:3; 14:10; contemptuous devaluation of certain community members — 1Cor 6:4) producing or risking the shame-condition in the recipient. The source-side (M06) is the inner disposition; the received face (M07-G Char 5) is the inner experience of being treated as nothing.

The practical consequence: when M06 opens, the same verse-corpus seen here from the Char 5 (shame-recipient) angle will need to be analysed from the contempt-er's angle. The exoutheneō verses in particular will appear as the primary cross-cluster shared evidence. M06's Phase 9 will see the relational complement from the other side.

### Theme 3 — The M12 ↔ M07 Innocence-Purity Polarity

Char 6's innocence vocabulary (niq.qa.von and qe.ha.von) belongs primarily to M12 (purity/holiness) as the terms' primary register but was retained in M07 because every verse evidences the innocence-shame polarity directly. The cluster synthesis confirms this was the right decision — and reveals why the distinction matters.

M12's primary concern is with purity as the condition of ritual, cultic, and moral holiness — the state of being clean in the sense required for approach to God in his holiness. M07's Char 6 is specifically concerned with innocence as the protective counter to guilt-shame — the inner moral state that removes the barrier of guilt from the person's approach to God. These are related but analytically distinct: purity (M12) is the broader condition of moral and ritual cleanness; innocence (Char 6) is specifically the condition in which no deliberate wrongdoing has been committed, and therefore no guilt-shame stands between the person and God.

The four Char 6 verses confirm this specific function. Gen 20:5's "integrity of my heart and the innocence of my hands" is a guilt-innocence claim — Abimelech is asserting that he acted without deliberate wrongdoing, which is the basis for his approach to God for protection. Psa 26:6's hand-washing enables approach to the altar — the hand-washing is the ritual symbol of the moral claim, not the claim itself. Psa 73:13's "I have kept my heart clean" is a sustained moral-integrity claim under the pressure of unexplained suffering — the psalmist doubts whether innocence-as-protection is real, but the innocence itself is maintained. Hos 8:5's "incapable of innocence" is the loss of the moral capacity to be guiltless before God — not merely a failure to attain ritual purity but the loss of the inner faculty through which innocence-as-counter-to-shame operates.

The M07↔M12 cross-cluster relationship is therefore: M12 holds the purity vocabulary in its full cultic and moral-holiness scope; M07 holds the specific shame-relevant function of the same terms — the inner moral state that protects against guilt-shame. When M12 opens, niq.qa.von and qe.ha.von will need to be analysed in their broader purity-holiness register. The Char 6 findings will be the M07-side of what should be a rich cross-cluster dialogue.

### Theme 4 — OT vs NT Vocabulary Arcs

The cluster's vocabulary concentration by Testament reveals a significant asymmetry. The OT vocabulary is quantitatively dominant: the bosh corpus (approximately 108 verses in the cluster, the majority in Char 1) alone exceeds the entire NT shame-vocabulary in the cluster. The OT vocabulary is also semantically richer in the experiential and judicial registers — the bosh, ka.lam, ke.lim.mah, sha.phel, and niq.qa.von families together constitute the cluster's primary lexical substance.

The NT's specific contributions, beyond intensification of existing roots, are three. First, christological focus: the NT shame-vocabulary is most fully developed in the context of Christ's passion, rejection, and resurrection reversal. Exoutheneō in Act 4:11 (rejection of the stone), atimazō in the parable of the tenants (Mar 12:4; Luk 20:11 — servants beaten and treated shamefully), atimia in 1Cor 15:43 (the mortal body sown in dishonor), the despising of the shame in Heb 12:2 — these are the NT's concentrated deployments of shame-vocabulary in the christological context. Second, community-ethics focus: the epistolary letters deploy the shame-vocabulary specifically for community formation — the Corinthian correspondence's shame-deployment (1Cor 6:5; 11:4-5; 14:35; 15:34), Paul's shame-resilience (2Cor 10:8; Phili 1:20), the community's management of shame between members (Rom 14:3; 1Th 5:20; 1Pe 3:9). This is a distinctively NT register not replicated in the OT. Third, the inverted-conscience: Phili 3:19 ("glory in their shame") is the NT's most explicit statement of the conscience whose evaluative standard has been entirely inverted, producing the worst form of the shame-field's corruption.

The vocabulary arc's theological significance: the OT establishes the shame-field in its full experiential, judicial, and promissory breadth; the NT focuses it through the christological lens and demonstrates its applicability to community formation and to the diagnosis of conscience-failure in its most extreme form.

### Theme 5 — Vertical vs Horizontal Shame

A major structural distinction runs through the cluster between vertical shame (shame before God) and horizontal shame (shame between persons). This distinction cuts across characteristic boundaries rather than mapping onto them.

Vertical shame is the dominant register of Char 1 M07-C (the guilt-shame in God's presence — Ezr 9:6), Char 4 (the conscience evaluated "in the sight of God" — 2Cor 4:2), and Char 6 (innocence enabling approach to God's altar — Psa 26:6). The vertical dimension is also present in Char 2's Day-of-the-Lord material (God bringing down all human pride so he alone is exalted — Isa 2:11) and Char 3's dishonour of God through hypocrisy (Rom 2:23).

Horizontal shame is the dominant register of Char 1 M07-A and M07-B (the psalmist shamed by enemies, the nation shamed by military defeat), Char 2's VCG-01 and VCG-02 (social demotion and military collapse), Char 3 (dishonour operates between persons in community), and Char 5 (contempt-produced shame in the relational field).

The resolution of vertical and horizontal shame differs structurally. Vertical shame (guilt before God) can be resolved through atonement and through the restoration of innocence — the barriers that guilt creates between the person and God are precisely what God's covenantal action addresses. Horizontal shame (shame from others, shame in community) is primarily resolved through divine vindication (God protecting those who trust him from enemies — the Psalm promises) and divine reversal (God choosing the despised, overturning contempt-verdicts — 1Cor 1:28; Act 4:11). The two resolution pathways correspond to the two axes: vertical resolution through atonement and moral restoration; horizontal resolution through divine vindication and eschatological reversal.

### Theme 6 — Shame Removed vs Shame Imposed

The cluster contains both: God imposing shame (as a judicial act, primarily in Char 2's VCG-03 and Char 1's M07-B enemy-shaming passages) and God removing shame (as an eschatological and redemptive act, primarily in Char 1's M07-A VCG-04 and Char 5's reversals).

The two modes are not in tension — they represent different axes of God's action in the shame-field. God imposes shame on the proud (Char 2's sha.phel VCG-03 — "God brings low and exalts"), on the enemies of his people (Char 1 M07-B's enemies put to shame), and on the idol-maker whose craft is exposed (Char 1 M07-B's idolatry register). God removes shame from the vulnerable (Char 1's unjust-wound sufferers — Isa 54:4), the repentant (Char 1 M07-C's corrective shame resolved through gateway-opening — Eze 43:10-11), and the eschatological community (Char 1's VCG-04 shame-removal promises).

The integrating principle: God's action in the shame-field is always moral — he opposes what exalts itself against him (imposing shame as correction) and vindicates what has suffered under false worth-assessments (removing shame as restoration). When God imposes shame, it is the moral correction of over-reach. When God removes shame, it is the moral vindication of those who have suffered under unjust shame-systems. Both are expressions of the same moral character: the God who alone assigns worth correctly cannot leave either the proud's over-exaltation or the vulnerable's unjust shame unaddressed.

### Theme 7 — Christological Convergence Points

The cluster's christological convergence is multi-dimensional, spanning at least five distinct christological moments.

(1) The servant's shame-bearing (Isa 50:6-7; Act 8:32). The servant who does not flinch from physical disgrace and whose face is set like flint is the foundational OT type for Chars 1 and 2. The NT application (Act 8:32's silence of the lamb read by the Ethiopian eunuch) confirms the christological reading — the servant's voluntary shame-acceptance points toward and is fulfilled in Christ's passion.

(2) The cross's shame despised (Heb 12:2). "Despising the shame" while enduring the cross "for the joy set before him" is the primary christological anchor for Char 1's voluntary-bearing mode. This verse sets the model for the community's own shame-bearing: the joy-motivated will overcoming shame's impairing pull. It is the cluster's most concentrated christological statement of volitional agency in the shame-field.

(3) The rejected stone made cornerstone (Act 4:11, citing Psa 118:22). Char 5's primary christological anchor: the contempt-verdict of the builders overturned by God's sovereign reversal in resurrection. This is the eschatological reversal of CHAR-5 in its most compressed form — rejection leading to vindication, contempt-shame becoming the cornerstone of the entire structure.

(4) The passion's contempt narrative (Luk 23:11; the parable of the tenants in Mar 12:4 and Luk 20:11). Herod and his soldiers treating Jesus with contempt and mockery (Char 5 in the passion), the violent treatment of the vineyard servants preceding the son's arrival (Chars 2, 3, 5 in the parable). These constitute the cluster's most concentrated intersection of multiple characteristics in a single narrative — the parable anticipating and the passion narrative fulfilling the full shame-mechanism operating against Jesus.

(5) The faith-and-shame promise (Rom 5:5; 9:33; 10:11). "The one who trusts will not be put to shame" — the NT's most explicit statement that faith in Christ is the operative counter to the shame-threat in the present age. This promise speaks primarily to Char 1's unjust-wound register but grounds the community's response to every form of shame the cluster names: the shame-protection is the promise of trust, extended to all who trust in the cornerstone.

These five convergence points confirm that M07's shame-field is one through which the christological narrative runs most richly. The passion is an event of shame (Chars 1, 2, 3, 5); the resurrection is its reversal (Chars 2, 5); the eschatological promise is the final removal of all shame (Char 1's VCG-04). Christ is simultaneously the one who bore the cluster's characteristics most completely and the one through whom the cluster's eschatological resolution becomes available.

### Theme 8 — Communal vs Solitary Registers

The cluster spans both communal and solitary registers, with different characteristics concentrating in different registers.

Primarily communal: Char 2 (military defeat and national collapse are collective events — whole armies and cities bear shame); Char 3 (dishonour operates relationally between persons in community — the poor man in Jam 2:6, the judge who neither fears God nor respects man in Luk 18:2); Char 5 (contempt typically operates through communal dynamics — the self-righteous treating others with contempt in Luk 18:9; the food-dispute contempt in Rom 14:3/10).

Primarily personal/individual: Char 1 M07-C (the individual's guilt-shame before God — Ezr 9:6 is Ezra's personal prayer; Psa 73 is one psalmist's crisis); Char 6 (the individual's maintained or lost innocence — Gen 20:5 is Abimelech's personal claim; Psa 26:6 is one worshipper's approach to the altar).

Mixed: Char 1 M07-A and M07-B (the individual psalmist's shame and national-communal shame share the same vocabulary and often the same verses); Char 4 (the individual conscience and the community's shared moral evaluations).

The cluster-level finding: shame is both a solitary (you face God alone with your moral condition) and a communal (you are embedded in worth-systems and contempt-systems) phenomenon. Its resolution is similarly both solitary (God's personal encounter with the individual's guilt-shame) and communal (God's reversal of the community's contempt-verdicts and the community's responsibility to protect the vulnerable from dishonour). Formation in the shame-field requires both dimensions.

### Theme 9 — Inversions and Corruptions

Every M07 characteristic has a functioning form and a corrupted form. Comparing these across the six reveals the cluster's corruption-map.

Char 1 (shame as experienced inner state): functioning form = corrective shame received and producing moral reorientation (2Ch 30:15; Eze 43:10-11); corrupted form = seared conscience unable to register shame's signal (Jer 6:15 — "they did not know how to blush").

Char 2 (humiliation as enforced abasement): no functioning-form corruption in the same sense (it is a judicial mechanism); corrupted reception = hardened defiance or dissolution without transformation (the nations "shorn of strength" who simply collapse without reorienting — Isa 37:27).

Char 3 (dishonour as relational worth-denial): functioning form = proper worth-recognition and acknowledgment; corrupted form = active worth-denial through self-righteous favoritism or hypocrisy (Rom 2:23 — the self-contradicting divided heart).

Char 4 (shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment): functioning form = conscience correctly identifying the shameful and renouncing it (2Cor 4:2); corrupted form = inverted conscience glorying in shame (Phili 3:19 — "their end is destruction"). This is the cluster's most extreme corruption: the evaluative standard itself has been replaced by its opposite.

Char 5 (shame produced by contempt and rejection): functioning form = acceptance and worth-recognition (Gal 4:14 — "received me as an angel of God"); corrupted form = contemptuous dismissal of others arising from self-righteous elevation (Luk 18:9 — "trusted in themselves that they were righteous").

Char 6 (innocence as structural counter to shame): functioning form = maintained innocence enabling approach to God (Psa 26:6); corrupted form = incapacity for innocence through sustained idolatry (Hos 8:5 — "how long will they be incapable of innocence?").

The corrupted forms cluster around three root causes: pride (the source of Chars 2, 3, and 5's corruptions), substitute-standard (the earthly orientation of Char 4's inversion), and sustained idolatry (the cause of Char 6's loss). These are not three independent causes but three expressions of the same fundamental failure: displacing God from the position that belongs to him. Pride places the self above God; substitute-standard places earthly things above God's sight; idolatry places the idol above God. The corruption of M07's characteristics is therefore a function of the vertical relationship's distortion — when God is displaced, the shame-field corrupts.

### Theme 10 — Eschatological Density

Comparing the six characteristics' eschatological evidence reveals striking variation in density and directness.

Highest density: Char 1. The shame-removal promise corpus (Isa 54:4; 61:7; Joe 2:26-27; Zep 3:19; Isa 45:17; Rev 3:18) makes Char 1 the M07 characteristic Scripture most explicitly promises to resolve in the eschaton. This is not accidental: shame as the experienced inner state is the cluster's primary characteristic (224 of 363 verses), and the eschatological promises are concentrated at the characteristic most personally felt.

High density: Char 5. Act 4:11 (rejected stone becoming cornerstone) is the paradigmatic eschatological reversal statement; 1Cor 1:28 (God choosing the despised to nullify what is) is the eschatological programme that Char 5's reversal-theme embeds. The contemplation of shame-from-contempt's eschatological reversal is among the NT's most concentrated promise-forms.

Moderate density: Chars 2 and 3. Char 2's eschatological horizon is the final Day-of-the-Lord completion and 1Cor 15:43's resurrection-transformation. Char 3's eschatological horizon is the final correct worth-assignment.

Lower density: Chars 4 and 6. Char 4's eschatological statement is Rev 16:15 — oriented forward but a call to vigilance rather than a promise-form. Char 6's eschatological orientation is through the Psa 73 lament's resolution — implied but not stated as an explicit promise.

The variation reveals a consistent principle: M07's eschatological orientation is strongest where the suffering is most acutely personal (Chars 1 and 5 — the psalmist's own shame; the contempt-wound inflicted on the person by others). The eschatological resolution of shame is most specifically directed at the most personally felt wounds. This is not a theological accident but a reflection of God's moral character: the God who takes unjust shame seriously enough to reverse it is the God who most explicitly promises to address the shame that has been most personally felt.

### Theme 11 — Unexpected Cross-Characteristic Patterns

Several patterns emerged from the 189-prompt cluster synthesis that were not visible at the per-characteristic level.

(1) The face-hand body-axis as the cluster's primary body-map. Shame collapses the face (Chars 1, 2 — face-covering, face-hiding, inability to lift the face before God and community); innocence is expressed through the hands (Char 6 — Gen 20:5; Psa 26:6). The face-hand polarity maps the cluster's body-axis: the face bears shame; the hands declare innocence. This asymmetry — shame on the face, innocence through the hands — is not visible from any single characteristic's analysis. The cluster-level emergence of this polarity is one of Phase 9's most distinctive anatomical findings.

(2) Unanimous silence on creativity. All six characteristics are silent on the creative faculty. The shame-field does not engage imagination or the capacity to originate. This is the most consistent negative finding across the entire cluster. Shame closes; it does not open. The implication: the eschatological resolution of shame (Char 1's VCG-04 — shame permanently removed and replaced by double portion and everlasting joy) may be a necessary precondition for the full flourishing of the image-bearer's creative capacities. The shame-field's suppression of the creative faculty suggests that shame-removal is not merely the repair of a wound but the liberation of a capacity.

(3) The conscience as integrating faculty. The condition of the conscience determines the outcome of every characteristic in the cluster. The seared conscience (Char 1's absence-mode), the inverted conscience (Char 4's VCG-03), and the incapacity for innocence (Char 6's Hos 8:5) are three faces of the same inner-being catastrophe: the conscience's failure as the primary shame-registering and shame-countering faculty. This conscience-centrality is not visible from any single characteristic — it only emerges when the three failure-modes are compared across the six.

(4) The two opposite sequences running through the same characteristics. The corrective sequence (guilt-shame → corrective action → restored approach) and the destructive sequence (sustained idolatry → evaluative inversion → incapacity for approach) use the same characteristics in opposite directions. This confirms that M07's characteristics are not fixed in their trajectory but directionally contingent on the will's orientation. The cluster's characteristics are terrain; the will determines which path is taken across that terrain.

(5) The developmental gap as the primary science-gap. The five developmental and neurological gaps identified at T7.3.4 cluster around a single theme: how the shame-field's inner-being characteristics form in the person over time. The verse evidence describes the resulting conditions (the seared conscience, the beggar-identity, the incapacity for innocence) but does not develop a theory of their formation. This represents the programme's most significant and consistent point of science-dialogue where the horizontal framework illuminates a dimension that the vertical framework does not address — not because the vertical framework is absent but because Scripture's interest is in the moral condition itself rather than in the developmental process by which it forms.
