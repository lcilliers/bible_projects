## §1 — Characteristics identified

Reading across the full 527-verse meaning corpus of M07's 34 terms, six distinct inner-being characteristics are present, plus a BOUNDARY group.

### CHAR-1 — Shame as experienced inner state (volume-split into CHAR-1a, CHAR-1b, CHAR-1c)

The primary and dominant characteristic of M07: shame as a felt, subjective inner experience — the collapse of dignity, confidence, and standing. The corpus evidences three distinct registers that together account for the majority of the cluster's verse mass. Because the combined verse volume exceeds the 40% distribution gate (§8.6), CHAR-1 is split into three sub-groups by a three-way axis: the relational direction and moral framing of the shame experience.

- **CHAR-1a (Shame as unjust inner wound):** Shame received by the innocent, the righteous, the suffering — shame as an undeserved inner wound inflicted by enemies, circumstances, or misplaced trust. Includes: shame from enemy aggression, shame from loss and bereavement, shame from disappointed trust in human powers, shame lifted by divine vindication, shame of the servant bearing disgrace for God's sake. Primary terms: bosh, ke.lim.mah, aischunē (Heb 12:2), ka.lam, bo.shet.

- **CHAR-1b (Shame as moral consequence and judgment):** Shame as the fitting, deserved outcome of sin, folly, idolatry, wickedness, or moral failure — shame falling on the guilty as the just consequence of their inner moral state. Includes: shame invoked on enemies and the wicked, shame from idolatry, shame as the natural fruit of pride/folly, absence of shame as sign of moral collapse (inability to blush). Primary terms: bosh, ka.lam, cha.pher, bo.shet, entrepō (corrective), kataischunō.

- **CHAR-1c (Shame before God — conscience and guilt):** Shame in the vertical divine relationship — the inner collapse of the person before God's holiness, arising from guilt-consciousness, covenant failure, or the soul's exposure before the holy. Includes: inability to lift the face before God, shame averted by trust/faith in God, shame as inner precondition for divine restoration, corrective shame awakening conscience, shame resolved through atonement. Primary terms: bosh, ka.lam, entropē, kataischunō, entrepō (disciplinary), aischunō.

### CHAR-2 — Humiliation as enforced abasement

Shame inflicted through an active downward movement of standing — the enforced lowering of a person, people, or pride-structure by social demotion, military defeat, physical exposure, or divine judgment. The emphasis is on the mechanism of abasement and the reversal of exaltation, not merely the felt shame experience. Primary terms: ka.lam (bodily/social humiliation), bosh (military defeat, cosmic abasement), ke.lim.mah (shame carried to death), atimia (dishonour of mortal existence), qa.lon (pride brought low), entrepō (expected abasement not given), fimoō (silencing as humiliation), qa.lah, qi.qa.lon.

### CHAR-3 — Dishonour as relational worth-denial

The relational dimension of shame — treating persons as having less worth than they possess; the active denial or stripping of honour through attitude, conduct, or social judgment. Distinct from core shame (felt inner state) and humiliation (enforced downward movement): dishonour is specifically the relational dynamic of worth-assignment. Primary terms: atimazō, atimia, kataischunō, qa.lon, paradeigmatizō, entrepō (absence of regard), qa.lah, fimoō (worker's worth).

### CHAR-4 — Shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment

The category of what is morally shameful, indecent, or degraded — the conscience's recognition that certain conduct or conduct-patterns violate inner propriety and cannot bear scrutiny before God or community. This is the evaluative/qualitative dimension of shame, not the experienced inner state. Primary terms: aischros, aschēmosunē. Secondary: aischunē (Phili 3:19 — inverted conscience; 2Cor 4:2 — shameful conduct quality), atimia (Rom 1:26 — dishonorable passions), bosh (Hos 2:5 — shameful conduct), bo.shet (Jer 3:24, 11:13, Hos 9:10), qa.lon (Hos 4:18 — perverted will).

### CHAR-5 — Shame produced by contempt and rejection

The shame experience arising from being treated with dismissive contempt or active rejection — the received, experiential face of the contempt-shame dynamic. The mechanism is contempt/rejection; the characteristic evidenced within M07 is the shame it produces in the recipient. Cross-register flag: M06 (contempt as the source-side of this dynamic). Primary terms: exoutheneō, loidoria, kakologeō, atimazō (contemptuous violence register), kataischunō (1Cor 11:22 — shaming the poor), ka.lam (protection from shaming the vulnerable), qa.lon (Pro 22:10 — scoffer's contemptuous verbal abuse), bosh (Psa 14:6 — wicked shaming the poor).

### CHAR-6 — Innocence as structural counter to shame

The inner moral-purity state that functions as the structural opposite and protection against shame — blameless conscience, clean inner will, and moral integrity as the inner condition that shields against guilt-shame, allows approach to God without shame, and whose absence sustains the shamed condition. Cross-register flag: M12 (innocence as primary purity register). Primary terms: niq.qa.von, qe.ha.von.

## §2 — Sub-group design

### M07-A — Shame as unjust inner wound

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1a — Shame as experienced inner state (unjust-wound register)

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where shame is experienced as an undeserved inner wound — felt by the innocent, the righteous, the suffering, the faithful. Shame here is not the consequence of personal moral failure but the inner impact of what is done to a person by enemies, circumstances, or the collapse of trust placed in unreliable human powers. The psalmist's perpetual covering of disgrace before enemies, the servant bearing reproach for God's sake, the righteous person protected from shame by divine refuge, the faithful people whose shame is promised removal by God — all evidence shame as an inner wound the person carries or is shielded from rather than deserves. The vocabulary of "covering" (shame as garment, as face-covering), "unjust exposure," "trust disappointed," and "divine lifting of shame" clusters here. Cross-register terms that route here include: the righteous person's posture against shame (aischunō: Phili 1:20, 1Pe 4:16), the apostles' shame for Christ (atimazō: Act 5:41), and the servant's silence under unjust disgrace (afōnos: Act 8:32 — cross-register M09 flag preserved).

### M07-B — Shame as moral consequence and judgment

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1b — Shame as experienced inner state (moral consequence / judgment register)

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where shame falls as the deserved, fitting outcome of moral failure, sin, idolatry, folly, or wickedness. Shame here is a moral verdict — the natural fruit of pride that collapses, the disgrace that coats those who commit abominations, the humiliation that overtakes enemies whose confidence in themselves or in idols is exposed as groundless. The absence of shame (inability to blush, the seared conscience) is also in this register — the moral collapse of those who can no longer feel the shame their conduct deserves. Corrective shame (shame invoked to produce moral change) belongs here as well — the community discipline producing shame in the disobedient. The vocabulary of "enemies put to shame," "idolaters confounded," "the wicked clothed with disgrace," and "no shame after abominations" clusters here.

### M07-C — Shame before God: conscience, guilt, and divine encounter

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1c — Shame as experienced inner state (vertical / divine-encounter register)

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where shame operates in the vertical relationship between the person and God — arising from guilt-consciousness before the holy, from covenant failure, or from the soul's exposure in the divine presence. Shame here is specifically the inner dynamic of the person standing before God: unable to lift the face (Ezr 9:6), recoiling at Christ's appearing (1Jo 2:28), protected from inner disgrace by faith in the cornerstone (1Pe 2:6; Rom 9:33), awakened to conscience by corrective shame (entropē: 1Cor 6:5; 15:34), silenced before atonement (Eze 16:63). The promise of not being put to shame in God's presence is the positive pole of this register. Shame as the fitting inner posture before divine grace ("be ashamed of your ways," Eze 36:32) and as the gateway to restored relationship with God (Eze 43:10-11) belong here.

### M07-D — Humiliation as enforced abasement

**Characteristic:** CHAR-2

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where the primary inner-being characteristic is the enforced downward movement of standing — humiliation through social demotion, military defeat, physical exposure, or divine judgment on pride. The mechanism of abasement is central: someone or something actively brings the proud, the strong, or the honoured low. The bodily and social visibility of the humiliation is prominent (shaved beards that prevent re-entry to public life; nakedness exposed before nations; the powerful descending to the pit bearing their shame). The vocabulary of "being brought low," "being made to descend," "nakedness exposed," "crown removed," and "pride laid low" clusters here. The enforced-abasement verses of ka.lam, bosh (military defeat and national collapse), ke.lim.mah (shame carried into death), and the silencing-as-humiliation register of fimoō (Mat 22:12, 22:34) all belong here.

### M07-E — Dishonour as relational worth-denial

**Characteristic:** CHAR-3

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where the primary inner-being characteristic is the relational denial of worth — treating persons as having less value than they possess, or stripping them of the honour that belongs to their standing. Dishonour here is a relational category: it operates between persons (or between person and God) as the failure or refusal to acknowledge worth. The absent regard of the unjust judge who neither fears God nor respects persons (entrepō: Luk 18:2, 18:4), the potter's assignment of vessels to dishonourable use (atimia: Rom 9:21), the covering of heads during prayer that signals disrespect toward divinely ordered authority (kataischunō: 1Cor 11:4-5), the disgrace of public exposure (paradeigmatizō: Heb 6:6 — willful betrayal dishonouring Christ). The worker protected from the dishonour of not receiving their due (fimoō: 1Cor 9:9, 1Ti 5:18) is the economic-justice edge of this register.

### M07-F — Shamefulness as moral-evaluative judgment

**Characteristic:** CHAR-4

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where the primary inner-being characteristic is the moral-evaluative judgment of what is shameful, indecent, or degraded — the conscience's recognition that certain conduct violates inner propriety and cannot bear scrutiny. This is the qualitative assessment of shame rather than the experienced state: actions are shameful (aischros — 1Cor 11:6, 14:35; Eph 5:12; Tit 1:11), deeds are indecent (aschēmosunē — Rom 1:27; Rev 16:15), desires are dishonorable (atimia — Rom 1:26), conduct is morally corrupt (aischunē — 2Cor 4:2). The inverted conscience that glories in what is actually shame (aischunē — Phili 3:19) and the perverted will that loves dishonour (qa.lon — Hos 4:18) belong here — these are the failure of the moral-evaluative faculty rather than the experience of shame itself. The idolatrous objects called "shameful things" (bo.shet — Jer 3:24, 11:13) and the degrading transformation into the shame-object's likeness (bo.shet — Hos 9:10) complete this register.

### M07-G — Shame produced by contempt and rejection

**Characteristic:** CHAR-5

**Cross-register flag:** M06 (contempt-projection as the source-side of this dynamic — the M06 and M07 registers are relational complements; Phase 9 T6 prompts should address this cross-cluster relationship)

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where shame is produced by contempt and rejection — the received face of dismissive devaluation. The mechanism is contempt (exoutheneō — all 11 verses: the dismissive inner attitude that treats others as of no worth); the characteristic within M07 is the shame that contempt inflicts on its recipient. Verbal contempt belongs here: reviling as a reputation-destroying attack (loidoria — 1Ti 5:14; 1Pe 3:9), hostile verbal contempt as public dishonour (kakologeō — Act 19:9), the scoffer's verbal abuse producing dishonour (qa.lon — Pro 22:10). The contemptuous treatment of the poor at the Lord's Supper (kataischunō — 1Cor 11:22) and the violent contempt of servants (atimazō — Mar 12:4; Luk 20:11) belong here. Protection from contempt-shaming of the vulnerable (ka.lam — Rut 2:15; 1Sa 25:7, 25:15) and the socially marginalised identity that contempt has created (Bartimaeus — apoballō: Mar 10:50) complete this sub-group.

### M07-H — Innocence as structural counter to shame

**Characteristic:** CHAR-6

**Cross-register flag:** M12 (innocence as primary purity register — these terms are in M07 because their verses engage the innocence-shame polarity; their primary purity content carries the M12 flag for Phase 9)

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses where inner moral innocence — clean conscience, blameless will, hands free of deliberate wrongdoing — functions as the structural protection against or counter to shame. Innocence here is not shame's opposite in a simple antonymic sense but its inner-being counterpart: the moral state that protects against guilt-shame (Gen 20:5 — Abimelech's innocent conscience as defence; Psa 26:6 — clean hands as basis for approaching God), that is expected to shield from shame-like suffering (Psa 73:13 — innocence maintained yet seemingly futile), and whose absence sustains the shamed condition before God (Hos 8:5 — incapacity for innocence keeping the people in disgrace). The innocence-shame polarity evidenced here is analytically significant for M07's understanding of shame's inner-being preconditions.

### M07-BOUNDARY — BOUNDARY terms pending researcher resolution

**Core description:** This sub-group holds the verses of terms whose Phase 3 verdict was BOUNDARY — terms whose cluster membership or verse-level meaning requires researcher decision before substantive sub-group assignment. Five BOUNDARY terms contribute to this sub-group:
- G2699 katatomē (1 verse — Phili 3:2): minimal inner-being content; cluster membership undecided
- H4893A mish.chat (1 verse — Isa 52:14): outward physical description; no inner shame content named by Pass A
- H5206 ni.dah (1 verse — Lam 1:8): M07/M12 boundary (filth producing shame outcome)
- H8213 sha.phel (24 verses): M07/M09 split — enforced humiliation of pride (M07) vs voluntary lowliness of spirit (M09); 24 verses; most analytically significant BOUNDARY item
- H8400 te.val.lul (0 verses): data gap — no Pass A meanings exist; CC verification of vc row state required
