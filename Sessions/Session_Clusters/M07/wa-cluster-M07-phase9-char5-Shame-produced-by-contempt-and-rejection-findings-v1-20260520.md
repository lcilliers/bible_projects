## T1 — Defining the Characteristic

**T1.1.1 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — "Shame produced by contempt and rejection." The name identifies: (1) the characteristic experience ("shame"); (2) its specific mechanism ("produced by contempt and rejection" — not by guilt, not by social demotion, but by the attitude of contemptuous dismissal or active rejection directed at the person). The name positions CHAR-5 as the received face of the contempt-shame dynamic whose source-side (contempt-projection) is M06's territory. CHAR-5 holds what the person experiences when contempt is directed at them.

---

**T1.1.2 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Exoutheneō (G1848): the primary term — to treat as of no account, to utterly despise, to regard as nothing. Root: ex (out of) + ouden (nothing) = to make out of nothing. This is the strongest contempt-vocabulary in the NT: the person being treated as if they were literally nothing. Atimazō (G0818) in VCG-02: to dishonour through violent contemptuous action. Loidoria (G3059): verbal reviling as reputation-attack. The root meanings together reveal CHAR-5's source-mechanism: the contempt-er assigns zero worth to the contempt-ed, producing shame in the recipient by the force of that worth-cancellation.

---

**T1.1.3 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 is relational by definition — it always arises from the interaction between a contempt-er and a contempt-ed. Directionally: contempt moves from the dismisser toward the dismissed, producing shame in the recipient. Constitutional implication: CHAR-5 engages the person's sense of worth (the inner faculty that registers their standing before others), which is affected by the contempt-verdict even when that verdict is false.

---

**T1.2.1 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 is primarily a condition produced by an external agent's act (contempt). The person does not produce CHAR-5 in themselves; it is inflicted upon them by another's dismissive attitude or active rejection. In VCG-01 (dismissive inner attitude), the contempt is internal to the contempt-er but produces an external social effect — the recipient is treated as of no account. In VCG-02 (violent rejection), the contempt is actively enacted. In VCG-03 (verbal contempt), contempt is communicated through speech.

---

**T1.2.2 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Three constituent elements: (1) Dismissive contempt as inner attitude (VCG-01): the inner disposition of treating others as of no account, producing shame through exclusion and devaluation. (2) Active contempt through violence, mockery, and rejection (VCG-02): contempt enacted through physical violence, public mocking, or willful rejection. (3) Verbal contempt and reputation attack (VCG-03): contempt communicated through reviling, hostile speech, slanderous verbal attack — shame produced through the destruction of the person's reputation.

---

**T1.2.3 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Shame produced by contempt and rejection is the condition of being treated as of diminished or no worth by another's active contempt — whether through dismissive inner attitude (VCG-01), violent mockery and rejection (VCG-02), or reputation-destroying speech (VCG-03). The mechanism is contempt (M06's characteristic); the characteristic within M07 is the shame produced in the recipient by that contempt. Cross-register note: M06 (contempt-projection) is the source-side; CHAR-5 is the received face of the same dynamic. Primary vocabulary: exoutheneō (the dominant term across VCG-01's 11 verses); atimazō (Mar 12:4; Luk 20:11 in VCG-02); loidoria (1Ti 5:14; 1Pe 3:9 in VCG-03).

---

**T1.3.1 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — The structural opposite of CHAR-5 is acceptance and worth-recognition — the act of receiving another as of genuine value. Gal 4:14 (vc=54647) names the positive opposite directly: "you did not scorn or despise me, but received me as an angel of God, as Christ Jesus." The Galatians' acceptance of Paul despite his weakness is the structural opposite of contempt-producing-shame.

---

**T1.3.2 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 excludes the genuine worth-recognition that should accompany the person. The commands against despising (Rom 14:3 — "do not despise the one who abstains"; 1Cor 16:11 — "let no one despise him"; 1Th 5:20 — "do not despise prophecies") reveal what CHAR-5 excludes: the recognition that what is being dismissed has genuine worth that the contemptuous attitude cancels.

---

**T1.3.3 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 is not CHAR-2 (enforced humiliation of standing): contempt-produced shame is the received experience of worth-cancellation, not a socially enacted downward movement of standing (though contempt can produce both simultaneously). CHAR-5 is not CHAR-3 (dishonour as relational worth-denial): contempt is a specific form of worth-denial; CHAR-3's worth-denial can arise from other causes (favoritism, sovereign assignment). CHAR-5 is the received shame, not the contempt itself (that is M06).

---

**T1.4.1 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Three operational modes (VCGs 01-03): (1) Dismissive inner attitude (VCG-01): the inner disposition of treating others as of no account — self-righteous contempt (Luk 18:9), contempt toward fellow believers over food practices (Rom 14:3; 14:10), contemptuous low regard for certain members (1Cor 6:4). (2) Active contempt through violence and mockery (VCG-02): the contempt is enacted physically — servants beaten (Mar 12:4; Luk 20:11), Jesus mocked (Luk 23:11), Jesus rejected by the builders (Act 4:11), Bartimaeus's beggar-identity formed by contempt (Mar 10:50). (3) Verbal contempt (VCG-03): reputation-destroying speech — reviling (1Ti 5:14; 1Pe 3:9), hostile verbal contempt (Act 19:9), the scoffer's verbal dishonour (Pro 22:10).

---

**T1.4.2 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 operates differently in each VCG. In VCG-01, the shame is produced through gradual social devaluation — the cumulative effect of being treated as of no account over time. In VCG-02, the shame arrives suddenly through violent or dramatic public contempt — these are acute events. In VCG-03, the shame is produced through speech and its effect on reputation — a more sustained, public, and socially mediated form.

---

**T1.4.3 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 has a specific speech-based mode: VCG-03 is constituted by verbal contempt. The reviling (loidoria) is the speech-act; the reputation-attack is the effect. 1Pe 3:9 (vc=37632) — "do not repay evil for evil or reviling for reviling, but on the contrary bless" — shows the speech-based form of CHAR-5 and the appropriate speech-based counter-response. The inner will is directed away from retaliatory verbal attack toward blessing.

Psa 14:6 (vc=65498) — "shame the plans of the poor" — shows CHAR-5 targeting the inner confidence and hope of the poor. Bartimaeus's beggar-identity (Mar 10:50) shows the accumulated inner-being effect of long-sustained contempt-shame: his identity has been formed around the shame-condition produced by society's contempt. 1Pe 3:9's call to bless rather than retaliate shows the acute immediate pull toward retaliatory speech when verbal contempt is received.

## T7 — Lexical, Literary, and Scientific Analysis

**T7.1.1 — (no inline label)**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Exoutheneō (G1848): root ex + ouden (nothing) = to treat as nothing. This is the most radical contempt-vocabulary in the NT: the zero-worth assignment. Atimazō (G0818): to dishonour through contemptuous action; root a-timē (without honour). Loidoria (G3059): verbal reviling, reputation-attack through hostile speech. The roots together reveal CHAR-5's mechanism: the contempt-er assigns zero worth (exoutheneō), strips honour through contemptuous treatment (atimazō), or attacks reputation through speech (loidoria). The shame produced in the recipient is the inner experience of having one's worth cancelled by another's active dismissal.
