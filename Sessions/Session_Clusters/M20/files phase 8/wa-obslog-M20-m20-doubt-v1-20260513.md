### Step 1 — Read all glosses against T1 characteristic definition

Reading the 14 glosses cold, without reference to inherited VCG labels or prior findings, looking for natural characteristic groupings:

**Anxiety / Worry cluster:** to worry (merimnaō), concern (merimna), be anxious (da.ag)
These three share the same inner territory: the inner person preoccupied with threatening or uncertain circumstances — an affective-cognitive state that turns the person's attention inward toward perceived threat or loss. Constitutional location: mind/heart. Faculties engaged: thought, feeling, attention. Impact: distraction from present reality, displacement of trust. Structural opposite: peace, trust, contentment. Causal direction: toward anticipated threat or loss.

**Despair / Hopelessness cluster:** to despair (ya.ash H), to despair (exaporeō G), to despair (apelpizo G)
Three terms glossed "to despair." All name the collapse of inner expectation or forward orientation. Constitutional location: heart/soul. Faculties engaged: will, feeling, forward-orientation. Impact: cessation of effort, giving up. Structural opposite: hope. Causal direction: arising from overwhelming circumstances or accumulated futility.

However: apelpizo warrants immediate scrutiny. Its Mounce definition is "to expect nothing in return" and its sole verse (Luk 6:35) is a positive generosity text — the absence of self-seeking as posture of love. This is not despair as a distress state. It does not share the T1 profile of the other two despair terms. → FLAG for potential BOUNDARY or cluster reassignment.

**Discouragement / Loss of heart cluster:** to lose heart (ekkakeō), be disheartened (ka.ah), disheartened (ka.eh), be discouraged (athumeō), fainthearted (oligopsuchos)
These five share the profile of inner courage failing — the person's inner resolve or strength diminishing under external pressure, suffering, or provocation. Constitutional location: heart (the seat of courage/resolve). Faculties engaged: will, feeling, agency. Impact: withdrawal from action, need for encouragement. Structural opposite: courage, resolve, perseverance. Causal direction: produced by opposition, suffering, or provocation.

Note: ka.ah and ka.eh are a verb/adjective pair from the same root — near synonyms. They likely warrant the same sub-group.
Note: athumeō's legacy registry is R035 (covetousness) — a striking mismatch; its gloss and its one verse (Col 3:21) are clearly discouragement vocabulary. Legacy registry assignment appears erroneous.

**Doubt cluster:** to doubt (distazō), double-minded (dipsuchos)
Both name inner division or wavering — failure of single-minded commitment or trust. However, their profiles diverge on examination:
- distazō: faith wavering between trust and hesitation — an epistemological/trust failure in a specific moment (Mat 14:31, Mat 28:17)
- dipsuchos: divided allegiance between God and world — a dispositional moral-character pathology, not a momentary wavering

These share the surface of "division" but may describe different faculties and different causal directions. dipsuchos has strong prior findings pointing it toward moral-character and soul registries (182-SD005, DIM-112-002). → Keep provisionally together but flag tension for Phase 4 control read.
