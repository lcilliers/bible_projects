## §B — Pass-B VCG design (revised)

### VCG-B-A1: Anxiety as choking weight — displacing spiritual receptivity and fruitfulness

**Dimension:** The primary distress register — anxiety (with riches, desires, pleasures) operating as a smothering force that prevents the word from taking root and producing fruit. The heart is weighed down. No volitional choice depicted; this is a condition that descends and operates without the person choosing it.

**Member verses:** Mat 13:22 · Mar 4:19 · Luk 8:14 · Luk 21:34 (4 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 13:22 — the parable of the thorns names the phenomenon most analytically; the word is received but choked by cares operating in concert with other disordering forces.

---

### VCG-B-A2: Anxiety about material provision — futility of worried self-securing

**Dimension:** Anxious preoccupation with food, drink, clothing, the body, tomorrow — material self-securing as the object. Addressed with a futility argument (cannot add a single hour) and a theological alternative (prayer, trust in God's provision). The command form implies the person participates in maintaining the state.

**Member verses:** Mat 6:25 · Mat 6:27 · Mat 6:28 · Mat 6:31 · Mat 6:34 · Luk 12:22 · Luk 12:25 · Luk 12:26 · Phili 4:6 (9 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 6:25 — the foundational statement: life and body named as objects; the invitation to trust named as the alternative.

---

### VCG-B-A3: Anticipatory anxiety about speech and defence under threat

**Dimension:** Anxiety directed toward a specific future performance situation — what to say before authorities, how to defend oneself. Anticipatory and specific in object; the remedy named is Spirit-provision in the moment.

**Member verses:** Mat 10:19 · Luk 12:11 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 10:19 — the command not to be anxious about speech before authorities; the Spirit named as the remedy.

---

### VCG-B-A4: Anxiety as proliferation — the inner person scattered across many things

**Dimension:** Anxiety spreading across multiplicity, producing a troubled and scattered inner state. Jesus explicitly names the phenomenon (anxious about many things) and its structural contrast (the one-thing orientation). This is the attentional-proliferation face of anxiety — the inner person unable to gather itself because it is pulled in too many directions simultaneously.

**Member verses:** Luk 10:41 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** Luk 10:41 — Jesus directly names and diagnoses the inner state; a prime verse for the proliferation dimension.

---

### VCG-B-A5: The anxiety-faculty rightly directed — merimnaō as the substance of love and devotion

**Dimension:** The positive-register dimension of the characteristic. The same word (merimnaō) used for destructive anxiety also names the intensity of directed concern for God's things, for God's pleasure, and for others' welfare. This illuminates what anxiety misdirects: the inner faculty of directed attentive concern is in itself the faculty of love. When turned toward God or neighbour it is devotion and care; when turned toward temporal self-securing it becomes anxiety. The characteristic is thus revealed as directional — not a pathology of the faculty itself but a pathology of its object.

**Member verses:** 1Cor 7:32 · 1Cor 7:33 · 1Cor 7:34 · 1Cor 12:25 · Phili 2:20 (5 verses)

**Anchor:** 1Cor 7:32 — the unmarried man merimnaō-ing about the Lord's things names the positive pole most directly; the same verb, the same intensity, directed toward God.

---

### VCG-B-A6: Relational anxiety — worried concern for an absent or endangered person

**Dimension:** da.ag naming a parent's worried, troubled attention toward an absent child. The object is relational and personal. The anxiety is voiced as a question seeking resolution. This is anxiety as the dark face of relational love — the same care that binds a parent to a child becomes anxiety when the child is absent or at risk.

**Member verses:** 1Sa 9:5 · 1Sa 10:2 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** 1Sa 10:2 — the father's voiced distress ("what shall I do about my son?") most directly evidences relational anxiety seeking but not finding resolution.

---

### VCG-B-A7: Anxiety in the face of concrete external threats — and its structural opposite

**Dimension:** da.ag applied to anxiety about real, specific, named dangers — enemies, sword, famine. Two sub-dimensions present: (a) anxiety producing moral failure (Isa 57:11: dread leads to lying); (b) anxiety as the expected response to genuine threat (Jer 38:19, 42:16). The structural-opposite verse (Jer 17:8) is analytically essential: the tree that is not anxious in genuine drought defines the characteristic by showing its absence in the same circumstances that would normally produce it. Rootedness in God creates non-anxiety under genuine threat — making this the most precise positive statement of what the characteristic, at its core, is.

**Member verses:** Isa 57:11 · Jer 38:19 · Jer 42:16 · Jer 17:8 (4 verses)

**Anchor:** Jer 17:8 — the structural-opposite verse; the clearest statement of what anxiety is in the OT, shown by its absence. (Secondary anchor: Jer 38:19 for the distress register.)

---

### VCG-B-A8: Anxiety to be cast onto God — the commanded transfer grounded in divine care

**Dimension:** The person currently carrying real anxieties is commanded to cast them onto God, with the theological ground: he cares for you. The verse presupposes the person is already in the anxious state and names the remedy as relational — a transfer of weight from the person to God, grounded not in effort but in God's active care.

**Member verses:** 1Pe 5:7 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** 1Pe 5:7 (only verse)

---

### VCG-B-A9: Pastoral burden — anxiety as the weight of love carried by the one who cares

**Dimension:** merimna naming the daily burden of apostolic love — Paul's anxiety for all the churches as the cost of caring for those entrusted to him. This is the positive-burden dimension: not disordered self-concern but the weight that comes from genuine love and responsibility. Complements VCG-B-A5 (directed attentive care) by showing care as something borne over time, not merely directed in the moment.

**Member verses:** 2Cor 11:28 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** 2Cor 11:28 (only verse)

---

### VCG-B-A10: Anxiety as the fuel of contrition — da.ag driving sorrow over sin

**Dimension:** da.ag naming the inner energy of genuine contrition — the anxious, troubled quality of the inner person who acknowledges sin. The anxiety here is not directed outward toward threat but inward toward personal wrongdoing, becoming the affective force that drives the person to confession. This illuminates anxiety as a faculty that, when rightly directed toward one's own sin, fuels genuine sorrow and repentance rather than disordered worry.

**Member verses:** Psa 38:18 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** Psa 38:18 (only verse)
