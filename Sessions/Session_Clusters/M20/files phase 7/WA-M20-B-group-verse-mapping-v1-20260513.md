## §B — Pass-B VCG design

### VCG-B-B1: Despair as the limit of inner endurance — capacity exceeded and the edge not crossed

**Dimension:** Two faces of the outer limit of bearable inner experience. 2Cor 1:8: the capacity exceeded — burdened beyond strength, despaired of life itself; the inner person can no longer hold. 2Cor 4:8: the edge approached and not crossed — perplexed but not driven to despair; the person held short of the terminus. Together they define the outer boundary of the characteristic: what it looks like when it is reached, and what it looks like when the person is sustained short of it.

**Member verses:** 2Cor 1:8 · 2Cor 4:8 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** 2Cor 1:8 — despair of life itself; the most total and direct statement of despair as capacity-exceeded.

---

### VCG-B-B2: Despair as volitional act — surrender, exhausted pursuit, and the inner signature it leaves

**Dimension:** ya.ash as something the inner person enacts or reaches through a process. Three sub-dimensions held together: (a) the heart actively handed over (Ecc 2:20 — volitional surrender); (b) despair as the natural terminus of exhausted pursuit (1Sa 27:1 — the logic of giving up when options run out); (c) despair producing a recognisable quality of speech and thought (Job 6:26 — the despairing person's words are wind, bearing the mark of the state). All three evidence despair as an active inner movement — not merely suffered but enacted or reached.

**Member verses:** Ecc 2:20 · 1Sa 27:1 · Job 6:26 (3 verses)

**Anchor:** Ecc 2:20 — "gave my heart up to despair" — the volitional act most directly named; the heart is the agent of surrender.

---

### VCG-B-B3: Despair as declaration — the word spoken or withheld

**Dimension:** ya.ash as a verbal act — the declaration of hopelessness that performs and hardens the inner state. Two poles: Isa 57:10 (the declaration not made — "you did not say 'it is hopeless'"; the characteristic illuminated by its resistance, the person who was weary but did not speak the word of capitulation) and Jer 2:25 (the declaration made — "it is hopeless, I have loved foreigners"; the word spoken that closes off the path of return). Together they show despair as a speech-act with consequences for the inner person's future orientation.

**Member verses:** Isa 57:10 · Jer 2:25 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** Jer 2:25 — the declaration made and its consequence most directly evidenced; the word of hopelessness spoken as a self-closing statement.

---

### VCG-B-B4: Despair weaponised — hopelessness deployed to justify rebellion

**Dimension:** ya.ash used not as an overwhelming inner experience but as a strategic posture — the people say "that is in vain" to foreclose the call to repentance and justify continuing in their own plans. The language of despair (futility, hopelessness) is deployed to close down the possibility of change. This is the morally darkest dimension of the characteristic: despair as a weapon the inner person uses against its own potential for transformation.

**Member verses:** Jer 18:12 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** Jer 18:12 (only verse)
