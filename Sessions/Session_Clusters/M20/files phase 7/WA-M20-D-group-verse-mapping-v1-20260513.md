## §B — Pass-B VCG design

### VCG-D-D1: Double-mindedness as dispositional indecision — the divided inner person unstable in all ways

**Dimension:** dipsuchos as a dispositional state — the inner person not settling, not committing, hedging between orientations. Two faces: consequence (Jam 1:8 — unstable in all his ways; the division produces total pervasive instability) and remedy (Jam 4:8 — purify your hearts, you double-minded; the heart is the seat where the division resides and where it must be resolved). The characteristic is structural rather than situational — it shapes all of life's orientation, not merely one decision.

**Member verses:** Jam 4:8 · Jam 1:8 (2 verses)  
**Anchor:** Jam 1:8 — the consequence most directly evidences the inner state: double-mindedness produces instability in all ways.

---

### VCG-D-D2: Doubt as situational faith-wavering — failing to move from seeing to trusting

**Dimension:** distazō as faith failing to complete the movement from seeing to trusting in a specific encounter moment. Both verses place doubt in the presence of the miraculous: Peter on water (Mat 14:31) and disciples at the resurrection (Mat 28:17). In Mat 14:31 Jesus names the doubt, questions it, and acts — the remedy is immediate and physical (his hand). In Mat 28:17 the doubt coexists with worship — the same group, the same sight, produces both responses. Doubt here is not incompatible with being present and responding, only with completing the move into full commitment and trust.

**Member verses:** Mat 14:31 · Mat 28:17 (2 verses)  
**Anchor:** Mat 14:31 — Jesus names, questions, and remedies the doubt in a single verse; the situational-wavering phenomenon most directly evidenced.
