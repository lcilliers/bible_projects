## 2. T1 framework applied

A distinct inner-being characteristic requires:

1. Identifiable constitutional location (heart, mind, will, conscience, soul, spirit, or body)
2. A distinguishable set of inner faculties engaged (perception, thought, memory, feeling, conscience, will, agency)
3. A recognisable impact — definable effect on the person, relationships, or action
4. A structural opposite — named or implicit
5. A distinguishing causal direction or directional object

## 3. Provisional sub-groups

### M20-A — Anxiety and Worry

**Terms:** H1672 da.ag · G3309 merimnaō · G3308 merimna

**Characteristic:** The inner person in a state of anxious preoccupation — thought and feeling directed toward anticipated threat, uncertain outcomes, or unmet needs. The characteristic is an affective-cognitive orientation that displaces trust and peace, turning the person's inner attention toward what may go wrong or be lost.

**T1 test:**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 1. Constitutional location | Mind and heart — the seat of thought and affective orientation |
| 2. Faculties engaged | Thought (preoccupation), feeling (affective anxiety), attention (orientation toward threat) |
| 3. Impact | Displacement of trust; spiritual unfruitfulness (merimna choking the word, Luk 8:14); bodily/practical burden; inhibition of prayer |
| 4. Structural opposite | Peace (eirēnē), trust/faith, contentment (amerimnos — untroubled) |
| 5. Causal direction | Oriented toward anticipated threat, uncertain future, unmet material need, or relational concern |

**Result: PASSES T1**

**Notes:** merimnaō carries a dual register noted in prior findings (DIM-7-001) — anxious worry (2709-001/002) and volitional directed care (2709-003, e.g. 1Cor 12:25; Phi 2:20). The positive-care register may require its own VCG within M20-A or may surface a BOUNDARY question for Phase 4. Flagged for Phase 4 scrutiny.

### M20-B — Despair and Hopelessness

**Terms:** H2976 ya.ash · G1820 exaporeō

**Characteristic:** The collapse of the inner person's forward-orientation — the state in which hope and expectation are exhausted and the person gives up the pursuit of a good outcome. The characteristic is not merely negative feeling but the volitional-affective act of ceasing to expect, to pursue, or to sustain effort.

**T1 test:**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 1. Constitutional location | Heart and soul — the seat of hope and forward-reaching desire |
| 2. Faculties engaged | Will (cessation of pursuit), feeling (affective collapse), forward-orientation (the capacity to expect) |
| 3. Impact | Giving up of effort or expectation; declaration of futility; inner person brought beyond what it can sustain (exaporeō: 2Cor 1:8, 4:8) |
| 4. Structural opposite | Hope (elpis, tiqvah); expectation; perseverance |
| 5. Causal direction | Arising from overwhelming external circumstances, accumulated futility, or the perception that nothing more is possible |

**Result: PASSES T1**

**Note on corpus size:** ya.ash has 6 verses; exaporeō has 2. The combined sub-group corpus is 8 verses. Statistical patterns will be limited (data-quality flags already noted for both terms). Findings must be tightly verse-grounded.

### M20-C — Discouragement and Loss of Heart

**Terms:** G1573 ekkakeō · H3512A ka.ah · H3512B ka.eh · G0120 athumeō · G3642 oligopsuchos

**Characteristic:** The inner person's courage and resolve diminishing under sustained opposition, suffering, or provocation — the state of having lost heart, becoming faint in spirit, or falling into discouragement. The characteristic is distinguished from despair by its relational and recoverable quality: these terms consistently appear alongside commands or provisions for encouragement and restoration.

**T1 test:**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 1. Constitutional location | Heart (the seat of courage and resolve — lev, kardia, thumos) |
| 2. Faculties engaged | Will (resolve weakening), feeling (affective discouragement), agency (diminished capacity for action) |
| 3. Impact | Withdrawal from active engagement; vulnerability to giving up; need for external encouragement from others or from God |
| 4. Structural opposite | Courage (tharseō), resolve, perseverance; parakaleō (encouragement) appears as the active counter in multiple contexts |
| 5. Causal direction | Produced by external opposition, suffering, tribulation, or provocation — the characteristic is responsive to external pressure, not self-generated |

**Result: PASSES T1**

**Internal notes:**
- ka.ah (verb) and ka.eh (adjective) share the same Hebrew root. They will share a VCG within M20-C; their shared verse (Psa 109:16, Eze 13:22, Dan 11:30) is already reflected in the data.
- athumeō's legacy registry is R035 (covetousness) — an apparent misclassification. The gloss, Mounce definition, and its sole verse (Col 3:21: children provoked to discouragement) all place it firmly in the discouragement characteristic. This will be corrected through the sub-group assignment directive.
- oligopsuchos has only 1 verse (1Th 5:14). Finding grounding will be minimal.

### M20-D — Doubt and Inner Division (provisional)

**Terms:** G1365 distazō · G1374 dipsuchos

**Characteristic (provisional):** The inner person failing to maintain single-minded orientation — either wavering between trust and hesitation in a specific moment (distazō) or sustaining a divided allegiance between God and the world as a dispositional pattern (dipsuchos). Both name a failure of inner unity, though they differ in temporal scale and causal structure.

**T1 test:**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 1. Constitutional location | Mind and soul (nous, psuchē) — the seat of orientation and allegiance |
| 2. Faculties engaged | Thought (epistemological wavering — distazō), conscience and will (moral-dispositional split — dipsuchos) |
| 3. Impact | Instability in all ways (dipsuchos: Jam 1:8); failure of faith-action (distazō: Mat 14:31); inability to receive from God (Jam 1:7) |
| 4. Structural opposite | Single-mindedness, whole-heartedness, faith (pistis); isopsuchos (like-minded, harmonious — the positive counterpart to dipsuchos) |
| 5. Causal direction | distazō: situational — triggered by threatening circumstances that expose faith's limits. dipsuchos: dispositional — the person whose loyalty is structurally divided. ⚠ These differ. |

**Result: PROVISIONALLY PASSES T1 — causal-direction tension noted**

**Tension flagged for Phase 4:** distazō and dipsuchos share the surface of "inner division" but their profiles diverge at criterion 5. distazō is an epistemological/affective failure in a specific crisis moment. dipsuchos is a dispositional moral-character pathology with prior findings linking it strongly to the moral-conscience cluster (DIM-112-002; SD pointers 182-SD005, DIM-112-SD005). Phase 4 control read must assess whether these genuinely belong in the same sub-group, or whether dipsuchos warrants BOUNDARY or cluster reassignment. Researcher confirmed (OQ-002): retain together for Phase 4 to decide.

**Note on corpus size:** distazō has 2 verses; dipsuchos has 2 verses. Combined corpus of 4 verses. This is the smallest sub-group in the cluster.

## 4. BOUNDARY terms

**Why BOUNDARY:** na.dad's primary semantic field is physical wandering, flight, and dispersal. Phase 2 UT review set aside all 13 untouched verses as physical-movement uses with no inner-being content. The root family is NADAD (filth); related words are in the impurity/filth semantic field. Legacy registry is R086 (impurity), not a negative-affect cluster. The inherited group (5571-001) frames it as "inner experience of wandering" — but this framing requires validation against the G-status verses before it can be confirmed. Cluster fit is unconfirmed.

**Phase 4 action required:** Read all G-status verses for na.dad. Determine whether any carry genuine inner-being content at the term level, or whether the term is consistently operating in physical/external registers. If physical: recommend cluster reassignment. If inner-being content is evidenced: promote to an appropriate sub-group or confirm BOUNDARY with VCG.

### G0560 apelpizo — to despair

**Why BOUNDARY:** The gloss "to despair" conflicts with both the Mounce definition ("to expect nothing in return") and the term's sole verse (Luk 6:35: "lend, expecting nothing in return"). The inherited group (402-001) frames apelpizo as "absence of self-directed hope as posture of genuine love" — a positive characteristic, not a distress state. This term does not share the inner-being profile of M20-B (despair as collapse of hope). It carries prior findings from the kindness registry (OBS-099 series) framing it as part of the generous love vocabulary.

**Phase 4 action required:** Read Luk 6:35 in full context. Confirm whether apelpizo in this verse carries any negative-affect inner-being content, or whether it is functioning as a positive volitional-dispositional term. If the latter: recommend cluster reassignment — apelpizo likely belongs in a love/generosity cluster, not M20.
