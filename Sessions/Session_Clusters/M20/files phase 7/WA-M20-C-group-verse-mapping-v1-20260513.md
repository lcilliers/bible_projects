## §B — Pass-B VCG design

### VCG-C-C1: Discouragement produced by interpersonal provocation

**Dimension:** The relational face of discouragement — the inner state of discouragement created by a specific act of provocation. The causal chain is explicit and interpersonal: one person's action directly produces the inner state of another. This is discouragement inflicted through relationship.

**Member verses:** Col 3:21 (1 verse)  
**Anchor:** Col 3:21 (only verse). Term: athumeō

---

### VCG-C-C2: Discouragement inflicted on the vulnerable — targeted by cruelty and deceptive speech

**Dimension:** The disheartened person as the specific object of cruelty or deception. Two instances: the brokenhearted pursued to death (Psa 109:16), and false prophets deliberately disheartening the righteous through false speech (Eze 13:22). In both cases the inner state of the victim makes them a target rather than a random casualty. Discouragement is inflicted, not incidentally produced.

**Member verses:** Psa 109:16 · Eze 13:22 (2 verses — shared between ka.ah and ka.eh)  
**Anchor:** Eze 13:22 — the deliberate, deceptive disheartening of the righteous most directly evidences the inflicted-on-the-vulnerable phenomenon.  
**Terms:** ka.ah + ka.eh (both terms present in both verses — dual-term VCG)

---

### VCG-C-C3: Discouragement producing inner retreat — loss of resolve under pressure

**Dimension:** The king who, facing superior military force, withdraws and turns back. Loss of resolve expressed immediately in outward action — the inner state (fear, discouragement) and the behavioural consequence (retreat) are named in the same verse. The inner and the outer collapse together.

**Member verses:** Dan 11:30 (1 verse — shared between ka.ah and ka.eh)  
**Anchor:** Dan 11:30 (only verse). Terms: ka.ah + ka.eh

---

### VCG-C-C4: The fainthearted soul — diminished courage requiring encouragement

**Dimension:** oligopsuchos as the soul (psuchē) reduced in its inner capacity for courage and endurance. The term is etymological: oligos (little/few) + psuchē (soul) — small-souled. The prescribed response is paraklēsis (encouragement, being drawn alongside), not correction or practical assistance. The characteristic is located specifically in the soul's diminished capacity, not merely in circumstance.

**Member verses:** 1Th 5:14 (1 verse)  
**Anchor:** 1Th 5:14 (only verse). Term: oligopsuchos

---

### VCG-C-C5: Loss of heart in sustained effort — long-haul endurance failing under temporal frustration

**Dimension:** ekkakeō as the failure of perseverance in contexts of sustained effort without visible return — persistent prayer (Luk 18:1), apostolic ministry (2Cor 4:1), doing good over time (Gal 6:9; 2Th 3:13), bearing another's suffering (Eph 3:13). The discouragement accumulates from ongoing effort without present fruit, not from a single crisis. The antidotes are theological (mercy received, future harvest, inner renewal) rather than circumstantial — relief does not come from circumstances changing but from the person's orientation being redirected to what is not yet visible.

**Member verses:** Luk 18:1 · 2Cor 4:1 · Gal 6:9 · Eph 3:13 · 2Th 3:13 (5 verses)  
**Anchor:** Luk 18:1 — the connection between loss of heart and the failure of persistent prayer is the most analytically direct statement of the characteristic in this context.  
Term: ekkakeō

---

### VCG-C-C6: Loss of heart and the inner person's renewal — the outer/inner distinction

**Dimension:** 2Cor 4:16 makes the analytically precise inner-being statement: the outer person wastes away while the inner person is renewed day by day. Loss of heart is named as specifically an inner-person risk — one that physical deterioration cannot produce when the inner person is being renewed by God. This is the only verse in M20-C that explicitly locates the characteristic in the inner person and names the renewal mechanism that counters it.

**Member verses:** 2Cor 4:16 (1 verse)  
**Anchor:** 2Cor 4:16 (only verse). Term: ekkakeō

---

### VCG-C-C7: The crushed and sinking — total inner overwhelm of the helpless

**Dimension:** ka.ah in its most acute form — the helpless person crushed, sinking down, and falling under superior might. The imagery is physical but the referent is the inner state of the overwhelmed victim: complete collapse, with no capacity for resistance. The perspective is entirely from within the devastated inner person, not from the action of the aggressor.

**Member verses:** Psa 10:10 (1 verse)  
**Anchor:** Psa 10:10 (only verse). Term: ka.ah
