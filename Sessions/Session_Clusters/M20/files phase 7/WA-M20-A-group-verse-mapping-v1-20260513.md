## §B — Pass-B VCG design (fresh from meanings)

### VCG-B-A1: Anxiety as choking weight on the inner person — displacing spiritual receptivity

**Phenomenon:** anxiety (together with riches, desires, pleasures) operating as a smothering force on the heart that prevents the word from taking root and producing fruit. The person receives the word but cannot complete the process — anxiety chokes the inner person's productive capacity. No volitional choice is depicted; this is a condition that descends on the person.

**Member verses:** Mat 13:22 · Mar 4:19 · Luk 8:14 · Luk 21:34 (4 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 13:22 — the thorns-and-cares parable names the phenomenon most directly and analytically; the word is received but choked.

**Per-verse note:**
- Mat 13:22: cares + deceitfulness of riches — the parable's explicit naming of the pair that chokes
- Mar 4:19: cares + riches + desires — the triad that operates as a choking force
- Luk 8:14: cares + riches + pleasures — fruitfulness prevented, not reception; the word is heard but does not mature
- Luk 21:34: the heart weighed down — the weight image; hearts dulled to the coming day

---

### VCG-B-A2: Anxiety about material provision — futility of worried self-securing

**Phenomenon:** anxious preoccupation with food, drink, clothing, the body, tomorrow — the person's inner attention turned toward material self-securing. Jesus addresses it with a futility argument (cannot add a single hour) and a theological alternative (prayer, trust in God's provision). The command form implies the person participates in the state and can be turned from it.

**Member verses:** Mat 6:25 · Mat 6:27 · Mat 6:28 · Mat 6:31 · Mat 6:34 · Luk 12:22 · Luk 12:25 · Luk 12:26 · Phili 4:6 (9 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 6:25 — the foundational statement of the teaching; life and body named as objects, the alternative framed.

**Per-verse note:**
- Mat 6:25: life, food, drink, body — the objects of material anxiety listed
- Mat 6:27 / Luk 12:25: the futility argument — anxiety cannot change outcomes
- Mat 6:28: clothing — extends the objects to all bodily covering
- Mat 6:31: triple repetition of the command — anxiety as persistent temptation
- Mat 6:34: tomorrow — anxiety as temporal over-reach
- Luk 12:22–26: parallel; discipleship context
- Phili 4:6: the summary remedy — prayer and supplication as the alternative to anxiety about anything

---

### VCG-B-A3: Anticipatory anxiety about speech and defence under threat

**Phenomenon:** anxiety directed toward a specific future performance situation — what to say before authorities, how to defend oneself. The anxiety is anticipatory and specific in object. The remedy named is Spirit-provision in the moment, not preparation.

**Member verses:** Mat 10:19 · Luk 12:11 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** Mat 10:19 — the explicit command not to be anxious about what to say when delivered to authorities.

---

### VCG-B-A4: Anxiety as attentional scattering — the inner person pulled by multiplicity

**Phenomenon:** anxiety as the scattering of inner attention across many things, producing a troubled and distracted inner state. Jesus names the phenomenon explicitly ("anxious about many things") and contrasts it with the one-thing orientation of the non-anxious person. The phenomenon is attentional, not primarily affective.

**Member verses:** Luk 10:41 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** Luk 10:41 — the only verse; Jesus directly names and diagnoses the inner state.

**Note:** thin corpus (1 verse). May be merged with VCG-B-A3 in Phase 7 if the researcher determines the attentional-scattering and performance-anxiety phenomena are not sufficiently distinct for separate VCGs. Flagged.

---

### VCG-B-A5: Directed positive care — merimnaō in the attentive-concern register

**Phenomenon:** merimnaō naming directed attentive concern — for the Lord's things, for God's pleasure, for others' welfare. The inner state is ordered care, not disordered anxiety. The same verb that describes trapped material worry also describes the apostle's concern for others and the unmarried person's attention to God.

**Member verses:** 1Cor 7:32 · 1Cor 7:33 · 1Cor 7:34 · 1Cor 12:25 · Phili 2:20 (5 verses)

**Anchor:** 1Cor 7:32 — the unmarried man anxious about the Lord's things names the positive-directed-care register most explicitly.

**⚠ FLAG — OQ-007:** These verses do not carry the anxiety characteristic of M20-A. The inner-being content is directed, ordered care — positive orientation toward God or others. They may be candidates for wrong-face set-aside (inner-being content carried by a different register of the term) or for transfer to a care/love cluster. **Researcher decision required before Phase 7 directive is scoped.**

---

### VCG-B-A6: Anxiety about a named person's welfare — relational worry from absence or danger

**Phenomenon:** da.ag used to name a parent's worried, troubled concern for an absent or endangered child. The object is relational and personal, not material or existential. The anxiety is voiced as a question ("what shall I do about my son?") — it reaches outward seeking a resolution it cannot find alone.

**Member verses:** 1Sa 9:5 · 1Sa 10:2 (2 verses)

**Anchor:** 1Sa 10:2 — the father's voiced distress most directly evidences the phenomenon; anxiety named and expressed.

---

### VCG-B-A7: Anxiety in the face of concrete, named external threats

**Phenomenon:** da.ag applied to anxiety about real and specific dangers — named enemies, sword, famine. The anxiety has a genuine object; the threats are not imagined. Isa 57:11 extends this by showing anxiety producing moral failure (deception). Jer 17:8 names the structural opposite: the person rooted in God is not anxious even in genuine drought — providing the theological ground against which threat-anxiety is measured.

**Member verses:** Isa 57:11 · Jer 38:19 · Jer 42:16 · Jer 17:8 (4 verses)

**Anchor:** Jer 38:19 — Zedekiah's fear of the Chaldeans most directly evidences the anxiety-about-concrete-threat phenomenon.

**Note on Jer 17:8:** placed in this group as the structural opposite — evidencing what anxiety is not (the non-anxious state under genuine threat). If the researcher prefers Jer 17:8 set aside (it does not evidence the anxious state itself), VCG-B-A7 reduces to 3 verses. Flagged as OQ-009.

---

### VCG-B-A8: Anxiety to be cast onto God — the transfer of burden grounded in divine care

**Phenomenon:** the person currently carrying real anxieties is commanded to cast them onto God, with the theological ground named: he cares for you. The verse presupposes anxiety as a real current burden; the phenomenon is the commanded transfer, not the anxiety itself.

**Member verses:** 1Pe 5:7 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** 1Pe 5:7 (only verse)

**Note:** may merge with VCG-B-A2 (which also names anxiety plus remedy — Phili 4:6). The distinction: Phili 4:6 is the summary command not to be anxious about anything, with prayer as the mechanism; 1Pe 5:7 is the transfer of already-present anxieties onto God with relational grounding. The phenomena are adjacent but distinguishable. Keeping separate; researcher may merge in Phase 7.

---

### VCG-B-A9: Pastoral burden as carried weight of love

**Phenomenon:** merimna naming the daily burden of apostolic care — Paul's anxiety for all the churches as the weight of relational responsibility carried by one who loves those entrusted to him. This is not disordered anxiety but the proper cost of love.

**Member verses:** 2Cor 11:28 (1 verse)

**Anchor:** 2Cor 11:28 (only verse)

---

### VCG-B-A10: Sorrow over sin — da.ag in the contrition register

**Phenomenon:** da.ag naming grief and contrition over personal sin, not anxiety about external threat. The inner state is directed backward (toward past wrongdoing) rather than forward (toward future threat).

**Member verses:** Psa 38:18 (1 verse)

**⚠ FLAG — OQ-008:** This verse does not carry the anxiety characteristic. The inner-being content is sorrow/contrition, which belongs to a different semantic domain. **Proposed disposition: set aside as wrong-face — the inner-being content in this verse is contrition, not anxiety.** Researcher decision required.
