## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The verse evidence reveals that CHAR-3 reflects and distorts the divine nature as the ultimate and only legitimate ground of boasting. Jer 9:23 (vc=33342, M08-C-VCG-01): the wise must not boast in wisdom, the mighty not in might, the rich not in riches — three prohibited objects that together identify all creaturely achievement as illegitimate boasting-ground. The verse's positive implication: God alone, as the one to be known, is the legitimate object of glory. 1Cor 1:29–31 (vc=33877, vc=33878, M08-C-VCG-01/03): God's sovereign design "so that no human being might boast in the presence of God" and "let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" — God is the one who structures the created order to direct all boasting toward himself. CHAR-3 (self-directed boasting) reflects, by inversion, that God is the sole legitimate object of the glorying-impulse that CHAR-3 redirects toward the self.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-3]** S — Scripture does not attribute CHAR-3 (self-directed boasting) to God within §3.B. The God-directed boasting of M08-C-VCG-03 (Rom 5:2, vc=33874; 1Cor 1:31, vc=33878; Phili 3:3, vc=33879) is not God boasting about himself but the believer glorying in God — a human act directed toward God. God as the subject of self-directed boasting is not evidenced. The evidence is silent on God possessing CHAR-3.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The silence is structurally significant: CHAR-3 (self-glorification) would require a subject who inflates their own standing beyond what they deserve or beyond proper creaturely acknowledgment. God, being fully what he is, cannot self-inflate — there is no gap between his actual worth and his self-presentation. 1Cor 4:7 (vc=33871, M08-C-VCG-01): "what do you have that you did not receive? If then you received it, why do you boast as if you did not receive it?" — the premise of CHAR-3 is misattribution of what was received as self-generated. God receives nothing from outside himself; all goods originate from him, making the logical structure of CHAR-3 (claiming as self-generated what was received) inapplicable to God.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The verse evidence distinguishes between a created purpose (glorying/exulting in something) and CHAR-3's fallen self-directed form. The VCG-03 material evidences that the capacity to glory, exult, and boast is positively deployed when God-directed: Rom 5:2 (vc=33874, M08-C-VCG-03): boasting in hope of God's glory; 1Cor 1:31 (vc=33878): "let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord"; Jam 1:9 (vc=33880, M08-C-VCG-03): the lowly believer "boasts" in his God-given exaltation — all evidencing the glorying-impulse functioning as created when directed toward God and received goods. CHAR-3 (M08-C-VCG-01, self-directed condemned boasting) is this same created capacity misdirected toward the self, creaturely achievement, wealth, or power.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The evidence distinguishes clearly. The glorying/exulting capacity is created and legitimate (VCG-03 evidence above — God-directed glorying is commanded, modelled, and approved). The self-directed form of boasting (VCG-01) is consistently condemned as fallen: 1Jo 2:16 (vc=33865, M08-C-VCG-01): "the pride of life [alazoneia] is not from the Father but is from the world" — explicitly distinguishing the worldly-origin (fallen) of CHAR-3's VCG-01 form from the Father. Eph 2:9 (vc=65604, M08-C-VCG-01): salvation by grace through faith "not a result of works, so that no one may boast" — God's design specifically excludes the ground for CHAR-3's VCG-01 form. CHAR-3's condemned self-directed form is a response to the fallen condition; the underlying boasting-capacity is created.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes — but only for CHAR-3's VCG-03 (God-directed) and VCG-02 (Pauline examined) registers. 1Th 2:19 (vc=33913, M08-C-VCG-03): "what is our hope or joy or crown of boasting before our Lord Jesus at his coming? Is it not you?" — boasting as an eschatological act before Christ; Phili 2:16 (vc=33910, M08-C-VCG-03): "my pride on the day of Christ" — boasting in the eschatological day is the goal. 2Cor 1:14 (vc=33907, M08-C-VCG-03): "on the day of our Lord Jesus you will boast of us as we will boast of you." For M08-C-VCG-01 (condemned self-boasting), the eschatological orientation is toward elimination: 1Cor 1:29 (vc=33877): God's election designed so that "no human being might boast in the presence of God" — self-boasting has no positive eschatological future.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 expresses the divine image through the created glorying-capacity — the human person's capacity to exult in, celebrate, and publicly affirm what they consider supreme. This capacity is genuine image-bearing: the human person, made to glorify God, possesses the faculty of glorying that is meant to overflow in God-directed praise (VCG-03). CHAR-3's VCG-01 form is this capacity misdirected: 1Cor 4:7 (vc=33871, M08-C-VCG-01) — the person who boasts as if they had not received their gifts is using the glorying-capacity to attribute to the self what should be attributed to God. The divine image's glorying-capacity is genuine; its self-direction is the distortion.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 in its condemned self-directed form is exclusively creaturely. God as the sole source of all good cannot engage in the misattribution that defines VCG-01 CHAR-3 (1Cor 4:7: boasting as if one had not received — inapplicable to God who receives nothing but is himself the source). However, the glorying-capacity that CHAR-3 distorts has a divine analogue: God delights in his own glory and directs his people to glory in him — the God-directed boasting of VCG-03 is a creaturely participation in the divine self-glorification that belongs properly to God. CHAR-3's condemned form is exclusively creaturely; the underlying capacity participates in something genuinely divine.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3's presence (VCG-01 self-directed form) reveals the divine image's glorying-capacity turned inward, making the self rather than God the object of exultation. 1Cor 1:29 (vc=33877, M08-C-VCG-01): God's design is to shut down human self-boasting "in the presence of God" — its presence before God is an image-disorder. Jam 4:16 (vc=33886, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting "in arrogance" is "evil" — the image-bearing capacity for public celebration of what is good is directed toward the self and judged as moral failure. The God-directed boasting of VCG-03 (Rom 5:2; 1Cor 1:31; Phili 3:3) evidences the restored image-bearing condition: the glorying-capacity is reoriented toward God and received goods.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes. 1Cor 1:29–31 (vc=33877, vc=33878, M08-C-VCG-01/03) deploys the full typological structure: God's sovereign election of the weak is designed to eliminate human self-boasting, and this design finds its eschatological expression in the cross — "let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord." The cross functions typologically as CHAR-3's definitive counter: at the cross, all human achievement-boasting is structurally evacuated (the crucified Lord eliminates all human credential-ground — 2Cor 11:18/30; Gal 6:14). Gal 6:14 (vc=33869, M08-C-VCG-02): "may I never boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by which the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world" — the cross as the typological telos of the examined-boasting discourse.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The direction runs from human self-boasting through the cross as counter to eschatological God-directed boasting as the restored end. CHAR-3's VCG-01 form (human self-boasting in wisdom, might, wealth) is the fallen starting point. The cross evacuates all such grounds (Gal 6:14; 1Cor 1:29–31) as the christological pivot. The VCG-03 end-point is eschatological God-directed glorying (1Th 2:19; Phili 2:16). The typology's direction: human self-boasting → cross-mediated evacuation → God-directed boasting as restored image-bearing. The direction is through the cross, not around it.

**[CHAR-3]** E — Typological use is evidenced (cross as counter to self-boasting; eschatological God-directed boasting as telos). See T0.4.1–2.

## T1 — Description and Classification (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The programme name is "Boasting and self-display." The compound name encodes two registers: "boasting" names the speech-act dimension (the verbal assertion of one's own worth, achievement, or credentials — kauchaomai/ha.lal), and "self-display" names the visible/social presentation dimension (the public making-known of the self as worthy — alazoneia, alazōn). The name signals that CHAR-3 is constitutively public and external: unlike CHAR-1 (inner dispositional elevation) or CHAR-2 (volitional transgressive act), CHAR-3 moves into speech and social visibility. Jam 4:16 (vc=33886, M08-C-VCG-01): "you boast in your arrogance" — the arrogance (CHAR-1 substrate) finding verbal expression in boasting (CHAR-3).

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Primary Hebrew terms: ha.lal (H1984H) in its self-directed boasting sense — the verb meaning to shine, to celebrate, to praise, used self-directedly as self-glorification; 1Ki 20:11 (vc=33353, M08-C-VCG-01): "let not him who puts on his armour boast like him who takes it off" — premature glorification before achievement is complete. Ro.hav (H7296): prideful excess — Psa 90:10 (vc=34222, M08-C-VCG-01): life's best span is "toil and trouble," undercutting the inner boast about its worth. Primary Greek terms: kauchaomai (G2744) — to boast, exult, glory; its noun forms kauchēma (G2745, the object or ground of boasting) and kauchēsis (G2746, the act of boasting); these span all three VCGs — the same terms cover condemned, examined, and God-directed registers. Alazoneia (G0212) and alazōn (G0213) — the inner boasting disposition and the boaster as a person-type (Jam 4:16, vc=33866; 1Jo 2:16, vc=33865; 2Ti 3:2, vc=33868; Rom 1:30, vc=33867).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The name carries directional and relational implications. Directional: "boasting" is always about something — the direction of the boast determines its legitimacy. Jer 9:23 (vc=33342, M08-C-VCG-01) identifies three wrong directions (wisdom, might, wealth) that imply one right direction (knowing God). The kauchaomai family inherits this directional structure — the same verb boasting toward self (VCG-01), toward God (VCG-03), or toward legitimate examined grounds (VCG-02). Relational: boasting/self-display requires an audience — CHAR-3 is constitutively social; it is performed for others' reception. Pro 25:14 (vc=33358, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting of a gift never given — the gift-claim is for social consumption and produces nothing. Constitutional: alazoneia (Jam 4:16, vc=33866; 1Jo 2:16, vc=33865) names the inner disposition that generates the boasting act.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 is primarily a communicative/expressive act (the speech-act of boasting and the social act of self-display) with a dispositional substrate (alazoneia — the inner boasting-orientation). The act-form dominates: the kauchaomai vocabulary is primarily verbal — to boast, to exult in, to commend oneself. Jam 4:16 (vc=33886, M08-C-VCG-01): "you boast in your arrogance. All such boasting is evil" — the boasting is the act; the arrogance is its source (CHAR-1 as substrate). The dispositional substrate: alazoneia (1Jo 2:16, vc=33865, M08-C-VCG-01): "the pride of life [alazoneia] is not from the Father but is from the world" — an inner orientation that generates the boasting act. CHAR-3 is the verbal/public expression of CHAR-1's inner elevation.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 has significant internal complexity due to its three-VCG structure. M08-C-VCG-01 (26 verses, condemned self-boasting): ha.lal self-directed, alazoneia, condemned kauchaomai uses — the straightforwardly fallen form. M08-C-VCG-02 (21 verses, Pauline examined boasting discourse): 2Cor 10–12 corpus — boasting examined, bounded, and redirected; legitimate within God-assigned spheres; CHAR-3's impulse subjected to discernment. M08-C-VCG-03 (23 verses, God-directed boasting): kauchaomai directed wholly toward God, relational commendation, eschatological confidence — the redeemed form. The three VCGs represent three registers of the same underlying impulse-to-glory: fallen-misdirected (VCG-01), examined-redirected (VCG-02), redeemed-God-directed (VCG-03).

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Boasting and self-display is the inner impulse to self-glorify through words, visible claims, and public assertion of one's own worth, achievement, credentials, or future plans — the outward/verbal expression of CHAR-1's inner elevation. The characteristic spans three registers evidenced in §3.B: (a) condemned self-directed boasting in creaturely assets (Jer 9:23, vc=33342; 1Jo 2:16, vc=33865; Jam 4:16, vc=33886); (b) the Pauline examined-boasting discourse — the same impulse subjected to theological examination within apostolic ministry (2Cor 10–12, M08-C-VCG-02); (c) God-directed boasting as the redeemed form — the glorying-impulse wholly redirected to God, received goods, and the community of faith (Rom 5:2, vc=33874; 1Cor 1:31, vc=33878; Phili 3:3, vc=33879). Primary vocabulary: kauchaomai/kauchēma/kauchēsis family (Greek), ha.lal self-directed (Hebrew), alazoneia/alazōn (inner boasting disposition).

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The structural opposite is evidenced through contrast. Pro 27:2 (adjacent context to Pro 27:1, vc=33359, M08-C-VCG-01): "let another praise you, and not your own mouth" — receiving praise from others rather than self-asserting is the structural opposite of boasting. 1Cor 4:7 (vc=33871, M08-C-VCG-01): "what do you have that you did not receive?" — the opposite posture to CHAR-3 is the receiver's acknowledgment of received goods as received; gratitude rather than self-glorification. The full opposite of CHAR-3 is VCG-03's God-directed boasting (1Cor 1:31: "let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord") — the glorying-impulse directed wholly away from self toward God is CHAR-3's structural redemption and structural opposite simultaneously.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — CHAR-3 (VCG-01 form) excludes and resists: (a) Honest self-assessment: 2Cor 10:12 (context of vc=33888, M08-C-VCG-02): comparing oneself with others and measuring oneself by oneself — Paul identifies this as the epistemic failure underlying self-commendation; (b) Acknowledgment of received goods: 1Cor 4:7 (vc=33871, M08-C-VCG-01): "why do you boast as if you did not receive it?" — self-directed boasting denies the received quality of one's capacities; (c) Integrity between inner and outer: 2Cor 5:12 (vc=33872, M08-C-VCG-02): Paul contrasts boasting in outward appearance with what is in the heart — CHAR-3's surface-boasting resists inward honesty; (d) Patience: Pro 27:1 (vc=33359, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting about tomorrow resists the patient non-claim over an uncertain future.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Three boundaries. First, CHAR-3 ends where God-directed glorying begins: VCG-03 (Rom 5:2; 1Cor 1:31; Phili 3:3) is not CHAR-3 in its condemned form — the same kauchaomai verb, directed toward God and received grace, is evidenced as appropriate and commanded. The boundary is the direction of the boast, not the act of boasting itself. Second, CHAR-3 ends where legitimate relational commendation within examined grounds begins: VCG-02 (2Cor 10:13, vc=33888: Paul constrains his boasting to God-assigned spheres) is examined, bounded self-commendation rather than condemned self-boasting. Third, CHAR-3 ends where CHAR-1 (inner disposition) begins: Jam 4:16 (vc=33886) sequences "arrogance" (CHAR-1 substrate) → "you boast" (CHAR-3 act); the boasting is CHAR-3, the arrogance its CHAR-1 source.

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Five distinct operational modes are evidenced. (a) Achievement-credential boasting: Jer 9:23 (vc=33342, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting in wisdom, might, wealth — the three forms of creaturely capacity claimed as grounds for self-glorification. (b) Premature glorification: 1Ki 20:11 (vc=33353, M08-C-VCG-01): "let not him who puts on his armour boast like him who takes it off" — boasting before the outcome is achieved. (c) Empty social self-promotion: Pro 25:14 (vc=33358, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting of a gift never given; Pro 20:14 (vc=33357, M08-C-VCG-01): the buyer's boast of a bargain as social duplicity. (d) Future-certainty presumption: Pro 27:1 (vc=33359, M08-C-VCG-01): boasting about tomorrow as if the future is secure. (e) Inner boasting-disposition: alazoneia (Jam 4:16, vc=33866; 1Jo 2:16, vc=33865) — the settled inner orientation of self-glorifying display as a way of being in the world.

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes, significantly. By direction: the kauchaomai family operates identically at the linguistic level across three directions — self-directed (condemned, VCG-01), God-directed (approved, VCG-03), examined-within-limits (VCG-02). The direction entirely determines the moral valence. By context: ha.lal self-directed in Hebrew wisdom literature names premature, creaturely-asset, and empty boasting (Jer 9:23, Pro 20:14, 25:14, 27:1); the Pauline kauchaomai discourse (2Cor 10–12, VCG-02) operates in the specific context of apostolic self-defence against false teachers whose boasting claims challenged Paul's authority. By constitutional level: alazoneia is the dispositional (inner) substrate; kauchaomai is the speech-act expression; self-display is the social/visible form.

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — Yes — speech is CHAR-3's primary operational mode. The kauchaomai verb is inherently communicative: one boasts to someone, before someone, about something. 1Ki 20:11 (vc=33353, M08-C-VCG-01): the proverbial warning against boasting "like him who takes off his armour" — boasting as public speech addressed to those who can hear. Pro 25:14 (vc=33358): boasting of a gift never given — the false social claim requires a listener. 2Ti 3:2 (vc=33868, M08-C-VCG-01): alazōn in the vice catalogue — the braggart as a social person-type whose characteristic is public self-assertion. **T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-3]** E — The primary dimension is communicative/expressive — CHAR-3 is constitutively a speech-act and social-display phenomenon. Its vocabulary (kauchaomai: to boast, to exult aloud; ha.lal: to shine, to celebrate publicly) names acts of public assertion. This distinguishes CHAR-3 from CHAR-1 (dispositional) and CHAR-2 (volitional-transgressive): CHAR-3 is the outward-verbal expression that the other two characteristics generate. The alazoneia/alazōn terms (inner boasting disposition/braggart person-type) name the dispositional substrate but the characteristic itself is primarily the speech-act: Jam 4:16 (vc=33886, M08-C-VCG-01): "you boast [kauchaomai] in your arrogance. All such boasting is evil."

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**
