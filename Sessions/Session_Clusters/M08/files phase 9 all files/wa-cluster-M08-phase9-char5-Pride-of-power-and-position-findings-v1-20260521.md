## T1 — Description and Classification (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The programme name is "Pride of power and position." The name encodes the specific medium through which this form of pride operates: "power" names structural force (military might, physical strength, national capacity — the ga.on proud-might register), and "position" names social and material standing (wealth, fortified height, authority). Together they distinguish CHAR-5 from CHAR-1 (dispositional elevation), CHAR-2 (volitional defiance), CHAR-3 (verbal boasting), and CHAR-4 (cognitive conceit) — CHAR-5 is the structural-power register of pride. Lev 26:19 (vc=33982, M08-E-VCG-01): "the pride of your power" — the compound phrase is native to the evidence itself.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Six distinct terms constitute CHAR-5's vocabulary. Ga.on (H1347) in the proud-might subset — national pride/power/might as inner confidence: Eze 30:6 (vc=33996, M08-E-VCG-01): "Egypt's proud might" — the nation's structural power generating collective inner pride. Hupsēlofroneō (G5309) — wealth-pride, literally "high-mindedness about riches": 1Ti 6:17 (vc=60878, M08-E-VCG-02): "not to be haughty [hupsēlofroneō]" for those who are wealthy. Ga.vah (H1361) — to be proud/exalted: Eze 28:5 (vc=34046, M08-E-VCG-02): "your heart became proud [ga.vah]" through wealth. Shal.le.tet (H7986) — imperious self-will: Eze 16:30 (vc=57959, M08-E-VCG-02): "impetuousness is located in the heart." A.din (H5719) — voluptuous ease/self-indulgence: Isa 47:8 (vc=9563, M08-E-VCG-02): Babylon's love of pleasures as the inner ground for self-sufficiency declaration. Ma.rom (H4791) — height, lofty place used as a power-strategy: Jer 49:16 (vc=34167, M08-E-VCG-02): physical height as the proud heart's false security. All terms name the same inner reality: structural goods (strength, wealth, height, ease) misused as the ground for proud self-sufficiency.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The name carries relational and constitutional implications. Relational: "power and position" are inherently relational — they are defined relative to others (power over others; position above others). Eze 16:49 (vc=33979, M08-E-VCG-01): Sodom's CHAR-5 is evidenced by failure to aid the poor — the structural superiority expressed in contempt and neglect of the vulnerable. Constitutional: the ga.on proud-might vocabulary names both the structural external power and the inner confidence that rests on it — CHAR-5 is constitutionally anchored in the heart (Eze 28:5 — heart proud; Eze 16:30 — impetuousness in the heart) even though its medium is external structural goods.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 is primarily a dispositional state — the inner arrogance and self-confidence that structural power generates and sustains. The ga.on proud-might vocabulary names both the inner disposition (pride as an inner state) and its structural basis (might/power/strength). Lev 26:19 (vc=33982, M08-E-VCG-01): "the pride of your power" — pride (ga.on) is the inner state; power is its ground. Eze 28:5 (vc=34046, M08-E-VCG-02): "your heart became proud" — the dispositional elevation is explicitly named as the characteristic, with wealth as its generating condition. CHAR-5 is a settled inner posture of power-grounded self-sufficiency and contempt for others, sustained by structural goods.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 has constituent elements across two VCGs. M08-E-VCG-01 (8 verses): national proud-might — collective structural power (military, national, institutional) generating collective inner pride (Lev 26:19; Eze 7:24; 16:49; 24:21; 30:6; 30:18; 32:12; 33:28). M08-E-VCG-02 (9 verses): wealth, position, and self-secured elevation — material prosperity, fortified height, and ease as individual or national grounds for pride (Isa 47:8; Jer 49:16; Jer 51:53; Eze 16:30; Eze 19:11; Eze 28:5; Obd 3; Hab 2:9; 1Ti 6:17). Both VCGs evidence the same inner-dynamic (structural goods → inner arrogance) through different structural media (national military might vs wealth/height/position).

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Pride of power and position is the inner arrogance that arises when strength, social authority, material prosperity, or national might becomes the ground for self-exaltation and contemptuous elevation above others — pride expressed through a specific medium. It operates as: (a) collective national proud-might — the community's structural power becoming the inner ground for arrogant self-confidence and resistance to God (Lev 26:19, vc=33982; Eze 30:6, vc=33996; Eze 33:28, vc=33999); (b) individual/national wealth-pride — material success causing the heart to become proud (Eze 28:5, vc=34046; 1Ti 6:17, vc=60878) or generating declarations of self-sufficiency (Isa 47:8, vc=9563); (c) positional-height pride — physical and social elevation as a strategy of self-secured power that generates pride (Jer 49:16, vc=34167; Obd 3, vc=65674; Hab 2:9, vc=34169). Cross-register flag: M23 (strength/power/dominion) — several terms remain in M08 because their verses evidence strength misused as self-exaltation.

**[CHAR-5]** E — The structural opposite is evidenced through contrast. 1Ti 6:17 (vc=60878, M08-E-VCG-02): "not to be haughty, nor to set their hopes on the uncertainty of riches, but on God, who richly provides everything to enjoy" — trust in God as provider rather than in riches as the ground of self-sufficiency is the named structural opposite. Eze 16:49 (vc=33979, M08-E-VCG-01): the structural opposite of Sodom's power-pride-and-ease is aiding the poor and needy — using structural goods in service of others rather than as the ground for self-exaltation. The structural opposite of CHAR-5 is the stewardship posture: receiving structural goods from God as gifts to be stewarded for others, not claimed as self-sufficient grounds for elevation.

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — CHAR-5 explicitly excludes: (a) Trust in God as the genuine source of power and wealth: 1Ti 6:17 (vc=60878, M08-E-VCG-02) — "set their hopes on the uncertainty of riches" rather than God; Lev 26:19 (vc=33982, M08-E-VCG-01): "the pride of your power" is broken by God — implying it resisted acknowledging God as the source; (b) Service to the vulnerable: Eze 16:49 (vc=33979, M08-E-VCG-01) — Sodom's power-pride paired with failure to aid the poor and needy; (c) Humility before God's power: the repeated "God will bring down proud might" pattern (Eze 30:6; 30:18; 32:12) implies CHAR-5 resists the recognition that God's power supersedes structural human power.

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Three boundaries. First, CHAR-5 ends where legitimate stewardship of power and wealth begins: possessing strength, authority, or wealth is not CHAR-5; CHAR-5 is the inner arrogance that uses those goods as the ground for self-exaltation. 1Ti 6:17 (vc=60878, M08-E-VCG-02) implies that the wealthy can avoid CHAR-5 through proper hope-placement. Second, CHAR-5 ends where CHAR-1 begins: CHAR-1 is the general dispositional elevation of the self; CHAR-5 is the specific form of that elevation grounded in structural power. The relationship is that CHAR-5 is a specific instance of CHAR-1's dispositional pattern through the medium of structural goods. Third, CHAR-5 is distinct from M23 (strength/power/dominion) content: the cross-register flag identifies that several CHAR-5 terms primarily belong to M23; they remain in M08 specifically because the §3.D verses evidence strength misused as self-exaltation, not strength in its legitimate exercise.
