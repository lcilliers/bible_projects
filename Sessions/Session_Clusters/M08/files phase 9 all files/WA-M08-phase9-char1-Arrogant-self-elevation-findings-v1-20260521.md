## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence reveals that God alone has the prerogative of genuine self-exaltation and that arrogant self-elevation in the human person is a usurpation of what belongs exclusively to God. In Isa 2:11 and 2:17 (M08-A2-VCG-01; M08-A3-VCG-04) "the Lord alone will be exalted in that day" — the day of judgment is the day when the divine monopoly on true exaltation is reasserted. Psa 75:7 (M08-A4-VCG-05) makes the structural logic explicit: "it is God who executes judgment, putting down one and lifting up another" — exaltation originates solely from God's sovereign agency. The day-of-the-Lord corpus (M08-A3-VCG-04: Isa 2:12, 10:33; Deu 32:27) confirms: whatever lifts itself up stands in God's own domain. CHAR-1 therefore reflects the divine attribute of true exaltation — not by imitating it but by competing with it.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence does not attribute arrogant self-elevation to God; rather, it consistently frames CHAR-1 as the creature's counterfeit claim to what God alone possesses. The 174 set-aside verses — largely the divine-majesty register of rum, ga.on, ga.vah — carried M22 content (divine genuine exaltation) rather than M08 content; they were correctly set aside at Phase 5.5. What emerges from the remaining corpus is a strong implicit contrast: God's true exaltation (set aside in M22) versus the creature's false self-elevation (present in CHAR-1). Dan 11:36 (M08-A1-VCG-02) captures the essential movement: CHAR-1 is the will's attempt to occupy God's singular position. CHAR-1 is not divine; it is anti-divine.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence's silence about God possessing arrogant self-elevation is analytically significant. The Phase 5.5 set-aside of 174 M22-register verses confirms that Scripture distinguishes God's genuine exaltation from human arrogant self-elevation. The silence implies that CHAR-1 is a specifically creaturely distortion — the creature attempting to occupy a position that only belongs to the uncreated God. This is confirmed negatively by Isa 14:13 (M08-A4-VCG-05): the will that says "I will ascend to heaven; above the stars of God I will set my throne on high" precisely names the movement as a transgression of a boundary that should not be crossed.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The verse evidence is silent on a created purpose for arrogant self-elevation. No verse in the 149-verse corpus evidences CHAR-1 functioning as an appropriate expression of the created order. 1Sa 16:7 (M08-A1-VCG-03; M08-A4-VCG-05) comes closest to a positive register — it is man's tendency to prize outward height, which God explicitly corrects — but this corrects rather than affirms the tendency. The absence of a created-purpose register suggests CHAR-1 is best understood as a distortion of a genuine created capacity rather than a created purpose in its own right.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence uniformly evidences CHAR-1 as a fallen characteristic. No verse treats arrogant self-elevation as appropriate, created, or divinely intended. The wisdom tradition (Pro 16:5 — M08-A1-VCG-03: "everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination to the Lord"; Pro 16:18 — M08-A4-VCG-04: "pride goes before destruction") treats it as constitutively disordered. The NT vice catalogues (2Ti 3:2; Rom 1:30 — M08-A4-VCG-01) position CHAR-1 as part of the characterology of moral collapse, not created design. Mar 7:22 (M08-A4-VCG-01) explicitly names pride among "evils that come out of a person" — constitutively internal but disordered.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence consistently orients CHAR-1 toward a future humbling rather than a future fullness. The day-of-the-Lord material (M08-A3-VCG-04: Isa 2:11, 2:12, 2:17; Isa 10:33) identifies the eschatological future as the definitive reversal of CHAR-1 — "the haughty looks of man shall be brought low, and the Lord alone will be exalted in that day." Eze 31:14 (M08-A3-VCG-04) establishes the structural future: "no trees... may grow to towering height... for they are all given over to death." CHAR-1 has no positive eschatological trajectory; its trajectory is toward judgment and reversal, not fullness.

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence evidences CHAR-1 as a distortion of the divine image rather than an expression of it. CHAR-1 thus evidences a capacity gone wrong: the created capacity for self-knowledge, dignity, and standing before God, bent back on itself and claiming the position of God rather than receiving it as gift.

## T1 — Description and Classification

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The programme name is "Arrogant self-elevation." This compound name signals three essential elements: (a) it is a movement (elevation — upward); (b) the subject is the self (self — the inner person as agent); (c) the mode is arrogant (not legitimate, not appropriate, not God-given). The name distinguishes CHAR-1 from legitimate dignified self-awareness and from God's genuine exaltation. The elevation metaphor is directly rooted in the primary vocabulary: rum (to be high, to lift up), ga.on (loftiness, pride), ga.vah (to exalt), ga.vo.ah (high), hupsēlos (high, lofty) — the height/elevation root runs through both testaments.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The Hebrew height vocabulary (rum, ga.on, ga.vah, ga.vo.ah, ge.eh, ge.ut, go.vah, ge.vah, ga.a.vah) is rooted in the physical metaphor of upward movement — to be high, to rise, to soar. These terms name the same inner reality through the spatial metaphor of height: pride is the self ascending above its proper station. The Greek vocabulary (huperēfanos/huperēfania — above + appear; hupsēlos — high; huperfroneō — think above) carries the same metaphor but with greater precision about the perceptual and cognitive register: the proud person appears or considers themselves above others. The definitional convergence is the spatial/directional metaphor of elevation against proper creaturely limits.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The name carries all three. Directional: the characteristic moves upward — the self lifts above its proper station. Relational: the elevation is always measured against others (God, other humans, divinely-established limits) — it is inherently comparative. Constitutional: the evidence locates CHAR-1 in the heart (M08-A1: 30 verses with the "lifted-up heart" idiom), the eyes (M08-A2: 11 verses), the spirit (Pro 16:18 — M08-A1-VCG-03), and the general dispositional core (M08-A4: 68 verses) — penetrating to the deepest constitutional levels.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — CHAR-1 is primarily a disposition — a settled inner orientation of the self toward its own elevation — that also produces acts and expressions. The distinction is evidenced by the contrast between the constitutive/dispositional vocabulary (Pro 16:5 — "everyone who is arrogant in heart"; Pro 16:18; Dan 4:37 — "those who walk in pride") and the act-form vocabulary (Num 15:30 — "acting with a high hand"; 1Ki 11:26 — "lifted up his hand"). The disposition precedes and generates the acts; the wisdom corpus in M08-A4-VCG-04 treats CHAR-1 as a stable inner characteristic with predictable consequences rather than a momentary state.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence evidences constituent elements across the four sub-groups. The seat-of-pride axis (heart / eyes / national-collective / general dispositional) identifies four registers within a single characteristic. Within the sub-groups, the evidence distinguishes: (a) prosperity-triggered elevation of the heart (M08-A1-VCG-01), (b) royal/power-triggered elevation (M08-A1-VCG-02), (c) constitutive/habitual pride (M08-A1-VCG-03; M08-A4-VCG-04), (d) ocular/postural expression (M08-A2), (e) collective/national pride (M08-A3), and (f) volitional self-assertion against God (M08-A4-VCG-05). CHAR-1 is complex in structure — one inner disposition manifesting through multiple triggers, registers, and expressions.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Arrogant self-elevation is the constitutive inner movement in which the self — through the heart (the primary named seat), but also expressed through the eyes, posture, and general dispositional orientation — lifts itself above its proper creaturely station before God and others, claiming standing, honour, or status that does not belong to it. It operates as a disposition that generates specific acts (volitional assertion, boastful speech, domineering action), has identifiable triggers (prosperity, power, beauty, achievement), and produces predictable consequences (relational fracture, divine opposition, and structural self-defeat). The characteristic spans both individual and collective subjects.

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The structural opposite is explicitly named in multiple verses. Psa 131:1 (M08-A1-VCG-03; M08-A2-VCG-01) names it in both registers simultaneously: "my heart is not lifted up; my eyes are not raised too high" — the deliberate restraint of the heart and eyes from self-elevation. Jam 4:6 and 1Pe 5:5 (M08-A4-VCG-01) name it explicitly: "God gives grace to the humble" — humility (M09) is the named opposite. Pro 29:23 (M08-A4-VCG-04): "one's pride will bring him low, but he who is lowly in spirit will obtain honour." The structural opposite of CHAR-1 is M09 (humility/lowliness of spirit).

CHAR-1 is specifically the movement that competes with or usurps God's position — not the mere possession of dignity or standing. The boundary with CHAR-3 (boasting) is that CHAR-1 is the dispositional posture from which boasting emerges, while CHAR-3 is the verbal/public expression of that posture.
