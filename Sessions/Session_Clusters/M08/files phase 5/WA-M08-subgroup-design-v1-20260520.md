## §1 — Characteristics identified

Five characteristics are evidenced across M08's 469-verse substantive corpus:

### CHAR-1: Arrogant self-elevation — the dispositional core
**Definition:** The inner state in which the heart or will lifts itself above its proper station before God and others, claiming standing, honour, or status that does not belong to it. This is the settled, constitutive posture of pride — pride as a disposition of being rather than a single act.

**Evidence base:** The Hebrew "lifted-up heart" idiom (Deu 8:14; Pro 18:12; Eze 28:2; Dan 11:12; Hos 13:6; 2Ch 26:16 etc.), the ge/ge.eh/ge.ah/ga.a.vah/ga.on human-pride corpus, the Greek huperēfania/huperēfanos/hupsēlos vocabulary, the rum self-exaltation verses, ga.vah's pride verses, and the haughty-eyes idiom across multiple terms. The heart is consistently named as pride's seat (Psa 101:5; Pro 16:5; Jer 48:29; Eze 31:10; Dan 5:20); the spirit (Ecc 7:8; Dan 5:20) and the eyes (Psa 18:27; Isa 2:11; Pro 6:17; Isa 5:15) are secondary registers.

**Carried in sub-group:** M08-A

### CHAR-2: Presumptuous defiance — pride as willful transgression
**Definition:** The inner state in which the will asserts itself against known divinely-established limits, appointed authority, or revealed commands with a "boiling-over" force — pride-as-deliberate-defiance rather than merely elevated self-regard. This is the volitional form of pride: the self translating its elevation into deliberate transgression.

**Evidence base:** The Hebrew zid corpus (boiling presumption overriding divine command, Deu 1:43; Deu 18:20; Neh 9:16 etc.), the za.don corpus (presumptuous defiance of religious and legal authority, Deu 17:12; Jer 49:16; Obd 3 etc.), the zed corpus (arrogant disregard for God's law and commandments, Psa 119:21–122; Psa 86:14 etc.), authadēs (self-will dismissing all authority, Tit 1:7; 2Pe 2:10), tolmētēs (reckless presumption against divine beings, 2Pe 2:10), and the M08-relational archō verses (domineering self-will in position, Mar 10:42; Luk 12:45; Luk 3:8).

**Carried in sub-group:** M08-B

### CHAR-3: Boasting and self-display — pride's verbal and public form
**Definition:** The inner impulse to self-glorify through words, visible claims, and public assertion of one's own worth, achievement, credentials, or future plans — pride moving outward through speech and self-presentation. This includes both its condemned forms and Paul's complex engagement with what constitutes legitimate vs illegitimate boasting.

**Evidence base:** The Greek kauchaomai/kauchēma/kauchēsis family (primary Greek boasting vocabulary, 32 + 11 + 12 verses), alazoneia and alazōn (boasting as inner disposition, 4 verses), and the ha.lal (boast) self-directed subset (1Ki 20:11; Psa 10:3; Psa 49:6; Psa 52:1; Psa 97:7; Pro 20:14; Pro 25:14; Pro 27:1; Jer 9:23; Jer 49:4 — ~10 verses). Also ro.hav (Psa 90:10 — ironic engagement with life's supposed boast).

**Carried in sub-group:** M08-C. Note: Phase 7 VCG design must distinguish within M08-C between (a) self-directed boasting condemned, (b) Paul's examination of legitimate vs illegitimate boasting grounds, and (c) the God-directed boasting register (where kauchaomai and ha.lal overlap with M22). The M22 cross-register flag travels with M08-C.

### CHAR-4: Vain conceit — inflated self-estimate in the mind
**Definition:** Pride operating specifically in the cognitive faculty as a false, inflated self-assessment — the mind's wrong and ungrounded picture of itself that produces blindness to truth, fuels controversy, generates communal disorder, and causes the person to claim resources, status, or recognition beyond what is warranted.

**Evidence base:** The Greek inflation family: tufoō (being puffed up with conceit, 3 verses), fusioō (inflated inner self — partisan pride, intellectual pride, ungrounded mental fantasy, 7 verses), fusiōsis (conceit as communal disorder, 1 verse), huperfroneō (thinking more highly of oneself than warranted beyond the measure of faith, 1 verse).

**Carried in sub-group:** M08-D

### CHAR-5: Pride of power and position — arrogance in strength, wealth, and authority
**Definition:** The inner arrogance that arises when strength, social authority, material prosperity, or national might becomes the ground for self-exaltation and contemptuous elevation above others. Pride here is expressed through a specific medium — position, resource, or capacity — making it the structural-power register of M08.

**Evidence base:** ga.on's proud-might sub-corpus (Eze 30:6, 30:18, 32:12, 33:28; Lev 26:19; Eze 7:24; Eze 24:21); the archō domineering-authority verses (Mar 10:42 — rulers lording over; Luk 12:45 — domineering abuse of authority); hupsēlofroneō (haughtiness tied to material prosperity, 1Ti 6:17); ga.vah's wealth-induced-pride verse (Eze 28:5); shal.le.tet (imperious self-will, Eze 16:30); a.din (voluptuous ease feeding prideful self-sufficiency, Isa 47:8); some ma.rom verses (Jer 49:16; Obd 3; Hab 2:9 — fortified elevation as power strategy); Eze 19:11 (qo.mah — visible dominance of rulers).

**Carried in sub-group:** M08-E. The M23 (strength/authority) cross-register flag from Phase 3 travels with M08-E.

## §2 — Sub-group design

### M08-A — Arrogant Self-Elevation — the Dispositional Core

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation)
**Split-axis:** None (single characteristic, no volume-split required: 149V = 32.0% of 465 substantive — within gate)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of arrogant self-elevation as an inner-being disposition: the movement of the heart or will by which the self lifts itself above its proper station before God and others. It is pride as an inner posture — settled, habitual, and constitutive of the person's orientation. The evidence shows this disposition consistently located in the heart (the Hebrew lev/heart-lifting idiom), the spirit (ga.vo.ah in the spirit), and the eyes (haughty eyes as pride's outward register). It drives the self to claim standing, honour, or status that properly belongs to God alone, to treat others with contempt from an elevated self-assessment, and to imagine itself beyond the reach of divine judgement. This is M08's foundational characteristic: the inner pride from which arrogance, boasting, and presumption all flow.

**Primary term carriers:** ge.eh (9V), ge (1V), ge.ah (1V), ga.a.vah — pride verses (12V), ga.on — human-pride verses (~21V), ga.vah — pride verses (~14V), go.vah — pride verses (5V), ga.vo.ah (9V), ge.vah mti6038 (3V), ge.vah mti103 (1V), gav.hut (2V), ge.ut — pride verses (4V), ga.ah Job 10:16 (1V), rum — self-exaltation verses (~35V), H7312 rum — pride verses (5V), sha.chats (1V), zud (1V), huperēfania (1–2V), huperēfanos (5V), hupsēlos (3V), filautos (1V), qo.mah — most verses (6V), some ga.on and ga.a.vah (see detail)

**Verse count:** ~149 (estimated; CC confirms from vc_ids)

### M08-B — Presumptuous Defiance — Pride as Willful Transgression

**Characteristic:** CHAR-2 (Presumptuous defiance)
**Split-axis:** None (45V = 9.7% — well within gate)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of presumptuous defiance: pride expressing itself as a boiling-over self-will that deliberately overrides divinely-established limits, appointed authority, and revealed commands. The inner state is not merely elevated self-regard but active, volitional self-assertion against a known constraint. The evidence comes from the Hebrew zid (boiling presumption overriding divine command), za.don (insolent defiance of religious and legal authority), and zed (arrogant disregard for God's law and order), alongside authadēs (self-will dismissing authority entirely), tolmētēs (reckless presumption against divine beings), and the M08-relational verses of archō (domineering authority and presumptuous self-assertion in narrative contexts). Pride here has moved from disposition to act — it is pride-as-rebellion, pride-as-contempt-for-authority, pride-as-deliberate-transgression. The characteristic is the inner mechanism driving presumptuous sin (Psa 19:13), the false prophet claiming God's authority (Deu 18:20), and the ruler abusing unchecked power (Luk 12:45).

**Primary term carriers:** zid (9V), za.don (11V), zed (13V), authadēs (2V), tolmētēs (1V), archō M08-relational verses (8V: Mat 16:22, Mat 24:49, Mar 8:32, Mar 10:41, Mar 10:42, Luk 3:8, Luk 11:53, Luk 12:45), ra.hav-assertive Isa 3:5 (1V)

**Verse count:** ~45

### M08-C — Boasting and Self-Display — Pride's Verbal and Public Form

**Characteristic:** CHAR-3 (Boasting and self-display)
**Split-axis:** None (70V = 15.1% — within gate)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of boasting and self-display: the inner impulse to self-glorify through words, visible claims, and public assertion of one's own worth, achievement, credentials, or future plans. Where M08-A names the dispositional posture and M08-B names the defiant act, M08-C names the expressive form — pride moving outward through speech and self-presentation. The evidence spans the full Greek boasting vocabulary (kauchaomai, kauchēma, kauchēsis), the inner boasting-disposition words (alazoneia, alazōn), and the ha.lal (boast) self-directed sub-corpus. The characteristic includes both its straightforwardly condemned forms (boasting of one's own wisdom, might, wealth, or future plans; empty self-promotion; premature self-glorification before battle is won) and the Pauline examination of when, what, and in whom to boast — which involves sustained reflection on self-directed pride's inversion through grace. Note: Phase 7 VCG design will distinguish within this sub-group between (a) condemned self-directed boasting, (b) Paul's examined boasting discourse, and (c) the God-directed glorying register (M22 cross-register flag) carried by kauchaomai and ha.lal. Cross-register flag: M22 (praise/thanksgiving/glory) travels with M08-C.

**Primary term carriers:** kauchaomai (32V), kauchēma (11V), kauchēsis (12V), alazoneia (2V), alazōn (2V), ha.lal-boast self-directed verses (10V), ro.hav (1V)

**Verse count:** ~70

### M08-D — Vain Conceit — Inflated Self-Estimate in the Mind

**Characteristic:** CHAR-4 (Vain conceit)
**Split-axis:** None (12V = 2.6% — well within gate)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of vain conceit: pride operating specifically in the cognitive faculty as an inflated, ungrounded self-assessment — the mind's false picture of itself. While M08-A addresses the dispositional posture of the heart, M08-D addresses the mind's wrong and inflated estimation of its own worth, capacity, or spiritual standing. The evidence comes from the Greek inflation vocabulary: tufoō (puffed up with conceit — inflated self-regard that leads to the devil's own condemnation and fuels craving for controversy), fusioō (inflated inner self — through partisan attachment to leaders, through intellectual pride over knowledge, through ungrounded mental fantasy without factual basis), fusiōsis (conceit as inner inflation generating communal disorder and conflict), and huperfroneō (thinking more highly of oneself than warranted, beyond the measure of faith God has given). This is pride as cognitive self-deception — the miscalibrated inner faculty that damages relationship, community, and access to truth.

**Primary term carriers:** tufoō (3V), fusioō (7V), fusiōsis (1V), huperfroneō (1V)

**Verse count:** 12

### M08-E — Pride of Power and Position — Arrogance in Strength, Wealth, and Authority

**Characteristic:** CHAR-5 (Pride of power and position)
**Split-axis:** None (17V = 3.7% — well within gate)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of pride-of-power and position: the inner arrogance that arises when strength, social authority, material prosperity, or national might becomes the ground for self-exaltation and contemptuous elevation above others. This is M08's structural-power register — pride expressed through the specific medium of position, resource, or capacity rather than as a generalised inner posture. The evidence includes the ga.on proud-might vocabulary in its national-power register (Egypt's proud might and Israel's pride-of-power as inner strength and national confidence targeted for divine humiliation), the archō domineering-authority verses (rulers lording it over others; the unfaithful servant's abusive assumption of unchecked authority), wealth-fuelled haughtiness (hupsēlofroneō — the wealthy warned against trusting riches; ga.vah — the heart becoming proud through material success), the imperious self-will of shal.le.tet (acting without shame or restraint), the voluptuous ease of a.din feeding prideful self-sufficiency, and the fortified-elevation register of ma.rom and qo.mah where physical height becomes an expression of power-pride. Cross-register flag: M23 (strength/power/dominion) travels with M08-E.

**Primary term carriers:** ga.on — proud-might verses (Lev 26:19; Eze 7:24; Eze 24:21; Eze 30:6; Eze 30:18; Eze 32:12; Eze 33:28 = 7V), ga.vah Eze 28:5 (1V), hupsēlofroneō 1Ti 6:17 (1V), shal.le.tet Eze 16:30 (1V), a.din Isa 47:8 (1V), ma.rom — power-register verses (Jer 49:16; Jer 51:53; Obd 3; Hab 2:9 = 4V), qo.mah Eze 19:11 (1V), Eze 16:49 [ga.on] (1V)

**Verse count:** ~17

## §5 — Structural observation (researcher awareness required)

**Two holding sub-groups (M08-F and M08-G) are not characteristics.** They are routing mechanisms for the cross-register content of M08's polysemic terms.

M08-F holds ~120 verses whose individual inner-being content is divine majesty or God-directed exaltation (M22 register) — these are in M08 terms that STAY because of M08-relational content elsewhere, but as individual verses they belong analytically to M22. Phase 8.5 will resolve them (ROUTE-TO-CLUSTER M22 or SET-ASIDE).

M08-G holds ~52 verses that have no discernible inner-being state in their individual meaning — the temporal/narrative archō markers and positive-assertiveness ra.hav verses. Phase 8.5 will SET-ASIDE these.

Together these holding sub-groups represent 172 of the 470 total verse assignments (~37%) and will be empty by Phase 9. Their size reflects the polysemic range of M08's height vocabulary and the archō term's dominance of the cross-register count.

**The 5 substantive characteristics** (M08-A through M08-E) carry 293 verses among them — these are the verses that will generate Phase 9 catalogue findings. Distribution within gate: M08-A 149V (32.0%), M08-C 70V (15.1%), M08-B 45V (9.7%), M08-E 17V (3.7%), M08-D 12V (2.6%).

**Researcher note:** The two holding sub-groups and the BOUNDARY sub-group are a natural consequence of M08's polysemic term set (particularly rum with 92 verses across both the self-exaltation and divine-exaltation registers) and the archō term (56 verses, most of which are temporal markers). This is anticipated under §6.3.2 (terms STAY because of M08-relational verses even when most of their corpus lies elsewhere). No researcher decision is required to proceed — CC builds the Phase 6 directive using this design; Phase 8.5 resolves M08-F and M08-G after VCG design (Phase 7) completes.
