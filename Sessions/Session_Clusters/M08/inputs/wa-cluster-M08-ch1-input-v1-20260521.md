The cluster-synthesis: the most consistent immediate response across the cluster is the victim's appeal to God (CHAR-1 and CHAR-2) — the experience of pride from the receiving end drives the person toward divine appeal, not direct resistance.
- *T1.5.2* — _Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster comparison shows variation by register: CHAR-2 evidences the most consistent response pattern (victims consistently turn to God for rescue). - *T1.5.3* — _Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly._
  → **[CLUSTER]** S — All five characteristics are silent on the immediate internal experience of the person exercising the characteristic (as distinguished from the victim's response or the external consequence). The cluster-wide silence on the inner phenomenology of the pride-possessor is analytically significant: the evidence consistently describes pride from outside (its acts, its characterisation, its consequences) rather than from inside (the felt experience of self-elevation in the moment). - *T1.6.1* — _What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster comparison reveals a consistent pattern: all five characteristics produce deepening over time, and the end-state is more severe than the initial form. CHAR-1 produces progressive self-sealing — the pride closes off the inner faculties that would correct it. CHAR-2 produces the scoffer's settled identity (Pro 21:24, M08-B-VCG-02) through repeated transgression hardening into dispositional contempt. - *T1.6.2* — _What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?_
