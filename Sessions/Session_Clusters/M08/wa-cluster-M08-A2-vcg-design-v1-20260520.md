## VCG M08-A2-VCG-01 — Haughty Eyes — Pride in the Gaze

**Description:** Verses specifically naming the eyes or gaze as the register of pride — haughty eyes as what God abhors, actively brings down, or contrasts with the posture of humility. The eye functions here as the window onto the inner disposition: haughty eyes indicate an inward elevation that has become visible. The vocabulary of the look, the lifted gaze, and the high-lifted eyelids names the specific bodily register through which pride announces itself. Includes the direct prohibitions against lifting the eyes in pride (Psa 131:1 — eyes not raised high as the deliberate posture of humility), the divine opposition to the haughty-eyed (Psa 18:27 — haughty eyes brought down by God), the hatred God directs at the haughty eye (Pro 6:17 — haughty eyes first in the list of things the Lord hates), the portrait of the high-lifted eyelids as inner contempt made visible (Pro 30:13), and the judgment that specifically names the eyes/looks as what will be humbled (Isa 2:11 — haughtiness in the looks of men; Isa 2:17 — haughtiness of man humbled; Isa 5:15 — inner haughtiness expressed in the eyes).

**Anchor:** vc_id=34247 — Pro 6:17 — H7311A rum — "Haughty eyes appear first in a list of things the Lord hates, locating pride in the gaze — an outward expression of an inward elevation of self that God finds abominable." The anchor places haughty eyes as the lead item in God's abomination-list, making it the most definitionally primary statement of the eye-register.

## VCG M08-A2-VCG-02 — Outward Display and Body-Language of Pride

**Description:** Verses evidencing pride's visible register in broader outward display and bodily bearing beyond the eyes specifically: the necklace-like display of pride (Psa 73:6), the ostentatious posture and wanton glances of the daughters of Zion (Isa 3:16), the haughty face-posture that shuts out seeking God (Psa 10:4), and the head lifted up in the posture of pride (Job 10:16). These verses evidence the same CHAR-1 content — the inner self-exaltation becoming outwardly visible — but through display-posture and bodily orientation rather than the eye/gaze specifically. In Psa 73:6 pride is worn like an ornament; in Isa 3:16 it walks in deliberate ostentatious display; in Psa 10:4 the face shuts out God; in Job 10:16 the head rises in self-exaltation and is met with divine opposition. The common thread is the embodied expression of an elevated inner self.

**Anchor:** vc_id=34052 — Isa 3:16 — H1361 ga.vah — "The daughters of Zion are described as haughty — their inner exaltation/pride expresses itself outwardly in ostentatious posture and wanton glances, making this a case of self-exalting pride rather than joyful exultation." The anchor captures the full outward-display dimension: posture, movement, and gaze as a deliberate embodied expression of inner pride.

- vc=33945 — Job 10:16 — H1342 ga.ah — Head lifted up in pride; God would hunt like a lion — self-exaltation triggers divine opposition.
- vc=34074 — Psa 10:4 — H1363 go.vah — Haughty face-posture shutting out seeking God; pride in thought-life producing atheistic self-sufficiency.
