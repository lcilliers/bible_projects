## VCG M08-B-VCG-01 — Boiling Presumption — The Zid Register (Overriding Divine Command)

**Description:** Verses where pride takes the specific form of a will that "boils over" against a known divine prohibition or command — the zid vocabulary of presumptuous self-will that overrides obedience. The inner-being content is the deliberate, willful quality of the transgression: Egypt's boiling insolence overflowing into oppressive action (Exo 18:11); willful premeditated attack from a boiling presumption in the will (Exo 21:14); Israel defying God's command at Kadesh with an inner boiling-over of self-will (Deu 1:43); the punitive pedagogy designed to eliminate boiling presumption from the community (Deu 17:13); the false prophet who presumes to speak what God has not given (Deu 18:20 — capital offense); Egypt and Israel's historical acting-arrogantly (Neh 9:10; 9:16; 9:29 — persistent inner refusal to submit); and Babylon's prideful defiance against the Holy One (Jer 50:29). The characteristic of this VCG is the boiling quality: pride not as settled disposition but as a suddenly-overflowing willful assertion against a known divine limit.

**Anchor:** vc_id=34119 — Deu 18:20 — H2102 zid — "The prophet who presumes to speak words God has not commanded acts out of an inner boiling self-will that overrides divine authority, and the penalty is death — showing presumptuous inner assertion as a capital offense." The anchor captures the zid register at its most extreme consequence: boiling presumption against divine authority as a capital crime.

## VCG M08-B-VCG-02 — Arrogant Insolence Against Authority — The Zed/Za.don Register

**Description:** Verses where pride expresses itself as arrogant insolence toward God's law, appointed religious and legal authority, divine revelation, and the faithful. The za.don vocabulary (insolent defiance of established order) and zed vocabulary (arrogant presumption against divine commandments) share the register of pride-as-contempt-for-authority, both specific institutions and the law of God generally. Za.don drives disobedience of appointed authority (Deu 17:12), the false prophet's arrogant claim to speak for God (Deu 18:22), and produces disgrace and strife (Pro 11:2; 13:10). Zed produces presumptuous sins seeking dominion over the will (Psa 19:13), hostility rooted in disregard for divine authority (Psa 86:14), and insolent wandering from God's commandments across the extended Psalm 119 sequence (Psa 119:21, 51, 69, 78, 85, 122 — six verses of arrogant predatory action against the law-keeper). The scoffer is the meeting-point of both (Pro 21:24 ×2 — zed and za.don). Edom's self-deceptive heart arrogance (Obd 3) and Edom/Babylon's heart-located conviction of invulnerability (Jer 49:16; Jer 50:31–32) extend the register. Youth's insolence to elder as social-order contempt (ra.hav, Isa 3:5) closes the sub-group.

The anchor names the za.don register directly and at its most institutionally concentrated.

## VCG M08-B-VCG-03 — Self-Willed Presumption in NT Narrative — Domineering Authority and Irreverent Boldness

**Description:** Verses from the NT narrative and epistolary tradition evidencing presumptuous defiance in the register of domineering authority, self-willed dismissal of legitimate constraint, and irreverent boldness against divine beings. The archō verses cluster around the domineering-self-will pattern: Peter's presumptuous correction of Jesus (Mat 16:22; Mar 8:32 — self-elevated will resisting submission), the wicked servant assuming unchecked authority (Mat 24:49), the disciples' rivalry over status and preeminence (Mar 10:41), rulers lording it over others as the negative example (Mar 10:42), heritage-based self-assurance as a form of boastful self-reliance (Luk 3:8), the scribes' domineering stance seeking to trap (Luk 11:53), and the unfaithful servant's domineering self-will under delayed accountability (Luk 12:45). Authadēs (Tit 1:7; 2Pe 2:10) names the self-willed disposition that dismisses all authority — disqualifying for leadership and producing irreverence toward divine beings. Tolmētēs (2Pe 2:10) names the reckless presumption that refuses to tremble before the glorious ones.

**Anchor:** vc_id=55569 — Luk 12:45 — G0757 archō — "The term marks the onset of the unfaithful servant beating fellow servants after assuming his master is delayed; his self-elevated assumption of unchecked authority reflects an inner disposition of domineering self-will, directly touching the pride-cluster's theme of arrogant abuse of power." The anchor captures the domineering-authority form at its most complete: unchecked assumption of power in the master's absence.
