## VCG M08-D-VCG-01 — Intellectual and Partisan Conceit — Inflation Through Knowledge and Faction

**Description:** Verses evidencing the puffed-up inflation of the mind through intellectual pride, partisan attachment, or ungrounded mental fantasy. Knowledge inflates while love builds up (1Cor 8:1 — the paradigm verse of intellectual conceit); being puffed up in favor of one leader against another produces factional arrogance that goes beyond Scripture (1Cor 4:6); some presume Paul will not return and are arrogantly puffed up in contemptuous self-confidence (1Cor 4:18); the inner puffed-up state will be exposed as empty boasting when tested by real spiritual capacity (1Cor 4:19); the Corinthians are puffed up over a serious moral failure instead of mourning, with inner inflation preventing proper grief and corrective action (1Cor 5:2); and the "puffed up" state arising from ungrounded mental fantasy in Col 2:18 — conceit rooted in sensuous imagination rather than genuine spiritual knowledge.

**Anchor:** vc_id=33935 — 1Cor 8:1 — G5448 fusioō — "Knowledge inflates the inner person while love builds up others; the puffed-up self-inflation of intellectual pride is set in direct opposition to love's outward, constructive orientation." The anchor captures the cognitive pride register at its most analytically precise: knowledge as the trigger of inflation, love as the structural opposite.

## VCG M08-D-VCG-02 — Conceit as Miscalibrated Self-Assessment — Overreaching the Measure of Faith

**Description:** Verses evidencing vain conceit specifically as the inner person's wrong and inflated estimate of its own worth, standing, or capacity — the miscalibrated self-image. Huperfroneō names thinking of oneself more highly than warranted, beyond the measure of faith God has given each person (Rom 12:3 — the most economical cognitive-pride definition in the NT). Tufoō in its three occurrences evidences: inflated self-regard leading to the same condemnation as the devil (1Ti 3:6 — the new convert puffed up by leadership too quickly); conceit paired with complete lack of understanding, blinding to truth and fueling craving for controversy (1Ti 6:4); and swollen conceit grouped with recklessness and love of pleasure over God, distorting judgment and inverting values (2Ti 3:4). Fusiōsis names conceit as an inner inflation that generates communal disorder (2Cor 12:20). These verses share the cognitive-miscalibration register: the inflated inner image that produces blindness, controversy, and disordered communal life.

**Anchor:** vc_id=60866 — Rom 12:3 — G5252 huperfroneō — "The command not to think of oneself more highly than warranted addresses the mind directly; haughtiness is located in self-assessment that overreaches the measure of faith God has given each person." The most direct and definitional statement of vain conceit as overreaching self-assessment — cognitive pride named at its source.

**Members (5 verses):**
- vc=60866 — Rom 12:3 — G5252 huperfroneō — Not to think of oneself more highly than warranted; self-assessment overreaching the measure of faith.
- vc=33924 — 1Ti 3:6 — G5187 tufoō — New convert puffed up with conceit; inflated self-regard leads to devil's condemnation.
- vc=33925 — 1Ti 6:4 — G5187 tufoō — Conceit paired with lack of understanding; inflated state blinds to truth and fuels controversy.
- vc=33926 — 2Ti 3:4 — G5187 tufoō — Swollen with conceit alongside recklessness; inner inflation distorting judgment.
- vc=33942 — 2Cor 12:20 — G5450 fusiōsis — Conceit as inner inflation generating communal disorder and conflict.
