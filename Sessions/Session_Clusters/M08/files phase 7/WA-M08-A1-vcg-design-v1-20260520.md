## VCG M08-A1-VCG-01 — Prosperity-Induced Heart-Elevation

**Description:** Verses evidencing pride as the upward movement of the heart triggered by material prosperity, abundance, military success, or divine blessing — a satisfaction-to-self-exaltation dynamic where outward increase corrupts the inward orientation. The pattern is consistent: God gives, the heart receives, the heart lifts itself up, and forgetting God follows. The inner movement is not a sudden rebellion but a gradual displacement of gratitude by self-sufficiency. The theological diagnosis across these verses is that wealth and success are the particular triggers that inflate the heart above its proper station. Includes both the warned-against form (Deu 8:14; Deu 17:20 in the prosperity context) and the documented historical failure (2Ch 32:25; Eze 28:5; Hos 13:6).

**Anchor:** vc_id=34232 — Hos 13:6 — rum — "The heart is lifted up in response to fullness and satisfaction — prosperity produces inner exaltation, and that elevated heart leads directly to forgetting God; self-sufficiency generates inner pride." The anchor captures the sequence (fullness → lifted heart → forgetting God) most directly and generatively.

## VCG M08-A1-VCG-02 — Royal and Conquering Heart-Elevation

**Description:** Verses evidencing pride as the elevation of a ruler's, conqueror's, or prince's heart above proper creaturely and divine limits — specifically the self-exaltation of those who hold power or achieve victory and translate their elevated position into a claim of supremacy or divine status. The subject is consistently one who exercises authority: a king, a prince, a military conqueror, a rebel. The heart's self-exaltation here is expressed through the claiming of supreme status (Eze 28:2 — "I am a god"), total willful self-assertion against the divine order (Dan 11:36 — "above every god by his own will"), the pride of military conquest (Dan 11:12 — heart exalted after victory), and the visible grandeur that the heart becomes proud of (Eze 31:10 — tree's proud heart because of its height). Includes also the usurpation-through-rebellion pattern (1Ki 11:26–27 — Jeroboam lifting his hand against the king as the inner impulse to self-exalt against royal authority). Dan 5:20 (heart lifted up and spirit hardening together = Belshazzar) is transitional between this register and VCG-03's constitutional-condition register — assigned here for its explicit royal-ruler subject. The characteristic of this VCG is pride-of-power expressed as the heart's inner claim to the position God alone holds.

**Anchor:** vc_id=34040 — Eze 28:2 — H1361 ga.vah — "The prince of Tyre's heart is called 'proud,' and he has inwardly claimed divine status — this self-exaltation is located explicitly in the heart, the seat of identity, making his arrogance a fundamental distortion of inner being." The most explicit statement of royal heart-elevation as a divine-status claim.

## VCG M08-A1-VCG-03 — The Proud Heart as Constitutive Inner Condition

**Description:** Verses evidencing pride as a settled, dispositional characteristic of the heart — not tied to a specific trigger (prosperity, conquest) or a specific ruling subject, but named as the constitutional inner condition that defines a person's, community's, or people's orientation before God. These verses characterise pride-of-heart as what God finds abominable (Pro 16:5), as the immediate precursor to ruin (Pro 18:12), as the source of boastful contemptuous speech (Psa 17:10), as pride's identified inner seat (Psa 101:5 — "arrogant heart"), as what must be deliberately denied and held back (Psa 131:1), as the seat of Moab's pride cluster (Jer 48:29×2), as the fatal error of the heart that soars in arrogance (Jer 49:16), as what Ephraim and Samaria's defiant speech flows from (Isa 9:9 — "pride and arrogance of heart"), and as a heart-sourced vice in the NT lists (Mar 7:22 — huperēfania from within the heart; Luk 1:51 — arrogant in the thoughts of their hearts). Also includes Pro 21:4 (haughty eyes and proud heart together named as sin — heart as constitutional seat alongside the eye-register), 1Sa 16:7 (God looks at the heart while humans prize outward height — the heart as the real locus of worth and pride), and Psa 17:10 (inner hardness of heart producing boastful speech). Pro 16:18 (go.vah — haughty spirit precedes fall) is included here as the spirit-seat parallel to the heart-seat idiom.

**Anchor:** vc_id=12083 — Pro 16:5 — H1364 ga.vo.ah — "Arrogance is located specifically in the heart, making it a deep inner disposition — the Lord considers such heart-pride an abomination that will certainly be punished." The most economical statement of the proud heart as constitutive inner condition meeting divine opposition.
