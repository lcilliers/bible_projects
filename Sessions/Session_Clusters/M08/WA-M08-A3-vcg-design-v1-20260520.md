## VCG M08-A3-VCG-01 — Moab's Pride and Neighbouring Nations — The Publicly-Known Collective Arrogance

**Description:** Verses where Moab's pride (and closely related Edom, Ammon) is the named subject — described as a publicly-known, reported inner characteristic that underlies the entire posture of self-importance, haughtiness, and empty boasting. Moab is the paradigm case in the OT of national pride as a settled, corporate inner characteristic: the pride is publicly reported ("we have heard of the pride of Moab"), documented in its layers (Jer 48:29 — pride, arrogance, loftiness, haughtiness of heart clustered together), identified as the root of taunting contempt against God's people (Zep 2:10), and met with the removal of the proudly exultant from God's holy community (Zep 3:11 × 2 — both ga.a.vah and ga.vah). Edom's soaring self-exaltation to eagle-heights (Obd 4) carries the same national-arrogance pattern.

**Anchor:** vc_id=33989 — Isa 16:6 — H1347 ga.on — "Moab's pride is the foundational inner characteristic reported — 'we have heard of the pride of Moab' — from which his arrogance, insolence, and empty boasting all flow."

## VCG M08-A3-VCG-02 — Babylon's Pride — National Splendor Destined for Total Ruin

**Description:** Verses where Babylon's (and Ephraim's) pride is the subject — corporate arrogance as self-glorying national splendor that defines the city's greatness and ensures its complete destruction. The king of Babylon's pomp descends to Sheol (Isa 14:11); Babylon's magnificence will be overthrown like Sodom (Isa 13:19); God purposes to defile and dishonor the pompous self-exaltation of Tyre (Isa 23:9); the proud crown of Ephraim's drunken leaders is a fading flower already decaying (Isa 28:1; 28:3); Sodom's inner pride expressed in gross sin provokes God's immediate removal (Eze 16:50). The characteristic is pride as pompous self-glorying splendor that invites exemplary divine judgment.

**Anchor:** vc_id=33987 — Isa 13:19 — H1347 ga.on — "Babylon's pride is expressed as national splendor and self-glorying magnificence; its coming overthrow like Sodom frames that pride as the very quality that makes its ruin total and exemplary."

## VCG M08-A3-VCG-03 — Israel's Pride — The Self-Incriminating Inner Characteristic of God's People

**Description:** Verses where Israel's, Judah's, or Jerusalem's corporate pride is the named subject — a distinctive register because it is the covenant people's own arrogance, which operates as self-incrimination (Hos 5:5 — testifying against Israel to its own face), blocks the return to God (Hos 7:10 — proud disposition actively preventing repentance), drives idolatry (Eze 7:20 — beauty redirected toward idolatry by pride), provokes divine revulsion (Amo 6:8 — God abhors the pride of Jacob), and becomes the ironic ground of a divine oath against the people (Amo 8:7 — God swears by the pride of Jacob). Also includes Judah and Jerusalem's national inner posture God will spoil (Jer 13:9), the prophet's grief over pride driving the community to captivity (Jer 13:17), the proud dismissive period when Jerusalem treated Sodom with contempt (Eze 16:56), and similar patterns in Philistia (Zec 9:6) and Assyria (Zec 10:11).

## VCG M08-A3-VCG-04 — Day-of-the-LORD Universal Collective Humbling — All Lofty Pride Brought Low

**Description:** Verses evidencing pride in a universal, eschatological, or cosmic-collective register — the day of the Lord as the comprehensive judgment of everything lifted up and lofty. This VCG does not name a single nation but the collective of all pride-bearing beings: God's day targets everything proud and lofty (Isa 2:12 ×2 — ge.eh and rum); the lofty pride of humanity will be humbled (Isa 2:11; Isa 2:17); the great and lofty will be hewn down by God's terrifying power (Isa 10:33 ×2 — qo.mah and rum); whatever has exalted itself is subject to cutting down. Also includes: pride as an active inner force allied with human striving that God lays low (Isa 25:11); the boasting Assyrian king's pride expressed through felling the tallest cedars (2Ki 19:23; Isa 37:24); the great tree's heart proud of its soaring stature (Eze 31:10 qo.mah); the ambition to tower above all others that God's judgment terminates (Eze 31:14 qo.mah); the perverse social elevation of what is base (Psa 12:8); and the enemy's collective prideful misattribution of victory (Deu 32:27). The register is cosmic: pride as the universal human posture that the eschatological day of the Lord specifically targets.

The most comprehensive single-verse statement of the day-of-the-Lord as the judgment of all lofty pride.
