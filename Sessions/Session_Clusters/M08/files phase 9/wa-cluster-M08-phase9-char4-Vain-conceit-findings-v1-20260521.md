## T1 — Description and Classification (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The programme name is "Vain conceit." "Conceit" names the cognitive register — a false mental conception of the self; "vain" names the emptiness of that conception — inflated without actual substance, as fusioō (to puff up) names the condition of being swollen beyond one's actual content. The name precisely targets CHAR-4's defining feature: a mind that has constructed an inaccurate, inflated picture of itself. The name distinguishes CHAR-4 from CHAR-1 (dispositional self-elevation), CHAR-2 (volitional transgression), and CHAR-3 (verbal self-display) — CHAR-4 is the inner cognitive form of pride. Rom 12:3 (vc=60866, M08-D-VCG-02): "do not think of yourself more highly than you ought" — huperfroneō names precisely this: thinking (froneō) above (huper) what is warranted.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Three distinct Greek terms evidence the definitional dimensions. Fusioō (G5448) — to inflate, to puff up; the pneumatic metaphor of inflation (wind or air filling and expanding what was solid) names the absence of actual substance beneath the self-inflation. 1Cor 8:1 (vc=33935, M08-D-VCG-01): "knowledge puffs up [fusioō], but love builds up" — inflation as the cognitive response to received knowledge without the relational anchor of love. Tufoō (G5187) — to be wrapped in smoke, to be puffed up with conceit; the smoke metaphor names cognitive obscuring — the mind clouded so that it cannot see clearly. 1Ti 6:4 (vc=33925, M08-D-VCG-02): conceit paired with "understanding nothing." Huperfroneō (G5252) — to think above (huper + froneō) what is warranted; Rom 12:3 (vc=60866, M08-D-VCG-02): "do not think of yourself more highly than you ought, but think with sober judgment." All three terms name the same cognitive register through different metaphors: inflation (fusioō), smoke-obscuration (tufoō), and above-measure thinking (huperfroneō).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The name carries constitutional implications primarily. "Conceit" locates the characteristic in the cognitive faculty — the mind's self-assessment function. Rom 12:3 (vc=60866, M08-D-VCG-02): "think of yourself" — the froneō root directly addresses the thinking/mind faculty. The constitutional implication is that CHAR-4 operates at the mind/cognitive level rather than the heart/volitional level (CHAR-1/2) or the speech/social level (CHAR-3). Relational implications: 1Cor 13:4 (vc=33936, M08-D-VCG-01): "love is not arrogant [fusioō]" — CHAR-4 is relationally incompatible with love; the inflated cognitive self-picture displaces the other-oriented cognition that love requires.

**[CHAR-4]** E — CHAR-4 is primarily a cognitive/epistemic phenomenon — a false, inflated inner mental assessment of the self. Rom 12:3 (vc=60866, M08-D-VCG-02): the command addresses the thinking faculty directly — froneō (to think, to set the mind). 1Ti 6:4 (vc=33925, M08-D-VCG-02): conceit and "understanding nothing" paired — CHAR-4 is a cognitive condition producing cognitive failure. Col 2:18 (vc=33941, M08-D-VCG-01): "inflated [fusioō] by his sensuous mind" — the mind itself is named as the seat of the inflation.
