## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across all five characteristics, the cluster reveals a single structural logic: each characteristic reflects, by inversion or usurpation, a divine attribute that belongs to God alone. What surfaces when the five are compared is the graduated scope of the usurpation. CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation) usurps God's prerogative of genuine exaltation — "the Lord alone will be exalted in that day" (Isa 2:11, M08-A3-VCG-04) names the eschatological resolution that CHAR-1 has deferred. CHAR-2 (Presumptuous defiance) usurps God's sovereign role as the one who establishes the limits and authorities the will transgresses. CHAR-3 (Boasting and self-display) usurps God's status as the sole legitimate object of the glorying-impulse — the same impulse that 1Cor 1:31 (M08-C-VCG-03) commands toward God. CHAR-4 (Vain conceit) usurps God's role as the giver of the accurate measure within which the mind is to assess itself (Rom 12:3, M08-D-VCG-02). CHAR-5 (Pride of power and position) usurps God's status as the sole genuine source of all power and might, with Isa 47:8 (M08-E-VCG-02) taking this to its theological extreme — the creature declaring "I am, and there is no one besides me," claiming the divine I-am. The cluster as a whole reveals that M08 is the cluster of misattribution: every characteristic involves the creature claiming for the self what is genuinely God's — his exaltation, his authority, his glory, his measure, his power.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Scripture does not attribute any of the five M08 characteristics to God. The cluster-level finding is uniform: all five characteristics are evidenced as exclusively creaturely distortions. The analytical significance of this unanimous silence varies by characteristic. For CHAR-1, CHAR-4, and CHAR-5, the silence is definitionally necessary — each presupposes a gap between actual worth and claimed worth (CHAR-4: fusioō, inflation beyond actual substance; CHAR-5: structural goods claimed as self-sufficient basis) that cannot exist for God. For CHAR-2 and CHAR-3's VCG-01 form, the silence reflects structural asymmetry: CHAR-2 requires an authority to defy that is external to the self; CHAR-3's self-directed boasting presupposes misattribution of received goods as self-generated. What the cluster-level comparison surfaces is that this uniform silence is not incidental — it is the cluster's systematic confirmation that M08 names distortions of the created order rather than creaturely analogues to divine attributes.

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The uniform silence across all five characteristics, read together, establishes a cluster-level principle: M08 characteristics are not defective forms of something God possesses in a pure form — they are creatureliness-specific disorders. This distinguishes M08 from clusters whose characteristics have genuine divine analogues (love, wisdom, grief in God). The cluster-level comparison between CHAR-1 (usurping God's exclusive exaltation), CHAR-3's VCG-03 (where the glorying-impulse does have a divine analogue — God delights in his own glory), and CHAR-4 (whose cognitive self-assessment has no divine analogue) reveals an internal differentiation: CHAR-3 uniquely has a positive divine-analogue dimension (God-directed glorying), while CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-4, and CHAR-5 have no positive divine-analogue form whatsoever. The silence of the four, compared with CHAR-3's partial positive, reveals that M08 sits at the boundary between pure-disorder clusters (where all characteristics are exclusively fallen) and mixed clusters (where the same faculty has both a fallen form and a created/redeemed form). CHAR-3 pulls M08 toward the mixed end; the other four hold it at the pure-disorder end.

## T1 — Description and Classification

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The five names encode five distinct registers of the same underlying disorder, and reading them together reveals a taxonomy of pride's expressions. "Arrogant self-elevation" (CHAR-1) names the dispositional-directional register — the inner upward movement of the self. "Presumptuous defiance" (CHAR-2) names the volitional-authority register — the will acting beyond its authorised limits. "Boasting and self-display" (CHAR-3) names the communicative-social register — the inner elevation moving into speech and visible self-assertion. "Vain conceit" (CHAR-4) names the cognitive-epistemic register — the mind's false self-picture. "Pride of power and position" (CHAR-5) names the structural-medium register — pride expressed through and sustained by received structural goods. The cluster-synthesis finding from the five names together: M08 is not a cluster of a single vice with varying intensities — it is a cluster that maps pride across the full range of inner-being faculties (disposition, will, communication, cognition, structural stewardship) and the full range of human existence (inner posture, transgressive act, public speech, self-knowledge, relationship to power).

## Appendix — Synthesis prose

### Theme 1 — The cluster's internal architecture: CHAR-1 as generative head

The 189-prompt cross-characteristic comparison establishes that M08's five characteristics are not five parallel expressions of a single vice at comparable constitutional depth. They form a hierarchy with CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation) as the dispositional head from which the other four derive as faculty-specific expressions.

The generative chain runs: filautos (self-love — 2Ti 3:2, M08-A4-VCG-01, the root motivation) → CHAR-1 (the dispositional self-elevation of the heart as the foundational inner state) → and from CHAR-1 the other four branch: CHAR-2 as the volitional activation of CHAR-1's self-elevation when the will encounters a specific divine authority or limit; CHAR-3 as the communicative expression of CHAR-1's inner elevation through speech and social self-display; CHAR-4 as the cognitive sub-form of CHAR-1 — the same self-elevation applied specifically to the mind's self-assessment faculty; and CHAR-5 as CHAR-1 expressed through and sustained by received structural goods (power, wealth, military might, ease).

This architecture is confirmed by vocabulary evidence: the g-'-h root family (ga.on, ga.vah, ga.a.vah, ge.eh) runs through CHAR-1 and CHAR-5; the huper- Greek prefix (huperēfanos — CHAR-1; huperfroneō — CHAR-4; hupsēlofroneō — CHAR-5) connects three characteristics at the root level; the vice-catalogue sequence filautos → huperēfanos → alazōn (2Ti 3:2) maps the generative chain from root to CHAR-1 to CHAR-3 within a single verse.

The practical implication for Session C framing: the five characteristics are not interchangeable alternatives — they are constitutively related. Addressing CHAR-2 without attending to CHAR-1's dispositional ground is treating the transgressive act without its inner source. Addressing CHAR-4 without attending to CHAR-1's underlying self-elevation is treating the cognitive inflation without its generative posture. The cluster's effective treatment point is CHAR-1's heart-level dispositional orientation.

### Theme 2 — The paradox of prosperity: M08 as a blessing-triggered cluster

The most counter-intuitive finding to emerge from the 189-prompt comparison is the cluster's consistent relationship to received goods. All five characteristics are generated by genuine blessings — prosperity (CHAR-1: Deu 8:14, M08-A1-VCG-01), received knowledge (CHAR-4: 1Cor 8:1, M08-D-VCG-01), structural power and wealth (CHAR-5: Eze 28:5, M08-E-VCG-02; 1Ti 6:17, M08-E-VCG-02), military success (CHAR-1: Dan 11:12, M08-A1-VCG-02), and ease (CHAR-5: Eze 16:49, M08-E-VCG-01; Isa 47:8, M08-E-VCG-02). M08 is not a suffering-generated cluster; it is a prosperity-generated cluster.

This finding runs against intuition: one might expect pride to arise from deprivation (overcompensating for lack) rather than from abundance. The evidence corrects this: pride is what happens when received goods are misattributed. The person who receives God's genuine blessings (prosperity, knowledge, power, beauty) and fails to acknowledge God as their source activates the M08 characteristics — the heart is lifted by what it has received rather than oriented toward the giver.

CHAR-3's VCG-02 is the cluster's only counter-evidence: Paul's examined boasting in 2Cor 10–12 is generated by suffering and weakness (2Cor 12:9, M08-C-VCG-02: "I gladly boast of my weaknesses"). The suffering-generated boasting of VCG-02 is the cluster's internal corrective — it models what happens to the glorying-impulse when suffering strips away the creaturely goods that would otherwise generate VCG-01 self-boasting. Suffering, in CHAR-3's evidence, purifies the boasting-impulse by removing its illegitimate objects.

The pastoral implication is significant: the conditions of divine blessing are simultaneously the conditions of M08's greatest danger. Deu 8:14's warning is the cluster's programmatic statement — "when you eat and are full... and your heart is lifted up, and you forget the Lord your God." The antidote is not poverty or deprivation but the formation of gratitude and acknowledgment: knowing the giver of the gift prevents the gift from becoming the ground of CHAR-1's self-elevation.

### Theme 3 — The OT/NT vocabulary arc across the cluster

The vocabulary comparison across the five characteristics reveals a systematic theological development from OT to NT that is one of M08's most significant analytical findings.

The OT vocabulary is characterised by: spatial metaphor dominance (rum — to be high; g-'-h root — to rise, swell; ma.rom — height; ga.on — rising grandeur); collective/national register emphasis (the ga.on proud-might of Egypt, Israel, Sodom, Edom, Babylon); and legal-judicial address (Deu 17:12/18:20's covenant-law framing of CHAR-2; the prophetic-oracle address to nations in CHAR-5).

The NT vocabulary shifts in three directions: first, toward psychological and motivational precision (authadēs — self-pleasing will; filautos — self-love as root motivation; hupsēlofroneō — wealth-grounded cognitive haughtiness); second, toward social-interaction precision (huperēfanos — appearing above others in the social field; alazōn/alazoneia — the braggart's social-display posture from Hellenistic social vocabulary); third, toward cognitive characterisation (fusioō — inflating beyond actual substance; tufoō — obscured by one's own smoke; huperfroneō — thinking above warranted measure).

The most significant OT/NT developmental finding: CHAR-4 (Vain conceit) is entirely NT — there is no Hebrew OT equivalent in M08's evidence base for the specific form of cognitive self-inflation that fusioō/tufoō/huperfroneō name. This suggests that the NT period — specifically the Pauline community correspondence — developed the programme's most precise vocabulary for cognitive self-assessment pride, addressing the specific intellectual and partisan contexts of early Christian communities (1Cor 4:6/8:1; Col 2:18).

CHAR-3 undergoes the most extensive NT development: the OT offers ha.lal in its self-directed boasting sense (Jer 9:23; Pro 27:1; 1Ki 20:11), while the NT develops the full three-register kauchaomai structure (condemned/examined/God-directed) in the Pauline corpus. This is M08's most complete OT-to-NT theological development — the glorying-capacity receives its fullest treatment in the NT's engagement with the cross and the apostolic ministry.

### Theme 4 — The cluster's self-sealing perceptive pattern

Across all 33 T3.1 prompts (perception faculty), every M08 characteristic evidences the same structural pattern: perceptive closure precisely at the channel that would correct the characteristic. This is the cluster's most analytically consistent finding and requires cluster-level attention.

CHAR-1 closes perception of God (Psa 10:4, M08-A2-VCG-02: "there is no God" as the endpoint of functional pride). CHAR-2 removes God from the perceptive field (Psa 86:14, M08-B-VCG-02: "they do not set God before them"). CHAR-3's VCG-01 form closes perception of self-inconsistency (Rom 2:23, M08-C-VCG-01: boasting in the law while breaking it, unable to perceive the discrepancy). CHAR-4 closes perception of truth comprehensively (1Ti 6:4, M08-D-VCG-02: "understanding nothing"). CHAR-5 closes perception of actual vulnerability (Obd 3, M08-E-VCG-02: "the pride of your heart has deceived you, you who say in your heart, 'who will bring me down?'").

The pattern's implication: M08 characteristics are constitutively self-sealing — each closes the specific perceptive channel through which it would be corrected. This explains why the cluster's consistent corrective mechanism is external catastrophic reversal (divine breaking, imperial collapse, judicial exposure) rather than internal self-correction. The person bearing any M08 characteristic cannot perceive from within their own state that they are in that state — the characteristic has closed the perceptive apparatus that would generate that perception.

This is the cluster-level analogue of what wisdom literature names in Pro 16:18 (M08-A1-VCG-03) — "pride goes before destruction": the destruction is not merely the consequence of pride but the mechanism by which pride's self-sealing perceptive closure is overcome. When the structural goods that CHAR-5 rests on are removed, the perceptive barrier to vulnerability is broken; when CHAR-4's cognitive inflation is exposed by Paul's apostolic confrontation (1Cor 4:19, M08-D-VCG-01), the epistemic closure is interrupted. The external corrective must overcome what the internal corrective cannot reach.

### Theme 5 — M08 as a justice cluster: CHAR-5 and the poor

One of the cluster-synthesis findings that does not surface within any single characteristic but only in the comparison is the direct connection between M08's structural-power register (CHAR-5) and social injustice. Eze 16:49 (M08-E-VCG-01) is the key verse: "this was the guilt of your sister Sodom: she and her daughters had pride, excess of food, and prosperous ease, but did not aid the poor and needy."

This verse identifies CHAR-5 (pride of power and position) as the root sin that produces social injustice — the failure to aid the poor is not Sodom's primary sin; it is the consequence of its primary sin, which is pride-in-structural-ease. The cluster-synthesis confirms this: CHAR-5's self-sufficiency conviction ("I am, and there is no one besides me" — Isa 47:8, M08-E-VCG-02) structurally excludes the neighbour in need from the moral horizon. Once the person declares ontological self-completeness, the neighbour's need registers as irrelevant.

This finding establishes M08-CHAR-5 as the inner-being root of social injustice in the programme's evidence: structural power-pride systematically produces contempt for the vulnerable. The practical implication runs from the programme's analytical vocabulary into pastoral and ethical application: addressing poverty and social neglect requires attending to M08's structural-power characteristics, not merely to the surface behaviour of neglect.

The counter-evidence from CHAR-5's blocking condition (1Ti 6:17–18, M08-E-VCG-02) confirms the cluster-level implication: the corrective to CHAR-5 is generosity — "ready to share" — which is the relational posture that CHAR-5's self-sufficiency claim specifically excludes. Generosity is structural anti-CHAR-5.

### Theme 6 — CHAR-3's unique position: the cluster's redemptive register

The cluster synthesis surfaces CHAR-3 (Boasting and self-display) as the only M08 characteristic with an internally evidenced redemptive form. This distinction is unique within M08 and significant for the cluster's overall theological identity.

The three registers of CHAR-3 — condemned (VCG-01), examined (VCG-02), and God-directed (VCG-03) — mean that CHAR-3 is simultaneously an M08 characteristic (in its VCG-01 form) and the model for its own redemption (in VCG-02/03). The same kauchaomai verb that names the condemned boasting of VCG-01 names the eschatological community-boasting of VCG-03 (1Th 2:19, M08-C-VCG-03). The same ha.lal root that names the self-directed boasting condemned in Jer 9:23 (M08-C-VCG-01) names the God-directed glorying of the M22 register.

This makes CHAR-3 the cluster's distinctive characteristic: it alone evidences that the underlying human capacity (the glorying-impulse) is created and legitimate. The glorying-impulse does not need to be suppressed; it needs to be redirected. 1Cor 1:31 (M08-C-VCG-03) — "let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord" — does not prohibit boasting but commands it toward its proper object. This is unique within M08: for CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-4, and CHAR-5, there is no commanded positive form — there is only the corrected inverse (humility, reverent submission, sober judgment, generous stewardship). For CHAR-3, there is a commanded positive form of the characteristic itself.

The implication for Session C: CHAR-3 should be presented as the cluster's most positive-valence characteristic — the one in which the evidence shows both the fallen form (VCG-01) and the redeemed form (VCG-02/03) of the same human capacity. This protects against a reading of M08 that condemns the glorying-impulse entirely, when the evidence commends it in its properly directed form.

### Theme 7 — The cluster's christological convergence

The typological analysis (T0.4) surfaces M08's christological convergence points. The five characteristics point toward christological material from three different directions.

First, the cross as the evacuation of boasting-grounds (CHAR-3, Gal 6:14, M08-C-VCG-02; 1Cor 1:29–31, M08-C-VCG-01/03): the cross removes all human credential-grounds and redirects the glorying-impulse toward God alone. This is M08's most direct christological address — the cross is the structural answer to CHAR-3's self-directed boasting.

Second, the servant-authority inversion of domineering authority (CHAR-2, Mar 10:41–42, M08-B-VCG-03): Jesus names the Gentile domineering-authority pattern (CHAR-2's domineering-authority mode) and inverts it through servant-authority. The christological counter to CHAR-2 is not merely the prohibition of domineering but the positive modelling of the servant posture.

Third, the adversarial paradigm connection (CHAR-4, 1Ti 3:6, M08-D-VCG-02): human cognitive self-inflation participates in "the condemnation of the devil" — the adversarial figure whose original pride is the paradigm case of CHAR-4. The christological implication runs through the inverse: Christ, who "did not consider equality with God a thing to be grasped" (Phili 2:6 — not cited in §3 but thematically adjacent), models the cognitive humility that is CHAR-4's structural opposite.

The cluster's christological material concentrates in CHAR-2 and CHAR-3 — the volitional-authority and communicative-boasting registers. This is consistent with the NT's sustained attention to authority patterns (CHAR-2 addressed through servant-authority) and the boasting discourse (CHAR-3 addressed through the cross and the examined-boasting discipline). CHAR-1, CHAR-4, and CHAR-5's christological connections are primarily eschatological rather than incarnation-or-cross-specific.

### Theme 8 — Unexpected pattern: the cluster's affective profile

One finding that the 189-prompt structure surfaces clearly but which had not been explicitly anticipated is M08's distinctive affective profile. The cluster comparison (T3.4) reveals that M08 characteristics are characteristically pleasant for the person bearing them — the affective experience of pride is positive (confidence, ease, felt competence, satisfaction of social recognition, secure structural power).

This distinguishes M08 from other potential disorder-clusters in the programme that might generate unpleasant inner experience as a signal of dysfunction. M08 characteristics generate positive inner affect: CHAR-1's aspiration feels like legitimate confidence; CHAR-2's transgressive will feels like righteous self-assertion; CHAR-3's boasting generates the satisfaction of social recognition; CHAR-4's knowledge-conceit produces the felt competence of intellectual superiority; CHAR-5's ease generates the secure pleasantness that Isa 47:8's a.din names.

The exception is CHAR-3's VCG-02/03 registers, which generate joy through suffering — a paradoxical affective profile in which the positive affect (joy, overflowing comfort — 2Cor 7:4, M08-C-VCG-03) arises from conditions of difficulty and weakness. This is the cluster's only sustained positive affective counter-example — and it is generated by the inverse of the conditions that generate the other characteristics' positive affect (suffering vs ease).

The practical implication: M08 characteristics will not be identified by the person bearing them through affective signals — pride feels good. The cluster's identification requires either external confrontation (the prophetic oracle, the apostolic correction, the wisdom teacher's warning) or the cultivation of the perceptive faculties that pride closes (T3.1.2).
