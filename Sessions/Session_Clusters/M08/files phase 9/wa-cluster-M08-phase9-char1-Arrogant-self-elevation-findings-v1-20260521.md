## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence reveals that God alone holds the prerogative of genuine exaltation, and that CHAR-1 is the creature's usurpation of that singular divine position. The structural logic is stated explicitly in the day-of-the-Lord material: Isa 2:11 (vc=34601, M08-A3-VCG-04) — "the lofty pride of men shall be humbled, and the Lord alone will be exalted in that day"; Isa 2:17 (vc=34602, M08-A3-VCG-04) — "the lofty pride of men shall be brought low, and the Lord alone will be exalted in that day." Both verses frame CHAR-1's humbling as the condition for the restoration of God's exclusive exaltation. Psa 75:7 (vc=34230, M08-A4-VCG-05) states the governing principle: "it is God who executes judgment, putting down one and lifting up another" — exaltation is God's sovereign act, not the creature's. CHAR-1 therefore reflects, by inversion, the divine attribute of true exaltation: the characteristic is what arises when the creature competes for what belongs to God alone.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Scripture does not attribute arrogant self-elevation to God. The 174 Phase 5.5 set-aside verses (the M22-register rum, ga.on, ga.vah material) carried divine-majesty content that belongs to God's own register and was correctly excluded from CHAR-1's corpus. What the remaining §3 evidence consistently shows is CHAR-1 as the creature's counterfeit of what God legitimately holds: Dan 11:36 (vc=34235, M08-A1-VCG-02) — the king "exalts himself and magnifies himself above every god" by his own will, explicitly placing CHAR-1 as a movement against God's divine position; Eze 28:2 (vc=34040, M08-A1-VCG-02) — "your heart is proud, and you have said, 'I am a god, I sit in the seat of the gods'" — the creature claiming divine status is what CHAR-1 names at its sharpest. The silence of the evidence regarding God possessing CHAR-1 is itself the finding: arrogant self-elevation is exclusively creaturely, a distortion that competes with the divine rather than reflecting it.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The silence is theologically load-bearing. The §3 corpus consistently deploys CHAR-1 as what stands against God — against his judicial authority (Psa 94:2, vc=33952, M08-A4-VCG-02: the proud "deserve" divine retribution), against his sole supremacy (Isa 2:11–12, M08-A3-VCG-04: "the Lord alone will be exalted"), against his revealed character (Isa 37:23, vc=34304, M08-A4-VCG-05: raising the voice against "the Holy One of Israel"). The fact that God is never the subject of CHAR-1 in this corpus — while God is consistently the one who opposes, humbles, and reverses CHAR-1 — places CHAR-1 outside the divine image as such. The characteristic is not an image-bearing quality distorted; it is the creature's attempt to occupy God's own position, which makes it categorically different from characteristics that are genuine creaturely analogues to divine attributes.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The verse evidence is silent on any created purpose for arrogant self-elevation. Every verse in §3 treats CHAR-1 as either condemned (Pro 16:5, vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03: "everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination to the Lord"), causally linked to destruction (Pro 16:18, vc=34073, M08-A1-VCG-03: "pride goes before destruction"), or identified as a fallen inner movement to be opposed and reversed (Psa 18:27, vc=34242, M08-A2-VCG-01: "the haughty eyes you bring down"; Jam 4:6, vc=33931, M08-A4-VCG-01: "God opposes the proud"). No verse in the corpus treats self-elevation as a God-given capacity functioning properly. The silence regarding a created purpose is analytically significant: CHAR-1 appears to have no positive functional role in the evidence — it is consistently the disorder, not the design.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence consistently places CHAR-1 within the fallen condition, not the original created design. Mar 7:22 (vc=33928, M08-A4-VCG-01) situates pride explicitly among "the evil things that come out of a person" — constitutively internal but categorised as moral corruption. Pro 16:5 (vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03) — "everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination to the Lord" — treats CHAR-1 as a universal feature of the fallen inner person, not as created design. The NT vice catalogues (Rom 1:30, vc=33932, M08-A4-VCG-01: haughtiness among vices of a morally disordered mind; 2Ti 3:2, vc=33933, M08-A4-VCG-01: arrogance as a defining inner trait of last-days moral collapse) position CHAR-1 within the characterology of human fallenness. No verse in §3 attributes CHAR-1 to the original created order. The evidence is consistent: CHAR-1 is a fallen characteristic.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness — something the person is moving toward, not only what they currently are?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence consistently orients CHAR-1 toward a future humbling and reversal, not toward any fullness. The day-of-the-Lord material makes this eschatological trajectory explicit: Isa 2:11 (vc=34089, M08-A2-VCG-01): "the haughty looks of man shall be brought low"; Isa 2:12 (vc=33957, M08-A3-VCG-04): "the Lord of hosts has a day against all that is proud and lofty, against all that is lifted up — and it shall be brought low"; Eza 31:14 (vc=54400, M08-A3-VCG-04): "all this is in order that no trees by the waters may grow to towering height... for they are all given over to death." The structural wisdom maxim (Pro 16:18, vc=34073, M08-A1-VCG-03) — "pride goes before destruction" — identifies CHAR-1's orientation as toward collapse, not toward fullness. CHAR-1 has no positive eschatological trajectory; it moves toward the judgment that reverses it.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person — what aspect of being made in God's likeness does it instantiate?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence does not frame CHAR-1 as an expression of the divine image but as its distortion. The evidence that comes closest to addressing this is Eze 28:2 (vc=34040, M08-A1-VCG-02): the prince of Tyre's heart is proud and he claims "I am a god, I sit in the seat of the gods" — the divine-image capacity to stand before God and exercise authority over creation is here twisted into a claim to be God rather than a creature who bears God's image. Isa 14:13 (vc=34233, M08-A4-VCG-05): "You said in your heart, 'I will ascend to heaven; above the stars of God I will set my throne on high'" — the capacity of the human will to aspire and declare is directed against the divine order rather than within it. CHAR-1 instantiates the divine-image capacity for self-directed agency and aspiration, but misdirects it: the creature uses image-bearing capacity to claim the Creator's position.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue to something in God?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence confirms CHAR-1 as exclusively creaturely, not shared with God. The 174 set-aside M22-register verses that carry the vocabulary of divine exaltation (rum, ga.on in the divine-majesty register) were excluded at Phase 5.5 precisely because they belong to God's own legitimate exaltation, not to CHAR-1. What remains in §3 is consistently the creature's movement against God's position: Psa 75:6–7 (vc=34301, vc=34230, M08-A4-VCG-05) — "not from the east or from the west... comes lifting up, but it is God who executes judgment, putting down one and lifting up another." This structural declaration excludes any human claim to self-elevation as legitimate: the creature does not share in the divine prerogative of exaltation. CHAR-1 is the creature reaching for what God alone holds, which confirms its exclusively creaturely character.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence reveals that CHAR-1's presence marks the divine image in severe disorder. The most direct evidence is Luk 1:51 (vc=33930, M08-A1-VCG-03): God "has scattered the proud in the thoughts of their hearts" — the very thoughts of the heart, the inner person's deepest cognitive-volitional centre, are named as the site of arrogance. Psa 10:4 (vc=34074, M08-A2-VCG-02): "in the pride of his face the wicked does not seek him; all his thoughts are, 'there is no God'" — CHAR-1 produces functional atheism, the displacement of God from the inner horizon entirely, which is the divine image oriented away from its source and ground. Pro 16:5 (vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03): "everyone who is arrogant in heart is an abomination to the Lord" — the image-bearer's heart becomes what God finds most repugnant. Conversely, Psa 131:1 (vc=34059, vc=34224, M08-A1-VCG-03, M08-A2-VCG-01) evidences the alternative: the heart not lifted up and eyes not raised high as the image-bearing posture of creaturely lowliness before God.

Second, the day-of-the-Lord material: Isa 2:12 (vc=33957, M08-A3-VCG-04): "the Lord of hosts has a day against all that is proud and lofty" — CHAR-1 is deployed as the eschatological target of divine judgment, the characteristic whose universal humbling constitutes the eschatological reversal. The Isa 14:13 declaration (vc=34233, M08-A4-VCG-05) — "I will ascend to heaven; above the stars of God I will set my throne" — functions as the paradigm statement of CHAR-1's final form, typologically representing all creaturely self-elevation at its extreme.

## T1 — Description and Classification (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The programme name is "Arrogant self-elevation." The name encodes three essential elements that the verse evidence directly substantiates. First, the direction: elevation — an upward movement, rooted in the spatial metaphor running through the Hebrew vocabulary (rum — to be high/to rise; ga.on — rising grandeur; ga.vah — to exalt/rise proudly) and the Greek (huperēfanos — appearing above; hupsēlos — high). Second, the agent: self — the inner person as the one doing the lifting, not receiving it from outside or from God; Psa 75:6 (vc=34301, M08-A4-VCG-05) — "not from the east or from the west... comes lifting up" — names the illegitimacy of self-directed elevation. Third, the mode: arrogant — the elevation is illicit, claimed rather than given, competing with what belongs to God alone. The name already distinguishes CHAR-1 from legitimate dignified standing (which the evidence is silent on as CHAR-1) and from God's genuine exaltation (M22, set aside at Phase 5.5).

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — At the definitional level, the primary terms converge on a single spatial-metaphorical core: upward movement of the self beyond proper station. Hebrew rum (H7311A) — to be high, to rise, to lift up — is the most voluminous term; its active form evidences the will's upward drive (Num 15:30, vc=65731, M08-A4-VCG-05: acting "with a high hand" as deliberate self-exaltation against God; Isa 14:13, vc=34233, M08-A4-VCG-05: "I will ascend to heaven"). Hebrew ga.vah (H1361) — to exalt, to rise proudly — is most closely associated with the heart-seat idiom (Eze 28:2, vc=34040, M08-A1-VCG-02: "your heart is proud"; 2Ch 26:16, vc=34044, M08-A1-VCG-01: "he grew proud"). Greek huperēfanos (G5244) — from huper (above) + phainomai (to appear) — names the person who presents themselves above others, adding a visible/social precision the Hebrew vocabulary does not emphasise (Luk 1:51, vc=33930, M08-A1-VCG-03: arrogance in "the thoughts of their hearts"; Jam 4:6, vc=33931, M08-A4-VCG-01: the inner stance that draws divine opposition). Before deeper analysis, the terms uniformly name: upward, self-directed, beyond proper creaturely limit.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The name carries all three. Directional: the characteristic moves upward — Deu 17:20 (vc=34231, M08-A1-VCG-01): "his heart may not be lifted up above his brothers"; Dan 11:36 (vc=34235, M08-A1-VCG-02): the king "exalts himself and magnifies himself above every god." The direction is constitutively comparative — elevation is always elevation over someone or something. Relational: the upward movement is measured against God and other human beings simultaneously; Psa 138:6 (vc=12086, vc=34300, M08-A4-VCG-02) evidences the relational consequence: "the haughty he knows from afar" — divine relational distance as the structural outcome. Constitutional: the evidence immediately locates CHAR-1 in the heart (Pro 16:5, vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03: "arrogant in heart"), the spirit (Pro 16:18, vc=34073, M08-A1-VCG-03: "haughty spirit"), and the eyes/posture (Pro 6:17, vc=34247, M08-A2-VCG-01: "haughty eyes") — pointing to a characteristic that penetrates to the deepest constitutional levels of the person.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — CHAR-1 is primarily a disposition — a settled inner orientation of the self toward its own elevation — that also takes act-forms and expresses outwardly as a condition. The dispositional classification is directly evidenced: Dan 4:37 (vc=34094, M08-A4-VCG-04): "those who walk in pride" — pride as a habitual gait, a settled mode of moving through life; Isa 16:6 (vc=33943, M08-A4-VCG-04): "how proud he is" — pride as a known, reported stable characteristic; Pro 16:5 (vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03): "everyone who is arrogant in heart" — a constitutive inner quality. The act-form is also evidenced: Num 15:30 (vc=65731, M08-A4-VCG-05): acting "with a high hand" — a specific volitional episode; 1Ki 11:26 (vc=34237, M08-A1-VCG-02): "lifted up his hand against the king" — a singular act of self-assertion. The disposition precedes and generates the acts; the wisdom corpus (Pro 16:18, vc=34073, M08-A1-VCG-03; Pro 16:5, vc=12083) treats CHAR-1 as a stable inner characteristic with predictable causal consequences, not as a momentary state.

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — CHAR-1 is complex in structure. The seat-of-pride axis that generated the four sub-groups (M08-A1 through M08-A4) is itself evidence of internal differentiation within a single characteristic: the same inner movement of self-elevation is named through the heart (Pro 16:5, vc=12083, M08-A1-VCG-03: "arrogant in heart"), the eyes (Pro 6:17, vc=34247, M08-A2-VCG-01: "haughty eyes"), the national/collective register (Isa 16:6, vc=33989, M08-A3-VCG-01: "we have heard of the pride of Moab"), and the general dispositional form (Pro 16:18, vc=34073; Rom 1:30, vc=33932, M08-A4-VCG-01). CHAR-1 has constituent elements — triggers, sub-registers, and generative roots — while remaining a single characteristic identity.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic — encapsulating its constitutional location, the faculties it primarily engages, and its impact on the person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Arrogant self-elevation is the constitutive inner movement in which the self — located primarily in the heart (M08-A1: Pro 16:5, vc=12083; Eze 28:2, vc=34040) but also manifesting through the eyes and posture (M08-A2: Pro 6:17, vc=34247; Pro 30:13, vc=34249) and attributable to nations and collectives (M08-A3: Isa 16:6, vc=33989; Hos 5:5, vc=34000) — lifts itself above its proper creaturely station before God and others, claiming standing, honour, or status that does not belong to it. It operates as a settled disposition (Dan 4:37, vc=34094: "those who walk in pride") that is generated by a self-love root (2Ti 3:2, vc=1899: filautos) and activated by prosperity, power, beauty, or achievement (Deu 8:14, vc=34223; 2Ch 26:16, vc=34044; Eze 28:17, vc=34047).
