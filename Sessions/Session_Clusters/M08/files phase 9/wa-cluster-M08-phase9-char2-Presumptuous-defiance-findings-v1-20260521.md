## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The verse evidence reveals that CHAR-2 (Presumptuous defiance) reflects the divine nature by standing in direct opposition to it: CHAR-2 is the creature's will asserting itself against the very limits God has established, and the evidence consistently frames God as the one who establishes those limits and judges their violation. Neh 9:10 (vc=34125, M08-B-VCG-01): "you performed signs and wonders against Pharaoh and all his servants and all the people of his land, for you knew that they acted arrogantly against our ancestors" — God's counter-action to CHAR-2 reveals his character as the sovereign who defines the limits the will transgresses. Deu 17:12 (vc=34110, M08-B-VCG-02): the man who acts presumptuously against the appointed judge "shall die; so you shall purge the evil from Israel" — God's ordination of authority structures is what CHAR-2 violates. CHAR-2 does not image any divine attribute positively; it images the creature's attempt to claim the sovereignty over limits that belongs to God alone.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** S — Scripture does not attribute presumptuous defiance to God within the 45-verse §3.A corpus. God is consistently evidenced as the one who establishes the authority and revealed commands that CHAR-2 transgresses, and as the one who judges CHAR-2. Deu 18:20 (vc=34119, M08-B-VCG-01): "the prophet who presumes to speak a word in my name that I have not commanded him to speak" — God is the source of the command that the presumptuous prophet violates. The evidence is entirely silent on God possessing the boiling inner self-will that defines CHAR-2.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The silence is analytically significant: CHAR-2 (the will boiling over against divinely-established limits) is constitutively impossible for God to possess, since God himself establishes those limits. The characteristic requires an external authority to defy — there is no authority external to God against which God could presume. Deu 17:12 (vc=34110, M08-B-VCG-02) and Deu 18:20 (vc=34119, M08-B-VCG-01) both frame CHAR-2 as transgression of limits that God himself has ordained (the appointed judge, the commanded word). The silence confirms CHAR-2 as a characteristically creaturely distortion: only a creature who stands under authority can defy that authority presumptuously.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-2]** S — The verse evidence is entirely silent on any created purpose for presumptuous defiance. Every verse in §3.A treats CHAR-2 as a transgression to be condemned, purged, or punished. Deu 17:13 (vc=34124, M08-B-VCG-01): "all the people shall hear and fear and not act presumptuously again" — the explicit goal is eradication of the behaviour, not any affirmation of its purpose. No verse in the 45-verse corpus evidences CHAR-2 serving a positive created function.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence consistently places CHAR-2 within the fallen condition. The historical narrative of Israel's repeated presumptuous defiance frames it as the pattern of the fallen community's response to God's commands: Neh 9:16 (vc=34120, M08-B-VCG-01): "our ancestors acted presumptuously and stiffened their neck and did not obey your commandments"; Neh 9:29 (vc=34126): "even after warnings, they acted presumptuously again" — the repetition underscores that CHAR-2 is a characteristic feature of fallen humanity's disposition toward divine authority, not a designed capacity. Authadēs in Tit 1:7 (vc=42740, M08-B-VCG-03) — the opposite of what an overseer must be — confirms CHAR-2 as a moral failure to be overcome, not a design feature.

**[CHAR-2]** E — CHAR-2 expresses the divine image not as a proper expression but as its distortion. Isa 13:11 (vc=34104, M08-B-VCG-02): "I will put an end to the pomp of the arrogant" — CHAR-2 is the image-bearing capacity for purposeful, energetic agency misdirected into self-assertion against the very author of that capacity. ## T1 — Description and Classification (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The programme name is "Presumptuous defiance." The name encodes two essential elements: (a) defiance — the characteristic is directed against an external authority (divinely-established limits, appointed persons, revealed commands); and (b) presumptuous — the will acts beyond what it has been authorised to claim, assuming a right or freedom it does not possess. The name distinguishes CHAR-2 from simple disobedience (which might be reluctant or passive) and from CHAR-1 (elevated self-regard): CHAR-2 is the active, forceful, deliberate assertion of the self against a specific known limit. The Hebrew root zid (boiling, seething) is directly encoded in "presumptuous" — the characteristic is not cool calculation but a will that boils over, as Deu 1:43 (vc=34123, M08-B-VCG-01) evidences: Israel "acted presumptuously and went up into the hill country" despite God's explicit prohibition.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The primary terms converge on a single core: forceful self-willed assertion against an external authority. Hebrew zid (H2102) — to boil, seethe; the root metaphor is of a liquid boiling over its container — the will exceeding the boundaries established by divine command. Deu 18:20 (vc=34119, M08-B-VCG-01): "the prophet who presumes [zid] to speak a word in my name that I have not commanded." Hebrew za.don (H2087) — insolence, presumptuousness — the quality that drives wilful transgression; Pro 13:10 (vc=34113, M08-B-VCG-02): "by insolence [za.don] comes nothing but strife." Hebrew zed (H2086) — the arrogant/insolent person who treats divine law with contempt; Psa 119:21 (vc=34098, M08-B-VCG-02): "you rebuke the insolent [zed], accursed ones, who wander from your commandments." Greek authadēs (G0829) — self-pleasing, self-willed, refusing to be constrained by others; Tit 1:7 (vc=42740, M08-B-VCG-03): an overseer must not be "self-willed [authadēs]." Greek tolmētēs (G5113) — one who dares recklessly; 2Pe 2:10 (vc=4670, M08-B-VCG-03): the "bold man" who does not tremble before glorious ones. All terms name the same inner reality from different angles: the will's self-authorised transgression.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The name carries all three. Directional: CHAR-2 moves against — against the authority that established the limit, against the command that defined it. Deu 1:43 (vc=34123, M08-B-VCG-01): "you acted presumptuously and went up" — the movement is upward and against despite divine prohibition. Relational: CHAR-2 requires a relational other (God, appointed authority, revealed law) against whom to assert; it is inherently comparative and oppositional. Psa 86:14 (vc=34097, M08-B-VCG-02): "the insolent have risen up against me, a band of ruthless men seek my life; they do not set God before them." Constitutional: the zid/zed/za.don vocabulary roots CHAR-2 in the will — the volitional faculty, not the affective or cognitive. It is an act of the will's self-assertion more than a felt impulse or a cognitive error.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — CHAR-2 is primarily an act-form (the volitional act of transgressing a known limit) with a dispositional substrate that generates and sustains it. The act-form evidence: Deu 1:43 (vc=34123, M08-B-VCG-01) — "you acted presumptuously and went up" — a specific transgressive episode; Deu 18:20 (vc=34119) — "the prophet who presumes to speak" — a specific wilful speech-act. The dispositional substrate evidence: Pro 21:24 (vc=34096, vc=34114, M08-B-VCG-02) — "haughty, arrogant, 'scoffer' is his name; he acts with arrogant pride [za.don]" — za.don as the settled characteristic of the scoffer; Eze 7:10 (vc=34118, M08-B-VCG-02): arrogance "has blossomed, pride has budded" — dispositional growth imagery. CHAR-2 is primarily a characteristic of the act-register (distinct from CHAR-1's dispositional register), but sustained acts of presumptuous defiance generate the za.don dispositional identity evidenced in the scoffer.

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — CHAR-2 has constituent elements differentiated by direction of transgression and vocabulary register. Three registers are evidenced: (a) M08-B-VCG-01 (9 verses) — the zid-boiling register: direct presumptuous defiance of a named divine command or prohibition (Deu 1:43; Deu 18:20; Neh 9:10/16/29); (b) M08-B-VCG-02 (25 verses) — the zed/za.don insolence register: contemptuous disregard for divine law, appointed authority, and revealed truth, generating social insolence and relational aggression (Psa 119 corpus, Pro 11:2, Pro 13:10, Jer 50:31); (c) M08-B-VCG-03 (11 verses) — the NT self-willed/presumptuous-daring register: authadēs (self-willed dismissal of legitimate constraint), tolmētēs (reckless presumption before divine beings), archō M08-relational verses (domineering authority-assertion in narrative). These are constituent registers within a single characteristic — all involving the will asserting itself against a known limit — not separate characteristics.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Presumptuous defiance is the inner-being state in which the will — located constitutionally in the volitional faculty and expressed through the stiff-necked refusal to submit — asserts itself against known divinely-established limits, appointed human authority, or revealed divine commands with a boiling-over force. It is the volitional/active form of pride: where CHAR-1 elevates the self in disposition, CHAR-2 activates that elevation as a deliberate transgression of a specific boundary. Primary vocabulary: zid (the boiling-over internal force of the transgressive will), za.don (the insolent inner quality that characterises the habitual defier), zed (the person-type of arrogant law-contempt). It generates predictable consequences: disgrace (Pro 11:2, vc=34109, M08-B-VCG-02), strife (Pro 13:10, vc=34113), and eschatological destruction (Mal 4:1, vc=34107, M08-B-VCG-02).
