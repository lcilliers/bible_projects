## §6.3.2 — Verse-level relationship test for TRANSFERS (CRITICAL)

A TRANSFERS verdict requires more than "the term's meaning corpus aligns primarily with another cluster's characteristic." Corpus-primary-register reasoning alone is too aggressive.

**The test, verse by verse:**

1. Read every verse in the term's meaning corpus.
2. For each verse, ask: *does this verse evidence any relationship with or impact on M08's characteristic (pride, arrogance, boasting)?* "Relationship" is broad: direct evidence, structural opposite (humility-by-contrast), instrument-of, response-to, protective-against, in-tension-with.

**Verdict rule:**
- If **any** verse evidences a relationship with M08 → **STAYS** with cross-register flag in the rationale.
- If **no** verse evidences any M08 relationship → **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster}** confirmed (accidental placement).

The cross-register flag travels with the term into Phase 5/7 and informs Phase 9 T6 (Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics) findings.

**Worked example (M07 v1 → v2 revision, 2026-05-19):** `G1848 exoutheneō` — v1 verdict TRANSFERS-TO-M06 on corpus-primary-register grounds (contempt). v2 verdict STAYS with M06 cross-register flag: every verse evidences the contempt-shame relational dynamic in M07. The dynamic itself was M07-relational content.

For M08, the same principle applies: a term that primarily names humility (M09), strength (M23), or majesty (M22) but whose verses repeatedly evidence pride-by-contrast or pride-being-judged → STAYS with cross-register flag.

## Disallowed BOUNDARY reasons (per v2_8 §6.3.1)

BOUNDARY may **NOT** be assigned solely because:

- The term's meaning corpus is predominantly horizontal (human-to-human) rather than vertical (God-directed).
- The meanings describe sensory / material / circumstantial inner-being rather than overtly spiritual inner-being.
- The meanings include corrupt, illicit, or morally-negative inner-being content (predatory pride, illicit ambition, etc.). **Pride is intrinsically morally negative** — that is its analytical signature; not a disqualifier.
- The meanings describe an inner-being state you would prefer not to be in scope.

Inner being is the entire human inner life — no theological narrowing.

## Valid BOUNDARY reasons (cite one of these three)

1. **Cluster-membership undecided** — corpus is genuinely on the borderline between this cluster and another's; transfer destination unclear; researcher decision needed.
2. **Homonymic / polysemic spread** — the term's meaning corpus covers two or more distinct registers and the term may need sense-split treatment.
3. **Supportive / qualifying register** — the term's meanings enhance or qualify M08's characteristic without itself carrying it as a primary characteristic.

## Specific signals to attend to (M08 pre-debate notes from CC)

### A. Height vocabulary already filtered at Phase 1

`rum`, `qo.mah`, `ga.vo.ah`, `ma.rom`, `hupsēlos`, `ga.on` etc. had most non-pride uses set-aside at Phase 1 (divine majesty, literal stature, honourable lifting). Their is_relevant corpus is therefore concentrated on pride content — these are likely STAYS without much debate.

### B. Strength / power / authority / dignity registers

Several M08 terms come from non-pride home registries (strength reg#187, power reg#196, authority reg#197, dignity reg#47, boldness reg#16, ambition reg#3). Their meanings may carry pride-as-misuse-of-strength / misuse-of-authority content, OR may carry the home-registry's primary register with no pride dimension. **Apply §6.3.2 verse-level relationship test:** if any verse evidences a pride dimension, STAYS with cross-register flag; if none does, TRANSFERS.

Likely cases to examine carefully:

- **strength / power terms** (e.g. `koach`, `dunamis`, `cha.zaq`, `e.gozar`) — does the corpus speak of misused strength → arrogance, OR of strength as enabling capacity (no pride)?
- **authority terms** (`exousia`, `archē`, `archō`) — pride-in-rule, OR neutral institutional authority?
- **boldness** (`parresia`, `tharseō`, `tolmaō`) — godly courage vs arrogant overreach?
- **ambition** (`filotimia`, `prōtokathedria`-related) — usually pride-relevant
- **delight / self-glorying** (some `cha.phets` uses, `kauchaomai` — proud-boasting, godly-boasting?)

### C. Volitional terms with potential pride dimension

`will`, `mind`, `self-control` terms: a term naming the will may show up in M08 only when the will is the seat of self-exalting elevation. Verse-level test required.

### D. Likely STAYS terms (clear primary pride vocabulary)

The following are extremely likely to STAY (clear primary pride/arrogance/boasting content):

- `huperēfania`, `huperēfanos`, `alazōn`, `alazoneia` — Greek pride proper
- `ga.on`, `ga.a.vah`, `ga.a.von`, `go.vah` — Hebrew pride/majesty
- `kauchaomai`, `kauchēma`, `kauchēsis` — boasting family
- `halal` (when "to boast/glory" sense — distinguish from praise sense)
- The "haughty" / "lifted-up-in-self" uses of `rum`, `ga.vo.ah` that survived Phase 1

One-sentence verdict for each is sufficient.
