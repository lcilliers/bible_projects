## §2 — Sub-group design

### M08-A1 — Heart-Elevation — Pride Seated in the Heart

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation)
**Split-axis:** seat_of_pride: heart
**Verse count:** ~34 (11.5% of 295)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of arrogant self-elevation specifically as it is located and named in the heart — the Hebrew lev and Greek kardia as the explicit seat of pride. The evidence is the "lifted-up heart" idiom that runs across M08's Hebrew corpus: prosperity causing the heart to be lifted up and forget God (Deu 8:14); the king's heart not to be raised above his brothers (Deu 17:20); pride explicitly located in the heart as what drives unfaithful action (2Ch 26:16; 2Ch 32:25); the heart's inner elevation as the direct precursor to destruction (Pro 18:12); the proud heart of the tree that becomes the cause of its felling (Eze 31:10); wealth making the heart proud and corrupting wisdom (Eze 28:17); the prince of Tyre whose heart claimed divine status (Eze 28:2); the heart being lifted up after military victory (Dan 11:12); the heart and spirit hardening together in Belshazzar's pride (Dan 5:20). In the NT, huperēfanos locates arrogance in the thoughts of the heart (Luk 1:51) and huperēfania names pride as a heart-sourced vice (Mar 7:22). The heart is M08's most frequently named inner organ for pride's constitutional seat. This sub-group carries the anatomical core of the dispositional characteristic.

**Primary term carriers:** ga.vah heart-idiom verses (2Ch 26:16, 2Ch 32:25, Psa 131:1, Pro 18:12, Jer 49:16, Eze 28:2, Eze 28:17, Eze 31:10); rum heart-idiom verses (Deu 8:14, Deu 17:20, Dan 11:12, Dan 11:36, 1Ki 11:26-27, Hos 13:6, Eze 31:10); go.vah heart verses (2Ch 32:26, Eze 31:10); ga.vo.ah heart-named verses (1Sa 16:7, Psa 101:5, Pro 16:5); ge.eh Jer 48:29; ga.a.vah Isa 9:9; ga.on Job 35:12; ge.ut Psa 17:10; H7312 rum heart verses (Pro 21:4, Isa 10:12, Jer 48:29); zud Dan 5:20; huperēfania Mar 7:22; huperēfanos Luk 1:51

### M08-A2 — Eye-Elevation and Outward Bearing — Pride's Visible Register

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation)
**Split-axis:** seat_of_pride: eyes_and_outward_bearing
**Verse count:** ~11 (3.7% of 295)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of arrogant self-elevation as it registers in the outward-facing organs and bearing of the inner person — the eyes, the gaze, the posture, the neck. Pride is not merely felt inwardly; it manifests in the way the self presents itself to the world through stance and bearing. The evidence is the "haughty eyes" vocabulary: haughty eyes that God actively brings down (Psa 18:27); haughty eyes as first in the list of things the Lord hates (Pro 6:17); lofty eyes and high-lifted eyelids as the physical register of inner contempt directed downward at others (Pro 30:13); eyes not raised high as the deliberate inner posture of humility (Psa 131:1); haughtiness expressed in the looks of men that will be humbled on the day of judgment (Isa 2:11; Isa 2:17); the haughty face-posture that shuts out any seeking of God (Psa 10:4); haughtiness expressed in the eyes of those brought low (Isa 5:15); pride worn like a necklace as an outward display rooted in inner self-exaltation (Psa 73:6); the daughters of Zion's ostentatious posture and wanton glances as the expression of inner pride (Isa 3:16); and Job's head being lifted up in pride as the outward-bearing form of self-exaltation (Job 10:16). This sub-group catches the body-language of pride — the inner posture that has become visible.

**Primary term carriers:** rum eye-posture verses (Psa 18:27, Pro 6:17, Pro 30:13, Psa 131:1); gav.hut (Isa 2:11, Isa 2:17); go.vah Psa 10:4; ga.vo.ah Isa 5:15; ga.a.vah Psa 73:6; ga.vah Isa 3:16; ga.ah Job 10:16

### M08-A3 — National and Collective Pride — Pride as a Corporate Inner Characteristic

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation)
**Split-axis:** seat_of_pride: national_and_collective
**Verse count:** ~37 (12.5% of 295)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of arrogant self-elevation when the subject is a nation, people, city, king-as-representative, or collective — pride as a corporate inner characteristic attributed to a whole social body. The evidence is dominated by the ga.on corpus's national-pride register: Moab's pride (reported as publicly known — Isa 16:6; Jer 13:9; Jer 48:29; Zep 2:10); Babylon's pride as national splendor that makes its ruin total and exemplary (Isa 13:19; Isa 14:11; Isa 23:9); Israel's pride that testifies against itself (Hos 5:5; Hos 7:10); the pride of Jacob as what God abhors (Amo 6:8) and swears by (Amo 8:7); Ephraim and Samaria's pride and arrogance of heart (Isa 9:9 — in ga.a.vah); the lofty pride of collective humanity on the day of judgment (Isa 2:11; Isa 2:17; Isa 2:12); the proud crown of Ephraim's leaders (Isa 28:1; Isa 28:3); the great tree's proud heart as a national allegory (Eze 31:10; Eze 31:14). The collective frame is consistent: pride is attributed to a people as their defining inner characteristic, producing relational contempt, national misfortune, and divine judgment. This is M08's political and social face — not merely one person's arrogance but a community's constitutive inner posture.

**Primary term carriers:** ga.on national verses (16V: Isa 13:11, 13:19, 14:11, 16:6, 23:9; Jer 13:9, 48:29; Eze 7:20, 16:56; Hos 5:5, 7:10; Amo 6:8, 8:7; Zep 2:10; Zec 9:6, 10:11); ga.a.vah national verses (Isa 13:11, 16:6, 25:11, Jer 48:29, Zep 3:11); ge.ut (Isa 28:1, 28:3); ga.vah collective (Eze 16:50, Obd 4, Zep 3:11); go.vah Jer 48:29; ge.vah Jer 13:17; H7312 rum (Isa 2:11, 2:17); rum collective (Deu 32:27, Isa 2:12, Isa 10:33); qo.mah (2Ki 19:23, Isa 10:33, Isa 37:24, Eze 31:10, Eze 31:14); ge.eh Isa 2:12

### M08-A4 — General Dispositional Pride — Individual Self-Elevation (Unspecified Seat)

**Characteristic:** CHAR-1 (Arrogant self-elevation)
**Split-axis:** seat_of_pride: general_dispositional_individual
**Verse count:** ~69 (23.4% of 295)

**core_description:** This sub-group represents the characteristic of arrogant self-elevation in its general form: individual pride as a settled inner disposition without a specific named inner organ (heart, eyes) or collective framing (nation, people). This is the broadest expression of the characteristic — the proud person described in terms of their inner posture, their relationship with God and others, and the consequences of their elevation, without the verse identifying whether the seat is heart or eyes or the subject is personal or national. It includes the Proverbs wisdom maxims about pride's self-defeating consequences (Pro 16:18; Pro 16:19; Pro 29:23); the vice catalogues of Romans and 2 Timothy where arrogance appears among other inner-being failures (Rom 1:30; 2Ti 3:2); the proud who make themselves an obstacle to relationship with God (Psa 94:2; Psa 138:6; Psa 140:5; Jam 4:6; 1Pe 5:5); the psalmic portraits of the arrogant enemy's inner contempt (Psa 123:4; Psa 36:11; Psa 31:18); the general warning-and-consequence pattern (Pro 15:25; Job 40:11; Job 40:12; Job 22:29; Job 33:17); and the rum self-exaltation verses that are individual and non-specific (Num 15:30; Psa 75:4–7, 75:10; Isa 14:13; Isa 37:23; Pro 14:29). Self-love as the generative root of pride (2Ti 3:2 filautos) also belongs here as the dispositional origin of the whole M08 cluster. This is the largest seat-of-pride sub-group because the majority of pride-vocabulary in wisdom and prophetic literature describes individual pride as a general inner characteristic without specifying organ or collective frame.

**Primary term carriers:** huperēfanos (4V: Rom 1:30, 2Ti 3:2, Jam 4:6, 1Pe 5:5); hupsēlos (3V); filautos (1V); ge (1V); ge.eh (7V: Job 40:11, 40:12; Psa 94:2, 123:4, 140:5; Pro 15:25, 16:19); ge.ah Pro 8:13; ga.a.vah (6V: Psa 10:2, 31:18, 31:23, 36:11; Pro 29:23; Psa 59:12 — wait, 59:12 is ga.on); ga.on individual (4V: Psa 59:12, Pro 8:13, Pro 16:18, Job 35:12 — noting Pro 8:13 also in ge.eh... see reconciliation note); ge.ut Isa 26:10; ga.vah individual (Pro 17:19, Jer 13:15); go.vah Pro 16:18; ga.vo.ah individual (1Sa 2:3, Job 41:34, Psa 138:6, Ecc 7:8, Eze 21:26); ge.vah (Job 22:29, Job 33:17); ge.vah Dan 4:37; sha.chats Job 41:34; ra.hav proud Psa 40:4; rum general individual (~15V); qo.mah 1Sa 16:7; ga.on individual (Psa 59:12, Pro 16:18)

*Reconciliation note for CC: Pro 8:13 appears in both ge.ah (mti_id=96) and ga.on (mti_id=98) as they both occur in the same verse. These are different vc_ids (different term/verse pairs) — both route to M08-A4.*
