## VCG M08-A4-VCG-01 — Self-Love as Root and NT Vice-Catalogue Pride

**Description:** Verses naming self-love as the generative root of the M08 cluster (filautos, 2Ti 3:2) and the NT vice-catalogue uses of huperēfanos and hupsēlos — pride as a defining trait of moral disorder in the last days and in worldly self-orientation. The 2 Timothy 3 list places self-love at the head of a sequence that includes boastfulness and arrogance; Paul's Romans 1 catalogue names haughtiness among the vices of a mind given over to moral disorder; and huperēfanos in James 4:6 and 1 Peter 5:5 establishes divine opposition to the proud as a structural relational reality. The hupsēlos verses (Luk 16:15; Rom 11:20; Rom 12:16) ground this VCG in the social register: pride that esteems what peers honour, pride that elevates self over others in the community, pride that fractures harmony by setting itself above the lowly. The two Mar 7:22 entries (huperēfania from within the heart as an inner-heart characteristic alongside other corruption) — while nominally heart-seated — appeared in M08-A4 in the meanings report; they are placed here as the NT definitional statement of pride as a constitutive inner-being vice alongside self-love and arrogance.

**Anchor:** vc_id=1899 — 2Ti 3:2 — G5367 filautos — "Self-love heads the list of end-times vices, establishing the inward orientation toward the self as the root from which pride, boasting, and arrogance all flow." The anchor names the root from which the entire dispositional pride cluster grows.

## VCG M08-A4-VCG-02 — God Actively Opposing the Proud — Divine Opposition as the Structural Frame

**Description:** Verses where God's active opposition to, reversal of, and judgment upon the proud is the primary framing content — pride addressed as what invites divine response, whether destruction of what the proud have built (Pro 15:25), the watchful eyes of God on the haughty (2Sa 22:28), the haughty being kept at relational distance (Psa 138:6 ×2), God challenging Job to look at the proud and abase them (Job 40:11–12), the proud's plans becoming the instrument of their own ruin (Psa 140:5; 140:8), and the general portrait of the proud person as one whose inner arrogance deserves divine retribution (Psa 94:2). This VCG is not primarily about the phenomenology of pride but about the divine logic that meets it.

The anchor states the structural logic most directly: God's watchfulness directed specifically at the haughty will.

## VCG M08-A4-VCG-03 — Psalmic Enemy Portraits — Pride as Predatory Social Aggression

**Description:** Verses in the psalmic tradition where pride is evidenced as the inner driving force of an oppressor or enemy — arrogance operating as agency to harm, trap, oppress, and crush the vulnerable. Pride here is not abstract self-elevation but active, relational, predatory. Arrogance drives the wicked to pursue the poor (Psa 10:2), blinds the wicked to the divine standard that will judge them (Psa 10:5), enables contemptuous oppressive speech against the righteous (Psa 31:18), operates as a personified foot trampling and hand driving away (Psa 36:11), traps the enemies in the snare of their own arrogance (Psa 59:12), leads others astray (Psa 40:4 — ra.hav), attacks from a position of felt superiority (Psa 56:2), speaks malice and lofty threats from felt superiority (Psa 73:8), and crushes the psalmist's soul with inner contempt (Psa 123:4). The social and relational damage of the proud person's arrogance is this VCG's distinctive content.

The anchor names the predatory-agency register directly: pride as what drives harmful action toward others.

## VCG M08-A4-VCG-04 — Wisdom Maxims — Pride's Self-Defeating Consequences and Dispositional Portrait

**Description:** Verses in the wisdom tradition (Proverbs, Job, Ecclesiastes, Daniel) that characterise pride as an inner disposition with inherent self-defeating consequences — the wisdom insight that arrogance is not only morally wrong but structurally self-destructive. Pride precedes destruction (Pro 16:18); pride brings the bearer low (Pro 29:23); making one's door high links to destruction (Pro 17:19); the proud person who gains by conquest is contrasted with the lowly-spirited (Pro 16:19); wisdom hates pride alongside arrogance and perverted speech (Pro 8:13 ×2 — ge.ah and ga.on both name it as incompatible with fearing God); a hasty temper exalts folly (Pro 14:29 — unrestrained inner state elevating what should be brought low); pride in evil men blocks divine response (Job 35:12); God disciplines to conceal pride (Job 33:17); pride draws downfall (Job 22:29); pride in the spirit makes a person worse (Ecc 7:8); pride as a habitual posture one walks in (Dan 4:37 — Nebuchadnezzar's testimony); inner haughtiness blocks hearing God (Jer 13:15); Leviathan's supreme self-assertion (Job 41:34 ×2 — ga.vo.ah and sha.chats); and Moab's pride as a settled inner disposition of self-importance (Isa 16:6 ge — the wisdom-register portrait).

## VCG M08-A4-VCG-05 — Individual Self-Exaltation Against God — The Willed Act of Inner Elevation

**Description:** Verses evidencing the individual, active, willed act of inner self-exaltation against God and divine authority — the inner posture that refuses the proper creaturely limits and asserts its own supremacy. This VCG clusters around the rum vocabulary in its individual, non-royal, non-national form: the "high hand" as an inner posture of raised self-assertion against God (Exo 14:8; Num 15:30), the prohibition against self-elevation that only God can properly grant (Psa 75:4–10 complex — five verses prohibiting lifting the horn and asserting that exaltation belongs to God alone), the Assyrian king's pride expressed in mockery against the Holy One (2Ki 19:22 ×2; 2Ki 19:23; Isa 37:23 ×2; Isa 37:24 — ma.rom and rum), the will's declaration of intent to self-exalt to heaven and above God's stars (Isa 14:13 — the paradigm text of the willed self-elevation), the divine reversal of what is exalted (Eze 21:26), height as self-secured elevation that God will dismantle (Isa 26:5), the perceptual closure of the wicked to the LORD's majesty (Isa 26:10), and the broad individual prohibition/warning pattern (Psa 66:7; 1Sa 2:3; 1Sa 16:7 qo.mah). Also includes ma.rom verses showing the inner arrogance as directed against God's holy one (2Ki 19:22, 19:23 — Assyrian arrogant mockery against the Holy One).

**Anchor:** vc_id=34233 — Isa 14:13 — H7311A rum — "The will declares in the heart an intent to self-exalt to heaven and above God's stars; this inner volitional self-elevation is the essence of the pride portrayed, originating in the heart's ambition." The paradigm text of the self-exaltation will at its most absolute.
