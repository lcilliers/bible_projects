## VCG M08-C-VCG-01 — Condemned Self-Directed Boasting

**Description:** Verses evidencing boasting as a straightforwardly condemned inner act directed at the self's own attributes, achievements, or future — the boast of one's own wisdom, might, wealth, or future plans; premature self-glorification before victory is secured; empty self-promotion; cunning self-serving duplicity in boasting. The ha.lal self-boasting subset (10 verses: premature boasting before battle, wicked boasting of soul's desires, trust in riches as source of pride, boasting of evil, misplaced glorying in idols, self-serving buyer's duplicity, empty boasting of gift never given, presumptuous boasting about tomorrow, Jer 9:23 prohibition, Jer 49:4 self-deception) establishes the Hebrew frame; alazoneia (Jam 4:16; 1Jo 2:16) and alazōn (Rom 1:30; 2Ti 3:2) establish the Greek inner-disposition-of-self-display frame; and the kauchaomai condemned passages complete it (Rom 2:17 Law as ground for self-distinction; Rom 2:23 proud religious claim vs conduct; 1Cor 1:29 grace shutting down self-congratulation; 1Cor 3:21 boasting in human leaders; 1Cor 4:7 illusion of self-origination; 1Cor 5:6 pride spreading corruption; Gal 6:13 using converts as trophies; Eph 2:9 grace excluding works-based grounds; Jam 4:16 ×2 kauchaomai and kauchēsis). Also ro.hav (Psa 90:10 — life's supposed boast undercut as toil and trouble).

**Anchor:** vc_id=33343 — Psa 52:1 — H1984H ha.lal — "Boasting of evil is the proud inner posture of a powerful person whose will is directed toward harm; it is set against God's enduring steadfast love as its moral opposite." The anchor places self-directed boasting in direct moral contrast with divine love — the most starkly defined opposition of the register.

## VCG M08-C-VCG-02 — The Pauline Boasting Discourse — Examining Legitimate vs Illegitimate Self-Commendation

**Description:** Verses from Paul's extended examination of what constitutes legitimate vs illegitimate boasting — the "fool's speech" in 2 Corinthians 10–12, the principles of bounded self-commendation, and the radical inversion of boasting's object through weakness and the cross. Paul acknowledges boasting as ordinarily the posture of the foolish (2Cor 11:16) and labels his own boastful confidence as foolish speech contrary to the mind of Christ (2Cor 11:17); he exposes rivals' self-serving boasting (2Cor 11:12) and flesh-based self-promotion (2Cor 11:18); he inverts boasting by making weakness its content (2Cor 11:30; 12:9 — the ground of glory); he constrains legitimate self-commendation to God-given spheres (2Cor 10:13) and refuses credit for others' work (2Cor 10:15–16); he would rather die than relinquish his ground for boasting in costless service (1Cor 9:15–16); and he reaches the radical conclusion that the cross utterly displaces self as the object of pride (Gal 6:14). Also includes Paul's examining the inward vs outward basis of boasting (2Cor 5:12 ×2) and the conscience-grounded self-assessment (2Cor 1:12). The M22 cross-register flag is present in 2Cor 10:17 (all legitimate boasting directed toward the Lord) — that verse is in VCG-02 as part of Paul's bounded-boasting principle; VCG-03 handles the fully God-directed register.

**Anchor:** vc_id=33870 — 2Cor 12:9 — G2744 kauchaomai — "Paul gladly boasts of weaknesses because Christ's power is perfected there; boasting is fully redirected from self-sufficiency to dependence, making weakness the very ground of glory." The anchor captures the fullest inversion of the boasting faculty — the Pauline discourse at its most radical.

## VCG M08-C-VCG-03 — Grace-Grounded and God-Directed Boasting — The M22 Cross-Register

**Description:** Verses where the boasting faculty is directed wholly away from the self and toward God, community, or the eschatological gift of grace — the M22 cross-register content that lives within M08's boasting vocabulary. Kauchaomai here evidences boasting in hope of God's glory (Rom 5:2–3), in received reconciliation (Rom 5:11), directed to the Lord alone as the only legitimate object (1Cor 1:31; 2Cor 10:17), grounded in Christ Jesus and none in the flesh (Phili 3:3), directed communally outward toward others' endurance and faith (2Th 1:4), and the lowly believer's legitimate boasting in the dignity God has granted (Jam 1:9). Kauchēsis/kauchēma evidences relational/eschatological boasting: Paul's relational boasting about the Corinthians (2Cor 7:4; 7:14×2; 8:24; 9:2; 9:3; 9:4), the Thessalonians as Paul's boast before Christ (1Th 2:19), mutual boasting as future relational vindication (2Cor 1:14), Christ as the object of the community's pride (Phili 1:26; 2:16 — eschatological), holding fast to confident boasting in hope as constituting belonging (Heb 3:6), and Paul's conscience-testimony-grounded boasting as integrity before God (2Cor 11:10). Rom 3:27 (faith's logic eliminating all grounds for human boasting) belongs here as the theological foundation for the grace-directed register. Rom 15:17 (Paul's boasting in Christ Jesus and God-given work) closes the register. **Cross-register flag: M22 (praise/thanksgiving/glory) — the God-directed and grace-grounded verses evidence content that overlaps with the praise-of-God characteristic M22 will develop.**

**Anchor:** vc_id=33878 — 1Cor 1:31 — G2744 kauchaomai — "All legitimate boasting must be directed to the Lord alone; the inner person's desire for pride in achievement is redirected so that its only proper object is God, not the self." The anchor states the governing principle of the whole VCG: God as the sole legitimate object of boasting.
