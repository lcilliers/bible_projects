### M38-E-VCG-01

- Verse count: 9
- Anchor vc_id: 29619

**Context description:** The ordered sequence of self-covering before mediating for others: the high priest must first atone for himself and his household before he can effect atonement for others. This VCG captures the inner-being logic of the priestly covering sequence — the principle that an uncovered mediator cannot cover others, that the priest's own sin-laden state must be resolved before he stands between God and the people. The subjective weight here is the ordered readiness of the mediator: consecration, anointing, holy garments, and self-atonement are prerequisites for the work of intercession. The full Day of Atonement sequence (Lev 16) is the primary locus, extending to ordination and Levitical consecration wherever the 'priest covered first' logic governs.

### M38-E-VCG-02

- Verse count: 7
- Anchor vc_id: 29627

**Context description:** The comprehensive scope of what is covered on the Day of Atonement and the annual-reset function: sanctuary, tent of meeting, altar, priests, and all the assembly are covered in sequence, with nothing in the worshipping community left unaddressed. These verses evidence the inner-being phenomenon of total, structured corporate cleansing — the conscience of the whole community unburdened from a year's accumulated sin-guilt through a single divinely mandated event. The annual perpetual statute frames this as a recurring reset of Israel's standing before God, with the scapegoat providing the complementary image of guilt physically removed and banished. The Day of Atonement's exclusive, work-stopped focus on covering underscores that the community's entire relational standing before God depends on this mechanism.

### M38-E-VCG-03

- Verse count: 12
- Anchor vc_id: 29611

**Context description:** Priestly atonement restoring individuals from ritual uncleanness — skin disease, bodily discharges, childbirth, corpse-contact — to clean standing before God. The inner-being movement in all these verses is from a state of bodily-conditioned uncleanness (which carries real guilt-weight before a holy God) through priestly sacrifice to full restoration of purity and relational standing. Accessibility is a sub-note: provision of scaled-down offerings for the poor ensures that the covering mechanism reaches every economic level, making the inner-being need for cleansing universally addressable. The priest's mediating act is the bridge between the defiled state and the restored state; the person's own self-purification is necessary but insufficient without the priestly atonement that finalizes clean standing.

### M38-E-VCG-04

- Verse count: 13
- Anchor vc_id: 29644

**Context description:** The regularized, scheduled provision of sin-offering atonement for the corporate community — the prescribed sacrificial machinery that keeps Israel's collective guilt covered before God on a recurring, institutional basis. These verses evidence the inner-being phenomenon of structured communal guilt-management: appointed feasts, monthly and annual cycles, and the ongoing priestly and Levitical vocation all supply the ritual means by which corporate sin is addressed before it accumulates into catastrophe. The inner-being register is one of maintained standing rather than crisis response — the community's continuous covered state before God is sustained by the ordered, mediated offering system. The prince's and community's responsibility to resource and sustain this system (Ezekiel, Nehemiah) extends the phenomenon into post-exilic institutional commitment.

### M38-E-VCG-05

- Verse count: 3
- Anchor vc_id: 29638

**Context description:** Atonement as urgent wrath-interruption: priestly intercession that arrests active divine judgment already in motion. The inner-being register here is crisis and peril — plague has broken out, God's anger is not prospective but present, and the atoning act is the pivot between destruction and survival. Moses ascending to intercede after the golden calf (Exo 32:30) and Aaron running with incense into the plague (Num 16:46–47) share this register: the mediator places himself between divine wrath and the endangered people at personal cost, and the outcome of atonement is survival rather than mere forgiveness. Phinehas's zealous priestly action (Num 25:13) belongs here because its motivation and effect is the same crisis-aversion logic, with a covenant of perpetual priesthood as the distinctive reward for wrath-intercepting zeal.
