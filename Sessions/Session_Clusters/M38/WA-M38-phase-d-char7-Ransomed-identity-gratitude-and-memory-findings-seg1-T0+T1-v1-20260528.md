**T1.8.2 — Sustained Effect — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The evidence across the sub-group depicts several sustained inner-being effects. (1) *Stable identity*: over time, the person whose inner being is shaped by ransomed gratitude knows who they are with a stability that does not depend on present circumstance — "I am the ransomed of the LORD" is a durable identity marker (2Sa 7:23; Deu 7:8; Isa 35:10). (2) *Sustained rejoicing*: the language of Isa 35:10 and 51:11 — "everlasting joy upon their heads" — explicitly names the long-term affective state of the ransomed as joyful in a way that displaces sorrow and sighing permanently, not temporarily. (3) *Ethical consistency*: the memory of ransom produces sustained motivation toward just treatment of others (Deu 15:15; 24:18), suggesting that the characteristic shapes the moral character over time, not merely producing occasional acts of generosity. (4) *Deepened trust*: 2Sa 4:9 and 1Ki 1:29 show David with accumulated trust grounded in repeated experienced ransom — the characteristic compounds over time as each rescue deepens the soul's confidence in the ransomer. (5) *Intercessory confidence*: the sustained posture of appealing to God on the basis of covenant ransom (Deu 9:26; Neh 1:10) becomes a habitual mode of prayer.

---

**T1.1.3 — Sustained Effect — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Sustained possession of ransomed identity gratitude establishes the following. *States*: a condition of settled gladness that has replaced sorrow (Isa 35:10; 51:11); a state of safety-in-God that has replaced existential fear (Isa 12:2; Psa 34:22); a state of belonging that has replaced exile and subjugation (Jer 31:11; Zec 10:8). *Qualities*: integrity correlated with the ransomed identity (Psa 26:11 — "I walk in my integrity and plead for ransom"); obedience enabled by freedom from oppression (Psa 119:134); dignity replacing shame (Isa 29:22). *Capacities*: the capacity to intercede on the basis of covenantal memory (Deu 9:26); the capacity to motivate ethical action from gratitude (Deu 15:15); the capacity to sustain hope in the face of mortal threat (Psa 49:15; Hos 13:14). *Orientations*: consistent loyalty to the God who ransomed, particularly in the face of alternatives (Deu 13:5); a habitual drawing-from rather than desperate grasping — the "wells of salvation" drawn with joy (Isa 12:3) suggest a freely available resource that the ransomed person habitually accesses.

---

**T1.2.3 — Sustained Effect — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, and the difference is significant. The immediate response is characterized by intensity, movement, and transition — the eruption of joy (Isa 25:9), the displacement of fear (Isa 12:2), the urgency of intercessory appeal (Psa 44:26), the instinctive trust-response (2Sa 4:9). These immediate responses have a kinetic quality: the inner being is moving from one state to another. The sustained effects, by contrast, are characterized by *stability, habituation, and settled orientation*. The "everlasting joy" of Isa 35:10 and 51:11 is not repeated eruptions of ecstasy but a crown — a permanent adornment of the ransomed state. David's accumulated trust in 2Sa 4:9 and 1Ki 1:29 is the settled ground of an experienced life, not a fresh discovery. The ethical responsiveness of Deu 15:15 and 24:18 describes a habitual moral orientation, not a single act of gratitude. The sustained effect thus takes the intensity of the immediate response and transforms it into character — a durable inner-being condition that no longer requires fresh stimulus to sustain itself but has been woven into the person's identity and habitual orientation.

**T1.3.3 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The evidence yields several enabling conditions. (1) *Honest acknowledgment of need and helplessness before threat*: the intercessory pleading of VCG-02 (Psa 25:22; 44:26; Deu 9:26) shows that genuine reception of ransom requires the inner posture of those who know themselves to be in danger they cannot escape unaided. (2) *Turned-toward-God will*: by contrast with Hos 7:13's refused ransom, the enabling condition is a will oriented toward the ransomer rather than turning away in self-protective deception. (3) *Willing repentance*: Isa 1:27 explicitly conditions Zion's ransom on the inner turn of justice and righteousness — those who turn receive it. (4) *Trust grounded in God's prior acts*: the intercessory use of redemptive memory (Deu 9:26; Neh 1:10) shows that reception is enabled by an inner orientation that holds God's past acts as a reliable basis for present appeal — a trust-posture that makes the approach to God possible. (5) *Self-surrender to God as faithful ransomer*: Psa 31:5 — "into your hand I commit my spirit, for you have ransomed me" — shows that complete self-surrender is both enabled by and further deepens reception of the ransomed state.

---

**T1.4.3 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The evidence is specific. (1) *Forgetting the redemptive event*: Psa 78:42 names this as the primary internal blockage — when the memorial capacity fails to hold the day of redemption, the characteristic's identity-constituting and motivating power is lost. Forgetting here is active suppression or passive erosion of formative memory. (2) *Deceptive self-direction and rebellion*: Hos 7:13 shows a will actively turned away from offered ransom through lies — the person who says one thing while fleeing another, refusing the rescue God extends. The inner condition is one of self-deceptive evasion of dependence on God. (3) *Reliance on human ransoming power*: Psa 49:7 establishes that the belief that a human being can supply ransom from death represents a fundamental misorientation — a kind of false hope that prevents reception of the only ransom that is real. (4) *Spiritual distance combined with shame without appeal*: the contrast between Psa 44:26 (which does appeal, enabling reception) and a hypothetical despair that does not appeal suggests that a shame or defeat that refuses to cry out blocks the intercessory mode of reception. (5) *Pride in self-merit*: Deu 7:8 explicitly rules out any basis of ransom in the people's merit — self-sufficiency forecloses the receptive posture.

**T1.5.3 — Conditions of Reception — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Two types of non-reception are evidenced, with distinct inner-being states. (1) *The forgetter* (Psa 78:42): the inner being is in a state of identity-dissolution — no longer knowing themselves as ransomed, they are unmoored from the relational and ethical motivations that ransomed identity provides. The psychological-spiritual texture is ingratitude passing into faithlessness, with the self now defined by present appetites or fears rather than by covenant history. The danger of this state is that it is often unreflective — the person may not experience it as active refusal but simply as drift. (2) *The rebel* (Hos 7:13): the inner being is in a state of active deceptive refusal — knowing ransom is offered but turning away from it through self-protective lies. This state has an element of willful spiritual self-harm: the person remains "in self-imposed spiritual danger" precisely because the will has refused the rescue that was available. The inner-being texture here is one of suppressed need meeting self-generated alternative narratives, producing a person who appears to function but is in fact unransomed and therefore ultimately endangered. Both states share the characteristic of remaining outside the identity-restructuring and affective-transforming effects that received ransom produces.

**T1.6.3 — Dimension Classification — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — The primary inner-being dimension is *volitional-memorial* — operating most fundamentally through the will and the memorial faculty together. The case for this classification rests on the characteristic's most persistent operational pattern: the remembered fact of ransom directly generates will-orientation and ethical action (Deu 15:15; 24:18; Psa 119:134). The characteristic is not primarily *affective* (though affect is strongly present) because the verse evidence most consistently emphasizes what the ransomed person *does* and *will* as a consequence of their identity — how they treat slaves, how they pray, whether they remain loyal or defect, how they obey God's precepts. The memorial dimension is primary alongside the volitional because without the active, commanded holding of the redemptive event (Deu 15:15: "you shall remember"), the motivational power of the characteristic is lost (Psa 78:42). The characteristic is an *active memorial orientation that generates will-shaped ethical and relational response*, making the volitional-memorial the primary dimension.

---

**T1.7.3 — Dimension Classification — What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Support for volitional-memorial as primary is drawn from across the sub-groups. VCG-01 (the most substantial group, 8 verses) is entirely structured around the command to remember and the ethical-volitional consequences: Deu 15:15 ("you shall remember that you were a slave… therefore I command you to do this"), Deu 24:18 (memory of redemption → command to treat the vulnerable justly), Deu 13:5 (redemption memory → the gravity of volitional betrayal), Mic 6:4 (God citing the Exodus as covenant ground for present obligation). The structural logic is consistently: remember the ransom → the will is oriented. VCG-04 (5 verses) explicitly treats ransom as enabling will-freedom — Psa 119:134 asks for ransom from oppression so that obedience to God's precepts becomes possible; the ransom is the precondition for free volitional response. Psa 78:42 confirms the classification by negation: the primary failure is memorial (they forgot), which produces the primary volitional failure (faithlessness and ingratitude). Even the intercessory mode (VCG-02) is volitional-memorial: the memory of ransom is actively deployed through the will's decision to appeal to God.

---

**T1.8.3 — Dimension Classification — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?**

**[CHAR-7]** E — Yes, two secondary dimensions are clearly present and one tertiary. The first secondary dimension is *affective*: the sustained joy (Isa 35:10; 51:11), the erupting gladness (Isa 25:9; Psa 71:23), the displacement of fear by trust (Isa 12:2), and the soul refreshed at wells of salvation (Isa 12:3) all point to a rich affective dimension — particularly in VCG-06 where the characteristic's culminating register is explicitly emotional transformation. The affective dimension does not compete with the volitional-memorial primary because the evidence places joy as the *outcome* of ransomed identity rather than its motivational core; the will and memory generate the ethical and relational response, which then issues in joy. The second secondary dimension is *relational-identity*: the characteristic fundamentally restructures the person's relational standing — they are the people God ransomed (2Sa 7:23; Jer 31:11; Zec 10:8) — and this identity operates at the level of relational self-constitution. This dimension does not compete with the primary either, since the identity-constituting effect of ransom is what makes the memorial and volitional aspects possible — it is the constitutional ground. The tertiary dimension is *cognitive-epistemic* (knowing what God did, understanding the covenant history), but this appears as a precondition for the memorial dimension rather than as an independent dimension in its own right.
