Cluster M38 — Salvation, Redemption and Deliverance.

M38's 13 active terms span three related semantic registers. Pass A produces verse-specific meanings + keywords that name what each verse actually evidences about inner-being salvation/atonement/gift content. Pass A does not pre-judge whether the three registers integrate into one cluster characteristic (a Phase B question).

The three registers:

1. **Salvation / rescue / deliverance vocabulary**
   - G4982 sōzō (to save), G4990 sōtēr (savior), G4991 soteria (salvation), G4992 sōtērion (saving)
   - H3444 ye.shu.ah (salvation), H6299 pa.dah (to ransom)
   The rescue, deliverance, salvation proper — God's saving action and the inner-being state of being saved/rescued/ransomed.

2. **Propitiation / atonement vocabulary**
   - G2433 hilaskomai, G2434 hilasmos, G2435 hilastērios, G2436 hileōs
   - H3722B ka.phar (to cover/atone)
   The means by which salvation is effected — the inner-being state of being atoned-for, mercy received through propitiation.

3. **Giving / gift vocabulary where context is salvation-relevant**
   - G1431 dōrea, G1434 dōrēma
   Where the gift carries salvation-relevant inner-being signal.

WHAT THE PASS A MEANING SHOULD NAME (when present in the verse)

- The specific salvation-state the verse evidences (rescued from what; saved to what)
- The atonement-mechanism the verse evidences (covered how; propitiated through what)
- The inner-being faculty engaged: heart trusting, soul rejoicing, will turning, conscience cleansed, body healed alongside spirit, etc.
- The relational direction: God-to-person, person-to-God, person-to-person rescue
- The temporal register: past rescue, present state, future hope of salvation
- The structural opposite implicit in the verse: perishing, danger, sin-covered, wrath-faced

DISCRIMINATIONS TO WATCH

- sōzō has senses: physical rescue (from enemies, storm, death-by-illness), eschatological/spiritual salvation, healing-with-spiritual-overtone. Pass A meaning should name which sense the verse evidences. Keywords should reflect that distinction (e.g. `rescue physical` vs `rescue eschatological` vs `healing spiritual`).
- ka.phar in this set is the personal-atonement sense (object-of-atonement contexts have been set aside at UT review). Pass A should name what is atoned, by whom, with what effect on the inner being.
- pa.dah here is the salvation-bearing redemption sense (the legal/procedural firstborn-redemption uses have been set aside at UT review). Distinguish covenant-redemption from individual personal ransom where the verse carries that distinction.
- Propitiation terms (hilask-) are theologically dense — the Pass A meaning should name what the verse adds to the propitiation register (foundational definition, Christological reference, intercession, etc.) rather than restating "propitiation occurs".
- Gift terms (dōrea, dōrēma) — name what gift, for whom, with what inner-being effect.

KEYWORDS — atomic vocabulary that aggregates across the corpus

Reach for the same keywords across verses that name the same inner-being operation. The clustering depends on this consistency. Suggested family vocabulary (use where it fits; do not force):

- Salvation-states: `rescued`, `delivered`, `ransomed`, `safety`, `preservation`, `salvation eschatological`, `salvation present`
- Atonement-states: `atoned`, `covered`, `forgiven`, `propitiated`, `cleansed conscience`, `wrath averted`
- Faculty engaged: `heart trusting`, `soul rejoicing`, `will turning`, `faith exercised`, `hope sustained`
- Relational direction: `god-saving`, `christ-saving`, `god-propitiated`, `mediator interceding`
- Inner response: `gratitude`, `praise`, `joy salvation`, `trust deepened`, `peace received`
- Implied opposite: `perishing`, `wrath-facing`, `sin-laden`, `endangered`

These are suggestions — let the verse evidence drive. Do not force vocabulary where it does not fit.
