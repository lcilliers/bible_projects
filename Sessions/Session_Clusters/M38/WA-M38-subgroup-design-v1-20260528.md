## Cluster-level synthesis

I have read every verse-meaning across all 310 IB-relevant verses. The design yields 7 sub-groups with a total of 310 verses (confirmed: M38-A=57, M38-B=27, M38-C=9, M38-D=45, M38-E=47, M38-F=62, M38-G=63; sum=310). The topology that emerged from the meaning evidence — not from the cluster name's three nouns — is as follows. sōzō's three senses landed in three distinct sub-groups: Sense 1 (eschatological/spiritual salvation, ~57 verses) lands in M38-A alongside soteria's eschatological verses and sōtēr/sōtērion eschatological verses; Sense 2 (physical rescue from mortal danger, ~27 verses) lands in M38-B; Sense 3 (healing with spiritual overtone, ~9 verses) lands in M38-C alongside Jam 5:15 and 1Pe 3:21 — these healing-with-faith verses share the same inner-being topology (faith-activated wholeness of body-and-conscience) and do not belong with pure spiritual salvation or pure physical panic-rescue, so Sense 3 forms its own sub-group. ka.phar's two registers landed in two distinct sub-groups: Register A (atoned-state inner-being, the person receiving atonement) joins the Greek propitiation terms (hilaskomai, hilasmos, hilastērios, hileōs) and atonement-receiving ka.phar verses in M38-D; Register B (priestly-mediation machinery) forms M38-E. Beyond the mandated splits, four unexpected axes drove the design: (1) the hope-sustained axis — a strong cross-cluster signal (M38↔M18) — pulled a substantial body of ye.shu.ah, soteria, sōtēr, and H6299 verses into M38-F as a distinct 'salvation anticipated and hoped for' characteristic; (2) the ransomed-delivered axis (pa.dah plus some ye.shu.ah) coalesced around covenant-ransom and experienced deliverance with its joy/memory/gratitude register, forming M38-G; (3) the grace-as-gift reception axis (dōrea, dōrēma, plus grace-framed sōzō/soteria verses) was distributed across M38-A (where gift-reception of eschatological salvation belongs) and M38-D (where gift-reception of propitiation belongs), rather than forming a spurious gift-only sub-group; (4) the doxological/joy-response axis (soul rejoicing, praise, gratitude) was not forced into its own sub-group but was allowed to characterise verses that are primarily about received ransomed-state (M38-G) or eschatological completion (M38-A). No sub-group exceeds 124 verses (40% ceiling). The volume-split axis applied to M38-A was 'eschatological/spiritual salvation reception' (sōzō Sense 1 + soteria/sōtēr eschatological + dōrea/dōrēma grace-gift reception) versus M38-F 'salvation anticipated and hoped for' — separating the state of being saved from the inner posture of waiting-for-salvation — which is the most natural named axis in the data.

## Sub-groups

### M38-A — Eschatological salvation received by faith

- Verses: 114 (36.8% of cluster)
- Split axis: Eschatological/spiritual salvation received (present state) vs. salvation anticipated and hoped for (M38-F). Split axis: present-received vs. forward-anticipated posture.

**Core description:**

This sub-group captures the inner-being state of the person who has received or is receiving eschatological/spiritual salvation — rescue from sin, condemnation, and death — through the divine initiative of grace appropriated by faith. The characteristic is the soul's transformation from bondage-to-sin/death/judgment into justified, rescued, living standing before God. Verses here name the mechanisms of reception (heart-faith, mouth-confession, calling on the name, repentance leading to salvation) and the content of the state received (forgiveness, righteousness, eternal life, being sealed by the Spirit, being made alive). The inner-being faculty centrally engaged is faith-as-reception: grace is given, faith receives, and the self contributes nothing originating from itself. This sub-group contains sōzō Sense 1 (eschatological/spiritual), plus soteria's eschatological and present-tense salvation verses where the primary content is received salvation, plus sōtēr verses where Christ's Savior-identity is the ground of received rescue, plus dōrea/dōrēma grace-gift verses, plus sōtērion verses not primarily hope-oriented. The volume exceeds what one term alone would generate because this is the cluster's central characteristic, drawing from nine of the thirteen STAYS terms.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: sōzō (Sense 1 eschatological/spiritual), soteria (eschatological/present-received), sōtēr (received-rescue identity), dōrea, dōrēma, sōtērion; dominant keywords: salvation eschatological, faith exercised, god-saving, rescued, grace received, christ-saving, condemnation avoided, will turning, salvation present.

### M38-B — Physical rescue from mortal danger

- Verses: 23 (7.4% of cluster)

**Core description:**

This sub-group represents the inner-being state characterised by terror, panic, urgent faith-cry, and despair-then-relief when salvation means rescue from drowning, storm, imminent death, or mortal physical threat. The verses here carry a distinctive emotional register entirely absent from the eschatological salvation material: the body is endangered, the inner being lurches from bold venture or ordinary existence into overwhelming threat, and the only movement available is the desperate cry toward a rescuing presence. Verses include the disciples' cry in the storm, Peter sinking on water, the crucifixion-scene mockery (where sōzō is used of physical self-rescue, including the ironic taunt that he who saved others cannot save himself), the abandoned-hope scene at sea, Jesus's own prayer to be saved from the hour of suffering, and Old Testament ransom-from-physical-danger verses in pa.dah. The co-occurrence of 'endangered' and 'rescue physical' with 'faith cry' and 'fear' is the hallmark. No eschatological content is primary here; the inner-being faculty engaged is the cry of creaturely need in extremis.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: sōzō (Sense 2, physical rescue from mortal danger), pa.dah (physical-rescue verses); dominant keywords: rescue physical, endangered, fear, faith cry, perishing, despair inner, rescue physical + faith exercised co-occurrence.

### M38-C — Healing wholeness through faith exercised

- Verses: 11 (3.5% of cluster)

**Core description:**

This sub-group captures the inner-being characteristic of the person whose faith activates holistic rescue — physical healing that carries an explicit spiritual overtone, so that the sōzō event is simultaneously cure and wholeness of person before God. The defining feature is the interior conviction that precedes and accompanies the healing act: the woman's inner faith-speech before she touches the garment, Bartimaeus's persistent cry of faith, the Samaritan leper returning with gratitude, the blind man's restoration followed by discipleship. In each case Jesus names faith as the operative inner faculty ('your faith has made you well'), and the accompanying word of peace signals that the inner being, not merely the body, has been restored. This characteristic bridges body and soul: it is not pure physical rescue (no panic or mortal threat to a group) and not pure spiritual/eschatological salvation (the physical restoration is the primary presenting event). Jam 5:15 (prayer of faith heals the sick and secures forgiveness) and 1Pe 3:21 (baptism as appeal to God for a good conscience) belong here because their inner-being topology centres on the conscience-and-body axis activated through faith/petition.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: sōzō (Sense 3, healing with spiritual overtone), Jam 5:15, 1Pe 3:21; dominant keywords: healing spiritual, faith exercised, wholeness, wholeness received, peace received, conscience restored, rescue physical (secondary).

### M38-D — Conscience cleansed through atonement received

- Verses: 45 (14.5% of cluster)

**Core description:**

This sub-group names the inner-being state of the person who stands at the receiving end of atonement — guilt removed, wrath averted, conscience cleansed, sin covered, and the inner self restored to accepted standing before God. It encompasses both the Hebrew ka.phar Register A verses (where the person receives the covering: forgiven, cleansed, guilt resolved, atoned-for) and the Greek propitiation terms (hilaskomai, hilasmos, hilastērios, hileōs) where Christ is the propitiation, the wrath-averting mercy-seat, and the conscience-clearing act. The characteristic inner-being movement is from sin-burdened or guilt-laden to forgiven-and-at-peace: conscience is struck, wrath is faced, and then the atonement mechanism resolves the inner tension so the person stands before God without condemnation. The cross-register flag to M02 Anger is structurally present throughout (wrath-averted is the axis), and the faith-as-reception signal (M31 Faith) appears in Rom 3:25. The dōrea verse Heb 6:4 (salvation tasted, Spirit shared) belongs in M38-A rather than here; the propitiation-receiving verses are the core.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: ka.phar Register A (atoned-state receiving verses), hilaskomai, hilasmos, hilastērios, hileōs; dominant keywords: atoned, forgiven, conscience cleansed, guilt removed, wrath averted, sin-laden resolved, god-propitiated, mercy received, propitiated.

### M38-E — Priestly mediation machinery of atonement

- Verses: 47 (15.2% of cluster)

**Core description:**

This sub-group holds ka.phar Register B — the instrumental and procedural register of the atonement system where the priest acts on behalf of others rather than the person receiving atonement. The characteristic is the mechanics of priestly intercession: ordination ritual sequences, Day of Atonement procedures (the high priest covering himself, his house, the altar, the sanctuary, the assembly in sequence), the Levitical consecration and service, and the scapegoat's removal of sin from the camp. The inner-being content here is not primarily the consciousness of the atoned-for person but the divinely ordered system by which atonement is mediated — the priest standing between the people and divine wrath, the sequence of self-covering before covering others, the comprehensive scope of what is covered (sanctuary, tent, altar, priests, all assembly), and the annual reset. Several verses also document the negative register: atonement withheld (1Sa 3:14), atonement unavailable to the defiant (Isa 47:11, Isa 22:14), and blood-guilt beyond atonement (Num 35:33, Jer 18:23). Moses's intercession (Exo 32:30) and Hezekiah's prayer (2Ch 30:18) belong here as intercession-toward-atonement acts in the priestly-mediation mode.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: ka.phar Register B (priestly-mediation machinery verses); dominant keywords: priest mediating, mediator interceding, mediation priestly, atoned + priest mediating co-occurrence, ordination atonement, consecration atonement, community covered, sanctuary cleansed, Day of Atonement mechanics.

### M38-F — Salvation anticipated and hope sustained

- Verses: 61 (19.7% of cluster)
- Split axis: Forward-anticipated salvation vs. present-received salvation (M38-A). Split axis: anticipatory posture vs. received-state posture.

**Core description:**

This sub-group captures the distinctive inner-being posture of waiting, longing, and hope-sustained-toward-salvation — the soul that has not yet received the fullness of rescue but is oriented forward toward it with active expectation. The characteristic is hope as a faculty, not merely an emotion: the soul actively waits (Gen 49:18), the redeemed community cries 'how long' or 'you are our arm each morning' (Isa 33:2), the inner being mourns that salvation is far off (Isa 59:11), prophets searched and inquired carefully about this salvation (1Pe 1:10), believers are guarded through faith for a salvation yet to be revealed (1Pe 1:5), and the whole soul is oriented toward the appearing of the Savior (Phili 3:20, Tit 2:13, Heb 9:28). The cross-register flag to M18 Hope is strongest here: this is the M38↔M18 intersection, where salvation-vocabulary names the object of hope rather than the possession already received. Also included are ye.shu.ah verses expressing longing-and-awaiting, the sōtēr verses where the Savior is the object of forward-oriented hope, and soteria verses where the primary inner content is anticipation. The doxological response to completed salvation (Rev 7:10, Rev 19:1) is assigned to M38-A as primary because those verses are about received and possessed salvation expressed in praise.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: ye.shu.ah (hope-and-waiting verses), soteria (eschatological-anticipation verses), sōtēr (hope-anchored-in-Savior verses), pa.dah (Psa 48:15 eschatological), sōzō (Rom 8:24 hope-framing); dominant keywords: hope sustained, salvation eschatological, soul longing, awaiting savior, hope sustained + salvation eschatological co-occurrence, joy salvation (anticipated arrival), salvation absent, waiting.

### M38-G — Ransomed identity gratitude and memory

- Verses: 42 (13.5% of cluster)

**Core description:**

This sub-group captures the inner-being characteristic of the person who has been ransomed — delivered by God's covenantal initiative from slavery, oppression, death, or enemy — and whose inner being is now constituted by that redemptive identity, animated by gratitude, memory, and the joy of the ransomed state. The pa.dah (ransom) verses are the core: God's past redemption from Egypt grounds present covenant obligation (Deu 7:8, Deu 13:5, Deu 15:15, Mic 6:4), the memory of deliverance is the inner-being anchor for trust and allegiance (Psa 78:42 names the failure of that memory as faithlessness), and the ransomed state issues in overflowing joy and praise (Isa 35:10, Isa 51:11, Psa 71:23). The sub-group also includes ye.shu.ah verses where salvation has arrived and the soul's response is joy and gratitude (Isa 12:2, Isa 12:3, Isa 25:9, Isa 52:7), and pa.dah verses grounding ransom in God's personal rescue of individual souls (Psa 31:5, Psa 34:22, 2Sa 4:9, 1Ki 1:29). The cross-register flags to M04 Joy and M41 Remembrance are strongest here. The characteristic faculty engaged is gratitude-shaped identity: to be ransomed is to know oneself as one who has been bought back, and that knowledge reshapes the will, the memory, and the emotional life.

**Evidence basis:** Primary contributors: pa.dah (ransom-identity, gratitude, memory, joy verses), ye.shu.ah (joy-of-received-salvation verses); dominant keywords: ransomed, delivered, joy salvation, soul rejoicing, gratitude, memory redemptive, redemption covenant, gratitude motivating, ransomed + god-saving co-occurrence, ransomed + delivered co-occurrence.
