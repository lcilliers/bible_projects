**T6.1.1 — Co-occurrence — Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across all seven characteristics, three co-occurrence partners emerge with sufficient cross-characteristic density to constitute cluster-level patterns rather than individual findings. The first is *forgiveness/conscience-cleansing*, which appears as the most frequent adjacent characteristic in Char-1 (VCG-02, 03, 04, 10), Char-3 (Jam 5:15; 1Pe 3:21), and Char-4 (virtually every VCG in the Levitical corpus). The third is *hope/anticipation*, which appears in Char-6 by definition and co-occurs in Char-1 (eschatological assurance), Char-7 (joy of the ransomed), and Char-2 (the faith-cry as hope enacted under duress). The notable asymmetry is that *fear/terror* and *mockery/irony* are co-occurrences unique to Char-2 and have no parallel in the other six characteristics — physical rescue under mortal threat generates phenomenological states (panic, despair, irony of withheld self-rescue) that do not appear in the soteriological, liturgical, or memorial registers. The cluster's co-occurrence landscape thus divides: forgiveness, wrath-aversion, and hope unite the seven characteristics in shared inner-being territory, while fear-terror and formative-memory are register-specific, appearing only where embodied crisis (Char-2) or temporal community formation (Char-7) are the presenting contexts.

**T6.2.1 — Sequential Relationships — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — A shared sequential architecture surfaces across five of the seven characteristics, despite their divergent registers. The dominant pattern is a three-phase movement: *antecedent deficit state → operative mechanism → resultant inner-being state*. In Char-4, this appears as guilt/sin-awareness → priestly atonement → conscience cleansed/forgiven. In Char-5, it appears as communal uncleanness → priestly mediation procedure → atoned/safe community. In Char-1, it appears as hearing/word-received → faith exercised → salvation received → peace/praise. In Char-3, it appears as interior conviction formed → sōzō enacted → peace/discipleship granted. In Char-7, it appears as divine ransom accomplished → memory and identity formation → ethical or joyful response. This structural homology across liturgical, somatic, eschatological, and memorial registers is the cluster's most significant sequential finding: M38 Salvation is organized around a consistent three-phase logic regardless of which characteristic instantiates it. Two characteristics diverge from this pattern. Char-6 exhibits a *two-phase anticipatory sequence*: covenantal promise received → sustained forward-oriented waiting, with the third phase (reception) present only in promise, not yet in experience — it is the sequence arrested at phase two, awaiting phase three. Char-2 exhibits the same three-phase logic (mortal threat → faith-cry → rescue) but introduces a critical asymmetry: the rescue is conditional on divine response and is not guaranteed, producing the bifurcation between cry-and-rescue (VCG-01) and cry-abandoned-into-despair (VCG-05). This conditionality is absent from Chars-1, 3, 4, and 5, where the mechanism reliably produces the outcome. The cluster-level finding is therefore: the sequential structure of M38 is unified at the formal level but asymmetric at the outcome level — most characteristics narrate a completed sequence, while Char-6 inhabits the uncompleted sequence and Char-2 introduces genuine outcome-uncertainty into what is otherwise a theologically assured movement.
