## §3. Researcher direction on BOUNDARY cases (mandatory inputs to B.2 design)

Both BOUNDARY verdicts from B.1 have been resolved by the researcher with explicit direction. These resolutions are MANDATORY inputs to the sub-group design:

### §3.1 sōzō (G4982) — homonymic_polysemic resolved

Researcher direction: **Keep all 93 IB verses in M38; design sub-group structure that holds the three discriminable senses as separate sub-groups.**

The three senses identified in B.1:

1. **Eschatological / spiritual salvation** (~57 verses) — Christ saves from sin / future judgment / for kingdom. Inner content: bondage-to-sin resolved, eternal life received, faith exercised.
2. **Physical rescue from mortal danger** (~27 verses) — from drowning, illness, mortal danger. Inner content: terror, panic, urgent faith-cry, despair-then-relief.
3. **Healing with spiritual overtone** (~9 verses) — physical cure with explicit spiritual register. Inner content: faith made you well, wholeness sought, spiritual-physical bridge.

B.2 must design sub-groups such that these three senses are operationally distinct. Note: the senses may map cleanly to three separate sub-groups, or sense (3) may merge with sense (1) or (2) — let the meaning evidence decide. But sense (1) and sense (2) should not be combined into a single sub-group.

### §3.2 ka.phar (H3722B) — supportive_qualifying resolved

Researcher direction: **Keep all 77 IB verses in M38; design a priestly-mediation sub-group that holds the instrumental verses, separate from the atoned-state inner-being verses.**

The two registers identified in B.1:

1. **Atoned-state inner-being** (~45 verses) — conscience cleansed, guilt removed, forgiven, wrath averted. The inner-being state of the person at the receiving end of atonement.
2. **Priestly-mediation machinery** (~30 verses) — the priest acting, ordination ritual sequence, Day of Atonement mechanics, the mediator covering on behalf of others. The instrumental register.

B.2 must design sub-groups such that these two registers are operationally distinct.
