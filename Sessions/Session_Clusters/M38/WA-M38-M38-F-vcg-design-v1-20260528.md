## VCGs

### M38-F-VCG-01

- Verse count: 11
- Anchor vc_id: 55480

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being posture of active, forward-oriented waiting and longing for salvation not yet received. The soul is suspended between the promise and its fulfillment — waiting is itself the characteristic inner act. These verses evidence an inner being that consciously orients itself toward divine rescue as its only source, experiencing the tension of hope deferred without collapsing into despair. The waiting is volitional and expectant, not passive resignation: the soul names God as its deliverer (Gen 49:18), the community pleads as those whose only arm is God (Isa 33:2), the inner being mourns that salvation is far off (Isa 59:11), prophets searched intensely for a salvation they would not themselves receive (1Pe 1:10), and believers are actively held through faith toward a salvation not yet revealed (1Pe 1:5). The dominant inner faculty is hope as active orientation — the soul leaning forward toward what it has not yet grasped.

### M38-F-VCG-02

- Verse count: 8
- Anchor vc_id: 55491

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of eschatological security — salvation understood as an everlasting, enduring reality that time, cosmic decay, and enemy power cannot erode. The inner being finds its anchor not in present circumstances but in the permanent character of God's saving work that outlasts all created things. These verses evidence a soul that has grasped salvation as categorically eternal: contrasted with a perishing cosmos (Isa 51:6), outlasting enemy decay generation to generation (Isa 51:8), figured as the city's very walls (Isa 26:1, Isa 60:18), and extended to all nations in scope (Isa 49:6, Isa 52:10, Luk 3:6). The characteristic inner-being state is settled confidence grounded not in anything present and visible but in the unshakeable permanence of God's salvific purpose.

**Member vc_ids:** [55485, 55491, 55492, 55498, 55489, 55493, 9071, 51531]

### M38-F-VCG-03

- Verse count: 6
- Anchor vc_id: 9050

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of the Savior as the personal object of eschatological hope — verses where the soul's forward orientation is explicitly anchored in the appearing or identity of the Savior (sōtēr) rather than in salvation as an abstract event. The inner being is characterized by a relational, person-directed expectancy: citizenship is in heaven from which the Savior is awaited (Phili 3:20), the blessed hope is the glorious appearing of the great God and Savior (Tit 2:13), Christ will appear a second time to bring salvation to those eagerly waiting (Heb 9:28), and the living God as Savior of all grounds the soul's hope (1Ti 4:10). The Savior's past appearing already abolished death and unveiled immortality (2Ti 1:10), so the soul's hope is anchored in a revealed person whose eschatological return is the object of sustained anticipation. The inner faculty is hope as personal trust oriented toward the returning Christ.

**Member vc_ids:** [9048, 9050, 51562, 9047, 9060, 9051]

### M38-F-VCG-04

- Verse count: 4
- Anchor vc_id: 55482

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of salvation as divinely assured imminence and militant certainty — verses where the accent falls on God's active, urgent, warrior-like movement toward completing the deliverance he has promised. The soul is not merely waiting passively but is being addressed by divine declarations that salvation will not be withheld and that God himself is mobilized toward rescue. God rides a chariot of salvation into cosmic battle (Hab 3:8), arms himself as divine warrior with a helmet of salvation (Isa 59:17), declares he will not rest until Jerusalem's salvation blazes forth (Isa 62:1), and is revealed as the one who helps in a divinely appointed day (Isa 49:8). The inner-being response is assurance and stabilized confidence — not because the soul has achieved this but because divine intent is irreversibly bent toward completion.

**Member vc_ids:** [55482, 55497, 55499, 55490]

### M38-F-VCG-05

- Verse count: 6
- Anchor vc_id: 8968

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of salvation experienced within present circumstances — rescue, comfort, and sustaining grace received in the midst of ongoing life rather than anticipated at an eschatological horizon. The characteristic inner content is salvation as present reality: already possessed in hope though not yet fully visible (Rom 8:24), counting affliction as producing salvation in others (2Cor 1:6), expecting personal deliverance from a crisis through prayer and the Spirit (Phili 1:19), the Lord's patience in delay itself being salvation (2Pe 3:15), Job's confidence before God as the ground of his present hope (Job 13:16), and salvation as an enduring abundance alongside wisdom (Isa 33:6). These verses share the inner-being quality of finding or appropriating salvation as something already operative in the present moment, even when its fullness remains future.

**Member vc_ids:** [55500, 55488, 8968, 51546, 51550, 51566]

### M38-F-VCG-06

- Verse count: 7
- Anchor vc_id: 51557

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of salvation as received gift and bestowed identity — verses where the soul's posture is one of reception, inheritance, and assurance of what is already granted or certain to be given. Salvation shapes the inner being's dignity and status: believers are heirs for whom angels minister (Heb 1:14), the author confidently asserts his readers possess things belonging to salvation (Heb 6:9), an entrance into the eternal kingdom is richly provided (2Pe 1:11), the Spirit is poured out richly through the Savior as overflowing gift (Tit 3:6), and God's goodness and kindness appeared as Savior revealing the character of rescue (Tit 3:4). Mary's spirit exults in God as her personal Savior, naming completed rescue as the ground of worship (Luk 1:47), and the Davidic horn of salvation is raised as covenantal fulfillment of ancestral promise (Luk 1:69). The inner faculty is receptive trust and assured identity, the soul inhabiting the gift rather than reaching toward it.

### M38-F-VCG-07

- Verse count: 1
- Anchor vc_id: 55486

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of confronting the failure or absence of salvation — specifically the collapse of human effort to achieve deliverance and the anguish of a salvation hoped for but experienced as absent. These verses evidence a soul that has encountered the limits of creaturely rescue and is left with either despair or the forced recognition that only God can save. Human labor at deliverance produces nothing (Isa 26:18), the community groans that salvation is far off (Isa 59:11 — dual), and the soul's inner movement is grief, mourning, and the acknowledgment of human incapacity. This VCG is the shadow-side of M38-F: salvation anticipated means that its absence is acutely felt, and the inner being is marked precisely by the unfulfilled longing that exposes the impossibility of self-rescue.
