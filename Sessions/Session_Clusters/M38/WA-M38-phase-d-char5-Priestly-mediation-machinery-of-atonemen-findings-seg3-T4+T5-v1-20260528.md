**T4.1.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence consistently shows God as the initiating source of the atonement machinery itself. At Lev 8:34 (vc_id=29606, M38-E-VCG-01), the meaning is explicit: "The LORD commanded the entire seven-day ordination ritual specifically to make atonement for the priests — the divine mandate frames the atonement as God's own initiative to establish a covered, holy priesthood." God is not merely receiving the atonement; he is the one who designs, mandates, and institutes the covering mechanism. At Lev 16:34 (vc_id=29628, M38-E-VCG-02), the annual atonement is a "perpetual statute" — divinely mandated recurring provision, not a human initiative. At Num 8:19 (vc_id=29634, M38-E-VCG-04), the Levites are "given" by God to serve and make atonement so that plague does not strike — the entire Levitical mediating structure is God's gift to protect Israel before his own holiness. The direction is therefore: God architectures the priestly mediation system and commands its operation, making the atonement machinery itself a divine movement toward the human community's sustained covered standing before him.

**T4.2.1 — Divine Interface — Human to God — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence shows the characteristic operating in the human-toward-God direction primarily through the priest's mediating action, which is itself a structured movement of the community toward God. At Lev 16:17 (vc_id=29623, M38-E-VCG-01), the high priest enters the Holy Place alone to make atonement — this solitary entry is the community's directed approach to God at its most concentrated point. At Lev 16:30 (vc_id=29625, M38-E-VCG-02), the Day of Atonement produces "total cleanness" and "the conscience unburdened" — the outcome of the human priestly movement is restored relational standing before God. Moses's intercession at Exo 32:30 (vc_id=29664, M38-E-VCG-05) is an explicit human-toward-God movement under the weight of the people's sin: he ascends to God with the stated intention to "make atonement" for the people's grave sin. At Neh 10:33 (vc_id=29646, M38-E-VCG-04), the community commits to sustain the sin-offering system as part of covenant renewal — a corporate volitional movement toward maintaining access to God. The human-to-God movement is thus mediated, structured, and priestly rather than unmediated personal approach.

**T4.1.2 — Divine Interface — God to Human — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — is it conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence points to a primarily covenantal and responsive basis, not unconditional extension. The atonement machinery is covenantal in that it is embedded within the Mosaic covenant structure — the statutes are given to Israel as part of the covenant relationship (Lev 16:34, vc_id=29628: "annual atonement for all of Israel's sins is established as a perpetual statute"). It is responsive in that specific offerings are required to activate specific atonement-effects: at Lev 15:15, 30 (vc_id=29617–29618), bodily discharge uncleanness requires bringing two birds; at Num 6:11 (vc_id=29632), accidental Nazirite defilement requires specific sacrifice before the atonement is granted. God does not extend atonement apart from the prescribed mechanism — the mechanism itself is God's covenantal provision, but the human side must engage it. The basis is thus best characterized as covenantal-conditional: God provides the system unconditionally as his covenantal gift, but the activation of atonement for specific persons and situations is conditional on proper use of the prescribed mechanism.

**T4.2.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence reveals several dimensions of required inner posture. First, self-atonement precedes intercession for others: Lev 9:7 (vc_id=29607) and Lev 16:6, 11 (vc_id=29619, 29621) together establish that the mediator must first resolve their own sin-laden state before approaching God on behalf of others — uncovered approach is implicitly prohibited. This requires an honest acknowledgment of one's own sinful condition. Second, solitary weight-bearing: Lev 16:17 (vc_id=29623) describes the high priest entering alone, carrying "the weight of the entire community's standing before God" — the inner posture is one of solidarity-burden, the mediator willingly bearing others' guilt-weight before God. Third, urgency and sacrificial willingness: Moses at Exo 32:30 (vc_id=29664) ascends with "uncertain outcome" and "sacrificial will to stand between them and divine wrath" — the inner posture includes personal risk-acceptance. Aaron's running with incense into the plague (Num 16:46–47, vc_id=29638–29639) evidences similar urgency. The posture overall is: covered-self, burdened-with-others, willing-to-risk.

**T5.5.2 — Formation and Sanctification — What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The evidence reveals that the priestly mediation machinery of atonement functions as the maintenance infrastructure of corporate sanctification rather than its generative source. The role is preservative and restorative: the community's holy standing before God, once established, requires ongoing atoning mediation to be maintained against the accumulation of sin and uncleanness. The annual reset (Lev 16:34, vc_id=29628), the monthly and feast-day sin offerings (Num 28:22, 28:30, 29:5 — vc_id=29641–29643), and the Aaronic ministry described in 1Ch 6:49 (vc_id=29644) all position this characteristic as the scaffold that keeps the community in sanctified standing over time. The longer-arc role is therefore: without this mediation machinery, sanctification-standing deteriorates through guilt accumulation; with it, the community is continuously returned to clean standing before God. It is not the engine of sanctification but the mechanism that prevents its erosion.
