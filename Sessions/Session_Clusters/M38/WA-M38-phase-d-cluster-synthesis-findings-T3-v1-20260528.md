# M38 Phase D Stage B — cluster synthesis — T3 findings (re-run)

**Tier:** T3 — 33 prompts

---

**T3.1.1 — Perception — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty — the inner senses including hearing, sight, taste, touch, smell, and spiritual discernment — and if so, which inner sense and how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across the seven characteristics, inner hearing and inner sight are the dominant perceptive modes, but their relationship to the characteristic differs structurally. Hearing is the entry channel for characteristics whose saving content arrives as proclaimed word (CHAR-1: "word of salvation" received through hearing; CHAR-7: the herald's announcement). Inner sight operates differently depending on whether the characteristic is anticipatory or retrospective: CHAR-6 projects sight forward toward an invisible eschatological horizon; CHAR-7 recovers sight as the experiential marker of rescue from the pit. Spiritual discernment — the capacity to perceive what physical senses cannot access — appears across CHAR-1, CHAR-2 (Noah's reception of unreceived warning), CHAR-3 (faith-seekers perceiving Jesus's healing identity despite physical impairment), and CHAR-6. The cluster's most revealing contrast is between CHAR-2 and CHAR-3: in CHAR-2, physical perception of danger hyper-activates and narrows the inner being to terror; in CHAR-3, physical perception is precisely what is bypassed or irrelevant, with spiritual apprehension operative in its place. CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 show perception functioning preparatorily — as the trigger for atonement-seeking (perceiving one's guilt, perceiving the holy presence) — rather than as the constitutive mode of the characteristic itself. The cluster pattern thus distinguishes characteristics where perception is the entry-point of salvific content (CHAR-1, CHAR-7) from those where perception is the precipitating input preceding the characteristic's core operation (CHAR-2, CHAR-4, CHAR-5), and from one characteristic where the entire posture is a training of perception toward the unseen (CHAR-6).

**T3.2.1 — Cognition — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty — knowing, understanding, discerning — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster reveals a consistent pattern: cognition is engaged across all seven characteristics, but never as the originating or causally sufficient faculty. In every case something else — hearing, conscience, affect, crisis, divine warning — activates the characteristic, and cognition participates as a secondary, integrating function. The most significant differentiator is the type of cognitive engagement. CHAR-5 and CHAR-4 engage procedural and diagnostic cognition respectively — knowing the prescribed sequence, knowing that one has sinned — without requiring speculative or exploratory understanding. CHAR-6 and CHAR-1 engage the most demanding cognitive modes: investigative searching (1Pe 1:10), temporal discernment (the appointed day), and the formation of wisdom (2Ti 3:15). CHAR-2 produces the starkest spectrum within a single characteristic: acute mortal crisis bypasses deliberative cognition entirely in panic mode, while the Gethsemane deliberation represents cognition held steady and deepened under maximum anguish. CHAR-3 operates at the minimal threshold — enough cognitive content to generate a conditional proposition ("if I touch…"), but not extended theological reasoning. Across the cluster, the finding that emerges is that salvation-cluster characteristics do not require cognitive sophistication as a precondition for operation: CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 function for the economically humble, the accidentally sinning, and those whose only available cognitive act is recognizing guilt. Cognition serves the characteristic; the characteristic does not arise from cognition.

**T3.3.1 — Memory — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty — the holding and retrieving of inner-being reality across time — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster divides sharply on memory: CHAR-7 is constitutively memory-dependent (the characteristic cannot exist without active retrieval of the past ransom event), while CHAR-2 is constitutively memory-independent (the entire sub-group is present-tense and situationally immediate, with memory absent across every VCG). This contrast is the cluster's most revealing polarity. Between these poles, a gradient appears: CHAR-1 uses remembered historical rescue as a warning resource and temporal anchor but does not depend on memory as its generative centre; CHAR-6 draws on generational covenantal memory as the infrastructure that makes hope possible, supplying the content that anticipation projects forward. CHAR-4 introduces the theologically distinctive bidirectional memory pattern: divine memory cleared (God remembers sins no more), divine memory redirected to positive remembrance, while human memory of past sin persists after covering, producing humility and silence. CHAR-5 institutionalises memory through annual-cycle structure — the Day of Atonement is the community's regularised confrontation with accumulated sin-memory. CHAR-3's memory engagement is inferential: prior testimony about Jesus functions as substrate enabling present conviction, but memory is not the operative faculty in the healing moment itself. The cluster pattern reveals that memory is most central to characteristics with a retrospective identity-structure (CHAR-7) and most absent from characteristics defined by acute present-moment crisis (CHAR-2).

**T3.4.1 — Affect — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty — feeling and emotional experience — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Every characteristic in the cluster engages affect, but the affective register, intensity, and phase differ markedly. CHAR-2 is the most affect-saturated: terror, anguish, weeping, despair, and reverent awe are not accompaniments to the characteristic but constitutive of it — affect is the phenomenological register within which the entire dynamic occurs. CHAR-6 spans the widest affective range (anguish, exultation, eager longing, confident rest, communal distress) and is notable for being affectively permissive — neither suppressing negative affect nor resolving it prematurely. CHAR-1 shows a three-phase affective arc: pre-salvific grief and urgency, reception peace and awe, ongoing gratitude and eschatological joy. CHAR-4 produces a specifically chastened register: not euphoria but relief-under-shame — the burden lifted while contrition remains. CHAR-5 concentrates affective engagement in the mediator (zeal, solidarity-burden, urgency) while the prescribed procedural structure itself is largely affect-neutral. CHAR-3's primary affective contribution is transformative: fear displaced by peace, urgency channelled into healing. CHAR-7's affective culmination is the most comprehensive: sorrow and sighing flee entirely, replaced by everlasting joy (Isa 35:10). The cross-cluster pattern reveals that affect is most fully engaged not in cognitive or procedural characteristics (CHAR-4, CHAR-5 in their institutional form) but in those where crisis, anticipation, or identity-transformation press upon the whole inner person.

**T3.5.1 — Creativity — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty — imagination and the capacity to originate — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** S/E — The cluster is predominantly silent on creativity, with one significant exception and one partial engagement. CHAR-6 is the sole characteristic with substantial creativity engagement: the soul that must hold an invisible salvation as real generates imaginative figuring — city walls, divine warrior's helmet, blazing torch, cosmic chariot — because the eschatological object exceeds any single image and requires imaginative construction to be held present. CHAR-2 provides the only other evidenced engagement: Noah's ark-building in VCG-04, where faith in a warning about unseen catastrophe generates sustained creative-productive action. Critically, this is the exception within CHAR-2; its crisis modes (panic-cry, despair) completely bypass creativity. CHAR-1, CHAR-3, CHAR-4, CHAR-5, and CHAR-7 are all silent. The cluster pattern is itself a structural finding: six of seven characteristics do not engage the creative faculty because their operative posture is receptive, responsive, or prescribed rather than originating. The one characteristic that generates creative imagination (CHAR-6) does so precisely because it must make present to the inner being a reality not yet visible — imagination is recruited where perception and memory cannot reach. The systematic silence elsewhere reveals that the salvation-cluster characteristics are constitutively receptive in mode: salvation is received, atonement is performed by another, healing is received through faith, ransom is remembered — none of these are creative acts.

**T3.6.1 — Volition — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty — the capacity to choose — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Volition is engaged across all seven characteristics, making it one of the cluster's most universally operative faculties. However, the cluster reveals three distinct volitional modes. The first is volitional surrender: CHAR-1 requires the will's surrender of self-preservation (Mat 16:25 cluster), and CHAR-2 in VCG-03 presents the most complete volitional surrender in the entire cluster — Jesus's deliberate choice not to exercise the rescue-cry, accepting the appointed hour. The second is volitional activation: CHAR-3 is constitutively volitional — the woman presses through the crowd, Bartimaeus persists against opposition, the father pleads with determination. The third is volitional constraint within divine prescription: CHAR-5 is most clearly this mode — the priest must comply with the ordained sequence, and volitional freedom is exercised within those boundaries. CHAR-6 adds the dimension of sustained volitional discipline — choosing to wait, to align conduct morally, to set hope — across time. CHAR-4 reveals the asymmetry most clearly: volition is engaged in the approach to atonement (bringing sacrifice, praying) but is not volitionally productive of the atonement itself, which is received. CHAR-7 shows volition both freed and directed by the characteristic: ransom removes oppressive constraint and reorients the will toward obedience and ethical action. The cluster finding is that salvation-related characteristics consistently engage volition but never as the causally sufficient faculty — the will participates without being the origin of salvation.

**T3.7.1 — Agency — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty — the capacity to act, initiate, and make happen — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Agency is engaged across the entire cluster, but its distribution and direction reveal the cluster's defining theological structure: divine agency is primary and causally originating in every characteristic; human agency is derivative, responsive, and enabled. This asymmetry is most explicit in CHAR-1 (God saves; humans call, confess, endure), CHAR-4 (God atones causally; humans activate the reception channel), and CHAR-6 (human effort at self-rescue produces nothing; divine saving agency acts with certainty). CHAR-5 presents the most structurally concentrated priestly agency — the priest acts, initiates, and mediates while the community receives — making the agency asymmetry between mediator and recipient a constitutive feature of the characteristic. CHAR-3 is the most distinctive agency finding in the cluster: Jesus's attribution formula ("your faith has made you well") elevates human agency to named secondary causation within a divinely-effected event, an elevation unique in the cluster. CHAR-2 reveals agency at its limits: mortal threat strips normal agency until only the cry remains, and what happens to the inner being at that zero-point defines the characteristic. CHAR-7 generates agency as overflow: the ransomed identity produces specific initiatives (intercession, ethical action, communal solidarity) without being reducible to any one of them. The cluster's consistent pattern is that human agency is neither bypassed nor self-sufficient — it is real, named, and required, while remaining constitutively dependent on prior divine initiative.

**T3.8.1 — Moral Evaluation — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty — the capacity to assess against a standard of right, wrong, good, and true — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Moral evaluation is engaged across all seven characteristics but in two structurally distinct modes. The first is diagnostic-categorical moral evaluation: CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 engage moral evaluation primarily by applying a given taxonomy (sin/guilt/uncleanness vs. covered/clean) to persons and situations. The moral framework is pre-given; the evaluative act is applying it. The second is deliberative-normative moral evaluation: CHAR-1, CHAR-2 (in VCG-03), CHAR-6, and CHAR-7 engage the evaluative faculty in weighing standards, assessing conduct, and forming moral judgments that shape subsequent inner-being orientation. The most revealing cross-characteristic comparison is between CHAR-2's VCG-02 (the mockers apply power-logic as their moral standard and fail to make the true moral evaluation) and CHAR-1's salvific framework (the cross, evaluated as foolishness by those perishing, is correctly evaluated as God's power by those being saved). Both involve moral evaluation of the same object producing opposite assessments — revealing that the salvation cluster as a whole creates a new moral-evaluative framework rather than simply applying a prior one. CHAR-7 shows moral evaluation most explicitly calibrated by received grace: betrayal is not merely wrong but specifically aggravated by prior ransom. Across the cluster, moral evaluation is never absent but is consistently shaped by the salvific reality each characteristic embodies — the standard by which things are assessed is itself transformed by the characteristic.

**T3.9.1 — Conscience — Does this characteristic engage the conscience — the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E/S — Conscience engagement divides the cluster along a clear structural line. CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 are the most conscience-saturated: CHAR-4 is definitionally a conscience-event (guilt registered, conscience struck, conscience cleansed), and CHAR-5 exists as the divinely-provided mechanism for resolving the conscience-weight that accumulates before a holy God. CHAR-1 and CHAR-3 engage conscience in a two-phase pattern: conscience activated as precondition (godly grief, awakened urgency, desire for a good conscience) and conscience addressed as outcome (cleansing received, good conscience restored). CHAR-2 is completely silent on conscience — the most definitionally significant silence in the cluster. The panic-cry of CHAR-2 is terror in the face of mortal physical threat, not the conscience's witness in the face of moral transgression; this silence confirms CHAR-2's boundary distinctness from guilt-driven characteristics. CHAR-6 engages conscience marginally and in background mode: hope rests on a conscience-cleared confidence (Job 13:16) or is deepened by conscience-aware acknowledgment of moral failure (Isa 59:11 context), but conscience is not the generative center of the characteristic. CHAR-7 shows conscience as simultaneously sensitised (ransomed identity sharpens awareness of betrayal's gravity) and ultimately resolved (total ransom from iniquity addresses accumulated guilt). The cluster finding is that conscience is the structural hinge between characteristics that address moral guilt before God and those that address mortal danger, with CHAR-2's silence marking the phenomenological boundary.

**T3.10.1 — Conscientiousness — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness — the integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Conscientiousness — the integrated coordination of moral awareness, volition, and sustained action — is engaged across six of seven characteristics, with CHAR-2 providing the significant exception. CHAR-1 generates conscientiousness as its ongoing inner-being expression through the perseverance cluster (Phili 2:12 as paradigmatic instance). CHAR-5 requires and exercises conscientiousness in the mediator: self-covering first (moral awareness of one's own state), precise sequencing (volitional compliance), and crisis response (immediate integrated action). CHAR-6 makes conscientiousness the behavioral expression of sustained hope: imminent salvation does not produce moral passivity but heightened integration of awareness, will, and conduct. CHAR-3's most revealing conscientiousness instance is the returning Samaritan leper — Jesus's distinction between him and the nine confirms that the sōzō-wholeness circuit is most completely realised where conscientiousness completes the faith-response. CHAR-7 shows conscientiousness as the characteristic's ethical telos: the Deuteronomic pattern (remember → be motivated → act) is a conscientious coordination that the characteristic is specifically designed to produce. CHAR-2's crisis modes (panic-cry, despair) bypass conscientiousness structurally — mortal threat reduces the inner being below the threshold of integrated character response. This contrast between CHAR-2 (sub-conscientious by structural necessity) and CHAR-6 (conscientiousness as the characteristic's behavioral expression) reveals the cluster's widest operational range in this faculty.

**T3.11.1 — Relational Capacity — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity — the constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Relational capacity is the most universally and consistently engaged faculty across the entire cluster — all seven characteristics engage it, and in every case the engagement is structurally central rather than incidental. The cluster reveals three relational structures. The first is bilateral (human-divine): CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-3, CHAR-6, and CHAR-7 all involve direct personal orientation of the inner being toward God or Christ as the named relational object of faith, petition, waiting, or gratitude. The second is triadic (mediator-God-people): CHAR-5 introduces the three-party structure that defines priestly mediation — the characteristic exists because direct human-divine relational standing requires a relationally-qualified intermediary to restore it. CHAR-4 is the most explicitly relational in its problem-definition: guilt is a disruption of the person's standing before God, and atonement is relational restoration. The third is horizontal: CHAR-1 and CHAR-7 extend relational capacity toward others — CHAR-1 toward the saved community (Act 2:47) and missionary concern (1 Cor 7:16), CHAR-7 toward the vulnerable (Deu 15:15). The cluster's most distinctive relational finding is the comparison between CHAR-2 and CHAR-5: CHAR-2 intensifies and activates vertical relational capacity under mortal threat (the cry as the most urgent relational act possible), while CHAR-5 sustains and exercises it through sustained mediatorial solidarity with both God and the people. The consistent pattern is that every salvation characteristic in M38 is irreducibly relational in its operational structure.

**T3.1.2 — Perception — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster reveals a gradient from perception-enabling to perception-bypassing, organised by the relationship between physical and spiritual sensory modes. CHAR-6 and CHAR-1 most clearly enable and deepen perception: CHAR-6 trains the inner being to perceive beyond physical sight toward a divinely real but invisible salvation (Rom 8:24's hope as perception of the unseen); CHAR-1 enables spiritual sight unavailable to the unsaved (Simeon's eyes, Heb 6:4's taste of the heavenly gift). CHAR-3 presents the cluster's most theologically significant pattern: physical perception is precisely bypassed in favour of spiritual apprehension (Luk 8:50 commands faith against the perceptual report that the child is dead), revealing that this characteristic's operation requires the subordination of physical sense to spiritual discernment. CHAR-2 produces a mixed picture: sensory hyper-activation in panic mode (narrowing perception to the threatening stimulus) and spiritual-perception deepening in faith-obedience mode (Noah perceiving divine warning about things not yet seen). CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 are perceptively preparatory rather than transformative: the characteristic presupposes adequate perception in the mediator and is triggered by prior perceptive recognition, but does not itself transform the faculty. CHAR-7's key contribution is that ransomed identity restores perceptual capacity that danger had occluded — "his soul sees the light" as the experiential marker of rescue from the pit. Across the cluster, the consistent direction is perception-enabling when the characteristic is fully operative, with narrowing or bypassing occurring only under crisis conditions.

**T3.2.2 — Cognition — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster divides into two groups regarding cognition's directionality. The enabling-deepening group includes CHAR-1 (knowledge of salvation, wisdom-for-salvation through Scripture, truth-reception), CHAR-6 (the most cognitively demanding — investigative searching, reinterpretation of delay, protective sobriety), and CHAR-7 (ransomed identity as fixed epistemic anchor deepening accurate assessment of reality). The bypassing group includes CHAR-2 (panic mode bypasses deliberation entirely), CHAR-3 (faith precedes and partially bypasses extended theological cognition — the woman acts on minimal conditional inference), and CHAR-5 (divine prescription partially supplants open cognitive reasoning — the priest follows procedure rather than exercising open discernment). CHAR-4 is the most revealing: atonement-received is not dependent on cognitive achievement (it functions for the accidentally sinning, the economically humble, the irregularly prepared), and divine forgiveness (God "remembering sins no more") bypasses the cognitive dimension entirely — the forgiveness is divine action, not human comprehension. The cross-cluster finding is that cognitive demanding-ness correlates with the characteristic's temporal horizon: eschatological anticipation (CHAR-6) and identity-constitution through remembered grace (CHAR-7) are the most cognitively demanding characteristics, while acute crisis (CHAR-2) and institutional provision (CHAR-4, CHAR-5) bypass cognition structurally. This reveals that cognition serves as the faculty of sustained temporal orientation rather than immediate crisis response.

**T3.3.2 — Memory — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E/S — The cluster reveals a structural polarity in memory effects. CHAR-7 enables and deepens memory most directly — the characteristic's entire operational logic requires active memory maintenance, and its absence produces the characteristic's collapse. CHAR-6 deepens memory by activating and drawing on covenantal reserves: stored prophetic memory supplies the content that hope projects forward. CHAR-4 is the most theologically complex: divine memory is cleared and redirected while human memory of past sin persists, producing humility — atonement does not erase the human's own memory of guilt; it covers the guilt before God. CHAR-5 structures and institutionalises community memory through the annual-cycle mechanism: the Day of Atonement forces regularised confrontation with accumulated sin-memory rather than allowing the community to drift into forgetting. CHAR-1 stabilises memory in an enabling direction: recalled historical rescue functions as warning resource and temporal anchor for present faith. CHAR-3 is only inferentially engaged: prior testimony about Jesus functions as enabling substrate, but memory is not the operative faculty in the healing moment. CHAR-2 is completely and consistently silent — the only characteristic in the cluster that produces zero engagement with memory, confirming its constitutive present-tense, situationally-immediate nature. The cluster finding is that memory engagement tracks with whether the characteristic has a retrospective identity-structure: CHAR-7 and CHAR-4 (in its divine-memory dimension) are most deeply memory-constituted, while CHAR-2's silence marks the opposite pole.

**T3.4.2 — Affect — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Every characteristic in the cluster enables or deepens affect; none impair it. The cluster's most significant differentiator is not the direction of affective engagement but its quality and phase. CHAR-2 intensifies affect to its most acute register — the characteristic is constituted by affect under crisis conditions, producing the widest intensity range (terror to reverent awe). CHAR-1 produces a distinctive three-phase affective sequence: pre-salvific grief → reception peace → sustained gratitude and joy. CHAR-7 produces the most comprehensive affective transformation: the ransomed state does not merely add positive affect but displaces sorrow and sighing entirely (Isa 35:10). CHAR-6 is affectively permissive — it holds grief, confidence, eagerness, and distress within the same sustained hope-posture rather than resolving or suppressing them. CHAR-4 produces the cluster's most complex and least euphoric affective register: relief under shame, contrition maintained alongside covering received — a chastened rather than celebratory affect. CHAR-5 concentrates affective expression in the mediator (zeal, urgency, solidarity-burden) while the prescribed procedural structure is affect-neutral, and the community receives affective relief as outcome. CHAR-3 transforms affect rather than eliminating it: fear is displaced by peace, urgency is channelled toward wholeness. Across the cluster, no characteristic bypasses or impairs affect — the consistent pattern is that these characteristics either amplify existing affect, redirect it, or produce new affective states as their constitutive outcome.

**T3.5.2 — Creativity — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** S/E — The cluster is predominantly silent or bypassing on creativity, with CHAR-6 as the sole enabling exception. CHAR-6 enables and deepens creative imagination specifically because its object is invisible: the soul that must hold an unseen future salvation as real generates imaginative figuring — the diversity of imagery (city walls, helmet, torch, chariot) reveals that no single creative figure suffices and imaginative energy is continuously recruited. CHAR-2 bypasses creativity structurally in its crisis modes: mortal threat and despair eliminate creative capacity from the inner being's available repertoire, and Noah's ark-building (the one enabling instance) is distinctive precisely for its extended time-horizon separating it from acute crisis. CHAR-1, CHAR-3, CHAR-4, CHAR-5, and CHAR-7 are all consistently silent. The cluster pattern is revealing: six of seven characteristics are receptive, responsive, or prescribed in their operational structure — salvation received, atonement executed, healing received through faith, ransom remembered — and none of these require or produce creative origination. The cluster finding is that imagination is engaged only where the characteristic's object is temporally and spatially absent (CHAR-6's eschatological horizon) and bypassed where the object is immediately present, historically past, or procedurally prescribed. This reveals that creativity's role in the salvation cluster is specifically as the faculty of making-present-the-absent rather than origination as such.

**T3.6.2 — Volition — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition in the person — including its three aspects: capacity, interaction with other characteristics, and the constraints under which it operates?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster reveals that all seven characteristics engage volition without impairing it, but that they produce markedly different volitional effects. CHAR-3 most clearly strengthens volition — the faith-seeker's determined will presses through opposition, and Jesus's attribution formula confirms that the volitional act was causally operative. CHAR-7 is the most explicitly volitional-liberating: ransom removes oppressive constraint on the will (Psa 119:134) and reorients its direction toward obedience and ethical action toward others. CHAR-1 requires and sustains volition across three phases (initial turning, sustained perseverance, missionary redirection) while providing divine enabling that makes the volitional engagement possible (grace interacting with the will). CHAR-4's volitional pattern is asymmetric in the cluster's most theologically distinctive way: volition is required in approach to atonement, but atonement itself is received, not volitionally generated — the post-atonement consequence is volitional release and moral reorientation. CHAR-5 constrains volition within divine prescription while preserving genuine volitional engagement within that framework (crisis intercession as the clearest instance of genuine risk-taking will). CHAR-2's volitional range — from compression to a single cry-act to maximum deliberate surrender in Gethsemane — reveals that the quality of inner-being resources determines whether mortal threat compresses or deepens volitional capacity. CHAR-6 sustains volitional discipline across time as its characteristic output: waiting, aligning conduct morally, approaching God persistently despite risk.

**T3.7.2 — Agency — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster consistently enables agency without bypassing or impairing it, but does so within a structurally asymmetric framework: divine agency is always primary, and human agency is always derivative and responsive. The most enabling characteristics for human agency are CHAR-3 (where Jesus's attribution formula names the person's inner act as causally operative) and CHAR-7 (where ransom generates specific agentic outputs across multiple domains: intercession, ethical action, communal solidarity). CHAR-1 redirects agency: it does not produce passivity but reorients and empowers human agency toward God and others, making missionary agency one of its characteristic outputs. CHAR-2 reveals agency at its constitutive limit: mortal threat strips human agency to its irreducible minimum (the cry), and what the inner being does at that point defines the characteristic. CHAR-5 presents the cluster's most asymmetric agency distribution: priestly agency is maximal and sustained, while the recipient community's agency is structurally minimised — they receive rather than act. CHAR-4 enables agency sequentially: the approach requires human agency, the atonement is received by divine agency, and the post-atonement state releases and redirects human agency (Isa 6:8). CHAR-6 both enables limited genuine agency (waiting, searching, enduring) and forecloses self-rescue agency — not as impairment but as proper calibration of creaturely limitation. The cluster's defining agency-pattern is that human agency is elevated but never autonomous, derivative but never eliminated.

**T3.8.2 — Moral Evaluation — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster shows moral evaluation enabled or deepened across all seven characteristics, with bypassing occurring only in CHAR-2's crisis modes and partially in CHAR-5's procedural structure. The most significant cluster-level finding is how the characteristics interact with the moral evaluative standard itself. CHAR-1 does not merely apply a prior moral framework but creates a new one: the cross becomes the evaluative criterion distinguishing those being saved (who correctly evaluate it as God's power) from those perishing (who evaluate it as folly). CHAR-4 requires full moral evaluation as its precondition — sin must be accurately assessed as genuinely guilty — while also revealing that moral evaluation alone without atonement-coverage produces permanent condemnation (the negative pole). CHAR-6 makes moral evaluation eschatologically motivated: the nearness of salvation sharpens the inner being's awareness of the gap between present conduct and the righteous standard, producing moral urgency rather than passivity. CHAR-7 provides the most calibrated moral evaluation: the ransomed identity makes ingratitude, injustice, and betrayal specifically more culpable — moral evaluation is sharpened by received grace. CHAR-5 engages diagnostic rather than deliberative moral evaluation — applying a given taxonomy rather than generating new moral understanding. CHAR-2's panic mode bypasses moral evaluation structurally, though its VCG-03 deliberation represents moral-purposive evaluation at the highest level. Across the cluster, moral evaluation serves the characteristic rather than generating it.

**T3.9.2 — Conscience — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E/S — The cluster reveals that conscience is neither bypassed nor impaired by any characteristic, but its role differs sharply by position in the salvific sequence. CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 are conscience-addressing characteristics: they take conscience-weight as their presenting problem and provide the mechanism for its resolution. CHAR-4 cleanses conscience; CHAR-5 provides the divinely-ordained means by which that cleansing occurs. Critically, neither silences the conscience: CHAR-4 shows conscience remaining active post-covering (Eze 16:63's shame and silence), and CHAR-5 enables genuine conscience-clearing rather than conscience-suppression. CHAR-1 both activates and cleanses conscience in sequence: godly grief (conscience pierced) leads to repentance-leading-to-salvation, which then clears what was activated. CHAR-3's conscience engagement progressively deepens across its VCGs: physical peace (implied conscience-rest) in VCG-01–02, explicitly cleared conscience in VCG-03 — revealing that the characteristic's full interior reach is conscience as terminus. CHAR-7 both sensitises and resolves conscience: ransomed identity sharpens the conscience's awareness of betrayal's gravity while the hope of total ransom (Psa 130:8) provides the conscience's ultimate resolution. CHAR-6 engages conscience in background mode — as the awareness against which hope is maintained rather than as its generative centre. CHAR-2 is definitively silent — its terror-and-cry has no conscience dimension, marking the phenomenological boundary between mortal-danger crisis and guilt-driven spiritual states.

**T3.10.2 — Conscientiousness — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster reveals a clear pattern: all characteristics except CHAR-2 enable or deepen conscientiousness, and CHAR-2's bypassing in crisis mode is structural rather than pathological — mortal threat reduces the inner being below the threshold of integrated character response by necessity. The most striking enabling characteristic for conscientiousness is CHAR-6: sustained hope-toward-salvation does not remain interior but produces integrated outer life — moral awareness, chosen alignment, and disciplined conduct converge. CHAR-1 generates conscientiousness as the ongoing expression of received salvation, particularly through the perseverance cluster: "work out your own salvation with fear and trembling" is the paradigmatic integrated response. CHAR-7 produces conscientiousness as its ethical output — the Deuteronomic pattern (remember ransom → be motivated → act toward the vulnerable) is a conscientious coordination that the characteristic is specifically designed to generate. CHAR-5 requires conscientiousness from the mediator at every stage while enabling it from the recipient through the provision that makes integrated response possible. CHAR-3's completing instance (the returning Samaritan leper) reveals that conscientiousness is where the sōzō-wholeness circuit reaches its fullest expression — the healing-wholeness is more fully realised in the person who integrates awareness, will, and action in response. CHAR-4 shows conscientiousness operating proximately (in approach to atonement) and subsequently (in post-atonement moral turning), but not as a deepened character-disposition over time. The cluster identifies conscientiousness as the behavioral expression of sustained salvific orientation.

**T3.11.2 — Relational Capacity — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity in the person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Relational capacity is the single faculty that is enabled or deepened across every characteristic without exception — no bypassing or impairing is evidenced anywhere in the cluster. This makes relational capacity the most universally operative faculty in the entire M38 salvation cluster. Three relational structures appear. The bilateral human-divine structure (characteristic directing the inner being toward God/Christ as personal object) is present in CHAR-1, CHAR-2, CHAR-3, CHAR-6, and CHAR-7. The triadic mediatorial structure (God — priest — people) is the defining form of CHAR-5, where the priest's qualified standing before God is the precondition for the people's relational restoration. CHAR-4 is most explicitly relational in its problem-definition: guilt is a disruption of the person's standing before God; atonement is relational restoration; and the entire mechanism (substitutionary sacrifice, propitiation, forgiveness) is constitutively dyadic. The cluster's most revealing comparison is between CHAR-2 and CHAR-5 in their relational depth: CHAR-2 intensifies and activates relational capacity under mortal crisis (the most urgent human-divine appeal), while CHAR-5 sustains it through the mediator's solidarity with both God and community over institutional time. CHAR-7 is distinctively bilateral in both directions — the ransomed identity generates both deepened vertical capacity (trust, allegiance, gratitude toward God) and horizontal capacity (empathic justice toward the vulnerable). The cluster-level finding is that salvation, in all its modes, is constitutively relational — it neither exists nor operates outside of genuine personal connection.

**T3.1.3 — Perception — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with perception reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative pattern across the seven characteristics reveals that perception functions differently at each stage of the salvific movement rather than uniformly. For CHAR-1 and CHAR-7, perception is the entry channel: the salvific word must be heard, the salvific light must be seen for the characteristic to be activated. For CHAR-2 and CHAR-3, perception is the differential factor: CHAR-2's hyper-activated sensory perception of mortal threat generates the fear-cry; CHAR-3's faith precisely bypasses that same perceptive mode, substituting spiritual apprehension for physical sense-report. This contrast between the two is the cluster's sharpest perceptive polarity — both characteristics involve crises, but one is constituted by terror-at-what-is-seen and the other by trust-despite-what-is-seen. For CHAR-4 and CHAR-5, perception is preparatory and presuppositional: the mediator must perceive the holy presence and the person must perceive their guilt-state, but the characteristic itself is not perceptive in its core operation. CHAR-6 is the cluster's most distinctive perceptive characteristic: it is constitutively a re-orientation of perception toward an invisible eschatological horizon — the entire characteristic trains the inner being to perceive in a trans-empirical mode. The cluster's overall finding is that perception is neither the seat nor the primary content of any salvation characteristic, but functions as the entry-point, trigger, or presuppositional context upon which the characteristic's deeper operation depends.

**T3.2.3 — Cognition — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with cognition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative pattern across seven characteristics reveals a consistent structural principle: cognition is required without being generative. No characteristic in the cluster arises from cognitive effort alone, and no characteristic is inaccessible to those of limited cognitive development. The gradient from minimal-cognitive-threshold (CHAR-4: atonement functions for the accidentally sinning and economically humble; CHAR-2: panic-cry bypasses deliberation entirely) to cognitively demanding (CHAR-6: investigative searching, reinterpretation of delay; CHAR-7: sustained memorial reflection) reveals that cognitive engagement scales with the characteristic's temporal horizon rather than its spiritual depth. Acute-crisis characteristics (CHAR-2) and institutionalised provision (CHAR-4, CHAR-5) operate at low cognitive threshold; temporally extended, identity-constituting, and anticipatory characteristics (CHAR-6, CHAR-7, CHAR-1) require sustained cognitive engagement. The cluster's decisive negative finding comes from CHAR-1: 1 Cor 1:21 establishes that human wisdom, even at its maximum cognitive achievement, failed to reach God — cognition could not generate the salvific reality that the characteristic embodies. This applies structurally to the entire cluster: the salvation-cluster characteristics are divinely given and humanly received, not cognitively constructed. Cognition serves as the faculty that receives, processes, and integrates what is given from outside — it is receptive and integrative, never originating.

**T3.3.3 — Memory — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with memory reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative memory pattern across the seven characteristics reveals a fundamental structural distinction within the cluster between temporally-constituted and temporally-immediate characteristics. CHAR-7 is the most memory-constituted: its entire operational logic is retrospective retrieval, and without active memorial maintenance it dissolves. CHAR-2 is its exact structural opposite: constitutively present-tense, situationally immediate, requiring no memorial retrieval — the mortal threat in the present moment is sufficient to generate the full dynamic. Between these poles, the cluster reveals a spectrum: CHAR-6 uses stored covenantal memory as the infrastructure of forward-oriented hope (past divine promises supply the content that anticipation projects toward the future); CHAR-4 shows the theologically distinctive divine-human memory asymmetry (divine memory cleared and redirected, human memory of guilt persisting as humility); CHAR-5 institutionalises community memory through annual-cycle confrontation with accumulated sin; CHAR-1 stabilises and warns through recalled historical rescue; CHAR-3 depends inferentially on prior testimony about Jesus but does not activate memory as its operative moment. The cluster finding is that memory's role tracks with whether the characteristic has a retrospective identity-structure (CHAR-7 most, CHAR-4 distinctively), a historical-promise structure (CHAR-6, CHAR-1), or an acute-crisis structure (CHAR-2 silent). This reveals that the salvation cluster encompasses both time-spanning and time-puncturing characteristics — both those that require memorial depth and those that operate precisely where memory has no time to function.

**T3.4.3 — Affect — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with affect reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The universally rich affective engagement across all seven characteristics reveals that salvation in M38 is constitutionally affective — it cannot be abstractly described without distortion, because every mode of salvific operation touches and transforms the emotional life. However, the pattern reveals three distinct affective functions across the cluster. First, affect as the characteristic's phenomenological medium: CHAR-2 is most purely this — the characteristic is not merely accompanied by affect but occurs within and through affect (terror, anguish, reverent awe are the very texture of the mortal-danger experience). Second, affect as the characteristic's outcome: CHAR-1 and CHAR-7 produce affect as their consequential end-state — peace, joy, gratitude, everlasting displacement of sorrow — as what the characteristic issues in. Third, affect as the characteristic's permissive context: CHAR-6 holds the widest affective range (grief, confidence, eagerness, exultation, communal distress) within a sustained hope-posture, revealing that salvific hope does not suppress or resolve negative affect prematurely but holds all registers within its framework. CHAR-4's distinctively chastened affective register — relief under shame, contrition maintained — reveals that atonement-received does not produce affective simplicity but moral-emotional complexity. CHAR-5 concentrates affective energy in the mediator while the procedural structure remains affect-neutral, revealing that the same characteristic can be affectively charged at the agent-level and affectively neutral at the structural level. The cluster confirms that affect is the inner being's responsiveness to salvation's reality, not its generator.

**T3.5.3 — Creativity — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with creativity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** S/E — The pattern of overwhelming silence on creativity across six of seven characteristics — with CHAR-6 as sole substantive exception — is itself the cluster's definitive finding on this faculty. The silence is not incidental but structurally revealing: the salvation cluster characteristics are constitutively receptive, responsive, and prescribed rather than originating or generative. Salvation is received (CHAR-1), ransom is remembered (CHAR-7), atonement is executed by another and received (CHAR-4, CHAR-5), healing is received through faith (CHAR-3), crisis-cry is the inner being's most reduced available act (CHAR-2). None of these require or produce creative origination. CHAR-6's exception proves the rule: imagination is recruited specifically because the characteristic's object — eschatological salvation not yet visible — cannot be reached by perception, memory, or received proclamation. The soul that waits for an invisible future must figure it imaginatively or lose its grip on it. The diversity of imagery generated (city walls, divine warrior's helmet, blazing torch, chariot of battle) reveals that the invisible horizon is too large for any single creative figure — it demands continuously renewed imaginative work. This makes CHAR-6 the cluster's only intrinsically creative characteristic by structural necessity, not by preference. The wider cluster finding is that salvation, as a cluster, is more fundamentally a posture of reception than of origination — the inner being is constituted by what it receives and holds, not by what it creates.

**T3.6.3 — Volition — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with volition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The universal volitional engagement across all seven characteristics — with no bypassing or impairing evidenced anywhere — reveals that the M38 salvation cluster is constitutively volitional: none of its modes operates without the will's participation, even when that participation is radically reduced (CHAR-2's single cry-act) or constrained within prescription (CHAR-5's obedient compliance). The most revealing cross-cluster contrast is between CHAR-2's volitional compression (mortal threat reduces volition to its irreducible minimum, revealing what the will does when stripped of all other options) and CHAR-3's volitional activation (faith-seeking presses through opposition, revealing the will as the characteristic's primary motor). Together, these establish the volitional range within the cluster: from the cry as the sole available volitional act to the determined press-through as the most volitionally robust single act. The cluster's theological centre-of-gravity on volition is the consistent finding that human volition is engaged without being causally sufficient: CHAR-4 is most explicit (volition required in approach, but atonement itself is received), CHAR-1 is most theologically sustained (the will turns, calls, confesses, endures — but within divine enabling that makes those acts possible). CHAR-6's volitional discipline (choosing to wait, to align morally, to approach God persistently across time) reveals that the posture of hope is not a feeling that arrives but a discipline that must be repeatedly chosen. The cluster confirms that salvation is for willing persons, not despite them, while the will itself is moved by divine initiative.

**T3.7.3 — Agency — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with agency reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The cluster's agency pattern reveals a single defining structural principle operating across all seven characteristics: divine agency is causally originating and sufficient; human agency is genuinely real but constitutively derivative. This asymmetry is not a marginal theological gloss but the structural feature that organises the entire cluster's agency evidence. The most explicit instance is CHAR-6 (human effort at self-rescue produces nothing; God saves with certainty), but the same structure underlies CHAR-1 (God sends, saves, exalts; humans call and confess), CHAR-4 (God provides the blood; humans bring sacrifice to activate the reception channel), and CHAR-5 (the priest enacts what God prescribed; the community receives what the priest enacts). CHAR-3 is the cluster's most distinctive agency instance: Jesus's attribution formula elevates human agency to named secondary causation, constituting the person as a genuine collaborative participant in divinely-effected salvation without making their agency causally primary. CHAR-2 reveals what happens to agency at its creaturely limit: mortal threat strips all human agency except the cry, and the cry is itself a relational act directed to divine agency as the only remaining resource. CHAR-7 generates agency as the characteristic's overflow — the ransomed identity produces intercession, ethical action, and communal solidarity as its consistent agentic expressions. The cluster reveals that salvation is not achieved by human agency or passively imposed without it, but received through genuine responsive participation within a divinely-initiated movement.

**T3.8.3 — Moral Evaluation — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with moral evaluation reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The consistent engagement of moral evaluation across all seven characteristics reveals that M38's salvation cluster is morally serious in its entire structure — it does not bracket ethics or render moral evaluation irrelevant to its operation. However, the cross-cluster pattern reveals that the characteristics engage moral evaluation in two fundamentally different modes. The diagnostic-categorical mode (CHAR-4, CHAR-5) applies a pre-given moral taxonomy (sin/guilt/uncleanness vs. covered/forgiven) without generating new moral understanding. The deliberative-normative mode (CHAR-1, CHAR-6, CHAR-7) requires the inner being to form moral judgments, assess authenticity, evaluate culpability, and calibrate conduct against eschatological standards. The cluster's most theologically significant finding on moral evaluation is the cross-cluster standard-shift: CHAR-1 establishes that salvation creates a new moral evaluative framework (the cross as God's power vs. folly), CHAR-7 calibrates moral evaluation against received grace (making betrayal specifically aggravated), and CHAR-6 makes eschatological anticipation morally motivating rather than neutralising. Together, these reveal that the salvation cluster does not operate within a static moral framework but transforms the very standard by which the inner being evaluates truth, action, and accountability. The characteristics do not merely apply moral evaluation; they recalibrate it by making salvific grace the highest evaluative reference point.

**T3.9.3 — Conscience — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscience reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The comparative conscience pattern reveals the deepest structural boundary within the cluster. CHAR-4 and CHAR-5 are conscience-constituted: they exist as the divinely-provided mechanism for the conscience-weight that accumulates before a holy God, and their entire structure is organised around guilt registered, guilt addressed, and conscience cleared. CHAR-1 and CHAR-3 engage conscience in a sequential two-phase pattern: activation (conscience awakened to sin, urgently seeking rescue) followed by cleansing (conscience cleared as the characteristic's inner terminus). This sequential pattern reveals that conscience functions as both the threshold-faculty (what must be activated before the salvific turn) and the terminus-faculty (what must be addressed for the characteristic to reach its inner-being completion). CHAR-7's dual conscience function — sensitising (making betrayal more weighty) and resolving (promising total ransom from iniquity) — is unique in the cluster. CHAR-6 engages conscience in background mode, neither as its generative centre nor as its direct object, but as the awareness-context that gives the hope-posture its moral gravity. CHAR-2's complete and consistent silence is the cluster's most definitionally significant conscience finding: it marks the phenomenological boundary between guilt-driven spiritual states (CHAR-1, CHAR-4) and mortal-danger crisis (CHAR-2), confirming that the fear-faith dynamic and the conscience-guilt dynamic, though both interior, are phenomenologically distinct. The cluster as a whole reveals that conscience is the inner being's moral self-witness that salvation must both activate and ultimately resolve.

**T3.10.3 — Conscientiousness — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscientiousness reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

CHAR-3's completing instance (the returning leper vs. the nine) is the cluster's most pedagogically explicit conscientiousness finding: Jesus himself marks the difference between wholeness received and wholeness completed through integrated response, suggesting that the sōzō-event reaches its fullest expression where conscientiousness closes the circuit. The cluster-level finding is that conscientiousness is salvation's behavioral expression over time — the outer shape of an inner life genuinely oriented toward God and transformed by the salvific realities the cluster embodies.

**T3.11.3 — Relational Capacity — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with relational capacity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The universal and consistent engagement of relational capacity across all seven characteristics — with deepening evidenced in every case and bypassing or impairing evidenced nowhere — reveals the cluster's most fundamental structural principle: salvation, in every mode evidenced in M38, is irreducibly relational in its constitution. It does not occur in isolation, cannot be self-generated, and does not produce isolation as its outcome. The cluster reveals that relational capacity is the faculty that both enables the salvific movement (the person's constitutional equipment for genuine connection with God is what makes receiving salvation possible) and is transformed by it (connection deepened, enmity resolved, standing restored, solidarity generated). Three relational architectures appear. The bilateral (human-divine) architecture is the cluster's most universal form, present in every characteristic. The triadic (mediator-God-people) architecture in CHAR-5 reveals that some relational breaches are too severe for direct restoration and require a relationally-qualified intermediary. The horizontal (human-human) architecture in CHAR-1 and CHAR-7 reveals that vertical relational restoration consistently generates and motivates horizontal relational capacity — the reconciled person becomes relationally equipped for just and compassionate engagement with others. The cluster's defining theological claim, surfacing from the comparison, is that salvation is not primarily an inner-being state that happens to have relational implications — it is a relational event from which inner-being transformation flows.
