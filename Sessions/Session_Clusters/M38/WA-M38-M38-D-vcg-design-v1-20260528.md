## VCGs

### M38-D-VCG-01

- Verse count: 11
- Anchor vc_id: 29586

**Context description:** This VCG names the core inner-being movement of the person who receives priestly atonement and is declared forgiven — guilt is objectively removed, conscience is cleared, and the person stands before God in restored standing. The mechanism is sacrificial mediation: a specified offering, priest-enacted, results in the explicit pronouncement of forgiveness. The inner-being register is sin-burdened moving to forgiven-and-at-peace, with the conscience shift being the definitive marker. These verses share the direct outcome-structure: sin presented, atonement made, forgiveness granted — the tightest functional cluster in the sub-group.

### M38-D-VCG-02

- Verse count: 3
- Anchor vc_id: 29585

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon where atonement is structurally grounded in substitution — life given for life, blood offered for soul, the offerer's identity transferred to the sacrifice through ritual act. The worshipper's inner being is covered not by moral improvement but by the substituted life of the animal accepted in their place. These verses share the theological axis of substitutionary mechanism as the ground of the covering received: Lev 1:4 (hand-laying as identification), Lev 17:11 (blood as life-for-life), and the priestly ordination meal of Exo 29:33 (consecration sealed through atoned status). The inner-being state produced is 'accepted through another's life,' which is distinct from the simple forgiveness-outcome cluster.

**Member vc_ids:** [29594, 29585, 29591]

### M38-D-VCG-03

- Verse count: 5
- Anchor vc_id: 29592

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of atonement as ransom, remembrance, and sustained relational coverage — where the covering is not tied to a specific sin-event but to the ongoing status of a life held before God. The equal-ransom motif (Exo 30:15–16) establishes that every soul requires identical covering regardless of status, and that the atonement act keeps the person in divine remembrance and protection. The warriors' voluntary gold offering (Num 31:50) and Hezekiah's royal intercession for sincere but ritually irregular seekers (2Ch 30:18) and David's guilt-seeking-resolution inquiry (2Sa 21:3) share the register of atonement as an ongoing relational posture rather than a one-time sin-specific transaction. The inner-being movement is toward sustained acknowledged standing before God.

**Member vc_ids:** [29592, 29593, 29665, 29663, 29660]

### M38-D-VCG-04

- Verse count: 7
- Anchor vc_id: 29068

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of God himself acting as the source and agent of atonement — where the covering comes from divine initiative, compassion, or love rather than from human ritual offering. The psalmist is relieved of sin that overwhelms because God covers it (Psa 65:3); God repeatedly atones for Israel's iniquity out of compassion and restrains wrath (Psa 78:38); the community pleads for atonement for his name's sake (Psa 79:9); Isaiah's lips are cleansed by the divine coal-touch (Isa 6:7); atonement through steadfast love and faithfulness (Pro 16:6); the New Covenant promise that God will be hileōs toward iniquities and remember sins no more (Heb 8:12); and God's prior love sending his Son as hilasmos (1Jo 4:10). The unifying inner-being register is atonement received as pure gift from divine initiative, producing conscience-cleared relief that the person could not achieve by their own action.

**Member vc_ids:** [29655, 29587, 29656, 29649, 29651, 29071, 29068]

### M38-D-VCG-05

- Verse count: 4
- Anchor vc_id: 29069

**Context description:** This VCG names the NT propitiation cluster — where Christ is the wrath-averting, sin-covering provision received by faith, making atonement universally and definitively available. These verses share the Greek hilaskomai/hilasmos/hilastērios register and the Christ-as-mechanism axis: Rom 3:25 (Christ set forth as hilastērios through his blood, received by faith); Heb 2:17 (Christ as merciful high priest making propitiation through solidarity); 1Jo 2:2 (Christ as hilasmos for the whole world); and Luk 18:13 (the tax collector's cry for propitiatory mercy — the inner-being posture of crushed, sin-aware dependence on divine mercy). The inner-being register across these verses is the conscience-cleared, wrath-averted state produced by Christ's once-for-all mediatorial act, received through faith and contrition rather than repeated ritual.

**Member vc_ids:** [29069, 29066, 29067, 29065]

### M38-D-VCG-06

- Verse count: 5
- Anchor vc_id: 29652

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of atonement withheld, unavailable, or permanently refused — where guilt remains unresolved and the inner person faces judgment unrelieved. The atonement-pathway is closed: by persistent priestly sin (1Sa 3:14), by God's judicial declaration (Isa 22:14), by the defiant city's helplessness before disaster (Isa 47:11), by the prophet's inverse intercession calling for enemies' guilt not to be covered (Jer 18:23), and by the uncoverable blood-guilt of murder (Num 35:33). The inner-being register is the weight of permanent, unresolved guilt — the mirror image of conscience-cleared atonement received, where instead the conscience remains condemned and wrath is not averted. This negative pole is structurally essential to the sub-group's meaning.

### M38-D-VCG-07

- Verse count: 4
- Anchor vc_id: 29588

**Context description:** This VCG names the inner-being phenomenon of atonement producing shame, contrition, corporate guilt-bearing, or eschatological transformation — where the receipt of covering generates a secondary inner-being movement beyond simple forgiveness. Eze 16:63 uniquely depicts humbled, wordless shame as the response to being atoned-for, where remembered guilt and forgiveness coexist. Deu 21:8 shows corporate conscience-clearing through intercessory prayer for blood guilt. Isa 27:9 links atonement to genuine inner and cultic turning from idolatry. Dan 9:24 frames atonement as an eschatological, once-for-all cosmic act producing everlasting righteousness. These verses share the register of atonement received as transformative event with lasting inner-being consequences — shame, corporate identity, moral turning, or cosmic culmination — rather than the immediate individual forgiveness-outcome.
