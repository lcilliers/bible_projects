## §3. Sample meanings (2 per term)

### G1431 dōrea — free gift

- **Joh 4:10**
  - meaning: Jesus speaks of the gift of God as living water — dōrea here names an unrecognized divine gift whose reception requires recognizing the giver; the inner-being state is potential transformation from spiritual thirst to living sustenance.
  - keywords: ['gift divine', 'life-giving', 'thirst spiritual', 'recognition needed', 'god-saving']
- **Act 2:38**
  - meaning: The gift of the Holy Spirit is promised to all who repent and are baptized — dōrea names the Spirit as the salvation-bearing divine gift given upon repentance, linking the inner turning of will with the reception of divine presence.
  - keywords: ['gift divine', 'spirit received', 'will turning', 'forgiven', 'salvation present']

### G1434 dōrēma — free gift

- **Rom 5:16**
  - meaning: Unlike the single trespass that brought condemnation, the free gift following many trespasses brings justification — the inner-being state moves from guilt-accumulated to justified-by-gift, with the gift's magnitude matching the extent of sin.
  - keywords: ['justification received', 'gift reversing', 'condemnation overcome', 'guilt covered', 'sin multiple', 'grace exceeding']

### G2433 hilaskomai — to propitiate

- **Luk 18:13**
  - meaning: The tax collector's prayer 'be merciful to me, a sinner' — hilaskomai here is a plea for propitiatory mercy from one who stands at distance, confessing unworthiness; the inner being is crushed, sin-aware, and wholly dependent on divine mercy.
  - keywords: ['propitiated', 'wrath facing', 'sin-laden', 'contrition', 'mercy sought', 'conscience struck', 'god-saving']
- **Heb 2:17**
  - meaning: Christ became fully human to serve as a merciful high priest and make propitiation for the people's sins; the inner being's guilt is addressed by a sympathetic mediator whose solidarity enables effective atonement.
  - keywords: ['propitiated', 'atoned', 'mediator interceding', 'guilt covered', 'mercy received']

### G2434 hilasmos — propitiation

- **1Jo 2:2**
  - meaning: Christ himself is the propitiation — the wrath-averting, sin-covering provision — not merely for a limited group but for the whole world, making atonement universally available to the sin-laden.
  - keywords: ['propitiated', 'wrath averted', 'atoned', 'sin covered', 'universal scope', 'christ saving']
- **1Jo 4:10**
  - meaning: God's prior love, not human initiative, is the source of propitiation; he sent his Son as the sin-covering sacrifice, so the inner-being receives atonement as pure gift from divine love.
  - keywords: ['propitiated', 'wrath averted', 'love divine', 'atoned', 'gift received', 'god saving', 'sin covered']

### G2435 hilastērios — propitiation

- **Rom 3:25**
  - meaning: Christ is set forth by God as the propitiation through his blood, received by faith — the inner-being effect is that wrath is averted and former sins are passed over, establishing the foundational mechanism of atonement.
  - keywords: ['propitiated', 'wrath averted', 'blood atonement', 'faith exercised', 'forgiven', 'christ-saving']

### G2436 hileōs — propitious/gracious

- **Heb 8:12**
  - meaning: God promises to be merciful toward iniquities and to remember sins no more; the inner being is freed from the burden of remembered guilt — propitiation produces total forgiveness and conscience cleared.
  - keywords: ['forgiven', 'wrath averted', 'guilt released', 'conscience cleansed', 'mercy received']

### G4982 sōzō — to save

- **Mat 1:21**
  - meaning: Jesus is named and defined by his mission: to save his people from their sins — the salvation is eschatological/spiritual rescue, targeting the inner person's bondage to sin as the structural opposite.
  - keywords: ['salvation eschatological', 'sin removed', 'rescue spiritual', 'christ saving', 'people delivered', 'name defined']
- **Mat 8:25**
  - meaning: The disciples cry 'Save us' in mortal danger of drowning — this is physical rescue from perishing, with the inner being gripped by terror and crying out in urgent, desperate faith.
  - keywords: ['rescue physical', 'perishing', 'fear', 'cry urgent', 'faith exercised', 'endangered']

### G4990 sōtēr — savior

- **Luk 1:47**

- **Luk 2:11**
  - meaning: The angelic birth announcement names the newborn as Savior, Christ, and Lord — sōtēr identifies the person of Jesus as the singular source of salvation, grounding hope in a historical event with eschatological scope.
  - keywords: ['christ-saving', 'savior identified', 'hope grounded', 'salvation announced', 'god-saving']
