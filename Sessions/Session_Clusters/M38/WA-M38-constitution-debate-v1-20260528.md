## Cluster-level summary

Of the 13 terms, 11 receive STAYS verdicts and 2 receive BOUNDARY verdicts. No terms are transferred out. The cluster as constituted is broadly coherent around the characteristic of divine redemptive action received by the human person — salvation, atonement, and gift-in-salvation-context all share the structural feature of God acting to rescue/cover/give, and the human inner being standing at the receiving end (guilt resolved, wrath averted, soul rescued, gratitude arising). The three semantic registers are genuinely inter-dependent: atonement is the mechanism that grounds eschatological rescue; gift-language names the mode of reception; salvation-language names the outcome. They cannot be cleanly separated at the verse level without fracturing the theological unity the corpus evidences. Two genuine BOUNDARY cases emerge: (1) G4982 sōzō warrants BOUNDARY on homonymic/polysemic grounds — its physical-rescue sense (27 verses: drowning, illness, mortal danger) is functionally distinct from its eschatological sense, and the physical verses' inner-being content (fear, urgent physical faith-cry) may warrant sense-split treatment or sub-group-by-sense design, though no verse is entirely disconnected from M38's characteristic; (2) H3722B ka.phar warrants BOUNDARY on supportive/qualifying grounds — its 77-verse corpus is dominated by priestly-mediation machinery (priest mediating appears in the majority of verses) which is a relational-act register rather than an inner-being state at the receiving end; the priestly-mediation axis is so pervasive that the term may function as a qualifying/instrumental register rather than the primary characteristic itself, though its atonement outcomes (conscience cleansed, guilt removed, forgiven) do map to M38. The analyst notes that the 'priest mediating' sub-axis in ka.phar is the cluster's most significant structural tension: it describes the agent and mechanism of atonement rather than the inner-being state of the atoned-for person, and researcher decision is needed on whether this instrumentality belongs in M38 or in a ministry/mediation cluster. The gift terms (G1431, G1434) are thin in verse count but their meanings consistently narrate the reception of salvation-relevant divine gift; they STAY with a cross-register flag toward M39 Blessing. All salvation-register terms (G4990, G4991, G4992, H3444, H6299) STAY with various cross-register adjacencies noted.

### G4982 sōzō — to save (Greek)

**Verdict:** BOUNDARY
**BOUNDARY reason:** homonymic_polysemic

**Rationale:** The 93-verse corpus divides with textual clarity into at least three discriminable senses that do not share the same inner-being content: (1) eschatological/spiritual salvation (e.g., Mat 1:21: 'save his people from their sins — spiritual rescue targeting the inner person's bondage to sin'; Luk 7:50: 'your faith has saved you — spiritual/eschatological salvation through faith, including forgiveness, with peace as the inner-being state'; Mar 16:16: 'belief and baptism yield eschatological salvation; unbelief yields condemnation'); (2) physical rescue from mortal danger (Mat 8:25: 'Save us in mortal danger of drowning — physical rescue, with the inner being gripped by terror and crying out in urgent faith'; Mat 14:30: 'Peter sinking cries Lord save me — physical rescue, inner being moving from bold venture to panic'; Act 27:20: 'all hope of physical rescue was abandoned — sōzō in its physical/temporal rescue sense, inner state being despair and lost hope'); (3) healing with spiritual overtone (Mat 9:22: 'your faith has made you well — healing with spiritual resonance, with faith as the inner-being faculty that activates the saving act'; Mar 5:34: 'bridges physical restoration and spiritual wholeness, with faith named as the inner faculty'). The physical-rescue sense (sense 2) is functionally distinct — its inner-being content (terror, panic, despair, urgent physical faith-cry) is not the inner-being state of 'being saved from sin/judgment' but the state of being rescued from drowning or storm. While all verses have some relationship with M38 (preventing clean transfer), the polysemic spread is substantial enough that sense-split treatment is needed. Researcher decision required on whether physical-rescue verses remain in M38 as a sub-group or are routed to a physical-danger/deliverance register.

**Evidence verses cited:**

- Mat 1:21 — Jesus named and defined by his mission: to save his people from their sins — the salvation is eschatological/spiritual rescue targeting the inner person's bondage to sin
- Mat 8:25 — physical rescue from perishing, with the inner being gripped by terror and crying out in urgent, desperate faith — not the eschatological inner state
- Luk 7:50 — sōzō is spiritual/eschatological salvation through faith, including forgiveness, with peace as the inner-being state conferred upon one previously burdened by sin
- Act 27:20 — sōzō in its physical/temporal rescue sense, with the inner state being one of despair and lost hope — no eschatological content present in the verse

### H3722B ka.phar — to cover (Hebrew)

**Verdict:** BOUNDARY
**BOUNDARY reason:** supportive_qualifying

**Rationale:** The 77-verse corpus divides into two distinct registers within the atonement domain: (a) the inner-being state of the atoned-for person (conscience cleansed, guilt removed, forgiven — e.g., Lev 4:20: 'priestly atonement results in the definitive inner-being outcome of forgiveness — guilt is objectively removed and the conscience cleared'; Lev 16:30: 'the Day of Atonement effects comprehensive cleansing of all sins — the inner being is unburdened from the full year's accumulated sin-guilt'; Isa 6:7: 'the burning coal's touch enacts atonement — guilt is removed and sin covered, cleansing the conscience so the prophet stands before God without condemnation'); and (b) the priestly-mediation machinery (the priest's acts, ordination, ritual sequence, Day of Atonement mechanics — e.g., Lev 9:7: 'Aaron commanded to offer atonement for himself first and then for the people — establishing that an uncovered mediator cannot cover others'; Lev 16:17: 'the high priest enters the Holy Place alone to make atonement for himself, his house, and all Israel — his solitary mediation carries the weight of the entire community's standing'; Num 16:47: 'Aaron's swift priestly action with incense makes atonement and arrests the plague — mediated covering'). Register (a) maps cleanly to M38's characteristic. Register (b) describes the instrumental agent and mechanism — the priestly act itself — which is a qualifying/enabling process rather than the inner-being state at the receiving end. The 'priest mediating' keyword dominates approximately 30+ verses of the 77, creating a body of meaning that consistently describes what the priest does rather than what the atoned-for person experiences. This is a valid supportive/qualifying register concern per §6.1.4. Researcher decision is needed: sense-split treatment separating the atoned-for inner state (STAYS in M38) from the priestly-mediation instrumentality (possible route to a ministry/service cluster or sub-group boundary) is the most precise resolution, but this cannot be pre-resolved without researcher direction.

**Evidence verses cited:**

- Lev 4:20 — priestly atonement through the sin offering results in the definitive inner-being outcome of forgiveness — guilt is objectively removed and the conscience cleared before God
- Lev 16:17 — the high priest enters the Holy Place alone to make atonement for himself, his house, and all Israel — his solitary mediation carries the weight of the entire community's standing before God
- Lev 16:30 — the Day of Atonement effects comprehensive cleansing of all sins before the LORD — the inner-being outcome is total cleanness, the conscience unburdened from the full year's accumulated sin-guilt
- Num 16:47 — Aaron's swift priestly action with incense makes atonement and arrests the plague — the inner-being community standing between wrath and safety through mediated covering
