**T2.1.1 — Spirit-Level Location — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across seven characteristics, explicit spirit-level location is sparse but not absent: three characteristics produce one identified instance each (Char-1: 1 Cor 5:5; Char-6: Luk 1:47; Char-7: Psa 31:5), while four characteristics (Char-3, -4, -5, and partially -2) surface no explicit pneuma/rûaḥ location at all. Characteristics operating primarily through ritual mechanism (Char-5), conscience-clearing (Char-4), and faith-activated healing (Char-3) show no spirit-level signature at all, suggesting that procedural and mediating functions of salvation operate below the spirit register's threshold. The cluster as a whole reveals that spirit-level location in M38 is not a universal marker of salvific depth but appears selectively, at points where the self is either preserved at its irreducible minimum (Char-1), surrendered at its deepest center (Char-7), or flooded with joy at salvation's arrival (Char-6). Spirit-level engagement marks intensity and ultimacy, not ubiquity, across this cluster.

**T2.2.1 — Soul-Level Location — Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics register at the soul level, making soul-location the single universal constitutional marker across the entire M38 cluster. The evidence, however, reveals two distinct modes of soul-engagement. In Char-1, -3, -6, and -7 the soul is the receptive and experiential center: it receives salvation as its telos (Char-1: 1 Pe 1:9), forms interior faith (Char-3), sustains hope as a lived inner posture (Char-6), and is the primary object and subject of ransom (Char-7). In Char-2, -4, and -5 the soul appears as the endangered or guilt-burdened entity that requires external intervention: the soul in mortal distress crying out (Char-2), the soul targeted for atonement-covering (Char-4: Lev 17:11), and the community-soul carrying accumulated guilt needing annual discharge (Char-5). This bifurcation reveals a structural logic within the cluster: some characteristics address the soul as deficient and burdened, others engage it as the receiving and flourishing center of salvific action. The cluster's consistency at the soul level indicates that salvation in M38 is irreducibly personal and inner-being directed — no characteristic operates exclusively at external, ritual, or cognitive levels without also registering in the soul's own experience. Soul-level location is the constitutional floor of the entire cluster.

**T2.3.1 — Heart — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Heart-location is present across five of seven characteristics but follows a gradient of explicitness. Char-1 and Char-3 show the strongest and most explicit heart-location: saving faith is constitutively heart-located (Rom 10:9–10; kardia named directly), and healing-faith operates from a "heart trusting" interior conviction formed before any word is spoken. Char-6 and Char-7 show implicit but functionally substantiated heart-location: hope-sustained-waiting engages volitional, affective, and moral registers that are heart-functions even without the lexeme, and Deuteronomic memory-of-ransom operates through heart-willing and motivation. Char-4 surfaces indirect evidence — heart-directed-toward-God as the qualifying condition for atonement-received (2Ch 30:18) — placing the heart at the threshold rather than the center of the characteristic. Char-2 and Char-5 are explicitly silent: the fear-faith cry in mortal danger and the priestly atonement machinery both operate without any heart-location language across their full verse corpora. The cluster-level finding is that heart-location correlates with the degree to which a characteristic requires personal interior agency — believing, trusting, willing, remembering. Characteristics that are structurally received from outside (Char-5 priestly machinery) or primarily soul-wide in register (Char-2 mortal-danger cry) do not recruit the heart's integrating function.

The mind's comparative absence across Char-2, -3, -4, -5, and -7 signals that salvation in this cluster is pre-cognitive in its operative core: it does not require the mind to generate it, only to receive and hold it. Mind-location marks a secondary instrumental register, not a primary constitutional home for any characteristic in the cluster.

**T2.7.1 — Body — Direction — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?**

The cluster reveals that salvation in M38 is constitutively embodied in all three modes simultaneously, but no single direction governs the whole cluster. The mediating direction is unique to the atonement characteristics (Char-4, -5), suggesting that the incarnational structure of atonement requires a distinct body-logic not present in the other characteristics.

The cluster-level finding is that origination in M38 is structurally theocentric across all characteristics — even where human interior activity precedes the observable divine act (Char-3), the saving outcome is attributed entirely to God. The human inner-being contributes the mode of reception (faith, will-surrender, plea) but never the source. This asymmetry between divine origin and human reception mode is a defining structural feature of the cluster as a whole.

**T2.10.1 — Constitutional Movement — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Constitutional movement is evidenced across all seven characteristics, and the comparison reveals two dominant directional patterns with a third distinct pattern. The first and most common pattern is outside-in-then-out: divine external initiative enters the inner being (soul, spirit, conscience), transforms it, and then issues outward through body and ethical action (Char-1, Char-4, Char-5, Char-7). Char-6 shows a recursive rather than linear pattern: divine promise received as hope → sustained inner posture → expressed in body and affect → renewed by ongoing faith. The cluster-level observation is that constitutional movement in M38 salvation is never unidimensional: every characteristic crosses at least two constitutional levels, and several (Char-1, -3, -7) can be traced across three or four levels in sequence. The movement is consistently oriented toward increasing interiority and depth as its telos — the soul's reception, the conscience's clearing, the spirit's self-surrender — even when the initiating point is external and the concluding expression is bodily.
