### M38-B-VCG-01

- Verse count: 3
- Anchor vc_id: 9025

**Context description:** The desperate cry of the endangered self: verses in which an individual inner being lurches from ordinary existence or bold venture into acute mortal threat, and the only movement available is an urgent, panic-driven cry to a rescuing presence. The inner-being trajectory is terror → desperate faith-cry → rescue (or hoped-for rescue). The body is visibly imperilled — drowning, enemies closing in, physical dissolution — and the cry is the sole act of the creaturely will. These verses share the co-occurrence of 'endangered,' 'fear,' 'urgent cry,' and the movement of faith under duress, making them the purest register of the sub-group's hallmark phenomenon.

**Member vc_ids:** [9026, 9025, 13830]

### M38-B-VCG-02

- Verse count: 7
- Anchor vc_id: 9029

**Context description:** Crucifixion mockery and the irony of withheld self-rescue: verses in which sōzō is deployed sarcastically by observers to demand or expect that Jesus physically rescue himself from the cross, while the deeper irony is that his refusal to exercise this capacity is precisely what enacts salvation for others. The inner-being phenomenon is not faith or fear but the collision between creaturely logic (self-preservation) and redemptive surrender: the one who saved others will not save himself, and the taunts inadvertently testify to both dimensions. These verses share the keywords 'mockery,' 'irony salvation,' 'self withheld,' and 'rescue physical' as their distinguishing cluster.

**Member vc_ids:** [9002, 9027, 9028, 9029, 9030, 9031, 9032]

### M38-B-VCG-03

- Verse count: 2
- Anchor vc_id: 8990

**Context description:** The Son's own anguished petition: verses in which Jesus himself — as the one endangered by the appointed hour of suffering and death — engages in desperate, reverent prayer asking the Father to rescue him from physical death, yet submits that anguish to redemptive purpose. The inner being here is uniquely layered: genuine creaturely distress and anguished petition coexist with deep reverence and volitional surrender. These verses stand apart from both the disciples' panic-cry (VCG-01) and the mockery irony (VCG-02) because the subject is the Son himself, the petition is heard, and the resolution is not physical rescue but willed surrender.

**Member vc_ids:** [9033, 8990]

### M38-B-VCG-04

- Verse count: 2
- Anchor vc_id: 51563

**Context description:** Corporate and household physical deliverance through faith-action: verses in which the rescue from mortal physical threat is not a single individual's panic-cry but a corporate or familial deliverance, and the inner-being agency is not terror but active, reverent, obedient faith that produces embodied action over time. Noah builds the ark; the Benedictus announces rescue from enemies for a whole people. The inner-being quality distinguishing these verses is faith expressed through sustained obedience and anticipatory reverence, producing safety for those entrusted to one's care — a register entirely absent from the storm-cry or crucifixion-irony verses.

**Member vc_ids:** [51533, 51563]

### M38-B-VCG-05

- Verse count: 1
- Anchor vc_id: 9005

**Context description:** Abandoned hope and total despair in extremis: the single verse in which the inner-being state is not a cry or a surrender but the collapse of all expectation of rescue — hope itself is given up, and the inner state is one of pure despair and resignation to perishing. This is sōzō in its purely temporal-physical sense, but the phenomenological register is the inverse of the faith-cry: rather than crying out, the endangered inner being falls silent in hopelessness. This verse stands alone because no other verse in the sub-group captures the specific inner-being movement of hope's extinction as the primary phenomenon.
