**T1.1.1 — Name and Naming — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Across the seven names, a structural pattern emerges that is not visible within any single characteristic: every name encodes a directional vector. M38-A's "received by faith" points inward-from-above; M38-B's "from mortal danger" points outward-toward-safety; M38-C's "through faith exercised" points forward-through-instrument; M38-D's "through atonement received" points inward-from-mechanism; M38-E's "machinery of atonement" points outward-toward-others-and-God simultaneously; M38-F's "anticipated and hope sustained" points forward-toward-not-yet; M38-G's "ransomed identity gratitude and memory" points backward-into-past-constituting-present. The cluster therefore discloses salvation operating on a full temporal and directional compass: backward memory (G), present condition (D), present crisis (B, C), forward anticipation (F), and ultimate destiny (A), with a mediatorial axis (E) that spans all directions at once. A second convergence: six of seven names contain a passive or receptive element — "received," "rescued," "wholeness through," "cleansed through," "anticipated," "ransomed" — signaling that across M38, the initiating agency is consistently divine, not human. The single apparent exception, M38-E ("machinery"), names the human mediatorial apparatus, yet even this machinery exists because divine authorization constitutes it. M38-C's "exercised" is the strongest human-active term in any name, yet the name's structure still places "healing wholeness" prior to faith as the telos. A significant asymmetry surfaces between M38-A and M38-G: both name salvation as received, but M38-A names the mode of reception (faith) while M38-G names the permanent identity-effect of having been ransomed. Reception is punctiliar in A; identity-restructuring is ongoing in G. M38-B and M38-F share temporal urgency as a naming signal — "mortal danger" and "not yet received" both foreground extremity — while M38-D and M38-C share the conscience and wholeness register, making them the cluster's interior-transformation pair. M38-E stands structurally apart from all others: it alone names a vocation rather than an inner-being state, yet its presence in the cluster signals that salvation's inner-being reality requires institutional mediation as its scaffolding. The names together constitute a theology of salvation's full architecture: crisis, mediation, reception, memory, anticipation, and identity.

**T1.2.1 — Kind — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — When the seven findings are compared, the cluster reveals that no single ontological category — act, disposition, condition, quality — suffices for any one characteristic, and that the distribution of complexity differs markedly across them. M38-A is the most ontologically layered: act (reception), condition (being saved), disposition (endurance), and identity (the saved person) are all simultaneously operative. M38-D is closest to pure condition — a passively received state of cleared conscience — though an enabling act (contrition, self-presentation) is embedded within it. M38-G similarly names a condition (ransomed identity) that is strongly dispositional and contains memorial acts as constituent operations. M38-B and M38-C are most act-oriented but in different modes: M38-B is a crisis-activated transient condition requiring an act (the cry), while M38-C is a composite act (interior conviction + outward expression + reception event) that produces a new condition. M38-F is the most explicitly compound: disposition (settled orientation toward salvation) plus condition (the not-yet state imposed by temporal location) plus recurring volitional acts. M38-E stands apart categorically: it is a vocationally-constituted enacted complex, which the finding names as "neither simple act nor pure disposition" but an "enacted vocation with inner-being concomitants." This is the only characteristic in the cluster that is primarily constituted by what is done rather than by what is undergone or held. A critical asymmetry: M38-B and M38-C both engage the Hebrew/Greek root sōzō and both involve crisis, yet M38-B's primary phenomenological structure is affective-volitional (terror driving cry), while M38-C's is volitional-cognitive (formed conviction driving action). The sōzō semantic overlap between characteristics does not collapse them into the same ontological kind. The cluster finding is that M38 salvation operates simultaneously in all ontological registers — act, condition, disposition, quality, enacted vocation — and that the seven characteristics are not redundant but each occupies a distinct ontological position within the same semantic field.

**T1.3.1 — Boundary — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — the inner-being reality that stands against or excludes it?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Comparing the structural opposites across the seven characteristics reveals that M38 salvation is bounded by a small set of recurring anti-structures that appear in multiple characteristics simultaneously, but with different emphases. The first and most pervasive anti-structure is self-sufficiency: M38-A is opposed by self-generated works and self-preserving clinging; M38-B by self-sufficient non-cry; M38-C by fear as self-protective inward contraction; M38-F by self-reliant striving that produces nothing; M38-G by self-directed deception refusing ransom. Five of seven characteristics identify some form of self-sufficient inner posture as their structural opposite. The second recurring anti-structure is despair-as-closure: M38-B names extinct hope as the state in which the faith-cry ceases; M38-F names despair-collapse as the point where sustained hope dissolves; M38-A names perishing as the active alternative to salvation. The third is defiance or deliberate refusal: M38-D names permanent unresolved guilt through persistent, unacknowledged sin; M38-G names deceptive rebellion against offered ransom; M38-A names willful truth-refusal. A structural asymmetry appears between M38-E and all others: M38-E's structural opposite is uncovered self-assertion in the sacred domain and passive non-response to wrath — both of these are failures of the mediatorial vocation specifically, not failures of creaturely reception. This makes M38-E's boundary structurally different from the others: it is bounded not by the creature's failure to receive but by the mediator's failure to act. M38-C's primary structural opposite — fear — is distinctive in that fear is also the precipitating context for M38-B, which depends on terror to activate the faith-cry. Fear is the opposite of M38-C but the occasion of M38-B. This is the sharpest intra-cluster divergence among structural opposites: the same inner-being state (fear) activates one characteristic and opposes another.

**T1.4.1 — Modes of Operation — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven findings together reveal a cluster-wide pattern: every characteristic operates in multiple modes rather than one, but the modal architectures differ by constitutional type. M38-A evidences five modes (reception, awakening, transformation, endurance, anticipation of glory) arranged sequentially as a developmental arc. M38-F evidences five modes arranged not as sequence but as simultaneous layers of the same posture (declaration, arming, motivation, communal solidarity, endurance). M38-D evidences five modes that are structurally parallel to M38-E's five: both organize around a before-and-after logic of uncovered/covered, with M38-D focusing on the conscience of the recipient and M38-E on the enacted sequence of the mediator. This is the cluster's deepest convergence: M38-D and M38-E are phenomenologically paired, one describing what is received when atonement is made and the other describing what is enacted in making it. M38-B and M38-C share a crisis-displacement mode, but in M38-B the displacement is of hope (by despair) or of self-sufficiency (by cry), while in M38-C the displacement is of fear (by faith). The direction is different: M38-B moves from security to terror to cry; M38-C moves from fear to faith to wholeness. M38-G's six modes include one (intercessory mode) that also appears in M38-E (crisis-intercession), M38-D (prayer-as-atonement-seeking), and M38-F (corporate petition). Intercession thus surfaces as a cross-cluster modal bridge, present in at least four of seven characteristics as an operative mode. The significant modal divergence is between M38-E and all others: M38-E's modes are primarily enacted-sequential (prerequisite, solidarity-bearing, comprehensive maintenance, individual restoration, crisis-intercession), while all other characteristics have at least one mode that is interior-dispositional without requiring outward sequence. M38-E requires the external enacted structure; the others can operate in interior-only modes.

**T1.5.1 — Immediate Response — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The immediate responses across the seven characteristics converge on three dominant registers but diverge sharply in texture. The first convergence is peace: M38-A (Luk 7:50), M38-C (Mar 5:34), and M38-G (displacement of fear in Isa 12:2) all name peace or fear-displacement as the primary immediate inner-being state following reception. The second convergence is joy or exultation: M38-F (Luk 1:47) and M38-G (Isa 25:9; Psa 71:23) both evidence immediate affective eruption when salvation arrives. The third convergence is moral reorientation or activated will: M38-A (faith exercised as turning), M38-D (shame and contrition in Eze 16:63), M38-F (will aligned toward righteousness), and M38-G (gratitude generating obligation) all show the will or conscience moving immediately in response. However, M38-B is structurally distinct: its immediate response is the cry itself, which means the characteristic's immediate response is not to having received rescue but to the moment of crisis — the cry is both the exercise of the characteristic and the response to the threatening context. This makes M38-B the only characteristic in which the immediate response and the characteristic's operation are nearly identical rather than sequential. M38-E evidences a distinctive immediate response: weight-taking — the mediator's first movement upon beginning the atonement sequence is a solemn assumption of burden, not relief. This contrasts with every other characteristic in which the immediate response involves some form of relief, peace, joy, or turning. M38-E's immediate response is burden-assumption, not burden-release. The cluster finding is that immediate responses cluster by position relative to the transaction: recipients (A, C, D, F, G) tend toward peace, joy, or moral reorientation; the mediator (E) moves toward burden-bearing; and the crisis-actor (B) collapses the response into the act itself.

**T1.6.1 — Sustained Effect — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The sustained effects across the seven characteristics converge on four domains that span the full cluster. First, all characteristics that evidence sustained effect produce some form of stable identity or settled orientation as a long-term outcome: M38-A produces settled assurance; M38-B produces faith-heir identity (Heb 11:7); M38-C produces conscience-integrity; M38-D produces cleared-conscience relational access; M38-F produces settled security against cosmic dissolution; M38-G produces stable ransomed identity. The identity-formation function is the most consistent sustained effect across M38 as a cluster. Second, moral formation is evidenced in five of seven: M38-A (Tit 2:11), M38-D (Pro 16:6 — turning from evil), M38-E (habituated solidarity-consciousness), M38-F (righteous conduct from salvation's nearness), M38-G (ethical consistency toward the vulnerable). This confirms that M38 salvation's sustained effect is not merely psychological or positional but volitional-behavioral. Third, perseverance capacity appears in M38-A, M38-B (completed obedience), M38-C (following-orientation), and M38-F (endurance under affliction) — salvation sustained over time produces the capacity to continue rather than defect. An important divergence: M38-D uniquely produces sustained shame alongside freedom (Eze 16:63), which is the only case across the cluster where the sustained effect includes a negative affective state as an appropriate and permanent component of the inner-being landscape. All other characteristics' sustained effects are uniformly positive in valence (peace, joy, assurance, identity). M38-D alone names an enduring humility-under-guilt as a proper sustained state for the person who has received atonement. M38-E's sustained effect is distinctive in being mediator-directed: it forms the priest rather than the recipient, producing comprehensive attentiveness and crisis-readiness as ongoing vocational dispositions. No other characteristic produces its sustained effect primarily in the one who enacts rather than the one who receives.

**T1.7.1 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — When enabling conditions are compared across all seven characteristics, the cluster reveals a consistent core surrounded by characteristic-specific differentiations. The core shared condition is some form of acknowledged human incapacity combined with an orientation of will toward God as the source of rescue: M38-A names awakened conscience and receptive meekness; M38-B requires recognition of genuine helplessness; M38-C requires fear displaced and interior conviction formed; M38-D requires contrition and faith; M38-F requires acknowledged inability to self-rescue; M38-G requires a will turned toward the ransomer. Across six of seven characteristics, the fundamental enabling condition is the same: the inner being must know itself as unable to self-generate the salvation it needs, and must be oriented toward God rather than toward its own capacity. M38-E diverges here structurally: its enabling conditions are covered status, authorized identity, and solidarity-consciousness — conditions that presuppose vocation and appointment rather than personal need. The mediator does not need to feel helpless; the mediator needs to be properly prepared and genuinely burdened. This is the cluster's most significant enabling-condition asymmetry: the mediatorial characteristic enables through preparation-and-authorization, while all other characteristics enable through need-recognition-and-trust. A secondary convergence appears in M38-B, M38-C, and M38-G: all three explicitly require relational orientation toward a specific person (the rescuer, Christ, the ransomer) rather than merely a general orientation toward God. The conditions become more personally specific in these three than in M38-A and M38-F, where the enabling condition is more broadly faith or acknowledged dependence. M38-D adds a condition unique in the cluster: sincere heart-seeking even amid ritual impurity is sufficient for atonement (2Ch 30:18) — suggesting that the inner sincerity can override procedural deficiency, a principle not evidenced in any other characteristic's enabling conditions.

**T1.8.1 — Dimension Classification — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The primary dimension classifications across the seven characteristics reveal both convergence and meaningful partition. Three characteristics claim the volitional as primary: M38-A (will/volitional, with faith-faculty as specific operation), M38-C (volitional-faith), and M38-E (volitional-executive, enacted and ordered will). M38-F claims volitional-affective as primary with volitional dominant. M38-G claims volitional-memorial as primary. M38-B claims affective-volitional as primary with affective element providing the occasion and the volitional element providing the act. M38-D alone claims the moral-relational conscience dimension as primary. The cluster therefore shows a strong bias toward the volitional dimension as primary across all seven, but with four distinct modifiers: faith-directed (A, C), executive-enacted (E), memorial (G), and affective-charged (B, F). The single outlier is M38-D, whose primary classification is the conscience — not the will, not the affect, but the moral-relational faculty of standing-before-God-awareness. This is structurally significant: M38-D is the characteristic in which something is primarily done to the inner person (the conscience is cleansed by external act) rather than primarily operated by the inner person (faith is exercised, will turns, mediator enacts). The classification reflects the passivity of reception that the characteristic's name signals. A further asymmetry: M38-E's volitional-executive and M38-B's affective-volitional are the most body-engaged dimensions in the cluster — M38-E because the enactment is bodily and sequential, M38-B because the crisis is physical and the cry is voiced. The more eschatological and dispositional characteristics (A, F, G) are less body-engaged and more interior-consciousness oriented. The cluster's dimensional map thus shows salvation operating from the body outward (B, E) through the conscience (D) and the volitional-faith act (A, C) to the memorial-volitional complex of identity (G) and the forward-oriented volitional-affective hope (F).

**T1.1.2 — Name and Naming — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — When the primary lexical anchors of all seven characteristics are laid side by side, several structural observations emerge that no single characteristic's finding can surface. First, sōzō (G4982) and its cognates (sōtēr, sōtēria) function as shared definitional root across M38-A, M38-B, M38-C, and M38-F, while Hebrew yeshuah (H3444) anchors M38-F's OT register. This shared root creates genuine semantic density: the same Greek verb is claimed by four characteristics as definitionally central, which means the programme's sub-group distinctions are not lexically separate but are distinctions of usage, context, and semantic emphasis within a single root. The cluster does not consist of seven different words for salvation but of seven different configurations of the same salvific vocabulary. Second, ka.phar (H3722) anchors both M38-D and M38-E — M38-D as ka.phar received (the atoned-state), M38-E as ka.phar enacted (Register B). The single Hebrew root divides into two sub-group definitions based on the direction of the action (toward the recipient's conscience vs. in the hands of the mediator). This is the cluster's most explicit lexical bifurcation: one root, one boundary, two characteristics. Third, pa.dah (H6299) anchors M38-G and also appears in M38-B (Psa 69:18), creating a lexical bridge between ransomed-identity memory and physical-crisis rescue. Pa.dah's transactional-release semantic (ransom by price) colours both: physical rescue in crisis and identity constituted by ransom share the same underlying transfer-logic. Fourth, the Greek hilasmos/hilaskomai/hilastērios group (M38-D) is unique to that characteristic across the cluster and carries the most specifically theological semantic weight — propitiation, standing-shifted, obstacle-removed. No other characteristic's primary terms carry this relational-positional specificity at the definitional level. The lexical map of M38 thus shows: a shared sōzō core (A, B, C, F), a bifurcated ka.phar axis (D, E), a pa.dah bridge (B, G), and a hilasmos uniqueness (D).

**T1.2.2 — Kind — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Every one of the seven characteristics presents as composite rather than simple at first encounter — this is a cluster-wide finding of structural complexity. However, the nature and internal logic of the complexity differs significantly across characteristics, and this variation is itself informative. M38-A's complexity is sequential and developmental: its constituents (divine initiative, human reception, resulting state, ongoing process, eschatological hope) unfold in temporal order and require each other causally. M38-D's complexity is layered rather than sequential: conscience, cleansing, and atonement are the three named constituents, with faith and substitutionary identification as further embedded elements — all present simultaneously rather than serially. M38-E's complexity is procedural and institutional: its five constituent elements are ordered steps in a repeated enactment rather than aspects of a single inner state. M38-G's six constituent elements (identity, memorial retention, gratitude, trust, intercessory appeal, hope) are the richest inventory in the cluster and operate across the most distinct faculties simultaneously. The comparison reveals that M38-A, M38-B, and M38-F share a temporal-sequential structure: each has a before (danger, not-yet, sin-laden), a during (faith-act, anticipation, suffering), and an after (rescue, received salvation, final glory). M38-C, M38-D, and M38-G share a more simultaneous structure: their constituents are co-present facets of a single inner state rather than stages in a process. M38-E alone has a procedural-sequential structure that is externally ordered rather than internally generated. The cluster finding is that M38 salvation is structurally complex throughout, but the organizing logic of complexity is temporal-sequential in crisis and hope characteristics (B, A, F), simultaneous-faceted in condition and identity characteristics (C, D, G), and externally-procedural in the mediatorial characteristic (E).

**T1.3.2 — Boundary — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The exclusions across the seven characteristics form a coherent negative map of M38 salvation's architecture. The most widely shared exclusion is works-righteousness or self-generation of the salvation state: M38-A explicitly excludes self-generated works (Eph 2:8, Tit 3:5), M38-C resists external-only or ritual-only cleansing, M38-D excludes self-generated moral improvement as atonement substitute, M38-F resists self-reliant striving toward deliverance, M38-G resists self-sufficiency as the basis of identity. Five characteristics converge in excluding the self as the generative source of salvation. This exclusion is the cluster's most consistent boundary and represents the defining structural contrast between M38 salvation and every alternative form of inner-being orientation. A second widely shared exclusion is ongoing self-condemnation after atonement received: M38-D's Heb 8:12 citation explicitly resists persistent self-accusation, and M38-A's assurance texts similarly exclude doubt-as-default for the saved inner being. However, this sits in productive tension with M38-D's other boundary: Eze 16:63 names enduring shame as an appropriate sustained effect — suggesting that the boundary against self-condemnation is specifically against self-condemnation as a substitute for atonement received, not against all memory of guilt. M38-E's exclusions are categorically different from all others: it excludes uncovered mediation, partial coverage, economic exclusion of the poor, and passive non-response to wrath. These are all failures of the mediatorial function rather than failures of the recipient's inner posture. M38-B's exclusions (faithless demand, despairing closure, self-sufficient mockery) are all failures of the crisis-cry mode. The cluster thus shows that the boundary of each characteristic is defined by the failure mode specific to its structural position: recipients fail by self-sufficiency or despair, mediators fail by uncovered presumption or passivity.

**T1.4.2 — Modes of Operation — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics evidence contextual and directional variation in their mode of operation, but the axes of variation differ by characteristic in ways that reveal the cluster's structural logic. The individual-corporate axis is shared across the most characteristics: M38-A operates self-directed or missionally-outward; M38-B operates individually or for household; M38-C operates self-directed or intercessorily; M38-D operates for individual or corporate assembly; M38-E operates for individual restoration or full community coverage; M38-F operates personally or communally; M38-G operates personally or as covenantal-corporate identity. The individual-corporate variation is thus genuinely pan-cluster — every characteristic of M38 can operate in both registers, and the distinction between personal and communal salvation is not restricted to any single characteristic. The temporal axis also appears across multiple characteristics: M38-A distinguishes initial reception from sustained possession; M38-B distinguishes acute crisis from extended obedient response; M38-C distinguishes crisis-intensity from composure-level; M38-F distinguishes present rescue from eschatological completion. The present-eschatological tension is a recurring axis of variation. The unique axis in M38-E is the directional Godward/humanward split: the mediatorial characteristic operates simultaneously in two directions that no other characteristic shares — toward God (propitiation) and toward the community (solidarity-bearing). All other characteristics operate in one primary direction even when they have self-other variation. M38-D's unique axis is human-initiated versus divine-initiated: in early VCGs the mechanism flows human-to-God through the priest; in later VCGs it reverses and God acts unilaterally. This reversal — from human-initiated mechanism to divine-initiated covenant action — is unique to M38-D and anticipates the NT christological fulfillment without requiring it structurally.

**T1.5.2 — Immediate Response — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The immediate response varies within every characteristic — not one of the seven findings claims a single consistent immediate response across all its verse evidence. But the pattern of variation is itself revealing when compared across the cluster. M38-A's variation tracks with the existential register of the encounter (quiet fulfillment, urgent alarm, joyful recognition, astonishment) — different emotional textures of the same fundamental turn toward Christ. M38-B's variation tracks with whether rescue is being received, denied, or mocked — the same sōzō-language appears in entirely different phenomenological registers (terror-cry, mockery-taunt) that produce entirely different immediate responses. M38-C's variation is structured: peace where healing is the central gift (haemorrhage narrative), following-movement where sight is (Bartimaeus), conscience-cleared where moral restoration is primary. The immediate response in M38-C correlates with the specific dimension of wholeness received. M38-D's variation is the most instructive in the cluster: "forgiven" (spare, positional) contrasts with shame-and-silenced-contrition (Eze 16:63), vocation-readiness (Isa 6:7), and contrition-before-declaration (Luk 18:13) — showing that the same atonement-received event produces markedly different immediate inner-being expressions depending on the person's moral-spiritual context. M38-F's variation tracks with the relationship between the soul and the salvation being encountered (exultation when personally received, mourning when absent, sobriety when promised). M38-G's variation maps onto distinct temporal-contextual registers (joy at arrival, motivational gratitude in retrospect, intercessory appeal in urgency, deepened trust in experience). The cluster finding is that immediate-response variation is systematic and meaningful rather than arbitrary: the variation tracks the position of the encountering soul relative to the salvific event — receiving, remembering, awaiting, or witnessing its denial — and each position generates a distinct but coherent immediate response.

**T1.6.2 — Sustained Effect — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Comparing sustained effects across the seven characteristics reveals that M38 salvation as a cluster produces four consistent long-term inner-being outcomes regardless of which specific characteristic is in view. First, identity-stabilization: M38-A produces settled eschatological security, M38-B produces faith-heir identity, M38-C produces conscience-integrity as new constitution, M38-D produces cleared relational access to God, M38-F produces identity as heir of salvation, M38-G produces stable ransomed selfhood. The formation of a stable identity grounded in God's salvific act is the most universal sustained effect across M38. Second, moral-volitional reorientation: all seven characteristics establish some form of changed will-direction — toward perseverance (A), toward deepened dependence (B), toward continued following (C), toward evil-avoidance (D), toward comprehensive mediatorial attentiveness (E), toward righteous conduct (F), toward ethical generosity (G). Salvation as a cluster reshapes the will in every mode. Third, perseverance capacity: A, B, C, F, and G all explicitly evidence the capacity to continue under pressure as a sustained effect — the five crisis or temporal characteristics all develop endurance. M38-D and M38-E are the characteristics in which perseverance is less foregrounded, suggesting that the condition-based and mediatorial characteristics emphasize settled standing over forward endurance. Fourth, intercessory capacity: M38-D (vocation and service from cleared conscience), M38-E (crisis-readiness for self-placement between wrath and community), M38-G (memorial as ground for intercessory appeal), and M38-F (communal sustaining capacity) all establish some form of outward-oriented service or prayer as a sustained outcome. Salvation in M38 is not merely personal benefit but generates mediatorial and intercessory orientation as a characteristic sustained effect.

**T1.7.2 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The blocking conditions across the seven characteristics converge more tightly than the enabling conditions and reveal the cluster's three primary internal threats. The first blocking condition appearing across the widest range of characteristics is self-reliance or self-sufficiency: M38-A (wealth, works, truth-refusal), M38-B (faithless demand, presumption), M38-C (fear as self-protective contraction, taking benefit without relational acknowledgment), M38-D (pride, self-justification), M38-F (self-reliant striving), M38-G (deceptive self-direction refusing ransom). Six of seven characteristics identify self-directed inner orientation as a primary block. The second blocking condition is despair-as-closure: M38-A (passive neglect), M38-B (despairing closure), M38-F (despair-collapse under deferred hope). Three characteristics specifically name the extinction of hope as a blocking condition — distinct from self-reliance but equally effective as a prevention. The third is persistent, unacknowledged sin or defiance: M38-D (entrenched sin closing the atonement pathway), M38-G (deceptive rebellion against offered ransom), M38-A (willful truth-refusal). M38-E's blocking conditions are structurally distinct: presumptuous uncovered mediation, wrong identity-markers, deliberate high-handed sin outside the system's scope. These are not creaturely failure-to-receive conditions but mediatorial failure-to-enact conditions. M38-C's unique blocking condition in the cluster is gratitude-deferred reception — taking physical benefit without relational acknowledgment. This is the only blocking condition in the cluster that operates after initial reception rather than before it, suggesting that the full characteristic can be obstructed even after partial engagement. No other characteristic evidences this mid-process blockage. The cluster reveals that M38 salvation is blocked at three points: before engagement (self-sufficiency, defiance), during engagement (despair, distorted posture), and after initial reception (M38-C's ungrateful self-continuation).

**T1.8.2 — Dimension Classification — What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The supporting evidence for each characteristic's primary dimension classification reveals which faculty the verse evidence most consistently names, commands, or presupposes. The cluster finding is that direct naming of inner faculties in the verse evidence is unevenly distributed: M38-A and M38-C have the most explicit faculty-naming (faith, heart-believing, confessing, interior conviction, "your faith has made you well"), with the volitional-faith classification supported by repeated direct attribution. M38-B's affective-volitional classification is supported by the most explicit affective vocabulary in the cluster — "was afraid," "my soul is troubled," "loud cries and tears" — which appears across multiple distinct verse groups rather than only in edge cases. M38-D's conscience classification is supported by the most consistent outcome vocabulary: "forgiven," "guilt removed," "sin covered" — all of which are conscience-register terms appearing in eleven consecutive Levitical verses, making this the most lexically dense single-faculty evidence in the cluster. M38-E's volitional-executive classification is supported by the structural character of all five VCGs: every group organizes around what the priest does rather than what the priest feels or believes. The enacted sequence is the evidence. M38-F's volitional-affective evidence is the most dispersed across its VCGs — both volitional (waiting, directing, pleading) and affective (mourning, exulting, groaning) evidence appears in every VCG, making the dual classification well-distributed. M38-G's volitional-memorial classification is supported by the most explicitly command-structured evidence in the cluster: Deu 15:15, Deu 24:18, Mic 6:4 all issue direct imperatives grounded in remembered ransom, making the memorial-generating-will structure the explicit logical form of the key texts. **T1.1.3 — Name and Naming — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?**

The constitutional implication is most profound in M38-G and most vocational in M38-E — two distinct senses of "what a person fundamentally is."

**T1.4.3 — Modes of Operation — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — and if so, how does it function?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Six of seven characteristics evidence a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — only M38-E (priestly mediation machinery) is primarily enacted rather than voiced, though even M38-E contains a speech-adjacent mode in Moses's spoken declaration of mediatorial intent (Exo 32:30). The universal presence of speech across the cluster is a finding: M38 salvation consistently generates, requires, or is constituted partly by verbal expression. In M38-A, speech is constitutive: "confessing with the mouth" (Rom 10:9-10) and "calling on the name" (Act 2:21) are not expressions of a pre-existing salvation but acts that partly constitute its reception — the confession completes and publicly enacts the inner volitional turning. In M38-C, speech operates on two levels: inner speech (formed interior conviction) and outer cry (Bartimaeus) — making M38-C the only characteristic in the cluster with explicit inner-speech as a distinct operative mode. In M38-D, speech is priestly-declarative: the priest's pronouncement of forgiveness constitutes the forgiven state rather than describing it — a performative speech act. In M38-F, speech is address-to-God and communal-petition: it both expresses and sustains the hope-posture simultaneously. In M38-G, speech takes three forms: intercessory prayer deploying redemptive memory, praise and song as overflow, and proclamation of salvation to others. A significant convergence: M38-A, M38-B, M38-D, and M38-G all include speech directed toward God as petition, confession, or memorial-invocation. This cluster-wide pattern of speech-toward-God as a constitutive mode confirms that M38 salvation is irreducibly relational and requires vocal-relational expression — it does not operate in a purely interior silent register.

**T1.6.3 — Sustained Effect — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — Every finding across the seven characteristics affirms that the sustained effect differs from the immediate response, and the cluster-wide comparison reveals a consistent structural pattern in how that difference operates. The immediate responses, across all seven characteristics, are characterized by intensity, movement, and transition: the crisis-cry, the peace-settling, the weight-taking, the will-turning, the joy-erupting, the fear-displacing, the contrition-striking. They are all kinetic — the inner being is in passage from one state to another. The sustained effects, by contrast, are uniformly characterized by stability, habituation, and dispositional depth: settled assurance (A), faith-heir identity (B), conscience-integrity (C), cleared relational access (D), habituated solidarity-consciousness (E), cosmic-scale security (F), stable ransomed selfhood (G). The shift from immediate to sustained is therefore a shift from kinesis to stasis, from acute to chronic, from event to character. Three characteristics show a specific additional quality in this transition: M38-A shows deepening (assurance replaces initial faith-urgency, not merely continuing it); M38-C shows direction-change (peace at reception becomes following-orientation sustained); M38-D shows qualitative moral integration (positional forgiveness becomes dispositional evil-avoidance and vocation-readiness). These three show that the sustained effect is not merely a settled version of the immediate response but is ontologically different from it — the person in sustained possession of M38-C is a disciple-follower, not simply a settled version of the faith-exerciser. M38-E's finding explicitly confirms this cluster pattern: the immediate response is weight-taking in the moment of atonement enactment; the sustained effect is the habituated solidarity-consciousness and crisis-readiness formed over years of repeated mediatorial service. The immediate and sustained are the same characteristic in different temporal register: kinetic at entry, dispositional at depth.

**T1.7.3 — Conditions of Reception — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — The seven findings collectively construct a composite portrait of the non-receiving inner being within M38. The comparison reveals that non-reception takes two structurally distinct forms across the cluster, and that these forms are not evenly distributed. The first form is active resistance: M38-A names willful truth-refusal and self-protective clinging; M38-D names defiance-and-contempt and persistent entrenched sin; M38-G names deceptive self-direction that refuses offered ransom; M38-B names faithless demand treating the rescuer as instrument. In this form, the inner being encounters salvation and actively turns away — not through ignorance but through some form of self-protective, self-reliant, or deceptive will-orientation. The second form is passive closure: M38-B names despairing resignation (Act 27:20); M38-F names the self-reliant laborer who produces futility; M38-C names ungrateful self-continuation (the nine lepers). In this form, the inner being either collapses inward (despair) or continues outward without acknowledgment — not aggressively resistant but functionally closed. The comparison reveals that M38-A, M38-D, and M38-G concentrate the most explicit active-resistance language, while M38-B, M38-C, and M38-F evidence more passive-closure states. M38-E is the exceptional case: its non-reception analysis focuses not on the community member who fails to receive the characteristic's benefit but on the mediator who fails to enact it properly — giving the characteristic a unique inward-facing non-reception profile that concerns the agent rather than the recipient. A significant cluster observation: M38-C uniquely evidences a partial-non-reception state — the nine lepers receive physical cure but not full sōzō. This is the only characteristic across all seven in which the inner-being of the non-receiver has already received something of the characteristic. Partial reception with relational non-completion is a distinct non-reception mode evidenced only in M38-C.

**T1.8.3 — Dimension Classification — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?**

**[CLUSTER]** E — All seven characteristics evidence secondary dimensions, and no finding claims that secondary dimensions compete with or displace the primary classification. The cluster-wide finding is therefore that M38 salvation is multi-dimensional in every characteristic, with a consistent primary and enriching secondaries in each case. However, the secondary dimensions themselves show important patterns of recurrence and distribution. The relational dimension appears as a secondary in M38-B, M38-C, and M38-E — in all three cases as the relationship with the divine other being constitutive to but not primary in the characteristic. The cognitive-noetic dimension appears as secondary in M38-A, M38-C, M38-F, and M38-G — in all four cases serving the primary volitional or memorial dimension by supplying the content (promise comprehended, conviction formed, delay interpreted, ransom remembered) that the will then acts upon. The cognitive dimension is consistently instrumental to the volitional primary across the cluster: mind serves will in M38. The affective dimension appears as secondary in M38-A, M38-C, M38-D, M38-G, and M38-F — in five characteristics. In M38-F the affect is so prominent it is named as co-primary (volitional-affective). In M38-G it is named as secondary but rich. In M38-D uniquely, the affective secondary includes shame-and-contrition as a proper sustained affective state rather than merely peace or joy. The conscience dimension appears as secondary in M38-A, M38-C, and M38-F, but as primary only in M38-D. This asymmetry confirms that the conscience is engaged across the cluster — M38 salvation is not conscience-indifferent in any characteristic — but that M38-D is the characteristic specifically constituted by conscience-transformation. The absence of competing secondaries across all seven findings suggests that M38's dimensional structure is genuinely layered rather than fractured: the primary and secondary dimensions work together rather than pulling against each other in every instance.
