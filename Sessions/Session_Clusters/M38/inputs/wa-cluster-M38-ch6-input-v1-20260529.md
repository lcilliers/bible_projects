**Key verses for Ransomed identity gratitude and memory:**

**Deu 15:15** (H6299 pa.dah) [M38-G-VCG-01]
> Deu 15:15 You shall remember that you were a slave in the land of Egypt , and the Lord your God redeemed you; therefore I command you this today .
_Meaning of the term here:_ Memory of God's past redemption from slavery grounds the command to treat slaves generously; the inner being is motivated by gratitude for having been ransomed.

**Deu 9:26** (H6299 pa.dah) [M38-G-VCG-02]
> Deu 9:26 And I prayed to the Lord , ‘O Lord God , do not destroy your people and your heritage , whom you have redeemed through your greatness , whom you have brought out of Egypt with a mighty hand .
_Meaning of the term here:_ Moses intercedes appealing to God's past redemption of the people as grounds for mercy; the inner-being register is pleading trust grounded in remembered deliverance.

**2Sa 4:9** (H6299 pa.dah) [M38-G-VCG-03]

_Meaning of the term here:_ David appeals to God as the one who has personally redeemed his life from all adversity, expressing a soul-level trust grounded in experienced deliverance.

**Psa 119:134** (H6299 pa.dah) [M38-G-VCG-04]
> Psa 119:134 Redeem me from man’s oppression , that I may keep your precepts .
_Meaning of the term here:_ The psalmist asks to be redeemed from human oppression so that obedience to God's precepts becomes possible; ransom here enables the will's free response to God.

**Hos 13:14** (H6299 pa.dah) [M38-G-VCG-05]
