The cluster finding is that immediate-response variation is systematic and meaningful rather than arbitrary: the variation tracks the position of the encountering soul relative to the salvific event — receiving, remembering, awaiting, or witnessing its denial — and each position generates a distinct but coherent immediate response.

- *T1.6.3* — _Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?_
  → **[CLUSTER]** E — Every finding across the seven characteristics affirms that the sustained effect differs from the immediate response, and the cluster-wide comparison reveals a consistent structural pattern in how that difference operates. The immediate responses, across all seven characteristics, are characterized by intensity, movement, and transition: the crisis-cry, the peace-settling, the weight-taking, the will-turning, the joy-erupting, the fear-displacing, the contrition-striking. The sustained effects, by contrast, are uniformly characterized by stability, habituation, and dispositional depth: settled assurance (A), faith-heir identity (B), conscience-integrity (C), cleared relational access (D), habituated solidarity-consciousness (E), cosmic-scale security (F), stable ransomed selfhood (G). The shift from immediate to sustained is therefore a shift from kinesis to stasis, from acute to chronic, from event to character. Three characteristics show a specific additional quality in this transition: M38-A shows deepening (assurance replaces initial faith-urgency, not merely continuing it); M38-C shows direction-change (peace at reception becomes following-orientation sustained); M38-D shows qualitative moral integration (positional forgiveness becomes dispositional evil-avoidance and vocation-readiness). These three show that the sustained effect is not merely a settled version of the immediate response but is ontologically different from it — the person in sustained possession of M38-C is a disciple-follower, not simply a settled version of the faith-exerciser. M38-E's finding explicitly confirms this cluster pattern: the immediate response is weight-taking in the moment of atonement enactment; the sustained effect is the habituated solidarity-consciousness and crisis-readiness formed over years of repeated mediatorial service. The immediate and sustained are the same characteristic in different temporal register: kinetic at entry, dispositional at depth.
