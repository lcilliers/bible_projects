## Opening structural observation

M06-C differs from M06-A and M06-B in a critical way: the H8581 ta.av root carries three directional forms within the same term — the person's righteous abhorrence of evil (group 252-001), the inverted abhorrence directed against justice (group 252-002), and divine sustained abhorrence toward covenant violators (group 252-003). A single root covers all three directions. This is unique in the cluster so far — sa.ne in M06-A required different roots for different registers, but ta.av holds multiple directions within one root. The evaluative direction is not encoded in the term but determined entirely by the context.

This means M06-C's primary analytical question is not "which terms name which phenomena" but "which direction is the abhorrence running" — and the answer is entirely from the verse context, not from the term itself.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**T0.1.1** — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**E** — God is extensively the subject of abhorrence in M06-C. Psa 106:40 (group 252-003, anchor): "he abhorred his heritage." Psa 5:6 (group 252-001): "the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man." Amo 6:8 (group 248-001, H8374 ta.av): "I abhor the pride of Jacob." Isa 66:24 (group 1283-001, de.ra.on): "they shall be an abhorrence to all flesh." Eze 8:10 (group 247-001, she.qets): the detestable objects of idolatrous devotion which God names as she.qets.

What this reveals about God: he has a moral recoil from corruption, pride, and covenant violation that is visceral and total. It is not a measured judicial assessment but an inner-being response of revulsion. The term ta.av carries physical resonance — the sense of something that cannot be tolerated in one's presence. God's abhorrence reveals that he is not indifferent to evil but constitutionally repelled by it at the level of his inner being.

**T0.1.2** — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God?

**E** — Yes. Psa 5:6, Psa 106:40, Amo 6:8 all name God as the subject. The attribution is not anthropomorphic hedging — the terms are the same used for human abhorrence. God's inner being is named as the location of the revulsion (Psa 5:6: "the Lord abhors").

**T0.1.3** — What does divine possession of this characteristic reveal?

**E** — It reveals that abhorrence of evil is not merely a human moral response but a constitutive feature of God's character. More significantly: when the human person abhorres what God abhors (group 252-001, Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law"), the human abhorrence is a participation in God's own inner-being reality. The characteristic is shared between God and the aligned person — which means it has a positive place in the properly ordered inner life, not only in God.

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

**T0.2.1** — What purpose does abhorrence serve in the human person as created?

**E** — Group 252-001 shows this directly. Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law." The abhorrence of falsehood is the negative pole of the love of truth — it is what the love of truth feels like in the presence of its opposite. Deu 23:7: "you shall not abhor an Edomite...you shall not abhor an Egyptian" — the command not to abhor persons in certain relational contexts implies that abhorrence is a real inner capacity that can be directed. The command regulates the direction, not the capacity itself. This suggests abhorrence is a created inner capacity that serves the purpose of marking what is genuinely repugnant to God's character.

**T0.2.2** — Part of original design, response to the fallen condition, or both?

**E** — The capacity appears to belong to the created design — God possesses it, and the righteous person shares it in alignment with God. But the most prominent uses in M06-C (groups 252-002, 252-003, 247-001) show the capacity inverted or directed against what is good — abhorrence turned against justice (Amo 5:10) and truth (Mic 3:9) is a fallen expression of the capacity. Both dimensions are evidenced: the created capacity and its fallen inversion.

**T0.2.3** — Orientation toward a future fullness?

**E** — Group 1283-001 (de.ra.on) names this most directly. Dan 12:2: "some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt" — the abhorrence that is the appropriate inner-being response to rebellion against God becomes, at the eschatological horizon, the permanent relational condition of the unrighteous. Isa 66:24: "they shall be an abhorrence to all flesh" — the characteristic reaches its fullest expression at the eschatological moment when all moral reality is made plain. The future fullness of abhorrence is its eschatological completion — when what is genuinely abhorrent is finally and permanently recognised as such.

### T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression

**T0.3.1** — How does this characteristic express the divine image?

**E** — Abhorrence of evil expresses the divine image by aligning the human person's inner moral recoil with God's own. Psa 119:163: the one who abhors falsehood and loves God's law is the person whose inner moral response mirrors God's. The capacity for visceral moral recoil from evil is the image-bearer's participation in God's own moral constitution — not merely a rule followed but a constitution shared.

**T0.3.2** — Shared between God and human, or exclusively creaturely?

**E** — Shared. God abhors (Psa 5:6, Psa 106:40, Amo 6:8). The righteous person abhors (Psa 119:163). The commands not to abhor in certain directions (Deu 23:7) treat the capacity as real and regulable. This is a shared characteristic, more like righteous hatred (M06-A, group 550-NEW-02) than like disordered person-directed hatred.

**T0.3.3** — What does its presence or absence reveal?

**E** — Group 252-002 (inverted abhorrence): Amo 5:10: "they abhor him who speaks the truth." When abhorrence is directed against justice and truth rather than against evil, it is a marker of profound image-disorder. The capacity itself remains active — these people are capable of abhorrence — but it is turned against what it was made to align with. The presence of inverted abhorrence reveals a moral constitution that has been systematically reversed.

### T0.4 — Typological Significance

**T0.4.1** — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?

**E** — Rev 21:8 (group 14-002, bdelussomai): "the detestable" (bdelussomai, passive participle) are listed among those whose portion is the second death. The term names persons whose inner character has become abhorrent in God's sight as a final, permanent condition. This is the eschatological typology: the person who has made themselves abhorrent through persistent moral corruption becomes the embodiment of what the characteristic names. The typological direction is from the moral quality (abhorrence-worthy character) to its eschatological consequence.

De.ra.on in Dan 12:2 is cited in the NT (implicitly in Mark 9:48 and Rev 14:11) — the everlasting abhorrence becomes the signature of final judgment. The characteristic participates in the eschatological vocabulary of Scripture at its most ultimate register.

**T0.4.2** — Direction of typology?

**E** — The typological direction in both cases is from the present moral-inner condition (what has become abhorrent through sustained rebellion) to the eschatological reality (the permanent condition of being an abhorrence). The human instance anticipates the divine-eschatological verdict.

Not applicable — typological use is evidenced.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

**T1.1.1** — What does the name "Abhorrence" signal?

The name signals a visceral inner response — stronger than contempt, more bodily in its register, more immediate in its onset. Abhorrence is not the cool evaluative verdict of contempt (M06-B) but the gut-level recoil from what is morally repugnant. The name carries the physical resonance of the Hebrew root: ta.av is rooted in the sense of something being nauseating, not merely unworthy.

**T1.1.2** — What do the primary terms reveal?

**H8581 ta.av** — the dominant term (18 verses across three groups). The root carries the sense of loathing — something that cannot be tolerated, that produces inner recoil. The crucial feature is that it can be directed in any direction: toward evil (righteous abhorrence), toward justice (inverted abhorrence), toward persons (divine abhorrence of covenant violators). The direction is not encoded in the root.

**H8374 ta.av** — a distinct but cognate root (H8374 vs H8581), appearing only in Amos 6:8. It names divine loathing directed specifically at pride. The proximity of the two ta.av roots suggests they share the same core sense but are lexically distinguished.

**H8263 she.qets** — "detestable thing" — used for objects of abhorrence, particularly idols and ritually unclean creatures. The term names what is worthy of abhorrence rather than the inner disposition of abhorrence itself. Its inclusion in M06-C is for its group 247-002 use (commanded inner detestation) rather than its ritual-classification uses (which are set aside).

**H1860 de.ra.on** — a rare term (only Isa 66:24 and Dan 12:2), naming abhorrence as a permanent eschatological condition. The term functions as the terminus of the characteristic — the final state rather than a recurring response.

**G0948 bdelussomai** — the Greek term for inner revulsion from evil (Rom 2:22) and the state of being abhorrent in God's sight (Rev 21:8). The Greek root carries the sense of stench — something that causes revulsion in the way an offensive smell does.

**T1.1.3** — Directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**E** — The characteristic is primarily **moral-evaluative** in its directionality — it is triggered by the perception of something as morally repugnant. Unlike hatred (relational hostility) and contempt (worth-evaluation), abhorrence is a **moral recoil** — the inner being moving away from what violates its moral constitution. Constitutional implication: the recoil appears to be located at the level of the conscience and moral evaluation faculty rather than in the heart's relational orientation or the mind's cognitive assessment.

### T1.2 — Kind

**T1.2.1** — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is abhorrence?

**E** — A **visceral moral response** — stronger in its bodily-resonance register than contempt, less sustained-dispositional than hatred. The bdelussomai root (stench-based Greek term) and the ta.av root both carry physical resonance — the inner recoil has a quasi-somatic quality. Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor" — the pairing with hatred suggests they are distinguishable experiences even when co-occurring: hatred is the relational disposition, abhorrence is the visceral recoil.

**T1.2.2** — Simple or constituent elements?

**E** — The constituent elements from the verse evidence: perception of the morally repugnant → visceral inner recoil → moral verdict (this cannot be tolerated) → maintained distance or action of separation. The recoil precedes the verdict — it is a pre-cognitive response that the moral evaluation faculty then names and confirms.

**T1.2.3** — Working description:

Abhorrence (M06-C) is a visceral inner moral recoil from what is perceived as fundamentally repugnant to the person's moral constitution — constitutionally located at the conscience/moral-evaluation level, engaging perception, moral evaluation, and the body's resonance registers, and producing maintained distance from the object of revulsion. It is shared between God and the aligned person, directionally determined entirely by context (the same capacity can be rightly or wrongly directed), and at its eschatological terminus becomes a permanent condition of the unrighteous.

### T1.3 — Boundary

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite?

**E** — The structural opposite is not love (that is hatred's opposite) but **moral acceptance, tolerance, or indulgence** — the inner posture that admits what should be rejected. Deu 7:26: "you shall not bring an abominable thing into your house" — the structural opposite is the inner state that welcomes what should be repelled. More precisely, the opposite of righteous abhorrence of evil is the absence of moral recoil — moral indifference or moral accommodation of what is genuinely repugnant.

**T1.3.2** — What does abhorrence explicitly exclude?

**E** — It excludes proximity: Job 30:10: "they abhor me; they keep aloof from me" — the abhorrence immediately produces physical and relational distance. It excludes tolerance of the object in one's space (Deu 7:26). It excludes the moral indifference that would allow the abhorrent thing to remain.

**T1.3.3** — What is abhorrence not?

**E** — It is not hatred: hatred is sustained relational hostility toward a person; abhorrence is visceral moral recoil from a quality or condition. One can abhor someone's actions without hating the person. It is not contempt: contempt is the cool evaluative verdict of worthlessness; abhorrence is the visceral recoil from what is morally repugnant. Contempt dismisses; abhorrence recoils. The affective and faculty distinctions are real.

---

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**T1.4.1** — Distinct modes?

**E** — Three modes from the verse evidence:

1. **Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001):** The person's visceral recoil from evil, falsehood, and what violates moral truth — aligned with God's own abhorrence. Psa 119:163: the simultaneous hating and abhorring of falsehood. This is the moral recoil of a functioning conscience.

2. **Inverted abhorrence (group 252-002):** The same capacity directed against justice and truth — Amo 5:10, Mic 3:9. The capacity functions, but its direction has been reversed. The inner recoil now responds to righteousness as if it were the repugnant thing.

3. **Divine abhorrence (groups 252-003, 248-001):** God's sustained inner revulsion toward those who have persistently corrupted themselves. Psa 106:40: he abhorred his heritage. This is not momentary but sustained — the prolonged accumulation of covenant violation has produced a settled divine revulsion.

**T1.4.2** — Does it operate differently by context or direction?

**E** — Markedly. The moral quality of the abhorrence is entirely determined by its direction. The same inner capacity — visceral moral recoil — produces either the most morally ordered response (righteous abhorrence of evil, aligned with God) or the most disordered response (abhorrence of justice, aligned with corruption). The term itself carries no moral valence; the context supplies it entirely.

**T1.4.3** — Speech-based mode?

**S** — No speech-mode is directly evidenced for M06-C. The characteristic operates as an inner response rather than a verbal act. The ne.a.tsah blasphemous-speech mode belongs to M06-B. Abhorrence is characteristically non-verbal in this corpus.

### T1.5 — Immediate Response

**T1.5.1** — Immediate inner-being response to encountering this characteristic?

**E** — For the recipient of abhorrence: Job 19:19: "all my intimate friends abhor me, and those whom I loved have turned against me." The immediate consequence of being abhorred by intimate friends is social abandonment — turning away. Job 30:10: "they abhor me; they keep aloof from me; they do not hesitate to spit at the sight of me." The immediate physical response to abhorrence is distancing — spitting, keeping away. The abhorrence is so strong it produces bodily expression.

For the holder of abhorrence: the immediate response appears to be the recoil itself — a pulling away from the object. Deu 7:26: "you shall utterly detest and abhor it, for it is devoted to destruction" — the commanded abhorrence is a commanded pulling-away, a commanded maintained distance.

**T1.5.2** — Consistent across verse evidence?

**E** — Consistently: abhorrence produces distance. Whether divine (Psa 106:40: "he abhorred his heritage"), human-social (Job 30:10: "they keep aloof"), or commanded-ritual (Deu 7:26: "you shall abhor it"), the immediate consequence is separation from the object of abhorrence.

**T1.5.3** — Where evidence is silent on immediate response, note explicitly.

The phenomenology of the moment of abhorrence-onset — what it feels like as it arises — is not described in the verse evidence. The verses name the state and its consequences, not its internal onset-experience.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**T1.6.1** — What does abhorrence produce over time?

**E** — In its righteous form (group 252-001): sustained abhorrence of evil produces stable moral orientation — the person is constitutionally set against what God opposes. The sustained effect is moral reliability: the one who abhors falsehood (Psa 119:163) is reliably oriented against it, not merely situationally. In its divine form (group 252-003): sustained divine abhorrence toward covenant violators produces judgment and exclusion — Psa 106:40's "he abhorred his heritage" is followed by Israel's subjection to enemies.

In its eschatological terminus (group 1283-001): the sustained effect of being abhorrent becomes permanent — Dan 12:2's "everlasting contempt" and Isa 66:24's "they shall be an abhorrence to all flesh" name a final, irreversible condition. The sustained effect of being the object of rightful abhorrence reaches its completion at the eschatological horizon.

**T1.6.2** — What states or capacities does sustained abhorrence establish?

**E** — In the rightly-directed form: sustained abhorrence of evil establishes the inner-being as constitutionally opposed to corruption — making the person reliably resistant to what they abhor. In the inverted form (group 252-002): sustained abhorrence of justice establishes moral inversion — the person is now constitutionally repelled by righteousness. Both are stable inner orientations produced by sustained operation of the characteristic.

**T1.6.3** — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?

**E** — Yes. The immediate response is a recoil (pulling away). The sustained effect is a constitutional orientation — the inner being has organised itself permanently around the recoil, making it a stable posture rather than a momentary response. The immediate recoil is an event; the sustained effect is a character quality.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

**T1.7.1** — What inner conditions enable righteous abhorrence to take hold?

**E** — Moral alignment with God's character. Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law" — the love of God's law is what generates the abhorrence of falsehood. The enabling condition is a prior love of what is true and good, which makes the encountering of its opposite naturally repugnant. The abhorrence is downstream of the love.

**T1.7.2** — What blocks righteous abhorrence?

**E** — Moral inversion, visible in group 252-002. The leaders in Amo 5:10 and Mic 3:9 have so thoroughly organised themselves around corruption that justice has become the thing that produces revulsion. The blocking condition is the sustained habituation to evil that has reversed the direction of the moral recoil.

**T1.7.3** — Inner state of the person who encounters abhorrence from another?

**E** — Job 19:19 and 30:10: the person who is abhorred by intimate friends experiences total social abandonment — even those who loved them turn against them. The inner impact is isolation, exposure, and the loss of all relational shelter. This is a more intense form of the reproach experience (M06-E) — being the object of abhorrence is a stronger social-inner condition than being the object of mere contempt.

---

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

**T1.8.1** — Primary dimension?

**E** — **Moral Character** is the primary dimension — abhorrence is the moral evaluation faculty's most visceral response, operating at the level of the conscience's deepest reactions. The characteristic is fundamentally a moral-constitutive response: it names what the inner being's moral constitution finds intolerable, not merely what it evaluates as wrong. The difference from contempt (also Moral Character): contempt is the cool verdict; abhorrence is the visceral recoil. Both engage moral evaluation but at different temperatures and depths.

**T1.8.2** — Evidence?

**E** — Every group in M06-C involves a moral evaluation of something as repugnant: God abhorring pride (248-001), the righteous abhorring falsehood (252-001), the corrupt abhorring justice (252-002), God abhorring covenant violators (252-003), the eschatological abhorrence of the unrighteous (1283-001). The moral evaluation faculty is the constant across all groups; the direction changes.

**T1.8.3** — Secondary dimensions?

**E** — **Emotion — Negative** is secondary: abhorrence has an affective quality more intense than contempt — the visceral recoil carries a felt register. **Dependence/Creatureliness** may be secondary in the eschatological dimension (de.ra.on): the permanent condition of being an abhorrence names the creaturely position of those who have definitively rejected their Creator.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

### T3.1 — Perception

**E** — Abhorrence is triggered by perception — the morally repugnant thing must first be seen or encountered. But abhorrence also shapes subsequent perception: once something is abhorred, it becomes perceived as repugnant in all future encounters. Group 247-001 (she.qets, idolatrous objects): Eze 8:10 shows the prophet being brought to see the detestable things engraved on the wall — the vision makes visible what has been provoking divine abhorrence. The perception of the abhorrent thing is itself a moral event.

The inverted form (group 252-002) shows perception corrupted: those who abhor justice (Amo 5:10) perceive the truth-speaker as the repugnant thing. Their perceptive faculty has been reorganised around the inverted abhorrence.

---

### T3.2 — Cognition

**E** — Rom 2:22 (bdelussomai) shows the cognitive dimension most clearly. The person claims to abhor idols — a cognitive position they hold. But the apostle's challenge ("do you rob temples?") exposes that the cognitive claim does not correspond to an actual inner state of abhorrence. The cognitive faculty can assert abhorrence without the genuine visceral recoil being present. This is the gap between claimed abhorrence and genuine abhorrence — a cognitive performance without the inner reality.

---

### T3.3 — Memory

**S** — Memory is not prominently engaged in M06-C. Abhorrence is characteristically present-focused — triggered by current perception of the repugnant — rather than sustained by memory of past injury (which is sa.tam's mechanism). The eschatological permanence of de.ra.on is not a memory-sustained state but a permanent condition. Silent on memory engagement.

---

### T3.4 — Affect

**E** — Abhorrence carries the strongest affective register of the three M06 sub-groups examined so far. The physical resonance of the ta.av and bdelussomai roots (loathing, stench) names an affective quality that is visceral, embodied, and intense. Psa 106:40: God "abhorred his heritage" — the term carries weight that ta.av's root implies. The affective register of abhorrence is **disgust** — the moral version of the physical response to what is nauseating. Unlike contempt (cool dismissal) or hatred (intense hostility), abhorrence is the disgust-register: strong, visceral, pulling away.

---

### T3.5 — Creativity

**S** — No evidence that abhorrence engages the creative faculty. Like contempt, abhorrence is characteristically non-creative — it recoils rather than plans. The creative faculty is not visibly engaged. Silent.

---

### T3.6 — Volition

**E** — Abhorrence organises the will toward distance and separation. Deu 7:26: "you shall utterly detest and abhor it, for it is devoted to destruction" — the commanded abhorrence is a commanded volitional orientation away from what is abhorrent. The will is shaped by the abhorrence to maintain separation. The inverted form: the leaders who abhor justice (Mic 3:9) have a will organised away from what is right and toward what is crooked — "who make crooked all that is straight" — the volitional acts follow the direction of the abhorrence.

---

### T3.7 — Agency

**E** — Abhorrence drives agency toward separation and against the object. Psa 5:6: "the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man" — followed immediately by God's destructive action against them. The divine abhorrence produces divine action. Isa 66:24: the dead bodies of those who rebelled against God are an abhorrence — the divine agency of judgment has acted against them in accordance with the abhorrence. Abhorrence-driven agency is separation-producing and, in God's case, judgment-producing.

---

### T3.8 — Moral Evaluation

**E** — Moral evaluation is the faculty that names and confirms the abhorrence. The characteristic operates at a level deeper than moral evaluation (the visceral recoil precedes the verdict) but the moral evaluation faculty is what identifies the object as worthy of abhorrence and maintains the response as morally valid. Rom 2:22's challenge works precisely here: the claimed abhorrence of idols (a moral evaluation position) is exposed as not corresponding to an actual inner state.

---

### T3.9 — Conscience

**E** — This is M06-C's primary faculty. Abhorrence is what a functioning conscience feels in the presence of moral violation. Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001) is the conscience's deepest response to what is genuinely repugnant — Psa 119:163's "I hate and abhor falsehood" is a conscience-statement, not merely a preference. Inverted abhorrence (group 252-002) is the sign of a conscience that has been comprehensively corrupted — it now recoils from the right thing and welcomes the wrong. The direction of abhorrence is the most sensitive indicator of the conscience's condition.

---

### T3.10 — Conscientiousness

**E** — Righteous abhorrence undergirds reliable conscientiousness in the same way righteous hatred does (M06-A). The person who genuinely abhors corruption is constitutionally resistant to it. The inverted abhorrence (group 252-002) produces a form of anti-conscientiousness: the leaders who abhor justice are those who actively work against it ("make crooked all that is straight" — Mic 3:9). Their conscientiousness has been inverted — they are diligent in the wrong direction.

### T3.11 — Relational Capacity

**E** — Job 19:19 and 30:10 show abhorrence as relational-capacity-destroying in the recipient. Being abhorred closes all relational doors — even intimate friends abhor and turn away. The relational capacity of those who abhor the recipient is closed entirely. This is more total than hatred (which at least maintains the hostile relational connection) and more total than contempt (which dismisses but does not necessarily avoid all contact). Abhorrence produces complete relational withdrawal.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

### T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human

**E** — Extensively evidenced. God's abhorrence toward covenant violators (group 252-003, 9 verses) and toward pride (group 248-001, Amo 6:8) is sustained and serious. Psa 106:40: "he abhorred his heritage" — the divine inner response to sustained covenant violation is not mere displeasure but moral revulsion. The basis is consistently the accumulation of moral failure: "he acted very abominably in going after idols" (1Ki 21:26). God's abhorrence is never arbitrary but always grounded in what the person or people have become through their choices.

### T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God

**E** — In the righteous direction: Psa 119:163's abhorrence of falsehood "but I love your law" is an act of covenantal alignment — the person's abhorrence moves toward God by moving against what God opposes. The abhorrence is an expression of love for God in the register of moral recoil from what he opposes. In the inverted direction: group 252-002's abhorrence of justice is the inner movement away from God — those who abhor what God loves are moving against him in their inner constitution.

### T4.3-4.4 — Human Interface

**E (from receiving side)** — Job 19:19, 30:10: being the object of abhorrence from intimate friends is the most socially and personally devastating experience in the M06 corpus. The abhorrence produces complete social abandonment and physical expressions of revulsion (spitting). Isa 49:7: the Servant is "deeply despised, abhorred by the nation" — receiving abhorrence from the community is named as the deepest form of social rejection. The giving of abhorrence between humans appears in the buz context (M06-B, group 316-001) and here — the comfortable abhorring the suffering, the corrupt abhorring the righteous. These are inverted forms of the characteristic.

### T4.5 — Boundaries

**E** — Abhorrence crosses all relational boundaries: God abhorring his own people (Psa 106:40), intimate friends abhorring Job (Job 19:19), nations abhorring the Servant (Isa 49:7), the righteous abhorring falsehood (Psa 119:163). The relational scope is unlimited. What determines whether abhorrence is appropriate is not the relational proximity of the object but the moral quality of what is being abhorred.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

### T5.1 — Transformation

**E** — The most dramatic transformation in M06-C is the inversion visible in group 252-002. What begins as a moral capacity (the ability to abhor) undergoes a directional reversal — those who once might have abhorred evil now abhor justice. This inversion is not sudden; Deu 31:20's predictive sequence (prosperity → despising → covenant breaking) suggests a progressive drift in which the moral constitution is gradually reorganised. The transformation is not a disappearance of the capacity but a reorientation of it. The capacity survives; its direction has been reversed.

---

### T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States

**E** — From the verse evidence:

1. **Love of what is true/good** — the prior state that makes abhorrence possible (Psa 119:163: love of God's law precedes abhorrence of falsehood)
2. **Encounter with the morally repugnant** — the trigger
3. **Visceral recoil** — the immediate response of the conscience
4. **Moral verdict** — the evaluation faculty confirms the recoil as appropriate
5. **Maintained distance** — the sustained orientation away from the object
6. **Constitutional formation** — the abhorrence becomes a stable inner orientation

In the inverted sequence: sustained habituation to corruption → gradual reversal of the moral recoil direction → abhorrence of justice as the new resting state.

---

### T5.3 — Mechanism of Change

**E** — In the righteous form: the mechanism is the **sharpening of the conscience through immersion in what is good**. Psa 119:163: "through your precepts I get understanding; therefore I hate every false way" — the same sequence applies to abhorrence. Understanding of what is true makes the encounter with falsehood more viscerally intolerable.

In the inverted form: the mechanism is **habituation to corruption** — progressive immersion in what is morally repugnant until the conscience's alarm signal is redirected. The conscience does not go silent; it is retrained.

---

### T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction

**E** — Job 19:19 and 30:10 show abhorrence as both a cause of suffering (the sufferer is abhorred by those around him) and as something experienced in extremity. Psa 107:18: "they loathed any kind of food, and they drew near to the gates of death" — physical abhorrence (loathing of food) as an expression of the extremity of suffering. Isa 49:7: the Servant's being "abhorred by the nation" is part of the suffering pattern that characterises his mission. Abhorrence and suffering are consistently linked — either as cause (abhorrence produces social abandonment and suffering in the recipient) or as context (suffering can produce the conditions in which abhorrence arises).

---

### T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification

**E** — Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001) participates positively in sanctification: Psa 119:163 names it as the negative pole of a sanctified moral orientation. The person being formed into the image of God is being formed toward what God loves and away from what God opposes — and the abhorrence is the felt quality of that movement away.

Inverted abhorrence (group 252-002) names the anti-sanctification dynamic: abhorring justice (Amo 5:10, Mic 3:9) is the sign that the formation process has been running in the wrong direction for long enough that the moral constitution has been reversed. These leaders are formed, but formed toward corruption.

### T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory

**E** — The most concentrated eschatological evidence in the entire M06 cluster is in M06-C's group 1283-001. Dan 12:2: "some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt (de.ra.on)." Isa 66:24: "they shall be an abhorrence to all flesh." The characteristic reaches its eschatological terminus as a permanent condition — those who have made themselves abhorrent through sustained rebellion against God are named, at the final horizon, as an abhorrence. The eschatological trajectory of abhorrence is its completion: the moral verdict that was made during history is confirmed and made permanent.

The righteous abhorrence trajectory (group 252-001) anticipates the eschatological elimination of what is abhorrent — when falsehood, pride, and corruption are finally removed, the abhorrence that was directed at them will have completed its purpose.

**S** — T2.8 found no generational deposit from abhorrence. T5.7 closes accordingly. The eschatological permanence of de.ra.on is a final-state consequence, not a generational transmission.

Together they name the complete response to encountering falsehood.

The three characteristics occupy the same moral territory but at different depths and temperatures: contempt (cool, evaluative), hatred (intense, relational), abhorrence (visceral, constitutional). ## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

### T7.1 — Lexical

**T7.1.1-3** — Primary terms and semantic range:

H8581 ta.av is the dominant term — 18 verses across three groups. Its root carries loathing/nauseating resonance. The crucial lexical feature is its directional neutrality: the same root covers righteous abhorrence (252-001), inverted abhorrence (252-002), and divine abhorrence (252-003). The semantic range is determined entirely by the direction of the abhorrence, not by the term itself. This is lexically unusual — most inner-being terms carry moral valence in their root; ta.av does not. The moral quality is entirely contextual.

H8263 she.qets (detestation) names primarily what is worthy of abhorrence (objects of idolatrous devotion, ritually unclean creatures) rather than the inner disposition of abhorrence. The term's primary function in M06-C is in group 247-002 (commanded inner detestation) — the active directed form — while its ritual-classification uses are set aside.

H1860 de.ra.on is a terminal term — it appears only twice, both in eschatological contexts, naming the permanent condition of abhorrence rather than the ongoing response. It is the characteristic at its eschatological endpoint, not in its active operation.

G0948 bdelussomai (Greek): the stench-root gives the clearest physical resonance — moral revulsion expressed through the body-metaphor of offensive smell. The two NT uses (Rom 2:22 — genuine vs hypocritical abhorrence; Rev 21:8 — the abhorrent person at the eschatological horizon) cover the full arc: the living response and the final condition.

**T7.1.4** — Vocabulary distinctions: the vocabulary covers the inner disposition (ta.av), the object of abhorrence (she.qets), and the final condition of the abhorrent person (de.ra.on). This is a different kind of vocabulary architecture from M06-A — rather than covering different forms of the same inner state (act, disposition, persistence), M06-C's vocabulary covers different positions in the abhorrence relationship: the inner response, the object, and the terminus.

**T7.1.5** — Structural opposite: **moral acceptance/tolerance** of what is repugnant, and specifically the commanded forms are reversed: "you shall not abhor" (Deu 23:7 — the command not to abhor persons in certain relational contexts). The positive opposite is the welcoming or accepting of what should be rejected.

**T7.1.6** — Person-type: The person of abhorrent character (bdelussomai in Rev 21:8 — "the detestable") is a person-type at the eschatological level.

**T7.1.7** — Seeking term: **S** — not applicable.

**T7.1.8-9** — LXX/NT: **G** — CC database query required. Bdelussomai is the NT/LXX primary term for ta.av in many contexts; the stench-metaphor crosses from Hebrew to Greek vocabulary.

**T7.1.10** — Full vocabulary arc: The arc moves from inner visceral response (ta.av) through the naming of its object (she.qets) to its final eschatological expression as a permanent condition (de.ra.on). The arc shows abhorrence as the most eschatologically weighty characteristic in the cluster — it is the only one that names its own terminus as an eternal state.

### T7.2 — Literary

**T7.2.1** — Primary anchor: **Psa 119:163** (group 252-001): "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law." The term ta.av functions here as the visceral intensification of the hatred — the same object (falsehood) is both hated (relational hostility) and abhorred (moral recoil). The pairing in one verse of sa.ne and ta.av is the clearest textual demonstration that the two characteristics are distinct but co-occurring responses to the same moral reality.

**T7.2.2** — Literary forms: Wisdom/Psalm dominates (Psa 119:163, Psa 106:40, Psa 5:6); Prophetic second (Amo 5:10, 6:8, Mic 3:9, Isa 66:24); Apocalyptic/Eschatological third (Dan 12:2, Rev 21:8). The prophetic and apocalyptic forms give M06-C its eschatological weight — abhorrence is a characteristic with a strong end-times register.

**T7.2.3** — Key arguments: Dan 12:2's bifurcation argument is the most structurally significant: resurrection leads to two outcomes — everlasting life or shame and everlasting contempt/abhorrence. The argument is a binary: the permanent condition of the righteous (life) and the permanent condition of the rebel (abhorrence). No third option. The logic is totalising: abhorrence, when it has completed its arc, names the final relational position of those who made themselves abhorrent.

**T7.2.4** — Contextual settings: Eschatological (Dan 12:2, Isa 66:24, Rev 21:8), liturgical (Psa 119:163, Psa 106:40), prophetic-covenantal (Amo 5:10, 6:8, Mic 3:9). The eschatological setting is unique to M06-C in the cluster — no other sub-group has this concentration of final-state evidence.

**T7.2.5-6** — Primary anchor unique revelation: Psa 119:163 reveals what no other verse reveals: that hatred and abhorrence are simultaneously operative responses to the same object — they are distinct but concurrent inner-being phenomena. This verse is the clearest evidence that the two characteristics are not synonyms but co-constitutive dimensions of the full moral response.

### T7.3 — Science

**T7.3.1** — Primary field: **Moral psychology and disgust research** — specifically the science of moral disgust, which investigates how disgust operates as a moral emotion. Secondary: **neuroscience** (the neural substrates of disgust and moral disgust; insula involvement in moral revulsion).

**T7.3.2** — Convergences:

**Convergence 1 — Moral disgust as a distinct emotion:** Psychological research distinguishes physical disgust (response to contamination) from moral disgust (response to moral violation). Moral disgust research (notably Jonathan Haidt's work on the moral emotions) identifies disgust as a foundational moral emotion that operates faster than cognition — the visceral recoil precedes the reasoned verdict. This maps precisely onto M06-C's structure: the ta.av root's physical resonance and the pre-cognitive quality of the abhorrence response both align with the moral disgust model.

**Convergence 2 — The bidirectionality of moral disgust:** Research shows that moral disgust can be directed at genuinely repugnant moral violations (righteous disgust) or can be recruited by in-group politics to condemn what is not genuinely repugnant (misdirected disgust). This maps onto M06-C's inverted abhorrence (group 252-002): the leaders who abhor justice are exhibiting misdirected moral disgust — the same emotional-moral capacity, directed against what it was not designed to oppose.

**Convergence 3 — Disgust and social exclusion:** Research documents that moral disgust produces social exclusion of the disgusting object or person — the strongest form of social rejection. Job 30:10's spitting and keeping aloof, and Job 19:19's total social abandonment, correspond precisely to what the social psychology of moral disgust predicts.

**T7.3.3** — Divergences:

**Divergence 1 — The permanence of eschatological abhorrence:** Psychological research treats moral disgust as a functional emotion that can be regulated, redirected, or resolved. The eschatological register of de.ra.on (Dan 12:2, Isa 66:24) names a permanent condition — a final moral verdict that cannot be appealed or resolved. The science has no category for permanent moral revulsion; it works with modifiable emotional states.

**Divergence 2 — The divine subject:** As with M06-A and M06-B, the science cannot engage God as subject of abhorrence. The divine abhorrence (groups 252-003, 248-001) is beyond the scientific lens.

**T7.3.4** — Science gap: Research on **moral emotion formation** — how moral disgust is shaped, trained, or reversed through social and formative processes — is developed in moral developmental psychology. The M06-C evidence (inverted abhorrence in group 252-002) documents the reversal of abhorrence from its rightful direction, but the mechanism of that reversal is not articulated in the verses. The developmental psychology of how moral emotions are trained or corrupted would add mechanistic description to the inverted-abhorrence phenomenon.
