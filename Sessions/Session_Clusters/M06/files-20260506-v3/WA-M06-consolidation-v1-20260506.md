## Method

For each prompt, the question is: do all seven sub-groups produce the same answer, or do the answers differ? Where all sub-groups agree, a single cluster-level finding is recorded. Where answers differ, the sub-group-specific answers are retained with their sub-group label.

Prompts where all sub-groups were silent (S) are recorded once as cluster-level silence. Prompts where some sub-groups are evidenced and others silent are recorded as mixed, with the evidenced sub-groups named.

This consolidation works tier by tier through the 189 prompts, recording only the structure of agreement/disagreement — the content lives in the sub-group files.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**T0.1.1** — Cluster-level or sub-group-specific?

**Sub-group-specific.** The divine nature relationship differs fundamentally across sub-groups:
- M06-A: God is extensively the subject of hatred (soul-located, object-specific) — shared with the righteous person
- M06-B: God holds minimal contempt (reactive/judicial only) — not a characteristic he possesses originating
- M06-C: God is extensively the subject of abhorrence (ta.av) — shared with the righteous person
- M06-D: Cruelty is not a divine characteristic — God's severe judgment is not cruelty in his inner being
- M06-E: God removes reproach rather than extending it — he is the answer to the characteristic
- M06-F: God adopts the adversary posture (Exo 23:22) — as covenant advocacy, not as inner hostility
- M06-G: God judges malice — he does not possess it

**Cluster-level finding from T0.1.1:** Within M06, the characteristics divide into those God shares with the righteous person (hatred of evil — M06-A group 550-NEW-02; abhorrence of evil — M06-C group 252-001; adversary posture in covenant faithfulness — M06-F, Exo 23:22) and those that are exclusively creaturely conditions of disorder (contempt, cruelty, malice) or conditions God removes (reproach).

**T0.1.2** — Sub-group-specific: explicit divine attribution varies by sub-group as above.

**T0.1.3** — Cluster-level finding: God's silence on possessing contempt (M06-B), cruelty (M06-D), and malice (M06-G) as inner characteristics is consistent and significant. These three are the characteristics with no righteous form and no divine sharing. The silence identifies them as exclusively fallen conditions.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1-1.2 — Name, Kind, Working Description

**Sub-group-specific entirely.** Each characteristic is definitionally distinct. The kinds differ across the seven sub-groups in a way that resists cluster-level collapse:

| Sub-group | Kind |
|---|---|
| A Hatred | Sustained inner disposition |
| B Contempt | Evaluative act becoming habit |
| C Abhorrence | Visceral moral recoil |
| D Cruelty | Character void (absence of limiting goods) |
| E Reproach | Inner-social condition (received) |
| F Hostility/Enmity | Relational structure generating inner responses |
| G Malice | Compound disposition (enmity + contempt + pleasure in destruction) |

**Cluster-level finding from T1.2:** The cluster contains three fundamentally different kinds of inner-being phenomena: (1) inner dispositions/capacities (A, B, C — things the inner being *holds*); (2) a character condition defined by absence (D — what the inner being *lacks*); (3) a received social-inner condition (E — what the inner being *bears*); (4) a relational structure (F — what the inner being *faces*); (5) a compound (G — what happens when several of the above combine). The cluster is a family, not a category.

### T1.3 — Boundary (Structural Opposites)

**Sub-group-specific:** Each characteristic has a different structural opposite:
- A Hatred ↔ Love (a.ha.vah)
- B Contempt ↔ Honour/reverence (ka.vod, yir.ah)
- C Abhorrence ↔ Moral tolerance/acceptance of evil
- D Cruelty ↔ Mercy and kindness (chesed)
- E Reproach ↔ Honour and vindication
- F Hostility ↔ Advocacy and covenant companionship
- G Malice ↔ Compassion (sorrow at another's suffering)

**Cluster-level finding from T1.3:** The structural opposites span multiple different characteristic clusters: Love (C17), Honour/reverence (worship/covenant cluster), Mercy (grace cluster), Compassion. This shows that M06 is not simply the opposite of one characteristic but the negative pole of a broad range of positive inner-being characteristics across multiple clusters.

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**Sub-group-specific.** Each characteristic has distinct modes. However, one cluster-level pattern emerges:

**Cluster-level finding from T1.4:** Every sub-group in M06 has a concealment or expression dimension — the characteristic moves between inner state and external expression in each case:
- A: concealment in speech (Pro 26:24) and expression through violence
- B: inner speech (Psa 10:13) and expression through behaviour
- C: expression through bodily recoil (spitting) and social distance
- D: expression through unrestrained destructive action
- E: expression through taunts and shaming speech received by the person
- F: expression through legal and adversarial contest
- G: expression through bodily celebration (clapping, stamping)

The inner-outer movement is universal in M06, though the specific modes differ.

### T1.5 — Immediate Response

**Sub-group-specific.** The immediate responses differ:
- A: rupture/closure in the person who is hated; settlement in the one who hates
- B: withdrawal and progressive estrangement
- C: visceral recoil and maintained distance
- D: appeal to God in the one who suffers under cruelty; no inner response named in the cruel person
- E: heart-breaking and cry for pity (Psa 69:20)
- F: appeal for advocacy and urgent response
- G: bodily celebration of another's destruction

**Cluster-level finding:** The immediate response of the *recipient* of M06 characteristics is consistently one of appeal, rupture, or cry for help — from God or from other humans. The receiving end of M06 produces the appeal posture. This is consistent across A (received hatred → appeal), D (under cruelty → appeal), E (reproach → appeal), F (facing adversary → appeal). M06 characteristics systematically generate the appeal-posture in those who receive them.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**Sub-group-specific.** However, a cluster-level pattern emerges:

**Cluster-level finding from T1.6:** All M06 characteristics that are held by the agent (A, B, C, D, G) produce **progressive self-corruption** in the agent over time. Pro 11:17 (cruelty hurts the cruel), Pro 26:26 (concealed hatred will be exposed), Hos 9:7 (accumulated hatred destroys prophetic capacity), the inverted abhorrence of Amos 5:10 (producing moral reversal), the never-ending enmity of Eze 25:15 (self-perpetuating). The characteristics are not neutral states but self-destructive ones in the agent. M06 characteristics harm those who hold them.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

**Sub-group-specific.** Enabling conditions differ across sub-groups. However:

**Cluster-level finding from T1.7:** The primary blocking condition for all M06 agent-side characteristics is the same: **love of what is good, paired with fear of the Lord and relational accountability**. Where these are present, the M06 characteristics cannot take hold or are constrained:
- Love covers offences (blocks hatred — Pro 10:12)
- Fear of the Lord blocks contempt (Mal 1:6)
- Love of truth makes its opposite abhorrent rightly (Psa 119:163)
- Fear of God (setting God before oneself) blocks cruelty (Psa 54:3)
- The adversary relationship is borne through relationship with God (F)
- Malice requires the prior loss of regard for the other's worth and the prior development of enmity (G)

The positive inner-being complex of love + fear of the Lord + accountability is the structural barrier to the entire M06 cluster.

## T2 — Constitutional Location

### Cluster-level findings:

**Spirit level:** Silent across all sub-groups. The spirit (ruach) is not named as the constitutional location of any M06 characteristic. This is a cluster-level silence — none of the hostility/contempt/hatred characteristics operates at the spirit level.

**Soul level:** Evidenced in M06-A (God's soul and David's soul — hatred), M06-G (explicitly twice — malice within your soul). Partial evidence in M06-C (ta.av's physical resonance implies soul-level). Not directly named in B, D, E, F. The soul-location is not universal in M06 but is present in its most severe forms.

**Heart level:** Evidenced in M06-A (Lev 19:17 — "in your heart"), M06-B (Psa 10:13 — "say in his heart"), M06-E (Psa 69:20 — "broken my heart", Psa 89:50). The heart is the constitutional location most consistently evidenced across M06 sub-groups — but as different relationships: hatred *lives* in the heart; contempt *speaks* from the heart; reproach *breaks* the heart. The same organ, different relationships to it.

**Generational deposit:** Only M06-A (hatred of God — Exo 20:5). The generational deposit is not a cluster-wide finding but a characteristic-specific one, unique to hatred of God.

**Constitutional ground statement:** The most precise constitutional ground statement in M06 is Psa 54:3 (M06-D): "they do not set God before themselves." This names the inner-constitutional condition that produces ruthlessness. It is sub-group-specific but the most definitive ground statement for any M06 characteristic.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

### Cluster-level findings:

**Moral Evaluation (T3.8) — cluster-wide engagement:** Every sub-group in M06 engages the moral evaluation faculty — but differently. Hatred bypasses accurate moral evaluation of the hated person. Contempt renders a wrong worth-verdict. Abhorrence is the moral evaluation faculty's deepest response to genuine evil (rightly directed) or its inversion. Cruelty represents the moral evaluation faculty's failure to register the worth of what should trigger mercy. Malice represents the moral evaluation faculty's complete corruption (evaluating destruction as good). Reproach is what happens when others' wrong moral evaluation of the person is felt as a social-inner condition. Hostility generates the appeal to God as the moral arbiter.

**Cluster-level finding from T3.8:** M06 is a cluster in which the moral evaluation faculty is the most universally engaged inner faculty — and in every agent-side sub-group except M06-F, the faculty is operating in a disordered or inverted way. M06 is the cluster that documents the moral evaluation faculty's failure modes.

**Conscience (T3.9) — cluster-wide relevance:** Conscience is engaged in all sub-groups:
- A: righteous hatred of evil is conscience expressed as disposition; person-directed hatred suppresses conscience
- B: contempt of reproof is the attack on conscience's external channel
- C: abhorrence is conscience's deepest felt response; inverted abhorrence is conscience reversed
- D: cruelty is the condition of conscience's restraining function having been removed
- E: reproach is the social-relational consequence felt when conscience's verdict (deserved) or others' wrong verdict (undeserved) is delivered
- F: vigilance (1Pe 5:8 — "be sober-minded") is conscience's alertness under adversarial conditions
- G: malice is the conscience's most complete corruption

**Cluster-level finding from T3.9:** The conscience is the primary faculty through which M06 is either exercised properly (righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence), resisted (when reproach is borne faithfully), or corrupted (contempt of reproof, inverted abhorrence, malice, ruthlessness). M06 maps the spectrum of the conscience's ordered, disordered, and reversed operation.

**Creativity (T3.5) — absent across most sub-groups:** M06-A (hatred recruits imagination into planning harm) is the only sub-group where creativity is evidenced. M06-B, C, D, E, F, G are all silent on the creative faculty. This silence is a cluster-level finding: M06 characteristics are not primarily creative; they suppress, bypass, or destroy rather than originate.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

### Cluster-level findings:

**T4.2 Human to God — universal pattern:** Every sub-group in M06 has a human-to-God dimension, but the direction differs:
- A: hatred of God (disordered) vs hatred of evil aligned with God (righteous)
- B: contempt of God (disordered) vs reverence as the alternative
- C: abhorrence of evil as covenant alignment; inverted abhorrence as covenant violation
- D: ruthlessness as the inner condition of those who do not set God before themselves
- E: appeal to God from within the reproach condition
- F: appeal to God as advocate in the adversary relationship
- G: God's judgment of malice named in all three verses

**Cluster-level finding from T4.2:** The M06 cluster consistently generates the God-ward movement in one of two forms: (1) the disordered movement away from God (hatred/contempt of God), or (2) the appeal and alignment movement toward God (appeal in suffering, alignment of righteous hatred with God's own). The cluster is never neutral about the God-ward dimension.

**T4.4 Receiving — cluster asymmetry:** M06-E (Reproach) is uniquely the sub-group that exists in the receiving register. All other sub-groups are agent-side. This asymmetry is the most structurally significant finding about the cluster's relational architecture: M06 documents both what the inner being generates (A, B, C, D, G) and what it experiences as a result of what others generate (E), with F as the structural-relational framework within which both occur.

**T4.6 Spiritual beings — only in M06-F:** 1Pe 5:8 (the devil as antidikos) is the only verse in the entire M06 cluster that names a spiritual being as the active agent of a characteristic. The cluster is primarily a human inner-being cluster; the spiritual-beings interface is present at a single point and in the specific form of the adversary relationship.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental

### Cluster-level findings:

**T5.2 — Sequence:** Every agent-side sub-group in M06 has a development sequence — the characteristic does not appear fully formed but develops through identifiable stages. The stages differ across sub-groups but the pattern of progressive development is universal. M06 characteristics are developmental phenomena.

**T5.4 Suffering — universal relationship:** Every sub-group in M06 has a documented relationship with suffering — either as its cause (A, B, C, D, G produce suffering in recipients), its context (F — facing adversary is a suffering context), or its content (E — reproach is the primary suffering in the cluster). M06 and suffering are structurally inseparable.

**T5.5 Formation — consistent negative and occasional positive:** The formation/sanctification relationship is present across all sub-groups:
- All agent-side M06 characteristics (A, B, C, D, G) participate in negative formation — they shape the person away from the likeness of God
- A and C have commanded righteous forms that participate in positive formation
- E (reproach borne faithfully) participates in positive formation through suffering
- F (facing the adversary) develops vigilance and appeal as formative inner postures

**Cluster-level finding from T5.5:** M06 is a cluster with a consistent negative formative trajectory (holding these characteristics forms the person away from God's likeness) and a surprising positive formative trajectory (the righteous forms of A and C, and the faithful bearing of E, contribute to sanctification). The cluster is not uniformly destructive — it contains formative pathways.

**T5.6 Eschatological trajectory — universal but varied:** Every sub-group has an eschatological dimension. The trajectories cluster into three types:
1. **Removal** (D, F): the characteristic simply ceases — cruelty comes to nothing; adversarial opposition is defeated
2. **Reversal** (B, E): those who held contempt bow before the despised; reproach is removed and replaced by honour
3. **Completion** (A, C): righteous hatred and abhorrence reach their completion when evil is finally eliminated; divine hatred of covenant violators reaches its eschatological expression in final judgment

**G (malice)** is judged and brought to nothing — closer to type 1.

## T6 — Structural Relationships

### Cluster-level findings:

**T6.1 Co-occurrence — cluster-wide patterns:**
- Love (a.ha.vah) co-occurs across A, C, E, F — it is the most consistent co-occurring characteristic across M06
- Iniquity/sin (a.von, chet) co-occurs across A, B, C, D — the M06 characteristics and iniquity travel together
- Shame/dishonour (bo.shet, ka.lon) co-occurs across B, C, E — the social-honour register is consistently present alongside the cluster's characteristics
- Pride (ga.on, ge.ah) co-occurs with D and B — pride is named as a generative condition of contempt and ruthlessness

**T6.2 Sequential — internal cluster causality:** The most important sequential finding is the cross-sub-group causal chain: M06-A (hatred) → M06-B (contempt) → M06-G (malice). Hatred is the entry disposition. When hatred is accompanied by contempt, the compound of enmity and contempt provides the conditions for malice to emerge when another's destruction is encountered. The cluster has an internal developmental trajectory from its simplest to its most severe form.

The second major sequence is M06-A through D → M06-E: the agent-side characteristics (hatred, contempt, abhorrence misdirected, cruelty, malice) produce the recipient-side condition (reproach). The cluster's causal architecture connects the agent-side and recipient-side through this production relationship.

**T6.5 Distinctions — cluster-level taxonomy:** The distinctions established across the sub-group passes produce a clear taxonomy of the differences within M06:
- Hatred vs Contempt: relational hostility vs worth-evaluation (faculty distinction)
- Hatred vs Abhorrence: sustained disposition vs visceral recoil (mode and depth distinction)
- Contempt vs Abhorrence: cool dismissal vs visceral disgust (affective register distinction)
- Cruelty vs Hatred: character void vs relational disposition (kind distinction)
- Malice vs Hatred: compound with pleasure element vs simple hostile disposition (constituent distinction)
- Reproach vs all others: received condition vs held characteristic (structural position distinction)
- Hostility vs all others: relational structure vs inner disposition (architectural distinction)

These distinctions are not trivial. They name the precise ways in which the cluster members are related but different — the family resemblance structure of the cluster.

**T6.6 Shared anchors — cluster-level map:** Key shared anchors across multiple M06 sub-groups:
- Dan 12:2: M06-C (de.ra.on) and M06-E (cher.pah) — eschatological horizon shared
- Psa 69:9/20: M06-E and the christological register — the reproach-receiving pattern
- Psa 45:7: M06-A and Christological studies
- Lev 19:17-18: M06-A (hatred) and love studies — the boundary verse
- Amo 5:10: M06-A (hatred of truth-speaker) and M06-C (abhorrence of truth-speaker) — both appear in this verse

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological

### Cluster-level findings:

**T7.1 Vocabulary architecture — cluster-level observation:**
The M06 vocabulary is structured differently across sub-groups, revealing different lexical maturity levels:
- M06-A: highly differentiated vocabulary (5 terms covering act, disposition, persistence, atmosphere, person-type) — the most lexically developed characteristic in the cluster
- M06-B: moderately differentiated (6 terms across distinct registers)
- M06-C: directionally neutral primary term (ta.av) — unique in the cluster; the direction is supplied entirely by context
- M06-D: internally coherent family of 4 terms all covering the same quality at different levels of abstraction
- M06-E: recipient-side vocabulary — the largest verse count in the cluster (81 verses) with terms covering acute/chronic/individual/corporate forms
- M06-F: relational-category vocabulary — terms name positions, not dispositions
- M06-G: single rare term (she.at) — the most lexically specific characteristic in the cluster

**T7.3 Science — cluster-level scientific framework:**
The primary scientific field for M06 as a whole is **clinical and social psychology** (sub-groups A, B, C, D, G all draw primarily on this field). Secondary: **neuroscience** (abhorrence, reproach — where bodily resonance and social pain are key findings). **Moral psychology** (contempt, abhorrence, malice). **Developmental psychology** (formation of hostile patterns over time, across multiple sub-groups).

The most significant cluster-level scientific divergence: **clinical psychology has no category for the moral-evaluative forms of M06 characteristics** — the righteous hatred of evil, the righteous abhorrence of falsehood, the commanded adversary posture are all beyond the science's framework, which treats all hostile dispositions as pathological or as problems to be reduced. The verse evidence maintains a positive space for these characteristics that the science cannot see.

## Summary of cluster-level findings (prompts where all sub-groups agree or can be collapsed)

### Findings that apply at the cluster level (not sub-group-specific):

1. **The spirit level is silent across all M06 sub-groups.** None of the cluster's characteristics is located at the ruach level.

2. **The heart is the most consistently evidenced constitutional location** — but the relationship to the heart differs across sub-groups (hatred *lives* in the heart; contempt *speaks* from the heart; reproach *breaks* the heart).

3. **The moral evaluation faculty is engaged by every sub-group** — in disordered, inverted, absent, or corrupt form in every agent-side sub-group; in its proper receiving form (resisting the wrong verdict) in M06-E.

4. **Conscience is the primary faculty through which M06 characteristics are exercised, resisted, or corrupted** — the cluster maps the conscience's ordered, disordered, and reversed operation.

5. **Love (a.ha.vah) co-occurs with M06 characteristics across multiple sub-groups** — it is the structural opposite of the cluster as a whole, not merely of any single characteristic.

6. **The primary blocking condition for all agent-side M06 characteristics is the same**: love of what is good + fear of the Lord + accountability to God. This complex provides the structural barrier to the entire cluster.

7. **M06 characteristics systematically generate the appeal-posture in those who receive them.** The receiving end of M06 produces the cry to God for deliverance and advocacy. This is consistent across A (received hatred), D (under cruelty), E (reproach), F (facing adversary).

8. **Every agent-side M06 characteristic is developmentally progressive** — none appears fully formed; all develop through identifiable stages.

9. **All M06 characteristics are self-destructive in those who hold them** — Pro 11:17 (cruelty), Pro 26:26 (concealed hatred exposed), Hos 9:7 (accumulated hatred destroys prophetic capacity), etc.

10. **The cluster has an internal causal sequence**: Hatred (A) → Contempt (B) → [with pleasure element] → Malice (G). And: Agent-side characteristics (A, B, C, D, G) → Reproach in the recipient (E).

11. **M06 and suffering are inseparable** — every sub-group documents a relationship with suffering either as its cause, context, or content.

12. **The eschatological trajectory for the whole cluster** is the removal, reversal, or completion of these characteristics — none persists in the redeemed order except as the permanent condition of those who have definitively chosen them (de.ra.on — M06-C).

13. **Clinical and social psychology has no category for the righteous forms** of M06 characteristics. The verse evidence's positive space for righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence, and righteous adversarial posture is a cluster-level genuine divergence from the scientific lens.

14. **The cluster is a family, not a category.** It contains dispositions, a character void, a received condition, a relational structure, and a compound. They belong together because they are all in the inner-being territory organised against what is good, true, and valued — but they are not the same kind of thing.
