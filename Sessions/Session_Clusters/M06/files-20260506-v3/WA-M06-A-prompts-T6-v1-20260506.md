## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

### T6.1 — Co-occurrence

**T6.1.1** — Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?

**E** — Reading directly from the span data (the terms listed alongside the target term in each verse):

**Love (a.ha.vah / H0160)** — the most frequent co-occurring characteristic across M06-A. It appears in the span data of 2Sa 13:15 (hatred greater than love), Pro 10:12 (hatred stirs strife / love covers offences), Pro 15:17 (hatred vs love as household atmosphere), Psa 109:5 (hatred returned for love), Ecc 9:6 (love and hate as paired orientations), Amo 5:15 (hate evil / love good), Psa 45:7 (hated wickedness / loved righteousness), Psa 119:113 (hate double-mindedness / love your law), Psa 139:21-22 (hate those who hate God), Eze 16:37 (loved and hated), Judg 14:16 (you hate me / you do not love me). Love is the structural pair of hatred across the full corpus — they appear together in nearly every thematic register.

**Jealousy / Envy (qin.ah / H7068)** — appears in Ecc 9:6 ("their love and their hate and their envy") and Eze 35:11 ("anger and envy...because of your hatred"). Envy and hatred appear as co-occurring inner states in contexts of relational rivalry and national conflict.

**Iniquity / Sin (a.von / H5771, chet / H2399)** — appears in the span data of Exo 20:5 (those who hate me / visiting iniquity), Lev 19:17 (not hate / lest you incur sin), Hos 9:7 (great iniquity and great hatred), Psa 36:2 (his iniquity cannot be found out and hated). Iniquity and hatred are linked consistently — either hatred arises from iniquity, or hatred of evil is what exposes iniquity.

**Abomination (to.e.vah / H8441)** — appears with hatred in Deu 12:31 (things the Lord hates / abominable thing), Pro 6:16 (things the Lord hates / abomination), Jer 44:4 (abomination that I hate). The divine hatred of sin and the abomination-designation are structurally paired.

**Shame / Disgrace (bo.shet / H0954, ka.lon / H7036)** — appears with hatred in Job 8:22 (those who hate you will be clothed with shame), Psa 44:7 (put to shame those who hate us), Psa 86:17 (those who hate me...put to shame). The outcome of having hatred directed at you reversed by God is consistently shame falling on the hater.

**T6.1.2** — What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?

**E** — The co-occurrence pattern reveals three things:

First, hatred and love are structurally inseparable in the biblical vocabulary — they function as a pair throughout the corpus. They are not merely opposites but each defines the other's field. The verse evidence never presents hatred in a vacuum; it is almost always positioned against love as its structural counterpart.

Second, hatred's co-occurrence with envy (Ecc 9:6, Eze 35:11) suggests that hatred belongs to a family of characteristics organised around relational hostility and rivalry — not merely negative emotion but structured opposition to another's good.

Third, hatred's co-occurrence with shame as an outcome of the hater's exposure (Job 8:22, Psa 44:7, Psa 86:17) shows a consistent consequence-pattern: hatred directed at the righteous eventually produces shame in the hater, not in the hated. The structural reversal — the hater becoming the shamed — is a recurring outcome in the verse evidence.

### T6.2 — Sequential Relationships

**T6.2.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?

**E** — Two clear sequences visible from the verses:

**Sequence 1 — Hatred → Strife (M06 internal):** Pro 10:12: "Hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all offences." Hatred precedes and produces strife as its sequential consequence. The sequence is causal: the inner state (hatred) generates the social outcome (strife).

**Sequence 2 — Hatred → Deception:** Pro 10:18: "the one who conceals hatred has lying lips." Pro 26:24: "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips and harbours deceit in his heart." Hatred produces deception as the mechanism of its concealment. The concealed hatred recruits the speech capacity into dishonesty.

**Sequence 3 — Hatred → Violence / Murder (in legal contexts):** Deu 19:11: "if anyone hates his neighbour and lies in wait for him and attacks him and strikes him fatally." Num 35:20: "if he pushed him out of hatred...so that he died." The law treats hatred as what precedes and legally defines lethal violence — the sequence is: inner disposition (hatred) → deliberate act (lying in wait) → lethal outcome (murder).

**Sequence 4 — Moral understanding → Righteous Hatred:** Psa 119:104: "through your precepts I get understanding; therefore I hate every false way." Understanding precedes and produces righteous hatred. The sequence runs in the opposite direction from the disordered sequences above: the external input (precepts) generates the inner evaluation (understanding) which settles as a disposition (hatred of falsehood).

**T6.2.2** — What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?

**E** — The sequences are causal in both directions. Hatred causes strife, deception, and violence (in the disordered register). Understanding causes righteous hatred (in the ordered register). The causality in the legal contexts is explicit — the law treats hatred as the cause that transforms an act from manslaughter into murder. The causality in Psa 119:104 is grammatically explicit ("therefore"). These are not correlations but presented as productive relationships.

### T6.3 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships

**T6.3.1** — Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one and by what mechanism?

**E** — From the verse evidence:

**Hatred → Strife:** Pro 10:12 names hatred as the producer of strife. The mechanism is the breakdown of the relational capacity — hatred makes peaceful engagement impossible (Gen 37:4), which generates conflict.

**Hatred → Deception / Lying:** Pro 10:18, 26:24, 26:28. Concealed hatred produces lying lips as the mechanism of maintaining the false exterior. The lying tongue is named as the instrument of hatred's concealment.

**Hatred → Shame (in the hater):** Job 8:22: "those who hate you will be clothed with shame." Psa 44:7, 86:17: the hater is put to shame when God acts. The mechanism is divine reversal — what hatred intended for the hated falls back on the hater.

**Hatred → Spiritual blindness:** Hos 9:7 (group 1199): accumulated communal hatred produces "the prophet is a fool; the man of the spirit is mad." The mechanism is the corruption of the communal inner climate — hatred fills the space in which spiritual discernment operates.

**T6.3.2** — Is this characteristic produced by another?

**E** — Yes. From the verse evidence:

**Jealousy / perceived favouritism → Hatred:** Gen 37:4 — the brothers' jealousy of their father's love for Joseph produces hatred. The mechanism is the perception of a relational injury (being valued less) that generates hostility toward the perceived beneficiary.

**Violated honour / trust → Hatred:** Gen 27:41 (Esau, perceived theft of blessing), 2Sa 13:22 (Absalom, Tamar's violation). Moral injury — real or perceived — produces settled hatred when not resolved.

**God's action → Hatred (redirected):** Psa 105:25: "He turned their hearts to hate his people." An external divine action produces hatred in the Egyptians directed at Israel. This is a distinct producer — hatred introduced from outside the person's own experience.

**T6.3.3** — Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another constitute part of this one?

**E** — From the span data of M06-G (malice, she.at — though in M06-G not M06-A): Eze 35:11 lists "anger and envy...because of your hatred" — hatred is named as the ground from which malice operates. Within M06-A itself: sa.tam (group 1200 — grudge-bearing) is constituted by hatred plus persistence plus memory — it is a compound form in which hatred is a constituent element alongside the temporal dimension of the grudge.

Hatred is also part-constituent of contempt in some verse contexts — Pro 5:12: "how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof" — the hatred of discipline and the despising of reproof appear together as aspects of the same inner refusal, suggesting that in this context hatred and contempt are co-constituents of a single orientation of resistance to formation.

### T6.5 — Distinctions

**T6.5.1** — Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?

**E** — The nearest neighbour is **contempt (M06-B)**. Both are inner dispositions of the person turned against something. The distinction is precise from the verse evidence:

Hatred (M06-A) is a **relational hostility** — the inner being is set against a person or thing in sustained enmity. It is directional and personal. Gen 37:4: the brothers hated Joseph — their inner being was turned against him as a person.

Contempt (M06-B, ba.zah, na.ats) is a **worth-evaluation** — the inner being assigns low or no value to what it despises. Pro 5:12: "how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof" — this verse shows both operating in the same context but distinguishable: the hatred is the hostile orientation toward discipline as a relational encounter; the despising is the heart's evaluation of reproof as worthless.

The clearest distinguishing test: one can hate without despising (Absalom hated Amnon with moral force — he did not regard Amnon as worthless but as guilty). One can despise without hating (a person may despise wealth or status without being in sustained enmity with it). The distinction is between relational hostility and worth-evaluation.

**T6.5.2** — Where the evidence shows apparent overlap, what is the precise boundary?

**E** — The clearest overlap is in Pro 5:12 and in verses where the disordered form of each appears in the same person toward the same object. Psa 5:5-6 shows both divine hatred ("you hate all evildoers") and divine abhorrence ("the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man") in adjacent verses toward overlapping objects. In these cases the terms are not interchangeable — hatred names God's relational stance of opposition; abhorrence (ta.av) names the quality of God's moral recoil from what is repugnant to his character. They overlap in their object but differ in the faculty they engage.

**T6.5.3** — Is the distinction one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?

**E** — The distinction between hatred and contempt is primarily of **kind** — they engage different inner faculties (relational capacity vs moral evaluation faculty). The distinction between hatred and abhorrence is primarily of **kind** as well — relational hostility vs visceral moral recoil — though they often co-occur toward the same object (Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood"). The distinction is not of degree (one being a stronger form of the other) but of what aspect of the inner being is engaged.
