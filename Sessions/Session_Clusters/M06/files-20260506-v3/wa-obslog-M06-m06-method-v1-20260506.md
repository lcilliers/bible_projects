### Step 2: The missing terms — what they reveal

**Six terms were not assigned in the original debate:** cha.raph, ba.ash, tsa.rar, ra.tsad, afilagathos, sho.tet.

**Additionally, nine terms were assigned with uncertainty (marked *).** Reading their actual group descriptions against the groupings reveals the following:

**H8146 sa.ni (hated)** — Group 1204: "state of being disfavoured or unloved — an inner relational orientation of lesser love or active disfavour that carries legal and relational consequence." This is not a disposition of the agent; it is a condition of the recipient. The group description names it as the experience of being on the receiving end of hate. This does not fit Group A (hatred as agent's disposition). It belongs with Group E (reproach/received condition) — or it may constitute a distinct sub-form: *being hated as an inner relational condition*. The Deuteronomy 21 context (the unloved wife) shows that being hated/disfavoured has its own inner-being shape — the person lives under a relational condition that shapes their existence. **Observation: sa.ni sits closer to E than to A.**

**H7590 shut (to despise)** — Group 3054: "contempt of surrounding nations toward Israel — and God's judgment that removes and avenges that contempt." This is contempt as a *received* corporate condition, not an agent-held disposition. The agent is the nations; Israel is the object. The inner-being content is in both the nations' contempt (agent) and Israel's experience of being despised (recipient). **Observation: shut has a dual register — agent-contempt (B) and received contempt (E). Its one verse sits firmly in the received/corporate register.**

**H7589 she.at (scorn)** — Group 3042: "malice as an inner disposition of the soul — the soul's rejoicing in another's destruction, driven by deep-seated enmity and contempt." This is not merely scorn or contempt — it is *malice*: the soul actively rejoicing in destruction. This is a distinct inner-being phenomenon. Malice combines hatred (A) and contempt (B) into a compound that has its own character: it is contempt that takes pleasure in destruction. **Observation: she.at may name a compound state — malice — that sits at the intersection of A and B but is neither alone. It could warrant recognition as a distinct form within the family.**

**H0963 biz.za.von (contempt)** — Group 3046: "contempt as social-inner contagion — one act of defiance spreading the inner disposition of contempt through a whole society." This names a *social dynamic* of contempt — how it propagates from one person's act to an entire social system. The inner-being content is real (contempt is the disposition spreading) but the distinctive feature is its contagious, systemic character. **Observation: biz.za.von belongs in B but names a dimension of contempt not captured in the B description — its social propagation.**

**H5007B ne.a.tsah (contempt)** — Group 153: "blasphemous contempt as the inner disposition of rebellion — the speech-act of reviling that reveals the heart's rejection of what is holy." This is contempt directed specifically at God through blasphemous speech. The group ties it to the heart's inner state of rejection. **Observation: belongs clearly in B, with a specific divine-directed sub-form.**

**H5007A ne.a.tsah (contempt)** — Group 154: "disgrace as an inner condition in extremity — the shame-experience accompanying crisis that overwhelms all capacity for response." This is not contempt held by an agent — it is disgrace *experienced* in a crisis state. The two 2Ki/Isa verses (Hezekiah's crisis: "a day of distress, rebuke, and disgrace") show the term functioning as the inner experience of being in a condition of crisis-shame. **Observation: ne.a.tsah A belongs firmly in E (experienced condition), not B.**

**H7009 qim (adversary)** — Group 3025: "adversary as the object of God's righteous judgment — and the inner response of the person whose enemy is dealt with by God." This names the adversary but the inner-being content is in the *response* of the person — the relief, vindication, or gratitude when God acts against their enemy. **Observation: qim generates inner-being content in the one who *has* an adversary, not in the adversary itself. It belongs with F but note the distinctive angle: it is about the inner experience of having one's enemies judged.**

**G5195 hubrizō (to mistreat)** — Group 2034: "mistreatment violating inner-relational dignity — experience of shame through insolent treatment, endured by servants and apostles." This is entirely from the recipient's perspective — the inner experience of having one's dignity violated. **Observation: belongs firmly in E.**

**H8263 she.qets (detestation)** — Group 127 and new group 3591. Group 127: "objects of idolatrous inner devotion — detestable things toward which misdirected worship is oriented." Group 3591: "commanded inner reaction of detestation — worshipper's required inner-being orientation of revulsion toward unclean objects." Two directions: the objects of detestation (group 127) and the commanded inner orientation of detestation (group 3591). **Observation: group 3591 (commanded revulsion) belongs clearly in C. Group 127 (objects of misdirected worship) is an abomination cluster term — it may sit at the boundary of M06 scope. The term has a foot in both registers.**

**H8374 ta.av (to loathe)** — Group 128: "divine inner loathing directed specifically at pride — God abhors arrogance of Jacob." One verse, one group. This is divine abhorrence of a specific inner-being quality (pride/arrogance). **Observation: belongs in C, with the distinctive note that this is God's revulsion directed at a *human inner-being quality* (pride) rather than at an act or object.**

### What changed — the methodological difference

The previous approach (as documented in the session log for Registry 077 honesty/righteousness, and referenced in the global correction made in late April 2026) processed verses by classifying them against pre-formed categories. The analytical output was built from the classifications, not from the verses. This produced findings that were coherent in structure but not grounded in what the texts actually said. The fluency of the output concealed the gap between simulated and genuine analytical work.

The present approach reversed the sequence:

1. **Read first.** Every verse is read from its text. The verse text is authoritative. Memory is never authoritative. The document containing the verse text is consulted before any observation is recorded.

2. **Group from reading.** Groups are formed from what the reading finds, not assigned from pre-formed categories. When 164 verses of sa.ne produced five distinct phenomena, that discovery came from reading — not from the label "hatred" being applied to all of them.

3. **Write on discovery.** Every observation is written as it is made. Nothing is accumulated in memory and reconstructed later. The obslog is the live working surface.

4. **Apply prompts to differentiated evidence.** The 189 catalogue prompts were applied to groups (13 differentiated groups for M06-A) not to an undifferentiated mass of 164 verses. This meant each prompt could be answered precisely — because the evidence being interrogated was a coherent group with a defined anchor, not an averaged-out aggregate.

5. **Record silence as silence.** Where the verses do not address a prompt, the honest answer is "S — silent." This discipline prevents the gap-filling that produced the previous failures. Several T2 prompts (spirit-level location) and several T7 prompts (LXX mapping, dimensional sharing counts) are recorded as S or G rather than answered from assumption.

The previous approach would have produced one group for sa.ne, one set of answers for the 189 prompts, and a single description of "hatred" that averaged five distinct phenomena into one undifferentiated account. The account would have been internally consistent but analytically false.

The present approach produced:
- Five distinct phenomena within sa.ne alone, each with its own anchor verse, direction, faculty engagement, and consequence
- A seven-stage developmental sequence that is traceable to specific verses at each stage
- A genuine scientific divergence (commanded righteous hatred has no category in clinical psychology) that the previous approach would never have surfaced — because the previous approach would not have differentiated group 550-NEW-02 from group 1601
- The identification of Lev 19:17 as the primary anchor for a specific reason: it uniquely names the constitutional location, the alternative, and the culpability of the inner state before any act — three things no other verse does simultaneously
- A precise statement of the relationship between moral evaluation and hatred: the quality of hatred is entirely downstream of the quality of the moral evaluation that grounds it. This is a structural claim about how the inner being works that is directly traceable to the verse evidence.

These are findings. They could not have been produced by the previous method.
