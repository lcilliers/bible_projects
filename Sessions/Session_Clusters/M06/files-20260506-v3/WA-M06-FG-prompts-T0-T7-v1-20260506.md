# M06-F — Hostility/Enmity

## Opening structural observation

M06-F has 9 verses across 4 groups. Its defining structural feature is that the inner-being content throughout resides *in the person facing the adversary*, not in the adversary's own inner state. The adversary/opponent terms name a relational category — the position of another person relative to the self — rather than naming an inner disposition of that person. The inner-being phenomena generated by M06-F are: appeal, urgency, vigilance, and relief — all in the one who faces the adversary, not in the adversary. This makes M06-F behave differently from every other sub-group in the cluster: it names a relational structure that generates inner responses rather than naming the inner dispositions of those who exercise hostility.

---

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1.1** — God's nature reflected?

**E** — Exo 23:22 (anchor for group 7009-001, a.yav): "I will be an enemy to your enemies and an adversary to your adversaries." God explicitly adopts the adversary posture on Israel's behalf. This is a significant self-attribution: God positions himself in the adversary relationship in service of covenant faithfulness. It reveals that the adversary posture is not intrinsically wrong — God can take it up. What makes an adversary relationship godly or ungodly is not the structure of opposition but what is being opposed and why.

1Pe 5:8 (antidikos): the devil is named as the adversary — the structured relational opposition of an adversarial spiritual being. God's own adversary posture in Exo 23:22 is the covenantal mirror of this.

**T0.1.2** — Explicitly attributed to God?

**E** — Yes. Exo 23:22 is explicit: God will be an enemy (a.yav) and an adversary to Israel's adversaries. God taking up the adversary posture is covenantal, responsive, and purposive — adopted in service of his people's protection, not as an originating inner disposition of hostility.

**T0.1.3** — What does God's adoption of this posture reveal?

**E** — That structural opposition can be an expression of love and covenant faithfulness. God positions himself as adversary not from hatred but from commitment. The adversary posture in God's case is entirely determined by its object and purpose — he becomes adversary to those who threaten his covenant people. This reveals that M06-F names a relational structure, not a moral quality in itself.

**T0.2.1** — Created purpose?

**E** — Isa 49:25: "I will contend with those who contend with you" — the contention (ya.riv) serves the purpose of justice and deliverance for the oppressed. The adversary relationship as context generates the appeal to God and the expectation of divine advocacy. Its created purpose is not the hostility itself but the relational structure that creates the context for appeal, justice, and covenant vindication.

**T0.2.2-3** — Design and future orientation?

**E** — The adversary structure belongs to fallen relational existence — God's adoption of the adversary posture (Exo 23:22) is responsive to the existence of enemies, not part of creation's original design. The future orientation: 1Pe 5:8's adversary (the devil) has a finite scope — the adversarial spiritual opposition will be defeated, and with it the adversary structure that generates the need for vigilance.

**T0.3.1-3** — Image-bearer expression?

**E** — The capacity to stand in structured opposition on behalf of justice and the oppressed reflects the divine image as exercised by God in Exo 23:22. The image is expressed not through the possession of hostility as an inner quality but through the capacity to take up structured opposition when covenant faithfulness requires it. The absence of the inner-being response (appeal, vigilance) to genuine adversarial opposition would indicate an image-bearing capacity for advocacy that is dormant.

**T0.4.1-3** — Typological use?

**E** — 1Pe 5:8's naming of the devil as antidikos (adversary/accuser) places M06-F in the cosmic spiritual-conflict register. The adversary structure that operates at the human-legal level (Mat 5:25, Luk 12:58, 18:3) is typologically connected to the cosmic adversary register. The legal opponent in human life points toward the cosmic spiritual adversary; the appropriate response (urgency, vigilance, appeal) operates in both registers. The typological direction is from human-legal adversary → cosmic spiritual adversary → the call to sober vigilance.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1 — Name and Naming**

"Hostility/Enmity" names a relational structure of structured opposition — the adversary relationship — rather than an inner disposition held by an agent. The name captures both the objective relational structure (the person is in the position of adversary) and the inner quality of the relationship (hostile, opposed, in contest). The double label signals that M06-F covers both: the structured legal-relational form (enmity as position) and the inner quality of opposition (hostility as orientation).

**T1.1.2** — Primary terms:

**H3401 ya.riv** — "adversary, one who contends" — names the person who contends against another in legal or relational conflict. The root carries the sense of legal contention — the ya.riv is the opponent in a legal dispute. Three verses, all in the appeal register (Psa 35:1, Isa 49:25, Jer 18:19).

**G0476 antidikos** — "adversary, opponent, accuser" — the Greek legal term for the opposing party. Four verses: two legal (Mat 5:25, Luk 12:58), one human-appeal (Luk 18:3), one cosmic (1Pe 5:8 — the devil as antidikos). The most diverse term across registers.

**H0340 a.yav** — "enemy, to be hostile" — one verse only (Exo 23:22). The root names hostile disposition as a relational-moral stance. God adopts this stance toward Israel's enemies.

**H7009 qim** — "adversary" — one verse only (Job 22:20). The adversary as the object of divine judgment; the inner response is relief at the adversary's destruction.

**T1.1.3** — Directional implications:

**E** — M06-F is constitutively relational — the adversary relationship always involves two parties in structured opposition. Unlike the other M06 sub-groups which name one party's inner disposition, M06-F names the relational structure between parties. The inner-being content is generated *by* the structure in the one who faces it, rather than being the structure itself.

**T1.2 — Kind**

**E** — M06-F is a **relational category** that generates inner-being responses rather than an inner-being disposition. It is structurally different from all other M06 sub-groups. The ya.riv, antidikos, a.yav, and qim terms all name a position or role in a relational structure, not an inner quality of the person occupying that position. The inner qualities generated — appeal (Psa 35:1), urgency (Mat 5:25), vigilance (1Pe 5:8), relief (Job 22:20) — are inner-being responses to the relational structure, not the structure itself.

**T1.3 — Boundary**

**E** — The structural opposite of the adversary relationship is the covenantal-companion or advocate relationship — God contending *for* rather than *against*. Exo 23:22 shows this most clearly: God as adversary to Israel's adversaries is simultaneously God as advocate for Israel. The adversary relationship and the advocacy relationship are structural opposites from the same root (a.yav, ya.riv are used for both God's opposition to enemies and the enemy's opposition to Israel).

**T1.4 — Modes of Operation**

**E** — Three modes:

1. **Legal/judicial mode (antidikos):** Mat 5:25, Luk 12:58, 18:3 — the adversary in a legal dispute. The mode generates urgency and the call to settle before judgment arrives.

2. **Covenantal-advocacy mode (a.yav, ya.riv):** Exo 23:22, Psa 35:1, Isa 49:25 — God as adversary on behalf of the covenant person. The mode generates appeal and confidence in divine advocacy.

3. **Cosmic-spiritual mode (antidikos):** 1Pe 5:8 — the devil as the adversary who prowls seeking to devour. The mode generates sober watchfulness and the need for resistance grounded in faith.

**T1.5 — Immediate Response**

**E** — The immediate inner response to the adversary relationship is consistently **appeal** — to God, to the judge, to a mediator. Psa 35:1: "contend, O Lord, with those who contend with me." Luk 18:3: "give me justice against my adversary." Jer 18:19: "hear me, O Lord, and listen to the voice of my adversaries." The person facing an adversary immediately turns outward for advocacy and justice. The characteristic generates the appeal-posture as its primary inner response.

**T1.6 — Sustained Effect**

**E** — Sustained operation of the adversary relationship produces vigilance (1Pe 5:8: "be sober-minded; be watchful") and sustained appeal (Luk 18:3: "kept coming to him"). The sustained effect of facing an adversary is a heightened inner alertness and persistent orientation toward the advocate. For the adversary's victims: Job 22:20's relief at the adversary's defeat names the inner release from the sustained condition of being opposed.

**T1.7 — Conditions of Reception**

**E** — The adversary relationship is not chosen — it is entered into when another person or power takes up the adversarial posture toward the person. The condition that makes it bearable is having an advocate who can contend on one's behalf (Exo 23:22, Isa 49:25). The condition that makes it most threatening is facing the adversary without an adequate advocate — which is why 1Pe 5:8's cosmic adversary generates the command to active spiritual resistance rather than passive endurance.

**T1.8 — Dimension Classification**

**E** — **Relational Disposition** is the primary dimension — M06-F names a relational structure of structured opposition. Unlike M06-A through E where the relational dimension is secondary or tertiary, in M06-F the relational structure is the primary feature. **Agency/Power** is secondary — the adversary relationship is characteristically one of power contest, and the inner responses it generates (appeal, vigilance) are responses to a power differential.

# M06-G — Malice

## Opening structural observation

M06-G has 3 verses, 1 group, 1 term (she.at), all from Ezekiel, all naming the inner disposition of nations toward Israel's destruction. The verse evidence is thin in count but unusually precise in constitutional location: Eze 25:6 locates the malice "within your soul" (nephesh) — the most explicit soul-location of any characteristic in the M06 cluster. Despite the thin count, the three verses establish the characteristic with clarity.

The compound nature of malice (as identified in the control read) is confirmed by the three verses: Eze 25:6 shows the pleasure (rejoicing, clapping); Eze 25:15 shows the enmity (never-ending, purposive destruction); Eze 36:5 shows the contempt (utter contempt, wholehearted joy). Hostility + contempt + soul-level pleasure in destruction are all present across the three verses.

---

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**T0.1** — **S** — Malice (she.at) is never attributed to God in M06-G. God appears in these verses only as the one who names and judges the malice of the nations. The characteristic is not shared between God and the image-bearer — it is exclusively a condition of moral disorder, with no righteous or divine form. The divine response to malice is judgment (Eze 25:6-7: "I have stretched out my hand against you...that you may know that I am the Lord").

**T0.2** — **S** — No created purpose for malice. Like M06-D's cruelty, malice has no righteous form and appears entirely as a fallen condition. Unlike cruelty (which is a void), malice is an active compound — it is the presence of something genuinely evil (pleasure in another's destruction) rather than the absence of something good.

**T0.3** — **E** — Malice is among the most pronounced expressions of image-distortion in M06. The divine image includes the capacity for genuine rejoicing — but it is the rejoicing in what is good, beautiful, and true. Malice inverts this: it is the soul's rejoicing in destruction, which is the most complete inversion of the image-bearing capacity for joy. The soul was made to rejoice in life; malice rejoices in its ending.

**T0.4** — **S** — No typological use evidenced.

## T1 — Definition

**T1.1 Name and Naming** — "Malice" names the compound inner disposition that takes pleasure in the destruction of another. The name carries both the ancient (Latin malitia — badness, wickedness) and biblical sense: the soul's active rejoicing in harm done to another, driven by hatred and contempt operating together. Unlike the other M06 characteristics which are single-register (hatred, contempt, abhorrence, cruelty), malice is specifically compound — it requires the co-presence of multiple hostile orientations.

**T1.1.2 Primary term:** H7589 she.at — appears only in these three verses. The root carries the sense of malice, wickedness, spite. The term is rare in the Hebrew Bible, which is consistent with malice being a specific, recognisable inner-being reality that required its own term precisely because it was not identical with any of the adjacent characteristics.

**T1.2 Kind** — **E** — Malice is a **compound disposition** — constituted by the simultaneous operation of hostility (enmity, never-ending opposition), contempt (utter contempt), and affective pleasure (rejoicing, wholehearted joy) in the destruction of another. It is not a simple emotion or single disposition but a compound that produces something qualitatively different from its constituent parts. The pleasure is the element that distinguishes malice from hatred (which does not take pleasure in harm) and from contempt (which dismisses but does not celebrate destruction).

**T1.2.3 Working description:** Malice (M06-G) is a compound inner disposition of the soul — the simultaneous operation of sustained enmity, contempt for the other's worth, and active pleasure in their destruction — explicitly located in the soul (nephesh), producing bodily expression (clapping, stamping), and characterised by self-perpetuating intensity (the pleasure renews itself in the continued destruction). It has no righteous form, no created purpose, and no partial expression — it is constitutively what it is when its three constituent elements are simultaneously present.

**T1.3 Boundary** — **E** — The structural opposite of malice is compassion — the inner disposition that takes pain in another's suffering rather than pleasure. The compound opposite would require: solidarity rather than enmity, recognition of worth rather than contempt, and sorrow at harm rather than joy. Compassion is the compound characteristic that is the full opposite of malice's compound structure.

**T1.4 Modes of Operation** — **E** — One mode, but with distinctive features:

1. **Soul-level pleasure in destruction:** The primary mode. Eze 25:6: "rejoiced with all the malice within your soul" — the soul's positive affective orientation toward another's suffering.
2. **Purposive destruction:** Eze 25:15: "took vengeance with malice of soul to destroy in never-ending enmity" — the malice drives toward active destruction as its goal.
3. **Contempt-compound:** Eze 36:5: "wholehearted joy and utter contempt" — the joy and the contempt are simultaneous and inseparable.

**T1.5 Immediate Response** — **E** — The immediate expression of malice is bodily: Eze 25:6 — "you have clapped your hands and stamped your feet." The inner soul-level pleasure immediately expresses through physical celebration. The body enacts what the soul is experiencing. This is the most somatic inner-expression pattern in M06 — the soul's malice produces visible physical jubilation.

**T1.6 Sustained Effect** — **E** — Eze 25:15: "never-ending enmity" — malice is self-perpetuating. The pleasure in destruction renews itself with each new occasion of the other's suffering. Unlike hatred (which may diminish without fresh provocation) or cruelty (which is a character condition), malice sustains itself through the ongoing satisfaction it draws from the continued harm of its object. The sustained effect is a deepening inner orientation toward the other's destruction as the source of the soul's pleasure.

**T1.7 Conditions** — **E** — Sustained enmity toward another, combined with the loss of regard for their worth (contempt), and the acquisition of a twisted inner pleasure from the reversal of their fortunes. The three constituent elements must be simultaneously present for malice to be present in its full form. Malice is not produced in a moment but through the sustained coexistence of all three inner orientations.

**T1.8 Dimension** — **E** — **Emotion — Negative** is the primary dimension — specifically, the negative affective orientation of the soul that takes pleasure in harm. But uniquely in M06-G, the characteristic also carries **Relational Disposition** (the enmity) and **Moral Character** (the contempt) as constituent rather than secondary dimensions. Malice is the only characteristic in M06 where three dimensions are constitutive rather than primary and secondary.

**T3.8 Moral Evaluation** — **E** — The moral evaluation faculty has been so thoroughly inverted that destruction is evaluated as good and the other's loss is evaluated as gain. The moral evaluation faculty is corrupted at its most fundamental level: what should produce sorrow produces joy.

**T3.11 Relational Capacity** — **E** — Malice destroys the relational capacity toward the object of malice completely. The one who rejoices in another's destruction has no residual relational connection toward them — the other exists only as the occasion for pleasure in their suffering. The relational capacity has been perverted into its opposite: what was made for genuine connection now orients toward the other's destruction with joy.

The "never-ending enmity" of Eze 25:15 meets divine judgment that ends it. The characteristic that claimed perpetuity is met by the only perpetual reality — divine justice.

## T6 — Structural Relationships

**T6.1 Co-occurrence** — **E** — Eze 35:11 (sin.ah, M06-A) names "anger and envy...because of your hatred" alongside malice-adjacent vocabulary — showing that malice shares verse-space with hatred and envy as compound co-dispositions. Eze 36:5 pairs "wholehearted joy and utter contempt" — showing malice and contempt (M06-B) as simultaneous orientations.

**T6.3 Constitutive** — **E** — Malice is constituted by elements from M06-A (enmity), M06-B (contempt), and a unique additional element (pleasure in destruction) that is not present in either alone. It is the only characteristic in M06 that is constituted by elements from two other sub-groups plus a distinctive additional element. ## T7 — Evidential and Methodological

**T7.1 Lexical** — H7589 she.at appears only in these three verses — making it the rarest lexical item in M06. The rarity is not incidental: malice as a compound is a specific and recognisable inner-being condition that required naming precisely because it was not reducible to its constituent parts. The root's sense of spite/malice is coherent with its three-verse usage. No structural opposite term exists in the M06 vocabulary — the opposite (compassion) belongs to a different cluster.

**T7.2 Literary** — All three verses are prophetic judgment oracles from Ezekiel directed at specific nations. The literary form is the oracle of judgment — God naming the inner condition and pronouncing its consequence. The form is significant: malice is not described from inside (as Psa 69's reproach is described) but observed and named from outside by God. We see it through divine address, not through the inner testimony of the one who holds it. **Primary anchor:** Eze 25:6 — it names the constitutional location ("within your soul"), the compound structure (rejoiced with all the malice), and the bodily expression (clapped hands, stamped feet). It is the most anatomically complete statement of the characteristic in the corpus.

**T7.3 Science** — Primary field: **moral psychology and aggression research** — specifically the psychology of Schadenfreude (pleasure in another's misfortune) and revenge. Convergence: research on Schadenfreude documents that pleasure in another's suffering is a distinct psychological phenomenon, associated with prior feelings of inferiority or envy toward the suffering person, and characterised by the activation of reward pathways in the brain — the pleasure is neurologically real. This maps directly onto M06-G: the nations' malice toward Israel combines prior enmity (inferiority/envy-adjacent, especially in Edom's case) with the reward of seeing the powerful brought low. Divergence: the "never-ending enmity" (Eze 25:15) describes a depth and persistence of malice that goes beyond Schadenfreude research's typical scope — the science works with situational pleasure in another's misfortune, not with a constitutive soul-level orientation toward perpetual enmity and destructive pleasure. The verse evidence names something more total than the science's category.
