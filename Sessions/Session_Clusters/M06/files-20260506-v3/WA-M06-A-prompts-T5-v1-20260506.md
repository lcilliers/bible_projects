## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

### T5.1 — Nature of Transformation

**T5.1.1** — Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person — and if so, does it change the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both?

**E** — Both, but in opposite directions depending on the register.

In the disordered register (groups 1601, 1200): hatred transforms the person's condition by progressively narrowing the inner life around the object of hatred. Gen 37:4-8 shows the transformation unfolding in steps — each new provocation deepens the hatred ("they hated him even more"), and what begins as a response to perceived favouritism settles into an inner organising principle that makes peaceful speech impossible. By Gen 37:18-20 the brothers are plotting murder — the inner condition has been transformed by the sustained hatred. The person has not merely acquired an attitude; they have become organised differently inside.

In the righteous register (group 550-NEW-02): hatred of evil transforms the person's orientation toward their condition. The person who genuinely hates evil is not merely choosing differently in individual moments — they are oriented differently toward the entire moral landscape. Psa 45:7 names this as the ground of anointing: "you have loved righteousness and hated wickedness; therefore God, your God, has anointed you." The inner orientation of the righteous king has been formed in a particular direction, and that formation has consequence.

**T5.1.2** — Is the transformation reversible or irreversible in the verse evidence?

**E** — In the disordered register: the evidence is largely silent on reversal within M06-A. No verse in this sub-group describes a person moving from sustained hatred of a person to love or reconciliation of that person. The closest is Gen 50:15-21 — Joseph's brothers fear his hatred, but Joseph himself moves toward them in forgiveness (though his inner journey is not described using M06-A terms). The transformation that hatred produces appears to be highly resistant to reversal — it reshapes the inner life in a direction that requires counter-formation to undo.

In the eschatological frame: Psa 81:15 shows that the person whose defining inner orientation is hatred of God will "cringe toward him" at the end — but that is exposure and collapse, not reconciliation. The fate "lasts forever." The verse implies that a hatred settled deeply enough becomes irreversible at the final horizon.

**T5.1.3** — If the evidence is silent on transformation, note explicitly.

Not applicable — transformation is evidenced.

### T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States

**T5.2.1** — Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person?

**E** — Yes, and the sequence is visible across multiple groups.

The most complete sequence is in group 1601, traceable through the Joseph narrative and through the Proverbs wisdom material:

1. **Triggering perception** — Gen 37:4: "when his brothers saw that their father loved him more than all his brothers." Something is perceived as an injury or injustice.
2. **Inner settlement** — Gen 37:4: "they hated him." The perception has produced a settled inner state.
3. **Surface manifestation** — Gen 37:4: "they could not speak peacefully to him." The inner state affects outward behaviour, making normal relational exchange impossible.
4. **Intensification** — Gen 37:5, 8: each new provocation ("they hated him even more") deepens the disposition.
5. **Concealment** — Pro 26:24: "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips." As social pressure requires, the surface presentation diverges from the inner state.
6. **Volitional organisation** — Gen 27:41 (sa.tam): "then I will kill my brother Jacob." The hatred recruits the will into a plan.
7. **Action or exposure** — Pro 26:26: "his wickedness will be exposed in the assembly." The inner state eventually surfaces through either action or exposure.

**T5.2.2** — What are those states, and what does the sequence reveal about how this characteristic works?

**E** — The sequence reveals that hatred is not static. It is a developing inner-being reality that moves through identifiable phases, each building on the previous. The critical transition is between stages 2 and 3 — once the inner state has settled, the relational consequences begin to appear even before the person fully chooses to act. The surface manifestation (inability to speak peacefully) is not a deliberate act but a symptom — the inner disposition is already shaping behaviour before the will has consciously organised around it.

The second critical transition is between stages 3-4 and stage 5 — the move to concealment. This is where the characteristic becomes most dangerous: the hatred continues developing internally while the exterior presents normalcy. The gap between inner state and external presentation widens, and the inner state continues to intensify without the corrective pressure that honest relational engagement would provide.

**T5.2.3** — If the evidence is silent on sequence, note explicitly.

Not applicable — sequence is evidenced.

### T5.3 — Mechanism of Change

**T5.3.1** — What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change in the person?

**E** — The primary mechanism is **progressive inner organisation around the object of hatred**. The characteristic works by recruiting each inner faculty toward a single hostile orientation:
- Perception is narrowed to register injury-evidence
- Memory preserves the wound
- Cognition is recruited into concealment
- Imagination generates plans of harm
- Volition is organised toward a hostile end

This is not a sudden catastrophe but a progressive consolidation. The Joseph narrative shows it across time — the brothers' inner lives become increasingly organised around their hatred of Joseph until it produces the act of selling him into slavery.

In the righteous register (group 550-NEW-02), the mechanism is the same but the direction is different: progressive inner organisation around moral truth. Understanding increases → the hatred of falsehood deepens → the person becomes more consistently oriented toward truth. Psa 119:104: "through your precepts I get understanding; therefore I hate every false way" — this is a cumulative sequence, not a one-time event.

**T5.3.2** — Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?

**E** — Yes. In the person-directed register: the mechanism is largely driven by the self — the person's own perception, memory, and imagination sustain and intensify the hatred without requiring external input. In the communal register (group 1199, mas.te.mah): the mechanism is social — communal hatred is self-reinforcing across a community, producing a shared inner climate. In the divinely-redirected case (Psa 105:25: "he turned their hearts to hate his people"): the mechanism is external — a power outside the person reorganises the inner orientation.

**T5.3.3** — If the evidence is silent on mechanism, note explicitly.

Not applicable — mechanism is evidenced.

### T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction

**T5.4.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering or affliction?

**E** — Extensively. Hatred is one of the primary causes of suffering in the M06-A corpus. The psalms of lament (groups 1601, 3596) are suffused with the experience of being hated: Psa 69:4 ("those who hate me without cause"), Psa 25:19 ("many are those who hate me"), Psa 38:19 ("many are those who hate me wrongfully"). The suffering is named precisely — it is the experience of being on the receiving end of another's sustained hostile disposition.

In the Job material (group 1200, sa.tam): Job 16:9 names God as the perceived source of persistent hostile treatment — "he has torn me in his wrath and hated me; he has gnashed his teeth at me." Job's suffering is experienced as emanating from a hatred directed at him, even when the subject is God. The experience of suffering can produce the perception of being hated, whether or not actual hatred is the cause.

**T5.4.2** — Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce this characteristic in the person?

**E** — Suffering can reveal pre-existing hatred (Gen 50:15: the brothers' fear of Joseph's anticipated hatred reveals that they assume suffering produces retaliation, because that is how hatred works in them), and suffering can precipitate hatred where an underlying wound was not resolved (2Sa 13:22: Absalom's hatred of Amnon, sustained in silence while he waited for opportunity — the suffering of Tamar's violation became the ground of Absalom's settled enmity).

The clearest case of suffering producing hatred is Deu 1:27 (group 1202): Israel's suffering in the wilderness produced the false attribution of divine hatred — "because the Lord hated us he has brought us out of the land of Egypt." Suffering here generates the interpretation that one is hated, even when the suffering has a different source. Suffering distorts the perceptive faculty in the direction of reading the world through the lens of hatred.

**T5.4.3** — If the evidence is silent on the relationship to suffering, note explicitly.

Not applicable — the relationship to suffering is a major theme of M06-A.

### T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification

**T5.5.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic participating in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification?

**E** — In the righteous register (group 550-NEW-02): directly and positively. The commanded hatred of evil is not merely a one-time posture but a forming orientation. Exo 18:21 shows it as a qualification — men who "hate a bribe" are those whose character has been formed in a particular direction; the hatred of corruption is a mark of formed integrity, not merely a decision. Psa 45:7 shows it as the ground of anointing — the king's formation toward righteousness and against wickedness is what qualifies him for his role. This is formational language: the inner orientation has been shaped over time into a stable character quality.

In the disordered register (group 1601, 1200): the characteristic participates in formation in the negative direction. Pro 26:24-26 describes a person whose character has been formed around concealed hatred — the lying lips, the deceiving heart, the eventual exposure are all marks of a character that hatred has shaped over time into a particular kind of person. The formation is real, but it is formation toward corruption rather than sanctification.

**T5.5.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?

**E** — Hatred in the righteous register is a negative marker in the sanctification process — it names what the forming person is moving away from, and it provides the inner energy for that movement. The person being sanctified does not merely abstain from falsehood in a passive sense; they hate it, which means the opposition to falsehood has become constitutional rather than merely volitional. Pro 13:5: "the righteous hates falsehood" — this is a character description, not a behaviour description. The sanctified person has been formed into someone for whom the rejection of falsehood is an inner-being reality, not an effort.

In the disordered register: hatred's role in the negative formation arc is as the organising centre around which everything else is bent. Pro 5:12: "how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof!" — the retrospective confession of the person who resisted formation names the hatred of discipline as what closed them to the forming process. Hatred of correction is the inner-being mechanism by which sanctification is refused.

**T5.5.3** — If the evidence is silent on formation and sanctification, note explicitly.

Not applicable — formation evidence is present in both registers.

### T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory

**T5.6.1** — Does the verse evidence point this characteristic toward an eschatological fullness?

**E** — Yes, and with unusual directness. Psa 81:15 is the clearest statement within M06-A: "those who hate the Lord would cringe toward him, and their fate would last forever." The inner disposition of hatred toward God, when sustained to the end, produces a permanent relational consequence — "their fate would last forever" names an eschatological horizon that the hatred has made its own. The person's defining inner orientation has determined their final position.

Ecc 9:6 (group 1203) approaches from a different angle: "their love and their hate and their envy have already perished, and forever they have no more share in all that is done under the sun." Qohelet treats hatred as one of the constitutive orientations of mortal existence that ceases at death — which implies that hatred is not the ultimate state. The eschatological horizon for hatred-as-mortal-orientation is its own cessation.

Isa 60:15 (within group 1601): "whereas you have been forsaken and hated, with no one passing through, I will make you majestic forever, a joy from age to age" — the condition of being hated (received hatred) is addressed eschatologically by divine reversal. The hatred that Israel has borne is not the final word; God's making-majestic is.

**T5.6.2** — What does the present experience of this characteristic anticipate about its future fullness?

**E** — Two trajectories from the evidence:

For the person whose defining inner orientation is hatred of God (group 3596): the present disposition of hatred anticipates a final confrontation that the hatred cannot survive — Psa 81:15's cringing is not repentance but the forced acknowledgment of what the hatred opposed. The present hatred is moving toward its own exposure and permanent consequence.

For the person who hates evil in righteous alignment (group 550-NEW-02): the present hatred of evil anticipates a future in which evil is fully eliminated. When evil no longer exists, the hatred that was directed at it will have served its complete purpose. The righteous hatred is eschatologically oriented toward its own completion in the final defeat of what it opposes.

**T5.6.3** — If the evidence is silent on eschatological trajectory, note explicitly.

Not applicable — eschatological trajectory is evidenced in both registers.

### T5.7 — Deposit Consequence

**T5.7.1** — Where T2.8 identified a constitutional deposit from sustained operation of this characteristic, what developmental consequence does that deposit produce over time?

**E** — T2.8 established that sustained hatred of God produces a generational deposit: Exo 20:5 — "visiting the iniquity of the fathers on the children to the third and fourth generation of those who hate me." The developmental consequence documented in the verses is that the inner disposition of one generation shapes the covenantal standing of subsequent generations. The children of those who hate God inherit an iniquity-laden condition — they are born into the consequence of the prior generation's inner-being orientation.

Psa 105:25 shows the mechanism from the other side: "He turned their hearts to hate his people, to deal craftily with his servants" — God redirected Egypt's inner orientation toward hatred of Israel as part of his purposes. The Egyptians' children and the subsequent Pharaoh were formed in a social and inner environment shaped by that generational hostility. The deposit from one generation's hatred becomes the formational environment of the next.

**T5.7.2** — Does the evidence indicate generational consequence — a deposit carried forward beyond the individual?

**E** — Yes, explicitly. Exo 20:5 and Deu 5:9 are structural: the generational consequence is named as a constitutive element of the divine covenant — the hatred of God by one generation has consequences that reach "to the third and fourth generation." This is not merely a social inheritance (children learning hatred from parents) but a covenantal consequence — the iniquity is "visited," meaning God himself acts on the generational connection. The deposit is not only sociological; it has a theological dimension in which the covenant holds the generations together.

Within group 1199 (mas.te.mah, Hos 9:7-8): the communal inner climate of hatred has so corrupted the community's spiritual capacity that "the prophet is a fool; the man of the spirit is mad." The generation that carries the accumulated hatred has lost the capacity for spiritual formation — those who would guide the community toward God have been made useless by the inner climate that hatred has produced. This is generational deposit at the communal level.

**T5.7.3** — If T2.8 found no deposit, note explicitly and close T5.7.

Not applicable — deposit was evidenced in T2.8 and its consequences are described above.
