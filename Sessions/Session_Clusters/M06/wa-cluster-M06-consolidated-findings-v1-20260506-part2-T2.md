## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**T2.1.1** — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?

**[CLUSTER — all sub-groups]** No verse in any M06 sub-group explicitly locates any M06 characteristic at the spirit (ruach) level. The spirit is entirely silent across the cluster. Mal 2:16 contains "guard yourselves in your spirit" in a verse adjacent to marital faithlessness, but the command is to guard the spirit against faithlessness — not to locate the characteristic of hatred or contempt in the spirit. This is a cluster-level silence.

**T2.1.2** — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?

**[CLUSTER — all sub-groups]** Not evidenced for any sub-group. The verse evidence locates M06 characteristics in the heart (levav) and soul (nephesh), not in the spirit (ruach). The spirit-level is not named as the originating location of hatred, contempt, abhorrence, cruelty, reproach, enmity, or malice in any verse across the cluster.

**T2.1.3** — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**S — [CLUSTER]** Cannot be answered — spirit-level location is not evidenced for any sub-group.

**T2.1.4** — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note explicitly.

**[CLUSTER]** Spirit-level location is silent across the entire M06 cluster. None of the 429 connected verse rows across the cluster's seven sub-groups locates a M06 characteristic at the ruach level. This silence is itself a finding: the cluster of characteristics named by M06 operates below the spirit level — in the heart and soul registers — without penetrating to or originating from the spirit.

### T2.2 — Soul-Level Location

**T2.2.1** — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes, explicitly. Psa 11:5: "his soul (nephesh) hates the wicked and the one who loves violence" — God's hatred located in the soul. 2Sa 5:8: "hated by David's soul (nephesh)" — David's hatred is located in his soul. Isa 1:14: "my soul (nephesh) hates" — God's hatred again explicitly soul-located. The soul-location is direct and recurring.

**[B — Contempt]** Partially evidenced from the recipient's perspective. Psa 123:3-4: "our soul has had more than enough of the scorn of those who are at ease, of the contempt of the proud" — the soul (nephesh) is named as the organ that registers the weight of received contempt. No verse in M06-B names the soul explicitly as the location where contempt is held by the agent — in contrast to M06-A where God's soul is named as the seat of hatred.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Partially evidenced through the ta.av root's physical resonance and Psa 107:18: "they loathed any kind of food" — ta.av applied to the soul's revulsion from food in extremity. The soul-level connection is present though not explicitly named (nephesh) in the primary abhorrence texts.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Not explicitly named. The soul is not directly specified as the constitutional location of cruelty or ruthlessness in any M06-D verse. The characteristic appears to operate as a character quality without the specific soul-location statement.

**[E — Reproach]** From the recipient's perspective: Psa 123:3-4 — the soul is the organ that bears and registers the weight of received contempt and reproach. The soul is the receiving location for the condition, not named as holding the characteristic actively.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Not explicitly named. No M06-F verse places the adversary relationship in the soul.

**[G — Malice]** The most explicit soul-location in the entire M06 cluster. Eze 25:6: "malice within your soul (nephesh)" — the soul is named explicitly as the constitutional location. Eze 25:15: "malice of soul (nephesh)" — a second explicit naming. Both in the same sub-group. No other M06 characteristic is soul-located with this precision and repetition.

**T2.2.2** — What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in innermost personal experience?

**[A — Hatred]** The soul (nephesh) in these verses is the seat of the person's deepest personal experience — it is where the person's most fundamental orientations are located. When Psa 11:5 says God's soul hates, it names hatred as a disposition of God's innermost personal being, not merely a judicial decision or external stance. When David's soul hates (2Sa 5:8), the hatred is not merely a social attitude but an inner-personal reality. Soul-level location makes hatred a characteristic of the person's deepest self — which is why hatred in the heart (Lev 19:17) is treated as a sin even when it never produces an act.

**[G — Malice]** The explicit double naming of the soul as malice's location (Eze 25:6, 25:15) reveals that malice is not a surface-level attitude but a condition inhabiting the innermost personal being. The soul's rejoicing in another's destruction is the deepest possible form of the characteristic — it is not merely behavioural or cognitive but constitutive of the person's most fundamental experiential register. The soul was made to rejoice in what is life-giving; malice locates itself in the same deepest register and inverts it.

**T2.2.3** — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note explicitly.

**[B, D, F]** Soul-level location is not directly evidenced for contempt (M06-B), cruelty (M06-D), or hostility/enmity (M06-F). These characteristics operate at the heart/mind level without the explicit soul-naming that appears in M06-A and M06-G.

### T2.3 — Heart

**T2.3.1** — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes, explicitly and commandingly. Lev 19:17: "you shall not hate your brother in your heart (levav)" — the heart is named as the constitutional location of hatred in the foundational prohibitory verse. Pro 26:24: "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips and harbours deceit in his heart (levav)" — hatred concealed in the heart while lips present otherwise. Zec 8:17: "do not devise evil in your hearts (levav) against one another" — the heart as where hostile schemes originate.

**[B — Contempt]** Yes. Psa 10:13: "why does the wicked renounce God and say in his heart (levav), 'You will not call to account?'" — the heart is explicitly named as the location of the inner speech of contempt. Pro 5:12: "how I hated discipline, and my heart (lev) despised reproof" — the heart is the constitutional location of the despising of reproof. The heart is where the contemptuous verdict is formed before it is expressed in speech or behaviour.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Not explicitly named for abhorrence in M06-C — no verse locates ta.av or bdelussomai in the heart using the levav term. The characteristic operates at a level that the verse evidence describes as the conscience/moral-constitution level rather than specifically naming the heart.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Not explicitly heart-located in M06-D. The characteristic operates as a character quality whose inner-being ground (absence of God-consciousness) is cognitively framed (the mind's governance framework excludes God) rather than specifically heart-located.

**[E — Reproach]** Yes, as the organ that bears and is broken by reproach. Psa 69:20: "reproaches have broken my heart (lev), so that I am in despair" — the heart is explicitly named as the organ where reproach does its deepest damage. Psa 89:50: "I bear in my heart (levav) the insults of all the many nations" — the heart as the carrying-place of received insults. Unlike M06-A where the heart holds a hostile disposition, in M06-E the heart bears the weight of what others' hostility has done to it.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Not directly named as the constitutional location of the adversary relationship in M06-F's verse evidence.

**[G — Malice]** Not explicitly heart-located in M06-G. The soul is named twice; the heart is not mentioned in the three Ezekiel verses.

**T2.3.2** — What does the heart-location reveal?

**[A — Hatred]** The heart in Lev 19:17 is the integrating centre where intention, planning, and relational orientation converge. The prohibition "not in your heart" is precise: hatred at the heart level is the problem, even before any act. This means hatred is culpable as an inner state, not only as a behaviour. Pro 26:24 shows the heart as the hiding place — hatred can be active in the heart while the lips present false peace, revealing that the heart is where the real disposition lives, prior to and independent of speech or action. The heart's hatred is the primary moral problem; the act is its secondary expression.

**[E — Reproach]** The heart as the organ of integration means that reproach does not merely damage the person's social standing — it damages the integrating centre of who they are. "Reproaches have broken my heart" (Psa 69:20) names the shattering of the person's inner coherence, not merely their surface emotions. When the heart is broken, the person's capacity to integrate experience, maintain identity, and orient toward the future is compromised. This explains why reproach requires active forgetting for full restoration (Isa 54:4) — the damage is at the integrating level.

**T2.3.3** — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note explicitly.

**[C, D, F, G]** Heart-location is not explicitly evidenced for abhorrence (M06-C), cruelty (M06-D), hostility/enmity (M06-F), or malice (M06-G). Abhorrence operates at the conscience/moral-constitution level. Cruelty is located at the character-quality level (defined by absence). Hostility/enmity generates responses in the person facing it without being specifically heart-located. Malice is explicitly soul-located (Eze 25:6, 25:15).

### T2.4 — Mind

**T2.4.1** — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?

**[A — Hatred]** Not directly. No M06-A verse explicitly names the mind as the constitutional location of hatred. However, group 1199 (mas.te.mah, Hos 9:7): the accumulated communal hatred produces "the prophet is a fool; the man of the spirit is mad" — the characteristic has destroyed the community's cognitive-prophetic capacity. Psa 36:2: "he flatters himself in his own eyes that his iniquity cannot be found out and hated" — the mind's self-perception has been distorted by the hatred-avoidance.

**[B — Contempt]** Yes — the cognitive faculty is the ground of contempt. Psa 10:13: the contempt rests on a cognitive conviction ("you will not call to account") — the mind has evaluated the situation and produced an assessment that grounds the dismissal. Contempt is not unreasoned; it is a verdict the mind renders. The cognitive verdict then feeds the heart's evaluative posture.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Borderline — the cognitive faculty is engaged (the object must be perceived and evaluated as repugnant) but the mind is not named as the constitutional location. The abhorrence is pre-cognitive in its onset — the recoil precedes the reasoned verdict. Mind-location is not directly evidenced.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Indirectly. Psa 54:3's "they do not set God before themselves" names a cognitive-dispositional failure: the mind's governance framework has excluded God. The ruthlessness is not located in the mind but the cognitive absence of God-consciousness is what enables it.

**[E — Reproach]** Indirectly. Job 19:5: "if indeed you magnify yourselves against me and make my disgrace an argument against me" — reproach is being deployed cognitively as an argument against the person. The reproached person must cognitively resist that argument from inside the condition. Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man" — the person has internalised the social designation and now holds it as a cognitive self-assessment.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes. 1Pe 5:8: "be sober-minded (sophron)" — the cognitive faculty is explicitly commanded to engage when facing the cosmic adversary. Sober-mindedness is the disciplined cognitive-volitional response that the adversary relationship requires.

**[G — Malice]** Not explicitly mind-located. The malice operates in the soul and expresses through the body; the mind is not named.

**T2.4.2** — What does mind-location reveal?

**[B — Contempt]** The cognitive grounding of contempt reveals that it is not merely affective — it is a reasoned dismissal. The person who holds contempt has produced a cognitive assessment (this person/thing is beneath regard; God will not call to account) that functions as the premise from which the dismissal follows. This makes contempt particularly resistant to correction: to challenge the contemptuous person's behaviour without challenging their cognitive premise leaves the premise intact.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The command to sober-mindedness in 1Pe 5:8 reveals that the adversary relationship specifically targets the cognitive faculty — the prowling adversary seeks to catch the person when their cognitive vigilance is lowered. The mind's role is active surveillance and accurate assessment of the threat. The adversary relationship demands cognitive engagement in a way that most other M06 characteristics do not.

**T2.4.3** — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note explicitly.

**[A, C, G]** Direct mind-location is not evidenced for hatred (M06-A), abhorrence (M06-C), or malice (M06-G). Cognitive effects of these characteristics are evidenced as consequences but not as constitutional locations.

### T2.5 — Other Soul Subsets

**T2.5.1** — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?

**[A — Hatred]** The volitional sub-dimension of the soul. Gen 27:41: Esau's hatred produces a resolution — "then I will kill my brother Jacob" — the volitional resolution is explicit and internal. The settled hatred has organised the volitional capacity around a future act. The hatred does not merely exist as feeling; it has become an intention. The will as a soul-subset is engaged by hatred — hatred recruits the will into a hostile plan.

**[B — Contempt]** The volitional faculty: Gen 25:34 (Esau's despising of the birthright) — "and he ate and drank and rose and went his way" — the will acts in accordance with the contemptuous evaluation. Once the worth-verdict is rendered, the will acts as if the thing truly has no claim. Job 12:5: "in the *thought* of one who is at ease there is contempt for misfortune" — the word "thought" (Hebrew: 'ashton) names a cognitive-reflective faculty: the person's comfortable thought-life generates contempt.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The conscience as a soul-level organ is the most central sub-location for abhorrence. The characteristic operates at the level of the conscience's deepest reactions — the visceral recoil precedes and is then named by the moral evaluation faculty. The conscience (as the acute inner witness of moral reality) is abhorrence's most natural constitutional home.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The absence of fear of God as a soul-subset is the key constitutional observation. Fear of the Lord is a soul-level orientation governing the whole person. Its absence (Psa 54:3) is what M06-D names as the constitutional ground of cruelty and ruthlessness. The characteristic is defined by a soul-level absence rather than a soul-level presence.

**[E — Reproach]** The identity-level of the soul. Sa.ni (group 1664-001, Deu 21:15) names reproach as a total relational identity condition — the disfavoured wife is not merely experiencing episodes of reproach but inhabiting a position of permanent relational identity that shapes every legal and relational dimension of her existence. Isa 54:4 — "you will forget the shame of your youth, and the reproach of your widowhood you will remember no more" — the memory faculty (as a soul-subset) is named as the location where reproach persists after the external condition has changed, requiring active forgetting for restoration.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Not evidenced beyond the mind-location already noted (T2.4).

**[G — Malice]** The soul as a whole is the named location — the explicit double naming (Eze 25:6, 25:15) does not differentiate soul sub-faculties. The whole soul is implicated.

**T2.5.2** — If so, what is that location and what does it reveal?

**[A]** The will as the soul-subset engaged by settled hatred reveals that hatred is not merely a passive state — it actively recruits the volitional faculty. The movement from inner hostile disposition to volitional resolution (Gen 27:41: "then I will kill") shows that hatred does not remain inert but presses toward action. This is why Lev 19:17's prohibition of hatred "in the heart" addresses not only an emotion but a volitional orientation.

**[D]** The absence of fear of the Lord as a soul-level governing orientation reveals that cruelty is constitutionally a condition of the whole person, not merely a behavioural tendency. The soul that lacks the fear of God has lost the governing principle that would constrain destructive power in all its expressions.

**[E]** The memory faculty bearing reproach (Isa 54:4) reveals that the condition is not merely experiential but has been encoded at the deepest level of the person's inner history. This is why restoration from deep reproach requires not merely the cessation of the external condition but active new identity formation — the memory carries the reproach forward even when the situation has changed.

**T2.5.3** — If the evidence is silent, note explicitly.

**[F, G]** No soul subsets beyond those already named are evidenced for hostility/enmity (M06-F) or malice (M06-G, where the soul as a whole is named).

### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**T2.6.1** — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**[A — Hatred]** Several body-part links. Job 16:9 (sa.tam context): "he has gnashed his teeth at me" — the teeth as expressive instrument of the inner hostile state. The hand appears frequently as the instrument through which hatred's expression operates violently: 2Sa 22:18 — "those who hated me, for they were too mighty for me" — the strength of the haters' hands is what overwhelms. The body-part links in M06-A are moderate and indirect.

**[B — Contempt]** Thin. The primary body connection is through the speech organs — ne.a.tsah (group 1567-001) names blasphemous contempt expressed in speech. Isa 37:22: "she wags her head behind you" — contemptuous body language as the physical expression of the inner posture of dismissal. The body-part links in M06-B are primarily in the speech register.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Strong. Job 30:10: "they abhor me; they keep aloof from me; they do not hesitate to spit at the sight of me" — spitting is the body's physical expression of moral revulsion. The bdelussomai root itself carries the sense of stench — olfactory revulsion as the body-metaphor for moral recoil. The body's connection to abhorrence is the strongest of any M06 sub-group examined up to this point.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Strong, in the military and oppressive register. Jer 6:23, 50:42: "they lay hold on bow and javelin; they are cruel and have no mercy" — the entire bodily apparatus in military array as the expression of cruelty. Isa 25:4: "the breath of the ruthless is like a storm against a wall" — even the breath of the ruthless is characterised by the inner quality; the person's entire bodily presence is an expression of their ruthlessness.

**[E — Reproach]** Strong. Job 16:10: "they have struck me insolently on the cheek" — the physical blow as the bodily delivery of reproach. Luk 18:32: "he will be mocked and shamefully treated and spit upon" — spitting and shameful treatment as the bodily delivery of reproach upon Christ. Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man, scorned by mankind and despised by the people" — the image of the worm is the image of the bodily posture of the crushed and reproached person.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Thin. The body-part links in M06-F are primarily through the speech of appeal (Psa 35:1 — "contend, O Lord") rather than specific body parts. 1Pe 5:8's watchfulness implies bodily alertness but does not name a specific body part.

**[G — Malice]** Very strong — the most dramatic body expression in M06. Eze 25:6: "you have clapped your hands and stamped your feet and rejoiced with all the malice within your soul" — two specific body-part actions (hands clapping, feet stamping) named as the immediate bodily expression of the soul's malice. The inner soul-level pleasure produces spontaneous, celebratory physical expression.

**T2.6.2** — What is Scripture doing by making that link?

**[A — Hatred]** The gnashing of teeth (Job 16:9) is expressive — it names the body's involuntary participation in the inner hostile state. It shows that hatred, at its most intense, cannot be fully contained internally but erupts into somatic expression. The inner disposition erupts outward through the body before it has been consciously planned.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The spitting of Job 30:10 and the stench-metaphor of bdelussomai show Scripture using the body's most extreme rejection responses to name the severity of moral revulsion. The body that spits is communicating through its most visceral rejection gesture. Scripture is naming moral abhorrence as the inner-being equivalent of the body's most radical rejection response — the connection is not metaphorical hedging but anatomically precise.

**[G — Malice]** The clapping and stamping of Eze 25:6 show that the soul's malice cannot be contained inwardly but overflows into physical celebration. Scripture is showing that the inner-being state (pleasure in another's destruction) is so consuming that it produces spontaneous bodily expression. The body enacts what the soul is experiencing — making the inner state visible and confirming that malice is not a mild or controlled characteristic but one that possesses the whole person, including the body.

**T2.6.3** — If no body-part link is evidenced, note explicitly.

**[F]** Body-part links are thin for hostility/enmity (M06-F). The characteristic operates primarily through the inner responses it generates (appeal, vigilance) rather than through specific bodily expression.

### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**T2.7.1** — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — soul expressing through body, body feeding back to soul, or both?

**[A — Hatred]** Soul → body. The inner disposition of hatred produces somatic expression (gnashing of teeth, violent hands). No evidence in M06-A of the body feeding hatred back into the soul — the verses consistently show the inner disposition driving outward expression.

**[B — Contempt]** Soul → body. Inner contempt produces speech acts (ne.a.tsah) and body language (head-wagging). No evidence of reverse direction.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Soul → body primarily. The inner moral recoil finds expression through the body (spitting, keeping aloof). The bdelussomai root's stench-metaphor suggests the body-metaphor describes the inner state (not the body causing the state). However, the bodily resonance of ta.av suggests the possibility of a bidirectional relationship — the body's physical registers participate in abhorrence in a way that may reinforce the inner state.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Soul → body. The inner character quality of cruelty expresses through the body in destructive military action. "The breath of the ruthless" (Isa 25:4) — even the bodily breath carries the inner character quality outward.

**[E — Reproach]** Both directions evidenced. The bodies of others deliver reproach to the recipient (blows on the cheek, spitting — Job 16:10, Luk 18:32) — body → soul direction from outside. But the soul's reception of reproach then affects the person's own bodily posture: "I am a worm and not a man" (Psa 22:6) — the inner-soul condition of reproach is expressed through the person's own bodily self-perception and posture.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Limited body-characteristic link, so direction is not strongly evidenced.

**[G — Malice]** Soul → body. The malice is explicitly "within your soul" (Eze 25:6) and the clapping and stamping are its outward bodily expression. The direction is from soul to body.

**T2.7.2** — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**[A, B, D, F, G — Soul → Body]** The consistent soul-to-body direction confirms that M06 characteristics are constitutionally inner-being phenomena that express outward — not behavioural patterns that create inner states. This is the methodological ground for the programme's approach: the inner state is the primary moral concern, and the behaviour is its secondary expression. Addressing the behaviour without addressing the inner state leaves the characteristic intact (as Lev 19:17's prohibition of inner hatred demonstrates — it targets the root, not the fruit).

**[E — Both]** The bidirectional evidence for reproach confirms that it operates at the intersection of the inner being and the social-relational: others' bodies deliver it inward, and the person's inner state is then expressed through their own body. Reproach is a socially mediated inner-being condition — it is not purely internal or purely external but involves a loop between the social world and the inner self.

**T2.7.3** — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note explicitly.

**[F]** The body-characteristic link for hostility/enmity (M06-F) is thin, and the direction is not strongly evidenced. The characteristic operates primarily through inner responses (appeal, vigilance) rather than through somatic expression.

### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**T2.8.1** — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit — including generational consequence?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. Exo 20:5 and Deu 5:9 (group 3596, anchor Exo 20:5): "visiting the iniquity of the fathers on the children to the third and fourth generation of those who hate me." The sustained inner disposition of hating God produces a consequence that extends beyond the individual into the generations — not merely as social inheritance but as covenantal consequence. The iniquity is "visited," meaning God himself acts on the generational connection. The deposit is both sociological (children raised in a household of God-hatred) and covenantal-theological (the covenant holds the generations together under the consequence of the ancestors' inner orientation).

**[B — Contempt]** No explicit generational deposit evidenced. The social propagation of contempt (group 1281-001, biz.za.von — Est 1:18) shows that contempt spreads across a social system from one person's act, but this is lateral social contagion, not generational deposit. No verse names contempt as producing a constitutional bodily or generational consequence.

**[C — Abhorrence]** No explicit generational deposit evidenced. The eschatological permanence of de.ra.on (Dan 12:2, Isa 66:24) is the closest — but this is a final-state consequence, not a generational transmission. The permanent condition of being an abhorrence (Isa 66:24) is the eschatological terminus, not a deposit passed to subsequent generations.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** No explicit generational deposit evidenced. The corporate/national consequences of cruelty are named (covenantal judgment falls on ruthless nations) but not as a constitutional bodily deposit in the way M06-A's hatred of God produces generational iniquity-visitation.

**[E — Reproach]** A personal constitutional deposit (not generational). Isa 54:4: "you will forget the shame of your youth, and the reproach of your widowhood you will remember no more" — the fact that the reproach must be *forgotten* implies it has been deposited at a level where it persists in memory and shapes ongoing experience. The reproach has left a constitutional deposit in the person's inner life requiring not merely cessation of the external condition but active new identity formation and forgetting.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** No generational deposit evidenced.

**[G — Malice]** No generational deposit evidenced. The "never-ending enmity" (Eze 25:15) is self-perpetuating within the person or nation that holds it, not transmitted to subsequent generations through a constitutional deposit.

**T2.8.2** — What evidence supports or contradicts this?

**[A — Supports]** Exo 20:5 and Deu 5:9 are structural — the generational consequence is named as a constitutive element of the divine covenant, not an incidental observation. The pairing of "those who hate me" with the generational consequence appears in both the Exodus and Deuteronomy Decalogue texts, confirming its structural weight. Psa 105:25 provides a second dimension: "He turned their hearts to hate his people" — God redirected Egypt's inner orientation toward hatred of Israel as part of his purposes, and the subsequent Pharaoh was formed in a social and inner environment shaped by that generational hostility.

**[E — Supports]** Isa 54:4's requirement of active forgetting is the evidential basis for the personal deposit. The need to *forget* the reproach — not merely to cease experiencing it — implies it has been encoded in the memory at a level that persists independently of the external condition. Restoration is therefore not automatic but requires new formation.

**T2.8.3** — If the evidence is silent, note explicitly.

**[B, C, D, F, G]** Generational or constitutional bodily deposit is not evidenced for contempt (M06-B), abhorrence (M06-C), cruelty (M06-D), hostility/enmity (M06-F), or malice (M06-G). The generational deposit finding in M06-A (hatred of God) is characteristic-specific, not a cluster-wide finding.

### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**T2.9.1** — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[A — Hatred]** Multiple origins: (1) **From perceived injury or injustice** — Gen 37:4 (brothers' jealousy of father's favour → hatred of Joseph); Gen 27:41 (perceived theft of blessing → Esau's hatred); 2Sa 13:22 (Amnon's violation of Tamar → Absalom's hatred). (2) **From sustained moral corruption** — Hos 9:15 ("there I began to hate them. Because of the wickedness of their deeds") — divine hatred originates in the accumulation of covenant violation. (3) **From divine action** — Psa 105:25 ("He turned their hearts to hate his people") — hatred can originate from outside the person through divine action, introduced into the heart for his purposes. (4) **From righteous formation** — group 550-NEW-02: righteous hatred of evil originates from the person's love of God and alignment with his character (Pro 8:13: "the fear of the Lord is hatred of evil").

**[B — Contempt]** Two enabling origins: (1) **Pride and comfort** — Job 12:5: "in the thought of one who is at ease there is contempt for misfortune"; Psa 123:4: "the contempt of the proud" — the person's comfortable superiority generates contempt. (2) **Cognitive misreading of God's accountability** — Psa 10:13: the false conviction that God will not call to account is the cognitive premise from which contempt of God follows. (3) **Accumulated spiritual drift** — Deu 31:20: prosperity leads to spiritual satiation leads to turning to other gods leads to contempt of the covenant God.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Two origins: (1) **From love of what is good/true** — Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law" — the love of truth makes its opposite naturally repugnant. (2) **From divine action** — Psa 106:40: God's abhorrence of his heritage originates in the accumulated weight of covenant violation. (3) **From habituation to corruption** — inverted abhorrence (group 252-002): sustained immersion in what is morally wrong progressively reverses the direction of the moral recoil.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** One primary origin: **the removal of God from the inner governing framework** — Psa 54:3, 86:14: "they do not set God before themselves." When the awareness that God is present, attentive, and will call to account is removed from the person's operative inner framework, the restraining qualities (mercy, fear, accountability) have no ground to stand on. The cruelty is the condition that follows.

**[E — Reproach]** Three sources: (1) **From human hostility** — the disordered inner lives of M06-A through D produce the condition in their recipients. (2) **From divine covenantal judgment** — God imposes reproach as judgment on covenant violation (Eze 5:14, Jer 23:40). (3) **From voluntary acceptance** — Jer 15:15: "for your sake I bear reproach" — the prophet consciously takes up the condition in service of God.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship originates when another party positions themselves in opposition. Unlike M06-A through D which originate in the agent's inner condition, M06-F's origin is relational-structural — it depends on the posture another party takes.

**[G — Malice]** From the compound development of three prior conditions: sustained enmity (origin in M06-A's elements), contempt for the other's worth (origin in M06-B's elements), and the specific further development of taking pleasure in harm — which emerges when enmity and contempt are sustained alongside the other's suffering.

**T2.9.2** — Is the origin singular or multiple?

**[A]** Multiple — four distinct origin-paths evidenced (perceived injury, moral corruption, divine action, righteous formation). The origin determines the direction and moral quality of the hatred. **[B]** Multiple (pride, cognitive misreading, spiritual drift). **[C]** Multiple (love of good, divine action, habituation to corruption). **[D]** Effectively singular — the removal of God-consciousness is the named constitutional ground across all the M06-D verses. **[E]** Multiple (human hostility, divine judgment, voluntary acceptance). **[F]** Relational-structural — determined by the other party's posture. **[G]** Compound — requires three prior conditions simultaneously.

**T2.9.3** — Does the origin change across different Scripture contexts?

**[A]** Yes — the same term (sa.ne) names hatred originating from jealousy (Gen 37:4), from violated honour (Gen 27:41), from moral alignment with God (Pro 8:13), and from divine action (Psa 105:25). The term carries no origin-information; the context supplies it entirely.

**[C]** Yes — ta.av's directional neutrality (T1.1.2 finding) means its origin changes entirely by context: from love of truth (righteous form), from habituation to corruption (inverted form), from divine moral constitution (divine abhorrence).

**[CLUSTER]** The origins across M06 are genuinely diverse — the cluster does not have a single constitutional source. The verse evidence shows M06 characteristics arising from perceived injury, moral corruption, divine action, relational-structural positioning, and compound development of prior conditions. The diversity of origins is itself a finding about the cluster's nature: these characteristics can enter the inner being through many different pathways.

### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**T2.10.1** — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?

**[A — Hatred]** Evidence of movement within the inner being: Pro 5:12 — "how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof" — the hatred at the heart level produced a despising of reproof, which is a moral evaluation faculty operation. The hatred at heart level activated a contempt at the evaluation faculty level. The movement is not across body-soul-spirit levels but within the soul from disposition to evaluation and then to volitional intention (Gen 27:41: settled hatred → murderous intention).

**[B — Contempt]** The movement from inner cognitive verdict (mind) to heart-level evaluative posture to volitional action (will) to speech expression (mouth) to social contagion (biz.za.von — Est 1:18). Within the soul, contempt moves from cognitive assessment through evaluative heart to expressed behaviour.

**[C — Abhorrence]** From conscience-level response → moral evaluation faculty (confirming the recoil) → maintained distance → bodily expression (spitting, keeping aloof). The movement is from the deepest inner-being response upward through cognitive confirmation and outward through bodily expression.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** From the absence of the inner governing framework (God-consciousness removed from the person's operative centre) → absence of restraining character qualities → exercise of unrestrained destructive power → self-harm (Pro 11:17) and eventual divine judgment. The movement is from inner constitutional condition through to external destructive expression and ultimately to the person's own undoing.

**[E — Reproach]** From external social designation (others designate the person as shameful) → social delivery (taunts, insults, physical contempt) → inner reception at the heart (Psa 69:20) → restructuring of self-perception (Psa 22:6) → sustained condition of fear and diminishment. In the restoration direction: divine intervention → social reversal → restoration of identity (Isa 54:4 — forgetting the reproach).

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship generates a sequence of inner responses: the relational-structural positioning of an adversary → urgency and appeal in the person facing it → vigilance → relief at the adversary's defeat or removal.

**[G — Malice]** From soul-level pleasure in another's destruction → bodily expression (clapping, stamping) → purposive destructive action. The movement is from the soul's inner state outward through the body into active harm.

**T2.10.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of movement?

**[A]** The pattern visible from the verses: perceived injury → inner settlement (heart) → volitional organisation (plan/intention) → concealment in speech → eventual expression or action. Pro 26:24-26 shows the full internal sequence most clearly: hatred active in the heart (v.24), concealed in speech (v.24-25), eventually exposed by the assembly (v.26).

**[B]** Cognitive conviction (mind) → heart's evaluative verdict → volitional de-obligation (the will acts as if freed from the object's claim) → behavioural expression → social propagation.

**[CLUSTER]** The consistent cross-sub-group pattern: the characteristic begins in the inner being (soul, heart, or conscience level) and moves progressively outward — toward volitional organisation, then toward expression, then toward social or relational consequence. No M06 characteristic moves in the reverse direction (from external behaviour inward to establish the inner state). The inner-being origin is primary; the external expression is secondary.

**T2.10.3** — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note explicitly.

Not applicable — constitutional movement is evidenced across all sub-groups, though the specific sequence and levels differ.
