## Flags and observations for researcher attention

1. **Database linkage queries** — Four H8130 sa.ne verses (Exo 1:10, Deu 30:7, Psa 69:14, Psa 106:10) show no visible surface of the term in the verse text as rendered. These may reflect cases where sa.ne underlies "enemy/foe" in a translation that has not surfaced the term. Database check recommended before classification.

2. **H2778A cha.raph — Judg 5:18** — The verse text shows no taunting or contempt content. The term cha.raph has a sub-gloss meaning "to risk/expose" (not taunting). This may be a sub-gloss linkage instance similar to the DIM-187-SD001 concern about STEP sub-gloss architecture. Recommended: flag for database review.

3. **H8263 she.qets — Lev 11:11 and 11:13** — The borderline cases rest on whether a commanded inner orientation of revulsion ("you shall regard as detestable") is sufficient to make the term inner-being relevant. This is a genuine analytic question: does the commanded posture constitute characteristic content, or does the term merely label the object? Researcher decision required.

4. **H0887 ba.ash** — This term presents the most complex pattern. Literal physical stench (8 verses) should be set aside. Social-reputational stench metaphor cases vary: where the metaphor directly names an inner-relational disposition of hostility/moral revulsion in a person toward another (2Sa 10:6, 2Sa 16:21, 1Ch 19:6), the inner-being register is active. Where it names only a reputational consequence without surfacing the inner disposition of a specific person, it is weaker. Gen 34:30, 1Sa 13:4, and Psa 38:5 are genuine borderline cases.

5. **H7850 sho.tet — single verse, not relevant** — This term has only one verse, and it does not carry inner-being content as the term is rendered. The cluster assignment of sho.tet to M06 may warrant reconsideration.
