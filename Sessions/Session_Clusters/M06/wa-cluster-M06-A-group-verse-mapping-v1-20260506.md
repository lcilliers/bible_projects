## Method note

Every non-set-aside verse in M06-A must belong to at least one group. Where a verse carries more than one distinct inner-being phenomenon in the term, it receives multiple group assignments — one per phenomenon. Every group has at least one anchor verse. Existing group codes are retained where the description is modified. New groups receive provisional codes (M06-A-NEW-01 etc.) for CC to assign permanent IDs.

The existing groups in M06-A are: **1601**, **1200**, **1201**, **1202**, **1203**, **3590**, **1199**, **1602**.

All assignments are grounded in the verse text as read from the source document. Memory is not authoritative; the verse text is.

### GROUP 1601 — RETAINED AND REFINED
**Existing code:** 1601
**Existing description:** "Term names hatred as the inner moral disposition opposed to love — God's righteous hatred of evil and injustice; human hatred of neighbour and brother as the mark of moral disorder; and the psalmist's holy hatred of evil aligned with God"

**Assessment:** Group 1601 is a conglomerate — it holds at least four distinct phenomena under one description. ### GROUP 3590 — REVIEW AND DECISION
**Existing code:** 3590
**Existing description:** "Term names hatred received from others — those who hate me, my enemies — hate as an inner-being disposition coming from another person, directed at the speaker / object"

**Assessment:** The three verses assigned to 3590 (Exo 1:10, Deu 30:7, Psa 69:14, Psa 106:10) were flagged in the UT review as potentially being translation cases where sa.ne underlies "enemies" or "foe" rather than surfacing as "hate." Reading the verse texts confirms: in Exo 1:10 the word rendered is "enemies" — sa.ne is present in the span but the verse-text does not surface "hate." In Deu 30:7 similarly "foes and enemies." In Psa 69:14 "enemies." In Psa 106:10 "foe."

**Decision:** These four verses are edge cases where sa.ne underlies an "enemy/foe" translation rather than appearing as "hate." The inner-being content is real (the hatred that constitutes enmity) but the term is not surfaced directly. Group 3590's description as "hatred received from others" does not accurately describe what is happening in these verses — they are not about hatred being received but about the enemy-status of those who hate.

**Revised description for 3590:** Term underlies the concept of "enemy" or "foe" — the one who hates functions as the adversary, with the inner-being disposition of hatred implied by the adversary category rather than named directly.

**Verses retained in 3590:**

| Verse | Term | What the verse shows |
|---|---|---|
| Exo 1:10 | H8130 sa.ne | "They join our enemies" — sa.ne underlies "enemies"; the enemies are those whose inner disposition is hatred |
| Deu 30:7 | H8130 sa.ne | "Your foes and enemies who persecuted you" — sa.ne underlies "enemies" |
| Psa 69:14 | H8130 sa.ne | "My enemies" — sa.ne underlies "enemies"; hatred as the constitutive inner state of the enemy |
| Psa 106:10 | H8130 sa.ne | "The hand of the foe" — sa.ne underlies "foe" |

**Anchor verse for 3590:** **Exo 1:10** — "If war breaks out, they join our enemies and fight against us." The enemy category is constituted by the inner disposition of hatred that sa.ne names — the word underlies "enemies" here, establishing that the adversarial relationship is grounded in hatred.

**Flag for CC:** Group 3590 description should be updated per the revised description above.
