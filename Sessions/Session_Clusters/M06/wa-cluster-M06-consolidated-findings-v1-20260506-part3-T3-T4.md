## T3 — The Inner Faculties

### T3.1 — Perception

**T3.1.1** — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty — the inner senses — and if so, which and how?

**[A — Hatred]** The perceptive faculty is engaged at the point where hatred enters the inner life. Gen 37:4: "when his brothers *saw* that their father loved him more than all his brothers, they hated him" — the perception of favouritism is the trigger. Each new perception (the dream, the words — Gen 37:5, 8) intensifies the hatred. The perception does not merely register facts; it reads relational signals and feeds them to the disposition. In group 550-NEW-02 (righteous hatred): Psa 119:104 — "through your precepts I get understanding; therefore I hate every false way" — perception of truth actively generates hatred of its opposite. Understanding of what is true makes the encounter with falsehood more immediately and viscerally intolerable.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt distorts perception in a specific way: it causes the person to see the despised object through the lens of the low-worth verdict already rendered. Once Esau has despised the birthright, he cannot perceive it accurately — its value is invisible to him. Psa 10:3: the wicked "curses and renounces the Lord" — the perception of God has been filtered through a prior verdict of his irrelevance. Contempt is a perception-filter: what is despised becomes imperceptible in its actual worth.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Abhorrence is triggered by perception — the morally repugnant thing must first be seen or encountered. But abhorrence also shapes subsequent perception: once something is abhorred, it becomes perceived as repugnant in all future encounters. Group 247-001 (she.qets): Eze 8:10 — the prophet is brought to see the detestable things engraved on the wall — the vision makes visible what has been provoking divine abhorrence. In the inverted form (group 252-002): those who abhor justice (Amo 5:10) perceive the truth-speaker as the repugnant thing — their perceptive faculty has been reorganised around the inverted abhorrence.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty impairs the perceptive faculty in a specific direction: it prevents the person from registering the suffering of others as a claim on their conduct. Pro 12:10: "the righteous has regard for the life of his beast" — the righteous person perceives animal life as worthy of regard; the wicked person does not perceive it in this way. The cruel person's perception has been reorganised so that what should trigger mercy and restraint does not register as such.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach restructures perception. Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man" — the person perceives themselves through the lens of others' contempt. Isa 51:7: "fear not the reproach of man" — the command implies that reproach has reorganised the person's perception of threat, making every potential reproach-encounter something to be feared. The characteristic shapes how the person perceives themselves, others' regard, and the social environment.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** 1Pe 5:8: "be watchful" — the adversary relationship requires heightened perceptive alertness. The adversary who prowls must be perceived accurately and in time. The characteristic sharpens the perceptive faculty toward vigilant awareness of threat.

**[G — Malice]** Malice presupposes the perception of another's suffering and then evaluates that suffering as a positive good. Eze 25:6 — the nations see the land of Israel devastated and clap their hands. The perceptive faculty has been reorganised by malice so that the perception of another's destruction triggers pleasure rather than compassion.

**T3.1.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?

**[A]** Impairs in the disordered register — Psa 36:2: hatred-avoidance distorts self-perception; group 1199's mas.te.mah destroys prophetic perception entirely. Enables and sharpens in the righteous register — righteous hatred sharpens perception of moral reality (Psa 119:104).

**[B]** Impairs — contempt filters out the actual worth of the despised object. It produces a systematically distorted perception: what has been designated as worthless cannot be perceived as it actually is.

**[C]** Impairs in the inverted form (group 252-002 — abhorring justice means perceiving righteousness as the repugnant thing). Enables in the righteous form — sharpening the perception of what is genuinely morally repugnant.

**[D]** Impairs — the cruel person's perception fails to register the suffering of others as morally significant. The perceptive faculty's mercy-triggering function is absent.

**[E]** Distorts — reproach reorganises perception of the self (as worthless) and of the social environment (as threatening further reproach). The person under sustained reproach perceives threat where there may be none.

**[F]** Sharpens — the adversary relationship demands heightened perceptive vigilance (1Pe 5:8).

**[G]** Inverts — malice reorganises perception so that another's suffering triggers pleasure rather than compassion. The perceptive faculty is operating but in a systematically inverted direction.

**T3.1.3** — What does the pattern reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**[CLUSTER]** M06 characteristics are perception-organising phenomena. Each characteristic establishes a framework through which subsequent perceptions are filtered and interpreted. Rightly-directed characteristics (righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence) sharpen and clarify perception of moral reality. Disordered characteristics (person-directed hatred, contempt, inverted abhorrence, cruelty, malice) impair, bypass, or invert the accurate perception of persons, worth, and moral reality. The cluster as a whole reveals that how the inner being is oriented fundamentally shapes what and how the person perceives.

### T3.2 — Cognition

**T3.2.1** — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty — knowing, understanding, discerning?

**[A — Hatred]** In the righteous register: Psa 119:104 — "through your precepts I get understanding; therefore I hate every false way" — cognition (understanding through the precepts) produces directed hatred. The cognitive sequence runs: learning → understanding → hatred of the false. In the disordered register: Pro 26:24 — "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips and harbours deceit in his heart" — cognition is recruited into the service of the hidden hatred, managing the concealment.

**[B — Contempt]** The cognitive faculty is both the origin and the instrument of contempt. Psa 10:13: the cognitive conviction ("you will not call to account") is what generates the contemptuous inner speech. The mind renders a verdict that then operates as the ground of the dismissal. Sustained contempt also impairs cognition: Jer 23:17 — the community that despises God's word becomes receptive to false prophets. The contempt has corrupted the cognitive capacity to distinguish true from false prophetic address.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Rom 2:22 (bdelussomai) shows the cognitive dimension: the person claims to abhor idols — a cognitive position they hold. But the apostle's challenge ("do you rob temples?") exposes that the cognitive claim does not correspond to an actual inner state. The cognitive faculty can assert abhorrence without the genuine visceral recoil being present. This is the gap between claimed abhorrence and genuine abhorrence.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The cognitive element is present in Psa 54:3's "they do not set God before themselves" — a cognitive-dispositional failure. The mind's governance framework has excluded God. The ruthlessness is downstream of a cognitive framework that operates without the governing premise of divine accountability.

**[E — Reproach]** Job 19:5: "if indeed you magnify yourselves against me and make my disgrace an argument against me" — reproach is being deployed cognitively as an argument against the person. The cognitive dimension of reproach is its use as evidence — others use the condition of being reproached as proof of unworthiness. The reproached person must cognitively resist that argument from inside the condition.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** 1Pe 5:8: "be sober-minded" — the adversary relationship demands disciplined cognitive engagement. Clear-headed, accurate cognitive assessment of the threat is the prescribed response to the cosmic adversary.

**[G — Malice]** The moral evaluation faculty (cognitively expressed) has been so thoroughly inverted in malice that destruction is evaluated as good and the other's loss is evaluated as gain. Eze 36:5: "gave my land to themselves as a possession" — the malice is combined with acquisitive cognitive re-categorisation of the other's property as one's own legitimate prize.

**T3.2.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition?

**[A]** Righteous register: deepens and clarifies — hatred of falsehood is the product of sharpened understanding. Disordered register: corrupts — cognition is bent to produce false communication and manage the concealment of hatred.

**[B]** Impairs — sustained contempt destroys the capacity to distinguish true from false address (Jer 23:17). The community that despises God's word cannot discern between true and false prophets.

**[C]** Rom 2:22 shows that abhorrence can be claimed cognitively without the corresponding inner state — a form of cognitive performance without inner reality. This reveals a distinctive cognitive danger: the cognitive assertion of abhorrence can mask the absence of genuine moral recoil.

**[D]** Impairs in a specific direction — the cognitive framework operates without the governing premise of God-consciousness, producing systematically distorted moral reasoning.

**[E]** Distorts cognitive self-assessment — the reproached person internalises the social verdict and holds it as a cognitive self-understanding ("I am a worm").

**[F]** Sharpens — requires sober-mindedness and disciplined cognitive alertness.

**[G]** Inverts — the cognitive evaluation of the other's destruction as a good is the deepest cognitive distortion in M06.

**T3.2.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** Cognition and M06 characteristics are in a reciprocal relationship: accurate cognition (genuine understanding of moral reality) produces righteous forms of hatred and abhorrence; disordered M06 characteristics corrupt, impair, or invert cognition. The cluster is not merely an affective phenomenon — it engages and distorts the rational-cognitive dimensions of the inner life. The moral evaluation faculty is the primary cognitive organ engaged across M06.

### T3.3 — Memory

**T3.3.1** — Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** Memory is central to the persistence of hatred, particularly in group 1200 (sa.tam — grudge-bearing). Gen 27:41: Esau remembers the blessing theft. Gen 50:15: Joseph's brothers remember what they did to him — and fear he remembers it too. The grudge lives in memory; without memory it cannot persist. Sa.tam is essentially hatred preserved in memory across time — the grudge is the memory of the wound kept alive in the inner being.

**[B — Contempt]** Memory is not prominently engaged in M06-B. Contempt does not characteristically persist through memory of a specific injury; it persists through a habitual inner posture of evaluation. The retrospective lament of Pro 5:12 ("how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof") — here memory perceives retrospectively what the contempt cost. Memory is the faculty that looks back on the contempt and names its damage, but not the faculty that sustains the contempt itself.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Memory is not prominently engaged in M06-C. Abhorrence is characteristically present-focused — triggered by current perception of the repugnant — rather than sustained by memory of past injury. The eschatological permanence of de.ra.on (Dan 12:2) is not a memory-sustained state but a permanent condition.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Memory is not visibly engaged in M06-D. Cruelty is not characteristically sustained by memory of a wound — it operates as a settled character condition independently of specific memories.

**[E — Reproach]** Memory is the faculty through which reproach persists after the external condition has changed. Isa 54:4: "you will forget the shame of your youth, and the reproach of your widowhood you will remember no more" — the explicit naming of forgetting as what restoration requires shows that memory is the organ through which reproach is carried beyond its immediate experience. Psa 89:50: "I bear in my heart the insults of all the many nations" — the bearing is a memory-carrying function.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Memory is not prominently engaged in M06-F. The adversary relationship is present-active rather than memory-sustained.

**[G — Malice]** Memory is not specifically evidenced for M06-G. The "never-ending enmity" (Eze 25:15) suggests the characteristic sustains itself through ongoing satisfaction rather than through memory of past injury.

**T3.3.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory?

**[A]** Deepens in a specific direction: the hater's memory of the injury or perceived wrong is preserved and returned to repeatedly. The characteristic selectively preserves injury-memory while suppressing reconciling-memory. Gen 50:15 shows the brothers assuming Joseph has been doing the same — holding the memory of what they did to him.

**[E]** Both — reproach is deposited in memory (deepening it in the direction of the wound) and ultimately requires the active operation of forgetting (for restoration to be complete). The memory faculty is the organ through which the reproach persists beyond its occasion.

**T3.3.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[A]** Hatred is a memory-organising characteristic — specifically in its sa.tam (grudge-bearing) form. It preserves the memory of injury and supplies it as justification for the ongoing disposition.

**[E]** Reproach is a memory-depositing characteristic — it leaves its mark in the memory faculty, requiring divine intervention and active new formation to complete the healing.

**[CLUSTER — partial]** The memory faculty is most prominently engaged by the two characteristics most closely associated with the persistence of a past wound: sa.tam hatred (the wound remembered as grudge) and reproach (the wound deposited in memory as identity). The other sub-groups do not characteristically operate through memory.

### T3.4 — Affect

**T3.4.1** — Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes, substantially. 2Sa 13:15: "Amnon hated her with very great hatred" — the intensity qualifier "very great" names the affective weight. Psa 25:19: "with what violent hatred they hate me" — violent names the affective character. Job 16:9 (sa.tam): "he has gnashed his teeth at me" — the somatic expression signals the affective intensity beneath. Ecc 9:6: hatred is listed alongside love and envy as one of the constitutive affective orientations of the living person. The affective dimension is present across all groups, though it varies in intensity.

**[B — Contempt]** Characteristically affectively cool rather than hot. Job 12:5: "in the thought of one who is at ease" — the ease is the affective register: contempt is comfortable, relaxed, indifferent. Est 1:18 (biz.za.von) pairs contempt with wrath ("contempt and wrath in plenty") — but the wrath is a separate phenomenon. The distinctive affective register of contempt is cold indifference or comfortable dismissal, not heated hostility.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The strongest affective register in M06 up to this point. The bdelussomai root (stench-based) and the ta.av root both carry physical resonance — the inner recoil has a quasi-somatic quality. The affective register of abhorrence is moral disgust — the moral equivalent of physical nausea. Strong, visceral, pulling away from its object.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Pro 27:4: "wrath is cruel (akh.ze.riy.yut), anger is overwhelming" — cruelty is named as the quality that wrath possesses when unrestrained by mercy. The affective register is the heat of wrath without the cooling of mercy. It is not the cool indifference of contempt — it is affective intensity (wrath, anger) operating without the limiting counterweight.

**[E — Reproach]** M06-E has the most extensive affective evidence in the cluster. Psa 69:20: "broken my heart...in despair" — not sadness but despair. Psa 31:11: "an object of dread to my acquaintances" — the reproached person produces dread in others; their affective condition is visible externally. Neh 1:3: "great trouble and shame." Jer 51:51: "we are put to shame, for we have heard reproach" — shame as the inner-affective response to received reproach. The affective register of M06-E is despair, shame, and fear.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Mat 5:25: "come to terms quickly" — urgency is the dominant affective register. 1Pe 5:8's watchfulness implies alert attentiveness rather than fearful anxiety. Job 22:20: relief at the adversary's defeat names the affective release when the adversary relationship ends. The affective register is urgency → vigilance → relief.

**[G — Malice]** The most precisely named affective content in M06: "rejoiced" (Eze 25:6), "wholehearted joy" (Eze 36:5). The affective register is positive in quality — malice feels like joy to the one who holds it. This is what makes it distinctive and self-reinforcing: it is not an unpleasant inner state that might motivate its own cessation but a pleasurable one. The soul rejoices; the rejoicing deepens the malice.

**T3.4.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect?

**[A]** In the concealment register: impairs honest affective expression — the person must suppress the true affective state and perform its opposite (false peace). In group 1200 (sa.tam): transforms affect from acute anger into sustained smouldering state — the heat of the moment becomes the cold persistence of the grudge.

**[B]** Contempt is characteristically affectively flat — the cool dismissal. It does not intensify affect but evacuates it: what should produce respect and reverence produces nothing. It impairs the affective registering of worth.

**[C]** Abhorrence is affectively intense — it amplifies the moral-disgust register of the affective faculty. Righteous abhorrence deepens the affective response to moral violation; inverted abhorrence redirects that intensity toward what should be welcomed.

**[D]** Cruelty impairs the compassion-register of affect — the affective response to another's suffering that would normally trigger mercy is absent. Pro 12:10: even the cruel person's mercy-attempts are shaped by the underlying cruelty.

**[E]** Reproach impairs — it produces a sustained state of shame, fear, and despair that reshapes the whole affective orientation of the person toward their social environment.

**[F]** Sharpens urgency — the adversary relationship heightens affective alertness.

**[G]** Inverts — the normally aversive affective response to another's suffering (compassion, sorrow) is replaced by pleasure and joy. The affective faculty is operating but in the most complete inversion possible.

**T3.4.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** The affective registers across M06 are significantly differentiated: hatred (hot/intense), contempt (cool/indifferent), abhorrence (visceral/disgust), cruelty (unrestrained force), reproach/received (despair/shame/fear), hostility/enmity (urgent/alert), malice (perverse joy). These are not the same affective temperature or quality. The cluster contains the full range from the coolest (contempt) to the most visceral (abhorrence) to the most perversely positive (malice). The affective differentiation is analytically important — the characteristics are not merely the same hostility at different intensities.

### T3.5 — Creativity

**T3.5.1** — Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes, in the disordered register. Gen 27:41: Esau's hatred generates a plan — "then I will kill my brother Jacob" — the hatred has recruited imagination to construct a future scenario. Psa 41:7: "all who hate me whisper together about me; they imagine the worst for me" — the verb names a cognitive-creative act of projecting negative futures for the hated person. Pro 26:24: "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips" — the concealment requires creative construction of a false persona.

**[B — Contempt]** Not evidenced. Contempt is characteristically unreflective — it dismisses rather than plans. The creative faculty is not visibly engaged.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Not evidenced. Abhorrence recoils rather than plans. Silent.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Not evidenced. Cruelty operates through the absence of restraint rather than through creative planning. Silent.

**[E — Reproach]** Not evidenced from the recipient's perspective. The person bearing reproach is not characteristically creative in response — they are more typically constrained and paralysed (Job 31:34). Silent.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Not evidenced. The response to the adversary relationship (appeal, vigilance) does not characteristically engage the creative faculty.

**[G — Malice]** Not evidenced. Malice operates through the soul's pleasure in another's destruction rather than through creative origination.

**T3.5.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity?

**[A]** In the disordered register: distorts — channels the creative capacity into destructive planning and deceptive self-presentation. In the righteous register: no evidence of creativity being engaged; righteous hatred is more receptive (downstream of understanding) than creative.

**T3.5.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** The creative faculty is largely absent from M06. Only hatred (M06-A, disordered register) engages creativity, and the engagement is distorted toward destructive ends. The cluster as a whole suppresses, bypasses, or is indifferent to the creative faculty. M06 characteristics are not primarily creative — they destroy, dismiss, recoil, dominate, burden, or take pleasure in destruction. None of them originate new possibilities or beauty.

### T3.6 — Volition

**T3.6.1** — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** Deeply. Gen 27:41: Esau decides internally "then I will kill my brother Jacob" — the volitional resolution is explicit and internal. Num 35:20: "if he pushed him out of hatred or hurled something at him, lying in wait" — the "lying in wait" names a volitional act of prepared hostility. In the righteous register: Psa 26:5 — "I hate the assembly of evildoers, and I will not sit with the wicked" — the volitional "I will not" arises from and expresses the righteous hatred.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt organises the will away from the despised object. Gen 25:34: Esau's despising of the birthright produces a volitional act — he eats, drinks, rises, goes his way. The will acts in accordance with the verdict that the birthright has no binding claim. Num 15:31: "he has despised the word of the Lord and has broken his commandment" — the contempt produces the volitional choice to break.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Abhorrence organises the will toward distance and separation. Deu 7:26: "you shall utterly detest and abhor it, for it is devoted to destruction" — the commanded abhorrence is a commanded volitional orientation away from what is abhorrent. In the inverted form (group 252-002): the leaders who abhor justice (Mic 3:9) have a will organised away from what is right — "who make crooked all that is straight" — the volitional acts follow the direction of the abhorrence.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty organises the will toward unrestrained destructive action. The cruel person's will is not inhibited by mercy, restraint, or fear of God — the result is volition that acts without the limiting goods. Isa 13:9: the ruthless volitional pattern (destroy without mercy) gives the judgment vocabulary its intensity.

**[E — Reproach]** The volitional impact of reproach is primarily in the paralysis or constraint it produces. Job 31:34: "because I stood in great fear of the multitude, and the contempt of families terrified me, so that I kept silence, and did not go out of doors" — the reproach-fear produced volitional paralysis. The counter-volitional response: 1Th 2:2: Paul had "boldness in our God to declare the gospel" despite shameful treatment — the volitional choice to continue speaking despite reproach is grounded in God.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Mat 5:25: "come to terms quickly with your accuser" — the adversary relationship generates urgent volitional action toward resolution. 1Pe 5:8: "resist him, firm in your faith" — the cosmic adversary requires active volitional resistance.

**[G — Malice]** Eze 25:15: "took vengeance with malice of soul to destroy in never-ending enmity" — the malice drives toward active destruction as its volitional goal. The will is organised toward the perpetuation of the other's suffering.

**T3.6.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?

**[A]** Both enables (toward hostile action in the disordered register; toward righteous refusal in the ordered register) and constrains (righteous hatred closes off volitional options — "I will not sit with the wicked," Psa 26:5).

**[B]** Enables a specific volitional direction — away from the despised object — while closing off the volitional response of compliance and obedience. Contempt de-obligates the will from the claims of what has been despised.

**[C]** Organises the will toward separation (righteous form) or toward the destruction of what is good (inverted form). The volitional impact follows the direction of the abhorrence.

**[D]** Enables destructive agency without restraint. The characteristic removes the volitional brakes that fear of God and mercy would have applied.

**[E]** Impairs — reproach produces volitional paralysis (Job 31:34). The counter-movement toward boldness (1Th 2:2) is possible but requires a specific resource (boldness in God) to overcome the paralysis.

**[F]** Drives urgency and active resistance. The adversary relationship demands volitional response.

**[G]** Organises the will toward purposive destruction of the other, sustained across time ("never-ending").

**T3.6.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** Every M06 characteristic engages the volitional faculty — none is a purely passive inner state. Each organises the will in a specific direction: hatred (toward hostile action or righteous refusal), contempt (toward de-obligation and non-compliance), abhorrence (toward distance or toward destruction of good), cruelty (toward unrestrained destructive action), reproach (toward paralysis or boldness), hostility (toward urgent resolution or resistance), malice (toward purposive ongoing harm). The cluster consistently produces volitional organisation — M06 is not inert but active.

### T3.7 — Agency

**T3.7.1** — Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. Hatred is named as the inner ground from which hostile action originates. Num 35:20: "if he pushed him out of hatred or hurled something at him" — the hatred is the legal criterion that distinguishes murder from manslaughter. The act of pushing or hurling is the agency expression; the hatred is the inner-being ground that makes it murder. Gen 27:41: Esau's murder plan is the agency expression of his sa.tam.

**[B — Contempt]** Yes — contempt enables agency in a specific direction: it removes the felt obligation to act in a particular way toward the despised object. The priests who despise God's name (Mal 1:6) do not feel bound to honour the offering — their agency operates as if God's name has no claim. The characteristic de-obligates the agent from the demands the despised thing would otherwise make.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Yes. Psa 5:6: "the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man" — followed immediately by God's destructive action against them. The divine abhorrence produces divine action. Isa 66:24: the dead bodies of those who rebelled against God are an abhorrence — the divine agency of judgment has acted against them. Abhorrence-driven agency is separation-producing and, in God's case, judgment-producing.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** This is the faculty most visibly engaged in M06-D. Cruelty and ruthlessness are characteristically agentic — they exercise power without restraint. Group 1775-002 is almost entirely about the agency of the ruthless: "the most ruthless of nations" drawing swords, filling lands with the slain. The agency of the ruthless is unrestrained, massive, and directed toward destruction. M06-D is the most agency-saturated sub-group.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach reduces agency — the person under the condition has less effective agency because their social standing has been stripped. Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man" — the image of the worm is the image of something without effective agency. But the restorative dimension shows agency being restored: 1Sa 17:26: David takes up the challenge against Goliath specifically to "take away the reproach from Israel" — the restoration of honour is an act of agency. God is the primary agent of reproach-removal (Gen 30:23, Jos 5:9, Isa 25:8).

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship activates agency in two directions: (1) appeal to God as advocate (Psa 35:1, Isa 49:25) and (2) active resistance of the adversary (1Pe 5:8). Both are agentic responses generated by the relational structure.

**[G — Malice]** Eze 25:15: "took vengeance with malice of soul to destroy in never-ending enmity" — malice drives toward active destruction. The agency of malice is purposive and sustained — it seeks occasions to cause harm.

**T3.7.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?

**[A]** Enables and directs agency toward harmful ends in person-directed hatred; toward just ends in righteous hatred (Amos 5:15: "hate evil, and love good, and establish justice in the gate").

**[B]** Enables agency in the direction of non-compliance; removes the moral constraint that would limit agency. Contempt liberates the will from the perceived claim of the despised object.

**[C]** Enables agency toward separation; in the inverted form, enables agency toward the destruction of what is good.

**[D]** Enables and maximises agency without limit — cruelty removes the restraints that would normally bound agency. The result is maximal destructive agency.

**[E]** Impairs agency (paralysis through reproach) until restored by divine intervention or grounded boldness.

**[F]** Enables and urgently directs agency toward resolution or resistance.

**[G]** Enables purposive destructive agency sustained across time.

**T3.7.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** M06 characteristics are inherently agency-seeking — none remains purely internal. Every sub-group drives toward expression, action, and consequence. The legal texts (Deu 19:11: "if anyone hates his neighbour and lies in wait for him") treat the inner characteristic as the cause that makes a subsequent act what it is. The cluster's consistent agency-seeking nature confirms that inner-being characteristics are not neutral — they have trajectories that move toward external expression.

### T3.8 — Moral Evaluation

**T3.8.1** — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?

**[A — Hatred]** This is the faculty most centrally engaged in the righteous register. Pro 8:13: "the fear of the Lord is hatred of evil" — the moral evaluation of evil as evil is what produces the hatred. Psa 45:7: "you have loved righteousness and hated wickedness" — the moral evaluation of righteousness (as worthy of love) and wickedness (as worthy of hatred) is the operative faculty. In the disordered register: hatred bypasses or corrupts moral evaluation of the hated person (the hater cannot evaluate the hated person fairly because the hostile disposition prejudices the evaluation). Psa 36:2: the wicked "flatters himself in his own eyes" — his moral self-evaluation is corrupted by a related avoidance of having his iniquity "found out and hated."

**[B — Contempt]** The moral evaluation faculty is the primary operative faculty of contempt. Contempt is an act of moral evaluation — specifically, a wrong verdict rendered by the moral evaluation faculty. Every instance involves a wrong assessment of worth: Esau wrongly assesses the birthright as worth less than food; the priests wrongly assess God's name as not demanding honour; the comfortable person wrongly assesses the sufferer's misfortune as not deserving attention.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Moral evaluation is the faculty that names and confirms the abhorrence. The characteristic operates at a level deeper than moral evaluation (the visceral recoil precedes the verdict) but the moral evaluation faculty is what identifies the object as worthy of abhorrence and maintains the response as morally valid. Rom 2:22's challenge works precisely here: the claimed abhorrence of idols (a moral evaluation position) is exposed as not corresponding to an actual inner state.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The moral evaluation faculty is characteristically absent or impaired in M06-D. Pro 12:10: "the righteous has regard for the life of his beast" — the righteous person evaluates animal life as worthy of regard; the wicked person does not make this evaluation. The cruel person's moral evaluation faculty has ceased to register the worth of what would otherwise trigger mercy. The faculty is not inverted (as in M06-B's contempt of justice) but simply absent in its mercy-triggering function.

**[E — Reproach]** The moral evaluation faculty is engaged in cognitive resistance to reproach. Isa 51:7: "you who know righteousness, the people in whose heart is my law; fear not the reproach of man" — the internal moral knowledge (knowing righteousness) is the evaluative resource that enables the person to resist internalising the false social verdict. The faculty is called to evaluate the reproach itself as unjust and to maintain that evaluation against social pressure.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Moral evaluation is operative in the assessment of whether the adversary relationship is just: God's taking up of the adversary posture (Exo 23:22) is grounded in his moral evaluation of the situation. The human's appeal to God (Psa 35:1) implicitly calls on God's moral evaluation as the arbiter.

**[G — Malice]** The moral evaluation faculty has been most completely corrupted in M06-G. Eze 36:5: "wholehearted joy and utter contempt" — the moral evaluation of the other's destruction as a positive good is the deepest cognitive distortion in M06. The faculty is operating but has evaluated destruction as good and the other's suffering as deserved and pleasurable.

**T3.8.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?

**[A]** Righteous register: deepens and sharpens. Disordered register: corrupts — prevents fair evaluation of the hated person.

**[B]** Corrupts in both directions — the contemner evaluates the sacred as worthless (wrong verdict) and becomes unable to evaluate accurately because the verdict has already been rendered.

**[C]** Righteous abhorrence deepens moral evaluation — the person who genuinely abhors evil has a more sensitive and accurate moral evaluation faculty. Inverted abhorrence impairs — it produces systematically reversed moral verdicts.

**[D]** Impairs in the mercy-triggering direction — the moral evaluation faculty fails to register the worth of life and the claims of the vulnerable.

**[E]** The person bearing unjust reproach must actively maintain an accurate moral evaluation against social pressure — the faculty is strengthened when it successfully resists the false social verdict.

**[F]** Enables and grounds clear moral evaluation of the adversary situation.

**[G]** Most thoroughly corrupts — evaluates destruction as good.

**T3.8.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** The moral evaluation faculty is the most universally engaged inner faculty across M06, and in every agent-side sub-group except M06-F, it is operating in a disordered, inverted, absent, or corrupt form. The cluster is, at its core, the documentation of the moral evaluation faculty's failure modes. When the moral evaluation faculty functions rightly (righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence, the appeal to God's moral arbitration in M06-F), it produces ordered responses. When it fails, it produces the full range of M06's disordered characteristics.

### T3.9 — Conscience

**T3.9.1** — Does this characteristic engage the conscience?

**[A — Hatred]** Psa 36:2: the wicked flatters himself that his iniquity cannot "be found out and hated" — the conscience (the faculty that would find out and hate iniquity) is suppressed or evaded. Pro 5:12: "how I hated discipline, and my heart despised reproof!" — hatred of discipline is a suppression of the conscience's external channel. In the righteous register: the person who hates evil (group 550-NEW-02) has a functioning conscience that produces sustained opposition to what violates moral truth.

**[B — Contempt]** Group 172-002 (contempt of reproof) shows conscience's relationship to contempt most clearly. Pro 5:12: "my heart despised reproof" — the contempt of reproof is the contempt of the mechanism through which conscience operates. Reproof is conscience made external; despising reproof is the inner act of closing the conscience's external channel. The characteristic attacks the conscience's access to the person.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Abhorrence is what a functioning conscience feels in the presence of moral violation. Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001) is the conscience's deepest response to what is genuinely repugnant. Inverted abhorrence (group 252-002) is the sign of a conscience that has been comprehensively corrupted — it now recoils from the right thing and welcomes the wrong.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The conscience's restraining function is what cruelty has removed. Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel" — the conscience's alarm signal is not operative in restraining the person's conduct. Lam 4:3: the comparison with jackals (who nurse their young) shows that even the animal nurturing instinct is present where the cruel person's conscience has been silenced.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach and conscience interact in two directions. When reproach is deserved (group 322-002): Jer 31:19 — "I was ashamed, and I was confounded, because I bore the disgrace of my youth" — the conscience confirms the reproach as appropriate, producing shame and genuine penitence. When reproach is undeserved (group 322-001): the conscience is what enables the person to know the reproach is unjust and to appeal against it to God.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** 1Pe 5:8's "be sober-minded" includes conscience-awareness — the adversary relationship demands that the person maintain moral clarity about what they are facing and how to respond. The conscience keeps the person's response within the bounds of what is appropriate.

**[G — Malice]** Malice represents the conscience's most complete corruption — the faculty now rejoices at what should produce sorrow and finds pleasure in what should produce moral recoil. The conscience has not merely been suppressed (as in M06-D) but has been inverted to celebrate what it was made to condemn.

**T3.9.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience?

**[A]** Righteous hatred deepens and sharpens conscience. Disordered hatred suppresses conscience (hatred of discipline = refusing the conscience's external channel).

**[B]** Impairs — contempt of reproof attacks conscience's primary access mechanism.

**[C]** Righteous abhorrence is the conscience's deepest felt expression — it deepens the conscience. Inverted abhorrence is the conscience's comprehensive reversal — it impairs it fundamentally.

**[D]** Impairs by removal — the conscience's mercy-triggering and restraint-producing function has been silenced.

**[E]** The conscience is what distinguishes deserved from undeserved reproach — it is the internal moral compass that enables the person to navigate the condition accurately.

**[F]** Maintained and clarified through sober-mindedness.

**[G]** Most completely corrupts — the conscience celebrates harm rather than being alarmed by it.

**T3.9.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** The conscience is the primary faculty through which M06 characteristics are exercised (rightly), resisted, or corrupted. The cluster maps the full spectrum of the conscience's operation: sharpened (righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence), suppressed (hatred of discipline, cruelty's removed restraint), closed (contempt of reproof), maintained under pressure (M06-E's bearing of unjust reproach), and inverted (malice's pleasure in harm). M06 is the cluster that most comprehensively documents what happens to the conscience when the inner being is organised against what is good.

### T3.10 — Conscientiousness

**T3.10.1** — Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness?

**[A — Hatred]** In the righteous register (group 550-NEW-02): Exo 18:21 — men who "hate a bribe" are named as qualified for leadership. The hatred of bribery is not merely a moral attitude but a reliable predictor of conscientious conduct. The integrated response is: moral awareness (bribery is wrong) + volitional commitment (I hate it) + action (therefore I will not take it). Amo 5:15: "hate evil, and love good, and establish justice in the gate" — the commanded hatred of evil is the inner spring of conscientious public conduct.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt systematically dismantles conscientiousness in its domain. The priests of Mal 1 are the clearest example: their role requires conscientious attention to the honour of God's name; their contempt has made this conscientiousness impossible. They go through the motions while having internally rendered a verdict that the motions do not matter. Contempt produces performed conscientiousness — the appearance without the inner reality.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001) undergirds reliable conscientiousness: the person who genuinely abhors corruption is constitutionally resistant to it. The hatred of falsehood (Psa 119:163) makes the conscientious rejection of falsehood stable rather than effortful. In the inverted form (group 252-002): the leaders who abhor justice (Mic 3:9) are diligent in the wrong direction — they "make crooked all that is straight" — their conscientiousness has been inverted.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The ruthless nations (group 1775-002) exercise conscientiousness in their military campaigns — they are thorough and effective — but this conscientiousness is directed entirely toward destruction rather than toward the goods that would limit it. Pro 17:11: "an evil man seeks only rebellion" — conscientiousness without the limiting goods.

**[E — Reproach]** Jer 15:15: "for your sake I bear reproach" — the prophet's conscientiousness in his call produces reproach. The conscientious person (faithful to their calling, truthful in their speech) is precisely the one most exposed to reproach in a community that despises faithfulness. M06-E shows that conscientiousness does not protect from reproach — in a morally disordered context, it produces it.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** 1Pe 5:8's sober-minded watchfulness is a form of spiritual conscientiousness — the sustained, attentive practice of vigilance against the cosmic adversary.

**[G — Malice]** The malicious nations are conscientious in their pursuit of Israel's destruction — Eze 25:15: "to destroy in never-ending enmity." Their conscientiousness is organised entirely toward harm.

**T3.10.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness?

**[A]** Righteous hatred enables and sustains conscientiousness — the person who hates bribes (Exo 18:21) is reliably conscientious in contexts where bribery is possible. Disordered hatred produces performed conscientiousness that eventually breaks (Pro 26:26: "his wickedness will be exposed in the assembly").

**[B]** Impairs — contempt dismantles the inner ground of conscientiousness toward the despised object.

**[C]** Righteous abhorrence enables stable conscientiousness. Inverted abhorrence inverts it.

**[D, G]** Redirects conscientiousness toward destructive ends.

**[E]** Conscientiousness in the face of reproach is possible but requires the specific resource of relationship with God (1Th 2:2).

**T3.10.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** Conscientiousness built on righteous characteristics (righteous hatred, righteous abhorrence) is stable — grounded in settled inner opposition to what is wrong. Conscientiousness performed over disordered characteristics (concealed hatred, contempt beneath performance, cruelty masked as efficiency, malice as thoroughness) is either unstable (eventually exposed) or inverted (thorough in the wrong direction). The inner-being ground of conscientiousness matters: the same behavioural diligence can be grounded in entirely different inner orientations, producing either genuine or corrupted conscientiousness.

### T3.11 — Relational Capacity

**T3.11.1** — Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity?

**[A — Hatred]** Most comprehensively affected faculty. Gen 37:4: "they hated him and could not speak peacefully to him" — the direct consequence of hatred is the loss of peaceful speech, which is the basic instrument of relational connection. Pro 15:17: "a fattened ox and hatred with it" — the presence of hatred in a household makes even abundance miserable. The relational capacity, when organised by hatred, is not destroyed but inverted — the capacity for connection remains but is directed against rather than toward the other.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt closes the relational capacity toward the despised object entirely. Isa 1:4: "they have despised the Holy One of Israel, they are utterly estranged" — contempt produces total estrangement. Unlike hatred which organises the relational capacity against the hated person, contempt removes the person from the relational field altogether — they are beneath regard, which means beneath relational engagement.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Abhorrence destroys the relational capacity toward the object completely through social abandonment. Job 19:19: "all my intimate friends abhor me, and those whom I loved have turned against me." Job 30:10: "they keep aloof from me; they do not hesitate to spit at the sight of me." This is more total than hatred (which at least maintains the hostile relational connection) and more total than contempt (which dismisses but does not necessarily avoid all contact). Abhorrence produces complete relational withdrawal.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty destroys the relational capacity in a specific way: it removes the person from the relational register of mercy and care, replacing it with domination and destruction. Lam 4:3: the daughter of the people has become "like the ostriches in the wilderness" — the nurturing relational capacity has been removed. The relational capacity remains in a functional sense (the cruel person relates to others) but has been stripped of the qualities that make relation genuinely human.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach assaults the relational capacity from the outside: Psa 31:11: "those who see me in the street flee from me" — the social isolation reproach produces is total. But the relational capacity toward God intensifies: the extensive appeal literature of M06-E (Psa 69:13, Jer 15:15, Joel 2:17, Lam 5:1) shows the relational capacity toward God intensifying as the relational capacity toward other humans is stripped. The characteristic strips horizontal relationships and drives the person toward the vertical relationship.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship reorganises the relational capacity — the person in the adversary relationship turns their relational capacity toward God (appeal) and away from the adversary (opposition). The adversary relationship is itself a relational structure that generates the appeal-posture as the primary relational response.

**[G — Malice]** Malice destroys the relational capacity toward the object of malice completely — the other exists only as the occasion for pleasure in their suffering. The relational capacity has been perverted into its opposite: what was made for genuine connection now orients toward the other's destruction with joy.

**T3.11.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity?

**[A]** Impairs deeply in person-directed register; the righteous register (550-NEW-02) may enable genuine relational capacity toward the righteous by clarifying who shares one's moral orientation.

**[B]** Impairs by removing the despised person from the relational field entirely.

**[C]** Impairs by producing total relational withdrawal; righteous form enables relational capacity toward those who share the moral orientation.

**[D]** Impairs by replacing relational mutuality with domination and destruction.

**[E]** Impairs horizontal relational capacity; intensifies vertical (God-ward) relational capacity.

**[F]** Reorganises rather than impairs — the relational capacity is redirected toward appeal.

**[G]** Most completely inverts — the relational capacity is directed toward destruction of the other with pleasure.

**T3.11.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[CLUSTER]** M06 characteristics consistently impair, invert, or destroy the relational capacity toward the object of the characteristic. No agent-side M06 characteristic enables or deepens the relational capacity toward the person who is the object of the hostility. The cluster documents the full range of ways the relational capacity can be degraded: organised against (hatred), removed from (contempt), withdrawn from (abhorrence), dominated (cruelty), perverted into pleasure at harm (malice). The horizontal relational capacity is consistently the most damaged faculty in M06. M06-E's intensification of the God-ward relational capacity is the inverse finding: the damage to horizontal relationships drives the person toward the vertical relationship with God.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

### T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human

**T4.1.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. God's hatred is directed toward the human person in two registers: (1) toward wickedness and those whose inner disposition is hatred of him (groups 3595, 3596 — 33 verses). Psa 5:5: "you hate all evildoers." Psa 11:5: "his soul hates the wicked." Deu 7:10: "repays to their face those who hate him, by destroying them." (2) The divine-to-human movement is consistently a response to what the human has done or become. Hos 9:15: "there I began to hate them. Because of the wickedness of their deeds" — the divine hatred is responsive to human choices.

**[B — Contempt]** Minimally and only in reactive/judicial form. Deu 32:19: "the Lord saw it and spurned them (na.ats), because of the provocation of his sons and his daughters." Lam 2:6: "the Lord has...spurned (na.ats) king and priest." These are judicial responses to Israel's prior contempt of him. God's movement toward the human in M06-B is not the extension of contempt as a disposition but the execution of a judicial response.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Yes, extensively. Group 252-003 (9 verses): Psa 106:40: "he abhorred his heritage." Amo 6:8: "I abhor the pride of Jacob." Psa 5:6: "the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man." God's abhorrence is sustained and serious — a real inner-being response to persistent covenant violation and corruption. The basis is always the accumulation of moral failure, never arbitrary.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Only in the descriptive sense of Isa 13:9 (the severity of divine judgment described using the cruelty term). The divine movement toward the human in M06-D is consistently the removal and judgment of the ruthless (Isa 29:20, 13:11) and the deliverance of those who suffer under them (Isa 49:25, Jer 15:21, 20:11). God is the answer to cruelty, not its practitioner toward the human person.

**[E — Reproach]** God acts to remove reproach from the human person (the dominant divine movement), and also imposes it as covenantal judgment (secondary, judicial). Removal: Gen 30:23 — "God has taken away my reproach." Jos 5:9 — "today I have rolled away the reproach of Egypt from you." Isa 25:8 — "the reproach of his people he will take away from all the earth." Imposition: Eze 5:14 — "I will make you a desolation and an object of reproach among the nations." Even the imposition is reversible (Eze 36:15).

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes — Exo 23:22: "I will be an enemy to your enemies and an adversary to your adversaries." God takes up the adversary posture on the human person's behalf. This is not God extending enmity toward the human but God positioning himself as adversary to those who threaten the human. God's movement toward the person is as advocate and contender.

**[G — Malice]** God acts as the judge of malice, not as its subject or practitioner. Eze 25:6-7: "because you have clapped your hands and stamped your feet and rejoiced with all the malice within your soul against the land of Israel, therefore, behold, I will stretch out my hand against you." God's movement toward the human person who exercises malice is judicial response.

**T4.1.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic?

**[A]** God's hatred is consistently responsive and morally grounded — never arbitrary. Hos 9:15: "because of the wickedness of their deeds." Jer 12:8: "she has lifted up her voice against me; therefore I hate her." Every instance of divine hatred in M06-A has an explicit moral grounding.

**[B]** Reactive and judicial — in response to Israel's prior contempt of God.

**[C]** In response to the accumulated weight of covenant violation (Psa 106:40: "then the anger of the Lord was kindled against his people") and to persistent pride (Amo 6:8: "the pride of Jacob").

**[E — Imposition of reproach]** In response to covenant unfaithfulness: Eze 22:4: "you have become guilty by the blood that you have shed...therefore I have made you a reproach to the nations."

**[CLUSTER]** Across all sub-groups, God's engagement with M06 characteristics is consistently: (1) responsive to human moral choices, never arbitrary; (2) covenantally grounded; (3) aimed at justice, deliverance, or restoration. God does not extend M06 characteristics as character expressions toward innocent or righteous persons — his movement is always in response to what the human has become or done, or in advocacy for those who suffer under others' M06 characteristics.

**T4.1.3** — What does God's engagement with this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?

**[A]** God's hatred of evil and of those who persistently choose it reveals the depth and seriousness of his moral commitments. He is not indifferent to wickedness — his inner being (nephesh) is set against it. This means that when God acts in judgment against evil, he is not merely applying an external rule but expressing an inner disposition. Psa 5:5-6 shows the dual face: God hates evildoers and abhors the bloodthirsty — this is paired with God's grace toward those who seek him (v.7). The hatred and the welcome exist in the same God, directed toward different objects.

**[E]** God's movement to remove reproach from the human person reveals that he takes seriously the dignity and honour that belongs to persons made in his image. Reproach violates something God cares about restoring. The eschatological promise (Isa 25:8 — taking away reproach from all the earth) shows that the removal of this condition is part of God's ultimate purpose for creation.

**[F]** God's taking up of the adversary posture (Exo 23:22) reveals that his disposition toward the human person is covenantally committed — he will position himself against whatever threatens his covenant partner. His advocacy is not passive but active contention.

**T4.1.4** — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note explicitly.

Not applicable — God-to-human operation is evidenced for all sub-groups, though the form and basis differ.

### T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God

**T4.2.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God?

**[A — Hatred]** Two directions. (1) Human hatred of God (group 3596 — 14 verses): "those who hate me" (Exo 20:5) — hating God is the defining inner orientation of those in covenant rupture. (2) Human hatred of evil as covenant alignment (group 550-NEW-02): Psa 97:10: "O you who love the Lord, hate evil!" — hating what God hates is an act of covenant participation. These are opposite expressions of the same relational orientation toward God.

**[B — Contempt]** The dominant movement is the human's contempt directed toward God: despising his name (Mal 1:6), treating his table as despicable (Mal 1:7, 12), spurning his counsel (Psa 107:11), saying in the heart "you will not call to account" (Psa 10:13). The inner posture required to sustain this is covenantal presumption — the assumption that God's claims are negotiable. Contempt toward God operates from within the covenantal relationship, making it a form of covenant betrayal from within.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The righteous abhorrence of evil as covenant alignment: Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law" — the abhorrence of falsehood is the negative expression of what loving God's law means positively. In the inverted form: abhorring justice (Amo 5:10, Mic 3:9) is the inner movement away from God — those who abhor what God loves are moving against him in their inner constitution.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The ruthlessness of M06-D is the inner condition of those who have most comprehensively moved away from God — "they do not set God before themselves" (Psa 54:3). The ruthless person's inner movement is the maximum possible distance from God in the M06 cluster — not merely despising him or hating him but removing him from the inner governing framework entirely.

**[E — Reproach]** The dominant human-to-God movement in M06-E is appeal. Joel 2:17: "spare your people, O Lord, and make not your heritage a reproach." Psa 119:39: "turn away the reproach that I dread." Lam 5:1: "remember, O Lord, what has befallen us; look, and see our disgrace." The characteristic does not generate human contempt or hatred toward God — it generates the opposite: intense appeal from within the condition of being reproached.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Appeal is the dominant human-to-God movement. Psa 35:1: "contend, O Lord, with those who contend with me." Jer 18:19: "hear me, O Lord, and listen to the voice of my adversaries." The person facing an adversary turns to God and asks him to contend. The human-to-God movement in M06-F is the appeal-posture in the context of adversarial opposition.

**[G — Malice]** No verse in M06-G shows malice operating in the human person's movement toward God. The malice of the nations is directed at Israel; God is named only as the judge of that malice. Silent.

**T4.2.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required?

**[A]** For righteous hatred (alignment): the fear of the Lord as the prior orienting posture (Pro 8:13). For hatred of God (rupture): the person has turned against God in their innermost orientation — no specific prior posture is named, but the sustained covenant violation that produces it is documented.

**[B]** For contempt of God: covenantal presumption — the false inner conviction that God is not accountable or that his claims are negotiable.

**[E]** For appeal: awareness of need and trust that God can and will act. Jer 15:15: "for your sake I bear reproach" — the prophet's relationship with God is the inner posture from which the appeal is made.

**T4.2.3** — What does the human-to-God direction reveal about the person's relationship with God?

**[A — Hatred of God]** Names a state of total covenantal rupture. Psa 81:15: "those who hate the Lord would cringe toward him, and their fate would last forever" — the hatred has become so settled that even eventual forced acknowledgment cannot restore what has been lost.

**[A — Righteous hatred]** Psa 139:21: "do I not hate those who hate you, O Lord?" — the psalmist's relational orientation is so aligned with God's that they share God's inner disposition toward those who oppose him. This is among the most complete expressions of covenantal alignment in M06.

**[E, F — Appeal]** The appeal-posture reveals a person who, even in the condition of reproach or facing an adversary, maintains their fundamental relational orientation toward God as the source of justice and vindication. The horizontal relational field has been damaged; the vertical relational capacity intensifies in response.

**T4.2.4** — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note explicitly.

**[G]** Human-to-God operation is silent in M06-G (malice). The malicious person is not named as directing malice toward God — it is directed at other human beings (Israel). God appears only as the judge, not as the object.

### T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving

**T4.3.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another?

**[A]** Yes, extensively in groups 1601 and 1200. Hatred is extended as an ongoing orientation shaping all interaction: Gen 37:4 — brothers give hatred to Joseph through their inability to speak peacefully. 2Sa 13:22 — Absalom gives his settled hatred to Amnon through years of silent enmity before acting. Pro 26:24 — the hater gives hatred through disguised speech. The giving of person-directed hatred is not typically a discrete act but an ongoing orientation.

**[B]** Yes. Contempt is given in multiple contexts: the comfortable giving contempt to the suffering (Job 12:5), the nations giving contempt to Israel (group 1277-001), the proud giving contempt to the righteous (Psa 123:4), priests giving contempt to God through hollow worship (Mal 1:6-12).

**[C]** Yes, in the receiving register. Job 19:19: "all my intimate friends abhor me" — the intimate friends give abhorrence to Job. Isa 49:7: "abhorred by the nation" — the nation gives abhorrence to the Servant. The giving of abhorrence is documented through what the recipient experiences.

**[D]** Yes. Groups 6966-001, 6967-001 document the giving of cruelty: the cruel person gives cruelty through their conduct toward others. Group 1775-002 documents it from the receiving side.

**[E]** M06-E is the receiving sub-group — it documents what is given to the person rather than what the person gives. The one verse that speaks of giving-reproach-to-another as something to be avoided is Psa 15:3: "nor takes up a reproach against his friend" — the righteous person is characterised by not wielding reproach against others.

**[F]** The giving of the adversary relationship is structural — another party positions themselves in opposition. The human giving in M06-F is the taking-up of the adversary posture.

**[G]** Yes. The nations give malice to Israel: Ammon (Eze 25:6), the Philistines (Eze 25:15), Edom (Eze 36:5). The giving is documented through the soul's rejoicing in Israel's destruction and the purposive acts of destruction.

**T4.3.2** — What inner conditions in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?

**[A]** An unresolved perceived injury, loss, or violation that has not been covered (Pro 10:12: love covers offences; hatred keeps them open). Sa.tam (group 1200) requires the wound's memory to be actively preserved as the fuel.

**[B]** Pride and comfortable superiority (Job 12:5, Psa 123:4), or cognitive misreading of God's accountability (Psa 10:13).

**[D]** The removal of God-consciousness from the inner governing framework (Psa 54:3).

**[G]** The prior compound of enmity and contempt already present and active, with the additional development of pleasure in the other's harm.

**T4.3.3** — What must the person have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?

**[A]** The person must have received (or perceived) an injury that they have not released. In the righteous register: the person must have received the instruction that enables accurate moral evaluation (Psa 119:104).

**[D]** The person must have become the kind of person who operates without God in their governing framework — a condition that develops through sustained movement away from God-consciousness.

**[G]** The person must have developed the compound of enmity + contempt + the specific further development of pleasure in harm — each building on the previous.

**T4.3.4** — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note explicitly.

**[E]** M06-E is structurally the receiving end of the cluster. The giving direction is minimally evidenced — primarily through what others give to the person. Psa 15:3 shows the righteous person not giving reproach, but the giving of reproach is not M06-E's primary evidential register.

### T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving

**T4.4.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person?

**[A]** Yes — the receiving of hatred is documented across many verses. Gen 29:31: "Leah was hated" — the condition of being hated named as a state she inhabits. Gen 37:4: Joseph receives his brothers' hatred. Psa 69:4: "those who hate me without cause." Isa 66:5: "your brothers who hate you and cast you out for my name's sake." The receiving of hatred is experienced as a relational condition — not just a series of hostile acts but a state the person is placed in.

**[B]** Yes. Buz (group 316-001): the sufferer, the poor, and the righteous receive contempt from the comfortable and proud (Job 12:5, Psa 123:3-4). Shut (group 1277-001): Israel receives the contempt of surrounding nations (Eze 28:24). The one who receives contempt is placed outside the circle of the contemner's regard.

### T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries

**T4.5.1** — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance?

**[A]** Yes — the distinction is stark. The most intense and damaging hatred in M06-A operates within existing bonds: brothers hating a brother (Joseph's brothers), son hating father's heir (Esau/Jacob), husband withdrawing love from wife (group 550-NEW-01), Amnon's post-violation hatred of Tamar. Hatred within existing relational bonds violates the space where trust and expectation existed, which multiplies the damage.

**[B]** Contempt operates most destructively within covenant relationships: Israel's contempt of God (their covenantal partner). Between individuals: the comfortable person's contempt of the suffering is more damaging in close relationships (Job 12:5: "in the thought of one who is at ease").

**[C, D, G]** These characteristics operate across all relational distances without significant differentiation in the verse evidence — abhorrence, cruelty, and malice can be directed at those near or far.

**[E]** Reproach is most damaging when received from those close — Job 19:19: "all my intimate friends abhor me" — the reproach from intimate friends shatters where reproach from strangers might merely sting. Psa 55:12-13: "it is not an enemy who taunts me...but it is you, a man, my equal, my companion" — betrayal by a close associate is qualitatively different.

**T4.5.2** — Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?

**[CLUSTER]** Crosses all boundaries. Hatred operates between brothers (within family covenant), between spouses (marital covenant), between Israel and surrounding nations (across covenant boundaries), and between the human person and God (the ultimate covenant). Contempt crosses covenant boundaries: the nations hold contempt for Israel (shut, group 1277-001); Israel holds contempt for God (na.ats, group 172-001). Cruelty: the most ruthless of nations (M06-D) operate entirely outside any covenant relationship with Israel. No M06 characteristic is bounded by covenant proximity.

**T4.5.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope?

**[CLUSTER]** The relational scope of M06 characteristics is effectively unlimited. They can be directed at family members, neighbours, strangers, enemies, God's servants, and God himself. No relational category is immune. However, the covenantal context makes M06 characteristics more consequential when they operate within it — hate within covenant is treated as more serious precisely because it violates the relational terms the covenant established (e.g., Deu 31:20's sequence: prosperity → contempt → covenant-breaking).

**T4.5.4** — If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note explicitly.

Not applicable — relational boundary evidence is present across the cluster.

### T4.6 — Spiritual Beings Interface

**T4.6.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to spiritual beings?

**[A]** Thin. Job 16:9 (sa.tam) attributes hatred/grudge-bearing to God in a lament context ("he has torn me in his wrath and hated me; he has gnashed his teeth at me; my adversary sharpens his eyes against me") — Job's language moves between human and divine adversary, and the adversary figure is significant. Within the M06-A evidence itself, no verse explicitly names a spiritual being other than God as the subject of hatred.

**[B, C, D, E, G]** No evidence of contempt, abhorrence, cruelty, reproach, or malice operating in relation to angelic or adversarial spiritual beings in these sub-groups.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes — the most significant finding for the cluster. 1Pe 5:8: "your adversary the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour." The adversary relationship explicitly names the devil as the cosmic antidikos. This is the only verse in the entire M06 cluster that names an adversarial spiritual being as the active agent of a characteristic. The inner-being response commanded is sober watchfulness and firm resistance in faith — the same structural response as in the human-legal adversary context, but with the cosmic dimension added.

**T4.6.2** — Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity?

**[A]** Psa 105:25 is the strongest pointer: "He turned their hearts to hate his people" — God used the Egyptians' hatred as an instrument of his purposes. This shows that the characteristic can be directed or redirected by a higher agency. Whether adversarial spiritual beings do the same is not directly evidenced in M06-A, but the mechanism is visible: hearts can be turned toward hatred by a power external to the person.

**[F]** 1Pe 5:8: the devil actively pursues those who can be devoured — the adversary relationship in its cosmic form is the active instrument of adversarial spiritual activity.

**T4.6.3** — Is this characteristic communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry?

**[CLUSTER — all sub-groups]** No evidence of angelic mediation of any M06 characteristic across the cluster. Silent.

**T4.6.4** — If the evidence is silent on the spiritual beings interface, note explicitly.

**[B, C, D, E, G]** The spiritual-beings interface is silent for contempt (M06-B), abhorrence (M06-C), cruelty (M06-D), reproach (M06-E), and malice (M06-G). M06-F is the only sub-group where a spiritual being is explicitly named as the active agent of the characteristic (1Pe 5:8 — the devil as antidikos). The cluster's spiritual-beings interface is concentrated at a single point in a single sub-group.
