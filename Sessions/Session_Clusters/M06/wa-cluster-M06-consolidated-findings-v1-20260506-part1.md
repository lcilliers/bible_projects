## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**T0.1.1** — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**[A — Hatred]** The verses reveal that God himself is extensively the subject of hatred. Psa 11:5 locates it in his soul: "his soul hates the wicked and the one who loves violence." Isa 1:14 records God saying "my soul hates" disordered worship. Hos 9:15: "there I began to hate them. Because of the wickedness of their deeds." The pattern is consistent: God's hatred is a real inner-being disposition, located in his soul, always directed at wickedness and covenant violation — never arbitrary. This reveals that in God, hatred is the other side of his absolute commitment to righteousness. What he loves perfectly, he opposes perfectly. When the human person hates what God hates (Pro 8:13, Psa 97:10, Psa 139:21), they are aligning their inner being with God's own.

**[B — Contempt]** God holds minimal contempt as an originating characteristic. The two instances where na.ats is attributed to God (Deu 32:19 — "the Lord saw it and spurned them"; Lam 2:6 — "the Lord has spurned king and priest") are judicial responses to Israel's prior contempt of him, not an independent divine disposition. God does not regard his creation as beneath regard; he takes his own name, covenant, word, and people with full weight and seriousness. The relative silence is structurally significant: contempt — the posture of treating what is sacred as worthless — is not a characteristic of God toward his creation.

**[C — Abhorrence]** God is extensively the subject of abhorrence. Psa 106:40: "he abhorred his heritage." Psa 5:6: "the Lord abhors the bloodthirsty and deceitful man." Amo 6:8: "I abhor the pride of Jacob." The ta.av root carries physical resonance — the sense of something that cannot be tolerated in one's presence. God's abhorrence reveals that he is not indifferent to evil but constitutionally repelled by it at the level of his inner being. When the righteous person abhors falsehood (Psa 119:163), they are participating in God's own inner-being reality.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty and ruthlessness are not characteristics God possesses by inner nature. Isa 13:9 uses akh.za.ri to describe the day of the Lord's judgment in its severity ("the day of the Lord comes, cruel, with wrath and fierce anger"), but this describes the unsparing quality of the judgment, not a settled disposition of God's character. God is never named as cruel or ruthless by inner character in the way he is named as a hater of evil. Cruelty is defined by the absence of what God characteristically has — mercy, restraint, purposive care. It is therefore an anti-divine characteristic, naming the condition of persons in whom image-bearing capacities for restraint have been removed.

**[E — Reproach]** God's character is revealed in M06-E primarily through his response to the reproached, not through God being a subject of the characteristic. The dominant divine movement is removal: Gen 30:23 (God takes away Rachel's reproach), Jos 5:9 (God rolls away the reproach of Egypt), Isa 25:8 (God will take away the reproach of his people from all the earth). God also imposes reproach as covenantal judgment (Eze 5:14, Jer 23:40) — but even this is reversible. What the corpus reveals: God is constitutionally oriented toward the dignity and honour of persons made in his image. Reproach violates something God takes seriously enough to act to reverse.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Exo 23:22 is decisive: "I will be an enemy (a.yav) to your enemies and an adversary to your adversaries." God explicitly adopts the adversary posture on Israel's behalf. This reveals that the adversary structure is not intrinsically wrong — God can take it up. What makes an adversary relationship godly or ungodly is not the structure of opposition but what is being opposed and why. God's adversary posture is covenantal, responsive, and purposive — adopted in service of his people's protection.

**[G — Malice]** Malice is never attributed to God in the verse evidence. In Eze 25:6-7, 25:15, 36:5, God appears only as the one who names and judges the malice of the nations. The divine response to malice is judgment — not possession of the characteristic. Malice is the compound of enmity, contempt, and soul-level pleasure in another's destruction. None of these, in their compound form, is a characteristic God possesses. The absence is absolute and significant.

**[CLUSTER]** Within M06, the characteristics divide into those God shares with the righteous person (hatred of evil — M06-A; abhorrence of evil — M06-C; adversary posture in covenant faithfulness — M06-F) and those that are exclusively creaturely conditions of disorder (contempt — M06-B; cruelty — M06-D; malice — M06-G) or conditions God removes rather than holds (reproach — M06-E).

**T0.1.2** — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes, extensively. The same term (sa.ne) used for human hatred is used for God's hatred across multiple verses. The explicit attribution reveals that hatred of evil is not inherently a disordered or creaturely characteristic — it belongs to God's inner life. When the human person hates what God hates, the verses treat this as alignment with God, not as moral failure. Righteous hatred is therefore not merely a permitted human response but a participation in the divine inner life.

**[B — Contempt]** Minimally and only in a reactive/judicial form (Deu 32:19, Lam 2:6). The limited and reactive nature of divine contempt-attribution reveals that contempt as an originating inner posture is not a divine characteristic. Its significance in the human person is therefore as a disorder — an inversion of what should be reverence and honour — rather than as an expression of something God shares with them.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Yes, explicitly. Psa 5:6, Psa 106:40, Amo 6:8 all name God as the subject. The attribution reveals that abhorrence of evil is constitutive of God's character — not a secondary or reactive quality but an originating inner-being reality. When the human person shares this abhorrence (Psa 119:163), they are expressing the divine image.

**[D — Cruelty]** Only in the descriptive sense of Isa 13:9 (the severity of divine judgment). Never as a constitutive inner-being characteristic. The absence of attribution reveals that cruelty and ruthlessness belong to the category of characteristics that have no divine analogue — they are exclusively creaturely failures.

**[E — Reproach]** Not as a held characteristic. God imposes the condition covenantally (Eze 5:14) but does not himself inhabit the condition of being reproached as an inner-being state. The significance for the human person: reproach is a condition God takes seriously enough to act upon — both imposing it (as judgment) and removing it (as restoration). It matters to God.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes, explicitly in Exo 23:22. The attribution reveals that the adversary posture — structural opposition in service of covenant faithfulness — can be a righteous and even divine act. This makes M06-F unique among the cluster: it is the sub-group where the structural form (the adversary relationship) is shared with God in a positive sense.

**[G — Malice]** Not attributed. The absence reveals that malice — the compound of enmity, contempt, and pleasure in another's destruction — is purely creaturely, and specifically a condition of those in moral opposition to God. It has no divine analogue because its central element (pleasure in another's suffering) is incompatible with God's character.

**T0.1.3** — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?

**[A — Hatred]** Scripture is not silent — God's hatred is extensively attested. This prompt does not apply to M06-A in the sense of silence. The positive attribution is the finding.

**[B — Contempt]** The relative silence (only reactive, judicial uses of na.ats attributed to God) suggests that contempt as a settled inner posture of treating what is sacred as beneath regard is not a characteristic of the divine nature. Since human beings are made in God's image, and contempt is absent from God's originating character, contempt is a distortion of the image — an inversion of the reverence and honour-giving that belongs to the properly ordered creature.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Not applicable in the same sense — ta.av is extensively attributed to God. However, the silence on God abhorring persons gratuitously (his abhorrence is always grounded in what they have become or done) suggests that abhorrence, even in God, is always object-specific and morally grounded, never ambient or arbitrary.

**[D — Cruelty]** The silence is total for cruelty as an inner-being characteristic. This suggests that cruelty is among the furthest conditions from the divine nature — the opposite of mercy, restraint, and purposive severity, all of which God characteristically possesses. Cruelty's absence from God's nature means it cannot be an expression of the divine image; it can only be its negation.

**[E — Reproach]** God does not inhabit the condition of reproach as one who is reproached (he imposes and removes it). The silence on God experiencing reproach as an inner-social condition suggests that reproach belongs to the creaturely register — it is a condition that arises in the relational vulnerability of creatures among creatures, and in the covenantal context of faithfulness and unfaithfulness. It is not a condition of the divine being as such, though Christ's bearing of it (Psa 69:9 → Rom 15:3) is the redemptive exception.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The silence on God inhabiting a perpetual state of enmity toward his creation suggests that the adversary posture, while God can take it up (Exo 23:22), is not his natural orientation toward his creatures. His originating orientation is love and covenant; the adversary posture is adopted in response to specific conditions, not as a baseline.

**[G — Malice]** The complete silence on God possessing malice suggests it is as far from the divine nature as any characteristic in M06. Malice requires the coexistence of enmity, contempt, and pleasure in destruction — none of which characterises God's originating orientation toward his creation. The silence marks malice as an exclusively fallen and adversarial condition.

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

**T0.2.1** — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?

**[A — Hatred]** Group 550-NEW-02 (righteous hatred, anchor Pro 8:13) shows this directly. "The fear of the Lord is hatred of evil" — the verse makes hatred of evil constitutive of what it means to fear God. Psa 97:10 commands it: "O you who love the Lord, hate evil!" Psa 45:7 grounds the anointed king's authority in it: "you have loved righteousness and hated wickedness; therefore God your God has anointed you." The evidence shows that hatred of evil is not an incidental response but a necessary function of the properly ordered inner life — the negative pole of moral orientation that makes love of the good coherent. A person who loves righteousness without hating wickedness has an incomplete and unstable moral orientation.

**[B — Contempt]** No verse in M06-B suggests contempt serves a created purpose. Unlike righteous hatred, there is no commanded or praised contempt in M06-B. The characteristic appears entirely as a disordering of the inner being — the inversion of reverence and honour-giving. Whether contempt could theoretically have a righteous form (appropriate disregard for what is genuinely worthless) is not addressed by the verse evidence. The corpus treats contempt as a characteristically fallen disposition.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Group 252-001 shows the created purpose. The abhorrence of falsehood (Psa 119:163) is the negative pole of the love of God's law. The commanded abhorrence (Deu 23:7 — "you shall not abhor an Edomite...you shall not abhor an Egyptian") treats the capacity as real and regulable — implying it serves a proper function when rightly directed. The created purpose of abhorrence is to mark what is genuinely repugnant to God's character and to maintain the inner being's separation from it.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** No verse suggests cruelty serves a created purpose. It is defined by the absence of mercy, restraint, and fear of God — the condition produced when the inner governing framework that would limit destructive impulses is removed. Cruelty cannot be directed rightly because it is constitutively the absence of what directing rightly requires.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach does not serve a created purpose in the sense of a positive inner capacity. It is a condition imposed from outside — the experience of being an object of public contempt and shame. Unlike righteous hatred or abhorrence, there is no commanded righteous reproach. Reproach is what happens to the person, not what they exercise.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship serves the purpose of justice and covenant advocacy. Isa 49:25: "I will contend with those who contend with you" — the contention serves the purpose of deliverance for the oppressed. Exo 23:22 shows God adopting the adversary posture in service of covenant protection. The created purpose of the adversary-facing structure is not the hostility itself but the relational structure that creates the context for appeal, justice, and covenant vindication.

**[G — Malice]** No created purpose. Malice is the compound of enmity, contempt, and pleasure in destruction — all three of which represent the inversion or corruption of positive inner capacities. It has no righteous form and no created purpose.

**T0.2.2** — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**[A — Hatred]** Both. The righteous hatred of evil (group 550-NEW-02) belongs to the created design — it reflects God's own inner life and is described as the substance of fearing God, not as a compensatory response to sin. The bulk of the verse evidence (human-to-human hatred, group 1601, and grudge-bearing hatred, group 1200) documents hatred operating in the fallen condition, driving murder (Gen 27:41) and social violence. Whether person-directed hatred existed in any form before the fall is not directly addressed by the verses.

**[B — Contempt]** Fallen condition only. Every instance in M06-B is named as sin (1Sa 2:17), as covenant violation (Deu 31:20), or as the inner speech of the wicked (Psa 10:13). No verse presents contempt as a capacity serving the created design.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Both. The capacity to abhor belongs to the created design — God possesses it, and the righteous person shares it in alignment with God. The most prominent uses (groups 252-002 and 252-003) show the capacity inverted or directed against what is good — a fallen expression of the same capacity.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Fallen condition only. It is defined by the removal of God-consciousness (Psa 54:3), implying that when God was properly set before the person, the cruelty was absent. The condition is the product of removing the inner constraint that God-consciousness provides.

**[E — Reproach]** Fallen condition only. Reproach arises from the disordered inner lives of those who hate, despise, and mistreat others (M06-A through D). In a world without disordered inner lives, there would be no reproach.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Response to the fallen condition. God's adoption of the adversary posture (Exo 23:22) is responsive to the existence of enemies, not part of creation's original design. The adversary structure arises in a world where opposition exists.

**[G — Malice]** Fallen condition only. Malice is the compound of corrupted inner orientations — it has no place in the original created design.

**T0.2.3** — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. Psa 81:15: "those who hate the Lord would cringe toward him, and their fate would last forever" — hatred of God reaches its eschatological terminus in permanent reckoning. For righteous hatred (group 550-NEW-02): when evil is finally eliminated, the hatred directed at it will have completed its purpose. The righteous hatred is eschatologically oriented toward its own completion in the final defeat of what it opposes.

**[B — Contempt]** The eschatological trajectory is reversal, not completion. Isa 60:14: "all who despised you shall bow down at your feet." Num 14:23: "none of those who despised me shall see it." Those who held contempt meet eschatological reversal; those who were despised are vindicated. The characteristic's future orientation is its exposure and defeat, not its fulfilment.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The righteous abhorrence of evil (group 252-001) is oriented toward the future fullness when evil is finally eliminated. De.ra.on (group 1283-001) shows the eschatological terminus: Dan 12:2 — "shame and everlasting contempt" as the permanent condition of those who rebel. Abhorrence reaches its most complete expression when the eschatological moral verdict is rendered.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The future orientation is removal, not completion. Isa 29:20: "the ruthless shall come to nothing." Isa 13:11: "I will lay low the pompous pride of the ruthless." Cruelty does not move toward fullness but toward elimination from the created order.

**[E — Reproach]** The future orientation is complete removal and restoration of honour. Isa 25:8: "the reproach of his people he will take away from all the earth." Isa 54:4: "the reproach of your widowhood you will remember no more." The eschatological future for reproach is not its completion but its total removal followed by permanent honour.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary structure moves toward its own defeat. Job 22:20: "our adversaries are cut off." 1Pe 5:8's cosmic adversary has a terminus. The eschatological trajectory is the elimination of all adversarial opposition.

**[G — Malice]** The eschatological trajectory is divine judgment. Eze 25:6-7, 25:15-17 — God's judgment of the nations' malice is the response. The characteristic that claimed perpetuity ("never-ending enmity," Eze 25:15) meets divine judgment. It does not move toward fullness but toward its own ending.

### T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression

**T0.3.1** — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?

**[A — Hatred]** Hatred of evil expresses the divine image through moral alignment — hating what God hates, in the same direction and for the same reasons. Pro 8:13 makes this structural: "the fear of the Lord is hatred of evil." Psa 139:21: "do I not hate those who hate you, O Lord?" — the psalmist's hatred is consciously aligned with and derived from God's own. The image-bearing function of righteous hatred is its role as moral discernment made into an orientation: not just knowing that evil is wrong, but being constitutionally set against it.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt distorts rather than expresses the divine image. The image of God in the human person involves the capacity for accurate moral evaluation — assigning worth in accordance with what things actually are. Contempt inverts this: it assigns low worth to what has high worth (God's name, covenant, word). The capacity for worth-evaluation exists in the image-bearer; contempt is that capacity running in the wrong direction.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Righteous abhorrence (group 252-001) expresses the divine image by aligning the human person's inner moral recoil with God's own. Psa 119:163: "I hate and abhor falsehood, but I love your law" — the abhorrence of falsehood mirrors God's own. The image-bearing function is the conscience's participation in God's moral constitution: not merely following a rule but sharing a character response.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty expresses the negation of the divine image. Lam 4:3: "even jackals offer the breast; they nurse their young; but the daughter of my people has become cruel, like the ostriches in the wilderness." The comparison to ostriches — creatures who abandon their young — names cruelty as the failure of the most basic nurturing impulse. Even animals express what the cruel person has lost. The divine image includes mercy, restraint, and care for the vulnerable; cruelty is the condition of having lost these.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach attacks the divine image in the person who bears it. Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man, scorned by mankind and despised by the people" — the reproach has reduced the person, in their own experience and in the social register, to sub-human status. God's removal of reproach is therefore an act of image-restoration — the reinstatement of the honour that properly belongs to the image-bearer.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The capacity to stand in structured opposition on behalf of justice reflects the divine image as God exercises it in Exo 23:22. The image is expressed through the capacity for advocacy — taking up the adversary posture when covenant faithfulness requires it, on behalf of those who cannot contend for themselves.

**[G — Malice]** Malice is the most complete inversion of image-bearing joy in M06. The divine image includes the capacity for genuine rejoicing in what is good. Malice inverts this: it is the soul's rejoicing in destruction, which is the most complete inversion of the image-bearing capacity for joy. The soul was made to rejoice in life; malice rejoices in its ending.

**T0.3.2** — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively creaturely?

**[A — Hatred]** Shared. God hates (groups 3595, 3596 — extensively evidenced). The righteous person is commanded and praised for hating what God hates (group 550-NEW-02). Psa 139:21 shows the sharing is explicit and conscious: the psalmist's hatred is consciously derived from God's own orientation. This is not analogy but participation: the human person takes on God's own disposition as an act of alignment.

**[B — Contempt]** Not shared as an originating disposition. God's contempt-responses (Deu 32:19, Lam 2:6) are judicial and reactive, not expressions of an originating inner character. Contempt is a creaturely distortion — the inversion of reverence that belongs to the properly ordered creature before the Creator.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Shared. God abhors evil (Psa 5:6, Psa 106:40, Amo 6:8). The righteous person shares this abhorrence (Psa 119:163). The sharing is explicit in both the commands (Deu 23:7 regulates the direction, implying a shared capacity) and the psalmist's statements of alignment with God's own moral recoil.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Not shared. Cruelty is not a characteristic God possesses or shares with the image-bearer. It names the absence of what God has (mercy, restraint, purposive severity). It is exclusively a creaturely failure condition.

**[E — Reproach]** Not shared as a condition experienced. God removes reproach; he does not inhabit it. Christ's bearing of reproach (Psa 69:9 → Rom 15:3) is the redemptive exception — the Son entering the creaturely condition of being reproached in order to remove it.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Shared in the specific form of the adversary posture adopted in covenant faithfulness (Exo 23:22). God takes up the adversary relationship on behalf of his people. The capacity to stand in structured opposition as an act of advocacy is therefore shared — but only in its covenantal form, not in its hostile or adversarial form as directed against the person.

**[G — Malice]** Not shared. Malice has no divine form.

**T0.3.3** — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?

**[A — Hatred]** The presence of rightly-directed hatred (group 550-NEW-02) reveals the divine image operating — the person's inner life is aligned with God's. The presence of disordered person-directed hatred (group 1601 — "you shall not hate your brother in your heart," Lev 19:17) reveals image-disorder: the capacity meant to oppose evil has been turned against a fellow image-bearer. Job 34:17: "shall one who hates justice govern?" — hatred of justice is the disqualifying inner disposition, the sign that the image is disordered at its governing level.

**[B — Contempt]** Its presence is a direct indicator of image-disorder. Isa 1:4: "they have despised the Holy One of Israel, they are utterly estranged" — the contempt and the estrangement from God are sequential. Contempt of the sacred marks a person in whom the image's capacity for appropriate reverence and honour-giving has been inverted. The image is in disorder when the person despises what God values.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Righteous abhorrence reveals an image operating properly — the moral constitution is aligned with God's own. Inverted abhorrence (group 252-002 — abhorring justice) is among the clearest markers of a comprehensively reversed moral constitution: the capacity has been retrained to recoil from what it was made to receive and promote.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Psa 54:3's diagnostic is precise: "they do not set God before themselves." The image is in profound disorder when the person has removed God from their inner governing framework — when the consciousness of divine presence and accountability that would restrain destructive impulses is gone. Cruelty is the inner-being condition produced by the removal of God-consciousness from the person's operational centre.

**[E — Reproach]** For the one bearing reproach: the divine image in the bearer is not destroyed by reproach but assaulted, hidden, and temporarily eclipsed. The person can remain aligned with God even while their social status is destroyed — Psa 69:7: "for it is for your sake that I have borne reproach." For those imposing reproach: their contempt and hatred (M06-B, M06-A) reveal their image-disorder. God's restoration of honour to the reproached is an image-restoration act.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The capacity to stand in appeal before God when facing an adversary (Psa 35:1, Jer 15:15, Luk 18:3) reveals an image-bearing person who recognises their dependence and God's authority over all adversarial structures. The absence of this response — facing opposition without appeal — would indicate a form of self-sufficiency inconsistent with the creature's proper orientation.

**[G — Malice]** Its presence reveals one of the most severe forms of image-disorder: the soul's rejoicing in destruction indicates that the image's deepest capacity (genuine joy in what is good) has been inverted into rejoicing in its opposite. All three constitutive elements of malice (enmity, contempt, pleasure in destruction) represent inversions of image-bearing capacities.

### T0.4 — Typological Significance

**T0.4.1** — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. Psa 45:7 (group 550-NEW-02, anchor): "you have loved righteousness and hated wickedness; therefore God your God has anointed you" — cited in Heb 1:9 as fulfilled in Christ. The perfect hatred of wickedness is a defining characteristic of the Messianic King. The human instance (the righteous Davidic king) points toward and is fulfilled in the divine instance (the Son).

**[B — Contempt]** Yes. Isa 53:3 (group 1280-001, anchor for ba.zah): "he was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief; and as one from whom men hide their faces he was despised, and we esteemed him not." The experience of being despised becomes the central experience of the Servant's mission — the contempt directed at him is the contempt of those who do not recognise what they are dismissing.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Yes. Rev 21:8 (group 14-002, bdelussomai): "the detestable" are listed among those whose portion is the second death. The person whose character has become abhorrent in God's sight is named at the eschatological horizon as the embodiment of what abhorrence names. Dan 12:2's de.ra.on (everlasting contempt/abhorrence) participates in the NT's eschatological vocabulary.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** No typological use evidenced. The cruelty-vocabulary names conditions and character dispositions but is not deployed to point toward or participate in a reality beyond the immediate. This is consistent with cruelty having no righteous form and no participation in the redemptive pattern.

**[E — Reproach]** Yes. Psa 69:9: "the reproaches of those who reproach you have fallen on me" — cited in Rom 15:3 as fulfilled in Christ. The one who bears the reproaches directed at God becomes the central typological pattern of M06-E. Christ bears what the righteous sufferer of Psa 69 bore, but in ultimate and cosmic scope.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes. 1Pe 5:8's naming of the devil as antidikos (adversary/accuser) places M06-F in the cosmic spiritual-conflict register. The legal opponent in human life (Mat 5:25, Luk 18:3) points toward the cosmic spiritual adversary; the appropriate inner-being responses (urgency, vigilance, persistent appeal) operate in both registers.

**[G — Malice]** No typological use evidenced. The three Ezekiel verses name a condition in specific nations without typological deployment. Consistent with malice having no participation in the redemptive pattern.

---

**T0.4.2** — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?

**[A — Hatred]** Human-to-divine. The Davidic king's hatred of wickedness (Psa 45:7) establishes the pattern; the Son fulfils it perfectly (Heb 1:9). The human-royal instance points forward to the divine-messianic completion.

**[B — Contempt]** Human-to-revelation. The human contempt directed at the Servant reveals, through its wrongness, the reality of who is being dismissed. The direction runs from the human act of despising toward the theological reality of what is being missed.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Present moral condition to eschatological verdict. The person who has become abhorrent through persistent rebellion is named at the final horizon as the permanent embodiment of what abhorrence names. The direction runs from the present moral-inner condition (abhorrent character) to the eschatological confirmation and permanence of that condition.

**[E — Reproach]** Human to messianic fulfilment. The psalmist's experience of bearing reproach for God's sake (Psa 69) points forward to Christ who bears those very reproaches on behalf of all (Rom 15:3). The direction runs from the human suffering-for-God instance to the messianic bearing-on-behalf-of-all.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Human-legal to cosmic-spiritual. The legal adversary structure points toward the cosmic adversary structure; the human experience of facing an opponent in court points toward the experience of facing the devil as cosmic antidikos. The direction runs upward in register from the human-legal to the cosmic-spiritual.

---

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** No typological use evidenced.
**[G — Malice]** No typological use evidenced.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

**T1.1.1** — What is this characteristic called in the programme, and what does the name signal about its essential nature?

**[A — Hatred]** Called "Hatred." The name signals a sustained inner orientation — not a momentary reaction but a settled disposition of the person turned against something or someone. It signals duration and direction: the person is *against*, and they remain so.

**[B — Contempt]** Called "Contempt." The name signals an evaluative act — the inner posture of treating something as beneath regard. It signals downward assessment: the person renders a verdict of low or no worth against its object.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Called "Abhorrence." The name signals visceral inner response — stronger than contempt, more bodily in its register, more immediate. The abhorrence is a gut-level recoil from what is morally repugnant, not a measured verdict.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Called "Cruelty/Ruthlessness." The double label signals two related phenomena. Cruelty (akh.zar family) names an inner character quality — the disposition of merciless destructiveness. Ruthlessness (a.rits) names the inner moral character of those who operate without God before themselves. Both name the absence of mercy and restraint.

**[E — Reproach]** Called "Reproach." The name signals a received condition — something done to the person, not something the person does. It carries both the social (public) and the inner (felt) dimensions: reproach is not merely an external label but an inner experience that shapes the whole person's way of being in the world.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Called "Hostility/Enmity." The double label signals a relational structure of structured opposition — the adversary relationship — rather than a purely inner disposition. It covers both the structural form (enmity as position) and the inner quality of the relationship (hostility as orientation).

**[G — Malice]** Called "Malice." The name signals a compound inner state — not a single emotion but the simultaneous operation of enmity, contempt, and active pleasure in another's destruction. The name carries the sense of something specifically and deliberately harmful, not merely hostile.

**T1.1.2** — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?

**[A — Hatred]** Five terms:
- **H8130 sa.ne** — the primary verb "to hate," carrying the sense of sustained hostility. The grammatical range (active, passive, agent-noun, infinitive construct) encodes hatred as a condition, a defining characteristic of a person-type, and a legally operative prior state. The most attested term (139 active verses).
- **H8135 sin.ah** — the noun form "hating/hatred as a condition" — hatred as a thing that subsists in a person or relationship, with causal force (Pro 10:12: "hatred stirs up strife").
- **H7852 sa.tam** — persistent grudge-bearing hatred that waits, smoulders, and drives toward action. Distinct from sa.ne: it is not momentary but persistently hostile.
- **H4895 mas.te.mah** — hatred as a communal inner climate or accumulated hostile atmosphere pervading a community (Hos 9:7-8).
- **H8131 se.na** — Aramaic cognate of sa.ne; used only as a person-type designation in Dan 4:19.

**[B — Contempt]** Six terms across distinct registers:
- **H5006 na.ats** — the dominant term (25 verses); spurning, rejecting as insignificant. Almost exclusively vertical: human person treating God, his word, or his servants as beneath regard.
- **H0959 ba.zah** — to despise; broader object range (birthright, Servant, God's name). Names the inner act of treating as valueless.
- **H0937 buz** — contempt as experienced; most present in contexts where contempt is received. The most socially-relational of the terms.
- **H0963 biz.za.von** — contempt as social contagion; one act spreading the disposition through a community (Est 1:18).
- **H5007B ne.a.tsah** — blasphemous contempt expressed in speech; the speech-act of reviling that reveals the heart's rejection.
- **H7590 shut** — contempt from surrounding nations toward Israel; always from the receiving side and always met by divine judgment.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Five terms:
- **H8581 ta.av** — the dominant term; loathing/nauseating resonance. Crucially directionally neutral: the same root covers righteous abhorrence of evil (252-001), inverted abhorrence of justice (252-002), and divine sustained abhorrence (252-003). The direction is entirely contextual.
- **H8374 ta.av** — a cognate root (distinct Strong's number); only in Amo 6:8 for divine loathing of pride.
- **H8263 she.qets** — detestation; names primarily what is worthy of abhorrence (idolatrous objects) rather than the disposition itself.
- **H1860 de.ra.on** — a terminal term; appears only in Isa 66:24 and Dan 12:2, naming abhorrence as a permanent eschatological condition.
- **G0948 bdelussomai** — the Greek stench-root; moral revulsion expressed through the body-metaphor of offensive smell. Two NT uses: genuine vs hypocritical abhorrence (Rom 2:22) and the abhorrent person at the eschatological horizon (Rev 21:8).

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Four terms in a coherent family:
- **H6184 a.rits** — the dominant term (20 verses); "ruthless, violent, terrifying." The root carries the sense of terrifying power exercised without restraint. Psa 54:3 names its inner-being ground: those who "do not set God before themselves."
- **H0394 akh.za.ri** — "cruel" as an inner quality characterising persons and nations. Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel" — the quality pervades all the person's acts.
- **H0393 akh.zar** — "cruel" as a disposition named in specific acts (Job 30:21, Lam 4:3).
- **H0395 akh.ze.riy.yut** — "cruelty" as abstract noun; Pro 27:4: "wrath is cruel (akh.ze.riy.yut)" — names cruelty as the quality wrath possesses when mercy is absent.

**[E — Reproach]** Five terms covering different registers:
- **H2781 cher.pah** — the dominant term (72 verses); reproach, disgrace, taunt, insult. Operates across individual and corporate levels with the same word — from "reproaches have broken my heart" (Psa 69:20) to "I will make you a desolation and an object of reproach among the nations" (Eze 5:14).
- **H5007A ne.a.tsah** — disgrace as an inner condition in extremity (2Ki 19:3); the acute overwhelming shame-experience when crisis produces incapacity.
- **H8146 sa.ni** — the disfavoured/unloved; chronic relational condition of being the less-loved spouse with legal consequences (Deu 21:15).
- **H8103 shim.tsah** — derision; the mocking contempt of enemies (Exo 32:25).
- **G5195 hubrizō** — to mistreat shamefully; insolent treatment that violates the person's dignity. More agentive than cher.pah: it names the act of the one who mistreats.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Four terms naming relational positions:
- **H3401 ya.riv** — adversary/one who contends; legal contention register. Always in the appeal context (Psa 35:1, Isa 49:25, Jer 18:19).
- **G0476 antidikos** — adversary/opponent/accuser; the Greek legal term for the opposing party. Spans legal (Mat 5:25, Luk 12:58), human-appeal (Luk 18:3), and cosmic (1Pe 5:8 — the devil) registers.
- **H0340 a.yav** — enemy, to be hostile; one verse (Exo 23:22) where God adopts this posture.
- **H7009 qim** — adversary as the object of divine judgment; one verse (Job 22:20); the inner response is relief.

**[G — Malice]** One term:
- **H7589 she.at** — malice, spite, wickedness. Appears only in three Ezekiel verses. The root carries the sense of active malice rather than mere hostility. Its rarity is consistent with its specificity: malice as a compound is a recognisable inner-being reality requiring its own term because it is not reducible to its constituent parts.

**T1.1.3** — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**[A — Hatred]** Directional and relational. All five terms are inherently relational — they require an object. The direction of the hatred is its defining feature. Constitutional implication: Lev 19:17 locates it "in your heart"; 2Sa 5:8 locates it "in David's soul." The characteristic is at home in both heart and soul.

**[B — Contempt]** Strongly directional: almost universally directed upward (creature toward Creator, covenant, sacred things) or received downward (from nations toward Israel). Constitutional: Psa 10:13 — "say in his heart" — the heart is the constitutional location.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Moral-evaluative in directionality — triggered by the perception of moral repugnance. Unlike hatred (relational) and contempt (worth-evaluation), abhorrence is a moral recoil — the inner being moving away from what violates its constitution. Constitutional: appears to operate at the level of conscience and moral evaluation faculty.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Constitutional — the diagnostic of Psa 54:3: "they do not set God before themselves" names the inner-constitutional ground. Directional: outward-directed toward others as victims. Relational: the characteristic of the oppressor, expressed in the relationship between the powerful and the vulnerable.

**[E — Reproach]** Directional: entirely received — all five terms name what is done to the person, not what the person holds. Constitutional: Psa 69:20 — "reproaches have broken my heart"; Psa 89:50 — "I bear in my heart the insults." The heart is the location where reproach is carried and felt.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Constitutively relational — the adversary relationship always involves two parties in structured opposition. Unlike other M06 sub-groups which name one party's inner disposition, M06-F names the relational structure between parties. The inner-being content is generated by the structure in the one who faces it.

**[G — Malice]** Soul-constitutional: Eze 25:6 explicitly names the soul as the location ("malice within your soul"). Directional: outward toward another's destruction. Relational: always directed at a specific other whose suffering provides the occasion for the soul's pleasure.

### T1.2 — Kind

**T1.2.1** — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is this?

**[A — Hatred]** Primarily a **disposition** — a settled, durable inner orientation. Gen 37:5 shows hatred intensifying over time. Gen 27:41 shows sa.tam settling and persisting. Pro 10:12 treats sin.ah as a force that "stirs up strife" — acting from within as ongoing pressure. Also has a **condition** form (sa.ni — being hated as a lived condition) and an **act** form (the momentary triggering). The dominant kind across the corpus is disposition.

**[B — Contempt]** Primarily an **inner act of evaluation** — the heart assigning low worth to what deserves high worth. But contempt can become habitual (Mal 1:6-12 shows priests in sustained patterns). It operates as both a discrete inner act and a habitual orientation depending on the verse context.

**[C — Abhorrence]** A **visceral moral response** — stronger in its bodily-resonance register than contempt, less sustained-dispositional than hatred. The bdelussomai and ta.av roots carry physical resonance — the inner recoil has a quasi-somatic quality. The recoil precedes the cognitive verdict: it is a pre-cognitive response that the moral evaluation faculty then names and confirms.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Primarily a **character quality** — not a disposition triggered by circumstance but a settled quality that defines what the person is. Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel" — the character quality is so pervasive it colours even acts that appear merciful. Psa 37:35: "a wicked, ruthless man, spreading himself like a green laurel tree" — an established, flourishing character quality.

**[E — Reproach]** An **inner-social condition** — a state the person inhabits with both outer social (being publicly regarded as shameful) and inner (feeling the weight) dimensions. It is neither a disposition (held by the agent) nor an act (performed) but a condition in the full sense — a state of being in which the person exists.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** A **relational category that generates inner-being responses** — the adversary relationship names a position or role in a relational structure, not an inner quality of the person occupying that position. The inner qualities generated (appeal, urgency, vigilance, relief) are responses to the structure, not the structure itself.

**[G — Malice]** A **compound disposition** — constituted by the simultaneous operation of sustained enmity, contempt for the other's worth, and active pleasure in their destruction. It is not a simple emotion but a compound that produces something qualitatively different from its constituent parts.

**T1.2.2** — Is this characteristic simple or does it have constituent elements?

**[A — Hatred]** Has constituent elements: (1) triggering perception of injury or injustice; (2) inner settlement of the hostile disposition; (3) surface manifestation in changed behaviour (inability to speak peacefully); (4) intensification with new provocations; (5) concealment or expression; (6) volitional organisation toward action.

**[B — Contempt]** Has constituent elements: (1) perception of the object; (2) moral evaluation rendering a low-worth verdict; (3) volitional response — acting as if freed from the object's claim; (4) optional speech-expression of the inner verdict.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Has constituent elements: (1) perception of the morally repugnant; (2) visceral inner recoil (conscience-level response); (3) moral verdict confirming the recoil as appropriate; (4) maintained distance from the object.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Has constituent elements: (1) absence of God-consciousness as the governing inner framework; (2) absence of mercy as a restraining character quality; (3) capacity for unrestrained destructive power; (4) the self-harm component (Pro 11:17 — the cruel person hurts themselves).

**[E — Reproach]** Has constituent elements: (1) social assignment by others (designation as shameful); (2) inner reception (the person feels the weight); (3) relational isolation produced by the condition; (4) identity pressure as the designation challenges who the person is.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Has constituent elements generated by the relational structure: (1) the adversarial positioning of another party; (2) the urgency and appeal it generates; (3) the vigilance it commands; (4) the relief when it is removed.

**[G — Malice]** Compound with three constituent elements: (1) sustained enmity (M06-A elements); (2) contempt for the other's worth (M06-B elements); (3) active pleasure in the other's destruction (the unique additional element that distinguishes malice from its constituents). All three must be simultaneously present for malice to be present in its full form.

**T1.2.3** — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?

**[A — Hatred]** Hatred (M06-A) is a settled inner disposition of the person oriented against another person, quality, or reality — constitutionally located in the heart and soul, engaging the relational and volitional faculties, producing either sustained enmity (when directed at persons) or moral alignment (when directed at evil in the pattern of God's own hatred). It is inherently relational and directional, always requiring an object, intensifying when not resolved, and concealing itself when expression is dangerous.

**[B — Contempt]** Contempt (M06-B) is an inner act and habit of the moral evaluation faculty in which the person assigns low or no worth to what is sacred, covenantally significant, or inherently worthy — constitutionally located in the heart, engaging the moral evaluation and cognitive faculties, producing a closed inner posture that excludes the despised object from its proper claim on the person's honour, compliance, or reverence.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Abhorrence (M06-C) is a visceral inner moral recoil from what is perceived as fundamentally repugnant to the person's moral constitution — constitutionally located at the conscience and moral-evaluation level, engaging perception and the body's resonance registers, producing maintained distance from the object of revulsion. It is shared between God and the aligned person, directionally determined entirely by context, and at its eschatological terminus becomes a permanent condition of the unrighteous.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Cruelty/Ruthlessness (M06-D) is a constitutive inner character quality of the person — not a disposition triggered by circumstance but a settled quality defining what the person is — characterised by the absence of mercy, restraint, and God-consciousness, and expressed in the unrestrained exercise of destructive power toward others. It has no righteous form; it is constitutively defined by the absence of what would make it ordered.

**[E — Reproach]** Reproach (M06-E) is the inner-social condition of being publicly designated as an object of shame and contempt — received from outside but felt and borne at the level of the heart and personal identity. It is constitutional in that it shapes the whole person's way of existing in the world, yet its source is in the disordered inner lives of those who hate, despise, and mistreat. Its eschatological trajectory is complete removal and the restoration of honour.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Hostility/Enmity (M06-F) is a relational structure of structured opposition — the adversary relationship — that generates characteristic inner-being responses in the person who faces it: appeal, urgency, vigilance, and relief. The inner-being content resides in the person facing the adversary (not in the adversary's own inner state), and the appropriate response is grounded in relationship with God as the one who contends for the person.

**[G — Malice]** Malice (M06-G) is a compound inner disposition of the soul — the simultaneous operation of sustained enmity, contempt for the other's worth, and active pleasure in their destruction — explicitly located in the soul (nephesh), producing bodily expression (clapping, stamping), and characterised by self-perpetuating intensity (the pleasure renews itself in the continued destruction of the other).

### T1.3 — Boundary

**T1.3.1** — What is the structural opposite?

**[A — Hatred]** Love (a.ha.vah). Pro 10:12: "hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all offences." 2Sa 13:15: "the hatred with which he hated her was greater than the love with which he had loved her." Ecc 9:6: "their love and their hate...have already perished." The pairing is explicit and structural throughout. The opposite is not the absence of hatred but the positive disposition of love.

**[B — Contempt]** Honour and reverence (ka.vod, yir.ah). Mal 1:6: "a son honours his father...If then I am a father, where is my honour? And if I am a master, where is my fear?" The structural opposite of contempt is the active attribution of appropriate worth — honouring what deserves honour.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Moral acceptance or tolerance of what is repugnant. Deu 7:26: "you shall not bring an abominable thing into your house" — the structural opposite is the inner state that welcomes what should be repelled. More precisely: the absence of moral recoil, moral indifference to genuine evil.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Mercy and kindness (chesed). Pro 11:17: "a man who is kind (chesed) benefits himself, but a cruel man hurts himself." The structural opposite is not merely the absence of cruelty but the active presence of mercy and kindness as constitutive character qualities.

**[E — Reproach]** Honour and vindication (ka.vod). Isa 60:15: "whereas you have been forsaken and hated...I will make you majestic forever, a joy from age to age." The structural opposite is not merely the absence of reproach but the positive attribution of honour, dignity, and public recognition of worth.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Advocacy and covenant companionship — God contending for rather than against. Exo 23:22 shows God as adversary to Israel's adversaries, which is simultaneously God as advocate for Israel. The structural opposite of facing an adversary is having an advocate who contends on one's behalf.

**[G — Malice]** Compassion — the inner disposition that takes pain in another's suffering rather than pleasure. The compound opposite: solidarity rather than enmity, recognition of worth rather than contempt, sorrow at harm rather than joy.

**T1.3.2** — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?

**[A — Hatred]** In person-directed form (group 1601): peaceful speech (Gen 37:4 — "could not speak peacefully to him"), forgiveness and reconciliation, covenantal solidarity. In hatred of discipline (Pro 12:1, 15:10): growth, correction, and formation. The characteristic resists reconciliation — the verses show no pathway from hatred to restoration that does not first pass through the cessation of the hatred.

**[B — Contempt]** Compliance with what is despised (Num 15:31: contempt precedes commandment-breaking). Correction — group 172-002 (contempt of reproof) names the closing of the person to the formation that would come through instruction. Covenantal participation: Deu 31:20 — "despise me and break my covenant" — contempt and covenant-breaking are sequential.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Proximity to the abhorrent object: Job 30:10 — "they keep aloof from me." Tolerance of the object in one's space (Deu 7:26). The moral indifference that would allow the abhorrent thing to remain.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Mercy ("have no mercy" — Jer 6:23, 50:42). Fear of God (Psa 54:3, 86:14). Care for the vulnerable (Lam 4:3). Regard for life (Pro 12:10: "the righteous has regard for the life of his beast"). Every inner capacity that would hold destructive power in check.

**[E — Reproach]** Honour (Mal 2:9 — the opposite of reproach). Confident public presence (Psa 31:11 — "those who see me in the street flee from me"). Pro 6:33: "his disgrace will not be wiped away" — the most severe reproach excludes even the possibility of restoration without divine intervention.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship generates urgency and appeal — it resists passivity and complacency. 1Pe 5:8: "be sober-minded; be watchful" — the characteristic demands active vigilance.

**[G — Malice]** Compassion for the other's suffering. Recognition of the other as a person whose suffering matters. Any restraint on the pleasure taken in their destruction.

**T1.3.3** — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?

**[A — Hatred]** Anger is not hatred. Hatred is settled; anger can be momentary. Gen 27:41 shows sa.tam (settled hatred) persisting after the heat of anger has passed — the grudge remains when the emotion dissipates. Deu 19:6 distinguishes judicial killing from hateful murder by the absence of prior hatred: "he had not hated his neighbour in the past." Contempt (M06-B) is adjacent: contempt is a worth-evaluation (treating as beneath regard), hatred is relational hostility (being against). One can despise without hating; one can hate without despising.

**[B — Contempt]** Not hatred: hatred is sustained relational enmity; contempt is a worth-evaluation that can be directed at things and words as much as persons. Not abhorrence (M06-C): abhorrence is visceral moral recoil; contempt is cool evaluative dismissal. Not anger: contempt can be cool and comfortable (Job 12:5 — "in the thought of one who is at ease"). Contempt is the cool verdict; abhorrence is the visceral recoil; hatred is the sustained enmity.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Not hatred: hatred is sustained relational opposition; abhorrence is visceral moral recoil that may be momentary. Not contempt: contempt is the cool dismissal of worth; abhorrence is the intense visceral recoil from what is morally repugnant. One can be contemptuous without being repulsed; one can be repulsed without being contemptuous.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Not hatred: hatred is a relational hostile disposition toward a specific person or thing; cruelty is a character quality that operates independently of any specific relational object. A cruel person is cruel even to those they do not hate. Not anger: Pro 27:4 distinguishes cruelty from wrath and anger — wrath and anger are emotional states; cruelty is the quality wrath has when mercy is absent from it.

**[E — Reproach]** Not guilt: guilt is an inner conviction of wrongdoing; reproach is the social-inner condition that may or may not correspond to actual wrongdoing. Many verses document unjust reproach (Psa 69:4: "those who hate me without cause"). Not shame: shame can be an inner state independent of social designation; reproach is specifically the social-outer designation felt inwardly. They often co-occur (Psa 69:19: "my reproach, and my shame and my dishonor") but are distinguishable.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Not hatred (M06-A): hatred is an inner disposition of the agent; the adversary relationship is a relational structure. Not cruelty (M06-D): the ruthless person is characterised by an inner character void; the adversary person occupies a relational position that may or may not involve the inner character qualities of the other sub-groups. An adversary can be just (God in Exo 23:22) or unjust (the devil in 1Pe 5:8).

**[G — Malice]** Not hatred alone: hatred does not necessarily take pleasure in harm; malice specifically does. Not contempt alone: contempt dismisses; malice celebrates destruction. Not cruelty: cruelty is the character void (absence of mercy); malice is the positive presence of pleasure in harm. All three can co-occur but they are distinct inner-being realities.

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**T1.4.1** — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?

**[A — Hatred]** Four modes: (1) **Concealment mode** — hatred operates within while the person presents a false exterior (Pro 10:18: "the one who conceals hatred has lying lips"; Pro 26:24: "whoever hates disguises himself with his lips and harbours deceit in his heart"). (2) **Accumulation mode** — hatred intensifies when fed by new provocations (Gen 37:5, 8: "they hated him even more"). (3) **Settlement mode** — sa.tam: the hatred settles into a plan (Gen 27:41: Esau says internally "then I will kill my brother Jacob"). (4) **Expression mode** — hatred breaks through into visible behaviour when restraint fails (Eze 35:11: "the anger and envy that you showed because of your hatred against them").

**[B — Contempt]** Four modes: (1) **Inner speech mode** — the contempt is articulated privately (Psa 10:13: "say in his heart, 'You will not call to account'"). (2) **Behavioural mode** — the inner verdict produces action in accordance with it (Num 15:31: "because he has despised the word...he has broken his commandment"). (3) **Speech-act mode** — ne.a.tsah: blasphemous contempt expressed outward in speech (Neh 9:18, 9:26, Eze 35:12). (4) **Contagion mode** — biz.za.von: contempt spreading from one person's act through a community (Est 1:18).

**[C — Abhorrence]** Three modes: (1) **Righteous abhorrence** — the person's visceral recoil from evil, falsehood, and what violates moral truth, aligned with God's own (Psa 119:163). (2) **Inverted abhorrence** — the same capacity directed against justice and truth (Amo 5:10, Mic 3:9). (3) **Divine sustained abhorrence** — God's settled inner revulsion toward those who have persistently corrupted themselves (Psa 106:40, Amo 6:8).

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Three modes: (1) **Character-quality mode** — cruelty as the persistent inner quality characterising the person (Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel"). (2) **Oppressor-disposition mode** — ruthlessness as the inner character of those who do not set God before themselves (Psa 54:3). (3) **Terror-as-instrument mode** — the ruthless nations deployed by God as instruments of judgment (Eze 28:7, 30:11, 32:12 — "the most ruthless of nations").

**[E — Reproach]** Four modes: (1) **Personal acute mode** — ne.a.tsah: the overwhelming shame-experience in a moment of crisis (2Ki 19:3). (2) **Personal chronic mode** — cher.pah (individual): the sustained inner-social condition of the person publicly designated as shameful (Psa 69:7). (3) **Corporate-covenantal mode** — cher.pah (communal): reproach as the condition of a whole people among the nations (Eze 5:14). (4) **Dignity-violation mode** — hubrizō: reproach as the experience of having one's dignity actively stripped (1Th 2:2).

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Three modes: (1) **Legal/judicial mode** — antidikos: the adversary in a legal dispute generating urgency (Mat 5:25, Luk 12:58, 18:3). (2) **Covenantal-advocacy mode** — a.yav, ya.riv: God as adversary on behalf of the covenant person, generating appeal and confidence (Exo 23:22, Psa 35:1, Isa 49:25). (3) **Cosmic-spiritual mode** — antidikos: the devil as the adversary who prowls seeking to devour (1Pe 5:8), generating sober watchfulness.

**[G — Malice]** One mode with three distinctive features: **Soul-level pleasure in destruction** as the primary mode (Eze 25:6: "rejoiced with all the malice within your soul"); **purposive destruction** driving actively toward harm (Eze 25:15: "to destroy in never-ending enmity"); **contempt-compound** where joy and contempt are simultaneous and inseparable (Eze 36:5: "wholehearted joy and utter contempt").

**T1.4.2** — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?

**[A — Hatred]** Significantly. When directed at evil (group 550-NEW-02): hatred operates as moral discernment in expression — it stabilises the inner orientation toward truth and against falsehood. When directed at persons (group 1601): hatred operates as relational enmity — isolating, concealing, corrosive. When hatred of God (group 3596): hatred operates as a constitutive orientation that defines the person's entire covenantal standing. When communal-atmospheric (group 1199 — mas.te.mah): hatred operates as a shared inner climate that destroys collective spiritual capacity.

**[B — Contempt]** Yes. When directed at God and the sacred (groups 172-001, 172-003, 1567-001): contempt is the inner posture of rebellion — devaluing the covenantal relationship itself. When operating between persons (group 316-001): contempt is social dismissal experienced by the low-status person. When operating at the national level (groups 1281-001, 1277-001): contempt functions as collective inner disposition toward another people.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Decisively different by direction. The same ta.av root covers: righteous abhorrence of evil (252-001 — most morally ordered response), inverted abhorrence of justice (252-002 — among the most morally disordered), and divine abhorrence of covenant violators (252-003). The direction determines everything; the root itself carries no moral valence.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Yes. In personal contexts (Pro 11:17, 12:10): cruelty as character quality corrupting interpersonal relationships. In national/military contexts (Jer 6:23, Eze 28:7-32:12): ruthlessness as national character. In cosmic-judicial context (Isa 13:9): the quality applied to the day of the Lord's judgment as a description of its severity.

**[E — Reproach]** Yes — varies by duration (acute vs chronic), scope (individual vs communal), and source (active dignity-stripping vs passive social designation). The inner experience varies correspondingly.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Significantly. God as adversary (Exo 23:22) is covenantal advocacy. Human facing legal adversary (Mat 5:25) is urgent resolution. Human facing cosmic adversary (1Pe 5:8) is active spiritual vigilance. The same structural form generates very different inner-being responses depending on who the adversary is and what is at stake.

**[G — Malice]** Limited variation — all three verses (Eze 25:6, 25:15, 36:5) show the same mode across different nations (Ammon, Philistia, Edom). The characteristic appears structurally consistent across national contexts.

---

**T1.4.3** — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?

**[A — Hatred]** Yes. Pro 10:18: "the one who conceals hatred has lying lips" — speech as the instrument of concealment. Psa 109:3: "they encircle me with words of hate" — speech as the vehicle of hostile expression. Zec 8:17: "love no false oath, for all these things I hate" — God's hatred of false speech shows that covenant-faithful speech is what hatred of deception produces. The characteristic operates through speech in both its concealed (deceptive) and expressed (hostile) forms.

**[B — Contempt]** Yes, two speech modes: (1) **Inner speech** (Psa 10:13 — "say in his heart, 'You will not call to account'") — the private verdict articulated internally. (2) **Public speech-act** (ne.a.tsah — Neh 9:18, 9:26, Eze 35:12) — blasphemous contempt expressed audibly.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Silent on a speech-based mode. The characteristic operates as an inner response rather than a verbal act. The speech modes of M06-B (contempt) and M06-D (taunting — cha.raph, BOUNDARY) are distinct from abhorrence. M06-C is characteristically non-verbal.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Silent on speech mode. Cruelty operates through action and through the absence of restraint, not primarily through verbal expression.

**[E — Reproach]** Yes, extensively. Reproach is characteristically communicated through speech: the taunt, the mockery, the insult. Lam 3:61: "you have heard their taunts, O Lord." Psa 79:12: "the taunts with which they have taunted you." Jer 20:8: "the word of the Lord has become for me a reproach and derision all day long." Speech is the primary vehicle through which reproach is delivered and maintained.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Yes — appeal is the characteristic speech mode. Psa 35:1: "contend, O Lord, with those who contend with me" — the facing of an adversary generates the vocal appeal to God. Luk 18:3: "give me justice against my adversary" — the petition is the defining inner-outer expression of M06-F's characteristic response.

**[G — Malice]** No speech mode evidenced. The characteristic expresses through bodily action (Eze 25:6: clapping hands, stamping feet) rather than through speech. Silent on speech mode.

### T1.5 — Immediate Response

**T1.5.1** — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?

**[A — Hatred]** From the person who is hated: Psa 69:20: "reproaches have broken my heart, so that I am in despair. I looked for pity, but there was none." The immediate inner response to being hated is a shattering of the inner self — heart-breaking — followed by the cry for pity. Gen 37:4: the person who is hated finds that peaceful speech becomes impossible ("could not speak peacefully to him") — relational closure is immediate. From the one who receives a triggering injury and begins to hate: settlement of a hostile inner posture (Gen 37:4 — hatred immediate upon perceiving favouritism).

**[B — Contempt]** For the recipient: Psa 123:3-4: "our soul has had more than enough of the scorn...of the contempt of the proud" — the soul registers saturation and cries for relief. Job 31:34: "the contempt of families terrified me" — fear is the immediate affective response to received contempt. For the holder: the inner verdict is rendered — a private assessment of worthlessness applied to the object.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The immediate response is the recoil itself — the inner being pulling away from the object. Deu 7:26: "you shall utterly detest and abhor it" — the commanded abhorrence is a commanded pulling-away, a maintained distance. Job 30:10: "they abhor me; they keep aloof from me" — the immediate bodily and relational consequence of abhorrence in the holder is distance-keeping.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** From the recipient (group 1775-002): Job 6:23: "deliver me from the adversary's hand...redeem me from the hand of the ruthless" — the immediate inner response of those under ruthless domination is appeal to God as the only power that can counteract the ruthless. Isa 25:4: God is the stronghold and shelter — the immediate response to ruthlessness in the victim is the cry for refuge. For the perpetrator: no immediate inner response is described.

**[E — Reproach]** Psa 69:20: "reproaches have broken my heart, so that I am in despair." The immediate response is heart-breaking — a shattering of the inner self. The secondary immediate response: "I looked for pity, but there was none, and for comforters, but I found none" — the reaching out for relational support, and the compounding of the condition when that support is absent.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Appeal. Psa 35:1: "contend, O Lord, with those who contend with me." Luk 18:3: "give me justice against my adversary." Jer 18:19: "hear me, O Lord, and listen to the voice of my adversaries." The immediate inner response to the adversary relationship is the turning to God for advocacy and justice.

**[G — Malice]** Bodily expression: Eze 25:6 — "you have clapped your hands and stamped your feet." The soul's malice immediately produces celebratory bodily expression. The inner soul-level pleasure immediately expresses through physical celebration — the most somatic immediate expression in M06.

---

**T1.5.2** — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence?

**[A — Hatred]** Consistent in direction: hatred consistently produces rupture or closure. The specific form varies — acute (Gen 37:4 — immediate inability to speak peacefully) or sustained (Gen 27:41 — immediate internal murderous resolution) — but the pattern of inner breaking or inner organisation around a hostile intention is consistent.

**[B — Contempt]** For the recipient: consistently the burden on the soul and the cry for relief. For the contemner's interior: the inner verdict is consistently private and unexpressed publicly.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Consistent: abhorrence produces distance. Whether divine (Psa 106:40), human-social (Job 30:10), or commanded-ritual (Deu 7:26), the immediate consequence is separation.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** For the recipient: consistently appeal to God. For the perpetrator: no immediate inner response named — the characteristic is described from the outside.

**[E — Reproach]** Consistent in direction: inner breaking followed by the impulse toward appeal. The form varies — acute crisis (2Ki 19:3), sustained lament (Psa 69), corporate appeal (Joel 2:17) — but the pattern holds.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Consistent: the adversary relationship generates appeal and urgent response. This is the defining characteristic of M06-F's inner-being content.

**[G — Malice]** Three verses — consistency is limited by the thin corpus. The bodily expression is consistent across all three (the nations celebrate Israel's suffering). Whether the immediate internal pleasure-of-malice onset is consistent is not described phenomenologically.

---

**T1.5.3** — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note explicitly.

**[A]** The process by which hatred takes hold internally — how it moves from triggering perception to settled disposition — is not described in the verses. **[D]** The inner experience of the cruel/ruthless person — what cruelty feels like from within — is entirely silent. **[G]** The phenomenology of malice-onset — what it feels like as the soul begins to rejoice in another's destruction — is not described.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**T1.6.1** — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?

**[A — Hatred]** In the hater: progressive narrowing of the inner life around the object of hatred. Pro 10:18: concealed hatred requires lies — the person becomes complicit in self-deception. Hos 9:7: accumulated communal hatred produces "the prophet is a fool; the man of the spirit is mad" — the spiritual perceptive capacity is obliterated by the accumulated inner climate. In the hated: sustained hatred from others produces despair (Psa 69:20), fear of abandonment (Gen 50:15), and the chronic condition of disfavour (group 550-NEW-01 — the unloved wife across Deuteronomy and Genesis).

**[B — Contempt]** Progressive estrangement from the object of contempt. Isa 1:4: "they have despised the Holy One of Israel, they are utterly estranged" — the estrangement is the sustained effect. In group 172-002 (contempt of reproof): the sustained effect is the closure of the formation pathway — the person becomes permanently closed to correction. In the communal register (Jer 23:17): the community that despises God's word becomes receptive to false prophets — the contempt has corrupted the capacity to distinguish true from false.

**[C — Abhorrence]** In the righteous form (group 252-001): sustained abhorrence of evil produces stable moral orientation — the person is constitutionally set against what God opposes. In divine form (group 252-003): sustained divine abhorrence toward covenant violators produces judgment and exclusion. In the eschatological terminus (group 1283-001): the sustained effect of being abhorrent becomes permanent — "everlasting contempt" (Dan 12:2) and "they shall be an abhorrence to all flesh" (Isa 66:24).

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** In the perpetrator: Pro 11:17 — "a cruel man hurts himself." Job 15:20: "the wicked man writhes in pain all his days, through all the years that are laid up for the ruthless." The sustained effect of cruelty on the cruel person is their own undoing. In the victim (group 1775-002): sustained exposure to ruthless domination strips all relational and social shelter, producing the ongoing experience described in Isa 25:4 — those under the ruthless seek shelter as from a storm.

**[E — Reproach]** In the individual: sustained reproach restructures the person's inner life — their perception of themselves (Psa 22:6: "I am a worm and not a man"), their relation to others (Psa 31:11: "those who see me in the street flee from me"), and their volitional capacity (Job 31:34: "I kept silence, and did not go out of doors"). In the corporate register: sustained reproach produces communal shame that becomes a defining identity (Jer 24:9 — "a reproach, a byword, a taunt, and a curse in all the places where I shall drive them"). Isa 54:4 shows that restoration from deep reproach requires active forgetting — the reproach has been laid down in memory so deeply that removal is not automatic.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Sustained operation of the adversary relationship produces vigilance (1Pe 5:8: "be sober-minded; be watchful") and sustained appeal (Luk 18:3 — "kept coming to him"). The sustained effect is a heightened inner alertness and persistent orientation toward the advocate.

**[G — Malice]** Eze 25:15: "never-ending enmity" — malice is self-perpetuating. The pleasure in destruction renews itself with each new occasion of the other's suffering. The sustained effect is a deepening inner orientation toward the other's destruction as the source of the soul's pleasure — the characteristic does not cycle down but intensifies.

---

**T1.6.2** — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained possession of this characteristic establish?

**[A — Hatred]** Readiness for retributive action (Gen 50:15 — anticipated). Corruption of speech (Pro 10:18, 26:24). In the communal register (group 1199): spiritual blindness — the capacity for prophetic discernment destroyed.

**[B — Contempt]** Covenantal closure — the person is no longer capable of receiving what God or wisdom offers because the evaluative faculty has rendered those things as beneath regard.

**[C — Abhorrence]** Righteous form: reliable moral resistance to what is abhorred — the person is constitutionally opposed to corruption. Inverted form (group 252-002): constitutionally repelled by righteousness — an established moral reversal.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel" — the character quality so thoroughly organised the person that even mercy-attempts are shaped by the cruelty. The sustained condition: an inner constitution in which every expression — including apparent mercy — is coloured by the absence of the restraining goods.

**[E — Reproach]** Fear: Isa 51:7 — "fear not the reproach of man" — the command implies sustained reproach has produced fear as a settled inner state. Identity fragmentation: Psa 22:6 — the sustained social designation has been internalised and restructures self-perception.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Sustained vigilance and persistent appeal — the characteristic establishes a posture of active spiritual alertness.

**[G — Malice]** The "never-ending" quality of malice (Eze 25:15) names the self-sustaining inner orientation: the soul finds its pleasure in the object's destruction and seeks more occasions for that pleasure.

---

**T1.6.3** — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?

**[CLUSTER]** Consistently yes across all sub-groups. In every case, the immediate response is acute — rupture, verdict, recoil, appeal, breaking — and the sustained effect is structural: the inner being has been reorganised around the characteristic and it has become constitutive of the person's orientation. What began as an event has become a condition. This is universal across M06: the cluster documents characteristics that develop from acute responses into structural inner conditions when sustained.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

**T1.7.1** — What inner conditions enable this characteristic to take hold?

**[A — Hatred]** Perceived injustice or loss (Gen 37:4 — brothers' perception of favouritism). Violated trust or honour (Gen 27:41 — perceived theft of blessing; 2Sa 13:22 — Amnon's violation of Tamar). For hatred of God: moral and spiritual drift (Hos 9:15 — "there I began to hate them. Because of the wickedness of their deeds").

**[B — Contempt]** Pride and comfort (Job 12:5: "in the thought of one who is at ease there is contempt for misfortune"; Psa 123:4: "the contempt of the proud"). Cognitive misreading of God's accountability (Psa 10:13: "you will not call to account" as the inner premise from which contempt of God follows).

**[C — Abhorrence]** The enabling conditions for righteous abhorrence: love of what is true and good (Psa 119:163 — "I love your law" is the prior condition that makes falsehood abhorrent). For inverted abhorrence: sustained habituation to corruption progressively reverses the direction of the moral recoil.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** The removal of God-consciousness from the inner governing framework (Psa 54:3 — "they do not set God before themselves"). The absence of mercy as a restraining character quality. The absence of regard for life (Pro 12:10 — the righteous person's regard for animal life is what the cruel person lacks).

**[E — Reproach]** Social isolation (Psa 69:20b — "I looked for pity, but there was none") — when the reproached person has no relational resource, reproach takes hold most completely. For corporate reproach: the community's own faithlessness (group 322-002) removes the divine protection that would have prevented it.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The adversary relationship is entered into when another party takes up the adversarial posture. The condition that makes it most threatening is facing the adversary without an adequate advocate.

**[G — Malice]** Sustained enmity toward another, combined with the loss of regard for their worth (contempt), and the progressive acquisition of a twisted inner pleasure from the reversal of their fortunes. All three constituent elements must be simultaneously present.

---

**T1.7.2** — What inner conditions block or prevent this characteristic from taking hold?

**[A — Hatred]** Love as the primary blocking condition: Pro 10:12 — "love covers all offences" — what could become the seed of hatred is covered rather than nursed. Lev 19:17's command to frank confrontation prevents the inner settlement of hatred by keeping the relational channel open.

**[B — Contempt]** Fear of the Lord blocks contempt (Mal 1:6 — its absence is what makes contempt possible). The posture of openness to formation blocks contempt of reproof (Pro 15:5 — "whoever heeds reproof is prudent").

**[C — Abhorrence]** The enabling condition for righteous abhorrence (love of what is good) simultaneously blocks its inversion. Sustained immersion in what is true and good sharpens the moral recoil from its opposite rather than dulling it.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Fear of God (setting God before oneself) blocks ruthlessness (Psa 54:3's diagnostic by implication). Mercy and kindness as settled character qualities (Pro 11:17) block cruelty. The righteous person's regard for life (Pro 12:10) prevents the cruel pattern.

**[E — Reproach]** Relationship with God as the source of identity and worth: Isa 51:7 — "you who know righteousness, the people in whose heart is my law; fear not the reproach of man." The identity established in God cannot be overwritten by the social designation of reproach.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Having an advocate who can contend on one's behalf (Exo 23:22, Isa 49:25) makes the adversary relationship bearable and resistible.

**[G — Malice]** Compassion for the other — the genuine capacity to be pained by another's suffering blocks the development of pleasure in it. The prior conditions (enmity and contempt) being blocked would prevent malice from developing its compound form.

**[CLUSTER]** The primary blocking condition for all agent-side M06 characteristics is the same: love of what is good + fear of the Lord + relational accountability. This complex provides the structural barrier to the entire cluster. The verse evidence is consistent: where these are present and active, the M06 characteristics cannot take permanent root.

---

**T1.7.3** — What is the inner state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?

**[A — Hatred]** Job 31:29: "if I have rejoiced at the ruin of him who hated me" — the righteous person does not move into reciprocal hatred when hated. Exo 23:5: "if you see the donkey of one who hates you lying down under its burden, you shall rescue it with him" — the command assumes it is possible to act for the benefit of the person who hates you without reciprocating. The inner state is not described directly but implied: the person maintains a different inner-being orientation — grounded in something beyond the relational exchange — that enables acting for the enemy's good.

**[B — Contempt]** Isa 49:7: the Servant is "deeply despised, abhorred by the nation" yet the promise of divine vindication is the inner resource that enables the despised to bear contempt without being destroyed or reciprocating it.

**[C — Abhorrence]** The person who abhorres evil rightly (group 252-001) maintains righteous abhorrence without being consumed by it — the abhorrence is directed at the right object and produces moral stability rather than disorder.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Group 1775-002 documents this most fully: the recipient of ruthlessness who does not reciprocate it is characterised by appeal (Job 6:23), trust in God as deliverer (Isa 25:4), and confidence that God will contend (Isa 49:25, Jer 15:21). The inner state is not passivity but active sustained appeal grounded in a different understanding of where ultimate power lies.

**[E — Reproach]** 1Th 2:2: Paul had "boldness in our God to declare the gospel" despite shameful treatment. Jer 15:15: "for your sake I bear reproach" — the prophet holds the reproach in the context of his relationship with God, which prevents identity destruction. The inner state involves the pain of reproach alongside a groundedness in God's regard that keeps the reproach from colonising the person's identity.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** The person who faces an adversary through appeal and vigilance rather than through reciprocal hostility is characterised by sober-mindedness (1Pe 5:8) and persistent petition (Luk 18:3).

**[G — Malice]** The verse evidence does not describe the inner state of those who are the objects of malice (Israel). The inner experience of being maliced is not documented in the three Ezekiel verses.

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

**T1.8.1** — What is the primary inner-being dimension?

**[A — Hatred]** **Relational Disposition.** Hatred is constitutively relational — it always requires an object, always involves the positioning of the self against another. Every group in M06-A involves a relationship: person against person, spouse against spouse, person against evil (in alignment with God's relational stance), God's relational stance toward sin.

**[B — Contempt]** **Moral Character.** Contempt is an act of moral evaluation — the heart assigning low or no worth to what deserves high worth. It operates through the moral evaluation faculty rather than primarily through the relational capacity. The moral failure is in the evaluation (wrong verdict about worth), not in the relational orientation.

**[C — Abhorrence]** **Moral Character.** Abhorrence operates as the moral evaluation faculty's most visceral response to genuine evil — at the level of the conscience's deepest reactions. The distinction from contempt: contempt is the cool verdict; abhorrence is the visceral recoil. Both engage moral evaluation but at different temperatures and depths.

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** **Moral Character** — but with a distinctive character: cruelty is **moral character by absence** — the condition of being constituted without the character qualities that would restrain destructive impulses. It is not a wrong act of moral evaluation but the absence of positive moral qualities (mercy, fear of God, restraint).

**[E — Reproach]** **Emotion — Negative.** Reproach is primarily an affective-experiential condition — the felt inner state of being publicly shamed. Psa 69:20's "broken my heart...in despair" names the affective dimension as the primary inner-being impact.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** **Relational Disposition.** M06-F names a relational structure of structured opposition. Unlike the other sub-groups where the relational dimension is secondary, in M06-F the relational structure is the primary feature.

**[G — Malice]** **Emotion — Negative** (compound). The primary affective orientation of the soul taking pleasure in another's destruction. Uniquely, M06-G's primary dimension is also constitutively relational and moral — the three dimensions are co-constituent rather than primary and secondary.

---

**T1.8.2** — What evidence supports this classification?

**[A]** Every group in M06-A involves a relationship: person against person (1601), spouse against spouse (550-NEW-01), person against evil in alignment with God (550-NEW-02), God's relational stance toward sin (3595, 3596).
**[B]** 1Sa 2:17: contempt of the Lord's offering named as "very great sin" — a moral failure. Gen 25:34: Esau's despising of the birthright is an act of wrong moral evaluation (valuing stew above covenant inheritance).
**[C]** Every group in M06-C involves a moral evaluation of something as repugnant: God abhorring pride (248-001), the righteous abhorring falsehood (252-001), the corrupt abhorring justice (252-002), God abhorring covenant violators (252-003).
**[D]** Pro 12:10: "the mercy of the wicked is cruel" — character-level operation pervasive through all acts. Psa 54:3's "do not set God before themselves" names the absent character quality.
**[E]** "Broken my heart" (Psa 69:20), "in despair," "I am a worm and not a man" (Psa 22:6), "great trouble and shame" (Neh 1:3) — consistently affective vocabulary throughout M06-E.
**[F]** Every M06-F term names a position or role in a relational structure, not an inner quality of the person occupying that position.
**[G]** Eze 25:6: "rejoiced with all the malice within your soul"; Eze 36:5: "wholehearted joy" — the affective content (joy, pleasure) is primary.

**T1.8.3** — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions?

**[A — Hatred]** Secondary: **Moral Character** (groups 550-NEW-02 and 3595 — hatred as moral quality and divine inner-being reality) and **Emotion — Negative** (the affective quality is present throughout, particularly in group 1200's sa.tam and 2Sa 13:15's "very great hatred").

**[B — Contempt]** Secondary: **Relational Disposition** (contempt has relational consequences — estrangement, Isa 1:4) and **Cognition** (the cognitive grounding of contempt — the prior verdict that God will not call to account, Psa 10:13).

**[C — Abhorrence]** Secondary: **Emotion — Negative** (the affective register of moral disgust) and **Dependence/Creatureliness** in the eschatological dimension (de.ra.on — the permanent condition of the unrighteous names the creaturely position of those who definitively rejected their Creator).

**[D — Cruelty/Ruthlessness]** Secondary: **Agency/Power** (cruelty operates through the exercise of power; ruthlessness is the disposition of those with power acting without divine constraint). **Emotion — Negative** (in the recipient register — group 1775-002 — fear, distress, longing for deliverance).

**[E — Reproach]** Secondary: **Relational Disposition** (reproach fundamentally alters relational standing). **Dependence/Creatureliness** (the corporate mode — Israel's corporate reproach marks their dependence on God for vindication and restoration). The extensive appeal literature of M06-E (Psa 69:13, Jer 15:15, Joel 2:17, Lam 5:1) is the voice of creatureliness in its most vulnerable form.

**[F — Hostility/Enmity]** Secondary: **Agency/Power** (the adversary relationship is a power contest; the inner responses it generates — appeal, vigilance — are responses to a power differential).

**[G — Malice]** The three dimensions (Emotion — Negative, Relational Disposition, Moral Character) are co-constituent rather than primary and secondary. M06-G is the only sub-group where multiple dimensions are constitutive of the characteristic rather than one primary and others secondary.
