## VCG definitions

### M10b-D-VCG-01 — Levitical boundary-laws: abomination as category-violation against created order

**Context description:** This VCG holds the Levitical corpus where specific sexual practices are declared abominations — acts that evoke divine revulsion by violating the divinely ordered distinctions of created order. The inner-being content is the act as a category-violation that makes the person morally unclean and socially expelled: both parties bear mutual guilt (Lev 20:13), the community must abstain collectively (Lev 18:26), prior inhabitants committed the acts and defiled the land itself (Lev 18:27), the penalty of being cut off names the covenantal consequence (Lev 18:29), abominable customs make the self unclean (Lev 18:30). The abomination mechanism here is the fundamental disordering of the created relationship against the divine standard. Cross-register: M10c (cultic/ritual defilement — the boundary-law and uncleanness register is concentrated in this VCG). Anchor: Lev 18:22 (1676) — a specific sexual act declared an abomination; the term names a category-violation that evokes divine revulsion; most direct statement of the boundary-violation mechanism.

**Members (6V):** 1676 (Lev 18:22), 65922 (Lev 18:26), 65923 (Lev 18:27), 65924 (Lev 18:29), 65925 (Lev 18:30), 65926 (Lev 20:13)

**Anchor:** 1676 (Lev 18:22)
