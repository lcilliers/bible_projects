## Reading and clustering

Reading all 17 verse-meanings in canonical order:

**blasfēmeō cluster analysis (11V):**
- Rom 2:24 (14357): Conduct causing others to blaspheme — moral failure of those who claim to represent God. The *cause* of blasphemy is inner moral failure. Outward-consequence register.
- Rom 3:8 (14358): False attribution of a bankrupt teaching — slander/misrepresentation. Speech-as-false-accusation.
- Rom 14:16 (14359): Misusing freedom causes good to be spoken of as evil — social defamation register.
- 1Cor 4:13 (14351): Slander received; apostle answers with entreaty. Speech-as-slander; non-retaliation response.
- 1Cor 10:30 (14360): Denouncing someone for exercising grateful freedom — negative speech attacking gratitude. Speech-as-defamation.
- Jude 8 (14354): Wilful inner rejection of authority combined with sensory self-indulgence. Blasphemy as defiant-will-against-authority.
- Jude 10 (14355): Contempt filling void of ignorance; rational comprehension bypassed; instinct-without-understanding destroys. Contempt-as-blasphemy.
- Rev 13:6 (14356): Sustained deliberate blasphemy against God's name, dwelling, heaven — total inner orientation of defiant contempt toward the divine.
- Rev 16:9 (14352): Hardened will under direct punitive pressure — cursing God and refusing to repent.
- Rev 16:11 (14350): Affliction deepens hardened will's hostility — blasphemy in pain.
- Rev 16:21 (14353): Will fully hardened — devastating judgment becomes occasion for defiant blasphemy.

Three clear VCG groupings emerge within blasfēmeō:
1. **Hardened defiance under judgment** (Rev 16:9, 16:11, 16:21; Rev 13:6; Jude 8; Jude 10): The evil of a will so hardened it turns divine pressure into increased defiance. Contempt as the expression of total inner resistance. 6V.
2. **Speech-causing-blasphemy / social defamation** (Rom 2:24, Rom 3:8, Rom 14:16, 1Cor 4:13, 1Cor 10:30): Blasphemy as the social/communicative consequence of moral failure, misrepresentation, or abuse of freedom. 5V.

**kakopoios cluster (5V):**
- Joh 18:30 (14378): Malicious false labelling of the innocent as evildoer — accusers' inner malice.
- 1Pe 2:12 (14377): False accusation of wrongdoing — honorable conduct silences it.
- 1Pe 2:14 (14379): Civil authority punishing wrongdoing — legal-consequence register.
- 1Pe 3:16 (14380): False wrongdoing charge — good conscience exposes falseness.
- 1Pe 4:15 (14381): Wrongdoing encompasses serious harmful acts (murder, theft, meddling) — what justifies suffering.

kakopoios splits: Joh 18:30 + 1Pe 2:12 + 1Pe 3:16 are the false-accusation register (3V). 1Pe 2:14 + 1Pe 4:15 are the actual-wrongdoer-punished register (2V). At 17V total for M10b-F, separating kakopoios into two VCGs would fragment a small group unnecessarily. The unifying theme is *wrongdoing as a charged and contested category* — whether rightly or wrongly applied. Keep as one VCG.

**atopos (1V):**
- Act 25:5 (65874): Legal wrongdoing — objectively assessable legal charge. Legal-charge register. Groups naturally with kakopoios.

**VCG design decision:**
3 VCGs:
- M10b-F-VCG-01: Hardened defiance and contemptuous blasphemy against God (blasfēmeō Rev + Jude cluster)
- M10b-F-VCG-02: Blasphemy as social defamation — moral failure causing dishonour (blasfēmeō Rom + 1Cor cluster)
- M10b-F-VCG-03: Wrongdoing as a charged category — false accusation and legal-offense register (kakopoios + atopos)

## VCG definitions

### M10b-F-VCG-01 — Hardened defiance and contemptuous blasphemy against God

**Context description:** This VCG groups the verses in which blasphemy is the direct expression of a will hardened against God — a total inner orientation of defiant contempt that intensifies rather than breaks under divine pressure. The apocalyptic Rev 16 cluster (9, 11, 21) names the ultimate form of this hardening: even catastrophic judgment becomes occasion for further cursing rather than repentance, revealing that the will at its most corrupt actively converts suffering into defiance. Rev 13:6 names the sustained, deliberate character of this orientation — blasphemy targeting God's name, dwelling, and heaven's inhabitants as a comprehensive act of contempt. Jude 8 and 10 add the inner mechanism: wilful rejection of legitimate authority combined with instinct replacing understanding, so contempt fills the void of knowledge with destructive force. Cross-register: M06 (contempt / hostile defiant rejection of authority — concentrated in this VCG). Anchor: Rev 16:11 (14350) — affliction deepening the hardened will's hostility rather than breaking it toward repentance; the most direct evidence of the inner-orientation-of-defiance mechanism.

**Members (6V):** 14354 (Jude 8), 14355 (Jude 10), 14356 (Rev 13:6), 14352 (Rev 16:9), 14350 (Rev 16:11), 14353 (Rev 16:21)

**Anchor:** 14350 (Rev 16:11)

---

### M10b-F-VCG-02 — Blasphemy as social defamation — moral failure causing dishonour

**Context description:** This VCG groups the verses in which blasphemy names either the social consequence of moral failure (conduct causing outsiders to dishonour God's name — Rom 2:24) or the communicative act of misrepresentation, false attribution, and verbal defamation (Rom 3:8, 14:16, 1Cor 4:13, 1Cor 10:30). The inner-being content shifts from the hardened will of the blasphemer to the moral failure of those who profess God while their conduct contradicts him, or the malicious intent of those who weaponise speech to defame. The evil is not open defiance against God but the subtler corruption of truth through slander, misrepresentation, or the social defamation of legitimate freedom. Cross-register: M06 (contempt expressed through speech — slander register). Anchor: Rom 2:24 (14357) — the most analytically direct case where inner moral failure produces external blasphemy as its consequence.

**Members (5V):** 14357 (Rom 2:24), 14358 (Rom 3:8), 14359 (Rom 14:16), 14351 (1Cor 4:13), 14360 (1Cor 10:30)

**Anchor:** 14357 (Rom 2:24)

---

### M10b-F-VCG-03 — Wrongdoing as a charged and contested category

**Context description:** This VCG groups the verses in which wrongdoing (kakopoios / atopos) is named as a social-legal charge — either truly applied to genuine harmful acts or falsely applied to the innocent. The inner-being content is predominantly the evil of false accusation (the malicious intent driving the false charge: Joh 18:30, 1Pe 2:12, 3:16) and the social consequence of actual wrongdoing attracting punitive authority (1Pe 2:14, 4:15). atopos (Act 25:5) anchors the purely legal-charge register. The VCG names wrongdoing as a *contested moral-legal category* — a space where the evil character of the wrongdoer meets the testimony of public accusation, which may be true or false. Cross-register: M10 (evil-as-act / legal-offense / punishable-wrong — kakopoios and atopos both carry this flag from Phase 3). Anchor: Joh 18:30 (14378) — the clearest case where false accusation drives the wrongdoing charge; the accusers' malicious false judgment is the inner-being content.

**Members (6V):** 14378 (Joh 18:30), 65874 (Act 25:5), 14377 (1Pe 2:12), 14379 (1Pe 2:14), 14380 (1Pe 3:16), 14381 (1Pe 4:15)

**Anchor:** 14378 (Joh 18:30)
