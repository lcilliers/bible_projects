## §1. Characteristics identified

Six distinct inner-being characteristics emerge from reading the 515 meaning-corpus verses across all 17 terms. The characteristics are grounded in the meaning-evidence; the keyword analytics confirm the axes.

### Characteristic 1 — Wickedness as settled person-identity

**Definition:** The wicked person as an inner-being type — the individual whose core moral character is defined by wickedness. Will corrupted, God actively excluded from the mind, inner orientation persistently bent toward harm and injustice. Wickedness is not merely a series of acts but an identity and disposition that determines the person's trajectory, relationships, and fate. The conscience is suppressed or displaced by an inner voice of transgression. The will refuses God, chooses evil companions, and schemes against the righteous. Divine judgment falls specifically on this person-type because their inner character warrants it.

**Evidence anchors:** The entire ra.sha corpus (179V) is the primary evidence base — the most extensive wicked-person description in Scripture, spanning Job, Psalms, Proverbs, and the Prophets. re.sha (9V) names wickedness as the abstract condition belonging to this person. mir.sha.at (1V) names the collective. 1Sa 17:28 (ro.a) names evil as located in the heart of a specific person.

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** will corrupted, will refusing, conscience suppressed, conscience absent, moral contrast, wickedness condemned, wickedness exposed, counsel rejected, judgment deserved, guilt objective.

**Keyword token counts (ra.sha + re.sha):** moral contrast (6), will refusing (5), will corrupted (4), divine judgment (4), conscience suppressed (4), wickedness condemned (4), guilt objective (3), judgment deserved (3), counsel rejected (3), wickedness punished (3).

### Characteristic 2 — Evil as constitutional inner nature

**Definition:** Evil as the structural condition of the inner person — not primarily the choice of the will moment by moment, but the constitutive nature from which corrupt choices flow. The heart is a treasury of evil that the person draws on; the inner nature is bent toward corruption as its default output. Humans constitutionally generate evil from within; the evil eye, the evil thoughts, the evil generation all name a collective or individual inner nature whose constitution is toward moral darkness. This characteristic is carried primarily by the Greek NT terms and captures the NT-distinctive analysis of evil as a constitutional human condition.

**Evidence anchors:** ponēros (63V) — the primary Greek term for the evil person and evil character; Mat 7:11 ("evil"), Mat 12:34-35 (heart as treasury), Heb 3:12 (evil/unbelieving heart), Heb 10:22 (evil conscience). kakia (10V of 11V — excluding Mat 6:34 outlier), ponēria (6V of 7V — excluding Eph 6:12 outlier), adikia (8V), faulos (1V).

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** constitutional evil, inner bent, heart evil, heart corrupted, heart corrupt, soul saturated, inner filling, inner disposition, vice clustering, character corrupt, corruption total, will corrupted, will defiant.

**Keyword token counts:** constitutional evil (2×ponēros), inner bent (2×ponēros), soul saturated (2×adikia+ponēros), character corrupt (5 total), heart evil (5 total), heart corrupted (4 total), inner filling (2×adikia+ponēros), inner disposition (5 total).

**Cross-register flag:** ponēros carries M27 cross-register flag for ~10-15V in the cosmic-evil-agent / evil-one / evil-age register. These verses remain in M10b-B; Phase 7 VCG design will separate the personal-evil-character VCGs from the cosmic-evil-agent VCGs within this sub-group.

### Characteristic 3 — Abomination: divine-revulsion judgment on moral character

**Definition:** The category of evil that specifically violates divine holiness and provokes divine revulsion — the inner moral condition or conduct that is detestable to God by virtue of inverting moral order, transgressing holiness-boundaries, or displaying the kind of corrupt inner character (crooked heart, proud heart, lying lips, perverted judgment) that God actively loathes. The inner-being dimension is the abominable person's state (conscience violated, shame silenced, heart crooked) and the divine inner response (revulsion, disgust, rejection, hatred). This characteristic is distinct from idolatrous abomination (Characteristic 4) — it applies to general moral-character and ethical-behavior failures that God names as abomination.

**Evidence anchors:** to.e.vah moral-character and ethical-behavior sub-corpus (~60V of 106V): the Proverbs ethical-abomination passages (Pro 3:32, 11:1, 11:20, 12:22, 15:8–9, 15:26, 16:5, 16:12, 17:15, 20:10, 20:23, 21:27, 24:9, 26:25, 28:9, 29:27), the Jeremiah/Ezekiel passages on conscience-silenced abomination (Jer 6:15, 8:12; Eze 9:4, 14:6, 36:31, 44:6-7), and other moral-character uses. bdelugma moral corpus (Luk 16:15 — pride producing abomination; Rev 17:4-5 — identity constituted by corruption; Rev 21:27 — incompatibility with holiness) = 4V. bdeluktos (Tit 1:16 — inner disconnect between profession and obedience) = 1V.

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** divine revulsion (dominant, 14 total — 13 from to.e.vah), conscience violated (5), act defiling + boundary transgressed + divine revulsion co-occurrence cluster, holiness violated (6), divine disgust (5), divine rejection (5), divine hatred (4), conscience suppressed (8 total, shared with Characteristic 1), blushing lost + shame absent co-occurrence pair.

### Characteristic 4 — Idolatrous abomination: the heart devoted to false gods

**Definition:** The specific form of abomination where the will conforms to detestable idolatrous practices, the heart goes after idol-objects, and worship is corrupted through devotion to what is not God. The inner-being dimension is the heart's defiance (Eze 11:21: heart going after detestable things), the will's conformity to abominable practices of other nations (Deu 18:9, 1Ki 14:24), the soul's delight in abominations (Isa 66:3), and the pride that converts beautiful things into idol-objects (Eze 7:20). This characteristic encompasses both the moral-character of idolaters and the spiritual-relational dimension of covenant betrayal through false devotion.

**Evidence anchors:** to.e.vah idolatry sub-corpus (~46V of 106V): Leviticus boundary-laws (Lev 18:22–30, 20:13), Deuteronomy warnings (Deu 7:25-26, 12:31, 13:14, 17:1, 18:9, 18:12, 23:18, 24:4, 32:16), the historical idolatry-reform narratives (1Ki 14:24, 2Ki 16:3, 21:2, 21:11, 23:13; 2Ch 28:3, 33:2, 34:33, 36:8, 36:14), Ezra's covenant-boundary concern (Ezr 9:1, 9:14), Isaiah's idol-folly passages (Isa 41:24, 44:19), Jeremiah's idolatry-abomination passages (Jer 2:7, 7:10, 16:18, 32:35, 44:4, 44:22), Ezekiel's idolatry corpus (Eze 5:9, 5:11, 6:9, 6:11, 7:3-9, 7:20, 8:6-17, 11:18-21, 12:16, 14:6, 16:2-58 passim, 18:12-13, 20:4-30 passim, 22:2-11 passim, 23:36, 33:26-29, 36:31, 43:8, 44:6-13), Malachi (Mal 2:11). shiq.quts (25V): predominantly idol-objects named as abomination. bdelugma desecration-event corpus (Mat 24:15, Mar 13:14) = 2V.

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** idol detestable (4), worship corrupted (6), abomination removed (5), defilement removed (3), reform enacted (2), defilement purged (2), devotion perverted (2), sacred violated, desolation enacted (3), will conformed (3), covenant abandoned.

**Cross-register flags:** M27 (idol-object / concrete-abomination-as-thing) — strong signal in shiq.quts and the bdelugma desecration verses. M10c (cultic/ritual defilement) — Leviticus and Deuteronomy boundary-law sub-corpus; Ezekiel defilement-of-land sub-corpus.

### Characteristic 5 — Iniquity as active inner scheming and evil generation

**Definition:** Evil as an ongoing active inner process — planned, cultivated, and generated from within. The iniquity that is devised on the bed, conceived in the mind, gestated in the heart, and born into harmful action. Evil as troublemaking energy: plotting against others, scheming oppression, cultivating a habitual pattern of harm. The inner person is the womb of evil; iniquity is not merely possessed but actively generated. This characteristic also encompasses the evil quality of corrupt deeds as the expression of an inwardly-defiled source — deeds whose inner origin is wickedness, and whose quality marks the one who commits them.

**Evidence anchors:** a.ven (71V, primary register): the evildoer-as-schemer/troublemaker corpus across Psalms (Psa 5:5, 6:8, 7:14, 14:4, 28:3, 36:3-4, 36:12, 41:6, 53:4, 55:3/10, 56:7, 59:2, 59:5, 64:2, 66:18, 92:7-9, 94:4/16/23, 101:8, 119:133, 125:5, 141:4, 141:9), Proverbs (Pro 6:12, 6:18, 10:29, 17:4, 21:15, 30:20), Isaiah (Isa 1:13, 10:1, 29:20, 31:2, 32:6, 55:7, 58:9, 59:4, 59:6, 59:7, 66:3), Jeremiah (Jer 4:14), Ezekiel (Eze 11:2), Hosea (Hos 6:8, 12:11), Micah (Mic 2:1), Habakkuk (Hab 1:3), Zechariah (Zec 10:2), and the Job iniquity sub-corpus (Job 4:8, 11:11, 11:14, 15:35, 21:19, 22:15, 31:3, 34:8, 34:22, 34:36, 36:10, 36:21). ro.a evil-deeds corpus (25V, excluding 1Sa 17:28 and the 2 M03 cross-register verses): Deu 28:20, Psa 28:4, Isa 1:16, Jer 4:4, 21:12, 23:2, 23:22, 25:5, 26:3, 44:22, Hos 9:15, and others.

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** mind scheming (3), will scheming (3), evil conceived (2), evil working (3), god uncalled (2), exploitation habitual (2), oppression active (2), will turning (3 in a.ven), inner state exposed (2), consequence matching (2), deeds evil (4 in ro.a), deeds corrupt (4 in ro.a), will forsaking (ro.a), evil way (ro.a).

### Characteristic 6 — Evil expressed in speech: blasphemy, defamation, and perverse testimony

**Definition:** The inner character of wickedness flowing outward through the instrument of speech — contemptuous defiance of God and of legitimate authority through blasphemy; the slander and false accusation that re-label the innocent as evildoers; the deceptive speech that misrepresents and manipulates. The inner-being dimension is the hardened will that generates defiant speech under judgment (Rev 16:9-21), the hostile contempt toward God that converts suffering into blasphemy, and the malicious intent that weaponises words against the innocent. This characteristic bridges M10b's evil-character domain with M06 (contempt/hostile speech).

**Evidence anchors:** blasfēmeō (11V): the full corpus. kakopoios (5V): the wrongdoing-as-charge corpus in 1 Peter and John. atopos (1V, Act 25:5): the legal-wrongdoing-charge register.

**Keywords driving this characteristic:** will defiant (2), witness corrupted (2), name dishonoured (1), hypocrisy causing (1), slander misrepresenting (1), moral inversion (1), accusation false (1), evil enacted (2×kakopoios), conduct accountable (2×kakopoios), false accusation (1), wrongdoing charged (1).

**Cross-register flag:** M06 (contempt / hostile speech / defiant rejection of authority) — blasfēmeō carries this flag from Phase 3.
