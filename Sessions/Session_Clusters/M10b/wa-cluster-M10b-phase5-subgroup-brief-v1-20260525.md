## THE PRIMARY OBJECTIVE — sub-groups represent characteristics (§8.0)

**This is the structural objective.** Read it carefully — it shapes every step.

1. **A sub-group represents a characteristic** — a single inner-being faculty/state. The default is **1 sub-group : 1 characteristic**.
2. **Volume-split is the documented exception.** When a characteristic's verse corpus would exceed **>206 verses** for M10b, split into multiple sub-groups by a documented split-axis. The characteristic identity persists across the splits.
3. **A sub-group serving two characteristics (SPLIT_SUBGROUP) is rare** — only when the same lexical sub-group contains different VCG registers serving different characteristics. Flag at Phase 5 if observed.
4. **Cross-register flags from Phase 3 inform characteristic identification.** A term carrying a cross-register flag may suggest a characteristic with relational content the flag names; the term's primary representation is still in M10b's sub-group structure.

## Process

### Step 1 — Identify the characteristics

Read across the meaning corpora (§2 of the constitution report) and the per-term keyword densities (§5 of the analytics report). Name the distinct **inner-being faculties / states** M10b's evidence expresses. Aim for an analytically clean list — for a cluster of M10b's scale (515 verses, 17 terms), **typically 4–6 characteristics** is realistic.

A characteristic is a single answer to: *"what specific inner-being faculty or state does this body of verses evidence?"*

**Candidate characteristics suggested by Phase 3 + the keyword analytics** (this is suggestive, not prescriptive — derive from the meanings):

- **The wicked person — settled inner orientation of moral evil** (ra.sha, re.sha, mir.sha.at as primary; kakia, ponēria as Greek parallels — the person whose moral character IS wickedness; conscience-suppressed, will-corrupted, evil-disposed)
- **Evil as the constitutional nature of conduct or being** (ponēros as primary; faulos, the abstract-evil register — what kind of conduct/character this is)
- **Abomination — moral-character judgment of what is detestable to God** (to.e.vah, bdelugma, bdeluktos as primary; the divine-revulsion + holiness-violated + boundary-transgressed pattern)
- **Wrongdoing as agent-defined evil-act** (kakopoios, adikia, atopos — the evil-doer-by-act register; M10 cross-register signal)
- **Blasphemy as evil-character expressed in speech against God** (blasfēmeō; M06 cross-register, hostile speech / defiant rejection)
- **Cosmic / supra-personal evil** — IF ponēros's cosmic-evil sub-corpus (Mat 13:19, 13:38; 1Jn; Eph 6:16) is volume-split out of the personal-evil-character characteristic, this becomes a sibling sub-group (still in M10b) with M27 cross-register flag.
- **Iniquity / trouble-state as evil-character condition** (a.ven primary; the polysemy needs VCG handling at Phase 7 but sub-group assignment may reflect dominant register)
- **Evil-state / sadness register** (ro.a primary; the M03-cross-register sub-corpus may merit a separate sub-group OR live with the wicked-person sub-group depending on verse-level dominance)

These cover ~5–7 candidates. Refine from the meaning evidence.

### Step 2 — Map each characteristic to its evidence

For each characteristic, note which **terms' verses primarily evidence it**. Some terms will be primary carriers; others will be secondary (their corpus splits across characteristics, or they're flagged cross-register).

### Step 3 — Design sub-groups to carry the characteristics

- **Default**: one sub-group per characteristic. Use natural codes (`M10b-A`, `M10b-B`, …).
- **Volume-split**: if a characteristic's verse evidence would exceed **>206 verses** (40% of 515), split that characteristic into multiple sub-groups by a named split-axis. **No characteristic at the term level exceeds the gate**, but a merged "wicked-person + evil-character" characteristic that captures ra.sha (179) + ponēros (63) + others would. If you design that merge, you must volume-split it (e.g., Hebrew-wicked-person vs Greek-evil-character; or person-as-wicked vs nature-as-evil; or settled-evil-orientation vs spreading-corruption).
- Each sub-group's `core_description` names: (a) the characteristic it represents, (b) the split-axis if it's part of a volume-split, (c) the inner-being content from the meanings.

**Note on M10b's distribution:** with 515 verses and only 17 terms, expect the design to settle around **5–7 sub-groups** (4–6 characteristics; volume-split unlikely unless wicked-person + evil-character merge).

### Step 4 — Assign every verse to exactly one sub-group

For each of the 515 Pass A meanings, record the sub-group assignment. Mapping form: **term-level with per-term split rules where needed**. For most M10b terms a term-level assignment will be sufficient; the exception is a.ven (71V, polysemous) which will need per-verse-range rules.

**Practical note on volume:** 515 verses is borderline-feasible for verse-by-verse listing in chat, but term-level rules + targeted per-verse exceptions for known polysemic terms (a.ven, possibly ro.a, possibly ponēros for the cosmic-evil sub-corpus) is the standard pattern. Output as JSON for CC's Phase 6 apply.

### Step 5 — No BOUNDARY sub-group required

Phase 3 produced **0 BOUNDARY verdicts**, so no BOUNDARY sub-group is created. Every is_relevant verse routes to a substantive sub-group.

### Step 6 — Flag SPLIT_SUBGROUP cases

If any sub-group on closer inspection contains VCG-level registers serving different characteristics (rare — most likely if you choose to keep a.ven's iniquity register and trouble register in the same sub-group rather than split-by-verse), flag it with the analytical question for Phase 7 attention.

### Step 7 — Multi-faceted terms

Terms whose verses span multiple sub-groups get primary + secondary records. Likely candidates:
- **to.e.vah** — if you design separate sub-groups for moral-character-abomination and idolatry-as-abomination, to.e.vah will span both (M10c + M27 cross-register flags travel here).
- **shiq.quts** — if its primary register lands in idolatry-as-concrete-abomination, but some verses name the moral-judgement explicitly.
- **a.ven** — already discussed.
- **ponēros** — if cosmic-evil is volume-split out, ponēros is primary in both.

## Discipline

1. **Read every verse-meaning** before designing sub-groups — at M10b's scale (515V across 17 terms), this is feasible per-term. Don't skip the large terms (ra.sha 179V, to.e.vah 106V, a.ven 71V, ponēros 63V): characteristic identification depends on what those corpora actually evidence.
2. **Characteristic-first design** (§8.0). Don't cluster by meaning-similarity until you've named the characteristics the cluster expresses.
3. **The 40% distribution gate is a volume-split trigger, not a fail signal.** The gate is **>206 verses** for M10b. If you design a characteristic-as-one-sub-group that captures >206V, split it.
4. **Cross-register flags travel forward.** When a flagged term lands in a sub-group, the flag is preserved in the rationale for Phase 7/9 consumption. Cross-register flags do NOT define sub-group boundaries — they note that the term participates in another cluster's analytical conversation too.
5. **a.ven is the cluster's polysemy hot-spot.** Phase 3 explicitly noted that VCG-level distinction is required at Phase 7. For Phase 5, decide whether a.ven goes to ONE sub-group (with the polysemy resolved at Phase 7 VCGs) or TWO sub-groups (per-verse-range split by dominant register). Either choice is acceptable; the rationale must be explicit.
6. **Cosmic-evil ponēros sub-corpus is the cluster's volume-split decision point.** Phase 3 flagged it as substantial (~10–15V of ponēros's 63). Decide whether (a) it stays integrated with the personal-evil-character sub-group (M27 cross-register flag at the term level), or (b) it splits out into its own sub-group (M27 cross-register flag at the sub-group level, signalling Phase 7 / Phase 9 T6 attention to the cosmic-evil dimension). Either is acceptable; rationale must be explicit.
