## Appendix — Synthesis prose

### Theme 1 — The wicked-righteous structural opposite (M26)

M10b is not a self-contained cluster — it is constitutively defined against M26 (Righteousness) as its structural foil. The antithetical pair (wicked/righteous — ra.sha/tsaddiq) appears across virtually every VCG in Char 1's 190-verse corpus: Gen 18:23-25 presupposes the categorical distinction as the frame for Abraham's intercession; Psa 1:1-6 organises its entire portrait of the two ways around the contrast; Mal 3:18 names the programme's goal as the ability to distinguish between the righteous and the wicked. The Proverbs antithetical couplets (Char 1 VCGs 06/08/09) build the entire wisdom-instruction tradition on this pair — every observation about the wicked is simultaneously an observation about the righteous by contrast.

This structural pairing extends across the other M10b characteristics. Char 3 (abomination) defines itself against its antithetical positive: Pro 3:32 — "the devious person is an abomination to the Lord, but the upright are in his confidence" — the structural opposite of abomination is divine confidence/intimacy (ratson — delight). Chars 3 and 4 share this antithetical structure: what provokes divine revulsion (to.e.vah) is contrasted with what provokes divine delight (ratson/ratsah — Pro 11:1/20; 15:8). Char 5 (iniquity-scheming) defines its evildoer-class (VCG-03) against those who call upon the Lord (Psa 14:4 — "do not call upon the Lord" as the evildoer's defining omission — confirmed by the person of 1Pe 3:16 with a good conscience as the structural opposite of Char 6). Char 6's withheld glory (Rev 16:9 — "they did not repent and give him glory") defines its structural opposite as glory-giving speech toward God.

At cluster scope: M10b and M26 constitute a single binary moral-characterisation system — the cluster cannot be understood without M26 as its structural counterpart. Session C chapter treatment of M10b should consistently position M26 as the background against which each characteristic is defined.

### Theme 2 — The three-part abomination mechanism

Chars 3 and 4 share the same to.e.vah vocabulary and a common structural mechanism consisting of three elements: (1) an act or condition that defiles, (2) a moral or covenantal boundary transgressed, and (3) the divine revulsion response that names what was done as abomination. This three-part mechanism operates across both characteristics but with distinct objects.

In Char 3 (moral-character abomination), the mechanism works as: inner moral character corruption (crooked heart, arrogant heart, dishonest practice) + moral boundary of God's holiness transgressed + divine revulsion verdict (to.e.vah). The mechanism is person-centred — the abomination arises from what the person is inwardly. Pro 16:5 is the clearest: arrogant heart → God's holiness-boundary transgressed → "abomination to the Lord."

In Char 4 (idolatrous abomination), the mechanism works as: idolatrous devotion to foreign deities/objects + covenantal-exclusivity boundary of the first commandment transgressed + divine revulsion verdict (to.e.vah/shiq.quts). The mechanism is devotion-centred — the abomination arises from what the heart is attached to. Eze 11:21 is the clearest: "heart goes after detestable things" → covenantal exclusivity transgressed → abomination verdict.

The two characteristics diverge at the level of what triggers the mechanism: moral character (Char 3) vs. devotional attachment (Char 4). Both share the same three-part structure and the same divine-revulsion response. The most analytically significant boundary case is Deu 27:15 (secretly made idol assigned to Char 3 for conscience-suppression content) and Eze 18:12-13 (idols plus ethical violations assigned to Char 4 for idolatrous context) — confirming that the boundary is content-based, not mechanism-based.

The three-part mechanism also explains why Char 3 functions as a meta-characteristic: it is the verdict-layer that applies the divine revulsion response to any inner condition that violates the moral or covenantal boundary. Where Chars 1, 2, 4, and 5 describe the defiling condition and the boundary transgressed, Char 3 names the divine-revulsion verdict that results. In this sense, all M10b inner evil is potentially abominable in the Char 3 sense — the abomination verdict is the divine response to any of the cluster's five primary inner-evil forms when they violate the holiness boundary.

### Theme 3 — M10b ↔ M27 boundary

Three INTEGRATION_NOTEs (Observations #80, #81, #82) map the M10b/M27 seam — confirming that the cluster sits adjacent to M27's register of cosmic-evil-agent, concrete-evil-objects, and calamity/ruin.

**Observation #80 (ra.sha forensic-verdict VCG):** The 5-verse M10b-A-VCG-01 corpus (Exo 23:7; Deu 25:1/2; 1Ki 8:32; 2Ch 6:23) carries ra.sha as a legal-status label ("the legally condemnable party") rather than as an inner-moral-character designation. These verses sit on the M10/M10b boundary — M10 addresses the act of moral transgression (guilt as an event), while M10b addresses the inner character of the person. The forensic-verdict register in VCG-01 belongs at the boundary between these two: it is the legal-public naming of the guilty person (M10 register) expressed through the M10b person-type term (ra.sha). The VCG is structurally separated and the M10 cross-register is confirmed.

**Observation #81 (ponēros cosmic-evil-agent VCG):** The 13-verse M10b-B-VCG-01 corpus carries ponēros in the cosmic-evil-agent register (the evil one — 1Jo 2:13/14; 5:18/19; Mat 13:19/38; Joh 17:15; Eph 6:16; 2Th 3:3) and the cosmic-evil-age register (Gal 1:4; Eph 5:16; 6:13 — "the present evil age"). This sub-corpus sits on the M10b/M27 boundary — M27 carries cosmic-evil-agent vocabulary (the adversarial spiritual being that is the source of cosmic evil), while M10b carries the personal constitutional evil of the human inner person. VCG-01's cosmic-evil sub-corpus provides the environmental context within which human constitutional evil operates (the evil one's dominion as the backdrop) — it is M27 register enclosed within the M10b characteristic because of ponēros vocabulary overlap.

**Observation #82 (a.ven trouble-suffered VCG):** The 7-verse M10b-E-VCG-01 corpus carries a.ven in the victim/affliction-received register — the harm that befalls the psalmist or community from external oppressors, not the iniquity-scheming of the agent. M27 (calamity/ruin) and M03 (grief/sorrow) are both implicated: Psa 55:3; 55:10; 94:16; Hab 1:3; Zec 10:2 carry a.ven as trouble received by victims (M27 register); Neh 2:2 and Ecc 7:3 carry a.ven as sadness/sorrow (M03 register). The VCG is structurally separated from the iniquity-scheming VCGs.

These three observations together reveal the M10b/M27 seam: the cluster addresses the moral-character side of inner evil, while M27 addresses the concrete-object, cosmic-agent, and calamity-as-consequence side. The seam is vocabulary-based (ponēros, a.ven, ra.sha all operate in multiple registers) rather than concept-based — the concepts themselves are distinct.

### Theme 4 — OT vs NT vocabulary arcs

The cluster has a distinctive OT/NT vocabulary structure that maps onto a theological emphasis difference across the two testaments.

The OT Hebrew vocabulary (Chars 1, 5 exclusively; Chars 3/4 primarily): emphasises the person-type and the inner condition. The RSH vocabulary suite (Char 1) constructs the wicked person as a recognisable inner-being type with full vocabulary support — the wicked person (ra.sha), wickedness as their condition (re.sha), their collective (mir.sha.at), their act (rasha'), and their structural opposite (tsaddiq). This vocabulary suite gives the OT unparalleled person-type precision. The a.ven vocabulary (Char 5) emphasises the active generative process — what the inner person is doing. The to.e.vah/shiq.quts vocabulary (Chars 3/4) emphasises what God's revulsion-response names as abominable.

What the OT emphasises that the NT does not: the wicked person as a comprehensive person-type with full vocabulary support; iniquity as an active generative process with a specific root (a-w-n); abomination as a relational category with precise vocabulary for both the moral-character and idolatrous sub-registers; the conscience-sequence within abomination (Char 3 VCG-04 — 11 conscience verses).

The NT Greek vocabulary (Chars 2, 6 primarily; Chars 3/4 partially): emphasises the constitutional structure and the speech-expression. The ponēros/kakia/adikia suite (Char 2) gives the NT a more structurally analytical vocabulary for the inner evil-constitution — naming it as constitutional (structural wrongness — adikia), settled malice (kakia), and comprehensive evil-quality (ponēros). The blasfēmeō/kakopoios vocabulary (Char 6) gives the NT a specific vocabulary for evil expressed through speech — absent from the OT equivalent.

What the NT emphasises that the OT does not: the constitutional-structural dimension of inner evil (Char 2's four-term suite); evil expressed through speech as a distinct category (Char 6); the cosmic-evil-agent as the background framework for constitutional human evil (Char 2 VCG-01).

Where they converge: the to.e.vah/bdelugma bridge (Chars 3/4) carries the same relational-verdict concept across both testaments. The person-type vocabulary (ra.sha in OT; ponēros substantive in NT) overlaps in naming the evil person as a type. The eschatological trajectory (judgment, elimination, exclusion from the divine community) is confirmed across both testaments.

### Theme 5 — Polysemy hot-spots across the cluster

All three INTEGRATION_NOTEs name polysemy hot-spots — single vocabulary terms operating in multiple analytically distinct registers simultaneously. Their cluster-level significance is that they reveal points where Scripture's inner-being vocabulary is broader than any single analytical category.

**a.ven (Char 5):** The a-w-n root generates a.ven that names the active scheming agent (VCG-02 — the plotter on the bed), the evildoer character-class (VCG-03 — the worker of iniquity), the accumulated inner condition (VCG-04 — cherished iniquity), the quality of expressed deeds (VCG-05 — evil of their deeds), and the trouble received by victims (VCG-01 — trouble dropped on the psalmist). These five registers are not ambiguities to be resolved but evidence of a genuinely comprehensive term — the root covers the full harm-cycle from inner vacuity to social impact. The polysemy is not a problem for the analysis but evidence of the Hebrew conceptual field's holistic character: the agent's iniquity (active) and the victim's trouble (received) are two faces of the same event named by the same root.

**ponēros (Char 2):** The ponē- root generates ponēros that names the constitutional personal evil (the evil person whose inner nature produces bad fruit — VCG-02/03/04), the cosmic evil-agent (the evil one — VCG-01), and the evil age (the present evil age — VCG-01). These three registers reflect a single consistent root-meaning (what is harmful, bad, evil) operating at three levels: personal, cosmic-personal, and cosmic-temporal. The polysemy reveals that the NT's ponēros vocabulary field is deliberately structured to connect the personal and the cosmic — the evil within the person and the evil that frames the person's world are both named ponēros, not accidentally but because the NT's theology of evil sees them as connected realities.

**ra.sha (Char 1):** The RSH root generates ra.sha that names the inner-moral-character (the wicked person as a type — primary M10b register) and the forensic-verdict legal-guilty status (the legally condemned party — VCG-01 5V). These two registers reflect the same root operating in two social contexts — the moral community (where ra.sha names the inner-character wicked person) and the judicial court (where ra.sha names the guilty verdict). The polysemy here is analytically resolved by context: when ra.sha appears in judicial/forensic contexts (Exo 23:7; Deu 25:1/2; 1Ki 8:32; 2Ch 6:23), the legal-guilty sense is primary; when it appears in wisdom, psalm, and prophetic contexts, the inner-moral-character sense is primary. The VCG structure correctly separates these registers.

At cluster scope: the three polysemies reveal the same analytical principle — the biblical vocabulary is holistic rather than taxonomically precise. Scripture names the same realities across their full range of occurrence rather than creating distinct terms for each analytically separable register. The programme's VCG architecture correctly handles this by separating the registers structurally while retaining them within the same characteristic.

### Theme 6 — The will-conscience-heart triad as the inner location of evil

The keyword analytics surfaced heart (89 tokens), conscience (68 tokens), and will (193 tokens) as the dominant inner-locus terms across the cluster's findings. The synthesis confirms that these three faculties together constitute the cluster's primary inner-being signature — every M10b characteristic engages this triad, but each engages it differently.

**Heart** is the universal constitutional location — confirmed across all six characteristics (T2.3.1). Every M10b form of inner evil is located in the heart: transgression speaking to the wicked deep in the heart (Char 1 — Psa 36:1), the evil treasury of the heart (Char 2 — Mat 12:35), the crooked and arrogant heart (Char 3 — Pro 11:20; 16:5), the heart going after detestable things (Char 4 — Eze 11:21), the heart busy with iniquity (Char 5 — Isa 32:6), the will refusing to repent at the heart level (Char 6 — Rev 16:9). The heart is the integrating organ of the entire cluster.

**Will** is the dominant primary faculty — engaged as the primary locus by four of six characteristics (Chars 1, 4, 5, 6). The will's settled orientation (Char 1 — the wicked person's way), its deliberate devotional choice (Char 4 — "not willing to listen" — Eze 20:8), its volitional scheming commitment (Char 5 — "this you have chosen" — Job 36:21), and its defiant refusal to repent (Char 6 — Rev 16:9) together confirm the will as M10b's primary inner-being faculty. The two exceptions (Char 2 — constitutional structure; Char 3 — relational-moral verdict) show inner evil as not exclusively volitional but also structural and responsive.

**Conscience** is the cluster's internal-arresting mechanism — engaged by all six characteristics through its suppression. The conscience-silencing evidence (Jer 6:15/8:12 — Char 3 VCG-04; Pro 30:20 — Char 5; Rev 16:9/11 — Char 6; Psa 10:4 — Char 1; Eze 8:17 — Char 4; Heb 10:22 — Char 2) confirms that every M10b form of inner evil targets and progressively silences the conscience. The conscience's health is the diagnostic instrument for the cluster: the awakened conscience grieves over abominations (Eze 9:4 — Char 3), recognises iniquity as domination-threatening (Psa 119:133 — Char 5), and maintains good conduct under false accusation (1Pe 3:16 — Char 6). The silenced conscience produces the cluster's terminal characteristics: "I have done no wrong" (Pro 30:20 — Char 5), "they did not repent" (Rev 16:9 — Char 6), "not at all ashamed" (Jer 6:15 — Char 3).

The triad's cluster-level significance: inner evil occupies the heart as its universal location, operates through the will as its primary faculty, and progressively silences the conscience as its internal-arresting mechanism. Any transformation of M10b inner evil must address all three: cleansing of the heart (Jer 4:14 — Char 5), transformation of the will (repentance — all six), and restoration of the conscience (Eze 36:31 — Char 3; Heb 10:22 — Char 2).

### Theme 7 — Blasphemy as the M10b ↔ M06 bridge

Char 6's blasfēmeō vocabulary carries an M06 (contempt/hostile speech/defiant rejection of authority) cross-register flag. The bridge is most visible in Jude 8 (VCG-01): "they reject authority and blaspheme the glorious ones" — the authority-rejection is the M06 element (contemptuous defiant stance toward legitimate authority) and the blasphemy is the CHAR-6 element (the speech-act that expresses the inner wickedness). The two are causally linked in the same verse: authority-rejection enables and produces blasphemous speech.

The bridge operates at two levels within Char 6. The VCG-01 direct-blasphemy register (Rev 13:6; 16:9/11/21; Jude 8/10) carries the bridge most clearly: the hardened will's contemptuous rejection of divine authority (M06) is expressed through the speech-act of cursing God's name (CHAR-6). These two registers — the inner contemptuous posture and its verbal expression — are analytically separable but empirically inseparable in the evidence.

Rom 2:24 (VCG-02) shows the bridge's indirect form: moral failure in conduct causes God's name to be blasphemed among the Gentiles — the believers' conduct (inner moral character) produces an outcome that belongs to M06 (dishonour to God's name in the social sphere). Here the inner moral failure (CHAR-6's constitutional-moral root) produces a social contempt outcome (M06's relational-dishonour domain).

At cluster scope: CHAR-6 serves as the speech-expression layer of the entire M10b cluster (T1.4.3) — what Chars 1-5 produce in the inner person, Char 6 delivers through speech. And Char 6's M06 bridge shows that the speech-delivery crosses into the contempt-domain: evil expressed through speech is not merely verbal but contempt-enacting. CHAR-6 is the constitutive connection between M10b (inner evil) and M06 (contemptuous relational act).

### Theme 8 — Divine response patterns

The cluster reveals five distinct divine response patterns, distributed across its six characteristics. These patterns are not interchangeable but correspond to the specific inner-evil form of each characteristic.

**Active moral hatred** (Chars 1 and 5): "his soul hates the wicked" (Psa 11:5); "you hate all evildoers" (Psa 5:5). This response is directed at the person-type — the settled wicked character and the active scheming evildoer. The hatred is not passively observational but morally engaged: God's character is in active moral opposition to the wicked inner orientation.

**Active revulsion** (Chars 3 and 4): the to.e.vah framework is relational-response rather than personal hatred — God turns away in revulsion from what is abominable. Pro 11:1; 16:5 name specific inner conditions as "abomination to the Lord" — the revulsion is the divine response to specific inner qualities (false balance, arrogant heart) rather than to the person per se. This is analytically distinct from hatred (Chars 1/5): the revulsion is directed at the condition/quality; the hatred is directed at the person who is constituted by that quality.

**Presence-withdrawal** (Char 4 specifically): Eze 5:11 — "I will withdraw. My eye will not spare, and I will have no pity." The divine presence removal is the characteristic divine response to the saturation of the sacred space with idolatrous abominations. This response is distinctive — it is not primarily a punishing response but a withdrawing one: God removes his presence from what cannot coexist with his holiness.

**Retributive return** (Chars 1 and 5): "He will bring back on them their iniquity" (Psa 94:23); "their condemnation is just" (Rom 3:8). The divine justice returns the evil upon the evildoer as its due consequence — a morally principled retributive response. The retributive return is proportional: the iniquity returned is the person's own iniquity, not a new divine evil.

**Redemptive desire** (all six): every characteristic also evidences a divine desire for the person's return before the eschatological terminus. Eze 33:11 (Char 1); Act 3:26 (Char 2); Eze 14:6 (Char 3/4); Job 36:10 (Char 5); Rev 16:9's implied missed repentance (Char 6). The redemptive desire operates alongside and in tension with the other four responses — the same God who hates the wicked also desires their turning.

The cluster reveals God's response to inner evil as neither simple nor uniform: hatred (Chars 1/5), revulsion (Chars 3/4), withdrawal (Char 4), retribution (Chars 1/5/6), and redemptive desire (all six) coexist as expressions of the divine holiness and love responding to the six forms of inner evil M10b names.

### Theme 9 — Constitutional vs volitional evil: a coherent ontology?

Char 2 (constitutional inner nature) treats evil as the default structural output of fallen human nature — what the person is constituted as at the generative level. Mat 7:11 ("you who are evil") and Mat 12:35 (the evil treasury from which evil things flow) locate evil at the level of what the person is rather than what they do. Char 1 (settled person-identity) treats wickedness as a stable inner alignment that has been chosen and consolidated — what the person has volitionally settled into. Char 5 (active inner scheming) treats iniquity as generated moment-by-moment through deliberate cognitive-volitional activity.

These three accounts appear to be in tension: if evil is constitutional (Char 2), is it also volitional (Chars 1/5)? The cluster's answer is that both accounts are simultaneously true at different analytical levels. Char 2 names the constitutional baseline that makes volitional evil possible and probable — the fallen constitutional condition is the ground from which the specific volitional orientations (Char 1) and generative processes (Char 5) arise. Without the Char 2 baseline (constitutional evil as the universal human starting condition), the Char 1 and Char 5 volitional specifics would have no inner-being ground to grow from.

The ontological account is layered: constitutional condition (Char 2 — the structural inner evil) → settled volitional orientation (Char 1 — the will's consolidated wickedness) → active generative process (Char 5 — the moment-by-moment scheming). These three characteristics do not contradict each other but describe the same inner-evil reality at three analytical levels of depth. The constitutional provides the structural ground; the settled orientation provides the directional form; the active scheming provides the productive expression.

Chars 3 and 4 add the divine-verdict layer: once the constitutional, settled, and scheming forms of inner evil are named, Chars 3 and 4 name the divine-revulsion verdict that those conditions elicit. Char 6 adds the expressive layer: the constitutional, settled, scheming inner evil finds its speech-expression through Char 6's blasfēmeō vocabulary. The cluster thus presents a coherent six-layer ontology of inner evil from its deepest constitutional ground through its settled orientation and active scheming through the divine verdict and to its speech-expression — each layer building on the previous.

### Theme 10 — Hardening, suppression, defiance: the trajectories of moral failure

The cluster documents three distinct trajectories of moral deterioration, concentrated in different characteristics.

**Hardening** is Char 6's defining trajectory: the will becoming more fixed against God under increasing divine pressure (Rev 16:9 → 16:11 → 16:21). Each divine judgment intensifies the hardened will's defiance rather than breaking it — the characteristic reinforces itself through each judgment-cycle. Hardening is the only M10b trajectory that specifically deepens under divine corrective pressure rather than persisting in its absence. This makes Char 6's trajectory the cluster's most extreme and the most analytically distinctive: it is the characteristic of the will that cannot be arrested by external means, even divine means, once fully established.

**Suppression** is Char 3's primary trajectory (and secondarily Chars 1, 2, and 5): the conscience being progressively silenced until it produces no arresting signal. Jer 6:15/8:12 document the terminus of this trajectory — "not at all ashamed; they did not know how to blush" — a conscience so thoroughly suppressed that it no longer produces the shame-signal that would arrest the abomination. Pro 30:20 (Char 5) documents the same terminus: "I have done no wrong" — the conscience co-opted to produce false self-exculpation rather than conviction. Suppression is the trajectory of the inner faculty whose function is to arrest evil but which has been progressively silenced by the very evil it should arrest.

**Defiance** is Chars 4 and 1's primary trajectory: the will's sustained commitment to the evil-form against the corrective influence of divine instruction and prophetic warning. Eze 20:8 ("not willing to listen; none of them cast away") shows sustained refusal to respond to the corrective instruction. Dan 12:10 shows the eschatological terminal state: "the wicked shall act wickedly" as a fixed, irreversible condition. Defiance is distinct from hardening (Char 6) in that it is the sustained persistence of the evil-form in the face of available correction, rather than the escalation of defiance under applied pressure.

The three trajectories have different reversibility profiles. Suppression (Chars 1, 3, 5) is the most reversible — the conscience can be reactivated by divine intervention (Eze 36:31 — restored memory producing self-loathing; Job 36:10 — divine ear-opening). Defiance (Chars 1, 4) is reversible while the person remains alive and corrective instruction is available (Eze 18:21 — "if the wicked person turns away from all his sins"). Hardening (Char 6) is the trajectory most closely approaching irreversibility — at the Rev 16 eschatological terminus, three successive judgments produce no repentance, suggesting that the reversibility window has effectively closed. The cluster's comprehensive teaching: all three trajectories are reversible prior to the eschatological terminus; divine intervention (ear-opening — Job 36:10; memory-restoration — Eze 36:31; the offer of abundant pardon — Isa 55:7) is the mechanism of reversal; and the urgency of response follows from the progressive narrowing of the reversal window as each trajectory deepens.
