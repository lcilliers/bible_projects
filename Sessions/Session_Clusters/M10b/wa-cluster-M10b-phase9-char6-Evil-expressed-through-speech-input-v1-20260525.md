## §1 — Characteristic 6 (Evil expressed through speech)

**Short name:** Evil expressed through speech
**Characteristic_id:** 52
**Definition:**

> The inner character of wickedness expressed through the instrument of speech — the defiant will that generates contemptuous blasphemy against God even under judgment, the hostile slander that falsely charges the innocent as evildoers, the deceptive misrepresentation that inverts moral reality through verbal manipulation. The hardened will interprets divine judgment as occasion for further defiance.

**Constituent sub-groups:** 1
**Total verses in scope:** ~17

## §3 — Verses in scope (per sub-group, canonical Bible order)

### M10b-F — Blasphemy and defaming evil — wickedness flowing through the instrument of speech

#### vc=14378 — Joh 18:30 (G2555 kakopoios) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **Joh 18:30 They answered him , “If this man were not doing evil , we would not have delivered him over to you .”**

**Pass A meaning:** The accusers define the arrested man as an evildoer to justify handing him over; the charge of wrongdoing is a social-legal accusation, and the inner-being content is their malicious false judgment rather than the accused's actual state.

#### vc=65874 — Act 25:5 (G0824 atopos) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **Act 25:5 “ So ,” said he, “let the men of authority among you go down with me, and if there is anything wrong about the man , let them bring charges against him .”**

**Pass A meaning:** Wrong (atopos) here refers to an objectively verifiable legal wrongdoing; the inner-being dimension is minimal — it functions as a legal category for chargeable misconduct rather than naming an inner moral state.

#### vc=14357 — Rom 2:24 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-02`

> **Rom 2:24 For , as it is written , “The name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles because of you .”**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy is triggered not by direct speech but by the moral failure of those who claim to represent God — their conduct causes outsiders to dishonour the divine name.

#### vc=14358 — Rom 3:8 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-02`

> **Rom 3:8 And why not do evil that good may come ?— as some people slanderously charge us with saying . Their condemnation is just .**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy here is the false attribution of a morally bankrupt teaching to someone — the slander that good ends justify evil means — and Paul affirms that such misrepresentation is justly condemned.

#### vc=14359 — Rom 14:16 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-02`

> **Rom 14:16 So do not let what you regard as good be spoken of as evil .**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy here describes the social result of misusing legitimate freedom — when good is spoken of as evil, others' consciences are damaged and God's gift is defamed.

#### vc=14351 — 1Cor 4:13 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-02`

> **1Cor 4:13 when slandered , we entreat . We have become , and are still , like the scum of the world , the refuse of all things .**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy (slander) is something the apostle receives but answers with entreaty — the inner response is non-retaliatory, though the slander reduces the slandered to the status of refuse.

#### vc=14360 — 1Cor 10:30 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-02`

> **1Cor 10:30 If I partake with thankfulness , why am I denounced because of that for which I give thanks ?**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy here is the act of denouncing someone for exercising a freedom performed with thanksgiving — the inner attitude of gratitude is attacked by others' negative speech.

#### vc=14377 — 1Pe 2:12 (G2555 kakopoios) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **1Pe 2:12 Keep your conduct among the Gentiles honorable , so that when they speak against you as evildoers , they may see your good deeds and glorify God on the day of visitation .**

**Pass A meaning:** Wrongdoing is what opponents falsely accuse believers of — the irony is that honorable conduct will eventually silence the accusation, as good deeds speak louder than slander.

#### vc=14379 — 1Pe 2:14 (G2555 kakopoios) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **1Pe 2:14 or to governors as sent by him to punish those who do evil and to praise those who do good .**

**Pass A meaning:** Wrongdoing is the behaviour civil authority is tasked to punish — those who do evil attract external corrective judgment, while right conduct receives praise, showing evil as socially destructive action.

#### vc=14380 — 1Pe 3:16 (G2555 kakopoios) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **1Pe 3:16 having a good conscience , so that , when you are slandered , those who revile your good behavior in Christ may be put to shame .**

**Pass A meaning:** Wrongdoing is what slanderers falsely charge believers with — a good conscience and honorable behaviour expose the false accusation, turning shame back on those who make it.

#### vc=14381 — 1Pe 4:15 (G2555 kakopoios) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-03`

> **1Pe 4:15 But let none of you suffer as a murderer or a thief or an evildoer or as a meddler .**

**Pass A meaning:** Wrongdoing encompasses serious harmful acts — murder, theft, meddling — that would justify suffering; believers are to ensure any suffering they bear is not deserved punishment for such acts.

#### vc=14354 — Jude 8 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Jude 8 Yet in like manner these people also , relying on their dreams , defile the flesh , reject authority , and blaspheme the glorious ones.**

**Pass A meaning:** Dreamers defile flesh, reject authority, and blaspheme glorious ones — showing that blasphemy here arises from a wilful inner rejection of legitimate authority combined with sensory self-indulgence.

#### vc=14355 — Jude 10 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Jude 10 But these people blaspheme all that they do not understand , and they are destroyed by all that they, like unreasoning animals , understand instinctively .**

**Pass A meaning:** Blasphemy is directed at what is not understood, revealing that the inner faculty of rational comprehension is bypassed — contempt fills the void of ignorance, and instinct without understanding destroys.

#### vc=14356 — Rev 13:6 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Rev 13:6 It opened its mouth to utter blasphemies against God , blaspheming his name and his dwelling , that is , those who dwell in heaven .**

**Pass A meaning:** The beast's mouth becomes the instrument of sustained, deliberate blasphemy targeting God's name, his dwelling, and heaven's inhabitants — a total inner orientation of defiant contempt toward the divine.

#### vc=14352 — Rev 16:9 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Rev 16:9 They were scorched by the fierce heat , and they cursed the name of God who had power over these plagues . They did not repent and give him glory .**

**Pass A meaning:** Despite being scorched by divine judgment, people curse God's name and refuse to repent — revealing a will hardened against God even under direct punitive pressure, with glory withheld.

#### vc=14350 — Rev 16:11 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Rev 16:11 and cursed the God of heaven for their pain and sores . They did not repent of their deeds .**

**Pass A meaning:** In pain and suffering people curse God rather than repent of their deeds, showing that affliction deepens the hardened will's hostility rather than breaking it toward repentance.

#### vc=14353 — Rev 16:21 (G0987 blasfēmeō) — VCG `M10b-F-VCG-01`

> **Rev 16:21 And great hailstones , about one hundred pounds each, fell from heaven on people ; and they cursed God for the plague of the hail , because the plague was so severe .**

**Pass A meaning:** Catastrophic hail drives people to curse God rather than seek him, showing that the will, fully hardened, interprets devastating judgment as cause for defiant blasphemy rather than contrition.
