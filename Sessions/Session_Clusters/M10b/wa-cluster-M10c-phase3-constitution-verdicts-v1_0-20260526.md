## Per-term verdicts

### G0167 akatharsia — impurity (mti_id=919) — STAYS

**Rationale:** Every verse in this 10-verse corpus centres the inner-being state of moral defilement as its primary content. The term operates exclusively in the NT inner-moral-defilement register: impurity located within the hidden inner life (Mat 23:27), arising from heart-level lusts (Rom 1:24), residing as a prior master over bodily members (Rom 6:19), characterised as morally chosen and unrepented (2Cor 12:21), incompatible with holy inner identity (Eph 5:3), and defined as the contrary inner condition that the divine call to holiness opposes (1Th 4:7). This is unambiguously M10c's characteristic.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M12 (Purity — structural opposite):** Purity is the explicit structural opposite in every verse — the inner state of holiness against which impurity is measured.
- **M10 (Sin — sin-act producing defilement-state):** Rom 1:24, 6:19, Gal 5:19 each show desire and sin as the generative act producing the impurity-state that M10c owns.

### G0169 akathartos — unclean (mti_id=5574) — STAYS

**Rationale:** The 30-verse corpus divides into three sub-registers, all of which evidence relationship with M10c's defilement-state characteristic. (a) The unclean spirits sub-corpus (~21 verses) describes defiling, afflicting, controlling spiritual agents whose inhabiting presence constitutes the defilement of a person — the person's inner and bodily life is corrupted, dominated, and controlled by the unclean force within them. This is defilement of the person as state, carried by an external spiritual agent, squarely M10c. (b) The ritual/categorical sub-corpus (Act 10:14, 10:28, 11:8; 1Cor 7:14) describes learned inner convictions about who is defiled and divine revelation overturning those categories — the unclean state as a condition of the inner person that determines access and relationship. (c) The moral sub-corpus (Eph 5:5; 2Cor 6:17; Rev 17:4, 18:2) describes unclean inner state as heart allegiance disorder and as the defining character of total corruption. All sub-registers evidence M10c.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M27 (Evil — cosmic evil agents):** The unclean spirits sub-corpus (~21 verses) sits squarely in M27's territory of hostile spiritual powers. M10c owns the defilement-state produced in the person; M27 owns the character of the agents responsible.
- **M12 (Purity — ritual discernment):** Act 10:14, 10:28, 11:8 — inner categories of who is clean or unclean; divine correction of those categories.
- **M10 (Sin — sin-act producing defilement):** Eph 5:5 — impure inner state as heart allegiance disorder equated with idolatry.

### G3394 miasmos — defilement (mti_id=1023) — STAYS

**Rationale:** The single verse (2Pe 2:10) names defilement explicitly as the inner-being state arising from indulgence in defiling passion combined with contempt for authority. The inner posture is bold wilfulness in which desire and pride together suppress reverence and restraint. The M10c defilement-state register is primary and direct.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M29 (Desire — desire as vehicle of defilement):** The defiling passion is the driving force through which the defilement-state is produced.
- **M08 (Pride — contempt for authority as companion of defilement):** The arrogant posture of contemning authority accompanies and intensifies the defilement.

### G3435 molunō — to defile (mti_id=5001) — STAYS

**Rationale:** All three verses evidence M10c's defilement-state register, each from a different angle. 1Cor 8:7 — conscience is the seat of defilement when a weak inner moral conviction is overridden by eating food associated with idols; the defilement is located in the conscience itself, not the food. Rev 3:4 — the few who have not soiled their garments are those who have maintained inner and outward moral integrity; the garment-defilement is the inner-moral condition being avoided. Rev 14:4 — those not defiled have kept bodies and inner lives in a state of consecrated purity; defilement is what total devotion to the Lamb forecloses.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M12 (Purity — structural opposite):** Purity is the state preserved in all three verses; defilement is what is either suffered (1Cor 8:7) or refused (Rev 3:4, 14:4).
- **M09 (Humility/Consecration):** Rev 14:4 — total consecration to the Lamb; self-denial and devotion as the inner posture that defines those not defiled.

### G3436 molusmos — defilement (mti_id=787) — STAYS

**Rationale:** 2Cor 7:1 — the single verse — explicitly locates defilement in both body and spirit, and calls for active self-purification from every defilement of body and spirit. This is the locus classicus of M10c's inner-being claim: defilement is declared to penetrate the full inner-outer constitution of the person. The defilement-state is the starting condition the verse calls the person to leave; the call to cleanse is the response. Unambiguous M10c.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M11 (Repentance/Cleansing — cleansing response):** The verse is primarily a call to active self-purification; the defilement-state is the condition being addressed.
- **M12 (Purity — perfecting holiness):** The full verse (2Cor 7:1) names "perfecting holiness in the fear of God" as the positive goal of the cleansing; purity is the structural opposite being pursued.

### H2930A ta.me — to defile (verb, mti_id=5581) — STAYS

**Rationale:** The 128-verse corpus is the core body of M10c's defilement vocabulary in Hebrew Scripture, spanning three major registers. (a) Ritual/bodily defilement (leprosy, corpse contact, bodily emissions, carcasses, childbirth, menstrual): M10c's bodily-defilement sub-register, which is foundational to the cluster's characteristic by definition. Even within the ritual register, inner-being content is present: Lev 11:43/44 frames wilful self-defilement as a volitional choice tied to imitating divine holiness; Lev 15:31 names defilement as existentially dangerous because it threatens the divine dwelling. (b) Sexual/moral-relational defilement (Gen 34; Lev 18; Num 5; Eze 18, 22, 33): adultery and sexual violations described as self-defiling acts; defilement is the inner-being state that moral transgression of covenantal bonds produces. Num 5's ordeal law treats defilement as an objective moral-covenantal condition with bodily consequences. (c) Idolatry/corporate defilement (Ezekiel corpus, Jeremiah, Hosea, Kings): defilement as the inner expression of a will straying from God; corporate defilement of sanctuary, land, and divine name; Eze 20:43 — the memory of defilement functioning as the inner mechanism of repentance. The §6.3.2 verse-level relationship test is met across all 128 verses.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M12 (Purity — structural opposite):** Virtually every verse carries purity as the structural opposite of the defilement-state named.
- **M10 (Sin — sin-act producing defilement-state):** The idolatry and moral violation sub-corpus (extensive Ezekiel, Hosea, Jeremiah passages) shows sin as the act producing the defilement-state M10c owns.
- **M10b (Wickedness — abomination contexts):** Ezekiel idolatry passages and the reforming uses in 2Ki 23 sit at the M10b abomination-register boundary alongside M10c.
- **M11 (Repentance/Restoration — cleansing response):** Eze 37:23, 43:7 and others where the goal of restoration is the people no longer defiling themselves.

### H2931 ta.me — unclean (adjective, mti_id=920) — STAYS

**Rationale:** The 66-verse corpus covers two sub-registers, both expressing M10c's characteristic. (a) The categorical/classificatory sub-corpus (extensive Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy) defines unclean as a legal/ritual status arising from bodily condition, category membership, or contact. Even within this apparently external register, inner-being content is present: Lev 10:10 calls priests to cultivate active moral-spiritual discernment as the faculty distinguishing unclean from clean; Lev 11:47 frames this discernment as a covenant-life discipline; Lev 13:45 has the person publicly declaring their own unclean state — inner reality of contamination verbalized outward; Num 9:7 records men grieving their exclusion from Passover due to an involuntary unclean state, showing the inner distress the unclean condition produces. (b) The moral-existential sub-corpus (Job 14:4; Isa 6:5, 64:6; Lam 4:15; Eze 22:26, 44:23; Hag 2:14) names uncleanness as the constitutional human condition (Job 14:4), as the inner state a prophet recognises in himself when confronted by divine holiness (Isa 6:5), as total corporate inner defilement rendering even righteous deeds polluted (Isa 64:6), and as the inner moral state of a community that corrupts all its worship (Hag 2:14). All 66 verses evidence M10c.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M12 (Purity — discernment and structural opposite):** Lev 10:10, 11:47, Eze 22:26, 44:23 — the discernment of clean from unclean is a recurring theme; purity is the structural opposite throughout.
- **M10 (Sin — sin producing unclean state):** The moral-existential and idolatrous sub-corpus evidences sin-act generating unclean-state.
- **M07 (Shame — stigma and social exclusion):** Lev 13:45/46 — enforced isolation and public self-declaration of contamination; Lam 4:15 — exiles cried "Unclean! Away!" and totally expelled.

### H5079 nid.dah — impurity (mti_id=918) — STAYS

**Rationale:** The 24-verse corpus covers niddah's primary domain (menstrual/bodily impurity) and its extension to moral-relational and metaphorical uses, both expressing M10c's defilement-state characteristic. (a) The menstrual/bodily sub-corpus (Lev 12, 15; Num 19; 2Ch 29:5; Ezr 9:11): niddah defines a time-bound bodily impurity state that transmits through contact and requires a standing community-wide cleansing provision (Num 19:9 — the ashes of the sin-offering heifer as a standing remedy for persistent impurity). 2Ch 29:5 frames impurity as a tangible corrupting presence in the Holy Place requiring deliberate inner and outward consecration to remove. (b) The moral-relational/metaphorical sub-corpus (Lev 18:19, 20:21; Lam 1:17; Eze 7:19/20, 18:6, 22:10, 36:17; Zec 13:1): Lev 18:19 treats the niddah boundary as a moral marker whose violation is transgression, not mere ritual oversight; Eze 36:17 uses niddah as a metaphor for the people's total inner moral uncleanness before God; Zec 13:1 promises a fountain for cleansing that will remove both sin and niddah-impurity from the community. All 24 verses evidence M10c.

**Primary cross-registers:**
- **M12 (Purity — structural opposite):** Purity is the structural opposite throughout; the niddah state is the condition purity overcomes.
- **M07 (Shame — stigma and social shame):** Menstrual exclusion uses carry overtones of stigma and relational separation (Lam 1:17).
- **M11 (Repentance/Cleansing — cleansing response):** Zec 13:1 (fountain for cleansing); Num 19:9 (standing cleansing provision for impurity) — the response to niddah is active cleansing.

## Cross-term observations (for Phase 5)

**1. M12 as universal cross-register.** Every term carries at least one M12 (Purity) cross-register flag. Purity is the structural opposite of defilement by definition. Phase 5 should anticipate the purity-defilement axis as a dominant sub-group organising principle.

**2. Unclean spirits sub-corpus (G0169, ~21 verses) — distinctive sub-register.** The NT unclean spirit verses describe defilement by external spiritual agency — a person's inner and bodily life inhabited, controlled, and corrupted by an unclean force. This is a materially different mechanism from moral self-defilement or ritual bodily defilement. M27 cross-register is strong. Phase 5 may warrant a distinct sub-group: defilement by external spiritual agency.

**3. Three major registers across the corpus:**
- *Ritual/bodily* — foundational sub-register, predominantly Hebrew terms (H2930A, H2931, H5079); involves contact, category, and bodily emission
- *Moral-inner* — NT register (all Greek terms) + Hebrew metaphorical extension (Eze 36:17, Isa 64:6, Hag 2:14); defilement as inner-being state of moral corruption
- *Corporate/covenantal* — land, sanctuary, name defiled by corporate sin; predominantly prophetic Hebrew (Ezekiel, Jeremiah, Hosea)

These three registers are present across multiple terms and will likely drive sub-group formation in Phase 5.

**4. M10 / M10b boundary management.** Many H2930A and H2931 verses simultaneously evidence sin-act (M10 register) and defilement-state (M10c register). The cross-register flags on both terms document this. Phase 7 cluster-level findings will need careful handling at this boundary.
