## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Evil as constitutional inner nature is definitionally opposed to God's nature rather than derived from it. Rom 9:14 (vc=65872 — VCG-06) poses and immediately rejects the charge of unrighteousness (adikia) against God — divine sovereign choice is explicitly not constitutionally evil. The verse establishes that God's nature is the structural antithesis of constitutional inner evil. Rom 12:9 (vc=14417 — VCG-06) commands the community to "abhor what is evil" — this command reflects the divine moral character that stands against evil as constitutional nature. 1Cor 13:6 (vc=14341 — VCG-06) frames love (the divine character) as refusing to "rejoice at wrongdoing" — unrighteousness is what love's opposite produces. The evidence collectively reveals God as constitutionally righteous and the human constitutional evil as an inversion of that righteousness.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Scripture explicitly denies constitutional inner evil to God. Rom 9:14 (vc=65872 — VCG-06) raises and immediately rejects the charge: "Is there injustice on God's part? By no means!" The denial is forceful and categorical. Heb 8:12 (vc=14342 — VCG-06) contrasts God's mercy toward human iniquities ("I will remember their sins no more") with the human constitutional condition — God stands over against human constitutional evil as the one who covers it by grace, not as one who shares it. The evidence is entirely consistent: constitutional inner evil is attributed exclusively to humans (Mat 7:11: "you who are evil"; Luk 11:13: the same), never to God.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Scripture is not merely silent but actively declarative in opposing this characteristic to God (T0.1.2). The analytical significance: evil as constitutional inner nature belongs exclusively to the creaturely sphere as a post-creation failure-condition. Its absence from God is not accidental silence but the positive ground from which the characteristic's seriousness is assessed. That humans are "constitutionally evil" (Mat 7:11 — VCG-02) while God is not means the characteristic represents the precise site where creaturely nature has departed from its original orientation toward the divine. The silence about God's possession is the theological ground for why the characteristic requires transformation (Act 3:26 — VCG-05 — turning from wickedness through divine blessing).

The evidence's consistent framing is remedial — the characteristic is what must be removed, not what was designed to be there.

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence presents evil as constitutional inner nature as a condition of fallen humanity, not the original created design. Mat 7:11 (vc=14438 — VCG-02) states "you who are evil" as a present fact about humans while simultaneously showing them capable of giving good gifts — the tension implies a baseline condition that is not original design but fallen reality. Mat 7:17-18 (vc=14439, 14440 — VCG-02) frames the diseased tree as the state the tree is in, not its designed condition. Tit 3:3 (vc=14367 — VCG-05) describes the former inner life as passing "days in malice and envy" and then being transformed by divine grace — the "former" framing implies a condition that can be and was departed from. The evidence consistently positions the characteristic as the fallen condition from which grace rescues, not a designed element.

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?**

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal about its essential nature?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The characteristic is named "Evil as constitutional inner nature." Three components matter analytically. "Evil" (ponēros / kakia / adikia / ponēria) names a moral-quality designation — not a specific act but a characterisation of what the person is at their inner core. "Constitutional" signals that this is not a transient state or episodic failure but a structural condition of the inner person — what they are constituted as. "Inner nature" signals that the characteristic concerns the generative architecture of the person's moral being, not its surface expressions. The name as a whole communicates that the evidence addresses the person whose inner generative nature is structurally oriented toward evil — not merely who has done evil things but from whom evil things necessarily flow. This differs from CHAR-1 (wickedness as settled person-identity) by emphasising structural-generative condition over settled directional orientation.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Four primary Greek terms carry this characteristic. ponēros (G4190, 63V): the most comprehensive term — spanning evil as a quality of persons, deeds, thoughts, and cosmic powers; at root it names what is characteristically bad or corrupt in a moral sense. kakia (G2549, 11V): specifically names settled inner badness — malice, ill-will, wickedness as an entrenched inner residue; the term's usage in Eph 4:31 (VCG-05), Col 3:8 (VCG-05), 1Pe 2:1 (VCG-05) consistently addresses it as something deeply seated requiring deliberate stripping off. adikia (G0093, 8V): unrighteousness — the condition of being structurally not-right, especially in relation to moral and legal norms; Rom 1:18 (VCG-05) names it as actively suppressing truth. ponēria (G4189, 7V): wickedness, villainy — typically naming active malicious intent; Mar 7:22 (VCG-05) lists it as an inner vice emerging from the heart. faulos (G5337, 1V): a term for what is cheap, bad, or worthless — applied to deeds in 2Cor 5:10 (VCG-06). Collectively the terms map evil across quality (ponēros), residual badness (kakia), structural wrongness (adikia), active malice (ponēria), and evaluative worthlessness (faulos).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The name "constitutional inner nature" carries constitutional implications most prominently. Mat 7:11 (vc=14438 — VCG-02): "you who are evil" — the "are" is constitutive, not episodic. Mat 7:17-18 (vc=14439-14440 — VCG-02): "a diseased tree cannot bear good fruit" — the constitutional metaphor directly names the inner generative architecture. Mar 7:23 (vc=14422 — VCG-03): "all these evil things come from within" — the inside is the constitutional source. The name also carries relational implications: the constitutional evil produces relational harm (3Jo 10 — VCG-04 — domineering malice against community; 2Jo 11 — VCG-04 — communicated through association; 2Th 3:2 — VCG-06 — faithlessness grounding moral depravity). The directional implication is that the characteristic generates evil outward (from the treasury — Mat 12:35, Luk 6:45 — VCG-03) rather than being received from outside.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Evil as constitutional inner nature is primarily a condition — a structural state of the inner person — rather than primarily an act, a disposition, or a quality. The constitutional-condition classification is supported by the tree-nature metaphor (Mat 7:17-18 — VCG-02): a tree's nature determines its fruit; the condition precedes and generates the output. It also has quality aspects (ponēros as a moral-quality term — Mat 5:45 — VCG-04) and disposition aspects (kakia as settled inner badness — Eph 4:31 — VCG-05; Col 3:8 — VCG-05). But the defining evidence for "condition" is the constitutional-baseline framing: Mat 7:11 (VCG-02) — "you who are evil" as a fact about human nature, not a description of a specific volitional alignment. The condition is what makes the dispositions and acts possible; it is the underlying structural reality from which they emerge.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The 6-VCG structure reveals multiple constituent elements. The characteristic has: (a) the constitutional baseline — human inner nature as evil by default (VCG-02, 8V: Mat 7:11-18; Luk 11:13; Mat 12:39; Mat 16:4; Luk 11:29); (b) the heart-as-treasury — the inner generative organ from which evil flows (VCG-03, 8V: Mat 6:23; Mat 9:4; Mat 12:34-35; Mat 15:19; Luk 6:45; Mar 7:23; Jam 2:4); (c) settled deep character of persons (VCG-04, 17V); (d) the cosmic-evil/evil-one dimension (VCG-01, 13V — cross-register); (e) saturating inner vices — kakia, ponēria, adikia filling the person (VCG-05, 20V); (f) evil conscience, progressive deterioration, and the command to abhor evil (VCG-06, 24V). These are not separate characteristics but constituent structural registers within the one constitutional-condition characteristic.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — Evil as constitutional inner nature is the structural condition of the human inner person in which the generative architecture of the heart, mind, and soul is oriented toward the production of evil as its default output. The heart is a treasury from which evil is actively drawn (Mat 12:35 — VCG-03; Luk 6:45 — VCG-03), all evil things come from within (Mar 7:23 — VCG-03), and the inner nature is diseased in a way that makes good fruit constitutionally impossible without transformation (Mat 7:17-18 — VCG-02). The condition saturates the person at multiple levels — the soul is filled with vice clusters (Rom 1:29 — VCG-05), the conscience is defiled (Heb 10:22 — VCG-06), and the mind is hostile (Col 1:21 — VCG-06). The characteristic is NT-distinctive: the Hebrew OT (Characteristic 1) identified the wicked person by their inner orientation; the NT (Characteristic 2) identifies the constitutional evil as the baseline condition of human nature from which that orientation flows.

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-2]** E — The evidence maps four distinct modes of inner operation. (1) As a treasury — the inner person actively draws from an inner store of evil (Mat 12:35 — VCG-03: "out of his evil treasure brings forth evil"; Luk 6:45 — VCG-03: "out of his evil treasure produces evil").
