## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The characteristic reveals God's nature primarily through the divine response to iniquity as an active inner process: God perceives, hates, and judges it. Psa 5:5 (vc=38237 — VCG-03): "you hate all evildoers" — God's active moral hatred is directed at those whose inner character is constituted by the working of iniquity; the hating is not passive disapproval but a settled divine stance toward those who persistently generate evil from within. Job 11:11 (vc=38206 — VCG-04): "For he knows worthless men; when he sees iniquity, will he not consider it?" — God perceives and registers iniquity even when hidden from others, revealing his character as omniscient moral witness. Psa 94:23 (vc=38249 — VCG-03): "He will bring back on them their iniquity and wipe them out for their wickedness" — God's character includes active retributive justice that returns the inner scheming of iniquity upon those who practise it. The evidence reveals God as the moral pole against which active inner scheming is measured and judged.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that reveal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Scripture explicitly denies iniquity to God. Isa 55:7 (vc=38200 — VCG-02): the unrighteous man is called to forsake "his thoughts" and "return to the Lord" — the Lord is the party toward whom the iniquity-shaped person must turn; God is the structural opposite of the iniquity-scheming inner life. Psa 66:18 (vc=38182 — VCG-04): "If I had cherished iniquity in my heart, the Lord would not have listened" — the conditional structure implies that God, as the one who listens, is characterised by the absence of cherished iniquity. Psa 5:5 (vc=38237 — VCG-03): God hates evildoers — the hating posture is the divine stance against what God does not possess. The non-attribution reveals that iniquity as active inner scheming is exclusively a creaturely failure — specifically, the misuse of the mind, heart, and will faculties that God designed for generation of good.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Scripture is actively declarative (T0.1.2) rather than merely silent. The silence about God scheming iniquity reveals the characteristic as the image-bearer's misuse of the generative inner capacities (mind devising, heart conceiving, will executing) that are meant to produce what is good. Isa 32:6 (vc=38181 — VCG-02): "his heart is busy with iniquity" — the heart's busyness with iniquity names the corruption of a generative capacity; the divine image designed human persons with generative inner faculties precisely so that good could be conceived and enacted from within. The silence about God plotting trouble on beds or conceiving mischief (Psa 7:14 — vc=38245 — VCG-02; Psa 36:4 — vc=38235 — VCG-02) names the characteristic as the inversion of what those faculties were for.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — The characteristic is named "Iniquity as active inner scheming and evil generation." Three components carry analytical weight. "Iniquity" names the moral category — wrongdoing conceived and produced from within. "Active inner scheming" specifies the generative mode: this is not passive moral failure but deliberate, premeditated inner activity. "Evil generation" names the output: the inner scheming produces evil as its child. The name signals that the characteristic is constitutively generative: Psa 7:14 (vc=38245 — VCG-02): "conceives evil… pregnant with mischief… gives birth to lies" — the birth metaphor is embedded in the characteristic's definitional core. Mic 2:1 (vc=38217 — VCG-02): "those who devise wickedness and work evil on their beds! When the morning dawns, they perform it" — the devising (inner) and performing (outer) sequence names both dimensions the name covers. The name orients toward the inner generative process, not merely the outer wicked act.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-5]** E — Two primary Hebrew terms carry this characteristic. a.ven (H0205G, 66V) — its root denotes emptiness, nothingness, and trouble; at the definitional level it names iniquity as a morally empty-but-troublesome quality — wrongdoing that is both hollow (without genuine moral substance) and actively harmful. The term covers the scheming agent's inner state and the trouble their scheming generates. ro.a (H7455, 13V evil-deeds sub-corpus) — from the root r-'-' (to be bad, evil, harmful); names the badness-quality of deeds. At the definitional level, ro.a names evil as a quality of deeds that flows from inner moral corruption. Deu 28:20 (vc=38439 — VCG-05): "the evil of your deeds" — ro.a names the moral quality attached to the acts. No NT Greek terms appear in this characteristic's corpus — it is exclusively OT Hebrew, which T7.1.8 will address. Definitionally: a.ven names the emptiness-that-troubles; ro.a names the badness-quality of the resulting deeds.
