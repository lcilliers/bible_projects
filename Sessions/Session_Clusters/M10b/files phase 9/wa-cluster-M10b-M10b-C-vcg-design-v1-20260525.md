### M10b-C-VCG-01 — Ethical and character-orientation abomination

**Context description:** This VCG holds the verses in which abomination attaches to dishonest commercial practice, unjust social dealings, and the character-orientation of a life whose entire directional trajectory is loathsome to God. The inner-being content is the will that deliberately deceives in daily transactions (false weights, unjust judgment), the character whose habitual way of life provokes divine loathing, and the person whose inner disposition is intrinsically repugnant to the community of the righteous. The unifying axis is the person-in-community whose moral character makes their everyday conduct abominable — not isolated acts of sacrilege but the sustained pattern of life that defines an inner orientation. Anchor: Pro 11:20 (1677) — most direct location of abomination explicitly in the heart; "those of crooked heart are an abomination to God" names the inner-state-as-abomination with exact clarity.

### M10b-C-VCG-03 — Corrupt worship: the unclean inner person's religious act

**Context description:** This VCG holds the verses where the abomination is the religious act — sacrifice, incense, prayer — performed by a person whose inner being is corrupt. The worship itself is rendered offensive to God because the inner moral condition of the worshipper contradicts the act. Pro 15:8 names the wicked person's sacrifice as repugnant; Pro 21:27 adds that evil intent deepens the abomination (God sees the heart behind the act); Pro 28:9 shows that deliberate rejection of divine instruction corrupts even the act of prayer; Isa 1:13 reveals that inner iniquity harboured while performing incense-offering turns the worship into an abomination. The inner-being content is the integrity-gap between the outer religious act and the inner moral condition — corrupt worship as abomination. Anchor: Isa 1:13 (1695) — incense offered alongside inner iniquity = abomination; the most direct statement of the inner-moral-condition-as-determinative-for-worship principle.

**Members (4V):** 1678 (Pro 15:8), 1693 (Pro 21:27), 65958 (Pro 28:9), 1695 (Isa 1:13)

**Anchor:** 1695 (Isa 1:13)

### M10b-C-VCG-04 — The silenced and awakening conscience before abomination

**Context description:** This VCG holds the verses where the key inner-being content is the conscience's relationship to abomination — whether it is wholly silenced (Jer 6:15, 8:12: no shame, no blushing), actively responsive (Eze 9:4: sighing and groaning over corporate abomination), called to redirect the will (Eze 14:6: turning the face away), or reawakened in self-loathing through restored memory (Eze 36:31). The Ezekiel 44 cluster (verses 6, 7, 13) extends this to the rebellious inner disposition saturated with abomination and the uncircumcised heart as the root defilement. Deu 27:15 names the heart's knowledge of wrong even while the will proceeds with the secret idol. Ezr 9:11 addresses communal moral contamination as a spreading defilement. Mal 2:11 names faithless mixed devotion as covenant-betrayal — the conscience violated through misplaced inner allegiance. The unifying thread is the faculty of moral self-awareness (conscience, memory, inner recognition) in its various states in relation to abomination. Anchor: Jer 6:15 (1680) — conscience wholly silenced, no inner check on detestable behaviour; the most direct evidence of the conscience-in-abomination mechanism.

**Members (11V):** 65937 (Deu 27:15), 66005 (Ezr 9:11), 1680 (Jer 6:15), 1696 (Jer 8:12), 1698 (Eze 9:4), 65984 (Eze 14:6), 1681 (Eze 36:31), 66001 (Eze 44:6), 66002 (Eze 44:7), 66003 (Eze 44:13), 66004 (Mal 2:11)

**Anchor:** 1680 (Jer 6:15)

### M10b-C-VCG-05 — Identity and choice constituted by abomination

**Context description:** This VCG holds the verses where abomination is either a category defined by divine declaration (Pro 6:16: the seven-things framework), the expression of an inverted moral appetite (Pro 13:19: fools repugnant to turn from evil — the will so bonded to evil that departing feels abhorrent), the consequence of a particular act of will or choice (Isa 41:24: choosing worthless idol makes the chooser abominable; Isa 44:19: absent discernment enabling defilement), or an identity constituted entirely by generating and propagating moral corruption (Rev 17:5: source and mother of all abominations; Rev 21:27: abominable inner dispositions incompatible with divine presence). These are the definitional and identity-level abomination verses — where abomination names a category, a will-state, or a total identity rather than a specific act or inner faculty. Anchor: Rev 17:5 (65878) — identity constituted entirely by the generation and propagation of moral corruption; the most concentrated statement of abomination-as-identity.

**Members (6V):** 65952 (Pro 6:16), 1687 (Pro 13:19), 65960 (Isa 41:24), 65961 (Isa 44:19), 65878 (Rev 17:5), 22 (Rev 21:27)
