## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Wickedness as settled person-identity is definitionally structured against the divine nature rather than derived from it. Job 34:10 states explicitly that it is "far be it from God that he should do wickedness, and from the Almighty that he should do wrong" (re.sha) — God's nature is declared the absolute structural opposite of what wickedness names. This structural opposition is the primary theological datum: wickedness does not reflect or image any divine attribute; it reveals the divine character by negation and contrast. Psa 11:5 reinforces this — "his soul hates the wicked" — establishing that wickedness is specifically the object of divine loathing, not a divine attribute. What the evidence reveals about God through this characteristic is therefore apophatic: God's righteousness, justice, and wholeness are defined in part by their absolute incompatibility with the inner orientation that wickedness describes. The wicked person is the anti-image of what God is, and the universe of 190 verses sustains this polarity throughout.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Scripture explicitly and forcefully denies this characteristic to God. Job 34:10 declares God incapable of wickedness (re.sha); Psa 36:1 portrays transgression as speaking within the wicked person "deep in his heart" while "there is no fear of God before his eyes" — structuring the wicked person's inner life as the space where God is absent. The absence of attribution to God is analytically significant: it means wickedness as settled person-identity is a purely creaturely failure-mode, with no divine analogue. Its significance in the human person is therefore as a measure of departure from the divine pattern — the further the inner person is from God (Psa 36:1; Mal 3:18 defines the wicked as "one who does not serve him"), the more completely this characteristic defines them.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Scripture is not silent but actively declarative on this point (Job 34:10). Nevertheless, the analytical question about the divine image remains important: because wickedness-as-settled-person-identity is constitutionally absent from God, it cannot be an aspect of the divine image that the human is designed to reflect. The verse evidence suggests that the human capacity for this characteristic represents a corruption or inversion of the image, not an expression of it. Psa 9:17 defines the wicked by their forgetting of God; Gen 18:23–25 presupposes that wickedness and righteousness are distinct moral statuses that map onto just and unjust outcomes respectively — the whole structure presupposes a divine moral order the wicked person violates. Wickedness-as-settled-identity is therefore the image-bearer in a state of image-corruption or image-inversion.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The evidence does not address what purpose wickedness as settled person-identity serves in the originally created human. This is analytically appropriate: the characteristic as defined is a settled inner orientation of moral corruption, which the evidence consistently presents as a failure-mode of creation, not a designed capacity. No verse in the corpus suggests that wickedness was part of original design or serves a constructive anthropological function. The evidence is silent about creative purpose precisely because wickedness-as-settled-identity is a description of the person after their inner orientation has turned against the created order. The purpose-question is not posed by the evidence; the evidence's universal testimony is that wickedness leads to instability, destruction, and divine judgment — not to any designed end.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence consistently positions wickedness-as-settled-person-identity as a condition of the fallen human, not the original created design. The proverb "out of the wicked comes wickedness" (1Sa 24:13 — vc=42648) treats wickedness as an inner source from which corresponding actions inevitably flow, but without attributing this orientation to original design. Eze 18:20–27 (M10b-A-VCG-07) addresses wickedness as a volitional orientation the will has taken and can reverse — its reversibility implies it is not constitutionally fixed by creation. The question of how wickedness entered the inner person is not directly addressed in this corpus (that belongs to M10/sin-act clusters); what is evidenced is the condition in its settled form. The corpus addresses the wicked person as an existing type, not as an explanation of human origins.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness — something the person is moving toward, not only what they currently are?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence orients wickedness-as-settled-person-identity toward a future state, but that future state is one of judgment and termination rather than fullness. The Ezekiel corpus (M10b-A-VCG-07) repeatedly depicts wickedness as a "way" heading toward death (Eze 3:18; 33:8; 33:11). Psa 37:10 states "in just a little while, the wicked will be no more." Job 20:5 notes "the exulting of the wicked is short." Dan 12:10 describes the wicked acting wickedly and being unable to understand, while the wise are refined — pointing to an eschatological bifurcation. The trajectory of wickedness-as-settled-identity is therefore toward exhaustion and dissolution, not forward fullness. The one counter-note is the Ezekiel repentance sequence: Eze 18:21–27 and 33:12–19 show that the wicked person can turn from this trajectory, which opens a different future — but that future belongs to the characteristic's reversal, not its completion.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Wickedness-as-settled-person-identity does not express the divine image; it suppresses and corrupts it. The evidence from M10b-A-VCG-02 (practical atheism) is definitional: Psa 10:4 describes the wicked person whose "every thought is 'There is no God'" — the most direct inversion of the image-bearing faculty by which the human is constitutionally oriented toward God. Psa 36:1 shows transgression speaking within the wicked person as an internal voice displacing the fear of God. Mal 3:18 defines the wicked as those who "do not serve him." The divine image in the human is corrupted precisely where the wicked person suppresses the God-directed orientation of their inner life. What the image-bearer was designed to be — morally directed toward God, capable of justice and righteousness — is inverted in the wicked person. The evidence does not suggest any positive expression of the divine image through wickedness; the characteristic is the image in suppression.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence is unambiguous: wickedness-as-settled-person-identity is exclusively a creaturely failure-state with no divine analogue and no creaturely-analogue relationship to God. Job 34:10 forecloses any shared possession. The closest the evidence comes to a divine parallel is in God's response to wickedness — his loathing of the wicked (Psa 11:5), his refusal to acquit them (Exo 23:7), his judgment that terminates their way (Psa 9:5). These divine responses show God acting as the moral antithesis to wickedness, not as its mirror. The structural opposition between God's righteousness and the wicked person's inner orientation (Gen 18:23–25; Mal 3:18) confirms that this characteristic belongs exclusively to the creaturely sphere as a form of failure.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The presence of wickedness-as-settled-person-identity reveals the divine image in a state of advanced corruption. The evidence from multiple VCGs maps this corruption: the wicked person excludes God from their mind (Psa 10:4 — VCG-02 — practical atheism), the transgression-voice displaces the God-voice in their heart (Psa 36:1 — VCG-02), their soul actively desires evil (Pro 21:10 — VCG-09 — "the soul of the wicked desires evil"), their moral compass is inverted (Pro 10:32 — VCG-08 — "the mouth of the wicked knows what is perverse"), and even their acts of mercy are cruel (Pro 12:10 — VCG-09). The image-bearing faculties — soul, heart, mind, will, moral perception — are all present but all maloriented. The absence of this characteristic (the righteous person) correspondingly reveals the divine image more fully functional — the Psa 1 contrast (VCG-04) structures the whole pattern. The presence of wickedness therefore maps the degree of image-corruption with granular precision.

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence contains limited but present typological use. The most analytically significant instance is Dan 12:10's eschatological frame (M10b-A-VCG-05): "the wicked shall act wickedly and none of the wicked shall understand" — here the wicked person is a type of those who will harden in the final purification rather than being refined. Isa 53:9 (M10b-A-VCG-04) is typologically important in the opposite direction: the servant is buried "among the wicked" despite having no wickedness in him — the wicked person's category functions typologically here as a contrast-type that reveals the servant's inner purity by negation. The Ezekiel repentance-corpus (VCG-07) does not function typologically in the strict sense but does prefigure the prophetic call to inner turning that the NT develops. The wicked-as-type is primarily eschatological (those destined for judgment) and contrast-typological (the negative against which the righteous, the servant, and the Messiah are defined).

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The typological direction is primarily from the human instance outward to the eschatological reality: the wicked person as encountered in the present evidence base is a type of those whose hardened inner orientation will persist into and be finalised at eschatological judgment (Dan 12:10; Isa 11:4 — "he shall kill the wicked"). The contrast-typological use in Isa 53:9 runs in the reverse direction — the divine servant establishes the pattern of inner purity against which the wicked serve as contrast. No typology runs from a divine instance to the human wicked person: since God is incapable of wickedness (Job 34:10), there is no divine pattern for wickedness that the human instantiates.

**[CHAR-1]** E — Typological use is evidenced (T0.4.1 above) and its direction described (T0.4.2). This prompt is satisfied by the findings at T0.4.1–T0.4.2.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The characteristic is named "Wickedness as settled person-identity." The name has three components, each analytically significant. "Wickedness" signals a moral-character category — not a discrete act but a quality of the inner person. "Settled" signals a stable, consolidated state rather than a transient condition or a series of episodes — it has become definitional for the person who possesses it. "Person-identity" signals that the characteristic names a type of inner-being person rather than an experience that passes through the person. The name as a whole communicates that the evidence concerns the human being whose moral character has been organised around wickedness as its centre of gravity — it is who the person is, not merely what they have done. This contrasts with M10 (sin-act, the doing) and M10c (defilement, the contagion effect) to which M10b-A is a sibling.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The primary Hebrew term ra.sha (H7563, 179 of 190 verses) is an adjective/noun designating the wicked person as a type — one constitutionally oriented toward evil. Its root covers both the moral-character meaning (the wicked person) and the forensic-legal meaning (the guilty, legally condemnable party — M10b-A-VCG-01's 5V carry this polysemy with M10 cross-register flag). re.sha (H7562, 9V) is the abstract noun counterpart: wickedness as a condition, state, and coercive force. mir.sha.at (H4849, 1V) names the collective — the wicked woman as type. The definitional revelation at this level is that Hebrew constructs an entire person-type vocabulary: the wicked person (ra.sha), wickedness as their condition (re.sha), and the wicked collective (mir.sha.at). This vocabulary suite is unusually complete, signalling that Scripture treats wickedness-as-person-identity as a coherent, classifiable inner-being category requiring multiple lexical tools to name adequately.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The name carries all three implications. Directional: the Ezekiel corpus (M10b-A-VCG-07, 22V) uses ra.sha consistently with "way" (Eze 3:18; 33:8; 33:11) — wickedness is a direction the will has taken, a trajectory the inner person is on. Isa 55:7 reinforces: "let the wicked forsake his way." Relational: the evidence locates wickedness relationally against the righteous (Gen 18:23; Psa 1:1; Mal 3:18), against God (Psa 10:4; 9:17 — forgetting God), and against the vulnerable (Psa 10:2; 37:14). Constitutional: Pro 21:10 ("the soul of the wicked desires evil") and the VCG-02 practical-atheism cluster show the characteristic lodged at the constitutional level of the inner person — soul, heart, and the deepest thought-life (Psa 10:4: "all his thoughts are 'There is no God'"). The name therefore is accurate to the evidence in all three registers.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence establishes wickedness-as-settled-person-identity as primarily a disposition — a stable, habitual inner orientation of the whole person toward evil, away from God, and toward harm to others. It is not primarily an act (that register belongs to M10 sin-act); it is not primarily a condition imposed from outside; it is the inner person's characteristic directional bent. Evidence: Psa 36:1 (transgression speaking as an inner voice, displacing God-fear — the disposition as an interior governance structure), Pro 4:19 (the way of the wicked characterised by moral blindness — a perceptual consequence of the disposition), 1Sa 24:13 ("out of the wicked comes wickedness" — the disposition as generative source). The characteristic also has qualities of a condition (it is stable and self-perpetuating) and a quality (it marks the person morally). But "disposition" is the primary classification: it is something the person habitually is in their inner orientation.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The VCG structure reveals multiple constituent elements within the characteristic. At minimum, the evidence maps: (a) practical atheism — the God-exclusion mechanism (VCG-02, 8V: Psa 10:4, 9:17, 36:1, etc.); (b) pride as the inner engine of directed hostility (VCG-03, 22V: Psa 10:2, 37:12-14, Isa 13:11); (c) moral hollowness — the soul oriented toward evil and the desires corrupted (VCG-09, 33V: Pro 21:10, 10:20, 12:10); (d) inner instability — the self-undermining momentum of wickedness (VCG-06, 21V: Pro 5:22, 10:24-30); (e) the directional-orientation — wickedness as a "way" the will can turn from (VCG-07, 22V: Eze 3:18, 33:11). These are not separate characteristics but constituent structural elements of the one characteristic. The characteristic is composite in this analytical sense — it has identifiable sub-structures within the settled person-identity.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Based on the 190-verse corpus, the working description is: Wickedness-as-settled-person-identity is a stable inner-being disposition in which the human person's constitutive faculties — will, soul, heart, moral perception — are organised around an orientation away from God and toward evil, injustice, and harm. The disposition is habitual and identity-defining: it generates the person's characteristic conduct, determines their relationship to God (non-service, Mal 3:18; forgetting, Psa 9:17; relational estrangement, Pro 15:29), shapes their relationships with others (exploitation, concealment, predation — VCGs 03, 08, 09), and carries within itself an inherent instability that moves toward dissolution and judgment. It is a constitutional orientation of the whole person, not a momentary state, distinguishable from the righteous person at the deepest inner level (Psa 7:9: "you who test the minds and hearts").

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The structural opposite is righteousness (tsaddiq / righteous person), which appears as the constant explicit contrast throughout the corpus. Gen 18:23–25 (VCG-04) is foundational: wickedness and righteousness are distinct inner conditions requiring distinct judgments. The contrast is sustained through the entire Psalms and Proverbs corpus (VCG-04, 18V of moral contrast; VCG-09, 33V of wickedness against divine justice). Psa 1:6 names the opposition structurally: "the Lord knows the way of the righteous, but the way of the wicked will perish." Isa 53:9 (VCG-04) provides the most concentrated single contrast: the servant buried among the wicked has "no violence in will, no deceit in speech" — righteousness defined against the wicked person's constitutive characteristics. M26 (Righteousness) is the primary cluster-scope structural opposite; the M10b-A evidence base is saturated with this relationship.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence shows wickedness-as-settled-person-identity explicitly excluding and resisting: (1) God — the God-exclusion mechanism is definitional (Psa 10:4; 9:17; 10:13 "renounce God from within their heart"; Mal 3:18 non-service; Pro 15:29 "the Lord is far from the wicked"); (2) moral correction — Pro 9:7 ("whoever corrects a scoffer gets himself abuse") and Isa 26:10 ("if favor is shown to the wicked, he does not learn righteousness") show resistance to correction as structural; (3) justice — the wicked pervert justice (Isa 5:23; Pro 17:23; Hab 1:4) and the Ezekiel corpus shows wickedness explicitly resisting the turn toward what is just (Eze 33:9: "he does not turn from his way"); (4) the righteous — active hostility toward the righteous is a structural feature (Psa 37:12-14; 11:2; 34:21 — VCG-03).

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The forensic-verdict VCG (M10b-A-VCG-01, 5V — Exo 23:7, Deu 25:1, 25:2, 1Ki 8:32, 2Ch 6:23) marks the boundary between wickedness-as-settled-person-identity and the legal-status use of ra.sha. In these verses, "wicked" is a judicial label applied to the guilty party in a legal dispute — it describes a socially adjudicated status rather than an inner moral orientation. This is a cross-register boundary with M10 (sin-act / forensic). Beyond this, the characteristic ends where the falling-away of a righteous person begins (Eze 18:24: "when a righteous person turns to wicked ways" — this is the moment of transition, not yet the settled condition). The characteristic is also distinct from isolated sinful acts (M10): the corpus's primary terms (ra.sha as person-type) consistently describe the person-type rather than the act-instance.

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence maps at least four distinct modes of inner operation. (1) As a voice — Psa 36:1: "transgression speaks to the wicked deep in his heart" — wickedness operates as an internal governance voice that displaces the divine fear. (2) As a treasury — the wicked person's soul and heart function as a store of evil from which conduct flows (Pro 21:10 "the soul of the wicked desires evil"; implied in VCG-08 speech-revealing cluster — Pro 12:6, 10:6). (3) As a way — wickedness operates as a directional orientation of the will (Eze 3:18; 33:11; Isa 55:7; Pro 4:14) — the inner person is on a trajectory. (4) As a self-generating bondage — Pro 5:22 "the iniquities of the wicked ensnare him, and he is held fast in the cords of his sin" — wickedness feeds itself through the acts it generates, creating a closed inner feedback loop. These four modes are distinct but co-present in the settled wicked person.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence shows variation by context and level. At the individual constitutional level, wickedness operates as the internal voice displacing God (Psa 36:1 — VCG-02) and the soul's desire-orientation (Pro 21:10 — VCG-09). At the social/relational level, it operates as directed hostility (VCG-03), predatory exploitation (Jer 5:26; Job 24:6), and the perversion of speech (VCG-08). At the institutional level, it operates as structural corruption of justice (Isa 5:23; Hab 1:4; Pro 17:23 — VCG-09). Eze 13:22 (VCG-07) shows it operating through prophetic enablement — false prophets encourage the wicked not to turn. The characteristic also operates differently in the phase of judgment versus the phase of apparent prosperity (Job 21:7 — VCG-10 — "why do the wicked live and grow mighty?" poses the paradox that under apparent prosperity the characteristic continues but its consequences are deferred).

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes. M10b-A-VCG-08 (12V) specifically evidences the speech-based mode. The wicked person's inner orientation expresses itself through the instrument of speech in multiple registers: concealment of violent intent (Pro 10:6, 10:11 — "the mouth of the wicked conceals violence"), lethal ambush (Pro 12:6 — "the words of the wicked lie in wait for blood"), active consumption of iniquity through speech (Pro 19:28), unreflective pouring out of evil (Pro 15:28), perverse speech as the signature of an inverted moral compass (Pro 10:32), and the social destruction of whole communities through speech (Pro 11:11 — "by the mouth of the wicked [a city] is overthrown"). Beyond VCG-08, Psa 36:1's internal voice of transgression is a speech-mode of the wickedness operating within the person before it expresses outward.

**T1.5.1 — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence addresses the response to encountering the wicked person from the outside rather than the inner experience of adopting the characteristic from within. From the outside: the evidence shows immediate moral recoil and avoidance as the appropriate response. Psa 26:5 (VCG-03): "I hate the assembly of evildoers, and I will not sit with the wicked" — deliberate moral separation as the will's first response. Psa 1:1 (VCG-04): the blessed person "walks not in the counsel of the wicked" — avoidance of the wicked person's counsel-register as the first step. Gen 18:25 (VCG-04) and Exo 23:7 (VCG-01) show the first response of the just judge: separation between wicked and righteous (the judicial impulse to name and distinguish). The response from the wicked person's own inner life to correction is the opposite: Pro 9:7 shows the immediate response is injury and abuse — wickedness resists its own naming.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The immediate response varies significantly by direction. For those outside the characteristic who encounter the wicked person: the evidence shows avoidance, separation, and appeal to divine judgment (Psa 3:7; 17:13; 26:5; 31:17 — VCGs 03, 05). For the wicked person themselves when confronted: the immediate response is aggression, defensive hardening, and rejection of correction (Pro 9:7; Isa 26:10 — VCG-09 and VCG-05). The variation is therefore consistent in pattern: the wicked person's characteristic inner response to any encounter that threatens their orientation is resistance and hardening. The evidence from Eze 33:9 generalises this: "if you warn the wicked to turn from his way, and he does not turn from his way, that person shall die in his iniquity" — the prototypical immediate response is non-turning.

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence is not silent; it is addressed above (T1.5.1–T1.5.2). The evidence does not address the inner phenomenology of initially adopting wickedness (the transition moment) — that is silent. What the evidence richly documents is the response of the established wicked person when encountered, corrected, or threatened.

**T1.6.1 — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The sustained operation of wickedness-as-settled-person-identity produces multiple inner consequences documented across VCG-06 (instability) and VCG-09 (moral hollowness). Over time: (1) inner instability intensifies — Pro 10:24 ("what the wicked dreads will come upon him"), Pro 10:25 (no lasting foundation), Pro 10:28 (expectation of the wicked perishing); (2) moral perception degrades — Pro 4:19 ("the way of the wicked is like deep darkness; they do not know over what they stumble") — wickedness progressively blinds the inner faculty of moral sight; (3) the soul's unsatisfied craving deepens — Pro 13:25 ("the belly of the wicked suffers want") — sustained wickedness produces chronic inner want; (4) relational distance from God becomes structural and irreversible without repentance — Pro 15:29 ("the Lord is far from the wicked"); (5) the inner bondage tightens — Pro 5:22 ("ensnared by his own iniquities, held fast in the cords of his sin") — wickedness generates the constraints that deepen it.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to this characteristic establish?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Sustained possession of wickedness-as-settled-person-identity establishes: (a) moral blindness as a perceptual state (Pro 4:19; Isa 26:10 — even grace cannot penetrate; Dan 12:10 — the wicked "cannot understand"); (b) spiritual restlessness as a persistent condition (Isa 57:20–21 — "like the tossing sea… there is no peace for the wicked"); (c) hardened defiance as an inner posture (Eze 3:19 — refusing to turn even when warned; Isa 26:10 — incorrigibility under favour); (d) an appetite for evil as a soul-level orientation (Pro 21:10 — "the soul of the wicked desires evil"); (e) a corrupted deliberative faculty (Job 21:16 — "the counsel of the wicked is far from me" — wickedness generates a morally alien mode of reasoning). These are stable states, not transient reactions.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes. The immediate response (T1.5.1–T1.5.2) is characterised by resistance, aggression, and rejection of correction. The sustained effect is deeper: it is constitutional degradation — the progressive reduction of the person's capacity for moral perception (Pro 4:19; Dan 12:10), the establishment of chronic inner want (Pro 13:25), and the intensification of inner bondage (Pro 5:22). The immediate response is behavioural; the sustained effect is constitutional. The difference is between the wicked person hitting back when confronted (immediate) and the wicked person becoming progressively less capable of moral sight and inner peace over the course of their life (sustained). Job 15:20 ("the wicked man writhes in pain all his days") is the sustained-effect statement.

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence is not framed around "receiving" wickedness as though it is something bestowed. The question is better addressed as: what inner conditions enable the settling of this characteristic in a person? The evidence suggests: (a) the pre-existing exclusion of God from the mind (Psa 10:4 — the wicked's practical atheism pre-exists and enables the settled orientation); (b) the abandonment of counsel from the righteous (Psa 1:1 — alignment with wicked counsel); (c) the decision to enter "the path of the wicked" (Pro 4:14 — VCG-07 — wickedness is entered as a path); (d) the silencing of the internal voice of God-fear (Psa 36:1 — the replacement of God-fear with transgression's voice is the enabling inner event). The characteristic settles where the God-orientation of the inner person has been abandoned.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence from VCG-04 (moral contrast) and VCG-07 (directional orientation) shows that the conditions blocking wickedness-as-settled-person-identity are: (a) active fear of God (its absence is definitional for the wicked — Psa 36:1; its presence in Psa 1:1 is the blessed person's condition); (b) the counsel of the righteous (Psa 1:1 — the blessed person avoids the wicked's counsel); (c) responsiveness to the prophetic call to turn (Eze 3:18–19; 33:8–19 — the wicked who turns is specifically the one who has not settled into the characteristic). The primary blocking condition is the sustained orientation of the inner person toward God — sustained non-service (Mal 3:18 defines the wicked by their non-service, so service is the blocking condition).

---
