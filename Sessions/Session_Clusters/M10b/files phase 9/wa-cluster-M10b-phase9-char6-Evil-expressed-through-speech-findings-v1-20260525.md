## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The characteristic reveals God's nature primarily through what the characteristic attacks: God's name, his dwelling, and his glory — the very realities that blasphemy targets. Rev 13:6 (vc=14356 — VCG-01): the beast "opened its mouth to utter blasphemies against God, blaspheming his name and his dwelling, that is, those who dwell in heaven" — God's name and dwelling are the specific objects of blasphemous contempt, revealing God as a named, dwelling, glory-bearing being whose character and presence are the pole against which defiant speech defines itself. Rev 16:9 (vc=14352 — VCG-01): "they cursed the name of God who had power over these plagues. They did not repent and give him glory" — the withheld glory reveals that God's character warrants and invites glory; blasphemy is precisely the refusal to render what is due. Rom 2:24 (vc=14357 — VCG-02): God's name is "blasphemed among the Gentiles" — his name exists to be honoured and known, not dishonoured. The characteristic reveals God as the morally serious, glory-bearing, name-holding personal being whose honour is the target of evil expressed through speech.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that reveal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Scripture does not attribute blasphemy or defamatory evil speech to God. God is consistently the object of blasphemy (Rev 13:6; 16:9; 16:11; 16:21 — VCG-01) and the one whose name is blasphemed by others' conduct (Rom 2:24 — VCG-02), never its subject. The non-attribution is emphatic: the entire VCG-01 corpus (6V) positions God as the one against whom defiant blasphemy is directed. This active non-attribution reveals that the characteristic is the polar inversion of the speech that God's nature calls forth — which is worship, glory, and honest testimony. Attributing evil speech to God would be a logical impossibility in the evidence's framework: God cannot blaspheme himself. The silence reveals the characteristic as exclusively creaturely, specifically as the misuse of the creaturely speech-faculty that was designed for truthful testimony about and toward God.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Scripture is not merely silent but actively declarative (T0.1.2): God is the target of blasphemy, not its agent. The silence about God speaking evil through speech reveals the characteristic as the inversion of the image-bearing speech-capacity. The human person as image-bearer was equipped with speech specifically to name, testify, and render glory to God and to represent reality truthfully. Evil expressed through speech — blasphemy, slander, false accusation — inverts every dimension of that design. Rom 14:16 (vc=14359 — VCG-02): "do not let what you regard as good be spoken of as evil" — the possibility of good being spoken of as evil presupposes that speech was designed to name good as good; the inversion is the failure of the image-bearing speech-function.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The characteristic is named "Evil expressed through speech." Three components signal its essential nature. "Evil" names the inner moral source — wickedness that originates from within the person. "Expressed through" signals the medium: speech is the instrument, not the source; the evil precedes its expression and uses speech as its vehicle. "Speech" specifies the mode of operation — the characteristic is constitutively verbal. The name signals that the characteristic is an expression-phenomenon: it requires the inner evil to exist before speech can express it, and requires speech as the specific channel through which inner wickedness becomes socially operative. This distinguishes CHAR-6 from characteristics where speech is incidental; here speech is constitutive. Rev 13:6 (vc=14356 — VCG-01): the beast "opened its mouth to utter blasphemies" — the opening of the mouth is the act through which inner defiant orientation becomes operational blasphemy.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — No primary Hebrew terms appear in the 17-verse corpus (exclusively NT Greek). Three Greek terms carry the characteristic. blasfēmeō (G0987, 11V) — the primary term: from blapto (to harm, injure) + phēmē (speech, reputation). At the definitional level, blasfēmeō names harmful speech — speech that injures a person's or God's reputation by misrepresentation. The harm is carried in the speech act itself. Rev 13:6 (VCG-01): blasphemies against God's name — the injury is to the divine name's honour. kakopoios (G2555, 5V) — from kakos (evil, bad) + poieo (to do, make): "evil-doer" or "one who does evil." As used in VCG-03, it names the identity attributed through hostile accusation — the charge that someone is an evil-doer. atopos (G0824, 1V — Act 25:5 — VCG-03) — from a (not) + topos (place): literally "out of place," hence irregular, improper, wrong. In legal context it names verifiable wrongdoing. These three terms span: harmful speech directed at God/persons (blasfēmeō), the evil-doer as charged identity (kakopoios), and legally defined wrongdoing (atopos).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — All three implications are present. Directional: "expressed through speech" names an outward direction — from inner evil through the speech-faculty into the social-relational world. Rev 16:9 (vc=14352 — VCG-01): people "cursed the name of God" — the inner hardened-will state moves outward through cursing speech. Relational: the characteristic is constitutively targeted at another — blasphemy is directed at God (VCG-01) or at persons through false accusation (VCG-03). Rom 2:24 (vc=14357 — VCG-02): God's name blasphemed "among the Gentiles" — the relational audience (Gentiles) is part of the characteristic's definition. Constitutional: Jude 8 (vc=14354 — VCG-01): the dreamers "reject authority and blaspheme the glorious ones" — the blasphemy arises from a constitutional inner posture of authority-rejection; the inner constitutional orientation (rejection of authority, sensory self-indulgence) produces the speech expression.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The characteristic is primarily an expressive-dispositional phenomenon: an expressive act (speech) that flows from and reveals an inner moral disposition (wickedness, hardened will, malicious false judgment). It is not merely an act (individual utterances) but the expression of a constitutive inner posture. Rev 13:6 (vc=14356 — VCG-01): "a total inner orientation of defiant contempt toward the divine" expressed through the opened mouth. Jude 8 (vc=14354 — VCG-01): the dreamers' blasphemy flows from their wilful inner rejection of authority and sensory self-indulgence — the speech is expressive of a settled inner disposition. The characteristic also has a social-perlocutionary dimension: the speech acts cause harm to others — defamation reduces the slandered to refuse (1Cor 4:13 — VCG-02) and causes God's name to be dishonoured (Rom 2:24 — VCG-02). It is an inner-moral-disposition expressed through speech with harmful social consequences.

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — The 3-VCG structure reveals three constituent registers. (a) Direct defiant blasphemy against God (VCG-01, 6V): the hardened will generating contemptuous speech against God himself — Rev 13:6; 16:9/11/21; Jude 8/10. The inner content is wilful defiance of God's authority. (b) Social defamation causing dishonour (VCG-02, 5V): the social consequence of moral failure (Rom 2:24) or the false attribution of wrong teaching (Rom 3:8) or speaking ill of the good (Rom 14:16) or slandering the apostle (1Cor 4:13) or denouncing thankful freedom (1Cor 10:30). Here the inner content ranges from conduct-failure to malicious misrepresentation. (c) False accusation as hostile legal charge (VCG-03, 6V): charging the innocent as evildoers through hostile social-legal processes — Joh 18:30; 1Pe 2:12/14/3:16/4:15; Act 25:5. These three registers constitute the full range of the characteristic.

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-6]** E — Evil expressed through speech is the socially operative expression of inner wickedness through the instrument of speech — spanning three modes: (1) direct defiant contempt for God uttered verbally by the hardened will under judgment (VCG-01); (2) harmful speech that dishonours God's name or damages persons' reputations through misrepresentation (VCG-02); (3) hostile false accusation that charges the innocent as evildoers through legal-social processes (VCG-03). The characteristic's inner content varies by VCG — from total will-defiance against God (Rev 16:9 — VCG-01) to malicious hostile judgment (Joh 18:30 — VCG-03) to reckless denunciation (1Cor 10:30 — VCG-02) — but the common thread is inner wickedness finding its socially operative form through speech: blasphemy, slander, false accusation, or harmful denunciation.
