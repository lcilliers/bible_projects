## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Idolatrous abomination reveals God's character primarily through the divine-jealousy and divine-exclusivity dimensions: God is a moral being whose holiness cannot coexist with false devotion. Deu 32:16 (vc=65938 — VCG-02): "They stirred him to jealousy with strange gods; with abominations they provoked him to anger" — the divine response is jealousy and anger, revealing that God's character includes a morally serious covenantal exclusivity that is violated by idolatrous attachment. Eze 5:11 (vc=65969 — VCG-05): because the sanctuary was defiled with abominations "I will withdraw. My eye will not spare, and I will have no pity" — God's holiness is incompatible with idolatrous defilement; the divine presence is forced to withdraw. Jer 44:4 (vc=65966 — VCG-04): "do not do this abomination that I hate" — God's character involves intense moral hatred of idolatrous devotion. The evidence reveals God as a relational being whose holiness is covenantally exclusive — he cannot share devotion with idol-objects without responding with jealousy, anger, and withdrawal.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that reveal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Scripture explicitly denies this characteristic to God: the divine cannot become an idolater or be abominable. However, Scripture does attribute God's intense revulsion-response to the characteristic: Jer 44:4 (vc=65966 — VCG-04): "this abomination that I hate" — God's hatred of idolatrous abomination is directly stated. Deu 12:31 (vc=1683 — VCG-02): "every abominable thing that the Lord hates they have done for their gods" — the Lord's hatred is the defining relational context within which idolatrous abomination is identified. Deu 7:25 (vc=1682 — VCG-02): idols are "an abomination to the Lord your God" — the relational identity of God is the standard. The non-attribution of the characteristic to God reveals that idolatrous attachment is exclusively a creaturely failure — the creature who was designed to give exclusive devotion to God has redirected that devotion to created objects. God's own character (exclusive, morally serious, jealous for undivided allegiance) is the positive standard against which idolatrous abomination is measured.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Scripture is not silent but actively declarative (T0.1.2): God hates idolatrous abomination rather than possessing it. The silence about God's possession reveals the characteristic as the inversion of the designed God-devotion faculty in the human person. The human person was created with the capacity for exclusive devotion — a capacity designed for God. When that capacity is attached to idol-objects, idolatrous abomination results. God's non-possession of the characteristic is the negative space that defines the characteristic: what makes idolatry an abomination is precisely that the devotion-faculty is attached to what is not God. The silence about God possessing false devotion confirms that CHAR-4 is constitutively a departure from the relational orientation the created human was designed for.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic is named "Idolatrous abomination." Two components carry analytical weight. "Idolatrous" specifies the register: this is abomination rooted in false devotion — the attachment of the heart/soul/will to idol-objects rather than to God. This distinguishes CHAR-4 from CHAR-3 (moral-character abomination), where the abominable quality resides in inner moral conditions (pride, dishonesty). "Abomination" signals the divine-relational verdict: the characteristic provokes God's active revulsion. The name as a whole signals that idolatrous abomination is the specific form of abomination arising from the devotion-capacity's misdirection: Eze 11:21 (vc=1631 — VCG-05): "those whose heart goes after their detestable things and their abominations" — the heart's directional faculty is what goes after idols; Isa 66:3 (vc=1633 — VCG-04): "their soul delights in their abominations" — the soul's delight-faculty is what is engaged. The name names the specific inner-being dynamics of false devotion.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — Two primary Hebrew terms and one Greek. to.e.vah (H8441, ~72V idolatry sub-corpus) — the general abomination term: see CHAR-3 for root analysis. Applied to the idolatrous register it names: idol-practices as abominable to God (Deu 7:25 — VCG-02: idols "an abomination to the Lord"), the person who practises divination as abominable (Deu 18:12 — VCG-02), and accumulated idolatrous acts as abominations (Eze 5:9 — VCG-05). shiq.quts (H8251, 25V) — from a root of loathing/abhorrence; specifically names detestable idolatrous objects — the idol-things themselves as objects of abhorrence. 1Ki 11:5 (vc=65898 — VCG-03): "Milcom the abomination of the Ammonites" — the foreign deity is named a shiq.quts. Eze 20:7 (vc=1634 — VCG-05): "the detestable things your eyes feast on" — the idol-objects as shiq.quts. bdelugma (G0946, 2V — Mat 24:15; Mar 13:14 — VCG-06): the sacrilegious desecration-event in the holy place. Definitionally: to.e.vah names the quality of divine revulsion toward idolatrous practice; shiq.quts names the specific idol-objects that are abhorrent; bdelugma names the event of sacrilegious desolation.

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?**

**[CHAR-4]** E — The characteristic is primarily a dispositional-attachment phenomenon — the sustained inner orientation of the devotion-faculty (heart, soul, will) toward idol-objects. It is not primarily an act (individual acts of idolatrous worship belong to M10c cultic defilement) but a dispositional condition: the habitual going-after of the heart (Eze 11:21 — VCG-05 — "heart goes after their detestable things") and the active delight of the soul (Isa 66:3 — VCG-04 — "soul delights in their abominations"). The characteristic also has a constitutional quality dimension: Hos 9:10 (VCG-04) — "became detestable like the thing they loved" — the sustained attachment transforms character quality. The best single classification is: a dispositional inner attachment of the devotion-capacity to idolatrous objects, producing accumulated abomination as its characteristic output.
