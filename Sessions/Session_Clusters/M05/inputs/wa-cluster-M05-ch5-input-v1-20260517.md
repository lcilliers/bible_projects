**T1 — Definition**

- *T1.1.1* — _What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?_
  → "Fellowship and Participation" — compound covering the relational bond (fellowship) and the active sharing-in (participation). The compound is apt — the koinōn- root family spans both the state and the act.
- *T1.1.2* — _What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?_
  → Greek koinōnia (shared-life bond), koinōnos (participant/sharer), katallagē (restoration of broken bond), homofrōn (same-minded), isopsuchos (same-souled), epoikodomeō (building upon a foundation). Together: fellowship is the irreducibly communal characteristic — every term requires at least two parties.
- *T1.1.3* — _Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?_
  → Constitutively relational — koinōnia cannot exist in a single person; every term requires the shared bond between parties.
- *T1.2.1* — _What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else…_
  → A relational-constitutive condition — the state of shared participation in a common life. Koinōnia is something one "has" (1Jo 1:3 — Group 1055) — a state of shared inner belonging, not primarily an act or a feeling.
- *T1.2.2* — _Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?_
  → Compound — 8 groups: vertical fellowship (koinōnia with God), horizontal sharing (koinōnia with financial dimension), inner-spiritual participation (koinōnos in divine nature and suffering), moral solidarity, unity of mind (homofrōn), shared concern (isopsuchos), reconciliation (katallagē), formative building-up (epoikodomeō).
- *T1.2.3* — _What is the current best working description of this characteristic — encapsulating its constitutional location, the faculties it primarily …_
  → Fellowship and participation is the inner-relational bond of shared life — holding things in common, participating together in a shared reality, belonging to one another through a mutually constitutive bond. Vertical (with God) and horizontal (with one another) simultaneously constituted. Primary location: spirit (Trinitarian ground), soul (isopsuchos), mind (homofrōn). Structural opposite: darkness and estrangement.
- *T1.3.1* — _What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — the inner-being reality that stands against or excludes it?_
  → Darkness and estrangement — walking in darkness while claiming fellowship (1Jo 1:6 — Group 1055); the state of enmity before katallagē (Rom 5:11 — Group 2069 implies the prior estrangement).
- *T1.3.2* — _What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?_
  → Participation with incompatibles (1Cor 10:20 — Group 1057 — "I do not want you to be participants with demons"); false fellowship (claiming without walking in light — 1Jo 1:6 — Group 1055); moral solidarity with evil (Mat 23:30 — Group 1058).
- *T1.3.3* — _What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?_
  → Not mere proximity or social co-presence (1Jo 1:6 — Group 1055 names false fellowship where presence exists without inner reality); not the same as love (love is the relational orientation; fellowship is the shared-participation bond); not friendship (friendship is love-oriented attachment; fellowship is holding-in-common).
- *T1.4.1* — _In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?_
  → Vertical participation mode (koinōnia with God — Group 1055); horizontal sharing mode (koinōnia with financial/practical expression — Group 1056); moral solidarity mode (koinōnos — Group 1058); reconciliation mode (katallagē — Group 2069); formative building mode (epoikodomeō — Group 2849).
- *T1.4.2* — _Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?_
  → Yes — the calling into vertical fellowship is divine initiative (1Cor 1:9 — Group 1055); horizontal sharing is volitional practice (2Cor 8:4 — Group 1056); moral solidarity in persecution is chosen participation (Heb 10:33 — Group 1058).
