## 1. Governing question

The 87 terms in cluster M05 share the broad domain of love, compassion and kindness. The task of Phase 3 is to identify whether this broad domain contains distinct inner-being characteristics — each with its own constitutional location, faculty engagements, recognisable impact, structural opposite, and causal direction — and to group the terms accordingly as a provisional hypothesis for Phase 4 to test.

The inner being is not made of separable bricks. The onion principle applies: sub-groupings are analytical peels, not divisions. The terms in this cluster compound and morph — che.sed can be love, kindness, or steadfast faithfulness depending on direction and object; ra.cham and eleos share a family resemblance but operate differently. The sub-groupings below name dominant characteristics, not exclusive categories.

**Provisional-by-definition note:** This is the sub-group most at risk of merger with M05-B (compassion) on the control read. Na.cham's complexity (to relent / to comfort / to be sorry) spans multiple sub-groups — the control read must determine its dominant cluster placement.

## 3. Observations for Phase 4

1. **M05-A is large (22 terms)** — the most likely sub-group to be revised in Phase 4. The philo- compounds may split, and the che.sed/agapē/fileō families may need to be distinguished further if the control read shows meaningfully different verse patterns.

2. **M05-B/C boundary is the most analytical uncertain** — the distinction between racham-compassion (B) and eleos-mercy (C) rests on the claim that mercy has a covenantal/judicial dimension that compassion does not. The control read must confirm this at verse level. If the distinction does not hold, B and C may merge.

3. **M05-F may dissolve** — the comfort/encouragement sub-group is a hypothesis. Na.cham and parakaleō are rich, multi-sense terms; the control read may show they belong in B (compassion) or that they deserve separate sub-group status for distinct reasons.

4. **BOUNDARY list is long** — this is expected for a cluster this size. Many expressive and relational terms have been placed here provisionally. Phase 4 may return several to characteristic-bearing status.

5. **The assembly terms (ekklēsia, miq.ra, a.tsa.rah)** — Amo 5:21 (Phase 2) confirmed that the inner moral state of participants is the operative factor. This is a strong argument for characteristic-bearing status rather than BOUNDARY. But the terms name the occasion, not the disposition. The control read must resolve this tension.

6. **Negative/structural-opposite terms** — aneleēmōn, anileōs, eleeinos, afilarguros, anexikakos — these are important for understanding the characteristic's boundary. Placement in their respective sub-groups is confirmed; the control read should attend to what they reveal about the characteristic they negate.
