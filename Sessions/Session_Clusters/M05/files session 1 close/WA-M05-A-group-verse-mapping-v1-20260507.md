**Status:** NEW (split from existing Group 1583)
**Description (refined):** Term names God's steadfast love as his defining inner attribute — the covenantal faithfulness that never ceases, is proclaimed in the divine self-disclosure formula, and is the ground of Israel's hope and petition even in desolation.

These require different anchors for the catalogue pass: the declaration-of-attribute verses answer prompts about divine character differently from the enacted-chesed verses. ### Group 1540 [RETAINED] — G0025 agapaō: Mutual love among believers and in marriage

**Status:** RETAINED
**Description:** Term names mutual love among believers and within marriage — the inner relational orientation that marks discipleship and covenant; love of brothers in the community and of spouses in the bond.

**Note:** This group combines two relational directions (community love, marital love) that are closely related but analytically distinguishable. The conglomerate-group test: does a single anchor verse work for both? Joh 13:34 ("love one another as I have loved you") establishes the community direction; Eph 5:25 ("husbands, love your wives as Christ loved the church") establishes the marital direction. Both share the same Christological pattern (love as Christ loved) — so a single anchor can cover both at the level of the love-as-Christological-pattern. RETAINED as one group.

**Anchor verse:** Joh 13:34 — *"A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another: just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another."* The newness is the Christological grounding — the measure and pattern of love among believers is now the self-giving love of Christ, not merely the golden rule.
