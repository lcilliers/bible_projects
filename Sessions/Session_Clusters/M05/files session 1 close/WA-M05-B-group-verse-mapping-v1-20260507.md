### Group 1596 [RETAINED AND REFINED] — H7356B ra.cha.mim: God's deep compassion as mother-tenderness

**Status:** RETAINED AND REFINED
**Description (refined):** Term names God's compassion as the deep inner movement of mercy — his womb-rooted tenderness toward those who suffer, his mother-like readiness to forgive, restore, and gather; also evidenced in the human compassion that serves as the analogical ground for the divine (the mother's yearning, Joseph's heart warming).

**Verse table (sample):**

| Verse | Term | What the verse shows |
|---|---|---|
| Gen 43:30 | H7356B | Joseph's compassion growing warm for his brother — ra.cha.mim as the human visceral compassion that prefigures the divine |
| 1Ki 3:26 | H7356B | Mother's heart yearning for her son — the womb-rooted origin of ra.cha.mim made visible in the human analogy |
| Psa 25:6 | H7356B | "Remember your mercy, O Lord, from of old" — God's compassion as a permanent inner attribute |
| Psa 51:1 | H7356B | "According to your abundant mercy" — appealing to the depth of God's compassion as the ground of forgiveness |
| Psa 103:4 | H7356B | "Who crowns you with steadfast love and mercy" — ra.cha.mim as one of the defining crowning attributes |
| Isa 54:7 | H7356B | "With great compassion I will gather you" — compassion as the mode of eschatological restoration |
| Lam 3:22 | H7356B | "His mercies never come to an end" — ra.cha.mim as the inexhaustible inner disposition |
| Zec 7:9 | H7356B | "Show kindness and mercy to one another" — ra.cha.mim as a human-to-human disposition commanded |

**Anchor verse:** Isa 54:7 — *"For a brief moment I deserted you, but with great compassion I will gather you."* The contrast between the brief desertion and the great compassion reveals the asymmetry within God's inner life: even divine judgment is temporary against the backdrop of the permanent compassion that defines him.

**Status:** RETAINED AND REFINED
**Description (refined):** Term names the withholding of pity as a judicial inner disposition — the inner setting aside of compassionate impulse in the service of righteous judgment; primarily in the Deuteronomic formula "your eye shall not pity."

**Verse table (sample):**

| Verse | Term | What the verse shows |

### Group 629-a [SPLIT FROM 629] — H2550 cha.mal: Human compassion (positive/genuine)

**Status:** NEW (split from existing Group 629)
**Description:** Term names human compassion as the inner disposition of pity that moves toward and spares those in need — whether a child, a prisoner, an animal, or an enemy; genuine compassion as the inner motivation for sparing.

**Rationale for split:** Group 629 (14 verses, described as "human compassion or its withholding") contains two analytically distinct phenomena: (a) genuine positive human compassion (Exo 2:6 — Pharaoh's daughter taking pity on Moses; 1Sa 23:21 — Saul acknowledging David's compassion; Eze 36:21 — God having concern for his name), and (b) disobedient sparing — where compassion for a commanded-destruction target is itself the sin (1Sa 15:3, 15:9, 15:15 — Saul sparing Agag). These require different anchors for the catalogue pass: Exo 2:6 evidences compassion as a positive inner virtue; 1Sa 15:9 evidences compassion as moral disobedience when the object of compassion should not be spared.
