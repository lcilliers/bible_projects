# M05-C — Mercy

### Group 1628 [RETAINED] — G1653 eleeō: Divine mercy as sovereign ground of salvation

**Description:** Term names the act of divine mercy — God having mercy on whom he wills as the ground of salvation and sovereign election.
**Verse sample:** Rom 9:15 "I will have mercy on whom I have mercy" | Rom 9:16 "it depends not on human will or exertion, but on God, who has mercy" | Rom 9:18 "he has mercy on whomever he wills" | 2Cor 4:1 "having this ministry by the mercy of God" | 1Ti 1:13-16 Paul received mercy as the foremost sinner
**Anchor verse:** Rom 9:16 — *"So then it depends not on human will or exertion, but on God, who has mercy."* The sovereignty of mercy — not conditioned by human effort — is the most definitive statement of divine mercy as the ground of salvation.

---

### Group 1629 [RETAINED AND REFINED] — G1653 eleeō: The mercy cry

**Description (refined):** Term names the cry for mercy — the supplicant's inner anguish and hope directed toward Jesus as healer and Saviour; mercy as what is sought in extremity.
**P-verse assignment:** Mar 5:19 (vrID=93008) — "how much the Lord has done for you, and how he has had mercy on you" — the healed demoniac directed to testify of the mercy shown him. This is eleeō in the received-mercy/testimony register. Placed in Group 1629 as mercy received and named.
**Verse sample:** Mat 9:27 "Have mercy on us, Son of David" | Mat 15:22 | Mat 17:15 | Mar 5:19 (P-verse) | Mar 10:47-48 Bartimaeus | Luk 16:24 Rich man in Hades | Luk 17:13 ten lepers | Luk 18:38-39 blind man
**Anchor verse:** Mar 10:48 — *"But he cried out all the more, 'Son of David, have mercy on me!'"* The persistence of the cry against social pressure reveals the inner anguish and hope that drives the mercy petition.

---

### Group 1630 [RETAINED] — G1653 eleeō: Human mercy as disposition receiving mercy in return

**Description:** Term names the human act of mercy as the inner disposition that receives mercy in return.
**Verse sample:** Mat 5:7 "Blessed are the merciful, for they shall receive mercy" | Mat 18:33 "Should you not have had mercy on your fellow servant?" | Rom 12:8 "the one who shows mercy, with cheerfulness"
**Anchor verse:** Mat 18:33 — *"And should not you have had mercy on your fellow servant, as I had mercy on you?"* The received mercy as the ground and measure of shown mercy is the most complete evidencing of the reciprocal mercy logic.

---

### Group 1631 [RETAINED] — G1655 eleēmōn: Mercifulness as inner character quality

**Description:** Term names mercifulness as an inner character quality — the blessed disposition of those who show mercy and the required quality of Christ as high priest.
**Verse sample:** Mat 5:7 "Blessed are the merciful" | Heb 2:17 "that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest"
**Anchor verse:** Heb 2:17 — *"Therefore he had to be made like his brothers in every respect, so that he might become a merciful and faithful high priest."* The Incarnation as the means of acquiring eleēmōn reveals that mercifulness requires the capacity for fellow-feeling that only embodied experience provides.

---

### Group 1632 [RETAINED] — G1656 eleos: God's mercy as defining inner attribute

**Description:** Term names God's mercy as his defining inner attribute — the ground of salvation, the content of covenant faithfulness, the origin of new birth.
**Verse sample:** Luk 1:50 "mercy from generation to generation" | Luk 1:72 "to show the mercy promised" | Luk 1:78 "the tender mercy of our God" | Eph 2:4 "God, being rich in mercy" | Tit 3:5 "according to his own mercy, by the washing" | 1Pe 1:3 "According to his great mercy"
**Anchor verse:** Eph 2:4 — *"But God, being rich in mercy, because of the great love with which he loved us."* The characterisation "rich in mercy" names mercy as a constitutive attribute of God's inner character — not occasional clemency but an inexhaustible inner abundance.

---

### Group 1633 [RETAINED] — G1656 eleos: Mercy as inner virtue and judicial reality

**Description:** Term names mercy as the inner-being virtue and judicial reality — what God desires above ritual, what the wise show, and what triumphs over judgment.
**Verse sample:** Mat 9:13 "I desire mercy, and not sacrifice" | Mat 12:7 same | Mat 23:23 "neglected the weightier matters of the law: justice and mercy" | Jam 2:13 "mercy triumphs over judgment" | Jam 3:17 "full of mercy and good fruits"
**Anchor verse:** Jam 2:13 — *"For judgment is without mercy to one who has shown no mercy. Mercy triumphs over judgment."* The triumph of mercy over judgment is the most precise statement of mercy's characteristic nature: not the absence of judgment but its override by the greater reality.

---

### Group 1625 [RETAINED] — G0415 aneleēmōn: Mercilessness as inner character failure

**Description:** Term names mercilessness as an inner character failure — one of the marks of those whose inner moral fabric has collapsed.
**Verse sample:** Rom 1:31 "foolish, faithless, heartless, ruthless (aneleēmōn)"
**Anchor verse:** Rom 1:31 — the structural opposite of mercy named as a characteristic of the person whose inner moral architecture has collapsed in the descending depravity sequence.

---

### Group 1626 [RETAINED] — G0448 anileōs: Mercilessness as the inner orientation forfeiting mercy

**Description:** Term names mercilessness as the inner orientation that forfeits mercy — and mercy as the disposition that triumphs.
**Verse sample:** Jam 2:13 "judgment is without mercy (anileōs) to one who has shown no mercy"
**Anchor verse:** Jam 2:13 — the reciprocal structure: the one who shows no mercy (anileōs) receives judgment without mercy; the one who shows mercy participates in mercy's triumph.

---

### Group 328 [RETAINED] — G1652 eleeinos: The pitiable condition

**Description:** Term names the pitiable inner condition — the state of the person who is spiritually destitute, whether through unrecognised poverty (Rev 3:17) or through misplaced hope (1Cor 15:19).
**Verse sample:** 1Cor 15:19 "we are of all people most to be pitied" | Rev 3:17 "not realizing that you are wretched, pitiable, poor, blind, naked"
**Anchor verse:** Rev 3:17 — *"you are wretched, pitiable, poor, blind, and naked."* The pitiable condition named in the context of self-deceived spiritual poverty is the most definitive evidencing of eleeinos: the condition that calls forth mercy is the very condition the person does not recognise themselves to be in.

### Group 1111 [RETAINED] — G5544 chrēstotēs: God's kindness as inner disposition

**Description:** Term names kindness as God's inner disposition of generous goodwill toward humanity — the divine attribute that leads to repentance, displayed in Christ, and contrasted with severity.
**Verse sample:** Rom 2:4 "God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentance" | Rom 3:12 "no one does good, not even one" (absence) | Rom 11:22 "the kindness and the severity of God" | Eph 2:7 "immeasurable riches of his grace in kindness" | Tit 3:4 (see also Group 1559 via filanthrōpia)
**Anchor verse:** Rom 11:22 — *"Note then the kindness and the severity of God."* The pairing of kindness and severity as two faces of the same relational power reveals the distinctive character of chrēstotēs: not soft indulgence but the benevolent face of divine covenantal engagement.

---

### Group 1112 [RETAINED] — G5544 chrēstotēs: Spirit-produced kindness in the believer

**Anchor verse:** Gal 5:22 — *"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness."* The Spirit-production establishes that chrēstotēs in its fullest form is not self-generated but the organic expression of indwelling Spirit.

---

### Group 1469 [RETAINED] — G5543 chrēstos: Goodness/kindness as inner quality

**Description:** Goodness/kindness as inner quality of God or persons — disposition of gracious favour expressed toward others; also includes the "easy/light" sense (Matt 11:30).
**Note:** Matt 11:30 ("my yoke is easy, my burden is light") uses chrēstos in the sense of "easy/pleasant" — this is the sensory sense (cf. Luk 5:39 set aside). The difference is that Matt 11:30 makes a claim about Christ's inner quality (his yoke is kind/gracious) while Luk 5:39 simply describes wine's pleasantness with no inner-being dimension. Matt 11:30 is retained.
**Anchor verse:** Luk 6:35 — *"He is kind to the ungrateful and the evil."* The divine kindness extended to the undeserving defines chrēstos at its most characteristic: not conditional goodwill but gracious favour irrespective of response.

**Anchor verse:** 1Cor 13:4 — *"Love is patient and kind."* The inclusion of chrēsteuomai within love's character description confirms that kindness is not a separate virtue but the mode by which love expresses itself in relational action.

### Group 1741 [RETAINED AND REFINED] — H5162G na.cham: Human act of comforting

**Description (refined):** Term names the human act of comforting — bringing relief to inner grief through presence, words, or care; includes failed comfort (Job's comforters) and absent comfort (Lamentations).

**Note:** This group contains both positive (genuine comfort given) and negative (failed/absent comfort) instances of na.cham. These are held together because both name the same characteristic from complementary directions — what comfort is known by both its presence and its absence.
**Anchor verse:** Isa 40:1 — *"Comfort, comfort my people, says your God."* The divine commissioning of comfort — the double "comfort" — establishes the characteristic as an urgent relational task directed toward the specific people of God in their grief.
