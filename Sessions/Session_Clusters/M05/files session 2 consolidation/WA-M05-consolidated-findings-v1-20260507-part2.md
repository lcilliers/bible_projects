## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

### T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?

**[A]** Partial — the Spirit is the source of love's indwelling (Rom 5:5 — Group 1542: "God's love poured into our hearts through the Holy Spirit"), making the Spirit the producer and channel of love. Explicit spirit-level location of the love characteristic itself is not directly evidenced; the Spirit is its origin and transmitter rather than its named location.

**[B]** Silent — no verse explicitly places compassion at the spirit level. The Spirit is not named as the constitutional location or producer of compassion in the M05-B verse evidence.

**[C]** Silent — no explicit spirit-level location in M05-C verse evidence.

**[D]** Partial — Spirit-production of kindness (Gal 5:22 — Group 1112) implies spirit-level origination without naming the spirit as the constitutional location of the characteristic itself.

**[E]** Ambiguous — 1Cor 4:21 (Group 1104): "love in a spirit of gentleness" — "spirit of gentleness" may indicate a spirit-level location or may be idiomatic. Insufficient for a confident spirit-level location claim.

**[F]** Partial — Lam 1:16 (Group 1741): "a comforter far from me, one to revive my spirit" — the comforter's function is spirit-revival, implying the characteristic addresses the spirit level. The "comfort of the Holy Spirit" (Act 9:31 — mentioned in context) is pneumatic comfort.

**[G]** Yes — explicitly. "Fellowship of the Holy Spirit" (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055); "any participation in the Spirit" (Phili 2:1 — Group 1055); Spirit-prayer in the building-up context (Jude 20 — Group 2849). M05-G has the clearest spirit-level location in the cluster.

**[CLUSTER]** Spirit-level location is the most variable dimension across M05. Only M05-G evidences explicit Trinitarian spirit-level location. M05-A, D, F have Spirit-origin or Spirit-production without explicit spirit-location. M05-B, C, E are substantially silent. This pattern suggests that the cluster's characteristics operate primarily at the soul/heart level (see T2.2 and T2.3), with M05-G as the exception grounded in the Trinitarian fellowship of the Spirit.

---

### T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate origination at the spirit level?

**[A]** Yes — Spirit as producer (Rom 5:5 — Group 1542) implies spirit-level origination in the NT register. The chesed vocabulary does not name spirit-level origin explicitly.

**[B]** Silent — no evidence for spirit-level origination of compassion.

**[C]** Silent — mercy's sovereign origin in God (Rom 9:15-16 — Group 1628) is divine rather than specified as spirit-level.

**[D]** Yes — Spirit-production (Gal 5:22 — Group 1112) implies spirit-level proximate origination.

**[E]** Yes — Spirit-production (Gal 5:23 — Group 1104) and Christological grounding (2Cor 10:1 — Groups 1102, 1104) imply spirit-level origination without explicit naming.

**[F]** Partial — "comfort of the Holy Spirit" (Act 9:31) implies Spirit as the origin of comfort in the community context.

**[G]** Yes — the Spirit's own fellowship is the constitutive ground (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055).

---

### T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal?

**[G]** For M05-G, spirit-level location reveals that fellowship and participation is not primarily a human social arrangement but a Spirit-constituted and Spirit-sustained reality. The community's koinōnia is grounded in the Spirit's own inner fellowship with the Father and Son — the human participates in something that already exists at the deepest level of divine inner life.

**[CLUSTER]** Where spirit-level location or origination is evidenced, it consistently reveals that the characteristic is not self-generated by the human person but received from and sustained by the divine life — the Spirit produces, transmits, or constitutes the characteristic. The characteristics in which Spirit-level evidence is clearest (M05-A, D, E, G) are also those where the cluster's teaching on spiritual formation is most developed.

---

### T2.1.4 — If silent on spirit-level location, note explicitly.

**[B]** Silent — compassion's constitutional location is somatic-visceral (bowel/womb) rather than spirit-level. This silence at the spirit level is a structural finding: the most somatic characteristic (M05-B) is the most silent on the spirit level.

**[C]** Silent — mercy's operation is in the judicial-covenantal frame rather than named at the spirit level.

**[E]** Substantially silent — the single "spirit of gentleness" phrase (1Cor 4:21 — Group 1104) is ambiguous.

### T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified as a soul-level phenomenon?

**[A]** Yes — the great commandment names the soul as one of the locations for love: "with all your heart and with all your soul" (Mat 22:37 — Group 1542). The soul (psuchē) of the king "longed" for Absalom (2Sa 13:39 — Group 1741 context via na.cham — M05-F); the soul as the seat of relational attachment is present.

**[B]** Yes — Joseph's heart (inner organs — the meeim root) yearned with compassion (1Ki 3:26 — Group 1601 context). The soul as the seat of compassion is present though the primary somatic vocabulary is bowel/womb.

**[C]** Silent — no explicit soul-level location in M05-C evidence.

**[D]** Silent — soul-level location is not evidenced for kindness or goodness. The characteristic operates primarily at the heart and conscience level.

**[E]** Silent — no explicit soul-level location.

**[F]** Yes — explicitly. Psa 94:19 (Group 3079): "your consolations cheer my soul." Tan.chum addresses the soul directly — the most explicit soul-level evidencing in M05-F.

**[G]** Yes — G2473 isopsuchos (Group 1795 — Phili 2:20): "same-souled" — the soul (psuchē) is the seat of the shared concern-orientation. Soul-level alignment is constitutive of this term.

---

### T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal?

**[A]** The soul as the seat of relational attachment confirms that love reaches the deepest personal inner level of experience — it is not merely an attitude or a behaviour but a configuration of the person's most central inner being.

**[B]** The soul-level connection for compassion (through the yearning inner organs) reveals that compassion is an intimate inner engagement, not merely a surface response.

**[F]** Soul-level location reveals that comfort reaches the most personal inner level — the soul carrying accumulated anxieties (cares of the heart) is the specific target of consolation. Comfort that does not reach the soul has not reached the person where they actually are.

**[G]** The soul as the location of isopsuchos means that genuine fellowship concern operates at the deepest personal level — "same-souled" names a soul-level alignment, not merely a practical agreement.

---

### T2.2.3 — If silent on soul-level location, note explicitly.

**[C]** Silent — mercy's primary frame is judicial-relational rather than explicitly soul-located.

**[D]** Silent — goodness and kindness operate primarily at the heart and conscience level; soul-level location is not evidenced.

**[E]** Silent — spirit and heart are the primary locations for gentleness; soul is not explicitly evidenced.

---

### T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?

**[A]** Yes, definitively and primarily. Mat 22:37 (Group 1542): "you shall love the Lord your God with all your heart." Pro 3:3 (Group 1584 — chesed written on the tablet of your heart). Rom 5:5 (Group 1542): "God's love poured into our hearts." The heart is love's primary named constitutional location.

**[B]** Yes — heart as secondary location. "My heart yearned for him" (1Ki 3:26 — Group 1601 — the mother's heart in the compassion context). Jer 31:20 (Group 1596): God's heart/inner-being yearning over Ephraim with compassion.

**[C]** Partial — the heart is implicated in mercy's operation (the beatitude's "pure heart" in Mat 5:8 adjacent to "blessed are the merciful" — Mat 5:7 — Group 1631) but not explicitly named as mercy's primary location in M05-C verses.

**[D]** Yes, emphatically. Mat 12:35 (Group 1097): "the good person out of his good treasure brings forth good." Luk 8:15 (Group 1097): "honest and good heart." 1Ti 1:5 (Group 1097): "love from a pure heart and a good conscience." The heart is the treasury from which goodness flows.

**[E]** Yes, definitively. 1Pe 3:4 (Group 1261 anchor): "the hidden person of the heart with the imperishable beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which in God's sight is very precious." This is the most explicit heart-location statement for any characteristic in M05.

**[F]** Yes. Phile 7 (Group 3075): "the hearts of the saints have been refreshed through you." Song 4:9 (Group 2741 — la.vav): "you have captivated my heart." The heart is the target and location of the comfort-operation.

**[G]** Partial — the heart is implied as the seat of the fellowship-bond (Phili 1:7 — context: "I hold you in my heart") but not explicitly named as such in the Group 1055 anchor. Homofrōn (Group 1813) engages the mind rather than explicitly the heart.

---

### T2.3.2 — What does heart-location reveal?

**[A]** The heart as love's primary location names love as the constitutive inner orientation of the whole person — not a mood, not an action, but the directing disposition of what the person is.

**[D]** The heart as the treasury of goodness (Mat 12:35 — Group 1097) reveals that goodness is constitutive and generative — it is stored in the person and produced from them, not performed externally. The heart-location names goodness as the deepest available inner source.

**[E]** The "hidden person of the heart" (1Pe 3:4 — Group 1261) reveals that gentleness is not a surface behavioural pattern but a depth-level heart quality — invisible to external observers, precious to God, and irreducible to performance. This is the most important heart-location statement in M05.

**[F]** The heart as the target of comfort reveals that genuine comfort reaches the integrating centre of the person — not merely the surface or social presentation of the distressed person.

**[CLUSTER]** The heart is the most consistently named constitutional location across M05. Every sub-group evidences heart-location, though the degree of explicitness varies. The cluster as a whole is heart-centred — its characteristics operate from, toward, and within the heart. This is a cluster-level finding.

---

### T2.3.3 — If silent on heart-location, note explicitly.

No sub-group is silent on heart-location. Heart is evidenced across all seven.

### T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?

**[A]** Yes — the great commandment includes "with all your mind" (dianoia — Mat 22:37 — Group 1542); Phili 4:8 (Group 1552 — M05-A context via prosfilēs — BOUNDARY): directing the mind to what is lovely. The mind is named as one of love's locations but is secondary to the heart.

**[B]** Silent — no explicit mind-location for compassion. The compassion characteristic is pre-cognitive in its triggering (somatic-visceral precedes deliberation).

**[C]** Silent — mind-location is not evidenced in M05-C verse data.

**[D]** Yes — Rom 12:2 (Group 1098): "transformed by the renewal of your mind, that by testing you may discern what is the will of God, what is good." Pronoeō (Group 2290) is explicitly a cognitive-purposive term: "give thought to do what is honourable."

**[E]** Yes — G3675 homofrōn (Group 1813 — though placed in M05-G): the frōn- root is the cognitive-evaluative faculty. Within M05-E proper, the wisdom-meekness link (Jam 3:13 — Group 1262) associates the mind's evaluative output with gentleness as its proper mode.

**[F]** Partial — the mind engages through Scripture-encouragement (Rom 15:4 — Group 3074): Scripture is received cognitively and its encouragement operates through the mind's engagement. But the mind is a channel rather than the primary location.

**[G]** Yes — homofrōn (Group 1813 — 1Pe 3:8): "unity of mind" — the frōn- root names the mind as the faculty whose shared orientation constitutes communal fellowship. This is the most explicit mind-location in M05-G.

---

### T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal?

**[A]** Mind-location reveals that love has a genuine cognitive dimension — it requires and produces knowledge (Hos 6:6 — "knowledge of God" as the companion of chesed; Joh 15:15 — friendship constituted by mutual knowledge-disclosure).

**[D]** Mind-location reveals that goodness requires cognitive discernment — the renewed mind is required to identify what goodness demands in concrete situations. Goodness without the cognitive capacity to recognise what is good cannot be practiced rightly.

**[G]** Mind-location reveals that fellowship has a cognitive-evaluative dimension — the community of shared mind (homofrōn) is not merely emotional agreement but cognitive-evaluative alignment. The community thinks together.

**[CLUSTER]** The mind is a secondary but real constitutional location across M05. Where evidenced, it serves as: the faculty that discerns what each characteristic demands (M05-A, D); the faculty whose shared orientation constitutes communal life (M05-G); and the faculty that receives scriptural encouragement toward hope (M05-F). The cluster's characteristics are not sub-cognitive but neither are they primarily cognitive — they operate from the heart through the mind into relational expression.

### T2.4.3 — If silent on mind-location, note explicitly.

**[B]** Silent — compassion is explicitly pre-cognitive in its triggering; the mind is not named as a location or faculty for M05-B.

**[C]** Silent on mind-location in M05-C verse evidence.

### T2.5.1 — Other soul-level locations beyond heart and mind?

**[A]** Strength (dunamis) — the great commandment's "with all your strength" (Mar 12:30 — Group 1542) names the volitional-energetic faculty alongside heart, soul, and mind.

**[B]** Bowel/entrails (splanchnon) — the most significant soul-subset location in M05. Splanchnizō (Group 1601) locates compassion in the gut/bowel level — the splanchnon is the inner organ register for compassion. This is M05-B's distinctive constitutional contribution.

**[D]** Conscience — "good conscience" appears in Act 23:1; 1Ti 1:5; 1Ti 1:19; 1Pe 3:16; 1Pe 3:21 (all Group 1097). Conscience as the inner registrar of goodness' faithfulness is the sub-group-distinctive finding of M05-D. No other M05 sub-group evidences good conscience as explicitly.

**[G]** Soul (psuchē) in isopsuchos (Group 1795 — Phili 2:20) — the soul as the seat of genuine shared concern.

---

### T2.5.2 — What does it reveal?

**[B]** The bowel/womb vocabulary of M05-B (splanchnizō, ra.cham from rechem) reveals that compassion is the most somatically grounded characteristic in the cluster — its primary constitutional location is in the gut-visceral level of the inner being. This is unique to M05-B in the cluster.

**[D]** Conscience as M05-D's distinctive inner location reveals that goodness has a self-monitoring mechanism — the person who habitually does good maintains a good conscience as an inner consequence, while abandoning goodness wrecks the conscience (1Ti 1:19 — Group 1097). The conscience is to goodness what the visceral stirring is to compassion: the inner registrar of the characteristic's operation.

**[E]** No significant soul-subset beyond heart is evidenced for gentleness.

**[F]** The spirit-revival function (Lam 1:16 — Group 1741) is the closest soul-subset evidence; no other specific soul-subset is explicitly named.

### T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**[A]** No direct body-part constitutional link for love itself. The kiss (filēma — BOUNDARY) and bosom (cheq — BOUNDARY) are the expressive somatic register but are BOUNDARY terms, not characteristic-bearing terms.

**[B]** Yes — emphatically. The bowel/womb vocabulary is constitutive: splanchnizō (Group 1601) locates compassion in the bowels/gut; ra.cham from rechem (womb) names the womb as the root-image for divine compassion. This is the most explicit body-part link in M05.

**[C]** Partial — Luk 1:78 (Group 1632): "splanchna eleous" — the tender mercy located in the inward parts (splanchna). This is the closest M05-C comes to a body-part link, and it reaches toward M05-B's somatic register. But it is a single phrase rather than a constitutive vocabulary.

**[D-G]** Silent — M05-D, E, F, G evidence no body-part constitutional link. These sub-groups are thoroughly inner and relational without somatic anchoring.

---

### T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that body-part link?

**[B]** The bowel/womb vocabulary is not incidental — it is definitional for compassion. The somatic grounding of ra.cham in the womb-root identifies compassion as the most constitutively bodily of the inner characteristics: it is what the body carries at its most generative and nurturing level. Scripture is naming the characteristic's constitutional depth by anchoring it in the generative-bodily location. The emotional-visceral stirring of splanchnizō enacts the same anchoring in Greek: compassion is felt in the gut before it is thought in the mind or chosen in the will.

---

### T2.6.3 — If no body-part link evidenced, note explicitly.

**[CLUSTER]** M05-B is the only sub-group with a constitutive body-part link. All other sub-groups (A, C, D, E, F, G) are silent on body-part constitutional location. This is a cluster-level structural finding: the cluster's inner characteristics are primarily heart-located and relationally expressed, with M05-B as the notable exception whose somatic grounding in the womb/bowel vocabulary is its defining constitutional contribution to the cluster.

---

### T2.7.1 — Where a body-part link exists, which direction does it run?

**[B]** The bowel/gut moves outward — the inner somatic stirring produces action. Luk 15:20 (Group 1601 — the father seeing the returning son): "he felt compassion, and ran and embraced him and kissed him" — the sequence is: perception → visceral stirring (splanchnizō) → bodily action (running, embracing). The direction is soul-to-body-to-action: the inner somatic response feeds into outward bodily expression.

---

### T2.7.2 — Consequence of that directionality?

**[B]** The soul-to-body direction of compassion's stirring confirms that compassion generates agency without deliberative gap — the visceral response moves the body into action before the mind has time to deliberate. This is structurally unlike M05-D (goodness), which requires deliberate volitional choice, or M05-E (gentleness), which requires restraint of the natural reactive impulse. Compassion's directional structure makes it the most naturally urgent of the cluster's characteristics.

---

### T2.7.3 — If no body-part link evidenced, note explicitly.

**[A, C, D, E, F, G]** Silent on body-characteristic direction. The characteristics operate from the inner (heart, soul, spirit) directly to relational expression without a constitutive somatic intermediate step.

M05-B's bowel/womb vocabulary is the strongest candidate for somatic constitutional grounding but even this is not evidenced as a lasting deposit — it describes the location of the compassion-stirring, not a permanent bodily change from sustained compassion.

### T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[A]** Dual — God as the exclusive source of love-as-constitutive-character (1Jo 4:8; Exo 34:6 — Group 1583-a); the Spirit as the proximate producer and channel (Rom 5:5 — Group 1542).

**[B]** Divine constitutive attribute — God's compassion is his own inner character (ra.chum in Exo 34:6 — Group 1594); it is not caused by anything external to God.

**[C]** Divine sovereignty — "I will have mercy on whom I have mercy" (Rom 9:15 — Group 1628); mercy cannot be coerced or generated by human merit. It originates in divine sovereign will.

**[D]** God as the exclusive source (Mar 10:18 — Group 1096 — "no one is good except God alone"); the Spirit as proximate producer (Gal 5:22 — Group 1112); divine pre-preparation of the good works (Eph 2:10 — Group 1098).

**[E]** Christological model and source (2Cor 10:1 — Groups 1102, 1104); Spirit production (Gal 5:23 — Group 1104); volitional seeking as the human pathway (1Ti 6:11 — Group 1261).

**[F]** God as primary named source (Isa 51:12 — Group 1742; 2Cor 1:3 — Group 3073); Scripture as secondary channel (Rom 15:4 — Group 3074); the suffering comforter as human channel (2Cor 1:4 — Group 602).

**[G]** Trinitarian origin — divine calling (1Cor 1:9 — Group 1055), Spirit-constitution (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055), Christ-mediated reconciliation (2Cor 5:18 — Group 2069).

**[CLUSTER]** All seven sub-groups name God as the ultimate source of the characteristic. The mediation paths vary (Spirit, Christ, divine calling, human channel) but the singular divine source is consistent. This is a cluster-level finding: M05 is a cluster in which all characteristics are constitutively derived from God — the human person does not generate any of them from their own resources.

---

### T2.9.2 — Singular or multiple origin?

**[CLUSTER]** Singular at source (God), multiple in mediation. The Spirit produces kindness and gentleness; Christ mediates reconciliation and models meekness; the divine self-disclosure formula grounds love and compassion simultaneously. The multiplicity is of mediation paths from a single divine source.

---

### T2.9.3 — Does the origin change across contexts?

**[A]** The chesed origin (OT) is the divine covenant-faithfulness; the agapē origin (NT) is specified as Spirit-mediated (Rom 5:5) and Christ-demonstrated (Joh 3:16). The ultimate source is consistent; the mediation language develops across testaments.

**[CLUSTER]** The origin does not change across contexts — God is the source in every sub-group and every context. The mediation mechanisms vary (Spirit-production in some; direct divine action in others; human-channel in others) but the divine origin is invariant.

---

### T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?

**[A]** Yes — Spirit-production → heart-indwelling → outward relational expression. Rom 5:5 (Group 1542): Spirit → heart; the great commandment names heart, soul, mind, strength — all levels; love reaches out to neighbour and enemy (Mat 5:44 — Group 1565).

**[B]** Yes — inner somatic stirring → bodily action. Luk 15:20 (Group 1601): compassion-stirring → running → embracing. The movement is inner-to-body-to-relational-act.

**[C]** Yes — divine mercy → received mercy → shown mercy → consequence (Mat 18:33 — Group 1630). The mercy-economy moves across levels from divine to human to outcome.

**[D]** Yes — inner heart-treasury → outward production of good (Mat 12:35 — Group 1097). The movement is heart → conduct.

**[E]** Yes — hidden inner heart quality (1Pe 3:4 — Group 1261) → visible outward conduct (Phili 4:5 — Group 1103: "let your reasonableness be known to everyone"). The movement is inner hidden → outwardly known.

**[F]** Yes — God's comfort → received by the person in affliction → given by the person to others in affliction (2Cor 1:4 — Group 602). The cascading structure moves from divine origin through human reception to human giving.

**[G]** Yes — the vertical (fellowship with God — Group 1055) and horizontal (fellowship among believers — Group 1056) are simultaneously constituted: "if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin" (1Jo 1:7 — Group 1055). Vertical and horizontal koinōnia are not sequential but co-constituted.

---

### T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern?

**[A]** Spirit-formation → heart-inscription → relational disposition → deliberate acts (the formation pattern evidenced across chesed and agapē).

**[B]** Perception of suffering → visceral stirring (pre-cognitive) → bodily response → relational act (the compassion-action sequence).

**[C]** Receiving mercy → mercy-obligation → showing mercy (or withholding it) → consequence (the mercy-economy sequence).

**[D]** Inner deposit (good treasury in heart) → inner production of good → outward expression (speech, act, conscience-maintenance).

**[E]** Inner settled quality (hidden in the heart) → deliberate expression outward in relational engagement (the hidden → known pattern).

**[F]** Divine → received → given → received by others (the cascading comfort structure of 2Cor 1:3-7).

**[G]** Vertical → simultaneously horizontal; or divine calling → community formation → eschatological inheritance (the formative sequence).

**[CLUSTER]** The dominant constitutional movement across M05 is inner-to-outer: from the inner constitutional location (heart, soul, spirit) outward through the person's action and relational engagement. This is a cluster-level structural finding. The exception and most complex pattern is M05-B, whose movement includes the somatic intermediate step (bowel-stirring) between inner perception and outward action. The cluster moves outward from a deeply interior origin — the characteristics are produced inwardly and expressed relationally.

---

### T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement evidenced, note explicitly.

Not applicable — constitutional movement is evidenced across all seven sub-groups.
