## Structural categories and their functions

### Category 1 — Physical locations and acts of relational expression

**Terms:** H2436G cheq (bosom/embrace), G5370 filēma (kiss), G2705 katafileō (to kiss)

**Structural function:** These terms name the bodily, spatial, and somatic register through which the cluster's inner characteristics are expressed outward. The bosom (cheq) is the physical location of intimacy — the space where cherishing is enacted in the body (Rut 4:16 — infant in the lap; 2Sa 12:3 — the lamb in arms; Isa 40:11 — God carrying lambs in his bosom). It names where love's inner disposition becomes physical proximity. The kiss (filēma, katafileō) is the enacted outward sign of the inner relational state: the holy kiss of greeting (Rom 16:16; 1Cor 16:20; 2Cor 13:12; 1Th 5:26; 1Pe 5:14) expresses the community's love for one another; the kiss of Judas (Mat 26:49; Mar 14:45; Luk 22:48) reveals the betrayal of love through its formal expression.

**Why BOUNDARY and not characteristic-bearing:** The bosom, kiss, and embrace name the expressive act or spatial location — the point where the inner characteristic contacts the body and the social world. The inner characteristics (love, compassion, fellowship) are what these terms express; the terms themselves name the expression rather than the characteristic. Cheq is where love is held, not love itself. Filēma is what love does with the face, not what love is.

**What these terms reveal about M05's characteristics:** The physical register of this cluster is substantial — love, compassion, and fellowship all have bodily expressions named in the vocabulary. This confirms that the cluster's inner characteristics are not merely cognitive or volitional but seek embodied expression. The cheq and filēma vocabulary gives body to what the inner life carries.

### Category 2 — Qualities of objects that direct inner attention

**Terms:** H5000 na.eh (lovely/fitting/becoming), G4375 prosfilēs (lovely/commendable/praiseworthy)

**Structural function:** These terms name the quality in an object or act that makes it fitting, beautiful, or praiseworthy — the attractiveness that draws the inner person's attention and evaluation toward it. Na.eh appears in aesthetic-evaluative contexts: "praise befits the upright" (Psa 33:1 — Group 1591); "your face is lovely" (Song 2:14, 4:3, 6:4 — Group 1591); the fitting and unfitting (Pro 17:7, 19:10, 26:1 — Group 1591). Prosfilēs appears in Phili 4:8 (Group 1552): "whatever is lovely, whatever is commendable... think about these things" — prosfilēs as one of the qualities that directs the mind's moral attentiveness.

**Why BOUNDARY and not characteristic-bearing:** Na.eh and prosfilēs name the quality of the object that elicits inner-being response — they describe what calls forth the love, admiration, or attention, not the love or attention itself. The lovely face (Song) draws love; the lovely quality (Phili 4:8) directs the mind. The directing quality is not the inner characteristic — it is what the characteristic responds to.

**What these terms reveal about M05's characteristics:** Love, attention, and appreciation are not directionless — they are constitutively directed toward objects that carry the qualities named by na.eh and prosfilēs. The BOUNDARY terms reveal that the cluster's characteristics are always responsive to something that is genuinely lovely, fitting, or commendable. This supports the prior finding from M05-A: love is constitutively directional, defined by its object. The lovely/fitting quality of the object is the trigger for the love-response.

### Category 3 — Freedom from disordered love

**Term:** G0866 afilarguros (not greedy / not a lover of money / free from love of money)

**Structural function:** Afilarguros names the inner character quality of freedom from the love of money — required of leaders (1Ti 3:3 — Group 1549; Heb 13:5 — Group 1549). Its group description names it as "freedom from love of money as inner character quality — required of those who lead."

**Why BOUNDARY and not characteristic-bearing:** Afilarguros is a negative qualifier — it names the absence of a disordered love orientation rather than the presence of a positive one. The inner character quality of freedom from wrong love is real — it is required, it can be possessed or lacked, and its absence disqualifies from leadership. However, it defines the person negatively (not loving money) rather than positively (loving God and neighbour). The positive love characteristic is in M05-A; afilarguros names the removal of an obstacle to that love.

**What this term reveals about M05's characteristics:** Afilarguros reveals that love-of-money is a structural distortion of the love characteristic — a misdirection of the love-orientation toward material goods. The requirement that leaders be free from this reveals that genuine love-orientation requires a cleared inner space — the love-of-money must be absent before the love of God and community can be primary. This is a diagnostic of the cluster's understanding of love as constitutively directional: the wrong direction (money) structurally crowds out the right direction (God, neighbour).

### Category 4 — Assembly occasions

**Terms:** G1577 ekklēsia (assembly/church), H4744 miq.ra (assembly/convocation), H6116 a.tsa.rah (solemn assembly)

**Structural function:** These three terms name the communal occasions — the gathered assembly — in which the cluster's characteristics are collectively expressed, tested, and embodied. Ekklēsia names the church as the gathered community (Acts, Pauline epistles — Group 1712 and predominantly NR in the BOUNDARY); miq.ra names the holy convocation of Israel (Lev 23 — predominantly NR); a.tsa.rah names the solemn assembly (Lev 23:36; Num 29:35; Deu 16:8; 2Ch 7:9; Joel 1:14; 2:15; Amos 5:21 — Group 2148).

**Why BOUNDARY and not characteristic-bearing:** The assembly terms name the occasion rather than the characteristic. What happens in the assembly — the love, fellowship, worship, compassion — belongs to the characteristics; the assembly itself is the social occasion that gathers and structures those expressions. The ekklēsia is the body in which koinōnia (M05-G) occurs; it is not itself koinōnia.

**The a.tsa.rah exception:** The group description for a.tsa.rah (Group 2148) claims characteristic-bearing status — "the solemn assembly as a collective inner-being occasion — the community gathered for worship and covenant renewal, where the inner moral state of participants is the decisive factor for its validity." Phase 2 confirmed Amos 5:21 as relevant: God's rejection of the assembly reveals that the inner moral state of the participants is the decisive factor. This was confirmed as a P-status verse and held in BOUNDARY pending resolution of OQ-003 from Phase 4. Per the Phase 4 decision, a.tsa.rah is retained in BOUNDARY: the inner moral state that validates the assembly belongs to M05-A (love), M05-D (goodness), and M05-G (fellowship) — the assembly term itself is not the characteristic but the occasion that tests and reveals it.

**P-status verse disposition — Amo 5:21 (a.tsa.rah):** This verse (God saying "I hate, I despise your feasts, and I take no delight in your solemn assemblies") names the divine rejection of the assembly whose inner moral reality does not match its outer form. The verse is held P-status in BOUNDARY. Researcher decision required: the verse evidences that the solemn assembly requires genuine inner characteristics (love, justice, goodness) to be valid — but this evidence supports the analysis of M05-A and M05-D rather than making a.tsa.rah itself characteristic-bearing. Recommendation: retain Amo 5:21 as BOUNDARY with the set-aside reason "assembly occasion named but inner characteristic content (love, justice) belongs to M05-A and M05-D; the assembly is not itself the characteristic." **This requires CC action — a VCREVISE patch to assign the verse as either set-aside in BOUNDARY or routed to an appropriate sub-group anchor.**

**What the assembly terms reveal about M05's characteristics:** The assembly occasions reveal that M05's characteristics are constitutively communal — they are tested and expressed in and through gathered community life. The solemn assembly that God rejects (Amos 5:21) reveals that the outer form of communal worship without the inner characteristics it is meant to embody is abhorrent. The assembly is the social institution within which love, fellowship, compassion, and goodness are either genuinely present or exposed as absent.

### Category 5 — Demonstrative and expressive acts

**Terms:** G1731 endeiknumi (to show/demonstrate), G1517 eirēnopoieō (to make peace), G1433 dōreō (to give/grant)

**Structural function:** These three terms name acts that demonstrate, express, or enable the cluster's characteristics without themselves being the characteristics.

**G1731 endeiknumi** (Group 3218): The act of showing or demonstrating — in its M05 context, endeiknumi demonstrates love (2Cor 8:24 — "give proof before the churches of your love"), divine power (Rom 9:17 — "that I might show my power in you"), God's patience and wrath (Rom 9:22 — showing wrath and power), and the work of the law written on hearts (Rom 2:15). The inner being is engaged as the source or recipient of what is shown — but the act of showing is not itself the characteristic. It reveals the characteristic; it is not the characteristic.

**G1517 eirēnopoieō** (Group 1712): Making peace — the one act in Colossians 1:20: "making peace by the blood of his cross." This is the specific act of Christ's reconciling work. The group description names it as "Christ's reconciling work that restores the broken relationship." This act belongs to M05-G's katallagē register — the peace-making act produces the reconciled relationship. The term names the act rather than the relationship. Its placement in BOUNDARY is appropriate: the making-of-peace is the means to fellowship; it is not fellowship itself.

**G1433 dōreō** (Group 2597): To give/grant — God's granting of all things needed for inner transformation (2Pe 1:3 — P-status verse in BOUNDARY; 2Pe 1:4 already assigned to M05-G Group 1057 via koinōnos). The group description names Group 2597 as "God's granting of all things needed for inner transformation — life, godliness, participation in the divine nature." The giving act enables the participation in divine nature (M05-G), but the term names the giving act not the participation itself.

**P-status verse disposition — 2Pe 1:3 (dōreō):** "His divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of him who called us to his own glory and excellence." This verse is held P-status in BOUNDARY under dōreō. The granting of all things for life and godliness is the divine act that enables the koinōnos/participatory transformation named in 2Pe 1:4 (already assigned to M05-G Group 1057). Recommendation: 2Pe 1:3 (dōreō, vrID=215044) should be assigned to Group 2597 (the giving/granting group) in BOUNDARY and marked G-status. The verse evidences the granting act without itself being a participation-characteristic verse. **This requires CC action — a VCREVISE patch to assign 2Pe 1:3 to Group 2597.**

**What these terms reveal about M05's characteristics:** The demonstrative and expressive acts reveal that the cluster's characteristics need to be shown, enacted, and enabled before they are fully real in the human community. Love must be demonstrated (endeiknumi); fellowship requires the peace-making that removes the barrier (eirēnopoieō); participation in divine life requires the divine granting act that makes it possible (dōreō). The BOUNDARY terms name the mechanisms and expressions through which the inner characteristics become visible and effective in the world.

## Summary of structural findings from BOUNDARY analysis

**Observation 1 — The cluster's characteristics require a body.** The cheq and filēma vocabulary (Category 1) reveals that love, compassion, and fellowship seek embodied expression. The cluster is not purely spiritual or cognitive but reaches toward somatic expression in embrace, proximity, and the gesture of the kiss. The BOUNDARY terms confirm that the inner characteristics of M05 are constitutively embodiment-seeking.

**Observation 2 — Attractiveness is constitutive of the love-response.** Na.eh and prosfilēs (Category 2) name the lovely/fitting quality that draws the inner person toward an object. This confirms that love and moral attention are not self-generated but responsive — they are called forth by what is genuinely lovely, fitting, or commendable. The BOUNDARY reveals that M05's characteristics are always directed toward objects that possess the drawing quality.

**Observation 3 — Freedom from disordered love is required for ordered love.** Afilarguros (Category 3) names the clearing of a specific obstacle — love-of-money — as a prerequisite for genuine love orientation. The BOUNDARY term reveals that the cluster's positive characteristics require the removal of competing misdirected love-orientations.

**Observation 4 — Assembly is the communal test of inner characteristics.** The three assembly terms (Category 4) reveal that the cluster's characteristics are tested and exposed in the communal occasion: the assembly that lacks inner love and justice is rejected by God (Amos 5:21). The BOUNDARY terms confirm that M05's characteristics are not private inner states but communally validated realities.

**Observation 5 — The characteristics need demonstrating, peace-making, and enabling acts.** Endeiknumi, eirēnopoieō, and dōreō (Category 5) reveal that the cluster's inner characteristics require: (a) demonstration to become visible in the community; (b) the prior act of peace-making to make fellowship possible where enmity stood; (c) divine granting to make inner transformation possible. The BOUNDARY terms name the indispensable supporting acts that the characteristics require.
