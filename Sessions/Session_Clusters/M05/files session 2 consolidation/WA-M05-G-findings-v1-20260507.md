## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**T0.1.1** — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**E** — Two explicitly divine references shape the sub-group's portrait of God. (1) The Trinitarian koinōnia: 2Cor 13:14 (Group 1055) — "the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ and the love of God and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all" — the Holy Spirit's fellowship is named as one of three divine realities offered to the community. The Spirit's inner reality is one of fellowship — the Third Person is constitutively a sharing, participating presence. (2) God as the reconciling agent: 2Cor 5:18-19 (Group 2069) — "All this is from God, who through Christ reconciled us to himself" — God is the one who initiates the restoration of broken fellowship. The character of God revealed: one whose inner communal life (Trinitarian koinōnia) overflows into the restoration of broken relationships and the constitution of a shared community.

**T0.1.2** — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does it reveal?

**E** — Yes. The "fellowship of the Holy Spirit" (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055) attributes koinōnia to the Third Person of the Trinity — fellowship is not only a human relational phenomenon but a divine one. 1Cor 1:9 (Group 1055): "God is faithful, by whom you were called into the fellowship of his Son, Jesus Christ our Lord" — God is the one who calls into fellowship; he initiates the relational participation. God's attribution of the characteristic reveals that fellowship is a structural feature of divine inner life, not merely a human social arrangement.

**T0.1.3** — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession, note explicitly.

**S** — Not applicable. Divine possession of koinōnia (as Trinitarian fellowship) and divine initiation of katallagē (reconciliation) are evidenced. No silence.

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

**T0.2.1** — Created purpose?

**E** — The most explicit purpose statement is 1Jo 1:3 (Group 1055): "that which we have seen and heard we proclaim also to you, so that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ." The purpose of the proclamation is fellowship — the gospel creates koinōnia. This establishes fellowship with God as the telos of creation and redemption simultaneously: the person is constituted for participation in the divine-human communal bond. Eph 2:20 (Group 2849): "built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Christ Jesus himself being the cornerstone" — the purpose of the community-formation is the building of a structure on the Christological foundation.

**T0.2.2** — Original created design, response to fallen condition, or both?

**P** — The evidence is entirely NT and does not address the original creation question directly. Katallagē (reconciliation — Group 2069) presupposes a broken relationship being restored — this is constitutively a response to the fallen condition. Koinōnia in its vertical dimension (fellowship with God — Group 1055) may reflect an original created purpose (the person made for communion with God), now restored through Christ. The evidence does not resolve this distinction explicitly — it presents fellowship and reconciliation as the present redemptive reality without addressing whether fellowship was part of the original design.

**T0.2.3** — Orientation toward future fullness?

**E** — Yes. 1Pe 5:1 (Group 1057) — "a partaker in the glory that is going to be revealed" — the koinōnos relationship extends toward eschatological glory. Eph 2:20 (Group 2849) — the community built on the Christological cornerstone is oriented toward completion. The fellowship that is being built up (epoikodomeō) moves toward an eschatological fullness.

### T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression

**T0.3.1** — How does this characteristic express the divine image?

**E** — The Trinitarian koinōnia (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055) establishes that fellowship is constitutive of the inner life of God. The human person's fellowship-capacity — the ability to hold things in common, to participate in shared life, to be reconciled and reconciling — images the communal nature of the divine Being. The person who participates in genuine koinōnia reflects the internal relational life of the Trinity outward into the human community.

**T0.3.2** — Shared between God and the human, or exclusively creaturely?

**E** — Genuinely shared. The "fellowship of the Holy Spirit" (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055) and "called into fellowship of his Son" (1Cor 1:9 — Group 1055) make fellowship a divine reality that the human person is invited to participate in. The sharing is participatory rather than symmetrical: the human participates in a fellowship that originates in and belongs to God; they are called into it, not constitutors of it.

**T0.3.3** — What does presence or absence reveal?

**E** — 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055): "if we say we have fellowship with him while we walk in darkness, we lie and do not practice the truth." The absence of genuine fellowship (the claim to fellowship without the reality) is identified as falsehood — a condition of self-deception. 2Cor 6:14 (Group 1056): "what fellowship has light with darkness?" — the absence of fellowship between incompatibles is not a failure but a structural impossibility. The presence of genuine fellowship is evidence of walking in the light; its absence or counterfeit is evidence of moral darkness.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

**T1.1.1** — Name and what it signals?

**E** — "Fellowship and Participation." The compound name covers two overlapping registers: fellowship (the relational bond of shared life — koinōnia, homofrōn, isopsuchos) and participation (the active sharing-in of a common reality — koinōnos, epoikodomeō, katallagē). The name signals that the characteristic is not merely social in the thin sense (being together) but constitutively participatory — holding something in common, sharing in a shared reality. The "and" in the name reflects the two faces of the same characteristic: the bond (fellowship) and the act of sharing-in (participation).

**T1.1.2** — Primary terms at the definitional level?

**E** — G2842 koinōnia: noun of participation/sharing — the bond of held-in-common life. G2844 koinōnos: the person who participates — the sharer, partner, participant. G2643 katallagē: reconciliation — the restoration of the broken fellowship bond. G3675 homofrōn: unity of mind/orientation — the communal inner alignment that fellowship produces. G2473 isopsuchos: same-souled — the genuinely shared inner disposition of concern. G2026 epoikodomeō: building-upon — the formative development of the community as the fellowship's ongoing construction.

**T1.1.3** — Directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**E** — Constitutively relational and directional. The koinōnia vocabulary requires at minimum two parties holding something in common — it cannot exist in isolation. Katallagē is directional: it moves from estrangement toward restored relation. Epoikodomeō is directional and progressive: it builds from a foundation toward a completion. The characteristic is constitutively communal — it cannot be understood in individualistic terms.

### T1.2 — Kind

**T1.2.1** — Kind of inner-being phenomenon?

**E** — A relational-constitutive phenomenon — the inner reality of shared belonging. 1Jo 1:3 (Group 1055) — "so that you too may have fellowship with us" — koinōnia is something one "has" — a state of shared inner belonging, not merely an activity. 2Pe 1:4 (Group 1057) — "become partakers of the divine nature" — koinōnos is the inner reality of participation in what another is. The characteristic is therefore a condition of being-in-shared-relation, not merely an act or disposition.

**T1.2.2** — Simple or compound?

**E** — Compound. The 8-group structure reveals distinct registers: (1) vertical fellowship with God (Group 1055 — koinōnia with Father, Son, Spirit); (2) horizontal fellowship among believers with generous sharing (Group 1056 — koinōnia as community life and financial contribution); (3) inner-spiritual participation in divine realities and shared ministry (Group 1057 — koinōnos as sharer in divine nature, sufferings, comfort); (4) moral solidarity — participation constituting alignment with another's cause (Group 1058 — koinōnos as moral co-participation); (5) community unity of inner orientation (Group 1813 — homofrōn); (6) genuine shared concern (Group 1795 — isopsuchos); (7) reconciliation as restored fellowship (Group 2069 — katallagē); (8) formative building-up of the community (Group 2849 — epoikodomeō). These are not identical but aspects of a single underlying reality: shared participation in a common life.

**T1.2.3** — Best working description?

**E** — Fellowship and participation is the inner-relational bond of shared life — holding things in common, participating together in a shared reality (divine nature, sufferings, mission, community), belonging to one another through a mutually constitutive relation. Its vertical dimension is participation in the divine life (1Cor 1:9; 1Jo 1:3; 2Pe 1:4 — Groups 1055, 1057); its horizontal dimension is the community life of shared belonging, generous sharing, and mutual concern (Groups 1056, 1813, 1795). Its formative dimension is the building-up of the community on its Christological foundation (Group 2849). Its restorative dimension is the reconciliation that restores broken fellowship (Group 2069).

### T1.3 — Boundary

**T1.3.1** — Structural opposite?

**E** — Darkness and exclusion. 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055): "if we say we have fellowship with him while we walk in darkness, we lie" — darkness is the structural opposite of koinōnia with God. 2Cor 6:14 (Group 1056): "what fellowship has light with darkness?" — the darkness-light antithesis is the structural opposite of koinōnia. For katallagē, the structural opposite is the state of estrangement — the broken relationship before reconciliation (Rom 5:11; 11:15 — Group 2069). For epoikodomeō, the structural opposite is destruction or failure to build — the work that does not survive (1Cor 3:14 — Group 2849 context).

**T1.3.2** — What does the characteristic exclude or resist?

**E** — Participation with incompatibles: 1Cor 10:20 (Group 1057) — "I do not want you to be participants with demons" — koinōnos with darkness is explicitly excluded. 2Cor 6:14 (Group 1056) — "do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers" — the fellowship bond cannot span the light-darkness divide. Mat 23:30 (Group 1058) — the disavowal of moral participation in the killing of the prophets — the characteristic excludes unwanted moral solidarity with evil.

**T1.3.3** — What is this characteristic not?

**E** — Fellowship is not merely proximity or social co-presence: 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055) names the false claim to fellowship — one can claim fellowship while walking in darkness, which means the external social relationship is possible without the inner reality. Fellowship is not the same as friendship (M05-A/filos): friendship is love-oriented attachment; fellowship is shared-belonging-in-a-common-reality. The distinction: friendship emphasises the relational bond; fellowship emphasises the shared participation in something held in common.

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**T1.4.1** — Distinct modes?

**E** — Five modes emerge from the evidence: (1) **Vertical participation**: the person sharing in the divine life — koinōnia with Father, Son, Spirit (Groups 1055, 1057); (2) **Horizontal sharing**: the practical-financial and communal expression of the participation bond — contributions for the poor, koinōnia as generous sharing (Group 1056); (3) **Moral solidarity**: participation constituting alignment with another's cause, for good or ill (Group 1058 — Mat 23:30; Phile 17; Heb 10:33); (4) **Reconciliation**: the restoration of the broken participation bond (Group 2069); (5) **Formative building-up**: the ongoing construction of the fellowship community on its foundation (Group 2849).

**T1.4.2** — Differently depending on context?

**E** — Yes. The vertical mode (koinōnia with God) operates through calling and gift: "you were called into the fellowship of his Son" (1Cor 1:9 — Group 1055). The horizontal mode operates through choice and practice: "take part in the relief of the saints" (2Cor 8:4 — Group 1056). The moral solidarity mode operates through being-with: "partners with those so treated" (Heb 10:33 — Group 1058). The reconciliation mode operates through divine initiative and human reception: "God reconciled us to himself" (2Cor 5:18 — Group 2069). The formative mode operates through sustained investment: "building yourselves up in your most holy faith" (Jude 20 — Group 2849).

**T1.4.3** — Communicative or speech-based mode?

**E** — Yes, in two forms. (1) The proclamation of fellowship: 1Jo 1:3 (Group 1055) — "that which we have seen and heard we proclaim also to you, so that you too may have fellowship with us" — proclamation is the means by which fellowship is extended. (2) The message of reconciliation: 2Cor 5:19 (Group 2069) — "entrusting to us the message of reconciliation" — the reconciliation is communicated through the proclaimed message. Both the creation of koinōnia and the restoration of katallagē are mediated through speech.

**T1.5.1** — Immediate inner response to receiving this characteristic?

**E** — Joy: Rom 5:11 (Group 2069) — "we also rejoice in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now received reconciliation" — rejoicing is the immediate response to received reconciliation. 2Cor 1:7 (Group 1057) — "as you share in our sufferings, you will also share in our comfort" — sharing in comfort is the immediate inner consequence of fellowship-in-suffering. The person who enters koinōnia receives both sharing in suffering and sharing in comfort simultaneously.

**T1.5.2** — Consistent?

**E** — Consistent in direction — joy, comfort, and belonging are the immediate inner responses. The joy of Rom 5:11 (Group 2069) and the comfort of 2Cor 1:7 (Group 1057) are consistent as positive inner states produced by fellowship and reconciliation.

**T1.5.3** — If silent, note explicitly.

**S** — The immediate inner response to epoikodomeō (building-up) is not directly named. The effect is structural (the community is built up) rather than immediately experiential for the individual. This is a partial silence for the formative register.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**T1.6.1** — Sustained effect over time?

**E** — Transformation into the divine nature: 2Pe 1:4 (Group 1057) — "become partakers of the divine nature" — the sustained participation in the fellowship produces a constitutive inner transformation. Stability and establishment: Col 2:7 (Group 2849) — "rooted and built up in him and established in the faith" — the building-up produces an established, rooted community. Act 20:32 (Group 2849) — "the word of his grace... is able to build you up and to give you the inheritance" — building-up toward inheritance is the sustained trajectory.

**T1.6.2** — States, qualities, capacities established?

**E** — (1) Genuine concern for the other: Phili 2:20 (Group 1795) — the isopsuchos person "will be genuinely concerned for your welfare" — the fellowship-orientation produces authentic other-concern. (2) Unity of mind: 1Pe 3:8 (Group 1813) — homofrōn as the settled communal unity of inner orientation that fellowship produces. (3) Inheritance: Act 20:32 (Group 2849) — the inheritance among the sanctified is the eschatological completion of the building-up.

**T1.6.3** — Sustained effect differs from immediate response?

**E** — Yes. The immediate response is joy and comfort (entry into the fellowship); the sustained effect is transformation into the divine nature (2Pe 1:4 — Group 1057), communal unity of orientation (Group 1813), and eschatological inheritance (Group 2849). The immediate is relational and affective; the sustained is constitutive and eschatological.

### T1.7 — Conditions of Reception

**T1.7.1** — Conditions enabling reception?

**E** — Walking in the light: 1Jo 1:7 (Group 1055) — "if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship with one another" — the condition for ongoing koinōnia is light-walking. The "if" establishes the conditionality explicitly. Faithful partnership in the gospel: Phili 1:5 (Group 1056) — "because of your partnership in the gospel from the first day until now" — active participation in the shared mission is the ongoing condition.

**T1.7.2** — Blocking conditions?

**E** — Walking in darkness: 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055) — the blocking condition is darkness-walking while claiming fellowship. 2Cor 6:14 (Group 1056) — incompatibility between righteousness and lawlessness, light and darkness — the blocking condition is fundamental moral incompatibility.

**T1.7.3** — Non-receiving inner state?

**E** — The claim to fellowship without the reality (1Jo 1:6 — Group 1055) — lying and not practicing the truth. The person who claims fellowship while in darkness is in a state of self-deception. Estrangement is the pre-katallagē state: the person before reconciliation (Rom 5:11 — Group 2069) is in the state of enmity — the structural opposite of the fellowship-state.

### T1.8 — Dimension Classification

**T1.8.1** — Primary dimension?

**E** — Relational-communal: the characteristic is constitutively communal — it cannot exist in a single person but only in the shared life of two or more parties. The koinōnia vocabulary is by grammatical and semantic definition a relational category.

**T1.8.2** — Evidence?

**E** — 1Jo 1:3 (Group 1055): "fellowship with us... our fellowship is with the Father and his Son" — double horizontal and vertical relational reality. 1Pe 3:8 (Group 1813): "unity of mind, sympathy, brotherly love" — communal character list. 2Cor 5:19 (Group 2069): "reconciling the world to himself" — relational restoration. All primary anchors name a relational, communal reality.

**T1.8.3** — Secondary dimensions?

**E** — (1) Moral: moral solidarity (Group 1058) engages moral evaluation; the fellowship bond constitutes moral alignment. (2) Formative: epoikodomeō (Group 2849) is a formative-developmental phenomenon. (3) Eschatological: participation in eschatological glory (Group 1057 — 1Pe 5:1) and inheritance (Group 2849).

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

### T3.1 — Perception

**T3.1.1-3** — Perception engagement?

**E** — Partial. The initiation of fellowship through proclamation (1Jo 1:3 — Group 1055: "that which we have seen and heard we proclaim") grounds fellowship in the apostolic eyewitness perception — the seeing and hearing of the apostles is what enables the fellowship's transmission. But perception is the credential for proclamation rather than the ongoing mechanism of the fellowship itself.

**S** — Perception is a founding condition for the apostolic koinōnia (the witnesses saw and heard) but is not the primary ongoing faculty engagement of the characteristic. Silence on perception as a central faculty for this sub-group.

---

### T3.2 — Cognition

**T3.2.1** — Cognition engagement?

**E** — Yes, through homofrōn. G3675 homofrōn (Group 1813 — 1Pe 3:8): "unity of mind" — the frōn- root is the cognitive-evaluative faculty root. The community of shared mind is a cognitive alignment. 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055): "we lie and do not practice the truth" — the false fellowship claim involves a cognitive failure (lying, not truthing). Phile 6 (Group 1056): "the sharing of your faith may become effective for the full knowledge of every good thing that is in us" — the koinōnia of faith is oriented toward full knowledge — a cognitive outcome.

**T3.2.2** — Enable, deepen, bypass, or impair?

**E** — Deepens: genuine fellowship produces fuller knowledge — Phile 6 (Group 1056). The sharing of faith within koinōnia generates an expanding cognitive apprehension of the good. Homofrōn (Group 1813) establishes that cognitive unity is a fruit and mark of genuine fellowship.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

### T7.1 — Lexical and Semantic Analysis

**T7.1.1** — Primary terms and roots.

**E** — Two primary root families and three single terms. (1) **Koinōn-** root (G2842 koinōnia, G2844 koinōnos): ko-in-ōnia — the prefix koi- relates to the idea of "together" or "common." The root names the state of sharing a common reality, holding things in common, participating together. (2) **Katallag-** root (G2643 katallagē): from kata (down) + allassō (to change, exchange) — a changing-back, a restoration of the exchanged/broken relation. (3) **G3675 homofrōn**: homo (same) + frōn (thinking/mindedness) — unity of mental orientation. (4) **G2473 isopsuchos**: isos (equal, same) + psuchē (soul) — same-souled, sharing the same inner disposition. (5) **G2026 epoikodomeō**: epi (upon) + oikodomeō (to build a house) — building upon a foundation.

**T7.1.2** — Grammatical range?

**E** — Noun (koinōnia — the shared life as an abstraction; katallagē — the reconciliation as an act/state); person-type noun (koinōnos — the participant, the one who shares); adjective (homofrōn, isopsuchos — qualities of the community); verb (epoikodomeō — the ongoing building act). The vocabulary spans noun, person-type, adjective, and verb — a reasonably comprehensive range. The koinōnos person-type is significant: the characteristic generates a specific identity (the sharer/participant) as its primary human expression.

**T7.1.3** — RD-T713-001 applies. No G-code raised.

**T7.1.4** — Disposition vs. act, received vs. given?

**E** — Yes. Koinōnia names the state of shared participation (a condition rather than an act). Koinōnos names the person who participates (disposition as identity). Epoikodomeō names the building act (ongoing formative agency). Katallagē names the restored state (received) and the ministry (given). The vocabulary covers both the state (koinōnia) and the act (epoikodomeō), both the received form (katallagē restored bond) and the given form (katallagē ministry).

This is the person-type term for M05-G: the person who participates in the shared life, who is a partner in the common mission. 2Pe 1:4 (Group 1057) — "partakers of the divine nature" — the koinōnos as the person constituted by participation in divine life.

**T7.1.10** — Full vocabulary arc?

**E** — The vocabulary arc of M05-G moves from the abstract state (koinōnia — the fellowship bond) through the active participant (koinōnos — the person who shares) to the restored bond (katallagē — reconciliation) and the communal inner alignment (homofrōn, isopsuchos — the mind and soul-unity of genuine fellowship) and the formative building (epoikodomeō — the ongoing construction). ### T7.2 — Verse and Literary Interpretation

**T7.2.1** — Function of primary term in primary verse?

**E** — In 1Jo 1:3 (Group 1055 anchor: "that which we have seen and heard we proclaim also to you, so that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ"), koinōnia functions as the telos of proclamation — the purpose for which the apostolic witness is shared is that others might "have" fellowship. The term functions as: (1) the goal of mission; (2) the named inner-relational state that the mission creates; (3) the link between horizontal (with us) and vertical (with the Father and Son). The dual use in a single verse confirms the characteristic's simultaneous horizontal-vertical structure.

**T7.2.3** — Logical structure of key arguments?

**T7.2.6** — What does the primary anchor reveal uniquely?

The verse also uniquely presents the horizontal and vertical fellowship as simultaneously necessary and mutually constitutive — "our fellowship is with the Father and his Son" is offered as the ground and content of the horizontal fellowship being extended, not as a separate matter.

### T7.3 — Human Science Frameworks

**T7.3.1** — Most useful framework?

**E** — Three frameworks are relevant. (1) **Social psychology of community and belonging** (research on social belonging, group cohesion, community formation — Baumeister and Leary's "belonging hypothesis"): koinōnia corresponds directly to the social-psychological need for belonging — the inner need for genuine shared-belonging in a group that accepts the person as a valued member. (2) **Reconciliation psychology** (approaches to conflict resolution and restorative justice — Tutu, Gobodo-Madikizela): katallagē corresponds to psychological frameworks of deep reconciliation that go beyond mere cessation of hostility to restored relationship. (3) **Organizational development and community of practice theory** (Wenger, Lave): epoikodomeō's building-on-a-foundation corresponds to the "community of practice" model of how communities of shared life develop shared competencies, identities, and practices.

**T7.3.2** — Where the framework illuminates?

**E** — Belonging research illuminates the negative register of M05-G: 1Jo 1:6 (Group 1055) — false fellowship (claiming belonging while walking in darkness) corresponds to research findings on the psychological harm of pseudo-belonging and social exclusion. The research on the health consequences of isolation and pseudo-community (false belonging) makes the koinōnia/false-koinōnia distinction empirically concrete. Reconciliation psychology illuminates 2Cor 5:18-19 (Group 2069): the finding that genuine reconciliation requires a non-counting of trespasses (as God does) before restored relationship is possible corresponds to psychological research on forgiveness as the necessary precondition for reconciliation.

**T7.3.3** — Where the evidence diverges?

**E** — The sharpest divergence is in the basis of fellowship. Social psychology grounds belonging in human social need and group dynamics — the fellowship bond is a natural product of human social constitution. The verse evidence grounds koinōnia in divine calling (1Cor 1:9 — Group 1055), Spirit-constitution (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055), and Christological reconciliation (2Cor 5:18 — Group 2069). The human fellowship is not self-constituted but divinely established. The further divergence is in the scope of participation: 2Pe 1:4 (Group 1057) — "partakers of the divine nature" — has no psychological equivalent. No human science framework includes participation in divine nature as a category.

**T7.3.4** — Aspects not addressed by verse evidence?

**E** — Community psychology raises the question of what happens to the fellowship community when it fails — when the koinōnia bond breaks down through conflict, sin, or betrayal. The evidence addresses the katallagē (restoration) but does not elaborate the process of community rupture that requires katallagē. This is a gap for further verse investigation: what does the breakdown of koinōnia look like internally, and what is the inner-being state of the person whose fellowship has been ruptured?

## Cross-sub-group synthesis additions (M05-G contribution)

**New finding — fellowship as relational container:** M05-G is not merely one of seven parallel characteristics but the relational bond within which all the other M05 characteristics operate. The finding that participation constitutes moral alignment — to be a koinōnos with someone is to be morally aligned with their cause (Mat 23:30; Phile 17; Heb 10:33) — reveals that fellowship is not morally neutral association. Fellowship creates moral responsibility. This is a programme-level finding: the inner bond of shared participation carries moral weight and moral consequence.

The "fellowship of the Holy Spirit" (2Cor 13:14 — Group 1055) names the Spirit as constitutive of the fellowship's inner reality — not merely as the producer of the characteristic (as in M05-D's Spirit-produced kindness) but as the participant whose own fellowship is what is shared. **New finding — the demonic as a negative koinōnos register:** 1Cor 10:20 (Group 1057) — "I do not want you to be participants with demons" — is the only verse in M05 that explicitly names an adversarial spiritual being as a koinōnos of the human person. Fellowship (M05-G) is love's communal form — the shared bond of life that love creates and sustains. M05-G is not the final characteristic in a sequence but the communal context that encompasses all the others: the seven sub-groups do not operate as separate individual virtues but as aspects of the single shared life that the Spirit constitutes in Christ.
