### 3.4 Key cluster-level findings established

**Constitutional location**
The heart is the dominant constitutional home across all M01 sub-groups, explicitly evidenced in M01-A (Deu 5:29; Jer 32:40), M01-B (Psa 27:3; 1Sa 28:5), M01-D (Job 23:16), M01-E (Job 37:1), M01-F (Deu 28:67; Pro 12:25), and M01-G (Joh 14:27). Spirit-level location is confined to two points in the entire 941-verse corpus: Isa 66:2 (trembling at God's word located in the spirit, M01-E-VCG-02) and 2Ti 1:7 (timidity as a spirit-level disposition not from God, M01-G-VCG-01). Complete silence on spirit-level location across M01-A, B, C, D, and F.

**Primary operating theatre**
The volitional faculty is fear's primary operating theatre, engaged across all seven sub-groups. Fear governs, channels, overrides, paralyses, dissolves, or redirects the will in every sub-group without exception.

**Ordered vs. disordered structural asymmetry**
A consistent asymmetry runs through every T3 faculty: ordered fear (M01-A reverential fear of God) enables, deepens, or integrates each inner faculty; disordered fear (M01-B severe, C, D, F, G) impairs, bypasses, distorts, or dissolves each. This asymmetry is the cluster's most structurally significant analytical pattern.

**Fear-of-God as the constitutive interior moral faculty**
Psa 36:1 (absence of fear as fundamental moral blindness), Rom 3:18, and the Lev 19 corpus (fear as the operative moral restraint where no human court sees) confirm that reverential fear of God is not merely adjacent to morality but is its interior ground. Social fear (M01-B-VCG-04) is identified as the primary suppressor of this faculty.

**Constitutional structure of the cluster**
Seven sub-groups are not seven kinds of fear but seven constitutive dimensions of a single characteristic: orientation (M01-A), acute event (M01-B), force (M01-C), inner collapse (M01-D), somatic expression (M01-E), temporal duration/anticipation (M01-F), and moral evaluation (M01-G). The cluster's unity is the inner person's registration of power and the volitional impact that registration produces.

**Eschatological fullness**
The cluster's eschatological fullness is: universal, perfected, permanent reverential fear of God (M01-A, Rev corpus — Rev 14:7; 15:4; 19:5), combined with complete freedom from every disordered fear (M01-B-VCG-06's "do not fear" commands reaching their eschatological completion; Heb 2:15 deliverance from fear-of-death slavery; 1Jo 4:18 perfect love casting out all punishment-anticipatory fear; Eze 34:28 "none shall make them afraid").

**Angelic mediation**
Angels are the primary interface through which divine holiness makes itself felt in the human inner life in the fear register — both producing theophanic fear (Luk 2:9; Mat 28:4) and commanding its release (Luk 1:13; 1:30; 2:10; Mat 28:5). This is the cluster's most concentrated instance of spiritual beings operating in the human-divine relational interface.

**Science framework alignment and divergence**
Science illuminates M01 at three specific points: Damasio's somatic marker theory (making M01-E's dense somatic vocabulary constitutive rather than merely expressive), LeDoux's subcortical-bypass model (explaining M01-B's "without deliberation" flight pattern), and Borkovec's worry model (mapping onto de.a.gah's cognitive-ruminating anticipatory anxiety). The primary divergence: science cannot adjudicate the normativity of fear of God (categorically right in Scripture, functionally assessed in science) and has no category for a commanded affective disposition that is simultaneously a virtue.

**BOUNDARY characterisation**
Of 12 BOUNDARY terms: 4 have empty inner-being corpora (za.a.vah, a.ruts, pa.lats, sham.mah — formal set-aside recommended at Phase 12); 3 have viable content for re-routing within M01 (ke.ra → M01-D; sar.ap.pim → M01-F; ademoneo → M01-D or M03); 3 belong in a separate non-M01 cluster (ta.mah, thambos → M24 astonishment/wonder; sha.vats → dying/agony cluster); 2 warrant further researcher decision (mish.bar, a.qah).

## 5. Thinking and debate log

### Phase 9 — Pre-writing cross-group analysis

Before writing any Phase 9 finding, a full cross-group read of the grouped report was conducted. Ten pre-writing pattern observations were noted in the obslog:

1. Seven sub-groups as constitutive dimensions rather than seven kinds of fear.
2. Spirit-level silence (only Isa 66:2 and 2Ti 1:7) — highly significant.
3. Heart as the dominant constitutional home (most consistently evidenced location).
4. Somatic density of M01-E (98 verses, richest in Scripture's emotional register per science extract).
5. Volitional faculty as the primary operating theatre across all sub-groups.
6. Faith/trust as M01-B's structural opposite (Mar 4:40; 5:36; 1Jo 4:18).
7. Fear-of-God as explicitly learnable, transmissible, and divinely implantable (Deu 14:23; Jer 32:40).
8. God uniquely active as both the object and producer of fear across the cluster.
9. Hebrew-Greek continuity with NT development (christological intensification, discriminatory development of servile vs. filial fear).
10. Complete silence on God possessing fear as an inner experience (set-aside of Deu 32:27 confirmed).

These observations anchored the cluster-scope entries throughout Phase 9 and drove the systematic analytical discipline of asking for every E-coded finding: what specific verse or VCG codes this?

### Phase 9 — Science framework (T7.3) decisions

Three decisions required explicit care:

(a) Normativity divergence: the framing that science cannot adjudicate the fittingness of fear of God was stated directly at T7.3.3 rather than softened. The commanded-virtue character of M01-A has no scientific category equivalent, and this was named plainly.

(b) Porges freeze-response mapping to M01-D: the dorsal vagal shutdown pattern (immobility, colour changes, muscle weakness, loss of coordinated speech) maps onto Dan 5:6 and Job 32:15 directly. This was used as illuminating without replacing the theological description.

(c) Damasio somatic marker theory and M01-E: the observation that M01-E's somatic vocabulary is constitutive rather than merely expressive (the body's state is integral to the fear-experience, not just its expression) was used as a genuine illumination — it makes the density of M01-E's vocabulary analytically significant rather than merely illustrative.

### Phase 10 — Key disposition reasoning

The most deliberate decision in Phase 10 was sbf.id=38 (R011 awe — DIM-11-001). This finding had the strongest argument for M01-relevance since it comes from the awe registry (the nearest neighbour to M01-A) and makes a claim directly about the God-ward orientation of fear/awe content. The decision to RESOLVE rather than CARRY-TO-SESSION-D was grounded in the finding's substance being demonstrably addressed by Phase 9: T1.8.1 [A] (affective-relational primary), T1.8.3 [A] (secondary dimensions integrate rather than compete), T3.4.1 [A] (reverence deepens rather than suppresses affect — Psa 2:11; Heb 12:28), and T0.3.1 [A] (God-ward relational orientation as constitutive of M01-A's image-bearing function). The finding's analytical claim is in the catalogue.
