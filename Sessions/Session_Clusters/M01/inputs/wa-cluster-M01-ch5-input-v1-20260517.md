**T1 — Definition**

- *T1.1.1* — _What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?_
  → M01-B is called "Acute Fear and Alarm." "Acute" signals the episodic, situationally-triggered character — the fear arises at a moment and addresses a specific stimulus. "Alarm" adds the reactive, involuntary dimension. The name distinguishes B sharply from A: where A is chronic and orienting, B is acute and responsive.

  → M01-B introduces: *de.chal* (H1763 Aramaic — fear/dread), *za.chal* (H2119B — to shrink in fear), *ya.gor* (H3016 — to dread, fear specific objects), *ekthambeo* (G1568 — to be amazed/alarmed), *ekthambos* (G1569), *ekfobos* (G1630 — terrified), *emfobos* (G1719 — afraid, alarmed), *ptoeō* (G4422 — to terrify/frighten), *ptoēsis* (G4423), *pturomai* (G4426 — to be startled/alarmed).
