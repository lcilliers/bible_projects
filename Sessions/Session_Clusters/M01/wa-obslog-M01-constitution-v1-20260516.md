### Phase 5 analytical working — sub-group formation

Reading all meanings in sequence. Clustering by what the inner-being content evidences — not by term glosses, not by inherited structure.

**Observation 1 — The dominant axis is not intensity but orientation.**
Reading across the ~948 meanings, the primary structural distinction that emerges is not how strong the fear is (mild/moderate/intense) but where it is directed: toward God, toward human threats, or toward divine action perceived as threatening. This is the axis that actually organises the corpus.

**Observation 2 — Fear-of-God forms a coherent, large sub-group.**
H3372G ya.re (191 verses), H3372H ya.re-revere (113), H3373 ya.re-afraid (64), H3374 yir.ah (43), H4172A mo.rah (11), H4172B mo.ra (11), G5399 fobeō (83 verses — the God-reverence strand), G5401 fobos (43 — the reverence strand), G6015 deos (1), H6343 pa.chad (48 — the "Fear of Isaac" and reverence material), H6345 pach.dah (1), gur (10 — the awe strand at Psa 22:23; 33:8). Meanings consistently describe a sustained, orienting inner posture of reverential awe toward God — not reactive alarm but a governing disposition of the inner life.

But importantly: this same word family (ya.re, pa.chad, fobeō) ALSO appears in acute-fear contexts. The words themselves are multi-faceted — their corpora span sub-groups. The question is whether the *meanings* cluster, not whether the *terms* cluster.

**Observation 3 — What the meanings actually show when read without the term labels.**
Reading the meanings without attending to which term they belong to, several clearly different inner-being phenomena emerge:

**Cluster A — Sustained reverential awe / Fear of God as governing orientation:**
Meanings that describe fear-of-God as an enduring inner disposition that governs conduct, worship, and covenant relationship. Not reactive — constitutive. E.g.: "reverential fear is the foundational inner disposition from which wisdom begins" (yir.ah, Pro 1:7); "fear of God as the motivating core of a devout life" (fobeō, Act 10:2); "fear is the inner disposition that qualifies a person to receive God's provision" (ya.re, Psa 111:5); "Levi feared God as the living covenantal response" (mo.rah, Mal 2:5). This is a large, coherent corpus.

**Cluster B — Acute fear-alarm before threat or the impossible:**
Meanings that describe sudden, reactive inner fear triggered by danger, the unexpected, or the supernatural — not a sustained orientation but an acute crisis-response. E.g.: "the inner being is overwhelmed and filled to capacity by terror at the supernatural presence" (fobeō, Luk 2:9); "the disciples are filled with great fear after Jesus calms the storm" (fobos, Mar 4:41); "the disciples' terror at the transfiguration is so overwhelming that they fall prostrate" (fobeō, Mat 17:6); "Saul's fear of Goliath so overwhelms him that he and all Israel flee" (ya.re, 1Sa 17:24); "the Moabites' overwhelming, paralyzing dread of Israel" (gur, Num 22:3).

**Cluster C — Terror as an overwhelming force / terror that encircles and destroys:**
Meanings describing terror as an active, assaulting force — often projected by God or projected by powerful entities onto their opponents. Not the person's response alone, but terror as a force that comes upon and overwhelms. E.g.: "terrors surround the wicked man on every side and pursue him relentlessly" (bal.la.hah, Job 18:11); "terror encircling on every side" (ma.gor, Jer 20:10); "God's terror sent as an advance weapon against enemies" (e.mah, Exo 23:27); "terror as the power Assyria wielded over the living" (chit.tit, Eze 32:23); "God-sent terror fell upon surrounding cities, paralyzing their will" (chit.tah, Gen 35:5); "divinely imposed dread seizes the inner being of distant nations" (pa.chad, Deu 2:25).

**Cluster D — Dismay / inner collapse under shock:**
Meanings describing a sudden inner shattering of composure — the collapse of the inner person when confronted with the impossible, catastrophic news, or overwhelming divine action. Distinguished from acute fear-alarm by its specific quality of *dissolution of the inner person's coherence*. E.g.: "the brothers are dismayed at Joseph's self-revelation and cannot answer" (ba.hal, Gen 45:3); "Belshazzar's inner person is struck so violently that his color changes and limbs give way" (be.hal, Dan 5:6); "dismay causes total inner collapse and desertion" (cha.tat, Isa 31:9); "Isaac's great trembling names the intensity of his inner shock when he discovers deception" (cha.ra.dah, Gen 27:33); "the psalmist's soul itself is greatly dismayed — the inner being, not just the body, is the seat of profound turmoil" (ba.hal, Psa 6:3).

**Cluster E — Trembling / the somatic expression of fear:**
Meanings describing involuntary bodily trembling as the physical expression of fear. Not the same as acute alarm (which focuses on the inner state) — trembling foregrounds the body's response. Often paired with other fear states but distinct in its somatic emphasis. E.g.: "the trembling is an involuntary bodily expression of inner shock" (cha.rad, Gen 27:33); "Moses trembled and did not dare look when confronted with God's self-identification — bodily trembling paired with inner paralysis" (entromos, Act 7:32); "trembling seizes the women together with astonishment as they flee the tomb" (tromos, Mar 16:8); "trembling at God's word is the inner posture God looks for, located in the spirit" (cha.red, Isa 66:2); "shuddering takes hold of Job's flesh the moment memory brings dismay" (pal.la.tsut, Job 21:6).

**Cluster F — Dread/anticipatory fear / ongoing anxiety:**
Meanings describing sustained inner dread that is forward-looking — dread of what is coming, anticipatory anguish, anxiety about the future. Not acute reaction but chronic orientation of worry. E.g.: "anxiety weighs down a man's heart — the inner seat is specifically the heart, and anxiety acts as a burden" (de.a.gah, Pro 12:25); "anxiety so saturates the inner person that even basic acts cannot be done without it" (de.a.gah, Eze 12:18); "Job names a dreaded thing that sat within him as an anticipated fear, and that dread has now materialized" (ya.gor, Job 3:25); "continual dread without assurance of life is a covenantal curse — unrelenting, consuming night and day" (pa.chad, Deu 28:66-67); "fear of death held people in lifelong inner slavery" (fobos, Heb 2:15).

**Cluster G — Timidity / cowardly fear / faithless inner disposition:**
Meanings describing fear as an inner *failure of constitution* — the disposition of cowardly shrinking that is condemned as moral weakness, failure of trust, or lack of faith. Distinct from acute alarm or sustained reverence — this is fear as a character defect. E.g.: "timidity is a spirit-level disposition God has not implanted, making it a failure of inner constitution" (deilia, 2Ti 1:7); "inner timidity or cowardly shrinking — commanding the heart not to yield to fearful recoiling" (deiliaō, Joh 14:27); "cowardice heads a list of vices that share the lake of fire — likely denoting apostasy under pressure" (deilos, Rev 21:8); "fear of man is an inner trap — a trembling anxiety before people that ensnares the person" (cha.ra.dah, Pro 29:25); "the servant's fear drove him to bury the talent — paralysing the will" (fobeō, Mat 25:25).

**Observation 4 — Multi-faceted terms.**
Several terms span multiple sub-groups clearly:
- **fobeō** (G5399, mti_id=292, 83 verses): primarily in A (reverence) and B (acute alarm), with G (timidity at Mat 25:25) and F (anticipatory dread at 2Cor 12:20; Gal 4:11)
- **fobos** (G5401, mti_id=266, 43 verses): primarily in A (reverence strand) and B (acute alarm), with C (terror as force at Rom 13:3)
- **ya.re/H3372G** (mti_id=298, 191 verses): primarily in A (reverence) and B (acute alarm), with G (the fearful who should not fear)
- **ya.re/H3373** (mti_id=1681, 64 verses): primarily in A (reverence) and B (acute alarm)
- **pa.chad-verb** (H6342, mti_id=291, 25 verses): primarily F (anticipatory dread) and A (reverence at Psa 119:161), with B (acute alarm)
- **pa.chad-noun** (H6343, mti_id=829, 48 verses): primarily C (terror as force — the "dread of the Lord" that falls on nations) and A (reverence — "Fear of Isaac")
- **cha.rad** (H2729, mti_id=305, 34 verses): primarily E (trembling) and B (acute alarm)
- **ba.hal** (H0926, mti_id=92, 33 verses): primarily D (dismay/collapse), with B and E
- **ra.gaz** (H7264, mti_id=1554, 21 verses): primarily E (trembling) with B and some F (inner agitation/distress)
- **gur** (H1481C, mti_id=290, 10 verses): primarily A (reverential awe at Psa 22:23; 33:8) and B (threat-dread at Num 22:3; Deu 1:17)

**Observation 5 — Cluster E (trembling) needs consideration.**
The trembling terms (cha.rad, cha.red, cha.ra.dah, entromos, tromos, re.a.dah, ra.gaz, ra.ad, ze.va.ah, rag.gaz, rog.zah, pal.la.tsut, sa.ar-verb, sa.ar-noun) describe the somatic dimension of fear. They are not a different inner-being *characteristic* from fear — trembling IS the expression of fear, not a separate phenomenon. But they do form a coherent corpus around the body's role in fear and specifically around the "trembling at God's word" tradition (cha.red, Isa 66). The question is whether trembling is a distinct enough phenomenon to be its own sub-group or whether it should be distributed across A, B, C, D based on what is causing the trembling. Reading the meanings: the trembling corpus has enough distinctive content (particularly the "trembling at God's word" community identity at Isa 66:2-5; Ezr 9-10; and the bodily-somatic dimension as a distinct register) to warrant its own sub-group. It is the physiological-expressive dimension of fear.

**Observation 6 — Social fear as a sub-group consideration.**
Several meanings describe fear directed specifically at human social pressure: fear of crowds governing rulers (fobeō, Mat 21:26; 21:46; Luk 20:19; 22:2; Mar 11:18; 11:32; 12:12), fear of the Jews governing behavior (fobos, Joh 7:13; 19:38; 20:19), fear of the people governing Saul's disobedience (fobeō, 1Sa 15:24). This social-fear register could be a sub-group but on reading it is actually a *variant of* acute fear-alarm (Cluster B) — the trigger is social rather than physical or supernatural, but the inner-being phenomenon is the same: acute fear governing the will. It does not require a separate sub-group.

**Final sub-group structure emerging from meanings:**

- **M01-A: Reverential Fear / Fear of God as governing orientation** — the sustained inner disposition of awe toward God that governs conduct, worship, and covenant
- **M01-B: Acute Fear and Alarm** — reactive fear before threat, danger, the impossible, or the supernatural; fear that strikes suddenly and overwhelms the will
- **M01-C: Terror as Overwhelming Force** — terror as an active assault on the inner person, often externally imposed or projected; the encircling, hunting, paralyzing quality of dread
- **M01-D: Dismay and Inner Collapse** — the sudden shattering of inner composure; shock that dissolves coherence and disables the person
- **M01-E: Trembling / Fear Made Somatic** — the involuntary bodily expression of fear; trembling, shuddering, quivering as the physiological register of fear
- **M01-F: Anticipatory Dread and Anxiety** — sustained forward-looking dread; the chronic inner burden of anxiety about threat, judgment, or disaster
- **M01-G: Timidity and Cowardly Shrinking** — fear as inner constitutional failure; cowardly recoiling that is condemned as faithlessness or moral weakness
- **M01-BOUNDARY** — 12 terms with empty corpora or unresolved analytical questions

#### M01-A VCG design (329 verses)

**A-VCG-05: Fear of God commanded away from human and false objects (fear directed correctly)**

**A-VCG-06: Fear of God as reverential conduct — worship, prayer, vows, and devotion**

**A-VCG-07: The fear of God that is learned, transmitted, and implanted**

#### M01-B VCG design (311 verses)

Reading the 311 meanings in M01-B, the following distinct phenomena emerge:

**B-VCG-01: Fear before the divine presence — theophany and the supernatural**
Meanings describing fear as the immediate involuntary inner response to encountering God, angels, or supernatural reality — the awe-terror of confronting what transcends natural categories. Gen 15:12 (e.mah descends on Abram); Exo 34:30 (fear of Moses's radiant face); Judg 6:23 (Gideon's terror at seeing the Lord face-to-face); Mat 17:6 (disciples prostrated by the Transfiguration); Luk 2:9 (shepherds overwhelmed by angelic glory); Act 10:4 (Cornelius staring in terror at angel); Luk 24:37 (disciples terrified thinking they see a ghost); Rev 1:17 (John collapsed as dead before the glorified Christ); Act 7:32 (Moses trembled before God's self-identification); Heb 12:21 (ekfobos — Moses terrified at Sinai); Mar 9:6 (ekfobos — terror disabling speech at Transfiguration); emfobos at Luk 24:5.

**B-VCG-02: Fear before divine power displayed in miracle or judgment**
Meanings describing awe-struck fear as the immediate community response to witnessing divine power at work in healing, exorcism, nature-control, or resurrection — the involuntary communal inner grip of reverential dread that follows a miracle. Mat 27:54 (centurion's confession-fear at signs at the cross); Mar 4:41 / Luk 8:25 (fear at Jesus calming the storm); Mar 5:15 / Luk 8:35 (crowd's fear at restored demoniac); Luk 5:26 (awe filling all at the healing of the paralysed man); Luk 7:16 (fear at the widow's son raised); Luk 8:37 (Gerasene fear at the exorcism); Act 5:5; 5:11 (fear at Ananias's death); Act 19:17 (Ephesian fear at defeat of exorcists); Rev 11:11 (fear at resurrection of the witnesses); Rev 11:13 (survivors terrified by the earthquake).

**B-VCG-03: Fear of human threat — military, political, and social danger**
Meanings describing the reactive inner dread triggered by specific human threats: armies, powerful rulers, social opposition, political danger, and hostile persons. This is threat-fear at the human level — distinct from divine encounter. Num 22:3 (Moab overwhelmed by Israel's numbers); Deu 20:1-3 (soldiers commanded not to fear a superior army); multiple military contexts throughout OT (Jos 8:1; 9:24; 10:2; Deu 1:21; 3:2; 3:22); Neh 2:2 (Nehemiah afraid before the king); Neh 6:9-14 (fear used as a weapon against Nehemiah's workers); 1Sa 17:11; 17:24 (Israel's fear at Goliath); 2Sa 3:11 (Ish-bosheth silenced by fear of Abner); Jer 22:25; Jer 39:17 (ya.gor — fear of specific dangerous men). Also: Daniel officials and kings fearing other powerful figures (de.chal corpus: Dan 5:19 — fear before Nebuchadnezzar's absolute power).

**B-VCG-04: Social fear — fear of crowd opinion and public reaction**
A distinct and coherent sub-register within M01-B where the fear is specifically directed at social consequences — crowd opinion, peer pressure, public exposure, or social exclusion. Mat 14:5 (Herod's fear of the crowd restraining his will); Mat 21:26; 21:46; Mar 11:18; 11:32; 12:12 (leaders' social fear governing their responses to Jesus); Luk 20:19; 22:2 (leaders prevented from acting by fear of the people); Joh 7:13; 19:38; 20:19 (fear of the Jews governing speech and behavior); Gal 2:12 (Peter's social fear driving withdrawal); 1Sa 15:24 (Saul confesses that fear of the people drove his disobedience); Joh 9:22 (parents' fear of Jewish authority governing their speech).

**B-VCG-05: Fear of God's judgment or future calamity — fear as the appropriate sober response**
Meanings where fear of God is commanded or appropriate but with an acute, episodic character (not the chronic reverent orientation of M01-A): the response to perceiving divine judgment about to fall, or the commanded sobriety of fearing God as the avenger. Mat 10:28 (fear God who can destroy body and soul in hell); Rom 13:3-4 (fear of governing authority as God's avenger); Dan 6:26 (de.chal — fear decreed for all peoples before the God of Daniel); Dan 7:7; 7:19 (de.chal — fear at the terrifying beasts of the vision); Luk 12:5 (fear God alone — acute redirecting command); Heb 4:1 (godly alertness lest one fall short of rest); 1Ti 5:20 (fear as deterrent in the community when sin is rebuked publicly); 2Cor 7:11 (fear as one of the intense responses to godly grief).

**B-VCG-06: Fear addressed and countered by God or his messengers**
Meanings where the primary content is "do not fear" — the command to cease fear, with the grounds given (divine presence, divine promise, divine power). This is a coherent corpus because the meaning in these verses is the inner fear being named, addressed, and countered. Luk 1:13 (Zechariah); Luk 1:30 (Mary); Luk 2:10 (shepherds); Gen 15:1 (Abram); Gen 21:17 (Hagar); Gen 26:24 (Isaac); Gen 46:3 (Jacob); Judg 6:23 (Gideon); 2Ki 6:16 (Elisha's servant); Mat 14:27; 28:5; 28:10; Joh 6:20; Rev 1:17; and the major Isaiah "fear not" corpus (Isa 41:10; 41:13; 41:14; 43:1; 43:5; 44:2). Note: many of these are already in M01-B; the VCG groups the "fear-countered" pattern specifically.
