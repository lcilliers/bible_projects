## M02-A-VCG-01 — Wrath as Covenantal Oath and Binding Consequence

**Context description:** Anger understood as the inner divine disposition that locks in binding consequences — covenantal judgments, sworn oaths, and definitive exclusions that result when wrath is fully aroused. The verses in this VCG consistently present wrath not as an eruptive emotion but as a settled inner verdict that issues in irrevocable outcomes: God's wrath grounds an oath that permanently excludes the wilderness generation from rest (Heb 3:11; 4:3); his anger at Moses results in Moses being barred from the promised land (Deu 1:37; 4:21); his anger at sin produces exile as a judicial consequence when it is finally activated (1Ki 8:46; 2Ch 6:36); wrath functions as the climactic force behind covenantal displacement (Deu 29:28; qe.tseph). The inner-being characteristic is wrath as settled moral verdict with binding, covenantal weight — an inner disposition that, when reached, closes relational and territorial access permanently or until specified conditions are met.

## M02-A-VCG-02 — Wrath as Eschatological and Future Judgment

**Context description:** Anger understood as an impending, future-oriented divine force from which people must flee, be rescued, or be delivered. The verses in this VCG present wrath not as a past historical event but as a coming reality whose imminence motivates urgency: people flee it through repentance (Mat 3:7; Luk 3:7), believers are delivered from it by Christ (1Th 1:10; 5:9; Rom 5:9), it is the destiny of those outside covenant (Eph 2:3; Eph 5:6; Col 3:6), and it reaches its supreme expression in Revelation as cosmic judgment (Rev 6:16–17; 14:10; 16:19; 19:15). The inner-being characteristic is wrath as a future-facing judicial force — a mounting, approaching reality that creates urgency in the human inner person and defines the poles of salvation and condemnation.

## M02-A-VCG-03 — Wrath as Active Judicial Force Against Ungodliness

**Context description:** Anger understood as an active, revealed, ongoing divine force that operates against human sin, ungodliness, and moral failure — not yet as eschatological finality but as a present or historically real enacted judgment. The verses in this VCG present wrath as: descending from heaven against sin now (Rom 1:18); accumulating against the hard heart (Rom 2:5); the inevitable outcome of self-seeking rebellion (Rom 2:8); the law's product (Rom 4:15); a historical judgment already falling on those who hinder the gospel (1Th 2:16); channeled through human institutions (Rom 13:4); and struck against communities for apostasy (2Ch 24:18; 29:8; Jer 10:10; 50:13; Zec 7:12). The inner-being characteristic is wrath as operative judicial force — the ongoing judicial dimension of divine anger that holds human sin accountable, enacted through natural law, historical event, and institutional authority.

## M02-A-VCG-04 — Human Anger as Moral Response and Authority Offense

**Context description:** Anger expressed by human persons — leaders, prophets, officials — as a morally grounded inner response to perceived disobedience, offense, or injustice, distinct from the divine wrath that dominates this sub-group. The verses in this VCG present human anger as: a moral response to willful disobedience (Exo 16:20; Lev 10:16; Num 31:14); an inner reaction that drives punitive authority (Gen 40:2; 41:10; Est 1:12; 2:21); wounded expectation (2Ki 5:11; Naaman); prophetic indignation at moral failure (2Ki 13:19; Elisha); rationally grounded strategic distrust (1Sa 29:4); and a human tendency requiring governance (1Ti 2:8; Jam 1:19–20). The inner-being characteristic is the human experience of anger in the register of moral authority — anger as a response to violations of what is right, proper, or owed.

## M02-A-VCG-05 — Wrath's Duration, Relenting, and Restoration

**Context description:** Anger understood in relation to its own temporal arc — the inner disposition of wrath as it persists, exhausts itself, relents, or gives way to mercy. The verses in this VCG focus specifically on the duration and trajectory of divine anger: psalms questioning whether anger will last forever (Psa 79:5; 85:5); the promise that God's anger turns away and comfort replaces it (Isa 12:1); the declaration that wrath will never be sustained indefinitely (Isa 57:16); wrath as a momentary surge contrasted with everlasting love (Isa 54:8); Israel's confession that wrath has been the context of their estrangement (Isa 64:5); and God's swearing that past anger is now permanently renounced (Isa 54:9). The inner-being characteristic is wrath as a temporal phenomenon — a divine inner disposition with a beginning, a duration, and a trajectory toward either exhaustion or relenting, which becomes the ground of petition, hope, and restoration.

**Member verses:**

| vc_id | Reference | Term | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 98 | Joh 3:36 | orgē | Anchor — wrath as settled, abiding condition contrasted with eternal life |
| 64615 | Psa 60:1 | a.naph | Anger experienced as withdrawal of divine protection, pleaded to be reversed |
| 1815 | Isa 12:1 | a.naph | (also VCG-01) Anger turned away, replaced by comfort |
| 64604 | Rev 6:17 | orgē | (also VCG-02) Irresistibility of wrath — none can stand |

**Note on VCG-05:** After careful reading, the temporal-arc verses are distributed across VCG-01 (covenantal exclusion as durable consequence) and VCG-04 (God's anger that can relent when conditions are met). Rather than forcing a thin standalone VCG-05, these verses are better distributed:

- Psa 79:5; 85:5; a.naph duration questions → VCG-01 (covenantal duration is the issue)
- Isa 12:1; Isa 54:8; 54:9; Isa 57:16 → VCG-03 (wrath's enacted force and its cessation)
- Joh 3:36 → best placed as the definitional complement to VCG-02 (settled abiding condition as the eschatological wrath-state)

**Revised decision:** Joh 3:36 is the anchor for a minimal temporal-contrast VCG. The verses that speak of wrath relenting (Isa 12:1; 54:8; 54:9; 57:16; 64:5; 64:9) are distributed to VCG-01 and VCG-03 respectively. VCG-05 covers the settled-abiding-wrath-state specifically as the sustained condition of those who reject the Son:

| vc_id | Reference | Term | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 98 | Joh 3:36 | orgē | Anchor — settled abiding wrath as ongoing condition contrasted with eternal life |

**Note:** This VCG is intentionally thin (1 verse) because Joh 3:36's meaning is specifically unique — wrath not as an event but as an ongoing existential condition resting on a person. This does not map cleanly to any other VCG in M02-A. If CC validation shows this is too thin for a standalone VCG, Joh 3:36 may be merged into VCG-02 (eschatological) with a note that it represents the realized/ongoing dimension of eschatological wrath.

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 98 (Joh 3:36 / orgē)

**Dual-membership:** None in this VCG.
