## M02-F-VCG-01 — Quarrelsome Disposition as Inner Character Flaw

**Context description:** Anger as a settled inner disposition toward contention — not a momentary eruption but a habitual orientaton of the inner person toward dispute, quarreling, and conflict. The verses in this VCG present: dispute-loving as an inner inclination that is named and resisted (1Cor 11:16), quarreling as an inner disposition of the corrupt person filling those given to sin (Rom 1:29), strife as a work of the flesh — an inner drive of the sinful nature (Gal 5:20), quarrels arising from an unhealthy inner craving for controversy (1Ti 6:4), word-quarrels driven by conceit and pride (1Ti 6:4 logomachia), quarrels about the law arising from foolish inner preoccupations (Tit 3:9), and Herod's quarrelsome fury as a hostile inner disposition driving political conflict (Act 12:20). The inner-being characteristic is contentious anger as a dispositional feature — a settled inner will toward quarrel, dispute, and relational conflict that characterises the person who has it.

## M02-F-VCG-02 — Strife as Inner-Relational Contention

**Context description:** Anger expressed as the relational atmosphere and outworking of inner contention — strife as the condition present in relationships when uncontrolled anger and prideful self-will go unchecked. The verses in this VCG present: strife rooted in insolence and proud inner refusal to submit (Pro 13:10), strife as an inner appetite that is morally aligned with sin (Pro 17:19), strife and fighting as the corrupt inner motive behind religious performance (Isa 58:4). The inner-being characteristic is anger as the relational-atmosphere output of inner pride and self-will — the strife that results when the inner disposition of quarrelsome anger goes unchallenged in relationship.

| 38084 | Pro 17:19 | mats.tsah | Strife as inner appetite willfully oriented toward conflict — morally aligned with sin |
| 38086 | Isa 41:12 | mats.tsut | Strife between adversaries rendered futile by divine intervention |

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 38085 (Isa 58:4 / mats.tsah)
Rationale: This verse exposes strife as an inner-motive phenomenon — the combative will underlying a religious act, making the act void before God. The inner-being characteristic of strife as a corrupting inner motive is named here most directly: not strife as a behaviour but strife as what the inner person is really doing underneath a pious surface.
