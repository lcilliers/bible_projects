### Design rationale — sub-group structure

Reading the corpora across all 43 terms, the following analytical clusters emerged from the meanings:

**M02-A — Divine Wrath as Judicial Force**
Rationale: A substantial body of verses — particularly the orgē (G3709, 33 verses), qe.tseph (H7110A, 28 verses), and a.naph (H0599, 14 verses) corpora — present anger not primarily as erupting heat but as a settled, purposeful force with covenantal, legal, and eschatological structure. The orgē corpus alone runs from Mat 3:7 (flee the wrath to come) through Rev 19:15 (winepress of final judgment), with judicial wrath at Rom 1:18; 4:15; 13:4–5; Heb 3:11; 4:3. This is a phenomenologically distinct register from burning heat. Terms: orgē, qe.tseph, qe.tsaph, a.naph, qa.tsaph, re.gaz, plus za.am (primary C, secondary A).

**M02-B — Burning Rage and Inner Heat**
Rationale: The largest sub-group by verse count, anchored on che.mah (H2534, 110 verses) and cha.rah (H2734, 87 verses). These corpora consistently evidence anger as thermal force: kindling (Gen 4:5; Exo 32:10), filling (Est 3:5; Dan 3:19), pouring out (Eze 7:8; Jer 42:18), burning (Exo 15:7; Psa 89:46), subsiding (Gen 27:44; Est 2:1). cha.ron (39 verses) is exclusively burning-anger register. cho.ri (5 verses), zal.a.phah (1), za.aph verb+noun, za.eph all share this heat phenomenology. orgizō placed here (8 verses) because its corpus is dominantly about the erupting state of anger driving action. Largest sub-group ~265 verses.

**M02-C — Indignation and Moral Displeasure**
Rationale: A quieter register distinct from both heat and judicial weight. za.am verb (H2194, 10 verses) presents indignation as a constant moral stance (Psa 7:11); za.am noun (H2195, 22 verses) shows indignation stored and purposefully directed. aganaktēsis (1 verse) is righteous moral anger proving repentance. za.eph (2 verses — Ahab sullen and brooding) is the softest anger in the cluster. be.esh (1 verse — king 'much distressed') is the mildest, inner displeasure at a situation. orgilos (1 verse) names dispositional anger-susceptibility as a character-level disqualifier. parorgismos (1 verse) — anger as overnight state that must not harden. Za.am dual-placed primary here, secondary M02-A for the most intense za.am noun verses.

**M02-D — Provocation — Anger Aroused**
Rationale: ka.as (H3707, 51 verses) is uniquely structured — it is almost never describing anger itself but always the act of provoking God to anger through idolatry (Deu 4:25 through Hos 12:14). This is a relational-dynamic term, not a state term. parorgizō (2 verses), paroxunō (2 verses), me.ri.vah (2 verses), na.phal (1 verse), and re.gaz (secondary) all share this directed, arousal-dynamic register. This sub-group is the M02 cluster's unique functional register: it describes how anger gets kindled, not what anger is.

**M02-E — Jealousy, Zeal and Possessive Passion**
Rationale: The four Hebrew jealousy terms (qa.na, qin.ah, qan.na, qan.no) form a coherent sub-group. Reading their corpora together: divine jealousy as identity-attribute (Exo 20:5; 34:14), divine protective zeal (Isa 42:13; Zec 8:2), human prophetic zeal (1Ki 19:10–14; Psa 69:9), marital jealousy as adjudicable inner state (Num 5:14–30), human covetous jealousy (Gen 26:14; 30:1; Ecc 4:4). Brief §Special handling note 1 considered: the corpus does not cleanly split divine zeal vs. human envy — both registers generate the same inner-claim-violation logic. Decision: one sub-group, not split.

**M02-F — Strife, Quarrel and Contentious Anger**
Rationale: eris (9 verses), thumomacheō (1), logomachia (1), filoneikos (1), mats.tsah (3), mats.tsut (1) all evidence anger expressed outward as ongoing contentious dispute. The phenomenology is not heat but relational agitation sustained in conflict. orgizō placed primary M02-B (erupting inner state); secondary M02-F for its quarrel-producing relational output. This sub-group is the smallest substantive sub-group (~15 verses from primary terms) but analytically distinct from both M02-B and M02-A.

**M02-BOUNDARY**
Rationale: 5 BOUNDARY terms from Phase 3 + tsur (no active corpus). antilogia, erethizō, zestos, ka.a.s, riv all held. ka.a.s (22 verses) and riv (48 verses) are the largest BOUNDARY holdings — researcher disposition will have significant consequence for downstream analysis.
