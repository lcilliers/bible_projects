## M02-C-VCG-01 — Divine Indignation as Sustained Moral Stance

**Context description:** God's indignation understood as a persistent, principled inner moral posture — not an eruptive emotion but a standing disposition that the righteous judge maintains against wickedness and covenant violation. The verses in this VCG present divine indignation as: a constant, daily disposition against wickedness (Psa 7:11), stored and purposefully timed for nations (Zep 3:8), determining a historical period with a fixed end point (Dan 8:19; 11:36), unrelenting and permanent toward Edom (Mal 1:4), sustained toward Jerusalem for seventy years (Zec 1:12), and describing an era of divine displeasure from which people can survive by hiding (Isa 26:20). The inner-being characteristic is indignation as God's standing moral verdict — held as an inner disposition and released or withdrawn on a principled, determined trajectory.

## M02-C-VCG-02 — Human Indignation: Righteous Moral Anger Proving Inner Character

**Context description:** Indignation in human persons as a morally revealing inner response — the kind of anger that proves the quality of the inner person: righteous indignation demonstrating genuine repentance (2Cor 7:11), indignation at what is wrong making a person morally legible, and dispositional anger-susceptibility as a character-level disqualifier (Tit 1:7). These verses focus on what the presence or absence of indignation reveals about the inner character. The inner-being characteristic is indignation as a moral inner indicator — the presence of righteous anger proving genuine repentance, and the dispositional tendency toward anger-susceptibility proving character unfitness.

**Member verses:**

| vc_id | Reference | Term | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 21707 | 2Cor 7:11 | aganaktēsis | Anchor — righteous moral anger against the wrong done, proving sincere repentance |
| 1824 | Tit 1:7 | orgilos | Quick-temperedness as character flaw disqualifying from leadership |
| 1825 | Eph 4:26 | parorgismos | Anger as inner state that must be resolved before hardening overnight |

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 21707 (2Cor 7:11 / aganaktēsis)
Rationale: This verse most directly names indignation as a morally revelatory inner response — it is listed among responses that prove sincere repentance. The verse defines the positive moral function of righteous indignation, making it the definitional statement for this VCG.

## M02-C-VCG-03 — Displeasure: Brooding and Situational Inner Discontent

**Context description:** The softest register of anger in M02 — a sullen, brooding, situational displeasure that does not erupt but withdraws, shuts down, or simply holds its discontented inner state. Ahab's vexed sullenness over Naboth's refusal expresses itself in lying down, refusing food, and withdrawal (1Ki 21:4; 20:43); Daniel's king is 'much distressed' at the situation his decree has created (Dan 6:14). The inner-being characteristic is displeasure as a brooding inner state — the person is not erupting with heat but is closed down, withdrawn, and held in inner conflict with their circumstances.

Rationale: This verse is the most direct description of brooding sullen displeasure as an inner state that shuts down normal life — Ahab lying down, refusing food, his brooding making him dysfunctional. It names the inner-being phenomenon of displeasure as withdrawal rather than eruption.
