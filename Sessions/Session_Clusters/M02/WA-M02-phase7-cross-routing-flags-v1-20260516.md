## Section 1 — Cross-sub-group routing observations

These are verses whose meaning, as recorded in Phase 2, may fit a different sub-group than where Phase 6 currently routes them. Sub-group structure is final (per Phase 7 brief); these are observations for CC to consider alongside the Phase 7 directive.

### Flag 1: vc_id 95 (Mar 3:5 / orgē / M02-A)

**Current routing:** M02-A (orgē primary)  
**Flag:** Jesus's anger is paired with grief, and the meaning is "morally grounded inner response to willful hardness of heart — not mere irritation but righteous indignation at spiritual obstinacy." This register — moral indignation arising from observation of wrongness, paired with grief — sits closer to M02-C (Indignation and Moral Displeasure) than to M02-A's judicial-force register.  
**Suggested disposition:** Retain in M02-A-VCG-04 (Human Anger as Moral Response) but with secondary note that the grief-pairing aligns the verse with M02-C-VCG-02 (Human Indignation). No sub-group change required; VCG-level dual-membership note sufficient.

### Flag 2: vc_id 64926 (Ecc 5:17 / qe.tseph / M02-A)

**Current routing:** M02-A (qe.tseph primary)  
**Flag:** "Wrath is listed alongside vexation and sickness as part of the inner misery characterizing the miser's existence; it describes a chronic angry inner state accompanying a life of futile toil." The meaning is personal human inner misery (wrath as chronic inner condition), not divine judicial wrath. The register sits closer to M02-C (vexation/displeasure as an inner condition) than M02-A.  
**Suggested disposition:** Move to M02-C-VCG-03 (Displeasure: Brooding and Situational Inner Discontent), or if CC prefers to keep qe.tseph verses in M02-A, note in verse_context that this verse's meaning is atypical within the term's corpus.

### Flag 3: vc_id 64773 (Neh 4:7 / cha.rah / M02-B)

**Current routing:** M02-B (cha.rah primary)  
**Flag:** "The coalition of surrounding enemies becomes very angry as Jerusalem's walls are visibly repaired; their incensed inner state is a collective hostility reaction to the restoration of the city." The primary inner-being content is hostile enmity at Israel's restoration — the anger is the vehicle, but the phenomenon named (enemies' collective hostility toward Israel's progress) is closer to M06 (Hate, Contempt and Hostility) than M02-B's burning-heat register.  
**Suggested disposition:** Retain in M02-B-VCG-02 (anger erupting into action) but note this verse's inner-being content is primarily hostility-register. M06 consideration flagged for Phase 10 inherited-findings reconciliation.

### Flag 4: vc_id 64787 (Isa 45:24 / cha.rah / M02-B)

**Current routing:** M02-B (cha.rah primary)  
**Flag:** "Those who were inwardly incensed against the Lord will come to shame — their anger directed against him leads to humiliation rather than vindication." The incensed state here is enemy rage against God — the dominant inner-being content is hostility/contention directed at the divine, closer to M06 territory.  
**Suggested disposition:** Retain in M02-B; minor flag. The shame consequence makes this verse also relevant to M07 (Shame), but the primary content remains anger as inner state. Note for researcher awareness only.

### Flag 5: vc_id 862, 865 (1Sa 1:6; 1Sa 1:7 / ka.as / M02-D)

**Current routing:** M02-D-VCG-02 (anger aroused in human persons)  
**Flag:** Both verses' meanings present Peninnah's provocation as causing deep grief/sorrow in Hannah, not anger. "The provocation is targeted, intentional, and designed to aggravate an existing inner wound" (1Sa 1:6); "produces an inner sorrow so intense she cannot eat" (1Sa 1:7). The inner-being content in the recipient (Hannah) is M03 Grief, not M02 anger. The term ka.as (to provoke) is correctly in M02-D for its provocation dynamic, but the evidenced inner-being state is grief.  
**Suggested disposition:** Retain in M02-D-VCG-02 because the term's role is provoking (the dynamic is anger-territory), but flag in verse_context.notes that Hannah's evidenced inner-being state is grief register. These two verses may also be candidates for secondary routing to M03 when M03 is analysed.

## Section 2 — Divine inner-state set-aside candidates

Per Phase 7 brief §1: verses that describe God's internal emotional experience as a divine inner-life event (analogous to M01's set-aside precedents) are candidates for set-aside. The programme scope is human inner being.

**Assessment:** After reviewing all 263 M02-B and 109 M02-A verses, the vast majority describe wrath enacted or revealed — these are in scope per the brief's guidance. The following two verses are noted for awareness but are **NOT recommended as set-aside candidates** — they remain in scope:

- vc_id 64675 (Isa 63:5 / che.mah) — "Finding no human help, God's own wrath became the sustaining force that upheld him in battle." This edges toward divine inner experience, but the verse is better read as divine saving action (wrath as the energy behind God's arm), which is in scope.
- vc_id 64723 (Zec 8:2 / che.mah) — "God's rage is the burning emotional intensity of his jealousy for Zion." This explicitly names an emotional state — but the jealousy-for-Zion context makes it a restorative-passion verse, not a private inner-state verse. In scope.

**No set-aside candidates recommended at this phase.** The brief's guidance is correct that the threshold is narrow and most divine-wrath verses are in scope.
