## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

---

### T2.1.1 — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — Yes. Eze 3:14 (M02-B-VCG-03) is the most direct evidence: "Ezekiel experiences rage as a felt inner heat in his spirit — a bitterness and burning intensity in his own inner being that accompanies the Lord's strong hand upon him as he is carried away." The spirit (*ruach*) is explicitly named as the location of the rage.

**[D-VCG-03]**
E — Act 17:16 (M02-D-VCG-03) locates the provocation in the spirit: "Paul's spirit was provoked deep within him — a sharp inner distress and indignation stirred by the sight of Athens filled with idols, locating the provocation in the spirit/inner person." The spirit is explicitly named.

**[C-VCG-01]**
E — Jer 15:17 (M02-C-VCG-01) describes God filling Jeremiah with indignation as a consequence of his prophetic calling — the indignation lodges within him and isolates him from social joy. While 'spirit' is not the precise term used here, the indwelling quality and its impact on Jeremiah's inner-relational life suggests spirit-level location.

---

### T2.1.2 — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?

**[B-VCG-03/04]**
E — The spirit-level location evidences in Eze 3:14 and Act 17:16 show anger arising at the spirit level. Additionally, 1Sa 11:6 (M02-B-VCG-02) shows Saul's anger as Spirit-empowered when the Spirit of God rushes upon him — the Spirit is the enabling power behind the anger's intensity and direction. This suggests that in its most fully calibrated form, anger is a spirit-level phenomenon — or at minimum, can be engaged and directed from the spirit level.

---

### T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**[B-VCG-03/04, D-VCG-03]**
E — Spirit-level location in Eze 3:14 and Act 17:16 reveals that anger/indignation at its deepest reaches the most interior dimension of the person. Eze 3:14's "felt inner heat in his spirit" suggests this is not a surface emotional experience but an engagement of the person at the level where God's Spirit and the human spirit most closely interact. Paul's spirit being provoked by idolatry positions the moral-perception faculty (which perceives wrongdoing) at the spirit level. The finding is that anger, at its most morally serious, originates from or engages the deepest stratum of the inner person.

---

### T2.1.4 — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.

**[C-VCG-03, F]**
S — The brooding-displeasure sub-group (M02-C-VCG-03: Ahab's sullen withdrawal) is silent on spirit-level location. The strife-and-quarrel sub-group (M02-F) is silent on spirit-level location — contentious anger is described at the relational and dispositional level without explicit spirit-location.

---

### T2.2.1 — Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?

**[B-VCG-03, A-VCG-04]**
E — Job 6:4 (M02-B-VCG-06) describes God's arrows as carrying "poison his spirit drinks" — the rage is experienced as internal spiritual poisoning. While 'soul' specifically is not always the named faculty, several verses locate the experience in the inner person as a whole: Jam 1:20 (M02-A-VCG-04) speaks of "human wrath" as an inner drive. The characteristic operates throughout the soul in its broader sense as the inner personal life.

**[D-VCG-01]**
E — Ecc 7:9 (M02-D-VCG-02) states "anger settles as a resident in the heart of fools" — the heart is the soul's integrating centre, and the verse evidences anger taking up residence there as a condition of the inner person as a whole.

---

### T2.2.2 — What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — Est 3:5 (M02-B-VCG-03) shows rage filling the inner being completely — "it takes possession of him." This total-possession description reveals that anger at its most intense is not a localized emotional flare but an experience that engages the entire inner personal life. The soul as the seat of personal experience is saturated and overridden by the anger.

---

### T2.2.3 — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.

**[E-VCG-01]**
S — The divine-constitutional-jealousy corpus (M02-E-VCG-01) is largely silent on soul-level location as the locus of the characteristic — it is presented as a constitutional identity attribute rather than a soul-level experience.

---

### T2.3.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?

**[D-VCG-02, B-VCG-02]**
E — Yes. Ecc 7:9 (M02-D-VCG-02) explicitly locates anger in the heart: "anger then settles as a resident in the heart of fools." Pro 19:3 (M02-B-VCG-02) states "his heart rages against the Lord" — the heart is the seat of the misdirected anger. Gen 4:5–6 (M02-B-VCG-02) implies heart-location through God's questioning of Cain's inner anger-state. Jam 3:14 (which is in M28 but cross-references the M02 register) locates bitter jealousy explicitly in the heart.

**[B-VCG-05]**
E — Psa 37:8 (M02-B-VCG-05) locates the heated inner state in the heart's functioning: the fretting-anger is a heart-state that must be relinquished.

---

### T2.3.2 — What does heart-location reveal about this characteristic's engagement of the heart's integrating function?

**[D-VCG-02, B-VCG-02]**
E — Heart-location reveals that anger engages the knowing, willing, and feeling dimensions of the heart's integrating function simultaneously. Ecc 7:9 (anger as heart-resident of fools) shows it engaging the moral-evaluative dimension of the heart — foolishness and anger are linked through the heart. Pro 19:3 (heart raging against the Lord) shows anger as a directing force in the heart — the will's energy is misdirected through the heart's anger. The heart-location reveals anger as an integrating inner-being condition, not a peripheral emotion.

---

### T2.3.3 — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-02]**
S — The eschatological-wrath corpus (M02-A-VCG-02) is largely silent on heart-location — it describes wrath as a future external threat rather than a located inner state. Rev 6:16–17 and related verses describe what people flee from, not what is located in the person's heart.

---

### T2.4.1 — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?

**[B-VCG-06, A-VCG-03]**
E — Isa 42:25 (M02-B-VCG-06) provides the key evidence: "the people failed to perceive or take it to heart — the inner faculty of understanding is closed off, blocking recognition of divine wrath." This is not a location of anger in the mind but reveals the mind as the faculty through which the anger's significance is processed (or failed to be processed). The characteristic engages the mind as the faculty of recognition and understanding.

**[B-VCG-05]**
E — Psa 37:1–8 (M02-B-VCG-05) suggests that fretting anger operates in the mind through comparison and evaluation of the wicked's prosperity — it is a cognitive-emotional state that begins with mental observation of perceived injustice.

---

### T2.4.2 — What does mind-location reveal about the aspect of mind engaged?

**[A-VCG-03, B-VCG-06]**
E — Isa 42:25 reveals that the mind's recognitive function (understanding, perception, heart-taking) is the faculty through which anger's significance must be processed. When this faculty is closed, the anger passes unrecognised. The mind engaged is the discernment-and-recognition faculty — not analytical reasoning but the deeper moral-perceptive function that reads the significance of what is happening.

---

### T2.4.3 — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.

**[E-VCG-01, C-VCG-03]**
S — The divine-constitutional-jealousy corpus (M02-E-VCG-01) and the brooding-displeasure corpus (M02-C-VCG-03) are silent on mind-location specifically. The constitutional jealousy is not primarily a cognitive phenomenon; the brooding displeasure is not located in analytical thought.

---

### T2.5.1 — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind?

**[B-VCG-03, D-VCG-03]**
E — Yes. The spirit is evidenced explicitly (Eze 3:14; Act 17:16) as noted in T2.1. Beyond heart, mind, and spirit, the evidence surfaces the "inner being" or "within" as a location: Est 1:12 (M02-B-VCG-03) locates the rage "within him" without specifying heart or mind; Dan 3:19 describes filling with fury that is visible in the face — an inner state that has a somatic signal.

---

### T2.5.2 — If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — The "within" location (Est 1:12 — inner fire located explicitly "within him") and the somatic-inner convergence (Dan 3:19 — filling visible on the face) reveal that anger is experienced as a whole-inner-person phenomenon that defies precise anatomical assignment to a single faculty. The characteristic saturates the inner person — spirit, heart, mind, and the felt bodily interior — simultaneously.

---

### T2.5.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-01]**
S — The covenantal-oath corpus (M02-A-VCG-01) is silent on specific soul-level sub-location — wrath in this register is described structurally (what it produces as covenant consequence) rather than anatomically (where it is seated in the inner person).

### T2.6.1 — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**[B-VCG-02]**
E — Yes, explicitly. Gen 4:5 (M02-B-VCG-02 / na.phal term in M02-D): Cain's anger shows outwardly as a fallen countenance — the face is the body-part evidenced. Gen 40:6 (M02-B-VCG-02): vexed agitation is visible on the officers' faces. Dan 3:19: the change of fury shows on Nebuchadnezzar's face. The face is the primary body-part link.

**[C-VCG-01]**
E — Psa 38:3 (M02-C-VCG-01) evidences a body-part link: divine indignation "removes soundness from flesh and health from bones" — bones are evidenced as the body-part site of indignation's impact on the recipient.

**[B-VCG-06]**
E — Isa 30:27 (M02-C-VCG-01) evidences lips and tongue as the body-parts through which God's indignation is expressed: "burning anger and fury that fill his very lips and tongue." Jer 6:11 (M02-B-VCG-03) shows wrath pressing from within and being released through speech — the mouth as expressive organ.

**[E-VCG-04]**
E — Pro 14:30 (M02-E-VCG-04) links envy to bones: "envy rots the bones." Bones are the body-part evidenced for jealousy's sustained effect.

---

### T2.6.2 — What is Scripture doing by making that link?

**[B-VCG-02]**
E — The face-link in Gen 4:5 and Dan 3:19 is expressive and indicative: the face reveals what is happening inwardly, making the inner state readable — the fallen countenance is the inner anger's external signal. Scripture uses this link to show that anger cannot be hidden — it discloses itself.

**[C-VCG-01]**
E — The bones-link in Psa 38:3 and Pro 14:30 is mediating and deposit-making: the indignation/envy penetrates to the structural interior of the body, leaving a consequence that goes beyond the immediate emotional episode. Scripture uses this link to show that anger received or harboured has a deep somatic consequence.

**[C-VCG-01]**
E — The lips-and-tongue link in Isa 30:27 is expressive and functional: the divine indignation takes a communicative form through speech-organs, showing that anger's most characteristic expression is verbal judgment. Scripture uses this link to show anger's communicative form.

---

### T2.6.3 — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-01, F]**
S — The covenantal-oath wrath corpus (M02-A-VCG-01) evidences no direct body-part link — wrath in this register is structural and judicial rather than embodied. The strife-and-quarrel corpus (M02-F) evidences no specific body-part link — quarreling is described as a disposition and relational dynamic, not with anatomical specificity.

---

### T2.7.1 — Which direction does the body-characteristic link run?

**[B-VCG-02]**
E — For the face-link (Gen 4:5; Dan 3:19 / M02-B-VCG-02/03): the direction is soul-to-body — the inner anger expresses outward through the face. The face is the expressive medium, not the source.

**[C-VCG-01]**
E — For the bones-link (Psa 38:3 / M02-C-VCG-01 and Pro 14:30 / M02-E-VCG-04): the direction may run both ways. Divine indignation penetrates the body of the recipient (soul → body, impact direction). But sustained jealousy rotting the bones (Pro 14:30) suggests that the habitual inner state deposits its consequence in the body, which then feeds back as physical deterioration affecting the person's whole condition. The bi-directional possibility is present.

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — For Dan 3:19 (filling visible in face): the direction is soul-to-body — the inner state of being filled with fury overflows into the face's expression.

---

### T2.7.2 — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**[B-VCG-02]**
E — The consistent soul-to-body direction for the face-link means that anger is fundamentally an inner event that cannot be concealed — it inscribes itself on the body involuntarily. This makes anger a constitutionally revealing characteristic: its presence discloses the moral state of the inner person to observers.

**[E-VCG-04]**
E — The possible body feedback in Pro 14:30 (envy rotting bones) means that sustained covetous jealousy deposits itself in the body, which then contributes to the degradation of the whole person. This is both a moral and a somatic consequence — the inner state damages the body, and the damaged body may reinforce the damaged inner state.

---

### T2.7.3 — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-01]**
S — No body-characteristic directional link is evidenced for wrath-as-covenantal-verdict (M02-A-VCG-01). This register operates at the structural-judicial level without somatic embedding.

---

### T2.8.1 — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body?

**[E-VCG-04, C-VCG-01]**
E — Pro 14:30 (M02-E-VCG-04) provides the most direct evidence: "envy rots the bones" — sustained jealousy leaves a physical deposit that deteriorates the body's structural interior. Psa 38:3 (M02-C-VCG-01) provides corroborating evidence: divine indignation "removes soundness from flesh and health from bones" — the indignation's sustained pressure on the recipient produces a bodily deposit of unsoundness.

**[A-VCG-01]**
E — Deu 5:9 (M02-E-VCG-01) provides evidence of a generational bodily consequence: divine jealousy punishes children for their fathers' unfaithfulness across generations — this is a trans-generational deposit, carried forward beyond the individual who originally bore the characteristic.

---

### T2.8.2 — What evidence supports or contradicts this?

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — Gen 27:44 (M02-B-VCG-03) evidences that rage's immediate deposit subsides with time and distance — the volatile inner state resolves rather than leaving a permanent deposit. This could be read as contradicting a permanent constitutional deposit for the burning-rage register. However, Pro 19:19 (M02-B-VCG-05) shows that the deeply ingrained person of great rage perpetuates the cycle — suggesting that repeated rage-episodes do deposit a dispositional groove that is difficult to escape.

---

### T2.8.3 — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-02]**
S — The eschatological-wrath corpus (M02-A-VCG-02) is silent on constitutional deposit — it describes wrath as an external future force, not as something that leaves a deposit in the person who bears it. The deposit-question belongs to the inner-experience and sustained-character registers of the cluster, not the eschatological-judicial register.

---

### T2.9.1 — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[D-VCG-01]**
E — The provocation corpus (M02-D-VCG-01) evidences external origin as the dominant pattern: idolatrous acts provoke God's anger from outside — the characteristic is aroused by external stimulus directed at a moral claim. Jer 11:17; Deu 4:25 name the external provocation as the originating cause.

**[B-VCG-04]**
E — Righteous human anger (M02-B-VCG-04) also originates from external moral encounter: Nehemiah's anger originates from hearing the oppression (Neh 5:6); Elihu's originates from observing theological failure (Job 32:2). The characteristic is received from perception of wrongdoing, not self-generated.

**[E-VCG-01]**
E — Divine jealousy (M02-E-VCG-01) has a different origin: it is a constitutional identity-attribute (Exo 34:14 — God's name is Jealous), not externally provoked. For God, jealousy is internally constitutive.

**[B-VCG-03]**
E — Possessing rage in human persons can arise from both external stimulus (Haman's rage provoked by Mordecai's refusal, Est 3:5) and from wounded internal pride (the king's inner fire at Vashti's refusal, Est 1:12). The origin is interactive — external trigger meeting an inner pride-investment.

---

### T2.9.2 — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — The origin is multiple. The cluster evidences at least four distinct origins: (1) external moral provocation (perceived wrongdoing from another agent — M02-D-VCG-01); (2) constitutional attribute (divine jealousy as identity-level — M02-E-VCG-01); (3) wounded pride-investment meeting non-compliance (M02-B-VCG-03: Est 1:12; 3:5); (4) Spirit-empowerment transforming news of injustice into fierce anger (1Sa 11:6; Judg 14:19 — M02-B-VCG-02). The origin is multiple and varies by the register of anger evidenced.

---

### T2.9.3 — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts?

**[D-VCG-01 vs B-VCG-04]**
E — Yes. In the idolatry-provocation context (M02-D-VCG-01), the origin is the external covenant-violating act. In the righteous-anger context (M02-B-VCG-04), the origin is the internal moral-perception faculty encountering injustice. In the jealousy context (M02-E-VCG-01), the origin is constitutional. The context of the anger determines which origin pathway is active.

---

### T2.10.1 — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?

**[B-VCG-02/03]**
E — Yes, consistently in the erupting-anger register. The sequence is: inner arousal (spirit/heart) → visible face-expression (body) → outward action. Exo 32:19 (M02-B-VCG-02): Moses' inner burning anger → immediate outward act (smashing tablets). Dan 3:19 (M02-B-VCG-03): inner filling with fury → visible facial transformation → changed commands. Gen 4:5 (M02-B-VCG-02): incensed inner state → fallen countenance (body signal). The movement is consistently from inner (spirit/soul) to outer (body/action).

**[C-VCG-01 → body]**
E — Psa 38:3 (M02-C-VCG-01): divine indignation moves from God's inner disposition → into the sufferer's body (removing soundness from flesh and health from bones). Movement from divine inner → human body.

**[E-VCG-01 → D-VCG-01]**
E — Deu 6:15 (M02-B-VCG-01): divine jealousy → kindling of anger → destructive action. The movement is from the identity-level constitutional attribute (jealousy) to the aroused anger state to enacted consequence.

---

### T2.10.2 — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?

**[B-VCG-02/03]**
E — The consistent sequence is: (1) inner arousal — kindling, filling, or seizing; (2) somatic signal — face falls or transforms; (3) outward act — decisive, consequential action. This three-stage sequence is evidenced in Gen 4:5 (inner → face → implied action), Dan 3:13–19 (inner → face → command), Exo 32:19 (inner burning → outward smashing). The pattern suggests anger's movement is directional and rapid — the inner-to-outer transit is not slow. The somatic signal is transitional: it marks the threshold between the inner state and the outward act.

---

### T2.10.3 — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[A-VCG-05]**
S — The settled-abiding-wrath-condition corpus (M02-A-VCG-05 / Joh 3:36) evidences no constitutional movement — wrath in this register is a static, abiding condition without described movement between levels.

**[C-VCG-01 sustained]**
S — Divine indignation as a sustained moral stance (M02-C-VCG-01: Psa 7:11 — daily disposition) is described without constitutional movement — it is a stable, persistent inner orientation rather than a moving, erupting state. Constitutional movement belongs primarily to the burning-heat registers.
