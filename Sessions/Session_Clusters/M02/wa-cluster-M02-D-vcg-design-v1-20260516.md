## M02-D-VCG-01 — Idolatry Provoking Divine Anger

**Context description:** The relational dynamic of human idolatry as an act that deliberately stirs God's anger — the provocation of a covenant breach directed specifically at God's exclusive claim. The verses in this VCG describe a repeated, recognizable pattern: Israel or its kings abandon God for images, and this act provokes his inner anger. The provocation is: framed as a direct stimulus (Deu 4:25), willfully intentional (2Ki 22:17; 2Ch 34:25), cumulative through generations (1Ki 16:33; 2Ki 23:26; Jer 32:30), and escalating in severity (Eze 8:17). The inner-being characteristic is idolatry as a relational offense — a human act whose effect is to arouse God's anger against his exclusive claim, making anger the result of a specific, directed provocation.

## M02-D-VCG-02 — Anger Aroused in Human Persons by Deliberate Provocation

**Context description:** The dynamic of a human person's anger being deliberately or repeatedly stirred by another's actions — someone targeting another's inner state to produce anger, grief, or vexation. The verses in this VCG include Peninnah deliberately provoking Hannah year after year (1Sa 1:6–7), Sanballat's provoked rage at Israel's wall-building (Neh 4:1), and the wicked man seeing the righteous prosper and being involuntarily provoked (Psa 112:10). The inner-being characteristic is anger aroused in a human person from the outside — a provoked inner state rather than a self-generated one.

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 862 (1Sa 1:6 / ka.as)
Rationale: This verse is the most direct and sustained example of deliberate human provocation — Peninnah's actions are described as intentional, repeated, and targeted at Hannah's inner wound. The structure is clear: agent (Peninnah), action (provoke), and result (Hannah's deep grief). This is the definitive human-provocation verse.

## M02-D-VCG-03 — Spirit Provoked by Moral Outrage

**Context description:** Anger aroused from within — the inner person stirred by what it encounters (idolatry, injustice, theological failure) without an external human agent deliberately targeting them. Paul's spirit is provoked at Athens by the sight of idols (Act 17:16); love is defined by its refusal to be provoked (1Cor 13:5); strife is named as something to avoid provoking (Gen 13:8); Meribah is defined by Israel's quarreling that provoked God (Num 27:14). The inner-being characteristic is spontaneous anger arising from within the inner person in response to what is observed or encountered — not from deliberate targeting but from the conscience's encounter with wrong.

| 38087 | Num 27:14 | me.ri.vah | Meribah: quarreling that provoked God, Moses and Aaron failing to uphold holiness |

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 136 (Act 17:16 / paroxunō)
Rationale: This verse is the most direct statement of the inner-person being provoked by moral encounter — Paul's spirit stirred "deep within him" by what he sees. The provocation is located explicitly in the spirit/inner person, arising from moral perception rather than from deliberate targeting.

## M02-D-VCG-04 — The Arousal of Anger: Inner Signal and Physical Expression

**Context description:** The moment anger arrives in the inner person — evidenced through physical signals, behavioral withdrawal, or diagnostic cues that reveal when anger has been kindled. Cain's face falls as his anger takes hold (Gen 4:5 / na.phal); vexed agitation is visible on the officers' faces (Gen 40:6 / za.aph); Nehemiah's great anger is an immediate emotional reaction (Neh 4:1 / cha.rah, see also VCG-01 for the provocation side). The inner-being characteristic is anger's arrival as a perceptible inner event — signaled by the body, made visible in comportment, readable by observers.

**Member verses:**

| vc_id | Reference | Term | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1173 | Gen 4:5 | na.phal | Anchor — face falling as immediate outward signal of anger arriving |
| 46122 | Gen 40:6 | za.aph | Vexed agitation visible on officers' faces |

**Note:** This VCG is intentionally thin (2 verses) because the physical-signal phenomenon is specifically evidenced by only these two verses in M02-D. The face-falling (na.phal) and visible-agitation (za.aph) are distinct enough to warrant their own VCG — they name the arrival and somatic expression of anger, not its content or consequence.

**Anchor verse:** vc_id 1173 (Gen 4:5 / na.phal)
Rationale: This verse names the arrival of anger through its bodily signal with direct, unambiguous clarity — "his face fell." The downcast face is the visible collapse of the angered inner person, making this the most direct evidence of anger's somatic expression as an inner-arrival signal.
