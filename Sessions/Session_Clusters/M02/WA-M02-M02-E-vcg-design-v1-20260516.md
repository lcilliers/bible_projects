## M02-E-VCG-01 — Divine Jealousy as Constitutional Identity-Attribute

**Context description:** God's jealousy understood as a defining constitutional attribute of his being — not a reaction to a specific offense but a fundamental property of who he is that governs all relationship with him. The verses in this VCG present divine jealousy as: his very name (Exo 34:14), constitutive of the first commandment (Exo 20:5), a consuming fire destroying what competes for exclusive allegiance (Deu 4:24), an immanent watchful attribute in Israel's midst (Deu 6:15), paired with holiness as the ground of his refusal to overlook unfaithfulness (Jos 24:19), and linked directly to active vengeance (Nah 1:2). The inner-being characteristic is jealousy as God's fundamental exclusive-claim attribute — the identity-level inner property from which all other jealousy expressions flow.

## M02-E-VCG-02 — Divine Protective Jealousy and Zeal for People and Name

**Context description:** God's jealousy expressed as a fierce, protective passion directed toward his people, his city, his land, and his name — a jealousy that fires actively in their defense and motivates restorative action. The verses in this VCG present: God's jealousy moving him from judgment to pity and restorative action (Joe 2:18), his exceedingly intense jealousy for Jerusalem motivating restoration (Zec 1:14; 8:2), jealousy burning like fire toward enemies who desecrate his land (Eze 36:5), his zeal as the inner driving force ensuring the survival of a remnant (Isa 37:32), and his zeal as the passionate commitment establishing the promised kingdom (Isa 9:7). The inner-being characteristic is jealousy as a protective, restorative inner passion — a fierce devotion to the honor and welfare of what is covenantally his.

## M02-E-VCG-03 — Human Zeal as Jealous Passion for God's Honor

**Context description:** Human jealousy directed toward God — the inner passion of persons who identify themselves with God's cause and are consumed by zeal for his honor. Elijah's repeated declaration of being very jealous for the Lord (1Ki 19:10; 19:14); Phinehas whose jealousy mirrors God's own (Num 25:11; 25:13); the psalmist consumed from within by zeal for God's house (Psa 69:9); the psalmist's zeal for God's words devouring him (Psa 119:139); Jehu's claimed zeal for God's honor (2Ki 10:16). The inner-being characteristic is human jealous passion directed upward toward God — a consuming, identifying inner ardor that makes the person feel God's honor as personally at stake.

## M02-E-VCG-04 — Human Possessive Jealousy and Covetous Envy

**Context description:** Jealousy in human persons directed at other humans — the inner passion of possessiveness, comparison, and covetous resentment at another's advantage or standing. Covering: marital jealousy as an involuntary possessing spirit (Num 5:14–30), brothers' jealousy at a sibling's favoritism (Gen 37:11), Rachel's consuming jealousy of Leah (Gen 30:1), competitive inter-tribal jealousy (Isa 11:13), envy of sinners that must be resisted (Pro 23:17; 24:1), jealousy of the violent man (Pro 3:31), and jealousy that produces rage fiercer than wrath (Pro 6:34; 27:4). The inner-being characteristic is possessive jealousy and covetous envy as a human inward passion — a reactive comparison-based inner state that directs itself at others' perceived advantage and can produce destructive intensity.
