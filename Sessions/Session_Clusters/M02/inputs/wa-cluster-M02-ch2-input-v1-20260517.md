The inner-being characteristic is anger as contentious relational force: quarreling as a corruption of community, strife rooted in pride and insolence, a dispute-loving disposition as an inner character tendency, and contentious anger that the wise avoid and fools cannot. Distinguished from M02-B (heat) and M02-A (judicial weight) by its relational outworking in ongoing conflict.

**Key verses for this characteristic:**

**Act 12:20** (G2371 thumomacheō) [M02-F-VCG-01]
> Act 12:20 Now Herod was angry with the people of Tyre and Sidon , and they came to him with one accord , and having persuaded Blastus , the king’s chamberlain , they asked for peace , because their country depended on the king’s country for food .
_Meaning of the term here:_ Herod's quarrelsome fury toward Tyre and Sidon is an intense, hostile inner disposition that drives political conflict, with the dependent cities seeking peace to resolve it.

**1Cor 11:16** (G5380 filoneikos) [M02-F-VCG-01]
> 1Cor 11:16 If anyone is inclined to be contentious , we have no such practice , nor do the churches of God .
_Meaning of the term here:_ A dispute-loving disposition—the inner inclination to be contentious—is named as something to be checked; it finds no support in apostolic or church practice, marking it as an inner tendency to be resisted.

_Meaning of the term here:_ Strife appears as a work of the flesh alongside enmity and jealousy, identifying it as an inner drive of the sinful nature that generates hostile conflict in relationships.
