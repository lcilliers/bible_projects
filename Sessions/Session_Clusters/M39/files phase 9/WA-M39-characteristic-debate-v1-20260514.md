## 2. Semantic domain analysis

### Domain A: Blessing/Grace — sovereign favour from greater to lesser

All terms in this domain share one fundamental inner-being dynamic: a disposition or enacted movement of the greater toward the lesser that constitutes favour, grace, or blessing. The inner-being characteristic is located at the intersection of will, relational capacity, and moral character — the inner orientation of one who gives what the receiver cannot earn or generate.

**Terms and their distinctive contribution within Domain A:**

- **H1288 ba.rakh (to bless, 249 verses):** The largest term in the cluster. Four prior groups name the four forms: God's sovereign covenantal gift of favour (1299-001); human blessing of God as worship (1299-002); human blessing of other humans as prophetic/covenantal declaration (1299-003); the ironic/euphemistic use (cursing under the name of blessing — 1299-004). The ironic group is analytically significant — it evidences the distorted or inverted form of the characteristic, parallel to M06's inverted conscience. The blessing-act is the most comprehensive term: it spans God's inner disposition, its outer enacted declaration, and the human response of worship-directed praise.

- **G5485 charis (grace, 67 verses):** The NT's primary grace term. Four groups: sovereign unmerited divine disposition (888-001); the sphere and power in which the believer stands (888-002); relational favour between persons (888-003); the animating power of apostolic mission (888-004). Root χαρ- shared with chairo (to rejoice) — grace and joy share a root; receiving grace produces joy as natural inner response (DIM-068-SD020). The vocabulary is structurally complete for grace's operation: disposition (chen, charis), act (chanan, charizō), completed state (charitoō), receiving side (tachanum). Charis is the dispositional/state noun.

- **H2603A cha.nan (be gracious, 72 verses):** The verbal form of grace — the act of being gracious or showing favour. One group: God's inner disposition of grace toward the supplicant, and the human cry that turns to God in petition and dependence (984-001). The CHANAN root generates the full supplication vocabulary (techinah, tahanun) — the receiving side of the grace dynamic emerges from the same root as the giving side. The verb carries both sides: God's act of being gracious AND the human act of pleading for grace. This dual ownership within the one term is analytically distinctive.

- **H2580 chen (favor, 67 verses):** The noun of grace/favour — the standing-in-favour that the greater grants. Three groups: divine relational favour (889-001); interpersonal favour (889-002); favour as inner character quality in speech/conduct/wisdom (889-003). Root: to bend toward, to show partiality — the greater inclines toward the lesser. The two-sided definitional structure: disposition of the giver (chen) and posture of the receiver (tachanum, from same root). Chen's 889-003 group (favour as character quality) is the closest to M39-B (goodness as quality) — the gracious person whose speech generates standing. This is a bridge point.

- **G5483 charizō (to give grace, 19 verses with 15 G-status + 4 Phase 2 new):** The verbal act of grace-giving — the concrete enactment. Prior groups include forgiving, giving sight (Luk 7:21), releasing prisoners (Act 3:14), granting political favour (Acts). The term spans the full range from the highest theological grace-giving to corrupted/political concession. The political-favour register (Phase 2 observations) evidences the term's breadth: grace-giving is a structural dynamic that appears in corrupted forms as political concession and in its fullness as divine bestowal.

- **G5487 charitoō (to favor, 2 verses):** NT coinage — coined because existing vocabulary could not name "standing-in-favour as a completed, continuing state." The perfect passive kecharitōmenē (Luk 1:28) encodes completed act with continuing state. This is the completed-state face of grace — the person who has been permanently transformed by being made the object of divine favour. Despite having only 2 verses, the prior analytical work on this term is rich. It names a distinct form of the grace characteristic: not the act of gracing but the permanent standing that results.

- **G2107 eudokia (goodwill, 9 verses):** Two groups: divine goodwill/pleasure as sovereign inner purpose (494-001); human goodwill/desire as inner orientation of longing for good (494-002). Mounce: "goodwill, good purpose, favor, pleasure, desire." This is the inner pleasure/disposition that grounds the act of blessing — the sovereign inner purpose from which God's blessing flows. It bridges Divine A (sovereign disposition) with M39-B (goodness/pleasure quality).

- **H2587 chan.nun (gracious, 13 verses):** Adjectival form — names the person-type. Almost exclusively divine epithet in the Exo 34:6 formula ("the Lord is gracious"). The settled disposition of the person to extend favour and mercy freely. This is the character-descriptor form of the grace characteristic — naming the person who embodies it rather than the act or disposition in the abstract.

- **G5486 charisma (gift/gracious gift, 14 verses):** One group: God's free gift of grace — the spiritual capacity given to each person for building up the community, and supremely the gift of eternal life (1301-001). From the CHARIS root. Names the grace in its gifted-capacity form — the endowment that constitutes the recipient as a person equipped for community participation. Unlike dōron (external offering), charisma names an inner capacity/endowment.

- **H2604 cha.nan (be gracious — Aramaic, 2 verses):** Extracted_thin. Root identical to H2603A. Two verses address mercy shown in moral action and prayer. Insufficient for independent sub-group analysis. Folded into M39-A alongside H2603A for Phase 4 treatment.

### Domain B: Goodness — the inner moral-affective quality of being good

All terms in this domain share the quality of goodness as an inner-being character state or evaluative reality. Unlike Domain A (directional movement of grace from greater to lesser), Domain B names what something or someone genuinely is — a quality of inner character, moral evaluation, and affective pleasure-in-good.

- **H3190 ya.tav (be good, 110 verses):** Three groups: heart glad/merry (632-001, affective); moral goodness/doing good (632-002, moral); inner state of being pleased or finding something good — approval, satisfaction, favour (632-003, evaluative/volitional). DIM-186-001 confirms: ya.tav "bridges the moral and affective dimensions within a single gladness/goodness term." This bridging character is ya.tav's defining feature. The Phase 2 set-asides were heavily from the adverbial intensifier and skill/proficiency uses — the genuine inner-being content of ya.tav centres on its moral-goodness and approval-of-good uses. The 632-003 group (approval/satisfaction/favour) risks overlap with M39-A's favour register. This is the primary tension to resolve in Phase 4.

- **H2895 tov (be pleasing, 48 verses):** Two groups: divine inner pleasure/disposition (from group descriptions, cross-referenced via §4.4 VCG table — tov-001 and tov-002). Tov is the broadest OT quality-of-goodness term: moral rightness, covenantal reliability, volitional preference, aesthetic pleasantness, divine pleasure. The Phase 2 relevant verses confirmed tov in: divine dispositional pleasure toward blessing (Num 24:1), covenantal blessing-outcome (Deu 5:33), aesthetic goodness-as-favour (Num 24:5), volitional judgment (2Sa 15:26, 19:37). The overlap with M39-A is real — tov naming divine pleasure/will is very close to eudokia (goodwill) in Domain A. Phase 4 must determine: is tov's "divine pleasure" dimension the same characteristic as eudokia/chen, or distinct?

### Domain C: Gift / Received endowment (BOUNDARY candidates)

- **G1435 dōron (gift/offering, 17 verses):** Six groups naming what the gift means in inner-being terms: worship-devotion (6837-001), relational conscience amid worship (6837-002), moral evasion via religious piety (6837-003), inadequacy of ritual to perfect the conscience (6837-004), salvation as free gift (6837-005), radical self-giving generosity from total inner trust (6837-006). The inner-being content is real and substantial — but it is carried by the context and the surrounding theological framework, not by dōron itself. Dōron names the material gift/offering; the inner-being work is done by the person's disposition. Provisionally BOUNDARY.

- **H7862 shay (gift/tribute, 3 verses):** One group: tribute brought to God as expression of inner homage, reverence, and acknowledgment of supremacy (2976-001). Extracted_thin; 3 verses. The inner content is the attitude of reverent homage — but this is closer to the worship/reverence cluster than the grace/goodness cluster. The tributary gift is not the same characteristic as blessing given or goodness possessed. BOUNDARY.

- **H2868 te.ev (be good — Aramaic, 1 verse):** One group: inner gladness at deliverance through trust in God (633-001). Registry of origin: R186 gladness. Only 1 verse. The "be good" gloss links it lexically to M39-B (goodness), but the actual group description names an affective gladness response — the gladness at deliverance. This is the gladness characteristic, not the goodness characteristic. The Aramaic cognate link to ya.tav is real but the register is different. OQ-003: consider whether te.ev belongs in M39 or should be reassigned to the gladness cluster (M-cluster to be identified). BOUNDARY pending OQ-003 resolution.

### Placement of H7521 ra.tsah (to accept/delight, 54 verses) — OQ-004

Ra.tsah occupies a structurally ambiguous position. Three existing groups:
- 795-001: Divine acceptance of offerings — God's inner-being pleasure in what is offered
- 795-002: Divine pleasure in persons/character — God's inner delight in the righteous
- 795-003: Human acceptance/favour — inner-being receipt of divine or human favour

Arguments for M39-A placement (grace/blessing sub-group):
- Divine acceptance of offerings and divine delight in persons are expressions of the grace dynamic — God's favour/pleasure is what makes an offering acceptable; the same inner disposition that generates blessing generates acceptance.
- The divine pleasure in persons (795-002) overlaps closely with eudokia (goodwill) in M39-A.

Arguments for M39-B placement (goodness sub-group):
- Ra.tsah names the pleasure of one who encounters the good — the satisfaction of moral evaluation. This is the "finding-good" register of ya.tav (632-003).
- The human receipt of favour (795-003) is the receiving side of grace, not the giving side — it may be a distinct characteristic.

Arguments for a distinct sub-group (M39-C):
- Ra.tsah's unique contribution: the inner pleasure/delight that flows from encountering what is good or acceptable — neither the giving of grace (M39-A) nor the quality of goodness (M39-B), but the inner satisfaction of acceptance itself.
- If the cluster's characteristic map is: blessing/grace (downward from God), goodness (quality of character), and acceptance/delight (the pleasure of receiving and approving) — then M39-C would be the experiential inner register of the grace-encounter.
- T1 test: constitutional location (heart — the seat of pleasure/delight), faculty (affect, moral evaluation), impact (the accepted offering/person stands in favour), structural opposite (rejection/non-acceptance), causal direction (toward what is offered/approved). All five could be satisfied, but the verse base of 54 for a single term is on the thin side for a distinct sub-group.

**Phase 3 decision: Leave ra.tsah as OQ-004 for Phase 4 control read. Do not pre-assign. Read the verses in Phase 4/5 before deciding.**

Phase 4 must determine whether these are M39-B terms that happen to also carry M39-A content (requiring wrong-face tracking at verse level), or whether these terms genuinely carry both characteristics and should be assigned to their dominant characteristic.
