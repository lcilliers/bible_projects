## §1 — Per-verse meaning list (Pass A output)

Pass A meanings recorded by group in obslog (wa-obslog-M39-sessionb-v1-20260514.md, Phase 6 Pass A section). 570 connected verses read. Key patterns by group:

- **2330-001 (chan.nun, 13vv):** Every verse names graciousness as God's constitutional inner attribute — the settled character from which grace flows. Exo 34:6 is the foundational self-declaration; all 12 subsequent verses echo or apply it.
- **795-001 (ra.tsah offerings, 20vv):** Divine acceptance is genuine personal pleasure that can be withheld. Corrupt offerings fail (Mal 1:10; Jer 14:12; Amo 5:22); right offerings accepted (Lev 1:4; Eze 20:40–41). The inner pleasure is real and engaged, not mechanical.
- **795-002 (ra.tsah persons, 9vv):** Divine delight in persons is character-responsive (1Ch 29:17; Psa 147:11), sovereign in election (1Ch 28:4; Isa 42:1), productive of joy in the accepted (Job 33:26), adorning (Psa 149:4).
- **795-003 (ra.tsah human, 23vv):** Ra.tsah at the human level — sought from God (Psa 40:13), received from others (Gen 33:10), misdirected (Psa 49:13; 62:4), withdrawn in crisis (Psa 77:7), remembered as hope (Psa 85:1).
- **494-001 (eudokia divine, 6vv):** Divine inner goodwill as sovereign purpose; Phili 2:13 is the penetrating verse — God's eudokia works inside the human volitional faculty.
- **494-002 (eudokia human, 3vv):** Human goodwill as inner orientation toward others' good (Rom 10:1) and purity of motivation (Phili 1:15).
- **5470-001 (charizō divine, 7vv):** Divine grace-giving — from the maximum (Son given, Rom 8:32) downward through forgiveness (Col 2:13), understanding (1Cor 2:12), honour (Phili 2:9).
- **5470-002 (charizō human forgiveness, 8vv):** Grace propagates — received grace becomes extended grace (Eph 4:32; Col 3:13). Phili 1:29 uniquely names suffering as grace-given.
- **888-001 (charis sovereign, 22vv):** Grace as mode of salvation, justification, calling, election. Logically exclusive of merit (Rom 11:6). Superabundant over sin (Rom 5:20). Reigning characteristic of the new creation (Rom 5:21).
- **888-002 (charis sphere, 13vv):** Grace as sustained standing (Rom 5:2), identity-constituting reality (1Cor 15:10), governing principle (Rom 6:14), source of differentiated gifts (Rom 12:6).
- **888-003 (charis favour, 13vv):** Relational favour — divine standing (Luk 1:30), social community grace (Act 2:47; 4:33), grace in speech (Luk 4:22), grace beyond reciprocity (Luk 6:32–34), corrupted political favour (Act 24:27; 25:3; 25:9).
- **888-004 (charis mission, 36vv):** Grace as the ground and environment of apostolic ministry — the gospel is "the gospel of the grace of God" (Act 20:24); faith is grace-produced (Act 18:27).
- **889-001 (chen divine, ~25vv):** Divine favour as the ground of preservation (Gen 6:8), election (Exo 33:12), and distinctive standing (Exo 33:16–17). Sought through petition (Gen 18:3).
- **889-002 (chen interpersonal, ~30vv):** Interpersonal favour as the condition of access, work, and relationship (Rut 2:2; Est 5:2; 7:3; 8:5). Sought, recognised, and treasured.
- **889-003 (chen character, ~12vv):** Grace as inner character quality expressed in wisdom, conduct, and speech (Pro 1:9; 3:22; 4:9; Psa 45:2). Nah 3:4: grace weaponised as deadly charm.
- **984-001 (cha.nan, 72vv):** The dominant pattern is supplication — the human cry for grace from weakness (Psa 4:1; 6:2; 51:1). Secondary: cha.nan as human generosity (Psa 37:21; 37:26).
- **989-001 (cha.nan Aramaic, 2vv):** Grace-practice toward the oppressed (Dan 4:27); sustained grace-petition as prayer-practice (Dan 6:11).
- **5471-001 (charitoō, 2vv):** The completed-state of grace — Mary permanently marked (Luk 1:28); the community blessed in the Beloved (Eph 1:6).
- **1301-001 (charisma, 14vv):** Grace as Spirit-endowed capacity for community — from eternal life (Rom 6:23) through the full charisma spectrum (1Cor 12) to irrevocable gifting (Rom 11:29).
- **1299-001 (ba.rakh divine, 89vv):** Blessing as creation's first act (Gen 1:22; 2:3); covenantal sovereign gift (Gen 12:2; 22:17); covenant-outcome blessing (Deu 7:13); Psalmic blessing of the righteous (Psa 5:12; 128:1–4).
- **1299-002 (ba.rakh worship, vv):** The human blessing of God as doxological acknowledgment — the creature recognising divine worth; grace generating its reflexive response.
- **1299-003 (ba.rakh priestly/prophetic, vv):** The human declaration of divine grace over others — Aaronic formula (Num 6:24), patriarchal blessing (Gen 14:19), priestly extension.
- **1299-004 (ba.rakh ironic, vv):** Grace vocabulary applied to its opposite — cursing God (Job 1:11; 2:9), self-blessing in greed (Psa 10:3), the linguistic corruption of blessing.
