## T4 — Relational Interfaces

### T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human

**T4.1.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Yes — this is the dominant and most extensively evidenced direction in M39-A. The verse landscape across all 23 groups is overwhelmingly God-as-subject. The modes: (1) Creation-blessing: Gen 1:22; 1:28; 2:3 (1299-001) — God blesses creatures and the Sabbath; the direction precedes human existence. (2) Covenantal blessing-promise: Gen 12:2; 22:17 (1299-001) — the Abrahamic covenant as the vehicle of downward grace. (3) Character-declaration: Exo 34:6 (2330-001) — God declaring his own gracious character. (4) Soteriological grace: Eph 2:8; Rom 3:24 (888-001) — God justifying through grace. (5) Acceptance and delight: ra.tsah groups 795-001 and 795-002 — God accepting offerings and delighting in persons. (6) Distribution of charismata: 1Cor 12:4 (1301-001) — the Spirit distributing gifts.

**[B — Goodness]** Yes — but as the affirming/evaluating direction rather than the initiating-gift direction. 1Ki 8:18 (542-001): "you did well that it was in your heart" — God evaluating and affirming human goodness. 2Ki 10:30 (632-002): "you have done well in carrying out what is right in my eyes" — God's moral endorsement of right action. Eze 36:11 (632-002): "I will do more good to you than ever before" — God giving goodness as a future gift to his people.

---

**T4.1.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** The basis is sovereign and character-grounded, not merit-earned: (1) Sovereignty: Rom 11:5 ("a remnant, chosen by grace") — election without merit-basis. (2) Character: Exo 34:6 (2330-001) — God extends grace because graciousness is who he is; the basis is his own character, not the recipient's worthiness. (3) Covenantal: the Abrahamic blessing (Gen 12:2–3) — grace extended within and through the covenant structure. (4) Exclusion of works: Rom 11:6 ("if it is by grace, it is no longer on the basis of works") — the basis explicitly excludes merit. The basis for God's grace is his own gracious character operating sovereignly.

**[B — Goodness]** The basis for God's affirmation of goodness is the alignment of human inner-being with God's own standard: 1Ki 8:18 — the good intention in the heart corresponds to what is right in God's sight. God affirms what coheres with his own character of goodness. Eze 36:11 — the divine gift of goodness is eschatologically unconditional (future blessing without stated condition).

**T4.1.3** — What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** God's disposition is constitutively toward-the-person in grace: the verse evidence (particularly the Exo 34:6 formula and its echoes — 2330-001) reveals that graciousness is not an occasional attitude but God's settled relational character toward humans. He bends toward the lesser (the CHANAN root meaning). He gives what the person cannot generate (Eph 2:8). He accepts those he has been petitioned by (984-001). He takes personal pleasure in those he has formed (Isa 42:1 — 795-002). God's disposition toward the human person in M39-A is one of constitutive favour, initiating love, and personal engagement.

**[B — Goodness]** God's disposition in the goodness direction: he evaluates and affirms inner goodness (1Ki 8:18), he gives goodness generously (Eze 36:11), and he is himself the standard of what good is (the "what is right in my eyes" formula across 632-002; 795-002). God's disposition toward the human in M39-B is that of the moral evaluator who is also the source and standard of goodness — he does not merely judge but gives what he requires.

**T4.1.4** — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.

**[A, B]** Not applicable to either sub-group. The God-to-human direction is the most extensively evidenced relational direction in M39.

### T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God

**T4.2.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Yes — extensively in two modes: (1) Supplication: the entire cha.nan corpus (984-001, 72 verses) is the human cry for grace directed upward to God — "be gracious to me, O Lord" (Psa 4:1; 6:2; 27:7; 51:1; etc.). This is the human moving toward God specifically in the grace-seeking posture. (2) Worship-blessing: ba.rakh group 1299-002 — the human blessing of God in worship (Psa 34:1; 103:1; 16:7). The blessing-directed-upward is the doxological response: the creature acknowledging God's worth through the grace-vocabulary directed upward.

**[B — Goodness]** Partial. The volitional-surrender register (tov group 542-001 — 2Sa 15:26: "let him do to me what seems good to him") shows the human surrendering their will to divine disposition — an act of movement toward God in trust. Pro 15:2 (632-002) — the wise tongue commending knowledge — approaches this through wisdom rather than direct God-directed movement.

---

**T4.2.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Three postures are named across the supplication corpus: (1) Need/affliction: Psa 6:2 ("I am languishing"); Psa 25:16 ("I am lonely and afflicted"); Psa 31:9 ("I am in distress") — the posture of acknowledged need is the ground of the petition. (2) Dependence: the supplicant's posture throughout 984-001 is dependent, not demanding — "be gracious to me" is a plea, not a claim. (3) The Exo 34:6 character-ground: Joe 2:13 ("return to the Lord your God, for he is gracious and merciful") — the ground of approaching God is his known character, not the suppliant's merit.

**[B — Goodness]** Trust and surrender: 2Sa 15:26 (tov — "let him do to me what seems good to him") shows the posture of complete inner surrender to divine goodness-judgment. The movement toward God in M39-B is through trust in the goodness of divine disposition.

---

**T4.2.3** — What does the human-to-God direction reveal about the person's relationship with God?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** The supplication corpus (984-001) reveals that the human-to-God direction in M39-A is structurally dependent and sustained: 72 verses of petition across the Psalms, Job, and prophetic literature show that the human person's relationship with God is characterised by ongoing need for grace. The relationship is not one of equals exchanging goods but of creature and Creator in which the creature's movement toward God is constitutively a plea for what cannot be self-generated. This is not a deficient relationship but the appropriate relational form for the creature who receives existence as grace.

**[B — Goodness]** The human-to-God direction in M39-B is less developed in the verse evidence — it appears primarily in the surrender-to-divine-goodness form (2Sa 15:26) rather than as petition. This is consistent with M39-B's primarily individual-constitutional character: goodness is what the person is before God, not primarily what the person seeks from God.

---

**T4.2.4** — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Not silent — human-to-God is the dominant mode of the cha.nan corpus and the ba.rakh worship corpus.

**[B — Goodness]** Partially present — trust-surrender (2Sa 15:26) and the goodness-affirmed-by-God pattern (1Ki 8:18). Less extensive than M39-A.

### T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving

**T4.3.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Yes — in three forms: (1) Forgiveness: charizō group 5470-002 (Eph 4:32; Col 3:13; 2Cor 2:7; 2Cor 2:10) — the human extending grace-forgiveness to another. (2) Generosity: cha.nan group 984-001 (Psa 37:21; 37:26: "he is ever lending generously, and his children become a blessing") — the gracious-generous person giving to others. (3) Priestly/prophetic blessing of others: ba.rakh group 1299-003 (Num 6:24; Gen 14:19; Gen 9:26) — the human mediating divine grace-blessing over another. (4) Chen as character quality: group 889-003 (Pro 1:9; 3:22; 4:9) — the gracious person whose wisdom-conduct produces grace in community.

**[B — Goodness]** Yes — through good action and good speech: ya.tav group 632-002 (the pattern of doing good toward others); Pro 15:2 (632-002 — the tongue of the wise commending knowledge); the communal approval texts (632-003 — goodness of judgment shared in community).

---

**T4.3.2** — What inner conditions in the giver enable genuine extension?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Reception must precede extension: Eph 4:32 ("as God in Christ forgave you") — the condition is having received divine forgiveness first. Col 3:13 ("as the Lord has forgiven you, so you also must forgive") — the same structure. Psa 37:26 ("he is ever lending generously, and his children become a blessing") — the generosity flows from the person who has received blessing. The science extract (Section 1) confirms: McCullough's gratitude model shows that the recognition of undeserved benefit motivates prosocial giving. The condition for genuine grace-extension is grace received.

**[B — Goodness]** The inner condition is the sustained moral orientation: ya.tav group 632-002's pattern (Psa 36:3 — ceasing to do good as a moral failure) implies the positive condition: the ongoing inner alignment with goodness enables continued good action. Gen 4:7 — "if you do well" — sustained goodness requires sustained inner orientation.

---

**T4.3.3** — What must the person have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** The verse evidence is explicit: grace must be received before it can be extended. This is the propagating structure confirmed by Eph 4:32; Col 3:13; Luk 7:42–43 (the one with the larger cancelled debt loves more). The science extract (Section 1 — McCullough's three-component model: benefit received, attribution to benefactor, recognition of disproportionate gift) confirms the psychological structure: gratitude produces prosocial reciprocity, which in the theological frame is the extension of grace to others. A person cannot genuinely extend grace who has not received and recognised grace.

**[B — Goodness]** The person must have cultivated the inner moral orientation through sustained practice. The science extract (Section 2 — virtue psychology, Aristotle: virtues are formed through habit) confirms: graciousness and goodness arise through repeated acts that consolidate into stable dispositions. The condition is formative: the person must have become good through sustained practice before good action flows naturally.

---

**T4.3.4** — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.

**[A, B]** Not silent — human-giving is evidenced in both sub-groups, more extensively in M39-A.

### T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving

**T4.4.1** — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Yes — extensively: Gen 6:8 (889-001 — Noah finding favour); Gen 33:10 (795-003 — Jacob received by Esau); Rut 2:2 (889-002 — Ruth finding favour); 1Sa 2:26 (542-001 — Samuel growing in favour); Luk 1:30 (888-003 — Mary finding favour); Act 7:10 (888-003 — Joseph receiving favour before Pharaoh). The receiving of grace/favour from another person is a pattern across both Testaments and across multiple terms.

**[B — Goodness]** Yes — in the receiving-evaluation form: 1Ki 8:18 (God's affirmation received by David — implicitly); 2Sa 3:36 (goodness of the king's action received by the people as approval). The community receiving and affirming goodness is the human-interface form.

---

**T4.4.2** — What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Enabling: the posture of need and openness. Gen 18:3 (889-001 — Abraham: "if I have found favour in your sight, do not pass by") — the acknowledgment of dependence on another's favour enables reception. Blocking: pride and self-sufficiency (the merit-claim pattern — Rom 11:6 in reverse: the person who believes they have earned what they need is closed to receiving it as grace). The science extract (Section 1 — Emmons/Mauss): entitlement eliminates the gratitude response; the perception that the benefit was owed blocks the grace-reception dynamic.

**[B — Goodness]** S — No explicit blocking condition for receiving the goodness characteristic from another in M39-B's verse set.

---

**T4.4.3** — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters but does not receive this characteristic?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Anguish and crisis: Psa 77:7 (795-003 — "will the Lord spurn forever, and never again be favorable?") — the person who encounters the absence of divine ra.tsah experiences existential anguish. The inversion form (1299-004 — Job 2:9; Nah 3:4) shows the person who encounters grace's form but in its corrupted state — either encountering grace in order to curse it or encountering grace weaponised against them.

**[B — Goodness]** Gen 4:7 — the person who does not do well encounters sin crouching at the door. The inner state of not-doing-well is hazardous: the characteristic's absence does not produce neutral ground but opens to the opposite.

---

**T4.4.4** — If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.

**[A, B]** Not silent. Both sub-groups evidence the receiving direction.

### T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries

**T4.5.1** — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Both within and across: (1) Within existing bonds: chen group 889-002 (the favour-dynamic within the servant-master or suppliant-benefactor relationship — Gen 18:3; Rut 2:2; Est 5:2). Ba.rakh group 1299-003 (the priestly blessing within Israel's covenantal structure — Num 6:24). (2) Across relational distance: ba.rakh group 1299-001 (Gen 12:3 — "in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed") — the Abrahamic blessing explicitly crosses tribal/national boundaries. Charis group 888-001 (Rom 4:16 — "guaranteed to all his offspring — not only to the adherent of the law but also to the one who shares the faith of Abraham") — grace crossing the Jew/Gentile boundary.

**[B — Goodness]** Primarily within existing social structure: the approval and favour-goodness texts (632-003; 542-001) operate within the person's relational context (community approval, royal approval, family wellbeing). The goodness characteristic does not show the same boundary-crossing pattern as M39-A.

---

**T4.5.2** — Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Crosses covenantal boundaries — this is one of M39-A's most significant structural findings. The Abrahamic blessing is explicitly universally scoped from its initial statement (Gen 12:3; 22:18 — "all families of the earth... all nations"). The chen favour dynamic operates outside explicit covenant: Gen 6:8 (Noah finding favour — pre-Abrahamic), Gen 33:10 (Jacob/Esau — across brotherly enmity), Rut 2:2 (Ruth — Moabite finding favour). Act 7:10 (Joseph before Pharaoh — favour operating entirely outside Israel's covenant structure). The grace-characteristic is not bounded by covenant membership.

**[B — Goodness]** Primarily within covenantal-social structure in the verse evidence. Eze 36:11 (eschatological goodness given to Israel) is covenantally framed. No verse in M39-B shows goodness explicitly crossing national or covenant boundaries in the way grace does.

---

**T4.5.3** — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic?

**[A — Blessing and Grace]** Universal scope at its outer limit: the Abrahamic blessing-to-all-nations pattern (1299-001) and the NT charis as the ground of Gentile inclusion (888-001 — Rom 4:16; Gal 1:15) establish that the grace-characteristic has no inherent relational boundary. Its scope is as wide as God's gracious character — constitutively toward every creature.

**[B — Goodness]** Community-scoped: M39-B's verse evidence positions goodness as primarily operative within the person's social-relational context. Its scope is not unlimited in the same way grace's is — goodness operates within existing relational structures more than across their outer boundaries.

---

**T4.5.4** — If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note this explicitly.

**[A, B]** Not silent. Both sub-groups have evidence for relational scope, differing in extent.
