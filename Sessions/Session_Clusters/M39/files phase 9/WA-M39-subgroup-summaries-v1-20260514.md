## M39-A: Blessing and Grace

The verses of M39-A establish blessing and grace as the founding movement of God's relationship to creation. The cluster opens at creation itself: Gen 1:22 and Gen 2:3 show God blessing creatures and the Sabbath before any human failing has occurred. Blessing is not remedial; it is constitutive. The sovereign favour of God initiates and sustains existence.

What the verses reveal as the inner-being characteristic: grace is a downward-initiated, personally engaged, unearned disposition of the greater toward the lesser. It cannot be compelled — ra.tsah in Mal 1:10 ("I have no pleasure in you...I will not accept an offering from your hand") and throughout the prophetic corpus shows that divine acceptance can be withheld, which means it is genuinely given when present. The inner being of God is engaged, not mechanically transacted.

The characteristic operates in four identifiable forms across the verses. First, the sovereign gift: ba.rakh across the Abrahamic promises (Gen 12:2, 22:17), charis in Eph 2:8 ("by grace you have been saved"), charisma in Rom 6:23 ("the free gift of God is eternal life"). Second, the sphere of standing: charis in Rom 5:2 ("this grace in which we stand"), chen in Gen 6:8 ("Noah found favour"), charitoō at Luk 1:28 (Mary "highly favoured"). Third, the act of extending grace: cha.nan across the Psalms as the divine response to the human cry for mercy (Psa 4:1; 51:1; 86:15), charizō in the forgiveness texts (Eph 4:32; Col 3:13). Fourth, the divine inner pleasure as the ground of blessing: eudokia in Luk 2:14 and Mat 11:26, ra.tsah in 1Ch 29:17 and Isa 42:1.

The characteristic is propagating: received grace becomes grace extended. Eph 4:32 makes this explicit — forgive as God forgave you. The grace-characteristic does not terminate in the recipient but generates a corresponding inner posture of gratitude, supplication, and extension.

## M39-B: Goodness

The verses of M39-B locate the goodness characteristic primarily in the heart as the seat of moral evaluation and affective response to what is genuinely good. The cluster's governing anchor is 1Ki 8:18 and 2Ch 6:8: "it was in your heart to build a house for my name — you did well that it was in your heart." The goodness is a heart-reality before it becomes an act; the inner quality of the good intention is itself what God names as good. This is the characteristic's most precise formulation: goodness as an inner being-quality, not merely an external performance.

Psa 119:71 extends this: "It is good for me that I was afflicted, that I might learn your statutes." The goodness-evaluative faculty is operating under pressure — the person formed by suffering assesses it as good in its ultimate effect. This names the moral evaluation faculty at depth, working against natural preference.

The gladness dimension (Pro 15:13; 17:22 — "a glad heart makes a cheerful face...a joyful heart is good medicine") reveals the somatic consequence of the goodness characteristic: the inner state of gladness-at-good affects the face and the body. The characteristic is not merely cognitive; it has physical expression. Its absence ("crushed spirit dries up the bones") makes the inverse equally clear.

The structural link to M39-A is embedded in Gen 4:7: "If you do well, will you not be accepted?" The moral goodness of action (M39-B) produces divine acceptance (M39-A's ra.tsah). The two sub-groups are not parallel but connected in sequence — goodness produces the condition in which grace-acceptance can operate. Pro 15:2, Jer 1:12, and Eze 36:11 (P-status verses awaiting group assignment) will enrich the goodness characteristic further in Phase 7.
