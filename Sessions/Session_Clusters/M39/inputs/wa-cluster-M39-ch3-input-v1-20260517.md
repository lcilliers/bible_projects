The Exo 34:6 formula — "the Lord, the Lord, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness" — is not a description of what God does on occasion but of what God is by self-declaration. Group 2330-001 (all 13 chan.nun verses) sustains this portrait: the formula recurs in Psa 86:15; 103:8; 111:4; 116:5; 145:8; Joe 2:13; Jon 4:2, naming graciousness as the stable identity of God across narrative, prophetic, and psalmic contexts. Within M39-A, ba.rakh group 1299-001 (Gen 1:22; 1:28; 2:3) shows that blessing is the first outward act of this character — God blesses at creation before any human need arises. Charis group 888-001 (Eph 2:8; 2Cor 12:9) locates this character in its NT fullness: grace is sovereign, transforming, and sufficient in weakness. The character of God reflected here is: initiating, abundant, undeserved, personally engaged, and structurally prior to any human response.

The significance for the human person: because grace originates in God's character and not in human merit, the human person's reception of blessing is not an achievement but a constitutive gift.
