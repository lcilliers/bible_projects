**VERDICT: BOUNDARY** — the dominant register is punishment/torment/fear (closer to M01-Fear or M35-Testing), but 2Pe 2:8 contributes genuine inner anguish content. Not strongly M03; not cleanly transferable either. Held for researcher decision.

**B — Mourning and Lamentation (the public / ritual act)**
Terms that name the social, formal, or outward form of mourning — the act of lamentation. sa.phad (29 vv — the classic ritual mourning verb), mis.ped (14 vv — lamentation as communal outpouring), koptō (2 vv — wailing), a.val (25 vv — to mourn, broadly), a.vel (7 vv — mourning as noun/state), e.vel (22 vv — mourning-period/social form), mar.ze.ach (1 v — house of mourning), ta.a.niy.yah (2 vv — mourning multiplied), pen.the.o (10 vv — to mourn), penthos (4 vv — grief/mourning noun), odurmos (1 v — mourning reported to Paul), koptō overlaps A/B — primary B.

**C — Sorrow and Inner Grief (the state)**
Terms that name grief as a settled inner condition or emotional state located in the soul/heart, distinct from its outward expression. lupeō (21 vv — to grieve, inner state throughout), perilupos (5 vv — soul-sorrowful to death), tu.gah (4 vv — grief as inner sorrow dissolving the soul), ya.gon (14 vv — sorrow lodged in the heart), sullupeō (1 v — Jesus grieved with anger), de.a.von (1 v — sorrow in the soul as languishing).
These terms characteristically describe the interior quality of the grief rather than its outward form.

**D — Anguish and Distress (crushing/pressing register)**
Terms naming the pressing, constricting, overwhelming quality of grief-anguish — the experience of being hemmed in, crushed, overwhelmed. tsar (28 vv), tsa.rah (62 vv), me.tsu.qah (7 vv), sunochē (2 vv), odunē (2 vv), odunaō (4 vv).

**E — Groaning and Sighing**

Terms naming the specific inner quality of bitterness that arises from grief, loss, and suffering — as distinct from anger-bitterness (which transferred to M02). me.mer (1 v — bitterness as sharp pain from son's failure), mam.ror (1 v — suffocating bitterness under divine pressure), mo.rah (1 v — life made bitter by relational friction), mor.rah (1 v — bitterness sealed in the heart), ma.ror (1 v — God filling the inner being with bitterness), pikrainō (3 vv — embittering as wounding another's spirit; and inner distress of prophetic weight).
These terms all show bitterness as an inner-being quality that arises from sorrow, loss, suffering, or divine dealings — not from anger. They form a coherent sub-group.
