**T2.3.2** — What does heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's function does this characteristic engage?

**[CLUSTER]**

### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**T2.9.1** — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — The corpus evidences multiple constitutional origins for grief, varying by sub-group. (1) Received from outside through loss and adversity: grief arises when what was bonded to is taken away — bereavement (M03-A-VCG-01), national catastrophe (M03-B-VCG-02), divine judgment (M03-B-VCG-03), siege-distress (M03-D-VCG-04). The external event produces the internal grief state. (2) Bestowed by God: "the sorrow in the soul as a languishing...God gives as punishment" (Deu 28:65 — M03-C-VCG-01 anchor); God puts the servant to grief (Isa 53:10 — M03-BOUNDARY); God fills with bitterness (Job 9:18 — M03-G-VCG-01 anchor; Lam 3:15 — M03-G-VCG-01 anchor). God can initiate grief directly in the inner being. (3) Generated from moral perception within the person: conscience-driven sighing (Eze 9:4 — M03-E-VCG-01); grief arising from witnessing injustice (Isa 53:10; Job 30:25 — M03-A-VCG-04); Peter's grief from conscience recognising past denial (Joh 21:17 — M03-C-VCG-01). (4) Self-inflicted: anguish self-inflicted through spiritual defection driven by greed (1Ti 6:10 — M03-D-VCG-02).

---

**T2.9.2** — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — The origin is clearly multiple. The corpus evidences four distinct origins: external loss/adversity (predominant), divine bestowal, internal moral perception, and self-infliction. This multiplicity reveals that grief is not a simple reactive state but a complex inner-being characteristic that can arise through multiple constitutional pathways. The divine-bestowal origin is theologically distinctive — it shows that God himself initiates grief in the inner person as part of redemptive or disciplinary purpose, making grief not merely a creaturely response but a divinely governed inner state.

---

**T2.9.3** — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — Yes. In M03-A-VCG-02 (penitential weeping), grief originates from moral self-recognition before God — the internal perception of one's own sin. In M03-A-VCG-01 (bereavement weeping), grief originates from the external event of death or separation. In M03-D-VCG-03 (covenantal discipline), grief originates from God's disciplinary use of adversity to press the will toward him. In M03-C-VCG-03 (Gethsemane), Jesus's grief originates from the Father's will and the approaching cross — a unique origin that is simultaneously personal and redemptive. The origin shifts from purely external to divinely-initiated to internally-generated depending on the context and the person's covenantal position.

### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**T2.10.1** — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — Yes. The corpus provides evidence of movement in multiple directions across constitutional levels.

**[A-VCG-04]**
E — Inner-to-outer (soul to body): the prophetic grief of Jer 13:17 (M03-A-VCG-04 anchor da.ma) is explicitly named as the "soul's hidden grief" becoming visible tears in the eyes — from soul-origin to body-expression. This is the paradigmatic inner-to-outer movement of grief in M03.

**[E-VCG-01]**
E — Inner-through-outer-to-God: Israel's groaning (a.nach — Exo 2:23) is involuntary inner expression that rises to God and triggers covenantal response. The movement is: inner suffering → somatic groan → rises to God → divine hearing and action. This three-stage movement (inner → somatic → divine) is the constitutional movement of the classic oppression-groaning pattern.

**[D-VCG-01]**
E — Inner-to-upward-to-God: the psalmic distress pattern shows anguish in the heart and soul moving upward in prayer to God (Psa 18:6 — M03-D-VCG-01 anchor): distress → cry to God → divine answer from his temple. This is inner-to-divine movement without the somatic intermediate: the constitutional movement is direct inner-being-to-God.

**[C-VCG-02]**
E — Inner-to-moral-reformation: godly grief in 2Cor 7:9-11 (M03-C-VCG-02 anchor) moves from inner grief through repentance into will-reshaping and moral energy. This is movement from affective condition to volitional-moral consequence — grief transforming the interior.

---

**T2.10.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?

**[CLUSTER]**
E — Two dominant movement patterns appear. Pattern 1 (Expressive): inner grief condition → somatic expression (tears, groaning) → external visibility → potentially reaching God or another person. Pattern 2 (Prayerful): inner distress condition → cry or groan directed to God → divine response and deliverance. Pattern 1 is the movement of M03-A and M03-E; Pattern 2 is the dominant movement of M03-D. A third pattern appears in M03-C-VCG-02: inner grief condition → repentance → moral reformation — grief moving through the person's inner life without necessarily passing through somatic expression. The patterns show that grief's constitutional movement has several forms depending on the context and the direction in which it is oriented.
