## §1. Weak M03 Content Flags (M03-A)

The following verses in M03-A have meanings that note weeping is not authentic grief but manipulative, performative, or directed at a non-grief object. These were assigned to M03-A VCGs during Phase 7 design, but the meanings suggest limited or absent inner-being grief content in the M03 register.

| vc_id | Reference | Term | Current sub-group | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 54565 | Judg 14:16 | ba.khah | M03-A | Meaning: weeping as manipulative emotional strategy to extract a secret — "directed outward as social pressure rather than expressing authentic inner sorrow" |
| 54566 | Judg 14:17 | ba.khah | M03-A | Meaning: sustained weeping as persistent emotional coercion — no authentic M03 inner grief evidenced |

**Observation:** These two verses are assigned to A-VCG-01 (Bereavement and Personal Loss Weeping) by default, but their meanings clearly note that the weeping is strategic, not grief-driven. They may be candidates for set-aside at Phase 8 or 9, or for a marginal note that this is manipulative-weeping rather than M03's characteristic.
**Recommendation:** Retain in current sub-group but CC to note in verse_context.notes. No sub-group change — this is a Phase 7 observation only.

## §2. Divine Inner-State Set-Aside Candidates

Per brief §1, the brief directs watching for verses where the content focuses on God or the Spirit *inwardly experiencing* grief as a passion/inner state rather than enacting it — analogous to M01's precedents.

### Assessment: No clear set-aside candidates identified

After reading the full corpus, no verses clearly warrant divine-inner-state set-aside in M03. The candidate verses are:

**Eph 4:30 (lupeō, vc 12400) — "the Holy Spirit can be grieved"**
The meaning record: "the Spirit's inner-being response to moral failure is described as grief, showing that the Spirit is personally affected by the community's choices." The brief §1 identifies this as borderline. On reading: this verse describes the Spirit's grief as a *responsive* inner-being phenomenon — grief as the Spirit's relational response to sin. This is characteristic-in-operation rather than a description of the Spirit's independent divine inner life as a passion. Per brief §1: "keep in." Assigned to M03-C-VCG-02 (Godly Grief). **Not a set-aside candidate.**

**Isa 63:9 (tsar and tsa.rah, vc_ids 65120 and 65162) — "in all their distress he was afflicted"**
The meaning: "God himself shares in Israel's distress; his solidarity is so complete that in all their distress he was afflicted." This is solidarity language — God participates in Israel's suffering, not a description of God's independent inner-state of distress. Per brief §1: keep. Assigned to M03-D-VCG-03. **Not a set-aside candidate.**

**Conclusion:** No divine-inner-state set-aside candidates identified in M03's corpus. The M03 cluster's grief vocabulary consistently describes human grief, with Eph 4:30 (Spirit's grief) and Isa 63:9 (divine solidarity) as the only proximate candidates — both retained per brief guidance.
