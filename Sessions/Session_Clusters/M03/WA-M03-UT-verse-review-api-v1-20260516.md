## Borderline entries — held for researcher decision (not in patch)

- **H0056 a.val** vr=2895 Jer 12:11 — The desolate land mourns 'to me' (to God), suggesting a quasi-personal lament directed at the divine, which edges toward the inner-lament register, but the subject remains the land.
  > Jer 12:11 They have made it a desolation ; desolate , it mourns to me. The whole land is made desolate , but no man lays it to heart .
- **H0056 a.val** vr=2901 Eze 31:15 — God 'caused mourning' at the cedar's fall and clothed Lebanon in gloom, suggesting divine-initiated mourning response that edges toward M03's divine-grief register, but the subjects are cosmic/natural.
  > Eze 31:15 “ Thus says the Lord God : On the day the cedar went down to Sheol I caused mourning ; I closed the deep over it, and restrained its rivers , and many waters were stopped . I clothed Lebanon in gloom for it, and all the trees of the field fainted because of it.
- **H0057 a.vel** vr=66912 Lam 1:4 — Roads personified as mourning due to absence of festival-goers; grief is communal/civic lament over Jerusalem's desolation, but the subject is inanimate roads, making inner-being location ambiguous.
  > Lam 1:4 The roads to Zion mourn , for none come to the festival ; all her gates are desolate ; her priests groan ; her virgins have been afflicted , and she herself suffers bitterly .
- **H5999 a.mal** vr=958 Ecc 2:22 — a.mal paired with 'striving of heart' — the combination of toil and inner heart-striving creates ambiguity between mere labour description and inner grief/anguish over futile effort.
  > Ecc 2:22 What has a man from all the toil and striving of heart with which he toils beneath the sun ?
- **H6869B tsa.rah** vr=521 Psa 78:49 — Distress here appears in a list of divine wrath attributes sent against Egypt — could be externally inflicted anguish or a distinct word; inner-being engagement is indirect.
  > Psa 78:49 He let loose on them his burning anger , wrath , indignation , and distress , a company of destroying angels .
- **H6869B tsa.rah** vr=537 Isa 30:6 — A land of trouble and anguish describes geographical/physical danger zone for the caravan; inner-being distress is implicit but the primary sense is external terrain.
  > Isa 30:6 An oracle on the beasts of the Negeb . Through a land of trouble and anguish , from where come the lioness and the lion , the adder and the flying fiery serpent , they carry their riches on the backs of donkeys , and their treasures on the humps of camels , to a people that cannot profit them.
- **H6869B tsa.rah** vr=553 Hab 3:16 — The verse portrays profound somatic inner distress (trembling body, quivering lips, rottenness in bones) alongside waiting for a 'day of trouble', blending inner anguish and external crisis ambiguously.
  > Hab 3:16 I hear , and my body trembles ; my lips quiver at the sound ; rottenness enters into my bones ; my legs tremble beneath me. Yet I will quietly wait for the day of trouble to come upon people who invade us .
- **H7451C ra.ah** vr=607 2Sa 12:18 — Servants fear David may 'do himself harm' in extreme grief — the ra.ah is the self-harm action, but the context is profound inner grief; the term itself targets the action not the sorrow.
  > 2Sa 12:18 On the seventh day the child died . And the servants of David were afraid to tell him that the child was dead , for they said , “ Behold , while the child was yet alive , we spoke to him, and he did not listen to us . How then can we say to him the child is dead ? He may do himself some harm .”
- **H7451C ra.ah** vr=619 2Ki 6:33 — The messenger says 'this trouble is from the Lord' in context of extreme siege famine — ra.ah is external calamity but the speaker's despair ('why wait?') edges toward inner anguish; borderline.
  > 2Ki 6:33 And while he was still speaking with them, the messenger came down to him and said , “ This trouble is from the Lord ! Why should I wait for the Lord any longer ?”
- **H7451I ra.ah** vr=156503 Jer 2:19 — ra.ah appears twice: once as sinful conduct that punishes and once as a bitter inner experience ('evil and bitter for you'), touching the M03 bitterness-of-soul register but primarily denoting moral evil.
