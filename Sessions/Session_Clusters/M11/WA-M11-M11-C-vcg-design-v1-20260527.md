## §2. VCG definitions

### M11-C-VCG-01 — NT divine release-of-sin proclamation

**Context:** The central NT proclamation that sins are released through Christ's name / covenant blood / repentance-faith. afesis as the NT release-of-sin noun (Act 2:38, 5:31, 10:43, 13:38, 26:18, Col 1:14, Eph 1:7, Luk 1:77, Luk 3:3, Luk 24:47, Mar 1:4, Mat 26:28); the eschatological-arc completion verses (Heb 9:22 blood-required-for-release, Heb 10:18 release achieved obviates further sacrifice); Luk 4:18 captivity-liberation proclaimed; plus the exaleifō sin-erasure verses (Act 3:19 sins blotted out, Col 2:14 legal debt nailed to cross).

**Members:** vc=17507 (Eph 1:7), vc=17508 (Mat 26:28), vc=17509 (Mar 1:4), vc=17510 (Luk 1:77), vc=17511 (Luk 3:3), vc=17512 (Luk 4:18), vc=17513 (Luk 24:47), vc=17514 (Act 2:38), vc=17515 (Act 5:31), vc=17516 (Act 10:43), vc=17517 (Act 13:38), vc=17518 (Act 26:18), vc=17519 (Col 1:14), vc=17521 (Heb 9:22), vc=17522 (Heb 10:18), vc=2266 (Act 3:19), vc=2268 (Col 2:14) — **17 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17508 (Mat 26:28) — covenant blood for the forgiveness of sins.

---

### M11-C-VCG-02 — Release foreclosed (sin against Holy Spirit)

**Context:** Mar 3:29 — the limit-condition for release. Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit places one beyond forgiveness; the inner state is a hardened, permanent guilt that cannot be released. Parallel to M11-B-VCG-04 (forgiveness foreclosed) and M11-A-VCG-08 (atonement denied) — the negative pole running across release / forgiveness / atonement registers.

**Members:** vc=17520 (Mar 3:29) — **1 verse**

**Anchor:** vc=17520 (Mar 3:29) — only member.

---

### M11-C-VCG-03 — Discipleship-call leaving

**Context:** G0863G (leave) used for the disciples' volitional detachment from livelihood, family, and prior life in immediate response to the Christic call. Inner-being content is decisive will-surrender; the released attachments are nets, boats, fathers, fields, houses, spouses, the unburied dead. This is the term's primary M30/M29 register per Phase 3 — not M11-primary content, but co-located in this sub-group because afiēmi-leave belongs here lexically. Phase D T6 will articulate the cross-register relationship.

**Members:** vc=17523 (Mar 10:29), vc=17524 (Mat 4:20), vc=17525 (Mat 4:22), vc=17526 (Mat 8:22), vc=17527 (Mat 19:27), vc=17528 (Mat 19:29), vc=17529 (Mar 1:18), vc=17530 (Mar 1:20), vc=17531 (Mar 10:28), vc=17532 (Luk 5:11), vc=17533 (Luk 9:60), vc=17534 (Luk 18:28), vc=17535 (Luk 18:29) — **13 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17529 (Mar 1:18) — the disciples immediately left their nets to follow.

---

### M11-C-VCG-04 — Reconciliation-prioritizing release / bond maintained

**Context:** The M11-primary G0863G verses — release operating in service of relational restoration or covenant-bond maintenance. Mat 5:24 (leave gift at altar to be reconciled first — relational repair prioritized over religious duty); 1Cor 7:11/12/13 (do not abandon spouse — bond maintenance); Heb 6:1 (leaving rudimentary stages to advance in faith — release as growth); Joh 8:29 (the Father has not left the Son — covenant bond preserved by faithfulness).

**Members:** vc=17542 (1Cor 7:11), vc=17543 (1Cor 7:12), vc=17544 (1Cor 7:13), vc=17545 (Mat 5:24), vc=17548 (Joh 8:29), vc=17549 (Heb 6:1) — **6 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17545 (Mat 5:24) — leave gift to reconcile first.

---

### M11-C-VCG-05 — Loyalty failure / abandonment of bond

**Context:** The negative inverse of VCG-04 — abandonment as failure of inner loyalty. The disciples flee Gethsemane (Mar 14:50, Mat 26:56); the hired hand abandons the sheep (Joh 10:12); the religious leaders abandon God's command for tradition (Mar 7:8); the weightier matters of justice/mercy/faithfulness are neglected (Mat 23:23); first love is abandoned (Rev 2:4 — the canonical inner-withdrawal that requires the M11-D return).

**Members:** vc=17536 (Rev 2:4), vc=17537 (Mat 23:23), vc=17538 (Mar 7:8), vc=17539 (Mat 26:56), vc=17540 (Mar 14:50), vc=17541 (Joh 10:12) — **6 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17536 (Rev 2:4) — abandoning first love.

---

### M11-C-VCG-06 — Eschatological separation (passive division)

**Context:** afiēmi-leave used for end-time separation — one taken and one left (Mat 24:40/41, Luk 17:34/35); the house left forsaken (Luk 13:35). Inner-being content is the consequential division of destiny; no active forgiveness or release in the redemptive sense. M38 (eschatological separation) is the primary register per Phase 3.

**Members:** vc=17550 (Mat 24:40), vc=17551 (Mat 24:41), vc=17552 (Luk 17:34), vc=17553 (Luk 17:35), vc=17554 (Luk 13:35) — **5 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17550 (Mat 24:40) — canonical one-taken-one-left.

---

### M11-C-VCG-07 — Volitional release for care / mission priority

**Context:** afiēmi-leave used for purposive release in service of higher mission or care — leaving blind guides alone (Mat 15:14, disengaging will from fruitless engagement); leaving ninety-nine to seek the one (Mat 18:12, Christological priority of the lost). Inner-being content is the will redirecting away from one object in order to attend to another.

**Members:** vc=17546 (Mat 15:14), vc=17547 (Mat 18:12) — **2 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17547 (Mat 18:12) — leave the ninety-nine to seek the one.

---

### M11-C-VCG-08 — Permission granted / welcoming openness

**Context:** G0863I-permit used in welcoming / receiving / non-resisting postures. Children-permitted-to-come (Mat 19:14, Mar 10:14, Luk 18:16); permission to fulfill righteousness (Mat 3:15); non-resistance generosity (Mat 5:40); Gamaliel's wait-and-see non-interference (Act 5:38). Inner-being content is the receptive, yielding will.

**Members:** vc=17626 (Mar 10:14), vc=17627 (Mat 3:15), vc=17628 (Mat 5:40), vc=17630 (Mat 19:14), vc=17635 (Luk 18:16), vc=17637 (Act 5:38) — **6 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17626 (Mar 10:14) — let the children come.

---

### M11-C-VCG-09 — Permission withheld / blocking / authority restraining

**Context:** G0863I-permit used in authoritative restraining / blocking / refusing postures. The negative inverse of VCG-08. Blocking kingdom access (Mat 23:13); refusing temple thoroughfare (Mar 11:16); withholding speech from demons (Mar 1:34); withholding companionship (Mar 5:19); blocking filial duty via tradition (Mar 7:12); priority-sequencing (Mar 7:27); ironic permission exposing hypocrisy (Luk 6:42, Mat 7:4); defiant refusal of burial (Rev 11:9); wilful abandonment of created order (Rom 1:27). Inner-being content varies — authoritative restraint, perception-failure, or moral hardening.

**Members:** vc=17629 (Mat 7:4), vc=17631 (Mar 5:19), vc=17632 (Mar 7:27), vc=17633 (Mar 11:16), vc=17634 (Luk 6:42), vc=17638 (Mat 23:13), vc=17639 (Mar 1:34), vc=17640 (Mar 7:12), vc=17642 (Rom 1:27), vc=17643 (Rev 11:9) — **10 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17638 (Mat 23:13) — refusing to let others enter the kingdom.

---

### M11-C-VCG-10 — Eschatological tear-wiping (restoration outcome)

**Context:** exaleifō used eschatologically — every tear, mourning, crying, and pain wiped away (Rev 7:17, Rev 21:4). The release-as-erasure register applied to grief rather than sin; the downstream restoration outcome of the completed atonement-forgiveness-repentance arc. M03/M04 cross-register flags per Phase 3.

**Members:** vc=2267 (Rev 21:4), vc=2269 (Rev 7:17) — **2 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=2267 (Rev 21:4) — every tear wiped, mourning ended.

## §3. Cross-VCG observations (Phase D + Phase E seeds)

1. **VCG-01 (release proclaimed) and M11-B-VCG-01 (NT direct forgiveness declarations)** are the lexical-Greek twin pair — `afesis`/`afiēmi`-forgive both proclaim sin-release; differentiated only by NT genre (`afesis` in proclamation contexts, `afiēmi`-forgive in pastoral encounter). Phase D T6.
2. **VCG-02 (release foreclosed)** parallels M11-B-VCG-04 (forgiveness foreclosed) and M11-A-VCG-08 (atonement denied) — the negative pole runs across all three M11-A/B/C registers. Phase D may synthesize the M11 limit-condition pattern.
3. **VCG-03 (discipleship-leaving) and VCG-06 (eschatological separation)** are the non-M11-primary registers of G0863G — collectively ~18 of the 32 G0863G verses. Observation seeded at B.2 for Phase D T6 cross-register articulation.
4. **VCG-04 (reconciliation-prioritizing) and VCG-05 (loyalty failure)** are the structural-pair within G0863G — the same lexical act of leaving operates either positively (leaving to reconcile, bond maintained) or negatively (loyalty collapsed, first love abandoned). The M11-D return verbs (`shuv`, `na.cham`) name the recovery from the VCG-05 state.
5. **VCG-10 (eschatological tear-wiping)** carries the M03/M04 cross-register flag from B.1 — the redemptive arc's downstream outcome. Phase D T6.
