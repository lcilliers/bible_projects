# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T3 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-D Repentance and turning (char_seq=4) — 33 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T3.1.1 Perception** **[CHAR-4]** E — Perception strongly engaged. Isa 44:19 the idol-maker's failure-of-reflection is failed-perception preventing turning. Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart" calls for active perceptive engagement. Isa 46:8 "recall to mind and stand firm." The turning-faculty requires perceptive recognition of misalignment.

**T3.1.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning **deepens** perception (recognizing what was done); **requires** perception as precondition (cannot turn without seeing the misalignment); **impaired** by closed perception (Isa 44:19 reflection-absent; Jer 8:6 self-examination-none).

**T3.1.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Pattern reveals turning as **perception-dependent** in a way M11-A/B/C are not. The cognitive-perceptive recognition is essential to the turning-act, not optional.

---

**T3.2.1 Cognition** **[CHAR-4]** E — Cognition centrally engaged. Recognition of sin (1Ki 8:47 "acknowledging sin"); memory of covenant (Deu 30:1); discernment of right path (Jer 26:13 "obedience restoring, conduct mended"). Cognition operates throughout the turning-process.

**T3.2.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning *enables* cognition through honest self-acknowledgment; *requires* cognition as precondition.

**T3.2.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D is more cognitively-anchored than M11-A/B/C — the recognition-element is necessary for genuine turning.

---

**T3.3.1 Memory** **[CHAR-4]** E — Memory is M11-D's most-engaged faculty. Deu 30:1 recall of covenant initiates turning; Deu 4:39 lay-it-to-heart; Lam 3:21 recall pivots grief to hope; Isa 46:8 memory summoned; Isa 44:19 idol-maker's memory-failure. Memory is constitutive — turning requires recall.

**T3.3.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning *requires* memory (no recall → no turning); *deepens* memory (the turning-act re-imprints covenant); *impaired* in negative-pole (Isa 44:19; Jer 8:6 absent self-reflection).

**T3.3.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Memory engagement reveals turning as memorial-act — the inner-being recalls covenant and re-orients toward it.

---

**T3.4.1 Affect** **[CHAR-4]** E — Affect strongly engaged. Job 42:6 self-abasement; Jer 31:19 shame and self-reproach; the na.cham contrition-vocabulary IS affective. Divine grief (Gen 6:6) is affectively-charged. Affect is constitutive of M11-D in a way it is not for M11-A or M11-B.

**T3.4.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning **deepens** appropriate affect (contrition); **requires** the affective-engagement (contrition is not optional). Negative pole's affect: hardness, lack of self-reflection-felt-wrongness.

**T3.4.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D is the cluster's most-affectively-engaged characteristic. Pattern: turning is felt-through, not just willed.

---

**T3.5.1 Creativity** **[CHAR-4]** S — Minimal direct engagement. Turning is largely received-call rather than creative-invention.

**T3.5.2** **[CHAR-4]** S — Indirect: post-turning may enable renewed creative-moral capacity.

**T3.5.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Not principally creativity-engaging.

---

**T3.6.1 Volition** **[CHAR-4]** E — Volition is **primary**. Every shuv-act is a volitional turn; every contrition-act involves willed self-abasement. Isa 46:8 "stand firm" is willed-stability. Mar 4:12 turning-required cross-evidence makes will-engagement essential.

**T3.6.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning *requires* volition (cannot turn passively); *develops* will-discipline (repeated turning forms turning-character).

**T3.6.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Volitionally-saturated. The volitional engagement is the most-defining faculty for M11-D after memory and affect.

---

**T3.7.1 Agency** **[CHAR-4]** E — Human agency in turning; divine agency in relenting; both real. Act 5:31 cross-evidence: "repentance is granted from above" — divine prompts; human turns.

**T3.7.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Develops human agency through formed turning-discipline; restores agency post-turning.

**T3.7.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Agency at both ends; the responsive divine-relenting is itself a divine-agential response to human-agential turning.

---

**T3.8.1 Moral Evaluation** **[CHAR-4]** E — Constitutively engaged. Turning presupposes moral evaluation (recognition of wrong); 2Ch 7:14 the humility-prayer-seeking-turning sequence requires moral-evaluative engagement; Jer 26:3 hearing-and-turning-from-evil involves discernment.

**T3.8.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Deepens moral evaluation through self-honest reflection.

**T3.8.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Moral evaluation is the cognitive ground from which turning operates.

---

**T3.9.1 Conscience** **[CHAR-4]** E — Conscience is the seat of recognition-of-sin in turning. 1Ki 8:47 "inner recognition... conscience stirred." Jer 8:6 the absence of conscience-stirring is the negative-pole. Conscience drives the turning-recognition.

**T3.9.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning *deepens* conscience through use; *requires* conscience as precondition; *impaired* by hardened-conscience (Jer 8:6).

**T3.9.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D engages conscience at the *recognition-and-prompting* level (the conscience moves the person to turn); M11-A engages conscience at the *clearing* level (post-atonement); M11-B at the *settling* level. The three conscience-engagements together produce the cluster's full conscience-restoration arc.

---

**T3.10.1 Conscientiousness** **[CHAR-4]** E — Conscientiousness in the post-turning life: integrated moral-awareness-volition-action (Jer 26:13 "obedience restoring, conduct mended"); the post-turning person walks integrated.

**T3.10.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Develops conscientiousness through repeated turning-and-correction.

**T3.10.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning is foundational to conscientiousness — the willingness to re-direct when wrong is the inner-attitudinal ground of conscientious living.

---

**T3.11.1 Relational Capacity** **[CHAR-4]** E — Relational capacity engaged: turning toward God re-establishes covenant relational capacity (1Ki 8:47 the exilic turning restores relational possibility); the post-turning capacity for proximity and intercession (Job 42:6 → Job 42:8 prayer-for-friends).

**T3.11.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Restores relational capacity through reconciliation-enabling; deepens through humility-formed relating.

**T3.11.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Pattern: turning is the inner-act that enables relational reset; the relational-capacity is restored through the turn.
