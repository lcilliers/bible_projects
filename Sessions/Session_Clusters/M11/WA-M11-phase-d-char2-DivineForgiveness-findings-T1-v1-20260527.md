# M11 Phase D — Char 2 (Divine forgiveness) — T1 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-B Divine forgiveness (char_seq=2)
**Tier:** T1 — Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27

---

**T1.1.1 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Named **Divine forgiveness** in the programme. The name signals (a) directionality (forgiveness *from God*), (b) the act of releasing a debt or guilt, (c) the relational outcome of standing-not-held-against. "Forgiveness" derives from "give" — the gift of release; theologically it is the divine giving away of what is owed against the offender. The name accurately captures both the gracious-character source (God gives) and the relational-effect (the offender is released).

---

**T1.1.2 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-2]** E — **H5545 sa.lach** (the OT divine-forgive verb, 45V): root meaning "to forgive/pardon"; used exclusively of God forgiving humans (never human-to-human in OT — though human-to-human release exists via other roots). **H5546 sal.lach** (adjective, 1V — Psa 86:5): "forgiving" as character attribute. **H5547 se.li.chah** (noun, 3V): "forgivenesses" as divine attribute. **G0863H afiēmi** (NT forgive sense, 37V): from apo + hiēmi, "to send away, let go"; used both of God-forgiving and human-forgiving. The Hebrew/Greek vocabulary jointly reveal: forgiveness is principally a divine act in the OT (only God forgives sins); the NT extends authorization to humans (Mat 6:12 the Lord's-Prayer reciprocal pattern; Joh 20:23 delegated authority).

---

**T1.1.3 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Strong directional implications: forgiveness moves *from* the offended party (God or wronged human) *toward* the offender. Strong relational implications: forgiveness restores the relationship damaged by the offense. Constitutional implication: forgiveness operates at the *standing-state* level (covenant standing restored) rather than at the inner-mechanism level (which is M11-A's territory). The directionality of M11-B is the *inverse* of M11-A: M11-A goes offender-toward-offended (mediated cover); M11-B goes offended-toward-offender (declared release). This inversion is structurally significant for cluster architecture.

---

**T1.2.1 — Kind**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness is principally a **disposition with corresponding act**. (i) **Disposition:** Psa 86:5 "forgiving" as stable inner character; Neh 9:17 "ready to forgive"; Dan 9:9 "forgiveness belongs to God's nature" — these are character-state statements. (ii) **Act:** Lev 4:20 "and they shall be forgiven"; 1Ki 8:30 "forgive when you hear" — the disposition expresses itself in discrete acts of release. (iii) **Condition outcome:** the recipient enters the new state of forgiven-standing (Rom 4:7 "blessed are those whose lawless deeds are forgiven"). Quality and act co-exist; M11-B is more disposition-anchored than M11-A (which is principally act).

---

**T1.2.2 — Kind**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Composite structure with three constituent elements: (i) **the offense / debt** being forgiven (sin, iniquity, trespass); (ii) **the offended party's release-act** (divine pronouncement; human heart-release); (iii) **the offender's restored standing** (forgiven, debt-cancelled, lawless deeds covered, sin remembered no more). A fourth element appears in human reciprocal forgiveness: (iv) **the offender's prior or anticipated repentance** (Luk 17:3–4 condition). Each operative forgiveness has all elements; the M11-B-VCG-04 negative pole shows the structure broken (offense present, release withheld or impossible).

---

**T1.2.3 — Kind**
**[CHAR-2]** E — **Working description:** The divine (and image-bearing human) act and disposition of releasing an offender from a moral debt or standing-against, restoring them to a position of being-not-held-against by the offended party. Constitutional location: the offended party's inner disposition + the offender's restored standing. Faculties engaged at the offended-party-end: will (to release), character (the inner disposition that makes release native), and at the offender-end: conscience (cleared) + standing-relational (restored). Impact: removes the debt from the standing-record; reconfigures the relationship around the released-state.

---

**T1.3.1 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The structural opposite of divine forgiveness is **withheld pardon / unforgivability** — VCG-04 names this directly: Mat 12:31–32 (blasphemy against Spirit unforgivable); 2Ki 24:4 (innocent blood unpardonable); Deu 29:20 (wilful self-deception); Jer 5:1/7 (radical scarcity of justice / ingratitude compounding); Lam 3:42 (transgression confessed yet forgiveness withheld). The structural opposite is the *closed condition of M11-B* — the inner-state where divine forgiveness is structurally inoperable. This parallels M11-A's negative pole (atonement denied) and M11-C's (release foreclosed) — the negative pole runs internally across the cluster's mechanism/declaration/release sub-groups.

---

**T1.3.2 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness explicitly excludes / resists: (i) **sustained, hardened rejection of the divine Spirit** (Mat 12:31–32, Luk 12:10) — the blasphemy-against-the-Spirit limit names an inner-being threshold beyond which the faculty of repentance has been foreclosed and so forgiveness is structurally impossible; (ii) **wilful self-deception under known covenant** (Deu 29:20); (iii) **persistent, repeated betrayal** (Jer 5:7 ingratitude compounding); (iv) **the absence of any genuine inward turning** (Mar 4:12 turning refused); (v) **refusing to forgive others** (Mat 6:15 explicit; Mat 18:35 the unforgiving servant). The exclusion list runs along inner-state lines — the offender's hardened inner posture, not the offense's severity per se.

---

**T1.3.3 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness (M11-B) ends and other characteristics begin at several boundaries. **Vs M11-A Atonement:** forgiveness is the *declared release*; atonement is the *operative cover-mechanism*. **Vs M11-C Release:** forgiveness is *specifically of sin/guilt-against-God*; release is *broader (debts, obligations, bondage)*. **Vs M11-D Repentance:** repentance is the *offender's inner turn* that opens forgiveness; forgiveness is the *offended-party's-release* that responds to or initiates. **Vs M11-E Reconciliation:** forgiveness is the *individual standing-release*; reconciliation is the *relational restoration outcome*. **Vs M05 Compassion:** compassion is the *underlying inner-character disposition*; forgiveness is the *specific act-and-state of guilt-release* that compassion can produce. Each boundary is real and definitional.

---

**T1.4.1 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness operates in at least six distinct modes evidenced across the corpus. (i) **Direct pronouncement** — Luk 7:48 "your sins are forgiven"; Mat 9:2 explicit declaration. (ii) **Petitionary-response** — 1Ki 8:30 forgiveness in response to prayer-toward-the-temple. (iii) **Intercession-mediated** — Exo 34:9 Moses; Num 14:19–20 Moses; Amo 7:2 prophetic intercession. (iv) **Reciprocal-conditional** — Mat 6:12, 14–15 the Lord's-Prayer reciprocal pattern. (v) **Repentance-triggered** — 2Ch 7:14 humility-prayer-seeking-turning; Isa 55:7 abundant pardon for the turning. (vi) **Character-attribute** — Psa 86:5 forgiveness as God's stable disposition. Each mode has its own conditions and theological emphasis; together they form the corpus's pattern.

---

**T1.4.2 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes — divine forgiveness operates differently by context. (i) **By covenantal-locus:** under the Sinai covenant (Lev 4–7 atonement-mediated), under the temple-prayer pattern (1Ki 8 / 2Ch 6), under the new covenant (Jer 31:34 complete and unremembered). (ii) **By relational-direction:** God-to-human (the dominant direction); human-to-human (Mat 6:12, 18:21–35); both (the reciprocal binding). (iii) **By inner-state:** forgiveness in response to confession (1Jo 1:9 the Christian-life pattern), forgiveness in response to turning (Isa 55:7), forgiveness in response to ignorance (Luk 23:34 Christ for executioners), forgiveness as preemptive (1Jo 2:12 already-forgiven assurance). (iv) **By eschatological-stage:** present-recurring vs new-covenant-total (Jer 31:34) vs eschatological-cosmic. The operation calibrates to context while holding the core release-of-debt logic.

---

**T1.4.3 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness has a strong **speech-mode component**, unlike atonement (which is principally act-based). The forgiveness-pronouncement is itself a speech-act: Luk 7:48 "your sins are forgiven" is the operative-words; Mat 9:2 "take heart, your sins are forgiven"; Mar 2:5 "son, your sins are forgiven." These are not merely communicating an already-completed act; the declaration *constitutes* the release in some sense (per the Mar 2:9–10 sequence where the visible-healing demonstrates the invisible-spoken-forgiveness was real). At the prayer-side, the petitioner's verbal asking is integral (1Ki 8:30 "when you hear the prayer"). The speech-mode operates throughout — divine forgiveness is communicated and constituted through speech to a greater degree than M11-A's body-mediated atonement.

---

**T1.5.1 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The immediate inner-being response to receiving divine forgiveness varies more in M11-B than in M11-A — the corpus depicts both **relief / gratitude** and the **silenced-humility** pattern, depending on context. (i) **Relief / gratitude:** Luk 7:47 "great love is the evidence of great forgiveness received... gratitude overflowing; heart transformed"; the forgiven-much woman responds in tearful gratitude. Rom 4:7 names blessedness as the post-forgiveness state. (ii) **Silenced humility:** Eze 16:63 (M11-A side; also applicable to M11-B receiving): the cover-despite-unfaithfulness produces mouth-closed. (iii) **Restored confidence:** 1Jo 2:12 "the assurance that sins are already forgiven... grounds the inner identity and standing of the believer." (iv) **Take-heart courage:** Mat 9:2 Jesus says "take heart" alongside the forgiveness — courage is enabled by the released state. The pattern: M11-B's immediate response carries more positive-affect than M11-A's; the recipient knows themselves released, and the response includes gratitude / restored standing / take-heart courage as well as humility.

---

**T1.5.2 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The immediate response varies. Pure relief / gratitude (Luk 7:47); silenced humility (Eze 16:63); restored identity (1Jo 2:12); take-heart (Mat 9:2). The variance reflects the *register* of the receiving — receiving forgiveness in the recognition of one's deep failure produces humility; receiving as ongoing-grace produces grateful confidence; receiving in a specific moment of need produces relief-and-take-heart. The corpus does not enforce one affect-pattern; it acknowledges the multi-modal felt-experience of being forgiven.

---

**T1.5.3 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The corpus is partially silent on the felt-experience of forgiveness-receiving in many institutional Levitical contexts — Lev 4–7 "and he shall be forgiven" records the standing-shift without naming the inner-felt response. The silence is consistent with institutional-ritual treating the standing-result as primary; the personal-felt response is left to the offerer's own experiential trajectory. Where the corpus *does* depict felt-response (Luk 7:47; 1Jo 2:12; Mat 9:2), the depictions are varied; no canonical-single felt-state is enforced.

---

**T1.6.1 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Sustained divine forgiveness produces in the inner-being a **stable forgiven-identity + ongoing confessional-and-forgiving disposition**. (i) **Forgiven-identity:** 1Jo 2:12 the assurance grounds inner identity; Rom 4:7 blessedness is the sustained state. The recipient lives knowing themselves released. (ii) **Ongoing confessional life:** 1Jo 1:9 names the sustained discipline — ongoing confession unlocks ongoing forgiveness; the cleansed walk requires continuing engagement. (iii) **Ongoing forgiving disposition toward others:** Mat 18:21–35 the parable makes forgiveness-extending the test of forgiveness-received; the sustained state should produce reciprocal practice. (iv) **Persistent gratitude / love:** Luk 7:47 the long-term-felt response of one forgiven much is love-as-gratitude. (v) **Reverent awe:** Psa 130:4 "the existence of forgiveness with God is itself the basis for reverent fear — pardon does not diminish awe but grounds it" — the sustained inner posture is awe + confidence.

---

**T1.6.2 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Sustained exposure establishes: (i) **Forgiven-identity** as ongoing self-knowledge; (ii) **Capacity to extend forgiveness** (forgiving as forgiven); (iii) **Confessional honesty** as ongoing posture (1Jo 1:9); (iv) **Love-and-gratitude** as character outflow (Luk 7:47); (v) **Reverent awe with confidence** (Psa 130:4 — both fear-of-God AND assurance-of-pardon); (vi) **Free petitionary access** (Psa 25:11 the freedom to plead under remaining guilt — "appeal for pardon not on personal merit but on God's own name, while acknowledging the weight of great personal guilt").

---

**T1.6.3 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes — the sustained effect differs from the immediate response. **Immediate** can include relief / take-heart / gratitude / silenced humility; **sustained** is more settled into identity-and-disposition: forgiven-identity (1Jo 2:12), ongoing confessional posture (1Jo 1:9), love-as-gratitude (Luk 7:47), reverent awe with confidence (Psa 130:4). The transition is from felt-event to constituted-character. The Mat 18:21–35 parable assumes this transition — the master expects the servant's forgiven-state to have become a forgiving-disposition over time; the servant's failure to internalize is what the parable condemns.

---

**T1.7.1 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Genuine reception enabled by: (i) **Confession** (1Jo 1:9 "the will's honest acknowledgment of sin triggers the divine faithfulness that releases guilt"); (ii) **Faith** (Mat 9:2 "Forgiveness is pronounced directly into the inner person of the paralytic in response to faith"; Act 10:43 "Belief is the inner act through which forgiveness of sins is received"); (iii) **Repentance / inner turning** (Mar 1:4 "repentance and forgiveness... preparation and outcome"; Mar 4:12 "turning required"; Isa 55:7 "abundant pardon... contingent upon genuine turning"); (iv) **Humble petition** (Psa 25:11 "appeal for pardon not on personal merit but on God's own name"; 2Ch 7:14 "humility, prayer, seeking, and turning"); (v) **Willing-to-forgive-others disposition** (Mat 6:12, 14–15 reciprocity); (vi) **Heart-engagement** (Mat 18:35 "Forgiveness must arise from the heart — not merely as formal release but as a genuine inner disposition"). The clustered enabling-conditions are confession + faith + repentance + humility + reciprocity + heart-engagement.

---

**T1.7.2 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Reception blocked by: (i) **Sustained blasphemous rejection** (Mat 12:31–32 blasphemy against Spirit — repentance foreclosed); (ii) **Wilful self-deception under covenant** (Deu 29:20); (iii) **Persistent ingratitude after benefit** (Jer 5:7 betrayal repeated); (iv) **Unforgivingness toward others** (Mat 6:15 "Refusing to forgive others' trespasses blocks one's own forgiveness — a closed heart toward others produces a closed divine response"); (v) **Absence of inner turning** (Mar 4:12 perception closed, will hardened); (vi) **Mere formal-release without heart-engagement** (Mat 18:35 the parable's central failure — surface forgiveness disqualifies). The negative-pole VCG-04 names these as inner-states that foreclose the faculty.

---

**T1.7.3 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The non-receiving state is **sealed-against-pardon hardness**, with specific shapes by sub-pattern. Sustained Spirit-rejection (Mat 12:31–32) produces structural unforgivability. Unforgiving disposition toward others (Mat 6:15; Mat 18:35) produces self-blocked divine-response. Wilful self-deception (Deu 29:20) produces excluded pardon. Compounded ingratitude (Jer 5:7) produces rhetorically-impossible pardon. The shared inner-state across cases: the offender's inner posture has fractured the conditions of receiving — either by rejecting the Source, refusing the inner-imitation, deceiving the self, or compounding the offense. The fracture is *inside the offender*, not in the divine-disposition (which remains "ready to forgive," Psa 86:5; Neh 9:17).

---

**T1.8.1 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Primary dimension: **relational-character** (forgiveness is a relational-disposition that operates as character-act). Secondary: moral-standing (operates on the offender's standing-state); volitional (the giving act); covenantal (operates within and constitutes covenant); cognitive-memorial (Jer 31:34 "remember sin no more" — operates on memory); affective-grateful (Luk 7:47 produces love-as-response).

---

**T1.8.2 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Relational-character is supported by: (i) Psa 86:5 / Neh 9:17 / Dan 9:9 forgiveness-as-divine-character; (ii) the reciprocal-binding (Mat 6:14–15) treats human-forgiving as character-extension; (iii) Mat 18:35 "heart forgiving" makes the heart-character the operative ground; (iv) Pro 16:6 (M11-A cross-evidence) the inner-character of hesed+emet operates in the forgiving mode. The pattern: forgiveness is an outflow of *who one is* relationally-toward-the-offender, not merely *what one does*.

---

**T1.8.3 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Secondary dimensions complement rather than compete. Moral-standing (Lev 4:20 declared forgiven) operates downstream of the character-disposition. Volitional (the act of release) is the disposition expressing itself. Covenantal (Sinai cover; new-covenant Jer 31:34) is the relational frame within which forgiveness operates. Cognitive-memorial (Jer 31:34 / Jer 50:20 unfindable sin) is the long-arc effect on divine memory. Affective-grateful (Luk 7:47) is the relational response. None contests the primary character-classification.
