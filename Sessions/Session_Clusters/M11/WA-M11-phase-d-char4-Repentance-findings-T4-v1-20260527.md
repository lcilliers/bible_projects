# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T4 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-D Repentance and turning (char_seq=4) — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T4.1.1 Divine Interface — God to Human** **[CHAR-4]** E — God-to-human strongly in divine-relenting register: Exo 32:14 relenting from disaster; Jon 3:10 relenting from announced judgment; Jer 18:8 conditional-relenting pattern; Joe 2:13 character-grounded relenting; 1Ch 21:15 / 2Sa 24:16 mid-execution relenting. The God-to-human direction in M11-D is **God's responsive movement** rather than initiating extension.

**T4.1.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Basis: responsive-conditional (Jer 18:8 conditional both ways); character-grounded (Joe 2:13 hesed); intercession-responsive (Exo 32:14; Amo 7:3/6); covenant-memorial (Psa 106:45). The relenting basis is multi-conditioned.

**T4.1.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Reveals **responsive compassion within sovereign character** — God genuinely responds to human turning yet remains constant in character; the responsiveness is the expression of, not the alternative to, the constancy.

**T4.1.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Narrower silence: the corpus does not depict God *initiating* turning in humans through direct intervention without their participation; the human-side turning is consistently a real human-act.

---

**T4.2.1 Divine Interface — Human to God** **[CHAR-4]** E — Human-to-God is the **dominant direction** in M11-D. shuv-turning is principally human-toward-God: 1Ki 8:47–48 the exile-prayer-turning; 2Ch 6:37–38 same; Deu 30:1–2 the future-restoration-via-turning; Isa 46:8 command to transgressors to turn. Human contrition (Job 42:6; Jer 31:19) is human-toward-God movement.

**T4.2.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Required posture: humble (Job 42:6); honest (1Ki 8:47 acknowledging sin); whole-person (na.cham contrition); persistent (Luk 17:3–4 cross-evidence about repeated turning).

**T4.2.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Direction reveals the relationship as **responsibly-engaged** — humans bear genuine responsibility for re-orienting toward God, and God responds to their turning.

**T4.2.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient evidence.

---

**T4.3.1 Human Interface — Giving** **[CHAR-4]** E — Human-to-human extension is less direct in M11-D than in M11-B. The relevant verses: prophetic-call-to-turning (Isa 46:8 to transgressors; Jer 26:3 / 26:13 / 26:19 calling for national turning) is a kind of giving — humans prompting other humans to turn. Job 42:6's contrition includes Job's subsequent intercession for friends (Job 42:8 adjacent context) — contrition + extension is paired.

**T4.3.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Inner conditions: the prophet must have themselves received understanding of God's character; the intercessor must have moral standing (Job 42 after his turning); the calling-to-turn requires authenticity.

**T4.3.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Receiving precedes giving — having turned oneself, one can credibly call others to turn. Same pattern as M11-A/B/C.

**T4.3.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Some silence — direct interpersonal-giving of repentance is less developed lexically.

---

**T4.4.1 Human Interface — Receiving** **[CHAR-4]** E — Receiving here is **receiving the call-to-turn** rather than receiving forgiveness or release. The Nineveh-narrative (Jon 3:10 cross-evidence — they received the call and turned). The prophetic-call corpus implies receiving-and-turning or receiving-and-refusing patterns.

**T4.4.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Enabled by hearing-and-acknowledgment; blocked by hardness (Jer 5:1 scarcity of justice cross-evidence; Jer 8:6 self-examination-absent).

**T4.4.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Non-receiving state: persisting-without-turning (Jer 8:6).

**T4.4.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Partial evidence.

---

**T4.5.1 Human Interface — Boundaries** **[CHAR-4]** E — Boundaries: the turning-call extends to covenant insiders (Israel's exile-prayers); to outsiders (Jon 3:10 Nineveh turning); to all who hear (the prophetic-universalizing intention).

**T4.5.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Crosses covenantal boundaries — the Jon 3:10 paradigm demonstrates that even non-covenant peoples can turn and receive divine relenting.

**T4.5.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Included: all who can hear and turn; excluded: those whose hardness prevents turning (Jer 8:6).

**T4.5.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.

---

**T4.6.1 Spiritual Beings Interface** **[CHAR-4]** S — Largely silent. The turning-faculty is depicted as human-divine without principal spiritual-being involvement in the M11-D verse set.

**T4.6.2** **[CHAR-4]** S — Adversarial-influence is implied at the resistance-to-turning pole (cf. M11-B Mat 12:31–32 Spirit-rejection) but not directly named in M11-D.

**T4.6.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Angelic ministry of turning is not directly evidenced.

**T4.6.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Spiritual-beings interface is largely silent in M11-D, consistent with the cluster pattern.
