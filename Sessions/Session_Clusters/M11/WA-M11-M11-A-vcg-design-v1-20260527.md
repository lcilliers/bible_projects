## §2. VCG definitions

### M11-A-VCG-01 — Day of Atonement core ritual

**Context description:** The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) corpus — the central comprehensive annual atoning event in which the high priest enters the holiest space, atones first for himself and his household, then for the sanctuary itself defiled by accumulated transgressions, and finally for all the people in concentric circles outward. The inner-being content is the entire community's collective guilt brought into one mediated covering act, with all human striving ceased so divine covering operates. Also includes Lev 6:30 (the most-solemn sin-offering whose blood enters the sanctuary — DoA-class atonement).

**Members:** vc=29526 (Lev 16:6), vc=29527 (Lev 16:10), vc=29528 (Lev 16:11), vc=29529 (Lev 16:16), vc=29530 (Lev 16:17), vc=29531 (Lev 16:24), vc=29532 (Lev 16:30), vc=29533 (Lev 16:32), vc=29534 (Lev 16:33), vc=29537 (Lev 23:28), vc=29511 (Lev 6:30) — **11 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29532 (Lev 16:30) — canonical DoA summary: "atonement on this day to cleanse you; from all your sins you will be clean before the Lord."

---

### M11-A-VCG-02 — Standard Levitical sin/guilt offering → forgiveness arc

**Context description:** The standard Levitical sacrificial mechanism by which sin and guilt are addressed through prescribed offering and priestly mediation, resulting explicitly in forgiveness (kip.per → sa.lach). Includes the foundational hand-laying identification (Lev 1:4), blood-as-life atonement theology (Lev 17:11), the iniquity-bearing role of the sin-offering itself (Lev 10:17), and the systematic forgiveness-following pattern across Lev 4–7, 19, Num 5, 15. Graduated provisions for poverty are housed here (the access principle is structural). The unintentional-sin sub-pattern (Lev 5:18, Num 15:25/28, Num 5:8) is included — it expresses the same mechanism applied to inadvertent culpability.

**Members:** vc=29501 (Lev 1:4), vc=29493 (Lev 4:20), vc=29502 (Lev 4:26), vc=29503 (Lev 4:31), vc=29504 (Lev 4:35), vc=29505 (Lev 5:6), vc=29506 (Lev 5:10), vc=29507 (Lev 5:13), vc=29508 (Lev 5:16), vc=29509 (Lev 5:18), vc=29510 (Lev 6:7), vc=29512 (Lev 7:7), vc=29513 (Lev 8:34), vc=29515 (Lev 10:17), vc=29492 (Lev 17:11), vc=29536 (Lev 19:22), vc=29538 (Num 5:8), vc=29543 (Num 15:25), vc=29544 (Num 15:28) — **19 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29493 (Lev 4:20) — canonical sin offering → forgiveness pattern.

### M11-A-VCG-03 — Cleansing-atonement (ritual defilement removed)

**Context description:** Atonement specifically deployed against ritual uncleanness — childbirth, skin disease, bodily discharges, corpse contact. The inner-being content is the cleansed standing restored when the defilement-precondition is addressed through atoning sacrifice. Distinct from VCG-02 because the precondition here is *defilement* (an unclean state) rather than *transgression* (a specific sin). M10c relational: these verses also evidence the M10c-M11 atonement-defilement nexus per the Phase 3 cross-register notes.

**Members:** vc=29516 (Lev 12:7), vc=29517 (Lev 12:8), vc=29518 (Lev 14:18), vc=29519 (Lev 14:19), vc=29520 (Lev 14:20), vc=29521 (Lev 14:21), vc=29522 (Lev 14:29), vc=29523 (Lev 14:31), vc=29524 (Lev 15:15), vc=29525 (Lev 15:30), vc=29539 (Num 6:11) — **11 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29520 (Lev 14:20) — "burnt offering and grain offering together complete the priestly atonement, and the result is that the formerly defiled person is declared clean."

### M11-A-VCG-04 — Priestly preparation / self-atonement / ordination

**Context description:** The mediator's own preparation for atoning work — the priest must first be cleansed before he can cleanse others. Includes the Levitical ordination atonement, the half-shekel ransom as priestly-life provision, and the Levite-as-protective-barrier role. The inner-being pattern: mediation requires the mediator's own prior covering.

**Members:** vc=29498 (Exo 29:33), vc=29499 (Exo 30:15), vc=29500 (Exo 30:16), vc=29514 (Lev 9:7), vc=29540 (Num 8:12), vc=29541 (Num 8:19), vc=29542 (Num 8:21) — **7 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29514 (Lev 9:7) — "the high priest is commanded to offer atonement first for himself and then for the people."

### M11-A-VCG-05 — Crisis / intercessory / wrath-averting atonement

**Context description:** Atonement deployed in moments of active divine wrath or imminent judgment — Moses's intercession after the golden calf, Aaron's incense interposing during plague, Phinehas's zeal-act averting wrath, the warriors' voluntary offering after battle. The inner-being content is urgent mediation under crisis, the covering act standing between accumulated guilt and its deadly consequence.

**Members:** vc=29571 (Exo 32:30), vc=29545 (Num 16:46), vc=29546 (Num 16:47), vc=29547 (Num 25:13), vc=29572 (Num 31:50) — **5 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29545 (Num 16:46) — "atonement via incense is urgently deployed to halt divine wrath already unleashed in plague."

### M11-A-VCG-06 — National / institutional / annual atonement

**Context description:** Atonement as ongoing institutional and national provision — Chronicles' corporate reformations, Hezekiah's restoration sacrifice, the second Passover, the Deuteronomic land-cleansing, the Ezekielian temple economics, Nehemiah's covenant offerings, and the festival-cycle sin offerings (Num 28–29) that reiterate the recurring need. Includes Lev 16:34 (annual statute) as the institutional framing of the DoA pattern.

**Members:** vc=29551 (1Ch 6:49), vc=29552 (2Ch 29:24), vc=29570 (2Ch 30:18), vc=29497 (Deu 21:8), vc=29564 (Deu 32:43), vc=29535 (Lev 16:34), vc=29553 (Neh 10:33), vc=29554 (Eze 45:15), vc=29555 (Eze 45:17), vc=29548 (Num 28:22), vc=29549 (Num 28:30), vc=29550 (Num 29:5) — **12 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29552 (2Ch 29:24) — "a ritual sin offering is made on behalf of the entire people, showing atonement as a corporate act."

### M11-A-VCG-07 — Divine self-atonement (God as agent)

**Context description:** Atonement where God himself is the active agent rather than the recipient of priestly mediation — Psalms' divine covering, Isaiah's seraph touch, eschatological cleansing of land/nation, the Eze 16:63 shame-producing reception of atonement. The inner-being pattern: when human resources for atonement are exhausted, God acts directly.

**Members:** vc=29495 (Eze 16:63), vc=29557 (Isa 27:9), vc=29556 (Isa 6:7), vc=29565 (Dan 9:24), vc=29562 (Psa 65:3), vc=29494 (Psa 78:38), vc=29563 (Psa 79:9) — **7 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29562 (Psa 65:3) — "when overwhelming iniquities exceed the person's capacity to manage them, God himself acts to atone."

### M11-A-VCG-08 — Atonement denied / impossible / false-covering

**Context description:** The negative pole of M11-A — atonement explicitly refused (1Sa 3:14, Isa 22:14, Jer 18:23), unable to be applied (Isa 47:11), or exposed as a self-deception (Isa 28:18 false-covenant covering annulled). The inner-being content names the limit of atonement: where guilt is sealed beyond covering, the inner resources to deal with it are exhausted, and the false sense of being covered is exposed as void.

**Members:** vc=29559 (1Sa 3:14), vc=29560 (Isa 22:14), vc=29569 (Isa 28:18), vc=29561 (Isa 47:11), vc=29568 (Jer 18:23) — **5 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29560 (Isa 22:14) — "guilt is declared unforgivable until death — atonement is explicitly denied."

### M11-A-VCG-09 — Inner moral disposition / interpersonal appeasement

**Context description:** Atonement that operates outside or beneath the priestly ritual frame — the Pro 16:6 inner-character mechanism (steadfast love and faithfulness atone), Pro 16:14's wisdom in defusing royal wrath, Jacob's calculated gift to soften Esau, David's reparations for Saul's bloodguilt. The inner-being content is the relational/character-based covering — atonement effected by motive, posture, or material acknowledgement rather than priestly sacrifice. M11-A connection sometimes via metaphorical extension; M07/M05/M44 cross-register flags carry per Phase 3 verdict.

**Members:** vc=29567 (2Sa 21:3), vc=29496 (Gen 32:20), vc=29566 (Pro 16:14), vc=29558 (Pro 16:6) — **4 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=29558 (Pro 16:6) — "iniquity is atoned for through the inner dispositions of steadfast love and faithfulness, not merely ritual."

---

### M11-A-VCG-10 — Christic atoning surrender (NT self-substitution)

**Context description:** The NT atoning death — Jesus voluntarily releasing his spirit at the cross, and granting permission for his disciples to go free in an act of protective self-substitution. The inner-being content is willed surrender as the central atoning act. These two G0863I afiēmi-permit verses sit in M11-A by content (atonement-register) despite the term's majority-register being M29 permission/volition; the per-verse split was settled at B.2 §3.3.

**Members:** vc=17641 (Mat 27:50), vc=17636 (Joh 18:8) — **2 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17641 (Mat 27:50) — "Jesus voluntarily released his spirit at death; the inner act is a willed surrender of life, a final yielding of self."

## §3. Cross-VCG observations (Phase D + Phase E seeds)

1. **The kip.per → sa.lach internal arc** (VCG-02 within M11-A → M11-B forgiveness as outcome) is the canonical OT atonement-to-forgiveness mechanism. Phase D T6 (Structural Relationships) will articulate this M11-A ↔ M11-B intra-cluster pair.
2. **DoA (VCG-01) vs ordinary sin offering (VCG-02)** — both are kip.per but operate at different scope (annual comprehensive vs individual / occasional). Phase D may articulate the scaling pattern.
3. **VCG-03 cleansing-atonement** carries the M10c-M11 atonement-defilement nexus flagged at Phase 3 and noted in M11 sub-group design §2.3 (M11-A core_description).
4. **VCG-07 divine-self-atonement and VCG-10 Christic surrender** form the canonical OT-to-NT arc of atonement initiative: God in the OT acts when human resources are exhausted; the NT names the substitutionary act as the resolution.
