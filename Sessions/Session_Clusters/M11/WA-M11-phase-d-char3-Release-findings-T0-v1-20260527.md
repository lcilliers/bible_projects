# M11 Phase D — Char 3 (Release) — T0 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-C Release (broader sense) (char_seq=3)
**Tier:** T0 — Divine Image (12 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27
**Sub-group & VCG context:** [`WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md`](WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md) §M11-C (68 verses across 10 VCGs)

**Characteristic framing:** Release in M11-C is the broader-than-divine-forgiveness register: release of sin, debt, obligation, attachment, possession, bondage, grief. The sub-group routes both M11-primary verses (NT divine release-of-sin proclamation; eschatological tear-wiping) and cross-register verses (volitional detachment for discipleship; permission-granting and withholding; eschatological separation). T0 findings focus on the M11-primary content while acknowledging the broader register.

---

**T0.1.1 — Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release reflects God's character as **the One who lets-go / gives-away / cancels-debt / wipes-clean**. Mat 26:28 names the divine release-of-sins as enacted through the new-covenant blood; Heb 9:22 explains the divine economy ("without blood there is no forgiveness"); Col 1:14 defines redemption itself as "the forgiveness of sins" (afesis). At the eschatological-restoration register, Rev 7:17 and Rev 21:4 (exaleifō) reveal God as one who wipes every tear — the character of comprehensive release extending beyond sin to all grief and pain. The character revealed: God releases what humans cannot release themselves from (sin, accumulated grief, the burden of records held against). The release-act expresses divine generosity and finality — once released, the held-against-record is gone.

---

**T0.1.2 — Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release is attributed to God explicitly. Act 2:38 (gospel formula: "for forgiveness of your sins"); Act 13:38 (forgiveness proclaimed); Act 26:18 (forgiveness as outcome of turning); Col 1:14 (release-as-redemption); Eph 1:7 ("riches of grace" enabling release-of-trespasses); Heb 9:22 / 10:18 (divine release through covenant blood). Mar 3:29 the negative-pole limit ("no forgiveness ever") names divine authority over release-extension and -withholding. **Significance for the human person:** the attribution establishes (a) release is a real divine act, not merely an inner-psychological release the person achieves; (b) the recipient receives an objective standing-shift, not just a felt-release; (c) the human extension of release (via afiēmi-let-go practices, M11-C-VCG-04 reconciliation-prioritizing) participates in this divine character.

---

**T0.1.3 — Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-3]** S — The M11-C corpus is not silent on divine release-of-sin (VCG-01 + VCG-10 are densely God-attributed). The narrower silence: at the *broader-release* registers (VCG-03 discipleship-leaving; VCG-06 eschatological separation; VCG-08/09 permission-granted/withheld), the corpus operates principally at the human-actor or Christ-as-incarnate-actor level — the verses do not centrally attribute these release-acts to God's character per se. This is consistent with the sub-group's cross-register nature: the M11-primary content is divine-attributed; the broader-release content is principally human-or-Christ-acting.

---

**T0.2.1 — Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release equips the person to be **let-go / cancelled / liberated** in multiple registers. (i) Released from sin's record (Act 3:19 blotted-out; Col 2:14 legal-debt nailed to cross). (ii) Released from bondage (Luk 4:18 "liberation proclaimed to captives... covers both external bondage and the inner state of those burdened by constraint"). (iii) Released from competing attachments for higher loyalty (M11-C-VCG-03 discipleship-leaving — equips the person for whole-hearted following). (iv) Released from grief at eschatological restoration (Rev 21:4 — "every tear, mourning, crying, and pain wiped away"). The integrated purpose: release equips the person to live unencumbered — by sin, debt, attachment, bondage, or sorrow — as appropriate to context.

---

**T0.2.2 — Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release is **principally response-to-fallen-condition** for its M11-primary content (sin-release; tear-wiping) — both presuppose the fall. For its broader content (discipleship-leaving; permission-granting), release operates more as **original-design exercise of moral agency** — humans have the created capacity to let-go-of (possessions, attachments) and to grant-or-withhold (permission). The integrated read: release-as-faculty is original; release-as-specific-deployment-against-fall-effects is response-to-fallen-condition. M11-C's sub-group breadth surfaces both dimensions.

---

**T0.2.3 — Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Eschatological orientation is strongest at the tear-wiping register (Rev 7:17, Rev 21:4 — VCG-10): "the total erasure of all inner suffering and grief is the final condition of the redeemed in the new creation." This is M11-C's most explicit future-fullness anchor. The divine-release-of-sin register (VCG-01) also has eschatological orientation in its NT-comprehensive Christic-blood-release (Mat 26:28; Heb 10:18 the once-for-all sacrifice). The discipleship-leaving register (VCG-03) is eschatologically oriented through its reward-promise (Mat 19:29 "reward eschatological"). Multiple eschatological strands converge in M11-C.

---

**T0.3.1 — Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Image-bearer expression operates differently across the sub-group's registers. (i) **Released-of-sin recipient** instantiates the divine-image as a forgiven moral agent (similar to M11-B T0.3.1). (ii) **Discipleship-leaver** instantiates the image through whole-hearted loyalty release (Mat 4:20–22; Mar 1:18–20; the disciples leaving nets/family for following) — the image-aspect of being-able-to-prioritize-supremely. (iii) **Reconciliation-prioritizer** (Mat 5:24; 1Cor 7:11–13) instantiates the relational-prioritizing image. (iv) **Welcoming-permitter** (Mar 10:14 "let the children come") instantiates the divine-welcome character — the kingdom-disposition of openness. (v) **Tear-wiper** is the eschatological-completion image — God's character of consoling restoration imaged by the redeemed-community's restored joy.

---

**T0.3.2 — Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Shared with structural-asymmetry. The divine release-of-sin (VCG-01) is exclusive to God in source but participated by humans via Joh 20:23-style delegation. The discipleship-leaving (VCG-03) is principally a creaturely act (humans leaving for Christ); the image-aspect is the *capacity to leave* which mirrors God's eternal not-clinging-to-status (Phil 2 christological pattern, adjacent). The permission-granting/withholding (VCG-08/09) is shared — humans exercise this as image-bearers; God exercises it as primary-source. Tear-wiping (VCG-10) is exclusively divine in agency (Rev 21:4 — *God* will wipe). The pattern: image-sharing varies by register, with sin-release and tear-wiping as principally-divine (with delegated extension) and detachment / permission as principally-creaturely (with divine pattern as model).

---

**T0.3.3 — Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Presence: the person who lives in the release-mode (released from sin, free to leave attachments for higher loyalty, welcoming toward others, capable of being-released-from-grief) shows an active divine image. Absence: the person who refuses release (clings to sin — Mar 3:29 the foreclosed condition; clings to attachments against higher call — Mat 19:22 the rich young ruler's adjacent register; refuses to permit/welcome — Mat 23:13 blocks-others-from-kingdom; cannot bear release-from-grief because never opened to comfort) shows a divine image disabled at the release-faculty. Mat 23:13 (M11-C-VCG-09) is particularly diagnostic — refusing to allow others into the kingdom is an inverted image-bearing.

---

**T0.4.1 — Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Typological use is dense. (i) **Christological:** Mat 26:28 covenant-blood release typologically completes Levitical-sacrificial release; Heb 9:22 explicitly types the Levitical blood-pattern toward Christ. (ii) **Eschatological:** Rev 7:17 / 21:4 tear-wiping anticipates the final state; Col 1:14 redemption-as-release anticipates eschatological-completion. (iii) **Discipleship-typological:** Mat 19:29 the disciples' leaving anticipates the eschatological reward; Luk 5:11 the fishermen leaving for following is paradigmatically-typological for Christian discipleship. The typological deployment runs across all three primary directions (covenantal, eschatological, christological).

---

**T0.4.2 — Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Direction varies by register but pattern-priority belongs to divine. (i) For sin-release (VCG-01): divine establishes pattern (sacrificial system → Christ's blood); human acts of forgive-others type-back-to-divine (Joh 20:23). (ii) For discipleship-leaving (VCG-03): Christ's own self-emptying (Phil 2 adjacent) establishes pattern; disciple's leaving images it. (iii) For tear-wiping (VCG-10): divine-act-with-no-human-analogue (only God wipes the eschatological tear). The pattern's divine-priority is constant; human participation varies by register.
