**T5.1.1 Nature of Transformation** **[CHAR-5]** E — Transforms both condition (enmity → peace; alienation → restored bond) and orientation (relational disposition reshaped). Col 1:22 names the inner-moral outcome: holy / blameless / above-reproach — character transformation as the inner-outcome.

**T5.1.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Largely irreversible at the divine-act level (Col 1:20 cosmic reconciliation is the eschatological-decisive act); subject to ongoing human responsive engagement at the receiving and ministering levels.

**T5.1.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Sufficient.

**T5.2.1 Sequence of Inner States** **[CHAR-5]** E — Sequence: prior-enmity → reception-of-the-offered-reconciliation → reconciled-state → ongoing reconciled-life with moral formation. Rom 5:10 traces the prior-enmity → reconciled-by-his-death → saved-by-his-life arc.

**T5.2.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reveals reconciliation as **transition from enmity to peace** with moral-character outcome. The sequence operates at relational-and-inner-formation levels together.

**T5.2.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Sufficient.

---

**T5.3.1 Mechanism of Change** **[CHAR-5]** E — Mechanism: **Christic-bodily-death + divine-initiative + ambassadorial-extension + recipient-uptake**. Col 1:20 (blood of the cross); 2Cor 5:18 (God's initiative); 2Cor 5:18–20 (ministry of reconciliation); 2Cor 5:20 (the appeal received).

**T5.3.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Mechanism varies by scope: cosmic (Christ's death suffices); inter-group (the cross + ministry); interpersonal (1Cor 7:11 marital).

**T5.3.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Ontology of how Christ's death effects cosmic reconciliation is divinely-given.

---

**T5.4.1 Suffering and Affliction** **[CHAR-5]** E — Christic suffering is the mechanism (Col 1:22 bodily death; Rom 5:10 his death). At the recipient-end, the post-reconciliation life includes potential continued affliction in fallen-world conditions — the reconciled state doesn't shield from all suffering.

**T5.4.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Christic suffering produces the reconciliation; recipient affliction reveals the depth of the reconciled bond (Rom 5 broader context — suffering produces endurance, character, hope).

**T5.4.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Sufficient.

**T5.5.1 Formation and Sanctification** **[CHAR-5]** E — Col 1:22 names the inner-formation outcome explicitly: holy, blameless, above-reproach. The reconciliation aims AT moral formation. The ambassadorial-ministry is itself formative (sustained reconciliation-extending forms the minister).

**T5.5.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Foundational + telic role. Foundational: the reconciled-state is the standing from which sanctification proceeds. Telic: Col 1:22 the formation-outcome (holy/blameless/above-reproach) is the aim toward which reconciliation works.

**T5.5.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Sufficient.

**T5.6.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Present-reconciliation anticipates cosmic-final reconciliation — Col 1:20 is the bridge.

**T5.6.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Sufficient.
