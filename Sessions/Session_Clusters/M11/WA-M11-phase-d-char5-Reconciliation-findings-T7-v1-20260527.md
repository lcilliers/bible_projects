# M11 Phase D — Char 5 (Reconciliation) — T7 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-E — 20 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.
**Science extract:** Section 3 (Reconciliation and restoration)

---

**T7.1.1 Lexical Roots** **[CHAR-5]** E — **G2644 katallassō** — kata + allassō (to change/exchange). Root meaning: an exchange-of-relational-state. Reconciliation is conceptualized as a *change* — the parties' standing toward each other is exchanged from enmity to peace. **G0604 apokatallassō** — apo + katallassō, intensified form: thorough / complete reconciliation. **G0580 apobolē** — apo + ballō (to cast away). Root reveals reconciliation as *exchange-of-relational-orientation* + the structural-paradox of casting-away (Rom 11:15).

**T7.1.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Verbal forms dominant (katallassō, apokatallassō). The aorist verbs (Rom 5:10 "we were reconciled") name discrete past acts; the participial uses (2Cor 5:18 "having reconciled") name the completed state. No noun-forms for "reconciler" in M11-E base.

**T7.1.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Semantic range: divine-human reconciliation (Rom 5:10; 2Cor 5:18); cosmic reconciliation (Col 1:20); interpersonal marital (1Cor 7:11); inter-group ethnic (Eph 2:16). The range covers individual to cosmic scope.

**T7.1.4** **[CHAR-5]** E — Vocabulary distinguishes katallassō (basic act) from apokatallassō (intensified/comprehensive). The intensified form is reserved for cosmic / inter-group cases.

**T7.1.5** **[CHAR-5]** S — No single antonym-term; enmity (echthra), hostility (echthra), enemies (echthroi) function as the structural-opposite vocabulary in cognate contexts (Rom 5:10 explicit echthroi-while-still-enemies).

**T7.1.6** **[CHAR-5]** S — No habitual-reconciler person-type term. Functional descriptor: "ministers of reconciliation" (2Cor 5:18 — diakonia tēs katallagēs).

**T7.1.7** **[CHAR-5]** S — Supplication-vocabulary is principally the gospel-appeal (2Cor 5:20 deomai "we implore") rather than a dedicated reconciliation-seeking term.

**T7.1.8** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation-vocabulary is principally NT-Greek; the OT carries the conceptual content through shalom-restoration / covenant-renewal language. The NT lexical-development of katallassō provides the dedicated reconciliation vocabulary the OT lacked.

**T7.1.9** **[CHAR-5]** E — *katallagē* (noun, "reconciliation") is NT-foregrounded as a salvation-content noun (Rom 5:11; 2Cor 5:18–19). The noun-form makes the reconciled-state thematizable.

**T7.1.10** **[CHAR-5]** E — Full vocabulary arc reveals reconciliation as the *NT-systematic-development* of the relational-restoration concept that OT carried through covenant-renewal imagery. The NT vocabulary makes the concept explicit, systematic, and Christologically-grounded.

---

**T7.2.1 Verse Function** **[CHAR-5]** E — In primary verse Col 1:20 ("through the blood of the cross... reconcile all things to himself"), apokatallassō functions as the *purpose-verb* of the cosmic-Christic act. The verse structures reconciliation as the *goal* of the Christic-act, with cosmic scope as the breadth. In 2Cor 5:18 ("God reconciled us to himself through Christ"), katallassō names the *completed divine act* with the ministry-entrusting as the consequent participation.

**T7.2.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Primary form: Pauline epistle. Responsible interpretation: requires doctrinal-argument tracking, the broader Pauline theology of cross + new-creation + ministry; awareness of inter-group concerns (Jew-Gentile in Eph; Christ-creation in Col).

**T7.2.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Argument structures: (i) **From-enmity-to-peace** (Rom 5:10): *Premise:* still enemies. *Premise:* reconciled by his death. *Conclusion:* shall be saved by his life. (ii) **Cosmic-scope** (Col 1:20): *Premise:* Christ's blood. *Conclusion:* all-things-reconciled. (iii) **Ministry-entrusted** (2Cor 5:18–20): *Premise:* God reconciled. *Premise:* entrusted the ministry. *Conclusion:* ambassadors appeal.

**T7.2.4** **[CHAR-5]** E — Contexts: christological-soteriological (Rom 5; Col 1; Eph 2); ecclesial-ministerial (2Cor 5); marital-interpersonal (1Cor 7:11); salvation-historical (Rom 11:15 Israel-Gentile).

**T7.2.5** **[CHAR-5]** E — Anchor candidates: **Col 1:20** (cosmic-scope anchor, most-theologically-developed); **2Cor 5:18** (ministry-of-reconciliation foundational); **Rom 5:10** (while-still-enemies anchor). For M11-E principal: Col 1:20 or 2Cor 5:18. The combination of all three names the M11-E theological core.

**T7.2.6** **[CHAR-5]** E — Col 1:20 uniquely reveals the **cosmic-scope of reconciliation** ("all things... in heaven and on earth") — no other M11-E verse states cosmic scope so explicitly. 2Cor 5:18 uniquely reveals the **delegated ministry-of-reconciliation** entrusted to humans.

---

**T7.3.1 Human Science Frameworks** **[CHAR-5]** E — Per science extract Section 3: **conflict-resolution research** (Pruitt, Rubin); **restorative-justice frameworks** (Zehr; Marshall — Restorative Justice: An Overview); **forgiveness-and-reconciliation research** (McCullough; the distinction between forgiveness-as-internal-process and reconciliation-as-relational-restoration). The McCullough distinction is key: reconciliation requires the offender's response and the relational-restoration, whereas forgiveness can be internal-individual.

**T7.3.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Illumination: (i) The forgiveness/reconciliation distinction in empirical research illuminates the M11-B/M11-E boundary (M11-B is more like "forgiveness-as-individual-release"; M11-E is "reconciliation-as-relational-restoration"). (ii) Conflict-resolution research illuminates the Eph 2:16 enmity-killing as a model for how deep group hostility can be dissolved. (iii) Restorative-justice illuminates Rom 11:15 (the paradox of casting-away enabling restoration) as a structural-mechanism question.

**T7.3.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Divergences: (i) Empirical conflict-resolution operates between human parties; biblical reconciliation centrally involves God as principal offended-party and initiator. (ii) Restorative-justice frameworks rest on offender-accountability; biblical reconciliation rests on the *innocent-substitute's-death* (Christ's death enabling reconciliation while the alienated party remains the offending one — Rom 5:10). The divergence at this point is theologically-distinctive. (iii) Empirical frames are silent on cosmic-scope reconciliation; Col 1:20 exceeds the empirical frame.

**T7.3.4** **[CHAR-5]** E — Follow-up: (i) **Inter-group reconciliation research** (Eph 2:16 Jew-Gentile pattern) parallels contemporary work on inter-religious / inter-ethnic reconciliation in conflict-postwar contexts; the gospel-pattern has empirical resonance. (ii) **Forgiveness-but-not-reconciliation cases** (McCullough recognizes that some forgive but do not reconcile) — the corpus implies that some receive forgiveness yet refuse the reconciled-relationship; Phase E synthesis might explore this gap. (iii) **The relational-aftermath of reconciliation** is empirically researched (trust-rebuilding, continued vulnerability) — the corpus's Col 1:22 holy/blameless/above-reproach formation parallels.
