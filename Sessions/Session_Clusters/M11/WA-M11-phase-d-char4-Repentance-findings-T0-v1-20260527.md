# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T0 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-D Repentance and turning (char_seq=4)
**Tier:** T0 — Divine Image (12 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27
**Sub-group & VCG context:** [`WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md`](WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md) §M11-D (42 verses across 9 VCGs)

**Characteristic framing:** Repentance and turning operates in two parallel registers — **divine relenting** (na.cham of God turning from judgment) and **human turning** (shuv, na.cham of contrition, ud structural-opposite). The corpus depicts the two as responsively-linked: human turning frequently triggers divine relenting; divine grief over human failure expresses the relational depth of the divine response.

---

**T0.1.1 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-4]** E — The corpus reveals God's character as **responsive yet sovereign** in the turning/relenting register. Responsive: God relents in response to intercession (Exo 32:14; Amo 7:3, 7:6) and to human turning (Jon 3:10; Jer 18:8). Compassionately motivated: Joe 2:13 "divine relenting over disaster is grounded in God's character — gracious, merciful, slow to anger — making inner relenting a natural expression of steadfast love rather than arbitrary change." Sovereign: 1Sa 15:29 "the Glory of Israel does not relent or change his mind the way a human would — divine constancy is contrasted with the human capacity for regret and reversal of purpose"; Num 23:19 the same. The dual character revealed: God's compassion can move God to relent (responsive); God's purposive will is unchanging in its core (sovereign). The two together: God can change his expressed-judgment without changing his character or covenant commitment.

---

**T0.1.2 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Both relenting and refusal-to-relent are attributed to God. Relenting: Exo 32:14 (Sinai context); Jer 18:8 (potter-and-clay paradigm); Jon 3:10 (Nineveh paradigm); Joe 2:13 (gracious character); 1Ch 21:15 (David's census). Refusal: 1Sa 15:29; Num 23:19; Psa 110:4 (oath irrevocable); Jer 4:28 (will fixed); Eze 24:14 (judgment fixed). Significance for the human: (a) the divine relenting establishes that genuine human turning matters — it changes the trajectory (Jer 18:8 the potter-paradigm); (b) the divine refusal establishes that the moral order is real and not infinitely-bendable; (c) both together establish that the relationship is genuinely-relational without being humanly-controllable.

---

**T0.1.3 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-4]** S — The narrower silence: the corpus does not depict God being *forced* to relent or *unable* to relent — relenting is always within God's sovereign character-disposition. The silence reveals: divine relenting is responsive but never determined-from-outside.

---

**T0.2.1 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Human turning equips the person to **return-from-sin / re-orient-toward-God** through the inner act of repentance. shuv (H7725N) is the canonical turning-vocabulary: 1Ki 8:47 "the inward act of turning the heart, acknowledging sin, and pleading with God"; 2Ch 6:37 "inward turning... accompanied by confession of sin... precedes and enables restoration"; Deu 30:1 "memory of covenant initiating repentance." The created purpose: humans are equipped to recognize misalignment with God, turn from it, and re-enter covenantal posture. na.cham at the human level (Job 42:6 "self-abasement... contrition genuine"; Jer 31:19 "contrition genuine, shame arising, self-reproach") names the inner-felt-experience of the turning. The faculty is faculty-of-moral-redirection.

---

**T0.2.2 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Human turning is principally **response-to-fallen-condition** — every shuv verse presupposes a prior straying that requires the return. However, the *capacity to turn* is part of original moral design — pre-fall persons have the volitional capacity to align-or-misalign; what changes after fall is the actual operation of the faculty being now needed for return-from-misalignment. Divine relenting is principally response-to-human-condition — God relents because of how God relates to people's turnings. The pre-fall divine character includes the disposition that would relent (Joe 2:13 character-grounded), but its operation as relenting-from-judgment is fall-conditioned.

---

**T0.2.3 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Yes — eschatological orientation present. Jer 31:34 (the new-covenant context implies the eschatological-fullness of turning — sin remembered no more presupposes complete turning); Joe 2:13–14 the corporate-turning anticipates the eschatological-restoration; Dan 9 the prophetic-intercession-pattern anticipates final restoration. The human-turning act is itself oriented toward final restoration; the divine-relenting pattern anticipates the eschatological mercy.

---

**T0.3.1 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Image-bearer expression is most directly in **the capacity to turn from wrong toward right**. The human shuv-act is the inner faculty by which the divine image's moral-orientation can be re-set. Job 42:6 (whole-person contrition); Jer 31:19 (the post-turning shame-and-self-reproach) — these are image-bearing moments where the moral-self-knowledge of the image is operating. Divine relenting (Exo 32:14; Jon 3:10) reveals God as the relenting-one; the human relenting (na.cham at Job 42:6) reveals image-bearing capacity to participate in the same disposition. The Pro-16:6-style hesed+emet character (M11-A cross-evidence) anchors here too: the formed character that turns naturally toward right.

---

**T0.3.2 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Shared with structural-asymmetry. (i) Divine relenting (God turning from announced judgment) is exclusively divine; humans cannot relent in this judicial sense. (ii) Human turning (turning from sin) is exclusively creaturely; God does not turn from sin (Heb 1 / Phil 2 adjacent — God is not the kind of being who sins). (iii) Both share the inner-faculty-of-turning *as faculty* — God's capacity to relent and humans' capacity to turn participate in a common "responsive-redirection" character; the asymmetry is in WHAT they turn from. (iv) The Christic-incarnational link: Christ as fully-divine + fully-human bears both — sinless yet capable of compassion-driven response.

---

**T0.3.3 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Presence: a person who turns when confronted with sin (Job 42:6; Jer 31:19; 2Ch 7:14 humility-prayer-seeking-turning) demonstrates the image at work. Absence: Jer 8:6 "no one relents of evil or questions their conduct — inner self-examination is entirely absent" — the negative-pole VCG-07 names the image-failure as the absent-turning. Mar 4:12 "perception closed... will hardened" — image-faculty disabled. The presence-or-absence of the turning-faculty is diagnostic of the image's operative state in the person.

---

**T0.4.1 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Typological use is present. (i) **Covenantal:** the shuv-recall of covenant (Deu 30:1) is typologically embedded in the covenant-mediating structure. (ii) **Eschatological:** Jer 31:34 (new-covenant) typologically anticipates the final-comprehensive turning/forgiveness pairing. (iii) **Christological:** Christ as eschatological turning-point inaugurating the new-covenant turning-mercy; though M11-D doesn't have the directly-NT-attestation that M11-A and M11-B have, the Christic-anchor is implicit through the gospel-call-to-repentance pattern. (iv) **Type-of-divine-character:** divine relenting in OT typologically signals the depth of divine character that will be fully revealed in the gospel's mercy.

---

**T0.4.2 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-4]** E — Both directions. The Joe 2:13 character-grounded relenting establishes that divine relenting is character-typed — it expresses who God is. The human-turning act (shuv) types-back to the divine pattern (humans turn because God is the kind of God who responds to turning). The bidirectional typology is consistent across the cluster.

---

**T0.4.3 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-4]** S — Catalogue fail-safe; typological use is evidenced.
