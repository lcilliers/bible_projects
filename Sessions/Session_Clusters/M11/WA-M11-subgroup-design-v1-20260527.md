## §1. Cluster framing under v3_0

The Phase 3 (= v3_0 B.1) verdicts concluded all 15 terms STAY. The cross-term observations §1 of the verdicts file proposed **one characteristic with three mechanisms** (Release / Atonement / Turning).

Under v3_0 §6.2.2 ("sub-groups represent characteristics"), the design re-reads the same evidence at characteristic-granularity. The five mechanisms identified in the verse evidence — atonement, divine forgiveness, broader release, repentance/turning, reconciliation — describe **five distinct inner-being phenomena** that are causally linked but ontologically distinct:

- **Different actors.** A priestly atonement act has a mediator; a divine forgiveness act has a sovereign pardoner; a repentance act has a contrite human turning; a reconciliation has both parties.
- **Different inner-being content.** The mediator *bears*; the pardoner *releases*; the offender *turns*; the parties *restore*.
- **Different verses.** Each mechanism has its own anchor verses and its own term family.

This produces **five sub-groups, five characteristics, 1:1 default** under v3_0 §6.2.2. The five remain causally linked — atonement enables forgiveness, repentance opens forgiveness, all converge on reconciliation — and that linkage is exactly the content of T6 (Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics) findings within the cluster at Phase D.

The cluster as a whole still names a coherent inner-being movement (sin → restoration); v3_0's contribution is that this coherent movement is built from distinguishable characteristic-states, each of which carries its own analytical weight.

## §2. Sub-group definitions

### §2.1 M11-A — Atonement

**Label:** Atonement
**Inner-being characteristic:** the priestly/sacrificial mechanism by which sin and defilement are covered, removed, or borne — the inner-being state of mediation that effects sin-release. Includes both the action-state of the mediator (bearing, covering, propitiating) and the resulting state of the one atoned-for (cleansed, brought to standing).

**Core description:** Atonement names the mechanism by which sin's effects are removed through a substitutionary or covering act — typically priestly, sacrificial, or self-giving. The Hebrew `kip.per` is the central OT atonement verb across the Day of Atonement, levitical sacrifice, defilement-cleansing (Lev 12–15), intercession-atonement (Num 16:46–47, Psa 65:3), and divine atonement corpus. The NT atoning surrender appears in two `afiēmi`-permit verses where Christ voluntarily releases his spirit (Mat 27:50) and grants free passage at his arrest (Joh 18:8) — these constitute the central atoning act of self-substitution. The inner-being content of atonement is the bearing/covering of sin from the mediator's side and the resulting cleansed standing from the recipient's side.

**Terms:**
- H3722A `kip.per` (to atone) — 81V
- G0863I `afiēmi` (permit) — 2V (vc=17636 Joh 18:8; vc=17641 Mat 27:50; per B.2 pre-decision §3.3)

**Verse count:** 83 (28.8% of substantive corpus)

**Anchor candidates for B.3:** Lev 16:30 (Day of Atonement summary); Mat 27:50 (Christ's atoning death).

---

### §2.2 M11-B — Divine forgiveness

**Label:** Divine forgiveness
**Inner-being characteristic:** the sovereign divine act of releasing sin's guilt — the inner-being state of being pardoned and the corresponding divine disposition of pardoning. The release is enacted by God; its inner-being content is the lifting of guilt's weight from the offender's standing.

**Core description:** Divine forgiveness names the act and state by which God releases sin's guilt — explicitly distinguished from atonement (the mechanism) and from inter-personal release (the broader Greek `afiēmi` family). The Hebrew `sa.lach` is the primary divine-forgiveness verb (Solomon's temple prayer corpus, new-covenant forgiveness in Jer 31:34, repentance-conditioned forgiveness, limits-of-forgiveness verses). `sal.lach` (forgiving, Psa 86:5) and `se.li.chah` (forgiveness as attribute, Neh 9:17 / Psa 130:4 / Dan 9:9) describe forgiveness as a stable divine character. The Greek `afiēmi`-forgive sense (G0863H, 37V) is the central NT divine forgiveness verb — divine authority to forgive sins, interpersonal forgiveness as mirror of divine, forgiveness in response to repentance.

**Terms:**
- H5545 `sa.lach` (to forgive) — 45V
- H5546 `sal.lach` (forgiving) — 1V
- H5547 `se.li.chah` (forgiveness) — 3V
- G0863H `afiēmi` (forgive sense) — 37V

**Verse count:** 86 (29.9% of substantive corpus) — largest sub-group, within 40% gate.

**Anchor candidates for B.3:** Jer 31:34 (new-covenant forgiveness; "I will remember their sin no more"); Mat 9:6 (divine authority to forgive on earth).

**Cross-register note:** `sal.lach` and `se.li.chah` carry M05 (Love/Compassion) overlap as divine-character attributes; `se.li.chah` carries an M01 (Fear) cross-register flag from Psa 130:4 (forgiveness as the ground of awe). These cross-registers will inform T6 findings, not sub-group placement.

### §2.3 M11-C — Release (broader sense)

**Label:** Release
**Inner-being characteristic:** the act and state of releasing — from sin's record, debt, obligation, bondage, possession, or relationship. Broader than divine forgiveness; this register names the *act of letting go* itself, applicable to sin-records, financial debts, family bonds, eschatological separations, and erasure-of-record acts. The inner-being content is the unencumbered freed state and the corresponding act of releasing.

**Core description:** This sub-group carries the Greek `afiēmi`/`afesis` release vocabulary in its broader sense beyond divine forgiveness — the act of letting go that may but need not have sin as its object. `afesis` carries the noun "release/forgiveness" across the NT (forgiveness-through-sacrifice, repentance-as-pathway, salvation as cognitive release-of-sin). `afiēmi`-leave (G0863G) carries the volitional-detachment register for the majority of its 32 verses (disciples leaving family/possessions, eschatological separations, abandonment) plus a minority of M11-relational verses (Mat 5:24 leaving to reconcile, Heb 6:1 leaving repentance-from-dead-works, Rev 2:4 abandoning first love, 1Cor 7:11 reconciliation to husband). `afiēmi`-permit (G0863I) carries permission/volition for the majority of its 18 verses. `exaleifō` (to blot out) is the radical erasure register — sin-records blotted out (Act 3:19, Col 2:14) and downstream eschatological wiping-away of tears (Rev 7:17, Rev 21:4).

The B.3 design will VCG-separate these registers within M11-C: a divine-release-of-sin VCG (afesis primary verses), a volitional-detachment VCG (G0863G majority), a permission/volition VCG (G0863I majority), an erasure VCG (exaleifō).

**Terms:**
- G0859 `afesis` (forgiveness/release) — 16V
- G0863G `afiēmi` (leave sense) — 32V
- G0863I `afiēmi` (permit sense) — 16V (the 18 IB verses minus the 2 atonement-relational verses routed to M11-A)
- G1813 `exaleifō` (to blot out) — 4V

**Verse count:** 68 (23.6% of substantive corpus)

**Anchor candidates for B.3:** Mat 26:28 (forgiveness through sacrifice; afesis); Act 3:19 (blotting out of sins; exaleifō).

**Cross-register notes (carry-forward to B.3 and Phase D):**
- `G0863G afiēmi` majority register is M30/M29 (volitional-detachment) per Phase 3 verdict; ~22 of 32 verses are non-M11-primary content (still routed to M11-C as release-broad). B.3 will VCG-separate.
- `G0863I afiēmi` majority register is M29 (permission/volition); ~16 of 18 verses are non-M11-primary content. B.3 will VCG-separate.
- These observations seed `cluster_observation` rows at Phase C (status=`open`, target_phase=`D`).

### §2.4 M11-D — Repentance / turning

**Label:** Repentance and turning
**Inner-being characteristic:** the inner human act of turning from sin toward God — the contrite movement that opens the way for forgiveness and reconciliation. Also covers divine relenting (na.cham) where God turns from a planned judgment, often in response to intercession or human turning. The characteristic is the inner act of *turning* itself, in both human and divine senses.

**Core description:** Turning names the inner human movement from sin toward God — repentance as the precondition or initiation of restoration. `shuv` (recall sense, H7725N) carries the inner turning of memory, covenant-recall, and inward turning in exile (Deu 30:1, 1Ki 8:47, 2Ch 6:37, Lam 3:21). `na.cham` carries divine relenting from judgment (Exo 32:12/14, Jer 18:8/10, Jon 3:10) and human contrition (Job 42:6, Jer 31:19). `ud` carries a single structural-opposite verse (Psa 119:61 — sustained faithfulness as the opposite face of the turning-from-abandonment that repentance addresses). The structural negatives of M11's repentance-characteristic also belong here (Jer 8:6 no one relents; Eze 24:14 divine refusal to relent).

**Terms:**
- H7725N `shuv` (recall) — 7V
- H5162H `na.cham` (relent/be sorry) — 34V
- H5749A `ud` (return) — 1V (marginal STAYS per B.1; structural-opposite)

**Verse count:** 42 (14.6% of substantive corpus)

**Anchor candidates for B.3:** Joe 2:13 (divine relenting from steadfast love); 1Ki 8:47–48 (heart-level turning with acknowledgement of sin).

**Observation seed for Phase C:** H5749A ud (Psa 119:61) is the programme's most marginal STAYS verdict (Phase 3 §6.3.2 structural-opposite reading). Log as `cluster_observation` at Phase C with `status='open'`, `target_phase='D'` — Phase D may surface whether the verse evidences M11 in a Phase D T6 reading or whether it should retroactively become BOUNDARY with set_aside disposition.

### §2.5 M11-E — Reconciliation

**Label:** Reconciliation
**Inner-being characteristic:** the relational restoration outcome — the inner-being state of being brought back into right relationship, and the act that effects it. Distinct from atonement (the mechanism) and forgiveness (the act of pardon): reconciliation names the *relational outcome* and the inner-being state of *being-at-peace* / *restored-to-standing*. Includes both interpersonal reconciliation (between estranged parties) and cosmic reconciliation (creation to Creator through Christ).

**Core description:** Reconciliation names the relational restoration as inner-being state. `apokatallassō` carries the cosmic and ecclesial reconciliation (Eph 2:16 enmity killed, Col 1:20 cosmic reconciliation, Col 1:22 holy-blameless-above-reproach as inner purpose). `katallassō` carries both interpersonal reconciliation (1Cor 7:11) and the central theological-heart 2Cor 5:18–20 ministry-of-reconciliation passage where God initiates restoration through Christ. `apobolē` (deprivation, Rom 11:15) names Israel's rejection as the mechanism through which reconciliation extends to the world — the obverse-mechanism of restoration.

**Terms:**
- G0604 `apokatallassō` (reconcile) — 3V
- G2644 `katallassō` (reconcile) — 5V
- G0580 `apobolē` (deprivation/rejection) — 1V

**Verse count:** 9 (3.1% of substantive corpus) — smallest substantive sub-group; well-within the lower bound (no minimum gate in v3_0 §6.2.7).

**Anchor candidate for B.3:** 2Cor 5:18–19 (the ministry of reconciliation; "not counting their trespasses against them").
