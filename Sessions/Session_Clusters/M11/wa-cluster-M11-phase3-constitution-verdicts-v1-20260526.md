**Primary cross-register:** M10 (the deprivation/rejection side of the paradox implies a guilt-that-required-resolution)

## Cross-term observations (Phase 5 notes)

### 1. Three-register composition: ONE characteristic, not three

All three registers (Release / Atonement / Turning) evidence the same inner-being movement — from sin/defilement/alienation to restored standing before God. The registers describe the mechanisms by which that movement is effected, not three separate movements. Key evidence:
- kip.per (atonement) consistently produces sa.lach (forgiveness) as its outcome — these are two stages of one arc.
- na.cham/shuv (repentance-turning) consistently appear as the precondition that opens sa.lach/afesis — turning is the inner human act that enables the release.
- katallassō/apokatallassō (reconciliation) names the relational outcome of the entire arc.

**Phase 3 conclusion:** All 15 terms constitute ONE cluster. The registers are Phase 5 sub-group axes, not cluster-split grounds. The programme's naming ("Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration") accurately reflects a three-mechanism cluster unified by one characteristic.

### 2. Internal pairing: kip.per ↔ sa.lach (Register B → Register A arc)

kip.per appears as the mechanism producing sa.lach as its declared outcome across at least 15-20 Leviticus/Numbers verses. This internal pairing will be the centrepiece of M11's Phase 5 sub-group design and Phase 7 VCG architecture. The two terms are analytically distinct but structurally inseparable within M11's characteristic.

### 3. Phase 5 distribution signal

H3722A kip.per has 81 verses — approximately 28% of the cluster's 288 active verses. This is under the 40% distribution gate but is the dominant term. The Phase 5 designer should expect kip.per to either constitute its own sub-group (Atonement register) or to be split across sub-groups (if the day-of-atonement corpus, the defilement-cleansing corpus, and the intercession-atonement corpus warrant separate sub-groups). Distribution gate will need to be checked post-Phase-5.

### 4. afiēmi sense-split — multi-faceted Phase 5 design

The three afiēmi sense-rows (G0863G leave, G0863H forgive, G0863I permit) are already sense-split in the inventory. G0863H (forgive, 37 verses) is clearly primary M11. G0863G (leave, 32 verses) and G0863I (permit, 18 verses) carry substantial non-M11 verse sets. Phase 5 will need to design with this in mind: G0863G and G0863I are multi-faceted terms (§8.2 Phase 5 rule 6); their non-M11 verses will need to be handled within substantive sub-groups appropriate to their actual meaning, not parked in BOUNDARY.

### 5. Key cross-cluster pairs for Phase 9 T6 findings

The following cross-cluster pairs are anticipated from Phase 3 reading, for Phase 9 T6 consideration:
- M11 ↔ M10: sin/guilt as the precondition for every forgiveness-act in M11 (structural pair — M10 names what M11 addresses)
- M11 ↔ M10c: defilement as the precondition for cleansing-atonement (kip.per in Lev 12–15, 1Jn 1:7 bloodcleansing context)
- M11 ↔ M07: shame as an outcome of received atonement (Eze 16:63) and as what forgiveness removes
- M11 ↔ M05: divine steadfast love/compassion as the inner disposition driving divine relenting and forgiveness (na.cham/sa.lach)
- M11 ↔ M01: forgiveness as the ground of fear/reverence (Psa 130:4 — se.li.chah)
- M11 ↔ M21: prayer/intercession as the mechanism of accessing forgiveness (sa.lach corpus; na.cham corpus)
