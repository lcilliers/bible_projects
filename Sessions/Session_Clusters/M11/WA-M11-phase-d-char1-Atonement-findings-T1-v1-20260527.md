**T1.1.1 — Name and Naming — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The characteristic is named **Atonement** in the programme — the standard English rendering of kip.per (H3722A, 81 of 83 corpus verses). "Atonement" historically derives from "at-one-ment" — the act that makes the parties one — and the verb-noun pair signals an act effecting reunion through cover. The Hebrew root kpr carries the underlying senses of "cover" (a roof or pitch covering, Gen 6:14) and "ransom" (Exo 30:12-16; M11-A-VCG-04). The English name therefore signals the essential nature accurately at the result level (parties brought to one), while the underlying Hebrew names the mechanism (cover/ransom). Across M11-A the name's surface meaning holds: every operative atonement act moves the parties from estrangement toward standing-together. The name does not signal *who* atones (mediator, divine actor, inner moral disposition all evidenced); it names *what the act effects* — restored at-one-ness via cover.

---

**T1.1.2 — Name and Naming — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — **H3722A kip.per** (the dominant term, 81V) carries three semantic poles in M11-A evidence: (i) priestly/ritual cover via blood sacrifice (Lev 16:30 anchor; Lev 4:20–6:7 corpus); (ii) ransom-as-life-substitution (Exo 30:15–16; Lev 17:11 "blood is the divinely appointed means of atonement because life itself resides in it"); (iii) inner-character-as-cover (Pro 16:6 "iniquity is atoned for through steadfast love and faithfulness"). **G0863I afiēmi** in M11-A (Mat 27:50, Joh 18:8) is not the standard NT atonement-vocabulary verb — afiēmi means "release/permit/let go" — but the two M11-A-routed verses use it for a will-surrender act that is functionally atoning (Christ's volitional release of his spirit; protective self-substitution). The definitional convergence: at the base level the characteristic is *life-substituting cover* — one life given to cover another's guilt — whether mediated ritually (kip.per) or enacted as personal self-giving (afiēmi).

---

**T1.1.3 — Name and Naming — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The name carries strong directionality and relationality, with constitutional implications subordinate. **Directional:** atonement is consistently *toward* an offended party (typically God; sometimes a wronged human party — Gen 32:20 Jacob's gift toward Esau; 2Sa 21:3 toward the wronged tribe). The atoning act moves *from* the offender or mediator *toward* the offended one to effect cover. **Relational:** every atonement act in the corpus presupposes a relationship under strain — guilt incurred, defilement contracted, alienation present. Atonement is unintelligible apart from a relational frame. **Constitutional:** the act operates on inner-being states (Lev 16:30 "inner cleansing from all sins"; Eze 16:63 produces lasting humility) but does so via instrumental mechanisms (sacrifice, mediation, surrender). The constitution involved is therefore composite — the inner state of guilt is addressed via an external instrument. The directional and relational dimensions orient inquiry primarily; constitutional questions arise downstream once the directional act has been mapped.

---

**T1.2.1 — Kind — What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is principally an **act** with downstream **condition** consequences, and in derivative cases a **disposition** or **quality**. As act: the verses overwhelmingly use kip.per as a verb of doing — the priest makes atonement (Lev 4:20–6:7), Moses makes atonement (Exo 32:30), God acts to atone (Psa 65:3), Christ enacts atonement (Mat 27:50). As condition consequence: the recipient enters a new condition of being-atoned-for / cleansed / forgiven (Lev 14:20; Lev 16:30; Lev 19:22 "the act of wrongdoing is both acknowledged and formally covered"). As disposition: Psa 86:5 and Neh 9:17 (M11-B) name God's readiness to forgive as a stable disposition; in M11-A specifically, Pro 16:6 names love+faithfulness as inner dispositions that themselves atone — disposition-as-mechanism. As quality: less directly evidenced; M11-A treats atonement primarily as something done, not as a static quality of a person. The principal kind is therefore **act** — atonement is something performed (by priest, intercessor, or God) that produces a condition in another.

---

**T1.2.2 — Kind — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is structurally **composite**, with at least four constituent elements visible at first encounter across M11-A. (i) **Object:** the guilt, sin, defilement, or estrangement to be addressed (Lev 4:35 "sin he hath sinned"; Lev 16:16 "uncleanness, transgressions, sins"; Lev 12–15 corpus defilement). (ii) **Mediator/Agent:** the priest, intercessor, or God who performs the act (Lev 16:6 high priest; Exo 32:30 Moses; Psa 65:3 God). (iii) **Substance/Mechanism:** the sacrificial offering, life-blood, ransom, or inner-disposition that effects cover (Lev 1:4 hand-laying identification; Lev 17:11 blood-as-life; Pro 16:6 love+faithfulness; Mat 27:50 Christ's spirit). (iv) **Outcome:** restored standing / cleanness / forgiveness for the recipient (Lev 4:20 "forgiven"; Lev 14:20 "declared clean"; Eze 16:63 "atonement covering"). Each operative atonement requires all four; the absence of any (e.g. no acknowledged object, no qualified mediator, no real mechanism) yields the negative-pole condition of VCG-08 (atonement denied / impossible / false-covering). The composite structure is not arbitrary — it tracks the relational/directional logic identified at T1.1.3.

---

**T1.2.3 — Kind — What is the current best working description of this characteristic — encapsulating its constitutional location, the faculties it primarily engages, and its impact on the person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — **Working description (Atonement):** An act in which a qualified mediator (priest, intercessor, or God) employs a life-substituting mechanism (sacrificial blood, ransom, or self-giving) to cover an inner-being condition of guilt, defilement, or estrangement, producing a restored standing for the recipient before the offended party. Constitutional location: the inner-being state of guilt/defilement is the seat addressed; cover is effected through external mediation; outcome registers at the same inner-being location as cleansed/forgiven standing. Faculties engaged: at the recipient level — chiefly the moral conscience (Lev 16:30 "conscience cleared") and the relational self (covenant standing restored, Lev 4:20); at the mediator level — the priestly office (Lev 16:32 "consecrated priest... designated agent"); at the divine level — God's compassion and justice held together (Psa 78:38 "compassion divine... wrath restrained"). Impact: removes accumulated guilt-weight, restores cleanness, re-enables proximity (Num 8:19 Levite atonement as protective barrier enabling people to draw near). The composite act has whole-person inner-being consequences.

---

**T1.3.1 — Boundary — What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — the inner-being reality that stands against or excludes it?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The structural opposite of atonement is **sealed unatoneable guilt** — the inner-being condition in which the atonement faculty has been refused, exhausted, or made impossible. M11-A-VCG-08 names this register directly: guilt declared unforgivable until death (Isa 22:14); house's iniquity beyond atonement by sacrifice — entrenched and unrepented to the point that no ritual covering can reach it (1Sa 3:14); plotters' iniquity not to be atoned (Jer 18:23); coming disaster impossible to atone for — inner resources for dealing with guilt entirely exhausted (Isa 47:11); false covering exposed — atonement that was thought secure annulled (Isa 28:18). The structural opposite is therefore not a different inner-being characteristic, but the **closed-state of this very characteristic** in a person or community. This is structurally significant: it shows the boundary of atonement is internal to itself (sealed-against-cover) rather than external (some other faculty competing with it). The cross-register parallel runs across M11-B-VCG-04 (forgiveness foreclosed) and M11-C-VCG-02 (release foreclosed).

---

**T1.3.2 — Boundary — What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement explicitly excludes / resists: (i) **self-deception about coverage** — Isa 28:18 names the false-covenant covering exposed and annulled, signalling atonement resists any pseudo-cover that is not divinely-authorized; (ii) **defiant wilful self-blessing** — Deu 29:20 (M11-B but the principle holds across M11-A) names wilful self-deception in the face of known obligation as placing the person beyond pardon; (iii) **unrepented entrenchment** — 1Sa 3:14 names atonement explicitly denied where the iniquity is so entrenched and unrepented that no ritual covering can reach; (iv) **moral-perception failure** — Isa 47:11 names the inability to atone as flowing from inner resources exhausted (i.e. the perception of the moral situation has collapsed). What atonement does NOT exclude (notably): horizontal/material/circumstantial inner-being content (Pro 16:14 wisdom defusing royal wrath; Gen 32:20 calculated gift; 2Sa 21:3 reparations to wronged tribe — these are interpersonal atonement and remain valid). The exclusion is not of register but of inner-being condition: atonement excludes the inner state of refusing to be atoned for.

---

**T1.3.3 — Boundary — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement (M11-A) ends and something else begins at several boundaries. **Atonement is not forgiveness (M11-B).** Atonement is the *mechanism* by which forgiveness is enabled; forgiveness is the *declared release of guilt* (the sa.lach/afiēmi outcome). The kip.per → sa.lach arc (M11-A-VCG-02 → M11-B-VCG-05) is causal: atonement made, forgiveness declared. Lev 4:20 et al. carry both verbs in sequence — distinct acts. **Atonement is not repentance (M11-D).** Repentance is the human inner turning (shuv) that opens the way for forgiveness; atonement is the covering mechanism that enables the forgiveness given to the repentant. **Atonement is not reconciliation (M11-E).** Reconciliation is the relational outcome of the entire arc; atonement is one link in the chain (Christic atonement → cosmic reconciliation, Mat 27:50 → Col 1:20). **Atonement is not release (M11-C).** Release (afiēmi-leave/permit/afesis) is the broader act of letting-go; atonement is the specifically-substitutionary subtype. **Atonement is not divine grief (M11-D-VCG-04).** Divine grief (na.cham) over human failure is M03 territory; divine atoning action is M11-A. The boundaries are causal and definitional, and they map to the cluster's other four characteristics.

**T1.4.1 — Modes of Operation — In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement operates in the inner person in at least five distinct modes evidenced across M11-A. (i) **Conscience-clearing** — Lev 16:30 "atonement... achieves inner cleansing from all sins, restoring the person to a state of purity before God"; the operative effect is a felt unburdening at the conscience. (ii) **Standing-restoration** — Lev 4:20 the person is "declared forgiven, guilt fully resolved"; the operative effect is reinstatement to covenant standing. (iii) **Defilement-cleansing** — Lev 14:20, Lev 12–15 corpus: atonement removes the unclean state that prevented proximity; the operative effect is renewed access to community/cult. (iv) **Wrath-averting** — Num 16:46–47 (Aaron's incense in the plague), Num 25:13 (Phinehas's zeal): atonement interposes between active divine wrath and its outworking; the operative effect is judgment arrested mid-execution. (v) **Image-bearing surrender** — Mat 27:50 Christ's voluntary release of spirit: atonement operates as the act of self-giving that effects redemption for others. Each mode engages different aspects of inner-being (conscience / standing / cleanness / safety / agency); the characteristic is multimodal at the inner-person level.

---

**T1.4.2 — Modes of Operation — Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement operates differently by context, direction, and constitutional level. **By context:** ritual atonement (Lev 16 DoA; VCG-01) operates as scheduled, comprehensive, community-scope cover; crisis atonement (Num 16:46–47, Exo 32:30; VCG-05) operates as urgent, intercessory, wrath-arresting; institutional atonement (1Ch 6:49, 2Ch 29:24; VCG-06) operates as ongoing structural provision. **By direction:** God-to-human atonement (Psa 65:3, Isa 6:7; VCG-07) operates as divine initiative when human resources are exhausted; human-to-God atonement (Lev 4–7 corpus; VCG-02) operates as offerer-via-priest mediated; human-to-human atonement (Gen 32:20 gift to Esau; 2Sa 21:3; Pro 16:14; VCG-09) operates as relational reparation. **By constitutional level:** atonement of bodily uncleanness (Lev 12–15; VCG-03) operates at the bodily/ritual level; atonement of moral guilt (Lev 4–7; VCG-02) operates at the conscience level; atonement of cosmic alienation (Dan 9:24, Mat 27:50; VCG-10) operates at the relational-cosmic level. The same root act (kip.per) calibrates to each.

---

**T1.4.3 — Modes of Operation — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — and if so, how does it function?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement has limited but real speech-mode operation. The atoning act itself is overwhelmingly **action-based, not speech-based** — sacrifice, blood, hand-laying, incense, surrender. However, four speech-aspects are evidenced. (i) **Declarative completion** — the priest's pronouncement of forgiveness (Lev 4:20–6:7 "and he shall be forgiven") is the speech-act that names the atonement complete; the speech does not effect the atonement but seals it as recognized. (ii) **Intercessory plea** — Moses's verbal appeal "atone for the people's sin" (Exo 32:30); Phinehas's zealous act prompts subsequent verbal covenantal announcement (Num 25:13); the speech mobilizes or accompanies the atoning action. (iii) **Inner moral disposition expressed** — Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness atone) and Pro 16:14 (wisdom defuses wrath) name speech-rich character dispositions as themselves atoning. (iv) **Christic verbalized surrender** — though Mat 27:50 names the act, the surrounding context includes Christ's "It is finished" (not in M11-A's evidence directly, but the act is verbalized at the moment of release). The speech-mode is therefore subordinate to the act-mode but not absent.

**T1.5.1 — Immediate Response — What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The first inner-being response evidenced to receiving atonement is a **shamed, silenced humility** — not euphoric release. Eze 16:63 names this most explicitly: "atonement produces shame and silencing in the inner person — the experience of being covered despite past unfaithfulness closes the mouth and opens the memory to lasting confounded humility." The recipient does not respond with proud joy at being covered; the response is awe and silence at having been covered by one against whom much was committed. This shape is corroborated by Job 42:6 (M11-D, post-divine-encounter dust-and-ashes contrition) and Jer 31:19 (post-turning shame and self-reproach). At the priestly level: Lev 16:30 "inner cleansing... restoring the person to a state of purity before God" implies the standing-shift but the corpus does not depict an euphoric immediate response. The atoning act addresses the inner state without producing manic relief — the immediate response is the gravitational settling of guilt's weight being lifted, accompanied by the recognition of what one has been covered from.

---

**T1.5.2 — Immediate Response — Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The immediate response varies somewhat across contexts but converges on the shamed-humility pattern at the personal-receiving level. **Variation observed:** (i) at the corporate level (Num 16:47 plague halted; Lev 16 DoA), the immediate response is more relief-at-judgment-averted than personal humility — the corpus images the community continuing on rather than collapsing in shame; (ii) at the cleansing-atonement level (Lev 14:20 person declared clean), the immediate response is structural re-entry into community rather than emotional response named; (iii) at the Christic level (Mat 27:50), the immediate response is registered in the surrounding context (temple curtain torn, centurion's confession) but M11-A itself does not depict the recipient response in this verse. **Convergence:** where the recipient's inner response is named — Eze 16:63, and the parallel Job/Jer verses — the pattern is silenced humility. The convergence suggests that *the more deeply atonement is internalized by a personal recipient, the more it produces sober humility rather than triumphant relief*.

---

**T1.5.3 — Immediate Response — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The corpus is largely silent on personal immediate-response in the majority of Levitical atonement verses (Lev 4–7, 12–15, Num 5, 15, 28–29; ~50 of 83 verses). The text records the priestly act and its declared outcome ("he shall be forgiven", "he shall be clean") without describing the recipient's inner experience. This silence is analytically significant — it suggests the corpus framework treats the *atoning act + standing-restoration* as the primary content, with the inner-experiential response being either presupposed or unnecessary to the analytical record. The silence may also reflect that recurring institutional atonement (Lev 16:34 perpetual statute) is too ordinary a process to warrant inner-experiential description in each instance. Where the corpus *does* describe inner response (Eze 16:63, and the related Job 42:6 / Jer 31:19), it is in extraordinary moments — not in the daily Levitical operation.

**T1.6.1 — Sustained Effect — What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Sustained atonement produces in the inner being a **stable cleansed standing** that enables continuous proximity to God and renewed moral capacity. Across the Levitical corpus, the cumulative effect is the people's covenant standing maintained over time (Exo 30:16 "remembrance maintained... atonement ongoing"; Lev 16:34 annual statute reaffirms standing every year). Sustained atonement enables sustained access (Num 8:19 Levite mediation as protective barrier enabling people to draw near; Lev 16 priestly access to holiest space). At the personal level, Eze 16:63 names the long-term effect as lasting humility — the inner being is permanently shaped by the memory of having been covered despite past unfaithfulness. Pro 16:6 names the inner-character effect: when love and faithfulness function as atonement, the person who lives in this mode acquires a sustained moral standing — atoning character as ongoing inner-being condition rather than discrete event. The cosmic-level sustained effect is named at Dan 9:24 — atonement contributes to a final cosmic resolution ending transgression and sin permanently. Sustained atonement therefore produces both maintained standing and progressive maturation toward final standing.

---

**T1.6.2 — Sustained Effect — What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Sustained exposure to atonement establishes: (i) **Cleansed standing** as ongoing state — the offerer remains "clean" until next defilement (Lev 14:20 declared clean; Lev 12:7/8); (ii) **Capacity for proximity** — the atoned person can enter cultic space, approach God, participate in community (Lev 16 sanctuary access via priestly atonement); (iii) **Humbled remembrance** as inner quality — Eze 16:63 the lasting confounded humility, a posture of self-knowledge in the light of unmerited cover; (iv) **Moral capacity rooted in character** — Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness as inner atoning disposition) shapes the person into one whose own character now functions in the atoning mode toward others; (v) **Mediating capacity** — the consecrated priest who has himself been atoned-for becomes capable of mediating atonement for others (Lev 9:7 sequence: priest's self-atonement first, then atonement for people); (vi) **Eschatological orientation** — the recurring atonement (Lev 16:34 perpetual statute) habituates the person to live with a future-fullness orientation (Dan 9:24 final atonement to come). Each is established by sustained, not single-event, exposure.

---

**T1.6.3 — Sustained Effect — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — the sustained effect differs structurally from the immediate response. **Immediate response** (T1.5.1) is silenced humility — the felt experience of being covered despite past unfaithfulness; emotionally charged, conscience-focused, individually-experienced. **Sustained effect** (T1.6.1–T1.6.2) is stable cleansed standing — the structural/relational state of being maintained in covenant standing; less emotionally charged, more constitutionally settled, communally-experienced. The transition is from event to state, from inner reverence to outer posture. Eze 16:63 names both registers and the transition between them: the immediate is mouth-closed shame; the lasting is confounded humility — a settled disposition that carries the memory of the immediate without remaining acute. Pro 16:6 names the further mature state where the person's own character now embodies the atoning disposition (sustained effect becomes a character quality of the recipient). The difference matters analytically because it cautions against analyzing M11-A purely as crisis-event (the cleansing of a guilt-state in a moment) and demands analysis of M11-A as constitutive of ongoing standing.

**T1.7.1 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Genuine reception of atonement is enabled by inner conditions evidenced across M11-A. (i) **Acknowledged guilt** — Lev 4:20–35 "the offerer's hand-laying transfers identification with the animal" — the offerer must recognize and identify with the guilt being covered; Num 5:8 "wrong objective" acknowledged. (ii) **Sincere intent** — 2Ch 30:18 "trusting that sincere intent can be covered by God's goodness" — even amid ritual impurity, sincere intent enables atonement to operate. (iii) **Active seeking** — 2Sa 21:3 "the king seeks to know what atonement is required" — the inward orientation of pursuing the mechanism of cover. (iv) **Confession or named acknowledgement** — Lev 5:18 unintentional sin still requires the bringing of an offering with the named act. (v) **Humility** — Lev 16:30 the cleansing happens in the context of the Day of Atonement's solemn assembly; the posture is humbled, not casual. (vi) **Faith in the divinely-authorized mechanism** — Lev 17:11 receiving the blood-as-life-for-life logic; rejection of self-supplied substitutes is implicit (Isa 28:18 false covering exposed). The enabling conditions cluster around acknowledged guilt + humble seeking + faith in the prescribed mechanism.

---

**T1.7.2 — Conditions of Reception — What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Reception of atonement is blocked, distorted, or prevented by inner conditions evidenced across VCG-08 and adjacent verses. (i) **Entrenched unrepentance** — 1Sa 3:14 "the house's iniquity is declared permanently beyond atonement by sacrifice — the guilt is so entrenched and unrepented that no ritual covering can reach it"; the inner condition is hardened persistence in the wrong. (ii) **Wilful self-deception** — Isa 28:18 (false-covenant covering exposed and annulled); the inner condition is the construction of a pseudo-cover the person trusts in place of the genuine. (iii) **Sustained moral failure exceeding the mechanism** — Isa 22:14 "guilt declared unforgivable until death — atonement is explicitly denied, marking the inner state as sealed against any possibility of restoration"; the inner condition is the willed perpetuation of the offending state. (iv) **Reverse-intercession (active prayer that atonement be denied)** — Jer 18:23 "deliberate prayer that atonement be denied, holding guilt permanently in divine sight"; the inner condition is the offender's malice having become so entrenched that even intercession recoils. (v) **Exhausted inner resources for moral resolution** — Isa 47:11 "the inability to atone signals that the inner resources for dealing with guilt are entirely exhausted"; the inner condition is moral collapse. Across these the common pattern is hardness/sealedness — atonement requires permeable inner ground to operate.

---

**T1.7.3 — Conditions of Reception — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The inner-being state of the non-receiving encounterer is structurally evidenced across VCG-08 — a state of **sealed, exposed guilt with no possibility of cover**. Isa 22:14 names the inner state as sealed against restoration; Isa 47:11 names it as helplessness-with-exhausted-resources; Jer 18:23 frames it as guilt held permanently in divine sight; Isa 28:18 frames it as exposed self-deception ("the atonement or covering people thought they secured is exposed as void, leaving them without protection"). The common phenomenology: the person remains in the presence of the offered (or potential) atonement but cannot or will not enter it. The inner state is therefore not absence-of-atonement (atonement was available or was offered) but **active separation from atonement** — a willed or hardened condition that excludes the very act that would address it. Crucially, the corpus does not name this state as a separate characteristic — it is **the closed state of atonement itself**, the structural opposite identified at T1.3.1. The person continues to bear the guilt that atonement would have covered, now with the additional weight of having refused or been excluded from cover.

**T1.8.1 — Dimension Classification — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The primary inner-being dimension of Atonement is **relational-volitional** (act-of-mediation directed toward restoring a relationship). The relational dimension dominates: every atoning act is structured around a relationship under strain (offender ↔ offended) and oriented toward restored standing. The volitional dimension supplies the active mode: atonement is something *done* — the priest, mediator, intercessor, or God *acts* (kip.per is overwhelmingly verbal). At the recipient end the dimension is **moral-conscience** (the inner state of guilt addressed) modulated by **standing-relational** (the covenant position restored). The corpus does not foreground the cognitive dimension prominently — atonement is not principally about knowing-better; nor the emotional dimension — atonement is not principally about feeling-better (Eze 16:63 explicitly produces *silenced* humility, not consolation). The fully-modal characterization: relational-volitional act-of-mediation operating on moral-conscience state to restore standing-relational position. Primary dimension: relational-volitional.

---

**T1.8.2 — Dimension Classification — What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The relational-volitional primary classification is supported by: (i) **Volitional verbiage:** kip.per occurs ~70x in M11-A as an active verb of doing — "to make atonement", "to atone for", "to cover" — overwhelmingly grammatically active (Lev 4:20–6:7 et passim). (ii) **Relational structure:** every atoning act has an offended party named — God most often, sometimes a wronged human (Gen 32:20; 2Sa 21:3). The act is *toward* someone; there is no atonement-in-the-abstract. (iii) **Mediator-recipient grammar:** the priest atones *for* the offerer (Lev 4:31 "the priest's atoning act covers the individual's sin"); the intercessor atones *for* the people (Exo 32:30). The relational triangulation (mediator–offender–offended) is constitutive. (iv) **Outcome restorative-relational:** the outcome is always "forgiven" / "clean" / "restored to standing" / "wrath averted" / "reconciled" — all relational-standing categories. (v) **Conscience-state addressed:** even where conscience-effects are named (Eze 16:63 humility, Lev 16:30 cleansing), they are downstream consequences of the prior relational-volitional act. The primary classification holds.

---

**T1.8.3 — Dimension Classification — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement carries clear secondary dimensions that complement rather than compete with the primary classification. **Secondary dimensions identified:** (i) **Moral-conscience** — atonement operates on guilt as a moral state (Lev 16:30 inner cleansing; Lev 19:22 sin acknowledged and covered); this is downstream of the relational-volitional act and therefore secondary rather than competing. (ii) **Embodied/ritual** — atonement involves bodily acts (blood, hand-laying, anointing, incense); these are mechanism-level features that serve the primary relational-volitional act. (iii) **Eschatological-temporal** — Dan 9:24 names atonement within a temporal framework (final cosmic resolution); this orients the primary act toward a future fullness without altering its essence. (iv) **Inner-character** — Pro 16:6 names atonement as flowing from inner dispositions of love + faithfulness; this elevates atonement from discrete-act-only to character-quality in derivative cases. **No competing dimension** — none of the secondary dimensions challenges the primary relational-volitional read; each operates within or downstream of it. The dimensional structure is therefore layered (volitional act + relational direction + moral-conscience target + embodied mechanism + eschatological orientation + character ground) rather than tensioned.
