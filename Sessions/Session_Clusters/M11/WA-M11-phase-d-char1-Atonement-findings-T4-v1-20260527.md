# M11 Phase D — Char 1 (Atonement) — T4 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-A Atonement (char_seq=1)
**Tier:** T4 — Relational Interfaces (24 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27
**Governing instruction:** [`wa-sessionb-cluster-instruction-v3_0-20260527.md`](../../../Workflow/Instructions/wa-sessionb-cluster-instruction-v3_0-20260527.md) §8
**Sub-group & VCG context:** [`WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md`](WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md) §M11-A

**Tier framing note:** T4 examines how the characteristic operates across relational interfaces — God↔human in both directions, human↔human (giving / receiving / boundaries), and the spiritual-beings interface. Atonement is constitutionally relational (T3.11), so most T4 prompts have rich M11-A evidence.

---

**T4.1.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — God-to-human operation is strongly evidenced across M11-A. (i) **Divine institution of the mechanism:** the entire Levitical sacrificial system is divinely commanded (Lev 16 DoA institution; Exo 29–30 priestly ordinances; Lev 17:11 blood-as-life-for-life logic divinely appointed) — God establishes the channel through which atonement reaches the human. (ii) **Divine direct atoning act:** Psa 65:3 "when overwhelming iniquities exceed the person's capacity to manage them, God himself acts to atone"; Psa 78:38 "divine compassion moves God to atone for iniquity and restrain wrath repeatedly"; Psa 79:9 "atonement for sin is pleaded as an act of divine glory"; Isa 6:7 "a divine touch removes guilt and atones for sin in a direct, transforming act"; Deu 32:43 "God's cleansing of his people's land is the culminating act of atonement at a national scale — it is a divine, not human, covering"; Eze 16:63 "atonement covering despite past unfaithfulness". (iii) **Christic divine-to-human:** Mat 27:50 and Joh 18:8 are the maximal God-to-human atonement — divine self-substitution. The God-to-human direction is the *originating* direction of atonement.

---

**T4.1.2 — Divine Interface — God to Human — What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — is it conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The basis is principally **covenantal-and-responsive**, with conditional and unconditional elements both evidenced. (i) **Covenantal:** the entire Levitical apparatus operates within the Sinai covenant — atonement is a provision *of the covenant*; God extends it because he has bound himself in covenant relationship (Lev 16:34 permanent statute; Exo 30:16 "remembrance maintained" — atonement maintains the covenant). (ii) **Responsive:** God's relenting and atoning often respond to intercession (Exo 32:14 after Moses pleads; Num 16:46–47 in response to Aaron's incense; Amo 7:3/6 after prophetic plea) or to human turning (Jon 3:10 — though this is M11-D, the principle applies). (iii) **Conditional:** Num 14:18, Jer 18:8/10 — divine relenting is conditioned on moral response in both directions. (iv) **Unconditional initiative (within covenant):** Psa 65:3 names God acting when human resources are exhausted — atonement extends even where the conditional element has failed; Psa 78:38 the divine compassion is repeated despite recurring failure. The composite picture: God extends atonement on a covenantal basis, responsively to intercession and turning, conditionally honoring the moral structure of the covenant, but with unconditional initiative when needed.

---

**T4.1.3 — Divine Interface — God to Human — What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — God's extension of atonement reveals a disposition of **patient, compassionate, restorative initiative** toward the human person. (i) **Patience:** Psa 78:38 "divine compassion moves God to atone for iniquity and restrain wrath repeatedly" — the repeated atoning act reveals patience-as-disposition; Neh 9:17 (M11-B) "ready to forgive" character. (ii) **Compassion:** Psa 78:38 explicit; Psa 79:9 atonement linked to divine glory and saving-character. (iii) **Restorative intention:** Lev 16:30 the goal is restoration to purity-before-God, not mere management of guilt; Eze 16:63 the eschatological covering produces sustained relationship. (iv) **Initiative:** Psa 65:3 the divine initiative when humans are exhausted; Deu 32:43 unilateral land-cleansing; Mat 27:50 self-giving initiative reaches the maximum. The disposition revealed is not aloof judgment but actively-engaged relational care. Pro 16:6 names the inner-character ground: God's hesed + emet (steadfast love + faithfulness) atone — the disposition that extends atonement is rooted in God's own covenant character.

---

**T4.1.4 — Divine Interface — God to Human — If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The evidence is not silent on God-to-human operation — it is densely evidenced (T4.1.1). The narrower silence to note: the corpus does not depict God-to-human atonement *bypassing* the mediator (priest or Christ). Even when God is the direct agent (Psa 65:3, Isa 6:7), the mediating mechanism (life-substitute, seraph-coal, eventually Christ) is present. The corpus does not evidence atonement-by-divine-fiat alone, without instrument. This silence is structurally informative — it suggests God's God-to-human operation through atonement is always through an instituted means, even when the institution-and-the-divine-act collapse in Christ.

---

**T4.2.1 — Divine Interface — Human to God — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Human-to-God operation is strongly evidenced in M11-A. (i) **Seeking through atonement:** 2Sa 21:3 "the king seeks to know what atonement is required to repair a broken relationship and restore blessing" — seeking-toward-God via discerning what atonement is required; Num 31:50 warriors' voluntary atonement-offering. (ii) **Supplication paired with atonement:** Exo 32:30 Moses rises to intercede and hopes the mediating act may cover; 2Ch 30:18 prayer for divine pardon paired with sincere intent. (iii) **Worship contextualized by atonement:** Lev 23:28 "all work must cease on the Day of Atonement because the entire day is devoted to the singular act of covering the people's guilt before God — human striving yields to divine covering" — atonement *constitutes* the worship-context; Lev 16:30 the DoA is the most-worship-saturated day. (iv) **Covenant maintenance:** Exo 30:16 atonement money "keeps the people in God's remembrance" — atonement is the human-side covenant maintenance act. The offerer's whole movement toward God is mediated by atonement.

---

**T4.2.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God — What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The required inner posture is **acknowledged guilt + humble submission to the divinely-given mechanism + earnest seeking**. (i) **Acknowledged guilt:** Lev 1:4 hand-laying identification with the substitute requires acknowledgment of one's guilt; Lev 5:18 unintentional sin — even ignorance-of-fact requires acknowledgment-when-discovered. (ii) **Humble submission:** Lev 23:28 cessation of all human striving on the DoA — the posture is yielding rather than striving; Lev 16:30 the recipient receives cleansing, does not produce it. (iii) **Earnest seeking:** 2Sa 21:3 the king's active searching; Exo 32:30 Moses's rising-to-intercede; Num 31:50 the warriors' voluntary action prompted by inner-conviction. (iv) **Faith in the prescribed mechanism:** Lev 17:11 the offerer must trust that blood-as-life-for-life operates as God says; rejection of self-supplied substitutes (Isa 28:18 false covering exposed). (v) **Confession-as-implicit:** Lev 5:5–6 (adjacent context) and the corpus pattern requires confession when guilt becomes known. The posture cluster is: acknowledged-guilt + humble-faith + active-seeking.

---

**T4.2.3 — Divine Interface — Human to God — What does the human-to-God direction of this characteristic reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The human-to-God direction of atonement reveals the relationship as **bondedly-corrigible** — bound by covenant, with corrigibility built in. The very existence of atonement-provision shows that the relationship is not "perfect-or-broken-forever" but "bound with built-in repair." Lev 16:34 (annual statute) names this as permanent provision — the relationship is structured to accommodate recurring failure and recurring restoration. Eze 16:63 names the post-atonement state of confounded humility — the person's relationship with God carries memory of having been covered, which deepens rather than threatens the bond. 2Sa 21:3's king-seeking-atonement shows that the relational orientation is responsibility-bearing — when the relationship is strained, the person actively seeks repair rather than evading or denying. Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness atone) names the maturest form: the inner character becomes itself relationally-restorative; the person's relationship with God shapes them into one whose own dispositions function in the atoning mode. The relationship revealed is intimate, structured, durable, and progressive.

---

**T4.2.4 — Divine Interface — Human to God — If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The evidence is not silent on human-to-God operation (T4.2.1–T4.2.3). The narrower silence worth noting: the corpus does not depict the offerer atoning *for* God toward themselves — the directionality is always *toward* the offended party (God), not *from* God toward the offerer-as-offended-party. This is theologically significant — the corpus structurally rejects any reading of atonement as God needing to be atoned-toward by humans for divine fault; the offense is always on the human side, and the atoning move is from the offender (human) toward the offended (God), or initiated by God toward humans where human resources fail.

---

**T4.3.1 — Human Interface — Giving — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another — and if so, how does it operate in that giving?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement is extended human-to-human in several modes. (i) **Mediator-to-recipient:** the priest atones for the offerer (Lev 4:20 "the congregation receives divine forgiveness — the inner burden of guilt is lifted"; Lev 4:31 "the priest's atoning act covers the individual's sin"); the priest's atoning service is itself a giving-of-atonement-to-the-people. (ii) **Intercessor-for-people:** Exo 32:30 Moses for Israel; Num 16:46–47 Aaron for the plague-stricken assembly; Phinehas's zeal-act for the people (Num 25:13). (iii) **Wronging-party to wronged:** Gen 32:20 Jacob's gift to Esau ("a gift sent ahead may soften the other's inner disposition and secure acceptance"); 2Sa 21:3 the king toward the wronged tribe; Pro 16:14 the wise person defusing royal wrath. (iv) **Interpersonal restoration:** Mat 5:24 (in M11-C-VCG-04 but evidencing the principle) "leaving the gift at the altar to be reconciled first." (v) **Pro 16:6 inner-character extension:** love + faithfulness as inner-character qualities exercised *toward another* atone — extending the disposition is itself the giving.

---

**T4.3.2 — Human Interface — Giving — What inner conditions or orientations in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Inner conditions enabling genuine human-to-human atonement-extension cluster around four orientations. (i) **Acknowledgment of the breach:** Gen 32:20 Jacob acknowledges Esau's prior wronging; 2Sa 21:3 the king acknowledges Saul's prior wrong; the offering presupposes recognized breach. (ii) **Hesed-emet character:** Pro 16:6 names the inner moral character (steadfast love + faithfulness) as the disposition that atones — a settled-toward-the-other quality. (iii) **Personal cost willingness:** Gen 32:20 the calculated gift requires significant material sacrifice; the priestly atoning act requires Lev 16:17 solitude and weight-bearing; Mat 27:50 maximal cost (Christ). (iv) **Self-prior-cleansing:** Lev 9:7 "the high priest is commanded to offer atonement first for himself and then for the people, showing that the mediator's own inner guilt must be resolved before he can cover others' guilt"; Lev 16:6 (DoA self-atonement first). The mediator must themselves be properly-oriented to genuinely give atonement. (v) **Wisdom-of-relational-perception:** Pro 16:14 the wise person reads the relational moment correctly.

---

**T4.3.3 — Human Interface — Giving — What does the evidence reveal about what the person must have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence reveals a clear precedence: **the person must have themselves been atoned-for before they can genuinely give atonement to others**. (i) **Priestly precedence:** Lev 9:7 the high priest atones for himself first, then for the people — self-cleansing precedes mediation; Lev 16:6 (DoA opening) the priest atones for his own guilt and household before entering the holiest space; Lev 8:34 the ordination cycle includes daily self-atonement before the priestly office is fully constituted. (ii) **Character precedence:** Pro 16:6 the inner character of hesed + emet that atones must have been formed in the giver — character-as-atoning-disposition presupposes character-formation upstream. (iii) **Christic logic:** Christ's atoning surrender (Mat 27:50) is *capable* of atoning for others because of who Christ is — the precedence here is incarnational rather than ritual, but the structural principle (mediator must be qualified to mediate) holds. (iv) **Wisdom precedence:** Pro 16:14 the wise person who atones via prudence has presumably been formed in wisdom prior to the moment of relational defusing. The pattern: self-receiving precedes other-giving; character-formation precedes character-extending.

---

**T4.3.4 — Human Interface — Giving — If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Evidence on the giving direction is rich (T4.3.1–T4.3.3); no global silence. Narrower silence: M11-A's giving-direction evidence is mostly *priestly-mediated giving* (priest atones for offerer) and *interpersonal compensatory giving* (Jacob to Esau). The corpus is relatively quieter on **giver-as-equal-peer** atoning extension — though Mat 5:24 (reconciliation between brothers, M11-C-VCG-04) gestures in this direction. This silence is partly because M11-A is the *mechanism* register and the peer-level mutual forgiveness/release operates more prominently in M11-B (forgiveness) and M11-C (release). The relative silence is structurally appropriate; not all the cluster's relational-giving work needs to live inside M11-A.

**T4.4.1 — Human Interface — Receiving — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another — and if so, how does it operate in that reception?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Reception is rich in M11-A. (i) **Recipient of priestly atonement:** Lev 4:20 the congregation receives forgiveness through priestly mediation; Lev 4:31, 5:10, 5:13, 5:16, 5:18, 6:7, 7:7, 19:22 the same pattern repeated — the offerer receives the mediated act and is declared forgiven. (ii) **Recipient of crisis-atonement:** Num 16:47 the dying assembly receives Aaron's interposing atonement; the rescue from plague is reception of the atoning act. (iii) **Recipient of inner-character atonement:** Eze 16:63 the recipient of divine cover responds with humility — the reception produces the inner state; Gen 32:20 Esau receives Jacob's gift (the corpus shows the gift sent; the response is in narrative context). (iv) **Recipient of Christic atonement:** Mat 27:50 + Joh 18:8 the disciples and the world receive what Christ enacts. The receiving direction operates through acknowledgment + humble acceptance + post-reception transformation (humility / cleansed standing / re-entry into community).

---

**T4.4.2 — Human Interface — Receiving — What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Reception is enabled and blocked by the same inner conditions identified at T1.7. **Enabling:** (i) acknowledged guilt (Lev 4:35 explicit; Lev 1:4 hand-laying as identification); (ii) humble submission to the prescribed mechanism (Lev 23:28 work-cease posture; Lev 17:11 faith in life-for-life logic); (iii) sincere intent even amid impurity (2Ch 30:18 "sincere intent can be covered by God's goodness"). **Blocking:** (i) entrenched unrepentance (1Sa 3:14); (ii) wilful self-deception with self-supplied cover (Isa 28:18); (iii) sustained hardness (Isa 22:14 sealed-state); (iv) exhausted moral resources (Isa 47:11 — inability arises from inner collapse). **Specific to inter-personal context** (Gen 32:20 Esau-receiving-Jacob's-gift): the receiver must read the giver's overture as genuine — refusal can occur via misread-intent. **Pro 16:14** (the king's wrath being defused): the receiver's anger must yield to the wise overture — if the receiver's wrath is sealed, even wise mediation fails. The enabling-vs-blocking conditions are consistent with the divine-interface pattern but with additional inter-personal-perception variables.

---

**T4.4.3 — Human Interface — Receiving — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic from another but does not receive it?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Per T1.7.3 the non-receiving state is **sealed, exposed guilt with no possibility of cover**. The M11-A negative pole (VCG-08) names this explicitly across five verses (1Sa 3:14; Isa 22:14; Isa 28:18; Isa 47:11; Jer 18:23). The person encounters the atoning provision (it is offered, available, or extended) but the inner posture excludes operative reception. The inner state has these features: (i) hardened persistence in the wronging state (1Sa 3:14 "entrenched and unrepented"); (ii) wilful self-deception with a false-cover the person trusts in place of the genuine (Isa 28:18); (iii) sealed against restoration (Isa 22:14 "the inner state as sealed against any possibility of restoration"); (iv) inner resources exhausted (Isa 47:11 "the inner resources for dealing with guilt are entirely exhausted"); (v) malice so entrenched that even intercession recoils (Jer 18:23 the prophet prays *that* atonement be denied). The state is structurally the same as the divine-interface non-reception state — atonement requires permeable inner ground in either direction.

---

**T4.4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving — If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Evidence on the receiving direction is rich (T4.4.1–T4.4.3); no notable silence. The corpus engages the receiving direction at every interface (priestly-mediated, crisis-mediated, interpersonal, divine-direct, Christic).

**T4.5.1 — Human Interface — Boundaries — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance or difference?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — operation differs subtly across these conditions. **Within existing bonds (covenant community):** the standard Levitical pattern operates within the established Sinai covenant (Lev 4–7 for Israelites; Num 15:25 communal atonement); the priestly mediation presupposes the priest's covenantal office; the offerer's inclusion is presupposed by their covenant membership. **Across relational distance/difference:** Num 15:26 "forgiveness extends to every person in the community, *including resident foreigners*, because the mistake was shared — collective inadvertent guilt brings collective forgiveness" — the boundary is widened to include the ger; Deu 32:43 the divine cleansing of land includes the nations in some readings. **Across breached bonds:** Gen 32:20 (Jacob–Esau) is atonement across a previously-broken bond; 2Sa 21:3 across a previously-violated covenant with a tribe. **Christic universalising:** Mat 27:50 + the M11-E reconciliation passages (Col 1:20 cosmic) suggest the bond-boundary is dissolved at the maximal scale. The pattern: atonement operates within established bonds as standard, extends across previously-breached bonds as repair, and universalises in the christological case.

---

**T4.5.2 — Human Interface — Boundaries — Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement primarily operates within covenantal contexts but evidences boundary-crossing in specific cases. (i) **Covenantal primary:** Lev 16:34 the perpetual statute *of the covenant*; Lev 17:11 the divinely-appointed means *for the covenant people*; Exo 30:16 atonement *keeping* the people in remembrance — the covenant frame is structural. (ii) **Covenantal-boundary inclusive of resident foreigners:** Num 15:26 (cited at T4.5.1) explicitly extends the communal forgiveness to ger — the boundary is not ethnic. (iii) **Boundary-crossing in interpersonal cases:** Gen 32:20 (Jacob–Esau, who is *not* in the Israelite covenant at this point); Pro 16:14 (the wise person and a king who is not necessarily named in covenant context). (iv) **Cosmic-Christic boundary-crossing:** Dan 9:24's eschatological atonement; Mat 27:50's universal-scope death; the M11-E reconciliation passages extend the cosmic scope (Col 1:20 "all things reconciled"). The dominant operation is within covenant; the crossings show the characteristic's principled non-ethnic extensibility. The covenant frame supplies the institutional form; the moral-relational content carries beyond ethnic boundaries.

---

**T4.5.3 — Human Interface — Boundaries — What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic — who is included and who is not?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence reveals atonement's relational scope as **wide-with-conditional-exclusion** rather than narrow. **Included:** the covenant community (Lev 4–7 corpus); resident foreigners (Num 15:26 ger); strangers receiving relational repair (Gen 32:20); the wronged tribe (2Sa 21:3); the universal-cosmic via Christ (Mat 27:50 → Col 1:20). **Conditionally excluded:** the entrenched-unrepentant (1Sa 3:14 — exclusion is by the entrenched person's own state); the wilfully self-deceived (Isa 28:18; Deu 29:20 noted in M11-B); the sealed-against-restoration (Isa 22:14); the explicit-malice case (Jer 18:23). The exclusion pattern is consistent: **inclusion is the default; exclusion is by inner-state-sealing**, not by ethnic/covenantal-boundary. This contrasts with what might be expected from a narrowly-ritualist reading — the corpus does not treat atonement as a privilege of insiders that excludes outsiders. The structural opposite of inclusion is not outsiderness but **closed inner state**.

---

**T4.5.4 — Human Interface — Boundaries — If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Evidence on relational boundaries is sufficient (T4.5.1–T4.5.3); no notable silence. The corpus surfaces enough cases (covenant insiders, ger inclusion, interpersonal cross-boundary, Christic universal) to establish the scope pattern.

**T4.6.1 — Spiritual Beings Interface — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial — and if so, how?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus is largely silent on the spiritual-beings interface. The closest evidence is indirect: (i) **Wrath-averting in plague contexts** (Num 16:46–47, M11-A-VCG-05) — the "destroying" is divine judgment expressed perhaps through an angelic agent (the destroying angel motif elsewhere) but the corpus does not name spiritual beings in M11-A; (ii) **Christic context** (Mat 27:50, M11-A-VCG-10) — the broader NT context has spiritual-beings significance (e.g. Col 1:20 cosmic reconciliation extends to "things in heaven"), but within the M11-A verse evidence itself the spiritual-beings frame is not directly engaged. Atonement is depicted as God-human-mediator-and-recipient relationships; spiritual beings are not the relational partners. The silence is structurally consistent with atonement being a human-side moral-relational repair, with God as the offended party — angelic and adversarial beings do not figure in the principal relational triangulation.

---

**T4.6.2 — Spiritual Beings Interface — Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity — something that can be attacked, distorted, or weaponised by adversarial spiritual powers?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus does not directly evidence atonement as a site of adversarial-spiritual attack or distortion. Within the verse evidence, the threats to atonement are inner-human (entrenched unrepentance, wilful self-deception, sealed hardness — VCG-08) rather than spiritually-external. The negative pole verses do not name adversarial agency producing the sealed states. **Indirect possibilities** (not directly evidenced in M11-A): Isa 28:18's false-covenant-covering could be read as adversarially-influenced self-deception (the prophet's denouncement targets people who have made covenants with death and Sheol), but the verse evidence focuses on the false-covering being human construction. The silence here is itself meaningful — the corpus treats atonement's principal threats as moral-internal rather than spiritual-external. This may suggest that adversarial distortion of atonement, where it occurs in other parts of Scripture, would be by way of cultivating the inner sealed-states that VCG-08 names.

---

**T4.6.3 — Spiritual Beings Interface — Is this characteristic communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry in the evidence?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus does not evidence angelic mediation of atonement. The mediators named are: priests (Levitical corpus), intercessors (Moses, Aaron, Phinehas, prophets), God himself (Psa 65:3, Isa 6:7 — though Isa 6:7's seraph could be read as angelic mediation of the divine touch, the seraph is more closely depicted as divine-presence-associated than as independent angelic agent), and Christ (Mat 27:50). The pattern is: atonement is mediated by humans-set-apart, by God directly, or by Christ; angelic ministry is not depicted as the mediating channel. This is structurally consistent with atonement being a human-side moral-relational repair toward God — the mediating role belongs to the human-side or to God's own initiative, not to other spiritual beings.

---

**T4.6.4 — Spiritual Beings Interface — If the evidence is silent on the spiritual beings interface, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus is substantially silent on the spiritual-beings interface (T4.6.1–T4.6.3). The silence is structurally meaningful: atonement's relational frame is God–human–mediator, not God–human–angelic-being or human–adversarial-being. Where adversarial or angelic dimensions appear in adjacent Scripture (e.g. Heb 1–2 angelic ministry; the Christological-cosmic reconciliation in Col 1:20), they extend M11-A rather than operate within it. The M11-A primary frame is moral-relational human-divine repair.
