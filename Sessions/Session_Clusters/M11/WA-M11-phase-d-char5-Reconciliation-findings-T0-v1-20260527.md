# M11 Phase D — Char 5 (Reconciliation) — T0 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-E Reconciliation (char_seq=5)
**Tier:** T0 — Divine Image (12 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27
**Sub-group & VCG context:** [`WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md`](WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md) §M11-E (9 verses across 4 VCGs)

**Characteristic framing:** Reconciliation in M11-E is the *relational outcome* of the cluster's redemptive arc — restored right relationship between previously-estranged parties. The corpus base is small (9V) but theologically pivotal: Pauline corpus (Rom 5, 11; 2Cor 5; Eph 2; Col 1) and 1Cor 7:11 (marital). All Greek; no OT base in this sub-group.

---

**T0.1.1 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-5]** E — The M11-E corpus reveals divine character as **initiator-and-completer of relational restoration**. 2Cor 5:18 names the structure directly: "God initiates reconciliation through Christ, drawing humanity out of alienation into restored relationship — and then entrusts participants with the ongoing ministry of that reconciling work." 2Cor 5:19 extends: "God's reconciling of the world involves not counting trespasses against people — the inner account of transgression is suspended as the basis for restored standing." Col 1:20 cosmic-scope: "through the blood of the cross, all things are to be reconciled to God — a cosmic restoration of right relationship between creation and Creator, healing universal alienation." The character revealed: God acts to restore the broken-relationship before the alienated party can; restoration is divinely-initiated and divinely-effected.

---

**T0.1.2 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation is explicitly attributed to God's act-through-Christ. 2Cor 5:18 "God reconciled us to himself through Christ." Col 1:20 "through the blood of the cross... all things... reconciled to God." Eph 2:16 "the cross is the means by which deep relational hostility is killed." Rom 5:10 "while still enemies, the inner posture of enmity toward God was overcome by Christ's death." **Significance for the human person:** the attribution establishes (a) reconciliation is real and divinely-grounded; (b) the human enmity was overcome by divine initiative, not by human merit-or-effort; (c) the human person can now stand in restored standing as covenantal reality.

---

**T0.1.3 Divine Nature Reflected**
**[CHAR-5]** S — The narrower silence: M11-E is NT-only; the OT corpus does not have the same explicit reconciliation-vocabulary. Hebrew shalom-relating and katallasso-cognate concepts exist in OT theology but are not in the M11-E verse evidence directly. The silence is structural — the reconciliation-vocabulary explicitly developed in the NT carries the theological category, with OT roots in covenant-restoration imagery.

---

**T0.2.1 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation equips the person to **be brought-back-into-right-relationship with God and others**. Col 1:22 names the equipping-purpose directly: "Reconciliation through Christ's bodily death aims at presenting the person holy, blameless, and above reproach — the inner and moral transformation is the purpose of restored standing." The created purpose has two layers: the relational state of restored-standing, and the inner-moral transformation that flows from it (holiness, blamelessness, above-reproach quality). 2Cor 5:20 names the inner equipping: the will is summoned to abandon enmity and receive the restored relationship being offered.

---

**T0.2.2 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation is **response-to-fallen-condition** — every M11-E verse presupposes alienation / enmity / estrangement that requires repair. No M11-E verse depicts reconciliation as a pre-fall design exercise. However, the *capacity for relationship* is original; what changes after fall is the need for the specific repair-mechanism that reconciliation supplies.

---

**T0.2.3 Created Purpose**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Strong eschatological orientation. Col 1:20 names cosmic reconciliation as the eschatological-completion outcome ("all things... in heaven and on earth"). The "ministry of reconciliation" (2Cor 5:18) operates in the present-age toward this eschatological-fullness. The Pauline-cosmic scope of M11-E is structurally eschatological — reconciliation's full operation is *toward* the new creation.

---

**T0.3.1 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation expresses the divine image principally through the capacity to participate in relational repair (Mat 5:24 cross-evidence in M11-C; the broader gospel-ministry pattern). Col 1:22 names the inner-moral outcome — the person presented holy / blameless / above-reproach is image-restored. 2Cor 5:20 names the ambassadorial role: humans participate as image-bearers in extending God's reconciling work.

---

**T0.3.2 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Shared with structural asymmetry. (i) Divine-initiating-reconciliation (2Cor 5:18 God initiates) is exclusive to God in source. (ii) Human-participating-in-ministry-of-reconciliation (2Cor 5:18 entrusted) is delegated participation — humans extend what God has originated. (iii) Interpersonal-reconciliation (1Cor 7:11 marital; Mat 5:24 brother) is principally creaturely but reflects the divine pattern. (iv) Christic-incarnation collapses the asymmetry maximally — Christ is the originator and human-participant simultaneously.

---

**T0.3.3 Image-Bearer Expression**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Presence: the reconciled person presented holy/blameless (Col 1:22) shows the image restored. The reconciler-as-minister (2Cor 5:18–20) shows image-extension. Absence: enmity-maintained (Rom 5:10 "while still enemies"); refusal to be reconciled (the implicit negative pole — 2Cor 5:20 "be reconciled to God" implies some don't accept the appeal); active hostility (Eph 2 pre-cross hostility). Mat 18:35-style refusal-to-extend would be image-failure.

---

**T0.4.1 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Strongly typological. (i) **Christological:** the cross is the typological-fulfillment-event for all OT covenant-restoration patterns (Eph 2:16 cross-as-mechanism; Col 1:20 blood-of-the-cross). (ii) **Eschatological:** Col 1:20 cosmic scope ("things in heaven, things on earth") is typologically-pointing toward final-comprehensive restoration. (iii) **Israel-Gentile-relational:** Rom 11:15 "the rejection of one people paradoxically becomes the very mechanism through which the world is brought into reconciliation" — typological-structure of how reconciliation extends.

---

**T0.4.2 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-5]** E — Divine establishes pattern; human ministry extends it. 2Cor 5:18 the structural order: God reconciles → entrusts ministry → ambassadors appeal. The pattern flows divinely-downward. Human-participation reflects-and-extends.

---

**T0.4.3 Typological Significance**
**[CHAR-5]** S — Typological use is explicit (T0.4.1). Catalogue fail-safe.
