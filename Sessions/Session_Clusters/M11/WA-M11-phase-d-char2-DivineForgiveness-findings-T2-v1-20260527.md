# M11 Phase D — Char 2 (Divine forgiveness) — T2 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-B Divine forgiveness (char_seq=2)
**Tier:** T2 — Constitutional Location (31 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27

**Tier framing:** Like M11-A, M11-B is principally an act-with-relational-outcome rather than a faculty resident in a person. Constitutional location findings split between (a) divine-agent location (where the disposition resides in God), (b) recipient-state location (where forgiveness lands inwardly in the offender), and (c) human-extender location (where the imitating capacity sits in the human-forgiving-others).

---

**T2.1.1 — Spirit-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Spirit-level location is evidenced in two registers. (i) **Divine Spirit-sourced authority** — the unforgivable-sin texts (Mat 12:31–32, Luk 12:10) name the Spirit explicitly as the locus whose rejection seals against forgiveness; this implies that operative divine forgiveness extends *via the Spirit* in some constitutive sense. (ii) **Spirit-cleansed conscience trajectory** — though M11-B doesn't articulate this directly, the broader NT pattern (Heb 9:14 conscience cleansed by the eternal Spirit) implies spirit-level forgiveness-application. Within M11-B itself, the spirit-language is most explicit in the negative pole (blasphemy-against-Spirit unforgivable) rather than the positive pole.

---

**T2.1.2 — Spirit-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The corpus does not directly indicate that forgiveness *originates* at the spirit level. It originates in divine character (Psa 86:5) and operates through relational acts. The spirit-level appears principally at the negative limit (Spirit-rejection forecloses pardon) rather than at the positive origin.

---

**T2.1.3 — Spirit-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Where spirit-level is engaged (Mat 12:31–32), the engagement reveals forgiveness's depth — the unforgivable is whatever rejects the Spirit. This suggests divine forgiveness ultimately operates at the spirit-level of contact between divine Spirit and human spirit; rupture there is uniquely terminal.

---

**T2.1.4 — Spirit-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The OT corpus (sa.lach, sal.lach, se.li.chah) does not explicitly use spirit-level language for divine forgiveness; it operates at the relational-act / standing-state level. The NT introduces the Spirit-limit language. The OT silence here is consistent with the developmental arc — the Spirit's role in forgiveness is more articulated in the NT.

---

**T2.2.1 — Soul-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Soul-level location: forgiveness *of the soul-bearing-guilt*. While M11-B doesn't have the explicit "soul" anchor that M11-A has (Lev 17:11), the soul-level operation is implicit in the depth-language: Lev 4:20 "the inner burden of guilt is lifted" — the lifting operates at the inner-being level; Jer 31:34 "remember sin no more" — divine memory's relationship to the soul-record is shifted; 1Jo 2:12 already-forgiven assurance grounds inner-identity at soul-level.

---

**T2.2.2 — Soul-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Soul-level reveals forgiveness as operating on the deepest seat of guilt and identity. The soul that bore the guilt is now released; the soul's identity is constituted around forgiven-ness (1Jo 2:12). At the eschatological extreme (Jer 31:34) divine memory itself no longer holds the soul against its past — the soul-level standing is total and final.

---

**T2.2.3 — Soul-Level Location**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Soul-language is not explicitly anchored in the M11-B corpus the way Lev 17:11 anchors M11-A. The soul-level operation is inferred from depth and identity language, not from explicit *nephesh* / *psychē* anchoring.

---

**T2.3.1 — Heart**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Heart-level location is strongly evidenced. (i) **Heart as seat of forgiving-disposition:** Mat 18:35 "Forgiveness must arise from the heart — not merely as formal release but as a genuine inner disposition"; Mar 11:25 "releasing grievances against others is the inner condition... heart open"; Mat 6:12/14/15 heart-forgiveness binds the reciprocal arc. (ii) **Heart as seat of divine inner-disposition:** 1Ki 8:39 "God's heart-knowledge enables just pardon"; 2Ch 6:30 the divine heart-knowing of each person's inner motive enables discerning forgiveness. (iii) **Heart of the receiver:** Mat 9:2 "take heart" — heart-courage is restored by forgiveness; Luk 7:47 great love proceeds from the heart-of-the-much-forgiven. The heart is M11-B's principal soul-faculty engagement.

---

**T2.3.2 — Heart**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Heart engages **willing** (the act of releasing), **moral awareness** (the inner perception that the offense is being released-from), and **feeling** (love-as-gratitude in response). Cognitive grasp is secondary. The heart's integrating function is engaged at the forgiver-end (the disposition to forgive) and the recipient-end (the felt response).

---

**T2.3.3 — Heart**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Heart-engagement is dense (T2.3.1–T2.3.2); no significant silence.

---

**T2.4.1 — Mind**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Mind-level engagement is moderate. (i) **Cognitive recognition of forgiveness received** — Luk 1:77 "Salvation is specifically defined as knowing that sins are forgiven; forgiveness is the cognitive and experiential heart of what salvation means." (ii) **Discerning recognition of guilt** — Act 8:22 "the intent of the heart is named as what requires forgiveness — the inner motive of wickedness is the specific object of the release being sought." (iii) **Memory engagement** — Jer 31:34 "remember sin no more" — divine memory operates at mind-level (the cognitive-memorial register). (iv) **Recognition of the limit** — Mat 12:31–32 the cognitive grasp of the blasphemy-against-Spirit limit involves mind-engagement.

---

**T2.4.2 — Mind**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Mind engages **understanding** (grasping what forgiveness is and is for, Luk 1:77), **discernment** (recognizing the specific object of guilt, Act 8:22), and **memory** (the divine memory's posture toward the sin, Jer 31:34). Cognitive thought is engaged but not as the primary seat; heart-disposition is primary.

---

**T2.4.3 — Mind**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Mind-engagement is evidenced (T2.4.1); the OT Levitical-corpus is somewhat silent on cognitive-engagement vs the heart / conscience engagement that dominates.

---

**T2.5.1 — Other Soul Subsets**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Additional soul-subsets engaged: (i) **Conscience** — the recipient's conscience is cleared (similar pattern to M11-A but operating at the standing-declared level rather than the guilt-covered level); 1Jo 1:9 "confession activating... conscience cleansed." (ii) **Will** — the will to confess (1Jo 1:9), the will to forgive others (Mat 18:35), the will to receive (Mat 9:2). (iii) **Memory** — divine memory (Jer 31:34, 50:20); human memory of forgiveness received (Luk 7:47 prompting love). (iv) **Identity** — 1Jo 2:12 forgiven-identity. Forgiveness is whole-soul like atonement, but the dominant soul-subset is heart-disposition (vs M11-A's conscience-clearing as dominant).

---

**T2.5.2 — Other Soul Subsets**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The additional locations reveal divine forgiveness as a whole-soul operation focused on the relational-identity level. Conscience cleared + will engaged + memory recalibrated + identity grounded together produce the forgiven-state. The pattern shows forgiveness as more identity-shaping than M11-A (which is more standing-restoring); the forgiven person is reconstituted in identity around being-forgiven.

---

**T2.5.3 — Other Soul Subsets**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Evidence is present (T2.5.1–T2.5.2); no significant silence.

---

**T2.6.1 — Body — Significance**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The M11-B corpus is largely silent on body-part links. The OT sa.lach corpus operates at the standing-and-relational level without invoking body-parts (in contrast to M11-A's blood / hand / head / head-anointing density). The NT afiēmi-forgive verses similarly operate at the inner-relational level. **Limited evidence:** Mat 9:6 the healing of the paralytic alongside forgiveness uses the body as a sign of the spoken-forgiveness — but the body-link is *demonstrative* of forgiveness's reality, not constitutive. M11-B is dramatically less embodied than M11-A.

---

**T2.6.2 — Body — Significance**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Where body-link appears (Mat 9:5–6, Mar 2:9–10), the function is **demonstrative-indicative** — the visible bodily healing demonstrates the invisible inner forgiveness is real. The body is not the seat or mechanism but the sign-validation. This functional role contrasts sharply with M11-A's functional-mediating body engagement.

---

**T2.6.3 — Body — Significance**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The corpus is largely silent on body-engagement for divine forgiveness directly. This silence is structurally meaningful: M11-B operates principally at the inner-relational-standing level rather than the embodied-ritual level. M11-A and M11-B together cover the body-mediated mechanism (M11-A) and the inner-relational outcome (M11-B); the cluster's architecture is well-designed.

---

**T2.7.1 — Body — Direction**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Where any body-engagement appears (Mat 9:5–6), the direction is *inner-forgiveness expressed through visible-healing as sign* — soul-to-body, not body-to-soul. This inverts M11-A's body-to-soul primary direction.

---

**T2.7.2 — Body — Direction**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The directional contrast with M11-A (body-to-soul vs soul-to-body) is structurally informative: M11-A operates *through* the body to address the soul; M11-B operates *from* the soul / divine-disposition outward, with body-engagement (where present) serving as evidence-of-the-inner-act rather than the mechanism. This confirms the cluster architecture: M11-A is the mechanism register, M11-B is the disposition / declaration register.

---

**T2.7.3 — Body — Direction**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Body-direction evidence is limited; the dominant operation is inner-relational without body-mediation.

---

**T2.8.1 — Body — Deposit**
**[CHAR-2]** S — No constitutional bodily/DNA deposit from sustained divine forgiveness is evidenced. The corpus operates at the standing / identity / relational levels. Generational consequence at the negative-pole IS evidenced (2Ki 24:4 innocent blood unforgivable extending across generations; Deu 29:20 wilful self-deception generational consequence) but as exclusion-pattern, not as positive-deposit.

---

**T2.8.2 — Body — Deposit**
**[CHAR-2]** S — No positive bodily deposit. The recurrent OT pattern (Lev 4–7; Solomon temple-prayer corpus assuming ongoing failure-and-forgiveness need) supports the no-deposit reading — if forgiveness produced a deposit, the recurrence wouldn't be needed. NT new-covenant total-forgiveness (Jer 31:34; Heb 10:14) effects something irreversible at the conscience-and-relational level but is not characterized as bodily-deposit.

---

**T2.8.3 — Body — Deposit**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Feeds T5.7: no bodily deposit; sustained effect is at identity / character / relational levels.

---

**T2.9.1 — Origin and Source**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Divine forgiveness originates in **God's character** (Psa 86:5 forgiving-as-character; Dan 9:9 forgiveness belongs to God's nature). For the human person, it is **received from God** (the dominant direction) and **extended to others as imaging of the divine source** (Mat 6:12, 18:21–35). At no point does the human originate forgiveness from autonomous self-resource — even when humans forgive humans, the corpus frames this as participation in the divine-character (Mat 6:14–15 reciprocal binding). The deepest source is divine character; the proximate operational sources are divine acts, prayer-responses, and human extensions-as-imitations.

---

**T2.9.2 — Origin and Source**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Origin is structurally singular (divine character) with multiple operational channels (direct pronouncement; intercession-mediated; reciprocal-conditional; new-covenant-total). The singular character-source unifies the channels; the multiple channels accommodate the varied human contexts of forgiveness-need.

---

**T2.9.3 — Origin and Source**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Deep origin (divine character) is constant; proximate operational origin varies. The shift across contexts mirrors M11-A's pattern — from institutional (Levitical priestly-mediation), through covenantal (Solomon temple), to christological (Mat 9:6 Son-of-Man earthly authority), to new-covenantal (Jer 31:34 total), to delegated-human (Mat 6:12, Joh 20:23). The variation is in form, not source.

---

**T2.10.1 — Constitutional Movement**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Constitutional movement: **divine-character → declared act → recipient-standing → recipient-identity / disposition outward to others**. (i) From God's inner disposition (Psa 86:5) → (ii) extended in act (Luk 7:48 declaration) → (iii) recipient's standing-released (Rom 4:7 blessedness) → (iv) recipient's inner-identity reconstituted (1Jo 2:12) → (v) recipient extending forgiveness outward (Mat 18:21–35 imitative-application). The movement is principally inner-to-inner / spirit-to-spirit / heart-to-heart, with the visible standing-shift as a middle stage.

---

**T2.10.2 — Constitutional Movement**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The pattern: divine-character-source → relational-act-of-extending → recipient-standing-shift → recipient-identity-reconstitution → outward-extension-to-others. Each stage is evidenced and the sequence is consistent. Variation occurs in compression (sometimes stages are collapsed in narrative depiction) and in the recipient's extension-to-others outcome (M11-B-VCG-02 reciprocal explicitly names this; in other cases the corpus stops at recipient-identity without articulating the further extension).

---

**T2.10.3 — Constitutional Movement**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Constitutional movement is evidenced; no significant silence.
