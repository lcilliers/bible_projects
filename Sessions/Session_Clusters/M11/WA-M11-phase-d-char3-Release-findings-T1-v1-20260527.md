# M11 Phase D — Char 3 (Release) — T1 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-C Release (broader sense) (char_seq=3) — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T1.1.1 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Named **Release** in the programme — broader-sense than M11-B Divine forgiveness. The name signals (a) the act of letting-go / cancelling / freeing; (b) the relational outcome of unencumbered standing. Across the corpus, *release* operates in registers: release-of-sin (M11-primary VCG-01); release-of-attachment (VCG-03 discipleship); release-of-grief (VCG-10 eschatological). The name accurately captures the let-go semantic core while preserving the polymorphic application.

---

**T1.1.2 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-3]** E — **Greek primary:** G0859 *afesis* (noun, 16V) — "release, remission, sending-away"; G0863 *afiēmi* (verb, three sense-rows: G0863G leave 32V, G0863I permit 16V); G1813 *exaleifō* (verb, 4V) — "to wipe out, erase, blot." The afiēmi family carries the let-go core; exaleifō adds the radical-erasure register. Root revelation: release is *separation-letting-go* (afiēmi = apo + hiēmi "to send away"); erasure is *complete-removal* (exaleifō intensifies); together they cover the spectrum from gentle-let-go to definitive-erasure.

---

**T1.1.3 — Name and Naming**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Directional implications: release moves *from* the one releasing *toward* freeing the released-object. Relational implications: where release operates on relational obligations (debt, marriage bond, discipleship attachment), the relational structure is reconfigured. Constitutional implication: release operates on the *attachment-or-debt-state* between persons or between a person and an obligation; it does not principally operate on the inner-being-state itself (though downstream effects on inner-being are evidenced — e.g. Luk 4:18 inner liberation from oppression).

---

**T1.2.1 — Kind**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release is principally an **act** (the act of letting-go / cancelling / permitting) with downstream-state consequences. The act-character is strong: the corpus uses verbal forms (afiēmi, afesis-as-resultant-noun). State-consequences: the released party enters the state of being-released (Col 2:14 record-cancelled); the discipleship-leaver enters the state of being-detached (Mat 19:29 having-left). Disposition: secondary — the *willingness to release* is dispositional (Mat 5:40 non-resistance; Pro 16:6 hesed-emet as releasing-character) but not the principal mode of operation.

---

**T1.2.2 — Kind**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Composite structure. (i) **What is being released** — sin (VCG-01), attachment (VCG-03), bond (VCG-04 reconciliation-prioritizing), grief (VCG-10). (ii) **Who is releasing** — God (VCG-01, VCG-10), the disciple-leaver (VCG-03), the welcoming-permitter (VCG-08). (iii) **Who or what is being freed** — the offender (sin-release), the disciple themselves (leaving-for-following), the child welcomed, the redeemed (tear-wiped). (iv) **What relational reconfiguration follows** — restored standing (sin-release), reoriented loyalty (discipleship), kingdom-access opened, eschatological joy. Each operative release has these elements; their variation produces the sub-group's diversity.

---

**T1.2.3 — Kind**
**[CHAR-3]** E — **Working description:** An act in which someone with the standing or authority to bind (God, owner, mediator, the will of the agent) lets-go of what was bound (sin's record, an attachment, an obligation, a captive state, a grief), producing for the freed party an unencumbered state in the relevant register (covenantal standing, devoted loyalty, kingdom-access, eschatological consolation).

---

**T1.3.1 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Structural opposite is **clinging / refusing-to-let-go / sealed-against-release**. VCG-02 names this at the sin-release level (Mar 3:29 the blasphemy-against-Spirit cannot be released-ever). VCG-09 names this at the permission-level (Mat 23:13 actively blocks-others-from-kingdom). VCG-05 names this at the bond-failure level (loyalty failure that produces abandonment which is itself a failed-release — the failure is in refusing to maintain a bond that should be maintained, the opposite direction of failed-release in VCG-09's blocking). The structural opposite varies by register but the unifying inner-state is *the will closed against let-go where let-go should operate*.

---

**T1.3.2 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release excludes / resists: (i) Sustained Spirit-rejection (Mar 3:29 release-of-sin foreclosed); (ii) Will-blocking of others' kingdom-access (Mat 23:13); (iii) Refusal to permit moral obligation (Mar 7:12 tradition blocking filial duty); (iv) Refusal to receive grief-consolation (the eschatological tear-wiping requires being-in-the-redeemed; Rev 21:4 implicit). Permission as exercised by Christ (Mar 1:34 withholding speech from demons) shows that release can include withholding as well as granting — release is not undiscriminating permission. The wise exercise of release-faculty includes knowing when to grant and when to withhold (Mat 10:23 / 5:40 contrast registers).

---

**T1.3.3 — Boundary**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Release (M11-C) ends and other characteristics begin at: **vs M11-B Divine forgiveness** — M11-B is specifically of sin-against-God in declared-release mode; M11-C is broader including non-sin release (attachment, grief, permission). **vs M11-A Atonement** — M11-A is the operative cover-mechanism; M11-C is the let-go outcome. **vs M11-D Repentance** — M11-D is the inner turn FROM sin/wrong; M11-C is the let-go OF sin/attachment/grief. **vs M11-E Reconciliation** — M11-E is relational restoration outcome; M11-C is the let-go that may enable it (Mat 5:24).

---

**T1.4.1 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Multiple modes evidenced. (i) **Proclamation** (afesis NT pattern, VCG-01); (ii) **Inner-volitional surrender** (VCG-03 discipleship-leaving); (iii) **Authoritative permission-granting** (VCG-08); (iv) **Authoritative restraint / withholding** (VCG-09); (v) **Eschatological wiping** (VCG-10); (vi) **Reconciliation-prioritizing** (VCG-04); (vii) **Loyalty failure mode** (VCG-05 — perverse-release, the failure-direction).

---

**T1.4.2 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Strongly context-differentiated. Each VCG is essentially a contextual-mode of release. The breadth of contextual operation is itself a structural feature of M11-C — the sub-group's coherence is in the let-go semantic core operating across many contexts, not in a unified institutional or relational frame.

---

**T1.4.3 — Modes of Operation**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Strong speech-mode. (i) **Sin-release pronouncement** (VCG-01 — Mar 1:4, Luk 24:47 proclamation; Mat 26:28 covenantal speech-act). (ii) **Permission-as-speech-act** (Mat 19:14 "let the children come"; Mar 10:14 parallel). (iii) **Christ's withholding-as-command** (Mar 1:34). Release-as-speech-act is structurally dense in M11-C.

---

**T1.5.1 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Varies sharply by register. (i) Sin-release: take-heart / restored-confidence (Mat 26:28 inaugurates the new-covenant freedom; Eph 1:7 grace-received); blessedness (Rom 4:7 in M11-B but parallel pattern). (ii) Discipleship-leaving: decisive-commitment without affective elaboration (Mat 4:20–22 "left and followed"). (iii) Welcoming-permission: joy / openness of those welcomed (Mar 10:14 children coming). (iv) Tear-wiping: ultimate-consolation (Rev 21:4 mourning ended). The register-variation reflects the sub-group's diversity.

---

**T1.5.2 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Varies. The corpus does not enforce one immediate-response pattern across the sub-group; each register has its own appropriate inner reaction.

---

**T1.5.3 — Immediate Response**
**[CHAR-3]** S — The discipleship-leaving verses (VCG-03) are largely silent on the leavers' affective experience — the corpus records the leaving-act without naming the inner-felt response. Silence is consistent with the decisive-commitment register where the act IS the response.

---

**T1.6.1 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Sustained effects vary. (i) Sin-released state: ongoing-redeemed (Col 1:14); blessedness (Rom 4:7); 1Jo 2:12 stable forgiven identity. (ii) Discipleship-detached state: ongoing detached-and-following — the apostles' continuing left-everything posture (Mat 19:27 retrospective claim). (iii) Welcomed-state: kingdom-access maintained (the children-blessed state extends). (iv) Tear-wiped state: the eschatological-restored is the final sustained state (Rev 21:4).

---

**T1.6.2 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Establishes various states: redeemed-identity, detached-loyalty, welcomed-access, consoled-completeness. The variety is the sub-group's coherence: each release-mode produces its appropriate sustained-state in the corresponding inner-being register.

---

**T1.6.3 — Sustained Effect**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Differs by register. For sin-release: immediate (declared) → sustained (settled-forgiven-identity, similar to M11-B). For discipleship-leaving: immediate (decisive act) → sustained (continuing detachment-and-following discipline). For tear-wiping: immediate IS sustained (the eschatological wipe is final; no transition needed). For permission: immediate (the act of granting/welcoming) → sustained (the access established).

---

**T1.7.1 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Enabling conditions vary. (i) Sin-release receiving: faith, confession, repentance (Act 2:38 "repent and be baptized for the forgiveness of your sins"; Act 26:18 turning); same conditions as M11-B. (ii) Discipleship-leaving: hearing-the-call + willingness-to-loose-attachment (Mat 4:20–22 immediate response). (iii) Welcoming-reception: humble openness to be received (children-as-paradigm in Mar 10:14). (iv) Tear-wiping reception: being-in-the-redeemed-community (Rev 7:17 those who came through tribulation).

---

**T1.7.2 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Blocking conditions: sustained-hardness (Mar 3:29 the sin-release-foreclosed limit); attachment to what should be left (the rich young ruler adjacent pattern); refusal to be welcomed (those who block-others Mat 23:13 cannot themselves receive the welcoming-disposition); rejection of the redeemed community.

---

**T1.7.3 — Conditions of Reception**
**[CHAR-3]** E — The non-receiving state varies by register: for sin-release foreclosed, sealed-Spirit-rejection (Mar 3:29). For discipleship-leaving refused, attachment-prioritized-above-call (Mat 19:22 adjacent — the young man sorrowed). For welcome-refused, self-exclusion from the welcoming community.

---

**T1.8.1 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Primary dimension: **volitional-let-go** (the act-of-releasing as central). Secondary: relational-standing (sin-release outcome); authoritative-pronouncement (permission-granting/withholding); affective-consolation (tear-wiping).

---

**T1.8.2 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Supported by: verb-dominance (afiēmi as primary), act-orientation across all VCGs, the consistent let-go semantic core. The volitional dimension is the most cross-register-stable feature.

---

**T1.8.3 — Dimension Classification**
**[CHAR-3]** E — Secondary dimensions vary by register more than for M11-A or M11-B — the sub-group's polymorphic nature shows in its dimensional secondaries (relational-standing for sin-release; affective-consolation for tear-wiping; authoritative-pronouncement for permission). The primary volitional-let-go unifies; the secondaries reflect register-context.
