# M11 Phase D — Char 2 (Divine forgiveness) — T3 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-B Divine forgiveness (char_seq=2)
**Tier:** T3 — Faculties (33 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27

---

**T3.1.1 — Perception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Perception is engaged moderately. (i) **Spiritual discernment** — Mar 4:12 "Forgiveness is withheld from those who do not truly turn — the verse links inner turning with the possibility of release, and the absence of turning with continued guilt"; the perceptive failure ("perception closed") forecloses reception. (ii) **Divine heart-knowledge** — 1Ki 8:39, 2Ch 6:30 "God's forgiveness is grounded in his unique knowledge of each person's heart, making pardon an act of intimate divine discernment" — divine perception at the heart-level enables just pardon. (iii) **Receiver's perception** — Mar 2:5 "seeing their faith" — Christ's perception of faith triggers the pronouncement; reciprocally the receiver's faith is itself a perceptive recognition.

---

**T3.1.2 — Perception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables and deepens* perception in the receiver (Luk 1:77 forgiveness is cognitive-experiential heart of salvation; recipient perceives more of God / self / mercy through forgiveness); *bypasses* in the cases of ignorance-being-forgiven (Luk 23:34 "citing their ignorance"); *impaired-blocking* in the negative pole (Mar 4:12 perception closed → forgiveness blocked).

---

**T3.1.3 — Perception**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Pattern reveals divine forgiveness as **perception-deepening at recipient end, perception-requiring at divine-discerning end, and perception-bypassing in ignorance-cases**. The corpus is not principally about perceptive grasp but about relational-disposition; perception serves the relational act without being its central seat.

---

**T3.2.1 — Cognition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Cognition is engaged in three modes. (i) **Recognition of forgiveness received** — Luk 1:77 "Salvation is specifically defined as knowing that sins are forgiven; forgiveness is the cognitive and experiential heart of what salvation means to the inner person." (ii) **Naming the specific object of guilt** — Act 8:22 "the inner motive of wickedness is the specific object of the release being sought" — cognitive identification of what is being forgiven. (iii) **Grasping the conditional structure** — Mat 6:14–15 the recipient must cognitively understand the reciprocal binding; failure to grasp produces the Mat 18 unforgiving-servant pattern.

---

**T3.2.2 — Cognition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables / deepens* cognition (Luk 1:77 cognitive-heart of salvation; growing understanding of grace through experiencing it); *bypasses* in ignorance-forgiven cases (Luk 23:34); does not principally impair cognition. The negative pole's cognitive-failure (Mat 18 unforgiving servant forgets his own forgiveness received) is a failure of the recipient's cognitive integration, not an effect of forgiveness itself.

---

**T3.2.3 — Cognition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness engages cognition but is not gated by cognitive completeness. The Levitical-and-temple-prayer pattern operates without requiring sophisticated theological grasp; the new-covenant pattern (Jer 31:34) operates through divine-act on memory rather than through cognitive transformation alone. M11-B is more cognitively-engaged than M11-A (Luk 1:77 makes forgiveness-knowledge cognitive-experiential heart) but still principally heart-disposition-anchored.

---

**T3.3.1 — Memory**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Memory is strongly engaged on the **divine side**. (i) Jer 31:34 "I will remember their sin no more" — divine memory's relationship to the offense is shifted; this is M11-B's most distinctive memorial-engagement. (ii) Jer 50:20 "iniquity and sin become unfindable" — the divine-memorial record is altered. (iii) Exo 30:16 (M11-A but relevant) the ransom-offering maintains the people in God's positive remembrance. (iv) Recipient memory: Luk 7:47 the much-forgiven holds the forgiveness in active memory and responds with love; Mat 18:32 the master expects the servant to remember being-forgiven.

---

**T3.3.2 — Memory**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *transforms* divine memory at the eschatological pole (Jer 31:34, 50:20 — divine memory ceases to hold the sin); *enables* recipient-memory of being-forgiven (Luk 7:47 generates ongoing love); *engages* memory across the institutional pattern. The unforgiving-servant parable (Mat 18:32 "the forgiven servant is reminded that his debt was cancelled because he pleaded — the inner act of pleading was the occasion of grace, and forgetting it is the failure the parable condemns") makes memory of being-forgiven essential to the imitative-extension.

---

**T3.3.3 — Memory**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Memory is structurally central to M11-B in a way it is not for M11-A. M11-A operates on memory at the recipient end (Eze 16:63 lasting humility); M11-B operates on memory at *the divine end* (Jer 31:34) AND the recipient end. This reveals divine forgiveness as ultimately a *memorial-reconstitution* — the divine record is rewritten; the recipient lives within the new record. The Mat 18 parable's verdict against forgetfulness shows that memory-of-being-forgiven is *constitutive* of the forgiven life.

---

**T3.4.1 — Affect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Affect is more engaged in M11-B than in M11-A. (i) **Divine compassion** — Mat 18:27 "Pity moves the master to release the servant from his debt entirely — compassion is the inner driver that produces the act of forgiveness"; Psa 78:38 (M11-A side, divine compassion). (ii) **Recipient love-and-gratitude** — Luk 7:47 "Great love is the evidence of great forgiveness received; the inner experience of being forgiven much produces love, while little forgiveness yields little love." (iii) **Restored joy / blessedness** — Rom 4:7 "blessedness is located in having lawless deeds forgiven and sins covered — the inner state of the one whose guilt has been lifted is declared a state of deep wellbeing." (iv) **Reverent awe with confidence** — Psa 130:4. Forgiveness has positive-affective texture that M11-A's gravity-bearing register lacks.

---

**T3.4.2 — Affect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables and deepens* multiple affects: love (Luk 7:47), blessedness (Rom 4:7), reverent awe (Psa 130:4), gratitude. It *requires* divine compassion as motivator (Mat 18:27); without that affective ground, the release-act does not occur. *Impair (negative pole):* the unforgiving inner-state is closed-heart (Mat 6:15) — affect is hardened against the offending other.

---

**T3.4.3 — Affect**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Pattern reveals M11-B as **affectively-rich** in a way M11-A is not. Where M11-A produces sober humility as its dominant affect, M11-B produces love + gratitude + blessedness + reverent confidence. This affective contrast is structurally informative: the *operative-cover-mechanism* (M11-A) is gravity-bearing; the *declared-release-disposition* (M11-B) is gratitude-evoking. Both are sober (atonement) and joyful (forgiveness) without contradiction; the two characteristics together complete the relational-emotional arc.

---

**T3.5.1 — Creativity**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Creativity is minimally engaged. The pardon-mechanism is divinely-given (Psa 86:5 forgiveness-as-character; not invented by humans); the human-side reciprocal extension (Mat 6:12) operates by extension, not by creativity. No substantive creativity-engagement evidence in M11-B.

---

**T3.5.2 — Creativity**
**[CHAR-2]** S — No direct evidence on creativity-effects from sustained forgiveness. Indirect: the forgiven recipient may have renewed creative-moral capacity (similar to M11-A's downstream implication) but this is inference.

---

**T3.5.3 — Creativity**
**[CHAR-2]** S — The minimal-creativity engagement reveals divine forgiveness as *received* rather than *invented*. The receiver participates in a divinely-given act, not in a creative work of their own design. This is theologically appropriate — forgiveness's authenticity depends on its not being self-supplied.

---

**T3.6.1 — Volition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Volition is strongly engaged. (i) **Divine volition** — Num 14:20 "God grants pardon immediately in response to the intercessor's plea, showing that divine forgiveness is a sovereign act of the will responding to intercession." (ii) **Recipient volition** — the will to confess (1Jo 1:9), to receive (Mat 9:2), to plead (Psa 25:11). (iii) **Forgiver volition (human extension)** — Mat 18:35 "Forgiveness must arise from the heart — not merely as formal release but as a genuine inner disposition"; Luk 17:3–4 repeated will-to-forgive. (iv) **Volition can be blocked** — Mat 6:15 closed-heart will refuses; Mat 18:35 same.

---

**T3.6.2 — Volition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables and deepens* volition at the recipient end (restored to freedom from guilt-paralysis); *requires* volitional engagement (cannot be received unwillingly); *requires* sustained will-discipline at the human-extending end (Luk 17:3–4 repeated forgiveness). Bypass in ignorance-cases (Luk 23:34). Impair: the negative pole is volitional-hardening (Mat 6:15; Mat 18:35).

---

**T3.6.3 — Volition**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Volitionally-saturated at every level — divine, recipient, human-extender. The reciprocal-conditional binding (Mat 6:14–15) makes volition the *responsible* element — humans choose whether they will operate in the forgiving mode and thereby choose whether they receive at all. The volitional discipline is sustained — Luk 17:4 "even seven times in a day."

---

**T3.7.1 — Agency**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Agency is engaged at the giving-end strongly. (i) **Divine agency** — Mar 2:7 "only God can forgive sins" — agency is exclusive to the divine origination; Num 14:20 sovereign act of will. (ii) **Christic agency** — Mat 9:6 Son-of-Man exercises divine agency on earth. (iii) **Delegated agency** — Joh 20:23 forgiveness as delegated authority. (iv) **Human-extending agency** — Mat 18:21–35 humans bear real moral authority to forgive (or withhold) at the interpersonal level. Forgiveness is therefore agentially-multi-leveled.

---

**T3.7.2 — Agency**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables* agency at the recipient end (restored to free action in covenant) and *develops* agency at the forgiver-end (the practice of forgiving extends agency). The Mat 18 parable shows that exercising the forgiveness-extending agency requires having received agency-from-being-forgiven first.

---

**T3.7.3 — Agency**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The agency pattern reveals divine forgiveness as **asymmetric but participable** — God is the originary agent; humans participate through extension and reciprocity. Like M11-A's mediator-recipient asymmetry, M11-B's giver-receiver asymmetry collapses christologically (Mat 9:6 Christ as both divine-and-human exercising the forgiveness-agency).

---

**T3.8.1 — Moral Evaluation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Moral evaluation is constitutively engaged. (i) Identification of guilt to be forgiven (Lev 4–7 sin-offering specifics; Act 8:22 the heart-intent specifically); (ii) Recognition of severity (Mat 12:31 limit of unforgivable); (iii) Discernment of repentance-as-condition (Mar 4:12; Isa 55:7; 2Ch 7:14); (iv) Divine moral evaluation that grounds pardon (1Ki 8:39 God's heart-knowledge enables just pardon). The Mat 18 parable's moral-evaluative comparison (master forgave much, servant won't forgive little) is the explicit moral-evaluative anchor.

---

**T3.8.2 — Moral Evaluation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables and deepens* moral evaluation — the forgiven person evaluates moral history in light of having been released (Luk 7:47 the much-forgiven recognizes the much; Eze 16:63 the lasting humility is moral self-knowledge). *Requires* moral evaluation as precondition — pardon is for specific identified guilt. *Impaired* by the negative pole's moral-evaluation failure (Deu 29:20 wilful self-deception is failed moral evaluation).

---

**T3.8.3 — Moral Evaluation**
**[CHAR-2]** E — M11-B is morally-evaluative throughout, like M11-A. The difference: M11-A's moral evaluation focuses on the *guilt requiring cover*; M11-B's focuses on the *moral significance of the release given* — the moral-evaluative content of the forgiver's act (Mat 18:27 compassion driving release as morally-evaluable disposition) and the receiver's responsibility (Mat 18:35 heart-forgiveness expected as moral-evaluative posture).

---

**T3.9.1 — Conscience**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Conscience is engaged. (i) **Conscience as locus of cleared accusation:** 1Jo 1:9 "the will's honest acknowledgment of sin triggers the divine faithfulness that releases guilt and purifies conscience"; the conscience receives the release. (ii) **Conscience as identity-grounding when forgiven:** 1Jo 2:12 "assurance grounds the inner identity and standing of the believer — guilt is already released, not conditionally pending" — the conscience is settled. (iii) **Conscience as accuser in absence:** Lam 3:42 "the absence of pardon is felt as inner wound" — the unrelieved conscience continues to bear. (iv) **Conscience as moved-by-compassion at forgiver-end:** Mat 18:27 compassion as conscience-driver.

---

**T3.9.2 — Conscience**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *clears* conscience (1Jo 1:9) and *settles* it (1Jo 2:12). Recurring conscience-engagement deepens through the ongoing-confession pattern (1Jo 1:9). M11-B clears the conscience at the declared-release level, while M11-A clears it at the guilt-covered level — together they produce the cleansed-conscience state.

---

**T3.9.3 — Conscience**
**[CHAR-2]** E — M11-A is the conscience-cleansing characteristic at the mechanism level; M11-B is the conscience-settling characteristic at the declared-release level. The two work together. Heb 9:14 / Heb 10:18 (M11-C-VCG-01 corpus) name the Christic-deepening that produces the once-for-all settled conscience — collapsing M11-A and M11-B's two-stage operation into a single completed act for the believer.

---

**T3.10.1 — Conscientiousness**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Conscientiousness is engaged at the forgiver-end strongly. (i) **Forgiver integration:** Mat 18:21–35 demands integrated moral-awareness-volition-action; the unforgiving servant's failure is failed integration (he received but did not extend). (ii) **Recipient integration:** 1Jo 1:9 the confession-and-receiving sequence requires the integrated response. (iii) **Sustained discipline:** Luk 17:4 repeated-forgiveness is a conscientiousness-test. The forgiveness-life requires integrated whole-person practice.

---

**T3.10.2 — Conscientiousness**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables and develops* conscientiousness through the reciprocal-binding discipline (Mat 6:12, 14–15). Practiced forgiveness shapes integrated moral persons. *Impaired* by the unforgiving-state (Mat 18:35 heart-failure).

---

**T3.10.3 — Conscientiousness**
**[CHAR-2]** E — M11-B's conscientiousness-development is principally relational-character formation — the recurring-extension of forgiveness to others develops the inner-character disposition that matches the divine. This is the formational dimension Pro 16:6 (M11-A cross-evidence) anticipates and Mat 18:21–35 expects.

---

**T3.11.1 — Relational Capacity**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Relational capacity is **the purpose** of divine forgiveness. Every M11-B verse operates relationally: God-toward-offender (sovereign release); human-toward-human (reciprocal extension); recipient-into-new-standing. Forgiveness's relational scope is wider than M11-A's because forgiveness operates at the standing-and-disposition level rather than the institutional-ritual level — it crosses contexts more readily.

---

**T3.11.2 — Relational Capacity**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness *enables, restores, and deepens* relational capacity. Restores blocked capacity (Mat 9:2 "take heart" — restored confidence to relate); deepens through humility-and-confidence balance (Psa 130:4); enables widening relational capacity (Mat 18:35 forgiving-from-heart implies enabling the relational repair with the offender).

---

**T3.11.3 — Relational Capacity**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Relational capacity is M11-B's principal target and effect. The characteristic exists for relational repair-and-extension. Like M11-A, it is constitutionally relational; unlike M11-A it operates more pervasively at the inter-personal level (the priestly-mediation of M11-A is principally divine-to-human; M11-B's reciprocal binding makes inter-personal forgiveness central).
