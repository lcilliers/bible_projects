# M11 Phase D — Char 2 (Divine forgiveness) — T4 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-B Divine forgiveness (char_seq=2)
**Tier:** T4 — Relational Interfaces (24 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27

---

**T4.1.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human**
**[CHAR-2]** E — God-to-human is the dominant direction. Densely evidenced: Luk 7:48 / Mat 9:2 / Mar 2:5 direct pronouncements; the Lev 4–7 atonement-outcome forgiveness sequence (M11-B-VCG-05); the Solomon temple-prayer corpus (VCG-06: 1Ki 8:30–50; 2Ch 6:21–39); the new-covenant unconditional forgiveness (Jer 31:34; Jer 50:20); the divine-character extension across the Psalms (Psa 86:5, 103:3, 130:4, 25:11).

---

**T4.1.2 — Divine Interface — God to Human**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Basis is principally **covenantal, character-grounded, and responsively-conditional**. (i) Covenantal: Lev 4–7 within Sinai; Solomon temple within the dwelling-relationship; new-covenant explicit (Jer 31:34). (ii) Character-grounded: Psa 86:5 "forgiving" as God's stable disposition; Num 14:18–19 Moses appeals to God's *steadfast love* as the basis. (iii) Responsively conditional: Isa 55:7 abundant pardon contingent on turning; 2Ch 7:14 humility-prayer-seeking-turning conditions; Mat 6:14–15 reciprocal. (iv) Unconditional initiative: Psa 86:5 "ready" disposition extends beyond the conditional moment; Christ's Luk 23:34 prayer for executioners is unconditional. The complex of bases mirrors M11-A but with character-disposition more foregrounded.

---

**T4.1.3 — Divine Interface — God to Human**
**[CHAR-2]** E — God's extension reveals a disposition of **patient, character-rooted, ready, and committed mercy**. Patient (Psa 78:38 repeated forgiveness across cycles of failure); ready (Psa 86:5; Neh 9:17 "ready to forgive"); character-rooted (Pro 16:6 hesed+emet as inner-mechanism); committed (Jer 31:34 "remember sin no more" — the divine commitment to release is total). The disposition is more relationally-warm than M11-A's gravity-bearing register: God is forgiving as character — not reluctantly persuaded.

---

**T4.1.4 — Divine Interface — God to Human**
**[CHAR-2]** S — God-to-human is densely evidenced (T4.1.1); the narrower silence: divine forgiveness in the OT operates principally through priestly-mediation or temple-prayer-mediation; direct unmediated God-to-human pronouncement is less common until the NT (Mat 9:6 inaugurates the direct earthly-authority). The mediated-vs-direct shift across the Testaments is itself a typological-structural finding.

---

**T4.2.1 — Divine Interface — Human to God**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Human-to-God is rich in M11-B. (i) **Confession** — 1Jo 1:9 confession activates the divine response; Lev 5 confession + offering; Lam 3:42 honest confession even when pardon is felt absent. (ii) **Prayer-toward-temple** — the entire VCG-06 corpus (1Ki 8 / 2Ch 6) is human petition for forgiveness oriented toward the divine dwelling. (iii) **Intercession on God's-name basis** — Psa 25:11, Dan 9:19 ("urgent, repeated petition for forgiveness... not on merit but on God's own name"). (iv) **Faith-receiving** — Mat 9:2 "faith responsive"; Act 10:43 "Belief is the inner act through which forgiveness of sins is received." Human-to-God movement in M11-B is principally petitionary + confessional + faith-receiving.

---

**T4.2.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Required inner posture: **honesty (confession), humility (no claim on merit), faith (trust in the promised response), and reciprocal-forgivingness (Mat 6:14–15)**. Confession + humility + faith + reciprocal practice form the inner posture cluster. The Lord's Prayer encodes this exactly.

---

**T4.2.3 — Divine Interface — Human to God**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The human-to-God direction reveals the relationship as **petitionary-and-honest** — the person can come honestly under their guilt and ask, with confidence (Psa 130:4 awe AND access). The relationship is durable enough to bear ongoing confession (1Jo 1:9 the Christian-life pattern) and structured to receive ongoing pardon. Even at the prophetic-extremity (Lam 3:42 pardon felt absent), the speaker still confesses honestly — the relational orientation is honest-engagement rather than evasion.

---

**T4.2.4 — Divine Interface — Human to God**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Human-to-God is densely evidenced; no notable silence.

---

**T4.3.1 — Human Interface — Giving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Yes — M11-B has explicit, dense evidence of human-to-human forgiveness-extension. (i) **Mat 6:12, 14–15** the reciprocal binding; (ii) **Mat 18:21–35** the unforgiving-servant parable — the canonical human-to-human extension teaching; (iii) **Luk 17:3–4** the repeated forgiveness instruction; (iv) **Mar 11:25** "releasing grievances against others is the inner condition that allows one to receive divine forgiveness in return"; (v) **Luk 23:34** Christ's intercession for executioners as human-form-of-forgiveness modelling. M11-B is the cluster's human-to-human forgiveness register (M11-C-VCG-04 has reconciliation-prioritizing but the explicit forgive-others teaching is M11-B).

---

**T4.3.2 — Human Interface — Giving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Inner conditions for genuine extension: (i) **Acknowledgment of having received** (Mat 18:32 "the forgiven servant is reminded that his debt was cancelled because he pleaded"); (ii) **Heart-forgiveness, not formal-only** (Mat 18:35); (iii) **Compassion as motivator** (Mat 18:27 pity-as-driver); (iv) **Persistent will** (Luk 17:4 even seven times in a day); (v) **Acceptance of repentance offered** (Luk 17:3 "when a brother repents"). The condition-cluster: gratitude + heart-engagement + compassion + persistence + receptivity to the other's turning.

---

**T4.3.3 — Human Interface — Giving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The person must have **received forgiveness from God first** (or at least been deeply confronted with their own forgive-need). Mat 18:32 makes this structurally explicit — the master assumes the servant should have remembered being forgiven much. The pattern: divine forgiveness received → human capacity to extend. Without the prior-receiving (or its functional equivalent — confronting one's own need), the human-extension lacks ground.

---

**T4.3.4 — Human Interface — Giving**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Human-extension evidence is rich; no notable silence.

---

**T4.4.1 — Human Interface — Receiving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Receiving from another person is evidenced more obliquely. The Mat 18 unforgiving-servant story shows the servant *receiving* the master's forgiveness (Mat 18:27) — this is the dominant receiving-evidence; though the master is God-figured in the parable, the reception-pattern is informative for human-to-human too. The Luk 17:3–4 instruction implies that the brother who repents will *receive* the forgiveness extended. Mat 6:12's reciprocal binding presupposes both giving and receiving in the human realm. **Within M11-B's specifically-Greek-or-Hebrew vocabulary, the receiving evidence is partial** — the explicit verses are mostly about divine-receiving, with human-receiving inferred from the reciprocal structure.

---

**T4.4.2 — Human Interface — Receiving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Enabling: acknowledged offense, willingness to be released, openness to relational repair. Blocking: pride (refusing to be in the debt-position), hardness (refusing to receive), continued offense (Mat 18 servant who received but didn't change).

---

**T4.4.3 — Human Interface — Receiving**
**[CHAR-2]** E — The non-receiving state mirrors the divine-non-receiving state: closed inner posture; pride or hardness; refusal to allow oneself to be released. The Mat 18 unforgiving servant complicates this — he received, but did not extend; his subsequent failure shows the reception was incomplete in some sense (received-without-internalization is itself a partial non-reception).

---

**T4.4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Receiving direction is partially silent in M11-B's verse evidence — much receiving-from-others is implicit rather than narrated. M11-C-VCG-04 (reconciliation-prioritizing) carries more explicit interpersonal-reception content.

---

**T4.5.1 — Human Interface — Boundaries**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Forgiveness operates differently across these. (i) **Within existing bonds** (covenant community): Lev 4–7; Solomon temple corpus. (ii) **Across breached bonds**: Luk 17:3–4 the repeated-forgiveness of a brother who has wronged. (iii) **Across relational distance**: Mat 5:44 (loving enemies — adjacent to M11-B in spirit); Luk 23:34 Christ for executioners. (iv) **Inclusive of foreigners**: Act 10:43 universal availability of forgiveness through Christ. (v) **Universal scope at limit**: Mar 3:28 universal forgivability. The boundary widens markedly in NT — from primarily covenant-context to universal-availability.

---

**T4.5.2 — Human Interface — Boundaries**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Operates within covenantal contexts and crosses covenantal boundaries. OT operation is principally covenantal (Israelite community); NT extends to universal (Act 10:43; Mat 28 great-commission implies global scope; Mar 3:28). The reciprocal binding (Mat 6:12 / 14–15) is universalizable — it applies wherever humans interact.

---

**T4.5.3 — Human Interface — Boundaries**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Inclusion is wide. **Included:** covenant insiders; ger / foreigners (Num 15:26 — M11-A's joint anchor); enemies (Luk 23:34); all who repent (Luk 17:3); all who believe (Act 10:43); all sin and blasphemy except against the Spirit (Mar 3:28). **Excluded:** sustained Spirit-rejection (Mat 12:31–32); wilful self-deception (Deu 29:20); unforgiving-others disposition (Mat 6:15 self-excluded); persistent betrayal (Jer 5:7). Like M11-A, exclusion is by inner-state-sealing, not by ethnic/covenantal boundary.

---

**T4.5.4 — Human Interface — Boundaries**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Evidence is sufficient.

**T4.6.1 — Spiritual Beings Interface**
**[CHAR-2]** E — More directly evidenced than M11-A. **Spirit-rejection limit:** Mat 12:31–32, Luk 12:10 name the Holy Spirit explicitly — blasphemy against the Spirit foreclosing pardon. This is the principal spiritual-being engagement in M11-B. The angelic-ministry interface is not directly evidenced in M11-B's verse set. The adversarial-distortion possibility is implied at the negative pole (Mat 6:15, Deu 29:20) — the wilful refusal to forgive may have adversarial roots, but the verses do not name adversarial agency directly.

---

**T4.6.2 — Spiritual Beings Interface**
**[CHAR-2]** E — Potentially. The Mat 12:31–32 unforgivable-sin connects to the adversarial-spiritual context (the Pharisees attributing Spirit's work to Beelzebul). This implies forgiveness IS a site of adversarial activity — the adversary's strategy is to seal the inner ground against the Spirit. The negative-pole VCG-04 verses are the most-likely site of adversarial influence; the verses themselves attribute the failure to the inner-human state (wilful self-deception, hardness) rather than naming adversarial agency directly.

---

**T4.6.3 — Spiritual Beings Interface**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Angelic-mediation of forgiveness is not directly evidenced in M11-B. The mediating channels named are priests (OT), Christ, and delegated-human-extenders (Joh 20:23). Angels are not named as mediating channels.

---

**T4.6.4 — Spiritual Beings Interface**
**[CHAR-2]** S — Spiritual-beings interface is partially silent except for the Mat 12:31–32 Spirit-engagement at the negative limit. The principal frame remains God–human–mediator/extender.
