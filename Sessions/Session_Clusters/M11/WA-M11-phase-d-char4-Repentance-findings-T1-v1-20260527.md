# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T1 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-D Repentance and turning (char_seq=4) — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T1.1.1 Name and Naming** **[CHAR-4]** E — Named **Repentance and turning**. The compound name signals the dual register — repentance (the inner-grief-and-reorienting act) AND turning (the volitional re-direction). Together they cover both the affective-cognitive (na.cham contrition) and the volitional-directional (shuv-recall) registers.

**T1.1.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — **Hebrew primary:** H5162H **na.cham** — to be sorry / relent / regret (34V, dominant by volume). H7725N **shuv** — to return, recall (7V; in M11-D the recall-sense of shuv). H5749A **ud** — to return (1V, marginal). Root meanings: na.cham covers grief / regret / change-of-mind (the affective-and-cognitive shift); shuv covers physical-or-figurative return (the directional shift). ud is less common but carries return / sustain meaning.

**T1.1.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Strong directional implications: turning is *from* something *toward* something else; the from-and-toward shape is constitutive. Relational implications: turning toward God (or away from sin toward God) re-establishes the covenant orientation. Constitutional: operates at the inner-volitional level (will-turning) with affective-cognitive accompaniment (regret-recognition).

---

**T1.2.1 Kind** **[CHAR-4]** E — Repentance is principally an **inner-act** (turning) with affective state (contrition / regret) and ongoing condition (the turned-orientation). It is more inner-act than M11-A's mediated-act and more inner-state than M11-B's standing-state. The inner-act-of-turning is M11-D's signature kind.

**T1.2.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Composite: (i) recognition of misalignment (cognitive); (ii) regret/contrition (affective — na.cham at human level); (iii) volitional turn (shuv directional); (iv) post-turning posture (humbled, returning, oriented). The four elements operate together for full repentance; partial-presence produces partial-repentance.

**T1.2.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — **Working description:** The inner human act of recognizing misalignment with God (or the right), grieving the misalignment, and volitionally re-directing the self toward God/right. In the divine register: God's responsive turning from announced judgment in response to intercession or human turning, expressing God's compassionate character.

---

**T1.3.1 Boundary** **[CHAR-4]** E — Structural opposite: **persisting-without-turning** (Jer 8:6 "no one relents of their evil") and **hardness / sealed-against-turning** (Mar 4:12-style perception-closed, will-hardened — though this is M11-B's negative pole verse, it parallels the M11-D failure). The opposite at the divine register: refusal-to-relent (1Sa 15:29; Eze 24:14 — divine constancy when no human turning is forthcoming).

**T1.3.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Excludes / resists: continued unrepentant-persistence (Jer 8:6); fear-driven turning-back-from-good (Exo 13:17 "resolve fragile, will wavering"); idolatry-betrayal (Isa 57:6); rebellion-to-the-point-of-exhausted-mercy (Jer 15:6).

**T1.3.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D ends and others begin: **vs M11-A Atonement** — atonement covers; turning re-orients. **vs M11-B Forgiveness** — turning enables forgiveness as condition; forgiveness is the response to turning. **vs M11-C Release** — release lets-go-of-attachment; turning is the related but distinct act of re-orienting-toward. **vs M11-E Reconciliation** — turning is the inner act; reconciliation is the relational outcome. **vs M03 Grief** — divine grief at human failure (na.cham at Gen 6:6) is M03 inner-content; the relenting-movement is M11-D.

---

**T1.4.1 Modes of Operation** **[CHAR-4]** E — Modes: (i) **Divine-relenting from announced judgment** (VCG-01, VCG-02); (ii) **Divine refusal-to-relent** (VCG-03); (iii) **Divine grief at human failure** (VCG-04); (iv) **Conditional relenting** (VCG-05); (v) **Human-contrition** (VCG-06); (vi) **Negative human relenting / resolve-collapse / no-turning** (VCG-07); (vii) **Inner heart-turning shuv-recall** (VCG-08); (viii) **Faithfulness-under-pressure ud structural-opposite** (VCG-09).

**T1.4.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Strongly context-differentiated. Divine vs human; positive vs negative; institutional vs experiential.

**T1.4.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Strong speech-mode at the divine-pronouncement level (relent-or-not announced through prophets); confession-mode at the human level (1Ki 8:47–48 the prayer of repentance is verbal).

---

**T1.5.1 Immediate Response** **[CHAR-4]** E — Immediate response in human-turning: contrition + self-abasement (Job 42:6 "dust and ashes"); shame and self-reproach (Jer 31:19); recognition of sin (1Ki 8:47 "acknowledging sin"). Immediate response in receiver-of-divine-relenting: rescue / restoration (Jon 3:10 Nineveh's deliverance); covenant-renewal (Exo 32 after Moses's intercession). The dominant affect: sober contrition rather than euphoric relief.

**T1.5.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Varies. Job 42:6's whole-person self-abasement is extreme; Jer 31:19 names shame-and-self-reproach; 1Ki 8:47–48 names heart-turning + confession. The corpus shows multiple shapes of immediate response, all in the sober register.

**T1.5.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Many shuv-verses (Deu 30:1; Isa 46:8) name the act-of-turning without describing the felt response.

---

**T1.6.1 Sustained Effect** **[CHAR-4]** E — Sustained: (i) Re-oriented heart (1Ki 8:47 the heart-turned-toward-God in exile); (ii) Lam 3:21 hope-from-recall — the memory-act sustains hope through grief; (iii) Sustained contrition-disposition (Jer 31:19 the ongoing self-reproach pattern); (iv) Restored standing post-divine-relenting (the people post-Jon 3:10 continue under renewed grace).

**T1.6.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Establishes: heart-orientation-toward-God; sober-self-knowledge; renewed standing; hope-from-recall (Lam 3:21).

**T1.6.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Differs: immediate is acute-contrition; sustained is settled-turned-orientation.

---

**T1.7.1 Conditions of Reception** **[CHAR-4]** E — Enabling: recognition of misalignment (1Ki 8:47 acknowledging sin); memory of covenant (Deu 30:1 recall); willingness to be confronted (Isa 46:8 command to transgressors); humble openness (2Ch 7:14 humility-prayer-seeking-turning).

**T1.7.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Blocking: continued blindness (Isa 44:19 idol-maker's failure to reflect); rebellion exhaustion (Jer 15:6); fear-driven evasion (Exo 13:17); wilful-self-deception (parallel to Deu 29:20 M11-B negative pole).

**T1.7.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Non-receiving state: Jer 8:6 conscience silent, self-examination absent, stubbornness moral. Mar 4:12 perception closed, will hardened (cross-register).

---

**T1.8.1 Dimension Classification** **[CHAR-4]** E — Primary: **volitional-directional** (the act of turning). Secondary: affective-contrite (regret / sorrow accompanying); cognitive-recognitive (recognition of misalignment); relational (toward God / from sin).

**T1.8.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Supported by: shuv being a directional verb; na.cham covering the change-of-direction (regret/relent); the from-and-toward structure of every turning act.

**T1.8.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Secondary dimensions complement: affective gives texture to turning (regret); cognitive grounds it (recognition); relational gives it telos (toward God).
