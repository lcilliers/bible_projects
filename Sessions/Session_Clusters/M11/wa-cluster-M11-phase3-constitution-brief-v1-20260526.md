## Your task — per-term verdict

For each of the **15 terms** in §2 of the constitution report, read the term's full meaning corpus and assign exactly one verdict:

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **STAYS** | The term's meaning corpus aligns with M11's characteristic (the inner-being movement from sin/defilement to restored standing — through release, atonement, or turning). Term remains in M11. |
| **STAYS (with cross-register flag)** | Per v2_9 §6.3.2: even where the term's primary register lies elsewhere, **STAYS** if any verse in the corpus evidences a relationship with M11's characteristic. Flag the primary register. |
| **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster_code}** | The term's meaning corpus aligns with a different cluster's characteristic AND no verse evidences any M11-relational content. Accidental placement. Name the target cluster from §4 of the constitution report. |
| **BOUNDARY** | The term's meaning corpus is supportive, qualifying, or genuinely undecided. Hold for researcher review. Cite the analytical question + which §6.3.1 valid reason applies. |

Each verdict must:
1. Cite specific verses or meaning patterns from the term's corpus.
2. Be grounded in the inner-being content of the meanings — not the gloss alone.

**Expected outcome (CC's pre-debate prediction):** all 15 terms STAY. The three registers represent different mechanisms of the same characteristic movement (sin/defilement → restored standing). Cross-register flags expected on most/all terms — most strongly to M10 (sin precondition) and M10c (defilement precondition for the cleansing terms). One or more terms may carry BOUNDARY if their corpus tips heavily toward another register (e.g. apobolē's single verse Rom 11:15 may or may not survive scrutiny).

## §6.3.2 — Verse-level relationship test for TRANSFERS (CRITICAL)

A TRANSFERS verdict requires more than "the term's meaning corpus aligns primarily with another cluster's characteristic." Corpus-primary-register reasoning alone is too aggressive — M07 Phase 3 v1→v2 reversed 8 false-positive TRANSFERS to STAYS-with-flag under this rule.

**The test, verse by verse:**

1. Read every verse in the term's meaning corpus.
2. For each verse, ask: *does this verse evidence any relationship with or impact on M11's characteristic — the inner-being movement of release/atonement/turning that restores standing after sin or defilement?* "Relationship" is broad: direct evidence, structural opposite, instrument-of, response-to, corporate effect.

**Verdict rule:**

- If **any** verse evidences a relationship with M11 → **STAYS** with cross-register flag in the rationale.
- If **no** verse evidences any M11 relationship → **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster}** confirmed (accidental placement).

## Disallowed BOUNDARY reasons (per v2_9 §6.3.1)

BOUNDARY may **NOT** be assigned solely because:

- The term's meaning corpus is predominantly external/relational (priestly mediation, sacrificial ritual) rather than vertical/inward. **M11 is BY DEFINITION about the inner-being movement enacted through outer means** (priestly mediation, sacrifice, declaration). Do not let "mediated through ritual" be a BOUNDARY ground.
- The meanings describe interpersonal forgiveness rather than divine forgiveness, or vice versa. The cluster spans both directions.
- The meanings describe an inner-being state you would prefer not to be in scope.

Valid BOUNDARY reasons (cite one of these three):

1. **Cluster-membership undecided** — corpus is genuinely on the borderline between M11 and another cluster, or the term should arguably constitute its own register-cluster (worth researcher consideration).
2. **Homonymic / polysemic spread** — the term covers two or more distinct senses that may need sense-split (afiēmi G0863G/H/I are already sense-split in the inventory; this is a model for how polysemy is handled).
3. **Supportive / qualifying register** — the term's meanings enhance / qualify M11's characteristic without itself carrying it as a primary characteristic.

## Specific signals to attend to (M11 pre-debate notes from CC)

### A. The 3-register composition question (CENTRAL)

M11's three registers (Release / Atonement / Turning) could plausibly constitute:

- **One characteristic** ("the inner-being movement from sin/defilement to restored standing") — supported by the integrated nature of all 15 terms in the overall biblical theology of salvation; supported by the analytics keyword overlap ("guilt released" + "atonement outcome" share guilt-removal content).
- **Two characteristics** with one being a structurally-distinct sub-characteristic (likely candidate: atonement-as-mechanism vs forgiveness-as-outcome). M10/M10c sibling-split precedent suggests this is feasible.
- **Three characteristics** — release (afesis/afiēmi/sa.lach), atonement (kip.per), turning (shuv/na.cham/katallassō). Strongest split case if Phase 3 finds the registers have minimal inner-being-content overlap.

Phase 3's task is **NOT to design sub-groups** (that's Phase 5). Phase 3 decides whether the 15 terms belong in one cluster at all. If Phase 3 finds the three registers are genuinely separate characteristics, surface this as a BOUNDARY / TRANSFER recommendation — the researcher may then consider a pre-Phase-5 cluster split (M10 → M10/M10b/M10c precedent).

**However:** the M11 cluster has been programmatically-named "Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration" — its three-fold name signals the programme's intention to hold all three registers together. The default expectation is **all STAY, with the 3-register composition becoming a Phase 5 sub-group design driver**.

### B. Likely STAYS — clear primary M11 vocabulary

These are extremely likely to STAY (clear M11 register):

- **H5545 sa.lach (45V)** — divine forgiveness verb; OT covenant-context forgiveness. STAYS.
- **G0859 afesis (16V)** — "release/forgiveness" noun; NT canonical forgiveness. STAYS.
- **G0863G afiēmi "release: forgive" (32V)** — NT forgiveness verb. STAYS.
- **H3722A kip.per (81V)** — "to atone" — covers, ransoms, makes propitiation. STAYS (atonement register).
- **G1813 exaleifō (4V)** — "to blot out" (sin/record). STAYS.
- **H5547 se.li.chah (3V)** — "forgiveness" noun (rare in OT). STAYS.
- **H5546 sal.lach (1V)** — "forgiving" (Ps 86:5). STAYS.

### C. Watch carefully — register-boundary questions

- **G0863H afiēmi "release: leave" (37V)** — "release" in non-forgiveness senses (let go, abandon, permit). Many uses (e.g. "leave father and mother" Matt 19:5) have nothing to do with sin-forgiveness. Test against §6.3.2 carefully. May STAY-with-flag (if any verse evidences M11 relationship) or BOUNDARY (homonymic/polysemic spread).
- **G0863I afiēmi "release: permit" (18V)** — "release" as "allow/permit." Even more clearly removed from forgiveness register. Same test.
- **H7725N shuv (7V)** — "to return." A massive Hebrew verb with thousands of uses; the 7 here are the M11-specific "return" register (turning back to God). Confirm against verses.
- **H5162H na.cham (34V)** — "to be sorry, relent." Divine and human relenting/repenting. Spans both anthropological repentance (human turning) and divine relenting (God's response). Confirm both senses serve M11's characteristic.
- **G0580 apobolē (1V)** — "deprivation/rejection" — Rom 11:15 only. The single-verse Pass A meaning frames Israel's rejection as the mechanism for world reconciliation. This is a borderline case — the term's *primary* gloss is "rejection," not "restoration." Test: does Rom 11:15 evidence a relationship with M11's characteristic? If yes (paradox of loss-as-mechanism-of-restoration), STAYS. If no, may TRANSFER (but no obvious target).
- **G0604 apokatallassō / G2644 katallassō** — "to reconcile" (NT). 8V combined. Distinct from forgiveness register but central to "restoration" — these are likely STAYS as primary M11 vocabulary in the restoration register.
- **H5749A ud (1V)** — "to return/recall." Single verse; confirm M11 relationship.
