# M11 Phase D — Char 5 (Reconciliation) — T3 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-E Reconciliation — 33 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T3.1.1 Perception** **[CHAR-5]** E — Perception engaged in receiving the ambassadorial-appeal (2Cor 5:20 — the message must be heard and received); recognition of being-formerly-an-enemy (Rom 5:10's logic requires perceptive recognition).

**T3.1.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation enables deepened perception of being-loved-while-still-an-enemy (Rom 5:10's astonishing fact). Bypasses perception only at the cosmic-scope level (Col 1:20 cosmic reconciliation operates through Christ regardless of any individual creature's grasp).

**T3.1.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Perception-engagement is partial — at the personal-reception end; less at the cosmic-scope end.

---

**T3.2.1 Cognition** **[CHAR-5]** E — Cognition engaged: 2Cor 5:19 "not counting trespasses" requires cognitive grasp of the record-not-counted-against; 2Cor 5:20 ambassadorial appeal requires recipient cognitive engagement.

**T3.2.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation enables deeper cognition of grace; requires cognition at the receiving stage.

**T3.2.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Cognition is engaged but not central; relational-outcome dominates.

---

**T3.3.1 Memory** **[CHAR-5]** E — Memory engaged: Rom 5:10 "while still enemies" — the reconciled person carries memory of the prior-enmity state and of the cost of reconciliation; this memory shapes ongoing reconciled-life.

**T3.3.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Memory grounds the ongoing-gratitude and prevents presumption.

**T3.3.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Memory engagement is significant but less foregrounded than in M11-B (where divine memory of sin is explicitly altered).

---

**T3.4.1 Affect** **[CHAR-5]** E — Affect engaged: the post-reconciliation peaceable state (implicit in Rom 5:10 the saved-by-his-life logic); the awe-and-gratitude of having-been-reconciled-while-still-an-enemy. The ambassadorial-ministry requires affective-investment in extending the message.

**T3.4.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation enables peace and gratitude.

**T3.4.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Affective content is inferred; the corpus emphasizes the act and state over the felt response.

---

**T3.5.1 Creativity** **[CHAR-5]** S — Minimal direct engagement. Reconciliation is received and extended, not creatively invented.

**T3.5.2** **[CHAR-5]** S — Indirect engagement at ambassadorial-extension level.

**T3.5.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Not principally creativity-engaging.

---

**T3.6.1 Volition** **[CHAR-5]** E — Volition engaged: 2Cor 5:20 "the will is summoned to abandon enmity and receive the restored relationship being offered"; 1Cor 7:11 "the will to dissolve the marriage bond is restrained, and reconciliation is presented as the preferred inner posture." Volition is decisive at the receiving and the maintaining ends.

**T3.6.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation requires willing-to-abandon-enmity; enables sustained reconciled-disposition.

**T3.6.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Volitionally-engaged at recipient and ministering levels.

---

**T3.7.1 Agency** **[CHAR-5]** E — Agency: divine (God reconciled, 2Cor 5:18); Christic (the cross as agential act); apostolic/ecclesial (the ministry-of-reconciliation delegation); recipient (receiving / extending).

**T3.7.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation enables agency-restored at recipient end; constitutes the apostolic-ambassadorial agency.

**T3.7.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Agency multi-leveled; Christic resolution of asymmetry (Christ as both divine-initiator and human-incarnate-mediator).

---

**T3.8.1 Moral Evaluation** **[CHAR-5]** E — Moral evaluation engaged: 2Cor 5:19 not-counting-trespasses presupposes the moral evaluation that there ARE trespasses being not-counted; Col 1:22 the inner-moral aim (holy/blameless/above-reproach) is moral-evaluative.

**T3.8.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation operates with moral evaluation and deepens it (the recipient evaluates moral history in light of reconciliation).

**T3.8.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Morally-evaluative throughout — the inner-moral outcome (holy/blameless/above-reproach) is the formal endpoint.

---

**T3.9.1 Conscience** **[CHAR-5]** E — Conscience engaged: Col 1:22 the presented-holy/blameless/above-reproach is a conscience-restored state. 2Cor 5:19 not-counting-trespasses implies the conscience freed-from-accusation.

**T3.9.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation enables / settles the conscience at the relational-standing level (M11-A clears guilt-cover; M11-B settles standing-declared; M11-E settles relational-bond).

**T3.9.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Conscience engagement in M11-E is at the relationally-completed state — the bond restored, the conscience can be at-peace at the relational-bond level.

---

**T3.10.1 Conscientiousness** **[CHAR-5]** E — Conscientiousness in the ambassadorial-ministry (2Cor 5:18–20 — integrated moral-awareness-volition-action of extending reconciliation); in the marital-bond maintenance (1Cor 7:11 — integrated will-restrained, bond-maintained).

**T3.10.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation develops conscientiousness through the ministry-discipline (sustained extension to others) and the relational-fidelity discipline.

**T3.10.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — M11-E is formational for whole-person reconciled-life with integrated practice.

---

**T3.11.1 Relational Capacity** **[CHAR-5]** E — Relational capacity is **the central engagement** of M11-E — the entire characteristic exists for relational-capacity restoration. Every M11-E verse operates on relational-bond categories.

**T3.11.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Restores blocked relational capacity (Rom 5:10 enmity overcome → relational capacity restored); deepens relational capacity (Col 1:22 the inner-moral transformation enables fuller relating); enables widening relational capacity (Col 1:20 cosmic scope; 2Cor 5:20 ambassadorial extension).

**T3.11.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — M11-E is **the relational-capacity characteristic** of the cluster — the one most-directly focused on restoring and extending relational bonds. M11-A/B/C/D contribute through their own registers; M11-E is the outcome.
