**T6.1.2 — Co-occurrence — What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The co-occurrence pattern reveals atonement as a **central, mechanism-supplying characteristic** that connects upstream (M10 guilt as precondition; M11-D repentance as opening condition) to downstream (M11-B forgiveness as outcome; M11-E reconciliation as relational fruit). This positions atonement not as one inner-being state among many but as the *mechanism-bridge* that the other characteristics flow through to produce their work. The Pro 16:6 (hesed + emet atone) case shows the inner-character internalization of this bridging function — when hesed and faithfulness operate as character, the person becomes capable of being the bridging agent for others.

**T6.2.1 — Sequential Relationships — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — three sequential relationships are explicit. (i) **Atonement → Forgiveness (kip.per → sa.lach):** the canonical Levitical sequence (Lev 4:20 "following priestly atonement, the congregation receives divine forgiveness"; Lev 4:26, 4:31, 4:35, 5:10, 5:13, 5:16, 5:18, 6:7, 19:22, Num 15:25, 15:26, 15:28). Atonement causally precedes forgiveness — the priestly atonement mediates between guilt and the declared release. (ii) **Repentance/turning → Atonement (M11-D → M11-A):** Num 16:46 the people's near-death distress prompts Aaron's atoning intervention; the structural pattern of human-turning-opens-atonement runs across the corpus (Lev 23:28 work-cease day is penitential-approach). (iii) **Atonement → Reconciliation (M11-A → M11-E):** Christic atonement (Mat 27:50, Joh 18:8) → cosmic reconciliation (Col 1:20). (iv) **Atonement → Standing-restoration (recipient state):** Lev 14:20 atonement → declared clean; Lev 16:30 atonement → inner cleansing. Atonement is consistently the *operative middle* in these sequences.

---

**T6.2.2 — Sequential Relationships — What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The relationships are principally **causal**, with developmental elements in the recurring institutional pattern. (i) **Causal:** the atonement → forgiveness arc is unambiguously causal — Lev 4:20 et al. use the "following atonement" → "is forgiven" sequence as cause-and-effect, not mere correlation. The priestly act *produces* the declared release. Similarly, Christic atonement *produces* cosmic reconciliation. (ii) **Developmental:** the recurring Levitical pattern (Lev 16:34 perpetual statute; Lev 4–7 case-by-case) embeds the causal sequence in a developmental arc — the community's ongoing covenant standing is developmentally maintained by recurring atonement-forgiveness-restoration cycles. (iii) **Correlational** elements: M11-A and M11-D co-occur correlatively where the verses don't explicitly causally-link them — e.g. Lev 23:28's day-of-atonement work-cease implies a penitential disposition but doesn't articulate the causal mechanism. (iv) **Constitutive vs causal:** the kip.per → sa.lach causal arc could also be read as *constitutive* — atonement is the inside of forgiveness's outside. T6.3.3 takes up this constitutive question.

**T6.3.1 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships — Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one, and by what mechanism?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement produces several other characteristics. (i) **Produces M11-B Forgiveness** (most direct): kip.per produces sa.lach via the priestly-mediation-and-declaration mechanism (Lev 4:20 et al.). The mechanism: atoning act covers guilt; priestly declaration recognizes the cover and releases the offerer from the standing of guilt. (ii) **Produces M11-E Reconciliation:** Christic atonement produces cosmic reconciliation (Mat 27:50 → Col 1:20 reading the M11-A → M11-E arc); the mechanism is that the maximal-mediated cover enables the relational restoration at cosmic scale. (iii) **Produces M01 Fear/Reverence:** Eze 16:63 "atonement produces shame and silencing in the inner person... opens the memory to lasting confounded humility" — humility-as-reverence is produced by atonement received; Psa 130:4 (M11-B context) "the existence of forgiveness... is itself the basis for reverent fear" — same logic. (iv) **Produces M19 Trust (downstream):** the cleansed standing enables continued covenant engagement which is M19-relational. The principal direct production: atonement → forgiveness and atonement → reverent humility.

**T6.3.2 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships — Is this characteristic produced by another — and if so, which one?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is enabled / occasioned by several other characteristics. (i) **Enabled by M11-D Repentance/turning:** Jer 18:8 / Jon 3:10 (M11-D verses) name turning as the condition that opens divine relenting — and divine relenting precedes/co-occurs with atonement-acceptance. Lev 23:28 the DoA work-cease implies penitential disposition. (ii) **Occasioned by M10 Guilt** (precondition): every M11-A act has M10-content as its precipitating cause — without guilt there is nothing to atone for; the priestly mechanism activates *in response to* the M10 condition. (iii) **Empowered by M05 Compassion / Hesed:** Psa 78:38 "divine compassion moves God to atone for iniquity and restrain wrath"; Pro 16:6 "iniquity is atoned for through steadfast love and faithfulness" — hesed as inner-mechanism is M05 character ground for M11-A action. (iv) **Catalyzed by M21 Prayer / Intercession:** Exo 32:30 Moses's intercessory prayer prompts the atoning act; Num 16:46 incense-prayer-and-atonement combined; Dan 9:19 (M11-B context, intercession). (v) **Christologically enabled by M30 Obedience:** Christ's willed surrender (Mat 27:50) is an act of obedience-unto-death that is itself the atonement. The producing chain is M10/M11-D → (M21 intercession) → M11-A → M11-B / M11-E.

**T6.3.3 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships — Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another characteristic constitute part of this one?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is **constitutive of M11-B Divine forgiveness** (where forgiveness has an OT-Levitical form) and is **constitutive of M11-E Reconciliation** (in the Christological form). (i) **Atonement is the inside of forgiveness:** the Lev 4–7 pattern shows sa.lach as the *declared outcome* of kip.per — there is no Levitical sa.lach without prior kip.per. Forgiveness in the OT-divine-mediated form has atonement as its constituent mechanism. NT divine forgiveness extends this: Heb 9:22 ("without blood there is no forgiveness", M11-C) makes the constitutive relationship explicit. (ii) **Atonement is the basis of reconciliation:** Col 1:20 "through the blood of the cross, all things are to be reconciled" — atonement constitutes the ground on which reconciliation operates. (iii) **What constitutes part of atonement:** the act-of-mediation is internally constituted by (a) acknowledged-guilt-target, (b) life-substitution-mechanism, (c) mediator-agency, (d) declarative-outcome. Of these, the mediator's character (M05 hesed in Pro 16:6; Christ's M30 obedience) and the offerer's M11-D acknowledgment constitute partial-inputs to atonement. So: atonement constitutes forgiveness and reconciliation; characteristics like M05, M11-D, M21 partially constitute atonement.

**T6.5.1 — Distinctions — Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — **M11-B Divine forgiveness** is the most closely resembling characteristic, and the precise distinction is **mechanism vs declared outcome**. Atonement (M11-A) is the *operative act* — the mediator's covering-mechanism, the life-substitution, the priestly intercession, the Christic surrender. Forgiveness (M11-B) is the *declared release* — the speech-act / divine-disposition naming the offerer as no-longer-bearing-the-guilt. They co-occur in the canonical Levitical formula (Lev 4:20 et al.) precisely because they are two stages of one arc: atonement made → forgiveness declared. They are causally linked (T6.3.1) but distinguishable: an atonement could in principle occur without the declaration (the act would be unrecognized); a forgiveness without atonement would be the negative-pole sealed-condition (Isa 22:14 atonement-denied, but the inner state is the absence of even the possibility of forgiveness). The distinction is operative-act vs verbal-outcome.

---

**T6.5.2 — Distinctions — Where the evidence shows apparent overlap, what is the precise boundary?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Apparent overlap with M11-B is sharpest at the Levitical post-atonement declarations (Lev 4:20 "and the priest shall make atonement for them, and they shall be forgiven"). The precise boundary: the kip.per verb and the sa.lach verb operate on different objects. Kip.per operates on the *guilt* (the inner-being condition); sa.lach operates on the *offender's standing*. The two are sequential — guilt is covered (kip.per), then the offender's standing is released (sa.lach). The boundary line runs at the *object* of the operation: atonement covers a condition; forgiveness releases a standing. Apparent overlap with M11-C (Release) — particularly via afiēmi-permit (G0863I) where 2 verses sit in M11-A and 16 in M11-C — was resolved at B.2 §3.3 (per-verse split). The boundary: G0863I in atonement-relational content (Mat 27:50, Joh 18:8 self-substitutive surrender) is M11-A; G0863I in permission/volition register (16 verses) is M11-C. The principle: lexical co-incidence is not analytical identity.

---

**T6.5.3 — Distinctions — Is the distinction between this characteristic and its nearest neighbour one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The M11-A / M11-B distinction is principally **of kind** (act vs declaration), with **directional** and **constitutional-level** elements supplementary. (i) **Of kind:** atonement is an operative mediating act; forgiveness is a declarative pronouncement of released standing. These are categorically distinct kinds of inner-being phenomena — an *act* of covering vs a *speech-act* (or divine inner-disposition) of releasing. (ii) **Of direction:** atonement is offerer-mediator-toward-offended-party; forgiveness is offended-party-toward-offender (declared release). The directions are inverse (M11-A flows toward God; M11-B flows from God). (iii) **Of constitutional level:** atonement operates more deeply on the inner-being condition (Lev 17:11 soul-level guilt covered); forgiveness operates more on the standing-state (Lev 4:20 declared forgiven). Both engage the conscience, but atonement at the lodged-guilt level and forgiveness at the declared-standing level. The composite distinction (kind + direction + level) clarifies why they are distinct characteristics even within one cluster.
