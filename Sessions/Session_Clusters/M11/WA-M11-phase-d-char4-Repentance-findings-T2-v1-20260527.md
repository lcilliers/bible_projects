# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T2 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-D Repentance and turning (char_seq=4) — 31 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T2.1.1 Spirit-Level** **[CHAR-4]** E — Spirit-level engaged in human contrition (Job 42:6 the spirit-level self-abasement; the breaking-of-spirit pattern). Divine relenting may involve spirit-level disposition (Joe 2:13 character-grounded).

**T2.1.2** **[CHAR-4]** S — Not principally spirit-level; principally heart-and-will level.

**T2.1.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Where engaged, spirit-level reveals turning's depth — Job 42:6 is whole-person, spirit-included.

**T2.1.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Most M11-D verses operate at heart/will-level rather than naming spirit explicitly.

---

**T2.2.1 Soul-Level** **[CHAR-4]** E — Soul-level operation explicit: 1Ki 8:47–48 "heart turning"; 2Ch 6:37 "heart turning, confession voiced"; Job 42:6 "self-abasement" at whole-person level. The soul-level depth varies but is real.

**T2.2.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Soul-level reveals turning's reach into inner-being; the turning is not surface but whole-person re-orientation.

**T2.2.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Soul-language not always explicit but inferred.

---

**T2.3.1 Heart** **[CHAR-4]** E — Heart-level is **primary** for M11-D. (i) Solomon's prayer corpus: 1Ki 8:47 "heart turning"; 2Ch 6:37 same. (ii) Deu 30:1 "heart recalling"; Deu 4:39 "lay it to your heart"; (iii) Lam 3:21 "memory recalled... mind redirected" — heart-mind integrated. (iv) Divine heart-knowledge of human heart enables just relenting (1Ki 8:39 cross-evidence). Heart is M11-D's principal soul-faculty.

**T2.3.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Heart engages **willing** (the will to turn), **moral awareness** (recognition of misalignment), **memory** (recall of covenant), and **feeling** (contrition / regret). All four heart-functions are engaged.

**T2.3.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Heart-engagement is dense.

---

**T2.4.1 Mind** **[CHAR-4]** E — Mind-engagement: Deu 30:1 "memory activating"; Isa 46:8 "memory summoned, mind recalled"; Lam 3:21 "memory recalled, mind redirected"; Deu 4:39 "mind recalling." The shuv-recall family particularly engages the mind / memory faculty for the act-of-turning.

**T2.4.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Mind engages **memory** (covenant-recall) and **discernment** (Mat 4:23 / 2Ch 7:14 cross-evidence). The cognitive-grasp of what is being turned-from is essential.

**T2.4.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Mind not as principal as heart, but significantly engaged.

---

**T2.5.1 Other Soul Subsets** **[CHAR-4]** E — Will engaged (the volitional turn); conscience engaged (recognition of sin); memory engaged (covenant recall); humility engaged (Job 42:6 self-abasement-as-soul-subset).

**T2.5.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Multiple soul-subsets together — turning is whole-soul.

**T2.5.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — None notable.

---

**T2.6.1 Body — Significance** **[CHAR-4]** E — Body engaged in **embodied contrition**: Job 42:6 "dust and ashes" — bodily-physical posture of self-abasement; Jer 31:19 contrition with somatic resonance. The shuv-act often has bodily-directional content (literally turning or returning physically as well as inwardly).

**T2.6.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Function: **expressive** (the body expresses the inner turning) and **embodied** (the act includes physical components — dust and ashes; the disciples' physical turning).

**T2.6.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Body-engagement is present but not centrally mechanical (vs M11-A's blood-mediation).

---

**T2.7.1 Body — Direction** **[CHAR-4]** E — Direction: **soul-to-body** (inner-turning expressed in bodily-posture, Job 42:6); body-to-soul reciprocal (the bodily act of dust-and-ashes deepens the inner contrition). Both directions evidenced.

**T2.7.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Reveals turning as whole-person — inner-and-outer integrated. Embodied turning (Job 42:6) is more constitutive than for M11-B but less than for M11-A.

**T2.7.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Body-direction is evidenced.

---

**T2.8.1 Body — Deposit** **[CHAR-4]** S — No bodily-DNA deposit from sustained repentance. Soul-level deposit: humility-as-character; turned-orientation; covenant-memory.

**T2.8.2** **[CHAR-4]** S — Consistent with M11-A/B/C T2.8 finding.

**T2.8.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Feeds T5.7.

---

**T2.9.1 Origin and Source** **[CHAR-4]** E — Human turning originates in the human person responding to divine call (Isa 46:8 command to transgressors); but the *capacity* and *prompting* are divinely-bestowed (Act 5:31 "repentance gifted... neither originates in the human will alone but are granted through divine exaltation of the Savior"). Divine relenting originates in God's character (Joe 2:13).

**T2.9.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Origin is **dual** in a way M11-A/B/C are not: the human-act has human-volitional origin AND divine-prompting / divine-grace upstream. The Act 5:31 cross-evidence (repentance and forgiveness paired-gifts from above) is structurally important.

**T2.9.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Origin shifts across contexts (institutional, prophetic-personal, eschatological), but the divine-prompted + human-volitional dual structure is consistent.

---

**T2.10.1 Constitutional Movement** **[CHAR-4]** E — Movement: **memory-recall → recognition-of-misalignment → contrite affect → volitional-turn → embodied-posture → restored-standing**. The full sequence is rare in single verses but the canonical pattern is reconstructed across the corpus (Deu 30:1 → 1Ki 8:47 → 2Ch 7:14 → Job 42:6 → Lam 3:21).

**T2.10.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — The sequence is **mind-and-memory-rooted** (vs M11-A's body-mediated; M11-B's word-act-mediated; M11-C's volitional-let-go). Each cluster characteristic has its distinctive sequence-shape.

**T2.10.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.
