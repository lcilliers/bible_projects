**T5.1.1 — Nature of Transformation — Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person — and if so, does it change the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement transforms **both** the person's condition AND their orientation to that condition. (i) **Condition-change:** Lev 14:20 the formerly defiled person is declared clean — actual condition-shift from defiled to clean; Lev 4:20 the offerer's standing changes from guilty-uncovered to forgiven; Lev 16:30 inner cleansing from all sins; the cleansed body re-enters community (Lev 12–15 corpus). (ii) **Orientation-change:** Eze 16:63 atonement produces lasting confounded humility — the recipient's orientation to their own moral history is reframed; the felt-shame is internalized as ongoing self-knowledge in light of unmerited cover. Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness atone) names the orientation-change as character-formation — the inner disposition of the person is reshaped over time. (iii) **Both together:** Lev 16:34 (annual statute) institutionally transforms both condition (recurring cleansing) and orientation (the community's sustained posture of approach-needing-cover). The transformation is dual; the corpus does not depict atonement as merely changing standing without inner re-orientation, nor as changing only inner disposition without addressing the condition.

---

**T5.1.2 — Nature of Transformation — Is the transformation produced by this characteristic reversible or irreversible in the verse evidence?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The transformation is **partially reversible at the OT institutional level** and **irreversible at the Christic level**. (i) **OT-institutional partial reversibility:** the very existence of Lev 16:34 (annual permanent statute) and the Lev 4–7 recurring pattern signal that atonement's effect requires recurring renewal — the cleansed state is not once-for-all but operates within a cycle; subsequent sin re-defiles and requires fresh atonement. The transformation produced by any single atonement is real but bounded by the next moral failure. (ii) **Christic irreversibility:** Mat 27:50 + Heb 10:18 (M11-C-VCG-01 "where forgiveness is fully achieved, no further sacrifice is needed — the completeness of guilt's release means the inner conscience no longer requires repeated ritual cleansing") signal that the Christic atonement produces an irreversible transformation that the Levitical recurrence anticipated but did not produce. (iii) **Memory-irreversibility:** Eze 16:63's lasting confounded humility is irreversible — once experienced, the memory persists. The pattern: condition-irreversibility was OT-partial / NT-full; orientation-irreversibility is enduring at the memory level across both Testaments.

**T5.1.3 — Nature of Transformation — If the evidence is silent on transformation, note this explicitly.**

Where ongoing transformation appears (Eze 16:63 lasting humility; Pro 16:6 character-formation; Lev 16:34 annual cycle), it is at extraordinary moments or in summary-statements rather than narrated through individual offerer's arcs.

**T5.2.1 — Sequence of Inner States — Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person — a before, during, and after?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes, the 4-stage sequence identified at T2.10.2 is structurally present and operates at the inner-state level. **Before:** guilt acknowledged / defilement recognized / standing-strained (Lev 4:35 sin he hath sinned; Lev 14:18 prior defilement; Eze 16:63 past unfaithfulness). **During:** the mediated act in progress — offerer brings, priest mediates, blood applied (Lev 1:4; Lev 17:11; Lev 16:24); the recipient's posture during the act is humble-yielded (Lev 23:28 work-cease). **After-immediate:** declared cleansing, forgiveness pronounced, conscience cleared (Lev 4:20 "the inner burden of guilt is lifted"; Lev 14:20 declared clean; Eze 16:63 silenced humility as immediate response). **After-sustained:** ongoing confounded humility, restored capacity for proximity, memory-deposit (Eze 16:63 lasting confounded humility; Exo 30:16 sustained remembrance). The inner-state sequence is: weighed-guilt → yielded-participation → released-cleansed → settled-humble-standing.

---

**T5.2.2 — Sequence of Inner States — What are those states, and what does the sequence reveal about how this characteristic works?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The sequence reveals atonement as **gravitationally-progressive** — the inner states move the person through deepening engagement rather than skipping any phase. (i) **Weighed-guilt** is the entry-state — the person must enter the act bearing the felt-weight of what requires cover; bypass of this state (the hardened-conscience case in VCG-08) prevents reception. (ii) **Yielded-participation** is the during-state — the work-cease posture (Lev 23:28) and the hand-laying identification (Lev 1:4) and the cessation of self-striving — the recipient yields to the divinely-instituted mechanism. (iii) **Released-cleansed** is the immediate-after — the structural shift from guilt-bearing to standing-restored happens at the moment of priestly declaration ("he shall be forgiven"). (iv) **Settled-humble-standing** is the sustained-after — the recipient now lives in the awareness of unmerited cover; humility is not a passing affect but a settled disposition. The sequence's lesson: atonement works *through* the inner-state progression, not around it. Skipping the entry-state produces VCG-08 sealed-condition; skipping the during-state produces self-supplied false-cover (Isa 28:18); skipping the sustained-state produces presumption — taking-grace-for-granted, which the corpus never sanctions.

**T5.2.3 — Sequence of Inner States — If the evidence is silent on sequence, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Sequence is evidenced (T5.2.1–T5.2.2). The corpus is partially silent on the **temporal duration** of the sequence — the verses do not specify how long the offerer remains in "weighed-guilt" before entering the priestly mediation, nor how the "settled-humble-standing" stabilizes over time. Duration is left to the offerer's own moral-experiential trajectory. The corpus structures the sequence-shape without specifying its tempo.

**T5.3.1 — Mechanism of Change — What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change in the person — discipline, encounter, gradual formation, sudden transformation, or something else?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement uses a composite mechanism of **structured encounter + life-substitution + declarative pronouncement**, varying across registers. (i) **Structured encounter:** the divinely-instituted ritual provides the form within which the encounter occurs (Lev 16 DoA; Lev 4–7 sin offerings; Exo 29–30 priestly ordinances). The encounter is structured, not spontaneous. (ii) **Life-substitution:** Lev 17:11 is the mechanism statement — life-for-life is the actual operative substance; the substitute's life covers the offerer's guilt-bearing life. The substitution is concrete, not metaphorical. (iii) **Declarative pronouncement:** the priestly declaration "he shall be forgiven" (Lev 4:20 et al.) is the speech-act that recognizes the completed mechanism — the declaration does not create the cover but seals it as recognized. (iv) **Sudden transformation in crisis cases:** Num 16:46–47 the urgency-pattern produces immediate-effect — wrath arrested mid-execution; Christic-atonement (Mat 27:50) is the maximally-sudden moment. (v) **Gradual formation in inner-character cases:** Pro 16:6 (hesed + emet as atoning disposition) operates through gradual character-formation rather than discrete ritual moment. The mechanism is therefore polymorphic across contexts, holding consistent in its life-substitution core.

---

**T5.3.2 — Mechanism of Change — Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — the corpus distinguishes mechanisms by context. (i) **Routine institutional context** — structured ritual mechanism (Lev 4–7; Lev 14:18–31 cleansing graduated provisions for poverty). (ii) **Day of Atonement** — comprehensive concentric-circle mechanism (Lev 16:6 → 16:11 → 16:16 → 16:24 → 16:33 stepwise expansion of scope: priest's self → household → sanctuary → people → assembly). (iii) **Crisis** — urgent interpositional mechanism (Num 16:46–47 incense in the dying assembly; Num 25:13 zeal-act). (iv) **Divine-self-atonement** — direct mechanism (Psa 65:3 God himself acts; Isa 6:7 seraph touch). (v) **Inner-character** — gradual-formation mechanism (Pro 16:6 disposition; Gen 32:20 calculated strategy). (vi) **Christic** — fully-integrated mechanism (Mat 27:50 willed surrender + bodily death + spirit-released — the mechanism collapses ritual/crisis/character distinctions into a single act). The corpus is consistent in its life-substitution-and-mediation core while flexible in form.

---

**T5.3.3 — Mechanism of Change — If the evidence is silent on mechanism, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Mechanism is densely evidenced (T5.3.1–T5.3.2). The corpus is partially silent on the *underlying ontology* of why life-for-life works — Lev 17:11 states the divine appointment but does not provide the metaphysical explanation; the principle is accepted as revealed. This silence is theologically significant — it positions atonement's mechanism as divinely-given rather than human-derivable.

**T5.4.1 — Suffering and Affliction — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering or affliction — as a response to it, a product of it, or a context for it?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement operates with suffering in three distinct relational modes. (i) **Mediator suffering:** the priest bears the weight of guilt in solitude (Lev 16:17 "the priest bears the weight of guilt for himself, his household, and the whole assembly, with no other human presence allowed"); this is mediator-suffering as integral to the act, not as side-effect. (ii) **Atonement as response to affliction:** Lev 12–15 the cleansing-atonement corpus addresses bodily affliction (childbirth, skin disease, discharges); Num 6:11 corpse-contact defilement; in these cases atonement is the divinely-given response to the affliction's residue. (iii) **Atonement as the agent that ends affliction:** Num 16:46–47 the plague-affliction is halted by Aaron's interposing atonement; Lev 16 DoA addresses accumulated guilt that would otherwise produce affliction. (iv) **Christic maximal-suffering:** Mat 27:50 the atoning act IS suffering — the surrender of life in agony. (v) **Pro 16:14** the wise person averts the king's wrath which could produce suffering for others. Suffering is integral to atonement's structure — borne by the mediator, addressed at its source, or averted by the act.

---

**T5.4.2 — Suffering and Affliction — Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce this characteristic in the person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Suffering **deepens and reveals** atonement-related character in the mediator and the recipient. (i) **Deepens in mediator:** Lev 16:17 priestly solitude during DoA mediation is depth-producing — the priest's office is deepened by the weight repeatedly borne; Phinehas's zeal-act (Num 25:13) and its eternal-covenant reward shape his role permanently. (ii) **Reveals in recipient:** Eze 16:63 the lasting confounded humility revealed by having received cover despite past unfaithfulness — the suffering of recognized unworthiness produces the deeper inner-being knowledge. (iii) **Produces in inner-character cases:** suffering through guilt produces the inner-disposition pattern of seeking-atonement; Job 42:6 (M11-D contrition) names the post-suffering self-abasement. (iv) **Tests in negative cases:** the negative pole (Isa 28:18 false-covering exposed) tests whether the offerer's prior suffering-of-guilt was real — exposed false-covenants reveal that the suffering was self-deceived. Suffering plays a generative role across atonement's registers without being a precondition for every instance.

**[CHAR-1]** S — Evidence on the atonement–suffering relationship is rich (T5.4.1–T5.4.2); no significant silence.

**T5.5.1 — Formation and Sanctification — Does the verse evidence show this characteristic participating in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification — shaping the person over time toward greater likeness?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — this is the T3.10.3 finding crystallized at T5. (i) **Priestly formation:** Lev 8:34 the daily ordination atonement during consecration is explicitly formational — the priest is being shaped through repeated atonement-engagement; Lev 9:7 the order of self-then-people atonement reflects formed-priestly-discipline; Exo 29:33–30:16 the priestly atonement-ordinances institute a formation-pattern. (ii) **Recipient formation:** Lev 16:34 (annual recurrence) embeds atonement in the recipient community's formational cycle — the people are shaped over time by recurring engagement with cover-need-and-cover-supplied. Eze 16:63 the lasting humility is a formational outcome — character shaped toward humility by the experience of unmerited cover. (iii) **Inner-character formation:** Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness as inner-character) names atonement-as-character-formation explicitly. (iv) **Universal-Christic formation:** Mat 27:50 + the broader NT trajectory positions the Christic atonement as the formative ground of all subsequent Christian sanctification (this is more developed in M11-C-VCG-01 NT corpus). Atonement is constitutively formational.

**T5.5.2 — Formation and Sanctification — What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement plays a **foundational and recurring** role in the formational arc. (i) **Foundational:** the person cannot enter or remain in covenant standing without atonement (Lev 4–7; Lev 16); standing itself is the foundation on which other character-formation builds. Without cleansed standing, the formational arc cannot begin. (ii) **Recurring:** the annual statute (Lev 16:34) means atonement is not a one-time foundation that is laid and left — it is repeatedly re-laid as the foundation that the formational arc continues to need; the formational arc requires sustained renewal. (iii) **Sanctifying-orientation:** Pro 16:6 the inner-character-as-atoning is the maturest formational outcome — the formed person now lives in the atoning mode toward others; sanctification produces atoning agents. (iv) **Eschatological-completion:** Dan 9:24 atonement contributes to the cosmic-resolution endpoint of the formational arc; Mat 27:50 + Heb 10:18 the Christic atonement completes what the recurring OT atonement could not. The formational role is therefore foundation-and-renewal in the present, with eschatological-completion as the trajectory.

**T5.6.1 — Eschatological Trajectory — Does the verse evidence point this characteristic toward an eschatological fullness — a future state toward which its present operation is oriented?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement's eschatological trajectory is explicitly evidenced. (i) **Dan 9:24 cosmic resolution:** "atonement for iniquity is one element of a cosmic resolution that ends transgression and sin permanently" — the eschatological endpoint is named. (ii) **Recurring annual statute as anticipatory:** Lev 16:34 the perpetual annual statute structurally anticipates a non-recurring final resolution — recurrence itself is the eschatological signal. (iii) **Eze 16:63 final atonement-reception:** the eschatological atonement produces "lasting confounded humility" — the future inner-being state of the redeemed; the verse positions atonement at the new-covenant restoration moment. (iv) **Christic culmination:** Mat 27:50 the willed surrender at the cross is the eschatologically-decisive atoning act — the eschatological fullness has broken into history. (v) **Cosmic-restoration outcome:** Deu 32:43 land-cleansing as eschatological; the broader Col 1:20 (M11-E) cosmic reconciliation as M11-A's eschatological fruit. (vi) **Eze 45:15/17 millennial-context:** the prince's atoning ordinances in the future temple vision. The trajectory points from present-recurring to future-final.

**T5.6.2 — Eschatological Trajectory — What does the present experience of this characteristic anticipate about its future fullness?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The present experience of atonement anticipates four features of its future fullness. (i) **Cessation of recurrence:** the present annual recurrence (Lev 16:34) anticipates a final-once-for-all atonement; Heb 10:18 (M11-C-VCG-01) makes explicit that the Christic atonement is the once-for-all act. (ii) **Comprehensive scope:** the present concentric-circle scope (Lev 16:33 sanctuary/altar/priests/people) anticipates the cosmic scope of the final act (Dan 9:24; Mat 27:50 → Col 1:20). (iii) **Internal deepening:** the present cleansed conscience (Lev 16:30) anticipates Heb 10:18's once-for-all cleansed conscience — the inner state stabilized. (iv) **Lasting humility:** Eze 16:63's confounded humility is presented as the *eschatological* response to atonement-received — anticipating the inner state of the redeemed in the new covenant fullness. The present experience is therefore typologically-shaped to anticipate what is coming; the experience itself is a foretaste with shape-isomorphism to the fullness.

**T5.7.2 — Deposit Consequence — Does the evidence indicate generational consequence — a deposit carried forward beyond the individual?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Generational consequence is partially evidenced, with structural complexity. (i) **Negative pole generational consequence** is explicit: 2Ki 24:4 (M11-B) "the shedding of innocent blood accumulates guilt so severe that forgiveness is withheld entirely"; Deu 29:20 wilful self-deception's consequences extending generationally; the atonement-denied pattern carries forward. (ii) **Institutional generational consequence (positive):** Lev 16:34 the annual statute embeds atonement in *generational* practice — each generation receives the inherited institution; Exo 30:16 the remembrance is generationally-maintained; the Phinehas covenant (Num 25:13) "perpetual covenant" passes the atoning-zeal role across generations. (iii) **Christic generational consequence:** Mat 27:50's atoning death generates the new covenant whose effects propagate generationally (the broader NT trajectory; M11-C-VCG-01 NT corpus). The positive generational consequence is therefore institutional-and-covenantal rather than bodily-genetic — it is the practice-and-memory of atonement that is generationally borne, not a constitutional bodily change.

**T5.7.3 — Deposit Consequence — If T2.8 found no deposit, note this explicitly and close T5.7.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Per T2.8.1–T2.8.3, the M11-A corpus does not evidence a bodily/DNA-level constitutional deposit from sustained atonement. T5.7 closes with confirmation: deposit consequence operates at soul / memory / character / institutional levels, not at body / DNA / genetic levels.
