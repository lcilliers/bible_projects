# M11 Phase D — Char 3 (Release) — T4 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-C Release (char_seq=3) — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T4.1.1 Divine Interface — God to Human** **[CHAR-3]** E — God-to-human strongly evidenced in sin-release (VCG-01: Mat 26:28; Eph 1:7; Heb 9:22; Col 1:14; Act 13:38), Christic-permission acts (VCG-08: Mar 10:14 Christ's welcome), and eschatological-tear-wiping (VCG-10: Rev 7:17, Rev 21:4 "God will wipe every tear"). The God-to-human register is most explicit at the M11-primary sub-VCGs.

**T4.1.2** **[CHAR-3]** E — Basis is covenantal (Mat 26:28 new-covenant blood; Eph 1:7 redemption through grace), eschatological (Rev 21:4 final-restoration character), and Christic-mediated (Mat 26:28 / Heb 9:22 the cross is the mechanism). For VCG-08 welcoming-permission, the basis is Christ's kingdom-disposition.

**T4.1.3** **[CHAR-3]** E — Divine extension reveals patient, restorative, welcoming character. Rev 21:4 in particular reveals God's character as one who completes consolation — "every tear, mourning, crying, and pain" — comprehensive in restorative scope.

**T4.1.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — The broader-release registers (VCG-03 discipleship; VCG-06 eschatological-separation) are silent on God-to-human direction — these are not principally God's act of releasing-toward but human-acts-of-leaving or divine-passive-acts-of-separation.

---

**T4.2.1 Divine Interface — Human to God** **[CHAR-3]** E — Human-to-God movement in M11-C: the discipleship-leaving (VCG-03) is principally human-toward-God movement — leaving competing attachments to follow Christ; this is a major human-to-divine direction. Sin-release receiving (Act 2:38, Act 10:43) involves human-to-God movement of faith and repentance.

**T4.2.2** **[CHAR-3]** E — Required posture: willing-to-leave, faith-to-receive, openness-to-be-welcomed. The discipleship-leaving cases (Mat 4:20–22 immediate response) name the inner posture as decisive-and-immediate.

**T4.2.3** **[CHAR-3]** E — Direction reveals the relationship as one of priority-restructuring — humans move toward God by letting-go of competing attachments. The discipleship-leaving pattern is the most-explicit M11-C content on relational direction.

**T4.2.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — VCG-09 (permission-withheld) is not principally about human-to-God movement.

---

**T4.3.1 Human Interface — Giving** **[CHAR-3]** E — Human extending release: VCG-04 reconciliation-prioritizing (Mat 5:24 leaving the gift to be reconciled — human extends the release of grievance); VCG-08 welcoming-permission (humans permit children to come; Act 5:38 Gamaliel non-interference); Joh 20:23 (delegated forgiveness-of-sins authority); Mat 5:40 non-resistance generosity.

**T4.3.2** **[CHAR-3]** E — Inner conditions: willingness-to-let-go (release of grievance, attachment, or possession); humility (welcoming children-as-paradigm); non-resistance (Mat 5:40); wisdom-of-discernment (Act 5:38 wait-and-see).

**T4.3.3** **[CHAR-3]** E — Likely depends on having-been-welcomed-into-the-kingdom (receiving precedes extending); the parallel to M11-B T4.3.3 holds — character-formation enables character-extending.

**T4.3.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — Sufficient evidence.

---

**T4.4.1 Human Interface — Receiving** **[CHAR-3]** E — Receiving evidenced in: VCG-08 (children-welcomed); the disciples being welcomed by Christ at calling-moment (Mat 4:20); the recipients of forgiveness via human-extender (Joh 20:23 delegated authority means there is a human-receiving direction). Tear-wiping recipients (Rev 7:17 "those who came through tribulation").

**T4.4.2** **[CHAR-3]** E — Enabled by openness, willingness to be received, humble posture. Blocked by self-exclusion (Mat 23:13 those who block others self-exclude); blocked by clinging to what should be released (Mat 19:22 adjacent register).

**T4.4.3** **[CHAR-3]** E — Non-receiving state varies: for sin-release foreclosed (Mar 3:29 sealed condition); for welcome refused (Mat 23:13 active blocking). The inner-state is closed-against-let-go.

**T4.4.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — Sufficient.

**T4.5.1 Human Interface — Boundaries** **[CHAR-3]** E — Boundary crossing is significant. Sin-release (VCG-01) extends universally through Christ (Act 10:43 "every one that believeth"). Welcoming-permission (VCG-08) crosses age (children) and class (Act 5:38 Gamaliel's tolerance). Discipleship-leaving (VCG-03) calls across previous-attachment boundaries.

**T4.5.2** **[CHAR-3]** E — Operates within covenantal frame (Mat 26:28 new-covenant) and across covenantal boundaries (Act 10:43 universal availability through faith). Welcoming-permission operates across status / age / nationality boundaries.

**T4.5.3** **[CHAR-3]** E — Included: all who believe (Act 10:43); the welcomed (children, M11-C-VCG-08 paradigm); the redeemed (Rev 7:17). Excluded: sustained-sealed (Mar 3:29); those who block others (Mat 23:13 self-excluded); those who refuse to receive.

**T4.5.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — Sufficient.

**T4.6.3** **[CHAR-3]** S — Angelic ministry of release is not directly evidenced.

**T4.6.4** **[CHAR-3]** S — Partial evidence at the demon-restraint and Spirit-rejection points; principal frame remains God-human-mediator.
