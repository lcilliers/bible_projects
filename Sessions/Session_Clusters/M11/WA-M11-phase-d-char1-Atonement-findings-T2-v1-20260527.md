# M11 Phase D — Char 1 (Atonement) — T2 findings

**Cluster:** M11 — Repentance, Forgiveness and Restoration
**Characteristic:** M11-A Atonement (char_seq=1)
**Tier:** T2 — Constitutional Location (31 prompts)
**Authored:** 2026-05-27
**Governing instruction:** [`wa-sessionb-cluster-instruction-v3_0-20260527.md`](../../../Workflow/Instructions/wa-sessionb-cluster-instruction-v3_0-20260527.md) §8
**Sub-group & VCG context:** [`WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md`](WA-M11-subgroup-vcg-verses-review-v1-20260527.md) §M11-A

**Tier framing note:** T2 asks where in the person atonement sits constitutionally. Atonement is structurally unusual — it is an *act done to or for a person* rather than a *faculty in the person*. The findings therefore locate (a) the inner-being state being addressed (typically guilt at the soul / conscience level), (b) the recipient-level effects (across spirit / soul / heart / mind / body), and (c) the mediator's operative location (priestly office; God's character; Christ's whole-person surrender).

---

**T2.1.1 — Spirit-Level Location — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Direct spirit-level location is evidenced in two M11-A verses. Mat 27:50 (M11-A-VCG-10) names Christ's atoning act as the voluntary release of his **spirit** at death — the atoning surrender is constituted at the spirit level. Joh 18:8 (M11-A-VCG-10) parallels with the self-substitution act of an inner-being agency that is whole-person but operates from the spirit-volitional core. Outside these two NT verses, the M11-A corpus does not name "spirit" explicitly as the location of atonement; the OT Levitical verses operate at the conscience / soul / ritual-body register without naming "spirit" as such. Spirit-level location is therefore evidenced but limited to the christological-NT moment.

---

**T2.1.2 — Spirit-Level Location — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The evidence does not indicate that atonement is *primarily* a spirit-level phenomenon. Where spirit-language appears (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8) it names the agent's whole-person surrender, not a localized spirit-faculty operation. The majority of M11-A operates at the soul / conscience / standing-relational level (Lev 17:11 "guilt lodged in the soul"; Lev 16:30 "inner cleansing"). Atonement *engages* the spirit (the christological case) and *involves* spirit-level surrender (the agent's whole-person commitment) but is not *primarily* spirit-level. Its primary location is the soul-conscience region where guilt resides and standing is restored.

---

**T2.1.3 — Spirit-Level Location — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Where atonement enters the spirit register (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8), it reveals atonement at its maximal depth — the act is whole-person, irreducible, and definitive. The release of the spirit is the deepest possible act of self-giving; nothing remains held back. This depth-marking is structurally important: it signals that atonement reaches as deep as the agent's spirit-level commitment, which is why the Levitical atonement's lesser depths (animal-substitute, ritual-mediated) operate as types pointing to the spirit-level fullness. The depth disclosed at the spirit level is the depth toward which all the lesser instances point.

---

**T2.1.4 — Spirit-Level Location — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The OT Levitical corpus (Lev 4–7, 16, 19, Num 5, 6, 8, 15, 16, 25, 28–31; ~70 verses) is silent on spirit-level location. These verses operate at the conscience / soul / ritual-body register. The silence is structurally consistent with the OT framework's tendency not to use "spirit" terminology for individual inner-being location (where it does, it is typically the divine spirit moving on a person, not an individual's own spirit faculty). The silence does not weaken the spirit-level evidence at the christological-NT moment (T2.1.1) — it situates that evidence as a deepening rather than a re-localization.

---

**T2.2.1 — Soul-Level Location — Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes. Lev 17:11 is the most explicit verse in M11-A: "guilt lodged in the **soul** is covered by the substitution of one life given for another." The Hebrew nephesh stands behind both the location of guilt and the substance of the life-substitute (the animal's life-nephesh covers the human's guilt-bearing nephesh). The verse names atonement's locational target as the soul. This is corroborated by Lev 16:30 (DoA atonement "achieves inner cleansing from all sins") — though the language is "inner" rather than "soul" explicitly, the depth and totality language tracks with soul-level. The atonement act is therefore explicitly soul-targeting at its central verse.

---

**T2.2.2 — Soul-Level Location — What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Soul-level location reveals atonement as addressing the deepest personal-experience seat of guilt and standing. Three implications follow from Lev 17:11's logic. (i) **Guilt is itself soul-lodged** — not merely a record kept externally; the offender's inner-being is the place where guilt resides. (ii) **Soul-level guilt requires soul-level substitution** — life-for-life because guilt is at the life-level, not at the act-level. (iii) **Atonement's reach is to the seat of the person** — what is covered is not the surface but the constitutional core. The recipient's innermost personal experience is therefore both the place where the unaddressed guilt was weighing and the place where the felt cleansing arrives (Lev 16:30 "conscience cleared"). Eze 16:63 confirms the depth: atonement received produces lasting confounded humility, which is soul-level reorientation.

---

**T2.2.3 — Soul-Level Location — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Soul-level location is not silent — Lev 17:11 is the explicit anchor. The remaining ~80 M11-A verses operate consistently with soul-level location even where the term "soul" is not used (they address guilt as inner-state, cover-as-inner-cleansing, etc.). No silence to note here; the evidence at the soul level is among the strongest in the M11-A corpus.

---

**T2.3.1 — Heart — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Heart-level location is evidenced in M11-A through two registers. (i) **Heart-level inner moral disposition as atoning mechanism** — Pro 16:6 "iniquity is atoned for through steadfast love and faithfulness" — the inner-character qualities are heart-seated, and they function as the atoning mechanism. (ii) **Heart as the seat of the inner state being addressed** — Eze 16:63 "atonement produces shame and silencing in the inner person — the experience of being covered despite past unfaithfulness closes the mouth and opens the memory to lasting confounded humility" — the felt response is heart-located (humility is a heart-condition); Gen 32:20 "the gift sent ahead may soften the other's inner disposition" — atonement-as-appeasement targets the offended party's heart. Heart-location is therefore evidenced at both the mechanism end (Pro 16:6) and the target end (Eze 16:63; Gen 32:20).

---

**T2.3.2 — Heart — What does the heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's integrating function (knowing, willing, feeling, moral awareness) does this characteristic engage?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Heart-location reveals atonement engaging primarily two of the heart's integrating functions: **willing** and **moral awareness**. (i) **Willing:** Pro 16:6 names love + faithfulness — both volitional postures of the heart — as the inner mechanism by which atonement operates; Gen 32:20 frames the strategic gift as an act of will calculated to soften another's will; Num 31:50 names the warriors' inner volitional response (voluntary offering) prompted by guilt-awareness. (ii) **Moral awareness:** Eze 16:63 names the post-atonement state as confounded humility — moral self-knowledge enabled by the unmerited covering; Lev 16:30 inner-cleansing involves a renewed moral conscience; 2Sa 21:3 the king's active seeking of atonement reflects engaged moral awareness. **Feeling** is engaged secondarily (the felt experience of being covered) but is downstream of will and moral awareness. **Knowing** (cognitive grasp) is engaged minimally; atonement does not principally inform the intellect.

---

**T2.3.3 — Heart — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Heart-location is not silent for inner-disposition atonement (Pro 16:6) or for atonement's effect on the recipient (Eze 16:63), but the majority of Levitical verses (Lev 4–7, 12–15 cleansing, Num 15:25–28 unintentional sin) are silent on heart-location explicitly. They operate at the conscience / standing register without naming the heart as such. The silence is consistent with the OT framework's use of "heart" (lev / levav) for moral-volitional integration rather than for the moment of a ritual act's operation. The silence does not weaken Pro 16:6 + Eze 16:63 as heart-level anchors.

---

**T2.4.1 — Mind — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Mind-level engagement is evidenced in a focused but limited way. (i) **Memory activated** — Eze 16:63 the post-atonement state explicitly engages memory: "opens the memory to lasting confounded humility" — the recipient's mind is the place where the act of cover is remembered and continues to operate; Exo 30:16 atonement money "keeps the people in God's remembrance" — atonement maintains a sustained-memory link. (ii) **Cognitive grasp of the mechanism** — Lev 17:11 (life-for-life logic) requires the recipient or offerer to apprehend the substitutionary logic; Heb 9:22 (blood-required-for-release, M11-C) extends this cognitive content into NT theological grasp. (iii) **Moral perception failure (negative pole)** — Isa 28:18 the false-covering exposed implies a prior failure of moral perception; Isa 47:11 inability to atone arises from inner resources exhausted. Mind-level engagement is thus present but operates more as memory-and-recognition than as central seat of the act.

---

**T2.4.2 — Mind — What does mind-location reveal — what aspect of the mind's function (thought, discernment, understanding) does this characteristic engage?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Where atonement engages the mind, the dominant aspect is **understanding through remembered narrative**, not abstract cognition. (i) **Remembrance:** Exo 30:16 and Lev 16:34 (annual statute) embed atonement in mind-level recurrence — the people remember the previous year's atonement and anticipate the next; the mind is the seat of this generational continuity. (ii) **Discernment:** 2Sa 21:3 "guilt from a past wrong compels active searching for the means of reconciliation" — the king's mind discerns that atonement is required; Pro 16:14 (wisdom defuses royal wrath) names atonement-by-prudence as a mind-of-wisdom operation. (iii) **Understanding:** Eze 16:63 "the experience of being covered despite past unfaithfulness... opens the memory to lasting confounded humility" — the recipient understands the depth of unmerited cover at the level of self-knowledge. Mind-engagement is therefore narrative-memorial and discernment-practical, not abstract-doctrinal at the M11-A level (the doctrinal grasp comes more in M11-B / Hebrews NT theology).

---

**T2.4.3 — Mind — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The majority of Levitical atonement verses do not explicitly engage the mind as a location — they describe the act and outcome without naming cognitive operations. The silence is consistent with atonement being primarily act-based (T1.2.1) rather than reflection-based. Where mind-engagement does appear (Eze 16:63; Exo 30:16; 2Sa 21:3), it is at extraordinary or narratively-rich moments. The silence does not weaken those evidenced cases; it situates them as the conscious-engagement pole within a broader act-pattern that operates partly below conscious-cognitive engagement (the ritual's force does not depend on the offerer's cognitive grasp of every detail).

---

**T2.5.1 — Other Soul Subsets — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind for this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — three additional soul-level locations surface. (i) **Conscience (moral-self-awareness):** Lev 16:30 "conscience cleared"; Lev 4:31 "conscience cleared"; the cleansed conscience is a recurrent post-atonement state. The conscience is a soul-faculty distinct from heart-willing or mind-knowing — it is the inner moral monitor. (ii) **Will/volition (will-as-soul-faculty):** Num 31:50 "warriors' inner awareness of guilt prompts voluntary propitiatory action" — the will responds to internal guilt-awareness with volitional act; Mat 27:50 "willed surrender" names the spirit-level act as a will-volitional operation. (iii) **Memory (mnemonic-faculty):** Eze 16:63 names memory engagement explicitly (T2.4.1); Exo 30:16 the corporate remembrance. Memory operates as a soul-subset distinct from intellect. The conscience, will, and memory together are the soul-subsets engaged beyond the standard heart/mind pair.

---

**T2.5.2 — Other Soul Subsets — If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The three additional locations reveal atonement as a *whole-soul operation*, not localized to a single faculty. (i) **Conscience as the seat of cleared guilt** reveals the atonement's primary work-site — what was uncovered (guilt) is now covered, and the moral monitor registers the change as cleansing. (ii) **Will as the seat of the recipient's responsive surrender** reveals atonement as soliciting a corresponding volitional act in the recipient (acknowledged guilt, voluntary offering, surrender to the mechanism). (iii) **Memory as the seat of the sustained effect** reveals atonement's persistence — the cleansing does not fade because the memory holds the act; Eze 16:63 explicitly names lasting confounded humility as a memory-mediated state. The collective revelation: atonement is whole-soul because it must address whole-soul guilt — partial faculties cannot bear soul-level guilt-cover.

---

**T2.5.3 — Other Soul Subsets — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The corpus is not silent on these additional soul-subsets (conscience, will, memory) where it explicitly engages them; the verses cited at T2.5.1 are direct. The corpus IS relatively silent on emotion-as-soul-subset for M11-A — atonement is not depicted as principally producing or operating through emotion (contrast with M03 grief or M04 joy, which are emotion-anchored). The emotion-silence is structurally consistent with T1.5.1 (immediate response is silenced humility, not euphoric relief). Emotion is engaged secondarily but not as a primary soul-subset for atonement.

**T2.6.1 — Body — Significance — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — multiple body-part links are evidenced in M11-A. (i) **Blood:** Lev 17:11 "blood is the divinely appointed means of atonement because life itself resides in it" — blood is *the* body-substance via which atonement operates. (ii) **Hand:** Lev 1:4 "the offerer's hand-laying transfers identification with the animal" — the hand is the body-part of identification-transfer; Lev 16:21 (scapegoat) operates via hand-laying. (iii) **Head:** Lev 14:18, 14:29 (oil on the head of the cleansed person) — the head is the body-part that receives the anointing signifying covered-and-restored standing. (iv) **Bodily-discharge-cleansed body:** Lev 12 (childbirth), Lev 14 (skin disease), Lev 15 (discharges), Num 6:11 (corpse contact) — the body's defiled state is what atonement-cleansing addresses. (v) **Whole body:** Mat 27:50 Christ's bodily death is the atoning act. The body-part links are dense.

---

**T2.6.2 — Body — Significance — If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The body-part links are predominantly **functional-mediating**, with secondary **expressive** and **indicative** registers. (i) **Functional-mediating:** the blood (Lev 17:11) is the actual mechanism of cover — its function is to substitute life-for-life; the body-part is not symbolic but operative. The hand (Lev 1:4) functions as the transfer-instrument of identification. The head-anointing (Lev 14:18) mediates the priestly act on the cleansed body. (ii) **Expressive:** the bodily acts of the priest at the DoA (Lev 16:6, 11, 17, 24) express the office and the gravity of the moment. (iii) **Indicative:** Lev 14:20 the cleansed body indicates the cleansed inner state (the formerly-defiled person is *declared clean*, the body's state being the visible indicator). (iv) **Christic-fully-functional:** Mat 27:50 Christ's bodily death is fully functional-mediating — the atoning act IS the bodily death, not symbolized by it. Scripture is doing serious operational work through the body — not merely metaphorical or pedagogical.

---

**T2.6.3 — Body — Significance — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Body-part links are densely evidenced (T2.6.1); no silence to note here. Within the M11-A corpus, body-part engagement is one of the strongest evidence dimensions — atonement is heavily embodied at every register (animal-blood, priestly-hand, recipient-head, body-defilement, Christic-death).

**T2.7.1 — Body — Direction — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The direction runs **both ways**, with priority on body-to-soul (the body-act effects the soul-state). (i) **Body-to-soul (primary direction):** Lev 17:11 the body-substance (blood) operates *on* the soul-state (guilt); Lev 1:4 the hand-laying *on* the body of the animal effects *the* identification that mediates atonement of soul-guilt; Lev 16:30 the bodily ritual produces "inner cleansing from all sins" — the body-act produces the soul-result. (ii) **Soul-to-body (secondary direction):** the offerer's inner acknowledgment of guilt is expressed through the body-act of bringing the sacrifice (Num 31:50 inner-awareness prompts voluntary bodily offering); Gen 32:20 the inner strategic disposition is expressed through the body-act of sending the gift; Pro 16:6 the inner character of love + faithfulness is expressed through embodied acts that atone. (iii) **Christic-collapsed-direction:** Mat 27:50 Christ's spirit-volitional surrender and his bodily death are inseparable — the directions collapse into one act.

---

**T2.7.2 — Body — Direction — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The bidirectional but body-to-soul-prioritized directionality has three consequences. (i) **Atonement is not a purely inner act** — the embodied mechanism is constitutive. A soul-state of guilt cannot be addressed by a soul-only operation; the body-mediated act is what reaches the soul. This contrasts with M11-D repentance, which is more soul-to-body (inner turning expressed externally). (ii) **The body's involvement is not optional or symbolic** — Lev 17:11 makes this explicit (blood is the appointed means *because* life-substance is in it). The body is the divinely-instituted access point to the soul's guilt-state. (iii) **The Christic moment intensifies this** — by combining whole-person bodily surrender with whole-person spirit-volitional surrender (Mat 27:50), the Christic act becomes the maximally-integrated atonement. The consequence: any theological reading of M11-A that abstracts away from the body is incomplete; atonement is constitutionally embodied.

---

**[CHAR-1]** S — Body-characteristic link is densely evidenced (T2.6.1) and direction is identified (T2.7.1); no silence to note here.

**T2.8.1 — Body — Deposit — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including DNA or generational consequence?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus does not evidence a constitutional bodily deposit (DNA-level or generational) from sustained atonement. The corpus operates at the act-and-outcome level (atonement made → cleansed standing) without describing physical changes that persist beyond the cleansed state itself. The annual recurrence of DoA (Lev 16:34 perpetual statute) implies that atonement does *not* leave a once-for-all bodily deposit at the OT level — the people need it again each year. The closest evidence to a sustained constitutional change is Eze 16:63's lasting humility, but that is soul-level (memory/character) not body/DNA-level. Generational consequence is referenced in M11-B-VCG-04 (forgiveness foreclosed sometimes proceeds generationally — Deu 29:20, 2Ki 24:4) but that is the *negative pole's* generational reach, not atonement's positive constitutional deposit.

---

**T2.8.2 — Body — Deposit — What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — **Contradicting any sustained bodily deposit at the OT level:** the annual recurrence requirement (Lev 16:34) is the strongest evidence that no sustained constitutional bodily change occurs from a single atonement. If it did, recurrence would be unnecessary. **Partially supporting a constitutional change at the NT-Christic level:** Heb 10:18 (M11-C-VCG-01) "where forgiveness is fully achieved, no further sacrifice is needed — the completeness of guilt's release means the inner conscience no longer requires repeated ritual cleansing" — implies the Christic atonement effects a constitutional shift that the Levitical sequence did not. But "the inner conscience" is conscience-state language, not body/DNA. The corpus is silent on the body/DNA dimension as such. **Generational dimension:** present at the negative pole (2Ki 24:4 innocent blood guilt extending generationally) but not at the positive deposit pole for atonement.

---

**T2.8.3 — Body — Deposit — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly. This finding feeds directly into T5.7.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — The M11-A corpus is silent on constitutional bodily/DNA-level deposit from sustained atonement (per T2.8.1, T2.8.2). Recorded for T5.7 (Transformation — Body) consideration: T5.7 should examine whether the corpus elsewhere (e.g. Christic-effects, eschatological-restoration verses) evidences a body-level transformation that completes what M11-A leaves silent here. The corpus's silence on body-deposit is structurally important — it suggests atonement's persistence is borne by memory and standing-state, not by physical change.

**T2.9.1 — Origin and Source — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally — is it generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is **bestowed by God** in its source-origin, **received-from-outside** in the recipient's experience, and **generated-from-within** only in the derivative inner-character cases. (i) **Bestowed by God (primary):** the entire Levitical sacrificial system is divinely instituted (Lev 16 is divine command; Exo 29–30 priestly ordinances); Psa 65:3 / Psa 78:38 / Psa 79:9 / Isa 6:7 name God as direct agent of atonement; Lev 17:11 names blood as *divinely* appointed mechanism. (ii) **Received-from-outside (recipient pole):** the offerer brings an offering (Lev 1:4); the people receive the priestly atonement (Lev 4–6 corpus); Eze 16:63 the recipient experiences cover *despite* their unfaithfulness — the source is unambiguously outside the recipient. (iii) **Generated-from-within (derivative cases only):** Pro 16:6 inner love + faithfulness atone — this is the rare case where atonement-as-character-disposition originates within. But even here the inner character is presumably bestowed-by-God upstream. The dominant origin is divine.

---

**T2.9.2 — Origin and Source — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The origin is structurally singular (divine institution / divine character) with multiple operational channels. **Singular origin:** every M11-A atonement traces ultimately to divine institution — the priestly system, the blood-as-life logic (Lev 17:11), the divine self-atonement initiative (Psa 65:3), the Christic atoning surrender (Mat 27:50). Even inner-character atonement (Pro 16:6) operates because love + faithfulness reflect God's own character. **Multiple operational channels:** the divine origin manifests through priest-mediation (VCG-02), through cleansing-ritual (VCG-03), through crisis-intercession (VCG-05), through national-institutional (VCG-06), through divine direct-act (VCG-07), through inner-character (VCG-09), through Christic-surrender (VCG-10). Each channel is a real operational origin for the recipient's experience but they all trace back to a single divine source. The structure is singular-source + multi-channel.

---

**T2.9.3 — Origin and Source — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts evidenced in Scripture?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The deep origin (divine institution) does *not* change; the proximate operational origin shifts across contexts. (i) **OT institutional context** — proximate origin is the priestly office acting on divine command (Lev 16:32 "consecrated priest is the designated agent"). (ii) **OT crisis context** — proximate origin is the intercessor's urgent act (Exo 32:30 Moses; Num 16:46–47 Aaron; Num 25:13 Phinehas). (iii) **OT direct-divine context** — proximate origin is God himself (Psa 65:3 "God himself acts to atone"). (iv) **NT christological context** — proximate origin is the incarnate Mediator (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8). (v) **Inner-character context** — proximate origin is the person's own hesed + emet (Pro 16:6). The shifts in proximate origin signal a progressive deepening from institutional through direct-divine to internalized-character; the deep origin remains constant. This shift-with-constancy is the typological pattern (T0.4) running through the constitutional dimension.

---

**T2.10.1 — Constitutional Movement — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement evidences constitutional movement in three directions. (i) **Body → soul (primary):** the body-mediated mechanism (blood, hand-laying, anointing) effects soul-state cleansing (Lev 17:11; Lev 16:30 "inner cleansing"). This is the dominant direction. (ii) **Spirit → body (christological):** Mat 27:50 the spirit-level act of volitional release is enacted through bodily death — the spirit's surrender takes embodied form. (iii) **Heart → embodied-act (inner-character):** Pro 16:6 the heart-seated love + faithfulness express themselves as embodied moral conduct that itself atones; Gen 32:20 inner strategic disposition produces embodied gift-act. The constitutional movement is therefore multi-directional, with body-to-soul as the dominant pattern of the corpus and spirit-to-body / heart-to-body as parallel patterns in special cases.

---

**T2.10.2 — Constitutional Movement — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The pattern is: **outer mechanism → inner state → relational standing → memory-deposit**. Worked sequentially: (i) outer mechanism (body-mediated act: blood, hand-laying, sacrifice, surrender) is performed (Lev 1:4; Lev 17:11; Mat 27:50); (ii) inner state of guilt is covered / cleansed (Lev 16:30; Lev 4:20); (iii) relational standing is restored (Lev 14:20 "declared clean"; Lev 4:35 "forgiven"); (iv) memory-deposit is left as the sustained inner-being effect (Eze 16:63 "lasting confounded humility"; Exo 30:16 "remembrance maintained"). This is the canonical sequence and it holds across registers — Levitical, christological, and inner-character. Variation: in inner-character atonement (Pro 16:6) the sequence runs more from heart-disposition → embodied act → other-person's standing-restoration → mutual memory; in crisis atonement (Num 16:46–47) the sequence is compressed and the "memory-deposit" is the covenant story of the act. The pattern is consistent in its four-stage structure even where the proximate origin shifts.

---

**T2.10.3 — Constitutional Movement — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Constitutional movement is evidenced (T2.10.1, T2.10.2); no silence to note here. The corpus is rich in constitutional-movement evidence because atonement's very logic involves crossing from outer-mechanism into inner-state and out into relational-standing.
