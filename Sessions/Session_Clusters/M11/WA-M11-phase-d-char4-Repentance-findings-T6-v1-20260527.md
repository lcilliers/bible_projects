# M11 Phase D — Char 4 (Repentance and turning) — T6 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-D — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T6.1.1 Co-occurrence** **[CHAR-4]** E — Strong co-occurrences: M11-B Forgiveness (turning enables forgiveness — Act 2:38; Mar 4:12; Isa 55:7); M11-A Atonement (turning + atonement combined in some Levitical-prophetic moments); M10 Sin (the misalignment-state turning addresses); M21 Prayer (the human-turning act is often in prayer-form — Solomon temple corpus); M03 Grief (divine grief over human failure — Gen 6:6 cross-register); M05 Compassion (Joe 2:13 — character-grounded relenting); M20 Anxiety (Exo 13:17 resolve-collapse cross-register).

**T6.1.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D is the **inner-act register** of the cluster — the human side's principal contribution. M11-A supplies mechanism; M11-B/C declare release; M11-D is what the human person *does* in the redemptive arc. The pattern places M11-D as the human-agency hinge.

**T6.1.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.

**T6.2.1 Sequential Relationships** **[CHAR-4]** E — M11-D → M11-A/B/C in many sequences: turning → forgiveness (Mar 4:12; Act 2:38); turning → atonement-receiving (offerer's repentant disposition enabling Levitical atonement); turning → release (Act 26:18). M11-D also produces M11-E reconciliation indirectly (turning is the inner act that opens to relational restoration).

**T6.2.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Causal — turning causally enables the downstream characteristics' operative effects.

**T6.2.3** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.

---

**T6.3.1 Causal and Constitutive** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning produces: enabling-conditions for forgiveness (M11-B); enabling-conditions for atonement-reception (M11-A); the inner-state from which reconciliation becomes possible (M11-E); ongoing humility (Eze 16:63-style post-cover humility is M11-D-adjacent).

**T6.3.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning is produced by: divine prompt (Act 5:31 repentance-as-gift); conviction (M10 cross-evidence); affliction (1Ki 8:47); prophetic call (Isa 46:8).

**T6.3.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Turning is constitutive of repentance (the act IS the repentance); constituted by recognition + contrition + volitional-turn elements (T1.2.2).

**T6.3.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.

**T6.4.1 Vocabulary and Root Sharing** **[CHAR-4]** E — Two principal root families: **na.cham** (regret/relent — extends across divine relenting AND human contrition with sense-flexibility); **shuv** (return/recall — used both as physical-return and as inner-turning). The na.cham family is M11-D-distinctive (M11-A doesn't use na.cham). The shuv family extends across M11-D (turning/recall sense) and elsewhere (return-as-restoration verbs). The marginal **ud** (Psa 119:61) is structural-opposite.

**T6.4.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Yes — na.cham root architecture is M11-D-distinctive; shuv-recall architecture is the human-turning core; ud root is the structural-opposite case.

**T6.4.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Vocabulary reveals turning as conceptually-distinct from atonement/forgiveness/release — it is the *human-inner-act* register with its own lexical-architectural identity.

**T6.4.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Sufficient.

**T6.5.1 Distinctions** **[CHAR-4]** E — Closest neighbour: **M11-B Forgiveness** as the response to turning. Distinction: M11-D is the inner-act of the offender; M11-B is the declared-release of the offended-party. They are causally linked but categorically distinct.

**T6.5.2** **[CHAR-4]** E — Boundary: shuv-act (M11-D human inner) vs sa.lach-pronouncement (M11-B divine declaration). Direction inverse (M11-D toward God; M11-B from God).

**T6.5.3** **[CHAR-4]** E — Distinction of **kind** (inner-act vs declared-release), **direction** (inverse), and **agent** (human vs divine).

**T6.5.4** **[CHAR-4]** S — Distinction-work required.
