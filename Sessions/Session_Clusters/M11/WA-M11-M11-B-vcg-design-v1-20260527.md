## §2. VCG definitions

### M11-B-VCG-01 — NT direct declarations of forgiveness on faith

**Context:** Jesus pronounces sins forgiven directly into the inner person, typically in response to faith. The paralytic corpus (Mat 9:2–6, Mar 2:5–10, Luk 5:20–24) and the sinful woman corpus (Luk 7:47–49) demonstrate the Son of Man's earthly authority to forgive sins, with onlookers' challenges that only God can forgive providing the theological frame. Joh 20:23 extends this authority to delegated commissioning.

**Members:** vc=17589 (Luk 7:47), vc=17590 (Mat 9:2), vc=17591 (Mat 9:5), vc=17592 (Mat 9:6), vc=17595 (Mar 2:5), vc=17596 (Mar 2:7), vc=17597 (Mar 2:9), vc=17598 (Mar 2:10), vc=17601 (Luk 5:20), vc=17602 (Luk 5:21), vc=17603 (Luk 5:23), vc=17604 (Luk 5:24), vc=17605 (Luk 7:48), vc=17606 (Luk 7:49), vc=17609 (Joh 20:23) — **15 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17605 (Luk 7:48) — "your sins are forgiven."

### M11-B-VCG-02 — Reciprocal / conditional forgiveness

**Context:** The teaching that forgiving others is the inner condition for receiving divine forgiveness. The Lord's Prayer's "forgive us as we forgive" pattern (Mat 6:12/14/15, Luk 11:4, Mar 11:25), the unforgiving servant parable (Mat 18:21/27/32/35) with its compassion-driving debt-release and its moral about heart-level forgiving, and the Luk 17:3/4 instruction to forgive repeatedly when repentance is offered.

**Members:** vc=17615 (Mat 18:35), vc=17616 (Mat 6:12), vc=17617 (Mat 6:14), vc=17618 (Mat 6:15), vc=17619 (Mat 18:21), vc=17620 (Mat 18:27), vc=17621 (Mat 18:32), vc=17622 (Mar 11:25), vc=17623 (Luk 11:4), vc=17624 (Luk 17:3), vc=17625 (Luk 17:4) — **11 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17616 (Mat 6:12) — canonical reciprocal-forgiveness petition.

### M11-B-VCG-03 — Repentance / turning unlocks pardon

**Context:** The condition that genuine repentance / turning / inner reorientation triggers divine forgiveness. Mar 4:12 (turning required for forgiveness); Jer 36:3 (hearing-and-turning unlocks iniquity-release); Isa 55:7 (abundant pardon for the wicked who forsake ways); 2Ch 7:14 (the canonical humility–prayer–seeking–turning sequence); Act 8:22 (repent of heart-wickedness for forgiveness).

**Members:** vc=17600 (Mar 4:12), vc=17610 (Act 8:22), vc=17672 (Isa 55:7), vc=17675 (Jer 36:3), vc=17689 (2Ch 7:14) — **5 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17689 (2Ch 7:14) — canonical humility→pardon sequence.

### M11-B-VCG-04 — Forgiveness foreclosed / unforgivable / withheld

**Context:** The negative pole — forgiveness explicitly refused or foreclosed. Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit (Mat 12:31/32, Luk 12:10) names the inner threshold beyond which pardon is structurally impossible. National-scale unforgiveness (2Ki 24:4 innocent blood, Deu 29:20 wilful self-deception, Jer 5:1 scarcity of justice, Jer 5:7 ingratitude-compounding-betrayal, Lam 3:42 absence of pardon).

**Members:** vc=17593 (Mat 12:31), vc=17594 (Mat 12:32), vc=17607 (Luk 12:10), vc=17668 (Deu 29:20), vc=17670 (2Ki 24:4), vc=17673 (Jer 5:1), vc=17674 (Jer 5:7), vc=17676 (Lam 3:42) — **8 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17593 (Mat 12:31) — canonical blasphemy-against-the-Spirit limit.

### M11-B-VCG-05 — Levitical atonement → forgiveness arc

**Context:** The canonical OT mechanism-to-outcome arc — kip.per (atonement) consistently produces sa.lach (divine forgiveness) as its declared outcome. This is the M11-A ↔ M11-B internal arc, here on the forgiveness side: the priestly atonement of Lev 4–7, 19:22, Num 15 culminates in God's declarative "and he shall be forgiven."

### M11-B-VCG-06 — Solomon's temple-prayer corpus

**Context:** The canonical OT corporate petition for divine pardon, anchored at Solomon's temple dedication (1Ki 8 and its 2Ch parallel). Forgiveness is the recurring petition framing every scenario of national distress — drought, famine, exile, captivity — with prayer toward God's dwelling as the inner movement, and divine hearing + forgiving as the prayed outcome.

**Members:** vc=17679 (1Ki 8:30), vc=17680 (1Ki 8:34), vc=17681 (1Ki 8:36), vc=17682 (1Ki 8:39), vc=17683 (1Ki 8:50), vc=17684 (2Ch 6:21), vc=17685 (2Ch 6:25), vc=17686 (2Ch 6:27), vc=17687 (2Ch 6:30), vc=17688 (2Ch 6:39) — **10 verses**

### M11-B-VCG-08 — Forgiveness as divine character attribute

**Context:** Forgiveness named not as a discrete act but as a stable inner divine character — God IS forgiving. Psa 86:5 (sal.lach, the only OT occurrence — "you, Lord, are forgiving"); Neh 9:17 (ready-to-forgive nature unexhausted by stiff-necked rebellion); Dan 9:9 (forgiveness belongs to God's nature); Psa 130:4 (the existence of forgiveness grounds reverent fear — the cross-register flag to M01 noted in B.1); Psa 103:3 (forgiveness of all iniquity as defining divine benefit); Psa 25:11 (pleading on God's name basis).

**Members:** vc=17671 (Psa 25:11), vc=17690 (Psa 103:3), vc=17693 (Psa 86:5), vc=17694 (Psa 130:4), vc=17695 (Neh 9:17), vc=17696 (Dan 9:9) — **6 verses**

### M11-B-VCG-09 — New covenant / total pardon / abundance

**Context:** Forgiveness as comprehensive eschatological resolution — sin remembered no more (Jer 31:34); guilt made unfindable (Jer 50:20); already-forgiven assurance grounding NT identity (1Jo 2:12); blessedness of having lawless deeds forgiven (Rom 4:7); all sins and blasphemies within scope (Mar 3:28).

**Members:** vc=17599 (Mar 3:28), vc=17611 (Rom 4:7), vc=17614 (1Jo 2:12), vc=17678 (Jer 31:34), vc=17692 (Jer 50:20) — **5 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17678 (Jer 31:34) — canonical new-covenant total forgiveness.

### M11-B-VCG-10 — Vow nullification forgiveness

**Context:** A distinct sa.lach mechanism — when a father or husband nullifies a daughter's / wife's rash vow, God forgives her the obligation. The inner-being content is the release of a self-imposed binding through legitimate authority's override; the pardon is for the violated-obligation guilt rather than for sin proper. Numerically small but structurally distinct from the rest of M11-B.

**Members:** vc=17665 (Num 30:5), vc=17666 (Num 30:8), vc=17667 (Num 30:12) — **3 verses**

**Anchor:** vc=17665 (Num 30:5) — canonical father-nullifies-vow pattern.

## §3. Cross-VCG observations (Phase D + Phase E seeds)

1. **M11-A-VCG-02 ↔ M11-B-VCG-05** — the canonical kip.per (atonement) → sa.lach (forgiveness) arc. Same verses are evidenced from both sides: the offering verses in M11-A produce forgiveness verses in M11-B. Phase D T6 will articulate as an intra-cluster structural relationship.
2. **VCG-01 (NT pronouncement) and VCG-08 (divine attribute) are the polar pair** — forgiveness as discrete sovereign act in time and as stable inner character. Phase D may articulate the act-vs-state pairing.
3. **VCG-04 (foreclosed) shares conceptual structure with M11-A-VCG-08 (atonement denied)** — the negative pole runs across both atonement and forgiveness registers. Phase D T6 may articulate the M11-internal pattern of the limit-condition.
4. **Psa 130:4 (VCG-08, vc=17694)** carries the M11 ↔ M01 cross-register flag from B.1 — forgiveness as the ground of reverent fear. Phase D T6 should articulate.
