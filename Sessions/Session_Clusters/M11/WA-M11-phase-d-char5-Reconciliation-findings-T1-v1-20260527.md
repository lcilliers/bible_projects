# M11 Phase D — Char 5 (Reconciliation) — T1 findings

**Characteristic:** M11-E Reconciliation (char_seq=5) — 24 prompts. Authored 2026-05-27.

---

**T1.1.1 Name and Naming** **[CHAR-5]** E — Named **Reconciliation**. The name signals (a) restoration to a prior-broken state, (b) bilateral-relational re-establishment, (c) the inner-being state of being-at-peace with the formerly-estranged party. The name carries strong directional implications: from-enmity-to-peace.

**T1.1.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — **Greek primary:** G2644 **katallassō** (5V — Rom 5:10, 1Cor 7:11, 2Cor 5:18–20) "to reconcile, exchange, restore relations"; G0604 **apokatallassō** (3V — Eph 2:16, Col 1:20, Col 1:22) "to reconcile completely / thoroughly" (intensified form); G0580 **apobolē** (1V — Rom 11:15) "deprivation, casting away" (used in the paradoxical-mechanism context). Root: katallassō from kata + allassō (to change / exchange) — reconciliation is a *change-of-relational-state*. The intensified apokatallassō names the comprehensive/cosmic reconciliation specifically.

**T1.1.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Strong relational implications (the act is between parties); strong directional (from estrangement to restored standing); constitutional implications (operates on the *relational-bond-state* between parties). The name accurately captures the relational-outcome character.

---

**T1.2.1 Kind** **[CHAR-5]** E — Reconciliation is principally a **state-and-outcome with corresponding act**. (i) State: the restored-standing between formerly-estranged parties (Rom 5:10 the post-reconciliation state of being-reconciled). (ii) Act: the divine-initiated act of reconciling (2Cor 5:18 God reconciled). (iii) Process: the ongoing ministry-of-reconciliation operating in the world (2Cor 5:18 "entrusted with the ministry").

**T1.2.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Composite: (i) the prior alienation / enmity between parties; (ii) the initiating-act (typically divine; Christ's death); (iii) the mechanism (the cross / the not-counting-trespasses); (iv) the responsive uptake (the alienated party's reception); (v) the restored standing.

**T1.2.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — **Working description:** The relational state in which previously-estranged parties are brought back into right relationship through a reconciling-act (typically divinely-initiated in Christ), with the inner-moral consequence of being-presented holy / blameless / above-reproach (Col 1:22).

---

**T1.3.1 Boundary** **[CHAR-5]** E — Structural opposite: **maintained-enmity** / unreconciled-state (Rom 5:10 names the pre-reconciliation state of being-still-enemies; Eph 2 the pre-cross hostility). The opposite is enmity-uninterrupted-by-reconciling-act.

**T1.3.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Excludes / resists: maintained-hostility (Eph 2:14–16 the hostility-being-killed implies hostility-as-the-thing-resisted); refusal to receive the offered reconciliation (2Cor 5:20 the appeal "be reconciled" implies the possibility of refusal).

**T1.3.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Ends and others begin: **vs M11-A Atonement** — atonement is the mechanism; reconciliation is the relational outcome. **vs M11-B Forgiveness** — forgiveness is the declared release of guilt-standing; reconciliation is the broader relational repair (includes forgiveness but goes beyond to relational re-establishment). **vs M11-C Release** — release lets-go-of-attachment; reconciliation restores-the-bond. **vs M11-D Repentance** — repentance is the inner-turning; reconciliation is the relational outcome of the entire turning-and-forgiveness arc.

---

**T1.4.1 Modes of Operation** **[CHAR-5]** E — Modes: (i) **Cosmic-divine reconciliation** (Col 1:20 — through the cross all things to God); (ii) **Inter-religious-group reconciliation** (Eph 2:16 — Jew-Gentile hostility killed); (iii) **Personal-divine reconciliation** (Rom 5:10 — individual reconciled to God); (iv) **Ministry-of-reconciliation extension** (2Cor 5:18–20 — apostolic and ecclesial ministry); (v) **Interpersonal marital reconciliation** (1Cor 7:11); (vi) **Paradoxical mechanism** (Rom 11:15 — Israel's rejection enabling world-reconciliation).

**T1.4.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Strongly differentiated by scope: cosmic (Col 1:20), inter-group (Eph 2:16), personal (Rom 5:10), interpersonal (1Cor 7:11). The mechanism (cross / blood) is consistent but the scope varies.

**T1.4.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Speech-mode in 2Cor 5:18–20 (ministry as appealing-speech: "we implore you on behalf of Christ, be reconciled to God"). The speech-act dimension is significant in the ministry-extension VCG-02.

---

**T1.5.1 Immediate Response** **[CHAR-5]** S — The M11-E corpus does not richly depict the immediate-felt response of being-reconciled at the individual level. The verses describe the act and state more than the felt-response. **Inferred:** the response is the inner-state of being-at-peace with the formerly-estranged party (Rom 5:10's logic — having been reconciled, we shall be saved by his life — implies an ongoing peaceable-state).

**T1.5.2** **[CHAR-5]** S — Variation in immediate response is not richly evidenced.

**T1.5.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — Limited evidence on immediate felt-response.

---

**T1.6.1 Sustained Effect** **[CHAR-5]** E — Sustained effect is the **ongoing reconciled-state** plus the **moral-formational outcome**. Col 1:22 names the moral outcome: presented holy, blameless, above-reproach. Rom 5:10 names the soteriological-sustained effect: saved by his life. The sustained effect is comprehensive — relational state + moral character + soteriological participation.

**T1.6.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Establishes: peaceable-relational state; moral-formational arc (holy / blameless / above-reproach); ambassadorial-role capacity (2Cor 5:18–20).

**T1.6.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Sustained differs from immediate principally in that immediate is the receiving-of-the-act; sustained is the lived-reconciled-life with moral-formational development.

---

**T1.7.1 Conditions of Reception** **[CHAR-5]** E — Enabling: hearing the appeal (2Cor 5:20); willingness to abandon enmity (Rom 5:10's logic); faith-receiving (the broader gospel-pattern).

**T1.7.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Blocked by: maintained-hostility / refusal-to-receive / continued enmity. The 2Cor 5:20 imploring-language implies that some refuse.

**T1.7.3** **[CHAR-5]** S — The non-receiving state in M11-E is less directly evidenced than in M11-A/B/C (the verses don't dwell on it).

---

**T1.8.1 Dimension Classification** **[CHAR-5]** E — Primary dimension: **relational-restorative**. Secondary: moral-formational (Col 1:22 the holy/blameless/above-reproach outcome); cosmic-eschatological (Col 1:20 scope); ambassadorial-participative (2Cor 5:18–20).

**T1.8.2** **[CHAR-5]** E — Supported by: every M11-E verse operates on relational structure; the act is between-parties; the outcome is relationally-defined.

**T1.8.3** **[CHAR-5]** E — Secondary dimensions complement: moral-formational is the inner-outcome; cosmic-eschatological is the scope; ambassadorial-participative is the participation-mode.
