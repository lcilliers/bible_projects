**T0.1.1 — Divine Nature Reflected — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The M11-A corpus reveals atonement as fundamentally a divine attribute and action — God is the one who covers what the creature cannot. The Psalter is direct: when overwhelming iniquities exceed the person's capacity to manage them, God himself acts to atone (Psa 65:3); divine compassion moves God to atone for iniquity and restrain wrath repeatedly (Psa 78:38); atonement for sin is pleaded as an act of divine glory, an expression of God's own honour and saving character (Psa 79:9). Isaiah's seraph touch enacts this directly — a divine touch removes guilt and atones for sin in a single transforming act (Isa 6:7). Eschatologically, atonement for iniquity is one element of a cosmic resolution that ends transgression and sin permanently (Dan 9:24); even the cleansing of the land is a divine, not human, covering (Deu 32:43). Pro 16:6 frames this at the character level: iniquity is atoned for through steadfast love and faithfulness, i.e. through hesed and emet — God's covenantal disposition itself functions as atonement mechanism, not merely as motivation for ritual. The character revealed across these verses is initiative-taking divine compassion: where human resources are exhausted, divine character supplies what is needed. (M11-A-VCG-07; M11-A-VCG-09 Pro 16:6.)

---

**T0.1.2 — Divine Nature Reflected — Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement is explicitly attributed to God across multiple registers. As act: God atones (Psa 65:3; Psa 78:38; Psa 79:9; Isa 6:7; Deu 32:43). As eschatological resolution: God effects the final atonement that ends sin permanently (Dan 9:24). As ontology of the Mediator: Mat 27:50 names the central atoning surrender as Christ's voluntarily-released spirit at death — the inner act of willed surrender being itself the atoning event. Joh 18:8 extends this — Christ's protective self-substitution at his arrest, granting permission for the disciples to go free, is the atoning act of standing in another's place. The attribution reveals that atonement in the human person is not merely a process applied to the person but participates in a divine character. When a priest atones (Lev 4–6 corpus, M11-A-VCG-02) or when an inner moral disposition atones (Pro 16:6), the act mirrors what God is and does. The significance for the human is structural: atonement is the place where divine character operates through human agents (priest, mediator, intercessor) to address what humans cannot resolve on their own. The whole sacrificial system is, in this reading, a divinely-authored participatory mechanism by which God's atoning character is enacted within history.

---

**T0.1.3 — Divine Nature Reflected — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?**

**[CHAR-1]** S — Scripture is not substantively silent about God's involvement in atonement; the M11-A corpus is explicit at both poles (God-as-agent in VCG-07; God-as-the-one-to-whom-atonement-is-directed in VCG-01/02/03/06). The narrower silence worth naming concerns one register: the verses do not depict God himself as needing atonement, only as the giver/receiver of it. This asymmetry is analytically significant — it confirms atonement as a creaturely-directional characteristic in its directionality even where God is its agent. The act of atoning belongs to divine character; the state of needing-atonement belongs to creaturely condition. The divine-image bearer participates in atonement either as recipient (the offerer cleansed) or as mediator (the priest acting on behalf of others), but never as God who himself is the source of the atonement-need. This shapes what Image-Bearer Expression (T0.3) can claim: humans bear the image as mediators and recipients of atonement, not as ultimate sources of it.

---

**T0.2.1 — Created Purpose — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created — what does it equip the person to be, do, or become?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement equips the person to be restored to right standing before God when their inner condition has become uncovered, defiled, or condemned. The Levitical mechanism (VCG-02) consistently shows the outcome — the offerer is forgiven (Lev 4:20, 4:26, 4:31, 4:35; Lev 5:10, 5:13, 5:16, 5:18; Lev 6:7; Lev 19:22). The cleansing-atonement corpus (VCG-03) shows it equipping the person to return to the community after defilement (Lev 12–15 corpus, Num 6:11). Lev 17:11 names the deepest purpose: blood is the divinely appointed means of atonement because life itself resides in it; guilt lodged in the soul is covered by the substitution of one life given for another — i.e. atonement is what equips the person whose soul-level guilt could not otherwise be discharged. At the relational level, 2Sa 21:3 and Gen 32:20 show atonement equipping the person to repair broken bonds (a king restoring a wronged tribe; a brother re-entering relationship). At the eschatological level, Dan 9:24 names atonement as equipping a cosmic resolution that ends transgression permanently. Across these, atonement equips the person to *re-stand* — to be brought back into a covenant standing they have lost, defiled, or never had.

---

**T0.2.2 — Created Purpose — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The verse evidence pulls strongly toward atonement as response-to-fallen-condition rather than feature-of-original-design. Every M11-A verse presupposes a guilt, transgression, defilement, or alienation requiring cover (e.g. "his sin which he hath sinned" Lev 4:35; "iniquities prevail against me" Psa 65:3; "unintentional sin" Num 15:25/28; "guilt of innocent blood" Deu 21:8). The Day of Atonement institution itself (VCG-01) is calibrated to the accumulated transgressions of the people — a permanent statute (Lev 16:34) precisely because the recurring need is permanent under fallen conditions. Pro 16:6 (love + faithfulness atone) is the closest the corpus comes to atonement as something other than fall-response — it frames atonement as a character disposition, but still oriented toward iniquity already present. There is no M11-A verse depicting atonement as exercised in an unfallen condition. The science extract (Section 3, kip.per coverage) corroborates: atonement-research is bound to wrongdoing, repair, and restorative justice — none of these are unfallen-condition categories. The answer is therefore: **atonement is response to the fallen condition** in its operational form, but its underlying mechanism (substitution, life-for-life covering) participates in a deeper divine character (Pro 16:6 hesed+emet) that pre-exists the fall and would exist apart from it.

---

**T0.2.3 — Created Purpose — Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness — something the person is moving toward, not only what they currently are?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement's eschatological orientation is explicitly evidenced. Dan 9:24 places atonement for iniquity within a cosmic resolution that ends transgression and finishes sin permanently — atonement here is not the recurring annual covering but a final eschatological event. Deu 32:43 names the divine cleansing of the land as a culminating act on a national scale. Eze 16:63 shows the eschatological reception of atonement producing lasting confounded humility — the recipient's inner state after definitive covering is silenced shame, oriented toward a future in which the covenant relationship is restored on a new basis. The NT trajectory (M11-A-VCG-10) names Christ's atoning surrender (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8) as the central act that establishes the future reality. Under this reading, every Levitical atonement is provisional and pointing toward a fullness — a fullness in which atonement is no longer an annual statute but a completed work whose effects spread outward in concentric circles like Lev 16:33 but at cosmic scale (Dan 9:24). Future-orientation is therefore intrinsic to the characteristic, not an add-on; the very recurrence of annual atonement (Lev 16:34) is a structural anticipation of a non-recurring resolution.

---

**T0.3.1 — Image-Bearer Expression — In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person — what aspect of being made in God's likeness does it instantiate?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Atonement instantiates two distinct aspects of divine image in the human person, both depicted across M11-A. First, the **mediator-aspect**: the priest who bears guilt on behalf of others (Lev 10:17 — "the sin offering is most holy precisely because it bears the congregation's iniquity"; Lev 16:17 — atonement accomplished in complete solitude with the priest bearing the weight of guilt for himself, his household, and the whole assembly) participates in God's own mediating disposition. Mat 27:50 and Joh 18:8 are the canonical NT extension: Christ's self-substitution is itself the divine image expressed in the human person at maximal extent. Second, the **recipient-aspect**: when a person is brought from defilement to clean (Lev 14:20 — burnt offering and grain offering together complete the priestly atonement, and the result is that the formerly defiled person is declared clean), the restored standing reflects the image of God's holy character now bearable in the person. Pro 16:6 binds the two — when a person's own steadfast love and faithfulness function as atonement, the human person enacts the divine character (hesed + emet) toward iniquity. The image-bearer expression of atonement is thus dual: humans image God by mediating cover for others and by receiving cover that restores them to the holiness God's image requires.

---

**T0.3.2 — Image-Bearer Expression — Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively a creaturely analogue to something in God?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The evidence shows atonement as genuinely shared, with structural asymmetry. Shared: the priest atones (Lev 4–7 corpus) and God atones (Psa 65:3, 78:38, 79:9). The shared aspect is the act of covering — the priest's offering and the divine act both produce the same result-form (release of guilt, restoration to standing). The science extract (Section 3) corroborates the structural homology: human atonement-acts (restoration, restitution, repair) participate in the same restorative-justice category as the divine atonement-act. Asymmetric: only God atones from his own resources without external supply (Psa 65:3 — God himself acts when human resources are exhausted). The priest atones via animal sacrifice (a divinely-supplied medium); the human moral disposition atones via love/faithfulness (a divinely-imparted character — Pro 16:6). Christ's atonement (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8) collapses the asymmetry: God in human nature atones via his own self-substitution. The verse evidence therefore suggests neither pure sharing nor pure analogue — it suggests participation: human atoning acts are real but derive their efficacy from the divine character they instantiate, and the divine atoning act enters human history through the incarnate Mediator.

---

**T0.3.3 — Image-Bearer Expression — What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — The presence and absence are both informative. **Presence:** when atonement operates in a person — whether received (Lev 4:20 offerer forgiven; Eze 16:63 atonement producing humility) or enacted (Pro 16:6 love/faithfulness atoning; Mat 27:50 Christ atoning) — the divine image is functionally active: the person stands in restored covenantal standing or operates as a mediator of restoration. The corpus shows this most clearly in the cleansing-atonement sequence (Lev 12–15, VCG-03): the formerly defiled person, once atoned for, is declared clean and re-enters the community as image-bearer in full. **Absence:** when atonement is denied (Isa 22:14, 1Sa 3:14, Jer 18:23) or impossible (Isa 47:11, Isa 28:18 false-covering exposed), the corpus signals not merely an unforgiven state but a sealed-against condition — the inner image is hardened against the very mechanism by which it would be restored. M11-A-VCG-08 ("atonement denied / impossible") names this condition; it is the structural opposite of image-restoration. Hardness of heart in the science-extract sense (Section 2) is the psychological analogue. The condition of the divine image in a person can therefore be read from the operability of atonement in them: where it operates, the image is being restored; where it is sealed against, the image is being progressively disabled.

---

**T0.4.1 — Typological Significance — Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically — deploying it to point toward or participate in a reality beyond the immediate (covenantal, eschatological, christological)?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Yes — atonement is one of the most heavily typological characteristics in Scripture. Three typological deployments are evidenced in M11-A. **Covenantal:** the entire Levitical sacrificial system (VCG-02, VCG-03, VCG-06) operates as a covenantally-instituted mechanism whose every act points beyond itself to the covenant relationship being maintained. **Eschatological:** Dan 9:24 deploys atonement typologically toward a final cosmic resolution that ends sin permanently — the recurring annual atonement (Lev 16:34, VCG-06) is itself a typological signal that a non-recurring resolution will come. Deu 32:43 (cleansing the land) is a national-scale typology of cosmic cleansing. **Christological:** the NT verses (Mat 27:50, Joh 18:8, VCG-10) explicitly position Christ's atoning surrender as the antitype of which the Levitical priestly atonement was the type. Lev 17:11's blood-as-life-for-life theology becomes a christological frame in Hebrews (citing the same logic). The Christic surrender does not abolish the type but fulfils it — the type was pointing at a reality, and the reality has now operated. The corpus thus uses atonement typologically in all three directions (covenantal upward, eschatological forward, christological forward).

---

**T0.4.2 — Typological Significance — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology — does the human instance point toward the divine, or does the divine instance establish the pattern for the human?**

**[CHAR-1]** E — Both directions are evidenced, with the structural priority belonging to the divine pattern establishing the human enactment. **Divine establishes pattern:** the Levitical sacrificial system is explicitly divinely instituted (Lev 16 is divine command; Exo 29–30 establishes the priestly ordinances). The human atoning acts (priest's offering; offerer's hand-laying; Levite's mediation) operate within a pattern that God himself has set. Pro 16:6 makes the structural claim plain — love and faithfulness atone *because* love and faithfulness are God's character first, exercised by the human as participation. **Human points toward divine:** typologically, the priest's atoning act prefigures Christ's atoning surrender (Mat 27:50; Joh 18:8). The human instance therefore is both pattern-receiver (it operates because God has established how atonement works) and pattern-pointer (it gestures toward the fuller divine instance to come). The structural priority is unidirectional in causation (God establishes; humans participate) but bidirectional in temporal-typological flow (the human OT instances point forward to a fuller divine instance in Christ). The corpus does not depict human atonement as a stand-alone creaturely invention later applied to divine reality.
