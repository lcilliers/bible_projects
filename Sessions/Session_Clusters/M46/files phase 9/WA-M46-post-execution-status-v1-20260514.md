## 3. Notable analytical findings from new terms

These are observations from reading the v4 report — they are not yet Phase 3 analysis, but they orient what Phase 3 will find.

**sha.men verb (H8080) — two VCGs of exceptional analytical significance:**

VCG 4697-001: "Term names the making fat of the inner heart as the judgment that removes perception — God causing the heart to become dull and unresponsive to his voice." The anchor verse is Isa 6:10 — the commissioning of Isaiah, where God instructs him to make the people's heart fat/dull. This is divine judicial hardening expressed through wealth/fatness vocabulary. The inner-being content is profound: prosperity-language applied to the mechanism of spiritual blindness.

VCG 4697-002: "Term names the spiritual fatness of prosperity that turns the heart from God — abundance producing inner dullness and self-sufficiency in place of dependence." This names the human-side correlate of 4697-001: the inner dynamic by which material prosperity leads the person toward self-sufficiency and away from God-dependence. Deu 32:15 (Jeshurun grew fat and kicked) is the archetype.

These two VCGs together capture the central M46 domain insight: material abundance is not morally neutral — it carries a systematic inner-being risk of producing dullness, self-sufficiency, and spiritual blindness. This is the thread that connects sha.men (fat/rich) to sha.men verb (to grow fat) to hon (wealth) to da.shen (prosperity) to cha.san (hoarding). The domain is coherent.

**autarkēs (G0842) — contentment as the counterpoint:**

VCG 4898-001: contentment as a learned inner disposition — the capacity to remain settled and sufficient regardless of external circumstances. Phil 4:11. DIM-29-001 finding attached, noting this is an acquired disposition, not a transient state. This is the positive inner-being response to material circumstance that stands in deliberate contrast to the fatness/dullness pattern of sha.men verb.

**cha.san (H2630) — the negated disposition:**

VCG 7109-001: the leader who does NOT hoard wealth but trusts God. Isa 23:18 — Tyre's merchandise will not be hoarded (cha.san) but given to God's people. The term appears negated — the refusal to hoard is the inner-being positive. This is significant: the domain expresses itself through contrast (not-hoarding as the disposition of consecration).

**a.dan (H5727) — delight in God's provision:**

VCG 3836-001: delighting in God's goodness as inner joy in divine provision. Neh 9:25 — Israel ate, were filled, grew fat, and luxuriated (a.dan) in God's great goodness. This is abundance received rightly — the joy-in-provision response that is the ordered form of what sha.men verb names in its disordered form.
