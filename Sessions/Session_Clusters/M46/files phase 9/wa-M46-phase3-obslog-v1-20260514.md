## Phase 3 — Characteristic debate from the gloss list

### Step 1: What does the gloss list tell us?

Observation: The gloss list falls into four natural vocabulary groups:

**Group A — Material state (what a person has or is):**
rich (plousios, a.shir, sha.men adj), riches (o.sher, ploutos, liparos), poor (ptōchos), wealth (cho.sen), substance (hon), fatness (mish.man), abundance (rov)

**Group B — Process (acquiring, becoming, losing):**
to enrich (a.shar, ploutizō), be rich (plouteō), to grow fat (sha.men verb), to prosper (da.shen), richly (plousiōs), be poor (ptōcheuō)

**Group C — Inner orientation toward material state:**
secure/at ease (sha.a.nan), self-sufficient (autarkēs), to hoard (cha.san), to luxuriate (a.dan)

**Group D — Moral/structural (what wealth does to the person, how character shapes wealth):**
These emerge from the VCG descriptions rather than the glosses themselves: wealth as moral category (cho.sen), wealth and inner-being orientation (hon), making fat of heart as judgment (sha.men verb), spiritual fatness turning heart from God (sha.men verb), contentment as learned disposition (autarkēs), complacent ease (sha.a.nan), consecration over self-accumulation (cha.san)

Observation: Groups A and B are primarily external/material in their glosses. Groups C and D are the inner-being facing content. The question for Phase 3 is whether a unified characteristic description can be found that holds across all four groups.

### Step 2: Reading the verse evidence — discovery by term group

#### sha.men verb (H8080) — VCGs already established; reading confirming verses

**Isa 6:10** (VCG 4697-001, G): "Make the heart of this people dull (sha.men), and their ears heavy, and blind their eyes; lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their hearts, and turn and be healed."
OBSERVATION: sha.men here names a divine act of judicial hardening — God making the heart fat/dull as an act of judgment on a people already resistant. The inner-being content is precise: the heart becoming fat = the loss of perceptual capacity. Fat/richness applied to the heart produces opacity, not vitality. This is an inversion of the expected valence of prosperity — fatness means impoverishment of inner perception.

**Jer 5:28** (UT → proposed RELEVANT, Phase 2): "They have grown fat (sha.men) and sleek. They know no bounds in deeds of evil; they judge not with justice the cause of the fatherless, to make it prosper, and they do not defend the rights of the needy."
OBSERVATION: Confirmed. sha.men (to grow fat) names the condition of the wicked who have prospered materially and, in their prosperous self-sufficiency, have abandoned moral accountability. Fatness here = moral insensibility and indifference. The inner-being sequence: material prosperity → growing fat → loss of moral attention → wickedness and injustice. VCG 4697-002 is confirmed by this verse.

**Discovery-1:** sha.men verb names a systematic inner-being dynamic: material prosperity (fatness) produces dullness of heart — the person who has grown fat becomes less perceptive, less accountable, less responsive to God and to justice. This is not incidental but constitutes one of the cluster's central findings.

#### sha.a.nan (H7600) — extensive prior record from R117

Reading across the 8 direct findings:
OBS-117-T2-016: sha.a.nan (413-001) is explicitly named as the NEGATIVE BOUNDARY of shalom — complacent ease is what peace is not. Amos 6:1 ("woe to those who are at ease in Zion") names the inner condition of self-generated security as a vice when it has no divine ground.

OBS-117-T2-022: sha.a.nan 413-001 appears alongside false peace (Jer 6:14) — complacent ease is a form of peace-counterfeiting; phenomenologically indistinguishable from genuine security but without its ground.

OBS-117-T2-074: complacent ease impairs agency — "the at-ease person fails to respond when they should." The comfortable feel of worldly security disengages moral responsiveness.

OBS-117-T2-083: authentic peace deepens conscientiousness; complacent ease (sha.a.nan 413-001) impairs it — "the at-ease person fails to respond to what conscience demands."

VCG 413-002: secure quietness — the POSITIVE form of sha.a.nan — the ease of God's people living in shalom. Isa 32:18 context.

**Discovery-2:** sha.a.nan provides M46 with a term that names both the disordered and ordered form of the ease produced by material security. Disordered: complacent ease that breeds contempt, moral blindness, and judgment (413-001). Ordered: divinely-grounded secure quietness that is the fruit of shalom (413-002). This is a directionally-determined term — same inner state, different ground, opposite moral valence.

#### plousios (G4145) / ptōchos (G4434) — reading key verses in the P pool

**Luk 18:23** (P): "But when he heard these things, he became very sad (perilupos), for he was extremely rich."
OBSERVATION: plousios in this verse is directly linked to an inner-being state — sadness (perilupos, deep grief). The man's wealth is not merely a material fact; it produces an inner response to the demand of discipleship. The richness is named as the reason for the grief. Inner-being content: wealth as the source of grief when it is threatened by kingdom demands.

**Luk 12:21** (P, plouteō): "So is the one who lays up treasure for himself and is not rich toward God."
OBSERVATION: plouteō (to be rich) is used to name two contrasting inner orientations: being rich toward oneself (self-directed accumulation) versus being rich toward God. The term itself is morally neutral but its directional object determines the moral valence. This is one of the cluster's clearest statements of the domain's inner-being logic.

**Luk 6:24** (P, plousios): "But woe to you who are rich, for you have received your consolation."
OBSERVATION: The woe to the rich names an inner-being condition: having received one's consolation in material wealth means no consolation remains beyond this life. The rich person's inner satisfaction in wealth is precisely their poverty in the eschatological register.

**Rev 3:17** (P, plousios): "For you say, I am rich, I have prospered, and I need nothing, not realizing that you are wretched, pitiable, poor, blind, and naked."
OBSERVATION: This is the cluster's most precise inner-being statement. The Laodicean self-declaration "I am rich" (plousios, plouteō) names a false inner assessment — the person who claims wealth as their inner reality is revealed by God to be, in the real inner register, wretched, pitiable, poor, blind, naked. The self-perceived wealth and the actual poverty are simultaneously present. Inner-being content: material wealth as a source of self-deception about one's actual condition before God. This verse is the cluster's ANCHOR candidate for the NT register.

**2Cor 8:9** (P, plousios + plouteō + ptōcheuō): "For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich (plousios), yet for your sake he became poor (ptōcheuō), so that you by his poverty might become rich (plouteō)."
OBSERVATION: Three M46 terms in a single verse — rich, became poor, become rich — operating in a Christological wealth-exchange. Christ's wealth (divine glory) was voluntarily surrendered; his poverty (incarnation, cross) was the means of enriching others. Inner-being content: wealth is not an end in itself but a transferable good whose voluntary surrender enables others' enrichment. This transforms the domain's valence entirely — the paradigmatic act in the wealth domain is not accumulation but voluntary impoverishment for others' gain.

**Mat 5:3** (P, ptōchos): "Blessed are the poor (ptōchos) in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."
OBSERVATION: ptōchos is extended explicitly into the inner register — "poor in spirit." The Mounce definition confirms: "the poor in spirit are not lacking in spirit, but have the positive moral quality of humility, realizing they have nothing to offer God but are in need of his free gifts." This is poverty as inner posture — the recognition of inner neediness before God. Inner-being content: material poverty vocabulary transferred to name an inner disposition of dependence and humility toward God.

**Mar 12:42-43** (P, ptōchos): Poor widow putting in two copper coins — Jesus declares she gave more than all.
OBSERVATION: The poor widow is named by Jesus as the greater giver — the inner disposition of the poor (giving out of poverty, not surplus) is declared superior to the giving of the rich. Material poverty does not diminish the inner moral status of the giver; it may intensify it.

**Discovery-3:** The plousios/ptōchos pair operates in the NT with extraordinary depth. "Rich" and "poor" are not merely economic categories — they name inner conditions (Rev 3:17), eschatological trajectories (Luk 6:24), Christological patterns (2Cor 8:9), and spiritual dispositions (Mat 5:3). The domain of wealth is where Scripture names the deepest questions of inner identity, self-assessment, and ultimate orientation.

OBSERVATION: The pretence cuts both ways — claiming false wealth and concealing real wealth. The inner-being content: the self-presentation of wealth is itself a moral act; wealth can be performed or concealed. This is an inner truthfulness/deception dimension of the wealth domain.

**Pro 30:15** (P): "Three things are never satisfied; four never say, 'Enough' (hon)."
OBSERVATION: hon = "enough" — the inner condition of insatiability. The person for whom hon never says enough is constitutionally insatiable. Inner-being content: the inability to be satisfied is a moral condition of the inner person, not merely a material circumstance.

#### o.sher (H6239) — reading key verses

**Pro 11:28** (P): "Whoever trusts in his riches (o.sher) will fall, but the righteous will flourish like a green leaf."
OBSERVATION: o.sher here names riches as the object of trust — the inner act of trusting in riches is named as the basis of falling. This directly confirms the inner-being domain: wealth is not a moral category in itself, but what the person does with wealth in their inner life (trusting it) determines their trajectory.

**Pro 8:18** (P): "Riches (o.sher) and honor are with me, enduring wealth (hon) and righteousness." — Wisdom speaking.
OBSERVATION: Both o.sher and hon appear together in Wisdom's self-description. Wisdom claims to carry riches, enduring wealth, and righteousness as a unified package. Inner-being content: when wealth comes through Wisdom, it is accompanied by righteousness — the two are not in tension when properly ordered.

**Ecc 5:13-14** (P, checking): "There is a grievous evil that I have seen under the sun: riches (o.sher) were kept by their owner to his hurt, and those riches (o.sher) were lost in a bad venture."
OBSERVATION: o.sher kept "to one's hurt" — riches hoarded by the owner to their own damage. The inner-being content: the act of keeping/holding riches (not hoarding per cha.san but possessing) can itself become harmful. The grief is that the riches kept were ultimately lost — the inner pain of losing what was hoarded.

**Ecc 9:11** (P): "The race is not to the swift, nor the battle to the strong, nor bread to the wise, nor riches (o.sher) to the intelligent..."
OBSERVATION: o.sher is placed in a wisdom observation about the unpredictability of material outcomes. Wisdom does not guarantee wealth; wealth does not follow intelligence predictably. Inner-being content: the disconnect between inner virtue (wisdom, intelligence) and material outcome (riches) is a wisdom finding — the inner person cannot control material outcome.

#### a.shir (H6223) — reading key verses

**Pro 22:2** (P): "The rich (a.shir) and the poor meet together; the Lord is the maker of them all."
OBSERVATION: The rich and poor as social-moral categories that meet in a shared creatureliness before God. Inner-being content: the shared status before God undercuts the social hierarchy of wealth — both are made by the same Creator.

**Pro 28:11** (P): "A rich man (a.shir) is wise in his own eyes, but a poor man who has understanding will find him out."
OBSERVATION: a.shir (rich man) is directly connected to an inner distortion — being "wise in his own eyes." Wealth produces a particular form of self-assessment error: the rich mistake their material success for wisdom. The poor man with genuine understanding sees through this. Inner-being content: wealth distorts self-perception and creates false wisdom.

**Mic 6:12** (P): "Your rich men (a.shir) are full of violence; your inhabitants speak lies, and their tongue is deceitful in their mouth."
OBSERVATION: a.shir (rich men) are characterised here by violence and deceit. The wealth descriptor is used as a moral category — the rich men of Jerusalem are morally corrupt. Inner-being content: the prophetic tradition connects wealth with the moral corruption of violence and deception.

#### a.shar (H6238) — reading key verses

**Hos 12:8** (P): "Ephraim has said, 'Ah, but I am rich (a.shar); I have found wealth for myself; in all my labors they cannot find in me iniquity or sin.'"
OBSERVATION: a.shar in the form of self-declaration: "I am rich." Ephraim's inner claim of wealth is paired with a claim of innocence — I am rich AND without sin. This is the delusional self-assessment that wealth produces: material success read as moral vindication. God's response (verse 9 context) is to expose the false claim. Inner-being content: wealth as the basis for false moral self-assessment; the prosperous person who mistakes abundance for righteousness.

**Pro 23:4** (P): "Do not toil to acquire wealth (a.shar); be discerning enough to desist."
OBSERVATION: The command to stop striving to become rich — the inner discipline of relinquishing the drive toward wealth. Inner-being content: the drive to enrich oneself is identified as something the wise person learns to hold back; discernment is required to stop the wealth-seeking momentum.

**Zec 11:5** (P): "'Blessed be the Lord, I have become rich (a.shar),' and their own shepherds have no pity on them."
OBSERVATION: a.shar in the mouth of those who exploit the flock — "I have become rich" as the self-congratulatory declaration of corrupt leaders. Inner-being content: the inner claim of blessing through wealth while abandoning pastoral care — wealth as the index of one's own success while disregarding others' suffering.

#### da.shen (H1878) — VCG 111-001 and 111-002

**Pro 11:25** (G, 111-001): "Whoever brings blessing will be enriched (da.shen), and one who waters will himself be watered."
OBSERVATION: da.shen (enriched/prospered) as the fruit of generosity. The one who blesses others is themselves enriched. Inner-being content: prosperity comes through generous inner orientation — the giver is enriched, not the hoarder.

**Pro 13:4** (G, 111-001): "The soul of the sluggard craves and gets nothing, while the soul of the diligent is richly supplied (da.shen)."
OBSERVATION: da.shen applied explicitly to the soul (ne.phesh) — "the soul of the diligent is richly supplied." Inner-being content: inner-being prosperity (the soul being fat/rich) is the fruit of diligence. This is one of the few verses where prosperity vocabulary is applied directly to the ne.phesh.

**Pro 28:25** (G, 111-001): "A greedy man stirs up strife, but the one who trusts in the Lord will be enriched (da.shen)."
OBSERVATION: da.shen (enriched) as the fruit of trusting in the Lord, set against greed as the source of strife. Inner-being content: the inner act of trust in God produces enrichment; the inner act of greed produces strife.

**Discovery-4:** da.shen (to prosper) consistently links inner character or inner orientation with material outcome: generosity produces enrichment; diligence produces soul-richness; trust produces enrichment. This is not a prosperity-gospel claim — it is a wisdom observation about the inner-material relationship. The soul can be rich (Pro 13:4). Inner richness and material richness are connected through character and trust.

#### cho.sen (H2633) — VCG 681-001

**Pro 15:6** (G, 681-001): "In the house of the righteous there is much treasure (cho.sen), but trouble befalls the income of the wicked."
OBSERVATION: cho.sen (treasure/wealth) in the house of the righteous vs trouble in the income of the wicked. The inner character (righteous/wicked) determines the moral quality of the wealth. Inner-being content: wealth is not morally neutral — righteousness produces treasure that is genuinely good; wickedness produces trouble even in gain.

**Isa 33:6** (G, 681-001): "and he will be the stability of your times, abundance (cho.sen) of salvation, wisdom, and knowledge; the fear of the Lord is Zion's treasure."
OBSERVATION: cho.sen is applied metaphorically to salvation, wisdom, knowledge, and the fear of the Lord — "abundance of salvation." Wealth vocabulary is used to name the fullness of divine provision. The fear of the Lord is Zion's cho.sen (treasure). Inner-being content: the domain of wealth vocabulary is applied to the inner realities of salvation and wisdom — the truly rich person has fear of the Lord as their treasure.

**Eze 22:25** (G, 681-001): "they have devoured human lives; they have taken treasure (cho.sen) and precious things."
OBSERVATION: cho.sen (treasure) as the object of prophetic corruption — the prophets of Jerusalem have seized treasure through violence. Inner-being content: the inner moral corruption of the prophets is expressed in the seizure of wealth from the vulnerable.

#### autarkēs (G0842) — VCG 4898-001

**Phil 4:11** (G, 4898-001): "I have learned, in whatever situation I am, to be content (autarkēs)."
OBSERVATION: autarkēs names a learned inner disposition — not an innate condition but an acquired capacity. Paul has learned contentment through experience across varying material circumstances. Inner-being content: the self-sufficient, settled inner posture that is not controlled by material change. The term names the inner resolution of the wealth domain — the person who has learned that their inner condition is not determined by material abundance or lack.

**Discovery-5:** autarkēs is the cluster's term for the ordered inner response to the wealth domain — contentment learned across circumstances. It stands as the counter-movement to the sha.men verb pattern (growing fat → dullness) and to the plousios self-deception pattern (Rev 3:17). The person who has learned autarkeia cannot be corrupted by prosperity or destroyed by poverty because their inner condition is not anchored to material circumstance.

#### cha.san (H2630) — VCG 7109-001

**Isa 23:18** (G, 7109-001): "Her merchandise and her wages will be holy to the Lord. It will not be stored or hoarded (cha.san), but her merchandise will supply abundant food and fine clothing for those who dwell before the Lord."

The hoarding instinct is precisely what the eschatological order reverses. Inner-being content: hoarding (cha.san) is named as the natural but disordered disposition of the wealthy; its negation (not-hoarding, releasing wealth for others) is the ordered disposition.

### Step 3: Emerging structural pattern

Reading discovery, not imposed framing. What has emerged from the verse-by-verse reading:

**The wealth domain has a consistent inner-being structure across the OT and NT:**

The material state of wealth (richness, prosperity, abundance) functions as a domain of inner-being testing that operates in three consistent dimensions:

**Dimension 1 — The distortion axis:**
Wealth (abundance, fatness, richness) systematically produces inner-being distortions:
- Dullness of heart and loss of perception (sha.men verb, Isa 6:10; Jer 5:28)
- Complacent ease and moral blindness (sha.a.nan, Amos 6:1)
- False self-assessment: "I am rich" as identity claim concealing actual poverty (Rev 3:17; Hos 12:8; Pro 28:11)
- Insatiability — the inability to say "enough" (hon, Pro 30:15)
- Trust misplaced: "trusting in riches" as the basis of falling (o.sher, Pro 11:28)
- Wickedness and violence as the fruit of unchecked wealth (a.shir, Mic 6:12; sha.men verb, Jer 5:28)

**Dimension 2 — The ordering axis:**
Wealth received through right inner orientation is good and ordered:
- Wisdom carries riches and righteousness together (o.sher + hon, Pro 8:18)
- Righteousness produces good treasure; wickedness produces trouble (cho.sen, Pro 15:6)
- Generosity produces enrichment; diligence produces soul-richness (da.shen, Pro 11:25; 13:4)
- Trust in the Lord produces enrichment vs greed producing strife (da.shen, Pro 28:25)
- The eschatological gift of the fear of the Lord as true treasure (cho.sen, Isa 33:6)

**Dimension 3 — The resolution axis:**
The inner-being resolution of the wealth domain lies beyond wealth itself:
- Contentment as learned inner disposition independent of material circumstance (autarkēs, Phil 4:11)
- "Rich toward God" as the proper directional object of wealth-orientation (plouteō, Luk 12:21)
- Poverty in spirit as the inner disposition of dependence and humility before God (ptōchos, Mat 5:3)
- Voluntary impoverishment for others' enrichment as the Christological paradigm (2Cor 8:9)
- Not-hoarding as eschatological reversal — wealth released for the Lord's people (cha.san, Isa 23:18)

**The structural principle emerging:**
Wealth is a domain of inner-being testing where the inner person reveals — and has revealed — whether their ultimate orientation is toward themselves (self-accumulation, self-assessment, self-security) or toward God and others (trust, generosity, contentment regardless of circumstance). The domain does not produce a neutral inner state — it systematically either distorts (producing dullness, self-deception, false peace, insatiability) or is ordered (through wisdom, trust, contentment, generosity).

### Step 4: Cross-cluster contamination check

The cluster as constituted carries sha.a.nan (H7600) which comes from R117 peace and carries 8 direct findings from the peace cluster analysis. This creates a contamination risk: M46's characteristic debate could be drawn toward peace vocabulary rather than wealth vocabulary.

Assessment: sha.a.nan belongs in M46 because it names the inner state that material security produces — complacent ease. The peace findings (OBS-117 series) repeatedly use sha.a.nan to name the NEGATIVE BOUNDARY of shalom. This is not cross-cluster contamination; it is a term that operates in both the wealth domain (what material security produces as inner state) and the peace domain (what genuine shalom is not). The VCG descriptions (413-001 and 413-002) correctly capture both faces. Phase 3 should characterise sha.a.nan primarily through its wealth-domain face (what material security produces) and note the peace-domain boundary in a cross-cluster note.

Similarly, cha.san (H2630) comes from R187 strength; the VCG description connects it to the leader who does not multiply horses or wealth. This is an M46 term on its wealth face; the strength-cluster connection is secondary.

### Step 5: Borderline and silence findings

**Silence finding:** The cluster contains no term that directly names generosity as a wealth-domain characteristic from the positive direction. Giving, releasing, distributing wealth appears in the verse evidence (Mar 12:42; Luk 18:22; 2Cor 8:2; Isa 23:18) but only as the ordered response — the cluster has no term whose gloss is "generous" or "giving." This is analytically significant: the cluster names the domain of wealth and its distortions; the ordered response (generosity) is present in the verse evidence but is not a cluster term. It is visible through the negative of cha.san (not-hoarding) and through the ptōchos widow narrative, but the positive generous orientation belongs in other clusters (M05 Love, M34 Perseverance perhaps).

**Silence finding:** The cluster contains no term for the inner experience of poverty from the inside. The ptōchos material treats poverty primarily as a social category (the poor whom one should serve) rather than as an inner-being experience from the perspective of the poor person. Mat 5:3 (poor in spirit) and 2Cor 8:9 (Christ became poor) do address poverty from the inside, but the cluster's primary face is looking at wealth/poverty from the outside in. The exception is 2Cor 6:10 ("as poor, yet making many rich") — the inner experience of poverty as paradoxical enriching.

**Borderline — Group finding on the NT metaphorical uses:** Several ploutos/plousios verses were set aside as metaphorical (Rom 2:4, 9:23, 11:12, 11:33 — "riches of his kindness," "riches of his glory," etc.). These are metaphorical uses where wealth vocabulary is applied to divine attributes. They are correctly set aside from the primary M46 characteristic — but they constitute a secondary finding: the wealth vocabulary is the biblical idiom for abundance and fullness even when applied to God. This is an observation for the findings stage, not a characteristic finding.

### Cross-sub-group review findings

**CLUSTER PATTERN 1 — Complete spirit-level silence**
All four sub-groups: spirit-level silence. Not a sub-group finding — a cluster-level finding.
Record at: T2.1.4 [CLUSTER].
Text: The wealth domain is entirely absent from the spirit (ruach/pneuma) register across all 197 relevant verses and all four sub-groups. This is not silence in one sub-group or one term but a complete structural absence. The wealth domain engages the soul, heart, desire, body, and material circumstance — but not the human spirit. This distinguishes M46 from clusters where spirit-level vocabulary appears (trust, peace, fear). The implication: the wealth domain is constitutionally a mid-level and surface-level inner-being domain — it tests and reveals what is in the heart, mind, and desire-faculty, but does not originate at or primarily operate through the human spirit.

**CLUSTER PATTERN 2 — The volitional faculty as the hinge of the domain**
All four sub-groups engage the volitional faculty — but in opposing directions:
- M46-A: will disengaged by satiation (sha.a.nan), or engaged in refusal-of-God (Deu 32:15 kicking)
- M46-B: will engaged in volitional refusal toward the poor (lo a.vah — unwilling, 2Sa 12:4)
- M46-C: will commanded toward diligence and wisdom-shaped restraint (Pro 23:4)
- M46-D: will at its highest exercise in voluntary release (Gen 14:23; Luk 19:8; 2Cor 8:9)
This is a structural pattern: the wealth domain does not bypass the will but consistently reveals and tests the directional deployment of the volitional faculty. Record at T3.6.3 [CLUSTER] as a cluster-level supplement.

**CLUSTER PATTERN 3 — Moral evaluation faculty systematically challenged**
All four sub-groups show the moral evaluation faculty engaged and tested:
- M46-A: self-evaluation catastrophically failed (Rev 3:17 — not realising)
- M46-B: others evaluated by wrong criteria (Jam 2:2-4 — by material condition)
- M46-C: evaluative framework for wealth established by wisdom tradition
- M46-D: revaluation as the decisive inner act (Heb 11:26 — Moses's reckoning)
Cluster-level finding: the wealth domain is one of Scripture's primary proving grounds for the moral evaluation faculty. Every sub-group shows a distinct mode of moral evaluation engagement. Record at T3.8.3 [CLUSTER].

**CLUSTER PATTERN 4 — The directional character of the domain**
Every term in the cluster operates directionally — wealth is never a neutral state in the verse evidence. The inner-being content is always: wealth toward self or toward God/others; trust in riches or trust in God; becoming rich in the self-directed form or in the toward-God form. This is the cluster's governing structural principle. Record at T1.1.3 [CLUSTER] supplement.

**CLUSTER PATTERN 5 — Relational capacity as the visible surface of inner condition**
All four sub-groups show the relational capacity engaged — and relational behaviour toward the poor as the primary external indicator of internal wealth-domain condition:
- M46-A: relational capacity to God closed (cannot hear, see, turn)
- M46-B: relational capacity to others contracted (hardness toward poor, false friendship)
- M46-C: relational equality before God recognised (Pro 22:2 — rich and poor equally made)
- M46-D: relational capacity expanded toward the excluded (Luk 14:13; giving to poor)
Cluster-level finding: the person's treatment of the poor is the cluster's primary external index of internal wealth-domain condition across all four sub-groups. Record at T3.11.3 [CLUSTER] supplement.

**CLUSTER PATTERN 6 — The creative faculty absent throughout**
Complete silence on the creative faculty (T3.5) across all four sub-groups. The wealth domain does not engage imagination or origination in the cluster's verse evidence — except marginally in the imagined stronghold of Pro 18:11 (be.mash.kit-to). Not a gap but a structural absence. Record at T3.5.3 [CLUSTER].

**STRUCTURAL RELATIONSHIP — M46-A and M46-D are structural inverses**
M46-A describes the person who says "I am rich, I need nothing" (self-assessed sufficiency closing toward God).
M46-D describes the person who knows they have nothing to offer and is constitutively open to God (poverty of spirit, Mat 5:3).
These are not merely different inner conditions — they are structural inverses: the same domain (wealth) producing opposite inner orientations (self-assessed sufficiency vs acknowledged dependence). This structural relationship explains why the cluster's primary anchor is at M46-A (Rev 3:17) and the Christological resolution at M46-D (2Cor 8:9) — the domain's greatest risk and its paradigmatic resolution are structural inverses. Record at T6.2 [CLUSTER] as structural-relationship finding.

**STRUCTURAL RELATIONSHIP — M46-C is the generative condition for M46-D**
M46-C (ordering of character and wealth) names the inner conditions (diligence, trust, humility, fear of the Lord) that when present prevent M46-A and M46-B from forming, and when cultivated, lead toward M46-D's postures. The ordered character produces the ordered wealth that then enables the voluntary release and generosity of M46-D. M46-C is the formative middle: the wisdom-shaped character that prevents the distortions and enables the responses. Record as structural-relationship finding.

**STRUCTURAL RELATIONSHIP — M46-A produces M46-B as its expression**
Inner closure (M46-A) is the inner condition; hardness toward others and insatiability within (M46-B) are its visible expressions. The fat-hearted person (M46-A) is the one whose pity has eroded (M46-B) and whose capacity for satisfaction is gone (M46-B). M46-A names the constitutional condition; M46-B names how that condition manifests in relational behaviour and inner experience. The sub-groups are analytically distinct but causally related. Record as structural-relationship finding.
