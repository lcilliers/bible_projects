## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**T2.1.1** — Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?

**[CLUSTER]** S — The cluster's 197 relevant verses contain no explicit spirit-level (ruach / pneuma in the human spirit sense) location for any of the M46 vocabulary. The wealth vocabulary (plousios, ptōchos, hon, o.sher, cho.sen, sha.men, etc.) is not assigned to the human ruach in any verse. This is a complete silence across all four sub-groups.

---

**T2.1.2** — Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?

**[CLUSTER]** S — No verse in the cluster assigns origin to the human spirit. The wealth-domain characteristics originate at the soul level (heart, mind, desire — T2.2-T2.4 below) and in material circumstance (the condition of having or lacking material goods).

---

**T2.1.3** — What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**[CLUSTER]** S — no spirit-level evidence to interpret.

---

**T2.1.4** — If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.

**[CLUSTER]** The complete silence of spirit-level location across all 197 relevant verses and all four sub-groups is a significant finding. The wealth domain — unlike characteristics in clusters such as M19 (Trust) or M33 (Peace) where spirit-level language appears — operates at the soul level and below. This suggests that the wealth domain engages the middle and surface layers of the inner person (heart, mind, desire, body) but not the deepest spirit-level.

### T2.2 — Soul-Level Location

**T2.2.1** — Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?

**[C, D]** E — M46-C has the cluster's sole explicit soul-level location: Pro 13:4 — "the soul (ne.phesh) of the diligent is richly supplied (da.shen)." This is the only verse in the cluster where prosperity vocabulary is applied directly to the ne.phesh. The soul itself is made fat/rich by the character of diligence. This is a significant finding: prosperity vocabulary can describe an inner-being state at the soul level, not only a material condition.

**[D]** E — Mat 5:3 ("poor in spirit" — ptōchos tō pneumati) is the only verse that places poverty vocabulary at the inner-spirit level. The phrase "in spirit" here modifies the poverty — it is not material poverty but a quality of the inner spirit (whether pneuma here means the human spirit or the broader inner person is a hermeneutical question, but the explicit inner-being location is certain). This is M46's second explicit inner-being location for wealth vocabulary, and it is in M46-D.

**[A, B]** S — Neither sub-group has explicit ne.phesh or spirit-level location. The sha.men (fat), hon, o.sher, and plousios vocabulary in M46-A and M46-B is not explicitly assigned to the soul or spirit in the cluster's verse evidence.

---

**T2.2.2** — What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?

**[C]** E — Pro 13:4's ne.phesh + da.shen connection reveals that the ordered character-wealth relationship can produce an inner enrichment that reaches the soul itself — not merely a material condition but a richness of the innermost personal life. The soul of the diligent is not merely more productive; it is richly supplied at the soul level. This places M46-C's ordered wealth in direct connection with the person's deepest experienced reality.

**[D]** E — Mat 5:3's ptōchos tō pneumati reveals that the poverty of spirit (dependence and openness before God) is located at the innermost level — it is a quality of the inner spirit/person, not merely a circumstantial condition. This is the deepest constitutional location in the cluster.

---

**T2.2.3** — If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.

**[A, B]** The absence of explicit ne.phesh language in M46-A and M46-B is notable. The sha.men verb (fat heart) is close — the heart is the inner-life centre — but the explicit ne.phesh assignment is absent. Inner closure and hardness/insatiability operate at the heart level (T2.3) rather than the soul level.

### T2.3 — Heart

**T2.3.1** — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?

**[A]** E — M46-A has the cluster's most explicit heart-location. Isa 6:10: "make the heart (lev) of this people fat (sha.men)" — the heart is the specific location of the fat-hearted closure. The heart is the organ whose fatness produces the inability to see, hear, understand, and turn. This is heart-level location of the inner closure condition. Rev 3:17 ("not realising" — ouk oidas) names the heart's knowing-function as the faculty that has failed through inner closure.

**[B]** E — Pro 30:15 ("never says enough") and Ecc 4:8 ("eyes never satisfied with riches") locate insatiability in the inner life without naming the lev explicitly, but the context of Proverbs and Ecclesiastes wisdom discourse always locates moral and inner states in the heart. Psa 73:7 (in the broader context of M46-B's trust pattern) — "their hearts overflow with follies" — is not in the cluster but confirms the heart-location of the wealth-moral failure pattern across wisdom literature.

**[C]** E — M46-C has implicit heart-location through the character terms: diligence, trust, humility, and fear of the Lord are all heart-level dispositions in the wisdom tradition. Pro 28:25 ("the one who trusts in the Lord will be enriched") — ba.tach (trust) is consistently a heart-faculty in the OT. The connection between heart-character and material outcome is the core of M46-C.

**[D]** E — Phil 4:7 ("the peace of God will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus") immediately precedes the contentment passage — the learned contentment of Phil 4:11 is framed by the heart-guarding peace of God. The heart is the location where contentment is established and guarded. Luk 12:34 (not in the cluster but contextually adjacent) — "where your treasure is, there your heart will be also" — places the heart as the location where wealth-orientation is ultimately determined.

---

**T2.3.2** — What does the heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's integrating function does this characteristic engage?

**[A]** E — The heart's perceptive function is what inner closure engages and impairs. The fat heart cannot perceive (Isa 6:10: cannot see, hear, understand, turn). The heart's knowing-function (cognitive aspect) is also engaged: "not realising" (ouk oidas, Rev 3:17) is a knowing-failure at the heart level. Inner closure engages the heart's perceptive and knowing functions — and impairs both.

**[B]** E — Insatiability engages the heart's desiring-function — the heart that can never say enough (Pro 30:15 — hon context) is a heart whose desire-capacity has become unbounded, unable to rest in satisfaction. Hardness engages (by its absence) the heart's compassion-function — the heart that should feel with the poor (cha.mal) and does not.

**[C]** E — M46-C engages the heart's willing and trusting functions: diligence (sustained volitional effort), trust (ba.tach — the heart's capacity to rest in God), humility and fear (orientational dispositions of the heart toward God). The ordering of character and wealth engages the heart's integrating function most fully — it is the character of the whole inner person that determines the quality of material life.

**[D]** E — Learned contentment (autarkēs, Phil 4:11) engages the heart's settled/resting function — the capacity of the heart to be still and sufficient in any circumstance. Poverty of spirit (Mat 5:3) engages the heart's receptive function — the capacity to be open and receiving before God. Voluntary release engages the heart's volitional function — the act of choosing to give or refuse wealth.

---

**T2.3.3** — If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.

**[CLUSTER]** Heart-location is present across all four sub-groups, though M46-A has the only explicit fat-heart language. The silence in M46-B and M46-C is a matter of degree (implicit rather than explicit heart-location), not absence.

### T2.4 — Mind

**T2.4.1** — Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?

**[A]** E — The closest mind-location in M46-A is the cognitive failure of inner closure: the person who does not realise their actual condition (ouk oidas, Rev 3:17) — the knowing-faculty has failed. Pro 18:11 (rich man's wealth as high wall "in his imagination" — be.mash.kit-to) is the most explicit mind-location in M46-A: the imagined security of wealth is located in the person's mental construct, their imagination. The mind has constructed a false security from material goods.

**[B]** S — M46-B does not have explicit mind-location. Insatiability is an affective-desire condition; hardness is a relational-compassion condition. Neither is located in the cognitive or discerning functions of the mind in the cluster's evidence.

**[C]** E — M46-C engages the mind through the wisdom-discernment dimension: Pro 23:4 ("be discerning [bi.nah — understanding] enough to desist from wealth-seeking") places the ordering of the wealth-drive in the understanding faculty. The wise person's recognition that wealth should not be urgently pursued requires active discernment (bi.nah) — a mind-faculty engagement.

**[D]** E — Heb 11:26 provides M46-D's most explicit mind-location: Moses "considered (hēgeomai — to think, evaluate, reckon)" the reproach of Christ as greater wealth than Egypt's treasures. The voluntary revaluation of wealth requires an explicit mental act — a cognitive reassessment of what constitutes genuine wealth. The mind is the location where the revaluation of wealth happens in M46-D.

---

**T2.4.2** — What does mind-location reveal about this characteristic's engagement with the mind's functions?

**[A]** E — Pro 18:11's "in his imagination" reveals that inner closure operates partly through the mind's constructive function — the imagination (mas.kith — from a root meaning to look at, to view) is the faculty through which the wealthy person builds their mental stronghold. The mind creates the security that the material goods only nominally support. This is the mind's constructive-imaginative function being misdirected toward a false security.

**[C, D]** E — M46-C engages bi.nah (understanding) as the faculty that enables the wisdom-shaped restraint on wealth-seeking. M46-D engages hēgeomai (to reckon, evaluate) as the faculty through which genuine wealth is distinguished from apparent wealth. Both are discernment-functions of the mind — the capacity to assess correctly what is truly valuable.

---

**T2.4.3** — If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.

**[B]** Mind-location is silent in M46-B. Hardness and insatiability operate at the relational-capacity and affective levels, not the cognitive-discernment level.

### T2.5 — Other Soul Subsets

**T2.5.1** — Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind for this characteristic?

**[A, B]** E — The cluster's M46-A and M46-B evidence surfaces desire (epithumia) as a relevant soul-subset location. Rev 18:14 (liparos — "your soul longed for all your delicacies") names psuchē (soul) and the longing/desire of the soul for material luxury. 1Ti 6:9 ("those who desire [boulomai] to be rich fall into temptation... and many senseless and harmful desires [epithumiai]") names the desire-faculty as the location of the wealth-drive that produces ruin. The desire-subset of the soul is the location where the wealth-orientation and insatiability are seated in M46-A and M46-B.

**[D]** E — Rev 18:14 also evidences the soul's longing/desire in M46-B context (the soul that longed for Babylon's luxurious goods). The soul as the seat of desire is present in both M46-B (desire for material luxury) and M46-D (in its inverted form — the soul oriented toward God rather than goods).

---

**T2.5.2** — If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?

**[A, B]** E — The desire-faculty (epithumia, boulomai, the psuchē's longing in Rev 18:14) is the constitutional location where the wealth-drive is seated before it becomes either inner closure or insatiability. The soul's capacity for desire — which in its ordered form is the longing for God and genuine flourishing — is in M46-A and M46-B turned toward material goods. The desire-faculty is revealed as the pivotal location: its direction (toward God or toward wealth) determines which sub-group's pattern emerges.

---

**T2.5.3** — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[C, D]** The desire-faculty is implicitly present in M46-C (the diligent person's sustained desire is ordered toward productive work, not wealth) and M46-D (the voluntary release requires the desire to be re-oriented from wealth to a higher valuation), but it is not named explicitly in those sub-groups' verses. The explicit desire-location is M46-A and M46-B territory.

### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**T2.6.1** — Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?

**[A]** E — The fat-heart cluster (sha.men verb) links inner closure to the heart as a physical-inner organ. Isa 6:10 names "heart" (lev), "ears" (ozen), and "eyes" (a.yin) as the three organs made fat/heavy/blind — the closing of the inner perceptual organs through the physical metaphor of fatness. This is the cluster's richest body-link: the physical fattening of organs describes the inner-perceptual closure.

**[A, B]** E — M46-A and M46-B share the body-link through the sha.men root: "grown fat (sha.men) and sleek" (Jer 5:28), "grew fat (sha.men), stout, and sleek" (Deu 32:15) — the physical condition of corpulence describes the inner condition of satiation and closure. The body's fatness is used as the outer sign of the inner fat-heartedness.

**[B]** E — Ecc 5:12 links insatiability to the stomach: "the full stomach (sova — satiation/fullness) of the rich will not let him sleep." The insatiable person's inner restlessness is connected to the physical stomach as the location of over-fullness that prevents rest.

**[C]** E — Pro 13:4 links ordered prosperity to the soul/bones through da.shen: "the soul of the diligent is richly supplied." Pro 15:30 extends this: "good news refreshes (da.shen) the bones" — the prosperity vocabulary is applied to the etsem (bones), naming the deep structural frame of the body as the location of inner-being renewal. This is M46's most striking body-link: the bones as the location where received good news (and ordered prosperity) produces renewal.

**[D]** S — M46-D does not have explicit body-location evidence beyond the general physical contexts of poverty (the poor's physical condition) and giving (material goods transferred).

---

**T2.6.2** — If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link?

**[A]** E — The fat-organ link (Isa 6:10) is functioning emphatically: the physical metaphor of fattened ears and eyes captures the spiritual-perceptual closure in a visceral, unavoidable image. The fat organ cannot perform its function — the fat ear cannot hear, the fat eye cannot see. Scripture is using the body's failure-through-excess to name the soul's failure-through-abundance. The body-link is metaphorical but grounded in the physical reality of obesity impairing function.

**[C]** E — Pro 15:30 ("good news refreshes the bones") is functioning mediatively: the received blessing (good news) reaches the bones — the deepest structural level of the body — as the sign of comprehensive inner renewal. The bones are not merely the outer frame; in wisdom literature they are the location of the deepest inner vitality. Da.shen (to refresh/make fat) applied to bones names a renewal that is total — from the centre of the person outward.

---

**T2.6.3** — If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[D]** M46-D's voluntary release and learned contentment are not linked to specific body parts in the cluster's evidence.

### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**T2.7.1** — Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run?

**[A]** E — The sha.men body-link runs in both directions in different contexts. In Isa 6:10, the direction is soul-to-body: the inner condition of fat-heartedness is expressed in the functional impairment of the perceptual organs (cannot see, hear). In Deu 32:15 and Jer 5:28, the direction is body-to-soul: the physical condition of being fat/well-fed (material prosperity received into the body) produces the inner condition of turning from God. Both directions are evidenced.

**[C]** E — Pro 15:30's da.shen on bones runs soul-to-body: the received good news (an inner event — hearing and processing good news) produces renewal that reaches down into the bones. The direction is inward-reception → body-renewal.

---

**T2.7.2** — What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?

**[A]** E — The bidirectionality of M46-A's body-link confirms that the material-inner relationship is not one-way. Material prosperity (body-to-soul direction in Deu 32:15) can produce inner closure; and inner closure (soul-to-body direction in Isa 6:10) can produce perceptual organ failure. The body and soul reinforce each other in the production and maintenance of inner closure. This has implications for understanding how inner closure is sustained: it is not purely an inner condition but is maintained through both the body's material condition and the soul's perceptual incapacity.

---

**T2.7.3** — If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[B, D]** The body direction for M46-B (hardness and insatiability) and M46-D (ordered response) is not explicitly evidenced in the cluster beyond what is noted above.

### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**T2.8.1** — Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including DNA or generational consequence?

**[A]** E — The sha.men / fat-heart pattern has a potential generational dimension. Deu 32:15 is addressed to Israel as a people across generations: "Jeshurun grew fat and kicked" — the national pattern of prosperity-apostasy is a generational observation. Neh 9 recounts it across multiple generations of Israel's history. The prosperity-apostasy pattern is documented as a generational-transmission phenomenon: one generation's prosperity producing the next generation's inheritance of a culture of apostasy. Whether this is a biological deposit or a cultural-covenantal transmission is not specified, but the generational pattern is evidenced.

**[B, C, D]** S — No explicit body-deposit or generational-transmission language for M46-B, M46-C, or M46-D in the cluster's verse evidence.

---

**T2.8.2** — What evidence supports or contradicts this?

**[A]** E — Hos 10:1 ("the more his fruit increased, the more altars he built") names the proportional relationship between material prosperity and idolatry — suggesting a cumulative amplifying pattern across Israel's history, consistent with a generational deposit. The pattern appears across Hosea, Deuteronomy, and Nehemiah 9, all evidencing the same cross-generational cycle.

---

**T2.8.3** — If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**[B, C, D]** Body-deposit evidence is silent for these sub-groups. This silence feeds into T5.7 — deposit consequences are limited to M46-A's generational pattern.

### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**T2.9.1** — Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally — is it generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally?

**[A]** E — Inner closure has two origins evidenced in the cluster. Internal origin: the self-generated complacency of sha.a.nan (Amo 6:1 — "those who are at ease in Zion") and the self-declared wealth of Rev 3:17 — both are internally generated conditions produced by the person's own orientation toward their material prosperity. External-divine origin: Isa 6:10 names God as the agent who commissions the making-fat — "make the heart of this people fat" — in this case inner closure is externally imposed as judicial action. Both origins are present, representing different theological registers: self-generated complacency and divinely-imposed judicial hardening.

**[B]** E — Hardness and insatiability originate from the interaction between external material circumstance (wealth) and internal disposition. The wealth creates the conditions; the person's inner failure to maintain compassion and the capacity for satisfaction produces the hardness and insatiability. Mixed origin: external circumstance + internal disposition failure.

**[C]** E — The ordered character-wealth relationship originates from three sources: inner character (diligence, trust, humility — internally generated, Pro 13:4; 10:4), divine blessing (externally bestowed, 1Sa 2:7; Pro 10:22 — "the Lord's blessing makes rich"), and inheritance/providence (1Ki 3:13 — Solomon received wealth as a consequence of prior wisdom-choice, combining divine bestowal and human prior action). Multiple origins are evidenced.

**[D]** E — M46-D's characteristics originate differently. Poverty of spirit: an original constitutional design (the human is by nature dependent on God — Mat 5:3 does not name a process by which poverty of spirit is produced, suggesting it is constitutional). Voluntary release: a volitional act generated from within (Gen 14:23; Luk 19:8) in response to higher values (from without — encounter with Christ, or commitment to God). Learned contentment: generated from experience across circumstances (Phil 4:11 — "I have learned") combined with divine empowerment (Phil 4:13 — "through him who strengthens me").

---

**T2.9.2** — What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?

**[CLUSTER]** E — Multiple origins are the rule across all four sub-groups. The cluster does not evidence a single origin for any of its sub-group characteristics. This is consistent with the domain-cluster nature of M46: wealth is an external circumstance that interacts with internal dispositions, divine action, and historical-generational patterns to produce the inner-being outcomes the four sub-groups name. A single-origin account would be inadequate to the complexity of the evidence.

---

**T2.9.3** — Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts evidenced in Scripture?

**[A]** E — Yes, explicitly. In the Deuteronomic / Hosean context, inner closure arises from self-generated apostasy in the context of material prosperity (human-internal origin in the context of divine gift). In the Isaianic context (Isa 6:10), inner closure is externally imposed by divine commission as judgment (divine-external origin). The same phenomenological outcome (fat heart, perceptual closure) has different origins in different theological contexts.

### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**T2.10.1** — Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?

**[A]** E — M46-A evidences movement from body/circumstance to soul: the material condition of being fat/well-fed (body level) produces the inner condition of fat-heartedness (soul/heart level). This is the most clearly evidenced constitutional movement in the cluster. Additionally, Isa 6:10 shows judicial fat-heartedness producing perceptual-organ failure (soul → body direction): the heart's closure is expressed in the inability of the perceptual organs to function.

**[C]** E — M46-C evidences movement from soul to material: the soul's character (diligence, trust, humility — soul level) produces material outcome (wealth, sufficiency — material/circumstantial level). Pro 13:4 (soul of the diligent richly supplied) is the most explicit: soul-character → soul-richness (but the richness here is simultaneously inner and material).

**[D]** E — M46-D evidences the inverse movement: material circumstance (poverty or wealth) → inner posture (poverty of spirit, contentment) → material act (giving, releasing). The constitutional movement in M46-D is bidirectional: material circumstances are the context in which the inner posture is formed (contentment learned through material experience), and the inner posture then generates material acts (giving from poverty).

---

**T2.10.2** — What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?

**[A]** E — The M46-A sequence is: material provision received → satiation produced → fat-heartedness formed → turning from God / moral failure. This is the Deu 32:15 / Neh 9:25-26 / Jer 5:28 sequence, evidenced three times independently. The pattern is consistent: external material condition → internal spiritual condition → external moral/relational behaviour. The movement has a consistent direction from material to inner to relational/moral.

**[D]** E — The M46-D sequence is: encounter with God/Christ / prior value-commitment → inner revaluation → material act (release/giving). Moses (Heb 11:26) revalued first (counted reproach as greater wealth), then chose differently. Zacchaeus (Luk 19:8) encountered Christ first, then gave. The sequence: encounter/valuation → inner posture → act.

---

**T2.10.3** — If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**[B]** M46-B's constitutional movement is less explicitly sequenced. Hardness and insatiability appear as states rather than movements — the rich man's ignoring of Lazarus (Luk 16:19-21) is not narrated as a sequence but as a stable condition. No explicit constitutional movement sequence is evidenced in M46-B.

## CLUSTER-LEVEL ADDITIONS — Phase 9 cross-sub-group review

**T2.1.4** — cluster-level finding (complete spirit-level silence)

**[CLUSTER — all sub-groups]** The wealth domain is entirely absent from the spirit (ruach/pneuma) register across all 197 relevant verses and all four sub-groups. This is not silence in one sub-group or one term but a complete structural absence across the entire cluster. The wealth vocabulary (sha.men, da.shen, hon, o.sher, cho.sen, plousios, ptōchos, ploutos, autarkēs, and all cognates) does not appear in connection with the human spirit in any relevant verse. The wealth domain engages the soul (ne.phesh — Pro 13:4 explicitly), the heart (lev — Isa 6:10), the mind (Pro 18:11 imagination; Pro 23:4 understanding), the desire-faculty (1Ti 6:9; Rev 18:14), and the body (sha.men fat-heart; Ecc 5:12 stomach) — but not the human spirit. This structural silence indicates that the wealth domain is constitutionally a mid-level and surface-level inner-being domain. It tests and reveals what is in the heart, mind, and desire-faculty, but does not originate at or primarily operate through the human spirit. M46 is distinguished from clusters where spirit-level vocabulary is present by this complete absence.
