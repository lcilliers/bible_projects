**T1 — Definition**

- *T1.1.1* — _What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?_
  → The cluster is named "Abundance, Prosperity and Wealth." The name signals three related but distinct aspects: abundance (the condition of having much), prosperity (the dynamic of flourishing and increase), and wealth (the state of having material resources). The name does not signal an inner-being characteristic per se — it names a domain. This is consistent with the analytical finding that M46 is a domain cluster: material wealth and poverty as the domain in which inner-being characteristics operate, are tested, and revealed. The name's breadth (abundance / prosperity / wealth) reflects the semantic range of the 22 terms, which span from the neutral material descriptor (o.sher — riches) to the inner moral dynamic (sha.men verb — growing fat toward apostasy) to the resolved inner posture (autarkēs — contentment).
- *T1.1.2* — _What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?_
  → The primary Hebrew terms operate in four registers at first encounter. Material-state terms (o.sher, hon, cho.sen — riches, substance, wealth) name the condition of having material goods, with no intrinsic moral valence. Process terms (a.shar — to become rich; da.shen — to prosper/grow fat; sha.men verb — to grow fat) name the dynamic of material acquisition or increase, again without intrinsic moral valence but loaded with contextual moral weight. Inner-state terms (sha.a.nan — at ease/complacent; autarkēs — self-sufficient/content; cha.san — to hoard) name specific inner orientations in relation to material circumstance. Person-type terms (a.shir, plousios — the rich person; ptōchos — the poor person) name the social-moral category of persons defined by their material condition. The Greek terms (plousios, ploutos, plouteō, ploutizō, ptōchos, ptōcheuō) follow the same fourfold pattern but with the additional NT theological loading: plousios in Rev 3:17 and ptōchos in Mat 5:3 both carry inner-being extensions that exceed their Greek economic senses.
- *T1.1.3* — _Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?_
  → The cluster name "Abundance, Prosperity and Wealth" does not itself carry directional or relational implications — but the verse evidence immediately imposes them. The constitutional implication is that wealth is never a neutral inner state — it is always an inner-being testing domain in which the direction of the person's fundamental orientation is revealed and shaped.

- *T1.6.3* — _Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?_
  → The most significant sustained-vs-immediate divergence is in M46-A: the immediate experience of material abundance is pleasure (eating, being filled, delighting — Neh 9:25 with a.dan); the sustained effect is inner closure and apostasy (Neh 9:26 — they were disobedient and rebelled). The immediate is positive; the sustained effect is harmful. This divergence is the core dynamic of M46-A: what feels like abundance and satiation in the moment produces inner deterioration over time. The same divergence applies in M46-B: the immediate experience of wealth accumulation may feel like security (hon as strong city, Pro 18:11); the sustained effect is insatiability, grief, and hardness. For M46-D, the immediate-sustained relationship is a progression: immediate joy in voluntary release → sustained capacity for paradoxical richness in poverty.

The dimensions are related in causal sequences, not competitive.
