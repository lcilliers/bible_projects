  → Sustained M46-D postures establish: the capacity to "possess everything" while having nothing (2Cor 6:10); the peace of God (Phil 4:7-11 — the contentment passage is framed by the peace of God); and the kingdom possession of Mat 5:3 — the poor in spirit's present possession of the kingdom as an abiding state, not a future promise.
- *T1.7.1* — _What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?_
  → M46-D's enabling conditions: poverty of spirit is enabled by the absence of self-assessed sufficiency — the person who knows they have nothing to offer (Mat 5:3). Voluntary release is enabled by a higher valuation — Moses's looking to the reward (Heb 11:26), Zacchaeus's encounter with Jesus (Luk 19:6-8), Abram's commitment to God as his sole material provider (Gen 14:23). Learned contentment is enabled by lived experience across varying circumstances — it is learned "in whatever situation" (Phil 4:11 — emathon, I have learned, implies repeated experience).
- *T1.7.2* — _What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?_
  → Poverty of spirit is blocked by the self-assessment of wealth (Rev 3:17 — the Laodicean church's "I need nothing" is the specific block). Voluntary release is blocked by excessive attachment to wealth — the rich young ruler's extreme wealth (plousios sphodra, Luk 18:23) produced grief when release was commanded. Learned contentment is blocked by the drive to become rich (1Ti 6:9 — desire to be rich as the alternative orientation).
- *T1.7.3* — _What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?_
  → The person who encounters the demand for voluntary release but does not receive it is the rich young ruler (Luk 18:23) — he became very sad but could not release. His inner state: grief (perilupos) without resolution. This is the most precise description in the cluster of what non-reception of M46-D looks like: sorrow without action, encounter without transformation.
- *T1.8.1* — _What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?_
  → The primary dimension of M46-D (inner life beyond material circumstance) is Orientation/Trust toward God — poverty of spirit is the condition of dependence and openness before God; learned contentment is the inner independence from circumstance that reflects a trust-grounded settledness. Secondary dimension: Volition — voluntary release (Gen 14:23; Luk 19:8; 2Cor 8:9) is explicitly a volitional act.
- *T1.8.2* — _What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?_
  → Trust/Orientation evidence: Phil 4:11-13 (learned contentment explicitly grounded in "I can do all things through him who strengthens me" — the contentment is trust-grounded); Mat 5:3 (poor in spirit before God — orientation of dependence). Volition evidence: Gen 14:23 (Abram's deliberate refusal), Luk 19:8 (Zacchaeus's spoken resolution), 2Cor 8:9 (Christ's voluntary act of becoming poor).
