**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Jer 31:21 (primary anchor, 3578-A): shit functions as the command verb — "consider well the highway, the road by which you went." **T7.2.2** — What literary form carries the primary verse evidence — and what does that form require?

**[A — Conscience]** E — 1Cor 4:4: Pauline epistle — argumentative and pastoral. The epistolary form carries a carefully constructed theological argument. Responsible interpretation requires reading the epistle argument's logical structure and reading the narrative in its Acts context (early community's life).

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Jer 31:21: prophetic oracle (Jer 31 new covenant chapter). Psa 13:2: lament psalm. Psa 48:13: Zion psalm. Pro 24:32: wisdom. Exo 7:23: narrative. **T7.2.3** — What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence?

**[A — Conscience]** E — 1Cor 4:4: Premise 1: I am not aware of anything against myself (conscience is clear). Premise 2: A clear conscience does not constitute acquittal. **[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Jer 31:21: Premise: You have left the highway (implied). Commanded act: Set your attention on the road by which you went. Conclusion: Return. The attending is the epistemic precondition for the moral act of return.

**[A — Conscience]** E — 1Cor 4:4: judicial and apostolic — Paul accounting for his ministry under accusation. **[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Jer 31:21: covenantal and eschatological (new covenant chapter). Psa 48:13: liturgical and communal (Zion psalm). ## Cluster-Level Findings

**[CLUSTER — Finding 1: The epistemological structure of inner knowing]** E — The entire cluster organises around a single epistemological structure: human inner knowing (conscience and self-awareness) is real and morally significant, but constitutively limited and transparent to divine knowing that exceeds it. Evidence: 1Cor 4:4 (conscience gives a real verdict but does not acquit — the Lord judges); kardiognōstēs Act 1:24 and Act 15:8 (God knows hearts completely and acts decisively on that knowledge); enthumeomai Mat 9:4 (inner reflection is transparent to Christ's perception); autokatakritos Tit 3:11 (the self-condemning verdict does not require external pronouncement — the inner faculty has already spoken). This structure is present at every node of the cluster.

**[CLUSTER — Finding 2: OT/NT structural asymmetry]** S — M32-B is entirely OT Hebrew (shit, 7 verses, all OT). M32-A and BOUNDARY are entirely NT Greek (10 verses, all NT). No term bridges both testaments. The Hebrew tradition names the faculty of deliberate attending (shit) without naming conscience as a distinct faculty. The NT Greek tradition names conscience as inner moral co-knowing (suneidō, sunoida) and establishes the epistemological frame (kardiognōstēs). Registry finding 112-F006 confirms no direct Hebrew equivalent for the conscience vocabulary. The OT/NT divide maps exactly onto the sub-group boundary. These are not translations of each other — they are distinct conceptual traditions addressing the same inner-being territory from different angles.

**[CLUSTER — Finding 3: Spirit-level silence]** S — Spirit-level location is silent across the entire cluster. Neither conscience (M32-A) nor inner attention (M32-B) is explicitly located at the spirit level in any active verse. Heart (lev/kardia) and soul (ne.phesh) are named; spirit (ruach/pneuma) is absent throughout. This is a positive finding: these characteristics operate at the soul/heart constitutional level. The spirit-level is silent; not a gap in evidence but an absence in the testimony.

**[CLUSTER — Finding 4: Transparency to divine knowledge]** E — All three BOUNDARY terms and M32-A's primary anchor converge on a single theme: the person's inner knowing is transparent to God. kardiognōstēs: God knows hearts completely (Act 1:24, Act 15:8). enthumeomai: inner deliberation perceived by Christ (Mat 9:4). autokatakritos: conscience-verdict requires no external pronouncement (Tit 3:11). 1Cor 4:4: conscience operates under the awareness that God's judgment is above it. The cluster as a whole establishes that human inner knowing — whether attending, reflecting, or conscience-assessing — operates in a space fully transparent to divine knowledge.

**[CLUSTER — Finding 5: The attending-reflecting-assessing arc]** E — Across M32-B (shit: initial attending), BOUNDARY (enthumeomai: sustained deliberation), and M32-A (conscience: moral self-assessment), the cluster traces an emergent three-stage inner-being arc: attend → reflect → assess. This arc is not stated in any single verse but is the structural logic that distributes the terms across sub-groups and BOUNDARY. Sequential evidence: Jer 31:21 (attend → oriented return), Mat 1:20 (deliberate reflection → divine direction received), 1Cor 4:4 (conscience verdict → held under divine judgment). The cluster as a whole describes the full pathway of inner moral self-knowing.
