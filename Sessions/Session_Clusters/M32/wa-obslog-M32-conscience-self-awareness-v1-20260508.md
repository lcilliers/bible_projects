### P3.2 — Term-by-term reading against the T1 definition

**H7896K shit — to set: consider**
Gloss: "to set: consider" | Group: "inner act of setting attention — directing the mind to consider, take to heart, or attend carefully to a matter or person"
Reading: This is an act of directed inner attention — the deliberate engagement of cognition on a subject. It names a human volitional-cognitive action. It does have an identifiable faculty (attentive mind), a direction (toward an object), and a recognisable function (to take something seriously, to notice, to attend). However: is this conscience, or is it a cognitive act of deliberate attention that is prerequisite to conscience? The word does not itself carry moral self-assessment — it carries the act of focused inner attending. It is structurally close to "self-awareness" (the second named characteristic in the cluster title) but not obviously to "conscience" in the moral-assessment sense.
Provisional classification: Characteristic-bearing, but toward the self-awareness/attentiveness pole rather than the conscience/moral-assessment pole. Needs sub-group separation from the conscience terms.

**G4894 suneidō — be aware**
Gloss: "to share knowledge with; to be conscious (of oneself) aware" | Group: "conscious inner awareness — moral complicity or cognitive recognition — that informs volitional response"
Reading: This term explicitly names shared inner knowing — knowing something alongside oneself (the sun- prefix = with/together). The group description acknowledges two senses: moral complicity and cognitive recognition. Both inform volitional response. The verses show: Ananias keeping money back "with his wife's knowledge" (Act 5:2 — shared inner knowing of a morally charged act), Peter "realizing this" (Act 12:12 — cognitive recognition), and a city "learning" of a plot (Act 14:6 — cognitive awareness). The moral complicity sense is closest to conscience; the cognitive recognition sense shades toward general awareness.
Provisional classification: Characteristic-bearing, conscience pole. The moral-awareness sense dominates the relevant verses.

**G6083 sunoida — be conscious of**
Gloss: "to be conscious of, know" | Group: "inner self-awareness or conscience — the person's own consciousness of their moral state, which nonetheless remains subject to God's higher judgment"
Reading: 1 Cor 4:4 — "I am not aware of anything against myself, but I am not thereby acquitted. It is the Lord who judges me." This single verse shows conscience as moral self-assessment that is real but epistemically incomplete — the person can be clear in conscience yet still stand under a higher judgment. This is a sharply defined inner-being function: the person's own moral self-knowledge as distinct from God's definitive verdict. The term belongs directly on the conscience pole.
Provisional classification: Characteristic-bearing, conscience pole. Single verse but sharply evidenced.

**G0843 autokatakritos — self-condemned**
Gloss: "self-condemned" | Group: "condition of the inner person whose own beliefs or conduct become the instrument of their condemnation — the conscience turned against the self"
Reading: Tit 3:11 — "such a person is warped and sinful; he is self-condemned." This is not conscience as a functioning faculty — it is conscience as turned prosecutor. The person's own inner moral system has passed a verdict against them. This is a terminal state or an outcome, not a process or faculty. The root (katakrinō = to condemn) points to the judicial domain. The self- (auto-) prefix makes the condemnation self-generated. It sits at the far end of the conscience-functioning spectrum: conscience gone to verdict.
Provisional classification: This is genuinely conscience-related, but it names the collapse or inversion of conscience — the self-judged state — rather than conscience as functioning capacity. It is a T1 term in the cluster (it carries inner-being content), but it sits at the terminus of conscience's operation, not the faculty itself. It could be a sub-group in its own right ("conscience at verdict") or it could belong in a BOUNDARY designation as the condemned-conscience outcome. Decision deferred to Phase 4 control read. Recording as OQ-001 (see below).

**G1760 enthumeomai — to reflect on**
Gloss: "to think, ponder, reflect" | Group: "inner act of reflecting or considering — deliberate inner thought directed at a situation, a vision, or a moral question"
Reading: Mat 1:20 — Joseph "considered these things" (i.e., Mary's pregnancy) before the angel appeared; Mat 9:4 — Jesus perceiving the scribes' "thoughts/reflections" which are evil; Act 10:19 — Peter "pondering" the vision. The root ENTHUMĒ = reflection, and the related word G1761 enthumēsis = reflection. The term names deliberate inner rumination — the mind turning something over. In Mat 9:4, the content of the enthumeomai is explicitly moral (evil thoughts in hearts). In Mat 1:20 and Act 10:19, it is more purely cognitive deliberation on a situation. The term carries inner reflection but does not intrinsically carry moral self-assessment — it is the act of deliberate inner thought, which may or may not be conscience-related depending on its object.
Provisional classification: This sits more naturally at the self-awareness/reflection pole than the conscience/moral-assessment pole. Its registry origin (R085, imagination) and legacy cluster (C02) are consistent with this reading. The placement in M32 may be because inner reflection is prerequisite to conscience — you cannot have moral self-assessment without the capacity for inner reflection. But the term itself does not carry conscience; it carries the cognitive act of deliberate reflection.
Recording as OQ-002 (see below) — the question of whether enthumeomai belongs in M32 or is better placed elsewhere (e.g. M32 BOUNDARY, or a cluster with a stronger reflective/imaginative character).

**G2589 kardiognōstēs — heart-knower**
Gloss: "heart-knower of the heart" | Group: "God as the one who knows hearts — the divine inner-being knowledge of every person's innermost reality"
Reading: Act 1:24 — "You, Lord, who know the hearts of all"; Act 15:8 — "God, who knows the heart, bore witness to them." Both verses: God is the explicit subject. The term is a compound (kardia = heart + gnōstēs = knower). It names a divine attribute — God's direct epistemic access to the human inner being. This is not a human inner-being characteristic at all in the T1 sense — it is a divine action directed toward the human inner being. Its presence in this cluster is structurally unusual.
Reading the T1 definition against it: Constitutional location? God's, not the human's. Faculties engaged? Divine omniscience. Impact? On the human person's standing before God, not on the person's inner life per se. Structural opposite? God as heart-ignorant — which is theologically incoherent.
Provisional classification: This term does not meet the T1 definition as a human inner-being characteristic. It belongs in BOUNDARY (or possibly Type 2) as a term that contextualises or frames the conscience/self-awareness cluster by establishing that God's knowledge of the heart is the ultimate epistemological frame within which human conscience operates. The divine heart-knowing is not itself the human characteristic; it is the divine counter-point to human self-awareness. Recording as OQ-003.

**Sub-group A — Conscience (moral self-assessment)**
Terms: G4894 suneidō, G6083 sunoida

**Sub-group C — Conscience at Verdict (self-condemnation)**
Terms: G0843 autokatakritos
Character: Conscience in its terminal form — the state in which the person's own inner moral system turns against them and issues a condemnation. The conscience-faculty collapsed into a self-verdict.

**BOUNDARY — Divine Heart-Knowledge**
Terms: G2589 kardiognōstēs
Character: The divine counter-point to human self-awareness. God as the heart-knower establishes the ultimate epistemic frame within which conscience and self-awareness operate, but it is a divine attribute, not a human inner-being characteristic.

Role in the cluster economy: epistemological framing term. God's heart-knowledge is the definitive verdict above which the conscience cannot rise (1Cor 4:4 — "It is the Lord who judges me"). The term names the divine counter-point to human self-awareness: where the person knows themselves partially and under constraint, God knows them without remainder.

Evidence basis: Act 1:24 — invoked as the ground for decisive divine selection (God knows what humans cannot know about one another's hearts, so the lot-casting defers to him). Cross-BOUNDARY observation: kardiognōstēs and 1Cor 4:4 (M32-A anchor) are structurally paired — the sunoida verse establishes that conscience knows but does not acquit; the kardiognōstēs verses establish who does. The BOUNDARY term provides the divine side of the epistemic structure that M32-A's anchor verse opens.

**enthumeomai (G1760) — to reflect on**

Structural characterisation (T1.2.1):
enthumeomai names the act of sustained inner deliberation — the person turning something over in the mind. It occupies the BOUNDARY because it is the reflective capacity that underlies both conscience (moral self-assessment requires reflection on one's moral state) and inner attention (the sustained phase of what shit initiates). It is a prerequisite capacity, not a characteristic in its own right within M32.

Role in the cluster economy: reflective-capacity term. It is the cognitive deliberation that bridges the initial act of attending (M32-B) and the moral self-assessment of conscience (M32-A). Without the capacity to hold something in inner deliberation, neither conscience nor self-aware attending can produce their characteristic outcomes.

Evidence basis:

This verse establishes that inner deliberation is not hidden from God — which connects directly to kardiognōstēs.
- Act 10:19: Peter pondering the vision — deliberate inner engagement with a revealed reality, again followed by divine communication.

Cross-BOUNDARY observation: enthumeomai at Mat 9:4 (inner reflection transparent to Christ) and kardiognōstēs (God knows hearts) are structurally linked — both establish that the inner deliberative space is not private but transparent to divine knowledge. Together they describe the epistemological environment in which conscience and self-awareness operate: the person's inner life is transparent to God even when it is not transparent to the person themselves.

**autokatakritos (G0843) — self-condemned**

Structural characterisation (T1.2.1):
autokatakritos names the terminal state of conscience — the condition in which the person's own beliefs or conduct have become the instrument of their condemnation. It occupies the BOUNDARY because it names an outcome of the conscience faculty rather than the faculty itself. The characteristic is not the exercising of conscience but conscience having arrived at a self-issued verdict of condemnation.

Role in the cluster economy: outcome-terminus term. It shows where the conscience faculty leads when a person persists in known wrong — the conscience eventually condemns the person through their own inner moral framework. It is the negative complement to 1Cor 4:4 (M32-A): where sunoida gives a clear verdict and acknowledges God as the final judge, autokatakritos shows conscience having issued a condemning verdict that the person cannot escape because it comes from within themselves.

Evidence basis: Tit 3:11 — "such a person is warped and sinful; he is self-condemned." The single verse is diagnostic: the self-condemned person is not condemned by an external authority but by their own inner moral framework. The warped and sinful characterisation names the state of the person whose persistence in known wrong has caused their conscience to turn against them.

Cross-BOUNDARY observation: autokatakritos and sunoida (1Cor 4:4, M32-A anchor) are paired endpoints of the conscience spectrum. Sunoida shows conscience clear and operating within its proper epistemological limits. Autokatakritos shows conscience having produced a condemnatory self-verdict that no external authority needs to pronounce — the person has already condemned themselves.

**Pattern 1 — The epistemological structure of inner knowing (CLUSTER-LEVEL FINDING)**
The entire cluster — both sub-groups and all three BOUNDARY terms — organises around a single epistemological structure: the human person's inner knowing (conscience and self-awareness) is real, genuine, and morally significant, but it is constitutively limited and operates within an outer frame of divine knowing that exceeds it. This structure is evidenced at every point:
- M32-B (shit): the person can attend or refuse to attend — the faculty is real but volitionally bounded
- M32-A (sunoida, 1Cor 4:4): conscience gives a real verdict, but does not acquit — epistemically limited
- BOUNDARY (kardiognōstēs): God's knowing is the outer frame — he knows what conscience cannot finally know about itself
- BOUNDARY (autokatakritos): even the self-condemning verdict is bounded — it does not exhaust judgment
This is a cluster-level structural finding: M32 describes the inner-knowing faculty operating within an epistemological frame it does not itself control.

**Pattern 2 — OT/NT structural asymmetry (CLUSTER-LEVEL FINDING)**
All M32-B verses (7) are OT Hebrew. All M32-A and BOUNDARY verses (10) are NT Greek. No term bridges both testaments. The cluster shows two distinct vocabulary traditions addressing the same territory from different angles: the Hebrew tradition names the faculty of deliberate attending (shit); the NT Greek tradition names the conscience as inner moral co-knowing (suneidō, sunoida) and the epistemological frame (kardiognōstēs). These are not translations of each other — they are distinct conceptual contributions. The absence of a Hebrew conscience term and the absence of a Greek attending-faculty equivalent in this cluster is a significant structural finding about the development of inner-being vocabulary across the testaments.

**Pattern 3 — Spirit-level silence (CLUSTER-LEVEL FINDING)**
Neither M32-A nor M32-B explicitly locates conscience or inner attention at the spirit level. Heart (lev/kardia) and soul (ne.phesh) are named; spirit (ruach/pneuma) is absent across the entire cluster's active term evidence. This is a genuine silence — not a gap in the data but an absence in the testimony. Conscience and self-awareness in this cluster operate at the soul/heart constitutional level, not the spirit level. This is significant for the programme's spirit-soul-body boundary investigation.

**Pattern 4 — Transparency to divine knowledge (CLUSTER-LEVEL FINDING)**
All three BOUNDARY terms converge on a single theme: the person's inner knowing is transparent to God. kardiognōstēs: God knows hearts completely (Act 1:24, Act 15:8). enthumeomai: inner deliberation is perceived by Christ (Mat 9:4). autokatakritos: the conscience-verdict does not require external pronouncement — it is already known (Tit 3:11). And M32-A's 1Cor 4:4: conscience operates under the awareness that God's judgment is above it. The cluster as a whole establishes that the human inner knowing faculty — whether attending, reflecting, or conscience-assessing — operates in a space that is transparent to God.

**Pattern 5 — The attending-reflecting-assessing sequence**
Across M32-B (shit: initial attending), BOUNDARY (enthumeomai: sustained deliberation), and M32-A (conscience: moral self-assessment), the cluster traces a three-stage inner-being sequence: attend → deliberate/reflect → assess/know. This is not stated as a sequence in any single verse but is the structural logic that distributes the terms across the sub-groups and BOUNDARY. This is an emergent cluster-level finding — the cluster together describes the full arc of inner moral self-knowing.
