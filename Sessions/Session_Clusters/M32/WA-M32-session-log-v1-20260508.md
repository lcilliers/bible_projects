## 3. Key analytical findings from Phase 8

### M32-A (Conscience) — selected findings

**The epistemological structure of conscience (1Cor 4:4):** conscience gives a real moral verdict but does not acquit — it is epistemically limited, operating under God's higher judgment as its outer constraint. This is the governing structural insight of the sub-group and a primary anchor finding.

**Two modes of conscience:** moral complicity (Act 5:2 — Sapphira's inner co-knowledge of the deception) and moral self-assessment (1Cor 4:4 — Paul's clear conscience). A third mode (general cognitive awareness, Act 12:12, 14:6) shows the conscience-faculty's broader semantic range.

**NT coinage without Hebrew equivalent:** suneidō and sunoida represent a distinctively NT conceptual contribution — no direct Hebrew equivalent exists. The conscience vocabulary is a new inner-being category introduced in the NT period.

**Volition without compulsion:** conscience informs volitional response (Act 5:2 — Sapphira knows and proceeds anyway) but does not determine it. The faculty is real; its authority over volition is not absolute.

**Primary anchor:** 1Cor 4:4 — the verse that most fully defines the conscience faculty's nature, operation, and epistemological limits.

### M32-B (Self-Awareness / Inner Attention) — selected findings

**The conglomerate split:** the most consequential analytical act of Phase 6 — group 3578-001 split into active attending (Psa 13:2, Psa 48:13, Pro 24:32, Jer 31:21) and absent/refused attending (Exo 7:23, 1Sa 4:20, 2Sa 13:20). These groups require different anchors and carry different findings.

**The attending-outcome sequence:** attending → instruction (Pro 24:32); attending → accurate testimony (Psa 48:13); attending → orientation for return (Jer 31:21). The characteristic is consistently upstream of its outputs.

**Heart and soul as constitutional location:** Psa 13:2 explicitly collocates shit (inner attending) with le.vav (heart) and ne.phesh (soul). Heart is the dominant named location across the sub-group (Exo 7:23, 1Sa 4:20, 2Sa 13:20 all name heart). Spirit is absent.

**Jer 31:21 as primary anchor:** the verse where inner attending is directed at the self's own moral history (the road by which you went) as the divinely commanded precondition for covenant return. This verse places the attending faculty at the threshold of conscience and repentance — its most morally significant use.

**Willed refusal as morally culpable:** Exo 7:23 (Pharaoh refuses to take the plagues to heart) establishes that the non-exercise of the attending faculty is a morally evaluable act. The characteristic can be refused, and that refusal is morally significant.

### BOUNDARY structural notes

**kardiognōstēs:** divine epistemological frame — God's heart-knowledge is the outer court above conscience. Structurally paired with 1Cor 4:4.

**enthumeomai:** reflective-capacity term — the sustained inner deliberation that bridges initial attending (M32-B) and moral self-assessment (M32-A). Mat 9:4 establishes that inner reflection is transparent to divine perception.

**autokatakritos:** conscience at terminus — the self-condemned state where the conscience has turned prosecutor. Negative complement to 1Cor 4:4's clear conscience.

### Cluster-level findings (5 identified)

1. **The epistemological structure of inner knowing:** the entire cluster organises around a single structure — human inner knowing (conscience and self-awareness) is real and morally significant, but constitutively limited and transparent to divine knowing that exceeds it.

2. **OT/NT structural asymmetry:** M32-B is entirely OT Hebrew (shit); M32-A and BOUNDARY are entirely NT Greek. These are not translations of each other — distinct conceptual traditions addressing the same inner-being territory from different angles.

3. **Spirit-level silence:** neither M32-A nor M32-B explicitly locates conscience or inner attention at the spirit level. Heart and soul are named; spirit is consistently absent. This is a genuine finding about the constitutional location of these characteristics.

4. **Transparency to divine knowledge:** all three BOUNDARY terms and M32-A's primary anchor verse converge on a single theme — the person's inner knowing is transparent to God. Inner reflection, conscience, and self-knowing all operate in a space that God sees completely.

5. **The attending-reflecting-assessing sequence:** across M32-B (shit: initial attending), BOUNDARY (enthumeomai: sustained deliberation), and M32-A (conscience: moral self-assessment), the cluster traces an emergent three-stage arc: attend → reflect → assess. Not stated in any single verse but the structural logic that distributes the terms.
