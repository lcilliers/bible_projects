## Conglomerate-group finding

The existing group 1776 / 3578-001 was reviewed against the conglomerate-group split criterion (§9 Phase 6): can the group's verses be answered uniformly by a single anchor verse, or do different subsets require different anchors?

**Finding:** The 7 verses divide into two structurally distinct subsets:
- **Active engagement** — the faculty of inner attention is functioning: directing, sustaining, producing (Psa 13:2, Psa 48:13, Pro 24:32, Jer 31:21)
- **Absent / refused / redirected** — the faculty is not operating: deliberately not engaged, collapsed, or counselled away (Exo 7:23, 1Sa 4:20, 2Sa 13:20)

These subsets require different anchors. A catalogue prompt asking "what does this characteristic produce in the person?" would be answered from the active-engagement group (Jer 31:21 — instruction, repentance, return); the same prompt cannot be meaningfully anchored in the absent-attention group (where the characteristic is precisely not producing anything). The conglomerate criterion is met: **group 1776 / 3578-001 is split into two new groups.**

Existing group 1776 / 3578-001 is retired and replaced by groups 3578-A and 3578-B below.
