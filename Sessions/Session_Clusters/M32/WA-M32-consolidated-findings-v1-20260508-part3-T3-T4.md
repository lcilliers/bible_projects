## T3 — The Inner Faculties

### T3.1 — Perception

**T3.1.1** — Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Act 12:12 and 14:6: suneidō operates as inner perception — Peter perceives his situation; Paul and Barnabas perceive a threat. Act 5:2: Sapphira perceives her moral complicity. Conscience engages the perceptive faculty as its medium — inner knowing is a form of inner perception.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Psa 48:13: "consider well her ramparts, go through her citadels" — the attending follows visual/spatial observation. Pro 24:32: "I saw and considered it; I looked" — observation feeds the attending. The perceptive faculty (sight) is the intake that inner attention then engages.

**T3.1.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience deepens perception by adding moral weight to what is perceived — Act 5:2 shows perception of a morally charged situation held in inner moral awareness. Perception and conscience are integrated: conscience is a second-order faculty that works on what perception has registered.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Pro 24:32: attending deepens and converts perception — "I saw and considered it; I looked and received instruction." The attending transforms observation into instruction. It enables and deepens perception by directing it deliberately toward its object.

**T3.1.3** — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with perception reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience requires perception as its input — it assesses what has been perceived. This shows conscience as a second-order faculty: it works on what the perceptive faculty has registered, not directly on raw reality.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Perception without attending is insufficient (Exo 7:23: Pharaoh sees the plagues but does not attend). The characteristic is what transforms observation into understanding — perception is the starting point; attending is the deepening.

### T3.2 — Cognition

**T3.2.1** — Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Cognition is primary. All four verses show an act of inner knowing. 1Cor 4:4: Paul cognises his own moral state. Act 5:2: Sapphira cognises her complicity. The characteristic is essentially a cognitive act — specifically reflexive cognition directed at the self.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Cognition is the primary faculty named. All seven verses involve deliberate direction of knowing attention. Pro 24:32: attending produces instruction (cognitive output). Jer 31:21: attending produces self-knowledge of one's own history.

**T3.2.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition in the person?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience deepens cognition by directing it reflexively — toward the self's own moral state. 1Cor 4:4: the cognitive act of self-examination is conscience's primary operation.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attending enables and deepens cognition: Pro 24:32 (attending → instruction), Psa 48:13 (attending → accurate knowledge for testimony). The characteristic converts cognitive capacity into cognitive output.

**T3.2.3** — What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement reveal?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience is reflexive cognition — cognition turned on the self's own moral standing. This distinguishes it from ordinary knowing (directed outward) and names its specific inner-being function.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attention is directional cognition — cognition deliberately aimed at an object. The characteristic is what makes the cognitive faculty purposive rather than passive.

### T3.6 — Volition

**T3.6.1** — Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty?

**[A — Conscience]** E — The group description for 1643 (suneidō) states the knowing "informs volitional response." Act 5:2: Sapphira acts while holding the inner knowledge of the deception. Act 14:6: awareness → flight (volitional act). Conscience engages volition as the faculty it informs.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — The characteristic is essentially volitional. It is exercised by choice (Psa 48:13), refused by choice (Exo 7:23), suppressed by counsel (2Sa 13:20). Volition is constitutive of the characteristic — the act of setting attention is itself a volitional act.

**T3.6.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience informs but does not determine volition: Act 5:2 shows Sapphira knowing and proceeding anyway. The faculty is real; its authority over volition is not absolute. Conscience enables informed volition without compelling it.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attending enables purposive volition: Jer 31:21 (attend → return), Pro 24:32 (attend → instruction → wiser action). The characteristic equips volition with what it needs to act well.

**T3.6.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience is a moral informant for volition, not its controller. Act 5:2 establishes this: the conscience registers what is happening; the will proceeds. The relationship between conscience and will is advisory, not determinative.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attention is pre-volitional: it is the act that equips and prepares volition. The attending comes first; the action follows. Jer 31:21 shows this sequence most clearly: attend → return.

**T3.7.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency?

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attending enables and equips agency by providing instruction (Pro 24:32), direction for return (Jer 31:21), knowledge to transmit (Psa 48:13). The characteristic is an upstream enabler of agency.

**T3.7.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience → informed agency is a consistent pattern in M32-A: the knowing enables the appropriate response.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Attending → equipped agency is the dominant pattern in group 3578-A. The characteristic is productive — it consistently produces something the person can act on.

### T3.8 — Moral Evaluation

**T3.8.1** — Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty?

**[A — Conscience]** E — This is the central faculty engaged by M32-A. 1Cor 4:4: Paul exercises moral self-evaluation — examines himself and issues a verdict. Act 5:2: Sapphira holds a morally evaluative knowing of her complicity. Conscience is moral evaluation turned reflexively on the self.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Jer 31:21 and Exo 7:23 are the sharpest moral-evaluation instances. Jer 31:21: attending to own moral history enables moral self-evaluation. Exo 7:23: refusal to attend is itself morally culpable — the non-exercise of the faculty is morally evaluable.

**T3.8.2** — Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience is the moral evaluation faculty in its self-reflexive mode. It does not merely enable moral evaluation — it is moral evaluation applied to the self.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attending enables moral evaluation by providing its prerequisite: you cannot morally self-assess what you have not first attended to. The attending is what opens the person to moral evaluation of their own condition (Jer 31:21).

**T3.8.3** — What does the pattern reveal?

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience = self-reflexive moral evaluation. This is the defining pattern of M32-A.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — Inner attending is the access point for moral evaluation — it enables but does not itself constitute moral evaluation. Distinction from M32-A: shit opens the door; conscience walks through it.

**[A — Conscience]** E — Conscience is the characteristic itself.

**[B — Self-Awareness / Inner Attention]** E — M32-B is the enabling condition for M32-A. The attending faculty is what makes conscience possible.
