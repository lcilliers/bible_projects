## 1. Cluster label and scope

The cluster is named "Conscience and Self-Awareness." This name covers two distinguishable inner-being functions:

- **Conscience** — the faculty of moral self-assessment; the person's knowledge of their own moral standing, complicity, or condition
- **Self-awareness** — the person's deliberate inner attention; the cognitive act of reflecting on, attending to, or considering a matter

The cluster title names these as paired but they are not identical. The debate below examines how the six terms distribute across these two poles.

---

## 2. T1 characteristic definition applied

A T1 term carries a distinct inner-being characteristic with identifiable constitutional location, faculty engagement, recognisable impact, structural opposite, and distinguishable directional object.

Terms that carry the characteristic → assigned to sub-groups.
Terms that qualify/contextualise → BOUNDARY.
Terms that may not fit → flagged as OQ.

## 3. Term-by-term debate

### H7896K shit — to set: consider

**Registry:** R112 (mind) | **Legacy cluster:** C01 | **Occurrences:** 7 | **vc_status:** not_done

**Gloss reading:** "to set: consider" — the core image is the act of placing the mind on something; directing cognitive attention toward a matter or person. The related root forms (to set: make / put / appoint / accuse) all share the base sense of placing or positioning something with intention.

**Group description:** "inner act of setting attention — directing the mind to consider, take to heart, or attend carefully to a matter or person"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: the mind / attentive faculty
- Faculty: deliberate cognitive attention
- Impact: the matter is taken seriously; the person acts on what they have attended to
- Directional object: any matter, person, or situation — directionally neutral
- Structural opposite: failing to attend; dismissing; not taking to heart

**Characteristic determination:** This term carries a genuine inner-being characteristic — deliberate inner attention. It sits at the self-awareness pole of the cluster (the person directing their own cognitive attention) rather than the conscience pole (moral self-assessment). The cluster title's "self-awareness" label accommodates it, provided it is grouped with similarly-oriented terms and not conflated with the moral-assessment terms.

**Proposed sub-group:** Sub-group B (Self-Awareness / Inner Attention)

### G4894 suneidō — be aware

**Registry:** R026 (conscience) | **Legacy cluster:** C13 | **Occurrences:** 5 (3 active verse records) | **vc_status:** not_done

**Gloss reading:** "to share knowledge with; to be conscious (of oneself) aware" — the sun- prefix (= with, together) indicates shared knowing. The term names knowing something alongside oneself — inner co-knowledge.

**Group description:** "conscious inner awareness — moral complicity or cognitive recognition — that informs volitional response"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: inner consciousness; the knowing faculty
- Faculty: self-aware moral cognition
- Impact: informs volitional response — the person acts on the basis of what they know about their own involvement
- Directional object: one's own acts, knowledge, or standing
- Structural opposite: unknowing, unawareness, moral ignorance of one's own involvement

**Characteristic determination:** This is a conscience-pole term. The moral complicity sense (Act 5:2 — Ananias's wife's inner knowledge of what they kept back) is the sharpest illustration. The cognitive recognition senses (Act 12:12, Act 14:6) shade toward general awareness. The term belongs to the conscience sub-group on the basis of its structural home in R026 and the moral-awareness reading of Act 5:2.

**Note:** The non-moral uses (Act 12:12, 14:6) are worth flagging — the term can operate as cognitive awareness without explicit moral content. The Phase 4 control read will test whether these verses should be set aside or retained.

**Proposed sub-group:** Sub-group A (Conscience)

### G6083 sunoida — be conscious of

**Registry:** R026 (conscience) | **Legacy cluster:** C13 | **Occurrences:** 1 | **vc_status:** extracted_thin

**Gloss reading:** "to be conscious of, know" — closely related to suneidō (same sun- root, different verb form). This is the first-person self-knowledge form.

**Group description:** "inner self-awareness or conscience — the person's own consciousness of their moral state, which nonetheless remains subject to God's higher judgment"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: the moral conscience
- Faculty: self-aware moral knowledge
- Impact: the person holds a clear conscience but remains under God's judgment; the conscience-verdict does not exhaust the verdict
- Directional object: one's own moral state
- Structural opposite: moral self-ignorance

**Characteristic determination:** The single verse (1 Cor 4:4) is one of the sharpest conscience statements in the NT. The term is cleanly at the conscience pole. Its extracted_thin status (1 verse) limits statistical confidence but the verse is definitionally clear.

**Proposed sub-group:** Sub-group A (Conscience)

### G0843 autokatakritos — self-condemned

**Registry:** R024 (condemnation) | **Legacy cluster:** C13 | **Occurrences:** 1 | **vc_status:** not_done

**Gloss reading:** "self-condemned" — auto- (self) + katakrinō (to condemn). The person has condemned themselves by their own inner moral framework.

**Group description:** "condition of the inner person whose own beliefs or conduct become the instrument of their condemnation — the conscience turned against the self"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: the conscience / inner moral judge
- Faculty: the moral self-assessment faculty — but here in its inverted or terminal form
- Impact: the person stands condemned by their own inner moral system; the conscience has issued its own verdict
- Directional object: self
- Structural opposite: the self-acquitted, or the person whose conscience is clear

**Characteristic determination:** This term is ambiguous in a structurally important way. It names a **state** or **outcome** (being self-condemned) rather than a faculty. Conscience as a faculty produces self-condemnation as one possible outcome. The question is whether the outcome warrants its own sub-group, or whether it belongs in BOUNDARY as an outcome-marker.

**Decision deferred to Phase 4 (OQ-001).** Two options:
1. Sub-group C (Conscience at Verdict) — a small, single-term sub-group naming the terminal state of conscience
2. BOUNDARY — placed as an outcome-term that contextualises the conscience sub-group's operation without itself being a faculty

**Provisional placement:** Sub-group C (pending Phase 4 resolution of OQ-001)

### G1760 enthumeomai — to reflect on

**Registry:** R085 (imagination) | **Legacy cluster:** C02 | **Occurrences:** 9 (3 active verse records) | **vc_status:** vc_completed

**Gloss reading:** "to think, ponder, reflect" — from ENTHUMĒ (reflection). Related words: enthumēsis (reflection) and thumos (wrath/passion). The compound suggests inward-directed passionate thought — something stronger than neutral deliberation.

**Group description:** "inner act of reflecting or considering — deliberate inner thought directed at a situation, a vision, or a moral question"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: the inner reflective faculty (imagination/mind)
- Faculty: deliberate inner rumination — turning something over in the mind
- Impact: clarification of a situation or question; may produce a decision or response
- Directional object: a situation, vision, or moral question — directionally broad
- Structural opposite: unreflective action; impulsive response without inner deliberation

**Characteristic determination:** This term carries inner reflection as a genuine inner-being act. However, two concerns:

1. **Registry mismatch:** R085 (imagination) and legacy C02 are a different analytical home from the conscience cluster. The cognitive-reflective function of enthumeomai is not the same as moral self-assessment.

2. **Verse evidence:** Mat 1:20 (Joseph pondering what to do) and Act 10:19 (Peter pondering the vision) are deliberate cognitive reflection without explicit moral self-assessment. Mat 9:4 (scribes "thinking evil in their hearts") shows enthumeomai used for morally-charged reflection, but the moral character is in the content of the thought (evil), not intrinsic to the term.

**OQ-002 (active):** Does enthumeomai belong in M32, or does it belong in a cluster with stronger reflective/cognitive character? Its placement in M32 may be because inner reflection underlies conscience — you cannot have moral self-assessment without the capacity for deliberate inner reflection. If so, it belongs in Sub-group B alongside shit (inner attention). Alternatively, it may be better placed in BOUNDARY as the reflective-capacity term that underlies the conscience terms without itself being conscience.

**Provisional placement:** Sub-group B (Self-Awareness / Inner Reflection) — pending Phase 4 resolution of OQ-002

### G2589 kardiognōstēs — heart-knower

**Registry:** R183 (heart) | **Legacy cluster:** C01 | **Occurrences:** 2 | **vc_status:** extracted_thin

**Gloss reading:** "heart-knower of the heart" — compound of kardia (heart) + gnōstēs (knower). Names the one who knows hearts.

**Group description:** "God as the one who knows hearts — the divine inner-being knowledge of every person's innermost reality"

**T1 test:**
- Constitutional location: God's, not the human person's
- Faculty: divine omniscient knowledge of the human inner being
- Impact: the human stands known before God, even in what they cannot know of themselves
- Directional object: human hearts — but God is the subject, not the human
- Structural opposite: God as heart-ignorant — theologically incoherent

**Characteristic determination:** This term fails the T1 test as a human inner-being characteristic. The subject is divine. The faculty is divine. There is no human inner-being act named by the term. What the term does is establish the ultimate epistemic frame: God's heart-knowledge is the court above human conscience. This relationship — divine knowing vs human self-knowing — is structurally constitutive of the conscience cluster, but the term itself names the divine side of that relationship.

**OQ-003 (active):** Confirm BOUNDARY placement for kardiognōstēs. The term contextualises the cluster's human conscience terms by naming their epistemological outer limit (God knows what the human's conscience cannot fully know about itself). It is not a human inner-being characteristic.

**Provisional placement:** BOUNDARY — pending Phase 4 confirmation of OQ-003
