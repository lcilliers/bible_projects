### Tier T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**[M10-SYNTH] T0-GUIDE-01**
T0 is the theological ground tier for M10. Before reading any characteristic-level data in T0, Session C must hold one orienting finding: every M10 characteristic defines God's character by negation and contrast. God's holiness, mercy, patience, moral omniscience, faithfulness, and justice are each illuminated across T0 by what sin does against them. This means T0 does not primarily describe sin — it describes God. Sin is the darkness that makes God's light visible. Session C should read T0 not as background theology but as the active frame within which every window on sin is set. No chapter on sin should be written without first establishing whose character the sin is defined against.

**[M10-SYNTH] T0-GUIDE-02**
T0.2.1 returns S (silent on positive created purpose) for all 22 characteristics without exception. This is the single most consistent finding in the entire tier. Session C should not imply that sin serves any purpose in the reader's formation, development, or journey. It has no designed function. It equips nothing, enables nothing, fulfils nothing. It is purely a departure from design. This boundary should be held clearly in every chapter: sin is not a stage in a journey toward maturity; it is the interruption of that journey.

**[M10-SYNTH] T0-GUIDE-03**
T0.3.1 shows that every M10 characteristic operates by perverting an image-capacity rather than eliminating it. The will that sins is the same will that can confess and turn. The speech that blasphemes is the same speech designed to praise. The authority that transmits sin is the same authority designed to form community toward God. Session C should use this finding consistently: sin is serious precisely because it is the image's own capacities turned against their design — and restoration is always possible precisely because those capacities remain present. The image is not destroyed by sin; it is perverted.

**[M10-SYNTH] T0-GUIDE-04**
T0.4.1 shows that the Christological resolution is not confined to CHAR-14 (forgiveness) but is woven through at least seven characteristics as the eschatological answer embedded in the evidence itself: CHAR-1, CHAR-3, CHAR-11, CHAR-14, CHAR-16, CHAR-19, CHAR-22. Session C should not treat the Christological thread as a concluding addition to each chapter — it is embedded in the data of each characteristic and belongs at the natural convergence point within each window.

### Tier T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation

**[M10-SYNTH] T1-GUIDE-01**
T1 is the definitional tier — it names what each characteristic IS, how it operates, and what its structural opposite is. When Session C reads T1 across the 22 characteristics, the primary finding is the cluster's internal architecture: not all 22 are the same kind of thing. Some are acts (CHAR-1, 2, 9), some are processes (CHAR-4, 5, 6), some are conditions (CHAR-11, 12, 13), and some are constituted character states (CHAR-16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22). Session C should read T1 as the map of these distinctions before writing any chapter — mixing act-characteristics and character-characteristics in the same chapter will produce confusion.

**[M10-SYNTH] T1-GUIDE-02**
T1.2.3 (working descriptions) provides the most reader-accessible single-paragraph description of each characteristic. Session C should treat these as starting-point definitions for each characteristic referenced in a chapter, not as chapter headings in their own right. The working descriptions are accurate but analytical — Session C's task is to translate them into reader-facing language that carries the same precision without the programme vocabulary.

**[M10-SYNTH] T1-GUIDE-03**
T1.3.1 (structural opposites) is essential for Session C to read before writing any chapter. Each characteristic's structural opposite is named explicitly and often carries the most vivid language in the tier. The structural opposites collectively constitute a preliminary portrait of what sin's absence looks like — which is precisely the question that needs to be answered when sin's opposite is eventually described. Session C should flag these as a resource for that future work.

### Tier T2 — Constitutional Location

**[M10-SYNTH] T2-GUIDE-01**
T2 answers the question: where in the person does this characteristic live? The finding that matters most for Session C is this: the heart is the near-universal constitutional home of M10's characteristics. 19 of 22 characteristics locate explicitly in the heart. The three exceptions (CHAR-2, CHAR-7, CHAR-12) are analytically significant — their non-heart-location is what makes them distinctive. Session C should use T2 to establish that when Scripture talks about sin, it is talking about something that happens in the heart's deliberating, willing, knowing centre — not primarily in behaviour. Behaviour is the output; the heart is the origin.

**[M10-SYNTH] T2-GUIDE-02**
T2.1 (spirit-level) produces one finding of unique importance: CHAR-20 (Faithlessness) is the only characteristic in M10 with an explicit spirit-level command against it — Mal 2:15/16 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless." This is the only place in the cluster where the spirit (*rûaḥ*) is named as the specific level from which a sin-characteristic must be resisted. Session C should treat this as the cluster's deepest constitutional finding: faithlessness requires spirit-level vigilance, not merely behavioural restraint.

**[M10-SYNTH] T2-GUIDE-03**
T2.8 (constitutional deposit) is the tier's most practically important section for Session C. Every characteristic leaves a deposit — in the person's character, in the divine record, in the community or generation. No deposit is described as permanently beyond divine removal. Session C should use T2.8 to establish both the seriousness of sin's entrenchment (the deposits are real and deep) and the completeness of the resolution (every deposit is removable by divine action). These two truths together constitute the pastoral core of every window.

### Tier T3 — Inner Faculties

**[M10-SYNTH] T3-GUIDE-01**
T3 maps sin's engagement with every inner faculty: perception, cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, relational capacity. When Session C reads T3, the primary orientation finding is this: the conscience is the cluster's diagnostic instrument. Every characteristic has a conscience-engagement pattern — and reading those patterns across the 22 characteristics tells the story of sin's progression more clearly than any other single data source. Session C should treat T3.9 (conscience) as the narrative spine of sin's inner-being story.

**[M10-SYNTH] T3-GUIDE-02**
T3.9 (conscience) across all 22 characteristics reveals a three-stage trajectory that Session C should use as a structural frame: (1) conscience operational — firing, naming, producing guilt and confession; (2) conscience suppressed — present but overridden by the will; (3) conscience eliminated — non-functional, producing the shamelessness of Zep 3:5 and the animal-instinct destruction of Jude 10. The depth of sin's entrenchment in any characteristic is indexed by which stage of this trajectory the characteristic occupies. Session C should use this trajectory to show the reader how sin progresses rather than simply listing its forms.

**[M10-SYNTH] T3-GUIDE-03**
T3.6 (volition) shows that the will is the primary faculty across virtually the entire cluster — but its role changes dramatically as sin progresses: from active agent (choosing sin) through formed disposition (habituated) to captured slave (enslaved). Session C should read T3.6 alongside T3.9 to show the will and conscience as the two primary inner-being battlegrounds of sin: conscience is the witness; will is the decision-maker. When both are compromised, the person is constitutionally constituted in sin.

**[M10-SYNTH] T3-GUIDE-04**
T3.5 (creative faculty) is silent across most characteristics — but not CHAR-21 (perversion) and CHAR-22 (injustice). Pro 6:14's "perverted heart devises evil continually" and Psa 58:2's "in your hearts you devise wrongs" name the creative-devising capacity as captured by sin in these characteristics. Session C should note that sin in its advanced forms is not passive — the perverted and unjust person is actively, creatively, and continuously wrong. This distinguishes the advanced stage from moral negligence.

### Tier T4 — Relational Interface

**[M10-SYNTH] T4-GUIDE-01**
T4 is the relational tier — it maps sin as a relational event between the person and God, between persons, and within the spiritual landscape. The single most important reading guide for Session C is this: every characteristic's God-to-human response operates from two simultaneous poles — holiness that cannot ignore the sin, and steadfast love that keeps the relationship accessible. Neither pole cancels the other. Session C must hold both in every chapter. A chapter that presents only God's holiness produces despair; a chapter that presents only steadfast love produces presumption. The data demands both together.

**[M10-SYNTH] T4-GUIDE-02**
T4.2.1 shows that sin disrupts the human-to-God directional movement in three distinct ways: as anti-movement (the sinning act IS movement against God — Psa 51:4), as movement-blocker (preventing the approach to God), and as movement-reverser (redirecting away from God toward false objects). Session C should use this distinction to differentiate chapters: wilful sinning and sinful speech are anti-movement; conscience suppression and refusal to repent are movement-blockers; habitual defection and faithlessness are movement-reversers. The pastoral implication differs in each case.

**[M10-SYNTH] T4-GUIDE-03**
T4.4.3 (inner state when the characteristic is addressed) is the most positive data in the entire M10 dataset. The resolved state is not the absence of sin — it is the positive reconstitution of the person: blessedness, life, fitness for commission, teaching capacity, Spirit-indwelling, participation in divine nature, new self in God's likeness, restored covenant identity. Session C should read T4.4.3 as the destination toward which every chapter should point. The resolved state is not an afterthought — it is what the entire cluster is moving toward.

**[M10-SYNTH] T4-GUIDE-04**
T4.6 (spiritual beings) shows that adversarial spiritual involvement clusters at the structural level of sin — CHAR-11 (universal condition) and CHAR-12 (enslaving power) — rather than at the episodic level of individual sinning acts. Session C should use this finding carefully: not every sinning act involves direct adversarial engagement, but every sinner is operating within a domain that adversarial power has constituted. The distinction prevents both dismissal (pretending sin has no spiritual dimension) and overstatement (attributing every sinning act to demonic activity).

### Tier T5 — Transformation, Sequence, Mechanism

**[M10-SYNTH] T5-GUIDE-01**
T5 is the progression tier — it maps how sin moves through the person over time. The primary reading guide for Session C is this: every characteristic has a two-way sequence — a downward arc (deeper entrenchment) and a reversal arc (the way out). Without exception across all 22 characteristics. Session C should use both arcs in every chapter. The data is never purely bleak because the reversal is embedded in the same evidence as the descent. This is not pastoral optimism added to grim data — it is what the data itself shows.

**[M10-SYNTH] T5-GUIDE-02**
T5.2.1 (sequence of inner states) across all 22 characteristics reveals a consistent three-stage progression that Session C can use as a structural frame for the cluster's narrative: Act (CHAR-1, 2, 9, 10) → Trajectory (CHAR-4, 5, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13) → Character (CHAR-16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22). The act becomes a habit; the habit becomes a disposition; the disposition becomes the person's moral identity. Session C should present this progression not as a mechanical inevitability but as what the data consistently evidences when sin is not addressed.

**[M10-SYNTH] T5-GUIDE-03**
T5.6 (eschatological orientation) shows that every characteristic points toward a binary eschatological horizon: unaddressed sin moves toward judgment; addressed sin moves toward life and restoration. The language is consistent across the cluster: "he shall surely live" (Eze 33:15), "I will remember their sin no more" (Jer 31:34), "the creation itself will be set free from its bondage to corruption" (Rom 8:21). Session C should use this eschatological framing not as a chapter ending but as the horizon that gives every chapter its forward orientation — what the present state of sin is moving toward, in both directions.

### Tier T6 — Inter-characteristic Relationships

**[M10-SYNTH] T6-GUIDE-01**
T6 maps how the 22 characteristics relate to each other — which ones co-occur, which precede which, which produce which. The primary reading guide for Session C is the set of functional descriptions T6.1.2 produced for each characteristic: "the subjective-experiential face," "the objective-accumulation face," "the volitional act-face," "the relational-covenantal face," "the resolution characteristic." Session C should use these as the vocabulary for explaining to a reader why multiple characteristics are being discussed in the same chapter — they are different faces of the same reality.

**[M10-SYNTH] T6-GUIDE-02**
T6 confirms four natural groupings that Session C can use as chapter-scope guides:
- **Entry cluster**: CHAR-1, 2, 9, 10 — how sin enters
- **Trajectory cluster**: CHAR-4, 5, 6, 7 — how sin deepens (tightly adjacent triad: CHAR-4 ↔ 5 ↔ 6)
- **Condition cluster**: CHAR-11, 12, 13, 15 — the structural conditions of sin's existence
- **Character cluster**: CHAR-16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22 — what the person becomes
- **Resolution**: CHAR-3, 14 — the exit characteristics
Session C does not need to write one chapter per characteristic. These groupings can constitute the chapter architecture.

**[M10-SYNTH] T6-GUIDE-03**
T6.5.2 (boundary-case verses) identifies the verses that straddle two characteristics and belong at the joints between chapters rather than inside any one: Psa 51:3 (CHAR-13/17), Psa 58:2 (CHAR-21/22), Hos 6:7 (CHAR-19/20), Hos 13:12 (CHAR-18/13), Exo 34:7 (CHAR-18/19/1). Session C should use these boundary verses as transition passages between chapters — they are the cluster's own joints, showing how one window flows into the next.

### Tier T7 — Lexical, Literary, and Human Science

**[M10-SYNTH] T7-GUIDE-01**
T7.1 (vocabulary arc) provides a resource Session C should use selectively rather than exhaustively. The primary reading guide: each characteristic's vocabulary arc traces the OT-to-NT development of the concept. Some characteristics are exclusively OT Hebrew (CHAR-17, 18, 20 — no NT vocabulary); some are primarily NT Greek (CHAR-12); most span both. Session C should use the vocabulary arc to establish the characteristic's linguistic roots before translating it into reader-facing language — the etymology often carries the most vivid and precise image of what the characteristic IS.

**[M10-SYNTH] T7-GUIDE-02**
T7.3 (human science frameworks) is the tier's most directly useful resource for Session C's reader-facing writing. The finding that applies across the entire cluster: science illuminates the horizontal dimensions of sin (mechanism, social dynamics, psychological formation, institutional consequences) with considerable precision, and diverges from the data at the vertical dimension — the relational reality before God. Session C should use T7.3 to show the reader that their lived experience of sin's mechanisms (conscience suppression, habit formation, addiction-like bonding, institutional corruption) is recognised and named by the data — before adding the vertical dimension that science cannot supply.

**[M10-SYNTH] T7-GUIDE-03**
T7.3.3 (divergences between science and verse evidence) is the most important sub-section for Session C's use. The divergence falls at the same structural point across all 22 characteristics: science describes sin's horizontal effects; the biblical data adds what sin does before God — and what God does in response. Session C should use this divergence as a bridge to the reader who may engage with the horizontal reality before accepting the vertical. Start where the reader is (the recognisable human experience); move to where the data goes (the vertical relational reality that constitutes sin as sin).

## PART B — Observations File Findings for Database Capture

*Key findings from the thematic exploration session, captured as discrete findings suitable for database ingestion. These represent cluster-level synthesis across all 22 characteristics.*

---

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-01 — Sin Is the Cluster's Sole Domain**
M10 holds 22 characteristics covering sin in its full arc from act through inner state through condition through record through resolution. Not one characteristic in the cluster describes a primary inner-being reality that is not sin or sin-related. The cluster holds together as a single governing domain. CHAR-3 (confession) and CHAR-14 (forgiveness) are sin's resolution-facing characteristics rather than sin itself, but they are inseparable from sin as their referent and belong within M10 by necessity.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-02 — The Heart Is the Cluster's Constitutional Centre**
19 of 22 M10 characteristics locate explicitly in the heart. The three non-heart-locating characteristics (CHAR-2 unintentional sinning, CHAR-7 contagious sin, CHAR-12 enslaving power) are analytically significant by their exception. The heart is not one constitutional location among many — it is the cluster's primary inner-being anchor. Sin in M10 is primarily about what happens in the heart's deliberating, willing, knowing centre, not primarily about behaviour. Behaviour is the output; the heart is the origin.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-03 — The Conscience Is the Cluster's Primary Diagnostic Instrument**
Reading T3.9 across all 22 characteristics reveals a three-stage conscience-trajectory that constitutes the cluster's most important inner-being narrative: (1) operational conscience — firing, naming, producing guilt and confession; (2) suppressed/bypassed conscience — present but overridden; (3) eliminated conscience — non-functional, producing the shamelessness of Zep 3:5 and the animal-instinct destruction of Jude 10. The depth of sin's entrenchment in any person or characteristic is indexed by the stage of this trajectory reached. The conscience is M10's primary diagnostic instrument for assessing sin's severity.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-04 — Every Characteristic Has a Two-Way Sequence**
Every M10 characteristic carries both a downward arc (deeper entrenchment) and a reversal arc (the way back). Without exception across all 22 characteristics. The reversal arc is not pastoral optimism added to grim data — it is embedded in the same evidence as the descent. This is the cluster's most important structural finding for Session C: the data is never purely bleak. The resolution is always present in the evidence.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-05 — Sin's Constitutional Deposits Are Real but Never Permanently Irremovable**
T2.8 across all 22 characteristics shows that every characteristic leaves a constitutional deposit — in the person's character, in the divine record, in the community or generation. But not one deposit is described as permanently beyond divine removal. The language of removal is consistent across the cluster: "I will not remember your sins" (Isa 43:25), "as far as the east is from the west" (Psa 103:12), "I will cleanse them" (Eze 37:23), "he shall surely live" (Eze 33:15). The seriousness of sin's deposits and the completeness of God's removal are both held in the data simultaneously.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-06 — God's Response Has a Consistent Two-Pole Structure**
Across all 22 characteristics, God's response to sin operates from two simultaneous poles: holiness that cannot ignore the sin, and steadfast love (*hesed*) that keeps the relationship accessible. Neither pole cancels the other. This is not theological balance imposed on the data — it is what the data consistently produces at T4.1.2 across every characteristic. The character of God toward sinning humanity is always holy AND always loving. This dual structure is the most theologically compressed finding in T4 and the foundational frame for all Session C writing.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-07 — The Three-Stage Sin Progression**
Reading T5.2.1 across all 22 characteristics produces a consistent three-stage progression: (1) Act — CHAR-1, 2, 9, 10; discrete events, conscience potentially responsive; (2) Trajectory — CHAR-4, 5, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13; the act has become a pattern, conscience progressively bypassed; (3) Character — CHAR-16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22; the person IS the sin, character formed, shamelessness, deposits entrenched. The act becomes a habit; the habit becomes a disposition; the disposition becomes the person's moral identity. This is not a mechanical inevitability — it is what the data evidences consistently when sin is not addressed.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-08 — Sin Is a Collective Classification That Becomes an Entity**
Sin is not the same type of inner-being characteristic as love, grace, jealousy, or mercy. At the cluster level it is a collective classification — a description of status, a descriptor of departure from design. Its evidence is in the perversion of other characteristics (moral agency, speech, authority, desire, conscience, will). However, at the individual characteristic level — particularly CHAR-12 (enslaving power), CHAR-21 (perversion), CHAR-22 (injustice) — sin crosses a threshold and becomes an entity in its own right: active, generative, self-perpetuating. The perverted heart devises evil *continually* (Pro 6:14); sin *dwells within* (Rom 7:17); whatever overcomes a person *enslaves* them (2Pe 2:19). Sin begins as a classification of departure and becomes an active force with its own internal dynamics and trajectory.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-09 — Sin Without God Retains Horizontal Reality, Loses Vertical Depth**
Removing God from the M10 framework leaves a recognisable residue: the conscience's suppression and elimination, the will's trajectory from act to habit to captivity, relational damage, constitutional deposits, institutional corruption. These are real, serious, and recognisable without the theological frame. What disappears is the vertical dimension: no ultimate moral reference point, no divine record, no eschatological trajectory, no divine removal of what has accumulated. Without God, what the data calls sin becomes what the human sciences call moral failure, antisocial behaviour, or character disorder. Real. Serious. But not the same thing. The distinction between horizontal damage and vertical violation is the most important conceptual boundary for Session C to maintain.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-10 — The Resolved State Is More Than the Absence of Sin**
T4.4.3 across all 22 characteristics shows that the state after sin is addressed is not merely sin-absent. It is positively constituted: blessed (Psa 32:1), alive (Col 2:13), fitted for commission (Isa 6:7/8), Spirit-indwelt (Act 2:38), participant in divine nature (2Pe 1:4), bearing the new self in God's likeness (Eph 4:24), teaching others (Psa 51:13), dwelling securely with shame forgotten (Eze 39:26). The reconstituted person is more aligned with God's image than the pre-sin state explicitly described. This finding directly answers the question about sin's opposite: it is not merely sin-absent but something positively constituted — the territory that M13, M26, M11, and the positive clusters will describe.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-11 — The Path of Return Has a Consistent Structure**
T4.2.2 across all 22 characteristics shows that the human movement back toward God requires the same basic structure regardless of which characteristic is being addressed: (1) honest acknowledgment — naming the specific sin accurately without minimisation; (2) volitional turning — the will's active reorientation; (3) humility — appealing to mercy rather than claiming entitlement. Additional elements apply in specific characteristics (restitution for guilt and injustice; faith toward the specific forgiver for forgiveness; hope for faithlessness). The path back is not arbitrary or characteristic-specific. Whatever form of sin is being addressed, the structure of return is the same.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-12 — Adversarial Spiritual Involvement Clusters at the Structural Level**
T4.6 across all 22 characteristics shows that adversarial spiritual involvement is not uniformly present across M10. It clusters specifically at the structural characteristics: CHAR-11 (universal condition — "from the power of Satan," Act 26:18) and CHAR-12 (enslaving power — "of the devil," 1Jo 3:8; "the evil one does not touch him," 1Jo 5:18). The episodic characteristics (conscience suppression, refusal to repent, habitual defection, guilt, iniquity) are largely silent on adversarial involvement. The sinner is not necessarily engaging the devil in every sinning act — but they are operating within a domain that adversarial power has constituted. The individual episodic level is a human moral-responsibility event; the structural level has a spiritual-power dimension.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-13 — Science Is a Precise Conversation Partner for M10's Horizontal Dimensions**
The human sciences engage sin's horizontal dimensions with considerable precision: moral disengagement theory (Bandura) for CHAR-1, CHAR-4, CHAR-18, CHAR-19; habit formation research for CHAR-6; addiction research for CHAR-5, CHAR-12; attachment theory for CHAR-20; institutional sociology for CHAR-7; shame/guilt distinction (Tangney) for CHAR-17; procedural justice theory for CHAR-22; intergenerational trauma theory for CHAR-15. The divergence falls consistently at the vertical dimension — the relational reality before God, the divine record, the eschatological trajectory, the divine removal. Science illuminates the mechanism; the biblical data adds the relational and eschatological dimensions that science cannot supply.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-14 — M10's Role in Session D**
M10 will function as the cluster that anchors the understanding of sin's inner-being anatomy for the entire programme. In Session D cross-cluster synthesis, M10 provides: (1) the negative baseline against which positive inner-being characteristics (love, faithfulness, righteousness, joy, peace) are measured; (2) the constitutional deposit framework (T2.8) applicable across all clusters; (3) the conscience-trajectory model (T3.9) as the programme's primary inner diagnostic instrument; (4) the two-pole God-response structure (T4.1.2 — holiness and steadfast love) as the theological frame for all relational interface findings across the programme; (5) the classification-becomes-entity question (OBS-08) as a structural question to be tested against the positive clusters. M10's findings should be read by Session D as the shadow whose shape defines the light.

**[M10-SYNTH] OBS-15 — Session C Architecture for M10**
Based on the thematic exploration of all 8 tier files, Session C for M10 should not write one chapter per characteristic. The recommended architecture is six reader-facing windows, each drawing from multiple characteristics but telling a coherent portion of the sin story:
- **Window 1: The Entry Point** — How sin begins (CHAR-1, 2, 10, 11, 15)
- **Window 2: The Descent** — What happens when sin is not addressed (CHAR-4, 5, 6, 7, 12)
- **Window 3: Sin's Weight** — What sin accumulates before God and in the person (CHAR-13, 17, 18, 15)
- **Window 4: What Sin Makes of a Person** — How sin forms from the inside out (CHAR-16, 19, 20, 21, 22)
- **Window 5: Sin's Social and Relational Face** — What sin does to bonds and communities (CHAR-7, 8, 9, 20, 22)
- **Window 6: The Way Through** — The resolution (CHAR-3, 14, and the reversal arcs of all characteristics)
Each window should carry: the T0 theological frame, the T5 two-way sequence (downward and reversal), the T4 resolved state as horizon, and the T7 science-bridge for reader accessibility. The boundary-case verses from T6.5.2 belong at the transitions between windows.
