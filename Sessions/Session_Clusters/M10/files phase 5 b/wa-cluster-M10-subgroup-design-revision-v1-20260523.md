## §1. Candidate characteristics identified

Each characteristic is a single, named inner-being faculty or operation evidenced across multiple verses.

### Char-R1 — Wilful sinning

**Inner-being operation:** The will committing a known wrong — sinning as a deliberate relational act directed against God, another person, or the self, where the person knows what they are doing and does it.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Deliberateness is present; the will is not ignorant or enticed but choosing.  
**Keyword family:** `defection wilful` (16), `sin committed` (8), `sin relational` (8), `will defecting` (10), `defection willful` (15)  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~55V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary  
**Sample anchors:** Gen 39:9 (Joseph names it as sinning against God), Jos 7:20 (Achan's deliberate confession), 2Sa 12:13 (David: "I have sinned against the Lord"), Heb 10:26 (sinning deliberately after knowing truth), Dan 9:5 (cascade of confessed wilful failures)

---

### Char-R2 — Unintentional sinning

**Inner-being operation:** Sin committed without deliberate intent — violation that is real and morally consequential even though the inner person did not consciously defy God's command; once it surfaces to awareness, conscience is engaged and remedy is required.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Ignorance or inadvertence at the time of the act; awareness comes after.  
**Keyword family:** `violation inadvertent` (paired with `sin real` 4x, `ignorance` 3x), `unintentional violation`, `sin real`, `realization post-act`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~32V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary (Lev 4–6 block)  
**Sample anchors:** Lev 4:2 (unintentional; still real sin), Lev 5:17 (sinned unknowingly yet bears iniquity), Num 15:27 (unintentional sin still requires offering), Num 22:34 (Balaam: sin from ignorance requiring acknowledgment), 1Sa 14:33 (ritual transgression arising from urgency not defiance)

---

### Char-R3 — Confession: the conscience naming sin

**Inner-being operation:** The moment the inner person names a committed sin as sin — first-person confession, corporate declaration, or the conscience voicing an act already done. The faculty at work is conscience-in-confessional-mode: the will owns what was done.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Looking back at a completed act and naming it; distinct from guilt-recognition (which is the felt weight) and from repentance (which is the turning).  
**Keyword family:** `guilt acknowledged` (25), `sin acknowledged` (11), `confession verbal` (8), `confession corporate` (8), `conscience naming` (10)  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~42V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary  
**Sample anchors:** Exo 9:27 (Pharaoh: "I have sinned"), Num 21:7 (people: "we have sinned"), 1Sa 7:6 (Israel's corporate confession), Dan 9:5–15 (Daniel's fourfold layered confession), Luk 15:18–21 (prodigal's planned and spoken confession)

---

### Char-R4 — Conscience suppression and self-deception

**Inner-being operation:** The inner person who does not recognize, denies, or suppresses awareness of their own sinful state — conscience absent, self-blinded, or actively claiming innocence where guilt is real. The faculty is conscience in its suppressed or inverted state.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Not ignorance (Char-R2) but suppression — the person has enough information to know but refuses to see; distinguished from wilful sinning (Char-R1) by the deception of the self about one's own state.  
**Keyword family:** `conscience suppressed` (16), `self-deception` (10), `conscience absent` (10), `awareness lacking` (3 co-occ with `self-blindness`), `innocence claimed` (15 — especially when false)  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~28V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primarily, some M10-B  
**Sample anchors:** Jer 2:35 (sinning compounded by inner denial of having sinned), Jer 16:10 (people fail to recognize their own sinning), 1Jo 1:10 (denying sinfulness = making God a liar), Ecc 8:12 (sinner repeatedly does evil, treats prosperity as vindication), Pro 30:20 (eats, wipes mouth: "I have done no wrong")

---

### Char-R5 — Refusal to repent: departure withheld

**Inner-being operation:** The will that persistently refuses to turn from a sinful course — not ignorance, not occasional lapse, but the established inner orientation of choosing to remain in sin when departure is available and known to be required. The characteristic is the will's active withholding of turning.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Turning is known, available, and withheld — distinct from habitual defection (which is pattern-repetition) by the specific element of refusal when confronted.  
**Keyword family:** `turning withheld` (9), `departure refused` (14 across both sub-groups), `will refusing` (12), `sin persisting` + `turning withheld` (3 co-occ), `will clinging` (3 co-occ with `departure refused`)  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~35V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary; some M10-B  
**Sample anchors:** 2Ki 3:3 (Jehoram clung to Jeroboam's sin), 2Ki 15:18 (Menahem did not depart all his days), Eze 18:24 (turning from righteousness back to sin — the reversal of departure), Heb 3:17 (sinned in wilderness, prolonged defiance), 1Jo 3:6 (continuing to sin reveals the person has never truly known Christ)

---

### Char-R6 — Habitual defection: sin as settled pattern

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as a recurring, established pattern of the will — not a single act but a way of living; the will repeatedly chooses the same wrong course, making sinning a habitual orientation. The characteristic is the will's settled direction toward sin.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Pattern and repetition over time — distinguished from refusal-to-repent (Char-R5) by the temporal-accumulative dimension rather than the specific moment of refusal.  
**Keyword family:** `defection habitual` (19), `sin habitual` (9), `defection habitual` + `sin persistent` (3 co-occ), `sin persisting` (3 co-occ with `turning withheld`), `defection repeated`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~30V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary; some M10-B  
**Sample anchors:** Hos 4:7 (sinning intensifies with prosperity — compulsive escalation), Hos 8:11 (sinning ritualized and self-reinforcing), Hos 13:2 (sinning more and more — compulsion deepening), 1Jo 3:8 (habitual practice discloses allegiance), 1Ti 5:20 (persisting in sin — will entrenched)

---

### Char-R7 — Contagious sin: leadership transmitting moral defection

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as a moral direction set by a leader whose inner commitment to wrongdoing is transmitted to those under authority — the will of those who follow is bent by the influence of a corrupting leader. The characteristic is sin as socially and spiritually contagious, mediated through authority.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The sinning is transmitted through social/authority relationship, not chosen independently — distinguished from habitual defection (Char-R6) by the source in another person's will.  
**Keyword family:** `leadership corrupting` (8–10 across both sub-groups), `defection transmitted` (6 in M10-B, 3 co-occ with `sin contagious`), `sin contagious`, `contagion moral`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~32V  
**Source pool:** M10-A primary (Jeroboam pattern), some M10-B  
**Sample anchors:** 1Ki 14:16 (Jeroboam drew whole nation into sin), 1Ki 16:2 (charged with misusing authority to lead Israel into sin), 2Ki 17:21 (Jeroboam drove them from the Lord), Jer 32:35 (caused by corrupting leadership into child sacrifice), 2Pe 2:14 (enticing unstable souls — contagious inner corruption)

---

### Char-R8 — Political revolt as sinful act

**Inner-being operation:** Breaking submission to established authority — the wilful act of a subordinate people or person asserting independence against a governing relationship; in Scripture's moral vocabulary, this defection carries the weight of sinful transgression.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The sin is specifically political-relational defection — authority relationship broken; distinct from covenant-betrayal (M10-G) by the political rather than covenantal frame.  
**Keyword family:** `defection political` (3 co-occ with `authority rejected`, `submission broken`), `authority rejected` (3), `submission broken` (3), `loyalty severed`, `independence asserting`  
**Estimated verse volume:** 9V  
**Source pool:** M10-A (pa.sha secondary allocation — 9 verses only)  
**Sample anchors:** 1Ki 12:19 (Israel's ongoing rebellion against Davidic house), 2Ki 1:1 (Moab breaks political submission), 2Ki 8:20 (Edom revolts from Judah)

---

### Char-R9 — Sinful speech: blasphemy and slander (M06 cross-register)

**Inner-being operation:** Sin committed through the mouth directed against God or persons — verbal acts that originate in inner moral corruption (heart-generated: Mat 15:19; Mar 7:22) and express themselves as slander, contemptuous assertion, or blasphemous presumption.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The instrument of the sinful act is speech; the source is inner moral corruption or hostility.  
**Keyword family:** `corruption verbal` (5 in M10-H but present in M10-A blasfēmia terms), `speech perverted` (3 co-occ with `corruption verbal`)  
**Cross-register:** M06 (Hate, Contempt and Hostility) preserved throughout  
**Estimated verse volume:** 22V  
**Source pool:** M10-A (blasfēmia 18V + blasfēmos 4V)  
**Sample anchors:** Mat 15:19 (slander originates in the heart), Mar 7:22 (inner-generated evil), Joh 10:33 (blasphemy as presumptuous claim to divinity), Rev 13:6 (comprehensive verbal assault from the will)

---

### Char-R10 — Enticement, hypocrisy, apostasy: specialised sinful mechanisms (M14/M08/M31 cross-register)

**Inner-being operation:** Sin committed through a specific inner mechanism — desire exploited by enticement (deleazō: the desire-hook), identity falsified through performed hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai), or covenantal identity abandoned through apostasy (apostasia).  
**Distinguishing feature:** The sin operates through a specific inner faculty or mechanism rather than direct willing.  
**Keyword family:** (small corpus; mechanism-specific)  
**Cross-register:** M14 (deleazō), M08 (sunupokrinomai), M31 (apostasia)  
**Estimated verse volume:** 5V  
**Source pool:** M10-A small-corpus terms  
**Sample anchors:** Jam 1:14 (desire as internal hook), 2Pe 2:14 (insatiable desire bent to lure others), Gal 2:13 (hypocrisy as social moral contagion), Act 21:21 (apostasy as covenantal defection)

---

### Char-R11 — Sin as moral condition: the universal human state

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as a structural condition of the inner person — not primarily what they did but what they are under: every person is under sin's dominion, subject to it as a power, fallen short of the glory that was humanity's intended end.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Not the act of sinning but the condition of being a sinner; the universality of the state rather than individual acts.  
**Keyword family:** `sin condition` (6 in M10-B), `universality sin` (from hamartanō keywords), `guilt personal` (8 in M10-B) — especially verses framing sin as the shared human problem  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~50V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary (hamartia universal passages; chat.tat condition-state passages)  
**Sample anchors:** Rom 3:23 (all have sinned, fall short of glory), Rom 5:12 (sin entered the world, spread to all), 1Ki 8:46 (no one who does not sin), Joh 8:34 (practicing sin makes one sin's slave), Eph 2:1 (dead in trespasses — condition not act)

---

### Char-R12 — Sin as enslaving power: bondage of the will

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as an active force or power that masters the inner person, governing the will so that the person cannot freely choose otherwise — the will captive under sin's dominion.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Agency is compromised — the will is not free but enslaved; distinct from the universal condition (Char-R11) by the specific bondage-of-will element.  
**Keyword family:** `sin condition` paired with `will clinging`, `departure refused` (in M10-B: 7 instances of `departure refused`), `will persisting`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~30V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary  
**Sample anchors:** Joh 8:34 (practicing sin makes one its slave — will enslaved), Gen 4:7 (sin crouching at the door, its desire for you — predatory power), Rom 6:14/15 (no longer under sin's dominion), Gal 5:1 (do not return to yoke of slavery), 1Jo 3:9 (divine birth makes sustained sinning structurally incompatible)

---

### Char-R13 — Sin as burden: recorded weight before God

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as an objective moral weight existing before God — recorded, accumulated, not hidden from divine memory even if hidden from others; the inner person carries this record whether they feel it or not.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The weight exists in God's reckoning, not primarily in the conscience — distinct from guilt-as-felt-weight (M10-D) by its objective divine-record dimension.  
**Keyword family:** `sin recorded` (5 in M10-B), `sin accumulating` (6), `guilt accumulating` (4), `memory divine` (13 cluster-wide, concentrated in M10-B), `record divine` (8), `sin named`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~45V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary (chat.tat burden-of-sin passages; hamartia record passages)  
**Sample anchors:** Exo 32:32–34 (sin recorded, God will visit it on its appointed day), Rev 18:5 (her sins heaped to heaven — divine record at capacity), Gen 4:7 (sin crouching, its record awaiting), Lev 16:21 (all sins of Israel verbally named and transferred), Num 32:23 (your sin will find you out — record produces consequence)

---

### Char-R14 — Forgiveness sought and received: the sin-response of seeking pardon

**Inner-being operation:** The inner movement toward God in search of forgiveness — the will that turns and asks for sin's removal; and the divine response of granting, withholding, or declaring forgiveness. The characteristic is the inner act of seeking pardon and/or the experience of receiving it.  
**Distinguishing feature:** Directed toward the resolution of sin — distinct from confession (Char-R3, which names the sin) by the additional element of appealing to God for its removal.  
**Keyword family:** `forgiveness sought` (11 in M10-B, 9 cluster-lead), `pardon sought` (11), `forgiveness received` (14 cluster-wide), `forgiveness divine` (8), `burden lifted` (6), `guilt removed` (6), `guilt released`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~55V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary (hamartia forgiveness passages; chat.tat atonement-associated passages; kip.pu.rim; chat.tat sin-offering)  
**Cross-register:** M11 (Repentance, Forgiveness, Restoration) for kip.pu.rim (8V) and chat.tat sin-offering (4V)  
**Sample anchors:** Psa 51:1–2 (have mercy, blot out transgressions), Luk 7:47–48 (forgiven much, sin declared removed), 1Jo 1:9 (if we confess, faithful and just to forgive), Exo 30:16 (atonement money covering moral debt), Lev 16:34 (annual atonement for all Israel's sins)

---

### Char-R15 — Sin inherited and transmitted: the generational moral condition

**Inner-being operation:** Sin as a moral condition that enters through lineage — inherited from ancestors, transmitted through family or national belonging; the inner person carries a guilt or moral liability not only for their own acts but for the accumulated sinful history of those before them.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The source of the sin-condition is generational transmission — distinct from personal sin-as-condition (Char-R11) by its intergenerational origin.  
**Keyword family:** `guilt inherited` (10), `iniquity inherited` (7), `defection generational` (8), `guilt transmitted` (13 cluster-wide), `defection transmitted` (6 in M10-B), `sin inherited` (7), `iniquity generational`  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~40V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary; overlap with M10-A (Jeroboam transmitted pattern is Char-R7)  
**Sample anchors:** Lam 5:7 (fathers sinned and are gone, present generation bears their iniquities), Exo 20:5 (iniquity of fathers visited on children to third and fourth generation), Lev 26:39 (wasting away in the iniquity of their fathers), Jer 3:25 (sinning from youth spanning generations), Dan 9:8 (shame belonging to kings, princes, and fathers alike)

---

### Char-R16 — Wilful persistence in sin despite turning known

**Inner-being operation:** The inner state of refusing to release sin even while its wrongness is known — the will that clings to its sinful course after having been confronted with the requirement to turn; characterised by an active, chosen self-hardening. This is the obdurate will, not ignorant but entrenched.  
**Distinguishing feature:** The person knows the call to repent and actively resists it — distinct from refusal-to-repent (Char-R5, which is the act of withholding turning) by focusing on the sustained inner state of the entrenched will under confrontation.  
**Keyword family:** `departure refused` + `sin inherited` (4 co-occ), `conscience suppressed`, `will clinging` (3 co-occ with `departure refused`), `sin persisting` + `turning withheld`, `departure refused` + `turning withheld` (6 co-occ)  
**Estimated verse volume:** ~30V  
**Source pool:** M10-B primary (chat.tat persistence passages); some M10-A  
**Sample anchors:** 1Ki 8:46–47 (sinning and then confessing in exile — the sequence of sin and eventual turning), Neh 9:29 (stubborn shoulder, stiff neck — sinning against God's rules), Eze 33:12 (decisive act of sin that overrides prior righteousness), Jer 8:14 (sinning against God recognized only in consequence)
