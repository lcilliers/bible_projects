## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The 64-verse corpus reveals God's character across four dimensions. (1) *God as the one who hates perversity*: Pro 8:13 — "pride and arrogance and the way of evil and perverted speech I hate" — wisdom (personified as God's attribute) explicitly names hatred of perversity; (2) *God as the one who watches over the perversion of judgment*: Exo 23:8; Deu 16:19 — bribery blinds the wise and subverts the righteous; God's justice is the standard against which the perversion of justice is measured; (3) *God as the one who judges perversity*: Pro 21:12 — "The Righteous One observes the house of the wicked; he throws the wicked down to ruin"; Rev 19:2 — "he has judged the great prostitute who corrupted the earth with her immorality"; (4) *God as the one who disciplines rather than abandons the inwardly twisted*: 2Sa 7:14 — "when he commits iniquity, I will discipline him with the rod of men... not take my steadfast love from him" — God's response to perversity-as-iniquity is corrective discipline within sustained love rather than abandonment.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute perversion as inner inversion to God?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — One verse approaches this — Lam 3:9 — "he has blocked my ways with blocks of stones; he has made my paths crooked" — God is the subject of the crookedness-making action. However, the verse describes God's judicial action upon the sufferer (making their path crooked as discipline/judgment) rather than attributing moral perversity to God's character. God here acts as the one who twists the path, not as a morally perverted being. All other verses place perversity exclusively on the human side.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The silence (apart from Lam 3:9's judicial use) confirms that moral perversity is constitutively creaturely. God's nature is the straight standard against which perversion is defined: Pro 8:13 — wisdom hates perverted speech; Deu 16:19 — God's justice is what bribery subverts. The characteristic requires a prior moral order (which God embodies) that can be twisted; God is the source of the moral order, not subject to its distortion. Isa 29:16's "you turn things upside down" implies a right-side-up that God's own nature constitutes.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose perversion as inner inversion serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-21]** S — The corpus provides no evidence of any positive created purpose for perversion-as-inner-inversion. The characteristic is entirely a fallen-condition reality: the twisting of what was created straight and good. No verse presents moral perversity as a designed feature.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design or a response to the fallen condition?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Entirely a response to the fallen condition. Jer 2:21 — "I planted you a choice vine, wholly of pure seed. How then have you turned degenerate and become a wild vine?" — the degeneracy contrasts explicitly with the original planting of purity; the perversion is a corruption of what was first good. Eph 4:22 — "old self... corrupt through deceitful desires" — the corruption is attributed to the fallen "old self" and "deceitful desires" rather than original design. The characteristic emerges from the fall's distortion of what was created good.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that perversion as inner inversion is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes — in two directions. (1) *Judgment endpoint*: Rev 19:2 — "he has judged the great prostitute who corrupted the earth" — eschatological judgment falls on the corrupting agent; Pro 10:31 — "the perverse tongue will be cut off" — judgment silences perversity; (2) *Liberation from corruption*: Rom 8:21 — "the creation itself will be set free from its bondage to corruption and obtain the freedom of the glory of the children of God" — the eschatological fullness is freedom from corruption as a cosmic reality; 2Pe 1:4 — "having escaped from the corruption that is in the world because of sinful desire" — the believer's orientation is toward escape from corruption into divine nature.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does perversion as inner inversion express the divine image?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Perversion expresses the image through its *inverted exercise of the moral faculty*: the image-bearer is constituted with a morally ordered inner life — a mind, will, heart, and speech designed for alignment with God's order. Perversion is the twisting of these faculties against their designed grain. Isa 29:16 — the creature denying the potter's authority — is the most direct image-inversion: the image-bearer's cognitive faculty, designed to know God as creator, is so perverted it denies the creator relationship. The capacity for moral ordering (the image's positive dimension) is precisely what perversion distorts.

---

**T0.3.2 — Is perversion as inner inversion shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-21]** S — Exclusively creaturely in the moral sense. Lam 3:9's judicial use (God making paths crooked) is not God being morally perverted but God acting as judge. The characteristic in its inner-moral sense is exclusively creaturely.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence of perversion as inner inversion reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The presence of perversion reveals the image in its *internally distorted condition*: the faculties that were designed for moral alignment have been twisted against their grain. 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind and deprived of the truth" — the mind (the image's cognitive faculty) has been corrupted at its very seat of understanding. Eph 4:22 — "old self... corrupt through deceitful desires" — the desire-faculty of the image has been turned into the agent of the corruption. The image is not destroyed but its inner faculties (mind, will, heart, desire, speech) are bent away from the moral order they were designed to instantiate.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use perversion as inner inversion typologically?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes — through the vine-degeneracy type and the creature-creator inversion type. (1) *Vine type*: Jer 2:21 — "I planted you a choice vine... how then have you turned degenerate and become a wild vine?" — Israel's degeneracy is the anti-type of the faithfully-producing vine; this vine-type points toward the true vine (Joh 15 — in CHAR-20's adjacent cluster); (2) *Creature-creator inversion*: Isa 29:16 — "shall the potter be regarded as the clay... 'He did not make me'?" — the cognitive inversion of creature denying creator is the type of the ultimate eschatological reversal in which God will act as the definitive potter who reshapes the perverted creation.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — From human perversity-instance toward divine eschatological resolution. The vine's degeneracy (Jer 2:21 — human type) points toward the eschatological replanting/restoration; the creature's denial of the creator (Isa 29:16 — human perversity-type) points toward the eschatological re-establishment of the proper creator-creature order. Both directions: human perversity-instance → divine resolution.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Typological use is evidenced (T0.4.1 and T0.4.2). This prompt is superseded.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The programme names this characteristic "Perversion as inner inversion." Three signals. (1) *Perversion*: the English rendering covers moral distortion, twisting, and corruption — the turning of what was designed to be straight into something bent; (2) *As inner inversion*: the defining modifier — the perversity operates from within, not merely as external misbehaviour; the inversion is of the moral order from inside the person; (3) *Inner*: the characteristic is at the level of the person's moral faculties — mind, heart, will, speech — not merely behavioural. The name signals: the most comprehensive inner distortion-category in M10 — the person whose moral-inner constitution has been twisted against its grain.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Nine distinct term-families, the highest of any M10 characteristic. (1) **tah.pu.khah (H8419)** — "perverseness, frowardness" (VCG-01/03 dominant): the root *hāpak* means "to turn, to overturn" — what has been overturned; (2) **la.zut (H3891)** — "perversity, crookedness" (VCG-01 — Pro 4:24); (3) **sa.laph (H5557)** — "to subvert, to overthrow, to pervert" (VCG-02 — judgment perversion); (4) **a.vah (H5753A/B)** — "to be crooked, to twist, to do iniquity" (VCG-03 dominant); (5) **te.vel (H8397)** — "perversion, violation of natural/created order" (VCG-04 — Lev 18:23/20:12); (6) **sur (H5494)** — "to turn aside, to degenerate" (VCG-04 — Jer 2:21/17:13); (7) **cha.val (H2254B)** — "to destroy, to corrupt, to ruin" (VCG-05 OT — M03 cross-register); (8) **nav.lut (H5040)** — "lewdness, shame" (VCG-05 — Hos 2:10 — M07 cross-register); (9) **ftheirō/fthora/diaftheirō (G5351/5356/1311)** — NT "to corrupt, to destroy, corruption/decay" (VCG-05 NT dominant). The terms collectively reveal: perversion as twisting of the given order (tah.pu.khah/a.vah), violation of created categories (te.vel), judgment-subversion (sa.laph), and progressive decay (ftheirō/fthora).

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — All three. *Directional*: perversion always inverts something — a direction of moral movement that turns back on itself; Jer 3:21 — "perverted their way" — the directional turning of the life-path away from God; *Relational*: perversion damages the God-relationship and human relationships — Isa 29:16 — the creature denying the creator (relational inversion); Pro 6:14 — "continually sowing discord" — perversity fractures relational bonds; *Constitutional*: Eph 4:22 — "old self corrupt through deceitful desires" — the corruption is constitutional to the fallen "old self"; 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind" — the depravity is at the constitutional level of the mind's functioning.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is perversion as inner inversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The corpus reveals CHAR-21 as a *multi-faculty inner distortion condition* that operates as both a disposition and a process. (1) *Dispositional dimension*: Deu 32:20 — "a perverse generation" — perversity as a generational character trait; Pro 12:8 — "twisted mind" — perversity as a constitutive feature of the mind; 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind" — the depravity as settled condition; (2) *Process dimension*: Eph 4:22 — "is corrupt through deceitful desires" — active progressive corruption through desire; 2Pe 2:19 — "slaves of corruption" — the progressive capture; Jer 2:21 — "turned degenerate and become a wild vine" — a transformation-process of moral decay.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The corpus reveals five constituent elements corresponding to the five VCGs. (1) *Perversity of speech* (VCG-01 — 10V): tah.pu.khah — the tongue/lips as perversity's primary expressive medium; (2) *Perversion of judgment* (VCG-02 — 6V): sa.laph — bribery corrupting the cognitive-discernment faculty; (3) *Perversion of mind, will, and way* (VCG-03 — 19V): a.vah/tah.pu.khah — the twisted inner person whose mind and life-direction are bent; (4) *Sexual perversion and moral degeneracy* (VCG-04 — 4V): te.vel/sur — violation of created/family order; (5) *Moral corruption, decay, and lewdness* (VCG-05 — 25V): cha.val/nav.lut/ftheirō — comprehensive inner decay.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Working description: *Perversion as inner inversion (CHAR-21) is the inner state or disposition of moral distortion — twisting what is right, corrupting what was good, inverting the moral order from within. Five registers: (1) Perversity of speech (VCG-01 — 10V): "perverted heart devises evil, continually sowing discord" (Pro 6:14); "perverted speech" as wisdom's named hatred (Pro 8:13); (2) Perversion of judgment (VCG-02 — 6V): "bribe blinds the clear-sighted and subverts the cause of those who are in the right" (Exo 23:8); (3) Perversion of mind, will, and way (VCG-03 — 19V): "we have sinned and have acted perversely and wickedly" (1Ki 8:47); "twisted mind is despised" (Pro 12:8); "you turn things upside down!" (Isa 29:16); (4) Sexual perversion and moral degeneracy (VCG-04 — 4V): bestiality and incest named "perversion" (Lev 18:23/20:12); "turned degenerate and become a wild vine" (Jer 2:21); (5) Moral corruption, decay, and lewdness (VCG-05 — 25V): "old self corrupt through deceitful desires" (Eph 4:22); "depraved in mind" (1Ti 6:5); "slaves of corruption" (2Pe 2:19). Distinguished from CHAR-19 (boundary-crossing) by operating as inner structural distortion rather than knowing-violation, and from CHAR-18 (accumulated crime) by being the twisting of the moral faculty rather than the accumulated record.*

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of perversion as inner inversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The structural opposite is *moral straightness — the inner constitution aligned with God's created order*. Pro 8:13 — wisdom's hatred of perversion implies wisdom's straightness as the opposite; Pro 10:31 — "the mouth of the righteous brings forth wisdom, but the perverse tongue will be cut off" — the wisdom-producing righteous is the structural opposite; 2Pe 1:4 — "having escaped from the corruption that is in the world" — escape-from-corruption into divine-nature-participation; Eph 4:22-24 — "put off your old self which is corrupt" contrasted with "put on the new self, created after the likeness of God in true righteousness and holiness" — the new self in God's likeness is the structural opposite.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does perversion as inner inversion explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The characteristic explicitly excludes: (1) *Wisdom and knowledge*: Pro 2:12 — wisdom delivers from "men of perverted speech" — perversity and wisdom are mutually exclusive; 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind and deprived of the truth" — perversity of mind excludes truth; (2) *Inheritance of the kingdom*: 1Cor 15:50 — "the perishable cannot inherit the imperishable" — the corruptible state is constitutively incompatible with God's kingdom; (3) *Divine favour*: Deu 32:20 — God hides his face from a perverse generation; (4) *Proper function of judgment*: Exo 23:8 — bribery-perversion blinds and subverts what judgment is designed to produce.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is perversion as inner inversion not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Three boundaries. (1) *Not CHAR-19 (Transgression as boundary-crossing)*: CHAR-19 is the knowing step-over of a known line; CHAR-21 is the structural inner distortion that bends the moral faculty itself — a twisted mind (Pro 12:8) is not primarily transgressing a law but operating from a corrupted cognitive apparatus; (2) *Not CHAR-18 (Iniquity as accumulated crime)*: CHAR-18 is the accumulated moral-crime account before God; CHAR-21 is the inner structural corruption that generates those crimes — the twisted heart (Pro 6:14) that produces continuous evil; (3) *Not purely physical decay*: Several VCG-05 NT verses (1Cor 15:42/50; 2Cor 4:16; Col 2:22; Rom 8:21) address bodily perishability in a cosmological-anthropological sense more than moral inner-being content. These carry cross-register flags (M23/M35) — noted.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does perversion as inner inversion operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Five distinct inner modes. (1) *As a perverted heart continually devising evil*: Pro 6:14 — "with perverted heart devises evil, continually sowing discord" — the heart is the origin of the continuous evil-devising; (2) *As a depraved mind robbed of truth*: 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind and deprived of the truth" — the cognitive faculty is corrupted at its root; (3) *As corruption through deceitful desire*: Eph 4:22 — "corrupt through deceitful desires" — the desire-faculty is the active agent of the corruption; (4) *As cognitive-relational inversion*: Isa 29:16 — the creature denying the creator — the mind inverts the proper creature-creator relationship; (5) *As degenerative transformation*: Jer 2:21 — "turned degenerate and become a wild vine" — a process of inner moral transformation in the wrong direction.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does perversion as inner inversion operate differently depending on context?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes across the five VCG registers. (1) *Speech context* (VCG-01): perversity operates through the tongue — Pro 6:14 — twisted heart devising evil sown through speech; (2) *Judicial context* (VCG-02): perversity operates through bribery corrupting the discernment faculty (Exo 23:8); (3) *Personal moral context* (VCG-03): perversity operates as the twisted inner life direction — Jer 3:21 — "perverted their way and forgotten the Lord"; (4) *Sexual-created-order context* (VCG-04): perversity operates as violation of the categories God established in creation (Lev 18:23 — te.vel); (5) *Cosmic-spiritual context* (VCG-05): perversity operates as a universal corruption from which all of creation needs liberation (Rom 8:21).

---

**T1.4.3 — Does perversion as inner inversion have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes — VCG-01 (10 verses) is entirely the speech-perversion register. Pro 6:14 — perverted heart devises evil and sows discord through speech; Pro 8:13 — perverted speech named as wisdom's hated counterpart; Pro 10:31 — "the perverse tongue will be cut off"; Pro 23:33 — "your heart utter perverse things" — drunkenness producing heart-generated perverted speech. The speech-mode of perversion is the most extensively evidenced in the corpus: VCG-01 dedicates 10 verses entirely to the tongue/lips/mouth as perversity's primary communicative expression.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response to recognising one's perversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The immediate responses include confession and self-indictment. 1Ki 8:47 — "we have sinned and have acted perversely and wickedly" — immediate communal confession naming perversity specifically; Dan 9:5 — "we have sinned and done wrong and acted wickedly and rebelled" — the comprehensive confessional self-indictment; 2Sa 24:17 — "I have sinned, and I have done wickedly" — David's immediate self-indictment upon seeing the plague; Job 33:27 — "I sinned and perverted what was right" — confessional first-person recognition.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent or variable?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Variable. Confession and self-indictment (1Ki 8:47; Dan 9:5) are the productive responses. Deflection onto God appears in Pro 19:3 — "a man's folly brings his way to ruin, his heart rages against the Lord" — the immediate response to self-produced perversion is heart-rage against God rather than self-recognition. 2Pe 2:19 — those who promise freedom while enslaved to corruption — show no recognition response at all. Isa 29:16 — those who deny the creator — have an inverted response that produces further perversion rather than recognition. The range is from honest confession to deflection to complete non-recognition.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-21]** E — For VCG-04's sexual perversion (Lev 18:23/20:12 — bestiality and incest named "perversion"), the immediate inner experience of the person committing these acts is not described. The corpus names the acts and their classification as perversion and their penalty (death) but not the inner recognition or response of the one committing them.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does perversion as inner inversion produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Four sustained inner-being effects. (1) *Progressive corruption through desire*: Eph 4:22 — "corrupt through deceitful desires" — ongoing active corruption; (2) *Enslavement to the corrupting force*: 2Pe 2:19 — "slaves of corruption. For whatever overcomes a person, to that he is enslaved" — the sustained perversion produces volitional captivity; (3) *Destruction by one's own instincts*: Jude 10 — "destroyed by all that they, like unreasoning animals, understand instinctively" — the perversion produces self-destruction through unreasoning impulse; (4) *Deprivation of truth*: 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind and deprived of the truth" — sustained mind-perversion produces the inability to access truth.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained perversion establish?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Three established states. (1) *Delight in perversity*: Pro 2:14 — "who rejoice in doing evil and delight in the perverseness of evil" — sustained perversion establishes an affective orientation of joy-in-wrongdoing; (2) *Continual evil-devising*: Pro 6:14 — "continually sowing discord" — sustained perversity produces unceasing destructive scheming; (3) *Bondage to corruption*: Rom 8:21 — "bondage to corruption" — the created order itself is in sustained enslaved-corruption as a structural condition.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes. The immediate response (confession — 1Ki 8:47; self-indictment — Dan 9:5) is episodic. The sustained effects (active corruption through deceitful desires — Eph 4:22; enslavement — 2Pe 2:19; self-destruction by instinct — Jude 10; deprivation of truth — 1Ti 6:5) are constitutional states established over time. The most significant sustained effect is the transformation from agent-of-perversion to captive-of-corruption: 2Pe 2:19 — whatever overcomes a person enslaves them. The initial active perversion (the agent twisting) becomes passive enslavement (the corruption mastering the agent).

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable perversion as inner inversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Three enabling conditions. (1) *Deceitful desires*: Eph 4:22 — "old self corrupt through deceitful desires" — desires that deceive (promising satisfaction while delivering corruption) are the enabling agent; (2) *Absence of wisdom/God-fear*: Pro 8:13 — "the fear of the Lord is hatred of evil... perverted speech I hate" — the absence of God-fear removes the resistance to perversion; Pro 2:12 — wisdom delivers from perverted speech — wisdom's absence enables it; (3) *Material inducement*: Exo 23:8; Deu 16:19 — bribery as the enabling mechanism for judgment-perversion; the monetary inducement corrupts the discernment faculty.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or prevent perversion as inner inversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Three blocking conditions. (1) *Wisdom and God-fear*: Pro 8:13 — the fear of the Lord as hatred of evil including perverted speech; Pro 2:12 — wisdom delivering from perverted-speech people; (2) *Participation in the divine nature*: 2Pe 1:4 — "having escaped from the corruption that is in the world because of sinful desire" — participation in divine nature is the escape-mechanism from corruption; (3) *Putting off the old self*: Eph 4:22-24 — the deliberate act of putting off the corrupt old self; the actively renewed mind (Eph 4:23) is the blocking mechanism against deceitful-desire corruption.

---

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person consumed by perversion?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — 2Pe 2:12 names it most vividly: "like irrational animals, creatures of instinct, born to be caught and destroyed... will also be destroyed in their destruction" — the inner state is animal-instinctual operating without reason, heading toward destruction that matches their nature. 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind and deprived of the truth" — the inner state lacks the basic cognitive capacity to access truth. Jude 10 — destroyed by what they understand instinctively — the instinct has replaced moral reasoning as the inner governing faculty. The characterised inner state is: reason and moral faculty replaced by unreflective instinct, enslaved to the corruption that will ultimately destroy the self.

---

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — The primary dimension is **the moral faculty in its distorted/corrupted condition** — specifically the heart, mind, will, and desire as twisted from their designed moral orientation. Pro 6:14 — "perverted heart devises evil" — the heart as origin; 1Ti 6:5 — "depraved in mind" — the mind as primary corrupted faculty; Eph 4:22 — "corrupt through deceitful desires" — desire as corruption-agent. The characteristic operates at the level of the person's inner moral constitution rather than discrete acts or record-entries.

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — (1) Pro 6:14 — heart as the perverted origin of continual evil-devising; (2) 1Ti 6:5 — mind as the named perverted faculty; (3) Eph 4:22 — desire as the active corruption-agent within the inner person; (4) Pro 12:8 — "twisted mind" as the characterising inner trait; (5) Isa 29:16 — the mind's cognitive-relational inversion as perversion; (6) 2Cor 11:3 — "your thoughts will be led astray" — the thoughts as the corruption-target.

---

**T1.8.3 — Does perversion as inner inversion carry secondary dimensions?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes — three secondary dimensions. (1) *Speech dimension* (VCG-01 — 10V): tah.pu.khah — the tongue as the primary expressive organ of inner perversity; (2) *Social-discord dimension*: Pro 6:14 — "continually sowing discord"; Pro 16:28 — "a dishonest man spreads strife" — perversity damages communal bonds as a secondary effect; (3) *Bodily-decay dimension* (several VCG-05 NT verses): fthora — physical perishability; Rom 8:21/1Cor 15:42/50 — bodily corruption; these carry M23/M35 cross-register flags indicating their primary locus is beyond M10 inner-being content.

## T2 — Constitutional Location (31 prompts)

**T2.1.1 — Is perversion as inner inversion explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-21]** E — Yes — through two passages. (1) Job 17:1 — "my spirit is broken; my days are extinct; the graveyard is ready for me" — the spirit (*rûaḥ*) is the entity broken by corruption's action; though this is Job under affliction rather than Job's own perversity, it evidences the spirit as the level at which corruption's damage is registered; (2) Gal 6:8 — "for the one who sows to his own flesh will from the flesh reap corruption, but the one who sows to the Spirit will from the Spirit reap eternal life" — the Spirit-level sowing is set in opposition to the flesh-sowing that produces corruption; the Spirit is named as the counter-dimension where corruption does not operate.
