## VCGs

### M10-M-VCG-01 — Conscience suppression and self-blindness (8 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where the characteristic is the suppressed, absent, or inverted conscience — the person who fails to see their own sinful state. Judg 20:16 uses cha.ta in its literal physical sense (missing a target) with no inner-being moral content — a borderline verse placed here as the closest register. Job 35:6 detaches sin from any power to injure God, redirecting attention to its effect on the sinner alone, revealing an inability to see sin's full relational reality. Ecc 2:26 defines the sinner by not pleasing God — an orientation invisible to the sinner. Ecc 8:12 shows the sinner repeatedly doing evil yet treating prosperity as vindication — fundamentally the absence of the fear of God within. Jer 2:35 names the compound condition: sinning compounded by inner denial of having sinned, claiming innocence and assuming God's anger has passed. Jer 16:10 shows the people failing to recognise their own sinning despite facing severe judgment — conscience that has lost self-awareness. 1Cor 15:34 links ongoing sinning to a state of ignorance about God. Tit 3:11 (warped, sinful, self-condemned) names the person whose conscience implicitly witnesses against them while they remain unaware. Keyword axis: `conscience absent` (1), `self-deception` (1), `awareness lacking` (1), `moral absent` (1).
