## VCGs

### M10-F-VCG-01 — Transgression against God: rebellion in the divine-covenant relationship (27 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where transgression is directed against God himself as covenant partner — the will defying the one who gave Israel life, covenanted with them, and established their path. The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a relational act of rebellion: children rebelling against a nurturing father (Isa 1:2), leaders who should have represented the people before God instead transgressing against him (Isa 46:8), persistent multi-generational defiance (Eze 2:3), the rebel from birth whose will is constitutionally set against God (Isa 48:8). Includes the revolve-against-God register of pa.sha (pa.sha God-directed) and the 2Jo verse where transgressing means leaving behind Christ's teaching. Keyword axis `defection willful` (5), `defection habitual` (4), `rebellion inner` (2).

### M10-F-VCG-02 — Revolt: the inner defiance that speaks and acts against God (7 verses)

**context_description:** The sa.rah (revolt) corpus — verses where inner defiance against God is expressed as overt, voiced revolt: the false prophet teaching revolt against the Lord (Deu 13:5), persistent revolt making the whole head sick and heart faint (Isa 1:5), revolt spoken from the heart alongside lying words (Isa 59:13), revolt as false prophecy that draws judgment (Jer 28:16; 29:32), and revolt as the deep wilful turning away from God (Isa 31:6). One verse (Deu 19:16) uses revolt in a legal-accusation context. The inner-being characteristic is defiance as an active, embodied, voiced orientation against God — revolt that rises from the heart and finds speech as its vehicle.

**Anchor:** vc_id=35560 (Isa 59:13)

### M10-F-VCG-03 — Accumulated transgression crossing the threshold of divine tolerance (13 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where transgression is quantified and accumulated — crossing a threshold of divine patience that triggers irreversible judgment. The Amos oracles (1:3–2:6) exemplify this: "for three transgressions and for four" marks the moment when the count exceeds divine forbearance for each nation. Also: Dan 8:23 (transgressors pressing rebellion to its absolute limit), Dan 8:12/13 (transgression causing desolation), Dan 9:24 (transgression with a God-determined limit), Isa 59:12 (transgressions multiplied before God). The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a countable, accumulating moral excess whose fullness releases divine judgment. Keyword axis `transgression accumulating` (5), `threshold crossed` (5), `patience divine exhausted` (3 co-occ).

**Anchor:** vc_id=35709 (Amo 1:3)

**Members (13):**

### M10-F-VCG-04 — Transgression as law-violation: the NT parabasis / parabatēs register (15 verses)

**context_description:** The Greek parabainō, parabasis, and parabatēs corpus — verses where transgression is specifically defined as the crossing of a known legal boundary. The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a relational-legal concept requiring a law to violate: "where there is no law there is no transgression" (Rom 4:15). Covers: stepping over inherited communal boundaries (Mat 15:2/3), Judas's defection from sacred charge (Act 1:25), breaking the law while boasting in it (Rom 2:23/25/27), Eve's transgression through deceived perception (1Ti 2:14), transgression under the first covenant requiring redemption (Heb 9:15), every violation receiving just retribution (Heb 2:2), the law's function as what Galatians calls the period of transgressions (Gal 3:19), and showing partiality as law-violation convicted by conscience (Jam 2:9/11).

**Anchor:** vc_id=40857 (Rom 4:15)

### M10-F-VCG-05 — Adam-paradigm transgression: the foundational boundary-crossing (5 verses)

**context_description:** The paraptōma Adam-paradigm verses (Rom 5:14–20) — where one man's deliberate crossing of a direct divine command set the pattern for universal condemnation. The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a single, conscious, definitive act of will against a specific divine command whose consequences extend universally. Adam's transgression is the paradigm against which all subsequent sinning is measured (Rom 5:14), the pivot point that brought death as tyrant over all (Rom 5:17), and the event the law's entry was meant to multiply into conscious, nameable offenses (Rom 5:20). Distinct from VCG-04 (NT law-violation register, which is case-by-case) by the cosmic-paradigmatic scope.

**Anchor:** vc_id=40855 (Rom 5:14)

**Members (5):**
40871, 40872, 40873, 40874, 40875

### M10-F-VCG-06 — Transgression pardoned and erased: divine forgiveness of rebellion (13 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where transgression is the specific object of divine forgiveness — blotted out like a cloud (Isa 43:25; 44:22), passed over by God (Mic 7:18), erased to the furthest distance (Psa 103:12), removed from divine memory when the person turns (Eze 18:22/28), confessed and forgiven (Psa 32:1/5; 51:1). Also the servant who bears the people's transgression vicariously (Isa 53:5/8 — borne by the servant). The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a recorded moral rebellion that God's specific, active forgiveness can fully resolve. Keyword axis `forgiveness received` (14 cluster-wide), `burden lifted`, `record divine` erased.

### M10-F-VCG-07 — Transgression's oppressive weight: the burden that crushes and enslaves (14 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where transgression is experienced as an oppressive, binding, or crushing force — bound into a yoke on the neck (Lam 1:14), carrying staggering weight on the earth (Isa 24:20), a trap that snares and enslaves (Psa 19:13; Pro 29:6), an inner accumulation that produces despair and inner decay (Eze 33:10; Lam 1:22; Eze 33:12). Also the verses where transgression is personified as a moral force whose grip becomes the punishment (Job 8:4). The inner-being characteristic is transgression not merely as the act of crossing a boundary but as the oppressive ongoing condition that results from that crossing. Keyword axis `burden moral` (3), `transgression accumulated` (3).

### M10-F-VCG-08 — Transgression as interpersonal breach: wrong done between persons (19 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where transgression names a relational wrong between persons — Jacob's breach against Laban (Gen 31:36), the brothers' deliberate wrongdoing against Joseph (Gen 50:17), every breach of custodial trust (Exo 22:9), and the wisdom corpus where transgression is an interpersonal offense that love covers (Pro 10:12), that unrestrained speech multiplies (Pro 10:19; 17:19), that a person of good sense overlooks (Pro 19:11), or that a wounded brother fortifies against (Pro 18:19). The inner-being characteristic is transgression as a relational-horizontal wrong — the breach of a bond between persons — distinct from the vertical rebellion of VCG-01. Keyword axis `transgression relational` (3), `relational breach` (3).

### M10-F-VCG-09 — Transgression as personal moral breach: Job, Psalms, and wisdom confessional (34 verses)

**context_description:** The large personal-confessional and wisdom block — verses where transgression is a specific willful breach owned by an individual (Job's sustained protest of his absent transgression; Job 13:23/33:9/34:6; Psa 51:3/55 conscience-aware of transgression; Pro 28:13/24 concealed then confessed; Isa 57:4 children of transgression as inherited identity; Isa 58:1 not self-perceived). This VCG covers the personal-level transgression register: confessing specific wilful breaches (Psa 32:5; 51:3/5; Ezr 10:13), the wisdom observations about transgression's hiding and discovery (Pro 28:13/24), and the identity-level rebel (Isa 48:8; Isa 57:4; Eze 2:3 — inherited rebel status). Keyword axis `guilt acknowledged` (4), `conscience clear` / `conscience absent` (4 each, from those who claim or lack innocence).
