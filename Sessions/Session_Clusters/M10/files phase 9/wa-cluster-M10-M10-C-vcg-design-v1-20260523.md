### M10-C-VCG-01 — Sinner as social-moral category: exclusion and stigma

**context_description:** Verses where "sinner" functions as a recognized social-moral label that excludes from table fellowship, congregation, or honorable company. The inner-being characteristic is the sinner's identity as a person whose moral standing is treated as a contaminant by the surrounding community — morally stigmatized, socially excluded, viewed as defiling to those who associate with them. The keyword cluster `contamination feared`, `purity calculus`, `exclusion social`, `identity stigmatized`, `stigma moral` concentrates here. Jesus' practice of eating with sinners is scandalous precisely because their identity-as-sinners is treated by the religious establishment as a moral-contagion threat. The register spans OT wisdom (Psa 1:1/5 — the congregation of the righteous excludes sinners) through the NT Synoptic table-fellowship controversy and John 9's debate over Jesus' sinful status.

### M10-C-VCG-02 — Sinner as constituted moral identity: imputed and declared status

**context_description:** Verses where being a sinner is not primarily a social label but a constituted, imputed, or declared moral status — the person's inner moral condition as established by God's assessment, by the act of Adam, by Paul's theological claim, or by the person's own self-knowledge. The inner-being characteristic is sinner-identity as an ontological moral reality: what the person is before God, regardless of social perception. Rom 5:8 (Christ died "while we were still sinners" — the condition precedes and occasions grace), Rom 5:19 (constituted sinners through Adam's disobedience — imputed inner status), 1Ti 1:15 (Paul as foremost sinner — the heaviest moral self-identification). Also the sinner as capable of turning (Psa 51:13; Luk 15:7/10 — where the sinner's repentance occasions heavenly joy).

**Anchor:** vc_id=18810 (Rom 5:8 — "While we were still sinners, Christ died for us — the moral condition that Christ's love was demonstrated precisely when humanity was in its worst inner state")

### M10-C-VCG-03 — Sinner as extreme moral type: habitual wrongdoing defining total character

**context_description:** Verses where the sinner-designation marks persons or communities whose character is wholly and habitually consumed by wrongdoing — the extreme end of the sinner-identity spectrum. The inner-being characteristic is moral character defined entirely by sin: Sodom whose men were exceedingly wicked (Gen 13:13), the Amalekites designated sinners by God's own word warranting total destruction (1Sa 15:18), the sinful kingdom under God's gaze (Amo 9:8/10), sinners in Zion consumed by fear before God's fire (Isa 33:14). Also includes the OT wisdom verses that observe the sinner's inexorable lot (Pro 13:21 — disaster pursues them; Isa 1:28 — those who forsake the Lord are consumed). The keyword `character defining`, `sin extreme`, `sin lethal`, `defection fatal` concentrates here.

**Anchor:** vc_id=19164 (Gen 13:13 — "The men of Sodom are identified as habitual, extreme sinners whose wrongdoing is directed against the Lord himself, marking a deep moral disposition of rebellion")
