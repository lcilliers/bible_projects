## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Sinful speech reveals God's character through three lenses. (1) *God as the exclusive holder of divine prerogatives*: Mar 2:7 — "Who can forgive sins but God alone?" and Joh 10:33 — "you, being a man, make yourself God" — blasphemy is defined as the improper claiming of what belongs exclusively to God. The characteristic reveals God as the one whose authority, identity, and prerogatives are absolute and non-transferable. (2) *God as the one whose Spirit cannot be blasphemed without eternal consequence*: Mat 12:31 — "the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven" — God's Spirit is the one reality against which blasphemous speech carries irreversible moral weight. (3) *God as the one against whom the beast's constitutive speech is directed*: Rev 13:6 — "It opened its mouth to utter blasphemies against God, blaspheming his name and his dwelling, that is, those who dwell in heaven" — God's name, presence, and people are the targets of the most extreme form of sinful speech.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute sinful speech to God?**

**[CHAR-9]** S — No verse in the 22-verse corpus attributes blasphemy or sinful speech to God. God appears as the target of blasphemy (Rev 13:6), the one whose authority blasphemy presumes to claim (Mar 2:7; Joh 10:33), and the one whose Spirit suffers the unforgivable form (Mat 12:31) — never as a blasphemer. The characteristic is exclusively creaturely in both its slander and its blasphemy registers.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The silence on God blaspheming reveals two things. (1) God possesses speech — he speaks, names, declares, and pronounces — but his speech is always aligned with his own nature and truth; the creature's sinful speech is the perversion of the speech-capacity God gave. (2) Even the highest creaturely agents — angels — restrain blasphemous speech: Jude 9 — "even the archangel Michael... did not presume to pronounce a blasphemous judgment" and 2Pe 2:11 — "angels, though greater in might and power, do not pronounce a blasphemous judgment." If angelic speech is constrained from blasphemy, God's speech is infinitely more so. The silence confirms: sinful speech is the perversion of the image's speech-capacity, a capacity God gave and cannot himself misuse.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose sinful speech serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-9]** S — The corpus provides no evidence of any positive created purpose for sinful speech. The 22 verses uniformly present it as originating in inner moral corruption (Mat 15:19), requiring renunciation (Eph 4:31; Col 3:8), flowing from diseased pride (1Ti 6:4), and constituting the beast's defiant identity (Rev 13:1/5/6). No verse presents sinful speech as serving any designed function.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether sinful speech is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The evidence points to sinful speech as entirely belonging to the fallen condition. Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come evil thoughts... slander" — the heart's corruption is the origin; the corruption is the fallen condition, not the original design. 1Ti 1:13 — Paul describes his former blasphemy as rooted in "ignorant unbelief" — unbelief is a fallen-condition state. 2Ti 3:2's list of end-times character traits (including abusive/blasphemous disposition) is explicitly presented as a mark of moral deterioration, not original design.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that sinful speech is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The corpus evidences two eschatological trajectories. (1) *Judgment for unforgivable blasphemy*: Mat 12:31 — "the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven" — the unforgivable form carries forward permanently into eschatological consequence. (2) *The beast's blasphemy as eschatological-concentrated evil*: Rev 13:5/6 — the beast utters blasphemous words for "forty-two months" — a defined eschatological period. The beast's constitutive blasphemy is oriented toward the judgment that the Revelation context anticipates. The trajectory of sinful speech is toward eschatological consequence rather than any positive fullness.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does sinful speech express the divine image?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Sinful speech perverts one of the image's most central capacities: the gift of speech. The image-bearer is a speaking creature — created to name (Gen 2:19–20, contextually), to declare, to praise, and to communicate moral truth. Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come... slander" — the same heart-to-mouth channel designed for God-honouring speech is the channel through which slander flows. The image's speech-capacity is constitutively good; sinful speech is its inversion. Jude 9 and 2Pe 2:11 confirm that even the speech of angelic creatures is constrained from blasphemy — establishing that sinful speech is not merely a human failure but a creaturely violation of what speech is for.

---

**T0.3.2 — Does the evidence suggest sinful speech is shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-9]** S — Exclusively creaturely (T0.1.2). God cannot blaspheme because blasphemy requires either false claims about God (which God cannot make about himself) or speech originating in inner moral corruption (which God does not possess).

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence of sinful speech in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The presence of sinful speech reveals the image's condition across three levels. (1) *VCG-01 (slander from inner corruption)*: Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come evil thoughts... slander" — sinful speech from inner corruption reveals the heart's own moral condition; the speech is a diagnostic of the heart's state. 1Ti 6:4 — slander arising from "conceit and unhealthy craving" — the image's inner moral centre is diseased. (2) *VCG-02 (divine-authority usurpation)*: when sinful speech claims divine authority (Mar 2:7; Joh 10:33), the image has inverted its relationship to God — instead of the image reflecting God, the creature is claiming to be God. (3) *VCG-03 (constitutive defiance — beast)*: Rev 13:1 — "blasphemous names on its heads" — the most extreme image-condition: the entire character constituted by defiance of God rather than by reflection of him.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use sinful speech typologically?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Yes. VCG-02's blasphemy-charge against Jesus (Mat 26:65; Mar 14:64; Luk 5:21; Joh 10:33) operates typologically: the religious authorities' charge of blasphemy against Jesus — based on his claim to divine authority and his forgiveness of sins — is the type that ironically inverts; the one accused of blasphemy is actually the one who is God. The typological direction is: the human accusation of blasphemy (false, directed against the true Son of God) points toward the revelation of who Jesus is — the charge that is meant to condemn becomes the evidence that he is what he claims.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The direction is *ironic inversion*: the human accusation of blasphemy (Mat 26:65 — "He has uttered blasphemy") directed against Jesus is false — Jesus's claims are true, not blasphemous. The accusation-type (human religious authorities claiming Jesus blasphemes) points toward the antitype revelation: Jesus is genuinely God, making the blasphemy-charge itself the blasphemous misidentification. The human instance (false accusation) inversely reveals the divine reality (Jesus's genuine divine identity).

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Typological use is evidenced (T0.4.1 and T0.4.2). This prompt is superseded.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The programme names this characteristic "Sinful speech." The name encodes three things. (1) *Sinful*: the speech originates in and expresses inner moral corruption — it is not merely offensive or socially inappropriate but morally sinful, carrying the weight of transgression. (2) *Speech*: the characteristic is constitutively the speech-act — the externalisation of the inner corruption through the mouth. Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come evil thoughts... slander" — the inner (heart) and the outer (speech) are related as source and expression. (3) The name signals that the characteristic is defined by both its inner origin (sinful) and its outer form (speech) — distinguishing it from inner sinful states that remain unexpressed. The characteristic is the completion of the inner-to-outer movement: the corrupted heart speaks.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The primary term is **blasfēmia (G0988)** — noun — and **blasfēmos (G0989)** — adjective. The Greek root combines *blapto* (to harm, injure) and *phēmi* (to speak) — literally "harmful speech," "injurious speech." At the definitional level, the root reveals: sinful speech is speech that harms — directed at God (blasphemy in the theological sense) or at persons (slander). The 22-verse corpus is exclusively NT Greek; no OT Hebrew vocabulary appears in CHAR-9 (the OT pa.sha/cha.ta registers for verbal sin are covered in other characteristics). The Greek blasfēmia thus encompasses both the God-directed (blasphemy-proper) and person-directed (slander) registers — its semantic range is wider than the English "blasphemy" suggests.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — All three implications are present. *Directional*: sinful speech moves from inner (the corrupted heart — Mat 15:19) to outer (the spoken word). The direction is inner-to-outer, with the speech being the externalisation of the inner condition. *Relational*: sinful speech is always directed at a relational target — God (VCG-02: Mar 2:7; Joh 10:33; VCG-03: Rev 13:6) or persons (VCG-01: Eph 4:31; Col 3:8). The characteristic has no non-relational form — it is always aimed. *Constitutional*: Mat 15:19 — the heart is the constitutional origin; the speech is its symptom. The constitutional implication is: sinful speech reveals the heart's condition. 1Ti 6:4 — conceit and diseased will as the constitutional ground.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is sinful speech?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The corpus reveals sinful speech as operating across three distinct kinds. (1) *An act with an inner origin*: VCG-01's slander is an act (the speaking of harmful words) that originates in the heart's corruption (Mat 15:19). It is both an inner condition (corrupted heart) and an outer act (the speech). (2) *A presumptuous claim*: VCG-02's blasphemy-as-divine-authority-claim is an act of presumption — claiming what belongs to God alone (Mar 2:7; Joh 10:33). (3) *A constitutive identity*: VCG-03's beast-blasphemy is not primarily an act but a defining inner character — "blasphemous names on its heads" (Rev 13:1), "full of blasphemous names" (Rev 17:3) — the creature's very being is constituted by its defiance of God. The characteristic spans act, presumption, and constituted character.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The VCG structure reveals three distinct constituent elements. (1) *VCG-01 (slander/abusive speech from inner corruption)*: person-directed sinful speech originating in the heart's moral corruption — Mat 15:19; Mar 7:22; Eph 4:31; Col 3:8; 1Ti 6:4; 2Ti 3:2. (2) *VCG-02 (blasphemy as divine-authority claim)*: God-directed sinful speech that usurps divine prerogatives — Mat 12:31; Mat 26:65; Mar 2:7; Mar 3:28; Mar 14:64; Luk 5:21; Joh 10:33. (3) *VCG-03 (blasphemy as constitutive defiance)*: blasphemy as the defining character of the creature entirely given over to anti-God defiance — Rev 13:1/5/6; Rev 17:3; 2Pe 2:11; Rev 2:9. Additionally, Jude 9 and 2Pe 2:11 name the angelic constraint against blasphemous speech, establishing the creaturely boundary.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Working description: *Sinful speech (CHAR-9) is sin committed through the mouth — the externalisation of inner moral corruption in harmful, defaming, or presumptuous speech directed at God or persons. It operates across three registers: (1) slander and abusive speech generated from the corrupted heart and directed at persons (VCG-01 — "out of the heart come... slander," Mat 15:19); (2) blasphemy-as-divine-authority-claim — presumptuous speech usurping what belongs to God alone, treated in the NT judicial context as the charge against Jesus (VCG-02 — "He is blaspheming! Who can forgive sins but God alone?" Mar 2:7); (3) blasphemy-as-constitutive-identity — the creature whose very being is defined by defiant anti-God speech, most fully realised in Revelation's beast imagery (VCG-03 — "full of blasphemous names," Rev 17:3). The primary inner-being locus is the heart (Mat 15:19); the speech is the heart's outer expression.*

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of sinful speech?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The structural opposite is *edifying and truth-speaking speech* — speech that builds up, honours God, and speaks truthfully of persons. The corpus names the positive opposite through commands of renunciation: Eph 4:31 — "let all bitterness and... slander be put away from you" — implies speech that edifies in place of slander; Col 3:8 — "put them all away: anger, wrath, malice, slander... from your mouth" — implies a speech-set that is truthful, kind, and God-honouring. The structural opposite of VCG-02's divine-authority usurpation is proper acknowledgment of God's authority and identity. The structural opposite of VCG-03's constitutive defiance is the worship and praise that acknowledges God as God.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does sinful speech explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Sinful speech explicitly excludes: (1) *Truthful speech about God and persons*: Rev 2:9 — "the slander of those who say that they are Jews and are not" — sinful speech excludes honest identity; (2) *Proper acknowledgment of God's exclusive authority*: Mar 2:7 — "who can forgive sins but God alone?" — sinful speech claims divine prerogatives rather than acknowledging them; (3) *Creaturely constraint in speech*: Jude 9 — Michael withholds the blasphemous judgment — sinful speech resists the creaturely humility that acknowledges speech's limits before God; (4) *Heart-generated righteous speech*: Mat 15:19 — slander comes from the corrupted heart; pure heart-generated speech is what sinful speech corrupts and displaces.

---

**T1.3.3 — What is sinful speech not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Three boundaries. (1) *Not merely offensive speech without inner moral origin*: sinful speech in this corpus is specifically speech that originates in inner moral corruption (Mat 15:19 — from the heart) or constitutive identity (VCG-03). Culturally offensive speech without this inner-corruption origin falls outside the characteristic. (2) *Not every sin that involves speech*: the definition specifies "blasphemy and slander" — the blasfēmia/blasfēmos vocabulary specifically. Other speech-sins (lying, deceptive speech, idle words) fall in other M10 characteristics. (3) *Not the accusation of blasphemy itself*: the false charge of blasphemy against Jesus (VCG-02) is not itself CHAR-9 in its primary form — rather, the accusation reveals what blasphemy is by its false application to the one who cannot blaspheme because he is God.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does sinful speech operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Three inner modes. (1) *Heart-corruption externalised through speech (VCG-01)*: the characteristic operates as the externalisation of the heart's moral corruption — Mat 15:19 — the heart generates evil thoughts, and slander is their verbal expression. The inner mode is: corrupt heart → corrupting speech. (2) *Presumptuous will claiming divine status (VCG-02)*: the characteristic operates through the will's presumption — claiming for oneself (or treating others as claiming) what belongs to God alone (Mar 2:7; Joh 10:33). The inner mode is: presumptuous will → blasphemous claim. (3) *Constitutive identity-level defiance (VCG-03)*: the characteristic is not an act at all but the creature's total orientation against God — Rev 13:1 — blasphemy as the constitutive character. The inner mode is: total anti-God orientation → defiant speech as the self's expression.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does sinful speech operate differently depending on context?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Yes, across the three VCG contexts. (1) *Communal-relational context (VCG-01)*: slander operates between persons within community relationships — Eph 4:31; Col 3:8 address this within the church community; the characteristic damages interpersonal relationships. (2) *Judicial-christological context (VCG-02)*: blasphemy operates as a formal legal charge in the trial of Jesus (Mat 26:65; Mar 14:64) — the judicial context makes the speech-act a capital offense with death-sentence consequence. (3) *Eschatological-cosmic context (VCG-03)*: blasphemy operates as the defining characteristic of the eschatological anti-God power (Rev 13:1/5/6) — the cosmic context makes it the constitutive orientation of the creature entirely given over to God-defiance.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does sinful speech have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Yes — sinful speech is constitutively speech-based: it is definitionally an act of the mouth. Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come... slander" — the slander is the speech. Mat 26:65 — "He has uttered blasphemy" — the utterance is the act. Rev 13:5 — "a mouth uttering haughty and blasphemous words" — the mouth is named as the instrument. The speech-based mode is not a secondary feature but the defining form of the characteristic: without the externalisation in speech, the inner corruption remains CHAR-4/16 territory rather than CHAR-9.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response when sinful speech is encountered?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Two response patterns. (1) *Judicial/legal response*: VCG-02 — the immediate response to the charge of blasphemy is judicial verdict: Mat 26:65 — the high priest tears his robes and calls for witnesses; Mar 14:64 — "they all condemned him as deserving death" — the immediate response is escalation to capital punishment. (2) *Community correction*: VCG-01 — Eph 4:31; Col 3:8 — the immediate response prescribed is renunciation and replacement with edifying speech. The responses differ by context: blasphemy against God in the judicial context produces a death-verdict; slander in the community context produces a call to put it away.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent or variable?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Variable. Judicial condemnation (VCG-02 — Mat 26:65; Mar 14:64), community correction imperatives (VCG-01 — Eph 4:31; Col 3:8), and shock/questioning (Mar 2:7 — "why does this man speak like that?") represent different immediate responses shaped by the relational and legal context. The VCG-03 beast context does not evidence an immediate human response — the blasphemy is the beast's constitutive orientation rather than an addressable speech-act.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The inner phenomenological experience of the person who commits sinful speech — what they experience in the moment of blasphemy or slander — is not described in the corpus. The corpus describes the acts, their origins (the heart — Mat 15:19; conceit — 1Ti 6:4), and the responses they provoke, but not the inner experience of the speaker in the moment of sinful speech.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does sinful speech produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Two sustained inner-being trajectories. (1) *Progressive inner corruption deepened by the speech that expresses it*: 1Ti 6:4 — slander arising from conceit and unhealthy craving — the characteristic both expresses and reinforces the inner corruption that generated it; sustained slander deepens the diseased will. (2) *Permanent forfeiture of forgiveness in the unforgivable case*: Mat 12:31 — "the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven" — sustained and definitive blasphemy against the Spirit produces an irreversible inner condition: the permanent foreclosure of forgiveness.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained sinful speech establish?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Sustained sinful speech establishes: (1) *Diseased inner craving and conceit*: 1Ti 6:4 — sustained slander from conceit entrenches the conceit; the speaking reinforces the pride that generated it; (2) *Constitutive anti-God identity*: VCG-03 — the beast's sustained blasphemy (Rev 13:5 — "for forty-two months") establishes the creature's total identity around defiance of God; (3) *2Ti 3:2's end-times character*: the blasphemous disposition is the settled character of persons who have given themselves to self-love — the speech-pattern is the established character-type.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Yes. The immediate response is: judicial condemnation (VCG-02), community correction call (VCG-01). The sustained effect is: deepened inner corruption (1Ti 6:4 — conceit feeds on slander), constituted identity around the sinful speech pattern (VCG-03 — the beast's character), and the permanent foreclosure of forgiveness in the Spirit-blasphemy case (Mat 12:31). The sustained effect moves from the relational-consequential level (what others do in response) to the constitutional level (what the person becomes through sustained sinful speech).

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable sinful speech?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Three enabling conditions. (1) *Corrupt heart*: Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come evil thoughts... slander" — the corrupted heart is the primary enabling condition; (2) *Conceit and diseased craving*: 1Ti 6:4 — "puffed up with conceit... unhealthy craving for controversy" — pride and the appetite for controversy enable slander; (3) *Unbelief*: 1Ti 1:13 — Paul's former blasphemy arose from "ignorant unbelief" — the absence of saving knowledge of God removes the constraint against blasphemy. VCG-03's beast is the extreme form: total anti-God orientation as the constitutive enabling condition for constitutive blasphemy.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or prevent sinful speech?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The blocking conditions from the corpus: (1) *Creaturely humility and restraint*: Jude 9 — Michael "did not presume to pronounce a blasphemous judgment" — the inner condition of knowing one's creaturely limits restrains sinful speech; (2) *Renunciation and replacement*: Eph 4:31 — "let all... slander be put away from you" — the deliberate inner act of renunciation is the blocking condition; (3) *Knowledge of God (faith)*: 1Ti 1:13 — Paul's blasphemy was from "ignorant unbelief" — the converse is that knowing God in faith prevents blasphemous speech.

---

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters sinful speech but does not produce it?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Jude 9 and 2Pe 2:11 provide the clearest evidence: Michael and the angels do not pronounce blasphemous judgments even in contexts where they might seem warranted. The inner-being state of the non-blasphemer is: creaturely deference — "The Lord rebuke you" (Jude 9) — acknowledging that some pronouncements exceed creaturely authority and belong to God alone. The non-blasphemer's inner state is defined by the recognition of their own creaturely limits in speech.

---

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of sinful speech?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The primary inner-being dimension is **conscience/moral evaluation** in the sense that sinful speech is the external externalisation of the inner moral condition — but more precisely, the primary faculty engaged is the **heart as the moral-generating centre**. Mat 15:19 — "out of the heart come evil thoughts... slander" — the heart is the origin; the speech is the product. The heart's moral-generating capacity is the primary inner-being dimension: sinful speech is what the corrupted heart produces when it speaks.

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — (1) Mat 15:19 — the heart named as the source; (2) 1Ti 6:4 — slander traced to the inner conditions of conceit and diseased craving (not to external triggers); (3) 2Ti 3:2 — the abusive/blasphemous disposition as a character trait (inner formation, not external act); (4) VCG-03 — the beast's blasphemy as constitutive identity rather than isolated act — the deepest level of the heart's orientation expressed in constitutive speech. All evidence supports: the inner moral-generative capacity of the heart is the primary faculty; speech is its expression.

---

**T1.8.3 — Does sinful speech carry secondary dimensions?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Secondary dimensions: (1) *Will/volition*: 1Ti 6:4 — the "unhealthy craving for controversy" is volitional; Jude 9 — Michael "did not presume" — presume is volitional; Rev 13:5 — the beast's blasphemy is a permitted and willed act; (2) *Relational*: sinful speech is always directed at a relational target (God or persons) — the relational dimension is constitutive of the characteristic; (3) *Speech/communicative agency*: the spoken word is the characteristic's required form — without speech, the inner condition remains unexpressed as CHAR-9. These dimensions do not compete with the heart/moral-generative primary; they describe the channel (will), direction (relational), and form (speech) of the heart's expression.

## T2 — Constitutional Location (31 prompts)

**T2.1.1 — Is sinful speech explicitly located at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — Mat 12:31 — "the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven" — the Holy Spirit is named as the target of the most severe form of sinful speech. This is not spirit-level location of sinful speech in the practitioner but the identification of the Spirit as the one against whom sinful speech can be directed. The uniqueness of this formulation — blasphemy against the Spirit being unforgivable — implies that the Holy Spirit occupies a unique constitutive position in relation to sinful speech's gravity.

---

**T2.1.2 — Does sinful speech originate at the spirit level?**

**[CHAR-9]** S — No evidence locates the origin of sinful speech at the spirit level of the practitioner. The origin evidence points to the heart (Mat 15:19) and to inner conditions of conceit and craving (1Ti 6:4), not to the spirit-level constitutional faculty.

---

**T2.1.3 — What does spirit-level location reveal?**

**[CHAR-9]** E — The spirit-level dimension (Mat 12:31) reveals that sinful speech can reach a depth of irreversibility that operates at the spirit level of the person's relationship with God: blasphemy against the Spirit is not forgivable, indicating that this form of sinful speech closes off the Spirit's operation in a way that cannot be reversed. The spirit-level is engaged not as the origin of the speech but as what is ultimately at stake in the most severe form — the Spirit's sustaining, forgiving presence can be sinfully spoken against in a way that forecloses access to it.
