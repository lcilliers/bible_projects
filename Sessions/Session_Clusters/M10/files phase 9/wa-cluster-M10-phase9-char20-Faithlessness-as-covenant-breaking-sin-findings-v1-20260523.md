## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The 101-verse corpus reveals God's character across five dimensions. (1) *God as the one who is personally betrayed*: Lev 26:40 — "treachery that they committed against me"; Eze 39:23 — "they dealt treacherously with me"; Dan 9:7 — "treachery that they have committed against you" — the faithlessness is directed against God personally; it wounds the relational bond from within; (2) *God as the faithful covenant-keeper whose faithfulness is the measure of the betrayal*: Exo 21:8 — breaking faith implies a prior faith that was owed; 1Ch 10:13 — "Saul died for his breach of faith... he did not keep the command of the Lord" — faithfulness to divine command is the standard violated; (3) *God as the divine witness of covenant bonds*: Mal 2:14 — "the Lord was witness between you and the wife of your youth" — God himself is the named witness of the covenant whose breaking constitutes treachery; (4) *God as the one who judges treachery*: Eze 17:20 — "enter into judgment with him there for the treachery he has committed against me"; 2Ch 30:7 — "he made them a desolation"; (5) *God as the one who responds to confession of treachery*: Lev 26:40 — "if they confess their iniquity and... treachery" — God's response to honest confession of faithlessness is embedded in the text; restoration follows confession.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin to God?**

**[CHAR-20]** S — No verse in the 101-verse corpus attributes faithlessness to God. God appears as the one against whom faithlessness is directed (Lev 26:40; Dan 9:7), as the faithful witness (Mal 2:14), as the judge of treachery (Eze 17:20), and as the faithful standard against which Israel's unfaithfulness is measured. The characteristic is exclusively creaturely.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The silence is definitionally necessary: faithlessness requires a covenant bond whose violation constitutes the betrayal. God is the faithful party (the covenant-initiator) whose faithfulness is the very standard that makes human faithlessness intelligible as betrayal. Mal 2:10 — "Have we not all one Father? Has not one God created us? Why then are we faithless to one another?" — the appeal to God as common Father is the reason faithlessness toward one another is wrong; God's faithfulness is the constitutive ground that makes human faithlessness a moral failure. God cannot betray himself.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin serves in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-20]** S — The corpus provides no evidence of any positive created purpose for faithlessness. The characteristic is entirely a fallen-condition category: the breaking of the very covenant bonds that the human person was designed to inhabit and maintain. No verse presents unfaithfulness as a designed feature of the image-bearer.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin is part of the original created design or a response to the fallen condition?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Entirely a response to the fallen condition. Hos 6:7 — "but like Adam they transgressed the covenant; there they dealt faithlessly with me" — the comparison to Adam's covenant-breach locates the faithlessness paradigm at the fall; Isa 48:8 — "from of old your ear has not been opened... I knew that you would surely deal treacherously" — the foreknown constitutional unfaithfulness presupposes the fallen condition. No verse situates faithlessness within the original created design; the image-bearer was constituted for covenant faithfulness, not its betrayal.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin is oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes — in two directions. (1) *Judgment endpoint*: 2Ch 30:7 — "he made them a desolation" — sustained faithlessness reaches its judgment-endpoint; Eze 39:23 — God hid his face and delivered to adversaries; 1Ch 10:13 — "Saul died for his breach of faith" — the individual death-verdict; (2) *Resolution and forgetting*: Eze 39:26 — "they shall forget their shame and all the treachery they have practiced against me, when they dwell securely" — the eschatological fullness is the dwelling-secure state in which the treachery has been fully resolved and its shame eclipsed; Lev 26:40-42 — "if they confess their iniquity and the iniquity of their fathers in their treachery... I will remember my covenant" — the covenant-memory restoration is the positive eschatological orientation.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin express the divine image?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Faithlessness expresses the image through its *inverted relational capacity*: the image-bearer is constituted as a covenant-capable being — one who can make, sustain, and break binding relational commitments. Only a being made in God's image can enter a covenant with God (as Israel did) and then betray it as a moral act. Mal 2:14 — "the Lord was witness between you and the wife of your youth, to whom you have been faithless, though she is your companion and your wife by covenant" — the covenant-witnessing capacity and the covenant-betrayal capacity belong to the same image-bearing creature. The capacity for faithfulness (the image's positive dimension) is the prerequisite for faithlessness (its fallen expression).

---

**T0.3.2 — Is faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin shared between God and the human person, or exclusively creaturely?**

**[CHAR-20]** S — Exclusively creaturely (T0.1.2). God is the faithful standard; faithlessness is the creature's betrayal of the bond God constituted. The characteristic has no divine analogue.

---

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence of faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Faithlessness reveals the image in its *relationally broken condition*: the covenant-capable image-bearer has deployed their relational capacity against the covenant-partner rather than for them. 2Ch 36:14 — "all the officers of the priests and the people likewise were exceedingly unfaithful, following all the abominations of the nations. And they polluted the house of the Lord" — the image's relational capacity has been redirected from God-toward to God-against, and the consequence is the pollution of the very sanctuary that the image was designed to maintain. 1Ch 5:25 — "they broke faith with the God of their fathers, and whored after the gods of the peoples of the land" — the image's capacity for covenantal devotion has been transferred to false objects.

---

**T0.4.1 — Does Scripture use faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin typologically?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes — through two typological axes. (1) *The marital-faithlessness type of Israel's unfaithfulness to God*: Jer 3:20 — "as a treacherous wife leaves her husband, so have you been treacherous to me, O house of Israel" — the marital faithlessness is the type of the Israel-God covenant betrayal; Mal 2:14-16 — marital faithlessness is treated with the same theological seriousness as covenant-faithlessness toward God; the marriage-covenant is the human type pointing toward the divine-Israel bond; (2) *Adam-type covenant-breaking*: Hos 6:7 — "like Adam they transgressed the covenant; there they dealt faithlessly with me" — Adam's covenant-breach is the founding type of which Israel's faithlessness is the national repetition.

---

**T0.4.2 — If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The marriage-covenant type runs from human institution (marriage) toward divine-covenant reality: the faithlessness of a wife to her husband types the faithlessness of Israel to God. The direction is from human covenant-breaking (type) to divine-human covenant-breaking (antitype/reality). The Adam-type runs from the founding betrayal (Adam's covenant-breach) to the national repetitions (Israel's faithlessness to God). Both typological directions: human instance → divine-covenant reality.

---

**T0.4.3 — If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Typological use is evidenced (T0.4.1 and T0.4.2). This prompt is superseded.

## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The programme names this characteristic "Faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin." Three signals. (1) *Faithlessness*: not the discrete act of sin but the dispositional inner quality of the person who breaks covenant — the opposite of the faithfulness (*ʾĕmûnāh*) that the image-bearer was designed to sustain; (2) *As covenant-breaking*: the defining context — faithlessness is not generic disloyalty but specifically the breaking of a known covenantal bond; Mal 2:14 — "your wife by covenant" — the covenant is what makes the faithlessness betrayal rather than mere preference-change; (3) *Sin*: the moral-theological classification — faithlessness is not merely relational failure but sin against God and the covenant-order. The name signals: the most relational sin-category in M10 — the will that dissolves what God constituted to be binding.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Two primary term-families, both exclusively OT Hebrew with no NT vocabulary. (1) **ma.al (H4603/H4604)** — verb/noun "to commit a breach of faith, to act unfaithfully, to be treacherous" — VCG-01 dominant (unfaithfulness toward God); the root implies a violation of sacred trust, a transgression of what is consecrated or owed; Lev 5:15 — "breach of faith" in holy things; 1Ch 10:13 — "breach of faith with the Lord"; (2) **ba.gad (H0898)** — verb "to act treacherously, to be faithless, to betray" — VCG-02/03 dominant (interpersonal and constitutional treachery); the root names the inner disposition of betrayal; Mal 2:14 — "been faithless" (*bāgadtāh*) to the wife of one's youth; Isa 48:8 — "deal treacherously" as a foreknown constitutional bent. Both terms name the relational-covenantal dimension of the betrayal as the defining feature.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — All three. *Directional*: faithlessness is directed against someone — Lev 26:40 — "treachery that they committed against me"; Jer 3:20 — "treacherous to me, O house of Israel" — the direction is always against a covenant-partner; *Relational*: the characteristic is constitutively relational — Mal 2:14 — "though she is your companion and your wife by covenant" — the prior covenant-bond is what makes the faithlessness treachery rather than mere departure; *Constitutional*: Isa 48:8 — "from before birth you were called a rebel... I knew you would surely deal treacherously" — faithlessness is a constitutional inner bent that God foreknew; 2Ch 28:22 — "in time of his distress he became yet more faithless" — deepening under pressure confirms constitutional character.

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The corpus reveals CHAR-20 as primarily a *dispositional inner character that expresses through volitional acts of betrayal*. The three VCGs illuminate this: (1) VCG-01 — unfaithfulness toward God: Eze 14:13 — "a land sins against me by acting faithlessly" — faithlessness as the quality of a land's settled sin-posture; (2) VCG-02 — interpersonal treachery: Pro 11:3 — "the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them" — treachery as a crookedness of character; (3) VCG-03 — constitutional character: Isa 24:16 — "the traitors have betrayed, with betrayal the traitors have betrayed" — the person IS the betrayal; Isa 48:8 — foreknown constitutional bent. The characteristic is more disposition than discrete act — the will's settled orientation of betrayal.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Three constituent elements corresponding to the three VCGs. (1) *Unfaithfulness toward God* (VCG-01 — 57V): the primary covenant-betrayal directed upward; ma.al dominant; (2) *Relational treachery between persons* (VCG-02 — 34V): the horizontal faithlessness between persons within the covenant community; ba.gad dominant; (3) *Constitutional character of treachery* (VCG-03 — 10V): the settled inner character that IS faithlessness — the person who has become a traitor as a defining identity.

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Working description: *Faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin (CHAR-20) is the inner disposition of deliberate unfaithfulness — the will that breaks covenantal loyalty, betrays a trust, acts treacherously against God or persons. Three registers: (1) Unfaithfulness toward God (VCG-01 — 57V): the primary covenant-betrayal directed upward — Judah taken into exile "because of their breach of faith" (1Ch 9:1); Saul died "for his breach of faith" (1Ch 10:13); Ahaz became "yet more faithless" under distress (2Ch 28:22); (2) Relational treachery between persons (VCG-02 — 34V): the horizontal faithlessness within human covenant bonds — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless to the wife of your youth" (Mal 2:15); "like Adam they transgressed the covenant; there they dealt faithlessly with me" (Hos 6:7); marital ordeal (Num 5:12/27); (3) Constitutional inner character (VCG-03 — 10V): the settled identity of the betrayer — "the traitors have betrayed, with betrayal the traitors have betrayed" (Isa 24:16); "I knew you would surely deal treacherously" (Isa 48:8). Distinguished from CHAR-19 (transgression as boundary-crossing) by its relational-betrayal emphasis and from CHAR-18 (iniquity as accumulated crime) by its covenant-loyalty dimension. Cross-register flags with M13 (Truth/Faithfulness) and M31 (Faith/Unbelief) travel with all three VCGs.*

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The structural opposite is *covenant faithfulness — the settled inner disposition of steadfast loyalty to the bond*. Psa 25:3 — "they shall be ashamed who are wantonly treacherous" paired with "none who wait for you shall be put to shame" — the waiting-on-God is the structural opposite of treacherous waiting-for-self; Psa 119:158 — "I look at the faithless with disgust, because they do not keep your commands" — the command-keepers are the structural opposite; Mal 2:15/16 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and let none of you be faithless" — the guarded-spirit faithfulness; Pro 11:3 — "the integrity of the upright guides them, but the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them" — integrity (*tummāh* — blamelessness, completeness) is the named structural opposite.

---

**T1.3.2 — What does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The characteristic explicitly excludes: (1) *God's continued presence*: Jos 22:31 — "today we know that the Lord is in our midst, because you have not committed this breach of faith" — the absence of faithlessness is the condition of divine presence; Eze 39:23 — "I hid my face from them" — faithlessness drives God's face away; (2) *Divine blessing and stability*: Pro 13:15 — "the way of the treacherous is their ruin"; Pro 11:3 — "the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them"; (3) *Standing in the covenant community*: Eze 20:38 (CHAR-19 adjacent) — rebels purged from among Israel; 2Ch 30:7 — those who were faithless made "a desolation"; (4) *Reliable relational presence*: Pro 25:19 — "trusting in a treacherous man in time of trouble is like a bad tooth or a foot that slips."

---

**T1.3.3 — What is faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Three boundaries. (1) *Not CHAR-19 (Transgression as deliberate boundary-crossing)*: CHAR-19 is the knowing-crossing of a known boundary (law-violation requiring a law — Rom 4:15); CHAR-20 is the betrayal of a relational covenant bond (requiring a prior trust relationship); CHAR-20 adds the intimate-bond dimension — the betrayal of someone who counted on you; (2) *Not CHAR-5 (Refusal to repent)*: CHAR-5 is the ongoing hard heart that refuses to turn; CHAR-20 is the active act of betrayal within an existing covenant; the unfaithful person may not yet have refused repentance — their unfaithfulness may trigger remorse (Ezr 10:6 — Ezra mourned the faithlessness); (3) *Not mere social unreliability*: CHAR-20 requires a covenant context — the prior bond that is broken; Pro 25:19 — the treacherous man in time of trouble — this enters CHAR-20 territory when the trust was covenantal rather than mere social expectation.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Five distinct inner modes. (1) *As settled inner disposition*: Isa 48:8 — foreknown constitutional bent of the will; 2Ch 28:22 — deepening under distress rather than breaking; (2) *As act of inner allegiance-transfer*: 1Ch 5:25 — "broke faith with the God of their fathers, and whored after the gods of the peoples" — the will's active transfer of loyalty to false objects; (3) *As hidden inner duplicity*: Jer 12:6 — "though they speak friendly words to you" — the treachery is concealed behind friendly appearance; (4) *As inner hardening*: 2Ch 28:22 — "in the time of his distress he became yet more faithless to the Lord" — the will deepens its unfaithfulness under circumstances that would normally generate repentance; (5) *As spirit-level orientation*: Mal 2:15/16 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless" — the spirit is the named locus requiring vigilance against faithlessness.

---

**T1.4.2 — Does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin operate differently depending on context?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes across the three VCG registers. (1) *Vertical toward God* (VCG-01): faithlessness operates as direct covenant-betrayal against the God who constituted the bond; Eze 20:27 — treachery equated with blasphemy; the relational damage is at the deepest level; (2) *Horizontal toward persons* (VCG-02): faithlessness operates as betrayal of human trust within a known relational bond — marital (Num 5:12; Mal 2:14), fraternal (Job 6:15), civic (Judg 9:23); (3) *Constitutional character* (VCG-03): faithlessness operates as an identity-level character trait — Isa 24:16 (compounded betrayal), Isa 33:1 (the one who will be betrayed in turn), Isa 48:8 (foreknown from birth). VCG-01 is the primary theological register; VCG-02 the relational-human register; VCG-03 the character-level register.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes — in two modes. (1) *Treachery through friendly speech*: Jer 12:6 — "they speak friendly words to you" while acting treacherously — the verbal dimension of faithlessness is the friendly-word mask over the betraying action; Psa 59:5 — "those who treacherously plot evil" — speech that plans betrayal; (2) *Confession of faithlessness*: Lev 26:40 — "if they confess their iniquity and... treachery" — verbal acknowledgment before God; Ezr 10:2 — "we have broken faith with our God" — the community's verbal confession; Ezr 10:10 — "you have broken faith and married foreign women" — Ezra's verbal indictment. The speech-mode is either the mask of treachery (friendly words concealing betrayal) or its reversal through honest confession.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response to awareness of one's faithlessness?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The immediate responses vary. (1) *Grief and mourning in the witness*: Ezr 9:4 — "those who trembled at the words of the God of Israel... gathered around me while I sat appalled until the evening sacrifice" — the spiritually aware person's immediate response to witnessing faithlessness is appalled grief; Ezr 10:6 — Ezra "mourning over the faithlessness of the exiles," fasting and drinking nothing; (2) *Shame*: Dan 9:7 — "to us open shame" — the immediate experiential consequence; Psa 25:3 — "they shall be ashamed who are wantonly treacherous" — shame; (3) *Hardening*: 2Ch 28:22 — "in time of his distress he became yet more faithless" — the perverse response of deepened unfaithfulness; (4) *Despair and desolation*: Lam 1:2 — "all her friends have dealt treacherously with her" producing weeping bitterly.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent or variable?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Variable. Shame (Dan 9:7; Psa 25:3) is the appropriate affective response to recognised faithlessness. Grief and mourning (Ezr 9:4/10:6) is the prophetically-sensitive person's immediate response to witnessing others' faithlessness. Hardening (2Ch 28:22 — Ahaz becoming yet more faithless under distress) is the perverse immediate response of the constitutionally faithless person. Desolation (Lam 1:2 — weeping bitterly when friends deal treacherously) is the victim's immediate response to experienced faithlessness. The immediate response ranges from shame-to-repentance through grief-for-others through hardening to victimised desolation.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-20]** E — For VCG-03's constitutional-character register (Isa 24:16; Isa 33:1; Isa 48:8; Jer 3:8/11), the immediate inner response of the person who has been characterised by treachery from before birth (Isa 48:8) to their own constitutional unfaithfulness is not described. The corpus names the identity and its divine foreknowledge but not the moment-by-moment inner experience of the person constitutionally given over to faithlessness.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Four sustained inner-being effects. (1) *Progressive hardening*: 2Ch 28:22 — "in the time of his distress he became yet more faithless" — the sustained disposition deepens rather than breaks; the will's faithlessness becomes more entrenched the more it is enacted; (2) *Eventual ruin*: Pro 11:3 — "the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them"; Pro 13:15 — "the way of the treacherous is their ruin"; Pro 11:6 — "the treacherous are taken captive by their lust" — the sustained treachery produces the destruction of the self; (3) *Exile and desolation*: 1Ch 9:1 — Judah exiled "because of their breach of faith"; 2Ch 30:7 — "he made them a desolation"; Dan 9:7 — "open shame... in all the lands to which you have driven them"; (4) *Communal pollution*: 2Ch 36:14 — "exceedingly unfaithful... polluted the house of the Lord" — sustained faithlessness corrupts what the person is entrusted to protect.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained faithlessness establish?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Three established states. (1) *Crookedness of character*: Pro 11:3 — "the crookedness of the treacherous" — sustained faithlessness establishes a constitutive inner moral distortion; (2) *Capture by lust*: Pro 11:6 — "the treacherous are taken captive by their lust" — sustained treachery establishes an inner captivity to craving as the driving force of the will; (3) *Desire for violence*: Pro 13:2 — "the desire of the treacherous is for violence" — the sustained faithless orientation establishes appetite for harm as the settled inner drive.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes. The immediate response (shame — Dan 9:7; grief — Ezr 9:4; hardening — 2Ch 28:22) is episodic. The sustained effects (crookedness — Pro 11:3; captivity to lust — Pro 11:6; desire for violence — Pro 13:2; exile and desolation — 1Ch 9:1) are constitutional states established over time by unaddressed faithlessness. The most significant sustained effect is the progressive hardening: 2Ch 28:22's Ahaz — distress produced more faithlessness rather than repentance — the will has become constitutively set in the faithless direction and no longer responds to corrective pressure.

---

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Three enabling conditions. (1) *Pride*: 2Ch 26:16 — "when he was strong, he grew proud, to his destruction. For he was unfaithful to the Lord his God" — pride is the inner condition that gives rise to faithlessness; Uzziah's strength produced pride, and pride produced the overstepping of God-given limits; (2) *Absence of spirit-vigilance*: Mal 2:15 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and let none of you be faithless" — the command to guard implies that failure to guard is what enables faithlessness; the spirit is the inner domain where faithlessness originates and must be resisted; (3) *Divided or misplaced allegiance*: 1Ch 5:25 — "broke faith with the God of their fathers, and whored after the gods of the peoples" — allegiance transferred to false objects enables faithlessness to the true covenant-partner.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or prevent faithlessness?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Three blocking conditions. (1) *Spirit-vigilance*: Mal 2:15/16 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless" — the active inner guarding of the spirit is the most explicit faithlessness-prevention command in the corpus; it targets the exact origin-level of the characteristic; (2) *Fear of God*: 2Ch 30:7 — the fathers who were faithless are the negative example; their negative example presupposes the positive alternative of God-fearing loyalty; Ezr 9:4 — "all who trembled at the words of the God of Israel" — God-fear produces the response to faithlessness rather than the faithlessness itself; (3) *Honest awareness of covenant obligations*: Mal 2:14 — "the Lord was witness between you" — awareness that God witnesses the covenant prevents its casual breaking.

---

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who deepens in faithlessness under pressure?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — 2Ch 28:22 names it most precisely: "in the time of his distress he became yet more faithless to the Lord — this same King Ahaz" — the inner state is a will that has become constitutively oriented away from God such that the very pressure that would normally drive a person toward God drives this person further from him. Pro 11:6 — "the treacherous are taken captive by their lust" — the inner state under sustained faithlessness is captivity to the will's own craving; the person is not free to turn toward God because the lust has assumed agentive control. The hardened-under-distress state of 2Ch 28:22 is CHAR-20's most extreme dispositional portrait.

---

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — The primary dimension is **the will's covenantal orientation — the inner disposition of loyalty or betrayal within a known relational bond**. Mal 2:15/16 — "guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless" — the spirit is named as the inner locus requiring guarding; Isa 48:8 — the foreknown constitutional bent of the will toward treachery — the will's settled inner orientation is the characteristic's primary faculty. The characteristic is constitutively volitional and dispositional rather than cognitive or affective at the primary level.

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — (1) Mal 2:15 — "guard yourselves in your spirit" — the spirit as the named locus of faithlessness-danger; (2) 2Ch 28:22 — Ahaz becoming "yet more faithless" under distress — the will's sustained dispositional hardening; (3) Isa 48:8 — "I knew you would surely deal treacherously" — the foreknown constitutional inner bent; (4) 1Ch 5:25 — "broke faith with the God of their fathers, and whored after the gods" — the will's active allegiance-transfer; (5) Pro 11:6 — "the treacherous are taken captive by their lust" — the will enslaved by its own faithless orientation.

---

**T1.8.3 — Does faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin carry secondary dimensions?**

**[CHAR-20]** E — Yes — three secondary dimensions. (1) *Affective*: Jer 3:20 — "as a treacherous wife leaves her husband" — the emotional dimension of marital faithlessness; Lam 1:2 — weeping bitterly as victim of treachery — the affective impact on the betrayed; (2) *Social-communal*: Mal 2:10 — "why then are we faithless to one another, profaning the covenant of our fathers?" — faithlessness has a horizontal social dimension within the covenant community; (3) *Speech-based*: Jer 12:6 — friendly words masking treachery; Ezr 10:2 — verbal confession of faithlessness — the communicative dimension is secondary but present.
