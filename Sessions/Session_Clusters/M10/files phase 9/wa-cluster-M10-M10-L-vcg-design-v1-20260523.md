## VCGs

### M10-L-VCG-01 — Individual confession: the will naming its own sin (24 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where a specific individual names their committed sin as sin — first-person confessions where the conscience owns the act. Pharaoh confesses under plague pressure (Exo 9:27; 10:16); Moses confesses the people's great sin (Exo 32:30); Aaron links sin to foolishness (Num 12:11); Judas confesses betraying innocent blood (Mat 27:4); the prodigal's planned and spoken confession (Luk 15:18/21); Daniel's fourfold layered confession (Dan 9:5/8/11/15); Saul's self-interested confessions (1Sa 15:30; 26:21); Shimei's confession from inner knowledge (2Sa 19:20); David's heart-struck census confession (2Sa 24:10/17); David's "I have sinned greatly" (1Ch 21:8/17); Hezekiah's submission (2Ki 18:14); Nehemiah's sustained intercession (Neh 1:6); Job 33:27 (restored man's public confession); Micah's personal ownership of divine indignation (Mic 7:9). Keyword axis: `guilt acknowledged` (3), `sin acknowledged` (4), `confession verbal` (2), `contrition genuine` (3).

**Anchor:** vc_id=18936 (2Sa 12:13 — "David's confession 'I have sinned against the Lord' immediately met with forgiveness")

### M10-L-VCG-02 — Corporate and communal confession (9 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where confession is made by a community or on behalf of a nation — corporate acknowledgment of sin as a shared moral reality. Israel confesses forsaking God and serving the Baals (Judg 10:10); bare unqualified communal confession before God (Judg 10:15); Israel's corporate confession at Mizpah (1Sa 7:6); the people confess forsaking the Lord to serve Ashtaroth (1Sa 12:10); the exiles' communal confession spanning youth to the present day (Jer 3:25); sinning alongside inherited iniquity of the fathers (Jer 14:20); plea for God to act for his name's sake despite sin (Jer 14:7); communal confession including ancestors (Psa 106:6); past sins cancelled at the moment of turning (Eze 33:16). Keyword axis: `confession corporate` (4), `confession communal` (2), `guilt corporate` (3).

**Anchor:** vc_id=18931 (1Sa 7:6 — "Israel's corporate confession at Mizpah, accompanied by fasting — the public acknowledgment marking the turning of the will back toward God")

### M10-L-VCG-03 — Confession without transformed will: the hollow acknowledgment (1 verse)

**context_description:** The single verse in M10-L where confession is made but the will does not follow through — the people confess they have sinned yet immediately proceed on their own initiative, revealing sin acknowledged in the mouth but not yet transformed in the will (Num 14:40). This is confession's failure mode: the verbal act without inner repentance. Keyword axis: `repentance absent` (2), `confession hollow` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=18980 (Num 14:40)
