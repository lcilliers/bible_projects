## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design (12 prompts)

**T0.1.1 — What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — The three mechanisms reveal God's character through contrast and implication. (1) *God as the one who does not entice through desire*: Jam 1:14 — "each person is tempted when he is lured and enticed by his own desire" — the verse is set within a context (Jam 1:13 — not quoted but implied) that explicitly distinguishes God from the enticement mechanism: God does not entice toward evil. The mechanism of desire-enticement is constitutively not-God's mode of operation. (2) *God as the ground of covenantal identity*: Act 21:21 — the apostasy charge describes forsaking Moses — the covenantal framework given through Moses is the gift of God's self-revelation; forsaking that covenantal identity is a defection from what God established. (3) *God as the one whose image requires authenticity*: Gal 2:13 — sunupokrinomai (collective hypocrisy) falsifies identity and corrupts community integrity; authenticity before God and in community is what the characteristic destroys.

---

**T0.1.2 — Does Scripture explicitly attribute these mechanisms to God?**

**[CHAR-10]** S — No verse in the 5-verse corpus attributes enticement (deleazō), collective hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai), or apostasy (apostasia) to God. God does not entice through desire (Jam 1:13–14 context), does not act hypocritically (whose nature is consistent and true), and does not defect from his own covenantal commitments. All three mechanisms are exclusively creaturely.

---

**T0.1.3 — Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of these mechanisms, what does that silence suggest?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — The silence confirms the nature of each mechanism as a perversion of a God-given inner capacity. (1) *Desire*: God gave the creature desire as a designed faculty; enticement exploits what God gave. (2) *Social identity*: God gave the creature communal identity; hypocrisy falsifies what God designed for authentic community. (3) *Covenantal identity*: God gave Israel its covenantal identity through Moses; apostasy abandons what God established. The silence on God possessing these mechanisms confirms: each mechanism is the fallen-condition corruption of a created capacity that God gave and does not himself corrupt.

---

**T0.2.1 — What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose these mechanisms serve in the human person as created?**

**[CHAR-10]** S — The corpus provides no evidence of any positive created purpose for enticement, collective hypocrisy, or apostasy. All three mechanisms are presented as destructive: enticement draws toward sin (Jam 1:14), collective hypocrisy corrupts community integrity (Gal 2:13), apostasy abandons covenantal identity (Act 21:21). No verse presents them as serving any designed function.

---

**T0.2.2 — Does the evidence indicate whether these mechanisms are part of the original created design, or a response to the fallen condition?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — All three mechanisms belong to the fallen condition. Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed by his own desire" — the desire-faculty is created (designed) but the enticement-exploitation of it is fallen-condition operation. 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" — the training of the heart toward greed is the product of sustained fallen-condition formation. Gal 2:13's collective hypocrisy presupposes the fallen-condition tendency to conform to social pressure rather than authentic covenant identity. Act 21:21's apostasy presupposes a prior covenantal identity that can be abandoned — the abandonment is the fallen-condition movement.

---

**T0.2.3 — Is there evidence that these mechanisms are oriented toward a future fullness?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — 2Pe 2:14 — "Accursed children!" — the enticers face divine judgment as their trajectory; the orientation is toward eschatological condemnation rather than positive fullness. Act 21:21 — apostasy from the Mosaic covenantal framework points (within the Acts narrative) toward the question of the new covenant — the abandonment of the old covenantal framework is framed within the broader movement of the gospel's fulfillment, though this is contextual rather than directly stated in the verse. No verse in the corpus presents these mechanisms as oriented toward a future positive fullness.

---

**T0.3.1 — In what way do these mechanisms express the divine image?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Each mechanism perverts a distinct image-bearing capacity. (1) *Enticement perverts the image's desire-faculty*: Jam 1:14 — desire (*epithumia*) is a God-given motivational capacity of the image-bearer; enticement uses this capacity as the hook for temptation. The image's motivational capacity is the mechanism through which sin captures the person. (2) *Collective hypocrisy perverts the image's social-identity capacity*: Gal 2:13 — the image-bearer is designed for authentic communal identity; sunupokrinomai uses the capacity for social belonging to falsify identity through conformity. (3) *Apostasy perverts the image's covenantal-identity capacity*: Act 21:21 — the image-bearer is designed for covenant relationship with God; apostasy uses the capacity for identity-formation to abandon the covenantal framework.

**[CHAR-10]** S — Exclusively creaturely (T0.1.2). None of the three mechanisms applies to God.

**T0.3.3 — What does the presence of these mechanisms reveal about the condition of the divine image?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — The presence of each mechanism reveals a distinct image-condition. (1) *Enticement through desire*: 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" — the image's desire-faculty has been formed away from God and toward sinful appetite through deliberate training; the image-bearing capacity for motivation is captured by wrong objects. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: Gal 2:13 — "even Barnabas was led astray" — the image's social-identity capacity has been distorted to prioritise social conformity over covenantal authenticity; the image's communal dimension is operating through performance rather than integrity. ## T1 — Inner Being Faculty in Operation (24 prompts)

**T1.1.1 — What is this characteristic called — and what does the name signal?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — The programme names this characteristic "Specialised sinful mechanisms." The name signals three things. (1) *Specialised*: the mechanisms are not the general categories of sinning (wilful, unintentional, habitual) but specific named inner processes — enticement through desire, identity-falsification through collective hypocrisy, covenantal-identity abandonment through apostasy. Each mechanism names a distinct pathway through which sin captures the person. (2) *Sinful*: despite the diversity of mechanisms, all three result in moral failure — they are all pathways to sin, not merely morally neutral processes. (3) *Mechanisms*: the term signals that the characteristic is about the *how* of sinning — the inner operational process — rather than the *what* (the specific sinful act) or the *who* (the sinning person's character). CHAR-10 is the characteristic of sinning's methods.

---

**T1.1.2 — What do the primary Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Three distinct Greek terms, each naming a different mechanism. (1) **deleazō (G1185)**: *to lure, to entice, to bait* — from *delear* (bait). The fishing/trapping metaphor is embedded in the root: enticement is the placing of bait before the creature that draws it toward capture. Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed" — uses the term in the passive: the person is the one being drawn by their own desire. (2) **sunupokrinomai (G4942)**: *sun-* (together, with) + *hupokrinomai* (to act, to play a part, to pretend) — literally "to act hypocritically together/alongside." The compound names collective complicity in identity-performance. (3) **apostasia (G0646)**: *apo-* (away from) + *stasis* (standing) — "a standing away from," a departure, a defection. The root reveals: apostasia is a positional term — the person was standing in one place (the covenant) and has moved away from it.

---

**T1.1.3 — Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — All three mechanisms carry distinct directional and relational implications. *Enticement (deleazō)*: directional — the lure draws the person from their current position toward sin (Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed"); relational — the enticement operates through the person's own desire, making the relationship between the self's desire-faculty and the temptation the operative relational field. *Hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai)*: directional — the person moves from authentic identity toward performed identity; relational — the collective dimension (*sun-*) makes it constitutively social, requiring others to hypo-krinomai alongside. *Apostasy (apostasia)*: directional — a standing-away-from, a departure; relational — the departure is from a covenantal relational standing (the Mosaic covenant — Act 21:21).

---

**T1.2.1 — What kind of inner-being phenomenon is each mechanism?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Three distinct kinds across the mechanisms. (1) *Enticement*: a *process* — the act of being drawn through one's own desire toward sin (Jam 1:14). The characteristic in this mechanism is not a single discrete act but a process of being lured through the desire-faculty. (2) *Hypocrisy*: a *social-identity act* — the adoption of a performed identity alongside others (Gal 2:13 — "acted hypocritically along with him"). This is primarily an act with an identity-distorting consequence. (3) *Apostasy*: a *condition/event* — the state of having departed from covenantal standing (Act 21:21 — the accusation of "forsaking Moses"). The apostasia may be a specific event that produces an ongoing condition.

---

**T1.2.2 — Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it have constituent elements?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — The characteristic is constitutively composite — three analytically distinct mechanisms assembled under one characteristic because they share the property of being specific named inner pathways to sin. (1) *Enticement mechanism*: desire-faculty → bait presented → luring → capture (Jam 1:14; 2Pe 2:14/18). (2) *Hypocrisy mechanism*: social pressure → identity-performance adopted alongside others → authentic identity falsified → community corrupted (Gal 2:13). (3) *Apostasy mechanism*: covenantal identity held → departure decision → standing-away-from the covenantal framework (Act 21:21). The composite structure is acknowledged in the definition's triple formulation and in the three cross-cluster flags (M14/M08/M31).

---

**T1.2.3 — What is the current best working description?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Working description: *Specialised sinful mechanisms (CHAR-10) names three distinct inner pathways through which sin operates: (1) Enticement through desire — the person's own desire-faculty (epithumia) is the hook through which temptation lures and captures (Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed by his own desire"; 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" as the enticement apparatus; 2Pe 2:18 — enticement targeting those barely free from error); (2) Collective hypocrisy — social identity is falsified through joining in acted performance alongside others, with community-corrupting contagion effect (Gal 2:13 — "acted hypocritically along with him, so that even Barnabas was led astray"); (3) Apostasy — covenantal identity is abandoned through inner departure from the covenantal standing one has received (Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses"). Each mechanism is cross-registered with another cluster: M14 (desire/temptation), M08 (pride/hypocrisy), M31 (faith/unbelief).*

---

**T1.3.1 — What is the structural opposite of each mechanism?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Each mechanism has a distinct structural opposite. (1) *Enticement*: the structural opposite is the desire-faculty oriented toward God rather than toward sinful objects — desire that draws the person toward righteousness rather than sin. Jam 1:14's "his own desire" as the hook is opposed by the desire that is sanctified and directed toward what is good. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: the structural opposite is authentic communal identity — living truthfully in community without performing an identity one does not hold. Gal 2:13's "led astray by their hypocrisy" is opposed by the integrity Paul asserts in confronting Peter (Gal 2:11 context). (3) *Apostasy*: the structural opposite is covenantal faithfulness — remaining in the covenantal standing one has received (Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses" opposed by continuing in Moses's law).

---

**T1.3.2 — What does each mechanism explicitly exclude or resist?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) *Enticement (deleazō)*: excludes the person's resistance of their own desire as the mechanism's entry point (Jam 1:14); excludes the "unsteady souls'" capacity to maintain stability (2Pe 2:18 — targets those "barely escaping"). (2) *Collective hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai)*: excludes honest self-presentation before others (Gal 2:13 — the acted performance replaces authentic identity); excludes Barnabas's capacity for independent moral judgement (he was "led astray" by the collective hypocrisy). (3) *Apostasy (apostasia)*: excludes continued covenantal faithfulness (Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses" = abandon circumcision and ancestral customs).

---

**T1.3.3 — What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Three boundaries. (1) *Not CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning)*: the mechanisms of CHAR-10 name the pathways to sin rather than the wilful moral act itself. Enticement (Jam 1:14) is the luring process before the sinful act; hypocrisy (Gal 2:13) is the identity-falsification rather than a discrete moral act; apostasy (Act 21:21) is the departure from covenantal standing rather than a specific sin-act. (2) *Not CHAR-6 (Habitual defection)*: CHAR-10 names specific mechanisms — enticement through desire, hypocrisy, apostasy — not the settled habituation of the will toward sin. (3) *Not their respective cross-cluster partners*: deleazō is cross-registered with M14 (desire/temptation) — CHAR-10 covers the M10 moral-failure dimension of enticement; M14 covers the desire/temptation dimension separately.

---

**T1.4.1 — In what distinct ways do these mechanisms operate within the inner person?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Three distinct inner modes. (1) *Desire-exploitation (deleazō)*: the mechanism works through the desire-faculty — Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed by his own desire" — the inner motivational capacity is the mechanism's entry point and operative force. 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" — the desire has been deliberately formed toward sin through training, becoming a self-generating enticement apparatus. (2) *Identity-performance (sunupokrinomai)*: the mechanism works through the social-identity capacity — Gal 2:13 — "acted hypocritically along with him" — the inner identity is expressed through a social performance that does not match the true covenantal identity. (3) *Departure from covenantal standing (apostasia)*: the mechanism works through the will's decisional abandonment of the covenantal framework — Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses" names the departure as an act.

---

**T1.4.2 — Do these mechanisms operate differently depending on context?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Yes, across the three mechanisms. (1) *Enticement*: operates through the vulnerable points of the desire-faculty — 2Pe 2:18 specifically targets those "barely escaping from those who live in error" — the context of vulnerability shapes where enticement is most effective. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: operates specifically in communal-social contexts — Gal 2:13 — the presence of others who are acting hypocritically is required for sunupokrinomai to occur (the *sun-* prefix requires a social context). (3) *Apostasy*: operates in the context of covenantal-identity challenge — Act 21:21's accusation occurs in the context of the Gentile mission's challenge to the Jewish-covenantal framework.

---

**T1.4.3 — Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Limited speech evidence. 2Pe 2:18 — "speaking loud boasts of folly, they entice" — the enticement in this instance operates through speech: the loud boasting is the communicative vehicle through which the sensual enticement is delivered. Act 21:21 — "teach all the Jews... to forsake Moses" — the accusation implies that Paul's teaching (speech) is the mechanism through which apostasy is transmitted. Gal 2:13's hypocrisy is primarily behavioural (acting) rather than speech-based. The speech-based mode is present for enticement (2Pe 2:18) and apostasy-transmission (Act 21:21) but not dominant across the characteristic.

---

**T1.5.1 — What is the most immediate inner-being response when these mechanisms are encountered?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Different response patterns across the three mechanisms. (1) *Enticement*: Jam 1:14 — the immediate response is "lured" — the person is drawn toward the temptation through their desire; the luring is the immediate inner-being effect of encountering the enticement. 2Pe 2:18 — the "barely escaping" persons are the susceptible targets whose immediate response to enticement is toward re-capture. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: Gal 2:13 — Barnabas's "led astray" — the immediate response of the susceptible community member is to follow the collective performance rather than maintaining independent authentic identity. (3) *Apostasy*: Act 21:21 — the immediate response within the accusing community is alarm at the perceived defection from the covenantal framework.

---

**T1.5.2 — Is the immediate response consistent or variable?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Variable across the three mechanisms. Enticement's immediate response is being drawn toward the lure (Jam 1:14); hypocrisy's is social conformity (Gal 2:13); apostasy's is departure from covenantal standing (Act 21:21). Even within deleazō, the responses vary: Jam 1:14 describes the enticed person's inner drawing; 2Pe 2:14 describes the enticers' own inner formation; 2Pe 2:18 describes the targets' vulnerability. The characteristic's composite nature produces irreducible response variability across its three mechanisms.

---

**T1.5.3 — Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.**

**[CHAR-10]** S — The inner phenomenological experience of the person in the moment of being enticed, joining in hypocrisy, or departing from covenantal standing — what these mechanisms feel like from within — is not described in the corpus. The corpus describes the mechanisms and their consequences, not the inner subjective experience of undergoing them.

---

**T1.6.1 — What does each mechanism produce in the inner being over time?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) *Enticement*: 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" — sustained enticement through desire produces a formed inner character: the heart has been deliberately trained toward sinful appetite, making enticement the person's natural operating mode. The mechanism becomes self-perpetuating: desire trained toward sin produces more susceptibility to desire-based enticement. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: Gal 2:13 — "even Barnabas was led astray" — sustained collective hypocrisy corrupts the community's moral integrity; individuals lose their capacity for independent authentic identity. (3) *Apostasy*: Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses" — the departure from covenantal identity produces a person defined by the absence of the covenantal framework that previously constituted their identity.

---

**T1.6.2 — What states, qualities, or orientations does sustained operation of these mechanisms establish?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) *Enticement*: 2Pe 2:14 — "insatiable for sin" and "hearts trained in greed" — sustained enticement establishes a state of insatiability (desire that cannot be satisfied) and a trained inner character oriented toward sinful appetite; (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: sustained participation in collective hypocrisy establishes community-level inauthenticity — Gal 2:13's "led astray" implies that the authentic communal identity has been replaced by the performed one as the operating norm; (3) *Apostasy*: sustained departure from covenantal identity establishes a new identity defined by the departure — the person who has forsaken Moses is now in a position of non-covenantal standing.

---

**T1.6.3 — Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Yes. The immediate response is being drawn/lured (enticement), being led astray (hypocrisy), or departing (apostasy). The sustained effect is the formed character of 2Pe 2:14 ("hearts trained in greed"), the community's distorted integrity (Gal 2:13), or the established covenantal absence (Act 21:21). The sustained effect moves from the episodic (each act of enticement/hypocrisy/departure) to the constitutional (trained heart, corrupted community, absent covenantal standing).

**T1.7.1 — What inner conditions enable each mechanism?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) *Enticement*: Jam 1:14 — the enabling condition is the desire-faculty itself — "his own desire" is both the hook and the enabling condition; everyone has desire, making everyone susceptible; 2Pe 2:18 — "barely escaping from those who live in error" — residual vulnerability in those not yet firmly established is the specific enabling condition. (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: Gal 2:13 — the enabling condition is the social pressure of peer conformity combined with the presence of a prominent figure (Peter) modelling the hypocrisy; the social weight of the collective makes individual resistance difficult. (3) *Apostasy*: Act 21:21 — the enabling condition is the existence of a covenantal identity that can be abandoned; the mechanism requires a prior covenantal standing from which departure is possible.

---

**T1.7.2 — What inner conditions block or prevent each mechanism?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) *Enticement blocking*: the opposite of being "unsteady" (2Pe 2:18 — susceptibility) is being firmly established — a stable inner constitution not vulnerable to desire-exploitation; (2) *Hypocrisy blocking*: Gal 2:13's converse — the capacity to maintain authentic identity under social pressure, modelled by Paul's confrontation of Peter (Gal 2:11 context) — the inner integrity that resists collective performance; (3) *Apostasy blocking*: covenantal faithfulness maintained against departure pressure — the inner commitment to the covenantal identity that resists abandonment.

---

**T1.7.3 — What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters these mechanisms but does not succumb?**

**[CHAR-10]** S — The corpus does not directly evidence the inner-being state of the person who resists enticement, hypocrisy, or apostasy. Gal 2:11's Paul (confronting Peter rather than joining the hypocrisy) is the implicit positive exemplar, but his inner state during the confrontation is not described in the 5-verse corpus.

---

**T1.8.1 — What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Given the composite nature of the characteristic, different mechanisms engage different primary dimensions. (1) *Enticement (deleazō)*: the primary dimension is **desire/appetite** — the desire-faculty (epithumia) is constitutively engaged as the mechanism's entry point (Jam 1:14 — "his own desire"). (2) *Collective hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai)*: the primary dimension is **will and social identity** — the volitional act of adopting a performed identity alongside others (Gal 2:13). (3) *Apostasy (apostasia)*: the primary dimension is **will and covenantal identity** — the volitional departure from the covenantal standing (Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses"). Across the characteristic, **will/volition** is the common faculty: each mechanism operates through the will's response to (or capture by) the specific mechanism.

---

**T1.8.2 — What evidence supports this classification?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — (1) Jam 1:14 — "lured and enticed by his own desire" — the desire is the mechanism but the luring requires the will's yielding to the desire's pull; (2) Gal 2:13 — "acted hypocritically along with him" — the acting-alongside is a volitional adoption; (3) Act 21:21 — "forsake Moses" — forsaking (*apostasia*) is a volitional departure; (4) 2Pe 2:14 — "hearts trained in greed" — the training involves the volitional sustaining of the desire-appetite over time.

---

**T1.8.3 — Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Secondary dimensions by mechanism: (1) *Enticement*: the desire-faculty itself as a distinct inner dimension beyond mere volition — the appetitive/motivational capacity is the characteristic's operative site; (2) *Collective hypocrisy*: the social-relational dimension — the *sun-* prefix requires a communal context; (3) *Apostasy*: the covenantal-identity dimension — the departure is from a specific relational-covenantal standing. These secondary dimensions explain the three cross-cluster flags: M14 (desire-faculty dimension of enticement), M08 (identity-performance dimension of hypocrisy), M31 (covenantal-faith dimension of apostasy).

**T2.10.1 — Do these mechanisms move across constitutional levels?**

**[CHAR-10]** E — Yes — each mechanism shows a distinct constitutional movement pattern.
