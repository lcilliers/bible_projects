## VCGs

### M10-N-VCG-01 — Refusal to repent: departure withheld (18 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where the defining inner-being characteristic is the will's active refusal to turn from a sinful course — not ignorance, not inadvertence, but chosen non-departure. The Jeroboam-pattern refusal verses from Kings dominate: Jehoram clung to Jeroboam's sin and did not depart (2Ki 3:3); Jehu did not turn aside despite reforms (2Ki 10:29/31); Jehoahaz, Israel, Jehoash, Jeroboam II, Zechariah, Menahem, Pekahiah each "did not depart from Jeroboam's sins" (2Ki 13:2/6/11; 14:24; 15:9/18/24/28) — in each case the formula names a wilful act of will-retention: the choosing not to turn. Ahab treated Jeroboam's sins as trivial (1Ki 16:31 — dulled moral conscience). Stubborn shoulder and stiff neck alongside sinning against God's rules (Neh 9:29). The formerly righteous person who turns to sin — a conscious act eclipsing prior righteousness (Eze 18:24). The decisive sin that nullifies prior standing (Eze 33:12). Grace does not license sinning (Rom 6:15). Persisting in sin with entrenched will requiring public rebuke (1Ti 5:20). Deliberate sinning after knowing the truth — closing off the sacrificial remedy (Heb 10:26). Keyword axis: `turning withheld` (9), `departure refused` (7), `sin persisting` (5), `will fixed` (3), `will clinging` (1).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19042 (2Ki 3:3 — "Jehoram clung to Jeroboam's sin and did not depart from it, depicting sin as something to which the will clings with persistence, refusing the alternative of turning away")
