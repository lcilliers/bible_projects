## VCGs

### M10-U-VCG-01 — Sin as enslaving power: the will under bondage (9 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sin is explicitly named as a dominating master — not what one does but what one is enslaved to. Practicing sin makes one a slave to sin (Joh 8:34 — the definitive statement). The rhetorical question about continuing in sin to magnify grace (Rom 6:1); believers described as having died to sin, making continued living in it a contradiction (Rom 6:2). Sin as a universal imprisoning power under which Scripture confines everything, so that the promise can only come through faith (Gal 3:22). Anger not sin but must not linger unchecked (Eph 4:26 — borderline; placed here as the will's moment of potential surrender to sin's mastery). Habitual sinning discloses allegiance to the devil's originary sin (1Jo 3:8). Divine birth making sustained sinning structurally incompatible with who the person is (1Jo 3:9). Sin differentiated by whether it leads to death (1Jo 5:16). Christ's protection preventing the evil one gaining a foothold (1Jo 5:18). Keyword axis: `will enslaved` (1), `mastery sin` (1), `compulsion inner` (1), `bondage habitual` (1), `freedom lost` (1).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19720 (Joh 8:34 — "practicing sin makes one a slave to sin — sin as a dominating inner master subjugating the will")
