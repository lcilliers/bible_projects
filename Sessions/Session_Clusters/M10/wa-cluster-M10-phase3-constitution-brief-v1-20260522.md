## §6.3.2 — Verse-level relationship test for TRANSFERS (CRITICAL)

A TRANSFERS verdict requires more than "the term's meaning corpus aligns primarily with another cluster's characteristic." Corpus-primary-register reasoning alone is too aggressive.

**The test, verse by verse:**

1. Read every verse in the term's meaning corpus.
2. For each verse, ask: *does this verse evidence any relationship with or impact on M10's characteristic (sin, guilt, transgression as moral-failure act/state/experience)?* "Relationship" is broad: direct evidence, structural opposite (innocence-by-contrast, righteousness-by-contrast), instrument-of, response-to, protective-against, in-tension-with.

**Verdict rule:**

- If **any** verse evidences a relationship with M10 → **STAYS** with cross-register flag in the rationale.
- If **no** verse evidences any M10 relationship → **TRANSFERS-TO-{cluster}** confirmed (accidental placement).

The cross-register flag travels with the term into Phase 5/7 and informs Phase 9 T6 findings.

## Disallowed BOUNDARY reasons (per v2_8 §6.3.1)

BOUNDARY may **NOT** be assigned solely because:

- The term's meaning corpus is predominantly horizontal (human-to-human sin) rather than vertical (sin-against-God).
- The meanings describe sensory / material / circumstantial inner-being rather than overtly spiritual inner-being.
- The meanings include external-action content (the act of sinning alongside the inner state).
- The meanings describe an inner-being state you would prefer not to be in scope.

Inner being is the entire human inner life — no theological narrowing.

## Valid BOUNDARY reasons (cite one of these three)

1. **Cluster-membership undecided** — corpus is genuinely on the borderline between M10 and another cluster (most likely M10b wickedness, M10c defilement, M11 repentance/forgiveness, or M06 contempt for slander); transfer destination unclear; researcher decision needed.
2. **Homonymic / polysemic spread** — the term's meaning corpus covers two or more distinct registers and the term may need sense-split treatment.
3. **Supportive / qualifying register** — the term's meanings enhance or qualify M10's characteristic without itself carrying it as a primary characteristic (e.g. an atonement term that responds to sin without itself being a sin term).
