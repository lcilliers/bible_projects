## §1 — Characteristic 16 (The sinner as moral character)

**Short name:** The sinner as moral character
**Characteristic_id:** 40
**Definition:**

> The constitutive inner moral identity of the person defined by sin — not the act or the condition but what kind of person they are at their moral core. Hamartōlos and chat.ta register: sinner as recognisable moral category.

**Constituent sub-groups:** 1
**Total verses in scope:** ~64

---

## §2 — Sub-groups (capacity organisers — not the analytical unit)

### M10-C — The sinner as moral character

**core_description:** CHAR-3 (Sinner as moral character). The constitutive inner moral identity of the person defined by sin — not what they did or the condition they are in, but what kind of person they are at their moral core. The sinner as recognisable moral category (Mat 9:10/11), as person owning their sinful identity (Luk 5:8), as those constituted sinners (Rom 5:8/19), and as habitual extreme sinners whose moral character is total (Gen 13:13 — Sodom; 1Sa 15:18 — the Amalekites; Amo 9:8 — the sinful kingdom).

**Role under The sinner as moral character:** Single-sub-group representation (sub-group = characteristic 1:1).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 64

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M10-C-VCG-03` (14 verses) — Sinner as extreme moral type: habitual wrongdoing defining total character
- `M10-C-VCG-01` (30 verses) — Sinner as social-moral category: exclusion and stigma
- `M10-C-VCG-02` (20 verses) — Sinner as constituted moral identity: imputed and declared status
