## §1 — Characteristic 17 (Guilt as inner-being state)

**Short name:** Guilt as inner-being state
**Characteristic_id:** 41
**Definition:**

> The conscience's recognition of moral liability before God — the subjective inner experience of being guilty, of bearing a wrong that cannot be hidden. Conscience in its guilt-awareness mode. Cross-register flag (pu.qah): M03 (Grief).

**Constituent sub-groups:** 1
**Total verses in scope:** ~97

## §2 — Sub-groups (capacity organisers — not the analytical unit)

### M10-D — Guilt as inner-being state

**core_description:** CHAR-4 (Guilt as inner-being state). The conscience's recognition of moral liability before God — the subjective inner experience of being guilty, of having wronged God or another, of carrying a weight that cannot be hidden. Inner-being content is the conscience in its guilt-awareness mode: realisation (Lev 5:2/3/4/5 — guilt arising when a hidden violation becomes known), weight of acknowledged guilt (Psa 38:4/18; Ezr 9:6 — guilt risen above the head), indelibility before God (Jer 2:22 — no soap removes it), and the grace of guilt's removal (Isa 6:7). The a.von punishment sub-entry belongs here for its punitive-burden-as-inner-experience meanings (Gen 4:13 Cain; Eze 4:4–6 symbolically borne). The staggering-of-conscience term (pu.qah; M03 cross-register) belongs here as a single-verse expression of guilt-awareness.

**Role under Guilt as inner-being state:** Single-sub-group representation (sub-group = characteristic 1:1).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 97

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M10-D-VCG-04` (18 verses) — Guilt's punitive consequence: the burden of punishment borne
- `M10-D-VCG-01` (18 verses) — Guilt-recognition: conscience engaging after the act
- `M10-D-VCG-03` (16 verses) — Guilt as transferable burden: borne on behalf of another
- `M10-D-VCG-02` (19 verses) — Guilt as accumulated weight: communal and personal burden mounting
- `M10-D-VCG-05` (26 verses) — Guilt exposed, indelible, and removed: the divine perspective
