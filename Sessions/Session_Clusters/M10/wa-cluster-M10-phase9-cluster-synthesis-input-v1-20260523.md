## §1 — Cluster overview

M10 (Humility, Meekness and Submission) holds 6 inner-being characteristics:

| Char | Short name | One-line definition |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Wilful sinning | The will committing a known wrong — sinning as a deliberate relational act directed against God, another person, or the self, where the person knows what they are doing and does it. |
| 2 | Unintentional sinning | Sin committed without deliberate intent — real and morally consequential even though the inner person did not consciously defy God's command; conscience engages once the act surfaces to awareness. |
| 3 | Confession | The moment the inner person names a committed sin as sin — first-person confession, corporate declaration, or the conscience voicing an act already done. |
| 4 | Conscience suppression | The inner person who does not recognize, denies, or suppresses awareness of their own sinful state — conscience absent, self-blinded, or actively claiming innocence where guilt is real. |
| 5 | Refusal to repent | The will that persistently refuses to turn from a sinful course when departure is available and known to be required — turning actively withheld. |
| 6 | Habitual defection | Sin as a recurring, established pattern of the will over time — the will's settled direction toward sin, distinct from the specific moment of refusal. |
| 7 | Contagious sin | Sin as a moral direction set by a leader whose inner commitment to wrongdoing is transmitted to those under their authority — sin propagated through authority relationships. |
| 8 | Political revolt | Breaking submission to established authority — wilful political defection carrying the moral weight of sinful transgression. |
| 9 | Sinful speech | Sin committed through the mouth: blasphemy and slander originating in inner moral corruption, directed against God or persons. |
| 10 | Specialised sinful mechanisms | Sin operating through a specific inner mechanism — desire exploited by enticement (deleazō; M14 cross-register), identity falsified through hypocrisy (sunupokrinomai; M08 cross-register), covenantal identity abandoned through apostasy (apostasia; M31 cross-register). |
| 11 | Sin as universal condition | Sin as a structural condition of the inner person — not primarily an act but a state under which every person stands. |
| 12 | Sin as enslaving power | Sin as an active force or power that masters the inner person, governing the will so it cannot freely choose otherwise. |
| 13 | Sin as divine record | Sin as an objective moral weight existing before God — recorded, accumulated, named, not hidden from divine memory even if hidden from others. |
| 14 | Forgiveness sought and received | The inner movement toward God in search of forgiveness — the will that turns and asks for sin's removal; and the divine response of granting, withholding, or declaring forgiveness. |
| 15 | Generational sin | Sin as a moral condition that enters through lineage — inherited from ancestors, transmitted through family or national belonging. |
| 16 | The sinner as moral character | The constitutive inner moral identity of the person defined by sin — not the act or the condition but what kind of person they are at their moral core. |
| 17 | Guilt as inner-being state | The conscience's recognition of moral liability before God — the subjective inner experience of being guilty, of bearing a wrong that cannot be hidden. |
| 18 | Iniquity as accumulated moral crime | The inner dimension of accumulated moral wrongdoing — crime in its moral-spiritual sense, iniquity as measured, generational, heart-seated accumulation. |
| 19 | Transgression as deliberate boundary-crossing | The wilful defiance of known moral or covenantal order — the will that knowingly crosses a boundary or breaches a covenant. |
| 20 | Faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin | The inner disposition of deliberate unfaithfulness — the will that breaks covenantal loyalty, betrays a trust, acts treacherously against God or persons. |
| 21 | Perversion as inner inversion | The inner state or disposition of moral distortion — twisting what is right, corrupting what was good, inverting the moral order from within. |
| 22 | Injustice as moral failure of right conduct | The inner state/disposition of doing wrong toward persons — unjust conduct, unjust character, the moral faculty bent toward wrongdoing against others. |

Each characteristic produced 189 findings in its Phase 9 batch (1,323 rows total in `cluster_finding`).
