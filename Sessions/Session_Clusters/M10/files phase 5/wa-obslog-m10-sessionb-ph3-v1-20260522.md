### Step 1 — Characteristic identification working notes

Reading the meaning corpora (retained in context from Phase 3 session). Deriving characteristics from what the meanings actually evidence, not from gloss patterns.

**Working through the major-volume terms first (they drive the characteristic frame):**

**cha.ta (201V) — to sin:** Meanings evidence a wide register. The dominant characteristic is *sinning as a relational act* — the will committing a wrong against God, another person, or oneself. Multiple patterns: (a) confession/recognition of sinning (Pharaoh, David, the people); (b) sinning as defection from God's command; (c) sinning producing guilt and requiring atonement; (d) sinning as directed against a specific person (interpersonal wrong). The act-of-sinning is the primary inner-being characteristic: the will in the moment of moral failure.

**chat.tat H2403B (159V):** Meanings evidence sin as a moral object/reality — sin as predatory force (Gen 4:7); communal moral weight (Exo 32:30); priestly offering (Lev 4:3ff — ritual sense); persistent pattern of a king's apostasy (1Ki 16:31). The sin-as-objective-reality characteristic: sin exists as a thing that accumulates, can be forgiven, recorded, removed. The noun-state sense differs from the verb-act sense of cha.ta.

**a.von H5771G (162V) — iniquity: crime:** Meanings evidence iniquity as a moral crime / guilt-weight — borne by the individual (Lev 5:1), visited across generations (Exo 20:5), heart-seated (Eze 14:3/4/7), self-rotting (Lev 26:39). Primary characteristic: *iniquity as accumulated moral weight/crime* — the inner dimension of carrying guilt before God.

**hamartia G0266 (105V):** Meanings evidence sin as bondage/power/condition — sin as dominating master (Joh 8:34), universal human condition (Rom 3:9/23), burden requiring lifting (Joh 1:29), death-state (Joh 8:21/24). The NT elaboration of sin-as-condition and sin-as-power.

**pe.sha H6588 (90V) — transgression:** Meanings consistently evidence *deliberate rebellion/breach* — willful defection from covenant loyalty (Gen 31:36; Amo 1:3–2:6 — accumulated breach of nations). The rebellion-against-right-order characteristic: transgression as knowing, wilful boundary-crossing.

**a.von H5771H (27V) — iniquity: guilt** and **a.von H5771I (18V) — iniquity: punishment:** The guilt-sub-entry evidences the state of carrying guilt before God (Gen 44:16 — conscience; Isa 6:7 — borne on lips, removed by God; Jer 2:22 — indelible stain). The punishment-sub-entry evidences the punitive consequence dimension (Gen 4:13 — Cain's unbearable burden; Eze 4:4–6 — physically borne). These together form the *guilt-as-inner-state* characteristic — the conscience's recognition of moral liability.

**a.sham H0816 (31V) — be guilty:** Meanings primarily evidence the moment of guilt-recognition — conscience engaged when violation surfaces (Lev 4:13; 5:2/3/4/5). The subjective guilt-awareness dimension.
