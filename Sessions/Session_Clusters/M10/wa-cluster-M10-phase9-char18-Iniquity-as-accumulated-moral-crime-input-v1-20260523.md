## §2 — Sub-groups (capacity organisers — not the analytical unit)

### M10-E — Iniquity as accumulated moral crime

**core_description:** CHAR-5 (Iniquity as accumulated crime). The inner dimension of accumulated moral wrongdoing — crime in its moral-spiritual sense, the store of inner wrongdoing existing before God as a measured, generational, heart-seated reality. Distinct from guilt (which is the subjective recognition); iniquity-as-crime is the objective moral accumulation — what is known by God even when hidden from others (Psa 90:8), what has built up over generations (Exo 20:5), what is seated in the heart (Eze 14:3/4/7), and what must be confessed before restoration (Lev 26:40/41). The inner-being dimension is the person's moral account before God: the crime-weight carried whether felt or not.

**Role under Iniquity as accumulated moral crime:** Single-sub-group representation (sub-group = characteristic 1:1).

**Verses in this sub-group:** 162

**VCGs within this sub-group:**

- `M10-E-VCG-03` (28 verses) — Iniquity in divine memory: what God sees and does not forget
- `M10-E-VCG-02` (18 verses) — Generational iniquity: transmitted across family lines
- `M10-E-VCG-04` (29 verses) — Iniquity as personal moral crime: individually owned and judged
- `M10-E-VCG-01` (17 verses) — Priestly and cultic bearing of iniquity
- `M10-E-VCG-06` (21 verses) — Iniquity pardoned and removed: divine forgiveness resolves the moral debt
- `M10-E-VCG-05` (30 verses) — Iniquity as overwhelming accumulated weight
- `M10-E-VCG-08` (4 verses) — Iniquity heart-seated: the crime originating in the will
- `M10-E-VCG-07` (15 verses) — Iniquity as self-destructive force: the inner crime that ruins from within
