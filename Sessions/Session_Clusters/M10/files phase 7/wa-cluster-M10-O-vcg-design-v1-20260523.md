## VCGs

### M10-O-VCG-01 — Habitual defection: the will's settled pattern toward sin (14 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is characterised as a recurring, established orientation of the will — not a single act but a way of living. Pharaoh sinned again by hardening his heart once the plague ceased: sinning explicitly linked to the will's hardening driving repeated transgression (Exo 9:34). Sinning against the Lord more severe than against humans; when directed at God no mediator can intercede (1Sa 2:25). No one who does not sin — the universal habitual reality grounding Solomon's prayer (1Ki 8:46). Sinning against the Lord through fear of other gods — a transferred inner allegiance (2Ki 17:7). Even the righteous man cannot escape sinning — universality as the frame for the settled human condition (Ecc 7:20; 7:26; 9:2/18). Sinning as ritualized and self-reinforcing — altars multiplied for sinning produce more sinning (Hos 8:11). Sinning as persistent historical pattern from Gibeah (Hos 10:9). Sinning in the wilderness provoked 40 years of divine wrath (Heb 3:17). Continuing to sin revealing an inner state of never having known Christ (1Jo 3:6). Keyword axis: `defection habitual` (2), `sin habitual` (2), `universality sin` (2), `hardening` (1), `defection repeated` (1).
