## VCGs

### M10-K-VCG-01 — Unintentional sin and moral surprise: conscience engaged after the fact (10 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sin occurs without deliberate intent — inadvertent violation, hidden uncleanness becoming known, or rash speech without awareness of its implications — yet the sin is objectively real and the conscience must respond. Lev 4:2 (unintentional act still constitutes real sin). Lev 5:1 (willful silence of witness — borderline but real sin of omission). Lev 5:5 (sin committed then confessed). Num 15:27/28 (sinning unintentionally still requires offering; even inadvertent sin creates a condition before God). Balaam confesses sin arising from ignorance (Num 22:34). 1Sa 14:33/34 (ritual transgression from urgency not defiance). Lev 6:2/3 (deliberate deception against neighbour — here as borderline: wilful deception is in M10-J, but within M10-K context). Keyword axis: `violation inadvertent` (2), `sin real` (2), `ignorance` (1), `unintentional` (1).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19052 (Lev 4:2)

**Members (10):**

### M10-K-VCG-02 — Cultic purification: the ritual-cleansing register (8 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where cha.ta is used in its ritual-purification sense — cleansing a sacred space or object, consecrating it for service. Moses applies blood to the altar to purify it (Lev 8:15). Aaron addresses the people's collective condition through the sin offering (Lev 9:15). Atonement purifies the altar in Ezekiel's vision (Eze 43:20/22). The sanctuary purified of sin annually (Eze 45:18). Exo 29:36 (daily bull for atonement). Lev 19:22 (guilt offering followed by priestly atonement). The characteristic is sin as a ritual-cultic contaminant on sacred spaces requiring blood-rite cleansing, distinct from the personal moral-conscience register. Keyword axis: `purification ritual` (2), `consecration restored` (2), `defilement cultic` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19097 (Lev 8:15)

**Members (8):**
19074, 19084, 19097, 19098, 19079, 19107, 19108, 19110
