## VCGs

### M10-E-VCG-01 — Priestly and cultic bearing of iniquity (17 verses)

**context_description:** The Levitical block where iniquity is a moral-cultic burden borne by priests or Levites on behalf of the community — assigned as a structural feature of priestly service. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a real transferable moral weight that priestly persons absorb so Israel can approach God's holy space. The Levitical system institutionalised the bearing of iniquity as a protective buffer: the priest who enters the sanctuary improperly bears the iniquity personally (Exo 28:38/43); the sin-offering eaten by the priest on the congregation's behalf transfers their moral load (Lev 10:17); the scapegoat symbolically carries away the named iniquities of Israel (Lev 16:21/22). The keyword `iniquity bearing` (9 cluster-wide, concentrated here), `atonement communal`.

### M10-E-VCG-02 — Generational iniquity: transmitted across family lines (18 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity is transmitted as a moral inheritance across generations — the idolatrous fathers' crime visited on children to the third and fourth generation (Exo 20:5), the people wasting away in exile because of their fathers' iniquity as well as their own (Lev 26:39), and communities bearing the accumulated generational weight of ancestral wrongdoing (Neh 9:2; Jer 11:10; Lam 5:7; Isa 65:7). The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a spiritually transmissible moral condition: not merely that consequences extend to descendants, but that the crime's weight and its moral DNA pass through lineage. The keyword `guilt inherited` (6), `iniquity inherited` (5), `defection generational` (8 cluster-wide).

### M10-E-VCG-03 — Iniquity in divine memory: what God sees and does not forget (28 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where the defining characteristic of iniquity is its standing in God's awareness — he sees it when no one else does (Psa 90:8 — set fully in his light), he remembers it when others have forgotten (Hos 8:13; 9:9; 22:22/24; Jer 14:10/16:17), he exposes it as a hidden criminal act (Job 20:27; Hos 7:1; Isa 26:21), and he holds the record against the day of judgment. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a recorded moral fact before God, present in his awareness whether the person knows it or not. The keyword `memory divine` (5), `record divine`, `guilt surfacing` (5), `iniquity hidden` (3).

### M10-E-VCG-04 — Iniquity as personal moral crime: individually owned and judged (29 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity is a specific criminal wrong owned by the individual who committed it — not generational or corporate but personally attributed, personally borne, and personally judged. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as an identified crime that belongs to its perpetrator and cannot be displaced: Eli's knowing inaction made the crime his own (1Sa 3:13); David asks God to take away his iniquity (2Sa 24:10/1Ch 21:8); each person dies for their own iniquity and no other's (Jer 31:30; Eze 18:20); Job categorizes specific acts as iniquities deserving judicial punishment (Job 31:11/28). The keyword `iniquity personal` (4), `accountability individual`, `liability personal`.

### M10-E-VCG-05 — Iniquity as overwhelming accumulated weight: the crushing burden (30 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity functions as an active, accumulating weight that overwhelms the inner person — iniquities have gone over the head (Psa 38:4), overtaken the heart (Psa 40:12), clouded perception, drained vitality (Eze 7:13), and produced groaning and wasting (Eze 24:23; 7:16). Also the corporate verses where the sheer quantity of accumulated iniquity determines the severity of divine response (Job 22:5; Eze 28:18; Isa 64:6–7). The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a quantifiable, accumulating moral debt whose weight presses on the person or community and produces inner and outer collapse. The keyword `guilt accumulated` (9 cluster-wide), `accumulation moral` (3), `weight crushing` (3).

### M10-E-VCG-06 — Iniquity pardoned and removed: divine forgiveness resolves the moral debt (21 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity's removal is the central event — God forgives, God removes, God tramples underfoot, God covers. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a moral debt whose resolution requires divine action: it cannot be cleaned by soap (already in M10-D), but it can be pardoned by God (Num 14:19/Psa 25:11), removed in a single divine act (Zec 3:4/9), treaded underfoot by compassion (Mic 7:19), or borne by the servant so others go free (Isa 53:5/6/11). Also: the future promise that iniquity will be sought and not found (Jer 50:20), and the new covenant's declaration that God will forgive and not remember (Jer 31:34). The keyword `pardon sought` (6), `forgiveness divine` (3), `burden lifted`.

### M10-E-VCG-07 — Iniquity as self-destructive force: the inner crime that ruins from within (15 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity operates as an active, self-destructive force within the person or community — iniquity as the snare that catches the wicked in their own cords (Pro 5:22), as the structural flaw that causes sudden collapse (Isa 30:13), as a rotting force that wastes from within in exile (Lev 26:39 — in VCG-02, but the rotting-force dimension concentrates further in Eze), as the stumbling block that trips the person (Hos 14:1/27; Eze 7:19), and as the inner force that sweeps persons helplessly from God like wind (Isa 64:6). Also priests who crave the people's iniquity for gain (Hos 4:8). The keyword `guilt accumulating` (in VCG-05 primarily), but here the *self-destruction* dimension drives the grouping.

### M10-E-VCG-08 — Iniquity heart-seated: the crime originating in the will (4 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where iniquity is explicitly located in the heart or will as its source — not the outward act but the inner orientation from which the crime proceeds. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity as a heart-resident reality: Eze 14:3/4/7 most precisely name this — idols taken inward and positioned before the face, so that the crime originates in the will and then governs the entire orientation. Eliphaz charges that Job's iniquity actively schools his mouth — an interior moral corruption driving crafty speech (Job 15:5). The keyword `iniquity hidden` (hidden in the heart, Job 31:33 — in VCG-03), `guilt hidden` (3 in M10-E keywords).
