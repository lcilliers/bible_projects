## VCGs

### M10-A-VCG-01 — Personal confession: the will that names its own sin (42 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where the inner act of sinning surfaces through the person's own voice — first-person confessions directed at God or at another. The inner-being characteristic is conscience in its confessional mode: naming the act as sin, owning accountability, revealing moral reality. The range spans rulers (Pharaoh: Exo 9:27/10:16; Saul; David; Hezekiah), individuals (Judas; the prodigal; Aaron; Balaam; Shimei; Job 33:27), communities (Israel at Mizpah; the exiles in Babylon; Nehemiah's household), and prophets (Daniel's fourfold confession). Confession does not always produce transformed will (Num 14:40; 1Sa 15:30 — Saul's self-interested confession), but it names the inner moral fact.

### M10-A-VCG-02 — Sin directed against God: the vertical relational offense (28 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is explicitly framed as a relational act directed upward against God himself — his person, holiness, authority, or covenant. The inner-being characteristic is the vertical dimension of moral failure: the will's offense against the divine, the conscience aware that what was done strikes against God as its primary object. Covers deliberate idolatrous defection (Deu 9:16; Hos 4:7), disobedience named as sin-against-God (Jer 44:23; Eze 14:13), individual moral accountability before God (Eze 18:4/20), and specific acts of covenant violation (Jer 50:7; Neh 9:29). Psa 51:4 ("against you alone have I sinned") defines the register at its most concentrated.

**Anchor:** vc_id=18919 (Psa 51:4)

### M10-A-VCG-03 — Sin against the neighbour: interpersonal moral wrong (19 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is a relational act directed against another person — a brother, an innocent party, a superior, or a community member. The inner-being characteristic is sin as violation of a human bond: the conscience names the act as wrong toward a specific other, the will bears accountability for harm done. The NT hamartanō interpersonal corpus (Mat 18:15/21; Luk 17:3/4; 1Cor 8:12) specifies the register clearly. OT examples include sinning against innocent blood (1Sa 19:4/5), sinning against a neighbour through social wrongs (Lev 6:2/3), and sinning against authority (Gen 40:1; Exo 5:16).

### M10-A-VCG-04 — Sin requiring ritual remedy: the sacrificial-cultic register (29 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning triggers a prescribed sacrificial or ritual mechanism — the Levitical sin-offering system and altar purification in Ezekiel. The inner-being characteristic is sin as a moral-cultic reality requiring external mediation: the act committed (even unintentionally) creates a condition before God that only priestly intercession and sacrifice can resolve. The conscience is implicated even in inadvertent violations. The register runs from Lev 4:2 (unintentional sin) through the entire offering-system block (Lev 4–6) to the altar-purification verses in Eze 43/45 (minimal inner-being but preserving the cultic-sinning frame).

### M10-A-VCG-05 — Sin propagated by leaders: the Jeroboam contagion pattern (27 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is a moral course that a leader establishes and others perpetuate — the will of a leader sets an idolatrous path, and successive kings choose to walk in it rather than turn. The inner-being characteristic is sin as propagated direction of the will: the choosing not to turn is itself the sin. The repeated formula "did not depart from Jeroboam's sins" names a persistent volitional act — a will that clings to an inherited corrupt course. Also covers Manasseh's corrupting leadership transmitting sin to Judah (2Ki 21:11/16/17; 19039/40/41).

### M10-A-VCG-07 — Universal sinning: no one does not sin (24 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is characterised as universal — all sin, the condition is inescapable within mortal human existence, and this universality grounds both judgment and the provision for forgiveness. The inner-being characteristic is the fact-of-universal-sinning as a structural reality of human nature. Includes OT wisdom statement (1Ki 8:46; Ecc 7:20; 9:2), persistent sin patterns (Hos 4:7; 8:11; 10:9; 13:2 — escalating compulsion), and NT theological grounding of universality (Rom 2:12; 3:23; 5:12/14/16). Also includes Ecc variants of the universal sinner-state and related observation-wisdom on sin's inevitability.

### M10-A-VCG-08 — Restraint and vigilance: guarding the will against sinning (19 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where the inner-being focus is on preventing sinning — fear that guards the will, God's word stored in the heart, deliberate watch kept over the mouth, and the warning that the will must act quickly before sin takes hold. The inner-being characteristic is volitional restraint: the person exercises deliberate control to avoid sinning. Also covers the 1 John and Hebrews cluster where habitual sinning reveals (or is prevented by) the inner constitution — the new birth that makes sustained sinning structurally incompatible (1Jo 3:6/8/9), and deliberate sinning against truth that closes off remedy (Heb 10:26).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19073 (Exo 20:20)

### M10-A-VCG-09 — Accountability and innocence: the legal/relational status of sin (23 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning functions as a legal or relational status concept — the person who has or has not sinned, whose conscience is clear or burdened, whose moral standing is being assessed. The inner-being characteristic is sin as a moral-legal status: protestation of innocence (Job 33:9; Judg 11:27; 1Sa 24:11; Act 25:8; 2Sa 12:13 in VCG-01 for confessional register), accountability accepted or denied (Gen 43:9/44:32; Exo 5:16), sin as identifiable offense requiring evidence (Deu 19:15; Gen 31:39). Also covers specialised nuance verses: marriage not-sin (1Cor 7:28/36); sin as the negative of an obligation (1Sa 12:23 — ceasing to pray); the literal-physical Judg 20:16 (missing a target).

**Anchor:** vc_id=18967 (Judg 11:27 — "Sinning here is what Jephthah denies having done; his conscience is clear")

### M10-A-VCG-10 — Political revolt as sinful act: pa.sha secondary register (9 verses)

**context_description:** Verses from pa.sha H6586 allocated to M10-A as secondary placement, where the term names a political act of revolt — a subordinate nation or people breaking submission to an established authority. The nine verses document Moab, Edom, Libnah, and Israel breaking from Davidic or Israelite authority.

### M10-A-VCG-11 — Blasphemy and slander: sin-of-speech (M06 cross-register) (22 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is committed through the mouth directed against God or persons — the sinful-speech register of blasfēmia (18V) and blasfēmos (4V). The inner-being characteristic is sin originating in the heart that expresses itself through destructive verbal acts: slander listed among heart-generated evils (Mat 15:19; Mar 7:22), blasphemy as presumptuous claim to divine authority (Joh 10:33), the unforgivable blasphemy as crossing an inner boundary into permanent guilt (Mat 12:31), and slander as expression of inner hostility requiring renunciation (Eph 4:31; Col 3:8). Revelation's beast-blasphemy verses show the register at its most extreme: blasphemy as constitutive identity. Cross-register M06 (Hate, Contempt and Hostility) preserved throughout: these speech-acts carry a contempt dimension toward their object.

**Anchor:** vc_id=37618 (Mat 15:19)

### M10-A-VCG-12 — Enticement, hypocrisy, and apostasy: specialised sin-act mechanisms (5 verses)

**context_description:** Verses from three small-corpus cross-register-flagged terms. deleazō G1185 (3V; M14 Deceit): enticement operates through the inner faculty of desire — the hook that draws and captures the person is internal to them (Jam 1:14; 2Pe 2:14/18). sunupokrinomai G4942 (1V; M08 Pride): joining in hypocrisy is a sinful act of false-appearance producing social moral contagion (Gal 2:13). apostasia G0646 (1V; M31): apostasy as covenantal defection from inherited identity (Act 21:21). These three mechanisms share a common structure: sinning through a specific inner faculty or mechanism — desire deceived, appearance falsified, identity abandoned.
