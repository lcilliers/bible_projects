## VCGs

### M10-X-VCG-01 — Generational sin: the ancestral moral condition inherited (8 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinning is explicitly generational — spanning ancestors and the present generation, persisting across time as an inherited moral orientation, or traced to the first father as the origin of a pattern extending through history. The exiles' confession "we have sinned and acted perversely and wickedly" in captivity — sin as a condition owned in exile that prompts inner turning toward restoration (1Ki 8:47). Sinning as a persistent wilful act against God even amid his provision, compounded by open rebellion in the wilderness (Psa 78:17). Sinning persisted despite witnessed wonders, rooted in unbelief of the heart — the people's refusal to believe God's works (Psa 78:32). Sinning traced to the first father as an ancestral generational failure in the will persisting through appointed mediators (Isa 43:27). Sinning in response to God's anger, prolonged a long time — a sustained condition of the will across generations (Isa 64:5). Sinning as the defining identity of the cursed person in the new creation context (Isa 65:20). Sinning against God acknowledged as the cause of doom — Israel recognising their inner transgression (Jer 8:14). Past backsliding into idolatry — God promises rescue and cleansing from this inherited sinful pattern (Eze 37:23). Keyword axis: `defection generational` (1), `defection persistent` (1), `guilt acknowledged` (1), `repentance inward` (1), `sin persistent` (1).
