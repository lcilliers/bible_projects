## VCGs

### M10-R-VCG-01 — Slander and abusive speech: heart-generated inner corruption (9 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where sinful speech originates explicitly in the heart's moral corruption and flows outward as slander directed at persons. Slander listed among evils that originate inside the heart (Mat 15:19); appearing in a list of inner-generated evils from the heart (Mar 7:22); listed among destructive inner-outward dispositions — bitterness, wrath, anger — flowing from a corrupted relational will (Eph 4:31); slander rooted in inner hostility, stripped off as outward expression of inward corruption (Col 3:8); slander arising from diseased will and inflated ego (1Ti 6:4); abusive/blasphemous character trait in end-times list rooted in self-love (2Ti 3:2); Paul's former blasphemy rooted in ignorant unbelief (1Ti 1:13); false witnesses charge Stephen with blasphemous words (Act 6:11); Michael withholds blasphemous verdict, deferring to God (Jude 9). M06 cross-register: contempt toward the object is the connecting faculty. Keyword axis: `heart sourcing` (2), `inner origination` (2), `slander generated` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=37618 (Mat 15:19 — "Slander listed among evils that originate inside the heart, locating its source in the inner moral center")

### M10-R-VCG-02 — Blasphemy as divine-authority claim: presumptuous usurpation (7 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where blasphemy is specifically the verbal act of claiming or usurping divine prerogative — the inner presumption of claiming divine identity or authority that belongs to God alone. Blasphemy in general forgivable but against the Holy Spirit irreversible (Mat 12:31 — threshold-crossing). High priest declares Jesus' claim blasphemy (Mat 26:65). Scribes charge Jesus with blasphemy for forgiving sins (Mar 2:7). All blasphemies forgivable, setting up the singular exception (Mar 3:28). Council condemns Jesus to death for blasphemy (Mar 14:64). Pharisees identify Jesus' forgiveness declaration as usurpation of divine prerogative (Luk 5:21). Jews charge Jesus with blasphemy for claiming to be God (Joh 10:33). M06 cross-register: contempt toward divine authority. Keyword axis: `claim divine` (2), `authority divine` (2), `guilt irreversible` (1), `spirit resisted` (1).

**Anchor:** vc_id=37609 (Mar 2:7 — "blasphemy because he claims to forgive sins — presumptuous speech trespassing on God's exclusive role")

**Members (7):**
37606, 37608, 37609, 37610, 37611, 37612, 37613

### M10-R-VCG-03 — Blasphemy as identity: the beast's constitutive defiance (6 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where blasphemy is not a discrete speech-act but a constitutive identity-marker of a creature entirely defined by its defiance of God — the Revelation beast corpus plus the false verbal identity of false claimants. The beast bears blasphemous names inscribed on its heads (Rev 13:1); its mouth utters haughty blasphemous words as an empowered authority (Rev 13:5); blasphemy directed at God's name, dwelling, and people comprehensively (Rev 13:6); blasphemous names saturating the beast's whole nature (Rev 17:3). False verbal claim defaming God's community (Rev 2:9). Even angels restrain from blasphemous verdict against spiritual beings (2Pe 2:11). The characteristic is blasphemy as a total-being posture of defiance — the creature's nature defined by what it asserts against God. M06 cross-register: contempt as constitutive orientation. Keyword axis: `will restrained` (2), `presumption withheld` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=37615 (Rev 13:1 — "the beast bears blasphemous names inscribed on its very heads — blasphemy not an act here but a constitutive identity marker")

**Members (6):**
37623, 37615, 37616, 37607, 37617, 37627
