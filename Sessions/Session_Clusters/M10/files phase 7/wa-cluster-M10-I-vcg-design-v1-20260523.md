## VCGs

### M10-I-VCG-01 — Judicial and commercial injustice: the corruption of right process (20 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where injustice specifically operates within judicial or commercial contexts — partial judgment in courts (Lev 19:15; Psa 82:2; 2Ch 19:7), dishonest measurement (Lev 19:35; Deu 25:16), God's character as entirely without injustice (Deu 32:4; Psa 92:15; Job 34:10), and Ezekiel's sequence on the righteous person who abstains from injustice (Eze 18:8/24/26/33/13/15/18). The inner-being characteristic is injustice as a corruption of the faculty responsible for right judgment — the will that tilts the scales. M26 cross-register throughout. Keyword axis: `injustice foundational` (2), `partiality` (2), `dishonesty habitual` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=21728 (Psa 53:1 — "Injustice flows from the inner denial of God; the fool's heart-assertion produces corrupt, abominable conduct — inward godlessness drives outward wrongdoing")

### M10-I-VCG-02 — Injustice as oppression and harm to persons (33 verses)

**context_description:** The av.lah corpus — verses where injustice is the violent, predatory, or oppressive harm done against persons. The inner-being characteristic is injustice as moral wrongdoing directed outward: devised in the heart before enacted by hands (Psa 58:2), deliberately plotted through inward searching (Psa 64:6), a disposition that consumes wrongdoing like water (Job 15:16), the material used to build cities (Mic 3:10; Hab 2:12). Also: injustice of murder (2Sa 3:34), oppression by violent men (2Sa 7:10; 1Ch 17:9), and the wisdom/prophetic pattern where injustice is the inverse of righteousness. M26 cross-register throughout. Keyword axis: `wickedness external` (3), `injustice absent` (3), `will corrupted` (3).

**Anchor:** vc_id=21758 (Psa 58:2 — "Injustice is devised inwardly in the heart before it is enacted by the hands")

### M10-I-VCG-03 — Character of the unjust person: adikos, av.val, a.vil, adikēma (19 verses)

**context_description:** The NT and wisdom register where injustice names the moral character of a person — the unjust/unrighteous as a fixed inner disposition. adikos: unjust person receives sun and rain in common grace (Mat 5:45), unjust in small = unjust across all scales (Luk 16:10), unjust persons excluded from inheriting God's kingdom (1Cor 6:9), the unjust for whom Christ died (1Pe 3:18), the unrighteous under ongoing punishment (2Pe 2:9). adikēma: Gallio on actionable crime (Act 18:14), Paul denying wrongdoing before the council (Act 24:20), cosmic accumulated crimes (Rev 18:5). av.val: Bildad on the unrighteous home (Job 18:21), Job invoking the unjust as his adversary (Job 27:7), Job recalling breaking the unjust predator's power (Job 29:17), Job's appointed calamity for the unrighteous (Job 31:3), the unjust without shame (Zep 3:5). a.vil: God surrendering Job to ungodly/lawless people (Job 16:11). M26 cross-register throughout.

**Anchor:** vc_id=23890 (1Cor 6:9 — "the unrighteous whose character excludes them from inheriting God's kingdom")
