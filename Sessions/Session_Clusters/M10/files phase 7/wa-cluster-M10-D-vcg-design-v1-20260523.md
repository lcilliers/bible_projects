## VCGs

### M10-D-VCG-01 — Guilt-recognition: conscience engaging after the act (18 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where guilt surfaces through the inner process of coming to know what was done — the moment conscience engages after a violation, whether deliberate or inadvertent. Dominated by the a.sham realization block in Leviticus (Lev 4–6; Num 5): hidden violation becomes known, realization triggers obligation, realization leads to confession. The keyword axis `guilt realized` (7), `guilt incurred` (6), `conscience activating` (2), `realization post-act`. Guilt here is an inner state that arises when knowledge comes — objective moral reality independent of prior awareness, engaging the conscience as the bridge between the act and moral responsibility. Also includes a.shem (H0818) recognition verses and the Hos 5:15 recognition-opening-return verse.

**Anchor:** vc_id=18826 (Lev 5:5)

### M10-D-VCG-02 — Guilt as accumulated weight: communal and personal burden mounting (19 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where guilt is experienced as an accumulated, crushing inner burden — so vast it rises above the head (Ezr 9:6), grows with each new sin (2Ch 28:13), or is declared as 'great' in magnitude (Jer 30:14/15; Hos 10:10). Dominated by the ash.mah (H0819) communal-guilt corpus: Judah's corporate guilt before God through specific decisions (Ezr 9:6–15; 2Ch 24:18; 28:10/13), and the a.von H heavy-weight verses (Job 11:6; Jer 30:14/15; Hos 10:10). The keyword axis `guilt accumulated` (4), `weight crushing` (3), `debt moral` (4), `guilt bearing`. Distinct from VCG-03 (where guilt is transferred) by the emphasis on the sheer magnitude pressing down on the one who bears it.

**Anchor:** vc_id=18902 (Ezr 9:6)

### M10-D-VCG-03 — Guilt as transferable burden: borne on behalf of another (16 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where guilt functions as a moral burden that can be transferred, borne vicariously, or redirected between persons — the priestly bearing of Israel's guilt on the high priest's forehead (Exo 28:38), Abigail's offer to assume David's guilt (1Sa 25:24), the relational negotiations over where guilt rightly resides (2Sa 14:9/32; 19:19). The inner-being characteristic is guilt as a portable moral weight whose bearing is itself a moral act: the person who voluntarily assumes another's guilt, the person who contests guilt's attribution, and the God who exposes guilt that human self-justification would hide (Gen 44:16). The keyword axis `guilt transferred` (13 cluster-wide, concentrated here), `burden transferred`.

**Anchor:** vc_id=19642 (1Sa 25:24)

### M10-D-VCG-04 — Guilt's punitive consequence: the burden of punishment borne (18 verses)

**context_description:** Verses from the a.von I (punishment) sub-entry where guilt's weight is experienced specifically as punitive consequence — the penalty of iniquity physically, temporally, or symbolically borne. The inner-being characteristic is iniquity in its punishment dimension: the anguish of bearing what guilt produces. From Cain's unbearable punitive weight (Gen 4:13) through Ezekiel's symbolic lying on his side for 390 days corresponding to Israel's years of iniquity (Eze 4:4–6), to Babylon's and Zion's deserved punitive consequences (Jer 51:6; Lam 4:6). Punishment is proportional, temporal, and in some cases falls on those within reach of the guilty party. The keyword axis `punishment borne` (4).

### M10-D-VCG-05 — Guilt exposed, indelible, and removed: the divine perspective on moral liability (26 verses)

**context_description:** Verses where guilt is presented as a condition fully visible to God that cannot be concealed, that stains indelibly (Jer 2:22 — no soap removes it), or that God exposes and removes by his own action (Isa 6:7 — guilt taken away by seraph's touch). Includes: the verses showing guilt as a legal-moral state exposed through its consequences (Eze 22:4; Hos 13:1/16), guilt brought to divine remembrance despite human forgetfulness (Eze 21:23/24), guilt transparent to God even when hidden from others (Psa 69:5), and the single pu.qah verse (1Sa 25:31 — the staggering of conscience, M03 cross-register). Also the Amo 8:14 verse where guilt as idol is a defining identity marker. The keyword axis `guilt exposed` (3), `guilt surfacing` (in M10-D), `burden lifted` (for the removal register).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19635 (Jer 2:22)
