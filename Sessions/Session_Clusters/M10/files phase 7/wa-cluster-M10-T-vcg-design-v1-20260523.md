## VCGs

### M10-T-VCG-01 — Wickedness as defining inner condition (5 verses)

**context_description:** The rish.ah (H7564, 4V) and al.vah (H5932, 1V) corpus in M10-T where wickedness is a settled inner condition constituting the person or nation morally. Wickedness as consuming fire (Isa 9:18); wickedness as permanent national identity (Mal 1:4; 3:15; 4:1); iniquity as the defining moral characteristic making a people legitimate targets of judgment (Hos 10:9). The characteristic is wickedness as an enduring inner condition — what the person or community is, not merely what they do.

### M10-T-VCG-02 — Sin forgiven and addressed: the NT redemption register (40 verses)

**context_description:** The hamartia NT corpus where sin is the condition that divine action addresses — forgiven, covered, blotted out, redeemed. Jesus saves his people from their sins (Mat 1:21); declares sins forgiven (Mat 9:2–6; Mar 2:5–10; Luk 5:20–24); John's baptism for forgiveness (Mar 1:4/5; Luk 3:3); Acts proclamation of forgiveness through Jesus. Christ died for our sins (1Cor 15:3); epistles on sins covered/removed through Christ's blood. Paraptōma forgiveness (Eph 2:1 — dead in trespasses raised). Hamartēma forgivable (Mar 3:28). The characteristic is sin as the defined condition requiring and receiving divine redemptive action. Keyword axis: `forgiveness received` (4), `guilt removed` (4), `burden lifted` (3), `repentance turning` (4).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19671 (Mat 1:21)

### M10-T-VCG-03 — Sin as enslaving and dominating power (67 verses)

**context_description:** The Romans 6–7 and related hamartia passages where sin is not what one does but what masters one — sin as an active power, law, tyrant, indwelling force. Sin entered the world bringing death universally (Rom 5:12; 19722). Sin reigned in death (19732); its dominion denied over the believer through grace (Rom 6:12–22). Sin as an indwelling power hijacking agency from within (Rom 7:7–25). The Spirit's law liberates from sin's law of death (Rom 8:2/3/10). Sin as spiritual death-state (Eph 2:1 Joh 8:21/24). Universal bondage encompassing all humanity (Rom 3:9). Unbelief as the root sin (Joh 16:8/9). Also: sin producing knowledge through the law (Rom 3:20), sin's deceptive grip hardening the heart (Heb 3:13), sin defined as action not from faith (Rom 14:23), Gal 3:22 sin imprisoning everything. Keyword axis: `sin condition` (4), `spiritual death` (3), `authority releasing` (3).
