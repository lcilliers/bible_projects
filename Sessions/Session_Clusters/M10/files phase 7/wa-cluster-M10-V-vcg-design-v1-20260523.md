### M10-V-VCG-01 — Chet: personal moral liability before God (32 verses)

**context_description:** The chet H2399 corpus (33V in sub-group, minus 19163 in M10-C = 32V here) where sin is a personal moral liability carried before God — guilt borne personally, incurred through specific acts, or present at the origin of human existence (Psa 51:5 — sin at conception). The chet register focuses on individual moral accountability: sin incurred through specific wrong (Lev 19:17; 20:20; 22:9; 24:15; Num 9:13; 18:22/32; 27:3), sin as personal and non-transferable (Deu 24:16; 2Ch 25:4; Jer 31:30), sin originating in the heart before the outward act (Deu 15:9), duty neglected incurring sin (Deu 23:21/22; 24:15), sin as capital offense (Deu 21:22), cupbearer's recalled offense (Gen 41:9). Keyword axis: `guilt personal` (4), `sin personal` (2), `liability carried` (2), `death deserved` (2).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19132 (Psa 51:5 — "iniquity characterizes the womb-context of the psalmist's birth — sin at the deepest constitutional origin of human existence")

### M10-V-VCG-02 — Cha.ta.ah: sin as objective moral category requiring covering (9 verses)

**context_description:** The cha.ta.ah (H2401, 8V) and chat.ta.ah (H2403A, 2V) corpus where sin is named as an objective moral reality requiring divine covering or pardon. God's character includes forgiving sin alongside iniquity and transgression (Exo 34:7 — chat.ta.ah). Great sin brought upon the people by Aaron's leadership failure (Exo 32:21). Moses names the golden calf as a great sin (Exo 32:30/31). Covering of sin brings blessedness (Psa 32:1 — cha.ta.ah). Sin offering as ritual God has not required (Psa 40:6 — here cha.ta.ah refers to the sacrificial institution). Prayer itself can become sin when offered by the guilty (Psa 109:7). Jeroboam drove Israel away from following the Lord — the great sin (2Ki 17:21). Keyword axis: `sin objective` (in M10-W analytics), `sin named` (4 in M10-V overall).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19182 (Psa 32:1 — "sin covered brings blessedness — the covering by God restores the person to a state of acceptance")

**Members (9):**
19183, 19184, 37569, 19187, 19182, 19188, 19191, 19189, 19190

### M10-V-VCG-03 — Sin under divine scrutiny: Job's surveillance register (5 verses)

**context_description:** Five verses from the Job chat.tat block where sin is perceived as something God is actively searching for within Job — a hidden criminal fault under relentless divine inquiry. God's eyes searching Job for sin as a divine investigator (Job 10:6). Sin as the object of constant divine monitoring closing off any possibility of acquittal (Job 10:14). God enumerating and specifying sins that Job cannot himself account for (Job 13:23). God no longer surveilling his every sin — the envied future relief (Job 14:16). Job's rebellion added on top of existing sin by Elihu (Job 34:37). Keyword axis: `memory divine` (5 in M10-E but concentrated in M10-V Job block), `record divine` (4), `scrutiny divine` (4 cluster-wide).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19276 (Job 10:6 — "Job portrays sin as something God is urgently seeking within him — a hidden criminal fault that divine inquiry pursues")

**Members (5):**

### M10-V-VCG-04 — Jeroboam-sin legacy: the named sin as recorded pattern (46 verses)

**context_description:** The large chat.tat (H2403B) Kings-Samuel block where sin is the named, recorded moral pattern of Jeroboam's household and the succession of kings who perpetuated it. The sin is not primarily the act of sinning (M10-P/N) but the objective named sin-object: "Jeroboam's sins" as a recorded moral legacy that successive kings inhabit or depart from. 1Sa–2Ch block: sin of Eli's sons (contempt for the sacred, 19229); sin of demanding a king (19230); sin as specific transgression on a given day (19231); rebellion = sin of divination (19232); Saul seeking pardon (19233); David's conscience finding no sin (19234); Nathan's declaration "the Lord has put away your sin" (19235); Solomon's prayer (19236–38); golden calves "became a sin" (19239–75). Keyword axis: `sin recorded` (5), `sin accumulating` (5), `defection chosen` (4), `defection inherited` (4).

**Anchor:** vc_id=19235 (2Sa 12:13 chat.tat — "the Lord 'has put away your sin' — sin as a concrete moral stain requiring divine action to remove")
