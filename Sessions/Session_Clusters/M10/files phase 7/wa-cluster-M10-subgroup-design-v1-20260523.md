## §1. Characteristics identified

Nine distinct inner-being faculties/states are evidenced by the M10 meaning corpus. These are derived from reading the Pass A meanings across all major-corpus terms.

### Characteristic 1 — Sin as committed act

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The will in the moment of moral failure — sinning as a directed act against God, another person, or oneself. This is the *active* dimension: the person doing wrong, the conscience registering a committed fault, the will that defected, disobeyed, or violated. The meanings in this register describe sinning as something one does (verb-form vocabulary dominant), with the inner dimension being the volitional moral failure — conscience struck, the will that chose wrongly, the act that now requires accounting for.

Key evidence: cha.ta meanings covering confession (Pharaoh — Exo 9:27; David — 2Sa 12:13), protective restraint (Exo 20:20 — God's terrifying presence to deter sinning), interpersonal wrong (Mat 18:15 — sinning against a brother), and volitional defection (Deu 9:16 — quickly turning from the commanded way). Also: hamartanō's 37-verse corpus covering the full range of sinning-as-act; the slander/blasphemy terms (speech-act register of sinning); enticement vocabulary (deleazō — the mechanism that leads the inner person to sin through desire); the hypocrisy term (sinful act of false-appearance).

### Characteristic 2 — Sin as moral condition / state

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* Sin as an objective reality that the inner person carries — not primarily the moment of doing, but the condition of being under sin, burdened by it, dominated by it, needing it removed. The meanings in this register describe sin as a thing that accumulates (Exo 32:30 — great sin requiring atonement), is recorded against the person (Rev 18:5 — crimes heaped to heaven), enslaves (Joh 8:34 — practicing sin makes one its slave), and constitutes a death-state (Eph 2:1 — dead in trespasses). The inner-being characteristic is the condition of the person *under* sin, not the act of sinning.

Key evidence: hamartia's extensive corpus (universal human condition, bondage, power — Rom 3:9; Joh 8:34), chat.tat H2403B (sin as predatory force crouching at the door — Gen 4:7; sin as persistent king-pattern — 1Ki 16:31), paraptōma (death-state from trespass — Eph 2:1/5; Col 2:13), rish.ah (wickedness as defining inner condition — Isa 9:18; Mal 1:4), and the atonement/sin-offering terms (kip.pu.rim, chat.tat H2403H) whose verses reflect sin-as-condition requiring covering.

### Characteristic 3 — The sinner as moral character

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The constitutive inner identity of the person defined by sin — not the act (Char 1) or the condition (Char 2), but the person whose character is marked by sin as their essential moral description. The meanings in this register describe sinners as a recognisable social-moral category (Mat 9:10/11), as those whose inner character is sinfulness (Luk 5:8 — "I am a sinful man"; Rom 5:8 — "while we were still sinners"), as those constituted sinners (Rom 5:19 — imputed inner status), and as persons whose persistent pattern of sinning discloses their allegiance (1Jo 3:6/8/9). The characteristic is the *identity* dimension: what kind of person this is at their moral core.

Key evidence: hamartōlos (45V — the sinner as social-moral category, as object of Jesus's ministry, as constituted inner state), chat.ta (19V — habitual sinner as extreme moral character, including Sodom's men, Jeroboam's generational pattern).

### Characteristic 4 — Guilt as inner-being state

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The conscience's recognition of moral liability before God — the subjective inner experience of being guilty, of bearing a wrong that cannot be hidden, that weighs on the spirit, that demands response. The meanings in this register describe guilt as recognised (Gen 42:21 — brothers recognise their past cruelty as guilt in present distress; Gen 44:16 — guilt exposed by God; Lev 5:5 — realising guilt obligates confession), as a weight carried (Ezr 9:6 — guilt risen above the head; Psa 38:4/18 — iniquity as heavy burden on the will; Psa 51:5 — born into guilt; 1Sa 25:31 — conscience stumbling from guilt), as indelible before God (Jer 2:22 — no soap can remove it). The characteristic is the internal moral experience of guilt.

Key evidence: a.sham (31V — guilt-recognition process in conscience), a.shem (3V — guilty state), ash.mah (17V — guiltiness as inner burden/weight), a.von H5771H guilt sub-entry (27V — guilt as hidden, transferable, indelible burden), a.von H5771I punishment sub-entry (18V — guilt's punitive weight borne), pu.qah (1V — staggering of conscience from potential bloodguilt).

### Characteristic 5 — Iniquity as accumulated moral crime

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* Iniquity as the inner dimension of accumulated moral wrongdoing — crime in its moral-spiritual sense, not merely legal. The meanings evidence iniquity as a measured accumulation not yet full (Gen 15:16), as a generational inheritance (Exo 20:5 — visited on descendants), as a heart-seated reality (Eze 14:3/4/7 — idols taken into the heart), as a self-rotting force (Lev 26:39 — wasting away in iniquity in exile), as a burden requiring confession before restoration is possible (Lev 26:40/41). This is distinct from guilt (Char 4) in that guilt is the subjective recognition/experience, while iniquity-as-crime is the objective moral accumulation — the inner store of wrongdoing before God.

Key evidence: a.von H5771G crime sub-entry (162V — the full iniquity-as-crime corpus), al.vah H5932 (1V — iniquity at Gibeah as moral character of a community).

### Characteristic 6 — Transgression / rebellion as deliberate boundary-crossing

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The willful defiance of known moral or covenantal order — the inner act of the will that rebels, crosses a boundary it knows to exist, or deliberately breaches a covenant it is party to. The meanings consistently evidence this as *knowing* transgression: the nations' accumulated offenses (Amo 1–2 — transgressions that cross a threshold of divine tolerance), the people's deliberate departure (Isa 1:2 — children rebelling against a nurturing father), the will that multiplies transgressions (Isa 59:12/13 — rebellion as initiating act), Adam's paradigmatic violation of a direct command (Rom 5:14). The characteristic is the volitional dimension of conscious rebellion.

Key evidence: pe.sha H6588 (90V — the primary OT transgression corpus), pa.sha H6586 (37V — to transgress as rebellion-act), parabasis/parabatēs/parabainō (16V — NT law-crossing vocabulary), paraptōma (14V — trespass in its Pauline meaning, particularly the Adam-paradigm passages), sa.rah H5627 (8V — revolt/rebellion against God), apostasia G0646 (1V — apostasy as covenantal defection).

### Characteristic 7 — Faithlessness / treachery as wilful covenant-breaking sin

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The inner disposition of deliberate unfaithfulness — the will that breaks covenantal loyalty, betrays a trust, or acts treacherously against God or persons. This is distinct from generic sin-as-act (Char 1) and from transgression/rebellion (Char 6) in that it specifically names the *relational* dimension of sinful moral failure: the betrayal of a bond that was known and was owed. The meanings evidence treachery as a settled inner character (Isa 48:8 — constitutional unfaithfulness known to God before birth; Isa 24:16 — threefold emphasis on treachery as intensified inner character); as covenant-breaking toward God (Hos 6:7 — at Adam; Mal 2:15/16 — guard the spirit against faithlessness toward a wife); and as a community-wide moral disposition (Jer 9:2 — treachery so pervasive the prophet wishes to flee; 2Ch 36:14 — corporate unfaithfulness defiling the temple).

Key evidence: ba.gad H0898 (39V — to act treacherously; cross-register M13/M31), ma.al H4603 (34V — to be unfaithful; cross-register M13/M31), ma.al H4604 (28V — unfaithfulness; cross-register M13/M31).

### Characteristic 8 — Perversion / moral corruption as inner-being inversion

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The inner state or disposition of moral distortion — twisting what is right, corrupting what was good, inverting the moral order. The meanings evidence this as operating through speech (Pro 2:12/14 — twisted words; Pro 10:31/32 — perversity in the tongue; 1Cor 15:33 — corrupting good morals through bad company), through the mind's faculty (Pro 12:8 — twisted mind inviting contempt; 2Cor 11:3 — corrupting the mind's devotion to Christ; 1Ti 6:5 — mind depraved by corruption), through deliberate inner scheming (Pro 6:14 — perversity originates in the heart and drives continual evil-devising; Eph 4:22 — the old self corrupted through deceitful desires), and as a comprehensive inner moral degeneracy (Jer 2:21 — the vine turned wild from within; Eph 4:22; Jude 10 — destroyed from within by what is acted on instinctively). The characteristic is the *inversion* dimension: moral character that has been twisted away from what is right.

Key evidence: tah.pu.khah H8419 (10V — perversity as deep inner moral inversion), a.vah H5753B (10V — to pervert as deliberate moral distortion), a.vah H5753A (6V — to twist with M03 cross-register on anguish register), sa.laph H5557 (6V — perverting justice through bribery), ftheirō G5351 (7V — moral corruption active in the inner life), fthora G5356 (8V — corruption as inner bondage; bodily-decay cross-register M23/M35), diaftheirō G1311 (3V — depraved mind), sur H5494 (2V — degenerating from God), te.vel H8397 (2V — sexual perversion), la.zut H3891 (1V — perversity in speech), ho.phekh H2017 (1V — cognitive inversion), mut.teh H4297 (1V — structural injustice as moral distortion), cha.val H2254B (5V — moral corruption against God; M03 cross-register), nav.lut H5040 (1V — lewdness as shameful moral disposition; M07 cross-register).

### Characteristic 9 — Injustice as moral failure of right conduct

*What specific inner-being faculty or state does this evidence?* The inner state/disposition of doing what is wrong toward persons — unjust conduct, unjust character, the person whose moral faculty is bent toward wrongdoing against others. The meanings evidence injustice as arising from inner moral disposition (Psa 53:1 — fool's heart-denial of God produces unjust conduct; Psa 58:2 — devised in the heart before enacted by hands), as a fixed character trait (Luk 16:10 — unjust person is unjust across all scales, showing it as a stable inner disposition), as the opposite of righteousness (Deu 32:4 — God's character is without injustice; 1Pe 3:18 — the unjust for whom Christ died). M26 cross-register throughout: injustice is the structural opposite of righteousness, placing it in ongoing dialogue with M26. The M10 characteristic is the sinful inner moral-failure of justice-denial.

Key evidence: a.vel H5766A (20V — injustice in judicial, relational, characterological contexts), av.lah H5766B (32V — injustice as wilful wrongdoing arising from inner corruption), adikos G0094 (11V — unjust as stable inner moral disposition), av.val H5767 (5V — unjust one as moral character), a.vil H5760 (1V — ungodly lawless person), adikēma G0092 (3V — crime as moral-legal violation).

### M10-A — Sin as committed act

**subgroup_code:** M10-A  
**label:** Sin as committed act  
**Characteristic:** Characteristic 1 — Sin as committed act  
**core_description:** This sub-group carries the act-of-sinning characteristic: the will in the moment of moral failure, sinning as a relational act directed against God, another person, or oneself. The inner-being content is volitional — the conscience recognising a committed fault, the will that defected or chose wrongly, the act now requiring acknowledgment and accounting for. The corpus covers sinning as confession (the person who names what they have done), as deterrence (the inner person who must be restrained from sinning), as interpersonal wrong (sinning against a brother, a master, the Lord), and as habitual pattern of the will. Specialised verbal registers include: slander/blasphemy as sin-of-speech originating in the heart (G0988, G0989), enticement as the mechanism leading inner desire into sin (G1185), and hypocrisy as the sinful act of false-appearance (G4942). The characteristic is the active dimension — the moment and act of moral failure.

**Terms assigned as primary:** cha.ta H2398 (201V), hamartanō G0264 (37V), pa.sha H6586 (partial — rebellion-against-God verses; see §3 multi-faceted terms), blasfēmia G0988 (18V), pa.sha H6586 (37V, fully), blasfēmos G0989 (4V), deleazō G1185 (3V), sunupokrinomai G4942 (1V), nav.lut H5040 (1V), apostasia G0646 (absorbed — defection-as-act register; see §3)

### M10-B — Sin as moral condition

**subgroup_code:** M10-B  
**label:** Sin as moral condition / state  
**Characteristic:** Characteristic 2 — Sin as moral condition / state  
**core_description:** This sub-group carries the condition-of-sin characteristic: sin as an objective moral reality that the inner person carries — not primarily the act of doing wrong, but the state of being under sin's dominion, burdened by it, enslaved by it, needing it removed or covered. The inner-being content is the person's moral ontology — what they are under sin rather than what they did. The corpus covers sin as accumulated moral weight recorded against the person, as enslaving force mastering the will (Joh 8:34), as universal human condition into which all are born (Rom 5:12/19), as death-state from which God must raise (Eph 2:1/5), and as reality that must be atoned for or covered before the person can stand before God. The atonement/sin-offering terms (kip.pu.rim, chat.tat H2403H) are placed here because their meanings evidence sin-as-condition requiring covering — the problem that atonement addresses. Wickedness-as-inner-condition (rish.ah) also belongs here as the state-dimension of sinful moral character.

**Terms assigned as primary:** hamartia G0266 (105V), chat.tat H2403B (159V), paraptōma G3900 (14V), chat.ta.ah H2401 (8V), chat.ta.ah H2403A (2V), hamartēma G0265 (4V), kip.pu.rim H3725 (8V; M11 cross-register flag travels with), chat.tat H2403H sin-offering (4V; M11 cross-register flag travels with), rish.ah H7564 (4V), al.vah H5932 (1V — iniquity-as-condition register, 1v only)

### M10-C — The sinner as moral character

**subgroup_code:** M10-C  
**label:** The sinner as moral character  
**Characteristic:** Characteristic 3 — The sinner as moral character  
**core_description:** This sub-group carries the identity-of-sin characteristic: the constitutive inner moral identity of the person described as a sinner — not what they did (Char 1) or the condition they are in (Char 2), but what kind of person they are at their moral core. The corpus covers the sinner as social-moral category (Mat 9:10/11), as person owning their sinful identity (Luk 5:8), as those constituted sinners (Rom 5:8/19), and as habitual extreme sinners whose moral character is total (Gen 13:13 — Sodom; 1Sa 15:18 — the Amalekites; Amo 9:8 — the sinful kingdom).

**Characteristic:** Characteristic 4 — Guilt as inner-being state

## §3. Multi-faceted terms

Terms whose verses span more than one sub-group (primary + secondary assignment):

### pa.sha H6586 — to transgress (37V)
- **Primary:** M10-F (transgression/rebellion) — the majority of verses evidence deliberate rebellion against God or covenantal authority (Isa 1:2; Jer 2:8/29; Eze 2:3; Psa 37:38/51:13; Isa 43:27/48:8)
- **Secondary:** M10-A (sin-as-act) — approximately 10 verses use the term for political revolt sense (2Ki 1:1; 8:20/22; 2Ch 21:8/10; 2Ch 10:19; 1Ki 12:19; 2Ki 3:5/7) where the inner-being register is the wilful-defection-as-sinful-act rather than the rebellion-against-God register
- **Split rule:** primary M10-F; secondary M10-A for the Moab/Edom/political-revolt verses

### paraptōma G3900 — trespass (14V)
- **Primary:** M10-B (sin-as-condition) — the majority of verses evidence trespass in its condition/state register (Eph 2:1/5; Col 2:13 — dead in trespasses; 2Cor 5:19 — trespasses creating alienation; Gal 6:1 — caught in a trespass)
- **Secondary:** M10-F (transgression/rebellion) — the Adam-paradigm verses (Rom 5:15/16/17/18/20) evidence trespass as the foundational wilful boundary-crossing that set the pattern
- **Split rule:** Adam-paradigm verses (Rom 5:15–20) → M10-F primary; remaining (Rom 4:25; Rom 11:11/12; 2Cor 5:19; Gal 6:1; Eph 1:7; Eph 2:1/5; Col 2:13) → M10-B primary

### apostasia G0646 — apostasy (1V)
- **Primary:** M10-A (sin-as-act) — the single verse Act 21:21 (apostasy as accusation of teaching Jews to forsake covenant identity) evidences a sinful-act register more than a rebellion/boundary-crossing register
- No secondary; single verse fully to M10-A

### pu.qah H6330 — staggering (1V)
- **Primary:** M10-D (guilt-as-inner-state) — conscience stumbling from guilt; M03 cross-register flag travels with
- No secondary; single verse fully to M10-D
