## Context — why this revision is happening

The original Phase 5 design grouped M10-A as "Sin as committed act" (274V) and M10-B as "Sin as moral condition" (339V) — each presented as ONE characteristic. The 2026-05-23 keyword discovery pass revealed that these two sub-groups in fact contain **multiple distinct inner-being faculties** that were smoothed over at gloss level. The other 8 sub-groups (especially M10-F, which served as control) survived the keyword check; only M10-A and M10-B need revision.

**What the keyword analytics shows (sample faculty axes likely present):**

In M10-A (sin as committed act):
- Refusal-to-repent (`turning withheld`, `departure refused`, `sin persisting`)
- Conscience operations (`conscience naming`, `clear`, `suppressed`, `surfacing`)
- Will operations (`will refusing`, `corrupted`, `fixed`, `defiant`, `restrained`)
- Unintentional sin (`violation inadvertent`, `transgression objective`, `ignorance`)
- Confession dimensions (`verbal`, `communal`, `corporate`, `hollow`, `personal`)
- Habitual / pattern defection (`defection habitual`, `sin persisting`)
- Wilful defection (`defection wilful`)
- Political / relational rebellion (`authority rejected`, `defection political`, `submission broken`, `loyalty broken`)
- Atonement required by act
- Communal / corporate sin

In M10-B (sin as moral condition):
- Forgiveness sought / received / withheld
- Atonement (priestly mediation)
- Guilt removal / burden lifted
- Sin recorded / registered before God
- Sin accumulating
- Sin inherited / generational
- Sin as enduring condition / state
- Will clinging / refusing to release
- Spiritual death
- Wilful refusal to repent
- Conscience awakening / clearing
- Communal / corporate guilt
- Repentance / turning

(These are *candidates from the keyword evidence*, not a prescribed taxonomy. Derive the final characteristic list from the verses + their keywords. Combine/split as the evidence warrants. The same faculty may appear in both M10-A and M10-B with different complexion — that's fine; they can split or merge.)

## Your task

For M10-A (274V) and M10-B (339V) only, produce a revised characteristic decomposition. Each new characteristic becomes a new sub-group. Every existing vc_id in these two sub-groups must route to one of the new sub-groups.

**§8.0 principle (non-negotiable):** every sub-group represents ONE characteristic. If you find yourself writing a description with "this sub-group carries X **and** Y **and** Z", you've conflated faculties — split into separate sub-groups. The 274 + 339 verses likely produce 8–14 new sub-groups across M10-A and M10-B combined; the analytics suggests this scale.

### Process

1. **Read the keyword analytics report (§§2, 3, 4) FIRST** — it surfaces the faculty axes present in the cluster. The token families in §3 and co-occurrence pairs in §4 are evidence of which axes operate together vs separately.

2. **Read the per-verse keywords + meanings for M10-A** (input #4 first, then input #3 §M10-A). Group verses by inner-being faculty as the evidence accumulates — the keyword tags make this concrete. A faculty must be evidenced by multiple verses with coherent keyword overlap.

3. **Name each candidate characteristic** in one short label (e.g. "Refusal to repent", "Conscience strike on recall", "Unintentional sin"). For each: a 1–2 sentence definition naming the inner-being operation, a 3–5 word distinguishing feature versus its nearest neighbour, the keyword family it draws on, ~5 anchor verse references.

4. **Repeat for M10-B**. Where M10-B's evidence matches an M10-A characteristic, MERGE — produce a single revised sub-group spanning both pools. Where it doesn't, name new.

5. **Map every vc_id** in M10-A's 274 and M10-B's 339 to one of the new sub-groups (term-level rules with per-verse exceptions; the existing Phase 6 resolver handles this format).
