M10's term universe is stable: **63 terms** (0 transferred at Phase 4 — 57 STAYS + 6 BOUNDARY). The cluster is a post-split residual (the 2026-05-22 split carved off M10b wickedness-as-character and M10c defilement), so the term-set is unusually coherent for a cluster of this size.

**Substantive verse corpus:** **1,325 verse_context rows are_relevant** across the 63 terms. Pass A meanings on all 1,325 (Phase 2, 2026-05-22; one row added at Phase 3 from Mic 2:10 cha.val borderline resolution).

**Scale comparison vs M09:** ~12× the verse volume (1,325 vs 109), ~4× the terms (63 vs 17). The 40% distribution gate at this scale = **>530 verses per sub-group** — a generous ceiling, but several individual terms exceed half of it on their own (cha.ta 201V; chat.tat H2403B 159V; a.von H5771G 162V; hamartia 105V; pe.sha 90V). Expect more sub-groups than M09 had, and probable volume-splits on the larger characteristics.

Pass A meanings (Phase 2) are the **only analytical material** for sub-group design. Inherited VCGs, prior sub-group structure, anchors, and prior findings are explicitly suppressed (per v2_8 §2.3) so sub-groups emerge from the evidence-meanings, not from prior structure.

## THE PRIMARY OBJECTIVE — sub-groups represent characteristics (§8.0)

**This is the structural objective.** Read it carefully — it shapes every step that follows.

1. **A sub-group represents a characteristic** — a single inner-being faculty/state. The default is **1 sub-group : 1 characteristic**.
2. **Volume-split is the documented exception.** When a single characteristic's verse corpus would exceed the §8.6 distribution gate (40% of substantive verses = **>530 verses** for M10), split it into multiple sub-groups by a documented split-axis. The characteristic identity persists across the splits.
3. **A sub-group serving two characteristics (SPLIT_SUBGROUP) is rare** — only when the same lexical sub-group contains different VCG registers serving different characteristics. Flag any such case at Phase 5.
4. **Cross-register flags from Phase 3 inform characteristic identification.** A term carrying a cross-register flag may suggest a characteristic that the cluster's primary register doesn't fully cover — but the term still represents M10's characteristic in M10's sub-group structure.

## Process

### Step 1 — Identify the characteristics

Read across the meaning corpora (§2 of the constitution report) and name the distinct **inner-being faculties / states** M10's evidence expresses. Aim for an analytically clean list — for a cluster of M10's scale (1,325 verses, 63 terms), **typically 6–10 characteristics** is realistic.

A characteristic is a single answer to: *"what specific inner-being faculty or state does this body of verses evidence?"* For M10, candidate characteristics suggested by the Phase 3 patterns (this is suggestive, not prescriptive — derive your list from the meanings):

- **Sin as committed act** (the act of sinning, doing wrong, missing the mark) — primarily verb-form vocabulary (hamartanō, cha.ta)
- **Sin as state / condition** (sin as objective state of moral debt or failure carried by the person) — primarily noun-form vocabulary (hamartia, chat.tat, chet)
- **Sinner as moral character** (the person whose constitutive moral identity is sin) — hamartōlos, chat.ta
- **Guilt as inner state** (the conscience's recognition of wrongdoing, the state of being guilty before God) — a.sham, a.shem, ash.mah, the guilt-sub-entry of a.von
- **Iniquity as moral twist / accumulated weight** — a.von's crime + punishment sub-entries, the iniquity terms
- **Transgression as boundary-crossing / rebellion** — pe.sha, pa.sha, parabasis-family
- **Perversion as moral inversion** — a.vah, sa.laph, te.vel, the perversity family
- **Injustice as moral failure of justice** — a.vel, av.lah, adikos, adikēma, av.val (M26 cross-register)
- **Unfaithfulness as covenant-breaking sin** — ba.gad, ma.al verb/noun (M13/M31 cross-register)
- **Slander/blasphemy as sin of speech** — blasfēmia, blasfēmos (M06 cross-register)
- **Atonement / sin-response** — kip.pu.rim, chat.tat-sin-offering (M11 cross-register; possibly a dedicated supportive characteristic, possibly merged into a sin-and-its-remedy reading)
- **Corruption / decay produced by sin** — diaftheirō, ftheirō, fthora, sur
- **Wickedness-as-state** — rish.ah (note: most wickedness-as-character vocabulary moved to M10b; rish.ah remained in M10)

Refine, combine, or split these from the actual meaning evidence. The cross-register flags are reminders that these terms operate in two registers; their primary representation in M10 is still the M10 characteristic.

### Step 2 — Map each characteristic to its evidence

For each characteristic, note which **terms' verses primarily evidence it**. Some terms will be primary carriers; others will be secondary (their corpus splits across characteristics, or they're flagged cross-register).

### Step 3 — Design sub-groups to carry the characteristics

- **Default**: one sub-group per characteristic. Use natural codes (`M10-A`, `M10-B`, …, plus `M10-BND` or similar for the BOUNDARY sub-group).
- **Volume-split**: if a characteristic's verse evidence would exceed **>530 verses** (40% of 1,325), split that characteristic into multiple sub-groups by a named split-axis. The sin-family characteristic almost certainly needs this; the guilt-iniquity characteristic may need it.
- Each sub-group's `core_description` explicitly names: (a) the characteristic it represents, (b) the split-axis if it's part of a volume-split, (c) the inner-being content from the meanings.

**Note on M10's distribution:** with ~1,300 substantive verses and many large-corpus terms, expect the design to settle around 8–12 sub-groups (6–8 characteristics, of which 1–2 are volume-split).

### Step 4 — Assign every verse to exactly one sub-group

For each of the 1,325 Pass A meanings, record the sub-group assignment. Mapping form: term-level with per-term split rules where needed (matches M07/M08/M09 pattern). Output as JSON for CC's Phase 6 apply.

**Practical note on volume:** explicit verse-by-verse mapping for 1,325 verses in chat is infeasible. Design at the **term level** with per-term split rules (e.g., "all hamartanō verses → M10-A1 except 1Jo 3:6/8/9 → M10-A2 (sin-as-state register)"). CC will resolve to flat vc_id assignments at apply time.

### Step 5 — BOUNDARY sub-group

Create one BOUNDARY sub-group carrying the 5 verses from H0205H a.ven (4) + H2256D che.vel (1). The 4 empty-corpus BOUNDARY terms attach as members without verse contributions. Phase 8.5 resolves all of these.

### Step 6 — Flag SPLIT_SUBGROUP cases

If any sub-group on closer inspection contains VCG-level registers serving different characteristics (rare), flag it in the output with the analytical question.

### Step 7 — Multi-faceted terms

Some terms' verses may span multiple sub-groups (a.von's crime/guilt/punishment sub-entries are already split lexically; cha.ta may span sin-act and rebellion). Record primary + secondary assignments per term in the mapping.

## Discipline

1. **Read every verse-meaning** before designing sub-groups — at M10's scale, this means working through §2 of the constitution report term by term, sometimes scanning rather than reading every meaning. But don't skip large terms (cha.ta 201V, chat.tat 159V, a.von 162V, hamartia 105V): characteristic identification depends on what those corpora actually evidence.
2. **Characteristic-first design** (§8.0). Don't cluster by meaning-similarity until you've named the characteristics the cluster expresses.
3. **The 40% distribution gate is a volume-split trigger, not a fail signal.** If a characteristic carries >530 of M10's 1,325 substantive verses, split it into multiple sub-groups with a named axis.
4. **Cross-register flags travel forward.** When a flagged term lands in a sub-group, the flag is preserved in the rationale for Phase 7/9 consumption. Cross-register flags do NOT define sub-group boundaries — they note that the term participates in another cluster's analytical conversation too.
5. **BOUNDARY parking is permitted only for the 6 BOUNDARY-verdict terms.** Per §8.4.1, the BOUNDARY sub-group must contain only verses of BOUNDARY-verdict terms.
6. **Mic 2:10 cha.val** (vr_id=232182, the Phase 1 borderline now flipped to relevant) is in scope as part of cha.val's 5-verse corpus and routes to whichever sub-group cha.val's primary register lands in. **Pro 12:21 a.ven** (Phase 1 borderline, parked) is NOT in the corpus — it has no vc row.
