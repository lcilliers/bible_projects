## T6.1 — Co-occurrence

### T6.1.1

**Question:** Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?

**[CHAR-1] Wilful sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-1]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside wilful sinning in the corpus. (1) *CHAR-3 (Confession)*: the confession register is directly linked to wilful sinning across the corpus — 2Sa 12:13 ("I have sinned against the Lord" — confession immediately following sinning), Jos 7:20 (Achan's confession: "Truly I have sinned against the Lord God of Israel"), 1Sa 15:24 (Saul's confession: "I have sinned"). Confession appears as the expected immediate post-act response. (2) *CHAR-17 (Guilt as inner-being state)*: the Levitical corpus (VCG-04: Lev 4:22 — "realizes his guilt"; Lev 4:23 — "the sin which he has committed is made known to him") shows guilt-realisation occurring within the wilful-sinning context. (3) *CHAR-5 (Refusal to repent)*: Hos 4:7/13:2 show the negative trajectory from sinning to intensified sinning without turning — the absence of repentance is the adjacent characteristic at the negative end.

**[CHAR-2] Unintentional sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-2]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear alongside CHAR-2 in the corpus. (1) *CHAR-3 (Confession)*: Lev 5:5 ("when he realizes his guilt... and confesses the sin he has committed") — confession immediately follows realisation of inadvertent sin. Num 22:34 ("I have sinned") — Balaam's confession follows disclosure of inadvertent sinning. Confession is the prescribed adjacent characteristic activated by the realisation event. (2) *CHAR-14 (Forgiveness sought and received)*: Num 15:28 ("he shall be forgiven"), Lev 19:22 ("he shall be forgiven") — every prescribed remedy for unintentional sinning ends with forgiveness declared. Forgiveness-received is the terminal adjacent characteristic in the CHAR-2 arc. (3) *CHAR-17 (Guilt as inner-being state)*: Lev 5:5 ("when he realizes his guilt") — guilt-realisation is the post-disclosure inner-being state that characterises the transition into the remedy sequence.

**[CHAR-3] Confession** *(E)*

**[CHAR-3]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear alongside confession consistently. (1) *CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning)* — as the prior act that confession names: every individual confession in VCG-01 names a specific committed sin (2Sa 24:10 — the census; Mat 27:4 — the betrayal; Luk 15:21 — the far-country life). Confession follows sinning as its moral completion. (2) *CHAR-17 (Guilt as inner-being state)* — as the inner condition that precedes and grounds confession: 2Sa 24:10 — "David's heart struck him" names the guilt-state that drives the confession; 2Sa 19:20 — "your servant knows that I have sinned" names inner knowledge-of-guilt as the basis. (3) *CHAR-14 (Forgiveness sought and received)* — as the act that confession opens the door to: Eze 33:16 (sins not remembered after turning), Num 15:28 (adjacent corpus — forgiveness following the atonement the confession drives), 2Sa 24:10 — "please take away the iniquity of your servant" — the plea for forgiveness follows immediately.

**[CHAR-4] Conscience suppression** *(E)*

**[CHAR-4]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear alongside conscience suppression in the corpus. (1) *CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning)* — as the prior act that is not acknowledged: Jer 2:35 — the person has sinned (CHAR-1), and the conscience suppression is the refusal to acknowledge the sin; Ecc 8:12 — "does evil a hundred times" (CHAR-1 repeated) while conscience is absent (CHAR-4). (2) *CHAR-6 (Habitual defection)* — as the pattern that sustains conscience suppression: Ecc 8:12 — habitual sinning without conscience-registration is simultaneously habitual defection and conscience suppression. (3) *CHAR-3 (Confession)* — as the absent opposite: Jer 2:35 — "I have not sinned" is the explicit inversion of the confessional "I have sinned." The corpus defines CHAR-4 in large part by the absence of CHAR-3.

**[CHAR-5] Refusal to repent** *(E)*

**[CHAR-5]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear consistently. (1) *CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning)* — as the ongoing sinning that the refusal to repent sustains: Heb 10:26 — "go on sinning deliberately" — the deliberate ongoing sinning is both CHAR-1 (the sinning act) and CHAR-5 (the refusal to stop). (2) *CHAR-6 (Habitual defection)* — as the pattern the refusal to repent produces: the Kings corpus's "walked in them" (2Ki 13:6) and "all his days" (2Ki 15:18) describe the habitual defection that sustained refusal establishes. (3) *CHAR-7 (Contagious sin)* — as the communal transmission the refusal enables: "which he made Israel to sin" — the leader's refusal to repent is the mechanism through which the sinful course spreads to the community.

**[CHAR-6] Habitual defection** *(E)*

**[CHAR-6]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear alongside habitual defection in the corpus. (1) *CHAR-5 (Refusal to repent)* — as the volitional sustainer of habitual defection: 1Sa 2:25 — "they would not listen" and Exo 9:34 — "sinned yet again and hardened his heart" both evidence the volitional refusal-to-turn that sustains the habitual pattern. (2) *CHAR-4 (Conscience suppression)* — as the conscience-numbing that enables the pattern: Exo 9:34's progressive hardening operates through the conscience being progressively suppressed (each hardening reduces the conscience's signal strength); 1Sa 2:25's non-listening evidences the same numbing. (3) *CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning)* — as the repeated act that constitutes the habitual pattern: habitual defection is constituted by the accumulation of wilful sinning acts (Ecc 7:20 — "does good and never sins" — the universal sinning confirms all have the underlying acts; Hos 10:9 — "you have sinned... there they have continued").

**[CHAR-7] Contagious sin** *(E)*

**[CHAR-7]** E — Three adjacent characteristics appear consistently alongside CHAR-7 in the corpus. (2) *CHAR-6 (Habitual defection)* — as what the community's moral orientation becomes through sustained contagious sin reception: 1Ki 15:34 — "walking in the sin of Jeroboam which he made Israel to sin" — the community walks in the transmitted sin (habitual defection) as the result of the contagious sin reception. ### T6.1.2

**Question:** What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?

**[CHAR-1] Wilful sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-1]** E — The co-occurrence pattern reveals that wilful sinning is a *pivot characteristic* in the inner-being landscape: it sits between temptation (pre-act) and consequence (post-act) and is immediately flanked by conscience/guilt (CHAR-17) on the post-act side, confession (CHAR-3) as the prescribed response, and refusal to repent (CHAR-5) as the negative alternative. The characteristic does not stand alone but is constitutively connected to the entire arc of moral failure and its resolution. Its place in the landscape is as the *act-event* that triggers the entire downstream sequence.

**[CHAR-2] Unintentional sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-2]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places CHAR-2 as a *trigger characteristic* in a prescribed sequence: inadvertent sin → (realisation) → guilt (CHAR-17) → confession (CHAR-3) → remedy → forgiveness (CHAR-14). CHAR-2 is the event that initiates this entire downstream arc. Its place in the landscape is as the *inadvertent opening* of a moral sequence that the covenant system has specifically provided for. Unlike CHAR-1 (which triggers guilt, conscience, and potentially refusal to repent), CHAR-2's prescribed trajectory is uniformly toward remedy — the system is built precisely for this characteristic.

**[CHAR-3] Confession** *(E)*

**[CHAR-3]** E — The co-occurrence pattern establishes confession as the *central hinge* of the sinning-to-forgiveness arc: it receives CHAR-1 (the sin committed) and CHAR-17 (the guilt-state experienced) as its inputs, and it produces the opening for CHAR-14 (forgiveness) as its output. Confession sits between moral failure and moral restoration — it is neither the sin (CHAR-1) nor the forgiveness (CHAR-14) but the named acknowledgment that connects the two. Its place in the inner-being landscape is as the *pivot characteristic*: the moral episode cannot move from failure to restoration without passing through the naming event that confession is.

**[CHAR-4] Conscience suppression** *(E)*

**[CHAR-4]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places conscience suppression as the *failure-mode alternative* to the confession-to-forgiveness arc. Where genuine conscience engagement produces guilt (CHAR-17) → confession (CHAR-3) → forgiveness (CHAR-14), conscience suppression short-circuits the arc at its opening: the conscience does not fire (or is denied), so guilt is not experienced, confession does not occur, and forgiveness is not sought. CHAR-4 thus occupies the *alternative trajectory* in the inner-being landscape — not a step in the arc but a deviation from it at the very first step (conscience-recognition).

**[CHAR-5] Refusal to repent** *(E)*

**[CHAR-5]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places refusal to repent as a *sustaining characteristic*: it sustains CHAR-1 (wilful sinning) by preventing the turn that would end the sinning, sustains CHAR-6 (habitual defection) by providing the volitional commitment that makes habituation possible, and enables CHAR-7 (contagious sin) by modelling non-departure for the community. CHAR-5 is thus the inner-being mechanism that keeps multiple other characteristics active: it is the will-act that prevents the turning which would resolve them.

**[CHAR-6] Habitual defection** *(E)*

**[CHAR-6]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places habitual defection as the *dispositional endpoint* of the sinning characteristics: it is what CHAR-1 (wilful sinning) becomes when sustained without CHAR-3 (confession) or CHAR-4 (conscience suppression) taking hold and without CHAR-5 (refusal to repent) being resolved. CHAR-6 is the settled formational state that the other sinning characteristics produce when they run over time. Its place in the landscape is as the *most deeply formed* of the sinning characteristics — not the most acute (CHAR-5's volitional refusal is more actively resistant) but the most settled (the will's habituated direction toward sin as its constitutional default).

**[CHAR-7] Contagious sin** *(E)*

**[CHAR-7]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places contagious sin as the *scale-amplifier* of the sinning cluster: it takes the personal sinning (CHAR-1), the volitional refusal (CHAR-5), and the habitual pattern (CHAR-6) and amplifies them from the individual to the communal level through the authority mechanism. CHAR-7 does not introduce new sinning content but multiplies the reach of existing sinning content through the leader-community authority structure. Its place in the landscape is as the characteristic that explains the communal scale of sinning in the Kings narrative: why are whole nations drawn into sinning? Because the leader's CHAR-1/5/6 operates as CHAR-7 through the authority relationship.

**[CHAR-8] Political revolt** *(E)*

**[CHAR-8]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places political revolt as the *political-register expression* of the broader transgression (CHAR-19) and faithlessness (CHAR-20) dynamics. CHAR-8 occupies a distinctive niche: it is the characteristic that specifically names the will's transgressive boundary-breaking in the political-governing domain. Its place in the landscape is as the political-social manifestation of the same inner breaking-away that CHAR-19 and CHAR-20 name in the personal-moral and covenantal domains. The three characteristics share the breaking-away inner dynamic but operate in different registers: personal-moral (CHAR-19), covenantal-relational (CHAR-20), and political-social (CHAR-8).

**[CHAR-9] Sinful speech** *(E)*

**[CHAR-9]** E — The co-occurrence pattern reveals CHAR-9's unique position as the *speech-externalisation characteristic*: it occupies the point where inner characteristics (heart corruption — CHAR-4; wilful sinning — CHAR-1; contempt — M06) cross into their verbal expression. Sinful speech is what the inner conditions of contempt, conscience suppression, and wilful moral failure look like when they reach the mouth. Its place in the landscape is as the *outer face* of several inner characteristics — the speech-act completion of what the inner faculties have already determined.

**[CHAR-10] Specialised sinful mechanisms** *(E)*

**[CHAR-10]** E — The co-occurrence pattern places CHAR-10 as the characteristic of *sinning's pathways* — the mechanisms through which other sinning characteristics are reached or produced. CHAR-10 does not replace CHAR-1/6/7 but names the specific routes through which those characteristics are entered: enticement as the route into CHAR-1 (the sinful act), hypocrisy as the route into a CHAR-7-like spread, and sustained enticement as the route into CHAR-6 (the formed habitual character). CHAR-10 thus occupies a distinctive meta-level position: it characterises the *entry mechanisms* into other sinning characteristics.

**[CHAR-11] Sin as universal condition** *(E)*

**[CHAR-11]** E — The co-occurrence pattern reveals CHAR-11 as the *foundational ground-condition* of the M10 landscape: it is the structural reality upon which all other sinning characteristics rest. Every other sinning characteristic (CHAR-1 through CHAR-10, CHAR-13 through CHAR-22) is either a specific expression of CHAR-11 (specific acts, patterns, or mechanisms through which the universal condition manifests), a consequence of CHAR-11 (guilt — CHAR-17; iniquity as accumulated — CHAR-18), or its antidote (confession — CHAR-3; forgiveness sought — CHAR-14). CHAR-11 is not one characteristic among peers but the bedrock condition that gives all other M10 characteristics their moral weight and significance.

**[CHAR-12] Sin as enslaving power** *(E)*

**[CHAR-12]** E — The co-occurrence pattern reveals CHAR-12 as the *power-mechanism characteristic* within the M10 cluster: it names the active governing force that operates through CHAR-6 (habitual practice is how the enslaving power installs itself), within CHAR-11 (the universal condition is what the enslaving power operates within), using CHAR-4 (conscience suppression is the pathway of entry), and producing effects visible in community contexts (CHAR-7 patterns). CHAR-12 is not isolated but is the power-dimension that connects and underlies multiple other M10 characteristics.

Num 15:27/28 — CHAR-2 (inadvertent sinning) → offering brought (remedy) → forgiveness declared (CHAR-14). ### T6.2.2

**Question:** What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?

**[CHAR-1] Wilful sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-1]** E — The relationship is primarily *causal*: wilful sinning causes the conditions that follow. It causes guilt-liability (Eze 18:4 — sinning causes death; Lev 4:14 — sinning triggers the atonement requirement). It causes relational rupture that must be addressed through confession and forgiveness. It causes consequence (1Ki 8:33/35 — national disaster following sinning). The developmental relationship is secondary: sustained sinning without remedy develops into habitual defection (CHAR-6) and refusal to repent (CHAR-5). The sequence from wilful sinning to these states is developmental (requiring repetition and time) rather than immediate and causal.

**[CHAR-2] Unintentional sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-2]** E — The relationship is *causal*: CHAR-2 causes the moral liability (objective guilt condition) that drives the downstream sequence. The liability created by inadvertent sinning is the direct cause of the obligation to bring the offering (Num 15:27 — "he shall offer"), which causes the priestly atonement (Num 15:28 — "the priest shall make atonement"), which causes the forgiveness (Num 15:28 — "he shall be forgiven"). Each link in the chain is causally driven by the preceding one, with CHAR-2 as the originating cause. The developmental relationship (CHAR-2 developing into CHAR-6 or CHAR-5 through habit) is not evidenced in this corpus — the trajectory evidenced is always toward remedy, not toward hardening.

**[CHAR-3] Confession** *(E)*

**[CHAR-3]** E — The relationship is *causal* in both directions: sin (CHAR-1) causes the need for confession (CHAR-3); confession (CHAR-3) causes the condition in which forgiveness (CHAR-14) becomes possible. Sin creates the moral fact; confession names it; forgiveness resolves it. The sequence is also *developmental* in the Judges pattern (VCG-02): Judg 10:10/15; 1Sa 7:6; 12:10 show confession as part of a recurring cycle (sin → oppression → confession → deliverance → apostasy → repeat). Each iteration of the cycle is causal (confession causes deliverance); the pattern over time is developmental (the community is shaped by repeated cycles of confession and restoration).

**[CHAR-4] Conscience suppression** *(E)*

**[CHAR-4]** E — The relationship is *mutually causal and developmental*. Sinning (CHAR-1) without conscience-registration (CHAR-4) enables more sinning, which deepens conscience suppression, which enables more sinning — a feedback loop that is both causal (each causes the other) and developmental (the loop deepens the condition over time). The judgment sequence (Jer 2:35) is causal: conscience suppression causes the accumulation of liability that causes the announced judgment. The developmental dimension is primary: the corpus's portrait of conscience suppression is a condition that grows with time (Ecc 8:12 — "a hundred times"; Tit 3:11 — "warped" as a completed deformation).

**[CHAR-5] Refusal to repent** *(E)*

**[CHAR-5]** E — The relationship is primarily *developmental and causal*. Developmental: refusal to repent develops from initial non-departure into active clinging into constitutional hardening (T5.2). Causal: refusal to repent *causes* the habitual sinning (CHAR-6) that follows — the will's persistent non-departure is the causal mechanism through which wilful sinning (CHAR-1) becomes habitual defection (CHAR-6). The three characteristics in sequence (CHAR-1 → CHAR-5 → CHAR-6) form a causal-developmental chain: the sin initiates, the refusal to repent sustains, the habituation establishes.

**[CHAR-6] Habitual defection** *(E)*

**[CHAR-6]** E — The relationship is *developmental and causal*. Developmental: CHAR-1 (acts) + CHAR-5 (sustained non-departure) → CHAR-6 (formed dispositional pattern) → judgment. Each stage develops from the prior through an accumulation process. Causal: CHAR-6 causes the judgment consequences (Pro 11:31; Pro 13:22; Heb 3:17) and the epistemic blindness (1Jo 3:6). The developmental-causal chain runs: episodic sinning → sustained non-departure → formed dispositional pattern → institutional embedding → generational transmission → judgment. ## T6.5 — Distinctions

### T6.5.1

The distinction is at the point of inner awareness: CHAR-1 involves the will acting against known moral reality; CHAR-2 involves the will acting without that awareness, so that conscience engages only after the fact.

**[CHAR-2] Unintentional sinning** *(E)*

**[CHAR-2]** E — CHAR-1 (Wilful sinning) most closely resembles CHAR-2 — same primary term (cha.ta), partially overlapping verse corpus (Lev 4/5), same remedial system. **[CHAR-18] Iniquity as accumulated moral crime** *(E)*

**[CHAR-20] Faithlessness as covenant-breaking sin** *(E)*

**[CHAR-20]** E — The distinction is one of *relational depth and prior-bond requirement*: CHAR-19 is the boundary-crossing category (can operate without prior intimate bond — the Amos nations); CHAR-20 is the covenant-betrayal category (constitutively requires a prior covenant relationship). **[CHAR-21] Perversion as inner inversion** *(E)*

**[CHAR-22] Injustice as moral failure of right conduct** *(E)*

**[CHAR-22]** E — The distinction is one of *direction*: CHAR-21 is primarily *inward* (the twisted inner constitution); CHAR-22 is primarily *other-directed* (the unjust conduct toward persons).
