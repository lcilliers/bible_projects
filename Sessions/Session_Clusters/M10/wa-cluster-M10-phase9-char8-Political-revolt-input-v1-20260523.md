## §3 — Verses in scope (per sub-group, canonical Bible order)

### M10-Q — Political revolt

#### vc=35591 — 1Ki 12:19 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… So Israel has been in_
> **1Ki 12:19 So Israel has been in rebellion against the house of David to this day .**
> _against the house of David …_

**Pass A meaning:** Israel's ongoing rebellion against the house of David is stated as a persistent political and spiritual reality 'to this day,' showing transgression as a continuing relational rupture with its roots in a decisive break of loyalty and covenant.

#### vc=35592 — 2Ki 1:1 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… death of Ahab , Moab_
> **2Ki 1:1 After the death of Ahab , Moab rebelled against Israel .**
> _against Israel . …_

**Pass A meaning:** Moab's rebellion against Israel after Ahab's death is the political-military expression of transgression—a breaking away from a covenant or vassal relationship that constitutes an act of revolt.

#### vc=35593 — 2Ki 3:5 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… , the king of Moab_
> **2Ki 3:5 But when Ahab died , the king of Moab rebelled against the king of Israel .**
> _against the king of Israel …_

**Pass A meaning:** Moab's rebellion upon Ahab's death is a concrete political defection, a breaking of submission that constitutes transgression against the ruling authority.

#### vc=35594 — 2Ki 3:7 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… “The king of Moab has_
> **2Ki 3:7 And he went and sent word to Jehoshaphat king of Judah : “The king of Moab has rebelled against me. Will you go with me to battle against Moab ?” And he said , “I will go . I am as you are, my people as your people , my horses as your horses .”**
> _against me. Will you go …_

**Pass A meaning:** Moab's rebellion is cited as the reason Jehoshaphat is summoned to join battle, presenting transgression as a deliberate act of revolt against authority that demands a military response.

#### vc=35595 — 2Ki 8:20 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… 8:20 In his days Edom_
> **2Ki 8:20 In his days Edom revolted from the rule of Judah and set up a king of their own .**
> _from the rule of Judah …_

**Pass A meaning:** Edom's revolt from Judah's rule is a concrete act of breaking political submission, presenting transgression as a defection in which a subordinate people asserts independence against an authority.

#### vc=35596 — 2Ki 8:22 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… 2Ki 8:22 So Edom_
> **2Ki 8:22 So Edom revolted from the rule of Judah to this day . Then Libnah revolted at the same time .**
> _from the rule of Judah …_

**Pass A meaning:** Both Edom and Libnah revolted from Judah at the same time, showing transgression as an act of deliberate break with established authority, here resulting in permanent separation.

#### vc=35597 — 2Ch 10:19 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… So Israel has been in_
> **2Ch 10:19 So Israel has been in rebellion against the house of David to this day .**
> _against the house of David …_

**Pass A meaning:** Transgression here is Israel's ongoing rebellion against the Davidic house, described as a political and covenantal defection that has persisted to the narrator's present day, rooted in a decisive act of broken loyalty.

#### vc=35598 — 2Ch 21:8 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… 21:8 In his days Edom_
> **2Ch 21:8 In his days Edom revolted from the rule of Judah and set up a king of their own .**
> _from the rule of Judah …_

**Pass A meaning:** To transgress here is Edom's political revolt against Judah's authority; in context it expresses a decisive breaking away from an established rule, reflecting the root sense of willful separation from a governing relationship.

#### vc=35599 — 2Ch 21:10 (H6586 pa.sha) — VCG `M10-Q-VCG-01`

> _… 2Ch 21:10 So Edom_
> **2Ch 21:10 So Edom revolted from the rule of Judah to this day . At that time Libnah also revolted from his rule , because he had forsaken the Lord , the God of his fathers .**
> _from the rule of Judah …_

**Pass A meaning:** Revolt here by Edom and Libnah is causally linked to Jehoram forsaking God; the verse shows transgression as a defection that mirrors its instigator's own inner abandonment of the Lord, spreading outward as political fragmentation.
