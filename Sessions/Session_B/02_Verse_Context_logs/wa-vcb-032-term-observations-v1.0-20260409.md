## H3820A lev — mti_term_id 581

**Overview:** H3820A *lev* is the primary Hebrew anthropological term for the "heart" as the inner person. 331 verses examined. The term spans the full range of the inner being: cognitive, moral, affective, volitional, spiritual, relational, and conscience functions. 325 verses relevant; 6 set aside (spatial idioms and physical organ uses). 8 groups formed.

**Set-aside verses (is_relevant = 0):**
- 66601 Deu 4:11 — "heart of heaven": spatial idiom, no inner-being engagement
- 66613 Exo 15:8 — "heart of the sea": spatial idiom, no inner-being engagement
- 66617 Exo 28:30 — Urim/Thummim placed on Aaron's chest/garment: physical object placement
- 66589 2Sa 18:14 — javelins thrust into the heart of Absalom: physical organ, death blow
- 66664 Hos 13:8 — "tear open their breast": physical anatomy of bear's victim
- 66642 Exo 9:14 — "on you yourself": personal pronoun, lev not the inner-being carrier in this rendering
