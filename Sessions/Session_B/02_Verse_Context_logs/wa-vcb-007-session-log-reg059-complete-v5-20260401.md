## 2. Key analytical findings — Registry 059

### Treachery cluster (H0899A, H0900, H0901, H4603, H4604, H0898)
Six terms mapping faithlessness, unfaithfulness, and treachery. Together they cover: betrayal as inner moral character (ba.gad, be.ged, bo.ge.dot), covenant unfaithfulness toward God (ma.al), and treachery in intimate relationship (ba.gad, ma.al). Key verse: Jer 3:10 — treachery defined as the absence of wholehearted return; faithlessness as inner-outer disjunction. Flag for Session B.

### Diminished faith cluster (G3640 oligopistos, G6066 oligopistia)
Maps the inner condition of faith's smallness — insufficient trust producing fear, doubt, and anxious self-reliance. Flag for Session B: noun vs. adjective — same inner condition or different aspects?

### Disbelief cluster (G0569 apisteō, G0571 apistos)
Inner non-reception of divine truth; unbelief linked to spiritual blindness, defiled mind, and defiled conscience. Key verse: Luk 24:41 — disbelief coexisting with joy; inner non-assent distinguished from emotional response. Flag for Session B.

### Faithfulness cluster (H0529 e.mun, H0530 e.mu.nah, H0571G e.met, G4102H pistis, G4103 pistos)
Maps faithfulness across its full range: divine attribute (enduring, covenant-grounding), human quality (chosen, practised, rare), community designation (the faithful as moral category), and its absence (crisis, perishment, moral desolation). Key verses: Lam 3:23 (divine faithfulness renewed morning by morning), Psa 119:30 (I have chosen the way of faithfulness), Jer 5:1 (seek a faithful man).

### Faith cluster (G4102G pistis, G4100 pisteuō, G4104 pistoō)
The inner act of trust, receiving righteousness and life. Key verses: Rom 1:17 (righteous shall live by faith), Gal 2:20 (I live by faith in the Son of God), Mar 9:24 (I believe; help my unbelief), Rom 10:10 (with the heart one believes). The demons' belief (Jam 2:19) marks the limit of intellectual assent without inner transformation.

**Session B flag:** Registry 059 contains the programme's most concentrated inner-being vocabulary for the faith-faithfulness-treachery semantic field. The tension between faith as inner act vs. corporate content, and faithfulness as divine character vs. human choice, will be the central analytical challenge.
