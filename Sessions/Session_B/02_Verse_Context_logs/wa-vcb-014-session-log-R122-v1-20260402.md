## Methodological notes — Registry 122

Prayer registry is analytically clean — the vast majority of terms engage the inner being directly through the act of prayer. Key analytical distinctions:

- **G4336 proseuchomai** (81v) — Three groups distinguish prayer as surrender of the will (Gethsemane model), as instructed discipline (Sermon on Mount, persistence parables), and as corporate/intercessory practice. Rom 8:26 (Spirit praying with groanings too deep for words) is among the most profound inner-being verses in the NT.
- **G4335 proseuchē** (36v) — The imagery of prayers as golden bowls of incense ascending before the throne (Rev 5:8; 8:3/4) is the most elevated image of prayer as inner-being act becoming divine experience.
- **H3908 la.chash** — The "whispered prayer" of Isa 26:16 is the sole relevant verse; it names the desperate, intimate inner cry under divine discipline — a distinctively different form from the NT terms.
- **G1189 deomai** — Set-asides are the social "beg/ask" uses (civic appeals, rhetorical requests). The prayer-to-God uses all pass.
