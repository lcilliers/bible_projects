## Key Analytical Observations for Session B

**H0408 al (mti=1119, Reg 147):** The 76-verse relevant corpus of the prohibitive particle maps the negative shape of inner life — every inner state that is commanded *not* to be felt. The fear/dismay cluster (32 verses, primarily Isaianic) is the dominant grouping, constituting a coherent sub-corpus of divine reassurance formula. This is data about the range of inner states biblical authors recognised as requiring authoritative negation.

**Reg 157 (temptation):** The peirazō/peirasmos cluster is thematically coherent. Jam 1:14 ("lured and enticed by his own desire") provides the clearest anatomy of inner temptation: desire as the inner locus of the tempter's access. This verse should be a Session B anchor for the temptation cluster. Mat 26:41 ("the spirit is willing but the flesh is weak") establishes the internal bifurcation most clearly.

**Reg 158 (terror):** The Ezekiel 32 passage contributes 8 verses (chit.tit, mti=1151) showing terror as a political-psychological condition imposed on subject populations. This is an unusual inner-being use — terror as a societal inner condition rather than individual experience. Worth noting in Session B.

**H5462 sa.gar (mti=1149, Reg 156):** One highly significant verse — Psa 17:10, "They close their hearts to pity." The shutting/closing vocabulary applied to the heart as an inner act of hardening deserves close attention in Session B: it is a precise metaphor for the deliberate inner foreclosure of compassion.

**Reg 159 testimony — Psalm 119 cluster:** The testimony vocabulary produced a dense inner-being cluster in Psalm 119 — testimonies as the object of delight (heart), meditation, clinging, and soul-keeping. This is significant for understanding the inner-being dimension of Torah/testimony piety.

**Reg 160 (thought):** The thought vocabulary is the richest inner-being cluster in this batch. Key anchors for Session B: Phili 4:8 (the positive command to direct inner cognition), Rom 6:11 (reckoning as appropriation of theological reality), 2Cor 10:5 (taking every thought captive), Dan 7:28 (thoughts alarming the inner being, kept in the heart), Psa 139:2 (divine knowledge of human thought from afar), logizomai as the dominant Greek inner-reckoning term.

**G3982 peithō (mti=1194, Reg 163):** The persuasion vocabulary maps the inner territory of conviction, trust, and confidence with unusual precision. The contrast between confidence in the flesh (Phili 3:3-4) and confidence in the Lord produces a key inner-being tension for Session B: what is the proper object of inner reliance?
