### 1.2 Key Analytical Decisions — Registry 102

**Structural observation:** Registry 102 is titled "longing" but is dominated by terms from the ka.lah root family (H3615G/H/I/J, H3616, H3617, H3631) whose primary semantic range is completion, finishing, consuming, and destroying — not longing per se. Only H3615I and H3615J carry a longing sense in some contexts. The registry shows a 39% passage rate (95 of 242 verses relevant), markedly lower than Registry 100's 72%, reflecting the high proportion of procedural and military-destruction uses.

**H3615G (ka.lah — finish, 103 verses):** High set-aside rate (88 of 103). Only 15 passed. Key inner-being uses: prayer "spoken in the heart" (Gen 24:15, 24:45 — the servant's inward address to God); the spirit failing (Psa 143:7); God's love that never ceases (Lam 3:22); God's fury coming to an end (Isa 10:25); Dan 9:24's eschatological finishing of transgression. All procedural completions (tabernacle built, speeches given, battles ended, harvests reaped) set aside.

**H3615H (ka.lah — destroy, 53 verses):** Two groups formed around divine wrath consuming (Group 001) and moral failure producing consumption (Group 002). Exo 32:10-12, 33:3, 33:5 are notable — God's inner anger framed around his consuming intent and its intercession-shaped restraint.

**H3615I (ka.lah — decides, 10 verses):** All 10 relevant. Clear inner-being bifurcation: fixed determination of the will (Group 001: 1Sa 20:7/9/33, Est 7:7) and the soul consuming itself in longing (Group 002: Psa 119:81, 2Sa 13:39 — David's spirit longing for Absalom). The inclusion of Psa 119:81 here is notable — the soul "kalah-ing" for salvation, with hope in the word.

**H3615J (ka.lah — expend, 37 verses):** Strong inner-being corpus across two directions: the person's inner self being spent through grief, longing, and affliction (Psa 84:2 — soul faints for the courts of the Lord; Psa 31:10 — life spent in sorrow); and God's wrath spending itself (Eze 5:13, Lam 4:11). The inverse (God's provision not-spending, 1Ki 17:14/16) retained in Group 001 as expression of divine faithfulness through non-exhaustion.

**H4357 (mikh.lah) and H4944 (mash.shaq):** Both 1-verse, both purely material/physical, both researcher-confirmed as all-verses-fail. Confirmed by Leroux: "set aside the two flags."

**G0603 (apokaradokia):** Single verse but conceptually significant — Paul's compound coinage for creation's neck-straining expectation of the sons of God's revealing (Rom 8:19). A rare word that names the posture of the inner being stretched forward toward eschatological hope.

**H8264 (sha.qaq — to rush):** Only 2 of 6 verses relevant. The crucial verse is Psa 107:9 — "he satisfies the longing soul" — where sha.qaq names the rushing quality of the soul's desire that God meets. Isa 29:8's dream-hunger is the other relevant instance.
