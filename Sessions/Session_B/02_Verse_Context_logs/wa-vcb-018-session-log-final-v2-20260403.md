## Analytical observations — Registry 173

### Choosing terms (haireō family and related)

The cluster of Greek choosing terms (G0138, G0140, G4255, G4400, G4401, G0829, G0830) provides a coherent NT vocabulary of will:
- **Heb 11:25** (haireō): Moses "choosing rather to be mistreated with the people of God" — the will's deliberate preference against pleasure, for suffering with God's people.
- **2Cor 9:7** (proaireō): "each one must give as he has **decided** in his heart" — the heart's inner decision as the governing act of generosity.
- **2Pe 2:10** (authadēs): "bold and **willful**" — self-will as inner-being vice; the refusal of restraint.
- **2Cor 8:3** (authairetos): "of their own **accord**" — voluntariness as inner-being freedom in giving.

Together these terms map the NT's understanding of the will as an inner faculty that can choose, decide, presume, or give freely.

### ha.lakh (to go) — the walk theology

The ~59 retained verses from H1980G's 220 reveal the Hebrew Bible's sustained use of "going" as moral/spiritual orientation. Six groups:
1. **Divine commission** — "Go!" as prophetic inner-being call (Isa 6:8 anchor)
2. **Going after false gods** — spiritual whoredom expressed through directional metaphor (Jer 13:10 anchor)
3. **The way you should go** — divine guidance of inner direction (Isa 48:17 anchor)
4. **Seeking God** — inner-being movement toward the divine (Hos 5:6 anchor)
5. **Going toward death** — mortality confronted through directional metaphor (Job 16:22 anchor)
6. **"My heart has gone after my eyes"** — the inner mechanism of moral departure (Job 31:7 anchor — significant for programme)

**Job 31:7** is a programme-significant verse: "if my step has turned aside from the way and **my heart has gone after my eyes**" — the sequence (heart follows eyes) names the inner-being mechanism by which moral departure happens. This is a direct link between inner vision and inner volition.

### ya.shav (to dwell) — dwelling as inner posture

Of 216 verses, ~60 retained. The inner-being uses cluster around six patterns:
- **God enthroned** (Isa 6:1 anchor) — the divine session as inner vision
- **Sitting in darkness** (Isa 9:2 anchor) — spiritual darkness as dwelling condition; light as liberation
- **Eschatological rest** (Mic 4:4 anchor) — "each man under his vine, no one shall make them afraid" — inner peace as the absence of fear
- **Covenantal dwelling** (Hos 3:3 anchor) — "dwell as mine…you shall not play the whore" — intimate possession in the covenant
- **Arrogant aspiration** (Isa 14:13 anchor) — "I will sit on the mount of assembly" — the self-enthroning pride of the heart
- **Solidarity in suffering** (Job 2:13 anchor) — sitting with the sufferer for seven days without words

**Isa 14:13** is significant: the king of Babylon's inner aspiration is entirely expressed through three "I will" verbs plus the act of sitting. The self-enthroning will is a concentrated inner-being datum.

### ma.tay "how long?" — the inner cry

All 15 retained verses of H4970 are "how long?" cries. The pattern is uniform and coherent: the question names an inner-being condition of longing, anguish, or tested patience. Jer 4:14 is the most significant: "**How long shall your wicked thoughts lodge within you?**" — wicked thoughts dwelling in the inner being, the "how long" naming the urgency of inner cleansing.

### H5071 ne.da.vah — the freewill offering as inner-being vocabulary

All 25 verses retained. The freewill offering is the OT's primary vocabulary for voluntary inner-being devotion. **Psa 110:3** — "your people will offer themselves **freely** on the day of your power" — uses ne.da.vah metaphorically: the people themselves become the freewill offering. **Hos 14:4** — "I will love them **freely**, for my anger has turned from them" — divine freewill love named using this same vocabulary. The term spans human and divine voluntary inner-being movement.
