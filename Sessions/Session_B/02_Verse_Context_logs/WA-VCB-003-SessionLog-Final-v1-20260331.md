## Structural Observations for Downstream Sessions

**Observation 1 — Extraction artefacts:** Several terms in VCB-003 appear to have been included in their respective registries through co-occurrence extraction rather than lexical inner-being content — notably G1161 (de), G1577 (ekklēsia) in registry 19, and the physical womb terms (H7356A, H7358) in registry 23. This is an expected feature of comprehensive extraction and should be flagged to Claude Code for registry inventory review if desired.

**Observation 2 — Word-family integrity:** The *dokimos* cluster (registry 20), the *splanchnon* cluster (registry 23), and the *phthora/phtheirō* cluster (registry 31) are internally consistent and analytically coherent. Session B analysis will benefit from treating these as clusters within their registry pool analysis.

**Observation 3 — Consecration and calling:** Registries 19 and 28 both contain sub-terms whose specific sense is physical, spatial, or procedural. In both cases the inner-being content is concentrated in a subset of the term family. The structural contrast between the consecration-as-inner-act terms (*na.zar*, *qa.dash* consecrate/sanctify, *qa.dosh* saint) and the physical-space terms (Most Holy Place, Holy Place, sanctuary) is itself analytically significant — consecration in Scripture is an inner-being category that finds architectural expression, not the reverse.

**Observation 4 — The kidney (kil.yah) cluster:** H3629 is one of the most direct inner-being terms in the Hebrew corpus. Its two groups (seat of God's searching; seat of human inner experience) will anchor the conscience registry's analysis in Session B.

**Observation 5 — Corruption registry:** The corruption terms divide into two ontological levels — terms describing the moral corruption of the inner person (*sha.chat*, *ftheirō*, *fthora* in its sowing-to-flesh sense) and terms describing the eschatological domain of corruption (Hades, Gehenna, Pit/Sheol, *diafthora*). Both are inner-being relevant, but from different angles. Session B should distinguish these.
