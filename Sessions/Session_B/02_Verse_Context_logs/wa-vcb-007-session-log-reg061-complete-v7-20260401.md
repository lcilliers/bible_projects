## 3. Key analytical findings — Registry 061

### The fear-not formula (H3372G ya.re, Group 298-001)
The divine command "Fear not / Do not be afraid" is a pervasive inner-being act spanning Genesis to Revelation — present in ~60 verses of ya.re alone. This constitutes a distinct category: divine address to human inner dread, replacing it with peace grounded in the declaration of divine presence. The formula is the most widely-distributed single inner-being pattern in the Fear registry.

### Theophanic fear cluster
Multiple terms converge on the experience of overwhelming inner fear before divine presence: G1630 ekfobos, G1790 entromos, G5156 tromos, H2729 cha.rad (Group 305-001), G5399 fobeō (Group 292-001), H3372G ya.re. Together they map the inner phenomenology of the theophanic encounter — trembling, prostration, silencing, and the inability to look.

### Reverential fear as the proper inner orientation
The "fear of the Lord" cluster — H3374 yir.ah (269), G5401 fobos (266, Group 266-002), G5399 fobeō (292, Group 292-002), H3372G ya.re (298, Group 298-002), eulabeia/eulabeomai/eulabēs, theosebeia/theosebēs — maps the inner orientation of reverence as the foundation of wisdom, covenant faithfulness, and acceptable worship. A major convergence point for Session B.

### The trembling sub-cluster
H2729 cha.rad, H2731 cha.ra.dah, H2730 cha.red, G5156 tromos, G1790 entromos — five distinct trembling terms. Differentiations emerging: trembling at God's word (cha.red) as the receptive inner posture vs. panic-trembling before threats (cha.ra.dah) vs. body-expressing inner agitation (entromos, tromos). Flag for Session B.

### Security as the reversal of fear (H2729 cha.rad, Group 305-003)
The "none shall make them afraid" formula (~11 verses) constitutes a distinct inner-being group: eschatological and covenantal rest from fear. The promised absence of trembling is itself an inner-being condition.

### Anxiety, sorrow, and disheartening
H1672 da.ag (anxiety), H3512B ka.eh (disheartened), H2866 cha.tat (alarm producing withdrawal) — map the softer or chronic forms of inner fear: worry, discouragement, grief over sin. These are analytically important for the programme's understanding of the inner being's intermediate conditions between acute terror and settled peace.
