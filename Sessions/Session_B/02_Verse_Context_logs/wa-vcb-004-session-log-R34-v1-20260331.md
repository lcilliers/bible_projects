## 2. Key Observations

**H1285 (be.rit)** is the covenant term in its fullest expression. Five groups capture the distinct inner-being dimensions of covenant across the canon: (1) the unconditional divine commitment established with creation and the patriarchs, anchored in God's inner remembering (Gen 9:15 — "I will remember my covenant"); (2) the bilateral Sinai covenant requiring inner faithfulness, anchored in God as "the faithful God who keeps covenant with those who love him" (Deu 7:9); (3) the new covenant of inner transformation — law written on hearts rather than stone (Jer 31:33); (4) the Davidic covenant of everlasting steadfast love (2Sa 23:5); (5) the personal covenant of relational loyalty between persons, with Job 31:1 ("a covenant with my eyes") as the most inward expression.

**H7650 (sha.va)** — the swearing/oath term — produced four groups of which Group 4 is notable: the Song of Songs adjuration ("I adjure you … for I am sick with love") places oath language in the context of erotic longing and love. This is an inner-being use of the term not found elsewhere in the register.

**H3772H (ka.rat — make covenant)** Group 4 anchor (1Sa 18:3 — "Jonathan made a covenant with David, because he loved him as his own soul") is a significant inner-being covenant of friendship. The covenant is grounded in love of the other's soul — among the most direct inner-being expressions in the Covenant registry.

**H3772G (ka.rat — cut)** yielded only 5 relevant verses from 50, confirming the physical-cutting sense dominates this term's corpus. Jer 34:18 (the calf-cutting ritual as the basis for the self-imprecatory covenant oath) is the most significant inner-being contribution.

**G2537 (kainos — new)** extended beyond covenant proper to include new creation (2Cor 5:17), new self (Eph 4:24), and eschatological new realities (Rev 2:17; 21:5). These three groups are formally grouped under the covenant registry but represent inner-being realities that exceed the covenant concept strictly — flagged for Session D awareness.
