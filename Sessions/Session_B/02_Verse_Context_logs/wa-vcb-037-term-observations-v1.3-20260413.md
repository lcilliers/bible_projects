## Programme-level methodological note — indirect illumination principle

**Raised:** VCB-037 classification session | 2026-04-13
**Nature:** Hermeneutical open question — not a filter revision

**Observation:** Scripture frequently illuminates inner-being realities *indirectly* — through physical description, structural imagery, material analogy, and implicit comparison. The physical or structural term is the lens through which something about inner experience becomes visible, even when the connection is not stated explicitly in the verse. This is a genuine and distinctive feature of biblical literature, particularly Hebrew poetry and wisdom.

**Programme decision:** This principle is not applied at Verse Context stage. The current relevance filter asks whether a verse engages the inner being *through this term* — a direct test. Applying an indirect-illumination criterion at this stage would introduce inconsistency across the programme and vagueness that cannot be systematically controlled.

**Where this belongs:** Session D. The analogical and illuminative potential of physical/structural terms is a cross-registry synthetic question — it cannot be resolved term by term. Session D is the appropriate place to ask: where does the physical vocabulary of the fellowship/joining root family illuminate inner-being realities through analogy or implicit comparison?

**Session B instruction:** When Session B analyses fellowship terms, the analyst should note — without resolving — any verse where physical usage appears to carry analogical illumination of inner-being realities. These observations should be raised as SD pointers targeting Session D, not treated as classification decisions.

**SD pointer to be raised at Session B:** See sessionB-flags file.
