### mti_id=6330 | G4862 (sun) — with | 99 verses

**Term note:** Preposition "with/together." A highly functional grammatical particle with 99 verses. Per Section 3.5 (filter for grammatical/functional particles): *sun* passes the filter if it directs, intensifies, qualifies the scope of, or discloses an inner orientation through inner-being content. It fails if purely syntactic, social register, temporal connector, or procedural.

**Reading all 99 verses individually:**

The vast majority of these verses use *sun* in a purely syntactic or relational-locational sense ("he went with them," "Peter standing with the eleven," "she was with child," "they were with him," "he remained with the disciples," "went with Barnabas"). These are uses of *sun* as a positional/accompaniment preposition — no inner-being engagement through the particle itself.

A subset shows *sun* functioning in a theologically intensive way — specifically in Pauline constructions expressing co-death, co-resurrection, co-life, co-suffering with Christ, or co-identity with the body of Christ. In these, *sun* qualifies the inner-being content: it names not merely physical accompaniment but ontological/spiritual union and participation.
