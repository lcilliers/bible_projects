## Observations on Registry 19 Pattern

**Observation (not interpretation):** Registry 19 (calling) contains a very high proportion of terms that yield all-or-near-all set-aside results. Of 26 terms, 9 terms (G1161, G1577, G4377, H4744, H7121J, H7122H, H7122I, H7123, H7124, H7148) yield zero relevant verses, and several more yield only 1–5. The substantive inner-being content in the registry is concentrated in: G1941, G2564G, G2821, G2822, G3875, G4643, H7121G, H7121H, H7121I, H7621.

**Reflection:** The calling register in Hebrew and Greek is lexically broad. The word-family spans purely social/administrative calling, narrative naming, acoustic phenomena, liturgical assembly, and the theologically significant divine call to persons. Verse Context separates these clearly. The theologically rich inner-being content is real and substantial — but it is concentrated in a relatively small portion of the total verse pool.

**Note for pool analysis (Session B):** The inner-being calling concept is predominantly a Greek New Testament pattern (klēsis, klētos, kaleō in divine-call senses) with Hebrew roots in qa.ra (calling upon the name / divine call to identity) and she.vu.ah (oath-commitment). These will form the analytical core for Session B.
