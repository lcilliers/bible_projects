## How to use this document

These flags arise from the Verse Context classification process. They are not errors or problems — they are analytical observations and questions that could not be explored at the Verse Context stage (which intentionally defers interpretation to Session B). Each flag identifies a term, the analytical question raised, and the relevant registries for Session B.

The flags should be loaded alongside the Session B DataPrep and Analysis instructions when the relevant registries are processed. They represent the researcher's interpretive interests that emerged from the raw verse engagement.

## SBF-VCB015-001
**Term:** H5016 ne.vu.ah — prophecy | mti_id=1061 | Registry 124 (prophecy)
**Triggered by:** DF-001 flag resolution — retention of 2Ch 9:29 (bibliographic reference to "the prophecy of Ahijah")

**Analytical question:**
What does Scripture understand about prophetic capability as an inner-being capacity of the human person? The retention of a bibliographic reference to a named prophecy as a form of inner-being content raises a broader question: is prophecy (nevu'ah) something that happens *to* the inner person (Spirit-reception), something the inner person *does* (inner disposition toward God expressed as speech), or both? How does the corpus of na.va (H5012 — to prophesy) and ne.vu.ah (H5016) together illuminate the inner mechanics of prophetic activity?

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Examine the relationship between Spirit-reception and inner transformation in the na.va corpus (especially 1Sa 10:6 — "you will be turned into another man")
- Trace the inner-being preconditions for genuine vs. false prophecy (the false prophets prophesy "from their own hearts" — what does this reveal about the inner person as a source of speech?)
- Consider whether prophetic capability represents a distinct inner-being capacity not fully captured by existing register categories

**Relevant group codes:** 6050-001 (Spirit-empowered); 6050-002 (genuine commission); 6050-003 (inner deceit); 1061-001

## SBF-VCB015-002
**Term:** H4399 me.la.khah — work | mti_id=1068 | Registry 126 (purpose)
**Triggered by:** DF-004 flag resolution — retention of Sabbath prohibition corpus (Group 1068-002)

**Analytical question:**
What does the Sabbath work-prohibition reveal about the inner person's relationship to labour, rest, and covenantal time? The choice to cease work on the Sabbath is an inner volitional act — but what does it disclose about the nature of rest as an inner-being state? Is Sabbath rest a cessation of outer activity that creates space for inner orientation, a positive inner condition of its own, or a discipline that shapes the inner person's relationship to purpose and productivity?

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Explore Gen 2:2-3 (God resting from his work) alongside the Sabbath prohibition corpus — what does divine rest reveal about the inner-being meaning of work and cessation?
- Examine Hag 1:14 (the spirit stirred up — they worked on the house of the Lord) in relation to Jer 48:10 (cursed is the one who does the work of the Lord with slackness) — what inner disposition toward commissioned work does Scripture valorise?
- Consider the connection between me.la.khah (work/purpose registry) and the nefesh/ruach (soul/spirit) terms in other registries — is the spirit the seat of the drive to work?

**Relevant group codes:** 1068-001 (commissioned work); 1068-002 (Sabbath prohibition)

## SBF-VCB015-003
**Term:** H6593 pe.shet — flax | mti_id=6080 | Registry 128 (rebellion)
**Triggered by:** DF-006 flag resolution — retention of Hos 2:5, 2:9; Pro 31:13; Isa 42:3

**Analytical question:**
The physical material objects enumerated as the provisions of adulterous Israel's "lovers" (wool, flax, bread, water, oil — Hos 2:5) are the objects of misattributed desire and mislocated gratitude. What does the specificity of these material objects reveal about the inner mechanics of spiritual adultery and idolatry? Why does Scripture's portrayal of inner unfaithfulness so consistently move through material goods? Where does the inner person go wrong — in the desire itself, the object of desire, the attribution of provision, or something else?

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Examine the full Hos 2 context alongside the broader adultery/faithlessness vocabulary in the rebellion and seeking registries
- Consider the inner-being dynamics of attachment: what does Scripture reveal about how the inner person forms bonds to material provisions and misidentifies their source?
- Connect with the "seeking" registry (da.rash, sha.char) — the relationship between seeking God and seeking material goods as competing inner orientations
- The Pro 31:13 use (willing hands working with wool and flax) presents an instructive contrast: the same material worked with right inner orientation vs. Hosea's misoriented desire

**Relevant group codes:** 6080-001; and cross-registry connection with seeking (da.rash Group 1100-003), reconciliation, rebellion

## SBF-VCB015-004
**Term:** H0595 a.no.khi — I | mti_id=1102 | Registry 140 (seeking)
**Triggered by:** DF-012 flag resolution — retention of 68 relevant verses

**Analytical question:**
Does the emphatic first-person form (a.no.khi, in contrast to the common ani) disclose something about self-awareness, personal identity, and the experience of uniqueness as inner-being capacities? When God says "I" with emphasis ("I, I am he who blots out your transgressions" — Isa 43:25; "Fear not, Abram, I am your shield" — Gen 15:1), is the emphasis a disclosure of divine inner personhood? When humans use the emphatic form in contexts of fear, confession, or covenant declaration, does the emphasis signal a heightened inner-being self-awareness?

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Map the distribution of a.no.khi usage: divine emphatic self-disclosure vs. human emphatic self-disclosure — do they operate differently at the inner-being level?
- Consider whether Scripture's use of the emphatic pronoun reveals an implicit anthropology of personhood — that the "I" has a weight and gravity that the ordinary pronoun does not
- Explore the relationship between self-awareness (knowing oneself as "I") and the inner-being categories already established in the programme — is self-awareness a distinct inner-being capacity, or does it emerge from the interaction of other capacities (heart, mind, will)?
- Cross-reference with the identity/naming vocabulary elsewhere in the programme

**Relevant group codes:** 1102-001

## SBF-VCB015-005
**Term:** H4672 ma.tsa — to find | mti_id=1101 | Registry 140 (seeking)
**Triggered by:** DF-014 flag resolution — retention of 140 relevant verses across 4 groups

**Analytical question:**
"Finding" as an inner-being act — the capacity to discover, recognise, and encounter what one seeks. The corpus spans finding favour (relational), finding wisdom (character), finding rest (inner state), and God finding sin or faithfulness in a person. Together these suggest that "finding" is not merely a neutral discovery act but a relational and inner-being event. What does the programme's engagement with ma.tsa reveal about the inner person's capacity for discovery, recognition, and encounter?

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Trace the inner-being trajectory from seeking (da.rash — H1875) to finding (ma.tsa — H4672): is there a movement from inner orientation to inner encounter? What happens in the inner person when finding occurs?
- Examine the "finding favour" group (1101-001) in detail — what does the inner-relational structure of favour/grace involve? Why does finding favour require a disposition in both the one who finds and the one from whom it is found?
- Consider Jer 29:13 ("You will seek me and find me, when you seek me with all your heart") as a programmatic statement about the relationship between inner wholeness and finding
- Connect with the wisdom tradition's vocabulary of finding: what does finding wisdom (Pro 3:13; 8:17, 35) reveal about the inner person's orientation toward understanding?

**Relevant group codes:** 1101-001 (finding favour); 1101-002 (finding wisdom); 1101-003 (God finding moral condition); 1101-004 (finding rest)

## SBF-VCB015-006
**Term:** H0369 a.yin — nothing | mti_id=1110 | Registry 142 (self-control)
**Triggered by:** DF-015 flag resolution — retention of 78 relevant verses

**Analytical question:**
Why do some human beings experience "nothing" in terms of the awareness or presence of inner-being characteristics — no fear of God, no understanding, no comforter, no shepherd? Is this absence a deficit (the person lacks a capacity they should have), a condition (a state that can change), a judgment (what God allows in consequence of choices), or a description of inner dissolution? The corpus of a.yin suggests that Scripture is deeply attentive to inner-being absence as a significant phenomenon in its own right.

**Suggested Session B exploration:**
- Examine the range of inner-being qualities negated by a.yin across the corpus: no fear of God (Gen 20:11; Psa 36:1), no understanding (multiple Proverbs), no comforter (Lam 1:2, 9), no shepherd (Eze 34:5–6). What is the inner-being topology of these absences — do they form a pattern?
- Consider whether the absence of inner-being qualities is itself an inner state: is "no fear of God" a neutral emptiness, or an active inner orientation away from God?
- Connect with the self-control registry's broader concern: is the capacity for self-restraint itself an inner-being endowment that can be absent? What does H0369 in the self-control registry suggest about the relationship between restraint and the inner awareness of what one ought to restrain?
- Cross-reference with the shame vocabulary: does the absence of shame (H0954 bosh — "they were not ashamed" Gen 2:25) function differently from other inner-being absences?

**Relevant group codes:** 1110-001; cross-registry connection with shame (313-001), self-control (all groups), seeking (1100-003)
