## FLAG SB-037-001 — Indirect illumination principle: physical/structural terms and inner-being analogy

**Type:** Methodological pointer → SD pointer required at Session B
**Priority:** HIGH
**Registries implicated:** 62 (fellowship); potentially all registries in C02 and adjacent clusters
**Session target:** Raise SD pointer targeting Session D

### What was observed

During VCB-037 classification, a methodological question surfaced that could not be resolved at the Verse Context stage. Several terms in the fellowship/joining root family (chavar root, H2266–H4226) have substantial corpora of physical and structural usage — tabernacle construction, architectural joining, material binding. These were classified as set-aside (physical_only) under the current relevance filter.

The researcher identified that Scripture frequently illuminates inner-being realities *indirectly* through physical description, structural imagery, and implicit analogy. The physical term functions as a lens through which inner-being truth becomes visible — without the connection being stated explicitly in the verse. This is a recognised feature of biblical literature, particularly Hebrew wisdom and poetry.

### Why this was not resolved at Verse Context stage

The current relevance filter applies a direct test: does this verse engage the inner being *through this term*? An indirect-illumination criterion cannot be applied consistently at this stage without introducing vagueness that would compromise the programme's analytical integrity across all batches. The programme decision is to preserve the question rather than expand the filter.

### What Session B must do

During Session B analysis of Registry 62 (fellowship), when reading anchor verses and performing the six analytical passes:

1. **Note any verse or term group where physical or structural usage appears to carry analogical or implicit illumination of inner-being realities.** Do not treat this as a classification decision — treat it as an observation.

2. **Specific attention areas from this batch:**
   - H2266 (cha.var — to unite): tabernacle curtains *coupled into a single whole* (Exo 26:6, 26:11, 36:13, 36:18) — does the structural unity of the tabernacle illuminate inner-being unity, covenant, or belonging?
   - H4225 (mach.be.ret — joining): the seam/joining-point of the ephod — does structural joining in sacred garments carry analogical weight?
   - H2279 (cho.ve.ret — set): the paired curtain sets — does the two-becoming-one structure illuminate inner-being pairing or covenant?
   - H2266 (Psa 122:3): Jerusalem *bound firmly together* — does urban structural unity illuminate the gathered community's inner-being solidarity?

3. **Raise an SD pointer for Session D** on the question: *Where does the physical and structural vocabulary of the fellowship/joining root family (chavar root, Reg 62) illuminate inner-being realities through analogy or implicit comparison — and what does this reveal about how Scripture uses structural/material language to speak about inner-being unity, belonging, and covenant?*
