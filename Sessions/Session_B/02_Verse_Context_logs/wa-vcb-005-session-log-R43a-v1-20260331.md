## Structural Observations

**thelō / thelēma cluster:** The Greek will-cluster (G2309, G2307) together show a consistent tripartite inner-being pattern: (1) divine will as source and ground, (2) human will in alignment/submission, (3) human will in misdirection or capture. This structural coherence across the two terms will be significant for Session B analysis. G2307 adds the dimension of God's sovereign purposive will as an inner attribute of the divine person — a category that G2309 also touches but thelēma carries with greater theological weight.

**zēteō (G2212) in relation to thelō (G2309):** Both terms engage the directed will — thelō from the side of what the will inclines toward (the desiring), zēteō from the side of what the will pursues (the seeking). The inner-being picture is beginning to show a will-desire-seeking complex at the core of Registry 43.
