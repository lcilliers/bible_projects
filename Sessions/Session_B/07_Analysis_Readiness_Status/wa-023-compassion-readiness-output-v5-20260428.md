**Description:**

> Compassion is feeling the pain of another from the inside — not observing suffering from a distance but being moved by it in the gut. The Hebrew root is the word for womb, suggesting the tender, visceral, maternal quality of this response. God's compassion is described in these terms more than almost any other attribute: it is not merely that he decides to help but that he is moved — compelled — to help by what he sees. Compassion always faces outward toward the one suffering and produces action.

**sb_classification_reasoning:**

> Compassion originates at a somatic level (womb, gut, heart) and is felt as an inner-being orientation before producing outward action. The RACHAM/SPLANCHN vocabulary names a physically-located inner movement; the CHESED vocabulary names a settled character quality at the soul level. The soul-body interface classification applies primarily to the somatic sub-pattern; the characterological sub-pattern may require separate classification. Confidence: Medium.
