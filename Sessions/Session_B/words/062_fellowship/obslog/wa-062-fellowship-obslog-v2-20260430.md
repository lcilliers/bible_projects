#### Tier 1 — Definition — 24 prompts

**Q&A-013 | T1.1.1**
- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status: A
- Answer: The programme name "fellowship" signals shared participation — the state of holding something in common with another. The Greek koinōnia (literally "the-common-ness") and the Hebrew chavar family (to join, bind, be united with) both name the act and state of genuine participation rather than mere adjacency. The name immediately signals: fellowship is not proximity but sharing-in; not presence near but presence with in a participatory sense. OBS-062-001, OBS-062-017.
- Anchor verses: 1Jo 1:3, 2Pe 1:4
- Finding type: MEANING_OBSERVATION

**Q&A-014 | T1.1.2**
- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status: A
- Answer: The chavar family (H2266–H2279): root meaning of physical joining/binding — a wound joins force to body; a seam joins curtain to curtain; a companion joins life to life. The underlying logic is being-bound-to-something such that the bond is real and consequential. The koinōn family: from koinos (common) — koinōnia is the state of having-in-common; what is shared defines the fellowship. Together: Hebrew emphasises the binding/joining act; Greek emphasises the common object shared. Both point to genuine participation as the definitional core. OBS-062-005, OBS-062-007, OBS-062-017.
- Finding type: ETYMOLOGY
