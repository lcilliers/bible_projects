## Structural relationships (T6)


### T6.1 — Co-occurrence

**Prompt 1: Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?**

From v1 obslog Section 5 — strongest signals: R43 desire (6 shared verses — highest co-occurrence); R167 unity (3 shared verses); R184 spirit (3 shared verses); R192 comfort (3 shared verses). Secondary: R30 contrition and R151 sorrow (shared anchor Isa 53:5); R180 yielding (shared anchor 2Pe 1:4); R34 covenant (shared anchor Mal 2:14). OBS-062-028 through OBS-062-035.

**Prompt 2: What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?**

The co-occurrence pattern reveals fellowship as a relational-formative hub — it consistently appears with desire (the motivating state directing it), unity (its corporate outcome), spirit (its mediating agent), and comfort (its affective product through suffering). Fellowship does not stand alone but is always embedded in this surrounding network of inner-being realities. OBS-062-028 through OBS-062-035.


### T6.2 — Sequential Relationships

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?**

Fellowship consistently precedes character formation (Psa 119:63 → Jer 13:23 sequence). It follows desire-orientation (desire directs the fellowship). In 2Pe 1:4, it follows escape from corruption and is the culminating participatory state. Fellowship precedes unity (the fellowship-act produces unity as its corporate outcome). OBS-062-028, OBS-062-052, OBS-062-058.

**Prompt 2: What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?**

The fellowship → character formation sequence is causal (habitual fellowship causes character to form in conformity with the fellowship object). The desire → fellowship sequence is directional (desire directs where fellowship forms, not merely correlational). OBS-062-028, OBS-062-052.


### T6.3 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one, and by what mechanism?**

Fellowship produces: (1) hope (Ecc 9:4); (2) fixed moral character (Jer 13:23); (3) shared comfort (2Cor 1:7); (4) inner cleansing (Pro 20:30); (5) divine nature participation (2Pe 1:4); (6) unity (Eze 37:19). OBS-062-045, OBS-062-052, OBS-062-031, OBS-062-039, OBS-062-058.

**Prompt 2: Is this characteristic produced by another — and if so, which one?**

Fellowship is produced by: desire (R43 — the inner motivating state directing it); divine initiative (God joins — Eze 37:19); volitional self-joining (human Hithpael act). OBS-062-028, OBS-062-051, OBS-062-007.

**Prompt 3: Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another characteristic constitute part of this one?**

Fellowship is constituent of: unity (R167 — fellowship is the participatory inner reality of which unity is the corporate expression); sanctification (fellowship with God-fearers is the relational mechanism of character formation); divine nature participation (2Pe 1:4 — fellowship is the mode of ontological transformation). OBS-062-029, OBS-062-058.

### T6.5 — Distinctions

**Prompt 1: Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?**

R167 unity is the closest adjacent characteristic — unity is the corporate/structural outcome of which fellowship is the inner participatory reality. The distinction: fellowship names the inner act and state of sharing-in; unity names the resultant oneness of those who fellowship. Eze 37:19 deploys fellowship vocabulary to achieve unity as the explicit purpose. OBS-062-029, OBS-062-051.

**Prompt 2: Where the evidence shows apparent overlap, what is the precise boundary?**

Fellowship vs unity: kind distinction — fellowship is inner participatory reality; unity is the structural outcome. Fellowship vs covenant (R34): layer distinction — covenant is the legal/structural bond; fellowship is the inner relational experience of that bond (SP-062-007). Fellowship vs desire: sequential distinction — desire directs fellowship; they are not the same act. OBS-062-054, OBS-062-028.

**Prompt 3: Is the distinction between this characteristic and its nearest neighbour one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?**

Fellowship vs unity: kind distinction. Fellowship vs covenant: layer distinction (legal vs experiential). Fellowship vs desire: sequential/directional distinction (preceding vs motivating). OBS-062-028, OBS-062-029, OBS-062-054.

**Prompt 1: Does any verse in this characteristic's evidence base also function as a primary anchor in another characteristic's study?**

Isa 53:5 — shared with R30 contrition, R151 sorrow. 2Pe 1:4 — shared with R180 yielding. Phili 3:10 — shared with R209 likeness, R210 deadness. Mal 2:14 — shared with R34 covenant. 1Jo 1:3 — unique to R062. OBS-062-032, OBS-062-033, OBS-062-054.

**Prompt 2: What does the shared anchor reveal about the relationship between the two characteristics?**

2Pe 1:4 shared with R180 yielding reveals that divine nature participation requires an inner act of yielding (escape from corruption) as its prerequisite — fellowship and yielding are structurally adjacent at the point of ontological transformation. Phili 3:10 shared with R209 and R210 reveals that fellowship-through-suffering is simultaneously a conformity event (becoming like Christ in death) — fellowship is the relational mode; conformity is the constitutional outcome. OBS-062-033, OBS-062-055.
