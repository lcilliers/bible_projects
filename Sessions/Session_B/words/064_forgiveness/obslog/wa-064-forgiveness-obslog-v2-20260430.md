#### Tier 1 — Definition — 24 prompts

**Q&A-013 | T1.1.1**
- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status: A
- Answer: "Forgiveness" signals the release of a legitimate claim — not the denial that the claim existed but the cancellation of it. The Hebrew salach names a divine-exclusive releasing act. The Greek aphiēmi names a releasing/sending-away. The name signals: (1) an act (not a feeling); (2) releasing (not denying or minimising); (3) a claim that was legitimate (the offence was real). The programme name "forgiveness" therefore carries three implicit signals: something real was owed, the creditor releases the debtor, the debt is cancelled not transferred. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-004, OBS-064-005.
- Anchor verses: Psa 130:4, Mat 18:27
- Finding type: MEANING_OBSERVATION

**Q&A-014 | T1.1.2**
- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status: A
- Answer: H5545 (sa.lach — verb): to forgive; exclusively divine subject in OT. H5546 (sal.lach — adjective): forgiving as dispositional character attribute of God (Psa 86:5 alone). H5547 (se.li.chah — noun): forgiveness as abstract quality possessed by God (Psa 130:4, Dan 9:9, Neh 9:17). The three Hebrew forms name the same reality from three angles: act (verb), disposition (adjective), possession (noun). At the definitional level the Hebrew roots reveal that forgiveness is: exclusively divine at its source, characterological in God's being (it describes who God is, not just what God does), and a possession that God has and can extend. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-019, OBS-064-020.
- Anchor verses: Psa 86:5, Psa 130:4
- Finding type: ETYMOLOGY
