## Constitutional location and boundaries

Where this characteristic operates within the spirit/soul/heart/mind/body structure.


### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**Prompt 1: Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?**

The spirit (*ruach*) is not explicitly named as the locus of forgiveness in the primary anchor verses. The closest: the unforgivable sin is explicitly linked to the Holy Spirit (Mar 3:29 — blasphemy against the Holy Spirit), suggesting that the forgiveness transaction operates at the spirit level in its most critical instance. Joh 20:22-23 ("receive the Holy Spirit; if you forgive the sins of any, they are forgiven") links the Spirit's reception with the authority to declare forgiveness. OBS-064-027.

**Prompt 2: Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

Forgiveness originates in divine character — which Scripture associates with the Spirit's work. The access-postures (faith, repentance, confession) may engage the spirit as the deepest constitutional level at which genuine repentance operates. This is inferential from the verse evidence rather than explicit. Gap noted. OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 3: What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

If forgiveness operates at the spirit level (partially evidenced through the Spirit-link in Joh 20:22-23 and Mar 3:29), it reveals forgiveness as a characteristic that reaches the deepest constitutional centre — the place where genuine repentance originates and genuine love-response (Luk 7:47) is generated. Gap noted. OBS-064-027.

**Prompt 4: If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.**

The verse evidence is largely silent on explicit spirit-faculty naming as the primary locus of forgiveness. The Spirit is involved in the authority to declare forgiveness and the outer limit of the unforgivable sin, but the human spirit as the receiving-locus is not explicitly named. Gap noted.


### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?**

Yes — Luk 7:47 establishes a constitutive movement: forgiveness received → love produced. The movement is from the state of being forgiven (relational-status change) through the inner being to the emergence of love-orientation. Jer 31:34 establishes: forgiveness → direct knowledge of God. The constitutional movement is from guilt-bearing (blocked relational condition) through forgiveness to freed inner access. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?**

Sequence: guilt-bearing (blocked relational state) → forgiveness received (status change) → love emerges (inner-being response) → capacity to extend forgiveness (characterological formation). The sequence is directional and productive — each stage generates the next. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-029, OBS-064-032.


### T2.2 — Soul-Level Location

**Prompt 1: Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?**

The soul (*nephesh*) is implicated through the experience of guilt-burden and its release. Psa 32 (contextual to the forgiveness corpus — "when I kept silent, my bones wasted away... my strength was dried up") describes the pre-forgiveness state of guilt at the soul-life level. The relief of forgiveness would correspondingly engage the soul. Not explicitly named in the anchor verses. OBS-064-013.

**Prompt 2: What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?**

If guilt (R073 — 51 shared verses, strongest co-occurrence signal) operates at the soul level, then forgiveness — which addresses guilt — necessarily engages the soul level as well. The guilt-forgiveness dyad suggests soul-level engagement for forgiveness by structural derivation. Gap noted. OBS-064-011.

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.**

Verse evidence is largely silent on explicit soul-faculty naming for forgiveness. Gap noted — the soul-level engagement is inferred from the guilt-forgiveness structural relationship rather than directly evidenced.


### T2.3 — Heart

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

Yes — the heart is explicitly named as the required locus of genuine forgiveness in the human horizontal mode. Mat 18:35 — "so also my heavenly Father will do to every one of you, if you do not forgive your brother from your heart." The heart-locus requirement distinguishes genuine heart-level release from mere verbal declaration. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: What does the heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's integrating function (knowing, willing, feeling, moral awareness) does this characteristic engage?**

Heart-location reveals that genuine forgiveness is not performative or verbal but must penetrate to the moral-evaluative and volitional centre of the person. The heart that genuinely forgives has released the claim at the level where claims are held — the inner being's moral accounting. A verbal release without heart-level release is assessed as insufficient (Mat 18:35 implies this by naming "from your heart" as the required depth). OBS-064-031.


### T2.4 — Mind

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

Yes — cognition is explicitly engaged. Jer 31:34 ("I will remember their sin no more") names a cognitive act on God's part: the deliberate non-remembering of forgiven sin. This is a divine cognitive act with inner-being consequence — the forgiven person stands before God without the cognitive-relational burden of their sin being "remembered" (actively held against them). OBS-064-017.

**Prompt 2: What does mind-location reveal — what aspect of the mind's function (thought, discernment, understanding) does this characteristic engage?**

Forgiveness enables cognition — specifically, Jer 31:34 establishes that forgiveness produces direct knowledge of God ("they shall all know me"). The forgiveness removes the guilt-barrier that impedes direct cognitive-relational access to God. Forgiveness therefore enables a specific cognitive outcome: knowing God. OBS-064-017.


### T2.5 — Other Soul Subsets

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind for this characteristic?**

The splagchnizomai (visceral compassion) in Mat 18:27 — the master's gut-response that precedes his forgiveness of the servant — names the somatic-affective faculty as the origin-point of the forgiving act. The body's visceral response (splagchnizomai — from the bowels/intestines) is named as the felt precondition of the forgiving decision. OBS-064-022 (which references DIM-064-SD002 on splagchnizomai as somatic precondition). Correlation with R023 compassion already noted.

**Prompt 2: If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?**

The somatic compassion faculty (splagchnizomai) reveals that forgiveness has an affective-somatic entry point — the felt movement of compassion in the body is the origin of the volitional act of releasing the claim. The inner-being logic: somatic compassion-response → volitional release-of-claim → act of forgiveness. The body is not incidental but the initiating site. OBS-064-022.


### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

The somatic vocabulary of splagchnizomai (Mat 18:27) is the nearest body-part link — the bowels/intestines as the origin of the compassion that drives forgiveness. Beyond this, the wound vocabulary of Isa 53:5 (shared anchor with R030 contrition) links the mechanism of forgiveness to somatic suffering. OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?**

Scripture grounds the forgiving act in somatic compassion (Mat 18:27) and the mechanism of atonement in somatic suffering (Isa 53:5; Eph 1:7 — "through his blood"). The body-link places forgiveness in visceral human reality — it is not a cold cognitive act but an act with a somatic precondition (compassion felt) and a somatic cost-mechanism (suffering borne). OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.


### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**Prompt 1: Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?**

The direction runs from somatic compassion inward and upward to the volitional act (Mat 18:27 — somatic response → forgiveness decision). In the atonement mechanism, the direction runs from somatic suffering outward to released debt-status in others (Isa 53:5; Eph 1:7). OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 2: What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?**

The somatic-to-inner direction (compassion felt in the body → inner volitional release) means genuine forgiveness has a somatic origin. A forgiveness that is purely cognitive/volitional without the somatic compassion-movement lacks the depth the verse evidence identifies as the genuine article. The body's felt response is not incidental to forgiveness but part of its generative structure. OBS-064-022.


### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly. This finding feeds directly into T5.7.**

Evidence is silent on constitutional body deposit from forgiveness. T5.7 to close formally.


### T2.9 — Origin and Source

**Prompt 1: Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally — is it generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally?**

Forgiveness originates in divine character — specifically the divine readiness/disposition (sal.lach — Psa 86:5) and the divine possession of forgiveness (se.li.chah — Psa 130:4, Dan 9:9). The constitutional origin of forgiveness is entirely from God in its vertical form; in the horizontal human form, it is derived from having received the vertical. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-019, OBS-064-020.

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?**

Singular at the divine origin level (divine character as the source), multiple at the triggering level: (1) receipt of divine forgiveness generates capacity to extend horizontal forgiveness; (2) somatic compassion-movement triggers the volitional forgiving act (Mat 18:27); (3) divine initiative precedes human response-seeking. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts evidenced in Scripture?**

Yes — the origin shifts across modes. Covenantal-objective: priestly ritual mechanism. Divine relational-personal: Jesus's sovereign declaration. Human horizontal: received forgiveness as the generating ground. Intercessory: God's own character as the appeal-ground. OBS-064-014, OBS-064-022, OBS-064-031.


## Inner faculties engaged

How this characteristic engages the eleven inner-being faculties: perception, cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, and relational capacity.


### T3.1 — Perception

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty — the inner senses including hearing, sight, taste, touch, smell, and spiritual discernment — and if so, which inner sense and how?**

Yes — perception is engaged in both the giving and receiving directions. In the giving direction: the offended person must perceive the magnitude of the offence to be released — the correct perception of what is owed is the prerequisite for knowing what is being released. In the receiving direction: Psa 130:4 establishes that encountering divine forgiveness as a quality produces reverential awe — a perceptive-affective response to what is perceived about God. OBS-064-020, OBS-064-021.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty in the person?**

Forgiveness deepens and opens perception — specifically perception of divine character. Psa 130:4 (forgiveness encountered → reverential fear produced) implies that the encounter with forgiveness as a divine quality sharpens perception of who God is. The Pharisee's limited love (Luk 7:47 — "forgiven little, loves little") implies limited perception of the magnitude of the forgiveness — perception of the gift's magnitude determines the love-response. OBS-064-020, OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with perception reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The pattern: perception of forgiveness's magnitude determines the love-response. Shallow perception → shallow love. Deep perception → deep love. Forgiveness both requires and deepens perception of divine character. The perceptive faculty is both the gate to receiving forgiveness and the beneficiary of having received it. OBS-064-029.


### T3.10 — Conscientiousness

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness — the integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action — and if so, how?**

Yes — conscientiousness is engaged in the commanded forgiveness ("forgive seventy times seven" — Mat 18:22). Sustained willingness to forgive is a form of integrated moral-volitional character. The person who forgives habitually from the heart has integrated the act into their moral character. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness in the person?**

Sustained forgiveness-giving deepens conscientiousness — it forms the integrated moral character of one who habitually releases claims rather than accumulates them. Sustained forgiveness-receiving deepens conscientiousness by producing the love-orientation (Luk 7:47) and reverential-fear posture (Psa 130:4) that inform subsequent moral attention. OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscientiousness reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Forgiveness is a formation vehicle for conscientiousness — habitual forgiveness-giving shapes the person toward integrated moral generosity; habitual forgiveness-receiving shapes toward love and reverence. The pattern reveals both directions as formative of conscientious character. OBS-064-029, OBS-064-031.


### T3.11 — Relational Capacity

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity — the constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person — and if so, how?**

Relational capacity is the primary faculty engaged by forgiveness — the characteristic is constitutively relational (it requires a breach to be released). Forgiveness received restores relational capacity to God (Jer 31:34 — direct knowledge); forgiveness given restores relational capacity to the horizontal party. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity in the person?**

Forgiveness restores and enables relational capacity: the guilt-barrier is the primary obstacle to relational access, and forgiveness removes it. Jer 31:34 makes this most explicit: the permanent forgiveness of the new covenant enables permanent direct relational knowledge of God. Withholding forgiveness actively degrades relational capacity — Mat 18:34 images the withholder as imprisoned, relational connection severed. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with relational capacity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The relational capacity engagement pattern confirms the dimension classification and adds precision to it:

Forgiveness is not merely a relational characteristic in the sense of occurring within relationships — it is a **relational capacity restorer and enabler**. Its primary function in the inner being is to remove what blocks genuine relational connection (guilt, grievance, withheld claims) and to generate the inner states (love, trust, openness) that make genuine connection possible.

This distinguishes forgiveness from other relational characteristics: where love, compassion, and mercy primarily build and sustain relational connection, forgiveness specifically addresses the **ruptures and blockages** that prevent connection. Its relational function is curative and restorative before it is generative.

The pattern also reveals the **dual direction** of relational capacity in forgiveness: toward God (the primary movement in the OT corpus and in the NT access-postures) and toward other persons (the commanded horizontal movement). Forgiveness uniquely operates both relational directions simultaneously — the same characteristic that restores the human-divine bond calls for the restoration of the human-human bond.


### T3.2 — Cognition

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty — knowing, understanding, discerning — and if so, how?**

Cognition is explicitly engaged. Jer 31:34 names a divine cognitive act ("I will remember their sin no more") and produces a human cognitive outcome ("they shall all know me"). Forgiveness involves a cognitive act on God's part and enables a cognitive capacity on the human's part. OBS-064-017.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition in the person?**

Forgiveness enables cognition — specifically, it removes the guilt-barrier to direct knowledge of God (Jer 31:34). It deepens self-cognition: accurate assessment of what one has been forgiven is the prerequisite for proportionate love-response (Luk 7:47). The withheld-forgiveness condition impairs cognition: the unforgiving withholder is cognitively trapped in accounting the offender's debt (Mat 18:23-35). OBS-064-017, OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with cognition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The pattern reveals forgiveness as a cognitive-enabler: it removes barriers to knowing God (Jer 31:34), requires accurate self-assessment of received forgiveness (Luk 7:47), and is distorted by the cognitive trap of retained claims (Mat 18). Forgiveness deepens and clarifies cognition in multiple registers. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-029.


### T3.3 — Memory

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty — the holding and retrieving of inner-being reality across time — and if so, how?**

Memory is explicitly engaged — uniquely. Jer 31:34 ("I will remember their sin no more") names a divine memory act as the content of the forgiveness. The phrase "remember no more" does not denote amnesia but the deliberate non-active-use of the remembered sin against the person — God's memory is the archive of the claim; forgiveness is God's decision not to use that archive against the forgiven. OBS-064-017.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory in the person?**

Forgiveness transforms memory in both directions. Divine: God's memory of sin is permanently set aside in forgiveness (Jer 31:34). Human: the act of forgiving requires the offended person to release the claim held in memory — not to deny the memory but to choose not to hold it against the offender. Unforgiveness is precisely the continuing active use of memory against the offender (the "tormentors" of Mat 18:34 image the inner state of one whose memory continues to exact the debt). OBS-064-017, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with memory reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The memory engagement pattern reveals that forgiveness is fundamentally concerned with **the management of the past in the present** — how the record of what was done is held, released, and reframed across time.

Forgiveness does not make the past event disappear; it changes the relational and cognitive status of the past event. "Remember no more" (Jer 31:34) does not mean the event did not happen — it means it will no longer be held against the person. The past is acknowledged (the legitimate claim is real) and then released (the claim is cancelled). Memory's role is precisely this: to hold the past in the present for relational accounting — and forgiveness is the act that resolves that accounting.

This reveals forgiveness as a **temporally-oriented** characteristic — it addresses the accumulation of the past (guilt, grievance, debt) and releases the person (and the relationship) from being determined by it. No other characteristic in the data addresses the past-in-the-present dynamic as directly as forgiveness. This is a new analytical framing not named in existing observations.


### T3.4 — Affect

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty — feeling and emotional experience — and if so, how?**

Yes — deeply. Love (agapē) is the named downstream affective state produced by forgiveness received (Luk 7:47). Reverential fear (awe) is produced by encountering forgiveness as a divine quality (Psa 130:4). The visceral compassion (splagchnizomai — Mat 18:27) is the named affective precondition for extending forgiveness. Forgiveness therefore engages affect at both ends: as a prerequisite (compassion producing the act) and as an outcome (love and reverence produced by receiving the act). OBS-064-020, OBS-064-021, OBS-064-022, OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect in the person?**

Forgiveness deepens affect in the receiving direction (love produced, Luk 7:47 — proportional to the depth of the forgiveness received) and deepens reverential fear (Psa 130:4). In the giving direction, forgiveness requires and expresses somatic compassion. Withholding forgiveness degrades affect — the torment-state of Mat 18:34 is an affective state of inner imprisonment. OBS-064-029, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with affect reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The pattern reveals affect as simultaneously prerequisite (compassion enabling the act) and product (love and reverence as downstream states) of forgiveness. Forgiveness is not a cold transactional act but one constitutively involving the affective faculty at every stage. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-029.


### T3.6 — Volition

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty — the capacity to choose — and if so, how?**

Yes — volition is constitutive of the forgiveness act. The commanded human forgiveness (Mat 18:35 — "from your heart") requires the volitional act of releasing the claim. G0863G group 5376-001 is assigned Dimension 04 (Volition) — the act of releasing attachments as a volitional decision. Forgiveness is both commanded and willed; it cannot be merely felt without being chosen. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition in the person — including its three aspects: capacity, interaction with other characteristics, and the constraints under which it operates?**

Forgiveness redirects volition — from claim-retention toward claim-release. The volitional act of forgiveness is the internal moment of choosing to release rather than retain. This is confirmed by the "from your heart" requirement: the will must genuinely release, not merely perform the verbal act. Sustained forgiveness-giving may progressively redirect the will toward generosity of relational posture. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with volition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Volition is the decisive inner-being act of forgiveness — the moment of release. Without it, forgiveness is verbal performance rather than inner-being reality. The pattern reveals forgiveness as constitutively volitional: it is an act that must be chosen, not merely a feeling that arises. OBS-064-031.


### T3.7 — Agency

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty — the capacity to act, initiate, and make happen — and if so, how?**

Yes — both the forgiving act (agentive release of claim) and the seeking-forgiveness act (intercession — Dan 9:19 — agentive plea on behalf of others) involve agency. Jesus' forgiveness pronouncements (Luk 7:47-48) assert divine agency explicitly — the controversy passages in the Synoptics dispute who has the authority (agency) to forgive sins. OBS-064-015, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency in the person?**

Forgiveness transforms agency: the one who forgives exercises agentive release of the claim (a positive exercise of power); the one who receives forgiveness has their agency freed — the guilt-barrier removed, enabling direct access to God (Jer 31:34) and capacity to worship (Mat 5:24). Withholding forgiveness traps agency in the holding of the claim (Mat 18:34 — the self-imprisonment of the withholder). OBS-064-017, OBS-064-028, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with agency reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Agency is both exercised (in forgiving) and liberated (in receiving forgiveness). The pattern: forgiveness frees the agency of the forgiven person for positive engagement (knowing God, worshipping, extending forgiveness to others). Unforgiveness traps the agency of the withholder in its own claim. OBS-064-028, OBS-064-032.


### T3.8 — Moral Evaluation

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty — the capacity to assess against a standard of right, wrong, good, and true — and if so, how?**

Yes — moral evaluation is intrinsic to forgiveness. The act of forgiving requires accurate assessment of the moral offence (what is being released) and the moral standing of the parties (the offended has a legitimate claim; the offender is genuinely guilty). Psa 130:4's reverential-fear response to encountering divine forgiveness involves the moral evaluative recognition that God does not owe forgiveness but gives it. OBS-064-020, OBS-064-021.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation in the person?**

Forgiveness deepens moral evaluation — it requires the offended person to accurately assess both the reality of the offence (not denying it) and the decision to release the claim regardless. It also requires the recipient to accurately assess the magnitude of the forgiveness received (Luk 7:47 — proportionate love requires proportionate assessment). OBS-064-029.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with moral evaluation reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

The pattern: forgiveness requires the most precise moral evaluation the inner being is capable of — accurate assessment of what was owed, what is being released, and what has been received. Shallow moral evaluation produces shallow forgiveness and shallow love. Deep moral evaluation enables deep forgiveness and deep love. OBS-064-029.


### T3.9 — Conscience

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the conscience — the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — and if so, how?**

Yes — conscience is profoundly engaged. The pre-forgiveness guilt state (OBS-064-013 — referenced from the forgiveness corpus context) is the conscience's verdict on the offender's condition. 1Jo 1:9 (confession → forgiveness → cleansing) shows conscience delivering the verdict (confession = conscience's public acknowledgement) and forgiveness addressing it. OBS-064-013, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience in the person?**

Forgiveness received addresses and resolves conscience — the conscience that condemned is relieved by the forgiveness that clears the verdict. Hebrews 9:14 (contextual — "how much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our conscience") makes this explicit: the atoning blood directly purifies the conscience. OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscience reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Conscience is both the faculty that makes forgiveness necessary (by delivering the guilt-verdict) and the primary beneficiary of forgiveness received (the conscience purified by atonement). The pattern reveals forgiveness as constitutively conscience-engaging: it is the answer to conscience's question about moral standing. OBS-064-028.


## Relational interfaces

How this characteristic operates across the relational interfaces of the human person — God-to-human, human-to-God, human-to-human (giving / receiving / boundaries), and the spiritual beings interface.


### T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?**

Yes — the primary and dominant relational direction in this registry. God is the exclusive subject of H5545 (salach). The NT forgiveness pronouncements (Luk 7:47-48, Mar 2:5) are divine-to-human. Eph 1:7 ("through his blood, the forgiveness of our trespasses") names the divine mechanism. Col 2:13 ("having forgiven us all our trespasses") — divine agentive act. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — is it conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?**

God extends forgiveness on the basis of divine character and atoning mechanism, not human merit. Dan 9:19 ("for your own sake") grounds the intercessory plea entirely in God's own character. Eph 1:7 grounds forgiveness in Christ's blood — the divine provision, not human performance. The access-postures (faith, repentance, confession) are receptive conditions, not earning conditions. OBS-064-016, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 3: What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?**

God's extension of forgiveness reveals a divine disposition of deliberate claim-release — choosing to absorb the cost of the offence rather than exacting it. Psa 86:5 (sal.lach alongside goodness and steadfast love) reveals forgiveness-readiness as a constitutive divine character quality — not a reluctant exception but a stable attribute. OBS-064-019.


### T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?**

Yes — the seeking-forgiveness direction (human seeking divine forgiveness) is richly evidenced: Dan 9:19 (Daniel's intercession), Psa 130:4 (waiting for the Lord with hope for forgiveness), 1Jo 1:9 (confession → forgiveness), Act 2:38 (repentance for forgiveness). OBS-064-015, OBS-064-020, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?**

The inner posture required for the human-to-God direction: dependence (Dan 9:9 — grounding the plea in God's character, not human merit), honest self-assessment (1Jo 1:9 — confession names the condition accurately), and hope in divine forgiveness-character (Psa 130:4 — waiting for the Lord because forgiveness is with him). The posture is constitutively non-merit-claiming. OBS-064-016, OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 3: What does the human-to-God direction of this characteristic reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

The human-to-God direction reveals the person as constitutively dependent on divine forgiveness for restored relational access. The act of seeking forgiveness names the person's honest position: one who owes a debt they cannot pay and appeals to the one who can cancel it. This is a specific form of the Dependence/Creatureliness dimension — not general creaturely dependence but specifically moral-debt dependence. OBS-064-022.


### T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another — and if so, how does it operate in that giving?**

Yes — commanded horizontal forgiveness is richly evidenced. Mat 18:35 (forgive from the heart), Luk 17:3-4 (if your brother sins, rebuke him; if he repents, forgive him — seven times a day), Mat 18:22 (seventy times seven). Eph 4:32 ("forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you"). OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: What inner conditions or orientations in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?**

Inner conditions enabling genuine horizontal extension: (1) Having received divine forgiveness (Mat 18:23-35 structural logic — the servant who was forgiven much should forgive much); (2) Heart-level engagement (Mat 18:35 — "from your heart"); (3) Somatic compassion movement (Mat 18:27 — splagchnizomai as the felt origin of the forgiving decision). OBS-064-022, OBS-064-031, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 3: What does the evidence reveal about what the person must have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?**

Mat 18:23-35 makes this structurally explicit: the servant who genuinely received the master's forgiveness (the forgiven-10,000-talents state) would have been capable of extending forgiveness to the fellow servant (the owed-100-denarii claim). The inability to extend reveals that the vertical forgiveness was not genuinely received at the heart level. Prior receipt of vertical forgiveness is the enabling condition for extending horizontal forgiveness. OBS-064-032.


### T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another — and if so, how does it operate in that reception?**

Yes — horizontal forgiveness-receiving is implied throughout the commanded-forgiveness passages. Luk 17:3-4 presents both sides of the horizontal transaction: the one who sins and the one who forgives — implying the one who sins receives the forgiveness. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another person?**

Conditions for receiving horizontal forgiveness: repentance appears in Luk 17:3-4 ("if he repents, forgive him") as the named condition for horizontal forgiveness. This is structurally parallel to the divine direction (repentance as access-posture). Beyond repentance, the conditions for receiving horizontal forgiveness are not developed in the registry's anchor verses. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 3: What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic from another but does not receive it?**

Mat 18:23-35 presents the paradigm — the servant who received forgiveness but could not extend it is the structural failure case. The inner state of one who encounters horizontal forgiveness and cannot receive it would mirror the blocked-reception pattern. The verse evidence does not develop this directly. Gap noted. OBS-064-032.


### T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries

**Prompt 1: Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance or difference?**

Yes — covenantal context is constitutive. The OT forgiveness corpus is entirely covenantal (Israel before YHWH). The new covenant (Jer 31:34) is the most complete forgiveness statement. Mal 2:14 (shared anchor with R062 fellowship) joins the covenantal and relational dimensions explicitly. OBS-064-012, OBS-064-017.

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?**

The primary forgiveness scope is covenantal — within the covenant relationship with YHWH (OT) or through Christ (NT, Eph 1:7). The NT extends the scope through the universal offer — Act 10:43 ("everyone who believes in him receives forgiveness of sins") — making the covenantal access theoretically universal. The scope is not ethnically restricted in the NT. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 3: What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic — who is included and who is not?**

Scope: (1) Vertical — everyone who believes/repents/confesses (NT scope); (2) Horizontal — "your brother" (Luk 17:3 — relational community scope); (3) Intercessory — the community on whose behalf one intercedes (Dan 9:19 — national scope); (4) The outer limit — the unforgivable sin (Mar 3:29 — the one case of permanent exclusion). Scope expands from individual to national but reaches an absolute limit at the unforgivable sin. OBS-064-027.


### T4.6 — Spiritual Beings Interface

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial — and if so, how?**

The unforgivable sin (Mar 3:29 — blasphemy against the Holy Spirit) involves the Spirit as the agent whose role in forgiveness is being denied. This is the clearest evidence of forgiveness operating in relation to a spiritual being — the Holy Spirit is the one who mediates the forgiveness transaction, and the one who blasphemes him places himself outside the forgiveness arc. OBS-064-027.

**Prompt 2: Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity — something that can be attacked, distorted, or weaponised by adversarial spiritual powers?**

Mar 3:29's outer limit (blasphemy against the Holy Spirit) implies adversarial resistance to the Spirit's mediating role in forgiveness. Whether this involves a spiritual-beings adversarial structure is not developed in the registry's data. Gap noted. OBS-064-027.

**Prompt 3: Is this characteristic communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry in the evidence?**

Joh 20:22-23 ("receive the Holy Spirit; if you forgive the sins of any, they are forgiven") explicitly names the Spirit as the enabler of the authority to declare forgiveness. The Spirit strengthens and enables the forgiveness transaction — this is the clearest evidence of a spiritual being's positive role in the forgiveness interface. OBS-064-027.


## Formative and developmental dimension

How this characteristic shapes the person over time — nature of transformation, sequence of inner states, mechanism of change, suffering and affliction, formation and sanctification, eschatological trajectory, deposit consequence.


### T5.1 — Nature of Transformation

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person — and if so, does it change the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both?**

Both. Condition: forgiveness received transforms the person's standing before God (from guilt-bearing to guilt-free — the forensic condition changes). Orientation: the sustained receipt of forgiveness produces love-orientation (Luk 7:47) and reverential fear (Psa 130:4) as stable inner dispositions. OBS-064-029, OBS-064-020.

**Prompt 2: Is the transformation produced by this characteristic reversible or irreversible in the verse evidence?**

Jer 31:34 presents new covenant forgiveness as irreversible in its divine declaration ("I will remember their sin no more"). The eschatological dimension is permanent. In the human horizontal direction, individual acts of forgiveness may need to be renewed (Luk 17:4 — "seven times a day"). But the divine forgiveness declaration is structurally permanent once given in the new covenant frame. OBS-064-017.


### T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person — a before, during, and after?**

Sequence: (1) Offence/guilt present (prior condition); (2) Access-posture adopted (faith, repentance, confession — reception-preparation); (3) Forgiveness received (status-change); (4) Love-response generated (Luk 7:47); (5) Capacity to extend forgiveness formed (Mat 18:35 structural logic); (6) Extended forgiveness to others (horizontal expression). For the new covenant: steps 3 and 4 produce permanent new conditions (Jer 31:34 — known by God, sins remembered no more). OBS-064-017, OBS-064-022, OBS-064-029, OBS-064-032.

**Prompt 2: What are those states, and what does the sequence reveal about how this characteristic works?**

The sequence reveals forgiveness as a relational-transitional event — it moves the person from one inner condition (guilt-bearing, blocked access) through the act to a different inner condition (love-oriented, access-freed). Forgiveness is not a terminal state but a transitional act that produces further inner states. The "torment" of the unforgiver (Mat 18:34) reveals the alternative sequence when the transition is blocked: retained claim → inner imprisonment. OBS-064-001, OBS-064-029, OBS-064-032.


### T5.3 — Mechanism of Change

**Prompt 1: What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change in the person — discipline, encounter, gradual formation, sudden transformation, or something else?**

Two mechanisms: (1) Declaratory mechanism — the forgiveness pronouncement effects a status change. God's declaration ("your sins are forgiven") or the human's inner release creates the new condition. (2) Atoning mechanism — the blood of Christ (Eph 1:7) is the structural ground that enables the declaration. The atonement mechanism is the basis; the declaration is the application. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 2: Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?**

The mechanisms differ across modes: covenantal-objective mode uses the ritual-atonement mechanism (external, structural). Divine relational-personal mode uses the declaratory mechanism grounded in Christ's atonement. Human horizontal mode uses the heart-level-release mechanism (internal, volitional). OBS-064-014, OBS-064-022, OBS-064-031.


### T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering or affliction — as a response to it, a product of it, or a context for it?**

Yes — forgiveness is structurally linked to suffering. The atoning mechanism (Eph 1:7 — "through his blood"; Heb 9:22 — "without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness") grounds forgiveness in suffering on the forgiver's side. The somatic compassion (splagchnizomai — Mat 18:27) is a suffered response in the forgiver's body. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 2: Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce this characteristic in the person?**

Suffering is the cost-side of forgiveness on the forgiver's part. The divine cost is the highest: the death of Christ (Eph 1:7). The human cost is the absorbed wrong: the forgiver absorbs the cost of the offence rather than extracting it. Suffering does not produce forgiveness mechanically but is structurally necessary to it — the release of the claim always involves the claim-holder absorbing what was owed. OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.


### T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic participating in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification — shaping the person over time toward greater likeness?**

Yes — the commanded forgiveness ("seventy times seven" — Mat 18:22) positions forgiveness as a formation discipline. Habitual forgiveness-giving forms the character of the forgiver toward generosity of relational posture. The Eph 4:32 command ("forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you") places horizontal forgiveness within the sanctification framework of corporate Christian life. OBS-064-031.

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?**

Forgiveness functions in sanctification as both a discipline and a formation vehicle: the habitual act of releasing claims shapes the person toward the divine model (Eph 4:32 — "as God in Christ forgave you"). The person who habitually forgives is being progressively formed into the image of the divine forgiver. OBS-064-031.


### T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence point this characteristic toward an eschatological fullness — a future state toward which its present operation is oriented?**

Yes — Jer 31:34 is explicitly eschatological: the permanent new covenant forgiveness is the eschatological state in which sins are permanently released and the guilt-barrier is permanently removed. Col 2:13 ("having forgiven us all our trespasses") names the already-accomplished aspect of this eschatological forgiveness. OBS-064-017.

**Prompt 2: What does the present experience of this characteristic anticipate about its future fullness?**

Present forgiveness-received anticipates the permanent guilt-free standing before God (Jer 31:34 — "remember their sin no more"). Present love-orientation (Luk 7:47) anticipates the fullness of love in the eschatological condition. The present acts of horizontal forgiveness (Mat 18:22) anticipate the eschatological community in which relational breaches are fully and finally resolved. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-029.


### T5.7 — Deposit Consequence

**Prompt 2: Does the evidence indicate generational consequence — a deposit carried forward beyond the individual?**

Although T5.7 is closed on the body-deposit question, the generational dimension warrants a separate note because the data touches it obliquely:

DIM-064-SD015 raises the question of **corporate forgiveness** — whether a community's receipt of divine forgiveness has collective inner-being dimensions. Daniel's intercession (Dan 9:19) is on behalf of the nation; Moses intercedes for the nation (Num 14:19 referenced in DIM-064-SD015). The communal scope of forgiveness in the OT intercessory corpus implies that forgiveness can be received at a corporate level — and what is received corporately may carry generational consequence.

The new covenant promise (Jer 31:34) is addressed to the covenant community — "the house of Israel and the house of Judah" — and its scope ("from the least to the greatest") has a generational or at least corporate breadth.

*Gap noted:* Whether corporate receipt of forgiveness produces a generational deposit in the community (a different question from the body-deposit question of T2.8) is not examined in the data. This is a gap that DIM-064-SD015 identifies as a lower-priority Session D question.


## Synthesis — what T2 through T5 reveal as a whole


### Inter-tier syntheses

**SYN-INTER-064-008 | T1, T2** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1 establishes forgiveness as an act; T2 maps where the act is constitutionally located. The four modes (T1) each engage different constitutional levels (T2): covenantal-objective mode engages no named inner-being locus (external mechanism); divine relational-personal mode engages the full constitutional range (spirit, heart, cognition — via the access-postures of faith, repentance, confession); human horizontal mode explicitly requires the heart (Mat 18:35); intercessory mode requires the dependence-posture at the constitutional level of the plea. The somatic compassion origin-point (T2.5 — splagchnizomai) is the constitutional expression of the volitional-release act (T1.4.1 — the act must be chosen). Definition and constitutional location are co-determining: the mode of forgiveness determines which constitutional level is engaged.

**SYN-INTER-064-009 | T1, T3** *(outcome: D; structural: enabling)*

T1's four modes explain T3's faculty engagement pattern. The covenantal-objective mode (no inner-being prerequisite) corresponds to T3's absence of any faculty requirement for that mode. The divine relational-personal mode (faith, repentance, confession as access-postures) engages volition, conscience, and cognition. The human horizontal mode ("from your heart") requires volition, conscience, and moral evaluation. The intercessory mode requires the dependence-posture that engages cognition (accurate assessment of the grounds of the plea — God's character, not human merit) and volition. T1's definition of forgiveness as transitional (not terminal) is confirmed by T3's finding that every faculty engaged by forgiveness is deepened toward further engagement — not closed by the act but opened by it.

**SYN-INTER-064-010 | T1, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's four modes map directly onto T4's relational directions. Covenantal-objective mode = God-to-human (cultic ritual mediated); divine relational-personal = God-to-human (personal declaratory); intercessory = human-to-God on behalf of others; human horizontal = human-giving and human-receiving. The vertical-horizontal interdependence (T4 — the structural claim that receiving divine forgiveness enables extending human forgiveness) is the relational expression of T1's layered modes (the horizontal mode assumes the divine relational-personal mode has operated). T1's structural negative (retained claim = inner torment) is T4's receiving-blocked condition (Hos 4:17 parallel — wrong prior alignment blocks reception of right offering; Mat 18:35 — unforgiveness calling into question whether divine forgiveness was genuinely received).

**SYN-INTER-064-011 | T1, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: sequential)*

T1 establishes forgiveness as transitional (not terminal); T5 confirms this and maps the formation arc. T1's downstream states (love, reverence) are T5's immediate formation products. T1's sustained conditions (freed access to God, enabled worship, capacity to extend) are T5's longer-arc formation outcomes. T1's eschatological orientation (Jer 31:34 as the forward-pointing fullness) is T5's eschatological telos. The two mechanisms in T5 (declaratory and atoning) correspond to T1's two primary mode-types: the divine relational-personal mode operates through the declaratory-atoning mechanism; the human horizontal mode operates through the heart-level-release mechanism. T1 and T5 together reveal the complete arc from definition to formation endpoint.

**SYN-INTER-064-014 | T2, T3** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional locations map onto T3's faculty engagements. The heart-locus (T2.3 — Mat 18:35) corresponds to T3's volition, moral evaluation, and conscience faculties — all heart-level. The cognition-locus (T2.4 — Jer 31:34) corresponds to T3's cognition and memory faculty engagements. The somatic-compassion locus (T2.5 — splagchnizomai) corresponds to T3's affect faculty at its somatic depth. The constitutional movement (T2.10 — guilt-bearing → forgiveness → love) is the sequence in which T3's faculties are engaged: conscience identifies the guilt → volition releases the claim → affect produces the love-response. Constitutional location determines faculty engagement sequence.

**SYN-INTER-064-015 | T2, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: causal)*

T2's constitutional origin (divine character as the source — Psa 86:5, Psa 130:4) explains T4's God-to-human directional dominance. Because forgiveness originates exclusively in divine character at the deepest level, the primary relational direction is necessarily God-initiated toward the human. T2's heart-locus requirement (Mat 18:35) explains T4's giving-direction requirement: genuine horizontal forgiveness requires the same constitutional depth as the received vertical (heart-level in both cases). The somatic-compassion origin (T2.5) is the constitutional enabler of the horizontal-giving direction (T4.3): the person who genuinely gives forgiveness does so from a somatic-affective inner origin (splagchnizomai), not merely a verbal performance.

**SYN-INTER-064-016 | T2, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional movement (guilt-bearing → forgiveness → love → capacity to extend) is T5's formation sequence described at the constitutional level. T2's finding that the constitutional event produces further inner states (love, direct access to God) is T5's transitional-formative account. The body-deposit gap (T2.8 — data silent; T5.7 closed) means the formation is entirely inner-constitutional — the formative event leaves no somatic residue, which is consistent with T5's finding that forgiveness is transitional rather than permanently marking. The eschatological telos (T5.6 — Jer 31:34) is the permanent constitutional state that present forgiveness-events anticipate.

**SYN-INTER-064-019 | T3, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: enabling)*

T3's faculty engagements all point in the same relational directions that T4 identifies as primary. Conscience (T3.9) is engaged in both the seeking-forgiveness direction (human-to-God: confession as conscience's public verdict) and the giving direction (human horizontal: the conscience that recognises the offence to be released). Memory (T3.3) is engaged in the God-to-human direction (divine "remember no more" — Jer 31:34) and the human-giving direction (releasing the memory's active use against the offender). Volition (T3.6) drives the giving direction (heart-level release is chosen). The vertical-horizontal interdependence (T4 structural claim) is the relational expression of T3's conscience-and-volition faculty account: the person whose conscience has accurately received the magnitude of divine forgiveness has the volitional capacity to extend horizontal forgiveness.

**SYN-INTER-064-020 | T3, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: sequential)*

T3's faculty deepenings (cognition enabled — Jer 31:34; love produced — Luk 7:47; conscience purified — Heb 9:14; volition redirected) are T5's formation products described at the faculty level. The formation sequence (T5) is the longitudinal account of which T3's faculty engagements are the synchronic snapshots. The commanded forgiveness as sanctification discipline (T5.5 — Eph 4:32) is the formation vehicle through which the volitional faculty (T3.6) is habituated and the moral evaluation faculty (T3.8) is deepened. T3 and T5 together reveal the full formation arc: T3 shows what happens to each faculty in the moment of forgiveness; T5 shows what sustained habitual forgiveness does to the person over time.

**SYN-INTER-064-023 | T4, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: sequential)*

T4's vertical-horizontal interdependence (the structural claim that receiving divine forgiveness enables extending human forgiveness) is T5's formation arc described at the relational level. The formation sequence (T5 — forgiveness received → love generated → capacity to extend formed) is the inner development through which T4's structural interdependence is created: the person who genuinely receives divine forgiveness and generates the love-response (T5) is the person who has the genuine volitional capacity to extend horizontal forgiveness (T4). The eschatological telos (T5.6 — Jer 31:34) is also T4's permanent relational state: the guilt-barrier permanently removed produces permanently restored relational access to God.
