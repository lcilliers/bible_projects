## Lexical and semantic analysis (T7.1)

**T7.1.1 — What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms for this characteristic — and what do their root meanings reveal?**

H5545 (sa.lach — verb): to forgive; exclusive divine subject. H5546 (sal.lach — adjective): forgiving as divine character; Psa 86:5 sole occurrence. H5547 (se.li.chah — noun): forgiveness as divine possession; Psa 130:4, Dan 9:9, Neh 9:17. G0859 (afesis — noun): forgiveness as state/condition; predominantly in "forgiveness of sins" phrase. G0863G/H/I: three sub-entries of aphiēmi ("to release: leave"; "to release: forgive"; "to release: permit"). Root meaning H5545: uncertain etymology — a technical term for divine forgiveness with no clear concrete root image. Root meaning G0863: apo (away from) + hiēmi (to send/let go) = to send away, release a hold. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-004, OBS-064-005.

**T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term (noun, verb, adjective, participle) — and what does that range reveal about how the characteristic operates?**

H5545 grammatical range: Qal (active — God forgives), Niphal (passive — "they shall be forgiven" — the worshipper as receiver in the cultic context). The Niphal passive is the covenantal-objective mode's grammatical form — the worshipper receives the declared status without being named as the active agent. G0863 (aphiēmi) range: active (forgive, permit, leave), passive (be forgiven). G0859 (afesis) is a state-noun — the condition produced by the act. The grammatical range distinguishes: divine act (Qal H5545, active G0863H) vs creaturely received condition (Niphal H5545, G0859 noun). OBS-064-003, OBS-064-005.

**T7.1.3 — What is the semantic range of the primary term — across what breadth of meaning does it operate?**

Semantic range: (1) Divine cultic declaration (H5545 Niphal — "they shall be forgiven" in the atonement formula); (2) Divine character attribute (H5546 — God's dispositional readiness); (3) Divine possession (H5547 — forgiveness as quality God has); (4) State of being forgiven (G0859); (5) Personal declaratory forgiveness (G0863H — "your sins are forgiven"); (6) Commanded horizontal forgiveness (G0863H — human commanded to release claim); (7) Releasing attachment (G0863G — leaving family, goods for discipleship); (8) Permitting/granting access (G0863I); (9) Withholding permission (G0863I — moral obstruction). OBS-064-003, OBS-064-009, OBS-064-010.

**T7.1.4 — Does the vocabulary include terms that distinguish distinct aspects of this characteristic — disposition versus act, received versus given, condition versus quality?**

The vocabulary distinguishes: disposition (H5546 — God's character-readiness to forgive) vs act (H5545 — God's performing the act) vs possession (H5547 — the quality God has). Act vs state: G0863H (forgiving act) vs G0859 (state of having been forgiven). Given (God's act — Qal H5545) vs received (human's condition — Niphal H5545, G0859). The grammar is precise about who holds and who receives the initiative. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-005, OBS-064-019.

**T7.1.5 — Does the vocabulary include a term for the structural opposite or absence of this characteristic?**

The unforgivable sin (Mar 3:29 — G0863H negative form with "never," "eternal condemnation") is the explicit outer-limit negative. The structural negative of forgiveness is unforgiveness in the withholder (Mat 18:34-35 — the tormentors-state). Both are present: the lexical outer-limit negative (Mar 3:29) and the inner-being consequence of withheld forgiveness (Mat 18:34). OBS-064-027, OBS-064-032.

**T7.1.6 — Does the vocabulary include a person-type term — a term for the one who habitually possesses or exercises this characteristic?**

H5546 (sal.lach — adjective) names the person-type: "you, O Lord, are good and forgiving (sal.lach)" — God as the dispositional forgiver. G2844-adjacent: no specific "forgiver" person-type term but the agent is named by the active verb construction. The state-noun G0859 (afesis) identifies the one in the state of forgiveness. The person-type vocabulary is asymmetric: the Forgiver (divine, uniquely designated by H5546) and the forgiven (the one in the G0859 state). OBS-064-019.

**T7.1.7 — Does the vocabulary include a supplication or seeking term — a term for the act of seeking this characteristic from another?**

Yes — multiple supplication/seeking terms. Dan 9:19 ("O Lord, forgive" — direct supplication using H5545 imperative). Act 2:38 (Peter's command to "be baptized for the forgiveness of your sins" — which responds to the crowd's "what shall we do?" as supplication). 1Jo 1:9 (confession as the seeking-act). The seeking of forgiveness is a rich vocabulary event — petition, confession, intercession. OBS-064-015, OBS-064-022.

**T7.1.8 — What does the LXX use of the vocabulary reveal about continuity or development of this characteristic across the Testaments?**

The LXX applied aphiēmi/afesis to render OT forgiveness vocabulary (H5545 → aphiēmi in the cultic passages), and the NT continues this rendering — extending it to commanded human practice. The cross-testament development is the use of the same Greek vocabulary for both divine and human forgiveness, which is not present in the Hebrew (where H5545 is exclusively divine). This lexical bridging is analytically significant: the NT does not create a new vocabulary for human forgiveness but applies the divine-forgiveness vocabulary to the commanded human act. OBS-064-006.

**T7.1.10 — What does the full vocabulary arc reveal about this characteristic's complete semantic range?**

The vocabulary arc reveals forgiveness as a characteristic with two distinct lexical domains — a divine-exclusive Hebrew core (H5545 — salach) and a structurally shared Greek vocabulary (aphiēmi — used for both divine and human forgiveness). The arc moves from OT restriction (forgiveness is exclusively divine) to NT expansion (the same act is commanded of humans) through lexical bridging. This arc is unique in the programme: forgiveness is the one characteristic where the cross-testament vocabulary development carries an explicit structural claim — that human forgiveness is shaped by and modelled on divine forgiveness. OBS-064-003, OBS-064-006.


## Verse and literary interpretation (T7.2)

**T7.2.1 — What is the function of this characteristic's primary term within its primary verse — what role does it play in the sentence and argument?**

Psa 130:4 — H5547 (se.li.chah noun) functions as the ground of hope: "there is forgiveness with you, that you may be feared." The noun functions as the asserted divine quality on which the psalmist's hope rests. The function is declaratory-of-divine-character rather than describing an act already performed or requesting one. OBS-064-020.

**T7.2.2 — What literary form carries the primary verse evidence (narrative, psalm, wisdom, prophecy, epistle, apocalyptic) — and what does that form require for responsible interpretation?**

Literary forms: (1) Cultic/legal corpus — Lev 4:20, Num 15:25 (atonement formula); (2) Prophetic promise — Jer 31:34 (new covenant), Isa 53:5 (servant); (3) Penitential psalm — Psa 130 (waiting for forgiveness), Dan 9:19 (intercession); (4) Synoptic narrative — Mat 18:23-35, Luk 7:36-50 (forgiveness in encounter and parable); (5) Apostolic letter — Eph 1:7, Col 2:13, Eph 4:32. Each literary form carries a distinct mode: cultic = objective declaration; prophecy = eschatological promise; psalm = seeking posture; narrative = relational encounter; epistle = theological ground and ethical command. OBS-064-014.

**T7.2.3 — What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence — what are the premises and conclusions?**

Luk 7:47 argument structure: "her many sins have been forgiven — that is why she loved much; but one who is forgiven little loves little." The causal logic: magnitude of forgiveness → proportional love. This is a diagnostic-interpretive argument: Jesus uses the love visible in the woman's act to reveal the magnitude of her forgiveness. The argument structure is: love-visible → forgiveness-inferred; forgiveness-magnitude → love-magnitude (bidirectional inference). OBS-064-029.

**T7.2.4 — What contextual setting carries the primary verse evidence (judicial, liturgical, covenantal, communal, eschatological) — and what does that setting reveal?**

Settings: Cultic-legal (authoritative declaration within covenant ritual), Prophetic-eschatological (new covenant promise), Penitential (seeking posture — deepest human approach to God), Parabolic-narrative (encounter and conflict — Synoptic forgiveness controversies), Apostolic-theological (grounding and commanding). The parabolic-narrative setting carries the most developed inner-being analysis of forgiveness (Mat 18 — the withholder's inner torment; Luk 7 — the love-response). OBS-064-014.

**T7.2.5 — Does any verse function as the primary anchor for this characteristic — the verse that most fully and directly expresses its essential character?**

Jer 31:34 is the architecturally complete verse — it names the divine action (I will forgive), the comprehensive scope (their iniquity), and the permanent cognitive-relational consequence (remember their sin no more). Psa 86:5 is the most complete divine character statement (good and forgiving and abounding in steadfast love). Luk 7:47 is the most developed inner-being consequence statement. No single verse holds all three — the primary anchor trio is Jer 31:34, Psa 86:5, and Luk 7:47. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-019, OBS-064-029.

**T7.2.6 — What does the primary anchor verse reveal that no other verse reveals?**

Jer 31:34 uniquely reveals forgiveness as the ground of permanent direct knowledge of God — the guilt-barrier's removal is the prerequisite for unmediated divine-human cognition. No other verse makes this cognitive-relational consequence this explicit. Luk 7:47 uniquely reveals the love-production as quantitatively proportional to the forgiveness received — a causal mechanism visible nowhere else in the corpus. Psa 86:5 uniquely attests the adjectival person-type designation for the divine forgiver — the sole occurrence of sal.lach in the OT. OBS-064-017, OBS-064-019, OBS-064-029.


## Somatic dimension — body significance, direction, deposit

How (and whether) the characteristic is grounded in the body — whether Scripture links it to specific body parts, the direction of body↔soul interaction, and any constitutional deposit produced over time.


### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

The somatic vocabulary of splagchnizomai (Mat 18:27) is the nearest body-part link — the bowels/intestines as the origin of the compassion that drives forgiveness. Beyond this, the wound vocabulary of Isa 53:5 (shared anchor with R030 contrition) links the mechanism of forgiveness to somatic suffering. OBS-064-022.

**Prompt 2: If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?**

Scripture grounds the forgiving act in somatic compassion (Mat 18:27) and the mechanism of atonement in somatic suffering (Isa 53:5; Eph 1:7 — "through his blood"). The body-link places forgiveness in visceral human reality — it is not a cold cognitive act but an act with a somatic precondition (compassion felt) and a somatic cost-mechanism (suffering borne). OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.


### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**Prompt 1: Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?**

The direction runs from somatic compassion inward and upward to the volitional act (Mat 18:27 — somatic response → forgiveness decision). In the atonement mechanism, the direction runs from somatic suffering outward to released debt-status in others (Isa 53:5; Eph 1:7). OBS-064-022, OBS-064-028.

**Prompt 2: What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?**

The somatic-to-inner direction (compassion felt in the body → inner volitional release) means genuine forgiveness has a somatic origin. A forgiveness that is purely cognitive/volitional without the somatic compassion-movement lacks the depth the verse evidence identifies as the genuine article. The body's felt response is not incidental to forgiveness but part of its generative structure. OBS-064-022.


### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly. This finding feeds directly into T5.7.**

Evidence is silent on constitutional body deposit from forgiveness. T5.7 to close formally.
