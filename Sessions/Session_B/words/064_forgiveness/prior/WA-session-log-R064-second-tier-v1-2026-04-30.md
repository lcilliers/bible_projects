## 7. Key Findings — Substantive

### 7.1 Confirmed and structured (consistent with prior analysis)
The following prior findings were confirmed, structured, and in several cases given more precise analytical form than existed in the Session B output:

- Forgiveness as constitutively relational — dimension classification confirmed and grounded across all tiers
- The vertical-horizontal structural interdependence (F-003) — confirmed and extended with constitutional movement sequence
- The guilt-forgiveness structural pairing as the strongest co-occurrence signal (54 shared verses)
- The OT-to-NT developmental arc: salach-root divine exclusivity → aphiēmi shared human-divine act
- The four bases of divine forgiveness (covenantal, character-based, responsive, Christological) as a historical trajectory toward increasing unconditionality
- The heart-locus requirement (Mat 18:35) as the standard of genuine forgiveness
- The causal chain: forgiveness received → love produced (Luk 7:47) — confirmed as the primary inner-being causal statement in the corpus

### 7.2 New findings (not previously assembled)

Seventeen new findings were surfaced and flagged across the analysis. In priority order for programme significance:

1. **Seven-step constitutional movement sequence (T2.10):** Divine disposition → divine act → Spirit mediation → soul reception → soul transformation → soul exercise → bodily expression. The full sequence assembled for the first time.

2. **Forgiveness as primary conscience-resolution characteristic (T3.9):** Forgiveness addresses what conscience has registered, resolves what it holds. The 54-verse guilt co-occurrence is the structural confirmation. This framing — forgiveness as the primary conscience-resolution characteristic — is a new analytical synthesis.

3. **Three-stage memory arc (T3.3):** Record held (pre-forgiveness) → claim terminated (at the moment of forgiving) → memory of forgiveness as ongoing motivational ground (post-forgiveness). The temporal dimension of forgiveness through memory has not been assembled in this form.

4. **Hub position in motivational economy (T3.7):** Forgiveness receives compassion as motivational input and generates love/reverence/hope as motivational outputs. It occupies a hub position — both product and producer within the inner motivational system.

5. **Communicative/performative mode (T1.4):** Forgiveness as speech act that constitutes the relational reality it names — not merely announces it. Identified as a distinct fifth mode of operation not named in prior observations.

6. **Distinction between revocable formal receipt and irrevocable genuine receipt (T5.1):** The reversibility of forgiveness's transformation depends on the depth of reception. Mat 18:35 (revocation) and Jer 31:34 (non-recallable) are evidence for different depths of receipt.

7. **Threefold eschatological anticipation (T5.6):** Present forgiveness anticipates (1) complete knowing of God without barrier; (2) comprehensive redemption without remainder; (3) community wholeness without relational rupture. Not previously assembled.

8. **Aphiēmi root as cross-registry connector (T6.4):** The root formally connects forgiveness (G0863H) to calling/discipleship (G0863G as leaving-act) at the root level. The zero XREF count reflects a database gap, not the absence of the connection.

9. **Grace-mercy-forgiveness as dimensional cluster (T6.7):** All three share 3 analytical dimensions. Their distinctions are of specificity not kind — forgiveness as the specific moral-debt instance within the broader dispositional categories of grace and mercy.

10. **Dual role in formation arc (T5.5):** Forgiveness is simultaneously the repeating ground (received divine forgiveness renewing the foundation) and the repeated practice (extended human forgiveness forming the character).

11. **Image-bearer condition spectrum (T0.3):** From expressed (forgiveness active) → suppressed (withheld) → permanently foreclosed (Spirit permanently rejected). Not assembled in prior analysis.

12. **Body feedback direction via splagchnizomai (T2.7):** The gut-level compassion (Mat 18:27) is a body-to-soul feedback that generates the motivational impulse from which the volitional act of forgiving flows. Both somatic directions evidenced.

13. **Bidirectional perceptive effect (T3.1):** Received forgiveness may deepen spiritual perception; misunderstood or withheld forgiveness impairs it.

14. **Conscience as soul-subset location (T2.5):** The tight guilt co-occurrence implies conscience as the implicit soul-subset location — the faculty that holds the debt-charge that forgiveness resolves.

15. **Four-mechanism map by context (T5.3):** Declarative encounter, cosmic atonement, cultic ritual, gradual formation — each linked to specific covenant/literary contexts.

16. **Immediate vs. sustained effect distinction (T1.6):** Immediate response is an inner-being movement (love, fear, blessedness); sustained effect is the constitutional state established (afesis condition, love-orientation as disposition, hope as forward orientation).

17. **Self-forgiveness as unexamined dimension (T7.3):** The afesis corpus may carry evidence for inward self-directed forgiveness. Not examined in current data.
