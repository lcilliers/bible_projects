## Key Analytical Findings

The session produced a richer picture than the registry's Dimension Review framing had anticipated. The most significant findings by stage:

**Structure of mercy (Stage 2b T1):**
Q&A-017 established that mercy has four structurally distinct elements — felt disposition (*oiktirmos/racham*), atoning mechanism (*kipper/kaphar/hilasmos*), petition-response dynamic (supplication vocabulary), and spatial locus (*kapporet/hilastērios*). These are not synonyms but distinct facets of the same reality operating in different modes.

**Constitutional location (Stage 2b T2):**
Q&A-062 established that the human person's capacity to extend mercy is constitutionally dependent on prior reception of divine mercy — receiving before generating. Q&A-065 established triadic constitutional movement: soul→body (breast-beating, mercy-seeking), body→soul (blood mediating atonement to *nephesh*), and spirit-soul interface→relational expression. Q&A-075 established an asymmetric memory structure: divine mercy operates through wilful forgetting (Heb 8:12); received mercy activates human remembering (Eze 16:63). God no longer holds the record; the person is left holding the memory of what was forgiven, now in the register of wonder rather than accusation.

**Faculty engagement (Stage 2b T3):**
Q&A-072 established that mercy enables dual cognition — upward (access to divine knowledge, Dan 2:18) and downward (accurate self-knowledge, Eze 16:63) simultaneously. Q&A-092–093 established that mercy-seeking presupposes a functioning conscience (the cry for mercy names the inner condition accurately) and that received mercy deepens rather than bypasses conscience. Q&A-099 established mercy as the foundational enabler of relational capacity — not one relational characteristic among others but the mechanism by which genuine encounter, community membership, and compassionate extension become possible after failure.

**Relational interfaces (Stage 2b T4):**
Q&A-101–102 confirmed divine mercy operates on four simultaneous bases — sovereignty, covenant, compassion-motivation, and responsive movement — none exclusive, all perspectives on the same act. Q&A-103 confirmed the grace-supplication root architecture: *chanan* (God gracious) and *techinah* (human supplication) share the same root, encoding mercy's dialogic structure at the vocabulary level.

**Disposition and mechanism (Stage 2b M-009 / §N resolution):**
Q&A-199 resolved DIM-111-001: the relationship between mercy as divine inner disposition and atonement as structural mechanism is causal — the disposition produces the mechanism. Psa 78:38 is the decisive verse: "being compassionate, he atoned." The inner character of compassion motivates the atoning act; the mechanism does not generate the disposition.

**Cross-registry vision (Stage 2a, Stage 2b T6):**
Mercy occupies a structurally integrative position — it bridges the guilt complex (R073, 75 shared verses), the compassion complex (R023, 76 shared verses), the power-authority cluster (R187/196/197/198, all in top 25 co-occurrence), and the wisdom-character formation complex (Jam 3:17, shared anchors with R117/R125/R066). This integrative position was the dominant finding of T6.
