## 4. Key Findings

### New findings (selected high-priority)

1. **Conscience-contrition pairing** (T3.9): The most significant new finding. Contrition is the inner-being condition that arises when the conscience's witness is received rather than suppressed. Conscience provides the content; contrition enables conscience to reach constitutional depth. Together they constitute the complete inner-being response to moral failure — cognitive witness (conscience) + affective-constitutional reception (contrition). Session D significance: high.

2. **The double divine character** (T0.1): The holy-and-near-to-the-low character of God — assembled as the unified basis for what contrition images in the human person. The transcendence that produces contrite collapse and the nearness that revives the contrite are not in tension but are aspects of the same divine character.

3. **Typological inversion in Isa 53:5** (T0.4): The contrition vocabulary deployed typologically — human contrition (sinner crushed by moral condition) → Servant's vicarious crushing (innocent crushed for sinners) — with the direction of guilt inverted. Named here as typological for the first time.

4. **Contrition as threshold and gate** (T5.2): Contrition's position in the transformation sequence is the constitutive middle stage — neither the beginning (crushing/confrontation) nor the end (divine revival and restoration) but the threshold through which divine reviving reaches the inner being. The hardened posture (Jer 44:10) skips stage 2 and therefore never reaches stage 4.

5. **Constitutional four-level scope** (T2.10): Spirit, heart, soul, and body all appear in the verse evidence across the three modes. The depth of constitutional engagement varies with the severity of the crushing — a severity-to-depth pattern inferred from the verse distribution.

6. **Vertically relational by nature** (T3.11, T4.3–T4.4): Contrition has no constitutive giving or receiving direction between persons (T4.3 and T4.4 entirely silent). This is structurally consistent — contrition is the inner-being condition proper to asymmetric creature-Creator relationship. This may be unique or near-unique in the programme.

7. **Vocabulary self-containment** (T6.4): R030's crushing vocabulary has zero shared OWNER terms with other registries. Conceptual relationships with adjacent characteristics (repentance, brokenness, fear, pride) are established through verse-level co-occurrence and shared anchors rather than shared vocabulary. Boundaries must be analytically drawn rather than lexically found.

## 5. Gaps Identified

A new SD pointer should be raised for Session D: the vocabulary-level evidence for the conceptual connection between contrition and repentance is weaker than the descriptive claim implies. This needs cross-registry investigation at verse level.
