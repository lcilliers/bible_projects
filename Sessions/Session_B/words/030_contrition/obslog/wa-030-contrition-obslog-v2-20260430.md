**Q&A-013 | T1.1.1**
- Tier: T1 — Definition
- Component: T1.1 — Name and Naming
- Prompt: 1 — What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name signal about its essential nature?
- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status tag: A
- Notation: *Consistent with prior analysis*
- Answer: The programme name is "contrition" — from Latin *contritus* (ground to pieces). The name signals inner dissolution, not merely sorrow: contrition is the inner being reduced to its most basic, broken state. The Hebrew vocabulary confirms this: the primary roots (dkh — to crush/be crushed; ktt — to beat fine; nkh — to smite/strike) all name a physical process of reduction by external force. The name immediately signals passivity (being crushed rather than choosing to be sad), depth (reduction, not surface regret), and physical metaphor (the inner being treated as something solid that can be pulverised). OBS-030-007, OBS-030-015, OBS-030-016.
- Anchor verses: Psa 51:17, Isa 57:15
- Finding type: MEANING_OBSERVATION

- Disposition: ANSWERED
- Status tag: A
- Notation: *Consistent with prior analysis*
- Answer: Three root families: (1) dkh (H1792–H1794) — to crush/be crushed; generates both the verbal process (H1792) and the adjectival settled state (H1793A dak.ka — contrite; H1793B dak.ka — dust/pulverised). At the definitional level these terms reveal that contrition is: a passive condition (received, not self-generated), physically grounded (crushing, pulverising, striking), and expressed in settled adjectival states (the contrite person is the one who has been and remains crushed).
