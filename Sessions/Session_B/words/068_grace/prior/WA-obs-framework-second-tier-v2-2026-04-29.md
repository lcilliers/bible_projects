## Purpose and Design

The observation framework operates in two tiers.

The **first tier** defines the eight major analytical domains — the areas that together constitute a complete characterisation of any inner-being characteristic. It is documented in `WA-obs-framework-first-tier-v1_1-2026-04-29.md`.

The **second tier**, documented here, defines the constituent components within each first-tier domain. Each component is a named analytical area — a distinct aspect of the domain that must be examined for every word studied. The components together ensure that no analytical area is missed and that every word is examined on the same footing, enabling cross-word comparison and programme-level synthesis.

The governing prompts that operationalise each component in the analysis phase are maintained separately in the question catalogue (`WA-obs-question-catalogue-v2-2026-04-29.json`). This document carries the conceptual and methodological description of each component — what it is, why it matters, and what it is examining.

---

## Design Principles

**Universal applicability.** Every component applies to every word studied. Where a word has no evidence for a component, the finding is "not evidenced" — a complete and informative answer. Silence is data. A consistent pattern of silence across words on a given component is itself an analytical finding.

**Top-down derivation.** Components are derived from the first-tier scope descriptions — from the framework downward, not from individual word analyses upward. This ensures the component set is genuinely universal and not shaped by the profile of any single word.

**Separation of structure and operation.** This document defines what is examined (the components). The question catalogue defines how it is examined (the prompts). Both are required for complete analytical discipline; they are maintained separately because they serve different audiences and different systems.

**Cross-tier integrity.** Where a finding in one tier feeds into another, the relationship is made explicit. T2.8 (constitutional deposit) feeds into T5.7 (deposit consequence). T1 is visited twice — at the start and end of analysis. These cross-tier relationships are part of the methodological design, not incidental connections.

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

*The purposive frame. T0 asks why this characteristic exists — what it reveals about the nature of God whose image the person bears, and what created purpose it serves in the human person. Present throughout the programme; comes fully into its own in Session D.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T0.1 | Divine Nature Reflected | What this characteristic reveals about the nature or character of God — whether Scripture explicitly attributes it to God, and what that attribution or its absence reveals about the characteristic's place in the divine image |
| T0.2 | Created Purpose | What purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as designed — what it equips the person to be, do, or become; whether it belongs to the original created design, to the response to the fallen condition, or to both; and whether it is oriented toward a future fullness |
| T0.3 | Image-Bearer Expression | How this characteristic specifically expresses or instantiates the divine image in the human person — what aspect of being made in God's likeness it instantiates, and whether it is shared between God and the human person or is a distinctly creaturely analogue |
| T0.4 | Typological Significance | Whether and how Scripture deploys this characteristic typologically — using it to point toward or participate in realities that transcend the individual and the immediate; and the direction of that typology where present |

**Scope note:** T0 operates at two levels. At the level of the individual characteristic (addressed in Session B), it asks what this characteristic reveals about the divine image and created purpose. At the programme level (Session D), it asks what the full constellation of characteristics reveals about what it means to be made in the image of God. The second-tier components here address the individual-characteristic level only. The programme-level question is the governing concern of Session D.

## T1 — Definition

*A definition in the making. T1 establishes Name, Kind, and Boundary at the beginning of analysis and is revisited at the conclusion to receive what the research has found. The definition grows in precision and depth as research progresses.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T1.1 | Name and Naming | What this characteristic is called — the primary Hebrew and Greek terms — and what the name itself signals about essential nature at the definitional level, before deeper lexical analysis begins |
| T1.2 | Kind | What type of inner-being phenomenon this characteristic appears to be — an act, disposition, condition, quality, or something else; simple or internally complex; described as a working synthesis encapsulating constitutional location, faculties engaged, and impact on the person |
| T1.3 | Boundary | What this characteristic is not — the structural opposite that stands against or excludes it, and the limits of its operation; sharpens the description by contrast |
| T1.4 | Modes of Operation | The distinct ways this characteristic operates within and through the inner person — including communicative or speech-based modes where evidenced |
| T1.5 | Immediate Response | The first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering or receiving this characteristic |
| T1.6 | Sustained Effect | What this characteristic produces in the inner being over time — the states, qualities, capacities, and orientations established by sustained possession or exposure |
| T1.7 | Conditions of Reception | What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic; what conditions block, distort, or prevent reception; and what is the state of the person who encounters it but does not receive it |
| T1.8 | Dimension Classification | The primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary, with supporting evidence; secondary dimensions where present |

**Scope note:** T1 is visited twice — at the start of analysis (working description from current knowledge) and at the conclusion (updated to reflect what the research has found). T1.1 (Name) is the most stable element; T1.2 (Kind) and T1.3 (Boundary) are explicitly held open and reframeable. T1 does not ask where the characteristic is located, through which faculties it operates, how it relates to others, or how it develops. Those questions belong in T2–T6.

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

*T2 maps a characteristic onto the constitutional structure of the human person — identifying where it is seated, where it originates, and what Scripture is doing when it links the characteristic to constitutional elements and body parts.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T2.1 | Spirit-Level Location | Whether and how this characteristic is located or originates at the spirit level — the deepest level of the person and the primary site of encounter with God |
| T2.2 | Soul-Level Location | Whether and how this characteristic is a soul-level phenomenon — the seat of the faculties and their expression into innermost personal experience |
| T2.3 | Heart | Whether and how this characteristic engages or is located in the heart — the integrating centre of knowing, willing, feeling, and moral awareness |
| T2.4 | Mind | Whether and how this characteristic engages or is located in the mind — the faculty of thought and discernment within the soul |
| T2.5 | Other Soul Subsets | Whether the verse evidence surfaces soul-level locations beyond heart and mind — held open where the evidence surfaces them, not forced |
| T2.6 | Body — Significance | What Scripture is doing when it links this characteristic to a body part — whether the link is emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating |
| T2.7 | Body — Direction | Which direction the body-characteristic relationship runs — soul expressing through body, body feeding back to soul, or both — and the consequence of that directionality |
| T2.8 | Body — Deposit | Whether sustained operation of this characteristic leaves a constitutional deposit in the body or its design, including DNA and generational consequence |
| T2.9 | Origin and Source | Where this characteristic originates constitutionally — generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally; whether the origin is singular or multiple; and whether origin changes across contexts |
| T2.10 | Constitutional Movement | How this characteristic moves across constitutional levels — spirit to soul, soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions — and the sequence or pattern of that movement |

**Scope note:** Constitutional locations are not physical organs. The body and its structures are the design layer through which the constitutional person interfaces with the physical world. A characteristic may be expressed through the body without being housed there. T2 maps the constitutional what — where and from where. The why and how of origin, reception, and operation are examined in T3, T4, and T6 respectively. T2.8 cross-references T5.7 — the deposit mechanism is T2; the developmental consequence is T5.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

*T3 examines which of the person's eleven inner faculties a characteristic engages — and how. The faculties are the constitutional equipment the person brings to every inner-being encounter; they are not locations and are not themselves characteristics under study.*

| Component | Faculty | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T3.1 | Perception | The inner senses — five inner-sense analogues (hearing, sight, taste, touch, smell) plus a sixth faculty of spiritual discernment or intuition, provisionally seated at the spirit level |
| T3.2 | Cognition | Knowing, understanding, discerning — transcending the body; not housed in the brain |
| T3.3 | Memory | The temporal depth of the inner person — holding and retrieving inner-being reality across time; distinct from present cognition |
| T3.4 | Affect | Feeling and emotional experience |
| T3.5 | Creativity | The faculty of origination — encompassing imagination and the capacity to bring into being what did not exist; grounded in the image of the Creator God |
| T3.6 | Volition | The God-given capacity to choose — constitutionally given; examined across three aspects: capacity, interaction with other characteristics, and operating constraints |
| T3.7 | Agency | The capacity to act, initiate, trigger, and make happen — distinct from volition, which precedes it |
| T3.8 | Moral Evaluation | The capacity to assess against a standard — right, wrong, good, true |
| T3.9 | Conscience | The acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — distinct from moral evaluation as the broader faculty |
| T3.10 | Conscientiousness | The integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action |
| T3.11 | Relational Capacity | The constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person |

**Scope note:** For each faculty, T3 asks three things: whether this characteristic engages the faculty and how; whether it enables, deepens, bypasses, or impairs the faculty; and what the pattern of engagement or non-engagement reveals about the nature of the characteristic. The prompt structure is uniform across all eleven faculties — this ensures cross-word comparability of faculty engagement. Where a word does not engage a faculty, "not evidenced" is the complete and informative answer. The pattern of non-engagement across words is itself an analytical finding.

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

*T4 examines how a characteristic operates across the three relational interfaces of the human person — with God, with other humans, and with other spiritual beings. Direction matters: the divine and human interfaces each operate in two directions.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T4.1 | Divine Interface — God to Human | How this characteristic operates from God toward the human person — the basis on which God extends it (conditional, unconditional, covenantal, responsive) and what that reveals about God's disposition toward the person |
| T4.2 | Divine Interface — Human to God | How this characteristic operates in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, and covenant; and the inner posture required for that movement |
| T4.3 | Human Interface — Giving | How the person who possesses or has received this characteristic extends it toward others — and the inner conditions that enable genuine extension |
| T4.4 | Human Interface — Receiving | How the person receives this characteristic from another — and what inner conditions enable or block reception from a human source |
| T4.5 | Human Interface — Boundaries | Whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance; within covenantal contexts versus across covenantal boundaries; and what the evidence reveals about the relational scope of this characteristic |
| T4.6 | Spiritual Beings Interface | How this characteristic operates in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial; whether it is a site of adversarial activity; and whether it is communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry |

**Scope note:** The spiritual beings interface (T4.6) is currently underrepresented in the programme's evidence base. Where the verse evidence is silent on this interface, that silence is noted explicitly — it is not treated as evidence of absence. T4 asks how a characteristic operates across relational interfaces. It does not ask where it is constitutionally located (T2), through which inner faculties it operates (T3), or how it develops the person over time (T5).

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

*T5 examines how a characteristic shapes the person over time — the developmental arc, the mechanism of change, the relationship to suffering and formation, and the eschatological trajectory.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T5.1 | Nature of Transformation | Whether this characteristic changes the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both; and whether the transformation is reversible or irreversible |
| T5.2 | Sequence of Inner States | The progression of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person — the before, during, and after |
| T5.3 | Mechanism of Change | How this characteristic produces change — through discipline, encounter, gradual formation, sudden transformation, or other means; and whether the mechanism differs across contexts |
| T5.4 | Suffering and Affliction | The relationship between this characteristic and suffering or affliction — whether suffering deepens, tests, reveals, or produces this characteristic in the person |
| T5.5 | Formation and Sanctification | How this characteristic participates in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification — shaping the person over time toward greater likeness |
| T5.6 | Eschatological Trajectory | The ultimate direction this characteristic points toward — what present experience of this characteristic anticipates about its future fullness |
| T5.7 | Deposit Consequence | The developmental consequence of the constitutional deposit identified in T2.8 — what sustained operation produces over time and generationally; closed if T2.8 found no deposit |

**Scope note:** T5 is distinct from T3 (which asks what inner capacities are engaged) and T4 (which asks how the characteristic operates relationally). T5 asks about the developmental arc — the person before, during, and after the characteristic has done its formative work. T5.7 receives directly from T2.8 — where T2.8 identifies no deposit, T5.7 is closed with an explicit note.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

*T6 maps a characteristic within the wider landscape of the inner person — identifying connections, dependencies, sequences, overlaps, and distinctions with other characteristics in the programme.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T6.1 | Co-occurrence | Which characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence — and what the pattern of co-occurrence reveals |
| T6.2 | Sequential Relationships | Whether this characteristic consistently precedes, follows, or accompanies others in a sequence — and whether the sequence is causal, developmental, or correlational |
| T6.3 | Causal and Constitutive Relationships | Whether this characteristic produces another, is produced by another, or constitutes part of another — and the mechanism of that relationship |
| T6.4 | Vocabulary and Root Sharing | Which characteristics share vocabulary terms or root architecture with this one — and what that sharing reveals about conceptual relationships |
| T6.5 | Distinctions | Where apparent overlap with another characteristic requires careful differentiation — identifying the precise boundary and whether it is one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level |
| T6.6 | Shared Verse Anchors | Whether any verse functions as a primary anchor in both this and another characteristic's study — and what the shared anchor reveals |
| T6.7 | Dimensional Sharing | How many of this characteristic's confirmed analytical dimensions are shared with another characteristic — and what the pattern of sharing reveals |

**Scope note:** T6 asks how this characteristic relates structurally to other characteristics. It does not ask how it operates within the person (T2, T3) or how it develops the person over time (T5). T6 findings are particularly significant for Session D synthesis — the inter-characteristic map built across T6 work is the foundation for programme-level integration.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

*T7 is the epistemological basis underlying all other tiers. It defines where the knowledge of a characteristic comes from and how it is derived with integrity. T7 does not produce substantive findings — it validates and grounds the findings produced by T0–T6.*

| Component | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T7.1 | Lexical and Semantic Analysis | Original language vocabulary — Hebrew and Greek terms, root meanings, grammatical forms, semantic range, canonical development, LXX bridging, NT coinage — the primary linguistic evidence base from which all findings across every tier are drawn |
| T7.2 | Verse and Literary Interpretation | How the characteristic functions within its verse evidence — the word's role in the sentence, logical structure of the argument, literary form (narrative, psalm, prophecy, wisdom, epistle, apocalyptic), and contextual setting (judicial, liturgical, covenantal, communal, eschatological) |
| T7.3 | Human Science Frameworks | Psychology, moral philosophy, developmental psychology, sociology, anthropology, and related disciplines — applied as interpretive lenses and sanity checks; illuminate and organise what Scripture describes; do not add content |

**Scope note:** T7 is qualitatively different from T0–T6. The other tiers define the body of understanding; T7 defines the epistemological foundation from which understanding in every other tier is derived and validated. Human science engagement is selective and practical — not exhaustive. The programme draws on these frameworks where they help ensure completeness and coherence; it does not attempt to replicate specialist scholarship in each discipline.
