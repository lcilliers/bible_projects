## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

### T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected

**Prompt 1:** What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?

**Status: A**

Rom 8:32 (anchor 888-001) reveals God as one who does not spare his own Son but gives — giving at supreme personal cost as the defining expression of divine character. Eph 2:8 establishes grace as sovereign, unmerited divine disposition. Exo 33:17 (anchor 889-001) reveals grace grounded in prior divine knowing and personal address. The divine character reflected: one who gives freely to the undeserving, whose giving is not exhausted by cost, and whose favour is rooted in personal knowledge of the recipient.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds structure: the prompt clarifies that what is reflected is not merely generosity but the combination of sovereign freedom, personal knowing, and uncaused bestowal.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and what does that attribution reveal?

**Status: A**

Yes — extensively. G5485 charis in group 888-001 names grace as "sovereign, unmerited divine disposition." H2580 chen in 889-001 names "divine relational favour." G5487 charitoō (Luk 1:28 anchor) names God's act of bestowing grace as a completed act that transforms the recipient's standing. The attribution reveals that when grace appears in the human person — as character quality (889-003), as forgiving act (5470-002), as communicative quality (SD022) — it is always derivative: the human expression of grace is a secondary reflection of the divine original.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds structure: the derivative character of human grace is made explicit as a T0-level claim.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest?

**Status: S (inverted)**

The data contains no evidence of Scripture being silent about God and grace. Grace is among the most extensively attributed divine characteristics in the corpus (412 total verses, 194 OWNER active). The absence of silence is itself significant: grace is categorically a divine reality first, and its human occurrence is always secondary.

*New finding as a category: for Grace, the silence-question inverts — the characteristic is so thoroughly divine in attribution that the question of divine silence does not apply. This is a T0-level marker not all characteristics will share.*

### T0.2 — Created Purpose

**Prompt 1:** What purpose does this characteristic serve in the human person as created?

**Status: A**

Eph 2:8: grace as the means through which the person is constituted as saved. 1Cor 15:10: grace as the condition through which the person becomes what they are — identity-constituting. Rom 5:2: grace as the standing in which the person lives. Act 20:32: grace as the constructive force through which the inner person is built up. Together: a coherent four-part functional picture — access (standing before God), identity (becoming someone), formation (being built up), extension (giving what has been received to others).

*Adds structure: prior analysis addressed these functions separately across SD pointers. The prompt consolidates them under a single created-purpose heading.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Is this characteristic part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?

**Status: P**

The tension is present but not resolved. Grace as unmerited giving presupposes a recipient who has no claim — consistent with both creaturely status before God (the creature always receives; it cannot give to God from its own resources) and fallen status. SD008 (Eph 2:1-5: grace addressing the dead) and SD032 (grace inhabiting weakness) both point toward the fallen condition as the primary context. But whether grace as the relational disposition of the greater toward the lesser belongs to the original created order is not directly resolved.

*Gap identified: the question of grace in the original created order — before the fall, by virtue of being a creature — is not addressed in the prior analysis. This is a genuine T0 gap.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness?

**Status: A**

Yes. SD027 (Eph 2:7): "in the coming ages he might show the immeasurable riches of his grace in kindness toward us." The present experience of grace is real but partial; its fullness is eschatological. SD031 (Rom 5:2) and SD005 (hope and grace co-constituting the same inner standing) confirm the trajectory.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD027, SD005, SD031). Adds structure: scattered eschatological observations consolidated under a single T0 category.*

### T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression

**Prompt 1:** In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person?

**Status: A**

When the human person exercises grace — in forgiving (5470-002: charizō carrying both giving and forgiving), in speech quality (charis as gracious words, Luk 4:22, Col 4:6), in character disposition (889-003: gracious disposition expressed in conduct) — they express the likeness of the one whose primary characteristic is to give freely to the undeserving. The image-bearing dimension is the capacity for uncaused giving: giving not triggered by the recipient's merit but by the giver's disposition.

*Adds structure: prior analysis addressed grace-as-human-act primarily in relational terms. The prompt frames it as an image-bearing capacity — a distinct T0-level claim.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Is this characteristic shared between God and the human person, or a creaturely analogue?

**Status: A**

The data is explicit. charizō (G5483) carries both the divine act of giving (God graciously giving all things, Rom 8:32) and the human act of forgiving (Eph 4:32). The same term names both. This is not analogy — it is lexical identity. SD018 raises this precisely: when God forgives, is God doing the same inner-being thing as when God gives? The data does not resolve the question but confirms the lexical identity.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD018). Adds precision: lexical identity is a stronger image-bearing claim than mere resemblance.*

---

**Prompt 3:** What does the presence or absence of this characteristic reveal about the condition of the divine image?

**Status: P**

SD043 names acharistos (graceless, ungrateful) as the structural antithesis — the inner failure to register grace as grace. SD004 establishes pride as the counter-condition. SD033 (Eph 2:8) suggests surrender of self-contribution is the necessary inner condition for grace-reception. Together: where grace is absent, the image is impaired — the capacity for uncaused giving suppressed by pride, self-sufficiency, or ingratitude.

*Gap identified: the existing analysis addresses counter-conditions but does not frame them explicitly as image-impairment. The prompt surfaces this as a T0 observation not previously named.*

### T0.4 — Typological Significance

**Prompt 1:** Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically?

**Status: A**

Yes. SD023: charitoō is a biblical coinage — created for Luk 1:28 (Mary as the one graced), pointing toward the one who is "full of grace and truth" (Joh 1:14, SD042). SD038 reads Zec 12:10 as prophetic-typological: the outpouring of the spirit of grace is eschatological and christological, occasioned by looking on "him whom they have pierced."

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD023, SD038, SD042). Adds structure: these are named as typological deployments.*

---

**Prompt 2:** What is the direction of the typology?

**Status: A**

Divine-to-human. The divine instance establishes the pattern; the human instance is patterned on it. Eph 4:32: "forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you." SD026 (Zec 12:10: even the human response to grace is grace-given) establishes the most radical form of this directionality.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds category: directional patterns previously noted without naming them as a typological question.*

---

**Prompt 3:** If no typological use evidenced, note explicitly.

**Status: N** — typological use is evidenced; prompt 3 does not apply.

## T1 — Definition

### T1.1 — Name and Naming

**Prompt 1:** What is this characteristic called and what does the name signal?

**Status: A**

Grace. The name signals immediately: not merit, not debt, not obligation. The English "grace" carries both the relational sense (favour, goodwill) and the aesthetic sense (gracefulness, beauty) — both present in the Hebrew and Greek vocabulary (chen as both favour and beauty/charm, Prov 31:30; charis as both grace and gracious quality in speech). The name orients the enquiry before analysis begins.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds observation: the name itself encodes the uncaused character — a T1-level naming observation not previously drawn out.*

---

**Prompt 2:** What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level?

**Status: A**

H2580 chen (favour) signals relational standing — being seen with favour by one who has power to grant or withhold. H8469 tachanum (supplication) signals the receiving side — the posture of the creature appealing for what it cannot command. G5485 charis signals the full semantic arc. Together the primary terms reveal a two-faced definitional structure: the disposition of the giver (chen, charis) and the posture of the receiver (tachanum). The vocabulary embeds the relational architecture before analysis begins: grace requires both a giver and a receiver oriented toward each other.

*Adds structure: the two-faced definitional structure (giver/receiver) as a T1-level observation — the vocabulary encodes the relational architecture at first encounter.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications?

**Status: A**

Yes — directional and relational. The name orients the enquiry toward: a direction of movement (from the greater to the lesser — SD024 encodes this in the CHEN root); a relational structure (giver and receiver — grace is not solitary); a constitutional implication (the receiver is constitutionally dependent). The name does not foreground constitutional location, faculty engagement, or developmental arc — it foregrounds the relational event.

*Adds structure: prior analysis noted these implications separately. The prompt consolidates them as an orienting observation.*

### T1.2 — Kind

**Prompt 1:** What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be?

**Status: A**

All four kinds — and this is analytically significant. SD021's four semantic faces of charis establish that grace is: a divine **disposition** (face 1: 888-001); a **condition** created in the recipient (face 2: 888-002 — "this grace in which we stand"); a **capacity** gifted into the person (face 3: 888-004); a **quality** expressed outward in character and speech (face 4: 889-003). Grace is not reducible to any single kind. It is a disposition that creates a condition that produces a capacity that expresses itself as a quality — the four faces are sequential and constitutive, not parallel.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021). Adds structure: the prompt frames the four-face finding as a Kind question — a T1 observation.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Is this characteristic simple or internally complex?

**Status: A**

Internally complex at first encounter. The CHEN root family already reveals constituent structure: chen (disposition), chanan (the act of being gracious), chinnam (the logic of uncaused giving), tachanum/techinah (the receiving posture). The Greek similarly: charis, charizō, charitoō each name a distinct operation. The registry's 11 verse context groups confirm — each addressing a distinct operational face.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds structure: complexity is confirmed at first encounter from vocabulary alone.*

---

**Prompt 3:** What is the current best working description?

**Status: P**

The registry header provides: "Grace is undeserved gift — the giving of what is needed not because it has been earned but because the giver chooses to give it... Grace creates the recipient rather than rewarding them." Strong on relational and dispositional character but does not yet encapsulate constitutional location or faculty engagement. The sb_classification gives "Spirit-soul interface" as working constitutional placement.

*Gap identified: the Kind description is strong but incomplete — T2/T3 findings are required before the full working description can be written. Expected at the current stage.*

### T1.3 — Boundary

**Prompt 1:** What is the structural opposite of this characteristic?

**Status: A**

Multiple structural opposites are named in the data. The primary structural opposite is the logic of merit. SD043 names acharistos (G0884 — graceless, ungrateful) as the lexically embedded opposite. SD004 establishes pride (Reg 123) as the inner-being opposite. SD033 (Eph 2:8) establishes merit-claim as the operative counter-condition. Three distinct faces: acharistos (failure to register grace as gift), pride (elevation of self making grace unnecessary), merit-logic (the framework within which grace cannot operate).

*Adds structure: prior analysis addressed these in different SD pointers. The prompt consolidates them as Boundary — the structural opposite has three faces, each excluding grace differently.*

---

**Prompt 2:** What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?

**Status: A**

Grace explicitly excludes: debt (chinnam — for nothing, without cause); merit (Eph 2:8: "not your own doing; it is the gift of God"); self-sufficiency (2Cor 12:9: grace operates in weakness, not strength); ingratitude (acharistos — receiving the gift without registering it as gift); earning as a framework (wages, not grace — the NT merit/gift distinction).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds organisation.*

---

**Prompt 3:** What is this characteristic not — where does it end?

**Status: P**

The data identifies important but unresolved boundaries. Grace is not the same as chesed (SD025 proposes: chen initiates, chesed sustains). Grace is not the same as love (SD013: the boundary question is raised but not resolved). Grace is not mercy (proximity noted, not formally distinguished). Grace is not justice (SD047: open question). These boundary questions require Session D to resolve — the individual registry can identify the boundaries but cannot draw them precisely.

*Gap identified: the precise boundary between grace and its nearest neighbours is raised but not resolved. Boundary is one of the T1 elements that will be rewritten when Session D analysis is complete.*

### T1.4 — Modes of Operation

**Prompt 1:** In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?

**Status: A**

At least six distinct modes: (1) Sovereign bestowal — grace given from above, constituting the recipient's standing (888-001, 889-001); (2) Relational standing — the condition of being-in-grace (888-002: "this grace in which we stand"); (3) Enabling power — animating force of ministry and identity (888-004; 1Cor 15:10); (4) Forgiving act — release of debt patterned on divine forgiveness (5470-002); (5) Character quality — gracious disposition expressed in speech and conduct (889-003); (6) Supplication posture — the appeal of the creature to the greater for what it cannot supply (890-001).

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021, 11 verse context groups). Adds structure: six named modes — more complete than SD021's four-face model. Modes 4 (forgiving), 5 (character quality), and 6 (supplication) were not explicitly included in SD021's sequential model.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?

**Status: A**

Yes — direction is determinative. Grace operates differently depending on whether direction is God-to-human (constituting, bestowing, enabling), human-to-God (appealing, supplicating), or human-to-human (forgiving, expressing, extending). The sb_classification "Spirit-soul interface" suggests operation across constitutional levels — confirmed in SD021's sequential model and SD042 (incarnation as supreme somatic expression).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds structure: direction as the primary mode-determining variable is now explicit at T1 level.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation?

**Status: A**

Yes — explicitly. charis as gracious speech quality appears in Luk 4:22 ("gracious words") and Col 4:6 ("speech seasoned with grace"). SD022 identifies this as the chen/grace-as-speech-quality sense carried into Greek. SD036 adds Act 20:32: "the word of his grace, which is able to build you up" — grace operating constructively through proclaimed speech. The communicative mode is integral, not peripheral.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD022, SD036). Adds structure: the communicative mode confirmed at T1 level as a distinct and integral mode.*

### T1.5 — Immediate Response

**Prompt 1:** What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering this characteristic?

**Status: A**

The data surfaces multiple immediate responses — and they are counterintuitive. SD031 (Luk 1:28-30): the first response to grace-announcement is **fear**, not joy. SD038 (Zec 12:10): at the eschatological outpouring the first response is **mourning** — grief at the cost of what has been given. SD039 (Ruth 2:10): the first response to unexpected grace is **prostration** — the body enacting humility before articulation is possible. Fear, mourning, and prostration are the three documented immediate responses in the anchor evidence — all preceding joy, peace, or thanksgiving.

*New finding as a consolidated observation: prior analysis noted these responses in individual SD pointers but did not draw them together as a T1 pattern. The pattern is analytically significant: the immediate inner response to grace is consistently creaturely recognition (fear, grief, prostration) before positive states settle.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Is the immediate response consistent or does it vary?

**Status: P**

Variation with a common underlying structure. The responses differ (fear, mourning, prostration) but share a common structure: the creature encountering grace is first made aware of the distance between itself and the giver — the weight of what is being received — before moving to reception. Whether this holds across all 194 active verses cannot be confirmed from current data.

*Gap identified: the immediate response pattern is evidenced in three anchor contexts but not systematically examined across the full verse evidence.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Where silent on immediate response, note explicitly.

**Status: N** — evidence is present; pattern established though not exhaustively confirmed.

### T1.6 — Sustained Effect

**Prompt 1:** What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?

**Status: A**

The data is rich. Sustained reception of grace produces: **Identity constitution** (1Cor 15:10: "by the grace of God I am what I am" — SD004); **Inner standing** (Rom 5:2: being-in-grace as relational position — 888-002); **Enabling capacity** (Act 20:32: person built up; 1Cor 15:10: ability to work harder); **Tenderheartedness** (Eph 4:32: eusplanchnos as mediating inner state — SD030); **New appetite/desire reorientation** (SD049: grace possibly reordering desire); **Assurance** (SD029, Rom 8:32: inner confidence from grace-inference — reasoned, not merely felt).

*Consistent with prior analysis (multiple SD pointers). Adds structure: six distinct sustained effects consolidated — prior analysis addressed these individually; the prompt reveals their collective scope.*

---

**Prompt 2:** What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure establish?

**Status: A**

States: standing before God (Rom 5:2), identity constituted by grace (1Cor 15:10). Qualities: tenderheartedness (Eph 4:32), gracious speech and conduct (889-003), humility (yielding posture of Eph 2:8). Capacities: ability to work (1Cor 15:10), ability to forgive (5470-002), ability to extend grace to others. Orientations: forward-orientation toward eschatological fullness (Eph 2:7, SD027), dependence-orientation of the creature before God.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds organisation.*

---

**Prompt 3:** Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?

**Status: A**

Yes — significantly. The immediate responses (fear, mourning, prostration) are creaturely recognition responses — the first impact of encountering grace. The sustained effects are constitutive — they rebuild and orient the inner person over time. The movement is from recognition of the weight of grace (immediate) to inhabitation of grace as one's standing (sustained). This is not merely temporal but qualitative: the immediate response acknowledges what grace is; the sustained effect is what grace does to the person over time.

*New finding as a consolidated observation: prior analysis noted the sequential character of grace's operation but did not draw the immediate/sustained contrast as a T1 observation. Analytically useful and not previously named in these terms.*

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**Prompt 1:** Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level?

**Status: A**

Yes. Zec 12:10: "I will pour out on the house of David... a spirit of grace and pleas for mercy." The term "spirit of grace" locates the outpouring at the spirit level. The sb_classification confirms: "Spirit-soul interface." The spirit level is the primary reception point of grace — received at the deepest constitutional level.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds precision: spirit level is the point of reception, not of generation — grace arrives there from God.*

---

**Prompt 2:** Does the evidence indicate this characteristic originates at the spirit level?

**Status: P**

The origin question is directional: grace originates with God and is received by the human spirit. It does not originate in the human spirit — it arrives there. Spirit-level location in the human constitution is the point of reception, not the point of generation. This distinction is important and leads directly to T2.9 (Origin and Source).

*Adds precision: prior analysis used "spirit level" without consistently distinguishing location from origin. A useful T2 clarification.*

---

**Prompt 3:** What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?

**Status: A**

Grace reaches the deepest level of the person — the primary site of encounter with God. It does not operate only at the level of feeling, thought, or behaviour. SD008 (Eph 2:1-5: grace addressing the dead — the person without inner resource at the deepest level) and SD026 (grace producing even the capacity for supplication — reaching to the level of the person's capacity for response) both confirm that spirit-level operation means grace can reach what nothing else can reach.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing.*

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021).*

### 5. Prompt design observation — T3.6 (Volition)

The enable/deepen/bypass/impair option set does not fully capture the grace-volition relationship. The data points to a paradox (1Cor 15:10: genuine human effort and grace-as-agent simultaneously true) that the binary options cannot accommodate. A revision to the prompt catalogue is recommended: add "holds in paradox" or "operates in both directions simultaneously" as a valid answer option for T3.6. This may apply more broadly wherever a characteristic's relationship to a faculty is paradoxical rather than directional.
