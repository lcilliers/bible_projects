**Purpose:** Define second-tier components and governing prompts for every first-tier domain. Every prompt is designed to be answerable for every word in the programme — silence and absence are valid and informative answers.

---

## Design Principles

**Universal applicability.** Every prompt must be answerable for every word studied. Where a word has no evidence for a component, the answer is "not evidenced" or "silent" — this is a complete and useful answer, not a failure. Prompts must not presuppose a particular constitutional profile, relational pattern, or developmental arc.

**Silence is data.** A consistent pattern of silence across words on a given prompt is itself an analytical finding — it reveals something about the structure of the inner being or the limits of the evidence.

**Prompts govern completeness and consistency.** The prompt set for each tier ensures that no analytical area is missed and that all words are examined on the same footing. Cross-word analysis depends on every word having been asked the same questions.

**Direction of derivation.** Second-tier components are derived from the first-tier scope descriptions — top down from the framework, not bottom up from individual word analyses. The prompts operationalise the components.

**Two outputs per tier:**
- **Output A — Components:** The named analytical areas within the domain
- **Output B — Prompts:** The governing questions applied to every word

## T0 — Divine Image and Created Design

**First-tier summary:** The purposive frame. T0 asks why this characteristic exists — what it reveals about the nature of God whose image the person bears, and what created purpose it serves in the human person. Operates at two levels: the individual characteristic (Session B) and the programme as a whole (Session D).

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T0.1 | Divine Nature Reflected | What this characteristic reveals about the nature or character of God |
| T0.2 | Created Purpose | What purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as designed — what it equips the person to be, do, or become |
| T0.3 | Image-Bearer Expression | How this characteristic specifically expresses or instantiates the divine image in the human person |
| T0.4 | Typological Significance | Whether and how Scripture deploys this characteristic typologically — using it to point toward or participate in realities that transcend the individual and the immediate |

### Prompts

**T0.1 — Divine Nature Reflected**
- What does the verse evidence reveal about the nature or character of God that this characteristic reflects or images?
- Does Scripture explicitly attribute this characteristic to God — and if so, what does that attribution reveal about its significance in the human person?
- Where Scripture is silent about God's possession of this characteristic, what does that silence suggest about the characteristic's place in the divine image?

**T0.2 — Created Purpose**
- What does the verse evidence suggest about the purpose this characteristic serves in the human person as created — what does it equip the person to be, do, or become?
- Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic is part of the original created design, a response to the fallen condition, or both?
- Is there evidence that this characteristic is oriented toward a future fullness — something the person is moving toward, not only what they currently are?

**T0.3 — Image-Bearer Expression**
- In what way does this characteristic express the divine image in the human person — what aspect of being made in God's likeness does it instantiate?
- Does the evidence suggest that this characteristic is shared between God and the human person, or is it exclusively human — a creaturely analogue to something in God?
- What does the presence or absence of this characteristic in a person reveal about the condition of the divine image in that person?

**T0.4 — Typological Significance**
- Does Scripture use this characteristic typologically — deploying it to point toward or participate in a reality beyond the immediate (covenantal, eschatological, christological)?
- If typological use is present, what is the direction of the typology — does the human instance point toward the divine, or does the divine instance establish the pattern for the human?
- If no typological use is evidenced, note this explicitly.

## T1 — Definition

**First-tier summary:** A definition in the making. T1 establishes Name, Kind, and Boundary — visited at the beginning of analysis to orient enquiry and at the end to receive what the research has found. The definition grows in precision and depth as research progresses.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T1.1 | Name and Naming | What this characteristic is called and what the name signals |
| T1.2 | Kind | What type of inner-being phenomenon this characteristic appears to be — its constitutional location, the faculties it primarily engages, and its impact on the person |
| T1.3 | Boundary | What this characteristic is not — its structural opposite and the limits of its operation |
| T1.4 | Modes of Operation | The distinct ways this characteristic operates within and through the inner person |
| T1.5 | Immediate Response | The first or most immediate inner-being response to encountering or receiving this characteristic |
| T1.6 | Sustained Effect | What this characteristic produces in the inner being over time — states, qualities, capacities, orientations |
| T1.7 | Conditions of Reception | What inner conditions enable, block, or distort the reception of this characteristic |
| T1.8 | Dimension Classification | The primary inner-being dimension this characteristic belongs to, with supporting evidence |

### Prompts

**T1.1 — Name and Naming**
- What is this characteristic called in the programme — and what does the name itself signal about its essential nature?
- What do the primary Hebrew and Greek terms reveal at the definitional level — before deeper lexical analysis begins?
- Does the name carry directional, relational, or constitutional implications that orient the enquiry from the outset?

**T1.2 — Kind**
- What kind of inner-being phenomenon does this characteristic appear to be — an act, a disposition, a condition, a quality, or something else?
- Is this characteristic simple in structure or does it appear to have constituent elements at first encounter?
- What is the current best working description of this characteristic — encapsulating its constitutional location, the faculties it primarily engages, and its impact on the person?

**T1.3 — Boundary**
- What is the structural opposite of this characteristic — the inner-being reality that stands against or excludes it?
- What does this characteristic explicitly exclude or resist?
- What is this characteristic not — where does it end and something else begin?

**T1.4 — Modes of Operation**
- In what distinct ways does this characteristic operate within the inner person?
- Does this characteristic operate differently depending on context, direction, or constitutional level?
- Does this characteristic have a communicative or speech-based mode of operation — and if so, how does it function?

**T1.5 — Immediate Response**
- What is the first or most immediate inner-being response to receiving or encountering this characteristic?
- Is the immediate response consistent across the verse evidence, or does it vary?
- Where the verse evidence is silent on immediate response, note this explicitly.

**T1.6 — Sustained Effect**
- What does this characteristic produce in the inner being over time?
- What states, qualities, capacities, or orientations does sustained exposure to or possession of this characteristic establish?
- Does the sustained effect differ from the immediate response — and if so, how?

**T1.7 — Conditions of Reception**
- What inner conditions or orientations enable genuine reception of this characteristic?
- What inner conditions block, distort, or prevent reception?
- What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic but does not receive it?

**T1.8 — Dimension Classification**
- What is the primary inner-being dimension of this characteristic from the programme's dimension vocabulary?
- What evidence from the verse evidence supports this classification?
- Does this characteristic carry secondary dimensions — and if so, what are they, and do they compete with the primary classification?

## T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries

**First-tier summary:** T2 maps a characteristic onto the constitutional structure of the human person — spirit, soul (including heart, mind, and other named subsets), and body. It identifies where the characteristic is seated, where it originates, and what Scripture is doing when it links the characteristic to constitutional elements and body parts.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T2.1 | Spirit-Level Location | Whether and how this characteristic is located or originates at the spirit level |
| T2.2 | Soul-Level Location | Whether and how this characteristic is a soul-level phenomenon |
| T2.3 | Heart | Whether and how this characteristic engages or is located in the heart |
| T2.4 | Mind | Whether and how this characteristic engages or is located in the mind |
| T2.5 | Other Soul Subsets | Whether the evidence surfaces soul-level locations beyond heart and mind |
| T2.6 | Body — Significance | What Scripture is doing when it links this characteristic to a body part |
| T2.7 | Body — Direction | Which way the body-characteristic relationship runs |
| T2.8 | Body — Deposit | Whether sustained operation leaves a constitutional deposit in the body or its design |
| T2.9 | Origin and Source | Where this characteristic originates constitutionally — generated from within, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally |
| T2.10 | Constitutional Movement | How this characteristic moves across constitutional levels |

### Prompts

**T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location**
- Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?
- Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?
- What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?
- If the evidence is silent on spirit-level location, note this explicitly.

**T2.2 — Soul-Level Location**
- Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?
- What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?
- If the evidence is silent on soul-level location, note this explicitly.

**T2.3 — Heart**
- Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?
- What does the heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's integrating function (knowing, willing, feeling, moral awareness) does this characteristic engage?
- If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.

**T2.4 — Mind**
- Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?
- What does mind-location reveal — what aspect of the mind's function (thought, discernment, understanding) does this characteristic engage?
- If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.

**T2.5 — Other Soul Subsets**
- Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind for this characteristic?
- If so, what is that location, and what does it reveal?
- If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**T2.6 — Body — Significance**
- Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?
- If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?
- If no body-part link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**T2.7 — Body — Direction**
- Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?
- What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?
- If no body-characteristic link is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**T2.8 — Body — Deposit**
- Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including DNA or generational consequence?
- What evidence supports or contradicts this?
- If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly.

**T2.9 — Origin and Source**
- Where does this characteristic originate constitutionally — is it generated from within the person, received from outside, bestowed by God, or carried generationally?
- What does the evidence reveal about whether the origin is singular or multiple?
- Does the origin of this characteristic change across different contexts evidenced in Scripture?

**T2.10 — Constitutional Movement**
- Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?
- What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?
- If no constitutional movement is evidenced, note this explicitly.

## T3 — The Inner Faculties

**First-tier summary:** T3 examines which of the person's eleven inner faculties a characteristic engages — and how. The faculties are: perception (including six inner-sense analogues), cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, and relational capacity.

### Components

One component per faculty. For each faculty the same three analytical questions apply — these become the prompt structure.

| Code | Faculty | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T3.1 | Perception | The inner senses — five inner-sense analogues plus spiritual discernment |
| T3.2 | Cognition | Knowing, understanding, discerning — transcending the body |
| T3.3 | Memory | The temporal depth of the inner person — holding and retrieving inner-being reality across time |
| T3.4 | Affect | Feeling and emotional experience |
| T3.5 | Creativity | The faculty of origination — imagination and the capacity to bring into being what did not exist |
| T3.6 | Volition | The God-given capacity to choose |
| T3.7 | Agency | The capacity to act, initiate, trigger, and make happen |
| T3.8 | Moral Evaluation | The capacity to assess against a standard — right, wrong, good, true |
| T3.9 | Conscience | The acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction |
| T3.10 | Conscientiousness | The integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action |
| T3.11 | Relational Capacity | The constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person |

### Prompts

The same three prompts apply to every faculty (T3.1–T3.11). The faculty name fills the blank:

- Does this characteristic engage [faculty] — and if so, how? Describe the nature of the engagement.
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair [faculty] in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with [faculty] reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**Applied to each faculty in sequence:**

**T3.1 — Perception**
- Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty — the inner senses including hearing, sight, taste, touch, smell, and spiritual discernment — and if so, which inner sense and how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with perception reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.2 — Cognition**
- Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty — knowing, understanding, discerning — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with cognition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.3 — Memory**
- Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty — the holding and retrieving of inner-being reality across time — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair memory in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with memory reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.4 — Affect**
- Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty — feeling and emotional experience — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with affect reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.5 — Creativity**
- Does this characteristic engage the creative faculty — imagination and the capacity to originate — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair creativity in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with creativity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.6 — Volition**
- Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty — the capacity to choose — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition in the person — including its three aspects: capacity, interaction with other characteristics, and the constraints under which it operates?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with volition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.7 — Agency**
- Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty — the capacity to act, initiate, and make happen — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with agency reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.8 — Moral Evaluation**
- Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty — the capacity to assess against a standard of right, wrong, good, and true — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with moral evaluation reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.9 — Conscience**
- Does this characteristic engage the conscience — the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscience reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.10 — Conscientiousness**
- Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness — the integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscientiousness reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

**T3.11 — Relational Capacity**
- Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity — the constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person — and if so, how?
- Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity in the person?
- What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with relational capacity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?

## T4 — Relational Interfaces

**First-tier summary:** T4 examines how a characteristic operates across three relational interfaces — with God, with other humans, and with other spiritual beings (both angelic and adversarial). Direction matters: the divine interface operates in both God-to-human and human-to-God directions.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T4.1 | Divine Interface — God to Human | How this characteristic operates in the direction from God toward the human person |
| T4.2 | Divine Interface — Human to God | How this characteristic operates in the human person's movement toward God — seeking, supplication, worship, covenant |
| T4.3 | Human Interface — Giving | How the person who possesses or has received this characteristic extends it toward others |
| T4.4 | Human Interface — Receiving | How the person receives this characteristic from others — and what conditions enable or block reception |
| T4.5 | Human Interface — Boundaries | Whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relationships versus across relational boundaries, or within covenantal versus non-covenantal contexts |
| T4.6 | Spiritual Beings Interface | How this characteristic operates in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial |

### Prompts

**T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?
- What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — is it conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?
- What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?
- If the evidence is silent on God-to-human operation, note this explicitly.

**T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?
- What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?
- What does the human-to-God direction of this characteristic reveal about the person's relationship with God?
- If the evidence is silent on human-to-God operation, note this explicitly.

**T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another — and if so, how does it operate in that giving?
- What inner conditions or orientations in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?
- What does the evidence reveal about what the person must have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?
- If the evidence is silent on the giving direction, note this explicitly.

**T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another — and if so, how does it operate in that reception?
- What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another person?
- What is the inner-being state of the person who encounters this characteristic from another but does not receive it?
- If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.

**T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries**
- Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance or difference?
- Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?
- What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic — who is included and who is not?
- If the evidence is silent on relational boundaries, note this explicitly.

**T4.6 — Spiritual Beings Interface**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to other spiritual beings — angelic or adversarial — and if so, how?
- Is this characteristic a site of adversarial activity — something that can be attacked, distorted, or weaponised by adversarial spiritual powers?
- Is this characteristic communicated, strengthened, or mediated through angelic ministry in the evidence?
- If the evidence is silent on the spiritual beings interface, note this explicitly.

## T5 — Formative and Developmental Dimension

**First-tier summary:** T5 examines how a characteristic shapes the person over time — the developmental arc, the mechanism of change, the relationship to suffering and formation, and the eschatological trajectory. The constitutional deposit identified in T2.8 feeds into T5 as developmental consequence.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T5.1 | Nature of Transformation | Whether this characteristic changes the person's condition, orientation, or both |
| T5.2 | Sequence of Inner States | The progression of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person |
| T5.3 | Mechanism of Change | How this characteristic produces change — through discipline, encounter, transformation, or other means |
| T5.4 | Suffering and Affliction | The relationship between this characteristic and suffering or affliction as a formative context |
| T5.5 | Formation and Sanctification | How this characteristic participates in the longer arc of character development and sanctification |
| T5.6 | Eschatological Trajectory | The ultimate direction this characteristic points toward — what its present experience anticipates about a future fullness |
| T5.7 | Deposit Consequence | The developmental consequence of the constitutional deposit identified in T2.8 — what sustained operation produces over time and generationally |

### Prompts

**T5.1 — Nature of Transformation**
- Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person — and if so, does it change the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both?
- Is the transformation produced by this characteristic reversible or irreversible in the verse evidence?
- If the evidence is silent on transformation, note this explicitly.

**T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States**
- Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person — a before, during, and after?
- What are those states, and what does the sequence reveal about how this characteristic works?
- If the evidence is silent on sequence, note this explicitly.

**T5.3 — Mechanism of Change**
- What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change in the person — discipline, encounter, gradual formation, sudden transformation, or something else?
- Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?
- If the evidence is silent on mechanism, note this explicitly.

**T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering or affliction — as a response to it, a product of it, or a context for it?
- Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce this characteristic in the person?
- If the evidence is silent on the relationship to suffering, note this explicitly.

**T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic participating in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification — shaping the person over time toward greater likeness?
- What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?
- If the evidence is silent on formation and sanctification, note this explicitly.

**T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory**
- Does the verse evidence point this characteristic toward an eschatological fullness — a future state toward which its present operation is oriented?
- What does the present experience of this characteristic anticipate about its future fullness?
- If the evidence is silent on eschatological trajectory, note this explicitly.

**T5.7 — Deposit Consequence**
- Where T2.8 has identified a constitutional deposit from sustained operation of this characteristic, what developmental consequence does that deposit produce over time?
- Does the evidence indicate generational consequence — a deposit carried forward beyond the individual?
- If T2.8 found no deposit, note this explicitly and close T5.7.

## T6 — Structural Relationships with Other Characteristics

**First-tier summary:** T6 maps a characteristic within the wider landscape of the inner person — identifying connections, dependencies, sequences, overlaps, and distinctions with other characteristics in the programme.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T6.1 | Co-occurrence | Which characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence |
| T6.2 | Sequential Relationships | Whether this characteristic precedes, follows, or accompanies others in a consistent order |
| T6.3 | Causal and Constitutive Relationships | Whether this characteristic produces another, is produced by another, or constitutes part of another |
| T6.4 | Vocabulary and Root Sharing | Which characteristics share terms or root architecture with this one |
| T6.5 | Distinctions | Where apparent overlap with another characteristic requires careful differentiation |
| T6.6 | Shared Verse Anchors | Whether any verse functions as a primary anchor in both this and another characteristic's study |
| T6.7 | Dimensional Sharing | How many of this characteristic's confirmed dimensions are shared with another characteristic |

### Prompts

**T6.1 — Co-occurrence**
- Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently in the same verses as this one?
- What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?
- If no significant co-occurrence patterns emerge, note this explicitly.

**T6.2 — Sequential Relationships**
- Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?
- What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?
- If no sequential pattern is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**T6.3 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships**
- Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one, and by what mechanism?
- Is this characteristic produced by another — and if so, which one?
- Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another characteristic constitute part of this one?
- If no causal or constitutive relationship is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**T6.4 — Vocabulary and Root Sharing**
- Does this characteristic share vocabulary terms with other characteristics in the programme?
- Does vocabulary sharing extend to root-level architecture — a shared root that generates terms across two or more characteristics?
- What does vocabulary sharing reveal about the conceptual relationship between characteristics?
- If no significant vocabulary sharing is evidenced, note this explicitly.

**T6.5 — Distinctions**
- Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?
- Where the evidence shows apparent overlap, what is the precise boundary?
- Is the distinction between this characteristic and its nearest neighbour one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?
- If no significant distinction work is required, note this explicitly.

**T6.6 — Shared Verse Anchors**
- Does any verse in this characteristic's evidence base also function as a primary anchor in another characteristic's study?
- What does the shared anchor reveal about the relationship between the two characteristics?
- If no shared verse anchors are identified, note this explicitly.

**T6.7 — Dimensional Sharing**
- How many of this characteristic's confirmed analytical dimensions are shared with another characteristic in the programme?
- What does the pattern of dimensional sharing reveal about the relationship between this characteristic and those it shares dimensions with?
- If dimensional sharing data is not yet available, note this explicitly.

## T7 — Evidential and Methodological Foundation

**First-tier summary:** T7 is the epistemological basis underlying all other tiers. It defines where the knowledge of a characteristic comes from and how it is derived with integrity. T7 does not produce substantive findings — it validates and grounds the findings produced by T0–T6. Three components, established and stable.

### Components

| Code | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| T7.1 | Lexical and Semantic Analysis | Original language vocabulary — the primary linguistic evidence base |
| T7.2 | Verse and Literary Interpretation | How the characteristic functions within its verse evidence — the interpretive discipline applied |
| T7.3 | Human Science Frameworks | Psychology, moral philosophy, developmental psychology, sociology, anthropology — as interpretive lenses and sanity checks |

### Prompts

**T7.1 — Lexical and Semantic Analysis**
- What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms for this characteristic — and what do their root meanings reveal?
- What is the grammatical range of the primary term (noun, verb, adjective, participle) — and what does that range reveal about how the characteristic operates?
- What is the semantic range of the primary term — across what breadth of meaning does it operate?
- Does the vocabulary include terms that distinguish distinct aspects of this characteristic — disposition versus act, received versus given, condition versus quality?
- Does the vocabulary include a term for the structural opposite or absence of this characteristic?
- Does the vocabulary include a person-type term — a term for the one who habitually possesses or exercises this characteristic?
- Does the vocabulary include a supplication or seeking term — a term for the act of seeking this characteristic from another?
- What does the LXX use of the vocabulary reveal about continuity or development of this characteristic across the Testaments?
- Is there a term newly coined in the NT period for this characteristic — and if so, what does that coinage reveal?
- What does the full vocabulary arc reveal about this characteristic's complete semantic range?

**T7.2 — Verse and Literary Interpretation**
- What is the function of this characteristic's primary term within its primary verse — what role does it play in the sentence and argument?
- What literary form carries the primary verse evidence (narrative, psalm, wisdom, prophecy, epistle, apocalyptic) — and what does that form require for responsible interpretation?
- What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence — what are the premises and conclusions?
- What contextual setting carries the primary verse evidence (judicial, liturgical, covenantal, communal, eschatological) — and what does that setting reveal?
- Does any verse function as the primary anchor for this characteristic — the verse that most fully and directly expresses its essential character?
- What does the primary anchor verse reveal that no other verse reveals?

**T7.3 — Human Science Frameworks**
- Which human science framework (psychology, moral philosophy, developmental psychology, sociology, anthropology, or other) is most useful as an interpretive lens for this characteristic?
- Where the human science framework illuminates the verse evidence — making a finding more coherent or complete — what does it reveal?
- Where the verse evidence and the human science framework diverge, what does the divergence reveal?
- Does the human science framework surface any aspect of this characteristic that the verse evidence has not yet addressed — and does that absence require further verse investigation?
