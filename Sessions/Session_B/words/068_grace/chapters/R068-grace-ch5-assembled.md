## Structural relationships (T6)


### T6.1 — Co-occurrence

**Prompt 1: Which adjacent characteristics appear most frequently alongside this one in the verse evidence?**

From Section 5 of the data: seeking (R140: 40 verses), strength (R187: 15), love (R103: 14), desire (R043: 12), evil (R057: 12), will (R173: 11), calling (R019: 11), listen (R213: 11), peace (R117: 10), might (R198: 10), faith (R059: 10), guilt (R073: 10).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T6 framing.*

**Prompt 2: What does the pattern of co-occurrence reveal about this characteristic's place in the inner-being landscape?**

Several structural signals: **Seeking at 40 verses** is the dominant signal — the creature seeking grace is the most common relational configuration in the corpus (SD002). **Moral failure cluster** (evil 12, guilt 10, sin 6, transgression 6) confirms grace appears most fully where moral failure is most present. **Power cluster** (strength 15, might 10) confirms the 2Cor 12:9 structural connection (SD003). **Love at 14** as the strongest within-cluster signal raises the primary boundary question (SD013). The pattern reveals grace at the intersection of divine initiative and creaturely need.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T6 framing: the co-occurrence pattern as a whole reveals grace's structural position.*


### T6.2 — Sequential Relationships

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic consistently preceding, following, or accompanying another characteristic in a sequence?**

Multiple sequences. Grace **precedes**: faith (Eph 2:8: grace as sphere, faith as channel — SD006); repentance (SD003: Zec 12:10 — grace precedes and enables the turn); supplication (SD026: grace produces even the capacity for appeal). Grace **follows**: moral failure and guilt (consistently appears after recognition of failure); mourning (in SD038, mourning follows grace outpoured). Grace **accompanies**: hope (Rom 5:2 — SD005); calling (SD011).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T6 framing: the sequential relationships are complex — grace precedes some characteristics and follows others.*

**Prompt 2: What does the sequence reveal — is the relationship causal, developmental, or correlational?**

Primarily causal: grace precedes and produces faith, repentance, supplication, mourning (SD003, SD006, SD026, SD038) — not merely correlational. SD028 (Dan 9:23: God's response preceded the completion of Daniel's plea) suggests the relationship can be simultaneous rather than sequential. The boundary between grace preceding and grace accompanying is not always clear.

*Adds precision: the causal character of grace's relationships is a significant T6 finding.*


### T6.3 — Causal and Constitutive Relationships

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic produce another characteristic in the verse evidence — and if so, which one, and by what mechanism?**

Yes — multiple. Grace produces: seeking/supplication (SD026); mourning (SD038); tenderheartedness (SD030); joy (SD020 root-sharing); assurance/confidence (SD029); identity (1Cor 15:10 — SD004). Grace is highly generative — it produces a wide range of characteristics in the person who receives it.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T6 framing: the range of what grace produces is unusual.*

**Prompt 3: Is this characteristic a constituent element of another, or does another characteristic constitute part of this one?**

The data raises this through the CHEN root family (SD024): supplication (tachanum) is the receiving side of the same phenomenon — possibly a constituent element of the grace-complex. SD018 suggests forgiveness may be a subspecies of grace (charizō carrying both senses lexically). The SD013 grace-love question raises whether grace is a constituent of a larger love-disposition or a distinct characteristic. Not resolved.

*Gap identified: constitutive relationships are raised but not resolved.*


### T6.4 — Vocabulary and Root Sharing

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic share vocabulary terms with other characteristics in the programme?**

Yes — extensively. Term sharing ratio: 0.846 (11 of 13 terms shared). CHEN root family shared across pray (Reg 212, 10 shared Hebrew terms), mercy (Reg 111), kindness (Reg 99), forgiveness (Reg 64). charizō shared between grace and forgiveness. charis shared with rejoicing (Reg 132 — charis/chairo root connection — SD020).

*Consistent with prior analysis. High sharing ratio (0.846) is a structural signal — grace is lexically central to the C17 cluster.*

**Prompt 2: Does vocabulary sharing extend to root-level architecture — a shared root that generates terms across two or more characteristics?**

Yes. charis/chairo (G5485/G5463) root-sharing with rejoicing (SD020): characteristics sharing the charis root share a conceptual relationship to free, joyful giving. CHEN root family (SD024): the most complete root architecture in the OT vocabulary, spanning the full grace-circuit.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD020, SD024). Adds T6 framing.*

**Prompt 3: What does vocabulary sharing reveal about the conceptual relationship between characteristics?**

The high sharing ratio and root-level connections reveal that grace is lexically embedded within a cluster of conceptually related characteristics — not self-contained but constitutively linked to mercy, kindness, forgiveness, prayer, and rejoicing. Grace is the generative centre of the C17 relational cluster.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD014: C17 synthesis question). Adds T6 framing.*


### T6.5 — Distinctions

**Prompt 1: Which adjacent characteristic most closely resembles this one — and what precisely distinguishes them?**

Love (Reg 103, 14 co-occurring verses) is the most proximate — SD013 raises the boundary question but does not resolve it. SD025 proposes chen initiates / chesed sustains as a potential distinction within the grace-kindness pairing. The precise distinction between grace and love remains open.

*Gap identified: the grace-love boundary is the most important distinction question and is unresolved. A T6 gap requiring Session D.*

**Prompt 3: Is the distinction between this characteristic and its nearest neighbour one of degree, kind, direction, or constitutional level?**

SD025 proposal (chen initiates, chesed sustains) suggests a distinction of sequential function. The grace-love question may be a distinction of specificity (grace as uncaused love, love as the broader disposition). Both are proposals, not confirmed findings.

*Gap identified.*


### T6.6 — Shared Verse Anchors

**Prompt 1: Does any verse in this characteristic's evidence base also function as a primary anchor in another characteristic's study?**

Yes. Section 6 of the data: Pro 31:30 (shared with praise/R121); Zec 12:10 (shared with mourning/R113); Rom 5:2 (shared with hope/R78); 2Cor 12:9 (shared with pride/R123); Eph 2:8 (shared with debauchery/R39 and yielding/R180); Eph 4:32 (shared with heart/R183 and kindness/R99). Six shared anchor verses across seven other characteristics.

*Consistent with prior analysis.*

**Prompt 2: What does the shared anchor reveal about the relationship between the two characteristics?**

Each shared anchor encodes a structural relationship: Zec 12:10 + mourning: the grace-mourning sequence (SD038) — deepest grace-response is grief. Rom 5:2 + hope: grace and hope co-constituting the same inner standing (SD005). 2Cor 12:9 + pride: grace-in-weakness as structural antithesis of pride (SD004). Eph 2:8 + yielding: surrender of self-contribution as condition of grace-reception (SD033). Eph 4:32 + kindness: grace → tenderheartedness → kindness as causal sequence (SD030). The shared anchors are the most explicit structural relationship data in the programme.

*Consistent with prior analysis (Section 6). Adds T6 organisation.*


### T6.7 — Dimensional Sharing

**Prompt 1: How many of this characteristic's confirmed analytical dimensions are shared with another characteristic in the programme?**

Three confirmed dimensions: Relational Disposition (primary), Moral Character (one group), Dependence/Creatureliness (one group). SD003 notes that the C20 power cluster (strength, power, authority, might, dominion) shares all three grace dimensions — full dimensional overlap. Full dimensional sharing data across all characteristics is not in the current data package.

*Gap identified: dimensional sharing analysis is partial.*

**Prompt 2: What does the pattern of dimensional sharing reveal about the relationship between this characteristic and those it shares dimensions with?**

The full overlap with the C20 power cluster (SD003) reveals a structural connection: grace and power share all dimensions, and 2Cor 12:9 makes the connection explicit — grace consistently appears as the relational mode through which divine power reaches the creature precisely where human power fails.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD003). Adds T6 framing.*
