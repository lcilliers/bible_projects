# Chapter 2 — How It Works

**Registry 068 — Grace** · *Word Impact Description*

Operations, faculties, constitutional location, relational axis, formative dimension. Drawn from T2 (Constitutional Location), T3 (Inner Faculties), T4 (Relational Interfaces), and T5 (Formative & Developmental).

---

## Constitutional location and boundaries

Where this characteristic operates within the spirit/soul/heart/mind/body structure.


### T2.1 — Spirit-Level Location

**Prompt 1: Is this characteristic explicitly located at the spirit level in the verse evidence?**

Yes. Zec 12:10: "I will pour out on the house of David... a spirit of grace and pleas for mercy." The term "spirit of grace" locates the outpouring at the spirit level. The sb_classification confirms: "Spirit-soul interface." The spirit level is the primary reception point of grace — received at the deepest constitutional level.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds precision: spirit level is the point of reception, not of generation — grace arrives there from God.*

**Prompt 2: Does the evidence indicate that this characteristic originates in or is primarily a spirit-level phenomenon?**

The origin question is directional: grace originates with God and is received by the human spirit. It does not originate in the human spirit — it arrives there. Spirit-level location in the human constitution is the point of reception, not the point of generation. This distinction is important and leads directly to T2.9 (Origin and Source).

*Adds precision: prior analysis used "spirit level" without consistently distinguishing location from origin. A useful T2 clarification.*

**Prompt 3: What does spirit-level location reveal about the nature and depth of this characteristic?**

Grace reaches the deepest level of the person — the primary site of encounter with God. It does not operate only at the level of feeling, thought, or behaviour. SD008 (Eph 2:1-5: grace addressing the dead — the person without inner resource at the deepest level) and SD026 (grace producing even the capacity for supplication — reaching to the level of the person's capacity for response) both confirm that spirit-level operation means grace can reach what nothing else can reach.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing.*


### T2.10 — Constitutional Movement

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic move across constitutional levels — from spirit to soul, from soul to body, or across boundaries in other directions?**

Yes. SD021 provides the most explicit model: divine disposition received at spirit level → relational standing created at soul level → specific capacity gifted into the person → character expressed outward through conduct and speech. The sb_classification "Spirit-soul interface" confirms movement across at least two constitutional levels.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021). Adds T2 framing.*

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the sequence or pattern of that movement?**

The sequence (SD021): spirit-level reception → soul-level standing → capacity in the person → character expressed outward. Not reversible — outward expression does not produce spirit-level reception; it flows from it. However, SD029 (the grace-inference producing inner confidence) suggests cognitive engagement with grace can deepen the spirit-level reception — some bidirectional reinforcement within the sequence.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds note on possible bidirectional reinforcement — not previously named.*


### T2.2 — Soul-Level Location

**Prompt 1: Is this characteristic identified in the verse evidence as a soul-level phenomenon?**

Yes. Rom 5:2 ("this grace in which we stand") and 1Cor 15:10 ("by the grace of God I am what I am") both describe grace as a constitutive condition of the inner personal experience — the soul level. The standing, the identity, the capacity for effort — all soul-level expressions of what was received at the spirit level.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing: the soul level is where grace's effects become part of the person's stable inner condition.*

**Prompt 2: What does soul-level location reveal about this characteristic's place in the innermost personal experience?**

That grace is not merely a transient encounter but a constitutive inner condition. SD021's sequential model makes this explicit: divine disposition received at spirit level → relational standing created at soul level → specific capacity in the person → character expressed outward.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021).*


### T2.3 — Heart

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the heart?**

The heart is not explicitly named as the location of grace in primary anchor verses. However, Eph 4:32 names tenderheartedness (eusplanchnos — gut/heart compassion) as the mediating inner state that grace produces. This is heart-adjacent: grace produces a condition of the heart rather than being explicitly located there itself.

*Gap identified: heart-location of grace is not explicitly established. eusplanchnos is as close as the data comes — and this is a mediating effect, not a primary location.*

**Prompt 2: What does the heart-location reveal — what aspect of the heart's integrating function (knowing, willing, feeling, moral awareness) does this characteristic engage?**

Where eusplanchnos is the evidence (Eph 4:32, SD030), it reveals that grace's operation produces compassion-at-the-gut-level — affective at the deepest somatic-inner interface. This confirms grace reaches the affective level of the inner person, not only the cognitive or relational. A T2 and T3 (affect) finding simultaneously.

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent on heart-location, note this explicitly.**




### T2.4 — Mind

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence locate this characteristic in the mind?**

The mind is implicitly engaged. SD029 (Rom 8:32): grace producing inner confidence through reasoning — the person thinks their way into assurance that grace makes available. SD048 raises the question of whether right thinking about grace is itself a grace-enabled inner act. But grace is not explicitly located in the mind — the cognitive engagement is a consequence or mode of operation, not a constitutional location.

*Gap identified: mind-location is not established. Cognitive engagement with grace is real but frames it as a mode of operation (T1.4) rather than a constitutional location.*

**Prompt 2: What does mind-location reveal — what aspect of the mind's function (thought, discernment, understanding) does this characteristic engage?**

Where grace engages cognition (Rom 8:32 inference-structure), it reveals that grace produces a particular form of inner knowing — reasoned confidence derived from the logic of what God has done. This cognitive form of assurance operates independently of experiential feeling.

*Adds precision: grace operating cognitively independently of affect is a finding worth retaining even though mind is not established as a constitutional location.*

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent on mind-location, note this explicitly.**




### T2.5 — Other Soul Subsets

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence surface any soul-level location beyond heart and mind for this characteristic?**

Two candidates surface. The will (SD010: 11 verses with will, Reg 173 — the question of how grace and volition relate) and the affective subset (eusplanchnos — gut-compassion). Neither is formally established as a soul subset for grace in the current data.

*Gap identified: candidates present but not formally established.*


### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

Yes — extensively. **The eye** is the systematic somatic encoding of grace in the Hebrew chen corpus: SD041 identifies approximately 20 of 67 chen verses using "found favour in the eyes of..." — grace as being-beheld by the favourable gaze. **The supplication body**: prostration, weeping, fasting, sackcloth and ashes (Dan 9:3, SD001, SD038, SD039). **Gut/inner parts** (eusplanchnos — Eph 4:32, SD030). **The body as a whole** in Joh 1:14 (the Word became flesh — SD042).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds organisation: three distinct body-links — the eye (constituting standing), the supplication body (enacting inner posture), eusplanchnos (mediating grace outward).*

**Prompt 2: If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?**

- **The eye** (SD041): **constitutive** significance — the divine gaze constitutes the person's standing. Grace is being-seen by the one whose seeing matters.
- **The supplication body** (prostration, weeping, fasting — SD001, SD039): **expressive and indicative** — "the body enacts what the inner person is doing... the whole person brought into alignment with the inner posture of plea."
- **Eusplanchnos** (Eph 4:32, SD030): **functional** — the inner parts function as the mediating organ through which grace received flows outward as grace extended.
- **The flesh of the incarnation** (Joh 1:14, SD042): **paradigmatic** — the supreme somatic expression of grace; grace takes bodily form at its fullest.

*Adds structure: all significance types (constitutive, expressive, indicative, functional, paradigmatic) are present across the grace corpus — an unusually complete somatic coverage.*


### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**Prompt 1: Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?**

Both directions are evidenced. **Soul → body**: the inner posture of supplication and dependence is enacted somatically — prostration, weeping, fasting express the inner orientation outwardly (SD001, SD039). Gracious character quality expresses itself in gracious speech and conduct (889-003, SD022). **Body → soul (relational event → inner condition)**: the eye-formula — when God's eyes rest favourably on a person, this constitutes their standing before him. A relational-somatic event creates an inner condition.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing: grace operates bidirectionally through the body-soul interface — a constitutional finding.*

**Prompt 2: What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?**

Grace is a whole-person phenomenon. The body is integral to its operation — not merely an occasional expression. Grace cannot be adequately described as an inner-only phenomenon. It has a constitutive somatic dimension in both directions.

*New finding as a T2-level statement: prior analysis noted somatic elements but did not draw the bidirectionality conclusion at constitutional level.*


### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**Prompt 1: Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including DNA or generational consequence?**

SD042 (Joh 1:14: grace taking permanent bodily form in the incarnation) is the most radical body-deposit statement but is a unique christological event. The tenderheartedness (eusplanchnos, Eph 4:32) produced by grace may constitute a sustained inner-constitutional deposit — a changed affective responsiveness — but whether this constitutes a bodily deposit in the T2.8 sense is not addressed in the data.

*Gap identified: the constitutional deposit question is not resolved for Grace.*

**Prompt 2: What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

Singular in source (God), multiple in channel. Grace comes from God as the single source but is mediated through: direct divine bestowing (889-001, 888-001), the proclaimed word (Act 20:32), the forgiving act of another person (5470-002), the spirit poured out (Zec 12:10). Source singular; channels multiple.

*Adds precision: the source/channel distinction is not explicit in prior analysis.*

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly. This finding feeds directly into T5.7.**

Unambiguously: grace originates with God. It is received from outside the human person — not generated from within. This is constitutive of the definition. chinnam (H2600: for nothing, without cause) names the structural logic: grace is given for nothing, without cause in the recipient. SD026 radicalises this: even the capacity to receive grace (the supplication posture) is grace-given. Grace originates entirely outside the human person.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing: external origin is not merely a theological assertion — it is encoded in the vocabulary.*


## Inner faculties engaged

How this characteristic engages the eleven inner-being faculties: perception, cognition, memory, affect, creativity, volition, agency, moral evaluation, conscience, conscientiousness, and relational capacity.


### T3.1 — Perception

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the perceptive faculty — the inner senses including hearing, sight, taste, touch, smell, and spiritual discernment — and if so, which inner sense and how?**

Yes — multiple inner senses. **Inner sight**: the eye-formula of chen (SD041) — grace as being-seen by the one whose gaze constitutes standing. **Inner hearing**: SD012 (listen, Reg 213, 11 verses) — receptivity of hearing as the inner posture through which grace is received; the hardened heart refusing to hear is the condition that closes off grace. **Spiritual discernment**: Zec 12:10's grace-produced mourning suggests a discernment of what has been given that goes beyond the five inner senses.

*Adds structure: prior analysis addressed perception in individual SD pointers without examining it as faculty engagement. The prompt surfaces that grace engages at least three distinct inner senses — more perceptive coverage than most characteristics.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair the perceptive faculty in the person?**

Enables and deepens. Zec 12:10: grace outpoured produces the capacity to see ("when they look on me, on him whom they have pierced") — grace enables the inner sight that recognises what grace has cost. SD012: grace enables hearing-receptivity. The impairment is in the counter-condition: pride and hardened hearing impair perception, and the absence of grace may sustain that impairment.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds faculty framing.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with perception reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace is inherently perceptual in its operation — it requires the inner person to see, hear, and recognise what is being given. Grace that is not perceived is grace that does not land (SD043: acharistos). The perceptive faculty is integral, not incidental: grace requires recognition to complete its operation. This is why impaired perception simultaneously blocks reception — and why grace itself must first enable perception before it can be received.

*New finding as a T3-level statement: the integral relationship between grace and perception — requiring perception to complete its operation, and enabling that perception — is not previously stated in these terms.*


### T3.10 — Conscientiousness

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage conscientiousness — the integrated response of moral awareness, volition, and action — and if so, how?**

Yes. Eph 4:32 (5470-002): "Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you." The person integrates moral awareness (we have received forgiveness), volition (choose to forgive), and action (the forgiving act itself) — conscientiousness as the integrated response. The grace-agency paradox (SD034: 1Cor 15:10) also engages conscientiousness: working hard while holding the grace that works through you.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T3 framing.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscientiousness in the person?**

Enables and produces — but through a mediating inner state. SD030 (Eph 4:32): grace received → tenderheartedness produced → kindness and forgiveness extended. Grace produces the inner conditions from which conscientiousness flows; it is not a direct production but a mediated one. The inner state (grace received, tenderheartedness) generates the integrated outward response.

*Adds precision: grace produces conscientiousness through the mediating inner state of tenderheartedness.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscientiousness reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace is the constitutive ground of moral integration. The person formed by grace forgives not from calculation or discipline but because they have been forgiven, are tenderhearted, and that inner condition naturally integrates into a forgiving act. Grace produces moral coherence: inner state and outward act correspond, both arising from the same received reality.

*New finding: grace as constitutive ground of moral integration is not previously named as a T3 observation.*


### T3.11 — Relational Capacity

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the relational capacity — the constitutional equipment for genuine connection with another person — and if so, how?**

Yes — this is the primary faculty engagement of grace. Grace is constitutively relational (T1.8): it requires a giver and receiver, creates standing between persons, operates across the divine-human and human-human interfaces. Relational capacity is the faculty through which all of grace's operation is mediated.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T3 framing: relational capacity is not merely engaged — it is the primary faculty home of grace.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair relational capacity in the person?**

Enables and deepens. The person who has received grace can extend grace to others (5470-002), stand in gracious relational disposition (889-003), and approach God in supplication (890-001). The tenderheartedness (eusplanchnos, Eph 4:32) produced by grace deepens relational capacity — the inner softening enabling genuine connection and response to others' need.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T3 framing.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with relational capacity reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Relational capacity is the faculty most constitutively shaped by grace. The person formed by grace becomes more relationally capable. Grace is the primary relational faculty-developer in the programme's inner-being landscape — at least at the level of this registry's evidence.

*Adds T3-level statement: prior analysis addressed relational effects of grace; the prompt names grace as the primary relational faculty developer — a more structural framing.*


### T3.2 — Cognition

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the cognitive faculty — knowing, understanding, discerning — and if so, how?**

Yes. SD029 (Rom 8:32): grace produces inner confidence through reasoning — "he who did not spare his own Son... how will he not also graciously give us all things?" The inner-being state of confidence is derived by inference from the supreme grace-event. This is a cognitive act: the person thinks their way into the assurance that grace makes available.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD029). Adds T3 framing.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair cognition in the person?**

Enables a specific form: the grace-inference (SD029). SD048 raises the question of whether right thinking about grace is itself grace-enabled — implying grace enhances rather than bypasses cognition — but this is not confirmed in the data.

*Gap identified: the relationship between grace and cognition is partially addressed but not fully examined.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with cognition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace produces a particular cognitive form — reasoned assurance, not merely experienced certainty. Grace can be the ground of inner confidence even when experiential feeling is absent. This distinguishes grace's cognitive engagement from purely affective reception.

*Adds precision: grace operating cognitively independently of affect is a significant finding not previously stated in these terms.*


### T3.3 — Memory

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the memory faculty — the holding and retrieving of inner-being reality across time — and if so, how?**

Not directly addressed. However: SD029 (Rom 8:32 inference-structure): assurance derived from grace depends on remembering what God has done — the supreme grace-event functions as a remembered ground for current confidence. The lament-supplication tradition involves the creature appealing to God's prior acts of grace — a form of memory-grounded petition (SD005 and the weeping-supplication complex of SD005).

*Gap identified: memory faculty is implicitly engaged — particularly in the lament tradition and the grace-inference structure — but not directly addressed in the data.*


### T3.4 — Affect

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the affective faculty — feeling and emotional experience — and if so, how?**

Yes — extensively. Immediate responses are all affective: fear (Luk 1:28-30), mourning (Zec 12:10), weeping (SD038), joy (charis/chairo root-sharing — SD020). Sustained effects include tenderheartedness (eusplanchnos, Eph 4:32 — SD030). The supplication posture is affectively charged: "deep need and relational vulnerability" (890-001 description). Grace and joy share a root (SD020) — grace is inherently joy-productive.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T3 framing: affect engaged at both the receiving end (fear, mourning, weeping) and the sustained state end (tenderheartedness, joy) — a complete affective arc.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair affect in the person?**

Enables and deepens. Zec 12:10: grace outpoured produces mourning — the capacity to grieve what grace has cost. SD020: charis/chairo root-sharing — grace is inherently joy-productive, the ground of joy as a sustained inner state. Eph 4:32: eusplanchnos — grace softens the inner person affectively, enabling compassion-feeling. Grace enables appropriate affect: fear, mourning, and joy and peace are all appropriate responses to different aspects of grace.

*Adds structure: the enabling of appropriate affect — the specific affects corresponding to each encounter with grace — is a significant T3 finding.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with affect reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace is affectively generative — it produces specific, appropriate affects rather than a generic emotional response. The sequence (fear → mourning → joy/peace, or prostration → questioning → gratitude) reveals that grace produces an arc of affective responses corresponding to the stages of recognition, reception, and inhabitation. Grace shapes the affective life of the person over time toward appropriate responsiveness.

*New finding as a T3-level statement: grace as affectively generative — producing a structured arc of affects, not a single emotion — is not previously named in these terms.*


### T3.6 — Volition

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the volitional faculty — the capacity to choose — and if so, how?**

Yes — the most theologically contested engagement in the data. SD010 (11 verses with will, Reg 173): explicitly identified as "the most theologically contested question the grace registry raises." SD034 (1Cor 15:10: "I worked harder than any of them, though it was not I, but the grace of God"): genuine human effort and grace-as-agent simultaneously true. SD033 (Eph 2:8: "not your own doing") establishes the limit of volitional contribution to grace-reception.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD010, SD034). Adds T3 framing: volitional engagement is explicitly the most contested question — T3.6 is a priority area for further examination.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair volition in the person — including its three aspects: capacity, interaction with other characteristics, and the constraints under which it operates?**

Not resolved. SD010 holds three possibilities open: grace operates with the will (enabling it to turn), upon the will (overriding volitional resistance), or alongside the will (grace as the condition within which the will operates). SD034 suggests the "with the will" model. SD026 (even supplication posture is grace-given) pushes toward the "upon the will" model. The tension is not resolved.

*Gap identified: the volition-grace relationship is the most contested and least resolved question in the data.*

**Design note:** The enable/deepen/bypass/impair options do not fully capture what happens with grace and volition. 1Cor 15:10 holds them in paradox — a sustained both/and that the inner person inhabits without resolution. A revision to the prompt option set is recommended: add "holds in paradox" or "operates in both directions simultaneously" as a valid answer option for this characteristic and potentially for the catalogue as a whole.

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with volition reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace and volition are not simply competitive or cooperative — the relationship is more complex than either model captures. The paradox of 1Cor 15:10 may be the most accurate description: not a resolved either/or but a sustained both/and.


### T3.7 — Agency

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the agency faculty — the capacity to act, initiate, and make happen — and if so, how?**

Yes. Giving side: grace exercised through forgiving (5470-002), extending (888-004), expressing in speech (SD022) — all agency-requiring acts. Receiving side: 1Cor 15:10 — grace enables and animates the person's own agency ("I worked harder than any of them"). Grace does not suppress agency; it constitutes and energises it.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD034). Adds T3 framing.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair agency in the person?**

Enables and deepens. 1Cor 15:10: grace as the animating force enabling the person to work harder — not replacing their agency but being the power within which genuine effort occurs. Act 20:32 (grace building up): agency-enhancing function. The forgiving act (5470-002) requires and expresses full agency.

*Consistent with prior analysis.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with agency reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace is agency-constituting, not agency-replacing. The person who receives grace becomes more fully agentive — able to work, forgive, extend, speak graciously — not less. This distinguishes grace from deterministic models that would make it the suppression of human agency.

*Adds T3 framing: agency-constituting character of grace is a clear finding not previously named as a T3-level faculty observation.*


### T3.8 — Moral Evaluation

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the moral evaluation faculty — the capacity to assess against a standard of right, wrong, good, and true — and if so, how?**

Yes. Grace operates in direct relationship with moral evaluation — it is the characteristic that operates precisely where moral evaluation has concluded the person has no standing. The guilt registry (10 co-occurring verses) and the moral failure cluster (SD007: evil 12, sin 6, transgression 6) locate grace in proximity to moral failure. Grace presupposes the moral evaluation has produced a verdict — and then operates regardless of that verdict.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD007). Adds T3 framing: moral evaluation is engaged as the pre-condition that grace transcends.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair moral evaluation in the person?**

Neither bypassing nor impairing. Grace operates after moral evaluation, not instead of it. SD047 (justice and grace, 7 verses): holding both simultaneously requires a particular moral evaluation capacity. Grace may deepen moral evaluation by making the person more acutely aware of what they have received that they did not deserve.

*Gap identified: the relationship between grace and moral evaluation is touched but not directly examined.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with moral evaluation reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace produces moral clarity — sharpening rather than blunting moral awareness. The person who recognises grace has made a moral evaluation: they know they have received what they did not deserve. Grace sharpens rather than bypasses the moral evaluation faculty.

*New finding as a T3-level statement: grace as producing moral clarity is not previously stated in these terms.*


### T3.9 — Conscience

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic engage the conscience — the acute inner witness of sin, guilt, and conviction — and if so, how?**

Yes. SD007 (moral failure cluster: grace appearing most fully where the creature's moral condition is most exposed) and guilt co-occurrence (10 verses) establish that grace operates in the vicinity of the conscience. SD026 (Zec 12:10: the spirit of grace producing mourning when the pierced one is seen) suggests grace operates upon the conscience at its most acute — the recognition of what one's sin has cost is both the work of conscience and the first response to the full gift of grace.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T3 framing: conscience is engaged as the faculty through which grace's cost is recognised.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic enable, deepen, bypass, or impair conscience in the person?**

Deepens and enables. SD026 (Zec 12:10): the spirit of grace produces mourning — conscience at its deepest: not merely moral verdict but grief at the relational and personal cost. Grace enables the conscience to feel fully without destroying the person — because what the conscience reveals is held within the grace simultaneously given. Grace makes safe space for conscience to do its fullest work.

*New finding as a T3-level statement: grace as the condition within which conscience can operate fully without destroying the person is not previously stated in these terms.*

**Prompt 3: What does the pattern of engagement or non-engagement with conscience reveal about the nature of this characteristic?**

Grace and conscience are not opposed but mutually necessary. The deepest encounters with grace are moments of deepest conscience-awareness. Grace does not anaesthetise conscience — it enables it. And conscience, fully awakened, is the inner organ through which grace is most deeply received.

*New finding: the mutual necessity of grace and conscience — each requiring the other for fullest operation — is a significant T3 observation not previously stated.*


## Relational interfaces

How this characteristic operates across the relational interfaces of the human person — God-to-human, human-to-God, human-to-human (giving / receiving / boundaries), and the spiritual beings interface.


### T4.1 — Divine Interface — God to Human

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating from God toward the human person — and if so, how?**

Extensively — this is the primary and most evidenced direction. Group 888-001 (22 relevant verses), 889-001 (15 relevant verses), 5471-001 (2 verses) all name the God-to-human direction as primary. Anchor verses: Eph 2:8, Rom 8:32, Exo 33:17, Luk 1:28.

*Consistent with prior analysis.*

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the basis on which God extends this characteristic — is it conditional, unconditional, covenantal, or responsive?**

Not the recipient's merit (Eph 2:8); not the recipient's strength (2Cor 12:9: sufficient in weakness); grounded in divine character and purpose (Exo 33:17: "I know you by name" — prior divine knowing); grounded in the supreme sacrifice (Rom 8:32: he who did not spare his own Son). The basis is the giver's character and prior knowing, not the receiver's qualification. Grace operates in covenantal context (889-001: toward those God knows by name) — directed, even if uncaused by merit.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing: the basis is the giver's character plus prior knowing — neither merit-based nor arbitrary.*

**Prompt 3: What does God's extension of this characteristic reveal about his disposition toward the human person?**

Free giving at personal cost (Rom 8:32); knowing individuals by name and giving from that knowing (Exo 33:17); giving beyond what the creature can request (Eph 2:7: "immeasurable riches"); extending toward the dead and incapacitated, not only the guilty (SD008); giving even the capacity to receive (SD026). The disposition: sovereign generosity grounded in personal knowledge, uncaused by the recipient's merit, directed toward the person's constitution as recipient.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing.*


### T4.2 — Divine Interface — Human to God

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in the human person's movement toward God — in seeking, supplication, worship, or covenant — and if so, how?**

Yes — through the supplication vocabulary (890-001: tachanum, 18 active verses). Dan 9:3 (anchor): earnest supplication with fasting and sackcloth. Zec 12:10 (anchor): pleas for mercy poured out by the spirit of grace. The human-to-God direction of grace is the supplication posture — the creature's appeal for what it cannot supply.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing: the human-to-God direction of grace is supplication specifically — not worship or thanksgiving generically, but the appeal of the dependent creature to the gracious source.*

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the inner posture required for this movement?**

Deep need and relational vulnerability (890-001: "the plea for mercy that arises from deep need and relational vulnerability before God"). Somatic forms confirm: prostration (body enacting dependence), fasting (renouncing self-sufficiency), sackcloth and ashes (self-lowering). SD001: "the body enacts what the inner person is doing — the whole person brought into alignment with the inner posture of plea." The required posture is total creaturely self-lowering before the gracious source.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing.*

**Prompt 3: What does the human-to-God direction of this characteristic reveal about the person's relationship with God?**

The creature's constitutional dependence: the person approaches God in supplication having recognised they have no basis other than grace for the approach. SD035 (Rom 5:2 prosagōgē — access, approach): grace is the relational condition that transforms the threshold from impassable to open. The human-to-God direction of grace is the creature entering through the door that grace has opened.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing: prosagōgē as the relational access-concept is significant.*


### T4.3 — Human Interface — Giving

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being extended by one person toward another — and if so, how does it operate in that giving?**

Yes. Group 5470-002 (charizō as human forgiving act — 8 relevant verses): Eph 4:32 (anchor): "forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you." Group 889-003 (chen as gracious character quality): Pro 31:30 (anchor). Speech-grace (SD022: gracious words, speech seasoned with grace) as a further giving mode.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing.*

**Prompt 2: What inner conditions or orientations in the giver enable genuine extension of this characteristic?**

One must have received before genuinely giving. Eph 4:32: "as God in Christ forgave you" — the pattern is the divine gift received, and the human extension is grounded in that receipt. SD030 (eusplanchnos as mediating state): grace received → tenderheartedness produced → kindness and forgiveness extended. Grace extended from pride or self-righteousness is not grace in the structural sense.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD030). Adds T4 framing.*

**Prompt 3: What does the evidence reveal about what the person must have received or become before they can genuinely give this characteristic?**

They must have received grace (foundational reception); become tenderhearted (the mediating inner transformation — eusplanchnos); and passed through recognition of their own need (conscience-awareness, mourning) that makes giving genuine rather than condescending. The giver must be also a receiver who knows they are a receiver.

*Adds precision: the three conditions (received grace, tenderheartedness, recognition of own need) as a named sequence for the human giving-direction — not previously stated in these terms.*


### T4.4 — Human Interface — Receiving

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic being received by a person from another — and if so, how does it operate in that reception?**

Primarily God-to-human receiving is evidenced. Ruth 2:10 shows Ruth receiving favour from Boaz — a human-to-human grace event where Ruth is the receiver. The data addresses the receiving side primarily in the context of divine grace; human-to-human receiving is present but less developed.

*Gap identified: the inner conditions for receiving grace from another human person are not separately examined.*

**Prompt 2: What inner conditions enable or block reception of this characteristic from another person?**

By inference: the same conditions blocking reception of divine grace likely block reception of human grace — pride, merit-logic, ingratitude. Ruth 2:10 shows a reception-event: surprise, awareness of undeservingness, genuine openness. But systematic examination of human-to-human grace reception is not in the current data.

*Gap identified.*

**Prompt 4: If the evidence is silent on the receiving direction, note this explicitly.**




### T4.5 — Human Interface — Boundaries

**Prompt 1: Does the evidence indicate whether this characteristic operates differently within existing relational bonds versus across relational distance or difference?**

Both are evidenced. Within existing bonds: covenantal grace of chen in 889-001 (favour toward those God knows by name); Eph 4:32 (forgiving one another — within the Christian community). Across boundaries: Ruth 2:10 is paradigmatic — grace crossing ethnic, social, and covenantal boundaries (Ruth as a Moabite receiving favour from an Israelite). The cross-boundary operation is one of grace's most distinctive features.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T4 framing.*

**Prompt 2: Does this characteristic operate within covenantal contexts only, or does it cross covenantal boundaries?**

Both — and the cross-covenantal operation is theologically significant. The CHEN root's operation across Israelite and non-Israelite contexts (Gen 6:8: Noah; Gen 19:19: Lot; Ruth 2:10: Ruth) establishes that grace is not covenantally bounded even in the OT corpus. Luk 1:28-30 and Eph 2:8 extend this further in the NT.

*Adds T4 framing: cross-covenantal operation is established in the OT, not merely a NT development.*

**Prompt 3: What does the evidence reveal about the relational scope of this characteristic — who is included and who is not?**

The scope of grace is determined by the giver's character, not the recipient's relational position. Grace operates toward those who have no basis for it — foreigners, the dead, the morally failed, the incapacitated. The relational scope is: anyone who is in need and without basis — which is, structurally, every creature before God.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD008, SD032). Adds T4 framing.*

## Formative and developmental dimension

How this characteristic shapes the person over time — nature of transformation, sequence of inner states, mechanism of change, suffering and affliction, formation and sanctification, eschatological trajectory, deposit consequence.


### T5.1 — Nature of Transformation

**Prompt 1: Does this characteristic produce transformation in the person — and if so, does it change the person's condition, the person's orientation to their condition, or both?**

Yes — emphatically. 1Cor 15:10: "by the grace of God I am what I am" — identity-constituting transformation. Eph 2:1-5 (SD008): from dead to alive — the most radical condition-change in the corpus. 2Cor 12:9: not condition-change (weakness remains) but orientation-change (weakness-as-theatre-of-grace rather than weakness-as-problem). Both condition-change and orientation-change are evidenced.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD008, SD032). Adds T5 framing: both types present — unusually complete.*

**Prompt 2: Is the transformation produced by this characteristic reversible or irreversible in the verse evidence?**

The data suggests irreversibility for the deepest transformations. charitoō (Luk 1:28: kecharitōmenē) is a perfect passive — completed act with continuing state. 1Cor 15:10 is framed in terms of being ("I am what I am") — implying a permanent condition. However, grace "received in vain" (2Cor 6:1, referenced in SD pointers) suggests not every grace-reception produces irreversible transformation. The question is not fully resolved.

*Gap identified: the reversibility question is partially addressed but not resolved.*


### T5.2 — Sequence of Inner States

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence describe a sequence of inner states through which this characteristic moves the person — a before, during, and after?**

Yes — multiple sequences. SD031 (Luk 1:28-30): grace-announcement → fear → reassurance → receptivity. SD038 (Zec 12:10): grace outpoured → recognition of the pierced one → mourning → weeping → supplication. SD021 (charis four faces): divine disposition received → standing established → capacity gifted → character expressed. SD030 (Eph 4:32): grace received → tenderheartedness produced → kindness and forgiveness extended. All sequences share a common structure: something received → inner transformation → outward expression.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T5 framing: multiple sequences converging on a common structure.*

**Prompt 2: What are those states, and what does the sequence reveal about how this characteristic works?**

The sequences together reveal a common pattern: encounter with grace → creaturely recognition response (fear, mourning, prostration) → inner transformation (tenderheartedness, standing, capacity) → outward expression (forgiving, gracious speech, ministry). The creaturely recognition response is consistently present as the transitional state — the inner moment of registering the weight of what is given — before transformation settles. Grace does not bypass the person's recognition; it requires and produces it as part of the sequence.

*New finding: the creaturely recognition response as a consistent transitional state in all grace-sequences is not previously named as a T5 observation. A significant structural finding.*


### T5.3 — Mechanism of Change

**Prompt 1: What mechanism does this characteristic use to produce change in the person — discipline, encounter, gradual formation, sudden transformation, or something else?**

Multiple mechanisms evidenced: (1) **Encounter** — change through a specific relational event (Luk 1:28; Ruth 2:10); (2) **Spirit-outpouring** — the Spirit of grace poured out (Zec 12:10); (3) **Word** — grace operating through the proclaimed word as a constructive mechanism (Act 20:32); (4) **Inference** — change through the cognitive act of reasoning about what has already happened (SD029, Rom 8:32); (5) **Mediated transformation** — grace producing an intermediate inner state (tenderheartedness) that then flows outward (SD030, Eph 4:32).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T5 framing: five distinct mechanisms — encounter, outpouring, word, inference, mediated-transformation. Unusual richness for a single characteristic.*

**Prompt 2: Does the evidence distinguish between mechanisms in different contexts?**

Yes. Sovereign bestowal uses encounter/outpouring; formative development uses the word-mechanism (Act 20:32); cognitive assurance uses the inference mechanism (Rom 8:32); relational extending uses tenderheartedness-mediation (Eph 4:32). The mechanism adapts to the mode of grace's operation.

*Adds precision: prior analysis noted individual mechanisms without distinguishing them by context.*


### T5.4 — Suffering and Affliction

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic operating in relation to suffering or affliction — as a response to it, a product of it, or a context for it?**

Yes. 2Cor 12:9: "my grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness." Grace inhabits affliction — not resolving it but operating within it. SD032: "grace does not resolve weakness — it inhabits and operates within it. The inner-being transformation grace produces is not from weakness to strength but from weakness-as-problem to weakness-as-theatre-of-grace." SD008 (Eph 2:1-5): grace addressing the dead — the most extreme condition of incapacity.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD032, SD008). Adds T5 framing.*

**Prompt 2: Does suffering deepen, test, reveal, or produce this characteristic in the person?**

Grace inhabits suffering — it does not respond from outside. SD032: weakness is the context in which grace's power is most visible. Suffering does not produce grace — but is the primary context in which grace's distinctive character is most fully revealed. Suffering is revealing, not productive, of grace.

*Adds precision: the distinction between suffering as revealing (not producing) grace is a significant T5 observation not previously stated in these terms.*


### T5.5 — Formation and Sanctification

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence show this characteristic participating in the longer arc of character formation and sanctification — shaping the person over time toward greater likeness?**

Yes. Act 20:32: "the word of his grace, which is able to build you up" — oikodomeō (to build, construct) as the formation mechanism. 1Cor 15:10: grace constituting identity over time. Eph 4:32 sequence — a pattern repeated over time produces character. The gracious disposition of 889-003 (gracious character quality expressed in conduct and wisdom) is the fruit of formation by grace.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T5 framing.*

**Prompt 2: What does the evidence reveal about the role of this characteristic in that longer arc?**

Grace is not merely a stage in the formative arc — it is its animating principle. 1Cor 15:10: "it was not I, but the grace of God that is with me" — grace is the agent within formation, not its recipient. The person participates genuinely (they work harder) but grace is what works through them. Grace is the formative power, not merely a characteristic the person develops.

*Adds precision: grace as formative agent rather than formative product is a significant T5 distinction. Prior analysis noted it in SD034 without drawing it as a T5-level finding.*


### T5.6 — Eschatological Trajectory

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence point this characteristic toward an eschatological fullness — a future state toward which its present operation is oriented?**

Yes. Eph 2:7 (SD027): "in the coming ages he might show the immeasurable riches of his grace in kindness toward us." Rom 5:2 (SD005): "we rejoice in hope of the glory of God" — standing-in-grace carries a forward orientation toward glory.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD027, SD005). Adds T5 framing.*

**Prompt 2: What does the present experience of this characteristic anticipate about its future fullness?**

The "immeasurable riches" (Eph 2:7) — a fullness that the present experience is real but limited. Present grace is the deposit and foretaste; eschatological grace is the completion. SD021's sequential model implies that the full constitutional movement of grace will be complete and unimpeded in the eschatological state.

*Adds precision: the eschatological fullness completes the constitutional movement described in T2.10 and SD021.*


## Synthesis — what T2 through T5 reveal as a whole


### Inter-tier syntheses

**SYN-INTER-068-008 | T1, T2** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's four-kind structure (disposition, standing, capacity, character quality) maps onto T2's constitutional levels. The divine-disposition kind operates at the level of grace's origin (T2.9 — God's own disposition; not a human constitutional level). The standing kind operates at the soul level (T2.2 — Rom 5:2 "this grace in which we stand"). The capacity kind operates at the spirit-soul interface — the specific enabling for specific tasks (2Cor 12:9) is received at the deepest level and expressed through specific faculties. The character-quality kind operates at the soul-to-body level (T2.7 — gracious conduct and speech expressing the received inner character outward). T1's constitutional movement finding (SD021 four-faces sequence) is the longitudinal form of T2's spatial map: T2 identifies the constitutional levels; T1 identifies the movement between them.

**SYN-INTER-068-009 | T1, T3** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's immediate response pattern (fear → mourning → prostration as the creaturely recognition response) is the affective-volitional expression of T3's faculty engagement at the moment grace arrives. T3.4 (affect is the first named faculty engaged by grace) and T3.6 (the agency-constituting paradox of 1Cor 15:10) together explain why T1.5 finds the creaturely recognition response before positive states settle: grace first produces the affective recognition (fear, mourning) before constituting the agency through which the person responds (prostration as the body enacting the inner posture that grace has produced). T1's finding that grace requires creaturely recognition to complete its operation (T1.7) is T3.1's finding at the faculty level: grace requires and enables perception simultaneously.

**SYN-INTER-068-010 | T1, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's four modes of operation map onto T4's four relational directions. The sovereign-bestowal mode corresponds to the God-to-human direction (T4.1 — primary and dominant). The covenantal-relational-sustaining mode corresponds to the relational scope (T4.5 — grace operating within and across covenantal boundaries). The capacity-conferring mode corresponds to the human-to-God direction in its supplication register (T4.2 — SD026: grace giving even the capacity to appeal). The character-forming mode corresponds to the human-giving direction (T4.3 — gracious conduct and speech as the horizontal expression of received grace-character). The vocabulary's structural insight (T4 synthesis: giving-side and receiving-side built from the same root, chen/tachanum) explains T1's finding that grace requires the receptor's inner engagement to complete its operation — the vocabulary encodes the two-sidedness because grace is constitutively a two-sided circuit.

**SYN-INTER-068-011 | T1, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: sequential)*

T1's sustained-effect finding (immediate/sustained contrast as qualitative, not merely temporal — what grace does immediately is creaturely recognition; what it does over time is constitution of identity and character) maps onto T5's formation mechanisms. The encounter mechanism (T5) produces the immediate effects named in T1.5; the word, inference, and mediated-transformation mechanisms (T5) produce the sustained effects of T1.6. T5's five mechanisms collectively explain how the four kinds named in T1.2 are produced across different temporal and contextual registers: disposition-receipt is the encounter mechanism; standing is the spirit-outpouring mechanism; capacity is the mediated-transformation mechanism; character quality is the sustained word-and-inference mechanism. T1 names what is produced; T5 names how each kind is produced.

**SYN-INTER-068-014 | T2, T3** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional movement (spirit reception → soul standing → capacity → character expression) maps onto T3's faculty engagement sequence. The spirit-reception stage (T2.1) corresponds to T3.1 (perception — grace enabling the inner sight and hearing that receives it). The soul-standing stage (T2.2) corresponds to T3.9 (conscience — grace as the condition within which conscience can operate without destruction) and T3.8 (moral evaluation — the standing produces the clarity of moral self-assessment). The capacity-gifting stage (T2.1 — spirit level) corresponds to T3.7 (agency — capacity given constitutes agency for the task). The character-expression stage (T2.7 — outward through body-soul) corresponds to T3.10 (conscientiousness — the integrated moral-action expression of received grace-character). T2 maps the constitutional space; T3 maps the faculties active at each spatial level; the two tiers describe the same movement from orthogonal perspectives.

**SYN-INTER-068-015 | T2, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: causal)*

T2's origin analysis (grace does not originate in the human constitutional system — it arrives from God) and T4's directional analysis (God-to-human is primary and dominant) are the same finding from constitutional and relational perspectives respectively. The spirit level as reception point (T2.1) and the God-to-human direction as primary (T4.1) are mutually explanatory: because grace originates with God and is received at the spirit level, the primary relational direction is necessarily God-initiated toward the human spirit. The human-to-God direction (T4.2 — supplication) corresponds to T2.10's constitutional movement in reverse: the soul's recognition of need translates into the somatic enactment of supplication (prostration, fasting, sackcloth), which is T2.7's bidirectional body-soul finding. Constitutional location and relational direction are co-determining.

**SYN-INTER-068-016 | T2, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional movement (SD021 sequence: disposition → standing → capacity → character) and T5's five formation mechanisms describe the same formation arc from spatial and temporal perspectives. T2 identifies the constitutional levels through which grace moves; T5 identifies the mechanisms by which grace moves through each level in different contexts. The encounter mechanism (T5 — sudden specific relational event) produces the spirit-level reception (T2.1 — the initial point of arrival). The word mechanism (T5 — Act 20:32 — grace through proclaimed word) produces the soul-standing establishment (T2.2 — the word's sustained effect on the person's constitutive condition). The mediated-transformation mechanism (T5 — tenderheartedness → forgiving — Eph 4:32, SD030) corresponds to T2.7's bidirectional body-soul finding: the inner transformation mediates outward through the body-soul interface. T2.8's open question (body deposit) and T5.7's open status are formally aligned — both pending further investigation.

**SYN-INTER-068-019 | T3, T4** *(outcome: D; structural: parallel)*

T3's agency-constituting finding (grace constitutes rather than replaces agency — 1Cor 15:10) and T4's human-to-God direction (supplication as the human posture of appeal — requiring genuine human agency in the act of plea) are structurally aligned. The constituted agency (T3.7) is precisely the agency that supplication requires: the creature must genuinely appeal, not merely receive passively. T3's finding that grace and conscience are mutually necessary (T3.9) corresponds to T4's relational-access finding: grace transforms the threshold of human-to-God approach (prosagōgē — SD035) from impassable to open, which is the relational expression of T3.9's constitutional finding that grace creates the condition within which conscience can operate fully without destruction. T3 names the inner-being event; T4 names the relational consequence of the same event.

**SYN-INTER-068-020 | T3, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: sequential)*

T3's faculty findings are the synchronic expression of T5's diachronic formation arc. The creaturely recognition response (T5.2 — fear → mourning → prostration as the consistent transitional state in all grace-sequences) is the affective-faculty event (T3.4) at the moment of grace-arrival, which then constitutes the volitional-faculty alignment (T3.6 — the agency-constituting paradox) that enables the conscientious expression (T3.10). T5's formation arc (sustained work producing character-quality) is the longitudinal development of T3.9's finding (conscience operating fully within grace) into T3.10's finding (conscientiousness as the integrated moral character that grace constitutes over time). The person who has been formed by grace through the T5 formation arc is the person in whom T3's faculty engagements have been progressively deepened and integrated.

**SYN-INTER-068-023 | T4, T5** *(outcome: D; structural: parallel)*

T4's relational directions correspond to T5's formation mechanisms. The God-to-human direction (T4.1 — sovereign bestowal) corresponds to the encounter and spirit-outpouring mechanisms (T5.3 — sudden specific relational event; Zec 12:10). The human-to-God direction (T4.2 — supplication) corresponds to the sustained-formation arc of T5.5: the person who habitually supplicates is being formed into the character that supplicates, which is itself the character-quality kind identified in T1. The human-giving direction (T4.3 — gracious forgiving, speech-grace) corresponds to T5.3's mediated-transformation mechanism (grace → tenderheartedness → forgiving → gracious speech — SD030, Eph 4:32). T4 describes the relational shape of each mechanism; T5 describes the inner formation process each mechanism produces.
