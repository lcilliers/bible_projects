## Lexical and semantic analysis (T7.1)

**T7.1.1 — What are the primary Hebrew and Greek terms for this characteristic — and what do their root meanings reveal?**

H2580 chen (favour — root: to bend toward, to show partiality; the disposition of the greater who inclines toward the lesser). H8469 tachanum (supplication — root shared with chen: the appeal of the creature for grace). G5485 charis (grace — root χαρ, shared with chairo — to rejoice: grace as joyful giving). Root meanings reveal: grace is constitutively an inclination of the greater toward the lesser (chen root); inherently joyful in character (charis/chairo); the receiving-side vocabulary (tachanum) shares the same root as the giving-side (chen/chanan) — the vocabulary embeds the two-sided structure in a single root family.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.1.2 — What is the grammatical range of the primary term (noun, verb, adjective, participle) — and what does that range reveal about how the characteristic operates?**

charis operates across all grammatical forms: as noun (the state/quality), as verb through charizō (the act of giving/forgiving) and charitoō (the act of bestowing grace as a completed state). This range reveals that grace is conceptualised as object (received), action (given), and completed state (inhabited) — all three grammatical forms present, confirming the multi-kind character identified in T1.2. The Hebrew chen is primarily nominal; chanan (verbal form) names the act of being gracious.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing: grammatical range confirms the T1.2 multi-kind finding at the lexical level.*

**T7.1.3 — What is the semantic range of the primary term — across what breadth of meaning does it operate?**

charis spans: divine disposition, relational standing, enabling capacity, character quality, and gracious speech. Unusually broad — from the cosmic (sovereign divine disposition) to the interpersonal (gracious words in conversation). chen spans: divine favour, human relational favour, and inner character quality in conduct and speech. The range across both primary terms: from the most transcendent to the most intimate.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD021 four faces; term inventory). Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.1.4 — Does the vocabulary include terms that distinguish distinct aspects of this characteristic — disposition versus act, received versus given, condition versus quality?**

Yes — unusually completely. Disposition: chen (H2580), charis (G5485) as noun. Act: chanan (H2603A), charizō (G5483). Completed state/condition: charitoō (G5487) as perfect passive. Quality: chen in 889-003, charis in speech contexts. Received side: tachanum (H8469). Given side: chanan, charizō. The vocabulary is structurally complete — every dimension of grace's operation has a corresponding term.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing: structural completeness of vocabulary is a significant lexical finding.*

**T7.1.5 — Does the vocabulary include a term for the structural opposite or absence of this characteristic?**

Yes. G0884 acharistos (ungrateful, graceless — SD043): the lexically embedded structural opposite, built from the same root with the alpha-privative prefix. The term's existence within the root family confirms that the absence of grace is linguistically significant enough to require its own term.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD043).*

**T7.1.6 — Does the vocabulary include a person-type term — a term for the one who habitually possesses or exercises this characteristic?**

H2587 channun (gracious — XREF term, 13 occurrences): consistently used as a divine attribute term ("the Lord is gracious"). This is primarily a divine person-type term. A human person-type term (the gracious one as a human type) is less clearly evidenced.

*Gap identified: a clear human person-type term is not established.*

**T7.1.7 — Does the vocabulary include a supplication or seeking term — a term for the act of seeking this characteristic from another?**

Yes — explicitly. H8469 tachanum (supplication, 18 active verses, 890-001 group) and H8467 techinah (supplication, XREF, 24 occurrences). Both name the act of seeking grace from the greater. One of the most distinctive features of the grace vocabulary: the seeking term is built into the same root family as the giving term — the vocabulary encodes both sides of the grace-circuit in a single root.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD024). Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.1.8 — What does the LXX use of the vocabulary reveal about continuity or development of this characteristic across the Testaments?**

*Gap identified.*

**T7.1.9 — Is there a term newly coined in the NT period for this characteristic — and if so, what does that coinage reveal?**

Yes — charitoō (G5487, SD023). Does not appear in classical Greek. Coined to name "the act of making someone the object of divine favour as a completed, continuing state." SD023: "the need to create a new word suggests the concept of standing-in-favour exceeded what existing vocabulary could carry." The perfect passive kecharitōmenē (Luk 1:28) encodes completed act with continuing state.

*Consistent with prior analysis (SD023). Adds T7 framing: NT coinage signals that the biblical inner-being reality of standing-in-favour is conceptually unique.*

**T7.1.10 — What does the full vocabulary arc reveal about this characteristic's complete semantic range?**

The arc runs from: structural logic of uncaused giving (chinnam — for nothing, without cause) → divine disposition of inclination toward the lesser (chen) → act of being gracious (chanan) → relational favour constituting standing (chen/charis) → completed state of being-graced (charitoō/kecharitōmenē) → character quality expressed outward (chen in 889-003; charis in speech) → structural opposite (acharistos) → seeking/appealing side (tachanum/techinah). The arc covers the full grammar of the grace-circuit: its logic, primary expression, act, established state, character quality, absence, and seeking posture.

*New finding as a T7 observation: stating the full arc as a structural whole reveals its completeness — the vocabulary covers every face of the characteristic. Prior analysis addressed individual terms without stating the arc.*


## Verse and literary interpretation (T7.2)

**T7.2.1 — What is the function of this characteristic's primary term within its primary verse — what role does it play in the sentence and argument?**

For the primary programme anchor (2Cor 12:9, group 888-001): charis functions as the divine statement of sufficiency — the counter-assertion to Paul's request for relief. The term carries the weight of the entire argument: God's grace is the sufficient and complete response to weakness. In Eph 2:8 (888-001 second anchor): charis functions as the instrumental/causal basis of salvation — "by grace you have been saved" — the dative/instrumental of means, making grace grammatically the agent of the saving act.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.2.2 — What literary form carries the primary verse evidence (narrative, psalm, wisdom, prophecy, epistle, apocalyptic) — and what does that form require for responsible interpretation?**

Multiple forms: **Epistle** (dominant NT form — Rom, Eph, 1Cor, 2Cor carry the bulk of charis evidence); **Prophetic/apocalyptic** (Zec 12:10); **Narrative** (Ruth 2:10, Gen 6:8, Exo 33:17 — the chen-finding scenes); **Wisdom** (Pro 31:30); **Gospel narrative** (Luk 1:28-30). Each form requires different interpretive discipline: epistolary grace requires attention to theological argument; narrative grace requires attention to relational dynamics; prophetic grace requires attention to eschatological context.

*Adds T7 framing: multi-form literary coverage is a strength of the grace corpus — evidenced across the full range of biblical literary forms.*

**T7.2.3 — What is the logical structure of key arguments in the verse evidence — what are the premises and conclusions?**

Rom 8:32 (SD029): a fortiori argument — the greater gift (the Son) establishes the certainty of all lesser gifts. Inner-being product: assurance derived from logical inference, not experiential feeling. Eph 2:8: premise (saved by grace through faith) → necessary conclusion (excludes human merit). 2Cor 12:9: premise (grace sufficient) → inverted logic (power made perfect in weakness) → conclusion (boast in weakness). Three distinct argument structures, each producing a different inner-being result.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.2.4 — What contextual setting carries the primary verse evidence (judicial, liturgical, covenantal, communal, eschatological) — and what does that setting reveal?**

Multiple settings: **Covenantal** (Exo 33:17 — Sinai covenant context); **Personal/apostolic** (2Cor 12:9 — Paul's personal affliction); **Ecclesial** (Eph 2:8, Eph 4:32 — letters to Christian communities); **Liturgical/supplication** (Dan 9:3, Zec 12:10); **Relational-narrative** (Ruth 2:10 — harvest-field encounter). Contextual breadth confirms the universality of grace's operation.

*Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.2.5 — Does any verse function as the primary anchor for this characteristic — the verse that most fully and directly expresses its essential character?**

Fifteen anchor verses across 11 groups. For the programme, 2Cor 12:9 and Eph 2:8 carry the most cross-group significance. Zec 12:10 is the most structurally complex — carrying two groups and encoding the complete grace-supplication circuit. If one verse is named as primary: Eph 2:8 encodes the most compressed and complete statement of grace's definition: mechanism (by grace), channel (through faith), exclusion of human contribution (not your own doing), categorical identity (gift of God).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T7 framing.*

**T7.2.6 — What does the primary anchor verse reveal that no other verse reveals?**

Eph 2:8 uniquely encodes the mechanism, channel, exclusion, and categorical identity all in one verse. 2Cor 12:9 uniquely encodes the sufficiency-in-weakness paradox. The two primary anchors of 888-001 are complementary — each carries unique evidential weight not duplicated by the other.

*Adds T7 framing: dual anchors are complementary rather than redundant.*


## Somatic dimension — body significance, direction, deposit

How (and whether) the characteristic is grounded in the body — whether Scripture links it to specific body parts, the direction of body↔soul interaction, and any constitutional deposit produced over time.


### T2.6 — Body — Significance

**Prompt 1: Does the verse evidence link this characteristic to a specific body part?**

Yes — extensively. **The eye** is the systematic somatic encoding of grace in the Hebrew chen corpus: SD041 identifies approximately 20 of 67 chen verses using "found favour in the eyes of..." — grace as being-beheld by the favourable gaze. **The supplication body**: prostration, weeping, fasting, sackcloth and ashes (Dan 9:3, SD001, SD038, SD039). **Gut/inner parts** (eusplanchnos — Eph 4:32, SD030). **The body as a whole** in Joh 1:14 (the Word became flesh — SD042).

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds organisation: three distinct body-links — the eye (constituting standing), the supplication body (enacting inner posture), eusplanchnos (mediating grace outward).*

**Prompt 2: If so, what is Scripture doing by making that link — is it emphatic, functional, expressive, indicative, or mediating?**

- **The eye** (SD041): **constitutive** significance — the divine gaze constitutes the person's standing. Grace is being-seen by the one whose seeing matters.
- **The supplication body** (prostration, weeping, fasting — SD001, SD039): **expressive and indicative** — "the body enacts what the inner person is doing... the whole person brought into alignment with the inner posture of plea."
- **Eusplanchnos** (Eph 4:32, SD030): **functional** — the inner parts function as the mediating organ through which grace received flows outward as grace extended.
- **The flesh of the incarnation** (Joh 1:14, SD042): **paradigmatic** — the supreme somatic expression of grace; grace takes bodily form at its fullest.

*Adds structure: all significance types (constitutive, expressive, indicative, functional, paradigmatic) are present across the grace corpus — an unusually complete somatic coverage.*


### T2.7 — Body — Direction

**Prompt 1: Where a body-characteristic link exists, which direction does it run — does the soul express through the body, does the body feed back to the soul, or does it run in both directions?**

Both directions are evidenced. **Soul → body**: the inner posture of supplication and dependence is enacted somatically — prostration, weeping, fasting express the inner orientation outwardly (SD001, SD039). Gracious character quality expresses itself in gracious speech and conduct (889-003, SD022). **Body → soul (relational event → inner condition)**: the eye-formula — when God's eyes rest favourably on a person, this constitutes their standing before him. A relational-somatic event creates an inner condition.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing: grace operates bidirectionally through the body-soul interface — a constitutional finding.*

**Prompt 2: What is the consequence of that directionality for understanding the characteristic?**

Grace is a whole-person phenomenon. The body is integral to its operation — not merely an occasional expression. Grace cannot be adequately described as an inner-only phenomenon. It has a constitutive somatic dimension in both directions.

*New finding as a T2-level statement: prior analysis noted somatic elements but did not draw the bidirectionality conclusion at constitutional level.*


### T2.8 — Body — Deposit

**Prompt 1: Does sustained operation of this characteristic leave a constitutional deposit in the body or its design — including DNA or generational consequence?**

SD042 (Joh 1:14: grace taking permanent bodily form in the incarnation) is the most radical body-deposit statement but is a unique christological event. The tenderheartedness (eusplanchnos, Eph 4:32) produced by grace may constitute a sustained inner-constitutional deposit — a changed affective responsiveness — but whether this constitutes a bodily deposit in the T2.8 sense is not addressed in the data.

*Gap identified: the constitutional deposit question is not resolved for Grace.*

**Prompt 2: What evidence supports or contradicts this?**

Singular in source (God), multiple in channel. Grace comes from God as the single source but is mediated through: direct divine bestowing (889-001, 888-001), the proclaimed word (Act 20:32), the forgiving act of another person (5470-002), the spirit poured out (Zec 12:10). Source singular; channels multiple.

*Adds precision: the source/channel distinction is not explicit in prior analysis.*

**Prompt 3: If the evidence is silent, note this explicitly. This finding feeds directly into T5.7.**

Unambiguously: grace originates with God. It is received from outside the human person — not generated from within. This is constitutive of the definition. chinnam (H2600: for nothing, without cause) names the structural logic: grace is given for nothing, without cause in the recipient. SD026 radicalises this: even the capacity to receive grace (the supplication posture) is grace-given. Grace originates entirely outside the human person.

*Consistent with prior analysis. Adds T2 framing: external origin is not merely a theological assertion — it is encoded in the vocabulary.*


## Synthesis — what the language reveals


### Inter-tier syntheses

**SYN-INTER-068-012 | T1, T6** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's dimension classification (Relational Disposition as primary) and T6's co-occurrence pattern are mutually confirming. The seeking co-occurrence dominance (R140 — 40 verses) is the co-occurrence expression of T1's finding that grace requires the receptor's inner posture of appeal: seeking is the relational posture before grace arrives, and the vocabulary encodes this (tachanum — built into the same root as chen). T6's structural uniqueness finding (grace is not produced by any other characteristic) is T1's definitional consequence: because grace is constitutively the uncaused inclination of the greater toward the lesser, it cannot be produced by anything in the lesser's constitutional system. T1's definition predicts T6's structural uniqueness.

**SYN-INTER-068-013 | T1, T7** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T1's four-kind structure is encoded in T7's vocabulary with structural completeness. The disposition kind (chen, charis as noun) has dedicated terms. The act kind (chanan, charizō) has dedicated verbal forms. The completed-state kind (charitoō — NT coinage, G5487) required a new word because existing vocabulary could not carry the concept of standing-in-favour as a completed, continuing state. The character-quality kind (chen in conduct and speech — 889-003) is carried by chen's adjectival and behavioural register. The vocabulary is structurally complete for the four kinds — each kind has a corresponding lexical form. T7's NT coinage finding (charitoō) is the lexical evidence that T1's four-kind analysis is correct: the fourth kind (standing as completed state) required new vocabulary because it was conceptually unique.

**SYN-INTER-068-017 | T2, T6** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional origin finding (grace does not originate in the human constitutional system) and T6's structural uniqueness finding (grace is not produced by any other inner-being characteristic) are the same finding from constitutional and structural perspectives. They confirm each other from different analytical directions: T2 establishes that there is no constitutional source for grace within the human person; T6 establishes that there is no characteristic-level source for grace within the programme's inner-being landscape. The vocabulary's structural completeness (T7 — but relevant here as T6.4) reflects this constitutional uniqueness: because grace originates entirely from outside the human system, its vocabulary must encode the full two-sided circuit (giving and receiving) from within a single root, with no gap left for a generative human-side term.

**SYN-INTER-068-018 | T2, T7** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T2's constitutional levels are carried by specific grammatical forms in T7. The spirit-reception level (T2.1) is carried by the perfect passive form of charitoō (kecharitōmenē — the completed act of being-made-the-object-of-grace, with continuing state) — the most precise grammatical encoding of a constitutional arrival that has permanently changed the person's standing. The soul-standing level (T2.2) is carried by charis as noun (the inhabited state of favour) and chen as the relational standing-in-favour term. The character-expression level is carried by chen in its conduct/speech register (889-003) and charizō in its human-forgiving mode. T7's NT coinage finding (charitoō invented to carry the standing-in-favour concept) is the lexical evidence that T2's spirit-level reception was conceptually unprecedented — no prior vocabulary could name what happened constitutionally when grace-as-standing arrived.

The seeking co-occurrence dominance (R140 — 40 verses) is partly explained by T3.6's agency-constituting finding: seeking requires the constituted agency that grace provides for its own reception (SD026 — grace giving the capacity for supplication). The power cluster co-occurrences (strength R187 — 15 verses, might R198 — 10 verses) confirm T3.7's agency-constituting finding at the cross-registry level: grace producing agency is registered as co-occurring with power/strength across the corpus. T3's faculty findings are grounded in and confirmed by T7's primary anchor evidence. The perceptive-faculty finding (T3.1 — grace requires and enables perception) is grounded in the eye-formula of chen (SD041), Zec 12:10's grace-produced vision, and SD012's hearing-receptivity pattern. **SYN-INTER-068-025 | T4, T7** *(outcome: D; structural: constitutive)*

T4's most analytically distinctive finding (the vocabulary encodes the two-sided grace-circuit in a single root: chen/chanan for giving; tachanum/techinah for seeking) is T7's most significant root-level finding.
