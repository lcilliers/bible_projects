## Unit 3 — OWNER Terms: Lexical Foundation

**OBS-099-012:** adam (H0120G) is the generic Hebrew word for humanity as a category — man, mankind. mti=953, 162 verses in Reg 99. Its root family is ADaM (Hebrew: man/ground). Its meaning is strictly anthropological — it names the human being as a created, mortal, earthly creature. The term carries no inherent kindness semantic. Its presence in Reg 99 appears to derive from co-occurrence patterns where adam appears in contexts where kindness, moral character, or relational qualities of humanity are described — not because adam itself carries kindness meaning.

**OBS-099-013:** The four groups formed under adam (953-001 through 953-004) span four dimensions: Moral Character (twice), Dependence/Creatureliness, and Divine-Human Correspondence. This is a wide dimension spread for a single term. It reflects the fact that adam as "generic humanity" can appear in contexts addressing any aspect of the human inner life — it is a contextual lens term rather than a single-dimension carrier.
