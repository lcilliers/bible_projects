### Unit 3 — OWNER Terms: Lexical Foundation

The analytical OWNER terms (those with active groups — 43 terms) span several distinct semantic clusters within the peace/quiet/rest domain. I will work through the terms systematically by root family and language.

**HEBREW — Root family DAM (blood/silence): H1748, H1820, H1826A, H1826H, H1827**

**OBS-117-013:** The DAM root family is an unexpected presence in the peace registry. The primary meaning of DAM is blood, yet from this root comes a cluster of silence/stillness terms: H1748 dumam (silence adv), H1820 damah (to cease/cut off/destroy), H1826A damam (to silence: stationary — be silent, be still, wait, be dumb; Hiphil: make silent/cause to die), H1826H damam (to silence: silent — same range as 1826A), H1827 demamah (whisper, calm). The causative forms of H1826A include "make silent (cause to die)" — suggesting the deepest silence is death. The root connects inner stillness to mortality and cessation, not merely to tranquillity. This is a distinctive feature of the Hebrew silence vocabulary: silence carries the shadow of annihilation.

**OBS-117-014:** H1827 demamah "whisper, calm" is particularly notable — its meaning includes the faint sound of absolute stillness, the "still small voice" context (1 Kgs 19:12, though that specific verse is not listed here). The sense of demamah as "whisper" or "calm" positions this word at the threshold of divine speech: the deepest silence is where God speaks. This dimension — silence as the precondition or context for divine encounter — is analytically distinctive.

**HEBREW — Root family NUACH (rest): H2010, H4494, H4496H, H4771, H4774, H5117, H5118, H5183A, H5207**

**OBS-117-015:** The NUACH root family is the primary Hebrew rest vocabulary in this registry. The verb H5117 nuach (to rest) is the most semantically rich: Qal — to rest, settle down, remain, repose, have rest, be quiet; Hiphil — to cause to rest, give rest to, make quiet, cause to alight, set down, deposit, let lie, place, let remain, leave, depart from, abandon, permit; Hophal — to obtain rest, be granted rest, be left, be placed. The range is striking: nuach covers the whole spectrum from the inner state of rest, to the granting of rest, to the physical act of placing or depositing. The Hiphil forms reveal that rest in this root is transitive — rest can be given, caused, or imposed. It can mean both gift (give rest) and abandonment (leave, depart from).

**OBS-117-016:** H5207 nichoach "soothing, quieting, tranquillising" is analytically notable: it names the quality of sacrifice as pleasing or soothing to God. The root concept here is that worship produces a condition — in God — of acceptance and satisfaction. This is a highly distinctive usage: the inner-being orientation of God toward his people is affected by human acts of worship. This connects the peace vocabulary to sacrificial theology and to God's inner receptiveness.

**OBS-117-017:** H5183A nachat "quietness, rest" is glossed as "rest, quietness" with no sub-senses. Its group description assigns it to Moral Character (05): "quietness as an inner-being condition of peace, receptivity to wisdom, and strength before God." This positions quietness not merely as emotional state but as a character quality enabling wisdom and strength. This tripartite connection (quiet → wisdom → strength) is analytically significant.

**HEBREW — Root family HAS (to silence): H2013**

**OBS-117-018:** H2013 has "to silence" is an interjection — "hush, keep silence, be silent, hold peace, hold tongue, still" — with Hiphil: "to command to be silent." **OBS-117-019:** H2790A charash (to plow/plot) and H2790B charesh (be quiet) share a root — wood/crafting — creating an unusual polysemy: the same root covers both "to cut in, plough, engrave, devise, plot evil" AND "to be silent, be dumb, be speechless, be deaf." This root is also an XREF into R213 listen (shared terms H2790A and H2790B) — a significant cross-registry connection.

This single term covers all three dominant subjects (HUMAN, GOD, relational axis) and spans 5 dimensions — suggesting charesh "be quiet" is one of the most complex terms in the registry.

**HEBREW — Root family CHASHAH (be silent): H2814**

**OBS-117-021:** H2814 chashah "be silent, quiet, still, inactive" has causative forms covering both voluntary and caused silence: Qal — be silent, be still; Hiphil — exhibit silence, show inactivity, make silent/quiet, cause to be still.
