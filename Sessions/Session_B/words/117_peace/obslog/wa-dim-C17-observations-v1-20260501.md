### Group 2508-001 — Phase C

**Group code:** 2508-001 | **wa_dimension_index.id:** 9365
**context_description:** Term names shalom as the inner condition of the whole person — peace, welfare, and wholeness experienced inwardly; the opposite of fear, distress, or restlessness; the condition of the God-oriented soul

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names the inner condition directly.

**Reading the anchors:**
- Judg 6:23: Peace replacing fear in divine encounter — the inner shift from terror to peace. Experiential inner state.
- Psa 4:8: Lying down in peace, sleeping — the inner condition of rest and security. Experiential.
- Psa 119:165: Great peace for those who love the law — the inner condition as a sustained experiential state. Positive.
- Isa 26:3: Perfect peace (shalom shalom) for the trusting mind — the fullest positive inner condition.
- Lam 3:17: Soul bereft of peace — the absence of the positive inner condition; experiential deprivation.

**Dimension discernment:**
The characteristic described is the inner experiential condition of peace, rest, and wholeness — positive when present, negative when absent. The positive anchors dominate (4 of 5). Lam 3:17 shows the negative side — the soul bereft of peace — which is the Emotion–Negative dimension when peace is absent. However, the group's primary character is the positive condition. The group description specifies "the opposite of fear, distress, or restlessness" — confirming the positive-state framing (peace as the alternative to negative states).

**Fit check:**
- Emotion–Positive (01): "Inner states of pleasure, joy, delight, or satisfaction." Shalom as inner rest, welfare, wholeness is a positive inner state — but broader than pleasure/joy/delight. Shalom-as-inner-condition covers the full experiential peace of the whole person. This fits best.
- Emotion–Negative (02): Does not fit the primary character — the group is about the positive condition.
- Moral Character (05): Shalom here is not a stable quality of moral nature but an experiential condition. Does not fit.
- Dependence/Creatureliness (10): Isa 26:3 has trust (bitachon) as the ground of shalom — but the shalom itself is the result, not the posture of dependence. Does not fit as the primary.

**Assignment:** Emotion–Positive (01). The group primarily describes the positive inner condition of peace experienced as wholeness, rest, and welfare — the inner state of the God-oriented person when shalom is present. The negative instances (Lam 3:17, the longing-for-peace verses) are the foil that confirms the positive nature of the condition.

**Dominant subject:** HUMAN. The inner condition is borne by the human person throughout. God is the source/ground (Isa 26:3 — God keeps him in perfect peace) but the condition itself is the human experiential state.

**Notes:** Shalom as the positive inner experiential state of the whole person — richer than joy or pleasure, encompassing rest, welfare, security, and wholeness. The soul (nephesh) at Lam 3:17 is the most direct inner-being anatomical location.

**DIMENSION ASSIGNED:** 01 — Emotion–Positive | dominant_subject = HUMAN | confidence = CLAUDE_AI

### Group 2508-002 — Phase C

**Group code:** 2508-002 | **wa_dimension_index.id:** 9366
**context_description:** Term names shalom as the covenantal peace between God and his people — given by God, grounded in covenant, expressed in blessing, and capable of being withdrawn or eternally confirmed

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names the covenantal peace condition directly.

**Reading the anchors:**
- Num 6:26: "give you peace" — God giving shalom in the Aaronic blessing. The action is divine gift; the orientation is God toward his people.
- Psa 85:8: "he will speak peace to his people, to his saints" — God speaking shalom; his inner disposition of peace toward his people expressed in speech. The saint condition: not turning back to folly.
- Isa 54:10: "my covenant of peace shall not be removed" — God's covenant commitment of shalom, eternal and irrevocable.

**Dimension discernment:**
The characteristic described is God's covenantal orientation toward his people expressed as shalom — his giving, speaking, and sustaining of peace. The shalom here is the relational condition between God and his people, given from God's side. This is distinct from the inner experiential condition of group 2508-001 — here the emphasis is on God's disposition toward his people and the relational structure of covenant peace.

**Fit check:**
- Relational Disposition (06): "Inner orientation toward another — love, compassion, favour, attachment." God's giving, speaking, and covenanting of shalom is precisely his inner orientation of favour toward his people expressed as peace. This fits well.
- Emotion–Positive (01): The shalom here is not primarily the felt state but the covenantal relational structure. Does not fit best.
- Divine-Human Correspondence (11): Some verses show the human response (not turning to folly, making peace with God) mirroring the divine gift. However, the primary movement is unilateral from God — giving, speaking, covenanting. The human side is responsive rather than structurally mirroring. Not primarily DHC.

**Key analytical observation:** The group spans both God giving shalom (Num 6:26, Isa 54:10) and the human-side response (Gen 28:21 — Jacob in covenant; 1Sa 20:42 — Jonathan and David's covenant). When God is the subject the dimension is Relational Disposition (GOD). When the human is in view (receiving, responding, being in covenant), the relational peace is still the gift — God remains the source. The dominant characterisation across all anchors and most related verses is God's orientation of covenantal peace toward his people.

**Assignment:** Relational Disposition (06). The group primarily describes God's inner orientation of covenantal peace toward his people — expressed as giving, speaking, covenanting, and sustaining shalom.

**Dominant subject:** GOD. The covenantal peace is consistently God's action and commitment — he gives, speaks, covenants, withdraws, and re-establishes. The human is the recipient. GOD is the dominant subject.

**Notes:** Shalom as the covenantal relational orientation of God toward his people. Includes withdrawal (Jer 16:5 — I have taken away my peace) as the negative expression of the same relational characterisation. Eternal covenant of peace (Isa 54:10, Eze 37:26) is the eschatological-covenant endpoint of this orientation.

**DIMENSION ASSIGNED:** 06 — Relational Disposition | dominant_subject = GOD | confidence = CLAUDE_AI

### Group 2508-003 — Phase C

**Group code:** 2508-003 | **wa_dimension_index.id:** 9367
**context_description:** Term names shalom as the inner orientation of the person toward peace — as character, disposition, and telos; both its authentic pursuit and its false simulation

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names the orientation/disposition directly.

**Reading the anchors:**
- Psa 120:7: "I am for peace (ani shalom), but when I speak, they are for war!" — Shalom as the fundamental self-description of the psalmist's inner orientation. Peace is not what he does but what he is.
- Mal 2:6: "He walked with me in peace and uprightness, and he turned many from iniquity." — Shalom as the character quality of Levi's life before God; a stable orientation expressed in consistent conduct.

**Related verse sweep confirms:** Psa 37:37 (man of peace — ish shalom — as character type), Psa 34:14 (seek peace and pursue it — active moral imperative), Psa 120:6 (those who hate peace — the orientation can be hated), Pro 12:20 (those who plan peace have joy — planning peace as heart-disposition), Est 10:3 (Mordecai sought welfare/spoke peace — disposition of leadership), Jer 6:14/8:11/Eze 13:10/Mic 3:5 (false prophets crying Peace — the false simulation of the peace-orientation).

**Dimension discernment:**
The characteristic described is the stable inner orientation of the whole person toward shalom as a way of life, a fundamental disposition, a character quality. This is not a momentary emotional state — it is the settled direction of the will and the formed quality of character. Psa 120:7 — "I am for peace" — is the identity-level statement. Mal 2:6 — walking in peace — is the character-level statement.

**Fit check:**
- Moral Character (05): "Stable inner qualities of moral nature — goodness, justice, integrity, purity, faithfulness." The peace-orientation of the man of peace (Psa 37:37), the pursuit of peace as moral imperative (Psa 34:14), and walking in peace (Mal 2:6) are all stable character qualities. This fits well.
- Volition (04): "Inner acts of willing, purposing, choosing, desiring, and deciding." The pursuit of peace (Psa 34:14) and planning peace (Pro 12:20) have volitional elements. However, the deeper characterisation is of stable moral orientation rather than discrete acts of choosing. Volition note (§7.7): "Stable orientation of the will toward God or others → consider Relational Disposition." The will's orientation here is toward peace as a way of life — stable and character-forming.
- Relational Disposition (06): The peace-orientation is directed toward others (neighbours, community) in many verses. However, the primary characterisation is the inner character of the person (who they are) rather than their orientation toward a specific other (love/compassion toward another). Moral Character better captures the stable formed quality.

**Assignment:** Moral Character (05). The group primarily describes shalom as a stable inner quality of character — the person whose fundamental orientation is toward peace, who seeks it actively, and who can be falsely simulated by those whose inner character is actually opposed to it.

**Dominant subject:** HUMAN. The inner orientation is borne by human actors throughout — psalmists, the righteous, the wicked, prophets, Levi. GOD is not the dominant subject.

**Notes:** This group contains the richest ethical texture of the four shalom groups. The false-simulation dimension (prophets crying Peace when there is no peace) is the moral-negative face of the same characteristic — it demonstrates that shalom as inner orientation can be counterfeited, which confirms it as a genuine moral character quality capable of authentic and inauthentic instantiation.

**DIMENSION ASSIGNED:** 05 — Moral Character | dominant_subject = HUMAN | confidence = CLAUDE_AI

### Group 2508-004 — Phase C

**Group code:** 2508-004 | **wa_dimension_index.id:** 9368
**context_description:** Term names shalom as the peace that belongs to and proceeds from the messianic king — eschatological, comprehensive, embodied in his person and reign, and purchased through his suffering

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names the messianic peace condition.

**Reading the anchors:**
- Isa 9:6: "Prince of Peace" — shalom as the messianic king's title and identity. He does not merely bring peace; peace is his name.
- Isa 53:5: "upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace" — peace purchased vicariously through the Servant's suffering.
- Mic 5:5: "And he shall be their peace" — the ruler is himself the peace; shalom is his personal identity as the peace of his people.

**Related verse sweep confirms:** Psa 72:7 (peace abounding in the righteous king's days), Psa 85:10 (righteousness and peace kissing — convergence), Isa 9:7 (endless peace of the messianic reign), Isa 32:18 (people dwelling in peaceful habitation — the fruit of the reign), Isa 52:7 (herald publishing peace = the gospel of the reign), Isa 55:12 (led forth in peace = exodus of restoration), Isa 60:17/66:12 (peace as structural principle of restored community), Nah 1:15 (herald publishing peace), Zec 6:13 (counsel of peace between priest-king), Zec 8:12 (sowing of peace = eschatological era), Zec 9:10 (messianic king speaks peace to nations).

**Dimension discernment:**
The characteristic described is the peace that is ontologically identified with and proceeds from the messianic person and reign. This is structurally different from the other three groups:
- 2508-001: peace as human inner condition (Emotion–Positive)
- 2508-002: peace as God's covenantal gift (Relational Disposition, GOD)
- 2508-003: peace as human character orientation (Moral Character)
- 2508-004: peace as the messianic king's identity and gift — both GOD (in the divine appointment and eternal character of the peace) and the messianic human (in the person of the king)

**Key analytical tension:** Is this group primarily Relational Disposition (GOD giving peace through the king), or something else? The distinctive of this group is not that God gives peace — that is group 2508-002. The distinctive here is that the peace is *embodied in a person* — the king IS the peace (Mic 5:5; Isa 9:6 — Prince of Peace as title). The peace proceeds from his suffering (Isa 53:5), from his reign (Isa 9:7), from his governance (Zec 6:13). This is a richer structure than relational gift — it is the constitutive identification of a person with peace itself.

**Fit check:**
- Relational Disposition (06): God's orientation toward his people expressed through the messianic king. A partial fit — the peace is relational in the sense of God's favour expressed eschatologically. But the primary characteristic is not the divine disposition per se; it is the ontological identification of peace with the messianic person.
- Transformation (08): "Inner change — renewal, healing, purification." The peace purchased through suffering (Isa 53:5) involves transformation of the condition of those who receive it. A partial fit for some verses.
- Divine-Human Correspondence (11): "Inner-being characteristics that operate across the boundary between God and the human person." This dimension is designed precisely for characteristics that straddle the divine-human boundary. The messianic king is both divinely appointed and humanly embodied — Prince of Peace is a divine title on a human person; He shall be their peace (Mic 5:5) places peace in the person of the ruler. The peace purchased through suffering (Isa 53:5) is a human act with divine results for the human inner condition.

**Assessment:** DHC (11) is the strongest fit for this group. The peace here operates precisely at the divine-human boundary — it is the characteristic that belongs to the messianic king who is both the divine appointee (Prince of Peace, Mighty God in Isa 9:6's co-titles) and the human Servant (pierced, crushed, bearing chastisement). The peace cannot be reduced to either purely divine giving (group 2508-002) or purely human receiving (group 2508-001). It is the characteristic that bridges.

**Dominant subject:** Per §7.7 DHC guidance: "assign NONE where the subject is genuinely dual. Assign GOD or HUMAN where one pole dominates but the correspondence is analytically significant." The messianic person embodies both poles — the divine appointment and human enactment are equally present (Isa 9:6 lists both divine titles and human birth; Isa 53:5 names a human suffering that produces a divinely-structured peace). Dominant subject: NONE (genuinely dual — the divine-human identity of the messianic king cannot be resolved to one pole).

**Notes:** This group represents the programme's richest shalom data — the point where peace ceases to be a condition the human receives or a gift God gives and becomes a Person. "He shall be their peace" (Mic 5:5) is the most ontologically concentrated statement of peace in the Hebrew Bible. Cross-registry candidates: R130 reconciliation (Col 1:20 parallel in NT), messianic-identity registries. This is a strong Session D pointer candidate.

**DIMENSION ASSIGNED:** 11 — Divine-Human Correspondence | dominant_subject = NONE | confidence = CLAUDE_AI
