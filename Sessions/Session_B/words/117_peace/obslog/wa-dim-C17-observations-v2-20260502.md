### Group 2509-001 — Phase C (id=9369)

**context_description:** Term names shalom as the comprehensive inner condition of welfare and well-being — the wholeness of the person as divinely given, sustained, or promised

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names the welfare condition directly.

**Dimension discernment:**

Reading the anchors and related verses together:
- Psa 35:27 — God delights in the servant's welfare: the welfare is the object of divine care, experienced as inner condition by the servant.
- Jer 29:11 — Plans for welfare, future, and hope: comprehensive divine purposing for the person's wholeness.
- Psa 55:18 — Soul redeemed in safety: the soul (nephesh) bearing the safety-condition.
- Psa 38:3 — No health (sha.lom) in bones because of sin: somatic expression of welfare's absence due to inner disorder.
- Isa 38:17 — Welfare through suffering and divine love: paradoxical inner wholeness.
- Jer 29:7 — Seek welfare of the city; in its welfare you will find your welfare: participatory welfare.
- Isa 45:7 — God makes well-being: sha.lom as divine sovereign creation.

The characteristic is the comprehensive condition of welfare/wholeness of the whole person — body, soul, and life — as divinely given and sustained. This is a positive inner condition (when present) or a longed-for condition (when absent). Distinct from 2508-001 (which is the interior peace-as-felt-rest); 2509-001 is the welfare/wholeness dimension of shalom — comprehensive flourishing.

**Fit check:**
- Emotion–Positive (01): "Inner states of pleasure, joy, delight, or satisfaction." Welfare/wholeness is broader than emotional pleasure — it encompasses health, safety, prosperity, soul-safety. The experienced dimension is positive but the scope exceeds emotion. Partial fit.
- Relational Disposition (06): Some verses show God's orientation toward the person's welfare (Psa 35:27, Jer 29:11, Isa 45:7). But the primary characteristic is the welfare condition of the person, not God's orientation per se. Partial fit for God-dominant readings.
- Vitality/Existence (07): "Conditions of physical and inner life — health, strength, vitality, existence." Psa 38:3 (no health in bones), Psa 55:18 (soul redeemed in safety), Isa 38:17 (life delivered from the pit) — these are squarely in the vitality/existence register. The comprehensive welfare of the person encompasses physical soundness and soul-safety. This is the strongest fit for the wholeness-of-the-person dimension.

**Assessment:** The characteristic across this group is the comprehensive wholeness of the person — health, safety, soul-safety, prosperity — as a condition of their existence. This is most precisely named by Vitality/Existence (07), which covers conditions of inner and physical life. The key related verse Psa 38:3 ("no health [sha.lom] in my bones because of my sin") locates the welfare-absence directly in the body and names sin as its inner cause — the vitality/existence condition disrupted by inner disorder. Jer 29:7 and Isa 38:17 show welfare as comprehensive life-condition.

Emotion–Positive would apply if the group were primarily about the felt experience of wellbeing; Relational Disposition (GOD) would apply if the primary characterisation were God's orientation toward the person. Both are present but neither is the primary. The primary characteristic is the condition of wholeness itself — welfare as the state of the person's comprehensive existence.

**Assignment:** Vitality/Existence (07).

**Dominant subject:** Mixed — some verses foreground God creating/giving welfare (Isa 45:7, Jer 29:11, Jer 33:6), others foreground the human person experiencing it (Psa 38:3, Psa 55:18, Psa 73:3, Isa 38:17). The condition itself (welfare/wholeness) is borne by the human, even when divinely given. Dominant subject: HUMAN — the welfare condition belongs to and is borne by the human person.

### Group 406-001 — Phase C (id=9373)

**context_description:** Term names shalom as the divine identity commemorated in the altar name YHWH-Shalom — God identified as peace in the altar built after the peace-theophany, sealing the inner peace given in divine encounter

**Term type:** Characteristic term — sha.lom names God's identity as peace.

**Dimension discernment:**

Single anchor: Judg 6:24 — "Then Gideon built an altar there to the Lord and called it, The Lord Is Peace." This directly follows Judg 6:23 — "the Lord said to him, 'Peace be to you. Do not fear; you shall not die.'" The altar name YHWH-Shalom is Gideon's response to receiving divine sha.lom in the moment of terror at the theophany.

**What the group names:** God identified as sha.lom — not God giving peace (which would be Relational Disposition, GOD, as in 2508-002), but God named as peace itself. The altar name declares: YHWH IS peace. This is the same ontological structure as group 2508-004 (the messianic king IS peace, Mic 5:5 — He shall be their peace), but applied directly to YHWH.

**Fit check:**
- Relational Disposition (06, GOD): God's act of giving peace to Gideon in the encounter (Judg 6:23) is Relational Disposition. But the altar name (Judg 6:24) moves beyond the relational act to the divine identity — YHWH-Shalom names what God IS, not what God does.
- Divine-Human Correspondence (11): The altar name is a human response to divine self-disclosure — Gideon names God as peace after receiving peace in divine encounter. The characteristic (sha.lom as divine identity) operates at the boundary between God's self-revelation and the human recipient's recognition and commemoration of it.
- Moral Character (05, GOD): God's identity as peace could be framed as a stable quality of divine nature. But "divine identity commemorated in an altar name" is more precisely a DHC phenomenon — it is the characteristic that is named at the intersection of divine self-disclosure and human recognition.

**Assessment:** The altar name YHWH-Shalom is the human act of naming God as peace in response to divine self-disclosure. The characteristic (sha.lom as divine identity) is constituted at the divine-human boundary: God discloses peace (Judg 6:23), Gideon receives it, recognises it, and names God as its source and substance (Judg 6:24). This parallels the structure of 2508-004 (DHC — the messianic king who IS peace, divine and human) and is best assigned Divine-Human Correspondence (11).

Dominant subject: The altar name is a human act commemorating divine identity — the human subject (Gideon) names God (GOD). Neither pole alone captures the characteristic. DHC guidance applies: where the characteristic constitutes the divine-human boundary, NONE is appropriate when both poles are equally present. However, in this case the altar name is unambiguously a human act (Gideon builds and names), but the content of the name is God's identity (YHWH IS peace). The human act gives expression to a divine characteristic — GOD is the content, HUMAN is the agent of naming. Dominant subject: GOD — the characteristic named is God's identity as peace; the naming act is secondary.

**DIMENSION ASSIGNED:** 11 — Divine-Human Correspondence | dominant_subject = GOD | confidence = CLAUDE_AI
