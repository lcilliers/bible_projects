## Key Findings by Tier

### T0 — Divine Image and Created Design
- God's compassion is constitutive, visceral, maternal, temporally asymmetric with judgment, and sovereignly free
- The etymological direction is unusual: abstract (compassion) named after concrete (womb), not the reverse — human body as etymological ground of divine attribute
- Genuine sharing between God and human person (not merely analogy): confirmed by shared vocabulary, Christological grounding, and mirroring principle
- Typological use across three registers: Christological, covenantal, eschatological
- **Gap identified:** Whether compassion belongs to original created design vs response to fallen condition — not addressed in data; new gap for Session D

### T1 — Definition
- Compassion is four kinds simultaneously: character quality, inner event, condition of its object (pitiable), and externalised act
- Four root families identify four constituent dimensions
- Two structural opposites: willed judicial hardness AND unwilled prosperity-blindness
- Four modes of operation; variation across context, direction, and constitutional level
- Primary dimension: Relational Disposition; secondary: Emotion — Positive, Agency / Power, Vitality / Existence
- **Recurring gap:** The receiver's inner-being experience is entirely absent from the data (affects T1.5, T1.6, T1.7)

### T2 — Constitutional Location and Boundaries
- Spirit-level location entirely absent — three consecutive S ratings; new gap
- Soul-level location, heart-location, and body-part links all richly evidenced
- Eusplanchnos (tender heart) as named constitutional pre-condition — inner tenderness gradient identified
- Four body-part functions: constitutive (womb), expressive (gut), indicative (Synoptic pattern), mediating (heart in Hos 11:8)
- Bidirectional body-soul flow confirmed; soul-body integration essential — purely cognitive compassion is constitutionally incomplete
- **Key finding:** Four-stage constitutional movement sequence: somatic arousal → soul engagement → volitional engagement → outward action; consistently inside-out and bottom-up
- T2.8 — no body deposit established; T5.7 formally closed; generational consequence is covenantal-communal, not biological

### T3 — Inner Faculties
- Sight as structural trigger — compassion is perceptively dependent and merit-independent simultaneously
- Affect and moral action are structurally unified — not sequentially related; affect is the mechanism of movement
- Compassion is constitutively incomplete without outer expression (unique structural feature)
- Compassion converts moral knowledge into motivated action — unique position as motivational ground of conscientiousness
- Three relational modes: building/sustaining (chesed), restoring/repairing (splanchnizō), extending (Good Samaritan — new category not in instruction typology)
- Creativity entirely silent (three S ratings) — informative: compassion responds, does not originate
- **Key new gaps:** Conscience-healing unaddressed; developmental relationship between compassion and cognition untraced

### T4 — Relational Interfaces
- God's compassion operates on three simultaneous bases: sovereignly free, covenantally grounded, situationally responsive
- Compassion explicitly and repeatedly crosses covenantal boundaries (Nineveh, Samaritan/Jew, power relation)
- Chesed (within-bond sustaining) and splanchnizō (across-distance extending) map onto distinct relational registers
- Scope defined by perceived need, not moral standing or covenantal membership
- T4.6 entirely silent — spiritual beings interface absent across all four prompts
- **Most significant persistent gap:** Receiver's inner-being experience is the most critical unaddressed question in the registry; affects T4.4 P1–P4

### T5 — Formative and Developmental
- Transformation evidenced in both modes: condition-change and orientation-change
- Matt 18:27: received compassion can be forfeited by failure to extend it — integrated moral framework
- Formative cycle identified: after-state feeds back into before-state of future compassion-readiness
- Three mechanisms of change: encounter/sudden, covenantal-sustaining/gradual, participatory-ontological
- All four suffering-compassion relationships evidenced: reveals, tests, deepens, and produces
- Compassion plays dual role in sanctification arc: character target AND motivational ground making the formative relationship survivable
- Eschatological trajectory partially evidenced; human compassion's eschatological fullness is a gap

### T6 — Structural Relationships
- Love (R103) co-occurrence at 196/351 verses (55.8%) — compassion sits at centre of C17 gravity
- CHASAD root crossing compassion (C17) and shame (C06) — most structurally unexpected finding; programme-level question
- Exod 34:6 is the programme's most multiply-anchored verse (6 registries) — must be treated as cluster-level synthesis anchor
- Mat 9:36 shared with listen (R213) — underexplored connection
- Compassion/mercy distinction is the most critical unresolved boundary question; all three critical distinctions (mercy, love, grace) deferred to Session D
- T6.7 is programme-stage limited — dimensional sharing data unavailable until Session B DataPrep complete

### T7 — Evidential and Methodological
- Full vocabulary arc named: from womb (most intimate) to almsgiving (most institutional) — no other characteristic traverses this complete arc
- CHASAD root includes shame as the negative semantic pole embedded within the compassion vocabulary itself
- Dual primary anchor finding: Hos 11:8 (divine compassion at constitutional depth) and Luke 10:33 (human compassion as moral norm)
- Three key argument structures: compassion-mission logic (Matt 9:36), divine compassion-repentance ground (Joel 2:13), Good Samaritan's moral logic (Luke 10:25-37)
- Three human science frameworks identified: affective neuroscience (somatic vocabulary), virtue ethics (character-formation arc), attachment theory (eusplanchnos pre-condition)
- Three framework divergences: sovereignty vs automaticity, cross-boundary extension exceeds natural empathic scope, purposive suffering in Jas 5:11 requires teleological frame
- **New gaps:** Compassion fatigue/sustainability not addressed; LXX systematic analysis absent (NO_WORD_ANALYSIS flag on 98 terms)
