5. CHALAQ root: Reg 199 ↔ C18. Portion as divinely assigned judgment (C20) vs. portion as inheritance and belonging (C18). Coherent directional differentiation.

**Decision:** All five registries confirmed as-is. No reassignment proposed.

## Phase B.5 — Characteristic-Perspective Corrections

25 rewrites completed in-session. Key analytical decisions:

**am.mits (676-001 and 676-002):** Two groups from the same term, both term-centric. 676-001 (call to courage) assigned to Volition — the inner act of will against fear, not just the call itself. 676-002 (strengthening another) assigned to Relational Disposition — the compassionate act of care for another's inner capacity. This differentiation is analytically important: the same term does two different things in different contexts.

**oikos/oikia family (Reg 187):** A dense family of 18+ groups, nearly all with term-centric or abbreviated descriptions. Rewrites required precision to distinguish: the parabolic use of oikia as inner foundation (Mat 7:24 — 7054-001 and 639-002 both reference the same parable but name different terms). Both rewrites are coherent because oikia names the house-as-foundation and oikodomeō names the act of building.

**kratos (685-001 and 685-002):** Both previously term-centric ("kratos as sovereign dominion" and "kratos as source of empowerment" without naming what characteristic is served). Rewrites: 685-001 → divine power as the ground of the inner person's strength (Agency/Power, GOD); 685-002 → devotional awe before divine sovereignty (Agency/Power, GOD). Both correctly assign GOD as dominant subject.

**stereos/stereoō/stereōma family:** Three firmness terms (695-001, 7083-001, 7086-001), all needed rewriting from extremely minimal descriptions. All assigned Volition — the characteristic is the firmness of inner orientation and faith.

## Analytical Findings and Observations

### C20-level findings:

**1. The directionally-determined pattern reaches its highest density in C20.**
The strength/power/authority vocabulary is where the directionally-determined inner faculty principle (established in earlier clusters) is most intensely demonstrated. Nearly every major root family in Reg 187 generates both a positive and a negative register:
- Strength as divine gift / strength as misplaced trust (oz, ko.ach, cha.yil)
- Greatness ascribed to God / pride claiming greatness for self (ga.dal, go.del, ge.vu.rah)
- Refuge in God / false refuge in human security (ma.oz, cha.yil, ko.ach)
- Standing before God / standing in self-assertion (na.tsav, qo.mah)

This pattern is now confirmed as a structural principle across clusters C06–C20, with C20 being its clearest demonstration.

**2. Transformation is more prominent in C20 than expected.**
The empowerment vocabulary (endunamoō, dunamoō, enischuō, stereoō, bebaioō) consistently generates Transformation groups, not Agency/Power. This is analytically significant: the inner-being dimension of being strengthened by God is formation and change, not power-exercise. The human person does not exercise the power — they are changed by it. This distinction between wielded power (Agency/Power) and received transformation (Transformation) is a C20 contribution.

**3. The oikos/oikia family is unexpectedly multivalent.**
18+ groups from the household vocabulary span seven dimensions: Moral Character (foundation, readiness), Transformation (edification, incorporation), Agency/Power (spiritual captivity), Relational Disposition (belonging, allegiance), Volition (longing, surrender, household faith), Divine-Human Correspondence (divine indwelling), and Vitality/Existence (spiritual threat). The household functions as a structural metaphor for the inner life across its full range — not as a single characteristic but as a multidimensional matrix.

**4. The kingdom (basileia) vocabulary is the most dimensionally multivalent single term in C20.**
Basileia generates Transformation (entry condition), Volition (supreme object of desire), Moral Character (inheritance condition), and Relational Disposition (identity and belonging). No other term in the programme yet reviewed generates this range. This may require special treatment in Session D.

**5. The ba.al family in Reg 199 generates a significant theological directional pair.**
God as ba.al (husband/lord) in the prophetic restoration vision vs. ba.al as the false lord of misoriented allegiance. This is structurally parallel to the C07 zeal/jealousy dual-register finding (same inner capacity, sanctified or destructive depending on object). The ba.al root extends the pattern to the object of relational allegiance itself.

**6. Self-mastery as the highest form of dominion (ma.shal 1356-003).**
The group describing ma.shal as the rule of the inner person over themselves is analytically the most significant group in Reg 199: "he who rules his spirit than he who takes a city" (Pro 16:32). Self-mastery — dominion over one's own inner being — is presented as greater than military conquest. This is a programme-level finding about the biblical concept of dominion.
