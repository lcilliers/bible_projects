## Key Analytical Observations

**1. The drive-structure of C07:** Unlike C06 (receptive negative states — fear, shame, terror), C07 generates inner states that produce directed energy. Anger rises, envy consumes, hatred persists, zeal drives. The dimension that most distinguishes C07 from C06 is Volitional/Will (6 groups vs. 5 in C06) and Character/Disposition (19 groups vs. 12 in C06). The cluster's inner states tend to become dispositional.

**2. Theological/Divine-Human is exceptionally high:** 28 groups (22%) — higher than any other cluster reviewed so far. This reflects the substantial divine anger vocabulary in Reg 4 and the divine jealousy/zeal vocabulary in Regs 56 and 96. God's anger and God's jealousy are major biblical themes, and they occupy a significant portion of this cluster.

**3. The sanctified/destructive axis:** The zeal/jealousy vocabulary spans from the most destructive human passion (envy destroying community, 342-001) to the most positive human devotion (zeal for God, 341-002, 348-001) to a divine inner attribute (divine jealousy, 344-003, 2528-001). This axis — the same inner capacity directed toward different ends — is the most theologically rich finding in C07. It will be a primary Session B question.

**4. Anger mastery as apex inner virtue:** Group 79-004 (mastery of anger exceeds military conquest as demonstration of inner greatness) is one of the most striking single-group findings across the programme to date. It positions the governance of the anger-inner-being as the supreme expression of inner strength — a claim that carries significant anthropological and ethical weight.

**5. Vocabulary issue — Volitional/Capacity:** Three groups in the extract carried this non-standard label. All corrected. Claude Code verification recommended.
