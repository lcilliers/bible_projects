### Coherence assessment

The inner-being affinity holding C14 together is the volitional-surrender domain: the cluster addresses how the inner person orients, directs, submits, or refuses its will in relation to God and others. The core question is: who or what governs the inner person's willing? The registries approach this from multiple angles — bondage (compelled service or liberation), obedience (responsive compliance), submission and surrender (voluntary yielding of the will), will (the inner act and capacity of choosing), and yielding (giving over, bearing, producing). Together they constitute the inner-being territory of volitional disposition and its outcomes.

The affinity is genuine across all active registries. Bondage and obedience are complementary opposites in the same relational domain — one names compelled or transformed service, the other names responsive compliance. Submission and surrender name the dispositional postures of the willing inner person. Will names the capacity and act itself. Yielding names the outworking: what the inner life produces or gives when it surrenders to a governing orientation.

### Boundary observations

**Reg 173 (will) — 52 groups, highly diverse:** This is the largest registry in C14 and the most heterogeneous. The "will" registry appears to have captured a wide range of terms connected to inner determination, choice, negation, existence, and temporal marking via XREF linkages. Several terms (G3361 mē — negation particle; H3426 yesh — existential "there is"; H1836 de.nah — demonstrative "this"; H1980G halakh — "to go"; G1510 eimi — "to be"; G3195 mellō — imminence; H3427 yashav — "to dwell") are grammatical or existential terms whose inner-being relevance is highly contextual and indirect. The phase B review will assess each group on its merits, but this registry's breadth raises a cluster-coherence note: many of its groups may more naturally belong to other clusters (e.g. hope/expectation, lament, identity). No reassignment is proposed at this stage — the groups will be assessed individually.

**Reg 180 (yielding) — 69 groups, also highly diverse:** The yielding registry has captured a broad semantic field through XREF linkages: sowing/seed language (za.ra, ze.ra), giving/distributing language (apodidōmi, paradidōmi, dōron family), bearing language (ferō), water imagery (hudōr). The inner-being connection ranges from strong (self-surrender, giving from love) to tenuous (seed as offspring, water as eschatological promise). Phase B will assess each group individually. No reassignment proposed.

**Reg 137 (resolve) — 0 groups:** No verse context groups exist for this registry. Phase B has no material to assess; Phase C is not applicable. This is noted as a programme gap — the resolve registry has no inner-being verse evidence in the current database. No action required at this stage.
