## Key Analytical Observations

**1. The character-formation cluster:** C08 is the most analytically coherent cluster reviewed so far in terms of its internal unity. All registries contribute to a single theme: how the inner person is shaped into virtuous character — whether through divine action (humbling, courage-address), through volitional discipline (self-control, endurance), or through the ongoing orientation of the whole person toward God (devotion, humble walking).

**2. Divine attributes modelled as human virtues:** Patience (32-002, 33-002), gentleness (implied in divine condescension, 188-001), and humility (244-001, 2491-002) all appear as divine inner attributes that are simultaneously commanded as human character qualities. This structural pattern — God embodies the virtue and calls humans to embody the same — is a significant theological claim about the relationship between divine and human inner life. Session D should examine this pattern across the programme.

**3. The courage-fear cross-cluster link (DIM-33-SD001):** tharseō (courage command) and ya.re (fear-not command) are structurally parallel divine speech-acts to the inner being across C08 and C06. This is the programme's first confirmed cross-cluster parallel at the dimension-pattern level.

**4. Goodness vocabulary's inner conflict (881-005):** Group 881-005 (the good I want but cannot do) is one of the analytically most significant groups encountered across all eight clusters. It names a structural gap in the inner being — between desire and capacity — that is the heart of Pauline anthropology. This group alone warrants careful Session B treatment.
