## 2. Programme-Level Principles Established This Session

These principles govern all future dimension assignments across all clusters and Session B.

### 2.1 Cognitive vs. Volitional Dimension Distinction

**Principle:** The fact that the will can act upon a faculty does not make that faculty volitional. The dimension should name the *nature of the capacity itself*, not the relationship through which it can be engaged.

**Origin:** DR-8 decisions on 979-001 (ze.kher — remembrance) and 1188-001 (noēma — mind's contents). Both corrected from Volitional/Capacity (C01) to Cognitive/Mind.

**Application:** Remembrance is commanded (volitional act) but the capacity to hold content in living consciousness is cognitive. The mind's contents can be hardened or corrupted (volitional/spiritual forces acting on it) but the faculty itself is cognitive.

**Programme scope:** All clusters. When a group describes a cognitive faculty that can be commanded or directed, the dimension is Cognitive/Mind unless the governing characterisation is the act of directing itself.

### 2.2 Theological/Divine-Human vs. Spiritual/God-ward Distinction (Refined)

**Principle (confirmed from v2):** Theological/Divine-Human names inner-being engagements where the *constitutive structure* of the phenomenon is the divine-human relationship itself. Spiritual/God-ward names the soul's own faculties oriented *toward* God.

**New addition — Option A precedent:** Divine-attribute groups where the human inner-being engagement is contextually implied (not explicitly named in the anchor) may be assigned Theological/Divine-Human where the verses function as the ground of human theological reckoning, even when the term itself is predicated of God. This applies when the OWNER term classification was correct (confirmed by query). See 527-001, 524-001, 532-001.

**Diagnostic:** Check whether H8454/G4678 etc. is the OWNER term in the verse_context records. If the VCB analyst correctly classified the verse under that term, the group passes the inner-being filter even when the inner-being engagement is contextually carried.

### 2.3 Directive vcg_id Rule

**New learning:** When constructing CC directives for group verification extracts, vcg_ids must be taken from the dimension extract `verse_context_group_id` field, not from the `id` field of the dimension index. These are not the same value. Batch1 directive used wrong IDs; CC corrected and returned accurate data on re-extraction.

**Programme scope:** All future CC group verification directives.

The automated classifier (KEYWORD) made systematic errors in four areas:

Volitional vs. Cognitive:** The classifier matched "will," "purpose," and "capacity" keywords and labelled groups Volitional/Capacity or Volitional/Will even when the governing engagement was a cognitive faculty or act. Corrected: 979-001 (C01), 1188-001 (C01), 962-002, 1174-001, 3442-001, 3554-001, and others.

## 9. Cluster-Level Analytical Observations — C02

### 9.1 The cognitive cluster is not purely cognitive

C02's 171 groups distribute across 7 dimension categories. The cluster that Scripture calls the "cognitive and deliberative inner life" does not present cognition as a sealed domain. Three recurring integrations were found:

- **Cognitive-spiritual:** Knowledge rooted in the fear of the Lord; wisdom received from God; understanding seeking God. The cognitive faculty in Scripture is consistently oriented toward or dependent on God for its proper functioning.
- **Cognitive-moral:** Discernment as the cognitive faculty that produces moral discrimination; knowledge as moral condition affecting culpability; the hardened heart as cognitive failure with a volitional mechanism.
- **Cognitive-relational:** Being known by God; divine comprehension of the human person; the soul's thoughts transparent to divine perception. The cognitive interior is not private — it is constitutively known by God.

### 9.2 The directional-faculty pattern is pervasive in C02

12+ group pairs across 6 registries show the same inner faculty appearing in opposite moral orientations. This is too consistent to be coincidental. C02 may be the cluster that most clearly evidences this pattern because cognitive faculties are inherently more orientational (they are directed toward objects) than, say, emotional states. Session B should test this hypothesis against the verse data.

### 9.3 The dimension vocabulary is stretched but not broken

The 12-category vocabulary handled C02 adequately with one significant correction (the Theological/Divine-Human vs. Spiritual/God-ward distinction) and one open question (Identity/Selfhood — only one group, 963-003; may need expansion in NT clusters). The vocabulary does not have a category for the directional-faculty pattern, which remains an open research question for Session D.
