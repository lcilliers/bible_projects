## Key Analytical Observations

**1. C08/C09 symmetry:** The virtue cluster (C08) and vice cluster (C09) produce the same leading dimensions — Character/Disposition and Spiritual/God-ward. Both virtue and vice in Scripture are primarily characterological and fundamentally God-relational. This is a significant finding: the biblical inner-being vocabulary does not treat vice as merely behavioural or social, but as a condition of the inner person's orientation toward God.

**2. The pride/majesty dual-register:** The most analytically complex finding in C09. Pride in Scripture is not merely a human fault — it is the attempt to occupy the space that belongs to divine majesty. The Hebrew roots used for human pride are the same roots used for divine glory. This structural overlap is a theological claim embedded in the vocabulary itself. Session B must engage this directly.

**3. Weakness as anomaly:** Registry 170 (weakness) sits uneasily in C09. Paul's treatment of weakness is not a vice to be condemned but a paradoxical inner-being posture in which divine power is most fully expressed. This anomaly should inform the programme's overall taxonomy.

**4. Sloth's inner structure (1374-001):** The desire/will gap is one of the most precise single-group inner-being descriptions in the programme — desire without will. This is sloth at the level of inner-being anatomy, not merely behaviour.
