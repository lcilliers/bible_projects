### Coherence assessment

C13 is a large, thematically coherent cluster organised around the **moral-judicial inner life** — the inner-being territory where the human person stands before God as a moral agent, is assessed by divine justice, experiences the weight of sin and guilt, and responds through repentance, conscience, or disobedience. The cluster has two identifiable poles:

**Pole 1 — The moral-judicial frame (Reg 24, 73, 98, 26):** Justice, condemnation, guilt, and conscience form the evaluative infrastructure. The inner person is characterised as standing before a moral standard — whether God's justice (98), divine verdict (24), accumulated guilt (73), or inner moral witness (26). These registries share the frame of *moral accountability*.

**Pole 2 — The moral-volitional response (Reg 35, 50, 70, 130, 135):** Covetousness, disobedience, greed, reconciliation, and repentance describe inner orientations and acts in relation to the moral frame. These registries share the frame of *moral disposition and response* — the inner person either turning away from God (35, 50, 70) or being restored toward God (130, 135).

The cluster's governing inner-being question is: *what is the condition and movement of the inner person in relation to moral order, divine judgment, and the possibility of restoration?*

**Internal coherence:** Strong. All nine registries engage the moral-judicial dimension of the inner life. The cluster holds together analytically.

### Boundary observations

**Reg 35 (covetousness) — partial misfit.** Seven of its ten groups derive from the Greek thumos root family (prothumia, euthumeō, euthumos, athumeō, prothumos, prothumōs) — these are eagerness, cheerfulness, discouragement terms that belong more naturally to an emotional-motivational cluster than a moral-judicial one. Only three groups (778-001, 778-002 from pleonexia; 2079-001 from prothumōs) have genuine moral-judicial content. The thumos-root groups could sit in an emotional cluster (C01–C05 range) with better affinity. **This is a boundary observation — not a reassignment proposal.** Flagging for researcher awareness; the programme's cluster architecture decisions are outside Dimension Review scope. To be noted but not acted on here.

**Reg 26 (conscience) — partial misfit.** Two groups (1842-001 proginōskō, 1847-001 prognōsis, 1848-001 proeidon) are divine foreknowledge terms — cognitively and theologically interesting but not obviously within the moral-judicial frame. They carry a strong divine-cognitive character that might sit more comfortably in a cognition or theological cluster. The conscience terms (454-001 suneidō, 2739-001 sunoida, 53-group in Reg 73) are clearly at home here; the foreknowledge terms are the anomaly.

**Reg 98 (justice) — very large.** 50 groups is the largest registry in this cluster by a wide margin. Dimension Review for Reg 98 will require a split Phase C session.

**Reg 130 (reconciliation) — placement rationale is clear.** Reconciliation belongs in C13: it is the positive resolution of the broken moral-relational state that the cluster otherwise describes from the negative side. Its presence gives the cluster completeness — the arc from sin/guilt/condemnation through repentance to reconciliation is fully represented.
