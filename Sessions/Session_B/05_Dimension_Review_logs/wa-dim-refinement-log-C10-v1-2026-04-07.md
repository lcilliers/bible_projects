## Cross-Group Patterns — Phase C Summary

### Convergence observations

**1. Moral/Conscience dominates C10.** The cluster shows strong convergence on Moral/Conscience across every registry. Of 111 groups, approximately 44 will carry Moral/Conscience after the Phase C revisions. This is coherent — C10 is the moral-character cluster — but the density of Moral/Conscience designations across such a diverse vocabulary warrants Session B attention to whether this label adequately differentiates sub-dimensions within the moral domain (e.g., moral standing vs. moral assessment vs. moral character quality).

**2. Character/Disposition emerges as a significant dimension.** The programme's existing vocabulary includes Character/Disposition but the automated classifier rarely used it. Phase C analysis proposes this dimension for 10+ groups where the inner-being content is a stable dispositional quality rather than a moral judgment, spiritual orientation, or cognitive act. This is consistent across purity, goodness, sincerity, truthfulness, and uprightness registries.

**3. Spiritual/God-ward is the second most common dimension.** Approximately 25 groups carry Spiritual/God-ward, spanning the inner life oriented toward God in worship, prayer, confession, reception, and encounter. This is coherent for the C10 cluster — moral character in Scripture consistently has a God-ward orientation.

**4. Theological/Divine-Human groups mark divine initiative.** Groups assigned Theological/Divine-Human across C10 consistently describe divine character qualities and divine action toward humanity — not just the encounter, but the characterisation of God's inner being as the source of goodness, kindness, faithfulness, and righteousness. This is a coherent sub-pattern within C10.

### Divergence / vocabulary tensions

**1. No Emotional/Affective dimension exists in the programme vocabulary.** Several groups in C10 — particularly in innocence (930-007) and goodness (884-003) — describe inner states of anguish, dread, hope, longing, and delight that resist classification under Moral/Conscience, Spiritual/God-ward, or Character/Disposition. The programme vocabulary has a gap here. This is a significant vocabulary observation for Session D.

**2. Eschatological/transformational dimension not captured.** Groups in integrity (935-001) and faithfulness (872-007) describe the eschatological transformation of the inner person — a dimension distinct from any existing label. Theological/Divine-Human is used as the best available fit, but the programme should consider whether this dimension needs a dedicated label.

**3. Registry 77 named "honesty" but all terms are righteousness vocabulary.** All terms in this registry are *tsaddiq* (H6662) and *tsedeqah* (H6666) — standard Hebrew righteousness terms. The registry label does not match its actual vocabulary. This is a programme-level naming question.

**4. The alētheia cluster spans five dimensions.** No single dimension captures the *alētheia* word family's full range. Session B will need to address this deliberately.

## Vocabulary Observations

1. **Emotional/Affective gap:** The current 12-category vocabulary has no label for inner emotional or affective states. C10 reveals this gap clearly across multiple registries (innocence, goodness, uprightness). This is a programme-level vocabulary decision.

2. **Eschatological dimension:** The putting-on of incorruptibility (integrity, Reg. 92) and the transformation of ashes to beauty (faithfulness, Reg. 60, group 872-007) name a dimension of inner-being expectation and transformation that the vocabulary does not adequately label. Theological/Divine-Human is used as the best available fit but is not ideal.

3. **Character/Disposition underused by automated classifier:** Phase C reveals that Character/Disposition is the appropriate dimension for a significant number of groups that the automated classifier assigned to Moral/Conscience, Volitional/Will, or Relational/Social. The programme may want to review the keyword triggers for this category.

4. **Distinction between Moral/Conscience and Character/Disposition:** Several Phase C corrections clarified this boundary. Moral/Conscience is appropriate for: evaluative judgments of moral standing, moral assessment by God or community, moral failure and guilt, moral law. Character/Disposition is appropriate for: stable inner qualities of character (Spirit-produced or otherwise), dispositional orientations that are enduring rather than evaluative.
